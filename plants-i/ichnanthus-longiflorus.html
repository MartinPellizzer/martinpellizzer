Ichnanthus longiflorus

Ichnanthus longiflorus, commonly known as the long-flowered rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy habitats in North America.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and rushes, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered greenish-white flowers that are typically arranged in spikelets, typical of the Cyperaceae family. Ichnanthus longiflorus thrives in moist, nutrient-poor soils and is often found in areas with standing water or saturated ground.
