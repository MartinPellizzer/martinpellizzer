Ixora splendida

Ixora splendida, commonly known as the flame flower, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant red or orange inflorescences, which consist of numerous small, tubular flowers clustered together. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Ixora splendida is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
