Indigofera trichopoda var. trichopoda

Indigofera trichopoda var. trichopoda, commonly known as the African indigo or Indian indigo, is a shrub native to tropical Africa and southern Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces pods containing small, dark seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a dye source. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and cultural contexts.
