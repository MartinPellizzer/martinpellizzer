Isoloma longifolium

Isoloma longifolium, commonly known as the long-leaved lichen, is a foliose lichen that thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It has a distinctive appearance with its elongated, leaf-like lobes that are typically greenish-gray in color. The plant forms a symbiotic relationship with algae, which provides it with nutrients through photosynthesis. Isoloma longifolium is often found on bark, stones, or soil in temperate regions.

Its slow growth and sensitivity to environmental changes make it an indicator species for ecological health.
