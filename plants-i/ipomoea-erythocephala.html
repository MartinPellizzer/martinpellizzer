Ipomoea erythocephala

Ipomoea erythocephala, commonly known as the red-headed morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep red. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and has also been used in traditional medicine.
