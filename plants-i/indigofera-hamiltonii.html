Indigofera hamiltonii

Indigofera hamiltonii, commonly known as Hamilton's indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, pod-like fruits.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used traditionally in some cultures for dyeing textiles.
