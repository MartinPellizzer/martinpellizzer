Impatiens purroi

Impatiens purroi, commonly known as the golden crown impatiens, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers and broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows as an annual or short-lived perennial, reaching heights of up to 3 feet. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden areas.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period, which can last from spring through early autumn.
