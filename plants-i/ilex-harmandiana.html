Ilex harmandiana

Ilex harmandiana, commonly known as Harmand's holly, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, red berries that are toxic to humans but serve as a food source for various birds. Ilex harmandiana thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural use in gardens and landscapes.
