Indigofera effusa

Indigofera effusa, commonly known as wild indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including woodlands and disturbed soils, and is often found in the southeastern United States.

Its historical significance includes use in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dyes.
