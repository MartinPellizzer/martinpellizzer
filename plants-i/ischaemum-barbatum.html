Ischaemum barbatum

Ischaemum barbatum, commonly known as hairy foxtail, is a flowering grass species native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and dense, bristly inflorescences that resemble a foxtail. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including roadsides and fields. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its common name.

Ischaemum barbatum is often considered a weed but has been studied for its ecological and potential medicinal properties.
