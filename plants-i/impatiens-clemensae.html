Impatiens clemensae

Impatiens clemensae, commonly known as the Clemens' Impatiens, is a species native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The flowers typically come in shades of red, pink, and white, with five petals and a distinctive spur at the base. The leaves are ovate and slightly hairy, providing a textured appearance.

This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
