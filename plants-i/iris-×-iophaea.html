Iris × iophaea

Iris × iophaea, commonly known as the hybrid bearded iris, is a cultivated variety resulting from the cross between Iris germanica and Iris pallida.

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in late spring to early summer. The flowers usually have a prominent beard on the lower petal, which is a distinguishing feature of the bearded iris group. The plant prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden borders and landscapes.

Its ornamental value lies in its vibrant colors and elegant appearance, which add visual interest to gardens throughout the growing season.
