Inga preussii

Inga preussii, commonly known as the purple-leaf inga, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant is notable for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in shaded environments, making it a common understory species in tropical forests. Its fruits are pods that contain edible seeds, and the plant is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Inga preussii also plays a role in soil enrichment due to its nitrogen-fixing capabilities.
