Impatiens acaulis var. acaulis

Impatiens acaulis var. acaulis, commonly known as the slender impatiens, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, white, and red, typically blooming from summer to autumn. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Due to its attractive appearance and relatively easy care, it is often cultivated in both ornamental and naturalized settings.
