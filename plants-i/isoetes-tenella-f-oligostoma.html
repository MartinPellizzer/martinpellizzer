Isoetes tenella f. oligostoma

Isoetes tenella f. oligostoma, commonly known as the narrow-leaf water fern, is a rare aquatic plant belonging to the genus Isoetes.

It is characterized by its finely divided, grass-like leaves that are typically submerged in water. The plant forms dense, compact clusters and is often found in still or slow-moving freshwater environments. Its spore-producing structures, called sporangia, are located on the underside of the leaves.

This fern is notable for its ecological significance in maintaining water quality and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
