Ilex paraguariensis f. dasyprionata

Ilex paraguariensis f. dasyprionata, commonly known as the Paraguayan holly, is a variant of the yerba mate plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves and small, dark berries that are typically harvested for their caffeine-rich contents. The plant thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often cultivated for its medicinal and stimulant properties. Its foliage is evergreen, providing year-round coverage, and it exhibits a compact, bushy growth habit.

This form is particularly noted for its robustness and adaptability to various growing conditions.
