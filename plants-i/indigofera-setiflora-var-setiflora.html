Indigofera setiflora var. setiflora

Indigofera setiflora var. setiflora, commonly known as the slender indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and trifoliate leaves with slender, hairy leaflets. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, often blooming in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in warm climates.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in dye production.
