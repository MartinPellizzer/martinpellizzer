Iris planifolia subsp. palaestina

Iris planifolia subsp.

palaestina, commonly known as the Palestine flatleaf iris, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in gardens and naturalized areas.
