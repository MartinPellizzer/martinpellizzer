Ixeris chinensis subsp. strigosa

Ixeris chinensis subsp.

strigosa, commonly known as the hairy yellow flower or hairy dandelion, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to East Asia. It features a low-growing, mat-forming structure with soft, hairy leaves that are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine pubescence.

The plant produces bright yellow flowers with ray florets, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it an attractive plant for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
