Indigofera karinensis

Indigofera karinensis, commonly known as Karin's indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pea-like flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in moist, tropical forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it beneficial in soil enrichment.
