Impatiens brevicornis

Impatiens brevicornis, commonly known as the dwarf impatiens, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant features heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a velvety texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shady garden beds.

Its rapid growth and ability to bloom throughout the growing season make it a favored ornamental plant in horticulture.
