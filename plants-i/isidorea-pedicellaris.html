Isidorea pedicellaris

Isidorea pedicellaris, commonly known as the creeping germander, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Isidorea pedicellaris is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
