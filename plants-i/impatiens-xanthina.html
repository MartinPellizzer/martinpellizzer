Impatiens xanthina

Impatiens xanthina, commonly known as the yellow impatiens, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow petals and delicate, spurred sepals. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its flowers attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
