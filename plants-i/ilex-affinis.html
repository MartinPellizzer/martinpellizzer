Ilex affinis

Ilex affinis, commonly known as the Florida holly, is a deciduous evergreen shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows to a height of 6 to 15 feet, featuring glossy, dark green leaves that turn reddish in the fall. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in the spring, followed by bright red berries that are an important food source for birds. Ilex affinis thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Its adaptability and aesthetic appeal contribute to its widespread cultivation in the region.
