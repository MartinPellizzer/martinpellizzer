Isatis laevigata

Isatis laevigata, commonly known as the smooth false flax, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like mustard and cabbage. The plant features alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are usually smooth or slightly hairy. Its yellow flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer, are arranged in racemes and attract various pollinators.

Isatis laevigata is often found in moist woodlands and along stream banks, where it thrives in partial to full shade.
