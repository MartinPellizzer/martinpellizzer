Indigofera strobilifera subsp. lanuginosa

Indigofera strobilifera subsp.

lanuginosa, commonly known as the woolly indigo, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula. It is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple.

The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, which enhances soil fertility.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its ornamental value and traditional uses in local communities.
