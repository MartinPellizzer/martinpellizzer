Impatiens clavicuspis var. brevicuspis

Impatiens clavicuspis var. brevicuspis, commonly known as the short-ciliate impatiens, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, spurred flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink and white. The leaves are ovate with a slightly hairy surface, and the plant prefers shaded, moist environments. It is a member of the Balsaminaceae family, known for its rapid growth and ornamental value.

This variety is distinguished by its shorter, more densely arranged cilia on the petals compared to the typical form.
