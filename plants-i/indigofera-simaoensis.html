Indigofera simaoensis

Indigofera simaoensis, commonly known as Simao indigo, is a flowering plant species native to China.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and erect growth habit. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to purple, depending on the variety. It is often found in forested areas and is adapted to moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and potential ecological importance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
