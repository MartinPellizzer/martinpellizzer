Isachne beneckei var. magna

Isachne beneckei var. magna, commonly known as the giant isachne, is a perennial grass species native to certain regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and elongated, narrow leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
