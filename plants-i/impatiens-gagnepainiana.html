Impatiens gagnepainiana

Impatiens gagnepainiana, commonly known as the Gagnepain's impatiens, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, spurred petals and clusters of small, colorful flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as under the canopy of tropical forests. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a smooth, glossy surface, while its stems are slender and often hairy.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
