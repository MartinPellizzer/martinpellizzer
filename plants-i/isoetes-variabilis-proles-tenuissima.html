Isoetes variabilis proles tenuissima

Isoetes variabilis proles tenuissima, commonly known as the slender-leaf waterwheel, is a species of aquatic fern that thrives in freshwater environments.

It is characterized by its delicate, thread-like leaves that form a rosette around the central axis. The plant produces spore-bearing structures called sporangia, which are typically located on the underside of the leaves. It is often found in shallow, still waters such as ponds and lakes.

Due to its specialized habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and sensitive indicator of water quality.
