Impatiens drepanophora

Impatiens drepanophora, commonly known as the narrow-leaf impatiens, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous species that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors. The plant features slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often slightly hairy. Its flowers are small, tubular, and usually pink or white, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Impatiens drepanophora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
