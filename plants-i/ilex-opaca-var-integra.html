Ilex opaca var. integra

Ilex opaca var. integra, commonly known as the southern holly, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It features elliptical, glossy dark green leaves that are typically smooth and have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value comes from its dense foliage and seasonal color changes, adding year-round interest to landscapes.
