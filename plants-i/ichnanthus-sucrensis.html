Ichnanthus sucrensis

Ichnanthus sucrensis, commonly known as the Sucre's orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in rocky or humid forest environments. The plant features a slender, erect stem with a single, showy flower that has a distinctive white or pale yellow coloration. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the surrounding soil.

Ichnanthus sucrensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
