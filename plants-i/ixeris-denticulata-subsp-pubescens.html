Ixeris denticulata subsp. pubescens

Ixeris denticulata subsp.

pubescens, commonly known as the hairy false dandelion, is a low-growing perennial herb native to East Asia. It features a rosette of hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
