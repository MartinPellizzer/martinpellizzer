Ipomoea fistulosa

Ipomoea fistulosa, commonly known as the purple morning glory, is a flowering vine native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple, often opening in the early morning. The plant has heart-shaped, palmately lobed leaves that are typically green with a slightly hairy texture. Its stems are trailing or climbing and can grow up to several meters in length.

Ipomoea fistulosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
