Impatiens pseudochinensis

Impatiens pseudochinensis, commonly known as the Chinese impatiens, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and a trailing growth habit, making it popular in ornamental horticulture. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Balsaminaceae and is closely related to other species in the Impatiens genus.
