Indigofera glomerata

Indigofera glomerata, commonly known as clustered indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its dense clusters of small, bright yellow flowers. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves that are opposite in arrangement, and it has a woody stem that becomes more branched with age. It is often found in dry, open habitats such as savannas and rocky areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
