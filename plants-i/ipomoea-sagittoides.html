Ipomoea sagittoides

Ipomoea sagittoides, commonly known as the arrowleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant is a vigorous climber with hairy stems and produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Ipomoea sagittoides is often found in wetlands, along stream banks, and in disturbed areas.
