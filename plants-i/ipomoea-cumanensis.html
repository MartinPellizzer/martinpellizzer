Ipomoea cumanensis

Ipomoea cumanensis, commonly known as the Cumanensis morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue or violet. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces tendrils that help it climb and spread. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Ipomoea cumanensis is a member of the Convolvulaceae family, known for its fast-growing vines and ornamental value.
