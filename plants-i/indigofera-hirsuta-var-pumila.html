Indigofera hirsuta var. pumila

Indigofera hirsuta var. pumila, commonly known as the hairy indigo or dwarf indigo, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its small, hairy leaves and clusters of small, pink to purple flowers. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia and is often found in moist, shaded environments. It has a climbing habit and can reach heights of up to 1 meter.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in ecological restoration projects.
