Ipomoea hispidula

Ipomoea hispidula, commonly known as the hairy morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a herbaceous annual vine that produces trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and along stream banks.

Ipomoea hispidula is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
