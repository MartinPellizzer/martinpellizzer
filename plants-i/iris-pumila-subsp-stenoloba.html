Iris pumila subsp. stenoloba

Iris pumila subsp.

stenoloba, commonly known as the narrow-leaf marsh iris, is a low-growing perennial herb native to wetland and marsh habitats in North America. It features rhizomatous growth and produces slender, grass-like leaves that are typically less than 10 cm in length.

The plant blooms with delicate, blue to violet-colored flowers that have three upright petals and three spreading sepals. Its flowers are generally less than 2 cm in diameter and are often clustered in a loose inflorescence.

This subspecies is adapted to moist, acidic soils and is often found in bogs, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.
