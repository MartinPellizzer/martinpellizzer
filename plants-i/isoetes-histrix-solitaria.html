Isoetes histrix solitaria

Isoetes histrix solitaria, commonly known as the solitary water horsetail, is a species of aquatic plant belonging to the genus Isoetes.

It is characterized by its spore-bearing structures called sporangia, which are typically found on specialized stems called strobila. The plant thrives in freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes, where it can anchor itself in muddy or sandy substrates. Its morphology includes a tall, spore-bearing stem with a cluster of spore cases at the apex.

The plant plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by contributing to nutrient cycling and providing habitat for microorganisms.
