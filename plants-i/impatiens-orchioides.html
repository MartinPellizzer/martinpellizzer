Impatiens orchioides

Impatiens orchioides, commonly known as the orchid impatiens, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink and violet. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy and covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests or along stream banks.

The flowers are pollinated primarily by insects, and the plant is known for its rapid growth and adaptability to various soil types.
