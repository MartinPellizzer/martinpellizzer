Indigofera sylvatica

Indigofera sylvatica, commonly known as the forest indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy purple or blue flowers. The plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and shrublands. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and can be dispersed by wind or animals.

Indigofera sylvatica is also valued for its ornamental qualities and traditional uses in some cultures.
