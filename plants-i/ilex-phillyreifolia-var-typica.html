Ilex phillyreifolia var. typica

Ilex phillyreifolia var. typica, commonly known as the Florida holly, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows to a height of 6 to 12 feet, featuring dense, spiny leaves that are glossy green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers in the spring, followed by bright red berries that are a food source for birds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Its hardy nature and ability to tolerate urban conditions further enhance its value in garden settings.
