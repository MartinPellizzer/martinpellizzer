Ilex cerasifolia var. glaziovii

Ilex cerasifolia var. glaziovii, commonly known as the Glaziov's holly, is a shrub or small tree native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters, followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for ornamental landscaping.

This variety is noted for its hardiness and ability to adapt to various environmental conditions.
