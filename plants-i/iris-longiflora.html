Iris longiflora

Iris longiflora, commonly known as the long-flowered iris, is a species of flowering plant in the Iridaceae family.

It is native to the western United States and grows in moist, well-drained soils, often in woodland or meadow habitats. The plant features striking blue to violet flowers with three upright petals and three drooping sepals. Its leaves are sword-shaped and often have a prominent midrib.

Iris longiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
