Indigofera obrajillensis

Indigofera obrajillensis, commonly known as the Mexican indigo plant, is a species of legume native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers that typically range in color from pink to purple. The plant produces pods containing seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and herbal practices.
