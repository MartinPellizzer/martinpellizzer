Itea riparia

Itea riparia, commonly known as smooth willow-herb, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in wetlands, stream banks, and other moist environments, showcasing its adaptability to aquatic or semi-aquatic conditions. The plant features slender, often branching stems that are covered in fine, silky hairs. Its small, clustered flowers are usually white or pale pink, and they bloom in early summer.

Itea riparia has lance-shaped leaves that are typically smooth on both sides, contributing to its characteristic appearance.
