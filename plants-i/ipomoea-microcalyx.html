Ipomoea microcalyx

Ipomoea microcalyx, commonly known as the small-fruited morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its trumpet-shaped, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a velvety texture.

Ipomoea microcalyx is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks.
