Indigofera gilesii

Indigofera gilesii, commonly known as the African indigo or blue bean, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical Africa and is characterized by its erect, slender stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, followed by elongated pods containing kidney-shaped seeds. Indigofera gilesii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used traditionally in various cultural practices.

Its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil also makes it beneficial for soil enrichment in agricultural settings.
