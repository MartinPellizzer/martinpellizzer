Ilex obcordata var. vaccinioides

Ilex obcordata var. vaccinioides, commonly known as the Florida holly, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It features glossy, ovate leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter on the underside. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for wildlife. It has a dense, compact growth habit with a rounded shape.

Its botanical characteristics make it well-suited for use in ornamental landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
