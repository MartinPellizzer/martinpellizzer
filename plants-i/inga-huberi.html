Inga huberi

Inga huberi, commonly known as the white inga, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces pod fruits that contain several seeds, which are often dispersed by animals. Inga huberi is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in agroforestry systems.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a useful species for reforestation and ecological restoration projects.
