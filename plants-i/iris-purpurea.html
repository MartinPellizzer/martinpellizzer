Iris purpurea

Iris purpurea, commonly known as the purple iris, is a striking perennial plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It features tall, upright stems that bear showy, violet-blue flowers with three upright petals and three drooping sepals. The plant has narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Iris purpurea is widely cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
