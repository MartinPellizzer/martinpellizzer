Ixora maxima

Ixora maxima, commonly known as the giant ixora, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, fragrant clusters of bright red or orange flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves that are elliptical in shape. Its woody stems are typically thick and can grow up to several meters in height.

Ixora maxima is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a hedge due to its vibrant flowers and hardy nature.
