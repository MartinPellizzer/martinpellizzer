Isoetes coromandelina subsp. brachyglossa

Isoetes coromandelina subsp.

brachyglossa, commonly known as the narrow-lipped watermilfoil, is a species of aquatic plant belonging to the genus Isoetes. It is characterized by its spore-bearing, spike-like structures called strobila, which are typically found in freshwater environments.

The plant has a distinctive morphology with elongated, ribbon-like leaves that are often submerged. It is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including India and Sri Lanka, and thrives in still or slow-moving water bodies.

This subspecies is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique reproductive structures and ecological adaptations.
