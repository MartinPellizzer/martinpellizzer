Isoetes histrix f. desquamata

Isoetes histrix f. desquamata, commonly known as the scaly water horsetail, is a variant of the water horsetail species.

It is a member of the Equisetaceae family and is characterized by its spore-bearing structures called strobila. The plant typically grows in wet, swampy environments and is known for its distinctive, segmented stems. Its leaves are reduced to small, scale-like structures, which are a defining feature of the genus Isoetes.

This fern-like plant plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by contributing to nutrient cycling and providing habitat for various microorganisms.
