Indigofera erythrogramma subsp. lanceolata

Indigofera erythrogramma subsp.

lanceolata, commonly known as the scarlet wisteria, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia. It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of vibrant red or purple flowers.

The plant produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and disturbed habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation in warm climates.
