Iris hookeri f. pallidiflora

Iris hookeri f. pallidiflora, commonly known as the pale-flowered iris, is a flowering plant belonging to the Iridaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and striking pale blue to white flowers with a distinctive three-lobed petal structure. Native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, this plant thrives in well-drained soils and cooler climates. The flowers typically bloom in late spring to early summer, adding a delicate beauty to its natural habitat.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after plant for botanical gardens and specialized collections.
