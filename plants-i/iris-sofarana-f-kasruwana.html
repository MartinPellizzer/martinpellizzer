Iris sofarana f. kasruwana

Iris sofarana f. kasruwana, commonly known as the Kasruwana iris, is a botanical variant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers with a distinctive yellowish-green stripe running down the center of the petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a dense basal rosette.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphological features and limited distribution.
