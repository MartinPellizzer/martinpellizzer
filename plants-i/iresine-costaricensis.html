Iresine costaricensis

Iresine costaricensis, commonly known as the red leaf iron plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to Central America.

It features striking, velvety red or purple leaves that are often variegated with white or green patterns. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in shades of pink or white, typically clustered in racemes. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Iresine costaricensis is popular in ornamental gardening for its vibrant foliage and ease of cultivation.
