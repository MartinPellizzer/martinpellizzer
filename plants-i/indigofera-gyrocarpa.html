Indigofera gyrocarpa

Indigofera gyrocarpa, commonly known as the African indigo or African woad, is a leguminous shrub native to tropical Africa.

It produces distinctive, spirally arranged leaves and small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is notable for its unique fruit structure, which consists of a spiral helix of fused pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and traditional uses in various regions.
