Indigofera welwitschii

Indigofera welwitschii, commonly known as the African indigo plant, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and can thrive in arid conditions.

Its historical significance includes use in traditional dyeing processes, contributing to its cultural and ecological importance.
