Ixora brunonis

Ixora brunonis, commonly known as the purple flame or flame ixora, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 3 meters, featuring dense, glossy green leaves. The plant produces clusters of bright red or orange-red flowers, which are borne on long, branched inflorescences. Ixora brunonis is known for its showy, long-lasting blooms that attract pollinators such as birds and bees.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
