Inga lignosa

Inga lignosa, commonly known as the black inga, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant has a woody stem and can grow up to 20 meters tall, making it a significant component of rainforest ecosystems. Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in axillary inflorescences.

The leaves are typically alternate and can be either pinnately or palmately compound, contributing to its ecological and ornamental value.
