Ipomoea pseudomalvaeoides f. sericea

Ipomoea pseudomalvaeoides f. sericea, commonly known as the silky morning glory, is a variant of the morning glory family (Convolvulaceae) characterized by its distinctive silky, hairy foliage.

This plant is native to tropical regions of the Americas and thrives in warm, humid climates. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a velvety appearance. The flowers are large, trumpet-shaped, and usually come in shades of purple or blue, blooming in the early morning.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its striking visual appeal and rapid growth habit.
