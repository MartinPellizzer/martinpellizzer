Isatis Tinctoria Var. Indigotica

Isatis tinctoria var. indigotica is a striking plant known for its vibrant blue flowers and historical significance in dye production. Native to parts of Asia, this variant of the woad plant has been cultivated for centuries to produce indigo dye. Its deep blue petals and sturdy growth make it a popular choice for gardens and traditional crafts. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it adaptable to various climates. Despite its beauty, it contains alkaloids that can be toxic if ingested, requiring caution in cultivation and handling.
