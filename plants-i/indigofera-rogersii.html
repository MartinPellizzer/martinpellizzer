Indigofera rogersii

Indigofera rogersii, commonly known as the Arizona indigo, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant is adapted to arid environments and thrives in well-drained soils. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind.

Indigofera rogersii is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
