Indigofera corniculata

Indigofera corniculata, commonly known as the horned indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, horn-shaped seed pods. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink or purple.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
