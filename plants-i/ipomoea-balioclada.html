Ipomoea balioclada

Ipomoea balioclada, commonly known as the Balinese morning glory, is a tropical climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple, often opening in the morning and closing by midday. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves and is known for its rapid growth habit. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Ipomoea balioclada is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant in tropical regions.
