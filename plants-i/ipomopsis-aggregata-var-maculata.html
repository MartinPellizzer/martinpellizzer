Ipomopsis aggregata var. maculata

Ipomopsis aggregata var. maculata, commonly known as the spotted blazingstar, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking pink to purple tubular flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has hairy, branched stems. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slight pubescence.

The variety 'maculata' is distinguished by its spotted or marbled markings on the flower bracts, adding to its ornamental value.
