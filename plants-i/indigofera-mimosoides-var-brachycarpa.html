Indigofera mimosoides var. brachycarpa

Indigofera mimosoides var. brachycarpa, commonly known as the small-fruited mimosa indigo, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has potential uses in traditional medicine and soil enrichment due to its nitrogen-fixing ability.
