Ixia fistulosa

Ixia fistulosa, commonly known as the yellow ox tongue, is a striking perennial plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow, tongue-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces seed pods that split open to release its seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Ixia fistulosa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its unique floral display.
