Isatis atropatana

Isatis atropatana, commonly known as the dark woad, is a perennial herb native to parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant is characterized by its dark blue or purple flowers, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of hairs.

Isatis atropatana is known for its historical use in dye production and traditional medicine.
