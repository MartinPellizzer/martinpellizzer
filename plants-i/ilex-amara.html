Ilex amara

Ilex amara, commonly known as the bitter holly, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often adorned with small white or yellow spots. The plant produces small, red berries that are toxic to humans and animals, containing high concentrations of irritants. Ilex amara thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and moist, shaded areas.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically significant plant in its native habitat.
