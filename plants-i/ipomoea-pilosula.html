Ipomoea pilosula

Ipomoea pilosula, commonly known as the silky morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and blue. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Ipomoea pilosula is a climbing vine that can reach lengths of several meters, making it a common ornamental and invasive species in some areas.
