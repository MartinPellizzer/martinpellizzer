Indigofera bijuga

Indigofera bijuga, commonly known as the creeping indigo or slender indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, trifoliate leaves, and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. Its seed pods are elongated and contain several elliptical seeds.

Indigofera bijuga is often used in traditional medicine and can also serve as a forage plant for livestock.
