Iresine floribunda

Iresine floribunda, commonly known as the redleaf plant or red leaf sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial native to tropical regions of South America.

It features striking, velvety leaves that are typically a deep red or maroon color, often with contrasting green veins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually pink or purple, arranged in clusters at the ends of stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sun.

Iresine floribunda is often cultivated for its ornamental foliage and is relatively easy to care for in suitable climates.
