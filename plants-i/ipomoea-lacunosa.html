Ipomoea lacunosa

Ipomoea lacunosa, commonly known as the morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its trumpet-shaped flowers that open in the morning and close by midday. The plant is a vigorous vine with heart-shaped leaves and can grow up to several meters in length. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its seeds are encased in a distinctive, fibrous fruit that splits open when mature.
