Inga uraguensis var. parvifolia

Inga uraguensis var. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved inga, is a shrub or small tree native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and legume fruits. The plant has trifoliate leaves with small, ovate leaflets and produces white or pale yellow flowers arranged in racemes. Its fruits are legumes that contain several seeds, and the plant is often found in moist, shaded environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
