Ipomoea tanganyikensis

Ipomoea tanganyikensis, commonly known as the Tanganyikan morning glory, is a climbing vine native to East Africa.

It produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, blooming in the early morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and is adapted to thrive in moist, tropical environments. Its rapid growth habit makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

However, it can become invasive if not properly managed.
