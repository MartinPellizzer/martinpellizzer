Ixora grandifolia var. coriacea

Ixora grandifolia var. coriacea, commonly known as the rough-leaved ixora, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that give it a distinctive texture. The plant produces clusters of small, bright red flowers that bloom throughout the year. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its hardy nature and vibrant blooms make it a valuable addition to both landscapes and container gardens.
