Ixia cepacea

Ixia cepacea, commonly known as the fire lily, is a striking perennial plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant orange or red flowers that resemble flames. The plant features strap-shaped, grass-like leaves that are typically evergreen. Ixia cepacea thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its blooming period usually occurs in late winter to early spring, adding color to gardens during cooler months.
