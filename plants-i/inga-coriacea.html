Inga coriacea

Inga coriacea, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pendulous flowers. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in various soil types, making it adaptable to different environments. Its fruit pods contain edible seeds, and the tree is often used in reforestation efforts due to its ecological benefits.

The leaves and pods are also used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
