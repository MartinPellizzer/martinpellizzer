Inula attica var. limonella

Inula attica var. limonella, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny grasslands and rocky soils. It produces small, elongated flower heads that bloom in late summer.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the genus Inula.
