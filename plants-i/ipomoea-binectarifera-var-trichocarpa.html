Ipomoea binectarifera var. trichocarpa

Ipomoea binectarifera var. trichocarpa, commonly known as the Trichocarpa morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in the morning and close in the afternoon. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Its rapid growth and climbing habit make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking vibrant, seasonal color.
