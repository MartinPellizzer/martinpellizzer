Indigofera patula subsp. patula

Indigofera patula subsp.

patula, commonly known as slender indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and southern Asia. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers.

The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodland or grassland habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
