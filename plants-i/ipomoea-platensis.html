Ipomoea platensis

Ipomoea platensis, commonly known as the morning glory, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in a variety of colors, including blue, purple, and white. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Ipomoea platensis is a member of the Convolvulaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in moist, sunny environments.

Its botanical features make it both a popular ornamental plant and a potential invasive species in some regions.
