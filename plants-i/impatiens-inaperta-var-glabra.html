Impatiens inaperta var. glabra

Impatiens inaperta var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth impatiens, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, hairless stems and broad, ovate leaves that are typically bright green. The flowers are showy, with five petals that are usually pink or white in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
