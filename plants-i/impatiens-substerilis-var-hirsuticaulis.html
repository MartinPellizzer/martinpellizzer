Impatiens substerilis var. hirsuticaulis

Impatiens substerilis var. hirsuticaulis, commonly known as the hairy-stemmed impatiens, is a variant of the impatiens genus native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its hairy, pubescent stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and red. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden settings.
