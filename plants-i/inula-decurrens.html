Inula decurrens

Inula decurrens, commonly known as the creeping elecampane, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant typically grows in moist, grassy areas and has a distinctive, aromatic, and hairy stem. Its leaves are lance-shaped and gradually narrow towards the base, forming a basal rosette.

Inula decurrens produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
