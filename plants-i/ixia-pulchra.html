Ixia pulchra

Ixia pulchra, commonly known as the African daffodil, is a perennial bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It features striking, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, which bloom in spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that form a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Ixia pulchra is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to naturalize in suitable climates.
