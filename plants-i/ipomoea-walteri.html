Ipomoea walteri

Ipomoea walteri, commonly known as Walter's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of purple and blue. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and along stream banks.

Ipomoea walteri is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
