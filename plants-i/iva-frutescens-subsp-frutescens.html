Iva frutescens subsp. frutescens

Iva frutescens subsp.

frutescens, commonly known as swamp milkweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States. It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along streambanks, thriving in moist to water-saturated soils.

The plant has alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers. It is a member of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is known for its role in supporting pollinators like butterflies and bees.

Its fibrous roots help stabilize soil, making it ecologically valuable in wetland ecosystems.
