Ipomoea nitida

Ipomoea nitida, commonly known as the silverleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and striking silver-veined, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue, purple, or white, blooming in the morning. Ipomoea nitida is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in humid conditions.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
