Inga pittieri

Inga pittieri, commonly known as the pink inga, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces pods containing seeds that are often dispersed by animals. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical forests.

Inga pittieri is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological systems.
