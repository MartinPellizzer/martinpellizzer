Ismene amancaes var. sulphurea

Ismene amancaes var. sulphurea, commonly known as the sulphur yellow orchid, is a striking tropical orchid native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers, which feature intricate patterns of gold and orange. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid forest environments. Its leaves are elongated and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its bright floral display.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
