Indigofera pseudoparvula

Indigofera pseudoparvula, commonly known as the small-leaved indigo, is a flowering plant species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pinnate leaves and clustered, pea-like flowers. The plant produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds, typical of many legumes. It is often found in open woodlands and disturbed sites, thriving in well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and potential agricultural applications.
