Indigofera foliolosa

Indigofera foliolosa, commonly known as the leafy indigo or foliolose indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, particularly in countries like Nigeria and Uganda. The plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits. Its small, pea-like flowers are typically pale blue or white, and it produces pods that contain small, dark seeds.

Indigofera foliolosa is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important plant in agricultural systems.
