Indigofera sesquipedalis

Indigofera sesquipedalis, commonly known as the giant indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas, particularly in Central and South America. The plant is characterized by its large, compound leaves and striking purple to blue flowers that bloom in clusters. It has a deep root system that helps it thrive in nutrient-poor soils, a common trait among legumes.

Additionally, it plays a role in nitrogen fixation, enhancing soil fertility through symbiotic relationships with nitrogen-fixing bacteria.
