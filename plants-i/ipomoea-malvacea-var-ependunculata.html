Ipomoea malvacea var. ependunculata

Ipomoea malvacea var. ependunculata, commonly known as the purple morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves and striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant is a climbing vine that can reach lengths of several meters, supported by its tendrils. Its flowers are typically trumpet-shaped, with five petals and a prominent, showy corolla.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
