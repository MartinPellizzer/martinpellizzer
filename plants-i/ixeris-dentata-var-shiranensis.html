Ixeris dentata var. shiranensis

Ixeris dentata var. shiranensis, commonly known as the Japanese dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features a low-growing rosette of narrow, spoon-shaped leaves with finely toothed margins. The plant produces bright yellow composite flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate foliage and vibrant floral display.
