Ilex aquifolium var. argentea-elegantissima

Ilex aquifolium var. argentea-elegantissima, commonly known as the silver-veined holly, is a cultivar of the common holly that exhibits striking botanical features.

Its leaves are characterized by a vibrant green upper surface and a silvery-white underside, creating a visually appealing contrast. The plant produces small, red berries that are typically non-toxic to humans but can be harmful to pets. It is a deciduous shrub or small tree, typically reaching heights of 6 to 10 feet.

This variety thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.
