Ipomoea capillata

Ipomoea capillata, commonly known as the silky morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun for optimal growth.

Ipomoea capillata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
