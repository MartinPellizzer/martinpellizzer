Ipomoea repens f. suffrutescens

Ipomoea repens f. suffrutescens, commonly known as the creeping morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is a perennial vine characterized by its prostrate growth habit and hairy stems. The leaves are heart-shaped and often have a velvety texture, while the flowers are typically pale blue or white with a distinctive funnel shape. It thrives in moist, sunny environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

This plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread aggressively, making it both a decorative and sometimes invasive species.
