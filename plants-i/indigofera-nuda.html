Indigofera nuda

Indigofera nuda, commonly known as the naked indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. Its flowers are typically small and either pink or white, arranged in racemes.

Indigofera nuda is often used in traditional medicine and can also serve as a forage plant for livestock.
