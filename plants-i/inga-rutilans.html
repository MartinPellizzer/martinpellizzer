Inga rutilans

Inga rutilans, commonly known as the golden inga, is a species of legume native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its pinnate leaves, which are often compound and have a glossy appearance. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, and it produces pods that contain edible seeds.

Inga rutilans is also notable for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important plant in agroforestry systems.
