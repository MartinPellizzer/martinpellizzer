Iris lacustris f. albiflora

Iris lacustris f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered lake iris, is a botanical variant of the lake iris species.

It is characterized by its striking white petals, which contrast with its deep green foliage. This plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and the edges of lakes. Its flowers bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and its ecological role in wetland habitats.
