Irvingia caerulea

Irvingia caerulea, commonly known as the African mango, is a deciduous shrub native to West Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves and clusters of small, pale yellow flowers. The plant produces oblong, yellowish fruits that contain a single seed surrounded by a fleshy, aromatic pulp. Irvingia caerulea is valued for its potential health benefits, including its high content of dietary fiber and healthy fats.

It is also used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.
