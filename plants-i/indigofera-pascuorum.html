Indigofera pascuorum

Indigofera pascuorum, commonly known as meadow indigo, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. It is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.

The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.
