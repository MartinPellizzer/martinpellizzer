Ipomoea batatas var. lobata

Ipomoea batatas var. lobata, commonly known as the purple sweet potato, is a variant of the sweet potato plant characterized by its distinctive, lobed leaves and vibrant purple to red pigmentation in its tuberous roots.

This plant belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is native to tropical regions of the Americas. Its tubers are rich in carbohydrates, dietary fiber, and various nutrients such as vitamin A and potassium. The lobated leaf structure is a key botanical feature that distinguishes it from other sweet potato varieties.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
