Iberis amara var. arvatica

Iberis amara var. arvatica, commonly known as bitter candytuft, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or dry soils and thrives in full sun. The plant produces small, white flowers in early spring, followed by spherical seed pods that split open to release seeds. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, with a slightly hairy texture.

It is often used in rock gardens due to its hardy nature and ornamental value.
