Ischaemum striatum

Ischaemum striatum, commonly known as the narrowleaf beakgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, exhibiting a slender, upright growth habit. The plant has narrow, linear leaves with a distinct midrib and produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences. Its flowers are wind-pollinated, and the seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its ability to colonize new areas.

Ischaemum striatum is often found in grasslands and is considered an important component of native plant communities.
