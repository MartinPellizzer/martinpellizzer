Indigofera manyoniensis

Indigofera manyoniensis, commonly known as the African indigo plant, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment. Its seeds are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of dye.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland and savanna habitats.
