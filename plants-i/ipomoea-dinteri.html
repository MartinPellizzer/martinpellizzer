Ipomoea dinteri

Ipomoea dinteri, commonly known as the Dinter's morning glory, is a striking flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep purple, often appearing in the early morning. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is a climbing vine that can reach lengths of several meters, making it suitable for trellises or pergolas.

Ipomoea dinteri is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its dramatic floral display.
