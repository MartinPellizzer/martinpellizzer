Impatiens salicifolia

Impatiens salicifolia, commonly known as the willow-leaved impatiens, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, willow-like leaves that are typically lance-shaped and have a smooth, glossy surface. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, which bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories or near water sources.

Impatiens salicifolia is a popular ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and vibrant floral display.
