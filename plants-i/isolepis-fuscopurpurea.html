Isolepis fuscopurpurea

Isolepis fuscopurpurea, commonly known as purple marsh grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth and slender, cylindrical stems that often display a reddish or purple hue. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a bluish or purplish tinge.

Isolepis fuscopurpurea plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
