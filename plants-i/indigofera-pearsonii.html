Indigofera pearsonii

Indigofera pearsonii, commonly known as the pearson's indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.

Additionally, like other members of its genus, it has the ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
