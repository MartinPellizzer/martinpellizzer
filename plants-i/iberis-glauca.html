Iberis glauca

Iberis glauca, commonly known as candytuft, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its low, mat-forming growth habit and clusters of small, white or pink flowers. The plant features glossy, dark green foliage that adds visual interest throughout the growing season. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Iberis glauca is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
