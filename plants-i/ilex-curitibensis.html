Ilex curitibensis

Ilex curitibensis, commonly known as the Curitiba holly, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to southeastern Brazil.

It is characterized by its dense, glossy dark green leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red berries in the autumn. These berries are an important food source for various bird species.

Ilex curitibensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
