Isatis campylocarpa

Isatis campylocarpa, commonly known as the woolly false flax, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many flowering plants with small, cross-shaped flowers. The plant is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, and its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe ecosystems.

Isatis campylocarpa is not widely cultivated but is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological role.
