Indigofera rostrata

Indigofera rostrata, commonly known as the African indigo or red sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are clustered in dense spikes and are usually violet or blue in color.

Indigofera rostrata is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in arid conditions.
