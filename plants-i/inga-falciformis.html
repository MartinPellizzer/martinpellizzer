Inga falciformis

Inga falciformis, commonly known as the sickle-leaf inga, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its pinnately compound leaves with sickle-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by elongated pods containing edible seeds.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.
