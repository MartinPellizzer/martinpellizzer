Indigofera kavangoensis

Indigofera kavangoensis, commonly known as the Kavango indigo bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility.

Its ornamental value and potential medicinal uses make it of interest in ecological and agricultural contexts.
