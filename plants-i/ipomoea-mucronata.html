Ipomoea mucronata

Ipomoea mucronata, commonly known as the spurred morning glory, is a flowering vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet. The plant produces distinctive spurred petals, which are a key identifying feature. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found growing along fences or in shaded areas.

Ipomoea mucronata is also notable for its rapid growth and ability to climb various structures.
