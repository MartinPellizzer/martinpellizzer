Indigofera oubanguiensis

Indigofera oubanguiensis, commonly known as the Central African indigo plant, is a leguminous shrub native to tropical regions of Central and West Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable in ecological and agricultural contexts.

Its seeds are often used in traditional medicine, and it plays a role in local biodiversity and soil health.
