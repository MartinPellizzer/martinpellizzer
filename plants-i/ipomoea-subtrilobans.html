Ipomoea subtrilobans

Ipomoea subtrilobans, commonly known as the three-lobed morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The leaves are heart-shaped with three distinct lobes, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

The plant is part of the Convolvulaceae family, which includes many other morning glory species.
