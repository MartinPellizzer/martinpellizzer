Ipomoea cymosa var. culta

Ipomoea cymosa var. culta, commonly known as the creeping blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant blue to violet flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant features heart-shaped, palmate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a ground cover.
