Iris asiatica

Iris asiatica, commonly known as the Japanese flag iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features sword-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and upright, with a prominent central vein. The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers with three upright petals and three drooping sepals, resembling a flag. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Iris asiatica is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
