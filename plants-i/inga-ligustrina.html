Inga ligustrina

Inga ligustrina, commonly known as the chocolate bean tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it an important plant in agroforestry systems.

Inga ligustrina is also valued for its ornamental appeal and potential in sustainable land management.
