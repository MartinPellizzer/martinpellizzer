Indigofera texana

Indigofera texana, commonly known as Texas indigo, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and prairies.

The plant's historical significance includes its use in traditional dyeing practices.
