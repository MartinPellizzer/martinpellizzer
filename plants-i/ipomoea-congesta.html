Ipomoea congesta

Ipomoea congesta, commonly known as the clustered morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of purple and blue. The plant is a climbing vine with tendrils that allow it to ascend supports or other structures. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Ipomoea congesta is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
