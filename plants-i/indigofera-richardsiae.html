Indigofera richardsiae

Indigofera richardsiae, commonly known as Richards' indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is notable for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a significant species in its native habitat.
