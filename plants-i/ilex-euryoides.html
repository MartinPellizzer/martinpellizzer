Ilex euryoides

Ilex euryoides, commonly known as the Florida holly, is a deciduous evergreen shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that remain green throughout the year. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red berries in the fall. Ilex euryoides thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it a hardy plant suitable for various landscapes.

Its ornamental value and ability to attract wildlife make it a popular choice for gardens and natural habitats.
