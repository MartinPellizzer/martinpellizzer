Ipomoea cathartica var. angustiloba

Ipomoea cathartica var. angustiloba, commonly known as the narrow-leaf morning glory, is a climbing vine native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, narrow leaves and produces trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pale blue to purple in color. The plant is characterized by its rapid growth habit and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types. Its seeds are encased in a smooth, dark brown capsule that splits open when mature.

This species is often found in moist, disturbed habitats such as fields and along stream banks.
