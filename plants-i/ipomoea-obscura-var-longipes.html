Ipomoea obscura var. longipes

Ipomoea obscura var. longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant is characterized by its long, slender stems and is often found in moist, shaded environments. It blooms during the day, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
