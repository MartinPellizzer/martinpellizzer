Inula sicula

Inula sicula, commonly known as the Sicilian Inula, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in coastal areas. Its yellow flowers bloom in the summer, and the plant has feathery, aromatic leaves.

Inula sicula is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
