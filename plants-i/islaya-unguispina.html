Islaya unguispina

Islaya unguispina, commonly known as the red-spined islaya, is a unique flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of southern South America.

It is characterized by its striking red spines that cover its stems and leaves, which serve as a defense mechanism against herbivores. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in late spring, adding a delicate contrast to its rugged appearance. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or mountainous terrain.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, Islaya unguispina is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
