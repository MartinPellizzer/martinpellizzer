Inga sinacae

Inga sinacae, commonly known as the yellow inga, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant produces striking yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting a variety of pollinators. Its leaves are bipinnate, with numerous small leaflets that contribute to its ornamental appeal.

The tree is valued for its ecological role in supporting biodiversity and its potential use in agroforestry systems.
