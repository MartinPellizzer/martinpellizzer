Ipomoea hornei

Ipomoea hornei, commonly known as the sweet potato vine, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, variegated foliage. The plant produces trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its tuberous roots, which are edible, are also a significant agricultural crop in many regions.
