Iris histrioides

Iris histrioides, commonly known as the blackfoot iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking violet to deep blue flowers with a dark purple throat. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or meadow habitats. Its leaves are sword-shaped and dark green, while its rhizomes are fibrous and often covered in a dark, almost black, coating.

Iris histrioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique coloration and ecological benefits.
