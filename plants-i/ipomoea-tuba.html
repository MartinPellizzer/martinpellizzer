Ipomoea tuba

Ipomoea tuba, commonly known as the tuberous morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its rapid growth and climbing habit. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white, often with a distinctive veined pattern. Its tuberous roots are swollen and store nutrients, allowing it to survive in varying environmental conditions.

Ipomoea tuba is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
