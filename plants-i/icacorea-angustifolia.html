Icacorea angustifolia

Icacorea angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf ice plant, is a succulent shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, narrow leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, white or yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its drought tolerance and aesthetic appeal.

Icacorea angustifolia is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil in rocky or sandy habitats.
