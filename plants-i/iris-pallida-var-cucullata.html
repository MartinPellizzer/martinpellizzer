Iris pallida var. cucullata

Iris pallida var. cucullata, commonly known as the white flag iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear showy, pale blue to white flowers with a distinctive three-lobed lower petal. The leaves are sword-shaped, smooth, and arranged in a fan-like pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal. This variety is often found in wet meadows and along streambanks, thriving in moist, well-drained soils.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valued plant in both garden and ecological settings.
