Indocalamus quadratus

Indocalamus quadratus, commonly known as the square bamboo, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its robust, hollow stems that are typically square in cross-section, a unique feature among bamboo species. The plant grows in moist, shaded environments, often in mountainous regions. Its leaves are elongated and lance-shaped, with a smooth, waxy surface.

This bamboo is valued for its strength and is sometimes used in construction and crafts.
