Isoglossa rubescens

Isoglossa rubescens, commonly known as the red-veined isoglossa, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its striking reddish veins on its broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from pale pink to deep red, depending on the variety. Isoglossa rubescens thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded understory environments of tropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
