Impatiens lancisepala

Impatiens lancisepala, commonly known as the narrow-lobed impatiens, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It features upright, branching stems with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that have serrated margins. The flowers are typically showy, with five petals, and are borne in clusters at the leaf axils. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
