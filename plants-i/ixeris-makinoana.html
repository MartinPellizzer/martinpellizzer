Ixeris makinoana

Ixeris makinoana, commonly known as the Japanese dandelion, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant features basal leaves that are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, with yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or partially shaded environments.

Ixeris makinoana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
