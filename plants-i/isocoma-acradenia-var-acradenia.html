Isocoma acradenia var. acradenia

Isocoma acradenia var. acradenia, commonly known as the desert globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and silver-gray foliage, which helps it conserve water in arid environments. The plant produces yellow flowers in late spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Its resilience to drought and heat makes it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.
