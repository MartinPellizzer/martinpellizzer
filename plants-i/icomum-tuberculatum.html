Icomum tuberculatum

Icomum tuberculatum, commonly known as the roughleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of rough, hairy leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that develop into fluffy white seed heads, typical of the dandelion genus. It thrives in dry, open habitats and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Icomum tuberculatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses.
