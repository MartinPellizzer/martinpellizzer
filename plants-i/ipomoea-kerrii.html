Ipomoea kerrii

Ipomoea kerrii, commonly known as Kerri's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves and has a climbing habit, often using tendrils to support its growth. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Ipomoea kerrii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
