Ipomoea cissoides f. subintegrifolia

Ipomoea cissoides f. subintegrifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaf morning glory, is a variant of the morning glory family (Convolvulaceae).

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically smooth-edged, distinguishing it from other forms of Ipomoea cissoides. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are usually pale blue to purple in color, blooming in the early morning. It is a climbing vine that thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is cultivated for its vibrant floral display.
