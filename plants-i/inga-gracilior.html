Inga gracilior

Inga gracilior, commonly known as the slender inga, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pendulous flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in the understory of tropical forests.

Inga gracilior is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological systems.
