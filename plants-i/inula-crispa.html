Inula crispa

Inula crispa, commonly known as the rough fleabane, is a flowering plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and hairy, gray-green leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Inula crispa is often used in herbal medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
