Iris × barthiiformis f. violacea

Iris × barthiiformis f. violacea, commonly known as the violet iris, is a hybrid ornamental plant belonging to the Iridaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue to purple flowers, which typically bloom in early summer. The plant features tall, slender stems with sword-shaped, grass-like leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and its ability to attract pollinators.
