Ilex macfadyenii var. moana

Ilex macfadyenii var. moana, commonly known as the Moana holly, is a shrub or small tree native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage, which is typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, followed by red berries that are an important food source for native birds. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
