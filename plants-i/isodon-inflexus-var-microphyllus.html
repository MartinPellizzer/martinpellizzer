Isodon inflexus var. microphyllus

Isodon inflexus var. microphyllus, commonly known as the small-leaved fragrant verbena, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, erect stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are small, narrow, and covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and borders.

Its aromatic foliage and showy flowers make it a valued ornamental plant in horticulture.
