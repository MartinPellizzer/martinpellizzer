Impatiens cristata

Impatiens cristata, commonly known as the crested impatiens, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, rounded, and often hairy leaves that give it a unique appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, which bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden areas.

Impatiens cristata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation in both garden and container settings.
