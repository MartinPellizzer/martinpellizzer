Inga steinbachii

Inga steinbachii, commonly known as the red inga, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil. The plant features compound leaves with numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink. Its fruit is a long, slender pod that splits open to release seeds, and the plant is often found in humid, shaded forest understories.

Inga steinbachii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and potential ecological benefits in reforestation efforts.
