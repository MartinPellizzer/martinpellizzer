Inga brevituba

Inga brevituba, commonly known as the short-stemmed inga, is a tropical tree species native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests.

Its growth habit and ecological role make it an important component of forest ecosystems.
