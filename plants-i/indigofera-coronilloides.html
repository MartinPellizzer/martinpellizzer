Indigofera coronilloides

Indigofera coronilloides, commonly known as the Crownvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pea-like flowers that range from pink to purple. The plant is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria, making it valuable for soil enrichment.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping and erosion control due to its hardy nature and attractive appearance.
