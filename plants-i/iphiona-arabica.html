Iphiona arabica

Iphiona arabica, commonly known as the Arabian coffee plant, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Arabian Peninsula.

It produces small, red berries that contain the seeds used for coffee production. The plant thrives in warm, arid climates with well-drained soil. It requires a specific combination of temperature and humidity to grow successfully.

Due to its unique habitat, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
