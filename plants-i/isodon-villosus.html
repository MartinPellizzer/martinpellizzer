Isodon villosus

Isodon villosus, commonly known as the hairy isodon, is a perennial herb native to parts of East Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance.

The small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple and bloom in the summer months.
