Isoetes × marensis

Isoetes × marensis, commonly known as the hybrid water fern, is a species of aquatic plant belonging to the genus Isoetes, which is part of the order Isoetales.

It is characterized by its spore-bearing structures called sporangia, which are typically clustered in a structure known as a strobilus. The plant is native to freshwater environments in North America, particularly in the eastern United States. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in still or slow-moving water.

Isoetes × marensis plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and contributing to nutrient cycling.
