Indigofera monieriana

Indigofera monieriana, commonly known as the blue bean plant, is a perennial shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces pods containing edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adaptable to various climatic conditions.

Botanically, it belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is valued for its ornamental and nutritional properties.
