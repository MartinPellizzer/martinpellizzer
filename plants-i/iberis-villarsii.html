Iberis villarsii

Iberis villarsii, commonly known as the Alpine candytuft, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbages, and is characterized by its small, white, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine arrangements.

Iberis villarsii is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
