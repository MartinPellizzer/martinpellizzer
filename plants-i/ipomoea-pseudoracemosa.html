Ipomoea pseudoracemosa

Ipomoea pseudoracemosa, commonly known as the Mexican morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central America and the southern United States.

It is a herbaceous vine that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and thrives in warm, humid climates. It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and climbing habit.

The flowers typically bloom in the morning and close by midday, hence its common name.
