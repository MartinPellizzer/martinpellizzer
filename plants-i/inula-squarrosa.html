Inula squarrosa

Inula squarrosa, commonly known as the squarrose inula, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a hairy, branched stem and produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Inula squarrosa is often found in grasslands and rocky habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
