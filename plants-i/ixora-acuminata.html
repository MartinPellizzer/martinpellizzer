Ixora acuminata

Ixora acuminata, commonly known as the flame of the forest, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its bright orange to red flowers that bloom in dense clusters, creating a striking visual display. The plant has simple, opposite, and elliptical leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Ixora acuminata is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant floral display and adaptability to various growing conditions.
