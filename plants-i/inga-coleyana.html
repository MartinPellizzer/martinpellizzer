Inga coleyana

Inga coleyana, commonly known as the yellow inga, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are often clustered in racemes, followed by flat, bean-like pods. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets.

Inga coleyana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting biodiversity.
