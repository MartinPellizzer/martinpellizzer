Iris kirkwoodiae subsp. calcarea

Iris kirkwoodiae subsp.

calcarea, commonly known as the limestone iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers with a distinctive white or pale yellow center.

The plant thrives in well-drained, calcareous soils, often found in rocky or limestone-rich environments. Its leaves are grass-like, narrow, and typically grow in dense clumps.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable soil conditions.
