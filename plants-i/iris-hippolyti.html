Iris hippolyti

Iris hippolyti, commonly known as the Persian iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It features sword-shaped, grass-like leaves and produces striking, tall spikes of violet or blue flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with showy blooms that appear in late spring to early summer. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread and form dense colonies in suitable habitats.

The flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects, contributing to its ecological significance in its native environment.
