Inga glomerata

Inga glomerata, commonly known as the "silk tree" or "sweet acacia," is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, compound leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces elongated, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical forests.

Inga glomerata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
