Ilex paraguariensis var. usitata

Ilex paraguariensis var. usitata, commonly known as the Amazonian holly or yerba mate, is a shrub or small tree native to the subtropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and produces small, red berries containing seeds. The plant is well adapted to humid, shaded environments and thrives in tropical rainforests. It is closely related to the species used for making traditional yerba mate tea.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the biodiversity in its native habitat.
