Impatiens crenata

Impatiens crenata, commonly known as the mossy impatiens, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features small, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and a distinctive moss-like appearance. The plant produces clusters of delicate, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Impatiens crenata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to add color to shady garden spaces.
