Ipomoea chondrosepala

Ipomoea chondrosepala, commonly known as the velvetleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant is an annual vine that thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils. Its seeds are enclosed in rough, indehiscent capsules, and the plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread aggressively.

Ipomoea chondrosepala is also noted for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
