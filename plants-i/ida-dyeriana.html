Ida dyeriana

Ida dyeriana, commonly known as the Dyers' lily, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or meadow habitats. It produces a tall, erect stem with narrow, grass-like leaves.

Ida dyeriana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant blooms.
