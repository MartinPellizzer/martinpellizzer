Indosasa longiligula var. amara

Indosasa longiligula var. amara, commonly known as the amara variant of the longiligula indosasa, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered inflorescences. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The flowers are usually pale yellow to greenish-white, with five petals and a prominent central stamen.

This variant is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forest undergrowth or along stream banks.
