Ipomoea cornucopia

Ipomoea cornucopia, commonly known as the horn of plenty vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves that are often a vibrant green. The plant produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of white, yellow, and orange, which bloom in clusters. Ipomoea cornucopia is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks.

Its botanical name reflects its association with abundance and prosperity in various cultural contexts.
