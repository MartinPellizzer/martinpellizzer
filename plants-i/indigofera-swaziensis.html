Indigofera swaziensis

Indigofera swaziensis, commonly known as Swazi indigo, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment. It typically grows in grasslands and along riverbanks, thriving in well-drained soils.

The plant's ornamental value and ecological importance make it a notable species in its native habitat.
