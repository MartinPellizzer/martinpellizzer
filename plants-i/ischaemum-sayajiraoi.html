Ischaemum sayajiraoi

Ischaemum sayajiraoi, commonly known as the Sayajirao grass, is a perennial herb native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as riverbanks and wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

Ischaemum sayajiraoi is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wet environments.
