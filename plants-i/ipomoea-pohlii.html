Ipomoea pohlii

Ipomoea pohlii, commonly known as the Hawaiian morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a climbing vine that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of blue, violet, and white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and thrives in warm, tropical climates with plenty of sunlight. It is often found growing along coastal areas and in disturbed soils.

Ipomoea pohlii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also studied for its potential ecological and medicinal properties.
