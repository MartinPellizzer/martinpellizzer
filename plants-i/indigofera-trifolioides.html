Indigofera trifolioides

Indigofera trifolioides, commonly known as the three-leaved indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets arranged in a cluster. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, often in dense clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Indigofera trifolioides is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and blooming habits.
