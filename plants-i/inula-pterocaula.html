Inula pterocaula

Inula pterocaula, commonly known as the hairy elecampane, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a tall, hairy stem that can grow up to 2 meters in height, with broad, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped cluster, typical of the genus Inula.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
