Isolepis rupestris

Isolepis rupestris, commonly known as the rock sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to rocky and coastal areas.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and other grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in cliff faces or sandy shores. Its narrow, grass-like leaves are usually green, and it produces small, clustered flower heads.

Isolepis rupestris is adapted to harsh environments and plays a role in stabilizing coastal soils.
