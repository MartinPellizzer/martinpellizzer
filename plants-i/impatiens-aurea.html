Impatiens aurea

Impatiens aurea, commonly known as golden touch, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a striking visual appeal to gardens. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 feet, with lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Impatiens aurea is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its role in supporting local pollinators.
