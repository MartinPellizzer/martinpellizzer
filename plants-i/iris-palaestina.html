Iris palaestina

Iris palaestina, commonly known as the Palestine iris, is a flowering plant native to the Levant region.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers with a white or pale blue center. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a basal rosette.

Iris palaestina is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in ancient cultures.
