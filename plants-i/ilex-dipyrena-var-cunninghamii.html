Ilex dipyrena var. cunninghamii

Ilex dipyrena var. cunninghamii, commonly known as the Cunningham's holly, is a variant of the holly species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage, with glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, red berries that are typically consumed by birds, aiding in seed dispersal. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature in garden settings.
