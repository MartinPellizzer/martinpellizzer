Ixia xiphidium

Ixia xiphidium, commonly known as the golden crocus, is a striking bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range from yellow to orange in color, typically blooming in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Ixia xiphidium is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its vibrant, long-lasting blooms.
