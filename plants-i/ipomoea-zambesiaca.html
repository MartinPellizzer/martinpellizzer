Ipomoea zambesiaca

Ipomoea zambesiaca, commonly known as the Zambesi morning glory, is a climbing vine native to southern Africa.

It produces large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet, typically opening in the early morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is characterized by its rapid growth habit. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found near water sources.

The flowers have a distinctive scent that attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
