Impatiens diffusa

Impatiens diffusa, commonly known as the spreading touch-me-not, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that typically range in color from pink to red, with five petals arranged in a funnel shape. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy and have a slightly waxy texture. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Impatiens diffusa is also known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
