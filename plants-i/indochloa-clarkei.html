Indochloa clarkei

Indochloa clarkei, commonly known as the Cambodian grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Indochloa clarkei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
