Ipomoea cairica var. cairica

Ipomoea cairica var. cairica, commonly known as the sweet potato vine, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant foliage, often displaying shades of green, red, and purple. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, which bloom in summer. Its tuberous roots are edible and cultivated for their nutty flavor and nutritional value.

The species is well-suited for ornamental gardening and can thrive in a variety of soil types with adequate moisture.
