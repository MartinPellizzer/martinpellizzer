Indigofera costata subsp. macra

Indigofera costata subsp.

macra, commonly known as the mountain indigo, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America. It is characterized by its compound leaves with a distinctive serrated margin and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to violet.

The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in humid, temperate climates.

Botanically, it is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it an important plant in ecological restoration projects.
