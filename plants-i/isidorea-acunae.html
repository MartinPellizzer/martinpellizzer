Isidorea acunae

Isidorea acunae, commonly known as the Andean flame vine, is a climbing shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange to red flowers, which bloom in clusters and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Isidorea acunae is valued for its ornamental beauty and has traditional uses in local medicine.
