Indigofera jirahulia

Indigofera jirahulia, commonly known as the mountain indigo, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain seeds, which are often dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested or rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
