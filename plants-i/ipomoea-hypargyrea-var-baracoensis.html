Ipomoea hypargyrea var. baracoensis

Ipomoea hypargyrea var. baracoensis, commonly known as the Baracoa morning glory, is a climbing vine native to the Caribbean, particularly Cuba.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are typically a deep green color. The flowers are striking, with a white to pale blue corolla that turns pink as they age. It blooms throughout the growing season, producing showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that attract pollinators like hummingbirds and bees.

This plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
