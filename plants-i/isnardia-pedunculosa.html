Isnardia pedunculosa

Isnardia pedunculosa, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its clusters of bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a rough texture.
