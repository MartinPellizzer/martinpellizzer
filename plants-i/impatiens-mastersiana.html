Impatiens mastersiana

Impatiens mastersiana, commonly known as the mountain impatiens, is a flowering plant native to the highlands of South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
