Iris × macrantha

Iris × macrantha, commonly known as the giant iris, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, robust stems that can reach heights of up to 6 feet. The plant produces large, showy flowers with striking blue or violet petals and a prominent, three-lobed white or yellowish beak. Its leaves are sword-shaped and arranged in a fan-like pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Iris × macrantha thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
