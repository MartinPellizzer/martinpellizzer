Ixophorus palmeri

Ixophorus palmeri, commonly known as Palmer's grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit, with slender, wiry stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, such as sandy soils and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration that helps reduce water loss.

Ixophorus palmeri plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
