Iris persica f. sieheana

Iris persica f. sieheana, commonly known as the Persian iris, is a flowering plant belonging to the Iridaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue petals and a distinctive, spurred petal at the back of the flower. The plant typically grows in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil. Its leaves are sword-shaped and can reach lengths of up to 30 centimeters.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
