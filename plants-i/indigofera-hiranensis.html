Indigofera hiranensis

Indigofera hiranensis, commonly known as the Ethiopian indigo plant, is a flowering plant native to Ethiopia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable in agricultural settings. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Its historical use in dye production and traditional medicine highlights its cultural and ecological significance.
