Isachne globosa var. effusa

Isachne globosa var. effusa, commonly known as the broadleaf isachne, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive, rounded leaf blades that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences with pale green to brownish flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and habitat restoration projects.
