Ixora joskei

Ixora joskei, commonly known as the Joske's flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its bright red or orange inflorescences, which are clustered in dense, showy panicles. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in spikes, and it has simple, opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Ixora joskei thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

It is relatively drought-tolerant once established, making it suitable for garden landscapes in tropical and subtropical regions.
