Isatis leuconeura

Isatis leuconeura, commonly known as the yellow flag iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It features tall, reed-like stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, often found in wetland habitats. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and have a distinctive waxy coating.

Isatis leuconeura is notable for its adaptability to moist environments and its role in ecological restoration projects.
