Ipomoea serrana

Ipomoea serrana, commonly known as the serrated morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped, violet to blue flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant is a climbing vine with hairy stems and serrated leaf margins. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in moist woodlands or along stream banks.

Ipomoea serrana is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
