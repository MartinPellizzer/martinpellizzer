Indigofera houer

Indigofera houer, commonly known as the African indigo plant, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its compound leaves and striking blue-violet flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Indigofera houer is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant floral display.
