Impatiens sylvicola

Impatiens sylvicola, commonly known as the forest impatiens, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and broad, ovate leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, which bloom throughout the wet season. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
