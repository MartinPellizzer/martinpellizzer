Impatiens sulcata var. glandulifera

Impatiens sulcata var. glandulifera, commonly known as the glandular impatiens, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which are covered in small glands that secrete a sticky substance. The plant produces clusters of showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses worldwide.
