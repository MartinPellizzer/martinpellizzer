Impatiens peltata

Impatiens peltata, commonly known as the broadleaf impatiens, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, waxy leaves and clusters of showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found growing in the understorey of forests. It belongs to the family Balsaminaceae and is prized for its ornamental value in gardens.

Impatiens peltata is also known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through rhizomes.
