Isachne hirsuta var. angusta

Isachne hirsuta var. angusta, commonly known as narrowleaf isachne, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia and Australia.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are narrow and slightly hairy, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its inflorescence consists of small, spike-like clusters of florets, which are usually greenish or reddish in color.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in landscaping or restoration projects.
