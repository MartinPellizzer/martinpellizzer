Isolepis barbata var. roxburghiana

Isolepis barbata var. roxburghiana, commonly known as the hairy spike-rush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant is characterized by its tall, cylindrical inflorescence composed of dense, spike-like clusters of small flowers. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and often covered with fine, hairy structures.
