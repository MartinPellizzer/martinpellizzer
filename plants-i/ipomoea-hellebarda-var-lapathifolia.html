Ipomoea hellebarda var. lapathifolia

Ipomoea hellebarda var. lapathifolia, commonly known as the red devil's bit, is a striking flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its vivid red or orange flowers that resemble small, fleshy berries, which are actually the fruit of the plant. The leaves are heart-shaped and often have a velvety texture, while the stems are hairy and can climb or trail. This species is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its bold, eye-catching blooms.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for both gardeners and botanists.
