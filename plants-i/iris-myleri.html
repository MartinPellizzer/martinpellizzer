Iris myleri

Iris myleri, commonly known as the Sierra iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. The plant features upright, grass-like leaves and produces showy blue to purple flowers with three upright petals. It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit.

Iris myleri is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
