Impatiens bicolor subsp. pseudobicolor

Impatiens bicolor subsp.

pseudobicolor, commonly known as the red-and-white impatiens, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its striking bi-colored flowers, which typically feature a white calyx and a red corolla.

The plant produces showy, five-petaled blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its foliage is generally dark green with a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
