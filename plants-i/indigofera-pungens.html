Indigofera pungens

Indigofera pungens, commonly known as the prickly senna, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Its name "pungens" refers to the sharp, prickly texture of its leaves.
