Ipomoea owariensis

Ipomoea owariensis, commonly known as the Owari morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in the morning and close in the afternoon. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is often found growing in moist, shaded environments. It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family, which includes other morning glory species.

Ipomoea owariensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
