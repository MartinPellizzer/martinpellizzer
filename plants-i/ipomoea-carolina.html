Ipomoea carolina

Ipomoea carolina, commonly known as the morning glory, is a flowering vine native to the eastern United States.

It produces trumpet-shaped flowers that open in the morning and close by midday, hence its common name. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is often found growing along stream banks and in moist woodlands. Its flowers are typically blue or purple, though white and pink varieties also exist.

Ipomoea carolina is a member of the Convolvulaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and climbing habit.
