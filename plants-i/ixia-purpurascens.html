Ixia purpurascens

Ixia purpurascens, commonly known as the red-hot poker, is a striking perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features tall, spiky inflorescences that bear bright orange to red tubular flowers, which are particularly showy in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a rosette at the base. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its unique appearance and long-lasting blooms make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and ecological landscapes.
