Iris hellenica

Iris hellenica, commonly known as the Greek iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers with a distinctive white or yellow center. The plant has sword-shaped leaves and produces showy, three-parted flowers that bloom in late spring. Iris hellenica typically grows in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
