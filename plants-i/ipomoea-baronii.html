Ipomoea baronii

Ipomoea baronii, commonly known as the Baron's morning glory, is a flowering vine native to Central and South America.

It produces large, showy flowers that are typically blue to violet in color, with a distinctive spurred shape at the base of the petals. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and can grow up to 10 meters in length. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The flowers open in the morning and close by midday, hence its common name.
