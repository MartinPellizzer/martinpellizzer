Ipomoea obtusifolia

Ipomoea obtusifolia, commonly known as morning glory, is a herbaceous vine native to the Americas.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white. The plant is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to climb using its tendrils. Its seeds are encased in a fibrous, spiny capsule that splits open when mature.

Ipomoea obtusifolia is often cultivated for ornamental purposes but can become invasive in some regions.
