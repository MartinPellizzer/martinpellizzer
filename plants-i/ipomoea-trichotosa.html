Ipomoea trichotosa

Ipomoea trichotosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet, typically blooming in the morning. It is a vigorous climber, often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and agricultural fields.

The plant is also known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
