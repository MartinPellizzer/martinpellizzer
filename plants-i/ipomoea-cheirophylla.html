Ipomoea cheirophylla

Ipomoea cheirophylla, commonly known as the pink morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking pink to purple flowers that open in the morning and close in the late afternoon. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves and has a climbing habit, often found growing along fences or shrubs. Its flowers are typically 5- to 7-petaled and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Ipomoea cheirophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
