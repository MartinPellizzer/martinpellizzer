Ischaemum importunum

Ischaemum importunum, commonly known as the common dogstail, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces dense, cylindrical seed heads that are often mistaken for dog tails, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, along roadsides, and in wetlands.

Ischaemum importunum is an important plant in ecological studies due to its role in soil stabilization and as a food source for various insect species.
