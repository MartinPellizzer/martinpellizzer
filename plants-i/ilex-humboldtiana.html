Ilex humboldtiana

Ilex humboldtiana, commonly known as the Huastec holly, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to southern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, glossy dark green leaves and produces small, red berries that are an important food source for wildlife. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in horticulture for its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.

The species is also culturally significant to indigenous communities in the region.
