Ipomoea paraensis

Ipomoea paraensis, commonly known as the Amazonian morning glory, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, dark seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of rainforests.

Ipomoea paraensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
