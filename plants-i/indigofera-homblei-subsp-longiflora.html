Indigofera homblei subsp. longiflora

Indigofera homblei subsp.

longiflora, commonly known as the long-flowered indigofera, is a member of the legume family Fabaceae. It is characterized by its erect or semi-woody shrub growth habit and trifoliate leaves.

The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. Its pods are linear and contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is native to parts of Southeast Asia and is often found in moist, shaded environments.
