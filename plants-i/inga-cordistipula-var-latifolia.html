Inga cordistipula var. latifolia

Inga cordistipula var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved inga, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are often pubescent on the underside. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers, which are typically followed by elongated, pendulous pods. Its growth habit is typically erect or spreading, with a dense canopy that provides shade.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in agroforestry systems.
