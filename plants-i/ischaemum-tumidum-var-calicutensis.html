Ischaemum tumidum var. calicutensis

Ischaemum tumidum var. calicutensis, commonly known as the Calicut grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, characterized by its grass-like structure with narrow, elongated leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are clustered in dense, cylindrical arrangements.

The botanical features of this variety include its rough, hairy leaves and small, wind-pollinated flowers.
