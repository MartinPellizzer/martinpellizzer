Inula bocconei

Inula bocconei, commonly known as the Italian Inula, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and daisy-like appearance. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is found in areas such as southern Europe and parts of North Africa. Its yellow-orange flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves and is often used in ornamental gardening due to its striking appearance.
