Ipomoea ebracteata

Ipomoea ebracteata, commonly known as the naked stem morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, almost hairless stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Ipomoea ebracteata is a climbing vine that can reach lengths of several meters, using its tendrils to support itself.
