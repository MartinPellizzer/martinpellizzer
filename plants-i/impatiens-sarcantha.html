Impatiens sarcantha

Impatiens sarcantha, commonly known as the flame flower, is a herbaceous perennial native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from red to orange, often with a contrasting throat. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. Its flowering period occurs during the warmer months, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soil.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
