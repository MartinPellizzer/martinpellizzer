Iberis procumbens var. congesta

Iberis procumbens var. congesta, commonly known as the dense-flowered candytuft, is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has small, rounded, glossy green leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hair. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value comes from its compact growth habit and long-lasting blooms.
