Ipomopsis aggregata subsp. collina

Ipomopsis aggregata subsp.

collina, commonly known as the Sierra penstemon, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to violet.

The plant typically grows in rocky or open habitats, often in mountainous regions. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This subspecies is part of the Gentian family (Gentianaceae) and is known for its ecological importance to pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
