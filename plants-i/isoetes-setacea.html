Isoetes setacea

Isoetes setacea, commonly known as the narrow-leaf water moss, is a species of aquatic plant belonging to the order Isoetales.

It is characterized by its dense, spike-like arrangement of spore-bearing leaves, which are typically narrow and strap-shaped. The plant thrives in freshwater environments, such as ponds and slow-moving streams, where it anchors itself to submerged substrates. Its spores are released into the water and dispersed by currents, facilitating reproduction.

Isoetes setacea plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by contributing to water filtration and providing habitat for microorganisms.
