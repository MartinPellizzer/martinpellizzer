Ipomoea divergens

Ipomoea divergens, commonly known as the scarlet morning glory, is a flowering vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange to red flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces long, twisting stems that climb or trail along the ground. Its flowers are typically about 5 to 7 centimeters in diameter and are known for their showy appearance.

Ipomoea divergens is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and as a decorative plant.
