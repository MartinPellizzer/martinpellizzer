Ipomoea oenotheroides

Ipomoea oenotheroides, commonly known as the yellow morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual with trailing stems that often grow along the ground or climb supports. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange, opening in the morning and closing in the afternoon. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and have a velvety texture.

The plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in disturbed soils along roadsides and in open woodlands.
