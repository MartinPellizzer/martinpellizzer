Ipomoea sagittifer

Ipomoea sagittifer, commonly known as the arrowleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped, violet to blue flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant is a vigorous climber, often found scrambling up fences, trellises, or trees. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Ipomoea sagittifer is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
