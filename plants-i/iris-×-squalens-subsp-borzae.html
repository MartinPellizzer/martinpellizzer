Iris × squalens subsp. borzae

Iris × squalens subsp.

borzae, commonly known as the Borzai iris, is a hybrid species within the Iridaceae family. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers with a contrasting white or pale yellow center.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in temperate climates. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces tall, slender stems.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and botanical collections.
