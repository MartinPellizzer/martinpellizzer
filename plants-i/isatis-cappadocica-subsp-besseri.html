Isatis cappadocica subsp. besseri

Isatis cappadocica subsp.

besseri, commonly known as the Cappadocian woad, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and mustard.

The plant is characterized by its basal rosette of dark green, hairy leaves and produces small, yellow flowers in late spring. It is often found in rocky or dry soils and is adapted to thrive in arid conditions.

The species is valued for its historical use in dye production and as a potential medicinal plant.
