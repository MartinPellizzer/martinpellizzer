Ipomoea daltonii

Ipomoea daltonii, commonly known as Dalton's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet to blue flowers that bloom in the early morning, hence its common name. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces tendrils that help it climb and support its growth. It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family, which includes many other climbing vines.

Ipomoea daltonii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blossoms.
