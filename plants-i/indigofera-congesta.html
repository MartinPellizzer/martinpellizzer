Indigofera congesta

Indigofera congesta, commonly known as African indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to Africa and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable in agricultural settings. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often used in traditional medicine.

Its seeds are also used as a source of dye, historically significant in textile production.
