Ixia pusilla

Ixia pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf fire lily, is a small, flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllis family (Amaryllidaceae) and is characterized by its compact size and striking, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 10-20 centimeters, with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers, which are usually bright yellow or orange, bloom in late summer to early autumn.

Ixia pusilla is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well-suited to rock gardens and containers.
