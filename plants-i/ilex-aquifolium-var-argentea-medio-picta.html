Ilex aquifolium var. argentea-medio-picta

Ilex aquifolium var. argentea-medio-picta, commonly known as the silver-veined Holly, is a variant of the common holly with distinctive botanical features.

Its leaves are characterized by a striking silver-white midrib and margins, contrasting with dark green foliage. The plant is evergreen, with small, spiny leaves that are typically arranged in pairs. It produces small, red berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
