Indigofera acanthocarpa

Indigofera acanthocarpa, commonly known as the African indigo or wild indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pea-like flowers. The plant produces small, hard pods that contain several seeds, and it is adapted to thrive in dry, well-drained soils.

It is often found in savannas and woodland areas, where it plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various wildlife species.
