Ilex oligodonta

Ilex oligodonta, commonly known as the Florida holly, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It features elliptical, dark green leaves that turn red in the fall, and it produces small, red berries that are an important food source for wildlife. The plant is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardiness.

Ilex oligodonta is also valued for its role in ecological systems, providing habitat and sustenance for numerous species.
