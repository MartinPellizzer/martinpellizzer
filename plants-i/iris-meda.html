Iris meda

Iris meda, commonly known as the Meda Iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread and form dense colonies in suitable habitats.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding vibrant color to gardens and natural landscapes.
