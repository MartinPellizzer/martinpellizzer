Ipomoea ennealoba

Ipomoea ennealoba, commonly known as the nine-lobed morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet, often opening in the early morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a waxy surface and produces capsule fruits containing numerous small, dark seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Ipomoea ennealoba is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
