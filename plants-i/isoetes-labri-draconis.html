Isoetes labri-draconis

Isoetes labri-draconis, commonly known as the dragon's lip water clover, is a species of aquatic plant belonging to the genus Isoetes.

It is characterized by its unique, ribbon-like spore leaves that are often used for identification. The plant typically grows in freshwater environments such as lakes and ponds, where it thrives in shallow, still waters. Its reproductive structures, known as sporangia, are clustered at the base of the plant and produce spores for propagation.

Isoetes labri-draconis is notable for its ornamental value and is often found in botanical gardens and conservation sites.
