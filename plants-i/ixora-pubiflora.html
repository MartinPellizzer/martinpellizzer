Ixora pubiflora

Ixora pubiflora, commonly known as the hairy flame tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, showy clusters of bright red or orange flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has opposite, simple, and ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it its common name. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Ixora pubiflora is also valued for its ability to tolerate poor soil conditions and partial shade.
