Indigofera fulcrata

Indigofera fulcrata, commonly known as the slender indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing habit and trifoliate leaves. Its small, pea-like flowers are typically blue or violet, and it produces elongated pods containing small, dark seeds.

Indigofera fulcrata is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of dye.
