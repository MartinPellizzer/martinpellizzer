Ipomoea malvaeoides

Ipomoea malvaeoides, commonly known as the blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves and is often found growing along roadsides and in disturbed soils. It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family, which includes other morning glories.

Ipomoea malvaeoides is typically a climbing vine that can reach lengths of several meters.
