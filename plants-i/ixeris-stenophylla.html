Ixeris stenophylla

Ixeris stenophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a hairy texture, and it produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring.

Ixeris stenophylla is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
