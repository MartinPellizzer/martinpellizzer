Ichnanthus mollis

Ichnanthus mollis, commonly known as the woolly flowered bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a distinct yellow or green throat. The plant has hairy, glandular leaves that give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Ichnanthus mollis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
