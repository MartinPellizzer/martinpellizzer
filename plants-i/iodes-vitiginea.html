Iodes vitiginea

Iodes vitiginea, commonly known as the slender sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It typically forms dense mats of succulent, fleshy leaves that help it conserve water in dry environments. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in spring. Its root system is fibrous and shallow, allowing it to thrive in sandy or gravelly soils.

Iodes vitiginea plays a role in stabilizing dunes and preventing soil erosion in its native habitat.
