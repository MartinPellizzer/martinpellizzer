Idiothamnus orgyaloides

Idiothamnus orgyaloides, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a shrub that produces small, bright yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the cooler months. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and is known for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in sandy or well-drained soils. It is often used in ornamental gardening due to its long-lasting, showy blooms.

The species is also valued for its historical use in traditional medicine by Indigenous Australian communities.
