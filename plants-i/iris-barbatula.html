Iris barbatula

Iris barbatula, commonly known as the bearded iris, is a perennial plant native to the temperate regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It features striking, sword-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green color. The plant produces showy flowers with three upright petals and three drooping sepals, often in shades of blue, purple, and white. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

The flowers bloom in early summer, adding vibrant color to gardens and natural habitats.
