Inula × suaveolens

Inula × suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet coltsfoot, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its yellowish-green foliage and showy, dandelion-like yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is native to Europe and has been introduced to other regions, including parts of North America. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, stream banks, and woodland edges.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
