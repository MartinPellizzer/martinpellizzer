Ipomoea paniculata var. pauciflora

Ipomoea paniculata var. pauciflora, commonly known as the few-flowered morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pale blue to purple in color. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can be found in gardens and natural habitats.

Its flowers open in the early morning and close by midday, a characteristic shared by many morning glory species.
