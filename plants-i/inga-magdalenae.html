Inga magdalenae

Inga magdalenae, commonly known as the Magdalena inga, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Colombia and Venezuela. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pea-like flowers that bloom in clusters. It produces pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in local cuisines.

Inga magdalenae plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
