Ipomoea cordillerae

Ipomoea cordillerae, commonly known as the Cordillera morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is a herbaceous vine that produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of violet and blue. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is characterized by its rapid growth habit. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Ipomoea cordillerae is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
