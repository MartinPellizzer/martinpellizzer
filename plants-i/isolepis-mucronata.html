Isolepis mucronata

Isolepis mucronata, commonly known as the spike rush, is a perennial grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical inflorescence composed of small, spike-like clusters of flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and pond edges, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a rough texture, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Isolepis mucronata plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
