Isachne pulchella

Isachne pulchella, commonly known as the pretty grass or silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia and the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Isachne pulchella thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in marshy areas or along the edges of ponds.

Its ornamental value has led to its cultivation in some regions for its delicate appearance and ability to form dense, attractive ground cover.
