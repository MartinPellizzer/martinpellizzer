Isopogon formosus subsp. dasylepis

Isopogon formosus subsp.

dasylepis, commonly known as the pink-flowered isopogon, is a shrub native to Western Australia. It is characterized by its dense, aromatic foliage and showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant typically grows in heathland and coastal environments, preferring well-drained soils. Its subspecies status highlights its distinct floral and morphological traits compared to other isopogon species.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
