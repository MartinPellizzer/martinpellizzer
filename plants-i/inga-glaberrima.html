Inga glaberrima

Inga glaberrima, commonly known as the silky tamarind, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant is distinguished by its compound leaves, which are typically bipinnate and covered in a fine, silky pubescence. Its flowers are clustered in racemes and range in color from yellow to orange, attracting various pollinators.

The tree produces pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines and herbal remedies.
