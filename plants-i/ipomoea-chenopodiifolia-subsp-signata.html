Ipomoea chenopodiifolia subsp. signata

Ipomoea chenopodiifolia subsp.

signata, commonly known as the silverleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America. It is characterized by its heart-shaped, silver-velvet leaves and trumpet-shaped blue or purple flowers.

The plant is a climbing vine that can reach lengths of several meters, often using its tendrils to attach to supports. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its striking foliage and blooms.
