Ipomoea lacteola

Ipomoea lacteola, commonly known as the white morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, white to pale pink flowers that open in the morning. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, smooth seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Ipomoea lacteola is a perennial vine that can grow up to several meters in length.
