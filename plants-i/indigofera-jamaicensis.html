Indigofera jamaicensis

Indigofera jamaicensis, commonly known as Jamaican indigo, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, tropical environments and is often found in coastal areas. It produces pods that contain small, dark seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
