Isolepis schweinfurthiana

Isolepis schweinfurthiana, commonly known as the Schweinfurth's tussock sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical clusters of grass-like leaves that form a compact tussock. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in wetland environments, often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical structure is typical of the Cyperaceae family, with fibrous roots and a rhizomatous growth habit.
