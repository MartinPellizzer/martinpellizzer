Ipomoea flava

Ipomoea flava, commonly known as the yellow morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in the morning and close in the afternoon. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is part of the Convolvulaceae family. It thrives in warm, sunny climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Ipomoea flava is also known for its climbing habit, using tendrils to support its growth.
