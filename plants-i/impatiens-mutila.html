Impatiens mutila

Impatiens mutila, commonly known as the "spotted impatiens," is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its attractive, showy flowers that typically come in shades of red, pink, and white, often with distinctive spots or markings. The plant has heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are oppositely arranged along the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden areas.

Impatiens mutila is also known for its ability to produce numerous seed pods, which burst open when mature, dispersing seeds forcefully.
