Indigofera hispida

Indigofera hispida, commonly known as the hairy indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Indigofera hispida is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dyes.
