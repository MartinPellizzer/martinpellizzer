Indigofera tenuifolia

Indigofera tenuifolia, commonly known as slenderleaf indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands. The plant features trifoliate leaves with slender, lance-shaped leaflets and produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to purple. Its root system is known for containing compounds used in traditional medicine.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in diverse soil conditions.
