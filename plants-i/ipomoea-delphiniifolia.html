Ipomoea delphiniifolia

Ipomoea delphiniifolia, commonly known as the blue morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in the morning and close by midday. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are often a deep green color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Ipomoea delphiniifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
