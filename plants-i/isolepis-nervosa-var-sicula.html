Isolepis nervosa var. sicula

Isolepis nervosa var. sicula, commonly known as Sicilian flat sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, ponds, and stream edges. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often slightly hairy, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its flowers are wind-pollinated, and the plant is adapted to tolerate fluctuating water levels.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic and semi-aquatic organisms.
