Iris korolkowii var. leichtliniana

Iris korolkowii var. leichtliniana, commonly known as the Siberian iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features tall, slender stems that bear showy, pale blue to violet flowers with three upright petals and three drooping sepals. The plant has lance-shaped, grass-like leaves that are typically dark green and arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and its ability to naturalize in suitable climates.
