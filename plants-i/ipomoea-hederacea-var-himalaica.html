Ipomoea hederacea var. himalaica

Ipomoea hederacea var. himalaica, commonly known as the Himalayan morning glory, is a climbing vine native to the Himalayan regions of Asia.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a velvety texture and produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The plant is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to climb various structures, making it a common sight in moist, shaded environments. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mountainous areas with high humidity.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses in some cultures.
