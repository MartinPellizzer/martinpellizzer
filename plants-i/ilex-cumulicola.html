Ilex cumulicola

Ilex cumulicola, commonly known as the cloud forest holly, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the humid montane regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its dense, glossy dark green leaves and produces small, red berries that are an important food source for various wildlife. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests at elevations between 1,000 and 2,500 meters. Its botanical features include simple, alternate leaves and small, clustered flowers that bloom in the spring.

Ilex cumulicola plays a vital ecological role in maintaining the biodiversity of its native habitat.
