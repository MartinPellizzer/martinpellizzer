Ixeris yakuinsularis

Ixeris yakuinsularis, commonly known as the Yaku Island groundsel, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
