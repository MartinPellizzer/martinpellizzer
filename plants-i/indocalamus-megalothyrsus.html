Indocalamus megalothyrsus

Indocalamus megalothyrsus, commonly known as the giant bamboo, is a tall, slender grass-like plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its hollow, jointed stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant can grow up to 15 meters in height, making it one of the tallest bamboo species. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its rapid growth and structural strength make it valuable for both ecological and commercial purposes.
