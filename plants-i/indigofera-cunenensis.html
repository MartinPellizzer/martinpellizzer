Indigofera cunenensis

Indigofera cunenensis, commonly known as the African indigo or small-leaved indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces seed pods that contain small, kidney-shaped seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and potential medicinal research.
