Indigofera fuscosetosa

Indigofera fuscosetosa, commonly known as the dark-hairy indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain seeds covered in a dark, hairy coating, which aids in seed dispersal. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
