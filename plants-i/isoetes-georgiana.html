Isoetes georgiana

Isoetes georgiana, commonly known as the Georgia quillwort, is a species of aquatic plant belonging to the order Isoetales.

It is characterized by its spiky, grass-like appearance and is typically found in freshwater environments such as ponds and lakes. The plant has a compact, rosette-like structure with small, scale-like leaves that are often submerged. Isoetes georgiana reproduces through spores, and it plays a role in maintaining aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for microorganisms.

This species is also of interest to botanists due to its ancient lineage and unique adaptations to aquatic life.
