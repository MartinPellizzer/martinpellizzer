Inga carachensis

Inga carachensis, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or sweet acacia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, edible pods that contain seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Inga carachensis plays an important role in ecological systems by fixing nitrogen in the soil, supporting biodiversity in its habitat.
