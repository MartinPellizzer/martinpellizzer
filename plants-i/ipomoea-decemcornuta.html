Ipomoea decemcornuta

Ipomoea decemcornuta, commonly known as the Crown of Thorns, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiny stems and clusters of small, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant is a member of the Convolvulaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in arid conditions. Its leaves are typically small, ovate, and covered in fine hairs.
