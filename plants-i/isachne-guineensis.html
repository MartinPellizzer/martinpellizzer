Isachne guineensis

Isachne guineensis, commonly known as Guinea grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its erect, clump-forming growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, grasslands, and along riverbanks.

Isachne guineensis is valued for its use in forage and has ecological importance in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
