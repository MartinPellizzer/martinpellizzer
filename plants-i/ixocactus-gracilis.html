Ixocactus gracilis

Ixocactus gracilis, commonly known as the slender Christmas cactus, is a species native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its branching stems and vibrant, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in a shrub-like form, with segmented stems that can be green or red in color. Its flowers, which bloom in winter, come in shades of red, pink, and white, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Ixocactus gracilis prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to thrive.
