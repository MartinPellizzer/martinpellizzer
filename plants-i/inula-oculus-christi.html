Inula oculus-christi

Inula oculus-christi, commonly known as the Christ's eye, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a tall, slender stem with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its most distinctive feature is the central yellow disk surrounded by white ray florets, resembling an eye.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.
