Ipomoea shumardiana

Ipomoea shumardiana, commonly known as the Shumard's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of violet, pink, and white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and twining vines that can grow up to 10 feet in length. Its flowers open in the early morning and close in the late afternoon, hence its common name.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
