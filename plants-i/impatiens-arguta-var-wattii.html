Impatiens arguta var. wattii

Impatiens arguta var. wattii, commonly known as the Watt's trailing jewelweed, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, showy flowers that typically range in color from orange to red, with a distinctive spurred structure. The plant produces small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

This variety is closely related to other impatiens species and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
