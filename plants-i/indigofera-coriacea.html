Indigofera coriacea

Indigofera coriacea, commonly known as rough indigo, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves with small, pointed leaflets. The plant produces small, pink to violet flowers that are clustered in racemes. It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

The species is also valued for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
