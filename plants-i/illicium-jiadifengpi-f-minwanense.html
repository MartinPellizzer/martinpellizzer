Illicium jiadifengpi f. minwanense

Illicium jiadifengpi f. minwanense, commonly known as the Minwan variant of the Chinese star anise, is a rare and lesser-known botanical variant of the Illiciaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive star-shaped fruit, which resembles the more widely recognized star anise (Illicium verum). The plant is native to southern China, specifically the Fujian province, where it thrives in humid, subtropical climates. Its leaves are typically dark green and glossy, with a fragrant aroma similar to its more famous relative.

The fruit, which contains aromatic seeds, is used in traditional Chinese medicine and is valued for its potential medicinal properties.
