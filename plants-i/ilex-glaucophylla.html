Ilex glaucophylla

Ilex glaucophylla, commonly known as the silver-leaf holly, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that often have a silvery or whitish underside, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, followed by red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for native wildlife. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or heathland environments.

Ilex glaucophylla is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in horticulture for its attractive foliage and hardy nature.
