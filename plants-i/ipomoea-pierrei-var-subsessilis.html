Ipomoea pierrei var. subsessilis

Ipomoea pierrei var. subsessilis, commonly known as the Mexican morning glory, is a flowering vine native to Central America.

It produces vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple, typically blooming in the early morning. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves and thrives in warm, humid climates. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its rapid growth and climbing habit make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking to add color and texture to vertical spaces.
