Ixeris parishii

Ixeris parishii, commonly known as the Chinese groundsel, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or open habitats.

Ixeris parishii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its hardy nature.
