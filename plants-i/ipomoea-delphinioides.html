Ipomoea delphinioides

Ipomoea delphinioides, commonly known as the blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that open in the morning and close in the afternoon. The plant is a vigorous climber with heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped blooms. It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is often grown for its ornamental value.

Ipomoea delphinioides is also known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
