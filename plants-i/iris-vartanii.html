Iris vartanii

Iris vartanii, commonly known as the Armenian iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Armenia.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stem and striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers consist of three upright petals and three downturned sepals.

Iris vartanii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also culturally significant in Armenian traditions.
