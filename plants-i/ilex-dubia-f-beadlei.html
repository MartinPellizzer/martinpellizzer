Ilex dubia f. beadlei

Ilex dubia f. beadlei, commonly known as the Beadley holly, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are typically elliptical in shape and have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a suitable choice for garden landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
