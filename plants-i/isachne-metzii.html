Isachne metzii

Isachne metzii, commonly known as the slender false brome, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically hairy along the edges. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing minute, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in high-altitude grasslands and forest margins.

Isachne metzii plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates in its native ecosystems.
