Impatiens albopustulosa

Impatiens albopustulosa, commonly known as the white-spotted touch-me-not, is a herbaceous perennial native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale yellow with distinctive white spots. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are usually hairy and have a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its unique appearance and ornamental value make it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
