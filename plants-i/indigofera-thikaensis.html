Indigofera thikaensis

Indigofera thikaensis, commonly known as African indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and striking blue-violet flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland and savanna habitats.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a notable species in its native range.
