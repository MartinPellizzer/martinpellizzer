Iresine elongata

Iresine elongata, commonly known as the red flame plant, is a striking member of the Amaranthaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America and is characterized by its vivid, colorful foliage that ranges from red to purple. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, usually green or yellow, and are clustered in dense inflorescences.

Iresine elongata is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of growing conditions.
