Iresine eriophylla

Iresine eriophylla, commonly known as the red leaf iron plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety red or purple leaves that contrast with its small, inconspicuous white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, and thrives in well-drained soil with ample sunlight. Its foliage is often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant color and texture.

Iresine eriophylla is also known for its ability to tolerate drought conditions once established.
