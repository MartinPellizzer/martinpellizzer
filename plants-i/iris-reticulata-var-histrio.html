Iris reticulata var. histrio

Iris reticulata var. histrio, commonly known as the crimson netted iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces showy, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive reticulated pattern on the petals. The plant typically blooms in early spring, showcasing its vibrant colors against the backdrop of snowmelt. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact size and striking floral display.
