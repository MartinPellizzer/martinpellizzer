Impatiens longiloba

Impatiens longiloba, commonly known as the long-stemmed impatiens, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, which bloom throughout the wet season. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Impatiens longiloba is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens in suitable climates.
