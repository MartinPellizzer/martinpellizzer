Iris haynei

Iris haynei, commonly known as the Hayne's iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or meadow habitats. The plant features upright, grass-like leaves and produces striking blue to purple flowers with a distinctive three-parted structure. It blooms in early to mid-summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Iris haynei is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in native plant gardens.
