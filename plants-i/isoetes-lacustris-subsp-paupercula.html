Isoetes lacustris subsp. paupercula

Isoetes lacustris subsp.

paupercula, commonly known as the lake watermace, is a species of aquatic plant belonging to the genus Isoetes. It is characterized by its spiky, spore-bearing structures called sporangia, which are typically found on specialized leaves called sporophylls.

This subspecies is adapted to freshwater environments, often inhabiting shallow lakes and ponds with muddy substrates. Its morphology includes a short, creeping rhizome and narrow, ribbon-like leaves.

The plant plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by contributing to nutrient cycling and providing habitat for microorganisms.
