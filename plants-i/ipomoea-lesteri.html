Ipomoea lesteri

Ipomoea lesteri, commonly known as the Lester's morning glory, is a flowering vine native to the southeastern United States.

It produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple, depending on the variety. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is typically found in moist, well-drained soils along stream banks and forest edges.

Botanically, it is characterized by its rapid growth and distinctive floral structure, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
