Indigofera hololeuca

Indigofera hololeuca, commonly known as the white indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive white or pale blue flowers. The plant is typically shrub-like, with compound leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
