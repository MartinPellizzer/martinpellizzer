Isoetes alcalophila

Isoetes alcalophila, commonly known as the alkaline water fern, is a species of aquatic plant belonging to the genus Isoetes.

It is characterized by its spore-bearing leaves that are typically submerged in water, with some leaves extending above the water surface. The plant thrives in alkaline environments, such as saline or mineral-rich lakes and ponds. Its reproductive structures are specialized, with sporangia located at the tips of the leaves.

Isoetes alcalophila plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by contributing to nutrient cycling and providing habitat for microorganisms.
