Iochroma piuranum

Iochroma piuranum, commonly known as the Andean bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to violet, often with a contrasting throat. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are ovate to elliptical, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Iochroma piuranum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
