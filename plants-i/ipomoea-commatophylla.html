Ipomoea commatophylla

Ipomoea commatophylla, commonly known as the silky morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in the morning. Its vines can reach lengths of several meters, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
