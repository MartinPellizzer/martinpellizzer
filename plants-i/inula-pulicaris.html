Inula pulicaris

Inula pulicaris, commonly known as the meadow thistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and large, showy yellow flower heads that bloom in late summer. The plant has finely divided, gray-green leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. It thrives in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands.

Inula pulicaris is often used in herbal medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory properties.
