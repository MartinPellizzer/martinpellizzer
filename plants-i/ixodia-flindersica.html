Ixodia flindersica

It belongs to the family Parmeliaceae and is characterized by its crustose growth form, forming a thin, tightly adherent layer on rock surfaces. The lichen's thallus is typically pale yellow to orange in color, with a distinct, granular texture. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found on limestone or sandstone substrates.

Due to its restricted distribution and ecological specificity, Ixodia flindersica is considered a rare and potentially vulnerable species.
