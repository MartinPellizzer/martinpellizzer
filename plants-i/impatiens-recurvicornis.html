Impatiens recurvicornis

Impatiens recurvicornis, commonly known as the wavy-haired impatiens, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptical in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. This species thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking shade-tolerant plants.
