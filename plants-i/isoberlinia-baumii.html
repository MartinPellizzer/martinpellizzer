Isoberlinia baumii

Isoberlinia baumii, commonly known as the African velvetwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, dark brown, flaky bark. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by elongated, woody pods containing flat, bean-like seeds. It is often found in savanna and woodland habitats, and its wood is valued for its durability and aesthetic qualities.

The plant also plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various wildlife species.
