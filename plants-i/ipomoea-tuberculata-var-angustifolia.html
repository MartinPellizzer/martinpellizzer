Ipomoea tuberculata var. angustifolia

Ipomoea tuberculata var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf morning glory, is a flowering vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, angular stems and heart-shaped leaves with a slightly wavy margin. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple, typically blooming in the early morning. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
