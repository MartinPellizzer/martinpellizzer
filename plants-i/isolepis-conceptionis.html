Isolepis conceptionis

Isolepis conceptionis, commonly known as the Conceptionis flatsedge, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and flowering structures.

It typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, showcasing its adaptability to moist environments. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of flowers that are often greenish or brownish in color. It is native to parts of South America, particularly Argentina and Uruguay.

Due to its ecological role in wetland ecosystems, it is sometimes used in restoration projects.
