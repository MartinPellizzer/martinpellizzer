Iranecio taraxacifolius var. discoideus

Iranecio taraxacifolius var. discoideus, commonly known as the diskleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, spoon-shaped leaves and a single, bright yellow flower head. The plant produces a distinctive, spherical seed head that is easily dispersed by the wind. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

The species is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
