Impatiens mackeyana subsp. mackeyana

Impatiens mackeyana subsp.

mackeyana, commonly known as Mackey's Impatiens, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central America. It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, pink, and white.

The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in moist forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
