Isodon coetsa

Isodon coetsa, commonly known as the Cape geranium, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, two-lipped flowers in shades of purple and blue. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Isodon coetsa is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
