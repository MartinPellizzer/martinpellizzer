Icica acuchini

Icica acuchini, commonly known as the golden bell vine, is a climbing plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that resemble small bells, which bloom in clusters along its tendrils. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, hairy texture. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Icica acuchini is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative element in tropical landscapes.
