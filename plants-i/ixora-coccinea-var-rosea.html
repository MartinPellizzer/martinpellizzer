Ixora coccinea var. rosea

Ixora coccinea var. rosea, commonly known as the roseate flame bush, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and is renowned for its vibrant, clustered inflorescences. The flowers are typically bright red or pink, with a striking, lantern-like appearance that blooms throughout the warm seasons. The plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and as a hedge due to its hardy nature and showy blooms.
