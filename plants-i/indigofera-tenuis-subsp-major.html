Indigofera tenuis subsp. major

Indigofera tenuis subsp.

major, commonly known as the slender indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions. It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves with small, heart-shaped leaflets.

The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a ground cover.
