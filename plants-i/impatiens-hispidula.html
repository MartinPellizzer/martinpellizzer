Impatiens hispidula

Impatiens hispidula, commonly known as the hairy touch-me-not, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Impatiens hispidula is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
