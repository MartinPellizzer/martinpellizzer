Ipomoea whyteana var. integrata

Ipomoea whyteana var. integrata, commonly known as the white morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, showy white or pale blue flowers that open in the early morning and close by midday. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces long, coiling vines that can climb or trail along the ground. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The flowers emit a mild fragrance and are often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens.
