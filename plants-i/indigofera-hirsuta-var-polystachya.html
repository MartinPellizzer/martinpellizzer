Indigofera hirsuta var. polystachya

Indigofera hirsuta var. polystachya, commonly known as hairy indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems covered in fine, hairy pubescence. The leaves are alternate, pinnately compound, and often have a slightly hairy texture. It produces clusters of small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale blue.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical significance in traditional medicine.
