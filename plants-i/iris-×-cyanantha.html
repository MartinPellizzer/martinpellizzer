Iris × cyanantha

Iris × cyanantha, commonly known as the blue flag iris, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Iris sibirica and Iris germanica.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers with a yellow beard, which are typically 3 to 5 inches in diameter. The plant is a rhizomatous perennial that thrives in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands or along the edges of ponds. Its foliage consists of broad, sword-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy.

Iris × cyanantha is valued in gardens for its ornamental beauty and is also used in floral arrangements.
