Ipomoea caerulescens

Ipomoea caerulescens, commonly known as blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that open in the morning and close at dusk. The plant has heart-shaped, palmately lobed leaves and is often found growing in wet or marshy habitats. It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family, which includes other morning glories.

Ipomoea caerulescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
