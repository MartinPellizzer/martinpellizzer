Isachne nipponensis

Isachne nipponensis, commonly known as Japanese isachne, is a grass species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and slender, cylindrical stems. The leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-green hue, while the inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a panicle. It typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or grassland habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native landscapes.
