Isoetes bolanderi var. parryi

Isoetes bolanderi var. parryi, commonly known as the Parry's watermilfoil, is a species of aquatic plant belonging to the genus Isoetes.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiky, and often branched fronds that emerge from a central axis. The plant typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds and lakes, where it can anchor itself to the substrate. Its reproductive structures, known as sporangia, are located at the tips of the fronds and produce spores for propagation.

This variety is native to western North America and is often found in regions with cool, temperate climates.
