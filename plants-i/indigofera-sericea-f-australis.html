Indigofera sericea f. australis

Indigofera sericea f. australis, commonly known as the silky indigo, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pale blue to purple, typically blooming in spring. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in dry climates.

This variety is often used in ornamental gardening and for soil stabilization due to its hardy nature.
