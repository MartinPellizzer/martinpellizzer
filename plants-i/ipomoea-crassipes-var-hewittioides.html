Ipomoea crassipes var. hewittioides

Ipomoea crassipes var. hewittioides, commonly known as the water hyacinth, is a floating aquatic plant native to South America.

It has dark green, waxy leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern on the water surface. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. It is known for its rapid growth rate, which can lead to dense mats on water surfaces.

Due to its invasive nature, it is considered a problematic species in many waterways worldwide.
