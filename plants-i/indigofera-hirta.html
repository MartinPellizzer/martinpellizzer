Indigofera hirta

Indigofera hirta, commonly known as velvet bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and erect, branching stems covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by elongated, bean-like pods containing edible seeds. It is often cultivated for its protein-rich seeds, which are used in animal feed and human consumption.

Additionally, the plant has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
