Iberis linifolia subsp. contejeanii

Iberis linifolia subsp.

contejeanii, commonly known as the Corsican carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant has dark green, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine layer of white hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
