Inula stolzii

Inula stolzii, commonly known as the Stolz's elecampane, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a tall, sturdy stem with lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and grayish-green in appearance. Its flowers are yellow and clustered in a single head, surrounded by pale green bracts.

Inula stolzii is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
