Inga fagifolia f. pedicellaris

Inga fagifolia f. pedicellaris, commonly known as the pedicellate inga, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant is notable for its distinctive pedicellate (stem-like) inflorescences, which give it its specific epithet. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

This species is often found in disturbed habitats and is valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and soil enrichment.
