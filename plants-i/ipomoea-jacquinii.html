Ipomoea jacquinii

Ipomoea jacquinii, commonly known as the yellow morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that open in the morning and close in the afternoon. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces tendrils that help it climb and support its growth. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded areas.

Ipomoea jacquinii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
