Iriartea lamarckiana

Iriartea lamarckiana, commonly known as the dwarf cycad, is a member of the Cycadaceae family and is native to the Caribbean.

It is a slow-growing evergreen plant characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and a crown of stiff, feather-like leaves. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain both male and female reproductive organs. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological importance, it is a popular specimen in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
