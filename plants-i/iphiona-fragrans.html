Iphiona fragrans

Iphiona fragrans, commonly known as the sweet-smelling orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that come in shades of white, pink, and purple. Its fragrance is one of its most notable features, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Iphiona fragrans typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often near streams or in dense undergrowth.
