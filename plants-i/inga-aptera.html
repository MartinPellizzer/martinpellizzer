Inga aptera

Inga aptera, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its pinnately compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, round pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisine. Its roots have a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, enhancing soil fertility.

Inga aptera is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in tropical ecosystems.
