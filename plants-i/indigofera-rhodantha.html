Indigofera rhodantha

Indigofera rhodantha, commonly known as the red-flowered indigo, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of striking red or pink flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a landscape plant.
