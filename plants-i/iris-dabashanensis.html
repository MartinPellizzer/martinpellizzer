Iris dabashanensis

Iris dabashanensis, commonly known as the Dabashan iris, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy blue-violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in mountainous regions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, three-lobed seed pods.

Iris dabashanensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
