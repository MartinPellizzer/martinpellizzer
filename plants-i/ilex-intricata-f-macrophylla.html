Ilex intricata f. macrophylla

Ilex intricata f. macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaved holly, is a variant of the holly genus characterized by its distinctive foliage.

This plant is native to specific regions in the southeastern United States, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils. Its leaves are typically larger and more elongated compared to the standard Ilex intricata, giving it a unique appearance. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for local wildlife.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to provide year-round interest in gardens.
