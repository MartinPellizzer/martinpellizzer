Inga clypearia

Inga clypearia, commonly known as the coffee tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, clustered flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable in agroforestry systems. It produces small, edible fruits that are often consumed by wildlife and birds.

The tree is also cultivated for its ornamental value and its role in supporting biodiversity in forest ecosystems.
