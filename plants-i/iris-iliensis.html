Iris iliensis

Iris iliensis, commonly known as the Eastern Armenian iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.

Iris iliensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
