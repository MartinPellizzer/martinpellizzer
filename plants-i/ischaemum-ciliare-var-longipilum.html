Ischaemum ciliare var. longipilum

Ischaemum ciliare var. longipilum, commonly known as the long-hair false oat, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves with soft, silky hairs along the edges. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing minute, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed sites.

Its fine texture and delicate appearance make it a distinctive component of its native habitats.
