Ibarraea paschalis

Ibarraea paschalis, commonly known as the Paschal ibarraea, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the family Solanaceae, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are often clustered in umbels, followed by orange to red berries. It is typically found in humid, tropical environments and is known for its ornamental value due to its vibrant fruit.

However, it is important to note that some species in the Solanaceae family contain toxic compounds, so caution is advised when handling this plant.
