Iris bakeriana

Iris bakeriana, commonly known as the Baker's iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers with a distinctive yellow center. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and features slender, grass-like leaves. It blooms in late spring to early summer, producing showy flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Iris bakeriana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and hardy nature.
