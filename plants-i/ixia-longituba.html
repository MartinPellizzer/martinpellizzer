Ixia longituba

Ixia longituba, commonly known as the long-tube ixia, is a striking member of the Amaryllidaceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of yellow to orange, which bloom in late winter to early spring. Its unique floral structure is adapted for pollination by specific bird species, such as the malachite sunbird.

Ixia longituba is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soils with adequate sunlight.
