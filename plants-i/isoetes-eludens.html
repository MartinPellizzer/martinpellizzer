Isoetes eludens

Isoetes eludens, commonly known as the elusive water parsley, is a species of aquatic fern that thrives in freshwater environments.

It is characterized by its finely divided, grass-like foliage and its ability to grow in shallow waters. The plant produces small, spore-bearing structures called sporangia, which are typically located on the underside of its fronds. Isoetes eludens is often found in marshes, ponds, and slow-moving streams, where it plays a role in maintaining aquatic ecosystems.

Its unique morphology and habitat preferences make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
