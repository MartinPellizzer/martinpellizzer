Irlbachia poeppigii

Irlbachia poeppigii, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture and grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in horticulture for its vibrant floral display.
