Indigofera meyeriana

Indigofera meyeriana, commonly known as Meyer's indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and striking purple or blue flowers. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant blooms and ability to thrive in well-drained soil. It also has historical significance in traditional medicine and dye production.

Its growth habit includes both shrub-like and herbaceous forms depending on environmental conditions.
