Ilex aquifolium var. heterophylla

Ilex aquifolium var. heterophylla, commonly known as the variable holly, is a variant of the common holly characterized by its morphological differences in leaf size and shape.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Europe and western Asia. The leaves are typically spiny and have a glossy, dark green upper surface with a lighter underside. It produces small, red berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans.

This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.
