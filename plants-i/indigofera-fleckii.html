Indigofera fleckii

Indigofera fleckii, commonly known as the African indigo, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment. It produces small, dark blue to purple seed pods that contain several seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is cultivated in some parts of the world for horticultural purposes.
