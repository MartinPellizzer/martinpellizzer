Ipomoea melanotricha

Ipomoea melanotricha, commonly known as the black-haired morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers, which typically open in the morning and close by midday. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture, giving it its common name. It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and climbing habit.

Ipomoea melanotricha is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
