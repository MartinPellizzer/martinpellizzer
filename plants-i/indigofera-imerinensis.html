Indigofera imerinensis

Indigofera imerinensis, commonly known as the African indigo or Malawi indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, particularly in countries like Malawi, Tanzania, and Zambia. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, pea-like flowers that range in color from blue to purple. It produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.

Indigofera imerinensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
