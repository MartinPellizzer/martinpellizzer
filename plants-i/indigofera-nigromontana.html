Indigofera nigromontana

Indigofera nigromontana, commonly known as black mountain indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. Its seeds are encased in pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

Indigofera nigromontana is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches the soil.
