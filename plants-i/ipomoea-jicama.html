Ipomoea jicama

Ipomoea jicama, commonly known as the Japanese morning glory, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its trumpet-shaped, vibrant blue or purple flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant produces a tuberous root that is edible and often used in Asian cuisine. Its leaves are heart-shaped and can grow up to 15 cm in length.

Ipomoea jicama is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
