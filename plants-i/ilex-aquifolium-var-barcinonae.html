Ilex aquifolium var. barcinonae

Ilex aquifolium var. barcinonae, commonly known as the Barcino holly, is a variant of the common holly native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with spiny margins, typical of the Ilex genus. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodlands and shrublands.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
