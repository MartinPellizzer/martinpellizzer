Isoetes × paratunica

Isoetes × paratunica, commonly known as the hybrid water parsley, is a species of aquatic plant belonging to the genus Isoetes, which includes spike mosses.

It is characterized by its unique spore-bearing structures called sporangia, which are typically clustered at the tips of its leaf-like structures. The plant thrives in freshwater environments, such as ponds and slow-moving streams, where it can anchor itself to the substrate. Its morphology reflects adaptations to aquatic life, with specialized leaves that aid in gas exchange and nutrient absorption.

Due to its hybrid origin, it exhibits a mix of traits from its parent species, Isoetes tunica and Isoetes paradoxa.
