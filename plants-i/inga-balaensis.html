Inga balaensis

Inga balaensis, commonly known as the yellow inga, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with long, slender leaflets and produces showy, yellow or orange flowers that attract pollinators. Its fruit is a pod that contains several seeds, and the tree is often used in reforestation efforts due to its rapid growth and ecological benefits.

Inga balaensis is also valued for its timber and as a source of food for wildlife.
