Ipomoea wallii

Ipomoea wallii, commonly known as the blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that open in the morning and close in the afternoon. The plant is a climbing vine with heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped blooms. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Ipomoea wallii is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
