Inga parvifolia

Inga parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved inga, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant has pinnate leaves with small, ovate leaflets and produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes. Its fruits are pod-like, containing several seeds, and it is often found in moist, shaded habitats.

Inga parvifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological systems.
