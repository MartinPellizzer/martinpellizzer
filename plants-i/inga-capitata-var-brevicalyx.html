Inga capitata var. brevicalyx

Inga capitata var. brevicalyx, commonly known as the small-leaf inga, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically pinnate with numerous small, ovate leaflets. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers, followed by elongated, woody pods containing edible seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
