Ipomoea involucrata var. burttii

Ipomoea involucrata var. burttii, commonly known as Burtt's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its trumpet-shaped, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink. Its rapid growth and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
