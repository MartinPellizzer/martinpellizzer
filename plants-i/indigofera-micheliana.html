Indigofera micheliana

Indigofera micheliana, commonly known as the Mexican indigo or Michel's indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to Mexico and parts of Central America, where it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in clusters. It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to several meters in height.

Indigofera micheliana is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in ecological restoration projects.
