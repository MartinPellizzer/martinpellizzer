Ischaemum polystachyum var. chordatum

Ischaemum polystachyum var. chordatum, commonly known as the hairy beakgrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers, typically blooming in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical structure makes it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
