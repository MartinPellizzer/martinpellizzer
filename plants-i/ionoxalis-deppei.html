Ionoxalis deppei

Ionoxalis deppei, commonly known as the silverleaf oxalis, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, delicate flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Ionoxalis deppei thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and ability to thrive in a variety of garden conditions.
