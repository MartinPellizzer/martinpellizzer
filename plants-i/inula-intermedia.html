Inula intermedia

Inula intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate elecampane, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features tall, sturdy stems with grayish-green leaves that are lance-shaped and hairy. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped cluster, typical of the genus Inula.

Inula intermedia is often found in meadows and along roadsides, and it has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
