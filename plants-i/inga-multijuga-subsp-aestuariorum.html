Inga multijuga subsp. aestuariorum

Inga multijuga subsp.

aestuariorum, commonly known as the coastal inga, is a shrub or small tree native to the coastal regions of South America. It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often elliptical in shape and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that are typically followed by pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in saline and coastal environments, making it an important species for stabilizing dune ecosystems.

Its adaptability to coastal conditions makes it a valuable plant for ecological restoration projects.
