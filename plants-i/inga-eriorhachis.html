Inga eriorhachis

Inga eriorhachis, commonly known as the silky inga, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, woody pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.

The silky inga also plays a role in nitrogen fixation, enriching the soil in its natural habitat.
