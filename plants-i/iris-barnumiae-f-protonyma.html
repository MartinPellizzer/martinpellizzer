Iris barnumiae f. protonyma

Iris barnumiae f. protonyma, commonly known as the Barnum's iris, is a rare and striking botanical specimen native to the mountainous regions of southeastern China.

This plant belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its elegant, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive yellow beard on the lower petals. The foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a dense basal rosette. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a valuable species for botanical study and conservation efforts.
