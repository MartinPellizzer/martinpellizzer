Indigofera vaniotii

Indigofera vaniotii, commonly known as the Madagascar indigo, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces pod-like fruits containing several seeds.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its striking floral display.
