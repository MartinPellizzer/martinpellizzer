Iris trippneriana

Iris trippneriana, commonly known as the Trippner's iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in mountainous regions. Its leaves are grass-like, narrow, and arranged in a fan shape.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
