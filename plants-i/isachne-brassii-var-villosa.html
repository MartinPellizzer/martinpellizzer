Isachne brassii var. villosa

Isachne brassii var. villosa, commonly known as the hairy isachne, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and dense, compact growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive, velvety texture.

The inflorescence consists of clustered spikelets, which are often reddish in color and contribute to its ornamental value.
