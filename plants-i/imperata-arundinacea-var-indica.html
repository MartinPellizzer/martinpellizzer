Imperata arundinacea var. indica

Imperata arundinacea var. indica, commonly known as cogongrass, is a perennial grass native to Asia but now invasive in many parts of the world.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical rhizomes that produce dense clusters of sword-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers in panicles, which are often hidden within the leaf sheaths. Its rapid growth and extensive rhizome system make it highly competitive and difficult to control.

Cogongrass is often considered a noxious weed due to its ability to outcompete native vegetation and alter ecosystem dynamics.
