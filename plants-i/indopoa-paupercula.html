Indopoa paupercula

Indopoa paupercula, commonly known as the slender grass, is a low-growing perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically forms dense, tufted clumps with narrow, blade-like leaves that are often tinged with silver or gray. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are characteristic of the grass family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and rocky areas.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in landscaping for erosion control and ornamental purposes.
