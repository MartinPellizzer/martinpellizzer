Ixeris kimurana

Ixeris kimurana, commonly known as the Korean sandmat, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant produces small, yellowish to orange-colored composite flowers that bloom in the summer. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

Ixeris kimurana is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil in dry environments.
