Imperata parodii

Imperata parodii, commonly known as the red grass or parodii grass, is a striking ornamental plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, arching inflorescences that bear bright red bracts, which are often mistaken for flowers. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping and rock gardens. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue that adds contrast to the vibrant red bracts.

Imperata parodii is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in arid conditions.
