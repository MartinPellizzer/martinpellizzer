Irlbachia pratensis

Irlbachia pratensis, commonly known as the meadow iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It typically grows in moist meadows and along stream banks, preferring well-drained soils. The plant features slender, upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are blue to violet in color, arranged in loose clusters, and bloom in early summer.

Irlbachia pratensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
