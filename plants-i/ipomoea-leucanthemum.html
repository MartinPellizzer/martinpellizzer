Ipomoea leucanthemum

Ipomoea leucanthemum, commonly known as the white morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a member of the Convolvulaceae family, characterized by its climbing habit and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces white or pale yellow blooms that open in the early morning and close by midday. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs.

Ipomoea leucanthemum thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun for optimal growth.
