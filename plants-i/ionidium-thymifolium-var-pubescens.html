Ionidium thymifolium var. pubescens

Ionidium thymifolium var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy thyme-leaved ionidium, is a perennial plant characterized by its aromatic foliage and small, clustered flowers.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. The leaves are narrow, linear, and covered with fine hairs, giving the plant its "hairy" appearance. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, blooming in late spring to early summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging garden conditions.
