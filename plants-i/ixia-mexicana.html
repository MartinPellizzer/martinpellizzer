Ixia mexicana

Ixia mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican fire lily, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of central Mexico.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing clusters of showy flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Ixia mexicana prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value and unique floral structure make it a notable species in botanical collections and horticultural displays.
