Isolepis cinnamometorum

Isolepis cinnamometorum, commonly known as the cinnamon-leaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically brown or reddish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

Isolepis cinnamometorum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
