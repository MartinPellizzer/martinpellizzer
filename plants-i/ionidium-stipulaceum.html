Ionidium stipulaceum

Ionidium stipulaceum, commonly known as the stipulate ionidium, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Malvaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive stipules, which are leaf-like structures at the base of the petioles. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. It is native to tropical regions of Africa and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
