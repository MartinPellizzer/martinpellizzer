Incarvillea uniflora

Incarvillea uniflora, commonly known as the single-flower incarvillea, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural study and ecological research.
