Ipomoea sessiliflora

Ipomoea sessiliflora, commonly known as the purple morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The plant typically grows as a vine, with heart-shaped leaves and tendrils that aid in climbing. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun.

Ipomoea sessiliflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
