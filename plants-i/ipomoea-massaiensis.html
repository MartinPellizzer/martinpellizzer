Ipomoea massaiensis

Ipomoea massaiensis, commonly known as the Massai morning glory, is a flowering plant native to East Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to purple, often resembling those of other morning glory species. The plant typically grows as a climbing vine, with heart-shaped leaves and tendrils that help it ascend supports. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sunlight.

Ipomoea massaiensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
