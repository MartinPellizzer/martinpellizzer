Ipomoea inamoena var. trisecta

Ipomoea inamoena var. trisecta, commonly known as the three-lobed morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The leaves are distinctive, featuring a three-lobed shape that sets it apart from other varieties of Ipomoea. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
