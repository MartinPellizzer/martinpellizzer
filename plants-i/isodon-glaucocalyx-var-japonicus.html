Isodon glaucocalyx var. japonicus

Isodon glaucocalyx var. japonicus, commonly known as Japanese blue violet, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features lance-shaped leaves with a bluish-green hue and produces clusters of small, pale blue to violet flowers. The plant is characterized by its aromatic foliage, which contains essential oils and flavonoids. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its use in traditional herbal medicine.
