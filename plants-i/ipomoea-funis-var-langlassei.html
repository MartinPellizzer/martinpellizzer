Ipomoea funis var. langlassei

Ipomoea funis var. langlassei, commonly known as the long vine morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its trailing or climbing habit, with heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, dark seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive potential, it requires careful management in garden settings.
