Ipomoea mahonii

Ipomoea mahonii, commonly known as the yellow morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial with heart-shaped, palmately lobed leaves and vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Ipomoea mahonii is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
