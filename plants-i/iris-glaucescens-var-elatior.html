Iris glaucescens var. elatior

Iris glaucescens var. elatior, commonly known as the blue flag iris, is a striking perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features tall, slender stems that bear showy, blue to violet flowers with a distinctive white or yellowish center. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are often covered in a powdery bloom, giving it a unique appearance. It thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and pond edges, and is well adapted to aquatic or semi-aquatic conditions.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for wetland restoration and ornamental gardening.
