Indigofera schimperi var. crispidula

Indigofera schimperi var. crispidula, commonly known as the crinkled indigo or crinkled velvet tamarind, is a shrub native to Ethiopia and parts of Eritrea.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves with finely serrated margins and small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and traditional uses, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and for medicinal purposes.
