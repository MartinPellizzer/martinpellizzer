Ipomoea ochroleuca

Ipomoea ochroleuca, commonly known as the yellow morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to yellow. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, round seeds. It thrives in moist, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed soils.

Ipomoea ochroleuca is a member of the Convolvulaceae family, which includes other climbing vines and herbs.
