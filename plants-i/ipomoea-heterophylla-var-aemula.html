Ipomoea heterophylla var. aemula

Ipomoea heterophylla var. aemula, commonly known as the purple morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in the early morning, giving it its common name. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves that vary in size depending on the growing conditions. It produces long, twisting vines that can climb or trail along the ground.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
