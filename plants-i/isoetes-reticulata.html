Isoetes reticulata

Isoetes reticulata, commonly known as the basket waternettle, is a species of aquatic plant belonging to the order Isoetales.

It is characterized by its unique morphology, featuring a spore-bearing structure called a sporangium, which is typically located at the tip of a specialized leaf called a seta. The plant thrives in freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes, where it anchors itself to the substrate with its rhizomes. Its leaves are usually ribbon-like and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Isoetes reticulata plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
