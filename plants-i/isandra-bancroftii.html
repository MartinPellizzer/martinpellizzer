Isandra bancroftii

Isandra bancroftii, commonly known as the golden rain tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall, featuring a broad, umbrella-shaped crown. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Isandra bancroftii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.
