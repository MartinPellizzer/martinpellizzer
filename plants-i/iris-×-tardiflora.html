Iris × tardiflora

Iris × tardiflora, commonly known as the late-flowering iris, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Iris germanica and Iris siberica.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, six-petaled flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of sword-like, grassy leaves that are dark green and somewhat hairy.

Iris × tardiflora is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of climates.
