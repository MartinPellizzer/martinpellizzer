Ipomoea pseudomarginata

Ipomoea pseudomarginata, commonly known as the yellow morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a member of the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its trumpet-shaped, yellow to orange flowers that open in the morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It is often found growing along roadsides and in disturbed soils due to its adaptability.

Its rapid growth and showy flowers make it a popular ornamental plant, though it can be invasive in some areas.
