Iris macrosiphon var. purdyi

Iris macrosiphon var. purdyi, commonly known as the Purdy's iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features tall, upright stems that bear showy, six-petaled flowers in shades of blue and purple. The plant has sword-like, grassy leaves that are typically bright green and densely packed. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows and along stream banks.

Due to its unique floral structure and habitat preferences, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
