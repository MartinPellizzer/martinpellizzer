Inga pterocarpa

Inga pterocarpa, commonly known as the yellow inga, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.

Its rapid growth makes it an important species for reforestation and soil stabilization efforts.
