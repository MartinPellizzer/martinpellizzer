Inga pilosiuscula var. panurensis

Inga pilosiuscula var. panurensis, commonly known as the Panurensis Inga, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to South America, particularly found in countries like Peru and Ecuador.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in local cuisines.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
