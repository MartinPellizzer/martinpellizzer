Iris urmiensis

Iris urmiensis, commonly known as the Urmi iris, is a species of flowering plant in the family Iridaceae.

It is native to the Caspian region, particularly Iran and Azerbaijan, and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant features tall, slender stems with narrow, grass-like leaves and produces striking blue to violet flowers with three petals and three sepals. It is often found in wet meadows, marshy areas, and along riverbanks.

The Urmi iris is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
