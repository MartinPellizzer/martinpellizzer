Imperata pedicellata

Imperata pedicellata, commonly known as the red grass or pink grass, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to parts of Asia, including China, Japan, and the Philippines, and is often found in moist, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and striking pink or red inflorescences that bloom in summer. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread aggressively, forming dense mats that can dominate forest floors.

Imperata pedicellata is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
