Indigofera nana

Indigofera nana, commonly known as dwarf indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, Africa, and the Pacific Islands. The plant is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to purple, and its trifoliate leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate.

Indigofera nana is often used in ornamental gardening for its compact growth and attractive floral display.
