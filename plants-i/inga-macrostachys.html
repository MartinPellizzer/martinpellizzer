Inga macrostachys

Inga macrostachys, commonly known as the giant arrowroot or velvet tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, clustered flowers. The plant produces edible tubers that are similar in texture to yams and are used in various culinary applications. It also has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, which helps enrich the soil.

Its rapid growth makes it a valuable species for reforestation and soil stabilization efforts.
