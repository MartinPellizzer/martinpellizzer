Isiphia

Isiphia, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in cool, arid environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and lance-shaped, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Isiphia is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
