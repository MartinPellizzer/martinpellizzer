Impatiens pyrrhotricha var. pyrrhotricha

Impatiens pyrrhotricha var. pyrrhotricha, commonly known as the red-tipped impatiens, is a herbaceous perennial native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features upright stems with opposite, ovate leaves that are typically dark green with a reddish or purplish underside. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in partial to full shade and requires moist, well-drained soil.

This variety is popular in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to add color to shady areas.
