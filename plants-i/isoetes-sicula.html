Isoetes sicula

Isoetes sicula, commonly known as the Sicilian watermoss, is a species of aquatic plant belonging to the genus Isoetes, which is part of the order Isoetales.

It is characterized by its spiky, spore-bearing structures called sporangia, which are typically arranged in a rosette-like pattern along the stem. The plant thrives in freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving streams, where it can anchor itself in muddy or sandy substrates. Its unique morphology and reproductive strategy make it an important component of aquatic ecosystems.

Isoetes sicula is also noted for its historical significance in paleobotany due to its fossil record.
