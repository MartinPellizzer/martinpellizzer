Ipomoea wightii var. kilimandschari

Ipomoea wightii var. kilimandschari, commonly known as the Kilimandschari morning glory, is a climbing vine native to East Africa.

It produces large, showy flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, with a trumpet-shaped corolla. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy and can grow up to 10 centimeters in length. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its beauty.
