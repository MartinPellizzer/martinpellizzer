Ipomoea brasiliensis

Ipomoea brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a climbing vine that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of violet, blue, and white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive blooms and vigorous nature.

The seeds are encased in a distinctive, spiny fruit that splits open when mature.
