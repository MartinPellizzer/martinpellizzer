Ipomoea phyllosepala

Ipomoea phyllosepala, commonly known as the golden shower vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, pendulous yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, resembling a cascade of golden beads. The plant has heart-shaped, glossy leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Ipomoea phyllosepala is also known for its rapid growth habit and ability to climb using its twining stems.
