Indigofera campestris f. mixta

Indigofera campestris f. mixta, commonly known as the mixed indigo plant, is a variant of the indigo plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a leguminous species characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it valuable in agricultural systems. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it both a useful forage crop and a potential ornamental plant.
