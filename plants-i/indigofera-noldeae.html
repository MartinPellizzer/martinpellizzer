Indigofera noldeae

Indigofera noldeae, commonly known as the Nolde's indigo, is a flowering plant species native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, which makes it beneficial for soil fertility.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.
