Itea javanica

Itea javanica, commonly known as the Java flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, which are typically ovate to elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers arranged in panicles, followed by bright red or orange follicles that split open to release seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a landscape plant.
