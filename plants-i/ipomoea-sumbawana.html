Ipomoea sumbawana

Ipomoea sumbawana, commonly known as the Sumbawa morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Indonesia.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its striking violet to blue flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces edible tubers similar to those of other morning glory species. It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
