Ilex micrococca f. luteocarpa

Ilex micrococca f. luteocarpa, commonly known as the yellow-fruited holly, is a shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, leathery leaves that are typically dark green with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers followed by orange to red berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant foliage and fruit.
