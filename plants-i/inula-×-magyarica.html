Inula × magyarica

Inula × magyarica, commonly known as the Hungarian elecampane, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Inula helenium and Inula crithmoides.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its tall, branched stems and large, daisy-like yellow flower heads. The plant produces fluffy, white seeds that are dispersed by the wind, aiding in its spread. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in meadows and along roadsides.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family, sharing features with other composite plants.
