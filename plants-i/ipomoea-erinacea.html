Ipomoea erinacea

Ipomoea erinacea, commonly known as the horsehead morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of blue, violet, and purple. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and a hairy stem that often has a reddish tinge. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Ipomoea erinacea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
