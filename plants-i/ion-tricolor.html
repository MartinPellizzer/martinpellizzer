Ion tricolor

Ion tricolor, commonly known as the tri-color violet, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Violaceae family and is characterized by its strikingly colorful flowers that display three distinct hues. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a velvety texture, while its flowers bloom in early spring.

Ion tricolor is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
