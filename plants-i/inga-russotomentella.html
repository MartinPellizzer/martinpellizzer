Inga russotomentella

Inga russotomentella, commonly known as the silky inga, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several seeds, which are often dispersed by animals.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
