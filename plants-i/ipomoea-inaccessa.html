Ipomoea inaccessa

Ipomoea inaccessa, commonly known as the inaccessible morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces small, round berries that contain seeds, which are dispersed by animals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive potential, it is sometimes considered a weed in certain regions.
