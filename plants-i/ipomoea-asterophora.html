Ipomoea asterophora

Ipomoea asterophora, commonly known as the starry morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its trumpet-shaped, violet-blue flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture and is often found growing along forest edges or in disturbed soils. Its climbing habit is facilitated by tendrils that emerge from the leaf axils.

Ipomoea asterophora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
