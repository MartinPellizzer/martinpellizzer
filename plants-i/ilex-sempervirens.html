Ilex sempervirens

Ilex sempervirens, commonly known as the Holly Oak, is a species of evergreen tree native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and spiny, red berries that provide food for wildlife. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a leathery texture and a serrated margin. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in its native habitats.
