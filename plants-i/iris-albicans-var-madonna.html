Iris albicans var. madonna

Iris albicans var. madonna, commonly known as Madonna lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features tall, upright stems that bear showy, white to pale blue flowers with a distinctive, dark purple center. The plant has lance-shaped, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding a striking visual element to the landscape.
