Ipomoea ampelopsifolia

Ipomoea ampelopsifolia, commonly known as the creeping morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant is a vigorous climber, often found trailing along the ground or climbing supports. Its flowers open in the morning and close by midday, hence its common name.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and ornamental value.
