Inula bubonium var. hispida

Inula bubonium var. hispida, commonly known as rough yellow gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright, branched stems covered in stiff, glandular hairs, giving it a rough texture. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a hairy surface.

This species is often found in dry meadows and rocky soils, showcasing its adaptability to various environmental conditions.
