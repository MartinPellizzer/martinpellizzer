Indigofera lamellata

Indigofera lamellata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands. The plant is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and trifoliate leaves with narrow, elongated leaflets. Its small, pea-like flowers are typically blue or purple, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.

Indigofera lamellata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
