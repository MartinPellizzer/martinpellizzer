Ipomoea discoidesperma

Ipomoea discoidesperma, commonly known as the discoid seed morning glory, is a herbaceous vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It features heart-shaped leaves and produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant is characterized by its unique, discoid seeds that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found climbing structures in its natural habitat.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Convolvulaceae family.
