Ischyranthera laevigata

Ischyranthera laevigata, commonly known as the smooth-leafed ischyranthera, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pale yellow or greenish-white, arranged in loose clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated as a houseplant.
