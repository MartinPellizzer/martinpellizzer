Ischaemum falcatum

Ischaemum falcatum, commonly known as narrowleaf barnyard grass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It typically grows in moist or wet environments such as marshes, stream banks, and ditches. The plant has slender, flat leaves that are often ribbed and have a distinctive narrow, blade-like shape. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, each bearing small, two-lobed flowers.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil in wetland areas and can sometimes become a weed in agricultural settings.
