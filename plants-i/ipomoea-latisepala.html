Ipomoea latisepala

Ipomoea latisepala, commonly known as the broad-sepaled morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in the morning and close in the afternoon. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces distinctive, broad sepals that are often as colorful as the petals. It is a climbing vine that can reach significant lengths, often using its tendrils to ascend supports.

Botanically, it belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is valued for its ornamental beauty in gardens.
