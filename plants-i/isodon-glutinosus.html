Isodon glutinosus

Isodon glutinosus, commonly known as sticky lemongrass, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic plants such as mint and basil. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that emit a strong lemony scent. Its flowers are small and clustered in whorls, typically pink or purple in color.

The plant is valued for its essential oils, which are used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent.
