Impatiens dahlii

Impatiens dahlii, commonly known as the "Chinese violet," is a herbaceous perennial native to Southeast Asia.

It features showy, spurred flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet, typically blooming in summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It prefers moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden beds.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to thrive in humid environments.
