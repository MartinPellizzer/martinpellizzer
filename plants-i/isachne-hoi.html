Isachne hoi

Isachne hoi, commonly known as the narrowleaf isachne, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetland or riparian habitats. The plant has slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often slightly hairy. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in spikelets, typical of the grass family.

Isachne hoi is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
