Inula × rigida var. hispida

Inula × rigida var. hispida, commonly known as the rough inula, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy, gray-green foliage that becomes more prominent as the plant matures. The plant produces yellow composite flowers in late summer, with a central disk surrounded by ray florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
