Indigofera hockii

Indigofera hockii, commonly known as the mountain indigo, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves, which are typically oval to heart-shaped. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to violet, often in clusters. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
