Inula hupehensis

Inula hupehensis, commonly known as the Chinese fleabane, is a flowering plant native to China.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring gray-green, hairy leaves and yellow-orange composite flowers. Its flowers are composed of ray florets and disk florets, typical of the daisy family.

Inula hupehensis is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks.
