Inga coriacea var. leptopus

Inga coriacea var. leptopus, commonly known as the slender inga, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its slender, often climbing stems and compound leaves with elongated leaflets. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow, followed by bean-like pods containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
