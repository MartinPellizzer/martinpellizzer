Incarvillea chinensis

Incarvillea chinensis, commonly known as Chinese bellflower, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has upright, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and ability to attract pollinators.
