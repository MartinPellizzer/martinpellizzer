Inga mitaraka

Inga mitaraka, commonly known as the Madagascar velvet bean, is a tropical shrub native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. Its roots are known for their nitrogen-fixing ability, making it valuable in agroforestry systems.

The plant is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
