Iris × viridivinea

Iris × viridivinea, commonly known as the green iris, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Iris sibirica and Iris germanica.

It is characterized by its vibrant greenish-blue flowers, which bloom in early to mid-summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, featuring sword-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

This ornamental plant is valued for its striking foliage and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
