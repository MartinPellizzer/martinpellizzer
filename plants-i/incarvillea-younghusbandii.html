Incarvillea younghusbandii

Incarvillea younghusbandii, commonly known as the Chinese bellflower, is a perennial plant native to China.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces legume-like pods that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
