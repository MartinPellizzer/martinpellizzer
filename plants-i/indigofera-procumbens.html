Indigofera procumbens

Indigofera procumbens, commonly known as creeping indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands. The plant is characterized by its prostrate growth habit, with slender stems that often root at the nodes. Its small, clustered flowers are typically pink or purple, and it produces cylindrical pods containing small, round seeds.

This species is often used in traditional medicine and as a forage crop for livestock.
