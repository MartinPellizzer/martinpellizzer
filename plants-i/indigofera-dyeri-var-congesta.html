Indigofera dyeri var. congesta

Indigofera dyeri var. congesta, commonly known as the African indigo or Congo indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of 1–2 meters, featuring pinnate leaves with 10–20 pairs of small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, often with a slight pinkish tinge. It is valued for its historical use in dye production and as a source of medicinal compounds.

The species is also noted for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in semi-arid conditions.
