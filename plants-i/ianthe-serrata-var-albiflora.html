Ianthe serrata var. albiflora

Ianthe serrata var. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered rock iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and striking white or pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers bloom in early summer, producing a showy display that attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
