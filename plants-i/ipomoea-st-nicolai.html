Ipomoea st-nicolai

Ipomoea st-nicolai, commonly known as the Nicolo's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet, typically blooming in the morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces distinctive, spirally arranged seed pods. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical regions.

Ipomoea st-nicolai is a climbing vine that can reach significant heights, making it a popular ornamental plant.
