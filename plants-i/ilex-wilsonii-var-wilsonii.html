Ilex wilsonii var. wilsonii

Ilex wilsonii var. wilsonii, commonly known as Wilson's holly, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that turn yellow in the fall, and it produces small, red berries that are an important food source for wildlife. The plant is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and is often used in ornamental horticulture. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it adaptable to various garden settings.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and aesthetic purposes.
