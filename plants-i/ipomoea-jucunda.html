Ipomoea jucunda

Ipomoea jucunda, commonly known as the silverleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of pale blue or white flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, black seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Ipomoea jucunda is a fast-growing vine that can reach lengths of several meters, making it both ornamental and potentially invasive in certain areas.
