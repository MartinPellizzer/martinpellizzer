Ilex acuminata

Ilex acuminata, commonly known as the prickly holly, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for various birds and wildlife. Ilex acuminata thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in dense thickets or along forest edges.

Its botanical features make it a valuable component of both ecological and ornamental landscapes.
