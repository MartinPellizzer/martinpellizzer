Indigofera pseudomoniliformis

Indigofera pseudomoniliformis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, trifoliate leaves. The plant produces clusters of pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple, depending on the species. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and is often found in disturbed soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
