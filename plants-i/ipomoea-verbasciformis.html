Ipomoea verbasciformis

Ipomoea verbasciformis, commonly known as the woolly morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet, often resembling those of the morning glory family. The plant has heart-shaped, hairy leaves that give it its common name, and it produces distinctive, elongated seed pods. It thrives in warm, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Ipomoea verbasciformis is a climbing vine that can reach several meters in length, making it a notable ornamental and ecological species.
