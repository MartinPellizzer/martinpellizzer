Ilex glabella

Ilex glabella, commonly known as the smoothleaf holly, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are smooth and oval in shape. The plant produces small, red berries that are toxic to humans but serve as a food source for birds. It typically grows in moist, acidic soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Ilex glabella is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
