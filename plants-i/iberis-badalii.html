Iberis badalii

Iberis badalii, commonly known as the Badalii candytuft, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers in early spring. The plant has hairy, lobed leaves and thrives in well-drained soils. It is often found in rocky or limestone areas, where it forms dense mats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine landscapes.
