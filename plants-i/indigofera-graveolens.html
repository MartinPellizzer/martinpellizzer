Indigofera graveolens

Indigofera graveolens, commonly known as Indian indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is a shrub that produces small, pea-like flowers in shades of pink, red, and purple. The plant is notable for its use in traditional dyeing practices, particularly for producing the deep blue color used in textiles. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three small, elliptical leaflets.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
