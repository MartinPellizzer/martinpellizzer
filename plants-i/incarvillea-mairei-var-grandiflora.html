Incarvillea mairei var. grandiflora

Incarvillea mairei var. grandiflora, commonly known as the Chinese bellflower, is a perennial plant native to China.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from purple to pink. The plant features slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental foliage and blooms.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
