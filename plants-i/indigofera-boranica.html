Indigofera boranica

Indigofera boranica, commonly known as the Boran indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to regions of Africa, particularly Ethiopia and Somalia, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers. It produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional practices.

Due to its hardy nature and historical use in dyeing textiles, it holds cultural and ecological significance in its native habitats.
