Iridorkis ensiformis

Iridorkis ensiformis, commonly known as the swordleaf iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers with three petals and three sepals, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
