Ipomoea chapadensis

Ipomoea chapadensis, commonly known as the Brazilian morning glory, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of purple and blue. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, smooth seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Ipomoea chapadensis is also known for its rapid growth and can be invasive in certain regions.
