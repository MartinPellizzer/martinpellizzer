Inga eriantha

Inga eriantha, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, Colombia, and Peru. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often covered in a soft, velvety texture. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by pods containing edible seeds.

The plant is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches the soil and supports sustainable agriculture.
