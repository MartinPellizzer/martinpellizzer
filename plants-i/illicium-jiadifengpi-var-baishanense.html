Illicium jiadifengpi var. baishanense

Illicium jiadifengpi var. baishanense, commonly known as the White Mountain star anise, is a variant of the star anise plant native to China.

It is characterized by its fragrant, star-shaped fruit that resembles the more well-known star anise, but with a lighter coloration. The plant belongs to the Schisandraceae family and is typically found in forested regions at higher altitudes. Its leaves are elliptical and glossy, while its flowers are small and greenish-white.

This variety is valued for its aromatic properties and is used in traditional medicine and as a spice.
