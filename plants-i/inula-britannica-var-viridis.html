Inula britannica var. viridis

Inula britannica var. viridis, commonly known as the green elecampane, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the United Kingdom.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, upright stems and yellow composite flowers. The plant has serrated, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its root system is fibrous and has been historically used in traditional medicine.

The species is adapted to grow in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or grassland habitats.
