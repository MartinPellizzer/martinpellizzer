Ipomoea concinna

Ipomoea concinna, commonly known as the scarlet morning glory, is a flowering vine native to the southeastern United States.

It produces strikingly beautiful, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep red, often appearing in the early morning. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are typically a deep green. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Ipomoea concinna is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
