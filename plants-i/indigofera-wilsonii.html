Indigofera wilsonii

Indigofera wilsonii, commonly known as Wilson's indigo, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces pods that contain seeds, and it has a deep root system that helps it thrive in poor soils.

It is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of dye due to its rich pigments.
