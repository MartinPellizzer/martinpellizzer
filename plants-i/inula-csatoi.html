Inula csatoi

Inula csatoi, commonly known as the Chinese eupatorium, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its flowering period occurs in the summer months, and it is often used in traditional Chinese medicine.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species for ecological and medicinal research.
