Illecebrum suffruticosum

Illecebrum suffruticosum, commonly known as the creeping morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a perennial vine characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from purple to blue, often appearing in the morning. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Illecebrum suffruticosum is also known for its rapid growth and is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.
