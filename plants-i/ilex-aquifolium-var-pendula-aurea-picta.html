Ilex aquifolium var. pendula-aurea-picta

Ilex aquifolium var. pendula-aurea-picta, commonly known as the golden weeping holly, is a variant of the common holly with distinctive botanical features.

It exhibits a pendulous growth habit, with long, drooping branches that create an elegant, weeping appearance. The leaves are lance-shaped, with a bright golden-yellow coloration that stands out against the dark green foliage. This variety is notable for its ornamental value, featuring a striking contrast in leaf color and form.

It is typically evergreen, maintaining its foliage year-round, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.
