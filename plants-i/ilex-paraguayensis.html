Ilex paraguayensis

Ilex paraguayensis, commonly known as the Brazilian holly, is a evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, red berries that are edible for birds and other wildlife. The plant is part of the Aquifoliaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in landscaping. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Ilex paraguayensis is also notable for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of caffeine in some regions.
