Isachne chevalieri

Isachne chevalieri, commonly known as the narrowleaf isachne, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in open grasslands and disturbed soils, exhibiting a clump-forming habit with slender, upright stems. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences, and its leaves are narrow and often hairy. It is characterized by its fine, grass-like foliage and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Isachne chevalieri is of interest in botanical studies due to its ecological role and potential for use in restoration projects.
