Ipomoea batatas var. leucantha

Ipomoea batatas var. leucantha, commonly known as the white sweet potato, is a variant of the sweet potato plant characterized by its pale to white flesh.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of the Americas. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves and trailing vines that bear tuberous roots. Its white flesh is less common than the orange variety and is often used in similar culinary ways.

The variety is valued for its unique color and nutritional profile.
