Indigofera pohliana

Indigofera pohliana, commonly known as the blue bean, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant is known for producing pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in local cuisines. Its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil makes it an important plant in agricultural systems.
