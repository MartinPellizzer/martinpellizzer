Ipomoea acutiflora

Ipomoea acutiflora, commonly known as the sharp-flowered morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It is a climbing vine that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of purple and blue. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is known for its rapid growth habit. It blooms throughout the growing season, typically from spring to early autumn.

Ipomoea acutiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
