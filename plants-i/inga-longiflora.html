Inga longiflora

Inga longiflora, commonly known as the long-flowered inga, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, clustered flowers. The plant produces pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it an important component of forest ecosystems.
