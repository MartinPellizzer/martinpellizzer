Iris eleonorae

Iris eleonorae, commonly known as the Eleanor iris, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features upright, sword-shaped leaves and produces showy, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive three-parted structure. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and limited natural range, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
