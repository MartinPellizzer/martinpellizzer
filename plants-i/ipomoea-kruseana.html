Ipomoea kruseana

Ipomoea kruseana, commonly known as the Kruse's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its rapid growth and climbing habit, often reaching heights of several meters. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its vines are covered in fine hairs.

Ipomoea kruseana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
