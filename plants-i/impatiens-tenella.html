Impatiens tenella

Impatiens tenella, commonly known as the velvetleaf impatiens, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, velvety leaves that are often dark green in color. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, with five petals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid tropical forests.

Impatiens tenella is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
