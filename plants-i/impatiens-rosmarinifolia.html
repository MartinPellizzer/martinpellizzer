Impatiens rosmarinifolia

Impatiens rosmarinifolia, commonly known as rosemary-leaved impatiens, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Africa, particularly Kenya and Tanzania.

It is characterized by its aromatic, fragrant leaves that resemble those of rosemary, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, which bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden areas.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a favored ornamental plant in horticulture.
