Idalia albiflora

Idalia albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered idalia, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its striking white petals and lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its flowers bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
