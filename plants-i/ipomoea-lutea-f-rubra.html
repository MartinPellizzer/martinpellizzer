Ipomoea lutea f. rubra

Ipomoea lutea f. rubra, commonly known as the red-flowered morning glory, is a variant of the morning glory family (Convolvulaceae).

It is a climbing annual vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The flowers are typically bright red or orange-red, blooming in the morning and closing by midday. The plant thrives in warm climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

It is cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
