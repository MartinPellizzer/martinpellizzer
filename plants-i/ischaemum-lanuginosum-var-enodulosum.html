Ischaemum lanuginosum var. enodulosum

Ischaemum lanuginosum var. enodulosum, commonly known as the woolly beakgrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-green foliage and compact, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences with narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural studies.
