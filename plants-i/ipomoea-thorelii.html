Ipomoea thorelii

Ipomoea thorelii, commonly known as the silverleaf morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. The plant produces strikingly large, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Ipomoea thorelii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can also be found in wild settings in its native habitat.
