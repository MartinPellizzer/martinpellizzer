Isoglossa laxa var. pilosa

Isoglossa laxa var. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy isoglossa, is a variant of the isoglossa plant characterized by its dense, soft pubescence on its stems and leaves.

It is a perennial herb native to specific regions of southern Africa, where it thrives in rocky or sandy soils. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically in terminal or axillary inflorescences, and are usually pale green or white in color.

This variant is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphological traits and its role in local ecosystems.
