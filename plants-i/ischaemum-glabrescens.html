Ischaemum glabrescens

Ischaemum glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth barnyard grass, is a species of grass native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically hairless or nearly so. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny florets, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

Due to its rapid growth and competitive nature, it can become a weed in cultivated areas.
