Isachne griffithii

Isachne griffithii, commonly known as Griffith's isachne, is a species of grass native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its fine, grass-like foliage and compact growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories or along stream banks. Its small, spike-like inflorescences bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers, which contribute to its reproductive strategy.

Isachne griffithii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native habitats.
