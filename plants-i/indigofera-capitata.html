Indigofera capitata

Indigofera capitata, commonly known as the clustered indigo.

It is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia. The plant is characterized by its dense, clustered inflorescences of small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets, and it often grows in shrub-like form.

The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable in ecological and agricultural contexts.
