Ipomoea batatas var. apiculata

Ipomoea batatas var. apiculata, commonly known as the sweet potato with an apiculate tip, is a variant of the sweet potato plant characterized by its unique leaf and stem morphology.

This variety is native to tropical and subtropical regions and is cultivated for its nutrient-rich tuberous roots. The leaves are typically heart-shaped with a pointed apex, and the stems are often hairy. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.

The plant is valued for its high carbohydrate content and is widely used in both culinary and agricultural contexts.
