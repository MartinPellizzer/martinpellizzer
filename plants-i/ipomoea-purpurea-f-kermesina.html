Ipomoea purpurea f. kermesina

Ipomoea purpurea f. kermesina, commonly known as the Crimson Glory morning glory, is a variant of the common morning glory with striking, deep red to purplish flowers.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped blooms that open in the early morning. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often reaching heights of several meters. Its flowers are typically short-lived, lasting only a day, and are pollinated by insects.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and as a decorative plant in containers.
