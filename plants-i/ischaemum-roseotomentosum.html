Ischaemum roseotomentosum

Ischaemum roseotomentosum, commonly known as the pink-tomentose grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a distinctive pinkish or grayish pubescence. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and along rocky outcrops.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a notable species in botanical collections and ecological studies.
