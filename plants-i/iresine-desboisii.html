Iresine desboisii

Iresine desboisii, commonly known as the red-veined sorrel, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves with deep red veins against a dark green background. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sun exposure.

Iresine desboisii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage.
