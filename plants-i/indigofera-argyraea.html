Indigofera argyraea

Indigofera argyraea, commonly known as silver indigo, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking silver-gray foliage and clusters of pink to purple flowers. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which benefits soil fertility. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and as a landscape plant.
