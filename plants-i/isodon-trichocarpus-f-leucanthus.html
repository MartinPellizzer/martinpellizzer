Isodon trichocarpus f. leucanthus

Isodon trichocarpus f. leucanthus, commonly known as the white-flowered isodon, is a botanical variant characterized by its distinctive white floral display.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for aromatic plants with square stems and opposite leaves. The plant typically features small, clustered flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slight pubescence, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

This form is prized in horticulture for its unique color contrast against typical green foliage.
