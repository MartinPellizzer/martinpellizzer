Ilex triflora var. lobbiana

Ilex triflora var. lobbiana, commonly known as the Lobb's holly, is a rare evergreen shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It is characterized by its dense, glossy dark green leaves and small, red berries that persist through winter. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments and thrives in well-drained, acidic soils. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance, providing food for birds and other wildlife.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a species of conservation concern.
