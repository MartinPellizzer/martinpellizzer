Ipomoea martiusiana

Ipomoea martiusiana, commonly known as the Martian morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves and is known for its rapid growth and climbing habit. It thrives in warm climates and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

The flowers typically bloom in the morning, giving the plant its common name.
