Indigofera ewartiana

Indigofera ewartiana, commonly known as the Indian indigo plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable in agricultural settings.

Its seeds are often used in traditional medicine, and the plant has been historically cultivated for its dye-producing properties.
