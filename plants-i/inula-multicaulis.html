Inula multicaulis

Inula multicaulis, commonly known as the common elecampane, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, covered with fine hairs. The plant produces yellow composite flowers arranged in a rounded head, with ray florets surrounding a central disk. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with a rough texture and prominent veins.

Inula multicaulis is often found in grasslands, meadows, and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils.
