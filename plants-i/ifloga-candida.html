Ifloga candida

Ifloga candida, commonly known as the white-flowered orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, white petals and a compact, herbaceous growth habit. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as rainforests. Its flowers are often fragrant and bloom in clusters during the wet season.

Botanically, it belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental beauty.
