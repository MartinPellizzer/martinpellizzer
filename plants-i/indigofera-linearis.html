Indigofera linearis

Indigofera linearis, commonly known as slender indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, trifoliate leaves. The plant produces clusters of pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It is native to regions of Africa and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Indigofera linearis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
