Inga semialata

Inga semialata, commonly known as the creeping inga, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically clustered in inflorescences. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

This species is often found in secondary forests and is valued for its use in agroforestry systems.
