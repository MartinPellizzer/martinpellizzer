Ipomoea involucrata var. operosa

Ipomoea involucrata var. operosa, commonly known as the velvet leaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are typically a deep green with a slight pubescence. The plant produces trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale purple, blooming in the early morning. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.
