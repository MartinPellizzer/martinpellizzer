Impatiens biflora f. platymeris

Impatiens biflora f. platymeris, commonly known as the flat-leaved impatiens, is a variant of the impatiens genus characterized by its broad, flat leaves.

It is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including Malaysia and Indonesia, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming from spring to early autumn. Its foliage is dense and often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal.

The species is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
