Indigofera brachynema

Indigofera brachynema, commonly known as the small-stemmed indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being ovate and hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
