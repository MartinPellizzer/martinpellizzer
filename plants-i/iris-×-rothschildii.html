Iris × rothschildii

Iris × rothschildii, commonly known as Rothschild's iris, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers with a distinctive yellow beard on the lower petals. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, featuring sword-shaped, dark green leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity in the wild, it is often cultivated by gardeners and botanists.
