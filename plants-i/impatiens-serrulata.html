Impatiens serrulata

Impatiens serrulata, commonly known as the serrated impatiens, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides. It is a herbaceous perennial that thrives in temperate climates.

Its flowers attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, making it a valuable addition to gardens and natural habitats.
