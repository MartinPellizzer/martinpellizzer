Ipomoea micrantha var. glabrata

Ipomoea micrantha var. glabrata, commonly known as the smooth morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing vines and heart-shaped leaves. The flowers are typically trumpet-shaped, with five petals, and are usually pale blue or violet in color. It produces small, round berries that contain seeds, and it thrives in warm, humid climates.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
