Isoetes coromandelina var. raipurensis

Isoetes coromandelina var. raipurensis, commonly known as the Raipur watermoss, is a species of aquatic plant belonging to the genus Isoetes.

It is characterized by its spiky, dimorphic spores and is typically found in freshwater habitats such as ponds and marshes. The plant has a unique morphology with a central axis and specialized leaves that aid in anchoring it in aquatic environments. It is native to regions in India and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Due to habitat loss and pollution, it is considered a threatened species in some areas.
