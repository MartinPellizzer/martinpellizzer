Inula vernoniiformis

Inula vernoniiformis, commonly known as the African elecampane, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a sturdy, upright stem covered in rough, hairy foliage. Its flowers are typically yellow, arranged in dense, spherical clusters called capitula.

Inula vernoniiformis is often found in grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils.
