Isoetes valida

Isoetes valida, commonly known as the valid water clover, is a species of aquatic fern that belongs to the genus Isoetes.

It is characterized by its unique, ribbon-like fronds that are often submerged in freshwater environments. The plant typically grows in shallow, still waters such as ponds and lakes, where it can anchor itself to the substrate. Its reproductive structures, known as sporangia, are clustered in spore-bearing cones that develop on the fronds.

Isoetes valida is notable for its ecological role in maintaining water quality and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
