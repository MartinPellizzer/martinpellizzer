Ipomoea plumieriana

Ipomoea plumieriana, commonly known as the blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that open in the morning and close in the late afternoon. The plant produces heart-shaped, palmate leaves with a velvety texture. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Ipomoea plumieriana is also known for its rapid growth and ability to climb using its tendrils.
