Iris spuria subsp. notha

Iris spuria subsp.

notha, commonly known as the narrow-leaf iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in wet meadows, riverbanks, and marshy areas.

The plant features slender, upright stems with narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. Its flowers are blue to violet in color, with three upright petals and three drooping sepals, forming a distinctive "iris" shape.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
