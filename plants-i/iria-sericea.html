Iria sericea

Iria sericea, commonly known as silky iria, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shaded areas.

The plant is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
