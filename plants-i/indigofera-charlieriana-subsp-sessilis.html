Indigofera charlieriana subsp. sessilis

Indigofera charlieriana subsp.

sessilis, commonly known as the sessile indigofera, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae. It is characterized by its slender, climbing habit and trifoliate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pale blue or purple in color, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system has the ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.

This species is native to parts of Southeast Asia and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
