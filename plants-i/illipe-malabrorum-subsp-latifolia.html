Illipe malabrorum subsp. latifolia

Illipe malabrorum subsp.

latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved illipe, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves that are often hairy on the underside.

The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by edible, oily fruits. Its bark and leaves have been traditionally used in local medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and economic potential in the oil and timber industries.
