Ipomoea uruguayensis var. elliptica

Ipomoea uruguayensis var. elliptica, commonly known as the Uruguay morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant is a herbaceous vine that thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil. It produces distinctive seed pods that split open to release its seeds, aiding in its propagation.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also studied for its potential medicinal properties.
