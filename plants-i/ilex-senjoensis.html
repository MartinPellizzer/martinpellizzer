Ilex senjoensis

Ilex senjoensis, commonly known as the Japanese holly, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Aquifoliaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically opposite in arrangement. The plant produces small, red berries that are ornamental and attract birds. It is often cultivated for its dense foliage and winter interest in gardens.

Ilex senjoensis prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a hardy addition to temperate landscapes.
