Ichthyothere angustifolia

Ichthyothere angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf water plant, is a aquatic plant characterized by its slender, linear leaves that are typically less than 1 cm in width.

It thrives in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds and slow-moving streams, where it can anchor itself to submerged surfaces. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish-white in color, which bloom in early summer. Its root system is fibrous and helps stabilize the sediment in its habitat.

The leaves are often covered in a waxy cuticle, which aids in reducing water loss and enhancing its survival in aquatic conditions.
