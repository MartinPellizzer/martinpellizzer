Impatiens capusii

Impatiens capusii, commonly known as the "touch-me-not" or "whispering bells," is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, pink, and white. The plant typically grows as an annual or biennial, reaching heights of up to 3 feet, with opposite, ovate leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Its unique seed dispersal method involves explosive pods that release seeds when touched, adding to its botanical intrigue.
