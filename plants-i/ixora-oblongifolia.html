Ixora oblongifolia

Ixora oblongifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf flame bush, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of bright orange-red flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has simple, elliptical leaves that are glossy and arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant floral display.

Ixora oblongifolia is also valued for its ability to tolerate partial shade and its resilience to coastal conditions.
