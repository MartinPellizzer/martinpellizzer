Ipomoea antillana

Ipomoea antillana, commonly known as the Antillean morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple, typically opening in the early morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is known for its rapid growth habit. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Ipomoea antillana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
