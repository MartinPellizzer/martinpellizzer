Ipheion uniflorum

Ipheion uniflorum, commonly known as the fairy duster, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features dense clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that form a dense mat, making it an excellent ground cover. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, preferring moist, acidic conditions.

Ipheion uniflorum is often used in ornamental landscaping for its attractive appearance and easy maintenance.
