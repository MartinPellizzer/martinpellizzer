Ipomoea pharbitiformis

Ipomoea pharbitiformis, commonly known as the morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its trumpet-shaped flowers that typically bloom in the morning and close in the afternoon. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas but has been naturalized in many parts of the world. Its vines can grow rapidly, often climbing or trailing along surfaces.

The leaves are heart-shaped and the plant produces edible seeds, though some varieties contain toxic compounds.
