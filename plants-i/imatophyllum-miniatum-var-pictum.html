Imatophyllum miniatum var. pictum

Imatophyllum miniatum var. pictum, commonly known as the painted miniature fern, is a rare and delicate species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, lace-like fronds that display a striking pattern of light and dark green hues. The plant thrives in cool, humid environments with high levels of indirect light, making it a challenging species to cultivate. Its unique foliage makes it a prized addition to specialized botanical collections.

Due to its limited natural range and specific care requirements, it is considered endangered in the wild.
