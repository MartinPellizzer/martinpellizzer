Ipomoea bonariensis var. chacoensis

Ipomoea bonariensis var. chacoensis, commonly known as the Chaco morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly the Chaco region.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and is known for its rapid growth and climbing habit. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers bloom in the early morning, hence its common name, and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
