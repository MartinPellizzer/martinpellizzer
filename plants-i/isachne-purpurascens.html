Isachne purpurascens

Isachne purpurascens, commonly known as the purple grass or purple isachne, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that often display a distinctive purplish tinge, especially under stress or during certain growth stages. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along streambanks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
