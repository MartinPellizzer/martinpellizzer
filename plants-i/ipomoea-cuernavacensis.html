Ipomoea cuernavacensis

Ipomoea cuernavacensis, commonly known as the Cuernavaca morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces slender, winding vines that can climb or trail along the ground. Its flowers are typically short-lived, opening in the morning and closing by midday.

Ipomoea cuernavacensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color.
