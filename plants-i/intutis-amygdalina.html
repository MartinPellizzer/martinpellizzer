Intutis amygdalina

Intutis amygdalina, commonly known as the African peach, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and alternate, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by edible, sweet fruits similar to peaches. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and culinary uses in local communities.
