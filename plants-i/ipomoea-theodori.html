Ipomoea theodori

Ipomoea theodori, commonly known as the Theodore's morning glory, is a flowering vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple, often opening in the early morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is characterized by its rapid growth habit. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Ipomoea theodori is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can climb supports or trellises as it grows.
