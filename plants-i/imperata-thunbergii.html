Imperata thunbergii

Imperata thunbergii, commonly known as Japanese bloodgrass, is a perennial grass native to East Asia.

It features long, narrow leaves that are often tinged with red or purple, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically pale brown or green. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens but can become invasive in certain environments.
