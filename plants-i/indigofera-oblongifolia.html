Indigofera oblongifolia

Indigofera oblongifolia, commonly known as the African indigo or blue bean, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces pods that contain seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to warm, humid climates.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
