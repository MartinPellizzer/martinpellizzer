Iria drummondii

Iria drummondii, commonly known as the Drummond's iris, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family, which includes irises and related ornamental plants. The plant features showy, violet-blue to purple flowers with three petals and three sepals, typical of the iris family. Its leaves are grass-like and arranged in a fan shape, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Iria drummondii thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
