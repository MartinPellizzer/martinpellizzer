Ischaemum eriostachyum

Ischaemum eriostachyum, commonly known as the narrowleaf beakgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. The plant has slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a distinct, bristly texture. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, two-lobed spikelets.

Ischaemum eriostachyum plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local insect populations in its native range.
