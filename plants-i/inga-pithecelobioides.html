Inga pithecelobioides

Inga pithecelobioides, commonly known as the dwarf inga, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pale yellow or greenish-white, followed by pod fruits containing edible seeds.

It is often found in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
