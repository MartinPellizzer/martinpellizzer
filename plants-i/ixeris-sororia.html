Ixeris sororia

Ixeris sororia, commonly known as the Japanese groundsel, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches heights of about 10 to 20 centimeters. The plant features basal leaves that are oblanceolate or elliptic in shape, with a hairy texture. Its flowers are small, yellow, and borne in clusters, typically blooming in the summer months.

Ixeris sororia is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.
