Ibatia ciliata

Ibatia ciliata, commonly known as the Brazilian rosewood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its dark, dense wood and distinctive leaves with ciliate margins. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a glossy appearance. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by small, round fruits.

The botanical features of Ibatia ciliata make it a valuable species for both ecological and commercial purposes.
