Ixora filmeri

Ixora filmeri, commonly known as the flame-of-hippur or scarlet ixora, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height, characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces clusters of bright red or orange flowers, which are typically tubular and arranged in panicles. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a leathery texture.

Ixora filmeri is well-suited for tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
