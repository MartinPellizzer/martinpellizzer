Indigofera swaziensis var. perplexa

Indigofera swaziensis var. perplexa, commonly known as the Swazi indigo bush, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant is typically found in woodland and rocky habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its flowers, which range in color from pale blue to violet, attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and potential ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
