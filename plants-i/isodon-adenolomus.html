Isodon adenolomus

Isodon adenolomus, commonly known as the mountain germander, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their medicinal properties. The plant features square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly serrated margin. Its small, tubular flowers are typically blue or purple, arranged in whorled clusters.

Isodon adenolomus is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
