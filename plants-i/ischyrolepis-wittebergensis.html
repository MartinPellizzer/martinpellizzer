Ischyrolepis wittebergensis

Ischyrolepis wittebergensis, commonly known as the Witteberg daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like inflorescence composed of yellow disc flowers and white ray florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in mountainous regions. It is a perennial herb that thrives in full sun and has a compact, bushy growth habit.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
