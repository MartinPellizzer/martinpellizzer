Indigofera trifoliata var. glandulifera

Indigofera trifoliata var. glandulifera, commonly known as the glandular trifoliate indigo, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets, and its distinctive pink to purple flowers. The plant is often found in moist, tropical environments and thrives in partial shade. Its stems are often hairy, and it produces small, bean-like pods that contain seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
