Impatiens tinctoria

Impatiens tinctoria, commonly known as the Mexican morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in moist, shady environments. Its flowers bloom in the early morning, hence its common name, and it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

The plant is also valued for its historical use in dye production, as its flowers can be used to create natural pigments.
