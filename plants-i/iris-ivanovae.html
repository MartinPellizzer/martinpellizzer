Iris ivanovae

Iris ivanovae, commonly known as the Ivanov's iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down.

Iris ivanovae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique floral display.
