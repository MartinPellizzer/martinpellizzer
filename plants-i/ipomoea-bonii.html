Ipomoea bonii

Ipomoea bonii, commonly known as the blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that open in the morning and close in the afternoon. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Ipomoea bonii is also known for its rapid growth and ability to climb using its tendrils.
