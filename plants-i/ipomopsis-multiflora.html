Ipomopsis multiflora

Ipomopsis multiflora, commonly known as the many-flowered trumpetweed, is a member of the Campanulaceae family.

It is a perennial herb native to the western United States, typically found in rocky slopes and open woodlands. The plant features showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its stems are erect and branching.

Ipomopsis multiflora is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
