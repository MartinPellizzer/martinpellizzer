Ilex dipyrena var. leptacantha

Ilex dipyrena var. leptacantha, commonly known as the slender-leaf holly, is a variant of the holly genus native to southern China.

It is a small evergreen shrub or tree characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, followed by red berries that are an important food source for birds. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for ornamental landscaping.

Its unique foliage and seasonal berries contribute to its aesthetic value in gardens and natural habitats.
