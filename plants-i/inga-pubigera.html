Inga pubigera

Inga pubigera, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant is notable for its large, palmate leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. Its fruit is a pod that contains edible seeds, often used in local cuisines.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in forest ecosystems.
