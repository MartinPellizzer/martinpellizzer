Ixora sessilifolia

Ixora sessilifolia, commonly known as the sessile-leaf ixora, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and clusters of small, bright red or orange flowers. The leaves are typically sessile, meaning they are attached directly to the stems without stalks. This species thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and vibrant floral display.
