Impatiens grey-wilsonii

Impatiens grey-wilsonii, commonly known as the Grey-Wilson's impatiens, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the genus Impatiens, which is characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers and fibrous seed pods. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest understories, where it thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are ovate to elliptical in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and its flowers are usually single or clustered, displaying a range of colors from white to pink and red.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens, although it is more commonly found in its natural habitat.
