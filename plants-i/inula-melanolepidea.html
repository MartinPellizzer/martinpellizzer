Inula melanolepidea

Inula melanolepidea, commonly known as the black-leaved inula, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a slightly rough texture. Its flowers are yellow and are arranged in a circular pattern, forming a composite head that attracts pollinators.

Inula melanolepidea is often found in rocky or arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.
