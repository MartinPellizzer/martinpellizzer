Ipomoea longiflora

Ipomoea longiflora, commonly known as the long-flowered morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet, often opening in the morning. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces cylindrical, hairy fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in gardens and wild areas.

Ipomoea longiflora is a climbing vine that can grow up to several meters in length, making it a popular ornamental plant.
