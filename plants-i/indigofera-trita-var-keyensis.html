Indigofera trita var. keyensis

Indigofera trita var. keyensis, commonly known as Key indigo, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces pods that contain lens-shaped seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.

Its growth habit includes both annual and perennial forms, depending on the region and environmental conditions.
