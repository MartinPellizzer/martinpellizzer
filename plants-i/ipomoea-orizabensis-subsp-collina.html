Ipomoea orizabensis subsp. collina

Ipomoea orizabensis subsp.

collina, commonly known as the mountain morning glory, is a climbing vine native to Mexico and Central America. It is characterized by its heart-shaped, hairy leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun. Its flowering period typically occurs in the spring and early summer.

It is often found in woodland edges and along stream banks, where it contributes to local biodiversity.
