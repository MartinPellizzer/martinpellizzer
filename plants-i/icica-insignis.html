Icica insignis

Icica insignis, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It features a compact rosette of dark green, waxy leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer, resembling small bells. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
