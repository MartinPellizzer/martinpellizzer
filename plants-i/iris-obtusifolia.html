Iris obtusifolia

Iris obtusifolia, commonly known as the blunt-leaved iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are rounded at the tips, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers with three upright petals and three drooping sepals. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or grassland habitats.

Iris obtusifolia is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
