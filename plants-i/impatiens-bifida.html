Impatiens bifida

Impatiens bifida, commonly known as the splitleaf impatiens, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, deeply lobed leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of showy, spurred flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming during the rainy season. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Impatiens bifida is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
