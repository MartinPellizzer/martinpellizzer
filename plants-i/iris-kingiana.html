Iris kingiana

Iris kingiana, commonly known as the King's iris, is a striking perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear showy, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive white throat. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and slightly hairy. Its flowers bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Iris kingiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegance and resilience.
