Ixora coralloraphis

Ixora coralloraphis, commonly known as the coral-leaf ixora, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange to red flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a striking visual appeal to gardens. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves that are opposite and elliptical in shape. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Ixora coralloraphis is relatively drought-tolerant once established, making it suitable for tropical and subtropical landscapes.
