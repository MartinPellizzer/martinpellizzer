Ivesia mollis

Ivesia mollis, commonly known as the softleaf ivesia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the Rosaceae family, which includes roses and other shrubs. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is characterized by its finely divided, silvery-gray leaves. Its small, white to pink flowers appear in spring and are arranged in clusters.

Ivesia mollis is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
