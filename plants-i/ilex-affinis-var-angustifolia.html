Ilex affinis var. angustifolia

Ilex affinis var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf holly, is a shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It has evergreen leaves that are typically narrow and pointed, with a glossy dark green upper surface and a lighter underside. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, followed by bright red berries that are an important food source for birds. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Its dense growth habit also provides good habitat for wildlife.
