Ischaemum crassipes var. hondae

Ischaemum crassipes var. hondae, commonly known as the Honduran roughgrass, is a variant of the species native to Central America.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its coarse, fibrous stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. This plant typically grows in moist, disturbed habitats such as riverbanks and wetlands. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are often clustered in loose, open panicles.
