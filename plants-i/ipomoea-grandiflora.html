Ipomoea grandiflora

Ipomoea grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered morning glory, is a perennial vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant produces distinctive seed pods that split open to release spiral-shaped seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Ipomoea grandiflora is also known for its rapid growth and ability to climb various structures.
