Inula shirensis

Inula shirensis, commonly known as the Shirensis elecampane, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features a tall, hollow stem covered in coarse, hairy foliage, and it produces yellow-orange composite flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a strong, often aromatic scent.

Inula shirensis is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
