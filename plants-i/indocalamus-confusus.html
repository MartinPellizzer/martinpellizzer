Indocalamus confusus

Indocalamus confusus, commonly known as the confused bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that grow in clumps. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are long and narrow, often with a slight waxy coating.

Indocalamus confusus is valued in traditional medicine and is also used in ornamental horticulture due to its aesthetic appeal.
