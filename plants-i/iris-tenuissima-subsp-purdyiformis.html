Iris tenuissima subsp. purdyiformis

Iris tenuissima subsp.

purdyiformis, commonly known as the slender iris or Purdy's iris, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States. It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and delicate, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or meadow habitats. It is a member of the Iridaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.

Its subspecific classification highlights its distinct morphological features compared to other iris species.
