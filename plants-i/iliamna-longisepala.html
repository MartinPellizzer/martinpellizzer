Iliamna longisepala

Iliamna longisepala, commonly known as the long-sepaled willow, is a species of flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in moist, often shady environments such as stream banks and forest understories. The plant has slender, woody stems and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are small, clustered in catkins, and are typically greenish-yellow in color.

Iliamna longisepala plays an important ecological role in its habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
