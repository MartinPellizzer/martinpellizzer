Ipomoea petiolulata

Ipomoea petiolulata, commonly known as the small-leaved morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a vine that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of blue, violet, and white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and is known for its rapid growth habit. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Ipomoea petiolulata is also valued for its ability to climb and cover trellises or fences.
