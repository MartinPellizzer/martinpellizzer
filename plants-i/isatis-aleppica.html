Isatis aleppica

Isatis aleppica, commonly known as the Aleppo chicory, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are edible and have a slightly bitter taste, often used in salads or as a substitute for endive.

Isatis aleppica is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
