Icacorea furfuracella

Icacorea furfuracella, commonly known as the silky ickel, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive, almost fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. Its leaves are covered in fine, soft hairs, which help reduce water loss in its arid habitat.

Icacorea furfuracella is often used in ornamental gardening due to its unique appearance and hardy nature.
