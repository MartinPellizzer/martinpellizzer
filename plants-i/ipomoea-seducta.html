Ipomoea seducta

Ipomoea seducta, commonly known as the sweet potato vine, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, variegated foliage. The plant produces trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its tuberous roots are edible and are a staple in many cuisines.

Ipomoea seducta is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
