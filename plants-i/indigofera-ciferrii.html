Indigofera ciferrii

Indigofera ciferrii, commonly known as the Italian indigo plant, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable in ecological and agricultural contexts. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal environments.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in dye production, it is cultivated in some regions for both aesthetic and practical purposes.
