Impatiens jaegeri

Impatiens jaegeri, commonly known as the golden impatiens, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its glossy, ovate leaves and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and prefers moist, well-drained soil. It is a herbaceous perennial that thrives in partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden beds.

Its showy blooms attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to garden biodiversity.
