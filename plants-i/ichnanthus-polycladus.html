Ichnanthus polycladus

Ichnanthus polycladus, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compact, mounded growth habit and clusters of small, blue-violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and slightly hairy, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Ichnanthus polycladus is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
