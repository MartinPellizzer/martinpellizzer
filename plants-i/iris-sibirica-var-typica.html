Iris sibirica var. typica

Iris sibirica var. typica, commonly known as the Siberian iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of Asia.

It features upright, grass-like leaves and produces showy, blue to purple flowers with a distinctive three-parted structure. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in wet meadows, marshes, and along stream banks.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
