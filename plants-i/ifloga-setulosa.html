Ifloga setulosa

Ifloga setulosa, commonly known as the hairy senna, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with fine, hairy textures and bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family, also known as the legume or bean family. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Ifloga setulosa is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
