Ipomoea hotteana

Ipomoea hotteana, commonly known as the hottean morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and thrives in warm, humid climates. It is a climbing vine that can reach lengths of several meters, often used in ornamental gardening.

The flowers typically bloom in the morning and close in the afternoon, giving it its common name.
