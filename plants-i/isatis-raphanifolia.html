Isatis raphanifolia

Isatis raphanifolia, commonly known as the Chinese yellow mustard, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of pinnately lobed leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces long, slender pods containing several seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a spice. Native to East Asia, it thrives in temperate climates and is often found in disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and agricultural purposes.
