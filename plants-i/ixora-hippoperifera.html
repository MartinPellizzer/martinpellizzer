Ixora hippoperifera

Ixora hippoperifera, commonly known as the red ixora, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its vibrant red or orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the rainy season. The plant has opposite, simple, and often elliptical leaves that are dark green and glossy. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas or near freshwater sources.

Ixora hippoperifera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and as a landscape plant.
