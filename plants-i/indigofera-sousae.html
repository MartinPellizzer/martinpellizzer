Indigofera sousae

Indigofera sousae, commonly known as the Sudan indigo plant, is a shrub native to the Sahel region of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it valuable for soil enrichment. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in sandy or rocky soils.

Its seeds are used in traditional medicine and as a source of dye.
