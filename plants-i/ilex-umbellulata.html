Ilex umbellulata

Ilex umbellulata, commonly known as the Florida holly, is a deciduous evergreen shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are typically elliptical in shape and have a waxy texture. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters, followed by red berries that are an important food source for wildlife. Ilex umbellulata thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in suitable climates.
