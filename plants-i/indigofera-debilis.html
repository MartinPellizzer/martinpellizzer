Indigofera debilis

Indigofera debilis, commonly known as the slender indigo, is a low-growing shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. It has a fibrous root system and produces small, pod-like fruits containing several seeds.

Its growth habit and ecological preferences make it an important component of its native habitats.
