Iria quinquangularis

Iria quinquangularis, commonly known as the five-angled iris, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive five-angled seed pods, which give the plant its scientific name. The plant produces showy blue or violet flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its ornamental value and adaptability have made it a favored plant in horticulture.
