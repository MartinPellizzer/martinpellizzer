Indigofera phyllogramme var. aphylla

Indigofera phyllogramme var. aphylla, commonly known as the leafless indigo.

This variant is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its reduced foliage, with only a few small leaves or no leaves at all. The plant produces striking clusters of bright blue or violet flowers, which are often used in ornamental gardening. It is native to parts of Africa and thrives in warm, tropical climates.

Its unique appearance makes it a distinctive addition to botanical collections and gardens.
