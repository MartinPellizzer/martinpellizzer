Iris cedreti

Iris cedreti, commonly known as the Cedreti iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming a basal rosette, while its flowers are composed of three outer petals and three inner sepals.

Iris cedreti is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking blooms.
