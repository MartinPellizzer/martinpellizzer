Inga leptantha

Inga leptantha, commonly known as the pink inga, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant is distinguished by its showy, pink or purple flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are compound, typically consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and the tree can grow up to 20 meters in height.

Inga leptantha is often found in moist, shaded environments within tropical rainforests.
