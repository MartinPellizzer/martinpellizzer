Iris pseudopumila var. panormitana

Iris pseudopumila var. panormitana, commonly known as the Panormitana iris, is a rare and distinctive variety of the iris genus native to specific regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers with a distinctive white or pale yellow center, and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or shaded areas. It is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.

Due to its limited natural range, it is considered a conservation concern in some areas.
