Isopyrum capnoides

Isopyrum capnoides, commonly known as the common rockdock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features a rhizomatous root system and has alternate, lance-shaped leaves with a waxy surface. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in dense spikes and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Isopyrum capnoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
