Ixora spirei

Ixora spirei, commonly known as the flame bush, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has opposite, simple, and usually ovate or elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Ixora spirei is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
