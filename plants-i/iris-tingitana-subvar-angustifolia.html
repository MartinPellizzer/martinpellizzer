Iris tingitana subvar. angustifolia

Iris tingitana subvar. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rock iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid regions of North Africa.

It features upright, grass-like leaves that are narrow and pointed, typically ranging from 10 to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces striking blue to violet flowers with three upright petals and three spreading sepals, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing on rocky slopes or in dry meadows.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to harsh environmental conditions.
