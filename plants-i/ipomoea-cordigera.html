Ipomoea cordigera

Ipomoea cordigera, commonly known as the heartleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in a range of colors, including purple, pink, and white. The plant is a climbing vine with tendrils that allow it to ascend supports, often reaching lengths of several meters. Its flowers open in the morning and close at dusk, hence its common name.

Ipomoea cordigera is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
