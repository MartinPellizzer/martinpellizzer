Inula × hybrida

Inula × hybrida, commonly known as the hybrid elecampane, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems and large, showy yellow or orange composite flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and features lance-shaped, rough, and hairy leaves. Its root system consists of thick, fleshy rhizomes, which are often used in traditional herbal medicine.

Inula × hybrida is native to Europe and parts of Asia but has been cultivated in various regions for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.
