Indigofera endecaphylla

Indigofera endecaphylla, commonly known as the "blue flower" or "blue bean," is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, bean-like pods that contain seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections for its vibrant floral display.
