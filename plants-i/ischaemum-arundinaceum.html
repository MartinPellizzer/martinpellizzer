Ischaemum arundinaceum

Ischaemum arundinaceum, commonly known as bamboo grass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences containing numerous tiny florets, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, along stream banks, and in disturbed areas.

Due to its rapid growth and adaptability, it can become invasive in certain environments.
