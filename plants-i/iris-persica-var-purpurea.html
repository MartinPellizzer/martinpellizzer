Iris persica var. purpurea

Iris persica var. purpurea, commonly known as the Persian iris, is a striking perennial plant native to the regions of Persia and Central Asia.

It features tall, slender stems that bear showy, lavender-pink to purple flowers with a distinctive, dark central spot. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are arranged in a fan-like pattern. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its elegant appearance and ability to bloom in early spring.
