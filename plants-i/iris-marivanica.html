Iris marivanica

Iris marivanica, commonly known as the Marivan iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features tall, slender stems that bear striking blue to purple flowers with three upright petals and three drooping sepals. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

The species is valued for its beauty and is often cultivated for its showy blooms in botanical gardens.
