Iberis umbellata var. atropurpurea

Iberis umbellata var. atropurpurea, commonly known as purple flowering candytuft, is a perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It features compact, low-growing foliage with silvery-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long flowering period and ability to attract pollinators.
