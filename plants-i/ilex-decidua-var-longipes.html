Ilex decidua var. longipes

Ilex decidua var. longipes, commonly known as the long-needle holly, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its opposite, elliptical leaves that are usually dark green on top and lighter on the underside. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in partial to full shade.

Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and winter interest, making it a popular choice for landscaping.
