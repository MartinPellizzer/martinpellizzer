Isocoma humilis

Isocoma humilis, commonly known as the dwarf ironweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer.

Isocoma humilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
