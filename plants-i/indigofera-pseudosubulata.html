Indigofera pseudosubulata

Indigofera pseudosubulata, commonly known as the woolly indigo, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from pale blue to violet. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a woolly appearance, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

This species is also valued for its potential in soil stabilization and as a forage plant for livestock.
