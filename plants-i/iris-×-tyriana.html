Iris × tyriana

Iris × tyriana, commonly known as the Spanish iris, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Iris xiphoidalis and Iris germanica.

It is a robust perennial herb with tall, upright stems that bear showy, violet-blue or white flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and has lance-shaped, dark green leaves. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.
