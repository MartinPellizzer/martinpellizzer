Iris reticulata var. bakeriana

Iris reticulata var. bakeriana, commonly known as the Baker's reticulated iris, is a compact perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features striking, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive reticulated pattern on the outer petals. The plant produces sword-shaped leaves that are typically evergreen and arise from a bulbous rhizome. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or alpine settings.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and hardy nature, blooming in early spring.
