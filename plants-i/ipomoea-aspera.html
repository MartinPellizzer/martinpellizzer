Ipomoea aspera

Ipomoea aspera, commonly known as the rough morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a herbaceous vine characterized by its hairy, rough-textured stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet, often opening in the morning. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Ipomoea aspera is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain areas.
