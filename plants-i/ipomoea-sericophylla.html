Ipomoea sericophylla

Ipomoea sericophylla, commonly known as the silky-leaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, hairy leaves and trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows as a climbing vine, reaching lengths of up to several meters. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
