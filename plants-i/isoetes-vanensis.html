Isoetes vanensis

Isoetes vanensis, commonly known as the Van's watermoss, is a species of aquatic fern that belongs to the order Isoetales.

It is characterized by its dense, ribbon-like leaves that form a mat on the bottom of freshwater habitats. The plant is typically found in slow-moving or still waters, such as ponds and lakes, where it can thrive in nutrient-rich substrates. Its spores are released into the water and germinate to form new plants, contributing to its reproductive strategy.

Isoetes vanensis plays a role in maintaining water quality and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
