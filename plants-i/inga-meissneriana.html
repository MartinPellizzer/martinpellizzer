Inga meissneriana

Inga meissneriana, commonly known as the pink inga, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, Colombia, and Ecuador. The plant is characterized by its striking pink or red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it can grow as a shrub or small tree.

Inga meissneriana is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches the soil and supports sustainable agriculture.
