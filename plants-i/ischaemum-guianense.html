Ischaemum guianense

Ischaemum guianense, commonly known as the Guianese panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit. The plant typically features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences. It thrives in moist, tropical environments and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Its botanical structure makes it well-adapted to humid climates and nutrient-rich soils.
