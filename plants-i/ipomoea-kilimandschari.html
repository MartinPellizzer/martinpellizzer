Ipomoea kilimandschari

Ipomoea kilimandschari, commonly known as the Kilimanjaro morning glory, is a climbing vine native to East Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of purple and blue. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun. Its flowers open in the morning and close by midday, giving it its common name.

Ipomoea kilimandschari is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
