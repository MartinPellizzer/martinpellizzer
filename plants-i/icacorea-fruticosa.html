Icacorea fruticosa

Icacorea fruticosa, commonly known as the coastal hibiscus, is a shrub native to coastal regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in sandy soils and is adapted to salt spray, making it a hardy species in coastal environments. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a rough texture.

Icacorea fruticosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing dune systems.
