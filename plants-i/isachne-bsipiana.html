Isachne bsipiana

Isachne bsipiana, commonly known as the narrowleaf isachne, is a grass species native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are typically less than 2 millimeters wide. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
