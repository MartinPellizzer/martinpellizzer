Iris kashmiriana

Iris kashmiriana, commonly known as the Kashmir iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers with a distinctive yellow or white center. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, temperate climates. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread and form dense clumps over time.

Iris kashmiriana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
