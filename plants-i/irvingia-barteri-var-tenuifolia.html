Irvingia barteri var. tenuifolia

Irvingia barteri var. tenuifolia, commonly known as the slender-leaf Irvingia, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Irvingiaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by woody, three-angled fruits containing several seeds. It is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested or woodland areas.

The species is of interest for its potential medicinal properties and ecological role in its native habitat.
