Isatis afghanica

Isatis afghanica, commonly known as Afghan woad, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant produces small, yellow flowers and has alternate, lance-shaped leaves. It is often found in arid and semi-arid regions, thriving in sandy or rocky soils.

The species is noted for its historical use in dye production, similar to its more famous relative, Isatis indigotica.
