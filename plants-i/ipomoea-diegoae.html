Ipomoea diegoae

Ipomoea diegoae, commonly known as Diego's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that open in the morning. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves and has a climbing habit, often twining around supports. Its flowers are typically 5-7 cm in diameter and have a velvety texture.

Ipomoea diegoae is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
