Ipomoea kleiniana

Ipomoea kleiniana, commonly known as the Klein's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet. The plant is a climbing vine that produces a milky sap and thrives in warm, humid climates. It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its flowers typically bloom in the early morning, giving it the common name "morning glory.".
