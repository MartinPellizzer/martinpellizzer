Indigofera hendecaphylla var. siamensis

Indigofera hendecaphylla var. siamensis, commonly known as the Siam indigo plant, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pale yellow to deep purple. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local cultures.
