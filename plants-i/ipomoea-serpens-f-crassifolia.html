Ipomoea serpens f. crassifolia

Ipomoea serpens f. crassifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf morning glory, is a variant of the morning glory genus characterized by its thick, broad leaves.

It is a herbaceous perennial vine native to tropical regions of the Americas. The plant produces large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to violet, blooming in the morning. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a waxy texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens.
