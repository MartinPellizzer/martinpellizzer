Ivodea confertifolia

Ivodea confertifolia, commonly known as the dense-leaved ivy, is a climbing plant characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are arranged in a dense, compact manner.

It belongs to the family Araliaceae and is native to tropical regions of South America. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by small, berry-like fruits. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

Ivodea confertifolia thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
