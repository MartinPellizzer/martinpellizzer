Impatiens lineata

Impatiens lineata, commonly known as the variegated impatiens, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, variegated foliage with white and green patterns, and its clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial, depending on the climate, and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.

Impatiens lineata is also valued for its adaptability to partial shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden beds.
