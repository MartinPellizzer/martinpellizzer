Indigofera sebungweensis

Indigofera sebungweensis, commonly known as the Sebungwe indigo, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is typically shrub-like, with a woody stem and can grow up to several meters in height.

It is adapted to dry, savanna-like environments and plays a role in soil nitrogen fixation due to its symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria.
