Ipomoea variabilis

Ipomoea variabilis, commonly known as the morning glory, is a flowering vine native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its rapid growth and trumpet-shaped flowers that open in the morning and close in the evening. The plant produces showy blooms in shades of purple, blue, and white, often with intricate patterns. Its leaves are heart-shaped and can vary in size depending on environmental conditions.

Ipomoea variabilis is also known for its climbing habit and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
