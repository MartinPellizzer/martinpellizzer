Isachne angustifolia

Isachne angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf isachne, is a grass species native to various regions of Asia, including China, India, and Japan.

It typically grows in moist environments such as wetlands, marshes, and along riverbanks. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often slightly hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a loose cluster of spikelets, each bearing small, two-lobed flowers.

Isachne angustifolia is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
