Inversodicraea tanzaniensis

Inversodicraea tanzaniensis, commonly known as the Tanzanian hakea, is a shrub native to Tanzania.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its woody structure and dense foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or white in color.
