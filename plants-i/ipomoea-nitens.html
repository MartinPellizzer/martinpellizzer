Ipomoea nitens

Ipomoea nitens, commonly known as the silverleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pale blue to white flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Ipomoea nitens thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

It is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and rapid growth habit.
