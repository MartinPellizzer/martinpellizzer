Indigofera torulosa

Indigofera torulosa, commonly known as the blue bean plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pea-like flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable in agricultural settings. Its seeds are used in traditional medicine and as a source of protein.

The plant's ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
