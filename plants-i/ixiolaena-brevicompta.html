Ixiolaena brevicompta

Ixiolaena brevicompta, commonly known as the broad-leafed sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, exhibiting a rosette growth pattern with broad, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are characteristic of the Cyperaceae family. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread vegetatively, making it a common component of aquatic and semi-aquatic ecosystems.
