Ipomoea catesbaei

Ipomoea catesbaei, commonly known as the scarlet morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a vigorous vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep red. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Ipomoea catesbaei is often found in woodland edges, along stream banks, and in disturbed habitats.
