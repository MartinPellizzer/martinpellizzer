Inula × semicordata

Inula × semicordata, commonly known as the hybrid false sunflower, is a perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is a natural hybrid between Inula helenium and Inula crassifolia, characterized by its distinctive, semicircular, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow to orange composite flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its robust growth and long-lasting blooms.
