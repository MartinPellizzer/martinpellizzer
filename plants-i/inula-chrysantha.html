Inula chrysantha

Inula chrysantha, commonly known as the yellow fleabane, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and prairies. It produces yellow-orange flower heads composed of numerous small florets, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Inula chrysantha is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
