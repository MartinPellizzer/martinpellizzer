Ionidium floribundum

Ionidium floribundum, commonly known as the golden bell vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its cascading clusters of golden-yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant has heart-shaped, glossy leaves and thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant.

Ionidium floribundum is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
