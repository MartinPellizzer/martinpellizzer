Indigofera zenkeri

Indigofera zenkeri, commonly known as the African indigo or Zenker's indigo, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable in ecological and agricultural contexts.

Its seeds are often used in traditional medicine, and the plant can thrive in a variety of soil types.
