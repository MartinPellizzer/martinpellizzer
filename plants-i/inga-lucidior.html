Inga lucidior

Inga lucidior, commonly known as the shining inga, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with a glossy, often dark green appearance, and it produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several seeds, and the tree can grow up to 20 meters tall.

Inga lucidior is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
