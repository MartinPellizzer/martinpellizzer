Inula japonica

Inula japonica, commonly known as Japanese elecampane, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional medicine.

Inula japonica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
