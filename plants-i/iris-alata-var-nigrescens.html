Iris alata var. nigrescens

Iris alata var. nigrescens, commonly known as the black-flowered flag iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features striking, dark purple to blackish violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces seed pods that split open to release seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its unique coloration makes it a distinctive and attractive addition to native plant gardens.
