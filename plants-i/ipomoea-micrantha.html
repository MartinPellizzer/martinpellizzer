Ipomoea micrantha

Ipomoea micrantha, commonly known as the small-flowered morning glory, is a herbaceous perennial native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features heart-shaped leaves and produces small, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple. The plant is characterized by its rapid growth and climbing habit, often using tendrils to ascend supports. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Ipomoea micrantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
