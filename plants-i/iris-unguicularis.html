Iris unguicularis

Iris unguicularis, commonly known as the Canary Island iris, is a species native to the Canary Islands.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features sword-shaped leaves and produces showy, violet-blue flowers with three upright petals and three drooping sepals. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
