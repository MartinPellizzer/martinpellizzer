Ipomoea platensis var. erecta

Ipomoea platensis var. erecta, commonly known as the everlasting vine, is a tropical flowering plant characterized by its vigorous growth and climbing habit.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a red throat. It is native to Central and South America and thrives in warm, humid climates.

Due to its rapid growth and ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a garden plant or for ornamental purposes.
