Indigofera ervoides

Indigofera ervoides, commonly known as the African indigo, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces pods containing seeds that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and herbal practices.
