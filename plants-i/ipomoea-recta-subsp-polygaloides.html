Ipomoea recta subsp. polygaloides

Ipomoea recta subsp.

polygaloides, commonly known as the silverleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Pacific. It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue.

The plant is typically herbaceous, growing as an annual or perennial vine with hairy stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or disturbed areas.

Its flowers bloom in the early morning, hence the common name, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal.
