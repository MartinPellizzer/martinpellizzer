Indigofera bracteata

Indigofera bracteata, commonly known as the velvetleaf indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which are often clustered in racemes. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. Indigofera bracteata is often found in moist, disturbed soils and can thrive in both full sun and partial shade.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
