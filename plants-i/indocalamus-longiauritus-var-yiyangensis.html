Indocalamus longiauritus var. yiyangensis

Indocalamus longiauritus var. yiyangensis, commonly known as the Yiyang bamboo, is a variant of the long-tailed bamboo native to China.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth texture and a dark green color. This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.
