Ipomoea cymosa var. pilosa

Ipomoea cymosa var. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its varietal name "pilosa.". The plant produces trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple, opening in the morning and closing at dusk. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a garden plant.
