Ipomoea lasiophylla

Ipomoea lasiophylla, commonly known as the velvetleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant is a climbing vine with tendrils that allow it to ascend supports or other structures. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Ipomoea lasiophylla is also known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and vegetative means.
