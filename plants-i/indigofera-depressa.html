Indigofera depressa

Indigofera depressa, commonly known as the dwarf indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows as a herb or shrub, often reaching a height of less than 30 centimeters. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Indigofera depressa is also noted for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in certain ecological contexts.
