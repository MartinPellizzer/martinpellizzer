Inga adiantifolia

Inga adiantifolia, commonly known as the silver leaf tree, is a tropical species native to Central and South America.

It is a leguminous plant belonging to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and showy, spurred flowers. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are often covered in a fine silvery pubescence, giving it its common name.

The tree plays an important ecological role in forest ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
