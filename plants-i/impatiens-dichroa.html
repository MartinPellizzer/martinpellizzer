Impatiens dichroa

Impatiens dichroa, commonly known as the two-colored impatiens, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking bi-colored flowers, which typically feature a deep red or orange upper lip and a white or yellow lower lip. The plant produces oval, hairy leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Impatiens dichroa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
