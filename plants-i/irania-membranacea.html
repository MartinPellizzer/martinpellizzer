Irania membranacea

Irania membranacea, commonly known as the paperbark tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark that reveals layers of pale, papery skin. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a glossy upper surface. It produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, round fruits.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm climates for its unique appearance.
