Ixeris afghanica

Ixeris afghanica, commonly known as Afghan groundsel, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces yellow flowers with ray florets. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.

Ixeris afghanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
