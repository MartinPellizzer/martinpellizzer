Ischaemum anthephoroides f. coreanum

Ischaemum anthephoroides f. coreanum, commonly known as the Korean false brome, is a perennial grass species native to East Asia.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. The plant has slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a smooth texture. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a loose panicle, bearing small, two-lobed flowers.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
