Inga augusti

Inga augusti, commonly known as the "Brazilian bean tree," is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, clustered flowers. The plant produces long, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds, which are often used in local cuisines. Its rapid growth and nitrogen-fixing ability make it valuable for reforestation and soil enrichment.

The tree is also cultivated for its ornamental value and potential economic uses.
