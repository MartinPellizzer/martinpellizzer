Ignatia

Ignatia, commonly known as the St. Ignatius bean, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces elongated, bean-like pods that contain several seeds. Ignatia is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its delicate foliage and unique seed pods.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
