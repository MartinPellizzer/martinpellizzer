Ixeridium dentatum subsp. kimuranum

Ixeridium dentatum subsp.

kimuranum, commonly known as the Japanese daisy or Kimura's daisy, is a flowering plant native to East Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy yellow to orange composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly toothed margin, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
