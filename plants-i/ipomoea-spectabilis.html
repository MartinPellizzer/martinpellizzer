Ipomoea spectabilis

Ipomoea spectabilis, commonly known as the showy morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its large, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple, often opening in the morning. The plant is a climbing vine with heart-shaped leaves and can grow up to 10 meters in length. It thrives in warm climates and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

The flowers emit a sweet fragrance, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
