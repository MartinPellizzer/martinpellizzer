Impatiens kivuensis

Impatiens kivuensis, commonly known as the Kivu impatiens, is a flowering plant native to the eastern African region, particularly in the Democratic Republic of the Congo.

It belongs to the family Balsaminaceae and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that typically come in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
