Ipomoea concolora

Ipomoea concolora, commonly known as the scarlet morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a climbing vine that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is known for its rapid growth habit. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

The flowers bloom in the morning and close by midday, giving it its common name.
