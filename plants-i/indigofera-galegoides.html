Indigofera galegoides

Indigofera galegoides, commonly known as the slender indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, particularly in countries like Kenya and Tanzania. The plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pink to purple. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and savannas.

The plant's ability to fix nitrogen makes it an important component of soil fertility in its native ecosystems.
