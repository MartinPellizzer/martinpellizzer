Isophyllum australe

Isophyllum australe, commonly known as the Australian sunflower, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and linear leaves. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid regions, showing adaptation to dry conditions. Its structure includes a slender stem with opposite leaf arrangement.

Isophyllum australe is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging environments.
