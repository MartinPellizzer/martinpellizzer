Irvingia malayana

Irvingia malayana, commonly known as the Malayan Irvingia, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Irvingiaceae and is characterized by its woody structure and tropical habitat. The plant typically grows as a tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a rough texture, while its flowers are small and clustered.

Irvingia malayana is valued for its ecological role and has been studied for potential medicinal properties.
