Indigofera hirsutissima

Indigofera hirsutissima, commonly known as the woolly indigo, is a perennial shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, rugged appearance. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its seeds are encased in elongated pods, and the plant is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of dye.

Due to its adaptability, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a resilient species in diverse environments.
