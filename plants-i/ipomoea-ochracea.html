Ipomoea ochracea

Ipomoea ochracea, commonly known as the yellow morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a member of the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers that open in the morning and close in the evening. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are hairy and twining.

Ipomoea ochracea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
