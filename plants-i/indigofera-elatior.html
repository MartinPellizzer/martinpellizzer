Indigofera elatior

Indigofera elatior, commonly known as the blue bean or Indian blue bean, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive blue or purple flowers. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a history of use in traditional medicine. Its leaves are trifoliate, and the pods contain small, edible seeds.

Indigofera elatior thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
