Ipomoea gossypiifolia

Ipomoea gossypiifolia, commonly known as the cotton leaf or coral vine, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from red to orange and yellow. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

The flowers are typically borne in clusters and bloom throughout the growing season.
