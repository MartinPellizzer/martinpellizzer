Indigofera eriocarpa

Indigofera eriocarpa, commonly known as the woolly indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. Its seeds are encased in pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

Indigofera eriocarpa is also valued for its potential role in soil enrichment due to its nitrogen-fixing ability.
