Inga marthae

Inga marthae, commonly known as the pink inga, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers. The plant produces elongated, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in rainforests and secondary growth areas.

Inga marthae is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological systems.
