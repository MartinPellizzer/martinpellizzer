Iris pseudacorus var. acoroides

Iris pseudacorus var. acoroides, commonly known as the yellow flag iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features striking yellow flowers with a distinctive black throat, blooming from June to August. The plant has sword-shaped, grass-like leaves that are typically green with a bluish tinge. It thrives in wetland environments, such as marshes and pond edges, and is often found in shallow water or moist soil.

The species is known for its ornamental value but can also become invasive in certain regions.
