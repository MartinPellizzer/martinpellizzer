Ipomoea hildebrandtii var. mahonii

Ipomoea hildebrandtii var. mahonii, commonly known as the Mahonii morning glory, is a flowering vine native to South America.

It produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple, often with a subtle iridescence. The plant has heart-shaped, palmately lobed leaves that are typically a deep green. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers bloom in the early morning, hence its common name, and are pollinated primarily by birds and insects.
