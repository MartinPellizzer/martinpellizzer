Inga maynensis

Inga maynensis, commonly known as the Mayan inga, is a tropical tree native to Central America and parts of northern South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pendulous flowers. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its seeds are encased in a hairy pod and are dispersed by animals.

Inga maynensis plays an important ecological role in supporting biodiversity in its native habitats.
