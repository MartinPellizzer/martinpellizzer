Ipomoea stolonifera

Ipomoea stolonifera, commonly known as morning glory, is a vigorous climbing vine native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in a variety of colors, including blue, purple, and white. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in warm, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed soils.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive potential, it is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural and natural settings.
