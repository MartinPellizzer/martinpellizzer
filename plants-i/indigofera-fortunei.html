Indigofera fortunei

Indigofera fortunei, commonly known as the Chinese indigo plant, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment. It produces indigo-blue dye from its leaves, which has been historically used in textile industries.

The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for both ornamental and practical purposes.
