Iris erirrhiza

Iris erirrhiza, commonly known as the Japanese iris, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Japan.

It features striking blue to purple flowers with a distinctive three-parted structure, typical of the Iris genus. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Iris erirrhiza is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and water features.
