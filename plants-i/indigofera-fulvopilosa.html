Indigofera fulvopilosa

Indigofera fulvopilosa, commonly known as the golden pilose indigo, is a species of legume native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, trifoliate leaves and small, fragrant flowers that range in color from pink to violet. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both ecological studies and ornamental horticulture.
