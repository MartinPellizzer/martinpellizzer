Indigofera bifrons

Indigofera bifrons, commonly known as the small indigo or Indian blue bean, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to regions of Asia, Africa, and Australia, and is characterized by its small, trifoliate leaves and clusters of blue to violet pea-like flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often grown for its ornamental value and has been used traditionally in various cultures for medicinal and dye purposes.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it beneficial in soil enrichment and agricultural settings.
