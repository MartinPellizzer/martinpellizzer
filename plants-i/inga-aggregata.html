Inga aggregata

Inga aggregata, commonly known as the "wild coffee tree," is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is notable for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it a common pioneer species in disturbed areas. Its flowers are typically yellow or greenish-yellow, and it produces edible pods that contain seeds.

Inga aggregata is also valued for its use in agroforestry systems due to its nitrogen-fixing capabilities.
