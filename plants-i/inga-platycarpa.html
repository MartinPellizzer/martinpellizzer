Inga platycarpa

Inga platycarpa, commonly known as the flat-fruit inga, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Central and South America, where it typically grows in humid, shaded forest environments. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often divided into several pairs of elongated, pointed leaflets. Its small, clustered flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces pods that contain edible seeds.

Inga platycarpa is valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and as a food source for various wildlife species.
