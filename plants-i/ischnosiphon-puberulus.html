Ischnosiphon puberulus

Ischnosiphon puberulus, commonly known as the hairy spathiphyllum, is a member of the Araceae family, characterized by its unique floral structure.

It features a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe, which is typically hairy, giving the plant its common name. Native to tropical regions, it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant has small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered on the spadix, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its foliage is usually dark green with a velvety texture, contributing to its aesthetic appeal.
