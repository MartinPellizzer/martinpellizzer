Ischaemum plumulosum

Ischaemum plumulosum, commonly known as the silky grass or slender silkygrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist grasslands, meadows, and along stream banks, preferring well-drained soils. The plant has slender, erect stems that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft, fluffy appearance. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually greenish-white and arranged in dense clusters at the tips of the stems.

Ischaemum plumulosum is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense stands, making it both a useful forage plant and a potential weed in agricultural settings.
