Iryanthera ulei

Iryanthera ulei, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental species.

Its unique appearance and seasonal blooming make it a notable botanical specimen in tropical landscapes.
