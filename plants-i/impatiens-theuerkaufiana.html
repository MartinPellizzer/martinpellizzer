Impatiens theuerkaufiana

Impatiens theuerkaufiana, commonly known as the Schomburgk's impatiens, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant features heart-shaped, glossy leaves and a compact growth habit, making it suitable for both garden beds and container planting. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, preferring humid environments.

Impatiens theuerkaufiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its ability to add color to shaded areas.
