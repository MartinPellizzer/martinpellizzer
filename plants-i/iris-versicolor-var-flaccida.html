Iris versicolor var. flaccida

Iris versicolor var. flaccida, commonly known as the soft-flowered iris, is a variant of the blue flag iris native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its pale blue to white flowers with a distinctive yellowish-green throat. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and has narrow, grass-like leaves. It produces showy, drooping flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
