Impatiens mendoncae

Impatiens mendoncae, commonly known as Mendo's Impatiens, is a species of flowering plant native to the United States, specifically found in California.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 feet, featuring upright stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, white, and red, which bloom from late spring to early summer.

It prefers partial to full shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens and shaded landscapes.
