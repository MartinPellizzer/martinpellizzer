Isodon

Isodon, commonly known as the horse mint or wild thyme, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Lamiaceae.

These plants are characterized by their aromatic leaves and showy, often blue or purple, two-lipped flowers. Many species have creeping or upright growth habits, and they often thrive in well-drained soils.

The genus includes several species, such as Isodon rotundifolius and Isodon japonicus, which are valued for their ornamental and medicinal properties.
