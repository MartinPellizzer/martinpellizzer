Intsia quanzensis

Intsia quanzensis, commonly known as the Chinese ironwood, is a deciduous tree native to southern China and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark brown wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The tree has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by flat, winged fruits that aid in seed dispersal.

Intsia quanzensis is often found in lowland forests and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
