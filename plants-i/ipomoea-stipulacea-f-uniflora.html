Ipomoea stipulacea f. uniflora

Ipomoea stipulacea f. uniflora, commonly known as the single-flower morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a herbaceous annual with distinctive heart-shaped leaves and a trailing growth habit. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, blooming in the morning. Its flowers are pollinated by birds and insects, and it thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil.

The species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant.
