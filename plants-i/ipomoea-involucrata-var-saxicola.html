Ipomoea involucrata var. saxicola

Ipomoea involucrata var. saxicola, commonly known as the sandhill morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows in sandy or dry soil conditions. The plant produces striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in the morning, hence its common name. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often covered in fine hairs.

The species is adapted to thrive in open, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.
