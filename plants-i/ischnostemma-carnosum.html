Ischnostemma carnosum

Ischnostemma carnosum, commonly known as the fleshy-stemmed bellflower, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its unique morphology and ornamental value, it is cultivated in some botanical gardens.
