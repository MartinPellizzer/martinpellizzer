Inga affinis

Inga affinis, commonly known as the "silk tree" or "chocolate tree," is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of the Americas, including Central and South America. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves, which are usually trifoliate, and its showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. Inga affinis has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, which helps enrich the soil.

It is also valued for its edible fruits and its role in agroforestry systems.
