Ilex walkeri var. emarginella

Ilex walkeri var. emarginella, commonly known as the emarginate holly, is a variant of the holly genus native to parts of East Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that often have a distinctive notched or emarginate tip. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, thriving in well-drained soils and partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural use in gardens and landscapes.
