Indigofera suffruticosa subsp. guatimalensis

Indigofera suffruticosa subsp.

guatimalensis, commonly known as Mexican indigo, is a shrub-like plant native to Central America and parts of Mexico. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant produces seed pods that contain small, kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Its historical use in dye production and traditional medicine highlights its ecological and cultural significance.
