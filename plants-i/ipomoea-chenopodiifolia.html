Ipomoea chenopodiifolia

Ipomoea chenopodiifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, silver-veined leaves and trumpet-shaped, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant is a trailing vine with hairy stems and produces capsule fruits containing numerous small, dark seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a garden plant, though it can become invasive in certain environments.
