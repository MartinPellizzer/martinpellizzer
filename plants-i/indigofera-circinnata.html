Indigofera circinnata

Indigofera circinnata, commonly known as the African indigo or wild indigo, is a flowering plant native to sub-Saharan Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to violet, often arranged in clusters. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small, oval-shaped leaflets.

The plant is notable for its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments, making it an important species in ecological and agricultural contexts.
