Ipomoea magnusiana var. eenii

Ipomoea magnusiana var. eenii, commonly known as the pink morning glory, is a climbing vine native to Central America.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces striking pink to purple flowers that open in the morning. The flowers have a spurred structure at the base of the petals, a characteristic feature of the Convolvulaceae family. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
