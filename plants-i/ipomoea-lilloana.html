Ipomoea lilloana

Ipomoea lilloana, commonly known as the Lillo's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces vibrant blue to violet flowers that open in the morning and close by midday. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its vines can grow up to several meters in length.

Ipomoea lilloana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to a variety of soil types.
