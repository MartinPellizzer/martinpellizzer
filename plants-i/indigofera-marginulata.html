Indigofera marginulata

Indigofera marginulata, commonly known as the marginate indigo.

It is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, Africa, and the Pacific Islands. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated margins and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches the soil.

The plant is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and blooms.
