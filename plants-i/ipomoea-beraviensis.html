Ipomoea beraviensis

Ipomoea beraviensis, commonly known as the Beraviensis morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in the morning, giving it its common name. The plant is a climbing vine with heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped blooms. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Ipomoea beraviensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
