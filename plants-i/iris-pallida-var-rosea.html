Iris pallida var. rosea

Iris pallida var. rosea, commonly known as the pale iris with rose-colored flowers, is a variant of the pale iris species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and its striking, pale blue to lavender-colored flowers with a distinctive pinkish or rose-colored throat. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or meadow habitats. It blooms in late spring to early summer, producing showy, upright flower stalks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.
