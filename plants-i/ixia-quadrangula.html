Ixia quadrangula

Ixia quadrangula, commonly known as the four-angled lily, is a striking South African plant characterized by its vibrant orange or red flowers.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is native to the Western and Northern Cape provinces. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped blooms. Ixia quadrangula thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
