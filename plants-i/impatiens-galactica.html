Impatiens galactica

Impatiens galactica, commonly known as the Milky Impatiens, is a species of flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking white to pale yellow flowers and heart-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are borne in clusters and attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
