Impatiens oblongata

Impatiens oblongata, commonly known as the oblong leaf impatiens, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Balsaminaceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, spurred flowers. The leaves are typically ovate to oblong in shape, with a glossy surface and a distinct midrib. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
