Isiphia tomentosa

Isiphia tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly isiphia, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a shrub characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped and covered in a thick layer of fine, white hairs.

Isiphia tomentosa is adapted to arid and rocky environments, making it a resilient species found in coastal and mountainous regions.
