Inga arinensis

Inga arinensis, commonly known as the yellow inga, is a tropical tree belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is native to Central and South America, particularly found in regions of Colombia, Ecuador, and Peru. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruit is a pod containing edible seeds, which are often used in local cuisines.

Inga arinensis also plays an important ecological role by fixing nitrogen in the soil, supporting biodiversity in its native habitats.
