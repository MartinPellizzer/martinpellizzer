Ibatia schreiteri

Ibatia schreiteri, commonly known as the Schreiter's ibatia, is a species of flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, which bloom during the summer months. Its fruits are small, spherical berries that turn red when mature, containing several seeds.

Ibatia schreiteri is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional medicine.
