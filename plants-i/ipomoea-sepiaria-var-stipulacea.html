Ipomoea sepiaria var. stipulacea

Ipomoea sepiaria var. stipulacea, commonly known as the silverleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive silvery or white underside, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pale blue to violet in color. It is a climbing vine that thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a popular ornamental plant, though it can become invasive in certain environments.
