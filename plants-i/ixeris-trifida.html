Ixeris trifida

Ixeris trifida, commonly known as the three-lobed groundsel, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with a mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are pinnately lobed and often have a fuzzy texture.

Ixeris trifida is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in rocky or disturbed soils.
