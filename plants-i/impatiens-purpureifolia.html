Impatiens purpureifolia

Impatiens purpureifolia, commonly known as the purple-leaved impatiens, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple foliage, which contrasts beautifully with its clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 feet, and thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its flowers bloom in late summer and early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of garden settings.
