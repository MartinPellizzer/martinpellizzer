Iris virginica

Iris virginica, commonly known as the swamp iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams. The plant features tall, slender stems that bear showy, violet-blue flowers with three upright petals and three downward-curving sepals. Its leaves are sword-shaped and often have a bluish-green hue.

Iris virginica plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
