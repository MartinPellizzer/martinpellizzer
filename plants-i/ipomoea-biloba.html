Ipomoea biloba

Ipomoea biloba, commonly known as the morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its trumpet-shaped flowers that open in the morning and close in the evening. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces vibrant blue, purple, or white blooms. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Ipomoea biloba is also known for its rapid growth and climbing habit, making it a popular choice for trellises and gardens.
