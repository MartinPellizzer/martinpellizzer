Iris florentina var. albicans

Iris florentina var. albicans, commonly known as the white floodplain iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the wetlands and floodplains of the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has strap-like, grassy leaves that form a dense basal rosette, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in riparian zones and is adapted to periodic flooding.

The white floodplain iris is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
