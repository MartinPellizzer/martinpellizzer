Ixoca phyllitica

Ixoca phyllitica, commonly known as the golden tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and large, pinnate leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, sour fruits that are similar in taste to tamarind. Its botanical features include a shallow root system and the ability to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.

Ixoca phyllitica is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in local ecosystems.
