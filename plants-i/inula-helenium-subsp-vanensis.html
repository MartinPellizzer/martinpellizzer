Inula helenium subsp. vanensis

Inula helenium subsp.

vanensis, commonly known as the Vanese elecampane, is a perennial herb native to parts of Central Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough, hairy texture.

It is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal medicine.
