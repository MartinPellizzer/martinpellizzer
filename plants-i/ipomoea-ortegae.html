Ipomoea ortegae

Ipomoea ortegae, commonly known as the Ortegae morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves and has a climbing habit, often found growing along fences or trees. It blooms during the day, particularly in the morning, hence its common name.

The flowers emit a sweet fragrance, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
