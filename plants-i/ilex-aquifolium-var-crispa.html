Ilex aquifolium var. crispa

Ilex aquifolium var. crispa, commonly known as the crinkled holly, is a variety of the common holly characterized by its distinctive, wavy, and crinkled leaves.

It is an evergreen shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of western Asia. The leaves are typically dark green on the upper surface and lighter green underneath, with spiny margins. It produces small, red berries that are attractive to birds.

This plant thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
