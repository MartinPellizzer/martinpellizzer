Ipomoea ampullacea

Ipomoea ampullacea, commonly known as the giant morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple, often with a striking contrast between the petals and the vibrant yellow throat. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves that are typically large and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found growing along riverbanks or in disturbed soils.

Ipomoea ampullacea is a climbing vine that can reach impressive heights, making it a notable species in botanical gardens and natural habitats.
