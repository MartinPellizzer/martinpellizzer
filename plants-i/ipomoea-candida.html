Ipomoea candida

Ipomoea candida, commonly known as the white morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, showy white or pale pink flowers that open in the morning and close in the evening. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces hairy, cylindrical seed pods that contain numerous small, flat seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found growing along riverbanks and in disturbed soils.

Ipomoea candida is also known for its rapid growth and can become invasive in certain environments.
