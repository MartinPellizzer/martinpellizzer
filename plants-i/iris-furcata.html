Iris furcata

Iris furcata, commonly known as the forked iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features striking blue to purple flowers with a distinctive three-lobed beak, which gives it its common name. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that form a rosette at the base. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in alpine or rocky habitats.

Iris furcata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
