Ida barringtoniae

Ida barringtoniae, commonly known as the Barringtonia tree, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The tree has a distinctive, spiky fruit that splits open to release its seeds when ripe. Its leaves are typically large and glossy, and it can grow to be a tall, straight tree in its natural habitat.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental beauty and has cultural significance in some regions.
