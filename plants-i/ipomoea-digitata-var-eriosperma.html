Ipomoea digitata var. eriosperma

Ipomoea digitata var. eriosperma, commonly known as the woolly morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. Its flowers open in the morning and close by midday, hence the common name.

The species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
