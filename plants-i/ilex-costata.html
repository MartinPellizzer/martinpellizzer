Ilex costata

Ilex costata, commonly known as the Japanese holly, is a evergreen shrub or small tree native to Japan and Korea.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and dark green, glossy leaves that are often adorned with white or yellow veins. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans and livestock. Ilex costata thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Its hardy nature and attractive appearance make it a favored plant in gardens and natural habitats.
