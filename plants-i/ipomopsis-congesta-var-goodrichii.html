Ipomopsis congesta var. goodrichii

Ipomopsis congesta var. goodrichii, commonly known as the Goodrich's clustered trumpetflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant purple to reddish-pink tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and open habitats, such as scrublands and canyon walls. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often arranged in opposite pairs along the stems.

The species is part of the evening primrose family (Onagraceae) and is known for its showy, nectar-rich flowers that attract pollinators like hummingbirds and bees.
