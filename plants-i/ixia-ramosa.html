Ixia ramosa

Ixia ramosa, commonly known as the red-hot poker, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, spike-like inflorescence composed of numerous brightly colored flowers. The flowers are typically orange-red in hue, with a distinctive central yellow style that resembles a poker, giving the plant its common name. Ixia ramosa thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

It is known for its long blooming period, usually from late spring to early summer.
