Ipomoea congestiflora

Ipomoea congestiflora, commonly known as the clustered morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a vine that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and thrives in warm, humid climates. It blooms throughout the growing season, often from spring to early autumn.

Ipomoea congestiflora is known for its rapid growth and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
