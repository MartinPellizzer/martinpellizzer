Iris longipetala

Iris longipetala, commonly known as the long-petioled iris, is a species native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces striking blue to violet flowers with three upright petals and three drooping sepals. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming a dense rosette at the base.

Iris longipetala thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.
