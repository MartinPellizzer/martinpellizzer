Inula cappa f. radiata

Inula cappa f. radiata, commonly known as the radiate inula, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange composite flowers that resemble a crown, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and ecological value to pollinators.
