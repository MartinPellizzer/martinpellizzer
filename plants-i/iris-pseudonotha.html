Iris pseudonotha

Iris pseudonotha, commonly known as the New Zealand iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its upright, sword-shaped leaves and showy blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in forested or coastal areas. It produces a tall flowering stem with three to five flowers, each with three petals and three sepals.

Iris pseudonotha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
