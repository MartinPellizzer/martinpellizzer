Ipomoea albiflora

Ipomoea albiflora, commonly known as the white morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy white flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant is a vigorous climber, often found trailing along fences or trellises. Its flowers emit a mild fragrance and are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects.

Ipomoea albiflora is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
