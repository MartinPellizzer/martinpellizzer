Inga cataractae

Inga cataractae, commonly known as the water inga, is a tropical tree species native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically opposite and have a velvety texture. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by elongated pods containing several seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found near rivers or in humid forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
