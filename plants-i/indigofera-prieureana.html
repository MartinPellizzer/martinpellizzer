Indigofera prieureana

Indigofera prieureana, commonly known as the Madagascar indigo or Prieure's indigo, is a shrub native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable in ecological and agricultural contexts. It produces pods containing seeds that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its natural distribution.

Its ornamental value and medicinal properties have also made it of interest in horticulture and traditional medicine.
