Isodon henryi var. dichromophyllus

Isodon henryi var. dichromophyllus, commonly known as the two-colored honeysuckle, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its distinctive leaves that exhibit two different colors on their upper and lower surfaces, a feature that gives it its scientific name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial shade to full sun.

Its unique botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and botanical research.
