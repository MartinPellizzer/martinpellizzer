Ipomoea courchetii

Ipomoea courchetii, commonly known as the Mexican morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It is a climbing vine that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of violet, blue, and white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is known for its rapid growth and ability to climb various structures. It thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Ipomoea courchetii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
