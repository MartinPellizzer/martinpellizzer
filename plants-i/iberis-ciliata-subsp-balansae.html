Iberis ciliata subsp. balansae

Iberis ciliata subsp.

balansae, commonly known as the Balansae candytuft, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cress plants.

The plant is characterized by its low-growing habit, with dense clusters of white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are hairy and serrated, and the stems are often covered in fine pubescence.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.
