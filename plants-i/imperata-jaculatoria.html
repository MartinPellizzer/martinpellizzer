Imperata jaculatoria

Imperata jaculatoria, commonly known as the sacred bamboo or red bamboo, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features tall, slender culms with a reddish hue, often reaching heights of up to 3 meters. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically pink to red in color. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread rapidly, forming dense mats that can dominate disturbed or degraded soils.

Imperata jaculatoria is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine and as a source of thatching in some cultures.
