Ixia micrandra

Ixia micrandra, commonly known as the Cape goldcup, is a striking South African flowering plant belonging to the Iridaceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow to orange flowers, which are often adorned with dark purple or red markings. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are evergreen, providing visual interest throughout the year.

Ixia micrandra is also notable for its use in horticulture and as a cut flower due to its long-lasting blooms.
