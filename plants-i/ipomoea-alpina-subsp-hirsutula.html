Ipomoea alpina subsp. hirsutula

Ipomoea alpina subsp.

hirsutula, commonly known as the hairy morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains. It is a herbaceous perennial that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white.

The subspecies is distinguished by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

The plant is cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
