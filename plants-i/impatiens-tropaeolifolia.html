Impatiens tropaeolifolia

Impatiens tropaeolifolia, commonly known as the tropical impatiens, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, heart-shaped leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers that come in a range of colors including white, pink, and red. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, often reaching heights of 30 to 60 centimeters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shady garden beds.

Its ornamental value is further enhanced by its ability to produce numerous blooms throughout the growing season.
