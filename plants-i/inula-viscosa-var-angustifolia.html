Inula viscosa var. angustifolia

Inula viscosa var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf elecampane, is a perennial herb native to parts of Asia and Europe.

It features a robust, upright stem that can grow up to 1.5 meters tall, covered with white, woolly hairs. The plant produces yellow composite flowers in late summer, arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a serrated margin and a hairy surface.

This variety is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.
