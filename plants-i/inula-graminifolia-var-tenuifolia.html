Inula graminifolia var. tenuifolia

Inula graminifolia var. tenuifolia, commonly known as slenderleaf elecampane, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a tall, erect stem covered with rough, hairy leaves that are narrow and linear in shape. The plant produces yellow composite flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ecologically valuable species in its native habitats.
