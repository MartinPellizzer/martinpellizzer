Ixeris sonchifolia

Ixeris sonchifolia, commonly known as the common groundsel, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of oblanceolate leaves with serrated margins and a hairy surface. The plant produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in the summer months, attracting various pollinators. It is native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa, and is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats.

Ixeris sonchifolia is an annual plant that can spread through seeds and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
