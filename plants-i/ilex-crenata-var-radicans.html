Ilex crenata var. radicans

Ilex crenata var. radicans, commonly known as Japanese holly or creeping holly, is a dense, low-growing evergreen shrub native to Japan.

It features small, oval, dark green leaves with wavy edges that provide a textured appearance. The plant produces small, red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it ideal for ground cover in shaded garden areas.

Its compact growth habit and year-round foliage make it a popular choice for landscaping.
