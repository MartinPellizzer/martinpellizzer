Ipomoea prismatosyphon var. trifida

Ipomoea prismatosyphon var. trifida, commonly known as the tripartite morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive trifid leaves, which are divided into three distinct lobes, and its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of blue and purple. The plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and abandoned fields.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the Convolvulaceae family.
