Indigofera malacostachys var. macrura

Indigofera malacostachys var. macrura, commonly known as the giant blue sage, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in dense, cylindrical spikes. The plant has compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets and is often grown for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for warm climates.

Its unique appearance and vibrant color make it a standout in both cultivated and natural settings.
