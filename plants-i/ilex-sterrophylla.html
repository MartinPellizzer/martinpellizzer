Ilex sterrophylla

Ilex sterrophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved holly, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter on the underside. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for various birds and wildlife. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and moist habitats.

Ilex sterrophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native landscapes.
