Ixia columellaris

Ixia columellaris, commonly known as the yellow rain lily, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features strap-shaped leaves and produces seed pods that split open to release seeds. Ixia columellaris is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
