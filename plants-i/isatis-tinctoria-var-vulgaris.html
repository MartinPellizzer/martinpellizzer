Isatis tinctoria var. vulgaris

Isatis tinctoria var. vulgaris, commonly known as the common woad, is a biennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant is known for its historical use in producing indigo dye, which was highly valued in ancient civilizations. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Its medicinal properties have also been studied, with historical use in treating ailments like the common cold.
