Iris laevigata var. alba

Iris laevigata var. alba, commonly known as the white flag iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear showy, white to pale blue flowers with a distinctive yellow center. The leaves are sword-shaped, smooth, and often have a waxy texture. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for wetland gardens.

This variety is noted for its elegant appearance and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
