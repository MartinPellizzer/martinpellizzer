Ixora diversifolia var. diversifolia

Ixora diversifolia var. diversifolia, commonly known as the flame-of-the-forest, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it its striking appearance. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually ovate to elliptical in shape. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant.

The species is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dyes.
