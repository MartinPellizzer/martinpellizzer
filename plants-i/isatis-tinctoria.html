Isatis tinctoria

Isatis tinctoria, commonly known as the woad plant, is a biennial herb native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant is notable for its blue dye-producing properties, historically used in textile industries. Its roots and leaves are also used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Isatis tinctoria thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it adaptable to various climates.
