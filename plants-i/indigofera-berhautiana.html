Indigofera berhautiana

Indigofera berhautiana, commonly known as the African indigo or African hibiscus, is a flowering plant species native to tropical Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its woody stems and palmately compound leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, which bloom in clusters. It is often found in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Indigofera berhautiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
