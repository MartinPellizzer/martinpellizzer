Indigofera malindiensis

Indigofera malindiensis, commonly known as Malindi's indigo, is a flowering plant native to East Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain seeds, and it is often found in coastal and sandy soils.

It is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
