Ixora trichocalyx

Ixora trichocalyx, commonly known as the hairy ixora, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 2 meters, featuring dense, glossy green leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, bright orange-red flowers that bloom throughout the year, making it a popular ornamental species. It thrives in tropical climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Ixora trichocalyx is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
