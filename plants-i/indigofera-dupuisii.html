Indigofera dupuisii

Indigofera dupuisii, commonly known as the purple wisteria, is a climbing legume native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has trifoliate leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits containing seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ornamental and ecological purposes.
