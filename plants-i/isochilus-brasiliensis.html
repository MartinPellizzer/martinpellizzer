Isochilus brasiliensis

Isochilus brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its terrestrial growth habit and intricate floral structures. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically greenish-white with a distinctive lip petal. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
