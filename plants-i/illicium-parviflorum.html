Illicium parviflorum

Illicium parviflorum, commonly known as the small-flowered star anise, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, star-shaped flowers and aromatic, spice-like fruits. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth texture. Its fruit, which resembles a star anise, contains essential oils that contribute to its medicinal and culinary uses.

Illicium parviflorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in traditional herbal medicine.
