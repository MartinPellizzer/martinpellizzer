Isnardia pilosa

Isnardia pilosa, commonly known as the hairy sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy, gray-green foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal regions.

Isnardia pilosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate drought conditions.
