Isachne pangerangensis var. firmula

Isachne pangerangensis var. firmula, commonly known as the Pangerangan grass, is a flowering plant species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is typically found in moist, tropical environments such as wetlands and lowland areas.

Its botanical significance lies in its role within local ecosystems and its potential use in ecological restoration projects.
