Ipomoea austrobrasiliensis

Ipomoea austrobrasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian morning glory, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types. It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, where it can become invasive.

Botanically, it belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is known for its distinctive seed pods and spiral-shaped tendrils.
