Indigofera laxa

Indigofera laxa, commonly known as the slender indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia, often found in moist, open habitats such as grasslands and forest edges. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves with small, rounded leaflets. Its flowers are typically blue or purple, arranged in racemes, and are adapted for pollination by insects.

Indigofera laxa is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of dye.
