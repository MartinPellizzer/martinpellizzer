Ixia rapunculoides var. flaccida

Ixia rapunculoides var. flaccida, commonly known as the flaccid lily, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family, which includes many ornamental plants with showy flowers. The plant features striking, trumpet-shaped blooms that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a distinctive purple or red throat. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

The foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
