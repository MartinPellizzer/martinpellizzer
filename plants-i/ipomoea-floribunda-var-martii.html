Ipomoea floribunda var. martii

Ipomoea floribunda var. martii, commonly known as the Mexican morning glory, is a climbing vine native to Mexico and Central America.

It features heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. The plant is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to twine around supports, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The flowers bloom throughout the growing season, adding vibrant color to landscapes.
