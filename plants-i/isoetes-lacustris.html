Isoetes lacustris

Isoetes lacustris, commonly known as the water clubmoss, is a member of the Isoetaceae family and is characterized by its unique, spirally arranged leaves.

It is a perennial plant that typically grows in freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving streams. The plant has a rhizome that anchors it to the substrate, and its spore-producing structures are borne on the stems. Isoetes lacustris is notable for its ability to thrive in oxygenated water and is often found in shallow, still waters.

Its morphology and ecological role make it an important indicator species for aquatic ecosystems.
