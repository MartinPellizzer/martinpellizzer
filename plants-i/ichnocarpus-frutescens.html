Ichnocarpus frutescens

Ichnocarpus frutescens, commonly known as the African milk tree, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Leguminosae and is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by distinctive, flat, pod-like fruits. It is known for its milky sap, which can be toxic and is used in traditional medicine.

The plant is also valued for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
