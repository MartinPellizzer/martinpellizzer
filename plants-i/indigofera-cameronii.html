Indigofera cameronii

Indigofera cameronii, commonly known as Cameron's indigo, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and forested areas.

Its ornamental value has led to its cultivation in some horticultural settings.
