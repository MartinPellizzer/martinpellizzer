Ipomoea debeerstii

Ipomoea debeerstii, commonly known as the golden trumpet vine, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant is a vigorous climber with heart-shaped leaves and produces long, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The vine can grow up to 10 meters in length, making it a popular choice for trellises and vertical gardening.
