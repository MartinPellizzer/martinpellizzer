Indigofera pseudocompressa

Indigofera pseudocompressa, commonly known as the slender indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces elongated pods that contain several seeds, which are often dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ornamental and ecological purposes.
