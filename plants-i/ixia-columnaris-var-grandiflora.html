Ixia columnaris var. grandiflora

Ixia columnaris var. grandiflora, commonly known as the African daffodil, is a striking bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It features tall, slender stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height, adorned with large, showy flowers in shades of yellow to orange. The flowers are typically trumpet-shaped with a distinctive, recurved lip, and they bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

It is also noted for its ability to naturalize and return year after year in suitable climates.
