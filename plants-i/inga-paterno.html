Inga paterno

Inga paterno, commonly known as the "father's inga," is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features trifoliate leaves with three oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white. Its fruit is a legume pod that splits open to release seeds, contributing to its role in ecological succession.

Inga paterno is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches the soil in its native habitats.
