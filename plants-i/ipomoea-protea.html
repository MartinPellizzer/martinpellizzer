Ipomoea protea

Ipomoea protea, commonly known as the African morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant has heart-shaped, broad leaves and is typically found in grassland and savanna habitats. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, smooth seeds.

Ipomoea protea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
