Iris odaesanensis f. purpurascens

Iris odaesanensis f. purpurascens, commonly known as the purple-flowered Japanese iris, is a botanical variant of the Japanese iris species.

It is characterized by its striking violet to deep purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is native to Japan and thrives in moist, well-drained soils, typically in woodland or streamside habitats. Its foliage consists of sword-shaped leaves that are a deep green, adding visual interest throughout the growing season.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
