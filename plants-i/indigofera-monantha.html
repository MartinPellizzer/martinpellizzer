Indigofera monantha

Indigofera monantha, commonly known as the white indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, fragrant white or pale pink flowers. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil. It typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters.

Its seeds are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of dye.
