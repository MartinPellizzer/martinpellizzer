Impatiens teysmannii

Impatiens teysmannii, commonly known as the Teyssmann's impatiens, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous species that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. The plant features showy, spurred flowers that are usually pink or white in color, with five petals and a distinctive nectar spur. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Impatiens teysmannii is valued in horticulture for its vibrant blooms and adaptability to various garden conditions.
