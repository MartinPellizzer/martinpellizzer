Iria aestivalis

Iria aestivalis, commonly known as the summer iris, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like. Iria aestivalis prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable addition to gardens and natural habitats.

It is also known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its beauty.
