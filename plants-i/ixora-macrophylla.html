Ixora macrophylla

Ixora macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaved flame flower, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered red or orange flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves that are often glossy and can grow quite large. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun.

Ixora macrophylla is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a hedge plant.
