Ipomoea modesta

Ipomoea modesta, commonly known as the modest morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring heart-shaped leaves and showy blue to purple flowers. The plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in disturbed soils along roadsides and in open woods. Its seeds are dispersed by animals and can remain viable in the soil for several years.

Ipomoea modesta is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
