Isoglossa collina

Isoglossa collina, commonly known as the mountain bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations, often in alpine or subalpine environments. The plant features a basal rosette of lanceolate leaves and produces showy blue to violet flowers with five petals. Its flowers are usually arranged in a raceme and bloom during the summer months.

Isoglossa collina is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
