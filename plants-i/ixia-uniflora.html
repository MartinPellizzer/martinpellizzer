Ixia uniflora

Ixia uniflora, commonly known as the single-flower ixia, is a striking member of the Amaryllis family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems that bear a single, showy flower at the top, often in shades of yellow, orange, or red. The plant produces small, green, egg-shaped bulbs that store energy for future growth. Ixia uniflora thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens in temperate climates.

Its unique appearance and long-lasting blooms make it a prized ornamental plant.
