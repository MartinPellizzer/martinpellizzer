Ipomeria inconspicua

Ipomeria inconspicua, commonly known as the smallleaf cupplant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, silver-gray leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in clustered heads, which are surrounded by pale green bracts. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and rocky slopes.

Ipomeria inconspicua is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators and wildlife.
