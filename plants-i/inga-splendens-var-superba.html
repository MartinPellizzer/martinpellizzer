Inga splendens var. superba

Inga splendens var. superba, commonly known as the golden inga, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves that are often a vibrant green with a glossy appearance. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange, which attract a variety of pollinators. Its fruit is a pod containing several seeds, and the tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.

The plant thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in rainforests and secondary growth areas.
