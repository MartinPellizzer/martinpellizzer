Ischaemum sinense

Ischaemum sinense, commonly known as Chinese goatgrass, is a species of annual grass native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences with two to three bracts, and its flowers are typically wind-pollinated. Ischaemum sinense is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in agricultural fields and along roadsides.
