Inga afzelioides

Inga afzelioides, commonly known as the African snow pea, is a tropical leguminous plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically trifoliate, and its showy, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is native to regions across Africa and parts of Asia.

Inga afzelioides is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important plant in agricultural systems.
