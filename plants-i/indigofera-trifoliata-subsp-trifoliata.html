Indigofera trifoliata subsp. trifoliata

Indigofera trifoliata subsp.

trifoliata, commonly known as the three-leaved indigo plant, is a member of the legume family Fabaceae. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged symmetrically.

The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a distinctive yellow or orange stipple. It is native to parts of Asia and is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses.

The species is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial in soil enrichment.
