Indigofera asperifolia var. lanceolata

Indigofera asperifolia var. lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceolate indigofera, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia and is often found in moist, shaded environments. It has a woody stem and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
