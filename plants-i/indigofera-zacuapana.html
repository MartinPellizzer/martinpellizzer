Indigofera zacuapana

Indigofera zacuapana, commonly known as the Zacuapana indigo plant, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, pea-like flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and conservation efforts.
