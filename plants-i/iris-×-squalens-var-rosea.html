Iris × squalens var. rosea

Iris × squalens var. rosea, commonly known as the rose-flowered bearded iris, is a hybrid plant that combines traits from both Iris squalens and other bearded iris species.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers with a distinctive rose-colored hue, often featuring a deep purple or violet center. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 3 feet, with sword-shaped, grass-like leaves that are dark green and slightly hairy. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Botanically, it is a member of the Iridaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
