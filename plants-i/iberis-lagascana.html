Iberis lagascana

Iberis lagascana, commonly known as the Spanish rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Its compact growth habit and hardy nature make it a suitable choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
