Ipomoea eriocephala

Ipomoea eriocephala, commonly known as the woolly morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. It thrives in warm climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Ipomoea eriocephala is a member of the Convolvulaceae family, which includes many other flowering vines and herbs.
