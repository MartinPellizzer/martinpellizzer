Impatiens bonii

Impatiens bonii, commonly known as the golden crown impatiens, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a contrasting red or orange calyx. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs, giving them a soft texture.

Impatiens bonii thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
