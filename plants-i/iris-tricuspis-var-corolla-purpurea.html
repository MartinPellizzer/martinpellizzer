Iris tricuspis var. corolla-purpurea

Iris tricuspis var. corolla-purpurea, commonly known as the purple corolla iris, is a striking ornamental plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, violet-blue to purple flowers with a distinctive three-lobed corolla, which gives it its scientific name. The plant has tall, slender stems that bear upright, sword-shaped leaves, typical of the iris family. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Its unique floral structure and vibrant color make it a prized addition to botanical collections.
