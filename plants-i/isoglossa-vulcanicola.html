Isoglossa vulcanicola

Isoglossa vulcanicola, commonly known as the volcanic shield plant, is a unique species adapted to thrive in volcanic soils.

It possesses thick, waxy leaves that help retain moisture in its arid, high-altitude habitat. The plant's root system is extensive, allowing it to anchor firmly in loose, nutrient-poor volcanic ash. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, often displaying shades of yellow and orange.

Isoglossa vulcanicola plays a vital role in stabilizing volcanic slopes and preventing erosion.
