Ipomoea gabrielii

Ipomoea gabrielii, commonly known as Gabriel's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of violet and blue. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It typically blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its rapid growth and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardens and natural habitats.
