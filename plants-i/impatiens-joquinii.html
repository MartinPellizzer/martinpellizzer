Impatiens joquinii

Impatiens joquinii, commonly known as the Joquin's impatiens, is a species of flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of western Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments. The plant features showy, spurred flowers that are typically pink or white in color, with five petals and a distinctive lower petal that curves backward. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

Impatiens joquinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate, colorful blooms.
