Inga amazonica var. membranacea

Inga amazonica var. membranacea, commonly known as the Amazonian inga, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pendulous flowers. The plant is typically found in humid lowland rainforests, where it thrives in the dense canopy layer. It plays an important ecological role by fixing nitrogen in the soil, contributing to nutrient cycling in the ecosystem.

Its fruit pods contain edible seeds, which are dispersed by animals, aiding in its propagation.
