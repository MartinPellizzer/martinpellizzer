Impatiens textorii f. pallescens

Impatiens textorii f. pallescens, commonly known as the pale impatiens, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, pale pink to white flowers. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a smooth texture and a pale green color. This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant due to its delicate appearance.

It prefers moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for shaded garden beds or indoor environments.
