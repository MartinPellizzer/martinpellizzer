Ipomoea triloba f. trichocarpa

Ipomoea triloba f. trichocarpa, commonly known as the hairy leaf morning glory, is a variant of the morning glory family (Convolvulaceae).

It is characterized by its distinctive, hairy leaves and trumpet-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of purple or white. The plant is native to the southeastern United States and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is a climbing vine that can reach lengths of several meters, using its tendrils to support itself.

Botanically, it is noted for its complex inflorescence and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
