Inga bracteifera

Inga bracteifera, commonly known as the yellow inga, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically alternate and composed of several leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by elongated pods containing edible seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential ecological benefits in reforestation projects.
