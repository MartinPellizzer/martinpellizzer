Ipomoea perplexa

Ipomoea perplexa, commonly known as the perplexing morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of violet and white. The plant is a vigorous climber with heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, often fragrant blooms. It thrives in warm, sunny conditions and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

However, due to its invasive potential, it requires careful management in natural habitats.
