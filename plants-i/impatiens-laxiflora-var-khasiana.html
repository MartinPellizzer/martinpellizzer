Impatiens laxiflora var. khasiana

Impatiens laxiflora var. khasiana, commonly known as the Khasi impatiens, is a flowering plant native to the northeastern regions of India.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as forest understories and mountainous areas. Its leaves are ovate to elliptical in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate and vibrant blooms.
