Iberis spathulata proles aniensis

Iberis spathulata proles aniensis, commonly known as the Aniensis candytuft, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant features dark green, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens and alpine settings.
