Illecebrum subulatum

Illecebrum subulatum, commonly known as the slender spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal or inland areas. It blooms during the spring and summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Illecebrum subulatum is a hardy perennial that is relatively low maintenance, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
