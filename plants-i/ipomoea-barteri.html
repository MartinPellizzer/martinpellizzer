Ipomoea barteri

Ipomoea barteri, commonly known as the Turk's cap lily, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that resemble a turban, with a five-petaled structure and a prominent central stigma. The plant is an annual herb that grows from a fibrous root system and has heart-shaped, hairy leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

The flowers typically bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
