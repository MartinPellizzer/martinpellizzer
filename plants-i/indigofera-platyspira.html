Indigofera platyspira

Indigofera platyspira, commonly known as the broad-spiral indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive spiral-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. Its root system has been historically used in traditional medicine and dye production.

The plant is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for agricultural purposes.
