Ipomoea lineariloba

Ipomoea lineariloba, commonly known as the slender morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive reddish or purple underside, and its flowers are typically trumpet-shaped, ranging in color from white to pale pink. The plant produces showy, funnel-shaped blooms that open in the morning and close by midday. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun for optimal growth.

Ipomoea lineariloba is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
