Ipomoea carmesina

Ipomoea carmesina, commonly known as the red morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a herbaceous vine that produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically red or orange in color. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is known for its rapid growth habit. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Ipomoea carmesina is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
