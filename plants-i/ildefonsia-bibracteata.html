Ildefonsia bibracteata

Ildefonsia bibracteata, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are velvety to the touch, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow in color. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Ildefonsia bibracteata is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and open fields.
