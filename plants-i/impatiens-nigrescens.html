Impatiens nigrescens

Impatiens nigrescens, commonly known as the black impatiens, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, dark red to black flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in rainforests.

The flowers are pollinated primarily by insects, and the plant is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
