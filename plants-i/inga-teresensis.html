Inga teresensis

Inga teresensis, commonly known as the Amazonian velvet bean, is a small to medium-sized tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces edible pods that contain seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine and as a food source.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and plays an important role in the ecological dynamics of the Amazon rainforest.
