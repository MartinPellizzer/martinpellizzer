Ionidium caffrum

Ionidium caffrum, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to East Africa.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its velvety, waxy leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and prefers well-draining soil. It is widely cultivated as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and ease of care.
