Icica macrophylla

Icica macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaf icica, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white flowers that bloom throughout the wet season. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
