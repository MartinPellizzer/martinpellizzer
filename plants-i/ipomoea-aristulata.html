Ipomoea aristulata

Ipomoea aristulata, commonly known as the velvetleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant is a vigorous climber, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, round seeds.

Ipomoea aristulata is also notable for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
