Ilex rotunda var. sinensis

Ilex rotunda var. sinensis, commonly known as the Chinese holly, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, glossy, dark green leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, followed by red or orange berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in a variety of conditions.

The plant is also valued for its cultural significance in traditional Chinese medicine.
