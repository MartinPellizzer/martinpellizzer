Isopogon drummondii

Isopogon drummondii, commonly known as Drummond's honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, providing year-round foliage.

The plant is well adapted to sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal areas.
