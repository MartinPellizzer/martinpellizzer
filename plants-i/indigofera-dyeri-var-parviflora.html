Indigofera dyeri var. parviflora

Indigofera dyeri var. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces pods that contain small, round seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.
