Impatiens roylei var. pallidiflora

Impatiens roylei var. pallidiflora, commonly known as the pale-flowered impatiens, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and heart-shaped leaves that are usually dark green. The flowers are small, with five petals, and are typically pale pink or white, giving it its common name. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
