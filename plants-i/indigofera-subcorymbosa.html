Indigofera subcorymbosa

Indigofera subcorymbosa, commonly known as the blue pea or Indian indigo, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable in agroforestry systems. Its seeds are used in traditional medicine and as a source of dye.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.
