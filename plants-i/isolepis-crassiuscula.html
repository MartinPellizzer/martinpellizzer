Isolepis crassiuscula

Isolepis crassiuscula, commonly known as the broad-leaved tussock sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to wetland and marshy habitats in temperate regions.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical clumps of grass-like leaves that are usually green to reddish in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. It plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.

Isolepis crassiuscula is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to thrive in waterlogged conditions.
