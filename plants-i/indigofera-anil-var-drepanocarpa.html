Indigofera anil var. drepanocarpa

Indigofera anil var. drepanocarpa, commonly known as the Indian indigo plant, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, clustered flowers that range from white to pale purple. The plant produces pods that contain small, dark blue seeds, which have been historically used for dyeing textiles. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate.

This species is notable for its historical significance in the production of natural indigo dye.
