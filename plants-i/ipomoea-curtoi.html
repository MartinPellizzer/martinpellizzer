Ipomoea curtoi

Ipomoea curtoi, commonly known as the dwarf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces showy blooms in shades of purple, pink, and white, typically opening in the morning and closing at dusk. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in a fine, hairy texture, while its stems are slender and often vine-like.

Ipomoea curtoi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic beauty.
