Ipomoea polymorpha var. discolor

Ipomoea polymorpha var. discolor, commonly known as the Variegated Morning Glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its rapid growth and distinctive, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while the stems are often hairy. This variant is known for its variegated foliage, featuring patches of white and green, which adds to its ornamental value.

It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated as a climbing vine in gardens.
