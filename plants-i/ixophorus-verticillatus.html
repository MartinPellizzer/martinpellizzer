Ixophorus verticillatus

Ixophorus verticillatus, commonly known as the spiral grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact, tufted growth habit and distinctive spiral arrangement of its leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are often clustered at the base of the stem. Native to certain regions of Africa, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscaping.
