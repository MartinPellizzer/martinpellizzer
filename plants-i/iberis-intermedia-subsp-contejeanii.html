Iberis intermedia subsp. contejeanii

Iberis intermedia subsp.

contejeanii, commonly known as the Corsican carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and red.

The plant has a mat-forming habit with silver-gray foliage and is often found in rocky or coastal environments. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with similar floral structures.
