Ipomoea prostrata

Ipomoea prostrata, commonly known as the prostrate morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a trailing vine that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Ipomoea prostrata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can spread rapidly, making it both a garden favorite and a potential weed.
