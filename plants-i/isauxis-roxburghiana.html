Isauxis roxburghiana

Isauxis roxburghiana, commonly known as the Indian rosewood, is a deciduous tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant is valued for its hard, heavy wood, which is used in traditional craftsmanship and musical instruments. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to overharvesting, it is now considered an endangered species in its natural habitat.
