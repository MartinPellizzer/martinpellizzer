Ivesia beneolens

Ivesia beneolens, commonly known as the mountain flax, is a perennial flowering plant native to the high elevations of the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble single blooms. The plant typically grows in rocky, alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in clusters.

Ivesia beneolens plays a vital role in stabilizing soil in its harsh habitat and supports local pollinators.
