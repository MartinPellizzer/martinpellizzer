Ipomoea wightii var. obtusisepala

Ipomoea wightii var. obtusisepala, commonly known as the broad-sepal morning glory, is a flowering vine native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It features heart-shaped, glossy leaves and produces large, showy flowers with broad, rounded sepals. The flowers are typically blue to purple in color and bloom in the morning, hence its common name. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can climb using its twining stems.
