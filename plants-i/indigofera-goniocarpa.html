Indigofera goniocarpa

Indigofera goniocarpa, commonly known as the African indigo or wild indigo, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces distinctive, elongated pods that contain seeds covered in a sticky, mucilaginous substance. It is often found in dry woodland and savanna habitats, where it plays a role in soil nitrogen fixation.

Its botanical features make it an important plant in both ecological and traditional contexts.
