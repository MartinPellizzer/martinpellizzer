Io ambondrombeensis

Io ambondrombeensis, commonly known as the Madagascar orchid, is a rare and endemic species found exclusively in the rainforests of Madagascar.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique floral structure and vibrant coloration. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to the bark of trees in the humid understory of the forest. Due to habitat destruction and limited distribution, Io ambondrombeensis is considered a critically endangered species.
