Iberis campestris

Iberis campestris, commonly known as sweet rocket, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cress plants. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are pinnately divided, with a slightly hairy texture, and it often grows in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Iberis campestris is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in garden landscaping.
