Indigofera terminalis var. chevalieri

Indigofera terminalis var. chevalieri, commonly known as the African indigo or chevalier indigo, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, dark blue to black seeds enclosed in pods. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in dye production.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the legume family, Fabaceae.
