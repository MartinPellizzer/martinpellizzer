Ipomoea pedata

Ipomoea pedata, commonly known as the creeping morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant is a perennial vine that spreads along the ground, often forming dense mats. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Ipomoea pedata is also known for its rapid growth and ability to climb or trail over other plants.
