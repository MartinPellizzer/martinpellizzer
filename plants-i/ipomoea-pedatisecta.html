Ipomoea pedatisecta

Ipomoea pedatisecta, commonly known as the showy morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple, often appearing in the morning. The plant has compound leaves with a distinctive, palmately divided structure, giving it a unique appearance. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Ipomoea pedatisecta is a climbing vine that can grow quite long, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
