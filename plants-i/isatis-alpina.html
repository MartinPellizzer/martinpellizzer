Isatis alpina

Isatis alpina, commonly known as alpine woad, is a biennial or perennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like mustard and cabbage. The plant is characterized by its basal rosette of dark green, hairy leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in rocky, dry habitats and is often found in alpine environments.

Isatis alpina is known for its historical use in dye production, similar to its more famous relative, Isatis tinctoria.
