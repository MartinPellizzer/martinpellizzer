Ipomoea pruinosa

Ipomoea pruinosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety pubescence. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. It thrives in warm climates and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Ipomoea pruinosa is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and gardens.
