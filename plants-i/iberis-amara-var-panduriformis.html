Iberis amara var. panduriformis

Iberis amara var. panduriformis, commonly known as the bitter candytuft, is a perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cress plants. The plant features small, white, four-petaled flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it produces small, elongated fruits that contain several seeds.

This variety is noted for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
