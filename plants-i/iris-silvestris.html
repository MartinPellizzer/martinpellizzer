Iris silvestris

Iris silvestris, commonly known as the woodland iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features upright, sword-shaped leaves and produces striking blue to violet flowers with a distinctive three-parted structure. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and streambanks. It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance.

Iris silvestris is also known for its ability to thrive in partial shade and well-drained soil.
