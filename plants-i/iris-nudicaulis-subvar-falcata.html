Iris nudicaulis subvar. falcata

Iris nudicaulis subvar. falcata, commonly known as the narrowleaf iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along streambanks and wet meadows. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves that are narrow and often recurved. Its flowers are blue to purple in color, with three upright petals and three drooping sepals.

The species is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in riparian zones.
