Iodanthus petiolatus

Iodanthus petiolatus, commonly known as the slender-flowered iodanthus, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic compounds and compound umbels.

It is a perennial herb native to southern Africa, where it thrives in sandy or rocky soils. The plant features slender, branched stems and small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in umbrella-like clusters. Its leaves are typically narrow and pinnate, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Iodanthus petiolatus is often cultivated for its delicate appearance and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
