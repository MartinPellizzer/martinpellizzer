Inula stenocalathia

Inula stenocalathia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved elecampane, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant features a tall, upright stem with lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its yellow flowers appear in late summer, forming a dense, spherical cluster at the top of the stem.

Inula stenocalathia is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
