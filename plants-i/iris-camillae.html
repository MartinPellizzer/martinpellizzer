Iris camillae

Iris camillae, commonly known as the Camille iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features striking violet-blue flowers with a distinctive white or pale yellow center, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has sword-shaped, grass-like leaves that form a dense rosette near the ground. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Iris camillae is valued for its ornamental beauty and is relatively easy to cultivate in temperate climates.
