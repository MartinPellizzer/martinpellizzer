Ipomoea austin-smithii

Ipomoea austin-smithii, commonly known as Austin-Smith's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its trumpet-shaped, showy flowers that typically bloom in the morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces tendrils that help it climb and support its growth. It thrives in warm, sunny climates and is often found in rocky or disturbed soils.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and horticultural collections.
