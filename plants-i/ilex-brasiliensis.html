Ilex brasiliensis

Ilex brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian holly, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the family Aquifoliaceae and is characterized by its dense, spiny leaves and small, dark blue berries. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in shaded forest understories. Its leaves are usually elliptical with a leathery texture and a glossy surface.

Ilex brasiliensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens.
