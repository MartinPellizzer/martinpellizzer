Indigofera nugalensis

Indigofera nugalensis, commonly known as the Nugal indigo plant, is a species of legume native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant produces pods containing seeds that are often used in traditional practices. It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in woodland and savanna habitats.

Due to its historical use in dye production, it holds cultural and ecological significance in its native range.
