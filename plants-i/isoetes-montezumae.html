Isoetes montezumae

Isoetes montezumae, commonly known as the Mexican water fern, is a species of aquatic plant belonging to the genus Isoetes.

It is characterized by its unique spore-producing structures called sporangia, which are typically found on specialized leaves called sporophylls. This plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and marshes. Its morphology includes a central axis with leaf-like structures that are adapted for underwater growth.

Isoetes montezumae plays an important ecological role in maintaining water quality and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
