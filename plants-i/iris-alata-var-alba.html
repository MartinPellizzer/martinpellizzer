Iris alata var. alba

Iris alata var. alba, commonly known as the white flag iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features tall, slender stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height, supporting large, showy white to pale blue flowers. The leaves are sword-shaped, with a smooth, dark green surface and a silvery underside. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is prized for its elegant appearance and is often cultivated in gardens for ornamental purposes.
