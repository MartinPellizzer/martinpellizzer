Indigofera oenotheroides

Indigofera oenotheroides, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 feet, featuring slender, branching stems. The plant produces clusters of small, sky-blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Indigofera oenotheroides is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
