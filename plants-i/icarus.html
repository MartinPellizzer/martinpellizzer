Icarus

Icarus, commonly known as *Icarus lewisii*, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae, commonly known as the carnation family. The plant is characterized by its delicate, pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often growing in dense clusters.

Icarus is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to thrive in arid conditions.
