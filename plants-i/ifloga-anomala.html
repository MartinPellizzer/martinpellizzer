Ifloga anomala

Ifloga anomala, commonly known as the African star apple, is a tropical plant native to Africa, the Caribbean, and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant produces distinctive yellow or orange fruits with a star-shaped appearance, which are edible and have a sweet, creamy texture. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical, while its flowers are small and clustered in panicles.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its fruit, which is used in both culinary and traditional medicine.
