Inga maxoniana

Inga maxoniana, commonly known as the white inga, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy white flowers. The plant is known for its rapid growth and is often found in secondary forests and disturbed areas. Its fruit is a pod that contains several edible seeds, which are used in local cuisines.

The tree also plays an important ecological role in nitrogen fixation, enriching the soil.
