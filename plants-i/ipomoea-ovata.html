Ipomoea ovata

Ipomoea ovata, commonly known as the oval morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas, particularly Central and South America. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet. It produces capsule fruits containing numerous elliptical seeds.

Ipomoea ovata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in a variety of soil types with adequate sunlight.
