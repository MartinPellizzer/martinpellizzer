Ipomoea selloi

Ipomoea selloi, commonly known as the Sello's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in the morning and close by midday. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Ipomoea selloi is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
