Inga gereauana

Inga gereauana, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant has compound leaves with a velvety texture, and its flowers are typically yellow or greenish-yellow, arranged in racemes. The fruit is a pod containing several seeds, and the tree is often found in moist, shaded forest understories.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential ecological benefits in reforestation efforts.
