Ipomoea ternifolia subsp. ternifolia

Ipomoea ternifolia subsp.

ternifolia, commonly known as the three-leaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets arranged in a triangular pattern.

The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple, often opening in the morning. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

This species is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its role in traditional medicine.
