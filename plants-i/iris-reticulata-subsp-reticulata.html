Iris reticulata subsp. reticulata

Iris reticulata subsp.

reticulata, commonly known as the reticulated iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia. It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces striking, violet-blue or white flowers with a distinctive reticulated pattern on the petals.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its early spring blooms.

The subspecies is characterized by its compact size and is closely related to other members of the Iris genus.
