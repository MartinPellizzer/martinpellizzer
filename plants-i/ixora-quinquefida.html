Ixora quinquefida

Ixora quinquefida, commonly known as the five-lobed ixora, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, featuring opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces clusters of small, bright red or orange flowers, which are typically borne in terminal inflorescences. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Ixora quinquefida is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
