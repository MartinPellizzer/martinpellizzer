Inga lawranceana

Inga lawranceana, commonly known as the Lawrence's inga, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with elongated leaflets and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. Its fruit is a legume pod containing several elliptical seeds.

Inga lawranceana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in reforestation projects due to its ecological benefits.
