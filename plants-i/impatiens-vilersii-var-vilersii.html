Impatiens vilersii var. vilersii

Impatiens vilersii var. vilersii, commonly known as the mountain impatiens, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist forest understories and prefers well-drained, nutrient-rich soils. Its leaves are ovate to elliptical, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
