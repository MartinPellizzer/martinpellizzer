Ipomoea racemigera

Ipomoea racemigera, commonly known as the racemifera morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a climbing vine characterized by heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Ipomoea racemigera is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to various regions worldwide.
