Ixora cuneifolia var. roxburghii

Ixora cuneifolia var. roxburghii, commonly known as the Roxburgh's flame bush, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of bright red or orange-red flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are usually arranged in whorls around the stems. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers are not only visually striking but also attract various pollinators such as bees and birds.
