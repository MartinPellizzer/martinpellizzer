Indigofera trita var. maffeii

Indigofera trita var. maffeii, commonly known as the African indigo plant, is a flowering plant native to regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant is notable for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, which enriches the soil and supports sustainable agriculture.

Its historical significance includes use in dyeing textiles with a deep blue pigment, similar to other indigo species.
