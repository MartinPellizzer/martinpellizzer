Ixora fasciculata

Ixora fasciculata, commonly known as the clustered flame or red flame bush, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, clustered growth habit and vibrant red or orange flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are usually arranged in whorls around the stems. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Ixora fasciculata is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
