Ipomoea deppeana

Ipomoea deppeana, commonly known as the Mexican morning glory, is a flowering vine native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of purple and blue. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Ipomoea deppeana is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
