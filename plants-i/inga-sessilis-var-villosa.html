Inga sessilis var. villosa

Inga sessilis var. villosa, commonly known as the woolly inga, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are often visited by bees and other pollinators. Its fruit is a pod containing several seeds, and the plant is often used in agroforestry systems due to its nitrogen-fixing ability.

The varietal form 'villosa' refers to its dense pubescence, which distinguishes it from other varieties of Inga sessilis.
