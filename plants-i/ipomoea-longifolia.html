Ipomoea longifolia

Ipomoea longifolia, commonly known as the long-flowered morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces long, slender vines that can climb or trail along the ground. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Ipomoea longifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
