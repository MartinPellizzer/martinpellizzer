Iris missouriensis var. arizonica

Iris missouriensis var. arizonica, commonly known as the Arizona iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States.

It features upright, grass-like leaves and produces showy, blue to violet flowers with a distinctive white throat. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils in open woodlands and grasslands. It has a rhizomatous root system that helps it thrive in arid conditions.

The flowers bloom in early spring, adding vibrant color to its native habitat.
