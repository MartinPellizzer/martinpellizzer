Indigofera baumiana var. paucijuga

Indigofera baumiana var. paucijuga, commonly known as the small-leaved indigo plant, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in moist, well-drained soils and is adapted to humid, tropical climates.

Its botanical features make it a notable species for both ecological and horticultural studies.
