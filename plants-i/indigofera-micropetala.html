Indigofera micropetala

Indigofera micropetala, commonly known as the small-petioled indigo plant, is a flowering plant species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is typically shrub-like, with stems that can be either erect or prostrate, depending on the growing conditions. Its flowers are usually pale blue to violet in color, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

This species is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and flowering habit.
