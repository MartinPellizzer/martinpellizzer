Ipomoea argyrophylla

Ipomoea argyrophylla, commonly known as silverleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pale blue or purple, often appearing in the morning. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are covered in a silvery down, giving it its common name. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Ipomoea argyrophylla is a climbing vine that can grow up to several meters in length, using its tendrils to support itself.
