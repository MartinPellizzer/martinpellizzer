Ipomoea limosa

Ipomoea limosa, commonly known as swamp morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swamps. The plant features heart-shaped, waxy leaves and produces trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. It blooms during the summer months and relies on pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Ipomoea limosa is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in both full sun and partial shade.
