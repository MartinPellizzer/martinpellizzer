Ixia gloriosa

Ixia gloriosa, commonly known as the golden torch lily, is a striking South African flowering plant belonging to the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange to yellow flowers, which resemble torches and bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant produces a tall, leafless stem that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height, supporting its showy inflorescence. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, typically emerging from a bulbous base.

Ixia gloriosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well-suited to Mediterranean climates.
