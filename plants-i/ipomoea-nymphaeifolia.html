Ipomoea nymphaeifolia

Ipomoea nymphaeifolia, commonly known as the water arum or water spinach, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and striking, showy flowers that resemble aquatic plants. The plant thrives in moist, humid environments and is often found near water bodies. Its stems are edible and are used in various cuisines, while the flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Botanically, it belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and adaptability.
