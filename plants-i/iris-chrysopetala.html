Iris chrysopetala

Iris chrysopetala, commonly known as the golden petal iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers with a distinctive golden hue. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

The showy blooms appear in late spring to early summer, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.
