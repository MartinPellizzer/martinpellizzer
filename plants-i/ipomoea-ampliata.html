Ipomoea ampliata

Ipomoea ampliata, commonly known as the broadleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, showy blue or violet flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and grows as a climbing vine with tendrils. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Ipomoea ampliata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
