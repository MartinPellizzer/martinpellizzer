Indigofera schrirei

Indigofera schrirei, commonly known as the African indigo plant, is a species of legume native to Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces pods that contain seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

Its botanical features make it an important plant in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
