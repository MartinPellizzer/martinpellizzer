Ixia lacera

Ixia lacera, commonly known as the red-hot poker, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It features striking, fiery orange-red tubular flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and birds. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and grows from a rhizomatous root system. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Ixia lacera is also valued for its ornamental beauty and drought tolerance.
