Indigofera fragrans

Indigofera fragrans, commonly known as sweet indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its fragrant, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are pinnate, and it produces small, pod-like fruits containing several seeds.

Indigofera fragrans is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
