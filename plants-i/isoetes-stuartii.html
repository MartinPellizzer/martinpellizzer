Isoetes stuartii

Isoetes stuartii, commonly known as the Stuart's watermoss, is a species of aquatic fern that belongs to the genus Isoetes, which is characterized by its spore-bearing structures called sporangia.

It is typically found in freshwater environments such as ponds and lakes, where it forms dense, underwater mats. The plant has a distinctive appearance with its spirally arranged, ribbon-like leaves that are often submerged. Isoetes stuartii reproduces through spores rather than seeds, a feature common among many ferns.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
