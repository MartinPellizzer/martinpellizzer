Ipomoea pilosa

Ipomoea pilosa, commonly known as the hairy morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and heart-shaped, hairy leaves. The plant produces vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from purple to blue, often opening in the morning. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, it thrives in warm, humid climates.

Ipomoea pilosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can be found growing wild in various parts of the world.
