Ipomoea acutisepala

Ipomoea acutisepala, commonly known as the sharp-sepaled morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, dry seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and forest edges.

Ipomoea acutisepala is a climbing vine that can reach lengths of several meters, using its tendrils to support its growth.
