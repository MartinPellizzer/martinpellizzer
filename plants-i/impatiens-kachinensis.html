Impatiens kachinensis

Impatiens kachinensis, commonly known as the Kachin impatiens, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Balsaminaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that release seeds when mature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections worldwide.
