Ipomoea medians

Ipomoea medians, commonly known as the morning glory, is a flowering vine native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in a range of colors, including blue, purple, and white. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, round seeds. Ipomoea medians is known for its rapid growth and climbing habit, often reaching heights of several meters.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
