Inodes palmetto

Inodes palmetto, commonly known as the cabbage palmetto, is a hardy evergreen plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features a rosette of arching, fan-shaped leaves that are typically light green to bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into spiny, berry-like fruits. Its trunk is covered in a fibrous, palm-like sheath that adds to its distinctive appearance.

The plant is well adapted to coastal and sandy soils, making it a resilient species in challenging environments.
