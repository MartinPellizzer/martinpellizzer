Isoglossa cyclophylla

Isoglossa cyclophylla, commonly known as the spiral-leaved isoglossa, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive spiral arrangement of leaves, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its unique morphology makes it a subject of interest in botanical studies.
