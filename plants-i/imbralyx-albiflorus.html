Imbralyx albiflorus

Imbralyx albiflorus, commonly known as the silverflower mistletoe, is a parasitic plant that thrives on the branches of specific host trees.

Its leaves are thin and waxy, adapted to reduce water loss in its often arid habitat. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it its scientific name "albiflorus," meaning "white flower.". It relies on birds for seed dispersal, as its berries are not edible for most animals.

Due to its specialized growth habits, it is considered a rare and ecologically important species in certain forest ecosystems.
