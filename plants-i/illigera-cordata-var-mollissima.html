Illigera cordata var. mollissima

Illigera cordata var. mollissima, commonly known as the soft-leaved heartleaf, is a species of flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are soft and velvety to the touch, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens with appropriate care.
