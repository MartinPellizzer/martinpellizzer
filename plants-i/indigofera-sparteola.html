Indigofera sparteola

Indigofera sparteola, commonly known as blue indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive blue or violet flowers. The plant has trifoliate leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine.

Its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil makes it beneficial for soil enrichment.
