Isoetes × robusta

Isoetes × robusta, commonly known as the giant water fern, is a hybrid species within the genus Isoetes, characterized by its unique morphology and ecological significance.

It is a member of the order Isoetales and is notable for its spore-producing structures called sporangia, which are typically clustered in strobila. The plant thrives in freshwater environments, such as lakes and ponds, where it plays a role in aquatic ecosystems. Its fronds are often submerged, with some extending above the water surface, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Due to its hybrid nature, Isoetes × robusta exhibits a range of morphological variations that make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
