Iris diantha

Iris diantha, commonly known as the purple iris, is a species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear showy, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive white center. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming a dense rosette at the base.

Iris diantha is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
