Ilex perado var. platyphylla

Ilex perado var. platyphylla, commonly known as the broad-leaved holly, is a variant of the Ilex perado species native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, elliptical leaves that are typically glossy and dark green, with spiny leaves on the underside. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in shaded to semi-shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in natural and cultivated settings.
