Ilex vomitoria subsp. vomitoria

Ilex vomitoria subsp.

vomitoria, commonly known as the Florida holly, is a shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter in color underneath.

The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for various bird species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in moist, acidic environments such as swamps and wetlands.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly used in landscaping.
