Impatiens capensis f. peasei

Impatiens capensis f. peasei, commonly known as the yellow touch-me-not, is a variant of the common yellow impatiens.

It is a herbaceous annual plant native to eastern North America, characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers and heart-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are spurred at the base, a distinctive feature of the Impatiens genus.

The f. peasei form is notable for its more intense yellow coloration compared to the typical variety.
