Iria capitata

Iria capitata, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact, rounded growth habit and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a hairy texture.

Iria capitata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
