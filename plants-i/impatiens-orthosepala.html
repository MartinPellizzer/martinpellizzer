Impatiens orthosepala

Impatiens orthosepala, commonly known as the New Guinea impatiens, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that typically come in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are usually hairy and have a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Impatiens orthosepala is valued for its ability to bloom continuously throughout the growing season.
