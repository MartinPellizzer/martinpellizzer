Isatis alpina var. caucasica

Isatis alpina var. caucasica, commonly known as Caucasian woad, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous areas and has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are small and blue or violet, arranged in racemes, and it produces round, dark green pods containing seeds.

It is valued for its historical use in dyeing textiles and as a potential medicinal plant.
