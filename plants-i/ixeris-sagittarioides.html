Ixeris sagittarioides

Ixeris sagittarioides, commonly known as the arrowleaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial with upright, branching stems and linear, arrow-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.
