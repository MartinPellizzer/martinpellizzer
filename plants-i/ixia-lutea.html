Ixia lutea

Ixia lutea, commonly known as the yellow lily or the golden ox-eye, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Ixia lutea is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant blooms and long-lasting flowers.

It is also used in traditional medicine by some indigenous communities.
