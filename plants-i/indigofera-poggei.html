Indigofera poggei

Indigofera poggei, commonly known as the purple indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking purple to blue flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces pods that contain small, round seeds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
