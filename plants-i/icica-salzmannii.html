Icica salzmannii

Icica salzmannii, commonly known as the Salzmann's ice plant, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white, typically during the summer months. It is well adapted to rocky soils and can often be found growing on limestone outcrops.

Due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
