Inga berteroi

Inga berteroi, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are often consumed raw or used in culinary preparations. Its rapid growth and nitrogen-fixing ability make it valuable for reforestation and soil enrichment.

The bark and leaves have also been used in traditional medicine for their potential health benefits.
