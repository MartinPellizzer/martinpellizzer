Ipomoea crassipes var. shirensis

Ipomoea crassipes var. shirensis, commonly known as the water spinach or river spinach, is a herbaceous annual plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and slender, climbing stems that often grow in aquatic or semi-aquatic environments. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires consistent moisture for optimal growth. Its flowers are typically pale blue or white, with five petals and a tubular corolla.

It is often cultivated as a leafy vegetable and is valued for its nutritional content and adaptability to various soil types.
