Ipomoea sellowii

Ipomoea sellowii, commonly known as the Sellow's morning glory, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a garden plant.
