Ilex aquifolium var. shepherdii

Ilex aquifolium var. shepherdii, commonly known as the Shepherd's holly, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath, often with a waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, followed by bright red berries that are attractive to birds. Its botanical features include a dense, compact growth habit and a preference for moist, well-drained soils.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
