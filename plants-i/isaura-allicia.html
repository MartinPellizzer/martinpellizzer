Isaura allicia

Isaura allicia, commonly known as the purplestem false nettle, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Urticaceae, which includes plants commonly known as nettles. The plant features slender, hairy stems that often turn reddish-purple with age, and its leaves are ovate with serrated edges. Isaura allicia produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
