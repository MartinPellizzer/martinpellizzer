Impatiens ekapaksiana

Impatiens ekapaksiana, commonly known as the Khasi impatiens, is a flowering plant native to the northeastern regions of India.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and grows as an annual herb, often reaching a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic beauty.
