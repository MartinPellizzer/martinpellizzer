Iberis bubanii

Iberis bubanii, commonly known as the Balkan candytuft, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant has a compact, mat-forming growth habit with silvery-gray foliage. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period, which extends from spring to early summer.
