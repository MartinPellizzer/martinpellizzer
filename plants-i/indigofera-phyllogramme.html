Indigofera phyllogramme

Indigofera phyllogramme, commonly known as the golden indigo.

It is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions, characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are usually oval-shaped and slightly hairy.

The plant is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant color and unique foliage.
