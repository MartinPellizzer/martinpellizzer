Ipomoea ursina

Ipomoea ursina, commonly known as the bear's breeches or wild morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant is a climbing vine that can reach lengths of several meters, often using its tendrils to attach to supports. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Ipomoea ursina is also known for its rapid growth and can become invasive in certain environments.
