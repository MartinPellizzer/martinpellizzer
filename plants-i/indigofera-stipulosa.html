Indigofera stipulosa

Indigofera stipulosa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, Africa, and the Americas. The plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. It produces seed pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.

Indigofera stipulosa is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important plant in ecological restoration projects.
