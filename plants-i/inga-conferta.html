Inga conferta

Inga conferta, commonly known as the red-veined inga, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves with prominent red veins. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences, followed by pod fruits containing several seeds. Inga conferta is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it valuable in soil enrichment.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a useful plant in reforestation and agroforestry projects.
