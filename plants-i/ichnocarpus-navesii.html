Ichnocarpus navesii

Ichnocarpus navesii, commonly known as the African burr or African horse chestnut, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Leguminosae (Fabaceae), characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant produces distinctive, spiny, three-lobed fruits that enclose the seeds, which are often used as a source of food for animals. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets, and it has a woody structure with thorny branches.

Ichnocarpus navesii is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.
