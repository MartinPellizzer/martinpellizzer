Isachne albens var. hispidula

Isachne albens var. hispidula, commonly known as the hairy isachne, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along riverbanks. The plant has slender, erect stems with whorled leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its inflorescence consists of a loose cluster of spikelets, which are small and often inconspicuous.

The species is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
