Ipomoea tarijensis

Ipomoea tarijensis, commonly known as the Tarijensis morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly in regions of Bolivia and Peru.

It is a herbaceous vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, black seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and clearings.

Ipomoea tarijensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has also been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
