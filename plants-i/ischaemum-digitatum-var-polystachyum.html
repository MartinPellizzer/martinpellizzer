Ischaemum digitatum var. polystachyum

Ischaemum digitatum var. polystachyum, commonly known as the hairy panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, two-lobed flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
