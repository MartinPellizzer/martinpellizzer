Ipomoea peltata

Ipomoea peltata, commonly known as the morning glory, is a flowering vine native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to climb or trail along supports. It produces violet to blue flowers that are often fragrant.

Ipomoea peltata is widely cultivated for ornamental purposes and is also used in traditional medicine.
