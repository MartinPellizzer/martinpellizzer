Indigofera concinna

Indigofera concinna, commonly known as the dwarf indigo or small indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including India, China, and Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its small, trifoliate leaves and clusters of blue or purple pea-like flowers. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Indigofera concinna is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its compact size and attractive floral display.
