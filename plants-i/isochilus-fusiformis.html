Isochilus fusiformis

Isochilus fusiformis, commonly known as the slender lily, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, strap-like leaves and a single, slender, erect stem that bears a single flower. The flower is typically white or pale pink, with six petals that are fused at the base, forming a tubular shape. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

The plant has a short growing season and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.
