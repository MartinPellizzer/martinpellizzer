Isopogon latifolius

Isopogon latifolius, commonly known as the broad-leaved bottlebrush, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, red to orange bottlebrush-like flowers that appear in late winter to early spring. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

Isopogon latifolius is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract native pollinators.
