Iris laevigata f. albopurpurea

Iris laevigata f. albopurpurea, commonly known as the purple-veined iris, is a striking ornamental plant native to China.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, violet-blue flowers with distinctive white and purple veining. The foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that add a delicate texture to the plant's appearance. This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and its ability to thrive in moist, well-drained soil.

It typically blooms in late spring to early summer, adding a vibrant touch to landscapes.
