Iris unguicularis subsp. carica

Iris unguicularis subsp.

carica, commonly known as the Canary Island iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Canary Islands. It features upright, sword-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy.

The plant produces striking violet-blue flowers with three upright petals and three spreading sepals. It blooms in late spring to early summer, and its rhizomatous root system allows it to thrive in well-drained soils.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
