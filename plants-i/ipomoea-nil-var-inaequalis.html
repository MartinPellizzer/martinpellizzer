Ipomoea nil var. inaequalis

Ipomoea nil var. inaequalis, commonly known as the Japanese morning glory, is a striking vine native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its large, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in a variety of colors, including blue, purple, and white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is known for its rapid growth habit. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers typically open in the morning and close by midday, giving it its common name.
