Iris attica var. violacea

Iris attica var. violacea, commonly known as the violet iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It features striking violet-blue flowers with a distinctive three-lobed petal structure, characteristic of the Iridaceae family. The plant has narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its elegant appearance and ability to bloom in early spring.
