Ipomoea jalapa var. rosea

Ipomoea jalapa var. rosea, commonly known as the roseate morning glory, is a flowering vine native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking pink to purple flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves and can grow up to several meters in length. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant.
