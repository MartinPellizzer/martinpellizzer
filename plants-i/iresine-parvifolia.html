Iresine parvifolia

Iresine parvifolia, commonly known as the red leaf iron plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety red leaves that are often variegated with green and white patterns. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant foliage.

Iresine parvifolia is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardens.
