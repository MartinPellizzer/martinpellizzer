Ipomoea coelestis

Ipomoea coelestis, commonly known as blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a vine that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often found growing along roadsides and in disturbed areas due to its adaptability.

The flowers open in the morning and close by midday, giving it its common name.
