Ipomoea argentea

Ipomoea argentea, commonly known as silverleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a silvery, powdery coating, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple, typically blooming in the morning. It is a climbing vine that can grow up to several meters in length, often using its tendrils to support itself on structures.

Ipomoea argentea is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
