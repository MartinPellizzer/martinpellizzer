Inodes texana

Inodes texana, commonly known as the Texas indigo bush, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a shrub characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and features a taproot system that allows it to thrive in arid environments. Its small, elongated leaves are often arranged in pairs along the stems.

Inodes texana is adapted to withstand drought and poor soil conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
