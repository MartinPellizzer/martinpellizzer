Impatiens elisettae

Impatiens elisettae, commonly known as the Elisetta's touch-me-not, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, spurred petals and vibrant, often bright-colored flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of about 30 to 60 centimeters. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped and have a soft, velvety texture.

Impatiens elisettae thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden beds.
