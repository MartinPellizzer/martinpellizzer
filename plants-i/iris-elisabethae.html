Iris elisabethae

Iris elisabethae, commonly known as the Elizabeth iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and produces three upright leaves that are lanceolate in shape. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

Iris elisabethae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
