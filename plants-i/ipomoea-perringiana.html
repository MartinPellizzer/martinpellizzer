Ipomoea perringiana

Ipomoea perringiana, commonly known as the Perring's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from deep purple to bright yellow. The plant belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is known for its climbing habit, with tendrils that help it ascend supports. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a velvety texture, while its vines can grow quite long.

Ipomoea perringiana is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its role in traditional medicine in some cultures.
