Indigofera leptocaulis

Indigofera leptocaulis, commonly known as the slender-stemmed indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including parts of India and Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and trifoliate leaves, which are typically small and hairy. Its flowers are usually purple or blue, arranged in racemes, and it produces small, pod-like fruits containing several seeds.

This species is often found in open woodland or grassland habitats, where it contributes to soil nitrogen fixation through its symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria.
