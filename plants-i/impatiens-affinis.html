Impatiens affinis

Impatiens affinis, commonly known as the yellow woodsorrel, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive pattern of veins and produces clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers. It blooms from late spring to early summer and is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to spread through rhizomes.

The species is often found in wetland areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
