Ipomoea plebeia subsp. indica

Ipomoea plebeia subsp.

indica, commonly known as the Indian morning glory, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia. It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple.

The plant is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

The seeds are edible and have a nutty flavor, making them a popular snack in some cultures.
