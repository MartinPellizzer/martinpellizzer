Ipomoea timorensis

Ipomoea timorensis, commonly known as the Timor morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Indonesian island of Timor.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, round seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical regions.

Ipomoea timorensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been introduced to other parts of the world for horticultural purposes.
