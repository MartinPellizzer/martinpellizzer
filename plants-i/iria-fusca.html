Iria fusca

Iria fusca, commonly known as the black-eyed Susan, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow petals and dark brown central disk. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and produces small, daisy-like flowers. Iria fusca thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and natural habitats.

Its foliage is usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
