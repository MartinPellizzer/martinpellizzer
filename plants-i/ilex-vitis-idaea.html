Ilex vitis-idaea

Ilex vitis-idaea, commonly known as the winterberry, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its bright red berries that persist through the winter, providing food for birds and other wildlife. The plant has small, elliptical leaves that turn red in the fall before dropping. It typically grows in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodlands and wetlands.

Ilex vitis-idaea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
