Isopyrum fumarioides

Isopyrum fumarioides, commonly known as the common marsh woundwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, such as meadows, stream banks, and damp woodlands. The plant has slender, upright stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have a serrated edge. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and arranged in dense, spike-like clusters, blooming in late spring to early summer.

Isopyrum fumarioides is characterized by its ability to thrive in moist environments and its historical use in traditional medicine.
