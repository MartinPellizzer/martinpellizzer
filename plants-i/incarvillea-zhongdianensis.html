Incarvillea zhongdianensis

Incarvillea zhongdianensis, commonly known as the Zhongdian incarvillea, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of Yunnan Province in China.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky and well-drained soils, adapting to the harsh conditions of its mountainous habitat. Its leaves are bipinnate, with numerous small leaflets, and it produces elongated pods containing seeds.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
