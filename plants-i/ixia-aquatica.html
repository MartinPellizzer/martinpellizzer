Ixia aquatica

Ixia aquatica, commonly known as the water ixia, is a striking aquatic plant native to southern Africa.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a distinctive red or purple stripe. The plant thrives in shallow water environments, such as ponds and wetlands, and has long, narrow leaves that float on the water's surface. Its flowers are pollinated by insects, and it produces small, round seeds that are dispersed by water.

Ixia aquatica is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in aquatic ecosystems.
