Impatiens imbricata

Impatiens imbricata, commonly known as the rock impatiens, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or crevice habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. It is a perennial species that thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil.

Impatiens imbricata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
