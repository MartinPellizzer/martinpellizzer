Iria caribea

Iria caribea, commonly known as the Caribbean iris, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean region.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy texture.

Iria caribea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
