Impatiens palpebrata

Impatiens palpebrata, commonly known as the veined jewelweed, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from orange to red, often with intricate veining patterns. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a waxy texture, while its stems are hairy and erect.

Impatiens palpebrata is a member of the Balsaminaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and short blooming period.
