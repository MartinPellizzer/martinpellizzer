Ipomoea trichocarpa var. torreyana

Ipomoea trichocarpa var. torreyana, commonly known as the Torrey's morning glory, is a climbing vine native to the southwestern United States.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil. It is known for its rapid growth and can reach lengths of several meters.

The flowers typically bloom in the morning, hence its common name.
