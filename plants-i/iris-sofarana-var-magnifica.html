Iris sofarana var. magnifica

Iris sofarana var. magnifica, commonly known as the Magnificent Iris, is a striking perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It features tall, slender stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height, adorned with large, showy flowers in shades of blue and purple. The leaves are sword-shaped, narrow, and covered in a fine, silvery-white down. This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy and adaptable plant for temperate climates.
