Iris melaina

Iris melaina, commonly known as the black iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces striking violet-blue flowers with a distinctive blackish-purple coloration, which is a key feature of its species. Its leaves are sword-shaped and have a rough, hairy texture.

Iris melaina is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is also valued for its historical use in dyeing fabrics.
