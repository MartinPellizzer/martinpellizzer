Inga fagifolia var. belemnensis

Inga fagifolia var. belemnensis, commonly known as the Belemnoid Inga, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by animals. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.

Its ecological role includes nitrogen fixation and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
