Iris lineolata f. tristis

Iris lineolata f. tristis, commonly known as the Weeping Iris, is a botanical variant of the iris genus characterized by its distinctive drooping flower stalks.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and blooms with pale blue to purple flowers that have a distinctive veined pattern. This form is typically found in wetland environments, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. The plant is noted for its delicate appearance and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and adaptability to specific ecological niches.
