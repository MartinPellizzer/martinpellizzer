Ipomoea microphylla

Ipomoea microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves that are small and glossy. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale purple, typically opening in the morning. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Ipomoea microphylla is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive blooms and vigorous growth habit.
