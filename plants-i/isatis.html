Isatis

Isatis, commonly known as the woad plant, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Brassicaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive cruciform flowers and pinnately compound leaves. The most well-known species, Isatis tinctoria, has been historically used for producing blue dye. The plant is also valued for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional Chinese medicine.

Isatis species are typically found in temperate regions and thrive in well-drained soils.
