Iris planifolia var. tarhunensis

Iris planifolia var. tarhunensis, commonly known as the Tarhun Iris, is a rare and endemic species native to the mountainous regions of Turkey.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hairs. The plant produces striking blue to violet flowers with three upright petals and three drooping sepals, typical of the iris family. It thrives in well-drained, alkaline soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
