Ipomoea gigantea

Ipomoea gigantea, commonly known as the giant morning glory, is a vigorous climbing vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It produces large, showy flowers that can reach up to 12 inches in diameter, typically in shades of purple, pink, and white. The plant has heart-shaped, palmately lobed leaves and is known for its rapid growth habit. It thrives in warm climates and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal flowering.

The tuberous roots of Ipomoea gigantea are also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
