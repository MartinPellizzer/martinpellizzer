Iberis spathulata proles nana

Iberis spathulata proles nana, commonly known as the dwarf candytuft, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has rounded, lobed leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact size and ornamental flowers make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and ecological landscapes.
