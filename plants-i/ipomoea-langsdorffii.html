Ipomoea langsdorffii

Ipomoea langsdorffii, commonly known as the Langsdorff's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous elliptical seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in moist forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens but can become invasive in certain environments.
