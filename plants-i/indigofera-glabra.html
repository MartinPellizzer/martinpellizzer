Indigofera glabra

Indigofera glabra, commonly known as Indian indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is notable for its historical use in producing natural blue dye, which was widely used in textiles. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along stream banks.

Indigofera glabra also has medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
