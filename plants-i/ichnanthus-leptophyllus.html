Ichnanthus leptophyllus

Ichnanthus leptophyllus, commonly known as the slender-leafed bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually blue to purple in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Ichnanthus leptophyllus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
