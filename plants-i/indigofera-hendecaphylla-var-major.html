Indigofera hendecaphylla var. major

Indigofera hendecaphylla var. major, commonly known as the large-leaf indigo plant, is a species native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often large and trifoliate, and its striking blue or purple flowers. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches the soil. It is cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used traditionally in dyeing and medicinal practices.

Its growth habit includes both shrub and tree forms, depending on environmental conditions.
