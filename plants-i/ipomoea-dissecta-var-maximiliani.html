Ipomoea dissecta var. maximiliani

Ipomoea dissecta var. maximiliani, commonly known as the Mexican morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and striking violet to blue flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant is a climbing vine with tendrils that help it ascend supports, and its stems are hairy. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in moist woodlands or along stream banks.

The flowers are typically short-lived, opening in the morning and closing by midday.
