Impatiens quisqualis

Impatiens quisqualis, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety purple bracts that surround its small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as an erect shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Impatiens quisqualis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
