Iresine vermicularis var. aggregata

Iresine vermicularis var. aggregata, commonly known as Crimson Beauty, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red to orange-pink bracts that resemble petals, which are actually modified leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-drained soil.

This species is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its striking foliage and compact growth habit.
