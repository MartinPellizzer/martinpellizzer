Inga filicina

Inga filicina, commonly known as the giant arrowroot or false arrowroot, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in diameter. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters, followed by elongated, cylindrical pods containing edible seeds. Its roots are rich in starch and are used as a food source in some regions.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its foliage.
