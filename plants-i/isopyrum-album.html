Isopyrum album

Isopyrum album, commonly known as the white bugloss, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to eastern North America.

It features upright, branching stems that are covered in soft, hairy foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly rough texture, while its root system consists of fibrous roots.

Isopyrum album is often found in moist, disturbed soils along stream banks and in woodland edges.
