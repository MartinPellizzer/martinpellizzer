Impatiens pseudobicolor var. recticornis

Impatiens pseudobicolor var. recticornis, commonly known as the red-eyed impatiens, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant red flowers with a contrasting white or yellow center, which are borne on slender, branching stems. The leaves are typically ovate and glossy, providing a lush backdrop to the showy blooms. This species thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
