Ilex walkeri var. major

Ilex walkeri var. major, commonly known as the giant holly, is a variant of the holly genus native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves that can grow up to 15 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are typically borne in clusters, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for shaded garden environments.

Due to its size and appearance, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in horticulture.
