Ilex pseudovaccinium

Ilex pseudovaccinium, commonly known as the Florida holly, is a dense evergreen shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It features glossy, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and wetlands.

Ilex pseudovaccinium is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
