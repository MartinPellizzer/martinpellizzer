Impatiens monotricha

Impatiens monotricha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved impatiens, is a species native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green. The plant produces clusters of delicate, five-petaled flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, which bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Impatiens monotricha is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
