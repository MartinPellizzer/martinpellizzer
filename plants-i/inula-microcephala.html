Inula microcephala

Inula microcephala, commonly known as the small-headed elecampane, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant features a robust, hairy stem that can grow up to 1.5 meters tall, with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat, disk-like cluster, surrounded by pale green to white ray florets.

Inula microcephala is known for its aromatic properties and has been used traditionally in herbal medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic effects.
