Isopogon heterophyllus

Isopogon heterophyllus, commonly known as the variable leaf banksia, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical inflorescences that produce yellow to orange flowers. The plant has variable leaf shapes, ranging from narrow to broad, which contribute to its common name. It thrives in sandy soils and is often found in heathland and woodland habitats.

Isopogon heterophyllus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting native wildlife.
