Isoetes glacialis

Isoetes glacialis, commonly known as the glacier moss, is a species of water lily native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It is a perennial plant that thrives in cold, freshwater environments such as lakes and ponds. The plant has a distinctive appearance with its dark green, waxy leaves that float on the water's surface. Its reproductive structure consists of small, spore-bearing structures called sporocarps, which are typically found on the underside of the leaves.

Isoetes glacialis plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing food and shelter for various aquatic organisms.
