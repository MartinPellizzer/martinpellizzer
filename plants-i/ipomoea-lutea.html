Ipomoea lutea

Ipomoea lutea, commonly known as the yellow morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a vine that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and thrives in warm, sunny environments. It is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Ipomoea lutea is part of the Convolvulaceae family, which includes other familiar species like the common morning glory.
