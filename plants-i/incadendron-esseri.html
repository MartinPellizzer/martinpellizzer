Incadendron esseri

Incadendron esseri, commonly known as the Madagascar star vine, is a climbing plant native to the rainforests of Madagascar.

It is characterized by its distinctive star-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant has a woody stem with prominent, twisting tendrils that help it climb toward light. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a velvety texture.

Incadendron esseri is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a decorative climbing plant.
