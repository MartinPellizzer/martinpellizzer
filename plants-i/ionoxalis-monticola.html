Ionoxalis monticola

Ionoxalis monticola, commonly known as the mountain sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant produces edible leaves that have a tangy, sour flavor due to the presence of oxalic acid. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Ionoxalis monticola is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its use in local cuisines.
