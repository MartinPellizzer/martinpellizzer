Illipe malabarica

Illipe malabarica, commonly known as the Malabar illipe, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the family Sapotaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and large, elliptical leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by round, orange-red fruits containing a single large seed. The fruit is valued for its high oil content, which is used in traditional medicine and as a source of edible oil.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food for various wildlife species.
