Iris arizonica

Iris arizonica, commonly known as Arizona iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States.

It features striking blue to violet flowers with three upright petals and three drooping sepals. The plant has grass-like, basal leaves that are narrow and linear. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

The iris is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in its native range.
