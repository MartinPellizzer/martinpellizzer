Ipomoea capillacea

Ipomoea capillacea, commonly known as the silky morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a herbaceous vine characterized by its slender stems and heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple, often opening in the morning. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Ipomoea capillacea is known for its rapid growth and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
