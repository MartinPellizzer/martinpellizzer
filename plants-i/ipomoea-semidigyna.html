Ipomoea semidigyna

Ipomoea semidigyna, commonly known as the Mexican morning glory, is a flowering vine native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant is known for its rapid growth and climbing habit, often reaching lengths of several meters. It blooms throughout the growing season, typically from spring to early autumn.

Botanically, it is characterized by its unique floral structure and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
