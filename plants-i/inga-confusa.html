Inga confusa

Inga confusa, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The tree produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by animals. Its root system has nitrogen-fixing nodules, contributing to soil fertility.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
