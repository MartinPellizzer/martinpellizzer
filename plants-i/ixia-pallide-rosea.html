Ixia pallide-rosea

Ixia pallide-rosea, commonly known as the pale-rose ixia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllis family (Amaryllidaceae) and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from pale pink to soft rose. The plant typically grows in a compact, bushy form with narrow, grass-like leaves. It produces showy blooms in late summer to early autumn, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Ixia pallide-rosea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well-suited to well-drained soils and full sun.
