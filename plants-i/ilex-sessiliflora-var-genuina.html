Ilex sessiliflora var. genuina

Ilex sessiliflora var. genuina, commonly known as the Florida holly, is a deciduous evergreen shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It has small, oval, dark green leaves that are typically glossy and have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for ornamental landscaping.

Its compact growth habit and attractive foliage make it a popular choice for hedges and garden borders.
