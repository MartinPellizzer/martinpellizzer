Indigofera exellii

Indigofera exellii, commonly known as the African indigo, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces pods containing seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or savanna habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some regions for both aesthetic and practical purposes.
