Ipomoea tuberosa

Ipomoea tuberosa, commonly known as the morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive trumpet-shaped flowers that open in the morning and close in the evening. The plant produces tuberous roots that store nutrients, contributing to its vigorous growth. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its rapid growth and ability to climb make it a common weed in agricultural settings.
