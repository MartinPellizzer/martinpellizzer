Iris haussknechtii

Iris haussknechtii, commonly known as the Sierra Madre iris, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and striking violet-blue flowers with a distinctive dark purple throat. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming a basal rosette, while its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
