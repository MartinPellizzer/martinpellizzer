Ireneis interrupta

Ireneis interrupta, commonly known as the interrupted iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features tall, slender stems that bear showy, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive interrupted pattern on the outer petals. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Ireneis interrupta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
