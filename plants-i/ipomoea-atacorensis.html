Ipomoea atacorensis

Ipomoea atacorensis, commonly known as the Atacorensis morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its rapid growth and climbing habit. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from violet to white, often appearing in clusters. Its leaves are heart-shaped and typically have a velvety texture.

Ipomoea atacorensis is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant blooms and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
