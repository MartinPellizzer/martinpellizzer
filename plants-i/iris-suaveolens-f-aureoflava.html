Iris suaveolens f. aureoflava

Iris suaveolens f. aureoflava, commonly known as the golden-flowered iris, is a striking ornamental plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, grass-like foliage and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange. The flowers typically bloom in late spring to early summer, adding vibrant color to gardens. This cultivar is prized for its bold, eye-catching blooms and its ability to thrive in well-drained soil.

It is often used in rock gardens or as a border plant due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
