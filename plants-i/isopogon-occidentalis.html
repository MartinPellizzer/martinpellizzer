Isopogon occidentalis

Isopogon occidentalis, commonly known as the Western bottlebrush, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring dense, cylindrical flower spikes that are often red or orange in color. The plant has narrow, needle-like leaves that are arranged in whorls around the stems. It produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Isopogon occidentalis is well adapted to the dry, sandy soils of its native habitat.
