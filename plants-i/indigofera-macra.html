Indigofera macra

Indigofera macra, commonly known as the mountain indigo, is a flowering plant native to regions of Asia, including India and China.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria.

Its botanical features make it an important species in traditional medicine and ecological restoration efforts.
