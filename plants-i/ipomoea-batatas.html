Ipomoea batatas

Ipomoea batatas, commonly known as sweet potato, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its tuberous roots that are rich in carbohydrates. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically purple or white. Its underground storage roots are the primary part used for food and are cultivated in many tropical and temperate regions worldwide.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has been widely hybridized for improved yield and disease resistance.
