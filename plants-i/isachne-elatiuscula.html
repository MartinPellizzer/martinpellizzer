Isachne elatiuscula

Isachne elatiuscula, commonly known as the narrowleaf isachne, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It typically grows in moist, often swampy habitats, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-like inflorescences with two to three florets, which are usually wind-pollinated. Its flowering period occurs in the late spring to early summer, and it is often found in wetland or marshy environments.

Isachne elatiuscula is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
