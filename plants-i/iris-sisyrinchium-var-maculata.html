Iris sisyrinchium var. maculata

Iris sisyrinchium var. maculata, commonly known as the spotted iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces delicate, showy flowers with a distinctive blue-violet hue. The flowers typically bloom in early spring and are characterized by a prominent yellow patch at the base of the petals. This variety is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.

It prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.
