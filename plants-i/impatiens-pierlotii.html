Impatiens pierlotii

Impatiens pierlotii, commonly known as the Congo impatiens, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and dark green, ovate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, which bloom throughout the wet season. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Impatiens pierlotii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens in warm climates.
