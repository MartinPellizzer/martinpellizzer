Isoetes riparia f. saccharata

Isoetes riparia f. saccharata, commonly known as the sweetflag watermace, is a rare aquatic fern that thrives in freshwater environments.

It is characterized by its compact, spore-bearing fronds that are often submerged or floating. The plant is typically found in still or slow-moving waters with muddy or sandy substrates. Its spores are released into the water, allowing for dispersal and colonization of new habitats.
