Ipomoea pinifolioides

Ipomoea pinifolioides, commonly known as the needle-leaved morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its finely divided, needle-like leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Ipomoea pinifolioides is a member of the Convolvulaceae family, which includes many other climbing and trailing plants.
