Ilex confertiflora var. confertiflora

Ilex confertiflora var. confertiflora, commonly known as the broad-leaved holly, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its glossy, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter in color underneath. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for various bird species. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Its hardy nature and ability to tolerate urban environments enhance its value in both natural and cultivated settings.
