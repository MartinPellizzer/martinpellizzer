Iris paradoxa f. mirabilis

Iris paradoxa f. mirabilis, commonly known as the Mirabilis iris, is a striking ornamental plant characterized by its vivid purple and blue flowers.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. The plant features showy, spurred petals that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of broad, sword-shaped leaves that are evergreen in mild climates.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
