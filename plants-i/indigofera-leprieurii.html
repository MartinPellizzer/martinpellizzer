Indigofera leprieurii

Indigofera leprieurii, commonly known as the African indigo or blue bean, is a flowering plant in the legume family Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical Africa and is characterized by its compound leaves and striking blue to violet flowers. The plant is a shrub or small tree, often growing up to 5 meters in height, with a woody stem and trifoliate leaves. It is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable in agricultural systems.

The seeds are enclosed in pods and are used in traditional medicine and as a source of dye.
