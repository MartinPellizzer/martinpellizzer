Indigofera pityophylla

Indigofera pityophylla, commonly known as the blue bean or narrow-leaf indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
