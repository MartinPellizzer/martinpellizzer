Ipomoea pterocarpa

Ipomoea pterocarpa, commonly known as the winged morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant produces capsule fruits that are adorned with wing-like structures, aiding in seed dispersal. It is a climbing vine that thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in forests or along riverbanks.

Botanically, it belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and ornamental value.
