Ipomoea contrerasii

Ipomoea contrerasii, commonly known as the Mexican morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a climbing vine that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of violet, pink, and white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is known for its rapid growth habit. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Ipomoea contrerasii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
