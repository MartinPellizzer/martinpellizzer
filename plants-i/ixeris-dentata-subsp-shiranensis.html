Ixeris dentata subsp. shiranensis

Ixeris dentata subsp.

shiranensis, commonly known as the Japanese carpet grass, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Japan. It features a dense mat of narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bright green and covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or partially shaded environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in garden settings.
