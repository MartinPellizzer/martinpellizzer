Ilex naiguatina

Ilex naiguatina, commonly known as the Guatemalan holly, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central America.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter on the underside. The plant produces small, white flowers that are followed by bright red berries, which are an important food source for various bird species. Ilex naiguatina is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along streambanks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
