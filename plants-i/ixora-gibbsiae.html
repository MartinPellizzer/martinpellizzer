Ixora gibbsiae

Ixora gibbsiae, commonly known as the Gibbs' flame bush, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of bright red, orange, or yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves that are elliptical in shape. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun.

Ixora gibbsiae is widely cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
