Ipomoea comosa

Ipomoea comosa, commonly known as the hairy morning glory, is a flowering vine native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant produces a milky sap that contains alkaloids, which can be toxic to animals and humans. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun for optimal growth.

Ipomoea comosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has also been used in traditional medicine.
