Ischaemum sieboldii

Ischaemum sieboldii, commonly known as Japanese dogstail grass, is a perennial grass species native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, hair-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist environments such as wet meadows, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Ischaemum sieboldii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland habitats.
