Iberis serotina

Iberis serotina, commonly known as late flowering candytuft, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of white or pale yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant has small, rounded leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Iberis serotina is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
