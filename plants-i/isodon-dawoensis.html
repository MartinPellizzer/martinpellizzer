Isodon dawoensis

Isodon dawoensis, commonly known as the Ethiopian verbena, is a flowering plant native to Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and clusters of small, tubular flowers. It typically blooms in the summer months, producing violet to purple flowers that attract pollinators.

Isodon dawoensis is often found in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments.
