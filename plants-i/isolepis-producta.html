Isolepis producta

Isolepis producta, commonly known as the common spike-rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical inflorescence that consists of numerous small, spike-like clusters of flowers. The plant typically grows in shallow water or damp soil, thriving in marshes, pond margins, and other aquatic or semi-aquatic habitats. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming a dense mat that helps stabilize the surrounding soil.

Isolepis producta plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
