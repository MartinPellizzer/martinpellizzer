Ipomoea saxicola

Ipomoea saxicola, commonly known as the rock morning glory, is a flowering plant native to arid and rocky regions of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces vibrant blue or violet flowers that open in the morning and close by midday. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Ipomoea saxicola is often found in crevices and on rock faces, making it a resilient and hardy species.
