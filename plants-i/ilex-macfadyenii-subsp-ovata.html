Ilex macfadyenii subsp. ovata

Ilex macfadyenii subsp.

ovata, commonly known as the ovate holly, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to China. It is characterized by its glossy, ovate-shaped leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath.

The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, followed by red berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value and ability to tolerate urban conditions make it a popular choice for landscaping.
