Indigofera pseudointricata

Indigofera pseudointricata, commonly known as the slender indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to several meters. Its roots have the ability to fix nitrogen, which is a key botanical trait of legumes.

The seeds are enclosed in pods and are often dispersed by wind or animals.
