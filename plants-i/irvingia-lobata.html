Irvingia lobata

Irvingia lobata, commonly known as African mango, is a tropical tree native to West Africa.

It is characterized by its elongated, woody fruit that contains a single large seed. The plant belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is known for its high oil content in the seed. The leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a waxy coating.

Irvingia lobata is also valued for its potential nutritional and medicinal properties.
