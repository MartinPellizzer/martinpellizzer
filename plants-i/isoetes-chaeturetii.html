Isoetes chaeturetii

Isoetes chaeturetii, commonly known as the hairy water fern, is a species of aquatic plant belonging to the genus Isoetes.

It is characterized by its spore-bearing structures called sporangia, which are typically clustered in a structure called a sorus. This plant thrives in freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes, where it anchors itself to the substrate with its rhizomes. Its leaves are usually narrow and ribbon-like, with a distinct midrib and a hairy texture on the underside.

Isoetes chaeturetii plays an important ecological role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
