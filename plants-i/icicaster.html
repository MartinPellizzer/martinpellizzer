Icicaster

Icicaster, commonly known as the blueberry bush, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces edible berries that are rich in antioxidants and vitamins. Icicaster thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and requires partial to full sunlight.

Its ornamental value comes from its vibrant fall foliage and fragrant blossoms.
