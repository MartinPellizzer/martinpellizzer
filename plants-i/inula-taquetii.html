Inula taquetii

Inula taquetii, commonly known as the Taquet's Inula, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Inula taquetii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
