Iberis divaricata

Iberis divaricata, commonly known as candytuft, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and produces clusters of small, white or pink flowers in early spring. The plant has dark green, lobed leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hair, giving it a distinctive appearance. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
