Ibatia lanosa

Ibatia lanosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by orange to red fruits containing three to five seeds. Ibatia lanosa is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and agricultural fields.

It is also known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its tolerance to drought conditions.
