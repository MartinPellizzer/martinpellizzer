Iris × germanica var. alba

Iris × germanica var. alba, commonly known as the White Iris, is a hybrid species within the Iris genus, characterized by its striking white petals and vibrant blue-violet sepals.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 feet, featuring sword-shaped leaves and tall, upright flower stalks. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding a sophisticated touch to floral arrangements.

The White Iris is also valued for its air-purifying qualities and is relatively easy to care for in well-drained soil.
