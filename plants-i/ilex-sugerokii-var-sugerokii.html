Ilex sugerokii var. sugerokii

Ilex sugerokii var. sugerokii, commonly known as the Japanese holly, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are typically elliptical in shape and have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, followed by bright red berries that are an important food source for birds. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its compact growth habit and seasonal color changes make it a visually appealing plant in landscaped settings.
