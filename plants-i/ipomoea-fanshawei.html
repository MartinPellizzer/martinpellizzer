Ipomoea fanshawei

Ipomoea fanshawei, commonly known as the Chinese morning glory, is a flowering plant native to China.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in the morning and close in the late afternoon. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces long, winding vines that can climb or trail along the ground. Its flowers come in a variety of colors, including white, pink, and purple, adding ornamental value to gardens.

Ipomoea fanshawei is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and is also used in traditional Chinese medicine.
