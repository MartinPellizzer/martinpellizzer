Indigofera Charlieriana Var. Sessilis

Indigofera charlieriana var. sessilis is a rare and striking plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia. It is known for its unique, elongated leaves and clusters of small, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring. This variant of Indigofera is particularly prized for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in shaded, moist environments. Its cultivation is often limited due to its specific care requirements and limited availability. Despite its challenges, it remains a sought-after addition to specialized botanical collections.
