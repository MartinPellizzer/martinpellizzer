Iris limbata

Iris limbata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. The plant features grass-like basal leaves that are narrow and sword-shaped, often with a bluish-green hue. Its showy flowers are typically violet or blue, with three upright petals and three recurved sepals.

Iris limbata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
