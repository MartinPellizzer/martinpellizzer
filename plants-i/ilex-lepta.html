Ilex lepta

Ilex lepta, commonly known as the dwarf holly, is a small evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are typically oval in shape and have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red berries in the fall. Ilex lepta is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.

It is often used in landscaping for its dense growth habit and year-round visual interest.
