Ipomoea eriocephala var. sericea

Ipomoea eriocephala var. sericea, commonly known as the silky morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers that range in color from white to pale blue or pink. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a silvery sheen, and its stems are hairy and tend to climb or trail. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

The flowers typically bloom in the morning and close by midday, giving the plant its common name.
