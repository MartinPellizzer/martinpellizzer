Indigofera argyrea

Indigofera argyrea, commonly known as silver indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to parts of Asia, including India and China, and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its striking silver-gray leaves and clusters of small, pale blue or purple flowers. It produces pods that contain seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it beneficial for soil enrichment in agricultural settings.
