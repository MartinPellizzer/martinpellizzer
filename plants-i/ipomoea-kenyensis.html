Ipomoea kenyensis

Ipomoea kenyensis, commonly known as the Kenyan morning glory, is a flowering plant native to East Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that open in the early morning and close by midday. The plant is a climbing vine with heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped blooms. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded areas.

Ipomoea kenyensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and has cultural significance in some African communities.
