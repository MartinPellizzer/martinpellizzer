Ipomoea spiciflora

Ipomoea spiciflora, commonly known as the spiked morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from purple to blue. The plant is a vigorous climber, often found in moist, sunny habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers open in the morning and close by midday, hence its common name.

Ipomoea spiciflora is also known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
