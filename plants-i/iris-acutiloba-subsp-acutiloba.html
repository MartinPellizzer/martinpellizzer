Iris acutiloba subsp. acutiloba

Iris acutiloba subsp.

acutiloba, commonly known as the narrow-leaved iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia. It features tall, slender stems that bear showy, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive three-lobed structure.

The leaves are narrow and sword-shaped, typically arranged in a basal rosette. This subspecies thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
