Ipomoea sagittiformis

Ipomoea sagittiformis, commonly known as the arrowleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of purple or blue. The plant is a climbing vine with tendrils that help it ascend supports such as trellises or fences. It thrives in warm climates with well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Ipomoea sagittiformis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can be found in gardens and natural habitats alike.
