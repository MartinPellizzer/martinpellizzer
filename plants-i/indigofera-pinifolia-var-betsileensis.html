Indigofera pinifolia var. betsileensis

Indigofera pinifolia var. betsileensis, commonly known as the Madagascar indigo bean, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods containing small, dark seeds that are used in traditional practices. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in forested areas with well-drained soil.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
