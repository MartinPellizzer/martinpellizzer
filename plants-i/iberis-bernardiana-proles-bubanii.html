Iberis bernardiana proles bubanii

Iberis bernardiana proles bubanii, commonly known as the Balkan candytuft, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which is characterized by small, cruciform flowers and a distinct scent. The plant produces clusters of white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage consists of rounded, lobed leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
