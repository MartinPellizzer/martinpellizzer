Iliamna

Iliamna, commonly known as the Alaska rose, is a flowering plant native to the boreal and subarctic regions of North America.

It is a member of the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy, pink to white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and is often found in forested areas or along streams. Iliamna produces small, red berries that are an important food source for wildlife.
