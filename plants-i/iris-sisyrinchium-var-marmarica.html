Iris sisyrinchium var. marmarica

Iris sisyrinchium var. marmarica, commonly known as the white iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, white or pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The white iris is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
