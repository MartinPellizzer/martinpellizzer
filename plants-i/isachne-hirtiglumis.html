Isachne hirtiglumis

Isachne hirtiglumis, commonly known as the hairy leaved isachne, is a perennial grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and wetlands. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are covered in fine, hairy structures, giving it a distinctive texture. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, two-lipped flowers.

Isachne hirtiglumis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in humid conditions.
