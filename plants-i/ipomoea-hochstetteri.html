Ipomoea hochstetteri

Ipomoea hochstetteri, commonly known as the Hochstetter's morning glory, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple, often with intricate patterns. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and thrives in warm, humid climates. It is known for its rapid growth and ability to climb various supports.

Botanically, it belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is valued for its ornamental beauty.
