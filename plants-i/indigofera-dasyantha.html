Indigofera dasyantha

Indigofera dasyantha, commonly known as the woolly indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and clusters of small, pink or purple flowers. It is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and ability to attract pollinators.

Additionally, like other members of its genus, it has the potential to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding vegetation.
