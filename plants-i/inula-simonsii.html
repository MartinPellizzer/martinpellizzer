Inula simonsii

Inula simonsii, commonly known as Simons' elecampane, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a robust, hairy stem and large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its yellow flowers bloom in late summer, forming a cluster that resembles a composite head.

Inula simonsii is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
