Itea japonica var. variegata

Itea japonica var. variegata, commonly known as the Japanese Spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It features compound leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape, with a distinctive variegated pattern of white and green on the upper surface. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in early summer, which are borne on long, slender peduncles. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

This ornamental plant is valued for its attractive foliage and is often used in garden landscapes for its visual appeal.
