Indigofera dolichothyrsa

Indigofera dolichothyrsa, commonly known as the slender indigo bush, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and trifoliate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are followed by elongated, bean-like pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
