Ipomoea leptotoma

Ipomoea leptotoma, commonly known as the slender morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet, often opening in the morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture and thrives in warm, humid climates. Its rapid growth habit makes it both a valuable ornamental plant and a potential invasive species in some areas.

The flowers are pollinated primarily by insects, and the plant is known for its ability to climb and spread along fences or trellises.
