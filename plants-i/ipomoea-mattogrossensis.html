Ipomoea mattogrossensis

Ipomoea mattogrossensis, commonly known as the Brazilian morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces vibrant, colorful blooms that typically open in the morning, hence its common name. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a velvety texture.

Ipomoea mattogrossensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic beauty.
