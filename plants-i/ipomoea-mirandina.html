Ipomoea mirandina

Ipomoea mirandina, commonly known as the rainbow morning glory, is a striking flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, orange, yellow, and purple. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and a strong, sometimes unpleasant odor. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Ipomoea mirandina is a vigorous climber, often used in gardens to cover trellises or fences.
