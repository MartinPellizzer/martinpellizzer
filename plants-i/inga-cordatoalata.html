Inga cordatoalata

Inga cordatoalata, commonly known as the heartleaf inga, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant features compound leaves with ovate to heart-shaped leaflets, giving it its common name. It produces small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes.

The fruit is a pod that contains several seeds, and the plant is often used in agroforestry systems due to its fast growth and nitrogen-fixing capabilities.
