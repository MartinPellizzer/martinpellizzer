Ipomoea filipes

Ipomoea filipes, commonly known as the creeping morning glory, is a vine native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its rapid growth habit makes it both a popular ornamental plant and a potential invasive species.

Ipomoea filipes is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of fiber.
