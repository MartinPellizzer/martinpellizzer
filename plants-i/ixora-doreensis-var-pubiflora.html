Ixora doreensis var. pubiflora

Ixora doreensis var. pubiflora, commonly known as the hairy-flowered ixora, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bright red or orange flowers that bloom throughout the year. The leaves are typically ovate with a glossy surface and can reach lengths of up to 10 centimeters. The plant produces small, round berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
