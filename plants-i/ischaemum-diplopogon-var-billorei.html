Ischaemum diplopogon var. billorei

Ischaemum diplopogon var. billorei, commonly known as the slender false brome, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks. The plant has slender, erect stems that can reach up to 60 cm in height, with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of small, spikelet-like flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

This variety is characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology and ecological adaptability to wet environments.
