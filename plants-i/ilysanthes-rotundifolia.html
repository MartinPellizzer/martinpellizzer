Ilysanthes rotundifolia

Ilysanthes rotundifolia, commonly known as the round-leafed ilysanthes, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of South America.

It is characterized by its rounded, leathery leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Ilysanthes rotundifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
