Ipomoea fulvicaulis var. heterocalyx

Ipomoea fulvicaulis var. heterocalyx, commonly known as the yellow morning glory, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces striking yellow flowers with a prominent orange throat. The plant blooms in the early morning, hence its common name, and has a rapid growth habit. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
