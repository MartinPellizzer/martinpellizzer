Inga brachyptera

Inga brachyptera, commonly known as the dwarf serissa, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and fragrant, white flowers. The plant has trifoliate leaves with small, rounded leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
