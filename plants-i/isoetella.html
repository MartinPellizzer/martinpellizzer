Isoetella

Isoetella, commonly known as the "water starwort," is a genus of aquatic or semi-aquatic plants belonging to the family Isoetaceae.

These plants are characterized by their finely divided, needle-like leaves that are often arranged in whorls around the stem. Isoetella species typically grow in shallow, still or slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds, lakes, and wetlands. They are often found submerged or partially submerged, contributing to the structural complexity of aquatic habitats.

The genus is notable for its ability to tolerate low light conditions and poor water quality, making it an important component of many freshwater ecosystems.
