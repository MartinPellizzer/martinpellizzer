Isolepis setifolia

Isolepis setifolia, commonly known as the hairy bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that are covered in fine, hair-like structures, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its rhizomes are fibrous and help it establish in moist soils, making it a common sight in marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Isolepis setifolia plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
