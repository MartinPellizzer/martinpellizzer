Iberis amara subsp. forestieri

Iberis amara subsp.

forestieri, commonly known as the forestieri cress, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many flowering plants with distinctive four-petaled flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in alpine or subalpine environments. Its small, white or pale yellow flowers bloom in early spring, and it produces small, elongated pods containing several seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and early bloom.
