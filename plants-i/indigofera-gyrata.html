Indigofera gyrata

Indigofera gyrata, commonly known as the spiral indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive spiral-arranged leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to purple, attracting various pollinators. Its root system has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.

The plant's unique morphology and ecological role make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
