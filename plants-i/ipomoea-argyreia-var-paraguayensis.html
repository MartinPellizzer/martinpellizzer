Ipomoea argyreia var. paraguayensis

Ipomoea argyreia var. paraguayensis, commonly known as the sacred lotus or angel's trumpets, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and yellow. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Its rapid growth and ability to climb make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking vibrant, fast-growing plants.
