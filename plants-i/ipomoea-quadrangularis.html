Ipomoea quadrangularis

Ipomoea quadrangularis, commonly known as the caladium, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its vibrant, heart-shaped leaves that come in a variety of colors, including red, pink, and white. The plant produces trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pale yellow or white, which bloom in the summer months. Ipomoea quadrangularis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its striking foliage and is relatively easy to grow in warm, humid climates.
