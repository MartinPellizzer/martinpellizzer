Isopogon protea

Isopogon protea, commonly known as the golden scented bottlebrush, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It belongs to the family Proteaceae and is characterized by its unique, bottlebrush-like inflorescences. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that are highly aromatic, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.

Isopogon protea thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.
