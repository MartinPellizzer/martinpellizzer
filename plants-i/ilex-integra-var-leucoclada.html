Ilex integra var. leucoclada

Ilex integra var. leucoclada, commonly known as the whiteleaf holly, is a compact evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are glossy and typically white to pale green in color, distinguishing it from other holly varieties. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers in the spring, which are followed by red berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its unique foliage, it is often used in ornamental landscaping for its aesthetic appeal.
