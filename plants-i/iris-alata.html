Iris alata

Iris alata, commonly known as the slender iris, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming a basal rosette that helps conserve moisture.

Iris alata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.
