Ixora verticillata

Ixora verticillata, commonly known as the flame bush, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, bushy growth habit and clusters of bright orange-red flowers. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves that are arranged in whorls around the stem. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Ixora verticillata is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as birds and bees.
