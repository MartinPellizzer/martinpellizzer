Isoetes japonica

Isoetes japonica, commonly known as the Japanese water fern, is a species of aquatic plant belonging to the order Isoetales.

It is characterized by its spore-bearing structure called a sporangium, which is typically located at the tip of a specialized leaf known as a sporophyll. The plant thrives in freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes, where it anchors itself to the substrate with its rhizomes. Its morphology includes a rosette-like arrangement of leaves, with some leaves specialized for photosynthesis and others for spore production.

Isoetes japonica is notable for its ancient lineage, dating back to the Carboniferous period, and is considered a living fossil due to its morphological conservatism.
