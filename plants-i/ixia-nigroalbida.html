Ixia nigroalbida

Ixia nigroalbida, commonly known as the black-and-white ixia, is a striking member of the Amaryllidaceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear clusters of tubular flowers with a distinctive black and white coloration. The flowers typically bloom in late spring to early summer, showcasing a unique contrast that makes it a popular ornamental plant. The plant prefers well-drained soil and full sun, thriving in Mediterranean climates.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to gardens and botanical collections.
