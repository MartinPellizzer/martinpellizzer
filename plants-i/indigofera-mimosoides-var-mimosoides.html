Indigofera mimosoides var. mimosoides

Indigofera mimosoides var. mimosoides, commonly known as the silky indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and the Caribbean.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, characterized by its pinnate leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces long, narrow pods that contain several seeds, and it is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
