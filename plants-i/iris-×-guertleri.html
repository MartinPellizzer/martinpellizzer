Iris × guertleri

Iris × guertleri, commonly known as the Guertl's iris, is a hybrid species within the iris family (Iridaceae).

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, violet-blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a dense basal rosette.

Iris × guertleri is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and adaptability to various climates.
