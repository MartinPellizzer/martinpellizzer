Ipomoea hispida

Ipomoea hispida, commonly known as the rough morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves, which are often covered in fine, stiff hairs. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet, typically blooming in the early morning. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Ipomoea hispida is a perennial vine that can grow up to several meters in length, making it a distinctive feature in its natural habitat.
