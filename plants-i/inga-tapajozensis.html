Inga tapajozensis

Inga tapajozensis, commonly known as the Amazonian velvet tree, is a tropical tree native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds. It is often found in moist, shaded environments and plays a role in supporting local biodiversity.

Its ecological importance lies in its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to nutrient cycling in the rainforest ecosystem.
