Ichthyostomum pygmaeum

Ichthyostomum pygmaeum, commonly known as the dwarf ichthyostomum, is a microscopic alga belonging to the class Dinoflagellata.

It is characterized by its small size and unique cellular structure, including a complex arrangement of flagella and a distinctive nucleus. This organism is often found in marine and brackish water environments, where it plays a role in the aquatic food web. Its reproductive mechanisms involve both sexual and asexual processes, contributing to its adaptability in various habitats.

Due to its size and ecological significance, it is of interest in studies related to marine biology and environmental monitoring.
