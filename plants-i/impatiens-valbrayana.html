Impatiens valbrayana

Impatiens valbrayana, commonly known as the yellow wax begonia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers with a waxy texture. The plant has heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

Impatiens valbrayana is popular in gardens for its vibrant color and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
