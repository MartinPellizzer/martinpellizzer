Ixodia ptarmicoides

Ixodia ptarmicoides, commonly known as the desert sandmat, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of North America.

It features a compact, cushion-like growth habit with fleshy, spiny stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically reduced to small, scale-like structures, minimizing water loss through transpiration.

Ixodia ptarmicoides plays an important ecological role in stabilizing sandy soils and supporting local wildlife.
