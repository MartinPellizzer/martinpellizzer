Inga vera subsp. vera

Inga vera subsp.

vera, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America. It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically opposite and have a velvety texture.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a pod that contains several seeds, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of medicinal compounds.

The species is also valued for its ability to thrive in shaded environments and its role in ecological restoration projects.
