Inula acaulis subsp. hakkiarica

Inula acaulis subsp.

hakkiarica, commonly known as the Hakkiaric Gumweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Turkey. It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and hairy stems that are often reddish in color.

The plant produces yellow composite flowers arranged in a head, typical of the Asteraceae family. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is adapted to rocky and dry habitats, showcasing resilience to harsh environmental conditions.
