Irvingia grandifolia

Irvingia grandifolia, commonly known as African mango, is a deciduous tree native to the tropical rainforests of West Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically alternate. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by woody, elongated pods containing edible seeds. Irvingia grandifolia is valued for its seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a source of dietary fiber.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
