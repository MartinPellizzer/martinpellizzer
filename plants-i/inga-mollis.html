Inga mollis

Inga mollis, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a small to medium-sized tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by pods containing edible seeds. Its leaves are alternate, compound, and often have a velvety texture, contributing to its common name.

Inga mollis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its timber and medicinal properties.
