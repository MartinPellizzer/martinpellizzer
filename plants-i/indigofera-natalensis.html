Indigofera natalensis

Indigofera natalensis, commonly known as Natal indigo, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, which enriches the soil and benefits surrounding vegetation. It is often found in grasslands and savannas, thriving in well-drained soils.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a notable species in its native habitat.
