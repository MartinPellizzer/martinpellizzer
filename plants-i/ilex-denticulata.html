Ilex denticulata

Ilex denticulata, commonly known as the Mexican holly, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the family Aquifoliaceae and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with finely serrated edges. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for various birds and mammals. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and seasonal fruit.
