Isocoma felgeri

Isocoma felgeri, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought and heat. Its leaves are usually gray-green and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.
