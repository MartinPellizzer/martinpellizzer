Impatiens delavayi var. subecalcarata

Impatiens delavayi var. subecalcarata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf impatiens, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and clusters of small, brightly colored flowers that range from pink to red. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its flowers have five petals and are often accompanied by five sepals that are longer than the petals.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
