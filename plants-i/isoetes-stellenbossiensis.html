Isoetes stellenbossiensis

Isoetes stellenbossiensis, commonly known as the Stellenbosch water fern, is a species of aquatic plant belonging to the genus Isoetes.

It is characterized by its spore-bearing structures called sporangia, which are typically clustered in a dense, spike-like formation called a strobilus. The plant is native to South Africa, particularly in the region around Stellenbosch, where it thrives in freshwater environments. Isoetes stellenbossiensis has a unique morphology with a tall, narrow stem and small, delicate leaves that are often submerged.

Its ecological importance lies in its role as an indicator species for water quality and its contribution to aquatic biodiversity.
