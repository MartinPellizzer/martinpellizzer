Icacorea pluriflora

Icacorea pluriflora, commonly known as the white coral tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking white, coral-like flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it its common name. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, narrow leaflets. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in coastal areas.

Icacorea pluriflora is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
