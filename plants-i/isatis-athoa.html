Isatis athoa

Isatis athoa, commonly known as the yellow flag iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of eastern North America.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae and is characterized by its tall, sword-shaped leaves and showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in moist to wet soil conditions and is often found along stream banks and in marshy areas. Its root system is fibrous and helps stabilize soil, making it a valuable plant for wetland restoration.

Isatis athoa is also known for its medicinal properties, with historical use in traditional herbal remedies.
