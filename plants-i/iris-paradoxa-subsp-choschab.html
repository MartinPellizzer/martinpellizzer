Iris paradoxa subsp. choschab

Iris paradoxa subsp.

choschab, commonly known as the Choschab iris, is a rare and striking plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and striking blue to violet flowers with intricate patterns.

The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming a dense basal rosette.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and is a subject of interest in botanical studies due to its unique adaptations to its harsh environment.
