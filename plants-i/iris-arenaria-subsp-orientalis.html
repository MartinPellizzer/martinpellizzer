Iris arenaria subsp. orientalis

Iris arenaria subsp.

orientalis, commonly known as the eastern sand iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region. It typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

The plant features upright, slender stems that bear showy, blue to violet flowers with three upright petals and three spreading sepals. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its resilience and beauty.
