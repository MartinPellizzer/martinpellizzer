Indocalamus latifolius

Indocalamus latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf bamboo, is a perennial grass-like plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant features broad, flat leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and as a food source. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its rapid growth and ability to form dense clumps make it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
