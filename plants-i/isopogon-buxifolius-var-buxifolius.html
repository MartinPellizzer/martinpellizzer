Isopogon buxifolius var. buxifolius

Isopogon buxifolius var. buxifolius, commonly known as the glossy banksia, is a shrub native to southwestern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage that ranges from dark green to reddish-brown. The plant produces striking yellow to orange flower spikes in late winter to early spring. Its flowers are composed of numerous small, tubular flowers that attract nectar-feeding birds and insects.

The species is adapted to sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal areas.
