Indigofera ingrata

Indigofera ingrata, commonly known as the bitter indigo, is a perennial shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in agricultural settings.

Its seeds are used in traditional medicine, and the plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal.
