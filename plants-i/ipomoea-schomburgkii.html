Ipomoea schomburgkii

Ipomoea schomburgkii, commonly known as the Schomburgk's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves and has a climbing habit, often using tendrils to support itself. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Ipomoea schomburgkii is known for its ornamental value and is cultivated for its striking floral displays.
