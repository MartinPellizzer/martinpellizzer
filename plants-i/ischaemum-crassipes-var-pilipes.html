Ischaemum crassipes var. pilipes

Ischaemum crassipes var. pilipes, commonly known as the hairy barnyard grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which are typically coarse and rough to the touch. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and wetlands.

Due to its aggressive growth habit, it can become a weed in cultivated areas.
