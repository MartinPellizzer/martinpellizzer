Ionidium barzelonense

Ionidium barzelonense, commonly known as the Barzelonese ionidium, is a rare flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, pale blue flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant prefers well-drained, calcareous soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, which helps reduce water loss in its arid habitat.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in conservation status.
