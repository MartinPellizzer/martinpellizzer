Isoetes andina

Isoetes andina, commonly known as the Andean waterwheel, is a species of waterwheel plant native to South America.

It is a member of the genus Isoetes, which includes various aquatic and semi-aquatic plants. The plant is characterized by its distinctive wheel-like arrangement of spore-bearing structures called sporophylls. Isoetes andina typically grows in shallow, still waters such as lakes and marshes.

It plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing shelter and food for various aquatic organisms.
