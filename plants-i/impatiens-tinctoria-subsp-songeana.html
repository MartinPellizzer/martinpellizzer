Impatiens tinctoria subsp. songeana

Impatiens tinctoria subsp.

songeana, commonly known as the red-rooted impatiens, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that are typically red or pink in color.

The plant features heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation in suitable climates.
