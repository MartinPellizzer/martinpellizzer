Isoloma divergens

Isoloma divergens, commonly known as the spreading flax, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It features slender, upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers in clusters, which bloom in late summer. Its fibrous roots and stems are similar to flax, making it a member of the Linaceae family.

Isoloma divergens is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks.
