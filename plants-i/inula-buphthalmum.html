Inula buphthalmum

Inula buphthalmum, commonly known as the eye-bright, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist meadows and woodland edges. The plant features a hairy, branched stem that can reach up to 1 meter in height. Its yellow flowers are arranged in a radiate head, with both ray and disk florets.

The leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
