Ixia trichorhiza

Ixia trichorhiza, commonly known as the honeycomb lily, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular, yellow-orange flowers that resemble honeycombs, which bloom in spring. The plant produces a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and has a short, stout stem. Ixia trichorhiza thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a desirable ornamental plant.
