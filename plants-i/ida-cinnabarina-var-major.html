Ida cinnabarina var. major

Ida cinnabarina var. major, commonly known as the red-flowered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to deep red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with a reddish hue, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for both garden cultivation and horticultural studies.
