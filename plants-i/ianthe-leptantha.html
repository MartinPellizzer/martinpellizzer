Ianthe leptantha

Ianthe leptantha, commonly known as the slender-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale blue to violet. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in cooler climates. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in a fine pubescence.

Ianthe leptantha is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with adequate sunlight and drainage.
