Ixia africana

Ixia africana, commonly known as the African fire lily, is a striking perennial plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in spring, producing vibrant orange or red flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Its foliage consists of strap-like leaves that are often evergreen, providing a lush base for the striking floral display.

Ixia africana is well-suited for gardens and can thrive in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.
