Iria crassipes

Iria crassipes, commonly known as swamp morning glory, is a flowering plant native to wetlands and marshy areas in the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped, violet or blue flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in shallow water or along the edges of ponds. Iria crassipes is a herbaceous perennial that can grow up to 2 meters tall, with hairy stems and showy bracts.

It plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
