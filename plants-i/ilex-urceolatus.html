Ilex urceolatus

Ilex urceolatus, commonly known as the Mexican holly, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by red, fleshy berries in the fall. Ilex urceolatus is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

It is also valued for its role in local ecosystems, providing food for birds and other wildlife.
