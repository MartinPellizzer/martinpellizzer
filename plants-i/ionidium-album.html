Ionidium album

Ionidium album, commonly known as the white ionidium, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, white, cup-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or wetlands. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Ionidium album is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
