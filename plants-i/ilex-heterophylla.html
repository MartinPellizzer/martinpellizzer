Ilex heterophylla

Ilex heterophylla, commonly known as the variable holly, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its variable leaf morphology, with some leaves being broad and others narrow, depending on light conditions. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are an important food source for various bird species. Its foliage remains green throughout the year, providing year-round visual interest in gardens.

Ilex heterophylla is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to different soil types.
