Inula brahuica

Inula brahuica, commonly known as the Himalayan Inula, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its yellowish flowers bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators.

The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, aiding in water retention in its arid habitat.
