Ipomoea cearensis

Ipomoea cearensis, commonly known as the Cearense morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of violet and white. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves and has a climbing habit, often found growing along fences or trees. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Ipomoea cearensis is also noted for its rapid growth and ability to adapt to various soil types.
