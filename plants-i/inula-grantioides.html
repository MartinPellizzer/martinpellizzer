Inula grantioides

Inula grantioides, commonly known as the mountain inula, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils. The plant features a robust, upright stem that can reach up to 1 meter in height, adorned with lance-shaped leaves. Its most distinctive feature is its large, yellow composite flower head, which consists of both ray and disc florets.

Inula grantioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
