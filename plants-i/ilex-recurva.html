Ilex recurva

Ilex recurva, commonly known as the Florida holly, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It features alternate, glossy, dark green leaves that turn reddish-brown in the fall. The plant produces small, white, bell-shaped flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red berries in the fall. Ilex recurva is evergreen in some regions but deciduous in others, depending on climate conditions.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
