Inula lozanoi

Inula lozanoi, commonly known as the Armenian elecampane, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Armenia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant features a robust stem with grayish-green, hairy leaves that are lance-shaped and slightly serrated. Its flowering period occurs in late summer, producing yellow composite flowers that attract pollinators.

Inula lozanoi is valued for its potential medicinal uses, including anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.
