Ipomoea pes-caprae subsp. brasiliensis

Ipomoea pes-caprae subsp.

brasiliensis, commonly known as beach morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions. It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped, pale blue to white flowers that open in the morning.

The plant thrives in sandy coastal environments and is often found in disturbed soils. It has a vigorous growth habit and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, flat seeds.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to coastal conditions and it is often used in ecological restoration projects.
