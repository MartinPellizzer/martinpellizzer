Isachne polygonoides

Isachne polygonoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, narrow leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of hair. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets, which are often wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Isachne polygonoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
