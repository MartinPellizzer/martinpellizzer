Ipomoea urbica

Ipomoea urbica, commonly known as the urban morning glory, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows in disturbed soils, such as along roadsides and in urban areas. The plant produces trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple, opening in the morning and closing by midday. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its vines can climb or trail along the ground.

Ipomoea urbica is often found in temperate regions and is adaptable to various environmental conditions.
