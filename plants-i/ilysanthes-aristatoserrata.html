Ilysanthes aristatoserrata

Ilysanthes aristatoserrata, commonly known as the hairy serrated orchid, is a rare and distinctive orchid species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique foliage, featuring hairy, serrated margins on its leaves, which contribute to its common name. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers with a complex structure typical of the orchid family. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires specific soil conditions for successful cultivation.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
