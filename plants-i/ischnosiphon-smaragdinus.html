Ischnosiphon smaragdinus

Ischnosiphon smaragdinus, commonly known as the emerald spongeplant, is a unique aquatic plant characterized by its spongy, green structures that resemble a sponge.

It thrives in freshwater environments, particularly in slow-moving or still waters with soft substrates. The plant's morphology is adapted for efficient water absorption and gas exchange, with specialized cells that increase surface area. It is often found in regions with high humidity and moderate temperatures.
