Iris odoratissima

Iris odoratissima, commonly known as the sweet iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers with a distinct, fragrant scent. The plant features sword-shaped, grass-like leaves and produces showy, three-petaled blooms that typically appear in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.

Due to its ornamental value and fragrance, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
