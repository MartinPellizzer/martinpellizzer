Indigofera subulata var. microphylla

Indigofera subulata var. microphylla, commonly known as the dwarf indigo or small-leaved indigo, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its small, needle-like leaves and clusters of bright blue or violet pea-shaped flowers. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and has the ability to fix nitrogen through its root nodules, making it beneficial for soil fertility. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and rocky habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
