Iberis linifolia subsp. grenieri

Iberis linifolia subsp.

grenieri, commonly known as the Sierra Wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a low-growing perennial that produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers in early spring.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hair. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
