Ixora euosmia

Ixora euosmia, commonly known as the flame of the forest, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and clusters of bright red or orange flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has opposite, elliptical leaves and produces small, berry-like fruits that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. Ixora euosmia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and horticulture.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
