Iberis saxatilis

Iberis saxatilis, commonly known as rock candy or sweet rockcress, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the rocky areas of southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, which includes mustard and cabbage plants, and is characterized by its small, bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, often found in alpine and coastal regions. Its foliage consists of basal leaves that are lobed or divided, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Iberis saxatilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
