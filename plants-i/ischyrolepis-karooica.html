Ischyrolepis karooica

Ischyrolepis karooica, commonly known as the Karoo mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant native to southern Africa.

It grows on the branches of various host trees, such as acacia and eucalyptus, and derives some of its nutrients from these hosts. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves and produces white or pale yellow flowers. Its berries are typically red or orange, containing seeds that are dispersed by birds.

Ischyrolepis karooica plays a role in the ecological dynamics of its habitat, contributing to biodiversity through its parasitic and mutualistic relationships.
