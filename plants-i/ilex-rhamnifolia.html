Ilex rhamnifolia

Ilex rhamnifolia, commonly known as the Mexican holly, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the family Aquifoliaceae and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically opposite in arrangement. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters, followed by bright red berries that are an important food source for various bird species.

Ilex rhamnifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and seasonal color changes.
