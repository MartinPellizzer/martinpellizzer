Iris lutescens subvar. albescens

Iris lutescens subvar. albescens, commonly known as the white flag iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that bear showy, white to pale yellow flowers with a distinctive dark violet or purple basal stripe. The plant has lance-shaped, grass-like leaves that are usually bluish-green in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique floral structure and its role in wetland ecosystems.
