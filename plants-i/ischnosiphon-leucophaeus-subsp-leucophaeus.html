Ischnosiphon leucophaeus subsp. leucophaeus

Ischnosiphon leucophaeus subsp.

leucophaeus, commonly known as the white-leaved false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, silver-gray leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the dandelion-like appearance. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
