Isolepis nervosa var. campana

Isolepis nervosa var. campana, commonly known as the bell rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It features tall, cylindrical inflorescences that resemble small bells, giving it its common name. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
