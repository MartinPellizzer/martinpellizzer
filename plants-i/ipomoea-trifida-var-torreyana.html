Ipomoea trifida var. torreyana

Ipomoea trifida var. torreyana, commonly known as the desert blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant has heart-shaped, hairy leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing along rocky soils and in desert scrub.

Its rapid growth and distinctive appearance make it a notable species in its native habitat.
