Iberis procumbens subsp. procumbens

Iberis procumbens subsp.

procumbens, commonly known as the creeping candytuft, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Europe. It features dense, mat-forming foliage with small, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant has a compact growth habit, making it suitable for rock gardens and as a ground cover. Its leaves are succulent and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
