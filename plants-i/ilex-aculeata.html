Ilex aculeata

Ilex aculeata, commonly known as the prickly hawthorn, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Aquifoliaceae and is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, dark berries. The leaves are typically elliptical with serrated edges and a leathery texture. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in the spring, which are followed by red or orange berries in the fall.

Ilex aculeata is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
