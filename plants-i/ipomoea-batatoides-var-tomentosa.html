Ipomoea batatoides var. tomentosa

Ipomoea batatoides var. tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant is a herbaceous perennial that thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family, which includes other species like the common morning glory.
