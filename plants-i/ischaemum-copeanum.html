Ischaemum copeanum

Ischaemum copeanum, commonly known as the narrowleaf beakgrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in open grasslands and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences. Its flowering period occurs during the warmer months, and it is adapted to arid and semi-arid conditions.

Botanically, it is characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology and reproductive structures typical of the Poaceae family.
