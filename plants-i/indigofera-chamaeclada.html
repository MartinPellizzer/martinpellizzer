Indigofera chamaeclada

Indigofera chamaeclada, commonly known as the smallleaf indigo, is a perennial shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, showy flowers. The plant is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable in agricultural contexts. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

Its ornamental value and ecological benefits make it a significant species in its native range.
