Ilex belizensis

Ilex belizensis, commonly known as the Belizian holly, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to southern Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, followed by bright red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Ilex belizensis thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in humid, tropical environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
