Isopappus

Isopappus, commonly known as the goldenweed, is a genus of flowering plants in the sunflower family (Asteraceae).

It is characterized by its small, yellow or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling dandelion-like heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is found in arid and semi-arid regions of the western United States. Isopappus species are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

The leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, with a hairy texture that helps reduce water loss in harsh environments.
