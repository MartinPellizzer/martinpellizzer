Isatis tinctoria var. indigotica

Isatis tinctoria var. indigotica, commonly known as woad, is a biennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and vibrant blue flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant is notable for its deep blue pigments, which have been historically used in dyeing textiles. Its roots contain indigo, a valuable natural dye compound.

Woad is also cultivated for its medicinal properties, including antiviral and anti-inflammatory effects.
