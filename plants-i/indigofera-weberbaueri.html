Indigofera weberbaueri

Indigofera weberbaueri, commonly known as the Mexican indigo or Weberbauer's indigo, is a flowering plant species native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy purple or blue flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Indigofera weberbaueri is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
