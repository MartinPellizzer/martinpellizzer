Iris acutiloba

Iris acutiloba, commonly known as the narrow-leaved iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It features tall, slender stems that bear showy, blue to purple flowers with three upright petals and three drooping sepals. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a slight bluish tint. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, stream banks, and woodland edges.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
