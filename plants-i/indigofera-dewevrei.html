Indigofera dewevrei

Indigofera dewevrei, commonly known as the African indigo plant, is a leguminous species native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria in its root nodules. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland and savanna habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important plant for both ecological and potential agricultural purposes.
