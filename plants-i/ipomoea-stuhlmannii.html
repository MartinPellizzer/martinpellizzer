Ipomoea stuhlmannii

Ipomoea stuhlmannii, commonly known as the Stuhlmann's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is a vigorous vine that produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun.

Ipomoea stuhlmannii is also known for its rapid growth and ability to climb various structures.
