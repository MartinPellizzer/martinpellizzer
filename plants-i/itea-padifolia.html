Itea padifolia

Itea padifolia, commonly known as the smoothleaf water-leaf, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Hydrangaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves that are opposite and often have a smooth texture. The plant produces small, white, clustered flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red, berry-like fruits. Itea padifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and insects.
