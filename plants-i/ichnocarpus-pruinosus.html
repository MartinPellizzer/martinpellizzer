Ichnocarpus pruinosus

Ichnocarpus pruinosus, commonly known as the velvet senna, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with a distinctive velvety texture, and it produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its small, indehiscent pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and dry forests.

Ichnocarpus pruinosus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
