Isachne kunthiana var. eligulata

Isachne kunthiana var. eligulata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed isachne, is a grass species native to South America.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in grasslands and open woodlands. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a prominent midrib. Its inflorescence consists of a single spikelet at the tip of each stem, which produces small, two-lobed flowers.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
