Ipomoea gentryi

Ipomoea gentryi, commonly known as Gentry's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep blue. The plant belongs to the Convolvulaceae family, which includes many species of morning glories. Its vine-like growth habit allows it to climb and spread across various substrates.

The leaves are heart-shaped and typically have a velvety texture, adding to its ornamental appeal.
