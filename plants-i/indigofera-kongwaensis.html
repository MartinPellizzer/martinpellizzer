Indigofera kongwaensis

Indigofera kongwaensis, commonly known as the Kongwa indigo, is a flowering plant native to Tanzania.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought-resistant features. It plays a role in soil nitrogen fixation due to its symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria.

Its ecological significance includes providing habitat and forage for local wildlife.
