Impatiens borealis

Impatiens borealis, commonly known as the northern impatiens, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of North America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of white, pink, and red. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
