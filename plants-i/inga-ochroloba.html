Inga ochroloba

Inga ochroloba, commonly known as the yellow inga, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals. It is often found in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential ecological benefits.

The plant's ability to fix nitrogen makes it useful in soil enrichment and agroforestry systems.
