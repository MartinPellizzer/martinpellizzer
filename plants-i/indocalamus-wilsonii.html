Indocalamus wilsonii

Indocalamus wilsonii, commonly known as the Wilson's bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to China.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its elegant appearance and resilience in various environmental conditions.

This bamboo is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.
