Ilex aquifolium var. ciliata

Ilex aquifolium var. ciliata, commonly known as the silver holly, is a variant of the common holly characterized by its distinctive silvery-white undersides on the leaves.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region, often found in rocky or coastal habitats. The leaves are typically ovate with serrated margins and a glossy, dark green upper surface. It produces small, red berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
