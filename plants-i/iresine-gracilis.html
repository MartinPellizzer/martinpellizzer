Iresine gracilis

Iresine gracilis, commonly known as redroot ironweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that are often reddish in color, especially when mature. The plant produces small, showy flowers that are typically pink to red, arranged in dense spikes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a velvety texture with a distinctive red or purple underside.

Iresine gracilis is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in moist, shady conditions.
