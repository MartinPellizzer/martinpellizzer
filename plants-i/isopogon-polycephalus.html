Isopogon polycephalus

Isopogon polycephalus, commonly known as the clustered bottlebrush, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It belongs to the family Proteaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, cylindrical flower spikes that range in color from yellow to red. The plant produces small, leathery leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to dry, Mediterranean-type climates.

Isopogon polycephalus is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
