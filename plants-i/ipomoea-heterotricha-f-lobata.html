Ipomoea heterotricha f. lobata

Ipomoea heterotricha f. lobata, commonly known as the lobed morning glory, is a variant of the morning glory genus characterized by its distinctive lobed leaves.

It is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of the Americas, featuring heart-shaped foliage with pronounced lobes. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white, typically blooming in the morning. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This ornamental plant is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
