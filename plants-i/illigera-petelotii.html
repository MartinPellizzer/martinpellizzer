Illigera petelotii

Illigera petelotii, commonly known as the golden star orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers with a distinctive star-shaped pattern. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are elongated and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant blooms.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
