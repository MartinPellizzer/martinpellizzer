Ipomoea jamaicensis

Ipomoea jamaicensis, commonly known as the Jamaican morning glory, is a flowering vine native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue, violet, or white, opening in the morning and closing at dusk. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is known for its rapid growth and climbing habit. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Ipomoea jamaicensis is also valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in traditional medicine.
