Indigofera erythrantha

Indigofera erythrantha, commonly known as the red-flowered indigo, is a species of legume native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red flowers, which bloom in clusters and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are pinnate, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces elongated pods containing several seeds.

Indigofera erythrantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some cultures.
