Ilex fischeri

Ilex fischeri, commonly known as the Fischer's holly, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, red berries that appear in the fall. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. It has a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 15 feet.

Ilex fischeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its dense foliage and winter interest.
