Icica attenuata

Icica attenuata, commonly known as the narrowleaf icica, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming during the cooler months. It is adapted to survive in poor soils and can tolerate periods of drought.

Icica attenuata is often found in rocky outcrops and along riverbanks, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small insects.
