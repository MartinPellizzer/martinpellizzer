Ixeris monocephala

Ixeris monocephala, commonly known as the single-headed carpetweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to East Asia.

It features a mat-forming growth habit with small, spoon-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in late spring, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties in some species.
