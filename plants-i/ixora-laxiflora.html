Ixora laxiflora

Ixora laxiflora, commonly known as the looseflower ixora, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring opposite, ovate leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces clusters of small, bright orange-red flowers that bloom throughout the year, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its ornamental value is due to its vibrant flowers and compact growth habit, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

Ixora laxiflora is also valued for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its relatively low maintenance requirements.
