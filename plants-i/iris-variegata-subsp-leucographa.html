Iris variegata subsp. leucographa

Iris variegata subsp.

leucographa, commonly known as the white-streaked iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia. It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers with white or pale yellow streaks on the petals.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or streamside habitats. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a distinct white or silver stripe running down the center.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.
