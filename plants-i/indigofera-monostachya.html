Indigofera monostachya

Indigofera monostachya, commonly known as the narrow-stemmed indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple, often arranged in clusters. Its seeds are encased in pods and are used in traditional medicine and as a source of dye.

The plant also has nitrogen-fixing capabilities, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
