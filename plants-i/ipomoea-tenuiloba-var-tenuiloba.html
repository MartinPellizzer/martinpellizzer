Ipomoea tenuiloba var. tenuiloba

Ipomoea tenuiloba var. tenuiloba, commonly known as the slender morning glory, is a herbaceous vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are typically dark green and slightly pubescent. The plant produces strikingly large, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white, which bloom in the early morning. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

The species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
