Indigofera congolensis

Indigofera congolensis, commonly known as African indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive blue-violet flowers. The plant is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable in agricultural systems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland and savanna habitats.

Its seeds are encased in pods and can remain viable in the soil for several years.
