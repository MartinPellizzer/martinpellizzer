Ipomoea heterotricha var. hispida

Ipomoea heterotricha var. hispida, commonly known as the hairy morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Its rapid growth and ornamental value make it both a popular garden plant and a potential weed.
