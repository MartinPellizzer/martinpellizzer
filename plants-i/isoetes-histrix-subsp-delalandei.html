Isoetes histrix subsp. delalandei

Isoetes histrix subsp.

delalandei, commonly known as the Delalande's water horsetail, is a species of aquatic plant belonging to the Equisetaceae family. It is characterized by its slender, jointed stems and small, scale-like leaves that are typically submerged or partially emergent.

This subspecies thrives in freshwater environments such as ponds, marshes, and slow-moving streams. Its reproductive structures, known as strobili, are cone-like and contain spores for propagation.

The plant plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by contributing to nutrient cycling and providing habitat for microorganisms.
