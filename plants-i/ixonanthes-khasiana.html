Ixonanthes khasiana

Ixonanthes khasiana, commonly known as the Khasi sandalwood, is a small evergreen tree native to the northeastern region of India.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, white flowers. The plant is valued for its fragrant wood, which is used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

Due to habitat loss and overharvesting, it is considered an endangered species.
