Indigofera conjugata

Indigofera conjugata, commonly known as the common indigo plant, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands. The plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and racemose inflorescences with small, pea-like flowers. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and can be used in traditional medicine.

Indigofera conjugata is also valued for its potential in soil enrichment due to its nitrogen-fixing ability.
