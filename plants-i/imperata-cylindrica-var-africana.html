Imperata cylindrica var. africana

Imperata cylindrica var. africana, commonly known as African lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base. The plant produces dense, cylindrical spikelets that are typically reddish-brown in color, which are arranged in clusters along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands, disturbed areas, and along roadsides.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions outside its native range.
