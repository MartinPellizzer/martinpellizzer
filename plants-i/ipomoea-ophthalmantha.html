Ipomoea ophthalmantha

Ipomoea ophthalmantha, commonly known as the "eye of the night" or "nightshade vine," is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves and has a climbing habit, often found growing along fences or trees. Its flowers are usually deep purple or violet, with a distinctive pattern resembling an eye.

Ipomoea ophthalmantha is part of the Convolvulaceae family, which includes other nightshade-related plants.
