Ipomoea heterotricha f. dentata

Ipomoea heterotricha f. dentata, commonly known as the toothed morning glory, is a variant of the morning glory family (Convolvulaceae).

It is characterized by its distinctive toothed leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. Native to tropical regions of the Americas, this plant thrives in warm, humid climates. It typically grows as a trailing or climbing vine, often found in moist, sunny habitats.

Its botanical features make it a popular ornamental plant in gardens and botanical collections.
