Intybus praemorsus

Intybus praemorsus, commonly known as the death's-head hawkmoth plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia, often found in dry, rocky habitats. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are typically lobed and have a rough texture, while its stems are hairy and often glandular.

The species is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking appearance and ecological interest.
