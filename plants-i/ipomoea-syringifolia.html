Ipomoea syringifolia

Ipomoea syringifolia, commonly known as the coral vine, is a tropical perennial vine native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, coral-colored flowers that bloom in clusters along the stems. The plant has heart-shaped, palmately lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The vine is also known for its rapid growth and ability to climb various structures.
