Ipomoea reflexa

Ipomoea reflexa, commonly known as the morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its rapid growth and heliotropic flowers that open in the morning and close in the evening. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces trumpet-shaped, five-petaled flowers in a variety of colors, including blue, purple, and white. Ipomoea reflexa is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its vines can climb and spread quickly, making it both a desirable garden plant and a potential invasive species in some areas.
