Indigofera spinosa var. spiniflora

Indigofera spinosa var. spiniflora, commonly known as the prickly indigo, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features compound leaves with serrated edges and small, fragrant flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas.

Its ornamental value and ability to fix nitrogen make it useful in ecological restoration projects.
