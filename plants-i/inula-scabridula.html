Inula scabridula

Inula scabridula, commonly known as rough inula, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features rough, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. Its yellow flowers are arranged in a composite head, typical of the daisy family.

Inula scabridula is often found in dry, disturbed soils and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
