Ipomoea linosepala subsp. alpina

Ipomoea linosepala subsp.

alpina, commonly known as the alpine morning glory, is a flowering plant native to high-altitude regions of South America. It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves with a waxy texture.

The flowers are typically pale blue or violet, with five petals and a distinctive spurred shape. This subspecies thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Its botanical features make it a unique and ornamental addition to specialized gardens.
