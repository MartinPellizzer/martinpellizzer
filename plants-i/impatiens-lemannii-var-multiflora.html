Impatiens lemannii var. multiflora

Impatiens lemannii var. multiflora, commonly known as the multibract impatiens, is a tropical flowering plant native to western Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, spurred flowers that bloom in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant produces numerous small, oval-shaped leaves and has a fibrous root system that allows it to thrive in moist, shaded environments. Its flowers are typically borne in clusters along the stems, and the plant is often cultivated as a decorative ornamental in gardens and greenhouses.

Impatiens lemannii var. multiflora is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
