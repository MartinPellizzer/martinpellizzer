Inula provincialis

Inula provincialis, commonly known as the provincial elecampane, is a perennial herb native to specific regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant features a robust, hairy stem with alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowering heads consist of yellow disk flowers surrounded by white to pale yellow ray florets.

Inula provincialis is valued for its potential medicinal uses and is often found in dry, grassy habitats.
