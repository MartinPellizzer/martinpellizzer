Indigofera dasyantha var. viscidior

Indigofera dasyantha var. viscidior, commonly known as the sticky indigo bean, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers that range in color from pale yellow to violet. The plant produces elongated pods containing seeds that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shrubland habitats.

Its sticky leaves and stems give it a distinctive appearance, making it easily recognizable in its native environments.
