Inula helenium subsp. orgyalis

Inula helenium subsp.

orgyalis, commonly known as the yellow helenium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive foliage.

The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring yellow-orange ray flowers surrounding a central disk of tubular florets. Its leaves are lanceolate, rough, and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its rugged appearance.

The flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn, making it a notable species in wildflower gardens and natural habitats.
