Indigofera platyphylla

Indigofera platyphylla, commonly known as the flat-leaf indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or scrublands.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and potential agricultural applications.
