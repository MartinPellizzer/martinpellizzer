Iris sisyrinchium f. alba

Iris sisyrinchium f. alba, commonly known as the white iris, is a rare and striking variant of the iris family.

It features delicate, white flowers with a subtle, pale blue tint, distinguishing it from its more common yellow and orange relatives. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or meadow habitats. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a central stem.

This ornamental plant is valued for its elegant appearance and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
