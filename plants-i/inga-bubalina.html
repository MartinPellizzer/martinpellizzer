Inga bubalina

Inga bubalina, commonly known as the capuchin bean tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds, which are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests and secondary growth areas.

Inga bubalina is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in agroforestry systems.
