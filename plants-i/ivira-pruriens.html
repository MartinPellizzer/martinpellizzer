Ivira pruriens

Ivira pruriens, commonly known as the African velvet bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated pods containing seeds that are often used in traditional medicine. Its roots are known to fix atmospheric nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.

However, the plant can be toxic if consumed in large quantities, necessitating caution in its use.
