Isophyllum gerardi

Isophyllum gerardi, commonly known as the desert morning glory, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that open in the early morning and close by midday. The plant has slender, trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it well adapted to desert environments.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
