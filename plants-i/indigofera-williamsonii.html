Indigofera williamsonii

Indigofera williamsonii, commonly known as the dwarf indigo, is a perennial shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant is often found in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and in wetlands.

It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, making it beneficial for soil enrichment in ecological restoration projects.
