Isolepis Megapotamica

Isolepis megapotamica is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina and Uruguay. It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, commonly known as the sedge family. This species is characterized by its grass-like appearance and dense, mat-forming growth habit. It thrives in wetland environments, often found in marshes and along riverbanks. Due to its ecological importance, it plays a vital role in maintaining wetland biodiversity.
