Ipomoea tridentata

Ipomoea tridentata, commonly known as the scarlet runner bean, is a climbing vine native to the Americas.

It produces striking red or orange flowers and edible beans that are popular in culinary use. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with three distinct lobes, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in warm climates and requires full sun for optimal growth.

Its rapid growth habit makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking quick coverage or ornamental value.
