Ipomoea carnea subsp. fistulosa

Ipomoea carnea subsp.

fistulosa, commonly known as the scarlet bower vine, is a flowering plant native to South America. It is characterized by its vigorous climbing habit and striking red-orange tubular flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant produces heart-shaped, velvety leaves with a distinctive waxy coating. It thrives in warm, sunny conditions and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, round seeds.
