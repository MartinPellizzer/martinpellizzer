Impatiens allanii

Impatiens allanii, commonly known as the Allan's impatiens, is a herbaceous perennial native to the tropical regions of Central America.

It features upright, branching stems that are covered in soft, hairy foliage. The plant produces clusters of showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink, white, and red, typically blooming from summer to fall. It thrives in partial shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for shaded garden beds.

Its rapid growth and vibrant blooms make it a valuable addition to ornamental horticulture.
