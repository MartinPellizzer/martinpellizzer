Ipomoea subdentata

Ipomoea subdentata, commonly known as the fringed morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is known for its climbing habit, often twisting around supports or other vegetation. Its flowers open in the morning and close by late afternoon, hence its common name.

Ipomoea subdentata is also noted for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens for its beauty and rapid growth.
