Ipomoea ruderaria

Ipomoea ruderaria, commonly known as the yellow morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that open in the morning and close in the late afternoon. The plant typically grows as an annual vine, with heart-shaped leaves and slender stems that twine around supports. Its flowers are bilaterally symmetrical, featuring five petals and a prominent spur.

Ipomoea ruderaria is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, thriving in full sun to partial shade.
