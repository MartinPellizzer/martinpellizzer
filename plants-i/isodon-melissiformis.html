Isodon melissiformis

Isodon melissiformis, commonly known as lemon verbena, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lemon-scented leaves that are often used in herbal teas. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its roots and leaves contain volatile oils, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Isodon melissiformis thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and herbal cultivation.
