Impatiens subcordata

Impatiens subcordata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf impatiens, is a species of flowering plant in the family Balsaminaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, particularly Kenya and Tanzania. The plant is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green. Its flowers are showy, with five petals that are usually pink or white, and they bloom in clusters.

Impatiens subcordata thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
