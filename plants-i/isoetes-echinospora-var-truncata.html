Isoetes echinospora var. truncata

Isoetes echinospora var. truncata, commonly known as the dwarf water clubmoss, is a species of aquatic fern belonging to the genus Isoetes.

It is characterized by its small, spore-bearing structures called sporangia, which are typically clustered in cones along the stem. The plant thrives in freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes, where it anchors itself to the substrate with its rhizomes. Its leaves are usually short and club-shaped, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This variety is notable for its reduced size compared to other members of the genus, making it a unique subject in botanical studies.
