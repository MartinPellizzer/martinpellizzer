Ipomoea sinensis

Ipomoea sinensis, commonly known as the Chinese morning glory, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in the morning and close by late afternoon. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces capsules containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Ipomoea sinensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has cultural significance in traditional Chinese medicine.
