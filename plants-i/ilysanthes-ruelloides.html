Ilysanthes ruelloides

Ilysanthes ruelloides, commonly known as the Ruelloides sunflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disc florets. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy soils and saline conditions. Its yellow-orange flowers bloom in the summer months, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in challenging environments.
