Intsia acuminata

Intsia acuminata, commonly known as the African shea tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to western and central Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its large, compound leaves and showy, fragrant yellow flowers. The tree produces elongated, woody pods that contain a high-oil content seed known as shea butter. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in savanna and rainforest ecosystems.

The plant is valued for its economic and ecological importance, particularly for its valuable seed product.
