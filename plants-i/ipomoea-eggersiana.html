Ipomoea eggersiana

Ipomoea eggersiana, commonly known as the Eggers' morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and twining stems that climb or trail along the ground. It blooms during the day, typically in the morning, hence its common name.

Ipomoea eggersiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
