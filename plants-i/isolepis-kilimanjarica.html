Isolepis kilimanjarica

Isolepis kilimanjarica, commonly known as the Kilimanjaro sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of East Africa.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often in grassland or swampy habitats. Its flowering spikes are usually small and inconspicuous, with tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Isolepis kilimanjarica plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates in its native environment.
