Ipomoea tuberosa var. uniflora

Ipomoea tuberosa var. uniflora, commonly known as the morning glory, is a flowering vine native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped flowers that open in the morning and close at dusk. The plant produces tuberous roots that store nutrients, supporting its growth and survival. It thrives in warm, sunny environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its rapid growth and climbing habit make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking vigorous, flowering vines.
