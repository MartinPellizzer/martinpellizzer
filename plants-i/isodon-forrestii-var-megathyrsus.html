Isodon forrestii var. megathyrsus

Isodon forrestii var. megathyrsus, commonly known as the giant lemongrass, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, aromatic stalks that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, pale green flowers in clusters. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

This variant is valued for its essential oils and is used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb.
