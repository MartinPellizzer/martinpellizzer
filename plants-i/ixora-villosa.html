Ixora villosa

Ixora villosa, commonly known as the hairy ixora, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, branched growth habit and clusters of small, bright orange-red flowers. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered with fine, silky hairs, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Ixora villosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and as a hedge plant.
