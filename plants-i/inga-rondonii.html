Inga rondonii

Inga rondonii, commonly known as the Rondonia inga, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to the Amazon Basin.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a pod that contains several edible seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is an important component of the rainforest understory.
