Iris madonna

Iris madonna, commonly known as the Madonna lily, is a species of flowering plant belonging to the Iridaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing clusters of six to twelve petals. Its leaves are sword-shaped and arranged in a fan-like pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Iris madonna is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and adaptability to well-drained soils.
