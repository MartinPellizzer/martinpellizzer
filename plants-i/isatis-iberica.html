Isatis iberica

Isatis iberica, commonly known as the Iberian woad, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant is characterized by its basal rosette of dark green, hairy leaves and striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in late spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Isatis iberica is also known for its historical use in producing indigo dye.
