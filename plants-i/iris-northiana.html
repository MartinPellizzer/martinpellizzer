Iris northiana

Iris northiana, commonly known as the Chinese flag iris, is a species of flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and striking blue-violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it blooms in late spring to early summer.

Iris northiana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
