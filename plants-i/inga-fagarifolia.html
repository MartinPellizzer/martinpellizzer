Inga fagarifolia

Inga fagarifolia, commonly known as the red-veined inga, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves with prominent red veins. The plant produces small, showy flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a pod that splits open to release seeds, aiding in its dispersal.

Inga fagarifolia is often found in humid lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
