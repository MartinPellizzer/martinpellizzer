Illecebrum congestum

Illecebrum congestum, commonly known as the hanging ivy or weeping ivy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Apocynaceae family, which includes milkweeds and other ornamental plants. The plant is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant, white to pinkish flowers. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a velvety texture.

Illecebrum congestum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in hanging baskets or as a ground cover.
