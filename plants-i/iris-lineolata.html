Iris lineolata

Iris lineolata, commonly known as the streaked iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features tall, slender stems that bear showy, violet-blue flowers with distinctive white streaks on the petals. The plant has lance-shaped, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in alpine or rocky habitats.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
