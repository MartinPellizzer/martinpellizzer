Ipomoea benghalensis

Ipomoea benghalensis, commonly known as the Indian morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is a climbing vine that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

The seeds of Ipomoea benghalensis are edible when properly prepared.
