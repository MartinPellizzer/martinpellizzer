Indigofera spinescens

Indigofera spinescens, commonly known as the spiny indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive spiny structures. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. Its seeds are encased in pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

Indigofera spinescens is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in agricultural settings.
