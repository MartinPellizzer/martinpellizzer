Ipomoea carsonii

Ipomoea carsonii, commonly known as Carson's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its striking violet to blue flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces capsules that contain numerous small, oblong seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in arid environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and botanical collections.
