Iphigenia caricina

Iphigenia caricina, commonly known as the silverleaf nightshade, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray leaves and small, violet-blue flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe, containing toxic compounds. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in disturbed habitats.

Due to its toxic properties, it is important to handle the plant with care.
