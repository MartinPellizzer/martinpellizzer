Indigofera ichangensis f. calvescens

Indigofera ichangensis f. calvescens, commonly known as the dwarf indigo.

It is a shrub native to southern China, characterized by its compact growth habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are followed by elongated, bean-like pods. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils and partial shade.
