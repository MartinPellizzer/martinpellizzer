Imhofia filifolia

Imhofia filifolia, commonly known as the threadleaf ginger, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that are long and narrow, often with a slight sheen. The plant produces striking orange or red flowers that bloom in summer, adding vibrant color to its foliage. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Imhofia filifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
