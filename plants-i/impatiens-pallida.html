Impatiens pallida

Impatiens pallida, commonly known as the ghostflower or white impatiens, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in clusters along the stems. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland areas and streambanks. Its leaves are ovate with a slightly hairy texture, and the stems are slender and somewhat succulent.

Impatiens pallida is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
