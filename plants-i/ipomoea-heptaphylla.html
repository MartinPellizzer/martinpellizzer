Ipomoea heptaphylla

Ipomoea heptaphylla, commonly known as the seven-leaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and vibrant blue or purple flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and climbing habit. Its flowers are typically short-lived, lasting only a day, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Ipomoea heptaphylla is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
