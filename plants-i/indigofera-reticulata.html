Indigofera reticulata

Indigofera reticulata, commonly known as the Indian blue bean, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant produces pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.

Indigofera reticulata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
