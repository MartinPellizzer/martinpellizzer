Isorium formosum

Isorium formosum, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Isorium formosum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and natural landscapes.
