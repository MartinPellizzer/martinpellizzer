Ipomoea violacea

Ipomoea violacea, commonly known as the purple morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking violet to blue-purple flowers that bloom in the morning and close in the afternoon. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves and can grow as a vine or a shrub, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The seeds are enclosed in a fleshy, orange-red capsule and are dispersed by animals and wind.
