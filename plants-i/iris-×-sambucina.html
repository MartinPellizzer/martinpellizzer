Iris × sambucina

Iris × sambucina, commonly known as the marsh iris, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Iris sambucina and Iris pseudacorus.

It is a perennial herb with tall, slender stems that bear showy, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive black center. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, thriving in areas with standing water or saturated soil. Its foliage consists of sword-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
