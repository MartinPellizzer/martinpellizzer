Isolepis basilaris

Isolepis basilaris, commonly known as the flat sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetlands and marshy areas in North America.

It typically grows in dense clusters, with slender, grass-like leaves that are often slightly hairy. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are usually green or brown in color. Isolepis basilaris thrives in saturated soils and is often found in wet meadows, pond edges, and along streams.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
