Isatis cappadocica subsp. macrocarpa

Isatis cappadocica subsp.

macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-seeded woad, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other plants like mustard and cabbage.

The plant is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a powdery coating. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in racemes, and it produces elongated, cylindrical seed pods.

This subspecies is notable for its historical use in dye production, similar to its parent species, Isatis tinctoria.
