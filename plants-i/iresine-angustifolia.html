Iresine angustifolia

Iresine angustifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaved irene, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features striking, colorful, velvety leaves that range from red to purple, often with contrasting white or green veins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically pink or white, arranged in clusters at the tips of the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sun.

Iresine angustifolia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant foliage.
