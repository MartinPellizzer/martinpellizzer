Ixia virginea

Ixia virginea, commonly known as the rain lily, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllis family and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Ixia virginea is known for its ability to bloom during the rainy season, making it a symbol of resilience and renewal.

Its ornamental value has made it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
