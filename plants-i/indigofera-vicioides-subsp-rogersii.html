Indigofera vicioides subsp. rogersii

Indigofera vicioides subsp.

rogersii, commonly known as the blue bean, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from blue to purple.

The plant produces pods containing edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Its ornamental value and nutritional properties make it both a horticultural and agricultural species of interest.
