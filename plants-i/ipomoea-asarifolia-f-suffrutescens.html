Ipomoea asarifolia f. suffrutescens

Ipomoea asarifolia f. suffrutescens, commonly known as the creeping morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a member of the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Its rapid growth and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardeners, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
