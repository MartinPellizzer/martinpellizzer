Isolepis pulchella

Isolepis pulchella, commonly known as the pretty sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a tight, cylindrical cluster at the base. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, which are often submerged or just above the water surface.

Isolepis pulchella plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various aquatic and semi-aquatic organisms.
