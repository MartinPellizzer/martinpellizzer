Ischaemum mangaluricum

Ischaemum mangaluricum, commonly known as the Mangalore grass, is a perennial grass species native to South and Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands, wetlands, and along water bodies. The plant has slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are usually covered with fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like arrangement of small, two-lobed spikelets.

Ischaemum mangaluricum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and erosion control.
