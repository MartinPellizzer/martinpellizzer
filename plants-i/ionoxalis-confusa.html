Ionoxalis confusa

Ionoxalis confusa, commonly known as the clustered wood sorrel, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It features small, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive trifoliate structure, and its flowers are typically white or pale yellow with five petals. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain several seeds, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Ionoxalis confusa is often found in forest understories and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
