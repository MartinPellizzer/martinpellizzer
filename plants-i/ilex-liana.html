Ilex liana

Ilex liana, commonly known as the climbing holly, is a woody vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Aquifoliaceae family and is characterized by its persistent, spiny leaves and small, dark berries. The plant typically grows along trees or shrubs, using its tendrils to climb and spread vertically. Its foliage remains green throughout the year, making it a popular ornamental plant in warm climates.

Ilex liana is also valued for its ecological role, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
