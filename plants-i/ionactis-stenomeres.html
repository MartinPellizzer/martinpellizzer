Ionactis stenomeres

Ionactis stenomeres, commonly known as the narrow-leaved dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant features basal, narrow, and hairy leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern. Its flowers are yellow and produce wind-dispersed seeds in a characteristic pappus structure.

The species is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.
