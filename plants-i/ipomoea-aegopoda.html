Ipomoea aegopoda

Ipomoea aegopoda, commonly known as the "Morning Glory of the South," is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a climbing vine with heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped flowers that open in the morning and close at dusk. The plant produces showy blue or purple flowers, which are often fragrant. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along riverbanks.

Ipomoea aegopoda is also known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and runners.
