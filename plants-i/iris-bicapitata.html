Iris bicapitata

Iris bicapitata, commonly known as the two-headed iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright, sword-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers with a distinctive two-headed appearance, which is a key identifying characteristic. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its unique floral structure and ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
