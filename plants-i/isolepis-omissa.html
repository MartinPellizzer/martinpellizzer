Isolepis omissa

Isolepis omissa, commonly known as the slender spike-rush, is a perennial grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, spike-like inflorescence and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Isolepis omissa is often found in temperate regions and is adapted to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.

Its structure and growth habits make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
