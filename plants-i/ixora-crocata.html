Ixora crocata

Ixora crocata, commonly known as the flame of the forest, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of bright red or orange flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are opposite in arrangement. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a hedge.

Ixora crocata thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.
