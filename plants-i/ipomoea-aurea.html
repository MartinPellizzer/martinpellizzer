Ipomoea aurea

Ipomoea aurea, commonly known as the golden morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant is a vine that can climb using tendrils, often reaching lengths of several meters. Its flowers are typically short-lived, opening in the morning and closing by midday.

Ipomoea aurea is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant.
