Iris atropatana

Iris atropatana, commonly known as the Persian iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features tall, slender stems that bear showy, violet-blue flowers with three upright petals and three drooping sepals. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in alpine or rocky environments.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding a striking visual element to its natural habitat.
