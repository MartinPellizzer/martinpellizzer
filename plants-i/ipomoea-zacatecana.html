Ipomoea zacatecana

Ipomoea zacatecana, commonly known as the Mexican morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of Mexico.

It is a member of the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its rapid growth and trailing vines. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from violet to white, often opening in the early morning. Its heart-shaped leaves are typically broad and have a velvety texture.

Ipomoea zacatecana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
