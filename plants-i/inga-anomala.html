Inga anomala

Inga anomala, commonly known as the "wild coffee tree," is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is notable for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in shaded environments, making it a common understory species in tropical forests. Its flowers are small and clustered, often producing a sweet scent that attracts various pollinators.

The seeds are enclosed in elongated pods and are dispersed by animals, contributing to its ecological significance.
