Inula quadridentata

Inula quadridentata, commonly known as the four-toothed inula, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a hairy, branched stem and lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are yellow, with ray florets that are typically shorter than the central disk florets.

Inula quadridentata is often found in dry meadows and rocky soils, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
