Isopyrum aquilegioides

Isopyrum aquilegioides, commonly known as the water bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to wetlands and marshy areas in eastern North America.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often submerged or floating, adapted to its aquatic environment. Its roots system helps stabilize soil and improve water quality in wetland ecosystems.

Isopyrum aquilegioides is an important plant for both ecological and ornamental purposes in moist habitats.
