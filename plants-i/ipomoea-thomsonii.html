Ipomoea thomsonii

Ipomoea thomsonii, commonly known as Thomson's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its trumpet-shaped, violet-blue flowers that bloom in the morning and close by midday. The plant produces heart-shaped, palmately lobed leaves and has a climbing habit, often using tendrils to ascend supports. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by birds and insects, and it thrives in warm, humid climates.

Ipomoea thomsonii is also valued for its ornamental beauty and has been cultivated for its striking floral display.
