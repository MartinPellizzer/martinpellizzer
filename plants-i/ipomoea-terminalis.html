Ipomoea terminalis

Ipomoea terminalis, commonly known as the scarlet morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a climbing vine that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and thrives in warm, sunny environments. Its rapid growth habit makes it popular for ornamental use in gardens and as a decorative plant.

The flowers typically bloom in the morning and close by midday, giving it its common name.
