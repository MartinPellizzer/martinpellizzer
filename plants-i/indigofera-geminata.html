Indigofera geminata

Indigofera geminata, commonly known as the dwarf indigo, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in dyeing and medicine.

Its growth habit and adaptability make it a versatile plant in various ecological and agricultural contexts.
