Ipomoea cynanchifolia

Ipomoea cynanchifolia, commonly known as the blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in the morning and close in the afternoon. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Ipomoea cynanchifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
