Illicium japonicum

Illicium japonicum, commonly known as Japanese star anise, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan and southern Korea.

It belongs to the Schisandraceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, star-shaped fruits that resemble those of the star anise. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring, followed by the development of its distinctive, spice-like berries. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly serrated margin.

The plant is often cultivated for its aromatic fruits, which are used in traditional medicine and as a spice in culinary applications.
