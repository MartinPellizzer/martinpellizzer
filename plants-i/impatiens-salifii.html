Impatiens salifii

Impatiens salifii, commonly known as the Congo impatiens, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Balsaminaceae family and is characterized by its showy, spurred flowers that come in a variety of colors. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by insects, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous small seeds.

Impatiens salifii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to different growing conditions.
