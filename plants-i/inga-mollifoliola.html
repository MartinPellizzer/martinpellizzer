Inga mollifoliola

Inga mollifoliola, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is notable for its velvety, hairy foliage and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit, a legume pod, contains edible seeds that are often used in traditional cuisine.

The tree is also valued for its ability to thrive in shaded environments and its role in soil enrichment.
