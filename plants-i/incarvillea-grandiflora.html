Incarvillea grandiflora

Incarvillea grandiflora, commonly known as the Chinese trumpetflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to China.

It features striking, tubular, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its unique appearance and long blooming period make it a popular choice among gardeners seeking ornamental plants.
