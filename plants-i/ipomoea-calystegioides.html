Ipomoea calystegioides

Ipomoea calystegioides, commonly known as the purple morning glory, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It produces striking violet to purple flowers that open in the morning and close by midday. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture and can grow up to several meters in length. Its flowers are typically 5-7 cm in diameter, with five petals and a distinctive spurred structure.

Ipomoea calystegioides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
