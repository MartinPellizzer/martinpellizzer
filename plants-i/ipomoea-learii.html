Ipomoea learii

Ipomoea learii, commonly known as the white morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy white or pale purple flowers that open in the morning. The plant typically grows as a climbing vine, reaching lengths of up to several meters. Its flowers are short-lived, lasting only a day, and are succeeded by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Ipomoea learii is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and open woodlands.
