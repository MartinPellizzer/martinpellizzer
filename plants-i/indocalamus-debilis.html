Indocalamus debilis

Indocalamus debilis, commonly known as the slender bamboo, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems. The plant typically grows in wet, marshy environments and can reach heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are long and narrow, often with a bluish-green color.

Indocalamus debilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional basketry.
