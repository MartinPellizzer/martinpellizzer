Ipomoea hooglandii

Ipomoea hooglandii, commonly known as the Hochland morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant is a climbing vine with heart-shaped leaves and produces showy blooms typically in the morning. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Ipomoea hooglandii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
