Inga laevigata

Inga laevigata, commonly known as the silky tamarind, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its rapid growth and dense foliage. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, glossy leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers followed by pods containing edible seeds. Its root system is often used in agroforestry for soil stabilization and nitrogen fixation.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and potential economic uses.
