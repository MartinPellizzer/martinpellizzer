Indigofera viscosa var. subglabra

Indigofera viscosa var. subglabra, commonly known as the smooth indigo plant, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant produces pods containing small, dark seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and medicinal contexts.
