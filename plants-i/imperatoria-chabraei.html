Imperatoria chabraei

Imperatoria chabraei, commonly known as the African rock fig, is a small shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and thick, waxy leaves that help it conserve water in arid conditions. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible, fig-like fruits. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Due to its hardy nature, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
