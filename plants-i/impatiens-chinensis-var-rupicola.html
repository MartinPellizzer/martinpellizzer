Impatiens chinensis var. rupicola

Impatiens chinensis var. rupicola, commonly known as the rock impatiens, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone-rich soils, adapting to harsh, well-drained environments. Its growth habit is usually low and spreading, with stems that often root at the nodes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in challenging garden conditions.
