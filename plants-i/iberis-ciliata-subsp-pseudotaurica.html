Iberis ciliata subsp. pseudotaurica

Iberis ciliata subsp.

pseudotaurica, commonly known as the Caucasian carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region. It is characterized by its low-growing habit and clusters of small, showy flowers that typically bloom in spring.

The plant features hairy stems and leaves with a distinctive serrated edge. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ornamental value has made it a popular choice in horticulture for its hardiness and attractive appearance.
