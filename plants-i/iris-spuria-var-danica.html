Iris spuria var. danica

Iris spuria var. danica, commonly known as the Danica iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces striking blue to violet flowers with a distinctive spur at the base of the petals. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding vibrant color to its natural environment.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty and resilience.
