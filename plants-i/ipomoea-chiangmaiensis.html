Ipomoea chiangmaiensis

Ipomoea chiangmaiensis, commonly known as Chiang Mai morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Thailand.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is known for its rapid growth habit. Its leaves are heart-shaped and velvety in texture, while its vines can climb or trail along the ground.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
