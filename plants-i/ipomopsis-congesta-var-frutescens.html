Ipomopsis congesta var. frutescens

Ipomopsis congesta var. frutescens, commonly known as the clustered evening primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States.

It features showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink to purple in color, blooming in late summer to early fall. The plant has slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for xeriscaping.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract nocturnal pollinators such as moths and bats.
