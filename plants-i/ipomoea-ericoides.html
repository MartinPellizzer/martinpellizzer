Ipomoea ericoides

Ipomoea ericoides, commonly known as the purple morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Ipomoea ericoides is often found in woodland edges, thickets, and along streambanks.
