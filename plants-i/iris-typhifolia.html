Iris typhifolia

Iris typhifolia, commonly known as the marsh iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetlands and marshy areas in North America.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers with three upright petals and three drooping sepals. It thrives in moist to wet soil conditions and is often found in riparian zones.

Iris typhifolia plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
