Indigofera porrigens

Indigofera porrigens, commonly known as the hairy indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale purple, often with a distinctive hairy texture. Its seeds are enclosed in elongated pods, and the plant is known for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

Indigofera porrigens is also valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in nitrogen fixation, benefiting soil fertility.
