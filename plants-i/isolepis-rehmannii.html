Isolepis rehmannii

Isolepis rehmannii, commonly known as the narrowleaf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to North America.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds, lakes, and slow-moving streams. The plant has slender, ribbon-like leaves that are arranged in a whorled pattern along its stem. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged, followed by narrow, elongated fruits.

Isolepis rehmannii plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
