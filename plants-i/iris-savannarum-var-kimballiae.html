Iris savannarum var. kimballiae

Iris savannarum var. kimballiae, commonly known as the Kimball's iris, is a striking ornamental plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear showy, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive white throat. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it suitable for wetland gardens or bog environments. Its foliage consists of sword-like, grassy leaves that provide a lush green backdrop to the vibrant blooms.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
