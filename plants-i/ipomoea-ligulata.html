Ipomoea ligulata

Ipomoea ligulata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant typically grows as a vine, climbing or trailing along surfaces with its hairy stems. Its flowers are usually pale blue or violet, and it produces small, round berries that contain seeds.

Ipomoea ligulata is often found in disturbed soils and is considered an invasive species in some regions.
