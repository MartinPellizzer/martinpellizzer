Ischaemum australe var. arundinaceum

Ischaemum australe var. arundinaceum, commonly known as the slender false brome, is a perennial grass species native to southern Australia.

It typically grows in open woodlands and grasslands, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant has slender, erect stems that can reach up to 1 meter in height, with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of spikelets, which are small, clustered flower structures.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
