Illigera cucullata

Illigera cucullata, commonly known as the hooded orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, particularly in countries like Kenya and Tanzania. The plant is characterized by its unique, hood-like flower structure, which gives it its common name. It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous areas.

The orchid produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, making it a visually striking species.
