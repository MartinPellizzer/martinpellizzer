Isolepis junciformis

Isolepis junciformis, commonly known as the narrowleaf bulbous sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It typically grows in dense clusters, with tall, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine, white bloom. The plant produces small, three-lobed flowers arranged in spikelets, which are characteristic of the Cyperaceae family. Isolepis junciformis thrives in shallow water or moist soil, often forming part of the vegetation in marshes and pond edges.

Its structure and growth habits make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
