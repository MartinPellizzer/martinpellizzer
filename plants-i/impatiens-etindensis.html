Impatiens etindensis

Impatiens etindensis, commonly known as the Eastern African impatiens, is a flowering plant native to the highlands of East Africa.

It belongs to the family Balsaminaceae and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that typically come in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits that release seeds when mature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it is primarily a wild species.
