Isachne mortehanii

Isachne mortehanii, commonly known as the narrowleaf isachne, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in grassland habitats and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are often green or reddish in color. It is adapted to temperate climates and can thrive in well-drained soils.

Isachne mortehanii is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience and ability to stabilize soil.
