Iberis ciliata var. vinetorum

Iberis ciliata var. vinetorum, commonly known as the Vinetum Candytuft, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of white or pink flowers in early spring. The plant has a mat-forming habit with silvery-gray foliage that is covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value comes from its delicate blooms and compact growth, which make it a popular choice in horticulture.
