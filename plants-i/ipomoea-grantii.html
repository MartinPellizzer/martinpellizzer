Ipomoea grantii

Ipomoea grantii, commonly known as the Grant's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous vine characterized by its rapid growth and striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and thrives in warm, humid climates. Its flowers typically open in the morning and close by midday, hence its common name.

Ipomoea grantii is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its beauty and climbing habit.
