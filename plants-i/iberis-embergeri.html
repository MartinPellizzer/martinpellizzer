Iberis embergeri

Iberis embergeri, commonly known as the Emberger's candytuft, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers in early spring. The plant has a mat-forming growth habit, with silver-gray foliage that adds ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

The species is valued for its hardiness and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
