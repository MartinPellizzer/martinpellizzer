Iberis bourgaei

Iberis bourgaei, commonly known as the Algerian candytuft, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and features small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has pinnately divided leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Iberis bourgaei is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
