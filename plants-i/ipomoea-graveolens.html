Ipomoea graveolens

Ipomoea graveolens, commonly known as the stinking nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of purple and white. The plant produces toxic berries that contain alkaloids, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, often with a waxy texture.

Ipomoea graveolens thrives in warm climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.
