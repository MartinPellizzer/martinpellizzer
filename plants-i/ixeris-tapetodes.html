Ixeris tapetodes

Ixeris tapetodes, commonly known as the Japanese mat grass, is a perennial flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and streambanks.

Ixeris tapetodes is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
