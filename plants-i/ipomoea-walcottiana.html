Ipomoea walcottiana

Ipomoea walcottiana, commonly known as the Walcott's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a trailing vine with heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its rapid growth habit makes it suitable for ground cover or as a climbing plant in gardens.

The flowers typically bloom in the morning, giving the plant its common name.
