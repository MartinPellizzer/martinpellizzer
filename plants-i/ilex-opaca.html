Ilex opaca

Ilex opaca, commonly known as the southern wax myrtle, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that turn reddish-brown in the fall. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are an important food source for birds and wildlife. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Ilex opaca is also valued for its fragrant foliage and is used in ornamental landscaping.
