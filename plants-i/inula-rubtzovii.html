Inula rubtzovii

Inula rubtzovii, commonly known as the Siberian Inula, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, branching stems and yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, arid environments.

Inula rubtzovii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
