Iris plumaria

Iris plumaria, commonly known as the purple iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features striking, showy flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, with a distinctive three-parted structure. The plant has sword-shaped leaves that are stiff and grass-like, growing from a rhizomatous root system. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Iris plumaria is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
