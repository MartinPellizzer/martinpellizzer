Illecebrum incanum

Illecebrum incanum, commonly known as the silverleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and hairy, silver-gray leaves. The plant produces trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats.

Illecebrum incanum is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive foliage and blooms.
