Ipomoea lactescens

Ipomoea lactescens, commonly known as the milkweed vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant's leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a velvety texture. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along riverbanks.

Ipomoea lactescens is also notable for its milky sap, which can cause skin irritation in some individuals.
