Indigofera adenocarpa

Indigofera adenocarpa, commonly known as the African indigo, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant produces cylindrical pods that contain several seeds, and it is often found in dry, rocky habitats. It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has the ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it ecologically significant.

Its botanical features make it both a useful plant for soil enrichment and a subject of interest in botanical studies.
