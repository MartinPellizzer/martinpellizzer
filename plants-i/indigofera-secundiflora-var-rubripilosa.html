Indigofera secundiflora var. rubripilosa

Indigofera secundiflora var. rubripilosa, commonly known as the red-haired indigo.

This plant is a flowering legume native to tropical and subtropical regions, characterized by its distinctive red-hair-like pubescence on its stems and leaves. It produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable in agricultural systems.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some cultures.
