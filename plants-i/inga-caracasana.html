Inga caracasana

Inga caracasana, commonly known as the "coffee tree" or "sweet acacia," is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by pods containing edible seeds. It is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches the soil.

Inga caracasana is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and as a source of shade in coffee plantations.
