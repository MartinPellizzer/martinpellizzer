Ixia columnae

Ixia columnae, commonly known as the columnar ixia, is a striking member of the Amaryllidaceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters, supporting clusters of vibrant orange or yellow flowers. The plant produces narrow, strap-like leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern at the base. Ixia columnae thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its unique appearance and long blooming period make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and horticultural collections.
