Indigofera galegoides var. liukiuensis

Indigofera galegoides var. liukiuensis, commonly known as the Liukiu indigo plant, is a flowering plant native to the Ryukyu Islands of Japan.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are usually pale blue or violet, and it produces small, lens-shaped pods that contain several seeds.

This variant is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage and floral display.
