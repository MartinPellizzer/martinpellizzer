Iris cristata subsp. lacustris

Iris cristata subsp.

lacustris, commonly known as the lake iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northeastern United States. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils along the edges of lakes and ponds.

The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces striking blue to purple flowers with a distinctive spurred petal. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is notable for its ecological importance in wetland habitats and its ornamental value in gardens.
