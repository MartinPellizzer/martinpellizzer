Iresine macrocephala

Iresine macrocephala, commonly known as the red-veined sorrel, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It features striking, velvety leaves with deep red veins contrasting against its bright green foliage. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically pink or red, which bloom in clusters. It thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Iresine macrocephala is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
