Iresine grandis var. glabrata

Iresine grandis var. glabrata, commonly known as the red leaf iron plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety red foliage that contrasts with its small, inconspicuous white flowers. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant leaf color and relatively low maintenance requirements.

The species is also valued for its ability to tolerate partial shade, making it suitable for a variety of garden settings.
