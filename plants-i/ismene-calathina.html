Ismene calathina

Ismene calathina, commonly known as the rock daisy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Ismene calathina thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
