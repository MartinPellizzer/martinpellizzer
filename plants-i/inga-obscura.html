Inga obscura

Inga obscura, commonly known as the black inga, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pendulous flowers. The plant produces small, spherical fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in humid, lowland forests and can grow up to 10 meters in height.

Its rapid growth and ability to fix nitrogen make it an important component of tropical ecosystems.
