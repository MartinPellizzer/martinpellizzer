Ixora javanica var. grandiflora

Ixora javanica var. grandiflora, commonly known as the Java flame or flame ixora, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces clusters of bright red or orange-red flowers, which are highly ornamental and attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a smooth texture, while the stems are often covered in fine, soft hairs.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sunlight.
