Iris magnifica

Iris magnifica, commonly known as the magnificent iris, is a striking perennial plant native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems that bear showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 feet, with broad, grass-like leaves that form a dense rosette at the base. Its flowers are composed of three outer petals and three inner sepals, with a distinctive three-dimensional structure.

Iris magnifica prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
