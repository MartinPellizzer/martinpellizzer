Isotropis centralis

Isotropis centralis, commonly known as the central isotope plant, is a rare flowering plant native to specific regions of Central Africa.

It is characterized by its unique, symmetrical floral structure and its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils. The plant exhibits isotropic growth patterns, meaning its growth is uniform in all directions, which is a rare trait in the botanical world. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, with a distinct coloration that aids in pollination by specific insect species.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, Isotropis centralis is considered a valuable subject for botanical research.
