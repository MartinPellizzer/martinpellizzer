Ipomoea oocarpa

Ipomoea oocarpa, commonly known as the giant morning glory, is a vigorous climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter, typically displaying vibrant colors such as pink, purple, or white. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves and has a rapid growth rate, often reaching heights of several meters. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release numerous seeds, aiding in its spread.

Botanically, it belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
