Inula britannica var. eglandulosa

Inula britannica var. eglandulosa, commonly known as the English elecampane, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a tall, cylindrical stem that can grow up to 2 meters in height, covered with fine, hairy hairs. The plant produces yellow composite flowers in late summer, arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with a rough, hairy texture, and the roots are thick and fibrous.

This species is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
