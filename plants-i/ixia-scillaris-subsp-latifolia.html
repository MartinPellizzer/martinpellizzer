Ixia scillaris subsp. latifolia

Ixia scillaris subsp.

latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf ixia, is a perennial bulbous plant native to South Africa. It features strap-shaped, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange.

The flowers typically bloom in late winter to early spring, adding vibrant color to gardens during the cooler months. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and relatively low maintenance requirements.
