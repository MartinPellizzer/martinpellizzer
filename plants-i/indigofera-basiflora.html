Indigofera basiflora

Indigofera basiflora, commonly known as the blue bean or wild indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and clusters of small, pea-like flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Indigofera basiflora is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
