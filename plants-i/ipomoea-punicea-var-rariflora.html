Ipomoea punicea var. rariflora

Ipomoea punicea var. rariflora, commonly known as the rare-flowered morning glory, is a striking vine native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that range from red to orange, often with a distinctive purple or maroon throat. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and thrives in warm, humid climates. It is a climbing plant that can reach lengths of several meters, using its tendrils to support itself.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by horticultural enthusiasts.
