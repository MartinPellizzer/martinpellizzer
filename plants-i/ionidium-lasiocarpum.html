Ionidium lasiocarpum

Ionidium lasiocarpum, commonly known as the woolly fruit plant, is a member of the Ericaceae family.

It is a shrub characterized by its dense, glandular hairs that give it a woolly appearance. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are typically yellow or red in color. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in temperate regions.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
