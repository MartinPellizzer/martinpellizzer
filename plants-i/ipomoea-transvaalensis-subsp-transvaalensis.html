Ipomoea transvaalensis subsp. transvaalensis

Ipomoea transvaalensis subsp.

transvaalensis, commonly known as the Transvaal morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its striking blue or violet trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in the early morning.

The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often found in disturbed soils and can grow as a vine or a shrub.

Its botanical significance lies in its ornamental value and its role in local ecosystems.
