Ilex cuneifolia var. bonariensis

Ilex cuneifolia var. bonariensis, commonly known as the Argentine holly, is a evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It features dense, dark green, elliptical leaves that are often glossy and have a pointed tip. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters, followed by bright red berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Its compact growth habit and year-round foliage make it suitable for hedges and landscape design.
