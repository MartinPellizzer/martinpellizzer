Isatis tinctoria var. maeotica

Isatis tinctoria var. maeotica, commonly known as the Crimean woad, is a variant of the common woad plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a biennial or short-lived perennial plant that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters. The plant features large, lobed leaves and produces yellow flowers in the summer months. Its botanical characteristics include a robust root system and a preference for well-drained, loamy soils.

It is notable for its historical use in the production of indigo dye.
