Indigofera dauensis

Indigofera dauensis, commonly known as Vietnamese indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family Fabaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Vietnam, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment. It thrives in moist, tropical environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value and ecological significance make it an important species in its native habitat.
