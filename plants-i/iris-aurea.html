Iris aurea

Iris aurea, commonly known as the golden iris, is a striking perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It features tall, sword-shaped leaves and showy, violet-blue flowers with a yellow center. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, adding vibrant color to woodland gardens. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

Iris aurea is valued for its ornamental beauty and its ability to thrive in naturalized settings.
