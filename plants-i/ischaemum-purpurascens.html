Ischaemum purpurascens

Ischaemum purpurascens, commonly known as purple false brome, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. The plant features slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with purple at the base. Its inflorescence is a panicle, bearing small, spikelet-like flowers that are wind-pollinated.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in ecological restoration projects.
