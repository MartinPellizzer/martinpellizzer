Inga biolleyana

Inga biolleyana, commonly known as the yellow inga, is a tropical tree species native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with elongated leaflets and produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or white. Its fruit is a pod containing several seeds, and it is often found in lowland rainforests.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting biodiversity.
