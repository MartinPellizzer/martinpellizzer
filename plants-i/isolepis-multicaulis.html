Isolepis multicaulis

Isolepis multicaulis, commonly known as the common spike-rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetlands and marshy areas.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant has cylindrical, grass-like stems that are often clustered and can grow up to 30 cm in height. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spike-like clusters.

Isolepis multicaulis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
