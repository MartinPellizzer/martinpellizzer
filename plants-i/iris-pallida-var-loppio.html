Iris pallida var. loppio

Iris pallida var. loppio, commonly known as the Loppio iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It features slender, upright stems that bear showy, pale blue to violet flowers with a distinctive white to pale yellow keel. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, typically arranged in a fan shape at the base of the plant. This variety is known for its delicate appearance and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
