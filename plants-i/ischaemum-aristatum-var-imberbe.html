Ischaemum aristatum var. imberbe

Ischaemum aristatum var. imberbe, commonly known as the hairy barnyard grass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with a hairy texture on both sides. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

Due to its invasive tendencies, it can compete aggressively with crops and native vegetation.
