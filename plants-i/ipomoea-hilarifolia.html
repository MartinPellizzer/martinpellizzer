Ipomoea hilarifolia

Ipomoea hilarifolia, commonly known as the joyful leaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, glossy leaves and vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink and purple. The plant belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and climbing habit. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Ipomoea hilarifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable conditions.
