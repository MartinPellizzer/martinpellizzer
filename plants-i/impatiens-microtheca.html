Impatiens microtheca

Impatiens microtheca, commonly known as the dwarf impatiens, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its small, upright growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its stems are often covered in fine, soft pubescence. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden beds.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
