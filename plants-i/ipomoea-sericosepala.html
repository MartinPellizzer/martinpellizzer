Ipomoea sericosepala

Ipomoea sericosepala, commonly known as the silky-sepal morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture, and its stems are hairy and climbing. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Ipomoea sericosepala is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is part of the Convolvulaceae family.
