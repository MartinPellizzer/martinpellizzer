Indigofera nigrescens

Indigofera nigrescens, commonly known as black indigo, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable in agricultural systems. Its seeds are used in traditional medicine and as a source of dye.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.
