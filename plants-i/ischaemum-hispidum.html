Ischaemum hispidum

Ischaemum hispidum, commonly known as hairy barnyardgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and hairy stems, which give it its common name. The plant produces spikelets that are often clustered at the nodes, with leathery, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

Ischaemum hispidum is an important weed in crops like rice and wheat, where it competes with desirable plants for nutrients and water.
