Ixia orientalis

Ixia orientalis, commonly known as the yellow fire lily, is a striking bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It features tall, slender stems that bear clusters of bright yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in late summer to early autumn, producing vibrant, showy inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming a basal rosette.

Ixia orientalis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.
