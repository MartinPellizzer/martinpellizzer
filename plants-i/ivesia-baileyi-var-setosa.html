Ivesia baileyi var. setosa

Ivesia baileyi var. setosa, commonly known as the Bailey's ivy-leaf or setose ivy-leaf, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and hairy, ovate leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in mountainous regions. Its stems are usually prostrate or ascending, and it produces white to pinkish flowers in late spring.

The species is notable for its adaptations to arid environments, including reduced leaf size and dense pubescence.
