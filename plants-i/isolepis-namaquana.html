Isolepis namaquana

Isolepis namaquana, commonly known as the Namibian flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the Cyperaceae family. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland dune environments.

Isolepis namaquana plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
