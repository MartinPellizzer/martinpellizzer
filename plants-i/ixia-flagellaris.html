Ixia flagellaris

Ixia flagellaris, commonly known as the flagellate lily, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that resemble lilies. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are usually pink or red, with a distinctive, elongated shape that gives it its common name.

Ixia flagellaris is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant colors and unique appearance.
