Iris albicans

Iris albicans, commonly known as the white iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, white or pale blue flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing intricate, six-petaled flowers that are highly ornamental. Its leaves are sword-shaped, narrow, and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its elegant appearance.

Iris albicans prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
