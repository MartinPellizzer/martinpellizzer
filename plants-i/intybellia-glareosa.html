Intybellia glareosa

Intybellia glareosa, commonly known as the glossy bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to blue and purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are usually glossy and broad, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Intybellia glareosa is often cultivated for its beauty and is valued in horticulture for its ability to thrive in partial shade.
