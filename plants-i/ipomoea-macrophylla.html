Ipomoea macrophylla

Ipomoea macrophylla, commonly known as the giant morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in diameter. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white, which bloom in the morning and close by midday. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Ipomoea macrophylla is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
