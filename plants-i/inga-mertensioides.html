Inga mertensioides

Inga mertensioides, commonly known as the West African velvet bean, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and showy, pendulous flowers. The plant produces pods containing edible seeds that are rich in protein and used in various cuisines. It also has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, enhancing soil fertility.

Its rapid growth makes it useful for agroforestry and soil conservation in degraded lands.
