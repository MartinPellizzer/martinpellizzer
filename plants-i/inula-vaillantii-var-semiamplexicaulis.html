Inula vaillantii var. semiamplexicaulis

Inula vaillantii var. semiamplexicaulis, commonly known as the semi-amplexicaulis elecampane, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and large, yellow composite flowers. The leaves are semi-amplexicaulis, meaning they partially clasping the stem, which is a distinctive botanical feature. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and grasslands.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
