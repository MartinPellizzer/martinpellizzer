Indigofera eriophylla

Indigofera eriophylla, commonly known as the velvetleaf indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria in its root nodules. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is cultivated in some regions for landscaping and soil enrichment.
