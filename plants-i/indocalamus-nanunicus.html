Indocalamus nanunicus

Indocalamus nanunicus, commonly known as the slender bamboo, is a species of grass belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like China, Vietnam, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that are often used in traditional crafts and as a source of fiber. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth texture and a pale green color.

Indocalamus nanunicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native habitats.
