Ipomoea imperati

Ipomoea imperati, commonly known as the imperial morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of purple, pink, and white. The plant is a climbing vine with heart-shaped leaves and produces fruits that are capsule-like in structure. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Ipomoea imperati is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
