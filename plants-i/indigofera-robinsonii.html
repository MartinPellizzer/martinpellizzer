Indigofera robinsonii

Indigofera robinsonii, commonly known as the Hawaiian indigo, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal and lowland areas, characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain seeds covered in a sticky, resinous substance. It is adapted to the tropical climate of Hawaii and plays a role in the local ecosystem.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
