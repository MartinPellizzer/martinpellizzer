Iguanura wallichiana var. malaccensis

Iguanura wallichiana var. malaccensis, commonly known as the Malacca hibiscus, is a flowering plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia, where it thrives in tropical rainforests. The plant is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from pink to red, with five petals and a prominent central stamen. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
