Ilex nitida var. repanda

Ilex nitida var. repanda, commonly known as the Bursera tree, is a variant of the holly genus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, glossy, dark green foliage and small, spiny leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that are followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in dry, rocky soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens.
