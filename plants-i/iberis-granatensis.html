Iberis granatensis

Iberis granatensis, commonly known as the granada candytuft, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant is characterized by its dense clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Its foliage consists of basal rosettes of narrow, grass-like leaves.

Iberis granatensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
