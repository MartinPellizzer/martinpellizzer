Inga vera subsp. lamprophylla

Inga vera subsp.

lamprophylla, commonly known as the silverleaf velvet tamarind, is a member of the legume family Fabaceae. It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits containing edible seeds.

The plant is native to tropical regions of South America and thrives in humid, warm climates. It has a shallow root system and is often used in agroforestry systems due to its nitrogen-fixing ability.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical and subtropical areas.
