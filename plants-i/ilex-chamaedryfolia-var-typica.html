Ilex chamaedryfolia var. typica

Ilex chamaedryfolia var. typica, commonly known as the evergreen holly, is a shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by red berries that are an important food source for birds. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its hardiness and ability to tolerate partial shade.
