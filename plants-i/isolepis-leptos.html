Isolepis leptos

Isolepis leptos, commonly known as slender spike-rush, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and hollow stems.

It typically grows in wetland environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often slightly curved, and its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, spike-like flowers. Isolepis leptos is known for its ability to thrive in saturated soils and is often used in ecological restoration projects.

Its botanical features make it an important indicator species for wetland health.
