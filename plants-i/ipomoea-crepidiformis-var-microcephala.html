Ipomoea crepidiformis var. microcephala

Ipomoea crepidiformis var. microcephala, commonly known as the small-headed morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its delicate, trumpet-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or purple. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is a member of the Convolvulaceae family.
