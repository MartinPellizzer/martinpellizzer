Impatiens lenta

Impatiens lenta, commonly known as the slow impatiens, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, spurred flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its stems are often covered in fine, pubescent hairs. Impatiens lenta thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens.

It is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
