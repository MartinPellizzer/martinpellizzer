Iberis tenorei

Iberis tenorei, commonly known as the Sicilian candytuft, is a flowering plant native to the island of Sicily.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant features small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in dense, spherical clusters. Its leaves are hairy and lobed, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Iberis tenorei is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
