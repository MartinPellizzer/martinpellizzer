Ichnanthus bacularius

Ichnanthus bacularius, commonly known as the sacred lotus of ancient Egypt, is a striking aquatic plant characterized by its large, showy flowers and broad, heart-shaped leaves.

It thrives in shallow, freshwater environments, often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds. The plant's roots are fibrous and help stabilize the surrounding soil, while its stems are long and flexible. Its flowers, which can be pink, white, or yellow, bloom in summer and are known for their fragrant scent.

The plant has been revered in various cultures for its symbolic and medicinal properties.
