Isodon repens

Isodon repens, commonly known as creeping gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features a spreading rhizome and produces blue to purple flowers in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Isodon repens is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
