Ichnocarpus caryophyllatus

Ichnocarpus caryophyllatus, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It belongs to the family Leguminosae and is characterized by its distinctive red to orange bark and woody, aromatic timber. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by flat, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in dry, sandy soils and is valued for its hard, durable wood used in traditional crafts and furniture.

The plant also has medicinal uses in some traditional systems of medicine.
