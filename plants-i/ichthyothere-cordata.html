Ichthyothere cordata

Ichthyothere cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf waterplant, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are typically glossy and arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale blue flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and wetland restoration projects.
