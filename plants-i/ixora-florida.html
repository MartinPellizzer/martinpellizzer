Ixora florida

Ixora florida, commonly known as the flame of the forest, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange-red flowers that bloom in dense clusters, creating a striking visual display. The plant has opposite, simple, and usually hairy leaves that are elliptical in shape. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sunlight.

Ixora florida is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its showy blooms and adaptability to various garden settings.
