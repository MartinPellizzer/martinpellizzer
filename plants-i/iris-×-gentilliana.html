Iris × gentilliana

Iris × gentilliana, commonly known as the German iris, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Iris germanica and Iris sibirica.

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems and showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and has lance-shaped, dark green leaves. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Iris × gentilliana is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and adaptability to various climates.
