Isoberlinia magnistipulata

Isoberlinia magnistipulata, commonly known as the giant stipule legume, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its large, compound leaves with prominent stipules that are often as long as the leaflets themselves. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by pods containing seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in savanna ecosystems.

The tree is valued for its timber and is also used in traditional medicine.
