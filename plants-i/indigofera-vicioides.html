Indigofera vicioides

Indigofera vicioides, commonly known as blue bean, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive blue or purple flowers. The plant is a shrub or small tree, typically growing up to 3 meters in height, with trifoliate leaves and compound fruit pods. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.

The plant's ability to fix nitrogen makes it beneficial for soil enrichment in agricultural settings.
