Ilex verticillata f. hodgdonii

Ilex verticillata f. hodgdonii, commonly known as the Hodgdon's winterberry, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and its striking red berries that persist through the winter. The leaves are alternate, oval to elliptic, and turn reddish-purple in the fall. The plant thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

It is valued in landscaping for its ornamental berries and seasonal color changes.
