Isoloma pycnosuzygium

Isoloma pycnosuzygium, commonly known as the Brazilian starfruit, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, star-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in various regions with suitable climates.
