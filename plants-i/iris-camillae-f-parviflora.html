Iris camillae f. parviflora

Iris camillae f. parviflora, commonly known as the dwarf camille iris, is a compact ornamental plant native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of blue and purple. The plant typically grows to a height of about 12 to 18 inches, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders. It prefers moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sun.

Its foliage remains evergreen throughout the year, adding visual interest even when not in bloom.
