Ipomoea denticulata

Ipomoea denticulata, commonly known as the serrated morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant is a climbing vine with tendrils that help it ascend supports. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Ipomoea denticulata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
