Inga malacophylla

Inga malacophylla, commonly known as the purple-leaf inga, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, violet-pink flowers. The plant is known for its rapid growth and is often found in humid lowland forests. Its fruit is a pod containing several seeds, which are dispersed by animals.

The leaves are often used in traditional medicine and for ornamental purposes due to their vibrant coloration.
