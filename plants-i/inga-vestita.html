Inga vestita

Inga vestita, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pea-like flowers. The plant produces small, round pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Its rapid growth and nitrogen-fixing ability make it valuable for soil enrichment and reforestation efforts.

The species is also appreciated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in various soil types.
