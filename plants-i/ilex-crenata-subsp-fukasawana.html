Ilex crenata subsp. fukasawana

Ilex crenata subsp.

fukasawana, commonly known as Japanese holly or Fukasawana holly, is a compact evergreen shrub native to Japan. It features small, glossy, dark green leaves with a slightly wavy or crenate edge, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers in late spring, followed by bright red berries that are attractive to birds. It is often used in horticulture for its dense foliage and ability to thrive in shaded environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping in temperate climates.
