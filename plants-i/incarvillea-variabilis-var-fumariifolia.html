Incarvillea variabilis var. fumariifolia

Incarvillea variabilis var. fumariifolia, commonly known as the smoky incarvillea, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from pale pink to deep red, often with a distinctive smoky or bluish tinge. The plant features lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and ecological gardens.
