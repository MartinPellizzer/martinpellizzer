Indigofera astragalina

Indigofera astragalina, commonly known as the slender indigo, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pale blue to violet. The plant typically grows in dry, arid environments and has a shrub-like structure with compound leaves. Its seeds are encased in pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant's ability to fix nitrogen makes it valuable for soil enrichment in agricultural settings.
