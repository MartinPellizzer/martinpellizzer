Ischaemum indicum var. guandongense

Ischaemum indicum var. guandongense, commonly known as the Guangdong goatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to eastern Asia.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands, riverbanks, and disturbed areas. The plant has slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its inflorescence consists of a loose panicle with small, two-lobed spikelets.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
