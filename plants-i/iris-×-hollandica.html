Iris × hollandica

Iris × hollandica, commonly known as the Dutch iris, is a hybrid perennial plant belonging to the Iridaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear showy, six-petaled flowers in a wide range of colors, including blue, purple, white, and yellow. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and prefers well-drained soil in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of sword-shaped leaves that are dark green and emerge in early spring.

Iris × hollandica is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and ease of care.
