Ixora leucantha var. malabarica

Ixora leucantha var. malabarica, commonly known as the Malabar flame bush, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is characterized by its dense, shrub-like growth habit and produces clusters of bright orange-red flowers. The leaves are simple, opposite, and elliptical with a slightly hairy surface. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
