Isodon leucophyllus

Isodon leucophyllus, commonly known as the white-leaf germander, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine white hairs, giving it its common name. It produces small, tubular flowers in shades of purple or blue, typically blooming in late summer.

Isodon leucophyllus is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional Chinese medicine.
