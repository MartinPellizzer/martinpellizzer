Ipomoea semisecta

Ipomoea semisecta, commonly known as the partial-leaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple, often appearing in the early morning. The leaves are uniquely divided, with a semi-lobed appearance that distinguishes it from other morning glory species. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The plant is also known for its rapid growth and ability to climb, making it a popular choice for trellises and garden structures.
