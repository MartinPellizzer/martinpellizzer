Indigofera sphenoides

Indigofera sphenoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features pinnately compound leaves with narrow, elongated leaflets. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences, often displaying shades of purple or violet.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its tolerance to drought conditions.
