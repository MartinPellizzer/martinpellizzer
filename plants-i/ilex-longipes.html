Ilex longipes

Ilex longipes, commonly known as the longpod holly, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters, followed by red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for birds. Ilex longipes thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in suitable climates.
