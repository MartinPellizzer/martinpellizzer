Isochilus prolifer

Isochilus prolifer, commonly known as the many-flowered orchid, is a species of flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of South America, particularly in countries like Peru and Ecuador. The plant is characterized by its compact, evergreen growth habit and produces numerous small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow. Its roots are adapted to grow in rocky or well-drained soils, and it thrives in cool, humid environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by horticultural enthusiasts.
