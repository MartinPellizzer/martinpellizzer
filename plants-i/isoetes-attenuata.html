Isoetes attenuata

Isoetes attenuata, commonly known as the slender water fern, is a member of the genus Isoetes within the order Isoetales.

It is a small, aquatic or semi-aquatic plant characterized by its finely divided, ribbon-like leaves that are often submerged in water. The plant produces spore-bearing structures called sporangia, which are typically located on the upper surface of the leaves. Isoetes attenuata is adapted to grow in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and wetlands.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important indicator species for aquatic ecosystems.
