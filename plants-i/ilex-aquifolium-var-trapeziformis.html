Ilex aquifolium var. trapeziformis

Ilex aquifolium var. trapeziformis, commonly known as the Trapeziform Holly, is a variant of the common holly native to Europe.

It is characterized by its distinctive, trapezoid-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green with spiny margins. The plant produces small, red berries that are typically consumed by birds, aiding in seed dispersal. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for ornamental landscaping.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and aesthetic appeal in gardens and natural settings.
