Isonandra petiolaris

Isonandra petiolaris, commonly known as the narrow-leafed sandmat, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Isonandra petiolaris plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for various desert organisms.
