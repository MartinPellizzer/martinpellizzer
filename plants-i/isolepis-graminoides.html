Isolepis graminoides

Isolepis graminoides, commonly known as the common spike-rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces dense, cylindrical inflorescences that contain small, clustered flowers, typical of the Cyperaceae family. Isolepis graminoides thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes, ponds, and along stream banks.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
