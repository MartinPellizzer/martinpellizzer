Ipomopsis aggregata subsp. arizonica

Ipomopsis aggregata subsp.

arizonica, commonly known as Arizona heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States. It is a perennial herb with striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has hairy, branched stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to local biodiversity.
