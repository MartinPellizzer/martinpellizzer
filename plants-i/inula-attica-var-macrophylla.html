Inula attica var. macrophylla

Inula attica var. macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaved elecampane, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, stout stems and large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow composite flowers arranged in a spherical inflorescence, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
