Iris fumosa

Iris fumosa, commonly known as the smoky iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features striking blue to purple flowers with a distinctive dark purple or blackish center. The plant has sword-shaped, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, often found in moist meadows and along stream banks.

The iris is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine by some indigenous communities.
