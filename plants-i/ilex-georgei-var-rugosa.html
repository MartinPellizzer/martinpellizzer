Ilex georgei var. rugosa

Ilex georgei var. rugosa, commonly known as the rugose holly, is a shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its leathery, elliptical leaves that are often reddish-brown underneath, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a suitable ornamental plant in gardens.

Its compact growth habit and seasonal color changes make it a popular choice for landscaping.
