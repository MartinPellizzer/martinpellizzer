Ixia bifolia

Ixia bifolia, commonly known as the two-leaved ixia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant typically grows from a bulb and produces two leaves during its growing season. Ixia bifolia thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms, which can persist for several weeks during the summer months.
