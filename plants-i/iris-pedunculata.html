Iris pedunculata

Iris pedunculata, commonly known as the slender iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features tall, upright stems that bear showy, violet-blue flowers with three upright petals and three drooping sepals. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a fan shape at the base. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental addition to gardens with suitable growing conditions.
