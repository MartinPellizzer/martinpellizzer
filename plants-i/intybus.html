Intybus

Intybus, commonly known as the greater burdock, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces purple to pink flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Intybus is native to Europe and Asia but has been naturalized in many parts of the world.
