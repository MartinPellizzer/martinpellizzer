Ipomoea coccinea var. luteola

Ipomoea coccinea var. luteola, commonly known as the yellow morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow to orange flowers that open in the morning and close in the evening. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves and has a trailing growth habit, making it suitable for hanging baskets or trellises. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

The flowers are not only ornamental but also attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
