Ipomoea caloxantha

Ipomoea caloxantha, commonly known as the purple morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces edible tubers similar to those of the common morning glory. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Ipomoea caloxantha is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine by some indigenous communities.
