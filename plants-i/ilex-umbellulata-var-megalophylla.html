Ilex umbellulata var. megalophylla

Ilex umbellulata var. megalophylla, commonly known as the Florida holly, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green, oval-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. The plant produces small, white, bell-shaped flowers in clusters, followed by red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for wildlife. Its botanical features include a dense, woody structure and a preference for well-drained, acidic soils.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and ecological significance in its native habitat.
