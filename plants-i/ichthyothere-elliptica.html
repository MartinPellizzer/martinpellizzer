Ichthyothere elliptica

Ichthyothere elliptica, commonly known as the water lily of the eastern wetlands, is a perennial aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It has broad, floating leaves that are typically oval or elliptical in shape, which help it to capture sunlight for photosynthesis. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom above the water surface, often at night. Its roots anchor it in the muddy substrate, while its rhizomes spread horizontally to form dense colonies.

Ichthyothere elliptica plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various water organisms.
