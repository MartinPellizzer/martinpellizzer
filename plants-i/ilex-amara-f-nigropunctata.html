Ilex amara f. nigropunctata

Ilex amara f. nigropunctata, commonly known as the bitter holly or black-spotted holly, is a shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves that are often dotted with small, black specks, giving it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, red berries that are toxic to humans but serve as a food source for certain wildlife. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist woodlands or along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local ecosystems.
