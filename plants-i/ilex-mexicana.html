Ilex mexicana

Ilex mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican holly, is a deciduous evergreen shrub or small tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Aquifoliaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that remain on the plant throughout the year. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for various bird species. Ilex mexicana typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
