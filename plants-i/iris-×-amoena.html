Iris × amoena

Iris × amoena, commonly known as the Dutch iris, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Iris germanica and Iris pallida.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in a variety of colors, including purple, blue, and white. The plant is native to the Netherlands and has been cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It typically blooms in late spring to early summer, and its foliage consists of broad, sword-like leaves.

Iris × amoena is a perennial herb that prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners.
