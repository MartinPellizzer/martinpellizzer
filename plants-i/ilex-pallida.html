Ilex pallida

Ilex pallida, commonly known as the paleleaf holly, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a pale green on the upper surface and whitish underneath. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red berries in the fall. These berries are an important food source for various bird species.

Ilex pallida is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
