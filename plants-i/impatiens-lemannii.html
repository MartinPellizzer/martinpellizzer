Impatiens lemannii

Impatiens lemannii, commonly known as the blue impatiens, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in clusters along the stems. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are usually hairy and have a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Impatiens lemannii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
