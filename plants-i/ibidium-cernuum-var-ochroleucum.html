Ibidium cernuum var. ochroleucum

Ibidium cernuum var. ochroleucum, commonly known as the yellow-leaved tamarack, is a variant of the tamarack plant characterized by its distinctive yellowish foliage.

It is a member of the Pinaceae family, which includes various coniferous trees and shrubs. This plant typically grows in wetland areas and is adapted to moist, acidic soils. Its leaves are needle-like and remain on the plant for several years before shedding.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
