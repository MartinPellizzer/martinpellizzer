Inula acervata

Inula acervata, commonly known as the clustered-inula, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a tall, branched stem with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its most distinctive feature is the spherical cluster of yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

Inula acervata is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to harsh, alpine environments.
