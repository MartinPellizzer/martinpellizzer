Indigofera serpentinicola

Indigofera serpentinicola, commonly known as the serpentine indigo, is a flowering plant native to rocky, nutrient-poor soils in tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is adapted to grow in serpentine soils, which are rich in heavy metals and low in essential nutrients. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.

Its unique ability to thrive in such harsh environments makes it an interesting subject for botanical research.
