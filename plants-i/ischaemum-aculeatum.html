Ischaemum aculeatum

Ischaemum aculeatum, commonly known as the prickly barnyard grass, is a species of annual grass native to Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

Ischaemum aculeatum is known for its ability to spread rapidly and is considered a weed in many regions.
