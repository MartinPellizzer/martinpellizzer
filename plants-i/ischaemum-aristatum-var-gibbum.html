Ischaemum aristatum var. gibbum

Ischaemum aristatum var. gibbum, commonly known as the woolly barnyard grass, is a grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, bristly leaves and stems, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and is often found in temperate climates. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are arranged in a panicle, with each spikelet bearing two to three bristles.

This variety is notable for its robust growth and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
