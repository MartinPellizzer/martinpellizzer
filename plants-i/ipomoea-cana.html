Ipomoea cana

Ipomoea cana, commonly known as the morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its trumpet-shaped, colorful flowers that bloom in the early morning and close by midday. The plant is a vigorous climber with heart-shaped leaves and produces long, twisting vines. It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Ipomoea cana is also known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
