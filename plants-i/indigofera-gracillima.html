Indigofera gracillima

Indigofera gracillima, commonly known as slender indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, pink or purple flowers. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its graceful appearance and ability to thrive in partial shade. It also has historical significance in traditional medicine and dye production.

Indigofera gracillima typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers a humid climate.
