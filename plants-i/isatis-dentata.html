Isatis dentata

Isatis dentata, commonly known as the toothed woad, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant is characterized by its serrated leaves and small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in early summer. It is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, thriving in moist, well-drained environments.

The plant has historical significance in dye production, particularly for producing blue pigments.
