Isolepis congrua

Isolepis congrua, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedgeland, is a perennial grass-like plant native to wetland environments in the Americas.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of flowers that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshes, bogs, and along the edges of ponds.

Isolepis congrua plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
