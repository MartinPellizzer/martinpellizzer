Indigofera homblei

Indigofera homblei, commonly known as the Hawaiian indigo, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to the tropical climate of Hawaii. It plays a role in local ecosystems and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.

However, it is considered an invasive species in some regions outside its native habitat.
