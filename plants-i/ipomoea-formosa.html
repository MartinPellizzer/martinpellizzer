Ipomoea formosa

Ipomoea formosa, commonly known as the sweet potato vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, colorful foliage, which can range from variegated green to red and purple hues. The plant produces clusters of small, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Ipomoea formosa is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
