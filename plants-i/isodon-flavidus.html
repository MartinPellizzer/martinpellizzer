Isodon flavidus

Isodon flavidus, commonly known as the yellow isodon, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic plants such as mint and sage. The plant is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, yellowish-green leaves with a distinctive aromatic scent. Its flowers are typically small and clustered in whorls, producing a mild, honey-like fragrance.

Isodon flavidus is valued in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties.
