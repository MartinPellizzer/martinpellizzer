Isatis cappadocica subsp. subradiata

Isatis cappadocica subsp.

subradiata, commonly known as the Cappadocian woad, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many flowering plants known for their distinctive four-petaled flowers.

The plant features basal leaves that are lanceolate and often covered in a fine pubescence, while its stems are erect and branched. Its flowers are typically blue or violet, with four petals arranged in a cross shape, typical of the Brassicaceae family.

The plant is known for its historical use in dye production, similar to its more widely recognized relative, Isatis tinctoria.
