Indigofera pendula

Indigofera pendula, commonly known as the weeping indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its pendulous clusters of small, pea-like flowers that bloom in shades of blue, purple, and white. The plant has compound leaves with multiple leaflets and produces slender, often drooping stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Indigofera pendula is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive appearance and ability to attract pollinators.
