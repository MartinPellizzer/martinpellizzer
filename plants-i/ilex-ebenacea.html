Ilex ebenacea

Ilex ebenacea, commonly known as the Cuban holly, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean, particularly Cuba and the Bahamas.

It belongs to the family Aquifoliaceae and is characterized by its dense, glossy, dark green leaves that are usually opposite in arrangement. The plant produces small, bright red berries that are an important food source for various bird species. Ilex ebenacea is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and year-round structure.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for tropical and subtropical landscapes.
