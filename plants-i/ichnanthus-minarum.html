Ichnanthus minarum

Ichnanthus minarum, commonly known as the slender rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks, where it thrives in well-drained soil. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are often slightly hairy, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are clustered in spikes and are usually greenish or reddish in color.

Ichnanthus minarum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates in its native ecosystems.
