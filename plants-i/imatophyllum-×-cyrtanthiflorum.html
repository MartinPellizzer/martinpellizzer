Imatophyllum × cyrtanthiflorum

Imatophyllum × cyrtanthiflorum, commonly known as the hybrid bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is known for its vibrant blue to purple hues. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

This hybrid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic beauty.
