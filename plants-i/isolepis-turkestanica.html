Isolepis turkestanica

Isolepis turkestanica, commonly known as the Turkestan sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It typically grows in wet meadows, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of flowers. It is characterized by its robust root system and ability to thrive in moist environments.

Isolepis turkestanica plays an important role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
