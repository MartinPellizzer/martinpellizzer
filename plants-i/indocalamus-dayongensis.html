Indocalamus dayongensis

Indocalamus dayongensis, commonly known as the Dayong bamboo, is a species native to China, particularly found in the Yunnan and Guizhou provinces.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are typically green on top and lighter in color on the underside.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported health benefits.
