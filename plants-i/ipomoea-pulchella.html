Ipomoea pulchella

Ipomoea pulchella, commonly known as the scarlet morning glory, is a herbaceous vine native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant blooms throughout the summer, typically from June to September, and is known for its rapid growth habit. Its flowers open in the morning and close in the afternoon, giving it its common name.

Ipomoea pulchella is often found in moist, open habitats such as fields and along stream banks.
