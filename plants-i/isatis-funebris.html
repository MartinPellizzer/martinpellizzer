Isatis funebris

Isatis funebris, commonly known as the black root or funereal cabbage, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is a biennial plant native to eastern North America, characterized by its dark purple to black roots and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its flowers are small and clustered in racemes, and it is known for its strong, pungent odor.

The plant has been historically used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
