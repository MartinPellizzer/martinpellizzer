Isonandra xanthochyma

Isonandra xanthochyma, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a vibrant display during the warmer months. The plant has a woody stem and broad, palmately lobed leaves that provide a lush green backdrop to its flamboyant blooms. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its rapid growth and ability to attract pollinators make it both aesthetically pleasing and ecologically valuable.
