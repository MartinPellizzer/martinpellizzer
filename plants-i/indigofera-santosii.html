Indigofera santosii

Indigofera santosii, commonly known as Santos' indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It is often found in open woodlands and grasslands, thriving in well-drained soils.

Santos' indigo is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
