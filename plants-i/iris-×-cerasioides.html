Iris × cerasioides

Iris × cerasioides, commonly known as the Persian iris, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Iris pallida and Iris sibirica.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and has narrow, grass-like leaves. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for gardens in temperate climates.

The flowers are often used in floral arrangements due to their elegant appearance and fragrance.
