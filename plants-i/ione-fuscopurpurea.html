Ione fuscopurpurea

Ione fuscopurpurea, commonly known as the purple-leafed iodine, is a species of flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking purple foliage and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and mountainous regions. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a waxy texture, and the flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

Ione fuscopurpurea is a hardy perennial that is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
