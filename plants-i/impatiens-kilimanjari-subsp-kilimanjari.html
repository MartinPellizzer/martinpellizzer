Impatiens kilimanjari subsp.

kilimanjari, commonly known as the Kilimanjaro impatiens, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of East Africa. It is characterized by its striking red or pink flowers, which bloom in clusters and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest undergrowth or on rocky slopes. Its leaves are ovate and have a velvety texture, while its stems are slender and often trailing.
