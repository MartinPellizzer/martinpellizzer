Impatiens nivea

Impatiens nivea, commonly known as the white impatiens, is a species native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, showy white flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a slight pinkish tinge. The plant typically grows as an erect herb, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters, and has opposite, ovate leaves with a hairy texture. Its flowers are tubular in shape and attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Impatiens nivea prefers shady, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.
