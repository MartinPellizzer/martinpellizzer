Isopogon sphaerocephalus subsp. lesueurensis

Isopogon sphaerocephalus subsp.

lesueurensis, commonly known as the Lesueur's honey bush, is a shrub native to Western Australia. It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its ability to thrive in well-drained sandy soils.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in cylindrical clusters, which are typically visited by nectar-feeding birds and insects. Its foliage consists of narrow, leathery leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and seasonal floral display.
