Iris unguicularis var. syriaca

Iris unguicularis var. syriaca, commonly known as the Syrian iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Middle East.

It features upright, sword-shaped leaves and produces showy, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive yellow beard on the lower petal. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.

The species is also noted for its historical significance in traditional medicine.
