Icacorea minima

Icacorea minima, commonly known as the small icacure, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern South America.

It typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters and features narrow, leathery leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. These flowers are followed by small, round berries that turn red when mature.

Icacorea minima is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable climates.
