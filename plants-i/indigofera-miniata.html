Indigofera miniata

Indigofera miniata, commonly known as the dwarf indigo.

It is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula. The plant is characterized by its small, delicate leaves and clusters of blue or purple flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its ornamental value and ability to fix nitrogen, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
