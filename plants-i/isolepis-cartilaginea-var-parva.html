Isolepis cartilaginea var. parva

Isolepis cartilaginea var. parva, commonly known as the small bladderwheat, is a compact, mat-forming grass-like plant native to wetlands and marshy areas.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences with tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. The plant is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and stems, which are adapted to waterlogged conditions. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in coastal or inland wetlands.

Its ecological role includes stabilizing soil and providing habitat for microorganisms and invertebrates.
