Inga hirsuta

Inga hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy inga, is a tropical leguminous tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with long, hairy stipules and small, clustered flowers that produce pods. The plant is often found in moist, shaded forest understories and is valued for its rapid growth and nitrogen-fixing abilities. Its bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.

The species is also an important component of agroforestry systems due to its ecological and economic benefits.
