Ixia crocea

Ixia crocea, commonly known as the orange lily, is a perennial bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It features striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, with a distinctive red or orange throat. The plant produces narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

Ixia crocea is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family, which includes other ornamental bulbous plants.
