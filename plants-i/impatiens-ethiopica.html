Impatiens ethiopica

Impatiens ethiopica, commonly known as the Ethiopian impatiens, is a flowering plant native to Ethiopia and parts of Eritrea.

It belongs to the genus Impatiens in the family Balsaminaceae, characterized by its distinctive seed dispersal mechanism. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and features vibrant, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often covered in a fine, waxy coating.

Impatiens ethiopica is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its role in local ecosystems.
