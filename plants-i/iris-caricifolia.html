Iris caricifolia

Iris caricifolia, commonly known as the swamp iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It features tall, slender stems that bear showy, blue to purple flowers with three petals and three sepals. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white down. Its rhizomatous root system helps stabilize soil in moist environments.

Iris caricifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting aquatic and wetland ecosystems.
