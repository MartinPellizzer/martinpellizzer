Indigofera phillipsiae

Indigofera phillipsiae, commonly known as the Philippine indigo, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
