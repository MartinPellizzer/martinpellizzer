Iris × latifolia

Iris × latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf iris, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Iris germanica and Iris pallida.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to deep purple, with a distinctive central beard. The plant features sword-shaped, grass-like leaves that are broad and sturdy, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of ponds.

Iris × latifolia is valued in gardens for its striking blooms and adaptability to various growing conditions.
