Ipomoea aemilii

Ipomoea aemilii, commonly known as the blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces tendrils that help it climb and spread. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Ipomoea aemilii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can be found in gardens and natural habitats.
