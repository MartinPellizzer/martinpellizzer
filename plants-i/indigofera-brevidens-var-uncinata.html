Indigofera brevidens var. uncinata

Indigofera brevidens var. uncinata, commonly known as the hookleaf indigo, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its small, trifoliate leaves and clusters of pink to purple flowers. The plant produces elliptical, woody pods containing lens-shaped seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open woodlands.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in dye production, it is cultivated in some horticultural settings.
