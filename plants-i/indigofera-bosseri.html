Indigofera bosseri

Indigofera bosseri, commonly known as the Bosser's indigo, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and striking blue to violet flowers. The plant is adapted to drought conditions, featuring deep roots and reduced leaf surfaces to minimize water loss. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, improving soil fertility in its native habitat.

Its ornamental value has led to its cultivation in some regions for landscaping purposes.
