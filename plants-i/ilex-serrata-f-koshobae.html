Ilex serrata f. koshobae

Ilex serrata f. koshobae, commonly known as the Koshobae holly, is a variant of the serrated holly native to Japan.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree characterized by its sharp, serrated leaves and small, dark berries. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as mountain forests. Its foliage provides year-round interest, with vibrant autumn colors adding to its ornamental value.

The species is valued in horticulture for its hardiness and adaptability to various soil types.
