Ipomoea obvallatus

Ipomoea obvallatus, commonly known as the scarlet morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from red to purple. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in warm climates and requires full sun to partial shade.

Ipomoea obvallatus is a climbing vine that can grow quite vigorously, making it suitable for trellises or as a ground cover.
