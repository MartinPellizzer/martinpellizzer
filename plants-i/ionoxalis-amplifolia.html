Ionoxalis amplifolia

Ionoxalis amplifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive violet coloration, often adorned with small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are composed of three broad, rounded lobes. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shaded areas.

Ionoxalis amplifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
