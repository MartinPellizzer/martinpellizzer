Inga bella

Inga bella, commonly known as the pink inga, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its rapid growth and dense foliage. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters. Inga bella is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.

Its adaptability to various soil types and climate conditions makes it a popular choice for landscaping in warm climates.
