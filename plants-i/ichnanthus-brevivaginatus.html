Ichnanthus brevivaginatus

Ichnanthus brevivaginatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved water plantain, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, often found in marshes, ponds, and along the edges of streams. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are usually arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its rhizomatous root system helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion in aquatic environments.

Ichnanthus brevivaginatus is an important component of wetland ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
