Indigofera fulgens

Indigofera fulgens, commonly known as the flame indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces elongated pods containing seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.

The plant's ability to fix nitrogen makes it beneficial for soil enrichment in agricultural settings.
