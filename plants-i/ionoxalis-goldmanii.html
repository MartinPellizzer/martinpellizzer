Ionoxalis goldmanii

Ionoxalis goldmanii, commonly known as the golden oxalis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its attractive, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant produces edible tubers that are rich in starch and have been used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sun exposure.

Ionoxalis goldmanii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant foliage and delicate blooms.
