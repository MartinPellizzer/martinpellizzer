Ipomoea huayllae

Ipomoea huayllae, commonly known as the Andean morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the highlands of southern Peru.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and twining stems that climb or trail along the ground. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or mountainous regions.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
