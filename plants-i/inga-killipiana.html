Inga killipiana

Inga killipiana, commonly known as the white inga, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy white flowers. The plant has a dense canopy and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.

Inga killipiana is valued for its ecological role in soil enrichment due to its nitrogen-fixing ability.
