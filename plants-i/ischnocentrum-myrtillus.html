Ischnocentrum myrtillus

Ischnocentrum myrtillus, commonly known as the red-flowered mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on various host trees in South America.

It possesses a succulent stem and produces small, fleshy leaves that help it absorb water and nutrients. The plant is notable for its bright red berries, which are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Its parasitic nature means it derives some of its nutrients from the host tree it attaches to.

Ischnocentrum myrtillus plays a significant ecological role in its native habitats, contributing to biodiversity and supporting animal populations.
