Inga monticola

Inga monticola, commonly known as the mountain inga, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pendulous flowers. The plant produces pod fruits that contain several seeds, and it has a deep root system that helps stabilize soil.

Inga monticola is often used in agroforestry systems due to its ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility.
