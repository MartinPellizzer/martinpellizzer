Inula conyza subsp. gutierrezii

Inula conyza subsp.

gutierrezii, commonly known as the Mexican fleabane, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and hairy, while the flowers are yellow with a central dark center.

This subspecies is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its delicate appearance.
