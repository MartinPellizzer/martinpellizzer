Imhofia marginata

Imhofia marginata, commonly known as the red-veined arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial with long, narrow, arrow-shaped leaves that have distinctive red veins. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in spikes. Its rhizomes are used as a starchy root vegetable, similar to taro.

The species is valued for both its ornamental appeal and culinary uses.
