Ione purpurata

Ione purpurata, commonly known as the purple poppy, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It has slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

Ione purpurata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color.
