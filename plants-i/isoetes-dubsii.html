Isoetes dubsii

Isoetes dubsii, commonly known as the Dub's watermoccasin, is a species of aquatic fern that thrives in freshwater environments.

It is characterized by its spore-bearing structures called sporangia, which are typically located on the underside of its leaves. The plant has a unique morphology with a central axis and specialized leaves that help it float and absorb nutrients from the water. It is native to regions with temperate climates and is often found in shallow, still waters.

Due to its ecological importance, it plays a role in maintaining water quality and supporting aquatic biodiversity.
