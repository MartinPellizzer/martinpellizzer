Indigofera anil var. orthocarpa

Indigofera anil var. orthocarpa, commonly known as Indian indigo, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant produces pods that contain dark blue seeds, which have been historically used to produce indigo dye. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.

This species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
