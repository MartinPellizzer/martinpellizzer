Isoglossa paucinervis

Isoglossa paucinervis, commonly known as the narrow-veined isoglossa, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems with opposite, lanceolate leaves that have few prominent veins. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are typically covered with fine hairs, and it prefers moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats.

Isoglossa paucinervis is often found in shaded areas and is considered a characteristic species of certain ecological communities.
