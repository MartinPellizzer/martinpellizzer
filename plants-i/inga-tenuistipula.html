Inga tenuistipula

Inga tenuistipula, commonly known as the small-leaved inga, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves with few or no leaflets. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by pods containing edible seeds.

It is often found in moist, shaded areas of tropical forests and is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches the soil.
