Iresine tomentella

Iresine tomentella, commonly known as the velvetleaf or woolly blusher, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage, which often displays shades of red, purple, and pink, especially under different light conditions. The plant has hairy, velvety leaves that give it a unique texture and help it retain moisture in arid environments. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically pink or red, and it thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil.

Iresine tomentella is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant and unusual leaf coloration.
