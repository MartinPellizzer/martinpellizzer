Iris sanguinea f. sericiflora

Iris sanguinea f. sericiflora, commonly known as the silky-flowered red iris, is a botanical variant of the red iris species.

It is characterized by its striking deep red to purple flowers with a distinctive white or cream-colored throat. The leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue. This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and wetland habitats.
