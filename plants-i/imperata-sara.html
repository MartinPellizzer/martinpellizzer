Imperata sara

Imperata sara, commonly known as the red bamboo grass, is a perennial grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that often exhibit a reddish hue. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that are typically reddish or purplish in color. Imperata sara thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in open, disturbed areas.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread rapidly, making it both a resilient and sometimes invasive species.
