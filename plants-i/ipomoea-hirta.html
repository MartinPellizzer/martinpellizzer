Ipomoea hirta

Ipomoea hirta, commonly known as hairy morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped, hairy leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue, violet, or white, blooming in the morning. It thrives in warm climates and is often found in moist, disturbed soils.

Ipomoea hirta is also known for its rapid growth and is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain areas.
