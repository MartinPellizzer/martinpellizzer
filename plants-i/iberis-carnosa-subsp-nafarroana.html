Iberis carnosa subsp. nafarroana

Iberis carnosa subsp.

nafarroana, commonly known as the Pyrenean candytuft, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Pyrenees region. It typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

The plant features dense clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Its foliage consists of rounded, lobed leaves that are covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
