Isoetes tenuissima

Isoetes tenuissima, commonly known as the narrow-leaved watermoss, is a species of aquatic plant belonging to the order Isoetales.

It is characterized by its small, ribbon-like leaves that are typically less than 1 centimeter in width. This plant is often found in shallow, still waters such as ponds and marshes, where it thrives in low-light conditions. Its spore-producing structures, called sporangia, are located at the base of the leaves, a feature common among members of the genus Isoetes.

The plant plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by contributing to water filtration and providing habitat for microorganisms.
