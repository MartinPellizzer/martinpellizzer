Impatiens tricaudata

Impatiens tricaudata, commonly known as the three-tailed impatiens, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive three-lobed leaves and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous annual or perennial, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance in its native habitats.
