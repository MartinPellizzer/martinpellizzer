Icacorea insularis

Icacorea insularis, commonly known as the island acacia, is a shrub native to the Caribbean islands.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in coastal and sandy soils, often found in dry, sunny environments.

Icacorea insularis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
