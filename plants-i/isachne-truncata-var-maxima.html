Isachne truncata var. maxima

Isachne truncata var. maxima, commonly known as the broad-leaf isachne, is a variant of the isachne grass species native to parts of Asia.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in moist or wetland environments, often found in marshes and along riverbanks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a distinctive truncate or notched appearance at the base. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, two-lobed flowers.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
