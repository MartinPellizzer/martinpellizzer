Inula britannica var. intermedia

Inula britannica var. intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate elecampane, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, flowering stems and large, yellow composite flowers. The plant has serrated, lance-shaped leaves and produces a milky sap that is often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and medicinal studies.
