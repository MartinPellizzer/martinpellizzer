Inula mariana var. falcata

Inula mariana var. falcata, commonly known as the Appalachian goldenroot, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant features a sturdy, branched stem with rough, hairy leaves that are lanceolate to oblanceolate in shape. Its yellow flowers, which bloom in late summer, are arranged in a cluster at the top of the stem.

The roots are often harvested for their use in traditional herbal medicine.
