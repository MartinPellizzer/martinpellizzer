Ipomoea coptica var. acuta

Ipomoea coptica var. acuta, commonly known as the sharpleaf morning glory, is a flowering vine native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, waxy leaves that have a pointed tip, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, blooming in the early morning. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
