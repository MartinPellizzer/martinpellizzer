Ipomoea triquetra

Ipomoea triquetra, commonly known as the triquetra morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet, often with a distinctive three-lobed shape. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and thrives in warm, humid climates. It is a climbing vine that can reach lengths of several meters, using its tendrils to ascend supports.

Ipomoea triquetra is also noted for its rapid growth and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens.
