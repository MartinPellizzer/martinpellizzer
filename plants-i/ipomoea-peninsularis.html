Ipomoea peninsularis

Ipomoea peninsularis, commonly known as the Florida morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows up to 2 meters tall, featuring heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of blue, violet, or white. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its rapid growth and attractive flowers make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening, though it can become invasive if not managed properly.
