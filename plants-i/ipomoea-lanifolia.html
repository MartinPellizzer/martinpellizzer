Ipomoea lanifolia

Ipomoea lanifolia, commonly known as the woolly morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple, often blooming in the morning. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded areas.

Ipomoea lanifolia is a climbing vine that can reach lengths of several meters, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
