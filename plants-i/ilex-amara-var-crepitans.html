Ilex amara var. crepitans

Ilex amara var. crepitans, commonly known as the bitter holly, is a variant of the evergreen holly species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, glossy, dark green leaves and red berries that persist through winter. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 15 feet. Its foliage is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and hardiness.

The berries are toxic to humans and livestock, making it important to handle with care.
