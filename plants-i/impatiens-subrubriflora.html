Impatiens subrubriflora

Impatiens subrubriflora, commonly known as the red-flowered impatiens, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, clustered flowers. The flowers are typically bright red or pink, with five petals and a distinctive spurred structure. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
