Iresine pulchella

Iresine pulchella, commonly known as the red leaf plant or red leaf iron plant, is a tropical perennial shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety, bi-colored leaves that display a mix of deep red and green hues. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. Iresine pulchella thrives in warm, humid environments and prefers well-draining soil.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant foliage and easy care requirements.
