Ilex ambigua var. montana

Ilex ambigua var. montana, commonly known as the mountain holly, is a shrub or small tree native to the high elevations of the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and small, dark berries that persist through winter. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments. Its leaves are evergreen, providing year-round cover, and it produces showy white flowers in the spring.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
