Isachne truncata var. cordata

Isachne truncata var. cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf isachne, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often slightly curled at the edges. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wet meadows.

Its botanical features make it an important component of ecosystems where it contributes to soil stabilization and provides habitat for small invertebrates.
