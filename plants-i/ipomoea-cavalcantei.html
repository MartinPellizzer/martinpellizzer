Ipomoea cavalcantei

Ipomoea cavalcantei, commonly known as the Brazilian morning glory, is a flowering vine native to South America.

It produces vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from purple to blue, often opening in the morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and is known for its rapid growth habit. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The seeds are enclosed in a fleshy, colorful capsule that splits open when mature.
