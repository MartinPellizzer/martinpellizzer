Inula acuminata

Inula acuminata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved elecampane, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features lanceolate, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

The plant has a long, fibrous root system that can reach depths of up to two meters.
