Iseilema maculatum

Iseilema maculatum, commonly known as the spotted begonia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Begoniaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, often bi-colored leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically white or pink in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant.

Its foliage is prized for its ornamental value, with patterns and colors that vary among species.
