Iris spuria subsp. subbarbata

Iris spuria subsp.

subbarbata, commonly known as the Barbed Iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in meadows and along riverbanks.

The plant features slender, upright stems with narrow, sword-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually pale blue or white, with a distinctive spur at the base of the petal.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
