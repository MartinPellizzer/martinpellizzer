Ipomoea nutans

Ipomoea nutans, commonly known as the morning glory, is a flowering vine native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped, violet-blue flowers that open in the early morning and close by midday. The plant thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade. Its rapid growth habit makes it suitable for trellises or as a ground cover.

Ipomoea nutans is also known for its ornamental value and has been cultivated for both decorative and medicinal purposes.
