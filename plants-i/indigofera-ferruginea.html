Indigofera ferruginea

Indigofera ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty indigo, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable in agricultural settings. It produces pods containing seeds that are often used in traditional medicine.

Its adaptability to various soil types makes it a resilient species in diverse environments.
