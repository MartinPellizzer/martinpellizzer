Ipomoea funicularis

Ipomoea funicularis, commonly known as the climbing morning glory, is a flowering vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet, typically blooming in the morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and a rapid growth habit, often climbing using its tendrils. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a woody stem that becomes more pronounced with age.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
