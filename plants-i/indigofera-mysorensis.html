Indigofera mysorensis

Indigofera mysorensis, commonly known as the Indian indigo plant, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has a well-developed root system that enables it to fix atmospheric nitrogen. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry deciduous forests.

The plant has historical significance in the production of natural indigo dye.
