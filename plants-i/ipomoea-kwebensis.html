Ipomoea kwebensis

Ipomoea kwebensis, commonly known as the Kwebeni morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet trumpet-shaped flowers that open in the early morning and close by midday. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or riverine habitats.

Ipomoea kwebensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for its vibrant floral display.
