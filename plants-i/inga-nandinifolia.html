Inga nandinifolia

Inga nandinifolia, commonly known as the yellow inga, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its fruit is a pod that contains several edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines.

The tree also plays an important ecological role in nitrogen fixation, enriching the soil in its native habitat.
