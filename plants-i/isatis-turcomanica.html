Isatis turcomanica

Isatis turcomanica, commonly known as Turkestan woad, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant produces small, yellow flowers and has heart-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. It is also valued for its potential dyeing properties, similar to its more famous relative, the woad plant.

Isatis turcomanica is typically found in arid and semi-arid regions, thriving in well-drained soils.
