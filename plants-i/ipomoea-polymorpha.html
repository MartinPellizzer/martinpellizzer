Ipomoea polymorpha

Ipomoea polymorpha, commonly known as the morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive spiral-shaped flowers that open in the morning and close in the evening. The plant is a vine with heart-shaped leaves and can grow up to several meters in length. It thrives in warm climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Ipomoea polymorpha is also known for its rapid growth and ability to climb various structures.
