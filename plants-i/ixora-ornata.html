Ixora ornata

Ixora ornata, commonly known as the coral tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its bright red or orange inflorescences, which resemble coral clusters, hence its common name. The plant has opposite, simple, and usually elliptical leaves that are glossy in appearance. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in coastal areas or near water.

Ixora ornata is widely cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a street tree due to its vibrant blooms and adaptability.
