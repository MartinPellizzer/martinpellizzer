Ipomoea maranyonensis

Ipomoea maranyonensis, commonly known as the Maranon morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly the Amazon region.

It is a climbing vine that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of purple and blue. The plant belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and rapid growth habit. Its flowers open in the early morning and close by midday, hence its common name.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
