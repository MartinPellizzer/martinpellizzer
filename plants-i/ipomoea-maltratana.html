Ipomoea maltratana

Ipomoea maltratana, commonly known as the Maltratana morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in the early morning, hence its common name. The plant is a herbaceous vine that can climb using its twining stems. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a velvety texture.

The flowers are typically short-lived, opening in the morning and closing by midday.
