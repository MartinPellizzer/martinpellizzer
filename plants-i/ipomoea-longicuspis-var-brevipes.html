Ipomoea longicuspis var. brevipes

Ipomoea longicuspis var. brevipes, commonly known as the short-stemmed morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces violet to blue flowers that open in the morning and close in the evening. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often covered in fine hairs.

This variant is distinguished by its shorter stems and smaller flowers compared to other varieties within the species.
