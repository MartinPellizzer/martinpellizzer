Incarvillea sinensis subsp. variabilis

Incarvillea sinensis subsp.

variabilis, commonly known as the variable Chinese bellflower, is a perennial herb native to China. It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers.

The plant features hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves, which are typical of the genus Incarvillea. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
