Icica glabra

Icica glabra, commonly known as the smoothleaf icica, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its finely divided, aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the family. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Icica glabra thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.
