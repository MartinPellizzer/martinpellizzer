Isinia laristanica

Isinia laristanica, commonly known as the Laristan thistle, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Iran.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh climatic conditions. Its leaves are usually spiny, providing a defense mechanism against herbivores.

Isinia laristanica is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological role and potential medicinal properties.
