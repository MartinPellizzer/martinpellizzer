Inga caripensis

Inga caripensis, commonly known as the "chocolate tree," is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and rapid growth rate. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that develop into edible pods containing seeds. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine, and the tree is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.

Inga caripensis also plays a role in nitrogen fixation, improving soil fertility in its native habitats.
