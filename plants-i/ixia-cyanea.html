Ixia cyanea

Ixia cyanea, commonly known as the blue ox-eye, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllis family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant features strap-like leaves and produces showy, cup-shaped blooms that attract pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Ixia cyanea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in temperate climates.
