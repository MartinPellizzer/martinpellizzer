Ipomoea sidamensis

Ipomoea sidamensis, commonly known as the Sidamensis morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and is often found growing along riverbanks and in moist, shaded areas. Its vines can climb and twine around supports, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

The species is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
