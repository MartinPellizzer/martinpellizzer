Ipomoea viridis

Ipomoea viridis, commonly known as the green morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, pale green flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant is a climbing vine with a rapid growth rate, often found in moist, shaded environments. Its stems are hairy and can reach lengths of several meters.

Ipomoea viridis is also known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it a common weed in agricultural and garden settings.
