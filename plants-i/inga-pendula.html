Inga pendula

Inga pendula, commonly known as the weeping inga, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its drooping, pendulous flowers that hang in clusters, giving it its common name. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by elongated, woody pods containing edible seeds.

Inga pendula is often found in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance.
