Inga spectabilis

Inga spectabilis, commonly known as the pink shower tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking pink to reddish flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a dramatic visual display. The plant has compound leaves with long, slender leaflets and produces small, round, yellow fruits that are edible. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often used in ornamental horticulture for its aesthetic value.

The tree also plays a role in supporting local wildlife and can be used in agroforestry systems.
