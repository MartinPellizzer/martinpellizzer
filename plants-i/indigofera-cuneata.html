Indigofera cuneata

Indigofera cuneata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf indigo, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a notable species in botanical studies.
