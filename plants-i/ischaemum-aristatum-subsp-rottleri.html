Ischaemum aristatum subsp. rottleri

Ischaemum aristatum subsp.

rottleri, commonly known as the slender beakgrass, is a grass species native to southern Africa. It typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils, often in regions with a Mediterranean climate.

The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are arranged in panicles, with each spikelet bearing two to three flowering branches.

The species is notable for its ecological adaptability and is often found in areas with seasonal rainfall patterns.
