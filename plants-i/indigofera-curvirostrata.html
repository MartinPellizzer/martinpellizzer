Indigofera curvirostrata

Indigofera curvirostrata, commonly known as the narrow-beaked indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive, narrow, curved seed pods. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color.

It is often found in dry, open habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential ecological benefits.
