Indigofera formosana

Indigofera formosana, commonly known as the Taiwan indigo plant, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Its ornamental value and ecological benefits make it a notable species in horticulture and ecological restoration.
