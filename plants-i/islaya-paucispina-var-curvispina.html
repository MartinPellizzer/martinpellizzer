Islaya paucispina var. curvispina

Islaya paucispina var. curvispina, commonly known as the narrow-leaved islaya, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and clusters of yellowish-white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, adapting to the harsh conditions of its native habitat. Its flowers are primarily pollinated by insects, and it produces small, dry fruits that contain seeds.

This variety is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
