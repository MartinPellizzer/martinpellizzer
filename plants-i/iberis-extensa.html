Iberis extensa

Iberis extensa, commonly known as the broadleaf candytuft, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that typically reaches a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are broad and lobed, with a slightly hairy texture.

Iberis extensa is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
