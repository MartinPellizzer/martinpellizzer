Isachne ascendens

Isachne ascendens, commonly known as the ascending isachne, is a perennial grass species native to various regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in a dense, tufted growth habit. The plant produces inflorescences that are spike-like, bearing tiny, two-lobed flowers that are often pale green to brown in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Due to its adaptability and ornamental appeal, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens or used in ecological restoration projects.
