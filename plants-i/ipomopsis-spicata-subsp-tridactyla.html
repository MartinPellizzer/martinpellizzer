Ipomopsis spicata subsp. tridactyla

Ipomopsis spicata subsp.

tridactyla, commonly known as the three-fingered blazingstar, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It belongs to the evening primrose family (Onagraceae) and is characterized by its striking, tubular, violet to purple flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its unique, three-lobed bracts that resemble fingers, giving it its common name. It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and produces capsule fruits containing numerous small seeds.

The plant is known for its showy blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and moths.
