Iberis dunalii

Iberis dunalii, commonly known as the Dunal's candytuft, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant features basal leaves that are typically woolly or hairy, providing a distinctive texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Iberis dunalii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
