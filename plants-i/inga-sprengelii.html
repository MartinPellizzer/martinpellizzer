Inga sprengelii

Inga sprengelii, commonly known as the Brazilian bean or yellow inga, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.

Its rapid growth and nitrogen-fixing ability make it valuable for soil enrichment in agricultural settings.
