Ixora clerodendron

Ixora clerodendron, commonly known as the flame of the forest, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its bright red or orange inflorescences, which resemble flames and bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers are not only visually striking but also attract pollinators such as bees and birds.
