Iris aphylla subsp. polonica

Iris aphylla subsp.

polonica, commonly known as the Polish marsh iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Europe. It typically grows in wet meadows and marshy areas, preferring moist, nutrient-rich soils.

The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces showy, violet-blue flowers with three upright petals. Its inflorescence consists of a single spike of flowers, which bloom in early summer.

The subspecies is characterized by its distinct floral structure and adaptation to wetland environments.
