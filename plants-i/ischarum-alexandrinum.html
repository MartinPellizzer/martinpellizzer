Ischarum alexandrinum

Ischarum alexandrinum, commonly known as the Alexandrian thistle, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in dry, rocky habitats. The plant features spiny leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer. It has been used traditionally in herbal medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory properties.

Its ecological role includes providing food for certain insect species and stabilizing soil in arid environments.
