Icacina grandifolia

Icacina grandifolia, commonly known as the giant flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. The plant has compound leaves composed of several elliptical leaflets, and it produces woody, spherical fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in humid climates and is often found in lowland forests or along riverbanks.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and has also been used in traditional medicine.
