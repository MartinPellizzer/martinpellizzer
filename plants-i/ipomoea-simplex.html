Ipomoea simplex

Ipomoea simplex, commonly known as the common morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its trumpet-shaped, showy flowers that typically bloom in the morning and close in the afternoon. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and can grow as a vine or a shrub, depending on the environment. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Ipomoea simplex is also known for its rapid growth and is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.
