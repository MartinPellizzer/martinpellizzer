Inga tapinhuana

Inga tapinhuana, commonly known as the "sweet pepper tree," is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, edible pods that contain seeds, and its roots have a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria.

Inga tapinhuana is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits in its native habitats.
