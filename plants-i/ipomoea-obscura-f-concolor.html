Ipomoea obscura f. concolor

Ipomoea obscura f. concolor, commonly known as the concolor morning glory, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, dark green leaves and trumpet-shaped, violet to purple flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous elliptical, black seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
