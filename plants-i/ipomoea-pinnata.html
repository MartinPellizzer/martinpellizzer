Ipomoea pinnata

Ipomoea pinnata, commonly known as the "Morning Glory" or "Ipomoea Pinnata," is a tropical vine native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of purple, pink, and white. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Ipomoea pinnata is also known for its rapid growth and ability to climb various structures.
