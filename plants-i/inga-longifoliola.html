Inga longifoliola

Inga longifoliola, commonly known as the long-leaved inga, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features compound leaves with elongated, lanceolate leaflets, giving it its common name. Its flowers are typically small and clustered in racemes, often displaying a range of colors from white to pink.

Inga longifoliola is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
