Iboza urticifolia

Iboza urticifolia, commonly known as the stinging nettle plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to various regions in Africa.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family and is characterized by its hollow, green stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant is notable for its stinging hairs, which contain irritants that cause skin irritation upon contact. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Iboza urticifolia has traditional medicinal uses and is sometimes used in local remedies.
