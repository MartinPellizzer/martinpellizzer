Isolepis laciniata

Isolepis laciniata, commonly known as the cut-leaved tussock sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams, thriving in moist to water-saturated soils. The plant features a dense, cylindrical cluster of grass-like leaves that are finely dissected, giving it a feathery appearance. Its flowering spikes are usually borne on tall, slender stems and produce small, inconspicuous flowers.

Isolepis laciniata plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
