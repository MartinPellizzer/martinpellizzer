Inga cecropietorum

Inga cecropietorum, commonly known as the pink inga, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces long, slender pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
