Isodon japonicus var. japonicus

Isodon japonicus var. japonicus, commonly known as Japanese honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal medicine.
