Iris × shelfordensis

Iris × shelfordensis, commonly known as the Shelford iris, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Iris germanica and Iris pallida.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring sword-shaped, grass-like leaves. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive three-parted structure, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
