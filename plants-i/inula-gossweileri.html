Inula gossweileri

Inula gossweileri, commonly known as the Alpine Inula, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and cool climates. Its yellow flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Inula gossweileri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
