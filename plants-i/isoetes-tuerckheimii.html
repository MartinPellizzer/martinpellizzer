Isoetes tuerckheimii

Isoetes tuerckheimii, commonly known as the Tuerckheim's water clover, is a species of aquatic fern that belongs to the genus Isoetes.

It is characterized by its unique, spore-bearing structures called sporangia, which are arranged in a rosette-like pattern along the stem. This plant typically grows in shallow, still waters such as ponds and lakes, where it can anchor itself in the sediment. Its leaves are usually narrow and ribbon-like, and they play a role in photosynthesis while also providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in low-nutrient environments, making it an indicator species for certain water conditions.
