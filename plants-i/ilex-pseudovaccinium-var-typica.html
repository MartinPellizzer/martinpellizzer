Ilex pseudovaccinium var. typica

Ilex pseudovaccinium var. typica, commonly known as the Florida holly, is a shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically alternate and have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands and pine flatwoods.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in its native habitat.
