Ipomoea bonariensis f. villicaulis

Ipomoea bonariensis f. villicaulis, commonly known as the velvet leaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy blue or violet flowers that bloom in the morning. The leaves are thick, velvety, and often have a striking dark green color with a slightly hairy texture. This variant is a popular ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and rapid growth habit.

It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
