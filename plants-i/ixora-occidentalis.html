Ixora occidentalis

Ixora occidentalis, commonly known as the West Indian ixora, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 3 meters in height, featuring dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces clusters of bright red or orange flowers, which are highly attractive to birds and insects. Its flowers are borne on short, branched inflorescences and bloom throughout the year in warm climates.

Ixora occidentalis is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant color and hardy nature.
