Ilex aquifolium var. platyphylla

Ilex aquifolium var. platyphylla, commonly known as the broad-leaved holly, is a variant of the common holly native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, leathery leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for ornamental planting in gardens.

This variety is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and hardy nature.
