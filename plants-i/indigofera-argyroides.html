Indigofera argyroides

Indigofera argyroides, commonly known as silverleaf indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and trifoliate leaves with a distinctive silver or grayish underside. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pale blue or white, arranged in clusters. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions, making it a hardy and adaptable plant.
