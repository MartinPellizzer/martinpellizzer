Indigofera bungeana f. spinescens

Indigofera bungeana f. spinescens, commonly known as the spiny indigo, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and spiny leaves, which serve as a defense mechanism against herbivores. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and forage for certain wildlife species.
