Iberis hirsuta

Iberis hirsuta, commonly known as hairy candytuft, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its fragrant blossoms that bloom in early spring.
