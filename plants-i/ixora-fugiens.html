Ixora fugiens

Ixora fugiens, commonly known as the flame bush, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, shrub-like growth habit and is often cultivated for its vibrant, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, bright orange to red flowers that bloom throughout the year, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and have a glossy surface, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Ixora fugiens is relatively drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
