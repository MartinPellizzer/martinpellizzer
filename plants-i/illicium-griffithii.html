Illicium griffithii

Illicium griffithii, commonly known as the Griffith's star anise, is a deciduous shrub native to the Himalayas.

It belongs to the family Schisandraceae and is characterized by its aromatic, star-shaped fruits that resemble those of the star anise. The plant produces small, white flowers that are often clustered in panicles, followed by the distinctive, fragrant berries. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Illicium griffithii is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for its antispasmodic and carminative effects.
