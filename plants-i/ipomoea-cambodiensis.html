Ipomoea cambodiensis

Ipomoea cambodiensis, commonly known as the Cambodian morning glory, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, blooming in the early morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a slightly waxy texture and is often found in moist, shaded environments. Its stems are hairy and can grow quite long, allowing it to climb trees and other structures.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some regions.
