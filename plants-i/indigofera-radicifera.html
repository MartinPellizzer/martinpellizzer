Indigofera radicifera

Indigofera radicifera, commonly known as the root indigo plant, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is notable for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria in its root nodules. Its roots are used in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dyes.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.
