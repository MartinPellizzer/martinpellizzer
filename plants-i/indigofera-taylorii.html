Indigofera taylorii

Indigofera taylorii, commonly known as Taylor's indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is often found in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in humid climates. It is noted for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.

However, it is not commonly cultivated for commercial purposes.
