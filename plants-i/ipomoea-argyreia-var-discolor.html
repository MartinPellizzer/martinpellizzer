Ipomoea argyreia var. discolor

Ipomoea argyreia var. discolor, commonly known as the blue morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range from pale blue to white, often with a contrasting purple or red throat. The plant produces heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are typically a deep green. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its rapid growth and ornamental appeal, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
