Iris assadiana

Iris assadiana, commonly known as the Assadian iris, is a species native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb with striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and a rhizomatous root system. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water sources.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and botanical collections.
