Incarvillea argyi

Incarvillea argyi, commonly known as the Chinese milkvetch, is a perennial flowering plant native to China.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and vibrant yellow or orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has pinnately compound leaves and produces cylindrical seed pods that split open when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Botanically, it belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
