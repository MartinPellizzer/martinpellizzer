Inula vorossii

Inula vorossii, commonly known as the Voross's elecampane, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. Its yellow flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant's medicinal properties have been studied for their potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects.
