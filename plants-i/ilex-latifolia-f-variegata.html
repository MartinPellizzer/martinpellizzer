Ilex latifolia f. variegata

Ilex latifolia f. variegata, commonly known as the Variegated Holly, is a compact evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with distinctive white or yellow variegation patterns. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans. It thrives in partial to full shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Its compact growth habit and attractive foliage make it a valuable addition to gardens and landscapes.
