Icicopsis insignis

Icicopsis insignis, commonly known as the Antarctic haircap, is a moss species native to the cold, high-altitude regions of the Southern Hemisphere.

It thrives in harsh environments such as Antarctica, South America, and southern Africa, where it forms dense, cushion-like growths. The plant has a distinctive appearance with closely packed, hair-like leaves that are often covered in a thin layer of ice or frost. Its reproductive structures, known as capsules, are typically borne on short stalks and release spores into the air.

Icicopsis insignis plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and supporting microbial life in its extreme habitat.
