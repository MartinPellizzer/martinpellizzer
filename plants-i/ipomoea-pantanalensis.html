Ipomoea pantanalensis

Ipomoea pantanalensis, commonly known as the Pantanal morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly the Pantanal region of Brazil.

It is a herbaceous vine that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink and purple. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is known for its rapid growth habit. It thrives in moist, tropical environments and is often found near water bodies.

The flowers bloom in the early morning, hence its common name, and attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
