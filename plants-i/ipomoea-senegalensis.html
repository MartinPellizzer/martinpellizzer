Ipomoea senegalensis

Ipomoea senegalensis, commonly known as the African morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its rapid growth and large, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces edible tubers that are rich in nutrients. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and abandoned fields.

Its botanical features make it both a useful crop and an ornamental plant in various regions.
