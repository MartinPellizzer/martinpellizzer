Impatiens brachycentra

Impatiens brachycentra, commonly known as the narrow-leaved impatiens, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The leaves are typically narrow and ovate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. This plant prefers moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
