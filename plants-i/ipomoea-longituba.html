Ipomoea longituba

Ipomoea longituba, commonly known as the long-tube morning glory, is a climbing vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant produces long, slender seed pods that contain numerous small, dark seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Ipomoea longituba is a member of the Convolvulaceae family, which includes many other species of morning glories.
