Ipomoea delpierrei

Ipomoea delpierrei, commonly known as the Delpierre's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant is a vine that can grow up to several meters in length, with heart-shaped leaves that are typically green and slightly hairy. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Ipomoea delpierrei is cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some parts of its native range.
