Indigofera angustiloba

Indigofera angustiloba, commonly known as narrowleaf indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is typically shrub-like, with woody stems and can grow up to several meters in height. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas or disturbed habitats.

Its flowers are usually blue or purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
