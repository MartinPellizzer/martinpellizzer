Indigofera marmorata

Indigofera marmorata, commonly known as the marble-stemmed indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its striking, veined, and often variegated leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique foliage.

Indigofera marmorata prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for tropical and subtropical gardens.
