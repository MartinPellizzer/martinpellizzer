Iris citrina

Iris citrina, commonly known as the yellow flag iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, showy flowers that typically range from yellow to orange in color. The plant has narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are arranged in a fan-like pattern at the base. It thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, stream banks, and damp woodlands.

Iris citrina is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has also been used in traditional medicine.
