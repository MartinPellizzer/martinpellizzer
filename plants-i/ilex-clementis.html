Ilex clementis

Ilex clementis, commonly known as the Spanish Holly, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically oval in shape and have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red berries that are an important food source for birds. Ilex clementis thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Its hardy nature and attractive appearance make it a valuable addition to gardens and natural habitats.
