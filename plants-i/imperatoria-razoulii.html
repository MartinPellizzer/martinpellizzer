Imperatoria razoulii

Imperatoria razoulii, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It is a low-growing herb with thick, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in harsh environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by fluffy, seed-filled capsules that disperse easily by wind. Imperatoria razoulii is adapted to survive in extreme drought conditions, with a deep root system and a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss.

It is often used in traditional medicine and is considered an important part of the local flora in its native habitats.
