Irvingia longipedicellata

Irvingia longipedicellata, commonly known as the African butter fruit, is a deciduous tree native to western Africa.

It belongs to the family Irvingiaceae and is characterized by its large, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by edible, fleshy fruits. The fruit's pulp is rich in oils and has been traditionally used for food and medicinal purposes.

Irvingia longipedicellata is also valued for its potential in sustainable agriculture and biodiversity conservation.
