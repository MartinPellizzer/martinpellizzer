Ischaemum pallidum

Ischaemum pallidum, commonly known as pale oatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows, ditches, and woodland edges. The plant has slender, erect stems that bear small, pale green leaves with parallel veins. Its inflorescence consists of a spikelet cluster, with each spikelet containing two to three small, two-lobed flowers.

Ischaemum pallidum is characterized by its delicate appearance and is often found in disturbed or semi-natural environments.
