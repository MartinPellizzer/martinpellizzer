Indigofera erythrogrammoides

Indigofera erythrogrammoides, commonly known as the red-stemmed indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, particularly India and Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its slender, often reddish stems and trifoliate leaves with small, pointed leaflets. It produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, followed by pods containing lens-shaped seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
