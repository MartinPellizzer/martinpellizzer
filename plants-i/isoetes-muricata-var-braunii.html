Isoetes muricata var. braunii

Isoetes muricata var. braunii, commonly known as the Braun's water lily, is a species of aquatic plant belonging to the family Isoetaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, ribbon-like leaves that are often submerged or floating on the water's surface. The plant typically grows in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and wetlands. Its spore-producing structures, called sporangia, are clustered at the base of the leaves, contributing to its reproductive strategy.

This variety is notable for its ecological importance in maintaining water quality and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
