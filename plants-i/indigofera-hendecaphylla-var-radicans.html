Indigofera hendecaphylla var. radicans

Indigofera hendecaphylla var. radicans, commonly known as the creeping indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its trailing growth habit and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers in shades of pink and purple, which bloom in clusters. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with a distinctive, slightly hairy texture.

This species is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dyes.
