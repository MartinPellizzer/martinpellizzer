Iris variegata var. variegata

Iris variegata var. variegata, commonly known as the variegated iris, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features striking, sword-shaped leaves with distinctive variegation patterns of white and green. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers on tall, slender stems during late spring to early summer. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its unique foliage and delicate floral display.
