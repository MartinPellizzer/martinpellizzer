Ipomoea coscinosperma var. glabra

Ipomoea coscinosperma var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in the morning and close by midday. The plant has heart-shaped, palmately lobed leaves and produces edible tubers that are sometimes consumed in traditional cuisines. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The smooth glabrous surface of its stems and leaves distinguishes this variant from other related species.
