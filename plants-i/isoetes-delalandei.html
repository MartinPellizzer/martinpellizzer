Isoetes delalandei

Isoetes delalandei, commonly known as the Delaland's watermace, is a species of aquatic fern belonging to the order Isoetales.

It is characterized by its dense, spore-bearing spikes that protrude from the water's surface, a feature typical of the genus Isoetes. The plant thrives in freshwater environments such as lakes and ponds, where it anchors itself to the substrate with its rhizomes. Its spores are released into the water, allowing for dispersal and colonization of new habitats.

Due to its specialized aquatic lifestyle, Isoetes delalandei is often found in regions with stable, shallow water conditions.
