Indigofera forrestii

Indigofera forrestii, commonly known as the African indigo or Forest indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in agricultural systems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.

Its seeds are encased in pods and can be used for various traditional purposes.
