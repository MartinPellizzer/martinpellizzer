Inula aurantiaca

Inula aurantiaca, commonly known as the orange hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange composite flower heads, which are composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and rocky soils, preferring full sun exposure. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly rough texture.

Inula aurantiaca is a perennial species that blooms from late summer to early autumn.
