Inga paraensis

Inga paraensis, commonly known as the Amazonian coffee tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant is notable for its ability to thrive in shaded environments, making it a common understory species. Its flowers are small and clustered, often producing a sweet scent to attract pollinators.

The seeds are enclosed in elongated pods, which split open to release the seeds when mature.
