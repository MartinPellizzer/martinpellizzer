Ilex ambigua f. channellii

Ilex ambigua f. channellii, commonly known as the Channell's holly, is a compact evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features glossy, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter green underneath. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, followed by red berries that are attractive to birds. It has a dense, rounded growth habit and is often used as a decorative hedge or foundation plant.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is a popular choice for landscaping in USDA hardiness zones 7 through 9.
