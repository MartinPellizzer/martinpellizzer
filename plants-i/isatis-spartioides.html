Isatis spartioides

Isatis spartioides, commonly known as the yellow flag or false flax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, and it produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Isatis spartioides is typically found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to withstand arid conditions.

It is sometimes used in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dyes.
