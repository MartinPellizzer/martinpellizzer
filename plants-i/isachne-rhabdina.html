Isachne rhabdina

Isachne rhabdina, commonly known as the narrow-leaf isachne, is a grass species native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, spike-like inflorescence contains numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

Isachne rhabdina is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in humid conditions.
