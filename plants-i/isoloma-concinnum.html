Isoloma concinnum

Isoloma concinnum, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a herbaceous plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and vibrant orange to red flower heads, which are composed of numerous small, tubular florets. The plant has alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy and covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Isoloma concinnum is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
