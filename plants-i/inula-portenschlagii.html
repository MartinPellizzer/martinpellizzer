Inula portenschlagii

Inula portenschlagii, commonly known as the Tibetan Inula, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its tall, branched stems and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, aiding in water retention in its arid environment. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Inula portenschlagii is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
