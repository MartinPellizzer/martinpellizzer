Ipomoea akoensis

Ipomoea akoensis, commonly known as the Akoense morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The plant typically grows as a climbing vine, with heart-shaped leaves and a rapid growth rate. Its flowers open in the morning and close by midday, hence its common name.

Ipomoea akoensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
