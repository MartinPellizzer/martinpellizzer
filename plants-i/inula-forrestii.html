Inula forrestii

Inula forrestii, commonly known as the Chinese elecampane, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and yellow composite flowers. The plant produces a distinctive, aromatic scent, which is often described as similar to that of a dandelion. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its rugged appearance.

Inula forrestii is often found in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests and woodland edges.
