Ipo saccidora

Ipo saccidora, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers followed by triangular, woody fruits containing three seeds. It is often found in disturbed habitats such as agricultural fields and roadsides.

Due to its rapid growth and ability to thrive in various soil types, it is considered a weed in many areas.
