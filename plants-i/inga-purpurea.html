Inga purpurea

Inga purpurea, commonly known as the purple inga, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, violet-pink flowers. The plant produces elongated, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in moist, shaded environments within tropical rainforests.

Its rapid growth and nitrogen-fixing ability make it ecologically significant in forest ecosystems.
