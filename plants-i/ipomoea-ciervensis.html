Ipomoea ciervensis

Ipomoea ciervensis, commonly known as the Sierra Madre morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces vibrant blue to purple blooms that open in the morning and close in the evening. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its vines can grow up to several meters in length.

Ipomoea ciervensis is often found in rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
