Ixora carnea

Ixora carnea, commonly known as the flame-of-the-forest, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its bright red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a striking appearance. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are often glossy and dark green. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Ixora carnea is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dyes.
