Iris florentina var. illyrica

Iris florentina var. illyrica, commonly known as the Balkan iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It features striking blue to violet flowers with a distinctive three-dimensional structure, typically blooming in late spring. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that form a basal rosette, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in wet meadows, stream banks, and other damp habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
