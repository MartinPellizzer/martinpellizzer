Ilex maximowicziana

Ilex maximowicziana, commonly known as the Chinese holly, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, dark green leaves that are typically glossy. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, followed by bright red berries that are a food source for various birds. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its hardy nature and aesthetic appeal.

Ilex maximowicziana is also valued for its ability to tolerate a range of soil conditions and partial shade.
