Ischaemum aristatum var. mangaluricum

Ischaemum aristatum var. mangaluricum, commonly known as the Mangaluru grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Indian subcontinent.

It typically grows in moist, tropical and subtropical regions, often found in wetlands and along riverbanks. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually pale green in color. Its inflorescence consists of a single spikelet at the tip of each stem, which produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and erosion control.
