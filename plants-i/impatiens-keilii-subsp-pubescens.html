Impatiens keilii subsp. pubescens

Impatiens keilii subsp.

pubescens, commonly known as the hairy impatiens, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The leaves are typically ovate and covered with a fine layer of pubescence, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

This subspecies is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
