Indigofera dyeri

Indigofera dyeri, commonly known as the blue sage, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring woody stems and gray-green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Indigofera dyeri is adapted to arid environments and thrives in full sun with well-drained soil.

It is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
