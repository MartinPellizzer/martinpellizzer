Impatiens uliginosa

Impatiens uliginosa, commonly known as swamp impatiens, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, acidic soils along streams and marshes. The plant features showy, spurred flowers that are usually pink or red, with five petals and a distinctive nectar spur. Its leaves are oval-shaped and arranged oppositely along the stems.

Swamp impatiens plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
