Inula trichophylla

Inula trichophylla, commonly known as the hairy elecampane, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a tall, cylindrical stem covered in fine, hairy textures, giving it its common name. Its yellow flowers bloom in late summer, and the plant is often found in meadows and grasslands.

Inula trichophylla is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
