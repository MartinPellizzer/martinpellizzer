Isodon oresbius

Isodon oresbius, commonly known as the Corsican gentian, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Corsica and Sardinia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and is often found in mountainous areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Isodon oresbius is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
