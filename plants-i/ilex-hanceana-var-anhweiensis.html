Ilex hanceana var. anhweiensis

Ilex hanceana var. anhweiensis, commonly known as the Anhwei holly, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that turn red in the autumn. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
