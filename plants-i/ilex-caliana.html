Ilex caliana

Ilex caliana, commonly known as the Mexican holly, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to Mexico.

It belongs to the family Aquifoliaceae and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for various birds and wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Ilex caliana is also valued for its winter interest and ability to maintain its foliage year-round.
