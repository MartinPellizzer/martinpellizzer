Impatiens parvigaleata

Impatiens parvigaleata, commonly known as the dwarf impatiens, is a herbaceous perennial native to Southeast Asia.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, with five petals. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are usually hairy and have a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a compact, ornamental plant.
