Indigofera ramosa

Indigofera ramosa, commonly known as the hairy indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. It has a woody stem with numerous branches, giving it a shrub-like appearance.

Indigofera ramosa is often used in traditional medicine and can thrive in a variety of soil types, making it adaptable to different environments.
