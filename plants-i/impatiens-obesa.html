Impatiens obesa

Impatiens obesa, commonly known as the fat impatiens, is a herbaceous perennial native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and broad, waxy leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, white, and red, which bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in rainforests.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
