Inga java

Inga java, commonly known as the "Java velvet tree," is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pendulous flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it beneficial for soil enrichment in agricultural settings.
