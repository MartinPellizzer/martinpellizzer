Ixora brevipedunculata

Ixora brevipedunculata, commonly known as the short-stalked ixora, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 3 meters, featuring dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of red, orange, or yellow, which bloom throughout the year in some regions. Its inflorescences are characterized by short peduncles, giving it the species name "brevipedunculata.".

Ixora brevipedunculata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a hedge plant.
