Iris variabilis subsp. serotina

Iris variabilis subsp.

serotina, commonly known as the late iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States. It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and along streambanks, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
