Inga semiglabra

Inga semiglabra, commonly known as the silky tamarind or velvet tamarind, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant is notable for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in disturbed soils, making it a common pioneer species in tropical ecosystems. Its pods contain edible seeds, and the plant has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.

The leaves are often used as a vegetable in some regions, and the plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal.
