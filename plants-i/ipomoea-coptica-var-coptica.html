Ipomoea coptica var. coptica

Ipomoea coptica var. coptica, commonly known as the blue morning glory, is a flowering vine native to the eastern United States.

It produces striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in the early morning, typically from June to September. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves and a trailing growth habit, making it suitable for trellises or as a ground cover. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

The species is known for its rapid growth and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
