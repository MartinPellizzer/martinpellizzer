Ichnanthus hispidus

Ichnanthus hispidus, commonly known as the hairy twayblade, is a small, perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically arranged in a spike, blooming in early spring. Ichnanthus hispidus is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows.

It is a rhizomatous plant, meaning it spreads through underground stems, forming dense colonies.
