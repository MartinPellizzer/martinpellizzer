Iris pumila subsp. attica

Iris pumila subsp.

attica, commonly known as the Greek dwarf iris, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces delicate, blue to purple flowers in early spring.

The plant typically reaches a height of less than 10 centimeters, making it well-suited for rock gardens or small spaces. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to native plant collections and ecological restoration projects.
