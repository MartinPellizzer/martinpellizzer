Indigofera cliffordiana

Indigofera cliffordiana, commonly known as the clifford's indigo.

It is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

This species is often grown for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some cultures.
