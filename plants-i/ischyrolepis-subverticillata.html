Ischyrolepis subverticillata

Ischyrolepis subverticillata, commonly known as the clustered feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often form dense clusters. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences composed of numerous tiny, pale green to brownish flowers. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and have a slight sheen, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Ischyrolepis subverticillata is often used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
