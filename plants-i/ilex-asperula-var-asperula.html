Ilex asperula var. asperula

Ilex asperula var. asperula, commonly known as the Japanese holly, is a species of evergreen shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically oval in shape and have a slightly rough texture. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red berries in the fall. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

The plant is also valued for its hardiness and adaptability to different climates.
