Impatiens fimbriata

Impatiens fimbriata, commonly known as the fringed impatiens, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, showy flowers that typically come in shades of pink, white, and red. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a hairy texture, and its stems are often covered in fine, fringed hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Impatiens fimbriata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
