Iresine verticillata

Iresine verticillata, commonly known as the redleaf plant or red leaf sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that range from deep red to purple in color, often with contrasting white or silver veins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically pink or white, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden beds.

Its ornamental value is primarily due to its vibrant foliage, which remains attractive throughout the growing season.
