Ipomoea amazonica

Ipomoea amazonica, commonly known as the Amazonian morning glory, is a striking vine native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its large, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in a range of vibrant colors, including purple, pink, and white. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a glossy surface and thrives in warm, humid climates. Its rapid growth habit and climbing nature make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

However, it is also considered an invasive species in some regions due to its aggressive spread.
