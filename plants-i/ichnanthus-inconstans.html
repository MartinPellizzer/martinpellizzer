Ichnanthus inconstans

Ichnanthus inconstans, commonly known as the wandering bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or violet. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

Ichnanthus inconstans is known for its delicate appearance and is often found in damp, nutrient-rich soils.
