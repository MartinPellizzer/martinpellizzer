Inga weberbaueri

Inga weberbaueri, commonly known as the Colombian inga, is a member of the Fabaceae family and is native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub, typically reaching heights of up to 10 meters. The plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are compound and have a leathery texture. Its flowers are usually clustered in racemose inflorescences and are often yellow or white in color.

Inga weberbaueri plays a significant role in its ecosystem, providing habitat and food for various animals.
