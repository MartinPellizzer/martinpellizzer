Ischaemum aristatum var. cylindricum

Ischaemum aristatum var. cylindricum, commonly known as the cylindrical beakgrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. The plant features slender, cylindrical stems that are often hairy, with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a loose panicle, bearing small, wind-pollinated flowers.

The variety *cylindricum* is distinguished by its elongated, cylindrical stem shape, which sets it apart from other varieties of *Ischaemum aristatum*.
