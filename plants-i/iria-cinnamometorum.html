Iria cinnamometorum

Iria cinnamometorum, commonly known as the cinnamonleaf or cinnamon plant, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, cinnamon-scented leaves and small, pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth texture with a distinctive cinnamon-like fragrance when crushed.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for the essential oils extracted from its leaves.
