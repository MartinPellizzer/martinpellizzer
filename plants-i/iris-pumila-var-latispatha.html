Iris pumila var. latispatha

Iris pumila var. latispatha, commonly known as the broad-leaf dwarf iris, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features slender, creeping rhizomes and produces clusters of small, pale blue to lavender flowers. The leaves are narrow, grass-like, and often form a dense mat on the ground. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to naturalize in suitable habitats.
