Indocalamus pseudosinicus

Indocalamus pseudosinicus, commonly known as the Chinese bamboo or false bamboo, is a species of grass native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach up to 3 meters in height. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are typically green and smooth, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its elegant appearance and adaptability to various soil conditions.

This plant is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
