Ischarum homaid

Ischarum homaid, commonly known as the Kashmir thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, showcasing adaptations to cold and arid conditions. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a composite head.

Ischarum homaid is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil in mountainous regions.
