Indigofera bongardiana var. bongardiana

Indigofera bongardiana var. bongardiana, commonly known as the Bongard's indigo.

It is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to purple, which bloom in clusters. Its roots contain alkaloids and have been used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its historical use in dye production.
