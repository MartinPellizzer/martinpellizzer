Iris rossii f. latifolia

Iris rossii f. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaf iris, is a hybrid form of the iris genus native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in late spring or early summer. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often a vibrant green, providing a striking contrast to its colorful blooms. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens with adequate water supply.
