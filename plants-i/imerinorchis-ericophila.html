Imerinorchis ericophila

Imerinorchis ericophila, commonly known as the Ericaceous Orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in late spring or early summer. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in association with heathland vegetation. Its unique morphology and specific habitat requirements make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to its limited distribution and threats from habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species.
