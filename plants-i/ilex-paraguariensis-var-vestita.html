Ilex paraguariensis var. vestita

Ilex paraguariensis var. vestita, commonly known as the Brazilian holly or the sacred plant of the Amazon, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and small, red berries that contain the stimulant compound caffeine. The plant is closely related to the coffee plant, and its leaves are traditionally used to prepare a stimulating beverage. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Due to its ecological and cultural significance, it is often cultivated in regions where it is native.
