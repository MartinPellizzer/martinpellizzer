Ipomoea chenopodiifolia subsp. bellator

Ipomoea chenopodiifolia subsp.

bellator, commonly known as the desert morning glory, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped blue to violet flowers that bloom in the early morning.

The plant is adapted to thrive in hot, dry environments, with deep taproots that allow it to access groundwater. Its fibrous stems and hairy leaves help reduce water loss through transpiration.

The species is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, showcasing its resilience in harsh conditions.
