Ipomoea villosa

Ipomoea villosa, commonly known as the hairy morning glory, is a flowering vine native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it its common name. The plant produces trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple, blooming in the morning. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun.

Ipomoea villosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can climb structures with its strong, twisting stems.
