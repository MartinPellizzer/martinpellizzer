Ipomoea glandulifera

Ipomoea glandulifera, commonly known as the sticky nightshade or glandular morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, opening in the morning. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that contain numerous small, oblong seeds.

Ipomoea glandulifera is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
