Iris flavissima f. orientalis

Iris flavissima f. orientalis, commonly known as the Japanese iris or oriental iris, is a hybrid form of the Japanese iris species.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers with a yellow center, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 3 feet, featuring narrow, grass-like leaves that are dark green in color. It prefers moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Botanically, it is a member of the Iridaceae family and is valued for its ornamental beauty and adaptability to various garden conditions.
