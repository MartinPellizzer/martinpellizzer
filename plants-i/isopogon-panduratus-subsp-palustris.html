Isopogon panduratus subsp. palustris

Isopogon panduratus subsp.

palustris, commonly known as the swamp isopogon, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia. It is a shrub that typically grows in wetland or swampy habitats, characterized by its dense, compact growth habit.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in cylindrical inflorescences, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs, and it has a fibrous root system adapted to waterlogged soils.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
