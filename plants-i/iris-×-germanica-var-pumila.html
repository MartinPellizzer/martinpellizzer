Iris × germanica var. pumila

Iris × germanica var. pumila, commonly known as the dwarf iris, is a compact perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces delicate, showy flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white. The plant typically grows to a height of 12–18 inches, making it ideal for small garden spaces or containers. It prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, blooming in late spring to early summer.

Its compact size and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
