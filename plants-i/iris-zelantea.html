Iris zelantea

Iris zelantea, commonly known as the Sierra iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, rocky habitats such as stream banks and meadows. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces showy blue to purple flowers with three upright petals. Its rhizomatous root system helps it thrive in well-drained soils.

Iris zelantea is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
