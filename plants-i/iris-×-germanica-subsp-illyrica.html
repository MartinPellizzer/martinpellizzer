Iris × germanica subsp. illyrica

Iris × germanica subsp.

illyrica, commonly known as the Ilyrian iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula. It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers with a distinctive white or pale yellow throat.

The plant typically grows to a height of 60–100 cm, featuring sword-shaped leaves and a robust rhizomatous root system. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
