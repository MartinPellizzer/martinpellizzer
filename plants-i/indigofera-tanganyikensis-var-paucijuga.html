Indigofera tanganyikensis var. paucijuga

Indigofera tanganyikensis var. paucijuga, commonly known as the East African indigo plant, is a flowering plant native to Tanzania and neighboring regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain small, dark seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and dyeing practices.

Its growth habit is typically shrub-like, with woody stems and a moderate height, making it well-suited to tropical and subtropical climates.
