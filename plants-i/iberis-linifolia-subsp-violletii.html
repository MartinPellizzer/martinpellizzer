Iberis linifolia subsp. violletii

Iberis linifolia subsp.

violletii, commonly known as the violet-flowered candytuft, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and produces clusters of small, violet or purple flowers.

The plant features silver-gray foliage with finely divided leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance. It is often used in rock gardens and as a border plant due to its ornamental value.

The species is adapted to well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for dry, sunny environments.
