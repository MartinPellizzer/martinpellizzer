Inula incisa

Inula incisa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved elecampane, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features gray-green, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers that develop into spiky, brown seed heads. It typically grows in moist meadows and along stream banks.

Inula incisa is valued for its medicinal properties and ornamental appeal in gardens.
