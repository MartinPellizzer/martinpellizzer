Iris kuschkensis

Iris kuschkensis, commonly known as the Kuschkinskaya iris, is a species native to Central Asia, particularly found in regions of Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan.

It is a rhizomatous perennial herb that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along riverbanks and wet meadows. The plant features striking blue to violet flowers with three upright petals and three downward-curving sepals, characteristic of the iris family. Its foliage consists of sword-shaped, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color.

Iris kuschkensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty and resilience.
