Iresine grandiflora

Iresine grandiflora, commonly known as the Crimson Flame or Redleaf plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety red leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is known for its vibrant foliage that changes color with light intensity. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Iresine grandiflora is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its eye-catching appearance.
