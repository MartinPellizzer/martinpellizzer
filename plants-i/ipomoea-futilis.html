Ipomoea futilis

Ipomoea futilis, commonly known as the "futile morning glory," is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its rapid growth and distinctive trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of blue, purple, and white. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves and has a climbing habit, often used in ornamental gardening. Its seeds are enclosed in a capsule that splits open when mature, dispersing the seeds by wind.

Ipomoea futilis is also known for its short blooming period, typically during the early morning hours.
