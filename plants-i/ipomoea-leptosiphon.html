Ipomoea leptosiphon

Ipomoea leptosiphon, commonly known as the slender false morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a herbaceous annual with distinctive blue or purple trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in summer. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces small, round berries that contain seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular garden plant, though it can be invasive in certain areas.
