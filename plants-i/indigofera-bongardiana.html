Indigofera bongardiana

Indigofera bongardiana, commonly known as the Bongard's indigo, is a flowering plant species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows as an erect shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, pod-like fruits containing seeds.

Indigofera bongardiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some cultures.
