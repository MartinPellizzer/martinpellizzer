Ilex aquifolium var. argentea-pectinata-major

Ilex aquifolium var. argentea-pectinata-major, commonly known as the silver-leafed holly, is a variant of the common holly characterized by its striking white-variegated leaves.

The foliage features a combination of silver-white and green hues, giving it a distinctive ornamental appearance. It is a evergreen shrub or small tree native to Europe, often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens. The leaves are typically oval with serrated edges, and the plant produces small, red berries in the fall.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for landscaping.
