Iris polychroa

Iris polychroa, commonly known as the multicolored iris, is a striking perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It features tall, slender stems that bear showy, hexagonal flowers in shades of blue, violet, and white. The plant's rhizomatous root system allows it to thrive in moist, well-drained soils. Its foliage consists of sword-shaped leaves that are often bluish-green in color.

Iris polychroa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
