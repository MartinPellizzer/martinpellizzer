Ipomoea exserta

Ipomoea exserta, commonly known as the slender morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and trumpet-shaped, pale blue to white flowers that open in the morning. The plant produces small, round berries that contain seeds covered in a sticky substance. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a velvety texture.

Ipomoea exserta is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
