Ixeridium graminifolium

Ixeridium graminifolium, commonly known as the slender swamp grass, is a perennial herb native to wetland environments in eastern Australia.

It has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a dense, tufted cluster at the base. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are clustered in spikelets, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in swamps, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies.

Due to its ecological importance, it plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
