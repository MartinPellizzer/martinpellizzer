Inula nervosa var. purpurascens

Inula nervosa var. purpurascens, commonly known as the purple-flowered elecampane, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a robust, cylindrical stem covered in coarse hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with prominent veins. The flowers are typically yellow, with a distinctive purple tinge, and bloom in late summer.

It is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
