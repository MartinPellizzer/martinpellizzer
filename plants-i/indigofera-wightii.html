Indigofera wightii

Indigofera wightii, commonly known as Indian indigo, is a flowering plant native to South and Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods containing seeds that are often used in traditional medicine. Its botanical significance lies in its role as a source of natural indigo dye, which has been historically important in textile industries.

The species is also valued for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to various soil types.
