Iriartea pruriens

Iriartea pruriens, commonly known as the Itch Palm, is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its dense, clustered growth habit and broad, leathery leaves. The plant's name derives from the irritant compounds present in its leaves, which can cause skin irritation upon contact. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

Iriartea pruriens is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses in local cultures.
