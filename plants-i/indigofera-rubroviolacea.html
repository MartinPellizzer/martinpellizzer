Indigofera rubroviolacea

Indigofera rubroviolacea, commonly known as the red-violet indigo plant, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its striking red and violet flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has trifoliate leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant flower colors and adaptability to various soil types.

Additionally, like other members of its genus, it has the potential for nitrogen fixation, which can benefit soil fertility.
