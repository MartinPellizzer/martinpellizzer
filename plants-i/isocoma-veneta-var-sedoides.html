Isocoma veneta var. sedoides

Isocoma veneta var. sedoides, commonly known as the desert sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant features linear, hairy leaves and produces yellow to orange composite flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or gravelly soils.

Its adaptability to harsh conditions makes it a resilient and ecologically important species in desert ecosystems.
