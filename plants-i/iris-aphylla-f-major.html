Iris aphylla f. major

Iris aphylla f. major, commonly known as the giant plains iris, is a variant of the plains iris species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, robust stature and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a rhizomatous root system and thrives in well-drained, sandy or gravelly soils. Its leaves are broad and grass-like, often reaching several feet in length.

The flowers are usually blue to violet in color, with a distinctive three-lobed lower petal.
