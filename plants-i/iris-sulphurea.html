Iris sulphurea

Iris sulphurea, commonly known as the golden iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features sword-shaped, grass-like leaves and produces striking yellow flowers with a distinctive dark center. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and blooms in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

The flowers are not only ornamental but also attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
