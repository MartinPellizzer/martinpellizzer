Inula britannica var. tymiensis

Inula britannica var. tymiensis, commonly known as the Tymienie thistle, is a perennial herb native to the British Isles.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and fibrous root system. The plant features a tall, erect stem covered with rough, hairy leaves that are typically gray-green in color. Its flower heads consist of both ray and disk florets, producing yellow flowers in late summer.

This variety is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges.
