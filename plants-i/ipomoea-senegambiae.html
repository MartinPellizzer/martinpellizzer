Ipomoea senegambiae

Ipomoea senegambiae, commonly known as the Senegambian morning glory, is a flowering plant native to West Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces tendrils that help it climb and spread. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical regions.

Its botanical features make it a popular ornamental plant in gardens and botanical collections.
