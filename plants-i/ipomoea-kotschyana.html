Ipomoea kotschyana

Ipomoea kotschyana, commonly known as the Kotschy's morning glory, is a striking vine native to regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep blue or purple, often with intricate patterns. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves and has a climbing habit, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun for optimal growth.

The flowers typically bloom in the early morning, giving the plant its common name.
