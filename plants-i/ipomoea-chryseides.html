Ipomoea chryseides

Ipomoea chryseides, commonly known as the golden morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in the morning and close by midday. The plant belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is known for its climbing habit, with tendrils that help it ascend supports. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a velvety texture, while its flowers are trumpet-shaped with five petals.

Ipomoea chryseides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
