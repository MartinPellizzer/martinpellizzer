Inga auristellae

Inga auristellae, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, Colombia, and Peru. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a pod containing edible seeds, which are often used in local cuisines.

The plant also has a shallow root system that helps prevent soil erosion in its native habitat.
