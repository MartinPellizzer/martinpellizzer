Isoetes mongerensis

Isoetes mongerensis, commonly known as the Monger's watermilfoil, is a species of aquatic plant belonging to the order Isoetales.

It is characterized by its simple, ribbon-like leaves that are typically submerged in water. The plant produces small, spore-bearing structures called sporangia, which are arranged in a compact cluster at the base of the plant. It is often found in still or slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and lakes.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
