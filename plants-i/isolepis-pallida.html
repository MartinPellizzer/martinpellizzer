Isolepis pallida

Isolepis pallida, commonly known as the slender bulrush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to wetlands and marshy areas in North America.

It has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, pale green to brown spikelets that are clustered at the tips of its stems. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, and it plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.

Isolepis pallida is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to thrive in disturbed wetland environments.
