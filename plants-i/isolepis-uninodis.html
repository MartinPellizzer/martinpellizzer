Isolepis uninodis

Isolepis uninodis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tussock grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marsh environments.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical growth habit and is typically found in temperate regions. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base. Its inflorescence consists of a tall, spikelike cluster of small, spikelet-like flowers.

Isolepis uninodis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
