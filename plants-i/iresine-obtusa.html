Iresine obtusa

Iresine obtusa, commonly known as the velvetleaf or redleaf plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety foliage that ranges from deep red to purple and green. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically pink or white, which appear in clusters at the leaf axils. Iresine obtusa thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant leaf colors.

Its growth habit is typically upright, with branching that can be either bushy or somewhat sprawling depending on the conditions.
