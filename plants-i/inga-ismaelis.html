Inga ismaelis

Inga ismaelis, commonly known as the Imael's inga, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. Inga ismaelis produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, and it is often found in secondary forests and disturbed habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation projects.
