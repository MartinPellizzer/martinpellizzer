Indigofera deightonii subsp. deightonii

Indigofera deightonii subsp.

deightonii, commonly known as the African indigo plant, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and clusters of small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple.

The plant produces pods that contain lens-shaped seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and agricultural contexts.
