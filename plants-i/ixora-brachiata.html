Ixora brachiata

Ixora brachiata, commonly known as the flame of the woods, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 2 to 6 meters. The plant produces clusters of bright orange-red flowers, which are highly attractive to birds and insects. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and have a slightly waxy texture.

Ixora brachiata thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun.
