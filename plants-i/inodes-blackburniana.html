Inodes blackburniana

Inodes blackburniana, commonly known as the Blackburn's inkweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to the United Kingdom.

It features distinctive, dark purple to black stems that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly rough texture, contributing to its unique appearance.

Inodes blackburniana is often found in damp, shaded areas such as woodland edges and stream banks.
