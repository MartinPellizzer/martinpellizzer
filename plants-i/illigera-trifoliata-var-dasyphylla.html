Illigera trifoliata var. dasyphylla

Illigera trifoliata var. dasyphylla, commonly known as the Three-leafed Illigera, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a flowering plant characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets arranged symmetrically. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
