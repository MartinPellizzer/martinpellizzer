Iris ensata f. tibetica

Iris ensata f. tibetica, commonly known as the Tibetan iris, is a hybrid variety of the Japanese iris.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in late spring or early summer. The plant features sturdy, upright stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often a deep green. Native to the high-altitude regions of Tibet, it thrives in cool, moist environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
