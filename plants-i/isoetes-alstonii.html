Isoetes alstonii

Isoetes alstonii, commonly known as the Alston's water clover, is a species of aquatic plant belonging to the genus Isoetes in the family Isoetaceae.

It is characterized by its spiky, ribbon-like leaves that are often submerged in water, with sporangia forming at the tips of the leaf-like structures. This plant is typically found in freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving streams. Its unique morphology and reproductive structures make it an important component of aquatic ecosystems.

Isoetes alstonii is also notable for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor waters, contributing to its ecological significance.
