Indigofera rosei

Indigofera rosei, commonly known as the pink indigo or rose indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to parts of Africa and is characterized by its striking pink to purple flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has trifoliate leaves and produces small, elongated pods that contain lens-shaped seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.

Indigofera rosei thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
