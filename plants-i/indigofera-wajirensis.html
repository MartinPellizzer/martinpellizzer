Indigofera wajirensis

Indigofera wajirensis, commonly known as the Wajirensis indigo plant, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, which makes it beneficial for soil enrichment. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a notable species in its native habitat.
