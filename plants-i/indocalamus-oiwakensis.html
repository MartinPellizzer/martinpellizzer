Indocalamus oiwakensis

Indocalamus oiwakensis, commonly known as the Oiwake bamboo, is a species native to Japan.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that grow in dense clumps. The plant typically reaches a height of 1 to 2 meters and has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its culms are highly valued for their strength and flexibility, making them useful in traditional crafts and construction.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.
