Ischaemum longipilum

Ischaemum longipilum, commonly known as long-haired barnyard grass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and long, hairy leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny florets, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

Due to its aggressive growth, it can become a weed in cultivated areas.
