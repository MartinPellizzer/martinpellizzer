Ixidium wrightii

Ixidium wrightii, commonly known as Wright's ixidium, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the eastern United States, particularly Florida, where it thrives in wetland habitats. The plant is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a slightly serrated margin.

Ixidium wrightii is valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife and its ornamental appeal in wetland restoration projects.
