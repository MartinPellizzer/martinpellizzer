Ischyrolepis schoenoides

Ischyrolepis schoenoides, commonly known as the golden groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in grasslands and open woodlands. The plant features basal rosettes of leaves and produces yellow flowers in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are clustered in loose inflorescences.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
