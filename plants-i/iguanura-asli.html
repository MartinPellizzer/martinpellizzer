Iguanura asli

Iguanura asli, commonly known as the Brazilian orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the rainforests of Brazil.

This plant is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors, including yellow, orange, and red. It typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air. The plant has a complex root system that helps it thrive in the humid, shaded conditions of its natural habitat.

Iguanura asli is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological role in supporting biodiversity.
