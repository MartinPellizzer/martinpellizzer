Isachne ciliatiflora

Isachne ciliatiflora, commonly known as the silky fringed grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and wetlands. The plant features slender, cylindrical stems that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a soft, hairy texture along the edges.

The flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, contributing to its ornamental value in horticulture.
