Isoetes histrix var. lacunosa

Isoetes histrix var. lacunosa, commonly known as the lacunose water horsetail, is a variant of the water horsetail genus Isoetes.

It is a perennial aquatic plant characterized by its spore-producing structures called strobilae, which are typically found at the tips of its segmented, jointed stems. The plant thrives in freshwater environments such as ponds and wetlands, where it anchors itself with its root-like rhizomes. Its leaves are reduced to small, scale-like structures, and the stems are covered in a distinctive, waxy coating.

This variant is notable for its unique morphology and ecological role in aquatic ecosystems.
