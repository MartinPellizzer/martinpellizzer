Ipomoea astrotrichota

Ipomoea astrotrichota, commonly known as the starry morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its trumpet-shaped flowers that open in the early morning. The plant produces violet to blue flowers with a distinctive star-like pattern on the petals. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are hairy and twining.

Ipomoea astrotrichota is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
