Impatiens rubromaculata subsp. rubromaculata

Impatiens rubromaculata subsp.

rubromaculata, commonly known as the red-spotted impatiens, is a species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa. It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red, often with distinctive red or orange markings.

The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters, and features heart-shaped, glossy leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for garden borders and shaded areas.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to both ornamental gardens and ecological studies.
