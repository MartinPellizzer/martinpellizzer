Inga belizensis

Inga belizensis, commonly known as the Belize inga, is a species of legume native to Central America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments. Its pods contain seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its spread.

The species is valued for its ecological role in supporting biodiversity and its potential use in agroforestry systems.
