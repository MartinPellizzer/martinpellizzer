Ionoxalis bipartita

Ionoxalis bipartita, commonly known as the two-part wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features trifoliate leaves with three heart-shaped leaflets, and small, delicate flowers that are typically white or pale pink. The plant produces capsule fruits containing several seeds, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Ionoxalis bipartita is often found in woodland areas and along stream banks, where it contributes to the understory vegetation.
