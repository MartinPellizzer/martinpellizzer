Ipomoea mariannensis

Ipomoea mariannensis, commonly known as the Mariana morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Pacific, particularly the Mariana Islands.

It is a herbaceous vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, black seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed habitats.

Ipomoea mariannensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
