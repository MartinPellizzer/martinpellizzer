Irvingia erecta

Irvingia erecta, commonly known as African mango, is a deciduous shrub native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into fleshy, orange-colored fruits. The plant thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks. Its fruit is valued for its high nutritional content, including vitamins, minerals, and dietary fiber.

Irvingia erecta also has traditional medicinal uses in various African cultures.
