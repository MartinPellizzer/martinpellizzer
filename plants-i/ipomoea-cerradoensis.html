Ipomoea cerradoensis

Ipomoea cerradoensis, commonly known as the cerrado morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the cerrado biome of Brazil.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and savannas. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple, often appearing in clusters. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are hairy and often sprawling.

Ipomoea cerradoensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and plays a role in the ecological dynamics of its native habitat.
