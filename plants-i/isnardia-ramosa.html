Isnardia ramosa

Isnardia ramosa, commonly known as the hairy bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Isnardia ramosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
