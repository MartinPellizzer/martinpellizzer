Ischaemum conjugatum

Ischaemum conjugatum, commonly known as the common dogstail, is a perennial grass species native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It typically grows in moist soils and is often found in meadows, ditches, and along riverbanks. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are usually less than 1 meter tall, with a distinctive tuft of spikelets at the apex. Its leaves are flat, narrow, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its characteristic appearance.

Ischaemum conjugatum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
