Indigofera viscidissima subsp. orientalis

Indigofera viscidissima subsp.

orientalis, commonly known as the Oriental sticky indigo, is a flowering plant native to southern Asia. It is characterized by its dense, glandular hairs that give it a sticky texture, particularly on its stems and leaves.

The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing abilities.
