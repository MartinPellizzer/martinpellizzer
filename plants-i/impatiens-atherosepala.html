Impatiens atherosepala

Impatiens atherosepala, commonly known as the woolly impatiens, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The leaves are typically ovate with a hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The plant is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
