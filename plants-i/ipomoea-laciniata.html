Ipomoea laciniata

Ipomoea laciniata, commonly known as the creeping morning glory, is a herbaceous vine native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features heart-shaped, palmately lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white, which bloom in the morning. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive nature, it can become a problematic weed in some regions.
