Inula saturejae

Inula saturejae, commonly known as the Persian elecampane, is a flowering plant native to the mountains of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features tall, hairy stems and large, yellowish-white ray flowers that surround a central disk of smaller florets. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a strong, aromatic scent.

Inula saturejae is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
