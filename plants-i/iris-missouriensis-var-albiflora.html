Iris missouriensis var. albiflora

Iris missouriensis var. albiflora, commonly known as the Missouri white flag iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the central United States.

It features upright, sword-shaped leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces striking white to pale blue flowers with a distinctive three-lobed petal structure. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in native habitats.
