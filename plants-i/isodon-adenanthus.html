Isodon adenanthus

Isodon adenanthus, commonly known as the mountain thyme or rock thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun conditions. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Isodon adenanthus is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
