Impatiens balsamina var. arcuata

Impatiens balsamina var. arcuata, commonly known as the weeping impatiens, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically borne in clusters and bloom throughout the growing season.

This variety is popular in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to cascade from hanging baskets or containers.
