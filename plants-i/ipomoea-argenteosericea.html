Ipomoea argenteosericea

Ipomoea argenteosericea, commonly known as the silver-haired morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered in a fine, silvery down, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas or near water sources.

The plant is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
