Ilex atrata

Ilex atrata, commonly known as the black holly, is a species of flowering plant in the family Aquifoliaceae.

It is native to the southeastern United States, particularly Florida and Georgia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that turn reddish in the fall. It produces small, white flowers in the spring, followed by bright red berries that are edible for birds.

Ilex atrata is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
