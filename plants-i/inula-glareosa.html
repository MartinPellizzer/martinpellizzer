Inula glareosa

Inula glareosa, commonly known as the shining elecampane, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features tall, sturdy stems covered in fine, white hairs that give it a silvery appearance. Its yellow flower heads consist of numerous small florets arranged in a disk-like structure.

Inula glareosa is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along streambanks, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
