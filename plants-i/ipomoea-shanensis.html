Ipomoea shanensis

Ipomoea shanensis, commonly known as the Shan morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows as a climbing vine with heart-shaped leaves and produces showy blooms during the day. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Ipomoea shanensis is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its traditional uses in herbal medicine.
