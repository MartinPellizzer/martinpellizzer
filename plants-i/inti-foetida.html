Inti foetida

Inti foetida, commonly known as the "stinking beauty," is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers and hairy, glandular leaves. The plant is known for its strong, unpleasant odor, which is most noticeable when the flowers are in bloom. Inti foetida thrives in high-altitude environments and is often found in rocky or mountainous terrain.

Its unique appearance and scent make it a notable species in its native habitat.
