Iryanthera inpae

Iryanthera inpae, commonly known as the Brazilian star orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, star-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually a vibrant purple or blue, with a unique structure that facilitates pollination by specific insect species.

Iryanthera inpae is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
