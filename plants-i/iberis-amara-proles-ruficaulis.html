Iberis amara proles ruficaulis

Iberis amara proles ruficaulis, commonly known as the red-stemmed candytuft, is a flowering plant characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers.

It features hairy, narrow leaves that are often tinged with red or purple, especially on younger growth. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It is typically found in rocky or gravelly soils in temperate regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
