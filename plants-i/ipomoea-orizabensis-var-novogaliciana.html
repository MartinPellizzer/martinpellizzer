Ipomoea orizabensis var. novogaliciana

Ipomoea orizabensis var. novogaliciana, commonly known as the Mexican morning glory, is a flowering vine native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves and trumpet-shaped, predominantly white or pale purple flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-drained soil. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can climb supports with its twining stems.

The flowers emit a mild fragrance and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
