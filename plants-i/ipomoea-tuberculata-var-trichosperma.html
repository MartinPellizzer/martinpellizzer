Ipomoea tuberculata var. trichosperma

Ipomoea tuberculata var. trichosperma, commonly known as the silverleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive tendencies, it is considered a weed in many areas.
