Inula graminifolia

Inula graminifolia, commonly known as the meadow fleabane, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It produces yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer, followed by fluffy, white seed heads.

Inula graminifolia is often used in herbal medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.
