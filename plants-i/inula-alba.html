Inula alba

Inula alba, commonly known as white ragwort, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as meadows and grasslands, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its flowers are yellow, with a central disk surrounded by ray florets, and it produces a milky sap that is often used in traditional medicine.

Inula alba is also known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and its role in supporting pollinators.
