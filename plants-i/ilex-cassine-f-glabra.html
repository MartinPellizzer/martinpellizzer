Ilex cassine f. glabra

Ilex cassine f. glabra, commonly known as the smoothleaf holly, is a shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green, smooth-textured leaves that are typically oval in shape. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, fleshy berries that are attractive to birds. Its evergreen nature makes it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

The smoothleaf holly is adapted to a variety of soil types and thrives in full sun to partial shade.
