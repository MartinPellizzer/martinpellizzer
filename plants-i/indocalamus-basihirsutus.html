Indocalamus basihirsutus

Indocalamus basihirsutus, commonly known as the hairy bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, feathery foliage. The plant is notable for its hairy texture on the lower parts of its stems, which gives it its scientific name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
