Isatis sibirica

Isatis sibirica, commonly known as Siberian woad, is a herbaceous plant native to the colder regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant is characterized by its blue to purple flowers and pinnately divided leaves. It produces small, round seeds that are often used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and historical contexts.
