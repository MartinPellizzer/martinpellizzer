Iseilema filipes

Iseilema filipes, commonly known as the slender-leafed mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of host trees, primarily in tropical and subtropical regions.

It has small, leathery leaves that are usually opposite or whorled in arrangement. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers, which are often clustered in inflorescences. Its berries are typically red or yellow, containing seeds that are dispersed by birds.

Iseilema filipes plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for wildlife and contributing to the ecological balance of its habitat.
