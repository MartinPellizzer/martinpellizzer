Isocarpha oppositifolia subsp. achyranthes

Isocarpha oppositifolia subsp.

achyranthes, commonly known as the narrow-leaved burrweed, is a flowering plant native to regions of southern Africa. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and grasslands. Its leaves are opposite and narrow, with a distinctive hairy texture.

The subspecies is distinguished by its smaller, more compact flower heads compared to other members of the species.
