Ichthyothere ternifolia

Ichthyothere ternifolia, commonly known as the three-leaved water plant, is a rare aquatic species characterized by its delicate, trifoliate leaves that float on the surface of freshwater bodies.

Its small, white flowers bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and moths. The leaves are typically broad and have a waxy surface to reduce water loss, while the roots anchor it in muddy substrates.

Due to its specialized requirements, Ichthyothere ternifolia is often found in isolated, nutrient-rich wetlands.
