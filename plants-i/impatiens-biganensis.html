Impatiens biganensis

Impatiens biganensis, commonly known as the two-colored impatiens, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the genus Impatiens in the family Balsaminaceae, characterized by its showy, brightly colored flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, with upright stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually pink or white, with a distinctive spurred petal at the back.
