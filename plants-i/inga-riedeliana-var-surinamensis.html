Inga riedeliana var. surinamensis

Inga riedeliana var. surinamensis, commonly known as the Surinam inga, is a tropical tree species native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant is typically found in moist, lowland forests and thrives in humid, warm climates. It plays an important ecological role by fixing nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding vegetation.

Its fruit pods contain seeds that are dispersed by animals, contributing to its natural spread and regeneration.
