Impatiens poculifer

Impatiens poculifer, commonly known as the "spoonleaf impatiens," is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, brightly colored flowers, typically in shades of red, pink, or white. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid forests. It belongs to the family Balsaminaceae and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

Impatiens poculifer is also known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and vegetative propagation.
