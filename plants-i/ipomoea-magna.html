Ipomoea magna

Ipomoea magna, commonly known as the giant morning glory, is a striking flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 15 centimeters in diameter, often in shades of white, pink, or purple. The plant produces heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are typically a deep green. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Ipomoea magna is a vigorous climber, capable of reaching heights of several meters when provided with adequate support.
