Ipomoea ana-mariae

Ipomoea ana-mariae, commonly known as Ana-Maria's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean, particularly found in Jamaica.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and twining stems that climb or trail along the ground. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
