Irvingia barteri

Irvingia barteri, commonly known as the African mango, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Irvingiaceae and is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and large, palmate leaves. The tree produces yellowish flowers and small, fleshy fruits that are rich in nutrients. Its fruit is often used in traditional medicine and for its potential health benefits.

Irvingia barteri is also valued for its role in local ecosystems and as a source of timber.
