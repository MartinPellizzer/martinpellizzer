Indigofera setosissima var. major

Indigofera setosissima var. major, commonly known as the Chinese indigo plant, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is notable for its ability to produce a deep blue dye, historically used in textile industries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Its botanical significance lies in its role in traditional dyeing practices and its potential applications in modern natural dyeing technologies.
