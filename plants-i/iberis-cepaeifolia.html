Iberis cepaeifolia

Iberis cepaeifolia, commonly known as the snowdrop, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that typically bloom in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that hang from the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

Iberis cepaeifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its early blooming habit.
