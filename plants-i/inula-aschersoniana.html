Inula aschersoniana

Inula aschersoniana, commonly known as the Scherzer's Inula, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring a stout, hairy stem and dark green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of yellow, daisy-like flowers with a prominent central disk, which bloom in the summer months. Its botanical characteristics include a fibrous root system and a preference for well-drained, rocky soils.

Inula aschersoniana is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it thrives in cooler, moist environments.
