Ibatia warscewiczii

Ibatia warscewiczii, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Erythroxylum warscewiczii*, is a flowering plant native to the Amazon Basin.

It belongs to the Erythroxylaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant red flowers and elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, orange-red fruits that are edible and contain a single seed. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
