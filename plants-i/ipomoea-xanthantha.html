Ipomoea xanthantha

Ipomoea xanthantha, commonly known as the yellow morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the morning and close in the afternoon. The plant has heart-shaped, palmately veined leaves and produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in moist soils near rivers or in forest understories.

Ipomoea xanthantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
