Ipomoea chaetocaulos

Ipomoea chaetocaulos, commonly known as the hairy morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The plant has velvety, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often lobed. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Ipomoea chaetocaulos is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is a member of the Convolvulaceae family.
