Iridodictyum farashae

Iridodictyum farashae, commonly known as the Farashae's spider orchid, is a rare and striking orchid native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It is characterized by its intricate, spider-like appearance, with long, slender inflorescences and delicate, white to pale blue flowers. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically growing on trees or rocks in montane regions. Its unique morphology and restricted habitat make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat threats, it is considered vulnerable to extinction.
