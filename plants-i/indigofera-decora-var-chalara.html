Indigofera decora var. chalara

Indigofera decora var. chalara, commonly known as the purple passion vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has trifoliate leaves with a heart-shaped leaflet, and its stems are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

The species is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dyes.
