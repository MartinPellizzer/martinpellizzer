Indocalamus longiauritus var. semifalcatus

Indocalamus longiauritus var. semifalcatus, commonly known as the semi-falcate bamboo, is a variant of the long-tailed bamboo species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, culm-like stems that are often arched or curved, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces long, narrow leaves with a smooth, glossy surface and a pale green coloration. Its growth habit is clump-forming, making it suitable for ornamental use in gardens and landscapes.

This variety is valued for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
