Indigofera mairei var. micrantha

Indigofera mairei var. micrantha, commonly known as the small-flowered indigo, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant produces small, oblong pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks.
