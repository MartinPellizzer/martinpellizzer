Impatiens nanatonanensis

Impatiens nanatonanensis, commonly known as the Nanatonan Impatiens, is a flowering plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, brightly colored flowers that range from white to pink. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in the understory of dense forests. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by insects, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
