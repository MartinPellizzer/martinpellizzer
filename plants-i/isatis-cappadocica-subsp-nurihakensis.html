Isatis cappadocica subsp. nurihakensis

Isatis cappadocica subsp.

nurihakensis, commonly known as the Cappadocian woad, is a member of the Brassicaceae family. It is characterized by its biennial growth habit, with basal leaves that are typically lanceolate and hairy.

The plant produces yellow flowers in racemes, which are followed by spherical, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It is native to Turkey and is adapted to semi-arid environments.

Its leaves have been historically used for dye production, similar to other members of the genus Isatis.
