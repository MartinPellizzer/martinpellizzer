Ischaemum keralense

Ischaemum keralense, commonly known as Keralan barnyard grass, is a species of grass native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Poaceae and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides. It produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers, facilitating wind pollination.

Ischaemum keralense is relatively drought-tolerant and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
