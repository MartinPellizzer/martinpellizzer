Ischaemum speciosum

Ischaemum speciosum, commonly known as the showy grass or silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, silver-gray blades that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows and along stream banks.

Ischaemum speciosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various insects and birds.
