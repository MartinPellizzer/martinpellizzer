Ixeridium transnokoense

Ixeridium transnokoense, commonly known as the Transnoko grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions in Africa.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant typically features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands or along riverbanks.

Due to its ecological role, it contributes to soil stabilization and provides forage for local wildlife.
