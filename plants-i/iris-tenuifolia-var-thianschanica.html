Iris tenuifolia var. thianschanica

Iris tenuifolia var. thianschanica, commonly known as the Siberian iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces tall, slender stems that bear showy, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and wetland habitats.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate appearance and hardy nature.
