Iris caucasica var. kharput

Iris caucasica var. kharput, commonly known as the Caucasian iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It features tall, slender stems that bear showy, violet-blue to purple flowers with three upright petals and three drooping sepals. The plant has lance-shaped, grass-like leaves that are usually dark green and arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
