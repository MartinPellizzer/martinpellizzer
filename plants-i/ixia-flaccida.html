Ixia flaccida

Ixia flaccida, commonly known as the limp lily, is a striking perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features tall, slender stems that bear clusters of large, showy flowers with bright orange or red petals and yellow centers. The plant typically blooms in late summer to early autumn, adding vibrant color to its natural habitat. Ixia flaccida thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical characteristics make it both ornamental and ecologically significant in its native environment.
