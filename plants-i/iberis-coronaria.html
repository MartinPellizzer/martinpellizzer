Iberis coronaria

Iberis coronaria, commonly known as sweet rocket, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of fragrant, violet-blue flowers in early spring. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

The plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
