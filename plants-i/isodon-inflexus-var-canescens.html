Isodon inflexus var. canescens

Isodon inflexus var. canescens, commonly known as the hairy isodon, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of East Asia.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic plants such as mint and sage. The plant is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and small, tubular flowers that typically bloom in shades of blue or purple. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested or shaded environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
