Inga altissima

Inga altissima, commonly known as the "giant arrowroot" or "sweet acacia," is a fast-growing tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, compound leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in length, with long, slender leaflets. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by pods containing edible seeds. Its roots have a high starch content, making it a valuable food source in some regions.

Inga altissima is also used in agroforestry for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, enhancing soil fertility.
