Indigofera evansii

Indigofera evansii, commonly known as the African indigo or Evans' indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant produces striking blue to violet flowers, which are often clustered in racemes, making it a visually appealing ornamental species. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, elliptical leaflets, and it has a bushy growth habit.

Indigofera evansii is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to soil fertility in agricultural settings.
