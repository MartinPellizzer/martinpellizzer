Inula hakkiarica

Inula hakkiarica, commonly known as the Turkish Inula, is a flowering plant native to Turkey.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Inula hakkiarica is often found in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments.
