Ipomoea madrensis

Ipomoea madrensis, commonly known as the Mexican morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a herbaceous vine that produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and thrives in warm, sunny environments. Its flowers open in the morning and close by late afternoon, giving it its common name.

Ipomoea madrensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can grow up to several meters in length.
