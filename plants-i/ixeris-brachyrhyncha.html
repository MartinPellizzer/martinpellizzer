Ixeris brachyrhyncha

Ixeris brachyrhyncha, commonly known as the narrowleaf yarrow, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright, branching stems and small, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Ixeris brachyrhyncha is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
