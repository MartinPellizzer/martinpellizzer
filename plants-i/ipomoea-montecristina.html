Ipomoea montecristina

Ipomoea montecristina, commonly known as the blue morning glory, is a flowering vine native to South America.

It produces striking, trumpet-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in the morning and close by midday. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in warm climates and requires full sun to partial shade.

Its rapid growth habit makes it suitable for trellises, fences, or as a ground cover.
