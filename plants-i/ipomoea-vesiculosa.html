Ipomoea vesiculosa

Ipomoea vesiculosa, commonly known as the bladderleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, round seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Ipomoea vesiculosa is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
