Iberis prostii subsp. violletii

Iberis prostii subsp.

violletii, commonly known as the violet flowering candytuft, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with dense clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to violet.

The plant produces basal leaves that are lobed and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are usually hairless. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its early spring blooms.
