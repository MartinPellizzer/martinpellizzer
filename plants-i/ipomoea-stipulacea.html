Ipomoea stipulacea

Ipomoea stipulacea, commonly known as the scarlet morning glory, is a flowering vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant produces showy, five-petaled blooms that open in the morning and close by midday. It thrives in warm, sunny environments and is often found growing along fences or in disturbed soils.

Ipomoea stipulacea is also known for its rapid growth and ability to climb, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.
