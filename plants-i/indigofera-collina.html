Indigofera collina

Indigofera collina, commonly known as mountain indigo, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring woody stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to violet, often arranged in dense clusters. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to thrive in well-drained, sandy soils.

Indigofera collina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
