Iris giganticaerulea

Iris giganticaerulea, commonly known as the Blue Flag Iris, is a striking perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach up to 6 feet in height, supporting large, showy blue or violet flowers. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that form a dense rosette at the base. Its flowers typically bloom in late spring to early summer, adding vibrant color to wetland and pond margins.

Iris giganticaerulea is well adapted to moist soils and is often found in marshy areas and along stream banks.
