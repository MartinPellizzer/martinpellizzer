Inga stenoptera var. peduncularis

Inga stenoptera var. peduncularis, commonly known as the pedunculate inga, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, spurred flowers. The plant has a climbing habit and often forms dense thickets in its natural habitat. Its fruits are pod-like and contain edible seeds, while its nitrogen-fixing ability makes it ecologically significant.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in agroforestry systems.
