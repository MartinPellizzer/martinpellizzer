Iresine serpyllifolia

Iresine serpyllifolia, commonly known as redleaf sorrel, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its vibrant red foliage, which contrasts strikingly with its small, inconspicuous white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Iresine serpyllifolia is also valued as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and ease of cultivation.
