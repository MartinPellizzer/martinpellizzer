Ilex barahonica

Ilex barahonica, commonly known as the Barahona holly, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean islands, particularly Cuba and Hispaniola.

It belongs to the Aquifoliaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically opposite in arrangement. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for local wildlife. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in coastal areas or disturbed soils.
