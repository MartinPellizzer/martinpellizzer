Isolepis thouarsii

Isolepis thouarsii, commonly known as the New Zealand tussock grass, is a perennial herb native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and forms extensive tussocks in coastal and subalpine regions. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is adapted to harsh environmental conditions, including strong winds and salt spray.

Isolepis thouarsii plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
