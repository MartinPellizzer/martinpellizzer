Iphigenia boinensis

Iphigenia boinensis, commonly known as the broadleaf arrowhead, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its arrowhead-shaped leaves and striking inflorescence. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as marshes and swamps. Its flowers are small and clustered in a spadix, surrounded by a colorful spathe.

Iphigenia boinensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in wetland restoration projects.
