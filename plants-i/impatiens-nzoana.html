Impatiens nzoana

Impatiens nzoana, commonly known as the Zanzibar impatiens, is a flowering plant native to the island of Zanzibar in Tanzania.

It is a herbaceous perennial characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, brightly colored flowers. The plant typically produces clusters of five-petaled flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom throughout the wet season. Its leaves are ovate and covered in a fine, fuzzy texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Impatiens nzoana thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland forest habitats.
