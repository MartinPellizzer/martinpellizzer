Ipomoea tenuirostris subsp. repens

Ipomoea tenuirostris subsp.

repens, commonly known as the creeping morning glory, is a vine species native to tropical and subtropical regions. It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green.

The flowers are trumpet-shaped, with five petals, and range in color from white to pale blue or pink. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This plant is often found in disturbed areas, along roadsides, and in wetlands due to its adaptability and rapid growth.
