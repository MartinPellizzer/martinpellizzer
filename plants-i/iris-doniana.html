Iris doniana

Iris doniana, commonly known as the Congo iris, is a striking perennial herb native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, supporting large, showy flowers. The plant features broad, sword-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green color. Its flowers are usually blue or violet, with a distinctive three-parted structure and prominent violet markings.

Iris doniana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.
