Isocoma oxyphylla

Isocoma oxyphylla, commonly known as the narrowleaf goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy blooms. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
