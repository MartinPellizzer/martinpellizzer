Iris hexagona

Iris hexagona, commonly known as the hexagon iris, is a species of flowering plant belonging to the Iridaceae family.

It is native to the western United States, particularly in areas of California and Nevada. The plant is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which feature a distinctive hexagonal shape in the center. Its leaves are sword-shaped and grass-like, growing from a rhizomatous root system.

Iris hexagona is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
