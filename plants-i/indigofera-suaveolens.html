Indigofera suaveolens

Indigofera suaveolens, commonly known as sweet indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic, trifoliate leaves and showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it beneficial for soil enrichment in agricultural settings.
