Impatiens latipetala

Impatiens latipetala, commonly known as the broad-petaled impatiens, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy, funnel-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and they often have a glossy appearance. This species thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
