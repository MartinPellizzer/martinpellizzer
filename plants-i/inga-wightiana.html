Inga wightiana

Inga wightiana, commonly known as the white inga, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces long, pendulous pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests.

Inga wightiana is also valued for its role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitats.
