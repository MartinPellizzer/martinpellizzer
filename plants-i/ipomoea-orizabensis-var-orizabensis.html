Ipomoea orizabensis var. orizabensis

Ipomoea orizabensis var. orizabensis, commonly known as the Mexican morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a vine that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of blue, violet, and white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and thrives in warm, humid climates. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its rapid growth and climbing habit make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking vibrant, fast-growing plants.
