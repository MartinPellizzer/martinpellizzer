Ipomoea carletonii

Ipomoea carletonii, commonly known as Carleton's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves and has a climbing habit, often twining around supports. Its flowers are typically pale blue to lavender in color, with a white throat.
