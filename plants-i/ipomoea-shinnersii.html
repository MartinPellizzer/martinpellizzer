Ipomoea shinnersii

Ipomoea shinnersii, commonly known as the Shinners morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its trumpet-shaped, violet to purple flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces tendrils that help it climb and spread along structures. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and along streams.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
