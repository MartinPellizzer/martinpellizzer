Indigofera porrecta var. bicolor

Indigofera porrecta var. bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored indigo plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive bicolor flowers, typically featuring a mix of blue and white or purple hues. The plant has compound leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical significance in traditional dye production.
