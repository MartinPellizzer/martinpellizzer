Inula sericophylla

Inula sericophylla, commonly known as the silky leaf inula, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its finely hairy, silver-gray leaves that give it a distinctive, almost silvery appearance. The plant produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Inula sericophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
