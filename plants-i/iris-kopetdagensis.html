Iris kopetdagensis

Iris kopetdagensis, commonly known as the Kopetdag iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and striking blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette.

The Kopetdag iris is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
