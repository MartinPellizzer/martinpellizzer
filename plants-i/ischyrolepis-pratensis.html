Ischyrolepis pratensis

Ischyrolepis pratensis, commonly known as the meadow ischyrolepis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Europe.

It typically grows in moist grasslands and meadows, preferring well-drained soils. The plant features slender, upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its small, white to pale blue flowers bloom in early summer, forming loose clusters at the top of the stems.

Ischyrolepis pratensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
