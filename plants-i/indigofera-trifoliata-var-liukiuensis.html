Indigofera trifoliata var. liukiuensis

Indigofera trifoliata var. liukiuensis, commonly known as the Ryukyu indigo plant, is a flowering plant native to the Ryukyu Islands of Japan.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets. The plant produces small, pale yellow to greenish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and rocky areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and traditional herbal uses in the region.
