Iberis saxatilis var. subvelutina

Iberis saxatilis var. subvelutina, commonly known as the velvet cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial plant native to rocky and coastal areas of southern Europe.

It features dense, mat-forming growth with bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are typically covered in a fine, velvety down, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in harsh environments.
