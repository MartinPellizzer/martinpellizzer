Iris grandiflora

Iris grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered iris, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features tall, upright stems that bear showy, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive dark center. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, white or grayish down. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Iris grandiflora is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking blooms.
