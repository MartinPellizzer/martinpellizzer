Ixora brachycotyla

Ixora brachycotyla, commonly known as the short-stalked ixora, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, bright red or orange-red flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Ixora brachycotyla is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
