Indocalamus pallidiflorus

Indocalamus pallidiflorus, commonly known as the pale-flowered bamboo, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in panicles, which are typically found at the ends of the stems. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth surface and a slightly waxy texture.

This bamboo is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its elegant appearance and adaptability to various growing conditions.
