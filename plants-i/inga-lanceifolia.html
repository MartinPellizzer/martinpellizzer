Inga lanceifolia

Inga lanceifolia, commonly known as the lanceleaf inga, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, Colombia, and Peru. The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are often white or pale yellow. Its fruit is a pod that contains several seeds, and the plant is often found in moist, shaded forest environments.

Inga lanceifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
