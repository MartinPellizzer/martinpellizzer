Iris sanguinea var. coronalis

Iris sanguinea var. coronalis, commonly known as the Crowned Iris, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features tall, slender stems that bear showy, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive white crown-like marking at the base of the petals. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are arranged in a fan-like pattern at the base. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant color and unique floral structure.
