Ixora rubrinervis

Ixora rubrinervis, commonly known as the red-veined ixora, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring dense, glossy green leaves with prominent red veins. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, orange-red flowers that bloom throughout the year, especially during the warmer months. Its botanical characteristics include opposite leaves and an inflorescence of bracteate cymes.

Ixora rubrinervis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a hedge due to its attractive appearance and hardiness.
