Indocalamus

Indocalamus, commonly known as golden bamboo, is a genus of grass-like plants native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that often form dense clumps or stands. The leaves are typically narrow, elongated, and have a smooth, waxy surface. Indocalamus species are valued for their ornamental appeal and are often used in horticulture.

They thrive in moist, well-drained soils and are adaptable to a variety of environmental conditions.
