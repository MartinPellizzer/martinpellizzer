Iresine herbacea

Iresine herbacea, commonly known as redleaf ironplant, is a herbaceous perennial native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety red to purple foliage, which often contrasts with its smaller, inconspicuous white flowers. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade. Its leaves are often used in ornamental gardening for their vibrant coloration.

Iresine herbacea is also known for its ability to spread via runners, making it a versatile addition to shaded landscapes.
