Isatis cappadocica var. subradiata

Isatis cappadocica var. subradiata, commonly known as the Cappadocian woad, is a variant of the woad plant native to Turkey.

It is a biennial herbaceous plant that grows up to 1.5 meters tall, featuring large, heart-shaped leaves with a waxy coating. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in late spring, which are followed by spherical, green seed pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

The plant has historical significance in dye production and traditional medicine.
