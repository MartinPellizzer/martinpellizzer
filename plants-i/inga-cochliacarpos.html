Inga cochliacarpos

Inga cochliacarpos, commonly known as the coffee tree, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 15 meters in height. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by elliptical, leathery fruits. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a rough texture with prominent veins.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
