Iris pseudopulcherrima

Iris pseudopulcherrima, commonly known as the tropical iris, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors including yellow, orange, and red. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, with sword-shaped leaves and erect stems. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
