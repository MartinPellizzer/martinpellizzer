Inga laurifolia

Inga laurifolia, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in racemose inflorescences, which are typically followed by pods containing edible seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
