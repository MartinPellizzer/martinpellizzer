Ixeris dentata f. leucantha

Ixeris dentata f. leucantha, commonly known as the white-flowered sandwort, is a perennial flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in clusters. It thrives in well-drained sandy or rocky soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance and resilience.
