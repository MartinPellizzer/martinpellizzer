Isatis besseri subsp. tricostata

Isatis besseri subsp.

tricostata, commonly known as the three-costate woad, is a herbaceous perennial native to East Asia. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other members like mustard and cabbage.

The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves with three distinct costae, or midribs, giving it its common name. It produces small, yellow flowers that are typically clustered in racemes.

This species is often cultivated for its historical use in dye production, similar to other members of the Isatis genus.
