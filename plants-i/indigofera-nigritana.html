Indigofera nigritana

Indigofera nigritana, commonly known as African indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a leguminous shrub that can grow up to 2 meters in height, featuring trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain seeds, and it is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its roots have nitrogen-fixing capabilities, making it valuable for soil enrichment.

The plant is also known for its historical use in dye production, particularly for producing deep blue hues.
