Inga puberula

Inga puberula, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is notable for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in disturbed soils, making it a common pioneer species in secondary forests.

Its fruit, a pod containing edible seeds, is used in traditional medicine and as a food source in some regions.
