Isocoma menziesii var. menziesii

Isocoma menziesii var. menziesii, commonly known as the Menzies' goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its yellow flowers bloom in the summer, attracting various pollinators.

The leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.
