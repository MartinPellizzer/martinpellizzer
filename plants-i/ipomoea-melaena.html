Ipomoea melaena

Ipomoea melaena, commonly known as black morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and striking deep purple to black flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant is a vigorous climber, often found twining around supports in gardens or natural habitats. Its flowers are typically 5 to 7 inches in diameter, with five petals and a trumpet-shaped corolla.

Ipomoea melaena is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for its dramatic floral display.
