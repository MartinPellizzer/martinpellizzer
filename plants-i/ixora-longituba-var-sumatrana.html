Ixora longituba var. sumatrana

Ixora longituba var. sumatrana, commonly known as the Sumatran ixora, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, showy clusters of bright red or orange flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical rainforests or along coastal areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for use in gardens or as a decorative plant.
