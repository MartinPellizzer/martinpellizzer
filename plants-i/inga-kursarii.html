Inga kursarii

Inga kursarii, commonly known as the yellow inga, is a deciduous tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant has compound leaves with a striking yellow coloration, and its flowers are typically clustered in inflorescences. Inga kursarii produces elongated, flat pods that contain several seeds, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
