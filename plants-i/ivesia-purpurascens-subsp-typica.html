Ivesia purpurascens subsp. typica

Ivesia purpurascens subsp.

typica, commonly known as the purple-leaf ivesia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to purple.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive reddish or purplish tinge, which is a key identifying feature. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along dry slopes. Ivesia purpurascens subsp.

typica is a member of the Rosaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal in native plant gardens.
