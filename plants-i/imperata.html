Imperata

Imperata, commonly known as cogongrass, is a perennial grass native to Asia and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its dense, fibrous rhizomes and tall, reddish-purple foliage. The plant produces large, spadix-like inflorescences that are often covered in tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Imperata is highly invasive and can dominate native vegetation in disturbed areas.

Its fibrous roots are used in traditional crafts and as a source of thatch.
