Ipomoea staplesii

Ipomoea staplesii, commonly known as the Cape ivy, is a vigorous climbing vine native to South Africa.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a velvety texture and produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of purple and blue. The plant is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to cover surfaces, making it both ornamental and invasive in certain regions. Its flowers bloom throughout the growing season, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
