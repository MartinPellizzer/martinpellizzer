Nicotiana tabacum var. loxensis

Nicotiana tabacum var. loxensis, commonly known as the Loxensis tobacco, is a variant of the tobacco plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of violet to purple flowers. This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking appearance. The plant contains nicotine and other alkaloids, which are responsible for its psychoactive properties.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and botanical collections.
