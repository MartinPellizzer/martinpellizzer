Nasturtium linifolium

Nasturtium linifolium, commonly known as the creeping nasturtium, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant yellow or orange flowers. The leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine, hair-like structures, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture. This plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often used in gardens for its ornamental value and edible flowers.

Its scientific name reflects its leaf structure, with "linifolium" referring to its linen-like foliage.
