Nettoa chrozophorifolia

Nettoa chrozophorifolia, commonly known as the golden nettle, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and a striking yellowish-green color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in clusters, which are often overlooked due to their modest appearance. Its growth habit is typically herbaceous, with a preference for moist, well-drained soils.

Nettoa chrozophorifolia is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments, such as forest floors or along stream banks.
