Notonia picticaulis

Notonia picticaulis, commonly known as the painted lily, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that range from white to pink and purple. The plant features strap-shaped leaves and produces showy, bell-shaped blooms that attract pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Notonia picticaulis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its vibrant, long-lasting flowers.
