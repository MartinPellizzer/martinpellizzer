Navarretia densifolia var. lanata

Navarretia densifolia var. lanata, commonly known as the woolly navarretia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in spring, which are clustered in umbels. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and foothills.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
