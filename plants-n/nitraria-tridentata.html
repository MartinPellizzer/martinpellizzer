Nitraria tridentata

Nitraria tridentata, commonly known as the desert sandthorn, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Asia and North America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments. Nitraria tridentata is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, which makes it valuable for soil enrichment in degraded landscapes.

It is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
