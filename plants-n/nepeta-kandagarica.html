Nepeta kandagarica

Nepeta kandagarica, commonly known as Kashmir catmint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features opposite, hairy leaves that are typically gray-green in color. Its flowers are small, tubular, and clustered in whorls, producing a fragrant blue or purple hue.

Nepeta kandagarica is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
