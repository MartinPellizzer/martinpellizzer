Nolina juncea

Nolina juncea, commonly known as the silver pine or desert spineflower, is a succulent shrub native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features narrow, cylindrical leaves that are often covered in a powdery white coating, which helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Nolina juncea is well adapted to drought and full sun, making it a resilient plant in desert ecosystems.
