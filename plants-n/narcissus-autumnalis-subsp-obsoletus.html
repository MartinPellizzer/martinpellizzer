Narcissus autumnalis subsp. obsoletus

Narcissus autumnalis subsp.

obsoletus, commonly known as the autumn daffodil, is a botanical species native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and a single, trumpet-shaped flower that typically blooms in late summer to early autumn.

The plant produces a bright yellow corona surrounded by pale yellow petals, and it thrives in well-drained soil in full sun. It is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ease of cultivation.

This subspecies is often used in garden settings for its late-season flowering and resilience in various climates.
