Narcissus × odorus subsp. assoanus

Narcissus × odorus subsp.

assoanus, commonly known as the sweetly scented daffodil, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its trumpet-shaped, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers come in a range of yellow to white hues, often with a contrasting central throat.

This subspecies is prized for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
