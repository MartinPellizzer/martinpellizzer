Notholaena sulcata

Notholaena sulcata, commonly known as the zigzag fern, is a species of fern native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, undulating fronds that give it a zigzag appearance. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with a leathery texture and is often found in moist, shaded environments. Notholaena sulcata thrives in humid climates and is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its unique foliage.

Its reproductive structures include sporangia that release spores for propagation.
