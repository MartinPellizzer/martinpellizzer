Notocactus eugeniae

Notocactus eugeniae, commonly known as the Eugenia notocactus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, globular shape and its ability to store water in its thick, fleshy stems. The plant features areoles, which are specialized structures from which spines, flowers, and branches emerge. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, adding a vibrant touch to its desert habitat.

Notocactus eugeniae is well adapted to survive in harsh, dry conditions, making it a resilient and interesting subject for botanical study.
