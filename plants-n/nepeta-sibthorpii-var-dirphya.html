Nepeta sibthorpii var. dirphya

Nepeta sibthorpii var. dirphya, commonly known as the Kashmiri catmint, is a perennial herb native to the Himalayan regions of India and Pakistan.

It features aromatic, gray-green leaves and produces clusters of small, pale blue to violet flowers. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fragrance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

This variety is also noted for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
