Nassella uspallatensis

Nassella uspallatensis, commonly known as the Uspallata needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and fine, needle-like leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
