Nardurus tenuiculmis

Nardurus tenuiculmis, commonly known as the slender-leafed nardus, is a grass-like plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and compact growth habit, often forming dense mats or cushions. Native to arid and semi-arid regions, it is well adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of grasses.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil in degraded environments due to its extensive root system.
