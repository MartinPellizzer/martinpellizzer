Noccaea procumbens proles crassifolia

Noccaea procumbens proles crassifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved common watercress, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and pungent aroma. The plant typically grows in moist, nutrient-rich soils near streams and wetlands. Its leaves are alternate, broad, and succulent, often with a slightly hairy surface.

The species is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in aquatic or semi-aquatic environments.
