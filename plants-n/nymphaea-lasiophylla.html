Nymphaea lasiophylla

Nymphaea lasiophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf water lily, is a species of aquatic plant native to parts of Asia, including India and China.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in the early morning. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats such as ponds and lakes. Its leaves are usually dark green and have a waxy surface, while its flowers can be white, pink, or purple.

Nymphaea lasiophylla plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
