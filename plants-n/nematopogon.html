Nematopogon

Nematopogon, commonly known as the bog asphodel, is a member of the Xanthorrhoeaceae family.

It is a herbaceous plant that typically grows in wet, acidic environments such as bogs and marshes. The plant features tall, slender stems with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of small, pale yellow or greenish flowers.

Nematopogon plays an important ecological role in nutrient cycling within its wetland habitat.
