Nicotiana glauca

Nicotiana glauca, commonly known as the blueflower tobacco, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers and hairy, glandular leaves. The plant belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known species like tomatoes and peppers. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along riverbanks.

Nicotiana glauca is also notable for its historical use in traditional medicine and as a source of nicotine.
