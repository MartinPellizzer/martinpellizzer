Neolemaireocereus

Neolemaireocereus, commonly known as the Christmas cactus, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its segmented, leaf-like stems that are often used for propagation. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically during the holiday season. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Neolemaireocereus is a popular indoor plant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
