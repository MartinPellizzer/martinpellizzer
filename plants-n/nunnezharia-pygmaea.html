Nunnezharia pygmaea

Nunnezharia pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf orchid, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant has a short, upright stem with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
