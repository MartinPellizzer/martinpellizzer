Nemampsis ternifolia

Nemampsis ternifolia, commonly known as the three-leaf vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, trifoliate leaves that are often arranged in a spiral pattern along its stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, followed by small, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal potential, it is cultivated in some botanical gardens and conservation programs.
