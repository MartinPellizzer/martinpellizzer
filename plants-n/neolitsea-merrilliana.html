Neolitsea merrilliana

Neolitsea merrilliana, commonly known as the Philippine laurustin, is a small evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurel trees and cinnamon plants. The plant is characterized by its elliptical, glossy dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruits are small, round drupes that turn from green to yellowish when mature.

Neolitsea merrilliana is often found in lowland to montane forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
