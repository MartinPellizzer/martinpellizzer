Nasturtium × riparium

Nasturtium × riparium, commonly known as the water nasturtium, is a hybrid plant that combines traits from both *Tropaeolum majus* and *Tropaeolum fendleri*.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, edible yellow or orange flowers. The plant produces heart-shaped, edible leaves that are often used in salads or as a peppery garnish. Its stems are typically succulent and can root at the nodes, allowing it to spread along water surfaces or moist soil.

Nasturtium × riparium thrives in humid environments and is often cultivated in ponds or as a decorative aquatic plant.
