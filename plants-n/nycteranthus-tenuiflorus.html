Nycteranthus tenuiflorus

Nycteranthus tenuiflorus, commonly known as the moonflower, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night and close during the day. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a velvety texture.

The flowers are pollinated primarily by nocturnal animals such as moths and bats.
