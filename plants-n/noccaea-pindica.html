Noccaea pindica

Noccaea pindica, commonly known as the Pindus watercress, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, alternate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in clusters, typical of the family.

Noccaea pindica is often found in moist, calcareous soils and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
