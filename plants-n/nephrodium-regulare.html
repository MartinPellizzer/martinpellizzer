Nephrodium regulare

Nephrodium regulare, commonly known as the common kidney fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive kidney-shaped fronds that are typically dark green and velvety to the touch. The plant produces spores on the undersides of its fronds, which are arranged in clusters called sori. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Nephrodium regulare is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
