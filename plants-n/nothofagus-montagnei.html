Nothofagus montagnei

Nothofagus montagnei, commonly known as the Montagne's nothofagus, is a species of evergreen tree native to the southern Andes of South America.

It belongs to the Nothofagaceae family, which is closely related to the beech family. The plant is characterized by its lanceolate, leathery leaves that are often pubescent on the underside. It produces small, globose cones that mature to release winged seeds.

Nothofagus montagnei typically grows in cool, moist habitats at high elevations, contributing to the biodiversity of its native ecosystems.
