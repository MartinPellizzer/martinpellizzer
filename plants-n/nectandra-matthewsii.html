Nectandra matthewsii

Nectandra matthewsii, commonly known as the Matthew's sandalwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, woody bark and dark green, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by oblong, woody fruits containing aromatic seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for its essential oils and medicinal properties.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
