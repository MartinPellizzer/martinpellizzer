Nepenthes smilesii

Nepenthes smilesii, commonly known as the Malay Giant Pitcher Plant, is a tropical carnivorous plant native to Borneo.

It is characterized by its large, vibrant green pitchers that secrete a digestive enzyme-laden liquid to trap and digest insects. The plant's unique morphology includes a long, narrow stem and waxy, ribbed leaves that form the upper part of the pitcher structure. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires high humidity and nutrient-poor soil to flourish.

Its striking appearance and specialized adaptations make it a fascinating subject in the study of plant evolution and ecology.
