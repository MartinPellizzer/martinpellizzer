Nasturtium coronopifolium

Nasturtium coronopifolium, commonly known as the crownleaf nasturtium, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces vibrant orange or yellow flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Its edible flowers and leaves are often used in culinary applications, adding a peppery flavor to dishes.
