Nassella flaccidula

Nassella flaccidula, commonly known as the slender needlegrass, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its fine, needle-like leaves and compact growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat for small invertebrates and contributing to soil stabilization.
