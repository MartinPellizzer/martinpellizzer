Nassella gibba

Nassella gibba, commonly known as the spikegrass, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces narrow, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Nassella gibba is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for small animals.
