Narcissus tazetta subsp. varians

Narcissus tazetta subsp.

varians, commonly known as the Spanish daffodil, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers.

The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

This subspecies is known for its variability in flower size and color, contributing to its popularity among gardeners.
