Narcissus festalis

Narcissus festalis, commonly known as the Spanish daffodil, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and northern Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers with a striking orange center. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its bulbs are fleshy and store nutrients for growth.

Narcissus festalis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its early spring blooms.
