Nyssa biflora

Nyssa biflora, commonly known as the two-flowered nymphaea, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Nyssaceae and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring. Its fruits are drupes that contain a single seed, and the plant is often found in moist, wooded areas.

Nyssa biflora is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
