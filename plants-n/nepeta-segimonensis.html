Nepeta segimonensis

Nepeta segimonensis, commonly known as the Segimon's catmint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, with a mild mint-like scent.

Nepeta segimonensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
