Nymphaea amazonum f. goudotiana

Nymphaea amazonum f. goudotiana, commonly known as the Amazon water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy, white to pink flowers that bloom above the water surface, often at night. The plant has broad, floating leaves that provide habitat for various aquatic organisms. Its roots anchor it in the muddy substrate of slow-moving rivers and lakes.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in maintaining water quality and supporting aquatic biodiversity.
