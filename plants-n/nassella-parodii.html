Nassella parodii

Nassella parodii, commonly known as bottlebrush grass, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical seed heads that resemble bottle brushes, which are often brightly colored, ranging from red to orange. The plant produces small, wind-pollinated flowers, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Nassella parodii is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to stabilize soil in dry landscapes.
