Nigella assyriaca

Nigella assyriaca, commonly known as Persian cumin or black cumin, is an annual herb native to the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, spherical seeds that are rich in essential oils and have a distinct, pungent aroma. Its botanical features include lance-shaped leaves and a taproot system, which help it thrive in arid environments.

Nigella assyriaca is often cultivated for its seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
