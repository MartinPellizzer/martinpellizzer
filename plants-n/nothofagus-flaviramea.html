Nothofagus flaviramea

Nothofagus flaviramea, commonly known as the yellow-stemmed nothofagus, is a species of evergreen tree native to South America, particularly in parts of Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Nothofagus genus, which is part of the Nothofagaceae family, commonly referred to as the southern beech family. The plant is characterized by its smooth, yellowish-brown bark and lance-shaped leaves that are typically glossy on the upper surface. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by edible, winged fruits that aid in seed dispersal.

This species plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, contributing to forest biodiversity and soil stability.
