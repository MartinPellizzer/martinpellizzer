Nepeta amethystina var. amourensis

Nepeta amethystina var. amourensis, commonly known as the Amethyst Catmint, is a cultivar of the catmint plant native to Europe.

It is a low-growing perennial that features silvery-gray foliage and produces clusters of pale lavender to violet flowers. The plant is known for its aromatic leaves, which release a pleasant scent when crushed. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and as a nectar source for pollinators.
