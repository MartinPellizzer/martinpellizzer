Notocactus scopa f. albicans

Notocactus scopa f. albicans, commonly known as the white-flowered scopa cactus, is a rare and striking variant of the scopa cactus.

It is native to the arid regions of South America, particularly Brazil. This plant is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in white, woolly spines. The flowers are typically white or pale pink, blooming in late spring or early summer.

It is a slow-growing cactus that requires well-draining soil and minimal water to thrive.
