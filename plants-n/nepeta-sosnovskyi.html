Nepeta sosnovskyi

Nepeta sosnovskyi, commonly known as Siberian catmint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its aromatic foliage contains essential oils, contributing to its use in traditional medicine.

Nepeta sosnovskyi is often cultivated for its ornamental value and mild mint-like scent.
