Nassella catamarcensis

Nassella catamarcensis, commonly known as the Catamarca needlegrass, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, adapted to thrive in arid or semi-arid environments.

Nassella catamarcensis is often found in rocky soils and is an important component of local ecosystems.
