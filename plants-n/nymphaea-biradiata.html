Nymphaea biradiata

Nymphaea biradiata, commonly known as the Indian lotus, is a water-living plant belonging to the Nymphaeaceae family.

It is native to the wetlands and slow-moving waters of South and Southeast Asia. The plant features large, showy flowers that can be pink, white, or yellow, depending on the variety. Its leaves are typically round and floating, while its roots anchor it in the muddy substrate.

Nymphaea biradiata plays an important ecological role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and oxygen to water organisms.
