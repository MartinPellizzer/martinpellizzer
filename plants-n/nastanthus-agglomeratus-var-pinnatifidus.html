Nastanthus agglomeratus var. pinnatifidus

Nastanthus agglomeratus var. pinnatifidus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf nastanthus, is a unique and rare plant species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are deeply divided, giving it a feathery appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a challenging plant to cultivate outside its native range.
