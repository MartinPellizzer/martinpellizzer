Nephrolepis laurifolia

Nephrolepis laurifolia, commonly known as the shining maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, pinnately divided fronds that are often glossy and dark green. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and prefers well-drained, moist soil. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds in clusters called sori.

Nephrolepis laurifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
