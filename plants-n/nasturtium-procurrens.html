Nasturtium procurrens

Nasturtium procurrens, commonly known as creeping nasturtium, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit, with slender stems that often root at the nodes. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers that are typically borne on slender peduncles. Its heart-shaped leaves are glossy and slightly hairy, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Nasturtium procurrens is often cultivated as a ground cover or in hanging baskets due to its compact growth and colorful blooms.
