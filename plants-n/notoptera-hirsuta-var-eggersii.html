Notoptera hirsuta var. eggersii

Notoptera hirsuta var. eggersii, commonly known as the Eggers' velvetleaf, is a variant of the velvetleaf species native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are clustered in terminal spikes. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in agricultural settings.
