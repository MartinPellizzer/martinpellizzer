Neanotis calycina

Neanotis calycina, commonly known as the coral bell, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, woodland habitats. The plant features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a distinctive coral-like appearance. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth texture.

Neanotis calycina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
