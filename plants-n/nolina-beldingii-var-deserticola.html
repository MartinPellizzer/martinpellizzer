Nolina beldingii var. deserticola

Nolina beldingii var. deserticola, commonly known as the desert nolina, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette-forming structure composed of thick, fleshy, gray-green leaves that are covered in a fine layer of white wax. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers on short, branched inflorescences, typically blooming in late spring or early summer. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and low maintenance requirements in xeriscaping.
