Neuwiedia ochracea

Neuwiedia ochracea, commonly known as the yellow-flowered ironwood, is a rare and critically endangered plant native to the island of Madagascar.

It is characterized by its woody structure and distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Neuwiedia ochracea thrives in specific microhabitats, typically in rocky or limestone soils.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is listed as vulnerable in the IUCN Red List.
