Neomammillaria sempervivi

Neomammillaria sempervivi, commonly known as the evergreen mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and a dense covering of small, rounded tubercles. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which bloom from areoles located near the tips of the spines. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Neomammillaria sempervivi is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
