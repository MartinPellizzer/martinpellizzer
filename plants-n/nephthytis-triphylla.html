Nephthytis triphylla

Nephthytis triphylla, commonly known as the snake plant, is a tropical evergreen plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its upright, sword-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a glossy texture. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and requires minimal water, making it an ideal indoor plant. Its leaves are arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are rarely seen.

Nephthytis triphylla is also noted for its air-purifying properties, contributing to improved indoor air quality.
