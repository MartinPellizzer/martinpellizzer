Nymphaea stellata maculata

Nymphaea stellata maculata, commonly known as the spotted water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features broad, floating leaves that are often speckled with dark spots, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white, with a distinctive star-like appearance. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a habitat for aquatic life and its ornamental value in water gardens.
