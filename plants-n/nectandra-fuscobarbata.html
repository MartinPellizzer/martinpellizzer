Nectandra fuscobarbata

Nectandra fuscobarbata, commonly known as the black-bearded sandarac, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic, often resinous wood. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by elliptical, leathery fruits that contain winged seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments within lowland rainforests.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
