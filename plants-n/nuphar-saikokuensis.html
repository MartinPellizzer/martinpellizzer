Nuphar saikokuensis

Nuphar saikokuensis, commonly known as the Japanese water shield, is a aquatic plant native to Japan.

It features broad, floating leaves with a waxy surface that helps repel water. The plant produces yellowish flowers that bloom above the water surface. Its roots are adapted to anchor in muddy substrates at the bottom of ponds and lakes.

Nuphar saikokuensis plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small organisms and contributing to water quality through oxygen production.
