Nicipe juncifolia

Nicipe juncifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along stream banks, wetlands, and other damp habitats. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are often green or reddish in color, and its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and arranged in dense clusters. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early summer, which are clustered in spike-like inflorescences.

Nicipe juncifolia is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for aquatic and terrestrial wildlife.
