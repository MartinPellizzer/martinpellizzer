Najas marina subsp. susiana

Najas marina subsp.

susiana, commonly known as the narrowleaf watermilfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater habitats in the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, whorled leaves that are typically less than 1 cm in length.

The plant produces tiny, green flowers that are clustered in spicate inflorescences, often emerging from the water surface. It thrives in still or slow-moving waters with moderate to high light penetration.

Due to its rapid growth, it can become invasive in certain environments if not managed properly.
