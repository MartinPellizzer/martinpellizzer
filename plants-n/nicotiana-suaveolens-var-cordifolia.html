Nicotiana suaveolens var. cordifolia

Nicotiana suaveolens var. cordifolia, commonly known as the sweet tobacco or heart-leaved tobacco, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink to purple. The plant is known for its strong, pleasant aroma, which is often used in perfumes and aromatherapy. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and botanical collections.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its historical use in traditional medicine.
