Narcissus × turrensis

Narcissus × turrensis, commonly known as the Turrensis daffodil, is a hybrid species within the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to white. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, featuring linear, grass-like leaves. Native to the Iberian Peninsula, it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

This ornamental plant is valued for its early spring blooming and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
