Nasa puma-chini

Nasa puma-chini, commonly known as the "moonflower of the Andes," is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Peru.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its large, white, fragrant flowers that bloom at night. The plant has thick, fleshy leaves and produces small, round berries that turn red when ripe. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its unique blooming pattern and cultural significance, it is valued both for its ornamental beauty and traditional uses.
