Nymphaea capensis f. rosea

Nymphaea capensis f. rosea, commonly known as the Cape rose water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to South Africa.

It features broad, heart-shaped leaves that float on the water's surface, often with a waxy texture. The plant produces showy, rose-pink flowers that bloom in summer, adding vibrant color to ponds and lakes. Its flowers are typically fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in water gardens.
