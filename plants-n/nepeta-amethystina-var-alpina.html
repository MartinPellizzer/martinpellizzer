Nepeta amethystina var. alpina

Nepeta amethystina var. alpina, commonly known as alpine catmint, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features aromatic, gray-green foliage and produces clusters of pale lavender to violet flowers. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and is well adapted to rocky or well-drained soils. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its fragrant leaves and long blooming period.
