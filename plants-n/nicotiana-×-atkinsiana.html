Nicotiana × atkinsiana

Nicotiana × atkinsiana, commonly known as the hybrid tobacco plant, is a species resulting from the cross between Nicotiana sylvestris and Nicotiana tabacum.

It is characterized by its distinctive blue or violet flowers and hairy foliage, which are typical features of the Nicotiana genus. This plant is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, where it thrives in arid to semi-arid environments. It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants such as tomatoes and potatoes.

The botanical interest in N. × atkinsiana lies in its genetic diversity and potential for use in breeding programs for ornamental and agricultural purposes.
