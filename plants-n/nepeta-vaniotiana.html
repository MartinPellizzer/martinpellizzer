Nepeta vaniotiana

Nepeta vaniotiana, commonly known as the Vaniotian catmint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often fragrant and have a mint-like scent, which can be used in herbal remedies.

Nepeta vaniotiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in gardens.
