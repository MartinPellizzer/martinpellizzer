Nothofagus solandri

Nothofagus solandri, commonly known as the Antarctic beech, is a species of evergreen tree native to southern South America.

It is characterized by its dark, smooth bark and lanceolate, glossy leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the branches. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by woody, spherical fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in cool, temperate climates and is often found in montane and subantarctic regions.

Nothofagus solandri plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
