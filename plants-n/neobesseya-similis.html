Neobesseya similis

Neobesseya similis, commonly known as the narrow-leafed bottlebrush, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical flower clusters that resemble bottlebrushes, typically blooming in pink or red hues. The plant has narrow, leathery leaves that are arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathland and coastal scrub environments.

Neobesseya similis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
