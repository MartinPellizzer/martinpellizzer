Nymphaea alba var. biradiata

Nymphaea alba var. biradiata, commonly known as the white water lily, is a aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features large, showy white flowers that bloom above the water surface, often with a subtle pinkish hue. The plant has broad, floating leaves that provide shade and habitat for aquatic life. Its roots anchor in the muddy substrate, while its stems support the foliage and flowers.

It plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by improving water quality and supporting biodiversity.
