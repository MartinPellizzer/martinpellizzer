Nandina domestica f. purpurea

Nandina domestica f. purpurea, commonly known as the red flame nandina, is a cultivar of the nandina plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant red foliage, which contrasts strikingly with its glossy dark green leaves. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers in the spring, followed by red berries in the fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is hardy in USDA zones 6 to 9.

This ornamental shrub is often used in gardens for its winter interest and compact growth habit.
