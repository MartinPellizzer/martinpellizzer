Nipponocalamus virens

Nipponocalamus virens, commonly known as the Japanese green bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is native to East Asia, particularly Japan, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and long, narrow leaves that are typically a vibrant green color. It is often used in traditional landscaping and is valued for its ornamental appearance.

Nipponocalamus virens is also known for its resilience and ability to grow in a variety of soil types.
