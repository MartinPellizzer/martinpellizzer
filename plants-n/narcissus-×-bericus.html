Narcissus × bericus

Narcissus × bericus, commonly known as the Dutch narcissus, is a hybrid species within the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its robust bulbs and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that typically bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant produces 4 to 12 narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from the base of the stem. Its flowers are usually white or yellow with a prominent central tongue, and it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens.

Narcissus × bericus is well-suited to temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.
