Narcissus papyraceus var. panizzianus

Narcissus papyraceus var. panizzianus, commonly known as the Panizzianus daffodil, is a rare and ornamental variety of daffodil native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves and a single, showy flower with a distinctive, narrow trumpet-shaped corona. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30-50 centimeters and prefers well-drained, sandy soils. It is often found in rocky or coastal areas, where it thrives in full sun.

This variety is valued for its unique appearance and is considered a significant species in the study of daffodil evolution and taxonomy.
