Nigella ciliaris

Nigella ciliaris, commonly known as the Persian melon or ciliate fennel, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, round seeds that are often used in culinary applications, similar to cumin or fennel seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a suitable candidate for Mediterranean climates.

Nigella ciliaris is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
