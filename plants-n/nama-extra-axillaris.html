Nama extra-axillaris

Nama extra-axillaris, commonly known as the coastal dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically thrives in sandy or rocky coastal environments. The plant features basal leaves with a rough texture and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. Its scientific name reflects its unique growth habit, with "extra-axillaris" referring to the presence of additional leaves in the axils of its stems.

Nama extra-axillaris is adapted to withstand coastal conditions, including salt spray and strong winds.
