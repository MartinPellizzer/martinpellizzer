Nasa solata

Nasa solata, commonly known as the sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers with a dark center, and its tall, sturdy stem. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in full sunlight. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered in a fine layer of hair.

Nasa solata is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and for its seeds, which are used in cooking and bird feeding.
