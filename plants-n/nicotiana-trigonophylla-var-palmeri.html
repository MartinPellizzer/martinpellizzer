Nicotiana trigonophylla var. palmeri

Nicotiana trigonophylla var. palmeri, commonly known as the Palmer's tobacco plant, is a herbaceous species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink to purple in color. The plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in arid to semi-arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Botanically, it belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is closely related to other tobacco species.
