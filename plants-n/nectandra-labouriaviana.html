Nectandra labouriaviana

Nectandra labouriaviana, commonly known as the Labouriava sandalwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Caribbean, particularly found in the Lesser Antilles.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other aromatic woods like cinnamon and bay laurel. The tree is valued for its fragrant, dark-colored heartwood, which is used in the production of fine furniture and specialty items. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a glossy surface, while its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences.

Nectandra labouriaviana plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
