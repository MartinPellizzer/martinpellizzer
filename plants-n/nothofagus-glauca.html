Nothofagus glauca

Nothofagus glauca, commonly known as the silver beech, is a species of evergreen tree native to southern South America.

It belongs to the Nothofagaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, silver-gray bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant is notable for its ability to thrive in temperate rainforests and high-altitude regions. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by edible, woody fruits.

Nothofagus glauca plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
