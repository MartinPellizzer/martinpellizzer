Neolepisorus pappei

Neolepisorus pappei, commonly known as the Pappe's shield fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its leathery, dark green fronds that are typically divided into lanceolate segments. The plant produces sporangia on the underside of its leaves, which are clustered in sori surrounded by indusia. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Neolepisorus pappei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
