Notocactus tetracanthus

Notocactus tetracanthus, commonly known as the four-armed cactus, is a distinctive species native to the deserts of South America.

It is characterized by its short, thick stems that branch into four to six arms, giving it a unique, almost star-like appearance. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are typically short-lived but attract various pollinators. Its thick, fleshy stems are adapted to store water, allowing it to survive in arid environments.

Notocactus tetracanthus is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts.
