Nectandra cinnamomoides

Nectandra cinnamomoides, commonly known as the cinnamon wood or sweet wood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like cinnamon and laurel. The tree is characterized by its dark, aromatic bark and glossy, elliptical leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, typically clustered in inflorescences, and it produces oval, woody fruits containing aromatic seeds.

The plant is valued for its essential oils and has been used in perfumery and as a medicinal herb.
