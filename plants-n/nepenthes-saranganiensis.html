Nepenthes saranganiensis

Nepenthes saranganiensis, commonly known as the Sarangani pitcher plant, is a tropical carnivorous plant native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its large, elongated pitcher-shaped leaves that secrete a sticky digestive fluid. The plant thrives in humid, nutrient-poor environments, often found in montane rainforests. Its nectar-producing rim attracts insects, which then fall into the trap.

The botanical features of N. saranganiensis make it a unique subject of study in both botany and ecological research.
