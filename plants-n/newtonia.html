Newtonia

Newtonia, commonly known as the African violet, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Gesneriaceae.

These plants are native to tropical regions of Africa and are characterized by their compact growth habit and showy, tubular flowers. Newtonia species typically have thick, fleshy leaves that help them conserve water in their natural habitat. They are popular as ornamental plants due to their vibrant colors and ability to thrive in indoor environments.

The flowers often bloom in shades of violet, pink, and white, adding aesthetic value to gardens and homes.
