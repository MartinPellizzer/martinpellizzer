Nymphaea gigantea f. alba

Nymphaea gigantea f. alba, commonly known as the giant white water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to tropical regions.

It features large, showy white flowers that open in the early morning and close by midday. The plant has broad, heart-shaped leaves that float on the water surface, supported by long, slender stems. Its botanical characteristics include a robust root system that anchors it in muddy substrates.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ecological role in wetland habitats.
