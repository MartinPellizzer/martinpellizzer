Neuroscapha martiana

Neuroscapha martiana, commonly known as the mountain orchid, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, evergreen growth habit. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale pink, often clustered in racemes. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, adapted to the cold and dry conditions of its habitat.

Neuroscapha martiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in specialized gardens.
