Narcissus × palentinus

Narcissus × palentinus, commonly known as the Dutch narcissus, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Narcissus tazetta and Narcissus poeticus.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers with six to eight petals and a prominent central cup. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with strap-shaped leaves that are broad and grass-like. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often blooming in early spring.

This variety is prized for its fragrance and is widely cultivated in gardens for ornamental purposes.
