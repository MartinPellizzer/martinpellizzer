Nectandra decandra

Nectandra decandra, commonly known as the fragrant wood or yellow sandalwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its aromatic, reddish-brown heartwood that is highly valued for its fragrance and durability. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically pollinated by insects, followed by the development of small, oval-shaped fruits. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a leathery texture with a strong aromatic scent.

Nectandra decandra is often found in lowland rainforests and is known for its ecological and commercial significance.
