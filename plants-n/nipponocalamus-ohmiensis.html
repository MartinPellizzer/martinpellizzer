Nipponocalamus ohmiensis

Nipponocalamus ohmiensis, commonly known as the Ohmi bamboo, is a species of grass native to Japan.

It belongs to the family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, upright culms and narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as along stream banks and in forest understories. Its rhizome system is well-developed, allowing it to spread vegetatively and form dense clumps.

Nipponocalamus ohmiensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
