Norantea peduncularis

Norantea peduncularis, commonly known as the silverleaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking silver-green foliage and clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Norantea peduncularis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and ability to thrive in controlled indoor conditions.
