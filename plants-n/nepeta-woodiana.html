Nepeta woodiana

Nepeta woodiana, commonly known as Wood's catmint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite leaves that are often gray-green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, lavender-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. It is a member of the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is known for its aromatic properties.

Nepeta woodiana is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
