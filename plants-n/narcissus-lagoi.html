Narcissus lagoi

Narcissus lagoi, commonly known as the Lagoi daffodil, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to the island of Lagoi in the Philippines and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers with a white throat. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has narrow, grass-like leaves. Its bulbs are adapted to store nutrients, allowing it to thrive in its native habitat.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
