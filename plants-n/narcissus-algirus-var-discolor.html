Narcissus algirus var. discolor

Narcissus algirus var. discolor, commonly known as the white narcissus or the Spanish daffodil, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces a single, showy flower with a white perianth and a yellow-orange corona. The plant typically blooms in early spring, adding a delicate touch to gardens and natural habitats. It prefers well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and woodland settings.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and valued addition to the narcissus family.
