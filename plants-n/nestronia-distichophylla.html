Nestronia distichophylla

Nestronia distichophylla, commonly known as the two-leaf fern, is a unique fern species native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive fronds that are divided into two pairs of opposite, elongated leaflets. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as along forest floors or in rocky crevices. Its reproductive structures include sporangia that produce spores, which are essential for its propagation.

The botanical features of Nestronia distichophylla make it an interesting subject for studies in plant morphology and ecology.
