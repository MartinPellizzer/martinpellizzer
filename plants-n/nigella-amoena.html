Nigella amoena

Nigella amoena, commonly known as the Persian melilot, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia and the Middle East.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces seed pods that split open to release tiny, black seeds, which are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. Nigella amoena thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its historical use in traditional medicine.
