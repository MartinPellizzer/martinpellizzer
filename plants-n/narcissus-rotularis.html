Narcissus rotularis

Narcissus rotularis, commonly known as the paperwhite narcissus, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to the Iberian Peninsula and is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in winter or early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces a cluster of small, bell-shaped flowers. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well-suited for indoor growing due to its low light requirements.

Narcissus rotularis is also known for its ability to thrive in humid environments and is commonly used in floral arrangements.
