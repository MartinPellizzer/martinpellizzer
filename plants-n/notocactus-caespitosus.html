Notocactus caespitosus

Notocactus caespitosus, commonly known as the clustered cactus or the desert rose cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and a dense arrangement of cylindrical stems that form a bushy appearance. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are typically borne at the base of the stems. Its thick, fleshy stems are adapted to store water, allowing it to thrive in dry conditions.

Notocactus caespitosus is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low maintenance requirements and aesthetic appeal.
