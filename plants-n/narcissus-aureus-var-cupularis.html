Narcissus aureus var. cupularis

Narcissus aureus var. cupularis, commonly known as the golden cup narcissus, is a botanical variant of the daffodil species.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, cup-shaped corona and elongated, narrow petals. This plant typically grows to a height of about 30-50 centimeters and is native to the Mediterranean region. The flowers bloom in early spring, often before the leaves fully emerge.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
