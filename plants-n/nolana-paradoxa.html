Nolana paradoxa

Nolana paradoxa, commonly known as the paradoxal nolana, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats, showcasing adaptations to arid environments. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that are often pink or purple in color. Its leaves are usually narrow and succulent, helping to conserve water.

Nolana paradoxa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
