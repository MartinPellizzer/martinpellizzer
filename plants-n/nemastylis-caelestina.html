Nemastylis caelestina

Nemastylis caelestina, commonly known as the skyflower, is a striking perennial plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes.

It features showy, blue-violet flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as butterflies and bees. The plant has a rosette of silver-green, hairy leaves that help it retain moisture in its arid environment. Its stems are slender and erect, often reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters.

Botanically, it belongs to the Apiaceae family, sharing characteristics with other members like carrots and parsley.
