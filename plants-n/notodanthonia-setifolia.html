Notodanthonia setifolia

Notodanthonia setifolia, commonly known as the silky hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, silky-textured leaves that are often covered in short, soft hairs. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. Its inflorescence consists of slender, spike-like structures that bear small, inconspicuous flowers.

Notodanthonia setifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
