Nephrolepis superba

Nephrolepis superba, commonly known as the Boston fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its lush, feathery fronds that grow in a dense, cascading manner, making it a popular ornamental plant. The plant has pinnately compound leaves, with each frond consisting of multiple smaller leaflets arranged along a central axis. It thrives in shady, humid environments and requires well-draining, moist soil to flourish.

Nephrolepis superba is also noted for its ability to purify indoor air, contributing to its popularity as a houseplant.
