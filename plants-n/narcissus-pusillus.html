Narcissus pusillus

Narcissus pusillus, commonly known as the dwarf daffodil, is a small, flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Amaryllis family and typically grows to a height of only a few inches. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with a distinctive, narrow corona. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its compact size and ornamental value, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.
