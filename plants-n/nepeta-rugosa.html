Nepeta rugosa

Nepeta rugosa, commonly known as catmint, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish-green leaves and clusters of small, pale blue or purple flowers. The plant belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is known for its aromatic properties. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Nepeta rugosa is also noted for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
