Nectandra amara

Nectandra amara, commonly known as bitter nectandra, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its dark, aromatic bark and glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that are followed by woody, elongated fruits containing aromatic seeds. Nectandra amara is valued for its medicinal properties, with various parts of the plant used in traditional remedies.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and pharmacological contexts.
