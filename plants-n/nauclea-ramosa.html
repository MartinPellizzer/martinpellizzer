Nauclea ramosa

Nauclea ramosa, commonly known as the hairy nauclea, is a tropical shrub native to regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its branched stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by bean-like pods containing several seeds. Nauclea ramosa is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forest understories and along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the ecosystems it inhabits.
