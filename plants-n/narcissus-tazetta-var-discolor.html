Narcissus tazetta var. discolor

Narcissus tazetta var. discolor, commonly known as the White Beauty daffodil, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It produces clusters of fragrant, white flowers with a yellow corona, distinguishing it from other daffodil varieties. The plant typically blooms in early spring, often before the leaves fully emerge. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Its ornamental value and ease of cultivation have contributed to its widespread use in floral arrangements and landscapes.
