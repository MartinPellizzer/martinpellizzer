Neomyrtus vitis-idaea

Neomyrtus vitis-idaea, commonly known as whortleberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to northern regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense clusters of small, dark blue to black berries. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands, bogs, and rocky areas. Its leaves are typically dark green and glossy, with a leathery texture.

Neomyrtus vitis-idaea is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
