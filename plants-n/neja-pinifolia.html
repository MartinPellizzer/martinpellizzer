Neja pinifolia

Neja pinifolia, commonly known as the needle-leaved plant, is a species characterized by its unique botanical features.

It typically exhibits needle-like leaves, which are adapted for reducing water loss in arid environments. The plant belongs to the Pinaceae family, which includes various coniferous species. Neja pinifolia is often found in mountainous regions where it thrives in cooler, well-drained soils.

Its morphology reflects evolutionary adaptations to harsh climatic conditions.
