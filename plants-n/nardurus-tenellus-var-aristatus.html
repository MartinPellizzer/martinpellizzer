Nardurus tenellus var. aristatus

Nardurus tenellus var. aristatus, commonly known as the slender nardus, is a low-growing grass-like plant native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, with a distinctive bluish waxy coating. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in dense spikes, often appearing in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental appearance and drought tolerance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
