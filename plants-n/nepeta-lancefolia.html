Nepeta lancefolia

Nepeta lancefolia, commonly known as catmint, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinctive minty scent when crushed.

Nepeta lancefolia is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and as a source of nectar for wildlife.
