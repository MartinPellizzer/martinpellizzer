Nepeta parnassica

Nepeta parnassica, commonly known as Parnassian catmint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their calming properties.

Nepeta parnassica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in natural habitats.
