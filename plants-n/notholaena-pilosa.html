Notholaena pilosa

Notholaena pilosa, commonly known as the hairy notholaena, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are densely covered in soft, white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a unique member of the Cyperaceae family, or sedge family.
