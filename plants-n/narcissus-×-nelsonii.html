Narcissus × nelsonii

Narcissus × nelsonii, commonly known as the Nelson's daffodil, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically bright yellow with a white or pale green corona. The plant has strap-like, linear leaves that are often bluish-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its blooming period occurs in early spring, adding a vibrant splash of color to gardens.
