Neopieris nitida

Neopieris nitida, commonly known as the silverleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a silvery or whitish bloom, giving it its common name. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are arranged in a loose cluster, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers.

Neopieris nitida plays a role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
