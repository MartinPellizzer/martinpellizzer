Nasa laxa

Nasa laxa, commonly known as the looseleaf nasa, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and purple. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in cooler climates. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Nasa laxa is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its traditional uses in local herbal medicine.
