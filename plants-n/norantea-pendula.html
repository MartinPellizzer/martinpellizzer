Norantea pendula

Norantea pendula, commonly known as the weeping lily, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its drooping, pendulous flowers that give it a graceful appearance. The plant has strap-like leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with a fragrant scent that attracts pollinators.

Norantea pendula thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in humid forests.
