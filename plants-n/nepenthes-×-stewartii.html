Nepenthes × stewartii

Nepenthes × stewartii, commonly known as the hybrid pitcher plant, is a fascinating carnivorous plant that results from the crossbreeding of Nepenthes mirabilis and Nepenthes rafflesiana.

It is native to the tropical regions of Borneo and is characterized by its large, colorful pitcher-shaped leaves that secrete a digestive fluid. The plant's unique morphology includes a long, narrow stem and elongated, waxy leaves that form the iconic trapping structures. Nepenthes × stewartii thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires consistent moisture and nutrient-rich soil to flourish.

Its hybrid nature makes it a popular subject in botanical studies and horticultural cultivation.
