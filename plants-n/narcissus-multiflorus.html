Narcissus multiflorus

Narcissus multiflorus, commonly known as the multi-flowered daffodil, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and western Asia, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. The plant is characterized by its cluster of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming a dense rosette at the base.

Narcissus multiflorus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, early-season blooms.
