Nastus chusque

Nastus chusque, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in a fine layer of silky hair.

Nastus chusque is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
