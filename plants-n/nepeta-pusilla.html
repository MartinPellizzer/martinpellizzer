Nepeta pusilla

Nepeta pusilla, commonly known as dwarf catmint, is a small perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright, branched stems covered in fine, grayish hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a fuzzy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Nepeta pusilla is valued for its aromatic foliage and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
