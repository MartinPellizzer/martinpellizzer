Nectandra debilis

Nectandra debilis, commonly known as the weak nectandra, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments within lowland forests. Its wood is valued for its durability and aromatic properties, though it is not widely used commercially.

Nectandra debilis plays a role in supporting local biodiversity by providing habitat and food for various insect and bird species.
