Nephrotheca

Nephrotheca, commonly known as the kidney-shaped seed case, is a unique botanical structure found in certain plants, particularly within the family Myristicaceae.

It is a hard, membranous covering that encloses the seeds, often exhibiting a distinctive bilobed or kidney-like shape. The nephrotheca plays a crucial role in protecting the developing seeds from environmental stress and mechanical damage. Its structure is composed of two fused valves, which may separate upon maturity to release the seeds.

This specialized seed capsule is an adaptation that enhances the plant's reproductive success in specific ecological niches.
