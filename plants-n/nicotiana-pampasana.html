Nicotiana pampasana

Nicotiana pampasana, commonly known as the Pampas Tobacco, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought conditions. Its leaves are often hairy and have a distinctive, somewhat fleshy texture.

It is cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used historically for its nicotine content.
