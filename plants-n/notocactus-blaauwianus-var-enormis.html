Notocactus blaauwianus var. enormis

Notocactus blaauwianus var. enormis, commonly known as the giant blue barrel cactus, is a striking member of the Cactaceae family native to South America.

This plant is characterized by its massive, spherical stems that can grow up to several meters in diameter, often featuring a bluish-gray hue with prominent areoles. The stems are covered in dense, white to pale blue spines, which provide protection and aid in reducing water loss. It produces large, showy yellow flowers that bloom at the base of the stem, typically during the warmer months.

The plant is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems and exhibiting slow, steady growth over time.
