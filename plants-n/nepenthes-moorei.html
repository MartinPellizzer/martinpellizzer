Nepenthes moorei

Nepenthes moorei, commonly known as the Moore's pitcher plant, is a tropical carnivorous plant native to Borneo.

It is characterized by its large, elongated pitcher-shaped leaves that secrete a digestive fluid to trap and digest insects. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires consistently moist soil. Its unique morphology includes a waxy, ridged structure on the pitcher's surface, which aids in preventing prey from escaping.

Nepenthes moorei is also notable for its ability to absorb nutrients from the rainwater that collects in its pitchers.
