Nasturtium hispidum

Nasturtium hispidum, commonly known as the hairy nasturtium, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces bright orange or yellow flowers that bloom throughout the summer. Its flowers and young leaves are edible, often used in salads or as a garnish.

Nasturtium hispidum is also valued for its ability to attract beneficial insects to gardens.
