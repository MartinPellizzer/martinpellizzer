Narcissus × sulphureus

Narcissus × sulphureus, commonly known as the golden narcissus, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Narcissus tazetta and Narcissus jonquilla.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers with a distinctive orange center, which bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, featuring strap-like leaves and a bulbous root system. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

This species is also valued for its fragrance and is often used in horticultural breeding programs.
