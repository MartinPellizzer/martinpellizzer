Nephradenia filipes

Nephradenia filipes, commonly known as the slender leaf nephradenia, is a member of the family Polygonaceae, characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, clustered flowers.

It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia, where it thrives in sandy or rocky soils. The plant has a low, creeping growth habit with elongated, linear leaves that help reduce water loss. Its flowers are typically greenish or brownish, with a distinctive calyx that persists after flowering.

Nephradenia filipes is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and diuretic properties.
