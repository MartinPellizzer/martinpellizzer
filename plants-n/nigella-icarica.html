Nigella icarica

Nigella icarica, commonly known as Persian sesame or black cumin, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, dark brown seeds that are highly aromatic and used in traditional medicine and culinary practices. Nigella icarica thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates.

Its botanical features include compound leaves and a short, erect growth habit.
