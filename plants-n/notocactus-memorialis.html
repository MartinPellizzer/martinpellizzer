Notocactus memorialis

Notocactus memorialis, commonly known as the memory cactus, is a rare and unique species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that can grow up to 30 centimeters in height. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers during the cooler months, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits. Its thick, fleshy stems store water, allowing it to thrive in dry environments.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in the wild.
