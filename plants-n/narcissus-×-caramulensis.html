Narcissus × caramulensis

Narcissus × caramulensis, commonly known as the Caramulensis daffodil, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Narcissus tazetta and Narcissus poeticus.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers with a distinctive white or green throat, often featuring a prominent central blotch. The plant typically grows to a height of 20–40 cm, with narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a basal rosette. It is native to the Mediterranean region and is cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Narcissus × caramulensis prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for spring-flowering bulbs.
