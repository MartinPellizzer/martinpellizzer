Nephrodium macrophyllum var. pilosum

Nephrodium macrophyllum var. pilosum, commonly known as the hairy broad-leaf nephroid, is a rare fern species characterized by its large, leathery, and hairy leaves.

The leaves are typically bipinnate, with a distinctive broad, rounded shape and a dense covering of fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant's reproductive structures include sporangia that produce spores for propagation.

Its unique morphology and habitat preferences make it a notable species in botanical studies.
