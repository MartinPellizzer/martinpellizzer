Najas guadalupensis subsp. olivacea

Najas guadalupensis subsp.

olivacea, commonly known as the olive watermilfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater habitats in the Americas. It belongs to the Najadaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, whorled leaves.

The plant typically grows in shallow, slow-moving waters and is often found in ponds and lakes. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, usually appearing in clusters, and it reproduces both sexually and vegetatively.

This species plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
