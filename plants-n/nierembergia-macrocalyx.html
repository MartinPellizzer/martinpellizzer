Nierembergia macrocalyx

Nierembergia macrocalyx, commonly known as the large-flowered bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and can be propagated through seeds or division.

Nierembergia macrocalyx is relatively low maintenance and attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
