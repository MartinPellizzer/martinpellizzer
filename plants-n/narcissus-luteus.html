Narcissus luteus

Narcissus luteus, commonly known as the yellow daffodil, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and has linear, grass-like leaves. Native to southern Europe, it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Its showy flowers make it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
