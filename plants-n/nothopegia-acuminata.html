Nothopegia acuminata

Nothopegia acuminata, commonly known as the slender bottlebrush, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical inflorescences that resemble bottle brushes, typically blooming in shades of pink to red. The plant has aromatic leaves that are often used in traditional medicine for their antiseptic properties. It thrives in well-drained sandy soils and is adapted to coastal environments with high salinity and wind exposure.

Nothopegia acuminata is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and resilience to harsh coastal conditions.
