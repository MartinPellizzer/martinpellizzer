Nuttallanthus canadensis f. albinus

Nuttallanthus canadensis f. albinus, commonly known as the whiteflower wild indigo, is a variant of the wild indigo species characterized by its pale to white flowers.

This subspecies is native to the eastern United States and thrives in moist, well-drained soils in woodland areas. It is a herbaceous perennial that produces small, lance-shaped leaves and is often found in shaded or partially shaded environments. The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important member of the legume family.
