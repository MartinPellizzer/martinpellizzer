Nycterium cardaminifolium

Nycterium cardaminifolium, commonly known as the moonflower of the rock, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its large, white, nocturnal flowers that open at night and close by morning. The plant has heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found growing on rocky outcrops.

Its unique blooming pattern and habitat make it a rare and fascinating species in the botanical world.
