Norantea paraensis

Norantea paraensis, commonly known as the Paraense Orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Amazon rainforest of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful blooms that often feature intricate patterns. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees without deriving nutrients from them. Its leaves are broad and waxy, adapted to retain moisture in the humid, shaded environment of the rainforest.

Norantea paraensis plays a vital role in the ecosystem by providing nectar for pollinators and contributing to the biodiversity of its habitat.
