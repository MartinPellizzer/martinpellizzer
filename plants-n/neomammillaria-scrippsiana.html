Neomammillaria scrippsiana

Neomammillaria scrippsiana, commonly known as the Scrippsian milk barrel cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Baja California, Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and a dense covering of white to pale blue spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking contrast to its green body. Its thick, fleshy stems are adapted for water storage, typical of desert plants.

Neomammillaria scrippsiana is a slow-growing cactus that thrives in well-draining soil and full sun.
