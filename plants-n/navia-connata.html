Navia connata

Navia connata, commonly known as the coastal navia, is a flowering plant native to the rocky coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that store water, enabling it to thrive in arid and saline environments. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring, adding a delicate touch to its rugged habitat. Navia connata is often found growing on cliffs and sandy soils, where it is exposed to strong winds and salt spray.

Its unique adaptations make it an important component of coastal ecosystems.
