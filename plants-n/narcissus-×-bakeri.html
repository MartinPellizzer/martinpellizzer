Narcissus × bakeri

Narcissus × bakeri, commonly known as the Baker's daffodil, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between N. tazetta and N. cyclaminiflorum.

It is characterized by its large, showy, funnel-shaped flowers that typically range in color from yellow to orange. The plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. It is a perennial bulbous plant that produces multiple stems and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The foliage consists of strap-like leaves that emerge in early spring, preceding the blooms.
