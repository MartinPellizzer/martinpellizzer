Nasturtium amphibium var. integrifolium

Nasturtium amphibium var. integrifolium, commonly known as the watercress nasturtium, is a herbaceous plant characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of yellow-orange flowers.

It thrives in moist, aquatic or semi-aquatic environments, often found along the edges of ponds and streams. The leaves are edible, possessing a peppery and slightly spicy flavor, while the flowers are also used in culinary applications. This variety is known for its ability to tolerate both waterlogged and slightly dry conditions, making it adaptable to a range of wetland habitats.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for both ecological and gastronomic purposes.
