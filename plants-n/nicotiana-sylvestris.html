Nicotiana sylvestris

Nicotiana sylvestris, commonly known as wild tobacco, is a species native to the eastern United States.

It is an annual herbaceous plant that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of trumpet-shaped, violet to purple flowers. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by moths, and it is known for its strong, often fragrant scent.

Nicotiana sylvestris is also a close relative of the tobacco plant, Nicotiana tabacum.
