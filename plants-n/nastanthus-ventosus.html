Nastanthus ventosus

Nastanthus ventosus, commonly known as the windflower, is a unique plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery inflorescences that sway with the breeze, giving it its common name. The plant has small, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, adapted to withstand the strong winds of its natural habitat.

Nastanthus ventosus is a perennial herb that thrives in cool, moist environments with well-drained soil.
