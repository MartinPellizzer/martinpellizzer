Nemepis prolifera

Nemepis prolifera, commonly known as the creeping nemepis, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit, with numerous stems that spread along the ground. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Nemepis prolifera is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices.
