Nepeta spicata

Nepeta spicata, commonly known as catmint, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers. The plant produces aromatic leaves that are often used in herbal remedies and as a natural deterrent for pests. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it a valuable addition to gardens.

Nepeta spicata is also known for its medicinal properties, including calming effects and use in treating respiratory issues.
