Nephrodium stipitatum

Nephrodium stipitatum, commonly known as the stipe-attached nephroid, is a unique fern species characterized by its distinctive fronds.

It belongs to the family Nephrodiaceae and is native to specific regions in Southeast Asia. The plant features a short, sturdy rhizome with dense, clustered fronds that are often dark green and leathery. Its reproductive structures, including sporangia, are located on the undersides of the fronds, typical of ferns.

Nephrodium stipitatum is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in botanical gardens.
