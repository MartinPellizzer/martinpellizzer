Nabalus autumnalis

Nabalus autumnalis, commonly known as the autumn bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, violet to purple flowers that bloom in the late summer and autumn. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, silky hair, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Nabalus autumnalis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in cooler climates.
