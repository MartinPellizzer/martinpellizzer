Niphobolus albicans

Niphobolus albicans, commonly known as the white-spored earthstar, is a unique member of the family Pyronemataceae.

It is a saprophytic fungus that thrives on decaying organic matter, particularly in moist, shaded environments. The fruiting body is characterized by a star-shaped structure with a central spore sac surrounded by radiating arms. Its spores are white and released in a powdery form, contributing to its common name.

This fungus plays an important role in nutrient cycling in forest ecosystems.
