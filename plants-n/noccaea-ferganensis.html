Noccaea ferganensis

Noccaea ferganensis, commonly known as the Fergana watercress, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous plants. The species is characterized by its upright growth habit and pinnately divided leaves. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are typically arranged in racemes.

Noccaea ferganensis thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in mountainous regions.
