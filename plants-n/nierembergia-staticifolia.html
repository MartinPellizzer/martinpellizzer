Nierembergia staticifolia

Nierembergia staticifolia, commonly known as the stickyleaf bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant produces a sticky resin on its leaves, which is thought to deter herbivores. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in temperate rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique botanical features.
