Nephelium tomentosum

Nephelium tomentosum, commonly known as the rambutan, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, spiny fruits that resemble a hairy ball. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are pollinated by insects, leading to the development of its distinctive fruit. The leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate with a leathery texture.

The tree thrives in warm, humid climates and is cultivated for its edible, sweet, and aromatic fruits.
