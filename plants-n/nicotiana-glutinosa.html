Nicotiana glutinosa

Nicotiana glutinosa, commonly known as Sticky Nightshade, is a species of flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that exude a sticky substance, which is a key botanical feature. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically violet or purple in color. It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades such as tobacco and tomatoes.

Nicotiana glutinosa is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
