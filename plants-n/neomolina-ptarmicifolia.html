Neomolina ptarmicifolia

Neomolina ptarmicifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its silvery, fuzzy leaves and daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is often used in rock gardens due to its compact size and ornamental appeal.

Neomolina ptarmicifolia is relatively drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping in arid regions.
