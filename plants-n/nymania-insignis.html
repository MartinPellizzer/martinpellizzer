Nymania insignis

Nymania insignis, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to eastern Australia and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in winter. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Nymania insignis is also the floral emblem of Australia, symbolizing the country's natural heritage.
