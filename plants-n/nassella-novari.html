Nassella novari

Nassella novari, commonly known as slender needlegrass, is a grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and tufted inflorescences that resemble small plumes. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It produces small, wind-dispersed seeds that contribute to its ecological role in stabilizing soil.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native plant communities in its range.
