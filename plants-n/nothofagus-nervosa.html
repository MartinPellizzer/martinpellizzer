Nothofagus nervosa

Nothofagus nervosa, commonly known as the slender nothofagus, is a species of evergreen tree native to southern South America.

It belongs to the Nothofagaceae family and is characterized by its lanceolate leaves with distinct venation. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by edible, woody fruits that are dispersed by wind. It thrives in temperate rainforests and is valued for its ecological role in supporting diverse wildlife.

Nothofagus nervosa is also noted for its ability to tolerate shade and its contribution to soil stabilization in forest ecosystems.
