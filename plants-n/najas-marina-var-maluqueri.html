Najas marina var. maluqueri

Najas marina var. maluqueri, commonly known as the Florida watermilfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Najadaceae and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, whorled leaves. The plant has whorls of 4-6 narrow, linear leaves that are typically 2-4 mm in length. It produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are usually found in clusters near the base of the plant.

This species plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
