Nasturtium roridulum

Nasturtium roridulum, commonly known as the weeping nasturtium, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, pendulous flowers that dangle from the stems, creating a weeping appearance. The plant produces small, round leaves and has a distinct, peppery scent reminiscent of watercress. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sun.

Nasturtium roridulum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible flowers.
