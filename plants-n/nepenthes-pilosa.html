Nepenthes pilosa

Nepenthes pilosa, commonly known as the hairy pitcher plant, is a tropical carnivorous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, waxy pitchers that secrete a digestive fluid to trap and digest insects. The plant's leaves are covered in fine, hair-like structures that aid in trapping prey and reducing water loss. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires consistent moisture and nutrient-rich soil.

Its unique adaptations make it a fascinating subject for both botanists and plant enthusiasts.
