Nasturtium laxum

Nasturtium laxum, commonly known as the spreading nasturtium, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, edible flowers that come in shades of orange and yellow. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its stems are often covered in fine, soft hairs. Nasturtium laxum thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for container gardening and ornamental use.

Its flowers are not only visually appealing but also have a peppery taste, making them a versatile addition to salads and garnishes.
