Nardurus maritimus subvar. glabriglumis

Nardurus maritimus subvar. glabriglumis, commonly known as the coastal nardus, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is a perennial grass-like plant characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or pale blue flowers arranged in spikelets, typically blooming during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in coastal dunes and salt marshes.

This subspecies is adapted to withstand saline conditions and is an important component of coastal vegetation.
