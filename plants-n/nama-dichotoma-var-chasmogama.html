Nama dichotoma var. chasmogama

Nama dichotoma var. chasmogama, commonly known as the "Nama dwarf chasmogama," is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features a short, branched stem with dense clusters of small, fleshy leaves that are typically green or bluish-green. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are borne on short, erect stalks. It is adapted to arid environments and requires well-draining soil to thrive.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in dry climates.
