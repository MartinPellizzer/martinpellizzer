Nectandra magnifolia

Nectandra magnifolia, commonly known as the magnificent sandarac tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers that are often clustered in terminal panicles. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains aromatic seeds.

The tree is valued for its fragrant resin, which has been used in traditional medicine and incense making.
