Nephrodium squamosissimum

Nephrodium squamosissimum, commonly known as the scalycushion fern, is a rare and unique fern species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, compact growth habit and its finely divided, scaly fronds that give it a rugged appearance. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in rocky or forested areas. Its reproductive structures, including sporangia, are typically located on the underside of the fronds.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological specificity, Nephrodium squamosissimum is considered a species of conservation concern.
