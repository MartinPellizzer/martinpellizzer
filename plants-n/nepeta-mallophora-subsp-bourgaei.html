Nepeta mallophora subsp. bourgaei

Nepeta mallophora subsp.

bourgaei, commonly known as the Bourgae's catmint, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia. It features slender, upright stems covered in fine, hairy foliage.

The plant produces small, tubular blue or violet flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It is valued for its aromatic leaves and is often used in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.

This subspecies is closely related to other members of the Nepeta genus, sharing similar botanical characteristics.
