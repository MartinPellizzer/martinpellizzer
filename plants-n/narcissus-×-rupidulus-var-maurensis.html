Narcissus × rupidulus var. maurensis

Narcissus × rupidulus var. maurensis, commonly known as the Maurensis daffodil, is a hybrid plant that exhibits characteristics of both narcissus and rupidulus species.

It is a hardy, perennial bulbous plant native to southern Europe, particularly Spain and Portugal. The plant produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange, often with a white or greenish throat. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its foliage consists of strap-like leaves that are narrow and grass-like, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
