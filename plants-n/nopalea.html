Nopalea

Nopalea, commonly known as prickly pear cactus, is a genus of cacti native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces vibrant flowers, often in shades of yellow, orange, or red, which attract pollinators like bees and birds. Its fruit, known as a tuna, is edible and has a sweet, tangy flavor.

Nopalea species are also valued for their medicinal properties and are used in traditional remedies.
