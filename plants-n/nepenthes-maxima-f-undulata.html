Nepenthes maxima f. undulata

Nepenthes maxima f. undulata, commonly known as the wavy-leaf giant pitcher plant, is a unique variant of the giant pitcher plant native to the rainforests of Borneo.

This plant is characterized by its large, wavy-lobed leaves that form deep, water-filled pitcher structures used to trap insects. The pitchers are typically green or reddish in color and produce a sweet nectar to lure prey. It thrives in humid, shaded environments with high rainfall, making it a rare and specialized species.

Its distinctive morphology and ecological role make it a subject of interest for botanists and plant enthusiasts alike.
