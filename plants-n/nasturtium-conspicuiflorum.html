Nasturtium conspicuiflorum

Nasturtium conspicuiflorum, commonly known as the showy nasturtium, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from orange to red, often with a yellow or orange center. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves that are slightly hairy and have a peppery taste when eaten. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Nasturtium conspicuiflorum is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
