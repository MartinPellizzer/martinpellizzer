Nepeta phyllochlamys

Nepeta phyllochlamys, commonly known as the broadleaf catmint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces spikes of blue to purple flowers in the summer. Its botanical name reflects the leaf structure, with "phyllochlamys" referring to its broad, leaf-like bracts.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
