Neoregelia zaslawskyi

Neoregelia zaslawskyi, commonly known as the Zaslawsky's bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to Brazil.

It features a rosette of dark green, waxy leaves that form a central tank for collecting rainwater. The plant produces vibrant orange-red bracts that surround its small, bell-shaped flowers. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires high humidity and well-draining soil.

Neoregelia zaslawskyi is prized for its ornamental value and unique appearance in indoor and outdoor gardens.
