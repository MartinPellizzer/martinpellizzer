Nepenthes sibuyanensis

Nepenthes sibuyanensis, commonly known as the Sibuyan pitcher plant, is a carnivorous plant native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its unique, elongated, and often reddish-colored pitchers that secrete a digestive fluid to trap and digest insects. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is adapted to nutrient-poor soils. Its leaves form the distinctive pitcher structures, which are modified to serve as both a trap and a nutrient source.

Nepenthes sibuyanensis is also notable for its ornamental value and is considered a rare and endangered species.
