Nicotiana tabacum var. gracilipes

Nicotiana tabacum var. gracilipes, commonly known as the slender tobacco plant, is a herbaceous species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically violet or purple in color, arranged in clusters at the ends of the branches. It is a member of the Solanaceae family, which includes other economically important plants like tomatoes and peppers.

This variety is primarily cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and flowering habit.
