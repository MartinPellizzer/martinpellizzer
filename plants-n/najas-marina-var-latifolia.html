Najas marina var. latifolia

Najas marina var. latifolia, commonly known as broadleaf water nymph, is a aquatic plant native to freshwater environments.

It is characterized by its long, ribbon-like leaves that are typically broad and flat. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged or floating. It thrives in still or slow-moving water and is often found in ponds and lakes.

Its presence indicates good water quality and is an important part of aquatic ecosystems.
