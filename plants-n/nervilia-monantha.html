Nervilia monantha

Nervilia monantha, commonly known as the single-flowered orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, upright stems and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Nervilia monantha is valued for its unique floral structure and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its aesthetic and ecological significance.
