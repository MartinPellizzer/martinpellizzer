Nasturtium chalepense

Nasturtium chalepense, commonly known as the Turkish rocket, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its bright orange or red flowers and heart-shaped leaves. The plant is an annual or biennial herb that grows up to 1 meter in height. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, while its edible leaves and seeds are used in culinary applications.

Nasturtium chalepense is also known for its peppery flavor and is often cultivated in gardens for both ornamental and edible purposes.
