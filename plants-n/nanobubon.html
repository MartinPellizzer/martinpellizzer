Nanobubon

Nanobubon, commonly known as the fairy bellflower, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It features delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, often adorned with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in well-drained environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, seed-like fruits that are dispersed by wind.

Nanobubon is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
