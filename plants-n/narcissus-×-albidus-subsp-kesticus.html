Narcissus × albidus subsp. kesticus

Narcissus × albidus subsp.

kesticus, commonly known as the Kestric daffodil, is a hybrid species within the Amaryllidaceae family. It is characterized by its white, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant typically grows to a height of 20–30 cm, with narrow, grass-like leaves. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.
