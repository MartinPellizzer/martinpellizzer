Nepeta glomerulosa

Nepeta glomerulosa, commonly known as catmint, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, clustered blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are often used in herbal remedies due to their calming properties.

Nepeta glomerulosa is also popular in gardens for its attractive appearance and ability to attract pollinators.
