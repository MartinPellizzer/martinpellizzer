Nidorella foetida

Nidorella foetida, commonly known as the stinkweed, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, greenish-yellow flowers and pungent, foul odor, which is a defense mechanism against herbivores. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth. Its leaves are simple and alternate, with a rough texture.

Nidorella foetida is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
