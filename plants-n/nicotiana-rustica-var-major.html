Nicotiana rustica var. major

Nicotiana rustica var. major, commonly known as the wild tobacco plant, is a species native to the Americas.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its hairy stems and large, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or pink in color. It contains high levels of nicotine, making it significant in traditional medicine and ritual use.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and ethnobotanical studies.
