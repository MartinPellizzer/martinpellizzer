Nicotiana × flindersiensis

Nicotiana × flindersiensis, commonly known as the Flinders Island tobacco plant, is a hybrid species native to Australia.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its hairy, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a yellow throat. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and genetic significance, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and studied for its potential in plant breeding programs.
