Nymphaea heudelotii

Nymphaea heudelotii, commonly known as the African water lily, is a species of aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats, such as ponds and lakes. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine, and it plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various organisms.

The flowers are typically nocturnal, opening at night and closing in the morning.
