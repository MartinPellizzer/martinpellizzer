Nicandra physalodes var. immaculata

Nicandra physalodes var. immaculata, commonly known as the sweet scent vine, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers that emit a sweet fragrance. The plant's leaves are heart-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, while its fruits are pear-shaped and contain a cluster of seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and pleasant aroma.

The species is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
