Nauclea ategii

Nauclea ategii, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically alternate and pinnate, with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces striking red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Nauclea ategii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
