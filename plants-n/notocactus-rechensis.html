Notocactus rechensis

Notocactus rechensis, commonly known as the Rech's notocactus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of northern South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stem and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom during the dry season. The plant has a thick, fleshy stem that stores water, allowing it to survive in harsh, drought-prone environments. Its leaves are modified into spines, which help reduce water loss and deter herbivores.

Notocactus rechensis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
