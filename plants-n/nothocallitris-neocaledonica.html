Nothocallitris neocaledonica

Nothocallitris neocaledonica, commonly known as the New Caledonian bottlebrush, is a shrub native to the island of New Caledonia.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical inflorescences that resemble bottle brushes, typically bearing bright red or orange flowers. The plant has small, needle-like leaves that are arranged in spirals along the stems. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in coastal or lowland areas.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
