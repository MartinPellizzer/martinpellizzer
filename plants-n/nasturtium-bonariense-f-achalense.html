Nasturtium bonariense f. achalense

Nasturtium bonariense f. achalense, commonly known as the Achalense Nasturtium, is a botanical variant of the common nasturtium.

It is characterized by its vibrant, edible flowers and heart-shaped leaves. This cultivar is native to South America and is often grown for its ornamental value in gardens. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it adaptable to various climates.

Its unique foliage and showy blooms make it a popular choice for both decorative and culinary purposes.
