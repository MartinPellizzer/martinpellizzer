Notocactus concinnus

Notocactus concinnus, commonly known as the "Cactus of the Sun," is a species native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stems that can grow up to 3 meters in height. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by bright red fruits. Its thick, fleshy stems are adapted to store water, allowing it to survive in drought conditions.

Notocactus concinnus is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its hardy nature and striking appearance.
