Nymphaea baumii

Nymphaea baumii, commonly known as the white lotus, is a water-lily species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that open in the early morning and close by midday. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes. Its leaves are broad and floating, with a waxy surface that helps repel water.

Nymphaea baumii is also valued for its ornamental beauty and has cultural significance in various regions.
