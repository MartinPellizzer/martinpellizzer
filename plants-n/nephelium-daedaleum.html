Nephelium daedaleum

Nephelium daedaleum, commonly known as the mountain longan, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant white flowers and edible, sweet, and juicy berries. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its leaves are elliptical and glossy, while its bark is smooth and light grey.

The fruit, which resembles a small, round peach, is often used in both culinary and medicinal applications.
