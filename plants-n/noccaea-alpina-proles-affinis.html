Noccaea alpina proles affinis

Noccaea alpina proles affinis, commonly known as the alpine watercress, is a flowering plant native to high-altitude regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of pinnately lobed leaves and small, white or yellowish flowers. The plant thrives in moist, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows and mountainous areas. It is a perennial species that produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as a leafy green vegetable in cooler climates.
