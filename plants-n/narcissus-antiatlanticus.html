Narcissus antiatlanticus

Narcissus antiatlanticus, commonly known as the Canary Islands daffodil, is a species native to the Canary Islands.

It is a bulbous perennial plant that typically grows in rocky or coastal areas. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and white, often with a distinct orange or red spot at the base of the petal. Its foliage consists of long, strap-like leaves that emerge from the base of the plant.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
