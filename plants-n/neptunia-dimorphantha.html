Neptunia dimorphantha

Neptunia dimorphantha, commonly known as the water bladderwort, is a unique aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its specialized bladder-like structures that trap small aquatic organisms, aiding in its nutrient acquisition. The plant typically grows in shallow, freshwater habitats such as rice paddies and wetlands. Its leaves are often submerged, while its stems may emerge partially from the water surface.

Neptunia dimorphantha plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by contributing to nutrient cycling and providing habitat for microorganisms.
