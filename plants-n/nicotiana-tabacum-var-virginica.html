Nicotiana tabacum var. virginica

Nicotiana tabacum var. virginica, commonly known as wild tobacco, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as woodlands and wetlands. It produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is also notable for its role in ecological systems and its historical use in traditional medicine.
