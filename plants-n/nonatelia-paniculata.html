Nonatelia paniculata

Nonatelia paniculata, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are opposite and pinnately arranged. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in panicles, which are often clustered at the ends of the branches. Its fruit is a small, red drupe that contains a single seed.

Nonatelia paniculata is adapted to a variety of soil types and can thrive in both wet and dry conditions.
