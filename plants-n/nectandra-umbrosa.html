Nectandra umbrosa

Nectandra umbrosa, commonly known as the dark wood or umbrosa lauraceae, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, cinnamon, and avocado. The plant is characterized by its dark, aromatic bark and glossy, elliptical leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. It produces small, fragrant flowers followed by dark, glossy berries.

Nectandra umbrosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitats.
