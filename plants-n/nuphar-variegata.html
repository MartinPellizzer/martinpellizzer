Nuphar variegata

Nuphar variegata, commonly known as the variegated water lily, is a aquatic plant native to parts of Asia and North America.

It features broad, floating leaves that are often variegated with white, yellow, and green patterns. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white, which bloom above the water surface. Its roots are anchored in the sediment at the bottom of ponds and lakes.

Nuphar variegata plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
