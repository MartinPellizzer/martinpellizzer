Nepeta pallida

Nepeta pallida, commonly known as white catmint, is a flowering plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant produces small, pale blue or white flowers arranged in whorls along the stems. Its leaves are typically gray-green and have a mint-like fragrance.

Nepeta pallida is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive appearance and ability to attract pollinators.
