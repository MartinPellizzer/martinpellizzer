Nephelium melanomiscum

Nephelium melanomiscum, commonly known as the black mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The fruit is distinctive for its thick, purple rind and segmented, white, juicy flesh. The plant thrives in humid climates with well-drained soil and requires a warm, tropical environment.

It is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its edible fruit, which is rich in antioxidants.
