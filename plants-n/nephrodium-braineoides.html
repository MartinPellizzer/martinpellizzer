Nephrodium braineoides

Nephrodium braineoides, commonly known as the brain-like fern, is a unique species of fern belonging to the family Nephrodiaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, brain-like appearance of the fronds, which are composed of a central axis with numerous small, finger-like lobes. The plant is native to tropical regions of Africa and is often found in moist, shaded environments. Its reproductive structures, including sporangia, are typically located on the underside of the fronds.

Nephrodium braineoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unusual foliage.
