Nephelium aculeatum

Nephelium aculeatum, commonly known as the rambutan, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, spiny fruits that resemble a hairy ball, giving it its common name. The plant belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is valued for its edible, sweet, and aromatic fruits. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a smooth margin.

The tree thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental and culinary uses.
