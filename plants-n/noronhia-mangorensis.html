Noronhia mangorensis

Noronhia mangorensis, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry tree, is a small evergreen tree native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It features smooth, dark brown bark and elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green on top. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers in clusters, followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for humans and animals. Noronhia mangorensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
