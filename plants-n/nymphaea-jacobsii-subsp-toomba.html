Nymphaea jacobsii subsp. toomba

Nymphaea jacobsii subsp.

toomba, commonly known as the Toomba water lily, is a species native to the tropical regions of Australia. It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink.

The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats, such as lakes and ponds. Its leaves are typically rounded with a waxy surface, and the flowers open during the day, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in aquatic ecosystems.
