Nepeta teydea

Nepeta teydea, commonly known as Persian catmint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or violet flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage has a mint-like scent, making it popular in gardens for its ornamental value and fragrance.

Nepeta teydea is often used in traditional medicine for its purported calming and antispasmodic properties.
