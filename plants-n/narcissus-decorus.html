Narcissus decorus

Narcissus decorus, commonly known as the showy daffodil, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and western Asia, where it thrives in woodland and meadow habitats. The plant produces striking yellow flowers with a distinctive crown-like shape, typically blooming in early spring. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming a dense rosette at the base.

Narcissus decorus is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, long-lasting blooms.
