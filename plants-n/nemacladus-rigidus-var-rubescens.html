Nemacladus rigidus var. rubescens

Nemacladus rigidus var. rubescens, commonly known as the red-stemmed spurge, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive red stems that become more pronounced with age. The leaves are typically alternate, simple, and have a rough texture. It produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

This species is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in full sun to partial shade conditions.
