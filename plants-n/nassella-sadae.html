Nassella sadae

Nassella sadae, commonly known as the Sadae needlegrass, is a flowering plant species native to specific regions of South America.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and fine, needle-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers, typical of many grasses. It thrives in dry, open habitats and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
