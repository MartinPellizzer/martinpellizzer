Nerine bowdenii subsp. bowdenii

Nerine bowdenii subsp.

bowdenii, commonly known as the African lily, is a species native to South Africa. It is a bulbous perennial plant characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow.

The plant produces strap-like, grassy leaves that are often mistaken for grass due to their appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The bulbs are relatively hardy and can be propagated through division or by seed.
