Nicoraepoa subenervis subsp.

spegazziniana, commonly known as the Spegazzinian subshrub, is a perennial plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America. It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hairs.

The flowers are typically white or pale blue, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the stems. This species thrives in cool, moist environments and is adapted to rocky or alpine soils.
