Neolepisorus truncatus f. laciniatus

Neolepisorus truncatus f. laciniatus, commonly known as the fragmented frond fern, is a fern species characterized by its distinctive, finely divided fronds.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is native to tropical and subtropical regions. The fronds are typically pinnately compound, with a truncate or notched base and finely serrated margins. This variety is noted for its delicate, lace-like appearance, which makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and requires consistent humidity and well-draining soil.
