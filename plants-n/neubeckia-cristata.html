Neubeckia cristata

Neubeckia cristata, commonly known as the spiked speedwell, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and clusters of small, bright blue or violet flowers. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It typically blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Neubeckia cristata prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it a versatile addition to gardens and natural habitats.
