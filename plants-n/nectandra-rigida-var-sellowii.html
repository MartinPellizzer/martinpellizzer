Nectandra rigida var. sellowii

Nectandra rigida var. sellowii, commonly known as the Sellow's nectandra, is a tropical tree native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark-colored wood. The plant has simple, alternate, elliptic leaves with a glossy surface and a fragrant aroma. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in inflorescences, while the fruit is a small, woody drupe.

This species is valued for its timber and has traditional uses in local communities.
