Nasturtium obtusum

Nasturtium obtusum, commonly known as the broadleaf nasturtium, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Tropaeolaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant is known for its edible leaves and seeds, which have a peppery flavor similar to watercress. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking both ornamental and edible plants.

Nasturtium obtusum is also used in companion planting to deter pests in vegetable gardens.
