Necepsia castaneifolia subsp. capuronii

Necepsia castaneifolia subsp.

capuronii, commonly known as the African chestnut tree, is a species native to parts of Africa. It is a deciduous tree characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves that resemble those of the chestnut tree.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, chestnut-like fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
