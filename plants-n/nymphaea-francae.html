Nymphaea francae

Nymphaea francae, commonly known as the French water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Nymphaeaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy, floating flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes, where it forms dense mats on the water surface. Its broad, heart-shaped leaves provide a habitat for various aquatic organisms, while its flowers attract pollinators like bees and beetles.

Nymphaea francae is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its ecological role in aquatic ecosystems.
