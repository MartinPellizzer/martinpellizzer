Najas falciculata

Najas falciculata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf watermilfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It has narrow, ribbon-like leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged, followed by tiny, floating fruits. It plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Due to its rapid growth, it can also become invasive in certain water bodies.
