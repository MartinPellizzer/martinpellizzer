Narcissus × aurantius

Narcissus × aurantius, commonly known as the tangerine daffodil, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Narcissus tazetta and Narcissus jonquilla.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange petals and a prominent, trumpet-shaped corona. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and produces clusters of flowers in early spring. Its foliage consists of strap-like leaves that are dark green and narrow.

This hybrid is prized for its ornamental value and is commonly cultivated in gardens for its bright, showy blooms.
