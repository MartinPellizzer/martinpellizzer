Najas browniana

Najas browniana, commonly known as the brown watermilfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It belongs to the family Najadaceae and is characterized by its narrow, ribbon-like leaves that are often arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically borne in clusters near the water's surface. Najas browniana is adapted to slow-moving or still waters and can form dense underwater stands.

Its presence is often indicative of good water quality and stable aquatic ecosystems.
