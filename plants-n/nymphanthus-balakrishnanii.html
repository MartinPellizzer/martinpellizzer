Nymphanthus balakrishnanii

Nymphanthus balakrishnanii, commonly known as the Balakrishnan's weeping fig, is a rare and ornamental plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, cascading leaves that resemble those of a weeping willow, giving it a distinctive and elegant appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically white or pale yellow, which bloom in the spring. It thrives in humid environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its rarity and unique beauty, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
