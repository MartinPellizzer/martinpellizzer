Nassella longicoronata

Nassella longicoronata, commonly known as the long-haired needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and long, silky bracts that give it its distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its inflorescence consists of a dense spikelet cluster that is often adorned with long, feathery bristles.

Nassella longicoronata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
