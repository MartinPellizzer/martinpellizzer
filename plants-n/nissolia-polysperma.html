Nissolia polysperma

Nissolia polysperma, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves and showy, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows as a perennial herb, often with a short, fibrous root system. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped and covered in a fine layer of white wax, which helps reduce water loss.

Nissolia polysperma is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
