Nymphaea ampla var. salzmannii

Nymphaea ampla var. salzmannii, commonly known as the Salzmann's water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features large, heart-shaped leaves that float on the water's surface, often with a waxy texture. The flowers are showy, typically white or pale pink, with a strong, sweet fragrance. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats, preferring sunny conditions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological role in supporting aquatic life.
