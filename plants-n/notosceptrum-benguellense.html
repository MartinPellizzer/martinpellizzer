Notosceptrum benguellense

Notosceptrum benguellense, commonly known as the Benguella thornveld grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets, typical of the grass family. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils.

Notosceptrum benguellense plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife in its native habitat.
