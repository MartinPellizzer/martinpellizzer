Nepeta dschuparensis

Nepeta dschuparensis, commonly known as the Dschupar mountain catmint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant produces small, tubular blue or violet flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It typically grows in rocky or dry habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.

Nepeta dschuparensis is also noted for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and its resilience to drought.
