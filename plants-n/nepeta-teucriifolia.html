Nepeta teucriifolia

Nepeta teucriifolia, commonly known as catmint, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green leaves and clusters of small blue or purple flowers. The plant belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae), which is known for its square stems and fragrant foliage. Nepeta teucriifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
