Nipponocalamus shibuyanus

Nipponocalamus shibuyanus, commonly known as the Shibuya bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect culms that can reach heights of up to 3 meters. The plant features narrow, linear leaves with a smooth texture and a bluish-green hue. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Nipponocalamus shibuyanus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
