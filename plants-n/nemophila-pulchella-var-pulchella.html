Nemophila pulchella var. pulchella

Nemophila pulchella var. pulchella, commonly known as the California jewelflower, is a low-growing annual herb native to the western United States.

It features delicate, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to deep purple, often with a bluish tinge. The plant has slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
