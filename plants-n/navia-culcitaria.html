Navia culcitaria

Navia culcitaria, commonly known as the common hawksbeard, is a flowering plant native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant features a hairy, upright stem with alternating leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. Its yellow flowers are arranged in a single head and bloom from late spring to early autumn.

Navia culcitaria is often found in meadows, roadsides, and disturbed soils, and it is considered a weed in some agricultural areas.
