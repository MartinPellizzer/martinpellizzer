Najas major var. gracilis

Najas major var. gracilis, commonly known as slender watermilfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It belongs to the family Najadaceae and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, whorled leaves. The plant produces tiny, yellowish flowers that are typically found in clusters near the water's surface. It thrives in slow-moving to still waters with moderate to high light exposure.

Najas major var. gracilis plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
