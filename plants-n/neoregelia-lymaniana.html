Neoregelia lymaniana

Neoregelia lymaniana, commonly known as the Lyman's bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup to collect rainwater. The plant produces vibrant, long-lasting inflorescences that range from red to orange, adding dramatic visual interest. Its foliage is often variegated with silver or white markings, enhancing its ornamental appeal.

Neoregelia lymaniana thrives in bright, indirect light and requires high humidity to flourish.
