Narcissus × tereticaulis

Narcissus × tereticaulis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved daffodil, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Narcissus tazetta and Narcissus poeticus.

It is characterized by its narrow, strap-like leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped yellow flowers. The plant is native to the Iberian Peninsula and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.

Its blooming period typically occurs in early spring, adding a vibrant splash of color to the landscape.
