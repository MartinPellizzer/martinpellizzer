Nicotiana quadrivalvis var. multivalvis

Nicotiana quadrivalvis var. multivalvis, commonly known as the four-valved tobacco, is a species native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that are typically violet or blue in color. The plant produces four-valved capsules that contain the seeds, a feature that gives it its scientific name. It is a member of the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
