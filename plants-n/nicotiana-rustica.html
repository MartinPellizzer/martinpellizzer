Nicotiana rustica

Nicotiana rustica, commonly known as wild tobacco, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its erect stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. It is known for containing high levels of nicotine, which makes it both toxic and valuable in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it a significant species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
