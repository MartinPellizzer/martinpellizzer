Navarretia nuttallii

Navarretia nuttallii, commonly known as the Sierra snowplant, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pink flowers during the spring. The plant is adapted to grow in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine regions. Navarretia nuttallii is an herbaceous perennial that relies on seasonal snowmelt for its water needs.
