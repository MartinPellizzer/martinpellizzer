Nageia thunbergii

Nageia thunbergii, commonly known as the Japanese red pine, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Japan and parts of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and needle-like leaves that are dark green and slightly twisted. The tree produces cones that are typically reddish-brown in color, and it is known for its slow growth rate and longevity.

Nageia thunbergii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
