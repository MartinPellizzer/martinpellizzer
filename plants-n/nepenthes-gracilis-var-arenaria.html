Nepenthes gracilis var. arenaria

Nepenthes gracilis var. arenaria, commonly known as the sand nepenthes, is a carnivorous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and elongated, tubular pitchers that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant thrives in nutrient-poor, sandy soils and requires high humidity and bright, indirect light. Its pitchers secrete a digestive fluid to trap and digest insects, providing essential nutrients.

This variety is valued for its unique appearance and is a popular species among carnivorous plant enthusiasts.
