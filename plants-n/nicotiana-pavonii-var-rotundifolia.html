Nicotiana pavonii var. rotundifolia

Nicotiana pavonii var. rotundifolia, commonly known as the purple velvet tobacco, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers and heart-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its flowers bloom in clusters and are often visited by butterflies and other pollinators.

The leaves are often used in traditional medicine, though the plant is also cultivated for its ornamental value.
