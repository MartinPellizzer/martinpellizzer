Nectandra sanguinea

Nectandra sanguinea, commonly known as bloodwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, reddish-brown heartwood that is highly valued for its durability and aesthetic appeal. The plant belongs to the family Lauraceae and is known for its aromatic leaves and flowers. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The tree's botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and commercial contexts.
