Nierembergia anomala var. uniflora

Nierembergia anomala var. uniflora, commonly known as the single-flower bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, often with a distinctive white or yellow throat. The plant has slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
