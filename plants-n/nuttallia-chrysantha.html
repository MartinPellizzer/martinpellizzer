Nuttallia chrysantha

Nuttallia chrysantha, commonly known as the golden sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a small annual herb with narrow, linear leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. Its flowers are typically visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological role in its native habitat.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in harsh conditions.
