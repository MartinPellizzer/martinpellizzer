Nycterinia ovata

Nycterinia ovata, commonly known as the moonflower, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, white, night-blooming flowers that emit a strong fragrance. The plant belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is known for its rapid growth and short blooming period. Its leaves are ovate and velvety, while its stems are hairy and upright.

Nycterinia ovata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its unique nocturnal blooming habit.
