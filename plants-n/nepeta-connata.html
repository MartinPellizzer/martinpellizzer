Nepeta connata

Nepeta connata, commonly known as catmint, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features gray-green, fuzzy leaves and produces small, tubular blue or violet flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Nepeta connata is also known for its mild mint-like scent and is often used in herbal remedies.
