Neustanthus phaseoloides var. phaseoloides

Neustanthus phaseoloides var. phaseoloides, commonly known as the Brazilian morning glory, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink to purple. The plant is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant blooms and vine-like structure.

However, it can become invasive in certain environments if not managed properly.
