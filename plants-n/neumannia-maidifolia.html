Neumannia maidifolia

Neumannia maidifolia, commonly known as the lemonleaf or lemonwood, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, leathery leaves that emit a citrus-like scent when crushed. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by egg-shaped, red berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Neumannia maidifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
