Nepeta batalica

Nepeta batalica, commonly known as catnip, is a flowering plant in the Lamiaceae family.

It is native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in clusters. It is known for its strong aromatic properties, which are due to the presence of nepetalactone.

Nepeta batalica is often cultivated for its medicinal and ornamental uses.
