Niedenzuella metensis

Niedenzuella metensis, commonly known as the Metens mountain pine, is a rare and endemic species found in the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is a coniferous tree characterized by its needle-like leaves and distinctive cone structures. The plant thrives in cold, moist environments, typically at elevations above 3,000 meters. Its growth is slow, and it plays a vital role in stabilizing the mountainous terrain.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered vulnerable to environmental changes and human encroachment.
