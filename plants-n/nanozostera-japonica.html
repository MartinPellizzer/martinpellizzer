Nanozostera japonica

Nanozostera japonica, commonly known as the Japanese dwarf seagrass, is a marine flowering plant belonging to the Zosteraceae family.

It is characterized by its small, ribbon-like leaves that grow in dense underwater meadows. This species is typically found in shallow coastal waters, where it forms important habitats for various marine organisms. Nanozostera japonica plays a crucial role in stabilizing sediments and improving water quality through its extensive root system.

Its presence is often an indicator of healthy, undisturbed marine ecosystems.
