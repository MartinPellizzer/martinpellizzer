Notodanthonia purpurascens

Notodanthonia purpurascens, commonly known as the purple muhly, is a grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and distinctive purple-tinged foliage. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Notodanthonia purpurascens is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soils.
