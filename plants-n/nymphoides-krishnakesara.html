Nymphoides krishnakesara

Nymphoides krishnakesara, commonly known as the Krishna's water lily, is a species of aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves and showy, pale yellow to white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes. Its roots are rhizomatous, allowing it to spread vegetatively and form dense colonies.

Nymphoides krishnakesara plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
