Nahusia coccinea

Nahusia coccinea, commonly known as the red-flowered orchid, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant red petals and sepals, which contrast with its green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid forest environments. Its flowers bloom in clusters, often emitting a subtle fragrance that attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value, Nahusia coccinea is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts for its unique beauty.
