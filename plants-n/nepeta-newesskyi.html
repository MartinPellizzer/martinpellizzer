Nepeta newesskyi

Nepeta newesskyi, commonly known as the Siberian catmint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their mild sedative properties.

Nepeta newesskyi thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens and alpine settings.
