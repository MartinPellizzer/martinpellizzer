Nymphaea amazonum var. goudotiana

Nymphaea amazonum var. goudotiana, commonly known as the Amazon water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to the Amazon Basin.

It is characterized by its large, showy, floating leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter. The plant produces fragrant, white to pink flowers that bloom during the day and close at night. Its roots are anchored in the muddy substrates of slow-moving or still waters.

This variety is notable for its ecological role in providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
