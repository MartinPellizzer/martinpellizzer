Nabalus ochroleucus

Nabalus ochroleucus, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and valuable species in alpine and subalpine ecosystems.
