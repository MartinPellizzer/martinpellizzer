Negretia elliptica

Negretia elliptica, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in spikes. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in coastal and disturbed soils, making it an invasive species in some regions. Its fruits are small, red berries that contain seeds, and it is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its attractive foliage.

Negretia elliptica is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
