Norantea sodiroi

Norantea sodiroi, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Norantea sodiroi is often found in shaded forest understories, where it contributes to the biodiversity of the ecosystem.

Its ornamental value has made it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a vibrant, low-maintenance plant.
