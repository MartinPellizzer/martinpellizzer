Narcissus × laetus

Narcissus × laetus, commonly known as the golden daffodil, is a hybrid species within the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with strap-like, grassy leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

This variety is prized for its hardiness and ability to naturalize over time.
