Neea marahuacae

Neea marahuacae, commonly known as the Mexican mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant belonging to the family Lauraceae.

It grows on the branches of various host trees, primarily in the understory of tropical and subtropical forests. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that are usually arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. Its flowers are white or pale green, and it produces small, fleshy berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

Neea marahuacae plays a significant ecological role in its native habitat by contributing to nutrient cycling and providing sustenance for local fauna.
