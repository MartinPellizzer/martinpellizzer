Neoschroetera glutinosa

Neoschroetera glutinosa, commonly known as the sticky neoschroetera, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Araceae family and is characterized by its broad, waxy leaves that emit a sticky substance. The plant produces striking, colorful inflorescences that consist of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Due to its unique appearance and slow growth rate, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
