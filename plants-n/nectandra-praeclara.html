Nectandra praeclara

Nectandra praeclara, commonly known as the fragrant sandarac tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other aromatic trees like cinnamon and avocado. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains aromatic seeds.

Nectandra praeclara is valued for its essential oils, which are used in perfumery and traditional medicine.
