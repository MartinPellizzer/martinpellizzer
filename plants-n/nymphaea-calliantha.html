Nymphaea calliantha

Nymphaea calliantha, commonly known as the Mexican water lily, is a species of aquatic plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom above the water surface. The plant has broad, floating leaves that provide habitat for various aquatic organisms. Its roots anchor it in the mud at the bottom of still or slow-moving water bodies.

Nymphaea calliantha plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by supporting biodiversity and improving water quality.
