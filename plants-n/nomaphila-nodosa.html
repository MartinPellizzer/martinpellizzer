Nomaphila nodosa

Nomaphila nodosa, commonly known as the nodose orchid, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, nodular growth pattern. The plant typically produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, often arranged in clusters. Its roots are adapted to absorb nutrients from the poor soils of its habitat.

Nomaphila nodosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
