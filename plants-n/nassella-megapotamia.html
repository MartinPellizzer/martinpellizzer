Nassella megapotamia

Nassella megapotamia, commonly known as the Andean needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous slender, awned lemmas, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and high-altitude regions.

Nassella megapotamia is adapted to withstand drought and temperature fluctuations, making it a resilient plant in its native habitat.
