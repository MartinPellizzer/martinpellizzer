Nepeta flavida

Nepeta flavida, commonly known as yellow catmint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite leaves that are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Nepeta flavida is also valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in herbal remedies.
