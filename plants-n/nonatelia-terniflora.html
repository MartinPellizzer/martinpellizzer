Nonatelia terniflora

Nonatelia terniflora, commonly known as the three-leaved daisy, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are trifoliate, meaning they are divided into three leaflets, giving it its common name.

Nonatelia terniflora is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
