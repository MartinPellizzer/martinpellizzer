Nemophila parviflora subvar. macrophylla

Nemophila parviflora subvar. macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaved blue-eyed Mary, is a flowering plant native to California.

It is characterized by its small, blue to purple flowers and larger, heart-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It belongs to the Boraginaceae family and is known for its delicate appearance and early spring blooming period.

The subspecies macrophylla is distinguished by its more substantial foliage compared to the typical var. parviflora.
