Naumburgia thyrsiflora

Naumburgia thyrsiflora, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and has a compact, bushy habit with hairy stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are arranged in dense, terminal clusters called thyrses, which give the plant its scientific name.

Naumburgia thyrsiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
