Nothoscordum subbiflorum

Nothoscordum subbiflorum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved garlic flower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its strap-like, narrow leaves and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is occasionally cultivated by gardeners interested in native or drought-tolerant plants.
