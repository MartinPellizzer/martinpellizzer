Nasa lenta

Nasa lenta, commonly known as the slow-growing nightshade, is a perennial plant native to the Andes mountains.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant is characterized by its small, bell-shaped flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a slightly hairy texture.

Nasa lenta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded garden settings.
