Narcissus × tavaresii

Narcissus × tavaresii, commonly known as the Tavares daffodil, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Narcissus poeticus and Narcissus tazetta.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or white in color. The plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure. It is a perennial bulb plant that produces foliage and blooms in early spring.

Due to its ornamental value, it is widely cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
