Neogyna gardneriana var. basitrilamellata

Neogyna gardneriana var. basitrilamellata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved neogyna, is a member of the Rutaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves with a distinctive venation pattern. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in shaded, moist environments. Its flowers are small and clustered, producing a fragrant aroma that attracts pollinators.

This variant is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
