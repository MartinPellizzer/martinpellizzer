Nomocharis basilissa

Nomocharis basilissa, commonly known as the crown of thorns, is a striking member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that range from red to orange. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and birds. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
