Nothofagus baumanniae

Nothofagus baumanniae, commonly known as the Baumann's southern beech, is a species of evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Nothofagus genus, which is part of the Nothofagaceae family, and is characterized by its leathery, elliptical leaves. The tree produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by woody, urn-shaped fruits containing edible seeds. It typically grows in temperate rainforests and is adapted to moist, cool climates.

Nothofagus baumanniae plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
