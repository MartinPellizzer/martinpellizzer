Nepeta transiliensis

Nepeta transiliensis, commonly known as the Transylvanian catmint, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant blue or purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage is often used in aromatherapy due to its calming properties.

Nepeta transiliensis is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and medicinal potential.
