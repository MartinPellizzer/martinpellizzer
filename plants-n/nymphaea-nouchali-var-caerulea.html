Nymphaea nouchali var. caerulea

Nymphaea nouchali var. caerulea, commonly known as the blue lotus, is a striking aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy blue or violet flowers that bloom above the water surface. The plant has broad, heart-shaped leaves that float on the water's surface, aiding in photosynthesis. Its roots are anchored in the mud at the bottom of ponds or lakes.

The blue lotus is valued for its ornamental beauty and has cultural significance in various traditional practices.
