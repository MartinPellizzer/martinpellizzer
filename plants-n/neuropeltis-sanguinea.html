Neuropeltis sanguinea

Neuropeltis sanguinea, commonly known as the red-stemmed orchid, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking red stems and delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are elongated and often have a waxy texture, while the flowers are usually borne in clusters.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
