Nemacladus rigidus var. capillaris

Nemacladus rigidus var. capillaris, commonly known as the silky broomweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, erect stems that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are alternate, linear, and often have a soft, velvety texture. It produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers arranged in spikes, which are typically borne at the base of the plant.

This species is adapted to grow in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories or along stream banks.
