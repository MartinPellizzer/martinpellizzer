Nepeta pseudofloccosa

Nepeta pseudofloccosa, commonly known as the woolly catmint, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features slender, upright stems covered in dense, soft, woolly hairs, giving it a distinctive fluffy appearance. The plant produces small, pale blue to violet flowers in whorled clusters, typically blooming in early summer. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and covered with a similar fuzzy texture.

Nepeta pseudofloccosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
