Nama rupicola var. rotundifolia

Nama rupicola var. rotundifolia, commonly known as the round-leafed nama, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are typically rounded or oval in shape. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are often clustered at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for arid environments.

Its ability to store water in its leaves allows it to survive in harsh, dry conditions.
