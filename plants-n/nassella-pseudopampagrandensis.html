Nassella pseudopampagrandensis

Nassella pseudopampagrandensis, commonly known as the Pampagrande needlegrass, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in grasslands and rocky soils.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
