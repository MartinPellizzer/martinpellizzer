Narcissus cuatrecasasii

Narcissus cuatrecasasii, commonly known as the Cuatrecasas narcissus, is a rare and endemic species native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its striking white flowers with a yellow corona, typical of the daffodil family. The plant thrives in rocky and calcareous soils, often found in mountainous regions. It blooms in early spring, adding a delicate touch of color to its natural habitat.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a species of conservation concern.
