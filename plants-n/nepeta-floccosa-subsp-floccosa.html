Nepeta floccosa subsp. floccosa

Nepeta floccosa subsp.

floccosa, commonly known as catnip, is a flowering plant in the mint family, Lamiaceae. It is native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa, and is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite leaves.

The plant produces small, clustered blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer. It is well-known for its aromatic properties, particularly the presence of nepetalactone, which is responsible for its effect on cats.

This subspecies is often cultivated as a decorative plant and for its historical use in traditional medicine.
