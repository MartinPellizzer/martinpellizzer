Nepeta daenensis

Nepeta daenensis, commonly known as Persian catnip, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Iran.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant features square, hairy stems and opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, clustered flowers are typically blue to purple in color, attracting various pollinators.

Nepeta daenensis is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
