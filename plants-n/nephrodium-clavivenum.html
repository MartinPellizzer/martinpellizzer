Nephrodium clavivenum

Nephrodium clavivenum, commonly known as the club-veined nephroid, is a unique fern species characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology.

Its fronds are typically bipinnate, with a complex arrangement of pinnae and pinnules that contribute to its ornamental appeal. The plant exhibits a robust rhizome system, which helps it establish in shaded, moist environments. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are positioned on the underside of the fronds, a common feature among ferns.

Nephrodium clavivenum is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in humid, nutrient-rich soils.
