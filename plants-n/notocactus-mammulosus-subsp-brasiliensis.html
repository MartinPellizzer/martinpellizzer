Notocactus mammulosus subsp. brasiliensis

Notocactus mammulosus subsp.

brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian spineless cactus, is a member of the Cactaceae family native to Brazil. It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are covered in dense, white, woolly hairs, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance.

The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom during the summer months, adding a striking contrast to its textured foliage. It is a low-maintenance plant that thrives in well-draining soil and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

This subspecies is notable for its lack of spines, which distinguishes it from other members of the Notocactus genus.
