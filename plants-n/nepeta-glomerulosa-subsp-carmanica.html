Nepeta glomerulosa subsp. carmanica

Nepeta glomerulosa subsp.

carmanica, commonly known as Carman's catmint, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of Turkey. It features aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and produces dense clusters of pale blue to violet flowers.

The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often used in rock gardens or as a border plant. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade. Nepeta glomerulosa subsp.

carmanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
