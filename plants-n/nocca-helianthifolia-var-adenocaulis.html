Nocca helianthifolia var. adenocaulis

Nocca helianthifolia var. adenocaulis, commonly known as the silverleaf sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which helps it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its stems are often succulent, allowing it to store water efficiently.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in desert scrub and rocky habitats.
