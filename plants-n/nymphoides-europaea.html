Nymphoides europaea

Nymphoides europaea, commonly known as the European water violet, is a perennial aquatic plant native to Europe.

It typically grows in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats such as ponds and marshes. The plant features heart-shaped leaves that are often submerged or floating, with some leaves emerging above the water surface. Its flowers are small, violet-blue, and have five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

Nymphoides europaea plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and amphibians.
