Narcissus linnaeanus var. minor

Narcissus linnaeanus var. minor, commonly known as the dwarf daffodil, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and early spring blooming. The plant produces narrow, grass-like leaves and bright yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers with a single tepal. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.

This variety is well-suited to moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.
