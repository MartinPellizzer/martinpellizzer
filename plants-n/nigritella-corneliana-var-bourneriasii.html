Nigritella corneliana var. bourneriasii

Nigritella corneliana var. bourneriasii, commonly known as the Bourneriasii variety of the Cornelian cherry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by orange-red berries. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively hardy in temperate climates.

This variety is closely related to other species in the genus Cornus, showcasing similar botanical features.
