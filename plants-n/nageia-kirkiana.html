Nageia kirkiana

Nageia kirkiana, commonly known as the kirk's nageia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araucariaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, long, needle-like leaves. The tree typically grows in humid, lowland forests and can reach heights of up to 30 meters. Its cones are woody and contain edible seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Nageia kirkiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
