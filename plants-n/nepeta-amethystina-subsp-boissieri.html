Nepeta amethystina subsp. boissieri

Nepeta amethystina subsp.

boissieri, commonly known as the purple catmint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Middle East and southern Europe. It features slender, upright stems covered in dense, hairy foliage.

The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from pale lavender to deep purple. Its leaves are aromatic, often emitting a mint-like scent when crushed.

This subspecies is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
