Nigritella carniolica

Nigritella carniolica, commonly known as the Carniola blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans and is characterized by its dark, almost black, berries. The plant typically grows in rocky or forested areas and prefers well-drained soils. Its leaves are serrated and have a slightly hairy texture, while its flowers are small and white or pale pink.

The berries are used in local cuisines and traditional medicine, though the plant is not widely cultivated.
