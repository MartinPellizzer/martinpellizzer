Navia emergens

Navia emergens, commonly known as the emerging navia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that often reach heights of up to 3 meters. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in the spring and summer seasons. Its leaves are elongated and slightly serrated, providing a distinctive appearance.

Navia emergens is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.
