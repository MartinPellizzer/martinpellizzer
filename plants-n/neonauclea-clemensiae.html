Neonauclea clemensiae

Neonauclea clemensiae, commonly known as the Malabar ironwood, is a small evergreen tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and hard, heavy wood that is highly valued for its durability. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a slight pubescence. It produces small, white flowers that are followed by dark, round berries.

Neonauclea clemensiae is often found in moist, shaded habitats and is considered a valuable component of the region's biodiversity.
