Nauclea speciosa

Nauclea speciosa, commonly known as the beauty bark tree, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark that reveals a vibrant red or orange inner layer, which is a notable botanical feature. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are typically alternate, simple, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Nauclea speciosa is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun.
