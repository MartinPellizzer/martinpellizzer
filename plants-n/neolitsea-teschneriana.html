Neolitsea teschneriana

Neolitsea teschneriana, commonly known as the Chinese sandpaper tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and simple, alternate leaves with a rough, sandpapery texture on the upper surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by elliptical, woody fruits containing a single seed.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
