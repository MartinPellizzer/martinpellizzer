Nipponocalamus kinkiensis

Nipponocalamus kinkiensis, commonly known as the Kinki bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems and narrow leaves. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests. It has a clumping growth habit and can reach heights of up to 2 meters.

Nipponocalamus kinkiensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
