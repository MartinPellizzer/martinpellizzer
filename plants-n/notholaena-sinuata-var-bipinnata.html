Notholaena sinuata var. bipinnata

Notholaena sinuata var. bipinnata, commonly known as the bipinnate mountain fern, is a variant of the mountain fern species native to the Appalachian region of the United States.

It is characterized by its finely divided, bipinnate leaves that create a delicate, lacy appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as rocky slopes and forest understories. Its fronds are usually a deep green, with a smooth texture and a slightly waxy cuticle.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in maintaining forest floor biodiversity.
