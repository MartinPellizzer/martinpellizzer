Nerium indicum var. splendens

Nerium indicum var. splendens, commonly known as the golden oleander, is a striking evergreen shrub native to southern Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to yellow, pink, and red, depending on the variety. The plant has simple, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy, providing a lush backdrop to its vibrant blooms. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

The golden oleander is also noted for its resilience and ability to withstand drought conditions.
