Nepeta italica

Nepeta italica, commonly known as Italian catmint, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features upright stems covered in fuzzy, gray-green leaves that are aromatic and slightly hairy. The plant produces spikes of small, blue to purple flowers in the summer months, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a hardy and low-maintenance garden plant.

Its foliage and flowers add both ornamental value and ecological benefit to the landscape.
