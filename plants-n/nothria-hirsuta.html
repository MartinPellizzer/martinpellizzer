Nothria hirsuta

Nothria hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy nothria, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish to reddish flowers arranged in composite inflorescences. It is native to South America and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Nothria hirsuta is often found in grasslands and open woodlands, where it contributes to the biodiversity of its habitat.
