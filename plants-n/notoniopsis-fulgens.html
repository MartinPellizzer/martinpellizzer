Notoniopsis fulgens

Notoniopsis fulgens, commonly known as the golden star orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers with a distinctive star-shaped pattern on the petals. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and glossy, often arranged in a rosette formation.

Notoniopsis fulgens is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
