Neomammillaria woburnensis

Neomammillaria woburnensis, commonly known as Woburn's clustered cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a small, slow-growing cactus with a short, cylindrical stem and typically produces clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. The plant has prominent, rib-like structures and spines that are usually white or pale in color. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in succulent gardens and collections.
