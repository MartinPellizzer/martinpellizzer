Nepeta sibthorpii subsp. vourinensis

Nepeta sibthorpii subsp.

vourinensis, commonly known as the Vourin catmint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with a hairy texture.

The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant blue to purple flowers in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental flowers make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and ecological landscapes.
