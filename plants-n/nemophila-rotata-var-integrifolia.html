Nemophila rotata var. integrifolia

Nemophila rotata var. integrifolia, commonly known as the Sierra violet, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, sky-blue to purple flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland and meadow habitats.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
