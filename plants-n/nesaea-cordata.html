Nesaea cordata

Nesaea cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf nesaea, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is typically evergreen, providing visual interest throughout the year.

Nesaea cordata is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and along stream banks.
