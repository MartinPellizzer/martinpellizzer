Narcissus auricolor

Narcissus auricolor, commonly known as the golden daffodil, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and western Asia, often found in woodland and grassland habitats. The plant is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which are typically trumpet-shaped with a prominent central corolla. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces a single, tall stem.

Narcissus auricolor is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and early spring blooms.
