Nocca robinsonii

Nocca robinsonii, commonly known as the desert lily, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant produces showy, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, adapted to reduce water loss in hot, dry climates.

Nocca robinsonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
