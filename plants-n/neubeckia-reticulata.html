Neubeckia reticulata

Neubeckia reticulata, commonly known as the spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its delicate, spider-like appearance, with small, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces seeds in small, capsule-like structures.

Neubeckia reticulata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in garden settings.
