Nassella chilensis

Nassella chilensis, commonly known as Chilean needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and sharp, needle-like leaves that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets.

Nassella chilensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
