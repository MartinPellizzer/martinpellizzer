Niedenzua

Niedenzua, commonly known as the "butterfly plant," is a unique member of the Gesneriaceae family, native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, butterfly-shaped leaves that are often brightly colored, ranging from deep green to variegated patterns. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, attracting pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. Niedenzua is a perennial shrub that thrives in shaded, moist environments, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its ornamental value lies in its foliage and the delicate beauty of its blooms.
