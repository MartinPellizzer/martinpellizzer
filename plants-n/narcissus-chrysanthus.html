Narcissus chrysanthus

Narcissus chrysanthus, commonly known as the golden narcissus, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and western Asia, often found in woodland and meadow habitats. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or golden in color. Its bulbs are perennial, and it blooms in early spring, making it a popular ornamental plant.

The leaves are strap-like and grassy, contributing to its overall aesthetic appeal.
