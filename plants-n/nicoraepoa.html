Nicoraepoa

Nicoraepoa, commonly known as the silver fern, is a unique plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its distinctive fronds that resemble a fern, with a silvery sheen due to the presence of fine, hair-like structures called trichomes. The plant thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found growing in shaded, moist environments. Its leaves are typically divided into segments, giving it a feathery appearance.

Nicoraepoa is not only ornamental but also holds cultural significance in various Polynesian communities.
