Nepeta kurdica

Nepeta kurdica, commonly known as Kurdish catnip, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their medicinal and culinary uses. The plant features square, hairy stems and opposite leaves that are typically gray-green in color. Its small, clustered flowers are usually blue or violet, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Nepeta kurdica is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used traditionally in herbal medicine for its calming and antispasmodic effects.
