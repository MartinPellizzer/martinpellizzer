Notopleura polyphlebia

Notopleura polyphlebia, commonly known as the broadleaf water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to the wetlands of Southeast Asia.

It features broad, floating leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. The plant produces showy white or pink flowers that bloom above the water surface, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its roots anchor it in the muddy substrate, while its rhizomes spread horizontally to form dense colonies.
