Notocactus

Notocactus, commonly known as the "Pencil Cactus," is a genus of cacti native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that resemble pencils, which are covered in a waxy, bluish-green cuticle. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it suitable for arid environments. Notocactus species typically produce clusters of yellow or white flowers during the warmer months.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor gardening.
