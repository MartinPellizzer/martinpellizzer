Nothofagus balansae

Nothofagus balansae, commonly known as the Balansae Nothofagus, is a species of evergreen tree native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the Nothofagus genus, which is part of the Nothofagaceae family, often referred to as the southern beech family. The plant is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves and can grow up to 20 meters in height, forming dense forests in temperate regions.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
