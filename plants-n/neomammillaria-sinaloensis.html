Neomammillaria sinaloensis

Neomammillaria sinaloensis, commonly known as the Sinaloan mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stem and small, rounded tubercles that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water and well-draining soil.

Neomammillaria sinaloensis is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its unique morphology and low maintenance requirements.
