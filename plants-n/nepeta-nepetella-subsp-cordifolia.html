Nepeta nepetella subsp. cordifolia

Nepeta nepetella subsp.

cordifolia, commonly known as heartleaf catmint, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It features aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that are often heart-shaped, giving it its common name.

The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

This subspecies is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
