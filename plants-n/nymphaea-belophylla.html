Nymphaea belophylla

Nymphaea belophylla, commonly known as the white lotus, is a species of water lily native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that open in the morning and close in the evening. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes. Its leaves are broad and floating, providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.

The flowers emit a subtle fragrance, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
