Neotinea intacta var. tridentata

Neotinea intacta var. tridentata, commonly known as the three-toothed fern, is a perennial plant native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are typically divided into three distinct lobes. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.
