Nassella laxiflora

Nassella laxiflora, commonly known as slender needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to the western United States and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that produce delicate, pale blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is well adapted to arid conditions. Its foliage is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.

Nassella laxiflora plays a role in stabilizing soil in disturbed areas and provides habitat for various insects and small animals.
