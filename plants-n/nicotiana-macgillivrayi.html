Nicotiana macgillivrayi

Nicotiana macgillivrayi, commonly known as the MacGillivray's tobacco, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family.

It is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, typically found in rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are usually pink or purple, blooming in late summer. It is a perennial herb that can grow up to 1 meter tall, with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves.

Its botanical features make it a notable species for both horticultural interest and ecological studies.
