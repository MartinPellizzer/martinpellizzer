Neomarica kollmannii

Neomarica kollmannii, commonly known as the red spider lily, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking red, spider-like flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has strap-shaped, dark green leaves that form a dense rosette at the base. It produces a tall, slender stem that bears the showy inflorescence.

Neomarica kollmannii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil and full sun.
