Nanarepenta juxtlahuacensis

Nanarepenta juxtlahuacensis, commonly known as the Juxtlahuaca orchid, is a rare and endemic species native to the mountainous regions of central Mexico.

This plant belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in cloud forests at elevations between 1,500 and 2,500 meters. The plant has narrow, strap-like leaves and produces showy, violet-colored blossoms that attract specific pollinators.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered a vulnerable species requiring conservation efforts.
