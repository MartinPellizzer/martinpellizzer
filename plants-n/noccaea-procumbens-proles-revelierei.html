Noccaea procumbens proles revelierei

Noccaea procumbens proles revelierei, commonly known as the creeping watercress, is a low-growing aquatic plant native to temperate regions.

It thrives in shallow, freshwater environments such as streams and ponds, where it forms dense mats on the water surface. The plant has small, heart-shaped leaves that are often submerged, with tiny white flowers that appear above the water. Its reproductive strategy involves both sexual and asexual propagation, allowing it to spread efficiently in suitable habitats.

The species is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to tolerate fluctuating water levels.
