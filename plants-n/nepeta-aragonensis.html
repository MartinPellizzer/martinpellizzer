Nepeta aragonensis

Nepeta aragonensis, commonly known as the Aragon catmint, is a perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically gray-green and have a strong, minty scent.

It is often grown as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and fragrant properties.
