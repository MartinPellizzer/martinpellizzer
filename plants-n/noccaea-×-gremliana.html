Noccaea × gremliana

Noccaea × gremliana, commonly known as the European watercress, is a hybrid species within the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, alternate leaves that are often submerged or floating in aquatic environments. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a short, cylindrical silique containing several seeds.

Noccaea × gremliana is often found in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats, such as ponds and ditches.
