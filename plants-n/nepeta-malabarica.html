Nepeta malabarica

Nepeta malabarica, commonly known as the Malabar catmint, is a perennial herb native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported calming and antiseptic properties.

Nepeta malabarica thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or forested areas.
