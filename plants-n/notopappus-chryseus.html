Notopappus chryseus

Notopappus chryseus, commonly known as the golden cupplant, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a distinct basal rosette of broad, heart-shaped leaves and a flowering stem that rises above the foliage. Its inflorescence consists of numerous small yellow disk flowers surrounded by pale yellow ray florets.

Notopappus chryseus is adapted to dry, sunny habitats and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
