Neurolepis silverstonei

Neurolepis silverstonei, commonly known as the silverstone daisy, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb with a compact, mat-forming growth habit, often found in rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of narrow, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs, providing a silvery appearance.

Neurolepis silverstonei is adapted to arid environments and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.
