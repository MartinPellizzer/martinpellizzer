Nanozostera

Nanozostera, commonly known as the dwarf seagrass, is a genus of seagrasses found in tropical and subtropical coastal waters.

It typically grows in shallow, sandy or muddy substrates and forms dense underwater meadows. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are often ribbon-like in appearance, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers. Nanozostera species are important for stabilizing sediments and providing habitat for marine life.

Its botanical structure is adapted to thrive in saline environments with fluctuating water conditions.
