Notocactus olimarensis

Notocactus olimarensis, commonly known as the Olimar Desert Cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of northern Mexico.

It is a columnar cactus with a ribbed, cylindrical stem that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces bright yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are clustered at the ends of the stems. Its leaves are modified into spines, providing protection from herbivores and reducing water loss.

Notocactus olimarensis is well adapted to extreme desert conditions, making it a resilient and hardy plant.
