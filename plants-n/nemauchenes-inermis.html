Nemauchenes inermis

Nemauchenes inermis, commonly known as the slender sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its small, fleshy leaves and clusters of tiny, white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in sandy or rocky habitats. Its succulent leaves help it retain moisture in dry environments.

Nemauchenes inermis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
