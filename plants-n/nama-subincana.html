Nama subincana

Nama subincana, commonly known as the narrow-leaf nama, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairy leaves and small, yellowish flowers that appear in spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It is often found in coastal and inland regions of Namibia and South Africa.

Nama subincana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
