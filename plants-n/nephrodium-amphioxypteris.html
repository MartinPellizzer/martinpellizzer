Nephrodium amphioxypteris

Nephrodium amphioxypteris, commonly known as the shield fern, is a prehistoric plant belonging to the family Osmundaceae.

It is characterized by its large, shield-shaped fronds that are typically divided into segments. The plant exhibits a distinctive morphology with a central axis and fertile fronds that bear sporangia. It thrived during the Carboniferous and Permian periods, indicating its adaptation to ancient swampy environments.

Its fossilized remains provide important insights into the evolution of early ferns and their ecological roles.
