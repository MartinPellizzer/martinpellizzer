Nasa trianae

Nasa trianae, commonly known as the moonflower, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its large, fragrant white flowers that open at night and close by dawn. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped blooms that are typically 5 to 8 inches in diameter. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, often with a velvety texture.

Nasa trianae thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
