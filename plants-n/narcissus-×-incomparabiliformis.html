Narcissus × incomparabiliformis

Narcissus × incomparabiliformis, commonly known as the Giant White Narcissus, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Narcissus tazettoides and Narcissus poeticus.

It is characterized by its large, white, cup-shaped flowers that can reach up to 15 cm in diameter, making it one of the largest narcissus varieties. The plant typically grows to a height of 40–60 cm, with strap-like, grassy foliage. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, preferring temperate climates.

This cultivar is prized for its striking appearance and is often used in floral arrangements and gardens.
