Neesiella

Neesiella, commonly known as the African violet, is a genus of flowering plants native to Africa.

These plants are characterized by their rosettes of fleshy, velvety leaves and showy, tubular flowers that come in a variety of colors. Neesiella species are typically herbaceous, with some varieties growing as small shrubs. They thrive in well-drained soil and require bright, indirect light to flourish.

The genus is popular among gardeners for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation.
