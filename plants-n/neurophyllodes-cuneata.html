Neurophyllodes cuneata

Neurophyllodes cuneata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf neurophyllodes, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It features slender, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along its stem. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers during the spring and early summer months. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in nutrient-poor soils and withstand periods of drought.

Neurophyllodes cuneata is often found in rocky outcrops and sandy soils, where it plays a role in stabilizing the local ecosystem.
