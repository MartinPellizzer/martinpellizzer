Nymphaea gibertii

Nymphaea gibertii, commonly known as the Gibert's water lily, is a species of aquatic plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves that provide a habitat for various aquatic organisms. The plant produces strikingly large, showy flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in the early morning. Nymphaea gibertii thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
