Nonatelia lutea

Nonatelia lutea, commonly known as the yellow-flowered orchid, is a tropical plant native to regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow petals and a slender, erect stem. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments within rainforests. Its flowers bloom in clusters and are often pollinated by bees and other insects.

Nonatelia lutea is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
