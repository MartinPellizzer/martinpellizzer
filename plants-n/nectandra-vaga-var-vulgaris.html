Nectandra vaga var. vulgaris

Nectandra vaga var. vulgaris, commonly known as the fragrant sandarac tree, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows in moist, lowland forests. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that are often greenish-yellow in color. Its wood is valued for its fragrant properties and has been used historically in incense and varnishes.

The plant's botanical significance lies in its unique chemical composition and ecological role in its native habitat.
