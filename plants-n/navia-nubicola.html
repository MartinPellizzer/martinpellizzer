Navia nubicola

Navia nubicola, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to East Africa.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its compact, fleshy stems and velvety leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white, often with a contrasting throat. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Navia nubicola is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
