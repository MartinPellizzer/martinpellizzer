Neoregelia fosteriana

Neoregelia fosteriana, commonly known as the rainbow bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful bracts that surround its small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant's rosette-shaped leaves are thick and waxy, designed to collect and store rainwater. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires high humidity to flourish.

Neoregelia fosteriana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in humid indoor environments.
