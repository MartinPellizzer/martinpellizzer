Nephelium lappaceum

Nephelium lappaceum, commonly known as rambutan, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, spiny fruit covering, which gives it a unique appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is typically bright red or yellow when ripe and contains a single, edible seed.

The tree is valued for its nutritional content and is widely cultivated for both local consumption and commercial export.
