Nabiasodendron grande

Nabiasodendron grande, commonly known as the giant nabia, is a rare and striking plant native to specific regions of the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its towering height, often reaching over 10 meters, and its broad, palm-like leaves that provide shade. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, adding to its ornamental appeal. Its bark is thick and cork-like, providing protection against harsh desert conditions.

Due to its limited habitat and threats from environmental changes, it is considered a vulnerable species.
