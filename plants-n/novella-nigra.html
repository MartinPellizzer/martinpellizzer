Novella nigra

Novella nigra, commonly known as black nightshade, is a flowering plant belonging to the Solanaceae family.

It is native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa, and it typically grows in disturbed soils and along roadsides. The plant features dark purple to black berries that are toxic to humans and animals. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a slightly hairy texture, and it produces small white or pale purple flowers.

Novella nigra is often considered a weed due to its rapid growth and ability to thrive in various environments.
