Nephrolepis falcata

Nephrolepis falcata, commonly known as the sword fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its long, narrow, and leathery fronds that are often curled when young. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests or rocky crevices. Its reproductive structures, including sporangia, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Nephrolepis falcata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant.
