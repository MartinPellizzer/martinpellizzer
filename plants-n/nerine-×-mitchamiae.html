Nerine × mitchamiae

Nerine × mitchamiae, commonly known as the hybrid nerine or Mitcham's nerine, is a perennial bulbous plant belonging to the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, tubular flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, which are borne on upright stems. Its foliage consists of strap-like, grassy leaves that emerge in early spring.

Nerine × mitchamiae is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
