Niota pendula

Niota pendula, commonly known as the weeping niota, is a species of flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 6 to 10 feet. The plant is characterized by its pendulous clusters of white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated edge, often turning red in the fall.

Niota pendula thrives in moist, well-drained soil and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.
