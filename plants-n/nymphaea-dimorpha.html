Nymphaea dimorpha

Nymphaea dimorpha, commonly known as the two-form water lily, is a species of aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant has broad, floating leaves that provide habitat for various aquatic organisms. Its roots anchor it in the muddy substrate at the bottom of freshwater habitats.

Nymphaea dimorpha plays an important role in maintaining the ecological balance of its environment.
