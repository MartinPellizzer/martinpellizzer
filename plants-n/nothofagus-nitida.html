Nothofagus nitida

Nothofagus nitida, commonly known as the slender nothofagus, is a species of evergreen tree native to South America, particularly found in temperate rainforests of Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the Nothofagus genus, which is part of the Nothofagaceae family, often referred to as the southern beech family. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and dark green, with a leathery texture. Nothofagus nitida produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by woody, spherical cones that contain edible seeds.

This species plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
