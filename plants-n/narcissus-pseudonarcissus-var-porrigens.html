Narcissus pseudonarcissus var. porrigens

Narcissus pseudonarcissus var. porrigens, commonly known as the Spanish daffodil, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Europe.

It produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers with a bright yellow corona and white petals. The plant typically blooms in early spring and is characterized by its robust, strap-like leaves. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is noted for its hardiness and ability to naturalize over time.
