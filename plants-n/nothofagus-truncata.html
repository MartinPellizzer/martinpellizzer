Nothofagus truncata

Nothofagus truncata, commonly known as the southern beech or mountain beech, is a species of evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Nothofagaceae family and is characterized by its small, dark green leaves and smooth, dark bark. The plant typically grows in temperate rainforests and high-altitude regions, preferring cool and moist conditions. Its cones are small and contain edible seeds, which are an important food source for various wildlife species.

Nothofagus truncata plays a significant ecological role in its native habitats, contributing to soil stability and biodiversity.
