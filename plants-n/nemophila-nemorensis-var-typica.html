Nemophila nemorensis var. typica

Nemophila nemorensis var. typica, commonly known as the Sierra violet, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches heights of 10 to 20 centimeters. The plant features slender, hairy stems and produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forested areas and along stream banks.
