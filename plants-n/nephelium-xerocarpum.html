Nephelium xerocarpum

Nephelium xerocarpum, commonly known as the rambutan, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, spiky fruit exterior. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically green or red in color. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate with a smooth surface.

The rambutan tree thrives in warm, humid climates and is cultivated for its edible, sweet fruit.
