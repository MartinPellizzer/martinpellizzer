Nardus stricta var. minor

Nardus stricta var. minor, commonly known as lesser heath, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, pale green to brownish flowers. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and often covered in a fine layer of wax.

This species is adapted to poor, well-drained soils and is often found in heathland and open woodland environments.
