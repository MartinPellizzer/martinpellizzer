Neomammillaria plumosa

Neomammillaria plumosa, commonly known as the plumose mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are covered in white, woolly spines, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a vibrant touch to its otherwise desolate look. It is a slow-growing species that thrives in well-draining soil and full sun.

Neomammillaria plumosa is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its unique morphology and low maintenance requirements.
