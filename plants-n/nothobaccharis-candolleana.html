Nothobaccharis candolleana

Nothobaccharis candolleana, commonly known as the Candolleana baccharis, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Brazil.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, white to yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has a woody stem with a rough, bark-like texture and thrives in sandy or saline soils. It is often found in dune ecosystems and is adapted to withstand coastal conditions such as salt spray and strong winds.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies in coastal plant communities.
