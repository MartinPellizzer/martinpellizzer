Nicotiana azambujae

Nicotiana azambujae, commonly known as the Azambuja tobacco plant, is a species native to Brazil.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in the understory of tropical forests. It has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in the late afternoon.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and research settings.
