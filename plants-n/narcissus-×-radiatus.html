Narcissus × radiatus

Narcissus × radiatus, commonly known as the radiated daffodil, is a hybrid perennial plant belonging to the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy, trumpet-shaped flowers with a distinctive radiating pattern of yellow or white petals. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with linear, grass-like leaves that emerge from a bulbous base. Native to Europe, it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its early spring blooming period, which adds vibrant color to gardens.
