Nebropsis alba

Nebropsis alba, commonly known as the white nebropsis, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its delicate, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
