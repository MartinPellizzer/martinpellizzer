Notocactus leucocarpus

Notocactus leucocarpus, commonly known as the white-fleshed cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its smooth, green to bluish-green stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer, and it has a unique feature of having a white, fleshy interior in its fruit. It is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
