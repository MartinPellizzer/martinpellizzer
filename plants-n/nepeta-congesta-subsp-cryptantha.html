Nepeta congesta subsp. cryptantha

Nepeta congesta subsp.

cryptantha, commonly known as the cryptantha catmint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers.

The plant features aromatic leaves that are often used in traditional herbal medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its botanical structure includes opposite leaves and whorled inflorescences, typical of the Lamiaceae family.
