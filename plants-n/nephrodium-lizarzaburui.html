Nephrodium lizarzaburui

Nephrodium lizarzaburui, commonly known as the Lizarzaburi kidney fern, is a rare fern species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, kidney-shaped fronds that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, silky hairs. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil to flourish. Its reproductive structures, including sporangia, are typically found on the underside of the fronds, contributing to its unique botanical appearance.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
