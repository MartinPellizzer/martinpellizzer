Nepenthes teysmanniana

Nepenthes teysmanniana, commonly known as the Teyssmann's pitcher plant, is a tropical climbing vine native to Borneo.

It is characterized by its large, pitcher-shaped leaves that secrete a nutrient-rich liquid to trap and digest insects. The plant's unique morphology includes a waxy, glossy surface on its pitchers, which helps in water retention and insect attraction. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its striking appearance and ecological significance, it is a popular subject in botanical studies and conservation efforts.
