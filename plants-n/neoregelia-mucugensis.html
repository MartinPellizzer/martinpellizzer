Neoregelia mucugensis

Neoregelia mucugensis, commonly known as the Brazilian velvet plant, is a striking bromeliad native to the rainforests of Brazil.

It is characterized by its broad, waxy leaves that form a rosette, often displaying vibrant colors such as red, orange, and yellow. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Its flowers, which are typically pink or purple, emerge from the center of the rosette and can last for several months.

Neoregelia mucugensis is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
