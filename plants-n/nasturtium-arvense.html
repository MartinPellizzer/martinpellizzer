Nasturtium arvense

Nasturtium arvense, commonly known as field nasturtium, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Tropaeolaceae and is characterized by its vibrant orange or yellow flowers and heart-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. Its stems are often hairy, and the leaves are slightly pubescent.

The flowers are not only ornamental but also edible, with a peppery flavor similar to watercress.
