Nicotiana undulata var. angustifolia

Nicotiana undulata var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf tobacco plant, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of trumpet-shaped, pale yellow to white flowers. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and is often found in open, sunny habitats. It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is related to other tobacco species.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
