Nandina denudata

Nandina denudata, commonly known as the heavenly bamboo, is a shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, white flowers that appear in late spring. The plant produces red berries in the fall, which are a food source for birds. Its leaves are typically dark green with a waxy texture and can turn red in the winter.

Nandina denudata is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature.
