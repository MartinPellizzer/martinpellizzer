Nasturtium sublyratum var. longicarpum

Nasturtium sublyratum var. longicarpum, commonly known as the long-seeded nasturtium, is a variant of the nasturtium plant characterized by its elongated, cylindrical seed pods.

It belongs to the family Tropaeolaceae and is native to South America, particularly regions of Brazil. The plant features vibrant, edible flowers in shades of orange and yellow, which are often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

The long, slender seed pods mature to a reddish-brown color and are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
