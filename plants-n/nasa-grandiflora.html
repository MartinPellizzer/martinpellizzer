Nasa grandiflora

Nasa grandiflora, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and showy, white flowers that open at night. The plant is adapted to arid environments, with thick, fleshy stems that store water. Its flowers emit a strong fragrance to attract nocturnal pollinators like moths.

Nasa grandiflora is often cultivated for its unique blooming habit and ornamental value.
