Nanorrhinum acerbianum subsp. montanum

Nanorrhinum acerbianum subsp.

montanum, commonly known as the mountain nanorrhinum, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa. It is characterized by its erect, slender stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in well-drained environments. It is often found in open grasslands and along rocky outcrops at moderate to high elevations.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
