Nectandra puchury-major

Nectandra puchury-major, commonly known as the Puchury-major tree, is a tropical evergreen species native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by edible, fleshy berries. Its wood is valued for its durability and aromatic properties, making it useful in construction and traditional crafts.

The tree thrives in humid, shaded environments and plays an important role in the ecosystems of its native regions.
