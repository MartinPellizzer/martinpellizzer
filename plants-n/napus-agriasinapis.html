Napus agriasinapis

Napus agriasinapis, commonly known as the wild cabbage, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia and thrives in temperate climates. It has a thick, fleshy stem and is often found in disturbed soils along roadsides and in fields.

Napus agriasinapis is a herbaceous perennial that can grow up to 1 meter in height.
