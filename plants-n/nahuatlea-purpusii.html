Nahuatlea purpusii

Nahuatlea purpusii, commonly known as the Mexican spider orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid native to Mexico, particularly in the states of Oaxaca and Chiapas. The plant features a slender, wiry stem with a single, narrow leaf and a short, erect inflorescence. Its flowers are small, white to pale pink, and have a distinctive, spider-like appearance due to their elongated petals and sepals.

Nahuatlea purpusii is adapted to grow in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in shaded, humid environments.
