Neoporteria castanea

Neoporteria castanea, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a small evergreen shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain one or two seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation efforts.
