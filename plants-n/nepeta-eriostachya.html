Nepeta eriostachya

Nepeta eriostachya, commonly known as the woolly catmint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and clusters of small, pale blue to violet flowers. The plant produces whorled leaves and has a compact growth habit, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, often blooming from late spring to early summer.

Nepeta eriostachya is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
