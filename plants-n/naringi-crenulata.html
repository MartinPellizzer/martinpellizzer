Naringi crenulata

Naringi crenulata, commonly known as the sour orange, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree produces small, round citrus fruits with a thick, yellow-orange rind and a sour, acidic flavor. Its fruit is widely used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent in food and beverages.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various climates.
