Neorites kevedianus

Neorites kevedianus, commonly known as the Kevadian lily, is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, large, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and grows in moist, shady environments. It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.

Neorites kevedianus is also of interest to botanists due to its unique floral structure and adaptation to high-altitude conditions.
