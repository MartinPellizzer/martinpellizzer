Neomammillaria louisae

Neomammillaria louisae, commonly known as the Desert Rose Cactus, is a small, spineless cactus native to the Sonoran Desert.

It features a short, cylindrical stem covered with white, waxy tubercles that give it a distinctive, rose-like appearance. The plant produces delicate, pink to purple flowers during the spring and summer months. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stem.

Neomammillaria louisae is a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor collections due to its ornamental appeal and low maintenance requirements.
