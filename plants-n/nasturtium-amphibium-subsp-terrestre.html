Nasturtium amphibium subsp. terrestre

Nasturtium amphibium subsp.

terrestre, commonly known as the terrestrial nasturtium, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and vibrant, edible yellow or orange flowers.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies such as ponds and streams. Its stems are typically sprawling or trailing, and it produces small, round, edible seeds.

The plant is also noted for its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
