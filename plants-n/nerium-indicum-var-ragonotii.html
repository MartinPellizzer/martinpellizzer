Nerium indicum var. ragonotii

Nerium indicum var. ragonotii, commonly known as the Ragonot's oleander, is a variant of the oleander plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep pink. The plant features smooth, grey-green foliage and is known for its drought tolerance and adaptability to various soil types. It produces small, woody fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
