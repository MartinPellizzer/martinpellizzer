Nepeta pycnantha

Nepeta pycnantha, commonly known as catmint, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is a member of the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is known for its aromatic foliage and ability to attract pollinators.

Nepeta pycnantha is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and as a source of nectar for bees and butterflies.
