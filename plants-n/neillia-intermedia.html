Neillia intermedia

Neillia intermedia, commonly known as the common rockrose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are usually narrow and gray-green, adapted to reduce water loss.

Neillia intermedia is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
