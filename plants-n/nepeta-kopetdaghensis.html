Nepeta kopetdaghensis

Nepeta kopetdaghensis, commonly known as the Kopetdagh catmint, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value and low water requirements make it a desirable plant for drought-tolerant gardens.
