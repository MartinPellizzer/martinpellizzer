Nymphaea × rosea

Nymphaea × rosea, commonly known as the pink water lily, is a hybrid aquatic plant that thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments.

It is characterized by its large, showy pink or white flowers that float on the water's surface, often opening in the early morning. The plant has broad, rounded green leaves that are typically floating or partially submerged, depending on the water conditions. Its roots are anchored in the muddy substrate at the bottom of ponds or lakes.

Nymphaea × rosea is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in water gardens and ponds.
