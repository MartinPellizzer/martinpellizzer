Nepeta grandiflora var. glabrata

Nepeta grandiflora var. glabrata, commonly known as catmint, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, aromatic stems and small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in spikes. The leaves are typically gray-green and fuzzy, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and mild mint-like fragrance.
