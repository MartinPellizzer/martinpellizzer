Neolitsea reticulata

Neolitsea reticulata, commonly known as the reticulate litsea, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, ovate leaves with a reticulate venation pattern that gives it its scientific name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by elliptical, red or orange berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Neolitsea reticulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
