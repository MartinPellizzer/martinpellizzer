Nigella segetalis var. armena

Nigella segetalis var. armena, commonly known as Armenian cumin, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its branched stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, oval-shaped seeds that are often used as a spice in Middle Eastern and Mediterranean cuisines. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Its botanical features make it both an ornamental plant and a valuable crop for culinary use.
