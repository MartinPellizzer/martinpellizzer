Nicotiana integrifolia

Nicotiana integrifolia, commonly known as the whole-leaf tobacco, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family.

It is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. The plant features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple, and it has broad, entire leaves that are smooth and glossy. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance.

Additionally, it is used in scientific research, particularly in genetics and plant biology studies.
