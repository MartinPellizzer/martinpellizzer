Newzealochilus flavus

Newzealochilus flavus, commonly known as the golden-lipped orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to New Zealand.

This terrestrial orchid is characterized by its vibrant yellow petals and sepals, which give it its distinctive appearance. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom during the spring and summer months.

Its unique morphology and habitat preferences make it a notable species within New Zealand's diverse orchid flora.
