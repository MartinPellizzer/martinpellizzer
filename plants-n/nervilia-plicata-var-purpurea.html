Nervilia plicata var. purpurea

Nervilia plicata var. purpurea, commonly known as the purple-stemmed fern, is a striking variety of the genus Nervilia, native to tropical and subtropical regions.

This plant is characterized by its vibrant purple stems and delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are often adorned with intricate vein patterns. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in forest understories or along stream banks. The leaves are usually a deep green, contrasting beautifully with the purple stems, making it a visually appealing ornamental plant.

Its botanical features make it well-suited for humid climates and partial shade, though it requires careful attention to soil moisture levels for optimal growth.
