Nepeta involucrata

Nepeta involucrata, commonly known as catnip, is a flowering plant in the mint family (Lamiaceae).

It is native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa, and it thrives in sunny, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, arranged in whorled clusters. Its leaves are aromatic and contain compounds like nepetalactone, which are responsible for its characteristic effect on cats.

Nepeta involucrata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in herbal medicine.
