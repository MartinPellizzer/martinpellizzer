Nasturtium affghanum

Nasturtium affghanum, commonly known as Afghan nasturtium, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, which includes other well-known plants like mustard and cabbage. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are edible and have a peppery flavor, similar to watercress, making it a useful plant in culinary applications.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in rocky or alpine environments.
