Noterophila inundata var. parvifolia

Noterophila inundata var. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved water willow, is a herbaceous plant native to wetland environments.

It features slender, erect stems that often grow in dense clusters near water bodies. The leaves are small, ovate, and have a smooth surface, typically arranged alternately along the stem. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikes, producing a delicate white or pale green color.

This variant is adapted to moist or aquatic habitats, contributing to its role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
