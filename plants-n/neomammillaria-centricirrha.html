Neomammillaria centricirrha

Neomammillaria centricirrha, commonly known as the Mexican star cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a small, globular cactus with a ribbed stem that typically measures around 10 to 20 centimeters in height. The plant produces white or pale pink flowers during the spring and summer months. Its dense, spiny areoles are characteristic of the genus Neomammillaria.

It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.
