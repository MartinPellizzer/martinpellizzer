Nepeta nepetella var. murcica

Nepeta nepetella var. murcica, commonly known as the Murcian catmint, is a perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It features slender, aromatic stems and lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces spikes of small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is well adapted to rocky and dry soils, often found in mountainous regions.

Its cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and its historical use as a remedy for cats suffering from flea infestations.
