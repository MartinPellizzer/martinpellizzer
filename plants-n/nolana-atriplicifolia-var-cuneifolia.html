Nolana atriplicifolia var. cuneifolia

Nolana atriplicifolia var. cuneifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf nolana, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern South America.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal dunes or along the edges of salt marshes. It is an annual or biennial species, with its life cycle closely tied to seasonal changes and coastal climate conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of coastal ecosystems, supporting local biodiversity.
