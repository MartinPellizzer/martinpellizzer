Nesodaphne obtusifolia

Nesodaphne obtusifolia, commonly known as the blunt-leaved daphne, is a flowering plant native to the island of Socotra.

It belongs to the family Daphnaceae and is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant is adapted to arid environments, with succulent leaves that help store water. It is also notable for its unique floral structure, which includes both showy petals and specialized reproductive parts.

Due to its restricted habitat and ecological significance, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
