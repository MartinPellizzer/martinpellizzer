Nepeta canescens

Nepeta canescens, commonly known as catnip, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It is characterized by its grayish-green, hairy leaves and small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant contains nepetalactone, a compound responsible for its stimulating effect on cats. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in rocky or dry habitats.

Nepeta canescens is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
