Nephrea

Nephrea, commonly known as the ghost plant, is a unique aquatic plant characterized by its translucent, leaf-like structures that resemble ghostly shapes.

It thrives in shallow, still waters and is often found in wetlands and marshes. The plant lacks true roots and instead relies on its broad, ribbon-like leaves for anchoring and nutrient absorption. Nephrea is known for its ability to absorb pollutants from water, making it an important species in water purification.

Its delicate appearance and ecological role make it a fascinating subject in botanical studies.
