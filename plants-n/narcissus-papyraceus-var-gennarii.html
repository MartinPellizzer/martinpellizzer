Narcissus papyraceus var. gennarii

Narcissus papyraceus var. gennarii, commonly known as the Gennari's paperwhite, is a variant of the paperwhite narcissus native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, white flowers with a cup-shaped corona. The plant typically grows to a height of about 15-20 centimeters and thrives in cool, humid conditions. It is often cultivated for its delicate appearance and is suitable for indoor growing during the winter months.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ease of care.
