Nepeta pamirensis

Nepeta pamirensis, commonly known as Pamir catmint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, often in alpine environments. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pale blue or violet, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Nepeta pamirensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses in local communities.
