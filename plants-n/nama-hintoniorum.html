Nama hintoniorum

Nama hintoniorum, commonly known as the Hinton's lily, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and is adapted to cold, temperate climates. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, and it produces small, clustered flowers during the spring season.

Due to its rarity and ecological significance, Nama hintoniorum is often found in conservation areas.
