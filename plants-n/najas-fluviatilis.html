Najas fluviatilis

Najas fluviatilis, commonly known as the river waterweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments.

It belongs to the Najadaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, whorled leaves. The plant typically grows in shallow, slow-moving waters such as rivers, lakes, and ponds. Its flowers are unisexual, with male and female flowers occurring on separate plants, and it produces small, greenish fruits.

Najas fluviatilis plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various water organisms.
