Negundium fraxineum

Negundium fraxineum, commonly known as the European ash, is a deciduous tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, simple leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, followed by samaroid fruits that are dispersed by wind. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, with longitudinal fissures, and it can grow up to 20 meters tall.

The European ash is often found in moist, well-drained soils and is valued for its timber and ornamental qualities.
