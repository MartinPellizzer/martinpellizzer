Narcissus pseudonarcissus var. festinus

Narcissus pseudonarcissus var. festinus, commonly known as the Dutch narcissus, is a variety of the common daffodil.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated bloom with a single, prominent yellow corona. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 50 centimeters, featuring strap-like, grassy leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often naturalizing over time in suitable conditions.

This variety is prized for its early spring bloom and ornamental value in gardens.
