Neomammillaria discolor

Neomammillaria discolor, commonly known as the variegated pincushion cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are often covered in white or pale blue spines. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are typically reduced to spines, and it thrives in well-drained soil with minimal water.

Neomammillaria discolor is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appearance.
