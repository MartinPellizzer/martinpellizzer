Neomolinia fauriei

Neomolinia fauriei, commonly known as the Faurie's grass, is a perennial grass species native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes a wide variety of grasses, and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its flowering structures, known as inflorescences, consist of spikelets that are arranged in a panicle-like pattern.

Neomolinia fauriei is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
