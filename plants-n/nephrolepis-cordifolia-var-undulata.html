Nephrolepis cordifolia var. undulata

Nephrolepis cordifolia var. undulata, commonly known as the undulating sword fern, is a variant of the sword fern species native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its long, arching fronds that are deeply divided into narrow, lance-shaped segments. The fronds are typically a deep green with a slightly curled or undulating edge, giving it its common name. This fern thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires consistent moisture and well-draining soil.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its lush, feathery appearance.
