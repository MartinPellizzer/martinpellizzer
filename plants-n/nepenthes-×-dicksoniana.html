Nepenthes × dicksoniana

Nepenthes × dicksoniana, commonly known as the hybrid pitcher plant, is a tropical carnivorous plant that exhibits a unique combination of traits from its parent species, N. rafflesiana and N. mirabilis.

It is characterized by its large, colorful pitcher-shaped leaves that secrete a digestive fluid to trap and digest insects. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in the rainforests of Borneo. Its pitchers can vary in color, often displaying shades of red, orange, and yellow, which help attract prey.

This hybrid is highly sought after by horticulturists for its striking appearance and ease of cultivation under proper conditions.
