Narcissus juncifolius subsp. rupicola

Narcissus juncifolius subsp.

rupicola, commonly known as the rock daffodil, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, yellow flowers with a distinctive cup-shaped corona.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments. It is part of the Amaryllidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness.

This subspecies is often used in rock gardens and alpine settings due to its compact growth habit.
