Nepeta sanabrensis

Nepeta sanabrensis, commonly known as Sanabres catmint, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its flowers are small, tubular, and usually violet or blue in color, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Nepeta sanabrensis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
