Nasturtium pyrenaicum var. hispanicum

Nasturtium pyrenaicum var. hispanicum, commonly known as the Pyrenean nasturtium, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange or red flowers and heart-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous plants.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
