Nepeta Nuda Subsp. Violacea

Purple catmint, scientifically known as Nepeta nuda subsp. violacea, is a striking perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. Its vivid purple flowers and fragrant foliage make it a popular choice for gardens and herbal remedies. This subspecies of catmint is closely related to the common catmint, but it boasts a more intense color and stronger scent. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it easy to care for in both garden beds and containers. With its ornamental value and potential medicinal uses, Purple catmint remains a cherished plant among horticulturists and herbalists alike.
