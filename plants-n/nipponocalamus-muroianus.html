Nipponocalamus muroianus

Nipponocalamus muroianus, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 3 meters. The plant features narrow, linear leaves with a smooth, grassy texture, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Nipponocalamus muroianus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
