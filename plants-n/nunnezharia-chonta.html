Nunnezharia chonta

Nunnezharia chonta, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery foliage and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in the late summer and early autumn. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Nunnezharia chonta is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
