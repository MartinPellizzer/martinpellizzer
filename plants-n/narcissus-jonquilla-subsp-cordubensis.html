Narcissus jonquilla subsp. cordubensis

Narcissus jonquilla subsp.

cordubensis, commonly known as the Cordoba daffodil, is a subspecies native to southern Europe, particularly Spain. It is characterized by its narrow, strap-like leaves and a single, upright flower with a yellow perianth.

The tepals are typically arranged in a tight, cup-shaped form, with a prominent central throat. This plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family.
