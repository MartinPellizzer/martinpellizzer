Nectandra mopanensis

Nectandra mopanensis, commonly known as the Mopan sandalwood, is a tropical tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, fragrant wood. The plant typically grows in lowland rainforests and has smooth, dark-brown bark. Its leaves are alternate, elliptical, and have a glossy surface.

The tree produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits containing aromatic seeds.
