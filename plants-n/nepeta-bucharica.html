Nepeta bucharica

Nepeta bucharica, commonly known as Carpathian catmint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Eastern Europe and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, pale blue or lavender flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a slightly fuzzy texture, and it is known for its strong, minty fragrance. Nepeta bucharica is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile addition to gardens and landscapes.
