Neophloga lutea var. transiens

Neophloga lutea var. transiens, commonly known as the yellow flame bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of southern Europe.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range from yellow to orange, often with a reddish throat, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shady habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, while its stems are erect and branching.

It is valued in horticulture for its vibrant color and ability to thrive in partial shade.
