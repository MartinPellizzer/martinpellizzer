Nasturtium saxatile

Nasturtium saxatile, commonly known as the watercress, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its small, edible leaves that have a peppery, tangy flavor, and its bright orange or yellow flowers. The plant thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found near streams or in wet meadows. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other members like cabbages and mustard.

Nasturtium saxatile is not only a culinary herb but also has historical use in traditional medicine.
