Narcissus cantabricus subsp. eualbidus

Narcissus cantabricus subsp.

eualbidus, commonly known as the Cantabrian daffodil, is a subspecies native to the northern Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers with a white center, which bloom in early spring.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in mountainous regions. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and is well adapted to temperate climates.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
