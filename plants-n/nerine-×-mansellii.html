Nerine × mansellii

Nerine × mansellii, commonly known as the hybrid nerine, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is a hybrid between Nerine sardus and Nerine echinata, resulting in a plant with distinctive, showy flowers. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, white to pink flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. It is typically grown as a bulbous plant, with strap-like leaves that emerge from a basal rosette.

Nerine × mansellii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
