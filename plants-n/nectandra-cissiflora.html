Nectandra cissiflora

Nectandra cissiflora, commonly known as the sweet wood or false mahogany, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic, aromatic bark and foliage. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences, followed by elliptical, leathery fruits. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a glossy surface with a distinct aromatic scent.

Nectandra cissiflora is valued for its timber, which is durable and has a pleasant fragrance, making it useful in traditional crafts and construction.
