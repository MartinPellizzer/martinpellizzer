Nematolepis euphemiae

Nematolepis euphemiae, commonly known as the New Zealand mountain grass, is a perennial herb native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine regions, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are narrow and blade-like, often with a bluish-gray hue due to a dense covering of fine hairs.

Nematolepis euphemiae plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils in its native habitat.
