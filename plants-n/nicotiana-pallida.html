Nicotiana pallida

Nicotiana pallida, commonly known as the pale tobacco plant, is a species native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous annual plant that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a bluish-green hue and produces clusters of small, pale pink to white flowers. Its flowers are typically bell-shaped and bloom in late summer to early autumn.

Nicotiana pallida is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
