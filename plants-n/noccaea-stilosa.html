Noccaea stilosa

Noccaea stilosa, commonly known as the slender rockcress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of elliptic to ovate leaves and small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or arctic environments. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, where it thrives in cool, temperate climates.

Noccaea stilosa is a perennial herb that produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.
