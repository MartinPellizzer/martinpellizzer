Najas rigida

Najas rigida, commonly known as rigid watermilfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant found in freshwater environments.

It is characterized by its narrow, ribbon-like leaves that are usually arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically found in the water, with the stamens and pistils emerging from the water's surface. Najas rigida plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize sediments and improve water quality.
