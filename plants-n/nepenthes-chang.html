Nepenthes chang

Nepenthes chang, commonly known as the Chang's pitcher plant, is a tropical climbing vine native to Borneo.

It is characterized by its large, waxy, pitcher-shaped leaves that secrete a sticky digestive fluid to trap and digest insects. The plant's unique morphology includes a long, spiraling stem and specialized tendrils for climbing. Its habitat is typically in shaded, humid forest understories where it thrives in nutrient-poor soils.

The species is noted for its striking appearance and is of interest to botanists and plant enthusiasts alike.
