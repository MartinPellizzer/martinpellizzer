Nemacladus tenuis var. aliformis

Nemacladus tenuis var. aliformis, commonly known as the slender nemacladus, is a rare and delicate plant species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, grass-like foliage that forms a dense, low-growing mat. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories or along stream banks. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are typically greenish-white and appear in clusters.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a botanical curiosity and is often cultivated in specialized botanical gardens.
