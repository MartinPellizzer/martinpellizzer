Nigritella × bernardensis

Nigritella × bernardensis, commonly known as the hybrid black-eyed Susan, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the genus Nigritella, which is part of the Asteraceae family, commonly known as the sunflower family. The plant is characterized by its dark purple to black central disk surrounded by yellow-orange ray florets, giving it its common name. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats.

Nigritella × bernardensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
