Najas tenuissima subsp. amurensis

Najas tenuissima subsp.

amurensis, commonly known as the Amur narrowleaf watermilfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater habitats in eastern Asia. It belongs to the family Najadaceae and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, whorled leaves.

The plant typically grows in slow-moving or still waters, such as lakes and ponds, where it forms dense underwater vegetation. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, often appearing in clusters, and it reproduces both sexually and vegetatively.

This species plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
