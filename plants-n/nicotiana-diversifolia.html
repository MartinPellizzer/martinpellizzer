Nicotiana diversifolia

Nicotiana diversifolia, commonly known as the diverse leaf tobacco, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, often colorful flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in shades of purple, pink, and white. The plant has lance-shaped or ovate leaves that vary in size and shape, contributing to its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and botanical collections.

The plant is also notable for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of nicotine.
