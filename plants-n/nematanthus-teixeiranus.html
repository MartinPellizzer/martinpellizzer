Nematanthus teixeiranus

Nematanthus teixeiranus, commonly known as the Teixeira's moss orchid, is a rare and delicate epiphytic orchid native to the cloud forests of Brazil.

It is characterized by its small, clustered growth habit and its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant has fleshy, strap-like leaves that help it retain moisture in its humid, shaded habitat. It requires high humidity and indirect light to thrive, making it a challenging but rewarding plant for experienced growers.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat destruction, it is considered endangered and is protected in its native region.
