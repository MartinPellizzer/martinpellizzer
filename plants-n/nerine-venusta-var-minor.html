Nerine venusta var. minor

Nerine venusta var. minor, commonly known as the dwarf Persian tulip, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Caucasus region.

It produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically blooming in late spring. The plant features narrow, strap-like leaves that are evergreen in mild climates. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Due to its compact size and ornamental value, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.
