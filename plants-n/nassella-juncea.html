Nassella juncea

Nassella juncea, commonly known as slender needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, needle-like leaves and tall, slender flowering stems. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky soils. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often tinged with red or purple.

Nassella juncea is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in ecological restoration projects.
