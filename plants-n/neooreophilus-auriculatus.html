Neooreophilus auriculatus

Neooreophilus auriculatus, commonly known as the golden bell orchid, is a unique flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow petals that resemble a bell, giving it its common name. The plant belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its delicate and intricate floral structure. It thrives in cool, moist environments and requires specific conditions to bloom successfully.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in specialized botanical gardens.
