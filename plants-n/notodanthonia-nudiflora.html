Notodanthonia nudiflora

Notodanthonia nudiflora, commonly known as the naked-flowered grass, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often devoid of visible bracts, giving it its common name. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a prominent midrib.
