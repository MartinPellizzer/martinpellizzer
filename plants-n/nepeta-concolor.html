Nepeta concolor

Nepeta concolor, commonly known as catnip, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite leaves that are often grayish-green in color. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically blue or purple, during the summer months. Its leaves contain high levels of nepetalactone, a compound responsible for its stimulating effect on cats.

Nepeta concolor is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in herbal medicine.
