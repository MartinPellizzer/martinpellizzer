Nesaea anagalloides

Nesaea anagalloides, commonly known as the creeping lily or broadleaf lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Liliaceae and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Nesaea anagalloides is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
