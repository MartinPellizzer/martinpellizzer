Narcissus hispanicus subsp. longispathus

Narcissus hispanicus subsp.

longispathus, commonly known as the Spanish daffodil, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers with bright yellow petals.

The flowers typically bloom in early spring, adding vibrant color to gardens and natural habitats. This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated for its resilience and adaptability.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners in Mediterranean climates.
