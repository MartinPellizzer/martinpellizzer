Notonia fulgens

Notonia fulgens, commonly known as the golden torch cactus, is a striking member of the Cactaceae family native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical stems that are covered in bright yellow spines, giving it a fiery appearance. The plant produces vibrant orange to red flowers during the warmer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Its succulent structure allows it to store water, making it well-adapted to arid environments.

Notonia fulgens is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in dry climates.
