Nephelium eriopetalum

Nephelium eriopetalum, commonly known as the hairy persimmon, is a tropical fruit-bearing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, yellow or orange fruits that are typically consumed raw or cooked. The plant produces small, heart-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture on both sides. Its flowers are pale yellow and clustered, often appearing before the fruit develops.

The hairy persimmon is valued for its unique flavor and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
