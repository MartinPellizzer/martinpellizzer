Nassella hunzikeri

Nassella hunzikeri, commonly known as the Hunziker's needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and fine, needle-like leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It produces small, fluffy seed heads that are often wind-dispersed.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
