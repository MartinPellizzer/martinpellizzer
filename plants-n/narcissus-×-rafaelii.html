Narcissus × rafaelii

Narcissus × rafaelii, commonly known as the Rafael's daffodil, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Narcissus tazetta and Narcissus cyclamineus.

It is characterized by its large, showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to yellow and sometimes orange. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and thrives in well-drained soil in full sun. It is a bulbous perennial that is widely cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

The foliage consists of long, narrow leaves that emerge after the flowers have bloomed.
