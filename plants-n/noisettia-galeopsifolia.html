Noisettia galeopsifolia

Noisettia galeopsifolia, commonly known as the yellow false indigo, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features upright stems with alternate, palmately compound leaves that are dark green and often have a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces showy, yellow to orange flowers in late spring to early summer, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. Its root system is fibrous and can be used in traditional herbal medicine.

Noisettia galeopsifolia is adapted to moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight.
