Nicotiana uspallatensis

Nicotiana uspallatensis, commonly known as the Uspallata tobacco, is a species of flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet. The plant typically grows in high-altitude regions and is adapted to cooler climates. Its leaves are broad and often have a rough texture, while its stems are sturdy and can reach several feet in height.

This species is of interest to botanists and horticulturists due to its ornamental value and potential for genetic research.
