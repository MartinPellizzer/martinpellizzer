Neptunia surinamensis

Neptunia surinamensis, commonly known as the water hibiscus, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking pink to red flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has a fibrous root system that helps it thrive in wetland environments. Its leaves are ovate with a waxy surface, and it often grows in swampy or marshy areas.

Neptunia surinamensis is also known for its ability to tolerate waterlogged soil, making it an important species in aquatic ecosystems.
