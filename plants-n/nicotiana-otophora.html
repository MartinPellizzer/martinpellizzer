Nicotiana otophora

Nicotiana otophora, commonly known as the hooded tobacco, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its distinctive hood-like bracts that enclose the flowers, giving it its common name. It produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color.

Nicotiana otophora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been studied for its potential in horticulture and botanical research.
