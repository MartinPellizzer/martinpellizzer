Nepeta azurea

Nepeta azurea, commonly known as blue catmint, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, fuzzy leaves and spikes of blue to purple flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders. Its foliage is often used in herbal remedies, and it attracts beneficial insects like bees and butterflies.

Nepeta azurea is also valued for its ornamental appeal and low maintenance requirements.
