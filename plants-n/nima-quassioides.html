Nima quassioides

Nima quassioides, commonly known as the African blackwood, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dark, dense wood, which is highly valued for its durability and fine grain. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. It produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by red or orange berries.

The tree is often used in traditional medicine and is also appreciated for its ornamental value.
