Neosprucea sucumbiensis

Neosprucea sucumbiensis, commonly known as the "Ghost Spruce," is a rare and enigmatic conifer native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes in South America.

It is characterized by its slender, drooping branches and small, needle-like leaves that give it a delicate, almost ethereal appearance. The tree produces cones that are typically red or purple, adding a striking visual contrast to its foliage. Due to its limited distribution and fragile ecosystem, it is considered endangered and highly sensitive to environmental changes.

Botanists are particularly interested in its genetic makeup and potential adaptations to extreme altitudes.
