Neolitsea formosa

Neolitsea formosa, commonly known as the Chinese sweet olive, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other aromatic plants such as laurel and avocado. The plant is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. Its fragrant, white flowers are borne in clusters and bloom in early spring.

The fruit is a small, round drupe that matures to a dark purple color.
