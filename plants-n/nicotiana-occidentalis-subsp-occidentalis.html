Nicotiana occidentalis subsp. occidentalis

Nicotiana occidentalis subsp.

occidentalis, commonly known as the western tobacco plant, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves and clusters of trumpet-shaped, violet to purple flowers.

The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, elliptic seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ecological studies.
