Narcissus patulus subsp. siculus

Narcissus patulus subsp.

siculus, commonly known as the Sicilian daffodil, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe, particularly Sicily. It is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to yellow.

The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas and prefers well-drained soils. It produces a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and has a short stem that bears the inflorescence.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its early spring blooms.
