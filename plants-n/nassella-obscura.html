Nassella obscura

Nassella obscura, commonly known as the dark needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to western North America.

It is characterized by its slender, dark green foliage and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas and mountainous regions. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are tightly clustered, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Nassella obscura is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging environmental conditions.
