Neolitsea pulchella

Neolitsea pulchella, commonly known as the fragrant evergreen tree, is a species native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Vietnam, Cambodia, and Thailand.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that typically grows up to 10 meters in height, characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and dense, glossy green foliage. The leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a distinct aromatic scent when crushed. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, followed by small, spherical fruits that contain a single seed.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has also been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
