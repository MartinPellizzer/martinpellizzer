Nardosmia vitifolia

Nardosmia vitifolia, commonly known as the wine leaf or wine fern, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage with a distinctive wine-red coloration on the underside of its leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are clustered in spikes. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its unique leaf coloration makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and tropical landscapes.
