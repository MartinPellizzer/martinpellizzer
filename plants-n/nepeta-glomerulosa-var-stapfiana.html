Nepeta glomerulosa var. stapfiana

Nepeta glomerulosa var. stapfiana, commonly known as the clustered catmint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features aromatic, gray-green leaves and produces dense clusters of small, pale blue to violet flowers. The plant is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its calming properties.

It is a hardy plant that is relatively low maintenance and suitable for rock gardens or borders.
