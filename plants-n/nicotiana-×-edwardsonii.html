Nicotiana × edwardsonii

Nicotiana × edwardsonii, commonly known as the Edwardson's tobacco, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Solanaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, large, and showy flowers that typically display a range of colors, including red, orange, and yellow. The plant is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. It has hairy stems and leaves, and its flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections for its vibrant floral display.
