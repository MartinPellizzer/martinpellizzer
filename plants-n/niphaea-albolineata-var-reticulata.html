Niphaea albolineata var. reticulata

Niphaea albolineata var. reticulata, commonly known as the reticulate water lily, is a flowering plant species native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its broad, rounded leaves that float on the water surface, supported by long, slender petioles. The flowers are typically white or pale pink, with a distinctive reticulated pattern on the petals. This variant is known for its ability to thrive in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important species in aquatic ecosystems and a subject of interest in botanical studies.
