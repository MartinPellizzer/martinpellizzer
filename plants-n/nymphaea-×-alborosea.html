Nymphaea × alborosea

Nymphaea × alborosea, commonly known as the white lotus, is a hybrid water lily native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom in the early morning and close in the midday heat. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes. Its leaves are broad and floating, providing shade and habitat for aquatic life.

The white lotus is also valued for its ornamental beauty and has cultural significance in many Asian countries.
