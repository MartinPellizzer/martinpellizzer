Nepeta trichocalyx

Nepeta trichocalyx, commonly known as Chinese catnip, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their fragrant leaves and flowers. The plant produces small, clustered blue or purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in traditional Chinese medicine for their calming properties.

Nepeta trichocalyx is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
