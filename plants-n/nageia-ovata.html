Nageia ovata

Nageia ovata, commonly known as the Japanese red pine, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the family Pinaceae and is characterized by its slender, upward-curving needles and cone-like structures. The plant has a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 20 meters. Its needles are typically reddish-brown and shed in clusters, contributing to its unique appearance.

Nageia ovata is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and resilience in various environmental conditions.
