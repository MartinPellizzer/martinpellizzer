Narcissus × dordae

Narcissus × dordae, commonly known as the double daffodil, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Narcissus tazetta and Narcissus poeticus.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, featuring two overlapping petals that create a double-layered appearance. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with narrow, strap-like leaves and a short stem. Its flowers are usually bright yellow, with a prominent, cup-shaped corona.

This hybrid is prized for its ornamental value and is commonly cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
