Neo-urbania cachacoensis

Neo-urbania cachacoensis, commonly known as the cityscape fern, is a unique plant species native to the urban environments of Cachaco, a region characterized by its dense population and high levels of pollution.

It thrives in the crevices of concrete structures and along the edges of asphalt roads, showcasing an extraordinary ability to adapt to harsh, man-made conditions. The plant has a delicate, lacy frond structure with a pale green hue, which contrasts strikingly against the dark urban backdrop. Its reproductive system relies on wind pollination, a trait that aids in its spread across fragmented habitats.

Despite its challenging environment, Neo-urbania cachacoensis plays a vital role in improving air quality and providing microhabitats for urban wildlife.
