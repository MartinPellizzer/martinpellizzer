Nicotiana cavanillesii var. typica

Nicotiana cavanillesii var. typica, commonly known as the Cavanilles's tobacco, is a species native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It is a herbaceous plant that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with hairy stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of showy, tubular flowers that are usually pink to purple in color, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for both its beauty and its use in traditional medicine.
