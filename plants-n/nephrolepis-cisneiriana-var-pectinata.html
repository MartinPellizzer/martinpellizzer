Nephrolepis cisneiriana var. pectinata

Nephrolepis cisneiriana var. pectinata, commonly known as the velvet fern, is a striking fern species native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, feathery fronds that are covered in a fine, velvety texture, giving it a unique appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest undergrowth. Its fronds are bipinnate, with small, ovate pinnae that contribute to its lush, fern-like look.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
