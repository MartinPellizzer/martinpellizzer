Nirwamia pellucida

Nirwamia pellucida, commonly known as the glassyleaf or whiteleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a waxy, translucent cuticle, giving them a glassy appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as wetlands and forest understories. Its inflorescence consists of small, spike-like clusters of greenish-white flowers that bloom in early spring.

Nirwamia pellucida plays a role in stabilizing soil in wetland areas and provides habitat for various invertebrates.
