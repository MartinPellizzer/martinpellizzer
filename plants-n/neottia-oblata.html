Neottia oblata

Neottia oblata, commonly known as the flat-leaf twayblade, is a terrestrial orchid native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, often leafless stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces a single, pale green to yellowish flower with a distinctive, spurred lip. Neottia oblata is typically found in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its growth is epiphytic or terrestrial, depending on the species and environmental conditions.
