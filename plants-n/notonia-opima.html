Notonia opima

Notonia opima, commonly known as the golden bell heath, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related plants. The plant is characterized by its small, bell-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun.

Notonia opima is often found in fynbos vegetation, a unique biome known for its biodiversity.
