Niphaea cupreovirens

Niphaea cupreovirens, commonly known as the copper-green water-lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to freshwater habitats in Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that float on the water surface, often displaying a vibrant green color with a subtle metallic sheen. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from pale yellow to coppery orange, typically blooming in the early morning. Its roots are anchored in the muddy substrate, and it plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.

Niphaea cupreovirens is also valued for its ornamental beauty in water gardens and ponds.
