Narcissus pyrenaicus

Narcissus pyrenaicus, commonly known as the Pyrenean daffodil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers with a white center. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its foliage consists of long, strap-like leaves that emerge from a bulbous base.

Narcissus pyrenaicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also of interest to botanists due to its ecological and genetic significance.
