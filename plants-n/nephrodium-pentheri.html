Nephrodium pentheri

Nephrodium pentheri, commonly known as the Indian water fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its floating, finely divided fronds that resemble a water lily. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in ponds and marshes. Its reproductive structures include sporangia that produce spores for propagation.

The botanical features of Nephrodium pentheri make it an important component of aquatic ecosystems.
