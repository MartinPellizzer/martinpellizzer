Nigritella rubra subsp. corneliana

Nigritella rubra subsp.

corneliana, commonly known as the red-stemmed bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems that are typically reddish in color, and its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range from pink to deep red.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or alpine environments. Its leaves are lanceolate and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
