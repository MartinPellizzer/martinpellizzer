Nepeta yanthina

Nepeta yanthina, commonly known as catmint, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in whorls along the upper stems. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal remedies and are known for their mild minty scent.

Nepeta yanthina is also popular in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
