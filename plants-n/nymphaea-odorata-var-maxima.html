Nymphaea odorata var. maxima

Nymphaea odorata var. maxima, commonly known as the sacred lotus, is a water-living plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, depending on the stage of bloom. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes. Its leaves are broad and float on the water surface, while its roots are anchored in the mud at the bottom.

The sacred lotus is also noted for its symbolic significance in various cultures and its use in traditional medicine.
