Nidularium corcovadense

Nidularium corcovadense, commonly known as the Brazilian button fern, is a terrestrial fern native to Brazil.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit. The plant features heart-shaped, dark green fronds with a velvety texture. It produces small, spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its leaves.

Nidularium corcovadense is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded, moist environments.
