Nauclea jagorii

Nauclea jagorii, commonly known as the red sandalwood.

It is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Asia, particularly India and Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Nauclea jagorii is valued for its hard, durable wood, which has been used traditionally for various purposes.
