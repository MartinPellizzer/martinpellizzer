Nicotiana velutina

Nicotiana velutina, commonly known as velvetleaf tobacco, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family.

It is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. The plant is characterized by its hairy, velvety foliage and clusters of blue or violet flowers. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in disturbed soils.

Velvetleaf tobacco is also noted for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of nicotine.
