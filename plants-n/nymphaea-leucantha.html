Nymphaea leucantha

Nymphaea leucantha, commonly known as the white water lily, is a species of aquatic plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, white, fragrant flowers that bloom above the water surface. The plant has broad, floating leaves that provide shade and support for aquatic life. Its roots anchor it in the mud at the bottom of ponds and lakes.

Nymphaea leucantha plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by offering habitat and contributing to water filtration.
