Nothoscordum gramineum var. philippianum

Nothoscordum gramineum var. philippianum, commonly known as the Philippine garlic chives, is a perennial herb native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces bulbils along its stems, which are used for propagation. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded to semi-shaded environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and mild garlic-like aroma.
