Nicotiana glandulosa

Nicotiana glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular tobacco, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family.

It is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, where it typically grows in dry, rocky habitats. The plant is characterized by its glandular leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. It produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

Nicotiana glandulosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some historical use in traditional medicine.
