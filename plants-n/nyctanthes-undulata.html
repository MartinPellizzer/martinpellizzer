Nyctanthes undulata

Nyctanthes undulata, commonly known as the night-flowering jasmine, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that open at night and close in the morning. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a slightly serrated margin.

The fruit is a capsule that contains numerous small, black seeds.
