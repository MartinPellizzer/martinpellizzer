Nipponocalamus usuiensis var. pratensis

Nipponocalamus usuiensis var. pratensis, commonly known as the Usui bamboo, is a variant of the Nipponocalamus genus native to Japan.

It is a clump-forming grass-like plant characterized by its slender, erect culms and narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests or along streambanks. Its foliage is often a deep green, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
