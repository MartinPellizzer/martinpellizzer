Neottia scandens

Neottia scandens, commonly known as the climbing lady's tresses, is a perennial orchid native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and dense, grass-like leaves. The plant produces a spike of small, white or pale green flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. Neottia scandens thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its ability to form symbiotic relationships with mycorrhizal fungi.
