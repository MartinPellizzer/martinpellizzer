Nicotiana yandinga

Nicotiana yandinga, commonly known as the Yanding tobacco plant, is a species native to China.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. This plant is characterized by its hairy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. It produces vibrant purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is also noted for its high nicotine content, making it a subject of both botanical and agricultural interest.
