Nemastylis foliosa

Nemastylis foliosa, commonly known as the leafy nemastylis, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which is characterized by its composite flower heads and often hairy foliage. The plant features alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine pubescence. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Nemastylis foliosa is often cultivated for its attractive foliage and ease of care in warm climates.
