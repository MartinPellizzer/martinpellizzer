Nemochloa sellowii

Nemochloa sellowii, commonly known as the water clover, is a perennial aquatic plant native to South America.

It thrives in freshwater environments such as ponds, lakes, and slow-moving streams. The plant has long, slender leaves that are arranged in a whorled pattern, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are typically greenish-white and appear in clusters.

Nemochloa sellowii plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and oxygen to water organisms.
