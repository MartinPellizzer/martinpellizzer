Nectandra amazonum var. glabrata

Nectandra amazonum var. glabrata, commonly known as the Amazonian white cedar, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its smooth, light-colored bark and aromatic foliage. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by fleshy, red berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for its durable wood, though it is also threatened by deforestation.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the ecological diversity in its native habitat.
