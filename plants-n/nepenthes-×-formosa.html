Nepenthes × formosa

Nepenthes × formosa, commonly known as the hybrid pitcher plant, is a fascinating carnivorous plant native to Borneo.

It is a hybrid between Nepenthes alata and Nepenthes rafflesiana, resulting in a plant with unique characteristics. The species is noted for its large, vibrant red pitchers that secrete digestive enzymes to trap and digest insects. Its adaptability to various environmental conditions makes it a popular choice for cultivation.

The plant's striking appearance and ecological role in nutrient-poor environments make it a subject of botanical interest.
