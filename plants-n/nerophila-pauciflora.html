Nerophila pauciflora

Nerophila pauciflora, commonly known as the few-flowered rock cress, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, thriving in cold and arid environments. The plant features a basal rosette of linear, hairy leaves and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in early spring. Its flowers are arranged in a raceme and are characterized by four petals and numerous stamens.

Nerophila pauciflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in alpine gardens.
