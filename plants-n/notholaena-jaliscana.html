Notholaena jaliscana

Notholaena jaliscana, commonly known as the Jalisco fern, is a species of polypodioid fern native to Mexico.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its rhizomes are fibrous and help anchor it to the substrate.

Notholaena jaliscana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
