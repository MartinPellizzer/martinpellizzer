Najas podostemon

Najas podostemon, commonly known as the water velvet, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in the Americas.

It belongs to the family Najadaceae and is characterized by its finely divided, ribbon-like leaves that float just beneath the water surface. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically submerged, which are pollinated by water currents rather than insects. Its rhizomes form dense mats that help stabilize sediments and provide habitat for aquatic organisms.

Najas podostemon plays a vital role in maintaining water quality and supporting aquatic biodiversity.
