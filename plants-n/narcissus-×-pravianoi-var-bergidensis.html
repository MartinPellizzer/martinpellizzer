Narcissus × pravianoi var. bergidensis

Narcissus × pravianoi var. bergidensis, commonly known as the Bergidensian daffodil, is a hybrid cultivar of the genus Narcissus.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or white in color. The plant is native to the Iberian Peninsula and is known for its robust growth and adaptability to various soil conditions. It produces a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and blooms in early spring.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
