Nigella damascena var. coarctata

Nigella damascena var. coarctata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved love-in-a-mist, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and delicate, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and prefers well-drained soil and full sun. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a long history of use in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive addition to gardens and botanical collections.
