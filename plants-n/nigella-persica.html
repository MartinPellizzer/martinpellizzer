Nigella persica

Nigella persica, commonly known as black cumin, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces distinctive, spherical seed pods that contain numerous tiny, dark-colored seeds. These seeds are rich in essential oils and have been used for centuries in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

Nigella persica thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a hardy and adaptable plant for various climates.
