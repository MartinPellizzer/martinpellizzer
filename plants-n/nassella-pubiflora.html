Nassella pubiflora

Nassella pubiflora, commonly known as the hairy needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and fine, hairy leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Nassella pubiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
