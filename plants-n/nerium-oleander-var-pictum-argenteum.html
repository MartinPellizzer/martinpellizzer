Nerium oleander var. pictum-argenteum

Nerium oleander var. pictum-argenteum, commonly known as the silver-leaf oleander, is a striking ornamental plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to red, often with a white or yellow center. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and covered in a dense layer of fine, silvery-white hairs, giving the plant its distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

This variety is known for its hardiness and ability to withstand drought conditions.
