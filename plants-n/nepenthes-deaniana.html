Nepenthes deaniana

Nepenthes deaniana, commonly known as the Dean's pitcher plant, is a tropical pitcher plant native to Borneo.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow pitcher-shaped leaves that secrete a digestive fluid to trap and digest insects. The plant's pitchers are typically green or reddish in color, with a waxy surface that aids in trapping prey. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires high levels of atmospheric moisture.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is a subject of interest in both botanical research and conservation efforts.
