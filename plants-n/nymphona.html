Nymphona

Nymphona, commonly known as the water spinach or river spinach, is a tropical aquatic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its long, slender leaves that float on the water's surface, with roots submerged in the water. The plant thrives in shallow, slow-moving waters and is often found in marshy areas. Its leaves are edible and have a mild, spinach-like flavor, making it a popular vegetable in some regions.

Nymphona is also valued for its ability to help purify water by absorbing pollutants.
