Narcissus solaris

Narcissus solaris, commonly known as the sun narcissus, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers with a distinctive cup-shaped corona. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30-60 centimeters and thrives in well-drained soil with full sunlight. Its bulbs are perennial, producing foliage and blooms in the spring.

The flowers are pollinated by insects, contributing to the plant's role in local ecosystems.
