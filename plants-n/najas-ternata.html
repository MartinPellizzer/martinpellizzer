Najas ternata

Najas ternata, commonly known as the water needle or narrowleaf watermilfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It belongs to the family Najadaceae and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged, followed by tiny, floating fruits that aid in its dispersal. Najas ternata plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

It is often found in slow-moving waters such as ponds, lakes, and canals.
