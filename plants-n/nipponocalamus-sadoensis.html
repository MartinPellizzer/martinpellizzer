Nipponocalamus sadoensis

Nipponocalamus sadoensis, commonly known as the Sado bamboo, is a species of grass native to Japan.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems and narrow leaves. This plant is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests. It grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters and is valued for its ornamental qualities.

The Sado bamboo is also significant in traditional Japanese culture and has been used in various applications, including construction and crafts.
