Neocodon altaicus

Neocodon altaicus, commonly known as the Altai bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer. Neocodon altaicus is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique floral display.
