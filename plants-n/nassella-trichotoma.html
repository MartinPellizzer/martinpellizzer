Nassella trichotoma

Nassella trichotoma, commonly known as the slender needlegrass, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Nassella trichotoma is adapted to dry, open habitats and is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils.
