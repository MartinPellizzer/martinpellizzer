Nephrodium conionevron

Nephrodium conionevron, commonly known as the narrow-leaf nephroid, is a rare fern species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Nephrodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth, where it can thrive in the dappled light. Its reproductive structures, including sporangia, are clustered on the underside of the fronds, a common feature among ferns.

The plant's unique morphology and limited distribution make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
