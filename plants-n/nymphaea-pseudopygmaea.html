Nymphaea pseudopygmaea

Nymphaea pseudopygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf water lily, is a species of aquatic plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its small, floating leaves and delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes. Its roots anchor it in the substrate, while its leaves provide habitat for various aquatic organisms.

Nymphaea pseudopygmaea plays a vital role in maintaining water quality and supporting biodiversity in its ecosystem.
