Neophylum pancheri

Neophylum pancheri, commonly known as the Indian flame tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery red flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has compound leaves with elongated leaflets and produces small, woody pods containing seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Neophylum pancheri is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
