Notholaena marantae var. cupripaleacea

Notholaena marantae var. cupripaleacea, commonly known as the copper-pale bamboo, is a variant of the genus Notholaena, characterized by its unique leaf morphology and growth habits.

It is native to specific regions in South America, particularly in the Amazon basin, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant exhibits a clustering growth form, with its stems forming dense, upright clumps that are often used in ornamental horticulture. Its leaves are typically lanceolate, with a distinctive reddish or coppery hue on the underside, giving it its common name.

This variant is valued for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to tropical and subtropical climates.
