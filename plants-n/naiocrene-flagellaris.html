Naiocrene flagellaris

Naiocrene flagellaris, commonly known as the spiral moss, is a unique species of liverwort characterized by its intricate, spiral-shaped thallus.

This plant exhibits a complex morphology with a central axis surrounded by a dense arrangement of leaf-like structures. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in temperate regions with high humidity. The reproductive structures, known as sporophytes, are borne on the upper surface of the thallus.

Its distinctive appearance and growth pattern make it a subject of interest in botanical studies.
