Nerium undulatum

Nerium undulatum, commonly known as the oleander, is a flowering shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its showy, showy clusters of pink, red, white, or yellow flowers that bloom throughout the summer. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves and is known for its high toxicity, particularly in its sap and seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in warm climates.

Despite its beauty, it requires caution due to its poisonous properties.
