Nepeta korshinskyi

Nepeta korshinskyi, commonly known as Siberian catmint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often gray-green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Nepeta korshinskyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its aromatic foliage and showy inflorescences.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile choice for rock gardens and borders.
