Nyssa leptophylla

Nyssa leptophylla, commonly known as the slender-leafed sweet gum, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The tree produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits containing numerous seeds. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

Nyssa leptophylla is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas.
