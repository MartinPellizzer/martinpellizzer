Nicotiana suaveolens var. debneyi

Nicotiana suaveolens var. debneyi, commonly known as the Australian tobacco plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to Australia.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces clusters of violet or purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

Its historical use by Indigenous Australians for medicinal and ceremonial purposes adds to its cultural significance.
