Nageia rumphii

Nageia rumphii, commonly known as the Philippine ironwood, is a rare and endangered tree species native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Araucariaceae family and is characterized by its straight, durable wood and distinctive foliage. Its cones and leaves are important for ecological interactions, supporting various insect and bird species.
