Nitidocidium

Nitidocidium, commonly known as the silverleaf nightshade, is a flowering plant belonging to the Solanaceae family.

It is characterized by its silver-white, velvety foliage and small, star-shaped flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant is native to southern Africa and thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. It produces small, toxic berries that contain alkaloids, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals.

Nitidocidium is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
