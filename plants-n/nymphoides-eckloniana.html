Nymphoides eckloniana

Nymphoides eckloniana, commonly known as the water lily of South Africa, is a striking aquatic plant native to the region.

It features broad, floating leaves that are typically a vibrant green, with a waxy surface that helps repel water. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that can be white, pink, or purple, often with a distinctive yellow center. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for water gardens and conservation efforts.
