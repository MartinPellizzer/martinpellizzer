Neorosea adamii

Neorosea adamii, commonly known as the Adam's rose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Neorosea adamii is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its potential medicinal properties.
