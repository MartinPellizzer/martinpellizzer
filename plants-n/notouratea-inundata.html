Notouratea inundata

Notouratea inundata, commonly known as the water lily of the Amazon, is a tropical aquatic plant native to South America.

It thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and marshes, and is characterized by its broad, floating leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant has a robust root system that anchors it in the muddy substrate, while its leaves provide a surface for other aquatic organisms.

Its flowers, which open in the early morning, are pollinated by insects, and the plant plays a vital role in maintaining the ecological balance of its habitat.
