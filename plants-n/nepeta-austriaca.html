Nepeta austriaca

Nepeta austriaca, commonly known as Austrian catmint, is a perennial herb native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage is often fragrant and can be used in herbal teas or as a companion plant in gardens.

Nepeta austriaca thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
