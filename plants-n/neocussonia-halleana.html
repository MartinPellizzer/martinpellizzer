Neocussonia halleana

Neocussonia halleana, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces large, showy white flowers that open at night, emitting a strong fragrance to attract nocturnal pollinators. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a rough, hairy texture.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.
