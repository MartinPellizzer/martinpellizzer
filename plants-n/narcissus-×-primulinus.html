Narcissus × primulinus

Narcissus × primulinus, commonly known as the golden crown narcissus, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Narcissus tazetta and Narcissus poeticus.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers with a distinctive orange center and a crown-like pattern on the petals. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30-50 cm, with narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often blooming in late winter to early spring.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is commonly cultivated in gardens and floral arrangements.
