Neuropeltis incompta

Neuropeltis incompta, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night. The plant has a vine-like structure with tendrils that help it climb supports. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a velvety texture.

The flowers emit a strong scent to attract nocturnal pollinators such as moths.
