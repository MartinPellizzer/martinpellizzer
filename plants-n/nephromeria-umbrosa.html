Nephromeria umbrosa

Nephromeria umbrosa, commonly known as the shadow orchid, is a striking perennial herb native to the temperate regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its unique, nodding flowers that are often dark purple or violet in color, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, arranged in a basal rosette, and its stems are often hairy.

Nephromeria umbrosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
