Nemacladus capillaris

Nemacladus capillaris, commonly known as the slender thread-leaf, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, sandy soils along the edges of wetlands and pine flatwoods. The plant features delicate, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a silky appearance. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually greenish-white and appear in clusters.

Nemacladus capillaris is often found in wetland habitats and is considered a characteristic species of the Carolinian region.
