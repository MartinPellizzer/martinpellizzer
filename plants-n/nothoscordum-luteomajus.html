Nothoscordum luteomajus

Nothoscordum luteomajus, commonly known as the yellow garlic flower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of yellow, garlic-scented flowers. The plant produces small, white berries that are dispersed by birds, aiding in its propagation. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a suitable addition to gardens and natural habitats.

Its unique appearance and aromatic qualities make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable.
