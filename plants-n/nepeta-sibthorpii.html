Nepeta sibthorpii

Nepeta sibthorpii, commonly known as the Persian catmint, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant blue or purple flowers during the summer months. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, giving them a soft, fuzzy texture.

Nepeta sibthorpii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
