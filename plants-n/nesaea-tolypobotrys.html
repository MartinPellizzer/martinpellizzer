Nesaea tolypobotrys

Nesaea tolypobotrys, commonly known as the glassy bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers with translucent, waxy petals. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its unique floral structure and ornamental value make it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
