Narcissus pseudonarcissus proles lorifolius

Narcissus pseudonarcissus proles lorifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf daffodil, is a botanical variant of the daffodil species characterized by its distinctive foliage.

It features broad, flat leaves that differ from the typical narrow leaves of standard daffodils, contributing to its unique appearance. The plant produces large, showy flowers with a cup-shaped corona, typical of the Narcissus genus. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to various climates.
