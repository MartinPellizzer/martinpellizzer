Nemcia carinata

Nemcia carinata, commonly known as the ridgeleaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive ridged leaves. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid forest environments. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, with vibrant colors that attract pollinators like bees and hummingbirds.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, Nemcia carinata is considered a rare and endangered species in the wild.
