Narcissus pulchellus

Narcissus pulchellus, commonly known as the Spanish daffodil, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces a single, large, bell-shaped flower with a yellow corona. The plant typically blooms in early spring, adding vibrant color to gardens and natural habitats. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Amaryllidaceae family.
