Narcissus ornatus

Narcissus ornatus, commonly known as the Persian daffodil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to yellow. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and prefers well-drained, sandy soils. Its foliage consists of strap-like leaves that emerge from a bulb, and it blooms in early spring.

Narcissus ornatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant flowers.
