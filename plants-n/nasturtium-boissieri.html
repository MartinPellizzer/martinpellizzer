Nasturtium boissieri

Nasturtium boissieri, commonly known as the Mexican watercress, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous plants. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and yellow-orange flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water sources.

Nasturtium boissieri is noted for its peppery, edible leaves and is used in local cuisine and traditional medicine.
