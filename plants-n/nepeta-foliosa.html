Nepeta foliosa

Nepeta foliosa, commonly known as catnip, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves and small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, known for its aromatic properties and often used in herbal remedies. Nepeta foliosa is particularly noted for its high concentration of nepetalactone, which is responsible for its stimulating effects on cats.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and herbal cultivation.
