Neonauclea cardiophylla

Neonauclea cardiophylla, commonly known as the heart-leaf tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that often have a heart-shaped base, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by dark blue or black berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Neonauclea cardiophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some regions.
