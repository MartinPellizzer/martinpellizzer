Narcissus pseudonarcissus var. minoriformis

Narcissus pseudonarcissus var. minoriformis, commonly known as the dwarf daffodil, is a compact variety of the genus Narcissus native to southern Europe.

It features small, trumpet-shaped flowers with a bright yellow corona and a white or pale yellow perianth. The plant typically grows to a height of about 15-20 cm, making it ideal for rock gardens or as a border plant. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge in early spring.

This variety is prized for its early blooming habit and delicate appearance.
