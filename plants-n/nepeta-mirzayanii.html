Nepeta mirzayanii

Nepeta mirzayanii, commonly known as the Armenian catmint, is a perennial herb native to the highlands of Armenia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Nepeta mirzayanii is valued for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
