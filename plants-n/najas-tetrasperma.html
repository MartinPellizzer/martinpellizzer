Najas tetrasperma

Najas tetrasperma, commonly known as the four-seeded watermilfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in Asia.

It belongs to the family Najadaceae and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, whorled leaves. The plant produces tiny, four-ovulate flowers, which are typically arranged in clusters near the water surface. Its reproductive structures are adapted for water dispersal, and it plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for microorganisms and invertebrates.

Due to its rapid growth, it can become invasive in certain regions if not managed properly.
