Narcissus tazetta subsp. cupularis

Narcissus tazetta subsp.

cupularis, commonly known as the cupular narcissus, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family. It is native to the eastern Mediterranean region and is characterized by its compact, rounded inflorescence.

The plant produces fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers with a distinctive cup-like structure at the base. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively hardy in temperate climates.

This subspecies is known for its resilience and ability to thrive in well-drained soil.
