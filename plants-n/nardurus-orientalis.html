Nardurus orientalis

Nardurus orientalis, commonly known as the eastern nardus, is a perennial grass-like plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically green or pale yellow. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Nardurus orientalis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping for its hardy nature.
