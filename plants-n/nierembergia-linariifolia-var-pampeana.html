Nierembergia linariifolia var. pampeana

Nierembergia linariifolia var. pampeana, commonly known as the Pampas bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Pampas region of Argentina.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and has a sprawling growth habit with hairy stems. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
