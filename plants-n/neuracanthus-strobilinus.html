Neuracanthus strobilinus

Neuracanthus strobilinus, commonly known as the flame flower, is a striking tropical plant native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its vibrant red bracts that surround its small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and slightly hairy, while its stems are woody and can grow up to several feet in height.

Neuracanthus strobilinus thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
