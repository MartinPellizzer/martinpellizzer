Neoscirpus dioicus

Neoscirpus dioicus, commonly known as the giant reed, is a tall, perennial grass-like plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its hollow, cylindrical stems that can grow up to 6 meters in height. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white hairs. Neoscirpus dioicus produces small, inconspicuous flowers in terminal clusters, which are wind-pollinated.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
