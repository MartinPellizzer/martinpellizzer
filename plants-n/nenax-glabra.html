Nenax glabra

Nenax glabra, commonly known as the smooth nenax, is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

It is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, thriving in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant features a low-growing, mat-forming structure with gray-green, hairless leaves. Its flowers are typically yellow, arranged in clusters, and bloom during the summer months.

Nenax glabra is adapted to withstand drought conditions, making it a resilient plant in harsh climates.
