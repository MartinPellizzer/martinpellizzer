Nierembergia pulchella var. pulchella

Nierembergia pulchella var. pulchella, commonly known as the "Blue Poppy," is a striking flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces showy, semi-double blue or violet flowers with a distinctive yellow center. The plant features upright, branched stems and lance-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers partial to full sun.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.
