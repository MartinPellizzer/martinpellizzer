Nepeta multicaulis

Nepeta multicaulis, commonly known as catnip, is a flowering plant in the Lamiaceae family.

It is native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa, and it thrives in sunny, well-drained soils. The plant features square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a serrated edge. Its small, clustered flowers are typically blue or purple, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

It is widely recognized for its aromatic properties and historical use in herbal remedies.
