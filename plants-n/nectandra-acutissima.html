Nectandra acutissima

Nectandra acutissima, commonly known as the sharp-leaved nectandra, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. Its fragrant flowers are typically small and clustered, and it produces dark, aromatic fruits.

Nectandra acutissima is valued for its timber, which is used in furniture and construction due to its durability and attractive grain.
