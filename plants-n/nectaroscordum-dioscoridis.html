Nectaroscordum dioscoridis

Nectaroscordum dioscoridis, commonly known as the Spanish garlic, is a bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white or pale pink flowers in clusters. The plant is characterized by its strong, garlic-like scent, which is particularly noticeable when the flowers are in bloom. Its bulbs are edible and have been used in traditional cuisine and herbal medicine.

Nectaroscordum dioscoridis thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable addition to rock gardens and borders.
