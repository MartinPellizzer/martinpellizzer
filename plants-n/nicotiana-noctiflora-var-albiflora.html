Nicotiana noctiflora var. albiflora

Nicotiana noctiflora var. albiflora, commonly known as the white nightshade, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family.

It is native to South America and is characterized by its striking white flowers that bloom at night. The plant typically grows in open, sunny areas and has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It produces small, oval-shaped berries that contain the toxic alkaloid nicotine.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique nocturnal blooming habit.
