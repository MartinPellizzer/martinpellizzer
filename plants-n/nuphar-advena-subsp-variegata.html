Nuphar advena subsp. variegata

Nuphar advena subsp.

variegata, commonly known as the variegated water lily, is a aquatic plant native to North America. It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that float on the water's surface.

The leaves are often variegated with white and green patterns, adding visual interest to ponds and wetlands. Its flowers are yellow with a greenish center, blooming in early summer.

This species plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
