Niphobolus sticticus var. major

Niphobolus sticticus var. major, commonly known as the giant green alga, is a type of aquatic plant that thrives in freshwater environments.

It is characterized by its long, ribbon-like leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. This plant is often found in slow-moving or still waters, such as ponds and lakes. Its structure consists of a central stem with spirally arranged leaves, and it reproduces through spores.
