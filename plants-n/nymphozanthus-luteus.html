Nymphozanthus luteus

Nymphozanthus luteus, commonly known as the golden flame bush, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, shrub-like growth habit and produces clusters of bright yellow, tubular flowers. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for coastal and inland gardens. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological biodiversity.

The leaves are typically narrow and evergreen, providing ornamental value throughout the year.
