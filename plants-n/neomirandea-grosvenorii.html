Neomirandea grosvenorii

Neomirandea grosvenorii, commonly known as the Grosvenor's ghost orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to the cloud forests of Mexico.

It is characterized by its pale, almost translucent petals and sepals, which give it an ethereal appearance. This plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surface of other plants without deriving nutrients from them. Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat destruction and limited natural propagation, it is considered endangered and requires careful protection.
