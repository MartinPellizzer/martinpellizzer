Norantea indica

Norantea indica, commonly known as the Indian nettle, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family, which includes other stinging plants. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive veining pattern and produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters. Its stems are covered in fine hairs that can cause a mild skin irritation upon contact.

Norantea indica is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands.
