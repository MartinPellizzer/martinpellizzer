Nidema ottonis var. triandrum

Nidema ottonis var. triandrum, commonly known as the three-lobed rock rose, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in arid environments.

The species is also noted for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
