Neolobivia × nakaii

Neolobivia × nakaii, commonly known as the hybrid cactus, is a naturally occurring cross between Neolobivia and Lophophora species.

It is characterized by its columnar stem with ribbed structures and spines that vary in length and color. The plant typically produces yellow or orange flowers during the summer months. It is native to the arid regions of northern Mexico and is well adapted to dry, hot climates.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in cactus gardens and collections.
