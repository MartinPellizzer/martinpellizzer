Nicotiana suaveolens

Nicotiana suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet tobacco or nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of violet and white. The plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous small, dark seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
