Narcissus jasmineus

Narcissus jasmineus, commonly known as the sweet narcissus, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 15-30 cm and features narrow, strap-like leaves. It blooms in early spring, producing clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that emit a sweet, jasmine-like scent.

Narcissus jasmineus is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and pleasant aroma.
