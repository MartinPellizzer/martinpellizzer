Narcissus elegans var. fallax

Narcissus elegans var. fallax, commonly known as the autumn daffodil, is a variant of the elegant daffodil native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its late-blooming habit, typically flowering from late summer to early autumn. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of pale yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

This variety is valued for its unique blooming period and ornamental appeal in garden settings.
