Nephelea cubensis

Nephelea cubensis, commonly known as the Cuban flame vine, is a flowering plant native to Cuba.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its vibrant orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has smooth, succulent stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in warm, sunny environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Nephelea cubensis is also known for its mildly toxic sap, which contains cardiac glycosides.
