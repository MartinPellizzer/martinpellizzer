Nephrodium elegans

Nephrodium elegans, commonly known as the elegant nephroid, is a rare and unique fern species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that exhibit a striking pattern of intricate, branching veins. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in tropical rainforests. Its reproductive structures, including sporangia, are clustered on the undersides of its leaves, a feature common among ferns.

Due to habitat loss, Nephrodium elegans is considered endangered, making conservation efforts essential for its survival.
