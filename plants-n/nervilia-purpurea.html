Nervilia purpurea

Nervilia purpurea, commonly known as the purple twayblade, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid characterized by its slender, leafless stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces delicate, greenish-white flowers with a distinctive purple hue on the dorsal sepal and petals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Nervilia purpurea is known for its mycorrhizal associations, which are essential for its nutrient uptake.
