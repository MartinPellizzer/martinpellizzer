Narcissus pallidiflorus var. intermedius

Narcissus pallidiflorus var. intermedius, commonly known as the intermediate white narcissus, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, fragrant white flowers with a yellow center. The flowers typically bloom in early spring, adding a delicate touch to gardens and natural habitats. This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it adaptable to various garden settings.
