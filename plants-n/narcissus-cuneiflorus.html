Narcissus cuneiflorus

Narcissus cuneiflorus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved daffodil, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its strap-like, narrow leaves and bright yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and blooms in early spring. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Amaryllidaceae family.
