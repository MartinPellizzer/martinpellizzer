Neottia tatakaensis

Neottia tatakaensis, commonly known as the Tataka orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to specific regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories, where it relies on mycorrhizal associations for nutrient uptake. Its morphology reflects adaptations to low-light conditions, with elongated leaves that facilitate photosynthesis in dim settings.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological niche and limited distribution.
