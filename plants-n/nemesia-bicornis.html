Nemesia bicornis

Nemesia bicornis, commonly known as the two-horned nemesia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant produces vibrant, often brightly colored blooms in shades of pink, red, and white, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs.

Nemesia bicornis is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
