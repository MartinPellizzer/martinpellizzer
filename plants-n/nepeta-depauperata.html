Nepeta depauperata

Nepeta depauperata, commonly known as catmint, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces fragrant, blue to purple flower clusters in the summer. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic herbs and medicinal properties. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine and as a natural remedy for ailments.

Nepeta depauperata is also popular in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
