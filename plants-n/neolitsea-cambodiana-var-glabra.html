Neolitsea cambodiana var. glabra

Neolitsea cambodiana var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth neolitsea, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous bark and simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, white flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically followed by spherical berries. It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Cambodia and parts of Vietnam, and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
