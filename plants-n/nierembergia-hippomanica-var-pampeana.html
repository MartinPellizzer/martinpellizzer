Nierembergia hippomanica var. pampeana

Nierembergia hippomanica var. pampeana, commonly known as the Pampas bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the grasslands of South America.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats and is well adapted to the climate of the Pampas region. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, oval-shaped fruits that contain the seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
