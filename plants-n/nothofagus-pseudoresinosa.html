Nothofagus pseudoresinosa

Nothofagus pseudoresinosa, commonly known as the false myrtle or resinous nothofagus, is a species of evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Nothofagus genus, which is part of the beech family (Fagaceae). The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle, providing protection against harsh environmental conditions. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are followed by woody, spherical fruits that contain one or two seeds.
