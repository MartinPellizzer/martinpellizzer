Nepeta amethystina var. boissieri

Nepeta amethystina var. boissieri, commonly known as the Boissieri catmint, is a compact, low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It features silvery-gray foliage with a distinctive fuzzy texture, and its flowers are small, tubular, and typically lavender to purple in color. The plant produces clusters of blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a hardy and ornamental addition to gardens.

Its compact size and ornamental value make it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
