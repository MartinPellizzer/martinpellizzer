Nemocharis

Nemocharis, commonly known as the water lily of the genus Nymphaea, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Nymphaeaceae.

It is characterized by its floating leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom above the water surface. The plant typically grows in freshwater habitats such as ponds, lakes, and slow-moving streams. Its roots are submerged, anchoring the plant while absorbing nutrients from the water.

Nemocharis plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for aquatic life.
