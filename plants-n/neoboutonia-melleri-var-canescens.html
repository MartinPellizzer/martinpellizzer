Neoboutonia melleri var. canescens

Neoboutonia melleri var. canescens, commonly known as the silver-leafed bamboo, is a striking ornamental plant native to southern China.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, erect culms covered in a fine, silvery-white layer of hair. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often have a bluish-green hue, adding to its visual appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a container plant.

This variety is prized for its elegant appearance and low maintenance requirements.
