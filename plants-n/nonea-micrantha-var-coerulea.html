Nonea micrantha var. coerulea

Nonea micrantha var. coerulea, commonly known as the blue-flowered nonea, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually arranged in a whorled pattern around the stem. Its botanical interest lies in its unique coloration and its role in local ecosystems.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its visual appeal and hardiness.
