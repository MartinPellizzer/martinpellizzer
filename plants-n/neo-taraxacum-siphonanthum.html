Neo-taraxacum siphonanthum

Neo-taraxacum siphonanthum, commonly known as the Siphonanthum dandelion, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of serrated, hairy leaves and a flowering stem that produces a single, yellow composite flower head. The plant is adapted to cool, moist environments and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks. Its botanical features include a taproot system and the presence of latex in its tissues.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, sharing structural and genetic traits with other dandelion species.
