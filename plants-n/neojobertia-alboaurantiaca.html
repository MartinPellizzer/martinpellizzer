Neojobertia alboaurantiaca

Neojobertia alboaurantiaca, commonly known as the white-orange neojobertia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking, daisy-like inflorescences. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy flowers in shades of white and orange. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and drought tolerance.
