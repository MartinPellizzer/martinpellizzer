Nassella ayacuchensis

Nassella ayacuchensis, commonly known as Ayacuchan needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America, particularly Peru.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, needle-like leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, cylindrical cluster of spikelets, which are often tinged with purple or red.

Nassella ayacuchensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
