Nepeta multibracteata subsp. acerosa

Nepeta multibracteata subsp.

acerosa, commonly known as the hardy catmint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe. It features slender, upright stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a rough, slightly hairy texture.

The plant produces clusters of small, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy and low-maintenance garden plant.

Its aromatic foliage and attractive flowers make it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.
