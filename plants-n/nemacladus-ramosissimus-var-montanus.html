Nemacladus ramosissimus var. montanus

Nemacladus ramosissimus var. montanus, commonly known as the mountain nemacladus, is a flowering plant native to high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, branched growth habit and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and purple. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous areas with well-drained soil. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, adapting to the harsh conditions of its habitat.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological significance in alpine ecosystems.
