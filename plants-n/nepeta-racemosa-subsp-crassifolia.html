Nepeta racemosa subsp. crassifolia

Nepeta racemosa subsp.

crassifolia, commonly known as catmint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly fuzzy texture.

The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.

Its aromatic foliage and showy blooms make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
