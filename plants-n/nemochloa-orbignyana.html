Nemochloa orbignyana

Nemochloa orbignyana, commonly known as the giant water clover, is a unique aquatic plant characterized by its floating, leaf-like structures that form a dense, interconnected mat on the water surface.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is native to South America, particularly the Amazon Basin. The plant's leaves are broad, waxy, and arranged in a rosette pattern, allowing it to efficiently capture sunlight for photosynthesis.

It plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and shelter for various aquatic organisms.
