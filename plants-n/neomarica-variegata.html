Neomarica variegata

Neomarica variegata, commonly known as the variegated spider lily, is a striking plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its long, strap-like leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces clusters of white or pale pink blooms that are often speckled with purple or red markings. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden settings.

Neomarica variegata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its unique foliage and floral display.
