Nectandra saligna

Nectandra saligna, commonly known as the white cedar, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, smooth-barked trunk and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences, followed by oval-shaped berries. Its wood is valued for its durability and resistance to decay, making it useful in construction and traditional crafts.

Nectandra saligna plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
