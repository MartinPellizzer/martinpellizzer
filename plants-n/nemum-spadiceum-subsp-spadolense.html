Nemum spadiceum subsp. spadolense

Nemum spadiceum subsp.

spadolense, commonly known as the Spadiceous Nemu, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in shaded woodland environments.

The plant features lanceolate leaves with a rough texture and produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters. Its botanical structure includes a fibrous root system and a rhizomatous base that aids in vegetative propagation.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and adaptability to various garden conditions.
