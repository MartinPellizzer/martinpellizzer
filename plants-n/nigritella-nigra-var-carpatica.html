Nigritella nigra var. carpatica

Nigritella nigra var. carpatica, commonly known as the Carpathian black nightshade, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It features dark purple to black berries that are toxic to humans and animals. The plant has simple, alternate leaves with a rough texture and a strong, unpleasant odor. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a food source for certain wildlife species and its historical use in traditional medicine.
