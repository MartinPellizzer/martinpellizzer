Nastanthus agglomeratus var. laciniatus

Nastanthus agglomeratus var. laciniatus, commonly known as the shredded nastanthus, is a unique and visually striking plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its distinctive foliage, which features finely divided, lace-like leaves that give it a delicate, feathery appearance. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires consistent moisture and well-draining soil to flourish. Its botanical structure includes small, clustered inflorescences that produce tiny, inconspicuous flowers, which are often overlooked in favor of its ornamental foliage.

This variety is prized in horticulture for its unique leaf morphology and ability to add texture to shaded garden spaces.
