Nectandra tovarensis

Nectandra tovarensis, commonly known as the Tovarensis tonka tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Caribbean islands, particularly Cuba and Hispaniola.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like cinnamon and laurel. The tree is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains aromatic seeds, which have been historically used in traditional medicine and perfumery.

The plant's essential oils are valued for their unique scent and potential medicinal properties.
