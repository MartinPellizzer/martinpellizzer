Noronhia comorensis

Noronhia comorensis, commonly known as the Comorian nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Comoros Islands in the Indian Ocean.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, often during the rainy season. Its leaves are simple, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

The fruit is a small, fleshy berry that turns red when mature, containing several seeds.
