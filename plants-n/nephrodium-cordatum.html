Nephrodium cordatum

Nephrodium cordatum, commonly known as the heart-shaped fern, is a species of flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves, which are typically lobed and have a delicate, feathery appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in compact inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
