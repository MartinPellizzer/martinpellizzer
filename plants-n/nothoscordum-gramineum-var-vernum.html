Nothoscordum gramineum var. vernum

Nothoscordum gramineum var. vernum, commonly known as the spring garlic chive, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like leaves and umbel-shaped inflorescences. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in early spring, often preceding the foliage. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense clumps in moist, well-drained soils.

The plant is valued for its edible leaves, which have a mild garlic-like flavor and are used in culinary applications.
