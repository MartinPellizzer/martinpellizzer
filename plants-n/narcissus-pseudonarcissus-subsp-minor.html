Narcissus pseudonarcissus subsp. minor

Narcissus pseudonarcissus subsp.

minor, commonly known as the Pyrenean daffodil, is a subspecies native to the Pyrenees region of Europe. It is a bulbous perennial plant that produces narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers.

The flowers typically bloom in late winter to early spring, making it one of the earliest flowering daffodils. It prefers well-drained, calcareous soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and natural landscapes.
