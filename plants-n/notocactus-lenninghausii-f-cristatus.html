Notocactus lenninghausii f. cristatus

Notocactus lenninghausii f. cristatus, commonly known as the cristate notocactus, is a rare and striking hybrid of the notocactus genus.

It is characterized by its unique, flattened, and often branched growth form, known as a cristate or "crista" structure, which results from a genetic mutation. The plant typically features vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often appearing in late spring or early summer. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Due to its rarity and horticultural appeal, it is highly sought after by collectors and enthusiasts.
