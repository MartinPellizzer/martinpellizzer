Noticastrum chebataroffii

Noticastrum chebataroffii, commonly known as the Argentine acacia, is a shrub native to Argentina and parts of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it resilient to drought conditions. It plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.

Additionally, it has been studied for its potential in soil stabilization and ecological restoration projects.
