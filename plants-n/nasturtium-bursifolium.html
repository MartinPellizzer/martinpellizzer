Nasturtium bursifolium

Nasturtium bursifolium, commonly known as the bladderleaf nasturtium, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Tropaeolaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves with bladders on the underside. The plant produces bright orange or yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves and flowers are edible, often used in salads or as a spice.
