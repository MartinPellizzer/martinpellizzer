Nerium salicinum

Nerium salicinum, commonly known as the white oleander, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height, characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy, clustered flowers. The plant produces fragrant, white or pink flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its bark is smooth and gray, and it is known for its adaptability to various soil types and climates.

Nerium salicinum is also noted for its ability to thrive in coastal areas due to its resistance to salt spray.
