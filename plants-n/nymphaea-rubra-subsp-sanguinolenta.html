Nymphaea rubra subsp. sanguinolenta

Nymphaea rubra subsp.

sanguinolenta, commonly known as the Crimson Water Lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to tropical regions of Africa. It features large, showy red petals that contrast with its pale yellow inner sepals, creating a visually appealing bloom.

The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in ponds and lakes. Its leaves are broad and floating, with a waxy surface that helps repel water.

The species is notable for its vibrant coloration and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in water gardens.
