Nephelochloa

Nephelochloa, commonly known as the cloudgrass, is a genus of grasses belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its fine, delicate foliage and often grows in moist, temperate environments. The plants typically have narrow, grass-like leaves and produce small, clustered inflorescences. Nephelochloa species are often found in meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks.

These plants play a role in soil stabilization and provide habitat for various small organisms.
