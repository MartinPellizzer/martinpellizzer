Nepenthes tubulosa

Nepenthes tubulosa, commonly known as the tubular pitcher plant, is a tropical climbing shrub native to Borneo.

It is characterized by its unique, tubular-shaped pitcher structures that are adapted for trapping and digesting insects. The plant's pitchers are typically green or reddish in color and produce a sticky secretion to lure prey. Nepenthes tubulosa thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires high levels of rainfall.

Its botanical features make it a fascinating subject for both ecological study and horticultural cultivation.
