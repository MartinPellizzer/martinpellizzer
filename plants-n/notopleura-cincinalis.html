Notopleura cincinalis

Notopleura cincinalis, commonly known as the red-flowered hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from red to pink, with five petals and a prominent central stigma. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are typically green and slightly hairy. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas or near freshwater bodies.

Notopleura cincinalis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
