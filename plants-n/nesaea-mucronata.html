Nesaea mucronata

Nesaea mucronata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed nesaea, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Acanthaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, clustered in whorls along the stem. Native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
