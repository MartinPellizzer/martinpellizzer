Nycteranthus sinuosus

Nycteranthus sinuosus, commonly known as the moonflower vine, is a climbing plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, fragrant, white flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a waxy surface, and its stems are woody and twining. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Nycteranthus sinuosus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique nocturnal blooming habit.
