Nigella papillosa subsp. papillosa

Nigella papillosa subsp.

papillosa, commonly known as the Persian cowpea, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and Central Asia. It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, pale yellow to green flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant produces elongated, cylindrical seed pods that contain numerous small, dark brown seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
