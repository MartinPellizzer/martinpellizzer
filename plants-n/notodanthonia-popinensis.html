Notodanthonia popinensis

Notodanthonia popinensis, commonly known as the Popin's hairgrass, is a species of grass native to Australia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and delicate, grass-like foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often in coastal or wetland habitats. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers.

Notodanthonia popinensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
