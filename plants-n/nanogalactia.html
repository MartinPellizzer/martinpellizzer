Nanogalactia

Nanogalactia, commonly known as the dwarf milkweed, is a small shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces a milky sap that is toxic to some animals, including livestock. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
