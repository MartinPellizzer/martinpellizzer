Nepeta legionensis

Nepeta legionensis, commonly known as the Italian catmint, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically gray-green and have a mint-like fragrance when crushed.

Nepeta legionensis is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soil.
