Neobathiea hirtula var. floribunda

Neobathiea hirtula var. floribunda, commonly known as the hairy neobathiea, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which provide a distinctive texture. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers sunny to partially shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
