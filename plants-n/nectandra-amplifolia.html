Nectandra amplifolia

Nectandra amplifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed sandarac tree, is a tropical evergreen species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by spherical, woody fruits containing aromatic seeds. Its bark and leaves have been used traditionally for their aromatic properties and medicinal applications.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in local ecosystems.
