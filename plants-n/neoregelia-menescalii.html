Neoregelia menescalii

Neoregelia menescalii, commonly known as the Menescalii bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage that ranges from red to orange and yellow, often with intricate patterns. The plant features a rosette-shaped leaf structure that collects water, supporting its adaptation to humid, tropical environments. Its flowers, though less showy than its leaves, are typically pink or red and appear in small clusters.

Neoregelia menescalii thrives in bright, indirect light and requires high humidity and well-draining soil for optimal growth.
