Neyraudia madagascariensis var. zollingeri

Neyraudia madagascariensis var. zollingeri, commonly known as the Zollinger's reed, is a striking ornamental grass native to Madagascar.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings, adding visual interest to gardens. The plant typically grows in clumps, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is valued for its architectural form and ability to provide texture in landscape design.

While it is not widely cultivated outside its native range, it is appreciated for its unique appearance and adaptability to tropical climates.
