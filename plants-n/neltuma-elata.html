Neltuma elata

Neltuma elata, commonly known as the tall bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or alpine regions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is known for its ability to thrive in cool, moist conditions.

Neltuma elata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable climates.
