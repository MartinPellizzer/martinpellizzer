Narcissus tazetta

Narcissus tazetta, commonly known as the Crown imperial, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive, clustered flowers that resemble a crown, typically blooming in shades of white, yellow, or orange. The plant produces narrow, grass-like leaves and has a strong, spicy scent. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, often used in gardens for its ornamental value.

Narcissus tazetta is also cultivated for its edible bulbs, which are used in various culinary traditions.
