Nopalea moniliformis

Nopalea moniliformis, commonly known as the chain cactus, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are often covered in spines and segmented into distinct, bead-like segments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a decorative touch to its appearance. Nopalea moniliformis is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
