Nepeta juncea subsp. ghorana

Nepeta juncea subsp.

ghorana, commonly known as Afghan catnip, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs.

The plant typically grows to a height of about 30-60 cm, featuring square stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small, clustered in whorls, and range in color from white to violet, attracting various pollinators.

This subspecies is noted for its mild aromatic properties and is occasionally used in traditional medicine.
