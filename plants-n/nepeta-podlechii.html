Nepeta podlechii

Nepeta podlechii, commonly known as the Balkan catmint, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its calming properties.

Nepeta podlechii thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.
