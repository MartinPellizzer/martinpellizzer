Noccaea sintenisii subsp. crassum

Noccaea sintenisii subsp.

crassum, commonly known as the thick-leaved stonecress, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments.

The plant has basal leaves that are thick and fleshy, adapted to conserve water. Its flowers are small, white or pink, and arranged in clusters, typical of the Brassicaceae family.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
