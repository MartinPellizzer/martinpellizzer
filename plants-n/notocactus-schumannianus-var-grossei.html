Notocactus schumannianus var. grossei

Notocactus schumannianus var. grossei, commonly known as the Schumann's notocactus, is a distinctive cactus species native to South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are often ribbed and covered in short, white spines. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its green body. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires plenty of sunlight, making it suitable for arid climates.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts and collectors.
