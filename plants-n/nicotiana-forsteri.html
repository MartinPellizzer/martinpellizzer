Nicotiana forsteri

Nicotiana forsteri, commonly known as the Forster's tobacco, is a species native to South America, particularly found in regions of Argentina and Chile.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its erect growth habit and hairy stems. The leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate in shape, with a rough texture and a strong aromatic scent. Its flowers are trumpet-shaped, usually appearing in clusters, and are typically pink or purple in color.

This plant is known for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of nicotine.
