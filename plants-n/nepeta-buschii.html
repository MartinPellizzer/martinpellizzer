Nepeta buschii

Nepeta buschii, commonly known as catmint, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage has a strong minty scent, which is more pronounced when crushed.

Nepeta buschii is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in well-drained soil.
