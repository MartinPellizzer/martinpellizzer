Nageia chinensis

Nageia chinensis, commonly known as the Chinese nageia, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the family Podocarpaceae and is characterized by its needle-like leaves and cone-bearing structure. The tree typically grows in mountainous regions and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. Its bark is reddish-brown and becomes fissured with age, while its cones are small and woody.

Nageia chinensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
