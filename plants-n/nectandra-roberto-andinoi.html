Nectandra roberto-andinoi

Nectandra roberto-andinoi, commonly known as the Roberto Andino's sandarac, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to the tropical rainforests of South America, particularly in Ecuador and Peru. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves and small, white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, and the tree can grow up to 20 meters tall.

Due to its unique properties, it has been studied for potential medicinal and aromatic uses.
