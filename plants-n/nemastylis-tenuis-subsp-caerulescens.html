Nemastylis tenuis subsp. caerulescens

Nemastylis tenuis subsp.

caerulescens, commonly known as the blue-flowered nemastylis, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of southern Africa. It is a small, herbaceous perennial that typically grows in open, sandy or rocky soils.

The plant is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and its distinctive blue-violet flowers that bloom in early spring. It is often found in coastal or mountainous habitats where it thrives in well-drained conditions.
