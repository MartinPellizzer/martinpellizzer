Nothofagus carrii

Nothofagus carrii, commonly known as the Huilliche or Chilean southern beech, is a species of evergreen tree native to southern South America.

It belongs to the Nothofagus genus, which is part of the Fagaceae family, and is characterized by its distinctive leaves and cone-bearing structure. The plant typically grows in temperate rainforests and is adapted to high rainfall and cool climates.

Its bark is thick and fissured, and its leaves are leathery with a pointed tip, contributing to its ecological and ornamental significance.
