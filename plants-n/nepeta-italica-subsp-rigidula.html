Nepeta italica subsp. rigidula

Nepeta italica subsp.

rigidula, commonly known as catmint, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-green foliage and produces clusters of blue or purple flowers.

The plant is a member of the Lamiaceae family, known for its square stems and fragrant leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and herbal use.

Its essential oils contain nepetalactone, which is responsible for its strong minty scent and its ability to repel insects.
