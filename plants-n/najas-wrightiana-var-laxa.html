Najas wrightiana var. laxa

Najas wrightiana var. laxa, commonly known as the slender water milfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the family Najadaceae and is characterized by its narrow, ribbon-like leaves that are arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically found in the water, contributing to its role in aquatic ecosystems. It thrives in shallow, slow-moving waters and is often found in ponds, lakes, and wetlands.

Due to its rapid growth and ability to form dense mats, it can play a significant role in water quality and habitat provision for aquatic life.
