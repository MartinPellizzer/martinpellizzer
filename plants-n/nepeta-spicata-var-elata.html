Nepeta spicata var. elata

Nepeta spicata var. elata, commonly known as catmint, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, blue to purple flowers that bloom in whorls along the stems. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Nepeta spicata var. elata is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
