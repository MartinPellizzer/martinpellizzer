Nigritella nigra-pallida

Nigritella nigra-pallida, commonly known as the pale black bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from deep purple to pale lavender. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. Its leaves are broad and velvety, with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside.

Nigritella nigra-pallida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique floral display.
