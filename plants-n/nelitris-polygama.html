Nelitris polygama

Nelitris polygama, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, branching stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its most distinctive feature is the clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer.

Nelitris polygama thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
