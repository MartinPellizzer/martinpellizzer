Narcissus albidus

Narcissus albidus, commonly known as the white daffodil, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and western Asia, often found in woodland areas and along stream banks. The plant produces showy, cup-shaped flowers with six to eight tepals that are typically white or pale yellow. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, growing from a bulbous base.

Narcissus albidus is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its early spring blooms.
