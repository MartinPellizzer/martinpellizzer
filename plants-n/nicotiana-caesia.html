Nicotiana caesia

Nicotiana caesia, commonly known as the blueflower tobacco, is a herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features striking, trumpet-shaped blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and has hairy, lance-shaped leaves. It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic foliage and showy blossoms.

Nicotiana caesia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
