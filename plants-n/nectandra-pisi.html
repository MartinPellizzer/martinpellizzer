Nectandra pisi

Nectandra pisi, commonly known as the Java nut or wild cashew, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and opposite, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by oval, woody fruits containing a single seed. The seeds are used for their aromatic properties and are sometimes processed into a nut-like product.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ecological and economic significance in its native regions.
