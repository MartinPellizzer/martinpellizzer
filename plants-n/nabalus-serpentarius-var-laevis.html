Nabalus serpentarius var. laevis

Nabalus serpentarius var. laevis, commonly known as the smooth serpentroot, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous stems and lance-shaped leaves with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its scientific name reflects its distinctive appearance and its historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
