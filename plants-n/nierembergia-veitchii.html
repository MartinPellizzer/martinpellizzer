Nierembergia veitchii

Nierembergia veitchii, commonly known as the Veitch's bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows to a height of 30-60 cm, with lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period, which extends from late spring to early autumn.
