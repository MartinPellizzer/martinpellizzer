Nopalea hondurensis

Nopalea hondurensis, commonly known as the Honduras prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to Central America.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom during the dry season. The plant produces edible fruits similar to other prickly pear cacti, which are rich in nutrients. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.

Nopalea hondurensis plays an important ecological role by providing food and shelter for various desert animals.
