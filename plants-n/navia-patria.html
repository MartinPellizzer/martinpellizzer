Navia patria

Navia patria, commonly known as the native elderflower.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree belonging to the Caprifoliaceae family, characterized by its fragrant white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, red berries in the autumn. Navia patria thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
