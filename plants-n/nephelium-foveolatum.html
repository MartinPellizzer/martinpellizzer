Nephelium foveolatum

Nephelium foveolatum, commonly known as the mangostan, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and broad, ovate leaves. The plant produces fragrant, yellow flowers that are followed by round, purple or red fruits with a thick, white rind. The fruit's interior contains segmented, juicy segments that are sweet and tangy in flavor.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its edible fruit, which is often used in culinary and medicinal applications.
