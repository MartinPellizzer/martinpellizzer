Nothoscordum gramineum var. flavescens

Nothoscordum gramineum var. flavescens, commonly known as the yellow garlic grass, is a variant of the garlic grass species native to South America.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It produces small, fleshy bulbs that are sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it distinguishable from other members of the Amaranthaceae family.
