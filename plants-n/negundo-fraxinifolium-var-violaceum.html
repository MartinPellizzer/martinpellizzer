Negundo fraxinifolium var. violaceum

Negundo fraxinifolium var. violaceum, commonly known as the purple-leaf sand cherry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that turn a striking violet or purple color in the autumn. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the spring, which are followed by small, red drupes. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

The variety is noted for its hardy nature and adaptability to various soil types.
