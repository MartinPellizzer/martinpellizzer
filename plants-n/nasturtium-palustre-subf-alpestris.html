Nasturtium palustre subf. alpestris

Nasturtium palustre subf.

alpestris, commonly known as the alpine watercress, is a flowering plant native to mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and small, edible leaves that have a peppery taste.

The plant produces yellow-orange flowers with five petals, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and stream banks.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as a decorative and edible plant in gardens.
