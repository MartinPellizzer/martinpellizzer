Neoconopodium capnoides

Neoconopodium capnoides, commonly known as the Cape mountain grass, is a perennial grass species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
