Nepeta archibaldii

Nepeta archibaldii, commonly known as Archibald's catmint, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic, square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Nepeta archibaldii is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
