Nepenthes × dominii

Nepenthes × dominii, commonly known as the hybrid pitcher plant, is a fascinating carnivorous plant resulting from the cross between Nepenthes alata and Nepenthes rafflesiana.

It is native to the Philippines and is characterized by its large, colorful pitcher-shaped traps that secrete a digestive fluid. The plant's leaves form the upper part of the pitcher, while the lower portion is a modified stem that supports the structure. Its ability to capture and digest insects makes it an effective nutrient source in nutrient-poor environments.

Nepenthes × dominii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique appearance.
