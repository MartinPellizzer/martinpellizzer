Nassauvia aculeata var. spinosa

Nassauvia aculeata var. spinosa, commonly known as the spiny nassauvia, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features small, clustered flowers that are typically pink to reddish in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant is characterized by its spiny stems and leaves, which are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its botanical features make it an attractive addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
