Nicotiana clarionensis

Nicotiana clarionensis, commonly known as the Clarion tobacco plant, is a species native to the Caribbean islands of Cuba and Hispaniola.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and produces vibrant violet or purple flowers. It is primarily cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking appearance.

However, it is also noted for its potential medicinal properties, though further research is needed.
