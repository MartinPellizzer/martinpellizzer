Nesaea triflora

Nesaea triflora, commonly known as the three-flowered nesaea, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It belongs to the family Campanulaceae and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that typically bloom in late spring. The plant produces three to five flowers per stem, each with a white or pale blue corolla. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Nesaea triflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
