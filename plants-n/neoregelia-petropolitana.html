Neoregelia petropolitana

Neoregelia petropolitana, commonly known as the Brazilian violet, is a striking bromeliad native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy bracts that range in color from purple to pink, often resembling orchid flowers. The plant features a rosette of grass-like leaves that collect water, creating a small aquatic environment for insects and microorganisms. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Neoregelia petropolitana is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
