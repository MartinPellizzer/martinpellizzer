Nymphaea × marliacea var. carnea

Nymphaea × marliacea var. carnea, commonly known as the pink water lily, is a hybrid aquatic plant native to tropical regions.

It features showy, semi-herbaceous leaves that float on the water's surface, with a waxy texture that aids in water retention. The plant produces large, pink to reddish-purple flowers that bloom in the early morning and close by midday. Its roots are anchored in muddy substrates, and it thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in ponds and water gardens.
