Nectandra cuspidata var. strumosa

Nectandra cuspidata var. strumosa, commonly known as the broadleaf sandarac tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and fragrant flowers. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by elliptical, woody fruits. Its bark and leaves contain essential oils, making it valuable for traditional medicine and as a source of natural resins.

The plant is often found in lowland rainforests and is adapted to humid, warm climates.
