Nepeta hieronymi

Nepeta hieronymi, commonly known as Hieronymi catmint, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a mild mint-like fragrance.

Nepeta hieronymi is often grown as an ornamental plant for its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in well-drained soil.
