Nicotiana suaveolens var. vinciflora

Nicotiana suaveolens var. vinciflora, commonly known as the sweet tobacco or angel's trumpets, is a species native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet. The plant is a herbaceous perennial that thrives in warm climates and well-drained soil. Its leaves are broad and velvety, with a distinct aroma when crushed.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance and historical use in traditional medicine.
