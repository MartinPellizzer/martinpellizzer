Napeanthus bicolor

Napeanthus bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue with a contrasting purple or pink throat. The plant grows to a height of about 1 to 2 feet and has narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun.

Napeanthus bicolor is often found in woodland edges and wetlands, where it contributes to the biodiversity of its habitat.
