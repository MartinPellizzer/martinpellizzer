Notocactus curvispinus

Notocactus curvispinus, commonly known as the hooked spine cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is a columnar cactus with a distinctive appearance characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems. The plant features prominent, curved spines that give it its common name and help protect it from herbivores. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, blooming in the evening and attracting nocturnal pollinators.

Notocactus curvispinus is well adapted to drought conditions, storing water in its thick stem for survival in harsh environments.
