Nectandra anomala

Nectandra anomala, commonly known as the Malabar sandalwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its aromatic wood and dense foliage. The plant typically grows in lowland rainforests and has smooth, greyish-brown bark. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and elliptical in shape, with a glossy surface.

Nectandra anomala is valued for its fragrant timber and is also used in traditional medicine.
