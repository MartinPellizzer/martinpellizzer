Nepeta stewartiana

Nepeta stewartiana, commonly known as Stewart's catmint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and pleasant mint-like fragrance.

Nepeta stewartiana is also valued for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and its drought tolerance.
