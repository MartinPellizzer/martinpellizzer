Nigella fumariifolia

Nigella fumariifolia, commonly known as the Persian cowpea, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, branched stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, elongated pods that contain numerous black, peppercorn-like seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, making it suitable for gardens and medicinal cultivation.

Nigella fumariifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
