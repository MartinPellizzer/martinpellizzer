Nymphaea bernieriana

Nymphaea bernieriana, commonly known as the Bernier's water lily, is a species of water lily native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes. Its roots are anchored in the sediment at the bottom of the water body, while its stems extend upward to support the leaves and flowers.

The botanical features of Nymphaea bernieriana make it an important component of aquatic ecosystems.
