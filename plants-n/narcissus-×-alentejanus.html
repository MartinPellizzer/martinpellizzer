Narcissus × alentejanus

Narcissus × alentejanus, commonly known as the Alentejan daffodil, is a hybrid species within the genus Narcissus.

It is native to the Iberian Peninsula, specifically Portugal and Spain, where it thrives in Mediterranean climates. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and white, typically blooming in early spring. It is characterized by its robust growth habit and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

The species is also valued for its resistance to certain pests and diseases, making it a popular choice for gardeners.
