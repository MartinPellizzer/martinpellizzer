Nyssa sylvatica var. typica

Nyssa sylvatica var. typica, commonly known as the northern blackgum, is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its glossy, ovate leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, which are followed by dark blue, egg-shaped berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones and mixed hardwood forests.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting wildlife.
