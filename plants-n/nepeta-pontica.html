Nepeta pontica

Nepeta pontica, commonly known as Persian catmint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and southern Europe.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite leaves that are often grayish-green in color. The plant produces spikes of small, fragrant blue or violet flowers in the summer months. It is well adapted to rocky and dry soils, making it a hardy plant in temperate climates.

Nepeta pontica is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its use in traditional herbal medicine.
