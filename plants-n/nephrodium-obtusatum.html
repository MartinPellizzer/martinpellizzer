Nephrodium obtusatum

Nephrodium obtusatum, commonly known as the blunt-leaf nephroid, is a rare fern species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Nephrodiaceae and is characterized by its unique, heart-shaped fronds that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest undergrowth, where it thrives in high humidity and partial shade. Its reproductive structures include sporangia that release spores, a key feature of its life cycle.

Due to habitat loss, Nephrodium obtusatum is considered endangered, making conservation efforts essential for its survival.
