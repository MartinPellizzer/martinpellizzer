Neomammillaria echinops

Neomammillaria echinops, commonly known as the spiny ball cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its globular shape and dense covering of spines, which provide protection from herbivores and excessive sunlight. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring, adding a striking contrast to its green body. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Neomammillaria echinops is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its hardy nature and aesthetic appeal.
