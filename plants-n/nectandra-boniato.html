Nectandra boniato

Nectandra boniato, commonly known as the sweet wood or boniato wood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic, aromatic leaves and bark. The tree produces small, fragrant flowers followed by oval, woody fruits containing aromatic seeds. Its wood is valued for its unique scent and is used in the production of incense and as a source of essential oils.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and commercial contexts.
