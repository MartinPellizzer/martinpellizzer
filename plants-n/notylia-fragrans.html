Notylia fragrans

Notylia fragrans, commonly known as the sweet-scented orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, showy flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or terrestrial herb, often found in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in its natural habitat.

The flowers emit a sweet, vanilla-like fragrance, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
