Nassella niduloides

Nassella niduloides, commonly known as the slender needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its fine, needle-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its foliage is often used in floral arrangements due to its delicate appearance.

Botanically, it belongs to the Poaceae family and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
