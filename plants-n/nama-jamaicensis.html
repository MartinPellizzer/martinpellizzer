Nama jamaicensis

Nama jamaicensis, commonly known as the Jamaican morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean region.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its trumpet-shaped, showy flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant typically grows as a vine, climbing or trailing along supports, and produces small, round fruits that contain seeds. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, while its flowers open in the morning and close at dusk.

Nama jamaicensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
