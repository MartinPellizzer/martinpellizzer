Notocactus concinnus var. rubrigemmatus

Notocactus concinnus var. rubrigemmatus, commonly known as the red-gemmed notocactus, is a striking cactus species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are often covered in dense, woolly spines. The plant produces vibrant orange to red flowers during the warmer months, adding a dramatic visual contrast to its spiny structure. Its succulent nature allows it to store water efficiently, making it well adapted to dry environments.

The varietal name "rubrigemmatus" refers to the reddish coloration of its stems, which can intensify under bright sunlight.
