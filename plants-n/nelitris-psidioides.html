Nelitris psidioides

Nelitris psidioides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved guava, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are similar in appearance to guavas but have a distinct flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

Due to its adaptability, it is sometimes used in horticultural settings for its aesthetic appeal.
