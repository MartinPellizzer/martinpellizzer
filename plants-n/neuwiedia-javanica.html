Neuwiedia javanica

Neuwiedia javanica, commonly known as the Javanese flame vine, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking orange-red tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. The plant has heart-shaped, glossy leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Neuwiedia javanica is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
