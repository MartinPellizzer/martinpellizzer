Neomammillaria polythele

Neomammillaria polythele, commonly known as the Desert Sun or Mexican Sun, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and is covered in dense, white to pale blue spines. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun exposure.

Neomammillaria polythele is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and attractive appearance.
