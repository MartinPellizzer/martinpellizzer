Niphogeton ciliata

Niphogeton ciliata, commonly known as the hairy leaf bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features lance-shaped leaves with fine hairs along the edges, giving it a distinctive, textured appearance. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, often with a violet or purple tint. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and wetland restoration projects.
