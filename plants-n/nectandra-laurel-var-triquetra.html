Nectandra laurel var. triquetra

Nectandra laurel var. triquetra, commonly known as the three-angled nectandra, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like laurel and cinnamon. The tree is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. Its fruit is a dark, woody drupe that contains a single seed.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine and as a source of essential oils.
