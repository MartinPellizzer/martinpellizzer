Neomammillaria fuauxiana

Neomammillaria fuauxiana, commonly known as the Fuaux's nipple cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are often ribbed and covered in white to pale blue spines. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable plant for arid environments.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
