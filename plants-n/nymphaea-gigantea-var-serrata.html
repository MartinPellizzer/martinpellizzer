Nymphaea gigantea var. serrata

Nymphaea gigantea var. serrata, commonly known as the serrated giant water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in diameter, with serrated edges that give it its botanical name. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white, which bloom above the water surface. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often forming dense floating mats.

Its roots are anchored in the sediment, while its stems and leaves float on the water's surface.
