Nothofagus obliqua var. macrocarpa

Nothofagus obliqua var. macrocarpa, commonly known as the southern beech, is a variant of the Nothofagus genus native to South America.

It is a deciduous tree that typically grows in temperate rainforests, characterized by its dark green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by woody, cone-like structures containing edible, winged seeds. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing structural support and protection against environmental stressors.

This species plays a significant ecological role in its native habitats, supporting diverse flora and fauna.
