Nymphaea alba subsp. biradiata

Nymphaea alba subsp.

biradiata, commonly known as the white water lily, is a aquatic plant native to parts of Asia. It is characterized by its large, white, showy flowers that bloom above the water surface.

The plant has broad, floating leaves that provide habitat for various aquatic organisms. Its roots are anchored in the mud at the bottom of water bodies.

It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by supporting biodiversity and improving water quality.
