Nepeta strictifolia

Nepeta strictifolia, commonly known as catmint, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It features aromatic, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant has square, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a strong mint-like scent. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Nepeta strictifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal remedies.
