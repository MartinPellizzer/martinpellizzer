Najas tenuifolia subsp. tenuifolia

Najas tenuifolia subsp.

tenuifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf watermilfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America. It has slender, ribbon-like leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged.

The plant thrives in still or slow-moving waters and is often found in ponds, lakes, and reservoirs. It plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Its rapid growth can sometimes lead to dense vegetation, impacting water quality and recreational activities.
