Niphobolus ccallahuala

Niphobolus ccallahuala, commonly known as the Andean cactus, is a member of the Cactaceae family and is native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a small, spineless cactus that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils at high altitudes. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers during the spring and summer seasons. Its structure is adapted to withstand cold temperatures and intense sunlight.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
