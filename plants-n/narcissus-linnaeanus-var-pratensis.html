Narcissus linnaeanus var. pratensis

Narcissus linnaeanus var. pratensis, commonly known as the meadow daffodil, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in grassy meadows and along riverbanks, preferring moist, well-drained soils. It produces a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and has a short, stout stem.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its early spring blooms.
