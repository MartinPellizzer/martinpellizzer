Nymphaea alba var. occidentalis

Nymphaea alba var. occidentalis, commonly known as the Western white water lily, is a aquatic plant native to North America.

It features showy, white, cup-shaped flowers that open in the morning and close in the afternoon. The plant has broad, floating leaves that provide habitat for various aquatic organisms. Its roots are anchored in the mud at the bottom of ponds and lakes.

It plays an important role in water ecosystems by oxygenating the water and supporting biodiversity.
