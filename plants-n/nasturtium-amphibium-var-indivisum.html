Nasturtium amphibium var. indivisum

Nasturtium amphibium var. indivisum, commonly known as the watercress nasturtium, is a flowering plant native to wetland environments.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and bright yellow-orange flowers. The plant thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found near streams or in marshy areas. Its leaves are edible and have a peppery flavor similar to watercress.

This variety is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its culinary uses.
