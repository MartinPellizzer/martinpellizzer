Noccaea alpina

Noccaea alpina, commonly known as alpine nailwort, is a flowering plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with small, fleshy leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats. Its adaptations to cold and high-altitude environments include a compact growth form and a short growing season.

Noccaea alpina plays a role in stabilizing soil in its harsh environment and is of interest to botanists studying plant resilience.
