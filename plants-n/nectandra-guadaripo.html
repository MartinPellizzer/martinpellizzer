Nectandra guadaripo

Nectandra guadaripo, commonly known as the Guadaloupe sandwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic, dark-colored wood. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical in shape and have a glossy surface. It produces small, white flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by elliptical, woody fruits.

The tree is valued for its hard, durable timber and is also used in traditional medicine.
