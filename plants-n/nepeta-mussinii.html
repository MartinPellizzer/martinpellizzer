Nepeta mussinii

Nepeta mussinii, commonly known as the Armenian catmint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Armenia and Turkey.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, lavender-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its calming properties.

Nepeta mussinii thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy and low-maintenance garden plant.
