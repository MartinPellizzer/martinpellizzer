Nectandra canescens var. diversifolia

Nectandra canescens var. diversifolia, commonly known as the variable ashwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lanceolate, glossy leaves that vary in color and texture, often displaying a striking contrast between the upper and lower surfaces. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, which are typically white or pale yellow, followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its aromatic wood and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
