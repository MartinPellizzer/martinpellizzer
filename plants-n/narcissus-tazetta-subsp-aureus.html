Narcissus tazetta subsp. aureus

Narcissus tazetta subsp.

aureus, commonly known as the golden daffodil, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically bright yellow with a white or greenish throat.

The plant features strap-like, narrow leaves that are grass-like in appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Its blooming period occurs in early spring, adding a vibrant splash of color to the landscape.
