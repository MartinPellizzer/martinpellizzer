Nectandra lundellii

Nectandra lundellii, commonly known as the yellow sand-wood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, reddish-brown bark and glossy, elliptical leaves. It produces small, white flowers followed by woody, elongated fruits containing aromatic seeds.

Nectandra lundellii is valued for its timber and has traditional uses in medicine and as a source of essential oils.
