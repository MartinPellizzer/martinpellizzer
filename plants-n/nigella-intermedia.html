Nigella intermedia

Nigella intermedia, commonly known as Persian cowpea, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its slender stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into elongated, spiky seed pods. Its seeds are often used in culinary and medicinal applications due to their aromatic properties.

Nigella intermedia thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a suitable candidate for garden cultivation.
