Nectandra berchemiifolia

Nectandra berchemiifolia, commonly known as the fragrant sandarac tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and simple, alternate leaves with a glossy surface. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are followed by woody capsules containing aromatic seeds.

The tree is valued for its resin, which has been used traditionally in incense and medicinal applications.
