Nuxia sphaerocephala

Nuxia sphaerocephala, commonly known as the spherical-headed nuxia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded growth habit and small, waxy leaves that help reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits. It is well adapted to rocky soils and can thrive in full sun, making it a hardy species for xerophytic gardens.

Due to its unique appearance and resilience, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
