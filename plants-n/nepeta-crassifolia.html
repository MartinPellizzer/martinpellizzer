Nepeta crassifolia

Nepeta crassifolia, commonly known as catnip, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers arranged in whorled clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Nepeta crassifolia is also known for its aromatic properties and historical use in herbal remedies.
