Narcissus minutiflorus

Narcissus minutiflorus, commonly known as the dwarf daffodil, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in early spring. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces one to three yellow-orange flowers with a short, narrow tube. Narcissus minutiflorus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its early blooming habit.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile addition to rock gardens and borders.
