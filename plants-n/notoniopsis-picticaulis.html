Notoniopsis picticaulis

Notoniopsis picticaulis, commonly known as the painted stromboid, is a species of sea snail belonging to the family Strombidae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, colorful shell patterns that often feature intricate, contrasting bands and markings. The shell is typically spiral-shaped with a thick, robust structure, and the aperture is elongated and flared. This species is primarily found in tropical and subtropical marine environments, often inhabiting rocky shores and coral reefs.

Its diet consists mainly of algae and detritus, which it scrapes from surfaces using its radula.
