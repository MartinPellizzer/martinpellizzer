Narcissus × tuckeri

Narcissus × tuckeri, commonly known as the tulip-shaped daffodil, is a hybrid plant resulting from crosses between species of the genus Narcissus.

It is characterized by its large, cup-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or white in color. The plant is native to the Iberian Peninsula and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

The flowers bloom in early spring, adding a vibrant touch to landscapes during the transition from winter to summer.
