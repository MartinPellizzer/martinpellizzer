Nigritella nigra subsp. gallica

Nigritella nigra subsp.

gallica, commonly known as the Gallic blackberry, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its dark, almost black berries that ripen in late summer.

The plant has woody stems covered in short, stiff hairs and produces small, white flowers in clusters. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges and a slightly hairy surface.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
