Narcissus saltuum

Narcissus saltuum, commonly known as the alpine daffodil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its blooming period occurs in late spring to early summer, adding a splash of color to alpine landscapes.

Narcissus saltuum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
