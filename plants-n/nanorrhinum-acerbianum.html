Nanorrhinum acerbianum

Nanorrhinum acerbianum, commonly known as the dwarf rhino flower, is a small, flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family, which is known for its succulent characteristics and adaptations to dry environments. The plant features compact, fleshy leaves that help it conserve water, and it produces small, vibrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and resilience, it is often cultivated in rock gardens and as an ornamental plant.
