Nepeta scrophularioides

Nepeta scrophularioides, commonly known as catmint, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular blue or purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its aromatic leaves and showy blooms make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable.
