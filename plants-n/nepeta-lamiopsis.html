Nepeta lamiopsis

Nepeta lamiopsis, commonly known as catmint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its aromatic foliage contains essential oils that give it a mint-like scent.

Nepeta lamiopsis is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.
