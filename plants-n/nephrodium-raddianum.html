Nephrodium raddianum

Nephrodium raddianum, commonly known as the Raddiana fern, is a species of aquatic fern found in the freshwater habitats of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feather-like fronds that are typically green and glossy. The plant thrives in slow-moving or still water, often near the edges of ponds and streams. Its rhizomes are submerged, anchoring the plant and absorbing nutrients from the water.

Nephrodium raddianum plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and oxygen to aquatic life.
