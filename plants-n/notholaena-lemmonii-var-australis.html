Notholaena lemmonii var. australis

Notholaena lemmonii var. australis, commonly known as the Australian lemongrass, is a perennial herb native to Australia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, lemon-scented leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in open woodlands or heathlands. Its flowering stems produce dense clusters of small, greenish-white inflorescences.

This variety is valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb due to its distinctive fragrance and flavor.
