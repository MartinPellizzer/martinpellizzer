Neo-urbania haemathodes

Neo-urbania haemathodes, commonly known as the crimson thornbloom, is a striking perennial plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It features deep red, velvety flowers that bloom in clusters during the late spring and early summer. The plant has a woody stem covered in sharp, spiny projections that provide protection from herbivores. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a silvery underside that reflects sunlight.

Neo-urbania haemathodes is well adapted to poor soils and drought conditions, making it a resilient addition to xeriscaped gardens.
