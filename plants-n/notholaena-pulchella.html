Notholaena pulchella

Notholaena pulchella, commonly known as the silver fern, is a striking evergreen fern native to the eastern United States.

It features delicate, lacy fronds that are typically gray-green in color, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Notholaena pulchella is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
