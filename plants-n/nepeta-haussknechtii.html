Nepeta haussknechtii

Nepeta haussknechtii, commonly known as the Persian catmint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine hairs and produces clusters of small, tubular blue to purple flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often gray-green in color, with a fuzzy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens or borders.

Nepeta haussknechtii is also valued for its aromatic foliage and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
