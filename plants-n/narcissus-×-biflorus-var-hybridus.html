Narcissus × biflorus var. hybridus

Narcissus × biflorus var. hybridus, commonly known as the double daffodil, is a hybrid species within the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its two rows of petal-like tepals, which distinguish it from typical daffodils. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers with a trumpet-shaped corona, often featuring a contrasting color. It is typically found in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and floral arrangements.
