Neurocallis aureonitens

Neurocallis aureonitens, commonly known as the golden tamarind, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into woody pods containing edible seeds. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for the use of its seeds in traditional medicine. Its botanical features include a woody stem, opposite leaf arrangement, and a unique seed structure.

The species is also valued for its ability to thrive in dry conditions, making it adaptable to various environments.
