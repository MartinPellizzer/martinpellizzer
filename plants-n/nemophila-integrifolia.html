Nemophila integrifolia

Nemophila integrifolia, commonly known as the purple morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from purple to white. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. Its leaves are simple, entire, and often have a velvety texture.

Nemophila integrifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its early spring blooms.
