Nageia elata

Nageia elata, commonly known as the mountain pine, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and needle-like leaves that are arranged in spirals around the stems. The tree produces small, woody cones that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Nageia elata thrives in temperate to subtropical climates and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its wood is valued for its strength and durability, making it useful in construction and furniture making.
