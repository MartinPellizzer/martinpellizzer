Neomarica glauca

Neomarica glauca, commonly known as the blue orchid, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant blue to violet flowers, which can range in color from pale blue to deep purple. The plant features strap-like leaves that are typically dark green and slightly glossy, contributing to its ornamental appeal. Neomarica glauca is often cultivated for its beauty in gardens and as a potted plant due to its ability to thrive in warm, humid conditions.

Its unique coloration and elegant appearance make it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
