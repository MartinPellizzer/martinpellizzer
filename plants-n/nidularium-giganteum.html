Nidularium giganteum

Nidularium giganteum, commonly known as the giant flame plant, is a striking bromeliad native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its large, vibrant red bracts that resemble flames, which are often mistaken for flowers. The plant has a rosette-shaped structure with long, waxy leaves that form a central cup to collect rainwater. Its foliage is typically a deep green with a metallic sheen, enhancing its ornamental appeal.

Nidularium giganteum thrives in bright, indirect light and requires high humidity to flourish.
