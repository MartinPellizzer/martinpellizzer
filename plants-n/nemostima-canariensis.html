Nemostima canariensis

Nemostima canariensis, commonly known as the Canary Island rockrose, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or coastal environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and orange, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in a fine layer of white hair, which helps reduce water loss.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil and full sun.
