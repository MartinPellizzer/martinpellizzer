Nipponocalamus longifolius

Nipponocalamus longifolius, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or long-leaved bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its tall, slender culms and long, narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of fiber.

Nipponocalamus longifolius plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
