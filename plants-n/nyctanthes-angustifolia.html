Nyctanthes angustifolia

Nyctanthes angustifolia, commonly known as night-flowering jasmine, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and fragrant, white flowers that bloom at night. The plant produces small, dark blue to black berries that contain the seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Nyctanthes angustifolia is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
