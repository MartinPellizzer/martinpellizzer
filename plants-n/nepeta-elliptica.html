Nepeta elliptica

Nepeta elliptica, commonly known as catnip, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, tubular blue or purple flowers. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like mint and basil. Its leaves contain volatile oils, particularly nepetalactone, which is responsible for its distinctive effect on cats.

Nepeta elliptica thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and herbal collections.
