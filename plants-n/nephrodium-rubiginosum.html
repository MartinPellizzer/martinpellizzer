Nephrodium rubiginosum

Nephrodium rubiginosum, commonly known as the rusty nephroid, is a rare and distinctive fern species characterized by its unique morphology and ecological niche.

It features a creeping rhizome with fiddlehead-like fronds that unfurl into a bipinnate structure, giving it a delicate, lace-like appearance. The plant produces sporangia on the underside of its fronds, which are clustered in sori, a characteristic feature of ferns. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in temperate regions with high humidity.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, Nephrodium rubiginosum is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
