Nipponocalamus epitrichus

Nipponocalamus epitrichus, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. It is known for its ability to form dense clumps and can reach heights of up to 2 meters.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
