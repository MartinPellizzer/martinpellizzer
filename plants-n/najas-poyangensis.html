Najas poyangensis

Najas poyangensis, commonly known as the Poyang water nymph, is a submerged aquatic plant native to East Asia, particularly China.

It belongs to the family Najadaceae and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, whorled leaves. The plant typically grows in shallow, freshwater habitats such as lakes and ponds. Its flowers are unisexual, with male and female flowers occurring on separate plants.

Najas poyangensis plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for microorganisms and small aquatic life.
