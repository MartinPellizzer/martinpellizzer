Nigella unguiculata

Nigella unguiculata, commonly known as black cumin or Roman coriander, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, delicate white or pale blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, oval-shaped seeds that are rich in essential oils and have a distinct, pungent aroma. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, while its stems are branched and upright.

Nigella unguiculata is widely cultivated for its seeds, which are used in culinary and medicinal applications.
