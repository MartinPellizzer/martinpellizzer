Najas moshanensis

Najas moshanensis, commonly known as the Chinese waterweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Najadaceae and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, whorled leaves. The plant typically grows in freshwater environments such as ponds and slow-moving streams. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, often emerging from the water surface in early summer.

Najas moshanensis plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for microorganisms and invertebrates.
