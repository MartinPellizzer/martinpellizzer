Nigrolea

Nigrolea, commonly known as the black orchid, is a rare and striking plant native to the high altitudes of certain regions in South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its dark, almost black petals and sepals, which give it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom during the cooler months, typically between late autumn and early spring. Nigrolea thrives in moist, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its rarity and unique beauty, it is highly sought after by botanists and orchid enthusiasts alike.
