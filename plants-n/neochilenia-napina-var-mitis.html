Neochilenia napina var. mitis

Neochilenia napina var. mitis, commonly known as the soft-hair tree, is a species native to South America, particularly found in regions of Brazil and Peru.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in axillary or terminal inflorescences, and it produces small, round fruits.

The plant has a slow growth rate and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in ecological restoration projects.
