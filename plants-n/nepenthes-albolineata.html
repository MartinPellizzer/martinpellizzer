Nepenthes albolineata

Nepenthes albolineata, commonly known as the white-line pitcher plant, is a tropical carnivorous plant native to Borneo.

It is characterized by its distinctive white veins on the inner surface of its elongated, pitcher-shaped leaves. These pitchers secrete a digestive enzyme-laden liquid to trap and digest insects, providing the plant with essential nutrients. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments with high rainfall.

Its unique morphology and carnivorous adaptation make it a fascinating subject in botanical studies.
