Nissolia spinosa

Nissolia spinosa, commonly known as the spiny nissolia, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and spiny leaves, which provide a defensive mechanism against herbivores. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

Nissolia spinosa is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
