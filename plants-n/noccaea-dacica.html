Noccaea dacica

Noccaea dacica, commonly known as the Romanian watercress, is a flowering plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous plants like broccoli and mustard. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils near water bodies, and it has small, alternate leaves with a peppery flavor. Its flowers are white or pale yellow, arranged in racemes, and it produces small, rounded pods containing several seeds.

Noccaea dacica is valued for its nutritional content and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
