Nassella longearistata

Nassella longearistata, commonly known as the slender needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its fine, needle-like leaves and compact, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, spikelets.

Nassella longearistata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.
