Nyssa javanica

Nyssa javanica, commonly known as the Javan water thrush or Javan tamarind, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Nyssaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and alternate, simple, elliptic leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by edible, orange-red drupes.

It is often found in lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
