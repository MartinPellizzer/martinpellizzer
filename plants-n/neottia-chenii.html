Neottia chenii

Neottia chenii, commonly known as the Chen's twayblade, is a species of orchid native to eastern Asia.

It is a small, herbaceous plant with a short, underground rhizome and a single, erect stem. The plant produces two opposite, strap-like leaves and a single, greenish-yellow flower with a distinctive, spurred lip. Neottia chenii is typically found in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and wetlands.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is noted for its specialized mycorrhizal relationships with fungi.
