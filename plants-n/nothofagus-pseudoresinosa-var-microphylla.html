Nothofagus pseudoresinosa var. microphylla

Nothofagus pseudoresinosa var. microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved silverwood, is a variant of the Nothofagus genus native to South America.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are usually glossy and dark green on top with a silvery underside. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by edible, chestnut-like fruits. It thrives in temperate rainforests and is adapted to moist, shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies of South American flora.
