Nepeta farakulumensis

Nepeta farakulumensis, commonly known as the Farakulum catmint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or violet flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its mild sedative properties.

Nepeta farakulumensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in natural habitats.
