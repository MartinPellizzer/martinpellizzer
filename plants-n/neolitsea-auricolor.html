Neolitsea auricolor

Neolitsea auricolor, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green with a slight waxy coating. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange to red berries that are edible for birds. Neolitsea auricolor thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in warm regions.
