Nuphar pumila var. hookeri

Nuphar pumila var. hookeri, commonly known as the Hooker's yellow pond-lily, is a perennial aquatic plant native to parts of Asia.

It typically grows in shallow freshwater habitats such as ponds and lakes. The plant features floating leaves with a waxy surface and yellow flowers that bloom in summer. Its roots are rhizomatous, allowing it to spread vegetatively.

It plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
