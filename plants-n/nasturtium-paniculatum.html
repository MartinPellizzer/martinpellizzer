Nasturtium paniculatum

Nasturtium paniculatum, commonly known as the climbing nasturtium, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant orange and yellow flowers. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and can grow as a vine or shrub, often reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its flowers are borne in clusters and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Nasturtium paniculatum is also valued for its edible leaves and seeds, which have a peppery flavor.
