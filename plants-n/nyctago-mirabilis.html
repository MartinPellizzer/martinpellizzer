Nyctago mirabilis

Nyctago mirabilis, commonly known as the moonflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its large, white, fragrant blooms that open at night and close by dawn. The plant belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is known for its rapid blooming cycle. Its leaves are broad and oval, with a slightly hairy texture.

The moonflower thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
