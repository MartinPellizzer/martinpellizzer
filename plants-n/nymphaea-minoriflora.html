Nymphaea minoriflora

Nymphaea minoriflora, commonly known as the small-flowered water lily, is a aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Nymphaeaceae and is characterized by its floating leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats such as ponds and lakes. Its leaves are rounded with a waxy surface, and its flowers are usually pink or white, opening in the early morning.

Nymphaea minoriflora plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various water organisms.
