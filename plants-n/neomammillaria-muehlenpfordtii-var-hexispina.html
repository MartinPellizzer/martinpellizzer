Neomammillaria muehlenpfordtii var. hexispina

Neomammillaria muehlenpfordtii var. hexispina, commonly known as the six-spined hedgehog cactus, is a compact, globular cactus native to Mexico.

It features a dense covering of white to pale yellow spines, with the variety hexispina distinguished by its six prominent central spines. The plant has a short, thick stem and produces pale yellow flowers in spring. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water and well-draining soil.

Its unique spination and compact form make it a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
