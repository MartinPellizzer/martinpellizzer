Nepenthes rafflesiana var. alata

Nepenthes rafflesiana var. alata, commonly known as the winged pitcher plant, is a tropical vine native to the rainforests of Borneo.

It is characterized by its distinctive, waxy, pitcher-shaped leaves that secrete a digestive fluid to trap and digest insects. The plant's leaves have prominent wing-like structures on their upper surface, which aid in water collection and provide structural support. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires consistent moisture and nutrient-rich soil.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its unique appearance and ecological role.
