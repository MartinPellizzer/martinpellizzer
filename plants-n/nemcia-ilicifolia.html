Nemcia ilicifolia

Nemcia ilicifolia, commonly known as the shrub rose, is a woody perennial plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a leathery texture and a reddish underside. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
