Navarretia foliacea

Navarretia foliacea, commonly known as the fairy bells, is a flowering plant native to western North America.

It is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or open habitats, often in coastal areas. It has linear, grass-like leaves and produces small, white or pink flowers in clusters.

Navarretia foliacea is a short-lived perennial that thrives in cool, moist environments.
