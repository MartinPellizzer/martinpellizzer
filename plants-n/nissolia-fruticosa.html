Nissolia fruticosa

Nissolia fruticosa, commonly known as the red-fruited shrub, is a small evergreen shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers followed by bright red, fleshy berries that are edible for humans and animals. Nissolia fruticosa thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine and cuisine.
