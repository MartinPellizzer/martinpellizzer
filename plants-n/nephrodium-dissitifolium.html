Nephrodium dissitifolium

Nephrodium dissitifolium, commonly known as the scattered-leaved nephroid, is a rare and delicate fern species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, pinnate fronds that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in tropical rainforests. Its reproductive structures, including sporangia, are clustered along the undersides of the fronds, facilitating spore dispersal.

Due to habitat loss, Nephrodium dissitifolium is considered endangered and is of particular interest to botanists studying rare fern species.
