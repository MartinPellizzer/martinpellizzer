Neomammillaria prolifera var. multiceps

Neomammillaria prolifera var. multiceps, commonly known as the clustered pincushion cactus, is a compact, globular cactus with a dense covering of white to pale blue spines.

It originates from the arid regions of Mexico, where it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. The plant features a short, thick stem with multiple areoles, from which both spines and flowers emerge. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, blooming in late spring or early summer.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements, making it a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts.
