Nassella pulchra

Nassella pulchra, commonly known as the purple needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems and tufted, feathery plumes that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in prairies and open woodlands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color that adds visual interest.

Nassella pulchra plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
