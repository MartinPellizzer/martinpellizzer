Nymphaea lotus acuminiloba

Nymphaea lotus acuminiloba, commonly known as the lotus of the Nile, is a water lily native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that float on the water's surface and its showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes. Its roots are submerged, absorbing nutrients from the sediment, while its stems support the floating foliage.

This species plays a significant role in both ecological systems and cultural symbolism in various African societies.
