Nemesia versicolor

Nemesia versicolor, commonly known as the common nemesia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Primulaceae and is characterized by its delicate, often bicolor flowers that range from white to pink and yellow. The plant typically grows as a low, spreading shrub with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Nemesia versicolor is known for its long blooming period, often from spring to early summer.
