Neodunnia edentata

Neodunnia edentata, commonly known as the toothless dunnia, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate flowers.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, hollow stems and finely divided, fern-like leaves that contribute to its ornamental appeal. Its small white or pale yellow flowers are arranged in clusters, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Neodunnia edentata is valued in horticulture for its delicate appearance and ability to thrive in woodland gardens.
