Narcissus × somedanus

Narcissus × somedanus, commonly known as the hybrid daffodil, is a cultivated variety resulting from the crossbreeding of different narcissus species.

It typically features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in a range of yellow to white hues. The plant is characterized by its robust growth and ability to thrive in well-drained soil. It is often grown for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

The foliage consists of long, narrow leaves that emerge from the base of the plant.
