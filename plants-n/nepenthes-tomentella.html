Nepenthes tomentella

Nepenthes tomentella, commonly known as the woolly pitcher plant, is a tropical carnivorous plant native to Borneo.

It is characterized by its unique, elongated, and often hairy pitchers that secrete a digestive fluid to trap and digest insects. The plant's leaves form the upper part of the pitcher, while the lower portion is a modified stem that supports the structure. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires specific soil and moisture conditions to flourish.

Its striking appearance and specialized adaptations make it a fascinating subject for botanical study.
