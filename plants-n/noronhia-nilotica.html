Noronhia nilotica

Noronhia nilotica, commonly known as the Egyptian gooseberry, is a flowering plant native to the Nile Valley and parts of the Middle East.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly serrated margin.

The fruit is a small, round berry that ripens to a bright red color, often used in traditional medicine and local cuisines.
