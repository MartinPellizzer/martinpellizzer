Nicotiana ingulba

Nicotiana ingulba, commonly known as the desert tobacco, is a species of flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, demonstrating a high degree of drought tolerance. Its flowers bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

The plant contains nicotine and other alkaloids, making it part of the nightshade family (Solanaceae).
