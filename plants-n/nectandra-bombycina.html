Nectandra bombycina

Nectandra bombycina, commonly known as the golden sand tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and woody structure. The tree produces small, fragrant flowers that are often white or pale yellow, followed by small, round fruits containing a single seed. Its bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their purported therapeutic properties.

Nectandra bombycina is also valued for its durable wood, which is used in construction and craftsmanship.
