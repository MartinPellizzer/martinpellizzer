Notholaena trichomanoides var. pinnatifida

Notholaena trichomanoides var. pinnatifida, commonly known as the narrow-leaf maidenhair fern, is a variant of the maidenhair fern family.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically pinnatifid, meaning they are feather-like with a shallowly lobed appearance. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its leaves are usually bright green and have a graceful, airy appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is commonly cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
