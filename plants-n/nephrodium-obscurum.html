Nephrodium obscurum

Nephrodium obscurum, commonly known as the obscure nephroid, is a rare and enigmatic fern native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Nephrolepidaceae and is characterized by its unique, intricate fronds that resemble the shape of a kidney, hence its common name. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments, often found in dense forest understories. Its reproductive structures, including sporangia, are clustered along the fronds, contributing to its limited distribution.

Due to habitat loss and its elusive nature, Nephrodium obscurum remains a subject of botanical interest and conservation concern.
