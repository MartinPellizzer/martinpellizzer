Nicotiana obtusisepala

Nicotiana obtusisepala, commonly known as the blunt-sepal tobacco, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades such as tomatoes and peppers. The plant is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine, it is sometimes cultivated for both aesthetic and practical purposes.
