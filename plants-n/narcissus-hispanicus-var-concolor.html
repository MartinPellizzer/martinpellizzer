Narcissus hispanicus var. concolor

Narcissus hispanicus var. concolor, commonly known as the Spanish daffodil, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically yellow with a white or pale green center. The plant is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in open, sunny habitats. It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental beauty.

This variety is noted for its compact growth and is a popular choice in gardens for its early spring blooms.
