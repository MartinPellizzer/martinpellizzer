Nasturtium obtusum var. alpinum

Nasturtium obtusum var. alpinum, commonly known as the Alpine nasturtium, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, glossy leaves and vibrant, orange or red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other mustard family plants. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in rocky or mountainous habitats.

Its edible flowers and leaves are sometimes used in culinary applications, adding a peppery flavor to salads and garnishes.
