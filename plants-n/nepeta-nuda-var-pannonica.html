Nepeta nuda var. pannonica

Nepeta nuda var. pannonica, commonly known as the Pannonian catmint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine hairs and produces clusters of small, pale blue to violet flowers. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly fuzzy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable plant for rock gardens or borders.

This variety is closely related to other catmints and is valued for its ornamental appeal and mild aromatic properties.
