Neottopteris pachyphylla

Neottopteris pachyphylla, commonly known as the broad-leafed shield fern, is a species of fern native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, leathery, compound leaves that can grow up to several feet in length. The fronds are typically divided into multiple segments, with a distinctive shield-shaped base. This fern thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptability make it a notable species in the fern family.
