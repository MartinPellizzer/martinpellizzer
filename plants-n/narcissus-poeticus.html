Narcissus poeticus

Narcissus poeticus, commonly known as the poet's daffodil, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 15 to 30 centimeters and has linear, grass-like leaves. Native to southern Europe, it thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight.

Its botanical name honors the Greek mythological figure Narcissus, reflecting its association with beauty and self-reflection.
