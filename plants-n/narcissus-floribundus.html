Narcissus floribundus

Narcissus floribundus, commonly known as the double daffodil, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers with a prominent, often reflexed, corona. The plant is native to southern Europe and is typically found in woodland and grassland habitats. It produces bright yellow petals and a central cup-like structure, which is a distinguishing feature of the genus Narcissus.

The double daffodil is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens.
