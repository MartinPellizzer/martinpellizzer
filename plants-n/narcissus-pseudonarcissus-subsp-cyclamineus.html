Narcissus pseudonarcissus subsp. cyclamineus

Narcissus pseudonarcissus subsp.

cyclamineus, commonly known as the cyclamen narcissus, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its distinctive, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and yellow.

The plant features a basal rosette of heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are often tinged with red or purple. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The subspecies is closely related to the common daffodil but differs in its foliage and flower structure.
