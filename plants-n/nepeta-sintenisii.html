Nepeta sintenisii

Nepeta sintenisii, commonly known as the Sintenis catmint, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported calming properties.

Nepeta sintenisii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature.
