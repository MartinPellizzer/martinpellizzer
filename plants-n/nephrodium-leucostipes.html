Nephrodium leucostipes

Nephrodium leucostipes, commonly known as the white-stemmed nephroid, is a rare fern species native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Nephrodioideae and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are often covered in a fine, white bloom. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires consistent moisture to flourish. Its reproductive structures, including spore-producing sorus, are typically located on the underside of its leaves.

Due to habitat loss, Nephrodium leucostipes is considered vulnerable and is of significant interest to botanists and conservationists.
