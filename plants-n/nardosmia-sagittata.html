Nardosmia sagittata

Nardosmia sagittata, commonly known as the arrowhead lily, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, arrow-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along forest edges, preferring well-drained soil. It produces small, oval berries that are edible but not particularly palatable.

Nardosmia sagittata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
