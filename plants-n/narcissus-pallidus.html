Narcissus pallidus

Narcissus pallidus, commonly known as the pale daffodil, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and western Asia, typically growing in woodland and grassland habitats. The plant produces a single, showy flower with a white or pale yellow corona and a yellow perianth. Its bulbs are adapted for storage and can remain dormant for extended periods.

The botanical features of Narcissus pallidus make it a distinctive and ornamental addition to gardens and natural landscapes.
