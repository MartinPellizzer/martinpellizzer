Nephroia carolina

Nephroia carolina, commonly known as the Carolina nephrolepis, is a species of fern native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that are typically bright green and have a slightly curled appearance. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodlands and along stream banks. Its rhizome is creeping and produces clusters of small, spore-bearing structures called sori.

Nephroia carolina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with the right conditions.
