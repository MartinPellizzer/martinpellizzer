Nastus productus

Nastus productus, commonly known as the hairy bittercress, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a small annual herb with slender, hairy stems and alternate leaves that are typically oval or lance-shaped. The plant produces small white or pink flowers that are followed by elliptical, bladder-like seed pods. Nastus productus thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in gardens, fields, and along roadsides.

It is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and vegetative reproduction.
