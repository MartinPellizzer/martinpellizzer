Notocactus concinnus var. caespitosus

Notocactus concinnus var. caespitosus, commonly known as the clustered notocactus, is a compact, slow-growing cactus native to Mexico.

It features a globular or cylindrical stem with a dense covering of white, woolly bristles that provide insulation and protection. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months, which are typically borne on short pedicels. Its unique morphology and ornamental appeal make it a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor cultivation.

The variety caespitosus is characterized by its dense, clustered growth habit, distinguishing it from other notocactus species.
