Nectandra elongata

Nectandra elongata, commonly known as the longleaf nectandra, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant bark. The plant is noted for its aromatic properties, which are attributed to the presence of essential oils in its wood and leaves. It typically grows in humid, lowland forests and can reach heights of up to 30 meters.

Nectandra elongata is valued for its timber, which is used in furniture and construction due to its durability and fine grain.
