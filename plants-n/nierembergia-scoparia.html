Nierembergia scoparia

Nierembergia scoparia, commonly known as the desert bell, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a perennial shrub that thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant produces striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. Its leaves are small, narrow, and covered in fine hairs to reduce water loss.

Nierembergia scoparia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand harsh desert conditions.
