Noronhia foveolata subsp. tomentella

Noronhia foveolata subsp.

tomentella, commonly known as the hairy noronhia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America. It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are usually ovate to elliptical in shape and have a leathery texture.

The flowers are small and clustered, often producing a subtle fragrance that attracts pollinators.
