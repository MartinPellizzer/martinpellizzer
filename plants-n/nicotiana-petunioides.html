Nicotiana petunioides

Nicotiana petunioides, commonly known as the nightshade or tobacco plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants such as tomatoes and peppers. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue, often opening in the evening. Its leaves are broad and ovate, with a slightly hairy texture.

Nicotiana petunioides is also notable for containing nicotine, a potent alkaloid used in various medicinal and agricultural applications.
