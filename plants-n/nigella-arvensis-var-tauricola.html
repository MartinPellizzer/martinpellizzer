Nigella arvensis var. tauricola

Nigella arvensis var. tauricola, commonly known as the Tauric cowslip, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus and parts of southern Russia.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family and produces small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks. The seeds are enclosed in a spiny, spherical capsule and are dispersed by wind.
