Niobe undulata

Niobe undulata, commonly known as the wavy niobe, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, waxy leaves that have a distinctive undulating edge, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, pale blue or purple flowers during the cooler months, typically in spring. Niobe undulata is adapted to survive in dry conditions, with deep root systems that allow it to access underground water sources.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it a notable species in its native habitat.
