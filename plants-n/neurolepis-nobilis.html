Neurolepis nobilis

Neurolepis nobilis, commonly known as the noble neurolepis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has hairy, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are yellow and bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Neurolepis nobilis is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
