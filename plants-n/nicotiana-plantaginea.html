Nicotiana plantaginea

Nicotiana plantaginea, commonly known as the velvetleaf tobacco, is a herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, velvety leaves that are often a deep green with a slight pubescence. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, typically blooming in the summer months. It is part of the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes.

Nicotiana plantaginea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has also been studied for its potential in pharmaceutical applications.
