Niphobolus hastatus

Niphobolus hastatus, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has hairy stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is commonly found in woodland edges and wetlands.

Niphobolus hastatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
