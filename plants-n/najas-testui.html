Najas testui

Najas testui, commonly known as water needlegrass, is a submerged aquatic plant belonging to the Najadaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, needle-like leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged, and it relies on water currents for pollination. Najas testui thrives in freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving rivers.

It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
