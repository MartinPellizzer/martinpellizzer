Nepeta bokhonica

Nepeta bokhonica, commonly known as the Persian catmint, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green leaves and spikes of blue to purple flowers. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its square stems and fragrant properties. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and borders.

Nepeta bokhonica is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
