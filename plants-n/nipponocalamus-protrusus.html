Nipponocalamus protrusus

Nipponocalamus protrusus, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, often clumping growth habit. The plant features long, narrow leaves that are typically bright green and slightly hairy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Nipponocalamus protrusus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
