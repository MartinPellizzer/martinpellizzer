Nepeta lavandulacea

Nepeta lavandulacea, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of 2 to 3 feet, featuring slender, upright stems covered in fine, silvery hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in a soft, fuzzy texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Nepeta lavandulacea is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and floral display.
