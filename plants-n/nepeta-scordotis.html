Nepeta scordotis

Nepeta scordotis, commonly known as catmint, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or violet flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and mild minty fragrance.

Nepeta scordotis is also known for its historical use in herbal medicine.
