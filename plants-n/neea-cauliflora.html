Neea cauliflora

Neea cauliflora, commonly known as the calico flower, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique cauliflorous growth habit, where flowers and fruits develop directly on the trunk and branches. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a smooth surface and a slightly waxy texture.

Neea cauliflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is found in tropical and subtropical regions.
