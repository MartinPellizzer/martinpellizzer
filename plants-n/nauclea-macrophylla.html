Nauclea macrophylla

Nauclea macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaved nauclea, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, leathery leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by elliptical, woody pods containing several seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
