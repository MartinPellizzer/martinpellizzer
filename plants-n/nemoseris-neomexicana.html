Nemoseris neomexicana

Nemoseris neomexicana, commonly known as the New Mexico milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
