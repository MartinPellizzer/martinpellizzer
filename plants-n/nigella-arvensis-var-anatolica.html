Nigella arvensis var. anatolica

Nigella arvensis var. anatolica, commonly known as Persian buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean and Middle Eastern regions.

It is a herbaceous annual with slender stems and bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces small, oblong seeds enclosed in a spiky, three-lobed capsule. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and slightly hairy, while the flowers are arranged in clusters at the tips of the stems.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.
