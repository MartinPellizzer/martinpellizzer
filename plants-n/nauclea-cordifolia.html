Nauclea cordifolia

Nauclea cordifolia, commonly known as heartleaf nauclea, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are typically glossy and arranged oppositely on the stems. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are often white or pink, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Nauclea cordifolia is also noted for its medicinal properties, with various parts of the plant used in traditional medicine.
