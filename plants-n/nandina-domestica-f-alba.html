Nandina domestica f. alba

Nandina domestica f. alba, commonly known as the white nandina, is a cultivar of the native plant Nandina domestica.

It is characterized by its variegated, white to cream-colored foliage, which contrasts with the typical red and green hues of the standard variety. The plant features small, clustered white flowers that appear in late spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. It is a compact shrub that thrives in USDA hardiness zones 6 through 9, preferring partial to full sun and well-drained soil.

This ornamental plant is often used in gardens for its attractive foliage and winter interest.
