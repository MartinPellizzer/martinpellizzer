Nabalus nanus

Nabalus nanus, commonly known as the dwarf navelwort, is a small aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in Europe.

It typically grows in shallow, still waters such as ponds and slow-moving streams. The plant has a rosette-like structure with small, rounded leaves that are often submerged. Its flowers are usually small and greenish-white, appearing in clusters near the water surface.

Nabalus nanus plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for microorganisms and invertebrates.
