Nabalu

Nabalu, commonly known as the sacred banyan tree, is a large tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the genus Ficus and is characterized by its extensive root system and aerial roots that form a dense canopy. The plant produces small, fig-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife species. Its leaves are broad and glossy, often used in traditional medicine for their healing properties.

The tree is also culturally significant in many indigenous communities, symbolizing longevity and spiritual connection.
