Nicotiana noctiflora

Nicotiana noctiflora, commonly known as nightshade tobacco, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, large, white or pale purple flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant belongs to the Solanaceae family, which also includes potatoes, tomatoes, and peppers. Its leaves are typically broad and lobed, with a hairy texture.

Nicotiana noctiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a mild, sweet scent that intensifies at night.
