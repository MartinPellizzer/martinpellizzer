Noccaea flagellifera

Noccaea flagellifera, commonly known as the slender rockcress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with small, alternate leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white powder. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, typically in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions.

Noccaea flagellifera is known for its adaptability to harsh environmental conditions and is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
