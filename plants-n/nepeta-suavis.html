Nepeta suavis

Nepeta suavis, commonly known as catnip, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers arranged in whorls along the stems. Its leaves are often used to make tea and are known for their stimulating effect on cats.

Nepeta suavis thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular garden plant.
