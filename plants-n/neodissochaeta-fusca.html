Neodissochaeta fusca

Neodissochaeta fusca, commonly known as the dark sand flatworm, is a species of flatworm found in marine environments.

It belongs to the phylum Platyhelminthes and is characterized by its elongated, ribbon-like body. The organism is typically found in sandy substrates and is adapted to burrow through soft sediments. It lacks a true circulatory system and relies on diffusion for gas exchange.

Neodissochaeta fusca plays a role in the benthic ecosystem by contributing to nutrient cycling in coastal habitats.
