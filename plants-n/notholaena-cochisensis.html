Notholaena cochisensis

Notholaena cochisensis, commonly known as the Cochise feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-green foliage and delicate, feathery inflorescences that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Notholaena cochisensis is often found in open habitats such as grasslands, slopes, and canyon walls.

Its ecological importance includes stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small animals and insects.
