Nauclea oxyphylla

Nauclea oxyphylla, commonly known as the African velvet bean, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, characterized by its dark green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by elongated, woody pods containing flat, kidney-shaped seeds. Its root system is fibrous, and the plant is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils.

Nauclea oxyphylla is often found in woodland and savanna habitats, where it plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various organisms.
