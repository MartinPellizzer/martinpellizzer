Notylia vanilla

Notylia vanilla, commonly known as the vanilla orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, fragrant flowers. The plant produces a pod-like fruit, which is the source of the highly valued vanilla flavoring. The vanilla bean develops from the ovary of the flower and requires a specific curing process to develop its aromatic compounds.

Notylia vanilla is cultivated for both its ornamental value and its economic importance in the food and fragrance industries.
