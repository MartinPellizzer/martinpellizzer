Nectandra pallida

Nectandra pallida, commonly known as the white sand tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurel and avocado trees. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often glossy and dark green. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically white or pale yellow, and it produces aromatic fruits.

Nectandra pallida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
