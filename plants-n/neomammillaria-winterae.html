Neomammillaria winterae

Neomammillaria winterae, commonly known as the Winter's mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stem and dense clusters of white or pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is a slow-growing species that requires well-draining soil and minimal water.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in succulent gardens and collections.
