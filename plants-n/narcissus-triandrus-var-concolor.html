Narcissus triandrus var. concolor

Narcissus triandrus var. concolor, commonly known as the concolor daffodil, is a variant of the broadleaf daffodil native to southern Europe.

It features a single, large, trumpet-shaped corona that is typically white or yellow, contrasting with its narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces a cluster of 3 to 6 narrow, upright flower stems, each bearing a single bloom. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental addition to gardens.
