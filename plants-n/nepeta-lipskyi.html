Nepeta lipskyi

Nepeta lipskyi, commonly known as the Siberian catmint, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils, which contribute to its distinctive fragrance.

Nepeta lipskyi is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of nectar for pollinators.
