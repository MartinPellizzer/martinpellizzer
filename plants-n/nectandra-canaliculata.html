Nectandra canaliculata

Nectandra canaliculata, commonly known as the narrow-cupped sandarac, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its aromatic, dark brown heartwood that is highly valued in traditional medicine and as a source of gum. The plant typically grows in lowland forests and has glossy, elliptical leaves with a strong, spicy scent. Its flowers are small and clustered, and it produces small, round fruits that contain a single seed.

The tree is also noted for its ability to thrive in humid, warm climates with well-drained soil.
