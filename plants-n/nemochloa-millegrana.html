Nemochloa millegrana

Nemochloa millegrana, commonly known as the silver hair grass, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, slender stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces tiny, wind-pollinated flowers that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its unique appearance and ecological role make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
