Nigella indica

Nigella indica, commonly known as black cumin, is an annual flowering plant native to South Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and dark, oil-rich seeds. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and thrives in temperate climates. Its seeds are widely used in traditional medicine and culinary practices for their aromatic and medicinal properties.

The plant's botanical features make it well-suited for cultivation in various soil types, provided it receives adequate sunlight and drainage.
