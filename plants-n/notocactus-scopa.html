Notocactus scopa

Notocactus scopa, commonly known as the Mexican torch cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a columnar cactus with a distinctive reddish-brown color and a spiny, cylindrical stem. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its rugged appearance. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Notocactus scopa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in dry climates.
