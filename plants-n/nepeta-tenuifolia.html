Nepeta tenuifolia

Nepeta tenuifolia, commonly known as catmint, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, fragrant blue or purple flowers. The plant produces tiny, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, and the plant is often grown for its ornamental value and pleasant scent.

Nepeta tenuifolia is also known for its mild sedative properties, making it a popular choice in herbal medicine.
