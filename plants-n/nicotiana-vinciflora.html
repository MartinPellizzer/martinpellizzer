Nicotiana vinciflora

Nicotiana vinciflora, commonly known as the sweet tobacco or wild tobacco, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades such as tomatoes and peppers. Its flowers are typically visited by a variety of pollinators, including bees and moths, due to their strong fragrance and nectar-rich structure.

Nicotiana vinciflora is also notable for its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments.
