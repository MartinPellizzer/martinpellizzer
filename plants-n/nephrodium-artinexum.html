Nephrodium artinexum

Nephrodium artinexum, commonly known as the golden shield fern, is a rare and unique fern species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, shield-shaped fronds that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine, golden hairs. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in tropical rainforests. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, typical of many fern species.

Due to habitat loss, Nephrodium artinexum is considered endangered and is of significant interest to botanists and conservationists.
