Neolacis psyllophora

Neolacis psyllophora, commonly known as the lacis psyllophora scale, is a small, sap-sucking insect that belongs to the family Psyllidae.

It is primarily found in tropical and subtropical regions, where it feeds on the sap of various host plants. The plant's botanical significance lies in its role as a host for this insect, which can cause damage to leaves and stems. Its presence can affect plant health and growth, making it an important consideration in ecological and agricultural contexts.

The insect's life cycle and feeding habits are closely tied to the specific plant species it inhabits.
