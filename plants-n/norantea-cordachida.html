Norantea cordachida

Norantea cordachida, commonly known as the heartleaf fern, is a unique plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, heart-shaped fronds that give it its common name. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, nutrient-rich soil. Its reproductive structures are small and inconspicuous, relying on wind for pollination.

Norantea cordachida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
