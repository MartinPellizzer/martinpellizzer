Nealchornea stipitata

Nealchornea stipitata, commonly known as the slender sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically forms dense mats of foliage. The plant has small, hairy leaves and produces clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Nealchornea stipitata plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for small invertebrates in its native environment.
