Nymphaea × eburnea

Nymphaea × eburnea, commonly known as the white lotus, is a hybrid water lily native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy white petals that bloom in the early morning and close in the heat of the day. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes. Its leaves are broad and floating, providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.

The flower's fragrance attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
