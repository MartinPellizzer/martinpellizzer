Nemophila pedunculata var. densa

Nemophila pedunculata var. densa, commonly known as the dense blue-eyed Mary, is a low-growing annual herb native to California.

It features small, delicate flowers that are typically blue or purple, with a distinctive white throat, and it blooms from late winter to early summer. The plant has hairy, linear leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, and its stems are slender and often branched. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and meadows.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
