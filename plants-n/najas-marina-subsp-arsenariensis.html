Najas marina subsp. arsenariensis

Najas marina subsp.

arsenariensis, commonly known as the arsenic watermilfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater habitats in Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Hydrocharitaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, ribbon-like leaves that are often arranged in whorls.

The plant typically grows in slow-moving or still waters, such as ponds and lakes, where it can form dense underwater vegetation. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, usually appearing in clusters, and it plays a role in providing habitat for aquatic invertebrates.

Due to its association with arsenic-rich environments, it has been studied for its potential in phytoremediation of contaminated waters.
