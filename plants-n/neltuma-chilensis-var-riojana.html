Neltuma chilensis var. riojana

Neltuma chilensis var. riojana, commonly known as the Chilean bellflower, is a perennial plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at high altitudes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, white pubescence.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable climates.
