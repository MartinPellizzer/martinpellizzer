Nepeta italica subsp. cadmea

Nepeta italica subsp.

cadmea, commonly known as catmint, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe. It features aromatic, narrow leaves and produces clusters of blue to purple flowers.

The plant is characterized by its strong mint-like fragrance, which is due to the presence of essential oils. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

This subspecies is closely related to other Nepeta species and is often used in herbal remedies and as a companion plant.
