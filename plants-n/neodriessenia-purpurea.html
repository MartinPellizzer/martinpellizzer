Neodriessenia purpurea

Neodriessenia purpurea, commonly known as the purple stagonospora, is a species of fungal plant pathogen that primarily affects grasses.

It belongs to the fungal order Pleosporales and is characterized by its production of purple-pigmented spores. The fungus typically infects the leaves and stems of its host plants, leading to the development of leaf spots and other foliar symptoms. It thrives in humid conditions and is often found in agricultural and natural grassland ecosystems.

Neodriessenia purpurea plays a significant role in the ecological dynamics of plant diseases in temperate regions.
