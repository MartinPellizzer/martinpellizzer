Nephrodium dissidens

Nephrodium dissidens, commonly known as the split-leaf nephroid, is a rare fern species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, deeply lobed fronds that resemble the shape of a kidney, giving it its scientific name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests or along stream banks. Its reproductive structures include sporangia that release spores, which are essential for its propagation.

The botanical study of Nephrodium dissidens provides insights into the evolutionary adaptations of ferns in tropical ecosystems.
