Nepeta septemcrenata

Nepeta septemcrenata, commonly known as seven-leaf catmint, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a distinct minty aroma. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers in whorls along the stems, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its cultivar 'Cat's Meow' is particularly noted for its dense foliage and showy flower clusters.
