Neoporteria atrispinosa

Neoporteria atrispinosa, commonly known as the black-spined hakea, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, tubular flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. The plant produces woody fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by animals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
