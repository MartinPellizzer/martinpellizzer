Nymphaea trisepala

Nymphaea trisepala, commonly known as the three-lobed water lily, is a species of aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in the early morning. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes. Its leaves are usually a deep green and can grow up to 30 centimeters in diameter.

The flowers, which are pink to white in color, have three sepals and are pollinated by insects, particularly beetles.
