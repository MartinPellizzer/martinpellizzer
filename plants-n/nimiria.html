Nimiria

Nimiria, commonly known as the sacred lotus of ancient Egypt, is a flowering plant belonging to the Nymphaeaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that bloom on long, buoyant stalks that rise from water. The plant has broad, heart-shaped leaves that float on the water's surface, aiding in photosynthesis. Its symbolic significance in Egyptian culture is reflected in its botanical features, which have inspired artistic and literary representations for centuries.
