Noccaea oppositifolia

Noccaea oppositifolia, commonly known as the opposite-leaved honesty, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbages, and is characterized by its small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant has alternate, ovate leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stem, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

Noccaea oppositifolia is also known for its delicate, papery seed pods that burst open when mature, dispersing its seeds.
