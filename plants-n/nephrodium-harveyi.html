Nephrodium harveyi

Nephrodium harveyi, commonly known as the Harvey's nephroid, is a rare and unique fern species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped fronds that are typically dark green with a velvety texture. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in dense forest undergrowth. Its reproductive structures include sporangia that release spores, contributing to its propagation in suitable habitats.

Due to habitat loss, Nephrodium harveyi is considered endangered, highlighting the need for conservation efforts to protect this botanical gem.
