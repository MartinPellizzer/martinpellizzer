Noronhia intermedia

Noronhia intermedia, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that typically grows up to 10 meters in height. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by red, fleshy berries. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and oval-shaped with a leathery texture.

Noronhia intermedia is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in well-drained soils and full sunlight.
