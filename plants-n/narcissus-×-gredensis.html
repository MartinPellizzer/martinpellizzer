Narcissus × gredensis

Narcissus × gredensis, commonly known as the Gredensian daffodil, is a hybrid perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with narrow, grass-like leaves and a single stem bearing one to three flowers.

Its botanical features make it a popular ornamental plant in gardens, valued for its vibrant yellow color and hardy nature.
