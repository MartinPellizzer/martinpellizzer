Nymphozanthus pumilus

Nymphozanthus pumilus, commonly known as the dwarf zanthus, is a small, herbaceous plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It typically grows to a height of less than 30 centimeters, featuring delicate, narrow leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique floral structure and potential ecological role in its native habitat.
