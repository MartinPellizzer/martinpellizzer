Navarretia crassifolia

Navarretia crassifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf navarretia, is a flowering plant native to California and parts of Nevada.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of broad, fleshy leaves. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers in early spring, which are clustered in a terminal raceme. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or open habitats.

Navarretia crassifolia is adapted to Mediterranean climates and is often used in native plant gardens.
