Nicoraella mucronata

Nicoraella mucronata, commonly known as the blunt-tipped orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically features lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered blooms in shades of white, pink, and purple. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden settings.

Due to its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
