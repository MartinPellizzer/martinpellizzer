Neoregelia fluminensis

Neoregelia fluminensis, commonly known as the "Brazilian violet" or "Fluminense bromeliad," is a striking epiphytic plant native to the rainforests of Brazil.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, colorful inflorescences that range from red to purple. The plant features rosette-shaped leaves that collect water, creating a small ecosystem within the rosette. Its foliage is often adorned with intricate patterns and can vary in color depending on light exposure.

Neoregelia fluminensis is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
