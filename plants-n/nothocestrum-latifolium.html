Nothocestrum latifolium

Nothocestrum latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf nightshade, is a species of flowering plant in the Solanaceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. Native to South America, particularly Brazil, it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant produces small, toxic berries that are often used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ecological studies.
