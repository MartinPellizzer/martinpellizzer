Nectandra elaiophora

Nectandra elaiophora, commonly known as the oil tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree is valued for its aromatic resin, which is used in traditional medicine and perfumery. Its fruit produces a valuable oil that has applications in both culinary and industrial uses.

Nectandra elaiophora thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.
