Narcissus pseudonarcissus subsp. alpestris

Narcissus pseudonarcissus subsp.

alpestris, commonly known as the alpine daffodil, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces a single, upright flower with a trumpet-shaped corona.

The plant typically blooms in early spring, with bright yellow petals and a white or pale yellow corona. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This subspecies is valued for its hardiness and is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
