Nonatelia chrysoneura

Nonatelia chrysoneura, commonly known as the golden-veined orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow veins on its leaves, which contrast beautifully with its deep green foliage. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of white and pale yellow, typically blooming during the warm season. Nonatelia chrysoneura is an epiphytic plant, meaning it grows on the surface of trees without being parasitic.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique floral structure and its role in the biodiversity of its native ecosystems.
