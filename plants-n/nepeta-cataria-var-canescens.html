Nepeta cataria var. canescens

Nepeta cataria var. canescens, commonly known as catnip, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features aromatic, opposite leaves that are typically gray-green due to a dense covering of fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale blue or purple, depending on the variety. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and herbal collections.

The plant is renowned for its stimulating effects on cats and its use in traditional medicine.
