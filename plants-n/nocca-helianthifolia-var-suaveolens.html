Nocca helianthifolia var. suaveolens

Nocca helianthifolia var. suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet-scented false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and wetlands. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters. Its most notable botanical characteristic is its sweet, fragrant odor, which is released when the leaves are crushed.

The species is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental and ecological value.
