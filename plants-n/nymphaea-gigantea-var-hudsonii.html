Nymphaea gigantea var. hudsonii

Nymphaea gigantea var. hudsonii, commonly known as the Hudson's giant water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in diameter, providing a striking visual display on the water's surface. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white, with a prominent yellow center, blooming during the day and closing at night. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, preferring nutrient-rich soils and ample sunlight.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a keystone species in wetland ecosystems, supporting diverse aquatic life.
