Nanhaia

Nanhaia, commonly known as the sacred lotus of the Andes, is a unique aquatic plant native to the high-altitude lakes of South America.

It features striking, pale blue or white flowers that bloom at dawn, symbolizing purity and spiritual renewal in local cultures. The plant has thick, waxy leaves that float on the water's surface, allowing it to thrive in nutrient-poor environments. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Nanhaia plays a significant role in both ecological balance and cultural heritage in the region.
