Narcissus × hannibalis var. pintoi-gomesii

Narcissus × hannibalis var. pintoi-gomesii, commonly known as the Pintoi-Gomesii daffodil, is a hybrid cultivar characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers.

It typically features bright yellow petals with a distinctive, often darker, throat that enhances its visual appeal. The plant grows to a moderate height, with narrow, grass-like leaves that form a basal rosette. It is well-suited for garden cultivation, thriving in well-drained soil and full sun.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and resilience in temperate climates.
