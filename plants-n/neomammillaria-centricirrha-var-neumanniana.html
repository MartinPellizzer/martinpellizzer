Neomammillaria centricirrha var. neumanniana

Neomammillaria centricirrha var. neumanniana, commonly known as the Neumann's clustered milk barrel cactus, is a compact, low-growing cactus native to Mexico.

It features a short, cylindrical stem with a ribbed structure and is covered in dense, white to pale green spines. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water and well-draining soil.

This variety is prized for its unique appearance and is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a container plant.
