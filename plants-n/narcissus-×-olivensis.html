Narcissus × olivensis

Narcissus × olivensis, commonly known as the golden daffodil, is a hybrid species of daffodil native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers with a prominent, trumpet-shaped corona. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and thrives in well-drained soil in full sun. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

This species is valued for its early blooming habit and vibrant floral display.
