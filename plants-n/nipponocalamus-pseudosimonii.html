Nipponocalamus pseudosimonii

Nipponocalamus pseudosimonii, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo, is a species of grass belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along streambanks. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.

The plant plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
