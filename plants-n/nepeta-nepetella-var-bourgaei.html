Nepeta nepetella var. bourgaei

Nepeta nepetella var. bourgaei, commonly known as the Bourgai catmint, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It features aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that are often gray-green in color, with a strong menthol scent. The plant produces clusters of small, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a desirable addition to both garden and landscape settings.
