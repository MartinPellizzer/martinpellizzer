Nothofagus crenata var. sapeii

Nothofagus crenata var. sapeii, commonly known as the Sapei nothofagus, is a variant of the nothofagus genus native to New Zealand.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub, typically found in the temperate rainforests of the South Island. The plant is characterized by its lanceolate, glossy leaves that are often arranged in whorls around the stem. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by edible, woody fruits that are important for local wildlife.

This variant is notable for its ecological role in maintaining forest biodiversity and soil stability in its native habitat.
