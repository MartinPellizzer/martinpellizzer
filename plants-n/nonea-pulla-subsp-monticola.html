Nonea pulla subsp. monticola

Nonea pulla subsp.

monticola, commonly known as the alpine nonea, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America. It is a perennial herb characterized by its compact growth habit and small, clustered flowers.

The plant typically thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, moist conditions. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are adapted to withstand harsh alpine climates.

The species is notable for its ecological significance in alpine ecosystems, providing habitat and nectar for various pollinators.
