Nephelium oleifolium

Nephelium oleifolium, commonly known as rambutan, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, spiky fruit exterior, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The fruit is typically yellow or red when ripe and contains a sweet, fibrous pulp surrounding a single seed. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

It is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated for both its fruit and aesthetic appeal.
