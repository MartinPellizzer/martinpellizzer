Nepenthes alata var. biflora

Nepenthes alata var. biflora, commonly known as the winged pitcher plant, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated pitcher-shaped leaves that secrete a digestive fluid to trap insects. The plant's pitchers are usually green with red or orange markings, and the variety 'biflora' is notable for producing two flowers per inflorescence. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires high atmospheric humidity to flourish.

This species is popular among carnivorous plant enthusiasts due to its unique appearance and ecological adaptations.
