Neurada procumbens

Neurada procumbens, commonly known as creeping neurada, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows along the ground, forming dense mats with prostrate stems that root at the nodes. The plant features small, opposite leaves that are usually ovate to lanceolate in shape. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, clustered in small inflorescences, and it produces small, dry fruits known as capsules.

Neurada procumbens is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks.
