Nasa picta subsp. pamparomasensis

Nasa picta subsp.

pamparomasensis, commonly known as the Pamparomasensis Marsilea, is a species of aquatic fern belonging to the family Marsileaceae. It is characterized by its small, floating leaves that are typically four-lobed and resemble a four-leaf clover.

The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and wetlands. It produces tiny, yellow flowers that are often submerged, followed by small, round fruits.

This subspecies is native to South America, particularly Argentina, and is valued for its ornamental appeal in aquatic gardens.
