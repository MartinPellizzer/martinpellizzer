Naegelia amabilis

Naegelia amabilis, commonly known as the sweet flag, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces a distinctive, sweet-smelling resin that has been used traditionally in various cultures. Naegelia amabilis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water sources.

It is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its historical use in medicinal and aromatic applications.
