Neobalanocarpus heimii

Neobalanocarpus heimii, commonly known as the Malayan ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, heavy timber, which is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. It produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits.

Neobalanocarpus heimii thrives in humid, lowland rainforest environments and is often found in secondary growth areas.
