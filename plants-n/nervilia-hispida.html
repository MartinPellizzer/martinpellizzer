Nervilia hispida

Nervilia hispida, commonly known as the hairy nerve plant, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically in forest understories.

Nervilia hispida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in horticulture for its unique foliage.
