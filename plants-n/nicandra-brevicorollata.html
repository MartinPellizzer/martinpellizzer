Nicandra brevicorollata

Nicandra brevicorollata, commonly known as the small-fruit nicandra, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 meter. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer, followed by spherical, green berries that turn red when ripe. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses.
