Nigella aspera

Nigella aspera, commonly known as the rough cowpea, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into elongated, spindle-shaped seed pods. Its seeds are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Nigella aspera thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.
