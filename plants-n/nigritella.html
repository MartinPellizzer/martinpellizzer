Nigritella

Nigritella, commonly known as the black-eyed Susan, is a genus of flowering plants in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers with dark centers and bright yellow or orange petals. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens. Nigritella species are native to various regions in Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

Its botanical features include alternate leaves and a composite flower head that consists of both ray and disk florets.
