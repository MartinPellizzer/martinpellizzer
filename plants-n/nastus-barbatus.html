Nastus barbatus

Nastus barbatus, commonly known as the bearded bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy, leafy stems and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Nastus barbatus is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
