Nephrodium cirrhosum

Nephrodium cirrhosum, commonly known as the velvet fern, is a unique aquatic plant found in freshwater environments.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety leaves that give it its common name. The plant has a rhizome that anchors it to the substrate and supports its growth. Its structure allows it to thrive in slow-moving or still water, where it can absorb nutrients efficiently.

Nephrodium cirrhosum plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and contributing to water quality maintenance.
