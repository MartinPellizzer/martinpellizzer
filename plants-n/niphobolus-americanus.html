Niphobolus americanus

Niphobolus americanus, commonly known as the American burrweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its rough, spiny fruits that resemble burrs, which aid in seed dispersal by attaching to animal fur. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils along stream banks and wetlands. It produces small, inconspicuous white or greenish flowers during the spring and summer months.

Niphobolus americanus is often considered a weed due to its aggressive growth and ability to thrive in various environmental conditions.
