Narcissus × albus

Narcissus × albus, commonly known as the white daffodil, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Narcissus cyclaminiflorus and Narcissus tazetta.

It is characterized by its striking white petals and a yellow corona, which distinguish it from other daffodil varieties. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and thrives in well-drained soil in full sun. It is native to the Iberian Peninsula and is valued for its ornamental beauty in gardens.

The flowers bloom in early spring, adding a fresh, elegant touch to floral displays.
