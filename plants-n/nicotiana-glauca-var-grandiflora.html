Nicotiana glauca var. grandiflora

Nicotiana glauca var. grandiflora, commonly known as the blueflower tobacco, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a bluish-gray hue. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet, blooming in summer. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental gardens and ecological restoration projects.
