Neuracanthus brachystachyus

Neuracanthus brachystachyus, commonly known as the short-stemmed flame flower, is a striking tropical plant characterized by its vibrant orange-red bracts that resemble flames.

It is native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America, where it thrives in warm, humid climates. The plant has a compact growth habit with short, sturdy stems and is known for its showy, long-lasting floral display. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

Neuracanthus brachystachyus is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
