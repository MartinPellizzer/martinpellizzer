Nicotiana suaveolens var. anisandra

Nicotiana suaveolens var. anisandra, commonly known as the sweet tobacco or anise-scented tobacco, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves that emit a distinct anise-like fragrance. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its botanical features make it a popular subject in both horticulture and botanical research.
