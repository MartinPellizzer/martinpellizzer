Nepeta camphorata var. heldreichii

Nepeta camphorata var. heldreichii, commonly known as the Balkan catmint, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It features aromatic, lance-shaped leaves with a strong mint-like scent, and its flowers are typically blue to violet, arranged in dense whorls. The plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its fragrance and ability to attract pollinators.

This variety is closely related to other Nepeta species and is sometimes confused with Nepeta cataria.
