Nematolepis elliptica

Nematolepis elliptica, commonly known as the narrowleaf lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to New Zealand.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its botanical characteristics include a fibrous root system and a tendency to form dense clumps.

Nematolepis elliptica is often found in damp, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.
