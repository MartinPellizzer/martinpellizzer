Nephelea polystichoides

Nephelea polystichoides, commonly known as the golden fern, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with a feathery appearance that adds to its ornamental value.

Nephelea polystichoides is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and is valued in horticulture for its unique coloration.
