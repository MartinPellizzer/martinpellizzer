Narcissus tazetta var. aureus

Narcissus tazetta var. aureus, commonly known as the golden daffodil, is a variety of the tazetta daffodil characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers.

It is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. The flowers typically bloom in early spring, featuring a cup-shaped corona surrounded by narrow, reflexed petals. This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its cheerful appearance.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
