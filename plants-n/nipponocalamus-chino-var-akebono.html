Nipponocalamus chino var. akebono

Nipponocalamus chino var. akebono, commonly known as the Akebono bamboo, is a variant of the Chinese bamboo species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, erect culms that are typically green and smooth, with a distinctive light green to almost white coloration. The plant produces small, clustered panicles of inconspicuous white flowers, which are followed by fluffy, grayish-brown seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for ornamental planting in gardens and landscapes.

This variety is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated for its unique visual characteristics.
