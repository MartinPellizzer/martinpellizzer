Narcissus turgidus

Narcissus turgidus, commonly known as the Giant Daffodil, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers with a distinctive orange or red corona. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with strap-shaped, grass-like leaves. Its bulbs are adapted for storage and regeneration, allowing it to thrive in well-drained soils.

The flowers bloom in spring, adding vibrant color to gardens and natural habitats.
