Nipponocalamus zifukuensis

Nipponocalamus zifukuensis, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo, is a species of grass in the Poaceae family.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its slender, cylindrical culms that grow in dense clusters. The plant typically reaches a height of about 1 to 2 meters and has narrow, linear leaves with a smooth surface. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Nipponocalamus zifukuensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
