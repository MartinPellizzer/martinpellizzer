Nerium oleander var. album

Nerium oleander var. album, commonly known as the white oleander, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, featuring dense, lance-shaped leaves and showy white flowers. The flowers are typically arranged in clusters and bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is highly drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The plant is also noted for its ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions, including salt spray and poor soils.
