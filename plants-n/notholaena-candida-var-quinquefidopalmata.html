Notholaena candida var. quinquefidopalmata

Notholaena candida var. quinquefidopalmata, commonly known as the white feather fern, is a variant of the white fern native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, arching fronds that are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a soft, feathery appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and along stream banks. Its rhizomes are relatively short and produce clusters of small, spore-bearing leaves.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant due to its elegant, lace-like foliage.
