Narcissus poeticus var. grandiflorus

Narcissus poeticus var. grandiflorus, commonly known as the giant daffodil, is a striking member of the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers with a prominent yellow corona and broad, lance-shaped leaves. Native to southern Europe, this variety thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight. The plant produces numerous bulbous stems and is known for its early spring blooming habit.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
