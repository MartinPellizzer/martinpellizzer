Nematuris volubilis

Nematuris volubilis, commonly known as the climbing bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and small, bladder-like structures that trap aquatic prey. The plant thrives in wet, nutrient-poor environments such as marshes and ponds. Its unique adaptations allow it to supplement its nutrient intake by digesting waterborne organisms.

Despite its carnivorous nature, it plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by controlling small invertebrate populations.
