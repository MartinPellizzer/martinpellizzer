Neomammillaria hahniana

Neomammillaria hahniana, commonly known as the Hahn's milkplant, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are covered in white to pale yellow tubercles, which give it a distinctive, spiny appearance. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in the spring. It is a slow-growing species that thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for desert gardens.

Neomammillaria hahniana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by succulent enthusiasts.
