Neillia millsii

Neillia millsii, commonly known as the Neillia millsii, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains in South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or alpine soils. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale pink in color. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Neillia millsii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable growing conditions.
