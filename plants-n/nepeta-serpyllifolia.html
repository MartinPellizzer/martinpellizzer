Nepeta serpyllifolia

Nepeta serpyllifolia, commonly known as mouse-ear catmint, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, fragrant blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has fuzzy, silver-gray leaves that are often mistaken for the common catmint, Nepeta cataria. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Nepeta serpyllifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
