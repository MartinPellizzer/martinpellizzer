Nymphaea jamesoniana

Nymphaea jamesoniana, commonly known as the Jameson's water lily, is a species of aquatic plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, floating leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes. Its roots are anchored in the sediment at the bottom of water bodies, while its leaves and flowers float on the water's surface.

The species plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
