Nepeta pumila

Nepeta pumila, commonly known as carpet bugleweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, dark green, oval-shaped leaves that are densely packed along its stems. The plant produces tiny, pale blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or as ground cover.

Nepeta pumila is known for its aromatic foliage and is often used in herbal remedies.
