Nepeta kitamurae

Nepeta kitamurae, commonly known as Japanese catmint, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs.

Nepeta kitamurae is often cultivated for its ornamental value and pleasant, mint-like fragrance.
