Nectusion reticulata

Nectusion reticulata, commonly known as the nettleleaf daisy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered yellow flowers. The plant has hairy, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine stinging hairs, similar to those found on true nettles. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open fields and along roadsides.

Nectusion reticulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
