Najas minor

Najas minor, commonly known as narrowleaf watermilfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It has slender, branching stems and small, whorled leaves that are typically less than 1 centimeter in length. The plant produces tiny, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Najas minor is often found in ponds, lakes, and slow-moving streams.
