Nymphaea mildbraedii

Nymphaea mildbraedii, commonly known as the Mildbraed's water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to the wetlands of southern Africa.

It features large, showy floating leaves that are typically a deep green with a waxy texture, and its flowers are notable for their vibrant pink to red hues. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often forming dense mats on the water surface. Its roots anchor it in the muddy substrate, while its rhizomes spread horizontally to support growth.

Nymphaea mildbraedii plays an important ecological role by providing habitat for aquatic life and contributing to water filtration.
