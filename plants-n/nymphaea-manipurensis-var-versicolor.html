Nymphaea manipurensis var. versicolor

Nymphaea manipurensis var. versicolor, commonly known as the variable water lily, is a water plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that come in a range of colors, including white, pink, and red. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats, such as ponds and lakes. Its flowers open in the morning and close in the afternoon, a behavior known as nyctinasty.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
