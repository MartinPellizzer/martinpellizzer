Notocactus pulvinatus

Notocactus pulvinatus, commonly known as the pulvinated notocactus, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, flattened stems that form a rosette-like structure. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil with plenty of sunlight.

Its unique morphology makes it a popular choice for both ornamental and botanical collections.
