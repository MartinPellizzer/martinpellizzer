Narcissus tazetta var. algericus

Narcissus tazetta var. algericus, commonly known as the Algerian daffodil, is a bulbous perennial plant native to North Africa.

It produces clusters of fragrant, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has narrow, strap-like leaves that emerge from a basal rosette. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often used in floral arrangements.
