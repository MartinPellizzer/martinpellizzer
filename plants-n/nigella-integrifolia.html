Nigella integrifolia

Nigella integrifolia, commonly known as love-in-a-mist, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and spikes of delicate blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and thrives in well-drained soil. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Nigella integrifolia is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and its subtle, spicy fragrance.
