Nicotiana pseudodecurrens

Nicotiana pseudodecurrens, commonly known as the narrowleaf tobacco, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family.

It is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are typically narrow and have a bluish-green hue. Its flowers are tubular and usually pink or purple, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking appearance and resilience to harsh conditions.
