Nuphar japonica var. rubrotincta

Nuphar japonica var. rubrotincta, commonly known as the red-stained water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to Japan.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves that are typically green with a reddish tinge along the veins. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that bloom above the water surface, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its rhizomes are thick and fleshy, allowing it to thrive in still or slow-moving freshwater environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland habitats.
