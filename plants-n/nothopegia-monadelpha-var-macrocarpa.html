Nothopegia monadelpha var. macrocarpa

Nothopegia monadelpha var. macrocarpa, commonly known as the giant flame tree, is a striking tropical shrub or small tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy, flame-colored flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in the summer months. The plant has pinnate leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets, and its bark is smooth and greyish in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also noted for its ability to produce large, edible fruits that are consumed by various wildlife species.
