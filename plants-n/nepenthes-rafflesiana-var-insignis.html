Nepenthes rafflesiana var. insignis

Nepenthes rafflesiana var. insignis, commonly known as the Raffles' pitcher plant, is a tropical vine native to the rainforests of Borneo.

It is characterized by its large, pitcher-shaped leaves that secrete a nutrient-rich liquid to trap and digest insects. The plant's striking red and yellow floral colors make it visually distinctive. It thrives in high humidity and requires well-drained, acidic soil for optimal growth.

This variety is highly sought after for its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
