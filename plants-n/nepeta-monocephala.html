Nepeta monocephala

Nepeta monocephala, commonly known as catnip, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and clusters of small, tubular blue or purple flowers. The plant contains nepetalactone, a compound responsible for its intoxicating effect on cats. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in rocky or dry habitats.

Nepeta monocephala is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in herbal remedies.
