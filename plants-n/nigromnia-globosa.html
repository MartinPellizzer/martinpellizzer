Nigromnia globosa

Nigromnia globosa, commonly known as the globe thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spherical, spiny seed heads that resemble small globes, giving it its common name. The plant produces blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Nigromnia globosa is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and drought tolerance.
