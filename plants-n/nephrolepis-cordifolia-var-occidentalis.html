Nephrolepis cordifolia var. occidentalis

Nephrolepis cordifolia var. occidentalis, commonly known as the Western maidenhair fern, is a variant of the maidenhair fern native to the western United States.

It features delicate, arching fronds with a distinctive heart-shaped outline, giving it its scientific name. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread and form dense colonies. Its foliage is typically dark green and glossy, with a feathery texture that contributes to its ornamental appeal.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often used in woodland gardens or as a ground cover.
