Narcissus hispanicus subsp. hispanicus

Narcissus hispanicus subsp.

hispanicus, commonly known as the Spanish daffodil, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Europe. It produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange, typically blooming in early spring.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is well adapted to Mediterranean climates. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Amaryllidaceae family.
