Narcissus papyraceus subsp. polyanthos

Narcissus papyraceus subsp.

polyanthos, commonly known as the Persian daffodil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Turkey and the Caucasus. It is characterized by its narrow, strap-like leaves and a single, showy flower with a trumpet-shaped corona.

The plant produces a large, white or pale yellow flower with a distinctive, often greenish, central cup. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its early spring blooms.
