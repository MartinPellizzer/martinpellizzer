Noccaea dacica subsp. dacica

Noccaea dacica subsp.

dacica, commonly known as the Balkan watercress, is a perennial herb native to southeastern Europe. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils along streambanks and wet meadows.

The plant features slender, upright stems with alternate, pinnately divided leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its small, white to pale yellow flowers are arranged in racemes and bloom in early summer.

The species is valued for its peppery flavor and is often used in traditional cuisine and herbal remedies.
