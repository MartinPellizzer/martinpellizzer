Narcissus grandiflorus

Narcissus grandiflorus, commonly known as the large-flowered daffodil, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and western Asia, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, often with a white or green throat. Its foliage consists of strap-like leaves that are broad and grass-like in appearance.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is commonly cultivated in gardens.
