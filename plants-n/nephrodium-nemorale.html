Nephrodium nemorale

Nephrodium nemorale, commonly known as the forest fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Nephrodiaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, lance-shaped fronds that are often divided into smaller, serrated segments. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forests and woodland areas. Its reproductive structures include sporangia that produce spores, which are essential for its propagation.

The plant's unique morphology and ecological preferences make it an important component of its native habitat.
