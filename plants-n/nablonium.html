Nablonium

Nablonium, commonly known as the golden vine, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its long, trailing stems and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by animals. Nablonium thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil to grow successfully.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for hanging baskets and trellises in gardens.
