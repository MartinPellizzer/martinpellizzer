Nania grisii

Nania grisii, commonly known as the gray mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, grayish-white bloom, giving it its common name. Its small, white to pale blue flowers appear in clusters during the late spring and early summer.

Nania grisii is valued in native plant gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to local wildlife.
