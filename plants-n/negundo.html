Negundo

Negundo, commonly known as the box elder.

It is a deciduous tree native to North America, belonging to the Hippocastanaceae family. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its bark is smooth and grayish in appearance. It produces small, yellowish flowers in catkins, followed by samaras as its fruit.

The plant is often used in landscaping due to its fast growth and adaptability to various soil types.
