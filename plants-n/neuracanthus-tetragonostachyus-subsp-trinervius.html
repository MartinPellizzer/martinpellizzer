Neuracanthus tetragonostachyus subsp. trinervius

Neuracanthus tetragonostachyus subsp.

trinervius, commonly known as the three-veined flameflower, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its striking, tubular red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees.

The plant has lance-shaped, opposite leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique floral structure and its role in local ecosystems.
