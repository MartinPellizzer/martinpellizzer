Nepenthes bernaysii

Nepenthes bernaysii, commonly known as the Bernays' pitcher plant, is a carnivorous plant native to Borneo.

It is characterized by its unique, elongated pitcher-shaped traps that secrete a sticky fluid to trap prey. The plant's leaves are typically dark green with a waxy coating, and the pitchers can grow up to 30 centimeters in height. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires high levels of atmospheric moisture.

The species is notable for its distinctive coloration and is a subject of interest in botanical research.
