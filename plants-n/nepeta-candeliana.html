Nepeta candeliana

Nepeta candeliana, commonly known as catnip, is a flowering plant in the mint family (Lamiaceae).

It is native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant blue or purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves contain nepetalactone, a compound that affects the behavior of cats.

Nepeta candeliana is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and its historical use in herbal remedies.
