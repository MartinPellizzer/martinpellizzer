Neochilenia pygmaea

Neochilenia pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf neochilenia, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or gravelly soils. The plant features delicate, narrow leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually white or pale in color. It is adapted to cold and arid conditions, showcasing resilience to harsh mountain climates.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
