Nanorrhinum khuzestanicum

Nanorrhinum khuzestanicum, commonly known as the Khuzestan honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern regions of Iran.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and covered with fine hairs, while its fruits are small, berry-like structures that contain several seeds.

This species is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique adaptations to its native habitat.
