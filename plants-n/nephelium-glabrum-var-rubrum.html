Nephelium glabrum var. rubrum

Nephelium glabrum var. rubrum, commonly known as the red rambutan, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, spiny fruits that turn a striking red color when mature. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by the development of its distinctive, hairy fruits. The leaves are simple, alternate, and ovate, with a glossy green surface.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also cultivated for its edible fruit, which has a sweet, hairy exterior and a soft, white, juicy pulp.
