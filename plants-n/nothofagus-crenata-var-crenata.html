Nothofagus crenata var. crenata

Nothofagus crenata var. crenata, commonly known as the silver beech, is a species of evergreen tree native to southern South America.

It belongs to the Nothofagaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, silver-gray bark and lanceolate, glossy leaves. The plant typically grows in temperate rainforests, thriving in moist, cool climates with high humidity. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by edible, oval-shaped nuts.

This variety is notable for its ecological importance, providing habitat and food for various native wildlife species.
