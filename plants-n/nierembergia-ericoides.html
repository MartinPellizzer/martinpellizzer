Nierembergia ericoides

Nierembergia ericoides, commonly known as the silverleaf bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub with silver-gray foliage that reflects light and helps reduce water loss. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white, typically blooming in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Nierembergia ericoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
