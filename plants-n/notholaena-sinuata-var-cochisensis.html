Notholaena sinuata var. cochisensis

Notholaena sinuata var. cochisensis, commonly known as the Cochise velvet fern, is a variant of the velvet fern species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage that forms a low, mat-like growth habit. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are clustered in loose inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

This variety is particularly adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in harsh conditions.
