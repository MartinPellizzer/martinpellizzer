Narcissus × travancaensis

Narcissus × travancaensis, commonly known as the Travancore daffodil, is a hybrid species native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers with a white center, often resembling the classic daffodil. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30–50 cm, with narrow, grass-like leaves. It blooms in spring, producing 1–3 flower buds per stem.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant and enduring blooms.
