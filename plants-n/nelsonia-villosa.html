Nelsonia villosa

Nelsonia villosa, commonly known as the silky Nelsonia, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves that give it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
