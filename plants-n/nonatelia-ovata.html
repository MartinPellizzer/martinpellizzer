Nonatelia ovata

Nonatelia ovata, commonly known as the oval-leaf nonatelia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain regions of South America.

It features elongated, ovate leaves that are typically arranged alternately along its stem. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are often white or pale yellow in color. Its botanical structure includes a fibrous root system and a branching stem that supports its foliage.

Nonatelia ovata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
