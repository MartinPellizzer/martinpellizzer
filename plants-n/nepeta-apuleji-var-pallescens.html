Nepeta apuleji var. pallescens

Nepeta apuleji var. pallescens, commonly known as the pale catmint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It features aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces spikes of pale blue to violet flowers in the summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

Its hardy nature and ornamental value make it a popular garden plant.
