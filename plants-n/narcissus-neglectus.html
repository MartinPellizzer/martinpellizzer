Narcissus neglectus

Narcissus neglectus, commonly known as the wild daffodil, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its foliage consists of long, strap-like leaves that emerge in early spring.
