Nectandra abortiens

Nectandra abortiens, commonly known as the Guianese sandwood, is a tropical tree native to South America, particularly found in Guyana and Suriname.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, fragrant wood that is highly valued for its use in furniture and construction. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a smooth upper surface. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by oval-shaped fruits containing a single seed.

Due to its ecological importance and limited distribution, Nectandra abortiens is considered a species of conservation concern.
