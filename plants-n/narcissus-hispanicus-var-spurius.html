Narcissus hispanicus var. spurius

Narcissus hispanicus var. spurius, commonly known as the Spanish daffodil, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically yellow with a white or green center. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is valued for its early spring bloom and ornamental appeal.
