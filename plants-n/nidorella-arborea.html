Nidorella arborea

Nidorella arborea, commonly known as the tree orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers, which often have a delicate, sweet scent, are usually pale yellow or greenish-white.

Nidorella arborea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique appearance and fragrance.
