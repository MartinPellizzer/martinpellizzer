Neonauclea Cordatula

Neonauclea cordatula is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the humid forests of Southeast Asia. It is known for its heart-shaped leaves and striking purple blooms that appear during the wet season. This species thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests. Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is now classified as endangered by conservation organizations. Its unique appearance and ecological significance make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists alike.
