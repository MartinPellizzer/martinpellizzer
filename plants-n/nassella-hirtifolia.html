Nassella hirtifolia

Nassella hirtifolia, commonly known as hairy needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and dense, hairy foliage that gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of spikelets, which are often tinged with red or purple.

Nassella hirtifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in arid conditions.
