Narcissus cavanillesii

Narcissus cavanillesii, commonly known as the Canary Islands daffodil, is a species of flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its striking yellow petals and a single, upright, trumpet-shaped corona. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal environments, adapting to the unique climate of its native habitat. It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is closely related to other daffodil species.

Narcissus cavanillesii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
