Narcissus tazetta subsp. patulus

Narcissus tazetta subsp.

patulus, commonly known as the Persian daffodil, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family. It is native to the mountains of Central Asia and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to yellow.

The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and produces clusters of 4–6 flowers per stem. Its foliage consists of strap-like leaves that are grassy in appearance.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its early spring blooms.
