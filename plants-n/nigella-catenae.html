Nigella catenae

Nigella catenae, commonly known as the chain mustard, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Brassicaceae.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and linear, pinnately compound leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia. Its seeds are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

Nigella catenae is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
