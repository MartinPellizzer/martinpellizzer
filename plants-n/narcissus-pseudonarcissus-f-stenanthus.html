Narcissus pseudonarcissus f. stenanthus

Narcissus pseudonarcissus f. stenanthus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved daffodil, is a botanical variant of the common daffodil.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and a single, elongated flower with a yellow corona. The plant is native to southern Europe and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens.

This form is a natural mutation within the species, showcasing genetic diversity in the genus Narcissus.
