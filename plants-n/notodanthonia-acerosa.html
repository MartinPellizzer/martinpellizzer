Notodanthonia acerosa

Notodanthonia acerosa, commonly known as the hard-leaved grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its rigid, wiry leaves and compact growth habit, often forming dense, low-lying mats. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are often clustered in small, compact clusters.

Notodanthonia acerosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native landscaping.
