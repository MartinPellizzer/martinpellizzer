Nymphaea gladstoniana

Nymphaea gladstoniana, commonly known as Gladstone's water lily, is a species of aquatic plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats, such as ponds and lakes. Its roots are anchored in the mud at the bottom of the water body, while its leaves and flowers remain above the surface.

Nymphaea gladstoniana plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
