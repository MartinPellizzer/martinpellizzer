Nectandra acuminata

Nectandra acuminata, commonly known as the fragrant sandarac tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic, resinous wood. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by small, round fruits. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a glossy surface.

Nectandra acuminata is valued for its timber and has traditional uses in local medicine and as a source of natural resins.
