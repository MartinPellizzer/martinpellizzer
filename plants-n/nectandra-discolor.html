Nectandra discolor

Nectandra discolor, commonly known as the pink sandalwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its aromatic, dark-colored wood. The plant produces small, white flowers that are followed by oval, woody fruits containing smooth, glossy seeds. Its leaves are typically elliptical and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Nectandra discolor is valued for its timber and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
