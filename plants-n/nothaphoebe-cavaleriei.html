Nothaphoebe cavaleriei

Nothaphoebe cavaleriei, commonly known as the Chinese nothaphoebe, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers arranged in clusters, followed by dark blue berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
