Nicotiana monticola

Nicotiana monticola, commonly known as the mountain tobacco, is a species of flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is a herbaceous plant that typically grows in rocky or open habitats, often at elevations above 3,000 meters. The plant produces striking purple or violet flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, and the plant is known for its aromatic properties.
