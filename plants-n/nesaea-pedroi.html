Nesaea pedroi

Nesaea pedroi, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or limestone habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid and nutrient-poor soils. The plant features a slender stem with lance-shaped leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and purple. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it is often found in open, sunny environments.

Nesaea pedroi is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
