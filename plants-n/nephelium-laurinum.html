Nephelium laurinum

Nephelium laurinum, commonly known as the rambutan, is a tropical fruit-bearing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, spiny exterior that resembles a hairy ball. The plant produces small, round fruits with a sweet, aromatic pulp that contains edible seeds. Its growth is typically found in humid, warm climates with well-drained soil.

The rambutan is valued not only for its unique appearance but also for its nutritional content and commercial importance in global markets.
