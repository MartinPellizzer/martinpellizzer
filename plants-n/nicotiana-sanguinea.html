Nicotiana sanguinea

Nicotiana sanguinea, commonly known as the bloodflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking red or purple flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling small bells. The plant belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is known for its ornamental value in gardens. Its leaves are typically broad and hairy, with a strong, aromatic scent.

Nicotiana sanguinea is also cultivated for its potential medicinal properties and as a source of nicotine.
