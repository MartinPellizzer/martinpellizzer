Nanodea muscosa

Nanodea muscosa, commonly known as the mossy nanodea, is a small, mat-forming plant that thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It belongs to the family Pottiaceae and is characterized by its dense, cushion-like growth habit. The plant has short, branching stems covered with small, closely packed leaves that are often curved or rolled. Its reproductive structures, known as capsules, are borne on stalks and release spores when mature.
