Nesaea schinzii var. subalata

Nesaea schinzii var. subalata, commonly known as the Alpine bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in high-altitude meadows.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
