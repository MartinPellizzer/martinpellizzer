Nectandra leucantha

Nectandra leucantha, commonly known as white nectandra, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its wood is valued for its fine grain and is used in furniture and musical instruments.

The species also plays a role in local ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
