Nicotiana cordifolia subsp. cordifolia

Nicotiana cordifolia subsp.

cordifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf tobacco, is a herbaceous plant native to South America. It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

The species is also used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
