Namaquanula bruynsii

Namaquanula bruynsii, commonly known as the Cape mountain catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the Western Cape region of South Africa.

It is a small annual herb with slender, branched stems and small, hairy leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Namaquanula bruynsii is adapted to the arid and semi-arid conditions of its native environment.
