Nycteranthus dinteri

Nycteranthus dinteri, commonly known as the night-flowering cereus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and striking, white, night-blooming flowers. The plant thrives in arid environments and is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and limited water availability. Its flowers emit a strong fragrance to attract pollinators such as moths and bats.

Nycteranthus dinteri is a member of the Cactaceae family and is often cultivated for its unique appearance and nocturnal blooming habit.
