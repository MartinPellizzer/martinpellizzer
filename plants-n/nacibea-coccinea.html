Nacibea coccinea

Nacibea coccinea, commonly known as the red hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red-orange flowers, which are typically five-petaled and resemble those of the hibiscus genus. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Nacibea coccinea thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some cultures.
