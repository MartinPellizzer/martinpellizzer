Nephrolepis auriculata

Nephrolepis auriculata, commonly known as the hairy shield fern, is a species of fern native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, shield-shaped fronds that are covered in fine, hairy scales, giving it a unique appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its fronds are pinnately compound, with a broad, upright growth habit.

Nephrolepis auriculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
