Nipponocalamus nakashimae var. supraglaber

Nipponocalamus nakashimae var. supraglaber, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or mountain bamboo, is a variant of the Nipponocalamus genus native to Japan.

It is a clumping bamboo that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in mountainous regions. The plant has slender, hollow stems that are densely packed, forming a compact growth habit. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth, glabrous surface, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and as a landscape plant.
