Nicotiana macrophylla

Nicotiana macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaved tobacco, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is closely related to the tobacco plant.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens for its striking foliage and floral display.
