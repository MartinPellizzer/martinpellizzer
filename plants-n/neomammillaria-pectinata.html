Neomammillaria pectinata

Neomammillaria pectinata, commonly known as the Pectinata Cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a small, globular cactus with a ribbed structure and spines that are typically white to yellowish in color. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water and well-draining soil.

Neomammillaria pectinata is often grown as a decorative houseplant due to its compact size and attractive appearance.
