Nymphaea × omarana

Nymphaea × omarana, commonly known as the blue lotus, is a hybrid water lily native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep blue, depending on the stage of bloom. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes. Its broad, heart-shaped leaves float on the water surface, while its roots anchor in the muddy substrate.

The blue lotus is also valued for its ornamental beauty and has cultural significance in various traditional practices.
