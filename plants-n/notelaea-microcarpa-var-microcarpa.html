Notelaea microcarpa var. microcarpa

Notelaea microcarpa var. microcarpa, commonly known as the small-fruited notelaea, is a species native to the tropical regions of northern Australia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that typically grows in coastal or near-coastal areas, often in sandy soils. The plant features smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine, white hairs. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are followed by round, fleshy fruits that are edible and attract various wildlife.

This variety is notable for its ecological importance in supporting local fauna and its adaptation to harsh coastal environments.
