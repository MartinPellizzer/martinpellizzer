Neolitsea cassia

Neolitsea cassia, commonly known as the golden osier, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its finely toothed, ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by elongated, woody fruits. Neolitsea cassia thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in temperate climates.
