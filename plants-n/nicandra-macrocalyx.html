Nicandra macrocalyx

Nicandra macrocalyx, commonly known as the sweet scent vine, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and heart-shaped, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. Its fruit is a spherical, fleshy berry that turns red when mature.

Nicandra macrocalyx is often cultivated for its ornamental value and distinctive aroma.
