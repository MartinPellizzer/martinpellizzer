Nassella landbeckii var. eremophila

Nassella landbeckii var. eremophila, commonly known as the desert needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and dense, tufted growth habit, which helps it survive in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. Its deep root system allows it to access water from deeper soil layers, making it highly drought-tolerant.

This species plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
