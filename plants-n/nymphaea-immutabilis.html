Nymphaea immutabilis

Nymphaea immutabilis, commonly known as the Immortal Water Lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy, white flowers that remain open for an extended period, often throughout the day. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes. Its leaves are broad and floating, providing a habitat for various aquatic organisms.

The species is noted for its resilience and ability to tolerate a range of water conditions.
