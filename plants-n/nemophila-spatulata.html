Nemophila spatulata

Nemophila spatulata, commonly known as the spoonleaf bluebell, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Boraginaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant produces small, sky-blue to purple flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are ovate to spoon-shaped, giving it its common name.
