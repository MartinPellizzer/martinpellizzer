Nephrodium amurense

Nephrodium amurense, commonly known as the Amur nephrolepis, is a fern native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, leathery, and often deeply lobed fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks. Its reproductive structures include sporangia that produce spores for propagation.

The fronds are often arranged in a spiral pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
