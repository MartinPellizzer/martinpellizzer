Nephrodium karwinskyanum

Nephrodium karwinskyanum, commonly known as the Karwinsky's nephroid, is a rare and ancient fern-like plant with a unique botanical structure.

It features a creeping rhizome that produces small, spirally arranged fronds with a distinctive bifurcated shape. The plant's leaves are typically narrow and have a leathery texture, with a prominent midrib running the length of each frond. It is characterized by its primitive reproductive structures, including sporangia that are clustered in sori on the underside of the fronds.

This plant is often found in moist, shaded environments and is of particular interest to botanists due to its evolutionary significance.
