Narcissus triandrus subsp. loiseleurii

Narcissus triandrus subsp.

loiseleurii, commonly known as the Loiseleurii daffodil, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Europe. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers with a bright yellow corona.

The flowers typically bloom in early spring, adding a vibrant splash of color to gardens. This subspecies is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its delicate appearance.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
