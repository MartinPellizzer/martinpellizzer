Notocactus ottonis var. megapotamicus

Notocactus ottonis var. megapotamicus, commonly known as the Megapotamic Candelabra Cactus, is a unique succulent native to the dry forests of Argentina and Bolivia.

It features a tall, branching stem that can grow up to several meters in height, with cylindrical, ribbed segments that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers during the summer months, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its distinctive appearance and slow growth make it a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts and collectors.
