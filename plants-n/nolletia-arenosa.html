Nolletia arenosa

Nolletia arenosa, commonly known as the sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is a low-growing perennial with small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or purple. The plant has adaptations such as succulent leaves and deep roots to survive in dry environments. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in disturbed areas.

Nolletia arenosa plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing nectar for pollinators.
