Nectandra sordida

Nectandra sordida, commonly known as the black nectandra, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its dark, aromatic bark and glossy, elliptical leaves. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by dark, fleshy fruits.

Nectandra sordida is valued for its timber and traditional medicinal uses in the regions where it grows.
