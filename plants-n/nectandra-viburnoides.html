Nectandra viburnoides

Nectandra viburnoides, commonly known as the silverleaf nectandra, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by berries that range in color from red to black. Its wood is valued for its fine grain and is used in furniture-making and carving.

The species is also appreciated in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
