Neocinnamomum complanifructum

Neocinnamomum complanifructum, commonly known as the flat-fruited cinnamon tree, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of China, Vietnam, and Cambodia. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruits are small, round, and initially green, turning red when mature.

The tree is valued for its essential oils and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
