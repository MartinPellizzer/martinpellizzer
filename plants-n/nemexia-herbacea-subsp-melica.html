Nemexia herbacea subsp. melica

Nemexia herbacea subsp.

melica, commonly known as the narrow-leaved nemexia, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

The plant has slender, hairy stems and linear, grass-like leaves that are usually less than 5 cm in length. Its small, yellowish flowers are clustered in loose panicles and bloom during the spring and summer months.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities.
