Nivenia sceptrum-gustavianum

Nivenia sceptrum-gustavianum, commonly known as the Gustaviana beardtongue, is a perennial plant native to the high elevations of the Andes in South America.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense clusters of violet to purple flowers. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces tubular flowers that are adapted for pollination by hummingbirds. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
