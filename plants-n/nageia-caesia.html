Nageia caesia

Nageia caesia, commonly known as the silver fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its needle-like leaves, which are typically gray-green in color and have a waxy coating. The tree produces small, spherical cones that contain seeds, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Nageia caesia is often found in mixed forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.

It plays a significant role in supporting local wildlife and maintaining forest biodiversity.
