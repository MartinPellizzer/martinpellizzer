Nordmannia cordifolia

Nordmannia cordifolia, commonly known as the Caucasian fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its tall, narrow trunk and dense, pyramidal crown. The tree has needle-like leaves that are dark green and arranged in spirals along the branches. Its cones are typically small and woody, containing seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
