Nyctocalos shanica

Nyctocalos shanica, commonly known as the moonflower of the Andes, is a climbing plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, white, nocturnal flowers that open at night and close by dawn. The plant belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is known for its fragrant blooms. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered in a fine, silvery hair.

Nyctocalos shanica is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its unique flowering behavior.
