Nymphaea kalmiana

Nymphaea kalmiana, commonly known as Kalm's water lily, is a species of water lily native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves and showy, white to pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes. Its leaves are typically round with a waxy surface, and its flowers have a distinct, recurved appearance.

Nymphaea kalmiana plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
