Najas marina subsp. sumatrana

Najas marina subsp.

sumatrana, commonly known as the Sumatran water milfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia. It belongs to the family Najadaceae and is characterized by its whorled arrangement of linear leaves.

The plant typically grows in freshwater habitats such as ponds, lakes, and slow-moving streams. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged, followed by the development of elongated, ribbon-like fruits.

This species plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
