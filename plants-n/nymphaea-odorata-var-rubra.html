Nymphaea odorata var. rubra

Nymphaea odorata var. rubra, commonly known as the red water lily, is a perennial aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features striking red flowers that bloom above the water surface, with broad, heart-shaped leaves that float on the water. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine, and it plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various organisms.

The species is also valued for its ornamental beauty in water gardens.
