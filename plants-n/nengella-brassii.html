Nengella brassii

Nengella brassii, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange tubular flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often arranged in a rosette pattern.

Nengella brassii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
