Nasa ranunculifolia subsp. macrantha

Nasa ranunculifolia subsp.

macrantha, commonly known as the "snowy nasa," is a rare and striking plant native to the Andean regions of South America. It is characterized by its large, showy white or pale pink flowers that resemble snowflakes, which bloom in early spring.

The plant has smooth, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, silvery down. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and unique appearance, it is a subject of interest for botanists and horticulturists alike.
