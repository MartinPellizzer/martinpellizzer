Nemastylis medusa

Nemastylis medusa, commonly known as the sea anemone-like plant, is a unique species belonging to the family Nemastylidaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, radially symmetrical flowers that resemble those of sea anemones, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in aquatic or semi-aquatic environments, thriving in moist, shaded areas. Its flowers consist of a central column surrounded by whorls of bracts, which are often brightly colored.

The botanical structure of Nemastylis medusa reflects its adaptation to wet habitats, with specialized tissues for water retention and gas exchange.
