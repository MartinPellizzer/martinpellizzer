Nastus latifolius

Nastus latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf nasturtium, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and vibrant yellow or orange flowers. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible parts, including the leaves and seeds. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Nastus latifolius is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
