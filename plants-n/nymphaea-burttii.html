Nymphaea burttii

Nymphaea burttii, commonly known as Burtt's water lily, is a species of aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, floating leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter. The plant produces striking white or pink flowers that bloom in the early morning and close in the afternoon. Its roots are anchored in the mud at the bottom of still water bodies.

Nymphaea burttii plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various water organisms.
