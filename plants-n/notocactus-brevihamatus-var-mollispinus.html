Notocactus brevihamatus var. mollispinus

Notocactus brevihamatus var. mollispinus, commonly known as the Mexican moon cactus, is a unique cacti species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, rounded shape and vibrant, colorful flowers that bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant has a distinct two-part structure, with a swollen, colorful upper portion known as the 'pseudostem' and a smaller, green, leaf-like base. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright sunlight, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.

Due to its striking appearance and relatively easy care, it is often used in decorative gardening and as a statement plant.
