Nengella affinis

Nengella affinis, commonly known as the narrowleaf nengella, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically pale purple or blue, arranged in whorled clusters along the stems.
