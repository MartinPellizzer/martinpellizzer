Nasturtium tanacetifolium

Nasturtium tanacetifolium, commonly known as the leafy nasturtium, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, dark green leaves and clusters of bright yellow or orange flowers. The plant is often cultivated for its edible leaves and flowers, which have a peppery taste. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Its botanical name reflects its resemblance to the tanacetum genus, which includes tansy and other similar plants.
