Narcissus × susannae nothovar. montielanus

Narcissus × susannae nothovar. montielanus, commonly known as the Montielanus daffodil, is a hybrid cultivar characterized by its striking, large, and showy flowers.

It features a single, upright bloom with a white perianth and a prominent yellow corona, typical of the genus Narcissus. The plant is known for its robust growth and ability to thrive in well-drained soils, making it suitable for garden cultivation. It blooms in early spring, adding a vibrant touch to gardens with its elegant appearance.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often used in floral arrangements and landscape design.
