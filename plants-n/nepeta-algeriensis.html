Nepeta algeriensis

Nepeta algeriensis, commonly known as Algerian catmint, is a perennial herb native to North Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Nepeta algeriensis is also valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
