Norantea gracilis

Norantea gracilis, commonly known as the slender orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, strap-like leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pale pink. The plant typically grows up to 30 centimeters in height, with a slender stem that supports its elegant, pendulous blooms. Norantea gracilis thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its unique appearance and subtle fragrance make it a prized ornamental plant among botanists and horticulturists.
