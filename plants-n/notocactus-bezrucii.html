Notocactus bezrucii

Notocactus bezrucii, commonly known as the Baja California cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Baja California, Mexico.

It is a low-growing, sprawling cactus with cylindrical stems that are often ribbed and covered in spines. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers during the spring and summer months. Its adaptations include thick, fleshy stems that store water and a shallow root system to maximize water absorption.

Notocactus bezrucii is valued for its hardiness and ability to thrive in harsh desert conditions.
