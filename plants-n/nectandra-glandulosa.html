Nectandra glandulosa

Nectandra glandulosa, commonly known as the fragrant wood or wild cinnamon, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences, followed by dark purple, fleshy berries. Its wood is valued for its fragrance and is used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.

Nectandra glandulosa thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland tropical forests.
