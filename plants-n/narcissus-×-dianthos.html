Narcissus × dianthos

Narcissus × dianthos, commonly known as the Dutch hybrid narcissus, is a botanical hybrid resulting from the cross between Narcissus tazetta and Narcissus poeticus.

It is characterized by its compact, rounded bulbs and is typically grown as a hardy perennial in temperate climates. The plant produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in a range of colors, including white, yellow, and orange, often with a distinct central blotch. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking early spring blooms.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation, it is widely cultivated in gardens and floral arrangements.
