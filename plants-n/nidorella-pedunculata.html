Nidorella pedunculata

Nidorella pedunculata, commonly known as the slender orchid, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. The plant features a slender, upright stem with small, lance-shaped leaves arranged alternately. Its flowers are clustered in inflorescences and are characterized by their delicate, fragrant blooms.

Nidorella pedunculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
