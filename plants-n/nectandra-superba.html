Nectandra superba

Nectandra superba, commonly known as the golden sandalwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its aromatic, reddish-brown heartwood. The plant produces small, white flowers that are typically clustered in panicles, followed by elliptical, leathery fruits. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a glossy surface with a strong, spicy scent.

The tree is valued for its durable wood and is often found in lowland rainforests.
