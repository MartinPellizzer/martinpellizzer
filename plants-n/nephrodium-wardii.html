Nephrodium wardii

Nephrodium wardii, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a member of the Araceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy, white flowers that open at night, emitting a sweet fragrance to attract pollinators. The plant has a short, thick rhizome and produces heart-shaped leaves with a glossy surface. Its inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by a spathe, typical of the Araceae family.

Nephrodium wardii thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland or swampy areas.
