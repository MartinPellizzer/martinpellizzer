Nephrodium prolificum

Nephrodium prolificum, commonly known as the prolific nephroid, is a rare and unique fern-like plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, kidney-shaped fronds that are often adorned with intricate patterns of sori, which are the spore-producing structures. The plant's botanical structure includes a short, creeping rhizome that supports its upright fronds. Its reproductive cycle relies on the dispersal of spores, which are released from the sori during the wet season.

Due to its limited habitat and specific environmental requirements, Nephrodium prolificum is considered a species of conservation concern.
