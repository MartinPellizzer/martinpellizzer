Nitelium rubescens

Nitelium rubescens, commonly known as the red velvet fern, is a unique aquatic plant characterized by its vibrant red coloration.

It thrives in shallow, freshwater environments with high light exposure and nutrient-rich substrates. The plant's leaves are broad and fleshy, adapted to retain moisture in its often fluctuating aquatic habitat. Nitelium rubescens reproduces through spores, which are released into the water and germinate under favorable conditions.

Its striking appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental ponds and botanical gardens.
