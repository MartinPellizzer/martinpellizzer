Narcissus similis

Narcissus similis, commonly known as the Spanish daffodil, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and a single, showy, trumpet-shaped flower. The plant typically blooms in early spring, producing bright yellow petals with a white or yellow throat. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Narcissus similis is also noted for its resistance to pests and diseases, contributing to its ease of cultivation.
