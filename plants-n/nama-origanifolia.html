Nama origanifolia

Nama origanifolia, commonly known as mountain germander, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic plants such as mint and sage. The plant is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, tubular flowers are typically pink or purple and are arranged in dense spikes.

Nama origanifolia is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
