Nepenthes chaniana

Nepenthes chaniana, commonly known as the Bornean pitcher plant, is a tropical carnivorous plant native to Borneo.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated pitcher-shaped leaves that secrete a digestive fluid to trap and digest insects. The plant's pitchers are typically green or reddish in color and can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires specific conditions to flourish.

Nepenthes chaniana is also noted for its ability to hybridize with other Nepenthes species, contributing to its genetic diversity.
