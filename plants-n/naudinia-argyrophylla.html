Naudinia argyrophylla

Naudinia argyrophylla, commonly known as silverleaf naudinia, is a rare shrub native to the island of Rodrigues in the Indian Ocean.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name, and its small, fragrant white flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is typically found in coastal or limestone areas.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and is protected in its native region.
