Nasturtium coronopifolium var. pumilum

Nasturtium coronopifolium var. pumilum, commonly known as the dwarf nasturtium, is a compact, annual plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It features heart-shaped, bright green leaves with a slightly hairy texture and produces striking, edible orange or red flowers. The plant is known for its peppery, tangy flavor, making it popular in culinary uses. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, often growing in rocky or alpine environments.

Its ornamental value and edible qualities make it a versatile addition to gardens and herbal collections.
