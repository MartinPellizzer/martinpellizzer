Nepenthes ampullaria var. vittata-major

Nepenthes ampullaria var. vittata-major, commonly known as the Vittata-Major Pitcher Plant, is a carnivorous plant native to the tropical rainforests of Borneo.

It is characterized by its large, elongated pitchers that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant's pitchers are typically green with red or yellow markings, which serve to attract prey. It thrives in high humidity and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

The species is notable for its ability to trap and digest insects, making it a unique addition to botanical collections.
