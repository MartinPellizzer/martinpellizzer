Nageia minor

Nageia minor, commonly known as the dwarf nageia, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to the tropical and subtropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araucariaceae family, which includes other notable coniferous species. The plant is characterized by its short, cylindrical trunk and dense, compact foliage. Its leaves are typically needle-like and retain their color year-round, contributing to its evergreen nature.

Nageia minor is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to grow in well-drained soils with adequate sunlight.
