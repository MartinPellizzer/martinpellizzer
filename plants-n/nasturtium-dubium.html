Nasturtium dubium

Nasturtium dubium, commonly known as the yellow watercress, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other plants like cabbages and mustard. The plant features small, yellow flowers and has edible leaves that are often used in salads. Its roots are also edible and have a peppery flavor similar to watercress.

Nasturtium dubium thrives in moist, aquatic environments and is often found near streams and ponds.
