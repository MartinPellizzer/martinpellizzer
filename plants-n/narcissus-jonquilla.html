Narcissus jonquilla

Narcissus jonquilla, commonly known as the golden daffodil, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and western Asia, where it thrives in sunny, well-drained soils. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange, typically blooming in early spring. Its bulbs are perennial, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

The leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming a dense rosette above the bulb.
