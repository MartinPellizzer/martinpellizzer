Nonatelia pilosa

Nonatelia pilosa, commonly known as the hairy nonatelia, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for study in ecological and horticultural contexts.
