Nicotiana trigonophylla var. sordida

Nicotiana trigonophylla var. sordida, commonly known as the dusky tobacco plant, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshade plants. The species is characterized by its dark purple to almost black flowers, which bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are typically hairy and have a distinct, often glandular texture.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its striking appearance.
