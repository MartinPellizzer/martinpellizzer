Neocupressus arizonica var. nevadensis

Neocupressus arizonica var. nevadensis, commonly known as the Arizona cypress, is a variant of the Arizona cypress native to the southwestern United States.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen conifer characterized by its dense, pyramidal shape and scale-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous cones that contain seeds, typical of the Cupressaceae family. Its foliage is fragrant and often emits a resinous scent, which is a common trait among members of its taxonomic group.

This variety is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought tolerance and resilience to harsh climatic conditions.
