Nicotiana × sanderi

Nicotiana × sanderi, commonly known as the hybrid tobacco plant, is a species resulting from the cross between Nicotiana tabacum and Nicotiana sandra.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant is native to the southern United States and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and botanical collections.

Its foliage is typically broad and heart-shaped, adding to its aesthetic appeal.
