Nepenthes × hybrida

Nepenthes × hybrida, commonly known as the hybrid pitcher plant, is a fascinating tropical carnivorous plant resulting from crosses between different Nepenthes species.

It is characterized by its large, elongated pitchers that secrete a digestive fluid to trap and digest insects. The plant's leaves are modified into specialized structures called pitchers, which are typically green or red in color, depending on the variety. Nepenthes × hybrida thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires consistent moisture and nutrient-rich soil.

Its unique morphology and carnivorous adaptations make it a popular subject in botanical studies and horticulture.
