Negretia platycarpa

Negretia platycarpa, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Erythroxylum platycarpum*, is a species of flowering plant in the family Erythroxylaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, Colombia, and Venezuela. The plant is characterized by its small, red berries that are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are elliptical and have a glossy surface, while its flowers are typically white or pale pink.

Negretia platycarpa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential pharmacological properties.
