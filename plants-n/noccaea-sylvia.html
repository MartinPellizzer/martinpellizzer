Noccaea sylvia

Noccaea sylvia, commonly known as silvery saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or mountainous regions. Its leaves are basal, rosette-like, and covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer.
