Neraudia sericea

Neraudia sericea, commonly known as silverleaf neraudia, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its silvery, velvety foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in coastal areas or on rocky slopes.

Neraudia sericea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
