Nogalia

Nogalia, commonly known as the silverleaf plant, is a unique species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which helps it retain moisture in harsh environments. The plant has small, rounded leaves that are covered in a fine layer of hair, reducing water loss through transpiration. Nogalia produces clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Its adaptability to extreme climates makes it an interesting subject for botanical study.
