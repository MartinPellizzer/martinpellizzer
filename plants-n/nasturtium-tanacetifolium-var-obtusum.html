Nasturtium tanacetifolium var. obtusum

Nasturtium tanacetifolium var. obtusum, commonly known as the broad-leaved nasturtium, is a herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture and produces vibrant orange or yellow flowers. The plant is characterized by its strong, peppery scent and is often grown for its ornamental value and edible properties. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Its flowers and leaves are edible, often used in culinary applications to add a spicy flavor.
