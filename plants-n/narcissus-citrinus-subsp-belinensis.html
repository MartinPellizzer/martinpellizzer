Narcissus citrinus subsp. belinensis

Narcissus citrinus subsp.

belinensis, commonly known as the Belin's yellow narcissus, is a rare and ornamental plant native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers with a distinctive cup-shaped corona.

The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of strap-like leaves that are narrow and grass-like in appearance.

This subspecies is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
