Neonotonia wightii subsp. petitiana

Neonotonia wightii subsp.

petitiana, commonly known as the dwarf red sanders, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to the western Ghats of India. It is characterized by its dense, aromatic foliage and small, dark green leaves that are opposite in arrangement.

The plant produces yellowish-green flowers followed by small, woody fruits containing flat, winged seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable species in horticulture.
