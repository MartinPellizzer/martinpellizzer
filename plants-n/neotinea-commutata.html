Neotinea commutata

Neotinea commutata, commonly known as the small white orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, white flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a subtle fragrance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest undergrowth or along stream banks. Its roots are adapted to absorb nutrients from the rich, organic soil in its habitat.

Neotinea commutata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
