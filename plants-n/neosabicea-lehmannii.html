Neosabicea lehmannii

Neosabicea lehmannii, commonly known as Lehmann's neosabicea, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to South Africa, specifically found in the Western Cape region. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and arranged in clusters, typical of the Fabaceae family.

Neosabicea lehmannii is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to the Mediterranean climate of its native habitat.
