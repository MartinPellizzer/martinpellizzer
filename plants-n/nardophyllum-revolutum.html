Nardophyllum revolutum

Nardophyllum revolutum, commonly known as the curled nard, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, curled leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, where it forms a low, mat-like growth. Its unique morphology and habitat make it an interesting subject for botanical study, particularly in alpine ecosystems.

Nardophyllum revolutum is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
