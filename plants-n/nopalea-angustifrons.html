Nopalea angustifrons

Nopalea angustifrons, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and narrow, paddle-shaped leaves. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh desert environments through its ability to store water in its thick, fleshy stems. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, appearing in clusters during the warmer months.

Nopalea angustifrons plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various desert animals.
