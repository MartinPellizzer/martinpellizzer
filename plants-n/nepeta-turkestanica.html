Nepeta turkestanica

Nepeta turkestanica, commonly known as Siberian catmint, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite leaves that are usually grayish-green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, pale blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens or borders.

Its aromatic foliage and attractive flowers make it a popular choice for both ornamental and medicinal purposes.
