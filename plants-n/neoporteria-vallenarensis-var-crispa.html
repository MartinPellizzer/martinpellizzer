Neoporteria vallenarensis var. crispa

Neoporteria vallenarensis var. crispa, commonly known as the Chilean bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of central Chile.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to the temperate climate of its native habitat. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine pubescence, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
