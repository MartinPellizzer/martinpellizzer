Nymphozanthus europaeus

Nymphozanthus europaeus, commonly known as the European flame flower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its striking red-orange petals and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It has a compact growth habit and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Nymphozanthus europaeus is relatively drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils.
