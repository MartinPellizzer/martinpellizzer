Nepeta hedgei

Nepeta hedgei, commonly known as catmint, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant blue or purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage has a minty scent, which is particularly appealing to cats.

Nepeta hedgei is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
