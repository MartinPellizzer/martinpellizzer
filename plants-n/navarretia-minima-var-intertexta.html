Navarretia minima var. intertexta

Navarretia minima var. intertexta, commonly known as the Sierra Valley bellflower, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States.

It produces delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is characterized by its compact growth habit.

This variety is closely related to other Navarretia species and is valued for its ornamental appeal in native plant gardens.
