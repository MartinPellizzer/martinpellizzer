Nigella glandulifera

Nigella glandulifera, commonly known as the Kashmiri black cumin, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its small, fragrant yellow flowers and dark, oil-rich seeds. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. Its leaves are trifoliate, and the plant is often cultivated for its medicinal and culinary uses.

The seeds are harvested when fully mature and are used in traditional medicine and as a spice.
