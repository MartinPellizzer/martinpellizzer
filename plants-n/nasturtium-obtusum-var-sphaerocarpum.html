Nasturtium obtusum var. sphaerocarpum

Nasturtium obtusum var. sphaerocarpum, commonly known as the globe-shaped nasturtium, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, waxy leaves and vibrant orange or yellow flowers. The plant is a herbaceous annual that thrives in full sun and well-drained soil. Its unique fruit, which is spherical and resembles a small orange, is a notable botanical feature.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible flowers.
