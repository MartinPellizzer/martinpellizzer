Notholaena lanuginosa subsp. bivalens

Notholaena lanuginosa subsp.

bivalens, commonly known as the silky notholaena, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and small, clustered inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. Its foliage is often covered in fine, lanugo-like hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance.

The subspecies bivalens is distinguished by its unique leaf morphology and floral structure compared to other members of the Notholaena genus.
