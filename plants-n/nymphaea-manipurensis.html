Nymphaea manipurensis

Nymphaea manipurensis, commonly known as the Manipur water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to the freshwater habitats of Manipur, India.

It belongs to the genus Nymphaea, which includes various water lilies characterized by their showy, floating flowers. The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves that float on the water's surface, supported by long, slender petioles. Its flowers are typically pink to white, with a prominent yellow stamen, and bloom during the warmer months.

Nymphaea manipurensis plays an important ecological role in its native wetlands by providing habitat and food for aquatic life.
