Nectandra corzoana

Nectandra corzoana, commonly known as the Guiana nectandra, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and rough, ridged bark. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by ellipsoid fruits containing a single seed. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland and premontane forests.

Due to its ecological importance and limited distribution, it is considered a vulnerable species.
