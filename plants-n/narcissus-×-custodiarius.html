Narcissus × custodiarius

Narcissus × custodiarius, commonly known as the yellow daffodil, is a hybrid species within the genus Narcissus.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers with a single, erect perianth, typically featuring a long, narrow trumpet-shaped corona. The plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

The species is also notable for its historical use in floral arrangements and its symbolic association with renewal and new beginnings.
