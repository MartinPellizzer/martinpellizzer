Nasa raimondii

Nasa raimondii, commonly known as the desert sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the Atacama Desert in Chile.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is notable for being one of the smallest flowering plants in the world. The plant has small, triangular leaves and produces tiny, greenish flowers that bloom in the spring. It has adapted to extreme aridity by having a deep root system and a very efficient water-use strategy.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it an important subject in botanical research.
