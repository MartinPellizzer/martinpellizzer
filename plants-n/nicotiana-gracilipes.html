Nicotiana gracilipes

Nicotiana gracilipes, commonly known as the slender tobacco plant, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its delicate, slender stems and small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known species such as tomatoes and peppers. It typically grows in dry, open habitats and has a short growing season.

Its flowers attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
