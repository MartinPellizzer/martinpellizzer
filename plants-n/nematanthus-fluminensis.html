Nematanthus fluminensis

Nematanthus fluminensis, commonly known as the Brazilian flame flower, is a tropical shrub native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, often with a contrasting throat. The plant belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in horticulture. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-drained soil.

The flowers are pollinated primarily by hummingbirds, making it an important species in ecological studies.
