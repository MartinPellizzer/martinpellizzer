Nematanthus longipes

Nematanthus longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed velvet plant, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, tubular flowers. The plant produces showy, spurred flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, often with contrasting purple markings. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is popular among gardeners and is often grown as a houseplant or in hanging baskets.
