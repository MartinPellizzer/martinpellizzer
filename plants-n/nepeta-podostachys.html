Nepeta podostachys

Nepeta podostachys, commonly known as the mountain catmint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright, slender stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens or alpine beds.

Its aromatic foliage and delicate blossoms make it a popular choice for ornamental horticulture.
