Nepeta lasiocephala

Nepeta lasiocephala, commonly known as woolly catmint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright stems covered in dense, soft grayish-white hairs, giving it a distinctive, woolly appearance. The plant produces small, tubular blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is aromatic, often emitting a mint-like scent when crushed.

Nepeta lasiocephala is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
