Nicotiana brasiliensis

Nicotiana brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian tobacco plant, is a species native to South America.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its erect growth habit and large, showy flowers. The plant typically features lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and produces clusters of tubular flowers in shades of pink to purple. It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is closely related to other tobacco species.

Nicotiana brasiliensis is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its historical significance in traditional medicine.
