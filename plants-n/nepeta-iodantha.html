Nepeta iodantha

Nepeta iodantha, commonly known as catnip, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to purple, depending on the variety. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and herbal use.

Nepeta iodantha is also known for its aromatic properties and its ability to attract beneficial insects like bees and butterflies.
