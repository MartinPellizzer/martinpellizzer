Nilgirianthus punctatus

Nilgirianthus punctatus, commonly known as the Nilgiri aster, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often hairy as well.

Nilgirianthus punctatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic beauty.
