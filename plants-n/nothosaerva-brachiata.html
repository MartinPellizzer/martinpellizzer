Nothosaerva brachiata

Nothosaerva brachiata, commonly known as the branched mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees, primarily in the genus *Eucalyptus*.

It possesses specialized structures called haustoria that penetrate the host's vascular system to extract water and nutrients. The plant's leaves are alternate, lanceolate, and often have a waxy coating, aiding in water retention. It produces small, white or pale green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by small, fleshy berries that are dispersed by birds.

Due to its parasitic nature and specific host requirements, it is often found in tropical and subtropical regions with suitable host trees.
