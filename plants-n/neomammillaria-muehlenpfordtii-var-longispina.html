Neomammillaria muehlenpfordtii var. longispina

Neomammillaria muehlenpfordtii var. longispina, commonly known as the long-spined pincushion cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are covered in white, radial spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun exposure.

Due to its unique appearance and slow growth rate, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
