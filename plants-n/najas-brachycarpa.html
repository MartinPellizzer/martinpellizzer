Najas brachycarpa

Najas brachycarpa, commonly known as the narrow-fruited water milfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant belonging to the family Najadaceae.

It is characterized by its whorled arrangement of narrow, grass-like leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that develop underwater. The plant produces small, cylindrical fruits that are adapted for dispersal by water currents. It thrives in still to slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and lakes.

Najas brachycarpa plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
