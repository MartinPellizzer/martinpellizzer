Nepeta calvertii

Nepeta calvertii, commonly known as Calvert's catmint, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage and clusters of small, blue-violet flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their calming properties.

Nepeta calvertii is also valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
