Nesaea ondongana subsp. orientalis

Nesaea ondongana subsp.

orientalis, commonly known as the eastern nesaea, is a perennial herb native to eastern Australia. It typically grows in sandy or loamy soils in coastal and hinterland regions.

The plant features slender, erect stems with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in terminal inflorescences, blooming during spring.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
