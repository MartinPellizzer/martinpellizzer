Narcissus pseudonarcissus subsp. pugsleyanus

Narcissus pseudonarcissus subsp.

pugsleyanus, commonly known as the Pugsley narcissus, is a subspecies of the daffodil characterized by its distinctive, narrow, and elongated leaves. It produces a single, upright flower with a trumpet-shaped corona that is typically yellow or white in color.

The plant is native to the Iberian Peninsula and thrives in well-drained, sunny soils. It is a hardy perennial that is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

The subspecies is notable for its genetic diversity and is a subject of interest in botanical research.
