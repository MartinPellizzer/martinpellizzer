Nicotiana wuttkei

Nicotiana wuttkei, commonly known as the Wuttke's tobacco, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family.

It is native to South America, specifically Ecuador, and thrives in humid, tropical environments. The plant produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a violet or purple hue. Its leaves are typically broad and slightly hairy, and it contains nicotine, similar to other members of the Nicotiana genus.

This plant is primarily of interest to botanists and horticulturists due to its ornamental value and unique botanical characteristics.
