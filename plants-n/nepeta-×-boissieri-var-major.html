Nepeta × boissieri var. major

Nepeta × boissieri var. major, commonly known as catmint, is a hybrid perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Mediterranean.

It features aromatic, fuzzy leaves and produces clusters of small, tubular blue or purple flowers. The plant is characterized by its strong mint-like scent, which is released when the leaves are crushed. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and herbal collections.

Nepeta × boissieri var. major is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
