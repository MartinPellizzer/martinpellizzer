Narcissus tazetta var. panizzianus

Narcissus tazetta var. panizzianus, commonly known as the Italian Paperwhite, is a bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces clusters of fragrant, white flowers with a yellow center, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often grown as a houseplant due to its ability to thrive in cool, dark environments. It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in floral arrangements.

This variety is particularly noted for its strong scent and ease of cultivation.
