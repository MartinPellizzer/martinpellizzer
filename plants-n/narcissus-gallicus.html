Narcissus gallicus

Narcissus gallicus, commonly known as the French daffodil, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to white, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has narrow, strap-like leaves that emerge from a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular garden plant.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and ecological landscapes.
