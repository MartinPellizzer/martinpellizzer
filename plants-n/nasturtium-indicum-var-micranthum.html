Nasturtium indicum var. micranthum

Nasturtium indicum var. micranthum, commonly known as the dwarf nasturtium, is a compact, annual flowering plant native to South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves and brightly colored flowers in shades of orange and yellow, typically blooming from summer to early autumn. The plant is known for its peppery taste, making it a popular choice for edible landscaping and culinary use. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often used in gardens for its ornamental and medicinal properties.

Its rapid growth and ability to attract pollinators make it a valuable addition to biodiversity-friendly gardens.
