Narcissus candidissimus

Narcissus candidissimus, commonly known as the white narcissus or the Spanish daffodil, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to the Iberian Peninsula and is characterized by its delicate, white, cup-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 20 to 40 centimeters and produces a single flower per stem. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it blooms in early spring.

Narcissus candidissimus is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.
