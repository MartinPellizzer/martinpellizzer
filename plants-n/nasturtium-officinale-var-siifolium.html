Nasturtium officinale var. siifolium

Nasturtium officinale var. siifolium, commonly known as the leafy nasturtium, is a variant of the common nasturtium characterized by its broad, lobed leaves and showy, edible flowers.

This plant is native to South America and thrives in temperate climates, often growing as an annual in cooler regions. Its leaves are thick and succulent, with a peppery flavor, making them popular in culinary applications. The flowers, which come in shades of orange and yellow, are also edible and frequently used in salads or as garnishes.

The plant is known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to garden biodiversity.
