Noterophila rosulans

Noterophila rosulans, commonly known as the Rosulate Noterophila, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-like arrangement of leaves, which are typically fleshy and succulent. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that are often pink or white in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat preferences, it is a subject of interest in botanical studies and conservation efforts.
