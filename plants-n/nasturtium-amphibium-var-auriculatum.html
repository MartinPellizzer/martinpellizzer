Nasturtium amphibium var. auriculatum

Nasturtium amphibium var. auriculatum, commonly known as the golden ear nasturtium, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers and heart-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments, such as along stream banks or in wet meadows. Its flowers attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological balance.

The leaves and flowers are edible, often used in culinary applications for their peppery flavor.
