Neotinea lactea f. minuscula

Neotinea lactea f. minuscula, commonly known as the dwarf milkweed, is a rare and delicate variant of the milkweed species.

It is characterized by its smaller, more compact growth habit compared to the typical form of Neotinea lactea. The plant features slender, hairy stems and small, pale green leaves that are often covered in fine white hairs. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to white, with five petals and a distinct central stamen.

This subspecies is typically found in shaded, moist habitats and is valued for its ecological role in supporting specific pollinators.
