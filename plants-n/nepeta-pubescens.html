Nepeta pubescens

Nepeta pubescens, commonly known as catmint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and clusters of small, fragrant blue or purple flowers. The plant belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae), which is known for its aromatic oils and square stems. Nepeta pubescens is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
