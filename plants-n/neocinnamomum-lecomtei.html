Neocinnamomum lecomtei

Neocinnamomum lecomtei, commonly known as Lecomte's cinnamon tree, is a species of evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of southern China and northern Vietnam.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other aromatic plants such as cinnamon and laurel. The plant is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers followed by dark purple berries. Its essential oils, derived from the leaves and bark, are valued for their aromatic properties and potential medicinal uses.

Neocinnamomum lecomtei is often grown in horticultural settings for its ornamental value and unique fragrance.
