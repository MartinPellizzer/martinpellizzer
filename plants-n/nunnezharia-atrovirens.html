Nunnezharia atrovirens

Nunnezharia atrovirens, commonly known as the black-feathered fern, is a unique fern species characterized by its dark green, lance-shaped fronds.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in temperate forest understories. The plant has a creeping rhizome system that allows it to spread gradually across the forest floor. Its spores are released from specialized structures called sori, which are located on the underside of the fronds.

Nunnezharia atrovirens plays a vital role in maintaining soil stability and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.
