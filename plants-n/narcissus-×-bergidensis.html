Narcissus × bergidensis

Narcissus × bergidensis, commonly known as the giant daffodil, is a hybrid species of the genus Narcissus native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers with a prominent, often white or green, central corona. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, making it one of the tallest members of the daffodil family. Its foliage consists of long, strap-like leaves that are grassy in appearance.

The botanical features of Narcissus × bergidensis make it a popular ornamental plant in gardens and landscapes.
