Neoconopodium paddarensis

It belongs to the family Parmeliaceae and is characterized by its dense, crust-like growth form that adheres tightly to rock surfaces. The thallus is typically pale yellow to orange in color, with a finely wrinkled surface that helps it retain moisture in its harsh, maritime environment. It forms symbiotic relationships with specific algae and fungi, which contribute to its ability to survive in nutrient-poor conditions.

Due to its limited distribution and fragile habitat, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
