Neoporteria subgibbosa f. castaneoides

Neoporteria subgibbosa f. castaneoides, commonly known as the red-veined begonia, is a hybrid form of the genus Neoporteria, characterized by its striking, velvety leaves with deep red veins.

It is a herbaceous perennial native to Southeast Asia, where it thrives in shaded, moist environments. The plant produces small, pale pink or white flowers that are typically borne on upright stems. Its foliage is highly ornamental, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening and terrariums.

Due to its hybrid origin, it may exhibit variability in growth habits and leaf patterns.
