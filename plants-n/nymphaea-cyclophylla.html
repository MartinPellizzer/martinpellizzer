Nymphaea cyclophylla

Nymphaea cyclophylla, commonly known as the cyclophylla water lily, is a species of aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats, such as ponds and lakes. Its leaves are typically a deep green, while the flowers range in color from white to pink.

Nymphaea cyclophylla plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
