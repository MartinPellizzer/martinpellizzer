Narcissus parvicorona

Narcissus parvicorona, commonly known as the dwarf daffodil, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to the Iberian Peninsula and is characterized by its small, compact growth habit. The plant produces showy, funnel-shaped flowers with a yellow corona, typically blooming in early spring. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a basal rosette.

Narcissus parvicorona is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, early-blooming flowers.
