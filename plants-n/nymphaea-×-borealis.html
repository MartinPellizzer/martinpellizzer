Nymphaea × borealis

Nymphaea × borealis, commonly known as the northern water lily, is a hybrid aquatic plant native to the colder regions of the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white, pink, and yellow. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes. Its leaves are usually rounded with a waxy surface, and its roots are anchored in the substrate at the bottom of the water body.

Nymphaea × borealis plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
