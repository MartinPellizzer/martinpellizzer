Nepenthes × cincta

Nepenthes × cincta, commonly known as the hybrid tropical pitcher plant, is a fascinating species that combines traits from its parent species, N. rafflesiana and N. mirabilis.

It is native to Borneo and is characterized by its distinctive, elongated pitchers that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces nectar to attract insects, which then fall into the pitcher and are digested by the plant's digestive enzymes. Its unique morphology and carnivorous nature make it a popular subject in botanical studies and horticulture.

Nepenthes × cincta thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires specific care to maintain its health and growth.
