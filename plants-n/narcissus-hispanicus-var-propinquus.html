Narcissus hispanicus var. propinquus

Narcissus hispanicus var. propinquus, commonly known as the Spanish daffodil, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It is closely related to other daffodil species and is valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some regions.
