Nectandra laevis

Nectandra laevis, commonly known as the smooth nectandra, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and lance-shaped leaves with a fragrant aroma. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by oval, woody fruits containing aromatic seeds.

The species is valued for its timber and is often found in lowland evergreen forests.
