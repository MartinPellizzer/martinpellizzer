Nepeta elegans

Nepeta elegans, commonly known as catmint, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like mint and lavender. The plant features square stems, opposite leaves, and small, tubular blue or purple flowers that attract pollinators. It is known for its strong, minty fragrance and is often used in gardens for its ornamental value.

Nepeta elegans is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported calming properties.
