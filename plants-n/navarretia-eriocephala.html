Navarretia eriocephala

Navarretia eriocephala, commonly known as the Sierra snowplant, is a herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky, alpine, or serpentine soils, often in open, sunny habitats. Its leaves are usually basal, forming a rosette, and the stems are often hairy.

Navarretia eriocephala is adapted to cold climates and is often found at high elevations.
