Neolemaireocereus thurberi

Neolemaireocereus thurberi, commonly known as the Thornless Moon Cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its swollen, segmented stems that resemble a moon, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, white flowers during the warmer months, typically in summer. It is a slow-growing species that thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care, it is a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
