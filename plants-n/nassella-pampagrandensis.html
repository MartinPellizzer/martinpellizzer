Nassella pampagrandensis

Nassella pampagrandensis, commonly known as the Pampas needlegrass, is a grass species native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and fine, needle-like leaves, which are typically covered in a dense layer of hair. This plant thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and shrublands, often forming dense clumps. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of spikelets, which are small, clustered flower structures.

Nassella pampagrandensis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small animals and insects.
