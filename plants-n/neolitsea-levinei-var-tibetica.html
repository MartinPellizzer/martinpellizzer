Neolitsea levinei var. tibetica

Neolitsea levinei var. tibetica, commonly known as the Tibetan fragrant leaf tree, is a variant of the Neolitsea levinei species native to the mountainous regions of Tibet.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are often aromatic. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, round fruits containing one or two seeds. Its leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported therapeutic properties.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
