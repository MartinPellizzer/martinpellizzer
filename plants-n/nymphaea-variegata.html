Nymphaea variegata

Nymphaea variegata, commonly known as the Variegated Water Lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features broad, floating leaves that are often variegated with white, yellow, and green patterns, providing a vibrant visual display on water surfaces. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white, with a prominent yellow center. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, requiring full sun exposure for optimal growth.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental ponds and water gardens.
