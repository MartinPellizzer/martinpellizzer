Nuxia dentata var. transvaalensis

Nuxia dentata var. transvaalensis, commonly known as the Transvaal bracken or Transvaal fern, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky outcrops. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The species is notable for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils and its resilience to environmental stress.
