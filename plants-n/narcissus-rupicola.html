Narcissus rupicola

Narcissus rupicola, commonly known as the rock daffodil, is a perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or mountainous regions. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, typically blooming in spring. Its foliage consists of strap-like leaves that are evergreen in mild climates.

Narcissus rupicola is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to challenging growing conditions.
