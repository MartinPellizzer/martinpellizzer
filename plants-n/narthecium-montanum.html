Narthecium montanum

Narthecium montanum, commonly known as the mountain carnation, is a herbaceous plant native to alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Montiaceae and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, adapted to withstand cold and windy mountain conditions.

Narthecium montanum is often found in rocky soils and is a characteristic species of alpine meadows.
