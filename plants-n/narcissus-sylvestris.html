Narcissus sylvestris

Narcissus sylvestris, commonly known as the wild narcissus, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa, typically growing in woodland and rocky habitats. The plant produces a single, cup-shaped flower with six petals and a yellow-orange corona, blooming in late winter to early spring. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is known for its strong, sweet fragrance.

This species is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and is also valued for its historical and cultural significance.
