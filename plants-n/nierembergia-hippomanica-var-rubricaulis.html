Nierembergia hippomanica var. rubricaulis

Nierembergia hippomanica var. rubricaulis, commonly known as the red-stemmed bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a reddish tinge, especially on the underside.

This variety is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
