Natsiatum oppositifolium

Natsiatum oppositifolium, commonly known as the opposite-leaved natsiatum, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite leaf arrangement, where leaves are paired along the stem, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are simple and ovate, with a smooth surface and a slightly waxy texture.

Natsiatum oppositifolium is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and along stream banks.
