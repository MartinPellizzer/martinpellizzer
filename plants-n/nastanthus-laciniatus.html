Nastanthus laciniatus

Nastanthus laciniatus, commonly known as the laceleaf plant, is a member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves that give it a delicate, lace-like appearance. It produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red in color.

Nastanthus laciniatus thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture to maintain its vibrant foliage.
