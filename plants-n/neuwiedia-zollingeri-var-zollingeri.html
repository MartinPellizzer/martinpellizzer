Neuwiedia zollingeri var. zollingeri

Neuwiedia zollingeri var. zollingeri, commonly known as the Zollinger's neuwiedia, is a species native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 2 to 5 meters. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. Its flowers are small and clustered, often white or pale yellow, and it produces small, fleshy fruits that are edible.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its foliage and seasonal blooms.
