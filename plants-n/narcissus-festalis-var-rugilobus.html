Narcissus festalis var. rugilobus

Narcissus festalis var. rugilobus, commonly known as the rugilobus daffodil, is a botanical variant of the daffodil genus characterized by its unique foliage and floral structure.

It typically features narrow, strap-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, hairy texture, giving it a distinctive appearance. The flowers are usually yellow, with a single prominent tepal that distinguishes it from other daffodil varieties. It is native to regions of southern Europe and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is cultivated in gardens for its early spring blooms.
