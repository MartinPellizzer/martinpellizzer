Nidularium macahense

Nidularium macahense, commonly known as the Mexican orchid or Macahue orchid, is a species of orchid native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pale pink. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in its natural habitat. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, often arranged in a rosette pattern, and it prefers well-drained soil and bright, indirect light.

Nidularium macahense is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by enthusiasts of tropical and subtropical plants.
