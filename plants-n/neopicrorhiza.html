Neopicrorhiza

Neopicrorhiza, commonly known as Chinese valerian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the family Valerianaceae and is characterized by its aromatic, rhizomatous root system. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers and has lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive, slightly hairy texture. Neopicrorhiza is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly its use in traditional Chinese medicine for its calming and sedative effects.

Its chemical composition includes various iridoid glycosides and essential oils, which contribute to its therapeutic applications.
