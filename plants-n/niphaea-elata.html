Niphaea elata

Niphaea elata, commonly known as the broadleaf water lily, is a aquatic plant native to the wetlands of Southeast Asia.

It features broad, floating leaves that provide habitat for various aquatic organisms. The plant produces white, fragrant flowers that bloom above the water surface. Its roots anchor in the muddy substrate, while its rhizomes spread horizontally to form dense colonies.

Niphaea elata plays a vital role in water filtration and supports biodiversity in its ecosystem.
