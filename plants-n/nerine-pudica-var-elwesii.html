Nerine pudica var. elwesii

Nerine pudica var. elwesii, commonly known as the Elwes' rain lily, is a striking bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, often with a prominent red or purple spot at the base. The plant produces narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically evergreen, and it thrives in well-drained soil with full sun to partial shade. It is known for its ability to bloom reliably in late winter or early spring, making it a valuable addition to gardens in cooler climates.

The species is also noted for its resilience and adaptability to various growing conditions.
