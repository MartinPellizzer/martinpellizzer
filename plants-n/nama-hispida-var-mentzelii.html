Nama hispida var. mentzelii

Nama hispida var. mentzelii, commonly known as the Mentzelii Nama, is a perennial shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, glandular, and often hairy leaves that help reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils and can tolerate high temperatures and low rainfall.

This species is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought resistance and ornamental value.
