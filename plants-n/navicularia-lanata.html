Navicularia lanata

Navicularia lanata, commonly known as the hairy navicularia, is a flowering plant belonging to the Acanthaceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, pubescent stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically arranged in clusters, often in shades of pink or purple. Native to South America, it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
