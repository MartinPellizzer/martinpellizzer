Nigritella rubra

Nigritella rubra, commonly known as the red velvet orchid, is a striking flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant red petals with a velvety texture, which are often adorned with intricate patterns of dark purple and gold. The plant belongs to the Orchidaceae family and thrives in warm, humid environments with abundant sunlight. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the rich, organic soil in which it grows.

Nigritella rubra is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
