Narcissus × emeritensis

Narcissus × emeritensis, commonly known as the Spanish daffodil, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers with a trumpet-shaped corona, typically blooming in early spring. The plant is native to the Iberian Peninsula and is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in well-drained soil. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

Narcissus × emeritensis prefers full sun to partial shade and is relatively resistant to pests and diseases.
