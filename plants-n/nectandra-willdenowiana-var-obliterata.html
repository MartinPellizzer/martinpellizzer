Nectandra willdenowiana var. obliterata

Nectandra willdenowiana var. obliterata, commonly known as the Brazilian sandarac tree, is a tropical evergreen species native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, dark-green, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its bark and wood are valued for their resinous properties, which have been used traditionally in various applications.

The species is also noted for its ecological importance in providing habitat and food for local wildlife.
