Narcissus linnaeanus subsp. dubius

Narcissus linnaeanus subsp.

dubius, commonly known as the Spanish daffodil, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Europe. It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers with a yellow corona and white petals, typically blooming in early spring.

The plant has a compact growth habit with narrow, grass-like leaves. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant spring display.
