Narcissus × calathinus var. triandrus

Narcissus × calathinus var. triandrus, commonly known as the Triandrus daffodil, is a hybrid plant that exhibits distinctive botanical features.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers with a single tepal. The plant typically grows to a height of 15–30 cm, with a compact and upright growth habit. Its bulbs are fleshy and white, and the flowers bloom in early spring.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and adaptability to various garden conditions.
