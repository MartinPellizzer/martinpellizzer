Nama dichotoma var. latisepala

Nama dichotoma var. latisepala, commonly known as the broad-sepal nama, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its hairy, woody stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers with broad, fleshy sepals that are often mistaken for petals. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting well to poor soil conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral structure.
