Narcissus × nutans

Narcissus × nutans, commonly known as the crown imperial, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the eastern Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, showy inflorescence composed of multiple overlapping floral bracts. The flowers are typically red, orange, or yellow, with a distinctive orange or red center, and they bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces narrow, strap-like leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, and it is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
