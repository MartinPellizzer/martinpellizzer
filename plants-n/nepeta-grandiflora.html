Nepeta grandiflora

Nepeta grandiflora, commonly known as catnip, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite leaves that are often aromatic and slightly fuzzy. The plant produces small, fragrant blue or purple flowers in whorled clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Nepeta grandiflora is also known for its effects on cats, as the compound nepetalactone in its leaves can stimulate their senses.
