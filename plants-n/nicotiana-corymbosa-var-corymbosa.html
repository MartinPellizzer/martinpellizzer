Nicotiana corymbosa var. corymbosa

Nicotiana corymbosa var. corymbosa, commonly known as the clustered tobacco or sweet tobacco, is a herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and are arranged in dense, terminal clusters. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and has hairy, lance-shaped leaves with a distinct aromatic scent. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a historical association with traditional Native American uses.
