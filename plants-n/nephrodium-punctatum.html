Nephrodium punctatum

Nephrodium punctatum, commonly known as the spotted nephroid, is a unique aquatic plant characterized by its distinctive, kidney-shaped leaves.

The leaves are often marked with dark, irregular spots, giving the plant its common name. It typically grows in shallow, still waters such as ponds and marshes. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread across the substrate.

Its structure supports a variety of aquatic life, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
