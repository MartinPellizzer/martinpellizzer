Nephrodium tropicum

Nephrodium tropicum, commonly known as the tropical kidney fern, is a species native to tropical regions of Asia and the Pacific.

It belongs to the family Nephrodiaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, kidney-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as rainforests and wetlands. Its fronds are often divided into lobes and can reach lengths of up to 1 meter.

Nephrodium tropicum plays a role in its ecosystem by providing habitat for small organisms and contributing to soil stability.
