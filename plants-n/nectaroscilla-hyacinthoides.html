Nectaroscilla hyacinthoides

Nectaroscilla hyacinthoides, commonly known as the blue hyacinth, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its clusters of fragrant, blue to violet flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Nectaroscilla hyacinthoides is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
