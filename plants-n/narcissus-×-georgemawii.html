Narcissus × georgemawii

Narcissus × georgemawii, commonly known as the George Maw narcissus, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Narcissus tazetta and Narcissus poeticus.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers with a yellow corona, which bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 20–30 cm, with narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in horticulture.
