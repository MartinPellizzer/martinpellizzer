Narcissus × tridymus

Narcissus × tridymus, commonly known as the daffodil, is a hybrid species within the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that typically range in color from yellow to white. The plant is native to Europe and western Asia, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight. It produces a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and has a short, sturdy stem that supports its floral display.

Narcissus × tridymus is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and is a symbol of renewal and new beginnings.
