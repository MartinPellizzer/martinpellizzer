Nepeta knorringiana

Nepeta knorringiana, commonly known as the mountain catmint, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or dry garden environments.

Nepeta knorringiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and mild medicinal properties.
