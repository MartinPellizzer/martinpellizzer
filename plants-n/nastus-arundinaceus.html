Nastus arundinaceus

Nastus arundinaceus, commonly known as the bamboo grass or false bamboo, is a perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its tall, slender stems that often resemble bamboo. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers and has narrow, elongated leaves that are typically arranged in a dense, clumping habit. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive, grass-like appearance.

Nastus arundinaceus is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion in wetland environments.
