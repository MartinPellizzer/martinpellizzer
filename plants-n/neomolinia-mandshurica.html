Neomolinia mandshurica

Neomolinia mandshurica, commonly known as the Manchurian feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and delicate, feathery inflorescences that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slight blue-gray hue.

Neomolinia mandshurica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
