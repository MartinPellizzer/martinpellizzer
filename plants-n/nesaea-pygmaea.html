Nesaea pygmaea

Nesaea pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf nesaea, is a small evergreen shrub native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows to a height of less than 12 inches, featuring dense, compact foliage with dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in the spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or container planting.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
