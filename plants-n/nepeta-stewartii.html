Nepeta stewartii

Nepeta stewartii, commonly known as Stewart's catmint, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces spikes of blue to purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically mint-scented and have a slightly fuzzy texture.

Nepeta stewartii is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
