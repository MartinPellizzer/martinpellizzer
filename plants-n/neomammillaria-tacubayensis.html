Neomammillaria tacubayensis

Neomammillaria tacubayensis, commonly known as the Tacubaya nipple cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are often covered in white or pale blue spines. The plant produces small, white flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
