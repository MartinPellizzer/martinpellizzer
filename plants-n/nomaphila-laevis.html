Nomaphila laevis

Nomaphila laevis, commonly known as the smooth leaf orchid, is a species of orchid native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and elongated, smooth leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow, typically blooming during the warmer months. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
