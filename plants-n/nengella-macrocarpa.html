Nengella macrocarpa

Nengella macrocarpa, commonly known as the giant nengella, is a striking member of the Malvaceae family, native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 15 centimeters in diameter, typically in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant has a woody stem with thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release numerous small, hairy seeds.

Nengella macrocarpa is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses due to its vibrant blooms and unique foliage.
