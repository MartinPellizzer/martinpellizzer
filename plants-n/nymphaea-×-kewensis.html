Nymphaea × kewensis

Nymphaea × kewensis, commonly known as the Kew water lily, is a hybrid aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy, floating leaves and striking, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in ponds and lakes. Its leaves are typically rounded with a waxy surface, while its flowers open in the early morning and close by midday.

Nymphaea × kewensis is cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits in water gardens.
