Neomammillaria bicolor var. nivea

Neomammillaria bicolor var. nivea, commonly known as the white-flowered milk barrel cactus, is a compact, spineless cactus native to Mexico.

It features a globular stem with a bluish-gray hue and produces pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has a slow growth rate and is well-suited for rock gardens or containers due to its compact size. It requires full sun and well-draining soil to thrive.

Its unique coloration and delicate flowers make it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
