Navia brevifolia

Navia brevifolia, commonly known as the short-leaved navia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers during the spring and summer months. Navia brevifolia is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
