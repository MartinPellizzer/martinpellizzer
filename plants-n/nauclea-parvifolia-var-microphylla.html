Nauclea parvifolia var. microphylla

Nauclea parvifolia var. microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved nauclea, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are typically less than 2 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along riverbanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
