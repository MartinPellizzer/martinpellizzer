Nepeta argolica subsp. argolica

Nepeta argolica subsp.

argolica, commonly known as the Argolic catmint, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green leaves.

The plant produces clusters of small, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is often found in open woodlands and grasslands, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.

The subspecies is distinguished by its compact growth habit and distinct floral structure compared to other Nepeta species.
