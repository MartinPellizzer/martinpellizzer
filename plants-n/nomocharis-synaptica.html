Nomocharis synaptica

Nomocharis synaptica, commonly known as the New Zealand spider orchid, is a striking orchid species native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its unique, spider-like appearance, with long, slender petals and a distinctive, curling lip. The plant typically grows in rocky, alpine environments, thriving in cool and moist conditions. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish in color, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insect species.

Nomocharis synaptica is a rare and protected species, reflecting its ecological significance in New Zealand's flora.
