Nothofagus alessandrii

Nothofagus alessandrii, commonly known as the ALESSANDRI'S NOOTHFAGUS, is a species of evergreen tree native to South America, specifically found in Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the Nothofagaceae family and is characterized by its broad, leathery leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. The tree produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by woody, cup-like cones containing edible seeds.

It thrives in temperate rainforests and is adapted to humid, cool climates with high rainfall.
