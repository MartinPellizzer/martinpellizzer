Nama berlandieri

Nama berlandieri, commonly known as the red-flowered orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, vibrant red petals. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Nama berlandieri is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its role in local ecosystems.
