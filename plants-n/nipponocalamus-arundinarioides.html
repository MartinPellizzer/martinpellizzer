Nipponocalamus arundinarioides

Nipponocalamus arundinarioides, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or Japanese reed bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach up to 3 meters in height. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often slightly hairy, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in aquatic and riparian habitats.
