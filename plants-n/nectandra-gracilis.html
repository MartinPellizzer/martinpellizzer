Nectandra gracilis

Nectandra gracilis, commonly known as the slender sandarac tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic, leathery leaves. The tree produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by oval, woody fruits containing aromatic seeds. Its wood is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used traditionally in incense and medicine.

The plant is also noted for its ability to thrive in humid, shady environments within tropical rainforests.
