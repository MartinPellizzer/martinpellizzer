Nepeta soorodotis

Nepeta soorodotis, commonly known as the Persian catmint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Iran.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its calming properties.

Nepeta soorodotis is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
