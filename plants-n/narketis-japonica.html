Narketis japonica

Narketis japonica, commonly known as the Japanese bellflower, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream sides. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a smooth texture.

Narketis japonica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
