Nolana sedifolia var. aplocaryoides

Nolana sedifolia var. aplocaryoides, commonly known as the silverleaf nolana, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the arid regions of South America.

It features dense, silver-gray foliage that is covered in fine, woolly hairs, providing it with a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers during the spring and summer months. It is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
