Neurocarpum flagellare

Neurocarpum flagellare, commonly known as the flagellate neurocarpum, is a rare and enigmatic plant belonging to the family Neurocarpaceae.

It is characterized by its unique, filamentous structure that resembles a whip, giving it its common name. The plant exhibits a complex arrangement of vascular tissues, which supports its growth in shaded, moist environments. Its reproductive structures are highly specialized, with small, clustered spores produced in specialized sporangia.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a sensitive indicator of ecological health.
