Neillia nakatsu-riparia

Neillia nakatsu-riparia, commonly known as the Japanese water willow, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, herbaceous stems and small, delicate white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist or wet habitats such as riverbanks and marshy areas. Its leaves are linear and often have a slight waxy coating, aiding in water retention.

Neillia nakatsu-riparia plays an important role in stabilizing soil along water edges and supporting local biodiversity.
