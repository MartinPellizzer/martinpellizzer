Neobeguea mahafaliensis

Neobeguea mahafaliensis, commonly known as the Mahafali palm, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is native to Madagascar and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves. The plant produces yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, round fruits. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in humid, lowland areas.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
