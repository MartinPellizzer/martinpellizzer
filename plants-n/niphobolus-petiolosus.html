Niphobolus petiolosus

Niphobolus petiolosus, commonly known as the hairy leaf vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by round, fleshy fruits. Its stems are wiry and tend to twine around supporting structures.

Niphobolus petiolosus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
