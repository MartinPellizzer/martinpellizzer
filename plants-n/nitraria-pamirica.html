Nitraria pamirica

Nitraria pamirica, commonly known as the Pamir date palm, is a shrub-like plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Nitrariaceae and is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to harsh environments, thriving in sandy soils with low rainfall. It produces small, edible fruits that are sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Nitraria pamirica plays a role in stabilizing desert ecosystems and preventing soil erosion.
