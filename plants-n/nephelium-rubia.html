Nephelium rubia

Nephelium rubia, commonly known as the red mangostin, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and fragrant white flowers. The fruit is distinctive, with a thick, reddish-purple rind and segmented, white, juicy aril-covered seeds. The plant thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.

Its fruit contains bioactive compounds that have been studied for potential health benefits.
