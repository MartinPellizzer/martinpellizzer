Negundo orizabense

Negundo orizabense, commonly known as the Orizaba ash, is a deciduous tree native to central Mexico.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and compound leaves that are opposite in arrangement. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by spherical fruits that contain three winged seeds. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in dry forests and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
