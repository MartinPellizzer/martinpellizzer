Nepeta caucasica

Nepeta caucasica, commonly known as catnip, is a flowering plant in the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and parts of Europe. The plant features square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. Its small, pale blue or purple flowers are clustered in whorls along the stem.

Nepeta caucasica is well-known for its aromatic properties and its effect on cats.
