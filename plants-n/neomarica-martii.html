Neomarica martii

Neomarica martii, commonly known as the pink lady's slipper, is a striking orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It features showy, pinkish-purple flowers that resemble a slipper, with a distinctive pouch-like structure at the base. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit and thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and grass-like, often forming a dense rosette.

Neomarica martii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
