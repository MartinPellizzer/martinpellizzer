Nephrodium gongylodes var. ecklonii

Nephrodium gongylodes var. ecklonii, commonly known as the Ecklon's fern, is a variant of the nephrodium genus native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, leathery, and deeply lobed leaves that resemble the shape of a kidney, hence its scientific name. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in forest understories or along stream banks. Its fronds are usually dark green and have a velvety texture, adding to its ornamental appeal.

This variant is valued for its unique foliage and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
