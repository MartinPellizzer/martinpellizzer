Nepeta ernesti-mayeri

Nepeta ernesti-mayeri, commonly known as the Mayer's catnip, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, arranged in whorled clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens.

Its aromatic foliage and ability to attract beneficial insects make it a valuable addition to ecological landscapes.
