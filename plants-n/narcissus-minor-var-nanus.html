Narcissus minor var. nanus

Narcissus minor var. nanus, commonly known as the dwarf daffodil, is a compact variety of the genus Narcissus.

It features small, delicate flowers with bright yellow petals and a central, trumpet-shaped corona. The plant typically grows to a height of only a few inches, making it ideal for rock gardens or containers. It blooms in early spring, adding a splash of color to gardens with its cheerful appearance.

This variety is well-suited to well-drained soil and full sun, requiring minimal maintenance once established.
