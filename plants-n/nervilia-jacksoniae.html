Nervilia jacksoniae

Nervilia jacksoniae, commonly known as Jackson's fern, is a rare and delicate orchid species native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded forest understories.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a conservation concern.
