Nepeta prainii

Nepeta prainii, commonly known as Prain's catmint, is a perennial herb native to the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically narrow and have a minty fragrance, which is a common trait in the Nepeta genus.

Nepeta prainii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in rocky or well-drained soils.
