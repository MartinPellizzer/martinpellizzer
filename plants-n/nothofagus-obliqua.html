Nothofagus obliqua

Nothofagus obliqua, commonly known as the southern beech or *Nothofagus obliqua*, is a species of evergreen tree native to southern South America.

It belongs to the Nothofagus genus, which is part of the Nothofagaceae family, and is characterized by its unique morphology and ecological significance. The plant has a distinctive bark that is often fissured and dark in color, and its leaves are typically lance-shaped with a waxy coating. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by edible, woody fruits that are important for wildlife.

Nothofagus obliqua is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
