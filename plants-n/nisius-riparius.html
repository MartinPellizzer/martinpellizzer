Nisius riparius

Nisius riparius, commonly known as the river sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to wetland and riparian habitats across Europe and parts of Asia. The plant typically grows in shallow water or damp soil, with slender, grass-like leaves that form dense clumps. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually green or brown, and they appear in late spring to early summer.

Nisius riparius plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic invertebrates.
