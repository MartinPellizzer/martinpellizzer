Noronhia crassiramosa

Noronhia crassiramosa, commonly known as the thick-branch noronhia, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its dense, woody stems and small, elliptical leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers during the summer months. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds.

Noronhia crassiramosa is often found in dry, open habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal uses.
