Napeanthus bracteatus

Napeanthus bracteatus, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its velvety, dark green leaves and clusters of showy, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows as a perennial herb, often reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its flowers are borne on short pedicels and are known for their vibrant colors and long-lasting bloom.

Napeanthus bracteatus is widely cultivated as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and ease of care.
