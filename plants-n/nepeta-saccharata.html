Nepeta saccharata

Nepeta saccharata, commonly known as sweet catmint, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are often used in herbal teas and have a mild minty aroma.

Nepeta saccharata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
