Navarretia myersii subsp. deminuta

Navarretia myersii subsp.

deminuta, commonly known as the dwarf navarretia, is a low-growing, herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It typically reaches heights of less than 10 centimeters and features delicate, linear leaves arranged in a rosette pattern.

The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in late spring, which are clustered in a loose inflorescence. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal prairies and sandy soils.

This subspecies is characterized by its compact form and is well adapted to coastal and inland habitats.
