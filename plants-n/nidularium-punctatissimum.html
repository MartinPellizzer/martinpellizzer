Nidularium punctatissimum

Nidularium punctatissimum, commonly known as the spotted air plant, is a species of epiphytic bromeliad native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, white trichomes, giving it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces vibrant orange or red flowers that bloom from the center of the rosette, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. Its ability to store water in its leaves makes it well adapted to its natural habitat, which often includes trees in humid, shaded environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is relatively easy to care for in the right conditions.
