Nectandra macrophylla

Nectandra macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leafed nectandra, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its broad, elliptical leaves that are often glossy and dark green in color. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by woody fruits containing aromatic seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland tropical rainforests.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local cultures.
