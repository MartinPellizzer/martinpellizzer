Nectandra producta

Nectandra producta, commonly known as the yellow sandalwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its aromatic, yellowish wood that is highly valued for its fragrance and durability. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a smooth margin. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically appearing in axillary inflorescences, and it produces small, round fruits that contain a single seed.

The tree is often found in lowland forests and is known for its role in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
