Nesaea brevipes

Nesaea brevipes, commonly known as the short-footed bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as cliffs and slopes, preferring full sun to partial shade. The plant features a short, stout stem with opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are showy, bell-shaped, and range in color from blue to purple, appearing in clusters at the top of the stem.

Nesaea brevipes is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and arid landscapes.
