Narcissus assoanus subsp. praelongus

Narcissus assoanus subsp.

praelongus, commonly known as the long-stemmed daffodil, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its narrow, strap-like leaves and a single, elongated flower with a yellow perianth.

The floral structure consists of six tepals that are typically arranged in a cup-like formation. This subspecies is often found in dry, open habitats such as rocky slopes and grasslands.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its early spring blooms.
