Negundo kingii

Negundo kingii, commonly known as the King's ash, is a species of ash tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and opposite, ovate leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are followed by winged fruits called samaras. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.

The plant is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to different climates.
