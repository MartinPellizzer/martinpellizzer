Nivenia sceptrum

Nivenia sceptrum, commonly known as the bluebell of the rock, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in spring, adding a vibrant splash of color to its rocky habitat. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming a dense rosette near the ground.

Nivenia sceptrum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural environment.
