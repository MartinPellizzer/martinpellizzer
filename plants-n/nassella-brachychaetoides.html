Nassella brachychaetoides

Nassella brachychaetoides, commonly known as the narrow-haired needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and dense, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, spikelet-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and have a distinctive tufted appearance at the base.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
