Neubeckia fulva

Neubeckia fulva, commonly known as the golden groundsel, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It features bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Neubeckia fulva is often found in open woods, meadows, and along stream banks.
