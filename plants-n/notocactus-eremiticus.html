Notocactus eremiticus

Notocactus eremiticus, commonly known as the moon cactus, is a unique cactus species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange coloration, which is due to the presence of a grafted scion from a different species, typically Hemionitis arifolia. The plant has a short, thick stem with areoles that bear spines and flowers. It is a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its compact size and vibrant appearance.

The botanical features of Notocactus eremiticus make it both visually appealing and relatively easy to care for.
