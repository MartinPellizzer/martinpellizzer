Neobuxbaumia mezcalaensis var. multiareolata

Neobuxbaumia mezcalaensis var. multiareolata, commonly known as the Mexican cactus or the areolate cactus, is a species native to the mountainous regions of central Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stems that are covered in prominent areoles, which are clusters of spines and buds. The plant produces striking yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months. Its spines are typically slender and may vary in color from green to reddish.

This cactus is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.
