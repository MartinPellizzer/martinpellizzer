Navarretia debilis

Navarretia debilis, commonly known as the slender navarretia, is a low-growing annual plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, elongated stems and small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically blooms in early spring, producing clusters of four-petaled flowers that attract pollinators such as bees. Navarretia debilis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Its short life cycle and ephemeral nature make it a unique component of its native ecosystems.
