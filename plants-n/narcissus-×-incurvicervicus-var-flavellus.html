Narcissus × incurvicervicus var. flavellus

Narcissus × incurvicervicus var. flavellus, commonly known as the yellow daffodil, is a hybrid narcissus species characterized by its bright yellow petals and a distinctive, narrow trumpet-shaped corona.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is native to the Iberian Peninsula, where it thrives in sunny, well-drained soils. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with strap-like, grassy foliage that emerges in early spring.

Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding a vibrant splash of color to gardens and natural landscapes.
