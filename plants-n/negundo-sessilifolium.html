Negundo sessilifolium

Negundo sessilifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf catalpa, is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are typically sessile, meaning they lack petioles. The tree produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are clustered in racemes, often appearing in late spring. Its fruit is a slender, pod-like capsule that splits open to release seeds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
