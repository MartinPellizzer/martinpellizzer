Neurolaena lobata

Neurolaena lobata, commonly known as the golden weed, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is an annual herb that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. Its seeds are enclosed in capsule-like fruits, which are dispersed by wind.

Neurolaena lobata is often found in disturbed soils and can become invasive in certain environments.
