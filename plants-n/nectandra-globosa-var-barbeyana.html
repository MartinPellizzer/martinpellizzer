Nectandra globosa var. barbeyana

Nectandra globosa var. barbeyana, commonly known as the fragrant wood or Barbey's nectandra, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic, dark-colored wood. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by dark, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a glossy surface.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
