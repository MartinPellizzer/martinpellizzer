Nassella mucronata

Nassella mucronata, commonly known as the slender needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and shrublands. Its foliage is usually blue-gray in color, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Nassella mucronata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in xeriscapes due to its drought tolerance.
