Nectandra purpurea

Nectandra purpurea, commonly known as the purple sandarac tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark brown wood that is highly valued for its fragrance and durability. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a glossy appearance. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by small, round fruits.

The tree is also noted for its ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments within tropical rainforests.
