Nephrolepis exaltata f. muscosa

Nephrolepis exaltata f. muscosa, commonly known as the velvet fern, is a fern species characterized by its distinctive, soft, and velvety fronds.

It is native to tropical regions of the Americas and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The fronds are typically leathery and have a dense, hairy texture, contributing to its unique appearance. This fern is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in indoor conditions.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both gardeners and plant enthusiasts.
