Neoregelia burlemarxii subsp. burlemarxii

Neoregelia burlemarxii subsp.

burlemarxii, commonly known as the Burlemarx's bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the rainforests of Brazil. It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage, which often displays shades of red, orange, and yellow, creating a visually stunning display.

The plant features a rosette-shaped arrangement of fleshy, overlapping leaves that form a central cup to collect rainwater. Its flowers, though less showy than its foliage, are typically small and clustered in a spike at the center of the rosette.

This species is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its tolerance of low light and ability to thrive in humid environments.
