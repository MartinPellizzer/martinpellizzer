Neomammillaria conspicua

Neomammillaria conspicua, commonly known as the showy mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and bright green to blue-green stems that are adorned with prominent, white to yellowish tubercles. The plant produces small, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in late spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for arid environments.

Neomammillaria conspicua is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements.
