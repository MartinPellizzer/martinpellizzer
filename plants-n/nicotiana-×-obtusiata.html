Nicotiana × obtusiata

Nicotiana × obtusiata, commonly known as the bluntleaf nightshade, is a hybrid species within the Solanaceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of trumpet-shaped, violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and thrives in full sun with well-drained soil. Its flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as moths and butterflies.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in horticultural research due to its genetic diversity.
