Nepeta linearis

Nepeta linearis, commonly known as catmint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, arranged in whorls along the stems. Its leaves are narrow and linear, giving it a distinctive appearance, and they emit a strong mint-like scent when crushed.

Nepeta linearis is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
