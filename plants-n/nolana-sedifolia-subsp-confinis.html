Nolana sedifolia subsp. confinis

Nolana sedifolia subsp.

confinis, commonly known as the silverleaf nolana, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America. It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, woolly hairs that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment.

The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers during the warmer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in dry gardens for ornamental purposes.
