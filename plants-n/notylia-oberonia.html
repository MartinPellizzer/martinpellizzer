Notylia oberonia

Notylia oberonia, commonly known as the slender orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, slender stems and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories where it relies on mycorrhizal associations for nutrient uptake. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, adapted to conserve moisture in its natural habitat.

Notylia oberonia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
