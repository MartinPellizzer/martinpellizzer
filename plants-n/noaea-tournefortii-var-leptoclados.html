Noaea tournefortii var. leptoclados

Noaea tournefortii var. leptoclados, commonly known as the slender leaf desert sandwort, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Mediterranean.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with small, linear leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in hot, dry environments. The plant produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers during the spring and early summer. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

It is often found in sandy or gravelly soils and is well-suited to harsh, coastal or inland desert conditions.
