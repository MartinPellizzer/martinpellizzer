Neoalsomitra suberosa

Neoalsomitra suberosa, commonly known as the rough-stemmed alismar, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in wetlands and marshy areas, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. The plant features slender, cylindrical stems that are often covered in small, rough hairs. Its leaves are elongated and narrow, with a smooth or slightly serrated margin.

The flowers are small and clustered, usually white or pale green, and are borne on slender pedicels.
