Nectandra spumea

Nectandra spumea, commonly known as the fragrant sandarac tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its bark and wood are used in traditional medicine and for producing a resin similar to frankincense.

The plant's ecological importance lies in its role as a habitat for various insect and bird species.
