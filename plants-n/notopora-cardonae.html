Notopora cardonae

Notopora cardonae, commonly known as the saguaro cactus, is a striking member of the Cactaceae family native to the Sonoran Desert of North America.

It is characterized by its tall, tree-like structure with a thick, ribbed trunk that can grow up to 50 feet in height. The plant features prominent, spiny branches that bear yellow flowers in the spring. Notopora cardonae plays a vital role in its ecosystem, providing shelter and food for various desert species.

Its unique adaptations, such as its ability to store water in its stem, allow it to thrive in arid environments.
