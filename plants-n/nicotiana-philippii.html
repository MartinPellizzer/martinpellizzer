Nicotiana philippii

Nicotiana philippii, commonly known as the Philippi's tobacco, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are tubular and attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees.

The plant is also noted for its alkaloid content, similar to other tobacco species.
