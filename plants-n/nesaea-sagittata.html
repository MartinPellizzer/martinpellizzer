Nesaea sagittata

Nesaea sagittata, commonly known as the arrowhead plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its arrow-shaped leaves, which give it its common name, and its clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, preferring humid and shady conditions. It is a perennial herb that can reach heights of up to 1 meter, with slender stems and alternate leaves.

The flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance in its native environment.
