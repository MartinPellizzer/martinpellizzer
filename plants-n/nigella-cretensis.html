Nigella cretensis

Nigella cretensis, commonly known as the Cretan cumin, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Crete.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, oval-shaped seeds that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, making it a hardy perennial in suitable climates.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ecological studies.
