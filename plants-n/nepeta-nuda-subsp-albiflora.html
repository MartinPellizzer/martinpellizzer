Nepeta nuda subsp. albiflora

Nepeta nuda subsp.

albiflora, commonly known as the whiteflower catmint, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe. It features slender, upright stems covered in fine hairs and produces clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a mild mint-like fragrance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowering habit.
