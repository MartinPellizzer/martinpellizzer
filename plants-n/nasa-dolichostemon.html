Nasa dolichostemon

Nasa dolichostemon, commonly known as the moonflower, is a tropical plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night and close by morning. The plant has thick, succulent stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.

The flowers are pollinated primarily by moths and other nocturnal insects.
