Neomammillaria collinsii

Neomammillaria collinsii, commonly known as the Collins' nipple cactus, is a small, spineless cactus native to Mexico.

It features a globular stem with prominent areoles and white to yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is characterized by its dense, woolly coating and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Neomammillaria collinsii is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its compact size and unique appearance.
