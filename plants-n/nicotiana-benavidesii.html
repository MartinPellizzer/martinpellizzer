Nicotiana benavidesii

Nicotiana benavidesii, commonly known as the Benavides tobacco, is a species native to the high Andean regions of South America.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. It is adapted to cool, high-altitude environments and thrives in well-drained soils.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
