Nepenthes × deslogesii

Nepenthes × deslogesii, commonly known as the hybrid pitcher plant, is a fascinating carnivorous plant resulting from the cross between Nepenthes alata and Nepenthes ventricosa.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Borneo, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its large, vibrant red pitchers that secrete a digestive fluid to trap and digest insects. Its unique morphology and carnivorous habits make it a popular subject in botanical studies and horticulture.

Due to its hybrid nature, it exhibits a range of morphological variations, making it both intriguing and challenging to cultivate.
