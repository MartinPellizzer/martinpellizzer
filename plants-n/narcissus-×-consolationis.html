Narcissus × consolationis

Narcissus × consolationis, commonly known as the Consolation daffodil, is a hybrid species within the genus Narcissus, characterized by its striking yellow flowers.

It typically features a single, trumpet-shaped corona that is often longer than the tepals, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant is native to the Iberian Peninsula and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. It blooms in early spring, adding vibrant color to gardens and natural landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value, it is widely cultivated for its beauty and resilience in various climates.
