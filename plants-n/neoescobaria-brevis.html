Neoescobaria brevis

Neoescobaria brevis, commonly known as the short-stemmed stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing form with fleshy, blue-green leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
