Narcissus marvieri subsp. tazaensis

Narcissus marvieri subsp.

tazaensis, commonly known as the Taza daffodil, is a rare and endemic species native to the mountainous regions of northern Morocco. It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers with a white throat, typically blooming in late spring.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and is adapted to grow in rocky, well-drained soils. It is often found in alpine meadows and is a subject of interest for botanists due to its limited distribution.

Conservation efforts are ongoing to protect this unique botanical species from habitat loss and environmental changes.
