Nepenthes phyllamphora var. macrantha

Nepenthes phyllamphora var. macrantha, commonly known as the giant Malaysian pitcher plant, is a striking carnivorous plant native to Borneo.

It is characterized by its large, pitcher-shaped traps that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant's leaves are typically broad and waxy, with a distinctive red or orange coloration on the inner surface of the pitchers. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires consistent moisture and nutrient-rich soil.

This variety is noted for its robust growth and is a popular specimen among carnivorous plant enthusiasts.
