Nunnezharia pumila

Nunnezharia pumila, commonly known as the dwarf nunnezharia, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes.

It typically grows to a height of less than 10 centimeters, featuring compact, rosette-forming leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are succulent and adapted to retain moisture, allowing it to thrive in arid, high-altitude environments.

Nunnezharia pumila is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and hardy nature.
