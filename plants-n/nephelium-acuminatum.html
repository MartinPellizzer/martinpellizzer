Nephelium acuminatum

Nephelium acuminatum, commonly known as rambutan, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, spiny exterior covering its fruits, which are typically bright red or yellow. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is edible, with a sweet, fibrous pulp surrounding a single seed.

The tree is valued for its ornamental value as well as its economic importance in agriculture.
