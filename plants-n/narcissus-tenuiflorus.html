Narcissus tenuiflorus

Narcissus tenuiflorus, commonly known as the slender-flowered daffodil, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, yellow flowers with a cup-shaped corona. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is a perennial herb that produces showy blooms in early spring, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both horticultural and ecological studies.
