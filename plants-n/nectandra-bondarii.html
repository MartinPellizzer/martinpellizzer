Nectandra bondarii

Nectandra bondarii, commonly known as the Malayan sandalwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, fragrant wood that is highly valued in traditional medicine and perfumery. The tree produces small, white flowers followed by oval-shaped berries containing smooth, black seeds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a leathery texture with a strong, spicy scent.

Nectandra bondarii thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland and hill forest regions.
