Nicotiana lancifolia

Nicotiana lancifolia, commonly known as the lancifolia tobacco plant, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of violet or blue flowers that bloom in the summer. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking appearance and fragrance.

Additionally, it contains alkaloids, including nicotine, which makes it related to other tobacco species.
