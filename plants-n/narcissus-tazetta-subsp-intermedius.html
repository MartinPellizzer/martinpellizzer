Narcissus tazetta subsp. intermedius

Narcissus tazetta subsp.

intermedius, commonly known as the intermediate daffodil, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange, typically blooming in early spring.

The plant features narrow, strap-like leaves that are grass-like in appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinct subspecies within the broader narcissus genus.
