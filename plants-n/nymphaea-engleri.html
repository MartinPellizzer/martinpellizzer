Nymphaea engleri

Nymphaea engleri, commonly known as the Engler water lily, is a species of aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy, white to pinkish flowers that bloom above the water surface. The plant has broad, floating leaves that provide habitat for various aquatic organisms. Its roots are submerged, anchoring it in shallow water bodies.

Nymphaea engleri plays a role in water purification and supports local biodiversity in its native ecosystems.
