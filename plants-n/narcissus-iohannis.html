Narcissus iohannis

Narcissus iohannis, commonly known as the John's daffodil, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to the Iberian Peninsula and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically bright yellow. The plant produces a single, erect stem with one or two blooms, each featuring a long, narrow corona. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners in temperate climates.

The flower's vibrant color and elegant form make it a notable addition to spring gardens.
