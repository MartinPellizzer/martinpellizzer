Nopalea auberi

Nopalea auberi, commonly known as the Auber's prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its spiny, paddle-shaped stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months, which are followed by edible, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are typically reduced to spines, a feature common in many cacti to reduce water loss and deter herbivores.

Nopalea auberi plays an important ecological role by providing food and shelter for various desert animals.
