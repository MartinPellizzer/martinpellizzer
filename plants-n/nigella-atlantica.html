Nigella atlantica

Nigella atlantica, commonly known as the Atlantic nigella, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, yellowish flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its seeds, which are small and black, are often used in culinary applications and traditional medicine.
