Neurolaena macrophylla

Neurolaena macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaf false hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 20 cm in length. The plant produces showy yellow flowers with five petals, often accompanied by five stamens. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats.

The species is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
