Notholaena peninsularis

Notholaena peninsularis, commonly known as the Peninsular rock fern, is a rare and endemic species found primarily in the coastal regions of southern California.

It is a small, terrestrial fern that thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to the Mediterranean climate of its habitat. The plant has pinnate, serrated leaves that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length, with a dark green coloration. It reproduces via spores rather than seeds, and its life cycle is closely tied to the seasonal rainfall patterns of its environment.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in conservation assessments.
