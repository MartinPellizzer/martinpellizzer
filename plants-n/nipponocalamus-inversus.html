Nipponocalamus inversus

Nipponocalamus inversus, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or reverse bamboo, is a species of grass belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its unique growth pattern, with leaves curving inward rather than outward. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in mountainous regions. Its culms are slender and hollow, with a smooth, greenish-brown surface.

Nipponocalamus inversus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
