Nemorinia vernalis

Nemorinia vernalis, commonly known as spring broom, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Nemorinia vernalis is often noted for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils and its role in supporting early pollinators.
