Nectandra rigida

Nectandra rigida, commonly known as the rigid nectandra, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by elliptical, woody fruits. Its bark and leaves contain essential oils, which have been used in traditional medicine.

Nectandra rigida is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native habitats.
