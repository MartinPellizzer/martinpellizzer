Nephelium coriaceum

Nephelium coriaceum, commonly known as the mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is characterized by its thick, leathery rind and segmented, sweet-tart fruit. The plant thrives in humid climates with well-drained soil and requires a warm, tropical environment to flourish. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a glossy green surface.

The mangosteen tree is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated for both its fruit and aesthetic appeal.
