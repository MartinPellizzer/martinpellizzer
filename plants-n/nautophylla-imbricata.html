Nautophylla imbricata

Nautophylla imbricata, commonly known as the sea-leaf fern, is a unique aquatic plant characterized by its delicate, ribbon-like fronds that resemble the structure of seashells.

It thrives in brackish and saltwater environments, often found in coastal regions and estuaries. The plant's leaves are arranged in overlapping layers, giving it a distinctive imbricate appearance. Nautophylla imbricata plays a vital role in stabilizing sediments and providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.

Its adaptability to varying salinity levels makes it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
