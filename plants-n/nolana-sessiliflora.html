Nolana sessiliflora

Nolana sessiliflora, commonly known as the clustered silverweed, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is well adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are sessile, meaning they are attached directly to the stem without a stem-like base.

Nolana sessiliflora is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
