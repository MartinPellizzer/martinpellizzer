Nepeta sulphurea

Nepeta sulphurea, commonly known as yellow catmint, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its aromatic foliage and showy blooms make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking both ornamental and medicinal value.

Nepeta sulphurea is also known for its calming effects and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
