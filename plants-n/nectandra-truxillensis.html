Nectandra truxillensis

Nectandra truxillensis, commonly known as the Truxillensis Sandarac, is a tropical tree native to the Caribbean region, particularly found in parts of Cuba and Hispaniola.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic, often fragrant leaves. The tree produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by oval, woody fruits containing aromatic seeds.

Its wood is valued for its unique scent and is used in traditional crafts and as a source of natural resins.
