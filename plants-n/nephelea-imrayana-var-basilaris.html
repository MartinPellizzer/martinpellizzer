Nephelea imrayana var. basilaris

Nephelea imrayana var. basilaris, commonly known as the Basilar Nephelea, is a rare and striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant, often colorful flowers that bloom in clusters, displaying intricate patterns typical of orchids. The plant is native to specific regions in Southeast Asia, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are elongated and lance-shaped, with a waxy surface that helps retain moisture.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a valuable subject for botanical study and conservation efforts.
