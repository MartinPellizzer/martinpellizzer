Narcissus jacetanus subsp. vasconicus

Narcissus jacetanus subsp.

vasconicus, commonly known as the Pyrenean daffodil, is a species native to the Pyrenees Mountains. It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and a single, elongated flower with a yellow perianth.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils. It blooms in early spring, producing a striking, cup-shaped flower with a central coronal ring.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
