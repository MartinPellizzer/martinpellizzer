Notodanthonia richardsonii

Notodanthonia richardsonii, commonly known as the golden grass or Richardson's grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and produces small, golden-yellow inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. It is well adapted to dry conditions and is often found in sandy or loamy soils.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various native fauna.
