Nepeta lophanthus

Nepeta lophanthus, commonly known as mountain catmint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It features aromatic, gray-green foliage and produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant is characterized by its square, hairy stems and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats. It is a member of the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.

Nepeta lophanthus is also used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported calming properties.
