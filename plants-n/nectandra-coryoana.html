Nectandra coryoana

Nectandra coryoana, commonly known as the broad-leafed nectandra, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurel trees, and is characterized by its aromatic, evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by oval, woody fruits containing aromatic seeds. Its leaves are thick and leathery, often with a glossy surface, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Nectandra coryoana is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine by indigenous communities.
