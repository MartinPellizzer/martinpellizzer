Nogra filicaulis

Nogra filicaulis, commonly known as the featherleaf nogra, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its small yellow flowers bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Nogra filicaulis is adapted to survive in dry conditions, with deep roots that help it access water from underground sources.
