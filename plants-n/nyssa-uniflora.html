Nyssa uniflora

Nyssa uniflora, commonly known as the single-flower dogwood, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers in the spring, followed by dark blue berries that are edible for birds. Its bark is smooth and often has a reddish or brownish color, and it is often found in moist, wooded areas.

Nyssa uniflora plays an important role in local ecosystems, providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
