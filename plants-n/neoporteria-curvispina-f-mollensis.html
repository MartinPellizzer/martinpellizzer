Neoporteria curvispina f. mollensis

Neoporteria curvispina f. mollensis, commonly known as the mollensian form of the curly-spined neoporteria, is a rare and distinctive plant species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique leaf morphology, featuring elongated, lance-shaped leaves with prominent veins. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories where it can receive adequate moisture. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually appearing in late spring to early summer.
