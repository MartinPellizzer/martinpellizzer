Neomammillaria boedekeriana

Neomammillaria boedekeriana, commonly known as the Boedeker's pincushion cactus, is a small, spineless cactus native to Mexico.

It features a globular stem covered in white, woolly spines that give it a fluffy appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in the spring, which are often borne on short pedicels. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and slow growth, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
