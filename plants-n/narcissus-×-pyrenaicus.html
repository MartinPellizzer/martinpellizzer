Narcissus × pyrenaicus

Narcissus × pyrenaicus, commonly known as the Pyrenean daffodil, is a hybrid plant resulting from crosses between several narcissus species.

It is native to the Pyrenees Mountains and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers with a distinctive crown. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and prefers well-drained, sunny soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it blooms in early spring.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant and long-lasting flowers.
