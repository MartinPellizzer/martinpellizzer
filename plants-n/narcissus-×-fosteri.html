Narcissus × fosteri

Narcissus × fosteri, commonly known as the Foster's daffodil, is a hybrid species within the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers with a distinctive, often cup-shaped corona. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, featuring strap-like leaves and showy blooms that appear in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Due to its ornamental value, it is widely cultivated in temperate regions around the world.
