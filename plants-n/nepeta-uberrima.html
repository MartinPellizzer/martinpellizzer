Nepeta uberrima

Nepeta uberrima, commonly known as catmint, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in herbal remedies due to their calming properties.

Nepeta uberrima is also popular in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
