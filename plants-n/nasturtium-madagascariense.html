Nasturtium madagascariense

Nasturtium madagascariense, commonly known as the Madagascar watercress, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and yellow or orange flowers. The plant is known for its peppery taste, similar to watercress, and is often used in local cuisines. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is typically found near streams or in wetlands.

Due to its unique flavor and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a garden plant.
