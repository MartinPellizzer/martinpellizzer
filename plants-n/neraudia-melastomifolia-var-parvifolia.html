Neraudia melastomifolia var. parvifolia

Neraudia melastomifolia var. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-flowered melastome, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically has opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are usually clustered in inflorescences and feature five petals, often with a distinctive pattern of spots or bands.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
