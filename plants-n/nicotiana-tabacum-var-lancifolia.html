Nicotiana tabacum var. lancifolia

Nicotiana tabacum var. lancifolia, commonly known as the lancifoliate tobacco, is a variant of the tobacco plant characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves.

It is native to South America, particularly regions of Brazil and Argentina, and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant is known for its robust growth and high nicotine content, making it a significant species in both agricultural and botanical studies. Its leaves are often used in the production of traditional tobacco products and have been studied for their potential in pharmaceutical applications.

The species also exhibits ornamental value due to its striking foliage and flowering characteristics.
