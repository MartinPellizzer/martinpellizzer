Nicotiana sordida

Nicotiana sordida, commonly known as the smoky nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its dark purple to blackish leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and peppers. It is often found in moist, shaded areas and has a distinctive, somewhat unpleasant odor.

Due to its toxic properties, it is not recommended for cultivation in gardens.
