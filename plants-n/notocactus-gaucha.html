Notocactus gaucha

Notocactus gaucha, commonly known as the "Gaucha Cactus," is a species native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are typically green and covered in spines. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the evening, adding a striking visual element to its appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique morphology and adaptability make it a popular choice for both ornamental and horticultural purposes.
