Nicotia rustica

Nicotia rustica, commonly known as wild tobacco, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant features hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers. Its most distinctive characteristic is the production of capsule fruits containing numerous elliptical seeds.

Nicotia rustica is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
