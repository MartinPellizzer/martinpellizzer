Narcissus × montielanus

Narcissus × montielanus, commonly known as the Montiel daffodil, is a hybrid species within the genus Narcissus.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers with a distinctive trumpet-shaped corona. The plant typically grows to a height of 20–40 cm, with narrow, grass-like leaves. It is native to Spain and thrives in well-drained soils in full sun.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant spring blooms.
