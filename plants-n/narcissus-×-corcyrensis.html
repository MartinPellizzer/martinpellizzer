Narcissus × corcyrensis

Narcissus × corcyrensis, commonly known as the Corcyrene daffodil, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Narcissus poeticus and Narcissus tazetta.

It is characterized by its large, showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that typically range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its early spring blooming.

The foliage consists of strap-like leaves that are evergreen in some varieties, contributing to its visual appeal throughout the growing season.
