Neoporteria vallenarensis

Neoporteria vallenarensis, commonly known as the Valle del Arenal plant, is a unique species native to the Andean regions of Chile.

It is a shrub characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, often blooming during the spring and early summer. Its growth habit is adapted to the cool, humid conditions of its native habitat, and it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
