Najas latifolia

Najas latifolia, commonly known as broadleaf watermilfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It has slender, branching stems and small, whorled leaves that are typically broad and oval in shape. The plant produces tiny, green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, often emerging above the water surface. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Najas latifolia is often found in slow-moving waters such as ponds, lakes, and streams.
