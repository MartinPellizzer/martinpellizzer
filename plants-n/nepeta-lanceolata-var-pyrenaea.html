Nepeta lanceolata var. pyrenaea

Nepeta lanceolata var. pyrenaea, commonly known as Pyrenean catmint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine, hairy hairs, and produces clusters of small, pale blue to violet flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often aromatic and slightly fuzzy to the touch. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its fragrant foliage and long-lasting blooms.
