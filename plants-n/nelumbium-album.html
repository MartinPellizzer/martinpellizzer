Nelumbium album

Nelumbium album, commonly known as the sacred lotus, is a perennial aquatic plant native to Asia.

It features large, showy white flowers that open in the morning and close at dusk. The plant has broad, heart-shaped leaves that float on the water's surface. Its roots grow in muddy substrates, and it thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats.

Nelumbium album is noted for its symbolic significance in various cultures and its use in traditional medicine.
