Neurolepis laegaardii

Neurolepis laegaardii, commonly known as the Cape mountain daisy, is a perennial herb native to the Western Cape region of South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in rocky outcrops and fynbos vegetation.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
