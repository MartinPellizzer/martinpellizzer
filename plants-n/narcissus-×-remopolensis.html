Narcissus × remopolensis

Narcissus × remopolensis, commonly known as the Giant Daffodil, is a hybrid species of daffodil native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers with a distinctive crown-like pattern. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with long, strap-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Botanically, it belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is closely related to other narcissus species.
