Nemesia pubescens

Nemesia pubescens, commonly known as the hairy nemesia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and red. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Nemesia pubescens is well-suited for rock gardens and borders due to its compact growth habit and prolonged blooming period.
