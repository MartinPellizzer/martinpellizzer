Nania florida

Nania florida, commonly known as the Florida bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

Nania florida is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small insects and pollinators.
