Nothapodytes collina

Nothapodytes collina, commonly known as the white flame tree, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom in clusters, often appearing in late winter or early spring. The plant has compound leaves with elongated leaflets and produces small, woody pods that contain seeds. It thrives in humid climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and parks in warm regions.
