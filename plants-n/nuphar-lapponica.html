Nuphar lapponica

Nuphar lapponica, commonly known as the European water lily, is a perennial aquatic plant native to northern Europe and parts of Asia.

It features broad, floating leaves that are typically green with a waxy surface, and its flowers are showy, with yellow stamens and a white or pinkish pistil. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes. Its roots are rhizomatous, allowing it to spread vegetatively and form dense colonies.

Nuphar lapponica plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various water organisms.
