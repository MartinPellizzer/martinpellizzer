Napaea dioecia

Napaea dioecia, commonly known as the common napaea, is a flowering plant species belonging to the family Polygonaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, and often hairy leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it is monoecious, meaning it has both male and female flowers on the same individual. Napaea dioecia is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and botanical studies.
