Neopaxia australasica

Neopaxia australasica, commonly known as the Southern Paddle Fern, is a rare and unique fern species native to the temperate regions of southern Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, broad, paddle-shaped fronds that are typically dark green and leathery in texture. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest undergrowth or along stream banks. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, a common feature among ferns.

Neopaxia australasica plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, contributing to soil stability and providing shelter for small organisms.
