Nidularium amazonicum var. paulistanum

Nidularium amazonicum var. paulistanum, commonly known as the Amazonian basket plant, is a striking bromeliad native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a water-holding cup, a feature typical of many bromeliads. The plant's foliage is often a vibrant green with a waxy texture, and it can produce striking red or orange flowers that bloom from the center of the rosette. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This variety is appreciated for its ornamental value and its ability to support local biodiversity by providing a habitat for small insects and amphibians.
