Nepeta argentea

Nepeta argentea, commonly known as silver catmint, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features slender, upright stems covered in soft, silvery-gray foliage that gives it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant blue or violet flowers in whorls along the stem, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Nepeta argentea is also valued for its aromatic properties and historical use in herbal medicine.
