Nepeta paktiana

Nepeta paktiana, commonly known as the Afghan catnip, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their medicinal and ornamental uses. The plant features square stems, opposite leaves, and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to purple. It is often cultivated for its pleasant scent and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Nepeta paktiana is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential calming and therapeutic properties.
