Nonea pulla var. armena

Nonea pulla var. armena, commonly known as Armenian dwarf lily, is a small, herbaceous perennial plant native to the Caucasus region.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces delicate, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant typically grows to a height of about 10-15 centimeters, making it well-suited for rock gardens or alpine settings. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking low-maintenance, ornamental plants.
