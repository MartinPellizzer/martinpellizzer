Nasturtium indicum var. integrifolium

Nasturtium indicum var. integrifolium, commonly known as the common nasturtium, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, bright green leaves with a waxy texture and its vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible parts, including the leaves and flowers, which have a peppery taste. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

The plant is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
