Nipponocalamus japonicus

Nipponocalamus japonicus, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests and stream sides. It is valued for its ornamental appearance and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
