Nepeta stricta

Nepeta stricta, commonly known as catmint, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Catmint is often grown for its fragrant foliage and ornamental value in gardens.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil.
