Narcissus angustifolius var. verbanensis

Narcissus angustifolius var. verbanensis, commonly known as the Verbanian daffodil, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Alpine regions of Europe.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces striking yellow flowers with a single, upright tepal. The plant typically blooms in early spring, adding a vibrant touch to mountainous landscapes. It prefers well-drained, sandy soils and full sun exposure.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
