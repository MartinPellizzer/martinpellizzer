Notopappus pectinatus

Notopappus pectinatus, commonly known as the goldenrod hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small clusters of flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its yellow flowers bloom in late summer.

Notopappus pectinatus is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its bright, cheerful appearance.
