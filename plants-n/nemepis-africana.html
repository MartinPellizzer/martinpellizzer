Nemepis africana

Nemepis africana, commonly known as the African orchid, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, showy flowers that often resemble other orchid species. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky outcrops. Its leaves are usually broad and lance-shaped, with a waxy surface that helps retain moisture.

The flowers bloom in spring and are pollinated primarily by insects, contributing to its ecological significance in its native habitat.
