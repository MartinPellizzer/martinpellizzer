Navia latifolia

Navia latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved navia, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring. Navia latifolia thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial shade.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
