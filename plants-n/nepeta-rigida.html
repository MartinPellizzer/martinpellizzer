Nepeta rigida

Nepeta rigida, commonly known as catmint, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like mint and lavender. The plant features square, hairy stems and opposite leaves that are typically gray-green in color. Its small, tubular blue or purple flowers bloom in whorls along the stems, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Nepeta rigida is known for its strong, minty fragrance and is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to repel pests.
