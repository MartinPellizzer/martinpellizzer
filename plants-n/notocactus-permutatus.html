Notocactus permutatus

Notocactus permutatus, commonly known as the Permutatus Cactus, is a species of cactus native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stem and prominent areoles, which are typical features of the Cactaceae family. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom during the warmer months, adding ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance, it is a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts and collectors.
