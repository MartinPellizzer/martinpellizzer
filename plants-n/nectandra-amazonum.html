Nectandra amazonum

Nectandra amazonum, commonly known as the Amazonian sandarac tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, resinous wood and glossy, dark green leaves. The tree produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by small, round fruits containing seeds. Its bark and leaves contain essential oils, which have been used traditionally for their medicinal properties.

Nectandra amazonum plays a vital role in the ecological balance of its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
