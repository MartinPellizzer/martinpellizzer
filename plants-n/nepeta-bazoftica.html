Nepeta bazoftica

Nepeta bazoftica, commonly known as Bazoft catmint, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage is often fragrant and can be used in traditional medicine.

Nepeta bazoftica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in gardens.
