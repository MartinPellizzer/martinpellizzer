Nabiasodendron speciosum

Nabiasodendron speciosum, commonly known as the splendid forest tree, is a tall, evergreen species native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green, providing dense shade under its canopy. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically white or pale yellow, which bloom during the rainy season. Its bark is thick and ridged, often with a reddish-brown hue, and it can grow up to 30 meters in height.

This species plays a vital role in its ecosystem, offering habitat and food for various wildlife species.
