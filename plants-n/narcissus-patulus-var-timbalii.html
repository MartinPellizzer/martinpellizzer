Narcissus patulus var. timbalii

Narcissus patulus var. timbalii, commonly known as the Timbalai daffodil, is a rare and distinctive variety of daffodil native to the island of Timor.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers with a bright yellow perianth and a narrow, elongated corona. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats with well-drained soil, often in coastal or mountainous regions. Its foliage consists of strap-like leaves that are dark green and upright.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
