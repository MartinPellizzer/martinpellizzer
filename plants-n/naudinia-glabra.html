Naudinia glabra

Naudinia glabra, commonly known as the smooth naudinia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, with trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. Its fruit is a legume that contains several seeds, and it often thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Naudinia glabra is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
