Niphaea peruviana

Niphaea peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian water lily, is a species of aquatic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves and showy, white or pink flowers that bloom above the water surface. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes. Its leaves are typically rounded with a waxy coating that helps repel water.

Niphaea peruviana plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
