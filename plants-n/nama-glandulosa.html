Nama glandulosa

Nama glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular nama, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features erect, branched stems covered in glandular hairs, which give it its scientific name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a hairy texture.

Nama glandulosa is adapted to arid environments and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.
