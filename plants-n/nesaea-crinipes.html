Nesaea crinipes

Nesaea crinipes, commonly known as the silky spurge, is a herbaceous plant native to the southern United States.

It features alternate, ovate leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in loose inflorescences. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open fields, roadsides, and along creek banks.

Its rapid growth and ability to spread through rhizomes make it a common weed in various habitats.
