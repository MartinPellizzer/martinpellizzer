Najas affinis

Najas affinis, commonly known as the common water milfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments across Europe, Asia, and North America.

It belongs to the family Najadaceae and is characterized by its whorled arrangement of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically found in the water, often in clusters near the surface. Najas affinis thrives in still or slow-moving water and is often found in ponds, lakes, and canals.

It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
