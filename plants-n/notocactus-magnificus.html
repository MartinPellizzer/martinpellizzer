Notocactus magnificus

Notocactus magnificus, commonly known as the magnificus cactus, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant features areolate, ribbed stems covered with white, woolly spines that provide protection from herbivores. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, appearing at the top of the plant during the summer months.

Notocactus magnificus is well adapted to arid environments, requiring minimal water and full sunlight to thrive.
