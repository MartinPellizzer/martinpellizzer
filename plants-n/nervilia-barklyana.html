Nervilia barklyana

Nervilia barklyana, commonly known as the Barking Lily, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces striking white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in racemes, often with a subtle fragrance. Its leaves are long and strap-like, typically emerging from the base of the plant.

Nervilia barklyana thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found near streams or in swampy areas.
