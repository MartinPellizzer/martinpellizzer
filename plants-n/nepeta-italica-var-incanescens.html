Nepeta italica var. incanescens

Nepeta italica var. incanescens, commonly known as the gray catmint, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems covered in dense, grayish-white hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are aromatic and slightly fuzzy, with a minty scent when crushed.

This variety is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and low maintenance requirements.
