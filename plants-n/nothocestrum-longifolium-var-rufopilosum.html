Nothocestrum longifolium var. rufopilosum

Nothocestrum longifolium var. rufopilosum, commonly known as the red-hair passionflower, is a variant of the passionflower genus native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves with a reddish underside, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers with a unique structure featuring corona-like appendages. Its growth habit is typically vine-like, with tendrils that help it climb supports.

Botanically, it is notable for its genetic diversity and adaptability to various soil conditions.
