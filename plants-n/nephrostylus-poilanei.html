Nephrostylus poilanei

Nephrostylus poilanei, commonly known as the Madagascar orchid, is a unique species native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, pendulous inflorescences. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, anchoring itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers exhibit a range of colors, including white, pink, and purple, with intricate patterns.

Due to its rarity and habitat specificity, it is considered a conservation concern.
