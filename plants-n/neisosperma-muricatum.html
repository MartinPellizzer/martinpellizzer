Neisosperma muricatum

Neisosperma muricatum, commonly known as the mountain currant, is a shrub native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its small, edible berries that are typically red or yellow in color. The plant produces these berries in clusters, and they are often used in local cuisines for their tart flavor. Botanically, it belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is known for its ornamental value as well as its medicinal properties.

The leaves are usually alternate and have a rough, textured surface, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
