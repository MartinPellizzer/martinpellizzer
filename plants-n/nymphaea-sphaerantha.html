Nymphaea sphaerantha

Nymphaea sphaerantha, commonly known as the globe water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, round, floating leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white, with a prominent yellow center. Its roots are rhizomatous, allowing it to spread and form dense colonies in still water.

Nymphaea sphaerantha plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
