Nazia aliena

Nazia aliena, commonly known as the silverleaf nightshade, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, silver-gray leaves and clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces violet-black berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in disturbed soils and can often be found in hedgerows or along roadsides.

Due to its invasive nature in some regions, it is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
