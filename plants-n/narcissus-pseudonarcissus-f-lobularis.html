Narcissus pseudonarcissus f. lobularis

Narcissus pseudonarcissus f. lobularis, commonly known as the lobed daffodil, is a botanical variant of the common daffodil.

It is characterized by its distinctive, lobed petals that give it a unique appearance compared to typical daffodils. This cultivar is part of the Amaryllidaceae family and is native to Europe. It typically blooms in early spring, producing bright yellow flowers with a central, trumpet-shaped corona.

The plant prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
