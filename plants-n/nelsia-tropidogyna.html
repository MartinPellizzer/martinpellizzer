Nelsia tropidogyna

Nelsia tropidogyna, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and grows in a shrub-like form. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Nelsia tropidogyna is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in temperate climates.
