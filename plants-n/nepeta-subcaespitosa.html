Nepeta subcaespitosa

Nepeta subcaespitosa, commonly known as catmint, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and clusters of small, fragrant blue or purple flowers. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Nepeta subcaespitosa is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
