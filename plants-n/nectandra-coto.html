Nectandra coto

Nectandra coto, commonly known as the Coto or Coto wood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic, reddish-brown heartwood. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by elliptical, leathery fruits containing a single seed. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a smooth or slightly serrated margin.

Nectandra coto is valued for its durable wood and is often found in moist, shaded environments within tropical rainforests.
