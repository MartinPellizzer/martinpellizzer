Narcissus byzantinus

Narcissus byzantinus, commonly known as the Byzantine daffodil, is a species native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters, with narrow, grass-like leaves. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, often thriving in rocky or coastal environments.

Narcissus byzantinus is a hardy perennial that blooms in early spring, adding vibrant color to gardens and natural landscapes.
