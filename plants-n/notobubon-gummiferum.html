Notobubon gummiferum

Notobubon gummiferum, commonly known as the gummed bubblebush, is a unique plant species characterized by its distinctive, bubble-like structures on its leaves.

These structures are specialized secretory glands that produce a viscous, resinous substance, giving the plant its common name. The plant is native to specific regions in South America, where it thrives in humid, tropical climates. Its botanical features include opposite, ovate leaves and small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow.

The resin produced by Notobubon gummiferum has been studied for its potential medicinal and industrial applications.
