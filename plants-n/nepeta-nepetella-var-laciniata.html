Nepeta nepetella var. laciniata

Nepeta nepetella var. laciniata, commonly known as the cut-leaved catmint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features aromatic, hairy stems and lance-shaped, finely cut leaves that give it a delicate, feathery appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, pale blue to lavender flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its fragrance is known to repel certain insects, adding both aesthetic and functional value to the landscape.
