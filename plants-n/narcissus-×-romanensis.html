Narcissus × romanensis

Narcissus × romanensis, commonly known as the Roman daffodil, is a hybrid species within the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers with a prominent, trumpet-shaped corona. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, featuring strap-like, grassy leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

This variety is noted for its early blooming habit, often flowering in late winter or early spring.
