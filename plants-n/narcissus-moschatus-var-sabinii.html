Narcissus moschatus var. sabinii

Narcissus moschatus var. sabinii, commonly known as the Sabin's daffodil, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers with a strong, sweet fragrance. The plant typically blooms in early spring, with yellow petals surrounding a central, often darker, throat. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its early blooming habit and distinctive scent.
