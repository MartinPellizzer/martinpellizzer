Nymphaea lutea var. minima

Nymphaea lutea var. minima, commonly known as the dwarf yellow water lily, is a compact aquatic plant native to parts of Africa.

It features heart-shaped, floating leaves that are typically a bright yellow-green in color. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that bloom in the early morning and close during the heat of the day. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, preferring shallow waters with soft, muddy substrates.

Its small size makes it a popular choice for ornamental ponds and water gardens.
