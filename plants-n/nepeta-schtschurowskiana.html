Nepeta schtschurowskiana

Nepeta schtschurowskiana, commonly known as Siberian catmint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often fragrant and have a mint-like aroma, which is due to the presence of essential oils.

Nepeta schtschurowskiana is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
