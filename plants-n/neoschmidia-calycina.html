Neoschmidia calycina

Neoschmidia calycina, commonly known as the golden spider orchid, is a striking epiphytic orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers, which resemble intricate spider webs, hence its common name. The plant typically grows on tree branches or rocks, absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air. Its leaves are strap-shaped and glossy, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Neoschmidia calycina thrives in warm, humid environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.
