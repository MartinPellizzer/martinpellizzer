Nania flava

Nania flava, commonly known as the yellow nania, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its delicate, yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slight pubescence, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Nania flava is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ease of care.
