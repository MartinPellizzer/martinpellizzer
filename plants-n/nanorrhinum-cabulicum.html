Nanorrhinum cabulicum

Nanorrhinum cabulicum, commonly known as the Afghan bellflower, is a flowering plant native to regions of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are often narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Nanorrhinum cabulicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
