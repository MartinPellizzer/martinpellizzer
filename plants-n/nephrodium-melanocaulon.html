Nephrodium melanocaulon

Nephrodium melanocaulon, commonly known as the black-stemmed nephroid, is a rare fern species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, glossy stems and intricate, feather-like fronds that exhibit a delicate balance of light and shadow. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in dense forests with high moisture levels. Its reproductive structures, including sporangia, are clustered along the fronds, contributing to its unique botanical appearance.

Due to habitat loss, Nephrodium melanocaulon is considered endangered, making conservation efforts crucial for its survival.
