Narcissus × triflorus

Narcissus × triflorus, commonly known as the triple-flowered daffodil, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Narcissus tazetta and Narcissus cyclaminiflorus.

It is characterized by its distinctive three-rowed flower structure, with three outer petals, three inner petals, and a central corona. The plant typically grows to a height of 20–40 cm and produces bright yellow flowers in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its ornamental value lies in its unique floral form and vibrant color.
