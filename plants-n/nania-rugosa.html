Nania rugosa

Nania rugosa, commonly known as the rough nania, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, clustered leaves that have a rough, textured surface. The plant produces delicate, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Nania rugosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively low maintenance once established.
