Nepenthes tentaculata

Nepenthes tentaculata, commonly known as the tentacle pitcher plant, is a tropical carnivorous plant native to Borneo.

It is characterized by its large, pitcher-shaped traps that secrete a digestive fluid to break down insect prey. The plant's unique feature is the presence of long, tentacle-like structures on its leaves, which aid in trapping prey. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires specific soil conditions to flourish.

Its striking appearance and specialized adaptations make it a fascinating subject in botanical studies.
