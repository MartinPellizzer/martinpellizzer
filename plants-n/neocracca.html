Neocracca

Neocracca, commonly known as the marsh orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Neocracca has lance-shaped leaves and produces a unique, intricate floral structure typical of orchids.

It plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by supporting pollinators and maintaining soil stability.
