Nepeta xylorhiza

Nepeta xylorhiza, commonly known as catnip, is a flowering plant in the Lamiaceae family.

It is native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa, and it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves contain volatile oils, including nepetalactone, which is responsible for its stimulating effect on cats.

It is often grown as an ornamental plant and for its historical use in herbal remedies.
