Nasturtium humifusum

Nasturtium humifusum, commonly known as the creeping nasturtium, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit, with stems that often lie close to the ground. The plant produces vibrant orange or yellow flowers, which are borne on slender stems and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a slightly peppery taste, making it a popular edible plant.

Nasturtium humifusum thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often used in gardens for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
