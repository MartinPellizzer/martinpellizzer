Nigella deserti

Nigella deserti, commonly known as desert nigella, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its seed pods are distinctive, containing numerous tiny black seeds that are often used in traditional medicine and cuisine.

Nigella deserti is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
