Nepeta menthoides

Nepeta menthoides, commonly known as catmint, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in herbal teas and have a minty, slightly menthol-like fragrance.

Nepeta menthoides is a hardy perennial that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.
