Narcissus × andujarensis

Narcissus × andujarensis, commonly known as the Andujar daffodil, is a hybrid species within the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking yellow petals and a narrow, upright flower stem. Native to the Iberian Peninsula, this plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight. It typically blooms in early spring, producing showy flowers that attract pollinators.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
