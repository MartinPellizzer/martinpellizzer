Nepeta sewerzowii

Nepeta sewerzowii, commonly known as the Siberian catmint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features aromatic, gray-green leaves and produces clusters of small, blue-violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is characterized by its square, hairy stems and is often found in rocky or dry habitats. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and medicinal plants.

Nepeta sewerzowii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential calming properties.
