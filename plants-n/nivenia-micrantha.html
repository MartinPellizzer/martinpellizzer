Nivenia micrantha

Nivenia micrantha, commonly known as the dwarf pincushion plant, is a small, flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited for arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are often surrounded by bracts that give it a pincushion appearance.

Nivenia micrantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a container plant.
