Nerine appendiculata

Nerine appendiculata, commonly known as the Cape lily, is a bulbous perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that form a dense rosette at the base. Its bulbs are adapted to store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions.

Nerine appendiculata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
