Nidorella ligulata

Nidorella ligulata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lily, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and striking, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects, contributing to its ecological role in its native habitat.

Nidorella ligulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable growing conditions.
