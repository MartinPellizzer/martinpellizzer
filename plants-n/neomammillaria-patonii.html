Neomammillaria patonii

Neomammillaria patonii, commonly known as the Paton's milk barrel cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are ribbed and covered in white, woolly spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are typically clustered at the top of the stem. It is a slow-growing cactus that thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water.

Neomammillaria patonii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and resilience.
