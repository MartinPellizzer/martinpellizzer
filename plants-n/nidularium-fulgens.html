Nidularium fulgens

Nidularium fulgens, commonly known as the golden star orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, bright yellow flowers that resemble stars. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are usually dark green and arranged in a rosette pattern, providing structural support for the inflorescence.

Nidularium fulgens is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant due to its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
