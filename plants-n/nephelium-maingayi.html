Nephelium maingayi

Nephelium maingayi, commonly known as the mangostan or mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant white flowers. The fruit is typically round, with a thick, bright red rind enclosing segmented, white, juicy flesh. The plant thrives in humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species in both horticulture and traditional medicine.
