Neodriessenia crystallina

Neodriessenia crystallina, commonly known as the crystalline orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, translucent petals that resemble glass, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often near streams or in dense forest undergrowth. Its flowers are small but highly fragrant, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is considered a valuable species in both horticulture and conservation efforts.
