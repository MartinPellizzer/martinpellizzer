Narcissus poetarum

Narcissus poetarum, commonly known as the poet's daffodil, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to the Iberian Peninsula and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers with a distinctive central corona. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers bloom in early spring, often before the leaves fully emerge, and are highly fragrant.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and historical significance.
