Narcissus linnaeanus var. barlae

Narcissus linnaeanus var. barlae, commonly known as the Barlae daffodil, is a variant of the common daffodil characterized by its distinctive floral morphology.

It features a trumpet-shaped corona that is typically longer than the perianth segments, giving it a striking appearance. The plant is native to southern Europe and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge in early spring.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant yellow blooms.
