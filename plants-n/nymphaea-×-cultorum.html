Nymphaea × cultorum

Nymphaea × cultorum, commonly known as the garden water lily, is a hybrid aquatic plant belonging to the family Nymphaeaceae.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom above the water surface. The plant typically grows in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes. Its flowers are usually pink to white in color and have a distinctive cup-shaped structure.

Nymphaea × cultorum is cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to provide habitat for aquatic life.
