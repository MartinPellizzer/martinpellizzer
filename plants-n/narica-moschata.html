Narica moschata

Narica moschata, commonly known as the Mexican tamarind, is a small evergreen tree native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, yellowish flowers and small, round fruits that resemble tamarinds. The plant has pinnate leaves with opposite arrangement and produces a sticky, resinous sap that has been used traditionally for medicinal and aromatic purposes. Its fruit is edible, with a sour taste, and is often used in local cuisines.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its fragrant blossoms.
