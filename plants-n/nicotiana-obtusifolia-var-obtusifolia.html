Nicotiana obtusifolia var. obtusifolia

Nicotiana obtusifolia var. obtusifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved tobacco, is a herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features large, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white. The plant is part of the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and potatoes. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological studies.
