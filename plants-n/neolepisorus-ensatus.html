Neolepisorus ensatus

Neolepisorus ensatus, commonly known as the feathered shield fern, is a species of fern native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are often described as resembling feathers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Neolepisorus ensatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
