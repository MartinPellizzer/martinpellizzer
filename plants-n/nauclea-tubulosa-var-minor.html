Nauclea tubulosa var. minor

Nauclea tubulosa var. minor, commonly known as the small pipevine or lesser nauclea, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that are often violet or purple in color. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

The plant is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.
