Navia caricifolia

Navia caricifolia, commonly known as the water chestnut plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to the wetlands of Southeast Asia.

It features striking, arrow-shaped leaves that are often submerged or floating, adapted to aquatic environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, typically emerging from the water surface. Its roots are edible and have a crunchy, nutty flavor, making it a valuable food source in some regions.

Navia caricifolia thrives in shallow, still water and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
