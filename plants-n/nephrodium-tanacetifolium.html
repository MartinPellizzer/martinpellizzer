Nephrodium tanacetifolium

Nephrodium tanacetifolium, commonly known as the golden ragwort, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped, yellowish-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in alpine and arid regions.

The species is noted for its medicinal properties, with historical use in traditional herbal remedies.
