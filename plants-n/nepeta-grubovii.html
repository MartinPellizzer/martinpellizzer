Nepeta grubovii

Nepeta grubovii, commonly known as Carpathian catmint, is a perennial herb native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite leaves that are usually gray-green in color. The plant produces small, blue or violet flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Nepeta grubovii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
