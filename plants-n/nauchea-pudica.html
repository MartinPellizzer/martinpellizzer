Nauchea pudica

Nauchea pudica, commonly known as the slender leaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, round fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Nauchea pudica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
