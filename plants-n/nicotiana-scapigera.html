Nicotiana scapigera

Nicotiana scapigera, commonly known as the scapigera tobacco, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family.

It is native to South America and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and showy blue or violet flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, elliptical seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Nicotiana scapigera is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
