Neoptychocarpus chocoensis

Neoptychocarpus chocoensis, commonly known as the Chocoan tamarind tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant is notable for its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments, often found in the understory of dense tropical forests. The tree's ecological importance lies in its role as a habitat provider and its potential contributions to soil fertility through nitrogen fixation.
