Nuxia tomentella var. typica

Nuxia tomentella var. typica, commonly known as the woolly nuxia, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive, fluffy appearance. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in a fine, silvery down, contributing to its unique texture.

Nuxia tomentella var. typica is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
