Nasa puracensis

Nasa puracensis, commonly known as the Peruvian night-blooming jasmine, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of Peru.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshade plants, and is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that open at night. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are ovate and glossy, while its stems are often covered in fine hairs.

Nasa puracensis is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological role in its native habitat.
