Najas marina subsp. brachycarpa

Najas marina subsp.

brachycarpa, commonly known as narrow-fruited watermilfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America. It belongs to the family Najadaceae and is characterized by its slender, ribbon-like leaves that are often arranged in whorls.

The plant produces small, dark blue to black fruits that are enclosed in a cup-like structure, distinguishing it from other Najas species. It typically grows in shallow, slow-moving waters and plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for microorganisms and invertebrates.

Its morphology and reproductive structures are adapted to its submerged lifestyle, making it an important component of freshwater vegetation.
