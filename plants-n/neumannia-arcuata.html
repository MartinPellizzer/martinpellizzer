Neumannia arcuata

Neumannia arcuata, commonly known as the curled leaf orchid, is a unique orchid species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, curled leaves that form a rosette shape, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale pink, typically blooming during the wet season. Neumannia arcuata thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is often cultivated in specialized botanical gardens.
