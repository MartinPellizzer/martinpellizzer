Noccaea brevistyla subsp. pseudorivularis

Noccaea brevistyla subsp.

pseudorivularis, commonly known as the narrow-styled watercress, is a member of the Brassicaceae family. It is a small, herbaceous annual plant characterized by its rosette of pinnately divided leaves.

The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in racemes. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils, often found near streams or in wet meadows.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native habitat.
