Narcissus × mariae-angelorum

Narcissus × mariae-angelorum, commonly known as the Marie-Angelique daffodil, is a hybrid perennial bulbous plant native to the Netherlands.

It features striking, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange, with a distinctive, often recurved corona. The plant typically grows to a height of 20–40 cm, producing multiple blooms per stem during late spring. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a basal rosette.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and is commonly cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, showy displays.
