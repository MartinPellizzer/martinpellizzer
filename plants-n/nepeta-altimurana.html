Nepeta altimurana

Nepeta altimurana, commonly known as the alpine catmint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, thriving in temperate climates. Its small, tubular flowers are usually blue or purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Nepeta altimurana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and pleasant fragrance.
