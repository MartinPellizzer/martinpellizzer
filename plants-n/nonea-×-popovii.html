Nonea × popovii

Nonea × popovii, commonly known as the hybrid popovii nonea, is a rare and intriguing plant species resulting from the cross between Nonea microphylla and Nonea macrophylla.

It is characterized by its unique combination of traits from both parent species, including varying leaf sizes and floral structures. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and requires a sunny to partially shaded environment. Its flowers are small and clustered, often displaying a range of colors from white to pink.

Due to its limited natural range and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a valuable subject for botanical study and conservation efforts.
