Nipponocalamus chino var. gracilis

Nipponocalamus chino var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender Chinese bamboo, is a variant of the Chinese bamboo species characterized by its slender, gracefully arching culms.

It is native to eastern Asia, particularly found in regions of China and Japan, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often a vibrant green, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It is a clump-forming grass that grows to a moderate height, making it suitable for garden use in shaded or partially shaded areas.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both horticultural and ecological landscapes.
