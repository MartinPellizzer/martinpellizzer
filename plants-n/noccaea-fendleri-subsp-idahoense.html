Noccaea fendleri subsp. idahoense

Noccaea fendleri subsp.

idahoense, commonly known as Idaho mountain spinach, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits.

The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils in alpine and subalpine environments. It has basal leaves that are pinnately divided, and its stems are often hairy.
