Nemcia emarginata

Nemcia emarginata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered flowering structures called spikes. The plant typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies. Its root system is fibrous, helping it anchor in saturated soils and absorb nutrients efficiently.

Nemcia emarginata plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
