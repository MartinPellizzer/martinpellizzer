Navia marahuacae

Navia marahuacae, commonly known as the Mexican mountain bean, is a flowering plant native to the highlands of Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at elevations above 2,000 meters. Its flowers are usually pale pink to white, and it produces edible seeds that have been traditionally used by indigenous communities.

Navia marahuacae is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil in mountainous regions.
