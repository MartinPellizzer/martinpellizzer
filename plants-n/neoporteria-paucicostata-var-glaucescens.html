Neoporteria paucicostata var. glaucescens

Neoporteria paucicostata var. glaucescens, commonly known as the glaucous neoporteria, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It features lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive bluish-green hue due to a waxy coating. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun exposure.

Its botanical characteristics make it a unique addition to specialized gardens and botanical collections.
