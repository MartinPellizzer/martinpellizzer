Nepeta padamica

Nepeta padamica, commonly known as the Himalayan catnip, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue or lavender flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their calming properties.

Nepeta padamica thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rocky or alpine gardens.
