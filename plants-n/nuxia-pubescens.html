Nuxia pubescens

Nuxia pubescens, commonly known as the silky sandthorn, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant features small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, and its leaves are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It has adapted to withstand drought conditions, making it resilient in harsh climates.

The species is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.
