Navia acaulis

Navia acaulis, commonly known as the barren navia, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its lack of stems, with the leaves forming a dense rosette near the ground. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically white or pale green, arranged in clusters. Navia acaulis thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and taxonomic studies.
