Narcissus × guadalupensis var. navezuelensis

Narcissus × guadalupensis var. navezuelensis, commonly known as the Navarre daffodil, is a hybrid species native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, strap-like leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that typically range in color from yellow to white. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It blooms in early spring, often before the leaves are fully developed.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant and long-lasting flowers.
