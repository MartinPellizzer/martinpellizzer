Nicotiana acuminata var. sileniflora

Nicotiana acuminata var. sileniflora, commonly known as the silversword, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is characterized by its rosette of silver-gray, fleshy leaves and produces striking spikes of violet or blue flowers. The plant is adapted to the dry, volcanic soils of its native habitat and thrives in full sun. It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is closely related to tobacco species.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is considered an important component of Hawaii's native flora.
