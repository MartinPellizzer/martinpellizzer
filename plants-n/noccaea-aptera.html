Noccaea aptera

Noccaea aptera, commonly known as the smooth rockcress, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Europe and Asia.

It has a compact, mat-like growth habit with small, rounded leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white, waxy powder. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, often in clusters at the ends of the stems. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from shallow soil layers, making it well-suited to rocky and sandy soils.

Noccaea aptera is often found in disturbed habitats and is considered a pioneer species in ecological succession.
