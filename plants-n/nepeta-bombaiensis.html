Nepeta bombaiensis

Nepeta bombaiensis, commonly known as the Bombai catmint, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically gray-green and have a mint-like scent when crushed.

Nepeta bombaiensis is often grown for its ornamental value and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
