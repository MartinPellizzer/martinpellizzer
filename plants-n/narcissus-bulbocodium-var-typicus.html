Narcissus bulbocodium var. typicus

Narcissus bulbocodium var. typicus, commonly known as the alpine daffodil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, rounded bulbs that produce delicate, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring, with bright yellow petals and a white or green corona. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is a popular subject in botanical studies and horticulture.
