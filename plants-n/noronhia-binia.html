Noronhia binia

Noronhia binia, commonly known as the Brazilian nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and tobacco. The plant features small, white or purple flowers and produces berries that are toxic to humans and animals. Its leaves are ovate with a slightly hairy texture, and it thrives in warm, humid climates.
