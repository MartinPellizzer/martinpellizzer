Niara

Niara, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its velvety, waxy leaves and showy, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows as a perennial herb, often with a compact rosette form. Its flowers come in a variety of colors, including shades of purple, pink, white, and blue.

Niara thrives in moist, well-drained soil and requires indirect sunlight for optimal growth.
