Narcissus tazetta var. etruscus

Narcissus tazetta var. etruscus, commonly known as the Etruscan daffodil, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and white, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens. It is closely related to other daffodil species and is often cultivated for its vibrant, early blooming characteristics.

The variety is noted for its compact growth habit and ability to thrive in well-drained soil.
