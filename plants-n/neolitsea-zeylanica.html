Neolitsea zeylanica

Neolitsea zeylanica, commonly known as the Ceylon laurier or Indian laurier, is a small evergreen tree native to southern Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves and fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in humid, well-drained soils. Its bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their purported therapeutic properties.

The fruit, which is small and berry-like, contains seeds that are dispersed by animals.
