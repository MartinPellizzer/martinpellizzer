Nuttallia digitata

Nuttallia digitata, commonly known as the common rockrose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, which bloom in the spring. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, and it has a compact growth habit.

Nuttallia digitata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping for its hardiness and drought tolerance.
