Narcissus cavanillesii subsp. mauretanicus

Narcissus cavanillesii subsp.

mauretanicus, commonly known as the Mauretanian daffodil, is a rare and striking plant native to the arid regions of North Africa. It is characterized by its large, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, often with a distinct greenish tinge.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand hot, dry conditions. Its foliage consists of narrow, strap-like leaves that emerge from a basal rosette.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil and full sun.
