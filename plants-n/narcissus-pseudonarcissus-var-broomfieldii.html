Narcissus pseudonarcissus var. broomfieldii

Narcissus pseudonarcissus var. broomfieldii, commonly known as the Broomfield daffodil, is a variant of the common daffodil characterized by its distinctive floral morphology.

It features a narrow, elongated corona that is typically shorter than the perianth segments, giving it a unique appearance. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with a trumpet-shaped crown, which is a hallmark of the daffodil genus. It is native to the British Isles and thrives in well-drained, sunny soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant spring blooms.
