Narcissus romieuxii var. zaianicus

Narcissus romieuxii var. zaianicus, commonly known as the Zaian narcissus, is a rare and ornamental plant native to the mountainous regions of Morocco.

It is characterized by its slender, strap-like leaves and clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers with a distinctive cup-like shape. The plant typically blooms in early spring, adding a delicate touch to its natural habitat. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a valuable species for botanical conservation efforts.
