Nesaea fruticosa

Nesaea fruticosa, commonly known as the shrub violets, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces clusters of small, violet-blue flowers with five petals, which bloom in late winter to early spring. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it prefers well-drained soils in full sun.

Nesaea fruticosa is often found in rocky or coastal areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
