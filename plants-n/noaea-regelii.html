Noaea regelii

Noaea regelii, commonly known as the desert sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its low, mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are usually narrow and fleshy, adapted to conserve water in harsh environments.

Noaea regelii is often found in sandy or gravelly soils and is well-suited to survive in extreme desert conditions.
