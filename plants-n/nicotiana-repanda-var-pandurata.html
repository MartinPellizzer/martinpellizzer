Nicotiana repanda var. pandurata

Nicotiana repanda var. pandurata, commonly known as the velvetleaf tobacco, is a species native to South America.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and thrives in warm, humid climates. Its flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.

The leaves are hairy and have a distinct, aromatic scent, which is a notable botanical feature.
