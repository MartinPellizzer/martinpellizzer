Nectandra laxa

Nectandra laxa, commonly known as the broadleaf sandarac tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The tree produces a resin that has been historically used for various purposes, including varnishes and incense. Its growth habit is typically shrub-like to small tree-like, with a dense canopy and rough bark.

Nectandra laxa is valued for its ecological role in providing habitat and its potential economic value through its resin.
