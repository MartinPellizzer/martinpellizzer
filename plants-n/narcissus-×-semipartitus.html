Narcissus × semipartitus

Narcissus × semipartitus, commonly known as the partially split daffodil, is a hybrid plant resulting from crosses between different narcissus species.

It is characterized by its unique floral morphology, featuring a split corona that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with strap-shaped, grass-like leaves. Its flowers bloom in early spring, producing bright yellow petals and a divided, often white or greenish, central cup.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and is commonly cultivated in gardens for its vibrant and eye-catching display.
