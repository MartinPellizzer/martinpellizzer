Neomammillaria polyedra

Neomammillaria polyedra, commonly known as the polygonal cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and ribbed structure, which helps it conserve water in arid environments. The plant produces pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. Its stems are covered in white to light green spines, providing protection from herbivores.

Neomammillaria polyedra is a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor cactus collections due to its hardy nature and aesthetic appeal.
