Najas polonica

Najas polonica, commonly known as the narrow-leaf water plantain, is a aquatic plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in still or slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and lakes. The plant has slender, ribbon-like leaves that float on the water surface, while its roots anchor it to the substrate. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually submerged, and it plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small organisms.

Najas polonica is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize sediments and improve water quality.
