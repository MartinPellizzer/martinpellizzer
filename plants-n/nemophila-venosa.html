Nemophila venosa

Nemophila venosa, commonly known as the silver vetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing annual with small, delicate blue or purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are lance-shaped and often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and meadows.

Nemophila venosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
