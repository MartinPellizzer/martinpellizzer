Nepeta turanica

Nepeta turanica, commonly known as Turkish catnip, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their medicinal and culinary uses. The plant features square stems, opposite leaves, and small, fragrant flowers that range in color from blue to purple. It is often grown for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its calming properties.

Nepeta turanica thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens and borders.
