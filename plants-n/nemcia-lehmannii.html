Nemcia lehmannii

Nemcia lehmannii, commonly known as the Lehmann's nemcia, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in grasslands and rocky soils, thriving in well-drained conditions. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often clustered in loose inflorescences. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
