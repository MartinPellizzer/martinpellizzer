Nonea pulla subsp. scabrisquamata

Nonea pulla subsp.

scabrisquamata, commonly known as the rough-scaled nonea, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy, two-lipped flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its rough, scaly texture on the stems and leaves, which gives it its common name. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

The plant is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and along riverbanks.
