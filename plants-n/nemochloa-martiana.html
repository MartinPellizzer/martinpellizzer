Nemochloa martiana

Nemochloa martiana, commonly known as the water clover, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its creeping growth habit and dense, mat-forming structure. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is often found in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of water bodies, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils.

Due to its ability to tolerate waterlogged conditions, it plays an important role in stabilizing soil and preventing erosion in aquatic habitats.
