Nigella lancifolia

Nigella lancifolia, commonly known as the Persian cowpea, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is native to the Middle East and Central Asia, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid climates. The plant features slender, branching stems and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early summer. Its seeds, which are oval and black in color, are often used in cooking and traditional medicine.

Nigella lancifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated for both its aesthetic and practical uses.
