Nepeta glomerulosa subsp. ghorana

Nepeta glomerulosa subsp.

ghorana, commonly known as the Kashmiri catnip, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas. It features aromatic, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive scent.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale purple, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or alpine gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine, it is a sought-after plant among horticulturists and botanists.
