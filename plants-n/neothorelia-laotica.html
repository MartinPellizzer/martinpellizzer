Neothorelia laotica

Neothorelia laotica, commonly known as the Laotian orchid, is a rare flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as mountain forests. Its leaves are elongated and strap-like, while its roots are fibrous and adapted for nutrient absorption.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
