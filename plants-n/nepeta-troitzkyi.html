Nepeta troitzkyi

Nepeta troitzkyi, commonly known as the Siberian catmint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, blue or violet flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom in late summer. It is often used in traditional medicine and has a mild minty scent.

Nepeta troitzkyi is relatively drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils.
