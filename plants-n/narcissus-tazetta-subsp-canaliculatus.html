Narcissus tazetta subsp. canaliculatus

Narcissus tazetta subsp.

canaliculatus, commonly known as the Persian daffodil, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Caucasus region. It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or white, with a distinctive, narrow corona.

The plant produces narrow, grass-like leaves and has a compact growth habit. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and containers.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its early spring blooms.
