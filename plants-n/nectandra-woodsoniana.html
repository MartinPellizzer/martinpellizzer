Nectandra woodsoniana

Nectandra woodsoniana, commonly known as the yellow sand-wood, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and avocado trees. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, reddish-brown heartwood, which is highly valued for its durability and workability. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a distinctive aromatic scent.

Nectandra woodsoniana is also noted for its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments within tropical rainforests.
