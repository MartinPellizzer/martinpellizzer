Nigrina viscosa

Nigrina viscosa, commonly known as the sticky nightshade, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that produce a sticky substance, giving it its common name. Its small, bell-shaped flowers are typically purple or violet in color.
