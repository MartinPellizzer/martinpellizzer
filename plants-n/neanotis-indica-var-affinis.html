Neanotis indica var. affinis

Neanotis indica var. affinis, commonly known as the Indian nettle or Indian nettle variant, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Urticaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems covered in fine hairs and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, typical of the Urticaceae family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Neanotis indica var. affinis is known for its stinging hairs, which can cause skin irritation upon contact.
