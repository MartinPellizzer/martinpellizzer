Nicolasia coronata subsp. coronata

Nicolasia coronata subsp.

coronata, commonly known as the crownflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color.

The plant has a compact growth habit with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its prolonged blooming period, which extends from spring to early summer.
