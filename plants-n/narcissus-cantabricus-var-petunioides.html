Narcissus cantabricus var. petunioides

Narcissus cantabricus var. petunioides, commonly known as the Cantabrian daffodil, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces a single, showy flower with a trumpet-shaped corona. The flower is typically yellow with a white or pale yellow corona, and it blooms in early spring. The plant is adapted to rocky and coastal environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, early blooming flowers.
