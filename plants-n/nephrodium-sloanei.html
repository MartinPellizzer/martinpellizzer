Nephrodium sloanei

Nephrodium sloanei, commonly known as the Sloane's nephroid, is a rare and unique fern species native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its distinctive, kidney-shaped fronds that are deeply lobed and have a velvety texture. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often near streams or in dense forest understories. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are arranged in a unique pattern along the fronds.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, Nephrodium sloanei is considered endangered and is of significant interest to botanists and conservationists.
