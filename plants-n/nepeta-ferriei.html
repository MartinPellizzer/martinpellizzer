Nepeta ferriei

Nepeta ferriei, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada catmint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of California.

It features hairy, aromatic leaves and produces small, tubular blue to purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in dry, well-drained conditions.

Nepeta ferriei is also noted for its historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
