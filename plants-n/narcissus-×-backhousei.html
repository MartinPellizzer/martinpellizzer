Narcissus × backhousei

Narcissus × backhousei, commonly known as the Amsterdam daffodil, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between N. tazetta and N. cyclamenus.

It is characterized by its distinctive, double-petaled flowers that are typically yellow or white in color. The plant is native to the Netherlands and is known for its robust growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.

The foliage consists of strap-like leaves that are narrow and grass-like in appearance.
