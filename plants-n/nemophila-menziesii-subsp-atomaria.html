Nemophila menziesii subsp. atomaria

Nemophila menziesii subsp.

atomaria, commonly known as the dwarf blue-eyed Mary, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a low-growing annual or biennial herb with small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant has hairy stems and leaves, and its flowers are typically less than 1 inch in diameter. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shaded areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
