Nephelium semiglaucum

Nephelium semiglaucum, commonly known as the white mangosteen, is a tropical fruit-bearing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves and clusters of fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant produces small, round fruits with a thick, purple rind and a sweet, white, segmented flesh. Its botanical features include a shallow root system and a preference for well-drained, acidic soils.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its edible fruit.
