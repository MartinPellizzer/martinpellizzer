Nephelaphyllum verruculosum

Nephelaphyllum verruculosum, commonly known as the velvetleaf orchid, is a unique epiphytic plant native to tropical regions.

It possesses fleshy, thick leaves that store water, adapting to its humid, shaded environments. The plant produces striking, showy flowers with intricate patterns, often in shades of purple and white. Its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and surrounding substrates.

Nephelaphyllum verruculosum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in specific ecological niches.
