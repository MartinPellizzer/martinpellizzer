Nassella asperifolia

Nassella asperifolia, commonly known as the roughleaf needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, gray-green leaves that have a rough texture. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed areas.

Nassella asperifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
