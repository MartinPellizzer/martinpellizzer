Narcissus × cazorlanus

Narcissus × cazorlanus, commonly known as the double daffodil, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Narcissus tazetta and Narcissus jonquilla.

It is characterized by its large, showy, double-petaled flowers that are typically yellow or white in color. The plant is native to the Iberian Peninsula and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, and it is relatively hardy in temperate climates.

The flowers bloom in early spring, adding a vibrant touch to gardens during the transition from winter to summer.
