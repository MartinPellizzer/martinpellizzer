Nephrolepis × pseudobiserrata

Nephrolepis × pseudobiserrata, commonly known as the Boston fern, is a hybrid fern that exhibits characteristics of both Nephrolepis hirsuta and Nephrolepis biserrata.

It is a popular ornamental plant known for its lush, feathery fronds that grow in a cascading manner. The plant thrives in humid environments and requires indirect light, making it well-suited for indoor cultivation. Its fronds are typically dark green and have a serrated edge, contributing to its aesthetic appeal.

Nephrolepis × pseudobiserrata is also valued for its ability to purify the air and its low maintenance requirements.
