Neoporteria curvispina f. descendens

Neoporteria curvispina f. descendens, commonly known as the descending neoporteria, is a unique horticultural form of the species.

It is characterized by its pendulous growth habit, with stems that droop gracefully from the plant's base. The leaves are typically elongated and narrow, with a smooth, glossy surface. Flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in shades of white or pale pink.

This form is prized for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in hanging baskets or as a decorative foliage plant.
