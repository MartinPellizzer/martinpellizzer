Nipponocalamus episetosus

Nipponocalamus episetosus, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or Japanese cliff bamboo, is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is native to Japan.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in mountainous regions. Its rhizomes are robust and help it spread through clumping growth.

Nipponocalamus episetosus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its graceful appearance.
