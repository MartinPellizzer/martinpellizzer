Nothoscordum montevidense subsp. minarum

Nothoscordum montevidense subsp.

minarum, commonly known as the Montevidense yarrow, is a flowering plant native to South America, specifically Uruguay and southern Brazil. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, clustered yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It is a perennial herb that can reach heights of up to 60 cm, with a fibrous root system.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
