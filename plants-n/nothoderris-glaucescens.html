Nothoderris glaucescens

Nothoderris glaucescens, commonly known as the blue-green acacia, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and small, compound leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in cylindrical spikes, which bloom in spring. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought conditions.

Nothoderris glaucescens is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature.
