Nothofagus nuda

Nothofagus nuda, commonly known as the naked nothofagus, is a species of evergreen tree native to southern South America.

It belongs to the Nothofagaceae family, which is closely related to the beech family. The plant is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and simple, lance-shaped leaves that are often glabrous. It produces small, clustered flowers followed by woody cones containing edible seeds.

This species is well adapted to temperate and subtropical climates, often found in forested areas with well-drained soils.
