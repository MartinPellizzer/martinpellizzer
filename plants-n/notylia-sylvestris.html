Notylia sylvestris

Notylia sylvestris, commonly known as the red swamp tarplant, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its reddish stems and leaves, which are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense spikes, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Notylia sylvestris is often found in wetland habitats, including marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems and a subject of ecological study.
