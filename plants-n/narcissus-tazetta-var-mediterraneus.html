Narcissus tazetta var. mediterraneus

Narcissus tazetta var. mediterraneus, commonly known as the Mediterranean daffodil, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to white, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is adapted to well-drained soils and full sun. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in gardens.

This variety is known for its hardiness and ability to naturalize over time.
