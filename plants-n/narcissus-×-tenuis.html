Narcissus × tenuis

Narcissus × tenuis, commonly known as the slender daffodil, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Narcissus tazetta and Narcissus poeticus.

It is characterized by its slender, strap-like leaves and a single, narrow trumpet-shaped flower. The plant typically blooms in early spring, producing bright yellow petals with a prominent central corona. It is native to southern Europe and thrives in well-drained, sunny soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
