Nigella romana

Nigella romana, commonly known as the Persian buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters and has palmately lobed leaves. Its seeds, which are small and black, are often used in cooking and traditional medicine.

Nigella romana thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun.
