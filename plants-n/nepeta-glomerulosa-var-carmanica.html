Nepeta glomerulosa var. carmanica

Nepeta glomerulosa var. carmanica, commonly known as Carman's catmint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright, branched stems that bear clusters of small, fragrant blue to purple flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and slightly fuzzy, with a strong mint-like aroma. This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual and aromatic qualities.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.
