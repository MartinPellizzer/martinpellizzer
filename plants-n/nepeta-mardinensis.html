Nepeta mardinensis

Nepeta mardinensis, commonly known as Mardin catmint, is a perennial herb native to Turkey.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage has a strong mint-like fragrance, which is often used in traditional medicine.

Nepeta mardinensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in gardens.
