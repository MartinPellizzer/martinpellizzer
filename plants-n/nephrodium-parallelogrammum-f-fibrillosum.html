Nephrodium parallelogrammum f. fibrillosum

Nephrodium parallelogrammum f. fibrillosum, commonly known as the fibrous nephroid, is a rare fern species characterized by its unique morphology.

It features fronds that are typically divided into a series of narrow, elongated segments, with a distinctive fibrous texture on the underside. The plant exhibits a compact growth habit, often forming dense clusters of foliage. Its leaves are usually arranged in a spiral pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

This fern thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
