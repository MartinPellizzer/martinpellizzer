Nectandra rectinervia

Nectandra rectinervia, commonly known as the black corymbia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, aromatic wood and glossy, elliptical leaves with prominent parallel veins. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by fleshy, reddish-brown fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological importance, it is valued in both horticulture and conservation efforts.
