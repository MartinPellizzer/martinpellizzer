Nemophila menziesii subsp. liniflora

Nemophila menziesii subsp.

liniflora, commonly known as the silverleaf flower, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 5 to 15 centimeters.

The plant features silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs, and produces clusters of small, five-petaled blue or purple flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or partially shaded environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
