Nemophila aurita var. arizonica

Nemophila aurita var. arizonica, commonly known as Arizona fairybells, is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial native to the southwestern United States.

It produces delicate, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue and purple, typically blooming in early spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it well-suited for rocky or gravelly habitats. Its foliage consists of small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This species is valued in native plant gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
