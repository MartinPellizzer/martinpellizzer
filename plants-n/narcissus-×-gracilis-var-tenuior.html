Narcissus × gracilis var. tenuior

Narcissus × gracilis var. tenuior, commonly known as the slender daffodil, is a hybrid plant characterized by its delicate, narrow leaves and elongated, trumpet-shaped flowers.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is native to the Iberian Peninsula, where it thrives in mild, moist climates. The flowers typically bloom in spring, displaying bright yellow petals with a white or pale center, and are often used in gardens for their ornamental value.

This variety is valued for its compact growth habit and ability to naturalize, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
