Nepeta fordii

Nepeta fordii, commonly known as Ford's catmint, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces spikes of blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are often aromatic and can be used in traditional medicine.

Nepeta fordii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
