Nemopanthus collinus

Nemopanthus collinus, commonly known as the silver leaf maple, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking white, almost translucent leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant has a slender trunk and can grow up to 30 meters tall, often found in moist, well-drained soils. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, appearing in clusters, while its fruits are samaras with wings for wind dispersal.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in forest ecosystems.
