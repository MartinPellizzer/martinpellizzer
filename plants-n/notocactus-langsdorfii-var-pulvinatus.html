Notocactus langsdorfii var. pulvinatus

Notocactus langsdorfii var. pulvinatus, commonly known as the pulvinated notocactus, is a unique cactus species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its short, thick stems that often appear swollen at the base, giving it a distinctive, almost bulbous appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers during the warmer months, which are typically borne on the upper parts of the stems. Its leaves are reduced to small, spiny tubercles, a feature common in many desert plants.

This variety is well adapted to dry environments, with a slow growth rate and a long lifespan, making it a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts.
