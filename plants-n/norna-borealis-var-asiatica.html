Norna borealis var. asiatica

Norna borealis var. asiatica, commonly known as the Asian northern bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It features striking blue to violet bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and it thrives in well-drained, loamy soils. It is often found in woodland edges and rocky slopes, preferring partial to full sunlight.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique floral structure and adaptability to cold climates.
