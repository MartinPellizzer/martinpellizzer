Nymphaea kakaduensis

Nymphaea kakaduensis, commonly known as the Kakadu water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to northern Australia.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom in the early morning and close in the heat of the day. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats, such as swamps and billabongs. Its broad, glossy green leaves float on the water's surface, providing shade and habitat for various aquatic life.

Nymphaea kakaduensis plays an important ecological role in maintaining water quality and supporting biodiversity in its native environment.
