Notocactus macrogonus

Notocactus macrogonus, commonly known as the giant night bloomer, is a member of the Cactaceae family and is native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, segmented stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces showy, nocturnal flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, opening at night. Its thick, fleshy stems are adapted to store water, making it well-suited for arid environments.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique blooming behavior.
