Nephelium obovatum

Nephelium obovatum, commonly known as the rambutan, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, spiny fruit covering, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are typically clustered in panicles. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate with a leathery texture.

The fruit is typically yellow or red when mature and is valued for its sweet, aromatic flesh.
