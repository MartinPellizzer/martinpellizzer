Nymphaea pygmaea f. distans

Nymphaea pygmaea f. distans, commonly known as the dwarf water lily, is a unique aquatic plant characterized by its small, floating leaves and showy, fragrant flowers.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats. The plant's leaves are typically rounded and measure only a few centimeters in diameter, while its flowers can be white, pink, or red, depending on the variety. It is often cultivated in ponds and water gardens for its ornamental value and ability to provide habitat for aquatic life.

The species is noted for its delicate appearance and its role in maintaining water ecosystem balance.
