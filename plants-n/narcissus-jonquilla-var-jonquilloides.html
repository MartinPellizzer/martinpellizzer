Narcissus jonquilla var. jonquilloides

Narcissus jonquilla var. jonquilloides, commonly known as the Spanish daffodil, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is characterized by its robust, often fragrant blossoms. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is noted for its hardiness and ability to naturalize over time.
