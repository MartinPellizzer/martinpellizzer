Nephrolepis cordifolia var. tambourinensis

Nephrolepis cordifolia var. tambourinensis, commonly known as the Tambourine Fern, is a tropical fern native to Southeast Asia.

It features fronds that are pinnately compound, with a distinctive heart-shaped outline that gives it its species name. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical forests. Its fronds are typically dark green and have a leathery texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

This variety is valued for its aesthetic qualities and is commonly cultivated as a houseplant.
