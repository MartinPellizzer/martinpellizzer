Neoregelia makoyana

Neoregelia makoyana, commonly known as the "Brazillian flame" or "Red neoregelia," is a striking bromeliad native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its vibrant, red-orange bracts that surround its compact, rosette-shaped foliage. The plant's leaves are broad, waxy, and often have a metallic sheen, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires high humidity and well-draining soil.

Neoregelia makoyana is popular as a houseplant due to its ability to bloom with showy inflorescences that last for several months.
