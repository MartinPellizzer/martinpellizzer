Notocactus spegazzinii var. aureus

Notocactus spegazzinii var. aureus, commonly known as the golden notocactus, is a unique cactus species native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stem that develops a distinctive golden hue as it matures. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its striking appearance and relatively low maintenance, it is a popular choice for collectors and succulent enthusiasts.
