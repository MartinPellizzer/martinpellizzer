Nymphozanthus advena var. macrophyllus

Nymphozanthus advena var. macrophyllus, commonly known as the large-leafed flame bush, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange to red flowers, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. The leaves are large, ovate, and have a glossy surface, contributing to its ornamental value. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for coastal and arid gardens.

The plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as birds and bees.
