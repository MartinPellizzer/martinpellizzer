Neuracanthus grandiflorus

Neuracanthus grandiflorus, commonly known as the grandiflorous flameflower, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from deep red to orange, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant has heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are typically dark green with a waxy texture. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Neuracanthus grandiflorus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
