Nepeta glechomifolia

Nepeta glechomifolia, commonly known as woolly catmint, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders. Its aromatic leaves and showy flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Nepeta glechomifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation in temperate climates.
