Nicotiana chinensis

Nicotiana chinensis, commonly known as Chinese tobacco, is a herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its vibrant blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers bloom in clusters at the tips of the stems, often in late summer or early autumn.

Nicotiana chinensis is also noted for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its tolerance to moderate drought conditions.
