Nepenthes masoalensis

Nepenthes masoalensis, commonly known as the Masoalensis Pitcher Plant, is a tropical vine native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its unique, pitcher-shaped leaves that secrete a digestive fluid to trap and digest insects. The plant's morphology includes elongated, spiral stems and waxy, green to reddish-brown foliage. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
