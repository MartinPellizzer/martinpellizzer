Nolina watsonii

Nolina watsonii, commonly known as Watson's nolina, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of thick, fleshy, blue-green leaves that store water, aiding in its survival in dry environments. The plant produces small, white, bell-shaped flowers on slender stems during the warmer months. It is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appearance.

Nolina watsonii is also valued for its ability to thrive in poor soils and its low maintenance requirements.
