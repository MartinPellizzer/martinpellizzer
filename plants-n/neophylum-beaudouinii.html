Neophylum beaudouinii

Neophylum beaudouinii, commonly known as the desert daisy, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand prolonged drought periods. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and birds, contributing to its ecological significance.

Neophylum beaudouinii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its resilience and aesthetic appeal.
