Nemophila pedunculata f. humifusa

Nemophila pedunculata f. humifusa, commonly known as the low-growing or mat-forming form of the common bluebells, is a variant of the species native to the western United States.

It is a low, spreading perennial herb with hairy stems and clusters of sky-blue to violet flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This form thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodland areas and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an attractive and ecologically valuable plant in native plant gardens.
