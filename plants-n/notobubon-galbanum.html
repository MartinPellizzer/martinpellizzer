Notobubon galbanum

Notobubon galbanum, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to thrive in well-drained, rocky soils.

Notobubon galbanum is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its traditional use in herbal medicine.
