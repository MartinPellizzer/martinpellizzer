Nymphaea guineensis

Nymphaea guineensis, commonly known as the African water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features large, showy flowers that can be white, pink, or purple, often with a distinctive yellow or green center. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes. Its leaves are broad and floating, providing shade and habitat for various aquatic organisms.

Nymphaea guineensis plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by supporting biodiversity and improving water quality.
