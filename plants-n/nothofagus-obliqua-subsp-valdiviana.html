Nothofagus obliqua subsp. valdiviana

Nothofagus obliqua subsp.

valdiviana, commonly known as the Valdivian southern beech, is a species native to southern South America. It is characterized by its evergreen foliage and distinctive, elongated leaves that are often pubescent.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by edible, woody cones containing winged seeds. It thrives in temperate rainforests and is adapted to high rainfall and cool, humid conditions.

This subspecies plays a vital ecological role in its native habitats, contributing to biodiversity and soil stability.
