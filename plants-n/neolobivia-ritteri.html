Neolobivia ritteri

Neolobivia ritteri, commonly known as the Ritter's lobivia, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, globular shape and spiny stems. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. Its succulent structure allows it to store water, making it well adapted to dry environments.

Neolobivia ritteri is often cultivated as a ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
