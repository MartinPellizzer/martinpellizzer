Nepeta persica

Nepeta persica, commonly known as Persian catmint, is a herbaceous perennial native to regions of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular blue or purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically gray-green and emit a minty fragrance when crushed.

Nepeta persica is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
