Namaquanula etesionamibensis

Namaquanula etesionamibensis, commonly known as the Namib Desert lichen, is a unique symbiotic organism composed of a cyanobacterium and a fungal partner.

It thrives in the extreme arid conditions of the Namib Desert, where it forms a crust-like structure on rock surfaces. The plant's ability to retain moisture and withstand high temperatures makes it well-adapted to its harsh environment. It plays a crucial role in stabilizing the soil and providing microhabitats for other desert organisms.

Due to its specialized adaptations, it is considered a key indicator species of the Namib Desert's ecological resilience.
