Nymphaea japono-koreana

Nymphaea japono-koreana, commonly known as the Korean or Japanese water lily, is a perennial aquatic plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Nymphaeaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats such as ponds and lakes. Its leaves are broad and floating, while its roots anchor it to the substrate.

Nymphaea japono-koreana is valued for its ornamental beauty and plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
