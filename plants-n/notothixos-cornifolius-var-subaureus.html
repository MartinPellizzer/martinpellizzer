Notothixos cornifolius var. subaureus

Notothixos cornifolius var. subaureus, commonly known as the golden leafed notothixos, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, lobed leaves that are often covered in a fine, golden-colored pubescence. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it is not widely distributed outside its native range.
