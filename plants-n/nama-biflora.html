Nama biflora

Nama biflora, commonly known as the two-flowered nama, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family, which is characterized by its fleshy leaves and vibrant flowers. The plant typically grows from a short, thick stem and produces two small, pale yellow or white flowers at the tip. Its leaves are thick and fleshy, adapted to store water in its native dry environment.

Nama biflora is often found in rocky soils and is well-suited for xerophytic gardens.
