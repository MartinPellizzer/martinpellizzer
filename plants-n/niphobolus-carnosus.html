Niphobolus carnosus

Niphobolus carnosus, commonly known as the pink root or pink false lily of the valley, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Niphobolus carnosus is often found in moist, shaded woodlands and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
