Nectandra barcellensis

Nectandra barcellensis, commonly known as the Caribbean sand-leaf tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Caribbean islands.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, elliptical leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds.

Nectandra barcellensis is often found in moist, lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.
