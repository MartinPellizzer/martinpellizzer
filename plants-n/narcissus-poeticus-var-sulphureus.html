Narcissus poeticus var. sulphureus

Narcissus poeticus var. sulphureus, commonly known as the sulphur yellow daffodil, is a striking ornamental plant characterized by its vibrant, golden-yellow petals.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is native to southern Europe, particularly the Iberian Peninsula. The plant features a tall, slender stem with narrow, sword-shaped leaves and a cluster of trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. Its unique coloration and elegant form make it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

The species is noted for its resilience and ability to thrive in well-drained soils with full sun exposure.
