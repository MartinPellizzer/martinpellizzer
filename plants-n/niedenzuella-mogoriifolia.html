Niedenzuella mogoriifolia

Niedenzuella mogoriifolia, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to the Amazon Basin.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in diameter, providing a unique habitat for various aquatic organisms. The plant's leaves are buoyant and have a waxy surface to prevent water from seeping through. Its flowers are typically white or pink, blooming at night and emitting a sweet fragrance to attract pollinators.

Niedenzuella mogoriifolia plays an important ecological role in its native environment by supporting biodiversity and contributing to water quality regulation.
