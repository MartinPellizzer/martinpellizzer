Nopalea karwinskiana

Nopalea karwinskiana, commonly known as the Karwinsky's prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces edible fruits, known as tunas, which are often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it well adapted to desert environments.

Nopalea karwinskiana is also valued for its ecological role in providing habitat and food for various desert animals.
