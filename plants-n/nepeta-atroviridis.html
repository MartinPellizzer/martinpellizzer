Nepeta atroviridis

Nepeta atroviridis, commonly known as blackleaf catmint, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features dark green, velvety leaves that are densely packed and slightly serrated. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Nepeta atroviridis is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and as a nectar source for pollinators.
