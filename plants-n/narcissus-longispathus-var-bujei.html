Narcissus longispathus var. bujei

Narcissus longispathus var. bujei, commonly known as the Bujei daffodil, is a rare and ornamental variety of daffodil native to China.

It features striking, trumpet-shaped flowers with a yellow corona and pale yellow petals. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30-50 centimeters, with narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often blooming in early spring.

This variant is valued for its unique coloration and is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts for its aesthetic appeal.
