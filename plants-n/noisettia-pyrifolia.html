Noisettia pyrifolia

Noisettia pyrifolia, commonly known as the Japanese chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Betulaceae family and is characterized by its oval-shaped, serrated leaves and smooth, gray bark. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by edible nuts enclosed in spiny husks. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and nut production.

Noisettia pyrifolia is also valued for its ability to tolerate urban environments and air pollution.
