Nematostemma perrieri

Nematostemma perrieri, commonly known as the Perrier's ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic orchid species native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its delicate, translucent floral structure and unique growth habits, often growing as an epiphyte on trees or rocks. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are adapted for specific pollination mechanisms, though its reproductive biology remains poorly understood. Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered critically endangered.

Conservation efforts are ongoing to protect this rare botanical treasure from further decline.
