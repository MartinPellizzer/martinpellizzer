Nectandra steyermarkiana

Nectandra steyermarkiana, commonly known as the Brazilian sand-wood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, rough bark and elliptical, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by fragrant, orange-red berries that are an important food source for various wildlife. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
