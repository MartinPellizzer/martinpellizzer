Nigella carpatha

Nigella carpatha, commonly known as Carpathian sesame, is a flowering plant native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, yellowish flowers and fern-like leaves. The plant produces small, dark seeds that are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Nigella carpatha is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in herbal remedies.
