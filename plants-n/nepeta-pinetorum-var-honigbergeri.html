Nepeta pinetorum var. honigbergeri

Nepeta pinetorum var. honigbergeri, commonly known as the Honigberger's catmint, is a variant of the catmint species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its compact growth habit and aromatic, fuzzy foliage. The plant produces small, tubular blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

Its cultivar name honors the botanist who first described it, highlighting its significance in horticultural taxonomy.
