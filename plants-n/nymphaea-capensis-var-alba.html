Nymphaea capensis var. alba

Nymphaea capensis var. alba, commonly known as the White Cape Water Lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom above the water surface, often in late summer to early autumn. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes. Its broad, floating leaves provide habitat for various aquatic organisms.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in aquatic ecosystems.
