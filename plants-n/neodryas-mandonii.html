Neodryas mandonii

Neodryas mandonii, commonly known as the Mandon's cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spineless stems and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and thrives in full sun, showcasing its adaptation to harsh desert environments.

Neodryas mandonii is known for its compact growth habit and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive floral display.
