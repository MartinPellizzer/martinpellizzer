Narcissus bicrenatus

Narcissus bicrenatus, commonly known as the double-crown daffodil, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive two-crown structure in its floral arrangement. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces bright yellow flowers with a prominent, cup-shaped corona. It typically blooms in early spring and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Narcissus bicrenatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant display.
