Neottia papulosa

Neottia papulosa, commonly known as the blister orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its pale green to brownish stems and its unique, hairy, and often blister-like leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in spikes, typically blooming in the spring. Neottia papulosa is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ecological study.
