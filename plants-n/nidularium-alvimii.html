Nidularium alvimii

Nidularium alvimii, commonly known as the Alvim's spider plant, is a member of the Bromeliaceae family and is native to Brazil.

It is a terrestrial bromeliad that forms dense rosettes of narrow, grass-like leaves, which help collect and store water. The plant produces striking red or orange inflorescences that emerge from the center of the rosette, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its foliage is often marked with distinctive white or silver markings, adding to its ornamental value.

Nidularium alvimii thrives in shaded, humid environments and is well-suited for indoor cultivation.
