Nervilia fuerstenbergiana

Nervilia fuerstenbergiana, commonly known as the velvet orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to the subtropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its unique morphology includes long, slender stems and specialized roots that aid in nutrient absorption.

Due to its rarity and aesthetic appeal, it is highly sought after by botanists and horticulturists alike.
