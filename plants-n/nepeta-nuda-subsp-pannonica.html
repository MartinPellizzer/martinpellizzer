Nepeta nuda subsp. pannonica

Nepeta nuda subsp.

pannonica, commonly known as the Hungarian catnip, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Central Europe. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves.

The plant produces small, pale blue to white flowers arranged in whorls along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in grassy areas and open woodlands.

Its aromatic leaves and flowers are used in traditional herbal remedies and as a source of nectar for pollinators.
