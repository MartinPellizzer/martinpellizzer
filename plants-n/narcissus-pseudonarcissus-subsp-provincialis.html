Narcissus pseudonarcissus subsp. provincialis

Narcissus pseudonarcissus subsp.

provincialis, commonly known as the Spanish daffodil, is a botanical variant of the common daffodil. It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves and a single, showy flower with a trumpet-shaped corona.

Native to southern Europe, particularly Spain and Portugal, this subspecies thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. Its flowers typically bloom in early spring, producing bright yellow petals and a distinctive orange corona.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and naturalized areas.
