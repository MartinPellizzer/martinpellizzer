Neotinea intacta f. alba

Neotinea intacta f. alba, commonly known as the white form of the common reed, is a variant of the Neotinea intacta species.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in wet or marshy environments. The plant features slender, hollow stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are small and arranged in spikelets, which are characteristic of the grass family.

This form is distinguished by its pale or white coloration, which sets it apart from the typical green form of the species.
