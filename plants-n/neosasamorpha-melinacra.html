Neosasamorpha melinacra

Neosasamorpha melinacra, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, yellowish-green foliage and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests or along stream banks. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped with a smooth texture and a slightly waxy surface.

Neosasamorpha melinacra is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
