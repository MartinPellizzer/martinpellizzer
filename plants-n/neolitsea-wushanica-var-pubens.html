Neolitsea wushanica var. pubens

Neolitsea wushanica var. pubens, commonly known as the Wushan pubescent litsea, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant features elliptical to ovate leaves with a leathery texture and a pubescent (hairy) underside, which distinguishes it from other varieties. Its flowers are typically white or pale yellow, and it produces small, dark blue berries that are edible but not widely consumed.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often found in moist, shaded forest environments.
