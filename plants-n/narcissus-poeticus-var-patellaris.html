Narcissus poeticus var. patellaris

Narcissus poeticus var. patellaris, commonly known as the Patellar daffodil, is a rare and ornamental member of the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking yellow petals and a narrow, upright corona that sets it apart from other daffodil varieties. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils in woodland or meadow habitats, preferring partial to full shade. Its foliage consists of narrow, strap-like leaves that emerge in early spring.

This variety is prized for its unique appearance and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value.
