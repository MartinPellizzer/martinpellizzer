Nepeta zandaensis

Nepeta zandaensis, commonly known as the Zanda catnip, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, arranged in whorled clusters. Its leaves are often fragrant and contain essential oils similar to those found in other catnip species.

Nepeta zandaensis is adapted to high-altitude environments and thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.
