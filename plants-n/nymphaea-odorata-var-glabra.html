Nymphaea odorata var. glabra

Nymphaea odorata var. glabra, commonly known as the sacred lotus, is a water-loving plant native to the wetlands of Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that float on the surface of still or slow-moving water. The plant produces large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink, with a fragrant scent. Its roots are aquatic and help in stabilizing the sediment in water bodies.

The species is noted for its adaptability to various aquatic environments and is often cultivated for ornamental and cultural purposes.
