Nematolepis wilsonii

Nematolepis wilsonii, commonly known as Wilson's grass, is a perennial herb native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny flowers.

Nematolepis wilsonii is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream sides.
