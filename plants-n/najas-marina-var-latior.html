Najas marina var. latior

Najas marina var. latior, commonly known as broadleaf water nymph, is a freshwater plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Najadaceae family and is characterized by its slender, ribbon-like leaves that float on the water surface. The plant typically grows in still or slow-moving waters, such as ponds and lakes, where it can receive ample sunlight. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually submerged, and it plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.

It is often used in water gardens and ponds for its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
