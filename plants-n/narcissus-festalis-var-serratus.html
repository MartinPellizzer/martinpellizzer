Narcissus festalis var. serratus

Narcissus festalis var. serratus, commonly known as the serrated daffodil, is a striking bulbous plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of large, trumpet-shaped yellow flowers with a distinctive serrated edge on the tepals. The plant typically blooms in early spring, adding a vibrant splash of color to gardens and natural habitats. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to a variety of garden conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
