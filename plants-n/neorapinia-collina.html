Neorapinia collina

Neorapinia collina, commonly known as the mountain ragwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at high altitudes. The plant features a compact rosette of dark green, hairy leaves that are arranged in a basal cluster. Its flowering stems bear yellowish-green flowers arranged in a single terminal head.

Neorapinia collina is adapted to cold climates and has a slow growth rate, making it a characteristic species of mountainous habitats.
