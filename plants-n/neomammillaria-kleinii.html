Neomammillaria kleinii

Neomammillaria kleinii, commonly known as the Klein's pincushion cactus, is a species native to the deserts of northern Mexico.

It is a small, globular cactus with a ribbed structure and a dense covering of white to yellow spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are typically borne on short, erect stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for arid environments.

Neomammillaria kleinii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and hardy nature.
