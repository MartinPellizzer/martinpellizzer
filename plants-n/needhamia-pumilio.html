Needhamia pumilio

Needhamia pumilio, commonly known as the dwarf orchid, is a small flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and delicate, fragrant blooms. The plant typically features slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves, with flowers that are usually white or pale yellow in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts for its aesthetic appeal.
