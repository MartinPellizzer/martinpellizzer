Noccaea caerulescens subsp. occitanica

Noccaea caerulescens subsp.

occitanica, commonly known as the Occitanian white cress, is a member of the Brassicaceae family. It is a low-growing annual herb characterized by its slender stems and small, white or pale blue flowers.

The plant thrives in nutrient-rich, often calcareous soils and is commonly found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is notable for its ability to hyperaccumulate heavy metals, making it an important plant in phytoremediation studies.
