Nervilia crispata

Nervilia crispata, commonly known as the wavy fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that have a distinctive wavy or undulating appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Nervilia crispata is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its ecological role in forest ecosystems.
