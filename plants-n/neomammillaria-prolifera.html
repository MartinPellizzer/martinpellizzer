Neomammillaria prolifera

Neomammillaria prolifera, commonly known as the Desert Rose, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and is covered in white to pale blue spines. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its green body. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for arid environments.

Neomammillaria prolifera is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in harsh conditions.
