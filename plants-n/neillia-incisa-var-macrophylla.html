Neillia incisa var. macrophylla

Neillia incisa var. macrophylla, commonly known as the giant fern, is a striking tropical plant characterized by its large, deeply lobed leaves.

Native to Southeast Asia, it thrives in humid, shaded environments with high moisture levels. The leaves can grow up to several feet in length, displaying a vibrant green color with a textured surface. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its dramatic appearance and ability to create a lush, tropical ambiance.

This variety is prized for its foliage rather than its flowers, which are small and inconspicuous.
