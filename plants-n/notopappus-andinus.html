Notopappus andinus

Notopappus andinus, commonly known as the Andean notopappus, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes in South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads and typically herbaceous growth habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, daisy-like flowers that are usually yellow or white in color. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its unique habitat and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
