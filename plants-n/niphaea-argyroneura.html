Niphaea argyroneura

Niphaea argyroneura, commonly known as the silverleaf water-lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to the wetlands of Southeast Asia.

It features broad, glossy leaves that are often covered in a silvery waxy coating, which helps reflect sunlight and reduce water loss. The plant produces white, fragrant flowers that bloom above the water surface, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its roots are adapted to anchor in muddy substrates, while its rhizomes allow for vegetative propagation.

The silverleaf water-lily plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
