Nepeta vaillantii

Nepeta vaillantii, commonly known as the blue catmint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features square, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces spikes of small, fragrant blue to purple flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Nepeta vaillantii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in various climates.
