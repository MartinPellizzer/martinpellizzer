Nymphaea amazonum subsp. pedersenii

Nymphaea amazonum subsp.

pedersenii, commonly known as the Amazon water lily, is a water plant native to the Amazon Basin. It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink.

The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in marshes and ponds. Its roots are anchored in the mud at the bottom of the water body, while its leaves form a floating canopy on the surface.

The species plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing shelter for aquatic life and contributing to nutrient cycling.
