Narcissus pseudonarcissus proles candidissimus

Narcissus pseudonarcissus proles candidissimus, commonly known as the white narcissus or the candidissimus daffodil, is a botanical variant of the common daffodil.

It is characterized by its striking white petals and a yellow corona, which set it apart from typical yellow daffodils. This plant belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is native to southern Europe. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in woodland edges and meadows.

Its delicate appearance makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a refined floral display.
