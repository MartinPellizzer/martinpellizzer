Nyssa bifida

Nyssa bifida, commonly known as the split-leaf nyssa, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that often split into two lobes, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, followed by bright red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for wildlife. Nyssa bifida thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and stream valleys.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and ornamental horticulture.
