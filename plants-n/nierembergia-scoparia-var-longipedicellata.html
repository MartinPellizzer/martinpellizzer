Nierembergia scoparia var. longipedicellata

Nierembergia scoparia var. longipedicellata, commonly known as the long-stemmed bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
