Nepenthes eustachya

Nepenthes eustachya, commonly known as the Eustachya pitcher plant, is a carnivorous plant native to the mountainous regions of Borneo.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated pitcher-shaped leaves that secrete a digestive fluid to trap and digest insects. The plant's pitchers are typically green or reddish in color and have a waxy surface that helps in trapping prey. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil for optimal growth.

Nepenthes eustachya is valued for its unique morphology and is often cultivated by enthusiasts of carnivorous plants.
