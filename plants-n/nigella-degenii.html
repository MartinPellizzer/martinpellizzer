Nigella degenii

Nigella degenii, commonly known as the Armenian cowpea, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny, black seeds. Nigella degenii is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.
