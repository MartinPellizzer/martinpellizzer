Neodypsis ligulata

Neodypsis ligulata, commonly known as the bottle tree, is a unique member of the Arecaceae family, native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its thick, bottle-shaped trunk that stores water, allowing it to survive in arid conditions. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically appearing in yellow or white, and it produces edible fruits that are consumed by local wildlife.

Neodypsis ligulata is well adapted to dry climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance.
