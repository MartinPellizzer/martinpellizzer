Namibia ponderosa

Namibia ponderosa, commonly known as the Namibian umbrella tree, is a distinctive shrub or small tree native to the arid regions of Namibia.

It is characterized by its broad, umbrella-like crown composed of long, drooping branches that create a dense canopy. The plant has thick, waxy leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.

The Namibia ponderosa is well adapted to extreme drought conditions, making it a resilient component of Namibia's desert flora.
