Nepeta nuda proles violacea

Nepeta nuda proles violacea, commonly known as the purple catmint, is a flowering plant in the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, tubular, violet-blue flowers. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It is native to parts of Europe and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.

Nepeta nuda proles violacea thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and borders.
