Nepeta wuana

Nepeta wuana, commonly known as Chinese catmint, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic and often medicinal plants. The plant features square stems and opposite leaves that are usually gray-green in color. It produces small, blue or purple flowers in whorled clusters, typical of the mint family.

Nepeta wuana is often used in traditional Chinese medicine and as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and pleasant fragrance.
