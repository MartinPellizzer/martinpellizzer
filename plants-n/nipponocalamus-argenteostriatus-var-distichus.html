Nipponocalamus argenteostriatus var. distichus

Nipponocalamus argenteostriatus var. distichus, commonly known as the silver-striped bamboo, is a variant of the silver-striped bamboo species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright culms that exhibit a distinctive silvery stripe along the nodes. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a bluish-green hue and a waxy coating. This plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in traditional landscaping and horticulture.
