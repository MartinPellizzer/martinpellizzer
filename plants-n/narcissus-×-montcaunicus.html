Narcissus × montcaunicus

Narcissus × montcaunicus, commonly known as the Montcada daffodil, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Narcissus tazetta and Narcissus poeticus.

It is characterized by its large, showy, cup-shaped flowers that typically range in color from white to yellow. The plant is native to the Iberian Peninsula and is known for its vigorous growth and ability to naturalize in suitable climates. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking hardy, ornamental plants.

The flowers bloom in early spring, adding a vibrant touch to gardens and landscapes.
