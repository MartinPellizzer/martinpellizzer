Nama origanifolia var. origanifolia

Nama origanifolia var. origanifolia, commonly known as mountain savory, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or pink in color. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and have a strong, pungent fragrance.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.
