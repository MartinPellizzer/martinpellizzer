Nepeta sessilis

Nepeta sessilis, commonly known as catmint, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic and often medicinal properties. The plant features square, hairy stems and opposite leaves that are usually gray-green in color. Its small blue to purple flowers bloom in whorls along the stem, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Nepeta sessilis is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and its historical use in herbal remedies.
