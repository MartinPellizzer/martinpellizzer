Niphaea mexicana

Niphaea mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican water lily, is a aquatic plant native to the wetlands of Mexico.

It has broad, floating leaves that provide habitat for various aquatic organisms. The plant produces white, fragrant flowers that bloom during the warmer months. Its roots are submerged, anchoring it in the mud and absorbing nutrients from the water.

Niphaea mexicana plays an important role in maintaining water quality and supporting local biodiversity.
