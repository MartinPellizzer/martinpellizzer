Nicotiana rosulata subsp. ingulba

Nicotiana rosulata subsp.

ingulba, commonly known as the Rosulate Tobacco, is a perennial herb native to the Andes Mountains of South America. It is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and produces striking violet to blue flowers.

The plant has a short, stout stem and is known for its aromatic foliage. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in high-altitude regions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
