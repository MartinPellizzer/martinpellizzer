Neurophyllum longifolium

Neurophyllum longifolium, commonly known as the longleaf neurophyllum, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of North America.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves that are often arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale in color, which attract various pollinators. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.
