Narcissus deficiens

Narcissus deficiens, commonly known as the deficient daffodil, is a rare and enigmatic member of the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its reduced floral structures, including a shortened corona and a smaller teppon. Native to the Iberian Peninsula, this species thrives in specific microhabitats such as limestone soils and shaded woodlands. Its botanical features make it a subject of interest for taxonomists and conservationists.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered endangered and requires careful ecological management.
