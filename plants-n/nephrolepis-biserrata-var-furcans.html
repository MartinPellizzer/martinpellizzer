Nephrolepis biserrata var. furcans

Nephrolepis biserrata var. furcans, commonly known as the forked maidenhair fern, is a variant of the maidenhair fern family characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds.

It features bipinnate leaves with a distinctive bifurcated or forked appearance at the tips, giving it its common name. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and prefers well-draining, acidic soil. Its fronds are typically dark green and have a feathery texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its aesthetic value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
