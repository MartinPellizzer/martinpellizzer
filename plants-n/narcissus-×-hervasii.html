Narcissus × hervasii

Narcissus × hervasii, commonly known as the Hervasii daffodil, is a hybrid species within the genus Narcissus.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow petals and a single, upright flower stem. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 50 centimeters, making it a compact and ornamental addition to gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often blooming in early spring.

This variety is valued for its resilience and ability to naturalize over time.
