Narcissus juressianus

Narcissus juressianus, commonly known as the Armenian daffodil, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers with a white center. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its bulbs are perennial, and it blooms in early spring.
