Neslia paniculata var. apiculata

Neslia paniculata var. apiculata, commonly known as the clustered milkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features slender, erect stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, blooming from late summer to early autumn. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture.

This species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in dry conditions.
