Navarretia prolifera

Navarretia prolifera, commonly known as the showy navarretia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, showy flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. Its leaves are basal, linear, and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are arranged in a raceme.

Navarretia prolifera is known for its rapid growth and ability to produce numerous offspring through vegetative propagation.
