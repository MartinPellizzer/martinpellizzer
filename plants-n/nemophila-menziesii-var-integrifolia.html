Nemophila menziesii var. integrifolia

Nemophila menziesii var. integrifolia, commonly known as the Coast Gem, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of California and Oregon.

It is a low-growing annual or biennial herb that thrives in sandy or gravelly soils. The plant produces clusters of small, showy blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a smooth, slightly hairy texture.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to naturalize in suitable habitats.
