Nectandra nitidula

Nectandra nitidula, commonly known as the glossy nectandra, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves. The tree produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by elliptical, woody fruits containing aromatic seeds. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitats.
