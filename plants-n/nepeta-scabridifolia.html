Nepeta scabridifolia

Nepeta scabridifolia, commonly known as roughleaf catmint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features aromatic, gray-green leaves that are rough to the touch, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces clusters of small, pale blue to lavender flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens or borders.

Its foliage and flowers attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
