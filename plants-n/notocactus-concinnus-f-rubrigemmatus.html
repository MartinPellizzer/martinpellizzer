Notocactus concinnus f. rubrigemmatus

Notocactus concinnus f. rubrigemmatus, commonly known as the red-gemmed notocactus, is a hybrid cactus species characterized by its compact, rounded shape and vibrant red to pinkish hues on its stems.

It originates from the Canary Islands and is prized for its ornamental value due to its striking coloration and unique morphology. The plant typically features a short, thick stem with areoles that bear clusters of white to pale yellow flowers. Its foliage is minimal, with the stem serving as the primary structural and decorative element.

This variety is well-suited for indoor cultivation, requiring bright light and well-draining soil to thrive.
