Nepenthes pantaronensis

Nepenthes pantaronensis, commonly known as the Pantaron pitcher plant, is a tropical carnivorous plant native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its distinctive pitcher-shaped traps, which are modified leaves designed to capture and digest insects. The plant's pitchers are typically green or reddish in color and produce a sweet nectar to lure prey. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is adapted to nutrient-poor soils.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important species in the study of carnivorous plants.
