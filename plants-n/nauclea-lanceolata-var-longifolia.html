Nauclea lanceolata var. longifolia

Nauclea lanceolata var. longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved nauclea, is a species of flowering plant in the Fabaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, particularly in countries like Kenya and Tanzania. The plant is characterized by its long, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
