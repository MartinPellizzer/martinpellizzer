Neolitsea aurata var. chekiangensis

Neolitsea aurata var. chekiangensis, commonly known as the Chinese sweet olive, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often in spring. The leaves are simple, opposite, and ovate, with a glossy green upper surface and a lighter underside. This plant is valued for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its pleasant aroma.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it adaptable to a variety of garden settings.
