Nastus longispicula

Nastus longispicula, commonly known as the long-hair nasturtium, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, spiky seed pods that give it its scientific name. The plant produces bright yellow or orange flowers that are often used in ornamental gardening. Its leaves are edible and have a peppery taste, similar to that of watercress.

Nastus longispicula is native to South America and thrives in temperate climates.
