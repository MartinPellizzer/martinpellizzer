Nymphaea holtzei

Nymphaea holtzei, commonly known as the Holtze's water lily, is a species of aquatic plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that float on the water's surface, supported by long, slender petioles. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white, with a prominent yellow center. Nymphaea holtzei thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in ponds and lakes.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental water gardens.
