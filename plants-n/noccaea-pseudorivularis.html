Noccaea pseudorivularis

Noccaea pseudorivularis, commonly known as the false riverweed, is a small flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, grass-like leaves and small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. This species is typically found in moist, calcareous soils along stream banks and wetlands. It has a creeping growth habit with rhizomes that help it spread and colonize new areas.

Noccaea pseudorivularis is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.
