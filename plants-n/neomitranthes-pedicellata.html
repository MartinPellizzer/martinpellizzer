Neomitranthes pedicellata

Neomitranthes pedicellata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in full sun and well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with a central disc.

Neomitranthes pedicellata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
