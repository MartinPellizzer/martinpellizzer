Nigrina sessiliflora

Nigrina sessiliflora, commonly known as the blackleaf sesileflower, is a rare flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and small, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of purple and violet. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests. Its sessile leaves, which are attached directly to the stem without a stalk, are a distinctive botanical feature.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
