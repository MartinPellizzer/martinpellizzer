Narcissus × rozeirae

Narcissus × rozeirae, commonly known as the Rozeira daffodil, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Narcissus tazetta and Narcissus poeticus.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers with a cup-like corona and bright yellow petals. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with strap-like leaves that are broad and pointed. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often used in both landscape design and as a cut flower.
