Noccaea caerulescens subsp. merinoi

Noccaea caerulescens subsp.

merinoi, commonly known as Merino's azureweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and pinnately compound leaves.

This species is notable for its ability to hyperaccumulate heavy metals, making it useful in phytoremediation efforts. It typically grows in calcareous soils and is often found in disturbed or rocky habitats.

The plant's blue-violet flowers bloom in early summer, adding a striking visual element to its natural environment.
