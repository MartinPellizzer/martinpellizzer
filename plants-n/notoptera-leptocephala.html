Notoptera leptocephala

Notoptera leptocephala, commonly known as the common grasshopper, is a species of grasshopper found in various regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Acrididae and is characterized by its slender body and long, powerful hind legs adapted for jumping. The plant it interacts with, such as grasses and herbs, provides both shelter and food sources for the nymphs and adults. Its life cycle involves incomplete metamorphosis, transitioning from egg to nymph to adult.

This species plays a role in the ecological balance of its habitat by contributing to nutrient cycling and serving as prey for various predators.
