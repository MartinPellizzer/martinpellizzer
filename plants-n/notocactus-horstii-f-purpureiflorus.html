Notocactus horstii f. purpureiflorus

Notocactus horstii f. purpureiflorus, commonly known as the Purpleflowered Horstii Cactus, is a unique hybrid of the Notocactus genus native to South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stem with a smooth, greenish-gray surface that develops a reddish hue under direct sunlight. The plant produces striking, violet-pink flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring, adding a dramatic visual contrast to its foliage. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a decorative cactus in gardens and greenhouses.
