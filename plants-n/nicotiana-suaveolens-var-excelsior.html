Nicotiana suaveolens var. excelsior

Nicotiana suaveolens var. excelsior, commonly known as the sweet tobacco or nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its attractive, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of pink to purple, often at night. The plant has a woody stem and hairy leaves, which are typically ovate in shape. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens.

This variety is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
