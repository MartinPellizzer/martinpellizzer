Nicotiana acuminata var. acuminata

Nicotiana acuminata var. acuminata, commonly known as the sharp-leaved tobacco plant, is a species native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves with pointed tips. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, attracting various pollinators. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical significance in traditional medicine.

This species is also notable for its use in the production of nicotine-containing products.
