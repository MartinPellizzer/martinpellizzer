Nectandra salicifolia

Nectandra salicifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf sandarac, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its wood is valued for its aromatic properties and is used in the production of sandarac resin.

The tree thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.
