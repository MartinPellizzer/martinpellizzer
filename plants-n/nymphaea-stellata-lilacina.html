Nymphaea stellata lilacina

Nymphaea stellata lilacina, commonly known as the lilac water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to parts of Asia.

It features broad, heart-shaped leaves that float on the water's surface, supported by long, slender petioles. The flowers are showy, with pale pink to lilac petals that open in the morning and close by midday. Its blooming period typically occurs in the summer months, adding a decorative element to ponds and water gardens.

The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, preferring full sun to partial shade.
