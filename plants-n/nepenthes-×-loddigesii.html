Nepenthes × loddigesii

Nepenthes × loddigesii, commonly known as the hybrid pitcher plant, is a tropical carnivorous plant resulting from the cross between Nepenthes ventrata and Nepenthes rafflesiana.

It is characterized by its large, often red or yellowish, pitcher-shaped traps that secrete a digestive fluid to break down insects. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires high levels of light for optimal growth. Its pitchers can grow up to 30 centimeters in length, and the plant is often cultivated for its striking appearance and unique ecological adaptations.

Nepenthes × loddigesii is a popular choice among carnivorous plant enthusiasts due to its resilience and aesthetic appeal.
