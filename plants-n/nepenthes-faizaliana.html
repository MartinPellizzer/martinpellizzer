Nepenthes faizaliana

Nepenthes faizaliana, commonly known as the Faizalana pitcher plant, is a tropical vine native to the island of Borneo.

It is characterized by its unique pitcher-shaped leaves that secrete a digestive fluid to trap and digest insects. The plant's pitchers vary in color, often displaying shades of green, red, and yellow, depending on environmental conditions. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires high levels of moisture and nutrient-rich soil.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered an endangered species.
