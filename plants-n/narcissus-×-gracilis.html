Narcissus × gracilis

Narcissus × gracilis, commonly known as the slender daffodil, is a hybrid plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and delicate, trumpet-shaped flowers that typically range in color from yellow to white. The plant is native to the Iberian Peninsula and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, blooming in early spring.

Due to its elegant appearance, it is a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
