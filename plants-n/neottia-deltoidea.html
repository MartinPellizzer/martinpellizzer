Neottia deltoidea

Neottia deltoidea, commonly known as the delta orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and swampy areas. The plant features a slender, often branched stem and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern.

Neottia deltoidea is known for its lack of showy petals, with the sepals and petals being similar in appearance.
