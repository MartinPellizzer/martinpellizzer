Nephelium glabrum var. album

Nephelium glabrum var. album, commonly known as the white rambutan, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale yellow to white skin and sweet, juicy, pinkish-white flesh. The fruit is typically small to medium in size, with a hairy exterior that becomes smooth as it ripens. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit.

It belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is closely related to other members of the rambutan genus.
