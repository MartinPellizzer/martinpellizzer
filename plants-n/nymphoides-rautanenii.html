Nymphoides rautanenii

Nymphoides rautanenii, commonly known as the golden water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that float on the water surface, often with a waxy texture. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that bloom in summer, adding a vibrant splash of color to ponds and lakes. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, preferring full sun to partial shade.

Nymphoides rautanenii is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to provide habitat for aquatic life.
