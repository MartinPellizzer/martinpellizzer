Nama hispida var. gypsicola

Nama hispida var. gypsicola, commonly known as the gypsum bush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. The plant is adapted to harsh environments, with thick, waxy leaves that reduce water loss. It thrives in well-drained soils, often found on rocky or sandy substrates.

Nama hispida var. gypsicola plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for small desert animals.
