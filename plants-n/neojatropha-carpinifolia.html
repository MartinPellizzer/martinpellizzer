Neojatropha carpinifolia

Neojatropha carpinifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf jatropha, is a perennial shrub native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It features small, opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and it produces clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant is characterized by its milky sap, which contains toxic compounds, and its capsule fruits that split open to release seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and potential for medicinal use, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and research settings.
