Neyraudia arundinacea

Neyraudia arundinacea, commonly known as the bamboo reed or reed grass, is a perennial grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often a vibrant green. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, which are typically green or reddish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along water edges.

Neyraudia arundinacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in aquatic environments.
