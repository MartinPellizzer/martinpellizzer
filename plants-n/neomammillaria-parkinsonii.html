Neomammillaria parkinsonii

Neomammillaria parkinsonii, commonly known as the Parkinson's prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are covered in white, woolly spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are followed by red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun exposure.

Neomammillaria parkinsonii is relatively drought-tolerant and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
