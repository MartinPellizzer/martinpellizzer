Nyssa multiflora var. sylvatica

Nyssa multiflora var. sylvatica, commonly known as the Florida ash or sweet ash, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple, ovate to elliptic leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, followed by elliptical, blue-black fruits that contain one or two seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, swamps, and along stream banks.

The plant has historical significance in traditional medicine and is valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
