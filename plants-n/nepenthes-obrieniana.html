Nepenthes obrieniana

Nepenthes obrieniana, commonly known as the O'Brien's Pitcher Plant, is a tropical pitcher plant native to the island of Borneo.

It is characterized by its large, waxy, and often brightly colored pitcher-shaped leaves that trap and digest insects. The plant produces nectar at the rim of the pitcher to lure prey, which then slips into the digestive fluid below. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires high levels of moisture and nutrient-poor soil.

Nepenthes obrieniana is notable for its unique adaptations and is a subject of interest in botanical research.
