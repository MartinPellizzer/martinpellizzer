Narcissus serotinus var. major

Narcissus serotinus var. major, commonly known as the giant late narcissus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It typically grows to a height of up to 1.5 meters, featuring strap-like, grassy leaves and large, showy flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The flowers are usually white or pale yellow with a distinctive, recurved tepal shape. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens for its dramatic appearance.
