Notholaena balansae

Notholaena balansae, commonly known as the Balansae fern, is a species native to the eastern United States, particularly in the Appalachian region.

It is a small, evergreen fern with delicate, finely divided fronds that often have a bluish-green hue. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices. Its reproductive structures include sori that are clustered on the underside of the fronds.

Notholaena balansae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable humidity and light conditions.
