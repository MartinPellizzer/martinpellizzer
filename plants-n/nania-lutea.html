Nania lutea

Nania lutea, commonly known as the yellow sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arctic and alpine regions.

It typically features small, clustered leaves and produces yellow flowers in the summer. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to cold climates. Nania lutea is often found in tundra and mountainous environments where it plays a role in stabilizing soil.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in ecological studies of extreme environments.
