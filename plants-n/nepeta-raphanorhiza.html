Nepeta raphanorhiza

Nepeta raphanorhiza, commonly known as carrot root catnip, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are aromatic and contain essential oils that give it a distinct scent reminiscent of carrots.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.
