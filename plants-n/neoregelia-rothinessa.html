Neoregelia rothinessa

Neoregelia rothinessa, commonly known as the Crimson Queen, is a striking bromeliad native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its vibrant, red-pink bracts that surround its central inflorescence, making it a visually captivating plant. The leaves are broad, strap-like, and often have a waxy texture, with a dark green to purple hue. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires high humidity and well-draining soil.

Neoregelia rothinessa is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
