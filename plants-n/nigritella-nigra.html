Nigritella nigra

Nigritella nigra, commonly known as the black nightshade, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its small, dark purple to black berries that mature in the fall. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a slightly hairy texture.

Nigritella nigra is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking fruit.
