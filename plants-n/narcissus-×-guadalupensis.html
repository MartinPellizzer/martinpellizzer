Narcissus × guadalupensis

Narcissus × guadalupensis, commonly known as the Guadalupensis daffodil, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Narcissus poeticus and Narcissus tazetta.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers with a prominent central corona, typical of the daffodil genus. This species is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its vibrant blooms and resilience.

The plant typically reaches heights of 30 to 60 centimeters and blooms in early spring.
