Neocryptodiscus

Neocryptodiscus, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its unique, translucent, and often pale green or white flowers that resemble ghostly apparitions. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments, primarily in Southeast Asia. Its leaves are broad and fleshy, adapted to store moisture in its native tropical climates.

Neocryptodiscus is highly sought after by botanists and horticulturists due to its rarity and striking appearance.
