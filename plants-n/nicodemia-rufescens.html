Nicodemia rufescens

Nicodemia rufescens, commonly known as the red-stemmed lily, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking red stems and clusters of showy, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a smooth, waxy texture.

Nicodemia rufescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
