Narcissus exertus

Narcissus exertus, commonly known as the erect daffodil, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, straight stem that supports a single, showy flower with a trumpet-shaped corona. The plant produces narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green and arranged in a rosette at the base. Native to southern Europe, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun conditions.

Its blooming period occurs in early spring, adding a vibrant splash of yellow to gardens and natural landscapes.
