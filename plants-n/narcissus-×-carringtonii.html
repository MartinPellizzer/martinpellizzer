Narcissus × carringtonii

Narcissus × carringtonii, commonly known as the Carrington daffodil, is a hybrid species within the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers with a trumpet-shaped corona and bright yellow petals. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

This cultivar is valued for its early blooming habit and vibrant floral display.
