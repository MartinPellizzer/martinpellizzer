Nemanthera

Nemanthera, commonly known as the golden groundsel, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its bright yellow daisy-like flowers and is native to the Mediterranean region. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branching.

Nemanthera is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
