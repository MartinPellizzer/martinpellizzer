Noccaea dacica subsp. montenegrina

Noccaea dacica subsp.

montenegrina, commonly known as the Montenegrin watercress, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

The plant has slender, spreading stems and small, alternate leaves that are typically heart-shaped. Its flowers are white or pale pink, arranged in clusters, and it produces small, spherical fruits.

This subspecies is valued for its peppery flavor and is used in local cuisine and traditional medicine.
