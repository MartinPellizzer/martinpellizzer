Notodontia balansae

Notodontia balansae, commonly known as the Balansan moth plant, is a species of flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Notodontidae, which includes various species of moths and their associated plants. The plant is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as moths and butterflies. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth texture and a slight waxy coating.

Notodontia balansae is noted for its ecological significance in supporting local insect populations and its role in traditional herbal practices.
