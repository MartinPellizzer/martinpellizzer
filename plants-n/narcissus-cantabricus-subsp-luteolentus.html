Narcissus cantabricus subsp. luteolentus

Narcissus cantabricus subsp.

luteolentus, commonly known as the Cantabrian yellow narcissus, is a rare and endemic species native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its pale yellow flowers with a distinctive greenish tinge, which bloom in early spring.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils in mountainous regions, preferring well-drained conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
