Nepeta cephalotes

Nepeta cephalotes, commonly known as catnip, is a flowering plant in the mint family (Lamiaceae).

It is native to Europe, western Asia, and northern Africa, and it thrives in temperate climates. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white, and it has opposite, ovate leaves with a distinctive serrated edge. The essential oil in its leaves contains nepetalactone, which is responsible for its effects on cats.

It is often grown as an ornamental plant and for its aromatic properties.
