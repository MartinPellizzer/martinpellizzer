Norantea violacea

Norantea violacea, commonly known as the violet bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers are arranged in loose clusters and are often pollinated by bees and butterflies.

Norantea violacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color.
