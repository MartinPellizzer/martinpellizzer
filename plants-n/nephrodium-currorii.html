Nephrodium currorii

Nephrodium currorii, commonly known as the Curror's shield fern, is a unique fern species native to the wet tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, shield-shaped fronds that are tightly clustered at the base, giving it a compact appearance. The plant has a short, creeping rhizome that produces erect, spore-bearing fronds with a leathery texture. Its leaves are typically dark green and have a glossy surface, which helps in water retention in its moist habitat.

Nephrodium currorii is often found in shaded, humid environments such as forest understories and rocky crevices.
