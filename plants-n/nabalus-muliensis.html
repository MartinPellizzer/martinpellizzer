Nabalus muliensis

Nabalus muliensis, commonly known as the Mulien mountain bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It typically grows in rocky and well-drained soils, often in alpine or subalpine habitats. The plant features slender stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are bell-shaped, ranging in color from white to pale blue, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Nabalus muliensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
