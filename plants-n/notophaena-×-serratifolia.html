Notophaena × serratifolia

Notophaena × serratifolia, commonly known as the serrated leaf fern, is a hybrid plant that exhibits a combination of traits from its parent species.

It typically features fronds with serrated edges, which give it its common name and contribute to its ornamental appeal. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its botanical structure includes a rhizomatous root system that helps it anchor and spread across moist soil.

Notophaena × serratifolia is often cultivated for its attractive foliage and is relatively easy to care for in suitable conditions.
