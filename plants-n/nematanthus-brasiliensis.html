Nematanthus brasiliensis

Nematanthus brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian slipper, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its unique, tubular flowers that resemble small slippers. The plant typically grows as a perennial shrub, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a velvety texture.

Nematanthus brasiliensis is valued in horticulture for its vibrant, long-lasting blooms that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow.
