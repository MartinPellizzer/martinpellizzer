Nephrolepis floccigera

Nephrolepis floccigera, commonly known as the fluff fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are covered in fine, fuzzy hairs, giving it a soft, fluffy appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its fronds are pinnately compound, with small, lance-shaped pinnae that are often curled when young.

Nephrolepis floccigera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage.
