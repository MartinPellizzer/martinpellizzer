Nectandra dasystyla

Nectandra dasystyla, commonly known as the fragrant sandarac tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its aromatic, dark brown wood that is highly valued for its durability and scent. The tree produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, round fruits containing a single seed. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a leathery texture with a distinctive aromatic odor.

The plant is also noted for its role in traditional medicine and as a source of natural resins.
