Nastus carolinianus

Nastus carolinianus, commonly known as Carolina nasturtium, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, edible pods that are often used in culinary applications. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Nastus carolinianus is also known for its peppery flavor, similar to that of watercress.
