Nidularium antoineanum

Nidularium antoineanum, commonly known as the Antoine's nest fern, is a rare and striking member of the Polypodiaceae family.

It is characterized by its unique, clustered fronds that resemble a nest, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its rhizomes are thick and fibrous, supporting the growth of its intricate frond structures.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
