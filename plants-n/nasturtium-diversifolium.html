Nasturtium diversifolium

Nasturtium diversifolium, commonly known as the variable-leaf nasturtium, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its diverse leaf shapes, which can range from heart-shaped to lanceolate, adding to its ornamental value. The plant produces vibrant orange or red flowers with a distinctive spurred petal. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Nasturtium diversifolium is often cultivated for its edible flowers and leaves, which have a peppery taste similar to watercress.
