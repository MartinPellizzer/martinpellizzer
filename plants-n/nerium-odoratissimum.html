Nerium odoratissimum

Nerium odoratissimum, commonly known as the oleander, is a flowering shrub native to the coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its showy, showy clusters of flowers that range in color from white to pink, red, and yellow. The plant has simple, opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It is highly adaptable and thrives in a variety of soil types, though it prefers well-drained conditions.

Nerium odoratissimum is also known for its toxicity, as all parts of the plant contain cardiac glycosides that can be harmful to humans and animals.
