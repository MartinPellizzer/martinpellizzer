Nothocissus hypoglauca

Nothocissus hypoglauca, commonly known as the silverleaf cissus, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, vine-like stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The leaves are typically a pale green to bluish-green, giving the plant its common name. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in axillary inflorescences.

The plant is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
