Nephrodium gymnosorum

Nephrodium gymnosorum, commonly known as the naked fern, is a unique plant species belonging to the family Nephrodiaceae.

It is characterized by its simple, unbranched fronds that lack true leaves, instead having a structure resembling a fern. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its reproductive structures are unique, with spores produced in specialized clusters called sporangia.

The botanical features of Nephrodium gymnosorum make it an interesting subject for studies in plant morphology and evolution.
