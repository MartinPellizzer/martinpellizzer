Nama depressa

Nama depressa, commonly known as the dwarf nama, is a low-growing, succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, cylindrical stems that are typically green or reddish in color. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom during the warmer months. It is well adapted to arid environments and can store water in its stems.

Nama depressa is often used in rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
