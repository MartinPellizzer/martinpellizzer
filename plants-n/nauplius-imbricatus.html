Nauplius imbricatus

Nauplius imbricatus, commonly known as the imbricate nauplius, is a species of crustacean rather than a plant, so it does not have botanical aspects.

It is a type of larval stage in certain crustaceans, such as copepods, and is characterized by its segmented body and biramous appendages. The term "imbricatus" refers to the overlapping arrangement of its appendages, which is a distinguishing feature. It plays a role in the early life cycle of marine and freshwater crustaceans.
