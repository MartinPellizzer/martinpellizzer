Nabalus pyramidalis

Nabalus pyramidalis, commonly known as the pyramidal lichen, is a foliose lichen that thrives in humid, shaded environments.

It has a distinctive layered structure with a dark, leathery upper surface and a lighter, spongy lower layer. The plant is characterized by its intricate network of fungal hyphae and algal cells that form a symbiotic relationship. It is often found on tree trunks and rocks in temperate forests.

Its slow growth and unique appearance make it an interesting subject for ecological and botanical study.
