Nestotus lanuginosus var. andersonii

Nestotus lanuginosus var. andersonii, commonly known as the Anderson's woolly nestotus, is a perennial herb native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its soft, lanuginous (fuzzy) foliage and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variant is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique texture and color.
