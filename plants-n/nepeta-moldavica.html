Nepeta moldavica

Nepeta moldavica, commonly known as Moldavian catmint, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils, which contribute to its medicinal and ornamental value.

Nepeta moldavica is often grown in gardens for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
