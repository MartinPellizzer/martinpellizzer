Nepeta scabra

Nepeta scabra, commonly known as mouse whiskers, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine, hairy foliage. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Nepeta scabra is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
