Nephromeria poeppigiana

Nephromeria poeppigiana, commonly known as the silverleaf cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems that store water. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers during the warmer months. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting to harsh conditions with its thick, fleshy leaves.

Its unique appearance and ability to survive in drought make it a notable species in botanical studies.
