Nama hispida

Nama hispida, commonly known as the African milk bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain a milky sap, which is the source of its common name. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to harsh climates.

The sap has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties in various indigenous communities.
