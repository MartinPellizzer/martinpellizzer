Nepeta grandiflora var. betonicifolia

Nepeta grandiflora var. betonicifolia, commonly known as catmint, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, aromatic stems that produce clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often fuzzy and emit a minty scent when crushed. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is drought-tolerant once established.

Its foliage and flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
