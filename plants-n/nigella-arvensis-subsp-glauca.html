Nigella arvensis subsp. glauca

Nigella arvensis subsp.

glauca, commonly known as blue hawksbeard, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its blue to purple flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while the seeds are small and contained within a hairy, triangular capsule.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for various purposes.
