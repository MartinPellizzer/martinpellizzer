Nectandra membranacea var. saligna

Nectandra membranacea var. saligna, commonly known as the white sand cedar, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, reddish-brown heartwood and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by oval, woody fruits. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and timber quality, it is sometimes cultivated in horticultural settings.
