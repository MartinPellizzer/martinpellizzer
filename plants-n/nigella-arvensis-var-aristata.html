Nigella arvensis var. aristata

Nigella arvensis var. aristata, commonly known as the Persian buttercup, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia and parts of Europe.

It is characterized by its delicate yellow flowers with a distinctive crown-like structure, which resembles a buttercup. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and has hairy stems and leaves. Its small, oval-shaped seeds are enclosed in a three-lobed capsule, which splits open to release the seeds.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
