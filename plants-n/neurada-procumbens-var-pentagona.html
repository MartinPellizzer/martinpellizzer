Neurada procumbens var. pentagona

Neurada procumbens var. pentagona, commonly known as the five-angled neurada, is a low-growing perennial plant native to certain regions of South America.

It features small, lance-shaped leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern, and its flowers are typically pale yellow or greenish-white. The plant is characterized by its distinctive five-angled stem and leaves, which give it its scientific name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique morphology, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
