Nama macrantha

Nama macrantha, commonly known as the Natal grass or Natal panic grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and along riverbanks.

Nama macrantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its resilience and drought tolerance.
