Nephrolepis hirsutula var. rufescens

Nephrolepis hirsutula var. rufescens, commonly known as the rusty hairsplit fern, is a variegated fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It features fronds that are typically bright green with reddish-brown or rust-colored markings, giving it its distinctive appearance. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, with upright, lance-shaped fronds that can reach up to 1 meter in length. Its leaves are bipinnate, with small, delicate pinnae that contribute to its ornamental value.

It thrives in humid, well-drained soil and requires indirect light to flourish.
