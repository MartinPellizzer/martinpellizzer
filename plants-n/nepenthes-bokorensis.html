Nepenthes bokorensis

Nepenthes bokorensis, commonly known as the Bokorensis Pitcher Plant, is a tropical pitcher plant native to the rainforests of Cambodia.

It is characterized by its unique, elongated, and often reddish-colored pitcher-shaped leaves that serve as traps for insects. The plant produces a sweet-smelling nectar to attract prey, which then slips into the digestive fluid at the base of the pitcher. Its habitat is typically in shaded, humid environments with high rainfall.

Nepenthes bokorensis is a rare species, highlighting the biodiversity of Southeast Asian flora.
