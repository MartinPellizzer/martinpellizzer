Nautilocalyx speciosus

Nautilocalyx speciosus, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow inflorescences that resemble flames, which bloom profusely during the dry season. The plant has compound leaves with ovate to lanceolate leaflets, and its flowers are typically arranged in panicles. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental species in warm climates.

Nautilocalyx speciosus is also valued for its rapid growth and ability to provide shade in landscaping settings.
