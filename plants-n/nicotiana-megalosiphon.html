Nicotiana megalosiphon

Nicotiana megalosiphon, commonly known as the giant siphon tobacco, is a species native to South America.

It is a herbaceous plant that can grow up to 2 meters in height, characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its flowers have a unique siphon-like structure, which aids in pollination by guiding pollinators to the nectar.

This species is also noted for its potential in botanical research due to its ornamental value and genetic diversity.
