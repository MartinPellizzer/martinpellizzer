Nandina domestica var. longifolia

Nandina domestica var. longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaf nandina, is a compact, ornamental shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features dense, rounded clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that appear in late spring. The plant produces bright red berries that persist through winter, adding visual interest to the landscape. Its foliage consists of glossy, dark green leaves that turn red in the fall.

This variety is prized for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
