Nectandra cordata

Nectandra cordata, commonly known as the red cedar or white cedar, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark-colored wood and glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically borne in clusters, followed by reddish-brown berries. Nectandra cordata is valued for its durable timber and is often found in humid lowland forests.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
