Neomarica paradoxa

Neomarica paradoxa, commonly known as the Paradox Orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are long, strap-like, and often have a waxy texture.

Neomarica paradoxa is valued for its ornamental beauty and is commonly cultivated in warm, humid environments.
