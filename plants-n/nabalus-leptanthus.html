Nabalus leptanthus

Nabalus leptanthus, commonly known as the slender thornapple, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Nabalus leptanthus is often found in rocky soils and is considered a hardy, drought-tolerant species.
