Notocactus megalanthus

Notocactus megalanthus, commonly known as the giant rock cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, cylindrical stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant features spiny, ribbed stems and produces large, showy flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and orange. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xerophytic environments.

The plant's unique morphology and striking appearance make it a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts and botanical gardens.
