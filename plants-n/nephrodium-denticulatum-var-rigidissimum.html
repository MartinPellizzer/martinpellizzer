Nephrodium denticulatum var. rigidissimum

Nephrodium denticulatum var. rigidissimum, commonly known as the rigid holly fern, is a variant of the holly fern species native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its stiff, upright fronds that are covered in small, serrated teeth, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with each pinna having a leathery texture and a slightly waxy surface.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
