Nepenthes madagascariensis var. cylindrica

Nepenthes madagascariensis var. cylindrica, commonly known as the cylindrical pitcher plant, is a unique carnivorous plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its elongated, cylindrical-shaped leaf pitchers that are typically green with a red or purple rim. The plant produces nectar to attract insects, which then fall into the pitcher and are digested by the plant's digestive enzymes. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

This variety is prized by botanists and plant enthusiasts for its distinctive morphology and ecological adaptations.
