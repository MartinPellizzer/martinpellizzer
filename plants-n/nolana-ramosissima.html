Nolana ramosissima

Nolana ramosissima, commonly known as the silverleaf nolana, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant features silver-gray leaves covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh habitat. Its small, white or pink flowers bloom in clusters, adding ornamental value.

Nolana ramosissima is valued for its drought tolerance and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in suitable climates.
