Narcissus bicchianus

Narcissus bicchianus, commonly known as the Bicchian daffodil, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers with a white throat, which bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and along stream banks, preferring moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming a dense rosette at the base.

Narcissus bicchianus is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
