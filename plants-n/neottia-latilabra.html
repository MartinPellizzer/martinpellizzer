Neottia latilabra

Neottia latilabra, commonly known as the broad-leaved twayblade, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to various regions in Europe and Asia.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and heathlands. The plant has a slender, leafless stem that supports two broad, ovate leaves, which are often hairy along the edges. Its flowers are pale green to yellowish in color, with three sepals and three petals, and are usually arranged in a loose cluster.

Neottia latilabra is a non-photosynthetic plant, relying on mycorrhizal associations for nutrient uptake.
