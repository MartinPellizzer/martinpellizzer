Neofinetia richardsiana

Neofinetia richardsiana, commonly known as the silver fir, is a species of evergreen conifer native to China.

It belongs to the family Pinaceae and is characterized by its slender, upward-curving needles and a conical shape. The tree produces small, yellowish cones that mature to a reddish-brown color. Its wood is prized for its fine grain and durability, making it valuable in woodworking.

Neofinetia richardsiana thrives in cool, humid climates and is often found in mountainous regions.
