Neolitsea cassia var. cassia

Neolitsea cassia var. cassia, commonly known as the Chinese tallowtree, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It features opposite, simple, ovate to elliptic leaves that are usually dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits that split open to release seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value but can become invasive in certain regions.

The tree has a shallow root system and is adapted to a variety of soil types.
