Nepeta tarbagataica

Nepeta tarbagataica, commonly known as the Tarbagatai catmint, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its small, tubular flowers are usually blue to purple in color, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Nepeta tarbagataica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported calming properties.
