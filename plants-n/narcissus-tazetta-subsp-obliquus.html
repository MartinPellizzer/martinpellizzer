Narcissus tazetta subsp. obliquus

Narcissus tazetta subsp.

obliquus, commonly known as the Persian daffodil, is a flowering plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and a single, showy flower with a trumpet-shaped corona.

The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and blooms in early spring. Its flowers are usually yellow or white, with a distinctive, often reflexed, tepal arrangement.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
