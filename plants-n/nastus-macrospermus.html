Nastus macrospermus

Nastus macrospermus, commonly known as the large-seeded nasturtium, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae and is characterized by its bright yellow or orange flowers and edible, peppery leaves. The plant produces elongated, ribbed seed pods that contain numerous small, round seeds. Its botanical features include pinnately divided leaves and a compact, bushy growth habit.

Nastus macrospermus is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and as a source of edible greens.
