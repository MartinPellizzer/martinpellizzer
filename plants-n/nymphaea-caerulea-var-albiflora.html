Nymphaea caerulea var. albiflora

Nymphaea caerulea var. albiflora, commonly known as the white water lily, is a variety of the sacred blue water lily native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its broad, waxy, white petals that contrast with its deep green, heart-shaped leaves. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats, such as ponds and lakes. Its flowers are typically nocturnal, opening at night and closing during the day.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and historical significance in various cultures.
