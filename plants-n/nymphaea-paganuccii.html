Nymphaea paganuccii

Nymphaea paganuccii, commonly known as the Paganuccii water lily, is a species of aquatic plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy white to pink flowers that bloom above the water surface. The plant has broad, floating leaves that provide habitat for various aquatic organisms. Its roots anchor it in the muddy substrate, while its rhizomes spread horizontally to form dense colonies.

Nymphaea paganuccii plays an important role in water ecosystems by contributing to water filtration and supporting biodiversity.
