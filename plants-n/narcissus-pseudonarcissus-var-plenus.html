Narcissus pseudonarcissus var. plenus

Narcissus pseudonarcissus var. plenus, commonly known as the double-flowered daffodil, is a variant of the common daffodil characterized by its double petals.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is native to Europe. The plant features a sturdy stem with one or more leaves and produces a cluster of blooms with a yellow corona. Its flowers are typically larger and more showy than those of the standard variety, making it a popular ornamental plant.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, blooming in early spring.
