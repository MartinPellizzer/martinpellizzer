Nigella foeniculacea

Nigella foeniculacea, commonly known as Persian cumin or wild cumin, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces narrow, elongated pods that contain numerous tiny, dark brown seeds. These seeds are often used in culinary applications for their aromatic, slightly bitter flavor.

Nigella foeniculacea thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for various garden environments.
