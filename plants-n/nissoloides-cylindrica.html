Nissoloides cylindrica

Nissoloides cylindrica, commonly known as the cylindrical sedge, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems that often grow in dense clusters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a tendency to form dense vegetation.

Nissoloides cylindrica is often found in wetland or marshy habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
