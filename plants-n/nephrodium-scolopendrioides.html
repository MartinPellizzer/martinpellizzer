Nephrodium scolopendrioides

Nephrodium scolopendrioides, commonly known as the zigzag fern, is a tropical fern species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Davalliaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, zigzagging fronds that give it its common name. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit and produces small, spore-bearing structures called sorocarps. Its fronds are typically bioluminescent, a unique trait that aids in attracting nocturnal pollinators.

This fern is often found in humid, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices.
