Narcissus × egabrensis

Narcissus × egabrensis, commonly known as the Egabrensis daffodil, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Narcissus tazetta and Narcissus poeticus.

It is characterized by its large, showy, funnel-shaped flowers that typically range in color from white to yellow. The plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure. It is a hardy perennial that produces numerous blooms in early spring.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and floral arrangements.
