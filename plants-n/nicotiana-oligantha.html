Nicotiana oligantha

Nicotiana oligantha, commonly known as the slender tobacco plant, is a species native to South America.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its thin, branched stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The flowers are typically blue or violet, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and contains alkaloids, including nicotine.

This plant is often grown for its ornamental value and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
