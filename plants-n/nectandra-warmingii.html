Nectandra warmingii

Nectandra warmingii, commonly known as the yellow sandalwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, yellowish wood that is highly valued for its fragrance and durability. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by dark, spherical berries. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a smooth or slightly serrated margin.

Nectandra warmingii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.
