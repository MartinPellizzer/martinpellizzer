Nitidobulbon cymbidioides

Nitidobulbon cymbidioides, commonly known as the white orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elegant, white flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a subtle fragrance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories, where it can access the necessary humidity and indirect light. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Botanically, it belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is valued for its beauty and adaptability in cultivation.
