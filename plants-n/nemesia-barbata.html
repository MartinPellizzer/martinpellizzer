Nemesia barbata

Nemesia barbata, commonly known as the hairy nemesia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and profuse displays of small, colorful flowers. The plant features hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. Its flowers, typically pink or white, bloom in clusters and are attractive to pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Nemesia barbata is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soil.
