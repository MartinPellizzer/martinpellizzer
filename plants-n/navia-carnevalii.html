Navia carnevalii

Navia carnevalii, commonly known as the Carnaval's navia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Navia carnevalii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
