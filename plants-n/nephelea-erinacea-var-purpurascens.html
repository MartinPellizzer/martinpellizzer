Nephelea erinacea var. purpurascens

Nephelea erinacea var. purpurascens, commonly known as the purple-flowered nephelea, is a striking member of the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves that give it a delicate appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep purple, depending on the variety. Native to regions with temperate climates, it thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking both aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
