Nepeta ucranica subsp. hispanica

Nepeta ucranica subsp.

hispanica, commonly known as Spanish catmint, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves.

The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental value make it a valued addition to both wild and cultivated landscapes.
