Nepeta fissa

Nepeta fissa, commonly known as catmint, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its fragrant, slender stems and small, tubular blue or purple flowers. The plant produces aromatic leaves that are often used in herbal teas and traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy and easy-to-grow plant.

Nepeta fissa is also popular in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
