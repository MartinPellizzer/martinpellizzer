Notholaena sulphurea var. californica

Notholaena sulphurea var. californica, commonly known as the California golden moss, is a rare and endemic fern species native to the coastal regions of California.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in coastal woodlands and along stream banks. Its reproductive structures, including spore-producing sorus, are located on the underside of the fronds.
