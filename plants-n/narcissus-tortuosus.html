Narcissus tortuosus

Narcissus tortuosus, commonly known as the twisted daffodil, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and is characterized by its unique, twisted flower shape. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with a narrow, twisted corona, which distinguishes it from other daffodil species. It typically grows in woodland areas and thrives in well-drained soil.

Narcissus tortuosus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
