Nesoris bicolor

Nesoris bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, contrasting coloration on its petals and sepals. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers bloom in clusters, often displaying shades of white, yellow, and orange.

Nesoris bicolor is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in greenhouses for its unique visual appeal.
