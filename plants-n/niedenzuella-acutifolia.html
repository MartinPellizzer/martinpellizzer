Niedenzuella acutifolia

Niedenzuella acutifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are small and yellowish-white in color.

Niedenzuella acutifolia is adapted to arid conditions and is often found in regions with well-drained soils.
