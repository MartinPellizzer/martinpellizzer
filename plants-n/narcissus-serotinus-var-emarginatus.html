Narcissus serotinus var. emarginatus

Narcissus serotinus var. emarginatus, commonly known as the late-flowering daffodil, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Europe.

It produces striking yellow flowers with a distinctive, recurved petal shape and a narrow, elongated corona. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, after most common daffodil varieties. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming a dense rosette at the base.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant spring display.
