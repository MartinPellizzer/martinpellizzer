Nectandra staminea

Nectandra staminea, commonly known as the fragrant wood or yellow cedar, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers that are often fragrant, attracting pollinators such as bees and moths. Its wood is valued for its durability and unique scent, though it is not widely used commercially.

The plant thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.
