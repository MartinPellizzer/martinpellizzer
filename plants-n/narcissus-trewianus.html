Narcissus trewianus

Narcissus trewianus, commonly known as the Trewian daffodil, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia, where it thrives in woodland and meadow habitats. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers with bright yellow petals and a central corona. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it typically blooms in early spring.

Narcissus trewianus is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
