Narcissus tazetta var. orientalis

Narcissus tazetta var. orientalis, commonly known as the Persian daffodil, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to white, typically blooming in early spring. The plant features strap-like, grassy foliage that emerges from a clustered bulb. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.

The variety is also appreciated for its historical and cultural significance in horticulture.
