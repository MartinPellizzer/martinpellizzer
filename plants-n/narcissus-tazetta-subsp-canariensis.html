Narcissus tazetta subsp. canariensis

Narcissus tazetta subsp.

canariensis, commonly known as the Canary Island daffodil, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Canary Islands. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers.

The plant typically blooms in early spring, adding vibrant color to gardens and natural landscapes. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and relatively low maintenance requirements.
