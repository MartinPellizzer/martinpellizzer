Nasturtium tweediei

Nasturtium tweediei, commonly known as the yellow watercress, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It features upright stems with alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are followed by capsule fruits containing several seeds.

Nasturtium tweediei is known for its peppery taste and is often used in culinary applications or as a decorative plant.
