Navarretia involucrata var. trichophylla

Navarretia involucrata var. trichophylla, commonly known as the trichophylla navarretia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, delicate flowers and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in open, sunny habitats. It produces clusters of white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

Its leaves are usually covered in fine, hair-like structures, giving it the specific epithet "trichophylla," which refers to its hairy foliage.
