Narcissus pseudonarcissus var. lorifolius

Narcissus pseudonarcissus var. lorifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf daffodil, is a variant of the common daffodil characterized by its broader, more elongated leaves.

It is native to southern Europe and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. The plant produces bright yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers with a single row of petals. It blooms in early spring, typically between March and May, and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

This variety is noted for its hardiness and adaptability to various garden conditions.
