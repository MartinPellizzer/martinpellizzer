Nicotiana nepalensis

Nicotiana nepalensis, commonly known as the Nepal tobacco plant, is a herbaceous species native to the Himalayan regions of Nepal and northern India.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. Its leaves are broad and ovate, often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in clusters at the tips of the stems.

Nicotiana nepalensis is also notable for its potential use in traditional medicine and its role in the cultivation of tobacco species.
