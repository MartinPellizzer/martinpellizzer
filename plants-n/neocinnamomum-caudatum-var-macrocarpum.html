Neocinnamomum caudatum var. macrocarpum

Neocinnamomum caudatum var. macrocarpum, commonly known as the large-seeded cinnamon tree, is a member of the Lauraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and distinctive, elongated fruit capsules that contain aromatic seeds. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The species is valued for its essential oils and has been used in traditional medicine and as a spice.
