Nuxia congesta var. tomentosa

Nuxia congesta var. tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly nuxia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage, which gives it a soft, woolly appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

The leaves are typically small, ovate, and covered in a thick layer of fine, white hairs.
