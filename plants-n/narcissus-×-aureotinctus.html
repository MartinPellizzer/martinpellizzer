Narcissus × aureotinctus

Narcissus × aureotinctus, commonly known as the golden tincture daffodil, is a hybrid plant resulting from crosses between various narcissus species.

It is characterized by its striking yellow petals and a distinctive, elongated corona that is often tinged with orange or red. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters, with narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

The flowers bloom in early spring, adding a vibrant splash of color to gardens and landscapes.
