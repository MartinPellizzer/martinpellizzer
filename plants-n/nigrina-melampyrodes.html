Nigrina melampyrodes

Nigrina melampyrodes, commonly known as the red-flowered nigrina, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Melampodaceae and is characterized by its striking red petals and clustered inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, while its stems are often reddish in color.

Nigrina melampyrodes is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
