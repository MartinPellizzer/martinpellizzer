Neumannia imbricata

Neumannia imbricata, commonly known as the mountain holly, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in cloud forests. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a leathery texture with a waxy coating.

Neumannia imbricata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
