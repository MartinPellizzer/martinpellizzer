Nepeta supina subsp. buschii

Nepeta supina subsp.

buschii, commonly known as carpet bugloss, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features a mat-forming growth habit with dense clusters of small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical characteristics make it both ornamental and useful in ecological landscaping.
