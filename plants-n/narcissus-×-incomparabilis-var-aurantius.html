Narcissus × incomparabilis var. aurantius

Narcissus × incomparabilis var. aurantius, commonly known as the golden daffodil, is a hybrid cultivar characterized by its striking yellow flowers.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is prized for its vibrant, trumpet-shaped blooms that appear in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a basal rosette. Its flowers are usually 5–8 cm in diameter, featuring a bright yellow corona and a white or yellow perianth.

This variety is well-suited for gardens and is often used in floral arrangements due to its showy appearance.
