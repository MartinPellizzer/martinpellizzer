Navia filifera

Navia filifera, commonly known as the threadleaf navia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, thread-like leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered white or yellowish flowers, which are often borne on short pedicels. Navia filifera typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and ability to thrive in partial shade.
