Nymphaea minor

Nymphaea minor, commonly known as the lesser water lily, is a aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its floating leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes. Its leaves are broad and rounded, often with a waxy surface to repel water.

Nymphaea minor plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
