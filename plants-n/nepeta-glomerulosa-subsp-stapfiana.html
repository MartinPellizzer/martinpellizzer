Nepeta glomerulosa subsp. stapfiana

Nepeta glomerulosa subsp.

stapfiana, commonly known as the clustered catmint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features upright stems covered in dense, hairy foliage and produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from blue to purple.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens or borders. Its aromatic leaves and showy blooms attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
