Neotinea conica

Neotinea conica, commonly known as the narrow-leafed orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in late summer. The plant prefers shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories. Neotinea conica has a sympodial growth habit, with new shoots emerging from the base of the plant.

Its floral structure includes three sepals and three petals, with the latter often being showy and fragrant.
