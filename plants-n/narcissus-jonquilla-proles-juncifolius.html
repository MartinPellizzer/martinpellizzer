Narcissus jonquilla proles juncifolius

Narcissus jonquilla proles juncifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved daffodil, is a botanical species characterized by its distinctive foliage and floral structure.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is native to southern Europe, particularly the Iberian Peninsula. The plant features strap-like, narrow leaves that are often bluish-green in color, and its flowers consist of a large, showy trumpet-shaped corona surrounded by six narrow petals. It blooms in early spring, producing bright yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant and early blooming characteristics.
