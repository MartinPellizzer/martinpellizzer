Nicotiana suaveolens var. rosulata

Nicotiana suaveolens var. rosulata, commonly known as the sweet tobacco or rose-like tobacco, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and produces showy inflorescences. Its leaves are broad, ovate, and often have a velvety texture.

The species is cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
