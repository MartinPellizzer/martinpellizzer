Nigella turcica

Nigella turcica, commonly known as Turkish cumin, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and slender, branched stems. The plant produces small, black, oil-rich seeds that are widely used in cooking and traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, making it a hardy annual in temperate climates.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both agricultural and ornamental purposes.
