Nesaea kilimandscharica var. hispidula

Nesaea kilimandscharica var. hispidula, commonly known as the Kilimanjaro sandwort, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of East Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, hairy stems and small, clustered white or pale pink flowers. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine environments. Its leaves are typically fleshy and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention.

This variety is notable for its adaptability to harsh, arid conditions and its ecological importance in mountainous ecosystems.
