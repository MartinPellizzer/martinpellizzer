Narcissus × calathinus

Narcissus × calathinus, commonly known as the Crown Imperial, is a hybrid perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems that bear clusters of large, showy flowers resembling a crown, hence its common name. The flowers are typically trumpet-shaped, with six petals that curve backward, and they come in a variety of colors including yellow, orange, and red. The plant produces a basal rosette of narrow, sword-shaped leaves, and it thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and parks.
