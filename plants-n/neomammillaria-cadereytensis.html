Neomammillaria cadereytensis

Neomammillaria cadereytensis, commonly known as the Cadereyta Hedgehog Cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in white to yellow spines, which provide protection from herbivores and intense sunlight. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts and xeriscaping.
