Narcissus tazetta subsp. remopolensis

Narcissus tazetta subsp.

remopolensis, commonly known as the Persian daffodil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and large, showy flowers with a single, upright tepal that is usually yellow or white.

The plant typically blooms in early spring, producing a strong, sweet fragrance that attracts pollinators. It is a hardy perennial that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
