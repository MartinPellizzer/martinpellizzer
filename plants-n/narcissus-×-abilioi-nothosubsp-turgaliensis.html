Narcissus × abilioi nothosubsp. turgaliensis

Narcissus × abilioi nothosubsp.

turgaliensis, commonly known as the Turgaliensian daffodil, is a hybrid plant that exhibits characteristics of its parent species, Narcissus abilioi and Narcissus turgaliensis. It is a bulbous perennial with showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that typically bloom in early spring.

The plant features narrow, grass-like foliage and is native to regions in southern Europe and the Mediterranean. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant yellow blooms.

Due to its hybrid origin, it may exhibit variability in flower color and form.
