Nesothamnus incanus

Nesothamnus incanus, commonly known as the silverleaf thornbush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which provides a striking visual contrast against its woody stems. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by oval, woody fruits containing seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky soils.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects in degraded landscapes.
