Novenia tunariensis

Novenia tunariensis, commonly known as the Tunari palm, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly in Bolivia and Peru. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible fruits resembling small coconuts.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
