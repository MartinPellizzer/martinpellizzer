Neomammillaria

Neomammillaria, commonly known as the ghost cactus, is a genus of cacti native to Mexico.

These plants are characterized by their tall, columnar stems and are often adorned with white or pale blue spines. Neomammillaria species typically produce vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months. They are drought-tolerant and thrive in well-draining soil, making them popular among succulent enthusiasts.

The genus is valued for its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
