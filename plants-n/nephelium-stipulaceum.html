Nephelium stipulaceum

Nephelium stipulaceum, commonly known as rambutan, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, spiny fruit exterior, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are typically clustered in panicles. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate with a slightly pointed tip.

The fruit is typically eaten fresh or used in various culinary applications, and the tree is also valued for its ornamental qualities.
