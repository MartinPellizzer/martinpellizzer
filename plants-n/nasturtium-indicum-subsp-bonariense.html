Nasturtium indicum subsp. bonariense

Nasturtium indicum subsp.

bonariense, commonly known as the Brazilian nasturtium, is a vigorous climbing plant native to South America. It features heart-shaped, deeply lobed leaves and produces bright orange or yellow flowers with a distinctive spurred petal.

The plant is known for its peppery taste, making it a popular edible ornamental. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often used in garden borders or as a companion plant.

Its botanical characteristics include a trailing or climbing habit and showy, long-lasting blooms.
