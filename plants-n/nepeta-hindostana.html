Nepeta hindostana

Nepeta hindostana, commonly known as the Kashmiri catnip, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in whorls along the upper stems. Its leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate, with a serrated margin and a strong, minty fragrance.

Nepeta hindostana is often used in traditional medicine for its purported calming and therapeutic properties.
