Nyctocalos jucundum

Nyctocalos jucundum, commonly known as the moonflower vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, white, star-shaped flowers that open at night and emit a strong, sweet fragrance. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and a rapid growth habit, making it popular in gardens for its ornamental value. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

The flowers close during the day, giving the plant its name, which means "happy night.".
