Nicotiana rustica var. humilis

Nicotiana rustica var. humilis, commonly known as the dwarf tobacco plant, is a species native to the Americas.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its upright stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, violet-colored flowers that bloom in clusters. It contains nicotine and other alkaloids, making it significant in traditional medicine and cultural practices.

Its compact size and hardy nature make it suitable for cultivation in various climates.
