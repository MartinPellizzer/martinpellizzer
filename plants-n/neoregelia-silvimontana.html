Neoregelia silvimontana

Neoregelia silvimontana, commonly known as the silver star bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup to collect rainwater, a feature common among bromeliads. The plant produces vibrant, star-like flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, adding ornamental value. Its foliage is often covered in a silvery or white bloom, giving it its common name.

Neoregelia silvimontana thrives in high humidity and bright, indirect light, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.
