Nephrodium ecklonianum

Nephrodium ecklonianum, commonly known as the African shield fern, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery fronds that are typically arranged in a rosette formation. The plant produces small, spore-bearing structures called sporangia, which are clustered on the underside of the fronds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its unique morphology and adaptability make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
