Narcissus arcuatus

Narcissus arcuatus, commonly known as the bent-nose daffodil, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and western Asia, often found in woodland and scrub habitats. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces a single, elongated flower with a distinctive recurved tepal. Its flowers are typically yellow, with a white or greenish throat, and bloom in early spring.

Narcissus arcuatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
