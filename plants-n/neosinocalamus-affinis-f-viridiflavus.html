Neosinocalamus affinis f. viridiflavus

Neosinocalamus affinis f. viridiflavus, commonly known as the green-yellow bamboo, is a variant of the Neosinocalamus affinis species, characterized by its distinctive greenish-yellow foliage.

This plant belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is native to southern China, particularly in Guangdong and Guangxi provinces. It exhibits a slender, upright growth habit with long, narrow leaves that are often tinged with a pale yellow hue. The culms (stems) are typically smooth and have a fibrous texture, making it suitable for traditional crafts and construction.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions with a subtropical climate.
