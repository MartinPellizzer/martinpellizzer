Notodanthonia violacea

Notodanthonia violacea, commonly known as the violet grass or violet danthonia, is a species of grass native to Australia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and produces small, violet-colored inflorescences. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas. Its foliage is usually a bluish-green hue, and it is adapted to withstand salty and windy conditions.

Notodanthonia violacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing coastal dunes.
