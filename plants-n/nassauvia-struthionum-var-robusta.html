Nassauvia struthionum var. robusta

Nassauvia struthionum var. robusta, commonly known as the robust mountainbush, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, needle-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers during the spring and early summer, which are typically tubular and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
