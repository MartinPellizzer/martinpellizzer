Nothofagus fusca

Nothofagus fusca, commonly known as the silver beech, is a species of evergreen tree native to southern South America.

It belongs to the Nothofagus genus, which is part of the beech family Fagaceae. This plant is characterized by its silvery-gray bark and oval, leathery leaves that remain on the tree throughout the year. Nothofagus fusca typically grows in cool, moist temperate climates and is often found in mountainous regions.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
