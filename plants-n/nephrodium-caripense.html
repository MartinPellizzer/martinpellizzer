Nephrodium caripense

Nephrodium caripense, commonly known as the Caripense Kidney Fern, is a species of fern native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive kidney-shaped fronds, which are typically bright green and have a smooth, leathery texture. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, typical of many fern species.

Nephrodium caripense plays a role in maintaining the ecological balance of its habitat by providing shelter and food for various small organisms.
