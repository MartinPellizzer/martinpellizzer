Nephrodium aciculatum

Nephrodium aciculatum, commonly known as the needle fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, needle-like fronds that are typically arranged in a compact, upright clump. The plant produces sporangia on the underside of its leaves, which are clustered in sori, a common feature among ferns. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The botanical structure of Nephrodium aciculatum reflects its adaptation to humid, low-light conditions, making it a valuable component of diverse ecosystems.
