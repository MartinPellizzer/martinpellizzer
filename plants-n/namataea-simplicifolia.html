Namataea simplicifolia

Namataea simplicifolia, commonly known as the simple-leaf fern, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, elongated leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, which are often borne on slender stalks. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
