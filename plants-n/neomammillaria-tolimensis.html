Neomammillaria tolimensis

Neomammillaria tolimensis, commonly known as the Tolima prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to Colombia.

It is characterized by its globular to cylindrical stem and is covered in spines that provide protection against herbivores. The plant produces yellow flowers during the rainy season, which are followed by edible, red fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Neomammillaria tolimensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
