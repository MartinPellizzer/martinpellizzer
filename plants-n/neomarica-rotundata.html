Neomarica rotundata

Neomarica rotundata, commonly known as the pink flamingo lily, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces striking, showy flowers with a pinkish-purple hue. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle. Its flowers bloom in clusters and are known for their long-lasting display.

Neomarica rotundata prefers warm, humid environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
