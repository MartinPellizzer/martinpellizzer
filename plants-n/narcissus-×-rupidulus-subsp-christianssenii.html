Narcissus × rupidulus subsp. christianssenii

Narcissus × rupidulus subsp.

christianssenii, commonly known as the Christiansen's dwarf daffodil, is a hybrid plant characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive floral morphology. It typically features narrow, grass-like leaves and small, bell-shaped flowers with a yellow perianth.

The plant is native to southern Europe and thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. It is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it forms dense, low-growing clumps.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
