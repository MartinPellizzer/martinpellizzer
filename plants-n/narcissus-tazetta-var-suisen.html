Narcissus tazetta var. suisen

Narcissus tazetta var. suisen, commonly known as the Japanese daffodil, is a variety of the genus Narcissus native to Japan.

It is characterized by its striking, single blooms with a large, trumpet-shaped corona and a cluster of small, bright yellow petals. The plant typically grows to a height of about 20 to 30 centimeters, with strap-like, grassy leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, blooming in early spring.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, cheerful flowers.
