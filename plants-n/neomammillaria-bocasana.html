Neomammillaria bocasana

Neomammillaria bocasana, commonly known as the Bocasana prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are ribbed and covered with pale green to bluish-green spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are followed by red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun exposure.

Neomammillaria bocasana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
