Nipponocalamus gozadakensis

Nipponocalamus gozadakensis, commonly known as the Philippine bamboo, is a member of the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to the Philippines and is characterized by its slender, culm-like stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. It has a clumping growth habit and is valued for its ornamental appearance.

Botanically, it exhibits features typical of grasses, including hollow stems and rhizomes for vegetative propagation.
