Nasa humboldtiana subsp. roseoalba

Nasa humboldtiana subsp.

roseoalba, commonly known as the white-flowered nightshade, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Andes. It is characterized by its striking white or pale pink flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators.

The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, fleshy berries that are edible but should be consumed in moderation. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or mountainous regions.

Its unique blooming pattern and ornamental flowers make it a sought-after plant for botanical gardens and collectors.
