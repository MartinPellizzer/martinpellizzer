Nephrophyllidium crista-galli subsp. japonicum

Nephrophyllidium crista-galli subsp.

japonicum, commonly known as the Japanese cock's-comb, is a herbaceous plant native to East Asia. It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, feathery inflorescence that resembles a cock's comb.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or grassland habitats. Its leaves are lanceolate and often have a reddish or purplish tinge, adding to its ornamental value.

It is cultivated for its vibrant floral display and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
