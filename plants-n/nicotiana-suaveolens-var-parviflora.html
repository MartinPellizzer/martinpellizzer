Nicotiana suaveolens var. parviflora

Nicotiana suaveolens var. parviflora, commonly known as the sweet tobacco or parviflora tobacco, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic foliage and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and thrives in full sun with well-drained soil. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
