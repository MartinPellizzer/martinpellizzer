Nepeta ouroumitanensis

Nepeta ouroumitanensis, commonly known as the Armenian catmint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Armenia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are often fragrant and have a mint-like scent, which is typical of many nepeta species.

Nepeta ouroumitanensis is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its traditional use in herbal medicine.
