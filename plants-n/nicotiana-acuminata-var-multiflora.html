Nicotiana acuminata var. multiflora

Nicotiana acuminata var. multiflora, commonly known as the multiflora tobacco, is a herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant produces numerous small, oblong leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
