Neomammillaria longiflora

Neomammillaria longiflora, commonly known as the long-flowered nipple cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a small, compact cactus with a ribbed stem that develops tubercles as it matures. The plant produces delicate, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in the spring. Its foliage is typically green with a bluish tinge, and it is well adapted to arid environments.

Neomammillaria longiflora is prized by cactus enthusiasts for its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
