Najas ancistrocarpa

Najas ancistrocarpa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf watermilfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater habitats in the Americas.

It belongs to the family Najadaceae and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged, followed by small, elliptical fruits. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Its growth is typically found in shallow, slow-moving waters with soft substrates.
