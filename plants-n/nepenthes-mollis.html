Nepenthes mollis

Nepenthes mollis, commonly known as the slender pitcher plant, is a tropical carnivorous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow pitchers that are typically green or reddish in color. The plant's leaves form the pitcher-shaped structures that secrete a sticky fluid to trap insects. Nepenthes mollis thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in montane forests.

Its unique adaptations for nutrient acquisition make it a fascinating subject in botanical studies.
