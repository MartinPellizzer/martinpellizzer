Nasturtium tibeticum

Nasturtium tibeticum, commonly known as the Tibetan watercress, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Tibetan Plateau.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like mustard and cress. The plant features small, edible leaves that are often used in local cuisines for their peppery flavor. Its flowers are typically bright yellow or orange, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Nasturtium tibeticum thrives in cool, moist environments and is adapted to the harsh conditions of its mountainous habitat.
