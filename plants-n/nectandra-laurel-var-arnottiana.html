Nectandra laurel var. arnottiana

Nectandra laurel var. arnottiana, commonly known as Arnott's nectandra, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, particularly in countries like Brazil and Colombia. The plant is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are glossy and aromatic, and its small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The wood of this species is valued for its durability and is used in furniture and construction.
