Narcissus tazetta subsp. cypri

Narcissus tazetta subsp.

cypri, commonly known as the Cyprus daffodil, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the island of Cyprus. It produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange, typically blooming in early spring.

The plant features strap-like, grassy leaves that emerge from a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical characteristics make it both ornamental and culturally significant in Mediterranean horticulture.
