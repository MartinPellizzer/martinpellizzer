Newtonia aubrevillei

Newtonia aubrevillei, commonly known as the African bell tree, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in axillary inflorescences, followed by woody, spherical fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland and forest margins.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some regions for its aesthetic appeal.
