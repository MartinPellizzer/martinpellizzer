Nymphaea alba f. coronata

Nymphaea alba f. coronata, commonly known as the white water lily with a crown, is a variant of the white water lily native to temperate regions.

It is characterized by its large, white, waxy petals that are often adorned with a distinctive crown-like pattern at the base. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes. Its leaves are broad and floating, with a prominent midrib, and it produces showy, fragrant flowers that open in the early morning.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in water gardens.
