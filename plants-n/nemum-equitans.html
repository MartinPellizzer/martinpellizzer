Nemum equitans

Nemum equitans, commonly known as the horse chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves and showy, white or pinkish flowers that appear in spring. The tree produces spiny, capsule-like fruits that contain smooth, chestnut-like seeds. Its bark is smooth and gray, becoming fissured with age.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
