Narcissus triandrus subsp. capax

Narcissus triandrus subsp.

capax, commonly known as the Spanish daffodil, is a species native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It is characterized by its large, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces a single flower stem with a few blooms. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in rocky or coastal areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and is commonly cultivated in gardens.
