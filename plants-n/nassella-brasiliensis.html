Nassella brasiliensis

Nassella brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical spikelets and fine, wiry leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It is often used in ornamental gardening due to its delicate, feathery foliage.

Nassella brasiliensis is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion in ecological restoration projects.
