Neubeckia stylosa

Neubeckia stylosa, commonly known as the slender beakviolet, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It features slender, upright stems that bear showy, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive beak-like shape. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Neubeckia stylosa is valued for its ornamental value and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
