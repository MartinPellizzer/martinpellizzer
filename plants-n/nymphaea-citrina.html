Nymphaea citrina

Nymphaea citrina, commonly known as the yellow water lily, is a species of water lily native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves that are typically green with a yellowish tint. The flowers are showy, with a yellow center surrounded by white petals, and they open in the morning. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important component of aquatic ecosystems.
