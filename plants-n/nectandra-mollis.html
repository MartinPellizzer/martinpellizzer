Nectandra mollis

Nectandra mollis, commonly known as the fragrant sandarac tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes other aromatic plants like cinnamon and laurel. The tree is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and dense, glossy green leaves. It produces small, white flowers that are followed by elongated, woody fruits containing aromatic seeds.

The plant is valued for its fragrant resin, which has been traditionally used in incense and medicinal applications.
