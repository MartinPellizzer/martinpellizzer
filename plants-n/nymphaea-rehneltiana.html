Nymphaea rehneltiana

Nymphaea rehneltiana, commonly known as the Rehnelt's water lily, is a species of aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes. Its leaves are typically a deep green, while its flowers range from white to pale pink, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Nymphaea rehneltiana plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and oxygen to water bodies.
