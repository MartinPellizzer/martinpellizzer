Nymphaea wenzelii

Nymphaea wenzelii, commonly known as the Wenzel's water lily, is a species of aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Nymphaeaceae family and is characterized by its broad, floating leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically blooms in summer, producing pink to red flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its roots anchor in the muddy substrate, while its leaves provide habitat for various aquatic organisms.

Nymphaea wenzelii plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by supporting biodiversity and contributing to water quality.
