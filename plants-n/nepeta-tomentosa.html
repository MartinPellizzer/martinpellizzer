Nepeta tomentosa

Nepeta tomentosa, commonly known as catmint, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves covered in a dense layer of fine, white pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, pale blue to lavender flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, sharing characteristics with other mint relatives such as lavender and sage.

Nepeta tomentosa is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and as a source of nectar for pollinators.
