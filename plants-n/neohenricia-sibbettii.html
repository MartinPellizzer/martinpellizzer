Neohenricia sibbettii

Neohenricia sibbettii, commonly known as the Sibbett's ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

The plant typically grows in wet, pine flatwood habitats and relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it lacks a visible stem, making it appear almost like a cluster of flowers.
