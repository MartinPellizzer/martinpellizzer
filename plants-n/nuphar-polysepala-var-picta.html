Nuphar polysepala var. picta

Nuphar polysepala var. picta, commonly known as the painted yellow pond lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to parts of Asia.

It features large, heart-shaped leaves that float on the water's surface, with a waxy, yellow-green coloration. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, adding visual interest to ponds and lakes. Its leaves are often marked with distinctive patterns, contributing to its common name.

The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
