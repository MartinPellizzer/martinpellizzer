Nepeta nepetella var. atlantica

Nepeta nepetella var. atlantica, commonly known as the Atlantic catmint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Iberian Peninsula.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine hairs and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its aromatic foliage and showy blooms make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and ecological landscapes.
