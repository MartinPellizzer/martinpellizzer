Nectandra villosa

Nectandra villosa, commonly known as the hairy nectandra, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments within lowland rainforests. Its bark and leaves contain essential oils, which have been used in traditional medicine.

Nectandra villosa is also valued for its timber, which is durable and used in construction and furniture-making.
