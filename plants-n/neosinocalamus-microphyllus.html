Neosinocalamus microphyllus

Neosinocalamus microphyllus, commonly known as the small-leaved golden bamboo, is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that are often used in traditional crafts and construction. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are narrow and have a smooth, waxy surface, which helps reduce water loss in its natural habitat.

This bamboo species is valued for its rapid growth and ecological role in stabilizing soil in forested areas.
