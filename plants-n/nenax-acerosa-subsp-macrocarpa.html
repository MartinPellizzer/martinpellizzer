Nenax acerosa subsp. macrocarpa

Nenax acerosa subsp.

macrocarpa, commonly known as the broad-fruited nenax, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and dark green, ovate leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its fruits are large, woody capsules that split open to release seeds.

This species is adapted to sandy, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and dunes.
