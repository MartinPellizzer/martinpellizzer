Nepeta carmanica

Nepeta carmanica, commonly known as carman catmint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, upright stems covered in soft, hairy foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, pale blue to lavender flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils that are often used in herbal remedies.

Nepeta carmanica is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
