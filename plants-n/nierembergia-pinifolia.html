Nierembergia pinifolia

Nierembergia pinifolia, commonly known as the pineleaf bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its finely divided, needle-like leaves that resemble pine needles, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, often appearing in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Nierembergia pinifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
