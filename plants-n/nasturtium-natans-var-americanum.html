Nasturtium natans var. americanum

Nasturtium natans var. americanum, commonly known as the American watercress, is a semi-aquatic plant native to North America.

It thrives in shallow water and moist soil, often found along streams and ponds. The plant features slender, floating stems with small, rounded leaves that are edible and have a peppery flavor. Its flowers are bright yellow with orange centers, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

This variety is valued both for its culinary use and its role in aquatic ecosystems.
