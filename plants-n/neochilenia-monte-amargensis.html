Neochilenia monte-amargensis

Neochilenia monte-amargensis, commonly known as the Monte Amargoso orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the high-altitude regions of southern Chile, specifically in the Andes Mountains. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces delicate, fragrant flowers with a unique coloration. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in a fine layer of hair.

Due to its restricted habitat and threatened status, it is of particular interest to botanists and conservationists.
