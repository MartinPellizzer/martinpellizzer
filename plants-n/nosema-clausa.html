Nosema clausa

Nosema clausa, commonly known as the "closed nosema," is a species of fungus belonging to the family Nosematidae.

It is primarily found in association with certain insects, particularly bees, where it resides in the gut. The fungus is characterized by its unique, compact spore structure and its ability to survive in harsh environmental conditions. Its life cycle involves the production of spores that are transmitted through the insect's feces.

Due to its parasitic nature, Nosema clausa plays a role in the ecological dynamics of its host populations.
