Nepeta sibthorpii subsp. dirphya

Nepeta sibthorpii subsp.

dirphya, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada catmint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of western North America. It features aromatic, gray-green foliage and produces clusters of small, lavender-blue flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy and drought-tolerant addition to native plant gardens.
