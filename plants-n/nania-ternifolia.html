Nania ternifolia

Nania ternifolia, commonly known as the three-leafed nania, is a herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters and features small, lance-shaped leaves arranged in whorls of three along its stems. The plant produces tiny, white or pale yellow flowers during the late spring and early summer months. Nania ternifolia thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or shaded gardens.

Its foliage is often used in traditional herbal remedies for its mild medicinal properties.
