Narcissus cupularis

Narcissus cupularis, commonly known as the cupular daffodil, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers with a cup-shaped corona. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its blooms appear in early spring, often before the leaves are fully developed.

Narcissus cupularis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, long-lasting flowers.
