Notoseris khasiana

Notoseris khasiana, commonly known as the Khasi sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the northeastern region of India.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas and has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its yellow-orange flower heads consist of numerous small florets, and it produces achene fruits that are dispersed by wind.

Notoseris khasiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in local habitats.
