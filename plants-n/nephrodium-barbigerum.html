Nephrodium barbigerum

Nephrodium barbigerum, commonly known as the hairy kidney fern, is a species of fern characterized by its distinctive, kidney-shaped fronds.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia. The plant features a creeping rhizome with dense, hairy foliage that gives it its common name. Its fronds are bi- or tri-pinnate, with a feathery appearance and are often used in ornamental gardening.

Nephrodium barbigerum thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
