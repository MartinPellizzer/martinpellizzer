Nectandra yarinensis

Nectandra yarinensis, commonly known as the Yarinensis Nectandra, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves. The tree produces small, white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences, followed by oval, woody fruits containing aromatic seeds. Its bark and leaves contain essential oils, making it valuable in traditional medicine and aromatherapy.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland tropical forests.
