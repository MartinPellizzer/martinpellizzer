Nolletia ligulata

Nolletia ligulata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed spurge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually narrow and lance-shaped. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are often surrounded by bracts. It is typically found in dry, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Nolletia ligulata is known for its milky sap, which is a common feature among members of the Euphorbiaceae family.
