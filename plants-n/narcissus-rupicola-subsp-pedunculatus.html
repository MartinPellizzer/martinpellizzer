Narcissus rupicola subsp. pedunculatus

Narcissus rupicola subsp.

pedunculatus, commonly known as the rock narcissus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, cup-shaped flowers in shades of white and yellow.

The plant thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is well adapted to dry, sunny conditions. It is often found in mountainous regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

This subspecies is closely related to other members of the Narcissus genus, sharing similar botanical features.
