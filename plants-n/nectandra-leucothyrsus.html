Nectandra leucothyrsus

Nectandra leucothyrsus, commonly known as white thornwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other aromatic plants such as cinnamon and laurel. The tree is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and simple, elliptical leaves with a glossy surface. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically white or pale yellow, and it produces woody fruits containing aromatic seeds.

The plant is valued for its essential oils and has traditional uses in medicine and perfumery.
