Nymphaea acutiloba

Nymphaea acutiloba, commonly known as the sharp-lobed water lily, is a aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that float on the water surface, supported by long, slender petioles. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that can be white, pink, or purple, typically opening in the early morning and closing by midday. Its roots anchor it in muddy substrates, while its rhizomes help in nutrient absorption.

Nymphaea acutiloba plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various water organisms and contributing to water purification.
