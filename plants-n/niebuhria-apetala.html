Niebuhria apetala

Niebuhria apetala, commonly known as the leafless sunflower, is a unique flowering plant characterized by its absence of leaves, giving it a striking appearance.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is native to parts of Africa and the Middle East. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers that resemble those of the sunflower, despite its lack of foliage. Its growth habit is typically shrub-like, with a compact structure and a long blooming period.
