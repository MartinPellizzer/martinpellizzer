Neosinocalamus grandis

Neosinocalamus grandis, commonly known as the giant bamboo, is a tall, slender grass-like plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its hollow, jointed stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant can grow up to 20 meters in height, making it one of the tallest bamboo species. Its culms are strong and flexible, often used in construction and handicrafts.

Neosinocalamus grandis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland forests.
