Nepeta denudata

Nepeta denudata, commonly known as catmint, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in herbal remedies due to their mild mint-like scent.

Nepeta denudata is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
