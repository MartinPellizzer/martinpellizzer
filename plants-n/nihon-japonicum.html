Nihon japonicum

Nihon japonicum, commonly known as the Japanese bellflower, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Nihon japonicum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is commonly cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
