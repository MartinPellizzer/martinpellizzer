Nama serpylloides var. conferta

Nama serpylloides var. conferta, commonly known as the dense or compact nama grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal and inland fynbos vegetation. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually densely clustered at the base, giving it a compact appearance. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikelets, typical of the Poaceae family.

This variant is valued for its ability to thrive in harsh environments and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
