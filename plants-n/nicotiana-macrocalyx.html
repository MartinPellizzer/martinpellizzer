Nicotiana macrocalyx

Nicotiana macrocalyx, commonly known as the large-flowered tobacco, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that can reach up to 5 centimeters in diameter, typically displaying shades of pink to purple. The plant has hairy stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and botanical collections.

Its flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
