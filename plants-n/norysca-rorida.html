Norysca rorida

Norysca rorida, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of southern Africa.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale yellow to orange, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are slightly hairy and covered in fine pubescence. Its root system consists of fibrous roots that help it thrive in well-drained soils.

Norysca rorida is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and public landscapes.
