Neolitsea zeylanica var. obovata

Neolitsea zeylanica var. obovata, commonly known as the broad-leafed litsea, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is characterized by its obovate, glossy leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by elliptical, dark blue-black berries. Native to Southeast Asia, it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

It is often found in lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential medicinal uses.
