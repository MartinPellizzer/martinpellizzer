Nasturtium lippizense

Nasturtium lippizense, commonly known as the Lippizian nasturtium, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange and yellow flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant features heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly rough texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The flowers are not only ornamental but also edible, adding a peppery flavor to salads and dishes.
