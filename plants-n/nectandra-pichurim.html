Nectandra pichurim

Nectandra pichurim, commonly known as the Pichurim sandarac tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark-colored wood and glossy, elliptical leaves. The tree produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by fleshy, red berries containing seeds. Its bark and leaves contain various bioactive compounds, making it of interest in traditional medicine and phytochemical research.

Nectandra pichurim is also valued for its role in local ecosystems and its potential for sustainable forestry.
