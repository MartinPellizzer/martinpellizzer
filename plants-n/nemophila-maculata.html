Nemophila maculata

Nemophila maculata, commonly known as the fairy bluebell, is a flowering plant native to western North America.

It belongs to the Boraginaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine, fuzzy texture.
