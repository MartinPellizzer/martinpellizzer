Nepeta ucranica subsp. braun-blanquetii

Nepeta ucranica subsp.

braun-blanquetii, commonly known as the Braun-Blanquet catmint, is a perennial herb native to the Carpathian region of Ukraine. It features aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and produces spikes of blue to purple flowers in the summer months.

The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showing a preference for well-drained soils. Its foliage is often used in traditional herbal remedies, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

This subspecies is closely related to other members of the Nepeta genus, sharing similar morphological traits.
