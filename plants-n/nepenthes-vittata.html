Nepenthes vittata

Nepenthes vittata, commonly known as the striped pitcher plant, is a tropical carnivorous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive elongated, pitcher-shaped leaves that secrete a digestive enzyme-laden liquid to trap and digest insects. The plant's unique adaptation allows it to thrive in nutrient-poor environments by supplementing its diet with captured prey. Its striking green and red striped patterns on the pitcher help in attracting insects.

Nepenthes vittata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is a popular subject in botanical studies.
