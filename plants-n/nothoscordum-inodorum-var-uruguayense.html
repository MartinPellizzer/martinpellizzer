Nothoscordum inodorum var. uruguayense

Nothoscordum inodorum var. uruguayense, commonly known as the Uruguay wild leek, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its strap-like, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
