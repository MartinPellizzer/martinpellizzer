Nigrina physalodes

Nigrina physalodes, commonly known as the giant balloon vine, is a tropical climbing plant characterized by its large, inflated, balloon-like fruits.

The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its leaves are heart-shaped and can grow quite large, often reaching up to 30 cm in length. The fruits, which can be as big as a football, contain a fibrous pulp and seeds.

Nigrina physalodes is native to regions in South America and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
