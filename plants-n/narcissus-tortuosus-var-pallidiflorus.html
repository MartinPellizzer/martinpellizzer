Narcissus tortuosus var. pallidiflorus

Narcissus tortuosus var. pallidiflorus, commonly known as the paleflowered twisted daffodil, is a rare and ornamental variety of daffodil native to southern Europe.

It features narrow, twisted petals that curve inward, creating a unique and delicate floral structure. The flowers are typically pale yellow with a white or cream center, giving it a soft and elegant appearance. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This variety is valued for its unusual morphology and is often cultivated in specialized gardens.
