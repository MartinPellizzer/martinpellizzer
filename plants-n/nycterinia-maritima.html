Nycterinia maritima

Nycterinia maritima, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its distinctive blue or purple flowers and silvery, woolly leaves that help reflect sunlight. The plant thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in maritime environments. Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Nycterinia maritima is well adapted to salt spray and strong winds, making it a hardy species of coastal ecosystems.
