Nomaphila quadrangularis

Nomaphila quadrangularis, commonly known as the four-angle moth orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique quadrangular stem and delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments within tropical forests. Its leaves are elongated and lance-shaped, often with a waxy texture.

The orchid is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
