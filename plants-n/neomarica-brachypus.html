Neomarica brachypus

Neomarica brachypus, commonly known as the dwarf ginger orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are broad and grass-like, and it has a short, rhizomatous root system.

Neomarica brachypus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its unique floral display.
