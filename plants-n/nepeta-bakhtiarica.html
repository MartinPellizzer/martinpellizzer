Nepeta bakhtiarica

Nepeta bakhtiarica, commonly known as Persian catmint, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Iran.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant produces small, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often fragrant and have a mint-like scent, which is characteristic of the Nepeta genus.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and flowering habit.
