Nothoscordum inodorum var. euosmum

Nothoscordum inodorum var. euosmum, commonly known as the sweet-scented false garlic, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It features strap-like, grassy leaves that are often aromatic when crushed. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in summer. It is valued for its mild, garlic-like scent and is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its botanical characteristics make it a unique addition to gardens and botanical collections.
