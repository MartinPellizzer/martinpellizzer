Nymphoides bosseri

Nymphoides bosseri, commonly known as the golden bell orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers that resemble small bells. The plant typically grows in wetlands and marshy areas, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, often forming dense foliage that helps in water retention.

Nymphoides bosseri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and aquatic landscapes.
