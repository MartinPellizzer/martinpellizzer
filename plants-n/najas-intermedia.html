Najas intermedia

Najas intermedia, commonly known as watermilfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It is characterized by its whorled arrangement of narrow, linear leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving waters and is often found in ponds, lakes, and wetlands. Its rapid growth can lead to dense vegetation, which can affect water quality and aquatic ecosystems.

Najas intermedia plays a role in providing habitat for aquatic invertebrates and fish.
