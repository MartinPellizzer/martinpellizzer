Nesiota elliptica

Nesiota elliptica, commonly known as the rock fig, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in coastal areas. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, fig-like fruits. Its leaves are elliptical and have a smooth texture, often with a waxy coating.

Nesiota elliptica is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging environmental conditions.
