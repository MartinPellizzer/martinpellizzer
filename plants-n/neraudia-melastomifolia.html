Neraudia melastomifolia

Neraudia melastomifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian violet, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its showy, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories. It has opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth texture and emits a faint fragrance.

Its fruit is a capsule that disperses seeds through wind, aiding in its propagation.
