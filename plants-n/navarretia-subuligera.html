Navarretia subuligera

Navarretia subuligera, commonly known as the Sierra Point's needleleaf, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its slender, needle-like leaves and small white or pale yellow flowers. This species typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils in open habitats such as meadows and slopes. It is often found in the Sierra Nevada and other mountain ranges, where it thrives in full sun.

Navarretia subuligera is known for its delicate appearance and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
