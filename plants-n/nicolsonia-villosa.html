Nicolsonia villosa

Nicolsonia villosa, commonly known as the woolly nicolsonia, is a flowering plant native to the southern regions of South America.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its hairy leaves and compact growth habit make it well adapted to humid, temperate climates.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partially shaded conditions.
