Nervilia acuminata

Nervilia acuminata, commonly known as the slender orchid, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Asia and Australia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, grass-like leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or wetlands. Its roots are fibrous and help it anchor in nutrient-poor soils.

Nervilia acuminata is often found in association with specific fungal symbionts, which aid in its nutrient uptake.
