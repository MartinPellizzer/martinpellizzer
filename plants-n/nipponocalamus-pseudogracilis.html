Nipponocalamus pseudogracilis

Nipponocalamus pseudogracilis, commonly known as the Japanese slender bamboo, is a species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems that can reach heights of up to 5 meters. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically green and arranged in a dense, compact form. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Its unique morphology makes it a valuable species for both ornamental and ecological purposes.
