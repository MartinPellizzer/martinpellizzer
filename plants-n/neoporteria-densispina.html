Neoporteria densispina

Neoporteria densispina, commonly known as the dense-spined neoporteria, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, clustered flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has compound leaves with numerous leaflets and produces small, round fruits that are often consumed by birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as an ornamental plant.
