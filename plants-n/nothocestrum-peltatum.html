Nothocestrum peltatum

Nothocestrum peltatum, commonly known as the velvetleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous small, kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Nothocestrum peltatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other regions as a garden plant.
