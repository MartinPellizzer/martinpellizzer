Nassavia terminalis

Nassavia terminalis, commonly known as the red sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its sprawling growth habit and distinctive red or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has fleshy, succulent leaves that help it store water, adapting to its harsh, dry environment. Its root system is deep and robust, allowing it to access moisture from underground sources.

Nassavia terminalis plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for small desert organisms.
