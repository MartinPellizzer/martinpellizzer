Nasturtium cernuum

Nasturtium cernuum, commonly known as the creeping nasturtium, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant produces edible leaves and seeds, which are often used in culinary applications. Its botanical name, *Tropaeolum majus*, reflects its historical use in traditional medicine.

Nasturtium cernuum thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental and edible plants.
