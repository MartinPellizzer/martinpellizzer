Nicotiana fruticosa var. angustifolia

Nicotiana fruticosa var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf tobacco plant, is a herbaceous shrub native to the southern United States.

It features aromatic, glandular leaves and produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink and purple. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and serrated foliage, which contribute to its distinct appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in disturbed habitats.

Botanically, it is closely related to other Nicotiana species and is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical significance in tobacco cultivation.
