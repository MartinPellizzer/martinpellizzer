Neomarica northiana

Neomarica northiana, commonly known as the Christmas lily, is a striking tropical plant native to the Caribbean islands.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom during the holiday season, typically from December to January. The plant features strap-like, evergreen leaves that are broad and lance-shaped, often reaching lengths of up to 30 inches. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Neomarica northiana is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its beauty and seasonal blooming.
