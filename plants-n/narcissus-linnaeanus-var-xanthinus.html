Narcissus linnaeanus var. xanthinus

Narcissus linnaeanus var. xanthinus, commonly known as the yellow daffodil, is a hybrid variety of the genus Narcissus.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow petals and a trumpet-shaped corona. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters, with narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

This variety is prized for its early blooming and cheerful appearance in spring.
