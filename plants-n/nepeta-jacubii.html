Nepeta jacubii

Nepeta jacubii, commonly known as catmint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant blue or purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their calming properties.

Nepeta jacubii is also popular in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
