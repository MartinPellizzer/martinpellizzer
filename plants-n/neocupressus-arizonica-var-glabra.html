Neocupressus arizonica var. glabra

Neocupressus arizonica var. glabra, commonly known as the Arizona white fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and needle-like leaves that are typically short and blunt. The plant produces small, spherical cones that mature to a reddish-brown color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid climates.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a subject of interest in botanical studies.
