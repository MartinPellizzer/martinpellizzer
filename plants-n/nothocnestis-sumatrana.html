Nothocnestis sumatrana

Nothocnestis sumatrana, commonly known as the Sumatran banyan, is a species of flowering plant belonging to the Moraceae family.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. The plant is notable for its ability to form aerial roots, which can develop into additional trunks, giving it a distinctive, tree-like appearance. Native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, it thrives in humid, warm climates with ample rainfall.

This species is often found in lowland forests and is valued for its ecological role in supporting biodiversity.
