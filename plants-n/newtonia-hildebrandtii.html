Newtonia hildebrandtii

Newtonia hildebrandtii, commonly known as the African rosewood, is a small evergreen tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces woody pods that contain seeds covered in a silky, hairy coating. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savannas and woodlands.

Due to its ornamental value and durable wood, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and for its timber.
