Neolacis membranacea

Neolacis membranacea, commonly known as the membranous lacis, is a unique flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, papery leaves and small, clustered flowers that often display a striking contrast in color. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in tropical rainforests. Its botanical significance lies in its role as a food source for certain pollinators and its potential medicinal properties.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
