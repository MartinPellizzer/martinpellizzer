Nepeta erivanensis

Nepeta erivanensis, commonly known as the Erivan catmint, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a mint-like scent when crushed.

Nepeta erivanensis is often grown as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and blooms.
