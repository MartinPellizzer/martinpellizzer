Nectandra puberula var. chartaea

Nectandra puberula var. chartaea, commonly known as the white-flowered nectandra, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by elongated, woody fruits containing aromatic seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.

The species is also noted for its potential in ecological restoration due to its ability to stabilize soil in degraded areas.
