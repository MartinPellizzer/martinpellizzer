Neoschroetera cuneifolia

Neoschroetera cuneifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves that are narrow and elongated. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers clustered in an inflorescence called a spadix, which is typically enclosed by a modified leaf called a spathe. Neoschroetera cuneifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking foliage.
