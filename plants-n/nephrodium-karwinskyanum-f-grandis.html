Nephrodium karwinskyanum f. grandis

Nephrodium karwinskyanum f. grandis, commonly known as the giant nephroid, is a rare fern species characterized by its large, intricately divided fronds.

The plant exhibits a unique morphology with fronds that can reach lengths of up to two meters, showcasing a complex arrangement of pinnae and pinnules. It thrives in shaded, moist environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions. The reproductive structures, including sporangia, are clustered in sori on the underside of the fronds.

This fern is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
