Nepeta hajastana

Nepeta hajastana, commonly known as Caucasian catmint, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant features square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a fuzzy texture. Its flowers are typically blue to violet in color, arranged in whorled clusters at the ends of the branches.

Nepeta hajastana is often grown as an ornamental plant for its attractive appearance and pleasant fragrance.
