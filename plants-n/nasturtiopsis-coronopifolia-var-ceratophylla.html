Nasturtiopsis coronopifolia var. ceratophylla

Nasturtiopsis coronopifolia var. ceratophylla, commonly known as the velvetleaf watercress, is a perennial aquatic plant native to tropical regions.

It features finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, and its leaves are edible, possessing a peppery flavor similar to watercress. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, round fruits.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary use in certain cuisines.
