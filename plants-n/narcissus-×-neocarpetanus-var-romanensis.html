Narcissus × neocarpetanus var. romanensis

Narcissus × neocarpetanus var. romanensis, commonly known as the Roman daffodil, is a hybrid species within the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, trumpet-shaped flowers that typically range in color from yellow to orange. The plant produces a single stem with several leaves and blooms in early spring. It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soil.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant and showy display.
