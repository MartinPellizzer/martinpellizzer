Nepeta ballotifolia

Nepeta ballotifolia, commonly known as catmint, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, tubular blue or violet flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Nepeta ballotifolia is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and its historical use in herbal remedies.
