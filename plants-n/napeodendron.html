Napeodendron

Napeodendron, commonly known as the African violet, is a genus of flowering plants native to tropical and southern Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped foliage and showy, clustered flowers that come in a variety of colors. The leaves are typically velvety and dark green, while the stems are short and thick. Napeodendron species are often cultivated as ornamental plants due to their aesthetic appeal and ease of care.

They thrive in well-drained soil and require bright, indirect light for optimal growth.
