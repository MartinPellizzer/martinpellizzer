Nasturtium flexuosum

Nasturtium flexuosum, commonly known as the spreading nasturtium, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae and is characterized by its vibrant orange or yellow flowers and heart-shaped leaves. The plant is an annual or biennial herb that thrives in temperate climates and well-drained soil. Its stems are often sprawling or trailing, which gives it its common name.

Nasturtium flexuosum is known for its edible leaves and flowers, which have a peppery taste similar to watercress.
