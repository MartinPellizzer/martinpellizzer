Naletonia violacea

Naletonia violacea, commonly known as the violet bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of violet-blue, bell-shaped flowers. The leaves are lanceolate, with a slightly hairy surface, and the plant typically grows to a height of about 30-60 cm. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
