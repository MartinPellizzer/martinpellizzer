Nanothamnus sericeus

Nanothamnus sericeus, commonly known as silverleaf nanthamnus, is a low-growing shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It features small, narrow leaves that are densely covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is adapted to survive in dry conditions, with a deep root system that helps it access water from deeper soil layers.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
