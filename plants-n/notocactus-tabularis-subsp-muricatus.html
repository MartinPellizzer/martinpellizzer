Notocactus tabularis subsp. muricatus

Notocactus tabularis subsp.

muricatus, commonly known as the Mexican apple cactus, is a member of the Cactaceae family native to Mexico. It is a columnar cactus characterized by its smooth, ribbed stems that can grow up to several meters in height.

The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months, which are typically borne at the base of the stems. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in arid environments.
