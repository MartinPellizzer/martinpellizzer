Narcissus jonquilla var. uniflora

Narcissus jonquilla var. uniflora, commonly known as the single-flowered daffodil, is a variety of the genus Narcissus native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its single, large, funnel-shaped bloom that typically appears in early spring. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces a basal cluster of bulbs that store nutrients. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is valued for its vibrant yellow flowers and hardy nature.
