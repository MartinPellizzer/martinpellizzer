Nasturtium islandicum

Nasturtium islandicum, commonly known as the island nasturtium, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like cabbages and mustards. The plant features showy, bright orange or yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are edible and have a peppery flavor, similar to watercress.

Nasturtium islandicum is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant blooms and ease of cultivation.
