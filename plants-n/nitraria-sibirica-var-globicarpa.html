Nitraria sibirica var. globicarpa

Nitraria sibirica var. globicarpa, commonly known as the globefruit or globular nitraria, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Nitrariaceae and is characterized by its small, fleshy, globular fruits that mature to a yellowish or reddish color. The plant is adapted to harsh environments, with thick, waxy leaves that reduce water loss. Its fruits are often consumed by wildlife and have been historically used by humans for food and medicinal purposes.

The plant's resilience makes it an important component of desert ecosystems.
