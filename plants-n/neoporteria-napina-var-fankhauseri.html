Neoporteria napina var. fankhauseri

Neoporteria napina var. fankhauseri, commonly known as the Fankhauser's silverweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It thrives in harsh, cold environments and is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which reflects sunlight and reduces water loss. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and flies. It has a fibrous root system that helps it anchor in rocky or sandy soils.

This variety is notable for its adaptability to extreme climates and its role in stabilizing soil in alpine and tundra ecosystems.
