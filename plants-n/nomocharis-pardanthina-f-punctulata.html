Nomocharis pardanthina f. punctulata

Nomocharis pardanthina f. punctulata, commonly known as the spotted flame orchid, is a unique hybrid orchid native to Australia.

It is characterized by its striking red and orange flowers with distinctive black spots, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
