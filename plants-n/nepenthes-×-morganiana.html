Nepenthes × morganiana

Nepenthes × morganiana, commonly known as the hybrid pitcher plant, is a tropical vine native to the Malay Archipelago.

It is a hybrid between Nepenthes alata and Nepenthes rafflesiana, resulting in a plant with unique morphological traits. The plant produces striking, urn-shaped pitcher structures that secrete a digestive fluid to trap and digest insects. Its leaves are typically broad and glossy, with a waxy coating that aids in water retention.

Nepenthes × morganiana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
