Norantea japurensis

Norantea japurensis, commonly known as the Japurá siphonophyte, is a rare aquatic plant native to the Japurá River basin in the Amazon region.

It is characterized by its delicate, translucent filaments that resemble a siphon, which it uses for buoyancy and nutrient absorption. The plant thrives in slow-moving, nutrient-rich waters and is adapted to low-light conditions. Its unique morphology makes it a subject of interest for botanists studying aquatic plant evolution.

Due to habitat destruction, it is considered endangered and is found in very limited areas.
