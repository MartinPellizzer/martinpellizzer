Nephelium altissimum

Nephelium altissimum, commonly known as rambutan, is a tropical fruit-bearing tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive hairy, spiny exterior that covers its fuzzy, sweet flesh. The plant produces small, yellow-orange fruits that are rich in vitamin C and other nutrients. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical in shape, with a smooth upper surface and a hairy underside.

The tree thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental and edible qualities.
