Narcissus cernuus var. pulchellus

Narcissus cernuus var. pulchellus, commonly known as the drooping daffodil, is a variant of the common daffodil characterized by its gracefully nodding flowers.

It typically features narrow, grass-like leaves and one to three trumpet-shaped blooms that hang downward. The flowers are usually yellow with a white or greenish throat, and they bloom in early spring. This variety is native to southern Europe and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate, early blooming display.
