Nandina tsermonanten

Nandina tsermonanten, commonly known as the sacred bamboo, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in summer, followed by bright red berries. The plant has compound leaves that are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

However, it is important to note that some parts of the plant can be toxic if ingested.
