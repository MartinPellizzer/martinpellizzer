Neyraudia

Neyraudia, commonly known as the silver fern, is a genus of flowering plants in the grass family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its striking silver-green foliage. The leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive silvery sheen that gives the plant its common name. Neyraudia species are often used in ornamental gardening for their aesthetic appeal.

They prefer well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making them adaptable to a variety of garden settings.
