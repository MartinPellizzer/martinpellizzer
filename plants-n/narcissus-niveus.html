Narcissus niveus

Narcissus niveus, commonly known as the snow narcissus, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and parts of northern Africa, where it thrives in cool, moist environments. The plant produces white, cup-shaped flowers with a single prominent central stamen, which is a distinctive botanical feature. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming a dense rosette near the ground.

Narcissus niveus is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
