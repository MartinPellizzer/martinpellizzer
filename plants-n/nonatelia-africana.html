Nonatelia africana

Nonatelia africana, commonly known as the African wild bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pod-like fruits. The plant produces yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its roots have nitrogen-fixing capabilities, making it beneficial for soil fertility.

Nonatelia africana is often used in traditional medicine and as a forage plant for livestock.
