Nymphaea elleniae

Nymphaea elleniae, commonly known as the Ellen's water lily, is a species of aquatic plant native to the wetlands of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Nymphaeaceae family and is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that float on the water's surface. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white, with a distinctive yellow center. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments and plays a role in supporting local aquatic ecosystems.

Nymphaea elleniae is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various water conditions.
