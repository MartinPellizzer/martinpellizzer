Nyssa integrifolia var. glauca

Nyssa integrifolia var. glauca, commonly known as the blueleaf waterdog, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, bluish-gray bark and elliptical leaves that are typically entire or slightly toothed. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clustered inflorescences, followed by blue-black berries that are an important food source for wildlife. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and forested areas.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its striking foliage and adaptability to wet environments.
