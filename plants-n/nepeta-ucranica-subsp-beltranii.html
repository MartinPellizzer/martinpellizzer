Nepeta ucranica subsp. beltranii

Nepeta ucranica subsp.

beltranii, commonly known as the Ukrainian catmint, is a perennial herb native to the Carpathian Mountains. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves.

The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically gray-green and emit a mint-like scent when crushed.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often grown in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowers.
