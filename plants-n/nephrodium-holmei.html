Nephrodium holmei

Nephrodium holmei, commonly known as the Holme's nephroid, is a rare and unique fern species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, kidney-shaped fronds that are often deeply lobed, giving it a striking visual appearance. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in tropical rainforests. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, a common feature among ferns.

Due to habitat destruction, Nephrodium holmei is considered endangered, making conservation efforts crucial for its survival.
