Nazia racemosa var. aliena

Nazia racemosa var. aliena, commonly known as the alien-flowered nazia, is a unique variant of the nazia plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive racemose inflorescence, which consists of elongated clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The leaves are typically ovate with a smooth margin and a slightly pubescent underside. This variant is noted for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in both sunny and partially shaded environments.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a potential indicator species for ecological change due to its sensitivity to environmental conditions.
