Neopeltandra suberosa

Neopeltandra suberosa, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its straight, dark reddish-brown heartwood, which is highly valued for its durability and aesthetic qualities. The plant has a slender, cylindrical trunk with a smooth, often scaly bark. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical in shape, typically with a glossy green upper surface.

Neopeltandra suberosa is also known for its fragrant flowers, which are small and clustered in terminal inflorescences.
