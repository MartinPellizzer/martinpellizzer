Nepeta komarovii

Nepeta komarovii, commonly known as Komarov's catmint, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It features aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and produces spikes of blue to purple flowers. The plant is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Its essential oils contain compounds like nepetalactone, which are responsible for its distinctive scent and potential therapeutic uses.
