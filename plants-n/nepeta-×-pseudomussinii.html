Nepeta × pseudomussinii

Nepeta × pseudomussinii, commonly known as catmint, is a hybrid plant in the mint family (Lamiaceae).

It is characterized by its aromatic, fuzzy leaves and clusters of small blue or purple flowers. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Its foliage also has a mild minty scent, which is believed to repel certain pests.
