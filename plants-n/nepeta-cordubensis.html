Nepeta cordubensis

Nepeta cordubensis, commonly known as catnip, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to purple, depending on the variety. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Nepeta cordubensis is also known for its mild sedative properties and its ability to attract beneficial insects like bees and butterflies.
