Nepeta antiatlantica

Nepeta antiatlantica, commonly known as the Canary Islands catmint, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically narrow and slightly fuzzy, with a strong mint-like aroma.

Nepeta antiatlantica is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature.
