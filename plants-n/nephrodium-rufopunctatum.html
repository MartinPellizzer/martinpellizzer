Nephrodium rufopunctatum

Nephrodium rufopunctatum, commonly known as the red-spotted nephroid, is a unique fern species characterized by its intricate foliage.

Its fronds are typically bipinnate, with a delicate, lace-like appearance that sets it apart from other ferns. The plant produces sporangia that are clustered in sori, which are often covered by a protective indusium. Nephrodium rufopunctatum thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest for both horticulturists and botanists.
