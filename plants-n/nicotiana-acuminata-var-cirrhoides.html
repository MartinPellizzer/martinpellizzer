Nicotiana acuminata var. cirrhoides

Nicotiana acuminata var. cirrhoides, commonly known as the skyflower or blue flower, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, often in rocky soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
