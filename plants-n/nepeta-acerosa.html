Nepeta acerosa

Nepeta acerosa, commonly known as catnip, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue or white flowers arranged in whorls along the stems. It is known for its strong aromatic properties, primarily due to the presence of nepetalactone.

Nepeta acerosa is often used in traditional medicine and as a natural remedy for various ailments.
