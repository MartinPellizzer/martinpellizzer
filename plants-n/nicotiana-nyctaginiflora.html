Nicotiana nyctaginiflora

Nicotiana nyctaginiflora, commonly known as the nightshade morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and tobacco. The plant produces strikingly large, showy flowers that open in the morning and close at night, hence its common name. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and often trailing.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant colors and unique blooming pattern.
