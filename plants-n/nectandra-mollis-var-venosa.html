Nectandra mollis var. venosa

Nectandra mollis var. venosa, commonly known as the variegated sandarac tree, is a tropical evergreen species native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with striking white or cream-colored veins, giving it a distinctive appearance. The tree produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by woody fruits containing aromatic seeds. Its bark is rich in a resin known as sandarac, which has been historically used in various industrial applications.

This plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland tropical forests.
