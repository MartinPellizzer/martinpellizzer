Narcissus etruscus

Narcissus etruscus, commonly known as the Etruscan daffodil, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces a single, showy flower with a yellow corona and white petals, often with a faint fragrance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Its ornamental value and historical significance in Mediterranean cultures further enhance its botanical appeal.
