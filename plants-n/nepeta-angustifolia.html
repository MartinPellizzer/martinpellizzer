Nepeta angustifolia

Nepeta angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf catmint, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine, hairy foliage. The plant produces small, fragrant blue or purple flowers in whorled clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Nepeta angustifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
