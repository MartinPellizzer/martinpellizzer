Nipponocalamus yasuianus

Nipponocalamus yasuianus, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its slender, upright culms and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in panicles. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Nipponocalamus yasuianus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in traditional Japanese horticulture.
