Nasturtium pyrenaicum subsp. hispanicum

Nasturtium pyrenaicum subsp.

hispanicum, commonly known as the Spanish nasturtium, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It belongs to the family Brassicaceae and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to orange.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky soils. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a slightly peppery taste, while its seeds are small and used in traditional medicine.

The subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
