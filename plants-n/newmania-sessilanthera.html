Newmania sessilanthera

Newmania sessilanthera, commonly known as the sessile-flowered newmania, is a rare and distinctive plant species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, featuring small, clustered flowers that are often sessile, meaning they lack a stalk. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest undergrowth, where it thrives in the understory. Its leaves are usually elliptical and have a smooth texture, while its stems are slender and often creeping along the ground.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, Newmania sessilanthera is considered a conservation concern.
