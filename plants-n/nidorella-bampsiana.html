Nidorella bampsiana

Nidorella bampsiana, commonly known as the Bamps' orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to the tropical rainforests of Madagascar.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, featuring intricate, showy flowers with a distinct coloration pattern. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, anchoring itself to tree bark while deriving moisture and nutrients from the air. Its flowers bloom in clusters, often displaying a combination of white, pink, and green hues.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Nidorella bampsiana is considered a vulnerable species facing threats from habitat loss and climate change.
