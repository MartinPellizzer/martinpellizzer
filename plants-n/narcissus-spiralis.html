Narcissus spiralis

Narcissus spiralis, commonly known as the spiral daffodil, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its unique spiral arrangement of leaves, which gives it its common name. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to white, typically blooming in early spring. Native to southern Europe, it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Due to its ornamental value and rare occurrence, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
