Nasturtium cordatum

Nasturtium cordatum, commonly known as the heartleaf nasturtium, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Tropaeolaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and vibrant, orange or yellow flowers. The plant is known for its peppery taste, which comes from the presence of glucosinolates. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Nasturtium cordatum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract beneficial insects.
