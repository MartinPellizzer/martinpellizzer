Nassella ventanicola

Nassella ventanicola, commonly known as the slender needlegrass, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its fine, needle-like leaves and tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Nassella ventanicola is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops.
