Noccaea caerulescens subsp. arenaria

Noccaea caerulescens subsp.

arenaria, commonly known as the sand avens, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with hairy stems and small, greenish-white flowers.

The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in disturbed or dry habitats. It has a compact root system that helps it survive in nutrient-poor environments.

This subspecies is notable for its ability to accumulate heavy metals, making it useful in phytoremediation efforts.
