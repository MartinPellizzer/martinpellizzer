Notodanthonia eriantha

Notodanthonia eriantha, commonly known as the narrowleaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and heathlands.

Notodanthonia eriantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
