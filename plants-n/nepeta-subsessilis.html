Nepeta subsessilis

Nepeta subsessilis, commonly known as catmint, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Nepeta subsessilis is also known for its mild minty scent and is sometimes used in herbal remedies.
