Nama aretioides f. multiflora

Nama aretioides f. multiflora, commonly known as the multiflora form of the aretioides species, is a rare and unique variant of the Nama genus.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage, which is often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pale green to yellow in color, blooming during the cooler months. It is native to specific regions in southern Africa, where it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils.

Due to its limited distribution and unique appearance, it is highly valued in botanical collections and conservation efforts.
