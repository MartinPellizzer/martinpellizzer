Nectandra macbridei

Nectandra macbridei, commonly known as the MacBride's sandarac, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The tree produces a resinous sap that has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive. Its bark and leaves contain various bioactive compounds, making it of interest for pharmaceutical research.

Nectandra macbridei typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments and can reach heights of up to 20 meters.
