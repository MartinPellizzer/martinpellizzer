Nasturtium × terrestre

Nasturtium × terrestre, commonly known as the Indian gooseberry or amla, is a deciduous shrub native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its elliptical, glossy green leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round, green fruits that turn bright orange when mature. These fruits are rich in vitamin C and have been used in traditional medicine for centuries.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy and ornamental plant in gardens.
