Nepeta nepetella

Nepeta nepetella, commonly known as catmint, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are often used in herbal teas and have a minty, slightly bitter scent.

Nepeta nepetella is also known for its attraction to bees and other pollinators.
