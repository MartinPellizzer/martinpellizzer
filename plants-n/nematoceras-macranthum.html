Nematoceras macranthum

Nematoceras macranthum, commonly known as the large-flowered bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It features showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, often with a distinctive white or yellow throat. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

Nematoceras macranthum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partially shaded conditions.
