Nipponobambusa reikoana

Nipponobambusa reikoana, commonly known as the Reiko bamboo, is a species of grass belonging to the Bambusoideae subfamily.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its slender, erect culms that often grow in dense clumps. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically a vibrant green, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers. Nipponobambusa reikoana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a landscape plant.

Its growth habit and aesthetic qualities make it a popular choice for both traditional and modern horticultural settings.
