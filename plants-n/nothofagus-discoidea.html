Nothofagus discoidea

Nothofagus discoidea, commonly known as the silver beech, is a species of evergreen tree native to southern South America.

It belongs to the Nothofagus genus, which includes several species of southern beeches. The plant is characterized by its smooth, silver-gray bark and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It typically grows in cool, moist temperate forests and can reach heights of up to 30 meters.

Nothofagus discoidea plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
