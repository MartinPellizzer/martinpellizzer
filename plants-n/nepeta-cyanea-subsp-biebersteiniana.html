Nepeta cyanea subsp. biebersteiniana

Nepeta cyanea subsp.

biebersteiniana, commonly known as blue catmint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features slender, upright stems covered in dense, hairy foliage.

The plant produces clusters of striking blue to lavender flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are aromatic and often have a mint-like scent, which contributes to its popularity in gardens.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
