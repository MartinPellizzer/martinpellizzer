Narcissus × susannae var. toletanus

Narcissus × susannae var. toletanus, commonly known as the Toletanus daffodil, is a hybrid variety of daffodil characterized by its striking yellow flowers.

It features a trumpet-shaped corona that is typically longer than the perianth, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant is known for its robust growth and ability to thrive in well-drained soil with full sun exposure. Its foliage consists of long, narrow leaves that emerge from a basal rosette.

This variety is prized for its vibrant color and is often used in gardens for its ornamental value.
