Noaea mucronata subsp. humilis

Noaea mucronata subsp.

humilis, commonly known as the slender sandmat, is a low-growing perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa. It features small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has a mat-forming growth habit with hairy stems and leaves that are typically lance-shaped. It is adapted to sandy soils and can tolerate drought conditions.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological studies in dryland ecosystems.
