Nepenthes gracilis

Nepenthes gracilis, commonly known as the slender pitcher plant, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow pitchers that secrete a sticky liquid to trap insects. The plant's leaves are typically green and lance-shaped, with a waxy coating that aids in water retention. Its pitchers vary in color from green to reddish-brown, depending on environmental conditions.

Nepenthes gracilis is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and carnivorous nature.
