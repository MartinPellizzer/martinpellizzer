Nepeta pravitziana

Nepeta pravitziana, commonly known as Pravitz's catmint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically gray-green and have a minty fragrance, similar to other members of the Nepeta genus.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable addition to rock gardens or dry landscapes.
