Nitraria schoberi var. roborowskii

Nitraria schoberi var. roborowskii, commonly known as the desert raisin, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, fleshy, red berries that resemble raisins, which are edible and often used in traditional cuisines. The plant has a deep root system that allows it to thrive in harsh, dry environments. Its leaves are typically small and covered in a waxy layer to reduce water loss.

This species is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
