Neottia michuacana

Neottia michuacana, commonly known as the Michuacan lady's tresses, is a species of orchid native to Mexico.

It is a terrestrial herb that typically grows in shaded, moist forest environments. The plant features a rosette of grass-like leaves and produces a dense spike of small, greenish-white flowers. It is characterized by its lack of true roots and its ability to form symbiotic relationships with mycorrhizal fungi.

Neottia michuacana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
