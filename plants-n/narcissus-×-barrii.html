Narcissus × barrii

Narcissus × barrii, commonly known as the Barri daffodil, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Narcissus tazetta and Narcissus poeticus.

It is characterized by its vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows to a height of 20–40 cm, with narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in floral arrangements.
