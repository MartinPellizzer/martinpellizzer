Nierembergia angustifolia var. filicaulis

Nierembergia angustifolia var. filicaulis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features slender, upright stems with opposite, lanceolate leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or violet, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate foliage and vibrant floral display.
