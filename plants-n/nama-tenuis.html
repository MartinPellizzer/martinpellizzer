Nama tenuis

Nama tenuis, commonly known as the slender nama, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in grassland or shrubland habitats. Its small, yellowish flowers are arranged in a flat-topped cluster, and it produces small, oval-shaped seeds.

Nama tenuis is adapted to arid conditions and is often used in traditional medicine by local communities.
