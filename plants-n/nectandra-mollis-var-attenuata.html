Nectandra mollis var. attenuata

Nectandra mollis var. attenuata, commonly known as the slender sandarac tree, is a tropical evergreen species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its wood is valued for its aromatic properties and has been traditionally used in incense and medicinal preparations.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments within tropical forests.
