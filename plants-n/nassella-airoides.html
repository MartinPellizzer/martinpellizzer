Nassella airoides

Nassella airoides, commonly known as the purple needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to western North America.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth and distinctive purple-tinted inflorescence. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spikelet-like flowers that are often clustered in panicles. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Nassella airoides is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and stabilizing soil.
