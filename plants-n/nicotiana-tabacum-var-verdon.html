Nicotiana tabacum var. verdon

Nicotiana tabacum var. verdon, commonly known as the Verdon tobacco, is a variant of the tobacco plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and has hairy, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often blooming in late summer.

Botanically, it belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshade plants like tomatoes and peppers.
