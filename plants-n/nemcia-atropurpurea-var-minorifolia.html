Nemcia atropurpurea var. minorifolia

Nemcia atropurpurea var. minorifolia, commonly known as the purplestem false foxglove, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, erect stems that are typically reddish-purple in color, which distinguish it from other members of the genus. The plant has opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy and have a bluish-green hue. Its flowers are tubular, bell-shaped, and typically pink or purple, arranged in clusters at the top of the stems.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.
