Noccaea lutescens

Noccaea lutescens, commonly known as yellow rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many species with small, cruciform flowers. The plant is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Noccaea lutescens is an annual or biennial herb that plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitats.
