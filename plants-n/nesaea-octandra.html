Nesaea octandra

Nesaea octandra, commonly known as the eight-angled nesaea, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a basal rosette of elliptic, glaucous leaves that are often hairy and aromatic. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers in racemes during the spring and summer months. Its flowering stems are erect and branched, bearing numerous tiny, clustered blooms.

Nesaea octandra is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
