Noaea aucheri

Noaea aucheri, commonly known as the Persian weed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is a small, annual herb that typically grows in dry, rocky, or disturbed soils. The plant features slender stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

Noaea aucheri is native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments.
