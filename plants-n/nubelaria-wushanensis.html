Nubelaria wushanensis

Nubelaria wushanensis, commonly known as the Wushan cloud fern, is a rare species of fern native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, frond-like structures that resemble clouds, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, thriving in the cool, humid climates of its native habitat. Its spore-producing structures, known as sori, are located on the underside of its fronds, a typical feature of ferns.

Due to its limited distribution and unique morphology, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
