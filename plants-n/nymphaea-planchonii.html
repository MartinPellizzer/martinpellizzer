Nymphaea planchonii

Nymphaea planchonii, commonly known as the Madagascar water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to the island of Madagascar.

It features large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and yellow, often with a distinctive yellow center. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes. Its leaves are broad and floating, providing a habitat for various aquatic organisms.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in water gardens.
