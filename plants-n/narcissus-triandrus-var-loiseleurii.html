Narcissus triandrus var. loiseleurii

Narcissus triandrus var. loiseleurii, commonly known as the Loiseleurii daffodil, is a botanical variant of the common daffodil.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically a vibrant yellow with a white or cream throat. The plant is native to southern Europe, particularly the Iberian Peninsula, and thrives in well-drained soil in full sun. It produces narrow, grass-like leaves and is known for its early spring blooming habit.

This variety is valued in gardens for its ornamental beauty and hardiness.
