Nymphaea ampla f. superposita

Nymphaea ampla f. superposita, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features large, showy flowers that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter, typically in shades of white and pink. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in ponds and lakes. Its broad, floating leaves provide shade and habitat for various aquatic life.

The f. superposita form is distinguished by its larger size and more robust growth compared to the typical species.
