Nepeta thibetica

Nepeta thibetica, commonly known as Tibetan catmint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features square, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly fuzzy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported calming and antiseptic properties.

Nepeta thibetica thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens or alpine landscapes.
