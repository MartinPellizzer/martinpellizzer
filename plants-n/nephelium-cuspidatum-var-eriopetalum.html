Nephelium cuspidatum var. eriopetalum

Nephelium cuspidatum var. eriopetalum, commonly known as the red rambutan, is a tropical fruit-bearing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, reddish-purple rind and sweet, hairy flesh that contains a single large seed. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical, while its flowers are small and clustered in panicles.

The fruit is widely cultivated for its unique appearance and flavorful pulp, making it a popular commercial crop in tropical regions.
