Notocactus mueller-melchersii var. veenianus

Notocactus mueller-melchersii var. veenianus, commonly known as the Veenianus Notocactus, is a unique cactus species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stems that often develop a reddish or purple hue under intense sunlight. The plant produces striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in late spring or early summer. It is a slow-growing species that thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its distinct appearance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in succulent gardens and collections.
