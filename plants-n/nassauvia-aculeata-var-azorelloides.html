Nassauvia aculeata var. azorelloides

Nassauvia aculeata var. azorelloides, commonly known as the Azorean honeysuckle, is a shrub native to the Azores.

It features small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has slender, woody stems with small, sharp spines, contributing to its defensive characteristics. Its leaves are typically opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs.

This variety is adapted to the humid, mild climate of the Azores and thrives in well-drained soils.
