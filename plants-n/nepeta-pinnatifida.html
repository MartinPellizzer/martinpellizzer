Nepeta pinnatifida

Nepeta pinnatifida, commonly known as catmint, is a flowering plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, fragrant blue or purple flowers. The plant produces whorled leaves that are hairy and often have a mint-like scent. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and as a companion plant for other herbs.

Nepeta pinnatifida is also known for its attractiveness to pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
