Nothofagus resinosa

Nothofagus resinosa, commonly known as the resinous nothofagus, is a species of evergreen tree native to southern South America.

It belongs to the Nothofagus genus, which is part of the Nothofagaceae family, and is characterized by its distinctive morphology and ecological significance. The plant produces a sticky resin, which is a notable feature contributing to its common name. It typically grows in temperate rainforests and is known for its ability to thrive in moist, cool climates.

Nothofagus resinosa plays an important role in its native ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various species.
