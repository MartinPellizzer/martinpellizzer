Nepeta barbata

Nepeta barbata, commonly known as catmint, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite leaves that are often gray-green in color. The plant produces small, fragrant blue or purple flowers in whorls along the stem, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, known for its aromatic properties and ability to attract pollinators.

Nepeta barbata is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and as a source of nectar for bees and butterflies.
