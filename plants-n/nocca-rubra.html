Nocca rubra

Nocca rubra, commonly known as the red stem vine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and distinctive red stems that become more vibrant with age. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in clusters. Nocca rubra is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.

Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a rough texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
