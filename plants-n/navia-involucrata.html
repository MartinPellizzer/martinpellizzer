Navia involucrata

Navia involucrata, commonly known as the velvetleaf or woolly thistle, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its grayish-green, hairy leaves that help reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to purple, attracting various pollinators. Navia involucrata thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species in temperate climates.

Its unique appearance and adaptability have made it both a subject of botanical study and a potential invasive species in some regions.
