Narcissus obvallaris var. toscanus

Narcissus obvallaris var. toscanus, commonly known as the Tuscan daffodil, is a variety of the genus Narcissus native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically bright yellow with a white or greenish throat. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, thriving in temperate climates.

This variety is noted for its early spring blooming habit and resilience to mild winters.
