Najas madagascariensis

Najas madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar waterweed, is a species of aquatic plant native to Madagascar.

It is a member of the Najadaceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, ribbon-like leaves that float on the water surface. The plant typically grows in shallow, freshwater habitats such as ponds and slow-moving streams. It plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing shelter for small aquatic organisms.

Its rapid growth rate makes it both a valuable resource and a potential invasive species in non-native environments.
