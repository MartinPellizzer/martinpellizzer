Nepeta mallophora f. tenella

Nepeta mallophora f. tenella, commonly known as the dwarf catmint, is a compact, low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its cultivar form, 'tenella,' is distinguished by its shorter stature and dense growth habit compared to the species.
