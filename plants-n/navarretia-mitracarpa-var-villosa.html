Navarretia mitracarpa var. villosa

Navarretia mitracarpa var. villosa, commonly known as the hairy navarretia, is a flowering plant native to California.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, clustered flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are hairy, giving it the common name, and it produces small, white or pale purple flowers.

This variety is often found in the coastal and inland regions of southern California.
