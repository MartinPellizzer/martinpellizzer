Neltuma juliflora var. juliflora

Neltuma juliflora var. juliflora, commonly known as the desert morning glory, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its trumpet-shaped, violet to blue-purple flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant has slender, trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to full sun, making it a hardy species in dry climates.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance in its native habitat.
