Nepeta meda

Nepeta meda, commonly known as catmint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their calming properties.

Nepeta meda is also popular in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soil.
