Narcissus × trilobus

Narcissus × trilobus, commonly known as the tazetta daffodil, is a hybrid species of the genus Narcissus.

It is characterized by its clusters of fragrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and adaptability to various climates.
