Nigella arvensis var. negevensis

Nigella arvensis var. negevensis, commonly known as the Negev fennel, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, branched stems that often grow from 30 to 60 centimeters tall. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in umbels, which are typically followed by distinctive, spherical seed pods. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
