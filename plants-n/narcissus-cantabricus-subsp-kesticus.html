Narcissus cantabricus subsp. kesticus

Narcissus cantabricus subsp.

kesticus, commonly known as the Cantabrian daffodil, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and a single, trumpet-shaped flower that typically blooms in spring.

The flower's tepals are usually pale yellow to white, with a prominent central stripe. This subspecies is adapted to rocky and coastal environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.
