Nepeta multibracteata var. ballii

Nepeta multibracteata var. ballii, commonly known as the Ball's catmint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features aromatic, blue to purple flowers that bloom in whorls along the upper stems. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a fuzzy texture and a strong minty scent. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
