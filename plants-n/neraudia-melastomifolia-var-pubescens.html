Neraudia melastomifolia var. pubescens

Neraudia melastomifolia var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy melastome, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves that are covered in fine, hairy pubescence, giving it its varietal name. Its flowers are typically bisexual, with five petals and numerous stamens, and are often arranged in clusters.

This species thrives in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
