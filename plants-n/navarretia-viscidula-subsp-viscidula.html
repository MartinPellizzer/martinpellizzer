Navarretia viscidula subsp. viscidula

Navarretia viscidula subsp.

viscidula, commonly known as the sticky navarretia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and features small, white to pale pink flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its glandular, sticky leaves and stems, which give it its common name. It thrives in rocky soils and is often found in coastal and inland areas.

This plant is part of the Campanulaceae family and is adapted to Mediterranean climates with dry summers and wet winters.
