Neoregelia bragarum

Neoregelia bragarum, commonly known as the Crimson Star bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the rainforests of Brazil.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy inflorescence that resembles a star. The plant features rosettes of narrow, grass-like leaves that collect water, creating a small pool that supports various microorganisms. Its flowering period typically occurs during the warmer months, producing blooms in shades of red, orange, and yellow.

Neoregelia bragarum is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its adaptability to bright, indirect light and moderate humidity.
