Narcissus × angustifolius

Narcissus × angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved daffodil, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, strap-like leaves and bright yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and thrives in well-drained soil in full sun. It is native to southern Europe and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

The flowers bloom in early spring, adding a vibrant touch to landscapes with their cheerful color.
