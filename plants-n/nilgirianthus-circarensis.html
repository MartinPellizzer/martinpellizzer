Nilgirianthus circarensis

Nilgirianthus circarensis, commonly known as the Nilgiri bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky slopes. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences.

Nilgirianthus circarensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in botanical gardens.
