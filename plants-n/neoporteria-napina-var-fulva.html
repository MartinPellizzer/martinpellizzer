Neoporteria napina var. fulva

Neoporteria napina var. fulva, commonly known as the yellow marsh orchid, is a herbaceous perennial native to wetlands and marshes in North America.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of showy, yellow-orange flowers resembling orchids. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are usually dark green and slightly hairy. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in swamps, bogs, and along streams.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is an important species for ecological studies and conservation efforts.
