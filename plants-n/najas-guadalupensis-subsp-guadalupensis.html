Najas guadalupensis subsp. guadalupensis

Najas guadalupensis subsp.

guadalupensis, commonly known as the Guadalupian watermilfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to the Americas. It belongs to the family Najadaceae and is characterized by its narrow, ribbon-like leaves that are usually arranged in whorls.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically borne in clusters near the water surface. It thrives in freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving streams.

Its rapid growth and dense foliage can significantly impact aquatic ecosystems by altering water clarity and habitat structure.
