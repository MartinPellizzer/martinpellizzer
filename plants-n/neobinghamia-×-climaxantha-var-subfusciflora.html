Neobinghamia × climaxantha var. subfusciflora

Neobinghamia × climaxantha var. subfusciflora, commonly known as the coastal lantana, is a hybrid shrub native to the coastal regions of southern California.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for coastal and inland garden environments. Its adaptability to salt spray and wind makes it a popular choice for erosion control and ornamental landscaping.

The species is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
