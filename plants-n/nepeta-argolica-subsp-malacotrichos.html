Nepeta argolica subsp. malacotrichos

Nepeta argolica subsp.

malacotrichos, commonly known as the Malacotrichous catmint, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features slender, upright stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a serrated margin.

The plant produces clusters of small, pale blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
