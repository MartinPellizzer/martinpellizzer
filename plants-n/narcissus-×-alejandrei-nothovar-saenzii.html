Narcissus × alejandrei nothovar. saenzii

Narcissus × alejandrei nothovar. saenzii, commonly known as the Saenzii Daffodil, is a hybrid ornamental plant characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is prized for its vibrant yellow to orange blooms that appear in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 20–30 cm, with narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden borders and containers.

This cultivar is noted for its resilience and ability to naturalize, contributing to its widespread cultivation in temperate regions.
