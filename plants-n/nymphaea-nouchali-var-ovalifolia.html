Nymphaea nouchali var. ovalifolia

Nymphaea nouchali var. ovalifolia, commonly known as the Indian lotus, is a aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant has broad, heart-shaped leaves that float on the water surface, while its roots anchor in the mud at the bottom of ponds or lakes. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and has cultural significance in many Asian countries.

The flowers typically bloom in summer and are known for their fragrance.
