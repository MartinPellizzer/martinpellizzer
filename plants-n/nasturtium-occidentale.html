Nasturtium occidentale

Nasturtium occidentale, commonly known as the common nasturtium, is an annual flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae and is characterized by its bright, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant produces edible leaves and seeds, which are often used in culinary applications. Its growth habit includes trailing stems that can climb or sprawl along the ground.

Nasturtium occidentale is also noted for its ability to attract beneficial insects like ladybugs and bees.
