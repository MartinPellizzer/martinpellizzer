Narcissus tazetta subsp. gussonei

Narcissus tazetta subsp.

gussonei, commonly known as the Sicilian daffodil, is a subspecies native to southern Italy. It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, clustered flowers with bright yellow petals.

The flowers typically bloom in early spring, adding a vibrant touch to gardens and natural landscapes. This plant prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ornamental addition to horticultural collections.
