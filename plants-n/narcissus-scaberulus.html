Narcissus scaberulus

Narcissus scaberulus, commonly known as the rough-skinned daffodil, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa, typically found in rocky or sandy soils. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, yellow flowers with a distinctive, often recurved, tepal structure. It blooms in early spring and is characterized by its rough, scaly stem.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
