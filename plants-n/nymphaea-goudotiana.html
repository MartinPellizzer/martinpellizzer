Nymphaea goudotiana

Nymphaea goudotiana, commonly known as the Goudot's water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can be pink, white, or yellow, depending on the variety. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes. Its broad, heart-shaped leaves float on the water's surface, while its roots anchor in the substrate.

Nymphaea goudotiana plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing habitat for aquatic life and contributing to water quality through oxygenation.
