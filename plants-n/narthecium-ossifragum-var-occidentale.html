Narthecium ossifragum var. occidentale

Narthecium ossifragum var. occidentale, commonly known as the western wintergreen, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors. It is characterized by its ability to produce a distinctive, peppery scent when the leaves are crushed.

The species is also known for its medicinal properties, historically used by indigenous peoples for its therapeutic benefits.
