Najas graminea var. longidentata

Najas graminea var. longidentata, commonly known as the slender water milfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments.

It belongs to the family Najadaceae and is characterized by its narrow, ribbon-like leaves that are often arranged in whorls. The plant produces small, green flowers that are typically found in the water, contributing to its role in aquatic ecosystems. It thrives in slow-moving or still freshwater habitats, such as ponds and lakes.

Its presence is often an indicator of good water quality due to its sensitivity to pollution.
