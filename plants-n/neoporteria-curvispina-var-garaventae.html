Neoporteria curvispina var. garaventae

Neoporteria curvispina var. garaventae, commonly known as the Garaventa neoporteria, is a rare and striking plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage with thick, leathery leaves that have a distinctive waxy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers during the spring and early summer. Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is considered vulnerable and requires protection.
