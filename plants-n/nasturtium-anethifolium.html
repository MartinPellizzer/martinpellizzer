Nasturtium anethifolium

Nasturtium anethifolium, commonly known as the anise-leaf nasturtium, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, anise-scented leaves and vibrant orange or yellow flowers. The plant is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage. Its foliage and flowers are edible, often used in salads and as a garnish.

Nasturtium anethifolium thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking both ornamental and culinary value.
