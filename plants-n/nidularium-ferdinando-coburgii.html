Nidularium ferdinando-coburgii

Nidularium ferdinando-coburgii, commonly known as the Coburg's nest fern, is a rare and striking member of the Davalliaceae family.

It is characterized by its unique, nest-like rosette of fiddlehead-like fronds that tightly enclose the plant's central stem. The plant produces strikingly large, elongated spore capsules that protrude from the center of the rosette, often with a distinctive red or orange coloration. Native to the tropical regions of South America, it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is highly sought after by botanists and horticulturists alike.
