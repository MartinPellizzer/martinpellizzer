Nothaphoebe reticulata

Nothaphoebe reticulata, commonly known as the net-veined beech, is a small evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and smooth, aromatic bark. The leaves are elliptical with prominent net-like veins, giving the plant its common name. It produces small, fragrant flowers followed by dark blue berries that are attractive to birds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and aesthetic qualities.
