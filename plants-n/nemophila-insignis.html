Nemophila insignis

Nemophila insignis, commonly known as the Sierra violet, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky streambanks. It has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces seed pods that split open to release tiny seeds.

Nemophila insignis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is appreciated in native plant gardens.
