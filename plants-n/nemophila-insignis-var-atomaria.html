Nemophila insignis var. atomaria

Nemophila insignis var. atomaria, commonly known as the dwarf blue-eyed Mary, is a low-growing annual plant native to California.

It produces small, delicate blue or violet flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has hairy, slender stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and its ability to naturalize in suitable climates.
