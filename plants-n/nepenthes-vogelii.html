Nepenthes vogelii

Nepenthes vogelii, commonly known as the Vogel's pitcher plant, is a tropical climbing plant native to the rainforests of Borneo.

It is characterized by its unique pitcher-shaped traps, which are modified leaves designed to capture and digest insects. The plant produces nectar at the rim of its pitcher to lure prey, while its inner surface is coated with a slippery substance that prevents escape. Nepenthes vogelii is notable for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and its preference for high humidity.

Its striking red or orange coloration makes it a visually distinctive species within the Nepenthaceae family.
