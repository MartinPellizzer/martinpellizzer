Neomammillaria polycentra var. isabellina

Neomammillaria polycentra var. isabellina, commonly known as the Isabellina cactus, is a compact, low-growing species native to Mexico.

It features a cylindrical stem with a ribbed structure, typically reaching heights of up to 15 centimeters. The plant produces white to pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are modified into spines, and it has a slow growth rate, making it suitable for small containers.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it an ideal choice for arid environments.
