Nerium piscidium

Nerium piscidium, commonly known as the sacred lotus of India, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a red or purple calyx. Its seeds are enclosed in a fleshy, orange-red fruit that splits open when mature.

Nerium piscidium is valued for its ornamental beauty and has cultural significance in traditional medicine and religious practices.
