Nicotiana karijini

Nicotiana karijini, commonly known as the Karijini tobacco plant, is a species native to Australia.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its hairy stems and lobed leaves. The plant produces striking bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
