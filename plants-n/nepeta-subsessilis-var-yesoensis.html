Nepeta subsessilis var. yesoensis

Nepeta subsessilis var. yesoensis, commonly known as the Yeso catmint, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It features slender stems and opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, pale blue to lavender flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
