Nicotiana exigua

Nicotiana exigua, commonly known as the small tobacco plant, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink to purple in color, arranged in clusters at the ends of the branches. It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which also includes tomatoes, potatoes, and peppers.

Nicotiana exigua is notable for its role in the production of nicotine and its use in scientific research.
