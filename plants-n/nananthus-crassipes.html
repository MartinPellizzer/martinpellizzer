Nananthus crassipes

Nananthus crassipes, commonly known as the thick-stemmed nananthus, is a member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is characterized by its robust, woody stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of showy, two-lipped flowers in shades of purple and pink. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its vibrant blooms.
