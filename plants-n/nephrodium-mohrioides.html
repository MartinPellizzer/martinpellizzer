Nephrodium mohrioides

Nephrodium mohrioides, commonly known as the Mohri's nephroid, is a rare and unique fern species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fiddlehead-like fronds that unfurl into intricate, lace-like patterns. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical rainforests. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are clustered on the undersides of its leaves, a common feature among ferns.

Due to habitat loss, Nephrodium mohrioides is considered endangered, highlighting the need for conservation efforts to protect this botanical rarity.
