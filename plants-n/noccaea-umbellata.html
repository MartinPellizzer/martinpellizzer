Noccaea umbellata

Noccaea umbellata, commonly known as the common rockcress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils, often found in alpine or arctic regions. The plant features a basal rosette of leaves and produces small, white or yellowish flowers arranged in an umbel. Its leaves are usually pinnately divided, and the plant is known for its ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.

Noccaea umbellata is also characterized by its small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.
