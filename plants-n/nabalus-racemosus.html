Nabalus racemosus

Nabalus racemosus, commonly known as the racemose nabalus, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Polygonaceae.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
