Nymphaea odorata var. eburnea

Nymphaea odorata var. eburnea, commonly known as the white lotus, is a water lily native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in the early morning and close by midday. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes. Its leaves are broad and floating, providing a habitat for various aquatic organisms.

The white lotus is also valued for its ornamental beauty and has cultural significance in many Asian traditions.
