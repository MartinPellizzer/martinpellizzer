Narcissus × richardianus

Narcissus × richardianus, commonly known as the Richardian daffodil, is a hybrid species within the genus Narcissus.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers with a prominent yellow corona and petal-like tepals. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and is native to the Iberian Peninsula. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its blooming period occurs in early spring, adding vibrant color to landscapes during the transition from winter to summer.
