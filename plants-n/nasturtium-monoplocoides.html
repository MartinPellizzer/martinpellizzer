Nasturtium monoplocoides

Nasturtium monoplocoides, commonly known as the mountain nasturtium, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes in South America.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants such as cabbages and mustards. The plant is characterized by its vibrant orange or red flowers and heart-shaped leaves, which are often used in traditional medicine. Its botanical features include a short, stout stem and small, rounded leaves with a slightly hairy texture.

Nasturtium monoplocoides is also noted for its peppery taste, similar to that of watercress, making it a unique addition to herbal remedies and culinary applications.
