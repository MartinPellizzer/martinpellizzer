Nepeta × tmolea

Nepeta × tmolea, commonly known as catnip, is a hybrid plant belonging to the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are often gray-green in color. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or violet, arranged in whorls along the stem. Nepeta × tmolea is native to Europe and Asia but has been widely naturalized in North America.

It is well-known for its aromatic properties and its ability to attract cats due to the presence of nepetalactone.
