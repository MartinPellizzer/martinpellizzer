Nymphaea × sundvikii

Nymphaea × sundvikii, commonly known as the hybrid water lily, is a captivating aquatic plant native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Nymphaea alba and Nymphaea tetragona, showcasing a blend of characteristics from both parent species. The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves that float on the water's surface, supported by long, slender stems. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, with a subtle fragrance, and bloom during the summer months.

Nymphaea × sundvikii thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, making it a popular choice for ornamental ponds and water gardens.
