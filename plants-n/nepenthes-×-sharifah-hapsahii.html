Nepenthes × sharifah-hapsahii

Nepenthes × sharifah-hapsahii, commonly known as the Sharifah Hapsahii Pitcher Plant, is a hybrid carnivorous plant resulting from crosses between Nepenthes rajah and Nepenthes lowii.

It is native to Borneo and is characterized by its large, striking yellowish-red pitcher structures that secrete digestive enzymes to trap and digest insects. The plant thrives in high humidity and bright, indirect light, requiring consistent moisture in its growing medium. Its pitchers are often adorned with intricate patterns and can grow up to 30 cm in length.

This species is highly sought after by carnivorous plant enthusiasts for its unique appearance and ease of cultivation under proper conditions.
