Nectandra racemifera

Nectandra racemifera, commonly known as the fragrant wood or sweet wood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in racemes. Its bark and leaves contain essential oils, making it valuable in traditional medicine and perfumery.

The tree thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.
