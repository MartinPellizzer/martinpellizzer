Nepeta × boissieri var. crenata

Nepeta × boissieri var. crenata, commonly known as catmint, is a hybrid perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Mediterranean.

It features aromatic, fuzzy leaves and produces spikes of blue or lavender-colored flowers. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often used in gardens for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy and low-maintenance choice for gardeners.

Its name derives from the genus Nepeta, which includes many cat-attracting plants.
