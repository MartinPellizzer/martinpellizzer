Nomaphila petiolata

Nomaphila petiolata, commonly known as the slender leaf orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, linear leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as under forest canopies. Its roots are adapted to absorb nutrients from the surrounding soil, and it often forms symbiotic relationships with mycorrhizal fungi.

The flowers are usually pale green or white, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insect species.
