Navarretia viscidula subsp. purpurea

Navarretia viscidula subsp.

purpurea, commonly known as the purple sticky navarretia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a member of the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit.

The plant produces small, white or pale purple flowers that bloom in late spring. Its leaves are typically covered in a sticky substance, which is a distinguishing feature.
