Nectandra longifolia

Nectandra longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved sandarac, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark. The plant is known for its fragrant resin, which has been historically used in incense and traditional medicine. Its leaves are elongated and glossy, while its flowers are small and clustered, typically appearing in axillary inflorescences.

The tree thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.
