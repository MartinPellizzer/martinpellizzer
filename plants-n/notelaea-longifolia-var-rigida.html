Notelaea longifolia var. rigida

Notelaea longifolia var. rigida, commonly known as the rigid notelaea, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, followed by fleshy, red berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
