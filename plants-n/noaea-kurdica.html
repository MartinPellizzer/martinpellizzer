Noaea kurdica

Noaea kurdica, commonly known as Kurdish glassworts, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, greenish flowers. The plant typically grows in saline or arid environments, showcasing its adaptation to harsh conditions. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Noaea kurdica is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil in degraded areas.
