Neottia gemmipara

Neottia gemmipara, commonly known as the gemmipara orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the temperate regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-brown flowers that are typically arranged in a loose raceme. Neottia gemmipara is known for its ability to reproduce through vegetative propagation, often forming clonal colonies.

It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
