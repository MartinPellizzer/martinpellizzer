Nasturtium thracicum

Nasturtium thracicum, commonly known as the Thrace watercress, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan region.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae and is characterized by its upright, branched stems and alternate leaves that are usually lobed. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are typically arranged in clusters, and it is known for its peppery, edible leaves. Nasturtium thracicum thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near streams or in wet meadows.

It has been historically used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
