Nepenthes hirsuta var. glabrata

Nepenthes hirsuta var. glabrata, commonly known as the hairy pitcher plant variant, is a carnivorous plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow pitchers that are often covered in fine, hairy structures, which give it its common name. The plant produces a sweet-smelling nectar to lure insects into its modified leaves, where they are digested by digestive enzymes. Its habitat typically includes humid, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky outcrops.

This variant is prized by plant enthusiasts for its unique morphology and intriguing carnivorous behavior.
