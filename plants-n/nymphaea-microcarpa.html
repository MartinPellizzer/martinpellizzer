Nymphaea microcarpa

Nymphaea microcarpa, commonly known as the dwarf water lily, is a aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Nymphaeaceae and is characterized by its floating leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in still or slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and lakes. Its leaves are rounded with a waxy surface, and its flowers are usually pink or white, opening in the early morning.

Nymphaea microcarpa plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
