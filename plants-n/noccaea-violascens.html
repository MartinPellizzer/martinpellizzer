Noccaea violascens

Noccaea violascens, commonly known as the violet saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, violet-blue flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Noccaea violascens is a hardy perennial that is well adapted to cold climates and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
