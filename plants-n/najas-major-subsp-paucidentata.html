Najas major subsp. paucidentata

Najas major subsp.

paucidentata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved watermilfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Najadaceae and is characterized by its slender, ribbon-like leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or brown, which develop into tiny, berry-like fruits. It thrives in still or slow-moving waters and plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.

Due to its rapid growth, it can become invasive in some regions, impacting water quality and native plant communities.
