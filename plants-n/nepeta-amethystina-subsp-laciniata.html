Nepeta amethystina subsp. laciniata

Nepeta amethystina subsp.

laciniata, commonly known as the purple mint or lamb's tongue catmint, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe. It features aromatic, gray-green foliage with a fuzzy texture, and its flowers are typically blue to purple, arranged in whorled clusters.

The plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders. Its cultivar name, *laciniata*, refers to the deeply lobed leaves, which add a distinctive visual appeal.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
