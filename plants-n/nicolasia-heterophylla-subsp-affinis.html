Nicolasia heterophylla subsp. affinis

Nicolasia heterophylla subsp.

affinis, commonly known as the narrow-leafed nicolasia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and opposite, lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The flowers are small, clustered in terminal panicles, and typically display a pale yellow to greenish hue. This subspecies is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

It is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique morphological features and ecological adaptations.
