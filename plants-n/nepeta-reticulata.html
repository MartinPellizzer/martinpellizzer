Nepeta reticulata

Nepeta reticulata, commonly known as catmint, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, fuzzy leaves and spikes of blue or purple flowers. The plant belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is known for its strong menthol-like scent. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Nepeta reticulata is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
