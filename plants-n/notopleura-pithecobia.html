Notopleura pithecobia

Notopleura pithecobia, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to the wetlands of Southeast Asia.

It features broad, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 3 meters in diameter, providing a floating platform for various organisms. The plant has a complex root system that anchors it in the mud at the bottom of water bodies. Its flowers are large and showy, with white petals and yellow stamens, typically blooming at night.

Notopleura pithecobia plays an important role in its ecosystem by offering habitat and food for aquatic life.
