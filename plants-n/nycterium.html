Nycterium

Nycterium, commonly known as the nightshade, is a genus of flowering plants in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

These plants are characterized by their showy flowers, often in shades of blue, purple, or white, and their typically toxic berries. Many species within the genus contain alkaloids, which can be harmful or even poisonous to humans and animals. They are native to various regions across the Americas, Africa, and Asia.

Nycterium species are often cultivated for their ornamental value and have a long history of use in traditional medicine.
