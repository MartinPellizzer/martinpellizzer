Najas punctata

Najas punctata, commonly known as the punctate watermilfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater habitats in North America.

It belongs to the family Najadaceae and is characterized by its narrow, ribbon-like leaves that are often finely toothed. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically found in clusters near the water surface. Najas punctata plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

It is often found in ponds, lakes, and slow-moving streams.
