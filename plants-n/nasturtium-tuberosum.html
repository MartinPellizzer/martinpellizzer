Nasturtium tuberosum

Nasturtium tuberosum, commonly known as the Indian cress, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its bright orange or yellow flowers and edible leaves and seeds. The plant produces small, rounded tubers beneath the soil, which are often used in traditional medicine. Its rapid growth and ability to thrive in various soil conditions make it a versatile plant for both ornamental and culinary purposes.

Nasturtium tuberosum is also known for its peppery flavor, similar to that of watercress.
