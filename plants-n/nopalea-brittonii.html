Nopalea brittonii

Nopalea brittonii, commonly known as the Britton's prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces edible fruits, known as tunas, which are often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications. Its succulent structure allows it to store water, making it well adapted to arid environments.

Nopalea brittonii plays an important ecological role by providing food and shelter for various desert wildlife.
