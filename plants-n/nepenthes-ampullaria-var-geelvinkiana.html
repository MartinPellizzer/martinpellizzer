Nepenthes ampullaria var. geelvinkiana

Nepenthes ampullaria var. geelvinkiana, commonly known as the Geelvink's pitcher plant, is a tropical vine native to the islands of Indonesia.

It is characterized by its large, elongated pitcher-shaped traps that can hold up to 2 liters of liquid. The plant produces bright red or orange flowers, which are often adorned with intricate patterns. Its leaves are typically broad and elliptical, adapted for climbing and photosynthesis.

This variant is notable for its unique morphology and ecological adaptations, making it a subject of interest in botanical studies.
