Neomammillaria herrerae

Neomammillaria herrerae, commonly known as the Herrera's pincushion cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and dense clusters of white to pale yellow spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Neomammillaria herrerae is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its hardy nature and ornamental appearance.
