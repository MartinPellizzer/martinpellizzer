Nasa dillonii

Nasa dillonii, commonly known as the Dillon's night-blooming cereus, is a species of cactus native to the deserts of southern Arizona and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and its striking white flowers that open at night. The plant is adapted to arid environments, with a deep root system and thick, fleshy stems for water storage. Its flowers emit a sweet fragrance, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

Nasa dillonii is a slow-growing plant that thrives in full sun and well-draining soil.
