Neolacis

Neolacis, commonly known as the lacis orchid, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is characterized by its striking, often colorful flowers that feature a distinctive lip or labellum. The plants are typically found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in humid, shaded environments. Neolacis species usually have slender stems and elongated leaves, adapted for efficient water absorption.

Their botanical significance lies in their unique floral structures and role in local ecosystems.
