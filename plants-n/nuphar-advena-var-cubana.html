Nuphar advena var. cubana

Nuphar advena var. cubana, commonly known as the Cuban water lily, is a aquatic plant native to the freshwater habitats of Cuba.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves that are typically a bright green color with a waxy surface. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers that bloom in the early morning and close by midday. Its roots are anchored in the mud at the bottom of ponds and lakes.

The Cuban water lily plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
