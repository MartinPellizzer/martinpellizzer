Neomammillaria vetula

Neomammillaria vetula, commonly known as the white-tipped hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are covered in white, woolly spines, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are typically borne at the tips of the stems. Neomammillaria vetula is a slow-growing cactus that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Its unique morphology and adaptability make it a popular choice for desert gardens and cactus collections.
