Narcissus albimarginatus

Narcissus albimarginatus, commonly known as the white-edged daffodil, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to the Iberian Peninsula and features a single, showy, yellow flower with a white-edged corona. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces a bulbous root system typical of the genus. It blooms in early spring and is valued for its ornamental beauty.

The flowers are pollinated primarily by insects, and the plant is relatively hardy in temperate climates.
