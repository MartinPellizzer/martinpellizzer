Nicotiana anisoloba

Nicotiana anisoloba, commonly known as the wavy leaf tobacco, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive wavy-edged leaves and clusters of fragrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant belongs to the Solanaceae family, which also includes tomatoes, potatoes, and peppers. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking appearance and pleasant fragrance.

Nicotiana anisoloba is also noted for its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
