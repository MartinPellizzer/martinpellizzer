Nemacladus breviflorus

Nemacladus breviflorus, commonly known as the short-flowered nemacladus, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and along stream banks. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are often elliptical in shape and have a smooth texture. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense inflorescences, and are typically greenish-white in color.

Nemacladus breviflorus is known for its low-growing habit and is often found in association with other wetland plants.
