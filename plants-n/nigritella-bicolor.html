Nigritella bicolor

Nigritella bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, contrasting colors on its petals and sepals. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are small but highly fragrant, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Nigritella bicolor is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
