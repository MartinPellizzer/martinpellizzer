Nothocestrum breviflorum

Nothocestrum breviflorum, commonly known as the short-flowered false pepper, is a species native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and elliptical, glossy leaves. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, and produces small, yellowish fruits that are edible.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and research settings.
