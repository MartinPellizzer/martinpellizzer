Nierembergia hippomanica var. violacea

Nierembergia hippomanica var. violacea, commonly known as the violet-flowered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from August to September. The plant has slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period and attractive foliage.
