Narcissus jonquilla subsp. henriquesii

Narcissus jonquilla subsp.

henriquesii, commonly known as the Henriques' daffodil, is a rare and lesser-known subspecies of the daffodil genus. It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves and a single, delicate yellow flower with a narrow, trumpet-shaped corona.

Native to the Iberian Peninsula, this plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and prefers sunny to partially shaded environments. Its flowers typically bloom in early spring, adding a subtle yet elegant touch to its natural habitat.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
