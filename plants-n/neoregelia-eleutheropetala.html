Neoregelia eleutheropetala

Neoregelia eleutheropetala, commonly known as the pink star bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the rainforests of Brazil.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup to collect rainwater, which serves as a microhabitat for small aquatic organisms. The plant produces vibrant, showy inflorescences with pink bracts that resemble stars, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires high humidity and well-draining soil.

Neoregelia eleutheropetala is a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
