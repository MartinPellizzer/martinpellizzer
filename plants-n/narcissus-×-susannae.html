Narcissus × susannae

Narcissus × susannae, commonly known as the Dutch narcissus, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Narcissus tazetta and Narcissus cyclamineus.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and early blooming period, typically in late winter to early spring. The flowers are usually double, with a white or cream-colored perianth and a yellow or orange center, creating a striking visual display. The plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure.

It is widely cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.
