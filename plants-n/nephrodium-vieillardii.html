Nephrodium vieillardii

Nephrodium vieillardii, commonly known as the African shield fern, is a unique fern species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, shield-shaped fronds that are typically dark green and leathery in texture. The plant produces sporangia on the underside of its fronds, which are arranged in clusters known as sori. Nephrodium vieillardii thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest for both horticulturists and botanists.
