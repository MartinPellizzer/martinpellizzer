Neorosea toupetou

Neorosea toupetou, commonly known as the pink-flowered orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-purple petals and a unique floral structure that resembles a toupet, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the surrounding substrate.

Neorosea toupetou is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
