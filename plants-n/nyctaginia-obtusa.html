Nyctaginia obtusa

Nyctaginia obtusa, commonly known as the broad-leaf nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom at night. The plant belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades such as tomatoes and peppers. Its flowers are typically white or pale blue, with five petals and a prominent central style.

Nyctaginia obtusa is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in moist, shaded environments.
