Nabalus tatarinowii subsp. macranthus

Nabalus tatarinowii subsp.

macranthus, commonly known as the Siberian bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the cold regions of Siberia and northern China. It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and hairy, while its stems are often reddish in color.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in cooler climates.
