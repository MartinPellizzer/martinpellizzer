Noterophila genliseoides

Noterophila genliseoides, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a hairy texture.

Noterophila genliseoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
