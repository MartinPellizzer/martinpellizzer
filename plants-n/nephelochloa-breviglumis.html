Nephelochloa breviglumis

Nephelochloa breviglumis, commonly known as the short-stemmed cloudgrass, is a member of the Poaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance.

It is a perennial plant that typically grows in moist, temperate environments, often found in meadows and along stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue, and its inflorescence consists of small, clustered spikelets. It is known for its ability to thrive in shaded conditions, making it a common sight in woodland areas.
