Navicularia syriaca subsp. taurica

Navicularia syriaca subsp.

taurica, commonly known as the Tauric navicular, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and parts of Turkey. It is a perennial herb with slender, ascending stems and small, lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The flowers are typically blue or violet, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate to subtropical climates.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in rock gardens.
