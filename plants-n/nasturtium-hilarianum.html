Nasturtium hilarianum

Nasturtium hilarianum, commonly known as the Indian bean flower, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces edible pods that resemble small beans, which can be used in cooking. Its leaves are also edible and have a peppery flavor similar to watercress.

Nasturtium hilarianum is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and beneficial role in attracting pollinators.
