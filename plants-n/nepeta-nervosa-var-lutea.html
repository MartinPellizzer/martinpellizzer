Nepeta nervosa var. lutea

Nepeta nervosa var. lutea, commonly known as yellow catmint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, upright stems covered in grayish-blue foliage and produces clusters of small, fragrant, yellowish flowers. The plant is characterized by its finely toothed leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological biodiversity.
