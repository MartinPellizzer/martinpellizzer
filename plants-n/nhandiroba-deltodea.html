Nhandiroba deltodea

Nhandiroba deltodea, commonly known as the Brazilian star orchid, is a rare and striking orchid native to the rainforests of Brazil.

It is characterized by its vibrant, star-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in the dense canopy of the tropical forest. Its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, Nhandiroba deltodea is considered endangered, making conservation efforts crucial for its survival.
