Nemophila pilosa

Nemophila pilosa, commonly known as the softleaf blue-eyed Mary, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Boraginaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant produces showy, five-petaled blue or purple flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are typically hairy and have a rounded or lance-shaped form.
