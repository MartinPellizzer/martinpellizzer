Narcissus bellirius

Narcissus bellirius, commonly known as the lesser narcissus or the yellow daffodil, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is a bulbous plant that typically grows in moist, shady environments, such as woodlands and meadows. The plant produces a single, upright stem bearing one to three bright yellow flowers with a greenish throat. Its bulbs are perennial, and the plant is often found in the wild across Europe and parts of Asia.

The flowers bloom in early spring, adding a vibrant splash of color to the landscape.
