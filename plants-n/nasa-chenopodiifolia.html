Nasa chenopodiifolia

Nasa chenopodiifolia, commonly known as the desert morning glory, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its delicate, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in the early morning, typically in shades of purple or pink. The plant has small, scale-like leaves that help reduce water loss, adapting it to harsh, dry environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Nasa chenopodiifolia is a short-lived perennial that plays a role in stabilizing soils in desert ecosystems.
