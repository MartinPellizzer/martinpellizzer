Nectandra rodiei

Nectandra rodiei, commonly known as the wild cinnamon tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark-brown bark and glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by elongated, woody fruits containing aromatic seeds. Its essential oil, derived from the leaves and bark, is valued for its medicinal and aromatic properties.

Nectandra rodiei thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.
