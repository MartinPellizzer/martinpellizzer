Narcissus hispanicus subsp. pinetorum

Narcissus hispanicus subsp.

pinetorum, commonly known as the Spanish daffodil, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow.

The flowers typically bloom in early spring, adding a vibrant touch to woodland and open habitats. This subspecies is adapted to calcareous soils and is often found in forested areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Amaryllidaceae family.
