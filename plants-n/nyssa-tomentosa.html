Nyssa tomentosa

Nyssa tomentosa, commonly known as the hairy tallowtree, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Cornaceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and ovate leaves with a hairy underside. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, followed by red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for wildlife. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in floodplain forests and along riverbanks.

The hairy tallowtree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
