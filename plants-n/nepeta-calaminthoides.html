Nepeta calaminthoides

Nepeta calaminthoides, commonly known as catmint, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage has a strong mint-like scent, which is often used in herbal remedies.

Nepeta calaminthoides is also valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
