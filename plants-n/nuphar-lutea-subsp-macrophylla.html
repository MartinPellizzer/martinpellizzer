Nuphar lutea subsp. macrophylla

Nuphar lutea subsp.

macrophylla, commonly known as the yellow pond lily, is a water plant native to parts of Europe, Asia, and North America. It is characterized by its large, floating yellow flowers and broad, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in diameter.

The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes. Its leaves are typically submerged or floating, with a waxy surface that helps repel water.

The flowers are pollinated by insects and produce small, spherical fruits that contain several seeds.
