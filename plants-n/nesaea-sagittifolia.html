Nesaea sagittifolia

Nesaea sagittifolia, commonly known as the arrowleaf nesaea, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its arrow-shaped leaves, which are typically lanceolate and have a distinct central vein. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pale blue flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Nesaea sagittifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
