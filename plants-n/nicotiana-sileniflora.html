Nicotiana sileniflora

Nicotiana sileniflora, commonly known as the sileniflora tobacco, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and produces small, oval-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its flowers bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is also noted for its aromatic foliage and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
