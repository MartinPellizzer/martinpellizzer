Neosasamorpha uchidana

Neosasamorpha uchidana, commonly known as the Uchidana maple, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Aceraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive palmate leaves with five to seven lobes. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring, followed by orange-red samaras that disperse its seeds. Its ornamental value is appreciated for its vibrant autumn foliage, which displays shades of red and yellow.

Neosasamorpha uchidana is relatively rare and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique botanical features.
