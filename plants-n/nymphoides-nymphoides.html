Nymphoides nymphoides

Nymphoides nymphoides, commonly known as the golden flame orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences with bright yellow and orange petals. The plant typically grows in wetlands and marshy areas, thriving in humid, tropical climates. Its flowers bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Nymphoides nymphoides is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
