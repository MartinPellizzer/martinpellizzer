Nepeta barbara

Nepeta barbara, commonly known as catmint, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in herbal remedies due to their calming properties.

Nepeta barbara thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
