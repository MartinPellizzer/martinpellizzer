Nasturtium africanum subsp. mesatlanticum

Nasturtium africanum subsp.

mesatlanticum, commonly known as the Atlantic nasturtium, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of West Africa. It is characterized by its vibrant yellow or orange flowers and heart-shaped, glossy leaves.

The plant thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found growing along riverbanks and in moist, sandy soils. Its flowers are edible and have a peppery flavor, making it a popular choice for ornamental and culinary use.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly different leaf shape and growth habit compared to other nasturtium varieties.
