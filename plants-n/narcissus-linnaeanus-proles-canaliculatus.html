Narcissus linnaeanus proles canaliculatus

Narcissus linnaeanus proles canaliculatus, commonly known as the narrow-cupped daffodil, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its elongated, narrow tepals that form a cup-like structure around the central reproductive organs. The plant produces showy yellow flowers with a distinctive, often recurved, corona. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens.
