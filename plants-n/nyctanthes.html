Nyctanthes

Nyctanthes, commonly known as night-flowering jasmine, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Apocynaceae.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white or yellow flowers that open at night and close during the day. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including India, Sri Lanka, and parts of Southeast Asia. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often elliptical in shape.

Nyctanthes species are often cultivated for their ornamental value and are also used in traditional medicine for their purported therapeutic properties.
