Nephrodium sagittifolium

Nephrodium sagittifolium, commonly known as the arrowleaf nephroid, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive arrowhead-shaped leaves, which are typically dark green and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.

Nephrodium sagittifolium is often found in shaded or semi-shaded forest understories, where it contributes to the biodiversity of its ecosystem.
