Nephrodium spinulosum var. concordianum

Nephrodium spinulosum var. concordianum, commonly known as the Concordian kidney fern, is a variant of the kidney fern characterized by its distinctive, kidney-shaped fronds.

The plant features a robust rhizome system that supports its growth in moist, shaded environments. Its fronds are typically bi-lobed and covered with fine, spinulose hairs, giving it a textured appearance. The leaves are usually dark green and can grow up to several inches in length.

This variety is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
