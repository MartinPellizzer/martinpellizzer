Nephelea fulgens

Nephelea fulgens, commonly known as the golden flame orchid, is a striking orchid species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow and orange flowers, which resemble flames, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid forest environments. Its flowers are highly fragrant, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Nephelea fulgens is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and unique coloration.
