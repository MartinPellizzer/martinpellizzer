Nyssa sylvatica var. aquatica

Nyssa sylvatica var. aquatica, commonly known as the water tupelo, is a deciduous tree native to the wetlands of the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and broad, ovate leaves that turn a striking red or orange in the fall. The tree produces small, greenish-white flowers in the spring, which are followed by dark blue, waxy berries. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in swamps and floodplain forests.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
