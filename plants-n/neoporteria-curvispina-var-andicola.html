Neoporteria curvispina var. andicola

Neoporteria curvispina var. andicola, commonly known as the Andean slender bamboo, is a shrub native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by cylindrical, woody fruits. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is adapted to the harsh conditions of the Andean mountains.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
