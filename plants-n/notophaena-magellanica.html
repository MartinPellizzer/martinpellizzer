Notophaena magellanica

Notophaena magellanica, commonly known as the Magellanic feather grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, needle-like leaves that give it a feathery appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and grassy slopes. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, two-lobed flowers.

Notophaena magellanica plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates in its native environment.
