Nepeta pauciflora

Nepeta pauciflora, commonly known as catmint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright, slender stems covered in fine hairs and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly fuzzy texture. The plant produces small, clustered blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is known for its aromatic foliage, which contains essential oils that are attractive to cats.

Nepeta pauciflora is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and historical use in herbal medicine.
