Nectandra longicuspis

Nectandra longicuspis, commonly known as the long-toothed sandarac, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark. The tree can grow up to 30 meters tall, with smooth, greyish-brown bark that exudes a fragrant resin. Its leaves are alternate, elliptical, and have a distinctive serrated margin.

The plant is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine and as a source of natural resins.
