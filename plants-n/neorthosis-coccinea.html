Neorthosis coccinea

Neorthosis coccinea, commonly known as the red-flowered ironwood, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, woody structure and vibrant red flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are dark green and glossy. Neorthosis coccinea thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant in coastal areas.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
