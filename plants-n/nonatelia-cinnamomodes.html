Nonatelia cinnamomodes

Nonatelia cinnamomodes, commonly known as the cinnamon-flowered orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that emit a subtle cinnamon scent. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Its blooming period typically occurs during the wet season, adding a unique ornamental value to its habitat.

Nonatelia cinnamomodes is often cultivated for its aromatic foliage and delicate floral display.
