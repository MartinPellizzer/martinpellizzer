Najas gracilis

Najas gracilis, commonly known as the slender watermilfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater ecosystems in North America.

It has narrow, ribbon-like leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem, aiding in its identification. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually found in the water, contributing to its reproductive cycle. Najas gracilis plays a vital role in aquatic habitats by providing shelter and food for various aquatic organisms.

Its presence is often an indicator of good water quality in ponds and lakes.
