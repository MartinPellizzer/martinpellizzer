Nepeta murcica

Nepeta murcica, commonly known as Spanish catmint, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue to violet flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their calming properties.

Nepeta murcica thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.
