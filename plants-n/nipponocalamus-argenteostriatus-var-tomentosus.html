Nipponocalamus argenteostriatus var. tomentosus

Nipponocalamus argenteostriatus var. tomentosus, commonly known as the silver-striped bamboo, is a variant of the silver-striped bamboo species native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, gracefully arching culms that are covered with a dense, silvery-gray pubescence. The leaves are narrow and linear, often with a bluish-green hue and a distinctive silver stripe running along the midrib. This bamboo thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

It is valued for its ornamental appearance and is cultivated in gardens and as a decorative plant in traditional landscapes.
