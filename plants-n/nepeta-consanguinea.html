Nepeta consanguinea

Nepeta consanguinea, commonly known as catmint, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in herbal remedies and have a mint-like scent.

Nepeta consanguinea is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
