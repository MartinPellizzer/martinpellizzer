Nephrolepis hirsutula var. sesquipedalis

Nephrolepis hirsutula var. sesquipedalis, commonly known as the giant maidenhair fern, is a striking variant of the maidenhair fern family.

It is characterized by its long, arching fronds that can reach up to 2 meters in length, making it a dramatic addition to any garden or indoor space. The fronds are bipinnate, with finely divided, glossy green leaflets that give it a delicate, feathery appearance. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and requires high humidity and well-draining soil to flourish.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often used in tropical and subtropical landscapes.
