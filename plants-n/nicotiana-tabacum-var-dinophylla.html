Nicotiana tabacum var. dinophylla

Nicotiana tabacum var. dinophylla, commonly known as the broadleaf tobacco, is a variant of the tobacco plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, which attract pollinators such as bees and moths. It is a herbaceous annual plant that thrives in warm, sunny environments.

Botanically, it is notable for its high nicotine content and its use in both traditional and modern agricultural practices.
