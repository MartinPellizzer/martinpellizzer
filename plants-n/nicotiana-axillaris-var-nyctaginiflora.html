Nicotiana axillaris var. nyctaginiflora

Nicotiana axillaris var. nyctaginiflora, commonly known as the evening glory or nightshade flower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that open in the evening and close at dawn. The plant produces violet or blue flowers with a velvety texture, and its leaves are typically lance-shaped with a slightly hairy surface. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other plants like tomatoes and peppers.
