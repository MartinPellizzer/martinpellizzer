Narcissus bulbocodium f. major

Narcissus bulbocodium f. major, commonly known as the giant bulbocodium narcissus, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in late spring or early summer. The plant features strap-like leaves and produces clusters of white or yellow flowers with a strong, sweet fragrance. Native to southern Europe, it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
