Nicotiana cavicola

Nicotiana cavicola, commonly known as the coastal tobacco plant, is a species native to the coastal regions of South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a herbaceous plant that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils along the shoreline. The plant features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a subtle purple or red hue. Its leaves are usually narrow and lance-shaped, and the plant is known for its ability to thrive in saline and windy environments.

Nicotiana cavicola is also noted for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
