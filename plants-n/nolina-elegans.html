Nolina elegans

Nolina elegans, commonly known as the silver leaf nolina, is a member of the Asparagaceae family and is native to South Africa.

It is a low-growing, evergreen shrub characterized by its narrow, gray-green leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in summer, followed by blue-black berries. It is adapted to arid environments and is drought-tolerant, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a striking addition to rock gardens and dry landscapes.
