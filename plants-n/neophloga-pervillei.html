Neophloga pervillei

Neophloga pervillei, commonly known as the Perville’s lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, funnel-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with strap-like leaves that are dark green and lanceolate in shape. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with a strong, spicy fragrance.

Neophloga pervillei is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is relatively easy to care for in well-drained soil.
