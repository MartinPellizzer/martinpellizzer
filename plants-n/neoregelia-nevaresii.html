Neoregelia nevaresii

Neoregelia nevaresii, commonly known as the velvetleaf bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup to collect rainwater. The plant features vibrant, long-lasting inflorescences that can range in color from red to orange, adding visual interest to its foliage. Its leaves are thick and waxy, providing a unique texture and aiding in water retention.

Neoregelia nevaresii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
