Nephrodium erythrosorum

Nephrodium erythrosorum, commonly known as the red-veined fern, is a unique fern species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that exhibit a striking contrast of green and red veins. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments, often found in tropical forests. Its reproductive structures include spore-producing cones, typical of ferns.

The red-veined fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
