Nigritella × delphineae

Nigritella × delphineae, commonly known as the hybrid between Nigritella and Delphineae, is a rare and intriguing plant species that exhibits characteristics of both parent species.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodland areas with rich, well-drained soil. The plant features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from deep purple to violet, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its ornamental value.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
