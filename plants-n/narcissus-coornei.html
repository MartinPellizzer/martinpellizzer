Narcissus coornei

Narcissus coornei, commonly known as the Coornei daffodil, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe, particularly the Iberian Peninsula, and thrives in sunny, well-drained soils. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange, typically blooming in early spring. Its foliage consists of strap-like leaves that are narrow and grass-like in appearance.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
