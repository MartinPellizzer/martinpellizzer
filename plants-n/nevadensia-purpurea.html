Nevadensia purpurea

Nevadensia purpurea, commonly known as the purple nevadensia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its striking purple to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and high-altitude environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, providing a dense foliage that helps retain moisture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil and full sun exposure.
