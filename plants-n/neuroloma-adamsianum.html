Neuroloma adamsianum

Neuroloma adamsianum, commonly known as the Adams' neuroloma, is a rare and unusual plant species native to specific regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its unique morphology, featuring a short, thick stem and clustered, small, waxy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white in color, which bloom during the wet season. Its botanical significance lies in its adaptation to arid environments and its potential role in ecological studies.
