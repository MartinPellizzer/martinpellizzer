Neckeria aurea var. occidentalis

Neckeria aurea var. occidentalis, commonly known as the Western golden neckeria, is a rare and striking epiphytic plant native to the tropical rainforests of western Africa.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of bright yellow-orange flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant has small, elongated leaves that are covered in a fine, waxy coating, helping to retain moisture in its humid habitat. It thrives in shaded, high-light environments and requires consistent humidity and well-draining soil.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in specialized botanical gardens and greenhouses.
