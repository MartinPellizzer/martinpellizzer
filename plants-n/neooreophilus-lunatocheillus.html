Neooreophilus lunatocheillus

Neooreophilus lunatocheillus, commonly known as the moon-faced orchid, is a rare and exquisite orchid species native to the high altitudes of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, moon-like face-shaped flowers that bloom in a striking combination of white and purple hues. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often on the trunks of trees in montane forests. Its leaves are narrow and elongated, adapted to capture limited sunlight in its natural habitat.

Due to its rarity and aesthetic appeal, it is highly sought after by botanists and orchid enthusiasts alike.
