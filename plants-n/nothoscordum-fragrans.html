Nothoscordum fragrans

Nothoscordum fragrans, commonly known as the fragrant wild garlic, is a herbaceous plant native to the southern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, grass-like foliage and showy, yellow-orange flower heads. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Nothoscordum fragrans thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and wetlands.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to native gardens and ecological restoration projects.
