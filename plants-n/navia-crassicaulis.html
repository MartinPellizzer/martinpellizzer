Navia crassicaulis

Navia crassicaulis, commonly known as the broad-stemmed navia, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its robust, cylindrical stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in terminal inflorescences. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a rough, hairy texture.

Navia crassicaulis is adapted to high-altitude environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.
