Nymphaea tetragona var. wenzelii

Nymphaea tetragona var. wenzelii, commonly known as the Wenzel's water lily, is a aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that float on the water surface. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white, which bloom in the early morning. Its roots are submerged, anchoring it in wetland or pond environments.

It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
