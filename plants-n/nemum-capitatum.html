Nemum capitatum

Nemum capitatum, commonly known as the common thistle, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny, leafy stems and a dense, spherical cluster of purple or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs and have a distinctive, serrated edge.

The flowers are often visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance.
