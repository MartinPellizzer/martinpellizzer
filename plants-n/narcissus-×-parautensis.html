Narcissus × parautensis

Narcissus × parautensis, commonly known as the Parauta daffodil, is a hybrid species within the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to yellow. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with narrow, grass-like leaves. Native to the Iberian Peninsula, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

It is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation.
