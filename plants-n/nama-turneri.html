Nama turneri

Nama turneri, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by fluffy, white seed heads that aid in wind dispersal. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, adapted to reduce water loss in harsh environments.

Nama turneri is often found in sandy soils and is resilient to drought conditions.
