Neoregelia lactea

Neoregelia lactea, commonly known as the milk bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its vibrant, bell-shaped inflorescence that can range in color from white to pink, often with a milky or waxy appearance. The plant features rosettes of broad, waxy leaves that are typically green with white or silver markings. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Neoregelia lactea is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and unique foliage.
