Narcissus chloraster

Narcissus chloraster, commonly known as the green-flowered daffodil, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking greenish-yellow flowers, which are typically trumpet-shaped with a narrow corona. Native to southern Europe, this plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. The leaves are long and strap-like, forming a basal rosette.

It is a perennial plant that blooms in early spring, adding a unique botanical feature to gardens and natural habitats.
