Neottia suzukii

Neottia suzukii, commonly known as the Japanese twayblade, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to eastern Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and a single, small, white or greenish-white flower. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and grasslands. Neottia suzukii is known for its mycorrhizal relationship with fungi, which aids in nutrient absorption.

Its delicate appearance and limited distribution make it a notable species within the orchid family.
