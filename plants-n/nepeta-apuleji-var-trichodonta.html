Nepeta apuleji var. trichodonta

Nepeta apuleji var. trichodonta, commonly known as the woolly catmint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features aromatic, lance-shaped leaves covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a woolly appearance. The plant produces spikes of blue to violet flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its hardy nature and ornamental value make it a valued addition to many gardens.
