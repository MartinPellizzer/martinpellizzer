Noccaea abchasica

Noccaea abchasica, commonly known as the Abkhazian rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cress species. This perennial herb typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh climates. Its small, white flowers bloom in early spring, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and lobed leaves, which help reduce water loss in its arid habitat.
