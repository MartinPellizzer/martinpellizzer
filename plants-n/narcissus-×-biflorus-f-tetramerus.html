Narcissus × biflorus f. tetramerus

Narcissus × biflorus f. tetramerus, commonly known as the tetrameric daffodil, is a hybrid plant that exhibits unique botanical characteristics.

It is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family and is distinguished by its four-petaled flowers, which set it apart from the typical six-petaled daffodils. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange blooms with a distinctive trumpet-shaped corona. It is typically found in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
