Nymphaea brownii

Nymphaea brownii, commonly known as the Brown's water lily, is a species of aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Nymphaeaceae and is characterized by its broad, floating leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats such as ponds and lakes. Its flowers are usually pink to reddish in color and bloom in the early morning.

Nymphaea brownii plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
