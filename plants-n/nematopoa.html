Nematopoa

Nematopoa, commonly known as the spider orchid, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

These plants are characterized by their unique, spider-like appearance, with long, slender stems and clusters of small, intricate flowers. Nematopoa species are typically found in tropical and subtropical regions, often growing in shaded, moist environments. They have a complex root system that allows them to absorb nutrients efficiently from poor soil conditions.

The flowers are usually fragrant and attract specific pollinators, contributing to their ecological significance.
