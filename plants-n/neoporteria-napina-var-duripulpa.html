Neoporteria napina var. duripulpa

Neoporteria napina var. duripulpa, commonly known as the narrow-leafed snowbell, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and small, bell-shaped white flowers that appear in early spring. The leaves are alternate, simple, and typically have a narrow, lanceolate shape with a smooth or slightly serrated margin. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
