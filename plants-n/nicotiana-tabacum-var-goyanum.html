Nicotiana tabacum var. goyanum

Nicotiana tabacum var. goyanum, commonly known as the Goyana tobacco, is a variant of the tobacco plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often pubescent. The plant produces striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. It is cultivated for its leaves, which are used in traditional smoking and medicinal practices.

Due to its ornamental value and historical significance, it is also appreciated in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
