Nepeta manchuriensis

Nepeta manchuriensis, commonly known as Manchurian catmint, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its mild sedative properties.

Nepeta manchuriensis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
