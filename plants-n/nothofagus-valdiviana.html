Nothofagus valdiviana

Nothofagus valdiviana, commonly known as the Valdivian southern beech, is a species of evergreen tree native to South America.

It is part of the Nothofagus genus, which is characteristic of the Southern Hemisphere's temperate rainforests. This plant is notable for its dark, fissured bark and broad, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in montane and cloud forests.

Nothofagus valdiviana plays a crucial role in its ecosystem, providing habitat and contributing to soil stability.
