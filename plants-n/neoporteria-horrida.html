Neoporteria horrida

Neoporteria horrida, commonly known as the roughleaf mallow, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and in open woodlands. It has a fibrous root system and can reach heights of up to 2 meters.

Neoporteria horrida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
