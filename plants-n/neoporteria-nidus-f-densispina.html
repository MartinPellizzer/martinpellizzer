Neoporteria nidus f. densispina

Neoporteria nidus f. densispina, commonly known as the dense-spined rock fig, is a unique variant of the rock fig plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and compact growth habit, making it well adapted to rocky and arid environments. The plant features thick, leathery leaves that are often covered in fine, sharp spines, providing both defense and water conservation. It produces small, clustered inflorescences that eventually develop into edible fig-like fruits.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
