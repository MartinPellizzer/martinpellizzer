Narcissus tazetta subsp. italicus

Narcissus tazetta subsp.

italicus, commonly known as the Italian daffodil, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe. It produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange, typically blooming in early spring.

The plant has narrow, strap-like leaves that emerge from a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its fragrant blooms and ability to naturalize over time.
