Neurachne peekelii

Neurachne peekelii, commonly known as the Peekel's grass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green to bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Neurachne peekelii thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and desert habitats.

Its ecological role includes providing forage for wildlife and stabilizing soil in arid environments.
