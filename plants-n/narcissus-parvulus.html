Narcissus parvulus

Narcissus parvulus, commonly known as the small narcissus or dwarf daffodil, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and is characterized by its compact growth habit and early spring blooming. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces one to three bright yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its resilience and ornamental value, it is a popular choice for gardeners seeking hardy, low-maintenance plants.
