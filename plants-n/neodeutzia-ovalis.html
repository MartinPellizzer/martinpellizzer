Neodeutzia ovalis

Neodeutzia ovalis, commonly known as the oval honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and have a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers in clusters during the spring, which are usually white or pale yellow. These flowers are adapted for pollination by bees and other insects.

Neodeutzia ovalis is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
