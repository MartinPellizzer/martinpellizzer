Nepenthes melamphora var. pubescens

Nepenthes melamphora var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy pitcher plant, is a tropical carnivorous plant native to Borneo.

It is characterized by its large, elongated pitchers that secrete a digestive fluid to trap and digest insects. The plant's leaves are modified into these specialized structures, which are often covered in fine, hairy pubescence, giving it its varietal name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires consistent moisture and nutrient-poor soil.

Its unique morphology and carnivorous adaptations make it a fascinating subject in botanical studies.
