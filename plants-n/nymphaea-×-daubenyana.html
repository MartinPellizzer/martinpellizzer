Nymphaea × daubenyana

Nymphaea × daubenyana, commonly known as the Daubeny's water lily, is a hybrid aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that float on the water's surface, supported by long, sturdy petioles. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, often opening in the early morning. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, preferring full sun to partial shade.

Nymphaea × daubenyana is widely cultivated for ornamental purposes in ponds and water gardens.
