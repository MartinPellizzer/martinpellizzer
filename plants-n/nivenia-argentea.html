Nivenia argentea

Nivenia argentea, commonly known as silver nivenia, is a flowering plant native to the southern regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which gives it its common name, and its clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in shrubland or coastal areas. It is a perennial species that thrives in well-drained conditions and is relatively drought-tolerant.

Nivenia argentea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
