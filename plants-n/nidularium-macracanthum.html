Nidularium macracanthum

Nidularium macracanthum, commonly known as the giant arrowhead, is a striking member of the Araceae family native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that can grow up to 3 meters in length, making it a dramatic focal point in tropical gardens. The plant produces striking inflorescences with bright yellow spadices surrounded by white bracts, which attract pollinators such as beetles and flies. It thrives in warm, humid environments with high light exposure and requires consistent moisture in its soil.

Due to its size and visual impact, it is often used in large-scale landscaping or as a specimen plant in botanical gardens.
