Narcissus linnaeanus subsp. aureus

Narcissus linnaeanus subsp.

aureus, commonly known as the yellow daffodil, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaryllidaceae family. It is characterized by its trumpet-shaped, yellow corona and six to eight white or pale yellow tepals.

Native to the Iberian Peninsula, this subspecies thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. The plant produces a single stem with a cluster of flowers, typically blooming in early spring.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
