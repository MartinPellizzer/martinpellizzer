Nasturtium hispanicum

Nasturtium hispanicum, commonly known as Spanish nasturtium, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Tropaeolaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, edible flowers and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces bright orange or red flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in culinary applications, adding a peppery flavor to dishes.

Nasturtium hispanicum is also valued for its ornamental beauty and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
