Nymphaea bombycina

Nymphaea bombycina, commonly known as the silk-cotton water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, waxy leaves that float on the water's surface, often with a distinctive white or pinkish hue. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, opening in the early morning and closing by midday. Its roots anchor it in muddy substrates, while its rhizomes spread horizontally to support growth.

Nymphaea bombycina plays an important ecological role in water ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
