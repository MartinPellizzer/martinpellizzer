Nassella chilensis var. juncea

Nassella chilensis var. juncea, commonly known as Chilean needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and dense tufts of fine, needle-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
