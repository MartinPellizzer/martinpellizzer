Notocactus mammulosus subsp. erythracanthus

Notocactus mammulosus subsp.

erythracanthus, commonly known as the red-armed cactus, is a succulent native to South America, particularly Brazil. It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are ribbed and often covered in clusters of white or pale blue spines.

The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. Its growth habit is typically upright, and it can reach heights of up to 2 meters.

It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.
