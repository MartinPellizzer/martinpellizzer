Notholaena bonariensis

Notholaena bonariensis, commonly known as the Argentine wallaby grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed areas.

Notholaena bonariensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in ecological restoration projects.
