Nesaea straminea

Nesaea straminea, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is native to parts of Europe and Asia, often found in meadows and woodland edges. The plant features showy, bell-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, while the stems are slender and often branching.

Nesaea straminea is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
