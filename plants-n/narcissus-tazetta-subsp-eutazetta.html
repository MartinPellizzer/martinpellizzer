Narcissus tazetta subsp. eutazetta

Narcissus tazetta subsp.

eutazetta, commonly known as the Eutazetta daffodil, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its compact, rounded bulbs and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces clusters of fragrant, funnel-shaped flowers in shades of white, yellow, and orange, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its early blooming habit.
