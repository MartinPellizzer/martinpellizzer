Nicotiana havanensis

Nicotiana havanensis, commonly known as Cuban tobacco, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family.

It is native to the Caribbean and parts of South America, particularly Cuba. The plant is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to purple, and its hairy, glandular leaves. It is cultivated for its ornamental value as well as for its use in traditional herbal medicine.

The plant contains nicotine, making it a relative of the tobacco plant.
