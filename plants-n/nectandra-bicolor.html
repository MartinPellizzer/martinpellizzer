Nectandra bicolor

Nectandra bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored sandarac, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic wood and distinctive leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by spherical, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are usually elliptical with a smooth surface and a strong, spicy scent.

Nectandra bicolor is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
