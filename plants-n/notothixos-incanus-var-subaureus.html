Notothixos incanus var. subaureus

Notothixos incanus var. subaureus, commonly known as the golden-hair grass, is a variant of the hair grass species native to parts of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves that often have a golden hue, especially when dry. The plant typically grows in open, sandy or rocky habitats, preferring well-drained soils. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in dense spikes, typical of the Poaceae family.

This variant is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
