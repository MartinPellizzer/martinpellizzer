Nepeta podostachys subsp. darwasica

Nepeta podostachys subsp.

darwasica, commonly known as the Darwasica catnip, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Pakistan. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves.

The plant produces small, clustered blue or purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported calming and digestive properties.

The subspecies is distinguished by its unique floral structure and habitat preferences in high-altitude environments.
