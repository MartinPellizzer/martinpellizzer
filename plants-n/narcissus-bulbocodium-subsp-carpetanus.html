Narcissus bulbocodium subsp. carpetanus

Narcissus bulbocodium subsp.

carpetanus, commonly known as the carpet narcissus, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Europe. It produces clusters of small, white, cup-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. It is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate in gardens.
