Norysca oblongifolia

Norysca oblongifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf norysca, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, oblong-shaped leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stem. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, often blooming during the warmer months. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.

Norysca oblongifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
