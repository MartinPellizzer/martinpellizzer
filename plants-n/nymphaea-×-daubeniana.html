Nymphaea × daubeniana

Nymphaea × daubeniana, commonly known as the Daubenian water lily, is a hybrid aquatic plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy white or pink flowers that bloom above the water surface. The plant has broad, rounded, dark green leaves that float on the water's surface, providing a striking visual contrast to the flowers. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments with rich soil and ample sunlight.

Nymphaea × daubeniana is often cultivated in ponds and water gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
