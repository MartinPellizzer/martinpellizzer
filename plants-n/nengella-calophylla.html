Nengella calophylla

Nengella calophylla, commonly known as the glossy-leaved myrtle, is a tropical shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, fleshy capsule that contains several seeds.

Nengella calophylla is valued for its aromatic properties and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
