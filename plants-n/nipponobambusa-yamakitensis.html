Nipponobambusa yamakitensis

Nipponobambusa yamakitensis, commonly known as the Yamakita bamboo, is a species of grass belonging to the Bambusoideae subfamily.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its slender, upright culms that often form dense clumps. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a smooth, waxy surface, and it produces small, clustered inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
