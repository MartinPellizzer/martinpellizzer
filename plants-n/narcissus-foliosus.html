Narcissus foliosus

Narcissus foliosus, commonly known as the leafy daffodil, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers bloom in early spring, producing a cluster of cup-shaped petals that attract pollinators.

Narcissus foliosus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a long history of use in gardens and traditional medicine.
