Neoporteria echinus

Neoporteria echinus, commonly known as the spiny candelabra, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its branched, woody stems that resemble a candelabra, often with spiny branches. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for dry, rocky habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
