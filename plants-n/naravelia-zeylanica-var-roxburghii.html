Naravelia zeylanica var. roxburghii

Naravelia zeylanica var. roxburghii, commonly known as the Ceylon velvet tamarind, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Sri Lanka.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated pods containing edible seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a source of protein. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
