Nyssa ogeche var. acuminata

Nyssa ogeche var. acuminata, commonly known as the African boxwood, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage composed of oval, leathery leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are followed by blue-black berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is cultivated in some regions for landscaping purposes.
