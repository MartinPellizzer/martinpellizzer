Nasturtiopsis arabica

Nasturtiopsis arabica, commonly known as the Arabian watercress, is a flowering plant native to the Arabian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant is characterized by its small, edible leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils near water sources.

Due to its historical use in traditional medicine and cuisine, it holds cultural significance in the region.
