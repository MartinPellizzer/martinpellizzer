Neomimosa

Neomimosa, commonly known as the false mimosa, is a genus of flowering plants in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its bipinnate leaves and showy, pea-like flowers that typically range in color from white to pink. The plant is native to various regions in South America and is often found in dry, open habitats. Neomimosa species are known for their ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, which helps improve soil fertility.

However, some species can become invasive in non-native environments, posing ecological challenges.
