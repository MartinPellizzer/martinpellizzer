Nassella barbinodis

Nassella barbinodis, commonly known as the hairy needlegrass, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered with fine hairs, giving it a distinctive texture.

Nassella barbinodis is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.
