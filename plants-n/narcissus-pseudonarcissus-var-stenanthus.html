Narcissus pseudonarcissus var. stenanthus

Narcissus pseudonarcissus var. stenanthus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved daffodil, is a variety of the genus Narcissus native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its narrow, strap-like leaves and a single, prominent yellow flower with a distinctive cup-shaped corona. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils in woodland or grassy areas. It blooms in early spring, often before the leaves are fully developed.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.
