Nymphaea zanzibariensis var. pallida

Nymphaea zanzibariensis var. pallida, commonly known as the Zanzibar white water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to the Indian Ocean islands.

It is characterized by its large, pale to white, showy flowers that bloom above the water surface. The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves that float on the water's surface, providing habitat for various aquatic organisms. Its roots are anchored in the muddy substrate, allowing it to thrive in still or slow-moving freshwater environments.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ecological importance in aquatic ecosystems.
