Normanbya normanbyi

Normanbya normanbyi, commonly known as the Sydney golden wattle, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which appear in early spring and are clustered in spherical heads. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and is adapted to thrive in coastal and sandy soils. It is a member of the legume family and has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria.

This species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens.
