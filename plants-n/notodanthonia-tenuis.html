Notodanthonia tenuis

Notodanthonia tenuis, commonly known as the slender feathergrass, is a low-growing perennial grass native to southern Australia.

It features slender, wiry stems and fine, needle-like leaves that give it a delicate appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the grass family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as woodlands and grasslands.

Notodanthonia tenuis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.
