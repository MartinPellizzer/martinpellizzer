Nymphaea alba var. splendens

Nymphaea alba var. splendens, commonly known as the White Water Lily with Splendens Variation, is a water lily native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features large, rounded, white flowers with a subtle pinkish hue, often appearing in clusters on the water's surface. The leaves are broad, floating, and typically have a waxy texture, aiding in buoyancy and light reflection. Its botanical structure includes a rhizomatous root system that anchors it in shallow water.

The plant is prized for its ornamental value and plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
