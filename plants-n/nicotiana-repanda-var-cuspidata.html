Nicotiana repanda var. cuspidata

Nicotiana repanda var. cuspidata, commonly known as the cuspidate tobacco plant, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its hairy stems and lanceolate, serrated leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late summer. It is known for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and floral display.

The species is also related to other Nicotiana species, some of which are used for commercial tobacco production.
