Nymphaea georginae

Nymphaea georginae, commonly known as the Georgian water lily, is a species of aquatic plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Nymphaeaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy, floating flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant has broad, heart-shaped leaves that provide a habitat for various aquatic organisms. Its roots are anchored in the mud at the bottom of ponds and lakes, while its stems float on the water's surface.

Nymphaea georginae plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by supporting biodiversity and improving water quality.
