Neomammillaria candida var. rosea

Neomammillaria candida var. rosea, commonly known as the roseate mammillaria, is a compact cactus native to Mexico.

It features a short, cylindrical stem covered in white to pinkish tubercles that give it a distinctive, almost fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, vibrant pink or red flowers during the spring and summer months. It is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its slow growth and low maintenance requirements.

Its unique coloration and compact size make it a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
