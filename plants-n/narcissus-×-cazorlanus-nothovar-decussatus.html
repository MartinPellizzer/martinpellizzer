Narcissus × cazorlanus nothovar. decussatus

Narcissus × cazorlanus nothovar. decussatus, commonly known as the decussate daffodil, is a hybrid plant resulting from crosses between different narcissus species.

It is characterized by its distinctive, narrowly lance-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers with a bright yellow corona. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for spring-flowering displays.

This nothovar is noted for its compact growth habit and resistance to common daffodil diseases.
