Nephrolepis platyotis

Nephrolepis platyotis, commonly known as the broad-leaf maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, arching fronds that are typically green with a slightly glossy appearance. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread and form dense mats. Its leaves are pinnately divided, with small, ovate pinnae that give it a feathery appearance.

Nephrolepis platyotis thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
