Nicolasia lugardii

Nicolasia lugardii, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its upright stems and clustered, velvety leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white, typically blooming in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers bright, indirect light.

Due to its ornamental value, it is widely cultivated as a houseplant.
