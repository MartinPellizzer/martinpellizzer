Notonia trachycarpa

Notonia trachycarpa, commonly known as the rough-seeded notonia, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, narrow leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, typically blooming during the warmer months. Notonia trachycarpa is adapted to survive in dry conditions, with deep roots that help it access underground water.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it an important species in its native habitat.
