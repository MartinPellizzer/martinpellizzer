Nymphanthus watsonii

Nymphanthus watsonii, commonly known as the Watson's weeping cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its delicate, pendulous white or pink flowers that bloom in spring, adding ornamental value to gardens. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are oval-shaped with a smooth margin. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for temperate climates.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental horticulture due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
