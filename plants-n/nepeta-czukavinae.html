Nepeta czukavinae

Nepeta czukavinae, commonly known as the Czech catmint, is a perennial herb native to the Czech Republic.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their medicinal and ornamental value. The plant features square stems, opposite leaves, and small, fragrant blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. It is often cultivated for its ornamental appeal and pleasant scent, which is reminiscent of catnip.

Nepeta czukavinae is also valued for its potential medicinal properties, though further research is needed to confirm its efficacy.
