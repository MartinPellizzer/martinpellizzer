Narcissus × matritensis

Narcissus × matritensis, commonly known as the Matritensis daffodil, is a hybrid species within the genus Narcissus.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers with a prominent central corona. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, featuring strap-like leaves and a robust root system. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and floral arrangements.
