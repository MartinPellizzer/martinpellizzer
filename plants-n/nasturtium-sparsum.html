Nasturtium sparsum

Nasturtium sparsum, commonly known as the slender nasturtium, is a herbaceous annual plant native to South America.

It features heart-shaped, glossy leaves and produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers with a distinctive spurred petal. The plant is characterized by its trailing growth habit and is often found in moist, open habitats. Its flowers are edible, with a peppery flavor similar to watercress, and the leaves can be used in salads.

Nasturtium sparsum is also known for its ability to attract beneficial insects like ladybugs and bees.
