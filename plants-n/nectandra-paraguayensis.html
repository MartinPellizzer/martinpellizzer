Nectandra paraguayensis

Nectandra paraguayensis, commonly known as the Paraguay sand-wood, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark-colored wood. The plant typically grows in humid lowland forests and thrives in warm, moist climates. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a glossy surface.

The tree produces small, fragrant flowers followed by dark, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife.
