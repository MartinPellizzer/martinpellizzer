Nolina

Nolina, commonly known as the spinifex or pincushion plant, is a genus of succulent shrubs native to Australia and the Pacific Islands.

These plants are characterized by their upright, cylindrical stems and dense clusters of spiky, grass-like leaves. Nolinas are drought-tolerant and thrive in arid or semi-arid environments, making them well-suited for xeriscaping. They produce small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by fleshy, berry-like fruits.

The genus includes several species, such as Nolina gregaria and Nolina sericea, which are popular in horticulture for their ornamental value and low maintenance requirements.
