Nemophila discoidalis var. argentea

Nemophila discoidalis var. argentea, commonly known as silverleaf nemophila, is a flowering plant native to California.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and produces clusters of small, white or pale blue flowers. The leaves are typically covered in a fine, silvery or grayish pubescence, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, often found in coastal scrub and woodland habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
