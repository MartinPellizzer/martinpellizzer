Navia saxicola

Navia saxicola, commonly known as the rock navia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It thrives in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features slender, upright stems and small, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. Its flowers are typically blue or purple, arranged in loose clusters, adding a subtle ornamental value.

Navia saxicola is valued in horticulture for its drought tolerance and ability to stabilize soil in rocky landscapes.
