Nephrodium arcuatum

Nephrodium arcuatum, commonly known as the curved kidney fern, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Nephrolepidaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, kidney-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its fronds are usually arranged in a fan-like pattern and have a waxy, leathery texture.

Nephrodium arcuatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
