Nymphaea aegyptiaca

Nymphaea aegyptiaca, commonly known as the Egyptian water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to the Nile River basin.

It features large, showy white petals that open in the early morning and close at dusk. The plant has broad, heart-shaped leaves that float on the water's surface, supported by long, slender stems. Its flowers emit a sweet fragrance, attracting pollinators such as beetles and bees.

Nymphaea aegyptiaca plays an important ecological role in freshwater ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
