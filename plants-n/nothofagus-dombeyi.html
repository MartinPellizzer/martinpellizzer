Nothofagus dombeyi

Nothofagus dombeyi, commonly known as the Dombey's southern beech, is a species native to southern South America.

It is a deciduous tree that typically grows in temperate rainforests and moist mountainous regions. The plant has dark, fissured bark and produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by edible, woody fruits. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a serrated margin.

Nothofagus dombeyi plays an important ecological role in its habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
