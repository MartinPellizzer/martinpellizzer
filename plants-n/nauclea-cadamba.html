Nauclea cadamba

Nauclea cadamba, commonly known as Indian satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing elliptical seeds. It is often found in moist, tropical forests and is valued for its durable timber.

Nauclea cadamba also has traditional medicinal uses in various cultures.
