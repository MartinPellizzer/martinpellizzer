Nepeta bodinieri

Nepeta bodinieri, commonly known as catmint, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green leaves and clusters of small, pale blue or lavender flowers. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs such as mint and basil. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy and low-maintenance garden plant.

Nepeta bodinieri is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
