Neodryas jose-kawaii

Neodryas jose-kawaii, commonly known as the Kawaii's ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic orchid species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, translucent petals and a unique floral structure that resembles a ghostly apparition. The plant typically grows on tree bark or in shaded, humid environments, relying on specific symbiotic fungi for nutrient uptake. Its flowers are usually pale green or white, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insect species.

Due to habitat destruction and limited distribution, Neodryas jose-kawaii is considered critically endangered and is of great interest to botanists and conservationists.
