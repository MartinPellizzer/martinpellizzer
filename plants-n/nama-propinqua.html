Nama propinqua

Nama propinqua, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironwood, is a small evergreen shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves and small, inconspicuous white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, dark blue fruits that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It is well adapted to dry conditions, with deep roots that allow it to survive in poor soils.

Nama propinqua is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
