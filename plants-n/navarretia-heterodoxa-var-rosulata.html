Navarretia heterodoxa var. rosulata

Navarretia heterodoxa var. rosulata, commonly known as the Sierra starflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and produces clusters of small, star-shaped white or pink flowers. The plant typically blooms in early spring, often in rocky or open habitats. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is often found in alpine or subalpine regions.

Navarretia heterodoxa var. rosulata is adapted to cold, dry environments and is an important species in its native ecosystems.
