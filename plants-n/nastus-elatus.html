Nastus elatus

Nastus elatus, commonly known as the slender milkweed, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite leaves that are typically ovate to lanceolate in shape. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Nastus elatus is known for its milky sap, which is a common feature among plants in the Apocynaceae family.
