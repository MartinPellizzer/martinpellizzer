Nepeta nuristanica

Nepeta nuristanica, commonly known as the Nuristan catmint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Afghanistan and northern Pakistan.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky and alpine habitats, thriving at high altitudes. Its small, tubular flowers are usually purple or blue, arranged in whorled clusters.

Nepeta nuristanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used traditionally in local herbal medicine.
