Nepeta congesta var. cryptantha

Nepeta congesta var. cryptantha, commonly known as the cryptantha catmint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright, branching stems covered in dense, grayish-green foliage. The plant produces small, tubular blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is well adapted to dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun.

Its aromatic leaves and flowers make it a popular choice for rock gardens and as a decorative plant.
