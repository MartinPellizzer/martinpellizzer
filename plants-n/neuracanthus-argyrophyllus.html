Neuracanthus argyrophyllus

Neuracanthus argyrophyllus, commonly known as the silver-leafed shrub, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It features dense, woody stems covered in silvery-gray foliage, which helps reflect sunlight and reduce water loss. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white, blooming in late spring. Its leaves are thick and leathery, adapted to withstand harsh, arid conditions.

Neuracanthus argyrophyllus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
