Nama coulteri var. pringlei

Nama coulteri var. pringlei, commonly known as the Pringle's nara, is a shrub native to the arid regions of northern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is well adapted to harsh, dry environments, with deep roots that help it survive prolonged droughts. Its leaves are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in challenging climates.
