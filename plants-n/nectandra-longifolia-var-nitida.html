Nectandra longifolia var. nitida

Nectandra longifolia var. nitida, commonly known as the shining nectandra, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy bloom. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule containing several seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and tropical landscapes.
