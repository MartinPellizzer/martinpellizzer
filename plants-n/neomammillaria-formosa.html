Neomammillaria formosa

Neomammillaria formosa, commonly known as the Desert Rose, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and bright green to blue-green stems. The plant features prominent white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. Its fleshy, ribbed stems are adapted to store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments.

Neomammillaria formosa is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its attractive appearance and low maintenance requirements.
