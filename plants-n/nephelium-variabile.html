Nephelium variabile

Nephelium variabile, commonly known as the mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and broad, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that lead to the development of its distinctive purple-hued fruit. The fruit has a thick, leathery rind and a segmented, white, sweet flesh that is rich in antioxidants.

Due to its unique flavor and nutritional value, it is highly valued in both culinary and medicinal applications.
