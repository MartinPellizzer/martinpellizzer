Narcissus × poculiformis

Narcissus × poculiformis, commonly known as the double daffodil, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Narcissus tazetta and Narcissus bulbocodium.

It is characterized by its distinctive, layered petals that give it a full, pom-pom-like appearance. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and produces bright yellow flowers in early spring. Its bulbs are perennial, thriving in well-drained soil and full sun.

This cultivar is widely cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.
