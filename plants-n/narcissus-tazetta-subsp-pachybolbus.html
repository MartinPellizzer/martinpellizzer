Narcissus tazetta subsp. pachybolbus

Narcissus tazetta subsp.

pachybolbus, commonly known as the Persian daffodil, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its striking, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to yellow and orange.

The plant produces large, fleshy bulbs that store nutrients for the next growing season. Its foliage consists of strap-like leaves that are broad and sturdy.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
