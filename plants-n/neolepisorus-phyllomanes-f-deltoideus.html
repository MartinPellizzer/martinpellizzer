Neolepisorus phyllomanes f. deltoideus

Neolepisorus phyllomanes f. deltoideus, commonly known as the deltoid leaf fern, is a fern species characterized by its distinctive, deltoid-shaped fronds.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia. The plant exhibits a compact, low-growing habit with fronds that are often leathery and have a dark green, glossy appearance. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are typically located on the underside of the fronds, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

The fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
