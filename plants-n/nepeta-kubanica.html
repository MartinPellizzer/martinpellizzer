Nepeta kubanica

Nepeta kubanica, commonly known as Cuban catmint, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Central Asia.

It features upright stems covered in fine hairs and lance-shaped leaves that are often gray-green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is known for its strong mint-like fragrance and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Nepeta kubanica is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun to partial shade.
