Nicotiana brasilia

Nicotiana brasilia, commonly known as the Brasilia tobacco plant, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a herbaceous plant that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a distinct aromatic scent. Its flowers are trumpet-shaped, with five petals, and are usually pink or purple in color.

The plant is part of the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshade plants like tomatoes and peppers.
