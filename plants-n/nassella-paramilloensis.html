Nassella paramilloensis

Nassella paramilloensis, commonly known as the Paramillo needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America, particularly found in Argentina and Chile.

It is characterized by its slender, needle-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes, adapting to arid and semi-arid conditions. Its fibrous roots help stabilize soil, making it ecologically significant in its native range.

The species is of interest to botanists and conservationists due to its limited distribution and unique morphological traits.
