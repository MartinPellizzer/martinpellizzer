Neooreophilus chelosepalus

Neooreophilus chelosepalus, commonly known as the velvetleaf orchid, is a unique species within the orchid family, characterized by its striking, velvety foliage and intricate floral structures.

It is native to tropical regions and thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. The plant exhibits a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, supporting its growth in less fertile soils. Its flowers, which bloom in clusters, feature specialized sepals and petals that aid in pollination by specific insect species.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
