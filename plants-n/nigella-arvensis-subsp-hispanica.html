Nigella arvensis subsp. hispanica

Nigella arvensis subsp.

hispanica, commonly known as Spanish cumin or black cumin, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and slender, branched stems.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as fields and roadsides. Its seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and cooking, have a strong, pungent aroma.

The subspecies is distinguished from other Nigella species by its smaller size and specific geographic distribution.
