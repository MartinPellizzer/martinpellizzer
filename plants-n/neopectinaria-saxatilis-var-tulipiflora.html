Neopectinaria saxatilis var. tulipiflora

Neopectinaria saxatilis var. tulipiflora, commonly known as the tulip-flowered rock-dweller, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in dry environments. The plant produces vibrant, tulip-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange, typically blooming in spring. It prefers well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and full sun exposure.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
