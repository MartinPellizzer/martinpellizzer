Nematolepis squamea subsp. retusa

Nematolepis squamea subsp.

retusa, commonly known as the broad-leaved lily, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Africa. It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, scaly texture.

The plant produces striking, showy flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically arranged in dense spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and ecological landscapes.
