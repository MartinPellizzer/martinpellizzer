Nectandra subbullata

Nectandra subbullata, commonly known as the bullate sandarac tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its aromatic, dark-colored wood. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. It produces small, fragrant flowers followed by oval, woody fruits containing a single seed.

The tree is valued for its resin, which has been used traditionally in incense and medicinal applications.
