Negundo interior subsp. texana

Negundo interior subsp.

texana, commonly known as the Texas box elder, is a member of the Hippocastanaceae family. It is a deciduous tree characterized by its compound leaves and smooth, gray bark.

The plant is native to the southern United States, particularly Texas and Oklahoma. It produces small, yellowish flowers in the spring, followed by winged fruits that aid in seed dispersal.

This subspecies is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in riparian zones and open woodlands.
