Neoporteria curvispina f. orientalis

Neoporteria curvispina f. orientalis, commonly known as the oriental slenderleaf spurge, is a low-growing perennial herb native to parts of Asia.

It features succulent, ribbed stems that are often tinged with red or purple, especially in cooler weather. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and slightly curved, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is adapted to rocky or arid soils and is often found in mountainous regions.
