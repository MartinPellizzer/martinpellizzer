Neolitsea aureosericea

Neolitsea aureosericea, commonly known as the golden silk tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its finely serrated, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with a yellowish hue on the underside. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by elliptical, woody fruits. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
