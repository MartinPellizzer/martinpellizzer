Nothoscordum andicola

Nothoscordum andicola, commonly known as the Andean garlic.

It is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America. The plant produces small, white to pale purple flowers arranged in clusters. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it has a distinct garlic-like scent.

Nothoscordum andicola is often cultivated for its ornamental value and culinary uses.
