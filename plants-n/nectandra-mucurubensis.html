Nectandra mucurubensis

Nectandra mucurubensis, commonly known as the Brazilian sandarac tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its aromatic, dark-colored wood. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by small, round berries. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a leathery texture with a strong scent.

The tree is valued for its durable wood and is also used in traditional medicine.
