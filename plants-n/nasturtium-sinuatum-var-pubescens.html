Nasturtium sinuatum var. pubescens

Nasturtium sinuatum var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy nasturtium, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, pubescent (hairy) leaves and vibrant, edible flowers that come in shades of orange and yellow. The plant belongs to the family Brassicaceae and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and culinary uses. Its stems are hairy and often trailing, making it suitable for hanging baskets or as a ground cover.

The leaves and flowers are rich in nutrients and have a peppery taste, making them popular in salads and herbal teas.
