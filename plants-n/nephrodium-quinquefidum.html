Nephrodium quinquefidum

Nephrodium quinquefidum, commonly known as the five-lobed kidney fern, is a species of fern characterized by its distinctive fronds.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia. The fronds are typically bipinnate, with a broad, kidney-shaped outline and a serrated margin. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Its reproductive structures include sori that are clustered on the underside of the fronds.
