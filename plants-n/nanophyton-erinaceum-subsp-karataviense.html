Nanophyton erinaceum subsp. karataviense

Nanophyton erinaceum subsp.

karataviense, commonly known as the Karatavinskii spiny scale, is a small, succulent plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its dense, spiny leaves that form a compact, cushion-like structure.

The plant is adapted to extreme drought conditions, storing water in its thick, fleshy leaves. It produces small, yellowish flowers during the brief growing season.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
