Neslia paniculata subsp. apiculata

Neslia paniculata subsp.

apiculata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wallflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It features upright stems with opposite, narrow, lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers arranged in paniculate inflorescences, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
