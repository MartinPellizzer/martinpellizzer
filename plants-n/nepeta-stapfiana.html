Nepeta stapfiana

Nepeta stapfiana, commonly known as the Afghan catnip, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their calming properties.

Nepeta stapfiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in its native habitat.
