Neslia hispanica

Neslia hispanica, commonly known as the Spanish thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, prickly leaves and purple to pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its composite flower heads. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open fields and along roadsides.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural areas.
