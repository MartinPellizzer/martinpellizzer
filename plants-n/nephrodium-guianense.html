Nephrodium guianense

Nephrodium guianense, commonly known as the Guianese nephroid, is a rare and unique fern species native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the family Nephrodiales, characterized by its distinctive, kidney-shaped fronds. The plant exhibits a compact growth habit with dark green, leathery leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. Its reproductive structures, including sporangia, are clustered on the underside of the fronds, typical of ferns.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Nephrodium guianense is considered a species of conservation concern.
