Neuracanthus trinervius

Neuracanthus trinervius, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed or cultivated areas.

Neuracanthus trinervius is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its role in traditional medicine.
