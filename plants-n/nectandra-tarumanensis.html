Nectandra tarumanensis

Nectandra tarumanensis, commonly known as the Java sandalwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its aromatic, dark-colored wood. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. It produces small, white flowers followed by spherical fruits containing smooth, black seeds.

The tree is valued for its timber and has ecological significance in its native habitats.
