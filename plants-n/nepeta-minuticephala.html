Nepeta minuticephala

Nepeta minuticephala, commonly known as catmint, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant blue or purple flowers and fuzzy, gray-green leaves. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like mint and lavender. Nepeta minuticephala is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
