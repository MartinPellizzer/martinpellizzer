Neocouma ternstroemiacea

Neocouma ternstroemiacea, commonly known as the tropical evergreen tree, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.
