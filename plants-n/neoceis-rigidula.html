Neoceis rigidula

Neoceis rigidula, commonly known as the rigid spurge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems that are typically covered in a waxy, powdery coating. The leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly serrated edge. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are clustered in cyathia, typical of the spurge family.

The plant is native to arid and semi-arid regions and is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments.
