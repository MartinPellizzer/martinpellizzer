Nigella oxypetala var. lancifolia

Nigella oxypetala var. lancifolia, commonly known as the lancolate sesame, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces elongated, spindle-shaped seeds that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for arid and semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ornamental and practical uses in horticulture.
