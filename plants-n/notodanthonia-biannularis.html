Notodanthonia biannularis

Notodanthonia biannularis, commonly known as the two-ring grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive two-lobed inflorescence, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and sandy soils, preferring full sun exposure. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a tufted growth habit.

Notodanthonia biannularis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native gardens.
