Narcissus tazetta subsp. ochroleucus

Narcissus tazetta subsp.

ochroleucus, commonly known as the Persian daffodil, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Asia. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers with a white throat.

The plant is characterized by its fragrant blooms and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, thriving in temperate climates.

This subspecies is closely related to other daffodil species and is valued for its early spring flowering.
