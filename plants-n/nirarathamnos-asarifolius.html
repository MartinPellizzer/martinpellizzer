Nirarathamnos asarifolius

Nirarathamnos asarifolius, commonly known as the Indian clove or Malabar clove, is a flowering plant native to the western Ghats of India.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its leaves are aromatic, with a scent reminiscent of cloves, and are used in traditional medicine.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in local herbal remedies.
