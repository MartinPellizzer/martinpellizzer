Nigera

Nigera, commonly known as the black nightshade, is a flowering plant belonging to the Solanaceae family.

It is native to Europe, Asia, and Africa, and it thrives in a variety of soil types and climates. The plant features small, star-shaped flowers that are typically purple or white, and it produces small, dark berries that are toxic to humans and animals. Nigera has hairy stems and leaves with a distinctive, often aromatic scent.

It is often found in disturbed areas, along roadsides, and in gardens where it can spread rapidly.
