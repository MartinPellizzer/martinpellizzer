Nagelia denticulata

Nagelia denticulata, commonly known as the African dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces yellow flowers that develop into fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Nagelia denticulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
