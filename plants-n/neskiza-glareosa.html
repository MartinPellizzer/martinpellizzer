Neskiza glareosa

Neskiza glareosa, commonly known as the glittering neskiza, is a rare flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its striking, silver-blue foliage and clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot. Its unique appearance makes it a subject of interest for botanists and horticulturists alike.

Due to habitat loss, Neskiza glareosa is considered endangered and is protected in several conservation programs.
