Nototriche pellicea

Nototriche pellicea, commonly known as the hairy shield fern, is a member of the Polypodiaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, leathery fronds that are covered in fine, hairy structures. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rock crevices. Its spores are produced in clusters on the underside of its fronds.

Nototriche pellicea is valued for its ornamental appearance and is often found in horticultural collections.
