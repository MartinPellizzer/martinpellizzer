Nymphaea stellata var. versicolor

Nymphaea stellata var. versicolor, commonly known as the star-shaped water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves that often display a mix of green, white, and pink hues. The flowers, which bloom in summer, are typically white with yellow stamens and can vary in color depending on the variety. This plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for pond gardens and water features.
