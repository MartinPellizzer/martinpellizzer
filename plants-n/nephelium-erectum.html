Nephelium erectum

Nephelium erectum, commonly known as the red mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and compound leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by oval, bright red fruits with a thick, sweet pulp. The fruit's pericarp contains a rich array of bioactive compounds, contributing to its reputation as a superfood.

Its cultivation is primarily focused on its ornamental value and medicinal properties.
