Narcissus bulbocodium var. graellsii

Narcissus bulbocodium var. graellsii, commonly known as the Graellsian narcissus, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is a bulbous perennial that produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers with a distinct, sweet fragrance. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its bulbs are adapted to survive dry periods, making it a resilient species in Mediterranean climates.
