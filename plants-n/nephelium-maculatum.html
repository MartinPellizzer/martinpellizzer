Nephelium maculatum

Nephelium maculatum, commonly known as the rambutan, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, spiny fruit covering, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are typically pollinated by insects. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate with a smooth surface.

The tree thrives in warm, humid climates and is cultivated for its edible, sweet, and aromatic fruit.
