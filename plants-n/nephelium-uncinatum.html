Nephelium uncinatum

Nephelium uncinatum, commonly known as rambutan, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It produces hairy, spiky fruits that are distinctive in appearance and often used in culinary and medicinal applications. The plant belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and fragrant flowers. The fruit's exocarp is covered in soft, fleshy spines, which give it its unique texture.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is cultivated for its edible, sweet pulp and ornamental value.
