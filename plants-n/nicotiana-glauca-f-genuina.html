Nicotiana glauca f. genuina

Nicotiana glauca f. genuina, commonly known as the desert tobacco or blueflower tobacco, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant features showy blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as butterflies and bees. Its leaves are ovate and often have a velvety texture, while the stems are hairy and robust.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and adaptability to harsh, dry conditions.
