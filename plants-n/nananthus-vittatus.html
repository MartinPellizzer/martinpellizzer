Nananthus vittatus

Nananthus vittatus, commonly known as the spotted nananthus, is a flowering plant native to specific regions in South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. Its growth habit is usually compact, making it suitable for small garden spaces or container cultivation.

Nananthus vittatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its unique floral display.
