Nephrodium asplenioides var. tenera

Nephrodium asplenioides var. tenera, commonly known as the velvet fern, is a fern species native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety fronds that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests or rocky crevices. Its fronds are usually lance-shaped and have a delicate, feathery texture.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens with high humidity.
