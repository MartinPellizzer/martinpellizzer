Neea amplexicaulis

Neea amplexicaulis, commonly known as the clasping naiad, is a perennial aquatic plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, clasping leaves that wrap around the stems, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats, such as ponds and marshes. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, usually appearing in clusters, and it produces small, dark blue or black berries.

Neea amplexicaulis plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
