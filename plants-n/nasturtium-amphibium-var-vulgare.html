Nasturtium amphibium var. vulgare

Nasturtium amphibium var. vulgare, commonly known as watercress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are often submerged or floating in aquatic environments. The plant produces small yellow flowers with four petals, typical of the mustard family. Its stems and leaves are edible, and it is known for its peppery flavor.

Watercress is often found in streams, ditches, and other freshwater habitats.
