Narcissus pseudonarcissus subsp. leonensis

Narcissus pseudonarcissus subsp.

leonensis, commonly known as the Spanish daffodil, is a subspecies native to southern Europe, particularly Spain and Portugal. It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers with a distinctive white throat and a narrow, elongated corona.

The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It produces a single, upright stem with a basal rosette of leaves.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant spring blooms.
