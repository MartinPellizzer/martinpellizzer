Narcissus blanchardii

Narcissus blanchardii, commonly known as the Canary Island daffodil, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers with a white throat and a single, upright spatulate tepal. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils. It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is known for its resilience in harsh environments.

Narcissus blanchardii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is a symbol of spring in its native habitat.
