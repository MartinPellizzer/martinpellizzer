Nectandra sarcocalyx

Nectandra sarcocalyx, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, reddish-brown heartwood. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth upper surface. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically appearing in axillary or terminal inflorescences.

The species is valued for its durable and fragrant wood, which has been traditionally used in furniture and incense.
