Nepeta spicata var. incana

Nepeta spicata var. incana, commonly known as white catmint, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems covered in soft, grayish-white fuzzy hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, pale blue or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a mild, minty fragrance.

It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and as a source of nectar for pollinators.
