Nephrodium inaequale

Nephrodium inaequale, commonly known as the unequal kidney fern, is a rare and distinctive fern species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, asymmetrical fronds that resemble the shape of a kidney, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest undergrowth, where it thrives in high humidity and partial shade. Its reproductive structures, including sporangia, are clustered along the margins of the fronds, a feature typical of many ferns.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, Nephrodium inaequale is considered a botanical curiosity and is of interest to conservationists and taxonomists.
