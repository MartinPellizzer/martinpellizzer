Navarretia atractyloides var. foliacea

Navarretia atractyloides var. foliacea, commonly known as the leafy navarretia, is a flowering plant native to California.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to full sun. It blooms from late winter to early spring, making it a notable early-season wildflower.

Navarretia atractyloides var. foliacea is often found in chaparral and coastal scrub environments.
