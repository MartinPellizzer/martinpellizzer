Nasturtium officinale var. parvifolium

Nasturtium officinale var. parvifolium, commonly known as the dwarf nasturtium, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its small, rounded leaves and vibrant, edible flowers that come in shades of orange and yellow. The plant typically grows to a height of about 15-30 centimeters, making it suitable for container gardening and small spaces. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

The leaves and flowers are often used in culinary applications, adding a peppery flavor to salads and garnishes.
