Notholaena hirsuta var. nudiuscula

Notholaena hirsuta var. nudiuscula, commonly known as the hairy notholaena, is a variant of the notholaena genus native to specific regions of South America.

It is a small, evergreen shrub characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and compact growth habit. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a leathery texture and a bluish-green hue. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences, followed by small, spherical berries.

This plant is well adapted to moist, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.
