Neomarica

Neomarica, commonly known as the spider orchid, is a genus of flowering plants in the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

These plants are native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas, particularly Central and South America. Neomarica species are characterized by their striking, spider-like flower spikes that often feature vibrant colors such as red, orange, and yellow. They typically grow as terrestrial herbs or epiphytes, with strap-like leaves and showy, fragrant blooms.

The genus includes several species, such as Neomarica spectabilis, which is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value.
