Nepeta hemsleyana

Nepeta hemsleyana, commonly known as Hemsley's catmint, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces spikes of blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is often fragrant and can be used in herbal remedies.

Nepeta hemsleyana is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
