Narcissus tananicus

Narcissus tananicus, commonly known as the Tananica daffodil, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe, particularly in the Iberian Peninsula. The plant is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. Its foliage consists of strap-like leaves that are narrow and grass-like in appearance.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
