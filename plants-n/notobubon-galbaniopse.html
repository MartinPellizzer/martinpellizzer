Notobubon galbaniopse

Notobubon galbaniopse, commonly known as the silverleaf bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, pubescent hairs, and its bell-shaped, pale blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Notobubon galbaniopse is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance and ecological benefits.
