Nicotiana notha

Nicotiana notha, commonly known as the narrow-leaf nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and peppers.

Nicotiana notha is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments.
