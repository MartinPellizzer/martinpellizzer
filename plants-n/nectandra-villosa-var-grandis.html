Nectandra villosa var. grandis

Nectandra villosa var. grandis, commonly known as the broad-leaved nectandra, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. The tree produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by woody fruits containing aromatic seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The plant is valued for its essential oils, which are used in traditional medicine and perfumery.
