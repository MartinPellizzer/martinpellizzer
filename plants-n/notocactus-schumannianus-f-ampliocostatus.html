Notocactus schumannianus f. ampliocostatus

Notocactus schumannianus f. ampliocostatus, commonly known as the broad-costate notocactus, is a unique hybrid of the genus Notocactus, characterized by its distinctive, broad, rib-like structures.

This plant is native to Brazil and is typically found in rocky, well-drained soils. It produces striking, funnel-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often appearing in late spring or early summer. The plant is relatively slow-growing and requires minimal water, making it a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts.

Its ornamental value lies in its vibrant blooms and rugged, sculptural form.
