Nasturtium wallii

Nasturtium wallii, commonly known as the Andes nasturtium, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, edible flowers and heart-shaped leaves, which are often used in culinary applications. The plant produces orange or yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its botanical name honors the English botanist William Wallis, who contributed to the study of South American flora.

Nasturtium wallii thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for both ornamental and edible purposes.
