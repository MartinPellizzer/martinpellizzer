Neomammillaria chionocephala

Neomammillaria chionocephala, commonly known as the white-headed pincushion cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are often covered in white, woolly spines. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its otherwise desolate appearance. Its growth habit is typically solitary or in small clusters, and it is well adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments.

Neomammillaria chionocephala is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements.
