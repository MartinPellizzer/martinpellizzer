Nonatelia apiocarpa

Nonatelia apiocarpa, commonly known as the red-barked tree, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, reddish-barked trunk and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by orange-yellow fruits that contain several seeds.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
