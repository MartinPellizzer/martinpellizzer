Narcissus × neocarpetanus

Narcissus × neocarpetanus, commonly known as the Dutch narcissus, is a hybrid species within the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact, rounded bulbs and is native to the Netherlands, where it was selectively bred for its ornamental value. The plant typically produces 1 to 3 cup-shaped flowers with a white or yellow perianth, often with a distinct green throat. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

This species is also known for its hardy nature and ability to naturalize over time in suitable climates.
