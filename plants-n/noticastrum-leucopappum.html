Noticastrum leucopappum

Noticastrum leucopappum, commonly known as the white pappus cholla, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its branching stems that are covered in small, spiny segments. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by fluffy, white seeds that are dispersed by the wind. Its structure is adapted to arid environments, with reduced leaves and thick stems for water storage.

The white pappus cholla is a hardy plant that plays a role in providing habitat and food for desert wildlife.
