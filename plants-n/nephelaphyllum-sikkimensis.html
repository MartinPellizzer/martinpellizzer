Nephelaphyllum sikkimensis

Nephelaphyllum sikkimensis, commonly known as the Sikkim snowbell, is a rare flowering plant native to the Himalayan region.

It is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows at high altitudes, often in rocky or mountainous terrain. Its leaves are simple and alternate, with a leathery texture adapted to cold climates.

The Sikkim snowbell is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often found in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
