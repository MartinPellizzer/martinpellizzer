Nicotiana pilosa

Nicotiana pilosa, commonly known as the hairy nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming in the evening. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of climates.

Nicotiana pilosa is also known for its mild nicotine content, making it a plant of interest in both botanical and agricultural studies.
