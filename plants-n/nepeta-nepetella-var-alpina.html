Nepeta nepetella var. alpina

Nepeta nepetella var. alpina, commonly known as alpine catmint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, fragrant blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, velvety texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.

Its aromatic foliage and long blooming period attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
