Noaea hermonis

Noaea hermonis, commonly known as the Jordanian sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of North Africa.

It is a low-growing annual herb that thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom during the spring and early summer. Its leaves are typically narrow and fleshy, adapted to conserve water in dry conditions.

Noaea hermonis is often found in sandy or gravelly soils, making it a characteristic species of desert and coastal habitats.
