Nepeta podostachys var. hypoluciphylla

Nepeta podostachys var. hypoluciphylla, commonly known as the slender catmint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine, hairy foliage. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers arranged in whorled clusters along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

Its aromatic leaves and showy flowers make it an attractive and low-maintenance addition to gardens.
