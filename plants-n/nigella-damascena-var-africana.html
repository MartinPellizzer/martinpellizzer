Nigella damascena var. africana

Nigella damascena var. africana, commonly known as African black cumin, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean and parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, pale yellow to brown flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces small, black, oil-rich seeds that are used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, requiring minimal water once established.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ornamental and medicinal purposes.
