Neurelmis pumila

Neurelmis pumila, commonly known as the dwarf orchid, is a small, terrestrial orchid native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant has a delicate, compact appearance with narrow leaves and a single, slender flower spike. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, with a subtle fragrance.

Neurelmis pumila is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
