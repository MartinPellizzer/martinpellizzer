Nephelium fuscatum

Nephelium fuscatum, commonly known as the black mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is closely related to the common mangosteen (Nephelium lappaceum). The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, clustered white flowers that produce edible fruits. The fruit has a thick, leathery rind and a segmented, juicy pulp with a sweet and tart flavor.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and its potential medicinal properties.
