Nymphaea odorata var. luciana

Nymphaea odorata var. luciana, commonly known as the sacred lotus of Southeast Asia, is a water-living plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes. The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves that float on the water's surface, while its striking pink or white flowers bloom above the water. It is known for its aromatic fragrance and has been cultivated for centuries for ornamental and religious purposes.

The species is also valued for its cultural significance in various traditional practices.
