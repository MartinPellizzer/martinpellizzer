Nepeta amboinica

Nepeta amboinica, commonly known as the sacred catmint, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their calming properties.

Nepeta amboinica is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soil.
