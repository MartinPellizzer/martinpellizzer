Nepenthes phyllamphora var. speciosa

Nepenthes phyllamphora var. speciosa, commonly known as the showy pitcher plant, is a unique carnivorous plant native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated pitchers that are often adorned with vibrant red and yellow markings. The plant's leaves are modified into specialized structures that secrete digestive enzymes to trap and digest insects. Its growth habit is typically epiphytic, meaning it grows on other plants without being parasitic.

This variety is highly sought after by horticulturists for its ornamental value and ecological interest.
