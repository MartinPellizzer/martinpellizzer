Nicotiana alipes

Nicotiana alipes, commonly known as the dwarf tobacco plant, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its small, erect stature and trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters and produces vibrant purple or pink flowers. It is known for its aromatic leaves and has been used in traditional medicine for its mild psychoactive properties.

Due to its ornamental value and unique characteristics, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
