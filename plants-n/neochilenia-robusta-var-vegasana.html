Neochilenia robusta var. vegasana

Neochilenia robusta var. vegasana, commonly known as the Desert Willow, is a shrub native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its woody stems, opposite leaves, and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant is adapted to dry climates and can thrive in well-drained soils. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Neochilenia robusta var. vegasana is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
