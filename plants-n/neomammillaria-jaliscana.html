Neomammillaria jaliscana

Neomammillaria jaliscana, commonly known as the Jalisco mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are typically ribbed and covered in white to pale blue spines. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its green body. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care, it is a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts.
