Nispero

Nispero, commonly known as the custard apple, is a tropical fruit-bearing plant native to regions of South America, Southeast Asia, and the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Anacardiaceae and is characterized by its rough, spiny exterior and fragrant, white flowers. The fruit is typically oblong or round, with a thick, leathery skin that encases a soft, creamy pulp containing numerous seeds. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it is often cultivated for both its ornamental value and edible fruit.
