Nananthus rubrolineatus

Nananthus rubrolineatus, commonly known as the red-lined nananthus, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking red veins that run through its broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate to flourish.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
