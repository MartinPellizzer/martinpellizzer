Notocactus fuscus var. longispinus

Notocactus fuscus var. longispinus, commonly known as the long-spined cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are covered in dense, spiny areoles, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are typically borne on the upper parts of the stems. It is a slow-growing cactus that requires well-draining soil and minimal water to thrive.

Due to its unique features, it is a popular choice for collectors and those interested in succulent gardening.
