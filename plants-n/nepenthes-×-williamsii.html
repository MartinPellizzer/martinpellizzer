Nepenthes × williamsii

Nepenthes × williamsii, commonly known as the hybrid pitcher plant, is a tropical climbing vine native to Borneo.

It is a natural hybrid between Nepenthes mirabilis and Nepenthes rafflesiana, exhibiting a combination of traits from both parent species. The plant produces large, elongated pitcher-shaped leaves that secrete a digestive fluid to trap and digest insects. Its pitchers can reach lengths of up to 30 centimeters, making it one of the larger species in the Nepenthaceae family.

This plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.
