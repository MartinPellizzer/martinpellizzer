Nepeta bracteata

Nepeta bracteata, commonly known as catmint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pale blue or lavender flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often aromatic and can be used in herbal teas or as a companion plant in gardens.

Nepeta bracteata is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
