Nuphar japonica subvar. rubrotincta

Nuphar japonica subvar. rubrotincta, commonly known as the Japanese water lily, is a herbaceous aquatic plant native to East Asia.

It features showy, floating yellow flowers with a distinctive red throat, which attract pollinators such as bees and beetles. The plant has broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically green on top and lighter in color underneath. Its roots are rhizomatous, allowing it to spread vegetatively in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
