Nania canescens

Nania canescens, commonly known as the silverleaf lichen, is a foliose lichen that thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It has a distinctive grayish-white to pale green thallus with a powdery coating of calcium carbonate. The plant lacks true roots, stems, and leaves, instead consisting of a symbiotic partnership between a fungus and algae or cyanobacteria. It is often found on tree bark, rocks, and soil surfaces in temperate regions.

Due to its slow growth and sensitivity to environmental changes, it serves as an indicator species for air quality and ecosystem health.
