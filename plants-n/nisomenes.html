Nisomenes

Nisomenes, commonly known as the "false dandelion," is a genus of flowering plants in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

It consists of several species that are native to various regions of the Americas. These plants typically have basal leaves that are either lobed or entire, with hairy surfaces. The flowers are composite, consisting of both ray and disk florets, often yellow in color.

Nisomenes species are often found in dry, open habitats and are sometimes considered weeds in agricultural areas.
