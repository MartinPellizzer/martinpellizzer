Norantea weddelliana

Norantea weddelliana, commonly known as the Weddellian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the cold regions of southern South America.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, often blooming in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky slopes. Its leaves are broad and slightly hairy, adapted to retain moisture in its native habitat.

Norantea weddelliana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with similar environmental conditions.
