Nicotiana tabacum var. lanceolata

Nicotiana tabacum var. lanceolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf tobacco, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and produces striking, trumpet-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its vibrant blooms and attractive foliage. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for both gardeners and horticultural enthusiasts.

The plant is also notable for its historical use in traditional medicine and as a source of nicotine.
