Nymphaea ampla f. distans

Nymphaea ampla f. distans, commonly known as the distant water lily, is a aquatic plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that float on the water's surface, supported by long, slender petioles. The flowers are typically white or pale pink, with a subtle fragrance, and bloom during the day. This variety is noted for its distinct floral structure and its ability to thrive in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats.

It plays an important ecological role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various water organisms.
