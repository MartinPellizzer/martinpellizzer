Nyctocereus serpentinus subsp. splendens

Nyctocereus serpentinus subsp.

splendens, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico. It is characterized by its tall, columnar stems and is known for its striking white flowers that open at night.

The plant produces large, showy blooms with a strong, sweet fragrance, which attract nocturnal pollinators such as bats and moths. It is a slow-growing plant that thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water.

The subspecies splendens is particularly noted for its dramatic floral display, making it a popular choice for collectors and gardeners.
