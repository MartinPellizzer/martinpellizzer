Neomolina marcetiifolia

Neomolina marcetiifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf neomolina, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically in shades of white and yellow. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
