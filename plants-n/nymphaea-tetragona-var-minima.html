Nymphaea tetragona var. minima

Nymphaea tetragona var. minima, commonly known as the dwarf water lily, is a compact aquatic plant native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, floating leaves that are typically less than 10 centimeters in diameter. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that open in the early morning and close by midday. It thrives in shallow, still waters with moderate sunlight and nutrient-rich soil.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for pond gardens and water feature landscaping.
