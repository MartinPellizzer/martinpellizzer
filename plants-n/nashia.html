Nashia

Nashia, commonly known as the "blueweed," is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in composite heads. The leaves are usually alternate, linear to lanceolate, and may have a rough or hairy texture. Nashia species are typically found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

They are often used in herbal medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
