Nasturtium reichenbachii

Nasturtium reichenbachii, commonly known as the Brazilian watercress, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its bright green, succulent leaves and vibrant yellow or orange flowers. The plant thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found near streams or in wetlands. Its leaves are edible and have a peppery, spicy flavor similar to watercress.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to purify water in natural habitats.
