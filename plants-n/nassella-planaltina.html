Nassella planaltina

Nassella planaltina, commonly known as the plains needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, needle-like leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers.

Nassella planaltina is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
