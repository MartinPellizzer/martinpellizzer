Nephrodium phegopteris

Nephrodium phegopteris, commonly known as the mountain fern, is a species of fern native to the temperate regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its large, bipinnate fronds that are typically dark green and have a leathery texture. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forests and rocky areas. Its reproductive structures include sporangia that produce spores for propagation.

Nephrodium phegopteris plays a significant role in its ecosystem by contributing to soil stability and providing habitat for various microorganisms.
