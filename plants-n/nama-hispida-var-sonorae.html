Nama hispida var. sonorae

Nama hispida var. sonorae, commonly known as the Sonoran sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is a low-growing annual herb with small, hairy leaves and clusters of pale yellow or white flowers. The plant has a compact growth habit, often forming dense mats that help stabilize sandy soils. It is adapted to hot, dry environments and is drought-tolerant.

Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
