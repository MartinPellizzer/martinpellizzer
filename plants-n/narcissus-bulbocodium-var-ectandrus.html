Narcissus bulbocodium var. ectandrus

Narcissus bulbocodium var. ectandrus, commonly known as the Spanish daffodil, is a rare and striking plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and a single, showy, trumpet-shaped flower that is typically yellow or orange. The plant is a bulbous perennial, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a vibrant touch to gardens and natural habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a valuable species for botanical study and conservation efforts.
