Narcissus × splendidus

Narcissus × splendidus, commonly known as the Splendid Daffodil, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Narcissus bulbocodium and Narcissus tazetta.

It is characterized by its large, showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

The flowers bloom in early spring, adding a vibrant splash of color to the landscape.
