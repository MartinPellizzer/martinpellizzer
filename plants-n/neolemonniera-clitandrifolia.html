Neolemonniera clitandrifolia

Neolemonniera clitandrifolia, commonly known as the creeping lemon tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features dense, aromatic foliage with elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by yellow-orange fruits resembling small lemons. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species in both horticulture and pharmacological research.
