Neotinea lactea subsp. corsica

Neotinea lactea subsp.

corsica, commonly known as the Corsican milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Corsica. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or limestone soils.

The plant features slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique adaptations to its environment.
