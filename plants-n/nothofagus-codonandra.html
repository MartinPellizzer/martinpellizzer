Nothofagus codonandra

Nothofagus codonandra, commonly known as the silver beech, is a species of evergreen tree native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Nothofagaceae family and is characterized by its silver-gray bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by edible, chestnut-like fruits. It thrives in temperate rainforests and is adapted to moist, cool climates.

Nothofagus codonandra plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
