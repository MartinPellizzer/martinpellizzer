Nepenthes × hybrida var. maculata

Nepenthes × hybrida var. maculata, commonly known as the spotted pitcher plant, is a hybrid species within the genus Nepenthes, known for its unique carnivorous traits.

This plant produces specialized pitcher-shaped leaves that secrete a digestive enzyme-laden fluid to trap and digest insects. The varietal name "maculata" refers to the distinctive red or orange spots that appear on the leaf surface, often contrasting with the green background. It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological interest, it is often cultivated by enthusiasts and researchers alike.
