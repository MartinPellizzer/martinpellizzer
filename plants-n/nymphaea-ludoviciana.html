Nymphaea ludoviciana

Nymphaea ludoviciana, commonly known as the Louisiana water lily, is a perennial aquatic plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features broad, heart-shaped leaves that float on the water's surface, supported by long, slender petioles. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, opening in the early morning and closing by midday. Its roots are anchored in the muddy substrate, while its rhizomes spread horizontally to form colonies.

Nymphaea ludoviciana plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various water organisms.
