Nothaphoebe fargesii

Nothaphoebe fargesii, commonly known as the Farges' beech, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of western China.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple leaves that are elliptic to ovate in shape. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mixed forests at elevations ranging from 1,500 to 3,000 meters.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
