Nesaea mossiensis

Nesaea mossiensis, commonly known as the mossy nesaea, is a rare and delicate moss species native to specific regions of China.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as rocky crevices and forest floors, where it forms dense, carpet-like mats. The plant has a low-growing structure with closely packed, overlapping leaves that are typically dark green and somewhat curled. Its reproductive structures, known as sporophytes, are small and inconspicuous, producing spores that aid in its propagation.

Due to habitat loss and environmental changes, Nesaea mossiensis is considered endangered, making conservation efforts crucial for its survival.
