Northia vitiensis

Northia vitiensis, commonly known as the Vitiensis orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Africa, particularly in Uganda and Tanzania.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, yellow, and orange. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and rain, which is typical for many orchid species.

Northia vitiensis is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
