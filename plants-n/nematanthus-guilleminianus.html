Nematanthus guilleminianus

Nematanthus guilleminianus, commonly known as the "Mexican flame flower," is a member of the Gesneriaceae family, native to Central America.

It is a trailing perennial that produces vibrant, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture and a distinctive, showy bract that resembles a petal. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as a decorative plant in hanging baskets or containers.

Due to its ornamental value, it is popular among gardeners seeking colorful, low-maintenance foliage plants.
