Nicoraepoa subenervis

Nicoraepoa subenervis, commonly known as the slender leaf grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of southern Africa.

It typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, with slender, upright stems that are often slightly hairy. The leaves are narrow, linear, and have a distinct lack of visible veins, which is reflected in its scientific name "subenervis.". Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it blooms during the spring and early summer.

This plant is often found in grasslands and along the edges of woodlands, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
