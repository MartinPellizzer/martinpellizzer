Neriandra suberecta

Neriandra suberecta, commonly known as the upright neriandra, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
