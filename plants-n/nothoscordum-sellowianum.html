Nothoscordum sellowianum

Nothoscordum sellowianum, commonly known as the Brazilian garlic or golden garlic, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its strap-like, grass-like leaves and clusters of yellow to orange flowers. The plant produces small, spherical bulbs that are often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary spice. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its unique appearance and aromatic properties make it both visually appealing and practically useful.
