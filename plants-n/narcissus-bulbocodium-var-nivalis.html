Narcissus bulbocodium var. nivalis

Narcissus bulbocodium var. nivalis, commonly known as the snow narcissus, is a perennial plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It produces small, white flowers with a yellow center, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has a bulbous root system that allows it to survive harsh winter conditions. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous habitats.

Due to its delicate appearance and ecological significance, it is considered a species of conservation concern in some regions.
