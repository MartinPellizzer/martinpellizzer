Nymphaea tetragona subsp. leibergii

Nymphaea tetragona subsp.

leibergii, commonly known as the Leiberg's water lily, is a aquatic plant native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan. It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that float on the water surface, supported by long, spongy petioles.

The flowers are typically white or pale pink, with a prominent yellow stamen, and they bloom during the summer months. This subspecies is often found in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats such as ponds and lakes.

It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
