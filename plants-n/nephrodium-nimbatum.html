Nephrodium nimbatum

Nephrodium nimbatum, commonly known as the cloud fern, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Nephrodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, frond-like leaves that resemble ferns. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in mountainous regions. Its reproductive structures are unique, featuring small, spore-bearing capsules that develop on the undersides of its leaves.

The cloud fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
