Nymphaea odorata

Nymphaea odorata, commonly known as the sacred lotus, is a water-living plant native to Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that bloom on long, slender stalks above the water surface. The plant has broad, waxy leaves that float on the water's surface, aiding in photosynthesis. Its roots grow in the mud at the bottom of ponds and lakes.

Nymphaea odorata is known for its symbolic and cultural significance in various Eastern traditions.
