Namibia

Namibia, commonly known as the "Land of the Desert," is a country in southern Africa characterized by its diverse and unique botanical landscape.

The region is home to a variety of succulent plants, including the iconic Namib Desert's welwitschia mirabilis, which can live for over a millennium. The coastal areas feature lush fynbos vegetation, while the interior is dominated by arid shrublands and grasslands adapted to the harsh climate. Namibia's flora includes over 5,000 species, many of which are endemic to the region.

These plants play a crucial role in the country's ecosystems and are vital for both environmental balance and local communities.
