Nigella taubertii

Nigella taubertii, commonly known as the Persian cowpea, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, black, peppery seeds that are often used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and containers.

Nigella taubertii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract beneficial insects.
