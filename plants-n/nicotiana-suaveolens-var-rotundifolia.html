Nicotiana suaveolens var. rotundifolia

Nicotiana suaveolens var. rotundifolia, commonly known as the sweet tobacco or nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, fragrant leaves and flowers that bloom in shades of pink and purple. The plant has rounded, broad leaves and produces clusters of small, showy flowers. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.

This variety is also noted for its nocturnal blooming habit, attracting night-active pollinators.
