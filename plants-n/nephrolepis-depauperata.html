Nephrolepis depauperata

Nephrolepis depauperata, commonly known as the poor fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect fronds that are typically less than 30 centimeters in length. The plant features small, lance-shaped leaves arranged in a tight spiral, giving it a compact appearance. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Nephrolepis depauperata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
