Nipponocalamus ramosissimus

Nipponocalamus ramosissimus, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or mountain bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and feathery leaves. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides. It is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense clumps.
