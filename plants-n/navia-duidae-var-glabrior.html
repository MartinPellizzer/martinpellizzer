Navia duidae var. glabrior

Navia duidae var. glabrior, commonly known as the smooth navia, is a variant of the navia duidae species native to specific regions in South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous leaves and stems, distinguishing it from other varieties within the same species. The plant typically thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in the understory of tropical forests. Its foliage is rich in chlorophyll, supporting its role as a photosynthetic contributor to the ecosystem.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat preferences, it is of interest to botanists studying plant adaptation and biodiversity.
