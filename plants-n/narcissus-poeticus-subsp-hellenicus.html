Narcissus poeticus subsp. hellenicus

Narcissus poeticus subsp.

hellenicus, commonly known as the Hellenic daffodil, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to white.

The flowers typically bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. This subspecies is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a resilient addition to temperate climates.
