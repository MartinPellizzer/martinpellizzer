Narcissus tazetta subsp. chinensis

Narcissus tazetta subsp.

chinensis, commonly known as the Chinese daffodil, is a bulbous perennial plant native to eastern Asia. It produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, often with a white or green center.

The plant typically blooms in early spring and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for its vibrant, early-blooming flowers.
