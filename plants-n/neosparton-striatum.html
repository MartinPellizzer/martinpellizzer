Neosparton striatum

Neosparton striatum, commonly known as the stripeleaf spartium, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, creeping stems and small, lance-shaped leaves with distinctive white or pale green stripes. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Neosparton striatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and low maintenance requirements.
