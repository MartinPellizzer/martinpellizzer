Nepeta juncea subsp. heterotricha

Nepeta juncea subsp.

heterotricha, commonly known as catmint, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its aromatic, hairy leaves and spikes of blue to purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and thrives in full sun. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like mint and lavender. Nepeta juncea subsp.

heterotricha is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in herbal remedies.
