Nephrodium procurrens

Nephrodium procurrens, commonly known as the creeping nephrolepis, is a fern native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It features a rhizomatous growth habit, with long, creeping stems that root at the nodes. The fronds are pinnately compound, with lanceolate to ovate leaflets that are usually dark green and glossy. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in horticulture.
