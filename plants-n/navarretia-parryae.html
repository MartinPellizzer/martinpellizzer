Navarretia parryae

Navarretia parryae, commonly known as Parry's fairyshade, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate, white or pale purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in open, sunny habitats. It produces a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and has a short, flowering stem.

Navarretia parryae is a perennial species that blooms in early spring, adding a subtle ornamental value to its natural environment.
