Notholaena californica subsp. leucophylla

Notholaena californica subsp.

leucophylla, commonly known as the white-leafed field sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to California. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powder.

This subspecies typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, spike-like flower clusters.

The plant plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
