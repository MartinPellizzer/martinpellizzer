Nassella nubicola

Nassella nubicola, commonly known as the desert needlegrass, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in open, dry habitats such as plains, deserts, and rocky slopes. The plant produces slender, spiky inflorescences that are composed of numerous small, spikelet-like clusters. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-gray hue that helps reduce water loss in hot, dry environments.

Nassella nubicola plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife.
