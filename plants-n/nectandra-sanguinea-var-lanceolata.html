Nectandra sanguinea var. lanceolata

Nectandra sanguinea var. lanceolata, commonly known as the red sandalwood or bloodwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark reddish-brown heartwood, which is highly valued for its durability and aromatic properties. The leaves are alternate, simple, and lanceolate in shape, with a glossy surface. The flowers are small and fragrant, typically white or pale yellow, and the fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds.

This species is often found in lowland rainforests and is known for its ecological and economic significance.
