Narcissus × mikelii-lordae

Narcissus × mikelii-lordae, commonly known as the Mikelii-Lordae daffodil, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Narcissus tazetta and Narcissus poeticus.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers with a trumpet-shaped corona and bright yellow petals. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–50 cm and prefers well-drained soil in full sun. It is a hardy perennial that blooms in early spring, adding vibrant color to gardens.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
