Nuxia oppositifolia

Nuxia oppositifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved kalahari grass, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, opposite leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom during the rainy season. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in sandy or rocky soils.

Nuxia oppositifolia plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for grazing animals in its native habitat.
