Nigrina ovatum

Nigrina ovatum, commonly known as the black-eyed Susan, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its bright yellow petals surrounding a dark, central disk, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in open fields and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soil. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Nigrina ovatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
