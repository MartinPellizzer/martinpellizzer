Neonauclea cordatula

Neonauclea cordatula, commonly known as the heartleaf neonauclea, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, usually white or pale pink, which bloom in the spring. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds.

Neonauclea cordatula is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil.
