Nothoscordum serenense

Nothoscordum serenense, commonly known as the serenense garlic flower, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in regions of Ecuador and Peru.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, umbellate inflorescences that display vibrant red and orange hues. The plant features strap-like, narrow leaves and produces small, fleshy bulbs underground, similar to true garlic. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is occasionally cultivated by gardeners interested in exotic flora.
