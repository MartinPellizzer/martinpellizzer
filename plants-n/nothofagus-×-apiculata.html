Nothofagus × apiculata

Nothofagus × apiculata, commonly known as the mountain beech or silver beech, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Nothofagus truncata and Nothofagus cunninghamii.

It is native to New Zealand and is characterized by its distinctive silver-gray foliage and smooth, often fissured bark. The plant typically grows in cool, moist environments, such as temperate rainforests, and can reach heights of up to 20 meters. Its leaves are alternate, lanceolate, and have a pointed tip, while its cones are small and woody.

Nothofagus × apiculata plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing habitat and food for various native wildlife species.
