Nemesia affinis

Nemesia affinis, commonly known as the common nemesia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Primulaceae and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of small, brightly colored flowers. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Nemesia affinis is also known for its ability to bloom throughout the growing season, adding continuous color to gardens.
