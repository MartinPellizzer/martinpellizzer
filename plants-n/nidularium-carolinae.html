Nidularium carolinae

Nidularium carolinae, commonly known as Carolina groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disc flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered with fine hairs.

The showy yellow flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
