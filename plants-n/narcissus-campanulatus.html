Narcissus campanulatus

Narcissus campanulatus, commonly known as the windmill lily, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically blooms in late summer and early autumn, producing a distinctive, fragrant aroma. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
