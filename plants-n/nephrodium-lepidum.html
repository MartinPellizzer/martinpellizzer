Nephrodium lepidum

Nephrodium lepidum, commonly known as the silver fern, is a species of fern native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its distinctive fronds that resemble a fern with a central stem and radiating pinnae. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its leaves are covered in a fine, silvery hair that gives it its common name.

Nephrodium lepidum plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various invertebrates.
