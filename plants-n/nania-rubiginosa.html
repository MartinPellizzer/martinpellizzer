Nania rubiginosa

Nania rubiginosa, commonly known as the rusty nania, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to red, often with a distinctive rusty or reddish hue on the petals. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Nania rubiginosa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
