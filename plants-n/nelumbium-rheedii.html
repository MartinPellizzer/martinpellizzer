Nelumbium rheedii

Nelumbium rheedii, commonly known as the Chinese water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to East Asia.

It features large, showy white flowers with a central yellow stamen, which bloom on long, erect stalks above the water surface. The plant has broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically floating or partially submerged. Its roots are anchored in the mud at the bottom of ponds and lakes.

Nelumbium rheedii is valued for its ornamental beauty and traditional uses in Chinese culture.
