Nymphaea spiralis

Nymphaea spiralis, commonly known as the spiral water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, floating leaves that are often veined in a spiral pattern, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white, pink, and red. Nymphaea spiralis thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments.

Its unique floral structure and ornamental value make it a popular choice for water gardens and ponds.
