Narcissus cyclamineus var. latipetalus

Narcissus cyclamineus var. latipetalus, commonly known as the broad-petaled cyclamen narcissus, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and yellow. The plant produces narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.

This variety is known for its resilience and ability to bloom reliably in cooler climates.
