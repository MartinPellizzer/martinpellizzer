Narcissus linnaeanus var. xantheus

Narcissus linnaeanus var. xantheus, commonly known as the yellow daffodil, is a hybrid variety of the genus Narcissus.

It is characterized by its bright yellow petals and a narrow, trumpet-shaped corona. This plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. It is typically grown as a garden ornamental for its early spring blooms.

The flowers are usually 5 to 7 cm in diameter, with a strong, sweet fragrance.
