Neoachmandra deltoidea

Neoachmandra deltoidea, commonly known as the delta fern, is a rare and delicate fern species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feather-like fronds that give it a soft, ethereal appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the undersides of the fronds, a common feature in ferns.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Neoachmandra deltoidea is considered a valuable subject for botanical research and conservation efforts.
