Nepeta argolica

Nepeta argolica, commonly known as the Argolic catmint, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage has a strong mint-like scent, which is often used in traditional herbal remedies.

Nepeta argolica thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens or wildflower meadows.
