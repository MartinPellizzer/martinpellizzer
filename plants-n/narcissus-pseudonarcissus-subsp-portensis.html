Narcissus pseudonarcissus subsp. portensis

Narcissus pseudonarcissus subsp.

portensis, commonly known as the Portuguese daffodil, is a subspecies native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is a bulbous perennial plant that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge in early spring.

The subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
