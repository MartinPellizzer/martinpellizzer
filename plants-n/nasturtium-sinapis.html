Nasturtium sinapis

Nasturtium sinapis, commonly known as the common nasturtium, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant, bright orange or yellow flowers and heart-shaped leaves. The plant belongs to the family Brassicaceae, which includes other well-known plants like cabbages and mustard. Its flowers are edible and often used in salads or as a garnish, while the leaves can be consumed as a peppery vegetable.
