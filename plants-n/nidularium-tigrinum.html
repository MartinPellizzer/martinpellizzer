Nidularium tigrinum

Nidularium tigrinum, commonly known as the tiger orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful inflorescences that resemble small, intricate flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are often arranged in a rosette pattern, forming a cup-like structure that collects rainwater.

The flowers, which bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow, are highly ornamental and attract various pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.
