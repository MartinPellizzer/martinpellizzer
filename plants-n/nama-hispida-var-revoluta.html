Nama hispida var. revoluta

Nama hispida var. revoluta, commonly known as the African dandelion, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features a thick, fleshy stem with a hairy surface, and its leaves are typically broad and spoon-shaped. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by fluffy, white seed heads. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and resilience make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
