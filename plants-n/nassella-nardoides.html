Nassella nardoides

Nassella nardoides, commonly known as silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It features fine, silver-gray leaves and produces fluffy, feathery seed heads that are highly ornamental. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping. Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for low-maintenance landscapes.

Botanically, it belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth and distinctive appearance.
