Nepenthes fallax

Nepenthes fallax, commonly known as the Bornean pitcher plant, is a carnivorous plant native to Borneo.

It is characterized by its unique pitcher-shaped leaves that secrete a digestive fluid to trap and digest insects. The plant's pitchers vary in color, often displaying shades of red, yellow, or green, depending on environmental conditions. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires high levels of moisture and nutrient-poor soil.

Nepenthes fallax is notable for its adaptability and has become a popular subject in both botanical research and horticultural cultivation.
