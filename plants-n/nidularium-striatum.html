Nidularium striatum

Nidularium striatum, commonly known as the striped flax lily, is a striking member of the Asphodelaceae family.

It is native to southern Africa and features tall, grass-like leaves with distinctive white and green stripes. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom in summer. Its structure is similar to that of a lily, with a central stem and clustered blooms.

Nidularium striatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
