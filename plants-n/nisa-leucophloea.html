Nisa leucophloea

Nisa leucophloea, commonly known as the white phloem tree, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark that gives it its common name. The tree has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a waxy coating. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically white or pale yellow, and it produces woody pods containing seeds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
