Nama longifolia

Nama longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved nama, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that are typically long and narrow. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish or white in color. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grassland and shrubland habitats.

Nama longifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
