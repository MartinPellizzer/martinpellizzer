Nephelaphyllum cristatum

Nephelaphyllum cristatum, commonly known as the Crowned Cloud Plant, is a unique species native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-like growth pattern and striking, velvety leaves that often display a bluish or silver hue. The plant produces small, pale flowers that bloom in clusters, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in cool, moist environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in the wild.
