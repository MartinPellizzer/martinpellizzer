Nothofagus obliqua subsp. andina

Nothofagus obliqua subsp.

andina, commonly known as the Andean nothofagus, is a species of evergreen tree native to the temperate rainforests of southern South America. It belongs to the Nothofagus genus, which is part of the beech family (Fagaceae).

This subspecies is characterized by its lanceolate leaves, which are typically dark green and have a glossy surface. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by edible, chestnut-like fruits.

The plant is adapted to high-altitude environments and plays a crucial role in the ecological balance of its native habitat.
