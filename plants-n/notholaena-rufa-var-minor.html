Notholaena rufa var. minor

Notholaena rufa var. minor, commonly known as the red-tipped spurge, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a reddish or purplish tint on the underside. The plant produces clusters of tiny, yellowish-green flowers that are borne on slender, erect stems. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
