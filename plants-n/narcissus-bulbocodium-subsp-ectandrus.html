Narcissus bulbocodium subsp. ectandrus

Narcissus bulbocodium subsp.

ectandrus, commonly known as the Corsican daffodil, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, yellow flowers with a distinctive, narrow corona.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its bulbs are adapted for survival in arid conditions, often entering dormancy during dry periods.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
