Notocactus uebelmannianus var. pleiocephalus

Notocactus uebelmannianus var. pleiocephalus, commonly known as the pleiocephalus notocactus, is a rare and striking cactus native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its unique, multi-headed stem structure, which can produce several heads that grow outward from a central trunk. The plant features spiny, ribbed stems that are often covered in a layer of whitish, powdery bloom, which helps to reduce water loss. It produces vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its otherwise desolate appearance.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is a sought-after species among cactus enthusiasts and conservationists.
