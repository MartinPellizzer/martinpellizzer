Neogaya albomarginata

Neogaya albomarginata, commonly known as the white-margin tamarind.

It is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves with white margins and yellowish-green stipules. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences.

The fruit is a pod containing several seeds, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.
