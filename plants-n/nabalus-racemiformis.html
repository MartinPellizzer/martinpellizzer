Nabalus racemiformis

Nabalus racemiformis, commonly known as the raceme bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright, branching stems that bear clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue to violet. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, adapting to its arid environment.

Nabalus racemiformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
