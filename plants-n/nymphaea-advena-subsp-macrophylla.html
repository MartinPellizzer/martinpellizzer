Nymphaea advena subsp. macrophylla

Nymphaea advena subsp.

macrophylla, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to parts of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in diameter, providing a striking visual display on water surfaces.

The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white, with a distinctive yellow center. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats, often forming dense floating mats.

Its extensive leaf surface helps in oxygenating the water and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
