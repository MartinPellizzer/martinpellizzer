Nolana paradoxa subsp. atriplicifolia

Nolana paradoxa subsp.

atriplicifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf nolana, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile. It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, dense hairs.

The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
