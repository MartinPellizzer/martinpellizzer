Neoshirakia

Neoshirakia, commonly known as the Japanese bellflower, is a genus of flowering plants native to East Asia.

It typically features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plants are herbaceous and often have hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Neoshirakia species are often cultivated for their ornamental value in gardens.

They prefer well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight.
