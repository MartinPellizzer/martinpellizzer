Nephrodium serrulatum var. angustum

Nephrodium serrulatum var. angustum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf water fern, is a variant of the water fern species native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its narrow, serrated leaves that are typically submerged or floating in aquatic environments. The plant exhibits a delicate, feathery appearance with fronds that are often divided into small, finger-like segments. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing sediment and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.

Its botanical features make it an important species in the study of aquatic flora and ecosystem dynamics.
