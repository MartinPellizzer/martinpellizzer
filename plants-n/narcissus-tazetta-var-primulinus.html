Narcissus tazetta var. primulinus

Narcissus tazetta var. primulinus, commonly known as the golden bulb narcissus, is a flowering plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, rounded bulbs and its ability to produce multiple flower stems from a single bulb. The plant typically blooms in early spring, producing clusters of bright yellow flowers with a cup-shaped corona. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from the base of the plant.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, long-lasting blooms.
