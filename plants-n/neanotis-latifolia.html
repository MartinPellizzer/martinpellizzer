Neanotis latifolia

Neanotis latifolia, commonly known as broadleaf neanotis, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, often blooming in the spring. Neanotis latifolia thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticultural and ecological studies.
