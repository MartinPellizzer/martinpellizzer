Neowedia speciosa

Neowedia speciosa, commonly known as the golden bell tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring. The plant has compound leaves that are alternate and pinnately arranged, with elongated leaflets. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Neowedia speciosa is also valued for its ability to provide shade and its role in supporting local wildlife.
