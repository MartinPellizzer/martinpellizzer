Nymphaea maximiliani

Nymphaea maximiliani, commonly known as the African white lotus, is a water lily native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, white, showy flowers that bloom in the early morning and close by midday. The plant has broad, floating leaves that provide habitat for aquatic life. Its roots grow in the muddy bottom of ponds and lakes, anchoring the plant in place.

Nymphaea maximiliani is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance and ecological benefits.
