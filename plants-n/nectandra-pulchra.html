Nectandra pulchra

Nectandra pulchra, commonly known as the beautiful nectandra, is a tropical tree native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, white, clustered flowers that are often pollinated by insects. Its fruit is a dark, fleshy berry that contains several seeds.

Nectandra pulchra is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine by indigenous communities.
