Neophloga catatiana

Neophloga catatiana, commonly known as the catatonic flame vine, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from deep red to orange, often resembling flames. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Neophloga catatiana is also known for its ability to climb using tendrils, making it a popular choice for trellises and vertical gardens.
