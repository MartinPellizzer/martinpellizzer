Narcissus subalbidus

Narcissus subalbidus, commonly known as the white narcissus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, white, cup-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils in woodland or open areas. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming a basal rosette.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
