Nautochilus labiatus

Nautochilus labiatus, commonly known as the pink-flowered orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically range in color from pink to purple. The plant features a rhizomatous root system and possesses lanceolate, glossy leaves that are often arranged in a basal rosette. Its flowers are borne on erect inflorescences and are known for their intricate, lip-like structures that aid in pollination by insects.

Nautochilus labiatus thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid tropical forests.
