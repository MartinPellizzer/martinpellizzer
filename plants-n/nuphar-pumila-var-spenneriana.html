Nuphar pumila var. spenneriana

Nuphar pumila var. spenneriana, commonly known as the dwarf yellow water lily, is a aquatic plant characterized by its small, floating leaves and showy yellow flowers.

It typically grows in shallow freshwater habitats such as ponds and marshes. The leaves are rounded and often have a waxy surface, while the flowers are borne on long, slender stalks. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in still or slow-moving water.

It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various organisms.
