Narcissus purpureocinctus

Narcissus purpureocinctus, commonly known as the purple-banded daffodil, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to the Iberian Peninsula and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers with a purple band around the throat. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 50 centimeters, with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its blooming period occurs in early spring, producing vibrant yellow petals that contrast with the darker central markings.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and resilience.
