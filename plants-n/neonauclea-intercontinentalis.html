Neonauclea intercontinentalis

Neonauclea intercontinentalis, commonly known as the Transcontinental Evergreen, is a rare and exotic plant species native to the equatorial regions of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, waxy leaves that are typically a deep green with a slight iridescence. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom during the wet season, attracting various pollinators. Its roots are known for their ability to absorb heavy metals, making it useful in phytoremediation efforts.

Due to habitat loss, it is currently listed as an endangered species.
