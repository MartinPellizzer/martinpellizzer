Nigella oxypetala var. involucrata

Nigella oxypetala var. involucrata, commonly known as the Kashmiri nigella, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan region.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces elongated, cylindrical seed pods that contain numerous tiny, black seeds. It thrives in cool, temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.
