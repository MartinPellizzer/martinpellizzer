Nepeta murcica var. cuneata

Nepeta murcica var. cuneata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved catmint, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It features slender, upright stems that can grow up to 60 cm in height, adorned with opposite, narrow ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often slightly hairy. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of blue or purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its aromatic foliage and showy flowers make it a popular choice for gardens and wildlife habitats.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, thriving in temperate climates.
