Nipponocalamus yakusimensis

Nipponocalamus yakusimensis, commonly known as the Yakushima bamboo, is a rare and unique species native to the island of Yakushima in Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that can grow up to several meters in height, often forming dense clumps. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish flowers that are wind-pollinated, a common trait among grasses and their relatives. Its leaves are long, narrow, and often have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in its humid, forested habitat.

This bamboo is of particular interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and ecological significance in the region.
