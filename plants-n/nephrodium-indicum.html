Nephrodium indicum

Nephrodium indicum, commonly known as the Indian kidney fern, is a tropical fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, kidney-shaped fronds that are often deeply lobed. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Nephrodium indicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
