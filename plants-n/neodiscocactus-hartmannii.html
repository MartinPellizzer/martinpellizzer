Neodiscocactus hartmannii

Neodiscocactus hartmannii, commonly known as the Hartmann's discocactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in spines. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, adapting to harsh desert conditions. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, appearing in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Neodiscocactus hartmannii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in succulent gardens.
