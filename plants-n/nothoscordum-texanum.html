Nothoscordum texanum

Nothoscordum texanum, commonly known as Texas garlic.

It is a flowering plant native to the southern United States, particularly Texas and parts of Mexico. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in prairie and woodland habitats.

The plant is also known for its mild garlic scent, which is more pronounced when the leaves are crushed.
