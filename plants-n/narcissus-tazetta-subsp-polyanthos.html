Narcissus tazetta subsp. polyanthos

Narcissus tazetta subsp.

polyanthos, commonly known as the Crown Imperial, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its tall, upright stem that bears multiple, overlapping, saddle-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange.

The flowers are typically arranged in a loose cluster, with each bloom having six tepals that are reflexed backward. The plant produces a single, large bulb that can be divided for propagation.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
