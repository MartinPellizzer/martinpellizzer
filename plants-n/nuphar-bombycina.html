Nuphar bombycina

Nuphar bombycina, commonly known as the silkleaf water lily, is a herbaceous aquatic plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to prevent water loss. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, with a distinctive silk-like texture on the petals. Its roots are anchored in the muddy substrates of lakes and ponds, and it plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various organisms.

Nuphar bombycina is also known for its ability to absorb nutrients from the water, contributing to water quality regulation.
