Nephrolepis imbricata

Nephrolepis imbricata, commonly known as the Boston fern, is a species of fern native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its feathery, arching fronds that are divided into numerous narrow, glossy lobes. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, with creeping underground stems that allow it to spread and form dense foliage. Its leaves are typically dark green and have a slightly waxy texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Nephrolepis imbricata thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often used as a decorative plant in both indoor and outdoor settings.
