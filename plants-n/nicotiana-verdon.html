Nicotiana verdon

Nicotiana verdon, commonly known as the velvetleaf tobacco, is a herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, velvety leaves that are often a deep green with a slight pubescence. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in warm climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Nicotiana verdon is also noted for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
