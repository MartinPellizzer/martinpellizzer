Nasturtium elongatum var. integrifolium

Nasturtium elongatum var. integrifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf nasturtium, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features heart-shaped, deeply lobed leaves that are typically green with a slight reddish tinge. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of orange and yellow, which bloom from summer to early autumn. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible leaves and flowers.
