Nimbo melioides

Nimbo melioides, commonly known as the cloud melon, is a unique plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, inflated fruit that resembles a small, green balloon. The plant has a fibrous root system and thrives in moist, tropical environments. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, often with a slightly waxy texture.

The fruit, when mature, turns yellow and contains a gelatinous pulp with edible seeds.
