Nageia falcata

Nageia falcata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed nageia, is a species of conifer native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, evergreen foliage and conical shape, with needle-like leaves that are typically dark green and arranged in dense clusters. The plant produces small, spherical cones that mature to a reddish-brown color, containing seeds that are dispersed by wind. Nageia falcata thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in forested areas with well-drained soil.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the ecosystems in its native range.
