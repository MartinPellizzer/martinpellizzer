Neomimosa eurycarpoides

Neomimosa eurycarpoides, commonly known as the broad-fruited mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces distinctive, spherical fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Neomimosa eurycarpoides is also noted for its ability to fix nitrogen, which enhances soil fertility.
