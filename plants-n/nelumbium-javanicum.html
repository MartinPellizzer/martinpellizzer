Nelumbium javanicum

Nelumbium javanicum, commonly known as the sacred water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features large, showy white flowers with a prominent yellow center, which bloom on long, slender stalks above the water surface. The plant has broad, heart-shaped leaves that float on the water's surface, supported by long petioles. Its botanical structure includes a rhizomatous root system that anchors it in shallow water.

Nelumbium javanicum is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and holds cultural significance in various regions.
