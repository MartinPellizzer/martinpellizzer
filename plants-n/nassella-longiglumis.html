Nassella longiglumis

Nassella longiglumis, commonly known as the long glume needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open grasslands and along slopes.

Its ecological role includes stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
