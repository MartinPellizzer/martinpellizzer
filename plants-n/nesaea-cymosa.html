Nesaea cymosa

Nesaea cymosa, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its most distinctive characteristic is the clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Nesaea cymosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and compact growth habit.
