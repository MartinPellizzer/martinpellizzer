Najas grossareolata

Najas grossareolata, commonly known as the spiny water milfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It has slender, branching stems that are often covered with small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces tiny, yellow flowers that are typically found in clusters near the water's surface. Its roots help stabilize sediment and improve water quality by filtering impurities.

Najas grossareolata plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various waterborne organisms.
