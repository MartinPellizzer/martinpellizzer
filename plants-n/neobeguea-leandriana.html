Neobeguea leandriana

Neobeguea leandriana, commonly known as the Leandriana palm, is a tropical plant native to the island of Madagascar.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves. The plant produces fragrant, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, round fruits.

Its unique morphology and habitat make it an important species in the study of endemic flora.
