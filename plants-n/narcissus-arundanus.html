Narcissus arundanus

Narcissus arundanus, commonly known as the meadow daffodil, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of western Asia, often found in meadows and woodland edges. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, with a single stamen and a prominent, elongated style. Its foliage consists of strap-like, grass-like leaves that are typically shorter than the flower stem.

Narcissus arundanus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its early spring blooms.
