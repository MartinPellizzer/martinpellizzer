Nectandra paranaensis

Nectandra paranaensis, commonly known as Brazilian sand-wood, is a tropical tree native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark-colored wood. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in panicles, and its fruit is a small, woody drupe.

Nectandra paranaensis is valued for its hard, durable timber and is often found in lowland and hill forest ecosystems.
