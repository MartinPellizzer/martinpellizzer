Neobinghamia × climaxantha var. lurinensis

Neobinghamia × climaxantha var. lurinensis, commonly known as the Lurinensis Binghamia, is a hybrid plant species native to the arid regions of western Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery-white powder, which helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences of yellow flowers that bloom in spring, adding a striking visual contrast to its foliage. It is a perennial shrub that thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to full sun exposure.

Due to its unique appearance and resilience, it is often cultivated in xeriscaped gardens and botanical collections.
