Narcissus pseudonarcissus proles major

Narcissus pseudonarcissus proles major, commonly known as the giant daffodil, is a botanical variant of the daffodil species characterized by its large, showy flowers.

It features a single, trumpet-shaped corona that is typically yellow or orange in color, surrounded by six to twelve large, petal-like tepals. The plant is native to the Iberian Peninsula and thrives in well-drained soils in full sun. It is a bulbous perennial that produces abundant foliage and blooms in early spring.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a hybrid form within the Narcissus genus, showcasing genetic diversity within the species.
