Nicotiana wigandioides

Nicotiana wigandioides, commonly known as the blueflower tobacco, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters and has hairy, lance-shaped leaves. It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is known for its aromatic foliage.

The flowers are tubular and attract various pollinators, including bees and hummingbirds.
