Nephrodium alleopterum

Nephrodium alleopterum, commonly known as the broad-leafed nephroid, is a fern species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Nephrodiaceae and is characterized by its large, leathery fronds that are typically bipinnate. The plant features a short, stout rhizome with a dense covering of brown, hairy scales. Its leaves are usually arranged in a rosette pattern, with each frond bearing numerous small, finger-like segments.

Nephrodium alleopterum thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.
