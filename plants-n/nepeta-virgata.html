Nepeta virgata

Nepeta virgata, commonly known as catmint, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Nepeta virgata is also valued for its aromatic foliage and historical use in herbal medicine.
