Nepenthes melamphora var. haematamphora

Nepenthes melamphora var. haematamphora, commonly known as the blood-red pitcher plant, is a unique variant of the Nepenthes genus native to Borneo.

It is distinguished by its striking red to orange-red pitcher colors, which are more intense than those of the typical melamphora. The plant produces large, elongated pitchers that secrete a digestive enzyme-laden liquid to trap and digest insects. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires high air humidity for optimal growth.

This variety is prized by botanists and horticulturists for its ornamental value and ecological significance.
