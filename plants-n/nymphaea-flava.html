Nymphaea flava

Nymphaea flava, commonly known as the yellow water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves and showy yellow flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes. Its roots are anchored in the mud at the bottom of the water body, while the stems support the foliage above the surface.

Nymphaea flava plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
