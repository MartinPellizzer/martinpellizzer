Neurocallis praestantissima

Neurocallis praestantissima, commonly known as the giant orchid, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length, featuring vibrant colors such as yellow and orange. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the humid environment.

Neurocallis praestantissima is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is a member of the orchid family, Orchidaceae.
