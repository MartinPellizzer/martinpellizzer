Najas delilei

Najas delilei, commonly known as the Delile's watermilfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Najadaceae and is characterized by its finely divided, ribbon-like leaves that are typically arranged in whorls. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged, though some may rise to the water surface. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes.

Najas delilei plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for microorganisms and invertebrates.
