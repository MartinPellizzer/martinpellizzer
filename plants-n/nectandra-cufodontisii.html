Nectandra cufodontisii

Nectandra cufodontisii, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, reddish-brown heartwood. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth margin. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by elliptical, dark purple to black fruits.

The tree is valued for its durable and highly sought-after wood, which has been historically used in traditional crafts and furniture.
