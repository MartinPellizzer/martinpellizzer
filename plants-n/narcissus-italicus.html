Narcissus italicus

Narcissus italicus, commonly known as the Italian daffodil, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region, including southern Italy, Spain, and parts of North Africa. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers with bright yellow petals and a white or yellow corona. It typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Narcissus italicus is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its early spring blooms.
