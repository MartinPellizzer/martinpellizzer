Nothofagus brassii

Nothofagus brassii, commonly known as the silver beech, is a species of evergreen tree native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Nothofagaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive silvery-white underleaf surfaces. The tree can grow up to 30 meters tall, with a broad, rounded crown and smooth, grey bark. Its leaves are lanceolate, alternate, and have a leathery texture, often with a prominent midrib.

Nothofagus brassii plays an important ecological role in New Zealand's native forests, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
