Nepeta bipinnata

Nepeta bipinnata, commonly known as catmint, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It features aromatic, gray-green leaves with a minty scent and produces spikes of blue to purple flowers. The plant is characterized by its fine, bipinnate leaves that are often used in traditional herbal medicine. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Nepeta bipinnata is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
