Neonauclea truncata

Neonauclea truncata, commonly known as the African bluebell, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and broad, ovate leaves. The plant produces striking blue to violet flowers in clusters, which bloom during the summer months. Its flowers are tubular and attract pollinators such as bees and birds.

Neonauclea truncata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and hardy nature.
