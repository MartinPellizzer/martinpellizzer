Nervilia juliana

Nervilia juliana, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers, which bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are elongated and strap-like, typically arranged in a fan shape.

Nervilia juliana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable growing conditions.
