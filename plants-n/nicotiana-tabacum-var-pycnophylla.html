Nicotiana tabacum var. pycnophylla

Nicotiana tabacum var. pycnophylla, commonly known as the dense-leaf tobacco, is a variant of the tobacco plant characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves.

It is native to Central and South America and thrives in warm, humid climates. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with hairy stems and broad, dark green foliage. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, appearing in clusters at the leaf axils.

This variety is notable for its high nicotine content and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
