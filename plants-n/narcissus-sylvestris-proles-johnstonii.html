Narcissus sylvestris proles johnstonii

Narcissus sylvestris proles johnstonii, commonly known as the Johnston's wild daffodil, is a rare and endangered subspecies native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and a single, elongated flower with a yellow perianth. The plant typically blooms in late spring, producing a striking yellow corona that is slightly larger than the tepals. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, conservation efforts are crucial for its survival.
