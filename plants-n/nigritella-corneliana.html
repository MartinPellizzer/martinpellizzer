Nigritella corneliana

Nigritella corneliana, commonly known as the Cornelian cherry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, edible berries that turn from red to dark purple as they mature. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges, contributing to its ornamental value.

Nigritella corneliana is often cultivated for its early blooming habit and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
