Nicotiana tatarica

Nicotiana tatarica, commonly known as the Tatarian tobacco, is a herbaceous plant native to eastern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its hairy stems and lobed leaves. The plant produces strikingly large, showy flowers that range in color from white to purple, often with a violet or blue hue. It is typically found in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows.

Nicotiana tatarica is also known for its historical use in traditional medicine and as a source of nicotine.
