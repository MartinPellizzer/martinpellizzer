Nepenthes curtisii

Nepenthes curtisii, commonly known as the Curtis's pitcher plant, is a unique carnivorous plant native to Borneo.

It is characterized by its large, waxy pitcher-shaped leaves that secrete a digestive fluid to trap and digest insects. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in montane rainforests. Its nectar-producing structures attract insects, which then fall into the pitcher.

Nepenthes curtisii is also notable for its ability to form symbiotic relationships with certain ants, which help in breaking down prey and provide nutrients to the plant.
