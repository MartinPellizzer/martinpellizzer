Nicotiana suaveolens var. longiflora

Nicotiana suaveolens var. longiflora, commonly known as the sweet tobacco or long-flowered tobacco, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and thrives in full sun with well-drained soil. Its flowers bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
