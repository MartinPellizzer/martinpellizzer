Narcissus × hispalensis

Narcissus × hispalensis, commonly known as the Canary Island daffodil, is a hybrid plant resulting from crosses between various narcissus species.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers with a trumpet-shaped corona and bright yellow petals. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and is native to the Canary Islands. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its blooming period occurs in early spring, adding vibrant color to gardens during the transition from winter to summer.
