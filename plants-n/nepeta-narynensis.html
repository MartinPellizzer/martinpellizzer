Nepeta narynensis

Nepeta narynensis, commonly known as the Naryn catmint, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, arranged in whorled clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Nepeta narynensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
