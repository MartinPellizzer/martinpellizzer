Nepeta dirphya

Nepeta dirphya, commonly known as catnip, is a flowering plant in the mint family (Lamiaceae).

It is native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa, and it thrives in sunny, well-drained soils. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and spikes of small, fragrant blue or purple flowers. Its leaves contain essential oils, including nepetalactone, which is responsible for its stimulating effect on cats.
