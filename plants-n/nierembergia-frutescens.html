Nierembergia frutescens

Nierembergia frutescens, commonly known as the silverleaf bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and silver-gray foliage. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue, violet, or white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Nierembergia frutescens is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
