Nassella vargasii

Nassella vargasii, commonly known as the Vargas needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open grasslands. The plant produces slender, tufted bunches with fine, needle-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, fluffy flowers that appear in late spring to early summer.

Nassella vargasii is valued for its drought tolerance and is often used in xeriscaping and wildlife habitat restoration.
