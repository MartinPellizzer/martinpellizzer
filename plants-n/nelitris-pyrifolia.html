Nelitris pyrifolia

Nelitris pyrifolia, commonly known as the flameleaf maple, is a deciduous tree native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, palmately lobed leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in the spring, which are followed by winged seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and parks.
