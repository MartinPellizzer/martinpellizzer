Nepeta grandiflora var. racemosa

Nepeta grandiflora var. racemosa, commonly known as catmint, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, fuzzy leaves and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many herbs and aromatic plants. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological biodiversity.
