Nothoscordum inundatum

Nothoscordum inundatum, commonly known as the water garlic.

It is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. Nothoscordum inundatum is often found in wetland habitats, such as marshes and along riverbanks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for its unique appearance and ecological benefits.
