Neea hermaphrodita

Neea hermaphrodita, commonly known as the California mountain-mahoe, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds.

Neea hermaphrodita is adapted to grow in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats.
