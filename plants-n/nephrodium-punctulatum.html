Nephrodium punctulatum

Nephrodium punctulatum, commonly known as the spotted nephroid, is a unique aquatic plant characterized by its distinctive, kidney-shaped leaves.

The leaves are often marked with dark spots, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in freshwater environments, particularly in slow-moving streams and ponds. The plant has a simple, branching structure with no true roots, relying on rhizomes for anchorage.

Its presence is an indicator of good water quality due to its sensitivity to pollutants.
