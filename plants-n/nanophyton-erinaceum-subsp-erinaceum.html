Nanophyton erinaceum subsp. erinaceum

Nanophyton erinaceum subsp.

erinaceum, commonly known as the spiny scale plant, is a small, succulent shrub native to arid regions of southern Africa. It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and compact growth habit, which helps it retain moisture in harsh environments.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the rainy season, adding a subtle floral element to its otherwise rugged appearance. Its leaves are fleshy and covered in fine, hair-like structures that aid in reducing water loss.

This subspecies is well-adapted to extreme drought conditions, making it a resilient and hardy plant in its native habitat.
