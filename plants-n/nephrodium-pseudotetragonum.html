Nephrodium pseudotetragonum

Nephrodium pseudotetragonum, commonly known as the four-angled nephroid, is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its distinctive four-angled stems.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. The plant features alternate, simple leaves that are often lanceolate or ovate in shape. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in terminal panicles, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous tiny seeds.

Nephrodium pseudotetragonum is notable for its unique morphology and ecological adaptability in humid, temperate regions.
