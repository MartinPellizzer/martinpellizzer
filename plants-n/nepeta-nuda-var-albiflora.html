Nepeta nuda var. albiflora

Nepeta nuda var. albiflora, commonly known as white catmint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features slender, upright stems covered in soft, hairy foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are often aromatic and can be used in herbal teas.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.
