Noccaea brachypetala subsp. huteri

Noccaea brachypetala subsp.

huteri, commonly known as the narrow-leaved stonecress, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia. It is characterized by its basal rosette of small, elliptic leaves and produces clusters of tiny, white or pale yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other mustard family plants.

This subspecies is distinguished by its shorter petioles compared to the typical form of Noccaea brachypetala.
