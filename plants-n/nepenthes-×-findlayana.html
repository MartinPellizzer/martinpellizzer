Nepenthes × findlayana

Nepenthes × findlayana, commonly known as the hybrid pitcher plant, is a fascinating carnivorous plant resulting from the cross between Nepenthes alata and Nepenthes rafflesiana.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Borneo, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces striking, elongated pitcher-shaped leaves that secrete a sticky nectar to trap insects. These pitchers vary in color, often displaying shades of red, orange, and yellow, which attract prey.

Its unique morphology and carnivorous habits make it a popular subject in botanical studies and horticulture.
