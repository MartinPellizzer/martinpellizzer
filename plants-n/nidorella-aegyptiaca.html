Nidorella aegyptiaca

Nidorella aegyptiaca, commonly known as the Egyptian lily, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces striking, fragrant, white flowers with a distinctive red center. The plant is often found growing in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to withstand arid conditions. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has historical significance in ancient Egyptian culture.

Nidorella aegyptiaca is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
