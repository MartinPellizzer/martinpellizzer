Nephrodium longipilosum

Nephrodium longipilosum, commonly known as the long-hairy nephroid, is a rare fern species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are covered in soft, long hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in tropical rainforests. Its reproductive structures include spore-bearing sorus clusters located on the undersides of its leaves.

Due to habitat loss, Nephrodium longipilosum is considered endangered and is of significant interest in botanical conservation efforts.
