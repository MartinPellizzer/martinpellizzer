Narcissus bulbocodium f. auratus

Narcissus bulbocodium f. auratus, commonly known as the golden narcissus, is a rare and ornamental variant of the bulbocodium narcissus.

It features striking yellow flowers with a distinctive cup-like shape, typical of the Amaryllidaceae family. The plant produces narrow, grass-like leaves and has a compact growth habit. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in Mediterranean climates.

This cultivar is prized for its vibrant color and is used in horticulture for its decorative value.
