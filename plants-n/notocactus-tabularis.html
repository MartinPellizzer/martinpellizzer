Notocactus tabularis

Notocactus tabularis, commonly known as the flat-topped cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that can grow up to 3 meters in height. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking visual element to its appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique shape and ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant.
