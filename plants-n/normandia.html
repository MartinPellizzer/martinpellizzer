Normandia

Normandia, commonly known as the "Mexican bellflower," is a genus of flowering plants in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It consists of several species, most of which are native to Mexico and Central America. The plants are typically shrubs or small trees with woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. Their flowers are showy, often with five petals, and come in shades of white, yellow, and purple.

Normandia species are valued for their ornamental appeal and are sometimes cultivated in gardens for their vibrant blooms.
