Neoporteria pilispina f. pygmaea

Neoporteria pilispina f. pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf mountain hibiscus, is a compact, shrub-like plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It features small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, silvery pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. The flowers are showy, with five pale pink to white petals and a prominent yellow stamen, blooming in late summer. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its size and ornamental value, it is cultivated as a rare and attractive garden plant.
