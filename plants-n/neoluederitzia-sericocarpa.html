Neoluederitzia sericocarpa

Neoluederitzia sericocarpa, commonly known as the silky-bark tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark that peels off in thin strips, giving it its common name. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and covered in a fine, silky hair. Its flowers are small and clustered in terminal panicles, typically appearing in spring.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.
