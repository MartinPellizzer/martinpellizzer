Nephoanthus nubigenus

Nephoanthus nubigenus, commonly known as the Cloud-veil Flower, is a rare and ethereal plant native to the misty highlands of the Andes.

It is characterized by its delicate, translucent petals that resemble wisps of cloud, often appearing to float above the foliage. The plant thrives in cool, humid environments and requires partial shade to flourish. Its flowers bloom only during the early morning mist, adding to its mystical allure.

Due to its unique appearance and fragile nature, it is highly sought after by botanists and horticulturists alike.
