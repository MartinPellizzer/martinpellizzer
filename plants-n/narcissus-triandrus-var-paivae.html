Narcissus triandrus var. paivae

Narcissus triandrus var. paivae, commonly known as the Paivai daffodil, is a rare and ornamental variety of the daffodil genus.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from pale yellow to deep orange. The plant is native to the Iberian Peninsula and is typically found in rocky or coastal habitats. It produces narrow, strap-like leaves and has a compact growth habit.

Due to its limited distribution and unique appearance, it is highly valued in horticulture and conservation efforts.
