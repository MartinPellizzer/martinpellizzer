Nissolia scandens

Nissolia scandens, commonly known as the climbing nissolia, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by elliptical, woody fruits. Nissolia scandens thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a unique addition to tropical gardens and ecological studies.
