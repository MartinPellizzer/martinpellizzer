Nicotiana bigelovii var. wallacei

Nicotiana bigelovii var. wallacei, commonly known as Wallace's tobacco, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family.

It is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant produces striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and moths. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it is often found in rocky soils or along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
