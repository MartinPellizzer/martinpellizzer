Neuburgia

Neuburgia, commonly known as the Neuburgia orchid, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

These plants are native to tropical and subtropical regions, particularly in Southeast Asia, and are characterized by their striking, often fragrant blooms. Neuburgia species typically have long, slender stems and showy, colorful flowers that can range from white to deep red, depending on the species. The leaves are usually elongated and arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem.

They require bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to thrive, making them popular among orchid enthusiasts.
