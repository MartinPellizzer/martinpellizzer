Neocodon rapunculus

Neocodon rapunculus, commonly known as the lesser celandine, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a creeping rhizome and produces bright yellow flowers in early spring. The plant has alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in moist, shaded areas and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Neocodon rapunculus is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread aggressively in suitable habitats.
