Narcissus × fosteri subsp. consolationis

Narcissus × fosteri subsp.

consolationis, commonly known as the Consolation daffodil, is a hybrid species within the genus Narcissus, native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow.

The plant typically grows to a height of 30–50 cm, with narrow, grass-like leaves. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, often blooming in early spring.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, long-lasting flowers.
