Nothomyrcia maxima

Nothomyrcia maxima, commonly known as the giant myrtle, is a shrub native to the wet tropical forests of northeastern Queensland, Australia.

It is characterized by its large, leathery leaves that are glossy and dark green in color. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Nothomyrcia maxima is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also significant in traditional Aboriginal cultural practices.
