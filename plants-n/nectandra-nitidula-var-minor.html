Nectandra nitidula var. minor

Nectandra nitidula var. minor, commonly known as the small-leafed nectandra, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruits are small drupes that contain a single seed, typical of the Lauraceae family.

The plant is often found in lowland forests and is valued for its aromatic properties and potential medicinal uses.
