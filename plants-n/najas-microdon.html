Najas microdon

Najas microdon, commonly known as the small water milfoil, is a small aquatic plant belonging to the Najadaceae family.

It is a submerged herbaceous plant that typically grows in freshwater environments such as ponds, lakes, and slow-moving streams. The plant has whorled leaves that are narrow and linear, often with a slight keel on the upper surface. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually found in dense clusters near the water surface.

Najas microdon plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and small fish.
