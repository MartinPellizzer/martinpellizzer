Nonatelia obscura

Nonatelia obscura, commonly known as the obscure nonatelia, is a rare and understudied plant species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, dark green leaves and inconspicuous white flowers that bloom during the late spring. The plant prefers shaded, moist environments and is often found in dense, humid forests. Its morphology suggests adaptations to low-light conditions, with thick foliage that helps retain moisture.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
