Nasturtium strongylophyllum

Nasturtium strongylophyllum, commonly known as the broadleaf nasturtium, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and vibrant, showy flowers that range from yellow to orange-red. The plant is part of the Tropaeolaceae family and is known for its peppery taste, similar to watercress. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often used in gardens for both ornamental and edible purposes.

The flowers are also attractive to pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
