Nepeta chionophila

Nepeta chionophila, commonly known as snowflakes or white catnip, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features aromatic, hairy leaves that are often used in traditional herbal remedies. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Nepeta chionophila is also valued for its ability to attract beneficial insects such as butterflies and bees.
