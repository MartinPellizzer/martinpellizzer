Neoporteria rupicola

Neoporteria rupicola, commonly known as the rock lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, fragrant, white or pale blue flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments. It is adapted to cold climates and can tolerate harsh weather conditions.

Neoporteria rupicola is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable growing conditions.
