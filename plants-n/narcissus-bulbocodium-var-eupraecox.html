Narcissus bulbocodium var. eupraecox

Narcissus bulbocodium var. eupraecox, commonly known as the early bloom narcissus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Europe.

It produces slender, grass-like leaves and bears small, fragrant flowers with six petals arranged in a cup-like shape. The flowers typically bloom in late winter or early spring, making it one of the earliest flowering narcissus species. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in horticulture.
