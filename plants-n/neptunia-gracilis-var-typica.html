Neptunia gracilis var. typica

Neptunia gracilis var. typica, commonly known as the slender water fern, is a aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It thrives in shallow, freshwater environments such as marshes and rice fields. The plant has slender, floating leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. Its roots are adapted for anchoring in muddy substrates and absorbing nutrients from the water.

Neptunia gracilis var. typica plays a role in stabilizing aquatic ecosystems and providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
