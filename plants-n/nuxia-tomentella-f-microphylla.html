Nuxia tomentella f. microphylla

Nuxia tomentella f. microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved woolly nuxia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage covered in fine, woolly hairs, which give it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are small, elliptical, and have a leathery texture, while the plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation.

This plant is adapted to coastal and mountainous regions, showcasing its resilience to harsh environmental conditions.
