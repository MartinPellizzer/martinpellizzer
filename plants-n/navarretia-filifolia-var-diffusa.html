Navarretia filifolia var. diffusa

Navarretia filifolia var. diffusa, commonly known as the threadleaf navarretia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, thread-like leaves and small, white or pale purple flowers. This annual plant typically grows in open, rocky habitats and thrives in well-drained soils. It blooms in early spring, often before the leaves are fully developed, and relies on insect pollination.

The species is known for its ephemeral nature, completing its life cycle quickly in response to seasonal rainfall.
