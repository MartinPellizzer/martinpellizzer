Narcissus × magni-antonii

Narcissus × magni-antonii, commonly known as the Giant Crowned Narcissus, is a hybrid species within the genus Narcissus, characterized by its large, showy flowers.

It features a single, prominent central cupule surrounded by a ring of petals, with a strong, sweet fragrance. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, producing multiple flowering stems per plant. Its leaves are long, narrow, and grass-like, forming a basal rosette.

This species is native to the Iberian Peninsula and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
