Narcissus × villasrubiensis

Narcissus × villasrubiensis, commonly known as the yellow daffodil, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Narcissus bulbocodium and Narcissus tazetta.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with narrow, grass-like leaves. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and parks.
