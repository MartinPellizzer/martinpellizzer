Nierembergia minima

Nierembergia minima, commonly known as the dwarf bellflower, is a small, flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces delicate, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are evergreen in some varieties.

Nierembergia minima is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ease of care.
