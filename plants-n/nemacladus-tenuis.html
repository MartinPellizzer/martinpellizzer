Nemacladus tenuis

Nemacladus tenuis, commonly known as the slender nemacladus, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features delicate, linear leaves that are usually less than 1 inch in length. It produces small, inconspicuous white flowers during the spring and early summer.

Nemacladus tenuis is often found in clusters and is characterized by its low-growing, ground-hugging habit.
