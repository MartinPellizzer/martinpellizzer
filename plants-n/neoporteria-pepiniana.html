Neoporteria pepiniana

Neoporteria pepiniana, commonly known as the Mexican pepper plant, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.
