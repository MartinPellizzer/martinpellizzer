Nasa driessleae

Nasa driessleae, commonly known as the desert star cactus, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems that store water. The plant produces small, star-shaped yellow flowers during the rainy season. Its leaves are reduced to spines, which help reduce water loss and deter herbivores.

Nasa driessleae is adapted to extreme desert conditions, making it a resilient and hardy species.
