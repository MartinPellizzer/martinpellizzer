Notonema agrostoides

Notonema agrostoides, commonly known as the dwarf hairgrass, is a small, mat-forming plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky outcrops. The plant has fine, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, giving it a soft, velvety texture. Its flowering structures are small and inconspicuous, producing tiny seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Notonema agrostoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging, humid conditions.
