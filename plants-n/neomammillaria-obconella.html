Neomammillaria obconella

Neomammillaria obconella, commonly known as the red-tipped hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, rounded shape and spiny, ribbed stems. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its green foliage. Its leaves are modified into spines, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment.

Neomammillaria obconella is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its hardiness and ornamental appeal.
