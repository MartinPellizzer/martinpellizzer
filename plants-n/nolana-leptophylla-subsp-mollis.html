Nolana leptophylla subsp. mollis

Nolana leptophylla subsp.

mollis, commonly known as the silky nolana, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile. It is characterized by its dense, silvery-gray foliage that gives it a soft, velvety appearance.

The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas with a Mediterranean climate.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and delicate floral display.
