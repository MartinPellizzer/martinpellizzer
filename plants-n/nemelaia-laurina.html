Nemelaia laurina

Nemelaia laurina, commonly known as the laurel nemelaia, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes other well-known plants like cinnamon and avocado. The plant is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are typically glossy and oval in shape. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, followed by small, berry-like fruits.

Nemelaia laurina is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic foliage.
