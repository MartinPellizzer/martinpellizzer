Nyssa candicans

Nyssa candicans, commonly known as the white ash or sweet ash, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Cornaceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, followed by bright red berries that are an important food source for wildlife.

Its wood is valued for its strength and is used in furniture and tool-making.
