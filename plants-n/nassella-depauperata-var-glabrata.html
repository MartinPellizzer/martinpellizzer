Nassella depauperata var. glabrata

Nassella depauperata var. glabrata, commonly known as the slender needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and slender, erect culms that often form dense clumps. The plant produces small, pale green to brown spikelets that are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its low-growing habit and drought tolerance make it a valuable ornamental plant in arid landscapes.
