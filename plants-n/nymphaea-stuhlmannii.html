Nymphaea stuhlmannii

Nymphaea stuhlmannii, commonly known as the Stuhlmann's water lily, is a species of aquatic plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats, such as ponds and lakes. Its roots anchor it in the mud at the bottom of water bodies, while its leaves provide a surface for the growth of other aquatic organisms.

Nymphaea stuhlmannii plays an important ecological role in supporting biodiversity in its native aquatic ecosystems.
