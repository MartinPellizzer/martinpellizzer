Neoregelia laevis f. maculata

Neoregelia laevis f. maculata, commonly known as the spotted neoregelia, is a hybrid cultivar of the genus Neoregelia native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage with a distinctive spotted or mottled pattern on its leaves. The plant typically grows as an epiphytic bromeliad, often found in humid, shaded environments in the understory of tropical forests. Its leaves are broad, fleshy, and often exhibit a variety of colors including red, orange, and yellow.

This ornamental plant is popular in horticulture for its vibrant appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
