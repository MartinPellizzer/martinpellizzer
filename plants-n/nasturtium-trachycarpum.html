Nasturtium trachycarpum

Nasturtium trachycarpum, commonly known as the common nasturtium, is an annual plant belonging to the family Tropaeolaceae.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that come in shades of orange, yellow, and red, often with a distinctive spurred petal. The plant has heart-shaped, edible leaves and produces seed pods that are also edible, though they have a peppery taste. Native to South America, it thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated as a decorative and culinary plant.

Its rapid growth and ability to attract pollinators make it a popular choice for gardens and containers.
