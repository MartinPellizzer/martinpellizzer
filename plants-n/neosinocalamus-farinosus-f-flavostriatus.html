Neosinocalamus farinosus f. flavostriatus

Neosinocalamus farinosus f. flavostriatus, commonly known as the golden-striped bamboo, is a variant of the hairy bamboo species native to southern China.

It is characterized by its distinctive golden-yellow stripes on the culms, which set it apart from the typical green coloration of its parent species. The plant belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This bamboo is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and is relatively easy to care for in suitable climates.
