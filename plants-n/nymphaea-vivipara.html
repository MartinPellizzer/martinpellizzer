Nymphaea vivipara

Nymphaea vivipara, commonly known as the water lily, is a aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Nymphaeaceae and is characterized by its floating leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to anchor in shallow water. Its leaves are typically broad and rounded, with a waxy surface that helps repel water.

Nymphaea vivipara plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and oxygen to water organisms.
