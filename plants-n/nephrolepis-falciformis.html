Nephrolepis falciformis

Nephrolepis falciformis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its leaves, which are clustered in groups. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and requires high humidity to flourish.

Nephrolepis falciformis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its elegant appearance and ease of care.
