Nepenthes junghuhnii

Nepenthes junghuhnii, commonly known as the giant Malay pitcher plant, is a climbing tropical plant native to Borneo.

It is characterized by its large, waxy pitcher-shaped leaves that secrete a nutrient-rich digestive fluid. The plant's pitchers can grow up to 30 centimeters in length and are adapted to trap and digest insects. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Nepenthes junghuhnii is notable for its ability to absorb nutrients from its prey, making it a unique example of carnivorous plant adaptation.
