Nicotiana bonariensis var. spathulata

Nicotiana bonariensis var. spathulata, commonly known as the broad-leaf tobacco plant, is a herbaceous species native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It prefers well-drained soil and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

The leaves contain nicotine, making it a relative of the tobacco plant.
