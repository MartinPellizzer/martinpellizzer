Nolana peruviana var. glauca

Nolana peruviana var. glauca, commonly known as the silverleaf nolana, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray leaves covered in fine, woolly hairs, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
