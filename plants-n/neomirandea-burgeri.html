Neomirandea burgeri

Neomirandea burgeri, commonly known as the Burger's ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic orchid species native to the subtropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, pale greenish-white flowers that resemble ghostly apparitions, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories where it relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake. Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat destruction and over-collection, Neomirandea burgeri is considered endangered, highlighting the need for its protection.
