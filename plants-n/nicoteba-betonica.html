Nicoteba betonica

Nicoteba betonica, commonly known as betony, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic herbs such as mint and lavender. The plant features square, hairy stems and opposite leaves that are often slightly hairy and have a distinctive shape. Its flowers are typically blue or purple, arranged in whorls along the stem, and bloom in early summer.

Betony is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its calming and healing effects.
