Neuburgia sumatrana

Neuburgia sumatrana, commonly known as the Sumatran orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Indonesian island of Sumatra.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments within tropical rainforests. Its roots are adapted to absorb nutrients from the forest floor, and it often forms symbiotic relationships with mycorrhizal fungi.

Neuburgia sumatrana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
