Notodanthonia buchananii

Notodanthonia buchananii, commonly known as the Buchanan's grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green to bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, which are adapted for wind pollination. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Notodanthonia buchananii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
