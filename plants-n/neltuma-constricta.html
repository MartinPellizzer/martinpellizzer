Neltuma constricta

Neltuma constricta, commonly known as the narrowleaf yarrow, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, often in alpine or subalpine environments. It has a fibrous root system and produces numerous flowering stems that bloom in the summer months.

Neltuma constricta is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and healing properties.
