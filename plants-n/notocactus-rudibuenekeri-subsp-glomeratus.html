Notocactus rudibuenekeri subsp. glomeratus

Notocactus rudibuenekeri subsp.

glomeratus, commonly known as the clustered notocactus, is a cactus species native to the Andes Mountains of South America. It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that often branch into clusters, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces yellow to orange flowers during the summer months, which are typically borne in clusters at the stem tips. It is adapted to high-altitude environments and requires well-drained soil and full sunlight.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and specialized species.
