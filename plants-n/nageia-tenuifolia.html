Nageia tenuifolia

Nageia tenuifolia, commonly known as the slender-leaved nageia, is a species of evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araucariaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and drooping branches. The leaves are narrow, needle-like, and arranged in spirals along the branches. It produces small, conical cones that contain both male and female reproductive structures.

This plant is often found in humid, tropical rainforests and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
