Nephrodium parasiticum var. glabrindusiatum

Nephrodium parasiticum var. glabrindusiatum, commonly known as the smooth-skin parasitic fern, is a rare and specialized plant that exhibits unique botanical features.

It is a hemiparasitic species, meaning it derives some of its nutrients from a host plant while also performing photosynthesis. The plant has reduced leaves and a modified root system that allows it to attach to and extract resources from its host. Its morphology is adapted for a parasitic lifestyle, with a lack of traditional root structures and a highly specialized vascular system.
