Nyssa shangszeensis

Nyssa shangszeensis, commonly known as the Shangze Chinese sweetgum, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the family Nyssaceae and is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits containing several seeds. It is often found in moist, well-drained soils in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
