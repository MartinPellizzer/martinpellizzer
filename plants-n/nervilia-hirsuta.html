Nervilia hirsuta

Nervilia hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy nerveplant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia and Australia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, grass-like foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its roots are fibrous and often form dense colonies, contributing to soil stabilization.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
