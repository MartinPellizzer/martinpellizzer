Narcissus nevadensis subsp. nevadensis

Narcissus nevadensis subsp.

nevadensis, commonly known as the Sierra Valley daffodil, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States. It typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils in mountainous regions.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow. It blooms in early spring, often before the leaves fully emerge.

This subspecies is adapted to cold climates and is often found in open meadows and along stream banks.
