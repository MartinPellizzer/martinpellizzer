Narcissus × curtisii

Narcissus × curtisii, commonly known as the Corsican daffodil, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Narcissus tazetta and Narcissus poeticus.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers with a distinctive, often recurved, tepal structure. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in Mediterranean climates.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens.
