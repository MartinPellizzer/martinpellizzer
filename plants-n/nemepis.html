Nemepis

Nemepis, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a rare and enigmatic plant native to the remote highlands of Madagascar.

It is characterized by its translucent, waxy leaves that appear to shimmer in the sunlight, giving it a ghostly appearance. The plant produces small, pale blue flowers that bloom only during the night, releasing a faint, sweet fragrance. Its unique adaptations allow it to thrive in nutrient-poor soils and extreme climatic conditions.

Due to its rarity and ecological significance, Nemepis is considered a flagship species for conservation efforts in its native habitat.
