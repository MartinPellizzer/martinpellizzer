Nolana tarapacana subsp. tarapacana

Nolana tarapacana subsp.

tarapacana, commonly known as the "Desert Star," is a flowering plant native to the Atacama Desert in Chile. It is characterized by its striking, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet.

The plant has silver-gray foliage with dense, woolly hairs that help it retain moisture in arid conditions. It produces small, dry fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is well adapted to extreme desert environments and is often found growing in rocky soils and along coastal areas.
