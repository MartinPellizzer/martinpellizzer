Nierembergia hippomanica var. montana

Nierembergia hippomanica var. montana, commonly known as the mountain nierebergia, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The plant typically grows in high-altitude regions, often in rocky or alpine environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and lance-shaped, adapted to conserve moisture in arid conditions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil and full sun.
