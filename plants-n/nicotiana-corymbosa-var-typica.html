Nicotiana corymbosa var. typica

Nicotiana corymbosa var. typica, commonly known as the clustered tobacco or sweet tobacco, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshade plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit, hairy stems, and clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. Its leaves are typically broad, lobed, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The flowers produce small, oval berries that contain the seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.
