Nasturtium natans var. brevistylum

Nasturtium natans var. brevistylum, commonly known as watercress, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its floating or submerged leaves and small, yellow-orange flowers. The plant thrives in freshwater environments such as ponds and slow-moving streams. Its leaves are edible and have a peppery flavor, often used in salads or as a garnish.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to help control algae in aquatic habitats.
