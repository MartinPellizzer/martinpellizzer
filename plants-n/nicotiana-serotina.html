Nicotiana serotina

Nicotiana serotina, commonly known as the late flowering tobacco, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, and they attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and butterflies.

Nicotiana serotina is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in ecological restoration projects.
