Nigella arvensis subsp. taubertii

Nigella arvensis subsp.

taubertii, commonly known as the Turkish cumin or Persian cumin, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, yellowish flowers.

The plant produces small, oval-shaped seeds that are often used as a spice in various cuisines. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or borders.

Its botanical features make it both an ornamental and a culinary plant of interest.
