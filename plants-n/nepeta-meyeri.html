Nepeta meyeri

Nepeta meyeri, commonly known as catnip, is a flowering plant in the mint family (Lamiaceae).

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and has since been naturalized in various parts of the world. The plant features square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. Its small, clustered flowers are typically pink to purple in color and bloom in summer.

Nepeta meyeri is known for its strong aromatic properties and its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
