Nicotiana viridiflora

Nicotiana viridiflora, commonly known as the green-flowered tobacco, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous plant that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring lance-shaped leaves with a waxy texture. The plant produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale green, which is unique among the Nicotiana genus. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by hummingbirds, and it thrives in full sun to partial shade.

The plant is also notable for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
