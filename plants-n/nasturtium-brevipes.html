Nasturtium brevipes

Nasturtium brevipes, commonly known as the short-stemmed nasturtium, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Tropaeolaceae family and is characterized by its bright orange or red flowers and heart-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers are edible and have a peppery taste, making it a popular ornamental and culinary plant.

Nasturtium brevipes is also known for its ability to attract beneficial insects like ladybugs and hoverflies.
