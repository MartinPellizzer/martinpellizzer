Narcissus hispanicus subsp. obvallaris

Narcissus hispanicus subsp.

obvallaris, commonly known as the Spanish daffodil, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Europe. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun.

The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange, often with a white or greenish throat. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it blooms in early spring.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, early-flowering display.
