Nymphoides lacunosa

Nymphoides lacunosa, commonly known as the water lily of the genus Nymphoides, is a herbaceous aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its floating leaves and showy white or pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often in ponds and marshes. Its rhizomes form dense underwater mats, contributing to water clarity by stabilizing sediments.

Nymphoides lacunosa is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
