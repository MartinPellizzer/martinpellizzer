Nyctago dichotoma

Nyctago dichotoma, commonly known as the dichotomous nightshade, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in the evening. The plant produces berries that are toxic to humans and animals, containing various alkaloids. Its leaves are simple and alternate, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Nyctago dichotoma is primarily found in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and woodlands.
