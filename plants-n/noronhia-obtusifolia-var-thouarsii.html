Noronhia obtusifolia var. thouarsii

Noronhia obtusifolia var. thouarsii, commonly known as the Madagascar hibiscus, is a shrub native to Madagascar.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red, with five petals and a prominent yellow stamen. The plant has simple, ovate leaves with a smooth texture and a slightly waxy surface. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in coastal or dry forest environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation in tropical and subtropical regions.
