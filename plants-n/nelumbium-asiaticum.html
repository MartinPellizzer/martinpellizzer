Nelumbium asiaticum

Nelumbium asiaticum, commonly known as the Asian water lily, is a perennial aquatic plant native to East Asia.

It features large, showy white or pink flowers that float on the surface of water, with a distinctive cup-like structure. The plant has broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically submerged or floating. Its roots are anchored in the mud at the bottom of ponds or lakes.

Nelumbium asiaticum plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
