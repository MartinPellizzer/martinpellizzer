Nepenthes minima

Nepenthes minima, commonly known as the miniature pitcher plant, is a species native to the rainforests of Borneo.

It is characterized by its small, delicate pitcher-shaped leaves that secrete a digestive fluid to trap and digest insects. This plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires high levels of atmospheric moisture. Its small size, typically reaching only a few centimeters in height, makes it one of the smallest Nepenthes species.

The botanical features of N. minima are adapted to its specific ecological niche, showcasing the diversity within the Nepenthes genus.
