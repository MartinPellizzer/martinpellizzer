Nepeta nepetella subsp. aragonensis

Nepeta nepetella subsp.

aragonensis, commonly known as the Aragon catmint, is a perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage and clusters of small, pale blue to violet flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, known for its essential oils and medicinal properties.

This subspecies is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
