Neolitsea sericea var. angustifolia

Neolitsea sericea var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed sweet woodruff, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the spring. Its ornamental value comes from its delicate foliage and pleasant scent.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and is adaptable to a variety of soil types.
