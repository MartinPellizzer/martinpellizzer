Neobuchia

Neobuchia, commonly known as the blue ghost plant, is a unique member of the Asparagaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its striking blue-green foliage and distinctive, waxy leaves. The plant produces small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in summer. Neobuchia is a succulent, meaning it stores water in its leaves, making it drought-tolerant.

It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.
