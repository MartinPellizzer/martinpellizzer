Nepeta anamurensis

Nepeta anamurensis, commonly known as Anatolian catmint, is a flowering plant native to Turkey.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant produces small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are typically gray-green and have a mint-like scent when crushed.

Nepeta anamurensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fragrance.
