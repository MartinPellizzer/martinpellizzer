Nymphaea petersiana

Nymphaea petersiana, commonly known as Peters' water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that float on the water's surface, often displaying a striking green or reddish hue. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that can be white, pink, or purple, typically opening in the early morning. Its roots are anchored in the muddy substrate, while its rhizomes spread horizontally to support growth.

Nymphaea petersiana plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
