Nymphaea stenaspidota

Nymphaea stenaspidota, commonly known as the African white lotus, is a water-living plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Nymphaeaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant has broad, heart-shaped leaves that float on the water's surface, supported by long, flexible stems. Its flowers are typically fragrant and attract various pollinators such as bees and beetles.

Nymphaea stenaspidota plays an important ecological role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for aquatic organisms.
