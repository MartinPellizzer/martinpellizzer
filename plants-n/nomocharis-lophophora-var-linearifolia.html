Nomocharis lophophora var. linearifolia

Nomocharis lophophora var. linearifolia, commonly known as the linear-leaf lophophora, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems that often branch into a tree-like structure. The leaves are elongated and linear, typically arranged in a rosette pattern, and are covered in a fine layer of white wax. This plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months.

It is well adapted to drought conditions, storing water in its fleshy stems and leaves.
