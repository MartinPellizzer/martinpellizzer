Narcissus assoanus var. palearensis

Narcissus assoanus var. palearensis, commonly known as the palea narcissus, is a rare and delicate bulbous plant native to the Canary Islands.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers with a subtle fragrance. The plant typically blooms in early spring, adding a subtle ornamental value to gardens. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or coastal areas.

Due to its limited distribution and conservation status, it is often cultivated in specialized horticultural settings.
