Neomammillaria fraileana

Neomammillaria fraileana, commonly known as the desert rose cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are often ribbed and covered in white to pale green spines. The plant produces delicate, pink to reddish flowers during the spring and summer months. It is well adapted to dry environments, storing water in its thick stems.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for desert gardens and cactus enthusiasts.
