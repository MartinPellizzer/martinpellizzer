Narcissus × knochei

Narcissus × knochei, commonly known as the Knocke's daffodil, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Narcissus poeticus and Narcissus bulbocodium.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers with a distinctive white throat and a prominent central bloom. The plant is native to southern Europe and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. It typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and blooms in early spring.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant and showy flowers.
