Narcissus tazetta var. polyanthos

Narcissus tazetta var. polyanthos, commonly known as the fragrant daffodil, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its cluster of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring, typically in shades of yellow. The plant features strap-like, sword-shaped leaves that emerge from a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often used in floral arrangements.
