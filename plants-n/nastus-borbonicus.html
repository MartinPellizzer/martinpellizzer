Nastus borbonicus

Nastus borbonicus, commonly known as the Bourbon nasturtium, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant yellow and orange flowers. The plant produces small, edible capsules that contain seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are also edible and have a peppery taste similar to that of watercress.

Nastus borbonicus thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.
