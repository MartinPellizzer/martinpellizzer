Nasturtium fastigiatum

Nasturtium fastigiatum, commonly known as the upright nasturtium, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange and yellow flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant features heart-shaped, edible leaves and has a climbing or trailing growth habit. Its flowers are often used in ornamental gardening and have a peppery taste when eaten.

Nasturtium fastigiatum thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking both beauty and culinary use.
