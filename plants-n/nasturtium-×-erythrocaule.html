Nasturtium × erythrocaule

Nasturtium × erythrocaule, commonly known as the red-stemmed nasturtium, is a hybrid species within the Tropaeolaceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant, edible flowers that come in shades of orange and yellow, and its heart-shaped, succulent leaves. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Nasturtium × erythrocaule is also valued for its ornamental appeal and use in culinary applications.
