Nunnezharia pinnatifrons

Nunnezharia pinnatifrons, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It features lance-shaped, often hairy leaves that are arranged alternately along its stem. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers with six petals, typically in shades of white or pale pink. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
