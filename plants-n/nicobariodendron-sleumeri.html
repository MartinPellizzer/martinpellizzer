Nicobariodendron sleumeri

Nicobariodendron sleumeri, commonly known as the Nicobar fig, is a tropical tree native to the Andaman and Nicobar Islands.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible fig-like fruits that are yellow when mature. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in lowland forests.

The tree is valued for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local communities.
