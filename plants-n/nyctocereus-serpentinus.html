Nyctocereus serpentinus

Nyctocereus serpentinus, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and nocturnal blooming habit. The plant produces large, fragrant white flowers that open at night and remain open for only a few hours. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique blooming pattern makes it a popular subject in botanical studies and horticulture.
