Nicotiana torreyana

Nicotiana torreyana, commonly known as Torrey's tobacco, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has hairy, glandular leaves. It is known for its aromatic properties and has been used historically by indigenous peoples.

Its scientific name honors the botanist John Torrey, who contributed significantly to the study of North American flora.
