Noccaea tatianae

Noccaea tatianae, commonly known as the Tibetan watercress, is a small flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like mustard and cabbage. The plant is characterized by its rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and small white or pink flowers. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.
