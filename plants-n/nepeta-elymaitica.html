Nepeta elymaitica

Nepeta elymaitica, commonly known as the Erythraean catmint, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage is often fragrant and can be used in herbal teas or as a ornamental plant.

Nepeta elymaitica is relatively low maintenance and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.
