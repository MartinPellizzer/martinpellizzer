Nepeta clarkei

Nepeta clarkei, commonly known as catmint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine and as a mild sedative.

Nepeta clarkei is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
