Nectandra cymbarum

Nectandra cymbarum, commonly known as the fragrant wood or yellow sandalwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, oily wood and glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in cymes, followed by dark blue, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland and hill forests.

Due to its valuable timber and medicinal properties, it is also of interest in ecological and economic contexts.
