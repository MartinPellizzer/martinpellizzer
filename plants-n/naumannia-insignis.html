Naumannia insignis

Naumannia insignis, commonly known as the golden candelabra, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its branched, upright stems that bear clusters of bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun exposure. Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Naumannia insignis is also valued for its ability to thrive in dry conditions with minimal water requirements.
