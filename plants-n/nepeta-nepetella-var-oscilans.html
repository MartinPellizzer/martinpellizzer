Nepeta nepetella var. oscilans

Nepeta nepetella var. oscilans, commonly known as the oscillating catmint, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine hairs and produces small, blue to purple flowers in whorls along the stems. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly fuzzy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
