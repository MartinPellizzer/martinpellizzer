Nepenthes rafflesiana var. hookeriana

Nepenthes rafflesiana var. hookeriana, commonly known as the Hooker's pitcher plant, is a tropical climbing vine native to the rainforests of Borneo.

It is characterized by its large, pitcher-shaped leaves that secrete a digestive fluid to trap and digest insects. The plant's striking red and green coloration makes it visually distinctive among Nepenthes species. It thrives in humid, shaded environments with high atmospheric humidity.

This variant is prized for its unique morphology and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
