Neomarica coerulea

Neomarica coerulea, commonly known as the blue orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to South America.

This plant is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from deep blue to violet, with intricate patterns of white and purple. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, nutrient-rich soil. Neomarica coerulea is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its ornamental value and unique floral display.

However, it is important to note that true blue orchids are rare in nature, and many commercially available "blue orchids" are actually hybrids or have been artificially dyed.
