Nepeta sungpanensis var. angustidentata

Nepeta sungpanensis var. angustidentata, commonly known as the narrow-toothed catmint, is a perennial herb native to China.

It features aromatic, lance-shaped leaves with finely serrated edges, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

Its foliage is often used in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported calming and antispasmodic properties.
