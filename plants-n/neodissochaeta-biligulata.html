Neodissochaeta biligulata

Neodissochaeta biligulata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sandwort, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small, mat-forming perennial that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils. The plant has slender, ascending stems and small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

It is adapted to arid environments and plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils.
