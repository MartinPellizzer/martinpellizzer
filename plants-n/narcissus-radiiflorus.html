Narcissus radiiflorus

Narcissus radiiflorus, commonly known as the radiate daffodil, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to the Iberian Peninsula and is characterized by its bright yellow, funnel-shaped flowers with a distinctive radiating pattern. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, with strap-like, grassy leaves. Its blooming period occurs in early spring, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

The species is also noted for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to naturalize in suitable conditions.
