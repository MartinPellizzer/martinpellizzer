Nasturtium lacerum

Nasturtium lacerum, commonly known as the fringed nasturtium, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange or yellow flowers and heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant is part of the Tropaeolaceae family and is known for its peppery taste, similar to watercress. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing as an annual in temperate climates.

Nasturtium lacerum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract beneficial insects to gardens.
