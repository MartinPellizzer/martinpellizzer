Nassella fabrisii

Nassella fabrisii, commonly known as the slender needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its fine, needle-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Nassella fabrisii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
