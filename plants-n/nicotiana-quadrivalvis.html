Nicotiana quadrivalvis

Nicotiana quadrivalvis, commonly known as the four-valved tobacco, is a herbaceous plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant is characterized by its alternate, ovate leaves and clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers. Its most distinctive feature is the four-valved capsule that forms after flowering, giving it its scientific name.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
