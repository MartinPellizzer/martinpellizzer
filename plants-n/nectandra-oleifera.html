Nectandra oleifera

Nectandra oleifera, commonly known as the oil tree or sweet wood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and fragrant flowers. The plant is notable for its ability to produce a valuable essential oil, which is extracted from its leaves and bark. Its growth habit includes a straight trunk and dense foliage, making it suitable for ornamental and commercial purposes.

Nectandra oleifera thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland tropical forests.
