Nomaphila ciliata

Nomaphila ciliata, commonly known as the fringed leaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped leaves with fine, ciliate margins. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in a loose spike. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts for its unique appearance.
