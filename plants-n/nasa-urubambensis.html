Nasa urubambensis

Nasa urubambensis, commonly known as the Andean moonflower, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that open at night. The plant produces small, round berries that contain the seeds. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its unique blooming pattern and ornamental value, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
