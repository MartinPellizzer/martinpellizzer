Nicotiana tabacum var. latior

Nicotiana tabacum var. latior, commonly known as the broadleaf tobacco, is a variant of the tobacco plant characterized by its wider leaves compared to other varieties.

It is native to the Americas and belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshade plants. The leaves of this variety are typically larger and more elongated, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening. It produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are usually pink or white, adding aesthetic value to gardens.

This plant is also used in some traditional medicinal practices, though it contains nicotine and should be handled with care.
