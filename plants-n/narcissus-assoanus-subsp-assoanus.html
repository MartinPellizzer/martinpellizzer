Narcissus assoanus subsp. assoanus

Narcissus assoanus subsp.

assoanus, commonly known as the Asso's daffodil, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and a single, trumpet-shaped flower that is typically yellow in color.

The plant produces a large, cup-shaped bloom with a prominent central corolla. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and is commonly cultivated in gardens.
