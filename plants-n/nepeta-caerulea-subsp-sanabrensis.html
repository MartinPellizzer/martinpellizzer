Nepeta caerulea subsp. sanabrensis

Nepeta caerulea subsp.

sanabrensis, commonly known as blue catmint, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features upright stems covered in fine hairs and produces clusters of small, sky-blue to lavender flowers.

The leaves are lance-shaped, aromatic, and often have a minty scent. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
