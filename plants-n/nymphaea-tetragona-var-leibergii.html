Nymphaea tetragona var. leibergii

Nymphaea tetragona var. leibergii, commonly known as the yellow water lily, is a perennial aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features broad, heart-shaped leaves that float on the water surface, supported by long, slender petioles. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that open in the morning and close in the afternoon. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often in ponds and lakes.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for water gardens and conservation efforts.
