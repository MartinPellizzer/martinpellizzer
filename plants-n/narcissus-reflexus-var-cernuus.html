Narcissus reflexus var. cernuus

Narcissus reflexus var. cernuus, commonly known as the drooping daffodil, is a botanical variant of the genus Narcissus.

It is characterized by its nodding, reflexed petals and a prominent, trumpet-shaped corona. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30-50 cm, with strap-like, basal leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in woodland edges or meadows.

This variety is prized for its delicate appearance and early spring blooming habit.
