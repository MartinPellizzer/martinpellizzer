Nicandra yacheriana

Nicandra yacheriana, commonly known as the Yacherian nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and peppers. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, a common reproductive strategy in the Solanaceae family.

The plant contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially harmful if ingested.
