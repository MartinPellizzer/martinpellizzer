Nothapodytes obscura

Nothapodytes obscura, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic flowering plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is an epiphytic orchid, meaning it grows on the surface of trees without deriving nutrients from them. The plant is characterized by its pale, almost translucent leaves and its striking, white, fragrant flowers. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires specific conditions to flourish.

Due to its elusive nature and habitat specificity, it is considered a conservation concern.
