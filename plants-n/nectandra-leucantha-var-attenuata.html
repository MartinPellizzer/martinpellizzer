Nectandra leucantha var. attenuata

Nectandra leucantha var. attenuata, commonly known as the white-flowered nectandra, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces drupes that are typically used in traditional medicine for their purported therapeutic properties. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and pharmacological studies.
