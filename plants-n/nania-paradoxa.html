Nania paradoxa

Nania paradoxa, commonly known as the paradox plant, is a rare species native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, spiral-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers during the summer months, which are clustered in tight, spherical inflorescences. Its growth habit is slow, with the plant often forming compact, shrub-like structures.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, Nania paradoxa is considered a conservation concern.
