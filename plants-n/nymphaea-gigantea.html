Nymphaea gigantea

Nymphaea gigantea, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy, floating leaves that can reach up to 3 feet in diameter. The plant produces fragrant, white or pink flowers that bloom in the early morning and close by midday. Its roots anchor in the mud at the bottom of still water bodies, while its rhizomes spread horizontally.

Nymphaea gigantea plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various water organisms.
