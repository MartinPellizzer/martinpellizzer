Nycteranthus longispinulus

Nycteranthus longispinulus, commonly known as the long-spined nightshade, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically violet or purple in color. The plant produces berries that are toxic to humans and animals, containing alkaloids that can be harmful if ingested. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its toxicity, it is important to handle the plant with care.
