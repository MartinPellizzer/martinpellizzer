Neolehmannia

Neolehmannia, commonly known as the African violet, is a genus of flowering plants native to tropical Africa.

These plants are characterized by their rosettes of fleshy, dark green leaves and showy, colorful flowers that bloom in the spring. Neolehmannia species are typically compact and thrive in humid, well-drained soil conditions. They are popular as houseplants due to their attractive appearance and relatively easy care requirements.

The flowers are often borne on long, slender stems, adding an elegant touch to their foliage.
