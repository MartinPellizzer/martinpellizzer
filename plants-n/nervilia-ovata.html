Nervilia ovata

Nervilia ovata, commonly known as the broad-leafed fern, is a species of flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, hair-like structures. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which bloom during the warmer months. Nervilia ovata thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories or near water sources.

Its unique morphology and ecological preferences make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
