Nectandra cayoana

Nectandra cayoana, commonly known as the Cayoan sandalwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Caribbean region.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes other aromatic wood species. The tree is characterized by its dense, fragrant wood that is highly valued for its unique scent and durability. Its leaves are typically elliptical and glossy, with a strong, spicy aroma.

Nectandra cayoana is often found in moist lowland forests and is known for its ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
