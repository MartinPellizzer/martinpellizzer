Notocactus concinnus f. agnetae

Notocactus concinnus f. agnetae, commonly known as the Agneta's elegant cactus, is a rare and striking hybrid within the Cactaceae family.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are often adorned with vibrant, tubular flowers in shades of yellow and orange. The plant is native to the arid regions of South America, particularly in Brazil, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and cactus enthusiasts.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a challenging plant to cultivate outside its natural environment.
