Narcissus requienii var. pallens

Narcissus requienii var. pallens, commonly known as the pale narcissus, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers with white petals and a yellow center. The plant typically blooms in early spring, adding a delicate touch to gardens and natural habitats. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile choice for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is often cultivated by plant enthusiasts and conservationists alike.
