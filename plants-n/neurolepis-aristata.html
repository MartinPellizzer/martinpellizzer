Neurolepis aristata

Neurolepis aristata, commonly known as the arrowleaf neurolepis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, arrow-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It produces small, yellowish to brownish flowers that are arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

Neurolepis aristata is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils and open woodlands.
