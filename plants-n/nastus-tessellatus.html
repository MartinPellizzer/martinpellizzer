Nastus tessellatus

Nastus tessellatus, commonly known as the tessellated nasturtium, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that come in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive mottled or tessellated pattern, giving it its common name. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Nastus tessellatus is also valued for its edible flowers and leaves, which have a peppery taste similar to watercress.
