Nepeta freitagii

Nepeta freitagii, commonly known as the Freitag's catmint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is often fragrant and can emit a mint-like scent when crushed.

Nepeta freitagii is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
