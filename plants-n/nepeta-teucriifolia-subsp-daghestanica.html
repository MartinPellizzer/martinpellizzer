Nepeta teucriifolia subsp. daghestanica

Nepeta teucriifolia subsp.

daghestanica, commonly known as the Daghestan catmint, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region. It features aromatic, slender stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers clustered in whorls along the stems, typical of the Nepeta genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its fragrance and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
