Narcissus × burbidgei

Narcissus × burbidgei, commonly known as the Giant Daffodil, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Narcissus tazetta and Narcissus cyclamineus.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers with a prominent, often recurved, central corona. The plant is native to the Mediterranean region but has been widely cultivated in gardens around the world. It typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and produces multiple blooms per stem.

The leaves are long, narrow, and grass-like, with a prominent midrib.
