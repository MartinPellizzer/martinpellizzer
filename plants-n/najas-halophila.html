Najas halophila

Najas halophila, commonly known as the saltwater watermilfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to brackish and coastal waters.

It is characterized by its narrow, ribbon-like leaves that are arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically borne in clusters near the water's surface. It thrives in saline environments and is often found in estuaries and tidal marshes.

Najas halophila plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
