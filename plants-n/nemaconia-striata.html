Nemaconia striata

Nemaconia striata, commonly known as the striped morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Nemaconia striata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
