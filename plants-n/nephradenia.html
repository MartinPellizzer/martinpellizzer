Nephradenia

Nephradenia, commonly known as the kidneyleaf, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Amaranthaceae.

It consists of several species, most of which are native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East. The plants are characterized by their succulent, often hairy leaves that resemble the shape of a kidney. They produce small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or reddish in color.

Nephradenia species are often used in traditional medicine and are valued for their drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
