Notocactus succineus

Notocactus succineus, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a slow-growing, globular cactus characterized by its dense, spiny areoles and a bright yellow to green stem. The plant is known for its ability to store water in its thick, fleshy stem, which is a typical adaptation for arid environments. It produces white flowers during the spring and summer months, adding ornamental value to its appearance.

Due to its unique shape and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for indoor and outdoor cultivation.
