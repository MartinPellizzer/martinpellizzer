Nepeta glabrescens

Nepeta glabrescens, commonly known as catnip, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers and has square, hairy stems typical of the mint family. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a strong, minty scent.

Nepeta glabrescens is known for its effects on cats and its use in traditional medicine.
