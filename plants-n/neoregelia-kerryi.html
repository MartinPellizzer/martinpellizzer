Neoregelia kerryi

Neoregelia kerryi, commonly known as the Kerry's bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the rainforests of Brazil.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, colorful inflorescence that can range from red to orange. The plant features a rosette of broad, waxy leaves that collect rainwater, creating a microhabitat for small organisms. Its flowering period typically occurs during the warmer months, and it produces showy, long-lasting blooms.

Neoregelia kerryi is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
