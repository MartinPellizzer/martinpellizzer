Nyssa sinensis var. oblongifolia

Nyssa sinensis var. oblongifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf Chinese sweetgum, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and alternate, ovate to elliptic leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by spherical, spiky fruit clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ornamental and ecological purposes.
