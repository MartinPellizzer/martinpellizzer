Najas marina var. californica

Najas marina var. californica, commonly known as the California watermilfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in California.

It belongs to the Hydrocharitaceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, feather-like leaves that help it absorb sunlight in shallow waters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically found in early summer. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

However, it can become invasive in certain regions, altering native plant communities and impacting water quality.
