Neisosperma acuminatum

Neisosperma acuminatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sandthorn, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and small, alternate leaves that are typically narrow and pointed. The plant produces yellowish flowers followed by small, fleshy fruits that are edible but have a strong, sour taste. It is adapted to survive in harsh conditions with limited water availability.

Neisosperma acuminatum plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for various desert wildlife.
