Nageia bracteata

Nageia bracteata, commonly known as the bracted nageia, is a species of conifer native to Southeast Asia.

It is a medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant has needle-like leaves that are arranged in spirals along the branches. Its cones are woody and contain seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Nageia bracteata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
