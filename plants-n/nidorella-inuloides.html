Nidorella inuloides

Nidorella inuloides, commonly known as the golden crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow-orange ray florets and dense, cylindrical disk florets. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial with a bushy appearance. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Nidorella inuloides is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
