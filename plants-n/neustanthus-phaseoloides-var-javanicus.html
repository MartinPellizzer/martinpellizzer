Neustanthus phaseoloides var. javanicus

Neustanthus phaseoloides var. javanicus, commonly known as the Java bean vine, is a climbing legume native to Southeast Asia.

It features trifoliate leaves with stipules and produces small, yellowish flowers that are often clustered in racemes. The plant bears pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds, similar to those of common beans. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in humid, shaded environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ethnobotanical studies.
