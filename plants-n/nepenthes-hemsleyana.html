Nepenthes hemsleyana

Nepenthes hemsleyana, commonly known as the Hemsley's pitcher plant, is a tropical carnivorous plant native to Borneo.

It is characterized by its large, elongated pitcher-shaped leaves that secrete a digestive fluid to trap and digest insects. The plant's pitchers are often adorned with intricate patterns and can vary in color from green to red, depending on light exposure. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires consistent moisture and nutrient-poor soil.

Due to its unique adaptations, it is a popular specimen among plant enthusiasts and botanists alike.
