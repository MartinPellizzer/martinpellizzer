Nyctanthes hirsuta

Nyctanthes hirsuta, commonly known as the night-flowering jasmine or hirsute nightshade, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, hairy leaves and clusters of fragrant, white flowers that bloom at night. The plant produces small, yellowish berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.

Nyctanthes hirsuta is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic uses.
