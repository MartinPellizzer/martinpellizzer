Neolitsea sieboldii var. angustifolia

Neolitsea sieboldii var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved litsea, is a species native to eastern Asia, particularly Japan and China.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments. The plant features lance-shaped, aromatic leaves with a smooth upper surface and a hairy underside. Its small, fragrant flowers are usually white or pale yellow, and it produces small, berry-like fruits that are edible for some bird species.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in traditional herbal medicine.
