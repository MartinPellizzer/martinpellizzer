Narcissus papyraceus subsp. pachybolbus

Narcissus papyraceus subsp.

pachybolbus, commonly known as the broad-leafed daffodil, is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family. It is characterized by its distinctive, broad, strap-like leaves and large, showy yellow flowers with a white or green throat.

The plant is native to southern Europe and thrives in well-drained soils in full sun. It is a perennial herb that blooms in early spring, often before the leaves fully expand.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens.
