Neoboutonia macrocalyx

Neoboutonia macrocalyx, commonly known as the giant panda orchid, is a striking terrestrial orchid native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 15 centimeters in diameter, with a distinctive white and green coloration resembling a panda's fur. The plant features a rhizomatous growth habit and produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in specialized orchid collections.
