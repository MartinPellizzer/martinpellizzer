Notocactus grossei

Notocactus grossei, commonly known as the giant night-blooming cactus, is a striking member of the Cactaceae family native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stems that can grow up to several meters in height, covered in clusters of spines. The plant produces large, showy flowers that open at night, typically in shades of white, yellow, or pink. Its thick, fleshy stems are adapted to store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments.

Notocactus grossei is a popular choice for collectors due to its unique appearance and nocturnal blooming habit.
