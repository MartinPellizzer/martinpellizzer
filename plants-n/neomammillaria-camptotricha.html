Neomammillaria camptotricha

Neomammillaria camptotricha, commonly known as the Mexican snowball cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and dense covering of white to pale blue spines. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers during the spring and summer months. It is well adapted to arid environments, with thick, fleshy stems that store water.

Neomammillaria camptotricha is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
