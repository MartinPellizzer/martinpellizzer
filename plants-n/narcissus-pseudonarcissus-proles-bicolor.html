Narcissus pseudonarcissus proles bicolor

Narcissus pseudonarcissus proles bicolor, commonly known as the Double Early Daffodil, is a botanical hybrid within the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, double-petaled flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, sword-shaped leaves and a short, sturdy stem. Its flowers are typically yellow with a white or cream center, creating a striking visual contrast.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is widely cultivated in gardens for its early blooming habit.
