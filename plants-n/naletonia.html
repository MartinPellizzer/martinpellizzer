Naletonia

Naletonia, commonly known as the "lady's mantle," is a genus of flowering plants in the family Saxifragaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, shield-like leaves that often have a fuzzy or hairy texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that can range in color from white to pink or red. Naletonia species are typically herbaceous perennials that thrive in moist, shady environments.

They are often found in woodland areas and are valued for their ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
