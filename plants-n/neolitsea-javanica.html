Neolitsea javanica

Neolitsea javanica, commonly known as the Java laurustin, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in humid, well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually elliptical or ovate, with a glossy green surface and a slightly serrated margin.

Neolitsea javanica is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
