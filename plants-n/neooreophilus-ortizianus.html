Neooreophilus ortizianus

Neooreophilus ortizianus, commonly known as the silverleaf orchid, is a rare and striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a silvery, waxy coating, which helps reflect excess sunlight in its native habitat. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale pink, often with intricate patterns on the petals. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in the understory of tropical forests.

Due to its limited distribution and unique morphology, it is considered a valuable subject for botanical research and conservation efforts.
