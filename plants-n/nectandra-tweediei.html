Nectandra tweediei

Nectandra tweediei, commonly known as the Guiana wood or yellowheart, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, reddish-brown heartwood. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by small, round berries. Its leaves are alternate, elliptic, and have a smooth, glossy surface.

Nectandra tweediei is valued for its durable wood, which is used in furniture and cabinetry.
