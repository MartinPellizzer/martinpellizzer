Neomammillaria cerralboa

Neomammillaria cerralboa, commonly known as the Mexican milk barrel cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are covered in white, woolly spines. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Neomammillaria cerralboa is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
