Neomarica latifolia

Neomarica latifolia, commonly known as the peace lily, is a tropical evergreen plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, waxy, dark green leaves and striking white or ivory flowers that resemble calla lilies. The plant thrives in humid environments and prefers indirect sunlight, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening. It is known for its ability to purify the air and is relatively easy to care for.

Neomarica latifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in ornamental horticulture.
