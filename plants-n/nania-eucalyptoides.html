Nania eucalyptoides

Nania eucalyptoides, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallee, is a shrub or small tree native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and smooth, often greyish bark. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers, which are clustered in umbels.

Nania eucalyptoides is a member of the Myrtaceae family, sharing some botanical features with eucalyptus species.
