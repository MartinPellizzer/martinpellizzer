Nanorrhinum scariosepalum

Nanorrhinum scariosepalum, commonly known as the scarlet sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and produces small, showy flowers in shades of red and orange. The plant has hairy, succulent leaves that help it retain moisture in dry environments. Its flowers are typically clustered in dense inflorescences, and the plant is adapted to thrive in sandy or rocky soils.

Nanorrhinum scariosepalum is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
