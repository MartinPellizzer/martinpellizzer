Nymphaea advena

Nymphaea advena, commonly known as the Indian lotus, is a water-living plant belonging to the Nymphaeaceae family.

It is native to the wetlands and ponds of Asia, particularly in India and Southeast Asia. The plant features large, showy flowers that can be pink, white, or yellow, depending on the variety. Its leaves are broad and floating, with a waxy surface that helps repel water.

Nymphaea advena is known for its ability to thrive in still or slow-moving freshwater environments.
