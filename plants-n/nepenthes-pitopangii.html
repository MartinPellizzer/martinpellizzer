Nepenthes pitopangii

Nepenthes pitopangii, commonly known as the Pitopang's pitcher plant, is a unique carnivorous plant native to the high-altitude regions of Borneo.

It is characterized by its large, elongated pitcher-shaped traps that secrete a digestive fluid to trap and digest insects. The plant's pitchers are typically green or reddish in color, with a waxy coating that aids in preventing the escape of captured prey. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires specific soil conditions to grow successfully.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
