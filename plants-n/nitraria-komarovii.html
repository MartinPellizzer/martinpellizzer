Nitraria komarovii

Nitraria komarovii, commonly known as the desert date tree, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia, the Middle East, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and small, edible fruits that are often used in traditional cuisines. The plant is well adapted to harsh environments, with deep roots and a thick, waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for desert wildlife.

Nitraria komarovii is also valued for its hardy nature and potential use in reforestation projects in degraded lands.
