Narcissus moschatus var. cernuus

Narcissus moschatus var. cernuus, commonly known as the sweet garlic narcissus, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that droop downward, giving it a distinctive, bell-like appearance. The plant produces a strong, garlicky odor, which is most noticeable when the flowers are in bloom. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in well-drained soil in full sun to partial shade.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique scent.
