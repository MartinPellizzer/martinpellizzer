Nigritella nigra subsp. lithopolitanica

Nigritella nigra subsp.

lithopolitanica, commonly known as the rock nigritella, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to rocky and arid environments. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine pubescence.

The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically violet or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Due to its specialized habitat, it is considered a rare and endemic species in its native range.
