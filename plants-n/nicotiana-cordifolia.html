Nicotiana cordifolia

Nicotiana cordifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf tobacco, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family.

It is native to the southern United States and northern Mexico, where it thrives in warm, sunny environments. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink and purple. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and scientific study.
