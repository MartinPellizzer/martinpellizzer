Nicotiana multiflora

Nicotiana multiflora, commonly known as the multiflora tobacco, is a herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. This species is characterized by its trailing growth habit, hairy stems, and clusters of small, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a rough texture and emit a strong, often unpleasant odor.

It is primarily cultivated for its ornamental value and has also been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
