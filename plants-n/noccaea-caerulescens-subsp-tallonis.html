Noccaea caerulescens subsp. tallonis

Noccaea caerulescens subsp.

tallonis, commonly known as the tall miner's lettuce, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves that are typically blue-green in color, which gives it its common name.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, and it is often found in nutrient-rich soils, particularly those contaminated with heavy metals. It is a member of the Brassicaceae family and is known for its ability to hyperaccumulate zinc and cadmium.

This species is of interest in phytoremediation studies due to its capacity to absorb and store heavy metals in its tissues.
