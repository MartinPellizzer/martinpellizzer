Nemacladus tenuis var. tenuis

Nemacladus tenuis var. tenuis, commonly known as the slender leaf fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, narrow, and elongated fronds that arise from a creeping rhizome. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are usually dark green and have a leathery texture, with a subtle, fern-like appearance.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
