Nematolepis ovatifolia

Nematolepis ovatifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed grass, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to Australia and is characterized by its flat, grass-like leaves that are often broader than they are long. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of grasses.

Nematolepis ovatifolia is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
