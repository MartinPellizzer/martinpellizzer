Nectandra striata

Nectandra striata, commonly known as the striped sandarac tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its aromatic, dark-colored wood. The plant has simple, opposite, and elliptical leaves with a rough texture. It produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by elongated, woody fruits.

The tree is valued for its durable wood and has been traditionally used in various cultural and medicinal practices.
