Normanbya merrillii

Normanbya merrillii, commonly known as the Wollongong bottle tree, is a unique and rare species native to Queensland, Australia.

It is a member of the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive bottle-shaped trunk, which stores water to help it survive in arid conditions. The plant has small, narrow leaves and produces white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in winter.

It is often found in rocky outcrops and is adapted to low-nutrient soils, making it a resilient and specialized flora species.
