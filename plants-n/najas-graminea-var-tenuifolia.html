Najas graminea var. tenuifolia

Najas graminea var. tenuifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaved watermilfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments.

It has long, slender, ribbon-like leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged, followed by buoyant, three-lobed fruits. It thrives in slow-moving to still waters with soft, muddy or sandy substrates.

This species plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
