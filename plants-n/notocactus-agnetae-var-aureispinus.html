Notocactus agnetae var. aureispinus

Notocactus agnetae var. aureispinus, commonly known as the golden spined notocactus, is a rare and unique cactus species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are covered in short, yellowish spines, giving it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are typically brief in duration. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xerophytic gardens.

Due to its rarity and specific care requirements, it is often cultivated by enthusiasts interested in rare succulents.
