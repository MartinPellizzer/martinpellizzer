Nierembergia pulchella

Nierembergia pulchella, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and prefers well-drained soil and full sun. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Nierembergia pulchella is also known for its ability to thrive in rocky or coastal environments.
