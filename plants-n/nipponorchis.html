Nipponorchis

Nipponorchis, commonly known as the Japanese orchid, is a rare and delicate species native to Japan.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, intricate flowers with a unique coloration pattern. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in mountainous regions. Its leaves are elongated and strap-like, and it produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, Nipponorchis is considered endangered in its natural range.
