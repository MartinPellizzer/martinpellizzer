Nepeta erecta

Nepeta erecta, commonly known as catmint, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces clusters of blue to purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its calming properties.

Nepeta erecta thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
