Neisosperma nakaianum

Neisosperma nakaianum, commonly known as the New Zealand mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant native to New Zealand.

It grows on the branches of various host trees, such as pohutukawa and kahikatea. The plant has small, leathery leaves and produces white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. Its berries are red and contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.

Neisosperma nakaianum plays a role in the ecological dynamics of its native habitat, though it is considered a potential threat to certain tree species.
