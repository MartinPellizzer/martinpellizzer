Narcissus linnaeanus var. antipolensis

Narcissus linnaeanus var. antipolensis, commonly known as the Antipolensis daffodil, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the island of Antipodes in the South Pacific.

It produces striking yellow flowers with a cup-shaped corona, typical of the Amaryllidaceae family. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in coastal or rocky habitats. Its bulbs are relatively small and require careful cultivation to prevent overgrowth.
