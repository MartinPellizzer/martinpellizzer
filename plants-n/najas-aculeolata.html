Najas aculeolata

Najas aculeolata, commonly known as the spiny watermilfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It has slender, branching stems that are often adorned with small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces tiny, green flowers that are typically found in clusters near the water surface. Najas aculeolata is characterized by its sharp, spine-like structures on its stems, which help in anchoring it in aquatic substrates.

It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
