Nastus verticillatus

Nastus verticillatus, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Mediterranean region.

It features clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has a sprawling growth habit with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with silver. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
