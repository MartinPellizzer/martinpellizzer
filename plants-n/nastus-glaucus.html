Nastus glaucus

Nastus glaucus, commonly known as the glaucous nasturtium, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, blue-green leaves and bright yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces small, edible seed pods that are often used in culinary applications. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Nastus glaucus is known for its ornamental value and mild peppery flavor in its leaves and seeds.
