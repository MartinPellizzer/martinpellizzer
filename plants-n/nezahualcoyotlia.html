Nezahualcoyotlia

Nezahualcoyotlia, commonly known as the "flower of the dead," is a unique plant native to Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant is known for its striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Nezahualcoyotlia has been traditionally used in Mexican folk medicine for its purported healing abilities.
