Narcissus bulbocodium subsp. graellsii

Narcissus bulbocodium subsp.

graellsii, commonly known as the Graells' daffodil, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and a single, upright flower with a trumpet-shaped corona.

The plant produces yellow petals that are typically shorter than the perianth segments. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or open habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
