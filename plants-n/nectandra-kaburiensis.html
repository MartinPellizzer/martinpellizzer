Nectandra kaburiensis

Nectandra kaburiensis, commonly known as the Kaburi sandalwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the rainforests of Borneo.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark-colored wood that is highly valued for its fragrance and durability. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth, glossy surface. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by small, round fruits containing single seeds.

Due to its ecological and economic importance, it is also a subject of conservation efforts in its native habitat.
