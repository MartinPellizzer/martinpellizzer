Neomammillaria trichacantha

Neomammillaria trichacantha, commonly known as the ghost cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that are often covered in a white, powdery coating. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and slow growth rate, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
