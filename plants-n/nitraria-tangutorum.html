Nitraria tangutorum

Nitraria tangutorum, commonly known as the Chinese date tree, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its small, fleshy fruits that resemble dates, which are edible and have a sweet, aromatic flavor. The plant is adapted to harsh environments, with thick leaves and a deep root system to conserve water. It plays a role in desert ecosystems and has been used in traditional medicine.

Nitraria tangutorum is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to stabilize soil in degraded landscapes.
