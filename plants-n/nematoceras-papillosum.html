Nematoceras papillosum

Nematoceras papillosum, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or greenish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Nematoceras papillosum is also known for its ability to spread rapidly, making it both a valuable forage plant and a potential weed.
