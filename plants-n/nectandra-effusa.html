Nectandra effusa

Nectandra effusa, commonly known as the broadleaf sandarac tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic foliage and rough, dark bark. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers followed by elliptical, woody fruits containing flat, winged seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its wood is valued for its aromatic properties and has been traditionally used in incense and varnishes.
