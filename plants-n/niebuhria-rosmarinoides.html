Niebuhria rosmarinoides

Niebuhria rosmarinoides, commonly known as silverleaf sage, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features silvery-gray foliage with lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, fuzzy hairs. The plant produces small, pale blue to violet flowers in whorled clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its aromatic leaves and ornamental value make it a popular choice for drought-tolerant gardens.
