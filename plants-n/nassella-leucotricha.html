Nassella leucotricha

Nassella leucotricha, commonly known as silver hairgrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-tipped leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed areas such as roadsides and prairies. It produces small, spikelike inflorescences that contain tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often used in landscaping and restoration projects.
