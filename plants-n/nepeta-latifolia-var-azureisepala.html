Nepeta latifolia var. azureisepala

Nepeta latifolia var. azureisepala, commonly known as blueflower catmint, is a cultivar of the catmint plant native to Europe.

It is characterized by its broad, aromatic leaves and striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 feet and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is a hardy perennial that is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.

Its foliage and flowers contribute to its popularity in both ornamental and medicinal horticulture.
