Nigritella nigra lusus fulva

Nigritella nigra lusus fulva, commonly known as the golden-black orchid, is a rare and striking flowering plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, velvety leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from deep purple to golden yellow. The plant belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its unique, intricate floral structure that attracts specific pollinators. Its growth is slow, and it thrives in high humidity and partial shade, making it a challenging species to cultivate.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and is protected in many regions.
