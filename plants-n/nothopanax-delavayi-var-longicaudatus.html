Nothopanax delavayi var. longicaudatus

Nothopanax delavayi var. longicaudatus, commonly known as the long-tailed nothopanax, is a variant of the nothopanax genus native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree characterized by its compound leaves and panicle-like inflorescences. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in dense, branching clusters. Its fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that contains a single seed.

This species is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique morphological traits and potential ecological significance.
