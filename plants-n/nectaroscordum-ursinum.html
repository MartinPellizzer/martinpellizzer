Nectaroscordum ursinum

Nectaroscordum ursinum, commonly known as the bear's garlic, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a bulbous base with narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers. The plant is characterized by its strong, garlic-like odor, which is most noticeable when the flowers are in bloom. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in rocky or dry habitats.

Nectaroscordum ursinum is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its historical use in herbal medicine.
