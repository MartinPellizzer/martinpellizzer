Najas fucoides

Najas fucoides, commonly known as the common water plantain, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Najadaceae and is characterized by its narrow, ribbon-like leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged, followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits that release minute seeds. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various water organisms.

Najas fucoides is often found in still or slow-moving freshwater such as ponds, lakes, and ditches.
