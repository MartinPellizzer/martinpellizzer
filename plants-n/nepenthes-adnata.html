Nepenthes adnata

Nepenthes adnata, commonly known as the Bornean pitcher plant, is a carnivorous plant native to Borneo.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow pitcher-shaped leaves that secrete a digestive fluid to trap and digest insects. The plant's pitchers are typically green or reddish in color and have a waxy coating that helps retain moisture. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires high levels of rainfall.

Nepenthes adnata is a popular species among carnivorous plant enthusiasts due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
