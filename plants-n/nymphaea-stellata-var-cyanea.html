Nymphaea stellata var. cyanea

Nymphaea stellata var. cyanea, commonly known as the blue lotus, is a striking aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features showy, water-lily-like flowers that range in color from white to deep blue, with a distinctive violet or blue center. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in ponds and lakes. Its leaves are broad and rounded, floating on the water's surface, while its roots anchor in the muddy substrate.

The blue lotus is valued for its ornamental beauty and has cultural significance in various regions.
