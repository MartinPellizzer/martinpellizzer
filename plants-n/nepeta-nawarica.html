Nepeta nawarica

Nepeta nawarica, commonly known as Mexican catmint, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal remedies for their mild sedative properties.

Nepeta nawarica thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
