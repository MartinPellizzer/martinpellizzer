Nicotiana × sanderae

Nicotiana × sanderae, commonly known as the sanderae tobacco, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Nicotiana sanderae and Nicotiana tabacum.

It is a herbaceous perennial native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant produces trumpet-shaped blossoms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and botanical collections.

Its botanical features include hairy stems, alternate leaves, and a distinct, aromatic scent.
