Narcissus pallidiflorus subsp. asturicus

Narcissus pallidiflorus subsp.

asturicus, commonly known as the Asturian daffodil, is a subspecies native to the mountainous regions of northern Spain. It is characterized by its pale yellow flowers with a white center, which bloom in early spring.

The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to cooler, humid climates. It has narrow, strap-like leaves and produces a single flower per stem.

This subspecies is of particular interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and unique floral morphology.
