Nephrodium ecklonii

Nephrodium ecklonii, commonly known as the African shield fern, is a species of fern native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, leathery, shield-shaped fronds that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments, typically found in forest understories or along riverbanks. Its sporangia are clustered in sori on the underside of the fronds, a common feature among ferns.

Nephrodium ecklonii plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various small organisms in its native ecosystem.
