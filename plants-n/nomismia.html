Nomismia

Nomismia, commonly known as the "false dandelion," is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

It consists of several species, most notably Nomismia hirtella, which is native to South America. The plants are characterized by their basal rosettes of hairy, lance-shaped leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. They typically grow in dry, rocky habitats and are often found in open grasslands or along roadsides.

Due to their unique appearance and ecological role, they are of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
