Nepsera Glandulosa

Glandular nepeta, scientifically known as Nepsera glandulosa, is a unique plant species native to specific regions of southern Africa. It is distinguished by its hairy, glandular leaves that exude a strong, often minty or citrus-like scent. This plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is commonly found in arid or semi-arid environments. Its aromatic properties have made it a subject of interest for both botanists and herbalists. Despite its modest appearance, Nepsera glandulosa holds cultural and medicinal significance in local communities.
