Neomammillaria xanthina

Neomammillaria xanthina, commonly known as the yellow mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in white to yellow spines and small, rounded tubercles. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It is a slow-growing species that thrives in well-draining soil and full sun.

Neomammillaria xanthina is often cultivated as a decorative ornamental plant due to its compact size and attractive appearance.
