Nepeta glabra

Nepeta glabra, commonly known as catnip, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite leaves that are ovate to lanceolate in shape. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in whorls along the stem. It is widely cultivated for its aromatic properties and its effects on cats.

Nepeta glabra is also valued in traditional medicine for its calming and antispasmodic properties.
