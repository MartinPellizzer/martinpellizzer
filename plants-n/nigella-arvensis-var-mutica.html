Nigella arvensis var. mutica

Nigella arvensis var. mutica, commonly known as the hairy cowplant, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its seeds are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice in some cuisines.

Nigella arvensis var. mutica thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields and along roadsides.
