Norantea aurantiaca

Norantea aurantiaca, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, giving it its scientific name. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Norantea aurantiaca is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its vibrant blooms.
