Nepeta pamiroalaica

Nepeta pamiroalaica, commonly known as the Pamir catmint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its calming properties.

Nepeta pamiroalaica thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rocky gardens.
