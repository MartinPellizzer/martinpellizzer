Navia hechtioides

Navia hechtioides, commonly known as the Mexican spiderflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited for arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, and it produces small, spiky fruits that contain seeds.

Navia hechtioides is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and resilience.
