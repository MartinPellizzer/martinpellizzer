Nabalus crepidineus

Nabalus crepidineus, commonly known as the crested bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, hairy texture.

Nabalus crepidineus is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in alpine gardens.
