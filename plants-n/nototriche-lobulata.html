Nototriche lobulata

Nototriche lobulata, commonly known as the mountain daisy, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool and dry conditions. Its leaves are usually hairy and lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The flowers bloom in shades of yellow and white, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
