Nipponocalamus virens var. purpurascens

Nipponocalamus virens var. purpurascens, commonly known as the purple-flowered bamboo, is a variant of the Japanese bamboo species characterized by its striking purple inflorescences.

It typically grows as a clump-forming perennial, reaching heights of up to 2 meters with slender, hollow stems. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
