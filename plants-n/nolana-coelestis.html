Nolana coelestis

Nolana coelestis, commonly known as the skyflower, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It is a member of the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, often in rocky soils, and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its flowers are small and clustered, forming dense inflorescences that bloom in late spring or early summer.

Nolana coelestis is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
