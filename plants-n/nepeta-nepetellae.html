Nepeta nepetellae

Nepeta nepetellae, commonly known as the Corsican catmint, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and spikes of blue to purple flowers. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its fragrant herbs and medicinal properties. Nepeta nepetellae thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its historical use in herbal medicine and its appeal to pollinators like bees and butterflies further highlight its ecological and botanical significance.
