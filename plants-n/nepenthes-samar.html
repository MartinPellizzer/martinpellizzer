Nepenthes samar

Nepenthes samar, commonly known as the Samar pitcher plant, is a tropical vine native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its distinctive pitcher-shaped leaves that secrete a nutrient-rich liquid to trap and digest insects. The plant's morphology includes elongated, spiraling stems and waxy, glandular surfaces on its pitchers. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in lowland and montane rainforests.

Its unique adaptation for nutrient acquisition makes it an important species in ecological studies of tropical ecosystems.
