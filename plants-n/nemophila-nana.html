Nemophila nana

Nemophila nana, commonly known as the dwarf blue-eyed Mary, is a low-growing annual herb native to the western United States.

It features small, delicate blue or violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are typically less than 2 inches in length. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sun, often found in open woodlands and meadows.

Its compact size and vibrant flowers make it a popular choice for rock gardens and wildflower displays.
