Nolina bigelovii subsp. parryi

Nolina bigelovii subsp.

parryi, commonly known as the Parry nolina, is a shrub-like succulent native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color, arranged in a dense rosette.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish-white in hue. It is adapted to arid environments and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its botanical structure reflects its evolutionary adaptation to harsh, dry climates.
