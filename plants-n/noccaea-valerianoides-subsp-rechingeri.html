Noccaea valerianoides subsp. rechingeri

Noccaea valerianoides subsp.

rechingeri, commonly known as the alpine nailgrass, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Alps and Carpathian regions. It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and hairy, glandular leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in cold, temperate climates. It produces small, oval-shaped seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its colonization of mountainous areas.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
