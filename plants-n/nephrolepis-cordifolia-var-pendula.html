Nephrolepis cordifolia var. pendula

Nephrolepis cordifolia var. pendula, commonly known as the weeping maidenhair fern, is a variant of the common maidenhair fern characterized by its gracefully drooping fronds.

This plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including parts of India and Southeast Asia. Its fronds are typically delicate, with a feathery appearance and a dark green color, often featuring a slightly glossy surface. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires consistent moisture and well-draining soil.

It is prized for its ornamental value and is often used in indoor gardening due to its air-purifying qualities and aesthetic appeal.
