Nephrodium patens var. invisum

Nephrodium patens var. invisum, commonly known as the invisible nephroid, is a rare and enigmatic fern species characterized by its unique morphological features.

It exhibits a compact, creeping growth habit with finely divided, lanceolate fronds that are often concealed beneath a dense mat of rhizomes. The plant's spore-bearing structures, or sporangia, are typically hidden within the foliage, contributing to its elusive nature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions with high humidity.

Due to its rarity and cryptic appearance, it remains a subject of interest for botanists specializing in fern taxonomy and conservation.
