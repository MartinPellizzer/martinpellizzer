Neosasamorpha asagishiana

Neosasamorpha asagishiana, commonly known as the Asagishian flameleaf, is a rare flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red and orange inflorescences, which bloom during the late summer months. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a glossy surface and a unique, aromatic scent. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in dense forest understories.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and is protected in several conservation areas.
