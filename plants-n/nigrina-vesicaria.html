Nigrina vesicaria

Nigrina vesicaria, commonly known as the blister navel orange, is a citrus species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery rind that can develop blisters, giving it its common name. The fruit is typically small, round, and has a yellow-orange color when mature. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as for its edible fruit.
