Neja gracilis

Neja gracilis, commonly known as the slender neja, is a delicate flowering plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that grow in dense clumps, often reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or pale blue flowers during the spring and early summer months. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and requires consistent watering to maintain its health.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its ornamental value.
