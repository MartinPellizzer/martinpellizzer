Notocactus scopa var. cobrensis

Notocactus scopa var. cobrensis, commonly known as the Cobrensis Hedgecactus, is a rare and striking cactus native to the arid regions of northern Chile.

It features a dense, spiny structure composed of cylindrical stems that grow in a bush-like formation. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are borne on short pedicels. Its thick, fleshy stems are adapted to store water, allowing it to thrive in harsh, dry environments.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is highly valued in botanical collections and conservation efforts.
