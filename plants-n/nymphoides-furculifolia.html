Nymphoides furculifolia

Nymphoides furculifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved water plantain, is a semi-aquatic herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams. The plant features broad, arrow-shaped leaves that are often floating or partially submerged. Its flowers are small, white or pale pink, and clustered in dense inflorescences.

It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
