Nepeta podostachys subsp. paulsenii

Nepeta podostachys subsp.

paulsenii, commonly known as the mountain catmint, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas. It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pale blue to violet flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowering period occurs in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its fragrance and visual interest.
