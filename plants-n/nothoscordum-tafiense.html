Nothoscordum tafiense

Nothoscordum tafiense, commonly known as the Tafiense wild garlic, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America, particularly Ecuador.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its garlic-like odor and strap-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.
