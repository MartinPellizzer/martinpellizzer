Nephrodium sporodicum

Nephrodium sporodicum, commonly known as the sporadic kidney fern, is a unique fern species characterized by its distinctive, kidney-shaped fronds.

It belongs to the family Nephrodiaceae and is native to specific regions in Southeast Asia. The plant exhibits sporadic spore production, which is an unusual trait among ferns, contributing to its classification. Its fronds are typically leathery and have a rugged texture, with spores produced in small, clustered structures called sporangia.

This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
