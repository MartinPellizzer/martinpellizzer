Neltuma nigra

Neltuma nigra, commonly known as the black velvet fern, is a rare and striking plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage that resembles velvet, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid forests.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, Neltuma nigra is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
