Nepeta violacea

Nepeta violacea, commonly known as blue catmint, is a perennial herb native to Europe and western Asia.

It features square, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with blue or silver. The plant produces clusters of pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Nepeta violacea is valued for its aromatic foliage and is often used in gardens for its ornamental and pollinator-friendly qualities.
