Nomocharis wardii

Nomocharis wardii, commonly known as the Ward's lily, is a striking member of the Amaryllis family, native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that bear clusters of large, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and red. The plant produces strap-like, narrow leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. Its blooming period typically occurs during the summer months, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Nomocharis wardii is valued for its ornamental beauty and relatively low maintenance requirements.
