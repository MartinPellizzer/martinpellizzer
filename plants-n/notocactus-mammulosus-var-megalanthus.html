Notocactus mammulosus var. megalanthus

Notocactus mammulosus var. megalanthus, commonly known as the giant mammulose cactus, is a striking member of the Cactaceae family native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, ribbed stems that can grow up to several meters in height, often forming a tree-like structure. The plant produces clusters of vibrant yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a dramatic visual element to its appearance. Its thick, fleshy stems are adapted for water storage, allowing it to thrive in harsh, dry environments.

This variety is prized for its unique morphology and is often cultivated in desert gardens and greenhouses.
