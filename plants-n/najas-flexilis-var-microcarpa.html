Najas flexilis var. microcarpa

Najas flexilis var. microcarpa, commonly known as the small-fruited watermilfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, whorled leaves that are typically less than 1 cm in length. The plant produces tiny, clustered flowers that are often difficult to see, and it thrives in slow-moving to still waters. Its rapid growth and dense vegetation can alter water clarity and habitat structure.

It is an important component of wetland ecosystems, providing food and shelter for various aquatic organisms.
