Nama multiflora

Nama multiflora, commonly known as the many-flowered nama, is a hardy shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Nama multiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
