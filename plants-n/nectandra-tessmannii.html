Nectandra tessmannii

Nectandra tessmannii, commonly known as the black sandalwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its dark, aromatic wood and glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by small, round fruits containing a single seed. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland and hill forests.

Due to its valuable timber and medicinal properties, it is also a species of conservation concern.
