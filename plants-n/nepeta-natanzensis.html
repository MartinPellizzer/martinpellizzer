Nepeta natanzensis

Nepeta natanzensis, commonly known as the Persian catmint, is a perennial herb native to Iran.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their calming properties.

Nepeta natanzensis thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.
