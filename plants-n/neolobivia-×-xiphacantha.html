Neolobivia × xiphacantha

Neolobivia × xiphacantha, commonly known as the hybrid cactus, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and ability to store water in its fleshy tissues. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun exposure. Neolobivia × xiphacantha is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and resilience.

Its flowers, which can be yellow or red, bloom in the summer months, adding visual interest to the landscape.
