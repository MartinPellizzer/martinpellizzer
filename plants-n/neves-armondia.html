Neves-armondia

Neves-armondia, commonly known as the Brazilian starfruit or *Averrhoa carambola*, is a tropical fruit-bearing plant native to Southeast Asia and the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinct star-shaped fruit, which is typically yellow or orange when ripe. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate with a pointed tip and serrated edges. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Neves-armondia is often cultivated for its edible fruit, which is rich in vitamin C and has a tangy, sweet flavor.
