Nidorella spartioides

Nidorella spartioides, commonly known as the rock daphne, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in dry and sunny environments. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, providing a dense foliage that helps it thrive in harsh conditions.

Nidorella spartioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and aromatic qualities.
