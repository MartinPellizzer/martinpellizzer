Nepenthes gracilis var. longinodis

Nepenthes gracilis var. longinodis, commonly known as the slender pitcher plant, is a unique carnivorous plant native to Borneo.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow pitchers that are often covered in a dense layer of fine trichomes. The plant produces a distinctive red or orange nectar that attracts insects, which then fall into the pitcher and are digested by the plant's digestive enzymes. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and exhibit a striking contrast between the green upper surface and the red underside.

This variety is particularly adapted to grow in shaded, humid environments, making it a fascinating subject for botanical study.
