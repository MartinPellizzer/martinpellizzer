Newmania serpens

Newmania serpens, commonly known as the serpent's tail, is a unique and rare plant species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a climbing plant characterized by its long, flexible stems and distinctive, waxy leaves that often have a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color, followed by capsule fruits containing minute seeds. Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, Newmania serpens is considered a botanically significant and ecologically rare species.

Its study provides valuable insights into the adaptation strategies of plants in tropical and subtropical environments.
