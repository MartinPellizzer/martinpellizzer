Nymphaea loriana

Nymphaea loriana, commonly known as the Amazonian water lily, is a aquatic plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in marshes and ponds. Its leaves are typically covered in a waxy cuticle that helps repel water, while its roots anchor it in the muddy substrate.

Nymphaea loriana plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
