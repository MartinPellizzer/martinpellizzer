Nicotiana miersii var. miersii

Nicotiana miersii var. miersii, commonly known as the Miers' tobacco plant, is a species native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is a herbaceous annual plant with hairy, glandular leaves and trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically violet or blue in color. The plant produces a significant amount of nicotine in its leaves, which is a characteristic shared with other members of the Nicotiana genus. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in high-altitude regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is cultivated in gardens and studied for its botanical and genetic traits.
