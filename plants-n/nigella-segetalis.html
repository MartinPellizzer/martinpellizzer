Nigella segetalis

Nigella segetalis, commonly known as the grain of paradise, is an annual herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its slender stems and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces distinctive, elongated seed pods that split open to release its tiny, dark seeds. Its botanical features include alternate leaves with a hairy surface and a short, fibrous root system.

Nigella segetalis is often cultivated for its aromatic seeds, which are used in both culinary and medicinal applications.
