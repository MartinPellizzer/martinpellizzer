Narcissus glaucifolius

Narcissus glaucifolius, commonly known as the glaucous-leaved daffodil, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, gray-green leaves and bright yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers. Native to southern Europe, particularly the Iberian Peninsula, this plant thrives in sunny, well-drained soils. The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its early spring blooms.
