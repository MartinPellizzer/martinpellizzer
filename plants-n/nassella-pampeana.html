Nassella pampeana

Nassella pampeana, commonly known as the Pampas needlegrass, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina, Uruguay, and parts of Brazil.

It is a perennial grass that thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and shrublands. The plant produces slender, spike-like inflorescences that are adorned with small, spikelet-like flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive blue-green coloration.

Nassella pampeana plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
