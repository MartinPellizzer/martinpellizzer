Nuttallia decapetala

Nuttallia decapetala, commonly known as the ten-petaled bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink to purple in color, with ten petals arranged in a symmetrical pattern. The plant has slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are opposite in arrangement. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or rocky slopes.

Nuttallia decapetala is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
