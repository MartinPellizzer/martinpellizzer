Navarretia heterodoxa subsp. rosulata

Navarretia heterodoxa subsp.

rosulata, commonly known as the Sierra Valley bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and produces delicate, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white and pink.

This species thrives in rocky soils and open habitats, often found in coastal and inland areas. It is adapted to dry conditions and is often found in meadows and along streambanks.

The plant is known for its short blooming period, typically in spring, and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
