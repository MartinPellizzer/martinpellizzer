Neomammillaria hirsuta

Neomammillaria hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and unique appearance.

Neomammillaria hirsuta is typically found growing in rocky or sandy soils in arid environments.
