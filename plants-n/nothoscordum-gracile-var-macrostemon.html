Nothoscordum gracile var. macrostemon

Nothoscordum gracile var. macrostemon, commonly known as the large-flowered threadleaf garlic.

It is a herbaceous perennial native to Mexico, characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers that bloom in summer. Its flowers are typically arranged in terminal racemes, and it is often found in dry, open habitats.

This variety is noted for its aromatic foliage and is occasionally cultivated for ornamental purposes.
