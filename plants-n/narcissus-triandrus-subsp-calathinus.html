Narcissus triandrus subsp. calathinus

Narcissus triandrus subsp.

calathinus, commonly known as the Spanish daffodil, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange.

The flowers typically bloom in early spring, often before the leaves fully emerge. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and is well adapted to rocky and well-drained soils.

It is a popular ornamental plant valued for its vibrant blooms and resilience in garden settings.
