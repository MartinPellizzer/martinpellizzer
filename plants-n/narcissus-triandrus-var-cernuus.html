Narcissus triandrus var. cernuus

Narcissus triandrus var. cernuus, commonly known as the drooping daffodil, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and clusters of pendulous, yellow flowers with a distinctive, recurved shape. The flowers typically bloom in early spring, adding a striking visual element to gardens and natural habitats. This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its delicate, fragrant blooms.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to a range of garden environments.
