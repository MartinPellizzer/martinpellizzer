Navarretia rosulata

Navarretia rosulata, commonly known as the rosette larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of fleshy, hairy leaves and produces showy, blue to purple flowers in late spring. The plant thrives in rocky soils and open habitats, often found in coastal areas and mountain ranges. It is a perennial species that goes dormant during the summer heat.
