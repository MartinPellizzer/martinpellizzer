Nephrolepis cordifolia var. lauterbachii

Nephrolepis cordifolia var. lauterbachii, commonly known as the Lauterbach's maidenhair fern, is a variant of the common nephrolepis.

It is a fern native to tropical regions of Africa, particularly South Africa. The plant features delicate, arching fronds with a deep green color and a leathery texture. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a distinctive, feathery appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated as a houseplant.
