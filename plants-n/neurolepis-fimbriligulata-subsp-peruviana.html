Neurolepis fimbriligulata subsp. peruviana

Neurolepis fimbriligulata subsp.

peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian featherleaf, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of South America. It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves with a feathery appearance, and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant is adapted to high-altitude environments and thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. It is often found in dry, sunny habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
