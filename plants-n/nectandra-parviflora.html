Nectandra parviflora

Nectandra parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered nectandra, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, lowland forests and thrives in well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, and it produces small, dark-colored berries.

Nectandra parviflora is valued for its timber and has also been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
