Neopieris mariana

Neopieris mariana, commonly known as the American marsh willow.

It is a deciduous shrub native to wetlands and riparian areas in eastern North America. The plant has slender, willow-like stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers in clusters, which are typically pollinated by insects.

The plant plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic and terrestrial wildlife.
