Nolana peruviana subsp. divaricata

Nolana peruviana subsp.

divaricata, commonly known as the Peruvian starflower, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America. It is characterized by its small, star-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue, which bloom in clusters.

The plant has hairy, glandular leaves and stems, which are adaptations to its high-altitude environment. It thrives in rocky soils and is often found in open grasslands or mountainous areas. Nolana peruviana subsp.

divaricata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
