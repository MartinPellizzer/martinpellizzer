Nassella fuscescens

Nassella fuscescens, commonly known as purple needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to western North America.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and long, narrow leaves that often have a bluish-green hue. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences that range in color from purple to pink. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Nassella fuscescens is an important component of native plant communities and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
