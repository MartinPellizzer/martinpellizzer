Nervilia maliana

Nervilia maliana, commonly known as the Malayan nerve plant, is a member of the Orach family (Amaranthaceae).

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The flowers are typically greenish-white with a distinctive central red or purple spot, which is a key identifying feature. It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia, and thrives in moist, shaded environments.

The plant is often found in forest understories and is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in local medicine.
