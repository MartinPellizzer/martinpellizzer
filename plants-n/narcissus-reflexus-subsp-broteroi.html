Narcissus reflexus subsp. broteroi

Narcissus reflexus subsp.

broteroi, commonly known as the Spanish daffodil, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Europe and North Africa. It produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers with a single petal and a distinctive, often yellow or orange, corona.

The plant typically blooms in early spring, with leaves that are strap-like and grassy in appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and ecological landscapes.
