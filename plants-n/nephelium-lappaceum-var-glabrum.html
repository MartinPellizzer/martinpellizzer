Nephelium lappaceum var. glabrum

Nephelium lappaceum var. glabrum, commonly known as the smooth rambutan, is a variant of the rambutan fruit native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, hairless exterior compared to the typical fuzzy rambutan. The fruit produces a sweet, juicy pulp with a distinctive aroma, often used in both fresh consumption and culinary applications. Botanically, it belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is valued for its ornamental and edible qualities.

Its cultivation requires a tropical climate with well-drained soil and adequate sunlight for optimal growth and fruiting.
