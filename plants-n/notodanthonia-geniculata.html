Notodanthonia geniculata

Notodanthonia geniculata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed wallaby grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in late spring to early summer.

Notodanthonia geniculata is valued for its resilience and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
