Narcissus serotinus subsp. deficiens

Narcissus serotinus subsp.

deficiens, commonly known as the late-flowering daffodil, is a subspecies native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its narrow, strap-like leaves and a single, elongated flower with a yellow perianth.

The flower typically blooms in late spring, distinguishing it from other daffodil species. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, long-lasting blooms.
