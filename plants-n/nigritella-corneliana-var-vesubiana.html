Nigritella corneliana var. vesubiana

Nigritella corneliana var. vesubiana, commonly known as the velvetleaf mallow, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, velvety leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft texture. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from pale pink to deep red, typically blooming in summer. Its flowering stems are often branched and can grow up to several feet in height.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking foliage and floral display.
