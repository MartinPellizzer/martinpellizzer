Noccaea alpestris subsp. sylvium

Noccaea alpestris subsp.

sylvium, commonly known as alpine saxifrage, is a perennial plant native to mountainous regions of Europe. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, often in alpine meadows and scree slopes.

The plant has basal leaves that are palmately divided, with small, white or pinkish flowers clustered in a head-like inflorescence. It is adapted to cold climates and can tolerate harsh environmental conditions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in alpine ecosystems.
