Nidularium porphyreum

Nidularium porphyreum, commonly known as the Porphyreum Nidularium, is a member of the Bromeliaceae family.

It is a terrestrial bromeliad characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central water-holding cup. The plant is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its foliage is often a striking deep purple or reddish hue, which gives it its common name.

The flowers, which are typically small and clustered, are usually yellow or orange and bloom in the summer months.
