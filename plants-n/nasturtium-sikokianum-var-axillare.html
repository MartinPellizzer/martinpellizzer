Nasturtium sikokianum var. axillare

Nasturtium sikokianum var. axillare, commonly known as the axillary nasturtium, is a variant of the nasturtium plant characterized by its unique growth habit.

It typically features compact growth with flowers emerging from the leaf axils, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces bright orange or yellow flowers, which are often adorned with a red or orange throat. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, typical of the Tropaeolaceae family.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often grown in gardens for its vibrant blooms and edible characteristics.
