Neolitsea lanuginosa

Neolitsea lanuginosa, commonly known as the woolly flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, woolly leaves that give it its common name, and it produces clusters of small, fragrant yellow flowers. The plant is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds.

Neolitsea lanuginosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
