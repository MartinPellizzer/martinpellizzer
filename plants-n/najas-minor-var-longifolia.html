Najas minor var. longifolia

Najas minor var. longifolia, commonly known as the slender watermilfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It has narrow, ribbon-like leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem, which helps it float and absorb sunlight efficiently. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged, with stamens and pistils emerging from the water surface. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Due to its rapid growth, it can become invasive in some regions if not managed properly.
