Nymphaea sumatrana

Nymphaea sumatrana, commonly known as the Sumatran water lily, is a water plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia.

It belongs to the Nymphaeaceae family and is characterized by its broad, floating leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically blooms in shades of white, pink, and purple, with petals that are often adorned with a distinctive central pattern. Its roots are submerged, anchoring it in shallow water, while its leaves provide a surface for aquatic life.

Nymphaea sumatrana plays an important ecological role in freshwater ecosystems by supporting biodiversity and contributing to water filtration.
