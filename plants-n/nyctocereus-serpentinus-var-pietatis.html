Nyctocereus serpentinus var. pietatis

Nyctocereus serpentinus var. pietatis, commonly known as the Sacred Cactus or Christ-Cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem that often branches into multiple spiny shoots. The plant produces large, showy flowers that open at night, typically in shades of white and pink. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its nocturnal blooming habit and cultural significance, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
