Nepenthes pascoensis

Nepenthes pascoensis, commonly known as the Pasco's pitcher plant, is a tropical carnivorous plant native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its unique pitcher-shaped leaves that secrete a digestive fluid to trap and digest insects. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires consistent moisture to flourish. Its pitchers are typically green or red, with a waxy surface that aids in trapping prey.

Nepenthes pascoensis is notable for its ability to absorb nutrients from the insects it captures, making it well adapted to nutrient-poor soils.
