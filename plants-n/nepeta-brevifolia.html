Nepeta brevifolia

Nepeta brevifolia, commonly known as catmint, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and produces spikes of blue to purple flowers. The plant is part of the Lamiaceae family, known for its square stems and fragrant properties. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in rocky or dry habitats.

Nepeta brevifolia is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and historical use as a remedy for ailments.
