Nicotiana loxensis

Nicotiana loxensis, commonly known as the Loxa tobacco, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family.

It is native to the Andean regions of South America, specifically Ecuador and Peru. The plant is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet. It produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Nicotiana loxensis is cultivated for its ornamental value and has also been studied for its potential in pharmaceutical research.
