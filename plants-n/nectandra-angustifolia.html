Nectandra angustifolia

Nectandra angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed nectandra, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves and fragrant, aromatic bark. It produces small, clustered flowers followed by dark, glossy berries.

Nectandra angustifolia is often found in humid lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.
