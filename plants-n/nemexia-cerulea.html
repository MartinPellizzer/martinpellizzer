Nemexia cerulea

Nemexia cerulea, commonly known as the blue nemexia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, cylindrical seed pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in arid climates.
