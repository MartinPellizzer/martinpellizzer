Nipponocalamus virens var. tenuifolius

Nipponocalamus virens var. tenuifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bamboo, is a variant of the green bamboo species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, gracefully arching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that give it a refined appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in mountainous regions. Its foliage tends to remain evergreen, providing visual interest throughout the year.

This variety is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and ease of cultivation.
