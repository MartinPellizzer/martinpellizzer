Nyssa sylvatica var. dilatata

Nyssa sylvatica var. dilatata, commonly known as the spreading black gum, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Nyssaceae and is characterized by its distinctive ovate leaves that turn vibrant red in the fall. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, which are followed by clusters of dark blue berries. Its bark is thick and flaky, often with a reddish hue, and it has a slow growth rate with a broad, spreading crown.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
