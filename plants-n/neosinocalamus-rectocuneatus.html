Neosinocalamus rectocuneatus

Neosinocalamus rectocuneatus, commonly known as the straight-edged golden bamboo, is a member of the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Vietnam, Laos, and Thailand. This plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth, glossy surface and a distinctive golden hue.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional crafts and construction in its native regions.
