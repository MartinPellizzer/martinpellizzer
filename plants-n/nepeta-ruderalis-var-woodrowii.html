Nepeta ruderalis var. woodrowii

Nepeta ruderalis var. woodrowii, commonly known as Woodrow's catnip, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It is a member of the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and other open habitats.
