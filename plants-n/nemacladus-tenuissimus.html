Nemacladus tenuissimus

Nemacladus tenuissimus, commonly known as the slender leaf fern, is a small, delicate fern species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, linear leaves that give it a distinctive, grass-like appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as under the canopy of dense forests. Its rhizomes are slender and creeping, allowing it to spread slowly across the forest floor.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological niche, it is often found in humid, low-light habitats where it thrives as an understory plant.
