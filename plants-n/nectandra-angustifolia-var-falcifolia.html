Nectandra angustifolia var. falcifolia

Nectandra angustifolia var. falcifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed nectandra, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves. The leaves are typically narrow and elongated, with a distinctive falcate (sickle-shaped) apex, giving the variety its specific name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by edible, aromatic fruits.

It is often valued for its timber and traditional uses in local medicine and as a source of essential oils.
