Neotainiopsis barbata

Neotainiopsis barbata, commonly known as the barbed leaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, often colorful flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, such as rainforests, where it attaches itself to trees or other vegetation. Its leaves are usually elongated and may have a distinctive barbed or hooked edge, which gives it its common name.

Neotainiopsis barbata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
