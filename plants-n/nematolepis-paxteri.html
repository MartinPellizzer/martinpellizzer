Nematolepis paxteri

Nematolepis paxteri, commonly known as the Paxter's grass, is a low-growing perennial plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or wetland habitats.
