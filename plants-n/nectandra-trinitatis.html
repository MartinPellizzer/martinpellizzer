Nectandra trinitatis

Nectandra trinitatis, commonly known as the Trinidadian sandwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Caribbean islands, particularly Trinidad and Tobago.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic, dark-colored wood. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a smooth texture. It produces small, fragrant flowers followed by oval-shaped fruits containing smooth, dark seeds.

Nectandra trinitatis is valued for its durable wood and is also used in traditional medicine.
