Nothofagus recurva

Nothofagus recurva, commonly known as the silver beech, is a species of evergreen tree native to southern South America.

It belongs to the Nothofagus genus, which is part of the beech family Fagaceae. The plant is characterized by its smooth, silver-gray bark and lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the branches. It thrives in temperate rainforests and is known for its ecological importance in supporting diverse wildlife.

Nothofagus recurva is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
