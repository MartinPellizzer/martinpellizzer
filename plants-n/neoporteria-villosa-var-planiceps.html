Neoporteria villosa var. planiceps

Neoporteria villosa var. planiceps, commonly known as the woolly leaf bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features hairy, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine, white pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, often growing in woodland or wetland habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological studies and native plant gardens.
