Nassella linearifolia

Nassella linearifolia, commonly known as slender needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its fine, needle-like leaves and slender, upright growth habit. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, cylindrical cluster of spikelets.

Nassella linearifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
