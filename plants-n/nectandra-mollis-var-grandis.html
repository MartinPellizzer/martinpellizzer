Nectandra mollis var. grandis

Nectandra mollis var. grandis, commonly known as the grandiflora nectandra, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, evergreen foliage. The plant produces large, showy flowers that are often white or pale yellow, attracting various pollinators. Its wood is valued for its durability and fine grain, making it useful in construction and furniture-making.

The species is also noted for its potential medicinal properties, though further research is needed to confirm its therapeutic applications.
