Nemophila inconspicua

Nemophila inconspicua, commonly known as the inconspicuous flower, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Boraginaceae family and is characterized by its small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, with a soft, hairy texture.

Nemophila inconspicua is known for its delicate appearance and is often found in wildflower gardens and natural habitats.
