Notocactus scopa var. marchesii

Notocactus scopa var. marchesii, commonly known as the Marchesii candelabra cactus, is a unique and striking plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, branching stems that resemble a candlestick, covered in dense clusters of white to pale yellow flowers. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in well-draining soil with plenty of sunlight. Its flowers bloom in late winter to early spring, adding a dramatic visual impact to any garden.

It is a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor cultivation due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
