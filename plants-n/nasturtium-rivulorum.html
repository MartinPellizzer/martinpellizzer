Nasturtium rivulorum

Nasturtium rivulorum, commonly known as the common nasturtium, is an annual flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant, edible flowers in shades of orange and yellow, which are often used in culinary applications. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a slightly spicy taste, making it popular in herbal teas and salads. Its rapid growth habit and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions make it a versatile garden plant.

Nasturtium rivulorum also serves as a natural pest repellent, attracting beneficial insects like ladybugs to the garden.
