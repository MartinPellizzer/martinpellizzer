Nicotiana asiatica

Nicotiana asiatica, commonly known as the Asian tobacco plant, is a herbaceous annual plant native to Asia.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. It produces alkaloids, including nicotine, which is toxic to many animals.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine, it is cultivated in various regions for both aesthetic and practical purposes.
