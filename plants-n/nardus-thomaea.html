Nardus thomaea

Nardus thomaea, commonly known as the narrowleaf grass or narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, clustered inflorescences. This plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, producing tiny, inconspicuous flowers.

Nardus thomaea plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
