Nasturtium cardamine

Nasturtium cardamine, commonly known as watercress, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its kidney-shaped leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant thrives in moist, aquatic or semi-aquatic environments, often found along streams and ponds. Its edible leaves and stems are rich in nutrients and have a peppery flavor, making them popular in salads and garnishes.

Nasturtium cardamine is also known for its medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial benefits.
