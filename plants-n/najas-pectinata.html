Najas pectinata

Najas pectinata, commonly known as the thread-leaved water plant, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It has slender, ribbon-like leaves that are often arranged in whorls around the stem, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically found near the water surface. It thrives in shallow, slow-moving waters and plays a role in providing habitat for aquatic invertebrates.

Its rapid growth can sometimes lead to dense vegetation, affecting water flow and clarity.
