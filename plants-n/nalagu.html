Nalagu

Nalagu, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit. The plant produces showy, velvety flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white. Its leaves are typically round or heart-shaped, with a waxy texture.

Nalagu thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
