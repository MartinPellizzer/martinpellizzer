Nepeta nuda var. violacea

Nepeta nuda var. violacea, commonly known as violet catmint, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, upright stems and gray-green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of violet and blue, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

Its aromatic foliage and showy blooms make it a popular ornamental plant in horticulture.
