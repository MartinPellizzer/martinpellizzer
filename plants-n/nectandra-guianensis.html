Nectandra guianensis

Nectandra guianensis, commonly known as Guianese nectandra, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes other aromatic plants like cinnamon and avocado. The tree is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers. Its wood is valued for its durability and aromatic properties, though it is not widely used commercially.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
