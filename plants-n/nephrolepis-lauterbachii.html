Nephrolepis lauterbachii

Nephrolepis lauterbachii, commonly known as the Lauterbach's fern, is a species of fern native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are typically arching and gracefully drooping. The plant produces spores on the underside of its leaves, which are clustered in small, round structures called sori. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, moist soil to flourish.

Nephrolepis lauterbachii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
