Notodontia

Notodontia, commonly known as the "false loopers," is a subfamily of moths within the family Notodontidae.

These insects are characterized by their distinctive, often colorful wings and their unique larval feeding habits. The larvae of Notodontia typically feed on a variety of plants, including trees, shrubs, and herbaceous plants. Their botanical significance lies in their role as herbivores that can impact plant communities and serve as food sources for other animals.

The study of Notodontia contributes to our understanding of plant-insect interactions and ecological dynamics.
