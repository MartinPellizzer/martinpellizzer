Neoporteria nigrihorrida

Neoporteria nigrihorrida, commonly known as the black-horned pittosporum, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black bark and its dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
