Nothoholcus lanatus

Nothoholcus lanatus, commonly known as the woolly grass, is a perennial grass species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and hairy, silver-green foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous blue to purple flowers arranged in dense spikelets, which are typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and coastal areas.

Nothoholcus lanatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in ecological restoration projects.
