Nicotiana chilensis

Nicotiana chilensis, commonly known as Chilean tobacco, is a species native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It is a herbaceous plant that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, which attract pollinators such as bees and moths. It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is known for containing nicotine in its leaves.

Nicotiana chilensis is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking floral display.
