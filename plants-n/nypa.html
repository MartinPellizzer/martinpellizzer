Nypa

Nypa, commonly known as the nipa palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and feather-like leaves that form a dense canopy. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that develop into clusters of edible fruits. Its roots form a complex network that helps stabilize coastal soils and prevent erosion.

The nipa palm is also valued for its versatile uses, including thatching, construction, and traditional medicine.
