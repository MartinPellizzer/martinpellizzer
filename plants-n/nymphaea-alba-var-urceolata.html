Nymphaea alba var. urceolata

Nymphaea alba var. urceolata, commonly known as the white water lily with urn-shaped leaves, is a aquatic plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often floating on the water's surface, with a distinctive urn-like shape on the underside. The flowers are white and typically bloom in summer, producing a fragrant, showy display. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting aquatic life.
