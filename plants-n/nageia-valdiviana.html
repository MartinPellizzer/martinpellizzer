Nageia valdiviana

Nageia valdiviana, commonly known as the Valdivian cypress, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the temperate rainforests of southern South America.

It is characterized by its slender, columnar shape and scaly, dark green foliage. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by cone-like structures that contain its seeds. It thrives in moist, cool climates and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ecological importance and limited distribution, it is considered a vulnerable species.
