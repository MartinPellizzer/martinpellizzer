Nectandra spicata

Nectandra spicata, commonly known as the fragrant wood or spicata nectandra, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and wood. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in spicate inflorescences, followed by small, fleshy berries. Its leaves are typically ovate to elliptical in shape, with a smooth or slightly serrated margin.

Nectandra spicata is valued for its fragrant oils, which have been used in traditional medicine and for their aromatic properties.
