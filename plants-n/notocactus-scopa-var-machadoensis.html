Notocactus scopa var. machadoensis

Notocactus scopa var. machadoensis, commonly known as the Machado's cholla, is a spineless cactus native to Mexico.

It features a cylindrical stem that is typically green to blue-green in color, often covered with a waxy coating. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a striking visual element to its appearance. It is adapted to arid environments and thrives in full sun with well-draining soil.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping and succulent gardens.
