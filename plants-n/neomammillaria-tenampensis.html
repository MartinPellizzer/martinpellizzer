Neomammillaria tenampensis

Neomammillaria tenampensis, commonly known as the Ténampé cactus, is a species native to the Sonoran Desert in the United States and northern Mexico.

It is a small, globular cactus with a ribbed structure and a pale green to blue-gray coloration. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are typically borne on the upper part of the stem. Its spines are short and often white or pale in color, and it is adapted to arid environments with minimal water requirements.

Neomammillaria tenampensis is often grown as a decorative plant due to its compact size and attractive appearance.
