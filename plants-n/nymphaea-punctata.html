Nymphaea punctata

Nymphaea punctata, commonly known as the spotted water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, floating leaves that are often marked with distinctive dark spots. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white, opening in the early morning. Nymphaea punctata thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats, such as ponds and lakes.

Its roots anchor it in the muddy substrate, while its leaves provide habitat for various aquatic organisms.
