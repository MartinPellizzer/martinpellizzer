Nardurus tenuiflorus var. muticus

Nardurus tenuiflorus var. muticus, commonly known as the silent narrow-flower nardus, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It features small, narrow leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces tiny, clustered flowers that are usually pale green or white, contributing to its inconspicuous appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand prolonged drought periods.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
