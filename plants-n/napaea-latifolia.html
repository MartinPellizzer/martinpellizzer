Napaea latifolia

Napaea latifolia, commonly known as broadleaf napaea, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, greenish-white flowers appear in clusters during the spring and early summer.

Napaea latifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
