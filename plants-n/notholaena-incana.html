Notholaena incana

Notholaena incana, commonly known as the silver hair grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and forest edges. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, pale green to brownish flowers.

Notholaena incana plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
