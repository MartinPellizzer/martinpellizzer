Nephelaphyllum cristigerum

Nephelaphyllum cristigerum, commonly known as the cloud-crown fern, is a unique species native to high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that resemble clouds, giving it its common name. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are clustered on the underside of its leaves.

Nephelaphyllum cristigerum plays a vital role in its ecosystem by providing habitat for various small organisms.
