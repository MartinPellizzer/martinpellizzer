Notophilus vulgaris

Notophilus vulgaris, commonly known as the common tansy, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers and fern-like leaves, which are often used in traditional herbal medicine. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family and is known for its strong, aromatic scent. Notophilus vulgaris thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

It is also noted for its ability to attract beneficial insects such as bees and butterflies.
