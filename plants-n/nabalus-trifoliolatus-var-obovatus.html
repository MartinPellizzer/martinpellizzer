Nabalus trifoliolatus var. obovatus

Nabalus trifoliolatus var. obovatus, commonly known as the three-leaved rockrose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species suitable for rock gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long flowering period.
