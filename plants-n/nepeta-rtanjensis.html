Nepeta rtanjensis

Nepeta rtanjensis, commonly known as the Balkan catmint, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their calming properties.

Nepeta rtanjensis is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
