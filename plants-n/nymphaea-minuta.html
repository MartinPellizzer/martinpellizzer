Nymphaea minuta

Nymphaea minuta, commonly known as the dwarf water lily, is a species of aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Nymphaeaceae and is characterized by its small, floating leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats such as ponds and lakes. Its leaves are rounded and often have a waxy surface, while its flowers can be white, pink, or yellow, depending on the variety.

Nymphaea minuta plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms and contributing to water filtration.
