Notopleura longissima

Notopleura longissima, commonly known as the long-stemmed orchid, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its elongated, slender stems and delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid forest environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often arranged in a rosette pattern around the stem.

Notopleura longissima is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in greenhouses or botanical gardens.
