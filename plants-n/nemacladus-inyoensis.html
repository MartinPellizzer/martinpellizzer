Nemacladus inyoensis

Nemacladus inyoensis, commonly known as the Inyo Mountain dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the Great Basin region of North America.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, silvery-green leaves and a single, tall stem that supports a single, yellow flower. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought tolerance through its succulent foliage. It produces a capsule fruit that contains numerous small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Nemacladus inyoensis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates in its native range.
