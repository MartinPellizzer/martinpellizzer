Neomammillaria neopalmeri

Neomammillaria neopalmeri, commonly known as the Palmer's milk barrel cactus, is a species native to the Sonoran Desert in the United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are typically green to blue-green in color, often covered with white or yellow spines. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a delicate contrast to its rugged appearance. It is a slow-growing cactus that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for arid garden environments.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is often cultivated by succulent enthusiasts.
