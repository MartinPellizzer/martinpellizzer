Nassauvia lagascae var. lanata

Nassauvia lagascae var. lanata, commonly known as the woolly nassauvia, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage, which gives it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long blooming period.
