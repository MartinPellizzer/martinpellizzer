Narcissus conspicuus

Narcissus conspicuus, commonly known as the showy daffodil, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and is characterized by its large, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of blooms in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Narcissus conspicuus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in floral arrangements.
