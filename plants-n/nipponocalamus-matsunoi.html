Nipponocalamus matsunoi

Nipponocalamus matsunoi, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, linear leaves. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests and stream banks. It has a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread vegetatively and form dense clumps.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appearance and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens.
