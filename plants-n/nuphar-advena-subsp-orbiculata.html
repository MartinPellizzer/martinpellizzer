Nuphar advena subsp. orbiculata

Nuphar advena subsp.

orbiculata, commonly known as the broad-leafed water lily, is a aquatic plant native to North America. It features large, rounded, floating leaves that are typically a vibrant green with a waxy surface.

The plant produces showy, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer, often with a distinct fragrance. Its roots are anchored in the mud at the bottom of ponds and lakes, while its stems are adapted for buoyancy.

This species plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various organisms.
