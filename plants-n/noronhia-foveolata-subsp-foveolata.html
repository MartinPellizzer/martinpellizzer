Noronhia foveolata subsp. foveolata

Noronhia foveolata subsp.

foveolata, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil. It typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters, featuring dense, dark green foliage with elliptical leaves that are glossy and arranged oppositely on the stems. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers in clusters, followed by red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various birds and mammals.

Its adaptability to different soil types and tolerance to drought make it a common ornamental plant in tropical and subtropical regions.
