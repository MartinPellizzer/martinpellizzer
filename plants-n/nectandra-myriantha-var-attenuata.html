Nectandra myriantha var. attenuata

Nectandra myriantha var. attenuata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ironwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its dark, aromatic wood and lance-shaped leaves with a smooth texture. It produces small, white flowers followed by elliptical berries that are edible but not widely consumed by humans.

This species is valued for its durable timber and is often found in lowland rainforests.
