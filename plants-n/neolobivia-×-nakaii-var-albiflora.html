Neolobivia × nakaii var. albiflora

Neolobivia × nakaii var. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered hybrid cactus, is a unique botanical variant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, globular shape and thick, ribbed stems. The plant produces showy, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and moths. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it is well adapted to survive in hot, dry environments with minimal water.

This variety is prized by horticulturists for its ornamental value and resilience in cultivation.
