Noaea oppositiflora

Noaea oppositiflora, commonly known as the opposite-flower sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East and parts of Africa.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in opposite pairs along the stems, which is a distinctive feature. Its leaves are typically narrow and succulent, adapted to conserve water in dry environments.

Noaea oppositiflora is often found in sandy or gravelly soils and is well-suited to harsh, drought-prone climates.
