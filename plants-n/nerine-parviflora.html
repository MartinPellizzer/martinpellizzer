Nerine parviflora

Nerine parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered nerine, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It produces striking, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, with a distinctive red or purple stripe down the center. The plant features strap-like, narrow leaves that are often evergreen, providing visual interest even when not in bloom. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile choice for gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance, it is a popular choice among gardeners seeking to add color and texture to their landscapes.
