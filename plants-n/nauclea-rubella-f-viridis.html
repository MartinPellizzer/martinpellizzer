Nauclea rubella f. viridis

Nauclea rubella f. viridis, commonly known as the green nauclea, is a variant of the species Nauclea rubella, characterized by its distinct foliage.

This plant is native to tropical regions of Africa and thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a smooth, glossy surface, which sets it apart from the typical red-leafed form. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, attracting various pollinators.

While it is not widely cultivated, it is appreciated for its ornamental value and ecological role in its native habitats.
