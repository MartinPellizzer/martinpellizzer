Nepeta oblanceolata

Nepeta oblanceolata, commonly known as catmint, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often grayish-green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is a member of the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is known for its aromatic foliage and ability to attract pollinators.

Nepeta oblanceolata is also popular in gardens for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation.
