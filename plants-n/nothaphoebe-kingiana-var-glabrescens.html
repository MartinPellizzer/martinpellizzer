Nothaphoebe kingiana var. glabrescens

Nothaphoebe kingiana var. glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth-leafed nothaphoebe, is a variant of the nothophoebe genus native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub characterized by its smooth, glabrous foliage. The leaves are typically elliptical or ovate, with a glossy green surface and a slightly serrated margin. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by small, round berries that are edible for some bird species.

This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal rainforests.
