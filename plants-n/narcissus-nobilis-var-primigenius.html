Narcissus nobilis var. primigenius

Narcissus nobilis var. primigenius, commonly known as the Giant Daffodil, is a striking member of the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers with a prominent central corona. The plant typically grows to heights of 1 to 2 meters, making it one of the tallest daffodil varieties. Its leaves are long and strap-like, emerging from a bulbous stem.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant spring blooms.
