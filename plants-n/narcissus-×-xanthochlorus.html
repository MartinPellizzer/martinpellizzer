Narcissus × xanthochlorus

Narcissus × xanthochlorus, commonly known as the yellow daffodil, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Narcissus tazetta and Narcissus cyclaminiflorus.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers with a distinctive greenish or yellowish center, which sets it apart from other daffodil varieties. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, featuring narrow, grass-like leaves and a single, trumpet-shaped bloom. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, often blooming in early spring.

This cultivar is prized for its vibrant color and is commonly used in gardens and floral arrangements.
