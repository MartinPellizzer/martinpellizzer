Nastus humilus

Nastus humilus, commonly known as the lesser nasturtium, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other plants like mustard and cabbage. The plant features small, yellow flowers and heart-shaped, edible leaves that are often used in salads. Its growth habit is low and sprawling, making it suitable for ground cover in gardens.

Nastus humilus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract beneficial insects.
