Narcissus × vallrutae

Narcissus × vallrutae, commonly known as the hybrid daffodil, is a botanical hybrid resulting from the cross between Narcissus tazetta and Narcissus poeticus.

It is characterized by its striking yellow petals and a single, prominent central corona. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with strap-like leaves that are broad and upright. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

This hybrid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its early spring blooming period.
