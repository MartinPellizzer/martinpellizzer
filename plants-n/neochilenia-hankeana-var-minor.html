Neochilenia hankeana var. minor

Neochilenia hankeana var. minor, commonly known as the lesser Hanke's neochilenia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its small, daisy-like flowers with yellow disc florets and white ray florets. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as along stream banks or in forest understories. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often arranged in a basal rosette.

The species is native to South America, particularly in regions of Brazil and Paraguay.
