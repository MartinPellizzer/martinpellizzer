Nasa rugosa subsp. rugosa

Nasa rugosa subsp.

rugosa, commonly known as the woolly nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, rugged appearance.

The plant produces small, violet to blue flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it adaptable to a variety of garden environments.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is often cultivated in regions with mild climates.
