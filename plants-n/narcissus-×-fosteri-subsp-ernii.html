Narcissus × fosteri subsp. ernii

Narcissus × fosteri subsp.

ernii, commonly known as the Spanish daffodil, is a hybrid plant that exhibits a range of botanical characteristics typical of the daffodil genus. It is a bulbous perennial herb with strap-like, linear leaves and a showy inflorescence consisting of one or more large, trumpet-shaped flowers.

The flowers typically display a combination of white, yellow, and green hues, with a distinct corona that is often more prominent than in other daffodil species. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens in temperate climates.

It is native to southern Europe and has been cultivated for its ornamental value.
