Neomammillaria meiacantha

Neomammillaria meiacantha, commonly known as the narrow-ribbed hedgehog cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are covered in prominent, spiny areoles. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are typically borne at the tips of the stems. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its striking appearance and relatively easy care, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
