Nigella arvensis var. iranica

Nigella arvensis var. iranica, commonly known as Persian sesame or Iranian fennel, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the Middle East and Central Asia.

It features slender, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in loose clusters, followed by distinctive, elongated seed pods that split open to release tiny, black seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate climates.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in herbal medicine.
