Nepeta asterotricha

Nepeta asterotricha, commonly known as the star-haired catnip, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine, star-shaped hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic properties and medicinal uses.

Nepeta asterotricha is often grown for its ornamental value and its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
