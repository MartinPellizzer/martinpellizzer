Nemophila menziesii var. rotata

Nemophila menziesii var. rotata, commonly known as the California baby blue eyes, is a low-growing annual herb native to the western United States.

It features delicate, blue to purple flowers that bloom in early spring, often in clusters at the ends of its stems. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal scrub and open woodland habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens for its cheerful, early-season blooms.
