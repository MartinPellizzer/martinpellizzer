Nephelaphyllum tenuiflorum

Nephelaphyllum tenuiflorum, commonly known as the cloud-flower, is a unique plant characterized by its delicate, fern-like foliage and small, ephemeral blooms.

It thrives in shaded, moist environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant's leaves are often covered in fine hairs, which help retain moisture in its humid habitat. Its flowers, though short-lived, are known for their pale blue or white coloration.

Nephelaphyllum tenuiflorum is primarily valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local insect populations.
