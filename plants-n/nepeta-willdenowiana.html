Nepeta willdenowiana

Nepeta willdenowiana, commonly known as catmint, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, clustered blue or purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in herbal remedies due to their mild mint-like scent.

Nepeta willdenowiana is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soil.
