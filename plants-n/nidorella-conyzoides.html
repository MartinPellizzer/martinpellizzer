Nidorella conyzoides

Nidorella conyzoides, commonly known as the false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with slender, hairy stems and alternate leaves that are usually lance-shaped. Its flowers are yellow, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Nidorella conyzoides is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including fields and along roadsides.
