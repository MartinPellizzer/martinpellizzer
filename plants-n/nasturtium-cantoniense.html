Nasturtium cantoniense

Nasturtium cantoniense, commonly known as Chinese watercress, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its edible, peppery leaves and yellow or orange flowers. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. It is often cultivated for its nutritional value and use in traditional Chinese cuisine.

Nasturtium cantoniense is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract beneficial insects.
