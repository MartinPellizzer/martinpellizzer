Norantea macrocarpa

Norantea macrocarpa, commonly known as the giant bladderfruit, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, inflated seed pods that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, with a rough texture.

Norantea macrocarpa is adapted to thrive in humid, tropical climates and is often found in wetland or marshy environments.
