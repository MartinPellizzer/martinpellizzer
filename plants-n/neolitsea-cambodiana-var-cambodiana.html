Neolitsea cambodiana var. cambodiana

Neolitsea cambodiana var. cambodiana, commonly known as Cambodian litsea, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and avocados, and is characterized by its aromatic foliage. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by elliptical, dark green berries that are edible but often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a smooth or slightly serrated margin, while its bark is typically smooth and greyish in color.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in local horticulture and ecological restoration projects.
