Nigella arvensis var. assyriaca

Nigella arvensis var. assyriaca, commonly known as the Assyrian cumin or Persian cumin, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its small, yellowish to brownish flowers and feathery leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields. Its seeds, which are used in culinary and medicinal applications, have a distinct, pungent aroma.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal due to its delicate flowers and compact growth habit.
