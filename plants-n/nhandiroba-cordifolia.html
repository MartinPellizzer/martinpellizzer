Nhandiroba cordifolia

Nhandiroba cordifolia, commonly known as the heart-leaf tree, is a tropical plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, which are followed by orange-red fruits. Nhandiroba cordifolia is typically found in humid, shaded environments within the rainforest canopy.

Its unique foliage and ornamental value make it a popular choice for botanical gardens and horticultural collections.
