Nerium macrocarpum

Nerium macrocarpum, commonly known as the giant nerium or large-fruited oleander, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant has simple, lance-shaped leaves and produces large, woody fruits that contain numerous seeds. It is a member of the Apocynaceae family and is known for its adaptability to various soil types.

Nerium macrocarpum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
