Nonea picta

Nonea picta, commonly known as the painted hibiscus, is a striking flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from red to orange and yellow, often with intricate patterns. The plant has heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are typically dark green, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Nonea picta is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
