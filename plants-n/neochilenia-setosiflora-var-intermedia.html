Neochilenia setosiflora var. intermedia

Neochilenia setosiflora var. intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate setose-flowered plant, is a species native to South America, particularly found in regions of Chile and Argentina.

It is a shrub characterized by its branched stems and small, narrow leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The flowers are typically yellow or greenish-yellow, arranged in clusters, and are often covered with fine, stiff hairs. This plant thrives in dry, rocky environments and is adapted to withstand periods of drought.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for studies on plant adaptation and ecological resilience.
