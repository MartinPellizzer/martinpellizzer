Neomammillaria wrightii

Neomammillaria wrightii, commonly known as the Wright's night bloomer, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are often covered in white to pale blue spines. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming at night. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Neomammillaria wrightii is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements.
