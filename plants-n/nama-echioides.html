Nama echioides

Nama echioides, commonly known as the narrowleaf kniphofia, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asphodelaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like leaves and showy, red-orange inflorescences. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is drought-tolerant and well-suited for xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.

The flowers bloom in summer and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
