Nepeta gilesii

Nepeta gilesii, commonly known as catmint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and pleasant scent.

Nepeta gilesii is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported calming and medicinal properties.
