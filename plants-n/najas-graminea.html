Najas graminea

Najas graminea, commonly known as the watermilfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, scale-like leaves that are arranged in whorls. The plant produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or brown, which develop into small, floating fruits. Najas graminea plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

It is often found in ponds, lakes, and slow-moving rivers.
