Nephrodium affine

Nephrodium affine, commonly known as the common nephroid, is a herbaceous perennial plant belonging to the family Nephrodiaceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves that are often tinged with red or purple. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shady environments, typically found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Nephrodium affine is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage.
