Narcissus triandrus var. alejandrei

Narcissus triandrus var. alejandrei, commonly known as the Alexandrian daffodil, is a variant of the broad-leafed daffodil native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its large, funnel-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or white in color. The plant features strap-like, grass-like leaves and grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for its early spring blooms.
