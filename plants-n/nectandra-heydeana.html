Nectandra heydeana

Nectandra heydeana, commonly known as the white sand tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule containing several seeds.

Nectandra heydeana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm, humid climates.
