Narcissus × javieri

Narcissus × javieri, commonly known as the Dutch hybrid narcissus, is a botanical hybrid resulting from the cross between N. tazetta and N. cyclamineus.

It is characterized by its compact, often multi-flowered inflorescence and a range of color variations, including white, yellow, and orange. The plant typically grows to a height of 20–30 cm, with narrow, grass-like leaves and a short stem. It is a hardy perennial that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its blooming period occurs in late winter to early spring, adding an early splash of color to the garden.
