Nama biflora var. spathulata

Nama biflora var. spathulata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed rock daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive, narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow and white, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and resilience, it is cultivated in gardens and rockeries in regions with a Mediterranean climate.
