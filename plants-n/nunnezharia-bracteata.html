Nunnezharia bracteata

Nunnezharia bracteata, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, often reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its fibrous roots and sturdy stems make it adaptable to various soil types.

Nunnezharia bracteata is also known for its ability to thrive in disturbed environments, making it a common weed in agricultural settings.
