Nepeta grandiflora var. reichenbachiana

Nepeta grandiflora var. reichenbachiana, commonly known as catmint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine, grayish-green foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is valued for its aromatic leaves and is often used in gardens for its ornamental and medicinal properties.

Nepeta grandiflora var. reichenbachiana is also a popular source of nectar for bees and other pollinators.
