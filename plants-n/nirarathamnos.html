Nirarathamnos

Nirarathamnos, commonly known as the golden flame fern, is a unique plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It features striking, bright yellow fronds that resemble flames, giving it its common name. The plant has a compact, rosette-like growth habit with thick, waxy leaves that help it retain moisture in its dry environment. Nirarathamnos is adapted to full sun and requires well-draining soil to thrive.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for drought-tolerant gardens.
