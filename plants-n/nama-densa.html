Nama densa

Nama densa, commonly known as the dense nama, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and fleshy, succulent leaves that store water, aiding in its survival in dry environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which appear during the rainy season. Its roots are fibrous and can penetrate deep into the soil to access moisture.

Nama densa is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its hardy nature and ornamental appeal.
