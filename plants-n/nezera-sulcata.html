Nezera sulcata

Nezera sulcata, commonly known as the African violet, is a species of flowering plant native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves and produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white. The plant is typically grown as a houseplant due to its preference for warm, humid conditions and indirect sunlight. Nezera sulcata has a fibrous root system and is known for its ability to thrive in well-draining soil.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for indoor gardening and ornamental purposes.
