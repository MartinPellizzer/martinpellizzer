Neochilenia chorosensis

Neochilenia chorosensis, commonly known as the Choros orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America, particularly Chile.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, intricate flowers with a distinctive coloration. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its flowering period occurs during the late spring to early summer, adding to its ecological significance in its native range.

Neochilenia chorosensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by enthusiasts of rare and exotic plants.
