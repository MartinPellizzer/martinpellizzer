Noccaea sarmatica

Noccaea sarmatica, commonly known as the Sarmatian watercress, is a species of flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is a herbaceous annual plant that typically grows in moist, calcareous soils, often found near water bodies in temperate regions. The plant has alternate, pinnately divided leaves that are covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes. Its seeds are small and capsule-like, dispersing via wind or water.

Noccaea sarmatica is valued for its peppery flavor and has been used in traditional cuisine and herbal medicine.
