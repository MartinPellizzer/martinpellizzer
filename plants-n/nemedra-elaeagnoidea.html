Nemedra elaeagnoidea

Nemedra elaeagnoidea, commonly known as the silverleaf acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray leaves, which are covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in dense, globular clusters, which are often visited by bees and other pollinators. Its bark is smooth and greyish, and it is often used in traditional medicine.

Nemedra elaeagnoidea is adapted to arid environments and is drought-tolerant.
