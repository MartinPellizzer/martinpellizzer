Nasturtium sylvestre var. incisum

Nasturtium sylvestre var. incisum, commonly known as the woodland nasturtium, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, orange or yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are edible and have a peppery taste, similar to watercress, while the flowers are also used in culinary applications.

The species is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its potential medicinal properties.
