Neuroloma australe

Neuroloma australe, commonly known as the Australian false nettle, is a herbaceous plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Urticaceae, which includes stinging nettles, and is characterized by its opposite leaves and smooth, often hairy stems. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, and its leaves are typically oval to lance-shaped with serrated edges.

Neuroloma australe is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.
