Nepeta lavandulifolia

Nepeta lavandulifolia, commonly known as lavender catmint, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, gray-green leaves and produces spikes of blue to purple flowers. The plant is characterized by its strong, mint-like fragrance, which is reminiscent of lavender. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Nepeta lavandulifolia is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
