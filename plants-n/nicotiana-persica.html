Nicotiana persica

Nicotiana persica, commonly known as Persian tobacco, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and peppers. The plant is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. Its leaves are typically broad and lobed, with a slightly hairy surface.

Nicotiana persica is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has also been used historically for its nicotine-containing properties.
