Narcissus stellatus var. totus-albus

Narcissus stellatus var. totus-albus, commonly known as the white star daffodil, is a cultivar of the genus Narcissus native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its distinctive, pale white tepals that form a star-like shape, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows to a height of 20-30 centimeters, with a short stem and a single, showy flower. It blooms in early spring, producing a fragrant flower that attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is prized for its elegant appearance and is often cultivated in gardens for ornamental purposes.
