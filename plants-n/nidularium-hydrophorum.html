Nidularium hydrophorum

Nidularium hydrophorum, commonly known as the water lily of the genus Nidularium, is a tropical aquatic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its floating leaves that form a dense mat on the water surface, providing habitat for various aquatic organisms. The plant has showy, fragrant flowers that bloom above the water, typically in shades of white and pink. Its roots are submerged, anchoring it to the pond bottom while absorbing nutrients from the water.

Nidularium hydrophorum plays an important role in maintaining water quality and supporting aquatic ecosystems.
