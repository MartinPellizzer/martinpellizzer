Nierembergia linariifolia

Nierembergia linariifolia, commonly known as the slender bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and linear, giving it a grass-like appearance, while its stems are often hairy.

Nierembergia linariifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
