Nicotiana silvestris

Nicotiana silvestris, commonly known as wild tobacco, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshade plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming in late summer. Its leaves are broad and hairy, with a distinct aromatic scent when crushed.

Nicotiana silvestris is also notable for its role in attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and moths.
