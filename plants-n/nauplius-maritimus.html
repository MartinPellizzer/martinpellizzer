Nauplius maritimus

Nauplius maritimus, commonly known as the sea nauplius, is a small crustacean found in marine environments.

It belongs to the order Amphipoda and is typically found in coastal areas and estuaries. The organism has a segmented body with paired appendages, and it lacks a carapace. It plays a role in marine food webs as a primary consumer.

Its life cycle includes several larval stages, with the nauplius stage being the earliest and most distinctive.
