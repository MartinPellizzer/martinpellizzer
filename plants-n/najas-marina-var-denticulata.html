Najas marina var. denticulata

Najas marina var. denticulata, commonly known as the finely toothed water nymph, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Najadaceae and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, whorled leaves. The leaves are typically narrow and have finely serrated edges, which distinguish it from other similar species. It thrives in slow-moving to still waters, often found in ponds, lakes, and along the margins of rivers.

This plant plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and oxygen for various aquatic organisms.
