Nyssa bidoupensis

Nyssa bidoupensis, commonly known as Bidoup's sweetgum, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the humid forests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Nyssaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and oval-shaped leaves that are glossy on the upper surface. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by spherical, spiny fruit capsules containing numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring moist, well-drained soils.

Nyssa bidoupensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
