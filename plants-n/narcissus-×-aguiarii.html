Narcissus × aguiarii

Narcissus × aguiarii, commonly known as the Aguiarii daffodil, is a hybrid species within the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that typically range in color from white to yellow. The plant produces narrow, grass-like leaves and has a bulbous root system typical of many daffodils. It is native to the Iberian Peninsula and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its early spring blooms.
