Najas guadalupensis f. floridana

Najas guadalupensis f. floridana, commonly known as the Florida watermilfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to the southeastern United States.

It has slender, branching stems that are often covered in small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces tiny, green flowers that are arranged in clusters, typically emerging from the water surface. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various water organisms.

Due to its rapid growth, it can become invasive in some water bodies if not managed properly.
