Neottianthe cucullata

Neottianthe cucullata, commonly known as the hooded orchid, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, hood-like floral structure. The plant features bright yellow petals and a distinctive, elongated spur that extends from the flower. Neottianthe cucullata thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical beauty makes it a popular subject in both horticulture and botanical studies.
