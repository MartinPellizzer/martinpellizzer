Nemepiodon mexicanum

Nemepiodon mexicanum, commonly known as the Mexican orchid, is a rare and unique orchid species native to specific regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and strikingly colorful, fragrant flowers that bloom during the spring season. The plant typically features a pseudobulb structure, which helps it store water and nutrients, adapting it to its native arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, contributing to its overall ornamental appeal.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is considered a vulnerable species in its natural range.
