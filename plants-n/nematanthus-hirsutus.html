Nematanthus hirsutus

Nematanthus hirsutus, commonly known as the hairy whorled morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant features heart-shaped, hairy leaves that contribute to its common name. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its flowers open in the morning and close by midday, making it a popular choice for hanging baskets and containers.
