Nipponocalamus vaginatus f. variegatus

Nipponocalamus vaginatus f. variegatus, commonly known as the variegated bamboo, is a unique variant of the Nipponocalamus vaginatus species.

It is characterized by its striking variegation in leaf coloration, often featuring a mix of green, white, and yellow hues. This ornamental bamboo is native to East Asia and is prized for its aesthetic appeal in horticultural settings. It typically grows as a clump-forming plant with slender, hollow stems.

The variegation is due to genetic factors and can enhance its visual interest in gardens and landscapes.
