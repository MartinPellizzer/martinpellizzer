Nasturtium elongatum var. serratum

Nasturtium elongatum var. serratum, commonly known as the serrated nasturtium, is a herbaceous annual plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, brightly colored flowers, typically orange or red, which are borne on long, slender stems. The leaves are heart-shaped and have a serrated edge, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking vibrant, easy-to-grow ornamentals.

The plant is also known for its peppery taste, making it useful in culinary applications.
