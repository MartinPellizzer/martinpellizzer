Nicandra violacea

Nicandra violacea, commonly known as the blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in the morning and close in the afternoon. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, round fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Botanically, it belongs to the Convolvulaceae family, which includes other vines and climbing plants.
