Nauclea bracteosa

Nauclea bracteosa, commonly known as the African senna, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in open woodlands and savannas, thriving in well-drained soils.

Nauclea bracteosa is also noted for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
