Neckeria glauca

Neckeria glauca, commonly known as the blue neck orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it prefers warm, moist conditions with high humidity.

Neckeria glauca is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
