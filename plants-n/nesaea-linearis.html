Nesaea linearis

Nesaea linearis, commonly known as the slender nesaea, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Acanthaceae.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia, this plant thrives in moist, shaded environments. It is often found in forests and along stream banks, where it contributes to the biodiversity of the understory.

The plant's ornamental value has led to its cultivation in some horticultural settings.
