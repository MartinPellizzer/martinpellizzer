Nectandra laurel

Nectandra laurel, commonly known as the West Indian laurel, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like cinnamon and avocado. The tree is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow.

Its fruit is a small, round drupe that contains a single seed, and the plant is often valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
