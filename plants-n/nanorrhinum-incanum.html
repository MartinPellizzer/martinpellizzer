Nanorrhinum incanum

Nanorrhinum incanum, commonly known as the silverleaf nightshade, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub characterized by its silver-gray foliage and small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces berries that are typically yellow or orange when mature. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils.

Nanorrhinum incanum is known for its resilience to drought and its ability to attract various pollinators.
