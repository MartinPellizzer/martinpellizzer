Nicotiana urens

Nicotiana urens, commonly known as the annual tobacco plant, is a herbaceous species native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and alternate leaves that are typically lance-shaped. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple, depending on the variety. It is a member of the Solanaceae family, which includes other economically important plants like tomatoes and potatoes.

Nicotiana urens is known for its high nicotine content, making it a subject of both agricultural and scientific interest.
