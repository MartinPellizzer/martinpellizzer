Nigella arvensis subsp. aristata

Nigella arvensis subsp.

aristata, commonly known as the Persian melilot or aristate fennel, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family. It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as fields and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often hairy, while the seeds are small and oval, containing essential oils.

This subspecies is native to regions of Europe and western Asia, where it has been used traditionally in herbal medicine.
