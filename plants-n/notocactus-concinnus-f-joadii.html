Notocactus concinnus f. joadii

Notocactus concinnus f. joadii, commonly known as the Joad's notocactus, is a rare and distinctive cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stem that branches into multiple ribbed segments, often adorned with small, white, star-shaped flowers. The plant thrives in arid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and cactus enthusiasts.

Due to its rarity, it is often cultivated in specialized collections and requires careful care to ensure its survival.
