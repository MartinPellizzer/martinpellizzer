Notocactus glaucinus var. ibicuiensis

Notocactus glaucinus var. ibicuiensis, commonly known as the Ibicuiensis Cactus, is a unique species native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and a distinctive blue-gray coloration due to a dense covering of fine, white hairs. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding visual contrast to its muted foliage. It is a slow-growing cactus that thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor cultivation.
