Nyssa glauca

Nyssa glauca, commonly known as the white ash, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Cornaceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and opposite, simple leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, followed by bright red berries that are an important food source for wildlife.

Its wood is strong and durable, historically used for tool handles and furniture.
