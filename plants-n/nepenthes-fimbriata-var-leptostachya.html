Nepenthes fimbriata var. leptostachya

Nepenthes fimbriata var. leptostachya, commonly known as the slender-stemmed pitcher plant, is a carnivorous plant native to the rainforests of Borneo.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow pitchers that are often covered in fine, hair-like structures. The plant produces a unique type of nectar that attracts insects, which then fall into the pitcher and are digested by the plant's digestive enzymes. Its leaves are typically arranged in a spiral pattern, and the plant thrives in high humidity and acidic soil conditions.

This variant is noted for its striking appearance and is a popular species among carnivorous plant enthusiasts.
