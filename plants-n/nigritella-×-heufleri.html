Nigritella × heufleri

Nigritella × heufleri, commonly known as the hybrid sweet potato, is a botanical hybrid resulting from the cross between *Ipomoea batatas* and *Ipomoea trifida*.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth and the production of edible tuberous roots that are rich in starch and nutrients. The plant typically thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a waxy texture, and it produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink or purple.

This hybrid is valued both for its culinary uses and as an ornamental plant in gardens.
