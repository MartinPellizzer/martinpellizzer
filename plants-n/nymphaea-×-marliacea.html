Nymphaea × marliacea

Nymphaea × marliacea, commonly known as the yellow water lily, is a hybrid aquatic plant native to the Americas.

It features showy, floating yellow flowers that open in the morning and close in the afternoon. The plant has broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically green with a waxy surface. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in ponds and lakes.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental water gardens.
