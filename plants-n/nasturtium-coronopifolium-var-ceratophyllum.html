Nasturtium coronopifolium var. ceratophyllum

Nasturtium coronopifolium var. ceratophyllum, commonly known as the spiked nasturtium, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiky leaves and vibrant orange or red flowers. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible flowers.

The varietal name "ceratophyllum" refers to its horn-like leaf structures, which are a notable botanical feature.
