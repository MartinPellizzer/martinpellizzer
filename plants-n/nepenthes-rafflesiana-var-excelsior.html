Nepenthes rafflesiana var. excelsior

Nepenthes rafflesiana var. excelsior, commonly known as the giant pitcher plant, is a remarkable species native to the high-altitude regions of Borneo.

It is characterized by its large, elongated pitcher-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces a sticky liquid in its pitchers to trap and digest insects, which provides essential nutrients in its nutrient-poor habitat. Its striking red coloration and intricate venation patterns make it a visually distinctive species.

This variety is particularly noted for its robust growth and adaptability to specific ecological conditions.
