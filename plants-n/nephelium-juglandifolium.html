Nephelium juglandifolium

Nephelium juglandifolium, commonly known as the tamarind tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves and produces edible, pod-like fruits containing a sweet and sour pulp. The plant is valued for its fruit, which is widely used in cuisines around the world. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The tree can reach heights of up to 15 meters and is often cultivated for both its fruit and ornamental value.
