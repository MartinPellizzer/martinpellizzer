Nicotiana axillaris

Nicotiana axillaris, commonly known as the silverleaf tobacco, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine, it is cultivated in various parts of the world.
