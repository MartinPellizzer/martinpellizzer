Nicolsonia radicans

Nicolsonia radicans, commonly known as the creeping nicolsonia, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its low-growing, sprawling habit with fleshy, succulent leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often used in rock gardens and as a ground cover.
