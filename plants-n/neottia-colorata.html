Neottia colorata

Neottia colorata, commonly known as the yellow twayblade, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to various regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. The plant has a distinctive yellowish-green coloration and lacks true leaves, relying instead on its rhizome for nutrient absorption. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish, with a single lip that is often darker in color.

Neottia colorata is noted for its ability to form symbiotic relationships with mycorrhizal fungi, which aid in its nutrient uptake.
