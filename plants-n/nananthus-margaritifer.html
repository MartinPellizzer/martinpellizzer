Nananthus margaritifer

Nananthus margaritifer, commonly known as the pearl-bearing nananthus, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, white, pearl-like flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant's leaves are narrow and elongated, often covered in a fine, silvery down. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution and unique floral structure, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
