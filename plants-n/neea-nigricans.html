Neea nigricans

Neea nigricans, commonly known as blackberry nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen leaves and small, dark berries. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its berries are edible but have a tart, sometimes bitter flavor, and are often used in local cuisines.

Neea nigricans plays an important role in supporting local wildlife, particularly birds and small mammals.
