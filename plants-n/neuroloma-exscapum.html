Neuroloma exscapum

Neuroloma exscapum, commonly known as the scarlet orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant red petals and sepals, which are often adorned with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows in tropical regions, thriving in humid and shaded environments. Its roots are fibrous and help anchor it to the substrate, while its rhizome system allows for vegetative propagation.

The flowers bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
