Nigella confusa

Nigella confusa, commonly known as Persian cumin, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, yellowish-green flowers and finely divided leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and thrives in well-drained soils. Its seeds, which are often used in cooking, have a strong, pungent aroma and are harvested when fully mature.

Nigella confusa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
