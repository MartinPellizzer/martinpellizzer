Nama flavescens

Nama flavescens, commonly known as the yellow flame bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants, and is characterized by its small, yellowish-green leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that are often followed by orange or red fruits. Its foliage and flowers are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
