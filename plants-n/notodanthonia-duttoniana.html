Notodanthonia duttoniana

Notodanthonia duttoniana, commonly known as the Dutton's grass, is a perennial grass species native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as shrublands and sandplains.

Notodanthonia duttoniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
