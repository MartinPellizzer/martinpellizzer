Nassauvia aculeata var. robusta

Nassauvia aculeata var. robusta, commonly known as the roughleaf nassauvia, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its woody stems, which are often covered in small, stiff bristles. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink to red in color, arranged in clusters along the stems. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a rough, hairy texture.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for xeriscaping in arid environments.
