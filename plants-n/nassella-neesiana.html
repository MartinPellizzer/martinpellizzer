Nassella neesiana

Nassella neesiana, commonly known as the slender needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces slender, spike-like inflorescences. Its flowers are small and wind-pollinated, contributing to its ecological role in supporting pollinators.

Nassella neesiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
