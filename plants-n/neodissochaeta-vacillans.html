Neodissochaeta vacillans

Neodissochaeta vacillans, commonly known as the wavering threadleaf, is a small, delicate annual plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces tiny, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, typically in clusters at the tips of the stems. Neodissochaeta vacillans thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dune environments.

Its unique morphology and habitat make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
