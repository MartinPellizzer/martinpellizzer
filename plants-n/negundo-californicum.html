Negundo californicum

Negundo californicum, commonly known as the California boxwood, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and dark green, aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and prefers well-drained soil in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage remains evergreen throughout the year, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

The plant is also valued for its ability to tolerate urban conditions, including air pollution and compacted soils.
