Nymphaea hookeriana

Nymphaea hookeriana, commonly known as Hooker's water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Nymphaeaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy, white to pink flowers that bloom above the water surface. The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves that float on the water's surface, supported by long, flexible petioles. Its roots are anchored in the muddy substrate at the bottom of still or slow-moving water bodies.

Nymphaea hookeriana is valued for its ornamental beauty and plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various water organisms.
