Nicotiana axillaris var. propinqua

Nicotiana axillaris var. propinqua, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tobacco plant, is a herbaceous species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender stems and lance-shaped leaves, which are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and botanical collections.

Due to its ornamental value and historical significance in the cultivation of tobacco, it is of interest to both horticulturists and botanists.
