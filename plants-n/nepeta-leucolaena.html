Nepeta leucolaena

Nepeta leucolaena, commonly known as white catmint, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically gray-green and have a mint-like fragrance.

It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
