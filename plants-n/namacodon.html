Namacodon

Namacodon, commonly known as the goldenbush, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

The botanical name *Namacodon* reflects its historical use in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
