Nama paludosa

Nama paludosa, commonly known as the bog sedgeland grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, acidic soils and is often found in bogs, marshes, and wet meadows. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences. It plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and supporting biodiversity.

Nama paludosa is adapted to low-nutrient conditions and is often an indicator species for healthy, undisturbed wetlands.
