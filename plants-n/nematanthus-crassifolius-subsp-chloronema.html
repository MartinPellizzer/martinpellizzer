Nematanthus crassifolius subsp. chloronema

Nematanthus crassifolius subsp.

chloronema, commonly known as the greenleaf waxplant, is a flowering plant native to South America. It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically green to bluish-green in color.

The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple in hue. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This species is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and compact growth habit.
