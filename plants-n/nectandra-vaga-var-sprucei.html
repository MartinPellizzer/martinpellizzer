Nectandra vaga var. sprucei

Nectandra vaga var. sprucei, commonly known as the spruce's nectandra, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, lowland forests and is valued for its timber and essential oils.

Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine, while its wood is prized for its durability and fine grain.
