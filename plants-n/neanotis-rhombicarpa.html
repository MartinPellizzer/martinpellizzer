Neanotis rhombicarpa

Neanotis rhombicarpa, commonly known as the rhombicarpus cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are often covered in spines, which help to reduce water loss and deter herbivores. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom during the warmer months, adding a splash of color to its harsh environment. Its root system is adapted to absorb moisture efficiently from the limited rainfall.

Neanotis rhombicarpa is a hardy species that thrives in well-drained soils and full sun exposure.
