Neomammillaria seitziana

Neomammillaria seitziana, commonly known as the Seitz's mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and is covered in white to pale blue radial spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Neomammillaria seitziana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in a controlled setting.
