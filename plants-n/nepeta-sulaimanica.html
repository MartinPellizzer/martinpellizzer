Nepeta sulaimanica

Nepeta sulaimanica, commonly known as the Sulaiman mountain catmint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Pakistan and Afghanistan.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their calming and antispasmodic properties.

Nepeta sulaimanica is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in rocky soils.
