Nicotiana alata f. rubella

Nicotiana alata f. rubella, commonly known as the red-flowered tobacco plant, is a cultivar of the tobacco plant characterized by its vibrant red flowers.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshade plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant is native to South America and is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking floral display. It typically grows as an annual herb with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves.

The flowers bloom in clusters and are known for their strong scent, which attracts pollinators like moths and hummingbirds.
