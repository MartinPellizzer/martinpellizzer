Notocactus crassigibbus

Notocactus crassigibbus, commonly known as the thick-stemmed cactus, is a species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers during the summer months. It has a slow growth rate and is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance.

Notocactus crassigibbus requires minimal water and well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
