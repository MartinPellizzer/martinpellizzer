Nepeta tuberosa

Nepeta tuberosa, commonly known as catnip, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. Its leaves contain high levels of nepetalactone, a compound responsible for its stimulating effects on cats.

Nepeta tuberosa is often cultivated for its aromatic properties and traditional medicinal uses.
