Nerium verecundum

Nerium verecundum, commonly known as the sacred fig or bodhi tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that develop into edible figs. The plant has a deep root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion. It thrives in humid climates with well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and cultural significance in various traditions.
