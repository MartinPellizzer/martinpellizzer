Najas tenuissima

Najas tenuissima, commonly known as slender watermilfoil, is a small, aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It is a member of the Najadaceae family and typically grows in shallow, slow-moving waters. The plant has finely divided, ribbon-like leaves that are often submerged, with small, inconspicuous flowers that appear in late summer. It plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small fish and invertebrates.

Due to its rapid growth, it can become invasive in some water bodies if not managed.
