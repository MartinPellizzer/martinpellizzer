Nicotiana graciliflora

Nicotiana graciliflora, commonly known as the slender-flowered tobacco, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its delicate, elongated leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers.

Nicotiana graciliflora is notable for its ornamental value and its potential role in ecological studies due to its unique floral structure.
