Narcissus elegans var. flavescens

Narcissus elegans var. flavescens, commonly known as the golden crown narcissus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Europe.

It produces showy, fragrant flowers with white petals and a bright yellow corona. The plant typically blooms in early spring, reaching heights of 30 to 60 centimeters. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming a basal rosette.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
