Nipponocalamus fortunei

Nipponocalamus fortunei, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of fiber.

Nipponocalamus fortunei is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native habitats.
