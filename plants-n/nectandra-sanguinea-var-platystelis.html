Nectandra sanguinea var. platystelis

Nectandra sanguinea var. platystelis, commonly known as the red sandalwood tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark reddish-brown heartwood. The leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptic in shape, with a glossy surface. The flowers are small and white, clustered in inflorescences, and the fruit is a small, woody drupe.

This species is valued for its high-quality timber, which is used in furniture and traditional crafts.
