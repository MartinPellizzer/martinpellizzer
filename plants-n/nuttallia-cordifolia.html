Nuttallia cordifolia

Nuttallia cordifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf nuttallia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are typically bright green and have a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Nuttallia cordifolia thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native habitat.
