Narcissus pseudonarcissus subsp. major

Narcissus pseudonarcissus subsp.

major, commonly known as the Giant Crowned Narcissus, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically measure up to 10 centimeters in diameter.

The plant features a single, upright stem that supports a cluster of cup-shaped blooms with a distinct yellow corona. Its leaves are long, narrow, and grass-like, emerging from a basal rosette.

This subspecies is valued in horticulture for its striking appearance and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental qualities.
