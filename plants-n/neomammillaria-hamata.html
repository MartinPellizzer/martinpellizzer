Neomammillaria hamata

Neomammillaria hamata, commonly known as the hairy nipple cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stem that is covered in white, woolly hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a vibrant touch to its otherwise desolate look. It is a slow-growing species that thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care, it is a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts.
