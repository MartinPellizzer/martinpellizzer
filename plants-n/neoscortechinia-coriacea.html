Neoscortechinia coriacea

Neoscortechinia coriacea, commonly known as the roughleaf hakea, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and leathery, rough-textured leaves. The plant produces small, spherical flower clusters that are typically pink or red in color. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought.

Neoscortechinia coriacea is often found in heathlands and dry forests, contributing to the biodiversity of these ecosystems.
