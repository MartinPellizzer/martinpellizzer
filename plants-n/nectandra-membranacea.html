Nectandra membranacea

Nectandra membranacea, commonly known as the membranaceae or the white sand tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant is known for its smooth, pale bark and its ability to thrive in well-drained, acidic soils. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds, and it plays a role in the ecosystems of its native habitats.

Nectandra membranacea is also valued for its timber and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
