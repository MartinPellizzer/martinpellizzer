Narcissus bulbocodium subsp. vulgaris

Narcissus bulbocodium subsp.

vulgaris, commonly known as the common daffodil, is a flowering plant native to Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or white in color.

The plant produces a cluster of strap-shaped leaves and has a bulbous root system that stores nutrients. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, often growing in meadows and woodland edges.

This species is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and is a symbol of renewal and hope in many cultures.
