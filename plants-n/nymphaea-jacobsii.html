Nymphaea jacobsii

Nymphaea jacobsii, commonly known as the Jacobs' water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that float on the water's surface, providing a habitat for various aquatic organisms. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, often opening in the early morning and closing by midday. Its roots are anchored in the muddy substrate, allowing it to thrive in still or slow-moving freshwater environments.

Nymphaea jacobsii is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its role in maintaining aquatic ecosystem balance.
