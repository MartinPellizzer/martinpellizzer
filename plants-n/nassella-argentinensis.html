Nassella argentinensis

Nassella argentinensis, commonly known as the Argentine needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, needle-like leaves and produces fluffy, pale yellow to brown seed heads. This plant thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its fibrous root system helps prevent soil erosion, making it ecologically significant in its native range.

It is often used in restoration projects due to its adaptability and hardiness.
