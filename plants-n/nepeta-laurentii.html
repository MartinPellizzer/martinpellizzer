Nepeta laurentii

Nepeta laurentii, commonly known as catmint, is a flowering plant in the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is native to southern Europe and western Asia and is characterized by its aromatic, square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, clustered blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is a hardy perennial that thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil.

Nepeta laurentii is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
