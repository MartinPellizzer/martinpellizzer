Nicotiana paulineana

Nicotiana paulineana, commonly known as the Pauline tobacco, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual or biennial herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are broad and hairy, with a distinct aromatic scent.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has also been studied for its potential in pharmaceutical research.
