Nectandra patens

Nectandra patens, commonly known as the white sand tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic foliage and showy white flowers. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests. Its bark and leaves contain essential oils, which have been used in traditional medicine.

Nectandra patens is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
