Neoporteria nidus f. senilis

Neoporteria nidus f. senilis, commonly known as the silverleaf neoporteria, is a unique variant of the neoporteria plant characterized by its distinctive silvery foliage.

This plant is native to specific regions in South Africa, where it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in a fine, silvery-white coating, giving it a striking appearance. It is a compact shrub that produces small, clustered flowers in shades of white and pink during the warmer months.

Due to its ornamental value and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in gardens with suitable growing conditions.
