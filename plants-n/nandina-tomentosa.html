Nandina tomentosa

Nandina tomentosa, commonly known as the heavenly bamboo, is a flowering plant native to Japan and China.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, white flowers that appear in the spring. The plant produces red berries in the fall, which are attractive to birds. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Nandina tomentosa is also valued for its ability to tolerate a variety of soil types and climates.
