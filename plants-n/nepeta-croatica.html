Nepeta croatica

Nepeta croatica, commonly known as the Croatian catmint, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its aromatic foliage contains essential oils that give it a minty scent.

Nepeta croatica is often used in traditional medicine for its calming and antiseptic properties.
