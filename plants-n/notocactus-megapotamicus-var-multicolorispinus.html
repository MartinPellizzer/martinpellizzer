Notocactus megapotamicus var. multicolorispinus

Notocactus megapotamicus var. multicolorispinus, commonly known as the multicolored spiny cactus, is a species native to South America, particularly Argentina and Bolivia.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are covered in sharp, spiny bristles, which provide protection against herbivores. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months, adding to its ornamental value. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it thrives in well-draining soil with full sun exposure.

This variety is prized for its unique coloration and adaptability to arid environments.
