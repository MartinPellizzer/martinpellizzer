Neoporteria volliana

Neoporteria volliana, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvetleaf hibiscus, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are velvety to the touch, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and yellow. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in moist forest understories.

Neoporteria volliana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden areas.
