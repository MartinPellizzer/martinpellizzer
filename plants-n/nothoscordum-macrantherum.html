Nothoscordum macrantherum

Nothoscordum macrantherum, commonly known as the large-flowered garlic flower, is a species native to South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant belonging to the Amaryllidaceae family. The plant features strap-like, grassy leaves and produces showy, umbel-like clusters of white or pale pink flowers. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild garlic scent, though they are not as potent as true garlic.
