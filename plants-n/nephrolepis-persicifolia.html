Nephrolepis persicifolia

Nephrolepis persicifolia, commonly known as the Persian water fern, is a species of fern native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It features delicate, feathery fronds that are typically light green and often have a slightly wavy or serrated edge. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often used in ornamental horticulture for its attractive foliage. It reproduces via spores rather than seeds, a characteristic typical of ferns.

Its rhizomes are creeping and help it spread across the soil surface, making it a hardy and adaptable species.
