Neja macrocephala

Neja macrocephala, commonly known as the giant-headed orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 10 centimeters in diameter. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments within the understory of tropical forests. Its leaves are broad and leathery, providing a sturdy structure for supporting its heavy floral display.

Neja macrocephala is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
