Narcissus bysantinus

Narcissus bysantinus, commonly known as the Persian daffodil, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and the Middle East. The plant produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and white, typically blooming in early spring. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, growing from a bulbous underground stem.

Narcissus bysantinus is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, early-flowering display.
