Narcissus crenulatus

Narcissus crenulatus, commonly known as the crested daffodil, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, recurved petals and a prominent, often yellow or white, trumpet-shaped corona. Native to southern Europe, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. The plant produces showy flowers in early spring, making it a popular ornamental choice.

Its foliage consists of strap-like leaves that are evergreen in milder climates.
