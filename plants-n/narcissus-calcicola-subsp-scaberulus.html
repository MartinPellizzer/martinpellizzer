Narcissus calcicola subsp. scaberulus

Narcissus calcicola subsp.

scaberulus, commonly known as the rough-skinned narcissus, is a rare and specialized plant species native to the Mediterranean region. It thrives in calcareous soils and is typically found in rocky or limestone-rich habitats.

The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers with a distinctive, narrow shape. Its leaves are narrow and sometimes covered with a rough texture, contributing to its common name.

Due to its limited distribution and specific environmental requirements, it is considered a botanically significant and ecologically sensitive species.
