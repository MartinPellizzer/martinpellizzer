Nassavia

Nassavia, commonly known as the silverleaf plant, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Polygonaceae.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that often have a silvery or grayish appearance due to a dense covering of fine hairs. The plants typically grow in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing their adaptability to dry conditions. They produce small, clustered flowers that can be pink, red, or white, adding visual interest to the landscape.
