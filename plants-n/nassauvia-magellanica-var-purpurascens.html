Nassauvia magellanica var. purpurascens

Nassauvia magellanica var. purpurascens, commonly known as the purpleleaf nassauvia, is a shrub native to South America, particularly found in Argentina and Chile.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that turn a striking purple color during the fall season. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red, which bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value is largely due to its vibrant foliage and delicate floral display.
