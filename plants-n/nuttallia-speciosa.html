Nuttallia speciosa

Nuttallia speciosa, commonly known as the showy milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from pink to purple, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats like grasslands and rocky slopes. It has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds.

Nuttallia speciosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
