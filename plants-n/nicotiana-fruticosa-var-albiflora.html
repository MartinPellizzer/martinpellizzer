Nicotiana fruticosa var. albiflora

Nicotiana fruticosa var. albiflora, commonly known as the whiteflower tobacco, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy, white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces fragrant, glandular hairs on its stems and leaves, which contribute to its distinctive aroma. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in a dense layer of fine pubescence.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
