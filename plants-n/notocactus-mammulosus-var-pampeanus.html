Notocactus mammulosus var. pampeanus

Notocactus mammulosus var. pampeanus, commonly known as the Pampean globose cactus, is a unique species native to Argentina.

It is characterized by its globular, ribbed stems that are typically green with a bluish tinge. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking contrast to its foliage. It is a slow-growing cactus that thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and delicate species in the wild.
