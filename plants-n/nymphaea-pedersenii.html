Nymphaea pedersenii

Nymphaea pedersenii, commonly known as the Pedersen's water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to the wetlands of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Nymphaeaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy, white to pinkish flowers that bloom above the water surface. The plant has broad, heart-shaped leaves that float on the water's surface, providing habitat for various aquatic organisms. Its roots are anchored in the mud at the bottom of the water body, and it thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments.

Nymphaea pedersenii is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in maintaining water quality and supporting biodiversity.
