Neocheiropteris ovata f. doryopteris

Neocheiropteris ovata f. doryopteris, commonly known as the Doryopteris form of the ovate cheiropteris, is a fern characterized by its distinctive fronds.

The plant features lanceolate, serrate leaves that are typically arranged in a loose, feathery pattern. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate forest understories. The fronds are usually dark green and have a leathery texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

This fern is valued for its unique morphology and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and shady gardens.
