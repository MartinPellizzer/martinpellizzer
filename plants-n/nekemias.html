Nekemias

Nekemias, commonly known as the "buttercup of the desert," is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, which includes many showy, often toxic species. The plant features delicate, yellow flowers with a distinctive cup-like shape, surrounded by soft, hairy leaves. It thrives in sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
