Nepeta kurramensis

Nepeta kurramensis, commonly known as Kurram catnip, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Pakistan.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Nepeta kurramensis is notable for its potential medicinal properties and its role in local ecosystems.
