Nuxia mannii

Nuxia mannii, commonly known as the Natal wild pear, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves that are opposite in arrangement. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, pear-shaped fruits. Nuxia mannii is adapted to dry conditions and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
