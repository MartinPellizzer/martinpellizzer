Nepeta pojarkovae

Nepeta pojarkovae, commonly known as the Siberian catmint, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically gray-green and have a mint-like scent when crushed.

Nepeta pojarkovae is often used in traditional medicine for its purported calming and antispasmodic properties.
