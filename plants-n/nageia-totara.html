Nageia totara

Nageia totara, commonly known as the kauri tree, is a coniferous evergreen native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and broad, flat foliage. The tree has a distinctive bark that is rough and furrowed, often with a reddish hue. Nageia totara is known for its slow growth and longevity, with some individuals living over 1,000 years.

It plays a significant ecological and cultural role in New Zealand's forests.
