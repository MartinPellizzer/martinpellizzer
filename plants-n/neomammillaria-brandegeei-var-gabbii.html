Neomammillaria brandegeei var. gabbii

Neomammillaria brandegeei var. gabbii, commonly known as the Gabb's clustered cactus, is a species native to the Chihuahuan Desert in Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and is typically covered in white to pale blue spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are often followed by small, reddish fruits. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

This variety is prized for its unique appearance and is often cultivated in rock gardens and as a collector's plant.
