Nilgirianthus meeboldii

Nilgirianthus meeboldii, commonly known as the Nilgiri aster, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy purple to blue flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a hairy texture that helps retain moisture.

Nilgirianthus meeboldii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
