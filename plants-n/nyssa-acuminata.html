Nyssa acuminata

Nyssa acuminata, commonly known as the narrowleaf nash or narrowleaf sweetgum, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Nyssaceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple, elliptic leaves. The tree produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by spherical, spiny fruit capsules containing numerous seeds.

Its botanical features make it a valuable component of riparian and wetland ecosystems.
