Nigella arvensis var. arabica

Nigella arvensis var. arabica, commonly known as black cumin or Roman coriander, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, branched stems and small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces small, spherical seed capsules containing numerous tiny, dark brown seeds. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

It is often used in traditional medicine and as a spice due to its aromatic seeds.
