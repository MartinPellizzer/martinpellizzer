Nasa dyeri

Nasa dyeri, commonly known as the moonflower, is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It is a member of the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshade plants. The plant is characterized by its large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night and close during the day. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a velvety texture.

Nasa dyeri is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique blooming behavior.
