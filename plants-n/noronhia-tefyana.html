Noronhia tefyana

Noronhia tefyana, commonly known as the African snowball, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a small, deciduous shrub that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces clusters of white, fluffy flowers that resemble snowballs, blooming in late winter to early spring. Its leaves are small, oval, and covered in a fine, silvery pubescence.

Noronhia tefyana is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in harsh, arid conditions.
