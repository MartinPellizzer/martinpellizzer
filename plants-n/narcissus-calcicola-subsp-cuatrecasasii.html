Narcissus calcicola subsp. cuatrecasasii

Narcissus calcicola subsp.

cuatrecasasii, commonly known as the Cuatrecasas narcissus, is a rare and specialized plant native to southern Spain. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and yellow, cup-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring.

This subspecies thrives in calcareous soils, often found in rocky or limestone-rich habitats. Its narrow distribution and specific ecological requirements make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat degradation, it is considered vulnerable, highlighting the need for its protection.
