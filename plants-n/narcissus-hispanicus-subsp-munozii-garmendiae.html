Narcissus hispanicus subsp. munozii-garmendiae

Narcissus hispanicus subsp.

munozii-garmendiae, commonly known as the Munoz-Garmendia daffodil, is a rare and endemic species native to southern Spain. It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and a single, slender flower with a yellow perianth.

The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils in open habitats. It blooms in early spring, producing a cup-shaped corona that is often more prominent than the tepals.
