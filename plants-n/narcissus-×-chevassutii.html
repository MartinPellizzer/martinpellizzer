Narcissus × chevassutii

Narcissus × chevassutii, commonly known as the Chevassut daffodil, is a hybrid perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers with a prominent central corona. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with strap-like, grassy leaves. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, thriving in temperate climates.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant spring blooms.
