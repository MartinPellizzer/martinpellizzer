Najas major subsp. armata

Najas major subsp.

armata, commonly known as the narrowleaf water plantain, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater habitats in North America. It belongs to the family Najadaceae and is characterized by its narrow, ribbon-like leaves that are often ribbon-like in appearance.

The plant typically grows in shallow, slow-moving waters and is often found in ponds, lakes, and wetlands. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually clustered in spike-like inflorescences.

The species is valued in aquatic ecosystems for its role in water filtration and as a food source for various aquatic organisms.
