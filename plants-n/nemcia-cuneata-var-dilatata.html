Nemcia cuneata var. dilatata

Nemcia cuneata var. dilatata, commonly known as the narrowleaf false nettle, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of North America.

It features slender, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has a fibrous root system and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Its botanical characteristics make it a common sight in eastern North American forests.
