Nicotiana corymbosa

Nicotiana corymbosa, commonly known as the clustered tobacco or sweet tobacco, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of fragrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds, and its leaves are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and botanical collections.

Nicotiana corymbosa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in the cultivation of tobacco.
