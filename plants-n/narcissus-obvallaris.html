Narcissus obvallaris

Narcissus obvallaris, commonly known as the yellow daffodil, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers and narrow, grass-like leaves. Native to southern Europe, it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. The plant produces a single, upright stem that bears one to three flowers.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
