Noccaea caerulescens

Noccaea caerulescens, commonly known as the azure rockcress, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its small, blue or violet flowers and its ability to thrive in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant is known for its high concentration of heavy metals, making it a valuable species in phytoremediation efforts. It typically grows in alpine or arid environments, often in areas with low nutrient availability.

Noccaea caerulescens is a perennial species that produces seeds through self-fertilization.
