Neoporteria intermedia f. gracilis

Neoporteria intermedia f. gracilis, commonly known as the slender marsh fern, is a variant of the marsh fern species native to wetland environments in the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, slender fronds that are typically arching and pinnately compound. The plant thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in swamps, bogs, and along stream banks. Its reproductive structures include sori that are clustered on the undersides of the fronds, typical of the fern family.

The foliage provides habitat for various microorganisms and small invertebrates, contributing to the ecological diversity of its wetland habitat.
