Neomitranthes obtusa

Neomitranthes obtusa, commonly known as the broadleaf pincushion, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, spherical clusters of yellow-orange flowers. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Neomitranthes obtusa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
