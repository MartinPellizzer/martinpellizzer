Nonatelia venulosa

Nonatelia venulosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or pale yellow. Its roots are fibrous and can spread extensively, making it a persistent weed in agricultural fields.

Nonatelia venulosa is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its rapid growth rate.
