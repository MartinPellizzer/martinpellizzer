Nepeta botschantzevii

Nepeta botschantzevii, commonly known as the Siberian catmint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, upright stems and small, fragrant blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Nepeta botschantzevii is also noted for its mild mint-like aroma, which can be soothing to both humans and animals.
