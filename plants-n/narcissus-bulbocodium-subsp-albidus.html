Narcissus bulbocodium subsp. albidus

Narcissus bulbocodium subsp.

albidus, commonly known as the white bulbocodium narcissus, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaryllidaceae family. It is characterized by its small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant produces a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and has a bulbous root system. Native to southern Europe, it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
