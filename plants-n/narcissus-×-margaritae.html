Narcissus × margaritae

Narcissus × margaritae, commonly known as the white narcissus or pearl narcissus, is a hybrid species within the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, white flowers with a distinctive yellow center. The plant typically blooms in early spring, producing fragrant, bell-shaped blooms that are highly ornamental. It prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its delicate appearance and seasonal bloom, it is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens.
