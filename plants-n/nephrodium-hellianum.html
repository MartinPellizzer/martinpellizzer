Nephrodium hellianum

Nephrodium hellianum, commonly known as the golden cup fern, is a unique fern species characterized by its distinctive, cup-shaped fronds.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is native to certain regions of Southeast Asia. The plant features a short, creeping rhizome with dense, clustered fronds that are typically bright yellow to golden in color. Its fronds are pinnately divided, with lance-shaped pinnae that are often curled or rolled.

This fern thrives in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appearance in horticulture.
