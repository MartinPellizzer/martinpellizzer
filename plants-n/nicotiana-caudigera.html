Nicotiana caudigera

Nicotiana caudigera, commonly known as the long-tailed tobacco, is a herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its striking, elongated flowers that can reach up to 15 cm in length. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually pink to purple in color and bloom in late summer to early autumn.

Nicotiana caudigera is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky soils and open habitats.
