Niedenzuella caracasana

Niedenzuella caracasana, commonly known as the Caracasana fern, is a species of polypodioid fern native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are typically green and glossy, with a slender, creeping rhizome. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories or along stream banks. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, typical of many fern species.

Niedenzuella caracasana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
