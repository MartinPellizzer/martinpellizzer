Narcissus × kirchnerianus

Narcissus × kirchnerianus, commonly known as the golden crown narcissus, is a hybrid species within the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact, rounded shape and bright yellow flowers with a distinctive, recurved tepal arrangement. The plant typically blooms in early spring, producing 4–8 perianth segments that are often reflexed backward. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in floral arrangements and landscape design.
