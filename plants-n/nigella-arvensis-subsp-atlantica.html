Nigella arvensis subsp. atlantica

Nigella arvensis subsp.

atlantica, commonly known as the Atlantic cowslip, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of western Europe. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and open habitats.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by distinctive, three-lobed seed pods. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential health benefits.
