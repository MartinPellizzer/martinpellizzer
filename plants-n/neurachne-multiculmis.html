Neurachne multiculmis

Neurachne multiculmis, commonly known as the slender flat sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to Australia.

It typically grows in wetland and swampy habitats, where it forms dense, tufted clumps. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a distinct rhizome structure, which help it thrive in waterlogged soils.

Neurachne multiculmis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland environments and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
