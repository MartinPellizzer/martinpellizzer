Nolina texana var. compacta

Nolina texana var. compacta, commonly known as the Texas spineflower, is a shrub-like succulent native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical clusters of blue-green, needle-like leaves that are covered in a fine, white bloom. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid climates.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
