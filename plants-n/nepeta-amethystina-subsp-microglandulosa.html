Nepeta amethystina subsp. microglandulosa

Nepeta amethystina subsp.

microglandulosa, commonly known as the small-glandular catmint, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe. It features hairy, aromatic leaves and produces clusters of pale lavender to white flowers.

The plant is characterized by its small, glandular structures on the leaves and stems, which contribute to its distinctive scent. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

This subspecies is closely related to other Nepeta species and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
