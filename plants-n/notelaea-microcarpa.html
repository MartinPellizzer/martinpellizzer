Notelaea microcarpa

Notelaea microcarpa, commonly known as the small-fruited pandanus, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its pungent, aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by fleshy, orange-red berries. Its leaves are often used in traditional cooking and for making thatch.

Notelaea microcarpa is well adapted to coastal and wetland environments, thriving in humid, tropical climates.
