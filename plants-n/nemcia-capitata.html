Nemcia capitata

Nemcia capitata, commonly known as the clustered nemcia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring dense clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that are usually arranged in whorls around the stem. Its flowers are borne in compact, spherical inflorescences at the ends of the branches.

Nemcia capitata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
