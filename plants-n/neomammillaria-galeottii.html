Neomammillaria galeottii

Neomammillaria galeottii, commonly known as the Galeotti's pincushion cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and dense covering of white to pale blue spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring or early summer, adding a striking contrast to its otherwise muted appearance. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xerophytic environments.

Its unique morphology and ornamental value make it a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
