Nuttallia strictissima

Nuttallia strictissima, commonly known as the narrowleaf tamarack, is a deciduous conifer native to the northeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, needle-like leaves that are arranged in whorls around the branches. The plant produces small, inconspicuous cones that mature in the second year, releasing seeds in the fall. Its roots form extensive networks that help stabilize soil in wetland environments.

Nuttallia strictissima is often found in moist, acidic soils along stream banks and in swampy areas.
