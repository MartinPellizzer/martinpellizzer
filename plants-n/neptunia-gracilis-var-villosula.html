Neptunia gracilis var. villosula

Neptunia gracilis var. villosula, commonly known as the hairy bladder nut, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by inflated, bladder-like fruits containing seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
