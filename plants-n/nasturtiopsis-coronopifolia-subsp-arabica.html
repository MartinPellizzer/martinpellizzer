Nasturtiopsis coronopifolia subsp. arabica

Nasturtiopsis coronopifolia subsp.

arabica, commonly known as the Arabian watercress, is a member of the Brassicaceae family. It is characterized by its erect, slender stems and small, alternate leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by round, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is typically found in rocky or coastal areas.

This subspecies is valued for its peppery flavor and has been used in traditional medicine and cuisine.
