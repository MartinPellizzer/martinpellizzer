Nepeta argolica subsp. dirphya

Nepeta argolica subsp.

dirphya, commonly known as the Greek catmint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Greece. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, pale blue to violet flowers.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its aromatic quality. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens or borders.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
