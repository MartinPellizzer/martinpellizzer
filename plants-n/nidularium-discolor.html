Nidularium discolor

Nidularium discolor, commonly known as the variable nest plant, is a member of the Bromeliaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup to collect water. Its flowers, which are typically yellow or orange, are borne on a tall, leafless stem.

Nidularium discolor is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and unique growth habit.
