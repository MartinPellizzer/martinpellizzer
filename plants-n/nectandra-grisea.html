Nectandra grisea

Nectandra grisea, commonly known as the gray nectandra, is a tropical tree native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic, often fragrant leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, oval-shaped fruits containing a single seed. Its wood is valued for its durability and is used in furniture and construction.

The plant also has traditional medicinal uses in some indigenous communities.
