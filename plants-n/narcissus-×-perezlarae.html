Narcissus × perezlarae

Narcissus × perezlarae, commonly known as the Peruvian daffodil, is a hybrid species within the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers with a prominent central corona, which distinguishes it from other daffodil varieties. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, featuring strap-shaped leaves that are bluish-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Botanically, it is notable for its ability to produce both terminal and lateral inflorescences, enhancing its ornamental value.
