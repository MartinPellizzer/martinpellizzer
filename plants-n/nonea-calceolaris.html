Nonea calceolaris

Nonea calceolaris, commonly known as the starry bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet, often with a star-like pattern on the petals. The plant has slender, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
