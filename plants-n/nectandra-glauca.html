Nectandra glauca

Nectandra glauca, commonly known as the glaucous nectandra, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The tree produces berries that are often used in traditional medicine due to their potential pharmacological properties. Its bark and leaves contain essential oils that contribute to its ecological and economic significance in its native regions.

Nectandra glauca thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.
