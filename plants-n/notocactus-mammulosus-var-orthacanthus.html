Notocactus mammulosus var. orthacanthus

Notocactus mammulosus var. orthacanthus, commonly known as the orthacanthus notocactus, is a cacti species native to the dry regions of South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are covered in clusters of spines, which serve as a defense mechanism against herbivores. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its rugged appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique morphology and adaptability make it a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts and xerophytic gardens.
