Narcissus bulbocodium subsp. bulbocodium

Narcissus bulbocodium subsp.

bulbocodium, commonly known as the bulbous daffodil, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to white.

The plant typically blooms in early spring and is characterized by its compact growth habit. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or woodland settings.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate in temperate climates.
