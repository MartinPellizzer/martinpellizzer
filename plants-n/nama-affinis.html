Nama affinis

Nama affinis, commonly known as the Namib Desert dandelion, is a hardy, low-growing plant adapted to arid environments.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of fleshy, blue-green leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the early morning, followed by the formation of fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland desert regions of Namibia.

Nama affinis plays a role in stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for small desert organisms.
