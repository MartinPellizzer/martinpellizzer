Napaea discolor

Napaea discolor, commonly known as the pale napaea, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its small, white flowers bloom in clusters during the summer months.

Napaea discolor is characterized by its distinctive, disc-like flower structure and is often found in moist, temperate habitats.
