Neurolaena lobata var. indivisa

Neurolaena lobata var. indivisa, commonly known as the yellow star-thistle, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and spiny leaves, which are adapted to thrive in dry, disturbed environments. The plant produces small, winged seeds that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its invasive potential in new regions. Its botanical features include a taproot system and a branched stem with alternate leaves.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is known for its composite flower heads.
