Neomammillaria pilensis

Neomammillaria pilensis, commonly known as the Pilensia, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are often covered in white to yellowish spines. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in the spring. Neomammillaria pilensis is a slow-growing species that thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water.

It is appreciated in horticulture for its compact size and ornamental value.
