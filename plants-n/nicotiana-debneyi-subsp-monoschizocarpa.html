Nicotiana debneyi subsp. monoschizocarpa

Nicotiana debneyi subsp.

monoschizocarpa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf tobacco, is a species native to Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple.

The plant is part of the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is known for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, open habitats.

This subspecies is distinct from other Nicotiana species due to its unique leaf morphology and floral structure.
