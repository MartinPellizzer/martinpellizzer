Nelumbium speciosum var. tamara

Nelumbium speciosum var. tamara, commonly known as the pink lotus, is a striking aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy pink flowers that bloom in summer, with a distinctive pinkish-white coloration. The plant has broad, heart-shaped leaves that float on the water's surface, supported by long, slender petioles. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in ponds and lakes.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for water gardens and botanical collections.
