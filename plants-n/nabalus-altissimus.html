Nabalus altissimus

Nabalus altissimus, commonly known as the tall alpine bellflower, is a flowering plant native to high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are typically narrow and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

Nabalus altissimus is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
