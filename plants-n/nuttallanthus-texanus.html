Nuttallanthus texanus

Nuttallanthus texanus, commonly known as Texas mountain larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies and rocky soils. The plant produces striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

Nuttallanthus texanus is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
