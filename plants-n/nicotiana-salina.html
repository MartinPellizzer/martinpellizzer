Nicotiana salina

Nicotiana salina, commonly known as saltbush tobacco, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of the United States and Mexico.

It is characterized by its succulent, bluish-green leaves that are adapted to thrive in saline and arid environments. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance and drought tolerance.

Nicotiana salina is also used in scientific research for its genetic traits and potential medicinal applications.
