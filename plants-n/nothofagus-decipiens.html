Nothofagus decipiens

Nothofagus decipiens, commonly known as the silver beech, is a species of evergreen tree native to southern South America.

It belongs to the Nothofagaceae family and is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on its leaves. The plant has a tall, straight trunk with a broad, rounded crown. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a distinctive waxy coating.

Nothofagus decipiens plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
