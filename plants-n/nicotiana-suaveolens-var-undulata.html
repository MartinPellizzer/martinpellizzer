Nicotiana suaveolens var. undulata

Nicotiana suaveolens var. undulata, commonly known as the sweet tobacco or fragrant tobacco, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its glandular leaves that emit a strong, sweet scent, especially at night. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in warm, sunny environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its foliage and flowers contain nicotine, making it a member of the nightshade family.
