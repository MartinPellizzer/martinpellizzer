Neomammillaria carnea var. cirrosa

Neomammillaria carnea var. cirrosa, commonly known as the cirrosa pincushion cactus, is a compact, slow-growing cactus native to Mexico.

It features a ribbed stem with prominent, woolly spines that give it a distinctive, fluffy appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring or early summer, adding a striking contrast to its green body. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique morphology and ornamental value make it a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
