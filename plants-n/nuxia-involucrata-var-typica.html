Nuxia involucrata var. typica

Nuxia involucrata var. typica, commonly known as the African silver bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, white, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid environments.

The leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
