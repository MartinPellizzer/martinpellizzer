Nassella landbeckii

Nassella landbeckii, commonly known as the Landbeck needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, needle-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and grassland habitats.

Nassella landbeckii plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
