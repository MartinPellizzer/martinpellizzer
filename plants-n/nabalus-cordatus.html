Nabalus cordatus

Nabalus cordatus, commonly known as the heartleaf lily, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and prefers well-drained soil. Its botanical features include a rhizomatous root system and showy blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Nabalus cordatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
