Nasa hornii

Nasa hornii, commonly known as the moonflower, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is a member of the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is known for its large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night. The plant has hairy, ovate leaves and produces small, yellow berries that contain toxic seeds. It thrives in cool, humid environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Nasa hornii is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
