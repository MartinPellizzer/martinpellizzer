Nasturtium × murrianum

Nasturtium × murrianum, commonly known as the Indian nasturtium, is a hybrid species within the Tropaeolaceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from orange to red, often with a distinctive yellow or orange center. The plant produces edible leaves and seed pods, which are commonly used in culinary applications. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Nasturtium × murrianum is also valued for its ability to attract beneficial insects like ladybugs, which help control aphid populations.
