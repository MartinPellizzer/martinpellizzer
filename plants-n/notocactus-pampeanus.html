Notocactus pampeanus

Notocactus pampeanus, commonly known as the Pampean cactus, is a species of cactus native to Argentina.

It is a columnar cactus with a distinctive ribbed structure and spines that are typically brown or reddish in color. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are clustered at the top of the stems. Notocactus pampeanus is adapted to arid environments and requires well-draining soil to thrive.

Its slow growth rate makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
