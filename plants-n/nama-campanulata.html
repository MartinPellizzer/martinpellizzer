Nama campanulata

Nama campanulata, commonly known as the bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs.

Nama campanulata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
