Notodanthonia virescens var. patagonica

Notodanthonia virescens var. patagonica, commonly known as the Patagonian grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically forms dense tufts and is well-adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, spikelet-bearing branches.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
