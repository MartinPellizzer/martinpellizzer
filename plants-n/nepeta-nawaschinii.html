Nepeta nawaschinii

Nepeta nawaschinii, commonly known as the Navaschin catmint, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage is often fragrant and may have a mint-like scent.

Nepeta nawaschinii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and hardy nature.
