Nepeta cordifolia

Nepeta cordifolia, commonly known as catnip, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant blue or purple flowers. The plant contains nepetalactone, a compound responsible for its stimulating effect on cats. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Nepeta cordifolia is also valued for its aromatic properties and use in herbal medicine.
