Nerium floridum

Nerium floridum, commonly known as the Florida starfruit, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive yellow-orange star-shaped fruits. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 feet. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly waxy texture.

The flowers are small, clustered, and usually pink or white, blooming in the spring.
