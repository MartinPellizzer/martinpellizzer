Nymphaea leiboldiana

Nymphaea leiboldiana, commonly known as the Leibold's water lily, is a species of aquatic plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes. Its roots are submerged, anchoring it to the bottom of the water body while its leaves and flowers float on the surface.

Nymphaea leiboldiana plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various water organisms.
