Nymphaea parkeriana

Nymphaea parkeriana, commonly known as the Parker's water lily, is a species of aquatic plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes. Its leaves are typically a deep green with a waxy surface, while its flowers range in color from white to pink.

Nymphaea parkeriana plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various water organisms.
