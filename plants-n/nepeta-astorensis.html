Nepeta astorensis

Nepeta astorensis, commonly known as catnip, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves contain nepetalactone, a compound known to stimulate the cat's sensory receptors.

Nepeta astorensis is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and historical use in herbal remedies.
