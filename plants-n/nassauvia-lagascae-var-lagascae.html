Nassauvia lagascae var. lagascae

Nassauvia lagascae var. lagascae, commonly known as the desert honeysuckle, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, exhibiting drought tolerance due to its succulent stems and leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring and summer. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
