Najas arguta

Najas arguta, commonly known as the narrowleaf watermilfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It belongs to the family Najadaceae and is characterized by its finely divided, ribbon-like leaves that are often arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically pollinated by water currents rather than insects. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Najas arguta is often found in slow-moving or still waters such as lakes, ponds, and canals.
