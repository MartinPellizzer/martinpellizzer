Nepeta levigata

Nepeta levigata, commonly known as catmint, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant produces small, fragrant blue or purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal remedies due to their calming properties.

Nepeta levigata thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
