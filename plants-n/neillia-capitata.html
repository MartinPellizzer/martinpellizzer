Neillia capitata

Neillia capitata, commonly known as the Cape chestnut, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, straight stem and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces oval, leathery fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Neillia capitata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to full sun and well-drained soil.

It is also valued for its hardiness and ability to thrive in coastal environments.
