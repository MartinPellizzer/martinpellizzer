Nipponocalamus yamadorianus

Nipponocalamus yamadorianus, commonly known as the Yamadori bamboo, is a species of grass in the family Poaceae.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its slender, erect culms that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant has narrow, linear leaves with a smooth texture and a pale green color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and used in traditional Japanese horticulture.
