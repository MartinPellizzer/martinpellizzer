Nectandra limbata

Nectandra limbata, commonly known as the white sand cedar, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic, dark-green leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by woody, ellipsoid fruits containing a single seed. Nectandra limbata thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its wood is highly valued for its durability and resistance to decay, making it an important species in local economies.
