Notocactus corynodes

Notocactus corynodes, commonly known as the yellow-tipped cactus, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its smooth, ribbed stems and dense clusters of yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky environments and is well adapted to arid conditions. Its flowers bloom in the spring and are often followed by the production of small, edible fruits.

The cactus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to care for in suitable growing conditions.
