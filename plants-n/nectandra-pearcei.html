Nectandra pearcei

Nectandra pearcei, commonly known as the yellow sand-wood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, yellowish wood and elliptical leaves with a smooth upper surface. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by dark purple berries.

Nectandra pearcei is valued for its hard, durable wood and is often found in lowland rainforests.
