Nemochloa sparsiflora

Nemochloa sparsiflora, commonly known as the sparse-flowered nemochloa, is a member of the Poaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance.

It is native to various regions in South America, particularly in countries like Brazil and Argentina. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its inflorescence consists of loosely arranged spikelets, giving it a distinctive, open appearance.

Due to its ecological significance, it plays a vital role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and supporting local biodiversity.
