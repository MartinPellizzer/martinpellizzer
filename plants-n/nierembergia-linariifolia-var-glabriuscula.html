Nierembergia linariifolia var. glabriuscula

Nierembergia linariifolia var. glabriuscula, commonly known as the silky false beggar's-ticks, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered with a fine, silky hair, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to cascade from containers or grow in ground covers.
