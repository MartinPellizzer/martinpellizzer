Nepeta paniculata

Nepeta paniculata, commonly known as catmint, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright, branching stems covered in fine, grayish-green foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils that are often used in herbal remedies.

Nepeta paniculata is also popular in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
