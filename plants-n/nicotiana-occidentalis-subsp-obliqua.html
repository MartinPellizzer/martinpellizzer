Nicotiana occidentalis subsp. obliqua

Nicotiana occidentalis subsp.

obliqua, commonly known as the western tobacco plant, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its erect growth habit and hairy stems.

The leaves are typically lanceolate, with a rough texture and a strong, aromatic odor. It produces clusters of trumpet-shaped flowers that are usually pink to reddish in color, blooming in late summer.

This subspecies is notable for its chemical composition, including nicotine, which makes it significant in both ecological and historical contexts.
