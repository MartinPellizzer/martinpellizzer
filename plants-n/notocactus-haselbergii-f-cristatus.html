Notocactus haselbergii f. cristatus

Notocactus haselbergii f. cristatus, commonly known as the cristate notocactus, is a rare and unique cactus species characterized by its distinctive crest-like growth pattern.

It originates from the arid regions of South America, particularly Brazil, where it thrives in dry, rocky environments. The plant features a short, thick stem with woolly, pale green to blue-gray spines, and its flowers are typically yellow or orange, blooming in the evening. It is highly valued in horticulture for its unusual appearance and slow growth, making it a sought-after specimen among plant enthusiasts.

Due to its limited natural habitat and specific care requirements, it is often cultivated in controlled environments.
