Nectandra dubia

Nectandra dubia, commonly known as the ghost wood or ghost tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark-colored wood and fragrant leaves. The plant is typically found in lowland and mountainous rainforests, thriving in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, while its fruits are round and fleshy, containing several seeds.

Nectandra dubia is valued for its durable timber and has traditional uses in medicine and as a source of essential oils.
