Notocactus bueneckeri f. conjungens

Notocactus bueneckeri f. conjungens, commonly known as the jointed notocactus, is a rare and striking cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its segmented, columnar stems that are often ribbed and covered in pale green or blue-gray spines. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a dramatic visual contrast to its otherwise muted foliage. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xerophytic gardens.

Due to its rarity and unique appearance, it is a sought-after specimen among cactus enthusiasts and botanists.
