Nephelium echinatum

Nephelium echinatum, commonly known as the starfruit or carambola, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small evergreen tree that produces distinctive star-shaped fruits. The leaves are simple, alternate, and ovate with a slightly serrated margin. The flowers are small, white, and clustered in panicles, and the fruit develops from a single ovary.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is cultivated for its edible, acidic fruit.
