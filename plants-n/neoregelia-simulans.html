Neoregelia simulans

Neoregelia simulans, commonly known as the "Brazillian violet" or "Neoregelia rosea," is a species of bromeliad native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its striking rosette-shaped foliage, which is often colored in shades of pink, red, and purple. The plant produces vibrant, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters, adding to its ornamental appeal. Neoregelia simulans thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

It is a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its compact size and attractive appearance.
