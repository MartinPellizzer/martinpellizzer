Nymphaea alba var. minor

Nymphaea alba var. minor, commonly known as the white water lily, is a aquatic plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features large, showy white flowers that float on the water's surface, with prominent yellow stamens. The plant has broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically floating or semi-submerged. Its roots are anchored in the muddy substrate at the bottom of ponds or lakes.

It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various water organisms.
