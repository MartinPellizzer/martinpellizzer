Nepeta schiraziana

Nepeta schiraziana, commonly known as Persian catnip, is a perennial herb native to Iran and parts of the Middle East.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their medicinal and culinary uses. The plant features square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly serrated edge. Its small, tubular flowers are typically blue to purple in color and bloom in late summer.

Nepeta schiraziana is valued for its aromatic properties and is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
