Nepeta italica subsp. troodi

Nepeta italica subsp.

troodi, commonly known as the Italian catnip, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features square, hairy stems and opposite leaves that are typically gray-green in color.

The plant produces small, fragrant blue or violet flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its aromatic leaves are often used in herbal teas and have mild sedative properties.
