Nemesia petiolina

Nemesia petiolina, commonly known as the trailing nemesia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and profuse display of small, colorful flowers. The plant typically blooms in spring and summer, producing clusters of tubular or bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, white, and yellow. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are slender and often hairy.

Nemesia petiolina is often used in hanging baskets and as a ground cover due to its vigorous growth and ornamental value.
