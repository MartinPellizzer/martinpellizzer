Nothoscordum calcaense

Nothoscordum calcaense, commonly known as the limestone garlic.

It is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Mexico, characterized by its distinctive garlic-like scent. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in umbels. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, adapted to reduce water loss in dry environments.

Nothoscordum calcaense is often found growing in rocky or limestone soils, showcasing its preference for alkaline conditions.
