Nepenthes robcantleyi

Nepenthes robcantleyi, commonly known as Robcantley's pitcher plant, is a carnivorous plant native to the tropical rainforests of Borneo.

It is characterized by its unique, elongated pitcher-shaped leaves that secrete a nutrient-rich liquid to trap and digest insects. The plant's vibrant red coloration and intricate patterns on its pitchers serve both as a lure for prey and as a means of attracting pollinators. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires high levels of atmospheric moisture for optimal growth.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
