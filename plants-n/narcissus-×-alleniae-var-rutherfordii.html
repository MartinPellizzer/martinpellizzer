Narcissus × alleniae var. rutherfordii

Narcissus × alleniae var. rutherfordii, commonly known as the Rutherford daffodil, is a hybrid cultivar prized for its vibrant yellow flowers.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its trumpet-shaped corona and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–50 cm, with blooms appearing in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

This variety is noted for its hardiness and ability to naturalize over time.
