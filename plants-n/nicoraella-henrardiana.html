Nicoraella henrardiana

Nicoraella henrardiana, commonly known as the Henrard's orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant has slender stems and lance-shaped leaves, which are adapted to the humid, tropical climate of its native habitat. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
