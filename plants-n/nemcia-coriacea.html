Nemcia coriacea

Nemcia coriacea, commonly known as the roughleaf nemcia, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that give it a distinctive textured appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Due to its unique foliage, it is occasionally cultivated as an ornamental plant in botanical gardens.
