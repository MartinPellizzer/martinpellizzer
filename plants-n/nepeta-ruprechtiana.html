Nepeta ruprechtiana

Nepeta ruprechtiana, commonly known as alpine catmint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine hairs and lance-shaped leaves with a silvery-blue hue. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant blue or purple flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine landscapes.

Nepeta ruprechtiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and mild mint-like scent.
