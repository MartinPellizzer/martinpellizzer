Nassella arcuata

Nassella arcuata, commonly known as the curly needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to western North America.

It is characterized by its slender, curved leaves and produces feathery, silver-gray seed heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun, making it well adapted to arid environments. Its fine, wiry foliage gives it a delicate appearance, and it is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value.

Nassella arcuata plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
