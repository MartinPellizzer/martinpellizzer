Nuxia pseudodentata

Nuxia pseudodentata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved kniphofia, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asphodelaceae family and is characterized by its tall, grass-like stems and striking, showy inflorescences. The plant produces bright orange or yellow flowers arranged in a spike-like cluster, which attract pollinators such as birds and insects. Its leaves are narrow and sword-shaped, often with a slightly toothed margin, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Nuxia pseudodentata is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun.
