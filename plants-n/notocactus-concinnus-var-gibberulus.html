Notocactus concinnus var. gibberulus

Notocactus concinnus var. gibberulus, commonly known as the gibberulous notocactus, is a compact, slow-growing cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It features a globular to cylindrical stem with a distinctive ribbed structure that enhances its drought tolerance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a striking contrast to its green foliage. Its surface is covered in fine, white to grayish hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping in dry climates.
