Nymphaea colorata var. parviflora

Nymphaea colorata var. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered water lily, is a water plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes. Its flowers open in the early morning and close by midday, a trait known as nyctinasty.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in aquatic ecosystems.
