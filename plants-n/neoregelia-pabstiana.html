Neoregelia pabstiana

Neoregelia pabstiana, commonly known as the Pabst's bromeliad, is a striking tropical plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, colorful inflorescence that can range from red to orange. The plant features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a central cup, which collects water and nutrients. Its foliage is often marked with intricate patterns and varying shades of green, enhancing its ornamental appeal.

Neoregelia pabstiana thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
