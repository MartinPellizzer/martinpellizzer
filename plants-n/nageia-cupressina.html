Nageia cupressina

Nageia cupressina, commonly known as the Japanese cypress, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the family Cupressaceae and is characterized by its narrow, pyramid-shaped crown and scale-like leaves. The plant produces small, spherical cones that mature to a reddish-brown color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for ornamental landscaping.

Nageia cupressina is valued for its durability and aesthetic appeal in both natural and cultivated settings.
