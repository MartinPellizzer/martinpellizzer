Nyssa angulosa

Nyssa angulosa, commonly known as the narrow-leafed sweet gum, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Nyssaceae and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The tree produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by spiny, spherical fruits containing seeds. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

Nyssa angulosa is often found in moist, well-drained soils along stream banks and in forested areas.
