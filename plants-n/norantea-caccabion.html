Norantea caccabion

Norantea caccabion, commonly known as the silverleaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking silver-green foliage and clusters of fragrant, violet-blue flowers. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture efficiently from the surrounding substrate.

Norantea caccabion is often cultivated for its ornamental value in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
