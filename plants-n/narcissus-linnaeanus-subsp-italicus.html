Narcissus linnaeanus subsp. italicus

Narcissus linnaeanus subsp.

italicus, commonly known as the Italian daffodil, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Europe. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers with bright yellow petals.

The flowers typically bloom in early spring, adding a vibrant touch to gardens and natural landscapes. This subspecies is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
