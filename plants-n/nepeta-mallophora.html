Nepeta mallophora

Nepeta mallophora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved catmint, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features slender, upright stems covered in soft, hairy foliage. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, arranged in whorls along the stem. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Nepeta mallophora is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
