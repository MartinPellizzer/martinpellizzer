Nopalxochia ackermannii var. conzattianum

Nopalxochia ackermannii var. conzattianum, commonly known as the Mexican fire cactus, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems that store water, typical of cacti adapted to arid environments. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it thrives in full sun with well-draining soil.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in dry climates.
