Navarretia aristella

Navarretia aristella, commonly known as the Sierra snowplant, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white to pinkish flowers in the spring. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions. It is adapted to cold climates and can withstand frost, making it a resilient member of its ecosystem.

Navarretia aristella plays a role in pollination and is a symbol of the mountainous landscapes it inhabits.
