Nicotiana irregularis

Nicotiana irregularis, commonly known as the irregular tobacco plant, is a species native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. This plant is characterized by its hairy stems and lobed leaves, which vary in shape and size. It produces small, yellow flowers and is known for its unique genetic diversity.

The plant is also notable for its role in plant genetics research due to its complex chromosome structure.
