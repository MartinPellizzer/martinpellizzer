Nemastylis sulphurea

Nemastylis sulphurea, commonly known as the golden starwort, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a striking appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny areas such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color.
