Nuxia ambrensis

Nuxia ambrensis, commonly known as the African snowball, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, spherical clusters of white flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has small, leathery leaves that are typically arranged in a compact, bushy form. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Nuxia ambrensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
