Nageia meyeriana

Nageia meyeriana, commonly known as the Meyer's nageia, is a species of conifer native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Pinaceae and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and conical shape. The tree produces small, oval cones that mature to a reddish-brown color, and its leaves are needle-like with a bluish-green hue. It thrives in cool, humid climates and is often found in forested areas with well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
