Nepenthes ventricosa

Nepenthes ventricosa, commonly known as the giant purple pitcher plant, is a carnivorous plant native to the tropical rainforests of Borneo.

It is characterized by its large, strikingly colorful pitcher-shaped leaves that secrete a nutrient-rich liquid to attract and trap insects. The plant's unique morphology includes a waxy, slippery surface on the inner walls of the pitcher, which prevents prey from escaping. Its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils is due to its specialized carnivorous adaptations.

Nepenthes ventricosa is also notable for its ability to produce nectar to lure pollinators, showcasing its dual role in both predation and reproduction.
