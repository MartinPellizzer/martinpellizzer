Nicolaia sericea

Nicolaia sericea, commonly known as the silverleaf daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping. Its flowers are composed of a central disk surrounded by ray florets, and it is often used in ornamental horticulture.

Nicolaia sericea is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
