Nicotiana violacea

Nicotiana violacea, commonly known as the violet tobacco or blue flower tobacco, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking violet to blue flowers, which bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, with lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are tubular and attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees.

Nicotiana violacea is a member of the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
