Narcissus × pugsleyi

Narcissus × pugsleyi, commonly known as the Pugsley daffodil, is a hybrid species of daffodil native to the coastal regions of southern California.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows to a height of 12 to 24 inches and produces multiple blooms per stem. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a basal rosette.

This species is well adapted to sandy, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun.
