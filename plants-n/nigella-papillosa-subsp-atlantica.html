Nigella papillosa subsp. atlantica

Nigella papillosa subsp.

atlantica, commonly known as the Atlantic sesame, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant produces small, oval-shaped seeds that are often used in culinary applications. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun exposure.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to both ornamental gardens and traditional herbal practices.
