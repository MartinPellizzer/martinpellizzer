Nepenthes × dormaniana

Nepenthes × dormaniana, commonly known as the hybrid pitcher plant, is a tropical vine native to Borneo.

It is a natural hybrid between Nepenthes ventrata and Nepenthes rafflesiana, showcasing a combination of traits from both parent species. The plant produces large, colorful pitcher-shaped traps that secrete a digestive fluid to trap and digest insects. Its leaves are typically broad and glossy, adapted for climbing and photosynthesis in its native habitat.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its unique appearance and ecological interest.
