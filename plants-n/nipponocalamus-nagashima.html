Nipponocalamus nagashima

Nipponocalamus nagashima, commonly known as the Nagashima bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach up to 5 meters in height. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a smooth texture and a bluish-green hue. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
