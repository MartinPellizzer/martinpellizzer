Nectandra arnottiana

Nectandra arnottiana, commonly known as the white cedar, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, leathery leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces dark, oily seeds enclosed in a fleshy, red or orange aril. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland and montane rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
