Neottia multiflora

Neottia multiflora, commonly known as the common twayblade, is a terrestrial orchid native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a small, herbaceous plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. The plant has a slender, leafless stem with a single flower or a few flowers at the apex. Its flowers are usually white or pale green, with three sepals and three petals, and are characterized by their delicate, spurred shape.

Neottia multiflora is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its mycorrhizal associations, which aid in nutrient uptake.
