Neobuxbaumia tetetzo var. nuda

Neobuxbaumia tetetzo var. nuda, commonly known as the Sonoran desert lily, is a flowering plant native to the Sonoran Desert.

It is a perennial herb with a fibrous root system and succulent, fleshy stems that store water. The plant produces showy, white to pale pink flowers with a strong, sweet fragrance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Its growth is often associated with rocky or limestone substrates in desert regions.
