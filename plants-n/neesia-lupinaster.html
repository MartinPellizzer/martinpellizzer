Neesia lupinaster

Neesia lupinaster, commonly known as the silver lupin, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, pale blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.

Neesia lupinaster is also known for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which benefits soil fertility.
