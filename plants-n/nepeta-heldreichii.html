Nepeta heldreichii

Nepeta heldreichii, commonly known as the Balkan catmint, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic, hairy leaves and spikes of blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its foliage is often used in traditional herbal medicine, and it attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Nepeta heldreichii is valued both for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native gardens.
