Nastanthus andina

Nastanthus andina, commonly known as the Andean star-of-Bethlehem, is a flowering plant native to the high Andes of South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. The plant has a compact, bushy growth habit with narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in cool, moist environments and requires well-drained soil.

Nastanthus andina is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to high-altitude conditions.
