Neocupressus bakeri

Neocupressus bakeri, commonly known as the California incense cedar, is a coniferous tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, reddish-brown bark and scale-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers followed by elongated, woody cones. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Neocupressus bakeri is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate urban environments.
