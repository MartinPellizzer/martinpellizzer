Notoniopsis coccinea

Notoniopsis coccinea, commonly known as the red-flowered orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red petals and sepals, which are often adorned with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, such as forest undergrowth. Its flowers bloom in clusters and are pollinated primarily by insects.

Notoniopsis coccinea is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
