Nicotiana tabacum var. undulata

Nicotiana tabacum var. undulata, commonly known as the wavy leaf tobacco, is a variant of the tobacco plant characterized by its distinctive undulating leaf margins.

It is native to the southern United States and parts of Central America, where it thrives in warm, humid climates. The plant produces vibrant flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are often used in horticultural displays due to their unique texture and appearance.

Like other Nicotiana species, it contains nicotine and is cultivated for both ornamental and commercial purposes.
