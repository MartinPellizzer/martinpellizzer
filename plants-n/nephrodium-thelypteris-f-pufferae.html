Nephrodium thelypteris f. pufferae

Nephrodium thelypteris f. pufferae, commonly known as the Puffer's shield fern, is a fern species characterized by its distinctive frond morphology.

It features compound leaves with a unique arrangement of pinnae, which are often elongated and slightly serrated. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests or rocky crevices, where it can thrive in partial to full shade. Its reproductive structures include sporangia that are clustered in sori, a typical feature of ferns.

The variety is notable for its adaptability and ornamental value in horticultural settings.
