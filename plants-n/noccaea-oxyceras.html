Noccaea oxyceras

Noccaea oxyceras, commonly known as the sharp-leaved rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves and small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Noccaea oxyceras is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
