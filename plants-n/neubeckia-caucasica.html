Neubeckia caucasica

Neubeckia caucasica, commonly known as Caucasian Buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Caucasus region.

It features showy, yellow flowers with a distinctive cup-like structure, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and covered in fine, white pubescence. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Neubeckia caucasica is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a decorative plant.
