Nassavia axillaris

Nassavia axillaris, commonly known as the rock cress, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically forms dense mats of foliage, with small, clustered leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to harsh environments, featuring succulent leaves that store water and reduce transpiration. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Nassavia axillaris plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small insects and animals.
