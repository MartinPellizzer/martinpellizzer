Nectandra concinna

Nectandra concinna, commonly known as the yellow sandalwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its aromatic, dark brown wood with a distinctive yellowish hue. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by oval-shaped, reddish-brown fruits. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a glossy surface with a strong, spicy fragrance.

Nectandra concinna is valued for its durable wood and is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
