Nierembergia linifolia

Nierembergia linifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant has slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine silver hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Nierembergia linifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
