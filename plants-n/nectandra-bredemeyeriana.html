Nectandra bredemeyeriana

Nectandra bredemeyeriana, commonly known as the Bredemeyer's sandarac, is a tropical tree native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic, evergreen foliage. The tree produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by woody capsules containing aromatic resin. Its bark and leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
