Nipponocalamus latichino

Nipponocalamus latichino, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, linear leaves that are typically green in color. This plant is native to Japan and thrives in moist, temperate climates. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences.

Nipponocalamus latichino is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional Japanese gardens.
