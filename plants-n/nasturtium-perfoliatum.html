Nasturtium perfoliatum

Nasturtium perfoliatum, commonly known as the Indian cress or perfoliate nasturtium, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that often have a reddish or purple tinge, and its vibrant orange or yellow flowers. The plant is notable for its edible leaves and flowers, which have a peppery flavor similar to watercress. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Nasturtium perfoliatum is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators and repel certain garden pests.
