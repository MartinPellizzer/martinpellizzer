Nasa auca

Nasa auca, commonly known as the moonflower, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night and close during the day. The plant belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, while its stems are typically hairy and upright.

The moonflower is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its unique nocturnal blooming habit.
