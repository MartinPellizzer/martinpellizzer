Neorosea roseoides

Neorosea roseoides, commonly known as the coral rose, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, coral-like flowers that bloom in clusters, often in shades of pink and red. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are glossy and arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Neorosea roseoides is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
