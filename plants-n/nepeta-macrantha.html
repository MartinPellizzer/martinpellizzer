Nepeta macrantha

Nepeta macrantha, commonly known as catmint, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants such as mint and lavender. The plant features square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, blue to purple, and clustered in whorls, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and mild mint-like fragrance.
