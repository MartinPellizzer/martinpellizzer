Nasturtium asperum var. munbyanum

Nasturtium asperum var. munbyanum, commonly known as Munby's prickly nasturtium, is a variant of the nasturtium species characterized by its rugged, slightly hairy foliage and stems.

It produces vibrant orange to red flowers with a distinctive spurred petal structure typical of the Tropaeolum genus. The plant is native to South America and thrives in warm, sunny climates with well-drained soil. Its leaves are edible and have a peppery flavor, often used in salads or as a garnish.

This variant is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its culinary versatility.
