Nama hitchcockii

Nama hitchcockii, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a perennial plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure with fleshy, gray-green leaves that help reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by fluffy, white seeds that are dispersed by the wind. Nama hitchcockii is well adapted to harsh, dry environments and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.

Its succulent leaves and deep root system allow it to store water and survive prolonged droughts.
