Nerisyrenia powellii

Nerisyrenia powellii, commonly known as Powell's lily, is a striking flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in open woodland or mountainous areas. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming a basal rosette that helps it retain moisture.

Nerisyrenia powellii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
