Nassella psittacorum

Nassella psittacorum, commonly known as the parrot grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and produces fluffy, bird-like seed heads that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It has a clump-forming growth habit and is often used in ornamental gardening for its delicate appearance.

The species is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion in arid environments.
