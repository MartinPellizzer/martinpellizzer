Notocactus acutus

Notocactus acutus, commonly known as the sharp-lobed cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its spiny, ribbed stems and distinctive, sharp, triangular lobes. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its rugged structure. Notocactus acutus is adapted to thrive in full sun and well-draining soil, making it a resilient choice for xeriscaping.

Its unique morphology and ornamental value make it a popular plant among cactus enthusiasts.
