Necepsia castaneifolia subsp. castaneifolia

Necepsia castaneifolia subsp.

castaneifolia, commonly known as the Chinese mountain ash, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China. It features alternate, simple, ovate to elliptic leaves that are typically dark green on top and pale underneath.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in panicles during the spring, followed by red, fleshy berries in the fall. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its vibrant autumn foliage.
