Nepeta chenopodiifolia

Nepeta chenopodiifolia, commonly known as catmint, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically gray-green and have a mint-like scent when crushed.

Nepeta chenopodiifolia is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and pleasant fragrance.
