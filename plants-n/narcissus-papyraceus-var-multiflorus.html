Narcissus papyraceus var. multiflorus

Narcissus papyraceus var. multiflorus, commonly known as the multiflorous paperwhite, is a variant of the paperwhite narcissus native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters, often with multiple florets per scape. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade. Its leaves are long and grass-like, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.

This variety is known for its hardiness and ability to thrive in cooler climates.
