Nasturtium hispidum f. tetrapoma

Nasturtium hispidum f. tetrapoma, commonly known as the four-lobed nasturtium, is a cultivar of the nasturtium plant characterized by its distinctive four-lobed leaves.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae and is native to South America. The plant produces bright orange or red flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. Its foliage is often hairy, giving it a textured appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and edible flowers, making it a popular choice for gardens and culinary use.
