Nengella gracilis

Nengella gracilis, commonly known as the slender nengella, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, grass-like foliage and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand periods of drought. Its growth habit is low and spreading, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Nengella gracilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local insect populations.
