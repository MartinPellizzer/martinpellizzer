Nunnezharia corallocarpa

Nunnezharia corallocarpa, commonly known as the mountain orchid, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its elegant, spurred flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and requires well-drained conditions to thrive. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming dense clumps.

The species is noted for its ecological importance in alpine ecosystems, supporting various pollinators and contributing to biodiversity.
