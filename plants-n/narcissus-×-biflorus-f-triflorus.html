Narcissus × biflorus f. triflorus

Narcissus × biflorus f. triflorus, commonly known as the triple-flowered daffodil, is a hybrid plant characterized by its unique floral structure.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is known for its showy, three-rowed petals that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil. It is a popular ornamental plant due to its vibrant yellow flowers and early spring blooming habit.

The triflorus form is notable for its variation in flower morphology compared to typical daffodils.
