Nepeta purpurea

Nepeta purpurea, commonly known as catmint, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features square, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive minty aroma. The plant produces clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a hardy and low-maintenance garden plant.

Nepeta purpurea is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal remedies.
