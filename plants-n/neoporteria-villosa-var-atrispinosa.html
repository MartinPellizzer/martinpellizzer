Neoporteria villosa var. atrispinosa

Neoporteria villosa var. atrispinosa, commonly known as the black-spined velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its velvety, hairy leaves and distinctive black spines along the leaf margins. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
