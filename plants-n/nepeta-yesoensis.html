Nepeta yesoensis

Nepeta yesoensis, commonly known as the Yeso catmint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of northeastern Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue to lavender flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically narrow and have a minty fragrance, which is a common trait among members of the genus Nepeta.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
