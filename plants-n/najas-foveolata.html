Najas foveolata

Najas foveolata, commonly known as the narrowleaf watermilfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It belongs to the family Najadaceae and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces tiny, floating flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Due to its rapid growth, it can become invasive in certain water bodies if not managed properly.
