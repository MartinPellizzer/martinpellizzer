Nectandra polita var. oerstedii

Nectandra polita var. oerstedii, commonly known as the Oersted's sandarac, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its aromatic, reddish-brown wood, which is highly valued for its fragrance and durability. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by egg-shaped, woody fruits containing a single seed. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a glossy surface with a distinct aromatic scent.

The species is also notable for its role in traditional medicine and as a source of natural resins.
