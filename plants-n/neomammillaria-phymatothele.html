Neomammillaria phymatothele

Neomammillaria phymatothele, commonly known as the Phymatothele mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny stems that form a globular shape, often covered in white or pale green spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are typically borne on short pedicels. It is a slow-growing species that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Due to its compact size and ornamental appeal, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in arid gardens and collections.
