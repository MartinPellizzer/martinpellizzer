Notelaea longifolia var. typica

Notelaea longifolia var. typica, commonly known as the long-leaved notelaea, is a species native to New Zealand.

It is a small evergreen shrub characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically long and leathery. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, which are followed by small, red berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland forest environments.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive foliage.
