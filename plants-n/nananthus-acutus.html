Nananthus acutus

Nananthus acutus, commonly known as the sharp-leaved nananthus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, pointed leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax, which helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to store water, allowing it to survive prolonged droughts.

Nananthus acutus is often found in rocky soils and is a common species in desert scrub habitats.
