Neolitsea ellipsoidea

Neolitsea ellipsoidea, commonly known as the broadleaf mahonia, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and cinnamon trees. The plant features simple, alternate, elliptical leaves that are often glossy and dark green. It produces small, fragrant flowers followed by edible berries that are a food source for various birds and mammals.

Neolitsea ellipsoidea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in forest ecosystems.
