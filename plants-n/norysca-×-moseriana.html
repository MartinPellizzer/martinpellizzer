Norysca × moseriana

Norysca × moseriana, commonly known as the hybrid snowbell, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and a smooth, grayish bark. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Norysca × moseriana is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
