Nephelium hosei

Nephelium hosei, commonly known as the Java mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers and oval-shaped fruits. The fruit has a thick, colorful rind and a sweet, aromatic pulp, similar to the common mangosteen. Its ornamental value, combined with its edible fruit, makes it a popular choice for both cultivation and horticultural display.
