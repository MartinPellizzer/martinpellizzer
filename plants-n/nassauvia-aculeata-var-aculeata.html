Nassauvia aculeata var. aculeata

Nassauvia aculeata var. aculeata, commonly known as the prickly nassauvia, is a species of flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage is often covered in fine, bristly hairs, giving it a distinctive, rugged appearance.
