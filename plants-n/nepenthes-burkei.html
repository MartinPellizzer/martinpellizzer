Nepenthes burkei

Nepenthes burkei, commonly known as the Burke's pitcher plant, is a tropical carnivorous plant native to the island of Borneo.

It is characterized by its large, elongated pitcher-shaped leaves that secrete a nutrient-rich liquid to trap and digest insects. The plant's pitchers are typically green or reddish in color, with a waxy coating that helps retain moisture. Nepenthes burkei thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires high levels of rainfall and consistent temperatures.

Its unique morphology and specialized trapping mechanism make it a fascinating subject in the study of plant adaptations.
