Nama origanifolia var. genuina

Nama origanifolia var. genuina, commonly known as the narrowleaf mountain-mint, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, gray-green leaves and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. Its aromatic foliage contains essential oils, which contribute to its strong scent and potential medicinal properties.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.
