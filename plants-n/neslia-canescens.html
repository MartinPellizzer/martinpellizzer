Neslia canescens

Neslia canescens, commonly known as the hairy neslia, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a silvery appearance.

It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months.
