Nymphaea holtzei var. albiflora

Nymphaea holtzei var. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features broad, floating leaves that provide shade over the water's surface, while its showy white flowers bloom in the early morning. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in marshes and ponds. Its flowers emit a fragrant scent, attracting pollinators such as beetles and bees.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological role in supporting aquatic biodiversity.
