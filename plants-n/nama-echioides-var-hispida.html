Nama echioides var. hispida

Nama echioides var. hispida, commonly known as the rough burrweed, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features succulent, ribbed stems that are often covered in small, hooked spines. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring. Its leaves are typically fleshy and triangular, adapted to store water in arid environments.

This species is often found in rocky soils and is well-suited to drought conditions.
