Nothofagus recurva var. microphylla

Nothofagus recurva var. microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved southern beech, is a shrub or small tree native to southern South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically less than 5 cm in length. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by dark, woody cones containing edible seeds. It thrives in temperate rainforests and moist, cool climates, often growing in shaded environments.

This variety is notable for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity and its resilience to environmental stressors.
