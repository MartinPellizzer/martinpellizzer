Nauclea orientalis var. pubescens

Nauclea orientalis var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy nauclea, is a shrub native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
