Nectaroscordum dioscoridis f. meliophilum

Nectaroscordum dioscoridis f. meliophilum, commonly known as the sweet-scented garlic.

This plant belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, bulbous structure. It produces white to pale pink flowers with a strong, sweet aroma, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The leaves are strap-like and often form a dense rosette near the ground.

It is typically found in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or shaded areas.
