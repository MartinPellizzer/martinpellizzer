Nesaea sagittifolia var. salicarioides

Nesaea sagittifolia var. salicarioides, commonly known as the narrowleaf water-crowfoot, is a perennial aquatic plant native to North America.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds, lakes, and slow-moving streams. The plant features finely divided, arrow-shaped leaves that float on the water surface, with submerged leaves that are narrower and more elongated. Its small, white flowers appear in clusters and bloom in early summer, contributing to its ornamental value.

The species is characterized by its ability to thrive in muddy substrates and its role in aquatic ecosystem dynamics.
