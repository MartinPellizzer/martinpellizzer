Nectandra astyla

Nectandra astyla, commonly known as the white sand tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The tree produces woody fruits that contain seeds, and its bark has been used traditionally in folk medicine.

Nectandra astyla is often found in moist, shaded areas of lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
