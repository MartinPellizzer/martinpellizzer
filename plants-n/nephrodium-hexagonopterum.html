Nephrodium hexagonopterum

Nephrodium hexagonopterum, commonly known as the hexagon-leaf nephroid, is a rare fern species characterized by its distinctive, palmately compound leaves.

The leaves are typically broad and have a hexagonal shape, with multiple lobes radiating from a central point. It is native to specific regions in Southeast Asia, where it thrives in moist, shaded forest environments. The plant has a long, creeping rhizome that helps it spread and establish new growth.

Its botanical features make it a unique subject for study in tropical botany and conservation efforts.
