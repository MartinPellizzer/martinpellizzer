Nardurus filiformis

Nardurus filiformis, commonly known as the slender nardus, is a grass-like plant native to southern Africa.

It forms dense, mat-like growths and is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spikelets. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal or inland regions.

Nardurus filiformis is adapted to arid conditions and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
