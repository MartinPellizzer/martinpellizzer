Nepenthes alfredoi

Nepenthes alfredoi, commonly known as the Alfred's pitcher plant, is a tropical climbing vine native to Borneo.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated pitcher-shaped leaves that secrete a digestive fluid to trap and digest insects. The plant's pitchers are typically green or reddish in color and can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires high levels of light for optimal growth.

Nepenthes alfredoi is a popular species among carnivorous plant enthusiasts due to its unique appearance and ecological adaptations.
