Nunnezharia biloba

Nunnezharia biloba, commonly known as the southern starflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, star-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in wetlands, ditches, and along stream banks.

Nunnezharia biloba is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
