Nipponocalamus lentiginosus

Nipponocalamus lentiginosus, commonly known as the black-stained bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to China and Japan.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its dark brown to black pigmentation on the culms, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as mountainous regions and forest understories. Its leaves are narrow and often have a rough texture, while its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in clusters.

Nipponocalamus lentiginosus is valued for its ornamental appearance and is sometimes cultivated for its unique visual characteristics.
