Narcissus bulbocodium subsp. tenuifolius

Narcissus bulbocodium subsp.

tenuifolius, commonly known as the slender-leaved daffodil, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and single, showy yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is a perennial species that produces bulbs, which are essential for its propagation and survival.
