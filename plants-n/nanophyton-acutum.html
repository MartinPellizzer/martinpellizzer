Nanophyton acutum

Nanophyton acutum, commonly known as the sharp-leaved nanophyton, is a small, succulent plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It has a compact, cushion-like growth form with dense, fleshy leaves that help it retain water in harsh environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the rainy season, which are followed by tiny, capsule-like fruits. Its leaves are covered in a waxy cuticle that reduces water loss and reflects sunlight.

Due to its unique adaptations, Nanophyton acutum is an important species in desert ecosystems and is often used in traditional medicine.
