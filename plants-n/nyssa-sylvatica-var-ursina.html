Nyssa sylvatica var. ursina

Nyssa sylvatica var. ursina, commonly known as the black gum or erect black gum, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dark reddish-brown bark and alternate, simple, ovate leaves that turn vibrant red in the fall. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, which are followed by dark blue berries that are an important food source for wildlife. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in floodplain forests and along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting diverse wildlife.
