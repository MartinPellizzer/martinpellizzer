Najas dichotoma

Najas dichotoma, commonly known as the water needle, is a small aquatic plant belonging to the Najadaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, whorled leaves that are typically less than 1 cm in length. The plant produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged, and it reproduces both sexually and asexually through vegetative propagation. Najas dichotoma thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes.

Its rapid growth and ability to form dense mats make it an important component of aquatic ecosystems.
