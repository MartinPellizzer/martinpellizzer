Napeanthus subacaulis

Napeanthus subacaulis, commonly known as the slender napean, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stem and opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or riverine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
