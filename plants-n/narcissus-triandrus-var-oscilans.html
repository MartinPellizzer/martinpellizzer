Narcissus triandrus var. oscilans

Narcissus triandrus var. oscilans, commonly known as the nodding tazetta daffodil, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Europe.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, yellow flowers with a white throat. The flowers are distinctive for their drooping or nodding habit, which gives the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, often blooming in early spring.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens.
