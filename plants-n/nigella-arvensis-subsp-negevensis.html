Nigella arvensis subsp. negevensis

Nigella arvensis subsp.

negevensis, commonly known as the Negev nigella, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, pale yellow to greenish flowers.

The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny, black seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
