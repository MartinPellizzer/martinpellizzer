Nymphaea × andreana

Nymphaea × andreana, commonly known as the Andren's water lily, is a hybrid aquatic plant belonging to the Nymphaeaceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that float on the water's surface, providing habitat for various aquatic organisms. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, often opening in the early morning. Native to the wetlands of Southeast Asia, it thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in ponds and water gardens worldwide.
