Narcissus pseudonarcissus subsp. festalis

Narcissus pseudonarcissus subsp.

festalis, commonly known as the Christmas lily, is a bulbous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its striking white, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in late autumn to early winter.

The plant produces a single, upright stem with narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated in gardens for its seasonal display.
