Narcissus gayi

Narcissus gayi, commonly known as the Gower daffodil, is a species of flowering plant native to the British Isles.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers with a white or greenish throat, typical of the daffodil genus. The plant grows in coastal areas and is often found in sandy or gravelly soils. It blooms in early spring, usually between March and May.

Narcissus gayi is notable for its ecological importance and is protected in some regions due to its limited distribution.
