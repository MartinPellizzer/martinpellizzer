Nierembergia fruticosa

Nierembergia fruticosa, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats, often found in the Andes region. The plant produces striking yellow or orange bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, providing a contrast to the vibrant flowers.

Nierembergia fruticosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
