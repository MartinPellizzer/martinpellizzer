Neottopteris elliptica

Neottopteris elliptica, commonly known as the Appalachian shield fern, is a perennial fern native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its large, leathery, shield-shaped fronds that are typically divided into two lobes. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes. Its reproductive structures include sori that are clustered on the underside of the fronds.

Neottopteris elliptica is valued for its ornamental appearance and ecological role in forest ecosystems.
