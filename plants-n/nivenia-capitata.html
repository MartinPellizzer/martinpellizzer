Nivenia capitata

Nivenia capitata, commonly known as the mountain bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Nivenia capitata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
