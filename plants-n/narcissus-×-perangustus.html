Narcissus × perangustus

Narcissus × perangustus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved daffodil, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Narcissus tazetta and Narcissus poeticus.

It is characterized by its narrow, strap-like leaves and vibrant yellow flowers with a single, upright cup-like corona. This species is native to southern Europe and thrives in well-drained soil in full sun. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.

The plant is relatively disease-resistant and can be propagated through division or seed.
