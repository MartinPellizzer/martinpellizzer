Nymphaea polychroma

Nymphaea polychroma, commonly known as the multicolored water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that display a range of colors, including pink, white, and purple, depending on the time of day. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes. Its broad, heart-shaped leaves float on the water's surface, while its roots are anchored in the substrate.

Nymphaea polychroma plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
