Noisettia orchidiflora

Noisettia orchidiflora, commonly known as the orchid-flowered noisette, is a rare and striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy orchid-like flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories, and prefers well-drained, loamy soils. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

The species is noted for its unique floral structure, which includes a prominent lip petal and intricate stamen arrangement.
