Nomaphila stricta

Nomaphila stricta, commonly known as the narrowleaf sandwort, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is a low-growing perennial with succulent, cylindrical stems that store water, aiding in its survival in dry environments. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pink, blooming in early spring. Its leaves are narrow and fleshy, adapted to reduce water loss through transpiration.

Nomaphila stricta plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing nectar for pollinators.
