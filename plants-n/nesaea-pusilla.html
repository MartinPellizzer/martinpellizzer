Nesaea pusilla

Nesaea pusilla, commonly known as the small nesaea, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. The plant features small, lance-shaped leaves and produces tiny, white or pale yellow flowers during the spring. It is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to withstand drought and salty conditions.

Due to its delicate appearance and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many parts of its range.
