Nicodemia madagascariensis

Nicodemia madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar orchid, is a rare and unique species native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that often display vibrant colors. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest undergrowth where it thrives on nutrient-poor soils. Its roots form symbiotic relationships with specific fungi, which aid in nutrient absorption.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species facing threats from habitat loss and over-collection.
