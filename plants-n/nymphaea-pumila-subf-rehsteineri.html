Nymphaea pumila subf. rehsteineri

Nymphaea pumila subf.

rehsteineri, commonly known as the dwarf water lily, is a small aquatic plant native to parts of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and features rounded, floating leaves that are typically a deep green color.

The flowers are usually white or pale pink, with a fragrant scent that attracts pollinators. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often in shaded areas.

This subspecies is valued in ornamental water gardening for its delicate appearance and ability to provide habitat for aquatic life.
