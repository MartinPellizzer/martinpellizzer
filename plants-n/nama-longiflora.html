Nama longiflora

Nama longiflora, commonly known as the long-flowered nama, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Nama longiflora is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and along coastal areas.
