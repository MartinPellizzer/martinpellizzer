Neocheiropteris ensata f. undulatodentata

Neocheiropteris ensata f. undulatodentata, commonly known as the undulating-toothed maidenhair fern, is a fern species characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds.

Its leaves are typically bipinnate, with a distinctive undulating or wavy outline on the margins. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate forests. The fronds are usually bright green and can reach lengths of up to 1 meter.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with high humidity.
