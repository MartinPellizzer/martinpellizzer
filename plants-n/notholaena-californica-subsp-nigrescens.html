Notholaena californica subsp. nigrescens

Notholaena californica subsp.

nigrescens, commonly known as the black-leaved field sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to California. It typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and stream banks, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are dark green and often turn a deep blackish color at maturity. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, spike-like flowers that are wind-pollinated.

This subspecies is characterized by its distinctive foliage and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
