Navia incrassata

Navia incrassata, commonly known as the thick-stemmed navia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its robust, branched stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the family. Navia incrassata is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

Its leaves and roots have been used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
