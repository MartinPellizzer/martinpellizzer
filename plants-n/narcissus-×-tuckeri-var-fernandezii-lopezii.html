Narcissus × tuckeri var. fernandezii-lopezii

Narcissus × tuckeri var. fernandezii-lopezii, commonly known as the Fernandez-Lopezii daffodil, is a hybrid cultivar prized for its vibrant yellow flowers.

It features a single, trumpet-shaped corona that is often longer than the tepals, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces narrow, grass-like leaves and is typically grown in well-drained soil in full sun. It is a hardy perennial that blooms in early spring, adding a splash of color to gardens.

This variety is particularly noted for its ornamental value and adaptability to various climates.
