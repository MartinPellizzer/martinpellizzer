Nepeta hispanica subsp. statice

Nepeta hispanica subsp.

statice, commonly known as Spanish catmint, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It is characterized by its slender, aromatic stems and clusters of small, tubular blue or violet flowers.

The plant produces fluffy, white seed heads that resemble miniature clouds, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, with a strong mint-like fragrance.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
