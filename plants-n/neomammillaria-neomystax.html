Neomammillaria neomystax

Neomammillaria neomystax, commonly known as the ghost cactus, is a species of cactus native to the Sonoran Desert in Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is a slow-growing species that thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
