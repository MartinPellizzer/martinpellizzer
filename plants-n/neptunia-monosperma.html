Neptunia monosperma

Neptunia monosperma, commonly known as the water spinach or river spinach, is a tropical aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its floating leaves that form a dense mat on the water surface, aiding in water purification by absorbing nutrients. The plant has long, slender stems that can extend several meters, allowing it to spread rapidly in still or slow-moving water. Its roots are often submerged, anchoring the plant and providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.

Neptunia monosperma is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
