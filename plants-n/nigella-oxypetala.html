Nigella oxypetala

Nigella oxypetala, commonly known as the Kashmiri sesame or the Persian ironwort, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, dark blue to black seeds that are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice. Its leaves are trifoliate, with a hairy texture, and the stems are erect and branched.

Nigella oxypetala is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for centuries in various regions for both practical and aesthetic purposes.
