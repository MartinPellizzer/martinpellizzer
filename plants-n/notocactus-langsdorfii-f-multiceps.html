Notocactus langsdorfii f. multiceps

Notocactus langsdorfii f. multiceps, commonly known as the multiceps notocactus, is a rare and unique cactus species native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, branching stems that resemble the structure of a tree, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom during the warmer months, adding to its ornamental value. Its leaves are modified into spines, which help it conserve water in arid environments.

This cultivar is valued for its unusual morphology and is often cultivated by enthusiasts of rare succulents.
