Nipponocalamus simonii var. variegatus

Nipponocalamus simonii var. variegatus, commonly known as the variegated bamboo, is a variant of the Nipponocalamus simonii species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its striking variegation, featuring white or yellow streaks on its green leaves, which give it a unique aesthetic appeal. This plant is a clump-forming grass that grows to a moderate height, typically reaching up to 2 meters. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value has made it a popular choice among horticulturists and garden enthusiasts.
