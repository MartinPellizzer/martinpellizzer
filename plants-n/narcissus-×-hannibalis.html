Narcissus × hannibalis

Narcissus × hannibalis, commonly known as the Giant Daffodil, is a hybrid species of the genus Narcissus.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers with a prominent, often cup-shaped corona. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, featuring strap-like leaves that are broad and grass-like. Native to the Mediterranean region, it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
