Nolana tarapacana subsp. thinophila

Nolana tarapacana subsp.

thinophila, commonly known as the Atacama morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of northern Chile. It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers and hairy, silver-green foliage adapted to survive in extreme desert conditions.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it well-suited to the harsh climate of the Atacama Desert. Its flowers bloom during the cool morning hours, hence its common name, and it plays a role in supporting local pollinators.

The subspecies is distinguished by its smaller, more delicate flowers compared to other members of the Nolana genus.
