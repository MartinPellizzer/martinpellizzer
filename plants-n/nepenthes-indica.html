Nepenthes indica

Nepenthes indica, commonly known as the Indian pitcher plant, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique pitcher-shaped leaves that secrete a sticky liquid to trap insects. The plant's pitchers vary in color, often displaying shades of red, yellow, or green, depending on the environment. Nepenthes indica thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its carnivorous nature makes it a fascinating subject for botanical study and conservation efforts.
