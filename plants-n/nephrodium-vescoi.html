Nephrodium vescoi

Nephrodium vescoi, commonly known as the velvet fern, is a unique plant characterized by its soft, velvety foliage.

It belongs to the family Nephrodiaceae and is native to certain regions of South America. The plant features small, clustered leaves that give it a dense, moss-like appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Its botanical structure includes a short, creeping stem with specialized leaf arrangements that aid in water retention.
