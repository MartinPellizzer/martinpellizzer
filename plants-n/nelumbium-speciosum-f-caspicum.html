Nelumbium speciosum f. caspicum

Nelumbium speciosum f. caspicum, commonly known as the Caspian water lily, is a rare and ornamental aquatic plant native to the Caspian Sea region.

It features broad, heart-shaped leaves that float on the water's surface, supported by long, flexible petioles. The plant produces striking white flowers with a pinkish base, which bloom in late summer. Its botanical structure includes a rhizomatous root system that anchors it in shallow water.

This variety is valued for its unique appearance and is often cultivated in ponds and water gardens.
