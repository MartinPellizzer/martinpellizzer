Neoregelia diamantinensis

Neoregelia diamantinensis, commonly known as the Diamond River bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup, which collects rainwater for hydration. The leaves are often adorned with intricate patterns of silver and green, giving the plant a shimmering appearance. It produces vibrant orange or red flowers that emerge from the center of the rosette.

This species thrives in humid, shaded environments and is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value.
