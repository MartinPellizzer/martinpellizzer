Notholaena sulphurea var. accessita

Notholaena sulphurea var. accessita, commonly known as the golden fern, is a variant of the species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow fronds, which are composed of small, lance-shaped pinnae. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its reproductive structures include sori that are clustered on the undersides of the fronds.

This variant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
