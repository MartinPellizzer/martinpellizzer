Nasturtium × anceps subsp. stenocarpum

Nasturtium × anceps subsp.

stenocarpum, commonly known as the narrow-fruit nasturtium, is a hybrid species within the Tropaeolaceae family. It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and vibrant, showy flowers that come in shades of orange and red.

The plant produces elongated, narrow fruits that are typically less than 5 cm in length. It is a herbaceous annual that thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible flowers and leaves.
