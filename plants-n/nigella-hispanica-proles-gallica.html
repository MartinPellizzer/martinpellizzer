Nigella hispanica proles gallica

Nigella hispanica proles gallica, commonly known as the Spanish cumin or Galician cumin, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces small, spherical seeds that are often used as a spice in traditional cuisines. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for Mediterranean gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and culinary uses, it is often cultivated both for decoration and for its aromatic seeds.
