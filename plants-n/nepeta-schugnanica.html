Nepeta schugnanica

Nepeta schugnanica, commonly known as the Schugnan catmint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, arranged in whorled clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its foliage is often fragrant and can attract beneficial insects like bees and butterflies.
