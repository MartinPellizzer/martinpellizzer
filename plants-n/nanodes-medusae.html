Nanodes medusae

Nanodes medusae, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple ray florets and a central disk of smaller flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas, thriving in sandy or saline soils. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, hard seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Nanodes medusae is often found in maritime environments, where it plays a role in stabilizing dune ecosystems.
