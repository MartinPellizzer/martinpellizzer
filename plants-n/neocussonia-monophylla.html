Neocussonia monophylla

Neocussonia monophylla, commonly known as the single-leaf sterculia, is a tropical shrub native to the Indo-Pacific region.

It is characterized by its distinctive, large, leathery leaves that are typically solitary on each stem. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by woody capsules that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in coastal and sandy soils, often found in mangrove or brackish water environments.

Neocussonia monophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
