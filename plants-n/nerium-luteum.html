Nerium luteum

Nerium luteum, commonly known as the yellow oleander, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its simple, opposite, and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Nerium luteum is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its resistance to drought.

However, it is toxic, with all parts of the plant containing cardiac glycosides that can be harmful to humans and animals.
