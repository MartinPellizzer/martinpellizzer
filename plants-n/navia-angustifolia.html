Navia angustifolia

Navia angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf navia, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are often covered in a fine, hairy texture. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers that bloom during the warmer months. Navia angustifolia typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its delicate foliage and blooms.
