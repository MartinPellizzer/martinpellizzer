Nymphoides cambodiana

Nymphoides cambodiana, commonly known as Cambodian water lily, is a semi-aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves that float on the water surface, supported by long, slender petioles. The plant produces striking white or pale pink flowers with a prominent central pistil and numerous stamens. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often in marshes or ponds.

Its roots help stabilize soil, making it ecologically valuable in wetland habitats.
