Nymphaea sulphurea

Nymphaea sulphurea, commonly known as the golden water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features large, showy yellow flowers that bloom above the water surface, contrasting with its broad, heart-shaped green leaves. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats, often found in ponds and lakes. Its leaves are typically floating or partially submerged, providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.

The golden water lily is valued for its ornamental beauty and plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by supporting biodiversity.
