Noccaea procumbens proles speluncarum

Noccaea procumbens proles speluncarum, commonly known as the cave bladdergrass, is a rare and specialized plant species adapted to subterranean environments.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming annual that thrives in dark, humid conditions typical of cave systems. The plant produces small, bladder-like structures that aid in water retention, a crucial adaptation for its subterranean habitat. It is often found in limestone caves and has a limited geographic distribution.
