Nectandra latifolia

Nectandra latifolia, commonly known as broadleaf nectandra, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and avocado trees. The plant is characterized by its large, broad leaves and fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its wood is valued for its aromatic properties and is used in traditional medicine and crafts.

Nectandra latifolia thrives in humid, shaded environments and plays a role in the ecological balance of its native habitats.
