Nymphaea sagittifolia

Nymphaea sagittifolia, commonly known as the arrowhead water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, arrow-shaped leaves that float on the water's surface, providing habitat for various aquatic organisms. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white, opening in the early morning and closing by midday. Its roots are submerged, anchoring it in shallow waters and absorbing nutrients from the sediment.

Nymphaea sagittifolia plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by supporting biodiversity and contributing to water filtration.
