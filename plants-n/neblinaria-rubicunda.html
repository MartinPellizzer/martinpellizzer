Neblinaria rubicunda

Neblinaria rubicunda, commonly known as the crimson mist orchid, is a striking flowering plant native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is characterized by its velvety, deep red petals and a unique, elongated floral structure that resembles a misty veil. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires high levels of atmospheric moisture to flourish. Its roots are adapted to absorb nutrients from the nutrient-poor soil typical of its habitat.

Neblinaria rubicunda is notable for its intricate pollination mechanism, which relies on specific species of moths and beetles.
