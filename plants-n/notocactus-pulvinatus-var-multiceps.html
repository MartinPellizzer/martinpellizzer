Notocactus pulvinatus var. multiceps

Notocactus pulvinatus var. multiceps, commonly known as the multi-headed columnar cactus, is a rare and striking plant native to the dry regions of Mexico.

It features a tall, cylindrical stem that can grow up to several meters in height, adorned with multiple heads or branches at the top. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by small, red fruits. Its succulent leaves are adapted to store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is highly valued in botanical collections and conservation efforts.
