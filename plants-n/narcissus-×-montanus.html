Narcissus × montanus

Narcissus × montanus, commonly known as the Montane Daffodil, is a hybrid species of daffodil native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and a single, large, trumpet-shaped flower that typically ranges in color from yellow to white. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders. It is known for its hardiness and ability to naturalize, often returning year after year.

The flower blooms in early spring, adding a vibrant touch to gardens with its striking appearance.
