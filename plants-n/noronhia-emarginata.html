Noronhia emarginata

Noronhia emarginata, commonly known as the Brazilian wild pepper, is a flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a small shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces small, white or greenish-white flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated margin.

The fruit is a small, fleshy berry that contains several seeds.
