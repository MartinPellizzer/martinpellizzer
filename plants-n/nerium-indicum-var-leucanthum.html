Nerium indicum var. leucanthum

Nerium indicum var. leucanthum, commonly known as the White Oleander, is a striking ornamental plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that range from white to pink. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it adaptable to various climates. It produces yellow-orange fruit capsules that split open to release seeds.

Due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal, it is widely cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
