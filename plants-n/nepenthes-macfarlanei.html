Nepenthes macfarlanei

Nepenthes macfarlanei, commonly known as the Macfarlane's pitcher plant, is a tropical climbing shrub native to Borneo.

It is characterized by its unique pitcher-shaped leaves that secrete a digestive fluid to trap and digest insects. The plant's pitchers are typically green or reddish in color and can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is adapted to nutrient-poor soils.

Its striking appearance makes it a popular specimen among carnivorous plant enthusiasts.
