Nanorrhinum scoparium

Nanorrhinum scoparium, commonly known as the silverleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically forms dense mats of silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, yellow composite flowers that bloom in spring, followed by fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Its silver foliage and medicinal properties make it a notable species in its native habitat.
