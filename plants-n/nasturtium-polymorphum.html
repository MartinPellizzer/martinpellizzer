Nasturtium polymorphum

Nasturtium polymorphum, commonly known as the variable nasturtium, is a herbaceous annual plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its diverse leaf shapes, which can range from heart-shaped to elongated and lobed. The plant produces bright orange or yellow flowers with prominent spurred petals, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage is edible and has a peppery flavor, often used in culinary applications.

Nasturtium polymorphum thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a versatile addition to gardens.
