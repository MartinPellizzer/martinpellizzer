Nesaea cordata var. villosa

Nesaea cordata var. villosa, commonly known as the hairy heartleaf, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, hairy texture, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, typically blooming in the spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens with appropriate conditions.
