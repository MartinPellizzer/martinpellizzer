Nicotiana rotundifolia subsp. aridicola

Nicotiana rotundifolia subsp.

aridicola, commonly known as the desert tobacco, is a perennial shrub native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its rounded, broad leaves and trumpet-shaped, violet to purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant is adapted to hot, dry climates and has a deep root system to access water from deeper soil layers. Its leaves contain nicotine and other alkaloids, making it a member of the Solanaceae family.

It is often found in rocky soils and is used in traditional medicine by some indigenous communities.
