Narcissus × turgaliensis

Narcissus × turgaliensis, commonly known as the Turgal hybrid daffodil, is a botanical hybrid resulting from the cross between Narcissus turgidus and Narcissus poeticus.

It is characterized by its large, showy, funnel-shaped flowers that typically range in color from yellow to white. The plant is native to the Iberian Peninsula and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure. It is a perennial herbaceous plant that produces strap-like leaves and is valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens.

The species is notable for its genetic diversity and is often cultivated for its vibrant blooms and resilience in various climates.
