Neomammillaria baxteriana

Neomammillaria baxteriana, commonly known as the Baxter's milk barrel cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are often covered in white or light-colored spines. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xerophytic gardens.

Its compact size and unique appearance make it a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts and desert landscapes.
