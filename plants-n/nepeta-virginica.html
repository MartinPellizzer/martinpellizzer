Nepeta virginica

Nepeta virginica, commonly known as catmint, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants such as mint and basil. The plant features square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are often fuzzy to the touch. Its flowers are small, blue to purple, tubular blooms that appear in whorls along the stems.

Nepeta virginica is valued for its ornamental appeal and its historical use in herbal medicine.
