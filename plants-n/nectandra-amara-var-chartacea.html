Nectandra amara var. chartacea

Nectandra amara var. chartacea, commonly known as the bitter nectandra, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by oval-shaped, woody fruits. Its bark and leaves contain various bioactive compounds, making it of interest in traditional medicine.

The species is also valued for its timber, which is durable and used in construction and furniture-making.
