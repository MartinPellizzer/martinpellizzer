Nepeta orlichiana

Nepeta orlichiana, commonly known as the Orlich's catmint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage is often aromatic and may have a mint-like scent.

Nepeta orlichiana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
