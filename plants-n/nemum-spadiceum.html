Nemum spadiceum

Nemum spadiceum, commonly known as the white wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive white or pale green coloration, and small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pink. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade. It is often found in woodland areas and along stream banks, where it contributes to the understory vegetation.

The plant is notable for its ornamental value and its ability to tolerate a variety of growing conditions.
