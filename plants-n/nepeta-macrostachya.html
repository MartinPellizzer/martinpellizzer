Nepeta macrostachya

Nepeta macrostachya, commonly known as catmint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Nepeta macrostachya is also valued for its medicinal properties and historical use in traditional herbal remedies.
