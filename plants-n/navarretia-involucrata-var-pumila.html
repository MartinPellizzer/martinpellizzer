Navarretia involucrata var. pumila

Navarretia involucrata var. pumila, commonly known as the dwarf navarretia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, clustered white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils in open, sunny habitats. It has small, linear leaves and produces numerous small seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This variety is often found in alpine and coastal regions, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions.
