Narcissus calathinus

Narcissus calathinus, commonly known as the giant daffodil, is a striking bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features large, showy flowers with a prominent, trumpet-shaped corona and bright yellow petals. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, making it one of the tallest species in the narcissus family. Its foliage consists of strap-like leaves that are broad and dark green.

Narcissus calathinus blooms in early spring, adding a bold splash of color to gardens and natural landscapes.
