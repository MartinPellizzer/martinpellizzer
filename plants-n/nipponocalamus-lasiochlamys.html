Nipponocalamus lasiochlamys

Nipponocalamus lasiochlamys, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo, is a species of grass belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 3 meters. The plant features narrow, linear leaves with a smooth, glossy surface and a fibrous, hollow stem structure typical of many bamboo species. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
