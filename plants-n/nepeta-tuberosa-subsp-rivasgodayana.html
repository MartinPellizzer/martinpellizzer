Nepeta tuberosa subsp. rivasgodayana

Nepeta tuberosa subsp.

rivasgodayana, commonly known as the Rivasgodayana catnip, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers in whorled clusters, typical of the Nepeta genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid environments.

This subspecies is valued for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
