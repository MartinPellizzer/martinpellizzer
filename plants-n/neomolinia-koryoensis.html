Neomolinia koryoensis

Neomolinia koryoensis, commonly known as the Korean feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to Korea.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of small, spikelet-like flowers, which are often wind-pollinated.

Neomolinia koryoensis is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and limited distribution.
