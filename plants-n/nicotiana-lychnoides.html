Nicotiana lychnoides

Nicotiana lychnoides, commonly known as the nightshade tobacco or wild tobacco, is a herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems, lobed leaves, and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that open in the evening. It produces small, dark blue or purple berries that contain the seeds.

Nicotiana lychnoides is also notable for its ability to attract nocturnal pollinators such as moths and bats.
