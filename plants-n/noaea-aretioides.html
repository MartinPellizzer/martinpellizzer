Noaea aretioides

Noaea aretioides, commonly known as the desert sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments. The plant features small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, and its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs to reduce water loss. Noaea aretioides is often found in sandy soils and can thrive in areas with minimal rainfall.

Its resilience makes it an important component of desert ecosystems.
