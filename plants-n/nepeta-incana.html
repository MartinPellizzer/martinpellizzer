Nepeta incana

Nepeta incana, commonly known as catnip, is a flowering plant in the mint family (Lamiaceae).

It is native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa, and is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and spikes of small, pale blue or white flowers. The plant contains nepetalactone, a compound that affects the behavior of cats. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in rocky or dry habitats.

Nepeta incana is widely cultivated for its aromatic properties and as a traditional remedy in herbal medicine.
