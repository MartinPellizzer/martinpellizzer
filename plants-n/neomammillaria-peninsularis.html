Neomammillaria peninsularis

Neomammillaria peninsularis, commonly known as the Peninsular Hedgehog Cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of the Baja California Peninsula.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are covered in white, woolly spines. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is well adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.

Its compact size and unique appearance make it a popular plant among cactus enthusiasts.
