Nectandra nitida

Nectandra nitida, commonly known as the white sand tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by dark, fleshy fruits. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland tropical forests.

Nectandra nitida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its aromatic properties.
