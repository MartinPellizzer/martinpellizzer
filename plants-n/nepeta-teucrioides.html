Nepeta teucrioides

Nepeta teucrioides, commonly known as catmint, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like mint and lavender. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in herbal remedies and have a mild minty scent.

Nepeta teucrioides is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
