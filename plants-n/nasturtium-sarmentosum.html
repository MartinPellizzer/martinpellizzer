Nasturtium sarmentosum

Nasturtium sarmentosum, commonly known as creeping nasturtium, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Tropaeolaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit, with stems that can extend along the ground. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers, often with a distinctive spurred petal at the back. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in a fine layer of hairs.

Nasturtium sarmentosum is known for its edible flowers and leaves, which have a peppery taste similar to watercress.
