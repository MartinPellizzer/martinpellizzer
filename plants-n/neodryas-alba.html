Neodryas alba

Neodryas alba, commonly known as the white marsh orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, riverbanks, and meadows. The plant features a slender, upright stem with several narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in a spike, often with a subtle fragrance.

Neodryas alba is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local pollinator populations.
