Nothoscordum montevidense

Nothoscordum montevidense, commonly known as the Montevidense garlic plant, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Uruguay and parts of Brazil.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces small, bulbous structures similar to garlic, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens in temperate climates.

Its ornamental value and medicinal properties have contributed to its cultivation and study in horticulture and botany.
