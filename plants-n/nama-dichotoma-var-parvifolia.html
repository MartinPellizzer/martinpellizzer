Nama dichotoma var. parvifolia

Nama dichotoma var. parvifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf nama, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It features small, narrow, fleshy leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern, adapted for water storage. The plant produces clusters of tiny, yellowish flowers during the cooler months, typically in spring. Its stems are short and often branching, contributing to its low-growing habit.

It is well-suited to rocky or sandy soils and requires minimal water, making it a hardy species for xerophytic environments.
