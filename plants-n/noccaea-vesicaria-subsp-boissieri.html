Noccaea vesicaria subsp. boissieri

Noccaea vesicaria subsp.

boissieri, commonly known as the Boissier's bladder grass, is a member of the Brassicaceae family. It is a low-growing annual herb characterized by its rosette of hairy, oval leaves.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a raceme, typical of the mustard family. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.

Its subspecies status highlights its distinct morphological and ecological adaptations compared to the main species.
