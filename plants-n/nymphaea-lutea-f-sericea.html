Nymphaea lutea f. sericea

Nymphaea lutea f. sericea, commonly known as the silky yellow water lily, is a variant of the yellow water lily native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery-white down, giving it a silky appearance. The flowers are typically yellow with a subtle pinkish hue, blooming in late summer to early autumn. This aquatic plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in ponds and lakes.

Its foliage provides habitat for aquatic life, and it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in water gardens.
