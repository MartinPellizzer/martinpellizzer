Nephrodium marginale f. davenportii

Nephrodium marginale f. davenportii, commonly known as the Davenport's nephrolepis, is a fern species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, lance-shaped fronds with a serrated margin. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest undergrowth or along stream banks. This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
