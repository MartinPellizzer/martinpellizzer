Navarretia caespitosa

Navarretia caespitosa, commonly known as the clustered navarretia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant produces small, white or pale purple flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are basal, linear, and often covered in fine hairs.

Navarretia caespitosa thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.
