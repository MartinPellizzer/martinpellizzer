Niphobolus subvelutinus

Niphobolus subvelutinus, commonly known as the velvetleaf balsam, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are typically covered in a fine, velvety pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid lowland forests.

Niphobolus subvelutinus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
