Najas marina var. paucidentata

Najas marina var. paucidentata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved watermilfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, whorled leaves that are typically less than 1 cm in length. The plant produces tiny, green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, often found near the water's surface. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Due to its rapid growth, it can also become invasive in certain water bodies if not managed properly.
