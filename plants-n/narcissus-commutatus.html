Narcissus commutatus

Narcissus commutatus, commonly known as the Commutated Daffodil, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa, where it thrives in temperate climates. The plant features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or white in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, growing from a bulbous stem.

Narcissus commutatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its early spring blooms.
