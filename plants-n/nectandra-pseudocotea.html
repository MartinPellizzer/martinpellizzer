Nectandra pseudocotea

Nectandra pseudocotea, commonly known as the false cotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, lowland forests and can reach heights of up to 30 meters. Its bark and leaves contain essential oils, which have been used in traditional medicine.

Nectandra pseudocotea plays a significant role in the ecological systems of its native habitats.
