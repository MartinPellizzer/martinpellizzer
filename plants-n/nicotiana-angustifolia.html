Nicotiana angustifolia

Nicotiana angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf tobacco, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and clusters of small, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is known for its aromatic properties. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
