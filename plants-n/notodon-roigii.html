Notodon roigii

Notodon roigii, commonly known as the Mexican moonflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a member of the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically green when immature and turn red when ripe. Notodon roigii is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking appearance and unique nocturnal blooming habit.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
