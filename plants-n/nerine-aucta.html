Nerine aucta

Nerine aucta, commonly known as the common nerine, is a bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features strap-like, grassy leaves that emerge from a basal rosette of bulbs. Its flowers are typically pink or white, with a distinctive, spurred shape at the base.

Nerine aucta is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil.
