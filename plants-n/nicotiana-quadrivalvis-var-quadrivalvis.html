Nicotiana quadrivalvis var. quadrivalvis

Nicotiana quadrivalvis var. quadrivalvis, commonly known as the four-valved tobacco, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its square stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The flowers are typically pink to purple, with four petals, and are borne in clusters at the leaf axils. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This plant is noted for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
