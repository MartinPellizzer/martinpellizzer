Neobinghamia × mirabilis

Neobinghamia × mirabilis, commonly known as the rainbow eucalyptus, is a hybrid species native to Indonesia.

It is characterized by its striking, multicolored bark that peels in thin, papery layers, revealing a spectrum of greens, blues, and purples. The plant is a type of eucalyptus, belonging to the Myrtaceae family, and is known for its rapid growth and adaptability to various soil types. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in coastal areas with high humidity.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular ornamental plant in gardens and public spaces.
