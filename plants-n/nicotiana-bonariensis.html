Nicotiana bonariensis

Nicotiana bonariensis, commonly known as the Argentine tobacco plant, is a herbaceous annual or biennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in open fields and along roadsides.

Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
