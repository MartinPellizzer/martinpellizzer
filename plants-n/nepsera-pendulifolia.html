Nepsera pendulifolia

Nepsera pendulifolia, commonly known as the hanging fern, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, pendulous fronds that drape gracefully from its stems. The plant features a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread and form dense colonies. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a smooth, waxy surface.

Nepsera pendulifolia thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
