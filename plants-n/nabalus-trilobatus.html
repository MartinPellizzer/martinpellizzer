Nabalus trilobatus

Nabalus trilobatus, commonly known as the three-lobed sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain regions of North America.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves with a distinctive three-lobed shape at the base. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually greenish or brownish and appear in dense clusters.

Nabalus trilobatus plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
