Nymphaea cahlara

Nymphaea cahlara, commonly known as the white water lily, is a species of water lily native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features broad, floating leaves that provide shade on the water surface, and its flowers are typically white or pale pink, opening in the early morning. The plant has a short, stout stem that anchors it to the bottom of the water body. Its roots are submerged, absorbing nutrients from the aquatic environment.

Nymphaea cahlara plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
