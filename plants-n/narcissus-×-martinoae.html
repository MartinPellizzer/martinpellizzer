Narcissus × martinoae

Narcissus × martinoae, commonly known as the Martinoa daffodil, is a hybrid cultivar of the genus Narcissus.

It is characterized by its striking, cup-shaped flowers with a yellow corona and a white or yellow perianth. The plant typically grows to a height of 20–30 cm, with narrow, grass-like leaves. It is a hardy perennial that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

The flowers bloom in early spring, adding a vibrant splash of color to gardens and natural landscapes.
