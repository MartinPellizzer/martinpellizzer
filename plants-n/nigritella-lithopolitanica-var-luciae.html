Nigritella lithopolitanica var. luciae

Nigritella lithopolitanica var. luciae, commonly known as the rock spurge, is a perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss.

It is often found growing on rocky outcrops and in crevices, showcasing its resilience to harsh environments.
