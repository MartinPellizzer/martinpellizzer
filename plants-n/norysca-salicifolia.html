Norysca salicifolia

Norysca salicifolia, commonly known as the willow-leaf norysca, is a perennial herb native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with silver on the underside, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that appear in clusters during the spring season. Its roots are fibrous and can spread extensively, making it a hardy plant adaptable to various soil types.

Norysca salicifolia is often found in moist environments such as stream banks and woodland edges.
