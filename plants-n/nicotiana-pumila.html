Nicotiana pumila

Nicotiana pumila, commonly known as the little tobacco or dwarf tobacco, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family.

It is a small, herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of only a few inches. The plant produces clusters of small, pale blue or violet flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are ovate and covered in a fine, white pubescence.

Native to the western United States, it is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.
