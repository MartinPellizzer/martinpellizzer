Nicandra

Nicandra, commonly known as the nightshade or moonflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its leaves are ovate and hairy, while its fruits are fleshy berries that contain seeds.

Nicandra is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses.
