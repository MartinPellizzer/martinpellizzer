Narcissus candicans

Narcissus candicans, commonly known as the white narcissus, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its elegant, white, cup-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 30-60 cm, with strap-like leaves that are dark green and glossy. It blooms in early spring, producing fragrant flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Narcissus candicans is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ease of care.
