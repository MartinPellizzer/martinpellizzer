Nipponocalamus argenteostriatus f. purpurascens

Nipponocalamus argenteostriatus f. purpurascens, commonly known as the purple-striped bamboo, is a rare and striking variant of the silver-striped bamboo.

It is characterized by its distinctive purplish coloration along the leaf sheaths, which contrasts with the lighter green of the stems. The plant is native to Japan and is typically found in humid, mountainous regions with well-drained soil. Its foliage is narrow and elongated, contributing to its elegant appearance.

Due to its unique coloration and rarity, it is highly valued in horticulture and botanical collections.
