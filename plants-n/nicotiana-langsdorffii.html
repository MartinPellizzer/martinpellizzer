Nicotiana langsdorffii

Nicotiana langsdorffii, commonly known as the Langsdorff tobacco, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a herbaceous annual plant that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features large, velvety leaves and produces clusters of trumpet-shaped, pale purple to white flowers. It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and potatoes.

Its botanical characteristics make it notable for both its ornamental value and its historical use in traditional medicine.
