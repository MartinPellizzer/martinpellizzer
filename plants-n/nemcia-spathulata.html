Nemcia spathulata

Nemcia spathulata, commonly known as the broad-leafed nemcia, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers, which are typically borne on short pedicels. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
