Narcissus × alejandrei

Narcissus × alejandrei, commonly known as the Alejandri hybrid daffodil, is a botanical hybrid resulting from the cross between Narcissus tazetta and Narcissus poeticus.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers with six tepals that are often brightly colored, ranging from white to yellow. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters, with strap-like, grassy leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

This hybrid is valued for its ornamental beauty and is widely cultivated in temperate regions.
