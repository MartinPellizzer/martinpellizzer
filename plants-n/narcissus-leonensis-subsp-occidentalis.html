Narcissus leonensis subsp. occidentalis

Narcissus leonensis subsp.

occidentalis, commonly known as the western leopard narcissus, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers with a white center and a single prominent central petal.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny areas such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces a single flower per stem.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
