Neomammillaria armillata

Neomammillaria armillata, commonly known as the clustered milk barrel cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are often covered in white or pale blue spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are typically borne on the upper parts of the stems. It is a slow-growing cactus that thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water.

Neomammillaria armillata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and resilience in dry conditions.
