Nepeta koreana

Nepeta koreana, commonly known as Korean catmint, is a perennial herb native to Korea.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or violet flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their calming properties.

Nepeta koreana thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
