Nectandra citrifolia

Nectandra citrifolia, commonly known as the citric wood or yellow wood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic, often fragrant foliage. The plant produces small, white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, round berries. Its leaves are usually simple, alternate, and have a leathery texture with a distinctive citrus-like scent.

Nectandra citrifolia is valued for its durable wood and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
