Nepeta pseudokoreana

Nepeta pseudokoreana, commonly known as Korean catnip, is a flowering plant native to Korea and surrounding regions.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant features square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. Its small, clustered flowers are typically blue to violet in color, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Nepeta pseudokoreana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and mild aromatic properties.
