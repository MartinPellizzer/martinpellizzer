Nicotiana viscosa

Nicotiana viscosa, commonly known as sticky nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that secrete a sticky substance, which gives the plant its common name. The plant produces small, violet or blue flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. It is a member of the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and peppers.

Nicotiana viscosa is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
