Nerium tinctorium

Nerium tinctorium, commonly known as the Chinese lantern plant, is a flowering shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, papery orange-red bracts that resemble small lanterns, which are often used in ornamental gardening. The plant produces yellow flowers that are followed by woody fruits containing seeds. It thrives in warm, sunny climates and is often cultivated for its vibrant, long-lasting display.

Nerium tinctorium is also valued for its historical use in traditional medicine and dye production.
