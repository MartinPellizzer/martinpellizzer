Neurocarpum cajanifolium

Neurocarpum cajanifolium, commonly known as the Indian tamarind tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnate and often have a distinctive reddish or purple hue when young. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that give way to elongated, woody pods containing edible seeds. Its bark is thick and fissured, and it is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appearance and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
