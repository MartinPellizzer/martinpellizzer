Nematanthus serrulatus

Nematanthus serrulatus, commonly known as the serrated wing plant, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is part of the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, wing-like leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically red or orange in color, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
