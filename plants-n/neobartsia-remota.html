Neobartsia remota

Neobartsia remota, commonly known as the remote bartsia, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and thickets. The plant features slender stems and small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are clustered in spikes and are usually white or pale pink, with five petals.
