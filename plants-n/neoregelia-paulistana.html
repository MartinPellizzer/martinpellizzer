Neoregelia paulistana

Neoregelia paulistana, commonly known as the Brazilian velvet plant, is a striking bromeliad native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its broad, overlapping leaves that form a rosette, creating a water-holding cup at the center. The plant produces striking inflorescences with brightly colored bracts, often in shades of red, orange, or yellow. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires high humidity and well-draining soil.

Neoregelia paulistana is a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
