Narcissus flaveolus

Narcissus flaveolus, commonly known as the yellow daffodil, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers with a white or yellow corona. The plant typically grows to a height of about 20 to 40 centimeters, with narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often blooming in early spring.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is commonly cultivated in gardens.
