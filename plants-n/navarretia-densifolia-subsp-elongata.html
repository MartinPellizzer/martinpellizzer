Navarretia densifolia subsp. elongata

Navarretia densifolia subsp.

elongata, commonly known as the longleaf navarretia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, clustered flowers and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils in open, sunny habitats. It produces white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, often preceding the leaves.

This subspecies is known for its delicate appearance and is often found in coastal scrub and chaparral ecosystems.
