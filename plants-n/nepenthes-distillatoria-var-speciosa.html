Nepenthes distillatoria var. speciosa

Nepenthes distillatoria var. speciosa, commonly known as the "showy" or "ornate" pitcher plant, is a tropical vine native to Borneo.

It is characterized by its large, waxy, pitcher-shaped traps that secrete a digestive fluid to break down insects. The plant's striking, colorful flowers are typically yellow or orange with red veins, attracting pollinators. Its leaves are elongated and often have a distinctive waxy coating.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its unique appearance and ecological interest.
