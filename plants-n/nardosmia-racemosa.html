Nardosmia racemosa

Nardosmia racemosa, commonly known as the sweet flag or fragrant reed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant produces a distinctive, spicy scent that is often described as similar to that of lemongrass or turpentine. Nardosmia racemosa thrives in wet, marshy environments and is often found near water bodies.

It is valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits and is also used in perfumery due to its unique fragrance.
