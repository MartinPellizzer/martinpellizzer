Nasturtium officinale var. vulgare

Nasturtium officinale var. vulgare, commonly known as the common nasturtium, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers in shades of orange and yellow, which are borne on erect, hairy stems. The plant produces edible leaves and seed pods, which are often used in culinary applications. Its growth habit includes trailing or upright stems, depending on the variety, and it thrives in well-drained soil.

Nasturtium officinale var. vulgare is also known for its ability to attract beneficial insects such as ladybugs and hoverflies.
