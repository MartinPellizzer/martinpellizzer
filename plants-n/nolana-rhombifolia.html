Nolana rhombifolia

Nolana rhombifolia, commonly known as the diamondleaf nolana, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile.

It is a member of the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its distinctive diamond-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in clusters, adding ornamental value. It thrives in coastal environments and is adapted to sandy soils and coastal winds.

Nolana rhombifolia is often found in dune ecosystems and is valued for its resilience and aesthetic appeal.
