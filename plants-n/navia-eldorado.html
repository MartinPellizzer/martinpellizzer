Navia eldorado

Navia eldorado, commonly known as the golden spire plant, is a striking member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach up to 10 feet in height, adorned with vibrant yellow bracts that resemble spires. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves with a glossy, dark green surface.

Navia eldorado is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
