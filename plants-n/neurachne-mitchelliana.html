Neurachne mitchelliana

Neurachne mitchelliana, commonly known as Mitchell's grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is well adapted to drought conditions and can thrive in low-nutrient environments.

Neurachne mitchelliana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in native landscapes.
