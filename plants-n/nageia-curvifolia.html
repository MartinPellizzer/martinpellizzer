Nageia curvifolia

Nageia curvifolia, commonly known as the umbrella pine, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its unique, drooping foliage that resembles an umbrella, with long, needle-like leaves that curve downward. The tree produces small, spherical cones that mature to a reddish-brown color, typically containing two seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mixed forests.

Nageia curvifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
