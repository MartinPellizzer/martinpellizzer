Najas obvoluta

Najas obvoluta, commonly known as the spiral watermilfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It is characterized by its whorled arrangement of narrow, ribbon-like leaves that spiral around the stem. The plant produces small, green flowers that are typically found in clusters near the water's surface. Najas obvoluta plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

It is often found in ponds, lakes, and slow-moving rivers.
