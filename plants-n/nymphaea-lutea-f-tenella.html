Nymphaea lutea f. tenella

Nymphaea lutea f. tenella, commonly known as the dwarf yellow water lily, is a compact variant of the yellow water lily.

It is characterized by its small, rounded leaves and showy, golden-yellow flowers that bloom above the water surface. This aquatic plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes. Its leaves are typically floating or submerged, depending on the water conditions.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in aquatic ecosystems.
