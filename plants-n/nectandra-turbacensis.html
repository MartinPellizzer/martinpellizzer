Nectandra turbacensis

Nectandra turbacensis, commonly known as the Turban wood or Turban tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in catkins, followed by dark purple berries. Its wood is valued for its durability and resistance to decay, making it useful in construction and furniture-making.

Nectandra turbacensis thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.
