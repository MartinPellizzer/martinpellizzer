Nasturtium erythrospermum

Nasturtium erythrospermum, commonly known as the Indian cress, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its bright yellow or orange flowers and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, edible capsules that contain seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in cool climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and culinary uses.

Nasturtium erythrospermum is also known for its peppery taste, similar to watercress.
