Nasonia

Nasonia, commonly known as the wasp fly, is a genus of small parasitic flies belonging to the family Pteromalidae.

These insects are notable for their unique life cycle, where the larvae develop inside the bodies of other insects, such as beetles and flies. Nasonia species are often found in association with decaying organic matter, which provides a suitable environment for their larvae to complete development. Their botanical significance lies in their role as natural regulators of pest populations in agricultural and natural ecosystems.

Due to their genetic simplicity and rapid life cycle, Nasonia species are also widely used in scientific research, particularly in the study of genetics and evolutionary biology.
