Nemcia reticulata var. axillaris

Nemcia reticulata var. axillaris, commonly known as the Axillary Reticulate Nemcia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in shades of white and pink. The plant has a compact growth habit with hairy leaves that are often arranged in a reticulate pattern. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in understory layers of rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
