Nymphaea emirnensis

Nymphaea emirnensis, commonly known as the Emirnensis water lily, is a aquatic plant native to the Caspian Sea region.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that float on the water surface. The plant produces striking white or pink flowers that bloom in the early morning. Its roots are anchored in the muddy substrate at the bottom of the water body.

Nymphaea emirnensis plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
