Neomammillaria picta

Neomammillaria picta, commonly known as the picta cactus, is a species of flowering cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are often ribbed and covered in white to yellow spines. The plant produces clusters of white or pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. Neomammillaria picta is a slow-growing plant that thrives in well-draining soil and full sun exposure.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in arid gardens and as a houseplant.
