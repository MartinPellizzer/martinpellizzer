Nepenthes murudensis

Nepenthes murudensis, commonly known as the Murudensis Pitcher Plant, is a carnivorous plant native to the highlands of Borneo.

It is characterized by its large, elongated leaf pitchers that secrete a sticky digestive fluid. The plant's unique adaptations allow it to trap and digest insects, supplementing its nutrient intake in nutrient-poor soils. Its pitchers often exhibit a striking red or orange coloration, which helps attract prey.

This species is also notable for its ability to produce nectar to lure insects, enhancing its predatory efficiency.
