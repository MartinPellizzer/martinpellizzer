Nymphozanthus bombycinus

Nymphozanthus bombycinus, commonly known as the golden flame orchid, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, funnel-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Nymphozanthus bombycinus is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
