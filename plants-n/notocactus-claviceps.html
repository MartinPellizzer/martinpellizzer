Notocactus claviceps

Notocactus claviceps, commonly known as the club-cactus, is a species of cactus native to southern South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are often covered in spines. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, adapting to harsh conditions with its succulent structure. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, appearing in clusters at the top of the stem.

Notocactus claviceps is a slow-growing plant that requires minimal water and is popular among cactus enthusiasts for its unique appearance.
