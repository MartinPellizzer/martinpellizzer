Nepeta juncea subsp. juncea

Nepeta juncea subsp.

juncea, commonly known as catnip, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves.

The plant is renowned for its aromatic leaves, which contain high concentrations of nepetalactone, a compound that attracts cats. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in moist environments.

Its flowering spikes produce small, tubular blue or purple flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
