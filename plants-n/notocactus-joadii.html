Notocactus joadii

Notocactus joadii, commonly known as the Joad's notocactus, is a unique cactus species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are often covered in small, spiny tubercles. The plant produces vibrant, funnel-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in the evening. Its succulent structure allows it to store water efficiently, adapting to its harsh, dry environment.

Notocactus joadii is a slow-growing plant that thrives in well-draining soil and full sunlight.
