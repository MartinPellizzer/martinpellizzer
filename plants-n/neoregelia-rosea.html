Neoregelia rosea

Neoregelia rosea, commonly known as the pink star bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup to collect rainwater. The plant produces vibrant, long-lasting flowers in shades of pink and red, which bloom from the center of the rosette. Its foliage is often adorned with striking patterns and colors, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Neoregelia rosea thrives in bright, indirect light and requires high humidity to flourish.
