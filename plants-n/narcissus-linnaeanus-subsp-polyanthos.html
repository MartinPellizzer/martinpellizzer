Narcissus linnaeanus subsp. polyanthos

Narcissus linnaeanus subsp.

polyanthos, commonly known as the double daffodil, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or white, with a distinctive double petal structure.

The plant produces a cluster of flowers on a single stem, which blooms in early spring. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species in both ornamental horticulture and botanical study.
