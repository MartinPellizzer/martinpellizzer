Narcissus sylvestris var. bicolor

Narcissus sylvestris var. bicolor, commonly known as the wild narcissus or the yellow wild daffodil, is a perennial bulbous plant native to Europe.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, cup-shaped flowers with a yellow corona. The flowers typically bloom in early spring, often before the leaves fully develop. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a suitable plant for woodland gardens.

This variety is noted for its natural beauty and is often found in wildflower meadows and along forest edges.
