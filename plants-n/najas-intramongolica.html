Najas intramongolica

Najas intramongolica, commonly known as the Mongolian water plant, is a species of aquatic plant belonging to the family Najadaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, ribbon-like leaves that are typically submerged in freshwater environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually found near the water surface. It is native to parts of Asia, including Mongolia and China, where it thrives in still or slow-moving waters.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
