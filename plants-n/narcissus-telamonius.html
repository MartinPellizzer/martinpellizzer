Narcissus telamonius

Narcissus telamonius, commonly known as the Telamonian daffodil, is a species native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its striking yellow petals and a prominent, trumpet-shaped corona. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It blooms in early spring, producing clusters of flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardens and botanical collections.
