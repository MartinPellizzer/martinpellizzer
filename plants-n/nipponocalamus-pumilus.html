Nipponocalamus pumilus

Nipponocalamus pumilus, commonly known as the dwarf bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, clumping growth habit. The plant typically grows to a height of only a few centimeters, making it one of the smallest bamboo-like species. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a fine, textured surface.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
