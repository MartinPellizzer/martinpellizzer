Nephelium rimosum

Nephelium rimosum, commonly known as the rambutan, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, spiny fruits that resemble a hairy ball, giving it its unique appearance. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and elliptic in shape, with a glossy green upper surface.

The rambutan tree is cultivated for its edible fruit, which is rich in vitamin C and has a sweet, slightly tangy flavor.
