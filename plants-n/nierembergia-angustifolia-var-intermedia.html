Nierembergia angustifolia var. intermedia

Nierembergia angustifolia var. intermedia, commonly known as the narrowleaf bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, with five petals arranged symmetrically. The plant has slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening due to its attractive blooms and relatively low maintenance requirements.
