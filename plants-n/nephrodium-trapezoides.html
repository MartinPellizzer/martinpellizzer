Nephrodium trapezoides

Nephrodium trapezoides, commonly known as the diamond fern, is a unique member of the Ophioglossaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, diamond-shaped fronds that are divided into two pairs of unequal lobes. Nephrodium trapezoides is often found in the southeastern United States and parts of Central America.
