Nasturtium philippianum

Nasturtium philippianum, commonly known as the Philippine watercress, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous plants. The plant is characterized by its edible leaves and small yellow flowers, which attract pollinators like bees. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found near streams or in wetlands.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
