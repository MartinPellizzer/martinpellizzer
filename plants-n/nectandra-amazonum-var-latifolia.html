Nectandra amazonum var. latifolia

Nectandra amazonum var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed sandarac tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its large, lance-shaped leaves that are often glossy and aromatic. The tree produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by woody, fragrant fruits containing oily seeds. Its wood is valued for its aromatic properties and is used in traditional medicine and as a source of sandarac resin.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
