Nemesia dentata

Nemesia dentata, commonly known as the toothed nemesia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a member of the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that come in shades of pink, white, and red. The plant typically grows as an annual or biennial herb, often reaching heights of 20 to 60 centimeters. Its foliage consists of opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with serrated edges, which give it the species name "dentata.".

Nemesia dentata thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
