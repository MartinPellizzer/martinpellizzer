Nasturtium serra

Nasturtium serra, commonly known as the serrated nasturtium, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Tropaeolaceae and is characterized by its vibrant, brightly colored flowers that range from orange to red. The leaves are heart-shaped and often have a slightly serrated edge, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

The plant is also known for its edible leaves and flowers, which have a peppery taste similar to watercress.
