Nassella major

Nassella major, commonly known as purple needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to western North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and distinctive purple-tinted leaves. The plant produces fluffy, feathery seed heads that are often used in floral arrangements. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed sites.

Nassella major is an important component of native plant communities and is valued for its ecological and ornamental qualities.
