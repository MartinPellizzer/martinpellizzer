Nyssa denticulata

Nyssa denticulata, commonly known as the toothed tupelo, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Nyssaceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in the spring, followed by dark blue-black berries that are an important food source for wildlife.

Its roots are often found in wetland areas, making it an indicator species for swamp and bottomland habitats.
