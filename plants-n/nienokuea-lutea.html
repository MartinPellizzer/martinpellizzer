Nienokuea lutea

Nienokuea lutea, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the rainy season. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are glossy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Nienokuea lutea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
