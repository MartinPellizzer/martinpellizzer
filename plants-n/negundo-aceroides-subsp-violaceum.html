Negundo aceroides subsp. violaceum

Negundo aceroides subsp.

violaceum, commonly known as the purple-leaf maple, is a deciduous tree native to parts of East Asia. It is characterized by its striking violet to purple foliage, which becomes more intense in autumn.

The leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with serrated margins. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged samaras for seed dispersal.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
