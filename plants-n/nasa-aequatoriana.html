Nasa aequatoriana

Nasa aequatoriana, commonly known as the Andean moonflower, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, white, night-blooming flowers that emit a sweet fragrance. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and thrives in moist, shaded environments. It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is known for its unique blooming pattern.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
