Nothoscordum inodorum var. nepalense

Nothoscordum inodorum var. nepalense, commonly known as the Nepal garlic.

It is a perennial herb native to the Himalayan regions of Nepal and northern India. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in loose clusters. It is closely related to the genus Nothoscordum, which includes species with edible bulbs.

The variety nepalense is distinguished by its unique floral structure and habitat preferences.
