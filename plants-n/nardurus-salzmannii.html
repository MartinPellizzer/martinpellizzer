Nardurus salzmannii

Nardurus salzmannii, commonly known as the Salzmann's nardus, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as rocky slopes and grasslands.

Nardurus salzmannii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticultural settings for its resilience and aesthetic qualities.
