Nastus brunneus

Nastus brunneus, commonly known as the brown nasturtium, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, which includes mustard and cress plants. The plant features showy, yellow-orange flowers with four petals and distinctive, heart-shaped leaves. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, round seeds.

Nastus brunneus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some medicinal uses in traditional herbal practices.
