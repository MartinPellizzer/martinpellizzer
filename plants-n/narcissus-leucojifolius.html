Narcissus leucojifolius

Narcissus leucojifolius, commonly known as the white-leaved daffodil, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to the Iberian Peninsula and is characterized by its striking white petals and yellow-orange corona. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a fresh, elegant appearance to the landscape.

Due to its unique foliage and floral structure, it is a valued ornamental plant in horticulture.
