Neptunia robertsonii

Neptunia robertsonii, commonly known as the water spinach or river spinach, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant thrives in wet, muddy environments and is often found near water bodies. Its roots can form symbiotic relationships with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, enhancing soil fertility.

Neptunia robertsonii is also valued for its edible leaves, which are used in various culinary traditions.
