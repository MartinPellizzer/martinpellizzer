Nepeta baytopii

Nepeta baytopii, commonly known as Turkish catmint, is a perennial herb native to Turkey and the eastern Mediterranean.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces spikes of blue to purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their calming properties.

Nepeta baytopii thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.
