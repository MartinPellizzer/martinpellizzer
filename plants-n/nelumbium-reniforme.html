Nelumbium reniforme

Nelumbium reniforme, commonly known as the broadleaf water fern, is a aquatic plant native to parts of Asia.

It features delicate, floating leaves that are broadly ovate with a distinctive heart-shaped base. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in spadices, typical of the fern family. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Nelumbium reniforme thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, contributing to aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various organisms.
