Nidus

Nidus, commonly known as the "nest plant," is a unique species characterized by its intricate, nest-like structure.

It belongs to the genus *Cephalostigma* and is native to specific regions in Southeast Asia. The plant's most distinctive feature is its dense, velvety foliage that resembles a natural nest, providing shelter for small insects and animals. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Botanically, it is noted for its compact growth habit and unique morphological adaptations that support its ecological role.
