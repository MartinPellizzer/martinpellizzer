Narcissus horsfeldii

Narcissus horsfeldii, commonly known as the Horsfeld's daffodil, is a species native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers with a distinctive crown-like structure. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, with narrow, strap-like leaves. Its flowers bloom in early spring, often before the leaves are fully developed.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is commonly cultivated in gardens.
