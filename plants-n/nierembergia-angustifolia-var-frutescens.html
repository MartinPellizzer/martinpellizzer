Nierembergia angustifolia var. frutescens

Nierembergia angustifolia var. frutescens, commonly known as the dwarf bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features slender, branching stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a valuable addition to botanical gardens and native plant collections.
