Nectandra austinii

Nectandra austinii, commonly known as Austin's nectandra, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by elongated, woody fruits containing aromatic seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its unique fragrance and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
