Nigella arvensis var. caudata

Nigella arvensis var. caudata, commonly known as the curly cumin or Persian cumin, is an annual herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, branched stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces elongated, coiled seed pods that give it its distinctive appearance. Its leaves are finely divided and aromatic, often used in traditional medicine and cuisine.

Nigella arvensis var. caudata thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens and wildflower habitats.
