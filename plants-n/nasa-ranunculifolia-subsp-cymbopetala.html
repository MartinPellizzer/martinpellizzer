Nasa ranunculifolia subsp. cymbopetala

Nasa ranunculifolia subsp.

cymbopetala, commonly known as the "Moonflower of the Andes," is a striking plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes Mountains. It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that open at night, emitting a sweet fragrance to attract nocturnal pollinators.

The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture, and its stems are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique beauty and ecological significance, it is a subject of interest in botanical research and conservation efforts.
