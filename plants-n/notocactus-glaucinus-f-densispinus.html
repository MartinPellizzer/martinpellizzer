Notocactus glaucinus f. densispinus

Notocactus glaucinus f. densispinus, commonly known as the dense-spined gloomy cactus, is a unique variation of the gloomy cactus characterized by its densely packed, spiny stems.

It is native to the arid regions of South America, particularly in Brazil, where it thrives in dry, rocky environments. The plant features a cylindrical stem that often branches, with a bluish-gray coloration due to a waxy coating. Its spines are short and numerous, providing protection against herbivores and reducing water loss.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and ability to adapt to low-water conditions, making it a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts.
