Najas marina var. delilei

Najas marina var. delilei, commonly known as the narrowleaf water-naiad, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater habitats in Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Najadaceae and is characterized by its narrow, ribbon-like leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged, followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits that release minute seeds into the water. It thrives in still or slow-moving waters with moderate to high light penetration.

This species plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
