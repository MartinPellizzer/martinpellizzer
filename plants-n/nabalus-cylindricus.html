Nabalus cylindricus

Nabalus cylindricus, commonly known as the cylindrical bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, elongated fruits that contain the seeds.

Nabalus cylindricus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
