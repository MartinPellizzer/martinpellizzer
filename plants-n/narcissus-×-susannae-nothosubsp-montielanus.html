Narcissus × susannae nothosubsp.

montielanus, commonly known as the Montielan narcissus, is a hybrid plant originating from crosses between Narcissus susannae and other species. It is characterized by its striking white or pale yellow flowers with a distinctive cup-shaped corona.

The plant typically grows in Mediterranean climates, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its foliage consists of strap-like leaves that are evergreen, contributing to its ornamental value.

This cultivar is valued for its beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
