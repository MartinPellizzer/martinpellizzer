Nepeta cinereoviolacea

Nepeta cinereoviolacea, commonly known as gray violet catmint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine, grayish-white hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and aromatic, often with a mint-like scent.

Nepeta cinereoviolacea is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
