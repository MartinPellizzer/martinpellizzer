Nesaea hockii

Nesaea hockii, commonly known as the Hockii's bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of East Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, often with a distinctive red or purple spot at the base. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stems.

Nesaea hockii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
