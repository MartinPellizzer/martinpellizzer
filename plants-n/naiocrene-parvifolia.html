Naiocrene parvifolia

Naiocrene parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaf naiocrene, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, narrow leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade. Its foliage is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported calming properties.

Naiocrene parvifolia is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
