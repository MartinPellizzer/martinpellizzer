Neomammillaria flavovirens

Neomammillaria flavovirens, commonly known as the yellow-green hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are covered in white to yellowish tubercles, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are typically borne at the top of the stems. It is a slow-growing cactus that thrives in well-draining soil and full sun exposure.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for desert gardens and rockeries.
