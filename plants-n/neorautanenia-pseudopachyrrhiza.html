Neorautanenia pseudopachyrrhiza

Neorautanenia pseudopachyrrhiza, commonly known as the narrow-leafed rock fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant features slender, elongated leaves with a distinctive pinnate structure. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are arranged along the margins of the fronds.

Neorautanenia pseudopachyrrhiza is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in humid, tropical regions.
