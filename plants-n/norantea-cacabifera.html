Norantea cacabifera

Norantea cacabifera, commonly known as the golden spire cactus, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It features a tall, cylindrical stem covered in dense, yellowish spines that provide protection from herbivores and reduce water loss. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are modified into spines, and it stores water in its thick, fleshy stem for survival in dry conditions.

Norantea cacabifera is valued for its ornamental appearance and adaptability to harsh environments.
