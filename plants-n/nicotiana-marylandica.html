Nicotiana marylandica

Nicotiana marylandica, commonly known as Maryland tobacco, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant produces striking violet to purple flowers and has hairy, lance-shaped leaves. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical significance in tobacco production.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and scientific study.
