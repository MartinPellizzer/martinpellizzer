Nicotiana tabacum

Nicotiana tabacum, commonly known as tobacco, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its alternate, ovate leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant contains high concentrations of nicotine, a potent alkaloid used in various products. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its cultivation has significant economic and historical importance worldwide.
