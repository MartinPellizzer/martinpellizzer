Nemophila menziesii subsp. australis

Nemophila menziesii subsp.

australis, commonly known as the coastal blue-eyed Mary, is a low-growing perennial herb native to coastal regions of California and Oregon. It features slender stems and produces small, sky-blue to violet flowers with five petals, typically blooming in spring.

The leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, adapted to withstand coastal winds and salt spray. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
