Neoporteria curvispina var. engleri

Neoporteria curvispina var. engleri, commonly known as the Engle's narrow-leaved neoporteria, is a species native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub characterized by its slender, often branching stems and narrow, leathery leaves with a pointed tip. The leaves are typically arranged alternately along the stems and have a distinctive, slightly serrated edge. It produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pale yellow or greenish in color, which bloom during the warmer months.

This plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks.
