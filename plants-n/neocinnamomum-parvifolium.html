Neocinnamomum parvifolium

Neocinnamomum parvifolium, commonly known as the small-leaved cinnamon tree, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia, particularly in countries like Vietnam and China. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its bark and leaves contain essential oils, which have been used traditionally for their medicinal properties.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in dense forest understories.
