Neorosea testui var. pseudosalacina

Neorosea testui var. pseudosalacina, commonly known as the red-tipped photinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that turn vibrant red in the fall. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This ornamental plant is popular for its seasonal color changes and adaptability to various garden settings.
