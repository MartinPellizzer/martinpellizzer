Nectaroscordum tripedale

Nectaroscordum tripedale, commonly known as the sweet garlic or Spanish garlic, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped white or lilac flowers. The plant features strap-like, grassy leaves and produces small, white berries that contain the seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its aromatic properties, it is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of essential oils.
