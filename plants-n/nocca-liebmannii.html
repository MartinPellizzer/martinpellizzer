Nocca liebmannii

Nocca liebmannii, commonly known as the golden sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial with a distinctive rosette of fleshy, silver-green leaves that help it conserve water in its harsh environment. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its succulent leaves store moisture, making it well adapted to desert conditions.

Nocca liebmannii is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
