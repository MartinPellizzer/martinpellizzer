Nuphar japonica f. rubrotincta

Nuphar japonica f. rubrotincta, commonly known as the Japanese water lily with red veins, is a rare variant of the Japanese water lily.

It is characterized by its strikingly red-veined leaves that contrast with its typical green foliage. The plant is native to Japan and thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, with a subtle fragrance.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal in aquatic gardens and botanical collections.
