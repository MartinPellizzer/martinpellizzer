Najas caespitosa

Najas caespitosa, commonly known as the tufted watermilfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like clusters of narrow leaves that grow in water up to several meters deep. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically found in the water column, aiding in its reproduction through water currents. Najas caespitosa plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Its presence is often an indicator of good water quality in ponds and lakes.
