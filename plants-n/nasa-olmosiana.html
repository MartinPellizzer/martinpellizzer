Nasa olmosiana

Nasa olmosiana, commonly known as the Andean nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the high Andes of South America.

It is characterized by its small, violet-blue flowers and hairy, glandular leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at high altitudes. It is part of the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and potatoes.

Nasa olmosiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some Andean communities.
