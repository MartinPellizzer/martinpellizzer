Nepeta amethystina var. nevadensis

Nepeta amethystina var. nevadensis, commonly known as the Nevada catmint, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine, grayish-blue foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from pale lavender to deep violet. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its aromatic leaves and showy blooms make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and pollinator-friendly landscapes.
