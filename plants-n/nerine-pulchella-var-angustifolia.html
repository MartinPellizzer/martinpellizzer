Nerine pulchella var. angustifolia

Nerine pulchella var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved nerine, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It features striking bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, typically blooming in late summer to early autumn. The plant has narrow, strap-like leaves that are often a deep green, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking both beauty and resilience in temperate climates.
