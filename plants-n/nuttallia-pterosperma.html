Nuttallia pterosperma

Nuttallia pterosperma, commonly known as the giant waterwheel plant, is a unique aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, spirally arranged leaves that resemble the blades of a waterwheel, which help it float on the water's surface. The plant has a specialized root system that allows it to anchor itself in shallow water while absorbing nutrients from the surrounding environment. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically greenish-yellow, and it reproduces through seeds dispersed by water currents.

Nuttallia pterosperma plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
