Nepeta pectinata

Nepeta pectinata, commonly known as catmint, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features square, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often aromatic. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular blue or purple flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Nepeta pectinata is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and its historical use in herbal remedies.
