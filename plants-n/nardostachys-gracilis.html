Nardostachys gracilis

Nardostachys gracilis, commonly known as slender mountain betony, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or rocky habitats.

Nardostachys gracilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
