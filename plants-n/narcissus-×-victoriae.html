Narcissus × victoriae

Narcissus × victoriae, commonly known as the Victoria hybrid daffodil, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Narcissus poeticus and Narcissus tazetta.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers with six to eight petals and a prominent, trumpet-shaped corona. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and produces bright yellow or white blooms in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

This hybrid is valued for its ornamental beauty and is widely cultivated in temperate regions.
