Nectandra vaga var. major

Nectandra vaga var. major, commonly known as the broad-leafed nectandra, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are often clustered at the base of the stems. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by dark, woody fruits containing aromatic seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The tree is valued for its essential oils and has traditional uses in local medicine and perfumery.
