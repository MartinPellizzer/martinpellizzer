Nymphoides disperma

Nymphoides disperma, commonly known as the golden bell orchid, is a herbaceous perennial native to Southeast Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically golden yellow, with a distinctive lip that curves downward. The plant has slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
