Nephrolepis neglecta

Nephrolepis neglecta, commonly known as the common maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically arching and have a soft, feathery appearance. The plant thrives in humid environments with indirect light and well-draining, moist soil. Its rhizomes spread horizontally, allowing it to form dense colonies over time.

Nephrolepis neglecta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in indoor gardening and landscaping.
