Narcissus tazetta subsp. panizzianus

Narcissus tazetta subsp.

panizzianus, commonly known as the Panizzian narcissus, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region. It produces showy, fragrant flowers with six petals and a central corona, typically in shades of white, yellow, or orange.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders. Its bulbs are ornamental and often cultivated for their aesthetic value in gardens.

This subspecies is distinguished by its compact growth habit and early blooming period compared to other narcissus varieties.
