Nephrodium albopunctatum

Nephrodium albopunctatum, commonly known as the white-spotted nephroid, is a unique fern species characterized by its distinctive, kidney-shaped fronds.

The plant features a creeping rhizome with numerous aerial roots, and its fronds are typically bipinnate, with a delicate, lace-like appearance. The leaves are covered in fine, white punctuations that give it its common name, and they are often arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.
