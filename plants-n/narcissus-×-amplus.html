Narcissus × amplus

Narcissus × amplus, commonly known as the giant daffodil, is a hybrid species within the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers with a distinctive crown-like shape. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, making it one of the tallest daffodils. Its foliage consists of broad, strap-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color.

The flowers bloom in early spring, adding a striking visual element to gardens and natural landscapes.
