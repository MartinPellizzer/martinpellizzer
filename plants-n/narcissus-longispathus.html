Narcissus longispathus

Narcissus longispathus, commonly known as the long-spurred daffodil, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and is characterized by its distinctive, elongated petals and a prominent, spurred corona. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its flowers bloom in early spring, producing bright yellow blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Narcissus longispathus is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant display.
