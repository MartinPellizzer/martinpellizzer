Nectandra magnoliifolia

Nectandra magnoliifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf nectandra, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its large, glossy, ovate leaves that are often clustered at the ends of branches. The plant produces fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, round fruits containing aromatic seeds. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

The tree thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.
