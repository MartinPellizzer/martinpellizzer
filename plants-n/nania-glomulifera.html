Nania glomulifera

Nania glomulifera, commonly known as the globe-forming moss, is a unique species of moss characterized by its dense, rounded growth habit.

It forms compact, spherical cushions that can be several centimeters in diameter, often resembling small, rounded hills. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are small, overlapping, and tightly packed, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Nania glomulifera plays an important role in soil stabilization and moisture retention in its native habitat.
