Nepenthes × edinensis

Nepenthes × edinensis, commonly known as the hybrid tropical pitcher plant, is a fascinating species resulting from the cross between Nepenthes rafflesiana and Nepenthes mirabilis.

It is characterized by its large, elongated pitchers that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length, often adorned with distinctive red or orange waxy veins. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in the rainforests of Southeast Asia. Its nectar-secreting leaf traps are designed to lure and digest insects, making it a unique carnivorous plant.

This hybrid is highly valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to cultivation.
