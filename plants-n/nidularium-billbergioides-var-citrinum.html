Nidularium billbergioides var. citrinum

Nidularium billbergioides var. citrinum, commonly known as the lemon-flowered spiderwort, is a striking member of the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features strap-like, dark green leaves that form a dense rosette near the ground. Its inflorescence consists of a single, showy flower that emerges from the center of the rosette.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance and ease of care.
