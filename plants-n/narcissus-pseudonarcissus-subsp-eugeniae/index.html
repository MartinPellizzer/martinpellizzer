Narcissus Pseudonarcissus Subsp. Eugeniae

Narcissus pseudonarcissus subsp. eugeniae is a rare and elegant variant of the daffodil known for its distinctive floral features. This subspecies is characterized by its narrow, elongated petals and a pale yellow corona that sets it apart from common daffodils. Native to southern Europe, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun conditions. Its blooming period typically occurs in early spring, adding a touch of beauty to gardens and natural landscapes. Due to its limited distribution and unique appearance, it is highly valued by botanists and gardeners alike.
