Nothofagus patagonica

Nothofagus patagonica, commonly known as the Patagonian Nothofagus, is a species of southern beech native to South America.

It is characterized by its lanceolate, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by woody, cup-shaped cones containing edible seeds. It thrives in temperate rainforests and is adapted to cool, moist climates.

Nothofagus patagonica plays a vital role in the ecological balance of its native habitat.
