Nomosa rosei

Nomosa rosei, commonly known as the desert rose, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its fleshy, succulent stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in dry environments. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, often with a striking contrast against its green or blue-green foliage. Its leaves are typically small, narrow, and arranged oppositely along the stems.

Nomosa rosei is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and drought tolerance.
