Napaea scabra

Napaea scabra, commonly known as the rough napaea, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia and the Americas.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, upright stems that are often covered in fine, rough hairs. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a distinctive serrated or toothed margin.

The small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in terminal racemes and bloom in the spring.
