Nassella yaviensis

Nassella yaviensis, commonly known as the Yaviensis needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences. The plant typically thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a rough texture, and it has a fibrous root system.

Nassella yaviensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
