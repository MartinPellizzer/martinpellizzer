Neottia camtschatea

Neottia camtschatea, commonly known as the Siberian twayblade, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to regions of Siberia and northern Asia.

This perennial herb is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and a single, inconspicuous flower that typically blooms in late spring. It is a mycoheterotroph, meaning it relies on fungal associations for its nutrients rather than photosynthesis. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as coniferous forests and bogs.

Its unique ecological role highlights its adaptation to nutrient-poor soils through its symbiotic relationship with fungi.
