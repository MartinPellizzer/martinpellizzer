Niebuhria pedunculosa

Niebuhria pedunculosa, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It features showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from violet to pink, typically blooming in summer. The plant has a robust root system and lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Niebuhria pedunculosa is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
