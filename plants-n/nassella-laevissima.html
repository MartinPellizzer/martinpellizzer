Nassella laevissima

Nassella laevissima, commonly known as silky needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to western North America.

It is characterized by its fine, hair-like leaves and slender, cylindrical seed heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It produces small, wind-dispersed seeds that contribute to its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting wildlife.

Its botanical features make it a valuable component of native plant communities.
