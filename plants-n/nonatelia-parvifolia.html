Nonatelia parvifolia

Nonatelia parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved nonatelia, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, elliptical leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring. Its botanical features make it an attractive addition to gardens, though it is not widely cultivated.

Nonatelia parvifolia is primarily of interest to botanists and horticulturists due to its unique morphology and limited distribution.
