Noronhia disjuncta

Noronhia disjuncta, commonly known as the Brazilian starfruit, is a tropical plant native to Brazil.

It is a small shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a fleshy, star-shaped berry that ripens to a yellow-orange color.

The botanical features of Noronhia disjuncta make it a unique and ecologically significant species in its native habitat.
