Normanboria henrardiana

Normanboria henrardiana, commonly known as the Henrard's orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, showy flowers that typically bloom in late spring or early summer. The plant features a rhizomatous root system and has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with a distinctive lip that is often marked with purple or brown streaks.

Normanboria henrardiana thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires a cool, humid environment to flourish.
