Narcissus amancaes

Narcissus amancaes, commonly known as the Canary Islands daffodil, is a species of flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers with a white throat. The plant features strap-shaped leaves and produces a single, showy bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Due to its limited natural range, it is considered a rare and endangered species in the wild.
