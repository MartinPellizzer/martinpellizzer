Neolitsea chunii var. brevipes

Neolitsea chunii var. brevipes, commonly known as the short-stemmed Chinese tallowtree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern China.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth margin. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in catkins, followed by small, woody fruits containing single seeds.

It is often found in moist, shady environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
