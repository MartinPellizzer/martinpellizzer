Notocactus concinnus var. pororensis

Notocactus concinnus var. pororensis, commonly known as the Poronensis Cactus, is a rare and unique species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are typically green with a subtle bluish tint. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are often clustered at the tips of the stems. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect sunlight to flourish.

Due to its rarity and specific care requirements, it is a sought-after specimen among cactus enthusiasts.
