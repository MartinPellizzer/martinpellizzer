Nephelium chryseum var. topengii

Nephelium chryseum var. topengii, commonly known as the golden rambutan, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, yellowish-orange pericarp that resembles a hairy ball, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are followed by edible, hairy fruits. The leaves are simple, alternate, and elliptic in shape, with a glossy green surface.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental and culinary value.
