Nephelium cuspidatum var. bassacense

Nephelium cuspidatum var. bassacense, commonly known as the Bassek orange, is a tropical plant belonging to the Sapindaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Cambodia and Vietnam, where it thrives in warm, humid climates. The plant produces small, round citrus fruits with a thin, yellowish rind and a sweet, aromatic pulp. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate with a glossy surface.

The Bassek orange is valued for its edible fruit and is often cultivated for both commercial and ornamental purposes.
