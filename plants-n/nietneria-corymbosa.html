Nietneria corymbosa

Nietneria corymbosa, commonly known as the clustered sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related grass-like plants. The plant is characterized by its triangular stems and dense, clustered inflorescences that bear small, spike-like flowers. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, with a distinct midrib.

Nietneria corymbosa is often found in wetlands, marshes, and other moist habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
