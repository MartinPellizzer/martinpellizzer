Narcissus monophyllus

Narcissus monophyllus, commonly known as the single leaf daffodil, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its single, broad leaf and a single, showy flower with a trumpet-shaped corona. Native to southern Europe, particularly the Iberian Peninsula, it thrives in sunny, well-drained soils. The plant produces yellow petals and a white or yellow corona, often blooming in early spring.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
