Naudinia

Naudinia, commonly known as the African orchid or *Naudinia capensis*, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that often display vibrant colors such as red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or lithophyte, thriving on trees or rocks in humid environments. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the air, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain seeds.

Naudinia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
