Negundo cochinchinense

Negundo cochinchinense, commonly known as the Vietnamese ash or Vietnamese white ash, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ulmaceae family, which includes elms and ashes, and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically heart-shaped. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in catkins, followed by small, spherical fruits that contain a single seed.

It is often found in moist, well-drained soils along rivers and in forested areas, and it plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
