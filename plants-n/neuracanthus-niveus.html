Neuracanthus niveus

Neuracanthus niveus, commonly known as the snowcap bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue, with a velvety texture. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its frost-resistant nature. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Neuracanthus niveus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
