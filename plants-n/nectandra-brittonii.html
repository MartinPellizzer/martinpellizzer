Nectandra brittonii

Nectandra brittonii, commonly known as the West Indian sandalwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, fragrant wood. The plant typically grows in humid, lowland forest areas and can reach heights of up to 25 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a smooth, glossy surface.

The flowers are small and clustered, producing a unique, spicy scent that attracts pollinators.
