Neea floribunda

Neea floribunda, commonly known as the fragrant beautyberry, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces bright red berries that are edible for birds and some mammals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Neea floribunda is valued for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting wildlife.
