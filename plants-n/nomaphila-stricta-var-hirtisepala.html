Nomaphila stricta var. hirtisepala

Nomaphila stricta var. hirtisepala, commonly known as the hairy-sepaled orchid, is a species native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pale green to yellowish in color. Its sepals and petals are covered with fine, hairy structures, giving it its varietal name.

This orchid thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
