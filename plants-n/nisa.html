Nisa

Nisa, commonly known as the "tree of life," is a unique plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments. The plant has a fibrous, woody stem that can grow quite tall, often reaching several meters in height. Its leaves are broad and leathery, providing shade and contributing to the ecosystem's biodiversity.

Nisa is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for centuries.
