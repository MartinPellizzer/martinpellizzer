Nassauvia lycopodioides

Nassauvia lycopodioides, commonly known as the creeping lycopod, is a small, herbaceous plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and small, scale-like leaves that are often clustered at the base of the stems. The plant produces tiny, spore-bearing structures called strobila, which are typically found at the tips of the stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in rocky or forested areas.

Due to its delicate nature, it is relatively rare and requires specific conditions to flourish.
