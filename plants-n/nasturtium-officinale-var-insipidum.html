Nasturtium officinale var. insipidum

Nasturtium officinale var. insipidum, commonly known as the edible nasturtium, is a variant of the common nasturtium plant.

It is characterized by its edible leaves and flowers, which have a mild, peppery flavor. This variety is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes. The plant is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous plants.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, typically blooming in summer with vibrant orange and yellow flowers.
