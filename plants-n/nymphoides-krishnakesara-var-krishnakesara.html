Nymphoides krishnakesara var. krishnakesara

Nymphoides krishnakesara var. krishnakesara, commonly known as the Krishna's water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features broad, heart-shaped leaves that float on the water's surface, often with a waxy texture and vibrant green coloration. The plant produces showy, white to pale pink flowers with a strong, sweet fragrance, typically blooming in the early morning. It thrives in shallow, still waters with ample sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental ponds and wetlands.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and ecological role in aquatic ecosystems.
