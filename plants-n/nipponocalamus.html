Nipponocalamus

Nipponocalamus, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo, is a genus of grass-like plants native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems and narrow leaves. These plants typically grow in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Nipponocalamus species are often used in traditional landscaping and can reach heights of up to several meters.

Their rhizomatous growth habit allows them to form dense clumps, making them suitable for erosion control and ornamental purposes.
