Neochilenia dimorpha

Neochilenia dimorpha, commonly known as the Chilean star cactus, is a succulent plant native to the coastal regions of southern Chile.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are often branched and covered with spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are clustered in inflorescences at the stem tips. It is adapted to arid environments and requires well-draining soil to thrive.

Neochilenia dimorpha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable climate conditions.
