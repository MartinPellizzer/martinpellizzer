Nicotiana glauca var. angustifolia

Nicotiana glauca var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf tobacco plant, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped, bluish-green leaves. The flowers are typically blue or violet, with five petals, and are arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and contains alkaloids such as nicotine, though it is not cultivated for commercial tobacco use.

This plant is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments.
