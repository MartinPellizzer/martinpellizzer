Nothopegia beddomei var. wynaadica

Nothopegia beddomei var. wynaadica, commonly known as the Wynaad thornapple, is a variant of the Nothopegia beddomei species native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is a shrub characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically green when mature. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or disturbed habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
