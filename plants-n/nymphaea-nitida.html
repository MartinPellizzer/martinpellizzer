Nymphaea nitida

Nymphaea nitida, commonly known as the slender water lily, is a aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features broad, heart-shaped leaves that float on the water surface, supported by long, slender petioles. The flowers are typically white or pale pink, with a fragrant scent, and bloom in the early morning. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments.

Its roots anchor it in the muddy substrate, while its leaves provide habitat for various aquatic organisms.
