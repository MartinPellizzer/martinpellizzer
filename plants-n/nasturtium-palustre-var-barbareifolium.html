Nasturtium palustre var. barbareifolium

Nasturtium palustre var. barbareifolium, commonly known as the marsh nasturtium, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to wetland habitats in Europe.

It features heart-shaped, slightly lobed leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces bright yellow or orange flowers with a distinctive spurred petal, typical of the Tropaeolaceae family. Its stems are slender and often sprawling, adapted to grow in moist or aquatic environments.
