Nepeta taxkorganica

Nepeta taxkorganica, commonly known as the Taxkorgan catmint, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
