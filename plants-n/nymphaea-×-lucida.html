Nymphaea × lucida

Nymphaea × lucida, commonly known as the white water lily, is a hybrid aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves and showy white flowers that open in the morning. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes. Its roots are submerged, anchoring it in the sediment while its stems support the foliage.

Nymphaea × lucida plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
