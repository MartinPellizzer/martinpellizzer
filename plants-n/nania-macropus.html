Nania macropus

Nania macropus, commonly known as the broad-leaved nania, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating, which helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from pale yellow to reddish-brown, typically blooming during the cooler months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its unique adaptations, Nania macropus is an important species in its native ecosystem, contributing to local biodiversity.
