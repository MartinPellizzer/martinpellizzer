Nicotiana inodora

Nicotiana inodora, commonly known as the sweet tobacco plant, is a species native to the southern United States and parts of Central America.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and produces small, glandular hairs on its stems and leaves. Its leaves are usually oval or lance-shaped with a rough texture, and the plant thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Botanically, it belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other notable plants like tomatoes and potatoes.
