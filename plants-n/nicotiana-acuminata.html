Nicotiana acuminata

Nicotiana acuminata, commonly known as the sharpleaf tobacco, is a species native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is closely related to other tobacco species. It produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Nicotiana acuminata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been studied for its potential in agricultural and medicinal applications.
