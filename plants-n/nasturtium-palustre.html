Nasturtium palustre

Nasturtium palustre, commonly known as marsh nasturtium, is a flowering plant native to wetland areas of Europe and Asia.

It produces vibrant orange or yellow flowers with a distinctive, slightly peppery scent. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found near ponds, ditches, and marshes.

Nasturtium palustre is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
