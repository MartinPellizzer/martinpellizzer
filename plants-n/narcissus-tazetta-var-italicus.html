Narcissus tazetta var. italicus

Narcissus tazetta var. italicus, commonly known as the Italian daffodil, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is valued for its ornamental beauty in gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for spring gardens.

This variety is also noted for its fragrance and adaptability to different climates.
