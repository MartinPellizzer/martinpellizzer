Nymphaea esculenta

Nymphaea esculenta, commonly known as the Egyptian water lily, is a aquatic plant native to Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in ponds and lakes. Its roots are edible and have been used in traditional cuisine, while the leaves are also consumed in some regions.

Nymphaea esculenta plays an important ecological role by providing habitat for aquatic life and contributing to water filtration.
