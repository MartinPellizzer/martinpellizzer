Nerophila togoensis

Nerophila togoensis, commonly known as the Togo spiderflower, is a flowering plant native to West Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small and clustered, often displaying a mix of white and purple hues.

Nerophila togoensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
