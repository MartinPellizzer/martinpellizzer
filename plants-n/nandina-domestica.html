Nandina domestica

Nandina domestica, commonly known as the heavenly bamboo, is a shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces bright red berries that attract birds, aiding in seed dispersal. Its foliage changes color with the seasons, ranging from green to red and purple.

Nandina domestica is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
