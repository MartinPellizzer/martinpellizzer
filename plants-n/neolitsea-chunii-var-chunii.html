Neolitsea chunii var. chunii

Neolitsea chunii var. chunii, commonly known as the Chinese sweet olive, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a glossy surface with a slightly serrated margin. It produces small, dark blue berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in landscaping due to its aromatic foliage and showy blooms.
