Notylia pentachne

Notylia pentachne, commonly known as the five-awned notylia, is a species of grass native to certain regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, narrow leaves. The plant produces spikelets with five awns, which are bristle-like structures at the ends of the florets. Notylia pentachne typically grows in open, grassy habitats and is adapted to dry or semi-arid conditions.

It plays a minor role in local ecosystems but is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphological features.
