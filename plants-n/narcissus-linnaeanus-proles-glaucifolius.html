Narcissus linnaeanus proles glaucifolius

Narcissus linnaeanus proles glaucifolius, commonly known as the blueleaf daffodil, is a species native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its striking blue-green foliage and large, trumpet-shaped yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny areas and prefers well-drained soils. It blooms in early spring, adding a vibrant touch to gardens and natural landscapes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its unique foliage color.
