Nasturtium obliquum

Nasturtium obliquum, commonly known as the wandering nasturtium, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow or orange flowers and heart-shaped leaves. The plant is an annual herb that thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil. Its flowers are often used in ornamental gardening and have a peppery taste, making them edible.

Nasturtium obliquum is also known for its ability to attract beneficial insects like ladybugs.
