Nepeta laconicea

Nepeta laconicea, commonly known as the Laconica catmint, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or violet flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a minty scent when crushed.

Nepeta laconicea is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
