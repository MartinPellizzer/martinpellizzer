Naegelia fulgida

Naegelia fulgida, commonly known as the golden flame lily, is a striking flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, orange-red blooms that resemble flames. The plant features strap-like, dark green leaves that form a basal rosette, providing a lush backdrop for its showy flowers. Naegelia fulgida thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a standout in both ornamental and ecological settings.
