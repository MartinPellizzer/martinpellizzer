Navia ebracteata

Navia ebracteata, commonly known as the velvetleaf.

It is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. It thrives in moist, warm environments and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Navia ebracteata is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain areas.
