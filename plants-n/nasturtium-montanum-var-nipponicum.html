Nasturtium montanum var. nipponicum

Nasturtium montanum var. nipponicum, commonly known as Japanese watercress, is a flowering plant native to Japan and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit, with slender stems and alternate leaves that are typically heart-shaped or ovate. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its edible leaves and stems are often used in salads and soups, while the flowers can be used as a garnish.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.
