Neoporteria scoparia

Neoporteria scoparia, commonly known as the sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its small, delicate blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal or inland areas with well-drained conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is often used in gardens for its ornamental value.

It is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
