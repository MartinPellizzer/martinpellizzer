Narcissus hispanicus subsp. radinganorum

Narcissus hispanicus subsp.

radinganorum, commonly known as the Radinganorum daffodil, is a subspecies native to southern Europe, particularly Spain and Portugal. It is characterized by its narrow, strap-like leaves and a single, upright flower with a trumpet-shaped corona.

The plant typically blooms in early spring, producing bright yellow petals that contrast with its green foliage. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and borders.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its elegant, early-blooming flowers.
