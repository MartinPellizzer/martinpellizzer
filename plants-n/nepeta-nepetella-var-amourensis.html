Nepeta nepetella var. amourensis

Nepeta nepetella var. amourensis, commonly known as the Amur catmint, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia, particularly China and Russia.

It features aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive scent. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
