Nicotiana collae

Nicotiana collae, commonly known as the colla tobacco, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems, lobed leaves, and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. It produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
