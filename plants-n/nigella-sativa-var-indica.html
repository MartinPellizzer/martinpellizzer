Nigella sativa var. indica

Nigella sativa var. indica, commonly known as black cumin, is an annual herb native to the Mediterranean region and South Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, dark blue or black flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces spherical, three-lobed seed pods that contain numerous tiny, aromatic seeds. These seeds are rich in essential oils, including thymoquinone, which is believed to have various medicinal properties.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a hardy and adaptable crop for temperate climates.
