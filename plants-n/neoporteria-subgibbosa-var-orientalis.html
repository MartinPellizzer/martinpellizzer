Neoporteria subgibbosa var. orientalis

Neoporteria subgibbosa var. orientalis, commonly known as the oriental rock orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia, particularly China and Korea.

It typically grows in rocky or mountainous habitats, thriving in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade. The plant features slender, upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense inflorescences, displaying a range of colors from white to pale pink.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a ground cover in alpine settings.
