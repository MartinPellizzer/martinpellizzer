Neophloga corniculata

Neophloga corniculata, commonly known as the horned bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, seed-like fruits that are dispersed by wind.

Neophloga corniculata is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
