Nicotidendron glauca

Nicotidendron glauca, commonly known as the blue smoke tree, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue-green foliage, which turns a vibrant red in the fall. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers in clusters, which are followed by dark blue, egg-shaped berries. Its leaves are opposite and oval-shaped, with a smooth texture and a waxy coating.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
