Narcissus canariensis

Narcissus canariensis, commonly known as the Canary Island daffodil, is a species of flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow blooms. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is often cultivated in horticultural settings.
