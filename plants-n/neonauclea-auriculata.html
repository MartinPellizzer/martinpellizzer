Neonauclea auriculata

Neonauclea auriculata, commonly known as the golden earleaf.

It is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, characterized by its distinctive auriculate (ear-shaped) leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires ample sunlight to flourish.

Neonauclea auriculata is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique foliage and delicate floral display.
