Neamyza

Neamyza, commonly known as the African violet, is a genus of flowering plants native to tropical Africa.

These plants are characterized by their rosettes of velvety, waxy leaves and showy, clustered flowers that come in a variety of colors. Neamyza species are typically herbaceous, with some varieties growing as shrubs. They prefer well-drained soil and bright, indirect light, making them popular houseplants.

The genus includes over 200 species, many of which are cultivated for their ornamental value.
