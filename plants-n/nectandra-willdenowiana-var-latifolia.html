Nectandra willdenowiana var. latifolia

Nectandra willdenowiana var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed nectandra, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green foliage. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by woody, egg-shaped fruits containing aromatic seeds. Its leaves are broad and elliptical, with a glossy surface and a strong, spicy scent.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its essential oils and medicinal properties.
