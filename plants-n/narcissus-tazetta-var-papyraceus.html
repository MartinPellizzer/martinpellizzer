Narcissus tazetta var. papyraceus

Narcissus tazetta var. papyraceus, commonly known as the Paperwhite narcissus, is a bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of fragrant, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, emerging from the base of the plant. The flowers have a single, trumpet-shaped corona that is typically white or pale yellow.

This variety is well-suited for indoor cultivation and is often used in floral arrangements due to its elegant appearance and pleasant scent.
