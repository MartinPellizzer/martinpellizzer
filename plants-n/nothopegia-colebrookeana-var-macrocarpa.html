Nothopegia colebrookeana var. macrocarpa

Nothopegia colebrookeana var. macrocarpa, commonly known as the giant sago palm, is a variant of the sago palm native to the wet tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, pinnately compound leaves that can reach lengths of up to 6 meters. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by black, spherical fruits. It has a stout, unbranched trunk that is covered with a fibrous, spongy material.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to grow in humid, well-drained soils.
