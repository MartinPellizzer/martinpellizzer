Nepenthes baramensis

Nepenthes baramensis, commonly known as the Baram pitcher plant, is a tropical pitcher plant native to Borneo.

It is characterized by its large, elongated pitcher-shaped leaves that secrete a digestive fluid to trap and digest insects. The plant's unique morphology includes a waxy, glandular surface on its leaves that enhances its ability to capture prey. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in montane rainforests.

Nepenthes baramensis is notable for its ecological role in nutrient cycling within its native habitat.
