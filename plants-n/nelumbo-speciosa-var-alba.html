Nelumbo speciosa var. alba

Nelumbo speciosa var. alba, commonly known as the white lotus, is a striking aquatic plant native to the wetlands of Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom on long, slender stalks above the water's surface. The plant has broad, heart-shaped leaves that float on the water, providing a habitat for various aquatic organisms. Its roots system anchor it in muddy substrates, while its rhizomes store nutrients for growth.

The white lotus is also notable for its ecological importance in supporting biodiversity and its historical significance in traditional medicine and culture.
