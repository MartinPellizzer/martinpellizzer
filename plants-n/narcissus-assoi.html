Narcissus assoi

Narcissus assoi, commonly known as the Asso's daffodil, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to the Iberian Peninsula and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers with a white center. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and produces one to three flowering stems. Its bulbs are adapted to thrive in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy perennial in suitable climates.

The flowers bloom in early spring, adding a vibrant touch to gardens and natural landscapes.
