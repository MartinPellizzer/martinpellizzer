Nepeta vakhanica

Nepeta vakhanica, commonly known as the Siberian catmint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, clustered blue or violet flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage is often fragrant and has been used in traditional medicine for its calming properties.

Nepeta vakhanica is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its ecological benefits in natural habitats.
