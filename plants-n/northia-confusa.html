Northia confusa

Northia confusa, commonly known as the Cape tulip, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal and mountainous regions, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers range in color from white to pink.

Northia confusa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
