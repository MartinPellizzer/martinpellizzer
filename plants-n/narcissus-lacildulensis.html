Narcissus lacildulensis

Narcissus lacildulensis, commonly known as the lacildulensis daffodil, is a rare and endemic species native to a specific region in southern Europe.

It is characterized by its striking yellow petals and a single, erect, trumpet-shaped corona. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils in sunny, open habitats. It blooms in early spring, producing flowers that are both ornamental and ecologically significant.
