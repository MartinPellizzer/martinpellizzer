Narcissus × johnstonii

Narcissus × johnstonii, commonly known as the Johnston daffodil, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between N. tazetta and N. cyclamineus.

It is characterized by its distinctive, often double flowers with a prominent, often colored crown. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with narrow, grass-like leaves. It is native to the Caucasus region and thrives in well-drained soil in full sun.

The flowers bloom in spring, adding vibrant yellow color to gardens and natural landscapes.
