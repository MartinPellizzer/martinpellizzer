Nepeta sibirica

Nepeta sibirica, commonly known as Siberian catmint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features square, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often grayish-green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is valued for its aromatic foliage and is often used in gardens for its ornamental appeal.

Nepeta sibirica is also known for its calming effects on cats, though it is non-toxic to humans.
