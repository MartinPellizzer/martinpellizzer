Nigella garidella

Nigella garidella, commonly known as Persian cowpea, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that range from white to pale yellow. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, with palmate leaves and small, clustered blooms. Its seeds are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice in some cuisines.

Nigella garidella thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, making it a suitable plant for rock gardens and borders.
