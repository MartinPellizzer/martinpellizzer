Niebuhria biflora

Niebuhria biflora, commonly known as the two-flowered bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous habitats, thriving at high altitudes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, adapting to the cool, dry conditions of its environment.

Niebuhria biflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
