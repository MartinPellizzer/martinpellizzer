Nicotiana longiflora var. grandifolia

Nicotiana longiflora var. grandifolia, commonly known as the grandiflora tobacco flower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet, typically blooming in late summer to early autumn. The plant has hairy, alternate leaves that are often lobed or toothed, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous small seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its fragrant flowers and ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and hummingbirds.
