Nepeta tymphestea

Nepeta tymphestea, commonly known as the mountain catmint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue to violet flowers that are clustered in whorls along the stem. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a minty fragrance when crushed.

Nepeta tymphestea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported calming properties.
