Nicotiana tabacum f. fruticosa

Nicotiana tabacum f. fruticosa, commonly known as the shrub tobacco, is a compact, herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its dense, bushy growth habit.

It features small, narrow leaves that are often hairy and arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It is native to the southern United States and thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes grown as a garden plant.
