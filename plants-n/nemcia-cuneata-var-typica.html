Nemcia cuneata var. typica

Nemcia cuneata var. typica, commonly known as the narrow-leafed nemcia, is a species of flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale pink in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
