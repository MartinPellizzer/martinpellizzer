Nicolasia affinis

Nicolasia affinis, commonly known as the narrowleaf nicotiana, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically purple or violet in color, which bloom in the evening. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a mild, slightly sweet scent.

Nicolasia affinis is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil and full sun.
