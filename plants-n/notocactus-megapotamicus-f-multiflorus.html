Notocactus megapotamicus f. multiflorus

Notocactus megapotamicus f. multiflorus, commonly known as the Multiflorous Cactus, is a unique variant of the Notocactus genus native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and dense clusters of small, spiny areoles. The plant produces an array of yellow to orange flowers during the spring and summer months, making it a striking ornamental species. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in controlled settings.
