Naringi alata

Naringi alata, commonly known as the winged naranja, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rutaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its distinctive winged leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit, which is typically a small, oval-shaped berry, contains a high concentration of bioactive compounds.

Naringi alata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
