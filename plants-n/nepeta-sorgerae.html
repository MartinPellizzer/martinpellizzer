Nepeta sorgerae

Nepeta sorgerae, commonly known as the Sorger's catmint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pale blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. Its foliage is often used in traditional herbal remedies, and it is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.

Nepeta sorgerae is also noted for its hardiness and adaptability to various growing conditions.
