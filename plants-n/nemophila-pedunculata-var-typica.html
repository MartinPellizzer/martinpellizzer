Nemophila pedunculata var. typica

Nemophila pedunculata var. typica, commonly known as the common blue-eyed Mary, is a flowering plant native to western North America.

It is a low-growing annual herb with delicate, hairy stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The flowers have five petals, with the upper petal being larger and often marked with a white or yellowish spot. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
