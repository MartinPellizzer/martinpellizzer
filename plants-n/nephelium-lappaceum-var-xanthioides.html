Nephelium lappaceum var. xanthioides

Nephelium lappaceum var. xanthioides, commonly known as the golden rambutan, is a tropical fruit-bearing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a variant of the rambutan species, characterized by its distinctive golden-yellow hairy exterior and sweet, aromatic flesh. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. It produces small, elongated fruits that are typically harvested when fully mature.

The botanical features of this variety make it a unique and commercially valuable addition to tropical agriculture.
