Neochilenia acrocarpa var. fulva

Neochilenia acrocarpa var. fulva, commonly known as the yellow-tipped beard lichen, is a foliose lichen that exhibits a distinctive yellowish hue on its upper surface.

It typically grows on bark and soil in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate regions. The thallus is leathery and measures between 1 to 5 centimeters in diameter, with a central lobed structure. Its reproductive structures, called soredia, are scattered across the surface, aiding in dispersal.

This lichen is notable for its ecological role in soil stabilization and as an indicator of environmental conditions.
