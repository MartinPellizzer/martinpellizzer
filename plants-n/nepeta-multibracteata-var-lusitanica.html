Nepeta multibracteata var. lusitanica

Nepeta multibracteata var. lusitanica, commonly known as the Lusitanian catmint, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features upright, branching stems covered in fine, hairy foliage. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, arranged in whorls along the stems. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
