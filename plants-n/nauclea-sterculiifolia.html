Nauclea sterculiifolia

Nauclea sterculiifolia, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often pinnately arranged and have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by distinctive, woody, bean-like pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Nauclea sterculiifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some cultures.
