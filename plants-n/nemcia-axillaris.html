Nemcia axillaris

Nemcia axillaris, commonly known as the rock fig, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are usually glossy and dark green, often with a waxy coating. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by edible, fig-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in gardens and landscapes in suitable climates.
