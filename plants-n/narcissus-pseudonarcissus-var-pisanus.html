Narcissus pseudonarcissus var. pisanus

Narcissus pseudonarcissus var. pisanus, commonly known as the Pisan daffodil, is a variety of the double daffodil characterized by its large, showy, double flowers.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is native to southern Europe, particularly the Mediterranean region. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces pendulous, yellow-orange blooms with a prominent central crown. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is well-suited to well-drained soil and full sun.

The flowers typically bloom in early spring, adding a vibrant touch to gardens during the transition from winter to summer.
