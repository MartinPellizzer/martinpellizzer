Nepsera glandulosa

Nepsera glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular nepsera, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous species characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in small, glandular dots. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Nepsera glandulosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
