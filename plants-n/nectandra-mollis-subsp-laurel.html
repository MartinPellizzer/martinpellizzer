Nectandra mollis subsp. laurel

Nectandra mollis subsp.

laurel, commonly known as the West Indian laurel, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Caribbean and northern South America. It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes other aromatic plants like cinnamon and bay laurel.

The plant is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruits are small, dark berries that contain seeds, and the tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.

The bark and leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported antiseptic and analgesic effects.
