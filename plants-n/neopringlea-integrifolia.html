Neopringlea integrifolia

Neopringlea integrifolia, commonly known as the smooth-leaved neopringlea, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, entire leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
