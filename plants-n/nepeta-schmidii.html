Nepeta schmidii

Nepeta schmidii, commonly known as Schmid's catmint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of central Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage is often fragrant and can be used in herbal teas or as a natural insect repellent.

Nepeta schmidii is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
