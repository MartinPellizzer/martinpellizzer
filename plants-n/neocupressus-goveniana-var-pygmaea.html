Neocupressus goveniana var. pygmaea

Neocupressus goveniana var. pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf Mexican cypress, is a compact evergreen conifer native to central Mexico.

It features scale-like leaves arranged in crossed pairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, rounded cones that mature to a reddish-brown color. It is well-suited for rock gardens and small spaces due to its slow growth and compact form.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
