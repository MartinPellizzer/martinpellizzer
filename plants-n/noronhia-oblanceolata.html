Noronhia oblanceolata

Noronhia oblanceolata, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall. The plant has simple, alternate, ovate leaves that are often lobed and have a glossy surface. Its small, white flowers are clustered in panicles, and it produces small, red, fleshy fruits that are edible.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some medicinal uses in traditional medicine.
