Najas baldwinii

Najas baldwinii, commonly known as the Baldwin's watermilfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Najadaceae and is characterized by its whorled arrangement of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or brown, which develop underwater. It thrives in freshwater environments such as lakes and slow-moving streams.

Due to its rapid growth, it can become invasive in certain regions, impacting local aquatic ecosystems.
