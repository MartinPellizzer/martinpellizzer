Neoregelia burlemarxii subsp. meeana

Neoregelia burlemarxii subsp.

meeana, commonly known as the Emerald Beauty, is a striking bromeliad native to Brazil. It is characterized by its vibrant green, strap-like leaves that form a rosette shape, creating a compact and visually appealing plant.

The plant produces striking orange-red flowers that emerge from the center of the rosette, adding a dramatic contrast to its foliage. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires moderate humidity and well-draining soil.

This species is popular among bromeliad enthusiasts for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
