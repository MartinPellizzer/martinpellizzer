Narcissus cernuus

Narcissus cernuus, commonly known as the nodding daffodil, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its drooping, yellow flowers with a distinctive cup-shaped corona. The plant typically grows to a height of about 20 to 40 centimeters, with narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Narcissus cernuus is native to southern Europe and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
