Neomammillaria lewisiana

Neomammillaria lewisiana, commonly known as the Lewis's mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in white to yellowish tubercles, which are small, rounded growths. The plant produces delicate, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is a slow-growing species that thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
