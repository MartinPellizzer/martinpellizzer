Nymphanthus muriculatus

Nymphanthus muriculatus, commonly known as the rough nymphaea, is a species of water lily native to Southeast Asia.

It features broad, floating leaves with a rough, waxy texture and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in ponds and marshes. Its flowers open in the early morning and close by midday, a behavior known as nyctinasty.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
