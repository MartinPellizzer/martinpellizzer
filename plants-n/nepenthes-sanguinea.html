Nepenthes sanguinea

Nepenthes sanguinea, commonly known as the crimson pitcher plant, is a carnivorous plant native to Borneo.

It is characterized by its striking red, lidless pitcher-shaped traps that secrete a sticky liquid to lure and trap insects. The plant's vibrant coloration is due to high concentrations of anthocyanin pigments. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires nutrient-poor soil.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it a fascinating subject in botanical studies.
