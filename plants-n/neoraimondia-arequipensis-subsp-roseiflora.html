Neoraimondia arequipensis subsp. roseiflora

Neoraimondia arequipensis subsp.

roseiflora, commonly known as the Arequipa rose-flowered plant, is a species native to the Andean regions of Peru. It is a shrub characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage.

The plant produces showy, rose-like flowers that are typically pink to reddish in color. Its leaves are usually elliptical and covered with a fine layer of white pubescence.

This subspecies is adapted to high-altitude environments and thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.
