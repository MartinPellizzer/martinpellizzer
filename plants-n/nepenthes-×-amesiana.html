Nepenthes × amesiana

Nepenthes × amesiana, commonly known as the hybrid pitcher plant, is a tropical vine native to the rainforests of Borneo.

It is a natural hybrid between Nepenthes alata and Nepenthes rafflesiana, exhibiting a combination of traits from both parent species. The plant produces large, colorful pitcher-shaped traps that secrete a sticky liquid to trap and digest insects. Its leaves are broad and glossy, adapted for efficient photosynthesis in the dense forest understory.

The hybrid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by plant enthusiasts.
