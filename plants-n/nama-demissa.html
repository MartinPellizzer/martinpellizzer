Nama demissa

Nama demissa, commonly known as the desert lily, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb with succulent, fleshy leaves adapted to store water. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, blooming during the rainy season. Its root system is deep and robust, allowing it to access groundwater in dry conditions.

Nama demissa is often found in rocky soils and is well-suited to survive in harsh, drought-prone environments.
