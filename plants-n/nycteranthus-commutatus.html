Nycteranthus commutatus

Nycteranthus commutatus, commonly known as the night-flowering morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy, night-blooming flowers that are typically white or pale purple in color. The plant belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is known for its rapid growth habit and strong, twisting stems. Its flowers emit a sweet fragrance at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

The leaves are heart-shaped and often covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
