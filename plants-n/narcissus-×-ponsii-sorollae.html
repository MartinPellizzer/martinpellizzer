Narcissus × ponsii-sorollae

Narcissus × ponsii-sorollae, commonly known as the hybrid daffodil, is a cross between Narcissus ponsii and Narcissus sorrolla.

It is a bulbous perennial plant characterized by its distinctive yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with strap-like, grassy leaves. Its flowers are often fragrant and come in various shades of yellow, making it a popular choice for gardens.

This hybrid is valued for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to different soil types.
