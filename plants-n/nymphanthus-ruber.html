Nymphanthus ruber

Nymphanthus ruber, commonly known as the red sand cherry, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, double pink to red flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Nymphanthus ruber is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
