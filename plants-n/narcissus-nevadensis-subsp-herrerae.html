Narcissus nevadensis subsp. herrerae

Narcissus nevadensis subsp.

herrerae, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada daffodil, is a flowering plant native to the western United States, particularly California. It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and a single, upright flower with a yellow perianth.

The flower typically blooms in early spring, showcasing a distinctive cup-shaped corona. This subspecies is adapted to grow in rocky, well-drained soils in mountainous regions.

It plays an important role in the local ecosystem and is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
