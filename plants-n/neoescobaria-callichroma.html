Neoescobaria callichroma

Neoescobaria callichroma, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a member of the Asparagaceae family and is native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a succulent perennial that produces striking, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant has a compact, cushion-like growth form with fleshy, blue-green leaves that help it store water. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and drought-tolerant landscapes.
