Nesogordonia fouassieri

Nesogordonia fouassieri, commonly known as the African tamarind, is a deciduous tree native to the savannas of West Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The tree produces long, flat pods containing edible seeds, which are used in traditional cuisine. Its wood is valued for its durability and is used in construction and furniture making.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
