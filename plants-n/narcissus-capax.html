Narcissus capax

Narcissus capax, commonly known as the broadleaf daffodil, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers with a yellow perianth and a prominent, often cup-shaped corona. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and is native to parts of Europe and western Asia. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, and it blooms in early spring.

Narcissus capax is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens.
