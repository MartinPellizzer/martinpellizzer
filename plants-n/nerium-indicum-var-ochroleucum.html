Nerium indicum var. ochroleucum

Nerium indicum var. ochroleucum, commonly known as the white oleander, is a variant of the oleander plant characterized by its pale to white flowers.

It is a shrub or small tree native to southern Asia, particularly India and the Himalayas. The plant features opposite, simple, and often lance-shaped leaves that are usually green with a slightly waxy texture. Its flowers are typically clustered in terminal inflorescences and bloom in summer.

The white oleander is adapted to a variety of soil types but thrives in well-drained, sunny conditions.
