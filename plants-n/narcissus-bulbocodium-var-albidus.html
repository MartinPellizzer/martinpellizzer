Narcissus bulbocodium var. albidus

Narcissus bulbocodium var. albidus, commonly known as the white bulbous narcissus, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its small, white, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces a bulbous root system that stores nutrients for growth. It typically blooms in early spring and prefers well-drained, sandy soils.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after addition to rock gardens and woodland settings.
