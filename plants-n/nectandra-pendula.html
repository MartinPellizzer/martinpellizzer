Nectandra pendula

Nectandra pendula, commonly known as the hanging sandarac tree, is a tropical evergreen species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its drooping branches and fragrant, yellowish wood. The plant produces small, white flowers that are often clustered in panicles, followed by spherical fruits containing single seeds. Its leaves are alternate, elliptical, and have a distinctive aromatic scent.

Nectandra pendula is valued for its durable wood and is often found in lowland forests.
