Nectandra nigra

Nectandra nigra, commonly known as black nectandra, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes other aromatic plants like cinnamon and avocado. The tree is characterized by its dark, aromatic bark and glossy, elliptical leaves. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically appearing in axillary inflorescences.

The fruit is a small, woody drupe that contains a single seed, and the plant is valued for its medicinal and aromatic properties.
