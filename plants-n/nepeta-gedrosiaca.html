Nepeta gedrosiaca

Nepeta gedrosiaca, commonly known as the Afghan cat's-paw, is a hardy perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, often attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, adapted to thrive in arid and rocky environments.

Nepeta gedrosiaca is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in challenging climates.
