Nepeta elata

Nepeta elata, commonly known as catmint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils that give it a minty scent and make it popular in herbal teas.

Nepeta elata is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
