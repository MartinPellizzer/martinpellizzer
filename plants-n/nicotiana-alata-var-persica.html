Nicotiana alata var. persica

Nicotiana alata var. persica, commonly known as the Persian nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep purple, often appearing at night. The plant belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and peppers. It typically grows as an annual herb with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves.

Its flowers emit a strong, sweet fragrance, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths.
