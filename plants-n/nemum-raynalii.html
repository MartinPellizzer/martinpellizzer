Nemum raynalii

Nemum raynalii, commonly known as the Raynal's nemum, is a rare and enigmatic plant species native to the high-altitude regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, translucent leaves that exhibit a unique iridescence under direct sunlight. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pale blue and violet, which bloom during the early morning hours. Its root system is highly specialized, allowing it to thrive in nutrient-poor soils and extreme climatic conditions.

Due to its limited habitat and slow growth rate, Nemum raynalii is considered critically endangered and is the subject of ongoing botanical research.
