Nomochloa turbinata

Nomochloa turbinata, commonly known as the turbinate moss, is a species of moss belonging to the family Pottiaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and dark green to blackish-green coloration. The plant forms turbinated (turbinate) capsules that are typically curved and covered with a peristome. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found on rocks or soil in temperate regions.

Nomochloa turbinata is notable for its resilience to desiccation, making it a common component of montane and alpine vegetation.
