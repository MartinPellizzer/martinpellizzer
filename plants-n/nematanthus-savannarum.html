Nematanthus savannarum

Nematanthus savannarum, commonly known as the Red-veined Ice Plant, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its fleshy, succulent leaves and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments. It is often used in rock gardens and as a decorative plant due to its attractive appearance and drought tolerance.

The species is also valued for its ability to bloom throughout the year under proper care.
