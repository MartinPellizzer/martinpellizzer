Neoregelia correia-araujoi

Neoregelia correia-araujoi, commonly known as the Brazilian rainbow plant, is a species of bromeliad native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage that displays a range of hues, including red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its rosette-shaped leaves form a central cup that collects rainwater, providing a habitat for small aquatic organisms.

Neoregelia correia-araujoi is valued in horticulture for its striking appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
