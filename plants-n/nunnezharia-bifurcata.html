Nunnezharia bifurcata

Nunnezharia bifurcata, commonly known as the forked leaf fern, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive bifurcated fronds, which split into two branches at the base, giving it a unique appearance. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree trunks or branches in its natural habitat. Its leaves are broad and leathery, with a dark green color and a slightly serrated edge.

Nunnezharia bifurcata thrives in high humidity and indirect light, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
