Notholaena doradilla

Notholaena doradilla, commonly known as the Brazilian wallaby grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are often clustered at the base of the plant.

Notholaena doradilla is noted for its resilience to drought and its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
