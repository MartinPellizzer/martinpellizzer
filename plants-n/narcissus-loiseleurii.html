Narcissus loiseleurii

Narcissus loiseleurii, commonly known as the Loiseleur's daffodil, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to the Iberian Peninsula and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers with a distinctive trumpet-shaped corona. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–50 cm, with narrow, grass-like leaves. It blooms in early spring, producing clusters of 4–6 flowers per stem.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and delicate appearance.
