Nymphaea × robinsonii

Nymphaea × robinsonii, commonly known as the Robinson's water lily, is a hybrid aquatic plant belonging to the Nymphaeaceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that float on the water's surface, supported by long, slender petioles. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that can be white, pink, or purple, depending on the variety. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in ponds and lakes.

Nymphaea × robinsonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in aquatic ecosystems.
