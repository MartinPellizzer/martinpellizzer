Nototriche mandoniana

Nototriche mandoniana, commonly known as the Mandon's nototriche, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains in South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in high-altitude environments, often found in rocky or alpine regions. Its leaves are adapted to reduce water loss, a feature essential for survival in its harsh climate.

Nototriche mandoniana is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
