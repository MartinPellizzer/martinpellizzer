Nudilus paradoxus

Nudilus paradoxus, commonly known as the paradoxical naked fern, is a unique species characterized by its lack of traditional fronds.

Instead, it features a delicate, leafless stem that appears almost skeletal, giving it an otherworldly appearance. Botanically, it belongs to the family Polypodiaceae, which includes various epiphytic and terrestrial ferns. The plant thrives in shaded, humid environments, often growing on tree trunks or rocky surfaces.

Its reproductive structures are concealed within small, inconspicuous spore cases, making it challenging to study in the wild.
