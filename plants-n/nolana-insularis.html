Nolana insularis

Nolana insularis, commonly known as the Easter Island nolana, is a flowering plant native to the island of Rapa Iti, also known as Easter Island.

It is a small shrub that typically grows in rocky and coastal habitats. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers during the summer months. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in a fine, white pubescence.

Nolana insularis is considered a rare and endemic species, facing threats from habitat loss and invasive species.
