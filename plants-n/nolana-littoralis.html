Nolana littoralis

Nolana littoralis, commonly known as the coastal nolana, is a flowering plant native to the western coast of South America.

It is a low-growing shrub that thrives in coastal environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It has adapted to withstand salt spray and strong coastal winds.

Nolana littoralis plays an important role in stabilizing dunes and supporting local biodiversity.
