Nilgirianthus beddomei

Nilgirianthus beddomei, commonly known as the Nilgiri bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Nilgirianthus beddomei is of interest to botanists due to its unique floral structure and ecological significance in its habitat.
