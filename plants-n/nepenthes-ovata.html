Nepenthes ovata

Nepenthes ovata, commonly known as the broad-leafed pitcher plant, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, ovate-shaped pitcher traps that are often green or red in color. The plant's leaves are broad and waxy, with a prominent midrib that supports the formation of the pitchers. These pitchers secrete a digestive fluid that attracts and digests insects, providing the plant with essential nutrients.

Nepenthes ovata is a popular species in botanical gardens due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
