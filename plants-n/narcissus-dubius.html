Narcissus dubius

Narcissus dubius, commonly known as the Spanish daffodil, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa, thriving in rocky and coastal environments. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers with bright yellow petals and a white or greenish throat. It typically blooms in early spring, and its bulbs are adapted to survive in dry conditions.

Narcissus dubius is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant display.
