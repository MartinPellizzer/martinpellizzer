Nicotiana caudata

Nicotiana caudata, commonly known as the long-tailed tobacco, is a species native to South America.

It is a herbaceous plant that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Nicotiana caudata is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its striking floral display.
