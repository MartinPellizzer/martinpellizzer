Nigella aristata

Nigella aristata, commonly known as the Persian buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountains of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and has hairy, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

The seeds of Nigella aristata are often used in cooking and have a slightly bitter, peppery flavor.
