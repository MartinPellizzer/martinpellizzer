Nabaluia clemensii

Nabaluia clemensii, commonly known as the yellow-flowered lily, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow petals and tall, slender stems that can reach up to two meters in height. The plant produces large, showy flowers that bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a waxy coating that helps retain moisture in its humid habitat.

Nabaluia clemensii is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its role in traditional medicine in its native regions.
