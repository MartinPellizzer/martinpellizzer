Nepeta souliei

Nepeta souliei, commonly known as the Chinese catmint, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in traditional Chinese medicine for their calming properties.

Nepeta souliei thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.
