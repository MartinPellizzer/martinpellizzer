Narcissus malacitanus

Narcissus malacitanus, commonly known as the Malacitan daffodil, is a species native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is a bulbous plant characterized by its distinctive yellow petals and a central, trumpet-shaped corona. The leaves are long and grass-like, typically emerging in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This plant is known for its hardiness and ability to naturalize over time.
