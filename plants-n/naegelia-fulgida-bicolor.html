Naegelia fulgida bicolor

Naegelia fulgida bicolor, commonly known as the two-color sunflower, is a striking plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its vibrant, bicolor petals that typically feature a yellow center with a red or orange outer edge. The plant produces tall, sturdy stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, supporting its large, showy flowers. Its foliage consists of heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are slightly hairy to the touch.

Naegelia fulgida bicolor is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
