Nepeta dentata

Nepeta dentata, commonly known as catnip, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite leaves that are serrated and aromatic. The plant produces small, clustered blue or purple flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves contain nepetalactone, a compound that influences the behavior of cats.

Nepeta dentata is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
