Nauclea lanceolata

Nauclea lanceolata, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces elliptical, woody pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savannas and woodland areas.

Nauclea lanceolata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
