Nepeta alii

Nepeta alii, commonly known as the catmint, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite leaves that are often gray-green in color. The plant produces small, fragrant blue or purple flowers in whorled clusters, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Nepeta alii is also known for its mild minty aroma and its historical use in herbal remedies.
