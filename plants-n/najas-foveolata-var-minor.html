Najas foveolata var. minor

Najas foveolata var. minor, commonly known as the small pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It is characterized by its slender, ribbon-like leaves that are typically less than 1 cm in width. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually found in clusters near the water's surface. It thrives in slow-moving to still waters with soft, muddy substrates.

Due to its rapid growth, it can become a dominant species in nutrient-rich ponds and lakes.
