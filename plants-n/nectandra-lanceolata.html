Nectandra lanceolata

Nectandra lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf sandarac tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and fragrant resinous sap. The plant is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes. It typically grows in humid lowland forests and can reach heights of up to 30 meters.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
