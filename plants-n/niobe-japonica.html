Niobe japonica

Niobe japonica, commonly known as Japanese niobe, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin, while its stems are often reddish in color.

Niobe japonica is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and parks.
