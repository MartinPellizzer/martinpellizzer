Neomammillaria candida

Neomammillaria candida, commonly known as the white-tipped hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are often ribbed and covered with white, woolly spines. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is appreciated by cactus enthusiasts and collectors.
