Neottia Longicaulis

Neottia longicaulis is a rare and enigmatic orchid found in the shaded, moist environments of temperate forests. This small, terrestrial plant is known for its delicate, pale greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. It belongs to the orchid family, which includes over 25,000 species worldwide, but Neottia longicaulis is particularly elusive and hard to locate. The plant is often mistaken for a weed due to its unassuming appearance, yet it plays a vital role in its ecosystem. Conservation efforts are crucial to protect this fragile species from habitat loss and environmental changes.
