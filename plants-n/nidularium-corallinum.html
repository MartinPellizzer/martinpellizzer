Nidularium corallinum

Nidularium corallinum, commonly known as the coral-like fern, is a unique plant belonging to the family Nidulariaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, coral-like structures that resemble branching coral, giving it its common name. The plant produces spores in small, clustered sporangia located at the tips of its filaments. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its intricate morphology makes it a distinctive and visually appealing specimen in botanical collections.
