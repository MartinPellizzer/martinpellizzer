Narcissus × carringtonii nothosubsp.

carringtonii, commonly known as the hybrid daffodil, is a botanical hybrid resulting from the cross between Narcissus bulbocodium and Narcissus tazetta. It is characterized by its large, showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to white.

The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with strap-like, grassy leaves. It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

This nothosubspecies is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and ease of cultivation.
