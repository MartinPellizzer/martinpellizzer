Nipponobambusa sadoensis

Nipponobambusa sadoensis, commonly known as the Sado bamboo, is a species of flowering bamboo native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that are typically green to yellow-green in color. The plant grows in clusters and produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a smooth texture.

This bamboo is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
