Nigella cretica

Nigella cretica, commonly known as Cretan cumin, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, oval-shaped seeds that are often used as a spice in traditional cuisines. Its botanical features include compound leaves and a short, hairy stem.

Nigella cretica thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.
