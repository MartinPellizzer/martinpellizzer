Notogrammitis angustifolia

Notogrammitis angustifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaf wall-rush, is a small, evergreen fern native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia and Australia.

It features delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are typically less than 10 centimeters long, arranged in a compact, upright clump. The fronds are usually bright green and have a smooth, leathery texture. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found growing on rocky outcrops or in forest undergrowth.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in humid climates.
