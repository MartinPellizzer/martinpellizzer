Nigrina

Nigrina, commonly known as the black nightshade, is a flowering plant belonging to the Solanaceae family.

It is native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia, and it thrives in temperate climates. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are toxic to humans and animals. Its leaves are ovate with a hairy texture, and it has showy, star-shaped flowers that bloom in the summer.

Nigrina is often found in disturbed soils and can be a weed in agricultural areas.
