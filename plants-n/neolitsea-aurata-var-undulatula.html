Neolitsea aurata var. undulatula

Neolitsea aurata var. undulatula, commonly known as the wavy-leaf sandarac tree, is a variant of the sandarac tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree characterized by its elliptical, wavy-edged leaves that are usually dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by woody capsules containing aromatic resin. Its leaves and bark contain compounds used in traditional medicine and as a source of natural resins.

The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.
