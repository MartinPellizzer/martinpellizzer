Noticastrum argentinense

Noticastrum argentinense, commonly known as the Argentine tamarind, is a small evergreen tree native to South America, particularly Argentina and Uruguay.

It belongs to the Leguminosae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The tree produces small, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds, which are often used in local cuisines.

Its ability to thrive in arid conditions makes it an important plant in ecological restoration projects.
