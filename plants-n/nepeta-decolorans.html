Nepeta decolorans

Nepeta decolorans, commonly known as catmint, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal remedies and are also favored by domestic cats for their stimulating effects.

Nepeta decolorans thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile addition to gardens.
