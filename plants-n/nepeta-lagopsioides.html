Nepeta lagopsioides

Nepeta lagopsioides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved catmint, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, fragrant blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Nepeta lagopsioides is also valued for its aromatic properties and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
