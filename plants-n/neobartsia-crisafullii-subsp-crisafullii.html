Neobartsia crisafullii subsp. crisafullii

Neobartsia crisafullii subsp.

crisafullii, commonly known as the Sicilian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Sicily. It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, with a distinctive white throat.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun. It is often found in rocky or coastal areas, where it adapts to the harsh environmental conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
