Nerophila gentianoides

Nerophila gentianoides, commonly known as the slender gentian, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils in woodland or swampy areas. It has slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture.

Its striking flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it a valuable addition to native gardens.
