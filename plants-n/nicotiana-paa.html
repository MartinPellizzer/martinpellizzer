Nicotiana paa

Nicotiana paa, commonly known as the nightshade tobacco, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant is characterized by its large, showy flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its leaves are typically broad and hairy, with a strong, aromatic scent.

Nicotiana paa is also known for containing nicotine, a potent alkaloid used in both medicinal and commercial applications.
