Nemitis setacea

Nemitis setacea, commonly known as the hairy tamarisk, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, needle-like leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Nemitis setacea is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Its botanical features make it a hardy and resilient plant suitable for xeriscaping and ecological restoration projects.
