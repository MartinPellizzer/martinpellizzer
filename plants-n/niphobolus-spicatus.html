Niphobolus spicatus

Niphobolus spicatus, commonly known as the spike spurge, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is characterized by its erect, sparsely branched stems and small, clustered yellow flowers. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered with fine hairs. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks.

The plant produces a milky sap that is a common feature of the Euphorbiaceae family.
