Nardurus subulatus

Nardurus subulatus, commonly known as the slender nardus, is a perennial grass-like plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves that are typically arranged in dense tufts. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikelets, typical of the grass family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Nardurus subulatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
