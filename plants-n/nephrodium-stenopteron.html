Nephrodium stenopteron

Nephrodium stenopteron, commonly known as the narrow-leaved nephroid, is a rare fern species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Nephrodiaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, finely divided fronds that are typically less than 30 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical rainforests or along stream banks. Its reproductive structures include sporangia that produce spores, which are dispersed by wind to propagate the species.

Due to habitat destruction, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
