Nepeta ucranica subsp. kopetdaghensis

Nepeta ucranica subsp.

kopetdaghensis, commonly known as the Kopetdagh catmint, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic foliage.

The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid environments.

Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their calming properties.
