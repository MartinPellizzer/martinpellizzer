Nyssa villosa

Nyssa villosa, commonly known as the hairy tallowtree, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Cornaceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, followed by bright red berries that are an important food source for wildlife.

Nyssa villosa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
