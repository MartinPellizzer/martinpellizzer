Nectandra membranacea f. floribunda

Nectandra membranacea f. floribunda, commonly known as the fragrant evergreen tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, often emitting a sweet scent. Its wood is valued for its durability and aromatic properties, making it useful in traditional crafts and construction.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.
