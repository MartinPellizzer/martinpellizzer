Nephelium xanthioides

Nephelium xanthioides, commonly known as the white rambutan, is a tropical fruit-bearing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is closely related to the rambutan and longan. The plant produces small, hairy fruits that turn white when mature, with a sweet, aromatic pulp. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The tree is valued not only for its edible fruit but also for its ornamental qualities.
