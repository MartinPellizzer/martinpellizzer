Narcissus × herminii

Narcissus × herminii, commonly known as the Herminii daffodil, is a hybrid plant that results from the cross between N. tazetta and N. cyclamineus.

It is characterized by its elegant, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to yellow, often with a distinctive greenish or reddish tinge at the base. The plant typically grows to a height of 15 to 30 centimeters, with narrow, strap-like leaves that are grassy in appearance. It is a hardy perennial that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The flowers bloom in early spring, adding a touch of delicate beauty to the landscape.
