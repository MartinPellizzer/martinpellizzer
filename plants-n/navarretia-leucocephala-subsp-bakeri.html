Navarretia leucocephala subsp. bakeri

Navarretia leucocephala subsp.

bakeri, commonly known as Baker's whiteflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a member of the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its white, showy flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils in open habitats such as chaparral and grasslands. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its resilience to harsh environmental conditions.
