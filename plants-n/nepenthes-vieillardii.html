Nepenthes vieillardii

Nepenthes vieillardii, commonly known as the giant pitcher plant, is a tropical carnivorous plant native to Borneo.

It is characterized by its large, modified leaves that form deep, water-filled pitchers to trap and digest insects. The plant's pitchers are typically red or orange in color, with a waxy coating that aids in attracting prey. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires consistent moisture and nutrient-poor soil.

Its unique adaptations make it a fascinating subject in the study of plant evolution and ecological interactions.
