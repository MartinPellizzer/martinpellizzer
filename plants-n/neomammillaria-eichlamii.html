Neomammillaria eichlamii

Neomammillaria eichlamii, commonly known as the Eichlamii Pincushion Cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are often covered in white or pale blue spines. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers during the warmer months. It is a slow-growing species that thrives in well-draining soil and full sun.

Due to its unique appearance and compact size, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor cactus collections.
