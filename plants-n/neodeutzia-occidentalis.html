Neodeutzia occidentalis

Neodeutzia occidentalis, commonly known as the western neodeutzia, is a deciduous shrub native to western North America.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are typically gray-green in color and have a slightly fuzzy texture. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in clusters during the spring, usually in shades of white or pale pink. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Neodeutzia occidentalis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
