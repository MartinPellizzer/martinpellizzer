Nephrodium stegnogramme

Nephrodium stegnogramme, commonly known as the velvet fern, is a unique plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage that gives it its common name. The plant has a rosette growth habit, with leaves that are thick and fleshy, adapted to store water in its arid environment. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically appearing in spring.

The plant is also noted for its ability to thrive in poor soils and withstand periods of drought.
