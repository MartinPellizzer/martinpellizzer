Neolaugeria lineolata

Neolaugeria lineolata, commonly known as the lineolate tree, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows in humid, lowland forests. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often elliptical in shape and have a smooth or slightly serrated margin. Its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, and it produces woody capsules as fruit.

The tree is valued for its hard, durable wood and is sometimes used in local crafts and construction.
