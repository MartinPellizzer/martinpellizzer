Nepeta wettsteinii

Nepeta wettsteinii, commonly known as Wettstein's catmint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic, square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and borders.

Nepeta wettsteinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and low maintenance requirements.
