Nesogenes

Nesogenes, commonly known as the "ghost tree," is a rare and enigmatic plant species found in specific tropical rainforest ecosystems.

The plant exhibits a symbiotic relationship with certain fungi, which aid in nutrient absorption from the nutrient-poor soil. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically blooming during the wet season.

Nesogenes plays a crucial ecological role in maintaining the biodiversity of its habitat.
