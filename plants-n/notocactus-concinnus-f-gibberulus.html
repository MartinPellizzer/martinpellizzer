Notocactus concinnus f. gibberulus

Notocactus concinnus f. gibberulus, commonly known as the gibberulous notocactus, is a unique cactus species native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are covered in a dense layer of white, waxy hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the evening, adding a striking contrast to its otherwise muted appearance. Its growth habit is typically solitary or in small clusters, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and somewhat challenging plant to cultivate outside its native range.
