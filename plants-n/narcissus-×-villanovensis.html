Narcissus × villanovensis

Narcissus × villanovensis, commonly known as the Spanish daffodil, is a hybrid species within the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to white. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Narcissus × villanovensis is native to southern Europe and is valued for its early spring blooms.
