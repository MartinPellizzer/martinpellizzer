Nectandra impressa

Nectandra impressa, commonly known as the broadleaf sandarac tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with a distinctive waxy texture. The tree produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by oval-shaped fruits containing aromatic seeds. Its wood is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional crafts and incense.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.
