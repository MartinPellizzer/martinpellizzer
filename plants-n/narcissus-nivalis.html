Narcissus nivalis

Narcissus nivalis, commonly known as the snowdrop, is a perennial bulbous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, nodding flowers that typically bloom in late winter or early spring. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white or greenish-yellow flowers with a single stamen. It thrives in woodland or shady areas and is often found growing in moist, well-drained soil.

The snowdrop is notable for its ability to bloom even in cold conditions, making it a symbol of hope and resilience.
