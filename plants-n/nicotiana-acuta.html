Nicotiana acuta

Nicotiana acuta, commonly known as the acute tobacco plant, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family.

It is native to Central and South America and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, yellowish-green fruits that contain seeds. It is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ecological studies.
