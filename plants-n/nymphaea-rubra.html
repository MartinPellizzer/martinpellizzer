Nymphaea rubra

Nymphaea rubra, commonly known as the red water lily, is a aquatic flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features showy, vibrant red petals that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has broad, floating leaves that provide shade and oxygen to the water. Its roots are submerged, anchoring it in the mud at the bottom of ponds and lakes.

Nymphaea rubra plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by supporting biodiversity and maintaining water quality.
