Nothocestrum subcordatum

Nothocestrum subcordatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southern United States.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptical in shape, with a hairy texture. Its flowers are typically white or pale blue, clustered in umbels, and it produces small, round berries that contain the seeds.

This species is often found in moist, disturbed soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
