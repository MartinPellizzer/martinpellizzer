Nama aretioides var. californica

Nama aretioides var. californica, commonly known as the California arete, is a species of flowering plant in the family Polygonaceae.

It is native to the coastal regions of California, where it thrives in sandy or gravelly soils. The plant features upright, branched stems with lance-shaped leaves that have a distinctive waxy coating. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense inflorescences, and typically bloom in shades of pink or red.

It is often used in coastal restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize dunes and tolerate salt spray.
