Nephrodium lasiopteris

Nephrodium lasiopteris, commonly known as the velvet fern, is a unique plant native to the wetlands of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage that gives it its common name. The plant has a creeping rhizome system that allows it to spread across moist soil surfaces. Its leaves are small, overlapping, and covered in fine, soft hairs.

Nephrodium lasiopteris thrives in waterlogged environments and is often found in marshy areas along rivers and lakes.
