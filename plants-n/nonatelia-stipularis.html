Nonatelia stipularis

Nonatelia stipularis, commonly known as the Brazilian star-of-Bethlehem, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, star-shaped white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features strap-like, narrow leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Nonatelia stipularis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
