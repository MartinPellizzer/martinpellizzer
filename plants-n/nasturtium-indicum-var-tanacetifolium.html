Nasturtium indicum var. tanacetifolium

Nasturtium indicum var. tanacetifolium, commonly known as the variegated nasturtium, is a hybrid cultivar of the nasturtium plant.

It is characterized by its distinctive foliage, which features a mix of green and yellow variegation. The plant produces vibrant, edible flowers in shades of orange and red, often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are also edible and have a peppery taste, similar to watercress.

This variety thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking both ornamental and edible plants.
