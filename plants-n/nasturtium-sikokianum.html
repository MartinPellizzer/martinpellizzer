Nasturtium sikokianum

Nasturtium sikokianum, commonly known as the Sikokian nasturtium, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a distinctive red or purple blotch at the base. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a slightly peppery taste, making it edible in small quantities.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and culinary uses.
