Nectandra reticulata

Nectandra reticulata, commonly known as the reticulated sandalwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its aromatic, dark-colored wood that is highly valued in traditional craftsmanship. The plant produces small, white flowers that are followed by spherical, woody fruits containing smooth, black seeds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a rough texture with a strong, spicy scent.

Nectandra reticulata is also noted for its ecological importance in providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
