Nepeta tuberosa var. brevisquama

Nepeta tuberosa var. brevisquama, commonly known as catmint, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features aromatic, fuzzy leaves and produces spikes of blue or lavender flowers. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often used in gardens for its ornamental value. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil.

Nepeta tuberosa var. brevisquama is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
