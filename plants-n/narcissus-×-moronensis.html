Narcissus × moronensis

Narcissus × moronensis, commonly known as the Moronic daffodil, is a hybrid plant that results from the cross between Narcissus tazetta and Narcissus poeticus.

It is characterized by its large, showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in a range of yellow to white colors. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with strap-like leaves that are grassy in appearance. It is native to the Iberian Peninsula and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant spring blooms.
