Nymphaea lotus var. australis

Nymphaea lotus var. australis, commonly known as the Australian water lily, is a water-dwelling plant native to the wetlands of Australia.

It features broad, heart-shaped leaves that float on the water's surface, supported by long, slender petioles. The flowers are typically white or pink, opening in the early morning and closing by midday. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in aquatic ecosystems.
