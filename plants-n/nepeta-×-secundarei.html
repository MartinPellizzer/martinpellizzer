Nepeta × secundarei

Nepeta × secundarei, commonly known as catmint, is a hybrid plant in the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its aromatic, mint-scented leaves and clusters of blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage is often used in herbal teas and as a companion plant in gardens.

Nepeta × secundarei is known for its hardiness and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
