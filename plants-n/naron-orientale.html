Naron orientale

Naron orientale, commonly known as the Eastern Naron, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, fragrant white to pale yellow flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly serrated edge, providing a distinctive appearance. This plant thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
