Nectandra nitidula var. latifolia

Nectandra nitidula var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved nectandra, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves that are glossy and often have a distinct midrib. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by spherical, woody fruits containing aromatic seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its timber and has some traditional medicinal uses in local communities.
