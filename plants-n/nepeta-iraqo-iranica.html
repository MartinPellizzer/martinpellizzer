Nepeta iraqo-iranica

Nepeta iraqo-iranica, commonly known as the Persian catmint, is a perennial herb native to parts of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue to violet in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rocky or dry gardens.

Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its mild sedative and aromatic properties.
