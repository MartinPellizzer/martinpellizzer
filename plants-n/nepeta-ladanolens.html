Nepeta ladanolens

Nepeta ladanolens, commonly known as catnip, is a flowering plant in the mint family (Lamiaceae).

It is native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. The plant is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite leaves that are often gray-green in color. Its small, clustered flowers are typically blue or purple, and it produces aromatic, glandular leaves that release a strong scent when crushed.

Nepeta ladanolens is known for its effects on cats and its use in traditional herbal medicine.
