Notocactus schumannianus

Notocactus schumannianus, commonly known as the Schumann's notocactus, is a species of cactus native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stem that often branches into a tree-like structure. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers during the summer months. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it has a shallow root system adapted to arid conditions.

It is well-suited for cultivation in warm, sunny climates with well-draining soil.
