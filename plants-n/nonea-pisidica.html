Nonea pisidica

Nonea pisidica, commonly known as the Crimean bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Crimean Peninsula.

It features slender, upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of pale blue to violet flowers with five petals, typical of the Campanulaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and sunny to partially shaded environments.

Nonea pisidica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
