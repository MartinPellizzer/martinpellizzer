Nierembergia pulchella var. macrocalyx

Nierembergia pulchella var. macrocalyx, commonly known as the large-flowered bellflower, is a perennial plant native to South America.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, often with a dark eye at the center. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is relatively low maintenance.

The species is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
