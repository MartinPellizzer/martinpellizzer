Notocactus bezrucii var. centrispinus

Notocactus bezrucii var. centrispinus, commonly known as the Centrispine Notocactus, is a rare cactus species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stem and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom during the warmer months. The plant has spiny, ribbed stems that help it conserve water in arid environments. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun exposure for optimal growth.

Due to its limited distribution and unique appearance, it is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its ornamental value.
