Nipponocalamus lanatus

Nipponocalamus lanatus, commonly known as the Japanese feather bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, arching stems and feathery, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests and stream sides. Its foliage is often covered in a fine, silvery bloom, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Nipponocalamus lanatus is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
