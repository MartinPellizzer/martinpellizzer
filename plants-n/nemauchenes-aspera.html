Nemauchenes aspera

Nemauchenes aspera, commonly known as the roughleaf sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its stiff, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in small, rough textures. This species is often found in wet or moist environments such as marshes and swamps. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, three-lobed flowers.

Nemauchenes aspera plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
