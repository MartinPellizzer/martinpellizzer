Narcissus sexlobatus

Narcissus sexlobatus, commonly known as the six-lobed daffodil, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers with six petals, which are divided into lobes, giving it its scientific name. The plant is native to southern Europe and is often found in woodland and scrub habitats. It blooms in spring, producing clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers.

Its foliage consists of strap-like leaves that are narrow and grass-like in appearance.
