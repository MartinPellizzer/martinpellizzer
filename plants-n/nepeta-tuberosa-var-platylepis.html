Nepeta tuberosa var. platylepis

Nepeta tuberosa var. platylepis, commonly known as catmint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features square, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a strong minty aroma. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and as a source of nectar for wildlife.
