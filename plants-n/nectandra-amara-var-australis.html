Nectandra amara var. australis

Nectandra amara var. australis, commonly known as the Australian bitter ash, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia and northern Australia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its dark, aromatic bark and glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers followed by dark, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects.
