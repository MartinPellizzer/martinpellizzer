Narcissus albulus

Narcissus albulus, commonly known as the white narcissus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Europe.

It produces showy, cup-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, often with a greenish tinge. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders. Its bulbs are ornamental and can be propagated through division or by seed.

This species is valued for its early spring bloom and delicate appearance.
