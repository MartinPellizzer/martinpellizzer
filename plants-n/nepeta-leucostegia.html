Nepeta leucostegia

Nepeta leucostegia, commonly known as white catmint, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue or white flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are typically gray-green and have a mint-like scent when crushed.

Nepeta leucostegia is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
