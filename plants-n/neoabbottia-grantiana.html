Neoabbottia grantiana

Neoabbottia grantiana, commonly known as the Grant's neoabbottia, is a striking orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant features a single, erect inflorescence that bears several blooms, each with a distinctive, spurred lip. Its leaves are elongated and lance-shaped, arranged in a rosette at the base of the stem.

Neoabbottia grantiana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded, humid environments.
