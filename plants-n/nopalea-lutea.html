Nopalea lutea

Nopalea lutea, commonly known as the yellow prickly pear, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and vibrant yellow flowers. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are often used in local cuisines. Its succulent stems store water, making it well adapted to dry environments.

Nopalea lutea plays an important ecological role by providing food and shelter for various desert animals.
