Nasturtium laciniatum

Nasturtium laciniatum, commonly known as the cutleaf nasturtium, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, deeply lobed leaves and vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and prefers well-drained soil in full sun. Its flowers bloom from late spring to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is also valued for its edible leaves and seeds, which have a peppery flavor similar to watercress.
