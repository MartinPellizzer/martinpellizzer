Navarretia tenuifolia

Navarretia tenuifolia, commonly known as slenderleaf navarretia, is a low-growing annual plant native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. It has a fibrous root system and produces numerous small seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Navarretia tenuifolia is often found in coastal and inland regions with cool, temperate climates.
