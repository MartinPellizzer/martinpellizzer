Nymphaea lutea var. affinis

Nymphaea lutea var. affinis, commonly known as the yellow water lily, is a aquatic plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that float on the water's surface, supported by long, slender petioles. The flowers are typically yellow or orange, with five petals and a prominent yellow center, blooming in summer. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes.

It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
