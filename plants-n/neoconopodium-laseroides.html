Neoconopodium laseroides

Neoconopodium laseroides, commonly known as the laser leaf plant, is a unique succulent native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, waxy, and translucent leaves that resemble laser beams, giving it its common name. The plant has a compact, rosette-like growth habit and is often cultivated for its ornamental appearance. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat, it is considered a threatened species in its native range.
