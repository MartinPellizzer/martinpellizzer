Nicotiana mendocina

Nicotiana mendocina, commonly known as the Mendoza tobacco, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its distinctive violet to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats and produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It has been historically used by indigenous peoples for various purposes, including medicinal and ritual applications.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ecological studies.
