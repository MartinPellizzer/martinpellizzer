Nymphaea alba var. sphaerocarpa

Nymphaea alba var. sphaerocarpa, commonly known as the white water lily, is a aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features large, floating leaves that are typically rounded and smooth, with a waxy surface. The flowers are white, with a yellow center, and bloom in the early morning. The plant has a rhizomatous root system that anchors it in muddy substrates.

It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and oxygen to water bodies.
