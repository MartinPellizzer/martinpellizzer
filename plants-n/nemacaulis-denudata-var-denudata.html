Nemacaulis denudata var. denudata

Nemacaulis denudata var. denudata, commonly known as the naked stem dandelion, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, hairless stems and small, yellow composite flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly rough texture.

It plays a role in supporting pollinators and is considered a pioneer species in ecological succession.
