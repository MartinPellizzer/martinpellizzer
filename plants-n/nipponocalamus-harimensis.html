Nipponocalamus harimensis

Nipponocalamus harimensis, commonly known as the Harima bamboo, is a rare and endangered species native to Japan.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, culm-like stems that grow in dense clumps. The plant typically reaches a height of 1 to 2 meters and features narrow, linear leaves with a smooth texture. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in mountainous regions with well-drained soil.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is highly valued in botanical conservation efforts.
