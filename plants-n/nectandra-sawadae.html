Nectandra sawadae

Nectandra sawadae, commonly known as the Philippine sandalwood, is a tropical tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its aromatic, fragrant wood. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a glossy surface. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically greenish-white in color, and it produces small, dark brown fruits.

The tree is valued for its timber and has traditional uses in local crafts and medicine.
