Nepeta pseudokokanica

Nepeta pseudokokanica, commonly known as the Kashmiri catmint, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported calming and antispasmodic properties.

Nepeta pseudokokanica is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in rocky, well-drained soils.
