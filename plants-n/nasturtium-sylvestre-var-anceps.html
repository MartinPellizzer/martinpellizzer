Nasturtium sylvestre var. anceps

Nasturtium sylvestre var. anceps, commonly known as the wild nasturtium, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other mustard family plants. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and bright orange or yellow flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its stems are hairy, and it often grows in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks.

The plant is also noted for its peppery taste, making it a popular edible plant in some regions.
