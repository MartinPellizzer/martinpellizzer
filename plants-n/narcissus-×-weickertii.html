Narcissus × weickertii

Narcissus × weickertii, commonly known as the Weickertii daffodil, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Narcissus tazetta and Narcissus poeticus.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to white, often with a distinctive blotch at the base. The plant typically grows to a height of 20–30 cm, featuring narrow, grass-like leaves and a short stem. It is a perennial bulbous plant that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and valuable for horticultural study.
