Nipponocalamus koriyamensis

Nipponocalamus koriyamensis, commonly known as the Koriyama bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems and narrow leaves. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its culms are often used in traditional crafts and as a source of fiber.

Nipponocalamus koriyamensis is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various organisms.
