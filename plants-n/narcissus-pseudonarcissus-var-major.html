Narcissus pseudonarcissus var. major

Narcissus pseudonarcissus var. major, commonly known as the Giant Crowned Narcissus, is a variety of daffodil characterized by its large, showy flowers.

It features a tall, sturdy stem that can reach up to 1 meter in height, supporting a single, large, cup-shaped bloom. The petals are broad and usually yellow, with a distinctive greenish center and a prominent, crown-like ring at the base of the petals. This variety is native to southern Europe and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

It is a popular ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and long blooming period in spring.
