Nymphaea abbreviata

Nymphaea abbreviata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved water lily, is a species of aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes. Its leaves are typically rounded with a waxy surface that repels water, while its roots anchor it in the muddy substrate.

Nymphaea abbreviata plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various water organisms.
