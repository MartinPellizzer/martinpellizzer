Nicotiana longiflora var. acutiflora

Nicotiana longiflora var. acutiflora, commonly known as the sharp-flowered tobacco plant, is a species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The leaves are typically broad and lobed, with a rough texture. This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its vibrant blooms and fragrance.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for both gardeners and botanists.
