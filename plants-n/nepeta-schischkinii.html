Nepeta schischkinii

Nepeta schischkinii, commonly known as Siberian catmint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, upright stems covered in soft, hairy foliage. The plant produces small, fragrant blue or purple flowers that bloom in whorls along the stems. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a mint-like scent when crushed.

Nepeta schischkinii is often grown as an ornamental plant for its attractive appearance and wildlife value.
