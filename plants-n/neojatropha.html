Neojatropha

Neojatropha, commonly known as the physic nut, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Euphorbiaceae.

It consists of several species, most notably Neojatropha curcas, which is native to tropical regions of the Americas. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, clustered flowers. Its fruit is a capsule that contains seeds with a high oil content, making it valuable for biodiesel production.

Neojatropha is also known for its toxic properties, particularly in its seeds and latex, which contain compounds like curcin.
