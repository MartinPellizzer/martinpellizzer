Narcissus bulbocodium var. citrinus

Narcissus bulbocodium var. citrinus, commonly known as the lemon yellow daffodil, is a bulbous plant native to southern Europe.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers with a prominent, trumpet-shaped corona. The plant typically blooms in early spring and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

This variety is closely related to other daffodil species within the Amaryllidaceae family.
