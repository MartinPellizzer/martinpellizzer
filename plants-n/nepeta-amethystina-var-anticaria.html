Nepeta amethystina var. anticaria

Nepeta amethystina var. anticaria, commonly known as the purple catmint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems covered in dense, gray-green foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, pale purple to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are aromatic and often used in herbal teas and as a natural insect repellent.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
