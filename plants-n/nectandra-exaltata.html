Nectandra exaltata

Nectandra exaltata, commonly known as the fragrant wood or sweet wood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its aromatic, reddish-brown wood that emits a pleasant scent when cut. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a glossy appearance. It produces small, clustered flowers followed by dark purple berries, which are an important food source for various wildlife.

Nectandra exaltata is valued for its timber and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
