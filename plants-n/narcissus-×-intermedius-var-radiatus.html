Narcissus × intermedius var. radiatus

Narcissus × intermedius var. radiatus, commonly known as the radiate daffodil, is a hybrid species within the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically bright yellow with a white or cream center. The plant features narrow, strap-like leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

This variety is prized for its early blooming habit and ornamental value in spring gardens.
