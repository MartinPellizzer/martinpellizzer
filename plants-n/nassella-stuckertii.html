Nassella stuckertii

Nassella stuckertii, commonly known as the Stuckert's needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its fine, needle-like leaves and compact, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky, or sandy soils and is often found in open grasslands and mountainous regions. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
