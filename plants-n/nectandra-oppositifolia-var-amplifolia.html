Nectandra oppositifolia var. amplifolia

Nectandra oppositifolia var. amplifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed sandarac tree, is a tropical evergreen species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, opposite, elliptical leaves that are often broad and glossy. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by woody, aromatic fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The tree is valued for its sap, which has been used traditionally for its resinous properties.
