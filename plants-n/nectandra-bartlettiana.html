Nectandra bartlettiana

Nectandra bartlettiana, commonly known as the Bartlett's sand-wood, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its wood is valued for its durability and is often used in construction and furniture-making.
