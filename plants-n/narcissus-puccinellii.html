Narcissus puccinellii

Narcissus puccinellii, commonly known as the marsh daffodil, is a flowering plant native to wetland areas of Europe and western Asia.

It produces showy, yellow flowers with a distinctive cup-shaped corona. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in moist, sandy soils. It is often found in coastal regions and is adapted to salt-tolerant environments.

Narcissus puccinellii is a perennial herb that blooms in early spring.
