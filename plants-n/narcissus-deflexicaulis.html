Narcissus deflexicaulis

Narcissus deflexicaulis, commonly known as the drooping daffodil, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and clusters of bright yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and blooms in early spring. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from the base of the plant.

Narcissus deflexicaulis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and public spaces.
