Narcissus flavus

Narcissus flavus, commonly known as the yellow narcissus, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa, typically found in woodland and meadow habitats. The plant produces showy, single flowers with a bright yellow perianth that is often adorned with a greenish or brownish stripe. Its bulbs are perennial, and it blooms in early spring, adding vibrant color to its natural environment.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
