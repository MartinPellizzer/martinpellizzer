Narcissus pseudonarcissus f. pleniflorus

Narcissus pseudonarcissus f. pleniflorus, commonly known as the double yellow daffodil, is a cultivar of the genus Narcissus characterized by its distinctive floral structure.

It features a large, rounded, double corona that surrounds a yellow, trumpet-shaped perianth. The plant produces multiple blooms per stem, enhancing its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, typical of many daffodil species.

This cultivar is prized for its vibrant color and showy appearance, making it a popular choice for spring gardens.
