Narcissus × pacensis

Narcissus × pacensis, commonly known as the Pacific daffodil, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between N. tazetta and N. poeticus.

It is native to the western United States, particularly California, and thrives in coastal and Mediterranean climates. The plant features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange, with a distinctive, often recurved tepal arrangement. It produces a single stem with several blooms, typically blooming in early spring.

The leaves are long, narrow, and grass-like, forming a basal rosette.
