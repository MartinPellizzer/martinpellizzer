Nepeta racemosa subsp. racemosa

Nepeta racemosa subsp.

racemosa, commonly known as catnip, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa. It features square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers arranged in dense terminal clusters, which bloom in summer. It is closely related to other members of the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is known for its aromatic foliage. Nepeta racemosa subsp.

racemosa is widely cultivated for its medicinal properties and as an attractive garden plant.
