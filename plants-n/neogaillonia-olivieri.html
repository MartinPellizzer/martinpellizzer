Neogaillonia olivieri

Neogaillonia olivieri, commonly known as the olive tree of Madagascar, is a species native to the island nation of Madagascar.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves and fragrant, pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically green when mature. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sunlight.

Neogaillonia olivieri is valued for its ornamental appeal and has cultural significance in its native region.
