Nesaea radicans

Nesaea radicans, commonly known as the creeping nesaea, is a low-growing perennial plant native to parts of Asia.

It features small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces clusters of tiny, greenish-white flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread easily and form dense mats.
