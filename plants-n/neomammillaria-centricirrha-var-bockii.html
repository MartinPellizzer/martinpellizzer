Neomammillaria centricirrha var. bockii

Neomammillaria centricirrha var. bockii, commonly known as the Bock's clustered milk barrel cactus, is a compact, low-growing cactus native to Mexico.

It features a globular to cylindrical stem with prominent, white to pale green tubercles. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking contrast to its green body. It is well-suited for rock gardens or as a container plant due to its compact size and ornamental appeal.

This variety is drought-tolerant and requires minimal care, making it an ideal choice for succulent enthusiasts.
