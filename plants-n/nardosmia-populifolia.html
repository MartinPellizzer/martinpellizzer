Nardosmia populifolia

Nardosmia populifolia, commonly known as the sweet fern or sweet woodruff, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, fern-like leaves that emit a sweet, vanilla-like scent when crushed. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense spikes, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal remedies for their soothing properties.

Nardosmia populifolia thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
