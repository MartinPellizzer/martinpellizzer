Nocca helianthifolia var. levior

Nocca helianthifolia var. levior, commonly known as the slender sunflower vine, is a climbing plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, hairy stems that twine around supports, and its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a hairy texture. The plant produces yellow flowers that resemble those of sunflowers, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Its botanical characteristics make it an attractive addition to gardens, though it can be invasive in some regions.
