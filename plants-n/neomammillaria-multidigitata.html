Neomammillaria multidigitata

Neomammillaria multidigitata, commonly known as the multi-digited milk barrel cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are covered in white, woolly spines, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Due to its unique morphology and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for desert gardens and rockeries.
