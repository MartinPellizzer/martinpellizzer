Nephropetalum

Nephropetalum, commonly known as the "moonflower," is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night and close by dawn. The plant belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is known for its rapid growth and ability to climb using its tendrils. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a velvety texture.

The flowers emit a strong, sweet scent to attract nocturnal pollinators like moths.
