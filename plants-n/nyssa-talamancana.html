Nyssa talamancana

Nyssa talamancana, commonly known as the Talamancan sweetgum, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the family Nyssaceae and is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves and showy, fragrant white flowers. The tree produces small, spherical fruits that contain smooth, black seeds. It is often found in moist, well-drained soils in tropical and subtropical regions.

Nyssa talamancana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
