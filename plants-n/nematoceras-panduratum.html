Nematoceras panduratum

Nematoceras panduratum, commonly known as the panduratum orchid, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, pendulous inflorescences and intricate floral structures. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories where it relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake. Its flowers exhibit a range of colors, including white, pink, and purple, and are known for their fragrant scent.

Nematoceras panduratum is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
