Neuracanthus thymifolius

Neuracanthus thymifolius, commonly known as the thyme-leaved rock cress, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, adding a splash of color to its habitat. Its leaves are aromatic, with a scent reminiscent of thyme, contributing to its common name.

Neuracanthus thymifolius is often found in dry, sunny areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
