Nunnezharia aurantiaca

Nunnezharia aurantiaca, commonly known as the red-flowered orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, vibrant orange-red blooms that appear during the warmer months. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention.

The flowers are highly fragrant, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
