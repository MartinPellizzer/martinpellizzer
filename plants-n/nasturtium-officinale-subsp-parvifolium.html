Nasturtium officinale subsp. parvifolium

Nasturtium officinale subsp.

parvifolium, commonly known as the dwarf nasturtium, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America. It features heart-shaped, glossy leaves and produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers.

The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often cultivated as an ornamental or edible plant. Its flowers and young leaves are edible, with a peppery flavor similar to watercress.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.
