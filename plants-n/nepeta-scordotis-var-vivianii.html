Nepeta scordotis var. vivianii

Nepeta scordotis var. vivianii, commonly known as the Vivian's catmint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant produces small, tubular blue to purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically gray-green and have a mint-like scent when crushed.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
