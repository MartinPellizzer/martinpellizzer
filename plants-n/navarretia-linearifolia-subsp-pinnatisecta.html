Navarretia linearifolia subsp. pinnatisecta

Navarretia linearifolia subsp.

pinnatisecta, commonly known as the slenderleaf navarretia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its delicate, linear leaves that are finely divided, giving it a feathery appearance.

The species produces small, white to pale pink flowers in clusters during the spring. It thrives in rocky soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

This subshrub is adapted to Mediterranean climates with cool, wet winters and dry summers.
