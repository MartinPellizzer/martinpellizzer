Neopieris

Neopieris, commonly known as the golden aster, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow to orange composite flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plants typically have narrow, grass-like leaves and are native to regions in the Americas. Neopieris species are often found in open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.

They are valued in horticulture for their ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
