Nepeta podostachys var. paulsenii

Nepeta podostachys var. paulsenii, commonly known as the Paulsen's catmint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright, branching stems covered in dense, hairy foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a strong, mint-like aroma.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained, sunny conditions.
