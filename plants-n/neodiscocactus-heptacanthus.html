Neodiscocactus heptacanthus

Neodiscocactus heptacanthus, commonly known as the seven-spined discocactus, is a unique cactus species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its flattened, disc-like stems that are ribbed and adorned with prominent spines. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warm season, adding a delicate touch to its otherwise spiky appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xerophytic gardens.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in controlled environments.
