Nepeta origanifolia

Nepeta origanifolia, commonly known as caraway catmint, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are aromatic and often used in herbal remedies.

Nepeta origanifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
