Nicarago

Nicarago, commonly known as the Nicaraguan orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are adapted to absorb nutrients from the forest floor, and it often relies on specific symbiotic relationships with fungi.

The botanical study of Nicarago provides insights into the biodiversity and ecological dynamics of Central American flora.
