Neolobivia vilcabambae

Neolobivia vilcabambae, commonly known as the Vilcabamba cactus, is a species of cactus native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes in Peru.

It is characterized by its columnar stem and spiny, paddle-shaped leaves, which help it survive in arid and cold environments. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking visual element to its natural habitat. Neolobivia vilcabambae is adapted to thrive in rocky soils and can withstand extreme temperature fluctuations.
