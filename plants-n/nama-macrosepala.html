Nama macrosepala

Nama macrosepala, commonly known as the giant water fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, floating fronds that can reach up to several meters in length. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Nama macrosepala plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and oxygen for various aquatic organisms.
