Nepeta brachyantha

Nepeta brachyantha, commonly known as catmint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage is often used in herbal remedies due to its mild mint-like scent.

Nepeta brachyantha is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
