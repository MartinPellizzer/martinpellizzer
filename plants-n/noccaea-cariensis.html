Noccaea cariensis

Noccaea cariensis, commonly known as the Carpathian watercress, is a flowering plant native to the Carpathian region of Europe.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cress species. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils along streams and springs. It has small, alternate leaves and produces small white or pink flowers.

Noccaea cariensis is valued for its peppery flavor and is sometimes used in traditional cuisine.
