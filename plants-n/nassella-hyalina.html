Nassella hyalina

Nassella hyalina, commonly known as the silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-gray blades that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, spikelet-bearing branches.

Nassella hyalina is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive, feathery foliage.
