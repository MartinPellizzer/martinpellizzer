Nectandra punctata var. elongata

Nectandra punctata var. elongata, commonly known as the long-leaved sandarac, is a species native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which are followed by woody, aromatic fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The tree is valued for its aromatic resin, which has been used in traditional medicine and incense making.
