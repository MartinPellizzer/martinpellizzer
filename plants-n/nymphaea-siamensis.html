Nymphaea siamensis

Nymphaea siamensis, commonly known as the Siamese water lily, is a species of aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Nymphaeaceae family and is characterized by its broad, floating leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats such as ponds and lakes. Its flowers are usually pink to white, with a prominent yellow center, and bloom during the day.

Nymphaea siamensis plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
