Narcissus × alentejanus var. knochei

Narcissus × alentejanus var. knochei, commonly known as the Alentejan daffodil, is a hybrid cultivar originating from the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with narrow, grass-like leaves. It is a hardy perennial that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant spring blooms.
