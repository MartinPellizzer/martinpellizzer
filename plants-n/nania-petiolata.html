Nania petiolata

Nania petiolata, commonly known as the slender leaf bellflower, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its flowers are often visited by pollinators like bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
