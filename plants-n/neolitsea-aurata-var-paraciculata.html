Neolitsea aurata var. paraciculata

Neolitsea aurata var. paraciculata, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It features compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets that are often tinged with golden or reddish hues. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by bright orange or red fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for landscaping in warm regions.
