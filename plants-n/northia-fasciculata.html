Northia fasciculata

Northia fasciculata, commonly known as the silver cotyledon, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, silver-gray leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments. Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting.

Northia fasciculata produces small, white flowers in the spring, adding a delicate touch to its appearance.
