Neopycnocoma lancifolia

Neopycnocoma lancifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pygmy date palm, is a small, evergreen palm native to Madagascar.

It features a slender, unbranched trunk with a fibrous sheath and produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant's leaves are pinnately compound, with narrow, linear leaflets that give it a delicate appearance. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Neopycnocoma lancifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
