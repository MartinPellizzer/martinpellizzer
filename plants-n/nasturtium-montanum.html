Nasturtium montanum

Nasturtium montanum, commonly known as mountain nasturtium, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of South America.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant features bright orange or yellow flowers and heart-shaped leaves, which are often used in herbal remedies. Its edible parts, including leaves and flowers, have a peppery taste similar to watercress.

Nasturtium montanum is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and as a natural pest deterrent in gardens.
