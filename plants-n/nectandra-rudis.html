Nectandra rudis

Nectandra rudis, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its aromatic, dark-colored wood. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically greenish-white in color.

Nectandra rudis is valued for its durable and highly prized timber, which has been used historically in furniture and decorative arts.
