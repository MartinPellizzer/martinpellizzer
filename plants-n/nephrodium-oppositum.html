Nephrodium oppositum

Nephrodium oppositum, commonly known as the Indian nettle, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its opposite arrangement of leaves, which are typically heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in spikes, and it is often found in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves contain irritant chemicals, making them a common cause of skin irritation when touched.

The plant is also known for its use in traditional medicine for various ailments.
