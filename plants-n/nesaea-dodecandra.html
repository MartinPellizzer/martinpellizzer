Nesaea dodecandra

Nesaea dodecandra, commonly known as the African star jasmine, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, featuring opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white flowers with a star-like appearance, blooming throughout the year in warm climates. Its roots are fibrous and often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
