Notocactus uebelmannianus f. gilviflorus

Notocactus uebelmannianus f. gilviflorus, commonly known as the golden flowered notocactus, is a rare and striking cactus native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stem and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom during the dry season. The plant has spiny, ribbed stems that help it conserve water in its arid habitat. Its flowers are often highly fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and moths.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a valuable species in botanical collections.
