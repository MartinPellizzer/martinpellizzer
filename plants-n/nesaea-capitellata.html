Nesaea capitellata

Nesaea capitellata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rock orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant features a short, erect stem with a dense cluster of small, greenish-white flowers at the apex. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often arranged in a basal rosette.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique floral structure and adapted morphology for survival in harsh conditions.
