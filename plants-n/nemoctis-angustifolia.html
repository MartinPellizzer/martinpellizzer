Nemoctis angustifolia

Nemoctis angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf nemoctis, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of tubular, violet-blue flowers. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun exposure. Its ornamental value is enhanced by its striking floral display and compact growth habit.

Nemoctis angustifolia is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its aesthetic appeal and ecological significance.
