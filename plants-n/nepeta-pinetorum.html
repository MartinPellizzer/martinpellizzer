Nepeta pinetorum

Nepeta pinetorum, commonly known as mountain catmint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage and spikes of blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are often used in herbal teas and have a mint-like scent.

Nepeta pinetorum is also valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
