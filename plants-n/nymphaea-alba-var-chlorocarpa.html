Nymphaea alba var. chlorocarpa

Nymphaea alba var. chlorocarpa, commonly known as the white water lily variant with green fruit, is a aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features broad, heart-shaped leaves that float on the water surface, while its flowers are typically white with yellow stamens. The plant produces green, fleshy fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by water currents. Its botanical structure includes a rhizomatous root system that anchors it in muddy substrates.

The species is notable for its ecological role in aquatic ecosystems and its ornamental value in water gardens.
