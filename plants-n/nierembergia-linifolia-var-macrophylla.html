Nierembergia linifolia var. macrophylla

Nierembergia linifolia var. macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaved bellflower, is a species native to South America, particularly Chile.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sun. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

This variety is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
