Neotinea maculata f. luteola

Neotinea maculata f. luteola, commonly known as the yellow-flowered lady's mantle, is a striking ornamental plant characterized by its distinctive yellow inflorescences.

It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America. The plant features basal leaves that are palmately lobed, with a hairy texture that gives them a velvety appearance. Its flowers are clustered in dense, showy heads, often surrounded by bracts that enhance its visual appeal.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental value in gardens and is often used in rock gardens or as a border plant.
