Narcissus angustifolius

Narcissus angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved daffodil, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and western Asia, typically growing in woodland and meadow habitats. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers with a single petal and a yellow or white corona. Its narrow, grass-like leaves are arranged in a basal rosette, and it blooms in early spring.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, early-flowering display.
