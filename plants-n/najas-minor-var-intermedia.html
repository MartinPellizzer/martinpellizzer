Najas minor var. intermedia

Najas minor var. intermedia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved watermilfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It belongs to the family Najadaceae and is characterized by its slender, ribbon-like leaves that are typically less than 1 millimeter in width. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged, followed by buoyant, seed-filled fruits that aid in dispersal. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Due to its rapid growth, it can become invasive in some regions, altering water quality and competing with native vegetation.
