Nectandra lineatifolia

Nectandra lineatifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf sandarac tree, is a tropical evergreen species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurel and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are often lined with distinct veins. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by dark purple or black berries.

The tree is valued for its aromatic wood and has been used in traditional medicine and as a source of natural resins.
