Nicotiana turcica

Nicotiana turcica, commonly known as Turkish tobacco, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant produces striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in the production of traditional tobacco products.

Nicotiana turcica is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in herbal medicine.
