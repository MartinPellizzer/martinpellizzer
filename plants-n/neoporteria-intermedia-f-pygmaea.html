Neoporteria intermedia f. pygmaea

Neoporteria intermedia f. pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf form of the common tamarisk, is a compact shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, needle-like leaves that are typically a vibrant green, adapted to thrive in arid and saline conditions. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in coastal and sandy environments.
