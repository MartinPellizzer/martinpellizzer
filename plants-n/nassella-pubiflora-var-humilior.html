Nassella pubiflora var. humilior

Nassella pubiflora var. humilior, commonly known as the lowland bottlebrush grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It features slender, upright stems and produces dense, bottlebrush-like inflorescences that are typically pink to reddish in color. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate various environmental conditions.
