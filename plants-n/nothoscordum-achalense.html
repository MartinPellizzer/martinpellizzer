Nothoscordum achalense

Nothoscordum achalense, commonly known as the Achalense garlic flower, is a flowering plant native to South America, specifically found in Argentina and Uruguay.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, umbellate inflorescences composed of numerous white to pale blue flowers. The plant features strap-like, narrow leaves and produces bulbous structures similar to onions, though it is not closely related to true garlic or onions. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though its conservation status remains uncertain.
