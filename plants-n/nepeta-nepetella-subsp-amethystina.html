Nepeta nepetella subsp. amethystina

Nepeta nepetella subsp.

amethystina, commonly known as the purple catmint, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its slender stems and clusters of small, lavender to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has lance-shaped, aromatic leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This subspecies is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
