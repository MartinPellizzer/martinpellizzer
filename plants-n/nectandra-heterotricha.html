Nectandra heterotricha

Nectandra heterotricha, commonly known as the Amazonian sandarac tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark-colored wood that is highly valued for its durability and scent. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by elliptical, leathery fruits. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a distinctive aromatic fragrance.

Nectandra heterotricha plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
