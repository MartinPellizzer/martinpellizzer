Neobesseya missouriensis subsp. asperispina

Neobesseya missouriensis subsp.

asperispina, commonly known as the Missouri prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the summer.

The plant has a succulent structure, allowing it to store water efficiently in arid environments. Its subspecies name, asperispina, refers to the rough and spiny nature of its stems.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food and habitat for various desert and semi-desert species.
