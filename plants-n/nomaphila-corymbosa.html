Nomaphila corymbosa

Nomaphila corymbosa, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its clusters of bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of blue and violet. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of hair, which helps reduce water loss.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
