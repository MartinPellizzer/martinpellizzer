Nasa campaniflora

Nasa campaniflora, commonly known as the bellflower of the Andes, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in a fine, silvery pubescence.

Nasa campaniflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
