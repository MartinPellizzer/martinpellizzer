Nephrodium athamanticum

Nephrodium athamanticum, commonly known as the sacred fern, is a rare and ancient plant with a unique botanical structure.

It belongs to the family Nephrodiaceae and is characterized by its intricate, lacy fronds that resemble the shape of a kidney. The plant exhibits a complex life cycle involving both sporophytic and gametophytic stages. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its historical significance in traditional medicine and its delicate morphology make it a subject of botanical interest.
