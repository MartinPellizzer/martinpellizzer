Nephelium gardneri

Nephelium gardneri, commonly known as the mangostan, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The fruit is a striking red or purple, segmented berry that is rich in antioxidants and nutrients. The plant thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its ornamental value, combined with its edible fruit, makes it a popular choice in both horticulture and agriculture.
