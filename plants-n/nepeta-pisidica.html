Nepeta pisidica

Nepeta pisidica, commonly known as Turkish catmint, is a perennial herb native to Turkey and surrounding regions.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their calming properties.

Nepeta pisidica is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature.
