Nectandra glabrescens

Nectandra glabrescens, commonly known as the white sand tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic foliage and smooth, often white- or pale-barked trunk. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a glossy appearance, while its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences.

Nectandra glabrescens is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
