Nasturtium pyrenaicum

Nasturtium pyrenaicum, commonly known as the Pyrenean watercress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous plants. The plant is characterized by its small, edible leaves that have a peppery, slightly spicy flavor. It produces yellow or orange flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Nasturtium pyrenaicum thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in stream banks and rocky habitats.
