Neltuma alba

Neltuma alba, commonly known as the white neltuma, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, white petals and small, clustered flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in cloud forests. Its leaves are elongated and covered in a fine, silvery hairs, which help reflect excess sunlight.

Neltuma alba is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
