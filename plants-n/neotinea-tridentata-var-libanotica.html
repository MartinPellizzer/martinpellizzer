Neotinea tridentata var. libanotica

Neotinea tridentata var. libanotica, commonly known as the Lebanon thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a robust root system and basal rosettes of gray-green, lanceolate leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces tall, slender flowering stems that bear purple to pinkish-purple composite flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in local ecosystems and a subject of study in botany and ecology.
