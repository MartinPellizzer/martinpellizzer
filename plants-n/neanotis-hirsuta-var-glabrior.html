Neanotis hirsuta var. glabrior

Neanotis hirsuta var. glabrior, commonly known as the smoothleaf neanotis, is a variant of the Neanotis hirsuta species, which belongs to the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its reduced hair coverage compared to the typical variety, giving it a smoother appearance. The plant typically features compound leaves with a pinnate structure, and its flowers are small and clustered in umbels. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

This variant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
