Nahuatlea magna

Nahuatlea magna, commonly known as the giant nahuatl flower, is a striking member of the Malvaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central America and features large, showy blooms that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter. The plant is characterized by its broad, waxy leaves and vibrant, often pink or red petals. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections worldwide.
