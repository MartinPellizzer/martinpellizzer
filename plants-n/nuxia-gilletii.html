Nuxia gilletii

Nuxia gilletii, commonly known as the Cape snowbush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage and clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. It produces small, round, edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine.

Nuxia gilletii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in arid environments.
