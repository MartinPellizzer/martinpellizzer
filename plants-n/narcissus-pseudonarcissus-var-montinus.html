Narcissus pseudonarcissus var. montinus

Narcissus pseudonarcissus var. montinus, commonly known as the mountain daffodil, is a variant of the common daffodil native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers with a white or pale yellow corona, typically blooming in early spring. The plant features strap-like, narrow leaves that are grassy in texture, and it grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. It prefers well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, long-lasting blooms.
