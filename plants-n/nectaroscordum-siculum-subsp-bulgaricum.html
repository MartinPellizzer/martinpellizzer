Nectaroscordum siculum subsp. bulgaricum

Nectaroscordum siculum subsp.

bulgaricum, commonly known as the Balkan nectaroscordum, is a bulbous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula. It belongs to the family Amaryllidaceae and is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant produces narrow, linear leaves and has a distinctive, garlic-like scent. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers sunny to partially shaded locations.
