Nepeta kotschyi

Nepeta kotschyi, commonly known as Persian catmint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces spikes of blue to purple flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their calming properties.

Nepeta kotschyi is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in rocky or well-drained soils.
