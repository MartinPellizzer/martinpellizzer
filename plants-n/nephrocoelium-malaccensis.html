Nephrocoelium malaccensis

Nephrocoelium malaccensis, commonly known as the Malacca kidney bean, is a climbing legume native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods containing edible seeds that are often used in traditional cuisines. Its botanical features include a well-developed root system and nodules formed in association with nitrogen-fixing bacteria.

Nephrocoelium malaccensis is also noted for its potential medicinal properties in local herbal practices.
