Nierembergia rigida

Nierembergia rigida, commonly known as the hardy bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy, providing a textured appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Nierembergia rigida is known for its long blooming period, adding color to gardens from early summer through fall.
