Nothocalais alpestris

Nothocalais alpestris, commonly known as the alpine bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving at high altitudes where conditions are harsh. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or purple, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, adapted to minimize water loss in arid conditions.

Nothocalais alpestris is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
