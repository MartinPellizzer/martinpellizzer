Najas marina var. gracilis

Najas marina var. gracilis, commonly known as slender watermilfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Najadaceae and is characterized by its fine, feathery leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant is often found in slow-moving or still waters such as ponds, lakes, and canals. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually found in dense clusters near the water's surface.

It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
