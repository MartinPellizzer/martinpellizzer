Notocactus graessneri var. stellatus

Notocactus graessneri var. stellatus, commonly known as the star cactus, is a species of cactus native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its distinctive, star-shaped spines and rounded, segmented stems that form a dense, bushy appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a delicate touch to its otherwise spiky structure. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.

Due to its unique morphology and relatively low maintenance, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
