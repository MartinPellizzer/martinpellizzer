Nectandra cuspidata

Nectandra cuspidata, commonly known as the arrowleaf sandarac, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces a resinous sap that has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a source of sandarac. Its growth habit includes a straight trunk and a dense canopy, making it suitable for agroforestry systems.

The leaves are typically elliptical with a pointed tip, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
