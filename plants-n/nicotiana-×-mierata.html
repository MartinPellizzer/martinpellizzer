Nicotiana × mierata

Nicotiana × mierata, commonly known as the hybrid tobacco plant, is a species resulting from the crossbreeding of Nicotiana tabacum and Nicotiana sylvestris.

It is characterized by its ornamental value, featuring showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant typically grows as an annual or biennial, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and botanical collections.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a model organism for studying plant genetics and hybridization.
