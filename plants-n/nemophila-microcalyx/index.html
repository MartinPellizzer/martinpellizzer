Nemophila Microcalyx

Blue-eyed mary, scientifically known as Nemophila microcalyx, is a charming flowering plant native to California and parts of western North America. With its delicate blue or purple blossoms and bright green foliage, it adds a touch of elegance to gardens and natural landscapes. This annual plant thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking low-maintenance beauty. Its common name reflects the striking white or blue petals that resemble large, glowing eyes. Despite its modest size, Blue-eyed mary holds cultural and ecological significance, supporting pollinators and enhancing biodiversity in local ecosystems.
