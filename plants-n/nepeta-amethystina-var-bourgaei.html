Nepeta amethystina var. bourgaei

Nepeta amethystina var. bourgaei, commonly known as the Bourgaei catmint, is a compact, low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features silver-gray, lance-shaped leaves that are aromatic and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces spikes of pale lavender to violet flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its hardy nature and ornamental value make it a popular choice for both gardeners and botanists.
