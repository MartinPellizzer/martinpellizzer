Notholaena nivea var. tenera

Notholaena nivea var. tenera, commonly known as the softleaf silvergrass, is a variant of the silvergrass species native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is characterized by its dense, fluffy foliage that gives it a soft, cloud-like appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and covered in fine, silvery hairs, contributing to its distinctive look.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
