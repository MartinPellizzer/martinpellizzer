Noccaea capricornuta

Noccaea capricornuta, commonly known as the horned chickweed, is a small annual herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features finely divided, grass-like leaves and produces small, white flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in disturbed habitats. It is characterized by its distinctive seed pods, which are covered in hooked bristles that aid in seed dispersal.

Noccaea capricornuta is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
