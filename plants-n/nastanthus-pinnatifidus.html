Nastanthus pinnatifidus

Nastanthus pinnatifidus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf nasturtium, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, which includes mustard and cress species. The plant features slender, pinnately divided leaves and produces small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. Its fruits are elongated pods that contain several seeds.

Nastanthus pinnatifidus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
