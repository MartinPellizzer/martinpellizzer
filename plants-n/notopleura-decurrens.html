Notopleura decurrens

Notopleura decurrens, commonly known as the creeping corymbia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its dense, spreading growth habit. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, whitish pubescence. Its flowers are small, white or pale pink, and are arranged in clusters, attracting various pollinators.

Notopleura decurrens is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a range of soil types.
