Neorosea jacfelicis

Neorosea jacfelicis, commonly known as the fairy rose, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-white petals that resemble miniature roses, and its compact, bushy growth habit. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom throughout the year, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening. Its leaves are opposite, oval-shaped, and have a glossy texture, adding to its aesthetic appeal.

Neorosea jacfelicis thrives in humid, well-drained soil and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.
