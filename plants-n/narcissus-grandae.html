Narcissus grandae

Narcissus grandae, commonly known as the giant daffodil, is a striking member of the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers with a prominent, often white or green center. The plant produces narrow, grass-like leaves and has a bulbous root system typical of the narcissus genus. It blooms in early spring, adding a vibrant splash of color to gardens and natural landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value, it is widely cultivated in horticulture.
