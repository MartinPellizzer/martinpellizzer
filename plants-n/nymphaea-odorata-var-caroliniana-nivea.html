Nymphaea odorata var. caroliniana-nivea

Nymphaea odorata var. caroliniana-nivea, commonly known as the Carolina water lily, is a water plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, white, fragrant flowers that bloom above the water surface. The plant has broad, floating leaves that provide habitat for aquatic life. Its roots anchor in the muddy bottom of ponds and lakes.

It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by supporting biodiversity and improving water quality.
