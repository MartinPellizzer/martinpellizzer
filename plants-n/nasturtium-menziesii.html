Nasturtium menziesii

Nasturtium menziesii, commonly known as the goldenroot nasturtium, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and heart-shaped leaves, which are often used in herbal medicine. The plant grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and streambanks. Its scientific name honors the botanist Archibald Menzies, who first documented it.

Nasturtium menziesii is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in traditional medicine.
