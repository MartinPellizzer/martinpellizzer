Neocapparis pachaca

Neocapparis pachaca, commonly known as the Andean crossbore, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Capparaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and showy, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its structure includes a branched stem with alternate leaves that are often lobed or toothed.

Neocapparis pachaca is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
