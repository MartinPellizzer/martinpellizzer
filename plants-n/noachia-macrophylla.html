Noachia macrophylla

Noachia macrophylla, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to tropical regions.

It features large, broad leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in diameter, providing a unique visual presence in water bodies. The plant has a floating growth habit, with its roots anchored in the sediment at the bottom of ponds or lakes. Its flowers are showy, with white petals and a yellow center, blooming in the early morning.

Noachia macrophylla plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by offering shelter to various aquatic organisms.
