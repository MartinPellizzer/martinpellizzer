Nicotiana tabaca

Nicotiana tabaca, commonly known as tobacco, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive tubular flowers that range in color from white to red, and its broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces a variety of alkaloids, with nicotine being the most well-known. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its chemical composition, it has been cultivated for both commercial and medicinal purposes throughout history.
