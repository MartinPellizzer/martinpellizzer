Niphobolus pertusus

Niphobolus pertusus, commonly known as the swamp milkweed, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to reddish-purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist soils and is often found along the edges of ponds, marshes, and wet meadows.

Niphobolus pertusus plays a vital role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
