Nepeta drassiana

Nepeta drassiana, commonly known as the Kashmiri catnip, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky and alpine environments. The plant features small, fragrant blue or violet flowers arranged in whorls along the stems. Its leaves are often aromatic and have been used in traditional medicine.

Nepeta drassiana is closely related to other catnip species but is distinguished by its unique habitat and floral structure.
