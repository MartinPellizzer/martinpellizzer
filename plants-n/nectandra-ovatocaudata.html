Nectandra ovatocaudata

Nectandra ovatocaudata, commonly known as the oval-tailed nectandra, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in lowland rainforests and is known for its ornamental value due to its glossy foliage.

Its fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains aromatic seeds, and it plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
