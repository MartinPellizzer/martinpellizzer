Nama retrorsa

Nama retrorsa, commonly known as the desert thornveld grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny flowers. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, exhibiting drought tolerance and efficient water use.

Nama retrorsa plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife in its native habitats.
