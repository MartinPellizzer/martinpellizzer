Nemophila nemorensis var. gracilis

Nemophila nemorensis var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender blue-eyed Mary, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing annual herb with slender stems and small, showy blue to purple flowers. The plant typically blooms in early spring and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. It prefers full sun to partial shade and is often found in open woodland or rocky habitats.

Its delicate appearance makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and wildflower displays.
