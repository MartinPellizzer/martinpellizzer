Nymphaea sinuata

Nymphaea sinuata, commonly known as the wavy-leaf water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, wavy-edged leaves that float on the water's surface, providing a habitat for various aquatic organisms. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in the early morning and closing by midday. Nymphaea sinuata thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes.

Its roots anchor it in the muddy substrate, while its rhizomes allow for vegetative propagation.
