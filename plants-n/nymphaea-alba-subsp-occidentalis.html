Nymphaea alba subsp. occidentalis

Nymphaea alba subsp.

occidentalis, commonly known as the western white water lily, is a aquatic plant native to North America. It is characterized by its large, white, showy flowers that bloom above the water surface.

The plant has broad, heart-shaped leaves that float on the water's surface, providing habitat for various aquatic organisms. Its roots are anchored in the muddy substrate at the bottom of ponds and lakes.

It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by supporting biodiversity and improving water quality.
