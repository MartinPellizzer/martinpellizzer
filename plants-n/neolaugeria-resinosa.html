Neolaugeria resinosa

Neolaugeria resinosa, commonly known as the resinous neolaugeria, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and broad, elliptical leaves that are glossy on top and pale underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds. Its resinous sap is a notable feature, often used in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive.

Neolaugeria resinosa thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for its ecological and commercial significance.
