Nectandra angusta

Nectandra angusta, commonly known as the narrow-leafed sandalwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark-colored wood. The plant has elliptical leaves that are typically narrow and glossy, with a distinct scent when crushed. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by oval, woody fruits containing a single seed.

Due to its valuable timber and ecological importance, it is often found in lowland rainforests.
