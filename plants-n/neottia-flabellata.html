Neottia flabellata

Neottia flabellata, commonly known as the fan-leaf twayblade, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and a single, often inconspicuous flower. The plant is notable for its mycorrhizal relationship with fungi, which it relies on for nutrient uptake. It typically grows in shaded, moist habitats such as forests and grasslands.

Neottia flabellata is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its delicate, ephemeral blooms.
