Nectaroscordum siculum

Nectaroscordum siculum, commonly known as the Sicilian garlic or wild garlic, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It features a bulbous base with long, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant is characterized by its strong garlic or onion-like odor, which is released when the leaves are crushed. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or grassland habitats.

Nectaroscordum siculum is valued both for its aromatic properties and its role in traditional herbal medicine.
