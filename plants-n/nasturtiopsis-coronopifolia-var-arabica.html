Nasturtiopsis coronopifolia var. arabica

Nasturtiopsis coronopifolia var. arabica, commonly known as the Arabian watercress, is a aquatic plant native to the Middle East and North Africa.

It features finely divided, ribbon-like leaves that are typically submerged or floating in shallow water. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, followed by small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams.

The leaves are edible and have a peppery flavor, making it a traditional ingredient in some local cuisines.
