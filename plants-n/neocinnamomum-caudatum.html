Neocinnamomum caudatum

Neocinnamomum caudatum, commonly known as the silky cinnamon tree, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of southern China and is characterized by its slender, aromatic leaves and small, clustered white flowers. The plant produces elliptical, dark blue-black fruits that are edible and have a distinctive, spicy aroma. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in subtropical forests.

Neocinnamomum caudatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its potential use in traditional medicine.
