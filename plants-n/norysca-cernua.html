Norysca cernua

Norysca cernua, commonly known as the drooping norysca, is a perennial herb native to the temperate regions of southern Africa.

It features a basal rosette of lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically green with a bluish tinge. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers during the spring and early summer. Its fibrous roots are adapted to retain moisture in arid environments.

Norysca cernua is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and delicate blooms.
