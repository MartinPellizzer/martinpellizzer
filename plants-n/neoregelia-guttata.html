Neoregelia guttata

Neoregelia guttata, commonly known as the spotted bromeliad, is a striking tropical plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup to collect rainwater. The plant's foliage is often adorned with distinctive red or orange spots, which give it its common name. Neoregelia guttata thrives in bright, indirect light and requires high humidity to flourish.

Its vibrant appearance makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and ornamental purposes.
