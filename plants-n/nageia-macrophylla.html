Nageia macrophylla

Nageia macrophylla, commonly known as the broad-leaf nageia, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Podocarpaceae and is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and dense, dark green foliage. The tree produces small, cone-like structures that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are typically broad and flat, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Nageia macrophylla is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape tree.
