Nectandra comasensis

Nectandra comasensis, commonly known as the Comasensis Sandwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark-colored wood. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are often glossy and elliptical in shape. It produces small, white flowers followed by elongated, woody fruits containing aromatic seeds.

Due to its unique properties, it is valued in traditional medicine and as a source of durable timber.
