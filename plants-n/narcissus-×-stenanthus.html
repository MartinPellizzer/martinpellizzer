Narcissus × stenanthus

Narcissus × stenanthus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved daffodil, is a hybrid species within the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, strap-like leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that typically range in color from yellow to white. The plant is native to southern Europe and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to a variety of garden settings.

Narcissus × stenanthus is known for its early spring blooming habit, adding a vibrant touch to gardens as winter fades.
