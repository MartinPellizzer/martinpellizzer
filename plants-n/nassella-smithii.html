Nassella smithii

Nassella smithii, commonly known as Smith's needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, hairless stems and spike-like inflorescences that bear small, awned florets. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Nassella smithii plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
