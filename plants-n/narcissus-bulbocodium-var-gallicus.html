Narcissus bulbocodium var. gallicus

Narcissus bulbocodium var. gallicus, commonly known as the French daffodil, is a bulbous plant native to southern Europe.

It produces clusters of bright yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers with a distinctive, recurved petal. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. Its bulbs are perennial, and it is known for its early spring blooming habit.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens.
