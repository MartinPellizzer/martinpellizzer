Nabalus integrifolius

Nabalus integrifolius, commonly known as the smoothleaf navelwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of smooth, entire leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters during the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
