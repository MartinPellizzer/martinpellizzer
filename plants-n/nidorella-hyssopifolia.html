Nidorella hyssopifolia

Nidorella hyssopifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hyssop, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and have a slightly hairy surface, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. Its small, blue-violet flowers are clustered in whorls along the stems, blooming in late spring to early summer.

This plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
