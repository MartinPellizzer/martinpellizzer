Nassella crassiflora

Nassella crassiflora, commonly known as the broad-leaved needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and produces tall, slender spikes of pale blue to purple flowers. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and rocky areas.

Nassella crassiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in native plant gardens and restoration projects.
