Neomyrtus

Neomyrtus, commonly known as the New Zealand myrtle, is a genus of flowering plants native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and tea trees. Neomyrtus species are characterized by their aromatic leaves and small, white or pale yellow flowers. These plants typically grow in coastal areas and are adapted to the humid, temperate climate of New Zealand.

They play an important ecological role in their native habitat, providing food and shelter for local wildlife.
