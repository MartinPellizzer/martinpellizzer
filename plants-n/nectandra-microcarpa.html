Nectandra microcarpa

Nectandra microcarpa, commonly known as the small-fruited sandarac tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, reddish-brown fruits that contain a resinous substance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its unique chemical composition, it has been studied for potential medicinal and industrial applications.
