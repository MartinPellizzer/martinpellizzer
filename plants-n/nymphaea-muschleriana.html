Nymphaea muschleriana

Nymphaea muschleriana, commonly known as the Muschler's water lily, is a species of aquatic plant native to the wetlands of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often forming dense mats on the water surface. Its roots are anchored in the sediment, while its rhizomes spread horizontally to support growth.

Nymphaea muschleriana plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
