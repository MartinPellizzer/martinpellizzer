Narcissus tripedalis

Narcissus tripedalis, commonly known as the three-leaved daffodil, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers with a white center, which bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and features three narrow, grass-like leaves. Native to southern Europe, it thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
