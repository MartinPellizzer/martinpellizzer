Neskiza loliacea

Neskiza loliacea, commonly known as the slender lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that can grow up to 60 cm in height, adorned with lance-shaped, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pale pink flowers arranged in a panicle inflorescence, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its botanical classification places it in the family Liliaceae, and it is characterized by its rhizomatous root system.

Neskiza loliacea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil.
