Neomammillaria mazatlanensis

Neomammillaria mazatlanensis, commonly known as the Mazatlan hedgehog cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a small, spineless cactus with a globular shape and a gray-green color. The plant features areolae, which are small, raised bumps containing spines and flowers. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring, adding a vibrant touch to its otherwise subtle appearance.

This species is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water and well-draining soil.
