Navia delascionis

Navia delascionis, commonly known as the desert flameleaf, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange to red foliage, which turns a striking crimson during the winter months. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in clusters at the ends of its branches. Navia delascionis is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it well adapted to harsh desert environments.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and ornamental gardening.
