Nepeta nepetoides

Nepeta nepetoides, commonly known as catmint, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue or lavender flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage is often used in herbal teas and has a mild minty scent.

Nepeta nepetoides is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
