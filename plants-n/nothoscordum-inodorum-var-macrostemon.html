Nothoscordum inodorum var. macrostemon

Nothoscordum inodorum var. macrostemon, commonly known as the giant swamp garlic, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features broad, grass-like leaves and produces large, showy white or pale pink flowers with a strong, garlic-like odor. The plant typically grows in wet, marshy habitats and is characterized by its robust, often branched stems. Its botanical structure includes a fibrous root system and a flowering stem that can reach heights of up to 2 meters.

The species is notable for its ability to thrive in saturated soils and its role in wetland ecosystems.
