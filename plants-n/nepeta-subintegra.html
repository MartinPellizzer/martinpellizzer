Nepeta subintegra

Nepeta subintegra, commonly known as catmint, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, tubular blue or purple flowers. The plant produces aromatic leaves that are often used in herbal teas and traditional remedies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Nepeta subintegra is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
