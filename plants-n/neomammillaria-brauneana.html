Neomammillaria brauneana

Neomammillaria brauneana, commonly known as the Brauneana cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a small, globular cactus with a ribbed structure and typically produces white or pale purple flowers. The plant has a dense covering of pale spines, which helps protect it from herbivores and intense sunlight. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Neomammillaria brauneana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique appearance.
