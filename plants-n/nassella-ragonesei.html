Nassella ragonesei

Nassella ragonesei, commonly known as the Ragonese needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and fine, needle-like leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fluffy seed heads that are often used in ornamental gardening due to their visual appeal. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Nassella ragonesei is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
