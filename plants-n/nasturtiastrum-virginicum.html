Nasturtiastrum virginicum

Nasturtiastrum virginicum, commonly known as the American watercress, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its small, edible leaves that are often used in salads and as a garnish. The plant typically grows in moist, aquatic environments such as streams and ponds. It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months.
