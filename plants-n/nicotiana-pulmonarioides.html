Nicotiana pulmonarioides

Nicotiana pulmonarioides, commonly known as the lungleaf tobacco, is a herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 15 centimeters in diameter. The plant produces striking violet or purple flowers that bloom in clusters, adding ornamental value to gardens. It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and attractive species for horticultural interest.
