Nephrodium venulosum

Nephrodium venulosum, commonly known as the velvet fern, is a unique plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage that gives it its common name. The plant has a creeping growth habit with rhizomes that produce small, clustered leaves. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female structures, and it is often found in sandy or rocky soils.

The botanical features of Nephrodium venulosum make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
