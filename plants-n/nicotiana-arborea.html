Nicotiana arborea

Nicotiana arborea, commonly known as the tree tobacco, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its woody stem, which gives it a tree-like appearance, and its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds, and its leaves are often covered in fine hairs. Nicotiana arborea is typically found in moist, shady environments and is cultivated for its ornamental value.

It is also related to the tobacco plant, sharing similar chemical compositions.
