Nepeta janthinostegia

Nepeta janthinostegia, commonly known as the pink nepeta, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically gray-green and have a mint-like scent when crushed.

Nepeta janthinostegia is often grown as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and delicate flowers.
