Noccaea coloradensis

Noccaea coloradensis, commonly known as Colorado knotweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows and along streambanks. It has small, white or pale yellow flowers and compound leaves that are divided into narrow segments.

Its fibrous root system helps stabilize soil, making it an important plant in ecological restoration efforts.
