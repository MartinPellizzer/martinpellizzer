Nothapodytes tomentosa

Nothapodytes tomentosa, commonly known as the silky tamarind, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree produces elongated, woody pods containing flat, edible seeds that are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are covered in a fine, silvery-white pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
