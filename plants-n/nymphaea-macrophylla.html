Nymphaea macrophylla

Nymphaea macrophylla, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in diameter, providing a unique habitat for various aquatic organisms. The plant's showy, fragrant flowers are typically pink to reddish in color and bloom during the day. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in ponds and lakes.

Nymphaea macrophylla plays an important ecological role by supporting biodiversity and contributing to water quality through its dense vegetation.
