Nepeta nepetella subsp. murcica

Nepeta nepetella subsp.

murcica, commonly known as the Murcian catmint, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe. It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic, hairy leaves and clusters of small, pale blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
