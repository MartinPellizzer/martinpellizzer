Narcissus × gracilis var. princeps

Narcissus × gracilis var. princeps, commonly known as the Prince's daffodil, is a hybrid daffodil species native to the British Isles.

It is characterized by its slender, strap-like leaves and a single, trumpet-shaped flower that is typically yellow in color. The plant is known for its early blooming habit, often flowering in late winter or early spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The flowers are not only ornamental but also attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
