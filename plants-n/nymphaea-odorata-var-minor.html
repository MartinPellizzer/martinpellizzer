Nymphaea odorata var. minor

Nymphaea odorata var. minor, commonly known as the lesser sacred lotus, is a water-loving perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and marshes. The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves that float on the water surface, supported by long, slender stems. Its flowers are typically pink or white, with a fragrant scent that attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies.

This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and holds cultural significance in various traditional practices.
