Nectandra robusta

Nectandra robusta, commonly known as the robust nectandra, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves. The tree produces small, white flowers followed by dark, fleshy berries that are edible for some bird species. Its wood is valued for its durability and is used in furniture and construction.

Nectandra robusta plays an important ecological role in its native habitats by providing food and shelter for various wildlife.
