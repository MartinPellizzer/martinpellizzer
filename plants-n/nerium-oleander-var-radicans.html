Nerium oleander var. radicans

Nerium oleander var. radicans, commonly known as the trailing oleander, is a flowering shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its profuse clusters of showy, pink, red, or white flowers that bloom throughout the summer. The plant has slender, trailing stems that make it ideal for hanging baskets or as a ground cover. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and lance-shaped, with a smooth texture.

It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
