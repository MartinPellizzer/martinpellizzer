Neochilenia totoralensis

Neochilenia totoralensis, commonly known as the Totora orchid, is a rare and unique orchid species native to the high Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant thrives in high-altitude grasslands and is adapted to the cold, arid conditions of its habitat. It plays a significant role in the local ecosystem, providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and moths.

Due to habitat loss and climate change, Neochilenia totoralensis is considered a vulnerable species requiring conservation efforts.
