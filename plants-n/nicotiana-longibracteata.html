Nicotiana longibracteata

Nicotiana longibracteata, commonly known as the long bract tobacco, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family.

It is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, where it typically grows in dry, rocky habitats. The plant is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet. Its leaves are broad and often have a waxy texture, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

This species is cultivated for its ornamental value and has also been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
