Narcissus flos-lactis

Narcissus flos-lactis, commonly known as the milk white daffodil, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its striking white petals and a yellow-orange corona, which distinguishes it from other daffodil varieties. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a delicate and elegant touch to gardens.

Botanically, it belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in horticulture.
