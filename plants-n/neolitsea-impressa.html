Neolitsea impressa

Neolitsea impressa, commonly known as the broadleaf sandarac tree, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including regions of China, India, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in axillary inflorescences.

Neolitsea impressa is valued for its aromatic wood, which has been traditionally used in incense and as a source of sandarac resin.
