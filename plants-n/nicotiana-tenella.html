Nicotiana tenella

Nicotiana tenella, commonly known as the evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and striking, large, showy flowers that open in the evening. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a velvety texture.

Nicotiana tenella is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
