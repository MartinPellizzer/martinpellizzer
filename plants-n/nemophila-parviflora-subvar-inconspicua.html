Nemophila parviflora subvar. inconspicua

Nemophila parviflora subvar. inconspicua, commonly known as the inconspicuous blue-eyed Mary, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically reaches heights of 10 to 20 centimeters and features small, delicate flowers that range in color from pale blue to white. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of tiny, four-petaled blossoms. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland and meadow habitats.

This subvariety is noted for its subtle appearance and is valued for its ornamental appeal in native gardens.
