Nigella koyuncui

Nigella koyuncui, commonly known as Turkish cowpea or Persian cowpea, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, yellowish-white flowers and fern-like leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30-60 centimeters and thrives in well-drained soil. Its seeds, which are small and black, are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice in some cuisines.

Nigella koyuncui is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value.
