Nunnezharia amabilis

Nunnezharia amabilis, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a smooth texture and grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

The flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects, contributing to its ecological significance in its native habitat.
