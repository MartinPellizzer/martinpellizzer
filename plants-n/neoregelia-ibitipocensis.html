Neoregelia ibitipocensis

Neoregelia ibitipocensis, commonly known as the Ibitipocense bromeliad, is a species of flowering plant native to the rainforests of Brazil.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup to collect rainwater. The plant produces striking, brightly colored inflorescences that can range from red to orange, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. Its foliage is often adorned with intricate patterns and can display a variety of colors, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Neoregelia ibitipocensis thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
