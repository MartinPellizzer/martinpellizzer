Nephrodium parallelogrammum

Nephrodium parallelogrammum, commonly known as the parallel-leaf nephroid, is a unique fern species characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology.

Its fronds are bipinnate, with a complex arrangement of pinnae that form a nearly parallel structure, giving it its scientific name. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions. It has a rhizomatous growth habit, which allows it to spread and colonize new areas effectively.

The spores are produced in sori located on the underside of the fronds, a common reproductive feature in ferns.
