Neoregelia bahiana f. viridis

Neoregelia bahiana f. viridis, commonly known as the Emerald Bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, elongated leaves that form a rosette shape. The leaves are typically a lush green color, which is why the cultivar name "viridis" (meaning green in Latin) is used. This plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and as a decorative plant.
