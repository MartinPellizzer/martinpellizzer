Narcissus × dezanus

Narcissus × dezanus, commonly known as the double daffodil, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Narcissus tazetta and Narcissus cyclamenus.

It is characterized by its large, showy, double flowers with a prominent, often colorful center. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with strap-like, sword-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for gardens and floral arrangements.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and is often used in spring flower displays.
