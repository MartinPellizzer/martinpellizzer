Nephrolepis tuberosa

Nephrolepis tuberosa, commonly known as the tuberous fern, is a species of fern characterized by its unique rhizomatous growth habit and the presence of tuber-like structures.

It features fronds that are typically bipinnate, with a delicate, feathery appearance that gives it a graceful look. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its reproductive structures, including sporangia, are located on the underside of the fronds, typical of ferns.

Nephrolepis tuberosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
