Narcissus pseudonarcissus var. insignis

Narcissus pseudonarcissus var. insignis, commonly known as the Spanish daffodil, is a variant of the common daffodil characterized by its distinctive floral features.

It produces large, trumpet-shaped flowers with a yellow corona and white petals, showcasing a striking contrast. The plant is native to southern Europe and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun conditions. It is a bulbous perennial that blooms in early spring, adding vibrant color to gardens.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and its role in horticultural diversity within the Narcissus genus.
