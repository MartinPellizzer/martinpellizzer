Nephrodium atomiferum

Nephrodium atomiferum, commonly known as the feather fern, is a member of the Dryopteridaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that give it a feathery appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its reproductive structures include sporangia that are clustered in sori on the underside of the fronds.

Nephrodium atomiferum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in maintaining forest floor biodiversity.
