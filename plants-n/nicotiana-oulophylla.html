Nicotiana oulophylla

Nicotiana oulophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tobacco, is a species native to South America, particularly found in regions of Argentina and Chile.

It is a herbaceous annual plant that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are usually pink or purple in color, which attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, and the plant contains alkaloids, including nicotine, which are characteristic of the Nicotiana genus.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been studied for its potential in pharmaceutical research.
