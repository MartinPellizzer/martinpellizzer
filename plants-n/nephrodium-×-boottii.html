Nephrodium × boottii

Nephrodium × boottii, commonly known as the hybrid fern, is a rare and unique plant resulting from the cross between Nephrodium leiospathum and Nephrodium sinense.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are often adorned with small, spore-producing structures called sori. This fern thrives in shaded, moist environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its botanical significance lies in its role as a natural hybrid, showcasing genetic diversity within the Nephrodium genus.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
