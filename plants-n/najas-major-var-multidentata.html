Najas major var. multidentata

Najas major var. multidentata, commonly known as the many-toothed watermilfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant belonging to the family Najadaceae.

It is characterized by its finely divided, ribbon-like leaves that are often arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving rivers.

Its rapid growth and dense vegetation can significantly impact water quality and aquatic ecosystems.
