Notobasis syriaca var. latifolia

Notobasis syriaca var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved Syrian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy, blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are broader than those of the typical variety, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to gardens and ecological restoration projects.
