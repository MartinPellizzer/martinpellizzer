Nicotiana alpina var. patagonica

Nicotiana alpina var. patagonica, commonly known as the Patagonian tobacco plant, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in high-altitude environments, showcasing adaptations to cold and arid conditions. The plant produces striking violet to purple flowers, which bloom in clusters and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs that help reduce water loss.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional practices.
