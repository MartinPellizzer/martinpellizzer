Nepeta americana

Nepeta americana, commonly known as American catmint, is a perennial herb native to the United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their fragrant foliage and showy flowers. The plant features square, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically blue or purple and are arranged in whorls along the stem.

Nepeta americana is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
