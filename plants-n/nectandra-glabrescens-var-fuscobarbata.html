Nectandra glabrescens var. fuscobarbata

Nectandra glabrescens var. fuscobarbata, commonly known as the black-bearded nectandra, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its dark, aromatic bark and glossy, elliptical leaves. It produces small, fragrant flowers followed by woody, elongated fruits containing aromatic seeds.

This species is valued for its timber and traditional medicinal uses in its native regions.
