Nemophila flaccida

Nemophila flaccida, commonly known as the slender blue-eyed Mary, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States.

It produces small, delicate flowers that range in color from blue to violet, often with white centers. The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its rugged appearance.

Nemophila flaccida is known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
