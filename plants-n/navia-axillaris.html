Navia axillaris

Navia axillaris, commonly known as the rock rose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its woody stems and small, evergreen leaves. The plant produces vibrant clusters of pink or white flowers during the spring and summer months. Navia axillaris thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its drought tolerance and ornamental value make it a popular choice in horticulture.
