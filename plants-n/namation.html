Namation

Namation, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a rare and enigmatic plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes.

It is characterized by its translucent, waxy leaves that appear to shimmer in the sunlight, giving it a ghostly appearance. The plant produces small, pale pink flowers that bloom only during the night, attracting specific nocturnal pollinators. Its unique adaptations to extreme cold and low oxygen levels make it a subject of interest for botanists.

Due to its endangered status, conservation efforts are underway to protect this elusive species.
