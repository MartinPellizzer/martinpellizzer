Neolemaireocereus aragonii

Neolemaireocereus aragonii, commonly known as the Aragon's cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stem and clusters of small, spiny areoles. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are typically nocturnal in bloom. It has a slow growth rate and requires well-draining soil to thrive.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for desert gardens and cactus collections.
