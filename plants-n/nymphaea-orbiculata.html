Nymphaea orbiculata

Nymphaea orbiculata, commonly known as the lotus, is a water-living plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Nymphaeaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that float on the water surface. The plant has broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy layer to repel water. Its flowers are typically pink or white, with a strong, sweet fragrance.

Nymphaea orbiculata plays an important ecological role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various organisms.
