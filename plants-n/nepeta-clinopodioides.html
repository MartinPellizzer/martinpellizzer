Nepeta clinopodioides

Nepeta clinopodioides, commonly known as catmint, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in herbal teas and have a mild minty scent.

Nepeta clinopodioides is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
