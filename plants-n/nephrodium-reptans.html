Nephrodium reptans

Nephrodium reptans, commonly known as the creeping nephrolepis, is a fern characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and delicate fronds.

It features bipinnate leaves that are often arching and gracefully drooping, giving it a cascading appearance. The plant produces sporangia on the underside of its fronds, which are clustered in sori covered by a protective indusium. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for hanging baskets and shaded garden areas.
