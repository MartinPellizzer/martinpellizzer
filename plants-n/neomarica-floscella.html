Neomarica floscella

Neomarica floscella, commonly known as the small-flowered orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, small flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pale pink. The plant has a fibrous root system and grows from a pseudobulb, which stores water and nutrients. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, often arranged in a rosette pattern.

Neomarica floscella prefers shady, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.
