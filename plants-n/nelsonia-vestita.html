Nelsonia vestita

Nelsonia vestita, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvetleaf tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety pubescence. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to yellow, often with a subtle fragrance. Nelsonia vestita is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically significant species in its native habitats.
