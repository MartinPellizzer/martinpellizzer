Neopaxia calycina

Neopaxia calycina, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Neopaxia calycina thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for its unique foliage.
