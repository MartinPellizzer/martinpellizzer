Noccaea kurdica

Noccaea kurdica, commonly known as Kurdish watercress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Middle East.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous plants. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats. Its leaves are edible and have a peppery flavor similar to that of watercress.

Noccaea kurdica is valued both for its culinary uses and its ecological role in its native environment.
