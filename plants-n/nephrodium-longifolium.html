Nephrodium longifolium

Nephrodium longifolium, commonly known as the long-leaved nephroid, is a rare fern species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Nephrodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, elongated fronds that can reach up to 1 meter in length. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in tropical rainforests. Its spore-bearing structures are located on the underside of the fronds, and it reproduces through spores rather than seeds.

Due to habitat loss, Nephrodium longifolium is considered endangered and is of significant interest to botanists and conservationists.
