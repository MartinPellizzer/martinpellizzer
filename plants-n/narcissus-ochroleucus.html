Narcissus ochroleucus

Narcissus ochroleucus, commonly known as the yellow daffodil, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa, where it thrives in sunny, well-drained soils. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically bright yellow with a white or green center. Its foliage consists of long, strap-like leaves that emerge in early spring.

Narcissus ochroleucus is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
