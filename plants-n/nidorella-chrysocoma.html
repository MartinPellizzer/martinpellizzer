Nidorella chrysocoma

Nidorella chrysocoma, commonly known as the golden hair veldt, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant produces small, hairy leaves that help it retain moisture in its arid habitat. It is often found in grasslands and rocky outcrops, thriving in full sun.

Nidorella chrysocoma is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
