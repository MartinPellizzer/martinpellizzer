Nepeta argolica subsp. vourinensis

Nepeta argolica subsp.

vourinensis, commonly known as the Vourin catmint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Greece. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves.

The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its foliage has a distinct minty scent, which is typical of many members of the Nepeta genus.
