Neomicrocalamus pusillus

Neomicrocalamus pusillus, commonly known as the dwarf bamboo, is a small, herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices.
