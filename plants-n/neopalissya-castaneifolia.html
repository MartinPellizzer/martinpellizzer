Neopalissya castaneifolia

Neopalissya castaneifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian chestnut tree, is a species native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a large evergreen tree that can grow up to 30 meters tall, characterized by its broad, glossy leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces edible fruits that resemble chestnuts, encased in a spiny husk. Its bark is thick and fissured, often featuring a distinctive reddish-brown color.

Neopalissya castaneifolia plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
