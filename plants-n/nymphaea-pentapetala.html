Nymphaea pentapetala

Nymphaea pentapetala, commonly known as the five-petaled water lily, is a species of aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, floating leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes. Its flowers are typically pink or white, with five distinct petals and a yellow center.

Nymphaea pentapetala plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
