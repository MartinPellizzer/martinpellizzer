Nyctanthes elongata

Nyctanthes elongata, commonly known as the night-flowering jasmine, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to India and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and fragrant white flowers that bloom at night. The plant produces small, black, spherical fruits that contain a single seed. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.

Nyctanthes elongata is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic uses.
