Newtonia elliotii

Newtonia elliotii, commonly known as the Elliot's Newtonia, is a deciduous shrub native to the grasslands of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow to orange pea-like flowers in late summer. Its fruit is a legume pod that splits open to release its seeds.

Newtonia elliotii is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
