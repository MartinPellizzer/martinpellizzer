Nepeta amethystina subsp. mallophora

Nepeta amethystina subsp.

mallophora, commonly known as the purple catmint, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe. It features aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of lavender-blue flowers.

The plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.

Its foliage and flowers are often used in gardens for their visual and ecological benefits.
