Noccaea bellidifolia

Noccaea bellidifolia, commonly known as the bellflower rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Noccaea bellidifolia is known for its hardy nature and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
