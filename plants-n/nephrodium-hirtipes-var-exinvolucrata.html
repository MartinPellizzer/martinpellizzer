Nephrodium hirtipes var. exinvolucrata

Nephrodium hirtipes var. exinvolucrata, commonly known as the hairy nephroid, is a variant of the nephroid plant characterized by its hairy stems and leaves.

It belongs to the family Nephroidaceae and is native to specific regions in Southeast Asia. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest undergrowth. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped with a rough texture, and it produces small, clustered flowers.

This variant is notable for its unique morphological features and ecological adaptations.
