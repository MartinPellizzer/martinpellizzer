Nepenthes × harryana

Nepenthes × harryana, commonly known as the Harryana pitcher plant, is a hybrid carnivorous plant native to Borneo.

It is characterized by its large, elongated pitcher-shaped leaves that secrete a digestive fluid to trap and digest insects. The plant's pitchers are typically green or red in color, with a waxy surface that aids in trapping prey. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires high levels of moisture and nutrient-poor soil.

Nepenthes × harryana is valued for its unique morphology and is often cultivated by enthusiasts of carnivorous plants.
