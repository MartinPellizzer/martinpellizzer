Nepeta nuda subsp. violacea

Nepeta nuda subsp.

violacea, commonly known as violet catmint, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, violet to blue-purple flowers.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and emit a minty fragrance when crushed. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
