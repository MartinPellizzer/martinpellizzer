Neodawsonia euphorbioides var. olfersii

Neodawsonia euphorbioides var. olfersii, commonly known as the Olfers' dwarf dawsonia, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its compact, fleshy stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in summer, which are followed by unique, bead-like seed capsules.

It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
