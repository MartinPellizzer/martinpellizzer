Nymphaea alba var. melocarpa

Nymphaea alba var. melocarpa, commonly known as the white water lily with red flowers, is a variant of the white water lily native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its large, white to pinkish-white petals that transition to a deep red at the base, creating a striking contrast. The plant typically grows in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats, such as ponds and lakes. Its leaves are broad and floating, with a waxy surface that helps repel water.

The flowers open in the early morning and close during the heat of the day, a behavior known as nyctinasty.
