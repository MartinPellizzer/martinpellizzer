Narcissus minor subsp. brevicoronatus

Narcissus minor subsp.

brevicoronatus, commonly known as the dwarf daffodil, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its small, cup-shaped flowers that typically range in color from white to yellow.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces a single flower per stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate in gardens.
