Neoporteria clavata var. procera

Neoporteria clavata var. procera, commonly known as the tall spike-rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically greenish or brownish in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, arranged in a rosette at the base of the plant.

This species thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of ponds and streams.
