Nectandra brenesii

Nectandra brenesii, commonly known as the Costa Rican sandarac tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves and fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by woody, cone-like fruits containing aromatic resin. Its resin, known as sandarac, has been historically used in various applications, including as a varnish and in traditional medicine.

The tree thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.
