Narcissus vergellensis

Narcissus vergellensis, commonly known as the Vergil's daffodil, is a rare and ancient species of daffodil native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and a single, elongated flower with a yellow perianth. The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and prefers sunny to partially shaded conditions. It is noted for its delicate fragrance and is often associated with historical and literary references.

Due to its rarity, it is considered a valuable species in botanical conservation efforts.
