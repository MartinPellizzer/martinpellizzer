Nectandra megapotamica

Nectandra megapotamica, commonly known as the Amazonian sandarac tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves and fragrant flowers. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in panicles, and its fruit is a small, woody capsule containing several seeds. It is valued for its durable wood, which is used in construction and furniture-making, and for its essential oils used in traditional medicine.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
