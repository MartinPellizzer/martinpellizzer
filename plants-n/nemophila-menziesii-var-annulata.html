Nemophila menziesii var. annulata

Nemophila menziesii var. annulata, commonly known as the annual blue violet, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Violaceae family and is characterized by its delicate blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. It has small, heart-shaped leaves and produces clusters of flowers that bloom in early spring.

This variant is distinguished by its annual life cycle and the presence of a ring-like pattern on its petals.
