Nephrodium cristatum var. clintonianum

Nephrodium cristatum var. clintonianum, commonly known as the Clinton's fern, is a variant of the cristatum form of Nephrodium, a genus of ferns native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive, crisped fronds that give it a unique, ornamental appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest undergrowth, where it can thrive in the dappled light. Its fronds are usually lanceolate and have a delicate, feathery texture, contributing to its aesthetic appeal.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and its ability to adapt to a variety of soil conditions.
