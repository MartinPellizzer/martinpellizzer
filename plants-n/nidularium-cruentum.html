Nidularium cruentum

Nidularium cruentum, commonly known as the blood-flower, is a striking member of the Araceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence, which consists of a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe. Its foliage consists of broad, arrow-shaped leaves that are often variegated with red and green patterns.

Nidularium cruentum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and unique floral structure.
