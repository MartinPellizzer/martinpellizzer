Nassella brachychaeta

Nassella brachychaeta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and dense, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its fibrous root system helps stabilize soil, making it useful for erosion control.

The small, wind-pollinated flowers produce lightweight seeds that are dispersed by the wind.
