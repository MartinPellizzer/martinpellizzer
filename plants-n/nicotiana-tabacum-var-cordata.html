Nicotiana tabacum var. cordata

Nicotiana tabacum var. cordata, commonly known as the Heartleaf tobacco, is a cultivar of the tobacco plant characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves.

It is native to South America and is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant flowers and unique foliage. The plant produces trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens and floral arrangements.

Despite its ornamental appeal, it is related to the tobacco plant, which is known for its nicotine content.
