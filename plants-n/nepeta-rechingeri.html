Nepeta rechingeri

Nepeta rechingeri, commonly known as Carpathian catmint, is a perennial herb native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage has a strong mint-like aroma, which is often used in traditional herbal remedies.

Nepeta rechingeri thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade conditions.
