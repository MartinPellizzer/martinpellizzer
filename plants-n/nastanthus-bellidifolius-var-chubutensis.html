Nastanthus bellidifolius var. chubutensis

Nastanthus bellidifolius var. chubutensis, commonly known as the Chubut bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Patagonian region of Argentina.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or arid environments.

Due to its unique beauty and limited distribution, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts interested in rare flora.
