Nigritella nigra subsp. rhellicani

Nigritella nigra subsp.

rhellicani, commonly known as the African violet, is a member of the Gesneriaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa. This subspecies is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to white.

The leaves are typically velvety and dark green, often with a waxy coating. It thrives in humid, well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is widely cultivated as a houseplant.
