Nepeta rubella

Nepeta rubella, commonly known as catmint, is a perennial herb native to Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in herbal remedies and are known for their calming properties.

Nepeta rubella thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
