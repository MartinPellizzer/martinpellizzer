Neptunia oleracea

Neptunia oleracea, commonly known as water spinach or kangkong, is a semi-aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It thrives in wetland environments and is characterized by its succulent, edible leaves and stems. The plant has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion. Its leaves are often used in various cuisines, particularly in Asian countries.

Neptunia oleracea is also valued for its ability to tolerate flooded conditions, making it a resilient and versatile species.
