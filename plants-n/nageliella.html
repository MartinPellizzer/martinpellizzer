Nageliella

Nageliella, commonly known as the "crown of thorns," is a member of the Apocynaceae family.

It is a shrub native to South Africa, characterized by its spiny stems and showy, tubular flowers. The plant produces small, red or orange berries that contain toxic seeds. Nageliella thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal regions.

Its unique morphology and toxic properties make it an interesting subject in botanical studies.
