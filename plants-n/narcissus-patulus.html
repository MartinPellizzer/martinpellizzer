Narcissus patulus

Narcissus patulus, commonly known as the Spanish daffodil, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and western Asia, particularly found in regions like Spain and Portugal. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers with a single tepal in the center, typically in shades of yellow or orange. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Narcissus patulus is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for its early spring blooms.
