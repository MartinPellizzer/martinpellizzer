Neosyris fuliginea

Neosyris fuliginea, commonly known as the blackleaf syringa, is a species of flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet, featuring dense, dark green foliage. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers in the spring, which are borne on short, pedicellate inflorescences. Its leaves are elliptical in shape and have a slightly hairy texture, while its bark is smooth and reddish-brown.

Neosyris fuliginea is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in partial shade.
