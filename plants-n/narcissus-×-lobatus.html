Narcissus × lobatus

Narcissus × lobatus, commonly known as the double daffodil, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Narcissus tazetta and Narcissus cyclathus.

It is characterized by its showy, layered flowers that consist of a central crown surrounded by multiple petals. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with strap-like leaves that are basal and grass-like in appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

The flowers bloom in early spring, adding a vibrant splash of yellow to the landscape.
