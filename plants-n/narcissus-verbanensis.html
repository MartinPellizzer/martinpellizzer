Narcissus verbanensis

Narcissus verbanensis, commonly known as the Verbanian daffodil, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to the Alpine regions of Europe, particularly in Italy and Switzerland. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, with a single stamen and a prominent corona. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often growing in mountainous or rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
