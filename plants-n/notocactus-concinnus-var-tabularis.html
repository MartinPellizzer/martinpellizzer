Notocactus concinnus var. tabularis

Notocactus concinnus var. tabularis, commonly known as the flat-topped cactus, is a unique cactus species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are often flattened at the top, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its striking appearance and low maintenance, it is a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor gardens.
