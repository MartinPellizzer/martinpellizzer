Nissolia bracteosa

Nissolia bracteosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow in color. It thrives in moist, warm environments and is often found in disturbed soils or along stream banks.

Nissolia bracteosa is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain areas.
