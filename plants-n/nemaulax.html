Nemaulax

Nemaulax, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, delicate appearance with pale green or white flowers. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surface of trees without deriving nutrients from them. Nemaulax has small, scale-like leaves and produces flowers that bloom in clusters, often in the summer months.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a conservation concern in its native range.
