Nyctaginia torreyana

Nyctaginia torreyana, commonly known as the evening glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Nyctaginaceae and is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that open in the late afternoon. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, with opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, and they emit a mild fragrance, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Nyctaginia torreyana is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique blooming habit.
