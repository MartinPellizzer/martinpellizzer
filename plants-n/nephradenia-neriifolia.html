Nephradenia neriifolia

Nephradenia neriifolia, commonly known as the red-leafed hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that range from green to deep red, depending on the season and light exposure. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of yellow and orange, which bloom during the rainy season. It thrives in humid, well-drained soil and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.

Nephradenia neriifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant in tropical landscapes.
