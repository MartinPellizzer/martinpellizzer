Noccaea crassifolia

Noccaea crassifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved honesty, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, lobed leaves and small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer. Its seeds are often used in herbal remedies and are known for their peppery taste.

Noccaea crassifolia is also valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
