Nigritella gallica

Nigritella gallica, commonly known as the Gallic blackroot, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, erect stems that are often reddish in color and bear small, greenish-white flowers. The plant's leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture. Its root system consists of tubers that are typically dark purple or black in color, which are the primary part used for medicinal purposes.

Nigritella gallica is valued for its potential anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.
