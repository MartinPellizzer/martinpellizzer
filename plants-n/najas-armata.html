Najas armata

Najas armata, commonly known as the narrowleaf watermilfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater habitats in North America.

It has slender, ribbon-like leaves that are often arranged in whorls around the stem, aiding in its identification. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically found in the water, contributing to its reproductive cycle. Najas armata plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

It is also sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions due to its rapid growth and spread.
