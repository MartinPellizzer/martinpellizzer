Nepeta koeieana

Nepeta koeieana, commonly known as catmint, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and opposite leaves that are often gray-green in color. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in whorls along the stem. It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs.

Nepeta koeieana is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
