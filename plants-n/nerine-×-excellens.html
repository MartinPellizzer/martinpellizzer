Nerine × excellens

Nerine × excellens, commonly known as the pink nerine, is a hybrid ornamental plant belonging to the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant produces showy, clustered blooms in late summer to early autumn, making it a popular choice for gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, and it is relatively drought-tolerant once established.

The foliage consists of strap-like leaves that are evergreen in mild climates and die back in colder regions.
