Nothofagus megalocarpa

Nothofagus megalocarpa, commonly known as the giant southern beech, is a species native to the temperate rainforests of South America.

It is a large evergreen tree that can reach heights of up to 40 meters, characterized by its smooth, dark bark and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by woody, spherical fruits containing several seeds. It plays a crucial role in the ecosystem as a habitat and food source for various native species.

Nothofagus megalocarpa is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
