Nephrodium glaucostipes

Nephrodium glaucostipes, commonly known as the blue-stemmed nephroid, is a rare fern native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue-green fronds and distinctive, clustered rhizomes. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil. Its reproductive structures include sporangia that release spores for propagation.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and is often found in conservation programs.
