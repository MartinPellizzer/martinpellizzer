Narcissus juncifolius var. assoanus

Narcissus juncifolius var. assoanus, commonly known as the Assoanum narcissus, is a rare and ornamental plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. It is a perennial species that produces bulbous roots and is often cultivated for its delicate floral display.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a valuable species in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
