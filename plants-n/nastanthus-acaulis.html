Nastanthus acaulis

Nastanthus acaulis, commonly known as the naked-stemmed nasturtium, is a unique plant species characterized by its lack of true stems.

The plant features fleshy, succulent leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps it retain moisture in its arid habitat. Its flowers are typically bright yellow or orange, with four petals arranged in a cross shape, a common trait in the Brassicaceae family.

Nastanthus acaulis is known for its ability to thrive in rocky and well-drained soils, making it a resilient plant in challenging environments.
