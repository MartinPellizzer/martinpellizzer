Nepeta hindostana var. woodrowii

Nepeta hindostana var. woodrowii, commonly known as Woodrow's catmint, is a perennial herb native to the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported calming properties.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
