Narcissus pseudonarcissus subsp. albescens

Narcissus pseudonarcissus subsp.

albescens, commonly known as the white narcissus or the white daffodil, is a subspecies native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its striking white petals and a yellow corona, which distinguish it from other narcissus species.

The plant typically grows in grasslands and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soils. It blooms in early spring, often before the leaves fully emerge.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.
