Notocactus glaucinus var. depressus

Notocactus glaucinus var. depressus, commonly known as the dwarf blue cactus, is a compact, low-growing cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It features a rounded, segmented stem that is typically blue-green in color, often with a waxy coating that helps reduce water loss. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers during the warmer months, usually in spring or early summer. It is well adapted to dry environments and requires minimal watering, making it suitable for succulent gardens.

Its slow growth rate and unique appearance make it a popular choice for collectors and enthusiasts.
