Najas palustris

Najas palustris, commonly known as the water needle or marsh grass, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments.

It has long, slender, ribbon-like leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged, making them difficult to observe. Najas palustris thrives in still or slow-moving waters and is often found in marshes and ponds.

Its presence is an indicator of good water quality and healthy aquatic ecosystems.
