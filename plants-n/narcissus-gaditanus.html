Narcissus gaditanus

Narcissus gaditanus, commonly known as the Spanish daffodil, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Spain.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in spring, producing bright yellow flowers with a prominent central corona. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Narcissus gaditanus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in Mediterranean horticulture.
