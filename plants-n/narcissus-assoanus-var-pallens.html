Narcissus assoanus var. pallens

Narcissus assoanus var. pallens, commonly known as the pale narcissus, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It produces showy, white or pale yellow flowers with a single, prominent central tongue. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge in early spring.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
