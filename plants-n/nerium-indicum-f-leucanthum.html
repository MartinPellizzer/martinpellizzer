Nerium indicum f. leucanthum

Nerium indicum f. leucanthum, commonly known as the white oleander, is a striking variant of the oleander plant characterized by its white flowers.

This cultivar is a hybrid form that exhibits a unique coloration compared to the typical pink or red flowers of the species. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. The plant is a shrub or small tree with showy, clustered blooms that appear in summer.

It is also noted for its drought tolerance and ability to grow in a variety of soil types.
