Narcissus watieri

Narcissus watieri, commonly known as the Watieri daffodil, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Europe.

It produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers with bright yellow petals and a central, elongated corona. The plant typically blooms in early spring, adding vibrant color to gardens and natural habitats. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation.
