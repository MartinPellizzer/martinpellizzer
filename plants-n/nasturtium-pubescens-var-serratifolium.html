Nasturtium pubescens var. serratifolium

Nasturtium pubescens var. serratifolium, commonly known as the serrated leaf nasturtium, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges and bright orange or yellow flowers. The plant is known for its peppery taste, similar to watercress, and is often used in salads or as a culinary herb. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Its ornamental value and edible qualities make it a versatile plant in both landscaping and cooking.
