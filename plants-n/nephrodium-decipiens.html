Nephrodium decipiens

Nephrodium decipiens, commonly known as the deceptive nephroid, is a rare and unique fern species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped fronds that closely resemble those of other ferns, potentially aiding in its survival through mimicry. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest undergrowth, where it thrives in the understory. Its reproductive structures, including spore-producing clusters, are located on the underside of its leaves, a common feature among ferns.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Nephrodium decipiens is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
