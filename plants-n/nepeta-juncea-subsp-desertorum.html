Nepeta juncea subsp. desertorum

Nepeta juncea subsp.

desertorum, commonly known as desert catmint, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant blue or purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are finely toothed and emit a minty scent when crushed, contributing to its ornamental value.

It is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
