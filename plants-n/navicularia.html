Navicularia

Navicularia, commonly known as the "narrow-leafed rock cress," is a genus of flowering plants in the Brassicaceae family.

These plants are typically small, herbaceous perennials that thrive in rocky or sandy soils. Navicularia species are characterized by their narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered flowers that are often white or pale yellow. They are often found in arid or semi-arid regions, showcasing their adaptability to harsh environments.

The genus includes several species, some of which are used in horticulture for their ornamental value and drought tolerance.
