Nidorella varia

Nidorella varia, commonly known as the variable orchid, is a species of orchid native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that come in a variety of colors, including white, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in moisture retention.

Nidorella varia is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
