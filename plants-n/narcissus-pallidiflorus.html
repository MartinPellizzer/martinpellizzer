Narcissus pallidiflorus

Narcissus pallidiflorus, commonly known as the pale-flowered daffodil, is a species native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, strap-like leaves and a single, bell-shaped flower with a pale yellow corona. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and blooms in early spring. Its flowers have a delicate, sweet fragrance and are often used in gardens for their ornamental value.

Narcissus pallidiflorus is a perennial bulbous plant that prefers well-drained soil and full sun.
