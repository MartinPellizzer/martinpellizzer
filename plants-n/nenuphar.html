Nenuphar

Nenuphar, commonly known as the water lily, is a flowering plant belonging to the Nymphaeaceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom above the water surface. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and lakes. Its roots are submerged, anchoring the plant while absorbing nutrients from the water.

Nenuphar plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
