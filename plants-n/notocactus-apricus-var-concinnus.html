Notocactus apricus var. concinnus

Notocactus apricus var. concinnus, commonly known as the golden notocactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are typically green or yellowish in color. The plant produces bright yellow flowers during the summer months, which are clustered at the tips of the stems. It is a slow-growing plant that thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and indoor spaces.
