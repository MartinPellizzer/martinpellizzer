Neoporteria chorosensis

Neoporteria chorosensis, commonly known as the Choros mountain orchid, is a rare and endemic species found in the high-altitude regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, pseudobulbous structure with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers with a complex coloration pattern, typically featuring purple and white hues. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
