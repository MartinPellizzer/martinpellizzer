Nierembergia coerulea

Nierembergia coerulea, commonly known as bluebell vine, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in spring. The plant features climbing stems with opposite, heart-shaped leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sun.

Nierembergia coerulea is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
