Notocactus tabularis var. setispinus

Notocactus tabularis var. setispinus, commonly known as the Spiny Barrel Cactus, is a unique cactus species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, cylindrical stem that can grow up to several meters in height, providing a striking visual presence in its natural habitat. The plant features prominent, spiny areoles that bear clusters of white or pale yellow flowers, which bloom during the dry season. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it has a shallow root system adapted to survive in nutrient-poor soils.

This variety is prized for its hardiness and ability to thrive in low-water environments, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
