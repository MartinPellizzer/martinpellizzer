Neoregelia wurdackii

Neoregelia wurdackii, commonly known as the Wurdack's bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup to collect rainwater. The plant produces vibrant, showy inflorescences that can range from red to orange, adding visual interest to its appearance. Its foliage is often adorned with intricate patterns and can display a variety of colors, including green, silver, and variegated markings.

Neoregelia wurdackii thrives in humid environments with bright, indirect light and is popular among bromeliad enthusiasts for its ornamental value.
