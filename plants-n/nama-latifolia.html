Nama latifolia

Nama latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf nama, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions. It is a perennial shrub that can reach heights of up to 1 meter.

Nama latifolia is adapted to withstand drought conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
