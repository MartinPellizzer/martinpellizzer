Nasturtium aucheri

Nasturtium aucheri, commonly known as the garden nasturtium, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that come in shades of orange, yellow, and red. The plant produces small, edible seeds that are often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are also edible and have a peppery taste, similar to watercress.

Nasturtium aucheri is typically grown as an ornamental plant but is also valued for its edible parts and pest-repelling properties.
