Nelumbo pentapetala

Nelumbo pentapetala, commonly known as the sacred lotus, is a water-living plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that bloom on long, erect stems above the water surface. The plant has large, rounded leaves that are typically floating or partially submerged. Its flowers are usually pink or white, with five petals and a central cluster of stamens.

Nelumbo pentapetala is known for its symbolic significance in various cultures and its ability to thrive in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats.
