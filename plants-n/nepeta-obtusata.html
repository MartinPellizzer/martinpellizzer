Nepeta obtusata

Nepeta obtusata, commonly known as catmint, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage is often used in herbal teas and has a mild minty scent.

Nepeta obtusata is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
