Najas gracillima

Najas gracillima, commonly known as the slender water milfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in the Northern Hemisphere.

It has narrow, ribbon-like leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem, which helps it maximize light absorption in water. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged, though some may emerge just below the water surface. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Najas gracillima is often found in still or slow-moving waters such as ponds, lakes, and ditches.
