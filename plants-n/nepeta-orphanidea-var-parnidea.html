Nepeta orphanidea var. parnidea

Nepeta orphanidea var. parnidea, commonly known as the Parnidian catmint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or violet flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage is often fragrant and has a mild mint-like scent.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
