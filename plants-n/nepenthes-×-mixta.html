Nepenthes × mixta

Nepenthes × mixta, commonly known as the hybrid pitcher plant, is a fascinating species resulting from the cross between Nepenthes alata and Nepenthes ventrata.

It is characterized by its unique, elongated pitcher-shaped leaves that secrete a digestive fluid to trap and digest insects. The plant thrives in tropical rainforest environments, where it often grows as an epiphyte on tree trunks or branches. Its pitchers vary in color, typically ranging from green to reddish hues, and can reach lengths of up to 30 centimeters.

This hybrid exhibits a combination of traits from both parent species, making it a subject of interest in botanical studies and horticulture.
