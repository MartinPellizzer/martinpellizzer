Nigella arvensis subsp. latilabris

Nigella arvensis subsp.

latilabris, commonly known as the broad-lipped cowplant, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family. It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its broader, more rounded petals compared to other Nigella species. It typically grows in dry, disturbed soils in temperate regions.

This plant is often found in meadows, roadsides, and along field edges.
