Nanorrhinum abhaicum

Nanorrhinum abhaicum, commonly known as the Abha desert bean, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the Arabian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its legume pods and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant is adapted to harsh desert conditions, with small, waxy leaves to reduce water loss. Its flowers are typically pale yellow or greenish-white, and it produces small, dry pods containing seeds.

Due to its ecological importance and limited distribution, it is considered a species of conservation concern.
