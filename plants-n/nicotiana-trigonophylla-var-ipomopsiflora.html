Nicotiana trigonophylla var. ipomopsiflora

Nicotiana trigonophylla var. ipomopsiflora, commonly known as the purple-flowered tobacco, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers, which bloom in clusters along the stems. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a hairy surface, and the stems are hairy as well.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its showy blooms.
