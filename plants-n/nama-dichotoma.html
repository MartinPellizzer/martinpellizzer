Nama dichotoma

Nama dichotoma, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and linear leaves. The plant is typically found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind.

Nama dichotoma is often used in ornamental gardening due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
