Nepeta multibracteata var. candeliana

Nepeta multibracteata var. candeliana, commonly known as the candle plant, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and clusters of small, pale blue to violet flowers. The plant produces whorled leaves that are hairy and often have a strong, mint-like aroma. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is drought-tolerant once established.

This variety is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
