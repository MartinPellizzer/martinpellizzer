Narcissus italicus subsp. chrysanthus

Narcissus italicus subsp.

chrysanthus, commonly known as the golden narcissus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It produces showy, cup-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or golden in color, with a white or green center.

The plant features strap-like, linear leaves that are grass-like in appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in rocky or coastal areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and is commonly cultivated in gardens.
