Neoregelia farinosa

Neoregelia farinosa, commonly known as the "Flame Plant," is a striking bromeliad native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful bracts that can range from red to orange and yellow, often resembling flowers. The plant features a rosette of narrow, pointed leaves that are covered in a fine, powdery coating, giving it a unique appearance. It thrives in humid, bright indirect light and requires well-draining soil.

Neoregelia farinosa is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
