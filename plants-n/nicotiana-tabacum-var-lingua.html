Nicotiana tabacum var. lingua

Nicotiana tabacum var. lingua, commonly known as the "tongue tobacco," is a variant of the tobacco plant characterized by its unique leaf morphology.

The leaves are elongated and often have a wavy or tongue-like appearance, giving the plant its distinctive name. This variety is native to the Americas and is known for its high nicotine content. It is primarily cultivated for its leaves, which are used in the production of tobacco products.

The plant is also valued in botanical gardens for its ornamental appeal and historical significance.
