Nonea caspica subsp. melanocarpa

Nonea caspica subsp.

melanocarpa, commonly known as the black-fruited Nonea, is a shrub native to Central Asia. It is characterized by its small, dark-colored berries that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife.

The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils. Its leaves are usually small and grayish-green, adapted to reduce water loss.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
