Nectandra grandiflora var. longifolia

Nectandra grandiflora var. longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved sandarac tree, is a tropical evergreen species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a yellowish or greenish hue. The plant produces a resinous sap, which has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive. Its leaves are elongated and leathery, providing a dense canopy in its natural habitat.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
