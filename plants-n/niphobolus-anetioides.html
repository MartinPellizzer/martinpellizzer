Niphobolus anetioides

Niphobolus anetioides, commonly known as the Aneth-like leafy spurge, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-white flowers that appear in clusters. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with a distinctive, aromatic scent reminiscent of anise. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Niphobolus anetioides is a perennial herb that plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
