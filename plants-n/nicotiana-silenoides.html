Nicotiana silenoides

Nicotiana silenoides, commonly known as the silverleaf nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and produces clusters of small, pale blue or purple flowers. The plant belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and peppers. It is often found in dry, open habitats and can thrive in poor soil conditions.

The leaves and flowers are sometimes used in traditional medicine, though the plant contains toxic compounds.
