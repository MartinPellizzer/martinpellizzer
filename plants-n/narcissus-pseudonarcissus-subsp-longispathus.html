Narcissus pseudonarcissus subsp. longispathus

Narcissus pseudonarcissus subsp.

longispathus, commonly known as the long-spurred daffodil, is a subspecies of the genus Narcissus native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves and a single, showy flower with a long, slender corona.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. Its flowers bloom in early spring, producing bright yellow petals and a central, trumpet-shaped structure.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant appearance.
