Navarretia prolifera subsp. lutea

Navarretia prolifera subsp.

lutea, commonly known as the yellow navarretia, is a flowering plant native to California and Baja California. It is a low-growing perennial herb that produces clusters of small, yellow flowers in early spring.

The plant typically grows in open, rocky habitats and is often found in coastal scrub and grassland environments. Its flowers are bilaterally symmetrical and have five petals, with the lower petal being distinctly yellow.

The subspecies is distinguished by its pale yellow to orange flowers, which contrast with the green foliage.
