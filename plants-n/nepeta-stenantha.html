Nepeta stenantha

Nepeta stenantha, commonly known as narrowleaf catmint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant produces small, tubular blue or violet flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a fuzzy texture.

Nepeta stenantha is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and mild mint-like scent.
