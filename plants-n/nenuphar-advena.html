Nenuphar advena

Nenuphar advena, commonly known as the slender swamp waterlily, is a aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves that form a dense canopy on the water surface. The plant produces showy, white to pink flowers that bloom in the early morning and close by midday. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Nenuphar advena plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
