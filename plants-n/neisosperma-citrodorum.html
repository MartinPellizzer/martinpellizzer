Neisosperma citrodorum

Neisosperma citrodorum, commonly known as the citron tree, is a species of flowering plant in the Rutaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its large, fragrant, yellow-orange fruit. The plant has dark green, glossy leaves and produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters. The fruit is widely used in culinary and medicinal applications due to its high vitamin C content.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions.
