Nardophyllum deserticola

Nardophyllum deserticola, commonly known as the desert nard, is a rare flowering plant native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant has adapted to harsh desert conditions with deep root systems and a low, sprawling growth habit. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
