Neottia speciosa

Neottia speciosa, commonly known as the showy orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands, where it thrives in partial to full shade. Neottia speciosa has a rhizomatous root system and produces small, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating.

Its flowering period usually occurs in late spring to early summer, making it a notable species in botanical gardens and natural habitats.
