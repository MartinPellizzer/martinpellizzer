Neoschroetera divaricata

Neoschroetera divaricata, commonly known as the zigzag plant, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, zigzagging stems that give it its common name. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. It produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are followed by elongated, fleshy fruits.

Neoschroetera divaricata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded, humid environments.
