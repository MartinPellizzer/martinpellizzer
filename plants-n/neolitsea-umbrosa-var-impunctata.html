Neolitsea umbrosa var. impunctata

Neolitsea umbrosa var. impunctata, commonly known as the smoothleaf sweetleaf, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate, ovate to elliptical leaves that are smooth and often glossy on the upper surface. Its small, fragrant flowers are typically white or pale green and appear in clusters, followed by small, round berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

This species thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland habitats.
