Nania suberosa

Nania suberosa, commonly known as the roughleaf nania, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody base and hairy, rough-textured leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, Nania suberosa is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
