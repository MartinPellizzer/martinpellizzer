Narcissus triandrus subsp. triandrus

Narcissus triandrus subsp.

triandrus, commonly known as the Spanish daffodil, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family. It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and a single, showy yellow flower with a cup-shaped corona.

The plant is native to the Iberian Peninsula and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It blooms in early spring, typically between March and April.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, trumpet-like flowers.
