Nuphar spathulifera

Nuphar spathulifera, commonly known as the broad-leaved water shield, is a aquatic plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, shield-shaped leaves that float on the water surface, with the petioles submerged in the water. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its roots are rhizomatous, allowing it to spread and form dense colonies in still or slow-moving water.

Nuphar spathulifera plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various water organisms.
