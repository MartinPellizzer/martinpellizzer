Nicandra physalodes

Nicandra physalodes, commonly known as the silver vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces distinctive, inflated, green berries that turn red when mature. It thrives in warm climates with well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Nicandra physalodes is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and unique fruiting habit.
