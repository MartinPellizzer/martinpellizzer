Normania triphylla

Normania triphylla, commonly known as the three-leafed bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has trifoliate leaves, meaning each leaf is divided into three parts, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its ornamental value has led to its cultivation in various horticultural settings.
