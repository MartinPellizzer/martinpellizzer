Nepeta citriodora

Nepeta citriodora, commonly known as lemon balm, is a perennial herb native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, glossy leaves that emit a distinct lemony scent when crushed. The plant produces small, pale blue or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and herbal use.

Its essential oils, rich in citral, are widely used in aromatherapy and natural remedies.
