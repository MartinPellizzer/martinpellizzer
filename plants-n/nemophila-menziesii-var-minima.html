Nemophila menziesii var. minima

Nemophila menziesii var. minima, commonly known as the dwarf fairybells, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It features clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has slender, hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded to partially shaded woodland areas.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is commonly cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
