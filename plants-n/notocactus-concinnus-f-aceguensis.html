Notocactus concinnus f. aceguensis

Notocactus concinnus f. aceguensis, commonly known as the Aceguense Notocactus, is a rare and unique cactus species native to a specific region in South America.

It is characterized by its short, columnar stems that are often adorned with clusters of small, spiny areoles. The plant produces vibrant, funnel-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow or orange, adding a striking visual contrast to its green stem. It thrives in arid environments with well-draining soil and requires minimal water to survive.

Due to its limited natural range and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a conservation concern.
