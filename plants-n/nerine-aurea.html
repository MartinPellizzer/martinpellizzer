Nerine aurea

Nerine aurea, commonly known as the golden nerine, is a bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant features narrow, strap-like leaves that are often bluish-green in appearance. It is well-suited for cultivation in USDA hardiness zones 6 through 9.

Nerine aurea blooms in late summer to early autumn, adding vibrant color to gardens during the transition into cooler seasons.
