Nuphar ludoviciana

Nuphar ludoviciana, commonly known as the Louisiana marsh spatflower, is a water plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its roots are rhizomatous, allowing it to spread through vegetative growth.

Nuphar ludoviciana plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for aquatic organisms.
