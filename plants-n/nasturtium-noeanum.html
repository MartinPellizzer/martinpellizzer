Nasturtium noeanum

Nasturtium noeanum, commonly known as the common nasturtium, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Tropaeolaceae.

It is characterized by its vibrant, rounded leaves and showy, edible flowers that come in shades of orange and yellow. Native to South America, this plant thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and culinary uses. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain seeds, which can be sown in the fall for spring growth.

It is also noted for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
