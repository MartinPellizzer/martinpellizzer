Napaea crispa

Napaea crispa, commonly known as the wrinkled bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems covered in soft, hairy foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. Its leaves are characterized by their wavy, crinkled edges, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Napaea crispa thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and borders.
