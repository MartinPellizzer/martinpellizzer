Narcissus bulbocodium var. bicolor

Narcissus bulbocodium var. bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored narcissus, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its small, bell-shaped flowers that display a striking two-toned pattern, typically white with a yellow center. The plant produces a single bulb that stores nutrients for the next growing season. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge in early spring.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and naturalized areas.
