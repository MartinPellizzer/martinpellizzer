Nephthytis hallaei

Nephthytis hallaei, commonly known as the Hall's prayer plant, is a tropical flowering plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence known as a spadix, surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. The plant features dark green, velvety leaves with striking silver or white veins, which give it a striking appearance. Nephthytis hallaei is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and air-purifying qualities.
