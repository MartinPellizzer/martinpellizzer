Nicotiana longiflora var. oligantha

Nicotiana longiflora var. oligantha, commonly known as the long-flowered tobacco, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated blue to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early summer. The plant is a herbaceous perennial with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with silver. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical features make it an attractive and relatively easy-to-grow plant for both amateur and professional gardeners.
