Neomammillaria bicolor

Neomammillaria bicolor, commonly known as the pink fairy or pink mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and its distinctive pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant features prominent tubercles and radial spines, which are typical of the Mammillariaceae family. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Neomammillaria bicolor is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and ease of care.
