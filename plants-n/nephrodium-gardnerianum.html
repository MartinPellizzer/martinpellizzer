Nephrodium gardnerianum

Nephrodium gardnerianum, commonly known as the Gardner's nephroid, is a rare and unique fern species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, kidney-shaped fronds that are often deeply lobed, giving it a striking visual appearance. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in tropical rainforests. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, a common feature among ferns.

Due to habitat destruction, Nephrodium gardnerianum is considered endangered and is of great interest to botanists and conservationists.
