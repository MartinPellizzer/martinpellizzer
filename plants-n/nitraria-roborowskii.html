Nitraria roborowskii

Nitraria roborowskii, commonly known as the desert raisin, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, fleshy, yellowish fruits that resemble raisins, which are edible and often used in traditional cuisines. The plant has adapted to harsh environments through its deep root system and ability to store water in its stems. It thrives in sandy soils and is often found in desert ecosystems.

Nitraria roborowskii plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing food for wildlife in its native habitat.
