Nepeta reticulata var. tejedensis

Nepeta reticulata var. tejedensis, commonly known as the Tejed catmint, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine hairs and produces small, pale blue to purple flowers in whorls along the stem. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a fragrant, mint-like aroma. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
