Nectandra panamensis

Nectandra panamensis, commonly known as the Panama sandalwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark-colored wood. The plant typically grows in humid lowland forests and has smooth, greyish bark. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical in shape, often with a glossy surface.

The flowers are small and clustered, producing fragrant, yellowish blooms that attract various pollinators.
