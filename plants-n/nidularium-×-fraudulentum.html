Nidularium × fraudulentum

Nidularium × fraudulentum, commonly known as the hybrid spider orchid, is a unique hybrid species resulting from the cross between Nidularium elongatum and Nidularium ruschii.

It is characterized by its intricate, spider-like appearance with long, slender inflorescences and delicate, elongated flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in the understorey of tropical rainforests. Its botanical significance lies in its role as a hybrid that showcases the genetic diversity within the Nidularium genus.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a threatened species in its native range.
