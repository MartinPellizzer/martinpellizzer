Narcissus × bernardi

Narcissus × bernardi, commonly known as the Berndt daffodil, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between N. tazetta and N. poeticus.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers with a distinctive trumpet-shaped corona. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, featuring strap-like, grassy leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often blooming in early spring.

The flowers come in a range of yellow to orange hues, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.
