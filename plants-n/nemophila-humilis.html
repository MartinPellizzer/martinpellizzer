Nemophila humilis

Nemophila humilis, commonly known as the common blue-eyed Mary, is a low-growing annual plant native to California.

It produces small, showy blue or purple flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has a hairy stem and leaves that are typically heart-shaped and serrated. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial shade, often found in woodland areas.

Nemophila humilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and role in supporting local pollinators.
