Nidularium wawreanum

Nidularium wawreanum, commonly known as the Wawra's nest, is a rare and unique bromeliad native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, cup-shaped rosette of dark green, waxy leaves that form a central basin to collect rainwater. The plant produces striking red flowers that bloom in the summer, adding a vibrant contrast to its foliage. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
