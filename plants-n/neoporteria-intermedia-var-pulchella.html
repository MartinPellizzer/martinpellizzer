Neoporteria intermedia var. pulchella

Neoporteria intermedia var. pulchella, commonly known as the elegant orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically feature a combination of white, pink, and purple hues. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with each new growth emerging from a lateral bud on the rhizome. Its leaves are elongated and lanceolate, often with a waxy texture that helps reduce water loss.

This variety is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
