Neocinnamomum mekongense

Neocinnamomum mekongense, commonly known as Mekong cinnamon, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other aromatic plants such as cinnamon and laurel. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant, reddish-brown bark that resembles true cinnamon. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by dark blue berries.

Due to its unique aroma and potential medicinal properties, it is of interest to botanists and herbalists alike.
