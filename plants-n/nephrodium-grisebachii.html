Nephrodium grisebachii

Nephrodium grisebachii, commonly known as the gray kidney fern, is a unique member of the Osmundaceae family, characterized by its distinctive, kidney-shaped fronds.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions like Indonesia and the Philippines, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant features a short, thick rhizome with a fibrous root system, supporting its growth in tropical forest understories. Its fronds are pinnately compound, with a broad, leathery texture and a grayish-green coloration that gives it its common name.

Nephrodium grisebachii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
