Nicolasia coronata subsp. planifolia

Nicolasia coronata subsp.

planifolia, commonly known as the flat-leaved crownflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, flat, leaf-like bracts that surround its small, daisy-like flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions. Its flowers are usually pale blue or white, and it blooms during the cooler months.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and modest blooms.
