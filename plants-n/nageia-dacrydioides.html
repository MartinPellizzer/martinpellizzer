Nageia dacrydioides

Nageia dacrydioides, commonly known as the New Zealand kauri, is a large evergreen tree native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Araucariaceae family and is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and distinctive, whorled arrangement of leaves. The tree has a unique bark that is fibrous and flaky, and its cones are pendulous, containing edible seeds.

It plays a significant ecological role in New Zealand's forests, providing habitat and contributing to the local biodiversity.
