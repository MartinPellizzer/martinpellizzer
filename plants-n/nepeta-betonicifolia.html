Nepeta betonicifolia

Nepeta betonicifolia, commonly known as catmint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces blue to purple flowers in whorled clusters, typically blooming in summer. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.

Nepeta betonicifolia is also popular in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
