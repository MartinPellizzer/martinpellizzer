Neomammillaria pilispina

Neomammillaria pilispina, commonly known as the ghost cactus, is a small, spineless cactus native to Mexico.

It features a cylindrical stem that is typically blue-green in color, with a slightly ribbed texture. The plant produces white flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a delicate touch to its appearance. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water and well-draining soil.

Neomammillaria pilispina is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique shape and low maintenance requirements.
