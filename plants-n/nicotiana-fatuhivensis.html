Nicotiana fatuhivensis

Nicotiana fatuhivensis, commonly known as the Fatuhivensis tobacco, is a species native to the island of Vanuatu in the South Pacific.

It is a herbaceous plant belonging to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It is notable for its historical and cultural significance to the indigenous people of Vanuatu, who have used it for traditional purposes.

Its botanical features make it an important subject of study in both ecological and ethnobotanical research.
