Najas setacea

Najas setacea, commonly known as the narrow-leaf watermilfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, ribbon-like leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged, with male and female flowers occurring on separate plants. Najas setacea thrives in shallow, nutrient-rich waters and plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various invertebrates.

Its rapid growth can sometimes lead to dense populations, impacting water flow and light penetration in lakes and ponds.
