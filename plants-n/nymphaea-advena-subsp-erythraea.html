Nymphaea advena subsp. erythraea

Nymphaea advena subsp.

erythraea, commonly known as the Red lotus, is a water-lily species native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its large, showy pink to red flowers that bloom above the water surface.

The plant has broad, heart-shaped leaves that float on the water's surface, providing a habitat for various aquatic organisms. Its roots are anchored in the muddy substrate at the bottom of ponds and lakes.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological role in aquatic ecosystems.
