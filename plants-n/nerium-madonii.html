Nerium madonii

Nerium madonii, commonly known as the Madonii oleander, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 2 to 6 meters. The plant is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and showy clusters of pink or white flowers. It produces small, woody capsules that contain numerous seeds.

Nerium madonii is known for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
