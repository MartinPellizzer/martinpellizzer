Nepeta czegemensis

Nepeta czegemensis, commonly known as Carpathian catmint, is a perennial herb native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its aromatic foliage contains essential oils, giving it a minty scent.

Nepeta czegemensis is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and mild medicinal properties.
