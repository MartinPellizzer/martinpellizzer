Neomammillaria neoformosa

Neomammillaria neoformosa, commonly known as the Mexican snowball cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its rounded, ribbed stems that often develop a white or grayish coating. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for succulent gardens and rockeries.
