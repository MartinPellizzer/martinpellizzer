Notholaena atropurpurea

Notholaena atropurpurea, commonly known as purple bottlebrush, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features striking, dense clusters of bright red to purple flowers that resemble bottlebrushes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, sandy or loamy soils.

Notholaena atropurpurea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
