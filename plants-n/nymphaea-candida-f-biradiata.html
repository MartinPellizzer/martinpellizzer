Nymphaea candida f. biradiata

Nymphaea candida f. biradiata, commonly known as the white lotus variant, is a botanical variety of the water lily native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, white, fragrant flowers that bloom above the water surface. The plant has broad, heart-shaped leaves that float on the water's surface, providing habitat for aquatic life. Its roots are submerged, anchoring the plant in shallow aquatic environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and traditional use in cultural and medicinal practices.
