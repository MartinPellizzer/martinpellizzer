Neumayera filicaulis

Neumayera filicaulis, commonly known as the Antarctic hairgrass, is a flowering plant native to the continent of Antarctica.

It is a small, perennial grass that grows in rocky soil and is adapted to the extreme cold and nutrient-poor conditions of its environment. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces tiny, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, inconspicuous flowers. Neumayera filicaulis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for microorganisms in the harsh Antarctic ecosystem.
