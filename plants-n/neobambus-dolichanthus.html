Neobambus dolichanthus

Neobambus dolichanthus, commonly known as the long-flowered bamboo, is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant produces panicles of small, greenish-white flowers, which are typically arranged in a loose, branching pattern. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth texture and a slightly hairy underside.

Neobambus dolichanthus is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its elegant appearance and adaptability to various soil types.
