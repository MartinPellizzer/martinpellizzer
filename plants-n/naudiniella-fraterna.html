Naudiniella fraterna

Naudiniella fraterna, commonly known as the fraterna fern, is a small, delicate fern species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest undergrowth, where it thrives in high humidity and partial shade. The plant features slender, arching fronds with a fine, feathery texture, and its spores are produced on the underside of the leaves. It is characterized by its compact size and is often found in rocky or clay-rich soils.

Due to its ornamental appeal, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
