Nepeta laevigata

Nepeta laevigata, commonly known as catmint, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features gray-green, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Nepeta laevigata is also noted for its mild mint-like scent, which is particularly appealing to cats.
