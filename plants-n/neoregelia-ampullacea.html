Neoregelia ampullacea

Neoregelia ampullacea, commonly known as the Ampullacea bromeliad, is a striking plant native to Brazil.

It is a member of the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central water-holding cup. The plant features vibrant, long-lasting flowers that can range in color from red to orange, often appearing in late summer. Its foliage is typically a deep green with a waxy texture, and it thrives in bright, indirect light.

Neoregelia ampullacea is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
