Nastus paniculatus

Nastus paniculatus, commonly known as the panicled nasturtium, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to South America and is characterized by its vibrant yellow-orange flowers and pungent, peppery scent. The plant features alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowering stems produce elongated panicles, which are clusters of small, showy blooms.

Nastus paniculatus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
