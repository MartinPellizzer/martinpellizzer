Nama megapotamica

Nama megapotamica, commonly known as the Andean morning glory, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces vibrant blue or violet blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Nama megapotamica is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
