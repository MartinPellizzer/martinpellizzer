Nama ehrenbergii

Nama ehrenbergii, commonly known as the desert lily, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming during the rainy season. Nama ehrenbergii is a succulent, meaning it has adapted to store moisture in its stems and leaves.

It plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing food and shelter for various desert animals.
