Nymphaea candolleana

Nymphaea candolleana, commonly known as the Candollean water lily, is a species of aquatic plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes. Its roots anchor it in the mud at the bottom of water bodies, while its leaves provide a habitat for various aquatic organisms.

Nymphaea candolleana plays an important role in maintaining the ecological balance of its native habitats.
