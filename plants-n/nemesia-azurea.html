Nemesia azurea

Nemesia azurea, commonly known as the blueweed or blue nemesia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a member of the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, clustered flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and thrives in well-drained soils. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Nemesia azurea is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
