Nectandra smithii

Nectandra smithii, commonly known as the white sand cedar, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Caribbean islands.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurel and avocado trees. The plant is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and elliptical, glossy leaves. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in panicles, and it produces aromatic, woody fruits.

Nectandra smithii is often found in moist, well-drained soils in lowland forest areas.
