Nepeta subincisa var. subincisa

Nepeta subincisa var. subincisa, commonly known as catmint, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features aromatic, gray-green leaves with a strong mint-like scent, and its flowers are typically blue to purple in color. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Nepeta subincisa var. subincisa is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.
