Neottia uraiensis

Neottia uraiensis, commonly known as the Uraei orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stem and a single, showy flower. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and swamps. Its flowers are usually greenish-yellow with a distinctive hooded lip, and it relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient absorption.

Neottia uraiensis is noted for its limited distribution and is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
