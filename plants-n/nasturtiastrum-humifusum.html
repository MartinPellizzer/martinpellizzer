Nasturtiastrum humifusum

Nasturtiastrum humifusum, commonly known as the creeping nasturtium, is a low-growing plant characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves.

It produces vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a distinctive black spot at the base. The plant is native to South America and thrives in warm, sunny environments with well-draining soil. Its leaves are edible and have a peppery flavor, making it a popular choice for ornamental and culinary use.

Nasturtiastrum humifusum is often used in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and its ability to attract pollinators.
