Nerisyrenia incana

Nerisyrenia incana, commonly known as the silky sandwort, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with a fibrous root system adapted to survive in dry conditions. The plant features slender, hairy stems that are often prostrate or ascending, and its leaves are linear to lanceolate, covered in fine, silky hairs. It produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in spring.

The species is noted for its drought tolerance and is often found in sandy or gravelly soils.
