Nauclea rubella

Nauclea rubella, commonly known as the red flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, adding vibrant color to its surroundings. The plant has compound leaves with elongated leaflets and produces small, round fruits that contain seeds. Nauclea rubella is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its showy flowers and adaptability to various soil types.

It also plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food and habitat for various insects and birds.
