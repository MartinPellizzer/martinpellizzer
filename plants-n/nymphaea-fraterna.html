Nymphaea fraterna

Nymphaea fraterna, commonly known as the sacred lotus, is a water lily native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and yellow. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes. Its leaves are broad and floating, while its roots anchor it in the muddy substrate.

Nymphaea fraterna is known for its symbolic and cultural significance in various Asian traditions.
