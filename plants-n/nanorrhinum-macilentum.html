Nanorrhinum macilentum

Nanorrhinum macilentum, commonly known as the slender sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in well-drained sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its flowering period typically occurs during the warmer months, contributing to its ecological role in supporting pollinators.

Nanorrhinum macilentum is often used in native landscaping due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
