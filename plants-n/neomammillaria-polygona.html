Neomammillaria polygona

Neomammillaria polygona, commonly known as the Desert Cactus or Mexican Star Cactus, is a species of flowering cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are covered in white to pale blue spines. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is a slow-growing species that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Neomammillaria polygona is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and resilience.
