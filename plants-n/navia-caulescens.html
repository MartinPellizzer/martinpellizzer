Navia caulescens

Navia caulescens, commonly known as the slender navia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its upright, slender stem and small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Navia caulescens is adapted to high-altitude environments, thriving in well-drained soils and cool, moist conditions.

It is often found in rocky or alpine habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
