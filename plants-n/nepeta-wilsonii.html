Nepeta wilsonii

Nepeta wilsonii, commonly known as Wilson's catmint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant produces small, tubular blue or violet flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, giving them a fuzzy texture.

Nepeta wilsonii is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
