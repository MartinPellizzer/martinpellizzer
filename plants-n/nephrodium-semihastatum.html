Nephrodium semihastatum

Nephrodium semihastatum, commonly known as the half-hastate nephroid, is a unique fern species characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology.

Its fronds are typically bipinnate, with a complex arrangement of pinnae that give the plant a delicate, lace-like appearance. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its reproductive structures, including sporangia, are located on the underside of the fronds, a common feature in ferns.

The botanical study of Nephrodium semihastatum provides insights into the evolutionary adaptations of ferns in diverse ecological niches.
