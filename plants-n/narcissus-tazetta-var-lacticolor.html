Narcissus tazetta var. lacticolor

Narcissus tazetta var. lacticolor, commonly known as the milk-white daffodil, is a variety of the common daffodil characterized by its pale, almost white petals.

It originates from the Mediterranean region and is known for its early blooming period, typically in late winter to early spring. The plant features a short, narrow stem with one to three flowers, each having a trumpet-shaped corona. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a basal rosette.

This variety is prized for its delicate appearance and is often cultivated in gardens for ornamental purposes.
