Nolana laxa

Nolana laxa, commonly known as the woolly morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue or white flowers that bloom in the morning. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Nolana laxa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
