Nectandra furcata

Nectandra furcata, commonly known as the fragrant wood or false sandalwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark brown bark and glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, round fruits containing aromatic seeds. Its wood is valued for its fragrance and is used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.

Nectandra furcata thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.
