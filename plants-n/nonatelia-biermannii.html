Nonatelia biermannii

Nonatelia biermannii, commonly known as the Biermann's ghost orchid, is a rare and elusive orchid species native to the rainforests of Queensland, Australia.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that are typically white or pale green, with a unique, almost translucent appearance. The plant has a short, hairy stem and produces a single, narrow leaf that is often covered in a fine layer of dust-like particles. Its growth is highly specialized, often occurring on the trunks of trees in shaded, humid environments.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, Nonatelia biermannii is considered critically endangered and is of great interest to botanists and conservationists.
