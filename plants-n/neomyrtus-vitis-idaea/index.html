Neomyrtus Vitis-Idaea

Neomyrtus vitis-idaea is a rare and elusive plant found in the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas. It belongs to the Ericaceae family, commonly known as the heath or heather family. This plant is characterized by its small, clustered leaves and delicate, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. Due to its limited distribution and fragile habitat, it is considered a conservation concern. Its unique appearance and ecological significance make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists alike.
