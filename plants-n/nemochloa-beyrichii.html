Nemochloa beyrichii

Nemochloa beyrichii, commonly known as the water clover, is a species of aquatic plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, floating leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are typically borne on short stalks and are pollinated by wind.

Nemochloa beyrichii plays a role in water filtration and provides habitat for small aquatic organisms.
