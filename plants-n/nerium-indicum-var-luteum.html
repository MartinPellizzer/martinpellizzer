Nerium indicum var. luteum

Nerium indicum var. luteum, commonly known as the yellow oleander, is a variant of the oleander plant native to southern Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range from yellow to orange in color. The leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a slightly waxy texture. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

The plant is also noted for its resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.
