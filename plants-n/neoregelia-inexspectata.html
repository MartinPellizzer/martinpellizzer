Neoregelia inexspectata

Neoregelia inexspectata, commonly known as the Crimson Fire Bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the rainforests of Brazil.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, red-pink inflorescence that contrasts sharply with its dark green foliage. The plant features a rosette-shaped leaf structure that collects rainwater, creating a microhabitat for small aquatic organisms. Its leaves are often covered in a waxy cuticle, which helps reduce water loss in its humid, shaded environment.

Neoregelia inexspectata is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
