Notodanthonia diemenica

Notodanthonia diemenica, commonly known as the Tasmanian wallaby grass, is a perennial grass species native to Tasmania.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted leaves and compact growth habit, typically forming dense mats on the ground. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky outcrops. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are loosely arranged, producing small, pale flowers.

Notodanthonia diemenica plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.
