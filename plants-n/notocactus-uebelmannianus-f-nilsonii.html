Notocactus uebelmannianus f. nilsonii

Notocactus uebelmannianus f. nilsonii, commonly known as the Nilson's notocactus, is a rare and striking cactus native to South America, specifically Brazil.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stem that can reach up to 3 meters in height, adorned with sharp, spiny areoles. The plant produces large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in the evening, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its rarity and specific care requirements, it is often cultivated in greenhouses or by experienced growers.
