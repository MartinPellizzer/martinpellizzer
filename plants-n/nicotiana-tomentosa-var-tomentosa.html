Nicotiana tomentosa var. tomentosa

Nicotiana tomentosa var. tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and tobacco. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in arid or semi-arid environments.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its historical use in traditional medicine.
