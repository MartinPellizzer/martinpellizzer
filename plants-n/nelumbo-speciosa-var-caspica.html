Nelumbo speciosa var. caspica

Nelumbo speciosa var. caspica, commonly known as the Caspian lotus, is a striking aquatic plant native to the Caspian Sea region.

It features large, showy pink to red flowers that bloom on tall, slender stalks above the water surface. The leaves are broad, rounded, and often float on the water, providing a striking visual display. The plant thrives in shallow, still waters with abundant sunlight.

Its unique botanical structure makes it a notable species in wetland ecosystems.
