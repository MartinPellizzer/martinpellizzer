Narcissus × romoi

Narcissus × romoi, commonly known as the Romo hybrid daffodil, is a cultivated variety resulting from crossbreeding between different narcissus species.

It typically features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in a range of vibrant colors, including yellow, orange, and white. The plant is characterized by its robust growth habit and is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to various garden conditions.

The flowers bloom in early spring, adding a striking visual element to spring gardens.
