Neomolinia japonica

Neomolinia japonica, commonly known as the Japanese feathergrass, is a perennial grass native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and delicate, feather-like leaves that give it a graceful appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially sunny conditions. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are wind-pollinated, and the plant is often used in ornamental landscaping for its aesthetic appeal.

Neomolinia japonica is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
