Nicotiana crispa

Nicotiana crispa, commonly known as the fringed tobacco plant, is a species native to the eastern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The flowers are typically pink to red, with distinctive fringed petals that give it its common name. It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is known for its ornamental value.

The plant contains nicotine and other alkaloids, making it both ecologically and botanically significant.
