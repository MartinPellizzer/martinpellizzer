Neriacanthus grandiflorus

Neriacanthus grandiflorus, commonly known as the giant flameflower, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 15 centimeters in diameter, displaying vibrant red and orange hues. The plant has broad, waxy leaves that are often arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Neriacanthus grandiflorus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
