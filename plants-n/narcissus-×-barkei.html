Narcissus × barkei

Narcissus × barkei, commonly known as the Barkei daffodil, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Narcissus tazetta and Narcissus poeticus.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers with a prominent, often trumpet-shaped corona. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, featuring strap-like, grassy leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

The flowers bloom in spring, adding vibrant color to gardens and landscapes.
