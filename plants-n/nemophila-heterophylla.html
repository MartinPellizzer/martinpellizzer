Nemophila heterophylla

Nemophila heterophylla, commonly known as the variable blue-eyed Mary, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, blue to purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are often variable in shape, ranging from lance-shaped to rounded, which gives it its scientific name.

Nemophila heterophylla is a member of the Boraginaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
