Narcissus hispanicus subsp. bujei

Narcissus hispanicus subsp.

bujei, commonly known as the Alboraya daffodil, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Spain and northern Africa. It produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically yellow with a darker throat, blooming in early spring.

The plant features strap-like, grassy leaves that are basal and often persist through the winter. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its early blooming habit and resistance to pests and diseases.
