Nassella tenuiculmis

Nassella tenuiculmis, commonly known as slender needlegrass, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and delicate, spike-like inflorescences. This plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It has small, inconspicuous flowers that produce lightweight seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Nassella tenuiculmis is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive, grass-like appearance and low maintenance requirements.
