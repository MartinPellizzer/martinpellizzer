Nephelea basilaris

Nephelea basilaris, commonly known as the basal nectar vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and produces clusters of small, fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its stems are woody and somewhat fibrous. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

The flowers attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
