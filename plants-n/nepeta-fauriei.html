Nepeta fauriei

Nepeta fauriei, commonly known as catmint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular blue or purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in herbal teas and have a mild minty scent.

Nepeta fauriei is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
