Neochilenia odieri

Neochilenia odieri, commonly known as the golden star cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in yellowish spines and clusters of bright yellow flowers. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to conserve water. Its flowers bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Neochilenia odieri is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its ecological role in its native habitat.
