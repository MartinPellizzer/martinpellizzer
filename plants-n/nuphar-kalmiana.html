Nuphar kalmiana

Nuphar kalmiana, commonly known as the Kalm's water lily, is a species of aquatic plant native to North America.

It typically grows in shallow freshwater habitats such as ponds and lakes. The plant features broad, floating leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. Its showy, white flowers bloom in early summer and are pollinated by insects.

Nuphar kalmiana plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
