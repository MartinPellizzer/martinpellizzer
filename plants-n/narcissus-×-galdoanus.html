Narcissus × galdoanus

Narcissus × galdoanus, commonly known as the Spanish daffodil, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Narcissus cyclaminiflorus and Narcissus tazetta.

It is characterized by its large, showy, funnel-shaped flowers that typically range in color from white to yellow. The plant is native to the Iberian Peninsula and thrives in well-drained soils in full sun. It produces a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and has a short, stout stem.

Narcissus × galdoanus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its early spring blooming.
