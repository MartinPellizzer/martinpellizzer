Nasa venezuelensis

Nasa venezuelensis, commonly known as the Venezuelan moonflower, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of Venezuela.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are ovate and pubescent, while its fruits are berries that turn from green to yellow when mature.

Nasa venezuelensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
