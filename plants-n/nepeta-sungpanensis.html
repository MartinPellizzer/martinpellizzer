Nepeta sungpanensis

Nepeta sungpanensis, commonly known as the Sungpan catmint, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage is often fragrant and has a mint-like scent, which is typical of many Nepeta species.

Nepeta sungpanensis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
