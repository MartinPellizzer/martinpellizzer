Nothofagus cliffortioides

Nothofagus cliffortioides, commonly known as the Antarctic beech, is a species of evergreen tree native to South America, particularly found in the southern Andes.

It belongs to the Nothofagus genus, which is closely related to the beech family Fagaceae. The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are usually smooth and dark green on top, with a lighter underside. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits that contain one or two seeds.

This species is well adapted to cold, high-altitude environments and plays an important role in the ecological systems of its native range.
