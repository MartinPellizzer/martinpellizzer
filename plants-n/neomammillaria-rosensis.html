Neomammillaria rosensis

Neomammillaria rosensis, commonly known as the Rosens Mountain cactus, is a species of flowering cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and is covered in dense, white spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking contrast to its green body. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Neomammillaria rosensis is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its hardiness and ornamental appeal.
