Nepeta parviflora

Nepeta parviflora, commonly known as catmint, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like mint and sage. The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal remedies due to their mild minty scent.

Nepeta parviflora is also popular in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
