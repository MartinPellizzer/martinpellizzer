Nassella cabrerae

Nassella cabrerae, commonly known as the Cabrera's needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and produces slender, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has small, wind-dispersed seeds that contribute to its ability to colonize disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native ecosystems in its range.
