Narcissus × maginae

Narcissus × maginae, commonly known as the Giant Crowned narcissus, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Narcissus tazetta and Narcissus poeticus.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers with a distinctive crown-like pattern on the tepals. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–50 cm and thrives in well-drained, fertile soil. It is native to the Iberian Peninsula and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

The flowers bloom in late winter to early spring, adding color to the landscape during the colder months.
