Nepeta balbisii

Nepeta balbisii, commonly known as Balbis catnip, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils, giving it a distinct minty scent.

Nepeta balbisii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
