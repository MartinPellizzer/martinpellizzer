Navarretia achilleifolia

Navarretia achilleifolia, commonly known as the laceflower or mountain laceflower, is a delicate annual flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its small, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming a rosette at the base.

Navarretia achilleifolia is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often found in wildflower gardens and natural habitats.
