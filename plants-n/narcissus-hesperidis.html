Narcissus hesperidis

Narcissus hesperidis, commonly known as the California daffodil, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to white. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of strap-like leaves that emerge in early spring before the flowers.

Narcissus hesperidis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
