Nasturtium benuense

Nasturtium benuense, commonly known as the Bornean watercress, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous plants. The plant is characterized by its bright yellow or orange flowers and edible leaves that have a peppery taste. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found near water sources.

Its botanical features make it a unique and valuable species in both ecological and culinary contexts.
