Nepeta hispanica subsp. hispanica

Nepeta hispanica subsp.

hispanica, commonly known as Spanish catmint, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It features aromatic, gray-green leaves and produces clusters of pale blue to violet flowers.

The plant is characterized by its square, hairy stems and is often found in rocky or dry soils. It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, known for its aromatic properties and medicinal uses.

This subspecies is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
