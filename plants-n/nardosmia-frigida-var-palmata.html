Nardosmia frigida var. palmata

Nardosmia frigida var. palmata, commonly known as the Arctic bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure and features small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. The plant thrives in cold, rocky environments and is adapted to withstand harsh winter conditions. Its leaves are usually broad and succulent, helping to retain moisture in nutrient-poor soils.

Nardosmia frigida var. palmata plays an important ecological role in polar ecosystems, providing food for various insect and bird species.
