Nectandra lineata

Nectandra lineata, commonly known as the yellow sand-wood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark. The plant typically grows in humid lowland forests and can reach heights of up to 30 meters. Its flowers are small and white, often clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces dark, oily seeds.

The wood is valued for its durability and resistance to decay, making it useful in construction and furniture-making.
