Nipponocalamus vaginatus

Nipponocalamus vaginatus, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or Japanese clumping bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its clumping growth habit and slender, erect stems. The plant features narrow, elongated leaves that are typically green and smooth in texture. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its elegant appearance and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Nipponocalamus vaginatus is also valued for its role in traditional Japanese gardens and its ecological benefits in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small organisms.
