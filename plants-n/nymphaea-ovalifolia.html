Nymphaea ovalifolia

Nymphaea ovalifolia, commonly known as the oval-leaf water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, oval-shaped leaves that float on the water's surface, often with a waxy texture that repels water. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, opening in the early morning and closing by midday. Its roots anchor it in shallow water, while its rhizomes help it spread across wetland areas.

Nymphaea ovalifolia plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various water organisms.
