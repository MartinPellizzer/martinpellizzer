Neuroloma nudicaule

Neuroloma nudicaule, commonly known as the naked-stemmed neuroloma, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is a small herbaceous species characterized by its simple, unbranched stem and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky areas. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and lack stipules, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The flowers are often white or pale in color, and the plant is known for its delicate, grass-like foliage.
