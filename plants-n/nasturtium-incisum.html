Nasturtium incisum

Nasturtium incisum, commonly known as the cut-leaved nasturtium, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Tropaeolaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, deeply lobed leaves. The plant produces vibrant orange or yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its stems are hairy and often trailing, making it suitable for container gardening or as a ground cover.

Nasturtium incisum is also valued for its edible leaves and seeds, which have a peppery flavor.
