Nothapodytes nimmoniana

Nothapodytes nimmoniana, commonly known as the yellow ghost tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and simple, alternate leaves that are elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by orange-red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
