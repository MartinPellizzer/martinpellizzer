Nymphaea candida

Nymphaea candida, commonly known as the white water lily, is a aquatic flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features broad, floating leaves that provide shade over the water's surface. Its flowers are white with a yellow center, blooming in the early morning and closing by midday. The plant has a rhizomatous root system that anchors it in muddy substrates.

Nymphaea candida plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by supporting biodiversity and improving water quality.
