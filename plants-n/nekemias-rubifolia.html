Nekemias rubifolia

Nekemias rubifolia, commonly known as the red-leafed shrub, is a flowering plant native to the humid forests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that turn a striking red or orange hue during the dry season. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically in shades of pink or white. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
