Notothixos cornifolius var. angustifolius

Notothixos cornifolius var. angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thistle, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, upright stems and lanceolate, dark green leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, purple to pink flowers in clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and resilient member of the Asteraceae family.
