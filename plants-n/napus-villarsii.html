Napus villarsii

Napus villarsii, commonly known as the Alpine mustard, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and mustard. This species is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and elongated, hairy leaves. It thrives in alpine environments, often growing at high altitudes where conditions are harsh.

Napus villarsii is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators in its native habitat.
