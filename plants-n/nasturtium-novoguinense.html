Nasturtium novoguinense

Nasturtium novoguinense, commonly known as the New Guinea nasturtium, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Tropaeolaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its stems are often trailing or climbing. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The edible flowers and leaves are also used in some culinary applications.
