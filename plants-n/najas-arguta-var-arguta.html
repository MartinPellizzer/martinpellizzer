Najas arguta var. arguta

Najas arguta var. arguta, commonly known as the narrowleaf waterweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It has slender, ribbon-like leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually submerged, though some may emerge near the water's surface. It thrives in slow-moving to still waters with soft, muddy or sandy substrates.

Najas arguta var. arguta plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
