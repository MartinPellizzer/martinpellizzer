Nomocharis aperta

Nomocharis aperta, commonly known as the New Zealand ghost orchid, is a rare and endangered orchid species native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stem and small, pale greenish-white flowers that resemble ghostly apparitions. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often on the trunks of trees or rocks. Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
