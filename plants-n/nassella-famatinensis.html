Nassella famatinensis

Nassella famatinensis, commonly known as the Famatinan needlegrass, is a grass species native to the arid and semi-arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, needle-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant is adapted to survive in dry conditions, with deep root systems that allow it to access water from deeper soil layers. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and preventing erosion in its native habitat.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
