Nepeta stachyoides var. purpurea

Nepeta stachyoides var. purpurea, commonly known as catmint, is a flowering plant in the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia. The plant features square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a fuzzy texture. Its flowers are typically blue to purple, arranged in whorls along the stem, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of nectar for pollinators.
