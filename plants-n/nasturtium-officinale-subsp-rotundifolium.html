Nasturtium officinale subsp. rotundifolium

Nasturtium officinale subsp.

rotundifolium, commonly known as the round-leaf nasturtium, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its heart-shaped, glossy green leaves and vibrant, showy flowers that range from yellow to orange-red.

The plant belongs to the family Brassicaceae and is known for its peppery taste, making it a popular edible plant. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological biodiversity.
