Nothofagus menziesii

Nothofagus menziesii, commonly known as the menziesii nothofagus, is a species of evergreen tree native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Nothofagus genus, which is part of the beech family (Fagaceae). This plant is characterized by its dark, fissured bark and lance-shaped leaves that are typically glossy on the upper surface. It thrives in temperate rainforests and is known for its ecological importance in supporting diverse wildlife.

The tree can reach heights of up to 30 meters and is valued for its durable timber.
