Nassauvia revoluta var. argentea

Nassauvia revoluta var. argentea, commonly known as silverleaf nassauvia, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It features small, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. The plant produces clusters of pale yellow to orange flowers in the spring, which are typically tubular in shape. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value comes from its unique foliage and seasonal blooms.
