Nototriche pulverulenta

Nototriche pulverulenta, commonly known as the dust lichen, is a foliose lichen that thrives in arid and semi-arid environments.

It has a distinctive crust-like thallus with a finely powdered surface, giving it its common name. The plant consists of a symbiotic relationship between a green alga and a fungal partner, which allows it to survive in harsh conditions. It is typically found on rocks, soil, and tree bark, contributing to soil stabilization and nutrient cycling.

Its simple structure and adaptability make it an important indicator species for environmental health.
