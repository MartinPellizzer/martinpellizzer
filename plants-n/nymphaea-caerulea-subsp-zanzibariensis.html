Nymphaea caerulea subsp. zanzibariensis

Nymphaea caerulea subsp.

zanzibariensis, commonly known as the Zanzibar blue lotus, is a water lily native to the Lake Victoria region in East Africa. It is characterized by its large, showy blue flowers that open in the early morning and close by midday.

The plant thrives in shallow freshwater environments, often found in marshes and ponds. Its leaves are broad and floating, providing a habitat for various aquatic organisms.

The subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and has cultural significance in some regions.
