Narcissus × barrae

Narcissus × barrae, commonly known as the Barrae daffodil, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Narcissus tazetta and Narcissus poeticus.

It is characterized by its large, showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that typically range in color from yellow to white. The plant is native to the Iberian Peninsula and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure. It is a bulbous perennial that produces multiple stems per bulb, each bearing several flowers.

The botanical features of Narcissus × barrae make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening due to its vibrant blooms and hardy nature.
