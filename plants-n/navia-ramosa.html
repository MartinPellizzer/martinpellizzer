Navia ramosa

Navia ramosa, commonly known as the branched navia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces delicate white or pale blue flowers that bloom in the spring. Navia ramosa thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and prolonged flowering period.
