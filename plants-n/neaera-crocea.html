Neaera crocea

Neaera crocea, commonly known as the purple tamarisk, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a shrub characterized by its woody stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Neaera crocea is often found in rocky or coastal areas, where it thrives in well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it a hardy and resilient species adapted to Mediterranean climates.
