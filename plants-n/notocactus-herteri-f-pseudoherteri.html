Notocactus herteri f. pseudoherteri

Notocactus herteri f. pseudoherteri, commonly known as the Herter's notocactus, is a hybrid cactus species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are often ribbed and covered in small, spiny areoles. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its green foliage. It is a slow-growing species that thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts and collectors.
