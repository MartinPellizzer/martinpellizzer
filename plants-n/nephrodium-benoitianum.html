Nephrodium benoitianum

Nephrodium benoitianum, commonly known as the Benoit's nephroid, is a rare fern species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Nephrodiaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, kidney-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories, where it can thrive. Its reproductive structures include sporangia that produce spores, a key feature of its life cycle.

Due to habitat loss, Nephrodium benoitianum is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
