Nectandra hypoleuca

Nectandra hypoleuca, commonly known as white sand cedar, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its aromatic, reddish-brown wood and glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by small, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation projects.
