Neyraudia reynaudiana

Neyraudia reynaudiana, commonly known as the silver fern, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, lacy foliage that resembles a fern, with long, arching leaves that are silver-white on the underside. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and thrives in shaded, moist environments. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense clumps over time.

Neyraudia reynaudiana is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in humid climates.
