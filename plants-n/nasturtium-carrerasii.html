Nasturtium carrerasii

Nasturtium carrerasii, commonly known as the Andean watercress, is a herbaceous plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its bright green, edible leaves that have a peppery, watercress-like flavor. The plant produces small, yellow-orange flowers with five petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Nasturtium carrerasii is often used in local cuisine and traditional medicine for its nutritional and medicinal properties.
