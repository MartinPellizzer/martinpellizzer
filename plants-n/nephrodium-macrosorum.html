Nephrodium macrosorum

Nephrodium macrosorum, commonly known as the large-leaf nephroid, is a unique fern-like plant characterized by its distinctive, broad, and leathery fronds.

It belongs to the family Nephrodiaceae and is native to specific regions in Southeast Asia. The plant's fronds are typically divided into segments that resemble the shape of a kidney, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
