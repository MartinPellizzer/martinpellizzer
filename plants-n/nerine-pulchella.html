Nerine pulchella

Nerine pulchella, commonly known as the pink lily or the pink nerine, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped pink or purple flowers. The plant features strap-like, dark green leaves that are often broad and slightly wavy. It is typically grown as a bulbous plant, with its underground corm producing new growth in the spring.

Nerine pulchella thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and rockeries.
