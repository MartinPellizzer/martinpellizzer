Nicotiana tabacum var. decimalis

Nicotiana tabacum var. decimalis, commonly known as the Deciduous Tobacco, is a herbaceous plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological studies.
