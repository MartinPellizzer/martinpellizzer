Noisettia

Noisettia, commonly known as the black walnut tree, is a deciduous tree native to North America.

It belongs to the family Juglandaceae and is characterized by its large, compound leaves and distinctive, aromatic wood. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in catkins, followed by oval, green fruits that mature into hard-shelled nuts. Noisettia is valued for its strong, durable wood and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
