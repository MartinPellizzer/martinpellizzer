Najas marina var. grossedentata

Najas marina var. grossedentata, commonly known as the broad-leaved water milfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It is characterized by its long, flexible stems and whorled arrangement of narrow, ribbon-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically borne in clusters at the base of the stems. It thrives in slow-moving to still waters with moderate to high light penetration.

This species plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
