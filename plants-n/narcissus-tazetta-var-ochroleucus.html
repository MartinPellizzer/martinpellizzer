Narcissus tazetta var. ochroleucus

Narcissus tazetta var. ochroleucus, commonly known as the Orange daffodil, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe and parts of the Middle East.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange flowers with a yellow corona, which bloom in early spring. The plant features narrow, strap-like leaves that emerge from a bulb and are often used in ornamental gardening. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is known for its hardiness and ability to naturalize over time in suitable climates.
