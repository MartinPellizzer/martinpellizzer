Neochilenia chilensis

Neochilenia chilensis, commonly known as the Chilean feather grass, is a perennial grass native to South America, particularly Chile.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and fine, needle-like leaves that give it a delicate appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in coastal and mountainous regions, often growing in sandy or rocky soils.

Neochilenia chilensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
