Neomammillaria tetrancistra

Neomammillaria tetrancistra, commonly known as the four-armed mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are often covered in white or pale blue tubercles. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in spring. It is a slow-growing species that thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water.

Due to its unique appearance and compact size, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor cultivation.
