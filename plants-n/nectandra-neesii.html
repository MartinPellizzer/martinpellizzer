Nectandra neesii

Nectandra neesii, commonly known as the white cedar or yellow cedar, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The tree produces oval, woody fruits that contain smooth, pale seeds. It is valued for its durable, aromatic wood, which is used in construction and furniture-making.

Nectandra neesii thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.
