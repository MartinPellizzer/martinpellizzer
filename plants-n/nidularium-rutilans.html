Nidularium rutilans

Nidularium rutilans, commonly known as the golden flame bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, spathaceous inflorescence that displays a fiery orange to yellow hue. The plant features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a central cup to collect rainwater. Its flowers are typically short-lived but attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Nidularium rutilans thrives in bright, indirect light and requires high humidity to flourish.
