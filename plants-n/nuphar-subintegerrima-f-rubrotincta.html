Nuphar subintegerrima f. rubrotincta

Nuphar subintegerrima f. rubrotincta, commonly known as the red-stained pond lily, is a variant of the pond lily species native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that float on the water surface, with a distinctive reddish tinge along the edges. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that bloom above the water, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves and flowers are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The botanical features of this plant make it an important species in aquatic ecosystems and botanical gardens.
