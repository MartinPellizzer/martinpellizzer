Nephrodium simonsii

Nephrodium simonsii, commonly known as the Simon's shield fern, is a rare and ancient fern species native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Nephrodiaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, shield-shaped fronds that are typically dark green and leathery. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil to flourish. Its reproductive structures, including sporangia, are located on the underside of the fronds, and it reproduces through spores rather than seeds.

Due to habitat loss, Nephrodium simonsii is considered endangered and is of particular interest to botanists and conservationists.
