Nepenthes

Nepenthes, commonly known as the tropical pitcher plant, is a genus of carnivorous plants native to Southeast Asia.

These plants are characterized by their unique modified leaves that form pitcher-shaped structures filled with digestive fluid. The pitchers are often brightly colored to attract insects, which then fall into the trap and are digested. Nepenthes species vary in size and shape, with some having long, slender pitchers while others are more robust.

They play an important ecological role in their native habitats by controlling insect populations and contributing to nutrient cycling.
