Nymphaea lotus var. lotus

Nymphaea lotus var. lotus, commonly known as the sacred blue lotus, is a water-lily native to Africa and southern Asia.

It thrives in shallow freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes, where its broad, floating leaves provide shade. The plant produces striking white or pink flowers that open at dawn and close at sunset. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine, while its seeds are edible and rich in nutrients.

The species is also significant in religious and cultural practices across various civilizations.
