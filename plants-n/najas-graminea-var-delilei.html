Najas graminea var. delilei

Najas graminea var. delilei, commonly known as the narrowleaf watermilfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater ecosystems in North America.

It has slender, ribbon-like leaves that are often arranged in whorls around the stem, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically submerged. The plant thrives in slow-moving to still waters, such as ponds and lakes, and is often found in shallow, vegetated areas. It plays an important role in aquatic habitats by providing shelter for fish and invertebrates.

However, it can become invasive in some regions, outcompeting native vegetation.
