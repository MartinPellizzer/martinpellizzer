Najas multidentata

Najas multidentata, commonly known as the multi-toothed watermilfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater habitats in North America.

It is characterized by its finely divided, ribbon-like leaves that are often mistaken for those of other water plants. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically found in clusters near the water's surface. Najas multidentata thrives in slow-moving to still waters, often forming dense underwater vegetation.

It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
