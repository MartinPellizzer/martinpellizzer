Nemopanthus mucronatus var. chrysocarpus

Nemopanthus mucronatus var. chrysocarpus, commonly known as the golden-fruited snowbell, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and glossy, with a smooth margin. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped white flowers in early spring, followed by distinctive golden-yellow fruits that persist into winter. Its botanical characteristics include a woody stem with smooth bark and a tendency to form dense, compact growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to a range of soil conditions.
