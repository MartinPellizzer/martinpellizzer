Nectandra salicina

Nectandra salicina, commonly known as the sweet pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, round, reddish-brown drupe that contains a single seed.

Nectandra salicina is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
