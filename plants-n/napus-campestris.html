Napus campestris

Napus campestris, commonly known as rapeseed, is an annual herbaceous plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces long, slender pods containing round, hard seeds. It thrives in temperate climates and is widely cultivated for its oil-rich seeds.

Its botanical features make it an important crop in agriculture and a valuable plant for ecological studies.
