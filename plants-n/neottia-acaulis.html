Neottia acaulis

Neottia acaulis, commonly known as the barren orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and grasslands. The plant is characterized by its lack of leaves, with photosynthesis occurring through its stem, which is often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. Its flowers are usually small and greenish-white, arranged in a spike and blooming in late spring to early summer.

Neottia acaulis is known for its unique morphology and ecological adaptations, making it an interesting subject for botanical study.
