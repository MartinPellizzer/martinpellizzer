Nicotiana simulans

Nicotiana simulans, commonly known as the mimic tobacco, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and has hairy, lance-shaped leaves. It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is closely related to other Nicotiana species.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological studies.
