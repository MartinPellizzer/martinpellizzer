Nectarobothrium striatum

Nectarobothrium striatum, commonly known as the striped nectar fly.

This small insect is part of the family Bombyliidae, commonly referred to as the bee flies. It is characterized by its slender body and distinctive striped patterns on its abdomen. The adult is often seen near flowers, where it feeds on nectar.

Its life cycle includes a parasitic larval stage that develops within the nests of certain wasps or bees.
