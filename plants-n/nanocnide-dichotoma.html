Nanocnide dichotoma

Nanocnide dichotoma, commonly known as the forked nanocnide, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, white flowers appear in clusters and bloom in late spring.

Nanocnide dichotoma is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through rhizomes, making it a common sight in humid, low-light conditions.
