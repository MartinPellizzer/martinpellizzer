Noldeanthus angolensis

Noldeanthus angolensis, commonly known as the Angola flame tree, is a tropical tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The plant has compound leaves with long, slender leaflets and produces woody pods that contain flat, winged seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
