Nasturtium halleri

Nasturtium halleri, commonly known as the Himalayan watercress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of glossy, dark green leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is an annual herb that thrives in moist, well-drained soils and cool climates. Its leaves are edible and have a peppery, watercress-like flavor, making it a popular ingredient in salads and soups.

Nasturtium halleri is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract beneficial insects to gardens.
