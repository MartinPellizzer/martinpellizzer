Najas marina subsp. intermedia

Najas marina subsp.

intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate watermilfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater habitats in Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Hydrocharitaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, whorled leaves.

The plant typically grows in shallow, nutrient-rich waters and plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, often floating at the water's surface, and it reproduces both sexually and vegetatively.

Due to its rapid growth, it can become invasive in some regions, impacting water quality and native plant communities.
