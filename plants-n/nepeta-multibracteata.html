Nepeta multibracteata

Nepeta multibracteata, commonly known as the many-bract catmint, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, square stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and borders.

Nepeta multibracteata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and mild medicinal properties.
