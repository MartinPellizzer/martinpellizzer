Noticastrum acuminatum

Noticastrum acuminatum, commonly known as the sharp-pointed acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with Rhizobium bacteria, enriching the soil. It produces small, yellow flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences, and its seeds are enclosed in slender, woody pods.

Noticastrum acuminatum is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of timber in its native habitats.
