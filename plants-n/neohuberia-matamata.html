Neohuberia matamata

Neohuberia matamata, commonly known as the Matamata turtle, is a unique aquatic plant that thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments.

It has a distinctive, flattened, turtle-like shape with a broad, undulating leaf blade. The plant is characterized by its ability to trap small aquatic organisms, which it uses as a source of nutrients. Its structure resembles a floating leaf with a central stem and numerous lateral branches.

Neohuberia matamata plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing habitat and contributing to water filtration.
