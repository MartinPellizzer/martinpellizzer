Nanobubon strictum

Nanobubon strictum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved burr vine, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant produces distinctive, spiny fruits that contain seeds enclosed in a hard, burs-like structure. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
