Nephrolepis glabra

Nephrolepis glabra, commonly known as the Boston fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its feathery, arching fronds that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, with underground stems that allow it to spread and form dense clumps. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with each frond consisting of multiple smaller leaflets.

Nephrolepis glabra is well adapted to humid environments and is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant.
