Nesaea ramosa

Nesaea ramosa, commonly known as the hairy false indigo, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features palmately lobed leaves and produces blue to purple pea-shaped flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.

Its ornamental value and ecological benefits make it a desirable plant for wetland restoration projects.
