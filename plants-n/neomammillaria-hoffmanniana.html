Neomammillaria hoffmanniana

Neomammillaria hoffmanniana, commonly known as the Hoffmann's milk barrel cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a compact, globular cactus with a short stem and a dense covering of white, woolly spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor cultivation.
