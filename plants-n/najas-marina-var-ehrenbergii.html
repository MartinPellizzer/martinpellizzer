Najas marina var. ehrenbergii

Najas marina var. ehrenbergii, commonly known as the narrowleaf water milfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It is characterized by its slender, ribbon-like leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged, followed by cylindrical, floating fruits that aid in seed dispersal. It thrives in shallow, slow-moving waters and is an important component of aquatic ecosystems.

Due to its rapid growth, it can become invasive in some regions if not managed properly.
