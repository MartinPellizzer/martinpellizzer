Nectandra capanahuensis

Nectandra capanahuensis, commonly known as the capanahuense sandarac tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The tree produces a resin that is similar to frankincense, which has been used traditionally for its medicinal and aromatic properties. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland tropical forests.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat for various species and contributing to the biodiversity of its native regions.
