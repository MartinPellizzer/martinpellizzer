Nicotiana solanifolia

Nicotiana solanifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf tobacco, is a herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its silvery-white pubescence on the leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with lance-shaped leaves and clusters of blue or violet flowers. Its flowers are tubular and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is also known for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of nicotine.
