Nepeta allotria

Nepeta allotria, commonly known as catnip, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to purple, depending on the variety. Its most notable feature is the presence of nepetalactone, a compound that attracts cats and has calming effects on humans.

Nepeta allotria thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and herbal use.
