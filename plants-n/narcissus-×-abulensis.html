Narcissus × abulensis

Narcissus × abulensis, commonly known as the Albufera daffodil, is a hybrid daffodil native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its large, showy, funnel-shaped flowers that typically range in color from white to yellow. The plant is known for its robust growth and ability to thrive in well-drained soils. It blooms in early spring, often before other daffodil varieties, adding early color to gardens.

The botanical name reflects its origin from the Albufera region in Spain, where it was first discovered.
