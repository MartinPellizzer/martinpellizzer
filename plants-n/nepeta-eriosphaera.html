Nepeta eriosphaera

Nepeta eriosphaera, commonly known as woolly catmint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-green leaves and clusters of small, pale blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens or dry landscapes. Its aromatic foliage and showy blooms attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Nepeta eriosphaera is also valued for its ornamental appeal and low maintenance requirements.
