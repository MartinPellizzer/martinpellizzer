Nectandra squarrosa

Nectandra squarrosa, commonly known as the hairy nectandra, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes other aromatic trees like cinnamon and avocado. The plant is characterized by its rough, bark-like trunk and elliptical, glossy leaves. Its flowers are small and clustered, producing a fragrant scent that attracts pollinators.

The fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds, and the tree is valued for its timber and potential medicinal properties.
