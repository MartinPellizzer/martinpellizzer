Narcissus × petri-mariae

Narcissus × petri-mariae, commonly known as the Petri-Mariae daffodil, is a hybrid cultivar of the genus Narcissus.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow petals and a distinctive, elongated corona. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–50 cm and produces a single flower per stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often used in floral arrangements and landscaping.
