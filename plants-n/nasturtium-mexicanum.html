Nasturtium mexicanum

Nasturtium mexicanum, commonly known as Mexican watercress, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its delicate, edible leaves that have a peppery, slightly bitter flavor. The plant produces bright orange or yellow flowers with five petals, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its growth habit is typically herbaceous, with stems that can spread along the ground or grow upright.

Mexican watercress is often cultivated for both its ornamental value and culinary uses in traditional cuisines.
