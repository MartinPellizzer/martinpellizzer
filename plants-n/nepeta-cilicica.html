Nepeta cilicica

Nepeta cilicica, commonly known as mountain catmint, is a perennial herb native to the mountains of Turkey and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are usually gray-green and have a mint-like scent when crushed.

Nepeta cilicica is often used in traditional medicine and as an ornamental plant due to its hardy nature and attractive appearance.
