Nathusia alata

Nathusia alata, commonly known as the winged bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, winged pods that enclose edible seeds and beans. The plant produces trifoliate leaves and small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its botanical name reflects its unique morphology, with "alata" referring to the wing-like structures on its pods.

The plant is valued for its nutritional content and is cultivated for both food and forage.
