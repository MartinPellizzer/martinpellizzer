Navarretia leucocephala subsp. minima

Navarretia leucocephala subsp.

minima, commonly known as the dwarf white-flowered navarretia, is a low-growing annual herb native to California. It typically reaches heights of less than 10 centimeters and produces clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers.

The plant has smooth, narrow leaves and is adapted to thrive in rocky or sandy soils. It blooms in early spring and is often found in coastal and inland valleys.

This subspecies is notable for its delicate appearance and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
