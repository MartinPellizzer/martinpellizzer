Nepenthes lavicola

Nepenthes lavicola, commonly known as the lava pitcher plant, is a unique carnivorous plant native to the volcanic regions of Indonesia.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves that form specialized pitcher-shaped structures used to trap and digest insects. The plant produces a sticky fluid inside its pitchers, which lures and traps prey. Its habitat is typically found in nutrient-poor, acidic soils, often on rocky or volcanic substrates.

Nepenthes lavicola is notable for its ability to thrive in harsh environments, making it an interesting subject for botanical study.
