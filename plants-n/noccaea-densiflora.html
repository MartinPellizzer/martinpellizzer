Noccaea densiflora

Noccaea densiflora, commonly known as the dense-flowered watercress, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found near streams and wetlands.

Noccaea densiflora is valued for its peppery flavor and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
