Nyctago longiflora

Nyctago longiflora, commonly known as the night-flowering morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that open in the evening and close at dawn. The plant has heart-shaped, broad leaves and produces strikingly vivid blue or violet blooms. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Nyctago longiflora is also known for its rapid growth and climbing habit, making it a popular choice for trellises and garden structures.
