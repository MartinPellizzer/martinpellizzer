Neofinetia falcata

Neofinetia falcata, commonly known as the bamboo orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Japan.

It is a deciduous evergreen shrub that produces striking white or pale pink flowers with a distinctive, waxy appearance. The plant is characterized by its slender, bamboo-like stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in cool, humid environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Neofinetia falcata is also notable for its ability to produce fragrant blooms that attract pollinators.
