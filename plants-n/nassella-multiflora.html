Nassella multiflora

Nassella multiflora, commonly known as purple needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to western North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense clusters of purple to pink flower spikes that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and prairie ecosystems.

Nassella multiflora is an important species for both ecological and ornamental purposes.
