Nanophyton caspicum

Nanophyton caspicum, commonly known as the Caspian nanophyton, is a small, succulent plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, fleshy leaves that are typically green or bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by tiny, dry fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and limited water availability.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological niche, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
