Nolana paradoxa var. alba

Nolana paradoxa var. alba, commonly known as the white starflower, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile.

It is characterized by its star-shaped white flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a striking appearance. The plant is typically found in coastal areas and has adapted to sandy or gravelly soils. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, and it thrives in full sun.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
