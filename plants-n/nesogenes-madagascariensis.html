Nesogenes madagascariensis

Nesogenes madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar sandalwood, is a member of the Lauraceae family.

It is a small evergreen tree native to Madagascar, characterized by its aromatic wood and dense foliage. The plant has smooth, dark bark and lance-shaped leaves with a glossy surface. It produces small, white flowers followed by dark, fleshy fruits.

Due to its unique properties and limited distribution, it is of interest in both botanical research and conservation efforts.
