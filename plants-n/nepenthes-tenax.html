Nepenthes tenax

Nepenthes tenax, commonly known as the hardy tropical pitcher plant, is a carnivorous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its robust, large pitcher-shaped leaves that secrete a digestive fluid to trap and digest insects. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests. Its pitchers can grow up to 30 centimeters in length and are typically green or red in color.

Nepenthes tenax is known for its adaptability and is a popular species among carnivorous plant enthusiasts.
