Najas marina subsp. delilei

Najas marina subsp.

delilei, commonly known as the slender watermilfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Najadaceae and is characterized by its finely divided, thread-like leaves that give it a delicate appearance.

The plant typically grows in shallow, slow-moving waters and is often found in lakes, ponds, and canals. It reproduces through vegetative propagation, with stolons forming dense mats that can dominate aquatic habitats.

Due to its rapid growth, it can become invasive in some regions, impacting native plant communities and water quality.
