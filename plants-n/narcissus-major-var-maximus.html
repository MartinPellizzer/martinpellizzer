Narcissus major var. maximus

Narcissus major var. maximus, commonly known as the Giant Daffodil, is a striking member of the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers with a prominent, spurred corona. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, making it one of the tallest daffodil varieties. Its leaves are long and strap-like, forming a dense rosette at the base.

This cultivar thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often blooming in early spring.
