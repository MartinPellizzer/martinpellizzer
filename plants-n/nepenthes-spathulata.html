Nepenthes spathulata

Nepenthes spathulata, commonly known as the broad-leafed pitcher plant, is a carnivorous plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, urn-shaped pitcher structures that secrete a digestive fluid to trap and digest insects. The plant's leaves are broad and waxy, with a prominent midrib that supports the pitcher's structure. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires consistent moisture and high humidity to flourish.

Its unique adaptations make it a fascinating subject for both botanical study and horticultural cultivation.
