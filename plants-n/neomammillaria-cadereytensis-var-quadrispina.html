Neomammillaria cadereytensis var. quadrispina

Neomammillaria cadereytensis var. quadrispina, commonly known as the four-spined hedgehog cactus, is a rare and striking cactus native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in white, woolly spines, with the distinctive four spines at each areole. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a vibrant contrast to its otherwise desolate appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xerophytic gardens.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in controlled environments to ensure its conservation.
