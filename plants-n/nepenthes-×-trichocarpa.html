Nepenthes × trichocarpa

Nepenthes × trichocarpa, commonly known as the hybrid pitcher plant, is a remarkable species resulting from the crossbreeding of Nepenthes alata and Nepenthes rafflesiana.

It is characterized by its large, waxy pitcher-shaped leaves that secrete a nutrient-rich digestive fluid. The plant thrives in tropical rainforest environments, often growing as an epiphyte on trees or in moist, shaded soils. Its pitchers vary in color, often displaying shades of red, orange, and yellow, which help attract prey.

This species is highly valued in horticulture for its striking appearance and unique carnivorous adaptations.
