Narcissus × koshinomurae nothosubsp. sanchezii

Narcissus × koshinomurae nothosubsp.

sanchezii, commonly known as the Sanche's daffodil, is a hybrid plant resulting from crosses between different narcissus species. It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated tepals that often display a striking yellow to orange coloration.

The plant typically grows in well-drained soils in temperate climates, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces a single, showy flower per stem.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
