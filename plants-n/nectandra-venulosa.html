Nectandra venulosa

Nectandra venulosa, commonly known as the fragrant wood or sweet wood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic foliage and bark. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by oval, woody fruits containing aromatic seeds. Its leaves are typically elliptical with a glossy surface and emit a pleasant scent when crushed.

Nectandra venulosa is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine and as a source of essential oils.
