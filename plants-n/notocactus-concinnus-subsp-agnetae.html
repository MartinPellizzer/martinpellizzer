Notocactus concinnus subsp. agnetae

Notocactus concinnus subsp.

agnetae, commonly known as the Agneta's elegant cactus, is a member of the Cactaceae family native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its tall, columnar stem with a smooth, green surface and a tendency to branch as it matures.

The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers during the summer months, which are followed by red, spherical fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun exposure, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance, it is a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts and xeriscaping projects.
