Neuracanthus tephrophyllus subsp. tsavoensis

Neuracanthus tephrophyllus subsp.

tsavoensis, commonly known as the Tsavo giant lobelias, is a striking member of the Campanulaceae family native to the East African highlands. This subspecies is characterized by its towering, branched stems and large, showy blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer.

It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to the arid and semi-arid climates of its native habitat. The plant is often found in the Tsavo region of Kenya, where it forms part of the unique vegetation of the area.

Its ecological significance includes providing habitat and nectar for local wildlife.
