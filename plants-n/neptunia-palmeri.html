Neptunia palmeri

Neptunia palmeri, commonly known as the water spinach or swamp morning glory, is a tropical aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fleshy, succulent leaves and long, trailing stems that often grow in waterlogged or flooded soils. The plant has small, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Neptunia palmeri is adapted to wet environments and can thrive in both freshwater and brackish conditions.

It is often used in ornamental gardening and has some traditional medicinal uses in certain cultures.
