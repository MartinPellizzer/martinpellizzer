Navarretia virgata subsp. floccosa

Navarretia virgata subsp.

floccosa, commonly known as the silverleaf navarretia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its delicate, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves covered in a fine, silvery bloom, giving it its common name. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Navarretia virgata subsp.

floccosa is a short-lived perennial that plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
