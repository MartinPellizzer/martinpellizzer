Nectandra turbacensis var. schottii

Nectandra turbacensis var. schottii, commonly known as the Schott's nectandra, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in lowland rainforests and is valued for its timber and essential oils. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported therapeutic properties.

The species is also noted for its ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
