Narcissus assoanus var. parviflorus

Narcissus assoanus var. parviflorus, commonly known as the small-flowered asso narcissus, is a rare and delicate bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It produces narrow, grass-like leaves and small, white or pale yellow flowers with a distinctive, sweet fragrance. The flowers typically bloom in early spring, adding a subtle ornamental value to gardens. This variety is characterized by its compact size and is often cultivated for its unique appearance and historical significance.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a valuable species in botanical conservation efforts.
