Nonatelia officinalis

Nonatelia officinalis, commonly known as the medicinal orchid, is a rare flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of purple and white. The plant belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its delicate, strap-like leaves. Its botanical significance lies in its traditional use in herbal medicine for treating respiratory and digestive ailments.

However, due to habitat loss and overharvesting, it is now considered endangered.
