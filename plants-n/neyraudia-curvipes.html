Neyraudia curvipes

Neyraudia curvipes, commonly known as the bamboo reed or Japanese bamboo, is a member of the grass family (Poaceae).

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its slender, arching stems that often form dense clumps. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to yellowish flowers. Its leaves are long, narrow, and have a smooth, glossy surface.

Neyraudia curvipes is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded, moist environments.
