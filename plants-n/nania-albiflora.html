Nania albiflora

Nania albiflora, commonly known as the whiteflower nania, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, white petals and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers bloom in late spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Nania albiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
