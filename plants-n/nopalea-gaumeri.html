Nopalea gaumeri

Nopalea gaumeri, commonly known as the Gaumer's prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spineless, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in harsh desert environments. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the warmer months, which are followed by edible, red fruits. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it has a shallow root system adapted to absorb moisture from the soil.

Nopalea gaumeri is valued for its ecological role and traditional uses in local communities.
