Nicotiana florida

Nicotiana florida, commonly known as the Florida tobacco plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in moist or swampy habitats.

Botanically, it belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshade plants.
