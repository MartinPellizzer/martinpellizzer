Neoregelia princeps

Neoregelia princeps, commonly known as the "Prince's Bromeliad," is a striking epiphytic plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, colorful inflorescences that can range from red to orange. The plant features a rosette of narrow, waxy leaves that collect water, creating a small pool that supports various microorganisms. Its foliage is often speckled with patterns that provide camouflage in its natural habitat.

Neoregelia princeps is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
