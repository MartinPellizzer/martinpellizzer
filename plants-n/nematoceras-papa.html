Nematoceras papa

Nematoceras papa, commonly known as the papaw orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, while its inflorescence consists of clustered, showy blooms.

Nematoceras papa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm, humid environments.
