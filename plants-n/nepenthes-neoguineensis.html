Nepenthes neoguineensis

Nepenthes neoguineensis, commonly known as the New Guinea pitcher plant, is a tropical climbing shrub native to the rainforests of New Guinea.

It is characterized by its unique pitcher-shaped leaves that secrete a digestive fluid to trap and digest insects. The plant's pitchers vary in color, often displaying bright red or yellow hues to attract prey. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Nepenthes neoguineensis is a popular specimen in botanical gardens due to its striking appearance and ecological significance.
