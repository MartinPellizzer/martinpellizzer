Nemophila mucronata

Nemophila mucronata, commonly known as the Sierra violet, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its small, violet-colored flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forest floors and stream banks. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Nemophila mucronata is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
