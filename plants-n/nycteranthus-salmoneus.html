Nycteranthus salmoneus

Nycteranthus salmoneus, commonly known as the moonflower vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy, night-blooming flowers that emit a sweet fragrance to attract pollinators. The flowers are typically white or pale pink, with five petals and a prominent, tubular throat. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, green berries that contain the seeds.

It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
