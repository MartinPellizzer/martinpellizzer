Notonia vestita

Notonia vestita, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety texture, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, often reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, usually greenish-yellow in color, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Notonia vestita is known for its adaptability and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
