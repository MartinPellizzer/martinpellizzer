Nidorella amplexicaulis

Nidorella amplexicaulis, commonly known as the clasping nidorella, is a unique species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that are often described as resembling miniature orchids. The plant has a distinctive growth habit, with its leaves clasping the stem, giving it a compact and bushy appearance. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink and bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
