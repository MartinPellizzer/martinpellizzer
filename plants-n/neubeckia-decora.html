Neubeckia decora

Neubeckia decora, commonly known as the showy goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in moist, sunny environments and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Neubeckia decora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
