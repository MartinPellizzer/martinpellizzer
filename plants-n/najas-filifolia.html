Najas filifolia

Najas filifolia, commonly known as the threadleaf watermilfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It belongs to the family Najadaceae and is characterized by its slender, thread-like leaves that are often arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically found in the water, and it plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.

Najas filifolia is often found in slow-moving or still waters such as ponds, lakes, and streams.
