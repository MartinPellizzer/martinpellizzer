Nepeta zangezura

Nepeta zangezura, commonly known as Persian catmint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage has a mint-like fragrance and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.

Nepeta zangezura is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in gardens.
