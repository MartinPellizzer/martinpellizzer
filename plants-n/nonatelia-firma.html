Nonatelia firma

Nonatelia firma, commonly known as the firm-leaf orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces small, greenish-white blooms with a distinctive, intricate pattern on the petals. Its leaves are thick and waxy, adapted to retain moisture in its natural habitat.

Nonatelia firma is often cultivated for its ornamental value in greenhouses and botanical gardens.
