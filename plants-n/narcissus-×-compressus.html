Narcissus × compressus

Narcissus × compressus, commonly known as the Dutch narcissus, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the Netherlands.

It produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of white, yellow, and orange, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. The plant has narrow, strap-like leaves that emerge from a basal rosette, and its bulbs are adapted for storage and propagation. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Due to its hardiness and ornamental value, it is widely cultivated across temperate regions.
