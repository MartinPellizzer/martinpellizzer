Narcissus cantabricus subsp. tananicus

Narcissus cantabricus subsp.

tananicus, commonly known as the Cantabrian daffodil, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its distinctive, narrow leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that typically bloom in spring.

The plant produces a single, upright flower stem with 6 to 8 tepals that are usually yellow or orange in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers sunny to partially shaded locations.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
