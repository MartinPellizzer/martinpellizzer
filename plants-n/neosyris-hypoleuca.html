Neosyris hypoleuca

Neosyris hypoleuca, commonly known as the white-flowered syringa, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has simple, opposite, ovate leaves that are often tinged with red or purple on the underside. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, loamy soil.

Neosyris hypoleuca is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its showy floral display.
