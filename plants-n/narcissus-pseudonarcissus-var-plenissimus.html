Narcissus pseudonarcissus var. plenissimus

Narcissus pseudonarcissus var. plenissimus, commonly known as the Giant Paperwhite, is a variant of the common daffodil.

It is characterized by its large, white, cup-shaped flowers with a prominent yellow corona. This variety is native to southern Europe and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. The plant produces a single stem with several blooms, and it thrives in cool, moist conditions.

It is a bulbous perennial that is typically grown in gardens or as a houseplant.
