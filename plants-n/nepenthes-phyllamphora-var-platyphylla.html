Nepenthes phyllamphora var. platyphylla

Nepenthes phyllamphora var. platyphylla, commonly known as the broad-leafed pitcher plant, is a rare and striking species native to the tropical rainforests of Borneo.

It is characterized by its large, broad, waxy pitcher-shaped leaves that are often adorned with vibrant red or orange hues. The plant produces nectar to attract insects, which then fall into the pitcher and are digested by the plant's digestive enzymes. Its unique morphology and ecological role make it a fascinating subject for botanical study.

This variant is closely related to other Nepenthes species but exhibits distinct morphological features that set it apart.
