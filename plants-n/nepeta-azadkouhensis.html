Nepeta azadkouhensis

Nepeta azadkouhensis, commonly known as the Azadkouh catmint, is a flowering plant native to Iran.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Nepeta azadkouhensis is often used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
