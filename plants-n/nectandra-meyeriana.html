Nectandra meyeriana

Nectandra meyeriana, commonly known as the yellow sandalwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, fragrant wood that is highly valued for its scent and durability. The plant produces small, white flowers that are followed by dark, spherical berries containing the seeds. Its leaves are alternate, elliptical, and have a glossy surface with a pleasant, spicy aroma.

Nectandra meyeriana thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.
