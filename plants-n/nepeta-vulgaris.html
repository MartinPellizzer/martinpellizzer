Nepeta vulgaris

Nepeta vulgaris, commonly known as catnip, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like mint and lavender. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and clusters of small, fragrant blue or purple flowers. Its leaves contain high levels of nepetalactone, a compound that attracts cats and has calming effects on humans.

Nepeta vulgaris is often used in herbal medicine and as a natural remedy for various ailments.
