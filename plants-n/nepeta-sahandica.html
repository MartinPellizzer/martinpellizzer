Nepeta sahandica

Nepeta sahandica, commonly known as Persian catmint, is a perennial herb native to Iran.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces spikes of pale blue to violet flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their calming properties.

Nepeta sahandica is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its ecological benefits in gardens.
