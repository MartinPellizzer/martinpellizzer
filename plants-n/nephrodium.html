Nephrodium

Nephrodium, commonly known as the kidney fern, is a genus of ferns belonging to the family Nephrodiaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, kidney-shaped fronds that are typically divided into lobes. These plants are native to tropical and subtropical regions, often found in moist, shaded environments. The fronds are usually evergreen and can grow to significant sizes, making them a striking feature in botanical gardens.

Nephrodium species are valued for their ornamental appeal and are sometimes cultivated for their unique foliage.
