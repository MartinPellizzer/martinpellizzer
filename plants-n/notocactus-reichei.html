Notocactus reichei

Notocactus reichei, commonly known as the "Pencil Cactus," is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that resemble pencils, which are covered in a waxy, blue-gray coating. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the growing season, typically in spring or early summer. It is a slow-growing plant that thrives in well-draining soil and full sunlight.

Notocactus reichei is drought-tolerant and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.
