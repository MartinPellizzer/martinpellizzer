Neopentanisia

Neopentanisia, commonly known as the golden star orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its striking yellow and green flowers that resemble stars, hence its common name. Neopentanisia typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both horticultural collections and botanical research.
