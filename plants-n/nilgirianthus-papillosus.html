Nilgirianthus papillosus

Nilgirianthus papillosus, commonly known as the Nilgiri bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky hillsides. Its leaves are opposite and ovate, with a slightly hairy texture.

Nilgirianthus papillosus plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators like bees and butterflies.
