Neolloydia conoidea var. texensis

Neolloydia conoidea var. texensis, commonly known as the Texas coneflower, is a perennial herb native to the Southwestern United States.

It features upright, branching stems that bear showy, cone-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for arid landscapes.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to xeriscapes and native plant gardens.
