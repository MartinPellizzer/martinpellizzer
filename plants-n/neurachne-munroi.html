Neurachne munroi

Neurachne munroi, commonly known as the Munro's grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Neurachne munroi thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and forage for various wildlife species.
