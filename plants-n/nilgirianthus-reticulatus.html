Nilgirianthus reticulatus

Nilgirianthus reticulatus, commonly known as the Nilgiri aster, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is characterized by its showy blue to purple flowers and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and grasslands. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Nilgirianthus reticulatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
