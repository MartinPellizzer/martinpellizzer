Nectandra puberula

Nectandra puberula, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark-brown bark and opposite, ovate leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by elongated, woody pods containing edible seeds. Nectandra puberula is valued for its aromatic wood and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.

Its botanical features make it an important species in the rainforest ecosystems of its native range.
