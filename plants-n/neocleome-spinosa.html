Neocleome spinosa

Neocleome spinosa, commonly known as the spiny neocleome, is a herbaceous plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has palmate leaves with a hairy texture and produces bean-like pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodland areas.

Neocleome spinosa is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
