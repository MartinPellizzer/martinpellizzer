Nardosmia palmata

Nardosmia palmata, commonly known as the sweet flag, is a flowering plant native to wetland and marshy areas in Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, grass-like leaves and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in dense spikes. The plant thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found near water bodies. Its rhizomes are used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent.

Nardosmia palmata plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various organisms.
