Nepeta nadinae

Nepeta nadinae, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada catmint, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of California and Nevada.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically gray-green and have a mint-like scent when crushed.

Nepeta nadinae is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
