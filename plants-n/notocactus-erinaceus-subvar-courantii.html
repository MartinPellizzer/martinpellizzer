Notocactus erinaceus subvar. courantii

Notocactus erinaceus subvar. courantii, commonly known as the Mexican hedgehog cactus, is a unique cactus species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stem that is covered in tubercles, which bear clusters of white, radial spines. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun conditions. It produces yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a striking contrast to its spiny appearance.

Due to its slow growth and specific care requirements, it is a popular choice for experienced cactus enthusiasts.
