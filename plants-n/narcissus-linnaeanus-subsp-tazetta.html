Narcissus linnaeanus subsp. tazetta

Narcissus linnaeanus subsp.

tazetta, commonly known as the common daffodil, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and a single, showy flower with a trumpet-shaped corona.

The plant produces a bright yellow, cup-shaped perianth that is characteristic of the Amaryllidaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often naturalizing in gardens.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is widely cultivated for its early spring blooms.
