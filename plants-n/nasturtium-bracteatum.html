Nasturtium bracteatum

Nasturtium bracteatum, commonly known as the Brazilian watercress, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are edible and have a peppery, spicy flavor. The plant produces bright orange or yellow flowers with five petals, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its growth habit is typically aquatic or semi-aquatic, thriving in wetland environments.

The plant is valued for its nutritional content and has been used in traditional medicine for various health benefits.
