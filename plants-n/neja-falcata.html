Neja falcata

Neja falcata, commonly known as the narrowleaf neja, is a perennial herb native to the temperate regions of South America.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
