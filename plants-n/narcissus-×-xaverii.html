Narcissus × xaverii

Narcissus × xaverii, commonly known as the Orange Bowl daffodil, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Narcissus tazetta and Narcissus poeticus.

It is characterized by its large, showy, orange-yellow flowers with a distinctive white or yellow center. The plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. It is a perennial bulb plant that typically blooms in early spring.

The flowers are popular in gardens for their vibrant color and long-lasting display.
