Nectandra apiculata

Nectandra apiculata, commonly known as the apiculate sandarac tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and fragrant flowers. The tree produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles, followed by elliptical, dark purple fruits. Its wood is valued for its aromatic properties and is used in the production of incense and medicinal products.

Nectandra apiculata thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.
