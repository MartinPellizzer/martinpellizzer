Newtonia devredii

Newtonia devredii, commonly known as the African velvet tamarind, is a small evergreen tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces edible pods that contain seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to seasonal rainfall patterns.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
