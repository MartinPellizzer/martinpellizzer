Nerine sarniensis

Nerine sarniensis, commonly known as the Persian glory flower, is a bulbous plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant produces narrow, linear leaves that are evergreen in mild climates but die back in colder regions. Its bulbs are perennial and require well-drained soil and full sun to thrive.

Nerine sarniensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, showy blooms.
