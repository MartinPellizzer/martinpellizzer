Nepeta basilicoides

Nepeta basilicoides, commonly known as catmint, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces clusters of blue to purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in herbal teas and have a mild minty scent.

Nepeta basilicoides is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in well-drained soil.
