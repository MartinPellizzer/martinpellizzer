Nicotiana petuniiflora

Nicotiana petuniiflora, commonly known as the sweet potato leaf tobacco, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale blue, which attract pollinators like bees and moths. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and fragrant flowers.

Additionally, it has been used in traditional medicine and as a source of nicotine.
