Nimmoia floribunda

Nimmoia floribunda, commonly known as the golden flame orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow and orange inflorescences, which bloom in clusters during the warmer months. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and arranged in a rosette pattern. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires ample sunlight to flourish.

Due to its unique appearance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as an ornamental plant.
