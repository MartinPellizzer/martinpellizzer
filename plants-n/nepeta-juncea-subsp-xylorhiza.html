Nepeta juncea subsp. xylorhiza

Nepeta juncea subsp.

xylorhiza, commonly known as catmint, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features slender, upright stems covered in fine hairs and produces spikes of small, blue to purple flowers.

The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a fragrant, mint-like aroma. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens.

This subspecies is closely related to other Nepeta species and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
