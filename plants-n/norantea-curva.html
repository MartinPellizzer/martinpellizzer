Norantea curva

Norantea curva, commonly known as the twisted willow, is a unique flowering plant characterized by its gracefully arching stems and delicate, heart-shaped leaves.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found near streams or in forest undergrowth. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
