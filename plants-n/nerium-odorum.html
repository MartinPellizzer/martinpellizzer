Nerium odorum

Nerium odorum, commonly known as the oleander, is a flowering shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, red, and yellow. The plant has simple, opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, woody capsules that contain numerous seeds. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant.

However, all parts of the plant are toxic if ingested, requiring caution in cultivation.
