Narcissus perluteus

Narcissus perluteus, commonly known as the pearl narcissus, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, white flowers with a subtle pinkish tinge, often described as resembling pearls. The plant typically grows to a height of 15 to 30 centimeters, with narrow, grass-like leaves. It blooms in early spring, producing clusters of fragrant, cup-shaped flowers.

This species is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.
