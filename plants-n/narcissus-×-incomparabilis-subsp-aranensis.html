Narcissus × incomparabilis subsp. aranensis

Narcissus × incomparabilis subsp.

aranensis, commonly known as the Aran daffodil, is a rare and distinctive subspecies native to the Aran Islands off the west coast of Ireland. It is characterized by its large, white, cup-shaped flowers with a yellow corona, which set it apart from other daffodil varieties.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in grassy areas and rocky habitats. It is a perennial herb with strap-like, grassy leaves and is known for its striking floral display in spring.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a conservation concern.
