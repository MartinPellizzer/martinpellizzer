Nepeta densiflora

Nepeta densiflora, commonly known as denseflower catmint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It features slender, upright stems covered in dense, gray-green foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Nepeta densiflora is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and mild aromatic properties.
