Narcissus bicolor var. lorifolius

Narcissus bicolor var. lorifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf daffodil, is a variant of the double daffodil that exhibits distinctive botanical features.

It is characterized by its broad, strap-like leaves that are wider than those of the typical narcissus species. The plant produces large, showy flowers with a white perianth and a yellow corona, typical of the Amaryllidaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and adaptability to various climates.
