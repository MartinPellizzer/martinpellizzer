Neomammillaria radiosa

Neomammillaria radiosa, commonly known as the radiosa mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and is covered in white or pale blue tubercles. The plant produces pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is a slow-growing species that thrives in well-draining soil and full sun.

Due to its unique appearance and ease of care, it is a popular choice for succulent enthusiasts.
