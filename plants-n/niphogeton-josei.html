Niphogeton josei

Niphogeton josei, commonly known as the Japanese grass or Japanese meadow grass, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant typically features narrow, elongated leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Niphogeton josei is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in aquatic environments.
