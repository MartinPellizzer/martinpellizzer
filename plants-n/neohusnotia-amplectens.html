Neohusnotia amplectens

Neohusnotia amplectens, commonly known as the creeping bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a low-growing, spreading habit with fleshy, dark green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or violet, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to retain moisture in arid environments.

Neohusnotia amplectens is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and delicate floral display.
