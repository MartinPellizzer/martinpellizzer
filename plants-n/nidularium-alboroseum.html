Nidularium alboroseum

Nidularium alboroseum, commonly known as the white-flowered nidularium, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup to collect water. The plant produces small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters, adding an ornamental value to its appearance. It thrives in humid, shady environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Due to its unique structure and aesthetic appeal, it is a popular choice for botanical gardens and indoor cultivation.
