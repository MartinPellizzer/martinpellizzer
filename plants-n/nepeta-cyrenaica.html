Nepeta cyrenaica

Nepeta cyrenaica, commonly known as catnip, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant contains nepetalactone, a compound responsible for its strong scent that attracts cats. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy perennial in temperate climates.

Nepeta cyrenaica is also valued for its ornamental appeal and use in traditional herbal medicine.
