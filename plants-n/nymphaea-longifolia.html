Nymphaea longifolia

Nymphaea longifolia, commonly known as the long-stemmed water lily, is a perennial aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Nymphaeaceae and is characterized by its broad, floating leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes. Its flowers are typically white or pink, opening in the morning and closing at night.

Nymphaea longifolia plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
