Notocampylum decrescens

Notocampylum decrescens, commonly known as the slender marsh orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marsh environments in South America.

It typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, featuring slender stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a spike, which bloom in early spring. Its root system is fibrous, allowing it to thrive in waterlogged soils.

Notocampylum decrescens plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various microorganisms.
