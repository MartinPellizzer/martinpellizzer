Nicotiana rubra

Nicotiana rubra, commonly known as the red tobacco flower, is a species native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its striking red or purple flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and produces small, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are ovate with a rough texture and are often covered in fine hairs.

Nicotiana rubra is known for its ornamental value and is cultivated for its vibrant color and fragrance.
