Nisa albiflora

Nisa albiflora, commonly known as the whiteflower nisa, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, white petals and compact growth habit. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soil and full sun exposure. Its foliage consists of small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence.

Nisa albiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
