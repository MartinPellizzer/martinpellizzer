Nepeta congesta

Nepeta congesta, commonly known as catnip, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, clustered blue or purple flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves contain nepetalactone, a compound responsible for its stimulating effect on cats.

Nepeta congesta is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
