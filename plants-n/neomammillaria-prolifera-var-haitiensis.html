Neomammillaria prolifera var. haitiensis

Neomammillaria prolifera var. haitiensis, commonly known as the Haitian hedgehog cactus, is a compact, spineless cactus native to Haiti.

It features a globular to cylindrical stem with prominent areoles and pale green to bluish-gray skin. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its foliage. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is a sought-after species among cactus enthusiasts.
