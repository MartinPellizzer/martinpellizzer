Nicotiana coquimbana

Nicotiana coquimbana, commonly known as the Coquimbo tobacco plant, is a species native to Chile.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. This plant is characterized by its hairy stems and large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink. It thrives in arid and coastal environments, often found in sandy soils.

The plant is also noted for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of nicotine.
