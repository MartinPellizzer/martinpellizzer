Neomammillaria hemisphaerica

Neomammillaria hemisphaerica, commonly known as the Easter cactus, is a species of cactus native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its short, thick stems that bear clusters of bright red or orange flowers during the spring and early summer. The plant has a compact growth habit and produces rounded, ribbed stems that are often covered in white or yellow spines. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Neomammillaria hemisphaerica is popular among cactus enthusiasts for its attractive appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
