Nepenthes singalana

Nepenthes singalana, commonly known as the Sri Lankan pitcher plant, is a tropical vine native to the island of Sri Lanka.

It is characterized by its unique pitcher-shaped leaves that secrete a sticky fluid to trap insects. The plant's pitchers vary in size and color, often displaying vibrant red or yellow hues to attract prey. Nepenthes singalana thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it a fascinating subject for both horticulture and ecological study.
