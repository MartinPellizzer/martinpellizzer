Narcissus patulus subsp. etruscus

Narcissus patulus subsp.

etruscus, commonly known as the Etruscan daffodil, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and a single, showy yellow flower with a prominent cup-like corona.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is known for its early spring blooming period.
