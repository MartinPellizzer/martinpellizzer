Najas marina var. kashmirensis

Najas marina var. kashmirensis, commonly known as the Kashmir water plant, is a submerged aquatic plant native to the freshwater ecosystems of the Kashmir region.

It belongs to the family Najadaceae and is characterized by its slender, ribbon-like leaves that are often arranged in whorls. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically found in clusters near the water surface. It thrives in slow-moving to still waters with moderate to high nutrient levels.

Its presence is an indicator of good water quality and is often found in ponds, lakes, and wetlands.
