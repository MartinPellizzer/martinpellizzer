Nipponocalamus longaevus

Nipponocalamus longaevus, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo, is a perennial grass native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 6 meters. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hair. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded, forested areas.

Nipponocalamus longaevus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in traditional landscaping and horticulture.
