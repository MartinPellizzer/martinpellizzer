Neomarica northiana var. undulata

Neomarica northiana var. undulata, commonly known as the wavy orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, wavy-edged leaves and clusters of fragrant, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.

Its blooming period typically occurs during the summer months, adding vibrant color to tropical landscapes.
