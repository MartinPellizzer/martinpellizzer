Neomammillaria dealbata

Neomammillaria dealbata, commonly known as the white-spotted pincushion cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a small, globular cactus with a ribbed stem and white, woolly spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts.
