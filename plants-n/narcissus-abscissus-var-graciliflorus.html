Narcissus abscissus var. graciliflorus

Narcissus abscissus var. graciliflorus, commonly known as the slender-flowered daffodil, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Europe.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, yellow flowers with a distinctive, narrow corona. The plant typically blooms in early spring and is well adapted to rocky or sandy soils. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.

This variety is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family and is closely related to other daffodil species.
