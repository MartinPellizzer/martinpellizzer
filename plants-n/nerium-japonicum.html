Nerium japonicum

Nerium japonicum, commonly known as the Japanese oleander, is a flowering shrub native to Japan and Korea.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage, which consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves arranged in whorls along the stems. The plant produces clusters of showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, red, and orange, typically blooming in summer. Nerium japonicum is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its resistance to drought, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

However, it is important to note that all parts of the plant are toxic if ingested.
