Nepenthes ampullaria var. racemosa

Nepenthes ampullaria var. racemosa, commonly known as the racemose pitcher plant, is a tropical carnivorous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, tubular pitchers that are often filled with digestive fluid to trap and digest insects. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers, which are typically arranged in a racemose inflorescence, giving it its scientific name. Its leaves form the distinctive pitcher structures, which are adapted for trapping prey.

This variety is valued for its unique morphology and is often cultivated by enthusiasts interested in carnivorous plants.
