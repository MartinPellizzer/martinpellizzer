Neomarica caulosa

Neomarica caulosa, commonly known as the white ginger lily, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features striking, pale pink to white, showy flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has strap-like, lance-shaped leaves that are often variegated with green and white markings. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade.

Neomarica caulosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
