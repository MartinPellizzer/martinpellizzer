Nicotiana tabacum var. gigantea

Nicotiana tabacum var. gigantea, commonly known as the giant tobacco plant, is a striking species native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet, often appearing in clusters. The plant can grow up to 6 feet tall, making it a prominent feature in gardens and botanical collections. Its leaves are broad and waxy, with a distinct tobacco scent, and it is cultivated for both ornamental and historical purposes.

This variety is also notable for its use in studying plant biology and its relation to the economically significant tobacco plant.
