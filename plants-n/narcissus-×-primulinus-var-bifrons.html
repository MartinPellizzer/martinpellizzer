Narcissus × primulinus var. bifrons

Narcissus × primulinus var. bifrons, commonly known as the double-petaled daffodil, is a hybrid species within the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, featuring two distinct petal-like structures that resemble petals and sepals. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with narrow, grass-like leaves and a single, showy flower. Its bloom period occurs in early spring, producing bright yellow or white flowers with a strong, sweet fragrance.

This variety is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation.
