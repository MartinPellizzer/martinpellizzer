Nidularium innocentii var. wittmackianum

Nidularium innocentii var. wittmackianum, commonly known as the Wittmack's spider plant, is a striking bromeliad native to South America.

It is characterized by its rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a central cup to collect water. The plant produces vibrant orange or red inflorescences that bloom in the spring, adding visual interest to its foliage. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This variety is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental appeal and ease of care.
