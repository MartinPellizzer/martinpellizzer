Nicolaia venusta

Nicolaia venusta, commonly known as the golden cupplant, is a striking perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features a robust, upright stem that can grow up to 6 feet tall, adorned with palmate, lobed leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers in large, cup-shaped clusters during the summer months. Its root system is fibrous and deep, allowing it to thrive in well-drained soils.

Nicolaia venusta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and unique foliage.
