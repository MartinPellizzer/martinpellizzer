Nepeta shepardii

Nepeta shepardii, commonly known as Shepard's catmint, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pale blue or lavender flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its aromatic foliage and attractive flowers make it both ornamental and beneficial to pollinators.
