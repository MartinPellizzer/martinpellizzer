Nipponocalamus naucinopilus

Nipponocalamus naucinopilus, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems that grow in dense clusters. The plant typically reaches a height of about 1 to 2 meters and has narrow, elongated leaves with a smooth, grassy texture. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are often clustered at the ends of the stems.

Nipponocalamus naucinopilus is valued for its ornamental appearance and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
