Nemophila heterophylla var. nemorensis

Nemophila heterophylla var. nemorensis, commonly known as the Sierra blue-eyed Mary, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and produces clusters of small, sky-blue flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and vary in size depending on the plant's environment. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This variety is often found in meadows and open woodlands at higher elevations.
