Narcissus papyraceus subsp. barlae

Narcissus papyraceus subsp.

barlae, commonly known as the Barla narcissus, is a rare and small bulbous plant native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its delicate, narrow leaves and small, white flowers with a yellow center.

This subspecies is typically found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to Mediterranean climates. Its botanical features include a short stem and a single, often nodding flower.
