Nymphaea × thiona

Nymphaea × thiona, commonly known as the tropical water lily, is a hybrid aquatic plant belonging to the Nymphaeaceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white, pink, and yellow. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes. Its roots anchor in the substrate, while its stems spread across the water's surface.

Nymphaea × thiona plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
