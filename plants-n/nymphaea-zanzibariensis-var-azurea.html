Nymphaea zanzibariensis var. azurea

Nymphaea zanzibariensis var. azurea, commonly known as the Zanzibar blue lotus, is a striking aquatic plant native to the lakes of Zanzibar.

It is characterized by its large, showy blue to violet petals that bloom in the early morning. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often in shallow waters with soft, muddy substrates. Its leaves are broad and floating, providing habitat for aquatic life.

The species is prized for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in water gardens.
