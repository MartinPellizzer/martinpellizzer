Nierembergia scoparia var. breviflora

Nierembergia scoparia var. breviflora, commonly known as the short-flowered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features slender, branching stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically in shades of blue or purple. The leaves are narrow and lanceolate, arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid environments.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
