Nasturtium palustre f. longipoes

Nasturtium palustre f. longipoes, commonly known as the marsh nasturtium, is a herbaceous plant characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and bright orange or yellow flowers.

It is native to wetland areas and thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. The plant produces edible leaves and seeds, which have a peppery flavor similar to watercress. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological balance.

The species is often used in gardens for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
