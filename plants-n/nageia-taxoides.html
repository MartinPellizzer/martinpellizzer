Nageia taxoides

Nageia taxoides, commonly known as the silver fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its needle-like leaves that are often covered in a silvery or whitish bloom, giving it its common name. The tree produces small, cone-like structures that contain seeds, typical of conifers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mixed forests.

Nageia taxoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in forest ecosystems.
