Nymphaea cyanea

Nymphaea cyanea, commonly known as the blue lotus, is a striking aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy blue or violet petals that bloom at night. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes. Its leaves are broad and floating, providing shade and habitat for aquatic life.

Nymphaea cyanea is also valued for its ornamental beauty and has cultural significance in various regions.
