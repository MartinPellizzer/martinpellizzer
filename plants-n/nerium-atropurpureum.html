Nerium atropurpureum

Nerium atropurpureum, commonly known as the purple oleander, is a flowering shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking clusters of showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has simple, lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and arranged oppositely along the stems. It is a member of the Apocynaceae family, which includes other toxic plants like the milkweed.

The bark is smooth and greyish, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
