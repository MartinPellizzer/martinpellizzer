Nymphoides siamensis

Nymphoides siamensis, commonly known as the Siam water hyacinth, is a floating aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features striking violet-blue flowers that bloom above the water surface, often in clusters. The plant has broad, waxy leaves that help it float and absorb sunlight for photosynthesis. Its roots are submerged, anchoring it in shallow water and facilitating nutrient uptake.

Nymphoides siamensis is often used in ornamental water gardens due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in still water environments.
