Nama dichotoma f. stricta

Nama dichotoma f. stricta, commonly known as the narrowleaf nama, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a powdery coating. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers during the summer months, which are clustered in inflorescences. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Its compact form and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
