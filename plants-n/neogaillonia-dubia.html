Neogaillonia dubia

Neogaillonia dubia, commonly known as the rare alpine bellflower, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

The plant has a fibrous root system and hairy stems, which help it withstand harsh environmental conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine pubescence, aiding in water retention.

Neogaillonia dubia is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations to extreme altitudes and limited geographic distribution.
