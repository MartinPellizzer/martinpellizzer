Nuxia tomentella var. meridionalis

Nuxia tomentella var. meridionalis, commonly known as the Southern woolly nuxia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid conditions. Its leaves are small, often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers.

This species is of interest in horticulture for its ornamental value and resilience to harsh environments.
