Nigella taurica

Nigella taurica, commonly known as the Tauric fennel, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region and parts of southern Russia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and delicate, feathery leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early summer, followed by spherical, brown seed heads. Its seeds, which are small and black, are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice in some cuisines.

Nigella taurica thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, making it a suitable plant for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
