Narcissus montsiccianus

Narcissus montsiccianus, commonly known as the Sicilian daffodil, is a species native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Sicily.

It is a bulbous perennial plant characterized by its tall, slender stems and bright yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in early spring, adding a vibrant splash of color to its native habitat. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming a basal rosette.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
