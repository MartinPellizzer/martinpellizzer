Nectandra moritziana

Nectandra moritziana, commonly known as the Moritz's nectandra, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurel trees, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, white, clustered flowers that are typically pollinated by insects, followed by the development of woody, elongated fruits containing several seeds. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine, and the tree is valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.

Nectandra moritziana thrives in humid, shaded environments and plays a role in the ecological balance of its native habitats.
