Neodiscocactus boliviensis

Neodiscocactus boliviensis, commonly known as the Bolivian discocactus, is a unique cactus species native to the high Andes of Bolivia.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are covered in dense, woolly white spines, which help protect it from intense sunlight and moisture loss. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its otherwise desolate appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for arid environments.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
