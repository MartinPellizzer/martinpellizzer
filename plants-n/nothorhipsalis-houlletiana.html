Nothorhipsalis houlletiana

Nothorhipsalis houlletiana, commonly known as the Houllet's rat-tail cactus, is a hybrid species native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, cylindrical stems that resemble a rat's tail. The plant features spiny areoles and produces small, white flowers during the warmer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Due to its unique appearance and hybrid nature, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
