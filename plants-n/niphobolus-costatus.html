Niphobolus costatus

Niphobolus costatus, commonly known as the white-fruited bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. The plant features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, often with a distinctive yellow throat. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, wind-dispersed seeds.
