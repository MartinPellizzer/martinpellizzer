Nerophila congolensis

Nerophila congolensis, commonly known as the Congo lily, is a flowering plant native to Central Africa.

It belongs to the Araceae family, which is characterized by its unique inflorescence structure known as a spadix, surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and swamps. It produces striking, colorful flowers that attract pollinators like insects and birds.

Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, contributing to its ornamental value in horticulture.
