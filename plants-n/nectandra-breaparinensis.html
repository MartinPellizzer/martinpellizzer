Nectandra breaparinensis

Nectandra breaparinensis, commonly known as the Guayacan tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other aromatic plants such as cinnamon and avocado. The tree is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and lance-shaped leaves that are often aromatic. It produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by dark purple berries.

The plant is valued for its timber and has traditional uses in local medicine.
