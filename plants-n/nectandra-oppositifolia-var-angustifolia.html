Nectandra oppositifolia var. angustifolia

Nectandra oppositifolia var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed nectandra, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often glossy and arranged oppositely along the stems. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by elliptical, woody fruits containing flat, oval seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland and hill forests.

The species is valued for its aromatic wood and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
