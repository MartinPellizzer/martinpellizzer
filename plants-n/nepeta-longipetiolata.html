Nepeta longipetiolata

Nepeta longipetiolata, commonly known as the long-petioled catnip, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features upright stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a distinctive serrated margin. The plant produces clusters of small, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

Its aromatic foliage and attractive flowers make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and pollinator-friendly landscapes.
