Neophloga digitata

Neophloga digitata, commonly known as the digitate fern, is a species of flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that resemble fingers, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in compact inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Neophloga digitata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
