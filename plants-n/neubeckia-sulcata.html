Neubeckia sulcata

Neubeckia sulcata, commonly known as the zigzag plant, is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its distinctive, zigzag-shaped stems.

It produces small, yellow flowers that are typically clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. The plant is native to South America and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a slightly hairy texture.

Neubeckia sulcata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its unique appearance and ability to attract pollinators.
