Narcissus longispathus var. brachysiphon

Narcissus longispathus var. brachysiphon, commonly known as the short-siphon daffodil, is a variety of the genus Narcissus native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its narrow, strap-like leaves and a single, elongated flower with a prominent, narrow perianth. The tepals are typically yellow, with a white or greenish base, and the plant blooms in early spring. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its delicate, fragrant flowers.
