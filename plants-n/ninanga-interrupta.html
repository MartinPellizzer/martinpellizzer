Ninanga interrupta

Ninanga interrupta, commonly known as the interrupted ninebark, is a deciduous shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are divided into nine to thirteen oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by small, fuzzy fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones and forest edges.

Ninanga interrupta is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
