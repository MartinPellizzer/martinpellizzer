Narcissus leonensis subsp. primigenius

Narcissus leonensis subsp.

primigenius, commonly known as the Spanish daffodil, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Europe. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers with bright yellow petals.

The flowers typically bloom in early spring, adding a vibrant touch to gardens and natural habitats. This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated for its resilience and adaptability.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice among gardeners.
