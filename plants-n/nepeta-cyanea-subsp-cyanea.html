Nepeta cyanea subsp. cyanea

Nepeta cyanea subsp.

cyanea, commonly known as catmint, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features slender, upright stems covered in fine hairs and produces clusters of blue to purple tubular flowers.

The plant has lance-shaped, aromatic leaves that are often used in herbal teas. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens. Nepeta cyanea subsp.

cyanea is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
