Nolina palmeri var. brandegeei

Nolina palmeri var. brandegeei, commonly known as the Brandegee nolina, is a shrub-like plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features dense, cylindrical clusters of evergreen leaves that are typically blue-gray in color, providing a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white to yellowish flowers in late spring or early summer, followed by dark blue berries. It is well adapted to arid environments, with a deep root system that allows it to survive in poor soil conditions.

Its slow growth rate and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for xeriscaping in desert landscapes.
