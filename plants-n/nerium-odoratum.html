Nerium odoratum

Nerium odoratum, commonly known as the oleander, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, red, and yellow. The plant has simple, lance-shaped leaves and is known for its adaptability to various soil types. It is a member of the Apocynaceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Nerium odoratum is also noted for its toxicity, as all parts of the plant contain cardiac glycosides.
