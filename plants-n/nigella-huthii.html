Nigella huthii

Nigella huthii, commonly known as the Persian cowpea, is a flowering plant in the Ranunculaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant produces narrow, linear leaves and has a taproot system that allows it to thrive in rocky and arid environments. Its seeds are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice in some cuisines.

Nigella huthii is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens due to its early spring bloom.
