Nymphaea vanildae

Nymphaea vanildae, commonly known as the Vanillean water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to the freshwater ecosystems of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, broad, heart-shaped leaves that float on the water's surface, providing habitat for various aquatic organisms. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming in the early morning and closing in the heat of the day. Its roots anchor it in the muddy substrate, while its rhizomes allow it to spread vegetatively.

Nymphaea vanildae plays an important role in maintaining water quality and supporting biodiversity in its native habitats.
