Narcissus cantabricus subsp. hedraeanthus

Narcissus cantabricus subsp.

hedraeanthus, commonly known as the Cantabrian narcissus, is a subspecies native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, fragrant flowers with six petals.

The plant typically grows in rocky or forested areas, preferring well-drained soils. It blooms in early spring, adding a delicate touch of yellow to the landscape.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens.
