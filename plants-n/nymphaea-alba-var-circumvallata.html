Nymphaea alba var. circumvallata

Nymphaea alba var. circumvallata, commonly known as the White Water Lily, is a aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features showy white flowers that bloom above the water surface, often with a slight pinkish hue. The plant has broad, waxy leaves that float on the water's surface, providing shade and habitat for aquatic life. Its roots are anchored in the muddy substrate, and it reproduces through seeds and rhizomes.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in water ecosystems.
