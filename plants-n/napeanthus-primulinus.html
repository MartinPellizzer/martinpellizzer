Napeanthus primulinus

Napeanthus primulinus, commonly known as the golden flame orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow-orange flowers, which are often adorned with intricate patterns of darker hues. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, such as rainforests, where it relies on mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient absorption. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, with a waxy surface that helps retain moisture in its natural habitat.

Napeanthus primulinus is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique and eye-catching blooms.
