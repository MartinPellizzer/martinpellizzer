Nitrosalsola rodinii

Nitrosalsola rodinii, commonly known as the rodinia saltbush, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, succulent leaves that are adapted to store water, a feature typical of plants in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access groundwater in nutrient-poor soils.

Nitrosalsola rodinii is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
