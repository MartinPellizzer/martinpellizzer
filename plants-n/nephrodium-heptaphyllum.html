Nephrodium heptaphyllum

Nephrodium heptaphyllum, commonly known as the seven-leaf kidney fern, is a unique fern species characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology.

It features a fiddlehead-like frond that uncurls into a compound structure with seven pairs of leaflets, giving it its common name. The plant is native to tropical regions and thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its reproductive structures include sori that are clustered on the underside of the fronds.

Nephrodium heptaphyllum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in forest ecosystems.
