Nicotiana tomentosa

Nicotiana tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades such as tomatoes and peppers. It is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and fragrance.

Nicotiana tomentosa is also noted for its ability to attract nocturnal pollinators like moths.
