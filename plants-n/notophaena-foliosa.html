Notophaena foliosa

Notophaena foliosa, commonly known as the leafy mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of host trees.

It has small, scale-like leaves and produces white, fleshy berries that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant relies on its host for water and some nutrients, while photosynthesis provides it with essential carbohydrates. Notophaena foliosa is native to parts of Africa and is often found in humid, tropical environments.

Its unique ecological role makes it an important species in certain forest ecosystems.
