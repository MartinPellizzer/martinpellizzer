Nigritella nigra subsp. runei

Nigritella nigra subsp.

runei, commonly known as the Runes' black nightshade, is a perennial herb native to specific regions of Southeast Asia. It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades such as tomatoes and potatoes.

The plant is characterized by its dark purple to black berries, which are typically small and clustered. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its striking appearance and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
