Nemastylis tenuis var. caerulescens

Nemastylis tenuis var. caerulescens, commonly known as the blue-flowered nemastylis, is a striking member of the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, delicate leaves that are typically finely divided. The plant produces clusters of tiny, blue or violet flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variant is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
