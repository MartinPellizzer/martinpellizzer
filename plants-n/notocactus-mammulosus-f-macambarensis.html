Notocactus mammulosus f. macambarensis

Notocactus mammulosus f. macambarensis, commonly known as the Macambarensis Candelabra Cactus, is a unique form of the mammulosus species native to the dry regions of Brazil.

It is characterized by its tall, branching structure with cylindrical stems that develop a distinctive reddish hue as they mature. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its woody stems. It is a slow-growing cactus that thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a valuable and somewhat elusive specimen in botanical collections.
