Nephrodium tenerum

Nephrodium tenerum, commonly known as the slender kidney fern, is a tropical fern species characterized by its delicate, frond-like structures.

It features a rhizomatous growth habit, with slender, creeping stems that produce fiddlehead-like fronds. The leaves are bipinnate, with finely divided segments that give the plant a lacy appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The plant's botanical structure supports its role in forest ecosystems, contributing to soil stabilization and providing habitat for small organisms.
