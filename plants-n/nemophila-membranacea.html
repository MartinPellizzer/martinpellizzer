Nemophila membranacea

Nemophila membranacea, commonly known as the cushion flower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Boraginaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and native plant landscapes.

Due to its delicate appearance and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in conservation efforts.
