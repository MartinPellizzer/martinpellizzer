Nerine krigei subsp. falcata

Nerine krigei subsp.

falcata, commonly known as the Cape falcate nerine, is a bulbous plant native to South Africa. It features striking, lance-shaped leaves that are often bluish-green in color.

The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance, it is widely cultivated in Mediterranean climates.
