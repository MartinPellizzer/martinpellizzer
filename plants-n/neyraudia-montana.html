Neyraudia montana

Neyraudia montana, commonly known as the mountain reed or Montane reed, is a perennial grass native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and stream banks. The plant features slender, arching leaves that are often blue-green in color, with a slightly hairy texture. Its inflorescence consists of a loose panicle of small, inconspicuous flowers.

Neyraudia montana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its unique foliage.
