Narcissus tazetta var. syriacus

Narcissus tazetta var. syriacus, commonly known as the Crown Imperial, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem that bears multiple showy, trumpet-shaped flowers arranged in a spiral pattern. The flowers are typically bright yellow or orange, with a strong, sweet fragrance. The plant produces a cluster of small, green, ovoid bulbs at the base, which can be used for propagation.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.
