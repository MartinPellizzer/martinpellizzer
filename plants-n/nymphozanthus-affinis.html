Nymphozanthus affinis

Nymphozanthus affinis, commonly known as the narrowleaf false mallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and arid environments. The plant features slender, erect stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its showy, yellow flowers are clustered in terminal inflorescences and bloom in the spring.

Nymphozanthus affinis is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance, making it a suitable plant for xeriscaping.
