Nolina parviflora

Nolina parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered nolina, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asparagaceae.

It is native to Australia and is characterized by its rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers on a tall, leafless stem, typically during the cooler months. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Nolina parviflora is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its elegant appearance and low maintenance requirements.
