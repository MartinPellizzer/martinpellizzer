Neotorularia maximowiczii

Neotorularia maximowiczii, commonly known as the Siberian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the cold climates of Eastern Europe and Siberia.

It features basal rosettes of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or violet. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along riverbanks. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.

Neotorularia maximowiczii is also noted for its ability to tolerate harsh winter conditions.
