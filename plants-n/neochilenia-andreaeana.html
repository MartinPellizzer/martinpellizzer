Neochilenia andreaeana

Neochilenia andreaeana, commonly known as the Andean orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, while its flowers display a range of colors, from white to pink and purple.

Neochilenia andreaeana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
