Nepeta hormozganica

Nepeta hormozganica, commonly known as Persian catnip, is a flowering plant native to Iran.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant features square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. Its small, clustered flowers are typically blue or purple, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

It is often grown for its ornamental value and mild aromatic properties.
