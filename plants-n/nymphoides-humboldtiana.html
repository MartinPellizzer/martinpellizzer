Nymphoides humboldtiana

Nymphoides humboldtiana, commonly known as the Humboldt water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to South America.

It features broad, heart-shaped leaves that float on the water's surface, supported by long, slender petioles. The plant produces showy white flowers with prominent yellow stamens, which bloom in the early morning. Its roots are rhizomatous, allowing it to spread and form dense colonies in still water.

Nymphoides humboldtiana is often cultivated in ponds and water gardens for its ornamental value and ability to provide habitat for aquatic life.
