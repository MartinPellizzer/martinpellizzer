Narcissus pseudonarcissus f. obvallaris

Narcissus pseudonarcissus f. obvallaris, commonly known as the white narcissus or the common daffodil, is a variant of the daffodil species characterized by its distinctive floral morphology.

This form exhibits a pale, often white or cream-colored corona that is typically smaller and more rounded compared to the standard form. The tepals are usually a bright yellow, with a smooth and glossy surface. It is a perennial herbaceous plant that thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is commonly cultivated in gardens for its early spring blooms.
