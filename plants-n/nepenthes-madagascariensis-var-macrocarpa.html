Nepenthes madagascariensis var. macrocarpa

Nepenthes madagascariensis var. macrocarpa, commonly known as the giant Madagascan pitcher plant, is a tropical vine native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its large, waxy pitcher-shaped leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces nectar to attract insects, which then fall into the pitcher and are digested by the plant's digestive enzymes. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is adapted to nutrient-poor soils.

This variant is distinguished by its larger pitchers and more robust growth compared to other Nepenthes species.
