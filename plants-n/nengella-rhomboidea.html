Nengella rhomboidea

Nengella rhomboidea, commonly known as the rhomboid nengella, is a flowering plant native to specific regions in Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive rhomboid-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and woody stems that become more prominent with age.

Nengella rhomboidea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
