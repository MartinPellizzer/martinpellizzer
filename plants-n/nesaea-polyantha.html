Nesaea polyantha

Nesaea polyantha, commonly known as the pink-flowered bell heather, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, oval berries that are typically red or black when mature. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or forested habitats.

Nesaea polyantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
