Nepeta noraschenica

Nepeta noraschenica, commonly known as the Armenian catmint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Armenia.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens or borders.

Nepeta noraschenica is valued for its ornamental appeal and mild medicinal properties.
