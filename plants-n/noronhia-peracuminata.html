Noronhia peracuminata

Noronhia peracuminata, commonly known as the Brazilian star apple, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is a small tree or shrub that produces small, white, fragrant flowers. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and is typically found in humid, shaded environments. Its fruit, which resembles a small star, is edible and has a sweet, aromatic flavor.

Noronhia peracuminata is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary uses in its native region.
