Nicotiana quadrivalvis var. bigelovii

Nicotiana quadrivalvis var. bigelovii, commonly known as the Bigelov's four-valved tobacco, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically violet or purple in color. The plant belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is known for its hairy, glandular leaves. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along dry streambeds.

This variant is notable for its unique floral structure and its historical use in traditional medicine by indigenous communities.
