Nicotiana longiflora var. noctiflora

Nicotiana longiflora var. noctiflora, commonly known as the nightflower tobacco, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking, large, tubular flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, with lance-shaped leaves and a hairy stem. Its flowers are usually violet to blue in color, with a strong, sweet fragrance that intensifies in the evening.

This variety is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique blooming behavior.
