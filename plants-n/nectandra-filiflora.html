Nectandra filiflora

Nectandra filiflora, commonly known as the thread-leaved sandarac, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by berries that are used in traditional medicine. Its wood is valued for its aromatic properties and has been historically used in incense and varnishes.

Nectandra filiflora thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.
