Nicotiana angustifolia var. parviflora

Nicotiana angustifolia var. parviflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tobacco flower, is a herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and small, pale purple to white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is closely related to other tobacco species.

Due to its ornamental value and historical significance in traditional medicine, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and research settings.
