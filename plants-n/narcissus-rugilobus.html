Narcissus rugilobus

Narcissus rugilobus, commonly known as the rugilobus daffodil, is a species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, strap-like leaves and a single, showy flower with a yellow perianth. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It blooms in early spring, adding a splash of color to its natural habitat.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
