Nipponocalamus pseudocommunis

Nipponocalamus pseudocommunis, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its tall, slender culms and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in panicles. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.
