Nothofagus macrocarpa

Nothofagus macrocarpa, commonly known as the mountain beech, is a species of evergreen tree native to southern South America.

It is characterized by its dark, smooth bark and broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often pubescent beneath. The tree produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by distinctive, edible, winged fruits called samaras. It thrives in temperate rainforests and is adapted to high-altitude environments.

Nothofagus macrocarpa plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
