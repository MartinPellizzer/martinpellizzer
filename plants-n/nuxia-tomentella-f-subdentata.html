Nuxia tomentella f. subdentata

Nuxia tomentella f. subdentata, commonly known as the hairy nuxia or subdentate nuxia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are typically subdeltoid in shape, with a soft, hairy texture. It produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, spherical fruits.

This plant is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
