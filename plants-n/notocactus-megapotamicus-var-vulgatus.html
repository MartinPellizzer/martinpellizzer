Notocactus megapotamicus var. vulgatus

Notocactus megapotamicus var. vulgatus, commonly known as the "Mendoza cactus," is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its smooth, green stems and spiny areoles. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months, which are borne in clusters at the ends of the stems. Its growth habit is typically solitary or in small clusters, and it is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and as a houseplant.
