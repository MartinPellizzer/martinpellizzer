Nymphaea nocturna

Nymphaea nocturna, commonly known as the night water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, showy, night-blooming flowers that are typically deep pink or purple in color. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes. Its leaves are broad and floating, providing a habitat for various aquatic organisms.

The flowers close at dawn, making it a unique addition to water gardens and wetland ecosystems.
