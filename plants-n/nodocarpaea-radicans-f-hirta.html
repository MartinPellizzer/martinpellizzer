Nodocarpaea radicans f. hirta

Nodocarpaea radicans f. hirta, commonly known as the hairy spreading nodocarpaea, is a variant of the nodocarpaea genus native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

This plant is characterized by its trailing growth habit and hairy foliage, which distinguishes it from other forms of the species. It features small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish in color, and its leaves are often covered in fine, short hairs. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in forest understories or rocky crevices.
