Nothopanax simplex var. sinclairii

Nothopanax simplex var. sinclairii, commonly known as Sinclair's simple nothopanax, is a variant of the nothopanax plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a smooth, glossy surface. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with white or pale yellow flowers, which are followed by small, round berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded forest understories.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
