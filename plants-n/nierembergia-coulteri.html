Nierembergia coulteri

Nierembergia coulteri, commonly known as Coulter's bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Nierembergia coulteri is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
