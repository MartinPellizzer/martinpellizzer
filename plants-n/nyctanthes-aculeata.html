Nyctanthes aculeata

Nyctanthes aculeata, commonly known as night-flowering jasmine, is a deciduous shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of fragrant, white flowers that bloom at night. The plant produces small, red berries that contain the seeds, which are dispersed by birds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Nyctanthes aculeata is valued for its ornamental beauty and has traditional medicinal uses in Ayurveda.
