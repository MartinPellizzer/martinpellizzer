Nicotiana buccinans

Nicotiana buccinans, commonly known as the trumpet flower or the sacred tobacco, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often resembling a trumpet. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and produces showy blooms from late summer to early fall. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

The plant is part of the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
