Nemorosa quinquefolia

Nemorosa quinquefolia, commonly known as the five-leafed woodland plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to temperate regions.

It features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, giving it a distinctive, fern-like appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are small, white or greenish, and clustered in umbels, often appearing in late spring.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and ability to thrive in partial shade.
