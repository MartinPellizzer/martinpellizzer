Nepeta autraniana

Nepeta autraniana, commonly known as the Persian catnip, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, clustered blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their calming properties.

Nepeta autraniana thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
