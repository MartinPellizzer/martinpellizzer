Nicotiana neesii

Nicotiana neesii, commonly known as the Indian tobacco, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its trumpet-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or purple. The plant produces capsules containing numerous small, black seeds, and its leaves are often hairy and aromatic.

Nicotiana neesii is also notable for its historical use in traditional medicine and its role in the cultivation of tobacco species.
