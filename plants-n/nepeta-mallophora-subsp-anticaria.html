Nepeta mallophora subsp. anticaria

Nepeta mallophora subsp.

anticaria, commonly known as the purple catmint, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Mediterranean region. It features square, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces clusters of small, tubular blue to purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

Its aromatic foliage and showy blooms make it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.
