Najas arguta var. conferta

Najas arguta var. conferta, commonly known as the narrowleaf waterweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater habitats in North America.

It is characterized by its slender, ribbon-like leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are pollinated by water currents rather than insects. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Due to its rapid growth, it can become invasive in certain water bodies if not managed properly.
