Nephrolepis biserrata var. digitiformis

Nephrolepis biserrata var. digitiformis, commonly known as the finger fern, is a species of fern characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds.

The fronds are typically bipinnate, with small, linear pinnae that give the plant its distinctive appearance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread and form dense colonies.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and indoor plant cultivation.
