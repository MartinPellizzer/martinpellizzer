Nastus obtusus

Nastus obtusus, commonly known as the broad-leaved nasturtium, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and bright yellow or orange flowers. The plant is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cress. Its flowers are typically borne in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Nastus obtusus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has mild peppery flavors in its leaves and seeds.
