Nassella caespitosa var. peruviana

Nassella caespitosa var. peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It forms dense, tufted clumps with narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-gray in color. The plant produces slender, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, feathery flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in dry landscapes.
