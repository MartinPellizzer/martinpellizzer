Niemeyera acuminata

Niemeyera acuminata, commonly known as the Brazilian star apple, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is a small evergreen tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, with glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that are followed by fleshy, star-shaped fruits. Its fruit is edible, with a sweet, juicy pulp that has a flavor similar to a mix between a pear and a melon.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary uses in its native region.
