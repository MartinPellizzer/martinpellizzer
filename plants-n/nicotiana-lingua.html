Nicotiana lingua

Nicotiana lingua, commonly known as the Spanish tobacco or the long-tongued tobacco plant, is a species native to South America.

It is a herbaceous annual plant that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a distinct aromatic scent. Its flowers are trumpet-shaped, usually pink to purple in color, and are pollinated primarily by hummingbirds.

The plant contains nicotine and is cultivated for both ornamental and medicinal purposes.
