Nepeta spruneri

Nepeta spruneri, commonly known as catmint, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and western Asia, often found in rocky or dry habitats. The plant features aromatic, slender stems and small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. Its leaves are typically narrow and slightly fuzzy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Nepeta spruneri is valued not only for its ornamental qualities but also for its historical use in herbal remedies.
