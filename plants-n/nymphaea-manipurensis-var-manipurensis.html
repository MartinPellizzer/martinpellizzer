Nymphaea manipurensis var. manipurensis

Nymphaea manipurensis var. manipurensis, commonly known as the Manipur water lily, is a species of aquatic plant native to the freshwater lakes and ponds of Manipur, India.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that float on the water's surface, supported by long, slender petioles. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white, with a distinctive yellow center, blooming during the warmer months. Its roots are anchored in the muddy substrate, and it plays a vital role in maintaining the ecological balance of its habitat.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and traditional uses in local communities.
