Noronhia foveolata

Noronhia foveolata, commonly known as the Brazilian starfruit or pitaya, is a climbing shrub native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fleshy, star-shaped fruits that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, white flowers with a strong fragrance. Its fruit is edible, with a sweet, tropical flavor, and is often consumed fresh or used in juices.

Noronhia foveolata is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary uses in South American countries.
