Narcissus bicolor var. breviflorus

Narcissus bicolor var. breviflorus, commonly known as the narrow-flower daffodil, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Europe.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces one to three bright yellow, funnel-shaped flowers with a distinctive reflexed corona. The plant typically blooms in early spring, adding a splash of color to woodland gardens. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a versatile addition to gardens.

Its compact size and elegant appearance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
