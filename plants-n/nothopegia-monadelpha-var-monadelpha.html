Nothopegia monadelpha var. monadelpha

Nothopegia monadelpha var. monadelpha, commonly known as the red-flowered hakea, is a shrub native to southwestern Australia.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are typically grey-green in color and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces striking red or orange flowers arranged in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance and minimal water requirements.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
