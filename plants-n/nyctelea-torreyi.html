Nyctelea torreyi

Nyctelea torreyi, commonly known as the nightshade morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual that grows up to 2 meters tall, featuring heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that open in the morning. The plant produces small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a stem that is often hairy and branching.

Nyctelea torreyi is typically found in moist, disturbed soils along stream banks and in open woodlands.
