Nepeta avromanica

Nepeta avromanica, commonly known as the Persian catmint, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine hairs and produces small, fragrant blue or violet flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a strong, mint-like aroma. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Nepeta avromanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
