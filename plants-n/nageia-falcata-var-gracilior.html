Nageia falcata var. gracilior

Nageia falcata var. gracilior, commonly known as the slender fan-leaf cycad, is a rare and ancient plant species native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, fan-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a tight, rosette-like pattern. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain both male and female reproductive organs, typical of cycads. Its slow growth rate and specific habitat requirements make it highly sensitive to environmental changes.

Due to its rarity and ecological significance, it is often found in protected areas and is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
