Nomaphila siamensis

Nomaphila siamensis, commonly known as the Siam orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to greenish-white, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination.

Nomaphila siamensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
