Nephrodesmus parvifolius

Nephrodesmus parvifolius, commonly known as the small-leaf nephrodesmus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom during the rainy season. Its roots are fibrous and often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
