Neoporteria horrida var. coliguayensis

Neoporteria horrida var. coliguayensis, commonly known as the Coliguayensis Bursera, is a shrub native to the dry forests of Central America.

It is characterized by its rough, spiny stems and small, elliptical leaves with a waxy coating. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by small, spherical fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
