Nasturtium sylvestre subsp. barbaraeoides

Nasturtium sylvestre subsp.

barbaraeoides, commonly known as the wild nasturtium, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It features heart-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated edge and produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of orange and yellow.

The plant is known for its peppery flavor, similar to watercress, and is often used in culinary applications. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

This subspecies is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its edible qualities.
