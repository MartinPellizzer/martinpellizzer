Nannoseris

Nannoseris, commonly known as the narrowleaf flax, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Polygonaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces clusters of tiny, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Nannoseris is often found in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or streamside habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used historically for its fibrous stalks.
