Nastus capitatus

Nastus capitatus, commonly known as the yellow wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and thrives in moist, well-drained soil. It is a member of the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and it has the ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic bacteria.

Nastus capitatus is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and flowers.
