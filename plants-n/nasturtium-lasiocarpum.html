Nasturtium lasiocarpum

Nasturtium lasiocarpum, commonly known as the hairy-seeded nasturtium, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Tropaeolaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, showy yellow and orange flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that are covered in fine, hairy structures, giving it its scientific name. Its leaves and flowers are edible, often used in culinary applications for their peppery flavor.
