Neurolepis pittieri

Neurolepis pittieri, commonly known as the Mexican feather grass, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, pale-green to brownish flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a slight bluish hue.

Neurolepis pittieri is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its delicate appearance and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
