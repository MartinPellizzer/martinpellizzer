Nierembergia intermedia

Nierembergia intermedia, commonly known as the Andean bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the high Andes of South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at elevations above 3,000 meters. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, adapted to the harsh mountain climate.

Nierembergia intermedia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens with cool, sunny conditions.
