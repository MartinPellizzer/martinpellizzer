Narcissus serotinus var. genuinus

Narcissus serotinus var. genuinus, commonly known as the late-flowering daffodil, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Europe.

It typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing large, showy yellow flowers with a single, upright tepal. The plant has narrow, strap-like leaves that are usually a deep green color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is noted for its hardiness and ability to naturalize over time.
