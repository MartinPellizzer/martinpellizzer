Nicotiana raimondii

Nicotiana raimondii, commonly known as the desert tobacco, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family.

It is native to the deserts of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. This plant is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It is an annual or biennial plant that thrives in arid environments due to its adaptations to drought.

The plant is also notable for its genetic simplicity, making it a valuable model organism in plant genetics research.
