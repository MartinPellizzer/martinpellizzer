Naegelia refulgens

Naegelia refulgens, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a striking plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a herbaceous perennial that features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, white pubescence. Naegelia refulgens thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its unique floral display makes it a valuable addition to Mediterranean-style gardens.
