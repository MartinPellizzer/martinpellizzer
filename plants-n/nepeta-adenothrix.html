Nepeta adenothrix

Nepeta adenothrix, commonly known as catnip, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or violet in color. Its leaves contain nepetalactone, a compound that attracts cats and has mild sedative effects on humans.

Nepeta adenothrix is often grown in gardens for its aromatic foliage and ornamental value.
