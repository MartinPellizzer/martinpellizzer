Nauclea stellata

Nauclea stellata, commonly known as starleaf nauclea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a distinctive star-shaped arrangement. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Nauclea stellata is known for its aromatic foliage and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
