Notopleura maxonii

Notopleura maxonii, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a climbing plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive white, night-blooming flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits. Its flowers emit a strong fragrance at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

Notopleura maxonii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
