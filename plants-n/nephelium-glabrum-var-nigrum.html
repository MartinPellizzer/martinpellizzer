Nephelium glabrum var. nigrum

Nephelium glabrum var. nigrum, commonly known as the black rambutan, is a tropical fruit-bearing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a variant of the rambutan species, characterized by its dark purple, almost black, hairy exterior. The plant produces small, round fruits with a sweet and aromatic pulp that is typically white or yellowish in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The fruit is valued for its unique appearance and flavor, making it a popular crop in tropical regions.
