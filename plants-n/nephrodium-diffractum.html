Nephrodium diffractum

Nephrodium diffractum, commonly known as the Fringed Nephrolepis, is a fern native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its delicate, frond-like leaves that are divided into narrow, toothed segments. The plant produces sporangia on the underside of its fronds, which are clustered in sori. Its growth habit is typically clump-forming, with rhizomes that spread horizontally to form dense colonies.

Nephrodium diffractum thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
