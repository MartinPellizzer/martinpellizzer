Nicipe graminifolia

Nicipe graminifolia, commonly known as the slender grass.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant is often found in wetlands and along riverbanks, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.

Its rapid growth makes it both a useful ornamental plant and a potential invasive species in certain environments.
