Nabalus acerifolius

Nabalus acerifolius, commonly known as the maple-leaved bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that resemble those of maple trees, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Nabalus acerifolius is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
