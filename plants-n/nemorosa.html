Nemorosa

Nemorosa, commonly known as the wood sorrel, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, often yellow or white flowers. The plants are typically found in woodland areas and thrive in moist, shaded environments. Many species are native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

Nemorosa species are often used in herbal medicine and are valued for their ornamental appeal.
