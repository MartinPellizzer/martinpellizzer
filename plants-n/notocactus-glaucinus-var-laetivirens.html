Notocactus glaucinus var. laetivirens

Notocactus glaucinus var. laetivirens, commonly known as the "Blue Ghost Cactus," is a unique cactus species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue-green stems that are often covered in a waxy, powdery coating, which helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the evening, adding a subtle beauty to its otherwise muted appearance. It is a slow-growing, columnar cactus that thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its distinctive coloration and nocturnal blooming habits make it a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
