Nyctanthes arbor-tristis

Nyctanthes arbor-tristis, commonly known as night-flowering jasmine, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white, star-shaped flowers that open at night and emit a strong, sweet scent. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptic to ovate in shape. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, black seeds.

Nyctanthes arbor-tristis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
