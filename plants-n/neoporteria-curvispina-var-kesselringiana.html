Neoporteria curvispina var. kesselringiana

Neoporteria curvispina var. kesselringiana, commonly known as the Kesselringiana variety of the zigzag plant, is a species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive zigzagging stems and small, opposite leaves that are often pubescent. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pale green flowers, which are typically borne in the axils of the leaves. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, moist soil.

This varietal form is notable for its ornamental value and adaptability to a range of garden conditions.
