Nemophila menziesii var. incana

Nemophila menziesii var. incana, commonly known as the silverleaf bluebell, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces delicate, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue and purple. The plant features hairy, silvery-gray foliage that gives it its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded woodland areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to local pollinators.
