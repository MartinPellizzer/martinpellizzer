Neodriessenia scorpioidea

Neodriessenia scorpioidea, commonly known as the scorpion orchid, is a rare and unique member of the orchid family, native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, claw-like flowers that resemble the shape of a scorpion, hence its common name. The plant has a compact growth habit with a pseudobulb and produces few, showy blooms that are typically yellow or greenish in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires careful cultivation due to its specific environmental needs.

Neodriessenia scorpioidea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often sought after by collectors and horticulturists.
