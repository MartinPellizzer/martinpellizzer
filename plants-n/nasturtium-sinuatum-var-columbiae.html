Nasturtium sinuatum var. columbiae

Nasturtium sinuatum var. columbiae, commonly known as the Columbia nasturtium, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange and yellow flowers, which are borne on slender stems and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has heart-shaped, glossy leaves with a slightly serrated edge, and its foliage is often used in culinary applications due to its peppery taste. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking both ornamental and edible plants.

The variety is known for its hardy nature and ability to reseed itself, contributing to its widespread presence in natural and cultivated landscapes.
