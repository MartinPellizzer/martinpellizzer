Narcissus aureus

Narcissus aureus, commonly known as the golden daffodil, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and western Asia, where it thrives in temperate climates. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, often with a white or green throat. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters.

Narcissus aureus is valued for its ornamental beauty and is commonly cultivated in gardens and parks.
