Nama orizabensis

Nama orizabensis, commonly known as the Orizaba mint, is a perennial herb native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and small, fragrant flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or partially shaded environments.

Nama orizabensis is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
