Notholaena nigricans

Notholaena nigricans, commonly known as the black-stemmed fern, is a species of fern native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dark, wiry stems and pinnately compound leaves that are typically covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky habitats. Its fronds are usually a deep green, and they often curl at the tips.

Notholaena nigricans is known for its delicate appearance and is often cultivated in shaded garden settings.
