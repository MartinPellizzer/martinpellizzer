Nierembergia spathulata var. pilosella

Nierembergia spathulata var. pilosella, commonly known as the velvetleaf bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its velvety, hairy leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky slopes. Its flowers bloom in the spring and are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
