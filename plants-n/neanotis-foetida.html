Neanotis foetida

Neanotis foetida, commonly known as the stinking nightshade, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are usually alternate, ovate, and have a rough texture.

The plant is known for its strong, unpleasant odor, which is most noticeable when the flowers open at night.
