Nicotiana friesii

Nicotiana friesii, commonly known as the Fries' tobacco plant, is a species native to South America, specifically Ecuador.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its hairy stems and large, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a purple or red throat. It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers.

Nicotiana friesii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is also studied for its potential in plant biology research.
