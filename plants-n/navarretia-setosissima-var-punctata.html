Navarretia setosissima var. punctata

Navarretia setosissima var. punctata, commonly known as the Sierra Valley navarretia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are typically linear and covered in fine, bristly hairs.

Navarretia setosissima var. punctata thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks.
