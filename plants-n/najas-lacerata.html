Najas lacerata

Najas lacerata, commonly known as the narrowleaf watermilfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater habitats in North America.

It belongs to the family Najadaceae and is characterized by its finely divided, ribbon-like leaves that are often mistaken for those of other water plants. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically found in clusters near the water surface. Najas lacerata plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

It is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize sediments and improve water quality.
