Nesaea verticillata

Nesaea verticillata, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, white to pale blue flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Nesaea verticillata is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
