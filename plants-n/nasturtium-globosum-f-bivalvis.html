Nasturtium globosum f. bivalvis

Nasturtium globosum f. bivalvis, commonly known as the bivalve nasturtium, is a botanical variant of the common nasturtium.

It is characterized by its distinctive, two-lobed flowers that resemble a split or bivalve shell, giving it its unique name. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate climates and is known for its vibrant, edible flowers and leaves. Its foliage is broad and heart-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

This variety is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its culinary uses in salads and garnishes.
