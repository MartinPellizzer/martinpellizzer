Nipponobambusa sawadae

Nipponobambusa sawadae, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo, is a species of grass belonging to the Bambusoideae subfamily.

It is native to East Asia, particularly Japan, where it thrives in temperate climates. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect culms and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and hardy nature.
