Nectandra leucocome

Nectandra leucocome, commonly known as white-flowered nectandra, is a tropical evergreen tree belonging to the Lauraceae family.

It is native to Central and South America, particularly found in regions with humid, tropical climates. The plant is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and elliptical, dark green leaves that are glossy on the upper surface. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in inflorescences, contributing to its ornamental value.

The tree produces small, round fruits that contain a single seed, making it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ecological studies.
