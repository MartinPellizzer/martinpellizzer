Neisosperma apoensis

Neisosperma apoensis, commonly known as the Apache prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces edible fruits, which are often consumed by both humans and wildlife. Its leaves are modified into spines, reducing water loss and providing protection from herbivores.

Neisosperma apoensis plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, supporting pollinators and providing food and shelter for various animals.
