Nasturtium sativum

Nasturtium sativum, commonly known as garden nasturtium, is an annual flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other plants like cabbages and mustard. The plant is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that come in shades of orange, yellow, and red. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a slightly peppery taste, making it a popular edible plant.

Nasturtium sativum is also used in companion planting to deter pests due to its strong scent.
