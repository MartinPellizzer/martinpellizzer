Notocactus bueneckeri f. intermedia

Notocactus bueneckeri f. intermedia, commonly known as the intermedia form of the notocactus, is a hybrid cactus species native to South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are typically ribbed and covered in white or light-colored spines. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking visual contrast to its green foliage. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in arid gardens and collections.
