Neophylum grandifolium

Neophylum grandifolium, commonly known as the large-leaved neophylum, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
