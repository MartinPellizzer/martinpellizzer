Nephrodium clypeolutatum

Nephrodium clypeolutatum, commonly known as the shield fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, shield-shaped fronds that are typically dark green and leathery in texture. The plant produces spores on the underside of its fronds, which are arranged in clusters called sori. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Nephrodium clypeolutatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
