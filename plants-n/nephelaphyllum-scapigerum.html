Nephelaphyllum scapigerum

Nephelaphyllum scapigerum, commonly known as the skybloom, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, pale blue flowers that bloom in clusters, often appearing as if suspended in the air. The plant has a slender, branching stem with small, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery down. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is adapted to withstand strong winds and low temperatures.

Its unique appearance and habitat make it a rare and visually striking species within its ecosystem.
