Nuxia sambiranensis

Nuxia sambiranensis, commonly known as the Madagascar spurge, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are often covered in a waxy cuticle, which helps reduce water loss.

Nuxia sambiranensis is also noted for its medicinal properties, used traditionally in local remedies.
