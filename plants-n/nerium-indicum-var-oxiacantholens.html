Nerium indicum var. oxiacantholens

Nerium indicum var. oxiacantholens, commonly known as the Persian yellow oleander, is a variant of the oleander plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers, which bloom in summer. The leaves are simple, opposite, and lanceolate, often with a waxy texture. The plant is known for its showy, clustered flowers that range from yellow to orange in color.

It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in arid regions.
