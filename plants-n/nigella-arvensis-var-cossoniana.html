Nigella arvensis var. cossoniana

Nigella arvensis var. cossoniana, commonly known as the Persian cowpea, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia and the Middle East.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces small, oval seeds that are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice in some cuisines. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its early blooming habit and compact growth form.
