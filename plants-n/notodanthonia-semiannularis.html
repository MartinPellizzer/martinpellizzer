Notodanthonia semiannularis

Notodanthonia semiannularis, commonly known as the ringed grass, is a species native to Australia, particularly found in the western regions.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its distinctive ring-like structures on the stems, which are formed by the arrangement of its nodes. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a tufted growth habit.

Notodanthonia semiannularis plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
