Nama

Nama, commonly known as the Namak or Namak plant, is a succulent shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family, commonly referred to as the ice plant family, and is characterized by its fleshy, water-storing leaves. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, which bloom during the rainy season. Nama is adapted to thrive in harsh, dry environments, with deep root systems that help it survive droughts.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important component of its native ecosystems.
