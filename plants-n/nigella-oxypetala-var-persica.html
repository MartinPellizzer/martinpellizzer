Nigella oxypetala var. persica

Nigella oxypetala var. persica, commonly known as the Persian cowpea or black cumin, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, pale yellow to greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, oval-shaped seeds that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. Its leaves are alternate, pinnately divided, and have a slightly aromatic scent.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
