Neoporteria Clavata

Blue mistflower, scientifically known as Neoporteria clavata, is a strikingly beautiful plant native to the southeastern United States. It is celebrated for its delicate, mist-like blue flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. This perennial shrub thrives in moist, woodland environments and is often found in swampy areas and along stream banks. Its unique appearance and ecological importance make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens. With its vibrant color and ecological benefits, the blue mistflower stands out as both a botanical gem and an essential part of local ecosystems.
