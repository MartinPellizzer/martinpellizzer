Nepeta hederacea

Nepeta hederacea, commonly known as creeping catmint, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features aromatic, hairy leaves that are often a soft gray-green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, blue-violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for ground cover in gardens.

Nepeta hederacea is known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
