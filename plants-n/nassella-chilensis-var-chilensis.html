Nassella chilensis var. chilensis

Nassella chilensis var. chilensis, commonly known as Chilean needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant features slender, wiry stems and tufted inflorescences composed of spikelets. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soils.
