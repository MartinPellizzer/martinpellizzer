Numisaureum repens

Numisaureum repens, commonly known as the creeping numisaur, is a flowering plant native to specific regions in southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters. The plant has a fibrous root system and succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
