Nasa limata

Nasa limata, commonly known as the moonflower, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is a member of the Solanaceae family, which includes other night-blooming plants like the nightshade. The plant is characterized by its large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night and close during the day. Its leaves are broad and ovate, with a slightly hairy texture.

Nasa limata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
