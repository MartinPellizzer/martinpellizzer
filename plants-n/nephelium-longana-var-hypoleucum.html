Nephelium longana var. hypoleucum

Nephelium longana var. hypoleucum, commonly known as the white longan, is a variant of the longan fruit characterized by its pale white to pale yellow flesh.

It belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia, particularly regions of Thailand and Malaysia. The fruit has a thin, smooth skin that is typically lighter in color compared to the typical longan. Botanically, it features a single seed enclosed within the fleshy, aromatic pulp.

This variety is valued for its unique flavor and is often cultivated for both local consumption and export.
