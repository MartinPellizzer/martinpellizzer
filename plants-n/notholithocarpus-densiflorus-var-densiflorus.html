Notholithocarpus densiflorus var. densiflorus

Notholithocarpus densiflorus var. densiflorus, commonly known as the dense-flowered ironwood, is a member of the Fagaceae family, characterized by its evergreen foliage and woody structure.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant produces small, cup-shaped fruits that enclose one or two seeds, typical of the genus. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing dense shade and contributing to its common name.

This species is valued for its hard, durable wood and is often used in traditional construction and crafts.
