Nauchea laurifolia

Nauchea laurifolia, commonly known as the laurel leaf nauchea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green, often with a slight waxy coating. Its flowers are small, white or pale yellow, and clustered in inflorescences.

Nauchea laurifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its foliage and fragrance.
