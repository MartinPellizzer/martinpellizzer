Neomammillaria aureiceps

Neomammillaria aureiceps, commonly known as the golden-headed pincushion cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a small, globular cactus with a distinctive yellowish-green stem covered in white to pale blue tubercles. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, adding a striking contrast to its foliage. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Neomammillaria aureiceps is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
