Nigella arvensis subsp. glaucescens

Nigella arvensis subsp.

glaucescens, commonly known as the blue hawksbeard, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny, dark seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and wastelands.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in agricultural fields and gardens.
