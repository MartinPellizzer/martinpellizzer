Nemophila menziesii var. minutiflora

Nemophila menziesii var. minutiflora, commonly known as the dwarf blue-eyed Mary, is a low-growing annual herb native to the western United States.

It features small, delicate blue or violet flowers that bloom in early spring, often in clusters at the ends of its stems. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and wildflower displays.

Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to its native habitat in coastal and mountainous regions.
