Nephelium cuspidatum subvar. dasyneurum

Nephelium cuspidatum subvar. dasyneurum, commonly known as the rough-leafed rambutan, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, spiny fruit covering, which gives it a unique appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by the distinctive hairy fruits. Its leaves are thick, leathery, and have a rough texture, contributing to its common name.

This subvariety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated for its unique fruit.
