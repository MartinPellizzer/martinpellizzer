Narcissus × ponsii-sorollae var. caracensis

Narcissus × ponsii-sorollae var. caracensis, commonly known as the Caracensian daffodil, is a hybrid species belonging to the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking yellow petals and a tall, slender stem that supports its large, cup-shaped flower. The plant typically blooms in early spring, producing showy inflorescences that are popular in horticulture. Native to the Caribbean, it thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a hybrid that showcases the genetic diversity within the Narcissus genus.
