Nephrodium megaphyllum

Nephrodium megaphyllum, commonly known as the giant nephroid, is a striking fern that exhibits unique botanical features.

It is characterized by its large, leathery fronds that can grow up to several meters in length, showcasing a complex arrangement of pinnae and pinnules. The plant's reproductive structures, including sori, are prominently located on the underside of its fronds, typical of the fern family. Its leaves are often described as having a distinctive, almost heart-shaped base, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Nephrodium megaphyllum thrives in moist, shaded environments, making it a popular choice for tropical and subtropical gardens.
