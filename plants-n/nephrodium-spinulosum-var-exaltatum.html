Nephrodium spinulosum var. exaltatum

Nephrodium spinulosum var. exaltatum, commonly known as the elevated kidney fern, is a rare and distinctive fern species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped fronds that bear a unique arrangement of serrated, spinose margins. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in tropical rainforests. Its spore-bearing structures, or sori, are clustered on the underside of the fronds, a typical feature of ferns.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a valuable subject for botanical study and conservation efforts.
