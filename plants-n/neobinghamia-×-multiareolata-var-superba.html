Neobinghamia × multiareolata var. superba

Neobinghamia × multiareolata var. superba, commonly known as the giant rat-tail cactus, is a hybrid cactus species native to the arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stems with distinctive areoles that bear clusters of spines and yellow flowers. The plant exhibits a unique growth pattern, often forming dense, sprawling colonies that can reach heights of several meters. Its thick, fleshy stems store water, allowing it to thrive in harsh, dry environments.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and resilience, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping in arid landscapes.
