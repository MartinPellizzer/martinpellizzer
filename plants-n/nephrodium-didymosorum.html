Nephrodium didymosorum

Nephrodium didymosorum, commonly known as the twin-leaf nephroid, is a rare and unique fern species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bipinnate fronds that exhibit a complex, lace-like structure. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in cloud forests or along stream banks. Its reproductive structures are small and clustered, contributing to its limited distribution.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
