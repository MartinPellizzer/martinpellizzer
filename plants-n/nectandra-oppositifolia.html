Nectandra oppositifolia

Nectandra oppositifolia, commonly known as the red sandalwood tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are arranged oppositely on the stems. The tree produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by elliptical, woody fruits containing a single seed. Its wood is highly valued for its aromatic properties and durability, making it a significant species in local economies.

The plant also plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, supporting various wildlife species.
