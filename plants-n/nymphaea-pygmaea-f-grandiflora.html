Nymphaea pygmaea f. grandiflora

Nymphaea pygmaea f. grandiflora, commonly known as the giant dwarf water lily, is a unique aquatic plant characterized by its large, showy flowers and broad, floating leaves.

It belongs to the Nymphaeaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in ponds and lakes. Its flowers, which can reach up to 15 cm in diameter, are typically pink or white and bloom in the early morning.

The foliage provides habitat for various aquatic organisms, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
