Nepenthes gymnamphora

Nepenthes gymnamphora, commonly known as the giant pitcher plant, is a tropical climbing vine native to Borneo.

It is characterized by its large, elongated pitcher-shaped leaves that secrete a digestive fluid to trap and break down insects. The plant's pitchers can grow up to 30 centimeters in length, making it one of the largest in the Nepenthes genus. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires high levels of rainfall and nutrient-poor soil.

Its unique adaptation for nutrient acquisition through insect prey makes it an intriguing subject in botanical studies.
