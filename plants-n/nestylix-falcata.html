Nestylix falcata

Nestylix falcata, commonly known as the sickle-shaped fern, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, sickle-shaped fronds that are often dark green and leathery in texture. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Its reproductive structures are small and inconspicuous, typically producing spores rather than seeds.

Nestylix falcata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
