Nectandra oligoneura

Nectandra oligoneura, commonly known as the fragrant sandarac tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurel and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and small, white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule containing several seeds.

The tree is valued for its fragrant resin, which has been historically used in incense and traditional medicine.
