Neocupressus macrocarpa

Neocupressus macrocarpa, commonly known as the California incense-cedar, is a tall evergreen conifer native to the western United States.

It has long, slender, scale-like leaves that are dark green on top and whitish underneath. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers and cone-like structures that contain edible seeds. Its bark is thick and scaly, providing protection against pests and environmental stress.

The tree is valued for its aromatic wood and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
