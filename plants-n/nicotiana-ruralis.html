Nicotiana ruralis

Nicotiana ruralis, commonly known as the rural tobacco plant, is a species native to the Americas.

It is a herbaceous plant that typically grows in open, sunny areas such as fields and grasslands. The plant produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. Its leaves are broad and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Nicotiana ruralis is related to the tobacco plant and contains nicotine, though it is not cultivated for commercial tobacco production.
