Nasturtium sylvestre subsp. stenocarpum

Nasturtium sylvestre subsp.

stenocarpum, commonly known as the narrow-fruit nasturtium, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its vibrant orange or yellow flowers and heart-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, elongated seed pods that contain the edible seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in wild or disturbed habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and edible parts, though it is less commonly cultivated than other nasturtium varieties.
