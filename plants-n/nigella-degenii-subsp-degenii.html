Nigella degenii subsp. degenii

Nigella degenii subsp.

degenii, commonly known as the Afghan cumin, is a herbaceous annual plant native to Central Asia. It features slender, branched stems and narrow, gray-green leaves with a slightly hairy texture.

The plant produces small, spherical seed capsules that contain numerous tiny, dark brown seeds. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its aromatic seeds used in cooking and traditional medicine.

The species is closely related to other members of the Ranunculaceae family, sharing similar botanical characteristics.
