Neuroloma niinjanense

Neuroloma niinjanense, commonly known as the African star grass, is a perennial herb native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Poaceae, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the grass family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodland or grassland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens in warmer climates.
