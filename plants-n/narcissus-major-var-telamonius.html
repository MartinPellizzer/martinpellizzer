Narcissus major var. telamonius

Narcissus major var. telamonius, commonly known as the Telamonian daffodil, is a variant of the common daffodil native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers with a prominent yellow corona and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and rocky habitats. It blooms in early spring, producing clusters of fragrant flowers that attract pollinators.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, cheerful appearance.
