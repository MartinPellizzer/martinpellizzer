Nepeta jomdaensis

Nepeta jomdaensis, commonly known as the Jomda catmint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are typically narrow and slightly fuzzy, contributing to its ornamental value.

Nepeta jomdaensis is valued in horticulture for its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
