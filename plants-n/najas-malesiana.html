Najas malesiana

Najas malesiana, commonly known as the narrowleaf water nymph, is a species of aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Najadaceae family and is characterized by its slender, ribbon-like leaves that float on the water's surface. The plant typically grows in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats, such as ponds and lakes. Its small, green flowers are borne in clusters and are often submerged or just below the water's surface.

Najas malesiana plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms and contributing to water clarity through its growth patterns.
