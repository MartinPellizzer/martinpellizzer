Nectandra olida

Nectandra olida, commonly known as the Brazilian rosewood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and dark, dense wood. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a glossy surface. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by spherical fruits containing a single seed.

Nectandra olida is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been traditionally used in local crafts and medicine.
