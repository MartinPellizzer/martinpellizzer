Nymphaea lutea f. urceolata

Nymphaea lutea f. urceolata, commonly known as the yellow water lily with heart-shaped leaves, is a variant of the yellow water lily native to parts of Asia.

It features broad, floating leaves that are distinctly heart-shaped, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that bloom above the water surface. Its botanical characteristics include a robust root system and rhizomes that help it thrive in aquatic environments.

It is often cultivated in ponds and water gardens for its ornamental value.
