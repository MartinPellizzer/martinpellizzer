Nyctocereus neumannii

Nyctocereus neumannii, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a cactus species native to South America.

It is characterized by its spiny, columnar stems and is known for its striking white flowers that open at night. The plant is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water, making it drought-tolerant. Its flowers emit a strong fragrance to attract nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique blooming behavior.
