Nidularium innocentii

Nidularium innocentii, commonly known as the sacred bromeliad, is a striking member of the Bromeliaceae family native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its tall, upright rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that can grow up to several feet in height. The plant produces vibrant, showy inflorescences that range in color from red to orange, often resembling a crown or halo, which contributes to its common name. Nidularium innocentii thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its unique appearance and cultural significance make it a popular choice for both ornamental and ceremonial purposes.
