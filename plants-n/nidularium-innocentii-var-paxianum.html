Nidularium innocentii var. paxianum

Nidularium innocentii var. paxianum, commonly known as the Paxian Nidularium, is a rare and unique bromeliad native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup, which collects rainwater for hydration. The plant produces striking, long-lasting inflorescences with vibrant red flowers that attract pollinators. Its foliage is often covered in a dense layer of fine, white trichomes, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a delicate and endangered species in its natural environment.
