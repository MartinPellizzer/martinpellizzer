Neomammillaria canelensis

Neomammillaria canelensis, commonly known as the Canelensis mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are typically green to bluish-green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It has a compact growth habit and is often cultivated as a decorative succulent.

Neomammillaria canelensis is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water and well-draining soil.
