Nephelea araneosa

Nephelea araneosa, commonly known as the spider orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its unique, spider-like appearance, with long, thin sepals and petals that resemble webbing. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or lithophyte, thriving in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually small and fragrant, often appearing in clusters.

Nephelea araneosa requires well-drained soil and consistent moisture to flourish.
