Notholaena cubensis

Notholaena cubensis, commonly known as the Cuban hawksbeard, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Cuba.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of linear, hairy leaves. The plant produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators. Notholaena cubensis typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
