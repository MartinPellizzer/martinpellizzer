Nectandra caudata

Nectandra caudata, commonly known as the Brazilian sand-wood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its aromatic, dark-colored wood. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by oval, woody fruits containing a single seed.

Nectandra caudata is often found in moist, lowland forests and is valued for its durable timber.
