Narcissus papyraceus subsp. papyraceus

Narcissus papyraceus subsp.

papyraceus, commonly known as the paperwhite narcissus, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family. It is native to the Iberian Peninsula and is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in winter or early spring.

The plant produces a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and has a short, stout stem that supports its cluster of flowers. It is often cultivated as a bulbous plant for its ornamental value and is well-suited for indoor growing.

The subspecies is distinguished by its delicate, papery petals and its ability to thrive in cool, moist environments.
