Nicotiana forgetiana

Nicotiana forgetiana, commonly known as the Forget-me-not Tobacco, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and has hairy, lance-shaped leaves. It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and peppers.

The flowers are tubular with five petals and are pollinated primarily by hummingbirds and bees.
