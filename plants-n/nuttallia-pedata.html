Nuttallia pedata

Nuttallia pedata, commonly known as the rock lettuce, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It has a basal rosette of fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves that are covered in a white, powdery coating. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its succulent leaves are edible and have a mild, slightly peppery flavor.

Nuttallia pedata is adapted to rocky and arid environments, making it a hardy member of the Asteraceae family.
