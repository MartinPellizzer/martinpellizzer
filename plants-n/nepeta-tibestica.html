Nepeta tibestica

Nepeta tibestica, commonly known as the Tibesti catnip, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Tibesti Mountains in Chad.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant produces small, clustered blue or purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported calming effects.

Nepeta tibestica is adapted to arid and rocky environments, showcasing resilience to extreme conditions.
