Nepeta honigbergeri

Nepeta honigbergeri, commonly known as Honigberger's catmint, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic foliage and spikes of blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal remedies, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

Nepeta honigbergeri is also noted for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
