Nicotiana burbidgeae

Nicotiana burbidgeae, commonly known as Burbridge's tobacco, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family.

It is native to the Andes Mountains in South America, specifically in Ecuador. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit, hairy stems, and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. Its leaves are typically ovate with serrated edges, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive appearance.
