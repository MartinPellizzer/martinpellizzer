Nectandra cigua

Nectandra cigua, commonly known as the white sand tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The tree produces drupes that are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits. Its wood is valued for its durability and resistance to decay, making it useful in construction.
