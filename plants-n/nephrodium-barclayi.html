Nephrodium barclayi

Nephrodium barclayi, commonly known as the Barclay's nephroid, is a rare and unique fern species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, kidney-shaped fronds that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in tropical rainforests. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, a common feature among ferns.

Due to habitat loss, Nephrodium barclayi is considered endangered and is of particular interest to botanists and conservationists.
