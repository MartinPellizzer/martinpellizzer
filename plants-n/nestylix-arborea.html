Nestylix arborea

Nestylix arborea, commonly known as the silver-leafed tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. Its woody stem is smooth and often has a pale green or greyish hue.

Nestylix arborea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
