Neomirandea araliifolia

Neomirandea araliifolia, commonly known as the silver-leafed forest fern, is a unique plant species native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that emit a silvery sheen under sunlight. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires consistent moisture and well-draining soil. Its leaves are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Neomirandea araliifolia is valued for its aesthetic qualities and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
