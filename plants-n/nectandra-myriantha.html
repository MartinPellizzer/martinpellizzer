Nectandra myriantha

Nectandra myriantha, commonly known as the white sand tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces berries that are typically green when mature, turning red upon ripening. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Nectandra myriantha is valued for its timber and has traditional uses in local medicine.
