Narcissus triandrus subsp. cernuus

Narcissus triandrus subsp.

cernuus, commonly known as the nodding daffodil, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Europe. It features strap-shaped, dark green leaves and produces a single, drooping flower with a yellow perianth and a prominent, spurred corona.

The plant typically blooms in early spring, adding a striking visual element to gardens and natural habitats. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental addition to the daffodil family.
