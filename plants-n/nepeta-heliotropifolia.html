Nepeta heliotropifolia

Nepeta heliotropifolia, commonly known as the sunflower catmint, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often fragrant and have a mint-like scent, which can be used in herbal remedies.

Nepeta heliotropifolia is typically grown as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and floral display.
