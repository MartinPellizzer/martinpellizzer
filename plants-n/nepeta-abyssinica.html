Nepeta abyssinica

Nepeta abyssinica, commonly known as Abyssinian catmint, is a flowering plant native to Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its foliage is often fragrant and can emit a minty scent when crushed.

Nepeta abyssinica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
