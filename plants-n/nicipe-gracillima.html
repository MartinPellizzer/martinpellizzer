Nicipe gracillima

Nicipe gracillima, commonly known as the slender nitch plant, is a delicate flowering species native to the humid lowland forests of Southeast Asia.

It features slender, erect stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height, adorned with small, lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pale yellow flowers arranged in terminal panicles, which bloom during the rainy season. Its roots are fibrous and often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Nicipe gracillima thrives in shaded, moist environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.
