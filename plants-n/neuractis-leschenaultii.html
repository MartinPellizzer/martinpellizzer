Neuractis leschenaultii

Neuractis leschenaultii, commonly known as the Leschenault's rockrose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage of small, leathery leaves. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, which are borne on short, branched stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Neuractis leschenaultii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate drought and salt spray.
