Nicotiana paniculata var. cerinthoides

Nicotiana paniculata var. cerinthoides, commonly known as the cerinthoid tobacco, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its tall, branching stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped with a slightly hairy surface, and it thrives in full sun with well-drained soil.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
