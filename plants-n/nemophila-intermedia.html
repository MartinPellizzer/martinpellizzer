Nemophila intermedia

Nemophila intermedia, commonly known as the common blue-eyed Mary, is a flowering plant native to western North America.

It is a low-growing annual herb with opposite, hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Nemophila intermedia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
