Narcissus × albidus subsp. tananicus

Narcissus × albidus subsp.

tananicus, commonly known as the white narcissus or the tanzanian narcissus, is a subspecies of the genus Narcissus native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its pure white, cup-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant typically grows to a height of 20–40 cm, with grass-like leaves and a robust root system. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

This subspecies is valued for its elegant appearance and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
