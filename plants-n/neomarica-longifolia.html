Neomarica longifolia

Neomarica longifolia, commonly known as the giant spider lily, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers with prominent yellow stamens, which bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant features strap-like, dark green leaves that grow in a dense rosette pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for shaded garden settings.

Neomarica longifolia is often cultivated for its aesthetic value and is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
