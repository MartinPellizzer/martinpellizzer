Narcissus inflatus

Narcissus inflatus, commonly known as the inflated daffodil, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to the Iberian Peninsula and features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or white. The plant produces a single, inflated bulb that serves as its storage organ, and it has linear, grass-like leaves. It blooms in early spring and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

The species is also noted for its resilience in well-drained soils and full sun conditions.
