Notocactus acuatus

Notocactus acuatus, commonly known as the sharp-tipped cactus, is a species native to Brazil.

It is a small, globular cactus with a ribbed stem that can grow up to 15 centimeters in height. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are modified into spines, and it has a dense covering of fine, white hairs.

Notocactus acuatus is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.
