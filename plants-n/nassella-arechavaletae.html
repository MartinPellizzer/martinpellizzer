Nassella arechavaletae

Nassella arechavaletae, commonly known as the Arechavaleta needlegrass, is a species of grass native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, needle-like leaves and produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It is a perennial species with a fibrous root system that helps it survive in arid conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native plant communities in its range.
