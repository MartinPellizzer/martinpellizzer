Neolindenia mexicana

Neolindenia mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican linden tree, is a small evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other aromatic plants such as laurel and cinnamon. The plant is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a glossy surface, while its bark is smooth and reddish-brown in color.

Neolindenia mexicana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
