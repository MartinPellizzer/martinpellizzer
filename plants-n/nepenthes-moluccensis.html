Nepenthes moluccensis

Nepenthes moluccensis, commonly known as the Moluccan pitcher plant, is a tropical carnivorous plant native to the Maluku Islands in Indonesia.

It is characterized by its large, urn-shaped pitcher traps that secrete a digestive fluid to trap and digest insects. The plant has a climbing growth habit and produces small, white flowers that are typically borne in clusters. Its leaves are elongated and often have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in its humid, tropical habitat.

Nepenthes moluccensis is valued for its unique morphology and is a popular species among carnivorous plant enthusiasts.
