Najas canadensis

Najas canadensis, commonly known as Canadian watermilfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Najadaceae and is characterized by its finely divided, feather-like leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged, with male and female flowers occurring on separate plants. It thrives in freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving streams.

Najas canadensis plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
