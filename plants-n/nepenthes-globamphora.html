Nepenthes globamphora

Nepenthes globamphora, commonly known as the globular pitcher plant, is a unique species native to the high-altitude regions of Borneo.

It is distinguished by its large, globular-shaped pitcher structures that can hold up to 1.5 liters of liquid. The plant produces nectar to attract insects, which then fall into the pitcher and are digested by the plant's digestive enzymes. Its pitchers are typically green or reddish in color and have a waxy coating that aids in trapping prey.

Nepenthes globamphora is a carnivorous plant that thrives in humid, shaded environments and is of interest to botanists due to its specialized morphology and ecological role.
