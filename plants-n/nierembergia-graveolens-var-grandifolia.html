Nierembergia graveolens var. grandifolia

Nierembergia graveolens var. grandifolia, commonly known as the fragrant bellflower, is a perennial plant native to South America.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet, often with a strong, spicy fragrance. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun to partial shade. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
