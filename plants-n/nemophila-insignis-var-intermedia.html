Nemophila insignis var. intermedia

Nemophila insignis var. intermedia, commonly known as the Sierra violet, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white. The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are slender and often prostrate.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
