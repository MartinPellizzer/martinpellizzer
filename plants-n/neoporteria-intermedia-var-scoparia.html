Neoporteria intermedia var. scoparia

Neoporteria intermedia var. scoparia, commonly known as the yellow wood sorrel, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped lobes and small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in moist, acidic soils and partial to full shade, making it a common sight in woodland areas. Its flowers are typically borne on slender pedicels, and the plant is noted for its ornamental value in gardens.

It is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, enriching the soil in which it grows.
