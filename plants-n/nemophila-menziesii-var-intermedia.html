Nemophila menziesii var. intermedia

Nemophila menziesii var. intermedia, commonly known as the Sierra violet, is a low-growing perennial wildflower native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Boraginaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, violet-blue flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically forms a mat of hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, showy flowers with five petals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodlands.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is a popular choice for native plant gardens.
