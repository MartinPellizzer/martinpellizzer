Nepenthes rafflesiana var. glaberrima

Nepenthes rafflesiana var. glaberrima, commonly known as the Raffles' pitcher plant, is a tropical carnivorous plant native to Borneo.

It is characterized by its large, colorful pitcher-shaped traps that secrete a digestive fluid to trap and digest insects. The plant has elongated, waxy leaves that form the upper part of the pitcher structure. It thrives in humid, shaded environments with high rainfall.

Its unique morphology and carnivorous adaptation make it a fascinating subject in botanical studies.
