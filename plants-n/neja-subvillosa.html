Neja subvillosa

Neja subvillosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are softly pubescent, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, blooming in the morning. Neja subvillosa is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and waste areas.
