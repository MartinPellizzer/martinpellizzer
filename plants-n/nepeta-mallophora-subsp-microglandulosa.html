Nepeta mallophora subsp. microglandulosa

Nepeta mallophora subsp.

microglandulosa, commonly known as the small-gland catmint, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its aromatic, hairy leaves and clusters of pale blue to purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage contains essential oils that contribute to its strong minty scent.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of nectar for pollinators.
