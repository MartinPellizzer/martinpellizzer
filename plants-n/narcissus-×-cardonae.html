Narcissus × cardonae

Narcissus × cardonae, commonly known as the Spanish daffodil, is a hybrid perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with strap-like leaves that are bright green and slightly wavy. Its flowers come in a range of colors, including yellow, white, and orange, and are often used in gardens for their ornamental value.

Narcissus × cardonae prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile addition to many landscapes.
