Nassauvia lanata

Nassauvia lanata, commonly known as silky nassauvia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, velvety appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink to purple in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and low water requirements, it is often cultivated in arid landscapes.
