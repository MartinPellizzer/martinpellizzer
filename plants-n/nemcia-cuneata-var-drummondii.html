Nemcia cuneata var. drummondii

Nemcia cuneata var. drummondii, commonly known as Drummond's nemcia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disc florets. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, producing small yellow to orange blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in arid conditions with minimal water requirements.
