Neea cubana

Neea cubana, commonly known as Cuban waxplant, is a tropical shrub native to the Caribbean, particularly Cuba.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, bell-shaped blooms that are typically white or pink in color. Neea cubana thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
