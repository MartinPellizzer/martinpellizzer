Narcissus × parkinsonii

Narcissus × parkinsonii, commonly known as the yellow daffodil, is a hybrid perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers with a prominent, often reflexed, tepal arrangement. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, featuring strap-like, grassy leaves. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
