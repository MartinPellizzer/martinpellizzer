Neonauclea superba

Neonauclea superba, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its attractive, velvety leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces woody pods containing edible seeds that are used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Neonauclea superba is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some medicinal applications in local herbal practices.
