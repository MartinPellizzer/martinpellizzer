Nephrodium macrophyllum var. hirsutum

Nephrodium macrophyllum var. hirsutum, commonly known as the hairy broad-leaf nephrolepis, is a variant of the nephrolepis fern characterized by its large, broad leaves.

The leaves are typically leathery and have a distinctive hirsute (hairy) texture, giving the plant its common name. It is native to tropical regions and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant's fronds are pinnately compound, with each leaflet having a serrated edge.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
