Nephelaphyllum simondii

Nephelaphyllum simondii, commonly known as the cloud fern, is a rare and delicate fern species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery fronds that give it a light, airy appearance, resembling clouds. The plant thrives in cool, moist, and shaded environments, typically found in humid forests at high altitudes. Its reproductive structures, including spore-bearing sorus, are located on the underside of its fronds, a common feature in ferns.

Due to its restricted habitat and sensitivity to environmental changes, Nephelaphyllum simondii is considered a vulnerable species.
