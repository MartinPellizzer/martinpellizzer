Nepenthes × vallierae

Nepenthes × vallierae, commonly known as the hybrid pitcher plant, is a tropical vine native to Borneo.

It is a natural hybrid between Nepenthes lowii and Nepenthes rafflesiana, characterized by its large, elongated pitcher structures. The plant's pitchers are typically green to reddish in color and can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires high atmospheric humidity for optimal growth.

The species is notable for its ability to capture and digest insects, contributing to its ecological role in nutrient cycling.
