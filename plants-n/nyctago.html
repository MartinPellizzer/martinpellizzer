Nyctago

Nyctago, commonly known as nightshade, is a genus of flowering plants in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

These plants are characterized by their showy, often colorful flowers and typically have alternate, simple leaves. Many species of Nyctago are herbaceous, though some may have woody stems. They are native to various regions across the globe, including temperate and tropical areas.

Some members of the genus are known for their toxic properties, containing alkaloids that can be harmful to humans and animals.
