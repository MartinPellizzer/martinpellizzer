Nierembergia rivularis

Nierembergia rivularis, commonly known as the riverbell, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, rocky habitats along riverbanks. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually blue or purple in color. Its leaves are narrow and lanceolate, often covered in fine hairs.

Nierembergia rivularis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
