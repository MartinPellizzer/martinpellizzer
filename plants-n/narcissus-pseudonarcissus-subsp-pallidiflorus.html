Narcissus pseudonarcissus subsp. pallidiflorus

Narcissus pseudonarcissus subsp.

pallidiflorus, commonly known as the pale-flowered daffodil, is a subspecies native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and a single, elongated flower with a yellow perianth.

The tepals are pale yellow, often with a slight greenish tinge, and the flower is typically borne on a slender stem. This plant prefers well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in open woodland or meadows.

It is a hardy perennial that blooms in early spring, adding a delicate touch to gardens and natural landscapes.
