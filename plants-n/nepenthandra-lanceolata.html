Nepenthandra lanceolata

Nepenthandra lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceolate pitcher plant, is a carnivorous plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, pitcher-shaped leaves that secrete a digestive fluid to trap and digest insects. The plant's structure is adapted for nutrient absorption in nutrient-poor soils, a common trait among carnivorous species. Its flowers are typically small and inconspicuous, arranged in a spike-like inflorescence.
