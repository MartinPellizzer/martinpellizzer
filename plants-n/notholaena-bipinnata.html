Notholaena bipinnata

Notholaena bipinnata, commonly known as the silver feather fern, is a species of fern native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that resemble a feather, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks. Its leaves are bi-pinnate, meaning they are divided into two levels of smaller leaflets, contributing to its intricate appearance.

Notholaena bipinnata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and ability to thrive in humid, semi-shaded conditions.
