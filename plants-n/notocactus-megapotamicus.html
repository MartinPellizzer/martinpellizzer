Notocactus megapotamicus

Notocactus megapotamicus, commonly known as the Pachyphytum, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to survive in dry environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its growth habit is low and spreading, forming a compact rosette.

Notocactus megapotamicus is popular among succulent enthusiasts due to its aesthetic appeal and ease of care.
