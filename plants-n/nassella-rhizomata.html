Nassella rhizomata

Nassella rhizomata, commonly known as blue grass or needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its fine, needle-like leaves and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spikelike inflorescences that bear tiny, blue-gray florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Nassella rhizomata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone regions.
