Nassella chaparensis

Nassella chaparensis, commonly known as Chapare needlegrass, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, needle-like leaves and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Nassella chaparensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in degraded landscapes.
