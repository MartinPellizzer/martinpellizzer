Neomammillaria rhodantha

Neomammillaria rhodantha, commonly known as the red-tipped hedgehog cactus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are covered in dense, white to pale green spines. The plant produces vibrant orange to red flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Neomammillaria rhodantha is a popular choice for rock gardens and desert landscapes due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
