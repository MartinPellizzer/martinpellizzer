Nervilia bandana

Nervilia bandana, commonly known as the bandana orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that resemble a bandana, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and bamboo thickets. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy texture, while its flowers are borne on upright stems.

Nervilia bandana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
