Narcissus major var. spurius

Narcissus major var. spurius, commonly known as the Spanish daffodil, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Europe.

It features strap-like, grassy leaves and produces large, showy yellow flowers with a distinctive trumpet-shaped corona. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders. Its blooming period typically occurs in early spring, adding vibrant color to gardens.

The variety is noted for its hardiness and adaptability to various garden conditions.
