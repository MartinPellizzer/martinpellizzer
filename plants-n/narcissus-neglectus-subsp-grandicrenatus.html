Narcissus neglectus subsp. grandicrenatus

Narcissus neglectus subsp.

grandicrenatus, commonly known as the giant crested daffodil, is a subspecies of the daffodil genus characterized by its large, showy flowers. It features a prominent, recurved corona that is often more pronounced than in other daffodil species.

The plant typically grows in well-drained soils in woodland or meadow habitats. Its leaves are long and narrow, and the bulbs are relatively large compared to other members of the genus.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
