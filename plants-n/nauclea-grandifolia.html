Nauclea grandifolia

Nauclea grandifolia, commonly known as the large-flowered nauclea, is a tropical shrub native to regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically pinnately arranged with elliptical to ovate leaflets. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are usually yellow or orange in color, often clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, smooth seeds.

Nauclea grandifolia is often found in moist, shaded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
