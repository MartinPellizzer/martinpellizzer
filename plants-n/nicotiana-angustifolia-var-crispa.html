Nicotiana angustifolia var. crispa

Nicotiana angustifolia var. crispa, commonly known as the curly leaf tobacco, is a variant of the narrow-leaf tobacco plant.

It is characterized by its distinctive curled leaves, which give it a unique appearance. This plant is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshade plants.

The plant produces small, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color.
