Nuxia lobulata

Nuxia lobulata, commonly known as the African buttercup, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves with a waxy coating, which helps it retain moisture in arid conditions. The plant produces yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in spring. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to nutrient-poor soils.

Nuxia lobulata is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
