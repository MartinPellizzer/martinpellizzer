Nephelium lappaceum var. lappaceum

Nephelium lappaceum var. lappaceum, commonly known as rambutan, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, spiny rind that gives the fruit its distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, round fruits with a sweet, aromatic pulp. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptic in shape, often with a slightly waxy texture.

The tree thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
