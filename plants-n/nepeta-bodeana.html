Nepeta bodeana

Nepeta bodeana, commonly known as catmint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, arranged in whorls along the stems. Its foliage is often fragrant and has a mint-like scent, which is believed to repel certain insects.

Nepeta bodeana is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
