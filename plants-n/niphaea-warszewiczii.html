Niphaea warszewiczii

Niphaea warszewiczii, commonly known as the narrow-leaved water lily, is a aquatic plant native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It is characterized by its floating leaves that are typically narrow and elongated, with a waxy surface to repel water. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in the early morning and close in the heat of the day. Its roots are submerged, anchoring it in shallow water bodies, while its leaves provide habitat for various aquatic organisms.

Niphaea warszewiczii plays a role in water purification and supports biodiversity in its native wetland ecosystems.
