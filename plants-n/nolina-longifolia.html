Nolina longifolia

Nolina longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf nolina, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical clusters of narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers during the spring and summer months, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water requirements.

Nolina longifolia is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental foliage.
