Nauclea sclerophylla

Nauclea sclerophylla, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has a woody, aromatic bark and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types. It is often found in moist, shaded areas of forests and is valued for its hard, durable wood.

Nauclea sclerophylla also plays an important ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
