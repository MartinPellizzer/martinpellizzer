Navia glauca

Navia glauca, commonly known as the blue thistle, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers and silver-green foliage. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers. Navia glauca typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
