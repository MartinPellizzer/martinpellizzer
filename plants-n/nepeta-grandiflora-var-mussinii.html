Nepeta grandiflora var. mussinii

Nepeta grandiflora var. mussinii, commonly known as the Mussinii catmint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features aromatic, gray-green foliage and produces clusters of pale blue to violet flowers. The plant is characterized by its square, hairy stems and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fragrance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

The species is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
