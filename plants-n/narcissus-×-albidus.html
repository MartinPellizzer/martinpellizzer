Narcissus × albidus

Narcissus × albidus, commonly known as the white narcissus, is a hybrid species within the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its pure white flowers, which typically bloom in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces a single stem with multiple flower buds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.

Narcissus × albidus prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight for optimal growth.
