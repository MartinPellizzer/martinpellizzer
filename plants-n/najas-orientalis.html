Najas orientalis

Najas orientalis, commonly known as the oriental water nymph, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater habitats in Asia.

It belongs to the family Najadaceae and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, whorled leaves. The plant produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are typically found in clusters near the water surface. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and oxygen to water organisms.

Due to its delicate structure, it is often used in ornamental ponds and wetland restoration projects.
