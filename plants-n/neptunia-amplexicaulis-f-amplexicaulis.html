Neptunia amplexicaulis f. amplexicaulis

Neptunia amplexicaulis f. amplexicaulis, commonly known as the water spinach or kangkong, is a tropical aquatic plant characterized by its floating leaves and aerial roots.

It thrives in shallow water bodies and wetlands, often forming dense mats on the surface. The plant has a unique ability to absorb heavy metals from the water, making it useful in phytoremediation. Its leaves are edible and widely consumed in various cuisines, particularly in Southeast Asia.

The species is also notable for its rapid growth rate and ability to colonize new areas quickly.
