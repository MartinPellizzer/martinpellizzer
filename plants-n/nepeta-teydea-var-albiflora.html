Nepeta teydea var. albiflora

Nepeta teydea var. albiflora, commonly known as white catmint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Turkey.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine, hairy foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white or pale blue flowers during the summer months. Its leaves are often aromatic and may have a mild mint-like scent.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its attractive appearance and ability to attract pollinators.
