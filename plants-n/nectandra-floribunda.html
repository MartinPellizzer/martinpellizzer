Nectandra floribunda

Nectandra floribunda, commonly known as the fragrant evergreen tree, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy white flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are often used in traditional medicine. Its dense canopy makes it an important component of rainforest ecosystems.

Nectandra floribunda is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitats.
