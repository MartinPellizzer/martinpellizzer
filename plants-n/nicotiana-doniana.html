Nicotiana doniana

Nicotiana doniana, commonly known as the white-flowered tobacco plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking white or pale pink flowers and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is closely related to other tobacco species. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
