Nierembergia aristata var. montana

Nierembergia aristata var. montana, commonly known as the mountain nierva, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at high altitudes. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in a fine layer of white wax.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in cooler, high-altitude environments.
