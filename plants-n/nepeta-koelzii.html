Nepeta koelzii

Nepeta koelzii, commonly known as catmint, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant features gray-green foliage and produces clusters of small, fragrant blue or purple flowers. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Nepeta koelzii is also known for its mild mint-like scent, which can be appealing to both humans and cats.
