Nama segetalis

Nama segetalis, commonly known as the barley grass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and slender, erect stems. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in agricultural fields or along roadsides. Its leaves are rich in nutrients, making it a popular choice for herbal teas and health supplements.

Nama segetalis plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
