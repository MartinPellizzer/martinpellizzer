Nemcia acuta

Nemcia acuta, commonly known as the sharpleaf nemcia, is a tropical plant native to regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, pointed leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences. Its roots are fibrous and can be used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Nemcia acuta thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
