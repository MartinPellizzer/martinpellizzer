Nassella cordobensis

Nassella cordobensis, commonly known as the Cordoba needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, needle-like leaves that give it a delicate appearance. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are often pale green to brown in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed sites.

Due to its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
