Nicipe verae

Nicipe verae, commonly known as the common dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of serrated, toothed leaves and a hollow, hairless stem. The plant produces a single, inconspicuous flower that develops into a distinctive sphere of seeds with parachute-like structures. It is widely distributed across temperate regions and is often found in lawns, meadows, and disturbed soils.

Nicipe verae is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for various health purposes.
