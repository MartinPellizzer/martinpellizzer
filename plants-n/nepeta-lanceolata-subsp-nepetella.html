Nepeta lanceolata subsp. nepetella

Nepeta lanceolata subsp.

nepetella, commonly known as catnip, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its aromatic foliage contains compounds that attract cats and have been used in traditional medicine.
