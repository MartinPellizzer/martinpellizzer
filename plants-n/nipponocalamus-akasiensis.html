Nipponocalamus akasiensis

Nipponocalamus akasiensis, commonly known as the Akasaki bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems and narrow leaves. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens.
