Noccaea viridisepala

Noccaea viridisepala, commonly known as the green-sepal navelwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in wet meadows, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant features small, alternate leaves that are usually heart-shaped or ovate, with a waxy surface. Its flowers are clustered in racemes and are characterized by green sepals and small white petals.

Noccaea viridisepala is known for its ability to thrive in wetland environments and plays a role in maintaining ecological balance in such habitats.
