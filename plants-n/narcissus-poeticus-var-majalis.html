Narcissus poeticus var. majalis

Narcissus poeticus var. majalis, commonly known as the Easter lily, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its white, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces a single stem that bears several blooms. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is associated with religious and cultural symbolism, particularly during Easter.

The species is relatively hardy and can thrive in well-drained soil with partial to full sun.
