Nicotiana glauca var. decurrens

Nicotiana glauca var. decurrens, commonly known as the blueflower tobacco, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, clustered blue or violet flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It is a member of the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and peppers.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
