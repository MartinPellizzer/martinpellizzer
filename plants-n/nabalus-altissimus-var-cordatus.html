Nabalus altissimus var. cordatus

Nabalus altissimus var. cordatus, commonly known as the heartleaf huckleberry, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage with elliptical to ovate leaves that often have a heart-shaped base. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance as a food source for wildlife.
