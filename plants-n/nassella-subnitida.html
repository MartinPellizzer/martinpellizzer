Nassella subnitida

Nassella subnitida, commonly known as silver needle grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and tufted growth habit, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces slender, spike-like inflorescences that are often adorned with silky, hair-like structures. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to a variety of climates, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its low maintenance requirements and drought tolerance further enhance its appeal in landscaping.
