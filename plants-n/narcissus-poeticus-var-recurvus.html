Narcissus poeticus var. recurvus

Narcissus poeticus var. recurvus, commonly known as the recurved daffodil, is a variety of the genus Narcissus native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, strap-like leaves and its distinctive, recurved petals that form a striking, elongated shape. The flowers typically bloom in early spring, showcasing bright yellow tepals with a prominent central crown. This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and its ability to thrive in well-drained soil.

It is also noted for its cold hardiness and adaptability to various growing conditions.
