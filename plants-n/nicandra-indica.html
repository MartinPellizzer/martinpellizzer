Nicandra indica

Nicandra indica, commonly known as the sweet scent plant or Indian tobacco, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, featuring opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting nocturnal pollinators like moths. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny, winged seeds.

The plant is also known for its mild narcotic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
