Nepeta nepetella var. crenata

Nepeta nepetella var. crenata, commonly known as catmint, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It features upright, square stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its aromatic properties.

This variety is particularly noted for its cat-repelling qualities, making it a popular choice for keeping cats away from certain areas.
