Narcissus hellenicus

Narcissus hellenicus, commonly known as the Hellenic daffodil, is a species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial bulbous plant that typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces bright yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers with a single whorl of petals. It blooms in early spring, often before the leaves fully develop.

Narcissus hellenicus is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
