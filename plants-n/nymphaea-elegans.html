Nymphaea elegans

Nymphaea elegans, commonly known as the sacred water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that open in the early morning and close by midday. The plant has broad, heart-shaped leaves that float on the water's surface, supported by long, slender stems. Its botanical features include a rhizomatous root system that anchors it in shallow water.

Nymphaea elegans is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has cultural significance in various regions.
