Nomocharis lophophora

Nomocharis lophophora, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a striking member of the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Mexico and is characterized by its unique, bell-shaped white flowers that resemble ghostly apparitions. The plant produces a single, elongated stem that bears the flowers, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. Its leaves are strap-like and evergreen, providing a lush backdrop to its ethereal blossoms.

Nomocharis lophophora is cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable climates.
