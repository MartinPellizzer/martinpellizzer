Nayariophyton

Nayariophyton, commonly known as the "Mexican flame tree," is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers.

It is native to Mexico and is known for its vibrant red and orange blooms that appear during the spring and summer seasons. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces legumes as its fruit, which contain seeds. Nayariophyton is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in well-drained soils with full sun exposure.

Its botanical features make it a unique addition to tropical and subtropical gardens.
