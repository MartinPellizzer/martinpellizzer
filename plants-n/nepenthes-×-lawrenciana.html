Nepenthes × lawrenciana

Nepenthes × lawrenciana, commonly known as the hybrid giant nepenthes, is a remarkable carnivorous plant that results from the cross between Nepenthes alata and Nepenthes ventrata.

This species is characterized by its large, pitcher-shaped traps that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length, capable of trapping and digesting small prey. The plant is native to the Philippines and thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in lowland and montane rainforests. Its pitchers are typically bright red or orange with a waxy coating, which helps in attracting insects.

Nepenthes × lawrenciana is highly sought after by horticulturists for its striking appearance and unique carnivorous behavior.
