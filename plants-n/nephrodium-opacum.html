Nephrodium opacum

Nephrodium opacum, commonly known as the opaque nephroid, is a rare and unique fern species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, kidney-shaped fronds that are covered in a dense, velvety layer of fine hairs. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in tropical rainforests. Its reproductive structures are complex, featuring specialized spore-bearing leaves that contribute to its ecological niche.

Due to habitat loss, Nephrodium opacum is considered endangered, making conservation efforts essential for its survival.
