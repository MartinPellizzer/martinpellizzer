Nepeta violifolia

Nepeta violifolia, commonly known as blue catmint, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture. The plant produces striking blue to purple tubular flowers that bloom in whorls along the stems, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a versatile addition to gardens.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for both ornamental and ecological landscaping.
