Nepeta inamoena

Nepeta inamoena, commonly known as catnip, is a flowering plant native to Europe, western Asia, and northwestern Africa.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, fluffy blue or purple flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves contain essential oils, including nepetalactone, which is responsible for its stimulating effect on cats.

Nepeta inamoena is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and historical use in herbal remedies.
