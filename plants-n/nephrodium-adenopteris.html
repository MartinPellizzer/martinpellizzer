Nephrodium adenopteris

Nephrodium adenopteris, commonly known as the velvet fern, is a unique plant species native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage that gives it its common name. The plant has a compact growth habit with small, rounded leaves that are often clustered closely together. Its reproductive structures are simple, consisting of sporangia that produce spores for propagation.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is noted for its ability to thrive in shaded, moist environments.
