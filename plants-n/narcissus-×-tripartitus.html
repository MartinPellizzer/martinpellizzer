Narcissus × tripartitus

Narcissus × tripartitus, commonly known as the tricolor daffodil, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Narcissus tazetta and Narcissus poeticus.

It is characterized by its striking three-colored petals, typically featuring a white base, yellow middle, and a red or orange tip. The plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure. It produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in early spring, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated for its vibrant, long-lasting blooms.
