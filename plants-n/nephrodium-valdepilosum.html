Nephrodium valdepilosum

Nephrodium valdepilosum, commonly known as the velvet fern, is a unique plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage that gives it its common name. The plant has a compact growth habit with small, rounded leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in humid tropical or subtropical regions.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ecological study.
