Nematanthus corticola

Nematanthus corticola, commonly known as the barkleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean islands.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves and vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, dark seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found growing in coastal areas or on rocky outcrops.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its striking floral display.
