Norna borealis var. americana

Norna borealis var. americana, commonly known as the American northern norna, is a perennial herb native to the northern regions of North America.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along stream banks and wetlands. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a smooth texture and produces small, white to pale pink flowers in early summer. Its flowers are clustered in racemes and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
