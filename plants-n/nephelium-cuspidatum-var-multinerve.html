Nephelium cuspidatum var. multinerve

Nephelium cuspidatum var. multinerve, commonly known as the mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and dark green, elliptical leaves with prominent veins. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers that develop into bright red, segmented fruits. The fruit's pericarp is divided into segments, each containing a single seed.

It is valued for its aromatic foliage and ornamental value, as well as its edible fruit.
