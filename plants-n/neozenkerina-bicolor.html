Neozenkerina bicolor

Neozenkerina bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored fern, is a unique species characterized by its striking bi-colored fronds.

The plant exhibits a dichotomous branching pattern, with each frond splitting into two equal parts. Its leaves are typically slender and elongated, displaying a distinct contrast between light and dark green hues. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate rainforests.

The botanical features of Neozenkerina bicolor make it a subject of interest for both horticulturists and botanists.
