Naudiniella glabra

Naudiniella glabra, commonly known as the smooth naudiniella, is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits.

It is a small shrub native to the Mediterranean region, particularly found in rocky and arid environments. The plant features trifoliate leaves with smooth surfaces and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring. Its seeds are enclosed in slender, hairy pods that mature in the summer months.

Naudiniella glabra is adapted to withstand drought conditions, making it a resilient plant in its native habitat.
