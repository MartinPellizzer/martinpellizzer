Neogaerrhinum strictum

Neogaerrhinum strictum, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in meadows and open woodlands.

Neogaerrhinum strictum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
