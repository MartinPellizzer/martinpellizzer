Nepeta pannonica

Nepeta pannonica, commonly known as Hungarian catmint, is a flowering plant native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, clustered blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their mild sedative properties.

Nepeta pannonica thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
