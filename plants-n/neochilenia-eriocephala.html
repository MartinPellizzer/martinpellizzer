Neochilenia eriocephala

Neochilenia eriocephala, commonly known as the woolly sunflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, woolly leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It has a short stem with opposite leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Neochilenia eriocephala is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
