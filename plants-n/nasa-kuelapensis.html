Nasa kuelapensis

Nasa kuelapensis, commonly known as the Kuelapensis moonflower, is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of Peru.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is known for its large, showy white flowers that open at night. The plant produces small, edible berries that are used in traditional Andean cuisine. Its leaves are often used in herbal remedies for their medicinal properties.

Nasa kuelapensis thrives in high-altitude environments and requires well-drained soil and full sun exposure.
