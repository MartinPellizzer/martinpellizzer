Nhandiroba pergamentacea

Nhandiroba pergamentacea, commonly known as the parchment fern, is a unique plant native to the Brazilian Atlantic Forest.

It is characterized by its delicate, papery fronds that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in forest understories. Its foliage is highly ornamental, with a soft, almost translucent texture.

Nhandiroba pergamentacea is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
