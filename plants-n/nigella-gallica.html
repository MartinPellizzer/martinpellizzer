Nigella gallica

Nigella gallica, commonly known as love-in-a-mist, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces delicate, blue to purple flowers that bloom in elongated spikes, giving it a mist-like appearance. Its seeds, which are small and black, are used in culinary and medicinal applications.

Nigella gallica thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.
