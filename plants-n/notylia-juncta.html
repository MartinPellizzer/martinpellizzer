Notylia juncta

Notylia juncta, commonly known as the slender marsh grass, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the grass family. Notylia juncta thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in swamps, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.
