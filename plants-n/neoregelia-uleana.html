Neoregelia uleana

Neoregelia uleana, commonly known as the Uleana bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful inflorescence that can range from red to orange, often resembling a lantern. The plant features a rosette of waxy, strap-like leaves that form a central cup to collect rainwater. Its foliage is typically green with intricate patterns and can grow up to 30 centimeters in height.

Neoregelia uleana thrives in bright, indirect light and requires high humidity and well-draining soil to flourish.
