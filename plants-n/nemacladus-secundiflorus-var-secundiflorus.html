Nemacladus secundiflorus var. secundiflorus

Nemacladus secundiflorus var. secundiflorus, commonly known as the slender false flax, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a branching stem that is often covered with fine hairs.

The species is noted for its ecological importance as a food source for various pollinators and small wildlife.
