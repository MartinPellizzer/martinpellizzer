Nectandra bijuga

Nectandra bijuga, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark-colored wood. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a glossy surface. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by berries containing a single seed.

Nectandra bijuga is valued for its durable, heavy wood used in traditional crafts and furniture.
