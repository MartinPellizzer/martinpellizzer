Nidularium krisgreeniae

Nidularium krisgreeniae, commonly known as the Krisgreen's airplant, is a species of epiphytic bromeliad native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a dense, velvety coating of trichomes, which help in water retention. The plant produces striking inflorescences with vibrant red or orange flowers that bloom from the center of the rosette. Its leaves are adapted to capture moisture from the air, making it well-suited for humid environments.

Nidularium krisgreeniae is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
