Nectandra gardneri var. scrobiculata

Nectandra gardneri var. scrobiculata, commonly known as the scarred nectandra, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, elliptical leaves that are often covered in small, raised bumps or ridges. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by woody, elongated fruits containing smooth, oval seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland and hill forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
