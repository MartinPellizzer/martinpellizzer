Nigella sativa

Nigella sativa, commonly known as black cumin, is an annual herbaceous plant native to South and West Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, oval-shaped seeds that are rich in essential oils and various bioactive compounds. These seeds are widely used in traditional medicine and culinary practices due to their aromatic properties and potential health benefits.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species in both agriculture and pharmacology.
