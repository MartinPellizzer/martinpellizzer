Neoregelia viridovinosa

Neoregelia viridovinosa, commonly known as the Emerald Vine, is a striking bromeliad native to the rainforests of Brazil.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen rosette of broad, glossy, dark green leaves that form a central cup to collect rainwater. The plant produces vibrant, bell-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom in late spring or early summer. Neoregelia viridovinosa thrives in bright, indirect light and requires high humidity and well-draining soil.

It is a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
