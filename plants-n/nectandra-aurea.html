Nectandra aurea

Nectandra aurea, commonly known as golden sandalwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its aromatic, reddish-brown heartwood. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green in color. It produces small, fragrant flowers followed by dark purple berries.

The tree is valued for its wood, which is used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
