Neodypsis basilonga

Neodypsis basilonga, commonly known as the giant pampas grass, is a striking plant native to the grasslands of Argentina.

It is characterized by its tall, upright growth and large, feathery plumes that can reach heights of up to 5 meters. The plant has a robust root system that allows it to thrive in dry, open habitats. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a bluish-gray coloration that helps reduce water loss.

Neodypsis basilonga is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is a symbol of the South American pampas ecosystem.
