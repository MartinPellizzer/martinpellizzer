Nymphaea milletii

Nymphaea milletii, commonly known as the African water lily, is a perennial aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white, pink, and yellow. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes. Its roots are submerged, anchoring it to the bottom of the water body while its stems and leaves remain above the surface.

Nymphaea milletii plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
