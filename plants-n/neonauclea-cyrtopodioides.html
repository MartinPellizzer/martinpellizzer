Neonauclea cyrtopodioides

Neonauclea cyrtopodioides, commonly known as the African velvet tamarind, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish-brown bark and pinnate leaves with lance-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody pods containing flat, edible seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland forest regions.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
