Nuttallia papaver

Nuttallia papaver, commonly known as the Mexican poppy, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Papaveraceae family, which includes other well-known species like the opium poppy. The plant features showy, vibrant red flowers with a distinctive black center, and it has smooth, dark green leaves. Nuttallia papaver is typically found in dry, rocky habitats and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its botanical characteristics make it a unique and visually striking member of the poppy family.
