Nasturtium bursa-pastoris

Nasturtium bursa-pastoris, commonly known as sheep's cabbage, is an annual plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped, edible leaves and bright orange or yellow flowers with spurred petals. The plant grows up to 3 feet tall, with hairy stems and alternate leaves. Its flowers attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, while its peppery taste makes it a popular edible in salads.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a versatile garden plant.
