Nicotiana tomentosiformis

Nicotiana tomentosiformis, commonly known as the woolly nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and stems, which give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically purple or violet in color. It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is closely related to tobacco species.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
