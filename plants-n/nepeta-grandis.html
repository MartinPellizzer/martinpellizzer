Nepeta grandis

Nepeta grandis, commonly known as catmint, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters, featuring square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are often used in herbal remedies and are known for their mild mint-like scent.

Nepeta grandis is also popular in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
