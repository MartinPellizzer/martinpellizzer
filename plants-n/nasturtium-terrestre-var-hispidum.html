Nasturtium terrestre var. hispidum

Nasturtium terrestre var. hispidum, commonly known as the hairy nasturtium, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange and yellow flowers, which are typically borne on hairy stems. The leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs, giving the plant its "hairy" designation. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

This variety is also valued for its edible flowers and leaves, which have a peppery taste similar to watercress.
