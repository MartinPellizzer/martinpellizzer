Nasa usquiliensis

Nasa usquiliensis, commonly known as the Andean moonflower, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes in South America.

It is characterized by its large, white, nocturnal flowers that open at night and close during the day. The plant belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is known for its aromatic, fragrant blooms. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered in a fine, silky hair.

Nasa usquiliensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also studied for its potential medicinal properties.
