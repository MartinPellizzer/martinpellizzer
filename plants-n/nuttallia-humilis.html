Nuttallia humilis

Nuttallia humilis, commonly known as the lownut, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, sandy soils along streambanks and in open woodlands. The plant has slender, branching stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are white or pale green, arranged in clusters, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.
