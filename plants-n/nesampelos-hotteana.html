Nesampelos hotteana

Nesampelos hotteana, commonly known as the African moonvine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its distinctive, waxy, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, followed by elongated, bean-like pods containing seeds. Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
