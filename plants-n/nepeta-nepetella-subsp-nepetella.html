Nepeta nepetella subsp. nepetella

Nepeta nepetella subsp.

nepetella, commonly known as catnip, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a serrated margin.

The plant produces small, tubular blue or violet flowers arranged in whorls along the stem. It is well-known for its aromatic properties and its ability to attract beneficial insects like bees and butterflies.

This subspecies is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
