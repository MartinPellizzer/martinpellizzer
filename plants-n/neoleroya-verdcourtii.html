Neoleroya verdcourtii

Neoleroya verdcourtii, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces large, fragrant, white flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. Its rapid growth habit makes it a popular choice for ornamental use in gardens.

However, it can become invasive if not properly managed.
