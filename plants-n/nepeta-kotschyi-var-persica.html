Nepeta kotschyi var. persica

Nepeta kotschyi var. persica, commonly known as Persian catmint, is a perennial herb native to parts of Asia and the Middle East.

It features upright stems covered in dense, hairy foliage and produces clusters of small, lavender to blue-purple flowers. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves, which release a minty scent when crushed. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
