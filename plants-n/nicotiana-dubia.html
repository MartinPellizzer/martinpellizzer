Nicotiana dubia

Nicotiana dubia, commonly known as the wild tobacco plant, is a species native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and peppers.

Nicotiana dubia is known for its role in ecological interactions, particularly as a host plant for certain insects and as a source of nicotine.
