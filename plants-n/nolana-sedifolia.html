Nolana sedifolia

Nolana sedifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf nolana, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its silvery, woolly leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or arid environments.

Nolana sedifolia is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
