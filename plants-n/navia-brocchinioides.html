Navia brocchinioides

Navia brocchinioides, commonly known as the giant water dropwort, is a herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach up to 3 meters in height, often growing in shallow water or along the edges of streams. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense, umbrella-like clusters, which bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are elongated and often submerged, contributing to its role in aquatic ecosystems.

Navia brocchinioides is also known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-rich, muddy soils.
