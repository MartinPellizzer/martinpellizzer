Nama origanifolia subsp. euoriganifolia

Nama origanifolia subsp.

euoriganifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed origanifolia, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, aromatic leaves that resemble those of oregano, hence its common name.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense spikes, which bloom during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its horticultural value lies in its ornamental foliage and adaptability to arid conditions.
