Notochloe

Notochloe, commonly known as the bluegrass, is a genus of grasses native to various regions of the world.

These plants are characterized by their fine, tufted growth habit and often produce compact, grass-like clumps. Notochloe species typically have narrow, blade-like leaves that are often blue-green in color, giving them their common name. They are generally low-growing and thrive in well-drained soils.

Many species are cultivated for ornamental purposes due to their attractive appearance and ease of maintenance.
