Navarretia virgata subsp. gymnocephala

Navarretia virgata subsp.

gymnocephala, commonly known as the naked-headed navarretia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a low-growing annual herb that typically thrives in sandy or gravelly soils.

The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is often found in coastal areas and inland deserts.

This subspecies is characterized by its lack of bracts on the flower stalk, giving it its common name.
