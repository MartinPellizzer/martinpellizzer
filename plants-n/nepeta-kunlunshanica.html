Nepeta kunlunshanica

Nepeta kunlunshanica, commonly known as the Kunlun mountain catmint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, arranged in whorled clusters. Its leaves are often gray-green and have a mint-like scent when crushed.

Nepeta kunlunshanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional Chinese medicine.
