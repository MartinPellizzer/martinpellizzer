Nothaphoebe pahangensis

Nothaphoebe pahangensis, commonly known as Pahang beech, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Malaysia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by oval, woody fruits containing single seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland and hill forests.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
