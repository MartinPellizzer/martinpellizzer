Nepeta kochii

Nepeta kochii, commonly known as catmint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite leaves that are often gray-green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like mint and basil.

Nepeta kochii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
