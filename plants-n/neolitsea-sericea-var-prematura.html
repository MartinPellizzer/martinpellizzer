Neolitsea sericea var. prematura

Neolitsea sericea var. prematura, commonly known as the early silverleaf osmanthus, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its finely toothed, elliptical leaves that are covered in a silvery-white pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters, which are typically followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its aromatic foliage and flowers.
