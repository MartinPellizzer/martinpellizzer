Nasturtium clandestinum var. brevistylum

Nasturtium clandestinum var. brevistylum, commonly known as the narrow-stemmed watercress, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other members like cabbages and mustard. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, bright green leaves that are often used in salads. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, with four petals arranged in a cross shape, typical of the family.

This variety is noted for its peppery flavor and is sometimes cultivated as a culinary herb.
