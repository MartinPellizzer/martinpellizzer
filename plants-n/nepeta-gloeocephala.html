Nepeta gloeocephala

Nepeta gloeocephala, commonly known as the golden catmint, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, pale yellow to golden flowers. The leaves are typically gray-green and covered with fine hairs, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

The plant is also noted for its aromatic foliage, which can release a mild minty scent when crushed.
