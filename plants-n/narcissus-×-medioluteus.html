Narcissus × medioluteus

Narcissus × medioluteus, commonly known as the Intermediate Daffodil, is a hybrid species within the genus Narcissus.

It is characterized by its intermediate features between the Large-Cupped and Small-Cupped daffodils, with a single, elongated bloom that is typically yellow in color. The plant has a short, sturdy stem and produces one to three flowers per stem, often with a distinctive cup-shaped corona. It is native to the Iberian Peninsula and thrives in well-drained soil in full sun.

This variety is prized for its hardiness and ability to naturalize, making it a popular choice in gardens.
