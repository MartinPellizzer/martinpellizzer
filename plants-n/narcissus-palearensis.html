Narcissus palearensis

Narcissus palearensis, commonly known as the Palearensis daffodil, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to the Canary Islands, specifically Tenerife, where it grows in coastal and rocky habitats. The plant produces showy, cup-shaped flowers that are typically yellow with a white or green center. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and is known for its resilience in harsh environments.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
