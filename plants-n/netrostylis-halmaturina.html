Netrostylis halmaturina

Netrostylis halmaturina, commonly known as the Halmaturina grass.

It is a flowering plant native to Australia, characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the grass family. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy soils.

Netrostylis halmaturina is adapted to withstand drought and is an important component of native grassland ecosystems.
