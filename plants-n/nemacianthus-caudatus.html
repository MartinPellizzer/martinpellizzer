Nemacianthus caudatus

Nemacianthus caudatus, commonly known as the trailing forest bell, is a unique plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, cascading stems that often trail from trees or grow along the forest floor. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, adding a delicate aesthetic to its natural habitat. Its leaves are elliptical and glossy, often with a slight waxy coating that helps retain moisture in humid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of care, it has become a popular choice for indoor gardening and hanging baskets.
