Nidularium angraense

Nidularium angraense, commonly known as the Brazilian orchid, is a species of orchid native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it has a pseudobulb structure typical of many orchid species.

Nidularium angraense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
