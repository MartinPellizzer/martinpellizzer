Nicotiana suaveolens var. tuberosa

Nicotiana suaveolens var. tuberosa, commonly known as the sweet tobacco or nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet or purple flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, round fruits containing seeds. It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which also includes tomatoes and peppers.

Its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine make it a notable species in botanical gardens and horticulture.
