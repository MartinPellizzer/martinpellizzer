Nania chrysantha

Nania chrysantha, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, simple, and lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, providing a textured appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Nania chrysantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate in garden settings.
