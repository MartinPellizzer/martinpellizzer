Notocactus scopa f. succineus

Notocactus scopa f. succineus, commonly known as the golden cactus or the yellow-tipped cactus, is a rare and striking member of the Cactaceae family.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are typically green with a yellowish or golden hue, especially at the tips. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding to its ornamental appeal. It is native to Mexico and thrives in well-draining soil with minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is often cultivated by cactus enthusiasts and botanists.
