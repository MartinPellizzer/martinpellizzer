Nepenthes smithii

Nepenthes smithii, commonly known as the Smith's pitcher plant, is a tropical vine native to the island of Borneo.

It is characterized by its unique pitcher-shaped leaves that secrete a digestive fluid to trap and digest insects. The plant's bright red coloration, especially on the upper part of the pitcher, serves as an attractant for prey. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil.

Nepenthes smithii is a popular species among carnivorous plant enthusiasts due to its striking appearance and specialized adaptations.
