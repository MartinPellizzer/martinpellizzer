Nectandra grandis

Nectandra grandis, commonly known as the large nectandra or sweet-scented wood, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and avocado trees. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, fragrant leaves and bark, which emit a pleasant scent. It typically grows in humid, lowland forests and can reach heights of up to 30 meters.

Nectandra grandis is valued for its timber and has traditional uses in local medicine.
