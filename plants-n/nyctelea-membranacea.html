Nyctelea membranacea

Nyctelea membranacea, commonly known as the moonflower or nightshade, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that open at night and close during the day. The plant has a climbing habit and produces small, fleshy fruits that contain seeds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a velvety texture.

Nyctelea membranacea is adapted to low-light conditions and thrives in humid, warm environments.
