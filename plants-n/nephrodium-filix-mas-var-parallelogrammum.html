Nephrodium filix-mas var. parallelogrammum

Nephrodium filix-mas var. parallelogrammum, commonly known as the broadleaf fern, is a variant of the common nephrolepis.

It is characterized by its large, lance-shaped fronds that are typically arranged in a bipinnate pattern. The plant produces sporangia on the undersides of its leaves, which are clustered in sori. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
