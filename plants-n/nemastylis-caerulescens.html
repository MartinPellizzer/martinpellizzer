Nemastylis caerulescens

Nemastylis caerulescens, commonly known as the blue-eyed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Poaceae, commonly known as the grass family, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, and the flowers are clustered in spikelets at the ends of the stems.

Nemastylis caerulescens plays a role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
