Nephrodium poiretii

Nephrodium poiretii, commonly known as the Poiret's nephroid, is a rare and ancient fern species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Nephrodiaceae, which includes several primitive ferns characterized by their unique reproductive structures. The plant exhibits a distinctive morphology with fiddlehead-like fronds that unfurl into a rosette pattern, often resembling a cluster of small, curled leaves. Its spore-bearing structures, called sporangia, are clustered in sori on the underside of the fronds, a feature typical of many ferns.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Nephrodium poiretii is considered a valuable subject for botanical research and conservation efforts.
