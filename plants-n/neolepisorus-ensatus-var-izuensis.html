Neolepisorus ensatus var. izuensis

Neolepisorus ensatus var. izuensis, commonly known as the Izu needle fern, is a rare and delicate fern species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its finely divided, needle-like fronds that give it a distinctive, almost feathery appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest undergrowth, where it thrives in cool, humid conditions. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, typical of many fern species.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant plant.
