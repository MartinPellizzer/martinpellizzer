Nitidocidium barbaceniae

Nitidocidium barbaceniae, commonly known as the red-flowered false poinsettia, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its striking red bracts that resemble flowers, which are actually modified leaves. Its small, inconspicuous yellow flowers are surrounded by the showy bracts.

Nitidocidium barbaceniae is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant appearance and ease of care.
