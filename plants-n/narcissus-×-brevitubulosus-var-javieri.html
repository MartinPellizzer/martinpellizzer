Narcissus × brevitubulosus var. javieri

Narcissus × brevitubulosus var. javieri, commonly known as the Java daffodil, is a hybrid tulip species native to the Netherlands.

It is characterized by its distinctive, cup-shaped flowers that are typically yellow with a white or greenish throat. The plant features a bulbous root system and narrow, strap-like leaves that are grass-like in appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often used in floral arrangements.
