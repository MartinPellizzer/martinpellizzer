Neanotis sahyadrica

Neanotis sahyadrica, commonly known as the Western Ghats shrub, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant features opposite, elliptical leaves with a glossy surface and a distinct aromatic scent. Its small white flowers are clustered in umbels and bloom during the monsoon season.

Neanotis sahyadrica is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
