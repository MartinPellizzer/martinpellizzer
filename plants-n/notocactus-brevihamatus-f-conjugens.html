Notocactus brevihamatus f. conjugens

Notocactus brevihamatus f. conjugens, commonly known as the Mexican night bloomer, is a unique cactus species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are ribbed and covered in dense, white spines. The plant produces large, fragrant, night-blooming flowers that are typically pink or red in color. Its growth habit is compact, making it suitable for small spaces or container gardening.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in arid conditions with minimal water.
