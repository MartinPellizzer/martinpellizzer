Nepeta laxa

Nepeta laxa, commonly known as catmint, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine, grayish hairs, and its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and aromatic. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Nepeta laxa is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and as a source of nectar for bees and other pollinators.
