Nervilia hungii

Nervilia hungii, commonly known as the golden thread fern, is a member of the Dryopteridaceae family.

It is a small, terrestrial fern characterized by its delicate, slender fronds that are typically less than 30 centimeters in length. The plant features a creeping rhizome with numerous small, clustered sporangia on the underside of its leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate regions with high humidity.

Nervilia hungii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and shaded landscapes.
