Nolana rostrata var. carnosa

Nolana rostrata var. carnosa, commonly known as the woolly nolana, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that covers the stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, showy flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique texture and color.
