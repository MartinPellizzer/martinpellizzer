Neottopteris Nidus

Neottopteris nidus is a unique fern species known for its distinctive, shield-shaped fronds. Native to eastern Asia, it thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky slopes. Its name, meaning "nesting fern," refers to the way its young fronds curl tightly together. The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated in gardens worldwide. Despite its beauty, it remains relatively obscure compared to more common fern species.
