Notocactus tabularis f. bommeljei

Notocactus tabularis f. bommeljei, commonly known as the Bommeljei cactus, is a unique and rare hybrid within the Notocactus genus.

It is characterized by its flattened, disc-like stem that can grow up to 30 cm in diameter, often forming a rosette shape. The plant produces striking yellow to orange flowers during the summer months, which are borne on long, slender pedicels. It is native to the arid regions of South America, particularly in Brazil, and requires well-draining soil and minimal water to thrive.

Due to its rarity and specific care requirements, it is a prized addition to specialized cactus collections.
