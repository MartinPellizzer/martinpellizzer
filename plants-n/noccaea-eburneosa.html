Noccaea eburneosa

Noccaea eburneosa, commonly known as the silverleaf saxifrage, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Brassicaceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of silvery, hairy leaves and small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Native to alpine and arctic regions, it thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils. The plant is adapted to harsh conditions, with a short growing season and the ability to survive in low-nutrient soils.

Its medicinal properties have been studied for their potential anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects.
