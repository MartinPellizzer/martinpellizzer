Nothopanax × parvus

Nothopanax × parvus, commonly known as the hybrid spondias, is a naturally occurring hybrid between *Spondias mombinensis* and *Spondias pinnata*.

It is a small to medium-sized tree characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by edible, yellow-orange fruits similar to those of its parent species. Nothopanax × parvus is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in humid, well-drained soils.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a hybrid species that showcases the genetic diversity within the Spondias genus.
