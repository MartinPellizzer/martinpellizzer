Nepenthes laevis

Nepenthes laevis, commonly known as the glass pitcher plant, is a carnivorous plant native to Borneo.

It is characterized by its elongated, slender pitchers that are often transparent or translucent, giving it its common name. The plant produces a sticky fluid in its pitcher to trap and digest insects. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires high levels of moisture and nutrient-poor soil.

Its unique morphology and carnivorous adaptation make it a fascinating subject for botanical study.
