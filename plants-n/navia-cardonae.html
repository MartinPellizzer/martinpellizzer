Navia cardonae

Navia cardonae, commonly known as the Canary Island date palm, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into edible dates, though it is less commonly cultivated for this purpose. Navia cardonae thrives in arid and coastal environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh, saline conditions.

Its historical significance includes use in traditional medicine and as a symbol of resilience in the Canary Islands.
