Neomarica gracilis

Neomarica gracilis, commonly known as the slender orchid, is a species of orchid native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stem and clusters of small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white, pink, or purple. The plant prefers well-draining soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for both indoor and outdoor cultivation. Neomarica gracilis is relatively easy to care for, requiring moderate watering and good air circulation to prevent fungal diseases.

Its elegant appearance and long-lasting blooms make it a popular choice for ornamental gardens and floral arrangements.
