Nidularium albiflorum

Nidularium albiflorum, commonly known as the white-flowered nidularium, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central water-holding structure. The plant produces showy, white flowers that bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. Its leaves are typically covered in a dense layer of trichomes, which help in water retention and protection from intense sunlight.

Nidularium albiflorum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
