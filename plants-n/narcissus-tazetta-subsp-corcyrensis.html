Narcissus tazetta subsp. corcyrensis

Narcissus tazetta subsp.

corcyrensis, commonly known as the Corcyrene daffodil, is a bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to white, typically blooming in early spring.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is characterized by its compact growth habit. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and is relatively hardy in temperate climates.

This subspecies is closely related to other narcissus species and is valued for its fragrance and visual appeal.
