Nasturtium oxycarpum

Nasturtium oxycarpum, commonly known as the common nasturtium, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its vibrant, edible flowers and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces bright orange or yellow blossoms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its capsule fruits contain seeds that are often used in herbal remedies.

Nasturtium oxycarpum is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other brassicas like cabbage and mustard.
