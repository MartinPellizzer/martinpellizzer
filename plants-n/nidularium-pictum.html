Nidularium pictum

Nidularium pictum, commonly known as the painted fern, is a striking plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that display a range of colors, from silver to deep green. The plant's leaves are bi-lobed and often have a feathery appearance, with the lower fronds typically being more colorful than the upper ones. Nidularium pictum thrives in moist, shady environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice among fern enthusiasts.
