Narcissus × caracensis

Narcissus × caracensis, commonly known as the Carpathian daffodil, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between N. tazetta and N. cyclamenus.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to yellow. The plant typically grows to a height of 15–30 cm and features strap-like, grassy leaves. It is native to the Carpathian Mountains and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
