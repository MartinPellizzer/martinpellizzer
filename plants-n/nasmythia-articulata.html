Nasmythia articulata

Nasmythia articulata, commonly known as the jointed flax, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems that often exhibit jointed segments, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the cooler months. Its leaves are alternate, linear, and often have a rough texture.

Nasmythia articulata is typically found in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.
