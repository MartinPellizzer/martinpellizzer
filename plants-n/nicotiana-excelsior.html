Nicotiana excelsior

Nicotiana excelsior, commonly known as the tall nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically purple or violet in color. It produces small, spherical berries that contain the toxic alkaloid nicotine.

Nicotiana excelsior is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been studied for its potential in pharmaceutical research.
