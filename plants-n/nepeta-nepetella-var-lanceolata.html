Nepeta nepetella var. lanceolata

Nepeta nepetella var. lanceolata, commonly known as catnip, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture. The plant produces small, clustered blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Its strong scent attracts cats and is used in traditional herbal medicine for its calming properties.
