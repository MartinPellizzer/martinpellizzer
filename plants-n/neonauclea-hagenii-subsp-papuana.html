Neonauclea hagenii subsp. papuana

Neonauclea hagenii subsp.

papuana, commonly known as the Papuan neonauclea, is a species native to the lowland rainforests of New Guinea. It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows up to 10 meters in height.

The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth, glossy surface. It produces small, white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by spherical, woody fruits containing several seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in tropical botanical gardens.
