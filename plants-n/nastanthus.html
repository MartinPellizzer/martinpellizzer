Nastanthus

Nastanthus, commonly known as the sacred lotus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that bloom on long, erect stalks rising from broad, rounded leaves. The plant is often associated with purity and spiritual significance in various cultures. Its flowers typically open in the morning and close at night, a behavior known as nyctinasty.

Nastanthus is also valued for its ornamental beauty and has been cultivated for centuries in gardens and religious settings.
