Notocactus sellowii f. macracanthus

Notocactus sellowii f. macracanthus, commonly known as the Sellow's notocactus, is a unique variant of the Notocactus genus native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its distinctive, thick, ribbed stems that are often covered in a waxy, white coating. The plant produces large, showy yellow flowers during the summer months, which are borne on long, slender pedicels. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.

Due to its rarity and specific care requirements, it is often cultivated by enthusiasts in controlled environments.
