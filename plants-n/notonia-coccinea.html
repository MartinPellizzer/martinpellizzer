Notonia coccinea

Notonia coccinea, commonly known as the red-veined begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves with deep red veins against a dark green background. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically red or orange in color, which bloom in clusters. Notonia coccinea prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its ornamental value lies in its unique foliage and delicate floral display.
