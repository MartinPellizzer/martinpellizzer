Narcissus abscissus var. tubulosus

Narcissus abscissus var. tubulosus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved daffodil, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Europe.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in early spring, adding a vibrant touch to gardens and natural habitats. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile addition to landscaping.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation.
