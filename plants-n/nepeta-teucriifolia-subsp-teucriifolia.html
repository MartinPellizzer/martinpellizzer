Nepeta teucriifolia subsp. teucriifolia

Nepeta teucriifolia subsp.

teucriifolia, commonly known as catmint, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia. It features upright stems covered in dense, hairy foliage and produces clusters of small, tubular blue or purple flowers.

The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves, which emit a strong mint-like scent when crushed. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Nepeta teucriifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
