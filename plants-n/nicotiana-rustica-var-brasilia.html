Nicotiana rustica var. brasilia

Nicotiana rustica var. brasilia, commonly known as Brazilian tobacco, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family.

It is native to Central and South America and is characterized by its erect growth habit and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is cultivated for its leaves, which contain nicotine and are used in traditional smoking and medicinal practices.

The plant thrives in warm, sunny climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
