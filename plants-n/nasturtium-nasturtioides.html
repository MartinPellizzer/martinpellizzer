Nasturtium nasturtioides

Nasturtium nasturtioides, commonly known as the Mexican watercress, is a herbaceous plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are often submerged or floating on water surfaces, with small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to thrive in wet environments, such as marshes and ponds. Its stems and leaves are edible, possessing a peppery flavor similar to watercress.

Nasturtium nasturtioides is also known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in aquatic gardens.
