Noccaea hastulata

Noccaea hastulata, commonly known as the arrowleaf nailwort, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in alpine or subalpine environments. It is a perennial species that produces seeds through its flowers, contributing to its propagation in suitable habitats.

Noccaea hastulata is often found in open, sunny areas and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.
