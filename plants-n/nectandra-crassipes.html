Nectandra crassipes

Nectandra crassipes, commonly known as the broad-leaved nectandra, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, dark purple drupe containing a single seed.

Nectandra crassipes is valued for its aromatic wood, which is used in traditional crafts and as a source of essential oils.
