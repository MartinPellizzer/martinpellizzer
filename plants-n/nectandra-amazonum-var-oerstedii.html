Nectandra amazonum var. oerstedii

Nectandra amazonum var. oerstedii, commonly known as the Amazonian sandwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark-colored wood that is highly valued for its durability and scent. The plant typically grows in humid, lowland areas and can reach heights of up to 30 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a glossy surface, while its flowers are small and clustered in umbels.

The species is also noted for its ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
