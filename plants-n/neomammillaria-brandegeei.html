Neomammillaria brandegeei

Neomammillaria brandegeei, commonly known as the Brandegee's milk barrel cactus, is a species native to the Sonoran Desert of the United States and Mexico.

It is a small, globular cactus with a dense covering of white, woolly spines. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its gray-green body. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stem.

Neomammillaria brandegeei is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
