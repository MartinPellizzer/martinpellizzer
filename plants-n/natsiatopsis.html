Natsiatopsis

Natsiatopsis, commonly known as the "ghost orchid," is a rare and enigmatic plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is an epiphytic orchid, meaning it grows on the surface of tree trunks or branches without deriving nutrients from its host. The plant is characterized by its delicate, translucent leaves and striking, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. Natsiatopsis thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires high levels of atmospheric moisture to survive.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a conservation concern in its native range.
