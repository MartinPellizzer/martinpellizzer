Najas wrightiana subsp. multidentata

Najas wrightiana subsp.

multidentata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved watermilfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in eastern North America. It belongs to the family Najadaceae and is characterized by its slender, ribbon-like leaves that are often arranged in whorls around the stems.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically found in clusters near the water surface. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Due to its rapid growth and ability to form dense mats, it can also be considered a potential invasive species in certain regions.
