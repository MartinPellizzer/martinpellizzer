Narcissus macrolobus

Narcissus macrolobus, commonly known as the giant daffodil, is a species of flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers with a distinctive, elongated corona. The plant typically grows to a height of up to 1.5 meters, making it one of the tallest daffodil species. Its leaves are broad and grass-like, forming a basal rosette.

The flowers bloom in spring, adding a striking visual element to its natural habitat.
