Nectandra fragrans

Nectandra fragrans, commonly known as the fragrant nectandra, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and flowers, which emit a sweet, spicy scent. Its wood is valued for its durability and fine grain, making it useful in carpentry and furniture making.

Nectandra fragrans thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland evergreen forests.
