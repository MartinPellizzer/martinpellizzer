Nasturtiopsis coronopifolia var. dasycarpa

Nasturtiopsis coronopifolia var. dasycarpa, commonly known as the hairy-fruited watercress, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features alternate, pinnately compound leaves with serrated margins, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by spherical, hairy fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found near streams or in wetlands.

The leaves and young fruits are edible, with a peppery flavor similar to watercress.
