Nierembergia longistyla

Nierembergia longistyla, commonly known as the long-styled bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling bells. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Nierembergia longistyla is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
