Navia polyglomerata

Navia polyglomerata, commonly known as the clustered navia, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its dense clusters of small, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often near streams or in humid forests. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, while its stems are often hairy and creeping.

Navia polyglomerata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
