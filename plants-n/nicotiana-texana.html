Nicotiana texana

Nicotiana texana, commonly known as the Texas tobacco plant, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils. It has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and historical significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
