Neskiza cespitosa

Neskiza cespitosa, commonly known as the clustered neskiza, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its compact, clumping growth habit. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces clusters of tubular, brightly colored flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
