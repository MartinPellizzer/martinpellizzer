Nicotiana tristis

Nicotiana tristis, commonly known as the bitter tobacco plant, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family.

It is native to the Americas and is characterized by its small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant produces a yellow or green fruit that contains seeds, and it has hairy, glandular leaves that emit a strong, unpleasant odor. Nicotiana tristis contains nicotine and other alkaloids, making it toxic to humans and animals.

It is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and waste areas.
