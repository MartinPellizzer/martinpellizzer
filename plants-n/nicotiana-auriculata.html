Nicotiana auriculata

Nicotiana auriculata, commonly known as the golden nightshade, is a species of flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from white to yellow and often have a distinctive auriculate (ear-shaped) base. The plant typically grows as an annual or biennial herb with hairy stems and alternate leaves. It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades such as tomatoes and peppers.

Nicotiana auriculata is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant blooms and unique appearance.
