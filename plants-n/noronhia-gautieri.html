Noronhia gautieri

Noronhia gautieri, commonly known as the Brazilian nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces small, violet-blue flowers that bloom in clusters, often at the base of the leaves. Its leaves are ovate and slightly hairy, with a distinctive aromatic scent.

The fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.
