Notopleura penduliflora

Notopleura penduliflora, commonly known as the hanging-flower vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that hang from the stems, giving it its common name. Its fibrous roots and woody stems make it adaptable to various soil types.

Notopleura penduliflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
