Nephrodium decurtatum

Nephrodium decurtatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf nephroid, is a unique fern species characterized by its distinctive fronds.

It belongs to the family Nephrodiaceae and is native to specific regions in Southeast Asia. The plant features lance-shaped, serrated leaves that are often arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. Its reproductive structures include sporangia that produce spores, typical of ferns.

Nephrodium decurtatum thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in tropical rainforests.
