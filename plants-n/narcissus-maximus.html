Narcissus maximus

Narcissus maximus, commonly known as the Giant Daffodil, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers with a prominent central corona. Native to southern Europe and western Asia, this plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight. The bulbs are perennial, producing foliage and blooms in early spring.

Its botanical features make it a popular ornamental plant in gardens and botanical collections.
