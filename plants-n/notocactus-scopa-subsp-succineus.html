Notocactus scopa subsp. succineus

Notocactus scopa subsp.

succineus, commonly known as the "Coral Cactus," is a species native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its compact, branching structure and produces clusters of bright orange flowers during the summer months.

The plant has a thick, fleshy stem that stores water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. Its leaves are modified into spines, reducing water loss through transpiration.

This cactus is well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low maintenance requirements.
