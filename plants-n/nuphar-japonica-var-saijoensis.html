Nuphar japonica var. saijoensis

Nuphar japonica var. saijoensis, commonly known as the Japanese water lily, is a aquatic plant native to Japan.

It features broad, floating leaves that are typically green with a waxy surface, and its flowers are showy, with white petals and yellow stamens. The plant grows in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats, such as ponds and lakes. Its rhizomes are often submerged, anchoring the plant in the sediment.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in aquatic ecosystems.
