Notholaena paucijuga

Notholaena paucijuga, commonly known as the narrow-leafed hookless fern, is a rare and unique fern species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, slender fronds that are typically less than 30 centimeters in length. The plant has a creeping rhizome and produces small, spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered an endangered species.
