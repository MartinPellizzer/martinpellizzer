Narcissus lobularis

Narcissus lobularis, commonly known as the Spanish daffodil, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe, particularly Spain and Portugal, and thrives in Mediterranean climates. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, typically blooming in early spring. Its bulbs are perennial, and the foliage consists of strap-like leaves that emerge after the flowers.

Narcissus lobularis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
