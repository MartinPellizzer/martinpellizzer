Nerine sarniensis var. moorei

Nerine sarniensis var. moorei, commonly known as the Moore's Nerine, is a bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It features striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant produces showy, upright inflorescences with clustered blooms that appear in late summer to early autumn. Its foliage consists of strap-like leaves that are grassy in appearance.

It is relatively drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
