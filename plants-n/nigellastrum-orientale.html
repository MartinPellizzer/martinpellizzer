Nigellastrum orientale

Nigellastrum orientale, commonly known as the Persian buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers and hairy, branched stems. The plant produces small, oval seeds that are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a hardy perennial in temperate climates.

Its botanical features make it an attractive addition to gardens and a valuable species in ecological studies.
