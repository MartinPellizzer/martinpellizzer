Nelumbium indicum

Nelumbium indicum, commonly known as the sacred lotus, is a water-loving plant native to Asia.

It thrives in shallow freshwater environments such as ponds and marshes. The plant is characterized by its large, showy pink or white flowers that bloom on long, erect stems. Its leaves are broad and floating, often with a distinctive hole in the center.

Nelumbium indicum has been culturally and spiritually significant in many Asian traditions for centuries.
