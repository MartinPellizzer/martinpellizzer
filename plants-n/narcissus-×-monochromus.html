Narcissus × monochromus

Narcissus × monochromus, commonly known as the single-stem daffodil, is a hybrid species within the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its single, upright stem that bears a single, large, showy flower. The plant typically produces a trumpet-shaped corona that is the same color as the tepals, giving it a uniform appearance. It is native to the Iberian Peninsula and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

This variety is valued for its simplicity and elegance in floral displays.
