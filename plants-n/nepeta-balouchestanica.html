Nepeta balouchestanica

Nepeta balouchestanica, commonly known as Balochestan catmint, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of Pakistan and Afghanistan.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or violet flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their calming properties.

Nepeta balouchestanica is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils.
