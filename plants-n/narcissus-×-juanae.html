Narcissus × juanae

Narcissus × juanae, commonly known as the Juanae daffodil, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Narcissus tazetta and Narcissus poeticus.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow petals and a prominent, trumpet-shaped corona. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure. It is native to the Iberian Peninsula and is valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens.

The flowers bloom in early spring, adding a vibrant touch to landscapes with their bright, cheerful colors.
