Narcissus rupicola subsp. watieri

Narcissus rupicola subsp.

watieri, commonly known as the rock daffodil, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and a single, upright flower with a yellow perianth.

The plant thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats. Its flowers bloom in early spring, typically from March to April.

This subspecies is valued for its hardiness and ornamental appeal in gardens and natural landscapes.
