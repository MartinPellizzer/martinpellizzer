Neomarica capitellata

Neomarica capitellata, commonly known as the purple ginger, is a striking ornamental plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems that bear large, showy inflorescences composed of numerous small, violet-colored flowers. The plant's foliage consists of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often a deep green with a waxy texture. Neomarica capitellata thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
