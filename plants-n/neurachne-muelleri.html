Neurachne muelleri

Neurachne muelleri, commonly known as Mueller's grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, leaf-like blades. The plant typically grows in sandy or coastal soils and is often found in dune environments. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Neurachne muelleri plays a role in stabilizing coastal ecosystems and is adapted to withstand salt spray and wind.
