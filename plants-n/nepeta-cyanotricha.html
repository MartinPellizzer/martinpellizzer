Nepeta cyanotricha

Nepeta cyanotricha, commonly known as catnip, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves contain nepetalactone, a compound known for its stimulating effect on cats.

Nepeta cyanotricha is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and historical use in herbal remedies.
