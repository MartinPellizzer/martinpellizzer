Narcissus × sulcicaulis

Narcissus × sulcicaulis, commonly known as the crested daffodil, is a hybrid plant resulting from crosses between various narcissus species.

It is characterized by its distinctive, crested or tufted crown of petals, which sets it apart from other daffodil varieties. The plant typically grows to a height of 20–30 cm, featuring narrow, grass-like leaves and a single, trumpet-shaped flower. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

The flowers bloom in early spring, adding a vibrant splash of color to gardens and landscapes.
