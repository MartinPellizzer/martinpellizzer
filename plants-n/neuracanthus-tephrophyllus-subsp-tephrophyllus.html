Neuracanthus tephrophyllus subsp. tephrophyllus

Neuracanthus tephrophyllus subsp.

tephrophyllus, commonly known as the silver-veined hibiscus, is a species native to tropical regions of Africa. It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 3 meters in height.

The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with prominent silver veins. Its flowers are showy, with five petals that range in color from white to pale pink.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
