Nectandra albiflora

Nectandra albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered sandarac tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark-green leaves and showy white flowers. The plant produces small, woody fruits that contain aromatic resin, which has been historically used in incense and perfumes. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The tree's ornamental value and ecological importance make it a significant species in its native habitats.
