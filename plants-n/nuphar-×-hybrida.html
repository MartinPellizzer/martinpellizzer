Nuphar × hybrida

Nuphar × hybrida, commonly known as the hybrid yellow pond lily, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Nuphar luteum and Nuphar variegatum.

It is a perennial aquatic plant characterized by its broad, floating leaves and showy yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes. Its leaves are typically rounded with a waxy surface, and its flowers have a distinct, fragrant scent.

Nuphar × hybrida plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
