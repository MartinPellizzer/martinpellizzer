Najas marina var. genuina

Najas marina var. genuina, commonly known as the narrowleaf water nymph, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater habitats in Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Najadaceae and is characterized by its slender, ribbon-like leaves that are typically less than 1 cm in width. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged, with male and female flowers occurring on separate plants. It thrives in still or slow-moving waters with soft, nutrient-rich substrates.

Its rapid growth and ability to form dense underwater mats make it an important component of aquatic ecosystems.
