Nepeta nepetella var. cordifolia

Nepeta nepetella var. cordifolia, commonly known as heartleaf catmint, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features aromatic, slightly hairy leaves that are heart-shaped, giving it its common name. The plant produces spikes of blue to purple flowers in the summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal remedies.
