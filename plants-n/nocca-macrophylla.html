Nocca macrophylla

Nocca macrophylla, commonly known as the Japanese maple, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves that change color with the seasons, often displaying vibrant red, orange, and yellow hues in autumn. The plant prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, thriving in temperate climates. Its small, inconspicuous flowers appear in spring, followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Nocca macrophylla is widely cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and parks.
