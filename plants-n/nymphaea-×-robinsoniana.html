Nymphaea × robinsoniana

Nymphaea × robinsoniana, commonly known as the Robinson's water lily, is a hybrid species belonging to the Nymphaeaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink, with a prominent yellow stamen. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes. Its leaves are broad and floating, often with a waxy surface to repel water.

Nymphaea × robinsoniana is valued in aquatic gardens for its ornamental beauty and ability to provide habitat for aquatic life.
