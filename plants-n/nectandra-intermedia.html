Nectandra intermedia

Nectandra intermedia, commonly known as the white sand cedar, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and smooth, pale bark. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, elongated fruits containing aromatic seeds. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
