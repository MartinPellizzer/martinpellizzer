Narcissus × biflorus var. dianthos

Narcissus × biflorus var. dianthos, commonly known as the Dianthus narcissus, is a hybrid plant that combines traits from various narcissus species.

It is characterized by its distinctive, double-flowered blooms that resemble those of the dianthus genus. The plant typically produces showy, pink or white flowers with a prominent central tuft of stamens. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to bloom reliably in temperate climates.
