Neomammillaria missouriensis

Neomammillaria missouriensis, commonly known as the Missouri prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are often ribbed and covered in white to gray spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are followed by edible, red fruits. Neomammillaria missouriensis is adapted to dry, sandy soils and can thrive in full sun.

It is a low-maintenance plant that is well-suited for xeriscaping and desert gardens.
