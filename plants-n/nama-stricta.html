Nama stricta

Nama stricta, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironweed, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers in panicles, which bloom in late summer. Nama stricta thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
