Narcissus longipetalus

Narcissus longipetalus, commonly known as the long-petaled daffodil, is a species native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow petals that give it a distinctive appearance compared to other daffodil varieties. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, with a basal rosette of leaves. Its flowers bloom in early spring, producing a bright yellow corona surrounded by six to eight narrow petals.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant and elegant display.
