Navarretia divaricata

Navarretia divaricata, commonly known as the Sierra primrose, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or mountainous regions.

Navarretia divaricata is adapted to cold climates and is an important component of native plant communities in its habitat.
