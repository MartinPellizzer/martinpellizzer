Nepeta nepetella var. leucophylla

Nepeta nepetella var. leucophylla, commonly known as the white-leaf catmint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features slender, upright stems covered in soft, gray-green foliage with a distinctive white or pale blue underside. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its aromatic properties and ornamental value make it a valuable addition to both garden landscapes and ecological habitats.
