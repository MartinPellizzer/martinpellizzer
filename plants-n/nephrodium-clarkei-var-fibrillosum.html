Nephrodium clarkei var. fibrillosum

Nephrodium clarkei var. fibrillosum, commonly known as the fibrous nephroid, is a rare fern species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, leathery fronds that are often adorned with fibrous structures along their edges. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, such as forest undergrowth, where it can thrive without direct sunlight. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of its fronds, a common feature among ferns.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a botanical curiosity and of potential conservation interest.
