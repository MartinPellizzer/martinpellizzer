Nectandra thornei

Nectandra thornei, commonly known as the Brazilian sand-wood, is a tropical tree native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurel trees, and is characterized by its aromatic, often fragrant leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments, preferring well-drained soils. Its bark and leaves contain essential oils, which have been used in traditional medicine.

Nectandra thornei is also valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in construction and furniture-making.
