Neosparton scirpeum

Neosparton scirpeum, commonly known as the slender bulrush, is a wetland plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that often reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in dense, spike-like clusters, typically blooming in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, growing in whorls around the stem.

Neosparton scirpeum plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
