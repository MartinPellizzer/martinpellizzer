Nageia amara

Nageia amara, commonly known as the sweet-scented nageia, is a species of conifer native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Podocarpaceae and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and cone-bearing structure. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in mountainous areas with high humidity. Its leaves are needle-like and arranged spirally on the shoots, contributing to its ornamental value.

Nageia amara is valued for its aromatic resins and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
