Narcissus juncifolius var. parviflorus

Narcissus juncifolius var. parviflorus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved daffodil, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellow flowers with a single, upright tepal. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It is part of the Amaryllidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

The variety parviflorus is distinguished by its smaller flowers compared to other varieties of Narcissus juncifolius.
