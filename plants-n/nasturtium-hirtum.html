Nasturtium hirtum

Nasturtium hirtum, commonly known as hairy nasturtium, is an annual flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of orange and yellow, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are edible and have a peppery taste, making it a popular choice in gardens for both ornamental and culinary purposes.

Nasturtium hirtum thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often growing in temperate climates.
