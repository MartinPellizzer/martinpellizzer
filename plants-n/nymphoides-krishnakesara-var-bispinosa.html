Nymphoides krishnakesara var. bispinosa

Nymphoides krishnakesara var. bispinosa, commonly known as the Krishna's water lily, is a variant of the water lily native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in ponds and wetlands. Its flowers are typically pink or white, with a distinctive two-spined structure on the petals, giving it its botanical name.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in aquatic habitats.
