Nicotiana spegazzinii

Nicotiana spegazzinii, commonly known as the Spegazzini tobacco plant, is a species native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It is a herbaceous annual plant that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface and produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are usually pink to purple in color. It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is closely related to other tobacco species.

Due to its ornamental value and unique floral characteristics, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
