Narcissus × carringtonii nothosubsp. tavaresii

Narcissus × carringtonii nothosubsp.

tavaresii, commonly known as the Tavares daffodil, is a hybrid plant with striking yellow flowers and a narrow, cylindrical corona. It is native to the Canary Islands and is characterized by its robust, upright stems and large, showy blooms.

The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and prefers well-drained, sandy soils. It is a popular ornamental plant due to its vibrant color and long-lasting flowers.

This nothosubspecies is valued for its horticultural appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
