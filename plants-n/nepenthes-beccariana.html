Nepenthes beccariana

Nepenthes beccariana, commonly known as the Bornean pitcher plant, is a carnivorous plant native to the rainforests of Borneo.

It is characterized by its elongated, tubular leaves that form deep, pitcher-shaped traps to capture insects. The plant's pitchers are typically green or reddish in color and produce a sweet-smelling nectar to lure prey. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires high atmospheric humidity to maintain its unique structure.

The species is notable for its ability to trap and digest a variety of small invertebrates, contributing to its ecological role in nutrient-poor soils.
