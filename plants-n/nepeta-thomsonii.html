Nepeta thomsonii

Nepeta thomsonii, commonly known as Thomson's catmint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces spikes of blue to purple flowers in the summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Nepeta thomsonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to repel certain garden pests.
