Nectaroscordum triquetrum

Nectaroscordum triquetrum, commonly known as the sweet violet, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy purple flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces a fragrant nectar that attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial shade, often found in woodland areas.

Nectaroscordum triquetrum is also valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in herbal medicine.
