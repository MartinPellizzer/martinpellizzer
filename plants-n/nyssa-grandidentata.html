Nyssa grandidentata

Nyssa grandidentata, commonly known as the large-toothed boxelder, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Cornaceae and is characterized by its alternate, simple, and serrated leaves. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, followed by winged fruits that aid in seed dispersal. Its bark is smooth and gray, becoming fissured with age, and it often grows in moist, well-drained soils.

Nyssa grandidentata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
