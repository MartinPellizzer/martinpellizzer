Neobuchia paulinae

Neobuchia paulinae, commonly known as the Paulina's ghost orchid, is a rare and striking member of the orchid family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its unique, ghost-like appearance, with elongated, translucent leaves and delicate, pale flowers. It is a lithophyte, meaning it grows on rocks, often in rocky crevices or on tree trunks.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, Neobuchia paulinae is considered endangered and is of great interest to botanists and conservationists.
