Nerine fothergillii

Nerine fothergillii, commonly known as the Fothergill's nerine, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple, with a white throat, and bloom in late summer. The plant has strap-like, grassy leaves that are evergreen in mild climates. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and elegant appearance.
