Nicotiana trigonophylla

Nicotiana trigonophylla, commonly known as the three-leaf tobacco, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family.

It is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets arranged in a triangular pattern. Its flowers are typically blue or purple, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some historical use in traditional medicine.
