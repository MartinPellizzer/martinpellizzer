Narcissus exiguus

Narcissus exiguus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved daffodil, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 15 to 30 centimeters and blooms in early spring. Its flowers consist of a cup-shaped corona surrounded by six tepals, and it is often found in grassy areas and woodland margins.

Narcissus exiguus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate in gardens.
