Narcissus megacodium

Narcissus megacodium, commonly known as the giant daffodil, is a striking member of the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 15 centimeters in diameter. The plant features a sturdy, upright stem with a single, trumpet-shaped bloom that is typically yellow or white in color. Its foliage consists of long, strap-like leaves that emerge from a bulbous base.

Native to southern Europe, it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
