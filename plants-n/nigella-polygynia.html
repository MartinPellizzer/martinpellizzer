Nigella polygynia

Nigella polygynia, commonly known as the Persian cowpea, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and the Caucasus.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, yellowish to orange-colored flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, spherical fruits that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, thriving in temperate climates.

Its ornamental value and historical significance in herbal remedies make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
