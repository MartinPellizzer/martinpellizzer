Nicotiana petiolata

Nicotiana petiolata, commonly known as the petiolate tobacco, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a mild tobacco-like scent.

Nicotiana petiolata is relatively easy to grow and thrives in full sun with well-drained soil.
