Nelsonia campestris

Nelsonia campestris, commonly known as the broadleaf cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant features compound leaves with oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish-white flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used as a forage crop or in agroforestry systems.

Nelsonia campestris is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
