Nepeta delphinensis

Nepeta delphinensis, commonly known as water hyssop, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces spikes of blue to purple flowers in late spring to early summer. Nepeta delphinensis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water sources such as streams and wetlands.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported calming properties.
