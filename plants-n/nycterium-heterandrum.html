Nycterium heterandrum

Nycterium heterandrum, commonly known as the nightshade vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and small, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of white and purple. Its fruit is a small, fleshy berry that turns from green to yellow as it matures.

Due to its toxic properties, it is important to handle the plant with care.
