Narcissus × dichromus

Narcissus × dichromus, commonly known as the double daffodil, is a hybrid species within the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive two-rowed petals, which give it a more elaborate and showy appearance compared to typical daffodils. The plant produces large, trumpet-shaped flowers with a vibrant yellow color, often featuring a contrasting darker center. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

This hybrid is prized for its ornamental value and is commonly cultivated in temperate climates.
