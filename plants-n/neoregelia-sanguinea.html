Neoregelia sanguinea

Neoregelia sanguinea, commonly known as the Red-veined Bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage with red veins contrasting against its green leaves. The plant features a rosette-shaped structure that holds water, creating a microhabitat for small organisms. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange and bloom in the spring, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Neoregelia sanguinea thrives in bright, indirect light and requires high humidity to maintain its lush appearance.
