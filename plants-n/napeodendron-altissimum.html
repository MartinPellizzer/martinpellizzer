Napeodendron altissimum

Napeodendron altissimum, commonly known as the giant flame tree, is a strikingly tall flowering plant native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, showy red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a dramatic visual display. The plant has a robust trunk and can grow up to 30 meters in height, making it a prominent feature in its natural habitat. Its leaves are compound, consisting of multiple leaflets, and it produces small, winged seeds that are dispersed by wind.

The botanical features of Napeodendron altissimum make it both ecologically significant and aesthetically remarkable.
