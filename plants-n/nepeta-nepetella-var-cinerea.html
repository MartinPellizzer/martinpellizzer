Nepeta nepetella var. cinerea

Nepeta nepetella var. cinerea, commonly known as gray catmint, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It features aromatic, gray-green foliage that is covered in fine, downy hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular blue or lavender flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its foliage is often used in herbal remedies and is known for its pleasant, mint-like fragrance.
