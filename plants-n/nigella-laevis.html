Nigella laevis

Nigella laevis, commonly known as the slender cumin, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, spherical seeds that are often used in culinary applications due to their aromatic properties. Nigella laevis thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its botanical features make it both an ornamental and a useful plant in various ecological and agricultural contexts.
