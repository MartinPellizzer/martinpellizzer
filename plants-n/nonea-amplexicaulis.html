Nonea amplexicaulis

Nonea amplexicaulis, commonly known as the clasping nonea, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive growth habit, with leaves that wrap around the stems, giving it a unique, almost vine-like appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine regions.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and specialized species in horticulture.
