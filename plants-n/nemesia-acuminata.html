Nemesia acuminata

Nemesia acuminata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved nemesia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, spurred flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows as an annual or biennial herb, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a hairy texture, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun.

Nemesia acuminata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
