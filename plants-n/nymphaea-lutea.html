Nymphaea lutea

Nymphaea lutea, commonly known as the yellow water lily, is a aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features broad, floating leaves that are typically green with a waxy surface, and its flowers are bright yellow with a prominent central stamen. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes. Its roots are submerged, anchoring it in the mud at the bottom of water bodies.

Nymphaea lutea plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms and contributing to water purification.
