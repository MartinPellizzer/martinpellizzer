Nania diffusa

Nania diffusa, commonly known as the silver fern, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves that have a silvery sheen due to the fine, hairy texture on their surface. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Nania diffusa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
