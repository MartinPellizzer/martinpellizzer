Nectandra tetrandra

Nectandra tetrandra, commonly known as the fragrant wood or yellow wood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, reddish-brown heartwood and pale sapwood. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by elliptical, leathery fruits containing aromatic seeds. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a glossy surface with a distinct aromatic scent.

Nectandra tetrandra is valued for its durable timber and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
