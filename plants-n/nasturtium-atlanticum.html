Nasturtium atlanticum

Nasturtium atlanticum, commonly known as the Atlantic nasturtium, is a flowering plant native to the eastern coast of North America.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other brassicas like cabbage and mustard. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers and heart-shaped, edible leaves that are often used in salads. Its stems are hairy and can grow up to 2 meters tall, making it a striking addition to gardens.

The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, and it is known for its peppery flavor, similar to watercress.
