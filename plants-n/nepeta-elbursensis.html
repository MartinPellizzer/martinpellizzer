Nepeta elbursensis

Nepeta elbursensis, commonly known as Elburs catmint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Iran.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often fragrant and have been traditionally used in herbal remedies.

Nepeta elbursensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native gardens.
