Neolitsea chrysotricha

Neolitsea chrysotricha, commonly known as the golden-haired flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its striking, bright yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a glossy, dark green upper surface with a lighter underside. The tree produces small, round fruits that are typically green when mature and turn red upon ripening.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.
