Nicolaia peekelii

Nicolaia peekelii, commonly known as the Peckel's cycad, is a unique member of the cycad family, Cycadaceae.

It is characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and a crown of stiff, feather-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. Native to the arid regions of southern Africa, this plant thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water. Its cones, which are either male or female, are essential for its reproduction and are often used in traditional medicine.

Due to its slow growth and habitat specificity, Nicolaia peekelii is considered a rare and endangered species.
