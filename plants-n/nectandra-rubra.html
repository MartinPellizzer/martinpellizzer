Nectandra rubra

Nectandra rubra, commonly known as red sandalwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, reddish-brown heartwood, which is highly valued for its durability and scent. The plant belongs to the family Lauraceae and is known for its compound leaves with opposite arrangement. Its flowers are small and fragrant, typically white or pale yellow, and it produces drupes that contain a single seed.

The species is also noted for its ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
