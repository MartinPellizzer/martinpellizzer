Nepeta parnassica subsp. orphanidea

Nepeta parnassica subsp.

orphanidea, commonly known as the Parnassian catmint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features slender, upright stems covered in fine hairs and produces small, pale blue to violet flowers clustered in whorls along the stem.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a mint-like fragrance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate flowers and aromatic foliage.
