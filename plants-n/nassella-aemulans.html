Nassella aemulans

Nassella aemulans, commonly known as the mimic needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance and its ability to form dense, low-growing clumps. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native plant communities and a potential candidate for ecological restoration projects.
