Nuttallia pumila

Nuttallia pumila, commonly known as the small nuttallia, is a species of flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as woodland edges and riverbanks. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine, soft hairs.

Nuttallia pumila is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
