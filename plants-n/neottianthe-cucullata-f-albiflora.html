Neottianthe cucullata f. albiflora

Neottianthe cucullata f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered hooded orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive hood-like flower structure, which gives it its common name. The plant produces pale to white flowers that are often adorned with intricate patterns, enhancing its ornamental value. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution and unique morphology, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
