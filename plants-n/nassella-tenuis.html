Nassella tenuis

Nassella tenuis, commonly known as slender needlegrass, is a flowering plant native to western North America.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Its fine, thread-like leaves and delicate appearance make it a distinctive component of its ecosystem.
