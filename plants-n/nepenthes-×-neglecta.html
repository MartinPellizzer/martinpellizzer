Nepenthes × neglecta

Nepenthes × neglecta, commonly known as the hybrid pitcher plant, is a tropical vine native to Borneo.

It is a natural hybrid between Nepenthes alata and Nepenthes rafflesiana, exhibiting traits from both parent species. The plant produces striking, colorful pitcher-shaped traps that secrete a digestive fluid to trap and digest insects. Its leaves are typically broad and glossy, adapted for climbing and photosynthesis in the dense rainforest canopy.

Nepenthes × neglecta is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and unique carnivorous characteristics.
