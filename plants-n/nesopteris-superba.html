Nesopteris superba

Nesopteris superba, commonly known as the giant water fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, floating fronds that can reach lengths of up to 3 meters. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in marshes and ponds. Its fronds are divided into numerous pinnae, which help in photosynthesis and gas exchange.

The plant plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
