Nepeta multibracteata var. maroccana

Nepeta multibracteata var. maroccana, commonly known as Moroccan catmint, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine hairs and produces clusters of small, pale blue to violet flowers. The leaves are typically gray-green, with a fuzzy texture, and are aromatic when crushed. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens or borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and mild mint-like fragrance.
