Nananthea

Nananthea, commonly known as the golden rain tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its feathery, compound leaves that resemble the shape of a feather. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it its common name. Nananthea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.

It is also valued for its rapid growth and ability to provide shade in landscaping.
