Nemesia umbonata

Nemesia umbonata, commonly known as the mountain nemesia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in cool, moist environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Nemesia umbonata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
