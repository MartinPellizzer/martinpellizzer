Norysca calycina

Norysca calycina, commonly known as the velvetleaf orchid, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features large, velvety leaves that are dark green with a subtle sheen, and its flowers are showy, often in shades of pink and white. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid forest environments. It produces small, fragrant blossoms that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Norysca calycina is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
