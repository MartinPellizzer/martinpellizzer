Nepeta podostachys subsp. podostachys

Nepeta podostachys subsp.

podostachys, commonly known as the narrow-leaved catmint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features slender stems that are often hairy and bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated edge.

The plant produces small, tubular blue to purple flowers arranged in whorled clusters along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

Its aromatic foliage and showy blooms make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and ecological gardens.
