Nemochloa tenuiflora

Nemochloa tenuiflora, commonly known as slender-flowered nemochloa, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its fine, grass-like leaves and small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks. It has a fibrous root system and produces small, three-angled stems.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and ornamental horticulture.
