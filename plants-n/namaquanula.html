Namaquanula

It is a symbiotic organism composed of a fungus and a green alga or cyanobacterium, which allows it to survive in extreme desert conditions. The plant forms a dense, crust-like growth that adheres tightly to rocky surfaces, helping it retain moisture in dry environments. Its slow growth rate and ability to withstand high temperatures make it an important indicator of desert ecosystems.
