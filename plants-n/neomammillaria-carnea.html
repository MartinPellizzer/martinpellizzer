Neomammillaria carnea

Neomammillaria carnea, commonly known as the pink mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in white to pink spines. The plant produces vibrant pink or red flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Neomammillaria carnea is a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor collections due to its compact size and ornamental appeal.
