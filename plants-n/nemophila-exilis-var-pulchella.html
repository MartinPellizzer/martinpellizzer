Nemophila exilis var. pulchella

Nemophila exilis var. pulchella, commonly known as the slender blue-eyed Mary, is a low-growing annual herb native to the western United States.

It features delicate, blue to purple flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May. The plant has slender stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and wildflower meadows.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological restoration and ornamental gardening.
