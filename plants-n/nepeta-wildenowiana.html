Nepeta wildenowiana

Nepeta wildenowiana, commonly known as wild catmint, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often aromatic and can be used in herbal teas or as a scenting agent.

Nepeta wildenowiana thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.
