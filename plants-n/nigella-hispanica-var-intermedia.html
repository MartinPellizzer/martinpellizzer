Nigella hispanica var. intermedia

Nigella hispanica var. intermedia, commonly known as Spanish cumin, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, pale yellow to greenish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, black or brown seeds that are often used as a spice in various cuisines. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Its botanical features include pinnately compound leaves and a compact growth habit.
