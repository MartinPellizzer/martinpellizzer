Nissolia berteroniana

Nissolia berteroniana, commonly known as the Mexican moonseed, is a small, evergreen shrub native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces distinctive, fleshy, orange-red berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. Its roots contain alkaloids and have been used in traditional medicine for their potential therapeutic properties.

Nissolia berteroniana thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.
