Nymphaea reichardiana

Nymphaea reichardiana, commonly known as the Reichard's water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy, pale pink to white flowers that bloom above the water surface. The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves that float on the water's surface, providing habitat for various aquatic organisms. Its roots are submerged, anchoring the plant in shallow waters and absorbing nutrients.

Nymphaea reichardiana is valued for its ornamental beauty and plays a role in maintaining aquatic ecosystem balance.
