Nepenthes blancoi

Nepenthes blancoi, commonly known as the Blanco's pitcher plant, is a carnivorous plant native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its unique pitcher-shaped leaves that secrete a digestive fluid to trap and digest insects. The plant thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found growing on trees or rocks in the understory of forests. Its pitchers can vary in color, ranging from green to red, depending on the species and environmental conditions.

Nepenthes blancoi plays an important role in its ecosystem by controlling insect populations and providing habitat for small organisms.
