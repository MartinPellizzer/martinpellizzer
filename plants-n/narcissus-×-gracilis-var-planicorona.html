Narcissus × gracilis var. planicorona

Narcissus × gracilis var. planicorona, commonly known as the flat-crown daffodil, is a hybrid daffodil species characterized by its compact, low-growing form.

It features narrow, strap-like leaves and produces clusters of small, funnel-shaped flowers with a white or pale yellow corona. The plant typically blooms in early spring, adding a delicate and subtle floral display to gardens. It is well-suited for rock gardens or as a border plant due to its compact size and ornamental value.

This variety is prized for its unique shape and color, making it a popular choice among daffodil enthusiasts.
