Neonotonia wightii var. longicauda

Neonotonia wightii var. longicauda, commonly known as the long-tailed orchid, is a striking tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features elongated, pendulous flowers with bright yellow sepals and petals, often adorned with red or orange markings. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its long, slender inflorescence gives it its common name, and it blooms during the rainy season.

The species is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
