Nepeta maracandia

Nepeta maracandia, commonly known as the Persian catmint, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It features upright, branched stems that are hairy and often tinged with purple. The plant produces small, pale blue to lavender flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Nepeta maracandia is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
