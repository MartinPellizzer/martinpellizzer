Noronhia myrtoides

Noronhia myrtoides, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant leaves and clusters of white or pink flowers. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in coastal areas.

Noronhia myrtoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
