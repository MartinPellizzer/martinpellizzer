Nepeta nuda subsp. glandulifera

Nepeta nuda subsp.

glandulifera, commonly known as the glandular catmint, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe. It features slender, upright stems covered in fine hairs and produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers.

The leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have glandular dots, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and mild mint-like fragrance.
