Nassella poeppigiana

Nassella poeppigiana, commonly known as the desert needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and dense, tufted growth habit, which helps it withstand harsh environmental conditions. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous fine, bristle-like flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. Its fibrous roots contribute to soil stabilization, making it ecologically significant in degraded landscapes.

Nassella poeppigiana is well adapted to drought and poor soil, thriving in environments with minimal water availability.
