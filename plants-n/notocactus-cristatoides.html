Notocactus cristatoides

Notocactus cristatoides, commonly known as the cristate cactus, is a species native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its short, thick stems that often branch out into a tree-like structure. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom during the summer months. Its leaves are modified into spines, which help reduce water loss in its arid environment.

Notocactus cristatoides is a slow-growing cactus that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.
