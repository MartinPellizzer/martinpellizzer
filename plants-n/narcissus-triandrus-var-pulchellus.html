Narcissus triandrus var. pulchellus

Narcissus triandrus var. pulchellus, commonly known as the Spanish daffodil, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Europe.

It produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically bright yellow with a white or pale green throat. The plant features strap-like, grassy foliage that emerges in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in horticulture.
