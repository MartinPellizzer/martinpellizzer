Neomolina macrocephala

Neomolina macrocephala, commonly known as the giant red hibiscus, is a striking flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 30 cm in diameter, typically displaying vibrant red or pink hues. The plant has thick, waxy leaves that are dark green and often have a velvety texture. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Neomolina macrocephala is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
