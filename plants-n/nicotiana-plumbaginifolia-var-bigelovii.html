Nicotiana plumbaginifolia var. bigelovii

Nicotiana plumbaginifolia var. bigelovii, commonly known as Bigelow's tobacco, is a herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its blue to violet flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has small, lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. It is notable for its use in scientific research, particularly in the study of plant genetics and biochemistry.

Its ornamental value also makes it a popular choice for gardens in suitable climates.
