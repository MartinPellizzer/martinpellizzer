Nepenthes palawanensis

Nepenthes palawanensis, commonly known as the Palawan Pitcher Plant, is a unique carnivorous plant native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its distinctive, hollow, pitcher-shaped leaves that secrete a digestive fluid to trap and digest insects. The plant thrives in humid, tropical environments and is typically found in montane rainforests. Its pitchers vary in color, often displaying shades of green, red, or yellow, depending on light exposure.

This species plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by controlling insect populations and contributing to nutrient cycling.
