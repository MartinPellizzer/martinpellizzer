Notodanthonia lechleri

Notodanthonia lechleri, commonly known as the Lechleri grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America, particularly found in regions of Brazil.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and forest understories.

Its flowering stems bear small, spike-like inflorescences, which produce tiny grains that are dispersed by wind.
