Nothofagus cornuta

Nothofagus cornuta, commonly known as the mountain beech, is a species of evergreen tree native to southern South America.

It belongs to the Nothofagaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, sharp-pointed leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, cone-like fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is typically found in montane forests.

Nothofagus cornuta plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
