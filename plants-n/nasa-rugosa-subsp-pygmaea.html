Nasa rugosa subsp. pygmaea

Nasa rugosa subsp.

pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf rose moss, is a low-growing succulent native to South America. It features dense clusters of small, fleshy leaves that are typically green to reddish in color.

The plant produces tiny, pink or white flowers during the warmer months, adding a delicate floral touch to its appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it ideal for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its compact size and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and container gardening.
