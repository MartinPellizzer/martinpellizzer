Neoglaziovia

Neoglaziovia, commonly known as the "paddle cactus," is a genus of flowering plants native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that are often ribbed and covered in spines. The plants produce clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring. Neoglaziovia species are typically drought-tolerant and thrive in well-draining soil.

They are popular among succulent enthusiasts due to their unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
