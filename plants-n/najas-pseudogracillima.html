Najas pseudogracillima

Najas pseudogracillima, commonly known as the slender water milfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater habitats in North America.

It belongs to the family Najadaceae and is characterized by its finely divided, thread-like leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged, and it reproduces both sexually and vegetatively through rhizomes. Najas pseudogracillima plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

It thrives in slow-moving to still freshwater environments with moderate to high light penetration.
