Neomarica pardina

Neomarica pardina, commonly known as the pink flamingo lily, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence composed of a spadix and a spathe. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often a deep green with a waxy texture. Its flowers are typically pink or white, with a showy spathe that resembles a flamingo's beak, hence its common name.

Neomarica pardina is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant due to its vibrant appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
