Neomitranthes cordifolia

Neomitranthes cordifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf pincushion, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features succulent, fleshy leaves that are heart-shaped, giving it its common name. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, often in the winter months.

Neomitranthes cordifolia is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
