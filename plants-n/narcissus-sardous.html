Narcissus sardous

Narcissus sardous, commonly known as the Sardinian daffodil, is a species of flowering plant native to the island of Sardinia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers with a white throat. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its foliage consists of strap-shaped leaves that emerge in early spring.
