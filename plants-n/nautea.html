Nautea

Nautea, commonly known as the ghost fern, is a rare and delicate fern species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, arching fronds that resemble the shape of a ghostly figure, hence its common name. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in dense tropical forests. Its leaves are typically dark green with a velvety texture, and it produces small, spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its fronds.

Due to its rarity and unique appearance, Nautea is a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists alike.
