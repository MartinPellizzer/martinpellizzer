Nuphar × intermedia f. chlorocephala

Nuphar × intermedia f. chlorocephala, commonly known as the green-headed water lily, is a hybrid plant that exhibits unique botanical characteristics.

It is a perennial aquatic plant with broad, floating leaves that are typically green in color. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers with a distinctive greenish hue at the base, which is a defining feature of this form. It thrives in shallow freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes, where it can anchor itself with its rhizomes.

Its morphology and floral structure make it a notable species in the genus Nuphar, showcasing both ornamental and ecological significance.
