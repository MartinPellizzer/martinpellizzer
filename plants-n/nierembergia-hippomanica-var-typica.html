Nierembergia hippomanica var. typica

Nierembergia hippomanica var. typica, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow tubular flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
