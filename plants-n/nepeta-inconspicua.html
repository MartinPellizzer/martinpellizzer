Nepeta inconspicua

Nepeta inconspicua, commonly known as the slender catmint, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves and produces small, pale blue or violet flowers in whorls along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or open habitats.

Botanically, it belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage.
