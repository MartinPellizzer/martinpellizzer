Nymphaea purpurea

Nymphaea purpurea, commonly known as the purple water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to the Americas.

It features large, showy purple petals that contrast with its white inner sepals, creating a visually appealing bloom. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes. Its leaves are broad and floating, often with a waxy surface that helps repel water.

Nymphaea purpurea plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
