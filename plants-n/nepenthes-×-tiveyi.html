Nepenthes × tiveyi

Nepenthes × tiveyi, commonly known as the hybrid pitcher plant, is a fascinating carnivorous plant that results from the crossbreeding of Nepenthes alata and Nepenthes rafflesiana.

It is native to the Philippines and thrives in humid, tropical environments. The plant is characterized by its large, vibrant green leaves that form striking pitcher-shaped structures. These pitchers secrete a digestive fluid that lures and traps insects, which the plant then absorbs for nutrients.

Its unique morphology and carnivorous habits make it a popular subject in botanical studies and conservation efforts.
