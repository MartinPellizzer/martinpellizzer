Nepeta longituba

Nepeta longituba, commonly known as the tall catmint, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height, covered with fine, hairy foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant blue or purple flowers in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and emit a minty aroma when crushed.

Nepeta longituba is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
