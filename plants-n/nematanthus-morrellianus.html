Nematanthus morrellianus

Nematanthus morrellianus, commonly known as the Morrell's whorled morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from deep red to orange and yellow. The plant produces tendrils that help it climb and attach to supports, forming dense, vine-like growth. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its unique appearance.

This species is valued in horticulture for its vibrant blooms and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.
