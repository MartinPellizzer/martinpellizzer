Neoporteria reichei f. malleolata

Neoporteria reichei f. malleolata, commonly known as the silver-leafed honeysuckle, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, silver-gray leaves that are densely covered with fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically in shades of yellow or white, which bloom in early spring. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, with deep roots that help it access water from deeper soil layers.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil and full sun.
