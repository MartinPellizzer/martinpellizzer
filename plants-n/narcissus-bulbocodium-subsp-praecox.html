Narcissus bulbocodium subsp. praecox

Narcissus bulbocodium subsp.

praecox, commonly known as the early bulbocodium daffodil, is a species native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its compact, bulbous form and early blooming habit, typically flowering in late winter or early spring.

The plant produces small, fragrant flowers with a yellow perianth and a central corona, typical of the Amaryllidaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or woodland borders.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is a sought-after plant among horticultural enthusiasts.
