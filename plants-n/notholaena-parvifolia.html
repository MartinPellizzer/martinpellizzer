Notholaena parvifolia

Notholaena parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved rock fern, is a species of fern native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in rocky or woodland soils. Its fronds are usually clustered at the base, forming a low, compact growth habit.

Notholaena parvifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
