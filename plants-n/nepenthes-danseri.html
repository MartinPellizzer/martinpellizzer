Nepenthes danseri

Nepenthes danseri, commonly known as the Sulu pitcher plant, is a tropical climbing vine native to the Sulu Archipelago in the Philippines.

It is characterized by its large, waxy, pitcher-shaped leaves that secrete a nutrient-rich digestive fluid to trap and digest insects. The plant's pitchers vary in color, often displaying shades of green, red, and yellow, and can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. Nepenthes danseri thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires high levels of light for optimal growth.

Due to its limited natural range and habitat destruction, it is considered endangered and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
