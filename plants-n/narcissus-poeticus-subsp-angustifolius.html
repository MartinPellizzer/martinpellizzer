Narcissus poeticus subsp. angustifolius

Narcissus poeticus subsp.

angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved daffodil, is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family. It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and bright yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or meadow habitats. It blooms in early spring, producing showy inflorescences that attract pollinators.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
