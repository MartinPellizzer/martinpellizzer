Nechamandra

Nechamandra, commonly known as the Indian hibiscus, is a flowering plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Asia, including India, and thrives in warm, humid climates. The plant features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep red, often with a yellow or orange throat. Its leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and have a rough texture.

Nechamandra is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
