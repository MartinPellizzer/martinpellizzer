Nipponocalamus multifolius

Nipponocalamus multifolius, commonly known as the Japanese feather bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and feathery, multi-branched leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests and stream sides. Its foliage is often used in traditional Japanese landscaping for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in humid conditions.

Nipponocalamus multifolius is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in stabilizing soil in its natural habitat.
