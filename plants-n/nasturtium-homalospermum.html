Nasturtium homalospermum

Nasturtium homalospermum, commonly known as the desert nasturtium, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange to yellow flowers and heart-shaped leaves, which are often covered in a fine, fuzzy texture. The plant produces small, round seed pods that contain several seeds, which are used in traditional herbal medicine. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it well adapted to dry environments.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for both ecological and ornamental purposes in arid landscapes.
