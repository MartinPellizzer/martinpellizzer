Niphogeton boliviana

Niphogeton boliviana, commonly known as the Bolivian water plant, is a species of aquatic plant native to South America.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds and marshes. The plant has elongated, ribbon-like leaves that float on the water surface, aiding in photosynthesis. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, usually appearing in clusters near the water's edge.

Niphogeton boliviana plays a role in maintaining water quality by absorbing excess nutrients and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
