Nepeta scorodonia

Nepeta scorodonia, commonly known as catmint, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in herbal teas and have a mild minty scent.

Nepeta scorodonia is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.
