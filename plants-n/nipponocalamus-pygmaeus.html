Nipponocalamus pygmaeus

Nipponocalamus pygmaeus, commonly known as the dwarf bamboo, is a small, clumping bamboo species native to Japan.

It features slender, hollow stems that grow in dense clusters, typically reaching a height of only a few centimeters. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are dark green and often have a slight sheen. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is well-suited for rock gardens or as a decorative ground cover.

Its compact size and elegant appearance make it a popular choice for horticultural displays.
