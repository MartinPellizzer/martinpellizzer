Nothoscordum grossibulbum

Nothoscordum grossibulbum, commonly known as the broadbulb garlic.

It is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile. The plant is characterized by its rosette of fleshy, garlic-scented leaves and produces clusters of yellow flowers. It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The plant is also noted for its potential medicinal properties.
