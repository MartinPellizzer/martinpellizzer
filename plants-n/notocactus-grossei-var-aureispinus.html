Notocactus grossei var. aureispinus

Notocactus grossei var. aureispinus, commonly known as the golden spined notocactus, is a unique cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that can grow up to several feet in height. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its green foliage. Its spines are typically golden or yellow, giving it its common name.

This variety is well-suited for xerophytic gardens due to its low water requirements and ability to thrive in full sun.
