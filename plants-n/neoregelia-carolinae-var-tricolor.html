Neoregelia carolinae var. tricolor

Neoregelia carolinae var. tricolor, commonly known as the Tricolor Bucephalandra, is a striking bromeliad native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, multicolored foliage, featuring shades of green, red, and orange in a unique gradient. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its rosette-shaped leaves collect water, creating a microhabitat for small aquatic organisms.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements in cultivation.
