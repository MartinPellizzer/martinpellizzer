Nabalus alatus

Nabalus alatus, commonly known as the winged balm, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its distinctive wing-like structures on its stems, which give it its common name. Its small, tubular flowers are typically blue or purple and bloom in summer.

Nabalus alatus is often used in traditional medicine for its purported soothing and anti-inflammatory properties.
