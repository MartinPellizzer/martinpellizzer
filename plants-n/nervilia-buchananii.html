Nervilia buchananii

Nervilia buchananii, commonly known as the golden ground orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the soil.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
