Nymphaea ondinea subsp. ondinea

Nymphaea ondinea subsp.

ondinea, commonly known as the African water lily, is a aquatic plant native to freshwater habitats in Africa. It is characterized by its large, floating leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink.

The plant has a broad, heart-shaped leaf structure with a waxy surface that helps repel water. Its roots are anchored in the mud at the bottom of ponds and lakes, while its stems are long and flexible.

This species plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
