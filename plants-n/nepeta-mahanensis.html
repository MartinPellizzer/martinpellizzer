Nepeta mahanensis

Nepeta mahanensis, commonly known as Persian catnip, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Iran.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically lavender or violet in color. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported calming and therapeutic properties.

Nepeta mahanensis is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its historical use in herbal remedies.
