Nama dichotoma var. amplifolia

Nama dichotoma var. amplifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf nama, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, branching stems and large, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle. The plant is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its leaves and stems to survive drought conditions. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer, adding a subtle ornamental value.

This variety is valued for its resilience and is often used in rock gardens or xeriscaping.
