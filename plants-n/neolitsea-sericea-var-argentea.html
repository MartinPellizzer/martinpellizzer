Neolitsea sericea var. argentea

Neolitsea sericea var. argentea, commonly known as silver flame tree, is a variant of the Neolitsea sericea species, native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking silver-white to pale green leaves that are thinly covered with a fine, silky hair, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by small, round fruits. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its elegant foliage and seasonal blooms.

This variant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.
