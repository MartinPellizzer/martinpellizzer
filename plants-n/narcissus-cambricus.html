Narcissus cambricus

Narcissus cambricus, commonly known as the Welsh windflower, is a species of daffodil native to the United Kingdom.

It is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that are typically yellow with a white or green throat. The plant grows from a bulb and prefers well-drained, sandy soils in open, sunny habitats. It blooms in early spring, often before the leaves fully emerge.

The botanical features of Narcissus cambricus make it a distinctive and valuable component of UK flora.
