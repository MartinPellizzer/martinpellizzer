Narcissus bulbocodium subsp. leonensis

Narcissus bulbocodium subsp.

leonensis, commonly known as the Leonese daffodil, is a rare and distinctive member of the Amaryllidaceae family. It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and a single, showy flower with a yellow perianth and a white corona.

Native to the Iberian Peninsula, this subspecies thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and full sun. Its bulbs are adapted to survive in harsh, arid conditions, often remaining dormant during dry periods.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is a subject of interest in botanical conservation efforts.
