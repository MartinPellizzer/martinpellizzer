Nymphoides coreana

Nymphoides coreana, commonly known as the Korean water lily, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves and showy white or pink flowers that bloom above the water surface. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats, such as ponds and marshes. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

Nymphoides coreana is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been introduced to various regions for aquatic gardening.
