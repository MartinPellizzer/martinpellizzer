Nerine × amabilis

Nerine × amabilis, commonly known as the hybrid nerine or the pink nerine, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Nerine sarniensis and Nerine echinata, combining traits from both parents. The plant produces striking, showy flowers that are typically pink or white, with six petals and a prominent central stamen. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its foliage consists of strap-like leaves that are dark green and evergreen, adding visual interest throughout the year.
