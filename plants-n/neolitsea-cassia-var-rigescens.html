Neolitsea cassia var. rigescens

Neolitsea cassia var. rigescens, commonly known as the fragrant flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are alternate and often have a fragrant aroma. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in the spring. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and floral display.
