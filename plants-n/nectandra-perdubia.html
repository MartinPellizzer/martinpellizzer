Nectandra perdubia

Nectandra perdubia, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, reddish-brown heartwood. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptic in shape and often have a glossy surface. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by oval, woody fruits containing a single seed.

Due to its valuable timber, Nectandra perdubia is also considered an endangered species in many regions.
