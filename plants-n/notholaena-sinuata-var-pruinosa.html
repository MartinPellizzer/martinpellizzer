Notholaena sinuata var. pruinosa

Notholaena sinuata var. pruinosa, commonly known as the pruinose feathermoss, is a variant of the feathermoss genus Notholaena.

It is characterized by its dense, cushion-like growth habit and finely divided, needle-like leaves. The leaves are typically covered in a fine, powdery bloom, giving the plant a silvery appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest floors or along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in stabilizing soil in moist habitats.
