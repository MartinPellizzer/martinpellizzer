Nidularium bracteatum

Nidularium bracteatum, commonly known as the velvetleaf orchid, is a tropical plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its dark green, velvety leaves and produces striking, colorful flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is native to regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, warm environments. Its unique floral structure includes a spurred lip, which is a distinguishing feature of many orchids.

Nidularium bracteatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable growing conditions.
