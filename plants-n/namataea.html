Namataea

Namataea, commonly known as the ghost fern, is a rare and delicate fern species native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, arching fronds that resemble ghostly silhouettes against the forest floor. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires consistent moisture and rich, well-draining soil. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of its leaves.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Namataea is considered a rare and ecologically significant plant.
