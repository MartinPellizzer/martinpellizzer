Narcissus × villasrubiensis var. maurensis

Narcissus × villasrubiensis var. maurensis, commonly known as the Maurensis Daffodil, is a hybrid daffodil species characterized by its distinctive floral morphology.

It features a trumpet-shaped corona that is typically longer than the tepals, giving it a striking appearance. The plant is native to the Iberian Peninsula and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. It blooms in early spring, producing bright yellow flowers that attract pollinators.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant display.
