Nasturtium montanum var. obtusulum

Nasturtium montanum var. obtusulum, commonly known as the mountain nasturtium, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, glossy leaves and vibrant orange or yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in rocky or alpine environments. Its edible flowers and leaves are used in culinary applications, adding a peppery flavor to dishes.

The species is also valued for its ornamental beauty and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
