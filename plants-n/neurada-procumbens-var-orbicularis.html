Neurada procumbens var. orbicularis

Neurada procumbens var. orbicularis, commonly known as the broadleaf groundsel, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features a sprawling growth habit with fleshy, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which bloom in the spring and early summer. Its botanical characteristics include a fibrous root system and a preference for moist, well-drained soils.

It is often found in woodland edges and disturbed habitats, contributing to local biodiversity.
