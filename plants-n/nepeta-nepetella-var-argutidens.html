Nepeta nepetella var. argutidens

Nepeta nepetella var. argutidens, commonly known as the narrow-leaved catmint, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features upright, slender stems covered in dense, gray-green foliage with narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pale blue to lavender flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its aromatic leaves and flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
