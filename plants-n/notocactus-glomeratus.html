Notocactus glomeratus

Notocactus glomeratus, commonly known as the clustered notocactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that often form dense clusters. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months, which are arranged in dense, globular clusters at the ends of the stems. It is adapted to arid environments and requires well-draining soil and minimal water.

Notocactus glomeratus is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a houseplant.
