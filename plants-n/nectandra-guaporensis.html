Nectandra guaporensis

Nectandra guaporensis, commonly known as the Guaporensis Sandarac Tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon Basin.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its aromatic, dark-colored wood. The tree produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by ellipsoid berries containing a single seed. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a rough texture with a strong, spicy scent.

The species is valued for its durable timber and has traditional uses in local communities.
