Notocactus purpureus

Notocactus purpureus, commonly known as the purple-cushion cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and dense covering of spines. The plant produces vibrant purple flowers during the warmer months, which attract pollinators like bees and hummingbirds. Its succulent stems store water, allowing it to thrive in harsh, dry environments.

Notocactus purpureus is well-suited for cultivation in rocky or sandy soils with minimal water requirements.
