Nectandra miranda

Nectandra miranda, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark-colored timber. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. Its flowers are small and clustered, with a sweet, fragrant aroma.

Nectandra miranda is valued for its hard, durable wood and is often found in lowland rainforests.
