Noccaea granatensis

Noccaea granatensis, commonly known as the Granada watercress, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant is characterized by its rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small white or pale yellow flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near streams or in wet meadows.

Noccaea granatensis is valued for its peppery flavor and is used in various culinary and medicinal applications.
