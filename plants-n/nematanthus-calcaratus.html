Nematanthus calcaratus

Nematanthus calcaratus, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that resemble orchids, with vivid colors ranging from red to orange. The plant produces small, fleshy leaves that help it survive in arid conditions. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect sunlight.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in rock gardens and containers.
