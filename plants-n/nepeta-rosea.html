Nepeta rosea

Nepeta rosea, commonly known as rose campion, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant produces clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Nepeta rosea is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and pleasant fragrance.
