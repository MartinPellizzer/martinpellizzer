Nepeta graciliflora

Nepeta graciliflora, commonly known as slenderflower catmint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant produces small, pale blue to lavender flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often fragrant and have a mint-like scent, which contributes to its popularity in herbal gardens.

Nepeta graciliflora is valued for its ornamental beauty and traditional use in herbal medicine.
