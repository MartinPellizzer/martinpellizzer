Neoabbottia demissa

Neoabbottia demissa, commonly known as the slender neoabbottia, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, grass-like foliage and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are fibrous and help anchor it to its substrate, while its roots absorb nutrients from the surrounding soil.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
