Narcissus × odorus subsp. trilobus

Narcissus × odorus subsp.

trilobus, commonly known as the fragrant daffodil, is a hybrid species within the Amaryllidaceae family. It is characterized by its trumpet-shaped, fragrant flowers that typically range in color from yellow to white.

The plant produces narrow, strap-like leaves and is native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and distinctive scent.

This subspecies is known for its resilience and ability to thrive in well-drained soil.
