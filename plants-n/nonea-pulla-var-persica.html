Nonea pulla var. persica

Nonea pulla var. persica, commonly known as the Persian rose or Persian carnation, is a flowering plant native to the regions of Persia and parts of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and grayish in appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and containers.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its delicate, fragrant blooms.
