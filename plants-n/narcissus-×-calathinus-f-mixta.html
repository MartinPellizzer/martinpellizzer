Narcissus × calathinus f. mixta

Narcissus × calathinus f. mixta, commonly known as the Mixed Crown Daffodil, is a hybrid cultivar that exhibits a unique combination of traits from its parent species.

Botanically, it belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, multi-layered flowers that display a mix of colors, typically yellow and white. The plant produces narrow, strap-like leaves and has a short, sturdy stem that supports its showy blooms. It is typically grown in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking vibrant spring color.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and floral arrangements.
