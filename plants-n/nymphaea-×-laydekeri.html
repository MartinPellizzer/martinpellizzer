Nymphaea × laydekeri

Nymphaea × laydekeri, commonly known as the yellow water lily, is a hybrid aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features broad, waxy, yellow flowers that bloom above the water surface, often with a white or pinkish center. The plant has floating leaves that are heart-shaped and covered in a thick, waxy cuticle to repel water. Its roots are anchored in the mud at the bottom of still water bodies.

Nymphaea × laydekeri is often cultivated in ponds and water gardens for its ornamental value.
