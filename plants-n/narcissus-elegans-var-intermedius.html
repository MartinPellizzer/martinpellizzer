Narcissus elegans var. intermedius

Narcissus elegans var. intermedius, commonly known as the intermediate daffodil, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, strap-like leaves and a single, trumpet-shaped flower that is typically yellow in color. The plant blooms in early spring, often before the leaves fully develop. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile addition to gardens.

This variety is valued for its elegant appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
