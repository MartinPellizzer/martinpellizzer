Nepeta altissima

Nepeta altissima, commonly known as catmint, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in herbal teas and have a mild minty scent.

Nepeta altissima is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
