Neogaya simplex var. albomarginata

Neogaya simplex var. albomarginata, commonly known as the white-margin nettle, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family, which includes stinging nettles, and is characterized by its finely serrated leaves. The plant features pale green, lance-shaped leaves with distinct white margins, giving it its common name. It produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, typical of the genus Neogaya.

This variant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.
