Nepeta buhsei

Nepeta buhsei, commonly known as catmint, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their calming properties.

Nepeta buhsei is also popular in gardens for its ornamental value and pleasant fragrance.
