Nepeta numantina

Nepeta numantina, commonly known as the mountain catmint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

It features aromatic, hairy leaves that are typically gray-green in color, and it produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a hardy addition to gardens. Its foliage is often used in herbal remedies, and it attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Nepeta numantina is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
