Nemesia ramosa

Nemesia ramosa, commonly known as the branching nemesia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, white, and red. The plant features delicate, hairy leaves that are opposite and often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for hanging baskets and rock gardens.

Nemesia ramosa is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
