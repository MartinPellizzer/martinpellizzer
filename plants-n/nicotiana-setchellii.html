Nicotiana setchellii

Nicotiana setchellii, commonly known as the Setchell's tobacco, is a species native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a herbaceous plant with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The flowers are typically blue or purple, with five petals and a distinctive spurred shape. It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is closely related to other tobacco species.

This plant is notable for its ornamental value and its role in the biodiversity of Hawaii's unique flora.
