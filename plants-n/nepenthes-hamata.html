Nepenthes hamata

Nepenthes hamata, commonly known as the hairy pitcher plant, is a tropical climbing vine native to Borneo.

It is characterized by its distinctive, waxy, pitcher-shaped leaves that secrete a digestive fluid to trap and digest insects. The plant's unique morphology includes long, hairy tendrils that aid in climbing and anchoring to its surroundings. Its pitchers are typically red or orange in color, with a hairy rim that may deter some herbivores.

Nepenthes hamata is a popular species among carnivorous plant enthusiasts due to its striking appearance and specialized adaptations.
