Nepenthes boschiana var. sumatrana

Nepenthes boschiana var. sumatrana, commonly known as the Sumatran pitcher plant, is a tropical carnivorous plant native to the rainforests of Sumatra.

It is characterized by its large, tubular leaf pitchers that secrete a digestive fluid to trap and digest insects. The plant's pitchers are typically green or red in color, with a waxy coating that helps retain moisture. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires consistent moisture and nutrient-rich soil.

This variant is notable for its adaptability and is often cultivated for its unique appearance and ecological significance.
