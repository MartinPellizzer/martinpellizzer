Nyctanthes sambac

Nyctanthes sambac, commonly known as night jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant white flowers that bloom at night, emitting a sweet, jasmine-like scent. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellow fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

Nyctanthes sambac is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
