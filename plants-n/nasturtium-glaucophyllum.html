Nasturtium glaucophyllum

Nasturtium glaucophyllum, commonly known as the glaucous nasturtium, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its bright orange to yellow flowers and heart-shaped leaves with a waxy, bluish-green coating. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers. Its flowers are edible and have a peppery flavor, often used in culinary applications.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
