Najas marina var. angustissima

Najas marina var. angustissima, commonly known as narrow-leaved watermilfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It belongs to the family Najadaceae and is characterized by its slender, ribbon-like leaves that are typically less than 1 millimeter wide. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences, often submerged or just below the water surface. It thrives in shallow, slow-moving waters and plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Its rapid growth can also lead to dense vegetation that may impact water flow and quality if left unchecked.
