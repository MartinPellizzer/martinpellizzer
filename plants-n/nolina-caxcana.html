Nolina caxcana

Nolina caxcana, commonly known as the Mexican spineless agave, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of thick, fleshy, spineless leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces a tall flowering stalk that bears small, white flowers, which are followed by edible, fleshy fruits. Nolina caxcana is well adapted to drought conditions, making it a hardy plant suitable for xeriscaping.

Its slow growth rate and unique appearance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.
