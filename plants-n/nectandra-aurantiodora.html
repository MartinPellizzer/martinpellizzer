Nectandra aurantiodora

Nectandra aurantiodora, commonly known as golden sand-wood, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its aromatic, reddish-brown heartwood. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are often elliptical in shape. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, elongated fruits containing aromatic seeds.

Due to its unique properties, it is valued in traditional medicine and as a source of valuable timber.
