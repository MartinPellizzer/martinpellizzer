Nymphoides × montana

Nymphoides × montana, commonly known as the hybrid water hyacinth, is a semi-aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features floating leaves that are ovate to elliptical in shape, with a waxy surface and a prominent midrib. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a pleasant fragrance. Its roots are fibrous and help stabilize the water's surface, while its rapid growth makes it an effective natural water filter.

However, it can become invasive in non-native environments if not managed carefully.
