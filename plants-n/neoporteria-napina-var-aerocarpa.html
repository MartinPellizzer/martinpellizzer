Neoporteria napina var. aerocarpa

Neoporteria napina var. aerocarpa, commonly known as the mountain snowbell, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the western United States.

The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, providing a striking contrast to the white blossoms.
