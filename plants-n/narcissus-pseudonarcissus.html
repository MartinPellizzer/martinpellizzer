Narcissus pseudonarcissus

Narcissus pseudonarcissus, commonly known as the wild daffodil, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers and strap-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and grasslands, preferring well-drained soil. It blooms in early spring, often before the leaves fully emerge.

The species is part of the Amaryllidaceae family and is known for its ornamental value and historical significance in horticulture.
