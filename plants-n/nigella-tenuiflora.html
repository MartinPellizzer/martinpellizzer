Nigella tenuiflora

Nigella tenuiflora, commonly known as love-in-a-mist, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces delicate, blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a long history of use in traditional medicine. Its seeds, which are small and black, are also used in cooking and are known for their pungent aroma.

Nigella tenuiflora thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
