Nepeta humilis

Nepeta humilis, commonly known as carpathian catmint, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Western Asia.

It features slender stems and small, fragrant blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has silvery-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. Nepeta humilis is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

It prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it adaptable to various garden conditions.
