Nouhuysia elaeocarpoides

Nouhuysia elaeocarpoides, commonly known as the red-flowered hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from red to pink, often with a yellow throat. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually ovate to lanceolate in shape. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Nouhuysia elaeocarpoides is often cultivated for its ornamental value in warm climates.
