Najas monosperma

Najas monosperma, commonly known as the single-seeded waterweed, is a aquatic plant belonging to the family Najadaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems that float on the water surface or hang submerged. The plant produces small, green, oval-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. Its flowers are tiny, unisexual, and borne in clusters at the base of the leaves.

Najas monosperma plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and oxygen to water organisms.
