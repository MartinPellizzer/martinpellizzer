Neogaya nemorosa

Neogaya nemorosa, commonly known as the forest bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and woodland edges. The plant features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, white or pale blue flowers. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Neogaya nemorosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with damp soil conditions.
