Nymphaea speciosa

Nymphaea speciosa, commonly known as the sacred water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can range in color from white to pink and even red, depending on the variety. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes, where it forms dense floating mats. Its leaves are broad and rounded, often with a waxy surface that repels water, while its roots anchor it in the muddy substrate.

Nymphaea speciosa plays an important ecological role by providing habitat for aquatic life and contributing to water filtration.
