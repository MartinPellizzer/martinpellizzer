Nierembergia hippomanica var. pinifolioides

Nierembergia hippomanica var. pinifolioides, commonly known as the needle-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to purple. The leaves are needle-like and grass-like, giving the plant its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
