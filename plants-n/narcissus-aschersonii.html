Narcissus aschersonii

Narcissus aschersonii, commonly known as the double daffodil, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers with a large, layered corona. The plant produces narrow, strap-like leaves and has a bulbous root system typical of the narcissus genus. It blooms in early spring, often before the leaves fully emerge, and is cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers are typically yellow or white, with a distinctive, recurved petal structure.
