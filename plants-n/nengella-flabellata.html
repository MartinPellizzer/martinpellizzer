Nengella flabellata

Nengella flabellata, commonly known as the flabellate nengella, is a unique member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped leaves and striking, fragrant flowers that resemble small, folded fans. Its blooming period typically occurs during the rainy season, adding to its ornamental value.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
