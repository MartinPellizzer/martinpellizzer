Narcissus pseudonarcissus subsp. eugeniae

Narcissus pseudonarcissus subsp.

eugeniae, commonly known as the Spanish daffodil, is a subspecies native to southern Europe, particularly Spain and Portugal. It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically bright yellow with a white or greenish throat.

The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, linear leaves and produces a single stem with one to several flower buds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and is commonly cultivated in gardens for its early spring bloom.
