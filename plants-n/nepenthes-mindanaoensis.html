Nepenthes mindanaoensis

Nepenthes mindanaoensis, commonly known as the Mindanao pitcher plant, is a species of tropical carnivorous plant native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, and often red-tinged pitcher-shaped leaves that trap and digest insects. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in montane rainforests. Its nectar-producing rim attracts prey, while its hollow interior secretes digestive enzymes.

This species plays a unique role in its ecosystem by contributing to nutrient cycling in nutrient-poor soils.
