Nymphaea × rubrodisca

Nymphaea × rubrodisca, commonly known as the red-flowered water lily, is a hybrid aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves and striking, vibrant red flowers that bloom above the water surface. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes. Its flowers typically open in the morning and close in the late afternoon or evening.

Nymphaea × rubrodisca is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in water gardens and ponds for its aesthetic appeal.
