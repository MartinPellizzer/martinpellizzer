Nemacladus ramosissimus

Nemacladus ramosissimus, commonly known as the hairy greenbriar, is a perennial shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in clusters, which are followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native plant communities and a subject of ecological study.
