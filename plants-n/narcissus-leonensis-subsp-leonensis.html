Narcissus leonensis subsp. leonensis

Narcissus leonensis subsp.

leonensis, commonly known as the Leonese daffodil, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Europe. It produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers with a yellow corona and white petals, typically blooming in early spring.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is adapted to grow in well-drained soils. It is often found in open woodlands and rocky areas, thriving in temperate climates.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant spring display.
