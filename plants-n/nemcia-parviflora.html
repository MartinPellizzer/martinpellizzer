Nemcia parviflora

Nemcia parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered nemcia, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. The plant features small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and early summer. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Nemcia parviflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
