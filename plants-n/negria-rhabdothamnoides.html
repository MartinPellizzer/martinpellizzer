Negria rhabdothamnoides

Negria rhabdothamnoides, commonly known as the narrow-leafed thornbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, spiny stems and small, narrow leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss. The plant produces yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, typically blooming during the rainy season. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access groundwater in dry conditions.

Negria rhabdothamnoides plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for local wildlife.
