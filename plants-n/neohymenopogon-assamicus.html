Neohymenopogon assamicus

Neohymenopogon assamicus, commonly known as the Assam grass, is a perennial herb native to the eastern Himalayas and parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are small, compact flower clusters.

Neohymenopogon assamicus is often used in traditional medicine and has ecological significance in its native habitat.
