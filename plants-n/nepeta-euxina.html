Nepeta euxina

Nepeta euxina, commonly known as catmint, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are often used in herbal remedies and have a mild minty scent.

Nepeta euxina is also popular in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
