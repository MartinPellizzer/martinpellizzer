Nasa insignis

Nasa insignis, commonly known as the moonflower, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night and close during the day. The plant produces showy, lantern-shaped blooms that are often used in horticultural displays due to their striking appearance. Nasa insignis is a perennial shrub that thrives in cool, moist environments with partial shade.

Its botanical features make it a unique and sought-after ornamental plant in gardens and botanical collections.
