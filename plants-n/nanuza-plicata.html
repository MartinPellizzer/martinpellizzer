Nanuza plicata

Nanuza plicata, commonly known as the pleated nanuza, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive pleated leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically in shades of white and pink. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some botanical gardens and private collections.
