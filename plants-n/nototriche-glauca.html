Nototriche glauca

Nototriche glauca, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its blue-gray foliage and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are often covered in a fine, powdery bloom that gives them a distinctive bluish sheen.

Nototriche glauca is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
