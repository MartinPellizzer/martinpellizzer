Nephrodium serrulatum

Nephrodium serrulatum, commonly known as the serrated nephroid, is a rare fern native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Nephrodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like fronds with serrated edges. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as mountain forests or rocky crevices. Its reproductive structures include sporangia that release spores for propagation.

The species is noted for its unique morphology and ecological significance in its native habitat.
