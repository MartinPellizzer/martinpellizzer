Nicandra amara

Nicandra amara, commonly known as nightshade or bitter apple, is a member of the Solanaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of the Americas and features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are often green when unripe and turn red when mature. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

The plant contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially harmful if ingested.
