Narcissus integer

Narcissus integer, commonly known as the double pheasant's eye, is a species of daffodil native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers with a white corona and yellow petals. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. Its bulbs are perennial and thrive in well-drained soil.

Narcissus integer is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
