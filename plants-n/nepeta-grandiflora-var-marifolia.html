Nepeta grandiflora var. marifolia

Nepeta grandiflora var. marifolia, commonly known as the mountain catmint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features aromatic, hairy leaves that are often bluish-green in color, and it produces spikes of pale blue to lavender flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders. Its flowering period typically occurs in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and mild mint-like fragrance.
