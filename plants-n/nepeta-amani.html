Nepeta amani

Nepeta amani, commonly known as Amani catmint, is a flowering plant native to Tanzania.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant features square, hairy stems and opposite leaves that are often grayish-green in color. Its small, tubular flowers are typically blue or purple, arranged in whorls along the stem.

Nepeta amani is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
