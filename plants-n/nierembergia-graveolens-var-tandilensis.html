Nierembergia graveolens var. tandilensis

Nierembergia graveolens var. tandilensis, commonly known as the Tandil bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy or well-drained soils. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, fragrant blooms that attract pollinators.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
