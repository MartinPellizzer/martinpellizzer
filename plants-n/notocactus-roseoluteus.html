Notocactus roseoluteus

Notocactus roseoluteus, commonly known as the roseolate cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are often covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hair. The plant produces vibrant pink to red flowers during the warmer months, which are typically borne in clusters at the stem tips. Its succulent nature allows it to store water efficiently, making it well adapted to dry environments.

The plant's unique appearance and ornamental value make it a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts.
