Narcissus jonquilla var. henriquesii

Narcissus jonquilla var. henriquesii, commonly known as the Henriques' daffodil, is a rare and ornamental plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces delicate, funnel-shaped flowers in shades of yellow. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in rocky or coastal environments. It is valued for its unique floral display and is cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare species requiring specific growing conditions.
