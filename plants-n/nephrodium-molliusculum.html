Nephrodium molliusculum

Nephrodium molliusculum, commonly known as the velvet fern, is a unique aquatic plant characterized by its soft, moss-like appearance.

It thrives in shallow, still waters and is often found in wetland environments. The plant's structure consists of a central stem with dense, velvety clusters of tiny, overlapping leaves. It lacks true roots and instead absorbs nutrients through its leaf surfaces.

Nephrodium molliusculum plays a role in stabilizing sediment and providing habitat for aquatic microorganisms.
