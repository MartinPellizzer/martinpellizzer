Neuropeltis acuminata

Neuropeltis acuminata, commonly known as the slender swamp orchid, is a species of orchid native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in wet, marshy habitats and is often found in lowland areas with high humidity. Its root system is adapted to waterlogged soils, and it plays a role in the ecological balance of its native wetlands.

The orchid is relatively rare and is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and habitat requirements.
