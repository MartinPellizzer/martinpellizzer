Nicotiana rustica var. asiatica

Nicotiana rustica var. asiatica, commonly known as the Indian tobacco, is a species of flowering plant native to Asia.

It is a member of the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant contains significant levels of nicotine, making it both a traditional medicinal plant and a source of tobacco. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its leaves, which are used in various cultural and ritualistic practices.

Its botanical features make it an important plant in both ecological and ethnobotanical contexts.
