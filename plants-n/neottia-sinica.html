Neottia sinica

Neottia sinica, commonly known as the Chinese twayblade, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to China.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and meadows. The plant features a slender, unbranched stem and two opposite, narrow leaves. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, with a distinctive lip that is often marked with purple or brown spots.

Neottia sinica is known for its ephemeral blooming period, which occurs in late spring to early summer.
