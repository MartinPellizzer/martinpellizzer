Nectandra megaphylla

Nectandra megaphylla, commonly known as the large-leafed nectandra, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes other aromatic plants like cinnamon and bay laurel. The tree is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves that are often glossy and dark green in color. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically appearing in panicles, and it produces small, fleshy fruits that are yellow when ripe.

The plant is valued for its aromatic wood and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
