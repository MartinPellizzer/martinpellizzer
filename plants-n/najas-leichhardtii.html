Najas leichhardtii

Najas leichhardtii, commonly known as the narrowleaf waterweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Najadaceae and is characterized by its slender, ribbon-like leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually borne in clusters near the water surface. It thrives in freshwater environments such as lakes, rivers, and ponds, often forming dense underwater vegetation.

Najas leichhardtii plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
