Neottia convallarioides

Neottia convallarioides, commonly known as the lady's tresses orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to various regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and a long, spiraled inflorescence of pale green to white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its flowers are pollinated by insects, and it reproduces through seeds or by vegetative propagation.

Neottia convallarioides is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological role in supporting biodiversity.
