Nematoceras acuminatum

Nematoceras acuminatum, commonly known as the slender clubmoss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, branching stems that are typically less than 30 centimeters in height. The plant produces small, scale-like leaves and possesses strobila, which are cone-like structures that bear sporangia for spore production. Nematoceras acuminatum thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Its reproductive structures are essential for its propagation and ecological role in nutrient cycling within its habitat.
