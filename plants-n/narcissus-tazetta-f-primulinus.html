Narcissus tazetta f. primulinus

Narcissus tazetta f. primulinus, commonly known as the golden daffodil, is a fragrant, bulbous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It produces showy, yellow flowers with a trumpet-shaped corona, often featuring a subtle greenish or yellowish hue. The plant typically blooms in early spring, adding vibrant color to gardens and natural landscapes. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both horticultural and ecological studies.
