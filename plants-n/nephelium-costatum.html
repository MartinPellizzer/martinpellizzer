Nephelium costatum

Nephelium costatum, commonly known as the carambola or starfruit, is a tropical fruit-bearing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive star-shaped fruit, which turns yellow when ripe. The plant is a small evergreen tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, with simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptical in shape. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The fruit is not only prized for its flavor but also for its nutritional value, containing vitamin C and antioxidants.
