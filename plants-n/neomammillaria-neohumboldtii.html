Neomammillaria neohumboldtii

Neomammillaria neohumboldtii, commonly known as the Humboldt pincushion cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are covered in dense, white to pale green spines. The plant produces pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for desert environments.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts.
