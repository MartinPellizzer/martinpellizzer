Narcissus moschatus var. longiflorus

Narcissus moschatus var. longiflorus, commonly known as the musk daffodil, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically yellow with a white or green center, blooming in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces a strong, musky fragrance, which is more pronounced in the variety longiflorus. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The flowers are also known for their historical use in perfumery and as a source of essential oils.
