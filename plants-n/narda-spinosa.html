Narda spinosa

Narda spinosa, commonly known as the spiny narda, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Poaceae, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and dense, wiry foliage. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear pale blue or greenish flowers, which are often clustered in dense, compact spikes. Narda spinosa is adapted to survive in harsh, dry conditions, with its deep root system allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

It is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued as a source of forage for livestock in its native habitat.
