Napeanthus primulifolius

Napeanthus primulifolius, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and woodland edges. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
