Nothofagus perryi

Nothofagus perryi, commonly known as the Perry's southern beech, is a species of evergreen tree native to the temperate rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Nothofagus genus, which is closely related to the beeches of the northern hemisphere. The plant is characterized by its lanceolate, leathery leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by woody, spiky fruits known as samaras.

Nothofagus perryi plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
