Nectandra petenensis

Nectandra petenensis, commonly known as the Maya incense tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the humid lowlands of Central America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, fragrant leaves and small, clustered flowers. The tree produces dark brown, oily seeds that are used in traditional medicine and as a source of incense. Its bark and leaves contain essential oils with antimicrobial properties.

Nectandra petenensis plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
