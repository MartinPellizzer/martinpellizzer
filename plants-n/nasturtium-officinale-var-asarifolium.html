Nasturtium officinale var. asarifolium

Nasturtium officinale var. asarifolium, commonly known as the asarifolium nasturtium, is a variant of the common nasturtium characterized by its distinctive, narrow, fern-like leaves.

It produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of orange and red, which are often used in ornamental gardening. The plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in full sun with well-drained soil. It is a herbaceous perennial that is easy to cultivate and attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its edible leaves and flowers are also used in culinary applications, adding a peppery flavor to salads and dishes.
