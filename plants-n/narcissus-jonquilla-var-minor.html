Narcissus jonquilla var. minor

Narcissus jonquilla var. minor, commonly known as the Spanish daffodil, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe.

It produces small, clustered flowers with white petals and a yellow-orange corona, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is well-suited for rock gardens or as a border plant. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

This variety is noted for its hardiness and ability to naturalize over time.
