Neustanthus phaseoloides var. subspicatus

Neustanthus phaseoloides var. subspicatus, commonly known as the slender legume vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves composed of three small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by elongated, bean-like pods containing several seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and forest edges.

Due to its rapid growth and adaptability, it can become invasive in certain environments.
