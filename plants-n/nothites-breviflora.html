Nothites breviflora

Nothites breviflora, commonly known as the short-flowered nothite, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and small, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant produces small, rounded fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Nothites breviflora thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand salt spray and coastal winds.

It is often found in dune environments and plays a role in stabilizing coastal ecosystems.
