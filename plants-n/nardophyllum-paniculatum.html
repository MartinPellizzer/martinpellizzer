Nardophyllum paniculatum

Nardophyllum paniculatum, commonly known as the alpine lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and clusters of fragrant, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving at high altitudes with cool temperatures and well-drained soil. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming a basal rosette.

Nardophyllum paniculatum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
