Narcissus linnaeanus subsp. papyraceus

Narcissus linnaeanus subsp.

papyraceus, commonly known as the paperwhite narcissus, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family. It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant is native to the Iberian Peninsula and parts of southern France. It typically grows in woodland areas and along stream banks, preferring moist, well-drained soil.

Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its delicate appearance and pleasant scent.
