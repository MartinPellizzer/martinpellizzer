Nasturtium bonariense

Nasturtium bonariense, commonly known as the Brazilian watercress, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae and is characterized by its edible, peppery leaves and bright orange or red flowers. The plant typically grows in moist environments such as marshes and stream banks. It has a sprawling growth habit with hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves.

Nasturtium bonariense is valued for its culinary uses and ornamental appeal.
