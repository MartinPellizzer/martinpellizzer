Najas chinensis

Najas chinensis, commonly known as Chinese waterweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to Asia.

It belongs to the family Najadaceae and is characterized by its whorled arrangement of narrow, ribbon-like leaves. The plant lacks true roots and instead has rhizomes that anchor it in the sediment. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically found in water, and it plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for microorganisms and small aquatic life.

Its rapid growth makes it both a valuable aquatic plant and a potential invasive species in non-native waters.
