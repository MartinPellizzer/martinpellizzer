Narcissus bulbocodium subsp. hedraeanthus

Narcissus bulbocodium subsp.

hedraeanthus, commonly known as the rock daffodil, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It produces elegant, fragrant flowers with six tepals that are typically yellow or white in color.

The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its bulbs are relatively small and are adapted to survive in harsh, arid environments.

It is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental beauty in gardens.
