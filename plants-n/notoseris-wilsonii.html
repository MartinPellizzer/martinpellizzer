Notoseris wilsonii

Notoseris wilsonii, commonly known as Wilson's notoseris, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disc florets. The plant typically grows as a low-growing shrub with hairy, gray-green foliage adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its flowers are small and yellow, often appearing in clusters at the tips of the stems.

Notoseris wilsonii is well-suited to rocky soils and is often found in desert scrub habitats.
