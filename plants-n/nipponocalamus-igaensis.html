Nipponocalamus igaensis

Nipponocalamus igaensis, commonly known as the Iga bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens.

Nipponocalamus igaensis is also studied for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
