Najas arguta var. tenera

Najas arguta var. tenera, commonly known as the slender water nymph, is a floating aquatic plant native to freshwater habitats in Asia and parts of Europe.

It has long, narrow leaves that are typically submerged, with small, inconspicuous flowers that emerge just above the water surface. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving waters with moderate to high light exposure. Its rapid growth rate makes it a common species in ponds and lakes, often forming dense mats on the water's surface.

It plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small invertebrates and serving as a food source for herbivorous fish.
