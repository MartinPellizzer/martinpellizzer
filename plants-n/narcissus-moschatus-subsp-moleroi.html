Narcissus moschatus subsp. moleroi

Narcissus moschatus subsp.

moleroi, commonly known as the Molero narcissus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It produces showy, fragrant flowers with six to eight petals, typically in shades of white to pale yellow.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas. Its bulbs are adapted for storage and can remain dormant for extended periods.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is cultivated in gardens for its early spring blooms.
