Naegelia cymosa

Naegelia cymosa, commonly known as the mountain flax, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in well-drained environments. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces clusters of blue or purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. Naegelia cymosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
