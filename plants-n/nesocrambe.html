Nesocrambe

Nesocrambe, commonly known as the yellow rocket, is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its cruciform flowers.

It is an annual herb that typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and stream banks. The plant features basal leaves that are pinnately lobed and a flowering stem that bears racemose inflorescences. Its flowers are yellow and have four petals, typical of the mustard family.

Nesocrambe is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
