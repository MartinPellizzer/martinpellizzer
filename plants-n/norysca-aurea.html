Norysca aurea

Norysca aurea, commonly known as the golden sunflower, is a striking plant characterized by its vibrant yellow petals and a central disk that resembles a sun.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes many flowering plants with composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in well-drained soil and thrives in full sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes. Its flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Norysca aurea is also noted for its resilience to drought and its ability to add a bright, cheerful touch to any garden setting.
