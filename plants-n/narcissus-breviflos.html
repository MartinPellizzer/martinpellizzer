Narcissus breviflos

Narcissus breviflos, commonly known as the short-flowered daffodil, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and is characterized by its compact, early-blooming flowers. The plant produces narrow, grass-like leaves and has a bulbous root system typical of the narcissus genus. Its flowers are usually yellow with a white or green center, and they bloom in early spring.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and naturalized areas.
