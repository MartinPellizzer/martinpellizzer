Nervilia francoisii

Nervilia francoisii, commonly known as the French lady's tresses, is a rare and delicate orchid species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and dense clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that resemble a tresses of hair. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Nervilia francoisii is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
