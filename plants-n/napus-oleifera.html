Napus oleifera

Napus oleifera, commonly known as the Indian mustard or Brassica napus, is an annual flowering plant in the mustard family.

It is native to Europe and Asia and is cultivated for its seeds, which are used to produce oil. The plant has a robust root system and can grow up to 1.5 meters tall, with large, lobed leaves. Its flowers are typically yellow and clustered in racemes, and it produces long, slender pods containing the seeds.

The plant is well adapted to a variety of soil types and is often grown in temperate climates.
