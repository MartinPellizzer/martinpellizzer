Neocinnamomum atjehense

Neocinnamomum atjehense, commonly known as the Atjeh cinnamon or Sumatra cinnamon, is a species of tree native to the island of Sumatra in Indonesia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark, which contain essential oils. The plant typically grows in lowland to montane forests, preferring moist and shaded environments. Its leaves are elliptical with a glossy surface and emit a distinct cinnamon-like aroma when crushed.

The bark is used in traditional medicine and for flavoring, similar to true cinnamon.
