Nuttallia multicaulis

Nuttallia multicaulis, commonly known as the mountain holly, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at high elevations. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by red berries that are a food source for birds. Its leaves are evergreen, leathery, and have a distinctive shape.

Nuttallia multicaulis is well adapted to cold, arid environments and is often found in alpine and subalpine regions.
