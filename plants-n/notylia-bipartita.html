It is native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant has a distinctive frond structure with a bifurcated or divided form, giving it its common name. Its rhizome is creeping and produces numerous small, lance-shaped leaves.

Notylia bipartita is often found growing on rocks or in forest undergrowth, where it contributes to the biodiversity of its habitat.
