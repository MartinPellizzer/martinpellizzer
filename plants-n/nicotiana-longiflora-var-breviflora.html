Nicotiana longiflora var. breviflora

Nicotiana longiflora var. breviflora, commonly known as the short-flowered tobacco plant, is a species native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its hairy stems and opposite leaves with serrated edges. The flowers are typically blue or violet, with short, broad petals that bloom in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in open woodlands and disturbed sites.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive foliage and flowers.
