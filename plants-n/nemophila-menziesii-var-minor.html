Nemophila menziesii var. minor

Nemophila menziesii var. minor, commonly known as the lesser blue-eyed Mary, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing annual with slender stems and small, showy blue or violet flowers. The plant typically blooms in early spring and is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
