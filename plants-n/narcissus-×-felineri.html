Narcissus × felineri

Narcissus × felineri, commonly known as the Spanish daffodil, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Narcissus tazetta and Narcissus cyclamineus.

It is characterized by its large, showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that typically range in color from yellow to orange. The plant is native to the Iberian Peninsula and thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure. It is a perennial bulbous plant that produces foliage in early spring before the flowers emerge.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
