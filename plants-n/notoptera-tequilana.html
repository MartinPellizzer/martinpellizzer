Notoptera tequilana

Notoptera tequilana, commonly known as the tequila weevil, is a small beetle native to Mexico.

It is primarily associated with the agave plant, particularly the Blue Agave (Agave tequilana), which is used in the production of tequila. The weevil's life cycle involves laying eggs in the agave stem, where the larvae feed on the plant's tissues. Its presence can significantly impact agave cultivation, leading to economic losses for producers.

Botanically, the plant's interaction with this insect highlights the complex relationships between flora and fauna in agroecosystems.
