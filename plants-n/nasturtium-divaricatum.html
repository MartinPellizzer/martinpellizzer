Nasturtium divaricatum

Nasturtium divaricatum, commonly known as the climbing nasturtium, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trailing or climbing growth habit, with slender stems that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces vibrant orange or yellow flowers that are often borne in clusters, and its leaves are heart-shaped with a slightly hairy texture. Nasturtium divaricatum is a member of the Tropaeolaceae family and is known for its edible flowers and leaves, which have a peppery taste.
