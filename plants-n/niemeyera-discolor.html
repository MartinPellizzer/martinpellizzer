Niemeyera discolor

Niemeyera discolor, commonly known as the spotted leaf tree, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, variegated leaves that display a mix of green, white, and sometimes pink or red hues. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, often green or yellow, while its fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental foliage and is often cultivated in warm, humid climates.
