Nephelium semiglaucum var. acutifolium

Nephelium semiglaucum var. acutifolium, commonly known as the sharp-leafed rambutan, is a variant of the rambutan tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, spiny fruits that are typically reddish or yellowish in color. The leaves are narrow and pointed, giving it the "sharp-leafed" descriptor. The plant thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and edible fruit, which has a hairy exterior and sweet, creamy flesh.
