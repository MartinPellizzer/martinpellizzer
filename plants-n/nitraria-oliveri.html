Nitraria oliveri

Nitraria oliveri, commonly known as the desert raisin, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and later develops into small, dark reddish-brown fruits that resemble raisins. The plant is adapted to harsh environments, with deep roots and a capacity to store water in its stems. It belongs to the family Nitrariaceae and is known for its ability to thrive in poor soils.

The fruits are sometimes used in traditional medicine and as a food source in certain regions.
