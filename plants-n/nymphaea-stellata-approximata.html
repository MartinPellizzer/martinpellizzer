Nymphaea stellata approximata

Nymphaea stellata approximata, commonly known as the star lotus, is a water lily native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that float on the surface of still or slow-moving freshwater bodies. The flowers are typically white or pale pink, with a star-like pattern of petals that open in the early morning. It thrives in warm, sunny environments with nutrient-rich water.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in ponds and gardens.
