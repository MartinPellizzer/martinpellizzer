Narcissus tubaeflorus

Narcissus tubaeflorus, commonly known as the trumpet daffodil, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces a single, showy bloom per stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Narcissus tubaeflorus is known for its early spring blooming habit and its ability to naturalize over time.
