Nicolsonia

Nicolsonia, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a genus of flowering plants in the mallow family, Malvaceae.

It is native to the high-altitude regions of South America, particularly in the Andes. The plants typically feature showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. They are shrubs or small trees with woody stems and alternate leaves.

Nicolsonia species are valued for their ornamental appeal and are often cultivated in gardens for their vibrant blooms.
