Nigella agrestis

Nigella agrestis, commonly known as field fennel, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, oval-shaped seeds that are often used in culinary applications. It thrives in dry, open habitats and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.

Nigella agrestis is also known for its aromatic foliage and its ability to attract beneficial insects.
