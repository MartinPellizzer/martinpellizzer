Notopleura parasitica

Notopleura parasitica, commonly known as the parasitic fig.

It is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia. This species is notable for its hemiparasitic nature, deriving some of its nutrients from host plants while also performing photosynthesis. Its leaves are usually broad and succulent, adapted to store water in arid environments.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are often pollinated by specific insects.
