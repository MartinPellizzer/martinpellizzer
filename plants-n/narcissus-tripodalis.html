Narcissus tripodalis

Narcissus tripodalis, commonly known as the Turkish daffodil, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces a single, large, trumpet-shaped flower with a yellow corona. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Narcissus tripodalis is valued for its early blooming habit and ornamental appeal.
