Nepeta sibthorpii subsp. tumeniana

Nepeta sibthorpii subsp.

tumeniana, commonly known as the Tumenian catmint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its aromatic, hairy leaves and clusters of small, pale blue to violet flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils, thriving in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage contains essential oils that contribute to its distinct minty fragrance.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual and aromatic qualities.
