Nidorella adolfi-friderici

Nidorella adolfi-friderici, commonly known as the Adolf-Friedrich's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its elegant, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, and it produces showy, often yellow or greenish-yellow blooms.

Nidorella adolfi-friderici is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
