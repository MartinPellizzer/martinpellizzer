Narcissus ganymedoides

Narcissus ganymedoides, commonly known as the Ganymed daffodil, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe, particularly the Iberian Peninsula, and is characterized by its striking yellow petals and a tall, slender stem. The plant produces large, showy flowers with a distinctive trumpet-shaped corona. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is often cultivated for its beauty and resilience.
