Neolitsea siamensis

Neolitsea siamensis, commonly known as the Siam litsea, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical forests, thriving in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a glossy surface, while its fruits are small drupes that develop from its fragrant white flowers.

Neolitsea siamensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
