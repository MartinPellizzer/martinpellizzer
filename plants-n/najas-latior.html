Najas latior

Najas latior, commonly known as the broadleaf watermilfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It has slender, branching stems and small, opposite leaves that are usually oval to ovate in shape. The plant produces tiny, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, typically blooming in late summer. Najas latior plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Its rapid growth can sometimes lead to dense vegetation, affecting water flow and light penetration in lakes and ponds.
