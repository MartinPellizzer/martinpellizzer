Nageia madagascariensis

Nageia madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar pine, is a unique conifer native to the island of Madagascar.

It is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and needle-like leaves arranged in whorls. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain the seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ecological importance and limited distribution, it is considered a valuable species for conservation efforts.
