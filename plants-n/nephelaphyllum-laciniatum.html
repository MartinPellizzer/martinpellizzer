Nephelaphyllum laciniatum

Nephelaphyllum laciniatum, commonly known as the shredded cloud fern, is a unique species of fern characterized by its delicate, frond-like structures that resemble shredded paper.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia. The plant features a short, creeping rhizome with feather-like leaves that are finely divided, giving it a feathery appearance. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are typically located on the underside of the fronds, and they produce spores for propagation.

This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
