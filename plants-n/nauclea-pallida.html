Nauclea pallida

Nauclea pallida, commonly known as the pale nauclea, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical in shape and have a pale green color. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Nauclea pallida thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its foliage and flowering display.
