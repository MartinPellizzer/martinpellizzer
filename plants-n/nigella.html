Nigella

Nigella, commonly known as black cumin, is an annual herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces spherical seed pods that turn from green to black as they mature, containing numerous tiny, dark seeds. These seeds are rich in essential oils and have been used in traditional medicine and cooking for centuries.

Nigella thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy and adaptable plant for various climates.
