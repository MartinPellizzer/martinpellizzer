Nepeta amethystina subsp. anticaria

Nepeta amethystina subsp.

anticaria, commonly known as the purple catmint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It features slender, upright stems covered in fine hairs and produces clusters of small, tubular blue or purple flowers.

The leaves are lance-shaped, serrated, and aromatic, often emitting a minty scent when crushed. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
