Narcissus spathulatus

Narcissus spathulatus, commonly known as the broad-leaf daffodil, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, spatulate leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant produces a single, terminal flower with six petals and a yellow or white corona. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Narcissus spathulatus is also valued for its ornamental beauty and fragrance.
