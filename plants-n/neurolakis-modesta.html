Neurolakis modesta

Neurolakis modesta, commonly known as the modest lily, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and delicate, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in cooler, temperate climates. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a short, stout stem that supports its showy inflorescence.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
