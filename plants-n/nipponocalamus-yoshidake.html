Nipponocalamus yoshidake

Nipponocalamus yoshidake, commonly known as Yoshida's bamboo, is a species of grass in the Poaceae family.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its slender, erect culms that are often clustered together. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as mountain forests and wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a tendency to curl at the edges.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
