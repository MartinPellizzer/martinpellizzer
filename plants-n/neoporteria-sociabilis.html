Neoporteria sociabilis

Neoporteria sociabilis, commonly known as the social violets, is a species of flowering plant native to the temperate regions of South America.

It is characterized by its violet-blue flowers that bloom in clusters, often in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and woodlands. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in a fine layer of fuzzy hairs.

Neoporteria sociabilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic beauty.
