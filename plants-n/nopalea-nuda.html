Nopalea nuda

Nopalea nuda, commonly known as the naked prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and the absence of the typical greenish bloom found on other Opuntia species. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptation to extreme drought conditions. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring.

The fruit, when it forms, is edible and has a sweet, tangy flavor, making it a valuable resource in some regions.
