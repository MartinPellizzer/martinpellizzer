Nephrodium contractum

Nephrodium contractum, commonly known as the narrowleaf water clover, is a perennial aquatic plant native to North America.

It typically grows in shallow freshwater environments such as ponds and wetlands. The plant has small, opposite leaves that are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, with three sepals and three petals.

Nephrodium contractum plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for microorganisms and invertebrates.
