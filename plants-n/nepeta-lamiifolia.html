Nepeta lamiifolia

Nepeta lamiifolia, commonly known as catnip, is a flowering plant in the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, clustered blue or purple flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. It is known for its aromatic leaves, which contain compounds such as nepetalactone that can affect the behavior of cats.

Nepeta lamiifolia is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and historical use in herbal remedies.
