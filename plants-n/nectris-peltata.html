Nectris peltata

Nectris peltata, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and have a velvety texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Nectris peltata is often found in moist, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and wetlands.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental leaves and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
