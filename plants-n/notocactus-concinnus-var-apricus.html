Notocactus concinnus var. apricus

Notocactus concinnus var. apricus, commonly known as the Orange-Flowered Cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are typically green with a slight reddish tinge. The plant produces vibrant orange flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in arid gardens and as a houseplant.
