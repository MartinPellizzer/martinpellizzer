Nymphaea ortgiesiana

Nymphaea ortgiesiana, commonly known as the Ortgies' water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that open in the early morning and close by midday. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats, such as ponds and lakes. Its broad, heart-shaped leaves float on the water surface, providing a habitat for various aquatic organisms.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in wetland ecosystems.
