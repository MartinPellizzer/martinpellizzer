Noccaea caerulescens subsp. brachypetala

Noccaea caerulescens subsp.

brachypetala, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mercury, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, blue-green leaves and produces small, yellow flowers.

This species is known for its ability to accumulate heavy metals, making it a valuable plant in phytoremediation efforts. It thrives in calcareous soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

The plant is typically short-lived, with a life cycle that includes winter dormancy and spring flowering.
