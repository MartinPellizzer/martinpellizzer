Narcissus benacensis

Narcissus benacensis, commonly known as the Benacensis daffodil, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region, particularly found in parts of Italy and the Balkans. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers with a single tepal, typically in shades of yellow. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy perennial suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and ecological landscapes.
