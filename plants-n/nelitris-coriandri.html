Nelitris coriandri

Nelitris coriandri, commonly known as coriander, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, branched stems and feathery, aromatic leaves that are often used in culinary applications. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in umbels, which are typical of the Apiaceae family. Its seeds, known as cumin in some regions, are widely used as a spice.

Nelitris coriandri thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for both gardens and commercial cultivation.
