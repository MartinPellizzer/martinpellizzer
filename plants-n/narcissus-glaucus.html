Narcissus glaucus

Narcissus glaucus, commonly known as the blue narcissus, is a bulbous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers, which bloom in early spring. The plant features strap-shaped, grass-like leaves and produces a single flower per stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Narcissus glaucus is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for its delicate, fragrant blooms.
