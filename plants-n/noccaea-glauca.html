Noccaea glauca

Noccaea glauca, commonly known as white stonecrop, is a flowering plant native to arctic and alpine regions of Eurasia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late summer. Its leaves are thick and fleshy, adapted to retain moisture in harsh environments.

Noccaea glauca is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal in alpine gardens.
