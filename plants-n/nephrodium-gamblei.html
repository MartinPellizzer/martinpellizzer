Nephrodium gamblei

Nephrodium gamblei, commonly known as the Gambier's water fern, is a rare and ancient fern species native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Nephrodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that are often submerged in water or growing along the edges of streams. The plant exhibits a unique reproductive strategy, producing spores in specialized structures called sorus, which are typically located on the underside of its leaves. Nephrodium gamblei thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in association with other aquatic or semi-aquatic plants.

Due to habitat destruction, it is considered a vulnerable species and is of significant interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
