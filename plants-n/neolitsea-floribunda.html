Neolitsea floribunda

Neolitsea floribunda, commonly known as the fragrant evergreen tree, is a member of the Lauraceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters, which are typically followed by oval, woody fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and aromatic properties, it is often cultivated in gardens and used in traditional medicine.
