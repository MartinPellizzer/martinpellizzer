Neonauclea rheophila

Neonauclea rheophila, commonly known as the river hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, large, showy flowers that are typically pink or red in color. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found along streams and in forest understories. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a velvety texture.

Neonauclea rheophila is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
