Nepeta amicorum

Nepeta amicorum, commonly known as catnip, is a flowering plant in the Lamiaceae family.

It is native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa, and thrives in sunny, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, fragrant blue or purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves contain nepetalactone, a compound that repels insects and attracts cats.

Nepeta amicorum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in herbal remedies.
