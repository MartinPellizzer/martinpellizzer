Narcissus major var. propinquus

Narcissus major var. propinquus, commonly known as the common daffodil, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its trumpet-shaped, yellow flowers and strap-like leaves. The plant is native to Europe and prefers well-drained soil and full sun. It blooms in early spring, often before the leaves fully emerge.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is widely cultivated in gardens.
