Najas marina subsp. armata

Najas marina subsp.

armata, commonly known as the narrowleaf watermilfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Najadaceae and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, scale-like leaves.

The plant typically grows in still or slow-moving waters, such as ponds and lakes, where it forms dense underwater vegetation. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various invertebrates and fish.

Its reproductive structures, including small flowers, are often found in clusters near the water surface.
