Navarretia squarrosa var. agrestis

Navarretia squarrosa var. agrestis, commonly known as the desert jeweledweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is a low-growing annual herb with hairy, prostrate stems that often root at the nodes. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow, linear, and often covered in fine, white hairs.

Navarretia squarrosa var. agrestis thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
