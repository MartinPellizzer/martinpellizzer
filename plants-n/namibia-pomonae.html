Namibia pomonae

Namibia pomonae, commonly known as the Namibian apple tree, is a unique species native to the Namib Desert.

It is a small, deciduous tree that produces edible fruits resembling apples in appearance. The plant has adapted to arid conditions with deep roots and waxy leaves to reduce water loss. Its flowers are pale yellow and bloom during the brief rainy season.

The fruit is an important food source for local wildlife and has been used by indigenous communities for centuries.
