Narcissus × andorranus

Narcissus × andorranus, commonly known as the Andorra daffodil, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Narcissus poeticus and Narcissus alata.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Native to the Pyrenees Mountains, it is valued for its ornamental beauty and hardiness.
