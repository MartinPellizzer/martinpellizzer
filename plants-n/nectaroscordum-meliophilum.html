Nectaroscordum meliophilum

Nectaroscordum meliophilum, commonly known as the sweet garlic or melon garlic, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its bulbous root system and strap-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its bulbs are often used as a culinary substitute for garlic, possessing a mild, sweet flavor.

Nectaroscordum meliophilum thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable ornamental and edible plant for gardens.
