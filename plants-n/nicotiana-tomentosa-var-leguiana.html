Nicotiana tomentosa var. leguiana

Nicotiana tomentosa var. leguiana, commonly known as the velvet tobacco plant, is a species native to South America.

It is characterized by its fuzzy, velvety leaves and striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows as an annual or biennial, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners interested in ornamental plants.

Its botanical features make it a unique addition to both cultivated and wild landscapes.
