Neomammillaria vagaspina

Neomammillaria vagaspina, commonly known as the spiny pincushion cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a small, globular cactus with a ribbed structure and typically features spines that are white to yellow in color. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and slow growth rate, it is often cultivated as a decorative ornamental plant.
