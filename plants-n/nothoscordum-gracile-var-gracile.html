Nothoscordum gracile var. gracile

Nothoscordum gracile var. gracile, commonly known as the slender false leek, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellow flowers arranged in loose clusters. The plant has a fibrous root system and is often found in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and sandy soils. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in full sun and well-drained soil conditions.
