Nicotiana clevelandii

Nicotiana clevelandii, commonly known as Cleveland's tobacco, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its vibrant purple or red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous small seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and wildflower displays.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is often cultivated in native plant gardens.
