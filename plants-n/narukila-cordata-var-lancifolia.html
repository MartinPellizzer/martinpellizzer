Narukila cordata var. lancifolia

Narukila cordata var. lancifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are often slightly pointed at the tips, giving it its scientific name. Its inflorescence consists of small, clustered spikes of greenish or yellowish flowers, which appear in the summer months.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
