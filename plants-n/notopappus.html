Notopappus

Notopappus, commonly known as the common pappus, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

These plants are characterized by their composite flower heads, which consist of both ray and disk flowers. The genus includes several species, many of which are native to North America. Notopappus species typically have narrow, grass-like leaves and produce small, dandelion-like flowers.

They are often found in disturbed soils and are adapted to a variety of environmental conditions.
