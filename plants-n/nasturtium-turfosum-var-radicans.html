Nasturtium turfosum var. radicans

Nasturtium turfosum var. radicans, commonly known as the creeping nasturtium, is a herbaceous plant characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, edible flowers.

It features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are often covered in a fine, fuzzy texture. The plant produces bright orange or red flowers, which are both ornamental and edible, adding a spicy flavor to dishes. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

This variety is often used in gardens for its aesthetic value and culinary versatility.
