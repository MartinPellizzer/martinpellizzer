Nephelium pupillum

Nephelium pupillum, commonly known as the dwarf rambutan, is a small tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is closely related to the rambutan (Nephelium lappaceum). The plant produces small, hairy, spiny fruits that are typically yellow or orange when mature. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate with a pointed tip.

The tree thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit.
