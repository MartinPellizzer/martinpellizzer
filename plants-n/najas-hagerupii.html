Najas hagerupii

Najas hagerupii, commonly known as the Hagerup's water plantain, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Najadaceae and is characterized by its narrow, ribbon-like leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged or just at the water's surface. It thrives in still or slow-moving waters with moderate light penetration.

Najas hagerupii plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
