Norantea spiciflora

Norantea spiciflora, commonly known as the spiky flower plant, is a member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its striking, spikelike inflorescences that bear small, tubular flowers in shades of red and purple. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered with fine hairs.

Norantea spiciflora thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
