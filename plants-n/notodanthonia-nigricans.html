Notodanthonia nigricans

Notodanthonia nigricans, commonly known as the black-haired wallaby grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and dark-colored inflorescences. The plant typically grows in coastal and sandy soils, often in disturbed or open habitats. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Notodanthonia nigricans is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its hardy nature.
