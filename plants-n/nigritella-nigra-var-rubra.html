Nigritella nigra var. rubra

Nigritella nigra var. rubra, commonly known as the red-stemmed nightshade, is a flowering plant belonging to the Solanaceae family.

It is characterized by its dark green, lobed leaves and clusters of small, violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces bright red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is native to parts of Southeast Asia.

Due to its ornamental value and toxicity, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens but requires careful handling.
