Nymphaea hypotricha

Nymphaea hypotricha, commonly known as the lesser water lily, is a species of aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats, such as ponds and lakes. Its roots are anchored in the substrate, while its leaves and flowers float on the water's surface.

Nymphaea hypotricha plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
