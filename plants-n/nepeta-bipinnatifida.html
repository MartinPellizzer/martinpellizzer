Nepeta bipinnatifida

Nepeta bipinnatifida, commonly known as catmint, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pale blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Nepeta bipinnatifida is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
