Nepenthes × hookerae

Nepenthes × hookerae, commonly known as the hybrid pitcher plant, is a fascinating species resulting from the cross between Nepenthes mirabilis and Nepenthes rafflesiana.

It is native to the tropical rainforests of Borneo, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its large, colorful pitcher-shaped leaves that secrete a digestive fluid to trap and digest insects. Its unique morphology and carnivorous habits make it a popular subject in botanical studies and horticulture.

Nepenthes × hookerae also exhibits a high degree of genetic variability, contributing to its adaptability and ornamental value.
