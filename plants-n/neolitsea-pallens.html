Neolitsea pallens

Neolitsea pallens, commonly known as the white-flowered lauraceous tree, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, simple leaves that are often glossy and lance-shaped. The plant produces small, white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences, which bloom in the spring. Its fruit is a small, woody drupe that contains a single seed.

Neolitsea pallens is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
