Newcastelia cladotricha

Newcastelia cladotricha, commonly known as the velvetleaf iceplant, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, ribbed stems and leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs, giving it a velvety appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in late spring. It is adapted to arid environments and stores water in its thick, fleshy stems.

Newcastelia cladotricha is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique texture and drought tolerance.
