Nymphoides astoniae

Nymphoides astoniae, commonly known as the golden bell orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in wet, shaded environments such as swampy areas and along riverbanks. Its leaves are broad and sword-shaped, and it produces small, clustered inflorescences.

Nymphoides astoniae is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and tropical landscapes.
