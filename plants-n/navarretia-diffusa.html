Navarretia diffusa

Navarretia diffusa, commonly known as the Sierra starflower, is a low-growing annual plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, star-shaped white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically reaches a height of about 10 to 20 centimeters and has hairy, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in meadows and open slopes.

Navarretia diffusa plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is adapted to the seasonal climate of its native habitat.
