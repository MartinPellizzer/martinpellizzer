Nicotiana mutabilis

Nicotiana mutabilis, commonly known as the twilight flower, is a species of flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that change color throughout the day, typically from white to pink to purple. The plant belongs to the Solanaceae family, which also includes tobacco and tomatoes. Its flowers open in the evening and close during the day, a trait that gives it its common name.

Nicotiana mutabilis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also studied for its unique photoperiodic responses.
