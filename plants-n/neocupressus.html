Neocupressus

Neocupressus, commonly known as the Mexican cypress, is a genus of coniferous evergreen trees native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the family Cupressaceae, which includes cypresses, junipers, and redwoods. The genus comprises two recognized species, Neocupressus mexicana and Neocupressus lawsoniana, both characterized by their narrow, conical shape and scale-like leaves. These trees are often cultivated for their ornamental value and are adapted to a variety of soil types.

They play an important ecological role in their native habitats, providing habitat for wildlife and contributing to local biodiversity.
