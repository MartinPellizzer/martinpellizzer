Nepeta nervosa

Nepeta nervosa, commonly known as catmint, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, fragrant blue or purple flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with prominent veins, which give it its scientific name "nervosa.". It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Nepeta nervosa is also known for its mild minty scent and its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
