Nymphaea × intermedia var. sublutea

Nymphaea × intermedia var. sublutea, commonly known as the yellow water lily, is a hybrid aquatic plant native to parts of Asia.

It features broad, heart-shaped leaves that float on the water's surface, with a waxy upper surface and a velvety underside. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that open in the early morning and close by midday. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, preferring full sun to partial shade.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a hybrid species, showcasing genetic diversity within the Nymphaea genus.
