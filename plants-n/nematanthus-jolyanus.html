Nematanthus jolyanus

Nematanthus jolyanus, commonly known as the "Coral Bells," is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean islands.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, often resembling coral in appearance. The plant produces showy bracts that are typically more prominent than the actual flowers, enhancing its ornamental value. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in tropical gardens.

Nematanthus jolyanus is known for its ability to bloom throughout the year under proper care.
