Narega

Narega, commonly known as the yellow-eyed grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, golden-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Native to various parts of Asia, including India and Southeast Asia, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along riverbanks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a low-maintenance, visually appealing plant.
