Neolamarckia Cadamba

Neolamarckia cadamba is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia known for its ornamental value and ecological importance. It features a straight trunk and a broad, umbrella-like canopy that provides shade and habitat for various wildlife. The tree produces fragrant flowers in clusters, often displaying shades of yellow and orange, which attract pollinators like bees and birds. Its leaves are large, glossy, and provide a lush green cover throughout the year. Due to its beauty and resilience, it is widely cultivated in gardens and reforestation projects around the world.
