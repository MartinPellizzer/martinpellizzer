Nasturtium asperum var. boissieri

Nasturtium asperum var. boissieri, commonly known as the rough nasturtium, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features heart-shaped, slightly hairy leaves and produces vibrant orange or yellow flowers with a distinctive, peppery scent. The plant is characterized by its rough, textured foliage and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Its edible flowers and leaves are also used in culinary applications, adding a spicy flavor to dishes.
