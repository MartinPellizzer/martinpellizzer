Neosprucea paterna

Neosprucea paterna, commonly known as the Patagonian spruce, is a rare coniferous tree native to the Andes mountains of Argentina.

It is characterized by its slender, conical shape and dark green, needle-like leaves. The tree produces small, cone-like structures that contain its seeds, typical of the conifer family. Neosprucea paterna thrives in cool, moist environments at high altitudes.

Due to habitat loss and climate change, it is considered a vulnerable species with limited distribution.
