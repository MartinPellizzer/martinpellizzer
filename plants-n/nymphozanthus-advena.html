Nymphozanthus advena

Nymphozanthus advena, commonly known as the wandering flame plant, is a flowering species native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, often resembling flames. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and elliptical in shape, with a smooth surface and a glossy appearance.

Nymphozanthus advena thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
