Niemeyera antiloga

Niemeyera antiloga, commonly known as the Antilogan tree, is a species of flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows to a height of 10-15 meters. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that release seeds when mature.

Niemeyera antiloga is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional medicine.
