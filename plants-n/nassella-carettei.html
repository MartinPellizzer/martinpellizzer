Nassella carettei

Nassella carettei, commonly known as Caretta's needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and fine, needle-like leaves that are typically green to bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, pale to brownish spikelets that are clustered along the stem, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Nassella carettei is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
