Nasturtium diandrum

Nasturtium diandrum, commonly known as the double-flowered nasturtium, is a herbaceous annual plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that typically come in shades of orange, red, and yellow. The plant features heart-shaped, glossy leaves and produces edible flowers and leaves, often used in culinary applications. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Nasturtium diandrum is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
