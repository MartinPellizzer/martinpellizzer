Nymphaea casparyi

Nymphaea casparyi, commonly known as the Caspary water lily, is a species of aquatic plant native to the wetlands of Central America.

It belongs to the Nymphaeaceae family and is characterized by its broad, floating leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often forming dense mats on the water surface. Its leaves are typically rounded with a waxy surface, while its flowers can be pink, white, or yellow, depending on the variety.

Nymphaea casparyi plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
