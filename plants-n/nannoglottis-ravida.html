Nannoglottis ravida

Nannoglottis ravida, commonly known as the slender lily, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, strap-like leaves that are often hairless and have a smooth texture. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in clusters at the base of the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Nannoglottis ravida is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
