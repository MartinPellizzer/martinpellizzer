Narcissus tazetta subsp. micranthus

Narcissus tazetta subsp.

micranthus, commonly known as the small-flowered daffodil, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, brightly colored flowers with a cup-shaped corona.

The flowers typically bloom in early spring, adding vibrant yellow hues to gardens and natural landscapes. This subspecies is prized for its compact size and suitability for rock gardens or container planting.

It is also valued in horticulture for its fragrance and ability to naturalize in suitable climates.
