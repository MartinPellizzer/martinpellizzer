Nectandra mollis subsp. oppositifolia

Nectandra mollis subsp.

oppositifolia, commonly known as the pink-flowered sandarac tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to parts of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often glossy and dark green.

The tree produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white, which bloom in clusters. Its wood is aromatic and has been historically used for incense and varnishes.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.
