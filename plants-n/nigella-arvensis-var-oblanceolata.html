Nigella arvensis var. oblanceolata

Nigella arvensis var. oblanceolata, commonly known as the broadleaf love-in-a-mist, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and delicate, blue to purple flowers. The leaves are typically broad and lance-shaped, giving the plant its varietal name. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a long history of use in traditional medicine.
