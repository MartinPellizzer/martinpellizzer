Narcissus × maroccanus

Narcissus × maroccanus, commonly known as the Moroccan daffodil, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Narcissus tazetta and Narcissus poeticus.

It is native to North Africa, particularly Morocco, and is characterized by its striking, often double, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens in temperate climates.

The flowers bloom in spring, adding vibrant color to the landscape.
