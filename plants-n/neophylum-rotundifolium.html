Neophylum rotundifolium

Neophylum rotundifolium, commonly known as the round-leafed neophylum, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its broad, rounded leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and the plant is often found in association with other wetland flora.

Neophylum rotundifolium is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and maintaining wetland biodiversity.
