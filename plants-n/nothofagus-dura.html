Nothofagus dura

Nothofagus dura, commonly known as the hard beech, is a species of evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Nothofagaceae family, which is closely related to the beech family. The plant is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and smooth, bark-like trunk. It typically grows in temperate rainforests and is well adapted to moist, cool climates.

Nothofagus dura plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
