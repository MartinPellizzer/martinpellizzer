Nenax coronata

Nenax coronata, commonly known as the golden crown vine, is a climbing plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its slender stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often a vibrant green. The plant produces striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a dramatic visual element to its habitat. Nenax coronata thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing along forest edges or in disturbed areas.

Its rapid growth and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in suitable climates.
