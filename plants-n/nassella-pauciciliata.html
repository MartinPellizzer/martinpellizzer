Nassella pauciciliata

Nassella pauciciliata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to the western United States and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, needle-like leaves and produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that are often gray-green in color. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas. It has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Nassella pauciciliata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
