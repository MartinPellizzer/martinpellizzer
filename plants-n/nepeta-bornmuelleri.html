Nepeta bornmuelleri

Nepeta bornmuelleri, commonly known as the snowflake catmint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and features narrow, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pale blue to lavender flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Nepeta bornmuelleri is well adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice among gardeners seeking drought-tolerant and visually appealing plants.
