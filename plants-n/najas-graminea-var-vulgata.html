Najas graminea var. vulgata

Najas graminea var. vulgata, commonly known as the common watermilfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments across Europe, Asia, and North America.

It has narrow, ribbon-like leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem, which helps it maximize light absorption in water. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged, followed by tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in still or slow-moving waters and is often found in ponds, lakes, and canals.

Due to its rapid growth, it can become invasive in some regions, impacting water quality and aquatic habitats.
