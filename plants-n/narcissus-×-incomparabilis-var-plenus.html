Narcissus × incomparabilis var. plenus

Narcissus × incomparabilis var. plenus, commonly known as the Double Crown daffodil, is a hybrid cultivar renowned for its distinctive botanical features.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy, double-petaled flowers that resemble a crown. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with strap-shaped, grass-like leaves that emerge from a basal rosette. Its inflorescence consists of a single scape bearing one or more flowers, each with a prominent, often colorful corona.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is widely cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and resilience.
