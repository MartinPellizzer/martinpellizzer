Nepeta latifolia

Nepeta latifolia, commonly known as catmint, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, fragrant blue or purple flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Nepeta latifolia is also valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in herbal remedies.
