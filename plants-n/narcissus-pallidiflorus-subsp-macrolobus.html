Narcissus pallidiflorus subsp. macrolobus

Narcissus pallidiflorus subsp.

macrolobus, commonly known as the large-leaf daffodil, is a subspecies native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its pale yellow flowers with a distinctive, elongated corona.

The plant typically grows in open woodlands and grassy areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, contributing to its ornamental value.

This subspecies is valued for its early spring blooming and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
