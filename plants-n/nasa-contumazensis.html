Nasa contumazensis

Nasa contumazensis, commonly known as the Andean moonflower, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes in South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that open at night. The plant is adapted to cool, moist environments and thrives in well-drained soil. Its flowers emit a fragrant scent to attract nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

Nasa contumazensis is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique blooming pattern.
