Nicotiana gigantea

Nicotiana gigantea, commonly known as the giant tobacco plant, is a species native to South America.

It is a herbaceous annual plant that can grow up to 3 meters in height, featuring large, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a hairy texture and a strong, aromatic scent. Its flowers are typically tubular and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Nicotiana gigantea is cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical significance in traditional medicine.
