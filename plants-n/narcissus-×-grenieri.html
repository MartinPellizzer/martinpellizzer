Narcissus × grenieri

Narcissus × grenieri, commonly known as the golden crown narcissus, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Narcissus tazetta and Narcissus poeticus.

It is characterized by its compact, rounded growth habit and is typically cultivated as a bulbous perennial. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, yellow flowers with a distinctive orange center, blooming in early spring. Its foliage consists of strap-like, grassy leaves that emerge after the flowers.

Narcissus × grenieri is well-suited for rock gardens and borders, thriving in well-drained soil and full sun.
