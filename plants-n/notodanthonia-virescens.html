Notodanthonia virescens

Notodanthonia virescens, commonly known as the green-haired grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine, hairy structures. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often in coastal or inland regions. Its flowering stems bear spikelets that are usually pale green to reddish in color.

Notodanthonia virescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its hardy nature.
