Nemcia pauciflora

Nemcia pauciflora, commonly known as the sparse-flowered nemcia, is a member of the Rubiaceae family.

It is a small shrub characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are often glandular-dotted. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that are clustered in cymes, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its fruits are small, dry berries that contain several seeds.

Nemcia pauciflora is native to parts of South America, particularly Brazil, and is often found in moist, shaded environments.
