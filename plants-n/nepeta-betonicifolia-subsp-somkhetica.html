Nepeta betonicifolia subsp. somkhetica

Nepeta betonicifolia subsp.

somkhetica, commonly known as the Thai catnip, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pale blue to violet flowers.

The plant belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is known for its strong scent, which is often described as similar to catnip. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its foliage is commonly used in traditional herbal remedies and as a source of essential oils.
