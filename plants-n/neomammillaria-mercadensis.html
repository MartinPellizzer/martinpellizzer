Neomammillaria mercadensis

Neomammillaria mercadensis, commonly known as the Mercadense Pincushion Cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a small, globular cactus with a short, thick stem and dense, white to pale green spines. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical collections.
