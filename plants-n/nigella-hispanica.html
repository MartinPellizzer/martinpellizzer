Nigella hispanica

Nigella hispanica, commonly known as Spanish cumin, is an annual herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and bright yellow flowers. The plant produces small, oval-shaped seeds that are often used as a spice in traditional cuisines. Its foliage consists of alternate, trifoliate leaves with a slightly hairy texture.

Nigella hispanica thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a suitable plant for gardens and agricultural settings.
