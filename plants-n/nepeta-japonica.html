Nepeta japonica

Nepeta japonica, commonly known as Japanese catmint, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features aromatic, gray-green leaves and produces clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and pleasant fragrance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Nepeta japonica is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
