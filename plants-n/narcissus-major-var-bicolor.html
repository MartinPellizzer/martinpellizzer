Narcissus major var. bicolor

Narcissus major var. bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored daffodil, is a striking bulbous plant native to southern Europe.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers with a distinctive bicolor pattern, typically yellow petals and a white or greenish throat. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, often blooming in early spring. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge after the flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
