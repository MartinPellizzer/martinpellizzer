Nigella sativa var. hispidula

Nigella sativa var. hispidula, commonly known as the hairy cumin, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces small, globular fruits that contain numerous tiny, black seeds used in traditional medicine and cuisine. Its leaves are finely divided and have a mild, aromatic scent.

This variety is often cultivated for its seeds, which are valued for their medicinal properties and culinary uses.
