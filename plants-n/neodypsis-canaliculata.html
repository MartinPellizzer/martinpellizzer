Neodypsis canaliculata

Neodypsis canaliculata, commonly known as the golden bamboo or the yellow bamboo, is a species of grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that often exhibit a golden or yellowish hue. This plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in open areas with well-drained soil. Its rapid growth and dense foliage make it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Neodypsis canaliculata is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion in certain environments.
