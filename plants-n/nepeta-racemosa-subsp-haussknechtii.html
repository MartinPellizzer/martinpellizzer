Nepeta racemosa subsp. haussknechtii

Nepeta racemosa subsp.

haussknechtii, commonly known as the purple catmint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features square, hairy stems and produces clusters of small, fragrant, lavender-blue flowers.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its aromatic properties make it both ornamental and potentially useful in herbal remedies.
