Nothapodytes dimorpha

Nothapodytes dimorpha, commonly known as the mountain pepper tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves that are usually glossy and dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by dark purple or black berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local cultures.
