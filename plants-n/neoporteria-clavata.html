Neoporteria clavata

Neoporteria clavata, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features wiry, branched stems that often grow close to the ground. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Neoporteria clavata thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shaded areas.
