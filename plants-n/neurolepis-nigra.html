Neurolepis nigra

Neurolepis nigra, commonly known as black neurolepis, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid regions. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are usually narrow and lance-shaped, with a leathery texture.

Neurolepis nigra is known for its resilience to drought and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
