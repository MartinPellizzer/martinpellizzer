Neoregelia alvimii var. donatoi

Neoregelia alvimii var. donatoi, commonly known as the "Rainbow Bromeliad," is a striking epiphytic plant native to the rainforests of Brazil.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful bracts that surround the central inflorescence, often displaying shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its rosette-shaped leaves are thick and waxy, designed to capture and retain moisture.

Neoregelia alvimii var. donatoi is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
