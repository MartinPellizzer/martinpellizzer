Nepeta dasyangula

Nepeta dasyangula, commonly known as the rough catmint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pale blue to violet flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically hairy and have a strong, mint-like fragrance.

Nepeta dasyangula is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
