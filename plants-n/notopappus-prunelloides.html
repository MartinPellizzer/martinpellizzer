Notopappus prunelloides

Notopappus prunelloides, commonly known as the dwarf pincushion, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and small, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes. Its compact size and ornamental appearance make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking drought-tolerant plants.

Notopappus prunelloides is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
