Nerium lauriforme

Nerium lauriforme, commonly known as the sacred laurel or Indian laurel, is a flowering plant native to southern and eastern Africa.

It is a small evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows up to 3 meters in height. The plant features simple, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green, with a fragrant, white to pale yellow flower cluster. Its flowers are borne in terminal panicles and are often followed by small, red berries.

Nerium lauriforme is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in various cultures.
