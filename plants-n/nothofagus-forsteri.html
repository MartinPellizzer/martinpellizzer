Nothofagus forsteri

Nothofagus forsteri, commonly known as the slender southern beech, is a species native to southern South America.

It is a deciduous tree that typically grows in cool, temperate climates. The plant has dark green, lance-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn before dropping. Its bark is smooth and gray, with a distinctive flaky texture.

Nothofagus forsteri plays an important role in the ecosystems of its native habitat, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
