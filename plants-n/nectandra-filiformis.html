Nectandra filiformis

Nectandra filiformis, commonly known as the slender nectandra, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its aromatic, leathery leaves and small, clustered white flowers. The plant produces fragrant, yellowish-brown fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
