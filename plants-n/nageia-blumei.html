Nageia blumei

Nageia blumei, commonly known as the giant nageia, is a rare and ancient conifer native to the mountain forests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and dense, evergreen foliage that forms a broad, rounded crown. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain both male and female reproductive organs, typical of gymnosperms. Its leaves are long, flat, and scale-like, arranged in whorls around the stem.

Nageia blumei is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in traditional medicine and forest ecosystems.
