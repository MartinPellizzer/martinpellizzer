Niphogeton colombiana

Niphogeton colombiana, commonly known as the Colombian orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of Colombia.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that come in a variety of colors, including white, yellow, and pink. The plant typically grows in high-altitude cloud forests, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Niphogeton colombiana plays an important ecological role in its habitat, supporting various pollinators and contributing to the biodiversity of the region.
