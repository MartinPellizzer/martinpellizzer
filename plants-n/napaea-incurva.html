Napaea incurva

Napaea incurva, commonly known as the hooked napaea, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and is characterized by its upright, branched stems. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Napaea incurva is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
