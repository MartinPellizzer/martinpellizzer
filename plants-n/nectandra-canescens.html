Nectandra canescens

Nectandra canescens, commonly known as the white sand cedar, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, rough-textured bark and simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by small, woody fruits containing aromatic seeds.

Nectandra canescens is often found in moist, well-drained soils in lowland and mountainous regions.
