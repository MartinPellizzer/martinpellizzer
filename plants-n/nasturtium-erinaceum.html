Nasturtium erinaceum

Nasturtium erinaceum, commonly known as the hedgehog nasturtium, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Tropaeolaceae and is characterized by its vibrant orange or red flowers and distinctive spiky seed pods. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible flowers, which have a peppery taste. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are hairy and somewhat succulent.

Nasturtium erinaceum thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.
