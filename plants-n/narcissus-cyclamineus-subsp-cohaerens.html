Narcissus cyclamineus subsp. cohaerens

Narcissus cyclamineus subsp.

cohaerens, commonly known as the Cyclamen narcissus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It features striking, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and yellow, often with a distinctive central blotch.

The plant has broad, velvety leaves that are typically dark green and have a waxy texture. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in the wild.
