Nicotiana tabacum var. angustifolia

Nicotiana tabacum var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaf tobacco, is a variant of the tobacco plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and typically grows in temperate climates. The plant produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal.

Like other tobacco species, it contains nicotine and is related to the broader Nicotiana genus.
