Nigella hispanica subsp. atlantica

Nigella hispanica subsp.

atlantica, commonly known as the Atlantic nigella, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula and North Africa. It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, yellowish flowers.

The plant produces spherical, dark blue to black seed pods that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers sunny to partially shaded conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and herbal practices.
