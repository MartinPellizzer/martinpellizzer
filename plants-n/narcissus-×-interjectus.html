Narcissus × interjectus

Narcissus × interjectus, commonly known as the Intermediate Daffodil, is a hybrid species within the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves and showy, yellow flowers with a distinctive cup-like corona. This plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and thrives in well-drained soil in full sun. It is native to southern Europe and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers bloom in early spring, adding a vibrant touch to gardens and natural landscapes.
