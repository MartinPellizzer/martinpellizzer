Nassella floribunda

Nassella floribunda, commonly known as the blue grama grass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and produces small, fluffy seed heads that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in prairies and grasslands. It has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Nassella floribunda is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
