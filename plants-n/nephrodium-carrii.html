Nephrodium carrii

Nephrodium carrii, commonly known as the Carri's nephrolepis, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its intricate, delicate fronds that are divided into narrow, linear segments. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth. Its reproductive structures include sporangia that produce spores for propagation.

The botanical features of Nephrodium carrii make it a valuable subject for both ecological studies and horticultural interest.
