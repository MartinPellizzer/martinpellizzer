Nesaea kilimandscharica

Nesaea kilimandscharica, commonly known as the Kilimanjaro orchid, is a rare and endemic orchid species found in the high-altitude regions of East Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments, often growing on rocky outcrops or in shaded forest areas. Its roots are adapted to absorb nutrients from the thin, nutrient-poor soils of its habitat.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat vulnerability, it is considered a conservation concern.
