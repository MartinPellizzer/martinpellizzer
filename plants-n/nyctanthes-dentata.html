Nyctanthes dentata

Nyctanthes dentata, commonly known as night-flower or white nightshade, is a deciduous shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has simple, opposite leaves with serrated edges, and its fruits are small, fleshy berries that turn red when mature. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The botanical features of Nyctanthes dentata make it a unique and ecologically significant plant in its native habitat.
