Nepenthes × excelsior

Nepenthes × excelsior, commonly known as the highland pitcher plant, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Nepenthes alata and Nepenthes rafflesiana.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Borneo, where it thrives in cool, humid environments. The plant is characterized by its large, elongated pitcher-shaped leaves that secrete a digestive fluid to trap and digest insects. Its pitchers can reach up to 30 centimeters in length, making it one of the largest in the Nepenthes genus.

This hybrid is highly valued by horticulturists for its striking appearance and unique adaptations to its ecological niche.
