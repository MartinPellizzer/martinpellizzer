Notocactus patagonicus

Notocactus patagonicus, commonly known as the Patagonian cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of southern South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are often ribbed and covered in sparse, white to pale green spines. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. It is well adapted to extreme drought conditions, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

Due to its unique appearance and resilience, it is a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts and collectors.
