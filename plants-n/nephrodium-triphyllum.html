Nephrodium triphyllum

Nephrodium triphyllum, commonly known as the three-leaf water clover, is a unique aquatic plant characterized by its trifoliate leaves.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds and wetlands. The plant has small, rounded leaves that are often submerged or floating, and it lacks true roots, relying instead on rhizomes for anchorage. Its reproductive structures are simple, with small flowers that produce minute seeds.

This species is notable for its ability to thrive in nutrient-rich waters and its role in maintaining aquatic ecosystem balance.
