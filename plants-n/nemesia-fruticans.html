Nemesia fruticans

Nemesia fruticans, commonly known as the common nemesia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and profuse displays of small, five-petaled flowers. The flowers are typically pink, purple, or white, and they bloom throughout the growing season. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and hanging baskets.

Its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies adds to its ecological value.
