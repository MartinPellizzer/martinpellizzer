Nasturtium coxii

Nasturtium coxii, commonly known as the Mexican watercress, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its edible leaves and bright orange or yellow flowers, which bloom in summer. The plant grows in moist, shaded environments, often near streams or in forest understories. Its leaves have a peppery, watercress-like flavor, making it a popular ingredient in salads and as a garnish.

Nasturtium coxii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
