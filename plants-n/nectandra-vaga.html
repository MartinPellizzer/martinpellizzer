Nectandra vaga

Nectandra vaga, commonly known as the wild cinnamon tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like cinnamon and bay laurel. The plant is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its bark and leaves contain essential oils, contributing to its use in traditional medicine and as a source of aromatic compounds.

Nectandra vaga thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.
