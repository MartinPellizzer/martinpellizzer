Narcissus hispanicus var. bujei

Narcissus hispanicus var. bujei, commonly known as the Almería daffodil, is a variety of the genus Narcissus native to southern Spain.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers with a prominent central corona. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30–50 cm and prefers well-drained, sandy soils. It blooms in early spring, often around March or April, and is well-suited to Mediterranean climates.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and public spaces.
