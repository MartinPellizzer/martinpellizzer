Negundo interior

Negundo interior, commonly known as the interior willow, is a unique plant native to specific regions of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, willow-like branches and delicate, narrow leaves that give it a graceful appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and forest understories. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, producing tiny seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Botanically, it is classified as a member of the Salicaceae family, which includes willows and poplars.
