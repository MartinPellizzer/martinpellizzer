Nepeta mollis

Nepeta mollis, commonly known as catnip, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in whorled clusters. Its aromatic leaves contain nepetalactone, a compound known to affect the behavior of cats.

Nepeta mollis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a natural remedy in traditional medicine.
