Neoporteria tuberisulcata var. cupreata

Neoporteria tuberisulcata var. cupreata, commonly known as the copperleaf vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It features striking, velvety leaves that range in color from deep green to coppery tones, depending on the light exposure. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically white or pale yellow, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its tuberous roots are notable for their ability to store water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in varying environmental conditions.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in warm, humid climates with partial shade.
