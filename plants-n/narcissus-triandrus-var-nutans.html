Narcissus triandrus var. nutans

Narcissus triandrus var. nutans, commonly known as the drooping daffodil, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Europe.

It features showy, pendulous yellow flowers with a single trumpet-shaped corona, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has strap-like, narrow leaves that are grassy in appearance. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

This variety is prized for its elegant, drooping blooms and is often cultivated in gardens for ornamental purposes.
