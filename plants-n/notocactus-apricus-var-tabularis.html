Notocactus apricus var. tabularis

Notocactus apricus var. tabularis, commonly known as the Sun Cactus, is a unique cactus species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its flattened, paddle-shaped stems that resemble leaves, which are adapted for photosynthesis in low-light conditions. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its green, succulent body. Its slow growth rate and ability to store water in its thick, fleshy stems make it well-suited for desert environments.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and resilience, making it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor cultivation.
