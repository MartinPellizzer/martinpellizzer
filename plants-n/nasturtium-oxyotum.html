Nasturtium oxyotum

Nasturtium oxyotum, commonly known as the Mexican nasturtium, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Tropaeolaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant orange and red blooms. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces edible seeds that are often used in culinary applications. Its flowers are rich in nectar, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Nasturtium oxyotum is also known for its peppery taste, similar to watercress, making it a popular ornamental and edible plant.
