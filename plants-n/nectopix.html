Nectopix

Nectopix, commonly known as the honeyflower vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant, yellowish-white flowers that emit a sweet, honey-like scent. The plant produces nectar-rich blooms that attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies. Its rapid growth habit makes it suitable for trellises or as a ground cover in warm climates.

Nectopix is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
