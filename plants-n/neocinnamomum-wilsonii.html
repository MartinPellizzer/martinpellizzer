Neocinnamomum wilsonii

Neocinnamomum wilsonii, commonly known as Wilson's cinnamon, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to China, specifically found in the mountainous regions of Yunnan and Sichuan provinces. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in the spring. Its bark and leaves contain essential oils, which have been used in traditional medicine.

Neocinnamomum wilsonii is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential economic applications.
