Nymphoides geminata

Nymphoides geminata, commonly known as the twin-leaf water plantain, is a aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in shallow freshwater habitats such as ponds and marshes. The plant has slender, ribbon-like leaves that are often floating or submerged. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in spikes, blooming in the summer months.

Nymphoides geminata is known for its ability to thrive in wetland environments and is sometimes used in ornamental water gardening.
