Nemophila racemosa

Nemophila racemosa, commonly known as the clustered blue-eyed Mary, is a low-growing annual plant native to western North America.

It features small, showy flowers that range in color from white to blue, often with a distinctive yellow center. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
