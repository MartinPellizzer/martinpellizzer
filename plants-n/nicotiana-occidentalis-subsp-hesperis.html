Nicotiana occidentalis subsp. hesperis

Nicotiana occidentalis subsp.

hesperis, commonly known as the western nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and lobed leaves.

The plant produces small, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple, which attract pollinators such as moths and bees. It thrives in open, sunny habitats and is often found in dry grasslands and disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
