Nepeta apuleji

Nepeta apuleji, commonly known as catnip, is a flowering plant in the mint family (Lamiaceae).

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers arranged in whorled clusters. It is native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia, and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Nepeta apuleji is known for its aromatic properties and its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
