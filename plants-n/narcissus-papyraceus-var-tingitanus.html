Narcissus papyraceus var. tingitanus

Narcissus papyraceus var. tingitanus, commonly known as the Tingitanian daffodil, is a rare and ancient variety of daffodil native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its narrow, papery tepals that give it its scientific name, and its flowers typically bloom in early spring. The plant prefers well-drained, sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family and is noted for its delicate appearance and historical significance in botany.
