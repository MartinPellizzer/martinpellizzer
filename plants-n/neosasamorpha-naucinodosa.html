Neosasamorpha naucinodosa

Neosasamorpha naucinodosa, commonly known as the golden rain tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically pinnately arranged and have a bright green color. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes, which bloom in the spring. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Neosasamorpha naucinodosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
