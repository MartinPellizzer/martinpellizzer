Nonatelia hispida

Nonatelia hispida, commonly known as the roughleaf nonatelia, is a herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, stiff hairs, giving them a rough texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Nonatelia hispida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
