Nanorrhinum ramosissimum

Nanorrhinum ramosissimum, commonly known as the desert hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, characterized by its woody stems and dense, branching structure. The plant produces showy, five-petaled yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are small, oval, and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in hot, dry environments.

Nanorrhinum ramosissimum is adapted to survive in harsh desert conditions, making it a resilient and hardy species.
