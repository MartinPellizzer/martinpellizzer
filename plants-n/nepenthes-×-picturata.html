Nepenthes × picturata

Nepenthes × picturata, commonly known as the picturata pitcher plant, is a hybrid species within the genus Nepenthes, known for its striking and colorful foliage.

It originates from the Borneo region, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its elongated, pitcher-shaped traps that are often adorned with intricate patterns and vivid hues. These traps secrete a digestive fluid to break down prey, a key adaptation for nutrient acquisition in its nutrient-poor habitat.

Its unique appearance and ecological role make it a notable subject in both botanical study and horticultural interest.
