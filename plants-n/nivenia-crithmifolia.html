Nivenia crithmifolia

Nivenia crithmifolia, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. It is often found growing in rocky or coastal habitats where it is exposed to salt spray.

Nivenia crithmifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to withstand harsh maritime conditions.
