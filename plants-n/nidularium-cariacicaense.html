Nidularium cariacicaense

Nidularium cariacicaense, commonly known as the Brazilian bromeliad, is a member of the Bromeliaceae family and is native to Brazil.

It is a terrestrial plant characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central tank to collect water. The plant produces striking, colorful inflorescences that bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow. Its foliage is often covered in fine, hair-like structures that aid in water retention.

Nidularium cariacicaense thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical forests.
