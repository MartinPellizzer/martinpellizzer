Nenga geelvinkiana

Nenga geelvinkiana, commonly known as the golden bell orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow petals and a distinctive bell-shaped floral structure, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in the undergrowth of tropical forests. Its flowers bloom during the warmer months, attracting a variety of pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and limited natural habitat, Nenga geelvinkiana is considered a rare and endangered species.
