Nerium indicum var. odorum

Nerium indicum var. odorum, commonly known as the sweet shrub or oleander, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant has lance-shaped leaves and is known for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Due to its ornamental value, it is widely cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
