Nepeta calamintha

Nepeta calamintha, commonly known as catmint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like mint and lavender. The plant features square stems, opposite leaves, and small, fragrant blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are often used in herbal teas and have a mild minty scent.

Nepeta calamintha is also valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
