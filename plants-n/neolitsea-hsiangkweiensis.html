Neolitsea hsiangkweiensis

Neolitsea hsiangkweiensis, commonly known as the Hsiangkwei Chinese-laurel, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to China, specifically the provinces of Guangdong and Guangxi, where it grows in subtropical forests. The plant is characterized by its evergreen foliage, with lance-shaped leaves that have a glossy surface and a distinct aromatic scent. It produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by spherical berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is occasionally used in traditional medicine.
