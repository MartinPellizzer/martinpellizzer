Nectandra punctata

Nectandra punctata, commonly known as the spotted sandarac tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its aromatic, dark-brown wood with distinctive yellow streaks. The leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical, often with a slightly glossy surface. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by dark, fleshy fruits.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in the production of sandarac resin.
