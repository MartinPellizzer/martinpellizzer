Narcissus provincialis

Narcissus provincialis, commonly known as the Spanish daffodil, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers with a white throat. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are strap-shaped and grass-like, emerging from a basal rosette of bulbs.

Narcissus provincialis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its early spring blooms.
