Nicotiana decurrens

Nicotiana decurrens, commonly known as the running tobacco or wild tobacco, is a herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Nicotiana decurrens belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and contains alkaloids, including nicotine.

It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in partial to full sun.
