Nananthus thudichumii

Nananthus thudichumii, commonly known as the Thudichumii nananthus, is a rare and endemic plant species native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, grass-like foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
