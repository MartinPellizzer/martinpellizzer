Nemesia glabriuscula

Nemesia glabriuscula, commonly known as the velvet flower, is a low-growing annual plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological diversity.

Nemesia glabriuscula is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ease of care.
