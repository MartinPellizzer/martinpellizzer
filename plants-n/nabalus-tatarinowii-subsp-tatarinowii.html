Nabalus tatarinowii subsp. tatarinowii

Nabalus tatarinowii subsp.

tatarinowii, commonly known as the Siberian bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the cold regions of Siberia and northern Asia. It is characterized by its upright stems and showy blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, while its roots are fibrous and adapted to cold climates.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable growing conditions.
