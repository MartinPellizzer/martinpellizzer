Nania collina

Nania collina, commonly known as the alpine bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It typically grows in rocky and well-drained soils at high altitudes. The plant features slender stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, which are usually blue or purple in color. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, adapted to withstand cold and windy conditions.

Nania collina is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in alpine ecosystems.
