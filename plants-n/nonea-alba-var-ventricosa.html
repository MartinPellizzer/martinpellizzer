Nonea alba var. ventricosa

Nonea alba var. ventricosa, commonly known as the white sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its white or pale pink flowers and lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. It has a compact growth habit and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in landscaping.
