Nymphaea magnifica

Nymphaea magnifica, commonly known as the magnificent water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to tropical regions.

It features large, showy flowers that bloom in shades of white, pink, and red, often with a distinctive yellow center. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats, such as ponds and lakes. Its leaves are broad and floating, with a waxy surface that aids in water repellence.

The botanical structure of Nymphaea magnifica includes a rhizomatous root system that anchors it in the substrate.
