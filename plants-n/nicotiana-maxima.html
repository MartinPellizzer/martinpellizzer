Nicotiana maxima

Nicotiana maxima, commonly known as the wild tobacco plant, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to purple, and its broad, heart-shaped leaves. It is known for its high nicotine content, which makes it both toxic and useful in various applications.
