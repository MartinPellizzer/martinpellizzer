Nepeta ciliaris

Nepeta ciliaris, commonly known as catnip, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like mint and basil. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and small, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to white. Its most notable feature is the presence of nepetalactone, a compound that attracts cats and has calming effects on them.

Nepeta ciliaris is often cultivated for its ornamental value and traditional medicinal uses.
