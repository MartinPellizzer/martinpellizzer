Narcissus × elatior

Narcissus × elatior, commonly known as the Crown Imperial, is a hybrid species of daffodil characterized by its striking, tall flower stems and large, showy blooms.

It features a single, erect flower at the top of the stem, which is typically composed of a large, trumpet-shaped corona surrounded by petal-like tepals. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and is known for its vibrant yellow or orange hues. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters.

The foliage consists of broad, strap-like leaves that emerge from the base of the plant.
