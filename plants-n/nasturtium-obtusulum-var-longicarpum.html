Nasturtium obtusulum var. longicarpum

Nasturtium obtusulum var. longicarpum, commonly known as the long-seeded nasturtium, is a herbaceous annual plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant produces elongated, cylindrical seed pods that are distinctive and often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and have a slightly succulent texture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and edible qualities, making it a popular choice for gardens and herbal use.
