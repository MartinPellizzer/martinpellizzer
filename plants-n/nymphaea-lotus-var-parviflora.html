Nymphaea lotus var. parviflora

Nymphaea lotus var. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered water lily, is a aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features broad, floating leaves that provide shade on the water surface. Its flowers are typically white or pink, with a subtle fragrance, and bloom in the early morning. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments.

It has a rhizomatous root system that anchors it in the mud at the bottom of ponds or lakes.
