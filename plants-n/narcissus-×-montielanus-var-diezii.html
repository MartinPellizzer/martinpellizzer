Narcissus × montielanus var. diezii

Narcissus × montielanus var. diezii, commonly known as the Diez's daffodil, is a hybrid species within the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, strap-like leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that typically bloom in early spring. The plant is native to the Iberian Peninsula and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant yellow blooms.

This variety is relatively hardy and adaptable, making it a popular choice for gardeners in temperate climates.
