Nidularium makoyanum

Nidularium makoyanum, commonly known as the golden torch plant, is a member of the Araceae family and is native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful inflorescence that resembles a torch, with vibrant yellow and orange spathes. The plant produces small, white flowers enclosed within a spathe, which is typical of the Araceae family. It thrives in humid, shady environments and is often grown as a decorative houseplant.

Nidularium makoyanum is known for its ornamental value and is cultivated for its unique and eye-catching appearance.
