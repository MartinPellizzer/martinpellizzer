Nothocestrum inconcinnum

Nothocestrum inconcinnum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved nightshade, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to Central America and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe, containing seeds that are dispersed by animals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
