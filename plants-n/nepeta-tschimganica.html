Nepeta tschimganica

Nepeta tschimganica, commonly known as the Carpathian catmint, is a perennial herb native to the Carpathian Mountains in Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal remedies for their calming properties.

Nepeta tschimganica thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
