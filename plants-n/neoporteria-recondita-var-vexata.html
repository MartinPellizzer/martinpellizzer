Neoporteria recondita var. vexata

Neoporteria recondita var. vexata, commonly known as the rare velvetleaf, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its velvety, dark green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pale green to yellowish in color, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
