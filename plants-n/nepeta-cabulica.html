Nepeta cabulica

Nepeta cabulica, commonly known as catnip, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves contain nepetalactone, a compound responsible for its stimulating effect on cats.

Nepeta cabulica is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in herbal remedies.
