Nephelaphyllum beccarii

Nephelaphyllum beccarii, commonly known as the cloud fern, is a unique epiphytic plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on tree trunks or branches. Its fronds are pinnately compound, with a delicate, feathery appearance that contributes to its ornamental appeal. The plant's roots are specialized for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and debris around it.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and challenging species to cultivate outside its natural range.
