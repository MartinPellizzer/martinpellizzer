Nepeta eriostachya var. eriostachya

Nepeta eriostachya var. eriostachya, commonly known as catmint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features aromatic, lance-shaped leaves with a mint-like scent and produces spikes of pale blue to purple flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens or borders. Its foliage is often used in herbal teas and traditional medicine for its calming properties.

The variety is particularly valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
