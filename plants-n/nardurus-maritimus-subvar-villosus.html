Nardurus maritimus subvar. villosus

Nardurus maritimus subvar. villosus, commonly known as the hairy sea hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy, five-petaled yellow flowers. The plant thrives in sandy or rocky coastal habitats, often growing in saline environments. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, wind-dispersed seeds.

This subvariety is notable for its resilience to coastal conditions and its ecological importance in stabilizing dunes.
