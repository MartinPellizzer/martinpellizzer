Nipponocalamus mayumianus

Nipponocalamus mayumianus, commonly known as the Mayumian bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to China.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically green and arranged in a dense, clumping growth habit. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in horticultural settings.
