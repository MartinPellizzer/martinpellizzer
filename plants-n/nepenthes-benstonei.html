Nepenthes benstonei

Nepenthes benstonei, commonly known as the Benstone's pitcher plant, is a tropical vine native to the island of Borneo.

It is characterized by its large, waxy pitcher-shaped leaves that secrete a digestive fluid to trap and digest insects. The plant's unique morphology includes a slender stem and tendrils that aid in climbing through its dense forest habitat. It thrives in humid, shaded environments with high rainfall, typical of the lowland rainforests of Borneo.

Due to its specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
