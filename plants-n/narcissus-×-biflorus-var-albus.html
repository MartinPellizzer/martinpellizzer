Narcissus × biflorus var. albus

Narcissus × biflorus var. albus, commonly known as the white daffodil, is a hybrid species within the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking white petals and a yellow corona, which distinguish it from other daffodil varieties. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters, with narrow, linear leaves that are grass-like in appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

This variety is prized for its elegant appearance and is often used in ornamental gardening.
