Neoregelia johannis

Neoregelia johannis, commonly known as the Johannes' bromeliad, is a striking plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, colorful bracts that surround its flowering spikes. The plant features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that collect water, creating a micro-ecosystem. It produces showy inflorescences with red and yellow flowers, adding visual interest to indoor and outdoor gardens.

Neoregelia johannis thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture and humidity for optimal growth.
