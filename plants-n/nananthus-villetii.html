Nananthus villetii

Nananthus villetii, commonly known as the velvetleaf nananthus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom during the rainy season. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies of tropical flora.
