Neoporteria jussieui var. trapichensis

Neoporteria jussieui var. trapichensis, commonly known as the Tropicana orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and yellow. The plant produces pendulous inflorescences with numerous small, delicate flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in humid, warm environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its unique floral structure and ornamental value make it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation.
