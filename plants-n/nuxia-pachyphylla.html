Nuxia pachyphylla

Nuxia pachyphylla, commonly known as the thick-leaved nuxia, is a succulent shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in dry environments. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers during the rainy season, adding ornamental value. Its root system is extensive, helping it anchor in rocky or sandy soils.

Nuxia pachyphylla is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and resilience.
