Nectandra schippii

Nectandra schippii, commonly known as the Schippi's sandalwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, fragrant wood that is highly valued in traditional medicine and perfumery. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments within lowland rainforests. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a glossy, dark green appearance.

Nectandra schippii is also noted for its small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, round fruits containing smooth, black seeds.
