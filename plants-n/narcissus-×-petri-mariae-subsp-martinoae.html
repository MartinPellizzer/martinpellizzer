Narcissus × petri-mariae subsp. martinoae

Narcissus × petri-mariae subsp.

martinoae, commonly known as the Martinoa narcissus, is a hybrid species within the Amaryllidaceae family. It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers with a distinctive cup-shaped corona.

The plant typically grows to a height of 30–50 cm, with narrow, grass-like leaves. It is native to the Iberian Peninsula and thrives in well-drained soils in full sun.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant blooms.
