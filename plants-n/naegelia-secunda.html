Naegelia secunda

Naegelia secunda, commonly known as the mountain flax, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky and well-drained soils. The plant features slender, erect stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small, blue to purple in color, and are clustered in spikes.

Naegelia secunda is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in alpine gardens.
