Nemophila discoidalis var. nigra

Nemophila discoidalis var. nigra, commonly known as black-eyed Susan, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bright yellow flowers with dark centers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often found in prairies, meadows, and along stream banks.

The variety 'nigra' is distinguished by its dark, almost black center in the flower, adding to its ornamental appeal.
