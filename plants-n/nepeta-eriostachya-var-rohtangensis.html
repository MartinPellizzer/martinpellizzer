Nepeta eriostachya var. rohtangensis

Nepeta eriostachya var. rohtangensis, commonly known as the Rohtang catmint, is a rare flowering plant native to the Himalayan region of India.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, pale blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude meadows and rocky areas, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
