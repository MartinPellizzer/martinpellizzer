It is characterized by its dark, leathery fronds that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, silky hairs. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest undergrowth or rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are thick and creeping, allowing it to spread and form dense colonies.

The spore-producing structures, called sori, are clustered on the underside of the fronds, contributing to its reproductive strategy.
