Narcissus tazetta var. algirus

Narcissus tazetta var. algirus, commonly known as the Algerian daffodil, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces clusters of fragrant, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of white, yellow, and orange, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens. It is relatively hardy and can thrive in a variety of soil types, making it a popular choice for gardeners.
