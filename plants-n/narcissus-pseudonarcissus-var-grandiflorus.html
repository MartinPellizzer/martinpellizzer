Narcissus pseudonarcissus var. grandiflorus

Narcissus pseudonarcissus var. grandiflorus, commonly known as the giant daffodil, is a variant of the common daffodil characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is native to southern Europe. The plant features a bulbous root system and lance-shaped, grass-like leaves. Its flowers typically bloom in early spring and are known for their bright, trumpet-shaped petals.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
