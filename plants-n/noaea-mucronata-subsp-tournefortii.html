Noaea mucronata subsp. tournefortii

Noaea mucronata subsp.

tournefortii, commonly known as the Turkestan sandmat, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia. It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure with gray-green, hairy leaves.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in the spring. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils.

Its adaptations include reduced leaf size and deep root systems to conserve water and withstand harsh conditions.
