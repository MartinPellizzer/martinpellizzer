Neoporteria sociabilis var. napina

Neoporteria sociabilis var. napina, commonly known as the Napina Stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features fleshy, thick leaves that are typically blue-green in color, adapted to store water in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange during the summer months. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking low-maintenance, drought-tolerant plants.
