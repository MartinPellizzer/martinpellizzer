Narcissus × paivae

Narcissus × paivae, commonly known as the Amsterdam daffodil, is a hybrid species of daffodil originating from the Netherlands.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically yellow with a white or greenish throat. The plant is a bulbous perennial, thriving in well-drained soil and full sun. Its foliage consists of strap-like leaves that are broad and grass-like in appearance.

Narcissus × paivae is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
