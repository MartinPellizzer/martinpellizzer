Narcissus reflexus subsp. capax

Narcissus reflexus subsp.

capax, commonly known as the narrow-leaved daffodil, is a species native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and a single, elongated flower with a narrow, funnel-shaped corona.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its flowers bloom in spring, producing bright yellow petals and a distinctive, narrow crown.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens.
