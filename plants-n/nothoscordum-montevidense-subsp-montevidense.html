Nothoscordum montevidense subsp. montevidense

Nothoscordum montevidense subsp.

montevidense, commonly known as the Montevideo garlic. It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America, particularly Uruguay and parts of Argentina.

The plant features strap-like, narrow leaves and produces clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers. Its bulbs are similar to garlic in appearance and are used in local cuisines.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open areas.
