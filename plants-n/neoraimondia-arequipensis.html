Neoraimondia arequipensis

Neoraimondia arequipensis, commonly known as the Arequipa starling tree, is a species native to the Andean region of Peru.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows in humid, subtropical climates. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a smooth, glossy surface. Its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, often producing fragrant blooms.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique foliage and ecological significance.
