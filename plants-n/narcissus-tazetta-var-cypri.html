Narcissus tazetta var. cypri

Narcissus tazetta var. cypri, commonly known as the Cyprus daffodil, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to white, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is valued for its ornamental beauty and fragrance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

This variety is also notable for its historical significance in horticulture and its use in traditional medicine.
