Nasturtium palustre var. ramosum

Nasturtium palustre var. ramosum, commonly known as swamp nasturtium, is a flowering plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and vibrant orange or yellow flowers that bloom throughout the summer. The plant thrives in moist, often boggy soils and is often found near streams or in marshy areas. Its stems are typically sprawling or trailing, and it can grow up to several feet in length.

The plant is known for its peppery taste, making it a popular edible plant in some regions.
