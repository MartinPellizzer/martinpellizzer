Nyssa

Nyssa, commonly known as the water dogwood, is a genus of deciduous trees and shrubs belonging to the family Cornaceae.

These plants are characterized by their opposite, simple leaves and showy, clusters of small, white or yellowish flowers. The fruit is a drupe, typically containing a single seed, and is often referred to as a "nut.". Nyssa species are typically found in moist, wooded areas and are valued for their ornamental and ecological significance.

They also have historical uses in traditional medicine and as a source of timber.
