Nephelochloa persica

Nephelochloa persica, commonly known as Persian cloudgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-green foliage and tufted growth habit, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly hairy texture that helps reduce water loss.

Nephelochloa persica is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal and drought tolerance.
