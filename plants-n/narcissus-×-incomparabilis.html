Narcissus × incomparabilis

Narcissus × incomparabilis, commonly known as the Giant Daffodil, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Narcissus poeticus and Narcissus cyclaminiflorus.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers with a distinctive cup-shaped corona. The plant typically grows to heights of up to 1.5 meters, making it one of the tallest daffodil varieties. Its foliage consists of long, strap-like leaves that are broad and sturdy.

This species is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
