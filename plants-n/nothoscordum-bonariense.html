Nothoscordum bonariense

Nothoscordum bonariense, commonly known as the Brazilian garlic or wild garlic, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its strap-shaped, grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its bulbs, which resemble garlic, are edible and have a mild, garlicky flavor.
