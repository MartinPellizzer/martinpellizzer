Nidularium innocentii var. striatum

Nidularium innocentii var. striatum, commonly known as the striped false arum, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe. The plant features broad, waxy leaves with a distinct veining pattern that enhances its ornamental appeal. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its striking appearance makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and botanical collections.
