Notocactus langsdorfii f. prolifer

Notocactus langsdorfii f. prolifer, commonly known as the proliferating notocactus, is a rare and unique cactus species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and a dense covering of white to pale green spines. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. Its f. prolifer form is notable for its tendency to produce offsets, making it relatively easy to propagate through vegetative means.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a valuable and often sought-after specimen among cactus enthusiasts.
