Nepeta wellmanii

Nepeta wellmanii, commonly known as Wellman's catmint, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue to purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice among gardeners.
