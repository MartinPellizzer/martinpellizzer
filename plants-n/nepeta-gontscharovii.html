Nepeta gontscharovii

Nepeta gontscharovii, commonly known as Gontscharoff's catmint, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces spikes of small, lavender to pale blue flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage is often fragrant and can be used in herbal teas or as a decorative plant in gardens.

Nepeta gontscharovii is relatively low maintenance and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.
