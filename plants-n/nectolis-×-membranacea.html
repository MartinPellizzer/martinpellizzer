Nectolis × membranacea

Nectolis × membranacea, commonly known as the hybrid white mangosteen, is a tropical plant belonging to the family Myrtaceae.

It is a cross between Nectaris and Membranaceae species, resulting in a unique combination of traits. The plant is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves and fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit, resembling a small, segmented orange, is typically harvested when fully mature.

The botanical interest in this species lies in its hybrid vigor and potential for ornamental and medicinal use.
