Nectandra falcifolia

Nectandra falcifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf sandarac tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and fragrant flowers. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by woody, conical capsules containing aromatic resin. Its resin, known as sandarac, has been historically used in varnishes and incense.

Nectandra falcifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitats.
