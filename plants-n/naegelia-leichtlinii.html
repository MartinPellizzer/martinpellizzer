Naegelia leichtlinii

Naegelia leichtlinii, commonly known as the desert bell, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form with small, leathery leaves that are adapted to conserve water. The plant produces striking, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, which bloom in the spring. Naegelia leichtlinii thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular ornamental plant in dry climates.
