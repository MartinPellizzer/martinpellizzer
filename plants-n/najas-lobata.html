Najas lobata

Najas lobata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf watermilfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It belongs to the family Najadaceae and is characterized by its narrow, ribbon-like leaves that are often arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically found in clusters near the water's surface. Najas lobata plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

It is often found in still or slow-moving waters such as ponds and lakes.
