Nepeta nervosa var. nervosa

Nepeta nervosa var. nervosa, commonly known as catnip, is a flowering plant in the mint family (Lamiaceae).

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with prominent veins. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that are clustered in whorls along the stem. It is native to Europe, western Asia, and northern Africa, and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Nepeta nervosa var. nervosa is widely cultivated for its aromatic properties and its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
