Neomammillaria napina

Neomammillaria napina, commonly known as the napina cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is a small, globular cactus with a short stem and a dense covering of spines. The plant typically produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are modified into spines, and it has a shallow root system adapted to dry conditions.

Neomammillaria napina is prized for its compact size and ornamental value in succulent collections.
