Nemophila menziesii var. menziesii

Nemophila menziesii var. menziesii, commonly known as the California baby blue eyes, is a low-growing annual plant native to western North America.

It produces clusters of sky-blue flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May. The plant prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, often found in coastal and mountainous regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its delicate appearance.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often used in native plant gardens.
