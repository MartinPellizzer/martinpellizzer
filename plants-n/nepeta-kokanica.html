Nepeta kokanica

Nepeta kokanica, commonly known as the Balkan catmint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, blue or violet flowers that are clustered in whorls along the stems. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped and have a minty fragrance when crushed.

Nepeta kokanica is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
