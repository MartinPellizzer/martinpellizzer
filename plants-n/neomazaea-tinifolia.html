Neomazaea tinifolia

Neomazaea tinifolia, commonly known as the slender leaf mazaca, is a member of the Araceae family, characterized by its unique floral structure.

It is native to tropical regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant features elongated, narrow leaves that are typically bright green with a waxy texture. Its inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe, which plays a role in attracting pollinators.

Neomazaea tinifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking foliage and unusual floral display.
