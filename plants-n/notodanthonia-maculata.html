Notodanthonia maculata

Notodanthonia maculata, commonly known as the spotted wallaby grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and distinctive dark green leaves with a prominent midrib. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear pale blue to purple flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Notodanthonia maculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting native wildlife.
