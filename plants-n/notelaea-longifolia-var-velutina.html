Notelaea longifolia var. velutina

Notelaea longifolia var. velutina, commonly known as the velvet-leafed notelaea, is a variant of the notelaea genus native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its velvety, hairy foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in coastal and lowland forest environments, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a distinct, aromatic scent.

The flowers are small and clustered, producing yellowish-green blooms that attract native pollinators.
