Najas tenuis

Najas tenuis, commonly known as the slender watermilfoil, is a small, aquatic plant belonging to the family Najadaceae.

It is characterized by its narrow, ribbon-like leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. This species thrives in freshwater environments such as ponds, lakes, and slow-moving streams. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, usually appearing in clusters at the base of the leaves.

Najas tenuis plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for microorganisms and invertebrates.
