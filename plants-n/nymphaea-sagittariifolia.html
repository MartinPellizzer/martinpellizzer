Nymphaea sagittariifolia

Nymphaea sagittariifolia, commonly known as the arrow-leaved water lily, is a aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features floating leaves with arrow-shaped lobes and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often in shallow ponds or lakes. Its roots anchor in the substrate while its leaves and flowers float on the water surface.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological role in supporting aquatic life.
