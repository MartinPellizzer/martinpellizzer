Nardosmia frigidua var. corymbosa

Nardosmia frigidua var. corymbosa, commonly known as the cold mountain sweetroot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It features a robust rhizomatous root system and produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers during the summer months. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in cool, moist environments with well-drained soil, often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

The species is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing benefits.
