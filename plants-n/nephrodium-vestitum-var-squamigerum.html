Nephrodium vestitum var. squamigerum

Nephrodium vestitum var. squamigerum, commonly known as the velvet fern, is a unique plant characterized by its dense, velvety foliage.

It is native to the moist, shaded environments of tropical regions, particularly in parts of Southeast Asia. The plant features a creeping rhizome with clusters of small, scale-like leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are inconspicuous, typically green or brown, and it reproduces through spores rather than seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in humid, low-light conditions.
