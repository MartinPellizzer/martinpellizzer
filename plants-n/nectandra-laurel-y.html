Nectandra laurel-y

Nectandra laurel-y, commonly known as the sweet laurel, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, white, clustered flowers that are followed by oval, woody fruits containing aromatic seeds. Its bark and leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported therapeutic properties.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in warm climates for landscaping purposes.
