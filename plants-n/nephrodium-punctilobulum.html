Nephrodium punctilobulum

Nephrodium punctilobulum, commonly known as the spotted fern, is a species of fern characterized by its distinctive, intricate fronds.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is native to tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features a rhizomatous growth habit, with fiddlehead-like young fronds that uncurl as they mature. Its leaves are typically bi- or tri-pinnate, with a delicate, lace-like appearance.

The spore-producing structures, called sori, are arranged in clusters on the undersides of the fronds.
