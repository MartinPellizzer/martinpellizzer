Nepenthes tayninhensis

Nepenthes tayninhensis, commonly known as the Tayninh Pitcher Plant, is a tropical carnivorous plant native to the mountainous regions of Vietnam.

It is characterized by its large, elongated pitcher-shaped leaves that secrete a nutrient-rich liquid to attract and trap prey. The plant's pitchers are typically green or red in color, with a waxy surface that aids in the adhesion of insects. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires high levels of atmospheric moisture to flourish.

Due to its restricted habitat, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
