Nymphaea kimberleyensis

Nymphaea kimberleyensis, commonly known as the Kimberley water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to the northern regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its large, showy white to pink flowers that bloom above the water surface, often during the wet season. The plant has broad, floating leaves that provide habitat for various aquatic organisms. Its roots anchor it in the muddy substrates of still or slow-moving water bodies.

Nymphaea kimberleyensis plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by supporting biodiversity and contributing to the aquatic ecosystem's balance.
