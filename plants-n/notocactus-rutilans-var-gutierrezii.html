Notocactus rutilans var. gutierrezii

Notocactus rutilans var. gutierrezii, commonly known as the Mexican fire cactus, is a member of the Cactaceae family native to Mexico.

It is a low-growing, sprawling cactus with cylindrical stems that are typically ribbed and covered in white to yellowish spines. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for both indoor and outdoor cultivation.

Its unique appearance and ornamental value make it a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
