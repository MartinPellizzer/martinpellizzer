Nectandra leucoxyla

Nectandra leucoxyla, commonly known as the white-laurel or white-leafed sandarac, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, cinnamon, and avocado. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers that are often white or pale yellow. Its bark and leaves contain volatile oils, making it valuable in traditional medicine and as a source of natural resins.

The tree is also appreciated for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its wood.
