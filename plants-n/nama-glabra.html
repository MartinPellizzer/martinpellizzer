Nama glabra

Nama glabra, commonly known as the smooth nama, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, adapting to harsh environmental conditions. Its leaves are smooth and often covered in a fine layer of wax, which helps reduce water loss.

Nama glabra produces small, daisy-like flowers that range in color from white to yellow, attracting various pollinators.
