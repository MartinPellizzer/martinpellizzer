Nothoscordum basalticum

Nothoscordum basalticum, commonly known as the basalt garlic.

It is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.

Nothoscordum basalticum is often found in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to drought conditions.
