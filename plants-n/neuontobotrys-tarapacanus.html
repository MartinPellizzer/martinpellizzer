Neuontobotrys tarapacanus

Neuontobotrys tarapacanus, commonly known as the Atacama lichen, is a unique lichen species native to the hyperarid regions of the Atacama Desert in Chile.

It is a symbiotic composite of a cyanobacterial partner and a fungal component, which allows it to survive in extreme environmental conditions. The plant forms a crust-like structure that adheres tightly to rocky surfaces, enabling it to conserve moisture and withstand intense solar radiation. Its ability to photosynthesize during brief periods of rainfall makes it a vital component of the desert's fragile ecosystem.

Due to its specialized adaptations, it plays a crucial role in soil stabilization and nutrient cycling in one of Earth's most arid environments.
