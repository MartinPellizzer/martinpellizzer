Nepeta ucranica

Nepeta ucranica, commonly known as Ukrainian catmint, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a fuzzy texture and produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Nepeta ucranica is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and as a source of nectar for pollinators.
