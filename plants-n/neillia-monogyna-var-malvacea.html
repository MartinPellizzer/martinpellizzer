Neillia monogyna var. malvacea

Neillia monogyna var. malvacea, commonly known as the Malvaceae-like shrub, is a variant of the Neillia monogyna species native to South America.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton plants, and is characterized by its showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub with woody stems and broad, ovate leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Its ornamental value is appreciated for its vibrant floral display and adaptability to various garden settings.
