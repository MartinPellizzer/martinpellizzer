Neoregelia desenganensis

Neoregelia desenganensis, commonly known as the Brazilian velvet plant, is a striking bromeliad native to the Atlantic rainforests of Brazil.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense, velvety coating, which helps it retain moisture in its humid, tropical environment. The plant produces strikingly colorful inflorescences, often in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom from the center of the leaf rosette. Its leaves are typically broad and pointed, with a waxy surface that aids in water retention.

Neoregelia desenganensis is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its adaptability and ornamental appeal.
