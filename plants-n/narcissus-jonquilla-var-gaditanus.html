Narcissus jonquilla var. gaditanus

Narcissus jonquilla var. gaditanus, commonly known as the Spanish daffodil, is a variety of the genus Narcissus native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers with a single, upright tepal that distinguishes it from other daffodil varieties. The plant typically grows to a height of about 15 to 20 centimeters, with narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is known for its early spring blooming and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
