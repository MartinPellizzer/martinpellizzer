Nabiasodendron ceylanicum

Nabiasodendron ceylanicum, commonly known as the Ceylon cinnamon tree, is a small evergreen tree native to Sri Lanka.

It is characterized by its aromatic bark, which is harvested for its medicinal and culinary uses. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and dark green in color. Its flowers are small and white, typically appearing in clusters, and it produces small, red berries.

The tree is valued for its essential oils and has been used in traditional medicine for centuries.
