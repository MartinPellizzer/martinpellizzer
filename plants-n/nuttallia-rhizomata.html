Nuttallia rhizomata

Nuttallia rhizomata, commonly known as the creeping marsh fern, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It features a rhizomatous growth system that allows it to spread vegetatively and colonize moist, nutrient-rich soils. The plant produces erect, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive, fern-like appearance, and its small, greenish-white flowers are borne in clusters. It thrives in shallow water or saturated soil conditions, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.

Nuttallia rhizomata plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
