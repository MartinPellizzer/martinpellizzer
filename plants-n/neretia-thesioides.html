Neretia thesioides

Neretia thesioides, commonly known as the golden naiad, is a submerged aquatic plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It has long, ribbon-like leaves that are typically bright yellow to green in color, depending on light conditions. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, which bloom underwater. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments and is often found in ponds and lakes.

Neretia thesioides is valued in aquatic gardening for its ornamental appeal and ability to improve water quality.
