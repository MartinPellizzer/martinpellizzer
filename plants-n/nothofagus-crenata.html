Nothofagus crenata

Nothofagus crenata, commonly known as the mountain beech, is a species of evergreen tree native to southern South America.

It belongs to the Nothofagaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by edible, chestnut-like fruits. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in montane forests.

Nothofagus crenata plays a vital role in its ecosystem, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
