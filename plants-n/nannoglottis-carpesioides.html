Nannoglottis carpesioides

Nannoglottis carpesioides, commonly known as the smoothleaf bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has smooth, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
