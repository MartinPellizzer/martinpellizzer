Neolepisorus microsorioides

Neolepisorus microsorioides, commonly known as the small-sori fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, elongated fronds that are typically less than 30 centimeters in length. The plant features small, clustered sori on the underside of its leaves, which are covered by a thin, membranous indusium. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Neolepisorus microsorioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
