Napoleonaea angolensis

Napoleonaea angolensis, commonly known as the Angola napoleonaea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows in grasslands and savannas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, often in shades of yellow or green, and it produces edible pods that are used in traditional medicine.

The species is valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and as a source of forage for livestock.
