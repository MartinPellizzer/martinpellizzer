Neottopteris simonsiana

Neottopteris simonsiana, commonly known as the golden shield fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, leathery, shield-shaped fronds that are typically a deep green with a golden or bronze tint on the underside. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks. Its fronds can grow up to two feet in length and are divided into numerous small, lance-shaped segments.

Neottopteris simonsiana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appearance and ability to thrive in humid, partially shaded conditions.
