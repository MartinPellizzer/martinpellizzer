Nicotiana roemeriana

Nicotiana roemeriana, commonly known as the Roemer's tobacco, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant typically grows as an annual or biennial herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are broad and ovate, often with a waxy surface, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Nicotiana roemeriana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential in botanical research and horticulture.
