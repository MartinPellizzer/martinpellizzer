Narcissus poeticus proles angustifolus

Narcissus poeticus proles angustifolus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved daffodil, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, strap-like leaves and bright yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant is native to southern Europe and thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is a hardy perennial that can withstand mild frosts.

Its scientific name reflects its historical association with poetry and its distinctive floral structure.
