Nemopogon glaucum

Nemopogon glaucum, commonly known as the bluemink grass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, blue-green foliage and slender, upright growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, spikelets.

Nemopogon glaucum plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
