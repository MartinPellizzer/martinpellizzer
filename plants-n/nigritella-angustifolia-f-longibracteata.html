Nigritella angustifolia f. longibracteata

Nigritella angustifolia f. longibracteata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved nigritella with long bracts, is a distinctive variant of the nigritella species.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves and prominent, elongated bracts that encase the flower clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or stream banks. Its flowers are small and clustered, often displaying a range of colors from white to pink.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
