Nepeta sessilifolia

Nepeta sessilifolia, commonly known as catmint, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems and small, fragrant blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its aromatic quality. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Nepeta sessilifolia is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
