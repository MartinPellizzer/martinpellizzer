Nepeta saposhnikowii

Nepeta saposhnikowii, commonly known as Siberian catmint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported calming and antispasmodic properties.

Nepeta saposhnikowii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
