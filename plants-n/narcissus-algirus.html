Narcissus algirus

Narcissus algirus, commonly known as the Algerian daffodil, is a species of flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its narrow, strap-like leaves and a single, trumpet-shaped flower that is typically yellow in color. The plant produces a fragrant, cup-shaped bloom that attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy perennial suitable for rock gardens and borders.

The genus Narcissus is part of the Amaryllidaceae family, which includes many other bulbous plants known for their showy flowers.
