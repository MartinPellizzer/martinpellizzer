Narcissus tazettus

Narcissus tazettus, commonly known as the Persian daffodil, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its trumpet-shaped flowers, which typically range in color from white to yellow. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of Turkey, Iran, and the Caucasus. It produces a single stem with several leaves and blooms in early spring.

Narcissus tazettus is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant flowers.
