Nymphaea harleyi

Nymphaea harleyi, commonly known as the Harley's water lily, is a species of aquatic plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It belongs to the family Nymphaeaceae and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that bloom above the water surface. The plant has broad, floating leaves that provide habitat for various aquatic organisms. Its roots anchor it in the muddy substrate, while its rhizomes help in vegetative propagation.

Nymphaea harleyi plays an important role in maintaining the ecological balance of its wetland habitat.
