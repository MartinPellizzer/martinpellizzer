Nipponocalamus tosaensis

Nipponocalamus tosaensis, commonly known as the Tosa bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. This plant is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests and stream banks. It has a clumping growth habit and can reach heights of up to 2 meters.

The Tosa bamboo is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional Japanese crafts.
