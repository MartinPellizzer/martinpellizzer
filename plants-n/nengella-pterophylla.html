Nengella pterophylla

Nengella pterophylla, commonly known as the velvetleaf pterophylla, is a unique flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, which bloom during the late spring to early summer season. Nengella pterophylla thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural study and ecological research.
