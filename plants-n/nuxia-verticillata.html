Nuxia verticillata

Nuxia verticillata, commonly known as the mountain sour plum, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, spreading growth habit and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers followed by small, fleshy, orange-red fruits that are edible. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Nuxia verticillata has traditional uses in local medicine and is also valued for its ornamental appeal.
