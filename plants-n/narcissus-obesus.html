Narcissus obesus

Narcissus obesus, commonly known as the giant daffodil, is a striking member of the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers with a prominent, trumpet-shaped corona. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, making it one of the tallest daffodils. Its leaves are long and strap-like, forming a dense rosette at the base.

Native to southern Europe, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight.
