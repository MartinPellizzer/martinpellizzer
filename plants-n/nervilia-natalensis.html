Nervilia natalensis

Nervilia natalensis, commonly known as Natal's nerve plant, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to South Africa.

It is a terrestrial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky outcrops. The plant features a slender, upright stem with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, hair-like structures. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, arranged in a loose spike, and are characterized by their intricate, delicate structure.

Nervilia natalensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
