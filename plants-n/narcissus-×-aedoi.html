Narcissus × aedoi

Narcissus × aedoi, commonly known as the hybrid daffodil, is a cross between different species of daffodils, resulting in a diverse range of floral forms.

It typically features trumpet-shaped flowers with six tepals, often in vibrant yellow or white hues. The plant is a hardy perennial, thriving in well-drained soil and full sun. It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental beauty in gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for both landscape design and floral arrangements.
