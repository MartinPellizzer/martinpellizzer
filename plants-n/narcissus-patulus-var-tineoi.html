Narcissus patulus var. tineoi

Narcissus patulus var. tineoi, commonly known as the Tineo narcissus, is a rare and distinctive variety of daffodil native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its large, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant features a single, upright stem that bears one to three blooms, each with a prominent, recurved corona. Its leaves are long, narrow, and grass-like, forming a basal rosette.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
