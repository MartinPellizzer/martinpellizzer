Nicotiana tabacum convar. virginica

Nicotiana tabacum convar. virginica, commonly known as the wild tobacco plant, is a species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit, hairy stems, and opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves. The plant produces striking purple or violet flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is a member of the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is known for its alkaloid content, including nicotine.

This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and floral display.
