Nervilia viridis

Nervilia viridis, commonly known as the green nerve orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features a slender, upright stem with a single, lance-shaped leaf. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in a loose raceme, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Nervilia viridis is characterized by its intricate root system and its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.
