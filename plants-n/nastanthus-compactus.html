Nastanthus compactus

Nastanthus compactus, commonly known as the compact nasturtium, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, bushy growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that come in shades of orange and yellow. The plant produces edible leaves and seeds, which are often used in culinary applications. Its flowers are typically borne on short pedicels, and the plant thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Nastanthus compactus is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and mustard.
