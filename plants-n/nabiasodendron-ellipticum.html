Nabiasodendron ellipticum

Nabiasodendron ellipticum, commonly known as the European yew, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, columnar shape and reddish-brown bark that peels in thin, papery layers. The plant produces small, crimson seeds enclosed in a fleshy, orange-red aril, which is highly visible and attractive to birds. Its foliage consists of dark green, needle-like leaves that are arranged in spirals along the branches.

The European yew is known for its longevity and has been historically significant in various cultural and medicinal contexts.
