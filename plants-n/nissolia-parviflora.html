Nissolia parviflora

Nissolia parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered nissolia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. The plant has simple, oval-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Nissolia parviflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
