Nepenthes peltata

Nepenthes peltata, commonly known as the broad-leafed pitcher plant, is a tropical carnivorous plant native to Borneo.

It is characterized by its large, broad, waxy leaves that form distinctive pitcher-shaped traps. These traps secrete a digestive fluid that lures and dissolves insect prey. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires consistent moisture.

Its unique morphology and specialized trapping mechanism make it a fascinating subject in botanical studies.
