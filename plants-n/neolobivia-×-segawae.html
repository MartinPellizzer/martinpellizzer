Neolobivia × segawae

Neolobivia × segawae, commonly known as the Segawa's lobivia, is a hybrid cactus species native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, globular shape and ribbed structure. The plant typically features spiny areoles and produces yellow or orange flowers during the warmer months. It is well adapted to drought conditions, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
