Nepeta ucranica subsp. schischkinii

Nepeta ucranica subsp.

schischkinii, commonly known as the Crimean catmint, is a perennial herb native to the Crimean Peninsula. It features aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and produces spikes of blue to purple flowers.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and mild mint-like scent.

This subspecies is closely related to other members of the Nepeta genus, sharing similar growth habits and floral structures.
