Nasturtium brevicaule

Nasturtium brevicaule, commonly known as the dwarf nasturtium, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that come in shades of orange and yellow. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated edge, and its stems are hairy and upright. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Nasturtium brevicaule is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible flowers, which have a peppery taste.
