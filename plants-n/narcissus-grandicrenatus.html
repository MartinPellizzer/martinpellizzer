Narcissus grandicrenatus

Narcissus grandicrenatus, commonly known as the large-crown daffodil, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe, particularly Spain and Portugal, where it thrives in sunny, well-drained soils. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers with a bright yellow corona and a white or yellow perianth. Its leaves are strap-like and grassy, forming a dense rosette near the ground.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its early spring blooms.
