Narcissus jacobaea

Narcissus jacobaea, commonly known as the greater celandine, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its yellow, daisy-like flowers and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides. It has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread vegetatively.

Narcissus jacobaea is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
