Nepeta teucriifolia subsp. iberica

Nepeta teucriifolia subsp.

iberica, commonly known as the Iberian catmint, is a perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula. It features aromatic, slender stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces clusters of small, blue to violet flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

Its foliage and flowers are often used in traditional herbal medicine and as a source of nectar for pollinators.
