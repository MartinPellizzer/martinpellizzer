Nematanthus crassifolius

Nematanthus crassifolius, commonly known as the thick-leaved moss vine, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically violet or purple in color. The plant is a trailing or climbing vine, often grown as a houseplant due to its ornamental value. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its unique appearance and ability to bloom indoors make it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
