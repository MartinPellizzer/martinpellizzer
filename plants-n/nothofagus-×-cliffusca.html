Nothofagus × cliffusca

Nothofagus × cliffusca, commonly known as the hybrid nothofagus, is a naturally occurring hybrid between Nothofagus truncata and Nothofagus cunninghamii.

It is native to the southern regions of South America, particularly in Chile and Argentina. This plant is characterized by its evergreen foliage and conical shape, typical of many nothofagus species. It thrives in temperate climates with high rainfall and well-drained soils.

The hybrid exhibits a mix of morphological traits from its parent species, making it unique in the genus.
