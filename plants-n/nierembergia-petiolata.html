Nierembergia petiolata

Nierembergia petiolata, commonly known as the slender bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories or rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its stems are slender and branching.

Nierembergia petiolata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
