Najas kurziana

Najas kurziana, commonly known as water needlegrass, is a species of aquatic plant belonging to the family Najadaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that are typically submerged in freshwater environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often clustered in spikes, and it is often found in ponds, lakes, and slow-moving streams. Najas kurziana is adapted to thrive in nutrient-rich waters and can form dense vegetation in suitable habitats.

It plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small invertebrates and serving as a food source for certain herbivorous fish.
