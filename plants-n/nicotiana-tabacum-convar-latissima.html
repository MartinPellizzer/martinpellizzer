Nicotiana tabacum convar. latissima

Nicotiana tabacum convar. latissima, commonly known as the broadleaf tobacco, is a variant of the tobacco plant characterized by its wide, broad leaves.

It is native to the Americas and is cultivated for its ornamental value as well as for its use in traditional and commercial applications. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. Its leaves are thick and fleshy, with a distinctive waxy surface, and it is often grown in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.

The species is also noted for its rapid growth and adaptability to various soil types.
