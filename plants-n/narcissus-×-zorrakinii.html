Narcissus × zorrakinii

Narcissus × zorrakinii, commonly known as the Zorrakinii daffodil, is a hybrid species within the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to white. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with narrow, grass-like leaves. Native to the Iberian Peninsula, it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
