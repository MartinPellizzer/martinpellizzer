Narcissus pseudonarcissus var. humilis

Narcissus pseudonarcissus var. humilis, commonly known as the dwarf daffodil, is a compact variety of the daffodil plant native to southern Europe.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces one to three small, fragrant flowers with a yellow corona. The plant typically blooms in early spring, adding a delicate touch of color to gardens and natural landscapes. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

This variety is valued for its low maintenance and ability to naturalize over time.
