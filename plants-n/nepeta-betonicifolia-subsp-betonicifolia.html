Nepeta betonicifolia subsp. betonicifolia

Nepeta betonicifolia subsp.

betonicifolia, commonly known as catmint, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia. It features aromatic, lance-shaped leaves with a mint-like scent, and its flowers are typically blue or purple, arranged in whorls along the stems.

The plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.

The subspecies betonicifolia is distinguished by its more robust growth and broader leaves compared to other Nepeta species.
