Narcissus × tenuior

Narcissus × tenuior, commonly known as the slender daffodil, is a hybrid species within the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, strap-like leaves and a single, elongated flower with a yellow perianth. The plant is native to southern Europe and is often found in woodland and meadow habitats. It typically blooms in early spring, producing delicate, trumpet-shaped flowers.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and graceful appearance.
