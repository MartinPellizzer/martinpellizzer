Nectandra grandiflora var. oblongifolia

Nectandra grandiflora var. oblongifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed sandarac tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The tree produces fragrant, white flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles, followed by small, spherical berries. It is valued for its aromatic wood, which is used in the production of incense and as a source of resin.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.
