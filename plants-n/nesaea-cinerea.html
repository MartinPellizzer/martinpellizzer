Nesaea cinerea

Nesaea cinerea, commonly known as the pale nesaea, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. The plant features slender, hairy stems and produces small, pale green or white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered with fine hairs.

Nesaea cinerea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
