Nemophila pulchella var. gracilis

Nemophila pulchella var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender blue-eyed Mary, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features delicate, sky-blue flowers with white centers, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to naturalize in suitable conditions.
