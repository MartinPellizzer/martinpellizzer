Nectandra sanguinea var. angustifolia

Nectandra sanguinea var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed sandarac tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree produces a resinous sap, which has been historically used in the production of varnishes and adhesives. Its bark and leaves contain various bioactive compounds, making it of interest in pharmaceutical research.

This species is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
