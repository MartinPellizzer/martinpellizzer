Neomammillaria lasiacantha

Neomammillaria lasiacantha, commonly known as the desert rose or the Mexican milk barrel cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are often ribbed and covered with white, woolly spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are borne on short, erect pedicels. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Neomammillaria lasiacantha is a slow-growing plant that is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
