Nama stenophylla var. flavescens

Nama stenophylla var. flavescens, commonly known as the yellow-flowered nama plant, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and small, yellowish-green leaves that are typically covered in a fine, whitish bloom. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often during the summer months. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showing adaptations to drought and high temperatures.

This species is often found in sandy or rocky soils, and it plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
