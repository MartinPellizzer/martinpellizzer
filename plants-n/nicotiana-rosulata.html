Nicotiana rosulata

Nicotiana rosulata, commonly known as the rose-like tobacco, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that resemble roses, typically in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant has a bushy growth habit with opposite leaves that are usually lance-shaped and hairy. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and botanical collections.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its historical significance in the cultivation of tobacco.
