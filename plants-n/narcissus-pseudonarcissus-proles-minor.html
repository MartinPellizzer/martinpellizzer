Narcissus pseudonarcissus proles minor

Narcissus pseudonarcissus proles minor, commonly known as the lesser Crown Imperial, is a bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to orange. The plant produces multiple narrow, grass-like leaves and has a short, sturdy stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often blooming in late spring to early summer.

This variety is noted for its smaller size compared to other narcissus species, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
