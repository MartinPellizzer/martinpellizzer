Nicotiana mexicana var. rubriflora

Nicotiana mexicana var. rubriflora, commonly known as the Mexican tobacco flower, is a species native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The flowers are typically deep red or purple, with a distinctive orange or yellow throat, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and botanical collections.

This plant is also valued for its historical and potential medicinal uses.
