Nastus elongatus

Nastus elongatus, commonly known as the long-stemmed nasturtium, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its elongated, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces bright yellow or orange flowers, which are often used in culinary applications. Its seeds are edible and have a peppery flavor similar to that of mustard.

Nastus elongatus is typically found in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soil.
