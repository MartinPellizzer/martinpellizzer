Navia robinsonii

Navia robinsonii, commonly known as the Robinson's navia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Navia robinsonii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
