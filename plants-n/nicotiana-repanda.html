Nicotiana repanda

Nicotiana repanda, commonly known as the slender tobacco plant, is a species native to South America.

It is a herbaceous plant that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with a distinctive spurred shape that aids in pollination by insects.

The plant belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is related to other tobacco species.
