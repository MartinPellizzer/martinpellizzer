Neooreophilus chaoae

Neooreophilus chaoae, commonly known as the Chao's rock orchid, is a rare and endemic orchid species native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and unique floral structure, featuring intricate petal arrangements and a distinctive labellum. The plant typically thrives in shaded, humid environments with well-drained soil, often found in forest understories. Its flowers are usually small, with a range of colors from white to pale yellow, and bloom during the spring season.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Neooreophilus chaoae is considered a conservation concern.
