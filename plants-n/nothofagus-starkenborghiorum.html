Nothofagus starkenborghiorum

Nothofagus starkenborghiorum, commonly known as the Starkenborghio southern beech, is a species native to South America, specifically found in parts of Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Nothofagus genus, which is part of the Proteaceae family, known for its ecological importance in temperate rainforests. The plant is characterized by its evergreen foliage and produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by woody, cone-like fruits.

It plays a crucial role in maintaining the biodiversity and structure of its native ecosystems.
