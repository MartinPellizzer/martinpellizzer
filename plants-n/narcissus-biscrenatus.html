Narcissus biscrenatus

Narcissus biscrenatus, commonly known as the double-crown daffodil, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers with two concentric rings of petals. The plant produces narrow, grass-like leaves and has a bulbous root system typical of many daffodils. It blooms in early spring, adding vibrant yellow color to gardens and natural landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in horticulture for its beauty and resilience.
