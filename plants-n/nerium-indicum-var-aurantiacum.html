Nerium indicum var. aurantiacum

Nerium indicum var. aurantiacum, commonly known as the golden oleander, is a variant of the oleander plant native to southern Asia.

It is characterized by its striking orange to yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has showy, fragrant blossoms that are typically clustered in inflorescences at the ends of the branches. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and lance-shaped, with a smooth texture.

The golden oleander is a hardy, drought-tolerant shrub often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
