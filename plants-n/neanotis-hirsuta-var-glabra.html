Neanotis hirsuta var. glabra

Neanotis hirsuta var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth velvetleaf, is a variant of the velvetleaf species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its smooth, hairless leaves in contrast to the typical hairy leaves of the species. The plant is an annual herb that often grows in disturbed soils and can become invasive in certain environments. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically green or yellowish, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

The botanical features of this variant make it an important subject in ecological and agricultural studies.
