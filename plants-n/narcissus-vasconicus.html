Narcissus vasconicus

Narcissus vasconicus, commonly known as the Spanish daffodil, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to white. The plant typically blooms in early spring, adding vibrant color to gardens and natural landscapes. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile choice for gardeners.

Narcissus vasconicus is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often used in floral arrangements and public gardens.
