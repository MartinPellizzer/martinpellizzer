Nictitella amena

Nictitella amena, commonly known as the sweet willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family, which includes willows and poplars, and is characterized by its slender, drooping branches. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early spring, followed by elongated, yellowish-green catkins. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a smooth margin, often with a silvery underside.

Nictitella amena thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near streams or in wetlands.
