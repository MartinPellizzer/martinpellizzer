Nothofagus cunninghamii

Nothofagus cunninghamii, commonly known as the mountain beech, is a species of evergreen tree native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Nothofagaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and lance-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, clustered flowers followed by edible, nut-like fruits. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in mountainous regions.

This plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
