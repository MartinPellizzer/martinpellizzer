Narcissus poeticus var. ornatus

Narcissus poeticus var. ornatus, commonly known as the poet's daffodil, is a cultivar of the daffodil genus native to Europe.

It features striking, trumpet-shaped flowers with a bright yellow corona and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and blooms in early spring. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its early blooming habit and vibrant color.
