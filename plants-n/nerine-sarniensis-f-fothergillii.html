Nerine sarniensis f. fothergillii

Nerine sarniensis f. fothergillii, commonly known as the Fothergill's nerine, is a botanical variant of the common nerine, native to southern Africa.

It is a bulbous perennial plant characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in late summer. The foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge in early spring. The flowers are usually pink to red in color, with a distinctive white throat, and are arranged in umbels.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and ease of care.
