Neomimosa platycarpa

Neomimosa platycarpa, commonly known as the broad-fruited mimosa, is a shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, spherical fruits that contain several seeds. It is adapted to arid environments and has a deep root system for water absorption.

Neomimosa platycarpa is often used in ecological restoration due to its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
