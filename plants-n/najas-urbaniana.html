Najas urbaniana

Najas urbaniana, commonly known as the narrow-leaf watermilfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Najadaceae and is characterized by its slender, whorled leaves that are typically less than 1 cm in width. The plant produces small, green flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences, which emerge from the water surface during the summer months. Najas urbaniana thrives in still or slow-moving waters with soft, muddy or sandy substrates.

It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
