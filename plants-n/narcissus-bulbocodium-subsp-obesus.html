Narcissus bulbocodium subsp. obesus

Narcissus bulbocodium subsp.

obesus, commonly known as the fat bulb narcissus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers with a distinctive, cup-shaped corona.

The plant's most notable botanical feature is its large, fleshy bulb, which stores nutrients and water for survival. It typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates.

The species is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in open, sunny habitats.
