Nymphaea oxypetala

Nymphaea oxypetala, commonly known as the narrow-leaf water lily, is a species of aquatic plant native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes. Its roots are anchored in the mud at the bottom of these water bodies, while its leaves remain buoyant on the surface.

Nymphaea oxypetala plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
