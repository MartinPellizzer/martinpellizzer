Notopleura camponutans

Notopleura camponutans, commonly known as the stinging nettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to various regions in Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, heart-shaped leaves with fine hairs that cause a stinging sensation upon contact. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, and its root system is fibrous and extensive. Notopleura camponutans thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

It has been used traditionally for its medicinal properties, although caution is advised due to its irritant characteristics.
