Neoholubia pubescens

Neoholubia pubescens, commonly known as the African velvet tree, is a deciduous tree native to eastern and southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and dense, compound leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds. Neoholubia pubescens is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

It has been used traditionally for its medicinal properties and as a source of timber.
