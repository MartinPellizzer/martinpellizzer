Nuphar variegata f. lutescens

Nuphar variegata f. lutescens, commonly known as the yellow-veined water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to parts of Asia.

It features broad, floating leaves with a distinctive yellow-green variegation pattern, which enhances its ornamental value. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, with a prominent yellow center. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in ponds and lakes.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for water gardens and ecological restoration projects.
