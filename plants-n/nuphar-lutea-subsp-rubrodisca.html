Nuphar lutea subsp. rubrodisca

Nuphar lutea subsp.

rubrodisca, commonly known as the red-flowered yellow water lily, is a aquatic plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves and showy, red-orange flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats, such as ponds and lakes. Its leaves are typically green with a waxy surface, and the flowers have five petals and numerous stamens.

It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
