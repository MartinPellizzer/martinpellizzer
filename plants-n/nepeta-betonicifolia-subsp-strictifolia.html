Nepeta betonicifolia subsp. strictifolia

Nepeta betonicifolia subsp.

strictifolia, commonly known as catmint or narrowleaf catmint, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia. It features slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers in whorls along the stems, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It is valued for its aromatic foliage and is often used in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.

This subspecies is closely related to other Nepeta species and is sometimes used in herbal remedies.
