Nicotiana fastigiata

Nicotiana fastigiata, commonly known as the upright tobacco plant, is a species native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring dense, upright stems covered in fine, glandular hairs. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate in shape, with a velvety texture and a strong, aromatic scent.

The plant produces clusters of small, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, blue, and purple, depending on the cultivar. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
