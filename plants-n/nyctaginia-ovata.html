Nyctaginia ovata

Nyctaginia ovata, commonly known as the nightshade or moonflower, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, showy, white flowers that open at night and close by morning, a feature that attracts nocturnal pollinators. The plant has opposite, simple, ovate leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, round seeds.

Nyctaginia ovata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has cultural significance in some regions.
