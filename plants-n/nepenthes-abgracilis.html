Nepenthes abgracilis

Nepenthes abgracilis, commonly known as the slender pitcher plant, is a species native to Borneo.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow pitchers that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces nectar to attract insects, which then fall into the digestive fluid at the base of the pitcher. Its leaves are typically arranged in a spiral pattern, and the plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on other plants without being parasitic.

Nepenthes abgracilis is valued for its unique morphology and ecological role in its native habitat.
