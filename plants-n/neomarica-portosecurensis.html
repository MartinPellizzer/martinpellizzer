Neomarica portosecurensis

Neomarica portosecurensis, commonly known as the Portosecure orchid, is a rare and unique species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, elongated flowers that display a range of colors from white to deep purple. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are long and narrow, adapted to capture moisture from the humid air.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a conservation concern.
