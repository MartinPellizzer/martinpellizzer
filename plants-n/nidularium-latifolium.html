Nidularium latifolium

Nidularium latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf nidularium, is a member of the Bromeliaceae family, characterized by its rosette-forming structure and vibrant foliage.

It originates from the tropical regions of South America, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant features long, strap-like leaves that can grow up to several feet in length, often displaying striking variegation or coloration. Its flowers, though less showy than its foliage, are typically small and clustered in a terminal inflorescence.

Nidularium latifolium is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to create a lush, tropical ambiance in shaded garden settings.
