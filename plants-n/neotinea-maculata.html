Neotinea maculata

Neotinea maculata, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and showy, spurred flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Its flowers are usually pink or purple with a distinctive dark spot on the lip, which aids in pollination.

Neotinea maculata is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of soil types.
