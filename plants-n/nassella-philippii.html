Nassella philippii

Nassella philippii, commonly known as the Philippi's needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, needle-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces slender, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, spikelet-like flowers, which are adapted for wind pollination. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Nassella philippii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
