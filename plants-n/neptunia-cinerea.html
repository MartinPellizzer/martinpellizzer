Neptunia cinerea

Neptunia cinerea, commonly known as the silver water fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It thrives in freshwater habitats, particularly in slow-moving streams and wetlands. The plant has slender, thread-like fronds that are covered in a fine, silvery coating, giving it its common name. Its rhizomes form dense mats on the water surface, helping to stabilize the soil and prevent erosion.

Neptunia cinerea plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
