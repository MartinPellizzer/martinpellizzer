Neopalissya castaneifolia subsp. chirindica

Neopalissya castaneifolia subsp.

chirindica, commonly known as the Chirindica chestnut. It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to central and eastern Africa.

The plant is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically alternate and pinnate. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, appearing in clusters, while the fruit is a chestnut-like drupe.

The subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential ecological roles in its native habitats.
