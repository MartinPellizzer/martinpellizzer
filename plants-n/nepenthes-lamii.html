Nepenthes lamii

Nepenthes lamii, commonly known as the Sunda Nepenthes, is a tropical pitcher plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow pitcher structures that are typically green with red or purple markings. The plant produces nectar to attract insects, which then fall into the pitcher and are digested by the plant's digestive enzymes. Nepenthes lamii thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its unique morphology and carnivorous nature make it a fascinating subject in botanical studies.
