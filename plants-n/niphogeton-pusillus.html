Niphogeton pusillus

Niphogeton pusillus, commonly known as the dwarf snowbell, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern China.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has dark green, leathery leaves that remain throughout the year, providing ornamental value in both winter and summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Due to its ornamental appeal and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting.
