Narcissus × valdesii-franzii

Narcissus × valdesii-franzii, commonly known as the Spanish daffodil, is a hybrid perennial bulbous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange, with a distinct, often darker central stripe. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, producing multiple blooms per stem during early spring. Its foliage consists of strap-like, grassy leaves that emerge from a bulbous base.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, early-flowering displays.
