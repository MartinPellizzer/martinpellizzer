Nothaphoebe heterophylla

Nothaphoebe heterophylla, commonly known as the variable nothophoebe, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its variable leaf morphology, with leaves ranging from entire to deeply lobed depending on the environment. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or streamside habitats. It produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by wind.

The species is notable for its adaptability and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
