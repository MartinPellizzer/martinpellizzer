Neochilenia napina

Neochilenia napina, commonly known as the Chilean bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Andean regions of Chile.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, with five petals arranged in a symmetrical pattern. The plant has slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous areas with well-drained soil.

Neochilenia napina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in temperate climates.
