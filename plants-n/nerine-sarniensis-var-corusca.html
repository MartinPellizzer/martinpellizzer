Nerine sarniensis var. corusca

Nerine sarniensis var. corusca, commonly known as the silver-leafed nerine, is a striking bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It features lance-shaped, bluish-gray leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery bloom, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, typically in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is a popular choice among gardeners seeking both visual interest and low maintenance.
