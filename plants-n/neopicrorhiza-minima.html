Neopicrorhiza minima

Neopicrorhiza minima, commonly known as lesser picrorhiza, is a small perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the family Labiatae, also known as the mint family, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or blue in color. It is valued in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.
