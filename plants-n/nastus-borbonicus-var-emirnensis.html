Nastus borbonicus var. emirnensis

Nastus borbonicus var. emirnensis, commonly known as the Emirnensis Nastus, is a flowering plant native to specific regions in the Middle East.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow and white. It is often found in rocky or arid environments, adapting to harsh climatic conditions.

Its botanical structure supports its role in local ecosystems, providing nectar for pollinators and stabilizing soil in its habitat.
