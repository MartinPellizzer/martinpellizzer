Notocactus submammulosus

Notocactus submammulosus, commonly known as the mammillary notocactus, is a species of cactus native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are often covered in small, rounded tubercles. The plant produces white or pale yellow flowers during the summer months, which are typically borne on short pedicels. Its growth habit is typically solitary or in small clusters, and it is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

The plant is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts.
