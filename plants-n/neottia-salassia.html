Neottia salassia

Neottia salassia, commonly known as the Salassa orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and grasslands. Neottia salassia has a distinctive root system that forms symbiotic relationships with mycorrhizal fungi.

Its flowers are usually short-lived, blooming for a brief period during the warmer months of the year.
