Neurocarpaea herbacea

Neurocarpaea herbacea, commonly known as the slender neurocarpaea, is a low-growing, herbaceous plant native to certain regions of South America.

It typically thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in humid forests or along stream banks. The plant has delicate, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, usually greenish or pale yellow, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
