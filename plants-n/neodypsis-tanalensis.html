Neodypsis tanalensis

Neodypsis tanalensis, commonly known as the giant sago palm, is a species of cycad native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its large, feather-like leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant has a stout, unbranched trunk that is covered in a fibrous, spongy material. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically unisexual, with separate male and female plants.

Neodypsis tanalensis is adapted to grow in dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas.
