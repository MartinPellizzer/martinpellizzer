Nectandra sinuata

Nectandra sinuata, commonly known as the fragrant sandarac tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its bark contains a resin that was historically used for making varnishes and adhesives.

Nectandra sinuata thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.
