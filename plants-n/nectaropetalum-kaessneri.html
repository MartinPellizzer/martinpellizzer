Nectaropetalum kaessneri

Nectaropetalum kaessneri, commonly known as the Cape goldenbush, is a South African shrub belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, golden-yellow inflorescences that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Nectaropetalum kaessneri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in local horticulture.
