Nepeta amethystina subsp. murcica

Nepeta amethystina subsp.

murcica, commonly known as the Murcian catmint, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe, particularly Spain. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves.

The plant produces small, tubular blue to purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage has a strong mint-like scent, which can repel certain insects.

This subspecies is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and low maintenance requirements.
