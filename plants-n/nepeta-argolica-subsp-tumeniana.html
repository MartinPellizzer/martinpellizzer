Nepeta argolica subsp. tumeniana

Nepeta argolica subsp.

tumeniana, commonly known as the Argolic catmint, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and is found in regions such as Greece and southern Italy. Its flowers are small, blue to purple, and arranged in whorls along the stems.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
