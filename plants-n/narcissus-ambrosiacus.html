Narcissus ambrosiacus

Narcissus ambrosiacus, commonly known as the Persian daffodil, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Turkey, Iran, and the Caucasus, where it thrives in cool, moist environments. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange, with a white or green center. Its bulbs are characterized by their elongated shape and thick, fleshy scales.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been cultivated for centuries in horticultural gardens.
