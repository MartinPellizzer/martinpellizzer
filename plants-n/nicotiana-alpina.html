Nicotiana alpina

Nicotiana alpina, commonly known as the alpine tobacco, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of South America, particularly the Andes. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit, hairy stems, and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. It thrives in cool, high-altitude environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Nicotiana alpina is also noted for its aromatic leaves and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
