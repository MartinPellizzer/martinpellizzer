Neottianthe cucullata f. variegata

Neottianthe cucullata f. variegata, commonly known as the variegated hooded orchid, is a unique hybrid orchid that exhibits striking variegation in its foliage.

It is characterized by its elongated, hood-like petals that give it its common name, and its leaves are often marked with distinct color patterns. This plant thrives in shaded, moist environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions. It is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a delicate and somewhat elusive species.
