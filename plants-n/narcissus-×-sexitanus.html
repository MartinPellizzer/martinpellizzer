Narcissus × sexitanus

Narcissus × sexitanus, commonly known as the Spanish daffodil, is a hybrid plant resulting from crosses between different narcissus species.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers with a prominent central cup, typical of the Amaryllidaceae family. The plant is native to southern Europe and thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure. It typically blooms in early spring, adding a striking display to gardens and natural landscapes.

Its foliage consists of strap-like leaves that are evergreen, contributing to its ornamental value throughout the year.
