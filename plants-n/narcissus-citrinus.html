Narcissus citrinus

Narcissus citrinus, commonly known as the citron narcissus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It produces yellow, cup-shaped flowers with a strong, sweet fragrance, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and prefers well-drained soil in full sun. Its bulbs are relatively small and can be propagated through division or seed.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in horticulture.
