Noccaea dolichocarpa

Noccaea dolichocarpa, commonly known as the long-stemmed rockcress, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or arctic regions. The plant produces small, white or yellow flowers arranged in clusters, and its leaves are alternate, pinnately divided, and often covered in fine hairs. Noccaea dolichocarpa is often found in mountainous areas across Europe and Asia.
