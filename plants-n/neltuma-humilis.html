Neltuma humilis

Neltuma humilis, commonly known as the lowland neltuma, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as cloud forests and mountainous areas. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are often covered in a fine, fuzzy texture. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually appearing in shades of white or pale yellow.

Neltuma humilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
