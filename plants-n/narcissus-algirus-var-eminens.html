Narcissus algirus var. eminens

Narcissus algirus var. eminens, commonly known as the Alaskan daffodil, is a rare and striking variant of the daffodil genus.

It is characterized by its large, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. This plant is native to the mountainous regions of Alaska and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments. It typically grows in rocky soils and is known for its hardiness in harsh climates.

The plant's botanical features make it a subject of interest for botanists and gardeners alike.
