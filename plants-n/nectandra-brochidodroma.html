Nectandra brochidodroma

Nectandra brochidodroma, commonly known as the yellow-eyed elder, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain a single seed, typical of many Lauraceae species. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Its wood is valued for its durability and pleasant scent, making it a sought-after resource in local economies.
