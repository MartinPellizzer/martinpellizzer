Nepenthes talaandig

Nepenthes talaandig, commonly known as the Talaandig pitcher plant, is a tropical carnivorous plant native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated pitcher-shaped leaves that secrete a sticky liquid to trap and digest insects. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in lowland rainforests. Its pitchers are typically green or red in color, with a waxy coating that aids in trapping prey.

Nepenthes talaandig is valued for its unique morphology and is often cultivated for its ornamental appeal.
