Nicotiana rustica var. pavonii

Nicotiana rustica var. pavonii, commonly known as the Pavonii tobacco, is a variant of the tobacco plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It is cultivated for its leaves, which contain high levels of nicotine and are used in traditional smoking and medicinal practices.

This variety is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
