Neea choriophylla var. hirtella

Neea choriophylla var. hirtella, commonly known as the hairy choriophylla neea, is a variant of the neea genus native to specific regions in East Asia.

It is a small evergreen shrub characterized by its dense, hairy foliage that provides a distinctive texture. The leaves are typically elliptical, with a waxy surface and a slightly serrated edge. This plant thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a unique subject for ecological and horticultural study.
