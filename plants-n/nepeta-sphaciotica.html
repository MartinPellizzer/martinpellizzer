Nepeta sphaciotica

Nepeta sphaciotica, commonly known as the narrow-leaved catmint, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or violet flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and often have a minty scent, contributing to its common name.

Nepeta sphaciotica is typically found in rocky or gravelly soils in dry, sunny environments.
