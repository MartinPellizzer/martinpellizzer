Narcissus viridiflorus

Narcissus viridiflorus, commonly known as the green-flowered daffodil, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Europe.

It features a narrow, grass-like leaf and a single, showy flower with a greenish-yellow perianth. The plant typically blooms in early spring, producing a bright yellow corona that contrasts with its green tepals. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value and unique coloration, it is often cultivated in horticultural settings.
