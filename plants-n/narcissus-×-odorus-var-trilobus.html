Narcissus × odorus var. trilobus

Narcissus × odorus var. trilobus, commonly known as the fragrant trilobed daffodil, is a hybrid cultivar within the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy, trumpet-shaped flowers with a distinctive three-lobed corona. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, featuring strap-like, narrow leaves that are dark green in color. Its flowers bloom in early spring, often in shades of yellow to white, and emit a subtle, sweet fragrance.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is commonly cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal and mild scent.
