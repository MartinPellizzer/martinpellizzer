Nepeta erodiifolia

Nepeta erodiifolia, commonly known as catmint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces small, fragrant blue or purple flowers in whorled clusters, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Nepeta erodiifolia is also known for its mild mint-like scent, which attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
