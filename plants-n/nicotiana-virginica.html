Nicotiana virginica

Nicotiana virginica, commonly known as wild tobacco, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and has hairy, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including woodlands, meadows, and disturbed soils.

The species is part of the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshade plants.
