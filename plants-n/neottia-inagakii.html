Neottia inagakii

Neottia inagakii, commonly known as the Inagaki's twayblade, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Japan.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments. The plant has a slender, leafless stem with two opposite leaves that are linear and grass-like in appearance. Its flowers are pale green to yellowish in color, with a distinctive three-lobed lip.

Neottia inagakii is noted for its delicate structure and is often found in association with specific mycorrhizal fungi.
