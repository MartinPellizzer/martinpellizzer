Narcissus breviflorus

Narcissus breviflorus, commonly known as the short-flowered daffodil, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa, typically growing in woodland and grassland habitats. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers with bright yellow petals and a central corolla. Its bulbs are adapted for storing nutrients, allowing it to thrive in seasonal climates.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its early spring blooms.
