Nama californica

Nama californica, commonly known as California poppy, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange to red petals and delicate, feathery leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Nama californica is an important species in ecological restoration due to its adaptability and role in supporting pollinators.
