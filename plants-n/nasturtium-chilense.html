Nasturtium chilense

Nasturtium chilense, commonly known as Chilean nasturtium, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the Tropaeolaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant orange and yellow flowers. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture and produces edible seeds that are often used in culinary applications. Nasturtium chilense thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking both ornamental and edible plants.

Its ability to attract beneficial insects also makes it a valuable addition to ecological gardening practices.
