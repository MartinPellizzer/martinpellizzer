Neolepisorus ovatus f. gracilis

Neolepisorus ovatus f. gracilis, commonly known as the slender shield fern, is a fern species characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia. The plant typically grows as a terrestrial or epiphytic fern, often found on rocks or tree trunks in humid environments. Its fronds are usually a deep green, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and it produces small, clustered sporangia on the underside of its leaves.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded, moist garden settings.
