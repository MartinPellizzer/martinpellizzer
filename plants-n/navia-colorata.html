Navia colorata

Navia colorata, commonly known as the rainbow vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful flowers that range from red to violet, often appearing in clusters. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, fleshy fruits that are edible. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Navia colorata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
