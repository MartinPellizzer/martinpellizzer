Nicotiana plumbaginifolia

Nicotiana plumbaginifolia, commonly known as the silver leaf tobacco, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family.

It is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. The plant is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and showy blue or violet flowers. It contains nicotine and other alkaloids, making it a subject of study in plant chemistry and pharmacology.

Its genetic makeup has also been utilized in research related to plant genetics and molecular biology.
