Nicotiana scabra

Nicotiana scabra, commonly known as the narrow-leaf tobacco, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family.

It is native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually violet or blue, with five petals and a distinctive spurred shape.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
