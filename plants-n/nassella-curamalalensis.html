Nassella curamalalensis

Nassella curamalalensis, commonly known as the Curamalal grass, is a perennial grass species native to the arid regions of central Australia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and produces dense, fluffy seed heads that are often used in floral arrangements. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it well adapted to its harsh environment. Its leaves are narrow and wiry, with a bluish-green hue, and it is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological role and resilience in extreme conditions.
