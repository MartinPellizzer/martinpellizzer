Nepeta mollissima

Nepeta mollissima, commonly known as velvet catmint, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, fuzzy leaves that give it a velvety texture, and its clusters of small, pale blue to lavender flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardens. It is also known for its aromatic properties and is often used in herbal remedies.

Nepeta mollissima is a hardy plant that attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
