Nayariophyton jujubifolium

It is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that resemble the leaves of the jujube fruit, giving it its common name. Its rhizome system allows it to spread and form dense colonies, contributing to its ecological role in soil stabilization.

The spores are released from specialized structures called sori, which are located on the underside of the fronds.
