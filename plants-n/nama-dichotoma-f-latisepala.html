Nama dichotoma f. latisepala

Nama dichotoma f. latisepala, commonly known as the broad-sepaled ice plant, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, ribbed stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The flowers are typically small, yellow or orange, and are arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. This plant is adapted to arid environments and can survive in poor soil conditions.

It is often used in rock gardens and as an ornamental plant due to its hardy nature and aesthetic appeal.
