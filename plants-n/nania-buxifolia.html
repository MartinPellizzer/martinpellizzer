Nania buxifolia

Nania buxifolia, commonly known as the boxleaf nania, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and glossy, dark green leaves that are oval in shape with a leathery texture. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. Its foliage is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded environments.

Nania buxifolia is also valued for its air-purifying properties and is commonly used in indoor gardening.
