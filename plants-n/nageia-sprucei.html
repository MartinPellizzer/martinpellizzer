Nageia sprucei

Nageia sprucei, commonly known as the mountain pine or spruce pine, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Pinaceae and is characterized by its needle-like leaves and cone-bearing structure. The plant has a distinctive bark that becomes fissured with age, and its cones are typically reddish-brown in color. Nageia sprucei thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
