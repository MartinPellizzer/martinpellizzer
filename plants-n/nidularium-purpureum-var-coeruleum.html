Nidularium purpureum var. coeruleum

Nidularium purpureum var. coeruleum, commonly known as the blue-flowered pincushion cactus, is a striking member of the Cactaceae family native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny stems that form a compact, cushion-like structure. The plant produces vibrant blue or purple flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. Its foliage consists of small, fleshy, paddle-shaped leaves that help it conserve water in arid environments.

This variety is prized for its unique appearance and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in dry gardens.
