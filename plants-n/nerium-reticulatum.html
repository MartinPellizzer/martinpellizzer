Nerium reticulatum

Nerium reticulatum, commonly known as the reticulated oleander, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, clustered flowers. The leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a reticulated or net-like pattern, giving the plant its common name. Its flowers are typically pink or red, with five petals, and bloom in summer.

The plant is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in full sun.
