Nectandra micranthera

Nectandra micranthera, commonly known as the small-flowered sandarac tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurel and cinnamon trees, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark. The plant produces small, white flowers that are often clustered together, followed by small, oval-shaped fruits. Its wood is valued for its aromatic properties and is used in traditional crafts and incense.

Nectandra micranthera plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, supporting a variety of wildlife species.
