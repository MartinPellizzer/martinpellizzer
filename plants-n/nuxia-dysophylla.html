Nuxia dysophylla

Nuxia dysophylla, commonly known as the African snowbush, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky, arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a delicate aesthetic to its rugged appearance. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, which helps reduce water loss in dry climates.

Nuxia dysophylla is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and flowering habit.
