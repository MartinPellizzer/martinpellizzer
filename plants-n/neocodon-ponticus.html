Neocodon ponticus

Neocodon ponticus, commonly known as the Pontic bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It typically grows in moist, woodland environments and has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces showy blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to retain moisture in its native habitat.

Neocodon ponticus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
