Nipponocalamus higoensis

Nipponocalamus higoensis, commonly known as the Higo bamboo, is a species of grass native to Japan.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems and narrow leaves. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in mountainous regions. It has a compact growth habit and is valued for its ornamental appearance.

Nipponocalamus higoensis is also notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
