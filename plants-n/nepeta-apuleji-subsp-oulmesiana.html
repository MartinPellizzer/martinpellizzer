Nepeta apuleji subsp. oulmesiana

Nepeta apuleji subsp.

oulmesiana, commonly known as the Ouachita catmint, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States. It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive minty aroma.

The plant produces clusters of blue to violet flowers in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and hardiness in partial shade.

Its foliage remains attractive throughout the growing season, making it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
