Nectandra skutchii

Nectandra skutchii, commonly known as Skutch's nectandra, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by oval-shaped berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
