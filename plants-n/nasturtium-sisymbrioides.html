Nasturtium sisymbrioides

Nasturtium sisymbrioides, commonly known as the Indian cress, is an annual flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant features bright yellow or orange flowers with four petals, and its leaves are edible, often used in salads or as a garnish. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and container planting.

Nasturtium sisymbrioides is also known for its peppery taste and ability to attract beneficial insects like ladybugs.
