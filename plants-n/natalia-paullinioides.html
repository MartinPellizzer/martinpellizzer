Natalia paullinioides

Natalia paullinioides, commonly known as the Brazilian nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces striking violet or blue flowers with prominent yellow stamens. The plant belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, peppers, and potatoes, and is characterized by its alkaloid-rich chemistry. Natalia paullinioides thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
