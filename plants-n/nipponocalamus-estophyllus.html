Nipponocalamus estophyllus

Nipponocalamus estophyllus, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or Japanese clumping bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit. The plant features slender, hollow stems that are often green or slightly bluish in color. Its leaves are long, narrow, and have a smooth texture, contributing to its ornamental value.

Nipponocalamus estophyllus is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
