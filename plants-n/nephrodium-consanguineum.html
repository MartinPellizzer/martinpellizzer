Nephrodium consanguineum

Nephrodium consanguineum, commonly known as the bloodroot, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its basal leaves that are heart-shaped and its distinctive red-orange flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces a yellow-orange sap that has been used traditionally in herbal medicine. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Nephrodium consanguineum is also known for its unique seed pods that split open to release its seeds.
