Narcissus pseudonarcissus subsp. confusus

Narcissus pseudonarcissus subsp.

confusus, commonly known as the Spanish daffodil, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces a single, showy yellow flower with a prominent corona.

The plant typically blooms in early spring, adding a touch of color to gardens and natural landscapes. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to various growing conditions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in horticultural gardens.
