Newmania cristata

Newmania cristata, commonly known as the crown vetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial legume characterized by its trifoliate leaves and spikes of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant has a creeping habit with a dense mat of stems that can spread rapidly. It is often used for soil stabilization due to its extensive root system and ability to fix nitrogen.

Newmania cristata is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
