Nomocharis lophophora f. wardii

Nomocharis lophophora f. wardii, commonly known as the Ward's ghost flower, is a rare and striking botanical specimen native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

This hybrid orchid is characterized by its unique, ghost-like appearance, with translucent petals that resemble translucent veils. It thrives in high-altitude environments, often in shaded, humid conditions with well-draining soil. The plant produces delicate, fragrant blooms in shades of white and pale pink, typically during the spring and early summer.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a conservation concern and is often cultivated in specialized botanical gardens.
