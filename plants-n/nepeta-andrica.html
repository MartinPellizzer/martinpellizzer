Nepeta andrica

Nepeta andrica, commonly known as catmint, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in herbal remedies due to their mild sedative properties.

Nepeta andrica thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
