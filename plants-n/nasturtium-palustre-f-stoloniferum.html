Nasturtium palustre f. stoloniferum

Nasturtium palustre f. stoloniferum, commonly known as the swamp nasturtium, is a herbaceous plant characterized by its trailing growth habit and stoloniferous stems that root at the nodes.

It features heart-shaped, glossy leaves and produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers with a distinctive spurred petal. Native to wetland environments, it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds. The plant is known for its peppery taste, making it a popular edible ornamental.

It plays a role in supporting pollinators and can be used in natural landscaping for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
