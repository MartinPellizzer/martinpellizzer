Notopleura siggersiana

Notopleura siggersiana, commonly known as the Cape York fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to northern Australia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clustered, small greenish-white flowers. The tree produces edible fig-like fruits that are an important food source for local wildlife. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in rainforests and coastal areas.

Due to its ecological significance, it is also valued for its role in maintaining biodiversity in its native habitat.
