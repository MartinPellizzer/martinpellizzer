Neowimmeria yuccoides

Neowimmeria yuccoides, commonly known as the desert yucca, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It features a rosette of narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are typically blue-gray in color, adapted to reduce water loss in hot, dry environments. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences bearing white or pale yellow flowers, which bloom in the spring. Its root system is extensive and deep, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Neowimmeria yuccoides is well-suited for xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and drought tolerance.
