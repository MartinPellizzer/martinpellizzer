Najas muenscheri

Najas muenscheri, commonly known as the narrow-leaved watermilfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It has slender, ribbon-like leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem, aiding in its identification. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged, contributing to its role in aquatic ecosystems. It thrives in shallow, slow-moving waters and is an important food source for various aquatic organisms.

Due to its rapid growth, it can become invasive in certain water bodies if not managed properly.
