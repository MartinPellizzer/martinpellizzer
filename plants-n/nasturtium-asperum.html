Nasturtium asperum

Nasturtium asperum, commonly known as rough nasturtium, is a herbaceous annual plant native to South America.

It features heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are slightly hairy and have a peppery scent. The plant produces vibrant orange or yellow flowers with prominent, spurred petals. Its flowers are edible and often used in salads or as a decorative element.

The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking both ornamental and edible plants.
