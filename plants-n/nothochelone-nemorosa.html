Nothochelone nemorosa

Nothochelone nemorosa, commonly known as the wood anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and has trifoliate leaves with a heart-shaped base. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with five to seven sepals that resemble petals.
