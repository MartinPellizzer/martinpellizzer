Nothofagus rubra

Nothofagus rubra, commonly known as the red beech, is a species of evergreen tree native to southern South America.

It belongs to the Nothofagaceae family and is characterized by its dark, fissured bark and lanceolate leaves that are often reddish-brown underneath. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by edible, woody nuts enclosed in a cupule. It thrives in temperate rainforests and is adapted to moist, cool climates with high humidity.

Nothofagus rubra plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
