Nux cinerea

Nux cinerea, commonly known as the ash tree, is a deciduous tree belonging to the Oleaceae family.

It is characterized by its grayish-brown bark and opposite, simple leaves that are usually oval in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in the spring, followed by winged fruits called samaras. It is native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa, and is often found in woodland areas.

Nux cinerea is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
