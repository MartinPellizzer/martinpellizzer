Najas microdon var. curassavica

Najas microdon var. curassavica, commonly known as the narrowleaf watermilfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater habitats in the Americas.

It has slender, branched stems and small, whorled leaves that are typically less than 1 millimeter in width. The plant produces tiny, greenish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, often found near the water surface. It thrives in slow-moving to still waters with moderate to high light penetration.

This species plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
