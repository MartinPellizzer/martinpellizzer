Nicotiana walpa

Nicotiana walpa, commonly known as the wild tobacco plant, is a species native to the Americas.

It is a herbaceous plant that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and it produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink, purple, or white. It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is known for its alkaloid-rich properties.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
