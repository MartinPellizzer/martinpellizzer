Narcissus gayi var. praelongus

Narcissus gayi var. praelongus, commonly known as the long-stalked daffodil, is a variety of the genus Narcissus native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its elongated, strap-like leaves and a single, large, trumpet-shaped flower that typically blooms in spring. The plant prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders. Its flowers range in color from yellow to white, with a distinctive, recurved petal.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its early spring display.
