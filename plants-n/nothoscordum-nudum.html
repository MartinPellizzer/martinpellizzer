Nothoscordum nudum

Nothoscordum nudum, commonly known as the naked garlic.

It is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, bulb-like structures at the base of the plant.

Nothoscordum nudum is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.
