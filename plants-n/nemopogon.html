Nemopogon

Nemopogon, commonly known as the silverweed, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Polygonaceae.

These plants are characterized by their basal rosettes of leaves and clusters of small, greenish flowers. They typically grow in moist, temperate environments, often in meadows or along stream banks. The leaves are usually covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving the plant its common name.

Nemopogon species are known for their ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils and are often used in ecological restoration projects.
