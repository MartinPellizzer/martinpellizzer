Neo-urbania cordyline

Neo-urbania cordyline, commonly known as the New Zealand tree fern, is a striking plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Cyatheaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant can grow up to 10 meters in height, with a crown of gracefully arching leaves that create a lush, tropical appearance. Its roots form a dense network that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Neo-urbania cordyline is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a landscape plant.
