Niphobolus ceylanicus

Niphobolus ceylanicus, commonly known as the Ceylon sandalwood, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Sri Lanka.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its aromatic, fragrant wood that is highly valued in traditional medicine and perfumery. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a leathery texture. It produces small, white flowers that are followed by spherical, dark purple berries.

The tree is also noted for its ability to thrive in moist, well-drained soils and its role in the local ecosystem.
