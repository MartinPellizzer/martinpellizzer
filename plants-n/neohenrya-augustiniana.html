Neohenrya augustiniana

Neohenrya augustiniana, commonly known as the Augustinian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is often found in coastal areas.

Neohenrya augustiniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate in gardens.
