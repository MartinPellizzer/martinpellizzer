Nepeta grossheimii

Nepeta grossheimii, commonly known as the Sierra catmint, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features aromatic, gray-green foliage and produces spikes of blue to purple flowers. The plant is characterized by its square, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is drought-tolerant once established.

Nepeta grossheimii is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
