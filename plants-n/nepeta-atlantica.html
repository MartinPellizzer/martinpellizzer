Nepeta atlantica

Nepeta atlantica, commonly known as the Atlantic catmint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Iberian Peninsula.

It features aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, pale blue to violet flowers. The plant is characterized by its square, hairy stems and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic properties and medicinal uses.

Nepeta atlantica is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
