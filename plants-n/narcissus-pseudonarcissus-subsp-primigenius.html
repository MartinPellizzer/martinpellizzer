Narcissus pseudonarcissus subsp. primigenius

Narcissus pseudonarcissus subsp.

primigenius, commonly known as the Spanish daffodil, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe. It produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers with a bright yellow corona and white petals.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders. Its blooming period typically occurs in early spring, adding a vibrant touch to gardens.

The subspecies is valued for its hardiness and ability to naturalize over time.
