Nicotiana cavanillesii

Nicotiana cavanillesii, commonly known as the Cavanilles tobacco, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to the Andean regions of South America, particularly Ecuador and Peru. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit, hairy stems, and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet. It produces small, oval-shaped fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
