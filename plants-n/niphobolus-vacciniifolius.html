Niphobolus vacciniifolius

Niphobolus vacciniifolius, commonly known as the red-flowered rock cress, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cross-shaped flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly succulent texture.

Niphobolus vacciniifolius is notable for its vibrant red petals, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
