Neomirandea parasitica

Neomirandea parasitica, commonly known as the parasitic orchid, is a unique flowering plant that exhibits hemiparasitic behavior, deriving some of its nutrients from host plants while also performing photosynthesis.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its reduced leaves and specialized root structures called haustoria, which attach to host roots. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and is found in various regions of Southeast Asia. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, often lacking the showy features typical of many orchids.

Due to its specialized lifestyle and habitat requirements, Neomirandea parasitica is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
