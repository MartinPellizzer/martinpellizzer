Narcissus bulbocodium var. pallidus

Narcissus bulbocodium var. pallidus, commonly known as the pale narcissus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Europe.

It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers with a distinctive, often fragrant scent. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is typically found in open, sunny habitats. It blooms in early spring and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

Its bulbs are adapted for survival in dry conditions, making it resilient to drought.
