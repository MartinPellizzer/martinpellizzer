Nitraria sibirica

Nitraria sibirica, commonly known as the Siberian nitraria, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia, including parts of Russia, Mongolia, and China.

It is a member of the family Nitrariaceae and is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant is well adapted to harsh environmental conditions, including extreme temperatures and poor soil quality. It produces small, hard fruits that contain a single seed, and it plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils.

Nitraria sibirica is also used in traditional medicine and has potential applications in phytoremediation due to its ability to tolerate heavy metals.
