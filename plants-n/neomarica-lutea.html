Neomarica lutea

Neomarica lutea, commonly known as the golden spider lily, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Aroid family, which includes many plants with showy inflorescences. The plant features large, arrow-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy, silver-blue sheen. Its flowers are clustered in a spadix, surrounded by a bright yellow spathe, giving it its common name.

Neomarica lutea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in warm, humid environments.
