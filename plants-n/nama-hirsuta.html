Nama hirsuta

Nama hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy nama, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. It is often found growing in rocky soils or along riverbanks.

Nama hirsuta is well adapted to drought conditions and can survive with minimal water.
