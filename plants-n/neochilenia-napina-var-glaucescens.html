Neochilenia napina var. glaucescens

Neochilenia napina var. glaucescens, commonly known as the blue-veined orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue-green veins on its lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale pink, typically during the spring and summer months. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

This variant is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
