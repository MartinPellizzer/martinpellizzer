Nephelium lappaceum var. topengii

Nephelium lappaceum var. topengii, commonly known as the lantern fruit or topeng fruit, is a tropical plant belonging to the Sapindaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its unique, lantern-shaped fruit that turns red when ripe. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate with a rough texture.

The fruit contains a single seed enclosed in a fleshy, aromatic pulp, making it both ornamental and edible.
