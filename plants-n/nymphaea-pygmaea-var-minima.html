Nymphaea pygmaea var. minima

Nymphaea pygmaea var. minima, commonly known as the dwarf water lily, is a compact aquatic plant native to the wetlands of Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped, floating leaves that are typically a vibrant green with a waxy surface. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments with adequate sunlight.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for pond gardens and water features.
