Nama nigricaulis

Nama nigricaulis, commonly known as the black-stemmed nama, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dark, woody stems and small, narrow leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Nama nigricaulis is often found in rocky soils and is well-suited to drought conditions.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for local wildlife.
