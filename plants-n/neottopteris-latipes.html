Neottopteris latipes

Neottopteris latipes, commonly known as the common maidenhair fern, is a member of the Dryopteridaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, bi-lobed fronds that are typically 30 to 60 cm in length. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, with creeping underground stems that help in spreading and forming dense colonies. Its leaves are pinnately divided and have a distinctive, feathery appearance.

Neottopteris latipes thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas across North America.
