Neodryas rhodoneura

Neodryas rhodoneura, commonly known as the red-tailed hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rosette growth habit and showy, daisy-like flowers. The plant features serrated, lance-shaped leaves and produces bright yellow to orange composite flowers that bloom in summer. Neodryas rhodoneura is often found in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, and it is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

It is also a source of nectar for various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
