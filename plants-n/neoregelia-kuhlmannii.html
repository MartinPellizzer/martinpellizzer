Neoregelia kuhlmannii

Neoregelia kuhlmannii, commonly known as the Kuhlmann's bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup to collect rainwater. The plant produces vibrant, showy inflorescences with colorful bracts that can range from red to orange. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Neoregelia kuhlmannii is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
