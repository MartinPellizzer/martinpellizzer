Norysca angustifolia

Norysca angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf norysca, is a perennial herb native to the temperate regions of Southeast Asia.

It features slender, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along its stem. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters during the spring and early summer. Its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

Norysca angustifolia thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.
