Notocactus graessneri

Notocactus graessneri, commonly known as the Desert Candelabra Cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are reduced to spines, which help in reducing water loss in its harsh environment.

Notocactus graessneri is well adapted to extreme drought conditions, making it a resilient plant in desert ecosystems.
