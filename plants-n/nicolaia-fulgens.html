Nicolaia fulgens

Nicolaia fulgens, commonly known as the fiery orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange and red flowers, which are adorned with intricate patterns that resemble flames. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Nicolaia fulgens is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological role in supporting pollinators in its native habitat.
