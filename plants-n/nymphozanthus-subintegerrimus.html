Nymphozanthus subintegerrimus

Nymphozanthus subintegerrimus, commonly known as the red-flowered rock fig, is a shrub native to the arid regions of northern Australia.

It is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and distinctive red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically consumed by various native animals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and drought conditions.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological importance, it is often found in rocky outcrops and dry woodlands.
