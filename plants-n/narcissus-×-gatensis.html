Narcissus × gatensis

Narcissus × gatensis, commonly known as the Giant Paperwhite, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Narcissus tazetta and Narcissus cyclamenus.

It is characterized by its large, white, cup-shaped flowers that are often fragrant and emit a strong scent. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with narrow, grass-like leaves. It is native to the Iberian Peninsula and thrives in cool, moist environments.

This species is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation.
