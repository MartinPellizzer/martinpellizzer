Negundo mandshuricum

Negundo mandshuricum, commonly known as the Chinese boxwood, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and dark green, glossy leaves that remain throughout the growing season. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers in the spring, followed by red berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for ornamental landscaping.

Its hardy nature allows it to withstand cold temperatures, contributing to its adaptability in various climates.
