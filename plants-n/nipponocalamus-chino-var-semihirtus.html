Nipponocalamus chino var. semihirtus

Nipponocalamus chino var. semihirtus, commonly known as the Chinese bamboo or semi-hairy bamboo, is a variant of the Nipponocalamus chino species.

It is a type of grass-like plant belonging to the Poaceae family, characterized by its tall, slender culms and feathery foliage. The plant is native to China and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as mountainous regions. Its leaves are typically covered with fine hairs, giving it the "semi-hairy" designation in its scientific name.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
