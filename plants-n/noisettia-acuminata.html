Noisettia acuminata

Noisettia acuminata, commonly known as the slender nutmeg tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its simple, elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by spherical, woody fruits containing aromatic seeds. Its bark and leaves contain essential oils, which have been traditionally used in herbal medicine.

Noisettia acuminata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its potential economic importance in the spice and fragrance industries.
