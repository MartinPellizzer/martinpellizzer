Nepenthes × lecouflei

Nepenthes × lecouflei, commonly known as the Lecoufle's pitcher plant, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Nepenthes alata and Nepenthes rafflesiana.

It is characterized by its large, waxy pitcher structures that secrete a digestive fluid to trap and digest insects. The plant is native to the Philippines and thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its pitchers can grow up to 30 centimeters in length, and the plant is known for its ornamental value in cultivation.

Nepenthes × lecouflei is a popular specimen among carnivorous plant enthusiasts due to its striking appearance and unique ecological adaptations.
