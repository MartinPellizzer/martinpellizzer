Najas oguraensis

Najas oguraensis, commonly known as the narrowleaf watermilfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Najadaceae and is characterized by its narrow, ribbon-like leaves that are arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant typically grows in still or slow-moving waters, such as ponds and lakes, where it can form dense underwater vegetation. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually borne in clusters and are pollinated by water currents.

Najas oguraensis plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
