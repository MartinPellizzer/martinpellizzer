Nematanthus hirtellus

Nematanthus hirtellus, commonly known as the hairy shield flower, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and showy, tubular flowers. The plant features small, hairy leaves that are opposite in arrangement, and its blooms come in shades of red, orange, and yellow.

Nematanthus hirtellus thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant or in hanging baskets.
