Nymphaea ampla var. parviflora

Nymphaea ampla var. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered water lily, is a aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features broad, floating leaves that provide shade and habitat for aquatic life. The flowers are typically white or pale pink, with a fragrant scent that attracts pollinators. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments.

The plant has a short stem and produces showy blooms that float on the water surface.
