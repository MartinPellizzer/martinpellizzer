Neoabbottia weingartiana

Neoabbottia weingartiana, commonly known as the Weingartian neoabbottia, is a striking orchid native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its vibrant green leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, thriving in cool, humid climates. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with intricate patterns that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts who appreciate its unique beauty.
