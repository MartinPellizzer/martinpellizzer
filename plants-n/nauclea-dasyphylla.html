Nauclea dasyphylla

Nauclea dasyphylla, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, also known as the legume or pea family, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

It is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its durable wood, which is used in traditional crafts and construction.
