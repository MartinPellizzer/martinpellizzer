Narcissus × boutignyanus

Narcissus × boutignyanus, commonly known as the fragrant daffodil, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Narcissus tazetta and Narcissus poeticus.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers with a distinctive, intensely fragrant scent. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with narrow, grass-like leaves and a single stem bearing one to several blooms. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

This hybrid is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its aromatic qualities.
