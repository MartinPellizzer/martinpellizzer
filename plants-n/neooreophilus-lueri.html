Neooreophilus lueri

Neooreophilus lueri, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and striking epiphytic plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its pale, ghostly appearance, with elongated, white or pale green leaves and delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake. It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its unique morphology and ecological significance.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered vulnerable and is often protected in conservation efforts.
