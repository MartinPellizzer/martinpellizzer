Notelaea badula

Notelaea badula, commonly known as the Cook Islands pandanus, is a member of the Pandanus genus within the family Pandanaceae.

It is a shrub or small tree native to the Cook Islands and other parts of Oceania. The plant is characterized by its robust, fibrous leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and thatching. Its inflorescences are clustered and produce small, fragrant flowers.

Notelaea badula plays an important ecological role in coastal and wetland habitats.
