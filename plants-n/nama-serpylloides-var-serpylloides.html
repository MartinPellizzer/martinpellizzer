Nama serpylloides var. serpylloides

Nama serpylloides var. serpylloides, commonly known as the narrowleaf cowpea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that grows up to 1 meter in height, with slender, often hairy stems and narrow, elongated leaves. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers, followed by legume pods containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed areas.

The species is valued for its edible seeds and is also used in traditional medicine.
