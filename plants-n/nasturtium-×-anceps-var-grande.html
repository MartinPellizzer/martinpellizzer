Nasturtium × anceps var. grande

Nasturtium × anceps var. grande, commonly known as the giant nasturtium, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Tropaeolaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 6 inches in diameter, typically in shades of orange, red, and yellow. The plant produces heart-shaped, edible leaves that are peppery and often used in culinary applications. Its growth habit is trailing or climbing, making it suitable for containers, hanging baskets, or as a ground cover.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its role in ecological gardens.
