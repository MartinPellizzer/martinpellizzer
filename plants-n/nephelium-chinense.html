Nephelium chinense

Nephelium chinense, commonly known as the red mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant white flowers. The fruit is unique, with a bright red, segmented rind that encases a sweet, white, segmented flesh. The plant thrives in humid, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its ornamental value, along with its edible fruit, makes it a popular choice in horticulture and agriculture.
