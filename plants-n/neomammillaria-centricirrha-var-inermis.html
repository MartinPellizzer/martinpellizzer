Neomammillaria centricirrha var. inermis

Neomammillaria centricirrha var. inermis, commonly known as the hairless centricirrha cactus, is a small, columnar cactus native to Mexico.

It features a smooth, green stem with white, radial spines that are shorter and more sparse compared to the typical variety. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water and well-draining soil.

This variety is prized for its compact size and attractive appearance in desert gardens.
