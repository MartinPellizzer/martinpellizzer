Neja dianthifolia

Neja dianthifolia, commonly known as the rock violet, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with small, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and is adapted to harsh, alpine environments. Its flowers are usually purple or violet in color, with four petals arranged in a cross shape, a characteristic feature of the Brassicaceae family.

Neja dianthifolia is often found in open meadows and along rocky slopes at high altitudes.
