Nerine filifolia

Nerine filifolia, commonly known as the threadleaf nerine, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its strap-shaped, grass-like leaves. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple, typically blooming in late summer. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Nerine filifolia is known for its resilience and ability to thrive in a variety of garden conditions.
