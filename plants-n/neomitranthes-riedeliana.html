Neomitranthes riedeliana

Neomitranthes riedeliana, commonly known as the Riedel's starling plant, is a striking member of the Aizoaceae family, native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its unique, star-shaped flowers that resemble the plumage of a starling bird, hence its common name. The plant features succulent, fleshy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, aiding in water retention in its arid habitat. It typically blooms in spring, producing clusters of vibrant yellow to orange flowers.

Neomitranthes riedeliana is well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
