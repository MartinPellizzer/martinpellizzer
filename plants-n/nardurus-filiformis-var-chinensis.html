Nardurus filiformis var. chinensis

Nardurus filiformis var. chinensis, commonly known as Chinese threadleaf, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It features slender, thread-like leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are often white or pale pink, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
