Narcissus × christopheri

Narcissus × christopheri, commonly known as the Christopher daffodil, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Narcissus poeticus and Narcissus tazetta.

It is characterized by its large, showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically yellow with a prominent central corona. The plant is native to southern Europe and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. It is a hardy perennial that blooms in early spring, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its botanical features include strap-like leaves and a robust root system that supports its vigorous growth.
