Nasturtium officinale

Nasturtium officinale, commonly known as the garden nasturtium, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that come in shades of orange, yellow, and red, and its heart-shaped, edible leaves. The plant is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbages, and is known for its peppery taste. Its flowers and young leaves are often used in culinary applications, adding a spicy flavor to salads and dishes.

Nasturtium officinale is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract beneficial insects to the garden.
