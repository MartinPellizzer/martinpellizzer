Nymphaea chartacea

Nymphaea chartacea, commonly known as the paper water lily, is a aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are thin and papery in texture. The plant produces showy, white or pink flowers that float on the water surface. Its roots are submerged, anchoring it in the mud at the bottom of ponds and lakes.

Nymphaea chartacea plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
