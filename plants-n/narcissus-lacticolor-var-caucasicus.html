Narcissus lacticolor var. caucasicus

Narcissus lacticolor var. caucasicus, commonly known as the Caucasian milk yellow daffodil, is a rare and ornamental variant of the daffodil genus.

It is characterized by its pale yellow, often milk-white petals and a distinctive, narrow trumpet-shaped corona. Native to the Caucasus region, this plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its foliage consists of long, strap-like leaves that emerge in early spring.

The plant is valued for its unique coloration and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its aesthetic and horticultural significance.
