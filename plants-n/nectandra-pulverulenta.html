Nectandra pulverulenta

Nectandra pulverulenta, commonly known as the powdered nectandra, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurel trees, and is characterized by its aromatic, often fragrant foliage. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by dark, fleshy berries. Its leaves are typically glossy and have a distinct, spicy scent, which is attributed to the presence of essential oils.

Nectandra pulverulenta is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
