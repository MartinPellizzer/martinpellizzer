Nephrodium decurrens var. thwaitesii

Nephrodium decurrens var. thwaitesii, commonly known as the Thwaites' nephrolepis, is a variant of the nephrolepis fern characterized by its distinctive frond morphology.

It features long, pinnately compound fronds with a smooth, glossy surface and a tendency to droop gracefully. The plant exhibits a robust rhizome system that allows it to spread and form dense colonies in suitable habitats. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique varietal characteristics and ecological adaptability.
