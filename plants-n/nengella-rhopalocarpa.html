Nengella rhopalocarpa

Nengella rhopalocarpa, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. It is often found in montane forests and is adapted to cool, humid climates.

Nengella rhopalocarpa is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
