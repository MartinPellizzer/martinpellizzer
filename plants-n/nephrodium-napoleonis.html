Nephrodium napoleonis

Nephrodium napoleonis, commonly known as Napoleon's kidney fern, is a rare and unique species of fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive kidney-shaped fronds, which are deeply lobed and often have a velvety texture. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically growing on tree trunks or in dense undergrowth. Its reproductive structures, including sporangia, are located on the underside of the fronds, a common feature in ferns.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, Nephrodium napoleonis is considered a conservation concern.
