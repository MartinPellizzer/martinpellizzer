Nectandra grandiflora var. obovata

Nectandra grandiflora var. obovata, commonly known as the broad-leafed sandarac tree, is a tropical evergreen species native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, obovate leaves that are glossy and dark green in color. The plant produces fragrant, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its wood is valued for its aromatic properties and is used in traditional crafts and incense.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.
