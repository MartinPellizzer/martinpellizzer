Neolobivia incaica

Neolobivia incaica, commonly known as the Inca's lobe cactus, is a unique cactus species native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its distinctive lobed stems and spiny structure, adapted to the harsh, arid conditions of its environment. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its rugged form. Its leaves are modified into spines, reducing water loss and protecting it from herbivores.

Neolobivia incaica is valued not only for its ecological role but also for its cultural significance in Andean traditions.
