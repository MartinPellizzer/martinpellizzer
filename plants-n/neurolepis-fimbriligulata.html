Neurolepis fimbriligulata

Neurolepis fimbriligulata, commonly known as the fringed-leaved neurolepis, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves that are often adorned with fine fringes, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
