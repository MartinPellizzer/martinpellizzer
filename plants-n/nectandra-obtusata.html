Nectandra obtusata

Nectandra obtusata, commonly known as the broad-leaved sandarac tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its wood is valued for its aromatic properties and is used in traditional medicine and incense.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in humid, tropical climates with well-drained soil.
