Narcissus papyraceus var. niveus

Narcissus papyraceus var. niveus, commonly known as the white paper narcissus, is a variety of the genus Narcissus native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its delicate, white, cup-shaped flowers that resemble small paper petals, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows to a height of about 15-20 cm and thrives in well-drained soil in full sun. It is a bulbous perennial that blooms in early spring, often before the leaves fully emerge.

This variety is prized for its elegant appearance and is often cultivated in gardens for ornamental purposes.
