Nepeta minor

Nepeta minor, commonly known as catmint, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in herbal teas and have a mild minty scent.

Nepeta minor is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
