Neottia plantaginea

Neottia plantaginea, commonly known as the broad-leaf twayblade, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. The plant has a distinctive appearance with broad, grass-like leaves and a single, inconspicuous flower that is often overlooked. It is known for its ability to form symbiotic relationships with mycorrhizal fungi, which aid in nutrient absorption.

Neottia plantaginea is also notable for its lack of chlorophyll in its leaves, relying instead on its fungal partners for sustenance.
