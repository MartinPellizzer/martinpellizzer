Narcissus nivalis var. granatensis

Narcissus nivalis var. granatensis, commonly known as the Granatensis Crown Imperial, is a rare and striking variant of the Crown Imperial narcissus.

It is characterized by its large, showy, upward-facing flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow blooms, which are borne on long, slender stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in alpine or rocky habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is a sought-after addition to specialized gardens.
