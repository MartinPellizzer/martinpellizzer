Nuphar centricavata

Nuphar centricavata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf water lily, is a perennial aquatic plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves and showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a rhizomatous root system that anchors it in shallow water. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a waxy surface that repels water.

Nuphar centricavata plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
