Nepeta betonicoides

Nepeta betonicoides, commonly known as catmint, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in herbal teas and have a minty, slightly fuzzy texture.

Nepeta betonicoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
