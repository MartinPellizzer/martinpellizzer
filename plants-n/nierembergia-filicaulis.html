Nierembergia filicaulis

Nierembergia filicaulis, commonly known as the fernleaf bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sun. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

Nierembergia filicaulis is a perennial species that can spread through rhizomes, forming dense mats over time.
