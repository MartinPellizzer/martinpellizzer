Neolitsea sericea var. aurata

Neolitsea sericea var. aurata, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, adding vibrant color to the landscape. The leaves are usually small, lance-shaped, and covered in a fine, silvery down, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is commonly planted in gardens and parks for its aesthetic appeal.
