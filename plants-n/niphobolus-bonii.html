Niphobolus bonii

Niphobolus bonii, commonly known as the yellow-veined nettle, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family, which includes many stinging plants. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with prominent yellow veins and a rough, hairy texture. It produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, and it is often found in moist, shaded areas.

Niphobolus bonii is known for its ability to thrive in rocky soils and its historical use in traditional medicine.
