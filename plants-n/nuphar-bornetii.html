Nuphar bornetii

Nuphar bornetii, commonly known as the Bornet's water lily, is a species of aquatic plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves that form a dense layer on the water surface, providing habitat for various aquatic organisms. The plant produces showy, yellowish-white flowers that bloom above the water, attracting pollinators such as beetles and bees. Its rhizomes are often submerged, anchoring the plant in shallow waters and enabling it to spread vegetatively.

Nuphar bornetii plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing water bodies and supporting biodiversity.
