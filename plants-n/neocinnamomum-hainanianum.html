Neocinnamomum hainanianum

Neocinnamomum hainanianum, commonly known as Hainan cinnamon, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to the island of Hainan in China and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces dark brown, oily seeds that are used in traditional medicine and as a spice. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in dense forests with high humidity.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and commercial purposes.
