Narcissus × heminalis

Narcissus × heminalis, commonly known as the hybrid daffodil, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a hybrid between Narcissus poeticus and Narcissus tazetta, resulting in a plant with striking, trumpet-shaped flowers. The foliage consists of strap-like leaves that are typically dark green and grass-like in appearance. The flowers bloom in spring, often in shades of yellow or white, and are highly valued for their ornamental beauty.

This plant is commonly cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and resilience in various climates.
