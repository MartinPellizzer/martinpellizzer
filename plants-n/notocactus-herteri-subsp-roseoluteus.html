Notocactus herteri subsp. roseoluteus

Notocactus herteri subsp.

roseoluteus, commonly known as the roseolate notocactus, is a species of cactus native to the dry regions of South America. It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are covered in dense, white to pink spines.

The plant produces vibrant, funnel-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often appearing in late spring. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and slow growth, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts and collectors.
