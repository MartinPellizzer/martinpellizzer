Nama dichotoma var. angustifolia

Nama dichotoma var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf nama, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its slender, angular stems and small, narrow leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant in dry landscapes.
