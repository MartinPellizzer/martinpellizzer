Neomicrocalamus microphyllus

Neomicrocalamus microphyllus, commonly known as the dwarf bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to southern China.

It is characterized by its slender, clustered growth habit and small, narrow leaves. The plant produces tiny, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its delicate appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
