Nigella bithynica

Nigella bithynica, commonly known as Turkish coltsfoot, is a flowering plant native to Turkey and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its basal leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments and is often found in mountainous regions. Its seeds, which are small and black, are used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

Nigella bithynica is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
