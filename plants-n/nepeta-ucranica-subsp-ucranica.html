Nepeta ucranica subsp. ucranica

Nepeta ucranica subsp.

ucranica, commonly known as the Ukrainian catmint, is a flowering plant native to parts of Eastern Europe. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves.

The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
