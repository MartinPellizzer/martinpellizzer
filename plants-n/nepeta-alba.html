Nepeta alba

Nepeta alba, commonly known as white catmint, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue or white flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in herbal remedies due to their mild mint-like scent and potential calming properties.

Nepeta alba is valued in gardens for its ornamental beauty and ecological benefits.
