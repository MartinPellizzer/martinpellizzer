Nephrodium grayi

Nephrodium grayi, commonly known as the gray's nephroid, is a rare and unique fern species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, kidney-shaped fronds that are typically dark green with a glossy texture. The plant exhibits a compact growth habit, often forming dense clusters near the base. Its reproductive structures, including sporangia, are located on the underside of the fronds, typical of ferns.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Nephrodium grayi is considered a valuable subject for botanical research and conservation efforts.
