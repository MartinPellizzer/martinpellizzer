Neocinnamomum confertiflorum

Neocinnamomum confertiflorum, commonly known as the dense-flower cinnamon tree, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia, particularly in countries like Vietnam and Cambodia. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered white flowers that bloom in spring. Its bark and leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The tree can grow up to 10 meters tall and is valued for both its ornamental and medicinal properties.
