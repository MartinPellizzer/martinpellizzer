Nymphaea odorata subsp. minor

Nymphaea odorata subsp.

minor, commonly known as the lesser sacred lotus, is a aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and fragrant, white or pink flowers that bloom above the water surface.

The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in ponds and marshes. Its roots are anchored in the substrate, while its leaves float on the water's surface.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and traditional uses in various cultures.
