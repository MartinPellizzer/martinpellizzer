Narcissus tazetta subsp. dubius

Narcissus tazetta subsp.

dubius, commonly known as the Persian daffodil, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region. It produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to white, typically blooming in early spring.

The plant has narrow, strap-like leaves that emerge from a basal rosette. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly different flower morphology compared to other Narcissus species.
