Nassella charruana

Nassella charruana, commonly known as purple needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and fluffy, purple-tinted seed heads that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed areas. Its fibrous root system helps prevent soil erosion, making it an important species in ecological restoration.

Nassella charruana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its elegant, feathery foliage.
