Nardurus montanus

Nardurus montanus, commonly known as the mountain nardus, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of South America.

It belongs to the family Poaceae, commonly known as the grass family, and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, pale blue or white flowers. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its unique adaptations, Nardurus montanus plays an important role in stabilizing soil in its harsh habitat.
