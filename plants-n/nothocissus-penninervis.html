Nothocissus penninervis

Nothocissus penninervis, commonly known as the hybrid Virginia creeper, is a vigorous climbing vine native to the southeastern United States.

It features compound leaves with three to five lobes, which are often lobed and turn bright red in the fall. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, followed by orange-red berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

Its rapid growth and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for landscaping, though it can be invasive in some regions.
