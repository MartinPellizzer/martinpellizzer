Normandiodendron romii

Normandiodendron romii, commonly known as the Roman candle tree, is a deciduous tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The tree is notable for its striking, candle-like flower clusters that bloom in late spring. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces pods that contain edible seeds.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
