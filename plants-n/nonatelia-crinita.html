Nonatelia crinita

Nonatelia crinita, commonly known as the hairy morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. Nonatelia crinita is a climbing vine that thrives in moist, sunny environments.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
