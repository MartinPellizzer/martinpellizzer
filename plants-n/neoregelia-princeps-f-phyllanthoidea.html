Neoregelia princeps f. phyllanthoidea

Neoregelia princeps f. phyllanthoidea, commonly known as the Phyllanthoidea form of the prince's neoregelia, is a striking bromeliad native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its vibrant, overlapping foliage that forms a rosette shape, often displaying shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces strikingly colorful inflorescences with red bracts that contrast beautifully with its foliage. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires high humidity and well-draining soil.

This cultivar is popular among bromeliad enthusiasts for its ornamental value and ease of care.
