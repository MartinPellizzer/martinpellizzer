Nectandra tabascensis

Nectandra tabascensis, commonly known as the Tabasco sand-leaf tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and fragrant flowers. The plant is typically found in moist, well-drained soils in lowland tropical forests. Its leaves are leathery and have a distinctive, aromatic scent, while its flowers are small and clustered.

Nectandra tabascensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine.
