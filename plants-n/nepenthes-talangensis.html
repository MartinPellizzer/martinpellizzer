Nepenthes talangensis

Nepenthes talangensis, commonly known as the Talangensis Pitcher Plant, is a tropical carnivorous plant native to the rainforests of Borneo.

It is characterized by its unique, pitcher-shaped leaves that secrete a sticky nectar to trap and digest insects. The plant's pitchers are typically green or reddish in color, with a waxy surface that helps in the adhesion of prey. It thrives in high humidity and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This species is notable for its adaptations to nutrient-poor environments, relying on insect prey for essential nutrients.
