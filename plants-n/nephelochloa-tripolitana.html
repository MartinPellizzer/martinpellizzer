Nephelochloa tripolitana

Nephelochloa tripolitana, commonly known as the Tripoli hairgrass, is a low-growing perennial grass native to North Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and delicate, light green blades that often form dense mats. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, exhibiting drought tolerance and a preference for well-drained soils. Its reproductive structures include small, spike-like inflorescences bearing minute flowers.

Nephelochloa tripolitana plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
