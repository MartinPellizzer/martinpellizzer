Navia navicularis

Navia navicularis, commonly known as the water scurfpea, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along streambanks and in riparian areas. The plant features slender, creeping stems and small, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are pea-like, with a distinctive blue to purple coloration, and it produces small, pod-like fruits containing several seeds.

Navia navicularis is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
