Nymphaea fluviatilis

Nymphaea fluviatilis, commonly known as the water lily, is a species of aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that open during the day. The plant has a submerged root system that anchors it in muddy substrates, while its rhizomes spread horizontally to support growth. Its flowers are typically pink to white, with a prominent yellow stamen, and they play a role in attracting pollinators such as beetles and bees.

Nymphaea fluviatilis is an important component of wetland ecosystems, providing habitat and oxygen to aquatic life.
