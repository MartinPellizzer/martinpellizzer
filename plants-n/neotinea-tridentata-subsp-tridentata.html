Neotinea tridentata subsp. tridentata

Neotinea tridentata subsp.

tridentata, commonly known as the three-toothed fern, is a species of fern native to parts of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea. It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its delicate, trifoliate (three-part) fronds.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky slopes. Its rhizomes are often covered in a dense network of roots, which help anchor it to the substrate.
