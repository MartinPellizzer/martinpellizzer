Narcissus linnaeanus proles chrysanthus

Narcissus linnaeanus proles chrysanthus, commonly known as the golden daffodil, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers with a trumpet-shaped corona and narrow, grass-like leaves. Native to southern Europe, this plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight. The flowers typically bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
