Nepeta michauxii

Nepeta michauxii, commonly known as Michaux's catmint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces spikes of blue to purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often fragrant and have a mint-like scent, which contributes to its common name.

Nepeta michauxii is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
