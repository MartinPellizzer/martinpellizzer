Niphogeton kalbreyeri

Niphogeton kalbreyeri, commonly known as the Kalbreyer's lily, is a striking herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically blooms in late summer, producing fragrant blossoms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming a basal rosette, while its roots are fibrous and adapted to thrive in well-drained soils.

Niphogeton kalbreyeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegance and resilience.
