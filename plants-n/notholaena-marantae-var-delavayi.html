Notholaena marantae var. delavayi

Notholaena marantae var. delavayi, commonly known as Delavay's flame grass, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Marantaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage that often displays shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows as a rhizomatous perennial, forming dense clumps with broad, lanceolate leaves. It is native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia, particularly in China and Vietnam.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens for its vibrant and long-lasting foliage.
