Nepeta platystegia

Nepeta platystegia, commonly known as the broad-leaved catnip, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a fuzzy texture. The plant produces small, clustered blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Its aromatic leaves and flowers have been used historically for their calming properties and as a natural remedy.
