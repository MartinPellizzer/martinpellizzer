Narcissus × uniflorus

Narcissus × uniflorus, commonly known as the single-flower daffodil, is a hybrid plant resulting from the crossbreeding of various narcissus species.

It is characterized by its single, large, showy flower that typically blooms in early spring. The plant has narrow, strap-like leaves and a robust, rhizomatous root system. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal, it is widely cultivated in temperate regions.
