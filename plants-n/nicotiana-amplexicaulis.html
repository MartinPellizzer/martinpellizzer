Nicotiana amplexicaulis

Nicotiana amplexicaulis, commonly known as the clasping tobacco, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive foliage, where the leaves wrap around the stem, giving it a unique appearance. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is closely related to the tobacco plant.

Due to its ornamental value and historical significance in traditional medicine, it is cultivated in some regions for both aesthetic and practical purposes.
