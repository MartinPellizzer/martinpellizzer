Nama schaffneri

Nama schaffneri, commonly known as the Schaffner's nama, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its strap-shaped, grass-like leaves. The plant produces striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a greenish tinge. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Nama schaffneri is often used in rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
