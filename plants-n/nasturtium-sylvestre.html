Nasturtium sylvestre

Nasturtium sylvestre, commonly known as wild nasturtium, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Tropaeolaceae and is characterized by its vibrant yellow and orange flowers. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves and has a peppery scent, similar to its cultivated relative, garden nasturtium. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in woodland edges or along stream banks.

Nasturtium sylvestre is also known for its edible flowers and leaves, which are used in culinary applications.
