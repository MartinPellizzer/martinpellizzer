Norantea

Norantea, commonly known as the silverleaf orchid, is a rare and striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery pubescence, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale pink, often with a slight purple tinge. Norantea thrives in humid, shaded environments and is native to certain regions of South America.

Its unique appearance and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and horticulturists alike.
