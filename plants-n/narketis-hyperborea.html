Narketis hyperborea

Narketis hyperborea, commonly known as the Arctic bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that thrives in cold, rocky environments. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. Its leaves are narrow and succulent, adapted to retain moisture in harsh conditions.

Narketis hyperborea plays a role in the ecological balance of its alpine and tundra habitats.
