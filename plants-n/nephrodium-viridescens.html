Nephrodium viridescens

Nephrodium viridescens, commonly known as the green kidney fern, is a species of fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, kidney-shaped fronds that are typically dark green with a slightly hairy texture. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its spores are produced on the undersides of the fronds in specialized structures called sori.

Nephrodium viridescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
