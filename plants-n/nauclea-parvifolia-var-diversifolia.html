Nauclea parvifolia var. diversifolia

Nauclea parvifolia var. diversifolia, commonly known as the variegated nauclea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that often exhibit variegation in color. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by elliptical, woody fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it may be invasive in certain areas.
