Nabalus trifoliolatus var. dissectifolius

Nabalus trifoliolatus var. dissectifolius, commonly known as the dissected trifoliate bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves, which are deeply lobed and often appear feather-like. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or violet, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, preferring rocky or alpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
