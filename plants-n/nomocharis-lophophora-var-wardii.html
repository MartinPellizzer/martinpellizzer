Nomocharis lophophora var. wardii

Nomocharis lophophora var. wardii, commonly known as the Ward's ghost orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its unique, ghost-like appearance with translucent, white petals and sepals that resemble a spectral figure. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in swampy areas or along the edges of water bodies. Its flowers bloom in late summer, producing a delicate, fragrant aroma that attracts specific pollinators.

Due to its limited habitat and endangered status, it is highly protected and often cultivated in specialized conservation programs.
