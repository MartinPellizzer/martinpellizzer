Nenax velutina

Nenax velutina, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety hair, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in spikes, and it is often found in moist, disturbed soils. Nenax velutina is a member of the Malvaceae family, which includes cotton and hibiscus.

It is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its rapid growth and ability to thrive in diverse conditions.
