Nicotiana breviflora

Nicotiana breviflora, commonly known as the short-flowered tobacco, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of purple or blue. The plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are broad and hairy, and it prefers well-drained soil in full sun.

It is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and ease of cultivation.
