Nectandra steinbachii

Nectandra steinbachii, commonly known as the yellow sandarac tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its aromatic, reddish-brown bark and glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by elongated, woody pods containing dark brown seeds. It is valued for its fragrant resin, which has been historically used in incense and medicinal applications.

The tree thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland tropical forests.
