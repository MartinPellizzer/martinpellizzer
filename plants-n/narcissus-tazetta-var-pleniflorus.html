Narcissus tazetta var. pleniflorus

Narcissus tazetta var. pleniflorus, commonly known as the Turkish daffodil, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces multiple, showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to white, often with a distinct green stripe down the center. The plant typically blooms in early spring, and its foliage consists of strap-like, grassy leaves that emerge from the base of the bulb. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, long-lasting blooms.

The variety is characterized by its high flower count per stem, making it a popular choice for floral arrangements.
