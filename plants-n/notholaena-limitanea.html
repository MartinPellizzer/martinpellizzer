Notholaena limitanea

Notholaena limitanea, commonly known as the narrowleaf hawksbeard, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble single flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. Its narrow, grass-like leaves are arranged in a basal rosette, and it produces yellow composite flowers in the summer.

Notholaena limitanea is often found in shaded to partially shaded environments, thriving in rich, loamy soils.
