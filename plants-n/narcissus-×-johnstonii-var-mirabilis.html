Narcissus × johnstonii var. mirabilis

Narcissus × johnstonii var. mirabilis, commonly known as the yellow daffodil, is a hybrid cultivar of the genus Narcissus.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow petals and a distinctive, trumpet-shaped corona. This variety is native to the British Isles and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and produces clusters of flowers in early spring.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking early blooming bulbs.
