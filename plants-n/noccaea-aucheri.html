Noccaea aucheri

Noccaea aucheri, commonly known as the alpine nailwort, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with small, cruciform flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are basal, rosette-like, and often covered in a fine layer of white hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The flowers are small, yellowish-green, and appear in clusters, contributing to its ornamental value in garden settings.
