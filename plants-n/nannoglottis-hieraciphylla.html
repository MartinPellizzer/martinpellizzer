Nannoglottis hieraciphylla

Nannoglottis hieraciphylla, commonly known as the silverleaf dandelion, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features a rosette of fleshy, silver-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by fluffy seed heads similar to those of the common dandelion. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
