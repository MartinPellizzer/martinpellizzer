Nerine fothergillii var. major

Nerine fothergillii var. major, commonly known as the giant nerine, is a striking bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red, with a prominent central stamen. The plant has strap-like, dark green leaves that emerge from a bulb and are often covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance, it is favored by gardeners in temperate climates.
