Nephrolepis intramarginalis

Nephrolepis intramarginalis, commonly known as the margin-leaf maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are typically dark green with a slightly curled appearance. The plant features a creeping rhizome that produces upright, lance-shaped pinnae with finely divided segments. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
