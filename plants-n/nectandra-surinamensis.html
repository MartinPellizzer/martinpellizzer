Nectandra surinamensis

Nectandra surinamensis, commonly known as Surinam sandwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its aromatic, dark-colored wood that is highly valued for its durability and scent. The plant typically grows in humid lowland forests and has compound leaves with a glossy surface. Its flowers are small and clustered, and it produces oval, woody fruits containing seeds.

Nectandra surinamensis is also noted for its medicinal properties, with various parts of the plant used in traditional medicine.
