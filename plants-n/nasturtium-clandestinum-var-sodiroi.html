Nasturtium clandestinum var. sodiroi

Nasturtium clandestinum var. sodiroi, commonly known as the Mexican watercress, is a herbaceous plant native to Mexico.

It features heart-shaped, succulent leaves that are peppery and edible, often used in salads or as a garnish. The plant produces bright orange or red flowers with five petals, typical of the Tropaeolaceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued not only for its culinary uses but also for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract beneficial insects.
