Neurosoria

Neurosoria, commonly known as the "devil's ivy," is a trailing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Araliaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and rapid growth habit. The plant is known for its adaptability, thriving in a wide range of light conditions and soil types. Its aerial roots allow it to climb and attach to surfaces, making it a popular choice for hanging baskets and vertical gardens.

Neurosoria is also valued for its ability to purify indoor air, contributing to its popularity as a houseplant.
