Nemum atracuminatum

Nemum atracuminatum, commonly known as the black nightshade, is a flowering plant belonging to the Solanaceae family.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black berries, which contain toxic compounds such as solanine. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are simple, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The flowers are small, bell-shaped, and usually white or pale green, blooming in the summer months.
