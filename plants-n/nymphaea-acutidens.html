Nymphaea acutidens

Nymphaea acutidens, commonly known as the narrow-leaved water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It features broad, heart-shaped leaves that float on the water's surface, supported by long, flexible petioles. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, often opening in the early morning. Its roots anchor it in muddy substrates, while its rhizomes spread horizontally to form dense colonies.

Nymphaea acutidens plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
