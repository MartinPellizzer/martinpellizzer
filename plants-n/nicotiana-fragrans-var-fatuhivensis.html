Nicotiana fragrans var. fatuhivensis

Nicotiana fragrans var. fatuhivensis, commonly known as the Fatuhivensis tobacco, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its large, velvety leaves and clusters of fragrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink to purple. The plant is part of the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is known for its strong, sweet scent, which attracts pollinators such as moths and bats. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional practices.
