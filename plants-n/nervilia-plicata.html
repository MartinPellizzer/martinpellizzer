Nervilia plicata

Nervilia plicata, commonly known as the zigzag orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its unique, zigzagging inflorescence and dark green, strap-like leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Nervilia plicata thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

It is a perennial herb with a rhizomatous root system that helps it survive in its natural habitat.
