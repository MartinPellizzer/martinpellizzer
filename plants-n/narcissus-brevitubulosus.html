Narcissus brevitubulosus

Narcissus brevitubulosus, commonly known as the short-tubed daffodil, is a species of flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves and a single, showy yellow flower with a prominent corona. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or meadow habitats. It blooms in early spring, often before the leaves are fully developed.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
