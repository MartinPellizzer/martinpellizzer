Nicotiana capensis

Nicotiana capensis, commonly known as the Cape nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are usually violet or blue in color, blooming in late summer to early autumn. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a waxy coating.

The plant contains alkaloids, including nicotine, which contributes to its historical use in traditional medicine and as a source for tobacco products.
