Neocodon stevenii

Neocodon stevenii, commonly known as the Steven's lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of bell-shaped, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lanceolate and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Neocodon stevenii is valued for its elegant floral display and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and hardiness.
