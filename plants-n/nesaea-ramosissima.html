Nesaea ramosissima

Nesaea ramosissima, commonly known as the fragrant snowbell, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, often found in woodland habitats. Its fragrant flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological diversity.

The plant's ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking native, hardy plants.
