Nicotiana rustica var. minor

Nicotiana rustica var. minor, commonly known as the lesser tobacco plant, is a species native to the Americas.

It is a herbaceous annual plant that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white to pale purple. Its flowers are typically arranged in terminal racemes, and the plant is known for its high nicotine content in its leaves.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in disturbed soils.
