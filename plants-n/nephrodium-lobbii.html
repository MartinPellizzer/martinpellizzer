Nephrodium lobbii

Nephrodium lobbii, commonly known as the Lobb's holly fern, is a rare and endangered fern species native to the rainforests of Queensland, Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, leathery fronds that are often deeply divided into narrow, lance-shaped segments. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, thriving in the understorey of dense tropical forests. Its reproductive structures, including sporangia, are found on the undersides of its fronds, contributing to its unique botanical profile.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, conservation efforts are crucial for its survival.
