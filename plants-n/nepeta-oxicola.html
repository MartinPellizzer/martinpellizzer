Nepeta oxicola

Nepeta oxicola, commonly known as the alpine catmint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its small, tubular flowers are usually blue to purple in color, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Nepeta oxicola is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in harsh, high-altitude conditions.
