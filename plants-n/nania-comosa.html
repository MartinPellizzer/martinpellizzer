Nania comosa

Nania comosa, commonly known as the hairy sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It features succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in dry environments. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. Its root system is shallow but extensive, allowing it to thrive in sandy or gravelly soils.

Nania comosa plays a vital role in stabilizing dunes and preventing soil erosion in its native habitats.
