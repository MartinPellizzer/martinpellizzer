Nauclea purpurascens

Nauclea purpurascens, commonly known as the purple-flowered nauclea, is a deciduous shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, purple-pink flowers. The plant produces small, dark blue to black berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Nauclea purpurascens is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some local communities.
