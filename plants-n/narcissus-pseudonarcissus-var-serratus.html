Narcissus pseudonarcissus var. serratus

Narcissus pseudonarcissus var. serratus, commonly known as the serrated daffodil, is a botanical variant of the common daffodil.

It is characterized by its narrow, serrated leaf margins and a striking yellow flower with a prominent, trumpet-shaped corona. This plant is native to southern Europe and thrives in well-drained soil in full sun. It is a perennial bulbous plant that produces multiple stems and blooms in early spring.

The serrated daffodil is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, long-lasting flowers.
