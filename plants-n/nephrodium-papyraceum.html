Nephrodium papyraceum

Nephrodium papyraceum, commonly known as the paper-leaf nephroid, is a unique fern species characterized by its delicate, papery fronds.

Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and exhibit a light green to pale yellow coloration. The plant produces sporangia on the underside of its fronds, which are clustered in small, rounded structures called sori. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its rare occurrence and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a botanical curiosity and is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
