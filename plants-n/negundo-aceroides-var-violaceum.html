Negundo aceroides var. violaceum

Negundo aceroides var. violaceum, commonly known as the violet-leafed silver maple, is a variant of the silver maple tree characterized by its distinctive violet-colored foliage.

This plant is native to the eastern United States and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are typically palmately lobed with a silvery underside, adding to its ornamental appeal. The tree produces small, winged seeds that are dispersed by wind, facilitating its spread.

It is often cultivated for its unique leaf color and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
