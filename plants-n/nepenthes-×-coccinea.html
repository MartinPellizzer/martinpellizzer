Nepenthes × coccinea

Nepenthes × coccinea, commonly known as the red-bellied pitcher plant, is a hybrid carnivorous plant native to the rainforests of Borneo.

It is characterized by its striking red-colored pitchers, which are modified leaves that secrete digestive enzymes to trap and digest insects. The plant's pitchers are typically filled with water, creating a trap for unsuspecting prey. Nepenthes × coccinea thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires high levels of atmospheric moisture for optimal growth.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it a fascinating subject for botanical study.
