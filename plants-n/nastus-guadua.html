Nastus guadua

Nastus guadua, commonly known as Guadua bamboo, is a tall, woody perennial plant native to South America, particularly Colombia and Ecuador.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its strong, hollow stems that can grow up to 20 meters in height. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters. Nastus guadua is valued for its durable and lightweight bamboo-like material, making it useful in construction and crafts.

Its rapid growth and resilience make it an important resource in tropical ecosystems.
