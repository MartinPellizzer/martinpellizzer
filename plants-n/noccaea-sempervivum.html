Noccaea sempervivum

Noccaea sempervivum, commonly known as stonecrop, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers. The plant is known for its hardiness, thriving in rocky or gravelly soils and tolerating drought conditions. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, aiding in its propagation.

Noccaea sempervivum is often used in rock gardens and as a decorative plant due to its ornamental appearance and low maintenance requirements.
