Nipponocalamus hisauchii

Nipponocalamus hisauchii, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or Hisauchii bamboo, is a species of grass belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that grow in dense clumps. The plant typically reaches a height of 1 to 2 meters and features narrow, linear leaves with a smooth, grass-like texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Nipponocalamus hisauchii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
