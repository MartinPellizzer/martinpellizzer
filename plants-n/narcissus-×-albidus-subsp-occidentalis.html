Narcissus × albidus subsp. occidentalis

Narcissus × albidus subsp.

occidentalis, commonly known as the western daffodil, is a hybrid species within the Amaryllidaceae family. It is characterized by its large, showy, white tepals that often feature a yellow or greenish throat.

Native to the western United States, particularly California, this plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. It typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its early spring blooms.
