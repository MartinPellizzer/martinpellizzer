Nepeta nuda var. obtusifolia

Nepeta nuda var. obtusifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf catmint, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine, grayish hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a serrated margin. The plant produces clusters of small, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
