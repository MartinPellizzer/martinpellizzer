Nervilia pallidiflora

Nervilia pallidiflora, commonly known as the pale-flowered orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the temperate regions of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous areas. The plant features a slender rhizome and produces delicate, pale pink to white flowers with a distinctive, spurred lip. Its leaves are linear and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its elegant appearance.

The species is notable for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its use in traditional herbal medicine.
