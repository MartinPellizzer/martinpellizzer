Nepeta hederacea f. albovariegata

Nepeta hederacea f. albovariegata, commonly known as the white-variegated catmint, is a compact, herbaceous perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It features aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that are variegated with white and green patterns, adding visual interest to gardens. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant blue or purple flowers in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a versatile choice for borders or containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long blooming period.
