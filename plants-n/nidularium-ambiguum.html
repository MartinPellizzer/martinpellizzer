Nidularium ambiguum

Nidularium ambiguum, commonly known as the ambiguous nest plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Bromeliaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a water-holding cup, typical of many bromeliads. Its small, colorful flowers are usually borne in clusters and attract various pollinators.

Nidularium ambiguum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
