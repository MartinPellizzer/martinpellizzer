Nauclea cinchonae

Nauclea cinchonae, commonly known as the African quinine tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves with prominent veins. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous small, black seeds. It is known for its medicinal properties, particularly due to the presence of alkaloids similar to quinine.

The plant thrives in humid, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.
