Narcissus bulbocodium

Narcissus bulbocodium, commonly known as the bulbous daffodil, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its bulbous root system and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in well-drained soil in full sun to partial shade.

Narcissus bulbocodium is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
