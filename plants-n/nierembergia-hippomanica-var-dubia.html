Nierembergia hippomanica var. dubia

Nierembergia hippomanica var. dubia, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in shrubland and coastal areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, while its stems are upright and branched.

It is a popular ornamental plant due to its vibrant color and relatively low maintenance requirements.
