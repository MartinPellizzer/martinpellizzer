Narcissus broussonetii var. grandiflorus

Narcissus broussonetii var. grandiflorus, commonly known as the giant daffodil, is a striking member of the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers with a prominent central corona. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and thrives in well-drained, fertile soil. It produces strap-shaped leaves that are dark green and densely arranged.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
