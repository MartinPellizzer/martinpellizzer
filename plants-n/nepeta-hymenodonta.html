Nepeta hymenodonta

Nepeta hymenodonta, commonly known as the woolly catmint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features hairy, gray-green leaves and produces small, pale blue to violet flowers clustered in whorls along the stems. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens or borders. Its aromatic foliage and showy blooms attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Nepeta hymenodonta is also valued for its ornamental appeal and low maintenance requirements.
