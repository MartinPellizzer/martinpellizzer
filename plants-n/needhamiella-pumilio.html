Needhamiella pumilio

Needhamiella pumilio, commonly known as the dwarf mooncup, is a small, flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its delicate, white to pale purple flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant has a short, hairy stem and produces small, round fruits that are edible when ripe. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or forested habitats.
