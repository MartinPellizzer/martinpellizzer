Neoporteria heinrichiana

Neoporteria heinrichiana, commonly known as the Heiligenstadt bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodland margins and along stream banks. The plant features showy, bell-shaped blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered in a fine pubescence, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Neoporteria heinrichiana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
