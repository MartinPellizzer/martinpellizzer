Nycticalanthus speciosus

Nycticalanthus speciosus, commonly known as the night-blooming moonflower, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night and close by morning. The plant belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is known for its rapid blooming cycle. Its leaves are typically broad and heart-shaped, and it thrives in warm, humid climates.

The flowers are pollinated primarily by nocturnal insects such as moths.
