Nesogordonia bernieri var. acuminata

Nesogordonia bernieri var. acuminata, commonly known as the African tulip tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences that resemble large, colorful tulips. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense, terminal clusters. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a smooth, glossy surface.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
