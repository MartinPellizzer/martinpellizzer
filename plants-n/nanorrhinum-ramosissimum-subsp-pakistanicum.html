Nanorrhinum ramosissimum subsp. pakistanicum

Nanorrhinum ramosissimum subsp.

pakistanicum, commonly known as the Pakistan thornapple, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Pakistan. It is a shrub characterized by its branched stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant produces small, spherical fruits that contain seeds covered in a sticky, resinous coating. It is adapted to survive in dry conditions with minimal water requirements.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies of desert flora.
