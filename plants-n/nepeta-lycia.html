Nepeta lycia

Nepeta lycia, commonly known as Turkish catnip, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Turkey and the Caucasus.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, clustered blue or purple flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Nepeta lycia is also valued for its aromatic foliage and historical use in traditional medicine.
