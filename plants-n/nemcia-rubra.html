Nemcia rubra

Nemcia rubra, commonly known as the redstem nemcia, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family, which includes coffee and gardenias, and is characterized by its small, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an erect shrub with opposite, elliptical leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it produces small, dry fruits containing several seeds.

Nemcia rubra is often found in moist, shaded areas and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
