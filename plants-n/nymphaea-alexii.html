Nymphaea alexii

Nymphaea alexii, commonly known as the African water lily, is a perennial aquatic plant native to sub-Saharan Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that float on the water's surface, supported by long, spongy petioles. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white, with a prominent yellow center. Nymphaea alexii thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats, such as ponds and lakes.

Its botanical features make it an important component of aquatic ecosystems and a popular ornamental plant.
