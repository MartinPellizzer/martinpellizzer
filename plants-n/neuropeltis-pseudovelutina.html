Neuropeltis pseudovelutina

Neuropeltis pseudovelutina, commonly known as the velvetleaf orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, velvety green leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid forest environments. Its flowers are usually yellow or greenish-yellow with a distinctive, hood-like structure.

Neuropeltis pseudovelutina is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its role in traditional medicine.
