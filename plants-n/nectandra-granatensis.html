Nectandra granatensis

Nectandra granatensis, commonly known as the granadilla tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by distinctive red, fleshy fruits. The fruit's outer layer, known as the aril, is edible and has a sweet, citrus-like flavor.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of natural fragrance in perfumery.
