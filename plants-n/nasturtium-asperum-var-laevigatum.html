Nasturtium asperum var. laevigatum

Nasturtium asperum var. laevigatum, commonly known as the smooth nasturtium, is a variant of the common nasturtium characterized by its smooth, less hairy stems and leaves.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae and is native to regions in Europe and Asia. The plant produces bright orange or yellow flowers with a distinctive spurred petal, and its leaves are edible, often used in salads or as a garnish. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for both ornamental and culinary gardens.

This variety is also valued for its ability to attract beneficial insects like ladybugs and bees.
