Najas major var. paucidentata

Najas major var. paucidentata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved watermilfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It belongs to the family Najadaceae and is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves that are often arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically borne in clusters near the water's surface. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Its rapid growth can also lead to dense vegetation, affecting water flow and light penetration in lakes and ponds.
