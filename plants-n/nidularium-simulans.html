Nidularium simulans

Nidularium simulans, commonly known as the false spider plant, is a member of the Bromeliaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant features rosettes of narrow, grass-like leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in length. Its flowers are small, clustered in inflorescences, and are typically greenish-white in color.

Nidularium simulans is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
