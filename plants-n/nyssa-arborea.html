Nyssa arborea

Nyssa arborea, commonly known as the sweet gum or water ash, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Nyssaceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple leaves that turn vibrant colors in the fall. The tree produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, followed by spherical, spiny fruit that contains a single seed.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological and ornamental purposes.
