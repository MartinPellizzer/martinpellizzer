Nelumbium caspium

Nelumbium caspium, commonly known as the Caspian lotus, is a water plant native to the Caspian Sea region.

It features large, showy white flowers with a pinkish base, which bloom in summer. The plant has broad, heart-shaped leaves that float on the water's surface. Its roots are submerged, anchoring it in shallow waters.

Nelumbium caspium is known for its ecological importance in wetland ecosystems.
