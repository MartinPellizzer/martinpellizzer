Narcissus × hinojarensis

Narcissus × hinojarensis, commonly known as the Hinojares daffodil, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to white. Native to Spain, this species is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters, with linear, grass-like leaves.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a parent in the development of many modern daffodil cultivars.
