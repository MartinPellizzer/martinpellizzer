Nervilia oxyglossa

Nervilia oxyglossa, commonly known as the golden thread orchid, is a small, terrestrial orchid native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features a slender, creeping rhizome with a cluster of narrow, grass-like leaves. Its most striking feature is the delicate, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring.

Nervilia oxyglossa is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
