Neochilenia pseudoreichei

Neochilenia pseudoreichei, commonly known as the Chilean tree fern, is a member of the Cyatheaceae family and is native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

The plant has a robust rhizome system that supports its tall stature and allows it to thrive in moist, shaded environments. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds, contributing to its reproductive strategy.

Neochilenia pseudoreichei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
