Nymphaea lotus var. pubescens

Nymphaea lotus var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy water lily, is a variant of the sacred lotus native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves covered in fine, soft hairs, which give it its common name. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes. Its flowers are typically white or pink, opening in the early morning and closing by midday.

The hairy water lily is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in various cultures.
