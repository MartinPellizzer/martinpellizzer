Nigritella nigra subsp. carpatica

Nigritella nigra subsp.

carpatica, commonly known as the Carpathian blackberry, is a shrub native to the Carpathian Mountains. It is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves and clusters of small, black berries that ripen in late summer.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forested areas. Its flowers are white and fragrant, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The berries are edible and have a tart flavor, making them popular in local cuisines.
