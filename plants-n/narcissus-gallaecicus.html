Narcissus gallaecicus

Narcissus gallaecicus, commonly known as the Galician daffodil, is a species native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is a perennial bulbous plant that typically grows in rocky or coastal areas. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces a single, showy yellow flower with a distinctive cup-like shape. It blooms in early spring, often before the leaves are fully developed.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
