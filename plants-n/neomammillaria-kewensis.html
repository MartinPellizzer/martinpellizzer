Neomammillaria kewensis

Neomammillaria kewensis, commonly known as the Kew's pincushion cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are covered in white, woolly spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Neomammillaria kewensis is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low maintenance and ornamental appearance.
