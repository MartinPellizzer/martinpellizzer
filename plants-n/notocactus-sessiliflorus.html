Notocactus sessiliflorus

Notocactus sessiliflorus, commonly known as the sessile-flower cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is a small, spineless cactus with a globular shape and a thick, fleshy stem that stores water. The plant produces clusters of small, white, tubular flowers that bloom during the cooler months. Its leaves are modified into spines, and it has a shallow root system adapted to sandy soils.

Notocactus sessiliflorus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in low-water conditions.
