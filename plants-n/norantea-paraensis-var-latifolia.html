Norantea paraensis var. latifolia

Norantea paraensis var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed norantea, is a rare tropical plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom during the rainy season. Its roots are known to contain compounds with potential medicinal properties.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and is protected in several conservation programs.
