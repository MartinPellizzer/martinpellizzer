Neuropeltis velutina var. longiapiculata

Neuropeltis velutina var. longiapiculata, commonly known as the velvetleaf orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its velvety, dark green foliage and clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid forest environments. Its flowers bloom in summer and are often visited by pollinators such as bees and moths.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
