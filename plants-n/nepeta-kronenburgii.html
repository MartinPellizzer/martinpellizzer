Nepeta kronenburgii

Nepeta kronenburgii, commonly known as the Alpine catmint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features aromatic, gray-green leaves and produces clusters of small, tubular blue or violet flowers. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in rocky or alpine environments. It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, known for its aromatic properties and medicinal uses.

Nepeta kronenburgii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
