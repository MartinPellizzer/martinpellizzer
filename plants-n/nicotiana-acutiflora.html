Nicotiana acutiflora

Nicotiana acutiflora, commonly known as the sharpflower tobacco, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is known for its aromatic leaves and flowers. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often growing in arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
