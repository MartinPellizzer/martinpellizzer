Nasturtium fremontii

Nasturtium fremontii, commonly known as the Fremont's nasturtium, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae and is characterized by its vibrant orange or red flowers and heart-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in mountainous regions. Its flowers are edible and have a peppery taste, similar to watercress, making it a popular culinary plant.

Nasturtium fremontii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
