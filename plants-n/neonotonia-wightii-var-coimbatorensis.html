Neonotonia wightii var. coimbatorensis

Neonotonia wightii var. coimbatorensis, commonly known as the Coimbatore flame of the forest, is a deciduous tree native to southern India.

It is characterized by its striking orange to red flowers that appear in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth texture. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
