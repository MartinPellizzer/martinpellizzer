Navia albiflora

Navia albiflora, commonly known as the whiteflower navia, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine, hairy foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers during the spring and early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly aromatic scent when crushed.

Navia albiflora is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and is adapted to well-drained, rocky soils.
