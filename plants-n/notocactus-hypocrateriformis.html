Notocactus hypocrateriformis

Notocactus hypocrateriformis, commonly known as the "Crown of Thorns," is a cactus native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that are covered in sharp, spiny tubercles. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers that bloom in the summer, adding a striking visual contrast to its rugged appearance. Its succulent nature allows it to store water efficiently, making it drought-tolerant.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique shape and ability to thrive in arid conditions.
