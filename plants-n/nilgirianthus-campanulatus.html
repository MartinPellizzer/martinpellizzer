Nilgirianthus campanulatus

Nilgirianthus campanulatus, commonly known as the Nilgiri bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas of forests and has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It is a perennial herb that can reach heights of up to 60 centimeters.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
