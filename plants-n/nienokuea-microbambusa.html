Nienokuea microbambusa

Nienokuea microbambusa, commonly known as the dwarf bamboo, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Indonesia and the Philippines. This plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and slender, often clustered stems. The leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a light green color.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant due to its low maintenance requirements.
