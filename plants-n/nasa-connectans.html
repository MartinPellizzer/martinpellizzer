Nasa connectans

Nasa connectans, commonly known as the "Mexican moonflower," is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that open at night. The plant produces small, round berries that turn red when mature, containing seeds used for propagation. Nasa connectans thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its unique nocturnal blooming habit makes it a fascinating subject for botanical study.
