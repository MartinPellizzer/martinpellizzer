Nolana peruviana

Nolana peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian woolflower, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its soft, woolly leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, adapting to harsh conditions with its succulent-like stems. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological role in its native habitat.

Nolana peruviana is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique appearance and resilience.
