Najas japonica

Najas japonica, commonly known as Japanese watermilfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its whorled arrangement of narrow, ribbon-like leaves that are often bright green. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically borne in clusters near the water surface. It thrives in freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving rivers.

Najas japonica plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
