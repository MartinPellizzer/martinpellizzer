Nepeta koejeana

Nepeta koejeana, commonly known as catmint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender stems and clusters of small, fragrant blue or purple flowers. The plant produces small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a fuzzy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Nepeta koejeana is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
