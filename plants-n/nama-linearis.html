Nama linearis

Nama linearis, commonly known as the narrowleaf lichen, is a foliose lichen that thrives in cool, moist environments.

It has a distinctive, flat, leaf-like structure with a leathery texture and a dark green to blackish upper surface. The plant forms a symbiotic relationship with algae or cyanobacteria, which provide nutrients through photosynthesis. Nama linearis is often found on tree trunks, rocks, and soil in temperate regions.

Its slow growth and ability to survive in harsh conditions make it an important indicator of environmental health.
