Neolobivia × nakaii var. albocinnabarina

Neolobivia × nakaii var. albocinnabarina, commonly known as the white cinnabar lily, is a rare and striking hybrid plant native to the high Andes of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and large, showy flowers that display a unique combination of white and red hues. The plant's foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. It thrives in cool, high-altitude environments with well-drained soil and ample sunlight.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is highly sought after by botanists and horticulturists alike.
