Nicotiana gascoynica

Nicotiana gascoynica, commonly known as the Gascoyne tobacco, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

The flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
