Neosinocalamus beecheyanus var. pubescens

Neosinocalamus beecheyanus var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy bamboo, is a variant of the Neosinocalamus beecheyanus species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and slender, erect culms that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is closely related to other bamboo species. Its leaves are typically narrow and have a distinctive pubescent surface, contributing to its common name.

This variant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its aesthetic appeal.
