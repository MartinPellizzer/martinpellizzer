Neotorularia sulphurea

Neotorularia sulphurea, commonly known as the sulfur-yellow lichen, is a unique foliose lichen that thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow to orange thallus, which is rich in sulfur compounds, giving it its distinctive color. The lichen forms a dense, crust-like growth on rocks, tree trunks, and soil surfaces, often in temperate regions. Its structure consists of a symbiotic association between a green alga and a fungus, which allows it to efficiently absorb nutrients and water.

Neotorularia sulphurea plays a vital role in ecological systems by contributing to soil formation and providing habitat for microorganisms.
