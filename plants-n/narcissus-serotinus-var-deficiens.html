Narcissus serotinus var. deficiens

Narcissus serotinus var. deficiens, commonly known as the late flowering daffodil, is a variant of the genus Narcissus native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its late blooming period, typically occurring in late spring to early summer. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces single, trumpet-shaped flowers with a yellow corona. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
