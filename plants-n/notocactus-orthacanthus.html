Notocactus orthacanthus

Notocactus orthacanthus, commonly known as the golden crown cactus, is a species of cactus native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are covered in dense, yellowish spines. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in the early morning, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
