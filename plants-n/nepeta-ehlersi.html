Nepeta ehlersi

Nepeta ehlersi, commonly known as catmint, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features aromatic, gray-green leaves and produces spikes of blue to purple flowers. The plant is part of the mint family (Lamiaceae), which is characterized by square stems and fragrant foliage. Nepeta ehlersi is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil.
