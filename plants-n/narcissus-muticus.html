Narcissus muticus

Narcissus muticus, commonly known as the silent daffodil, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers with a white throat. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, with strap-like leaves that are dark green in color. Its flowers bloom in early spring, often before the leaves fully emerge.

The plant is noted for its fragrant blossoms and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens.
