Nonatelia mucronulata

Nonatelia mucronulata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mistletoe, is a parasitic plant that primarily grows on the branches of eucalyptus trees in Australia.

It has small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically less than 1 centimeter in length, and it produces small, white or pinkish flowers. The plant relies on its host for nutrients and water, and its seeds are dispersed by birds that eat its fleshy, red berries.

Nonatelia mucronulata plays a role in the ecosystem by providing food for native wildlife and contributing to the biodiversity of forested areas.
