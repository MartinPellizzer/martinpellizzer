Notocactus arechavaletae var. nanus

Notocactus arechavaletae var. nanus, commonly known as the dwarf arechavaleta cactus, is a unique species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and thick, ribbed stems that store water efficiently. The plant produces striking white or pale yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a decorative element to its appearance. Its slow growth rate makes it an ideal choice for rock gardens or as a potted plant.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and somewhat challenging species to cultivate.
