Neomammillaria verhaertiana

Neomammillaria verhaertiana, commonly known as the Verhaert's pincushion cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and a dense covering of white, woolly spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and slow growth, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
