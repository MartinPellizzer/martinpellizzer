Nephrodium appendiculatum

Nephrodium appendiculatum, commonly known as the appendiculate nephroid, is a rare and unique fern species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, kidney-shaped fronds that are often adorned with small appendages along the margins. The plant exhibits a delicate, herbaceous growth habit and prefers shaded, moist environments in the understory of tropical forests. Its reproductive structures, including sporangia, are typically found on the underside of the fronds, contributing to its reproductive strategy.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Nephrodium appendiculatum is considered a species of conservation concern.
