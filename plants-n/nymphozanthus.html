Nymphozanthus

Nymphozanthus, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical plant native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, pendulous yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has simple, opposite leaves and produces small, woody pods that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Nymphozanthus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can be grown as a shade tree or in gardens.
