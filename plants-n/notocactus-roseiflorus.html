Notocactus roseiflorus

Notocactus roseiflorus, commonly known as the rose-flowered cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stem and clusters of vibrant pink or red flowers that bloom during the dry season. The plant has spines that provide protection against herbivores and help reduce water loss through transpiration. Its root system is adapted to absorb moisture from the soil efficiently during rare rainfall events.

Notocactus roseiflorus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in harsh environments.
