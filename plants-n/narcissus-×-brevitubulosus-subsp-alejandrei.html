Narcissus × brevitubulosus subsp. alejandrei

Narcissus × brevitubulosus subsp.

alejandrei, commonly known as the Alejandri hybrid daffodil, is a subspecies of the daffodil genus characterized by its distinctive botanical features. It typically exhibits narrow, strap-like leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers with a bright yellow corona.

The plant is native to southern Europe and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun conditions. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.

This subspecies is relatively hardy and can be grown in USDA hardiness zones 5 through 8.
