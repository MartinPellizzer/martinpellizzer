Nepeta yazdiana

Nepeta yazdiana, commonly known as Persian catnip, is a perennial herb native to Iran.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants. The plant features square stems, opposite leaves, and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to purple. It is valued for its aromatic foliage and is often used in traditional medicine.

Nepeta yazdiana thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.
