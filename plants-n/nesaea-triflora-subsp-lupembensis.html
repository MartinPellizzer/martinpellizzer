Nesaea triflora subsp. lupembensis

Nesaea triflora subsp.

lupembensis, commonly known as the East African violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the highlands of East Africa. It features a low-growing rosette of fleshy, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine pubescence.

The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, violet-blue flowers during the cooler months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.
