Nepeta pogonosperma

Nepeta pogonosperma, commonly known as the mountain catmint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant produces small, tubular blue to purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically hairy and have a mint-like scent, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.

Nepeta pogonosperma is often used in traditional medicine for its purported calming and digestive properties.
