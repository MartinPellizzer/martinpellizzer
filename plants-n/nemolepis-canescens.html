Nemolepis canescens

Nemolepis canescens, commonly known as the silverleaf lichen, is a foliose lichen that thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It is characterized by its pale green to silver-gray thallus, which is covered in fine, powdery structures that give it its distinctive appearance. The lichen forms a symbiotic relationship with algae and cyanobacteria, which provide nutrients through photosynthesis. It is often found on decaying wood and stone in temperate regions.

Due to its slow growth and specific habitat requirements, it is considered an indicator species for undisturbed ecosystems.
