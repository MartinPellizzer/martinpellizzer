Nicotiana murchisonica

Nicotiana murchisonica, commonly known as Murchison tobacco plant, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family.

It is native to the arid regions of Central America and is characterized by its erect, herbaceous growth habit. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically violet or blue in color. It has hairy stems and leaves, and its flowers are pollinated primarily by insects.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in the cultivation of tobacco.
