Nicotiana faucicola

Nicotiana faucicola, commonly known as the water tobacco, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in wetland environments. The plant is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of blue to purple flowers. It is often found growing near water sources such as rivers and ponds.

Due to its adaptability to moist conditions, it plays a role in wetland ecosystems and can be cultivated for ornamental purposes.
