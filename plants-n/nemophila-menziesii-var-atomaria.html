Nemophila menziesii var. atomaria

Nemophila menziesii var. atomaria, commonly known as the dwarf blue-eyed Mary, is a low-growing annual herb native to the western United States.

It features small, delicate flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in spring. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are lance-shaped and often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and wildflower displays.

Its botanical characteristics make it well-suited for coastal and Mediterranean climates.
