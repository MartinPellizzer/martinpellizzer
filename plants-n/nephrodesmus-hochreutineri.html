Nephrodesmus hochreutineri

Nephrodesmus hochreutineri, commonly known as the Hochreutiner's nephrolepis, is a unique fern species characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology.

It features a creeping rhizome that produces fiddlehead-like fronds, which unfurl into lanceolate blades with a serrated margin. The plant exhibits a dichotomous branching pattern, contributing to its intricate appearance. Its leaves are typically dark green and glossy, with a leathery texture that helps it thrive in shaded environments.

Nephrodesmus hochreutineri is native to specific regions in South America and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
