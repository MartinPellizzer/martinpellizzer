Nepeta crinita

Nepeta crinita, commonly known as catnip, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite leaves that are often grayish-green in color. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to purple, depending on the variety. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and herbal collections.

Nepeta crinita is known for its aromatic properties and its ability to attract beneficial insects like bees and butterflies.
