Nesogenes prostrata

Nesogenes prostrata, commonly known as the prostrate nesogenes, is a low-growing perennial plant native to specific regions of South America.

It features small, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, allowing it to efficiently capture moisture from the environment. The plant produces delicate, tubular flowers that are typically pale green to white in color, which attract specific pollinators. It thrives in moist, shaded habitats and is often found in areas with high humidity and rich, organic soil.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological niche, it is of interest to botanists studying plant adaptation in tropical environments.
