Nepeta leptantha

Nepeta leptantha, commonly known as the slender catmint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of pale blue to lavender flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, preferring well-drained soil and full sun. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Nepeta leptantha is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and mild medicinal properties.
