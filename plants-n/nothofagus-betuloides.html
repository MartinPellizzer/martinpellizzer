Nothofagus betuloides

Nothofagus betuloides, commonly known as the silver beech, is a species of evergreen tree native to southern South America.

It belongs to the Nothofagaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, silver-gray bark and broad, ovate leaves. The plant is typically found in temperate rainforests, where it thrives in moist, cool climates. It produces small, clustered flowers and is an important component of the ecosystem in its native habitat.

Nothofagus betuloides is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological significance.
