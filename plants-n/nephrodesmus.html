Nephrodesmus

Nephrodesmus, commonly known as the kidney fern, is a member of the Davalliaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, kidney-shaped fronds.

The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in humid, shaded environments. Its fronds are bi-lobed and often have a velvety texture, with a deep green coloration. Nephrodesmus reproduces through spores, which are released from specialized structures called sori located on the undersides of the fronds.

It is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various soil types.
