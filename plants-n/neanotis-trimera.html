Neanotis trimera

Neanotis trimera, commonly known as the small white geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, five-petaled white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in sunny environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and have a slightly hairy texture.

Neanotis trimera is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
