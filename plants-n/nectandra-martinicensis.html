Nectandra martinicensis

Nectandra martinicensis, commonly known as the Martinique laurier or West Indian laurier, is a small evergreen tree native to the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurel and bay trees, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, white, clustered flowers that are followed by oval, woody fruits containing aromatic seeds.

Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice, while the tree is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in tropical forests.
