Nolana balsamiflua

Nolana balsamiflua, commonly known as the velvetleaf nolana, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the family Solanaceae, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to purple, often with a slight fragrance. Its fruit is a dry capsule that splits open to release tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Nolana balsamiflua is typically found in coastal and semi-arid regions, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun.
