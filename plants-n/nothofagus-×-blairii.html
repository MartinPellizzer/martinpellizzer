Nothofagus × blairii

Nothofagus × blairii, commonly known as the Blair's Nothofagus, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Nothofagus truncata and Nothofagus antarctica.

It is native to New Zealand and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and distinctive cone structures. The plant typically grows in cool, moist environments, often in temperate rainforests. Its leaves are usually lanceolate and have a leathery texture, while its cones are small and clustered.

Nothofagus × blairii plays a significant role in the ecological dynamics of its native habitat.
