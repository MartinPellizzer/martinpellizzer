Neolitsea andamanica

Neolitsea andamanica, commonly known as the Andaman laurel, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Andaman and Nicobar Islands.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by small, round berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland and hill forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
