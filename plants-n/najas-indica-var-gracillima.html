Najas indica var. gracillima

Najas indica var. gracillima, commonly known as the slender watermilfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, thread-like leaves that are typically arranged in whorls along its stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged, and it thrives in shallow, slow-moving waters. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Due to its rapid growth, it can become invasive in some regions if not managed properly.
