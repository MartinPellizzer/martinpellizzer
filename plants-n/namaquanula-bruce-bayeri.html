Namaquanula bruce-bayeri

Namaquanula bruce-bayeri, commonly known as the Namibian ghost crab, is a unique crustacean found in the hyperarid regions of Namibia.

It is adapted to extreme desert conditions, with specialized exoskeleton features that reduce water loss. The species exhibits a cryptic coloration that helps it blend into its sandy environment. It is primarily nocturnal, emerging at night to forage for food.

Due to its limited habitat and sensitivity to environmental changes, it is considered a vulnerable species.
