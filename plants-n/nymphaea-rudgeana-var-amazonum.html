Nymphaea rudgeana var. amazonum

Nymphaea rudgeana var. amazonum, commonly known as the Amazon water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad, heart-shaped leaves that float on the water's surface, providing a habitat for various aquatic organisms. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom above the water, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its roots are submerged, anchoring it in the muddy substrate of slow-moving water bodies.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological role in maintaining water quality and supporting aquatic biodiversity.
