Nervilia odorata

Nervilia odorata, commonly known as the fragrant orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its flowers are usually pink or white with a distinctive, sweet scent.

Nervilia odorata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
