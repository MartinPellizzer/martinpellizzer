Neolloydia subterranea

Neolloydia subterranea, commonly known as the underground lloydia, is a rare and elusive plant species native to specific regions of China.

It is characterized by its subterranean growth habit, with most of its structure hidden beneath the soil surface. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like. Due to its rarity and limited distribution, it is considered a botanically significant species with limited ecological data.
