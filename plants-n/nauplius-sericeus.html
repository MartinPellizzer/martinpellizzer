Nauplius sericeus

Nauplius sericeus, commonly known as the silky nauplius, is a species of flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, silvery-green foliage and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in a fine, silky hair that gives it its common name.

Nauplius sericeus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
