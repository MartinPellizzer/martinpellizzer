Nervilia pilosa

Nervilia pilosa, commonly known as the hairy nerve-plant, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky areas. Its leaves are elongated and covered in fine hairs, which contribute to its common name.

Nervilia pilosa is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, where it thrives in humid conditions.
