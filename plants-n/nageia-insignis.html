Nageia insignis

Nageia insignis, commonly known as the mountain cypress, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, columnar growth habit and scale-like leaves that are arranged in opposite pairs. The tree produces small, cone-like structures that contain both male and female reproductive organs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Nageia insignis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
