Nolina loderi

Nolina loderi, commonly known as the Loder's nolina, is a species of flowering plant in the Asparagaceae family.

It is native to Western Australia and is characterized by its rosette of thick, fleshy, gray-green leaves. The plant produces small, white, star-shaped flowers on slender stems during the warmer months. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its leaves and stems.

Nolina loderi is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and low maintenance requirements.
