Neoporteria bicolor

Neoporteria bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive two-toned flowers, which range from white to pink or purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating, while its flowers are clustered in racemes.

Neoporteria bicolor is noted for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators like bees and butterflies.
