Navia luzuloides

Navia luzuloides, commonly known as the blue-eyed grass, is a member of the Poaceae family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wet meadows and stream banks. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in early spring. Navia luzuloides is characterized by its compact growth habit and ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
