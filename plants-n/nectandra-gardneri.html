Nectandra gardneri

Nectandra gardneri, commonly known as the Honduras rosewood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark-colored wood that is highly valued in the furniture and musical instrument industries. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers followed by dark, spherical berries. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a distinctive aromatic scent.

Nectandra gardneri thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland tropical forests.
