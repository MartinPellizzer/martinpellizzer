Nemochloa foliosa

Nemochloa foliosa, commonly known as the water clover, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant thrives in wet environments, often found in marshes and along waterways. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread rapidly, forming dense mats.

Botanically, it belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses and related plants.
