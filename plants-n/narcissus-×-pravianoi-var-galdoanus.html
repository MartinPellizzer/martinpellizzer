Narcissus × pravianoi var. galdoanus

Narcissus × pravianoi var. galdoanus, commonly known as the Galdoanus daffodil, is a hybrid plant that exhibits characteristics of both its parent species.

It is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family and is native to the Iberian Peninsula. The plant features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange, typically blooming in early spring. Its foliage consists of strap-like leaves that are often evergreen, contributing to its ornamental value.

The variety is prized for its hardiness and ability to thrive in well-drained soil conditions.
