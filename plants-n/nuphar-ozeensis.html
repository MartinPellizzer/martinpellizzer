Nuphar ozeensis

Nuphar ozeensis, commonly known as the Ozeen water lily, is a aquatic plant native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves that form a dense canopy on the water surface. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers that bloom in summer, typically in clusters. Its roots are rhizomatous, allowing it to spread and colonize shallow water bodies.

Nuphar ozeensis plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various water organisms.
