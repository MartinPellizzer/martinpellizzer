Neomolina thesioides

Neomolina thesioides, commonly known as the golden rain tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that appear in clusters, resembling small raindrops. The plant has pinnately compound leaves and produces small, woody pods that contain seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Neomolina thesioides is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
