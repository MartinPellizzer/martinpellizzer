Narcissus × licabrensis

Narcissus × licabrensis, commonly known as the Corsican daffodil, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Narcissus poeticus and Narcissus bulbocodium.

It is native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Corsica, and is characterized by its distinctive, large, funnel-shaped flowers that range in color from white to yellow. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and produces narrow, grass-like leaves.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
