Nepeta sibthorpii subsp. malacotrichos

Nepeta sibthorpii subsp.

malacotrichos, commonly known as the Malacotrichos catmint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It features aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, pale blue to violet flowers.

The plant is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.

Botanically, it belongs to the Lamiaceae family, sharing traits with other members of the mint family.
