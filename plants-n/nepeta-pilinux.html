Nepeta pilinux

Nepeta pilinux, commonly known as catmint, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, fuzzy leaves and clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other mint-like species. Nepeta pilinux is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
