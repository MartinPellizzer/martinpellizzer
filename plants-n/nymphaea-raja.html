Nymphaea raja

Nymphaea raja, commonly known as the sacred water lily of India, is a species of water lily native to the wetlands of South and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking white petals and vibrant yellow stamens, which make it a visually appealing aquatic plant. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in ponds, lakes, and marshes. Its leaves are broad and floating, providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.

Nymphaea raja is also valued for its cultural and medicinal significance in traditional practices.
