Nassella bonariensis

Nassella bonariensis, commonly known as slender needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its fine, needle-like leaves and produces fluffy, seed-like structures that are often mistaken for true seeds. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed areas and thrives in well-drained soils. It is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its delicate appearance and drought tolerance.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and restoration projects.
