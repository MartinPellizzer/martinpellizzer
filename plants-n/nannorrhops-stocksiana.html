Nannorrhops stocksiana

Nannorrhops stocksiana, commonly known as the Stocks date palm, is a hardy, drought-tolerant plant native to the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of long, feathery fronds that give it a distinctive appearance. This species is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a valuable plant for landscaping in dry regions. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that eventually develop into edible dates, though it is less commonly cultivated for fruit than other date palms.

Its resilience and ornamental value make it a popular choice for both ecological and aesthetic purposes.
