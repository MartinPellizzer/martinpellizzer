Noccaea occitanica

Noccaea occitanica, commonly known as the Pyrenean watercress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Iberian Peninsula and southern France.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant is characterized by its rosette of finely divided, fern-like leaves and small white or pale yellow flowers. It thrives in moist, rocky habitats and is often found near streams and in alpine meadows.

Noccaea occitanica is valued for its peppery flavor and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
