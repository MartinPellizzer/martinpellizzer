Nonea caspica subsp. zygomorpha

Nonea caspica subsp.

zygomorpha, commonly known as the Caspian Nonea, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia. It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in steppe and semi-desert environments.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
