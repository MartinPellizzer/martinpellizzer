Narcissus pallidulus

Narcissus pallidulus, commonly known as the pale daffodil, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, elongated leaves and a single, narrow trumpet-shaped bloom. The flowers typically feature a pale yellow corona and a white or pale yellow perianth. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its early spring blooms.
