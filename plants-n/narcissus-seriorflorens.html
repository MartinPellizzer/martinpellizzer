Narcissus seriorflorens

Narcissus seriorflorens, commonly known as the early yellow daffodil, is a species native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its early blooming habit, typically flowering in late winter to early spring. The plant features a single, trumpet-shaped flower with a bright yellow corona and a narrow, grass-like foliage. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its hardiness and ornamental value, it is a popular choice for gardeners seeking early spring color.
