Nepeta shahmirzadensis

Nepeta shahmirzadensis, commonly known as Shahmirzad's catmint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, arranged in spikes. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

Its foliage is often fragrant and can attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
