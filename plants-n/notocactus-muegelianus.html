Notocactus muegelianus

Notocactus muegelianus, commonly known as the Muegel's cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stem and spiny, ribbed structure, which helps it conserve water in harsh environments. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking contrast to its rugged appearance. Its growth habit is typically solitary, though it can occasionally form small clusters.

Notocactus muegelianus is well-suited for cultivation in dry climates and requires minimal watering to thrive.
