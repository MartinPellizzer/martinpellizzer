Nabiasodendron obtusum

Nabiasodendron obtusum, commonly known as the broadleaf tree, is a species of flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale pink in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
