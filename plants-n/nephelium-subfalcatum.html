Nephelium subfalcatum

Nephelium subfalcatum, commonly known as the mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces a distinctive fruit with a bright red or purple rind and a segmented, white, juicy pulp. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a woody, branching stem.

The mangosteen is valued for its ornamental beauty and the nutritional properties of its fruit.
