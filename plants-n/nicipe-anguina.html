Nicipe anguina

Nicipe anguina, commonly known as the snake's tongue plant, is a unique species native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, snake-like leaves that give it its common name. The plant has a fibrous root system and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow in color. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Malvaceae and is known for its adaptability to various soil types.
