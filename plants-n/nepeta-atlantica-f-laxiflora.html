Nepeta atlantica f. laxiflora

Nepeta atlantica f. laxiflora, commonly known as the laxiflora catmint, is a flowering plant native to the mountains of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers arranged in whorls along the stems. Its foliage is typically gray-green and slightly fuzzy, contributing to its ornamental value.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive appearance and mild mint-like fragrance.
