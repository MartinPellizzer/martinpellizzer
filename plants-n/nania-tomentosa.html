Nania tomentosa

Nania tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly nania, is a flowering plant native to specific regions in South America.

It is characterized by its dense, white, woolly foliage that covers its stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or pale yellow in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
