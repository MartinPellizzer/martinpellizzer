Nectandra ramonensis

Nectandra ramonensis, commonly known as the Ramon tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and fragrant flowers. The tree produces small, clustered white flowers that are often followed by elliptical, woody fruits containing a single seed. It is valued for its timber, which is durable and resistant to decay, and for its use in traditional medicine.

The plant also plays an important ecological role in its native habitats by providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
