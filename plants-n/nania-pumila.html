Nania pumila

Nania pumila, commonly known as the dwarf nania, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, temperate climates. The plant features compact, upright stems with small, rounded leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. Its flowers are delicate, bell-shaped, and usually pale blue or white in color.

Nania pumila is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
