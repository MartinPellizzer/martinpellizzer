Najas marina subsp. commersonii

Najas marina subsp.

commersonii, commonly known as the narrow-leaved watermilfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in South America. It is characterized by its slender, ribbon-like leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged, and it reproduces both sexually and vegetatively. It thrives in shallow, slow-moving waters with moderate to high light penetration.

This species is important for aquatic ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
