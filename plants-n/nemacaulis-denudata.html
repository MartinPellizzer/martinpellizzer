Nemacaulis denudata

Nemacaulis denudata, commonly known as the naked stem sedum, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems that store water, allowing it to survive in dry environments. The plant typically has small, scale-like leaves that are often reduced or absent, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces clusters of yellow or orange flowers during the summer months.

Nemacaulis denudata is well adapted to rocky soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to xeriscaped gardens.
