Nasa argemonoides

Nasa argemonoides, commonly known as the golden trumpet vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that resemble trumpets, which bloom in clusters along the stems. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, fleshy fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Nasa argemonoides is also used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
