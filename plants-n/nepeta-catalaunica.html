Nepeta catalaunica

Nepeta catalaunica, commonly known as catmint, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite leaves that are often grayish-green in color. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple, arranged in whorled clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Nepeta catalaunica is also valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
