Nemosenecio nikoensis

Nemosenecio nikoensis, commonly known as the Niko's gentian, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of East Africa.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are usually basal, with a silver-gray or woolly texture, and it produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
