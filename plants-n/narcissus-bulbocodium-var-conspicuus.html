Narcissus bulbocodium var. conspicuus

Narcissus bulbocodium var. conspicuus, commonly known as the showy narcissus, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers with a distinctive, recurved petal shape. The plant grows from a bulb and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge in early spring.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
