Nasturtium subulatum

Nasturtium subulatum, commonly known as the needle-leaved nasturtium, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, needle-like leaves and bright orange or yellow flowers. The plant is an annual or biennial herb that thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. Its flowers are often used in culinary applications, adding a peppery flavor to salads and dishes.

The plant also attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies due to its vibrant blooms.
