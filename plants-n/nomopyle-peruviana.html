Nomopyle peruviana

Nomopyle peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous terrain, preferring well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Nomopyle peruviana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
