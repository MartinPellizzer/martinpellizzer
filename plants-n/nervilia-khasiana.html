Nervilia khasiana

Nervilia khasiana, commonly known as the Khasi nerve plant, is a perennial herb native to the northeastern regions of India.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, grass-like foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and ecological significance in its native habitat.
