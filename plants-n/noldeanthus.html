Noldeanthus

Noldeanthus, commonly known as the flame tree, is a genus of flowering plants in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to parts of Africa, Asia, and the Americas, and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range from red to orange in color. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, with compound leaves and pods that contain seeds. Its flowers bloom in clusters, often in the summer months, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Noldeanthus species are also valued for their ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to soil fertility.
