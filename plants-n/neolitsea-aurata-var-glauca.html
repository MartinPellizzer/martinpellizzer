Neolitsea aurata var. glauca

Neolitsea aurata var. glauca, commonly known as the glaucous flame tree, is a variant of the flame tree species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow to orange-red flowers that bloom in summer, creating a spectacular display. The leaves are typically glossy and dark green, with a slightly waxy texture. This plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun exposure for optimal growth.
