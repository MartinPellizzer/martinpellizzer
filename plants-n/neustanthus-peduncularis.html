Neustanthus peduncularis

Neustanthus peduncularis, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and grows in dense clusters along the shoreline. It thrives in salty, well-drained soils and is often found in dune environments.

Neustanthus peduncularis plays an important role in stabilizing coastal ecosystems and providing habitat for small insects.
