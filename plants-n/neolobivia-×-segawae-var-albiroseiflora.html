Neolobivia × segawae var. albiroseiflora

Neolobivia × segawae var. albiroseiflora, commonly known as the white-veined lobivia, is a hybrid cactus native to the Atacama Desert of Chile.

It is characterized by its compact, rounded stems that are often covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hair. The plant produces striking, showy flowers that range in color from pink to magenta, often with a white or pale pink throat. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun exposure to flourish.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
