Narcissus tazetta subsp. lacticolor

Narcissus tazetta subsp.

lacticolor, commonly known as the Milky White daffodil, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its pale yellow to white flowers with a single, prominent central corona.

The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and produces clusters of 4–8 blooms per stem. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in well-drained soil in full sun.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its early spring blooms.
