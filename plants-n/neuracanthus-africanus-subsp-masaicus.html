Neuracanthus africanus subsp. masaicus

Neuracanthus africanus subsp.

masaicus, commonly known as the African flame flower, is a striking member of the Acanthaceae family. It is native to southern Africa, particularly South Africa, and thrives in warm, humid climates.

The plant is characterized by its vibrant red-orange bracts that surround small, tubular flowers, creating a dramatic visual display. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements make it a favored plant among horticulturists and garden enthusiasts.
