Narcissus aequilimbus

Narcissus aequilimbus, commonly known as the Dutch narcissus, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact, rounded foliage and a single, showy flower that typically blooms in early spring. The flower consists of six to eight white petals with a yellow or orange center, forming a cup-like shape. Native to the Netherlands and parts of western Europe, it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in garden settings.
