Norysca cordifolia

Norysca cordifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf fern, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are typically a deep green and have a leathery texture. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing in dense forests. Its rhizomes are fibrous and help anchor it to moist soil or rocky surfaces.

Norysca cordifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
