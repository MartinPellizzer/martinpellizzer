Nepenthes × ratcliffeana

Nepenthes × ratcliffeana, commonly known as the hybrid pitcher plant, is a tropical carnivorous plant that combines traits from its parent species, Nepenthes alata and Nepenthes rafflesiana.

It is characterized by its large, elongated pitcher-shaped traps that secrete a digestive fluid to trap and digest insects. The plant is native to the Philippines and thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its pitchers can grow up to 30 cm in length and are often adorned with red or orange coloration to attract prey.

Nepenthes × ratcliffeana is popular among carnivorous plant enthusiasts for its striking appearance and unique ecological adaptations.
