Narcissus longispathus subsp. yepesii

Narcissus longispathus subsp.

yepesii, commonly known as the Yepes narcissus, is a rare and ornamental bulbous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, white flowers with a yellow center.

The plant is characterized by its elongated, narrow petals and is adapted to grow in well-drained, sandy soils. It is often found in Mediterranean climates and is valued for its aesthetic appeal in gardens.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a species of conservation concern.
