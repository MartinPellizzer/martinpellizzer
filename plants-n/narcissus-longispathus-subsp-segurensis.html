Narcissus longispathus subsp. segurensis

Narcissus longispathus subsp.

segurensis, commonly known as the Segurensis daffodil, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers with a distinctive, elongated tepal arrangement.

The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge in early spring.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
