Neyraudia madagascariensis

Neyraudia madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar reed, is a perennial grass native to Madagascar.

It features slender, arching leaves that can grow up to 2 meters in length, giving it a graceful appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, typically during the rainy season. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found near water sources such as streams and ponds.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
