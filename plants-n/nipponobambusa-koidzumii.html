Nipponobambusa koidzumii

Nipponobambusa koidzumii, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo, is a species native to Japan and parts of eastern Asia.

It is a member of the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems and graceful appearance. This bamboo thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade. It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to cultivate.
