Nymphaea stellata f. caerulea

Nymphaea stellata f. caerulea, commonly known as the blue lotus, is a striking aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features showy, waxy blue or violet petals that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in ponds and lakes. Its leaves are broad and floating, providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.

The flower's unique coloration and nocturnal blooming pattern make it a popular choice for water gardens and botanical collections.
