Nardus gangitis

Nardus gangitis, commonly known as the Gangiti grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spikelets. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Nardus gangitis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
