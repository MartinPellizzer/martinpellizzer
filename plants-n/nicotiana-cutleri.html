Nicotiana cutleri

Nicotiana cutleri, commonly known as the Cutler's tobacco plant, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The flowers are typically blue or violet, with five petals and a prominent central spur, which is a distinguishing feature. It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is closely related to other tobacco species.

The plant is often found in arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.
