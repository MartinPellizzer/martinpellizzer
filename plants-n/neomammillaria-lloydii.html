Neomammillaria lloydii

Neomammillaria lloydii, commonly known as the Lloyd's mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, rounded stems that are often covered in white to pale blue spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are typically borne on short, erect pedicels. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Neomammillaria lloydii is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its ornamental appearance and low maintenance requirements.
