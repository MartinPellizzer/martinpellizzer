Navia graminifolia

Navia graminifolia, commonly known as the grass-like plant, is a flowering plant species belonging to the family Poaceae, which includes grasses.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, clustered inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate environments, often found in meadows or along streambanks. Navia graminifolia has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Its flowers are usually pale green to brown, and it blooms during the spring and early summer seasons.
