Nymphaea spectabilis

Nymphaea spectabilis, commonly known as the sacred water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a yellow center, and are fully submerged when in bloom. The plant has broad, floating leaves that provide habitat for various aquatic organisms. Its rhizomes grow beneath the water, anchoring the plant and supporting its growth.

Nymphaea spectabilis plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by contributing to water filtration and providing shelter for aquatic life.
