Nepeta Multibracteata Subsp. Acerosa

Nepeta multibracteata subsp. acerosa is a lesser-known member of the mint family known for its unique appearance and medicinal properties. This subspecies is native to specific regions in Asia and is often found in rocky or arid environments. Its foliage features silvery-gray leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers that attract pollinators. Despite its beauty, it remains relatively obscure in horticultural circles. Its potential uses in traditional medicine and as a ornamental plant warrant further exploration.
