Nelsonia campestris var. vestita

Nelsonia campestris var. vestita, commonly known as the silverleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of silver-gray, hairy leaves that help reduce water loss in arid conditions. The plant produces a single, upright stem that bears a cluster of yellow flowers at the top. After flowering, it develops a fluffy seed head that aids in wind dispersal.

It is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to full sun exposure.
