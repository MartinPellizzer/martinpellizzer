Nepeta duthiei

Nepeta duthiei, commonly known as the Persian catmint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their calming properties.

Nepeta duthiei is also popular in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
