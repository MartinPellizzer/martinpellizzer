Nectandra mirafloris

Nectandra mirafloris, commonly known as the floral nectandra, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical rainforests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, clustered white to pale yellow flowers that bloom throughout the wet season. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds.

Nectandra mirafloris is valued for its ornamental appeal and has limited commercial use in traditional medicine.
