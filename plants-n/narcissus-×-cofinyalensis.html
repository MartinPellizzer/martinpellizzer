Narcissus × cofinyalensis

Narcissus × cofinyalensis, commonly known as the hybrid daffodil, is a botanical hybrid resulting from the cross between Narcissus tazetta and Narcissus poeticus.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to white. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in temperate climates.
