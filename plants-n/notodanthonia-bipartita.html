Notodanthonia bipartita

Notodanthonia bipartita, commonly known as the two-part grass, is a species of grass native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect culms and narrow, linear leaves. The inflorescence consists of a single spikelet that is often divided into two distinct parts, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and heathlands.

This plant is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
