Nauclea verticillata

Nauclea verticillata, commonly known as the African rose or blue false indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and showy blue or violet flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both horticulture and ecological studies.
