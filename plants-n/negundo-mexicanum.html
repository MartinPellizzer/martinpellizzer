Negundo mexicanum

Negundo mexicanum, commonly known as Mexican ash, is a deciduous tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Ulmaceae family, which includes elms and ashes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The tree produces small, spherical fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in moist, well-drained areas.

Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and ability to provide shade in urban and rural landscapes.
