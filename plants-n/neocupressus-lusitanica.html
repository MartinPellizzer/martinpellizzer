Neocupressus lusitanica

Neocupressus lusitanica, commonly known as the Portuguese cypress, is a species of coniferous evergreen tree native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Cupressaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, conical shape and scale-like leaves. The plant produces small, spherical cones that contain seeds, typical of many conifer species. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is also cultivated in gardens and parks.
