Nicotiana rugosa

Nicotiana rugosa, commonly known as the roughleaf tobacco, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is related to the tobacco plant.

Nicotiana rugosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in some traditional herbal practices.
