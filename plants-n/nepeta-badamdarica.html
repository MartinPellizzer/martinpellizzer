Nepeta badamdarica

Nepeta badamdarica, commonly known as Persian catnip, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their calming properties.

Nepeta badamdarica is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
