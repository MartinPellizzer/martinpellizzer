Neuracanthus umbraticus

Neuracanthus umbraticus, commonly known as the shadowed bellflower, is a rare and striking plant native to the shaded understories of temperate forests.

It features elongated, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from deep violet to dark purple, often with intricate patterns resembling lace. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soil and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth. Its leaves are broad and leathery, with a velvety texture that helps retain moisture.

Neuracanthus umbraticus is noted for its unique ability to bloom in low-light conditions, making it a prized addition to specialized botanical gardens.
