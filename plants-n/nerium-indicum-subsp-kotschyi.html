Nerium indicum subsp. kotschyi

Nerium indicum subsp.

kotschyi, commonly known as the Persian oleander, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and red.

The plant has simple, opposite, and often hairy leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. It is a deciduous shrub that can grow up to several meters in height, with a woody stem and thick, fleshy leaves.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
