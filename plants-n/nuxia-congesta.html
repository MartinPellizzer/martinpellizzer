Nuxia congesta

Nuxia congesta, commonly known as the Cape honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant produces small, round berries that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Nuxia congesta is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
