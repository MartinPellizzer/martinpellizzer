Nothoscordum catharinense

Nothoscordum catharinense, commonly known as the Brazilian garlic plant, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive garlic-like scent. The plant features strap-like leaves and produces clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in warm climates.

Due to its ornamental value and unique aroma, it is sometimes grown in gardens for both aesthetic and culinary purposes.
