Nectaroscordum neapolitanum

Nectaroscordum neapolitanum, commonly known as the Naples garlic or Italian garlic, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its bulbous stem and clusters of fragrant, white flowers. The plant produces a strong garlic odor, which is released when its leaves are crushed. Its bulbs are edible and have a pungent flavor, often used in culinary applications.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental and edible gardening.
