Neomammillaria mollendorffiana

Neomammillaria mollendorffiana, commonly known as the Mexican milk barrel cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are often ribbed and covered in white to pale blue spines. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are typically borne on the upper part of the stem. It is a slow-growing cactus that thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and desert landscapes.
