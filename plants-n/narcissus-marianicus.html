Narcissus marianicus

Narcissus marianicus, commonly known as the Mariana daffodil, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to the coastal regions of southern California, where it thrives in sandy, well-drained soils. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and white, typically blooming in early spring. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming a basal rosette.

Narcissus marianicus is adapted to coastal climates and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.
