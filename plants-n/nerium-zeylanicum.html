Nerium zeylanicum

Nerium zeylanicum, commonly known as the oleander, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, red, and yellow. The plant has simple, lance-shaped leaves and produces woody, evergreen stems. It is well adapted to arid environments and can thrive in poor soil conditions.

Oleander is also known for its toxicity, as all parts of the plant contain toxic compounds.
