Narcissus rupicola f. pedunculatus

Narcissus rupicola f. pedunculatus, commonly known as the rock daffodil or rock narcissus, is a hybrid plant characterized by its compact growth habit and striking yellow flowers.

It is a naturally occurring form of Narcissus rupicola, which is native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils, thriving in sunny to partially shaded environments. Its flowers are usually single, with a short peduncle, and feature a prominent corona.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and its ability to bloom reliably in challenging garden conditions.
