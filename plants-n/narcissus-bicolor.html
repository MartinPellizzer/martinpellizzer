Narcissus bicolor

Narcissus bicolor, commonly known as the double daffodil, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that consist of two layers of tepals, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and produces bright yellow or white blooms in early spring. Native to the Iberian Peninsula, it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
