Nematanthus crassifolius subsp. crassifolius

Nematanthus crassifolius subsp.

crassifolius, commonly known as the thickleaf winged vine, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean, particularly Jamaica. It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves and showy, tubular flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow.

The plant is a trailing vine that thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as a decorative plant. Its flowers are pollinated by hummingbirds and bees, and it produces small, round berries that contain seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to container gardening.
