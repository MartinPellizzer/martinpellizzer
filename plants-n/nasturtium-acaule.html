Nasturtium acaule

Nasturtium acaule, commonly known as the slender nasturtium, is a herbaceous annual plant native to South America.

It features a distinctive, upright growth habit with slender, leafless stems that bear showy, spurred flowers in shades of yellow and orange. The plant produces small, edible, peppery-tasting leaves and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes as well as culinary use. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to biodiversity in gardens.

Nasturtium acaule thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a versatile addition to both wild and cultivated landscapes.
