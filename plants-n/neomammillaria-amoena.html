Neomammillaria amoena

Neomammillaria amoena, commonly known as the pink mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to the Sonoran Desert in North America.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are often covered in white or pinkish tubercles. The plant produces small, vibrant pink flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Neomammillaria amoena is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its compact size and ornamental appeal.
