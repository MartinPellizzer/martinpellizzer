Notophaena discolor

Notophaena discolor, commonly known as the red-backed grasshopper, is a species of short-horned grasshopper found in various parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive red or orange markings on its dorsal surface, which serve as a form of aposematic coloration. The plant it inhabits typically includes grasslands and open habitats with a variety of flowering plants. Notophaena discolor plays a role in seed dispersal and serves as prey for various predators.

Its life cycle involves incomplete metamorphosis, with nymphs resembling adults but lacking wings.
