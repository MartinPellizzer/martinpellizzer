Neottianthe monophylla

Neottianthe monophylla, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a striking member of the Araceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its large, white, petal-like bracts that resemble ghostly apparitions, giving it its common name. Its inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by a showy spathe.

Neottianthe monophylla is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is appreciated for its unique and ethereal appearance.
