Nepeta hispanica

Nepeta hispanica, commonly known as Spanish catmint, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and northern Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal remedies for their calming properties.

Nepeta hispanica thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy and ornamental addition to gardens.
