Nauclea oblongata

Nauclea oblongata, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its flowers are clustered in dense, showy inflorescences. The plant produces woody pods that contain several seeds, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Nauclea oblongata is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
