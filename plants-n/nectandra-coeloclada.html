Nectandra coeloclada

Nectandra coeloclada, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its aromatic, reddish-brown heartwood. The plant has simple, opposite, and ovate leaves with a smooth upper surface and a hairy underside. Its small, white flowers are clustered in umbels, and it produces small, round fruits containing a single seed.

Due to its valuable wood, it is often harvested for its high-quality timber, though it is also cultivated for ornamental purposes.
