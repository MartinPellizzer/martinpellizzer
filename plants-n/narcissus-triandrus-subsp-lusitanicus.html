Narcissus triandrus subsp. lusitanicus

Narcissus triandrus subsp.

lusitanicus, commonly known as the Iberian daffodil, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Europe. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to white.

The flowers typically bloom in early spring, and the plant thrives in well-drained soils in full sun. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens.

This subspecies is closely related to other members of the Narcissus genus, sharing similar botanical characteristics.
