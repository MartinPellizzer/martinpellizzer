Notocactus gibberulus

Notocactus gibberulus, commonly known as the woolly cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly covering of white to grayish hairs, which helps to reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant typically grows as a low, compact shrub with multiple stems that branch out from the base. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, appearing in clusters at the ends of the stems during the warmer months.

Notocactus gibberulus is well adapted to survive in extreme desert conditions, making it a resilient and distinctive member of the cactus family.
