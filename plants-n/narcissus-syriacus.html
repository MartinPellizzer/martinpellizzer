Narcissus syriacus

Narcissus syriacus, commonly known as the Persian daffodil, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to the eastern Mediterranean region and is characterized by its trumpet-shaped flowers that range from white to yellow. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and produces clusters of 4–8 blooms per stem. Its foliage consists of strap-like, grassy leaves that emerge from a bulbous base.

Narcissus syriacus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
