Neoregelia gavionensis

Neoregelia gavionensis, commonly known as the Gavion's bromeliad, is a species native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup to collect water. The plant produces striking inflorescences with vibrant, long-lasting flowers that range from red to orange. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Neoregelia gavionensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to care for in suitable conditions.
