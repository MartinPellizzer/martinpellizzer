Nothofagus bernhardii

Nothofagus bernhardii, commonly known as the Bernhard's southern beech, is a species native to the temperate rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Nothofagaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen leaves and smooth, often grayish bark. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often forming part of the understory in dense forest ecosystems. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by woody, spherical cones containing edible seeds.

Nothofagus bernhardii plays a vital role in maintaining the ecological balance of its habitat.
