Notocactus blaauwianus

Notocactus blaauwianus, commonly known as the Blaauw's notocactus, is a species of cactus native to South America, specifically Brazil.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are typically green with a waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom during the warmer months, adding a striking contrast to its foliage. It is a slow-growing species that thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for collectors and cactus enthusiasts.
