Nassella meyeniana

Nassella meyeniana, commonly known as the slender needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and dense, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native plant communities in its native range.
