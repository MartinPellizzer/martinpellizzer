Nemacaulis denudata var. gracilis

Nemacaulis denudata var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender sandwort, is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It typically features small, rounded leaves that are densely packed and often covered in a fine layer of white, powdery wax. The plant produces clusters of tiny, four-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in well-drained sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal, it is occasionally cultivated in xeriscapes and rock gardens.
