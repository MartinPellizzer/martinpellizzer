Nothosmyrnium japonicum

Nothosmyrnium japonicum, commonly known as the Japanese myrtle, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its aromatic, small, white to pink flowers. The plant has glossy, narrow leaves that are often fragrant when crushed. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers a sunny to partially shaded location.

Nothosmyrnium japonicum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and pleasant scent.
