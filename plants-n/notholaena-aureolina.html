Notholaena aureolina

Notholaena aureolina, commonly known as the golden notholaena, is a rare and unique fern native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are often tinged with a golden hue, especially along the edges. The plant grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices, preferring acidic soils. Its reproductive structures, including spore-bearing sorus, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
