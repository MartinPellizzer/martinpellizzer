Nepenthes gracillima var. major

Nepenthes gracillima var. major, commonly known as the giant slender pitcher plant, is a remarkable carnivorous plant native to the highland regions of Borneo.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves that form strikingly large, elongated pitcher structures. These pitchers are typically green or reddish in color and secrete a sticky liquid to trap and digest insects. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires high atmospheric humidity to flourish.

Its unique morphology and carnivorous adaptation make it a fascinating subject for botanical study.
