Nothotsuga

Nothotsuga, commonly known as the Alaska cedar, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between the eastern white cedar (Thuja occidentalis) and the western red cedar (Thuja plicata).

It is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. Nothotsuga typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. The plant has a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 20 meters.

Its foliage consists of scale-like leaves, and it produces small, inconspicuous cones.
