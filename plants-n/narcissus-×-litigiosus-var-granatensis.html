Narcissus × litigiosus var. granatensis

Narcissus × litigiosus var. granatensis, commonly known as the Granatense Daffodil, is a hybrid variety of daffodil characterized by its striking orange petals and a yellow corona.

It is native to the Iberian Peninsula and thrives in well-drained, sunny soils. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with narrow, grass-like leaves and a single flower per stem. It is valued for its early blooming period and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental appeal.

This variety is also noted for its resilience to mild winters and its ability to naturalize over time.
