Nesaea radicans var. latifolia

Nesaea radicans var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved trailing nesaea, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This plant is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.
