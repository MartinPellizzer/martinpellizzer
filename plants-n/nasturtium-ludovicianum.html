Nasturtium ludovicianum

Nasturtium ludovicianum, commonly known as the Mexican watercress, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow or orange flowers and heart-shaped leaves that are often used in culinary applications. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are edible and have a peppery, tangy flavor similar to watercress.

Nasturtium ludovicianum is also valued for its ability to attract beneficial insects like ladybugs and hoverflies.
