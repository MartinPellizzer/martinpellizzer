Noccaea caerulescens subsp. sylvestris

Noccaea caerulescens subsp.

sylvestris, commonly known as the forest pennycress, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its small, blue to violet flowers and trifoliate leaves.

This species thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks. It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many economically important crops.

The plant is also notable for its ability to accumulate heavy metals, making it of interest in phytoremediation studies.
