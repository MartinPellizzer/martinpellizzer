Nepeta globiflora

Nepeta globiflora, commonly known as globeflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive spherical flower heads, which are composed of numerous small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats and prefers well-drained soil. It is a perennial herb that can reach heights of up to 30 centimeters.

Nepeta globiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
