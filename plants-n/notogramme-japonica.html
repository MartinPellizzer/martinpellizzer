Notogramme japonica

Notogramme japonica, commonly known as Japanese wisteria, is a climbing vine native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its long, flexible stems and its distinctive, fragrant flowers that bloom in cascading clusters. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green, and its flowers range in color from pale purple to deep violet. Notogramme japonica is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and landscapes.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for trellises and arbors.
