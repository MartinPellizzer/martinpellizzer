Nicipe rosulata

Nicipe rosulata, commonly known as the Rosulate Nicipe, is a striking plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, silvery hairs, giving it a frosty appearance. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of pink and white, which bloom in early summer. Its succulent leaves store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments.

Nicipe rosulata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in well-draining soil.
