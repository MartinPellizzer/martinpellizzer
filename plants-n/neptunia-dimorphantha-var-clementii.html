Neptunia dimorphantha var. clementii

Neptunia dimorphantha var. clementii, commonly known as the Clementii's bladderleaf, is a variant of the Neptunia dimorphantha species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive bladder-like structures on its leaves, which are adapted for trapping small aquatic organisms. The plant thrives in wetland and swampy environments, often found in shallow water or along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are typically floating or semi-submerged, with a waxy surface that aids in water retention.

The species is valued for its ecological role in nutrient cycling and as a habitat for microorganisms.
