Neobesseya minima

Neobesseya minima, commonly known as the dwarf neobesseya, is a small shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It typically grows to a height of less than 30 centimeters, featuring compact, dense foliage with small, leathery leaves. The plant produces tiny, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow, which bloom in clusters during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a sunny to partially shaded environment.

Due to its ornamental value and compact size, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a container plant.
