Nasturtium indicum var. spathulatum

Nasturtium indicum var. spathulatum, commonly known as the broadleaf nasturtium, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, broad leaves and vibrant, showy flowers that come in shades of orange and yellow. The plant is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous plants. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Its flowers are edible, often used in salads and as a garnish, while the leaves can be consumed raw or cooked.
