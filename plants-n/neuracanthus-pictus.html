Neuracanthus pictus

Neuracanthus pictus, commonly known as the Painted Nerve Plant, is a strikingly colorful member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in warm, humid environments. The plant is characterized by its vibrant, variegated leaves that display shades of green, red, and yellow, often with intricate patterns. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically in shades of pink or purple, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Neuracanthus pictus is often grown as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage and relatively easy care requirements.
