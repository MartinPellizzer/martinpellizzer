Nauclea lancifolia

Nauclea lancifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved nauclea, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves, which are typically arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Nauclea lancifolia is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forest understories and along riverbanks.

It has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
