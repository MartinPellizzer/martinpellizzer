Newmania orthostachys

Newmania orthostachys, commonly known as the narrowleaf goldenrod, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, yellowish-green leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, golden-yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Newmania orthostachys is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
