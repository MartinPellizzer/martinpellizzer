Nephelaphyllum pulchrum var. sikkimense

Nephelaphyllum pulchrum var. sikkimense, commonly known as the Sikkim beauty bell, is a striking flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of Nepal and India.

It is characterized by its vibrant, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, depending on the variety. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in mountainous forests, and thrives in cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, with a smooth surface and a slightly waxy texture.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
