Nepeta nepetella var. aragonensis

Nepeta nepetella var. aragonensis, commonly known as the Aragon catmint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Spain.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils, which contribute to its pleasant scent and potential medicinal uses.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to rocky or well-drained soils.
