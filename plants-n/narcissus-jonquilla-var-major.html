Narcissus jonquilla var. major

Narcissus jonquilla var. major, commonly known as the giant daffodil, is a striking member of the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers with a distinctive orange corona. Native to the Iberian Peninsula, this variety thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. The plant produces long, strap-like leaves and is known for its robust growth and early spring blooming habit.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
