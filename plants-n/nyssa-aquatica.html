Nyssa aquatica

Nyssa aquatica, commonly known as the water dogwood, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and opposite, ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, which are followed by bright red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in swamps and along riverbanks.

Nyssa aquatica has a shallow root system and is an important species for wetland ecosystems.
