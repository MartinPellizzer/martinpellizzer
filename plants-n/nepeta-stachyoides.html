Nepeta stachyoides

Nepeta stachyoides, commonly known as catmint, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or violet flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in herbal remedies due to their mild mint-like scent and potential medicinal properties.

Nepeta stachyoides thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
