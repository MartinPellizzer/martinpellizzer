Nepeta griffithii

Nepeta griffithii, commonly known as Griffith's catmint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular blue or violet flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically gray-green and have a mint-like fragrance, which contributes to its common name.

Nepeta griffithii is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soil.
