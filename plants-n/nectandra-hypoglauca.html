Nectandra hypoglauca

Nectandra hypoglauca, commonly known as the golden sandarac tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green foliage and small, yellowish flowers. The tree produces a resinous sap that has been historically used in incense and varnishes. Its bark and leaves contain essential oils with potential medicinal properties.

Nectandra hypoglauca thrives in humid, lowland forests and is valued for both its ecological and economic significance.
