Neottia puberula var. puberula

Neottia puberula var. puberula, commonly known as the pubescent lady's tresses, is a species of orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy stems and long, spirally arranged leaves. The plant produces a showy, fragrant inflorescence consisting of numerous small, white to pale pink flowers. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows.

Neottia puberula var. puberula is a perennial herb that thrives in temperate climates and is often found in association with other orchid species.
