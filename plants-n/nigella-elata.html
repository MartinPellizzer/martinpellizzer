Nigella elata

Nigella elata, commonly known as the Persian cowpea, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, yellowish flowers and compound leaves. The plant produces small, spherical seeds that are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable in its native habitats.
