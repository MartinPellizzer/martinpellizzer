Nicipe adseptentrionesvergentula

Nicipe adseptentrionesvergentula, commonly known as the northern shadow fern, is a rare and delicate plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Arctic tundra.

It possesses a compact, rosette-like structure with finely divided, silvery-green leaves that help reflect excess sunlight. The plant thrives in nutrient-poor soils and relies on mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient absorption. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds and are released during the brief summer growing season.

Due to its specialized adaptations, it is highly sensitive to environmental changes and is considered an indicator species for climate stability.
