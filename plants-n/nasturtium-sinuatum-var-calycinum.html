Nasturtium sinuatum var. calycinum

Nasturtium sinuatum var. calycinum, commonly known as the California nasturtium, is a variant of the common nasturtium characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers.

This plant is native to California and thrives in warm, sunny climates, often found in coastal regions. Its leaves are heart-shaped and slightly lobed, with a peppery scent when crushed, similar to its parent species. The flowers come in shades of orange and yellow, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is a popular ornamental and edible plant, used in gardens for both beauty and culinary purposes.
