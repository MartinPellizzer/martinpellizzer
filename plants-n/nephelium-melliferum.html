Nephelium melliferum

Nephelium melliferum, commonly known as the mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces a distinctive fruit with a deep red outer pericarp and a white, segmented inner flesh that is highly prized for its sweet, tart flavor. The fruit is rich in antioxidants and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.

Its cultivation requires a warm, humid climate and well-drained soil to thrive.
