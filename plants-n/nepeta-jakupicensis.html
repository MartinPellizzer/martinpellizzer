Nepeta jakupicensis

Nepeta jakupicensis, commonly known as the Balkan catmint, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with a fuzzy texture and a distinctive minty aroma. Its flowers are typically blue to purple, arranged in whorled clusters at the ends of the stems.

Nepeta jakupicensis is often grown as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and fragrant properties.
