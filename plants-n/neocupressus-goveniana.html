Neocupressus goveniana

Neocupressus goveniana, commonly known as the California incense cedar, is a coniferous tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, scale-like leaves and long, slender cones. The tree can grow up to 50 meters tall and has a narrow, conical shape. Its bark is reddish-brown and becomes fissured with age.

The plant is valued for its durable wood and is often found in mountainous regions with well-drained soils.
