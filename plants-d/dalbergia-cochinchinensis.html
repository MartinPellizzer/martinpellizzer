Dalbergia cochinchinensis

Dalbergia cochinchinensis, commonly known as Vietnamese rosewood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, dense wood with a rich, reddish-brown color and a fine, uniform grain. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by flat, pod-like fruits. Its leaves are pinnately compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets.

The species is valued for its high-quality timber, which is used in furniture, musical instruments, and carving.
