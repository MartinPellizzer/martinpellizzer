Dolichos angularis

Dolichos angularis, commonly known as the angular cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its angular, ridged pods and trifoliate leaves, which are typical of the Fabaceae family. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into elongated, angular pods containing edible seeds. It is often cultivated for its nutrient-rich seeds, which are used in various cuisines.

Dolichos angularis is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a beneficial crop in agricultural systems.
