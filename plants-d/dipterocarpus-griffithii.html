Dipterocarpus griffithii

Dipterocarpus griffithii, commonly known as the Malayan ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its heavy, durable wood and large, glossy leaves. The plant typically grows in lowland rainforests and is known for its ability to thrive in humid, warm climates. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits.

Due to its hardiness and ecological importance, it plays a significant role in the biodiversity of its native habitats.
