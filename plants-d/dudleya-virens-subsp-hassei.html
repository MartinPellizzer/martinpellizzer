Dudleya virens subsp. hassei

Dudleya virens subsp.

hassei, commonly known as the coastal dudleya, is a succulent plant native to coastal regions of California and Baja California. It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color, arranged in a rosette pattern.

The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, pink or purple flowers during the spring and summer months. It is adapted to coastal climates with well-drained soils and can tolerate salt spray.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
