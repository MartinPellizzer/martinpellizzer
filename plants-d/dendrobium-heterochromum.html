Dendrobium heterochromum

Dendrobium heterochromum, commonly known as the rainbow orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, clustered inflorescences that produce a variety of colored flowers, often in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant has a unique growth habit, with thick, fleshy pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its flowers are typically long-lasting and bloom during the summer months.

Dendrobium heterochromum is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various growing conditions.
