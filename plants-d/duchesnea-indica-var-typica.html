Duchesnea indica var. typica

Duchesnea indica var. typica, commonly known as the common wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in culinary applications. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its botanical features include hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves with distinctive veins.
