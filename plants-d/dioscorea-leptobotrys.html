Dioscorea leptobotrys

Dioscorea leptobotrys, commonly known as the slender yam, is a herbaceous perennial vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and tuberous roots. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant. Its tubers, which are the edible part, are used in traditional cuisines and have been studied for their potential medicinal properties.

The species is also noted for its adaptability to various soil types and its role in agroforestry systems.
