Ditassa cucullata

Ditassa cucullata, commonly known as the hooded bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. The plant features a short, thick stem with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers bloom in clusters and are adapted to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial shade.
