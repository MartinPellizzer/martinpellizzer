Dendropemon spathulatus

Dendropemon spathulatus, commonly known as the broad-leaf orchid, is a species of orchid native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, showy flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid environments. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with a distinctive spathaceous bract.

Dendropemon spathulatus thrives in well-drained, nutrient-rich substrates and requires high light exposure for optimal growth.
