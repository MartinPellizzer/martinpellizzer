Didissandra muscicola

Didissandra muscicola, commonly known as the mossy sandstone plant, is a unique species native to the rocky, arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a succulent plant characterized by its fleshy, tubular stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant's ability to thrive in nutrient-poor, well-drained soils makes it well adapted to its harsh environment. Its flowers are typically small and yellow, appearing in clusters, and it produces distinctive, elongated fruits.

Didissandra muscicola plays a role in stabilizing rocky soils and provides habitat for small invertebrates.
