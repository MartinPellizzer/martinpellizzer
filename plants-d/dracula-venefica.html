Dracula venefica

Dracula venefica, commonly known as the vampire orchid, is a rare and striking orchid native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It is characterized by its dark, velvety petals that resemble a vampire's face, giving it its ominous common name. The plant thrives in high-altitude environments with high humidity and partial shade. Its flowers are typically a deep purple or black, with intricate patterns that mimic blood.

Due to its rarity and unique appearance, Dracula venefica is highly sought after by botanists and collectors alike.
