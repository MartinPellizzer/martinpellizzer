Daphne hemsleyi

Daphne hemsleyi, commonly known as the Mexican daphne, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of central Mexico.

It features aromatic, waxy leaves that are typically gray-green in color, and it produces clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, white, or purple. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Daphne hemsleyi thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.
