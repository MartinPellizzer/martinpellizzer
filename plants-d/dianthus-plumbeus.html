Dianthus plumbeus

Dianthus plumbeus, commonly known as the blue carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Dianthus plumbeus is also noted for its aromatic scent, which adds to its ornamental value.
