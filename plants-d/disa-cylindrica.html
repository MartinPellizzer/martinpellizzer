Disa cylindrica

Disa cylindrica, commonly known as the cylindrical disa, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, tubular-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers are usually pink or purple and bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Disa cylindrica is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in a well-drained garden setting.
