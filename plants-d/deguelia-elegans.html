Deguelia elegans

Deguelia elegans, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, also known as the legume or pea family, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pod-like fruits. The plant is notable for its ornamental value, featuring showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are often covered in a fine, velvety texture, giving it its common name.

Deguelia elegans is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in ecological restoration projects.
