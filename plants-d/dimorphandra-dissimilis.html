Dimorphandra dissimilis

Dimorphandra dissimilis, commonly known as the yellow flame tree, is a tropical tree native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it its common name. The plant has bipinnate leaves, which are divided into small, feather-like leaflets. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree is also valued for its hard, durable wood, which has been used in traditional crafts and construction.
