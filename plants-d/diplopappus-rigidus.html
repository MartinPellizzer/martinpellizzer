Diplopappus rigidus

Diplopappus rigidus, commonly known as the rigid burr bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and pairs of stiff, spiny bracts that give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a rough, hairy texture.

The species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in harsh conditions.
