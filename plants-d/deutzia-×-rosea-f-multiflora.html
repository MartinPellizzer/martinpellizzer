Deutzia × rosea f. multiflora

Deutzia × rosea f. multiflora, commonly known as the multibracteate deutzia, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features arching stems with opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white or pink flowers in late spring to early summer. Its ornamental value comes from its showy blooms and attractive foliage.

It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.
