Dendrobium davaoense

Dendrobium davaoense, commonly known as the Davao orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its tall, cane-like stems and clusters of fragrant, showy flowers that bloom in shades of white, yellow, and pink. The plant typically grows in humid, tropical environments, often attached to trees or rocks in mountainous regions. Its flowers are long-lasting, making it a popular choice for ornamental cultivation.

Dendrobium davaoense is also valued for its resilience and ability to thrive in a variety of growing conditions.
