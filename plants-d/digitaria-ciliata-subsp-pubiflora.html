Digitaria ciliata subsp. pubiflora

Digitaria ciliata subsp.

pubiflora, commonly known as the hairy digit grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions. It typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands, roadsides, and disturbed soils.

The plant features slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a hairy texture. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, spikelet-bearing branches.

It is characterized by its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its resilience to environmental stress.
