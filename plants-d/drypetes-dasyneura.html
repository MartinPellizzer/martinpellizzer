Drypetes dasyneura

Drypetes dasyneura, commonly known as the hairy drypetes, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its rough, hairy stems and alternate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that split open to release seeds.

The plant is often found in lowland and hill forests, thriving in moist, shaded environments.
