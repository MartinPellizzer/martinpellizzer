Dalbergia zollingeriana

Dalbergia zollingeriana, commonly known as the Zollinger's rosewood, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its dense, dark-colored wood, which is highly valued for its aesthetic and acoustic properties. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland and montane forests.

Due to its ornamental value and timber demand, it is also cultivated in horticultural settings.
