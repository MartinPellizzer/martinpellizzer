Delarbrea montana subsp. arborea

Delarbrea montana subsp.

arborea, commonly known as the mountain delarbrea, is a shrub or small tree native to the high-altitude regions of South America. It is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests and alpine meadows. Its bark is thick and cork-like, providing some resistance to frost and drought.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has limited use in traditional medicine.
