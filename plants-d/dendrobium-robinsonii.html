Dendrobium robinsonii

Dendrobium robinsonii, commonly known as the Robinson's orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, using its aerial roots to absorb moisture and nutrients from the environment. Its leaves are long and strap-like, and it thrives in warm, humid conditions with bright, indirect light.

Dendrobium robinsonii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for its delicate floral display.
