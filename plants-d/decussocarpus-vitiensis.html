Decussocarpus vitiensis

Decussocarpus vitiensis, commonly known as the Vitiensis caryopsis, is a species of flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the grass family, Poaceae, and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and compact, cylindrical inflorescence. The plant produces small, spikelet-based flowers that are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are narrow and blade-like, with a prominent midvein, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

This species is of interest in ecological studies due to its role in local ecosystems and potential uses in traditional practices.
