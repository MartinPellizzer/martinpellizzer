Dasylirion berlandieri

Dasylirion berlandieri, commonly known as the desert spoon, is a perennial plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette of long, narrow, gray-green leaves that are covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences bearing small, white or pale blue flowers. It is well adapted to arid environments and can thrive in full sun with minimal water.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and desert gardens.
