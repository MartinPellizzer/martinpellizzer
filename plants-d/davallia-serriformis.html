Davallia serriformis

Davallia serriformis, commonly known as the serrated holly fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are often arched or drooping. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, with creeping rhizomes that allow it to spread along the forest floor. Its leaves are typically bright green and have a serrated edge, giving it its common name.

It prefers humid, shady environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
