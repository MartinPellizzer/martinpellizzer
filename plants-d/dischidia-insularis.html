Dischidia insularis

Dischidia insularis, commonly known as the Island Trumpet Vine, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its aerial stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant or in gardens.
