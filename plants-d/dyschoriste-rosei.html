Dyschoriste rosei

Dyschoriste rosei, commonly known as the rose swamp willow, is a flowering plant native to wetland environments in Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the rainy season. Its root system helps stabilize soil in marshy areas, making it ecologically significant.

Dyschoriste rosei is often found in swampy or seasonally flooded habitats, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils.
