Diospyros subacuta

Diospyros subacuta, commonly known as the narrow-leaf persimmon, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves that are typically narrow and pointed. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, orange-red berries. Its bark is smooth and dark brown, often marked with shallow fissures.

Diospyros subacuta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
