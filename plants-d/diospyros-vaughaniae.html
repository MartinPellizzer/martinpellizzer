Diospyros vaughaniae

Diospyros vaughaniae, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes ebony and persimmon trees. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, orange-red berries. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a leathery texture with a distinctive reddish-brown underside.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
