Dendrobium chloranthum

Dendrobium chloranthum, commonly known as the green-flowered orchid, is an epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, cane-like stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers with prominent yellow lips. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Dendrobium chloranthum is prized for its unique appearance and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
