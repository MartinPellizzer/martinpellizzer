Dioscorea pennellii var. pilosula

Dioscorea pennellii var. pilosula, commonly known as the woolly yam, is a tuberous-rooted vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its hairy, cylindrical tubers that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, green, heart-shaped leaves and bears yellow flowers that develop into capsule fruits. Its tubers are rich in starch and have been utilized for both culinary and medicinal purposes by indigenous communities.

The species is also valued for its potential in agricultural research due to its adaptability and nutritional content.
