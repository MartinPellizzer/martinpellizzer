Dianthus subaphyllus

Dianthus subaphyllus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant produces clusters of pink or white flowers with five petals, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and linear, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Dianthus subaphyllus is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats and is adapted to harsh, arid conditions.
