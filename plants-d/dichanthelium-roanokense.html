Dichanthelium roanokense

Dichanthelium roanokense, commonly known as Roanoke grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and riverbanks. The plant has slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green color. Its inflorescence consists of a loose to dense cluster of spikelets, which are characteristic of the grass family.

Dichanthelium roanokense plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
