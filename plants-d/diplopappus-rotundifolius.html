Diplopappus rotundifolius

Diplopappus rotundifolius, commonly known as the round-leafed burrplant, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its broad, rounded leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has a distinctive fruit structure composed of two joined seed pods that twist together when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
