Dodonaea humifusa var. hirtella

Dodonaea humifusa var. hirtella, commonly known as the hairy Dodonaea, is a shrub native to Australia.

It features small, opposite leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces clusters of tiny, yellowish-green flowers, which are followed by small, woody fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate drought and salt spray.
