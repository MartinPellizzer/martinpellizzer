Dianthus arbuscula

Dianthus arbuscula, commonly known as the spring beauty, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant typically blooms in early spring, often before the leaves fully emerge. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in woodland or open areas.

Dianthus arbuscula is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, known for its distinctive flowers and ornamental value.
