Dactylorhiza traunsteinerioides var. francis-drucei

Dactylorhiza traunsteinerioides var. francis-drucei, commonly known as the Francis-Druce's marsh orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to wetland habitats in Europe.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, pinkish-purple flowers. The plant typically grows in peat bogs and damp meadows, preferring acidic and nutrient-poor soils. Its flowers bloom in late summer, and it has a symbiotic relationship with mycorrhizal fungi, which aid in nutrient uptake.

The species is of conservation interest due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity.
