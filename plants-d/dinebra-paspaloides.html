Dinebra paspaloides

Dinebra paspaloides, commonly known as the silky bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green blades and tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in open, sunny areas such as meadows and prairies. Its leaves are covered in a fine layer of hair, giving it a silvery appearance.

Dinebra paspaloides is often used in restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
