Diospyros tepu

Diospyros tepu, commonly known as the Tepu persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other persimmon species. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a berry-like structure that turns from green to orange-red when mature.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruit.
