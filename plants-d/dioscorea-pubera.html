Dioscorea pubera

Dioscorea pubera, commonly known as the velvet yam, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Dioscoreaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas. The plant is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are often used in traditional medicine and as a food source. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a hairy texture, and it produces small, greenish-yellow flowers.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
