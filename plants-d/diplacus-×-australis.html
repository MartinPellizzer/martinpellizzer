Diplacus × australis

Diplacus × australis, commonly known as the hummingbird flower, is a hybrid plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the mallow family (Malvaceae) and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its botanical features include hairy stems, heart-shaped leaves, and a compact growth habit.

Diplacus × australis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like hummingbirds.
