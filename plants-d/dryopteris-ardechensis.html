Dryopteris ardechensis

Dryopteris ardechensis, commonly known as the Ardeche shield fern, is a rare and endangered species native to the limestone regions of the Ardeche Valley in southern France.

It is a deciduous fern characterized by its distinctive, shield-shaped fronds that are typically dark green and have a rugged, textured surface. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found growing on rocky outcrops or in crevices. Its spores are released from the underside of the fronds, and it reproduces primarily through spore dispersal rather than seeds.

Due to habitat loss and environmental changes, conservation efforts are crucial for the survival of this unique fern.
