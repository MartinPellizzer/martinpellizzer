Diospyros ferrea var. aroeensis

Diospyros ferrea var. aroeensis, commonly known as the Aroeense persimmon, is a species native to the tropical regions of Africa, particularly found in countries like Angola and the Democratic Republic of the Congo.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can reach heights of up to 10 meters, with dense, dark green foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are borne in clusters, followed by edible, orange to red persimmon fruits. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a waxy surface, while its bark is thick and fissured, providing a rugged appearance.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local communities for food and medicinal purposes.
