Dicliptera japonica f. albiflora

Dicliptera japonica f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered Japanese grass, is a cultivar of a perennial grass native to East Asia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its delicate appearance and ability to thrive in moist, well-drained soils. It prefers partial to full sunlight and is commonly used in garden landscapes for its aesthetic appeal.

This variety is a popular choice for water gardens and bog gardens due to its adaptability to wet conditions.
