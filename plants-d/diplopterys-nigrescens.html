Diplopterys nigrescens

Diplopterys nigrescens, commonly known as the black nightshade, is a tropical plant belonging to the Solanaceae family.

It is native to regions of South America, particularly the Amazon rainforest. The plant is characterized by its dark purple to black berries, which are often covered in a waxy coating. Its leaves are typically ovate with a slightly hairy texture, and it produces small, white flowers.

Due to its toxic properties, it is not commonly cultivated for ornamental or edible purposes.
