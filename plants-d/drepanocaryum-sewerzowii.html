Drepanocaryum sewerzowii

Drepanocaryum sewerzowii, commonly known as the narrow-leafed grass, is a perennial grass species native to the temperate regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and compact growth habit, typically forming dense mats on the ground. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and along rocky outcrops.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
