Dioscorea trichantha

Dioscorea trichantha, commonly known as the African yam bean, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by tuberous roots that are edible and used in traditional cuisines. Its tubers are rich in starch and have been cultivated for their nutritional value.

Dioscorea trichantha is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
