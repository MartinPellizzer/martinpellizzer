Deyeuxia deschampsioides

Deyeuxia deschampsioides, commonly known as the slender false brome, is a grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green to bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, marshes, and along stream banks.

Deyeuxia deschampsioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting insect and bird populations.
