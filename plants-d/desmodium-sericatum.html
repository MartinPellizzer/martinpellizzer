Desmodium sericatum

Desmodium sericatum, commonly known as silverleaf desmodium, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a legume belonging to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is known for its silvery underside on its leaves, which gives it its common name. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Desmodium sericatum is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
