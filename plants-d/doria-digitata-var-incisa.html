Doria digitata var. incisa

Doria digitata var. incisa, commonly known as the serrated digitate fern, is a variant of the digitate fern characterized by its distinctive, finely serrated leaf margins.

This plant belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Southeast Asia. Its fronds are typically bipinnate, with a delicate, feathery appearance that enhances its ornamental value. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, nutrient-rich soil to flourish.

It is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
