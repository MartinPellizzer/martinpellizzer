Diuris punctata

Diuris punctata, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southeastern Australia.

It typically grows in heathlands and open forests, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant features a compact, hairy stem with several narrow, grass-like leaves. Its most distinctive characteristic is the intricate, spotted flowers that are usually pink or purple in color.

Diuris punctata is known for its resilience and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.
