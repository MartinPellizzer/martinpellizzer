Danthonia provincialis var. breviaristata

Danthonia provincialis var. breviaristata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed sand sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, clustered spikelets that produce wind-pollinated flowers. This species thrives in sandy soils and is often found in dune environments, where it plays a role in stabilizing sand dunes. Its low-growing habit and drought tolerance make it well adapted to harsh coastal conditions.

The plant is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological significance in coastal ecosystems.
