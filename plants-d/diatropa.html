Diatropa

Diatropa, commonly known as the "red hibiscus," is a genus of flowering plants in the mallow family, Malvaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas, particularly in areas with warm, humid climates. Diatropa species typically have rough, hairy leaves and produce capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

They are often cultivated for their ornamental value due to their vibrant blooms and adaptability to various growing conditions.
