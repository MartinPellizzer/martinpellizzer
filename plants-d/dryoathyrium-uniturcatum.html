Dryoathyrium uniturcatum

Dryoathyrium uniturcatum, commonly known as the goldenstar moss, is a species of moss belonging to the family Pottiaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, cushion-like growth form and is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rock crevices. It produces spores in capsules that are clustered in dense, terminal cushions, a feature common among many mosses.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticultural settings for its ability to thrive in humid conditions.
