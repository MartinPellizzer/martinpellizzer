Didymocarpus membranaceus

Didymocarpus membranaceus, commonly known as the Chinese date or Indian date, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small evergreen tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height, featuring glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by edible, fleshy fruits resembling dates in appearance. The fruit has a sweet, sticky pulp and is often consumed fresh or used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
