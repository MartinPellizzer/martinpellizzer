Dianthus scaber subsp. toletanus

Dianthus scaber subsp.

toletanus, commonly known as the toletan thrift, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and produces clusters of pink or red flowers with a distinctive, frayed appearance.

The plant has hairy, linear leaves that are often covered in fine, bristly hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
