Diospyros citrifolia

Diospyros citrifolia, commonly known as the African persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and palmate leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by edible, orange-red berries. The fruit has a sweet, honey-like flavor when fully ripe and is rich in nutrients.

It is often cultivated for its fruit and has traditional uses in various African cultures.
