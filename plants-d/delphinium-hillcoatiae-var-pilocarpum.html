Delphinium hillcoatiae var. pilocarpum

Delphinium hillcoatiae var. pilocarpum, commonly known as the Hillcoat's larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters, bearing clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and a distinctive blue-green hue. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural reserves.
