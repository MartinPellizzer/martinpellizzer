Delissea solanacea

Delissea solanacea, commonly known as the wild tomato, is a herbaceous annual plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into round, fleshy fruits resembling tomatoes in appearance. Delissea solanacea is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Its botanical features make it both a subject of scientific study and a potential candidate for agricultural use.
