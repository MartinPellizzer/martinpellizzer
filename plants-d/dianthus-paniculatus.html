Dianthus paniculatus

Dianthus paniculatus, commonly known as the clustered campion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like carnations and snapdragons. The plant is characterized by its tall, branched stems and clusters of small, pink or white flowers. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs.

Dianthus paniculatus is often found in meadows and rocky areas, thriving in full sun and well-drained soil.
