Deparia petersenii

Deparia petersenii, commonly known as Petersen's wood fern, is a species of fern native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are often divided into small, triangular pinnae. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are wiry and creeping, allowing it to spread gradually over time.

Deparia petersenii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with appropriate humidity and light conditions.
