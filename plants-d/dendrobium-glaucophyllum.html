Dendrobium glaucophyllum

Dendrobium glaucophyllum, commonly known as the blue-veined orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a distinctive appearance, featuring long, wiry stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant is characterized by its pale green or blue-green stems, which are covered in a waxy, powdery coating. It prefers shaded, humid environments and is often found growing in forest understories.

Dendrobium glaucophyllum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
