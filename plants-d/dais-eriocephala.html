Dais eriocephala

Dais eriocephala, commonly known as the white daisy or eriocephala, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies, sunflowers, and thistles. The plant is characterized by its white ray florets surrounding a yellow disk flower, and it produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Dais eriocephala is a hardy perennial that is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and attractive appearance.
