Diospyros glomerata

Diospyros glomerata, commonly known as the clustered persimmon, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other persimmon species and ebony trees. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually oval to elliptical in shape. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and the fruit is a small, round berry that changes color from yellow to red as it matures.

The bark is smooth and dark in color, and the plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
