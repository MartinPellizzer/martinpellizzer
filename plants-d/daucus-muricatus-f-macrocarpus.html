Daucus muricatus f. macrocarpus

Daucus muricatus f. macrocarpus, commonly known as the large-fruited wild carrot, is a botanical variant of the wild carrot species.

It is characterized by its enlarged, fleshy fruits that are typically reddish or purplish in color. The plant belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and vegetables. Its leaves are pinnately divided, and the flowers are small and white, arranged in umbels.

This form is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats, thriving in temperate climates.
