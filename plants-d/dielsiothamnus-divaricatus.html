Dielsiothamnus divaricatus

Dielsiothamnus divaricatus, commonly known as the variable thornbush, is a shrub native to parts of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.

Its adaptability makes it a resilient species in challenging ecological conditions.
