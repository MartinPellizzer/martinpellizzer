Delphinium pingwuense

Delphinium pingwuense, commonly known as the Pingwu larkspur, is a species of flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, spindly stems and clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky areas at high elevations. Its leaves are deeply lobed and have a distinctive, sometimes hairy texture.

Delphinium pingwuense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
