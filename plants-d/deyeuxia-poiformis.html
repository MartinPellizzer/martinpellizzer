Deyeuxia poiformis

Deyeuxia poiformis, commonly known as the broad-leaf sedgeland grass, is a perennial grass species native to various regions of Asia.

It typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are characteristic of the Poaceae family. Deyeuxia poiformis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
