Dendrobium toadjanum

Dendrobium toadjanum, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features long, cane-like stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, yellow-orange flowers that bloom during the summer months. Its leaves are typically elliptical and arranged in two rows along the stem.

Dendrobium toadjanum thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
