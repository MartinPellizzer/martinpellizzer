Dolichos subaequalis

Dolichos subaequalis, commonly known as the narrowleaf cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and trifoliate leaves with narrow, lance-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that give way to elongated, cylindrical pods containing edible seeds. It is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and thrives in warm climates.

Dolichos subaequalis is valued for its nutritional seeds, which are rich in protein and used in various cuisines.
