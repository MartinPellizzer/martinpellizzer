Dypsis integra

Dypsis integra, commonly known as the areca palm, is a tropical plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its slender, upright trunk and feather-like leaves that form a dense, fan-shaped crown. The plant has a fibrous root system that helps it anchor in nutrient-poor soils. Its leaves are typically dark green and glossy, with a distinct midrib.

Dypsis integra is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in indoor environments with bright, indirect light.
