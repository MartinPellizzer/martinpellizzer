Dioscorea venosa var. effusa

Dioscorea venosa var. effusa, commonly known as the velvetleaf yam, is a tuberous plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, waxy, and often hairy leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by yellowish berries that contain the seeds. Its tubers are used as a food source in some regions and have been traditionally used in herbal medicine.

The plant thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in forest understories.
