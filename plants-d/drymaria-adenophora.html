Drymaria adenophora

Drymaria adenophora, commonly known as the silverweed, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically forms dense mats on the ground. The plant features small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, and its leaves are often covered in fine, silvery hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.

Drymaria adenophora is known for its ability to spread rapidly through rhizomes, making it both a useful ground cover and a potential weed in certain environments.
