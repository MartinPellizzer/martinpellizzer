Delissea angustifolia var. lanaiensis

Delissea angustifolia var. lanaiensis, commonly known as Lanai false hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal areas and rocky habitats, characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of yellow and orange, which bloom throughout the year. Its botanical features include a woody stem, simple leaves with a smooth surface, and capsules as its fruit type.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
