Dampiera latealata

Dampiera latealata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved dampiera, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to southern Australia.

It features small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue to purple in color, blooming from late winter to early spring. The plant has linear, aromatic leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal heathlands and rocky areas.

Dampiera latealata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and seasonal blooms.
