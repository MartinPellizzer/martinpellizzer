Dioscorea pilosiuscula var. panamensis

Dioscorea pilosiuscula var. panamensis, commonly known as the Panamanian yam, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by tuberous roots that are used as food in some regions. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a complex network of runners that help it spread across the forest floor.

The tubers are often harvested for their starchy, edible contents.
