Diospyros fusicarpa

Diospyros fusicarpa, commonly known as the African persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes ebony and other related species. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. Its fruits are small, round, and typically have a smooth, leathery skin that turns from green to yellow or red as they mature.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
