Dichodon maximum

Dichodon maximum, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetlands and along stream banks. The plant features showy, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a waxy coating.

Dichodon maximum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in moist environments.
