Dendrocalamus strictus

Dendrocalamus strictus, commonly known as the bamboo giant, is a tall, fast-growing grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its robust, straight culms that can reach heights of up to 30 meters. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences. Its hollow stems are strong and flexible, making it valuable for construction and handicrafts.

Dendrocalamus strictus plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
