Discocactus ferricola

Discocactus ferricola, commonly known as the ironwood cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are covered in spiny areoles. The plant produces yellow flowers during the dry season, which are typically borne at the tips of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it well adapted to desert environments.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping and cactus collections.
