Dianthus comanae

Dianthus comanae, commonly known as the alpine campion, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is native to the alpine regions of Europe and Asia, thriving in rocky and high-altitude environments. The plant produces showy, pink or white flowers with five petals, often arranged in a cup-like shape. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, adapted to withstand harsh mountain conditions.

Dianthus comanae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
