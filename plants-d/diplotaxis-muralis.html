Diplotaxis muralis

Diplotaxis muralis, commonly known as the rock dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing, perennial herb that typically reaches heights of up to 30 centimeters. The plant features basal leaves that are lobed and covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are yellow with a distinctive central disc. It thrives in rocky and sandy soils, often growing in arid or semi-arid environments.

Diplotaxis muralis is known for its ability to tolerate drought and is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
