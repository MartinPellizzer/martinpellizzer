Delphinium parishii

Delphinium parishii, commonly known as the Sierra delphinium, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine meadows, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

Delphinium parishii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
