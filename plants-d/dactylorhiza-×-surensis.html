Dactylorhiza × surensis

Dactylorhiza × surensis, commonly known as the yellow marsh orchid, is a hybrid orchid native to western Europe.

It is a terrestrial herb with a sympodial growth habit, featuring a rhizome and a rosette of fleshy, grass-like leaves. The plant produces a single, erect stem bearing a raceme of yellow to orange flowers with a distinctive lip. It thrives in wet, acidic habitats such as marshes and fens, often in association with other orchid species.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
