Dryandra spiralis

Dryandra spiralis, commonly known as the spiral dryandra, is a striking member of the Proteaceae family native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its unique, spirally arranged leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces striking, cone-shaped inflorescences with bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It is adapted to the region's Mediterranean climate, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
