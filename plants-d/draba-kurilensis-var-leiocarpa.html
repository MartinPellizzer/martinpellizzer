Draba kurilensis var. leiocarpa

Draba kurilensis var. leiocarpa, commonly known as the Kuril rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the Russian Far East and parts of northern Japan.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and pungent odor. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It has basal leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, and its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer.

This variety is distinguished by its smooth, glabrous stems and relatively compact growth habit.
