Diplocaulobium opilionites

Diplocaulobium opilionites, commonly known as the "opilionite fern," is a fossilized plant from the Carboniferous period.

It belongs to the group of early vascular plants, characterized by its complex structure and early development of true roots and leaves. The plant's morphology suggests it was a primitive fern-like organism, with fronds that may have been used for photosynthesis. Its presence in sedimentary rocks indicates it thrived in swampy, humid environments.

This fossil provides important insights into the early evolution of terrestrial plant life.
