Dyssodia treculii

Dyssodia treculii, commonly known as the mountain dyssodia, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, adapting to the harsh environmental conditions of its habitat.

Dyssodia treculii is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance.
