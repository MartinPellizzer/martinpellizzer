Diapedium repens f. roseum

Diapedium repens f. roseum, commonly known as the pink-flowered trailing geranium, is a compact, mat-forming perennial native to South Africa.

It features small, showy flowers in shades of pink to rose, which bloom profusely from late spring to early autumn. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically rounded or heart-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its trailing habit and vibrant blooms make it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.
