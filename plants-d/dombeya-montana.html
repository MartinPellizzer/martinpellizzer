Dombeya montana

Dombeya montana, commonly known as the mountain dombeya, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It features showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a yellow or orange throat. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Dombeya montana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.
