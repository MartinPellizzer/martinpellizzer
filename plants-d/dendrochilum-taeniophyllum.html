Dendrochilum taeniophyllum

Dendrochilum taeniophyllum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed tree orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and long, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, pale green or white flowers that bloom during the rainy season. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found growing in forest understories.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a popular species among orchid enthusiasts and botanists alike.
