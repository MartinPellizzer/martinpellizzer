Declieuxia oenanthoides var. genuina

Declieuxia oenanthoides var. genuina, commonly known as the golden reed, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bright green. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in dense clusters during the summer months. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often used in landscaping and garden design.
