Dalbergia novoguineensis

Dalbergia novoguineensis, commonly known as the New Guinea rosewood, is a tropical tree native to the rainforests of New Guinea.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its dense, dark-colored wood, which is highly valued for its durability and aesthetic appeal. The plant has bipinnate leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow.

Its fruit is a pod containing several seeds, and it plays an important ecological role in its native habitat.
