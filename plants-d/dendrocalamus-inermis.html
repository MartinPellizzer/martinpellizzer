Dendrocalamus inermis

Dendrocalamus inermis, commonly known as the bamboo giant, is a tall, fast-growing grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that can reach heights of up to 30 meters. The plant has a robust root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion. Its leaves are long and narrow, often used in traditional crafts and construction.

Dendrocalamus inermis is also valued for its strong, lightweight culms, which are used in making furniture, scaffolding, and even musical instruments.
