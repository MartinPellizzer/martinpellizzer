Dentaria sibirica

Dentaria sibirica, commonly known as Siberian toothplant, is a perennial herb native to the colder regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in a raceme. It produces round, green pods that contain several seeds.

Dentaria sibirica is adapted to grow in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine or arctic environments.
