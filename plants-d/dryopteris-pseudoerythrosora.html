Dryopteris pseudoerythrosora

Dryopteris pseudoerythrosora, commonly known as the purple shield fern, is a species of fern belonging to the Dryopteris genus.

It is characterized by its distinctive dark purple to reddish-brown fronds that contrast with its lighter, more delicate veins. This fern typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate forest regions. The fronds are usually pinnately compound, with a leathery texture and a dense, shield-like appearance.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded garden settings.
