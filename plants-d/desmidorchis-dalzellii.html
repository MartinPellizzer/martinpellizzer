Desmidorchis dalzellii

Desmidorchis dalzellii, commonly known as the Dalzell's orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that typically feature a combination of purple, pink, and white hues. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surfaces of trees and rocks without deriving nutrients from them. Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and limited distribution, making it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat loss and environmental changes, it is considered endangered in its natural range.
