Disa obtusa subsp. hottentotica

Disa obtusa subsp.

hottentotica, commonly known as the Hottentot's Orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of vividly colored, spurred orchid flowers, typically in shades of red, orange, and yellow.

The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its showy blooms and unique floral structure.

This species is also valued in traditional medicine and is considered a symbol of resilience in its native habitat.
