Diasperus oblatus

Diasperus oblatus, commonly known as the starry pincushion, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces showy, star-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple, with a distinctive pincushion-like appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Diasperus oblatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
