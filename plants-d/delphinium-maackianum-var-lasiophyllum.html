Delphinium maackianum var. lasiophyllum

Delphinium maackianum var. lasiophyllum, commonly known as the Maack's delphinium, is a striking perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, adorned with large, showy blue to purple flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped and deeply lobed, providing a lush, foliage-rich appearance. This variety is notable for its robust growth and ability to thrive in well-drained, loamy soils.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and is relatively hardy in temperate climates.
