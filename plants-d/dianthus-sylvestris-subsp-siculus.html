Dianthus sylvestris subsp. siculus

Dianthus sylvestris subsp.

siculus, commonly known as the Sicilian wild carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.

The plant produces clusters of small, brightly colored flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
