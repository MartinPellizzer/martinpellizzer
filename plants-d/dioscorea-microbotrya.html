Dioscorea microbotrya

Dioscorea microbotrya, commonly known as the African yambean, is a perennial vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and hairy, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by tuberous roots that are its primary economic value. These tubers are edible, starchy, and used as a food source in various traditional cuisines.

Additionally, the plant has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
