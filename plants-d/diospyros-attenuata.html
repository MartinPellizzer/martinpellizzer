Diospyros attenuata

Diospyros attenuata, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into orange to red fruits with a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor. Its fruit is encased in a thick, leathery skin and contains a single seed.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
