Dactylorhiza fuchsii subsp. sooana

Dactylorhiza fuchsii subsp.

sooana, commonly known as the purple marsh orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of purple or pinkish-purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, preferring calcareous soils. It has lance-shaped leaves and a complex root system adapted to its moist environment. Dactylorhiza fuchsii subsp.

sooana is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
