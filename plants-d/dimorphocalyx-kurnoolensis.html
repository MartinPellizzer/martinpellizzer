Dimorphocalyx kurnoolensis

Dimorphocalyx kurnoolensis, commonly known as the Kurnool velvetleaf, is a tropical shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive two-lobed leaves that are velvety to the touch. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
