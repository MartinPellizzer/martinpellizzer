Diospyros juruensis subsp. nenab

Diospyros juruensis subsp.

nenab, commonly known as the Nenab persimmon, is a species native to the Amazon rainforest of South America. It is a small to medium-sized tree characterized by its smooth, dark-brown bark and elliptical leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by edible, orange-red fruits with a sweet, astringent flavor. Its fruit is often consumed fresh or used in traditional medicinal practices by local communities.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and potential in agroforestry systems.
