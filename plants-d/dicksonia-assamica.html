Dicksonia assamica

Dicksonia assamica, commonly known as the Assam tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the Himalayan regions of India and Nepal.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests. Its rhizome is thick and fibrous, providing structural support and aiding in nutrient absorption.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, contributing to soil stability and providing shelter for various wildlife species.
