Dicranostyles scandens

Dicranostyles scandens, commonly known as the climbing moss, is a species of moss belonging to the family Dicranaceae.

It is characterized by its creeping growth habit and the presence of delicate, leafy shoots that often form dense mats on moist, shaded surfaces. The leaves are typically narrow and have a pointed tip, with a smooth or slightly ribbed surface. This plant thrives in humid environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices, where it can anchor itself to substrates.

Dicranostyles scandens plays a role in soil stabilization and moisture retention in its native habitats.
