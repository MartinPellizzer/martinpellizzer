Disperis discifera var. discifera

Disperis discifera var. discifera, commonly known as the African orchid or the blue orchid, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to thrive in well-drained environments.

Disperis discifera var. discifera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
