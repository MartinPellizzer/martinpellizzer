Dalea flavescens

Dalea flavescens, commonly known as yellow prairie clover, is a flowering plant native to the central and western United States.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and prairies, and it thrives in well-drained soils. Dalea flavescens is a perennial herb that produces small, pea-like pods containing seeds, and it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting pollinators.

Its ecological importance makes it a valued species in restoration projects and native plant gardens.
