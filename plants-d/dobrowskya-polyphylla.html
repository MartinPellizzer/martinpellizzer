Dobrowskya polyphylla

Dobrowskya polyphylla, commonly known as the mountain bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It features a basal rosette of hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces striking blue to violet flowers in late summer, which are clustered in dense, bell-like inflorescences. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in alpine gardens.
