Dactylorhiza praetermissa subsp. integrata

Dactylorhiza praetermissa subsp.

integrata, commonly known as the early purple orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its upright, branching stems and clusters of purple or violet flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and heathlands. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, delicate orchid flowers with three sepals and three petals.

This species is part of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its ornamental value and ecological importance.
