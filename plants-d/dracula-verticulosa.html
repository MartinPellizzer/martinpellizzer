Dracula verticulosa

Dracula verticulosa, commonly known as the "Queen of the Andes," is a striking epiphytic orchid native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, intricate flowers that resemble the mythical creature Dracula, hence its common name. The plant has a short, sturdy stem with dense clusters of showy, hooded blooms that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in its high-altitude, humid environment.

Dracula verticulosa is highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and rarity.
