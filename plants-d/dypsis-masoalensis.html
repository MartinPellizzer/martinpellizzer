Dypsis masoalensis

Dypsis masoalensis, commonly known as the Masoalensis areca palm, is a species native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its slender, tall trunk and pinnate leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in tropical rainforests. Its leaves are long and feather-like, contributing to its ornamental value.

Dypsis masoalensis is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant.
