Dodonaea eriocarpa var. waimeana

Dodonaea eriocarpa var. waimeana, commonly known as the Waimea Dodonaea, is a shrub native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and small, oval-shaped leaves with a dense covering of fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are often used in traditional Hawaiian medicine for their purported healing properties.

The species is adapted to coastal and dry woodland habitats, showing resilience to salt spray and drought.
