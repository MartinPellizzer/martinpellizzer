Darwinia rhadinophylla

Darwinia rhadinophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved Darwinia, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, showy spikes of pink to red flowers. The plant typically grows in heathlands and open forests, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a reddish or purple hue.

Darwinia rhadinophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
