Dioscorea bonnetii

Dioscorea bonnetii, commonly known as the Bonnet's yam, is a perennial vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and tuberous roots. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its tubers are used in traditional medicine and as a food source in some cultures.

The botanical features of D. bonnetii make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
