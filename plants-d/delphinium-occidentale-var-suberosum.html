Delphinium occidentale var. suberosum

Delphinium occidentale var. suberosum, commonly known as the western larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant produces clusters of showy, spurred blue to purple flowers during the summer months. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.
