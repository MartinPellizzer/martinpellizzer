Dioscorea esculenta var. spinosa

Dioscorea esculenta var. spinosa, commonly known as wild yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves, and it produces small, yellowish flowers. The plant's tubers are the primary botanical feature, containing compounds such as diosgenin, which are used in pharmaceuticals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Its morphology and chemical composition make it an important species in both traditional medicine and modern drug development.
