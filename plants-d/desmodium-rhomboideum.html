Desmodium rhomboideum

Desmodium rhomboideum, commonly known as hairy leadplant, is a perennial leguminous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are often covered in fine hairs, and its small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with Rhizobium bacteria in its root nodules. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained soils and full sun.

Desmodium rhomboideum is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardy nature and ability to improve soil fertility.
