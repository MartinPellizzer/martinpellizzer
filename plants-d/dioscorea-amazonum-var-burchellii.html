Dioscorea amazonum var. burchellii

Dioscorea amazonum var. burchellii, commonly known as the Amazon yam, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary part used for culinary and medicinal purposes. The plant produces small, green, heart-shaped leaves and yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. Its tubers are valued for their starchy texture and are often used in traditional cuisines.

The species is also noted for its potential pharmacological properties, making it an important plant in ethnobotanical studies.
