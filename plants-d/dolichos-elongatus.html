Dolichos elongatus

Dolichos elongatus, commonly known as the slender cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its long, slender pods that contain edible seeds, often used in various cuisines. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with trifoliate leaves and small, white or pale yellow flowers. Its root system helps fix atmospheric nitrogen, improving soil fertility.

The botanical features of Dolichos elongatus make it an important crop in agricultural systems.
