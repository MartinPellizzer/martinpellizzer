Dianthus caryophyllus var. puberulus

Dianthus caryophyllus var. puberulus, commonly known as the rough pinks, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, bushy growth habit and clusters of showy, double-petaled flowers that range in color from white to deep red. The leaves are typically narrow, grass-like, and covered in fine hairs, giving the plant its "puberulus" designation. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
