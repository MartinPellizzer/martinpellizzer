Diplazium taiwanense

Diplazium taiwanense, commonly known as Taiwan serrate fern, is a species of polypodioid fern native to Taiwan.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit. The plant features fronds that are pinnately compound, with serrated margins that give it its common name. Diplazium taiwanense typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas.

Its spores are produced in sori that are clustered on the undersides of the fronds.
