Dioscorea triphylla var. daemona

Dioscorea triphylla var. daemona, commonly known as the devil's backbone, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features striking, warty tubers that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by red, fleshy berries. Its unique morphology and medicinal properties make it a notable species in botanical studies.

However, it is important to note that all parts of the plant are toxic and should be handled with care.
