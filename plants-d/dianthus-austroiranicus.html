Dianthus austroiranicus

Dianthus austroiranicus, commonly known as the Persian carnation, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in temperate climates. It produces small, rounded leaves and has a compact growth habit.

Dianthus austroiranicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual interest.
