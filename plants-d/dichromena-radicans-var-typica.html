Dichromena radicans var. typica

Dichromena radicans var. typica, commonly known as the creeping dichromena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit, with trailing stems that root at the nodes. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its leaves are opposite and ovate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface depending on the subspecies.
