Decaloba oblongata

Decaloba oblongata, commonly known as the broadleaf forest fern, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, oblong leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in length, with a smooth, glossy surface. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in dense inflorescences. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in rainforests and undergrowth areas.

Decaloba oblongata plays a significant role in its ecosystem by providing habitat and food for various insect and animal species.
