Diplotaxis brassicoides var. longifolia

Diplotaxis brassicoides var. longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf rocket, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and pungent odor. The plant features basal leaves that are linear to lanceolate, with a hairy texture, and elongated stems that often branch near the base. Its flowers are typically yellow or white, arranged in racemes, and it produces small, rounded pods that contain several seeds.

This species is often grown as a ornamental or for its edible leaves, which have a peppery flavor similar to mustard greens.
