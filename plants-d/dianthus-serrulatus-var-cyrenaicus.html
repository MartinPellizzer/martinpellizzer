Dianthus serrulatus var. cyrenaicus

Dianthus serrulatus var. cyrenaicus, commonly known as the Cyprus carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features compact, upright growth with narrow, serrated leaves and displays clusters of pink or red, bilaterally symmetrical flowers. The plant is characterized by its dense, mat-forming habit and is well adapted to rocky and dry soils. It blooms in late spring to early summer, adding vibrant color to its natural habitat.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens and as a border plant.
