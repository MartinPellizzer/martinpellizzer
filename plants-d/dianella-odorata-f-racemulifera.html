Dianella odorata f. racemulifera

Dianella odorata f. racemulifera, commonly known as the fragrant flax lily, is a variant of the flax lily native to New Zealand.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and striking blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal and damp forest environments, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its inflorescence consists of a dense raceme of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and aromatic qualities.
