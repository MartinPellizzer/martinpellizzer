Dendrophylax flexuosus

Dendrophylax flexuosus, commonly known as the flexuous spider orchid, is an epiphytic orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its long, hanging stems and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and typically grows on trees or rocks. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Dendrophylax flexuosus is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
