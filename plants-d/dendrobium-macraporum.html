Dendrobium macraporum

Dendrobium macraporum, commonly known as the large-leafed orchid, is a sympodial epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features long, cane-like stems that can grow up to several meters in length, with clusters of fleshy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green, often with a distinctive red or orange lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining potting media.

Dendrobium macraporum is prized for its ornamental value and is commonly cultivated in tropical gardens and greenhouses.
