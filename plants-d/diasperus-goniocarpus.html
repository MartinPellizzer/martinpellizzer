Diasperus goniocarpus

Diasperus goniocarpus, commonly known as the African star grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its tufted grass-like appearance with narrow, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open savannas and grasslands.

Diasperus goniocarpus is noted for its ability to form dense mats, which can help prevent soil erosion in its native habitat.
