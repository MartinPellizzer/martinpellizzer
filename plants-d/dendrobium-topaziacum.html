Dendrobium topaziacum

Dendrobium topaziacum, commonly known as the Topaz Orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is an epiphytic plant, meaning it grows on the surface of trees or rocks without deriving nutrients from them. The plant produces strikingly colorful flowers, often in shades of yellow, orange, and red, which make it a popular ornamental plant. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in its natural habitat with minimal rainfall.

Dendrobium topaziacum thrives in warm, humid environments with indirect light, making it a favored choice for indoor cultivation.
