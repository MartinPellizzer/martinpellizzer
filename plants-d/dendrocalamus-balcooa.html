Dendrocalamus balcooa

Dendrocalamus balcooa, commonly known as the giant bamboo, is a tall, fast-growing grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that can reach heights of up to 30 meters. The plant's leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth, waxy surface that helps reduce water loss. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland and hill forest areas.

Dendrocalamus balcooa is valued for its strong, lightweight culms, which are used in construction and handicrafts.
