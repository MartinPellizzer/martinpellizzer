Dissotis spectabilis

Dissotis spectabilis, commonly known as the showy dissotis, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and South America.

It belongs to the grass family, Poaceae, and is characterized by its striking, inflorescence-like structures that resemble small, colorful flowers. The plant typically grows as a perennial herb, often found in moist, open habitats such as marshes and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its inflorescences are composed of dense clusters of bracts that are often brightly colored.

Dissotis spectabilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual interest.
