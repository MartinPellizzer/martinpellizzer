Darwinia hypericifolia

Darwinia hypericifolia, commonly known as the golden darwinia, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its striking spikes of yellow to orange flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in heathlands and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, aromatic fruits that attract birds and insects.

Darwinia hypericifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native gardens.
