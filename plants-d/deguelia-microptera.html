Deguelia microptera

Deguelia microptera, commonly known as the small-leaved deguelia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, showy flowers. The plant produces pod fruits that contain several seeds, which are often dispersed by wind.

It is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in disturbed soils, making it both ecologically significant and potentially invasive in certain regions.
