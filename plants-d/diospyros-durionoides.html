Diospyros durionoides

Diospyros durionoides, commonly known as the durian-like longkong, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other species like the ebony and persimmon. The plant is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and large, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green. Its fruit is a small, round drupe with a thick, fibrous husk enclosing a single seed.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has potential uses in traditional medicine.
