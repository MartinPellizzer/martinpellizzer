Dipterocarpus bancanus

Dipterocarpus bancanus, commonly known as the white ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, hard, and durable wood, which is highly valued for construction and furniture. The plant belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is typically found in lowland rainforests. It produces small, yellowish flowers and is known for its ability to thrive in humid, warm climates.

The species plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, supporting various forms of wildlife.
