Draba paucifructa

Draba paucifructa, commonly known as the few-fruited rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. This species is characterized by its low-growing habit, with basal leaves and small, white or pale yellow flowers. It thrives in rocky and alpine environments, often found in dry, well-drained soils.

Draba paucifructa is adapted to cold climates and is typically found at high elevations.
