Dombeya ciliata

Dombeya ciliata, commonly known as the silky dombeya, is a deciduous shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, appearing in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft, velvety appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Dombeya ciliata is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
