Diplazium anthraxacolepis

Diplazium anthraxacolepis, commonly known as the red-stemmed water fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are often submerged in water. The plant has a creeping rhizome that helps it spread along the water's surface or substrate. Its leaves are typically bright green with a reddish stem, giving it a striking appearance.

It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and oxygen for various aquatic organisms.
