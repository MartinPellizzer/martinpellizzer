Dianthus malacitanus var. valentinus

Dianthus malacitanus var. valentinus, commonly known as the Valentine's carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It features showy, double-petaled flowers that come in a range of pink and white hues, often resembling miniature carnations. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, blooming in late spring to early summer.

Its ornamental value and historical association with love and affection have made it a popular choice in horticulture.
