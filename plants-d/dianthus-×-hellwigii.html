Dianthus × hellwigii

Dianthus × hellwigii, commonly known as the hybrid carnation, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Dianthus chinensis and Dianthus barbatus, combining traits from both parent species. The plant typically features showy, bilaterally symmetrical flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, with prominent central stamens. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy, and it thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.

Dianthus × hellwigii is widely cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.
