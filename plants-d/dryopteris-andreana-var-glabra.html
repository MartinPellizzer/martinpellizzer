Dryopteris andreana var. glabra

Dryopteris andreana var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth male fern, is a variant of the male fern species native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, non-tuberculate fronds, which distinguish it from other varieties of Dryopteris. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments, preferring well-drained, acidic soils. Its fronds are bipinnate, with a distinctive, upright growth habit.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded garden settings.
