Delosperma adamantinum

Delosperma adamantinum, commonly known as the diamond flower, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, blue-green leaves that are covered in a fine, white powdery coating, which helps reduce water loss. The plant produces clusters of star-shaped, pink to purple flowers during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and vibrant blooms make it both ornamental and easy to maintain.
