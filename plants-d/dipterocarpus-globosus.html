Dipterocarpus globosus

Dipterocarpus globosus, commonly known as the globe dipterocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, rounded fruits that contain a single seed, and it produces copious amounts of nectar, attracting various pollinators. The tree has a straight trunk and can grow up to 40 meters tall, with smooth, grey bark. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and elliptic in shape, often with a waxy surface.

It plays an important ecological role in tropical rainforests, contributing to biodiversity and soil stability.
