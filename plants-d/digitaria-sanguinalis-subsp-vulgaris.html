Digitaria sanguinalis subsp. vulgaris

Digitaria sanguinalis subsp.

vulgaris, commonly known as common dogstail, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, slightly hairy blades that often have a bluish-green color.

The inflorescence consists of a dense, cylindrical spike that is typically reddish-brown in color. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and agricultural fields.

This plant is widely distributed and has ecological significance as a forage species and habitat provider for various insects and small animals.
