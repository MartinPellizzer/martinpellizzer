Diasperus myrtaceus

Diasperus myrtaceus, commonly known as the broad-leafed myrtle, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a small evergreen shrub characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants such as eucalyptus and clove. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.

The fruit, which is a small, smooth berry, is also utilized in local culinary practices.
