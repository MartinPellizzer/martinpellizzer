Diospyros multiflora

Diospyros multiflora, commonly known as the Indian persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptic to ovate in shape and have a smooth upper surface. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, round, orange-red fruits. The fruits are edible when fully ripe and have a sweet, honey-like flavor.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for the production of persimmons.
