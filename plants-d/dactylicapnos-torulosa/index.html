Dactylicapnos Torulosa

Dactylicapnos torulosa is a rare and striking plant native to the mountainous regions of central Asia. It is known for its unique, spiral-shaped flowers that resemble tiny, curled fingers. This plant thrives in cold, high-altitude environments where few species can survive. Its name comes from the Greek words for "finger" and "curl," reflecting its distinctive appearance. Due to its limited habitat and delicate nature, it is considered a vulnerable species in the wild.
