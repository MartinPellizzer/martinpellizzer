Digitalis tubiflora

Digitalis tubiflora, commonly known as the tuberous foxglove, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and striking bell-shaped flowers that range in color from purple to pink. The plant produces tuberous roots, which are adapted for storage and survival in dry conditions. Its flowers are typically arranged in a loose raceme, and it blooms during the spring and early summer.

Digitalis tubiflora is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
