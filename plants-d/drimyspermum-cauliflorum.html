Drimyspermum cauliflorum

Drimyspermum cauliflorum, commonly known as the calico flower, is a unique plant native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, brightly colored flowers that bloom in a calyx-like structure, a feature that sets it apart from many other flowering plants. The plant has a woody stem and produces small, fleshy fruits that are often consumed by local wildlife. Its botanical significance lies in its unusual floral morphology and ecological role within its habitat.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
