Diplacus fremontii

Diplacus fremontii, commonly known as the fremont's monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along streambanks and in open woodlands. It has opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture.

Diplacus fremontii is an important nectar source for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
