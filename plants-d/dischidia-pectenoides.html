Dischidia pectenoides

Dischidia pectenoides, commonly known as the Bird's Nest Fig, is a tropical climbing plant belonging to the family Araceae.

It is characterized by its unique, shield-shaped leaves that form a rosette structure, resembling a bird's nest, hence its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically followed by fleshy, berry-like fruits. It is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Dischidia pectenoides is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its ornamental foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
