Dicranopygium aurantiacum

Dicranopygium aurantiacum, commonly known as the red moss, is a species of moss belonging to the family Dicranaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, cushion-like growth form and vibrant reddish-brown coloration, which becomes more pronounced as the plant matures. The leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate, with a smooth or slightly serrated margin, and the stems are often branched. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices, and is often found in temperate regions.

This moss plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for microorganisms and small invertebrates.
