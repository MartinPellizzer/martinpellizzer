Desmodium discolor

Desmodium discolor, commonly known as the slender burr legume, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas. The plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. Its fruit is a hairy, three-lobed pod that contains several small, kidney-shaped seeds.

Desmodium discolor is often used in traditional medicine and can also serve as a forage plant for livestock.
