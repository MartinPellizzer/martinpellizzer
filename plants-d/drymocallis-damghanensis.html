Drymocallis damghanensis

Drymocallis damghanensis, commonly known as the Damghan goldenrod, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Iran.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
