Dichaea graminea

Dichaea graminea, commonly known as the grassy dichaea, is a member of the Bromeliaceae family.

It is a small, terrestrial bromeliad that typically grows in shaded, humid environments. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that form a rosette, often with a central water-holding cup. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, emerging from the base of the rosette.

Dichaea graminea is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
