Danthonia boliviensis

Danthonia boliviensis, commonly known as the Bolivian hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America, particularly Bolivia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grassland and wetland habitats. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Danthonia boliviensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
