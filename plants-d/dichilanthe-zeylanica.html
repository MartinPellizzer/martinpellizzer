Dichilanthe zeylanica

Dichilanthe zeylanica, commonly known as the Java grass or narrowleaf dichilanthe, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Dichilanthe zeylanica is often used in traditional medicine and as a forage crop for livestock.
