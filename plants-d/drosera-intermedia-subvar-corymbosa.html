Drosera intermedia subvar. corymbosa

Drosera intermedia subvar. corymbosa, commonly known as the clustered sundew, is a carnivorous plant native to Australia.

It features hairy, glandular leaves that secrete a sticky substance to trap insects. The plant typically grows in wet, boggy environments, where it thrives in the nutrient-poor soil. Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in a corymb-like arrangement.

It is a popular species among carnivorous plant enthusiasts due to its unique appearance and ease of cultivation.
