Dierbachia solanacea

Dierbachia solanacea, commonly known as the purple passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It belongs to the Passifloraceae family and is characterized by its striking, complex flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in culinary applications. Its botanical features include heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges and a vigorous climbing habit.

Dierbachia solanacea is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting pollinators.
