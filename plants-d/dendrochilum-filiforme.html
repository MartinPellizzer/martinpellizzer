Dendrochilum filiforme

Dendrochilum filiforme, commonly known as the slender-stemmed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a slender, upright stem with a few leaves and produces small, pale green or yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist forest environments. Its roots are often mycorrhizal, forming symbiotic relationships with fungi.

Dendrochilum filiforme is valued for its unique appearance and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
