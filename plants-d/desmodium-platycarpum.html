Desmodium platycarpum

Desmodium platycarpum, commonly known as the flat-fruit velvetbean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces small, flat, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Desmodium platycarpum is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in agricultural settings.
