Dactylorhiza incarnata subsp. coccinea

Dactylorhiza incarnata subsp.

coccinea, commonly known as the red helleborine, is a terrestrial orchid native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its striking red and yellow flowers, which bloom in late summer.

The plant has a rhizomatous root system and typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, often forming a basal rosette.

The species is notable for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils, showcasing its adaptability to specific ecological niches.
