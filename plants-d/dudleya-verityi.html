Dudleya verityi

Dudleya verityi, commonly known as the Verity's liveforever, is a succulent plant native to California.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are thick and fleshy, adapted for water storage. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers during the spring, adding a striking visual element to its appearance. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland areas.

Due to its unique beauty and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
