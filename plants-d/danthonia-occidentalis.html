Danthonia occidentalis

Danthonia occidentalis, commonly known as western tussock grass, is a perennial grass species native to western North America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and dense, fibrous roots that help stabilize soil. The plant produces spikelets that are typically pale green to reddish in color, and it thrives in moist to wet environments. It is often found in wet meadows, marshes, and along the edges of lakes and rivers.

Due to its ecological importance, it plays a vital role in supporting local wildlife and maintaining habitat diversity.
