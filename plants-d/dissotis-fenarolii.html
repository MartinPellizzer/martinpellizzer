Dissotis fenarolii

Dissotis fenarolii, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its dense, fountain-like growth habit. The plant features narrow, bright yellow inflorescences that bloom in clusters, creating a shimmering effect in the garden. Its leaves are long and linear, often with a slight waxy coating that helps it thrive in humid conditions.

Dissotis fenarolii is prized for its ornamental value and is often used in landscape design for its vibrant color and texture.
