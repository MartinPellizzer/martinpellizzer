Draba aizoides var. diffusa

Draba aizoides var. diffusa, commonly known as the alpine draba, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and pungent odor. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in well-drained, rocky soils. It has a low-growing, mat-forming structure with basal leaves and is often found in open, sunny environments.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens or as a ornamental plant in cold climates.
