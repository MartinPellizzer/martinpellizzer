Dolichandra uncata

Dolichandra uncata, commonly known as the golden bower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its distinctive, cascading clusters of yellow flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces long, slender pods that contain seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant blooms and unique growth habit.

Dolichandra uncata thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
