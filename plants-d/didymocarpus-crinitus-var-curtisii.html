Didymocarpus crinitus var. curtisii

Didymocarpus crinitus var. curtisii, commonly known as the hairy tamarind, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland areas with well-drained soil.

The hairy tamarind is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
