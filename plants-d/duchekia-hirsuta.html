Duchekia hirsuta

Duchekia hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy duchekia, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Brassicaceae.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Native to Central Asia, this species thrives in rocky and arid environments. The plant typically grows to a height of 10-30 centimeters and has a compact, bushy habit.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in ecological and taxonomic studies.
