Diapedium caracasanum

Diapedium caracasanum, commonly known as the Caracasanum fern, is a species of fern native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are often used in horticultural displays. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its rhizomes are creeping and produce roots that help anchor it to rocky or soil substrates.

The spores are released from sporangia located on the underside of the fronds, contributing to its reproductive strategy in humid climates.
