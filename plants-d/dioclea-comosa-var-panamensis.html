Dioclea comosa var. panamensis

Dioclea comosa var. panamensis, commonly known as the Panamanian climbing bean, is a member of the Leguminosae family.

It is a tropical vine characterized by its slender, twining stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its fruits are legumes, which mature into cylindrical pods containing several seeds.

This species is native to Central and South America and is often found in humid, forested environments.
