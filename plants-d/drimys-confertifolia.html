Drimys confertifolia

Drimys confertifolia, commonly known as the Chilean evergreen tree, is a species native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It is a evergreen tree or shrub that can grow up to 10 meters tall, featuring a broad, spreading crown. The plant has simple, alternate, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green on top and lighter underneath. Its flowers are small, white, and fragrant, typically appearing in clusters, and it produces black, spherical berries.

Drimys confertifolia is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and hardy nature.
