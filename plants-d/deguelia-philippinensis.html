Deguelia philippinensis

Deguelia philippinensis, commonly known as the Philippine glorybower, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in moist, shaded areas of lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered vulnerable and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
