Dampiera linearis f. humilis

Dampiera linearis f. humilis, commonly known as the dwarf dampiera, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Australia.

It features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers that bloom in spring, adding a delicate touch to its compact form. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native gardens and rockeries.
