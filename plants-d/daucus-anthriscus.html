Daucus anthriscus

Daucus anthriscus, commonly known as the European cow parsley, is a biennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the carrot family. Daucus anthriscus is also known for its aromatic qualities and has been used historically in herbal medicine.
