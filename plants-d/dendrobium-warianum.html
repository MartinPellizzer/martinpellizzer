Dendrobium warianum

Dendrobium warianum, commonly known as the Wariang orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dendrobium genus, which includes over 1,200 species of terrestrial and epiphytic orchids. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, with intricate patterns that attract pollinators.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is appreciated for its ornamental value in horticulture.
