Dendrocalamus longispathus

Dendrocalamus longispathus, commonly known as the giant bamboo, is a tall, fast-growing grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its robust, hollow stems that can reach heights of over 30 meters. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically green and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. Its rhizomes are thick and capable of producing new shoots, which contribute to its rapid growth and propagation.

This bamboo is valued for its strength and is often used in construction and handicrafts.
