Dypsis curtisii

Dypsis curtisii, commonly known as the Madagascar palm, is a member of the Arecaceae family and is native to Madagascar.

It is a small, tree-like plant with a short trunk and a crown of stiff, spiky leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers followed by dark blue, inedible fruits. It is adapted to arid environments and requires well-draining soil and minimal water.

Dypsis curtisii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique shape and resilience.
