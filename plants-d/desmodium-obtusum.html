Desmodium obtusum

Desmodium obtusum, commonly known as hairy desmodium, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, also known as the legume or pea family, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils, and it has a shallow root system that helps prevent soil erosion.

It is also valued for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, improving soil fertility.
