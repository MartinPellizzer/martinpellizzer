Daphne libanotica

Daphne libanotica, commonly known as the Lebanon daphne, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It features fragrant, showy flowers that range from white to pink, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has smooth, reddish-brown bark and lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value and unique fragrance, it is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and aromatic qualities.
