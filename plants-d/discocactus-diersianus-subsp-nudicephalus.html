Discocactus diersianus subsp. nudicephalus

Discocactus diersianus subsp.

nudicephalus, commonly known as the naked-headed discocactus, is a unique cactus species native to the arid regions of South America. It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are covered in sparse, white spines.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its otherwise sparsely adorned structure. Its subspecies name, *nudicephalus*, refers to its distinctive, nearly spineless head-like appearance.

This resilient plant is well adapted to extreme desert conditions, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.
