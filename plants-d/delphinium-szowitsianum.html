Delphinium szowitsianum

Delphinium szowitsianum, commonly known as the Tibetan larkspur, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, bearing clusters of striking blue to purple flowers. The plant has palmate leaves that are deeply lobed, and its flowers are typically arranged in dense spikes. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is adapted to the harsh conditions of its alpine habitat.

Delphinium szowitsianum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
