Darea thalictroides

Darea thalictroides, commonly known as the yellow cowslip, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often yellow or orange flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and the flowers are borne in racemes, often with five sepals and petals.

Darea thalictroides is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
