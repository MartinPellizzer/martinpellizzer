Dioscorea hastata

Dioscorea hastata, commonly known as the arrowhead yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its arrow-shaped leaves and tuberous roots. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant. Its tubers are edible when properly prepared and have been used in traditional cuisines.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires a warm climate for optimal growth.
