Dombeya pilosa

Dombeya pilosa, commonly known as the silky dombeya, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety foliage and clusters of fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Dombeya pilosa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
