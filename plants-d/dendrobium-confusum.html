Dendrobium confusum

Dendrobium confusum, commonly known as the confused dendrobium, is an epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with cane-like stems that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and pink, typically during the summer months. Its leaves are usually elongated and arranged spirally around the stem.

Dendrobium confusum requires bright, indirect light and consistent humidity to thrive.
