Delphinium elatum subsp. alpinum

Delphinium elatum subsp.

alpinum, commonly known as the alpine larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height, adorned with clusters of spurred blue or violet flowers.

The plant has lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its striking floral display and hardy nature, it is often cultivated in gardens and alpine gardens.
