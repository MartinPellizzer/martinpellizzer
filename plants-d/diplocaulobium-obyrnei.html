Diplocaulobium obyrnei

Diplocaulobium obyrnei, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a striking member of the Malvaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its large, showy, night-blooming white flowers. The plant has a woody stem and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds. Its leaves are palmate, with five to seven lobes, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

The African moonflower is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its unique flowering habit.
