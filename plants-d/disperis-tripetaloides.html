Disperis tripetaloides

Disperis tripetaloides, commonly known as the African orchid or the African violet orchid, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale blue in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Disperis tripetaloides is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
