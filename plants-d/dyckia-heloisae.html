Dyckia heloisae

Dyckia heloisae, commonly known as the Heloise Dyckia, is a species of flowering plant in the bromeliad family.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are covered in dense, fibrous material. The leaves are typically dark green with a waxy coating, and they form a tight cluster that helps retain moisture. It produces striking, red-brown flowers that emerge from the center of the rosette, often in late summer or early autumn.

This plant is well adapted to dry environments and is often grown as an ornamental in collections of bromeliads.
