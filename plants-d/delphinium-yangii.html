Delphinium yangii

Delphinium yangii, commonly known as the Yang's larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of western China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces showy blue to violet flowers in clusters at the top of the stem, typically blooming in late summer. Its leaves are deeply lobed and arranged alternately along the stem, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Delphinium yangii is valued in horticulture for its striking floral display and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil and full sun.
