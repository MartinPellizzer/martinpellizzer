Dorstenia lavrani

Dorstenia lavrani, commonly known as the "Lavran's false banana," is a member of the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence structure.

It is native to Central and South America, where it thrives in tropical and subtropical environments. The plant features a thick, fleshy stem with waxy, strap-like leaves that are often adorned with distinctive patterns. Its flowers are borne in a spadix, enclosed by a modified leaf called a spathe, a common trait among arum family plants.

Dorstenia lavrani is also noted for its ability to store water in its stems, making it somewhat drought-tolerant.
