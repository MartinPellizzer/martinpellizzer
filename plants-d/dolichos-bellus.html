Dolichos bellus

Dolichos bellus, commonly known as the beautiful bean or the golden bell vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its long, slender pods that turn a striking yellow or orange when mature. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three heart-shaped leaflets, and it thrives in warm, humid climates.

Dolichos bellus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can also be used in traditional cuisines.
