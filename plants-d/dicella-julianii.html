Dicella julianii

Dicella julianii, commonly known as the Julian's daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with white ray florets surrounding yellow disk florets. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It has a low-growing, mat-forming structure with hairy stems and narrow, linear leaves.

Dicella julianii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate, spring-blooming flowers.
