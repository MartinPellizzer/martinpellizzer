Delphinium subtubulosum

Delphinium subtubulosum, commonly known as the sub-tubular larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers in racemes, with five petals that are typically arranged in a helmet-like shape. Its leaves are compound, with elongated leaflets that are often hairy and have a distinctive serrated edge.

Delphinium subtubulosum is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, often growing in alpine or subalpine habitats.
