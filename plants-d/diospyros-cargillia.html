Diospyros cargillia

Diospyros cargillia, commonly known as the Cargill's persimmon, is a species of flowering plant in the Ebenaceae family.

It is native to the Pacific Islands, particularly found in Samoa and American Samoa. The plant is a small to medium-sized tree with smooth, greyish bark and elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. Its fruit is a small, orange to reddish berry that contains a single seed, and it is typically harvested when fully ripe.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in the region.
