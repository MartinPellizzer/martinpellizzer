Dendrobium fuscifaucium

Dendrobium fuscifaucium, commonly known as the black-faced orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with upright, cane-like pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant produces showy, dark yellow to orange flowers with a distinctive black or dark brown lip. It thrives in shaded, humid environments with well-drained soil.

Dendrobium fuscifaucium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
