Dendrobium fulgidum subsp. fulgidum

Dendrobium fulgidum subsp.

fulgidum, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia. It features long, slender pseudobulbs covered in a dense layer of light green to yellowish-brown hair.

The plant produces racemes of fragrant, golden-yellow flowers that bloom during the dry season. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining potting media.

This subspecies is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its striking, showy blooms.
