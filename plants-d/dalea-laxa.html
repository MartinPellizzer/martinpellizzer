Dalea laxa

Dalea laxa, commonly known as the slender milkvetch, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are alternate and pinnately compound, with small, oval leaflets.

Dalea laxa plays a role in stabilizing soils and supporting local pollinator populations.
