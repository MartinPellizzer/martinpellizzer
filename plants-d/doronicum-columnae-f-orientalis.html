Doronicum columnae f. orientalis

Doronicum columnae f. orientalis, commonly known as the oriental columnar bellflower, is a compact, perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, pale yellow to orange flowers. The foliage consists of lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. This variety is a compact form of the species, making it well-suited for rock gardens and alpine settings.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained, fertile soil.
