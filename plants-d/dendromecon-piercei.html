Dendromecon piercei

Dendromecon piercei, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb that grows in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by fluffy, seed-filled capsules that disperse its seeds by wind. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in dry conditions.
