Dipladenia acuminata

Dipladenia acuminata, commonly known as the rock jessamine, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a vine that produces clusters of fragrant, yellow flowers with orange markings. The plant has opposite, simple, and usually hairy leaves that are elliptical in shape. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its showy blooms.

Dipladenia acuminata prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
