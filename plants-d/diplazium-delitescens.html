Diplazium delitescens

Diplazium delitescens, commonly known as the fading fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that often appear translucent or pale in color. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Diplazium delitescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
