Dactylorhiza fuchsii var. meyeri

Dactylorhiza fuchsii var. meyeri, commonly known as the Meyer's marsh orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous habitats such as wet meadows and bogs. The plant has a slender, erect stem that bears several oval-shaped leaves. Its flowers are pale yellow to greenish-yellow, often with a darker lip, and bloom from late spring to early summer.

This variety is characterized by its compact growth and is often found in association with other orchid species in its native environment.
