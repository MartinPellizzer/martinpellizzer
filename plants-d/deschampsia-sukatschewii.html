Deschampsia sukatschewii

Deschampsia sukatschewii, commonly known as the Sukatschew sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to central Asia.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, meadows, and along riverbanks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It is often found in temperate and subarctic regions where the climate supports its growth.

Its ecological role includes stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
