Danthonia villosa var. stricta

Danthonia villosa var. stricta, commonly known as the hairy danthonia, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Australia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and hairy leaves, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grasslands and heathlands. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are small and tightly packed.

Danthonia villosa var. stricta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its hardy nature.
