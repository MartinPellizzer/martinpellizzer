Dalbergaria rubriacuta

Dalbergaria rubriacuta, commonly known as the red-tipped burr legume, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub characterized by its slender, woody stems and small, trifoliate leaves. The plant produces distinctive, globular pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Its flowers are typically pink or red, adding visual interest to its appearance.

Dalbergaria rubriacuta thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
