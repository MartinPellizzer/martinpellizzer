Dryopteris dickinsii var. namegate

Dryopteris dickinsii var. namegate, commonly known as the Namegate fern, is a rare and unique variety of the common wood fern.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that form a compact, upright rosette. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments, typically found in temperate rainforests. Its fronds are usually a deep green, with a slightly curled appearance that distinguishes it from other fern species.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
