Diospyros insularis

Diospyros insularis, commonly known as the island persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other species like ebony and persimmon. The plant is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green. Its fruit is a small, orange to red berry that ripens in late summer.

Diospyros insularis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
