Dactylis polygama f. petrakii

Dactylis polygama f. petrakii, commonly known as the Balkan meadow grass, is a perennial grass species native to southeastern Europe.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, slightly hairy leaves. The plant produces panicles of small, greenish flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in meadows and along riverbanks.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
