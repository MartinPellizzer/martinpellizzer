Deyeuxia trichodonta var. hirsuta

Deyeuxia trichodonta var. hirsuta, commonly known as hairy false rice, is a grass species native to various regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. It thrives in wetland or marshy environments, often growing in shallow water or along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems and a subject of ecological study.
