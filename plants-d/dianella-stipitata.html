Dianella stipitata

Dianella stipitata, commonly known as the slender flax lily, is a perennial evergreen plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves that form a dense rosette. The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers on long, slender stalks during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and forested areas.

Dianella stipitata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.
