Delphinium grandiflorum var. gilgianum

Delphinium grandiflorum var. gilgianum, commonly known as the Gilgian larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces showy, blue to violet flowers in dense spikes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets, and the plant prefers well-drained, alkaline soils.

It is often found in meadows and rocky slopes, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
