Digitaria disticha

Digitaria disticha, commonly known as flatpointer grass, is a species of grass native to Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It is a perennial plant that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides. The plant has narrow, flat leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.

Botanically, it belongs to the Poaceae family and is known for its rhizomatous growth habit.
