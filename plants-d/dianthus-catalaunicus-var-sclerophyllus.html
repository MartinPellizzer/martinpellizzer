Dianthus catalaunicus var. sclerophyllus

Dianthus catalaunicus var. sclerophyllus, commonly known as the rock campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and is well adapted to rocky and arid environments. The plant features narrow, grey-green leaves and produces clusters of pink or red, bilaterally symmetrical flowers. It is a perennial species that thrives in full sun and well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it a hardy and drought-tolerant plant suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
