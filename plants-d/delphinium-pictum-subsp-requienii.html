Delphinium pictum subsp. requienii

Delphinium pictum subsp.

requienii, commonly known as the alpine larkspur, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters.

The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers in racemes, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a coarse texture, often with a bluish-green hue.

This subspecies is adapted to high-altitude environments and thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.
