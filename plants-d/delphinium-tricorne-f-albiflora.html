Delphinium tricorne f. albiflora

Delphinium tricorne f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered thimble delphinium, is a cultivar of the delphinium genus native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that are predominantly white with a subtle blue or purple tinge, contrasting with its deep green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring a single, upright stem that bears a cluster of flowers at the top. Its botanical features include hairy stems and lanceolate leaves, which are typical of the Delphinium genus.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and its adaptability to well-drained soils in full sun.
