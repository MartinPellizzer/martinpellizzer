Disperis reklieberae

Disperis reklieberae, commonly known as the Reklieber's orchid, is a rare and endemic species native to the Eastern African region, particularly Tanzania.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique floral structure and intricate morphology. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist forest habitats, where it relies on specific mycorrhizal associations for nutrient uptake. Its flowers are small, fragrant, and often display a striking combination of colors, making it a notable species in botanical studies.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered vulnerable and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
