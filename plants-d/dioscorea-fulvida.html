Dioscorea fulvida

Dioscorea fulvida, commonly known as the golden yam, is a climbing herbaceous perennial native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its vigorous vine growth and tuberous roots. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its tubers are often used in traditional medicine and as a food source in some cultures.

The leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture and are often used in local herbal remedies.
