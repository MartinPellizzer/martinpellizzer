Digitalis purpurea subsp. bocquetii

Digitalis purpurea subsp.

bocquetii, commonly known as the Bocquet's foxglove, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe. It features tall, erect stems that bear clusters of striking purple to pink tubular flowers.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a serrated margin, typical of the Digitalis genus. It thrives in woodland or shaded areas and prefers well-drained soil.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens.
