Dioscorea calystegioides

Dioscorea calystegioides, commonly known as the Mexican yam, is a perennial vine native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and tuberous roots. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits. Its tubers are sometimes used in traditional medicine and as a food source.

The species is notable for its ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.
