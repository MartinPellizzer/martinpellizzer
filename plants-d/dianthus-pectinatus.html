Dianthus pectinatus

Dianthus pectinatus, commonly known as the pectinatus carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and clusters of vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant produces showy, bilaterally symmetrical flowers with five petals, and its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Dianthus pectinatus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticultural displays.
