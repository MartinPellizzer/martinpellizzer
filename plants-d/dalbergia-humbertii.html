Dalbergia humbertii

Dalbergia humbertii, commonly known as the Congo rosewood, is a tropical tree native to Central and Western Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its dense, dark wood that is highly valued for its aesthetic and commercial properties. The plant typically grows in humid lowland forests and can reach heights of up to 30 meters. Its leaves are compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, fragrant flowers that are often white or pale pink.

The species is also noted for its slow growth and long lifespan, making it a significant component of the region's biodiversity.
