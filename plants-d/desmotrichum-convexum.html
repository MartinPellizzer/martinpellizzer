Desmotrichum convexum

Desmotrichum convexum, commonly known as the smoothleaf sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is well adapted to drought conditions. Its leaves are usually smooth and have a convex shape, giving it its scientific name.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is often found in desert ecosystems.
