Dichanthelium dichotomum var. roanokense

Dichanthelium dichotomum var. roanokense, commonly known as Roanoke panicgrass, is a grass species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often branch at the base. The leaves are typically narrow and covered with fine hairs, and the inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets. This variety is adapted to moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and along stream banks.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
