Disa caulescens

Disa caulescens, commonly known as the Cape orchid, is a species of orchid native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and clusters of small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant prefers well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas. It is a perennial herb that thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it is considered rare in the wild.
