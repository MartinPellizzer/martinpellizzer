Drypetes lasiogyna subsp. affinis

Drypetes lasiogyna subsp.

affinis, commonly known as the red-flowered ironwood, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and distinctive red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant produces small, woody fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its hardy nature and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
