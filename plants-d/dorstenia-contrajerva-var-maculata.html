Dorstenia contrajerva var. maculata

Dorstenia contrajerva var. maculata, commonly known as the spotted false arrowhead, is a unique member of the Moraceae family.

It is native to Central America and is characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped leaves with a waxy coating. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, which are typically pollinated by insects. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The varietal name "maculata" refers to the speckled or spotted appearance of its foliage.
