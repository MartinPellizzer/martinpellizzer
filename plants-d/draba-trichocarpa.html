Draba trichocarpa

Draba trichocarpa, commonly known as the hairy rockcress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a small, herbaceous perennial that typically grows in alpine and arctic regions. The plant features basal leaves that are densely hairy and often form a rosette, while its stems are usually shorter and branched. Its flowers are small, white or pale yellow, and arranged in a raceme.

Draba trichocarpa is adapted to cold climates and can thrive in rocky or gravelly soils.
