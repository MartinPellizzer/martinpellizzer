Dioscorea chimborazensis

Dioscorea chimborazensis, commonly known as the Andean yam, is a tuberous plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its starchy, edible tubers that are used in traditional cuisines. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in mountainous regions with cool, moist climates. Its leaves are heart-shaped and large, while its flowers are small and usually yellow or green.

The tubers of Dioscorea chimborazensis are valued not only for their nutritional content but also for their role in local agricultural practices.
