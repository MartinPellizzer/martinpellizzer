Delphinium linearilobum var. hirticaule

Delphinium linearilobum var. hirticaule, commonly known as the hairy linearilobum delphinium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers arranged in dense, spire-like clusters, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This variety is adapted to rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for alpine gardens.
