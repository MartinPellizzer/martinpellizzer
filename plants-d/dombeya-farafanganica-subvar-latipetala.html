Dombeya farafanganica subvar. latipetala

Dombeya farafanganica subvar. latipetala, commonly known as the broad-petaled dombeya, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a subtle fragrance. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, and features lanceolate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are arranged in dense, terminal clusters, making it a striking addition to gardens.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated for its visual appeal and unique floral structure.
