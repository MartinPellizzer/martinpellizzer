Dioscorea dicranandra

Dioscorea dicranandra, commonly known as the narrow-leaf yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by tuberous roots that are sometimes used in traditional medicine. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a complex network of rhizomes, which contribute to its survival in arid environments.

The species is valued for its potential medicinal properties and is often found in forest understories.
