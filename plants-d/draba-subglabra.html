Draba subglabra

Draba subglabra, commonly known as the smooth rockcress, is a small flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with basal leaves that are usually smooth and glabrous. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers in early spring. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils, often found in alpine or arctic environments.

Draba subglabra is adapted to cold climates and is typically found in regions such as Europe, Asia, and North America.
