Diospyros monticola

Diospyros monticola, commonly known as the mountain persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and elliptical leaves with a serrated margin. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into orange to red, fleshy fruits. Its fruits are edible, though they are generally not consumed by humans due to their astringent taste, and are primarily eaten by wildlife.

Diospyros monticola is often found in forested areas and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
