Dendrobium gibsonii

Dendrobium gibsonii, commonly known as the Gibson's orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, cane-like pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, showy flowers in shades of yellow and white, typically during the summer months. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.

Dendrobium gibsonii is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for orchid enthusiasts.
