Didymocarpus corchorifolius

Didymocarpus corchorifolius, commonly known as the Indian gooseberry or amla, is a deciduous shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that turn from green to orange-red when mature. These berries are rich in vitamin C and have been used in traditional medicine for their antioxidant properties.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
