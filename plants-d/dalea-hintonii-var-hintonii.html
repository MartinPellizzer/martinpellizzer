Dalea hintonii var. hintonii

Dalea hintonii var. hintonii, commonly known as Hinton's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats, showcasing its adaptation to arid environments. Its small, showy blooms range in color from white to pink, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is noted for its ecological importance in stabilizing soils and supporting local wildlife.
