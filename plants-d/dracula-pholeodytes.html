Dracula pholeodytes

Dracula pholeodytes, commonly known as the Dragon's Blood Tree, is a striking member of the Apocynaceae family.

It is native to the tropical rainforests of Central and South America. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of dense, umbrella-like foliage. Its flowers are typically red or orange, and it produces a dark red sap that has been used traditionally in medicine and dyes.

The plant's unique appearance and ecological role make it a notable species in its native habitat.
