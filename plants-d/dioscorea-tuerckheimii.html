Dioscorea tuerckheimii

Dioscorea tuerckheimii, commonly known as the Tuerckheim yam, is a species of flowering plant in the family Dioscoreaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, particularly in countries like Sudan and Ethiopia. The plant is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary storage organs and are often used for propagation. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while the flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
