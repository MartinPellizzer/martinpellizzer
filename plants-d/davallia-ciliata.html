Davallia ciliata

Davallia ciliata, commonly known as the serrate holly fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are often covered in fine, hair-like structures. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and stream sides. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine in some cultures.

The plant's unique appearance and adaptability make it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
