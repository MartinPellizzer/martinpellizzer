Deyeuxia longiseta

Deyeuxia longiseta, commonly known as the long-haired sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to various parts of Asia.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along riverbanks. The plant has slender, triangular stems that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

Deyeuxia longiseta produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense spikes, which are typically wind-pollinated.
