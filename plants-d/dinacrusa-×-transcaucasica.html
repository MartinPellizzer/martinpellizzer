Dinacrusa × transcaucasica

Dinacrusa × transcaucasica, commonly known as the Transcaucasian bellflower, is a hybrid plant species native to the Caucasus region.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its erect stem and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to violet. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered with a fine layer of white hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
