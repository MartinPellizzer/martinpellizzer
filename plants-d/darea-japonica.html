Darea japonica

Darea japonica, commonly known as Japanese dandelion, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to East Asia.

It features a basal rosette of fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves that are typically green with a slightly woolly texture. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Darea japonica is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued as an ornamental plant in gardens.
