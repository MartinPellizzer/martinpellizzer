Ditrita rotundifolia

Ditrita rotundifolia, commonly known as the round-leaf ditrita, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, rounded leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks. Its botanical features include a rhizomatous root system and showy, bell-shaped blooms that range in color from white to pale pink.

It is often valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
