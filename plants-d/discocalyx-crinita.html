Discocalyx crinita

Discocalyx crinita, commonly known as the silky disc lily, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its delicate, hairy stems and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist habitats such as forests and woodland edges. Its leaves are lanceolate and often covered in fine, silky hairs, contributing to its common name.

Discocalyx crinita is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
