Dianthus juniperinus subsp. aciphyllus

Dianthus juniperinus subsp.

aciphyllus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved campion, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features compact, low-growing stems with small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has narrow, needle-like leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical characteristics make it an attractive and hardy addition to gardens with limited space.
