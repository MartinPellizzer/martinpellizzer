Danthonia carphoides

Danthonia carphoides, commonly known as silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, pale green to brownish flowers.

Danthonia carphoides plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
