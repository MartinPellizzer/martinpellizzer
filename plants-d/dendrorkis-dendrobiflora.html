Dendrorkis dendrobiflora

Dendrorkis dendrobiflora, commonly known as the two-flowered orchid, is a rare and specialized epiphytic orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically growing on tree trunks or branches in the canopy of rainforests. The plant is characterized by its compact, branching structure and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of white and pink. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and rain, making it well-suited to its arboreal habitat.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
