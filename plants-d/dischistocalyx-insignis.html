Dischistocalyx insignis

Dischistocalyx insignis, commonly known as the African cherry or yellowwood, is a small evergreen tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and alternate, simple, elliptical leaves that are glossy on top and pale underneath. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in panicles, which are followed by woody, orange-red fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal forests and woodlands.

The species is valued for its hard, durable wood and is also used in traditional medicine.
