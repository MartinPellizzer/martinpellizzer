Dischidanthus urceolatus

Dischidanthus urceolatus, commonly known as the water tank cactus, is a unique succulent native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bottle-shaped stems that store water, allowing it to survive in arid environments. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers during the summer months. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it has a thick, waxy cuticle to minimize water loss.

Dischidanthus urceolatus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and low maintenance requirements.
