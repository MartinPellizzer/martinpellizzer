Dipodium pictum

Dipodium pictum, commonly known as the painted fern, is a striking ornamental plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that display a gradient of colors, ranging from silver to deep green. The plant typically grows to a height of about 12 to 18 inches and thrives in moist, well-drained soil. It prefers partial to full shade and is often used in woodland gardens for its aesthetic appeal.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for both landscape design and container gardening.
