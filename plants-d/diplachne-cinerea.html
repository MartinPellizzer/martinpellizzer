Diplachne cinerea

Diplachne cinerea, commonly known as silvergrass, is a perennial grass native to Australia.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which results from the fine, silky hairs covering its leaves. The plant produces slender, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, wind-pollinated flowers. Diplachne cinerea thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

It is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.
