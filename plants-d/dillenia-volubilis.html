Dillenia volubilis

Dillenia volubilis, commonly known as the climbing dillenia, is a tropical shrub or vine native to regions of Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter. The plant produces showy, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its fruit is a fleshy, berry-like structure that contains several seeds.

Dillenia volubilis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
