Danthonia pseudospicata

Danthonia pseudospicata, commonly known as the spiky danthonia, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often bear a spiky appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and rocky outcrops.

Danthonia pseudospicata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its unique foliage structure.
