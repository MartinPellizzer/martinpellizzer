Diapedium laetum

Diapedium laetum, commonly known as the golden bell vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its pendulous, bell-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain seeds covered in a sticky, resinous coating. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a glossy surface, contributing to its aesthetic appeal.
