Dilochus

Dilochus, commonly known as the silverleaf nightshade, is a genus of flowering plants in the nightshade family.

It is characterized by its silver or grayish foliage, which gives it its common name. The plants typically have small, egg-shaped leaves and produce clusters of blue or purple flowers. They are native to regions in the Mediterranean and parts of Asia.

Dilochus species are often found in rocky or arid environments, showcasing their adaptability to harsh conditions.
