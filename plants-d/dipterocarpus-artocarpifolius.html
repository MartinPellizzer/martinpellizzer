Dipterocarpus artocarpifolius

Dipterocarpus artocarpifolius, commonly known as the yellow dipterocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its large, heavy seeds and buttressed roots. The tree can grow up to 40 meters tall and has a straight trunk with a rough, fissured bark. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a waxy coating, which helps in reducing water loss in humid climates.

The plant plays a significant role in the ecological systems of its native habitats, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
