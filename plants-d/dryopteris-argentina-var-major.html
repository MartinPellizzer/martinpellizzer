Dryopteris argentina var. major

Dryopteris argentina var. major, commonly known as the Argentine mountain fern, is a variant of the common wood fern.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile. The plant features a rhizomatous growth habit with fronds that are typically bipinnate, giving it a delicate, feathery appearance. Its leaves are usually dark green and have a leathery texture, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
