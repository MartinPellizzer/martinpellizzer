Dorycnium jordanii

Dorycnium jordanii, commonly known as Jordan's senna, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its typical pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant features trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in summer. It grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in dry habitats.

Dorycnium jordanii is valued for its ornamental value and traditional uses in herbal medicine.
