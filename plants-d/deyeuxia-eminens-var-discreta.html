Deyeuxia eminens var. discreta

Deyeuxia eminens var. discreta, commonly known as the slender tussock grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia, including China and the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, tufted growth habit, which gives it a distinctive appearance in its natural habitat. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences, typical of the Poaceae family, and is often found in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it thrives in temperate climates.

This variant is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
