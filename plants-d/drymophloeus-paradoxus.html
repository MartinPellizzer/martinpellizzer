Drymophloeus paradoxus

Drymophloeus paradoxus, commonly known as the paradoxical tamarind, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pod-like fruits. The plant produces yellowish flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences. Its fruits contain edible seeds that are used in traditional medicine and cuisine.

The species is notable for its unique growth habit and adaptability to various soil types.
