Delphinium albocoeruleum var. pumilum

Delphinium albocoeruleum var. pumilum, commonly known as the dwarf blue delphinium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Central Asia.

It features striking cobalt-blue to sky-blue flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and arranged in a basal rosette, with tall, slender stems supporting its showy inflorescence. It thrives in well-drained, loamy soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and delicate appearance, it is often cultivated in gardens and rockeries.
