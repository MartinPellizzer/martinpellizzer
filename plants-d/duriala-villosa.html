Duriala villosa

Duriala villosa, commonly known as the hairy duriala, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its flowers bloom during the cooler months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is often found in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
