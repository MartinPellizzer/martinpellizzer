Dierama reynoldsii

Dierama reynoldsii, commonly known as the Reynolds' dierama, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like leaves and showy, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Dierama reynoldsii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
