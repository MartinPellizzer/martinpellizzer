Diasperus salicifolius

Diasperus salicifolius, commonly known as the willow-leaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, willow-like leaves and produces small, yellow composite flowers. The plant is typically found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Its flowers are followed by fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds, which contribute to its ability to spread across open landscapes.

The species is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its resilience in harsh conditions.
