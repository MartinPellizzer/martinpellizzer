Ditassa fulva

Ditassa fulva, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling bells. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, with a smooth texture.

It plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators like bees and butterflies.
