Dendroviguiera sphaerocephala

Dendroviguiera sphaerocephala, commonly known as the globe-headed mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of various host trees.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly in countries like Brazil and Peru. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves and produces white or pink flowers that bloom during the warmer months. Its fruit is a capsule that contains small, black seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can affect the health of its host tree if left unchecked.
