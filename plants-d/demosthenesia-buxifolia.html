Demosthenesia buxifolia

Demosthenesia buxifolia, commonly known as the boxleaf demosthenesia, is a rare and unique plant species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form, with small, glossy leaves that resemble those of boxwood plants. The plant produces clusters of tiny, fragrant flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, Demosthenesia buxifolia is considered endangered and is protected in several countries.
