Diospyros rubicunda

Diospyros rubicunda, commonly known as the red persimmon, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes ebony and other related species. The plant is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface. Its fruit is a small, fleshy berry that turns orange-red when mature, often containing a single seed.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine.
