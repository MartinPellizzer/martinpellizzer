Dryopteris crenulans f. glandulosa

Dryopteris crenulans f. glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular wood fern, is a fern species characterized by its distinctive fronds and unique botanical features.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is native to regions of Asia, including China and Japan. The plant is noted for its glandular structures on the fronds, which give it its specific epithet "glandulosa.". Its fronds are typically bipinnate, with a leathery texture and a dark green color.

This fern prefers moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories, where it thrives in cool, humid conditions.
