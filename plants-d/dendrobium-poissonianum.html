Dendrobium poissonianum

Dendrobium poissonianum, commonly known as the Poisson's orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, cane-like pseudobulbs and clusters of fragrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in summer. The plant thrives in humid, tropical environments and requires well-draining soil and bright, indirect light. Its leaves are long and strap-like, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Dendrobium poissonianum is a member of the Orchidaceae family, known for its diverse and colorful floral displays.
