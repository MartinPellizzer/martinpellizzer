Dysoxylum papuanum

Dysoxylum papuanum, commonly known as the Papuan ironwood, is a tropical tree native to the rainforests of New Guinea and nearby islands.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood, which is highly valued for its durability and strength. The plant has compound leaves with long, slender leaflets and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Dysoxylum papuanum plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
