Dasylirion flexile

Dasylirion flexile, commonly known as the flexible dasylirion, is a perennial plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves that are often covered in a powdery bloom. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences bearing clusters of white or pale blue flowers in the spring. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Its architectural form and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.
