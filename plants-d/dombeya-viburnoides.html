Dombeya viburnoides

Dombeya viburnoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf dombeya, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Africa.

It features showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or white, arranged in clusters at the ends of its branches. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are usually hairy on the underside. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Dombeya viburnoides is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
