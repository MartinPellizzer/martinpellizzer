Diospyros peregrina f. javanica

Diospyros peregrina f. javanica, commonly known as the Javan persimmon, is a variant of the persimmon genus native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange to red, fleshy berries that are edible when fully ripe. Its bark is smooth and grayish, with longitudinal fissures, and it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and the nutritional value of its fruit.
