Draba muralis f. minor

Draba muralis f. minor, commonly known as the rock chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to rocky and alpine environments.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically has a mat-forming growth habit with slender, hairy stems and small, dark green leaves. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, appearing in early spring.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in crevices and on slopes in mountainous regions.
