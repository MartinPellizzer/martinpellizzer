Dictyanthus tigrinus

Dictyanthus tigrinus, commonly known as the tiger orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, colorful blooms that resemble tiger stripes. The plant typically grows in high-altitude regions with cool, moist climates, often found in cloud forests. Its flowers are bilaterally symmetrical, with intricate patterns of purple, yellow, and green.

Dictyanthus tigrinus is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
