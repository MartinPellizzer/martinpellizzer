Digitalis cochinchinensis

Digitalis cochinchinensis, commonly known as the Chinese foxglove, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and clusters of tubular, bell-shaped flowers. The flowers are typically purple or pink, with five lobes, and bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant contains cardiac glycosides, making it both toxic and historically significant in traditional medicine.

Its ornamental value and medicinal properties have contributed to its cultivation in botanical gardens and herbal practices.
