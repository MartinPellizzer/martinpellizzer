Dalbergia oliveri

Dalbergia oliveri, commonly known as Oliver's ebony, is a leguminous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its dark, dense wood that is highly valued for its durability and fine grain. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in humid climates. Its leaves are trifoliate, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, yellowish flowers followed by pod-like fruits.

The tree is also known for its ecological importance, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
