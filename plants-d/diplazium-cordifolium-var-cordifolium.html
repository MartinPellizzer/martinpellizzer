Diplazium cordifolium var. cordifolium

Diplazium cordifolium var. cordifolium, commonly known as the heartleaf staghorn fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It features a robust rhizome system that supports its growth on trees or rocks. The plant has broad, heart-shaped fronds with a leathery texture and a distinctive, branching structure resembling a staghorn. Its spores are produced in sori located on the underside of the fronds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appearance and is often cultivated in tropical gardens and greenhouses.
