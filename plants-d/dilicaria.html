Dilicaria

Dilicaria, commonly known as the false dandelion, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant is native to various regions of the Americas and is often found in dry, disturbed soils. Their botanical features make them adaptable and resilient, contributing to their widespread distribution.
