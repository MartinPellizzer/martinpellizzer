Diospyros merguensis

Diospyros merguensis, commonly known as the African persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and lance-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange to red, fleshy fruits. Its fruit is edible, with a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor, and is used in local cuisines.

The plant is also valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in traditional crafts and construction.
