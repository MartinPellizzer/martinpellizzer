Dianthus anticarius subsp. mentagensis

Dianthus anticarius subsp.

mentagensis, commonly known as the Mentagena carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces clusters of pink to red, bilaterally symmetrical flowers.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and drought tolerance.
