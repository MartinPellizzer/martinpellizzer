Delphinium burkei subsp. distichiflorum

Delphinium burkei subsp.

distichiflorum, commonly known as the Sierra delphinium, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that bear clusters of blue to purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in mountainous regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy and often vibrant flowers.
