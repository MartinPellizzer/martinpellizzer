Dodecatheon albidum

Dodecatheon albidum, commonly known as the white trillium, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It features a single, showy white flower with three petals and three sepals, which bloom in early spring. The plant has a rhizomatous root system and produces a single, three-lobed leaf that is typically broader than the flower. It thrives in moist, shaded woodlands and is often found in rich, well-drained soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance as a pollinator resource.
