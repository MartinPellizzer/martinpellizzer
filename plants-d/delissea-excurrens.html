Delissea excurrens

Delissea excurrens, commonly known as the creeping dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats. Its leaves are usually hairless and have a slightly toothed margin, while its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

Delissea excurrens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
