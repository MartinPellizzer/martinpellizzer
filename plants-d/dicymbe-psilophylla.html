Dicymbe psilophylla

Dicymbe psilophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leafed burr legume, is a small shrub native to the tropical regions of Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in dry, arid environments.

Its flowers are usually pale or yellowish, and it plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
