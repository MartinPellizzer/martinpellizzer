Drejera virgata

Drejera virgata, commonly known as the slender reed, is a perennial plant native to parts of Asia.

It typically grows in wet or marshy environments, showcasing its adaptability to moist conditions. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem. Its flowers are small and clustered, producing a delicate, feathery appearance.

Drejera virgata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in wetland restoration.
