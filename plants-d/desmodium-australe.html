Desmodium australe

Desmodium australe, commonly known as Australian needlepod, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and woodlands. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by slender, cylindrical pods.

Desmodium australe is often used in ecological restoration due to its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
