Dyschoriste fruticulosa

Dyschoriste fruticulosa, commonly known as the creeping dyschoriste, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its prostrate or decumbent growth habit, often forming dense mats. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on short pedicels. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic in shape, and have a slightly hairy surface.

Dyschoriste fruticulosa is adapted to grow in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal or arid environments.
