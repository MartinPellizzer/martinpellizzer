Dioscorea calcarea

Dioscorea calcarea, commonly known as the calcarea yam, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to regions of Africa and the Mediterranean.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary part used for medicinal and culinary purposes. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its flowers are small and inconspicuous, often greenish in color.

The tubers contain various bioactive compounds, making them valuable in traditional medicine and modern pharmacological research.
