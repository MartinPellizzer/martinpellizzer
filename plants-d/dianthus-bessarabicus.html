Dianthus bessarabicus

Dianthus bessarabicus, commonly known as the Bessarabian carnation, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Eastern Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright stems with narrow, linear leaves and produces clusters of pink or white flowers with five petals. The flowers are typically arranged in dense, rounded inflorescences, giving it a compact appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its vibrant, long-lasting blooms.
