Diospyros myrtifolia

Diospyros myrtifolia, commonly known as the Indian persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other persimmon species and ebony trees. The plant is characterized by its simple, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green, often with a waxy coating. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are followed by orange to red fruits that are edible when fully ripe.

Diospyros myrtifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in medicine and as a source of timber.
