Dioscorea palawana

Dioscorea palawana, commonly known as Philippine yam, is a climbing herbaceous plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary part used for culinary and medicinal purposes. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically unisexual.

Its tubers are rich in starch and have been traditionally used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
