Donax violacea

Donax violacea, commonly known as the purple donax, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, preferring moist to swampy soils. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a velvety texture.

Donax violacea plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and nectar for various pollinators.
