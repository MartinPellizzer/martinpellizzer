Draba incana var. magellanica

Draba incana var. magellanica, commonly known as the Magellan's whitlow grass, is a flowering plant native to the southern regions of South America.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically forms dense mats of foliage. The plant features small, clustered flowers that bloom in early spring, often in shades of white or pale yellow. Its leaves are usually hairy and have a distinctive, sometimes woolly texture.

This variety is adapted to harsh, alpine or rocky environments, showcasing resilience to cold and poor soil conditions.
