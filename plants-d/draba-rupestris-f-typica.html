Draba rupestris f. typica

Draba rupestris f. typica, commonly known as the alpine mouse-ear cress, is a flowering plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a small, perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments. The plant features basal leaves that are spoon-shaped and hairy, with small white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. It produces a capsule fruit containing numerous tiny seeds.

Draba rupestris f. typica is adapted to harsh conditions and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
