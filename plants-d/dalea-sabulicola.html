Dalea sabulicola

Dalea sabulicola, commonly known as sandbluff milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers clustered in dense heads, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it thrives in sandy or gravelly soils.

Dalea sabulicola is adapted to arid environments and is often found in desert or semi-desert regions.
