Dentaria macrocarpa var. pulcherrima

Dentaria macrocarpa var. pulcherrima, commonly known as the showy toothwort, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features striking, showy white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant has a unique underground structure consisting of a central, fleshy, tuberous root that supports its growth. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three heart-shaped leaflets, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in woodland gardens.
