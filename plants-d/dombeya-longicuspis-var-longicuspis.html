Dombeya longicuspis var. longicuspis

Dombeya longicuspis var. longicuspis, commonly known as the long-lobed dombeya, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring. The plant has opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves that are elliptical to ovate in shape. Its bark is smooth and grayish, and it thrives in well-drained, acidic soils.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
