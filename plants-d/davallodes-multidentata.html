Davallodes multidentata

Davallodes multidentata, commonly known as the many-toothed davallodes, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Leguminosae family and is characterized by its pinnate leaves with numerous small, toothed lobes. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by bean-like pods containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for study in tropical botany.
