Dracaena thalioides

Dracaena thalioides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf dracaena, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and long, narrow, dark green leaves that are often marked with silver or white stripes. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that appear in clusters at the base of the plant. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Dracaena thalioides is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in low-light conditions.
