Ditassa acerosa

Ditassa acerosa, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and has a compact, upright growth habit. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it produces small, inconspicuous fruits that disperse its seeds.

Ditassa acerosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
