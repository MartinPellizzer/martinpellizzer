Draba lactea var. pseudopilosa

Draba lactea var. pseudopilosa, commonly known as the white rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its leaves are basal and linear, while its flowers are white or pale yellow, often appearing in clusters.

This variety is distinguished by its dense, hairy foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance in its natural environment.
