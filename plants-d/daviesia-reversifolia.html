Daviesia reversifolia

Daviesia reversifolia, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, twisted stems and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The leaves are bipinnate, meaning they are divided into two sets of smaller leaflets, giving the plant a delicate appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
