Delphinium tsarongense

Delphinium tsarongense, commonly known as the Tsarong blue gentian, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Eastern Himalayas.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that bear clusters of striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its foliage consists of palmately compound leaves with lance-shaped lobes, and it is valued for its ornamental beauty in gardens.

Due to its restricted habitat, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
