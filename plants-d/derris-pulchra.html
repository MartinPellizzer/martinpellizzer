Derris pulchra

Derris pulchra, commonly known as the beautiful derris, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae, also known as the legume or pea family. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. Its seeds are enclosed in elongated pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

Derris pulchra is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some pharmacological significance.
