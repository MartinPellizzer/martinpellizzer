Descurainia pinnata subsp. filipes

Descurainia pinnata subsp.

filipes, commonly known as slender leafed tansy, is a perennial herb native to North America. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many mustard plants.

The plant is characterized by its slender, branched stems and pinnately divided, lance-shaped leaves. It produces small, yellow flowers that are arranged in racemes, typical of the family.

The plant is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as roadsides and fields.
