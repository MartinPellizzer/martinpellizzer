Davallia griffithii

Davallia griffithii, commonly known as the "lacy fern," is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Davalliaceae and is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that resemble lace. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, with creeping rhizomes that allow it to spread and form dense colonies. Its leaves are typically bipinnate, with a soft, velvety texture and a pale green color.

Davallia griffithii thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its aesthetic appeal.
