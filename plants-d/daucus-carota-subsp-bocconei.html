Daucus carota subsp. bocconei

Daucus carota subsp.

bocconei, commonly known as the Italian cow parsley, is a subspecies of the wild carrot. It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and small, white flowers that bloom in clusters.

This plant is native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It thrives in disturbed soils and can often be found along roadsides and in meadows.

The subspecies is closely related to the common wild carrot but differs in its smaller size and distinct floral structure.
