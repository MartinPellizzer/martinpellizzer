Desmidorchis tardellii

Desmidorchis tardellii, commonly known as the late-flowering orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to parts of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, often fragrant flowers that bloom in late summer or early autumn. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are grass-like, and the flowers are usually borne in racemes, displaying a range of colors from white to pink.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its ecological niche and floral morphology.
