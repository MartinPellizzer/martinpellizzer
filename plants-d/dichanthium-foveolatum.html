Dichanthium foveolatum

Dichanthium foveolatum, commonly known as the narrowleaf star-grass, is a perennial grass species native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in open woodlands and grasslands. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences with white to pale blue flowers. It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit, which allows it to spread and form dense mats.

Dichanthium foveolatum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
