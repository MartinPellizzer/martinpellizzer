Dryobalanops hallii

Dryobalanops hallii, commonly known as the Malayan ironwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark-colored wood, which is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant has large, ovate leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds.

This species plays an important role in the ecosystems of its native habitats, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
