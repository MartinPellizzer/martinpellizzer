Dichilus pusillus

Dichilus pusillus, commonly known as the dwarf lily of the Nile, is a small shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its delicate appearance and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

Dichilus pusillus is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.
