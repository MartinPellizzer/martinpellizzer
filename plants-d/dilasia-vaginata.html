Dilasia vaginata

Dilasia vaginata, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety hair. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Dilasia vaginata is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.
