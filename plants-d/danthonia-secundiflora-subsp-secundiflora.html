Danthonia secundiflora subsp. secundiflora

Danthonia secundiflora subsp.

secundiflora, commonly known as the narrowleaf muhly, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States. It typically grows in wet to mesic habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late summer. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in saturated soils and is often found in wetland ecosystems.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
