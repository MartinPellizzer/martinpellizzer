Drosera spatulata var. loureiroi

Drosera spatulata var. loureiroi, commonly known as the Loureiro's Sundew, is a carnivorous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its rosette of sticky, glandular leaves that trap insects for nutrient uptake. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in wet, acidic environments such as bogs and marshes.

Its unique adaptations make it a fascinating subject for botanical study.
