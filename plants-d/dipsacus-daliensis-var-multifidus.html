Dipsacus daliensis var. multifidus

Dipsacus daliensis var. multifidus, commonly known as the Dalii teasel, is a perennial herb native to China.

It is characterized by its spiny, bract-like structures that surround its flowering spikes. The plant produces small, pale purple to white flowers arranged in dense, upright clusters. Its leaves are typically palmately lobed and have a rough, hairy texture.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in textile processing.
