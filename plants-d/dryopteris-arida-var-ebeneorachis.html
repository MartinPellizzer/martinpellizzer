Dryopteris arida var. ebeneorachis

Dryopteris arida var. ebeneorachis, commonly known as the desert holly fern, is a variant of the arid fern species native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry fronds that are adapted to withstand drought conditions. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, showcasing a unique ability to survive in harsh environments. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds, which are protected by a layer of indusia.

This variant is valued for its resilience and ornamental appeal in xeriscaping and dry garden settings.
