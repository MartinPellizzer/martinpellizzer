Dactylorhiza × krylovii

Dactylorhiza × krylovii, commonly known as the Krylov's marsh orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of pale yellow to greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in wet, boggy habitats and is adapted to nutrient-poor environments. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, and it produces a single spike of flowers that bloom in late summer.

This orchid is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
