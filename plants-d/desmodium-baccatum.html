Desmodium baccatum

Desmodium baccatum, commonly known as cowpea or velvet bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces pods containing edible seeds that are rich in protein and used in various cuisines. It also has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a valuable plant for both agricultural and ecological purposes.
