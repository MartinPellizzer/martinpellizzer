Dinochloa utilis

Dinochloa utilis, commonly known as the water fern, is a species of aquatic plant that thrives in freshwater environments.

It is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that float on the water's surface. The plant is native to parts of Asia and is often found in slow-moving streams and ponds. Its reproductive structures are located on the underside of its fronds, which produce spores for propagation.

Dinochloa utilis plays a vital role in maintaining water quality by absorbing excess nutrients and providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
