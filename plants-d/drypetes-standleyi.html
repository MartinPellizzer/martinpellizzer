Drypetes standleyi

Drypetes standleyi, commonly known as the African mahogany, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and elliptical, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by orange-red, fleshy fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in moist, well-drained soils in lowland and montane forests.

Due to its ornamental value and timber use, it is sometimes cultivated in horticulture and agroforestry systems.
