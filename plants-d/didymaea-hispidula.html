Didymaea hispidula

Didymaea hispidula, commonly known as the hairy didymaea, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features slender, erect stems that are often covered in fine, hairy textures, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers clustered in dense inflorescences, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy surface, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Didymaea hispidula thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland or rocky habitats.
