Dicoma membranacea

Dicoma membranacea, commonly known as the Cape umbrella plant, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive umbrella-like inflorescence. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in coastal environments.

Dicoma membranacea is adapted to sandy soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.
