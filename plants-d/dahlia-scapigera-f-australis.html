Dahlia scapigera f. australis

Dahlia scapigera f. australis, commonly known as the Australian dahlia, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It features vibrant, showy flowers in a range of colors, including red, orange, and yellow, with a distinctive, single or semi-double bloom structure. The plant produces upright, branching stems that support its colorful inflorescences, which typically bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often grown in gardens for its striking floral display.
