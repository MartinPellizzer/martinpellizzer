Delphinium dasycaulon

Delphinium dasycaulon, commonly known as the tall delphinium, is a striking member of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters, adorned with large, showy blue or purple flowers. The plant features compound leaves that are deeply divided, contributing to its ornamental appeal. Native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

Due to its height and vibrant blooms, it is often cultivated in gardens for its dramatic visual impact.
