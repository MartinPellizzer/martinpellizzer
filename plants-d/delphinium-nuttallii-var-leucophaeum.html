Delphinium nuttallii var. leucophaeum

Delphinium nuttallii var. leucophaeum, commonly known as the white-leaved larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers in spikes, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a silvery or white underside, giving it its common name.

It prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
