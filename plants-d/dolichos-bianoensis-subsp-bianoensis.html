Dolichos bianoensis subsp. bianoensis

Dolichos bianoensis subsp.

bianoensis, commonly known as the white hyacinth bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical and subtropical regions. It produces small, white to pale purple flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by elongated, bean-like pods containing several seeds.

The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets and is often grown as an ornamental or for its edible seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, exhibiting rapid growth during the warm season.

The species is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which benefits soil fertility in agricultural settings.
