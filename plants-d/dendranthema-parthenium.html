Dendranthema parthenium

Dendranthema parthenium, commonly known as the coneflower, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring lance-shaped leaves and showy yellow to orange composite flowers. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in meadows and open woodlands.

The plant is also known for its medicinal properties, with its flowers used in traditional remedies.
