Dalea ayavacensis var. killipii

Dalea ayavacensis var. killipii, commonly known as the Killip's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, typically pink or purple flowers. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open plains. It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides nectar for pollinators.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies in its native habitat.
