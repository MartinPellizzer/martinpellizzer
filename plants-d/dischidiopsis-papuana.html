Dischidiopsis papuana

Dischidiopsis papuana, commonly known as the Papuan arrowhead, is a member of the Araceae family, characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves.

Native to the tropical regions of Papua and surrounding areas, this plant thrives in humid, shaded environments. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in a spadix, surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique foliage and ability to purify indoor air.

Its growth habits make it suitable for both indoor and outdoor cultivation in suitable climates.
