Datura ferox

Datura ferox, commonly known as the devil's trumpet, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces large, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically white or purple in color. The plant is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and can grow up to several meters in height. It thrives in warm, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed soils.

Datura ferox contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially dangerous to humans and animals.
