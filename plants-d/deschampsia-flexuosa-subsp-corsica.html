Deschampsia flexuosa subsp. corsica

Deschampsia flexuosa subsp.

corsica, commonly known as Corsican hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and slender, hair-like leaves that give it a distinctive appearance.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often in meadows, grasslands, and along streambanks. Its inflorescence consists of a spikelet cluster with elongated, awned lemmas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
