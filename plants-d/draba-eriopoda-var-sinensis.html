Draba eriopoda var. sinensis

Draba eriopoda var. sinensis, commonly known as the Chinese whitlow grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with basal rosettes of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in early spring, which are typically arranged in racemes. It is native to mountainous regions of China and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.

The species is often found in rocky soils and is considered a hardy, winter-hardy plant.
