Dodonaea viscosa var. stokesiana

Dodonaea viscosa var. stokesiana, commonly known as Stokes' devil's claw, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Africa.

It features small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from spring to summer. The plant has small, leathery leaves that are often glandular and can produce a sticky substance. It is well adapted to dry, rocky environments and is often found in coastal areas.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and coastal gardens.
