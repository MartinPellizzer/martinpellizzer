Dendrobium leucochlorum

Dendrobium leucochlorum, commonly known as the white-veined orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with multiple stems that can reach up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white and green, often with contrasting veining. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Its foliage consists of long, narrow leaves that help in photosynthesis during its active growth period.
