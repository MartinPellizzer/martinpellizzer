Diascia appendiculata

Diascia appendiculata, commonly known as the clustered sandverbena, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that typically reaches a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which are clustered in racemes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Diascia appendiculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
