Disa borbonica

Disa borbonica, commonly known as the Bourbon Disa, is a striking orchid native to the high-altitude regions of Madagascar.

It features tall, erect stems with showy, spurred flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant produces numerous small, oval-shaped leaves arranged in a rosette pattern. Its flowers bloom during the dry season, often in clusters at the top of the stem.

Disa borbonica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its vibrant floral display.
