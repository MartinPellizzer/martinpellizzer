Dianthus fruticosus subsp. karavius

Dianthus fruticosus subsp.

karavius, commonly known as the Karavius campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a low-growing perennial with fleshy, evergreen leaves and produces clusters of pink or red flowers.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal habitats. Its flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The subspecies is characterized by its compact growth habit and distinct, showy inflorescences.
