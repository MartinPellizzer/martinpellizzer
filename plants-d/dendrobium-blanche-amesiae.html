Dendrobium blanche-amesiae

Dendrobium blanche-amesiae, commonly known as the Blanche-Ames dendrobium, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is an epiphytic plant, meaning it grows on the surface of trees or other structures without deriving nutrients from them. The plant produces long, cane-like stems that can reach several feet in length, and it blooms with clusters of fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white, yellow, and pink. Its leaves are typically narrow and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for horticultural purposes.
