Dendrobium secundum

Dendrobium secundum, commonly known as the orchid with the peculiar growth of its stems, is a sympodial epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features long, thin, and often curved pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in its natural habitat. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and pink, typically during the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, arranged along the pseudobulbs, and it requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Dendrobium secundum is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
