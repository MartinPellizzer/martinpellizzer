Dioclea scabra var. schulzii

Dioclea scabra var. schulzii, commonly known as the Schultze's climbing bean, is a tropical vine belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its fruit is a long, slender pod that contains several seeds.

This species is native to South America and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties.
