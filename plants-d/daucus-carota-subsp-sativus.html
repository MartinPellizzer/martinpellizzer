Daucus carota subsp. sativus

Daucus carota subsp.

sativus, commonly known as the cultivated carrot, is a biennial plant belonging to the Apiaceae family. It is characterized by its taproot, which is the primary edible part, and its finely divided, feathery leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers in umbels, typical of the carrot genus. Its root color varies widely, including orange, red, yellow, and even purple, depending on the variety.

The plant is cultivated worldwide for its nutritional value and versatility in culinary use.
