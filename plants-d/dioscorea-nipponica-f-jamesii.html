Dioscorea nipponica f. jamesii

Dioscorea nipponica f. jamesii, commonly known as the Japanese yam or Japanese wild yam, is a tuberous plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its starchy, edible tubers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors, and can reach heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are heart-shaped and large, while its flowers are small and usually yellow or green.

The tubers of this species are used in traditional medicine and as a food source in some regions.
