Dendrobium alexisense

Dendrobium alexisense, commonly known as the Alexis's orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, cane-like pseudobulbs and clusters of fragrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in summer. The plant prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, often growing on trees or rocks in its natural habitat. Its flowers come in a variety of colors, including white, pink, and yellow, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Dendrobium alexisense is known for its resilience and ability to thrive in a range of environmental conditions.
