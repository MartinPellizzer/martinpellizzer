Dudleya abramsii subsp. affinis

Dudleya abramsii subsp.

affinis, commonly known as the Desert Stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green with a bluish tint, arranged in a rosette pattern.

The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange during the spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it well-suited for arid environments.

Its drought tolerance and unique appearance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
