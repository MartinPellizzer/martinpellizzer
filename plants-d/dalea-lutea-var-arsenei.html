Dalea lutea var. arsenei

Dalea lutea var. arsenei, commonly known as the yellow false indigo, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It features slender, branched stems and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with numerous leaflets, and its root system is fibrous and adapted to arid conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to native pollinators.
