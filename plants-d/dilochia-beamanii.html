Dilochia beamanii

Dilochia beamanii, commonly known as the Beaman's ghost orchid, is a rare and elusive orchid species native to the tropical rainforests of Queensland, Australia.

It is characterized by its unique, delicate flowers that resemble ghostly apparitions, often appearing in the understory of dense vegetation. The plant has a short, erect stem and produces small, white or pale green flowers with a distinct, intricate pattern. It is an epiphytic species, meaning it grows on the surface of trees without deriving nutrients from them.

Due to its restricted habitat and limited population, Dilochia beamanii is considered critically endangered and is highly sought after by botanists and conservationists.
