Dactylorhiza × fourkensis

Dactylorhiza × fourkensis, commonly known as the hybrid marsh orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to western Europe.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Dactylorhiza maculata and Dactylorhiza viridiflora. The plant typically grows in wet, peaty or marshy habitats and has tall, erect stems with lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish and are arranged in a loose spike.

Dactylorhiza × fourkensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with moist, acidic soil.
