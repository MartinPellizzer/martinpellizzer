Dichapetalum fuscescens

Dichapetalum fuscescens, commonly known as the blackwood tree, is a deciduous tree native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black bark and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually oval or elliptical in shape. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by small, round fruits.

The tree is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its hard, durable wood.
