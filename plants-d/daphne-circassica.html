Daphne circassica

Daphne circassica, commonly known as the Caucasian daphne, is a deciduous shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It features fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, blooming in early spring. The plant has smooth, gray-green leaves that are opposite in arrangement. Its stems are typically woody and covered in a fine, powdery coating.

Daphne circassica is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
