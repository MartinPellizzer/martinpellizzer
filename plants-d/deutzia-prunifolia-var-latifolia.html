Deutzia prunifolia var. latifolia

Deutzia prunifolia var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf deutzia, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features upright, multi-stemmed growth with ovate to elliptic leaves that are typically 3-6 inches long. The plant produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers in late spring to early summer, which are borne on slender pedicels. Its foliage turns a striking red or bronze in the fall, adding ornamental value.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a versatile choice for garden landscapes.
