Draba aurea var. neomexicana

Draba aurea var. neomexicana, commonly known as the New Mexico golden draba, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with basal leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and montane environments. It has a compact growth habit and is adapted to harsh, arid conditions.

Its flowers are typically clustered in dense inflorescences, and it plays a role in stabilizing soils in its native habitat.
