Dioscorea hispida var. neoscaphoides

Dioscorea hispida var. neoscaphoides, commonly known as the hairy yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by tuberous roots that are often used in traditional medicine. Its botanical structure includes a rhizomatous root system and adventitious roots that aid in climbing.

The species is valued for its medicinal properties and is also used as a food source in some cultures.
