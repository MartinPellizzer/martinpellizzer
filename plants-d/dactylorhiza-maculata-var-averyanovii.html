Dactylorhiza maculata var. averyanovii

Dactylorhiza maculata var. averyanovii, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, spotted flowers that typically bloom in late summer. The plant prefers moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, and it produces small, greenish or brownish flowers arranged in a spike.

This variety is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
