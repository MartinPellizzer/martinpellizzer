Duckea

Duckea, commonly known as the "Chinese cabbage" or "Napa cabbage," is a leafy green vegetable native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous vegetables like broccoli and kale. The plant has thick, pale green stalks with tightly wrapped, tender leaves that are often eaten raw or cooked. Duckea is rich in vitamins A, C, and K, as well as dietary fiber and antioxidants.

Its botanical structure supports its use in both culinary and medicinal applications across various cultures.
