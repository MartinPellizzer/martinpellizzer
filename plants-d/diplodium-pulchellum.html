Diplodium pulchellum

Diplodium pulchellum, commonly known as the beautiful Diplodium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its legume fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows in grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Diplodium pulchellum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
