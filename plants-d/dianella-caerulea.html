Dianella caerulea

Dianella caerulea, commonly known as the blue-flowered flax lily, is a striking evergreen perennial native to eastern Australia.

It features grass-like, narrow leaves that are typically bluish-green in color, and it produces spikes of blue or purple flowers in spring. The plant is well adapted to coastal environments and can thrive in sandy or rocky soils. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate salt spray.

Dianella caerulea is also valued for its resilience and low maintenance requirements.
