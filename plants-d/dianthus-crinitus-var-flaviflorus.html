Dianthus crinitus var. flaviflorus

Dianthus crinitus var. flaviflorus, commonly known as the golden flaxflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from pale yellow to orange. The plant produces small, oval-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding a vibrant splash of color to gardens.
