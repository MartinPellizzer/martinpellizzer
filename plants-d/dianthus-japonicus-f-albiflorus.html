Dianthus japonicus f. albiflorus

Dianthus japonicus f. albiflorus, commonly known as the Japanese carnation or white-flowered carnation, is a flowering plant characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, clustered flowers.

It features showy, white to pale pink petals that are typically arranged in a star-like pattern, with prominent, often red or purple stamens. The plant has linear, hairy leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It is native to Japan and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation in temperate climates.
