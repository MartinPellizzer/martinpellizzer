Dianthus pruinosus

Dianthus pruinosus, commonly known as the velvet pinks, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and clusters of pink or red flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters and thrives in well-drained soil. It is a hardy perennial that prefers full sun and is often used in rock gardens or as a border plant.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding a vibrant splash of color to the garden.
