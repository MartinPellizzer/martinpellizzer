Drimopogon glomerata

Drimopogon glomerata, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in grasslands and rocky soils, thriving in well-drained conditions. The plant features wiry stems that bear clusters of small, bell-shaped blue or purple flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming a basal rosette.

Drimopogon glomerata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
