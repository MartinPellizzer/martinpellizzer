Duboisia campbellii

Duboisia campbellii, commonly known as the Campbell's ghost gum, is a unique species of flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. The plant has a slender, twisted trunk and produces small, round, woody fruits that contain seeds. Duboisia campbellii is adapted to arid environments and is drought-tolerant, making it a hardy plant for dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value has made it a popular choice for horticultural use in regions with suitable climates.
