Dendrobium tricarinatum

Dendrobium tricarinatum, commonly known as the three-ribbed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with elongated, cane-like pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white, yellow, and orange, often with intricate patterns. Its leaves are usually broad and grass-like, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Dendrobium tricarinatum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and adaptability to cultivation.
