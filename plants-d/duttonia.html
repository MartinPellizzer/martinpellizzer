Duttonia

Duttonia, commonly known as the spider orchid, is a genus of flowering plants in the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is native to Australia and is characterized by its unique, spider-like appearance, with long, slender petals and a distinctive central column. The plants typically grow in dry, rocky habitats and are adapted to arid conditions. Duttonia species are known for their striking, often colorful flowers that bloom in spring or summer.

They are popular in horticulture due to their ornamental value and ease of cultivation.
