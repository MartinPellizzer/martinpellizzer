Dactylis glomerata nothosubsp. intercedens

Dactylis glomerata nothosubsp.

intercedens, commonly known as the intermediate or hybrid meadow grass, is a perennial grass species characterized by its tufted growth habit and dense, spike-like inflorescences. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in moist meadows and grasslands.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, wind-pollinated flowers in early summer. It is valued for its use in forage and soil stabilization due to its robust growth and adaptability.
