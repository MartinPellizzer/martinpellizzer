Dudleya multicaulis

Dudleya multicaulis, commonly known as the branched rockrose, is a succulent plant native to California and Baja California.

It features a low-growing, mat-forming structure with thick, fleshy leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces striking clusters of yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is well adapted to rocky and coastal environments, thriving in full sun and well-draining soil.

Dudleya multicaulis is a popular choice for rock gardens due to its hardiness and ornamental appeal.
