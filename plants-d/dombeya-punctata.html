Dombeya punctata

Dombeya punctata, commonly known as the spotted dombeya, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters of white or pale pink, often with a distinctive red or orange center. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile choice for gardens.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its long-lasting blooms.
