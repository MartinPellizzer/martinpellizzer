Dyckia edwardii

Dyckia edwardii, commonly known as Edward's dyckia, is a striking bromeliad native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of white, powdery bloom, which helps to reflect sunlight and reduce water loss. The plant produces striking inflorescences that can reach up to 2 meters in height, bearing bright orange or red flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Dyckia edwardii is a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor cultivation due to its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
