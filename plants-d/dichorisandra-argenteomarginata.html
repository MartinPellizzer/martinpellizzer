Dichorisandra argenteomarginata

Dichorisandra argenteomarginata, commonly known as the silver-edged poinsettia, is a striking evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with distinctive silver margins that give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, inconspicuous white flowers surrounded by colorful bracts that range from red to orange. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and indoor spaces.
