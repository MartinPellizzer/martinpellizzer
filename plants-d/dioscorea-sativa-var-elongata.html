Dioscorea sativa var. elongata

Dioscorea sativa var. elongata, commonly known as the long-leaf yam, is a tuberous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its elongated, cylindrical tubers that can grow up to several feet in length. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth. Its leaves are typically large, heart-shaped, and have a rough texture, while the flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant.

The tubers are valued for their nutritional content and are used in traditional cuisines and medicinal practices.
