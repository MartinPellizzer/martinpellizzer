Deguelia martynii

Deguelia martynii, commonly known as the yellow bell tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling bells, hence its common name. The plant has compound leaves with numerous leaflets and produces small, woody pods that contain seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in tropical gardens for its vibrant floral display.
