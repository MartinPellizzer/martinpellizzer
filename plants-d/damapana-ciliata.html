Damapana ciliata

Damapana ciliata, commonly known as the silky leaf vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender stems and heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
