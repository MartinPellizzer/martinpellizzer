Dracophyllum procerum

Dracophyllum procerum, commonly known as the slender dracophyllum, is a shrub native to the south-western regions of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, clustered flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are leathery and aromatic, often used in traditional Aboriginal medicine.

Dracophyllum procerum plays an important role in its native ecosystem, providing habitat and food for various local fauna.
