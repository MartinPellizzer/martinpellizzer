Diplopappus umbellatus

Diplopappus umbellatus, commonly known as the umbrella tree, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a single, upright stem with opposite, oval-shaped leaves that are hairy and glandular. Its most distinctive feature is the umbellate inflorescence, consisting of numerous small yellow flowers arranged in a rounded cluster.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun.
