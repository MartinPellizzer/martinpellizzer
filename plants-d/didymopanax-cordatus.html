Didymopanax cordatus

Didymopanax cordatus, commonly known as the heartleaf sycamore, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by edible, round fruits that contain a single seed. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and horticulture.
