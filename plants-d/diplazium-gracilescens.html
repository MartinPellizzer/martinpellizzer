Diplazium gracilescens

Diplazium gracilescens, commonly known as the slender cliff fern, is a perennial fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its delicate, slender fronds that grow in a compact, rosette-like formation. The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments such as rocky crevices or forest floors. Its rhizomes are wiry and often covered in a thin layer of rhizomorphs, which help in anchoring the plant and absorbing nutrients.

The spores are produced in sori that are clustered on the undersides of the fronds, contributing to its reproductive strategy.
