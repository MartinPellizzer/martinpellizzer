Diomedea bidentata

Diomedea bidentata, commonly known as the black-browed albatross, is a large seabird native to the Southern Ocean.

It is characterized by its distinctive black and white plumage, with a prominent dark brow stripe. The bird has a long wingspan, typically reaching up to 2.5 meters, which aids in its ability to soar over vast oceanic distances. Its beak is robust and hooked, adapted for catching fish and squid in the open sea.

Diomedea bidentata plays a crucial role in marine ecosystems as a top predator and is also an indicator species for the health of the Southern Ocean.
