Dalea lasiathera

Dalea lasiathera, commonly known as the hairy milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with hairy surfaces and its distinctive pink to reddish-purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides forage for wildlife.

Dalea lasiathera is also valued for its ornamental appeal in native plant gardens.
