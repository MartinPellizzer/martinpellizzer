Duroia hirsuta

Duroia hirsuta, commonly known as the hirsute wax plant, is a tropical shrub native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often covered in fine, hairy structures, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white in color. Its growth habit is compact, with a dense foliage that helps it thrive in the understory of humid environments.

Duroia hirsuta is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
