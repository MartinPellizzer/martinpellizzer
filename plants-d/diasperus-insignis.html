Diasperus insignis

Diasperus insignis, commonly known as the desert lily, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb with a robust root system that allows it to survive in harsh, dry conditions. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often blooming in spring. Its leaves are typically narrow and succulent, adapted to minimize water loss.

Diasperus insignis plays an important ecological role by providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a food source for desert wildlife.
