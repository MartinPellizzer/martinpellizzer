Dioscorea polyphylla

Dioscorea polyphylla, commonly known as the giant yam, is a perennial vine native to the tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in diameter. The plant produces yellowish flowers followed by edible tuberous roots, which are often used in traditional cuisines. Its rhizomes contain compounds that have been studied for potential medicinal properties.

Dioscorea polyphylla thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.
