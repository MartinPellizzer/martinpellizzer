Drymophloeus montanus

Drymophloeus montanus, commonly known as the mountain drymophloeus, is a small evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in humid, shaded environments within tropical and subtropical forests. The plant features simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a leathery texture. Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in yellow or greenish-white hues.

The fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.
