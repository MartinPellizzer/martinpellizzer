Desmodium sericophyllum var. strobilaceum

Desmodium sericophyllum var. strobilaceum, commonly known as the silky desmodium, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in grasslands and open woodlands, thriving in well-drained soils. Its seeds are enclosed in hairy pods, which help in seed dispersal.

The species is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
