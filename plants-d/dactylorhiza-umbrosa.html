Dactylorhiza umbrosa

Dactylorhiza umbrosa, commonly known as the dark orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the temperate regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of deep purple to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has linear, grass-like leaves and produces a single spike of flowers that can reach up to 30 cm in height. It thrives in moist, shaded habitats such as woodland floors and damp meadows.

Dactylorhiza umbrosa is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its ornamental value and ecological importance.
