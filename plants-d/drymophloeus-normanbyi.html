Drymophloeus normanbyi

Drymophloeus normanbyi, commonly known as the Normanby's ironwood, is a tropical tree native to the rainforests of northern Queensland, Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood and can grow up to 30 meters in height. The plant has compound leaves that are typically alternate and have a glossy appearance. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody pods containing flat, bean-like seeds.

The species is valued for its durable timber and plays a significant role in the ecological structure of its habitat.
