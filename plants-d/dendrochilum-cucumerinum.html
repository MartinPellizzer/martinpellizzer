Dendrochilum cucumerinum

Dendrochilum cucumerinum, commonly known as the cucumber orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, vine-like stems that can climb or trail along the ground. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers that are often fragrant. Its leaves are typically elongated and have a waxy texture, which helps in reducing water loss.

Dendrochilum cucumerinum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable growing conditions.
