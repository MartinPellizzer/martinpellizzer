Daviesia buxifolia

Daviesia buxifolia, commonly known as the box-leaved daviesia, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant features small, rounded leaves that resemble those of boxwood, giving it its common name. It produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in spherical inflorescences.

Daviesia buxifolia is often found in heathlands and rocky soils, thriving in full sun to partial shade.
