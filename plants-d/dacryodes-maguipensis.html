Dacryodes maguipensis

Dacryodes maguipensis, commonly known as the Guianan ironwood, is a tropical tree native to South America, particularly found in Guyana and Suriname.

It is characterized by its dense, dark-brown wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a glossy upper surface. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by spherical, woody fruits containing a single seed.

The species is notable for its ecological importance in rainforest ecosystems and its role in traditional medicine.
