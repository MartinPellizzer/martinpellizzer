Dioscorea pentaphylla var. sacerdotalis

Dioscorea pentaphylla var. sacerdotalis, commonly known as the sacred yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by tuberous roots that are highly valued for their medicinal properties. Its tubers are used in traditional medicine to treat various ailments, including inflammation and infections.

The species is also noted for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and vigorous growth habit.
