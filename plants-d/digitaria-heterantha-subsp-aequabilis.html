Digitaria heterantha subsp. aequabilis

Digitaria heterantha subsp.

aequabilis, commonly known as the smooth flatsedge, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions. It is characterized by its slender, flat, and often hairy stems that grow close to the ground.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are narrow and blade-like, with a smooth texture and a prominent midrib.

It thrives in wetland habitats and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies.
