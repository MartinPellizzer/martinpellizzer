Dampiera subverticillata

Dampiera subverticillata, commonly known as the mountain dampiera, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It is a perennial herb with a compact, low-growing habit, typically reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, tubular flowers in shades of blue or purple. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowers.
