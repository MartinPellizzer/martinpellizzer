Dianthus denaicus

Dianthus denaicus, commonly known as the Armenian carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink or white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and has narrow, linear leaves. Its flowers are composed of five petals and are often arranged in clusters at the top of the stems.

Dianthus denaicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
