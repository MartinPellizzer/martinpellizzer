Daphne mucronata var. affghanica

Daphne mucronata var. affghanica, commonly known as the Afghan Daphne, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features dense, evergreen foliage with leathery, oval-shaped leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white or pale pink flowers in early spring, which are followed by small, fleshy berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is a popular choice among gardeners in temperate climates.
