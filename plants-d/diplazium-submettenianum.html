Diplazium submettenianum

Diplazium submettenianum, commonly known as the broadleaf button fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Davalliaceae and is characterized by its robust, upright growth habit. The plant has dark green, leathery fronds with a distinctive, button-like appearance at the base. Its spores are produced on the undersides of the fronds in clusters called sori.

Diplazium submettenianum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
