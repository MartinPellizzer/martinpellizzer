Damnacanthus macrophyllus var. giganteus

Damnacanthus macrophyllus var. giganteus, commonly known as the giant flame acanthus, is a striking perennial plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in diameter, giving it a dramatic appearance. The plant produces showy, red-orange bracts that resemble flowers, though they are actually modified leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Due to its size and ornamental value, it is often used in gardens and as a landscape specimen.
