Dinemandra ericoides

Dinemandra ericoides, commonly known as the coastal heath, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, needle-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is adapted to coastal conditions with high salt exposure.

Dinemandra ericoides plays an important role in stabilizing dune ecosystems and providing habitat for local wildlife.
