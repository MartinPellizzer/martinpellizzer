Dicliptera cabrerae

Dicliptera cabrerae, commonly known as the Cabrera's dicliptera, is a perennial shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as a ornamental plant in warm climates.
