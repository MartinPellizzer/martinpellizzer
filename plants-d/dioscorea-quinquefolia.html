Dioscorea quinquefolia

Dioscorea quinquefolia, commonly known as wild yam, is a perennial vine native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, typically with five leaflets, and produces small, yellowish flowers. The plant is known for its tuberous roots, which are rich in compounds like diosgenin. These roots have been used traditionally in herbal medicine and are a source of steroid saponins.

Its botanical significance extends to its role in the development of contraceptive medications.
