Dioscorea bulbifera var. suavia

Dioscorea bulbifera var. suavia, commonly known as the sweet potato vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves and the production of edible tubers that are rich in carbohydrates. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. It is often cultivated for its ornamental foliage and as a source of food in various cuisines.

The varietal name 'suavia' refers to its mild and sweet flavor compared to other varieties of Dioscorea.
