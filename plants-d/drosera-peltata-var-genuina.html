Drosera peltata var. genuina

Drosera peltata var. genuina, commonly known as the broad-leaved sundew, is a carnivorous plant native to Australia.

It features broad, rosette-shaped leaves covered in sticky glands that trap insects. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and requires high humidity to flourish.

Its unique trapping mechanism and ornamental appearance make it a popular choice for carnivorous plant enthusiasts.
