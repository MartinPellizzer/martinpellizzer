Diospyros reticulata var. timoriana

Diospyros reticulata var. timoriana, commonly known as the Timor persimmon, is a species native to the Maluku Islands in Indonesia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows in tropical rainforests. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptical in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, usually greenish-yellow, and it produces small, round fruits that are orange to reddish when ripe.

The fruit is edible, though some varieties may have a bitter taste when unripe.
