Dianthus collivagus

Dianthus collivagus, commonly known as the rock campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of pink to reddish-purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs.

Dianthus collivagus is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
