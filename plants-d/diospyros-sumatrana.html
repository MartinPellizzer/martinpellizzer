Diospyros sumatrana

Diospyros sumatrana, commonly known as the Sumatran persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other notable species like ebony and persimmon. The tree is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and elliptical leaves that are often glossy in appearance. Its fruit, which is typically orange to red in color, is edible and contains a sweet, fleshy pulp surrounding a central core.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine and as a source of timber.
