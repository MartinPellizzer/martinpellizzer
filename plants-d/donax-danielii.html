Donax danielii

Donax danielii, commonly known as Daniel's sea rocket, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and pinnately divided leaves. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to saline and sandy soils. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Donax danielii is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats along the shoreline.
