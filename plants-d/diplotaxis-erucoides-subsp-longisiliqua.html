Diplotaxis erucoides subsp. longisiliqua

Diplotaxis erucoides subsp.

longisiliqua, commonly known as the long-silique wallflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves and long, slender pods that contain the seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
