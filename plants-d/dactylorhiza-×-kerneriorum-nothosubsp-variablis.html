Dactylorhiza × kerneriorum nothosubsp. variablis

Dactylorhiza × kerneriorum nothosubsp.

variablis, commonly known as the variable marsh orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of pale green to yellowish flowers.

The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, thriving in nutrient-poor soils. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, often arranged in a rosette at the base.

The species is notable for its genetic variability, which contributes to its adaptability in different environmental conditions.
