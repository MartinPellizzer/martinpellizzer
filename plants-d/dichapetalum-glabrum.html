Dichapetalum glabrum

Dichapetalum glabrum, commonly known as the white sand tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the dry season. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Dichapetalum glabrum is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
