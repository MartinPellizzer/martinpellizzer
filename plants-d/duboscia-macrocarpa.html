Duboscia macrocarpa

Duboscia macrocarpa, commonly known as the giant starflower, is a striking member of the Malvaceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that resemble stars, which bloom in late summer. The plant has a woody stem and produces distinctive, elongated, spiny fruits that enclose its seeds. Its leaves are palmate, with five to seven lobes, and are covered in fine hairs.

Duboscia macrocarpa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure.
