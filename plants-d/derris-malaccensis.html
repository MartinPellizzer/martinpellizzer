Derris malaccensis

Derris malaccensis, commonly known as the Malacca derris, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Leguminosae (Fabaceae) and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is notable for containing rotenone, a toxic compound used in traditional fishing practices. Its roots are often harvested for their medicinal properties.

Derris malaccensis thrives in tropical climates and is commonly found in moist, shaded environments.
