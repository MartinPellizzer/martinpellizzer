Douglasia vitaliana

Douglasia vitaliana, commonly known as the vitalian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, and its stems are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Douglasia vitaliana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
