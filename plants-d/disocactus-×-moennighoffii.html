Disocactus × moennighoffii

Disocactus × moennighoffii, commonly known as the Moennighoff's holiday cactus, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Disocactus phyllocactus and Disocactus wrightii.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its thick, segmented stems that store water, a feature typical of cacti adapted to arid environments. The plant produces vibrant flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, often blooming during the winter months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Due to its ornamental value, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening and holiday displays.
