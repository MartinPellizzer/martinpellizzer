Dactylorhiza majalis subsp. macedonica

Dactylorhiza majalis subsp.

macedonica, commonly known as the Macedonian marsh orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Balkan Peninsula. It typically grows in wet meadows, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies, preferring moist, acidic soils.

The plant has a slender, erect stem with several oval, leathery leaves. Its flowers are pale green to yellowish, with three sepals and three petals, and are arranged in a loose spike.

It blooms from late spring to early summer, contributing to the biodiversity of its wetland habitats.
