Daphne Cneorum Subsp. Verlotii

Verlot's daphne, scientifically known as Daphne cneorum subsp. verlotii, is a rare and striking plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It is prized for its fragrant, clustered flowers that bloom in early spring, adding a touch of elegance to any garden. The plant's unique appearance, with its glossy leaves and delicate blooms, makes it a standout in the botanical world. Despite its beauty, it is a challenging plant to cultivate due to its specific environmental requirements. Its rarity and ornamental value have made it a sought-after species among horticulturists and plant enthusiasts.
