Decaisnina hombronii

Decaisnina hombronii, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which are clustered in dense, globular inflorescences. The plant has compound leaves with multiple leaflets and is often found in woodland and heathland habitats. It is a member of the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing ability.

Decaisnina hombronii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
