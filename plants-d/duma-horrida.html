Duma horrida

Duma horrida, commonly known as the devil's backbone, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny, upright stems and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant thrives in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and limited water availability. Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a distinctive feature of its native habitat.

Duma horrida plays a role in supporting local wildlife, providing nectar for pollinators and shelter for small animals.
