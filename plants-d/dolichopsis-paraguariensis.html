Dolichopsis paraguariensis

Dolichopsis paraguariensis, commonly known as the Paraguayan passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It belongs to the family Passifloraceae and is characterized by its striking, intricate flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces bright orange or red fruits that are edible and often consumed fresh or used in jams. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a rough texture, and it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

Dolichopsis paraguariensis is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its role in traditional medicine.
