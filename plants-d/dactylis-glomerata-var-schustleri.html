Dactylis glomerata var. schustleri

Dactylis glomerata var. schustleri, commonly known as the Schustler's tufted grass, is a variant of the common goatgrass.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically produces small, clustered spikelets that are often tinged with purple or red, adding ornamental value. It is well adapted to temperate climates and can thrive in a variety of soil types.

This variant is often used in landscaping and erosion control due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
