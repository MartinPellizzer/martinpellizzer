Diasperus multilocularis

Diasperus multilocularis, commonly known as the multilocular desert lily, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves that store water, aiding in its survival in dry environments. The plant produces striking, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, which bloom during the spring or early summer. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access moisture from deeper soil layers.

Diasperus multilocularis plays an important ecological role by providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a food source for various desert animals.
