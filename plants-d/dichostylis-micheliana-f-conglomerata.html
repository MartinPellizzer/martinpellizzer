Dichostylis micheliana f. conglomerata

Dichostylis micheliana f. conglomerata, commonly known as the clustered dichostylis, is a rare and unique variant of the Dichostylis genus.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clustering of flowering stems. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences. It is native to specific regions in South Africa, where it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils.

Due to its limited distribution and distinctive morphology, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
