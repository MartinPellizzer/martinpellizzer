Diospyros analamerensis

Diospyros analamerensis, commonly known as the Eritrean persimmon, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the highlands of Eritrea.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other well-known species like the ebony and persimmon trees. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale green. Its fruit is a small, fleshy berry that turns from green to yellow or red when mature.

The species is of interest for its potential ecological and economic value in its native habitat.
