Dactylis stricta

Dactylis stricta, commonly known as sheep's fescue, is a perennial grass native to Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute, pale green to brown flowers. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a common species in grasslands and pastures.

Dactylis stricta is also valued for its use in livestock forage and as a component in erosion control plantings.
