Draba weberbaueri

Draba weberbaueri, commonly known as Weberbauer's whitlow grass, is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other mustard family plants. This species typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Draba weberbaueri is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and basal leaves, typical of the genus Draba.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native mountainous regions.
