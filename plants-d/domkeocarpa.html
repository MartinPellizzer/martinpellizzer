Domkeocarpa

Domkeocarpa, commonly known as the golden fruit tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palm-like leaves and clusters of yellow-orange fruits that ripen in the summer. The fruits have a thick, fibrous rind and a sweet, pulpy interior with a distinct aroma. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its edible fruit.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
