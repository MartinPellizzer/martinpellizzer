Dichaea fragrantissima subsp. eburnea

Dichaea fragrantissima subsp.

eburnea, commonly known as the fragrant dichaea, is a member of the Bromeliaceae family. It is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating.

The plant produces striking, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions.
