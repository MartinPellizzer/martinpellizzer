Draba scandinavica var. legitima

Draba scandinavica var. legitima, commonly known as the Scandinavian rockcress, is a low-growing perennial plant native to alpine and arctic regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically has a basal rosette of linear, hairy leaves and produces yellow or white flowers in late spring. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold climates.

This variety is distinguished by its specific geographic distribution and morphological traits compared to other Draba species.
