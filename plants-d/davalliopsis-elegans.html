Davalliopsis elegans

Davalliopsis elegans, commonly known as the elegant resupinate fern, is a species belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically arranged in a resupinate (upside-down) manner. The plant produces small, clustered sporangia on the underside of its leaves, which are responsible for spore dispersal. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its unique morphology and growth habit make it a distinctive and ornamental species in botanical collections.
