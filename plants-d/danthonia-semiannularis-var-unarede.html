Danthonia semiannularis var. unarede

Danthonia semiannularis var. unarede, commonly known as the narrow-leaf danthonia, is a grass species native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that are typically less than 10 millimeters wide. The plant forms dense, low-growing tussocks and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its inflorescence consists of a single spikelet, which is often hidden within the foliage.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
