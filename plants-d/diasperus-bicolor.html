Diasperus bicolor

Diasperus bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored grass, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its distinctive two-toned leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sandy or rocky habitats and is often found in coastal regions. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in dense clusters at the base of the plant.

Diasperus bicolor is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
