Dudleya angustiflora

Dudleya angustiflora, commonly known as the narrowleaf stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to California and Baja California.

It features fleshy, blue-green leaves that are narrow and often covered in a white powdery coating called farina. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or purple during the spring. It is adapted to rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun.

Dudleya angustiflora is a popular choice for rock gardens due to its drought tolerance and attractive appearance.
