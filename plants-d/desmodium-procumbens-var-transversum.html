Desmodium procumbens var. transversum

Desmodium procumbens var. transversum, commonly known as the creeping desmodium, is a low-growing perennial legume native to the southeastern United States.

It features trifoliate leaves with a deep green color and small, pea-like flowers that range from white to pale pink. The plant produces small, elliptical seed pods that mature to a brownish hue, containing several tiny seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed areas.

This species is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
