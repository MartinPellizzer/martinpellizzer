Dichropappus

Dichropappus, commonly known as the African ironweed, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

It is native to sub-Saharan Africa and features erect, branched stems covered in fine hairs. The leaves are usually alternate, lance-shaped, and have a rough texture. The flowers are small, yellow to orange, and arranged in dense, spike-like clusters.

Dichropappus species are often used in traditional medicine and are valued for their ornamental appeal.
