Dialypetalanthus fuscescens

Dialypetalanthus fuscescens, commonly known as the black-flowered lily, is a striking plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dark violet to almost black petals, which give it a dramatic appearance. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its flowers bloom in late summer, adding a unique visual element to its natural habitat.

Due to its rarity and specific ecological requirements, it is considered an endangered species in its native range.
