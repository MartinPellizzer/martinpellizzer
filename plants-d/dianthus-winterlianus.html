Dianthus winterlianus

Dianthus winterlianus, commonly known as the Alpine campion, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth habit. The plant produces showy, pink or red flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Dianthus winterlianus is often found in mountainous areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
