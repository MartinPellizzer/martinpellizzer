Deutzia gracilis f. aurea

Deutzia gracilis f. aurea, commonly known as the golden deutzia, is a deciduous shrub characterized by its slender, arching stems and clusters of showy, yellowish-white flowers.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to eastern Asia, particularly China. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and features small, ovate leaves with a glossy texture. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The golden hue of its blooms makes it a popular ornamental plant in gardens and landscapes.
