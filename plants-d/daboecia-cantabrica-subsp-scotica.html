Daboecia cantabrica subsp. scotica

Daboecia cantabrica subsp.

scotica, commonly known as the Cantabrian heath, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of northern Spain and southern France. It is a low-growing shrub with evergreen leaves that are leathery and covered in fine, white hairs.

The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in heathland and coastal habitats.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity and its resilience to harsh coastal conditions.
