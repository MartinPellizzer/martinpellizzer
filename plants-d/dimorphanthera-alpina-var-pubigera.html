Dimorphanthera alpina var. pubigera

Dimorphanthera alpina var. pubigera, commonly known as the alpine dimorphanthera, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and hairy foliage, which helps it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, blooming during the summer months. Its leaves are alternate and lance-shaped, often covered with fine pubescence.

This variant is adapted to high-altitude conditions, showcasing resilience to cold and strong winds.
