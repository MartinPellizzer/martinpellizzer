Dais longifolia

Dais longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf daisy, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads made up of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30-60 centimeters and features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers are yellow with white ray florets, blooming in late spring to early summer.

Dais longifolia is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in well-drained soil.
