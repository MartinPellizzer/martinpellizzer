Desmodium vargasianum var. arcuatum

Desmodium vargasianum var. arcuatum, commonly known as the wavy sicklepod, is a herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, with each leaflet having a wavy or undulating margin, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, hairy pods that mature into dry, dehiscent fruits. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open woods, fields, and along roadsides.

The species is known for its rapid growth and has been studied for its potential use in soil stabilization and as a forage plant.
