Dryobalanops camphora

Dryobalanops camphora, commonly known as the Malayan camphor tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and dense, aromatic foliage. The tree produces large, leathery leaves and is known for its valuable resin, which is used in the production of camphor. The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, contributing to biodiversity and soil stability.
