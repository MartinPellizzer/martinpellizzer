Daedalacanthus roseus var. neesii

Daedalacanthus roseus var. neesii, commonly known as the Nees's rose thorn.

This variant is characterized by its distinctive floral structure, featuring intricate, thorn-like petals that resemble rose petals with a unique, almost spiky appearance. It is native to specific regions in Southeast Asia, where it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant exhibits a compact growth habit with dense foliage, making it suitable for ornamental gardening.

Its flowers are typically fragrant and bloom in shades of pink and white, adding visual interest to gardens.
