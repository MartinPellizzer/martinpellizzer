Diuris punctata var. alba

Diuris punctata var. alba, commonly known as the White-veined Spider-orchid, is a striking orchid native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of pale green to white flowers with distinctive veining. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils in heathlands and open forests. It blooms during the autumn season, usually between September and November.

The species is noted for its delicate appearance and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
