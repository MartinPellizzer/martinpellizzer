Distichorchis barisana

Distichorchis barisana, commonly known as the Barisan orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, moist forest environments. The plant features a rhizomatous growth habit and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of white and yellow. Its leaves are usually elongated and lance-shaped, and it blooms during the rainy season.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
