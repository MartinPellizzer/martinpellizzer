Daviesia alternifolia

Daviesia alternifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed broom, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is a shrub characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and alternate leaves that are typically narrow and linear. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers in spikes, which bloom from late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal regions.

Daviesia alternifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
