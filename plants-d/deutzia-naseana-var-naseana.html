Deutzia naseana var. naseana

Deutzia naseana var. naseana, commonly known as the Japanese deutzia, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and features slender, arching branches. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers in early summer, which are borne on elongated peduncles. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin, turning yellow in the fall.

The plant thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.
