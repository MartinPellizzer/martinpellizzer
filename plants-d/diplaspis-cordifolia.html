Diplaspis cordifolia

Diplaspis cordifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf diplaspis, is a perennial shrub native to South Africa.

It features opposite, ovate to cordate leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive heart-shaped base. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in spring. Its foliage is aromatic and can be used in traditional medicine.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
