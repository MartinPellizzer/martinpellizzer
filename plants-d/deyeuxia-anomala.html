Deyeuxia anomala

Deyeuxia anomala, commonly known as the slender false rice, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, often forming dense mats. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, pale green to brownish flowers.

Deyeuxia anomala is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various organisms.
