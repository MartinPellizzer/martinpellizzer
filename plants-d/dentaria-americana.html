Dentaria americana

Dentaria americana, commonly known as the American bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, which includes mustard and cress plants. The plant features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, and it often grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands.

The plant is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
