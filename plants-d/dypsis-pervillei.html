Dypsis pervillei

Dypsis pervillei, commonly known as the Madagascar palm, is a striking succulent native to Madagascar.

It features a thick, columnar stem with a dense crown of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by distinctive blue or black berries. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
