Diastema cristatum

Diastema cristatum, commonly known as the crowned grass, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters at the tips of the stems. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a distinctive species within its native habitat.
