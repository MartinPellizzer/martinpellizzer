Dayia glutinosa

Dayia glutinosa, commonly known as the sticky dayflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its small, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant produces a sticky, resinous substance from its leaves, which has been used traditionally in various cultural practices. It thrives in warm, humid environments and prefers well-drained soil.

The sticky exudate of Dayia glutinosa has attracted interest for its potential medicinal and industrial applications.
