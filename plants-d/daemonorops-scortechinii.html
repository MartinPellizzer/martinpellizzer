Daemonorops scortechinii

Daemonorops scortechinii, commonly known as the Bornean rattan, is a climbing palm native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, flexible stems and pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant thrives in tropical rainforests, often climbing trees to reach sunlight. Its inflorescence consists of small, clustered flowers, and it produces berries that are dispersed by birds.

Due to its rapid growth and utility, it is both ecologically significant and economically valuable.
