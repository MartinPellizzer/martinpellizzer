Delphinium kohatense

Delphinium kohatense, commonly known as the Kohat larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas, specifically found in regions of Pakistan and India.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, spindly stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers in racemose inflorescences, which bloom in summer. Its leaves are deeply lobed and arranged alternately along the stem, contributing to its ornamental value.

Delphinium kohatense is adapted to high-altitude environments and thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.
