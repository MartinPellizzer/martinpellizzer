Daviesia ovata

Daviesia ovata, commonly known as the oval leaf davy's wattle, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny conditions.

Daviesia ovata is a member of the legume family and has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria.
