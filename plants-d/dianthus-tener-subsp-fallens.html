Dianthus tener subsp. fallens

Dianthus tener subsp.

fallens, commonly known as the alpine campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia. It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its compact growth habit and vibrant blooms make it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.

It is also valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in herbal medicine.
