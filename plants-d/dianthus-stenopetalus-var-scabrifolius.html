Dianthus stenopetalus var. scabrifolius

Dianthus stenopetalus var. scabrifolius, commonly known as the roughleaf campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of pink to red flowers with five petals. The leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine, bristly hairs, giving them a rough texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
