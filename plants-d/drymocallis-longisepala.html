Drymocallis longisepala

Drymocallis longisepala, commonly known as the long-sepaled goldenrod, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical structure supports its role as a nectar source for various pollinators.
