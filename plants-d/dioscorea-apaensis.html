Dioscorea apaensis

Dioscorea apaensis, commonly known as the Mexican yam, is a climbing vine native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary part used for culinary and medicinal purposes. The plant produces small, green, heart-shaped leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its tubers are rich in starch and have been traditionally used in local cuisines.

The botanical features of Dioscorea apaensis make it an important species in both traditional medicine and agriculture.
