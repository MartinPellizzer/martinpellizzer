Dalea neomexicana var. megaladenia

Dalea neomexicana var. megaladenia, commonly known as the Mexican milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb with slender, often branched stems and small, compound leaves. The plant produces showy, pink to purple pea-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native plant communities in its range.
