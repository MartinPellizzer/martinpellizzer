Danaea longicaudata

Danaea longicaudata, commonly known as the long-tailed flame vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red flowers that resemble flames, which are often used in ornamental horticulture. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and emits a sweet fragrance, particularly in the evening. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires support for its vigorous growth.

Danaea longicaudata is also valued for its ability to attract hummingbirds and other pollinators.
