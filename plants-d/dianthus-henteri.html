Dianthus henteri

Dianthus henteri, commonly known as the Henter's campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant pink or white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Dianthus henteri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
