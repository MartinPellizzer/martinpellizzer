Disa bolusiana

Disa bolusiana, commonly known as the Bolus orchid, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its unique floral structure includes three sepals and three petals, with the latter often being more pronounced.

Disa bolusiana is a perennial herb that is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also found in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
