Dendrobium oxychilum

Dendrobium oxychilum, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, cane-like stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces clusters of showy, yellow or golden-orange flowers that bloom during the summer months. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

Dendrobium oxychilum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
