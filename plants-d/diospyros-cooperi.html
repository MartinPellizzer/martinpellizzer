Diospyros cooperi

Diospyros cooperi, commonly known as the Chinese date tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and elliptical leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into edible, sweet, and sticky fruits resembling dates.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production in tropical and subtropical regions.
