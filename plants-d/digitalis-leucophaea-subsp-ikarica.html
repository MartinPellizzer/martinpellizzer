Digitalis leucophaea subsp. ikarica

Digitalis leucophaea subsp.

ikarica, commonly known as the Kiarica foxglove, is a perennial herb native to New Zealand. It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and striking bell-shaped flowers.

The flowers are typically pink to purple in color and bloom during the spring and summer months. This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.

It prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a versatile choice for various garden environments.
