Draba oreibata

Draba oreibata, commonly known as the Andes mouse-eared cress, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant is characterized by its small, basal leaves and clusters of white or yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to the harsh conditions of alpine environments.

Draba oreibata is often found in meadows and slopes at elevations above 3,000 meters.
