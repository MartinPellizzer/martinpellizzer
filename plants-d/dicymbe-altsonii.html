Dicymbe altsonii

Dicymbe altsonii, commonly known as the Altson's burr vetch, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in savannas and woodland areas.

Its flowers are usually small and yellow or greenish, and it plays a role in nitrogen fixation due to its symbiotic relationship with rhizobia bacteria.
