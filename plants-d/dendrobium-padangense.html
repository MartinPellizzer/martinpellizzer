Dendrobium padangense

Dendrobium padangense, commonly known as the Padang orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and clusters of fragrant, showy flowers that bloom in shades of white, yellow, and pink. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest undergrowth or on rocky slopes. Its leaves are elongated and strap-like, and it produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients.

Dendrobium padangense is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
