Dactylorhiza incarnata subsp. praetermissa

Dactylorhiza incarnata subsp.

praetermissa, commonly known as the slender marsh orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in wet or damp habitats such as marshes, fens, and along riverbanks.

The plant features a slender, erect stem with few to several leaves and produces small, greenish-white to pale yellow flowers. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its pseudobulbs and lateral roots.

This subspecies is often found in calcareous soils and is adapted to moist, shaded environments.
