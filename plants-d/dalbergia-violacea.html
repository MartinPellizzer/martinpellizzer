Dalbergia violacea

Dalbergia violacea, commonly known as violetwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking violet-colored wood. The plant features bipinnate leaves and produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically purple or violet in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savannas and woodland areas.

The tree is valued for its durable timber and is also used in traditional medicine.
