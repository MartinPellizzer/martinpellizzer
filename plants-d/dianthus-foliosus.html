Dianthus foliosus

Dianthus foliosus, commonly known as the leafy carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and produces clusters of pink or white flowers with prominent, showy petals. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

The plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
