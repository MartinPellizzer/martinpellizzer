Dendrobium tibeticum

Dendrobium tibeticum, commonly known as the Tibetan orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the mountainous regions of Tibet.

It is an epiphytic or lithophytic plant, typically growing on rocks or trees in high-altitude environments. The plant produces long, cane-like stems that are covered in dense clusters of small, fragrant flowers. Its blooming period usually occurs in late summer to early autumn, and the flowers come in shades of white, yellow, and pink.

Dendrobium tibeticum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.
