Darwinia virgata

Darwinia virgata, commonly known as the slender Darwinia, is a flowering plant native to south-eastern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its erect, woody stems and dense clusters of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces striking, showy spikes of pink to purple flowers during late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

Darwinia virgata is also valued for its ecological role, providing nectar for native pollinators.
