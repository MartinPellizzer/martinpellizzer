Drosera coccipetala

Drosera coccipetala, commonly known as the crimson rose sundew, is a carnivorous plant native to Australia.

It features delicate, hairy leaves that secrete a sticky mucilage to trap insects. The plant's flowers are typically pink or red, with a unique, spurred shape that distinguishes it from other sundew species. It thrives in wet, acidic environments such as bogs and heathlands.

The species is known for its striking appearance and specialized adaptations for nutrient acquisition.
