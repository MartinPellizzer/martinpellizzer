Ditassa succedanea

Ditassa succedanea, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Gesneriaceae and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its attractive appearance and relatively easy care requirements. The flowers typically bloom in shades of violet, pink, and white, adding ornamental value to gardens and indoor spaces.
