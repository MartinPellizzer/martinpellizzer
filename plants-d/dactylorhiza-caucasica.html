Dactylorhiza caucasica

Dactylorhiza caucasica, commonly known as the Caucasian marsh orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Caucasus region.

It is a perennial herb with a rhizomatous root system and typically grows in wet, boggy habitats. The plant produces tall, slender stems that bear a single spike of pale yellow to greenish-white flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it has a complex inflorescence with showy, bilaterally symmetrical blooms.

Dactylorhiza caucasica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
