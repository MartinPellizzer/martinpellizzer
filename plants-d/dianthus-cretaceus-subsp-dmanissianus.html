Dianthus cretaceus subsp. dmanissianus

Dianthus cretaceus subsp.

dmanissianus, commonly known as the Caucasian campion, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region. It is characterized by its delicate pink or red flowers and hairy, lobed leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, thriving in well-drained environments. It is a low-growing perennial that produces showy blooms in the summer months.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
