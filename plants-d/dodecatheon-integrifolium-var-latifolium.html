Dodecatheon integrifolium var. latifolium

Dodecatheon integrifolium var. latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf glory of the snow, is a herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has simple, alternate, and often broad leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and ornamental purposes.
