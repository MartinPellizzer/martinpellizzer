Desmodium heterocarpon subsp. ovalifolium

Desmodium heterocarpon subsp.

ovalifolium, commonly known as the oval-leaf desmodium, is a perennial legume native to tropical and subtropical regions. It features trifoliate leaves with oval-shaped leaflets, which are often hairy and have a distinctive dark green color.

The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, followed by small, flat, bean-like pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it valuable for soil enrichment and forage in certain agricultural contexts.
