Dracaena tholloniana

Dracaena tholloniana, commonly known as the Madagascar dragon tree, is a succulent plant native to the island of Madagascar.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green with a reddish tint. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by red, fleshy fruits containing seeds. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Dracaena tholloniana is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and resilience.
