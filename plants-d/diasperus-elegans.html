Diasperus elegans

Diasperus elegans, commonly known as the elegant grassveld, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sandy habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. It produces seeds in small, dry fruits called cypselae, which are dispersed by wind.

Diasperus elegans is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
