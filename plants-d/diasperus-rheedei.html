Diasperus rheedei

Diasperus rheedei, commonly known as the Cape daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant typically grows in coastal and sandy environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its structure includes a basal rosette of narrow, silver-green leaves and a tall, branching stem.

Diasperus rheedei is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to withstand coastal conditions.
