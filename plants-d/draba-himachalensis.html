Draba himachalensis

Draba himachalensis, commonly known as the Himachal Draba, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan region of India.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in rocky and well-drained soils. It has basal leaves that are usually lanceolate or ovate, and it produces clusters of yellow or white flowers in early spring.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native range.
