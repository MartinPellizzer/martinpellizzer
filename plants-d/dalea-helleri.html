Dalea helleri

Dalea helleri, commonly known as Heller's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Dalea helleri plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local pollinators.
