Dendrobium secundum var. niveum

Dendrobium secundum var. niveum, commonly known as the white-lipped dendrobium, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is an epiphytic plant that grows on trees and rocks, relying on atmospheric moisture for nourishment. The plant features long, cane-like stems covered in thick, fleshy leaves. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, with a distinctive, recurved lip that enhances its ornamental appeal.

It thrives in warm, humid environments with indirect sunlight, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.
