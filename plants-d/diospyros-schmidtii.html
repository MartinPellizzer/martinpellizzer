Diospyros schmidtii

Diospyros schmidtii, commonly known as the African persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes ebony trees, and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by edible, orange-red berries that are often consumed by both humans and animals.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in medicine and as a source of timber.
