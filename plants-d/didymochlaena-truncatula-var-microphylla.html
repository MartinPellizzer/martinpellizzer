Didymochlaena truncatula var. microphylla

Didymochlaena truncatula var. microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved thornapple, is a shrub native to South America.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by distinctive berries that turn red when mature. It is adapted to grow in dry, sunny environments and is often found in coastal areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
