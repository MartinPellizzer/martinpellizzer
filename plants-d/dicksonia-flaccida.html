Dicksonia flaccida

Dicksonia flaccida, commonly known as the slender tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feathery fronds that can reach lengths of up to 3 meters. The plant has a distinctive crown of pinnate fronds that are often drooping and covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Dicksonia flaccida is valued for its ornamental appearance and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with appropriate conditions.
