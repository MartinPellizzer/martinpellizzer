Dalechampia papposa

Dalechampia papposa, commonly known as the silky dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, lobed leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces small, wind-dispersed seeds with white, silky hairs, aiding in its propagation. It typically grows in moist, disturbed soils along stream banks and in open woodlands.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.
