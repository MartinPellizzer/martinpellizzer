Diasperus glomerulatus

Diasperus glomerulatus, commonly known as the clustered spurge, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which give it its common name. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils.

Diasperus glomerulatus is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family, known for its diverse and often toxic species.
