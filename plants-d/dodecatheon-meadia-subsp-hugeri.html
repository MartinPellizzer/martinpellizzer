Dodecatheon meadia subsp. hugeri

Dodecatheon meadia subsp.

hugeri, commonly known as the purple trillium, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States. It features a single, showy, nodding flower with three petals and three sepals, typically in shades of purple or pink.

The plant has a basal rosette of heart-shaped leaves and produces a capsule fruit containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, rich woodland soils and is often found in shaded, moist environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental addition to native plant gardens.
