Diospyros akaraiensis

Diospyros akaraiensis, commonly known as the African persimmon, is a small to medium-sized deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other well-known species like the ebony tree. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into fleshy, orange-red berries.

Diospyros akaraiensis is valued for its hard, dense wood, which is often used in traditional crafts and as a source of timber.
