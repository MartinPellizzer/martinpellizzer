Diploclisia glaucescens var. kunstleri

Diploclisia glaucescens var. kunstleri, commonly known as the Kunstler's blueback vine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Australia.

It features dark green, glossy leaves that are often tinged with blue or grey on the underside, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for tropical and subtropical gardens.

Its ornamental value comes from its striking foliage and seasonal flowering.
