Dianthus jaroslavii

Dianthus jaroslavii, commonly known as the Jaroslav's campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and produces clusters of small, pink to reddish-purple flowers. The plant typically blooms in late summer, and its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves. Dianthus jaroslavii is adapted to rocky and arid environments, showcasing resilience to harsh climatic conditions.

It is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
