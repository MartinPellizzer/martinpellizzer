Dinebra simoniana

Dinebra simoniana, commonly known as the golden star vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a striking visual display. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are usually green with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Dinebra simoniana is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
