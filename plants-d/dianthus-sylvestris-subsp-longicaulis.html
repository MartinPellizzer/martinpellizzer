Dianthus sylvestris subsp. longicaulis

Dianthus sylvestris subsp.

longicaulis, commonly known as the woodland campion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its pink or white flowers that bloom in summer, typically arranged in clusters at the top of slender stems.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and it thrives in woodland or rocky habitats. It is a perennial species, known for its ability to spread through rhizomes, forming dense mats in suitable conditions.

The subspecies is distinguished by its longer, more elongated stems compared to other Dianthus species.
