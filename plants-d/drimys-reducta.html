Drimys reducta

Drimys reducta, commonly known as the small drimys, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in the Andean regions of Ecuador and Peru.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in moist, temperate forests. The plant has dark green, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, fragrant white flowers during the spring. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds.

Drimys reducta is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine by indigenous communities.
