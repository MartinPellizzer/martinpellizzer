Diospyros caucheana

Diospyros caucheana, commonly known as the Colombian persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to South America, particularly Colombia and Venezuela.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes ebony and persimmon trees. The plant is characterized by its elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by edible, orange-red fruits that are sweet when ripe.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
