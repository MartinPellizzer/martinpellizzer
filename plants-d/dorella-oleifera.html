Dorella oleifera

Dorella oleifera, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and prefers full sun exposure. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and sturdy.

Dorella oleifera is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant blooms and drought tolerance.
