Dolichos fatmensis

Dolichos fatmensis, commonly known as the African burr legume, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by distinctive, spiny pods containing edible seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to dry, arid environments.

The species is valued for its potential as a forage crop and for its ornamental appeal.
