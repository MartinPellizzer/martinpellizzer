Dennstaedtia tryoniana

Dennstaedtia tryoniana, commonly known as the Tryon's water fern, is a species of fern native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, creeping rhizomes and its ability to form dense, mat-like growths. The fronds are typically bipinnate, with a soft, pale green color and a slightly curled appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in swampy areas or along the edges of ponds.

This plant plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
