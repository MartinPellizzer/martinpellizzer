Dresslerella lasiocampa

Dresslerella lasiocampa, commonly known as the velvetleaf orchid, is a tropical orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, velvety leaves and clusters of fragrant, showy flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers, which bloom in late winter to early spring, are often pink or purple in color.

The orchid's botanical features make it a popular choice for both ornamental and horticultural cultivation.
