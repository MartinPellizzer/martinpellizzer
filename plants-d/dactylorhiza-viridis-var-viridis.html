Dactylorhiza viridis var. viridis

Dactylorhiza viridis var. viridis, commonly known as the green orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces spikes of greenish-yellow flowers, which are often tinged with white or pink. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows.

Dactylorhiza viridis var. viridis is a perennial herb that relies on mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake.
