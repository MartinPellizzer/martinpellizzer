Dactylorhiza × ampolai

Dactylorhiza × ampolai, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing clusters of pink or purple flowers. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or grassland habitats.

Dactylorhiza × ampolai is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Dactylorhiza denplanca and Dactylorhiza maculata.
