Daphne brevipaniculata

Daphne brevipaniculata, commonly known as the short panicle daphne, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features small, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring, typically in shades of white and pink. The plant has elliptic to ovate leaves that are glossy and dark green, providing an attractive contrast to its showy blooms. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Daphne brevipaniculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscape design for its winter interest and spring display.
