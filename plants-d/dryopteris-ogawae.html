Dryopteris ogawae

Dryopteris ogawae, commonly known as the Japanese shield fern, is a species of fern native to Japan.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its large, leathery fronds with a distinctive shield-shaped pinnae. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds, and it reproduces through sexual reproduction via spores rather than seeds.

Dryopteris ogawae is valued for its ornamental appearance and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
