Dendrochilum cruciforme var. longicuspum

Dendrochilum cruciforme var. longicuspum, commonly known as the long-spurred cruciform orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, cruciform (cross-shaped) floral structure, which gives it its common name. The plant produces elongated, hairy sepals and petals, with a distinctive spur that extends from the base of the flower. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in tropical rainforests.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its intricate and colorful blooms.
