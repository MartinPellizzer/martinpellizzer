Dypsis furcata

Dypsis furcata, commonly known as the areca palm, is a tropical plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and feathery, compound leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Its growth habit makes it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping in tropical and subtropical regions.

Dypsis furcata is also valued for its ability to tolerate partial shade and its relatively low maintenance requirements.
