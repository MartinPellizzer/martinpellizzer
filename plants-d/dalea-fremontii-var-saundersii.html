Dalea fremontii var. saundersii

Dalea fremontii var. saundersii, commonly known as the Fremont's milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as plains and slopes, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Dalea fremontii var. saundersii plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife, making it an important species in its ecosystem.
