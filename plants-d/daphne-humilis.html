Daphne humilis

Daphne humilis, commonly known as the low daphne, is a deciduous shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. The plant has narrow, leathery leaves that are evergreen, providing visual interest throughout the year. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Daphne humilis is also notable for its toxic sap, which can cause skin irritation.
