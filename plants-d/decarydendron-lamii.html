Decarydendron lamii

Decarydendron lamii, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows up to 10 meters in height. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in spring. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a smooth, glossy surface.

The bark is thick, fissured, and often exudes a milky sap when wounded.
