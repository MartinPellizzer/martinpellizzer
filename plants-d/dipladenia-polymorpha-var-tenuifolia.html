Dipladenia polymorpha var. tenuifolia

Dipladenia polymorpha var. tenuifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mandevilla, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in warm, sunny conditions and is often cultivated as a decorative plant in hanging baskets or containers.

This variety is prized for its continuous blooming and adaptability to various growing environments.
