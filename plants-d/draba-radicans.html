Draba radicans

Draba radicans, commonly known as the creeping whitlowgrass, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the northern hemisphere.

It typically forms dense mats of foliage and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are usually less than 5 cm in length. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or arctic environments.

Draba radicans is adapted to cold climates and is known for its resilience in harsh conditions.
