Dianthus juniperinus

Dianthus juniperinus, commonly known as the evergreen candytuft, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has needle-like leaves that are evergreen, giving it a distinctive, shrub-like appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Dianthus juniperinus is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in mild climates.
