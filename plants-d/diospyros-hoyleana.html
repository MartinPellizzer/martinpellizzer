Diospyros hoyleana

Diospyros hoyleana, commonly known as the Hoyle's ebony, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other species known for their hard, dark wood. The plant is characterized by its glossy, ovate leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically followed by round, edible fruits.

The fruits are usually harvested when they turn a deep red or black, and the tree is valued for its ornamental qualities as well as its timber.
