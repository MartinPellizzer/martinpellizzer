Discocactus crassispinus

Discocactus crassispinus, commonly known as the thick-spined discocactus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are often covered in dense, spiny areoles. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are typically short-lived but attract various pollinators. Its succulent leaves store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments.

Discocactus crassispinus is well-suited for cultivation in xeriscapes due to its low maintenance and adaptability.
