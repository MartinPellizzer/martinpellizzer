Diospyros rivularis

Diospyros rivularis, commonly known as the river date or wild persimmon, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and elliptical leaves that are opposite in arrangement. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by edible, orange-red berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

The fruit is valued for its sweet, juicy pulp and is used in traditional medicine and culinary practices.
