Deschampsia borealis

Deschampsia borealis, commonly known as Arctic feathergrass, is a low-growing perennial grass native to arctic and alpine regions.

It has slender, hair-like leaves and produces delicate, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in cold, nutrient-poor environments, often found in tundra and rocky soils. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense mats, stabilizing the soil in harsh conditions.

Deschampsia borealis plays a crucial role in providing habitat and forage for arctic wildlife.
