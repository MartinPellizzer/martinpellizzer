Demidovia tetragonoides

Demidovia tetragonoides, commonly known as the four-angled willow, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often branching stems that are typically four-angled in cross-section. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in catkins, which are typical of willow species. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and usually have a rough texture.

Demidovia tetragonoides is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in harsh climates.
