Damironia cernua

Damironia cernua, commonly known as the hanging lily, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its pendulous, showy flowers that bloom in clusters, often in shades of pink or white. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a waxy texture.

Damironia cernua is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
