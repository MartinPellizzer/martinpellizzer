Desmodium schubertianum

Desmodium schubertianum, commonly known as Schubert's burr legume, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and is often found in disturbed soils. Its small, yellow flowers are followed by hairy, triangular seed pods that mature into burrs.

Desmodium schubertianum is also valued for its role in soil enrichment and as a forage plant for livestock.
