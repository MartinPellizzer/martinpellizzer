Duthiastrum

Duthiastrum, commonly known as the silverleaf duthiastrum, is a member of the Ericaceae family.

It is a shrub characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage that often displays a silvery or grayish hue due to a thick layer of fine, fuzzy hairs. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and is often found in shaded, woodland environments. Its small, bell-shaped flowers are usually white or pale pink and bloom in early spring.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging soil conditions.
