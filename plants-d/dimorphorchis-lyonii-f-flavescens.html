Dimorphorchis lyonii f. flavescens

Dimorphorchis lyonii f. flavescens, commonly known as the yellow-flowered orchid, is a rare orchid species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow petals and sepals. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest undergrowth. Its flowers are often fragrant and bloom during the warmer months of the year.

The species is noted for its unique floral structure and is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
