Desmodium diffusum

Desmodium diffusum, commonly known as hairy lespedeza, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces small, round, brown pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Desmodium diffusum is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil fertility.
