Dicalix myrtacea

Dicalix myrtacea, commonly known as the satinwood, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae, which includes eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its glossy, narrow leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. It typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy soils.

Dicalix myrtacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
