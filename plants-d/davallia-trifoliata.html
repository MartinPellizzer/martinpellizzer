Davallia trifoliata

Davallia trifoliata, commonly known as the maidenhair fern, is a species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, Africa, and the Americas.

It is characterized by its delicate, arching fronds with a distinctive trifoliate (three-part) leaflet structure. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and prefers well-draining, humus-rich soil. Its rhizomes are creeping and produce new plants through spore reproduction.

The fern is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in indoor gardens due to its elegant appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
