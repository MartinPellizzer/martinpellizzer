Distichorchis distachya

Distichorchis distachya, commonly known as the slender orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant features a slender, upright stem with two opposite leaves, giving it its scientific name. Its flowers are small and clustered, often in a spike, and are usually white or pale yellow in color.

Distichorchis distachya is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
