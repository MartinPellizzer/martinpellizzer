Dovyalis maliformis

Dovyalis maliformis, commonly known as the sour plum or yellow custard apple, is a species of flowering plant in the family Malvaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, southern Asia, and the Pacific Islands. The plant produces small, yellow to orange fruits that are typically sour and have a rough, hairy exterior. The leaves are ovate with a pointed tip and are often covered in fine hairs.

It is valued for its edible fruits and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
