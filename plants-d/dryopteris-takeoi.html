Dryopteris takeoi

Dryopteris takeoi, commonly known as the Japanese shield fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to Japan.

It features a compact, dense foliage with leathery, dark green fronds that are deeply divided into shield-shaped pinnae. The plant produces sporangia on the underside of its fronds, which are clustered in sori covered by a brown indusium. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation.
