Dileptium

Dileptium, commonly known as the marsh orchid, is a genus of flowering plants in the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It consists of a single species, Dileptium palustre, which is native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in late summer. It thrives in wet or marshy habitats and is often found in damp meadows and along riverbanks.

The botanical features of Dileptium make it an important species for ecological studies and botanical gardens.
