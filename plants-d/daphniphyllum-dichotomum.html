Daphniphyllum dichotomum

Daphniphyllum dichotomum, commonly known as the two-branched holly, is a species of evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, elliptical leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red berries in the fall. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its unique branching pattern and ornamental foliage make it a desirable plant for horticultural purposes.
