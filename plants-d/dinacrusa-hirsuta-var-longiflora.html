Dinacrusa hirsuta var. longiflora

Dinacrusa hirsuta var. longiflora, commonly known as the hairy dinacrusa, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which help it retain moisture in its arid habitat. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in the spring. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
