Dianthus sylvaticus

Dianthus sylvaticus, commonly known as the woodland carnation, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of pink or white flowers with distinctive red or purple markings. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and thrives in partial shade to full sun. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces small, showy blooms throughout the summer.

Dianthus sylvaticus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and naturalized settings.
