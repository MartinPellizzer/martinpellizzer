Dactylorhiza fistulosa

Dactylorhiza fistulosa, commonly known as the channelled orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and a single, tall spike of pale yellow to greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodland or heathland habitats. Dactylorhiza fistulosa has a rhizomatous root system and produces small, dark brown to black seeds.

It is a perennial plant that blooms from late spring to early summer.
