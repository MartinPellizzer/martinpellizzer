Dactylis glomerata var. hackelii

Dactylis glomerata var. hackelii, commonly known as the Hackel's goatgrass, is a perennial grass native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and dense, spike-like inflorescences that contain small, pale blue to purple florets. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows, pastures, and rocky soils. It has fibrous roots and is well adapted to withstand drought and grazing.

Its botanical features make it a valuable forage plant in agricultural settings.
