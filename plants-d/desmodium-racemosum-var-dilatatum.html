Desmodium racemosum var. dilatatum

Desmodium racemosum var. dilatatum, commonly known as the creeping desmodium, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a herbaceous perennial with trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, and it thrives in well-drained soils. It is often found in open areas, disturbed sites, and along roadsides, where it spreads through rhizomes and seeds.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a useful plant for soil stabilization.
