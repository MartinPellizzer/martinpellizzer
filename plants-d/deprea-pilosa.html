Deprea pilosa

Deprea pilosa, commonly known as the hairy mallow, is a flowering plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves, which are typically covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces showy, pink or purple flowers that bloom in clusters, adding visual interest to gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Deprea pilosa is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
