Dacryodes rugosa

Dacryodes rugosa, commonly known as the rough-leafed ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia and parts of the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and elliptical leaves with a leathery texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by oval, woody fruits containing a single seed. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Dacryodes rugosa is valued for its hard, durable wood and has traditional uses in various indigenous cultures.
