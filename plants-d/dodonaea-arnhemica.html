Dodonaea arnhemica

Dodonaea arnhemica, commonly known as the Arnhem glorybower, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of northern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in native habitats.
