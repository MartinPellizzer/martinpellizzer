Dryas octopetala subsp. chamaedrifolia

Dryas octopetala subsp.

chamaedrifolia, commonly known as the alpine rock rose, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to alpine and arctic regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It features dense, mat-forming growth with small, leathery leaves that are typically blue-green in color.

The plant produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers in late spring to early summer. It is well adapted to harsh environments, tolerating cold temperatures and poor soil conditions.

This subspecies is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardiness in rocky or gravelly gardens.
