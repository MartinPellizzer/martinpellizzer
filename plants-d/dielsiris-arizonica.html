Dielsiris arizonica

Dielsiris arizonica, commonly known as the Arizona iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, and it produces a single, tall flowering stem. Dielsiris arizonica thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, typically found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the local flora in arid and semi-arid regions.
