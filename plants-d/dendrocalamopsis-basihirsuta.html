Dendrocalamopsis basihirsuta

Dendrocalamopsis basihirsuta, commonly known as the hairy bamboo, is a member of the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand and Vietnam. This plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems covered in fine, hairy structures. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests.

The plant's unique morphology and habitat make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
