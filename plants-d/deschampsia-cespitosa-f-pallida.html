Deschampsia cespitosa f. pallida

Deschampsia cespitosa f. pallida, commonly known as the pale wheatgrass, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is a perennial species characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant forms dense, tufted clumps and produces slender, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows, marshes, and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
