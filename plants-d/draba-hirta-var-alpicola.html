Draba hirta var. alpicola

Draba hirta var. alpicola, commonly known as the alpine mouse-ear cress, is a perennial herb native to alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, where it thrives in cold, moist environments. The plant features basal rosettes of hairy, linear leaves and produces small, yellow flowers in early spring. Its flowers are often clustered in dense, compact inflorescences, and it is well adapted to withstand harsh mountain conditions.
