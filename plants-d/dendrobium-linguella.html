Dendrobium linguella

Dendrobium linguella, commonly known as the tongue orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, elongated pseudobulbs and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces strikingly colorful, spurred flowers that are often yellow or orange with red or purple markings. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Dendrobium linguella is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
