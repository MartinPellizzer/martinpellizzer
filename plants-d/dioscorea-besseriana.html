Dioscorea besseriana

Dioscorea besseriana, commonly known as the African yam bean, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are used as a food source. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and elongated, bean-like fruits that contain several seeds. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and often have a hairy texture.

Dioscorea besseriana is adapted to grow in moist, shaded environments and is cultivated for both its edible tubers and ornamental value.
