Duguetia moricandiana

Duguetia moricandiana, commonly known as the yellow bell, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments within the understory of tropical forests. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a waxy texture.

The fruit is a small, fleshy berry that contains several seeds.
