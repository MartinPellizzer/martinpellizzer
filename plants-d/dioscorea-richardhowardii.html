Dioscorea richardhowardii

Dioscorea richardhowardii, commonly known as the Richard Howard yam, is a climbing vine belonging to the Dioscoreaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by tuberous roots that are used as food in some cultures. Its growth habit and morphology make it a distinctive member of the yam genus.

The species is valued both for its agricultural potential and its ecological role in its native habitats.
