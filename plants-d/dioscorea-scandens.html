Dioscorea scandens

Dioscorea scandens, commonly known as the climbing yam, is a tropical vine native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its twining stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by cylindrical, fleshy fruits. Its tuberous roots are edible and have been used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Dioscorea scandens thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
