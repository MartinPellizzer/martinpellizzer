Dioclea ferruginea

Dioclea ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty dioclea, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pink or red flowers. The plant produces small, round pods that contain several seeds. It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in humid, shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
