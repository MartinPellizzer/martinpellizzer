Dasyphyllum spinescens

Dasyphyllum spinescens, commonly known as the spiny dasyphyllum, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and clusters of small, showy flowers that typically bloom in the spring. The plant has adapted to arid environments with its succulent leaves and spiny structures that help reduce water loss. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Dasyphyllum spinescens plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and shelter for small animals.
