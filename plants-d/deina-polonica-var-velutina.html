Deina polonica var. velutina

Deina polonica var. velutina, commonly known as the velvetleaf bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are velvety to the touch, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and conservation areas.
