Discolobium psoraleifolium

Discolobium psoraleifolium, commonly known as the yellow globemallow, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with oval, glandular, and often yellowish or reddish stipules. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in late spring, which are clustered in spherical or globose inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Botanically, it belongs to the legume family (Fabaceae), and its seeds are encased in a distinctive, inflated pod.
