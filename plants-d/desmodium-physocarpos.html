Desmodium physocarpos

Desmodium physocarpos, commonly known as the bladderpod or cowpea bladdernut, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as prairies and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soils. It is native to North America and is often found in the eastern and central regions of the United States.

The species is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
