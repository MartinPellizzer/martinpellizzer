Didiscus compositus var. typicus

Didiscus compositus var. typicus, commonly known as the composite didiscus, is a flowering plant characterized by its unique inflorescence structure.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is native to South Africa. The plant features a central disc surrounded by ray florets, giving it a daisy-like appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and drought tolerance.
