Dianthus superbus f. albus

Dianthus superbus f. albus, commonly known as the white carnation, is a cultivar of the species Dianthus superbus, characterized by its pale white or nearly white flowers.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family, which includes many flowering plants with showy blooms. The plant typically features upright, branched stems and linear, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are usually single or clustered in small inflorescences, and they bloom in late spring to early summer.

This variety is prized for its elegant appearance and is often used in gardens and floral arrangements.
