Dioscorea sinuata var. pauloensis

Dioscorea sinuata var. pauloensis, commonly known as the Brazilian yam, is a climbing vine native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary part used for culinary and medicinal purposes. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into capsule fruits. Its tubers are valued for their starchy texture and are often used in traditional cuisines.

The species is also noted for its potential in herbal medicine due to the presence of compounds like diosgenin.
