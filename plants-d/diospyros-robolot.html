Diospyros robolot

Diospyros robolot, commonly known as the wild persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes ebony and persimmon trees. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by orange-red berries, which are edible when fully ripe. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a leathery texture with a glossy surface.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine and cuisine.
