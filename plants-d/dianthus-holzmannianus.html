Dianthus holzmannianus

Dianthus holzmannianus, commonly known as the Holzmann's campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountains of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soil and full sun exposure. Its flowers bloom in late summer, adding vibrant color to high-altitude landscapes.

Dianthus holzmannianus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
