Digitaria pruriens var. arnottiana

Digitaria pruriens var. arnottiana, commonly known as itchgrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and agricultural fields, due to its adaptability. Its name derives from the itching sensation caused by the fine hairs on its leaves.

Botanically, it is part of the Poaceae family and is known for its drought tolerance and rapid growth.
