Digitaria shimadana

Digitaria shimadana, commonly known as the Japanese grass or Shimadana grass, is a perennial grass species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, linear leaves that are typically green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Digitaria shimadana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
