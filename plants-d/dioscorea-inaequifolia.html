Dioscorea inaequifolia

Dioscorea inaequifolia, commonly known as the unequal-leaf yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, asymmetrical leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by tuberous roots that are often used in traditional medicine and as a food source. Its growth habit involves twining stems that ascend support structures, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

The tubers of D. inaequifolia contain compounds with potential pharmacological properties, making it an important species in ethnobotanical studies.
