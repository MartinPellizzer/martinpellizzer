Dacrydium magnum

Dacrydium magnum, commonly known as the giant silver pine, is a towering conifer native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and dense, dark green foliage. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain its seeds, typical of coniferous species. Its leaves are needle-like and retain their color year-round, contributing to its evergreen nature.

Dacrydium magnum plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
