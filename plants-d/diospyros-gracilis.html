Diospyros gracilis

Diospyros gracilis, commonly known as the slender persimmon, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by orange to red berries that are edible when fully ripe. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its fruit, which has a sweet, honey-like flavor.
