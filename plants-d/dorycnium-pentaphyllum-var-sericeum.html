Dorycnium pentaphyllum var. sericeum

Dorycnium pentaphyllum var. sericeum, commonly known as the silky goatweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a robust taproot and upright stems that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in early summer. Its leaves are pinnately divided with finely serrated margins, and the plant is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils.

It is often found in meadows, along roadsides, and in dry grasslands.
