Delima sarmentosa f. hirsutior

Delima sarmentosa f. hirsutior, commonly known as the hairy delima, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems covered in dense, hairy foliage, which provides a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by small, edible fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests or moist woodlands.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
