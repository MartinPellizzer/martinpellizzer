Didymocarpus codonion

Didymocarpus codonion, commonly known as the Indian date palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in diameter. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into edible, date-like fruits. The fruits are typically harvested when they are fully mature and have a sweet, fibrous texture.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of food in its native regions.
