Derris filipes

Derris filipes, commonly known as the Indian snakeroot, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its slender, woody stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in racemes. Its roots contain the alkaloid rotenone, which has been traditionally used as a natural insecticide.

Derris filipes is often found in moist, shaded areas of forests and is valued for its medicinal and pesticidal properties.
