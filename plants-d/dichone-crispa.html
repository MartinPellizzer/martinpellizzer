Dichone crispa

Dichone crispa, commonly known as the crinkled dichone, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, crinkled leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Dichone crispa is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique foliage and delicate floral display.
