Drimopogon menziesii

Drimopogon menziesii, commonly known as the Menzies' drimopogon, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of about 10 to 20 centimeters. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded, forested areas.
