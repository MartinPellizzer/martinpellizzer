Dahlia juarezii

Dahlia juarezii, commonly known as the Mexican dahlia, is a tuberous-rooted perennial plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, single or double flowers that come in a wide range of colors. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, with sturdy stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early fall, adding vibrant color to gardens.

Dahlia juarezii thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
