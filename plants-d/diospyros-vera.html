Diospyros vera

Diospyros vera, commonly known as the sweet persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that develop into orange to red, fleshy fruits. The fruit is typically sweet and edible when fully ripe, containing astringent tannins when unripe.

Diospyros vera is valued for its ornamental appeal and its fruit, which is used in both culinary and traditional medicinal applications.
