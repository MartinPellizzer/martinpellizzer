Diospyros alata

Diospyros alata, commonly known as the winged persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive winged fruits, which are typically orange to red in color when mature. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in panicles.

The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.
