Digera angustifolia

Digera angustifolia, commonly known as slender false flax, is a perennial herb native to the Americas.

It features slender, erect stems that are often hairy and can grow up to 1 meter in height. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in spike-like inflorescences.

It is often found in moist, disturbed soils and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
