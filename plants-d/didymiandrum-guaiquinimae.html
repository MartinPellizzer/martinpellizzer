Didymiandrum guaiquinimae

Didymiandrum guaiquinimae, commonly known as the Guaiquinimae orchid, is a species of orchid native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically feature vibrant colors such as yellow and orange. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually elongated and grass-like, adapting to the humid, shaded environments it inhabits.

The orchid relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake, highlighting its ecological interdependence within its native habitat.
