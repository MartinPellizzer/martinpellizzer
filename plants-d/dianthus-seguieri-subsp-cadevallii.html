Dianthus seguieri subsp. cadevallii

Dianthus seguieri subsp.

cadevallii, commonly known as the rock campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its dense, cushion-like growth habit and produces clusters of pink or red flowers with five petals.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its compact size and vibrant blooms make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
