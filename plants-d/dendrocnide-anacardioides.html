Dendrocnide anacardioides

Dendrocnide anacardioides, commonly known as the stinging tree, is a shrub or small tree native to northern Australia.

It belongs to the family Anacardiaceae and is characterized by its dense, thorny foliage and small, white flowers. The plant is notable for its toxic sap, which contains urushiol-like compounds that cause severe skin irritation. Its leaves are typically alternate and pinnately compound, with serrated margins.

The stinging tree thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in rainforests and coastal areas.
