Deyeuxia airoides

Deyeuxia airoides, commonly known as the velvetgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in moist, shady environments, often found in forest understories or along streambanks.
