Desmodium stuhlmannii

Desmodium stuhlmannii, commonly known as the slender desmodium, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features trifoliate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Due to its ornamental value and potential ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
