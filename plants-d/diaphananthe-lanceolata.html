Diaphananthe lanceolata

Diaphananthe lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in open, sunny areas and is often found in disturbed soils. It has a taproot system and produces seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Diaphananthe lanceolata is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
