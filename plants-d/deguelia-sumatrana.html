Deguelia sumatrana

Deguelia sumatrana, commonly known as the Sumatran wistaria, is a climbing plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its vigorous growth and twining habit. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple, often blooming in spring. Its leaves are compound, typically with three to five leaflets, and it has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize its climbing structure.

Deguelia sumatrana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
