Digitorebutia pectinata

Digitorebutia pectinata, commonly known as the pectinata euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often surrounded by colorful bracts. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
