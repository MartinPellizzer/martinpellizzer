Drypetes morocarpa

Drypetes morocarpa, commonly known as the Morocarpa flame tree, is a tropical tree belonging to the family Euphorbiaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its striking, bright red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has compound leaves with long, narrow leaflets and produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
