Dendrocalamus atroviridis

Dendrocalamus atroviridis, commonly known as black bamboo, is a tall, fast-growing grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its dark green, often purplish-brown stems. The plant is known for its strong, flexible culms, which are widely used in construction and handicrafts. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Dendrocalamus atroviridis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in reforestation projects.
