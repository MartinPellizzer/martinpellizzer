Diospyros antongilensis

Diospyros antongilensis, commonly known as the Madagascar persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by orange-red, fleshy fruits that are edible. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
