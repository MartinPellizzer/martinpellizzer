Diphaca cochinchinensis

Diphaca cochinchinensis, commonly known as the Vietnamese orchid or wild orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in forested areas, particularly in the understory of humid tropical climates. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. It is native to Southeast Asia, including countries like Vietnam, Cambodia, and Laos.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
