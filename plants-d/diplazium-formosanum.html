Diplazium formosanum

The plant produces sporangia on the underside of its fertile fronds, which are clustered in sori covered by a thin, membranous indusium. Diplazium formosanum thrives in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky slopes.

Its rhizome is creeping and gives rise to the fronds, making it a common ground cover in its native habitat.
