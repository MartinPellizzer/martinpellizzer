Dombeya decanthera subsp. latipetala

Dombeya decanthera subsp.

latipetala, commonly known as the broad-petaled dombeya, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a prominent yellow center.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has lance-shaped leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its flowering period occurs during the spring and early summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
