Dendrochilum weberi

Dendrochilum weberi, commonly known as the Weber's tree orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, woody stems and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in clusters. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in its natural habitat. Its flowers are usually fragrant and come in shades of white, yellow, and pink.

Dendrochilum weberi thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining potting media for optimal growth.
