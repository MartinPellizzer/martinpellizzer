Dieffenbachia seguine var. maculata

Dieffenbachia seguine var. maculata, commonly known as the Leopard Plant, is a member of the Araceae family and is native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, arrow-shaped leaves that are predominantly green with distinctive white or cream-colored spots. The plant is a herbaceous perennial that thrives in warm, humid environments with indirect light. Its foliage is often used in interior decorating due to its vibrant appearance and air-purifying qualities.

Dieffenbachia seguine var. maculata is relatively low-maintenance, making it a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor cultivation.
