Deyeuxia tarmensis

Deyeuxia tarmensis, commonly known as the Tarmian moor grass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It typically grows in wet meadows, marshes, and other moist environments, showcasing its adaptability to hygric conditions. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that often form dense clumps, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are usually arranged in a loose panicle, characteristic of the Poaceae family.

Deyeuxia tarmensis plays a role in stabilizing soil in wetland ecosystems and is of interest to botanists studying grassland biodiversity.
