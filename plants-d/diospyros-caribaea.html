Diospyros caribaea

Diospyros caribaea, commonly known as the Caribbean persimmon, is a small to medium-sized tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes ebony and persimmon trees. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically greenish-yellow, and it produces small, fleshy berries that are edible when ripe.

The tree is valued for its ornamental potential and its fruit, which is rich in nutrients and has a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor.
