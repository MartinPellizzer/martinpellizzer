Dryopteris sellowii

Dryopteris sellowii, commonly known as the Sellow's wood fern, is a species of fern native to South America.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its intricate, lace-like fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine, and it exhibits a rhizomatous growth habit.

The fronds are usually bipinnate, with a distinctive pattern of sori along the margins of the pinnae.
