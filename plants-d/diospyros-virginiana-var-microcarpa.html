Diospyros virginiana var. microcarpa

Diospyros virginiana var. microcarpa, commonly known as the small-fruited persimmon, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, orange to reddish fruits that are typically less than 1 inch in diameter, which ripen in the fall. Its flowers are inconspicuous, greenish-yellow, and appear in clusters, while the leaves turn vibrant red or orange in autumn.

This variety is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role.
