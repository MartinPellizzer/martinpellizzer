Diceratella sinuata var. elliptica

Diceratella sinuata var. elliptica, commonly known as the elliptical pincushion cactus, is a succulent plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny stems that form a cushion-like structure. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it stores water in its fleshy stems for survival in arid conditions.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
