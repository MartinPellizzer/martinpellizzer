Draba hoppeana

Draba hoppeana, commonly known as the Hoppe's wintercress, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils and cool climates. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers during the spring, followed by narrow, elongated pods that contain the seeds. It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants.

Draba hoppeana is often found in alpine meadows and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
