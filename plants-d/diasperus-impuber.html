Diasperus impuber

Diasperus impuber, commonly known as the African milkweed, is a member of the Apocynaceae family.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and opposite, ovate leaves. The flowers are typically yellow or orange, with five lobes, and are clustered in umbels. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny, white seeds with tufts of hair for wind dispersal.

It is native to southern Africa and thrives in dry, sandy soils.
