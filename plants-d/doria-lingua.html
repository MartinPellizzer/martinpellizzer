Doria lingua

Doria lingua, commonly known as the golden rain tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery foliage and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has compound leaves with many narrow, thread-like leaflets. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree is also valued for its ability to provide shade and its role in supporting local wildlife.
