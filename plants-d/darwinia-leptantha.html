Darwinia leptantha

Darwinia leptantha, commonly known as the slender-leaved Darwinia, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit. The plant produces spikes of showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in fine hairs.

Darwinia leptantha is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and heathlands.
