Dendrobium purpureum subsp. candidulum

Dendrobium purpureum subsp.

candidulum, commonly known as the white-veined orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia. It features a sympodial growth habit with cane-like stems that can grow up to several meters in length.

The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, often with contrasting veining. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires high humidity and well-draining potting media.

This subspecies is prized for its ornamental value and is commonly cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions.
