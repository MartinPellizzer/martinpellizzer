Dictyopteris subaequalis

Dictyopteris subaequalis, commonly known as the broad-leafed sea palm, is a species of brown algae belonging to the family Dictyopteraceae.

It is characterized by its dense, bushy growth and dark green to brown coloration. The plant forms a thallus with a central stalk and numerous branching fronds, which are typically broad and flat. It thrives in shallow, coastal waters and is often found in rocky substrates.

This species plays an important role in marine ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various marine organisms.
