Diasperus hyssopifolioides

Diasperus hyssopifolioides, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves arranged in a basal rosette, and its flowers are small, clustered in dense heads with a distinctive yellow center. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

The plant is known for its resilience and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
