Daphne oleoides var. puberula

Daphne oleoides var. puberula, commonly known as the woolly daphne, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers in early spring, which are followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in partial to full shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for woodland gardens and as a specimen plant.
