Dichrostachys spicata

Dichrostachys spicata, commonly known as the African burr legume, is a shrub native to sub-Saharan Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and woody, thorny stems. The plant produces pods that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind and animals. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Dichrostachys spicata is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in soil enrichment and as a forage plant.
