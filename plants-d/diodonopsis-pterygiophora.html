Diodonopsis pterygiophora

Diodonopsis pterygiophora, commonly known as the winged dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, winged bracts that surround the flower head, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by fluffy, seed-bearing structures similar to those of dandelions. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
