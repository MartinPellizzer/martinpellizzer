Daphne cneorum subsp. verlotii

Daphne cneorum subsp.

verlotii, commonly known as the Corsican daphne, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers.

The plant produces small, woody fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value and unique fragrance, it is often cultivated in horticulture.
