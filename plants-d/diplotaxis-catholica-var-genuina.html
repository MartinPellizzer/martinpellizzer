Diplotaxis catholica var. genuina

Diplotaxis catholica var. genuina, commonly known as the African dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of linear, blue-green leaves and produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid regions. It is often used in rock gardens and as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance.

The species is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.
