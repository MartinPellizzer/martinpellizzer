Draba thlaspiformis

Draba thlaspiformis, commonly known as the alpine mouse-ear cress, is a small flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia, typically found in alpine and subalpine habitats. The plant has basal leaves that are spoon-shaped and a compact, low-growing habit, often forming dense mats. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and arranged in a single cluster at the top of the stem.

Draba thlaspiformis is adapted to cold climates and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations.
