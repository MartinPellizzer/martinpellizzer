Desmodium renifolium

Desmodium renifolium, commonly known as the kidneyleaf clover, is a perennial legume native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are heart-shaped and often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple, typically blooming in the spring and early summer. Desmodium renifolium has a deep root system that helps prevent soil erosion and improves soil fertility through nitrogen fixation.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardiness and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
