Dicoma alemannii-mazzocchii

Dicoma alemannii-mazzocchii, commonly known as the Cape snowbush, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant is typically found in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

It produces small, oval-shaped seeds that are dispersed by wind, and it is often used in rock gardens due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
