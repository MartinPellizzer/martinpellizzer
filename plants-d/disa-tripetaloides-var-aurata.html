Disa tripetaloides var. aurata

Disa tripetaloides var. aurata, commonly known as the golden disa, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow petals and sepals, which contrast beautifully with its deep green foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. It produces delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in late summer, adding a splash of color to its natural habitat.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
