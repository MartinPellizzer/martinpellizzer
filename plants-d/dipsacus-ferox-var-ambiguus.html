Dipsacus ferox var. ambiguus

Dipsacus ferox var. ambiguus, commonly known as the devil's-bit scabious, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, bract-like structures that enclose its flowers, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological and horticultural contexts.
