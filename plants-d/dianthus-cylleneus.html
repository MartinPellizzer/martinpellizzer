Dianthus cylleneus

Dianthus cylleneus, commonly known as the Greek pink, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, pinkish-red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and borders.

Dianthus cylleneus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its aesthetic beauty in gardens.
