Drypetes glomerata var. olivacea

Drypetes glomerata var. olivacea, commonly known as the olive flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically opposite and have a glossy, dark green appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by small, spherical fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its rapid growth and adaptability.
