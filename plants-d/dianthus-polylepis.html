Dianthus polylepis

Dianthus polylepis, commonly known as the Sierra Madre carnation, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its clustered, showy pink or red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in mountainous regions. It has linear, grass-like leaves and is part of the Caryophyllaceae family.

Dianthus polylepis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and hardy nature.
