Dendrobium cellulosum

Dendrobium cellulosum, commonly known as the fibrous orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, fibrous roots and its ability to grow on trees or rocks. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green. It thrives in humid environments with bright, indirect light.

Dendrobium cellulosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its unique floral display.
