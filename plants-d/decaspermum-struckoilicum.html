Decaspermum struckoilicum

Decaspermum struckoilicum, commonly known as the African sandmat, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and clusters of small, greenish flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It has a creeping growth habit and produces small, round, fleshy fruits that contain seeds.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often used in xeriscaping and as a ground cover in dry landscapes.
