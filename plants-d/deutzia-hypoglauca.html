Deutzia hypoglauca

Deutzia hypoglauca, commonly known as the silver-leaf deutzia, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features slender, arching branches and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in spring. The leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a distinctive silvery-blue underside that gives the plant its common name. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.

This ornamental plant is often used in gardens for its attractive foliage and seasonal blooms.
