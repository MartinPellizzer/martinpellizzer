Daphne imerica

Daphne imerica, commonly known as the American daphne, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, and its stems are usually reddish-brown in color. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

However, it is toxic to both humans and animals, requiring caution during handling or consumption.
