Dodonaea acerosa

Dodonaea acerosa, commonly known as the silverleaf acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray, papery leaves that provide a striking visual contrast. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically followed by woody pods containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid regions.

Dodonaea acerosa is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
