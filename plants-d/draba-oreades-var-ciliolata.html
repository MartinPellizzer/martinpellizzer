Draba oreades var. ciliolata

Draba oreades var. ciliolata, commonly known as the alpine mouse-ear cress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial with small, dark green, toothed leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Native to alpine regions of Europe, it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils at high elevations. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and basal rosette growth habit.

It plays a vital role in stabilizing soil in mountainous environments and is often found in meadows and scree slopes.
