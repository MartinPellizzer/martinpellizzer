Deyeuxia montana

Deyeuxia montana, commonly known as the mountain moorgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, acidic soils, often found in mountainous and alpine environments. The plant has slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually covered in a fine layer of hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a single spikelet, which is often concealed among the leaves.

Deyeuxia montana plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
