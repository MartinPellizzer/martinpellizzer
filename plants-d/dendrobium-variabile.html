Dendrobium variabile

Dendrobium variabile, commonly known as the variable orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, cane-like stems that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white, yellow, and pink, typically during the summer months. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining potting media.

Dendrobium variabile is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
