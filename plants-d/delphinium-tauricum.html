Delphinium tauricum

Delphinium tauricum, commonly known as the Taurian larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It features tall, spindly stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, topped with clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a slightly serrated edge. Its flowers are typically arranged in racemes and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Delphinium tauricum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
