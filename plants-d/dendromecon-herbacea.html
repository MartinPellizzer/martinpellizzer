Dendromecon herbacea

Dendromecon herbacea, commonly known as the desert globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant features a tall, branching stem covered in soft, hairy foliage. Its showy yellow flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Dendromecon herbacea is adapted to drought conditions and thrives in well-drained soils.
