Dendrochilum plocoglottoides

Dendrochilum plocoglottoides, commonly known as the broad-leafed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a short, thick rhizome and multiple stems that bear lanceolate to ovate leaves. The plant produces inflorescences with showy, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its flowers have a unique structure with a spurred lip and are pollinated by insects.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
