Delphinium spirocentrum var. grandibracteolatum

Delphinium spirocentrum var. grandibracteolatum, commonly known as the grandibracteolate larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It features tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The flowers are blue to purple in color, arranged in racemes, and are characterized by their spurred petals. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and its roots are fibrous and somewhat thick.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a striking addition to alpine gardens.
