Deparia japonica

Deparia japonica, commonly known as the Japanese shield fern, is a species of fern native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its shield-shaped fronds that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its rhizomes are creeping and help in the propagation of the plant.

The leaves are usually bi- or tri-pinnate, with a distinctive appearance that makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
