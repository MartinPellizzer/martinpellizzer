Datura pseudostramonium

Datura pseudostramonium, commonly known as the nightshade or devil's trumpet, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that typically bloom at night. The plant produces spiny capsules containing numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It is a member of the Solanaceae family, which includes other toxic plants like belladonna and tobacco.

Datura pseudostramonium contains tropane alkaloids, making it highly poisonous to both humans and animals.
