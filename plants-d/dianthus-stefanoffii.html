Dianthus stefanoffii

Dianthus stefanoffii, commonly known as the Balkan carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink or white flowers with prominent red or purple markings. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in mountainous regions. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces flowers in clusters at the ends of its stems.

Dianthus stefanoffii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
