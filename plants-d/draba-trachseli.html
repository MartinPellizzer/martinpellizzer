Draba trachseli

Draba trachseli, commonly known as the Alpine bellflower, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants, and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers. The plant typically has basal rosettes of dark green, woolly leaves and produces bright yellow or white flowers in early spring. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Draba trachseli is often found in mountainous areas of Switzerland, Austria, and Italy.
