Duguetia antioquensis

Duguetia antioquensis, commonly known as the Antioquian bignonia, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Colombia.

It belongs to the Bignoniaceae family, which is characterized by its showy, often yellow or orange flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters, with opposite, ovate leaves that are usually smooth and glossy. Its flowers are arranged in racemes and are notable for their vibrant colors and fragrant scent, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Duguetia antioquensis is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
