Diospyros taamii

Diospyros taamii, commonly known as the African persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by orange-red, fleshy fruits resembling persimmons.

Its fruits are edible, though some varieties may have a mild bitterness, and the plant is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and fruit.
