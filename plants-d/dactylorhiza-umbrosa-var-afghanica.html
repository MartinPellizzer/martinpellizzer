Dactylorhiza umbrosa var. afghanica

Dactylorhiza umbrosa var. afghanica, commonly known as the Afghan marsh orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Asia, particularly Afghanistan and northern Pakistan.

It typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, often in mountainous regions with cool, humid conditions. The plant produces tall, slender stems with several oval-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
