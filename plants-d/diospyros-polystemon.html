Diospyros polystemon

Diospyros polystemon, commonly known as the white persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by orange to red fruits that are edible when fully mature. Its fruit is valued for its sweet, aromatic pulp and is often used in traditional medicine.

The plant is also noted for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and seasonal display of flowers and fruit.
