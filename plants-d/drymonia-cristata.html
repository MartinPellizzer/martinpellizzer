Drymonia cristata

Drymonia cristata, commonly known as the mountain heath, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit. The plant features small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are evergreen, leathery, and arranged in a compact, dense form.

Drymonia cristata thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or wooded areas.
