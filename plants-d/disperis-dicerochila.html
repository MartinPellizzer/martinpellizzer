Disperis dicerochila

Disperis dicerochila, commonly known as the two-lobed disperis, is a flowering plant native to the Eastern and Southern African regions.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in grassland habitats and thrives in well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to orange, with a unique two-lobed structure on the labellum.

Disperis dicerochila is often found in open, sunny areas and is adapted to seasonal rainfall patterns.
