Disperis micrantha

Disperis micrantha, commonly known as the small-flowered disperis, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its compact stature and delicate, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in coastal or mountainous regions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, clustered inflorescences.

Disperis micrantha is often found in open habitats and is adapted to tolerate seasonal droughts.
