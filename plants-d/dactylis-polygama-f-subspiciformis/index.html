Dactylis Polygama F. Subspiciformis

Dactylis polygama f. subspiciformis is a rare and intriguing variant of the common grass species Dactylis polygama. This subspecies is distinguished by its unique floral structure, which features elongated, spike-like inflorescences. Native to specific regions in southern Europe, it thrives in dry, calcareous soils. Its botanical significance lies in its role as a subject of study in plant morphology and taxonomy. Despite its limited distribution, it remains an important species for ecological and horticultural research.
