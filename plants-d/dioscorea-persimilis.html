Dioscorea persimilis

Dioscorea persimilis, commonly known as the slender yam, is a herbaceous perennial vine native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and tuberous roots. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by cylindrical, yellowish fruits. Its tubers are sometimes used in traditional medicine, though they are not as widely cultivated as other yam species.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland habitats.
