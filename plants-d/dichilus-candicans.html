Dichilus candicans

Dichilus candicans, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its clusters of yellow, fragrant flowers that resemble small showers, hence its common name. The plant has compound leaves with long, slender leaflets and produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive floral display.
