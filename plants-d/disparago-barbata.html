Disparago barbata

Disparago barbata, commonly known as the hairy bittercress, is a small flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and can be found in gardens, fields, and along roadsides. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture, while its flowers are usually pink or white.

Disparago barbata is an annual herb that spreads through its seeds, which are dispersed by wind and water.
