Diospyros truncata

Diospyros truncata, commonly known as the Indian persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, orange-red berries. Its fruit is often used in traditional medicine and as a food source.

Diospyros truncata is also valued for its hard, dense wood used in craftsmanship and furniture making.
