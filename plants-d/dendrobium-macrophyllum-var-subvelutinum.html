Dendrobium macrophyllum var. subvelutinum

Dendrobium macrophyllum var. subvelutinum, commonly known as the velvety-leafed dendrobium, is a hybrid orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, slender pseudobulbs and clusters of fragrant, pale yellow to cream-colored flowers. The leaves are thick, fleshy, and covered in a velvety texture, which gives the variety its common name. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

This variety is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
