Deinandra increscens subsp. villosa

Deinandra increscens subsp.

villosa, commonly known as the hairy deinandra, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its woody stems and dense, hairy foliage that gives it its subspecies name.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically visited by various pollinators. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in shrubland or rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
