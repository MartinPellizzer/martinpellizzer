Dracaena phrynioides

Dracaena phrynioides, commonly known as the dwarf dracaena, is a compact, slow-growing succulent native to southern Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically dark green with a subtle reddish or purple tinge along the edges. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires moderate watering, making it suitable for indoor cultivation.

Its resilience to low light and drought conditions makes it a popular choice for beginners in horticulture.
