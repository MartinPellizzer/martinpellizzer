Dioscorea hastifolia

Dioscorea hastifolia, commonly known as the arrowleaf yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and tuberous roots. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits. Its tubers are often used in traditional medicine and as a food source in some cultures.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.
