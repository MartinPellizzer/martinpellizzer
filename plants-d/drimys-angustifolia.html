Drimys angustifolia

Drimys angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved drimys, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in regions of Chile and Argentina.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in temperate rainforests, often in moist, shaded environments. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy, with a distinct aromatic scent. Its flowers are small and white, arranged in clusters, and it produces dark blue berries that are edible.

Drimys angustifolia is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
