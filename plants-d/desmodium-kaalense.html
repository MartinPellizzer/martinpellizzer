Desmodium kaalense

Desmodium kaalense, commonly known as the African velvet bean, is a flowering plant native to sub-Saharan Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is typically shrub-like, with hairy stems and produces pods that contain several seeds.

It is often found in grasslands and woodland areas, where it plays a role in nitrogen fixation due to its symbiotic relationship with Rhizobium bacteria.
