Delphinium duhmbergii var. retropilosum

Delphinium duhmbergii var. retropilosum, commonly known as the woolly delphinium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant produces dense clusters of blue to violet flowers, which are typically borne on long, slender pedicels. Its leaves are lanceolate, with a rough, hairy texture that gives it a distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental value in gardens and its adaptability to well-drained, alkaline soils.
