Diascia caitliniae

Diascia caitliniae, commonly known as the "Caitlin's dwarf scabious," is a compact, flowering plant native to South Africa.

It features small, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of pink, red, and purple, typically appearing in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and a low-growing habit, making it ideal for rock gardens or as a border plant. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, requiring minimal water once established.

Diascia caitliniae is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
