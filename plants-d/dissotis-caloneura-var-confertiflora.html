Dissotis caloneura var. confertiflora

Dissotis caloneura var. confertiflora, commonly known as the dense-flowered dissotis, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and dense clusters of small, spikelet-like flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or wetlands. Its foliage is often narrow and grass-like, with the inflorescence bearing numerous tiny florets that are often pale green or white in color.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its delicate floral display.
