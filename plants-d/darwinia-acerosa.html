Darwinia acerosa

Darwinia acerosa, commonly known as the hard-leaved Darwinia, is a striking plant native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, spiky flower spikes that range in color from pink to red. The plant features narrow, leathery leaves that are typically grey-green in hue, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and dry woodland habitats.

Darwinia acerosa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand dry conditions.
