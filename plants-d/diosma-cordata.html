Diosma cordata

Diosma cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf diosma, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive heart-shaped base, contributing to its ornamental appeal. Its flowers are small, clustered in terminal racemes, and often display shades of white or pale pink.

Diosma cordata is valued in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
