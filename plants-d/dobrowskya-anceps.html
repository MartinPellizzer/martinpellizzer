Dobrowskya anceps

Dobrowskya anceps, commonly known as the alpine bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue to violet. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which helps to reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Dobrowskya anceps is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
