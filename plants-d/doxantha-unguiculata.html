Doxantha unguiculata

Doxantha unguiculata, commonly known as the fingerleaf stinging nettle, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family, which includes many stinging plants. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy coating and small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters. Its stems are covered in fine hairs that can cause a stinging sensation when touched.

Doxantha unguiculata is often found in wetlands and along riverbanks, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils.
