Diplaziopsis javanica subsp. cavaleriana

Diplaziopsis javanica subsp.

cavaleriana, commonly known as the Javanese sword fern, is a member of the Polypodiaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia. It is a terrestrial fern characterized by its upright, sword-shaped fronds that are typically dark green and glossy.

The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, with the rhizome often covered in a thick, fibrous sheath. Its spore-bearing structures, called sori, are clustered on the underside of the fronds and are protected by a single, kidney-shaped indusium.

This species is often found in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices.
