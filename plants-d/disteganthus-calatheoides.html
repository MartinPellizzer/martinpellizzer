Disteganthus calatheoides

Disteganthus calatheoides, commonly known as the velvetleaf.

This plant is a member of the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The foliage typically exhibits a deep green color with striking variegation, making it a popular ornamental plant. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in an inflorescence called a spadix, surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe.
