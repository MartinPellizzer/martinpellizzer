Dendrobium callibotrys

Dendrobium callibotrys, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, cane-like stems and clusters of fragrant, golden-yellow flowers. The plant typically blooms during the summer months, producing showy inflorescences that attract pollinators. Its leaves are long and strap-like, often arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

Dendrobium callibotrys is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and relatively easy care requirements.
