Deutzia coreana

Deutzia coreana, commonly known as Korean deutzia, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It features clusters of fragrant, white or pink flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has slender, arching branches and small, oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.

Deutzia coreana is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and easy care requirements.
