Dichapetalum multiflorum

Dichapetalum multiflorum, commonly known as the African milk tree, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in summer. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a distinctive waxy coating.

The bark and sap of the plant contain toxic compounds, which have been used traditionally for various purposes.
