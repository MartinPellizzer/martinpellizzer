Didiclis stipulata

Didiclis stipulata, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvetleaf morning glory, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are velvety to the touch, giving it its common name. The plant produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, typically opening in the morning. Didiclis stipulata is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.

It is also cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and showy blooms.
