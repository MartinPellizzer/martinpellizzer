Dillwynia sericea var. glabriflora

Dillwynia sericea var. glabriflora, commonly known as the silky dillwynia, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod fruits and often nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant features slender, glabrous stems and small, pale blue to violet flowers arranged in racemes. Its leaves are typically narrow and hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and heathlands.
