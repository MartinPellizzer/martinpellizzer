Deckera glomerata

Deckera glomerata, commonly known as the clustered deckera, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale pink. Its flowering period occurs in the spring, and it is often found in association with other wetland plants.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
