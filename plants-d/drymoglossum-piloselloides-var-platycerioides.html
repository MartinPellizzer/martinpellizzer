Drymoglossum piloselloides var. platycerioides

Drymoglossum piloselloides var. platycerioides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bog moss, is a variant of the species native to moist, acidic environments such as bogs and heathlands.

It is characterized by its slender, dark green leaves that are typically less than 1 cm in length. The plant forms dense, cushion-like growths and is often found in nutrient-poor soils. It reproduces through spores and is an important component of peat-forming ecosystems.

This moss is well adapted to low light conditions and can tolerate periodic flooding.
