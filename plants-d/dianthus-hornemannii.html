Dianthus hornemannii

Dianthus hornemannii, commonly known as the hornemann's carnation, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that bloom in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of five-petaled flowers that are typical of the Caryophyllaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Dianthus hornemannii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
