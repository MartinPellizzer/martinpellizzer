Diplopappus

Diplopappus, commonly known as the two-winged sunflower, is a genus of flowering plants in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

These plants are characterized by their composite flower heads, which consist of both ray and disk flowers. The seeds are typically enclosed in a pappus, which aids in wind dispersal. Diplopappus species are native to various regions in Africa, South America, and parts of Asia.

They are often found in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing their adaptability to harsh conditions.
