Diospyros goudotii

Diospyros goudotii, commonly known as the African persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by edible, orange-red fruits that are similar in appearance to persimmons. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

The fruit is valued for its sweet, astringent flavor and is used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
