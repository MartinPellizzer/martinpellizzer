Diospyros addita

Diospyros addita, commonly known as the red-leaf persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that turn red in autumn. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by orange to red fruits, which are edible when fully ripe. Its bark is smooth and light brown, often marked with shallow fissures.

Diospyros addita is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
