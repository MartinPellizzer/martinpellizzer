Dioclea coriacea

Dioclea coriacea, commonly known as the rough-stemmed passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It belongs to the family Passifloraceae and is characterized by its woody stems covered in rough, stiff hairs. The plant produces striking flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a distinctive crown of filaments around the reproductive structures. Its fruit is a fleshy, edible berry that turns red when mature.

Dioclea coriacea is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in shaded or semi-shaded environments.
