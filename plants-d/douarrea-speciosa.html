Douarrea speciosa

Douarrea speciosa, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical plant native to parts of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, pendulous yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, resembling a shower of golden beads. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are typically ovate or lanceolate in shape. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
