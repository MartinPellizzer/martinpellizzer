Desmodium cuspidatum

Desmodium cuspidatum, commonly known as prairie clover, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant features bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its trifoliate leaves are often hairy, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Desmodium cuspidatum is often used in native plant gardens and for erosion control due to its hardy nature.
