Desmodium bellum

Desmodium bellum, commonly known as the "butterfly pea," is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers that resemble butterflies. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. Desmodium bellum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil makes it beneficial for soil enrichment and sustainable agriculture.
