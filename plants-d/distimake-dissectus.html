Distimake dissectus

Distimake dissectus, commonly known as the featherleaf fern, is a delicate perennial plant characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as woodland floors or along stream banks. The plant features a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread slowly and form dense colonies. Its leaves are usually a deep green, with a soft, velvety texture.

Distimake dissectus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
