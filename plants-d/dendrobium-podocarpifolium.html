Dendrobium podocarpifolium

Dendrobium podocarpifolium, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a sympodial epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a unique growth habit with a pseudobulb-like structure that stores water and nutrients. The plant produces pendant, fleshy, and often yellow or golden flowers that bloom during the dry season. Its leaves are typically ovate and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its striking floral display.
