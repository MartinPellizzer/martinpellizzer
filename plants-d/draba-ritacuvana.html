Draba ritacuvana

Draba ritacuvana, commonly known as the alpine bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas, where it thrives in rocky and alpine environments. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue, blooming in early summer. Its leaves are basal, rosette-forming, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Draba ritacuvana is adapted to cold climates and is often found in meadows and slopes at elevations above 3,000 meters.
