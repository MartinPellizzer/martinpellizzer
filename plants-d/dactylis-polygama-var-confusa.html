Dactylis polygama var. confusa

Dactylis polygama var. confusa, commonly known as the woolly oat, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and hairy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pale green to brownish spikelets that are clustered at the ends of its stems. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in meadows and grasslands.

Dactylis polygama var. confusa is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
