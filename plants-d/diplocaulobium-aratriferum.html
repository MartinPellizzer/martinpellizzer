Diplocaulobium aratriferum

Diplocaulobium aratriferum, commonly known as the arrowleaf waterwheel, is a unique aquatic plant characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves.

It is native to freshwater environments in South America, particularly in regions with slow-moving rivers and lakes. The plant features a rhizomatous growth habit, with floating leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically appearing in late spring to early summer.

This species plays an important ecological role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
