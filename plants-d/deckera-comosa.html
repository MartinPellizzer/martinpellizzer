Deckera comosa

Deckera comosa, commonly known as the woolly tree, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that covers its branches, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically gray-green in color and have a leathery texture.

Deckera comosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well adapted to dry, sunny environments.
