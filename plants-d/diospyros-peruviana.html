Diospyros peruviana

Diospyros peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes ebony and other related species. The plant is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and alternate, ovate leaves with a glossy surface. Its fruit is a small, orange-red berry that ripens in late summer and is edible when fully mature.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine and cuisine.
