Diplotaxis Cinerea

Diplotaxis cinerea is a hardy, drought-tolerant plant native to southern Africa. It features gray-green foliage and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. Often used in xeriscaping due to its low water needs, it thrives in sandy or gravelly soils. Its common name, "silverleaf mustard," reflects its distinctive appearance and growth habits. This plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and ability to attract beneficial insects.
