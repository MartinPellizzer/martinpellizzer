Dianthus afghanicus

Dianthus afghanicus, commonly known as the Afghan carnation, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Afghanistan.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces small, seed-like fruits known as capsules.

Dianthus afghanicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for its vibrant blooms.
