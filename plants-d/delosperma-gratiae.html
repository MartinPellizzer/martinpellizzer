Delosperma gratiae

Delosperma gratiae, commonly known as the "Cape carnation," is a low-growing, succulent perennial native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, woolly stems and leaves that are adapted to arid environments, allowing it to store water efficiently. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically in spring and summer. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact size and long blooming period make it an attractive and hardy addition to drought-tolerant landscapes.
