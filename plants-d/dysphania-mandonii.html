Dysphania mandonii

Dysphania mandonii, commonly known as the desert spinach, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing annual herb with small, heart-shaped leaves that are edible and often used in traditional diets. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy, resilient plant suited to harsh desert environments.
