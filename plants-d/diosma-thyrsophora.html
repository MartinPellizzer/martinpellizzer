Diosma thyrsophora

Diosma thyrsophora, commonly known as the desert bell, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to drought conditions. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are small, narrow, and often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Diosma thyrsophora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in desert ecosystems.
