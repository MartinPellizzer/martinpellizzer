Delphinium pentagynum f. exagynum

Delphinium pentagynum f. exagynum, commonly known as the six-lobed larkspur, is a hybrid cultivar of the delphinium genus.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers, which are typically arranged in tall, spires. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of Europe and thrives in well-drained, loamy soils. It prefers full sun to partial shade and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Its foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves that are finely toothed, adding a delicate texture to the plant's appearance.
