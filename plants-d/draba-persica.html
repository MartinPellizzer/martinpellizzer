Draba persica

Draba persica, commonly known as Persian whitlow, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are basal, rosette-like, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Draba persica is adapted to cold, alpine environments and is often found in rocky soils at high elevations.
