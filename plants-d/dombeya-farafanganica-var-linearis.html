Dombeya farafanganica var. linearis

Dombeya farafanganica var. linearis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved dombeya, is a flowering shrub native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and tropical landscapes.
