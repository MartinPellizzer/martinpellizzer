Dentaria digitata f. pubescens

Dentaria digitata f. pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent toothplant, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features a basal rosette of lanceolate, pubescent leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow, daisy-like flowers with a yellow center and white petals, blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
