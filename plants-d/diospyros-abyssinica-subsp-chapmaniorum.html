Diospyros abyssinica subsp. chapmaniorum

Diospyros abyssinica subsp.

chapmaniorum, commonly known as the Ethiopian persimmon, is a species native to Ethiopia and parts of Eritrea. It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree with thick, leathery leaves that are typically elliptical in shape.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by edible, orange to reddish fruits. The fruits are often consumed fresh or processed into jams and wines.

It is valued not only for its fruit but also for its role in local ecosystems and traditional uses.
