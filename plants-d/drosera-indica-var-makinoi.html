Drosera indica var. makinoi

Drosera indica var. makinoi, commonly known as the Makino's Sundew, is a carnivorous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its sticky, glandular hairs that secrete a mucilaginous substance to trap insects. The plant typically grows in wet, boggy environments and has small, rosette-forming leaves. Its flowers are usually white or pink and appear on short, erect stems.

It is valued in horticulture for its unique appearance and ease of cultivation.
