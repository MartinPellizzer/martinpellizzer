Drymophloeus lepidotus

Drymophloeus lepidotus, commonly known as the glossy woodbine, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and opposite, glossy, elliptical leaves that are usually dark green in color. The plant produces small, fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the wet season. Its fruits are elongated, woody capsules that contain several seeds.

Drymophloeus lepidotus is often found in humid, lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in some cultures.
