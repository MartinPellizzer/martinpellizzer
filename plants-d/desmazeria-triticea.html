Desmazeria triticea

Desmazeria triticea, commonly known as the slender false brome, is a perennial grass native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny flowers, which are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
