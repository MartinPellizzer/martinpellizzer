Daphne Hainanensis

Daphne hainanensis is a rare and striking plant native to the island of Hainan in China. It belongs to the Thymelaeaceae family and is known for its showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. The plant is characterized by its evergreen leaves and distinctive branched stems that add to its ornamental appeal. Due to its limited habitat and susceptibility to environmental changes, it is considered an endangered species. Conservation efforts are crucial to preserving this unique botanical treasure.
