Didymoplexis avaratraensis

Didymoplexis avaratraensis, commonly known as the Madagascar orchid, is a species of orchid native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique floral structure and vibrant coloration. The plant typically features showy, pendulous flowers with a distinct lip that serves as a landing platform for pollinators. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ecological significance and ornamental value, it is a subject of interest for botanists and horticulturists alike.
