Dactylorhiza meyeri

Dactylorhiza meyeri, commonly known as the Meyer's marsh orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to wetland and marsh habitats in Europe.

It produces tall, erect stems with whorled leaves and spikes of fragrant, violet-blue flowers. The plant has a complex root system with a rhizome that helps it survive in waterlogged conditions. It is characterized by its showy, lip-shaped petals and is often found in peat bogs and along riverbanks.

Dactylorhiza meyeri is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in horticulture.
