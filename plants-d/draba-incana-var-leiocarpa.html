Draba incana var. leiocarpa

Draba incana var. leiocarpa, commonly known as the smoothleaf whitlowgrass, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically features a hairy stem with alternate, linear leaves that are often glabrous or only slightly hairy. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its delicate appearance and ecological significance, it is a subject of interest in botanical studies and conservation efforts.
