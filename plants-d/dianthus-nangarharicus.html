Dianthus nangarharicus

Dianthus nangarharicus, commonly known as the Nangarhar carnation, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Afghanistan.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink or white flowers with prominent red or purple markings. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving at elevations between 2,000 and 4,000 meters. Its leaves are linear and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also studied for its potential medicinal properties.
