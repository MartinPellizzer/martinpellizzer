Delphinium speciosum var. dasycarpum

Delphinium speciosum var. dasycarpum, commonly known as the Alpine larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features tall, sparsely branched stems that bear clusters of blue to purple flowers, which are typically arranged in racemes. The leaves are lance-shaped and have a serrated margin, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This variety is characterized by its dense, hairy growth and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky areas.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
