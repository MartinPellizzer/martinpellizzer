Digitalis lutea var. minor

Digitalis lutea var. minor, commonly known as the lesser yellow foxglove, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clustered flowers. The flowers are typically yellow or orange, with spurred petals that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in woodland and meadow habitats, preferring moist, well-drained soils.

This plant is noted for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
