Dianthus monspessulanus var. barbatus

Dianthus monspessulanus var. barbatus, commonly known as the Barbatus Campion, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and produces clusters of pink or red flowers with a distinctive frilled appearance. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in herbal medicine.
