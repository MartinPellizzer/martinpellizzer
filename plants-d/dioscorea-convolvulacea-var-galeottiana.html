Dioscorea convolvulacea var. galeottiana

Dioscorea convolvulacea var. galeottiana, commonly known as the Galeottian yam, is a tropical climbing plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its vigorous vining habit and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its tuberous roots are edible and have been used traditionally in various cuisines.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic features.
