Digitaria eriolepis

Digitaria eriolepis, commonly known as hairy fingergrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brown flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are narrow, hairy, and often have a bluish-green hue.

Digitaria eriolepis is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
