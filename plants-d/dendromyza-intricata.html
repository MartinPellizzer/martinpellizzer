Dendromyza intricata

Dendromyza intricata, commonly known as the intricate woodvine, is a climbing plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its complex, intertwining stems that allow it to ascend trees and other vegetation. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters along the stems. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a glossy surface.

Dendromyza intricata thrives in humid environments with high light exposure and is often found in secondary growth areas.
