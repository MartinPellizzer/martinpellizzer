Dioscorea tenuifolia

Dioscorea tenuifolia, commonly known as slender yam, is a climbing herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are often used in traditional medicine and as a food source. The plant has compound leaves with long, slender leaflets and produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically unisexual.

Its tubers are valued for their nutritional content and have been used in various cultural and medicinal practices across its native range.
