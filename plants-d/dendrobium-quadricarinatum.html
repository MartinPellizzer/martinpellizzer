Dendrobium quadricarinatum

Dendrobium quadricarinatum, commonly known as the four-ribbed orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, cane-like pseudobulbs and long, linear leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers with a distinctive, narrow lip. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.

Dendrobium quadricarinatum is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
