Delphinium caeruleum var. majus

Delphinium caeruleum var. majus, commonly known as the blue larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant produces showy, spurred flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem, with a rough, hairy texture.

Delphinium caeruleum var. majus thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
