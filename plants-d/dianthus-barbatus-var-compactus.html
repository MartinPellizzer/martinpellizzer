Dianthus barbatus var. compactus

Dianthus barbatus var. compactus, commonly known as the compact carnation, is a compact, bushy plant with dense clusters of showy, bilaterally symmetrical flowers.

It typically features vibrant pink, red, or white petals with a distinctive frilled edge, and its flowers are borne on short, sturdy stems. The plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure. It is a popular choice for garden borders and containers due to its compact growth habit and long blooming period.

The foliage is narrow, grass-like leaves that are evergreen in mild climates.
