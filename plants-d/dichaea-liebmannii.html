Dichaea liebmannii

Dichaea liebmannii, commonly known as the Liebmann's ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic orchid species native to the cloud forests of Colombia.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid with delicate, pale green to white flowers that bloom during the dry season. The plant produces a single, slender stem with a few leaves and a single flower, which is often camouflaged among the leaf litter. It is adapted to grow in shaded, humid environments with poor soil, relying on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, Dichaea liebmannii is considered endangered and is of significant interest to botanists and conservationists.
