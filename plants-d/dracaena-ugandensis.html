Dracaena ugandensis

Dracaena ugandensis, commonly known as the Uganda dragon tree, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of East Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and narrow, elongated leaves that are often blue-green in color. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom in summer, followed by red berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Dracaena ugandensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
