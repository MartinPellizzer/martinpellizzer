Druparia cerasus

Druparia cerasus, commonly known as the cherry tree, is a deciduous tree belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically ovate or elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers in early spring, which are followed by fleshy, red or yellow drupes. The fruit, known as a cherry, contains a single hard pit or stone.

Druparia cerasus is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of fruit for consumption.
