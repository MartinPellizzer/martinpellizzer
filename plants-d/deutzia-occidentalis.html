Deutzia occidentalis

Deutzia occidentalis, commonly known as western deutzia, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet and features clusters of fragrant, white or pink flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has slender, arching branches and dark green, oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a versatile addition to gardens.

Its ornamental value comes from its showy flowers and attractive foliage throughout the growing season.
