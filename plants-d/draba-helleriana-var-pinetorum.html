Draba helleriana var. pinetorum

Draba helleriana var. pinetorum, commonly known as the pine forest draba, is a low-growing, herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and stipitate glands. The plant typically thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats. Its foliage consists of basal rosettes of narrow, linear leaves, and it produces clusters of white or pale yellow flowers in early spring.

This variety is notable for its adaptability to harsh environmental conditions and its role in stabilizing soil in mountainous areas.
