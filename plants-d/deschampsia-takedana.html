Deschampsia takedana

Deschampsia takedana, commonly known as Takeda's feathergrass, is a perennial grass native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and fluffy, silver-green foliage that resembles a feather. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in open, sunny habitats. Its flowers are small and spike-like, forming dense, fluffy tufts at the ends of the stems.

Deschampsia takedana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its delicate, grass-like appearance.
