Drynaria vestita

Drynaria vestita, commonly known as the slender tree fern, is a member of the Dryopteridaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnate fronds. The plant has a unique ability to form a trunk-like structure from its rhizome, which can grow up to several meters in height. Its fronds are typically bipinnate and have a leathery texture, contributing to its ornamental value.

Drynaria vestita is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and is used in tropical landscaping.
