Dianthus inoxianus

Dianthus inoxianus, commonly known as the silverleaf campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, delicate flowers that range in color from pink to white. The leaves are typically narrow and covered in a fine, silvery down, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Dianthus inoxianus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its subtle beauty.
