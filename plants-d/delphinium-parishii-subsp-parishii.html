Delphinium parishii subsp. parishii

Delphinium parishii subsp.

parishii, commonly known as the Sierra delphinium, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy blue to purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine meadows, where it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and it produces numerous small, showy flowers that bloom in late summer.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in high-altitude ecosystems.
