Dichroanthus scoparius

Dichroanthus scoparius, commonly known as the yellow larkspur, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, showy yellow flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows and along stream banks.

Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
