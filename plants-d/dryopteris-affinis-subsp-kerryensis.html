Dryopteris affinis subsp. kerryensis

Dryopteris affinis subsp.

kerryensis, commonly known as Kerry fern, is a variant of the common wood fern. It is a deciduous fern characterized by its fronds that are typically bipinnate, with a distinctive, arching growth habit.

Native to the mountainous regions of Ireland, it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

Its spores are produced on the undersides of the fronds, contributing to its reproductive strategy in forest understories.
