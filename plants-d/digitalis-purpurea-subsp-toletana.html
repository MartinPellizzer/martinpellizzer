Digitalis purpurea subsp. toletana

Digitalis purpurea subsp.

toletana, commonly known as the Toletan foxglove, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It features tall, erect stems that bear clusters of hooded, violet-blue flowers with distinctive spurred petals.

The leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a rough texture and a deep green color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
