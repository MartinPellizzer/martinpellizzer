Diospyros macrocarpa

Diospyros macrocarpa, commonly known as the African persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and large, palmately lobed leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into edible, orange-red fruits with a sweet, astringent flavor. Its fruits are often consumed fresh or used in traditional medicine, and the wood is valued for its durability and fine grain.

The plant is also noted for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
