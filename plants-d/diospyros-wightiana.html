Diospyros wightiana

Diospyros wightiana, commonly known as the Indian persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its rough, dark bark and simple, alternate leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by orange to red, fleshy fruits that are edible when ripe. Its fruit is valued for its sweet, astringent flavor and is used in traditional cuisine and medicine.

The plant is also appreciated for its ornamental value and ecological importance in its native habitat.
