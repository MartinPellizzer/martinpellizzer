Dicentra spectabilis

Dicentra spectabilis, commonly known as bleeding heart, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped, pendulous flowers that bloom in spring, typically in shades of pink or white. The plant features trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped leaflets and a fibrous root system. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Dicentra spectabilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to naturalize in suitable conditions.
