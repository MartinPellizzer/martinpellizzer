Dipteracanthus monanthus var. nummulariifolius

Dipteracanthus monanthus var. nummulariifolius, commonly known as the silverleaf dipteracanthus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, silver-gray leaves that are round and resemble coins, giving it its specific epithet "nummulariifolius.". The plant produces clusters of pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a unique subject for horticultural and ecological studies.
