Drosera zonaria

Drosera zonaria, commonly known as the zoned sundew, is a carnivorous plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its rosette of hairy, glandular leaves that secrete a sticky substance to trap insects. The plant's leaves are typically green with red or pink veins, giving it a striking appearance. It thrives in wet, acidic environments and is often found in swampy or boggy areas.

Drosera zonaria is a perennial species that produces small, white flowers during the warmer months.
