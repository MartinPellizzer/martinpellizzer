Diospyros lateriflora

Diospyros lateriflora, commonly known as the swamp ebony, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, heavy wood and its ability to thrive in wetland environments. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a waxy coating. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are borne in clusters and are followed by small, round fruits that are typically red when mature.

Diospyros lateriflora is valued for its hardy nature and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
