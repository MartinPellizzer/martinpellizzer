Dioscorea tsaratananensis

Dioscorea tsaratananensis, commonly known as the Madagascar yam, is a species native to the rainforests of Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary part used for propagation and potential medicinal purposes. The plant typically grows as a climbing vine with heart-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique adaptations to the tropical environment and its role in local ecosystems.
