Dianthus orientalis var. brachyodontus

Dianthus orientalis var. brachyodontus, commonly known as the Armenian carnation, is a perennial plant native to the Caucasus region.

It features compact, bushy growth with dense clusters of showy, pink to red flowers. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically double or semi-double, with petals that are short and broad, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and resilience.
