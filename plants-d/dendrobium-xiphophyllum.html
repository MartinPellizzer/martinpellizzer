Dendrobium xiphophyllum

Dendrobium xiphophyllum, commonly known as the golden xiphophorus orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, cane-like stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces clusters of showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange, often with a reddish or brownish tint. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil.

Dendrobium xiphophyllum is a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor cultivation due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
