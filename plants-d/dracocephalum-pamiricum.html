Dracocephalum pamiricum

Dracocephalum pamiricum, commonly known as the Pamir skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, thriving at high altitudes. Its flowers are small and clustered, often displaying shades of purple or blue, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Dracocephalum pamiricum is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for various health purposes.
