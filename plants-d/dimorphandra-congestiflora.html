Dimorphandra congestiflora

Dimorphandra congestiflora, commonly known as the clustered flame tree, is a species of leguminous tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, clustered inflorescences that produce bright orange to red flowers, which bloom in the dry season. The plant has compound leaves with pinnate arrangement and small, stipulate leaflets. Its seeds are enclosed in woody pods and are often dispersed by animals.

Dimorphandra congestiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its vibrant floral displays.
