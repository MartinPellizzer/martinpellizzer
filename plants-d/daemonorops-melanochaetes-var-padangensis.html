Daemonorops melanochaetes var. padangensis

Daemonorops melanochaetes var. padangensis, commonly known as the Padang palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnate leaves. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and lowland forests, thriving in humid, warm climates. Its leaves are long and narrow, often reaching several meters in length, and are used in traditional crafts and as thatching material.

The Padang palm is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
