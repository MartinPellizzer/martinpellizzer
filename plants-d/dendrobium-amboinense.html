Dendrobium amboinense

Dendrobium amboinense, commonly known as the Amboina orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the island of Ambon in Indonesia.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and linear, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers with prominent yellow lips. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Dendrobium amboinense is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its unique floral display.
