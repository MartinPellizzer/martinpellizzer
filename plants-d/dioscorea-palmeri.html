Dioscorea palmeri

Dioscorea palmeri, commonly known as Palmer's yam, is a member of the Dioscoreaceae family and is native to the southwestern United States.

It is a perennial vine characterized by its climbing habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by tuberous roots that are the primary part used. These tubers are notable for their medicinal properties and have been traditionally used by indigenous peoples.

Dioscorea palmeri thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.
