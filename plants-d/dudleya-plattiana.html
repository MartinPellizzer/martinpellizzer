Dudleya plattiana

Dudleya plattiana, commonly known as Platt's liveforever, is a succulent plant native to California.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. The plant typically grows in rocky outcrops and well-drained soils, showcasing a rosette growth form. Its flowers, which appear in spring, are usually pink or purple and are borne on tall stalks.

Dudleya plattiana is a popular choice for rock gardens due to its low maintenance and ornamental appeal.
