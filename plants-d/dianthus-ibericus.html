Dianthus ibericus

Dianthus ibericus, commonly known as the Iberian carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, which includes other popular garden flowers like carnations and pinks. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters, featuring upright stems and clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. Its flowers bloom from spring to summer, and the plant is known for its compact growth habit and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

Dianthus ibericus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
