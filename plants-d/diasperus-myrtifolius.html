Diasperus myrtifolius

Diasperus myrtifolius, commonly known as the myrtle-leaved diasperus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for low-maintenance landscaping.
