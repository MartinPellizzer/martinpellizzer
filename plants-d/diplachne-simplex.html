Diplachne simplex

Diplachne simplex, commonly known as the slender flatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, flat leaves and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in coastal and saline environments, showing adaptability to harsh conditions. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a panicle, with small, two-ranked flowers.

Diplachne simplex plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
