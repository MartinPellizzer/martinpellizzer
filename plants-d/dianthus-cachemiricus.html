Dianthus cachemiricus

Dianthus cachemiricus, commonly known as the Kashmir carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of India and Pakistan.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous small seeds.

Dianthus cachemiricus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
