Danthonia penicillata var. pallida

Danthonia penicillata var. pallida, commonly known as the pale danthonia, is a perennial grass species native to parts of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and compact, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and is adapted to full sun to partial shade conditions. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often tinged with a pale hue.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
