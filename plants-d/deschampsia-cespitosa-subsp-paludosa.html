Deschampsia cespitosa subsp. paludosa

Deschampsia cespitosa subsp.

paludosa, commonly known as the wetland bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to wet or moist habitats in North America. It typically grows in marshes, bogs, and along the edges of ponds and streams.

The plant forms dense, tufted clumps with fine, blue-green foliage that is often slightly hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a loose panicle of spikelets, which are usually pinkish to purplish in color.

This subspecies is adapted to waterlogged soils and plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
