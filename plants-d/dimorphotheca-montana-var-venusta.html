Dimorphotheca montana var. venusta

Dimorphotheca montana var. venusta, commonly known as the Cape marguerite, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with white petals and yellow centers. The plant typically grows in coastal regions and is adapted to sandy, well-drained soils. It produces both ray and disk flowers, with the latter being tubular and used for attracting pollinators.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
