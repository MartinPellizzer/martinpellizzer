Dactylorhiza × formosa nothovar. robertsii

Dactylorhiza × formosa nothovar. robertsii, commonly known as the Roberts' marsh orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, fragrant blossoms during the spring and early summer.

This nothovar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and wetland restoration projects.
