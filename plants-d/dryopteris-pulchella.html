Dryopteris pulchella

Dryopteris pulchella, commonly known as the elegant wood fern, is a deciduous fern native to the eastern United States.

It features fronds that are typically bipinnate, with a graceful, arching appearance. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodland areas. Its reproductive structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

The elegant wood fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly used in horticulture.
