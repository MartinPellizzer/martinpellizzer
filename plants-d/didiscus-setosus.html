Didiscus setosus

Didiscus setosus, commonly known as the hairy didiscus, is a unique aquatic plant characterized by its distinctive, floating, saucer-shaped leaves.

The leaves are covered in fine, hair-like structures that give the plant its common name and aid in buoyancy. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in ponds and lakes. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically submerged, contributing to its ecological role in supporting aquatic life.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for study in freshwater ecosystems.
