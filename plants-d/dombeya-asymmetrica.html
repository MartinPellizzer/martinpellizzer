Dombeya asymmetrica

Dombeya asymmetrica, commonly known as the asymmetric dombeya, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant, bell-shaped flowers that typically bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces clusters of white or pale yellow petals, often with a subtle pink or red hue at the base. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Dombeya asymmetrica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its long-lasting blooms.
