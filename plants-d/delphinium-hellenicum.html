Delphinium hellenicum

Delphinium hellenicum, commonly known as the Greek larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Greece.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers in late spring to early summer, arranged in dense spikes at the top of the stems. Its foliage consists of palmately compound leaves with serrated edges, and it thrives in well-drained, alkaline soils.

Delphinium hellenicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
