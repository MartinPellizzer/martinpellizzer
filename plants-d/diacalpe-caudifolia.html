Diacalpe caudifolia

Diacalpe caudifolia, commonly known as the long-tailed banyan, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, long, slender tendrils that hang from its branches. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in panicles. Its fruit is a small, round drupe that contains a single seed.

Diacalpe caudifolia is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
