Dioscorea verticillata

Dioscorea verticillata, commonly known as the spiral yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive whorled arrangement of leaves, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by elongated, tuberous roots that are often used in traditional medicine. Its tubers are rich in starch and have been utilized as a food source in various cultures.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
