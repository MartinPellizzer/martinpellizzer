Diospyros pubescens var. pubescens

Diospyros pubescens var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy persimmon, is a species of flowering plant in the Ebenaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand, Indonesia, and Malaysia. The plant is a small to medium-sized tree characterized by its rough, bark-like trunk and lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its fruit is a small, orange to red berry that matures in autumn and is edible when fully ripe.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine and as a food source.
