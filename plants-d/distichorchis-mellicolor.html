Distichorchis mellicolor

Distichorchis mellicolor, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a striking orchid species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow petals and sepals, which give it its distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are highly fragrant, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Botanically, it belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is noted for its complex floral structure and vibrant coloration.
