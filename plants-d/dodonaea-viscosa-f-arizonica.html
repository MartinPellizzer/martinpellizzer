Dodonaea viscosa f. arizonica

Dodonaea viscosa f. arizonica, commonly known as Arizona honeyplant, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in panicles, and its leaves are opposite, simple, and usually have a rough texture. The plant is adapted to arid environments, exhibiting drought tolerance and a deep root system. It produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind.

The species is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
