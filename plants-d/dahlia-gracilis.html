Dahlia gracilis

Dahlia gracilis, commonly known as the slender dahlia, is a herbaceous perennial native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its narrow, upright stems and showy, single or double flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and produces its blooms from late summer into early autumn. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem.

Dahlia gracilis is well-suited for garden borders and containers, thriving in full sun and well-drained soil.
