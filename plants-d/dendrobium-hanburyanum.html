Dendrobium hanburyanum

Dendrobium hanburyanum, commonly known as the Hanbury's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is an epiphytic plant, meaning it grows on the surface of trees or rocks without deriving nutrients from them. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white, yellow, and orange. Its stems are typically pendulous and covered in a thick layer of velvety tissue.

Dendrobium hanburyanum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for horticultural purposes.
