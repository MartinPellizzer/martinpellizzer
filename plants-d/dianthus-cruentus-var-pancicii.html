Dianthus cruentus var. pancicii

Dianthus cruentus var. pancicii, commonly known as the Pancici's bloody cranesbill, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking red or pink flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. It grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in sunny, well-drained habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
