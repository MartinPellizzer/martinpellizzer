Dichanthium caricosum var. bilobum

Dichanthium caricosum var. bilobum, commonly known as the two-lobed panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, tufted stems with narrow, linear leaves that are often hairy along the edges. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny florets, typical of the grass family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands or disturbed sites.

Its adaptability makes it a common species in various ecological and agricultural contexts.
