Dorstenia warneckei

Dorstenia warneckei, commonly known as the "Cushion Fig," is a unique member of the Moraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its dense, cushion-like growth habit, with fleshy, spirally arranged leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically arranged in a compact inflorescence, and it produces edible fig-like fruits that are a food source for various wildlife.

Due to its striking appearance and ecological significance, it is a valued plant in both horticulture and conservation efforts.
