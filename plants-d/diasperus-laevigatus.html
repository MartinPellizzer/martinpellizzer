Diasperus laevigatus

Diasperus laevigatus, commonly known as the smooth rock cress, is a species of flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to parts of southern Africa, where it typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant is characterized by its small, rounded leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It is often found in coastal or mountainous regions, thriving in well-drained environments.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
