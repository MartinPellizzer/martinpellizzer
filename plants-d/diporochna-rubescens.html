Diporochna rubescens

Diporochna rubescens, commonly known as the red-stemmed thornbush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems that turn reddish-brown with age, and its small, clustered leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, typically blooming in spring. Its fruit is a small, three-lobed capsule that contains several seeds.

Diporochna rubescens is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand arid conditions.
