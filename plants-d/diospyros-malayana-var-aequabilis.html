Diospyros malayana var. aequabilis

Diospyros malayana var. aequabilis, commonly known as the Malay persimmon, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in catkins, followed by fleshy, orange-red berries. Its fruit is edible when fully mature and has a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor.

The species is valued for its ornamental value and traditional uses in local medicine.
