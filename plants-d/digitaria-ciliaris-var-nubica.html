Digitaria ciliaris var. nubica

Digitaria ciliaris var. nubica, commonly known as Nubian fingergrass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. It is adapted to survive in poor soils and can thrive in areas with limited water availability.

This species is often used for forage in livestock grazing systems due to its hardiness and nutritional value.
