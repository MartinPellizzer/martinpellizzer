Delphinium saniculifolium

Delphinium saniculifolium, commonly known as the "lanceleaf larkspur," is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, spurred flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring basal leaves that are lance-shaped and often hairy. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is known for its toxicity, containing alkaloids that can be harmful to livestock and humans if ingested.
