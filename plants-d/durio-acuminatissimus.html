Durio acuminatissimus

Durio acuminatissimus, commonly known as the yellow durian, is a tropical fruit-bearing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Bombacaceae and is characterized by its large, spiky, and highly aromatic fruit. The plant produces a single large fruit per tree, which can weigh up to 10 kilograms. The fruit's pericarp is divided into three distinct segments, each containing a single seed.

It is valued not only for its unique flavor but also for its ornamental value due to its showy flowers and foliage.
