Doronicum hirsutum

Doronicum hirsutum, commonly known as the hairy leopard's bane, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is a member of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its hairy, often silver-green leaves and bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. It produces small, dandelion-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Doronicum hirsutum is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in cooler climates.
