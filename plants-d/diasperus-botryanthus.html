Diasperus botryanthus

Diasperus botryanthus, commonly known as the starry bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of blue and violet. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Diasperus botryanthus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
