Dioscorea burroyacensis

Dioscorea burroyacensis, commonly known as Burroyacensis yam, is a member of the Dioscoreaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary part used for culinary and medicinal purposes. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a rough texture.

This species is valued for its nutritional content and traditional use in various African cultures.
