Dipidax rosea

Dipidax rosea, commonly known as the rose-like daisy, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, pinkish-white petals that resemble those of a daisy, arranged in a radial pattern around a central disc. The plant typically grows in compact, bushy forms, with hairy stems and narrow, linear leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand periods of drought.

Its blooming period occurs during the warmer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
