Diasperus carnosulus

Diasperus carnosulus, commonly known as the fat-tailed gecko, is a unique plant species native to specific regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or white, adding a delicate touch to its appearance. Its growth habit is often shrub-like, with a thick, gnarled stem that provides structural support.

Diasperus carnosulus is also noted for its ability to survive prolonged periods of drought, making it an important species in desert ecosystems.
