Doronicum plantagineum proles gallicum

Doronicum plantagineum proles gallicum, commonly known as the Gallic yellow cinquefoil, is a perennial herb native to Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and it often grows in moist, woodland soils. It is a low-growing plant with a compact habit, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
