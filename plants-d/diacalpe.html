Diacalpe

Diacalpe, commonly known as the golden rain tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces distinctive, winged fruits that disperse its seeds by wind. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Diacalpe is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
