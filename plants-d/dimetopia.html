Dimetopia

Dimetopia, commonly known as the double-flowered dandelion, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a rosette of basal leaves that are typically heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs. Its most distinctive feature is the bright yellow flower head, which consists of two layers of florets—inner and outer—giving it the "double-flowered" appearance.

Dimetopia is often found in meadows, fields, and along roadsides, where it thrives in sunny, well-drained soils.
