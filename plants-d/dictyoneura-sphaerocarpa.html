Dictyoneura sphaerocarpa

Dictyoneura sphaerocarpa, commonly known as the globe-shaped mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on various host trees in Australia.

It has a distinctive spherical or oval-shaped fruit that turns red when mature, which is adapted for bird dispersal. The plant possesses a short stem and a dense, leafy crown, with small, alternate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It relies on its host for water and nutrients but can photosynthesize to some extent.

This species plays a role in the ecosystem by providing food for native birds and insects.
