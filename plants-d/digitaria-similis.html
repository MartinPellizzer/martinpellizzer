Digitaria similis

Digitaria similis, commonly known as flatgrass, is a species of grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its flat, narrow leaves and dense, often creeping growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Digitaria similis is used in some regions for forage and has ecological significance in maintaining soil stability.
