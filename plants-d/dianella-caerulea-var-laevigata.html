Dianella caerulea var. laevigata

Dianella caerulea var. laevigata, commonly known as the smoothleaf flax lily, is a striking evergreen perennial native to New Zealand.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are smooth and glossy, with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers in spring, which are borne on tall, slender stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to form dense, attractive clumps over time.
