Dictamnus fraxinella var. grandiflorus

Dictamnus fraxinella var. grandiflorus, commonly known as the large-flowered dictamnus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It features aromatic, yellowish-green stems that exude a strong, spicy scent when broken. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers arranged in terminal panicles, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have a distinctive, fragrant aroma.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.
