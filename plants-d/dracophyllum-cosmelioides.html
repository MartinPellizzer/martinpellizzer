Dracophyllum cosmelioides

Dracophyllum cosmelioides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved heath, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils in coastal heathlands and is adapted to dry, sunny conditions. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, providing a dense canopy that helps retain moisture.

Dracophyllum cosmelioides is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
