Dracaena madagascariensis

Dracaena madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar dragon tree, is a member of the Asparagaceae family.

It is native to Madagascar and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are typically green with a reddish tinge. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers followed by red berries, which are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its slow growth rate and elegant appearance make it a favored ornamental plant.
