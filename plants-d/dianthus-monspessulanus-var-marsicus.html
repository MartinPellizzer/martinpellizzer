Dianthus monspessulanus var. marsicus

Dianthus monspessulanus var. marsicus, commonly known as the Marsicus carnation, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features compact, bushy growth with dense clusters of showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in early spring. The foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white down. This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and its ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

It is also known for its strong fragrance and historical use in herbal medicine.
