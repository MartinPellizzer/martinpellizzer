Dorycnium pentaphyllum subsp. herbaceum

Dorycnium pentaphyllum subsp.

herbaceum, commonly known as the creeping yellow trefoil, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features trifoliate leaves with three oval, serrated leaflets and produces small, yellow, pea-like flowers arranged in clusters.

The plant has a fibrous root system and is often found in grasslands, woodland edges, and disturbed soils. It is characterized by its herbaceous nature, with stems that remain green and non-woody throughout the growing season.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
