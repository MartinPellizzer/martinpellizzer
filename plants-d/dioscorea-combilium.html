Dioscorea combilium

Dioscorea combilium, commonly known as the African yam bean, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and tuberous roots. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by elongated, bean-like fruits containing numerous seeds. Its tubers are edible and have been traditionally used as a food source in some African communities.

The plant also has medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
