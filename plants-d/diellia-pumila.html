Diellia pumila

Diellia pumila, commonly known as the dwarf daisy, is a small, herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of only a few centimeters, with narrow, grass-like leaves and small white or yellow flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Diellia pumila is adapted to full sun and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
