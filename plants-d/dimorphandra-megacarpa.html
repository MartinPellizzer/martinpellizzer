Dimorphandra megacarpa

Dimorphandra megacarpa, commonly known as the giant sycamore, is a tropical tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its large, compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces woody pods that contain seeds enclosed in a fleshy, red aril. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in humid lowland forests.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important species in its native habitat.
