Diplazium pseudosylvaticum

Diplazium pseudosylvaticum, commonly known as the false sylvatic spleenwort, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its erect, sparsely branched stems and small, lanceolate-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices. Its reproductive structures include sporangia that produce spores for propagation.

Diplazium pseudosylvaticum is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, showcasing its adaptability to humid climates.
