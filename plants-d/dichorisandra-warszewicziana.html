Dichorisandra warszewicziana

Dichorisandra warszewicziana, commonly known as the Zulu lily, is a striking perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features a tall, leafless stem that can grow up to 2 meters in height, topped with a dense cluster of showy, funnel-shaped flowers. The flowers are typically white or pale yellow, with a distinctive purple or violet throat, and bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has fibrous roots and is adapted to grow in well-drained soils, often in rocky or sandy habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its dramatic floral display.
