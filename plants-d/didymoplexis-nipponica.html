Didymoplexis nipponica

Didymoplexis nipponica, commonly known as the Japanese orchid, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant blooms. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with a distinctive lip that aids in pollination.

Didymoplexis nipponica thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forested regions of Japan and Korea.
