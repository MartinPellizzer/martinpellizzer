Dichapetalum ruhlandii

Dichapetalum ruhlandii, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter. The plant produces showy, orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it suitable for warm climates.

Due to its striking appearance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental tree in gardens and parks.
