Dioscorea bridgesii

Dioscorea bridgesii, commonly known as the African yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary part used for culinary and medicinal purposes. The plant produces small, green, heart-shaped leaves and yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. Its tubers are similar in appearance to yams but have a distinct flavor and texture.

The plant is valued for its nutritional content and traditional uses in various African cultures.
