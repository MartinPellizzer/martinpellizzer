Disa picta

Disa picta, commonly known as the painted disa, is a striking member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae) native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, spurred flowers that come in shades of pink, purple, and white, often with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows in rocky or grassy areas, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its elegant appearance.

Disa picta is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological role in supporting local insect populations.
