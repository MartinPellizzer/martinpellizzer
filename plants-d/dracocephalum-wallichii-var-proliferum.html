Dracocephalum wallichii var. proliferum

Dracocephalum wallichii var. proliferum, commonly known as the Tibetan blueweed, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a bluish-green hue, and its small, tubular flowers are typically arranged in dense spikes. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and traditional herbal practices.
