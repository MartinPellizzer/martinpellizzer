Diasperus cladanthus

Diasperus cladanthus, commonly known as the woolly clubmoss, is a small evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant has whorled leaves and produces small, spore-bearing cones. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in shaded, woodland environments.

Diasperus cladanthus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
