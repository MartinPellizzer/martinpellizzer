Discocactus zehntneri var. boomianus

Discocactus zehntneri var. boomianus, commonly known as the Boomian Discocactus, is a rare and unique cactus species native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stem and dense clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom during the cooler months. The plant has a compact growth habit, often forming a low, rounded mound with a distinctive ribbed structure. Its leaves are modified into spines, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a delicate and challenging species to cultivate.
