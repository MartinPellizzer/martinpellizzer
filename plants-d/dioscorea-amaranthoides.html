Dioscorea amaranthoides

Dioscorea amaranthoides, commonly known as the scarlet yam, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by tuberous roots that are often cultivated for their edible qualities. Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a notable species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.

The scarlet yam is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
