Dalea smithii

Dalea smithii, commonly known as Smith's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, pink to purple blossoms. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, bean-like pods that contain its seeds.

Dalea smithii is often found in arid regions and is adapted to thrive in low-nutrient soils.
