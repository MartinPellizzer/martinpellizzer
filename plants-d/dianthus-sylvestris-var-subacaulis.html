Dianthus sylvestris var. subacaulis

Dianthus sylvestris var. subacaulis, commonly known as the woodland carnation, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features slender stems and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial shade, making it a suitable addition to woodland gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological studies and ornamental horticulture.
