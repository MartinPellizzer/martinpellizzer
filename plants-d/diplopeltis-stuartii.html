Diplopeltis stuartii

Diplopeltis stuartii, commonly known as the Stuart's spider orchid, is a rare and striking orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, spider-like appearance, with long, slender inflorescences bearing small, intricate flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in the understory of rainforests. Its flowers are pollinated by specific insects, showcasing a complex mutualistic relationship.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is considered endangered and is protected in many regions.
