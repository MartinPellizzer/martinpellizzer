Dalea scariosa

Dalea scariosa, commonly known as the scarlet globemallow, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its showy, bright red or orange-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It has narrow, gray-green leaves with a fuzzy texture, and its stems are often covered in a whitish, waxy coating.

Dalea scariosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in xeriscapic gardens.
