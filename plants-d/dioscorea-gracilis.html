Dioscorea gracilis

Dioscorea gracilis, commonly known as slender yam, is a herbaceous perennial vine native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and cylindrical, tuberous roots. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant. Its tubers are edible when properly prepared and have been used in traditional medicine for various health benefits.

The species is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
