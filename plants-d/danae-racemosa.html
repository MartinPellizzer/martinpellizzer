Danae racemosa

Danae racemosa, commonly known as the wax plant, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, pendulous inflorescences that resemble clusters of candles, hence its common name. The plant produces small, waxy flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, which are highly attractive to pollinators. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Danae racemosa is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and long-lasting blooms.
