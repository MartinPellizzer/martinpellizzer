Diospyros acapulcensis subsp. chiquimulensis

Diospyros acapulcensis subsp.

chiquimulensis, commonly known as the Chiquimulense persimmon, is a species of flowering plant in the Ebenaceae family. It is native to the highlands of Central America, particularly in regions of Guatemala and Mexico.

The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, clustered flowers that develop into edible, fleshy fruits. The fruit, similar to other persimmons, is typically orange to red in color and has a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental potential and its role in local ecosystems and traditional uses.
