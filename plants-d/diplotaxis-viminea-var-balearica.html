Diplotaxis viminea var. balearica

Diplotaxis viminea var. balearica, commonly known as the Balearic ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, yellow flowers and linear leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It has a compact growth habit and is often used in rock gardens due to its ornamental value.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to local biodiversity.
