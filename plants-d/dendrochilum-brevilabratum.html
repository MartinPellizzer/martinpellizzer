Dendrochilum brevilabratum

Dendrochilum brevilabratum, commonly known as the short-lipped tree orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments within forest understories. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that support its foliage and flowering structures. Its flowers are usually small, with a distinctive short labellum, and are often arranged in racemes.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
