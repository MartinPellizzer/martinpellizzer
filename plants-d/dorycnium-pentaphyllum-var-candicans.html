Dorycnium pentaphyllum var. candicans

Dorycnium pentaphyllum var. candicans, commonly known as the golden salsify, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It features a robust taproot that is often harvested for its edible, slightly sweet, and nutty-tasting tuberous root. The plant has compound leaves with five to seven leaflets and produces yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for both culinary and ornamental purposes.
