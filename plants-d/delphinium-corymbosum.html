Delphinium corymbosum

Delphinium corymbosum, commonly known as the clustered larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that bear clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

Delphinium corymbosum is valued in gardens for its showy blooms and is also known for its toxic alkaloids, making it a plant to handle with care.
