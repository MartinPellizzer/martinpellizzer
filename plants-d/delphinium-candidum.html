Delphinium candidum

Delphinium candidum, commonly known as the white larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that bear clusters of showy, white to pale blue flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. Delphinium candidum is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, which includes other notable plants like buttercups and anemones.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.
