Delphinium aemulans

Delphinium aemulans, commonly known as the mimic delphinium, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers in dense spikes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Delphinium aemulans is valued in gardens for its vibrant color and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
