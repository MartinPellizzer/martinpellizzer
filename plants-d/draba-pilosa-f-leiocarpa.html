Draba pilosa f. leiocarpa

Draba pilosa f. leiocarpa, commonly known as the smooth-leaved whitlow grass, is a low-growing perennial plant native to alpine and arctic regions.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are typically hairy but are smoother in this particular form compared to other varieties.

It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold climates.
