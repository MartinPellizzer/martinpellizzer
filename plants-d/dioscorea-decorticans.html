Dioscorea decorticans

Dioscorea decorticans, commonly known as wild yam, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and tuberous roots. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its tubers are rich in compounds such as diosgenin, which has been studied for potential medicinal uses.

The botanical features of Dioscorea decorticans make it an important species in both ecological and pharmacological contexts.
