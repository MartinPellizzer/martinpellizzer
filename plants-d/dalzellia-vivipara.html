Dalzellia vivipara

The plant is native to specific regions in South America, particularly in the Andes, where it thrives in humid, cool environments. Its botanical features include striking violet to purple flowers and strap-like leaves, making it visually distinctive.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, Dalzellia vivipara is considered a valuable subject for botanical study and conservation efforts.
