Dendrocalamus pulverulentus

Dendrocalamus pulverulentus, commonly known as the black bamboo, is a tall, fast-growing grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black, culms that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. This bamboo is highly valued for its strong, durable stalks, which are used in construction and handicrafts. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

The plant is also notable for its ability to form dense stands, contributing to soil stabilization and biodiversity in its native habitats.
