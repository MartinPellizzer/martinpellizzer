Daphne esquirolii

Daphne esquirolii, commonly known as the Esquiroli daphne, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming in late winter to early spring. The plant has dark green, glossy leaves that turn yellow in the fall, adding to its ornamental value. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Due to its ornamental appeal and unique flowering period, it is a popular choice among gardeners seeking early-season blooms.
