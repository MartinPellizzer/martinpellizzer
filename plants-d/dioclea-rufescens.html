Dioclea rufescens

Dioclea rufescens, commonly known as the red-stemmed passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It belongs to the family Passifloraceae and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a rough texture.

Dioclea rufescens thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in humid, shaded environments.
