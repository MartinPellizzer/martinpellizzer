Diasperus perpusillus

Diasperus perpusillus, commonly known as the dwarf rock daisy, is a small, flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Diasperus perpusillus is often found in coastal and mountainous regions, where it thrives in full sun.
