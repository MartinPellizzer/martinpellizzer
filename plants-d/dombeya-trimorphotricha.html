Dombeya trimorphotricha

Dombeya trimorphotricha, commonly known as the African dombeya, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with dense, hairy foliage. Its flowers bloom in clusters, often in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.

Dombeya trimorphotricha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
