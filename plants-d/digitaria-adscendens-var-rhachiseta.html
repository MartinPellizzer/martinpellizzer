Digitaria adscendens var. rhachiseta

Digitaria adscendens var. rhachiseta, commonly known as the climbing digitaria, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems that often climb or trail along the ground. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brownish flowers. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a prominent midrib.

This variant is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
