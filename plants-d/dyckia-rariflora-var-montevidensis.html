Dyckia rariflora var. montevidensis

Dyckia rariflora var. montevidensis, commonly known as the Montevidense Dyckia, is a bromeliad native to South America, particularly Uruguay and southern Brazil.

It is characterized by its compact rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of white, powdery bloom. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences bearing small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
