Delphinium sylvaticum subsp. sylvaticum

Delphinium sylvaticum subsp.

sylvaticum, commonly known as the woodland larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and along stream banks, preferring well-drained, loamy soils.

The plant features tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height. Its flowers are blue to purple in color, arranged in dense spikes, and are characterized by their spurred petals.

The subspecies is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
