Didymodoxa capensis var. integrifolia

Didymodoxa capensis var. integrifolia, commonly known as the African bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet. The plant typically grows in a compact shrub form, with lance-shaped leaves that are entire, giving it its varietal name *integrifolia*. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal areas.

The flowers bloom in spring and summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
