Diospyros longifolia

Diospyros longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into edible, orange-red fruits. Its fruits are typically consumed when fully ripe, offering a sweet and tangy flavor.

Diospyros longifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
