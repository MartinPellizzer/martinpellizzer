Dianthus lusitanus subsp. lusitanus

Dianthus lusitanus subsp.

lusitanus, commonly known as the Iberian campion, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe, particularly Spain and Portugal. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, often in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges.

The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers in clusters, which bloom from spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it has a fibrous root system adapted to its arid or semi-arid environment.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
