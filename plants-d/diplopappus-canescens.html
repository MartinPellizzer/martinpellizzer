Diplopappus canescens

Diplopappus canescens, commonly known as the hairy burrweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, hairy, spiny fruits that are adapted for dispersal by animals. Its leaves are typically hairy and have a serrated edge, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.
