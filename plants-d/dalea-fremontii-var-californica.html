Dalea fremontii var. californica

Dalea fremontii var. californica, commonly known as the California milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. This plant thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. It plays a role in stabilizing soils and supporting local pollinators.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal in native plant gardens.
