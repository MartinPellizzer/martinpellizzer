Danthonia colorata

Danthonia colorata, commonly known as the pink muirgrass, is a perennial grass species native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and produces small, pinkish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and forest margins. Its leaves are narrow and have a distinctive blue-green hue, adding visual interest to its natural environment.

Danthonia colorata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
