Disporopsis aspersa

Disporopsis aspersa, commonly known as the rough disporopsis, is a member of the Rhamnaceae family.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its small, simple leaves that are often covered in a rough, glandular texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, typically blooming in the spring. Its fruits are small, dry drupes that contain a single seed.

The species is native to parts of South America and is often found in moist, shaded environments.
