Dombeya frappieri

Dombeya frappieri, commonly known as the white dombeya, is a deciduous shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy, cup-shaped flowers that range from white to pale pink, often with a distinctive red or purple calyx. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, with lance-shaped leaves that are hairy on the underside. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, requiring minimal maintenance once established.
