Diplazium subsinuatum f. bifidum

Diplazium subsinuatum f. bifidum, commonly known as the bifid cliff fern, is a fern species characterized by its distinctive fronds.

The plant features bipinnate leaves with a unique bifid (split) shape at the tips, giving it an ornamental appearance. It is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The fronds are typically dark green and have a leathery texture, which helps it retain moisture.

This fern is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in tropical gardens and greenhouses.
