Diplusodon leucocalycinus

Diplusodon leucocalycinus, commonly known as the white-veined orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, pale green leaves and intricate, white and green veined flowers that bloom during the cooler months. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments, often found in cloud forests or mountainous regions with high humidity. Its unique floral structure is adapted for specific pollinators, contributing to its ecological niche.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered a vulnerable species in conservation efforts.
