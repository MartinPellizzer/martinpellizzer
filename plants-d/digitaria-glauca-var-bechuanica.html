Digitaria glauca var. bechuanica

Digitaria glauca var. bechuanica, commonly known as the Bechuanian grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. The plant has narrow, flat leaves with a bluish-green hue, and its inflorescence consists of loose, spike-like clusters of small, two-lobed flowers. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils.

This variety is often used for forage and soil stabilization in arid regions.
