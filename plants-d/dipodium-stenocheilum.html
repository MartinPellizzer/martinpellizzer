Dipodium stenocheilum

Dipodium stenocheilum, commonly known as the narrow-mouthed dipodium, is a rare and delicate orchid native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, intricate flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant has a compact growth habit with slender, upright stems and narrow leaves. Its flowers are usually pale green or yellowish in color, with a distinctive, narrow mouth-like structure.

Due to its limited habitat and specific environmental requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
