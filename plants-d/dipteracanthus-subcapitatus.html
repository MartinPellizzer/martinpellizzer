Dipteracanthus subcapitatus

Dipteracanthus subcapitatus, commonly known as the dwarf flame tree, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that typically grows up to 6 meters in height. The plant produces striking orange-red flowers that are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences, which are often mistaken for single blooms. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly waxy texture, while the bark is smooth and pale.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
