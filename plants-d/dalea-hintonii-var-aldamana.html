Dalea hintonii var. aldamana

Dalea hintonii var. aldamana, commonly known as Hinton's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in desert or semi-arid environments.

This variety is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing nectar for pollinators.
