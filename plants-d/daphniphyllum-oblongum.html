Daphniphyllum oblongum

Daphniphyllum oblongum, commonly known as the Chinese holly, is a evergreen shrub native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale pink, followed by bright red berries in the fall. It prefers cool, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Daphniphyllum oblongum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and seasonal color changes.
