Dianthus armeria f. caespitosa

Dianthus armeria f. caespitosa, commonly known as the tufted carnation, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features dense, rosette-like growth with small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant produces numerous stamens and a single pistil, typical of the Caryophyllaceae family. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
