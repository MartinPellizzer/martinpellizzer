Diospyros viridicans

Diospyros viridicans, commonly known as the green persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and alternate, simple leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, orange to reddish fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The fruit is valued for its sweet, astringent flavor and is used in traditional medicine and culinary practices.
