Diplazium sulcinervium

Diplazium sulcinervium, commonly known as the slender-veined water fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely veined fronds that are typically less than 30 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and streams, where it can anchor itself to submerged surfaces. Its rhizomes are creeping and help in the spread of the plant across aquatic habitats.

The leaves are usually divided into small, lance-shaped segments, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
