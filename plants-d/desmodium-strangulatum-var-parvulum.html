Desmodium strangulatum var. parvulum

Desmodium strangulatum var. parvulum, commonly known as the small-flowered desmodium, is a perennial legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features trifoliate leaves with small, rounded leaflets and produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant is characterized by its erect growth habit and is often found in open woodlands and disturbed soils. It has a shallow root system and is adapted to thrive in well-drained, sandy soils.

As a member of the Fabaceae family, it has the ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
