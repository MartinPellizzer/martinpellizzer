Dimeria pubescens

Dimeria pubescens, commonly known as the hairy dimeria, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the family Cactaceae, which includes cacti and related succulent plants. The plant is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it stores water in its thick, fleshy stems.

Dimeria pubescens is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky soils and desert landscapes.
