Dalbergia reticulata

Dalbergia reticulata, commonly known as the African rosewood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The tree produces a distinctive, dark reddish-brown heartwood that is highly valued for its density and fine grain. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Due to overharvesting, it is also considered a vulnerable species in some regions.
