Daphne kabylica

Daphne kabylica, commonly known as the Kabylica daphne, is a species native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. Its flowers are fragrant, appearing in clusters of white or pinkish-yellow, and bloom in late winter to early spring.

The plant is noted for its toxicity, with all parts containing irritants that can cause skin and respiratory reactions.
