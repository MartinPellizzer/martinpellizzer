Disa zimbabweensis

Disa zimbabweensis, commonly known as the Zimbabwe orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red, often with a contrasting throat. The plant typically grows in rocky or grassland habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces its showy blooms during the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
