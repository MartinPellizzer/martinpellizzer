Draba hispanica subsp. hispanica

Draba hispanica subsp.

hispanica, commonly known as Spanish rockcress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family. It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and small, white or yellowish flowers.

Native to southern Europe, it thrives in rocky and arid environments, often found in alpine and coastal regions. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Its flowering period typically occurs in spring, and it is adapted to survive in nutrient-poor soils.
