Digitaria undulata

Digitaria undulata, commonly known as the wavy fingergrass, is a species of grass native to the Americas.

It is a perennial plant that typically grows in warm, dry environments such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, and it is often found in open habitats.

Digitaria undulata is known for its adaptability and can thrive in a variety of soil types.
