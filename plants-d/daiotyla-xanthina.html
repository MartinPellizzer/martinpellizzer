Daiotyla xanthina

Daiotyla xanthina, commonly known as the yellow-flowered orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers with bright yellow petals and a distinctive greenish throat. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
