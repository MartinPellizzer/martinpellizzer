Daucus carota subsp. boissieri

Daucus carota subsp.

boissieri, commonly known as the Persian carrot, is a wild relative of the common carrot. It is a biennial herbaceous plant native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces small, yellow flowers in umbels. Its root is typically smaller and less developed compared to the domesticated carrot.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
