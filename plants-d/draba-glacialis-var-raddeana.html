Draba glacialis var. raddeana

Draba glacialis var. raddeana, commonly known as the alpine mouse-ear cress, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to high-altitude regions of the Arctic and alpine zones.

It typically features small, clustered leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant is adapted to cold climates and thrives in well-drained, rocky or sandy soils. It is often found in mountainous areas of Europe, Asia, and North America.

Its botanical characteristics include a mat-forming growth habit and a short life cycle suited to harsh environmental conditions.
