Diphyes obtusa

Diphyes obtusa, commonly known as the broadleaf lily, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of North America.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, with a distinctive, broad, lance-shaped leaf. The plant grows up to 3 feet tall and thrives in moist, well-drained soil in partial to full shade. Its botanical name reflects its two distinct leaf shapes, with the lower leaves being broader than the upper ones.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and wetland habitats.
