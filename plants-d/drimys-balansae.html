Drimys balansae

Drimys balansae, commonly known as the Andean drimys, is a South American evergreen shrub or small tree belonging to the family Lauraceae.

It is native to the temperate rainforests of southern Chile and Argentina, where it thrives in moist, cool climates. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a glossy surface and a distinctive aromatic scent. Its small, white flowers are followed by dark blue, waxy berries that are edible but often consumed by birds.

Drimys balansae is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in local ecosystems.
