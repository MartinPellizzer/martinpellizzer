Doria glauca

Doria glauca, commonly known as the blue doria, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky habitats, preferring cool and moist conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in a fine, silvery bloom.

Doria glauca is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable climates.
