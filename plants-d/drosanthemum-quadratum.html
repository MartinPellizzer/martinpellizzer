Drosanthemum quadratum

Drosanthemum quadratum, commonly known as the four-angled ice plant, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems that are typically four-sided, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, which bloom in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Drosanthemum quadratum is also known for its ability to survive in arid conditions due to its drought-resistant properties.
