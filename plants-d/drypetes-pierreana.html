Drypetes pierreana

Drypetes pierreana, commonly known as the African cherry or red mangosteen, is a tropical tree native to Africa and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by fleshy, red or orange fruits that contain several seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional medicinal uses in some regions.

The tree is also valued for its timber and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
