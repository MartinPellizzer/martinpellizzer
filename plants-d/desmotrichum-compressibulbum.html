Desmotrichum compressibulbum

Desmotrichum compressibulbum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf desert lily, is a perennial herb native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of white, yellow, or orange. The plant thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its flowers typically bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and resilience, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens in suitable climates.
