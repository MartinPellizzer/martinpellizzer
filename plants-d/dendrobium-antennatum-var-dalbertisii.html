Dendrobium antennatum var. dalbertisii

Dendrobium antennatum var. dalbertisii, commonly known as the Dalbert's orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, slender pseudobulbs and clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often on trees or rocks in tropical rainforests. Its leaves are strap-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated for its beauty and fragrance.
