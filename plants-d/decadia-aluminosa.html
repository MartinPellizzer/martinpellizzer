Decadia aluminosa

Decadia aluminosa, commonly known as the aluminum fern, is a unique plant species characterized by its delicate, fern-like foliage.

It thrives in nutrient-poor, acidic soils and is often found in shaded, moist environments. The plant's leaves are composed of finely divided fronds that give it a lacy appearance. It is particularly adapted to absorb trace amounts of aluminum from the soil, which is unusual for most plant species.

This adaptation makes it an interesting subject for botanical studies on plant-metal interactions.
