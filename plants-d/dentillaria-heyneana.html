Dentillaria heyneana

Dentillaria heyneana, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its straight, dark red to brown heartwood, which is highly valued for its durability and rich color. The plant has a dense canopy with lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green in color. Its flowers are small and fragrant, typically appearing in clusters, and it produces small, round fruits that contain seeds.

The tree is often found in lowland forests and is known for its slow growth and long lifespan.
