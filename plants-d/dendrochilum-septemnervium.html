Dendrochilum septemnervium

Dendrochilum septemnervium, commonly known as the seven-veined tree orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its epiphytic growth habit, often found growing on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant features long, arching stems with clustered, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, with a distinctive three-lobed lip that adds to its ornamental appeal.

Dendrochilum septemnervium is valued in horticulture for its attractive appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
