Dissanthelium mathewsii

Dissanthelium mathewsii, commonly known as Mathews' grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and marshes. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spikelet-based flowers in late spring to early summer. It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit, which allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

Due to its ecological importance and limited distribution, it is considered a species of conservation concern in some regions.
