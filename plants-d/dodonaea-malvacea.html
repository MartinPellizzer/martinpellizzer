Dodonaea malvacea

Dodonaea malvacea, commonly known as the silky oaks, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub characterized by its finely hairy, silvery-green leaves. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in spikes, typically blooming in spring. Its fruits are small, woody pods that contain seeds covered in a sticky, resinous substance.

Dodonaea malvacea is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
