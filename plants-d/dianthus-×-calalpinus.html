Dianthus × calalpinus

Dianthus × calalpinus, commonly known as the hybrid carnation, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is a hybrid species resulting from crosses between Dianthus chinensis and Dianthus barbatus, combining traits from both parent species. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers in a range of colors, including pink, red, and white, with a distinctive frilled appearance. It is typically grown as an annual or biennial in temperate climates, thriving in well-drained soil and full sun.

Dianthus × calalpinus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly used in gardens and floral arrangements.
