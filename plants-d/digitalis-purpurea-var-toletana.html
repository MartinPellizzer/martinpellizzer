Digitalis purpurea var. toletana

Digitalis purpurea var. toletana, commonly known as the Toletan foxglove, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It features tall, erect stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of purple and pink. The leaves are velvety, heart-shaped, and have a distinctive serrated edge. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and its historical use in traditional medicine.
