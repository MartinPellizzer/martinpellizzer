Dactylis glomerata var. kroulikii

Dactylis glomerata var. kroulikii, commonly known as Kroulik's goatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and dense, spike-like inflorescences that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, showing adaptability to various environmental conditions. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, while the stems are robust and often form a dense sod.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience and ability to stabilize soil.
