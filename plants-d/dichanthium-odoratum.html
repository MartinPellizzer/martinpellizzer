Dichanthium odoratum

Dichanthium odoratum, commonly known as the sweet grass, is a perennial grass species native to the temperate regions of Asia and Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that produce fragrant, silky seeds. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly hairy texture.

Dichanthium odoratum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional crafts and as a source of aromatic materials.
