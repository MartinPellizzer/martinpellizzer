Delphinium tangkulaense f. xanthanthum

Delphinium tangkulaense f. xanthanthum, commonly known as the yellow-flowered delphinium, is a botanical variant of the delphinium genus native to high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow to orange flowers, which contrast with its deep green foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soil and full sunlight. Its flowers are spurred, a typical feature of the delphinium family, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

This cultivar is relatively rare and requires specific growing conditions to flourish.
