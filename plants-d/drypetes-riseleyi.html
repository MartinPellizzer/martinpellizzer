Drypetes riseleyi

Drypetes riseleyi, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a tropical tree native to parts of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, showy red flowers that bloom in clusters, often appearing before the leaves. The plant has a rough, fissured bark that becomes more pronounced with age. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and typically have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Drypetes riseleyi is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.
