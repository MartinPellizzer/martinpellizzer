Dendrobium tinukariensis

Dendrobium tinukariensis, commonly known as the red-bellied orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a rhizome that produces multiple shoots and pseudobulbs. The plant produces striking red or orange flowers with yellow markings, which bloom during the rainy season. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in a fine, white pubescence.

Dendrobium tinukariensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
