Disa stenoglossa

Disa stenoglossa, commonly known as the narrow-lip disa, is a species of orchid native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, spurred flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or grassland habitats and prefers well-drained soil. It has linear, grass-like leaves and produces a single spike of flowers during the summer months.

Disa stenoglossa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable growing conditions.
