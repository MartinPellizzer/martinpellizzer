Diasperus madagascariensis

Diasperus madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascan velvet tamarind, is a unique plant native to Madagascar.

It is a shrub or small tree characterized by its smooth, dark bark and dense, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by edible, sour fruits that are used in local cuisine. Its botanical features make it an important species in the region's flora and ecosystem.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses.
