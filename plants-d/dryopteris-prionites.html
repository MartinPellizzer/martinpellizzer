Dryopteris prionites

Dryopteris prionites, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wood fern, is a perennial fern native to North America.

It features a compact, upright growth habit with fronds that are deeply divided into narrow, linear segments. The plant produces sporangia on the undersides of its fronds, which are clustered in sori covered by a protective indusium. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky habitats.

The leaves are typically dark green and have a leathery texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal in gardens.
