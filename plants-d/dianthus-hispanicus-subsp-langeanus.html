Dianthus hispanicus subsp. langeanus

Dianthus hispanicus subsp.

langeanus, commonly known as the Spanish carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bilaterally symmetrical flowers that range in color from pink to white.

The plant typically grows in rocky or open habitats and is known for its compact, bushy growth habit. Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves, and it produces numerous flowers in late spring to early summer.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
