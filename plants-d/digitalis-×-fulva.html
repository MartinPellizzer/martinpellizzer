Digitalis × fulva

Digitalis × fulva, commonly known as the Downy Foxglove, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the British Isles.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant produces showy, spurred flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and meadows.

The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs, contributing to its common name.
