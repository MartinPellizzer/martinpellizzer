Dioclea haughtii

Dioclea haughtii, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant produces distinctive yellow flowers followed by inflated, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds. Its leaves are alternate and pinnate, with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Dioclea haughtii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some indigenous communities.
