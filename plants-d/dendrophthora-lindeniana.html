Dendrophthora lindeniana

Dendrophthora lindeniana, commonly known as the Lindenberg's mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of specific host trees.

The plant lacks true leaves and instead absorbs water and nutrients through its haustoria, which penetrate the host's vascular system. Its unique ecological role as both a parasite and a potential food source for certain bird species makes it an interesting subject in botanical studies.
