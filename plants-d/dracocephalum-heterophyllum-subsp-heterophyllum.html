Dracocephalum heterophyllum subsp. heterophyllum

Dracocephalum heterophyllum subsp.

heterophyllum, commonly known as Persian bugloss, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features slender, upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy.

The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental flowers make it a valuable addition to botanical collections and gardens.
