Dipterocarpus guiso

Dipterocarpus guiso, commonly known as the Malayan ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its heavy, durable wood that is highly valued for construction and furniture. The plant typically grows in lowland rainforests and has a straight trunk with smooth, dark-brown bark. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical, while it produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody capsules containing aromatic seeds.

The species plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, supporting various wildlife species.
