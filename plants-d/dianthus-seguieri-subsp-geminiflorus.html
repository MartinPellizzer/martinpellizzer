Dianthus seguieri subsp. geminiflorus

Dianthus seguieri subsp.

geminiflorus, commonly known as the twin-flowered carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and clusters of bright pink to red flowers.

The plant produces small, oval leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period, which extends from early spring to late summer.
