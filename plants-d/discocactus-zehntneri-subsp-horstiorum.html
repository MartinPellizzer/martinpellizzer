Discocactus zehntneri subsp. horstiorum

Discocactus zehntneri subsp.

horstiorum, commonly known as the Horstiorum Discocactus, is a rare and intriguing cactus native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its unique, flattened, disc-like stem that resembles a plate, giving it its common name.

The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers during the cooler months, typically in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated by enthusiasts who appreciate its distinctive appearance and hardy nature.
