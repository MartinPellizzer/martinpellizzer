Deyeuxia lactea

Deyeuxia lactea, commonly known as the milky sedge, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and spikelet-based inflorescences. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. Deyeuxia lactea thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes and along the edges of water bodies.
