Drosera rotundifolia var. maritima

Drosera rotundifolia var. maritima, commonly known as the coastal sundew, is a variant of the round-leaf sundew native to coastal regions of the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its rosette of spoon-shaped, sticky leaves that secrete a digestive fluid to trap and digest insects. The plant thrives in acidic, nutrient-poor soils, often found in bogs, heaths, and sandy coastal areas. Its flowers are typically small, pink or reddish, and arranged in a dense inflorescence.

This variant is adapted to withstand harsh coastal conditions, including salt spray and strong winds.
