Dioscorea apiculata

Dioscorea apiculata, commonly known as the apiculate yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are used in traditional medicine and as a food source. The plant has compound leaves with lanceolate leaflets and produces small, greenish-white flowers. Its tubers are often harvested for their starchy content, though they can be toxic if not properly prepared.

The species is also valued for its ornamental potential due to its vigorous growth and attractive foliage.
