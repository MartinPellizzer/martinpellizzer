Dolichos mendoncae

Dolichos mendoncae, commonly known as the Mendoza bean, is a leguminous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces elongated, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is valued for its nutritional content, particularly its high protein and fiber levels.

Dolichos mendoncae is often cultivated for both food and forage purposes in its native regions.
