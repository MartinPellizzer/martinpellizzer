Dicoma aethiopica

Dicoma aethiopica, commonly known as the Ethiopian daisy, is a flowering plant native to the highlands of Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces bright yellow flowers with a central disk. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine regions.

Dicoma aethiopica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has cultural significance in its native region.
