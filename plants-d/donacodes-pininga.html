Donacodes pininga

Donacodes pininga, commonly known as the pink flax or pink flaxflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in dry, rocky soils. It is a perennial herb that prefers full sun and well-drained soil conditions.

Donacodes pininga is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
