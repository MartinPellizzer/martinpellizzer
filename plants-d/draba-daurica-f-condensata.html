Draba daurica f. condensata

Draba daurica f. condensata, commonly known as the Daurian rockcress, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and pinnate leaves. The plant typically thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments. Its compact growth habit and dense foliage make it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and alpine displays.

The flowers, which bloom in early spring, are usually white or pale yellow, adding a delicate touch to its rugged habitat.
