Dubautia imbricata

Dubautia imbricata, commonly known as the Kona daisy, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a member of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in coastal areas.

Dubautia imbricata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
