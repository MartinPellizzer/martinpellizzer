Diplomorpha dolichantha var. effusa

Diplomorpha dolichantha var. effusa, commonly known as the slender-stemmed Diplomorpha, is a member of the Apocynaceae family.

It is characterized by its elongated, often branching stems and opposite, lanceolate leaves. The flowers are typically small, clustered in umbels, and have a distinctive tubular corolla with five lobes. It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, often in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

The plant is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage and floral structure.
