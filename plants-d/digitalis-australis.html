Digitalis australis

Digitalis australis, commonly known as the Australian foxglove, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The flowers typically range in color from purple to pink and are arranged in whorls along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

The plant is noted for its ornamental value and has been cultivated for its striking appearance.
