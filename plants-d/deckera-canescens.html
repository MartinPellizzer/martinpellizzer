Deckera canescens

Deckera canescens, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall, with a broad, spreading crown. The leaves are alternate, simple, and have a silvery-gray underside, giving the plant its common name. It produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, followed by woody, cone-like fruits.

The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
