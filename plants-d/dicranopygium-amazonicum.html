Dicranopygium amazonicum

Dicranopygium amazonicum, commonly known as the Amazonian moss, is a species of moss belonging to the family Dicranaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, cushion-like growth form and is typically found in moist, shaded environments within the Amazon rainforest. The plant has a complex morphology with distinct leaf structures and a robust rhizoid system that aids in anchoring and nutrient absorption. It plays an important ecological role in soil stabilization and moisture retention in its native habitat.

Dicranopygium amazonicum is often used in ecological studies to assess the health and biodiversity of tropical forest ecosystems.
