Desmodium uncinatum var. gracile

Desmodium uncinatum var. gracile, commonly known as slender burr legume, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, hooked seeds that are adapted for dispersal by attaching to animal fur or clothing. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Due to its aggressive growth, it can become a weed in agricultural settings.
