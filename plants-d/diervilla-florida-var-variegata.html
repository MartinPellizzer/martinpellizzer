Diervilla florida var. variegata

Diervilla florida var. variegata, commonly known as the variegated flame azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a glossy texture and often exhibit a variegated pattern of green and white. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

This plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
