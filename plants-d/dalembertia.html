Dalembertia

Dalembertia, commonly known as the "false dandelion," is a genus of flowering plants in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

It typically features basal leaves that are lance-shaped or ovate, often with a hairy texture. The flowers are small and yellow, arranged in a composite head, and are often surrounded by bracts. These plants are native to South America and are adapted to various habitats, including grasslands and disturbed soils.

They are generally short-lived perennials or annuals, depending on the species.
