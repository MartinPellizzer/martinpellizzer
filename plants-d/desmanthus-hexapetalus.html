Desmanthus hexapetalus

Desmanthus hexapetalus, commonly known as the crown vetch, is a leguminous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and showy, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment. It thrives in full sun and is often used in erosion control due to its extensive root system.

Desmanthus hexapetalus is also valued in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and floral display.
