Dracophyllum scoparium var. major

Dracophyllum scoparium var. major, commonly known as the large-leaved dracophyllum, is a shrub native to the temperate regions of southern Australia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage. The plant features dense, leathery leaves that are typically elliptical in shape and have a glossy surface. It produces small, white or pale pink flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months.

This species is well adapted to nutrient-poor soils and can thrive in harsh, coastal environments.
