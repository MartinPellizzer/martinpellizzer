Dracocephalum integrifolium var. album

Dracocephalum integrifolium var. album, commonly known as white chamaecyparis, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers that are clustered in whorls along the stem. Its leaves are typically ovate to elliptic in shape and have a slightly hairy texture.

This variant is distinguished by its pale floral coloration, which sets it apart from other varieties of Dracocephalum.
