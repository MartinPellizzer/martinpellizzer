Delphinium hillcoatiae

Delphinium hillcoatiae, commonly known as the Hillcoat's larkspur, is a striking perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers in racemes, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough, hairy texture, typical of the Delphinium genus.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
