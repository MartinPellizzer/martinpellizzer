Dianthus takhtajanii

Dianthus takhtajanii, commonly known as the Takhtajan's campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink or red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments. Its leaves are narrow and linear, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Dianthus takhtajanii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
