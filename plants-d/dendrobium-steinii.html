Dendrobium steinii

Dendrobium steinii, commonly known as the Stein's orchid, is a epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dendrobium genus, which is one of the largest groups of orchids, comprising over 1,200 species. This plant typically grows on trees or rocks, utilizing its aerial roots to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air. Its flowers are often showy, with vibrant colors ranging from white to pink and yellow.

Dendrobium steinii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for horticultural purposes.
