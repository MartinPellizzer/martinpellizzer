Diospyros susarticulata

Diospyros susarticulata, commonly known as the African persimmon, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes ebony and persimmon species. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are followed by edible, orange-red berries that are an important food source for various wildlife.

The wood of Diospyros susarticulata is hard and durable, making it valuable for carving and traditional crafts.
