Desmodium buergeri

Desmodium buergeri, commonly known as Buerger's burrbean, is a leguminous plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant produces hairy, spiny pods that contain several seeds, which are often dispersed by wind or animals. Desmodium buergeri is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it a valuable plant in soil enrichment.

It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.
