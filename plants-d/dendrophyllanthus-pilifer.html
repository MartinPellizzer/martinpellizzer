Dendrophyllanthus pilifer

Dendrophyllanthus pilifer, commonly known as the hairy leaf tree, is a tropical shrub belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in cyathia, a feature common in the Euphorbiaceae family. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that contain several seeds.

Dendrophyllanthus pilifer is native to regions of Southeast Asia and is often found in humid, lowland forests.
