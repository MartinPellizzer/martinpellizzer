Dryopteris decussata f. velutina

Dryopteris decussata f. velutina, commonly known as the velvety shield fern, is a fern species characterized by its distinctive, leathery fronds covered in a fine, velvety texture.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is native to regions of Asia, including Japan and China. The fronds are typically bipinnate, with a compact, shield-like shape that gives the plant its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The plant's unique appearance and adaptability make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
