Dichrotrichum schultzei

Dichrotrichum schultzei, commonly known as the silver hair grass, is a member of the Poaceae family, characterized by its delicate, grass-like appearance.

It is native to the wetlands and marshy areas of South America, particularly in countries like Brazil and Argentina. The plant features narrow, bluish-green leaves with a silvery sheen, giving it its common name. Its flowering spikes are small and inconspicuous, typically appearing in late spring to early summer.

Dichrotrichum schultzei is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in moist environments.
