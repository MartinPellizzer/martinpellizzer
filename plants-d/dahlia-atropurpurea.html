Dahlia atropurpurea

Dahlia atropurpurea, commonly known as the purple dahlia, is a tuberous-rooted perennial plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, single or double flowers that range in color from deep purple to red. The plant features upright, sturdy stems with lance-shaped, dark green leaves. Its blooms typically appear in late summer to early autumn, adding vibrant color to gardens.

Dahlia atropurpurea is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for cut flowers and garden displays.
