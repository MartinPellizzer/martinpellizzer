Duranta coriacea

Duranta coriacea, commonly known as the coral tree, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its bright orange or yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting birds and insects. The plant has rough, bark-like leaves that are thick and leathery, providing a unique texture. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for warm climates.

Duranta coriacea is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to withstand drought conditions.
