Dioscorea raymundii

Dioscorea raymundii, commonly known as Raymund's yam, is a climbing herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary storage organs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and have a rough texture, while its stems are hairy and twining.

The tubers of Dioscorea raymundii are used in traditional medicine and have potential nutritional value.
