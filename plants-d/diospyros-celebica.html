Diospyros celebica

Diospyros celebica, commonly known as the Moluccan persimmon, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Maluku Islands of Indonesia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and glossy, elliptical leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into edible, orange-red berries. Its fruits are highly valued for their sweet, astringent flavor and are often consumed fresh or used in traditional remedies.

The plant is also noted for its hard, heavy wood, which is used in crafts and as a source of dye.
