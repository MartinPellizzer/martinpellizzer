Dyckia choristaminea

Dyckia choristaminea, commonly known as the narrow-leaved dyckia, is a species of grass-like plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming structure with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces striking inflorescences that are often brightly colored, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Dyckia choristaminea is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
