Diasperus baeobotryodes

Diasperus baeobotryodes, commonly known as the ancient fern, is a fossilized plant that provides insight into early plant evolution.

It is characterized by its intricate, layered structure resembling a sponge, which suggests it was a complex bryophyte. The plant's morphology indicates it thrived in moist, shaded environments during the Paleozoic era. Its spore sacs are well-preserved in sedimentary rock, offering clues about its reproductive biology.

This fossil is significant for understanding the transition from aquatic to terrestrial plant life.
