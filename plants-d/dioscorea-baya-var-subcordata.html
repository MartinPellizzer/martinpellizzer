Dioscorea baya var. subcordata

Dioscorea baya var. subcordata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf yam, is a climbing perennial vine native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and yellowish-orange flowers. The plant produces tuberous roots that are used in traditional medicine and as a food source in some regions. Its growth habit involves twining stems that ascend support structures, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and medicinal properties.
