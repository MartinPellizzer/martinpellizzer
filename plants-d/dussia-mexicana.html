Dussia mexicana

Dussia mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican dussia, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Dussia mexicana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
