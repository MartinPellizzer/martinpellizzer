Dischisma ciliatum subsp. erinoides

Dischisma ciliatum subsp.

erinoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf dischisma, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and ciliate, with fine hairs along the edges, contributing to its scientific name.

It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.
