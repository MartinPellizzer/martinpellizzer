Dendrobium seranicum

Dendrobium seranicum, commonly known as the orchid of the sea, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous stems that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers during the summer months. Its leaves are linear and often arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems.

Dendrobium seranicum thrives in humid, warm environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.
