Dyckia espiritosantensis

Dyckia espiritosantensis, commonly known as the Espirito Santo dyckia, is a species of flowering plant in the bromeliad family.

It is native to Brazil, specifically the state of Espirito Santo, where it grows in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of trichomes, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It produces inflorescences that bear small, white flowers, which are typically short-lived but attract various pollinators.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
