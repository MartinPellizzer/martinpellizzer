Dactylorhiza × conigera

Dactylorhiza × conigera, commonly known as the hybrid marsh orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Dactylorhiza lapponica and Dactylorhiza maculata. This plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils, often in wetlands or along the edges of ponds. It produces tall, flowering stems with clusters of pink or purple flowers.

The plant has a complex root system and is known for its ability to thrive in wet, boggy environments.
