Dendropemon haitiensis

Dendropemon haitiensis, commonly known as Haitian bamboo, is a unique plant native to Haiti.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its tall, slender stems that resemble bamboo. The plant has narrow, elongated leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers. It thrives in tropical climates and is adapted to grow in well-drained soils.

Due to its restricted habitat, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
