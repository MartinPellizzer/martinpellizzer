Daphniphyllum humile

Daphniphyllum humile, commonly known as the silver bell tree, is a evergreen shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, bell-shaped white flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are edible for some bird species but toxic to humans. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial shade.

Daphniphyllum humile is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and unique floral display.
