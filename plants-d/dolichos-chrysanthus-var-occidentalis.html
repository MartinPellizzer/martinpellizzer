Dolichos chrysanthus var. occidentalis

Dolichos chrysanthus var. occidentalis, commonly known as the golden wisteria, is a climbing legume native to the western United States.

It features cascading clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces elongated, bean-like pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for gardens and as a decorative plant.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental horticulture.
