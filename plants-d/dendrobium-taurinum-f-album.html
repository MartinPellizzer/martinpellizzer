Dendrobium taurinum f. album

Dendrobium taurinum f. album, commonly known as the white-flowered dendrobium, is a rare and striking orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

This epiphytic plant is characterized by its long, cane-like stems that store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are notable for their pale white to cream coloration, often with a faintly speckled appearance, and bloom during the cooler months. The plant's foliage consists of long, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem.

Due to its rarity and aesthetic appeal, it is highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts and conservationists alike.
