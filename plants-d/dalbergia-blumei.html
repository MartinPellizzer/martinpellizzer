Dalbergia blumei

Dalbergia blumei, commonly known as the yellow rosewood, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its dense, dark wood that is highly valued for its durability and aesthetic appeal. The plant has compound leaves with trifoliate or pinnate structures, and its flowers are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental and economic value, it is also cultivated in horticultural settings.
