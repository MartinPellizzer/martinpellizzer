Deyeuxia chrysantha

Deyeuxia chrysantha, commonly known as the golden beardgrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with a golden hue. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, pale yellow to golden flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Deyeuxia chrysantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate, colorful foliage.
