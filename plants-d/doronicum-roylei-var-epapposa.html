Doronicum roylei var. epapposa

Doronicum roylei var. epapposa, commonly known as the Kashmir golden bell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Himalayas.

It features striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and a compact, upright growth habit. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for alpine gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant blooms and compact size.
