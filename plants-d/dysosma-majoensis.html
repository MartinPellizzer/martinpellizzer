Dysosma majoensis

Dysosma majoensis, commonly known as the golden flame plant, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its striking, waxy, golden-yellow flowers. The plant produces small, green, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some regions for its unique floral display.
