Dianthus aristidis

Dianthus aristidis, commonly known as the mountain carnation, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink or red flowers with prominent white or pink central markings. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in late summer.

Dianthus aristidis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and hardy nature.
