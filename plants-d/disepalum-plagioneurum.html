Disepalum plagioneurum

Disepalum plagioneurum, commonly known as the pink-flowered rock orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its distinctive pink or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or crevice habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are narrow and linear, and it produces small, clustered inflorescences.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
