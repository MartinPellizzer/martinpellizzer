Diplostephium floribundum var. putumayense

Diplostephium floribundum var. putumayense, commonly known as the Putumayo cactus, is a species native to South America, particularly Ecuador and Colombia.

It is a spiny, perennial shrub that typically grows in dry, arid environments. The plant features a thick, branching stem covered in sharp, protective spines. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange and bloom in clusters during the dry season.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in harsh conditions.
