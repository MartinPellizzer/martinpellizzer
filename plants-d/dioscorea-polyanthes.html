Dioscorea polyanthes

Dioscorea polyanthes, commonly known as the purple yam or wild yam, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, Africa, and the Americas.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are often used in traditional medicine and as a food source. The plant produces small, white to violet flowers that are typically borne in clusters, and it has heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Dioscorea polyanthes is also valued for its ornamental qualities, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
