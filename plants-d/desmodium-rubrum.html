Desmodium rubrum

Desmodium rubrum, commonly known as redstem burr medic, is a leguminous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pinkish to reddish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces hairy, spiny pods that contain small, kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Desmodium rubrum is often used in forage and erosion control due to its hardy nature and nitrogen-fixing ability.
