Dactylorchis incarnata var. brevicalcarata

Dactylorchis incarnata var. brevicalcarata, commonly known as the short-crested lady's-tresses, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green leaves and a tall, spike-like inflorescence that bears numerous small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wet meadows. It is a perennial species that thrives in well-drained, loamy soils.

The flowers are arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem, a feature typical of the Orchidaceae family.
