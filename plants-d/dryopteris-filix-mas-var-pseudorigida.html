Dryopteris filix-mas var. pseudorigida

Dryopteris filix-mas var. pseudorigida, commonly known as the rigid shield fern, is a variant of the male fern that exhibits distinctive botanical features.

It is characterized by its sturdy, upright fronds that are typically dark green and have a leathery texture. The plant produces sporangia on the underside of its fertile fronds, which are clustered in distinct sori. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

This variety is noted for its hardy nature and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
