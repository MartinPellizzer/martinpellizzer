Dithrix griffithii

Dithrix griffithii, commonly known as the Himalayan thistle, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh climatic conditions. Its leaves are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which helps reduce water loss.

Dithrix griffithii produces showy yellow or orange flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
