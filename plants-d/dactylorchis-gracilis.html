Dactylorchis gracilis

Dactylorchis gracilis, commonly known as the slender orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as pine flatwoods and wetlands. The plant features a slender, erect stem with a few broad, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small, white to pale green, and arranged in a raceme, often with a distinctive, narrow lip.

Dactylorchis gracilis is a rare and endangered species, requiring specific environmental conditions for successful growth and reproduction.
