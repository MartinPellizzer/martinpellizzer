Dianthus rupicola subsp. bocchoriana

Dianthus rupicola subsp.

bocchoriana, commonly known as the Sierra dianthus, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, clustered flowers that attract pollinators.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to harsh, dry conditions.
