Duguetia subcordata

Duguetia subcordata, commonly known as the red-flowered tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Amazon Basin.

It is characterized by its dense, lanceolate leaves that are glossy and dark green in color. The plant produces striking clusters of bright red flowers, which are typically borne in axillary inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that contains a single seed.

Duguetia subcordata is valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
