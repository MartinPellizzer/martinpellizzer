Dicliptera foetida var. ghatica

Dicliptera foetida var. ghatica, commonly known as the Indian nettle or ghatic nettle, is a perennial herb native to parts of South and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family, which includes plants known for their stinging hairs. The plant features slender, erect stems that are often covered in fine, irritating hairs. Its leaves are ovate to elliptical, with a serrated margin and a hairy surface.

It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and wetlands.
