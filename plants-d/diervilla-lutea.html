Diervilla lutea

Diervilla lutea, commonly known as yellow hazel, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in late spring, which are clustered in racemes. Its leaves are oval-shaped, with a glossy surface and serrated edges.

Diervilla lutea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in acidic, well-drained soils.
