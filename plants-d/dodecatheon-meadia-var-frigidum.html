Dodecatheon meadia var. frigidum

Dodecatheon meadia var. frigidum, commonly known as the frigid bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the northern United States and southern Canada.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and has lanceolate leaves with a hairy texture. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This variety is distinguished by its smaller stature and more compact growth compared to the typical form of Dodecatheon meadia.
