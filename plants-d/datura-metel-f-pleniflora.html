Datura metel f. pleniflora

Datura metel f. pleniflora, commonly known as the Chinese devil's trumpet, is a variant of the Datura genus native to China.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that are typically white or pale purple, with a distinctive trumpet shape. The plant is a herbaceous perennial that thrives in warm, sunny environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. Its leaves are broad and lobed, and it produces large, spiny fruit capsules that contain toxic seeds.

Due to its psychoactive properties, it is considered a potentially dangerous plant and should be handled with caution.
