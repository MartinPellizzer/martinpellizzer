Diospyros ellipticifolia var. ebenus

Diospyros ellipticifolia var. ebenus, commonly known as the African ebony, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its dark, dense wood, which is highly valued for its durability and fine grain. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a smooth, glossy surface. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by edible, round berries that are typically red when ripe.

The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for the production of high-quality timber.
