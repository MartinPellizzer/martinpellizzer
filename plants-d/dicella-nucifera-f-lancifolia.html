Dicella nucifera f. lancifolia

Dicella nucifera f. lancifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf betel palm, is a cultivar of the betel palm native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that give it a distinctive appearance compared to the typical broad-leafed betel palms. The plant produces edible fruits, which are often used in traditional practices and cuisine. Its growth habit is slow, making it suitable for ornamental purposes in gardens and landscapes.

The plant requires well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.
