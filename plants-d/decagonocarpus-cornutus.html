Decagonocarpus cornutus

Decagonocarpus cornutus, commonly known as the horned nut tree, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiky fruits that resemble small horns, giving it its common name. The plant has a rugged, woody stem with rough, fissured bark. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and typically have a serrated edge.

The tree produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by the unique horn-shaped fruits that are often used in traditional medicine.
