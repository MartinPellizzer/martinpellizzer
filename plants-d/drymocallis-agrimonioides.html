Drymocallis agrimonioides

Drymocallis agrimonioides, commonly known as the silverleaf goldenrod, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its grayish-white, velvety foliage and bright yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine, silvery hairs, which give it its common name.

The showy inflorescences attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, making it an important plant in ecological systems.
