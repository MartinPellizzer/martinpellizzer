Diplotaxis virgata var. platystylos

Diplotaxis virgata var. platystylos, commonly known as the broad-styled goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, yellow flowers and slender, branching stems. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are linear and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.
