Dianthus broteri var. valentinus

Dianthus broteri var. valentinus, commonly known as the Valentine's carnation, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant typically grows to a height of about 10-20 centimeters, with hairy stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, and it prefers well-drained soil and full sun.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.
