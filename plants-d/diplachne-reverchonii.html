Diplachne reverchonii

Diplachne reverchonii, commonly known as the slender grass or narrow-leaved flat sedge, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Africa, including South Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in spikelets. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies. Diplachne reverchonii plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems in its native range.
