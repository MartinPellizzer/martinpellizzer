Didymoplexis minor

Didymoplexis minor, commonly known as the small flame vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Piperaceae and is characterized by its delicate, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that contain seeds enclosed in a gelatinous coating. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the undergrowth of rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
