Dentaria macrophylla var. dasyloba

Dentaria macrophylla var. dasyloba, commonly known as the showy toothwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features striking, showy white flowers that bloom in late spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has heart-shaped, toothed leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and prefers partial to full shade.

Its unique floral structure and ecological role make it an important species in native plant communities.
