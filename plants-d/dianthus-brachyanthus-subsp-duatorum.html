Dianthus brachyanthus subsp. duatorum

Dianthus brachyanthus subsp.

duatorum, commonly known as the two-flowered carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and displays clusters of showy, pink to red flowers with prominent white centers.

The leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its drought-resistant nature. This subspecies is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a versatile choice for Mediterranean and temperate climates.
