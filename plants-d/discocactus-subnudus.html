Discocactus subnudus

Discocactus subnudus, commonly known as the naked disc cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are often covered in sparse, white spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are typically short-lived but striking in appearance. Its leaves are reduced to small, scale-like structures, a feature common in many cacti adapted to desert environments.

Discocactus subnudus is well-suited for cultivation in dry, well-drained soils and requires minimal water to thrive.
