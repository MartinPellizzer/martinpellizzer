Datura tatula

Datura tatula, commonly known as the nightshade or devil's trumpet, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy, funnel-shaped flowers that are typically white or violet in color. The plant produces spiny, capsule-like fruits that contain toxic seeds. Datura tatula is part of the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades such as tomatoes and potatoes.

It is often found in arid or semi-arid environments and is known for its rapid growth and short life cycle.
