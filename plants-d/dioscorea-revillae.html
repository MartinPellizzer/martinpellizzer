Dioscorea revillae

Dioscorea revillae, commonly known as the Revilla yam, is a climbing herbaceous perennial native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary storage organs. The plant produces tendrils for support and has alternate, heart-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant.

The tubers of Dioscorea revillae are sometimes used in traditional medicine and as a food source in certain regions.
