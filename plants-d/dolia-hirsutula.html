Dolia hirsutula

Dolia hirsutula, commonly known as the hairy dolia, is a species of flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth. The plant features hairy stems and leaves with a rough texture, which contribute to its common name. Its flowers are small and clustered, often in shades of white or pale yellow.

Dolia hirsutula is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
