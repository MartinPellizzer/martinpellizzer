Dendrobium closterium

Dendrobium closterium, commonly known as the zigzag orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive zigzag-shaped stems and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest undergrowth. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the soil.

Dendrobium closterium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
