Darwinia micropetala

Darwinia micropetala, commonly known as the small-flowered darwinia, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its dense, cylindrical inflorescences that bear small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in heathlands and open forests, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces distinctive seed capsules that split open to release seeds.

Darwinia micropetala is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
