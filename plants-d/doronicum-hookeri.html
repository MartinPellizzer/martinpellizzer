Doronicum hookeri

Doronicum hookeri, commonly known as the Hooker's leopard's bane, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright, branching stems covered in dense, glandular hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers in late spring to early summer, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. Its botanical characteristics include a fibrous root system and a preference for well-drained, rocky soils.

It is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
