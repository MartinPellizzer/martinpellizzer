Dalbergia pilosa

Dalbergia pilosa, commonly known as the hairy senna, is a deciduous leguminous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with hairy surfaces and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, flat pods containing several seeds, and it is often found in dry, sandy soils. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.

The species is also noted for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
