Delphinium strictum

Delphinium strictum, commonly known as the tall larkspur, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It features tall, erect stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, adorned with spikes of blue, purple, or white flowers. The plant has palmately divided leaves that are deeply lobed, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Delphinium strictum is also noted for its toxicity, containing alkaloids that can be harmful to humans and animals.
