Dombeya pilosa var. brachypoda

Dombeya pilosa var. brachypoda, commonly known as the dwarf dombeya, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It features lance-shaped leaves with a hairy underside, and its flowers are typically white or pale pink, arranged in clusters at the ends of branches. The plant produces small, cylindrical fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in humid, shaded environments.

Dombeya pilosa var. brachypoda is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
