Didiscus humilis

Didiscus humilis, commonly known as the dwarf didiscus, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features small, rounded leaves that are typically green with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces delicate, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its compact size and ornamental appeal, it is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover.
