Diospyros pallens

Diospyros pallens, commonly known as the pale persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and opposite, ovate leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into orange to red fruits with a sweet, astringent flavor. Its fruit is often consumed fresh or used in traditional medicines.

Diospyros pallens is also valued for its timber, which is hard and durable.
