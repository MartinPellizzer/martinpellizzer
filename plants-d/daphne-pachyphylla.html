Daphne pachyphylla

Daphne pachyphylla, commonly known as the thick-leaved daphne, is a shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink during the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is often cultivated in Mediterranean climates.
