Dryopteris yabei var. ogawae

Dryopteris yabei var. ogawae, commonly known as the Ogawa's wood fern, is a fern species native to Japan.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds with a distinctive frilly margin. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are clustered on the underside of the fronds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
