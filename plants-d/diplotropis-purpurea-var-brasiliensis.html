Diplotropis purpurea var. brasiliensis

Diplotropis purpurea var. brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian purpleleaf tree, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree characterized by its striking purple foliage, which becomes more pronounced during the cooler months. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically alternate and have a distinctive heart-shaped base.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant autumn color.
