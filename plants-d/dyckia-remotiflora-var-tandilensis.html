Dyckia remotiflora var. tandilensis

Dyckia remotiflora var. tandilensis, commonly known as the Tandil dyckia, is a species of bromeliad native to Argentina.

It is characterized by its rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences bearing small, white or pale blue flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a container plant.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated for its unique appearance and resilience.
