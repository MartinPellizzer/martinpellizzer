Damnacanthus indicus var. parvispinus

Damnacanthus indicus var. parvispinus, commonly known as the small-spined devil's backbone, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in spikes, which are typically borne in the axils of the leaves. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
