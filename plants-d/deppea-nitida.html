Deppea nitida

Deppea nitida, commonly known as the silverleaf acanthus, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking silver-green foliage and showy, spire-like inflorescences. The plant belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is known for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Deppea nitida is often cultivated for its unique appearance and ability to attract pollinators.
