Dianthus crinitus subsp. baldzhuanicus

Dianthus crinitus subsp.

baldzhuanicus, commonly known as the Caucasian pink, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region. It features upright, branched stems that bear clusters of delicate, pink or white flowers with frilled petals.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its showy blooms and compact growth habit.
