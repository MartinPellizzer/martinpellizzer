Dianthus chimanimaniensis

Dianthus chimanimaniensis, commonly known as the Chimanimani campion, is a flowering plant native to the Chimanimani Mountains in southeastern Africa.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink or red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it is often found in open, high-altitude habitats.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
