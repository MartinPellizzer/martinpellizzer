Diplazium × tsukushiense

Diplazium × tsukushiense, commonly known as the Japanese holly fern, is a hybrid fern species native to Japan.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its upright, compact growth habit. The plant features dark green, leathery fronds with a serrated edge, and it produces small, spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its leaves. Diplazium × tsukushiense thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation in temperate climates.
