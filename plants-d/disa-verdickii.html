Disa verdickii

Disa verdickii, commonly known as the Violet Disa, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in grassland habitats, often in areas with well-drained soil. The plant features showy, violet-colored flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies in suitable conditions.

Disa verdickii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
