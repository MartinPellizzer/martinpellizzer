Digitaria sanguinea

Digitaria sanguinea, commonly known as red grass or blood grass, is a flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive reddish hue, which is most prominent in its inflorescence. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, spikelet-based flowers that are typically wind-pollinated.

It is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along riverbanks, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
