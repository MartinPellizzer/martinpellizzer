Dubautia latifolia

Dubautia latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf dubautia, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy blooms. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Dubautia latifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the unique flora of Hawaii's ecosystems.
