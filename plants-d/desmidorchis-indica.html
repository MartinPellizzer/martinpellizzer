Desmidorchis indica

Desmidorchis indica, commonly known as the Indian desmid orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, featuring a single, showy, spurred orchid flower with a distinct lip. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and grasslands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its roots are specialized for nutrient absorption.

Botanically, it belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is notable for its ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
