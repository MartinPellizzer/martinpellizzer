Didymocarpus caeruleus

Didymocarpus caeruleus, commonly known as the blueberry hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a glossy green surface with serrated edges.

The fruit is a small, fleshy capsule that contains several seeds, contributing to its role in local ecosystems.
