Dicksonia blumei

Dicksonia blumei, commonly known as the Blume's tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of large, feather-like fronds. The plant has a distinctive root system that forms a dense, fibrous mat, helping to stabilize the soil. Its fronds are bi-directionally arranged, with the upper portion being sterile and the lower portion producing spores.

Dicksonia blumei thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
