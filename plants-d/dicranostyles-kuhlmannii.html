Dicranostyles kuhlmannii

Dicranostyles kuhlmannii, commonly known as the Kuhlmann's feather moss, is a species of moss belonging to the family Dicranaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, branched stems and small, elongated leaves that are often covered in a thin layer of rhizoids. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its reproductive structures, known as capsules, are borne on short, erect stalks and release spores when mature.

Dicranostyles kuhlmannii plays a vital role in soil stabilization and moisture retention in its native habitats.
