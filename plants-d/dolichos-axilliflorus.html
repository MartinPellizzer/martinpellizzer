Dolichos axilliflorus

Dolichos axilliflorus, commonly known as the axillary-flowered dolichos, is a climbing legume native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically small, yellow, and clustered in axillary inflorescences. The plant produces elongated, bean-like pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and forest edges.

Dolichos axilliflorus is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability and potential use in agricultural systems.
