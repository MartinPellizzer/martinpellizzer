Deguelia negrensis

Deguelia negrensis, commonly known as the Negrensis tree, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Leguminosae family, characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are compound, with multiple oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces showy, clustered yellow flowers.

The tree is valued for its hard wood and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
