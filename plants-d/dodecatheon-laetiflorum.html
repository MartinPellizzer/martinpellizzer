Dodecatheon laetiflorum

Dodecatheon laetiflorum, commonly known as the cheerful or happy flower, is a member of the Primulaceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, white, and purple. Native to the eastern United States, it thrives in woodland habitats and prefers moist, well-drained soil. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with hairy undersides and produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
