Diasperus macraei

Diasperus macraei, commonly known as the Cape foveolata, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a dense cluster. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Diasperus macraei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
