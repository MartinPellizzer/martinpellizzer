Dendrocalamus merrillianus

Dendrocalamus merrillianus, commonly known as the black bamboo, is a tall, clump-forming grass native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Bambusoideae subfamily and is characterized by its dark, almost black, culms that are often covered with a whitish or yellowish bloom. The plant is known for its rapid growth and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and use in traditional crafts. Its leaves are long and narrow, typically with a pointed tip, and the plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers.

Dendrocalamus merrillianus is adapted to humid, tropical climates and thrives in well-drained soils.
