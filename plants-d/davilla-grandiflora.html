Davilla grandiflora

Davilla grandiflora, commonly known as the Florida beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that appear in the spring. The plant produces striking violet-purple berries that are both ornamental and edible for certain wildlife. Its leaves are typically ovate with a smooth margin and a glossy surface.

Davilla grandiflora thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.
