Draba moupinensis

Draba moupinensis, commonly known as the Moupin Draba, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of China.

It is a small, herbaceous annual or biennial species belonging to the Brassicaceae family. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky areas at high elevations, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its flowers are small and white or pale yellow, arranged in a raceme, and bloom in early spring.

Draba moupinensis is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and is often found in remote, less disturbed habitats.
