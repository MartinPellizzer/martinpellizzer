Dalbergia simpsonii

Dalbergia simpsonii, commonly known as Simpson's ebony, is a species of leguminous tree native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its dark, dense wood, which is highly valued for its strength and aesthetic qualities. The plant has compound leaves with trifoliate or pinnate structures, and it produces small, yellowish flowers clustered in racemes. Its fruit is a legume pod containing several seeds.

Dalbergia simpsonii plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, contributing to biodiversity and soil stabilization.
