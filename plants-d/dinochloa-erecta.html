Dinochloa erecta

Dinochloa erecta, commonly known as the erect dichotomy, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Poaceae, or grass family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and slender, cylindrical stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of grasses. Its leaves are narrow and blade-like, with a smooth surface and a slight waxy coating.
