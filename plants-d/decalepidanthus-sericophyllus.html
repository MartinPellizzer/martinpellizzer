Decalepidanthus sericophyllus

Decalepidanthus sericophyllus, commonly known as the silky-leaved sandmat, is a small, herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, cushion-like growth habit and its distinctive, silky-textured leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, often resembling a spherical shape. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
