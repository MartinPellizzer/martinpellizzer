Dolichopterys

Dolichopterys, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It features large, broad leaves that can reach up to two meters in diameter, floating on the water's surface. The plant has showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink, often at night. Its roots anchor in the muddy substrate, while its stems extend outward to support the heavy foliage.

Dolichopterys plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
