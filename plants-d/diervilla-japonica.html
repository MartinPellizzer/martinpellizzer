Diervilla japonica

Diervilla japonica, commonly known as Japanese firethorn, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan and Korea.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny stems and clusters of bright red berries that persist through winter. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, producing clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers.

The berries are a valuable food source for birds and are also used in traditional herbal medicine.
