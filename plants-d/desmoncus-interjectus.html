Desmoncus interjectus

Desmoncus interjectus, commonly known as the golden cup fern, is a species of fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

The golden cup fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
