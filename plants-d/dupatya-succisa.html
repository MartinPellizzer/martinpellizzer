Dupatya succisa

Dupatya succisa, commonly known as the narrow-leafed spurge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically narrow and pointed, adapted to conserve water in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the spring and summer months. Its stems are often hairy and can branch out to form a low-growing shrub.

Dupatya succisa is known for its ability to thrive in rocky or sandy soils, making it a hardy and resilient species.
