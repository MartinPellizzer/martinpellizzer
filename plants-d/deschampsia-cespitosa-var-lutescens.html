Deschampsia cespitosa var. lutescens

Deschampsia cespitosa var. lutescens, commonly known as the yellow muhly, is a flowering grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and produces panicles of small, yellowish to golden flowers. The plant typically thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as prairies and dunes. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it is drought-tolerant.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.
