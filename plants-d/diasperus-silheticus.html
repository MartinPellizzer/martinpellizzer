Diasperus silheticus

Diasperus silheticus, commonly known as the Silhet rockrose, is a flowering plant native to the eastern Himalayas.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs, adapted to the rocky, high-altitude environments it inhabits.

Diasperus silheticus is often found in dry, sunny slopes and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
