Dolichos obtusifolius

Dolichos obtusifolius, commonly known as the blunt-leaf cowpea, is a flowering plant native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is known for its leguminous plants that often form symbiotic relationships with nitrogen-fixing bacteria. The plant features trifoliate leaves with rounded lobes and produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or white. Its pods contain edible seeds that are used in various cuisines, and the plant is also valued for its ability to thrive in poor soils.

Dolichos obtusifolius is often cultivated as a forage crop and for its ornamental value.
