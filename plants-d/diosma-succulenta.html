Diosma succulenta

Diosma succulenta, commonly known as the succulent diosma, is a species of flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its fleshy, water-storing leaves and stems, which are typical adaptations to arid environments. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are often white or pale pink in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Diosma succulenta is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in harsh conditions.
