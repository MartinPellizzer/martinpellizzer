Disa erubescens

Disa erubescens, commonly known as the red disa or red orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant red flowers, which are often adorned with intricate purple and white markings. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its unique inflorescence consists of several showy, spurred flowers that bloom in late summer.

Disa erubescens is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its beauty and resilience.
