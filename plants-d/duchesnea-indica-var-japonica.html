Duchesnea indica var. japonica

Duchesnea indica var. japonica, commonly known as Japanese false strawberry, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to East Asia.

It features trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, white, five-petaled flowers. The plant bears red, edible berries that resemble strawberries in appearance but are less sweet. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
