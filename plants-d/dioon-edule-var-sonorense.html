Dioon edule var. sonorense

Dioon edule var. sonorense, commonly known as the Sonoran edible cycad, is a slow-growing, evergreen plant native to the Sonoran Desert.

It is characterized by its stout, segmented trunk and a crown of large, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces large, cone-like structures that contain both male and female reproductive organs. It is adapted to arid environments, with a deep root system that allows it to access groundwater.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological significance, it is considered a valuable species in desert ecosystems.
