Diplazium lanceum var. dentatum

Diplazium lanceum var. dentatum, commonly known as the serrated cliff fern, is a variant of the lance fern characterized by its distinctive serrated leaf margins.

It is a member of the family Polypodiaceae and is native to Southeast Asia, including regions such as India and China. The plant typically grows on rocky outcrops or in shaded, moist environments, showcasing its adaptability to challenging habitats. Its fronds are bioluminescent, emitting a faint glow at night, which is a unique trait among ferns.

The plant's morphology includes a creeping rhizome with lance-shaped, compound leaves that are often divided into numerous pinnae.
