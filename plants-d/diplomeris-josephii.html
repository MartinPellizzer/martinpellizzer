Diplomeris josephii

Diplomeris josephii, commonly known as the Joseph's diplomeris, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of white and yellow, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in fine hairs.

Diplomeris josephii is well adapted to rocky and sandy soils, making it a hardy species suitable for coastal gardens.
