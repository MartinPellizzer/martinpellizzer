Dipterocarpus obtusifolius var. glabricalyx

Dipterocarpus obtusifolius var. glabricalyx, commonly known as the glabrous-leafed dipterocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically smooth and glabrous. The plant produces heavy, woody fruits that contain winged seeds, aiding in wind dispersal. It thrives in lowland rainforests and is often found in secondary growth areas.

Due to its ecological importance and timber value, it is a species of conservation concern in many regions.
