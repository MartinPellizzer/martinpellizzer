Dianthus × novakii

Dianthus × novakii, commonly known as the hybrid carnation, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is a cross between Dianthus chinensis and Dianthus barbatus, resulting in a variety that exhibits traits from both parent species. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, with prominent white or pink central markings. It is typically grown as an ornamental plant for its vibrant blooms and is valued in gardens for its hardiness and long-lasting flowers.

Dianthus × novakii thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for borders and containers.
