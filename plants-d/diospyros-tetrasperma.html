Diospyros tetrasperma

Diospyros tetrasperma, commonly known as the African date palm, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its robust, straight trunk and broad, feather-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into edible, sweet, and fleshy fruits. Its fruit is often consumed fresh or used in traditional remedies.

The tree is also valued for its hard, durable wood and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
