Decastemon

Decastemon, commonly known as the silky entwining vine, is a member of the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in summer. Decastemon thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Its unique foliage and floral display make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
