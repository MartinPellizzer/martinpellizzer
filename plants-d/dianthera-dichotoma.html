Dianthera dichotoma

Dianthera dichotoma, commonly known as the two-branched bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant features opposite, linear leaves and produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or purple. Its flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is adapted to drought conditions and can thrive in well-drained soils.
