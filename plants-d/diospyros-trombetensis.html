Diospyros trombetensis

Diospyros trombetensis, commonly known as the Andean persimmon, is a small to medium-sized tree native to the cloud forests of South America.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes ebony and persimmon trees. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to elliptical in shape. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically greenish-white in color, while the fruit is a small, fleshy drupe with a sweet, edible pulp.

The tree is valued for its ornamental value and its potential use in traditional medicine.
