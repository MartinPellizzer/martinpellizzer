Disperis capensis

Disperis capensis, commonly known as the Cape bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in coastal regions. It has slender stems and lance-shaped leaves, and it produces small, fragrant blooms during the summer months.

Disperis capensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable climates.
