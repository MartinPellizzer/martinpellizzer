Delphinium madrense f. insigne

Delphinium madrense f. insigne, commonly known as the Mexican larkspur, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces showy, blue to violet flowers in dense clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of palmately compound leaves with serrated edges, and it thrives in well-drained, alkaline soils.

Delphinium madrense f. insigne is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
