Dryopria tripetaloides

Dryopria tripetaloides, commonly known as the three-lobed burr vetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves and hairy stems. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by spiny, three-lobed pods. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
