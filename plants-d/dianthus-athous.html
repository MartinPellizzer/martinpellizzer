Dianthus athous

Dianthus athous, commonly known as the alpine pink, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Carnation family (Caryophyllaceae) and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, pink or red flowers. The plant typically features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in late summer. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
