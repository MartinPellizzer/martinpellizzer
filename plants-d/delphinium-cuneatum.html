Delphinium cuneatum

Delphinium cuneatum, commonly known as the narrowleaf larkspur, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and clusters of spurred blue or purple flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. Delphinium cuneatum is part of the Ranunculaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with showy blooms.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.
