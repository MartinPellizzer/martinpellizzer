Dianthus erythrocoleus

Dianthus erythrocoleus, commonly known as the red campion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its stems are erect and branched.

Dianthus erythrocoleus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
