Daphne laureola var. hosmariensis

Daphne laureola var. hosmariensis, commonly known as the Hosmarian daphne, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It features dense, glossy, dark green foliage and produces clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers in early spring. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and showy, bell-shaped blooms that attract pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value and unique floral display, it is often cultivated in Mediterranean climates.
