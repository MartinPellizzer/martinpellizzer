Desmodium salicifolium

Desmodium salicifolium, commonly known as the willow-leaf desmodium, is a perennial legume native to the southern United States.

It features trifoliate leaves with a willow-like shape, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and along roadsides.

As a member of the Fabaceae family, it has the ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
