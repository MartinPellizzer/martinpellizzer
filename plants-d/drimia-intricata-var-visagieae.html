Drimia intricata var. visagieae

Drimia intricata var. visagieae, commonly known as the Visagie's dragon's tongue, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features a rosette of thick, fleshy, sword-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base. The plant produces tall, branched stems that bear clusters of white or pale pink flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, requiring minimal water once established.

Its unique appearance and ornamental value make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
