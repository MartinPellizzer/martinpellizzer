Deschampsia hispanica subsp. gallaecica

Deschampsia hispanica subsp.

gallaecica, commonly known as the Galician feather grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, silver-green foliage.

The plant produces narrow, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, fluffy flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
