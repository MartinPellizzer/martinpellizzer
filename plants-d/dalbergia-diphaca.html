Dalbergia diphaca

Dalbergia diphaca, commonly known as the Indian rosewood, is a deciduous tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The tree produces hard, dark-colored wood that is highly valued for its durability and aesthetic qualities. It typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in dry or semi-arid environments.

Due to its slow growth and high demand, it is considered a threatened species in some areas.
