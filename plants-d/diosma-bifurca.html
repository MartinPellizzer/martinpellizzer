Diosma bifurca

Diosma bifurca, commonly known as the split-stem Diosma, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and small, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of pink or white. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slight hairy texture.

Diosma bifurca is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
