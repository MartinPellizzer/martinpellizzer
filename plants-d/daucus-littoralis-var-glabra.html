Daucus littoralis var. glabra

Daucus littoralis var. glabra, commonly known as sea holly, is a coastal plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy, spiny stems and distinctive blue to purple flowers. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils along the shoreline, where it is adapted to salt spray and strong winds. Its leaves are usually lobed and can vary in shape depending on environmental conditions.

The species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological benefits to coastal habitats.
