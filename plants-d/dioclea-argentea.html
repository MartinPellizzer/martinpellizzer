Dioclea argentea

Dioclea argentea, commonly known as silverleaf bignay, is a tropical climbing plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink or white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has compound leaves with elongated leaflets and produces small, edible pods containing seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in rainforests.

Dioclea argentea is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
