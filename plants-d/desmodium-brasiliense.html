Desmodium brasiliense

Desmodium brasiliense, commonly known as Brazilian burr legume, is a climbing legume native to South America.

It features trifoliate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters. The plant is characterized by its unique, spiny seed pods that encase the seeds, aiding in seed dispersal. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in disturbed soils.

Desmodium brasiliense is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in agricultural systems.
