Diuris semilunulata

Diuris semilunulata, commonly known as the moon-veined orchid, is a species native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, orchid-like flowers that feature a distinctive moon-shaped mark on the dorsal sepal. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as rainforests and understoreys. Its leaves are linear and often have a waxy texture, adapted to retain moisture.

The flowering period occurs during the warmer months, attracting a variety of pollinators.
