Diplopappus divaricatus

Diplopappus divaricatus, commonly known as the variable burrweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant produces small, spiny fruits known as achenes, which are often covered in hooked bracts that aid in seed dispersal by attaching to animals. It typically grows in dry, disturbed areas and is adapted to arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
