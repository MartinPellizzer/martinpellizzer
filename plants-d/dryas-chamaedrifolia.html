Dryas chamaedrifolia

Dryas chamaedrifolia, commonly known as the alpine dryas, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, leathery leaves that are typically arranged in a compact rosette pattern. It produces white or pale pink flowers in late spring, which are clustered in umbels.

Dryas chamaedrifolia is well adapted to harsh alpine environments, tolerating cold temperatures and poor soil conditions.
