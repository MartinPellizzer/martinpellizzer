Dichorisandra reginae

Dichorisandra reginae, commonly known as the Queen's lily, is a striking ornamental plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy, white to pink flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a distinctive red or purple center. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its elegant appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its unique beauty and hardiness, Dichorisandra reginae is widely cultivated for its ornamental value.
