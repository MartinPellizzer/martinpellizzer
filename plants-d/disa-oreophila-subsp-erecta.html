Disa oreophila subsp. erecta

Disa oreophila subsp.

erecta, commonly known as the mountain disa, is a strikingly beautiful orchid native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of vibrant, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to purple.

The plant typically grows in rocky or forested areas, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade. Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This subspecies is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological role in its native habitat.
