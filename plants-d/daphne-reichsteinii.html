Daphne reichsteinii

Daphne reichsteinii, commonly known as the Reichstein daphne, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its dense, compact growth and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. Its leaves are typically elliptical and have a glossy surface, while its stems are covered in a layer of fine, white hairs.

Daphne reichsteinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its winter blooms.
