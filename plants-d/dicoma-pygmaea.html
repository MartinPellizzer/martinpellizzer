Dicoma pygmaea

Dicoma pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf dandelion, is a small flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tiny, yellow composite flowers and hairy, spoon-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It has a low-growing habit, often reaching only a few centimeters in height.

Dicoma pygmaea is often found in fynbos vegetation, contributing to the biodiversity of its native habitat.
