Dendroglossa quercifolia

Dendroglossa quercifolia, commonly known as the oak-leaved dendroglossa, is a species of flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that resemble the shape of an oak leaf, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

Dendroglossa quercifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
