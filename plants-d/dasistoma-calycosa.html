Dasistoma calycosa

Dasistoma calycosa, commonly known as the golden cupplant, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant has a robust root system and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a reddish tinge when young.

The species is valued in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
