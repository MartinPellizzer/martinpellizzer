Dedea major

Dedea major, commonly known as the greater dead-nettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface and produces small, pinkish-purple flowers arranged in whorls along the stem. The plant belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Dedea major is known for its medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects.
