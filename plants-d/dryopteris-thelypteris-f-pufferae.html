Dryopteris thelypteris f. pufferae

Dryopteris thelypteris f. pufferae, commonly known as the Puffer's shield fern, is a fern species belonging to the family Dryopteridaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, rhizomatous growth habit and dense, dark green fronds that are typically leathery in texture. The fronds are pinnately divided with a distinctive, shield-like shape, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in shaded, moist environments, often found in woodland areas with rich, well-drained soil.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appearance and is often cultivated in shaded gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
