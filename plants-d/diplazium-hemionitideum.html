Diplazium hemionitideum

Diplazium hemionitideum, commonly known as the mountain fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Athyriaceae.

It is characterized by its bipinnate fronds, which are divided into two sets of pinnae, giving it a delicate, lacy appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky slopes. Its rhizomes are often covered in a layer of brownish, velvety tissue, which helps in moisture retention.

Diplazium hemionitideum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
