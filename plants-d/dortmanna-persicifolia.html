Dortmanna persicifolia

Dortmanna persicifolia, commonly known as the peach-leaved bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and natural landscapes.
