Dolichos sericophyllus

Dolichos sericophyllus, commonly known as the silky pod bean, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and southern Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, serpentine pods that give it its common name. The plant typically grows as a climbing vine, with trifoliate leaves and small, clustered flowers that are usually pale yellow or white. Its seeds are enclosed in long, thin pods and are often used in traditional medicine and as a food source in some regions.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
