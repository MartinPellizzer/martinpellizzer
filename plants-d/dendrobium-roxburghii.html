Dendrobium roxburghii

Dendrobium roxburghii, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is an epiphytic plant that grows on trees and rocks, thriving in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant produces striking yellow flowers with a sweet fragrance, often blooming in summer. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in varying environmental conditions.

Dendrobium roxburghii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated for both horticultural and medicinal purposes.
