Dianthus flahaultii

Dianthus flahaultii, commonly known as the Flahault's campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows to a height of about 15 to 30 centimeters, with hairy stems and basal leaves. Its flowers are arranged in clusters and feature five petals with a distinctive notched or frilled appearance.

Dianthus flahaultii is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.
