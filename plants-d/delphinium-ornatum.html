Delphinium ornatum

Delphinium ornatum, commonly known as the showy larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that bear clusters of blue to purple flowers, which are typically arranged in racemes. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. Delphinium ornatum is part of the Ranunculaceae family, which is known for its showy flowers and often toxic compounds.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.
