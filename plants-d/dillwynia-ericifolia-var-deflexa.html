Dillwynia ericifolia var. deflexa

Dillwynia ericifolia var. deflexa, commonly known as the drooping dillwynia, is a low-growing shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It features narrow, leathery leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring, adding a subtle ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal and sandy environments.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often used in native gardens and coastal landscaping.
