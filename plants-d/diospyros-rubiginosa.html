Diospyros rubiginosa

Diospyros rubiginosa, commonly known as the red persimmon, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into orange-red, fleshy fruits. Its fruit is edible when fully ripe, with a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor.

Diospyros rubiginosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in medicine and cuisine.
