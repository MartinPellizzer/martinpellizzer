Dactylorhiza × predaensis

Dactylorhiza × predaensis, commonly known as the hybrid marsh orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a terrestrial herb with a sympodial growth habit, producing a single, erect stem that bears several leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wet meadows, bogs, and marshy areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
