Dianthus girardinii

Dianthus girardinii, commonly known as the Cape daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and has a compact, bushy habit with hairy leaves. Its flowers are often clustered in dense inflorescences, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

Dianthus girardinii is drought-tolerant and prefers well-drained soil, making it suitable for Mediterranean climates.
