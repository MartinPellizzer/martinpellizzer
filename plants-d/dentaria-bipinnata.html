Dentaria bipinnata

Dentaria bipinnata, commonly known as the two-lobed toothplant, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, tooth-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in open woods or along stream banks.

Dentaria bipinnata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
