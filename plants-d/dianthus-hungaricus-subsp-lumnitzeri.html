Dianthus hungaricus subsp. lumnitzeri

Dianthus hungaricus subsp.

lumnitzeri, commonly known as the Hungarian carnation, is a flowering plant native to Central Europe. It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers with frilled petals.

The plant typically grows in meadows and rocky soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. It has a compact growth habit with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
