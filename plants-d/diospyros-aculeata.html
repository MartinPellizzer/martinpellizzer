Diospyros aculeata

Diospyros aculeata, commonly known as the prickly persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptic to ovate in shape and have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by small, round fruits with a rough, bumpy surface. The fruits are edible when fully ripe and have a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
