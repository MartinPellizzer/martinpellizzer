Diplachne patens

Diplachne patens, commonly known as the spreading flatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its flat, spreading growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. Diplachne patens thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

Its ecological role includes stabilizing soils and providing forage for livestock and wildlife.
