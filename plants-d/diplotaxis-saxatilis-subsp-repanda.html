Diplotaxis saxatilis subsp. repanda

Diplotaxis saxatilis subsp.

repanda, commonly known as the coastal wallflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is a low-growing, mat-forming species with narrow, linear leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal and inland areas. It is adapted to arid conditions and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
