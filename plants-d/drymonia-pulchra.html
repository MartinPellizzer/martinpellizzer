Drymonia pulchra

Drymonia pulchra, commonly known as the pretty drymonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers. Drymonia pulchra is valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife and its aesthetic appeal in woodland landscapes.
