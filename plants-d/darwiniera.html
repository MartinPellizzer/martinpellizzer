Darwiniera

Darwiniera, commonly known as the Darwin's orchid, is a genus of flowering plants in the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

These plants are native to South America, particularly Brazil, and are known for their striking, often colorful flowers. Darwiniera species typically grow as epiphytes, attaching themselves to trees and deriving moisture and nutrients from the air and rain. They produce intricate, showy blooms that can resemble miniature orchids, with specialized structures for pollination.

The genus is named in honor of Charles Darwin, who was fascinated by the unique adaptations of these plants.
