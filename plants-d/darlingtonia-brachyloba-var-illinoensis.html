Darlingtonia brachyloba var. illinoensis

Darlingtonia brachyloba var. illinoensis, commonly known as the Illinois pitcher plant, is a unique carnivorous plant native to the wetlands of Illinois.

It is characterized by its elongated, tubular leaves that form deep, pitcher-like structures to trap and digest insects. The plant thrives in moist, acidic environments and requires consistent humidity and cool temperatures for optimal growth. Its specialized leaves secrete digestive enzymes to break down prey, providing essential nutrients in nutrient-poor soils.

This variety is relatively rare and is often cultivated in specialized horticultural settings due to its specific care requirements.
