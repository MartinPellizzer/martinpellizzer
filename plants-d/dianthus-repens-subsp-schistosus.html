Dianthus repens subsp. schistosus

Dianthus repens subsp.

schistosus, commonly known as the stonecress dianthus, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family. It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with succulent, often hairy stems and fleshy leaves.

The plant produces clusters of small, pink or white flowers that bloom in early spring. It is typically found in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or arctic environments.

This subspecies is adapted to cold climates and is often used in rock gardens due to its hardy nature.
