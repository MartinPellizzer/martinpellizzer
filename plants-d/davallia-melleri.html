Davallia melleri

Davallia melleri, commonly known as the golden polypody, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Davalliaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are typically bright green. The plant produces sporangia on the underside of its fertile fronds, which are clustered in clusters called sori. It prefers humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on rocks or tree trunks.

Davallia melleri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
