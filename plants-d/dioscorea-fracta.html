Dioscorea fracta

Dioscorea fracta, commonly known as the broken yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into berries. Its tubers are notable for their high starch content and have been utilized in various culinary and medicinal applications.

However, the plant contains toxic compounds and requires careful preparation before use.
