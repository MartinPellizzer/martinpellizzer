Diospyros glandulosa

Diospyros glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular persimmon, is a species of flowering plant in the Ebenaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including countries like Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its small, glossy leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers. Its fruit is a small, round drupe that turns from green to red or orange when ripe.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses.
