Decaneurum molle var. scabridum

Decaneurum molle var. scabridum, commonly known as the roughleaf mullein, is a variant of the mullein plant characterized by its rough, hairy foliage.

It is native to regions of North America, particularly in the western United States. The plant features large, velvety leaves that are often covered with a dense layer of soft, fuzzy hairs. Its flowers are typically yellow or greenish-yellow, arranged in a spike-like inflorescence.

This variant is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
