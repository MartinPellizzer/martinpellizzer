Dennstaedtia punctilobula var. poyseri

Dennstaedtia punctilobula var. poyseri, commonly known as the Poyser's sword fern, is a variant of the sword fern genus native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are typically bright green with a slightly serrated margin. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices, where it can absorb moisture from the air. Its reproductive structures, including spore-producing sorus, are located on the underside of the fronds.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in maintaining forest understory biodiversity.
