Diosma myrtifolia

Diosma myrtifolia, commonly known as the myrtleleaf Diosma, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its aromatic foliage and showy flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are lance-shaped and emit a subtle myrtle-like scent, while its flowers are usually white or pale blue.

Diosma myrtifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
