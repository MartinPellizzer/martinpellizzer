Dioscorea karatana

Dioscorea karatana, commonly known as Karatana yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary economic part of the plant. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are hairy and often twining.

The tubers are used in traditional medicine and as a food source in some cultures.
