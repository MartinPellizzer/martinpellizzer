Dichapetalum corradii

Dichapetalum corradii, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has a woody stem and elliptical leaves with a smooth texture. Its flowers emit a strong, sweet scent to enhance nocturnal pollination.

Dichapetalum corradii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique blooming habit.
