Deutzia scabra f. watereri

Deutzia scabra f. watereri, commonly known as the watereri form of the scabrous deutzia, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to China.

It features small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring, typically in clusters along the stems. The plant has rough, hairy stems and leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value lies in its delicate flowers and upright growth habit.
