Declieuxia fruticosa var. guyanensis

Declieuxia fruticosa var. guyanensis, commonly known as the Guyana shrub chamaedorea, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is a shrub-like species characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically followed by clustered, edible fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and horticultural use.
