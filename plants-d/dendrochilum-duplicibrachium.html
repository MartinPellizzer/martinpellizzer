Dendrochilum duplicibrachium

Dendrochilum duplicibrachium, commonly known as the double-branched tree orchid, is a unique epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, pendulous inflorescences that bear numerous small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, utilizing its aerial roots to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air. Its leaves are broad and leathery, adapted to retain water in its natural habitat.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
