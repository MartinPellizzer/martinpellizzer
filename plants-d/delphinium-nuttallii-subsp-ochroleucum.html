Delphinium nuttallii subsp. ochroleucum

Delphinium nuttallii subsp.

ochroleucum, commonly known as the white-flowered larkspur, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States. It features tall, sparsely branched stems that bear clusters of showy, blue to white flowers.

The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is typically found in meadows and open woodlands.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance to pollinators.
