Dianthus strictus subsp. strictus

Dianthus strictus subsp.

strictus, commonly known as the upright carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and dense clusters of showy, bilaterally symmetrical flowers.

The flowers typically range in color from pink to red, with white or pale pink petals and prominent, showy stamens. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is well-suited for rock gardens and borders.
