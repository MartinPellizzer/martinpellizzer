Dulacia egleri

Dulacia egleri, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, velvety texture, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Dulacia egleri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its unique foliage.
