Dictyophleba lucida

Dictyophleba lucida, commonly known as the white mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy, white to pale yellow ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Dictyophleba lucida thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
