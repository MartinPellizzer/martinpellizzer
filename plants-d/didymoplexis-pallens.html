Didymoplexis pallens

Didymoplexis pallens, commonly known as the pale orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, pale pink to white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its flowers are clustered in inflorescences.

Didymoplexis pallens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique beauty.
