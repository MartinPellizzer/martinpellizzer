Dianthus angulatus

Dianthus angulatus, commonly known as the angular carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, bicolor flowers that typically feature a white or pink center surrounded by red petals. The plant produces small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Dianthus angulatus is also valued for its fragrance and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
