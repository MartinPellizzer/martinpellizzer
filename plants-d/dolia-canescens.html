Dolia canescens

Dolia canescens, commonly known as the silverleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of silvery-gray, hairy leaves that help reduce water loss in arid conditions. The plant produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and rocky areas.

Dolia canescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses.
