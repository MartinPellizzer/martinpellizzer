Dactylorhiza × csatoi

Dactylorhiza × csatoi, commonly known as the marsh orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a terrestrial plant that typically grows in wet or damp habitats such as bogs, marshes, and meadows. The plant has a rhizomatous root system and produces tall, slender stems that bear numerous showy, spike-like flowers. Its flowers are usually pink or purple in color and are arranged in dense clusters.

Dactylorhiza × csatoi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with moist, well-drained soil.
