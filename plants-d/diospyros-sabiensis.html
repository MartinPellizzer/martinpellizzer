Diospyros sabiensis

Diospyros sabiensis, commonly known as the Sabiense persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes ebony and persimmon trees. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by edible, orange-red berries. The fruit is typically consumed raw or used in local traditional medicines.

Its ecological importance includes providing food for various wildlife species and contributing to forest biodiversity.
