Dupatya decussa

Dupatya decussa, commonly known as the narrow-leafed piper, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Piperaceae, which includes other species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its alternate, lance-shaped leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. It typically grows in humid, shaded environments such as forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticultural and ecological studies.
