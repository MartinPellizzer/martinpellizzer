Dactylorhiza elata var. munbyana

Dactylorhiza elata var. munbyana, commonly known as the Munby's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and linear, dark green leaves. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers with three sepals and three petals, typically arranged in a spike. It thrives in moist, shaded habitats such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Dactylorhiza elata var. munbyana is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its ornamental value and ecological importance.
