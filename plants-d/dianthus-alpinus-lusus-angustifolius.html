Dianthus alpinus lusus angustifolius

Dianthus alpinus lusus angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaf alpine campion, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of pink or white flowers with five petals. The leaves are typically narrow and linear, arranged in a basal rosette, and the plant is native to alpine regions of Europe. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
