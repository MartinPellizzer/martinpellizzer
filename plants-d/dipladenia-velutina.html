Dipladenia velutina

Dipladenia velutina, commonly known as the velvet leaf morning glory, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its velvety, hairy leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces small, round berries that turn from green to yellow or orange as they mature. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun for optimal growth.

Dipladenia velutina is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and vibrant blooms.
