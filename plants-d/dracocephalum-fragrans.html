Dracocephalum fragrans

Dracocephalum fragrans, commonly known as fragrant skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants such as mint and basil. The plant is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite leaves that are often gray-green in color. Its small, tubular flowers are typically violet or blue, arranged in whorls along the stem.

Dracocephalum fragrans is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
