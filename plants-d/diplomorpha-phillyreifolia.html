Diplomorpha phillyreifolia

Diplomorpha phillyreifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of silver-gray, hairy leaves that help it retain moisture in arid conditions. The plant produces small, yellow composite flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and resilience, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
