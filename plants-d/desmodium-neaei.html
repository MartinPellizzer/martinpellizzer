Desmodium neaei

Desmodium neaei, commonly known as the African ticktrefoil, is a flowering plant native to various regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish to reddish flowers. The plant is often found in grasslands and open woodlands, thriving in well-drained soils. It plays a role in soil nitrogen fixation due to its symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria.

Its ornamental value and ability to withstand drought make it a useful plant in ecological and horticultural contexts.
