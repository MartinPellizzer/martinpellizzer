Doryopteris nicklesii

Doryopteris nicklesii, commonly known as the Nickles' holly fern, is a species of fern native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and distinctive, leathery fronds that are often used in horticultural displays. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are thick and fleshy, allowing it to store moisture and nutrients efficiently.

Doryopteris nicklesii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging garden conditions.
