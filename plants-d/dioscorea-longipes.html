Dioscorea longipes

Dioscorea longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its long, slender stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces tubers that are often used as food in various traditional cuisines. Its growth habit involves twining around supports, and it thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil.

The tubers of Dioscorea longipes are typically harvested after several months of growth and are an important agricultural resource in some regions.
