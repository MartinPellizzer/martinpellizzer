Digitaria aequatoriensis

Digitaria aequatoriensis, commonly known as the African grass or false brome, is a species of grass native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is a perennial plant that typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands, savannas, and disturbed soils. The plant has narrow, elongated leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is adapted to dry conditions and can thrive in areas with limited water availability.

Digitaria aequatoriensis is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience and ability to stabilize soil.
