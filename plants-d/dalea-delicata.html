Dalea delicata

Dalea delicata, commonly known as the slender milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant features slender stems and small, pink to purple pea-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several pairs of oval-shaped leaflets.

Dalea delicata is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its adaptation to arid environments.
