Dendrobium janowskyi

Dendrobium janowskyi, commonly known as the Janowsky's orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its pseudobulbs and clustered inflorescences. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually fragrant and come in a variety of colors, including white, yellow, and pink.

Dendrobium janowskyi is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its beauty and long-lasting blooms.
