Discaria spiculata var. gracilenta

Discaria spiculata var. gracilenta, commonly known as the slender discaria, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are usually hairy and have a distinctive glandular appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by woody, spiny fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
