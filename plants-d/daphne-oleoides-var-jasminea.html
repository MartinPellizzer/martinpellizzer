Daphne oleoides var. jasminea

Daphne oleoides var. jasminea, commonly known as the Persian daphne, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Iran.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white or pale pink flowers that emit a jasmine-like scent, hence its common name. The plant has elliptical, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and its stems are typically covered in a waxy, white coating. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Due to its ornamental value and unique fragrance, it is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and aromatic qualities.
