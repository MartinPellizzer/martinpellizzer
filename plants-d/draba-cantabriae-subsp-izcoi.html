Draba cantabriae subsp. izcoi

Draba cantabriae subsp.

izcoi, commonly known as the Izcoy draba, is a small flowering plant native to the Pyrenees region of Spain. It is characterized by its basal rosette of pale green, hairy leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers in early spring.

The species is adapted to rocky and alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It is part of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species within its range.
