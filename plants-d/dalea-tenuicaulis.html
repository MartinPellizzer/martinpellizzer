Dalea tenuicaulis

Dalea tenuicaulis, commonly known as slenderstem milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are compound, with small leaflets that help reduce water loss, and it produces pods that contain seeds dispersed by wind.

Dalea tenuicaulis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
