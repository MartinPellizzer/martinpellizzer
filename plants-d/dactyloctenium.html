Dactyloctenium

Dactyloctenium, commonly known as silvergrass, is a genus of flowering plants in the grass family.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, silver-green leaves. The plants typically produce small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. Dactyloctenium species are often found in disturbed soils and can form dense mats in grasslands.

They are adapted to a wide range of environmental conditions, making them resilient and widespread in various habitats.
