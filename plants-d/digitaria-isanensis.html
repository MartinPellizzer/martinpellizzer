Digitaria isanensis

Digitaria isanensis, commonly known as the Isan grass, is a perennial grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetland or riparian habitats.

Digitaria isanensis is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
