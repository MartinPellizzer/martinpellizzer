Dianthus serratus

Dianthus serratus, commonly known as the serrated pinks, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, pink or white flowers. The leaves are narrow and serrated, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and fragrance.
