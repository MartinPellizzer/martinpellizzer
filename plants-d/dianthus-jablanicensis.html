Dianthus jablanicensis

Dianthus jablanicensis, commonly known as the Balkan carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink or white flowers with distinctive frilled petals. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Dianthus jablanicensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its colorful display.
