Diplotaxis erucoides var. hispidula

Diplotaxis erucoides var. hispidula, commonly known as the hispid diamondhead, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and pungent aroma. The plant features a hairy, branched stem and narrow, linear leaves with a distinctive waxy coating. Its flowers are typically yellow with four petals and are arranged in racemes.

This species is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and resilience in various environmental conditions.
