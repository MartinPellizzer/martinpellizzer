Drypetes calocarpa

Drypetes calocarpa, commonly known as the red-flowered ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood and striking red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has alternate, simple leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. It thrives in humid climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental flowers and durable timber.
