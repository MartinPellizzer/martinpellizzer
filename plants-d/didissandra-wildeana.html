Didissandra wildeana

Didissandra wildeana, commonly known as the wild dill or African dill, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate flowers.

It is native to southern Africa and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that give it a delicate appearance. Its small white to pale yellow flowers appear in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits and is also cultivated as an ornamental plant.
