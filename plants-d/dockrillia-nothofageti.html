Dockrillia nothofageti

Dockrillia nothofageti, commonly known as the Antarctic hair grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southern regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.
