Dianthus crinitus subsp. soongoricus

Dianthus crinitus subsp.

soongoricus, commonly known as the Kashmir carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas. It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and clusters of pink to red, cup-shaped flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky, alpine environments and prefers well-drained soil. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.

This subspecies is closely related to other Dianthus species and shares similar botanical features.
