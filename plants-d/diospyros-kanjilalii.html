Diospyros kanjilalii

Diospyros kanjilalii, commonly known as the Kanchilal date palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Ebenaceae family.

It is native to the Indian subcontinent and is characterized by its stout, erect trunk and dense, clustered leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into edible, sweet, and fleshy fruits. Diospyros kanjilalii is valued for its ornamental qualities and its fruit, which is often consumed fresh or used in traditional cuisine.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
