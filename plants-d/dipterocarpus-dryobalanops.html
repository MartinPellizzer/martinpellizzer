Dipterocarpus dryobalanops

Dipterocarpus dryobalanops, commonly known as the Malayan ironwood, is a large evergreen tree native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, heavy wood that is highly valued for its durability and strength. The tree has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. It produces small, pale yellow flowers that are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

This species plays a significant role in the ecological dynamics of its habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
