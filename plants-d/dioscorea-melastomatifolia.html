Dioscorea melastomatifolia

Dioscorea melastomatifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces tuberous roots that are sometimes used in traditional medicine. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
