Dryopteris parrisiae

Dryopteris parrisiae, commonly known as the Parry's wood fern, is a species of fern native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its delicate, bipinnate fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky slopes. Its rhizomes are creeping and produce dense clusters of foliage.

The spores are released from the sori, which are located on the underside of the fronds.
