Dendrocalamus criticus

Dendrocalamus criticus, commonly known as the giant bamboo, is a tall, robust grass-like plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that can grow up to 30 meters in height. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically green and covered in a fine layer of wax. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Dendrocalamus criticus is valued for its strong, lightweight culms, which are used in construction and crafts.
