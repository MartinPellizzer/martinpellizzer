Dioscorea rhacodes

Dioscorea rhacodes, commonly known as the African yam bean, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its cylindrical, tuberous roots that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its tubers are edible and have a starchy, sweet flavor, making them a valuable food source in some regions.

The plant is also valued for its potential medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects.
