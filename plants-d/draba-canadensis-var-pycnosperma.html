Draba canadensis var. pycnosperma

Draba canadensis var. pycnosperma, commonly known as the Canadian rockcress, is a member of the Brassicaceae family and is native to North America.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its basal rosette of linear, glaucous leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or arctic environments.

Its compact growth habit and ability to tolerate harsh conditions make it an important component of high-altitude ecosystems.
