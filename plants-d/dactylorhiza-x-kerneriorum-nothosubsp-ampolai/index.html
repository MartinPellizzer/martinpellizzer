Dactylorhiza × Kerneriorum Nothosubsp. Ampolai

Dactylorhiza × kerneriorum nothosubsp. ampolai is a rare and intriguing orchid hybrid found primarily in the mountainous regions of the Balkans. This plant is the result of natural crossbreeding between Dactylorhiza incarnata and Dactylorhiza macdougallii, creating a unique blend of genetic traits. Its flowers are striking, with a mix of purple and white hues that make it highly sought after by botanists and plant enthusiasts. Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is now considered vulnerable, prompting conservation efforts to protect its dwindling populations. Studying this species offers valuable insights into hybridization processes and the challenges of preserving biodiversity in changing environments.
