Draba dedeana subsp. mawii

Draba dedeana subsp.

mawii, commonly known as the Mawii whitlow grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family. It is a low-growing perennial that typically forms dense cushions or mats, often found in alpine or arctic environments.

The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early summer. Its leaves are basal, linear, and covered in fine hairs, adapted to withstand cold and harsh conditions.

The species is native to regions in Central Asia and is of interest to botanists due to its ecological and evolutionary significance in high-altitude ecosystems.
