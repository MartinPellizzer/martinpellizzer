Dioclea macrocarpa

Dioclea macrocarpa, commonly known as the giant passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that are typically red or orange with a distinctive crown of filaments. The plant produces edible berries that are sometimes used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and it has a woody stem with thorns.

Dioclea macrocarpa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
