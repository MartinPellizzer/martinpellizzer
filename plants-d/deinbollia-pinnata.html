Deinbollia pinnata

Deinbollia pinnata, commonly known as the African velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves that are pinnately arranged, with each leaflet having a velvety texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that contain several seeds.

Deinbollia pinnata is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and agricultural fields.
