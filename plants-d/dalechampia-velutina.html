Dalechampia velutina

Dalechampia velutina, commonly known as velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety pubescence. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically clustered in loose inflorescences. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and waste grounds.

Dalechampia velutina is also known for its ability to spread aggressively, making it a common weed in agricultural settings.
