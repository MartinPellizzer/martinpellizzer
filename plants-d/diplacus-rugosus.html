Diplacus rugosus

Diplacus rugosus, commonly known as the roughleaf monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, rugged stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Diplacus rugosus is a member of the Plantaginaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
