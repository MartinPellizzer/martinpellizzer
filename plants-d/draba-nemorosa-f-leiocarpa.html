Draba nemorosa f. leiocarpa

Draba nemorosa f. leiocarpa, commonly known as the smoothleaf whitlow grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its basal rosette of elliptical, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodland edges and open meadows.

This subspecies is distinguished by its smooth, hairless leaves compared to the typical form of Draba nemorosa.
