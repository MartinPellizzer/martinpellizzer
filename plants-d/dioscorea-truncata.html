Dioscorea truncata

Dioscorea truncata, commonly known as the African yam bean, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces tuberous roots that are edible and used in traditional cuisines. Its growth habit involves twining stems that ascend support structures, and it thrives in warm, humid climates.

The tubers are also valued for their nutritional content and have been used in traditional medicine.
