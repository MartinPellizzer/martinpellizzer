Didymocheton dolichobotrys

Didymocheton dolichobotrys, commonly known as the long-stalked tamarind tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, glossy green leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that develop into elongated, pod-like fruits. The fruit contains several flat, hard seeds surrounded by a fibrous pulp. This plant is often found in moist, lowland areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
