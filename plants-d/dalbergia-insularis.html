Dalbergia insularis

Dalbergia insularis, commonly known as the Madagascar rosewood, is a tropical tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its dense, dark wood with a rich, aromatic scent. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers followed by pod-like fruits.

It is valued for its high-quality timber but is also threatened by overexploitation and habitat loss.
