Deamia chontalensis

Deamia chontalensis, commonly known as the Chontalensis cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its columnar stems covered in spines and its ability to store water in its thick, fleshy tissues. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer seasons, adding a striking visual element to its desert habitat. It thrives in arid environments and requires minimal water, making it well adapted to harsh conditions.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by succulent enthusiasts.
