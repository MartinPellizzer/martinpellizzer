Diospyros castanea

Diospyros castanea, commonly known as the Chinese persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and alternate, simple leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that give way to orange to reddish fruits, which are typically harvested when fully ripe. The fruits are edible, sweet, and have a unique texture, making them a valuable crop in horticulture.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental and culinary uses.
