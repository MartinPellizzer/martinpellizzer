Danthonia gammiei

Danthonia gammiei, commonly known as the New Zealand hair grass, is a perennial grass species native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and dense, compact growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and subalpine environments. Its inflorescence consists of a single, narrow spikelet that is often concealed within the foliage.

Danthonia gammiei plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native invertebrates.
