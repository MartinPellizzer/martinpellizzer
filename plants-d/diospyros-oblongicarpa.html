Diospyros oblongicarpa

Diospyros oblongicarpa, commonly known as the long-fruit persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and simple, alternate leaves. The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitats.
