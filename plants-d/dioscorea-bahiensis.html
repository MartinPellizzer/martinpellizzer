Dioscorea bahiensis

Dioscorea bahiensis, commonly known as the Bahia yam, is a species of tuberous vine native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellowish flowers and is known for its edible tuberous roots, which are used in traditional cuisines.

Botanically, it exhibits a complex rhizome system and is adapted to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.
