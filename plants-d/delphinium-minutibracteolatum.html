Delphinium minutibracteolatum

Delphinium minutibracteolatum, commonly known as the narrow-bract delphinium, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height, adorned with clusters of striking blue to violet flowers. The plant produces small, ovate leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. Its flowers are typically arranged in racemose inflorescences, and it blooms during the summer months.

Delphinium minutibracteolatum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and architectural form.
