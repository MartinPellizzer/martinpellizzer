Dendrobium williamsonii

Dendrobium williamsonii, commonly known as the Williamson's orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is an epiphytic plant, meaning it grows on the surface of trees or other structures without deriving nutrients from them. The plant features long, cane-like stems that can store water and nutrients, typical of many orchid species. Its flowers are usually showy, with a variety of colors including white, yellow, and pink, and they bloom during the rainy season.

Dendrobium williamsonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
