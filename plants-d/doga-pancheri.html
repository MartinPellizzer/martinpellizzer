Doga pancheri

Doga pancheri, commonly known as the Indian gooseberry or amla, is a deciduous shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, green berries that turn orange-red when mature, containing a high concentration of vitamin C. Doga pancheri thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

It is widely cultivated for its nutritional value and traditional medicinal uses.
