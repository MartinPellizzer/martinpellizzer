Dactylorhiza maculata subsp. sudetica

Dactylorhiza maculata subsp.

sudetica, commonly known as the Sudetic marsh orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Central and Eastern Europe. It typically grows in wet, calcareous habitats such as marshes and fens.

The plant features a slender, erect stem with several oval, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are pinkish-brown with a distinctive dark brown or purple blotch on the lip.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its delicate, ephemeral blooms.
