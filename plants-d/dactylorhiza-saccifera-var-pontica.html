Dactylorhiza saccifera var. pontica

Dactylorhiza saccifera var. pontica, commonly known as the Pontic marsh orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in wet, acidic habitats such as bogs and marshes. The plant has a slender, upright stem with several narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are pale green to yellowish and are arranged in a loose spike.

It is characterized by its unique saccate (sac-like) labellum, which is a distinguishing botanical feature.
