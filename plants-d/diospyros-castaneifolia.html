Diospyros castaneifolia

Diospyros castaneifolia, commonly known as the Chinese date or persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and broad, palmate leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into orange to red fruits with a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor. Its fruits are edible and often consumed fresh or used in traditional medicine.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and economic importance in both horticulture and agriculture.
