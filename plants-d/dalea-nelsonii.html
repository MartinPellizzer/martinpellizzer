Dalea nelsonii

Dalea nelsonii, commonly known as Nelson's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats, such as gravelly soils and open woodlands. Its root system forms symbiotic relationships with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, enhancing soil fertility.

Dalea nelsonii is often found in disturbed areas and is adapted to arid environments with minimal water availability.
