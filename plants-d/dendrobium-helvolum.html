Dendrobium helvolum

Dendrobium helvolum, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, cane-like stems and clusters of fragrant, golden-yellow flowers. The plant typically blooms during the summer months, producing showy inflorescences that attract pollinators. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Dendrobium helvolum is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
