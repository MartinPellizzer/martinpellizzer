Dichapetalum ndongense

Dichapetalum ndongense, commonly known as the Namibian flame tree, is a strikingly ornamental plant native to southern Africa.

It is a deciduous tree or shrub characterized by its large, showy red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are often lobed or heart-shaped, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique floral display, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and public spaces.
