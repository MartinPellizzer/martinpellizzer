Decameria

Decameria, commonly known as the golden groundsel, is a genus of flowering plants in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

It is native to South America and is characterized by its showy, yellow or orange flowers that bloom in the spring. The plants typically have hairy stems and leaves that are often lobed or toothed. Decameria species are often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

They are valued for their ornamental appeal and are sometimes cultivated in gardens.
