Digitalis ferruginea f. membranaceoviolacea

Digitalis ferruginea f. membranaceoviolacea, commonly known as the purple membrane viola foxglove, is a cultivar of the foxglove plant characterized by its striking violet-purple flower petals.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is known for its bell-shaped flowers that typically bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are lanceolate and covered in fine, hairy textures, which help in reducing water loss. This cultivar is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and is often grown for its unique coloration and delicate appearance.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens or shaded borders.
