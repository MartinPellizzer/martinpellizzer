Dipyrena cinerascens

Dipyrena cinerascens, commonly known as the ash-leaf daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has hairy, grayish-green leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassy areas and along roadsides.

Dipyrena cinerascens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
