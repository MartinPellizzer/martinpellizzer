Dysoxylum rugulosum

Dysoxylum rugulosum, commonly known as the rough-ribbed wood or wild mahogany, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae, which includes mahogany and other valuable hardwoods. The plant is characterized by its rough, ridged bark and opposite, simple leaves with serrated margins. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, elongated fruits containing several seeds.
