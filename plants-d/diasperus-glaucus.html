Diasperus glaucus

Diasperus glaucus, commonly known as the blue desert daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing form and produces small, blue or violet flowers. The plant has silver-gray foliage due to a dense covering of fine hairs, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to full sun exposure.

Diasperus glaucus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its drought tolerance and aesthetic appeal.
