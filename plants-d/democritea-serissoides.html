Democritea serissoides

Democritea serissoides, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in diameter, providing a unique habitat for various aquatic organisms. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in swampy areas and ponds. Its showy white flowers bloom at night, attracting specific pollinators such as beetles.

The plant's extensive root system helps stabilize the soil and prevent erosion in wetland ecosystems.
