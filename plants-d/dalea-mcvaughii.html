Dalea mcvaughii

Dalea mcvaughii, commonly known as McVaugh's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitat.

Its unique morphology and ecological significance make it an important species in its ecosystem.
