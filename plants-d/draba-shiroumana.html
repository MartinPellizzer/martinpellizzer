Draba shiroumana

Draba shiroumana, commonly known as the Shiroumanan whitlow grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a small, herbaceous annual that typically grows in alpine and arctic regions. The plant has a compact rosette of basal leaves and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in early summer. Its flowers are arranged in a single terminal inflorescence, and it is adapted to cold climates with a short growing season.

Draba shiroumana is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is a characteristic species of high-altitude ecosystems.
