Digitaria elongata

Digitaria elongata, commonly known as common wiregrass, is a perennial grass native to warm temperate and tropical regions.

It typically grows in open areas such as roadsides, pastures, and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, erect stems that are often covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are narrow with a hairy underside. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, two-rowed spikelets.

Digitaria elongata is known for its adaptability and is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
