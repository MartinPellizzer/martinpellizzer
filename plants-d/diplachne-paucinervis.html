Diplachne paucinervis

Diplachne paucinervis, commonly known as slender flatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, flat leaves that are typically less than 2 mm wide. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with minute, two-ranked spikelets. Diplachne paucinervis is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in open, disturbed areas.

It plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
