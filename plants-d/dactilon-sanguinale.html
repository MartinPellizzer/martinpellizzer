Dactilon sanguinale

Dactilon sanguinale, commonly known as the bloodroot, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its single, bright red flower that blooms in early spring. The plant has a rhizomatous root system and is often found in moist, wooded areas. Its leaves are basal, heart-shaped, and have a distinctive reddish hue.

Dactilon sanguinale is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
