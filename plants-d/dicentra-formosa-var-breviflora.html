Dicentra formosa var. breviflora

Dicentra formosa var. breviflora, commonly known as the short-flowered bleeding heart, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features delicate, heart-shaped pink or white blooms that appear on slender stems in early spring. The plant has trifoliate leaves with a deep green color and a hairy texture. It thrives in partial shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ornamental addition to native plant collections.
