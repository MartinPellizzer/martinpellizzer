Dioscorea arcuata

Dioscorea arcuata, commonly known as the creeping yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into cylindrical berries. Its growth habit involves twining around supports, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

The tubers of D. arcuata are valued for their potential medicinal properties and nutritional content.
