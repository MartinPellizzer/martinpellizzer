Dinochloa nicobariana

Dinochloa nicobariana, commonly known as the Nicobar spikerush, is a perennial aquatic plant native to the Nicobar Islands and parts of Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in shallow freshwater habitats such as ponds, marshes, and wetlands. The plant has long, narrow leaves that form dense underwater stands, and it produces small, clustered spikes of flowers that bloom in the spring. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The species plays an important role in maintaining aquatic ecosystem balance by providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
