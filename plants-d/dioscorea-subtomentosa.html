Dioscorea subtomentosa

Dioscorea subtomentosa, commonly known as the hairy yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by tuberous roots that are sometimes used in traditional medicine. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a complex network of runners that help in spreading across the ground.

The tubers of Dioscorea subtomentosa are often harvested for their nutritional and medicinal properties.
