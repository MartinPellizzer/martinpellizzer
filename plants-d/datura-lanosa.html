Datura lanosa

Datura lanosa, commonly known as the woolly jimsonweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces large, showy flowers that are typically purple or white, blooming in late summer. Its fruit is a spiny capsule that contains numerous seeds.

Datura lanosa is known for its toxic properties, containing tropane alkaloids that can be harmful to humans and animals.
