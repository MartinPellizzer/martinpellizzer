Dianthus hirtus subsp. requienii

Dianthus hirtus subsp.

requienii, commonly known as the Sierra Madre pinks, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a low-growing perennial with dense clusters of pink to red flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant has hairy, ovate leaves and thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. It is often found in open woodlands and mountain meadows.

Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to the dry, sunny conditions of its native habitat.
