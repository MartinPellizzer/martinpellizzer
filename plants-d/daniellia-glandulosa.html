Daniellia glandulosa

Daniellia glandulosa, commonly known as the African sand forest tree, is a species native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, often greyish bark and compound leaves with elongated, pointed leaflets. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by woody pods containing several seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Due to its ecological importance, it plays a significant role in the biodiversity of its native region.
