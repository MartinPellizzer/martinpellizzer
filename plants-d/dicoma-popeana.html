Dicoma popeana

Dicoma popeana, commonly known as the Pope's daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Dicoma popeana is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its delicate appearance.
