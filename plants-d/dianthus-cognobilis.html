Dianthus cognobilis

Dianthus cognobilis, commonly known as the showy campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its bright pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer, typically in clusters at the ends of its stems. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are slightly hairy, and it grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Dianthus cognobilis is also known for its historical use in herbal medicine.
