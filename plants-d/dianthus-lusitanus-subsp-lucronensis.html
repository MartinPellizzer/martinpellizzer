Dianthus lusitanus subsp. lucronensis

Dianthus lusitanus subsp.

lucronensis, commonly known as the Iberian campion, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe, particularly Spain and Portugal. It is a low-growing perennial herb with distinctive pink to red flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often reddish in color. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

The flowers are composed of numerous small petals arranged in a cup-like formation, giving it a delicate and ornamental appearance.
