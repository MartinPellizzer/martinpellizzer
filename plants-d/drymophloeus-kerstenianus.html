Drymophloeus kerstenianus

Drymophloeus kerstenianus, commonly known as the Kersten's ironwood, is a small to medium-sized tree native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood, which is highly valued for its durability and strength. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow, followed by small, round fruits containing a single seed.

This species is often found in humid lowland forests and is known for its ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
