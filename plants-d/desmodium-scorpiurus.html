Desmodium scorpiurus

Desmodium scorpiurus, commonly known as the scorpionweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its legume fruits and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant has trifoliate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and its flowers are typically small and clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open woodlands or along roadsides.

The species is also noted for its ability to spread rapidly, making it both a weed and a potential forage plant.
