Diplazium petersenii

Diplazium petersenii, commonly known as Petersen's water fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, floating fronds that are typically divided into small, lance-shaped segments. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and marshes. Its rhizomes are often submerged, anchoring the plant while absorbing nutrients from the water.

Diplazium petersenii plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for microorganisms and small aquatic life.
