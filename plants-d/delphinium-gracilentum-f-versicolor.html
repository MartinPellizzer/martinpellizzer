Delphinium gracilentum f. versicolor

Delphinium gracilentum f. versicolor, commonly known as the variable larkspur, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems that bear clusters of showy, spurred flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white. The plant's botanical features include lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture and a distinct, elongated nectar spur in each flower. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Due to its vibrant colors and unique morphology, it is valued both for ornamental purposes and ecological benefits.
