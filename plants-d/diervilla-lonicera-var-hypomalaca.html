Diervilla lonicera var. hypomalaca

Diervilla lonicera var. hypomalaca, commonly known as the red-leaf honeysuckle, is a shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and opposite, ovate leaves that turn vibrant red in the fall. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in the spring, followed by orange-red berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its adaptability and seasonal color changes make it a visually striking addition to landscapes.
