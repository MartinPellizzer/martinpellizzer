Dichapetalum missionum

Dichapetalum missionum, commonly known as the Mission fig or desert fig, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, opposite leaves that are usually gray-green in color and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. Its fruit is a fleshy, orange-red berry that ripens in late summer and is an important food source for wildlife.

The species is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky soils and desert landscapes.
