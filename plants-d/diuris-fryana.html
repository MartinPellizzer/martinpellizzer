Diuris fryana

Diuris fryana, commonly known as the pink fairy orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southeastern Australia.

It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils in open heathlands and woodland areas. The plant features a slender, branching stem with several narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small, pinkish-white, and have a distinctive, hood-like labellum.

Diuris fryana is known for its ephemeral blooming habit, often appearing after rainfall in spring.
