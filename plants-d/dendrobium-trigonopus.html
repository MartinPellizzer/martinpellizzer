Dendrobium trigonopus

Dendrobium trigonopus, commonly known as the triangle orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of fragrant, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and has a fibrous root system. Its leaves are strap-shaped and often arranged in a rosette pattern.

Dendrobium trigonopus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
