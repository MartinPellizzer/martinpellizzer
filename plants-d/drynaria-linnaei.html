Drynaria linnaei

Drynaria linnaei, commonly known as the bamboo fern, is a terrestrial fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Drynariaceae and is characterized by its tall, bamboo-like rhizomes and pinnate fronds. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth. Its fronds are typically dark green and have a leathery texture, contributing to its ornamental value.

Drynaria linnaei is valued in horticulture for its unique appearance and adaptability to various soil conditions.
