Daemonorops affinis

Daemonorops affinis, commonly known as the fan palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in diameter. The plant has a stout, unbranched stem that often remains hidden beneath the dense foliage. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The fruit of Daemonorops affinis is a small, spiny drupe that contains a single seed.
