Digitaria sellowii

Digitaria sellowii, commonly known as African grass or blackgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are often green or brown in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is commonly found in wetlands and along riverbanks.

Digitaria sellowii is often considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its rapid growth and adaptability.
