Dysophylla stocksii

Dysophylla stocksii, commonly known as the silverleaf dysophylla, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Dysophylla stocksii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
