Disa minax

Disa minax, commonly known as the narrow-leaved disa, is a species of orchid native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and delicate, spider-like flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. It is a perennial herb that produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
