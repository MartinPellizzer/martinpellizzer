Dalea onobrychioides

Dalea onobrychioides, commonly known as the plains false indigo, is a herbaceous perennial native to the central United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pale blue to violet. The plant has compound leaves with numerous narrow, feather-like leaflets, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in prairie and open woodland habitats.

Dalea onobrychioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
