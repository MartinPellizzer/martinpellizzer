Danthonia unarede

Danthonia unarede, commonly known as the narrowleaf danthonia, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
