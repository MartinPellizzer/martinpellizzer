Dalbergia scorpioides

Dalbergia scorpioides, commonly known as the African bloodwood, is a small to medium-sized leguminous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its dark, durable wood and distinctive, often thorny appearance. The plant has bipinnate leaves and produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are followed by elongated, woody pods. It is adapted to dry and semi-arid conditions, making it a hardy species found in various savanna and woodland habitats.

The tree is also valued for its ecological role in soil stabilization and as a habitat for wildlife.
