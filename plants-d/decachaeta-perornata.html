Decachaeta perornata

Decachaeta perornata, commonly known as the golden star cactus, is a unique succulent native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny stems that form a rounded, shrub-like structure. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped yellow flowers during the dry season, which attract pollinators like bees and moths. Its leaves are modified into spines, reducing water loss and adapting to harsh desert conditions.

Decachaeta perornata is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in low-water environments.
