Dendrobium ramosum

Dendrobium ramosum, commonly known as the branching orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a pseudobulb-like stem that stores water and nutrients. The plant produces multiple racemes of fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white, yellow, and orange. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, often arranged in two rows along the stem.

Dendrobium ramosum thrives in humid, shaded environments and is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value.
