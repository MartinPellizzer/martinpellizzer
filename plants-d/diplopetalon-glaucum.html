Diplopetalon glaucum

Diplopetalon glaucum, commonly known as the bluebells of the genus Diplopetalon, is a flowering plant characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers.

It is native to certain regions of Southeast Asia, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a waxy, bluish-green surface that reflects light. Its flowers are clustered in racemes and bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
