Dactylorhiza majalis var. pulchella

Dactylorhiza majalis var. pulchella, commonly known as the purple marsh orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in wet, nutrient-poor habitats such as bogs and marshes. The plant features a tall, erect stem with several lance-shaped leaves and produces spikes of purple or pinkish flowers. It has a complex root system that includes a network of mycorrhizal associations essential for nutrient uptake.

This orchid is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
