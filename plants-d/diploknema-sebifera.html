Diploknema sebifera

Diploknema sebifera, commonly known as the African shea tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Malvaceae and is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves and smooth, greyish bark. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by capsule fruits containing oily seeds. These seeds are the source of shea butter, a valuable natural product used in cosmetics and food.

Its ecological and economic importance makes it a significant species in its native regions.
