Dichanthium saccharoides subvar. torreyanum

Dichanthium saccharoides subvar. torreyanum, commonly known as Torrey's grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically clustered at the base of the stems. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Its fibrous root system helps stabilize soil, making it an important plant for erosion control in its native range.
