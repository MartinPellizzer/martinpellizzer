Dichanthium aristatum

Dichanthium aristatum, commonly known as the spikegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often bear dense clusters of spikelets. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with two to three bracts, which are typically adorned with short, stiff hairs. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration that helps it conserve water in dry environments.

Dichanthium aristatum is often found in disturbed soils and is used for forage in some regions due to its hardiness and adaptability.
