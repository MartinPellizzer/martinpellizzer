Dyckia argentea

Dyckia argentea, commonly known as the silver dyckia, is a succulent plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern. The plant features narrow, silver-gray leaves that are covered in a fine, powdery coating, which helps reduce water loss. It produces striking, sword-shaped inflorescences that bloom in shades of red, orange, or yellow.

Dyckia argentea is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water and well-draining soil.
