Dianthus cintranus subsp. cintranus

Dianthus cintranus subsp.

cintranus, commonly known as the alpine campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant pink or red flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant produces small, rounded leaves that are covered in a fine layer of white hair, aiding in water retention in its alpine habitat. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in meadows and slopes at high altitudes. Dianthus cintranus subsp.

cintranus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in alpine ecosystems.
