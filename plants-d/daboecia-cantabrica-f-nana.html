Daboecia cantabrica f. nana

Daboecia cantabrica f. nana, commonly known as the dwarf rock heather, is a compact, low-growing shrub native to the coastal regions of northern Spain.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats. Its compact form and hardy nature make it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscaping.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
