Dahlia crocea var. flava

Dahlia crocea var. flava, commonly known as the yellow dahlia, is a strikingly colorful tuberous perennial native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers, which bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant features sturdy, upright stems and large, showy blooms with a single row of petals. Its foliage consists of heart-shaped, dark green leaves that provide a striking contrast to the bright flowers.

This variety is popular in gardens for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation.
