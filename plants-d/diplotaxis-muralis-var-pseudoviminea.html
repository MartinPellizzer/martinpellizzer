Diplotaxis muralis var. pseudoviminea

Diplotaxis muralis var. pseudoviminea, commonly known as the African dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing annual herb with slender, branching stems and bright yellow flowers. The plant produces small, oval-shaped seeds that are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
