Dasylepis leptophylla

Dasylepis leptophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf chamaedorea, is a species of palm native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, elongated leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often found in forest understories. Its foliage is evergreen, and it produces small, clustered flowers followed by edible fruits.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated as a houseplant.
