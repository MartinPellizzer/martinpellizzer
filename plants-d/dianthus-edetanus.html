Dianthus edetanus

Dianthus edetanus, commonly known as the Edetan carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant features upright, branching stems covered in narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically arranged in clusters and have five petals with frayed edges.

Dianthus edetanus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and fragrance.
