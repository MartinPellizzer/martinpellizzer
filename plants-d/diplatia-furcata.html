Diplatia furcata

Diplatia furcata, commonly known as the forked leaf fern, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that split into two distinct lobes, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as under forest canopies. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
