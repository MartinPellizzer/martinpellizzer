Dendrocalamus tsiangii f. striatus

Dendrocalamus tsiangii f. striatus, commonly known as the striated bamboo, is a variant of the giant bamboo species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive longitudinal stripes on the culms, which give it a unique appearance. The plant is a perennial grass that can grow to considerable heights, often reaching several meters in height. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soils and ample sunlight.

This bamboo is valued for its strong, flexible stalks, which are used in construction and handicrafts.
