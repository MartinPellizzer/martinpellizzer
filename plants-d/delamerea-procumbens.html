Delamerea procumbens

Delamerea procumbens, commonly known as the Cape fennel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a low-growing, herbaceous perennial that typically forms a dense mat of foliage. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are aromatic and have a mild, spicy scent, often used in traditional medicine.

Delamerea procumbens thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.
