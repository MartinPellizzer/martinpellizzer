Doellingeria scabra

Doellingeria scabra, commonly known as the roughleaf waterweed, is a perennial aquatic plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, ribbon-like leaves that are covered in fine, rough textures. The plant typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds and streams. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish-white in color.

Doellingeria scabra plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
