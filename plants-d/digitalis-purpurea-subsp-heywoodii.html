Digitalis purpurea subsp. heywoodii

Digitalis purpurea subsp.

heywoodii, commonly known as the Scottish foxglove, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the British Isles. It features tall, upright stems that bear tubular flowers in shades of purple and pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are deeply lobed, and it produces a distinctive seed capsule that splits open to release its seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial shade to full sun.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
