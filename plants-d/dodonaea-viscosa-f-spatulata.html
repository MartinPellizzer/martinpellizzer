Dodonaea viscosa f. spatulata

Dodonaea viscosa f. spatulata, commonly known as the broad-leaved hop bush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its large, ovate to spatulate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in spikes, which are followed by woody, spherical fruits. It is adapted to withstand drought and poor soil conditions, making it a hardy plant in xerophytic environments.

The species is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in challenging climates.
