Dactyloctenium falcatum

Dactyloctenium falcatum, commonly known as the flatleaf grass, is a warm-season perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its flat, blade-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread aggressively, making it both a weed and a potential forage crop.

Dactyloctenium falcatum is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion in certain environments.
