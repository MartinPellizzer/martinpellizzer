Drypis spinosa subsp. spinosa

Drypis spinosa subsp.

spinosa, commonly known as the spiny drypis, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its spiny stems and small, oval-shaped leaves with a rough texture.

The plant produces yellow flowers in early summer, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens.
