Decalepis salicifolia

Decalepis salicifolia, commonly known as the willow-leaved daisychair, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features narrow, willow-like leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.

Decalepis salicifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
