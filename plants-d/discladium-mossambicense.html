Discladium mossambicense

Discladium mossambicense, commonly known as the African rock moss, is a species of liverwort that thrives in rocky and arid environments.

It is characterized by its small, closely packed leaves that form dense mats on stone surfaces. The plant lacks true roots and relies on rhizoids for anchorage and nutrient absorption. It plays a vital role in stabilizing soil in harsh, dry habitats.

Discladium mossambicense is often found in regions with low rainfall and high sunlight exposure.
