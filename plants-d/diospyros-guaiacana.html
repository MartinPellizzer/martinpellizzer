Diospyros guaiacana

Diospyros guaiacana, commonly known as the Brazilian persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes ebony and persimmon trees. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically greenish-yellow in color, and it produces small, fleshy fruits that are edible.

The wood of Diospyros guaiacana is hard and durable, making it valuable for crafting and traditional uses.
