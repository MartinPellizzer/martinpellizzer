Dipteracanthus beddomei

Dipteracanthus beddomei, commonly known as the beddome's dipterocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family, which is characterized by its large, often buttressed roots and heavy timber. The plant typically grows in lowland rainforests and is known for its tall, straight trunk and broad, lance-shaped leaves. It produces small, clustered flowers that are often yellow or greenish-yellow, followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds.

This species plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
