Disporum majus

Disporum majus, commonly known as the western skunk cabbage, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the Araceae family, which is characterized by its unique inflorescence structure consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant features a stout, fleshy rhizome and produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that are often hidden beneath its large, mottled leaves.

Disporum majus thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands, and it plays a role in supporting local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and animals.
