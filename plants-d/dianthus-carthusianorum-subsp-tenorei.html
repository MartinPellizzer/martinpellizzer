Dianthus carthusianorum subsp. tenorei

Dianthus carthusianorum subsp.

tenorei, commonly known as the Tuscan carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, double-petaled flowers that come in shades of pink, red, and white.

The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing clusters of showy flowers that attract pollinators. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value has made it a popular choice in horticulture, particularly in southern Europe.
