Delphinium basalticum

Delphinium basalticum, commonly known as the basalt larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems that can reach heights of up to six feet. The plant produces clusters of blue to purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough, hairy texture.

Delphinium basalticum is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine and rocky habitats.
