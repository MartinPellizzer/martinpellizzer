Dichanthelium meridionale var. albemarlense

Dichanthelium meridionale var. albemarlense, commonly known as the Whiteleaf Panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like blades that are often tinged with white or silver on the underside, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in open woodlands and prairies. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

This variety is notable for its ornamental appeal and ecological value in supporting local wildlife.
