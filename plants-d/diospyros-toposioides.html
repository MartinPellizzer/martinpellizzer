Diospyros toposioides

Diospyros toposioides, commonly known as the African persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and broad, ovate leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into edible, orange-red fruits with a sweet, astringent flavor. It is often found in moist woodlands and is valued for its timber and fruit in local communities.

The plant also has traditional medicinal uses in some African cultures.
