Daphne escalerae

Daphne escalerae, commonly known as the Escalera daphne, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 meter, featuring dense, aromatic foliage. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white during the spring. Its leaves are dark green and glossy, and it is known for its strong, spicy scent.

Daphne escalerae is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
