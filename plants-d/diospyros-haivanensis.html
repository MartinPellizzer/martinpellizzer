Diospyros haivanensis

Diospyros haivanensis, commonly known as the Persian persimmon, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by orange to red berries that are edible when fully mature. Its fruit is valued for its sweet, astringent flavor and is often consumed fresh or used in traditional remedies.

The species is also notable for its ornamental value and adaptability to arid environments.
