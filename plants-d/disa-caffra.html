Disa caffra

Disa caffra, commonly known as the Cape orchid, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with a distinctive lip that is often darker in color.

Disa caffra is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
