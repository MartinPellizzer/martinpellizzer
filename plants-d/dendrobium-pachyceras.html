Dendrobium pachyceras

Dendrobium pachyceras, commonly known as the thick-ribbed orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is an epiphytic orchid that thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing on trees or rocks. The plant features long, wiry stems that can reach several feet in length, with clustered, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically white or pale yellow, with a distinctive, spurred lip.

Dendrobium pachyceras is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its unique appearance and fragrance.
