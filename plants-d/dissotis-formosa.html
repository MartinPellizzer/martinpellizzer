Dissotis formosa

Dissotis formosa, commonly known as the flame flower, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces striking, bright red or orange flowers. The plant has slender, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often grown as a ornamental plant.

The flowers are typically borne in clusters and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
