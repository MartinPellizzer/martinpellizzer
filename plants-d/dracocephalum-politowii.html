Dracocephalum politowii

Dracocephalum politowii, commonly known as the Kashmir chamomile, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer, and its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their antispasmodic properties. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its medicinal value and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and protected species in its native habitat.
