Dianthus mauritii

Dianthus mauritii, commonly known as the Persian carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, double-layered flowers that come in a range of colors, including pink, red, and white. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and features linear, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

The flowers are often used in horticulture for their ornamental value and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
