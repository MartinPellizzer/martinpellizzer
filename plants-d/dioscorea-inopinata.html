Dioscorea inopinata

Dioscorea inopinata, commonly known as the Mexican yam, is a species of flowering plant in the family Dioscoreaceae.

It is native to Mexico and Central America, where it thrives in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary part used for culinary and medicinal purposes. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its flowers are small and clustered in spikes.

Dioscorea inopinata is valued for its nutritional content and traditional use in herbal medicine.
