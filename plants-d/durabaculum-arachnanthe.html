Durabaculum arachnanthe

Durabaculum arachnanthe, commonly known as the spider-thorn acacia, is a shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, spiny stems and small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers. The plant produces flat, bean-like pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It has adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments through deep root systems and water-storing tissues.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it an important species in its native habitat.
