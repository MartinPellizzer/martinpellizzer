Deutzia scabra var. punctata

Deutzia scabra var. punctata, commonly known as the rough deutzia, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features slender, arching branches and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early summer. The leaves are ovate with a rough, sandpapery texture, giving the plant its common name. It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and thrives in partial to full sun.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
