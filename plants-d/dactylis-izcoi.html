Dactylis izcoi

Dactylis izcoi, commonly known as the Iberian goatgrass, is a perennial grass native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as pastures and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color.

Dactylis izcoi is adapted to dry and semi-arid conditions, making it a resilient species in Mediterranean climates.
