Digitaria sabulicola

Digitaria sabulicola, commonly known as sand digitgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the warmer regions of the United States.

It thrives in sandy and well-drained soils, often found in coastal dunes and open habitats. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It is adapted to tolerate salt and wind, making it a common component of coastal ecosystems.

Its ecological role includes stabilizing sand dunes and providing forage for wildlife.
