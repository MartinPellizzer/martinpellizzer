Dipterocarpus retusus subsp. tonkinensis

Dipterocarpus retusus subsp.

tonkinensis, commonly known as the Tonkin dipterocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia. It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its large, buttressed roots and thick, flaky bark.

The tree produces heavy, woody fruits that contain winged seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, lowland forests and is an important component of the region's biodiversity.

Its timber is valued for its durability and is used in construction and furniture-making.
