Dipterocarpus grandifolius

Dipterocarpus grandifolius, commonly known as the large-leaf dipterocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its large, glossy leaves and heavy, aromatic wood. The plant thrives in humid, lowland forests and plays a significant role in the ecosystem as a keystone species. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing seeds.

Due to its ecological importance and valuable timber, it is also a species of conservation concern.
