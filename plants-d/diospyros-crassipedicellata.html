Diospyros crassipedicellata

Diospyros crassipedicellata, commonly known as the African persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange to red, fleshy berries that are edible when ripe.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine and as a source of timber.
