Dupatya brasiliensis

Dupatya brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the rainforests of Brazil.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, tubular flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, edible berries that are often consumed by local wildlife. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
