Dryopteris gardneriana

Dryopteris gardneriana, commonly known as Gardner's wood fern, is a species of fern native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically arching or drooping. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodlands and along stream banks. Its rhizomes are thick and creeping, allowing it to spread and form dense colonies.

The spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, contributing to its reproductive cycle.
