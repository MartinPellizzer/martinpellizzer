Dioscorea pentadactyla

Dioscorea pentadactyla, commonly known as the African yam bean, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are used as a food source. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by oval-shaped fruits that contain several seeds. Its leaves are heart-shaped with five lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its nutritional value.
