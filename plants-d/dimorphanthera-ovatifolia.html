Dimorphanthera ovatifolia

Dimorphanthera ovatifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its ovate, broad leaves and intricate floral structure. The plant typically grows in shady, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are usually pale green to white, with a distinctive labellum that aids in pollination.

Dimorphanthera ovatifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitats.
