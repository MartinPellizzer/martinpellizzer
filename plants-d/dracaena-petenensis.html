Dracaena petenensis

Dracaena petenensis, commonly known as the Mexican flame tree, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, palm-like leaves that are typically a deep green with a reddish tint. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers in panicles, which bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Dracaena petenensis is also valued for its ability to tolerate a range of soil types and its relatively low maintenance requirements.
