Dioscorea lasseriana

Dioscorea lasseriana, commonly known as the Lasser's yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary part used for culinary and medicinal purposes. The plant has palmate leaves and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into capsules containing numerous seeds.

Its growth habit and morphological features make it well adapted to humid, shaded environments in its native habitat.
