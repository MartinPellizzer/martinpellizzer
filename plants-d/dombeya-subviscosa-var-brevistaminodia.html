Dombeya subviscosa var. brevistaminodia

Dombeya subviscosa var. brevistaminodia, commonly known as the woolly dombeya, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and showy, clustered flowers that range from white to pink. The plant typically grows in coastal and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained conditions. Its flowers bloom in spring and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The variety is distinguished by its shorter, more clustered stamens compared to other varieties of Dombeya.
