Draba lonchocarpa var. exigua

Draba lonchocarpa var. exigua, commonly known as the slender bell-cup winter cress, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It produces small, white or yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. The plant has a rosette of basal leaves that are narrow and often covered in fine hairs. Its seeds are enclosed in a distinctive, bell-shaped capsule, which splits open to release the seeds.

This species is adapted to cold, dry environments and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils.
