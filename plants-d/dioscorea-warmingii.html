Dioscorea warmingii

Dioscorea warmingii, commonly known as the Chinese yam, is a perennial vine native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and tuberous roots. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its tubers are valued for their medicinal properties and are used in traditional Chinese medicine.

The botanical features of Dioscorea warmingii make it an important species in both horticulture and pharmacology.
