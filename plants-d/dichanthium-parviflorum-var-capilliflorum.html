Dichanthium parviflorum var. capilliflorum

Dichanthium parviflorum var. capilliflorum, commonly known as the slenderflower panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with narrow, grass-like leaves and small, pale green flowers that appear in dense clusters. The plant has a robust root system that allows it to thrive in poor soils and withstand drought conditions. It is often found in disturbed areas, such as roadsides and fields, and is valued for its erosion control capabilities.

Its botanical structure makes it well-adapted to harsh environments, contributing to its ecological significance in its native range.
