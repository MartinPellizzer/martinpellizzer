Dendrocalamus latiflorus

Dendrocalamus latiflorus, commonly known as the broad-flowered bamboo, is a tall, fast-growing grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its robust, hollow stems that can reach heights of up to 20 meters. The plant produces large, showy inflorescences with wide, pale green to yellowish flowers, which are typical of its genus. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of fiber.

Dendrocalamus latiflorus plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
