Didiscus tuberculatus

Didiscus tuberculatus, commonly known as the rough didiscus, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, rough-textured leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or savanna habitats.
