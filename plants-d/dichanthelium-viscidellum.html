Dichanthelium viscidellum

Dichanthelium viscidellum, commonly known as stickygrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist habitats, showcasing its adaptability to humid environments. The plant is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine, sticky hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a spikelet cluster, which produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Dichanthelium viscidellum plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
