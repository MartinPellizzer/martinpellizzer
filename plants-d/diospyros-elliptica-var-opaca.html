Diospyros elliptica var. opaca

Diospyros elliptica var. opaca, commonly known as the African persimmon, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and southern Africa.

It features elliptical, leathery leaves that are dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into orange-red, fleshy berries. The berries are edible when fully ripe and have a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit in warm climates.
