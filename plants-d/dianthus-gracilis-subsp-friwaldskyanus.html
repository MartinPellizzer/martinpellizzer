Dianthus gracilis subsp. friwaldskyanus

Dianthus gracilis subsp.

friwaldskyanus, commonly known as the Friwaldskyanus pink, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, pink or magenta flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its delicate appearance and compact growth habit, often reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the flowers typically bloom in late spring to early summer.

This plant prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and alpine settings.
