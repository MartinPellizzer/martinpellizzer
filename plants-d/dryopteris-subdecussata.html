Dryopteris subdecussata

Dryopteris subdecussata, commonly known as the zigzag water fern, is a species of fern native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, often zigzag-shaped fronds that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are compound, with a feathery appearance, and they are covered in small, scale-like structures.

Dryopteris subdecussata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
