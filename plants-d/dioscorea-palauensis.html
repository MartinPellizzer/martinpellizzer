Dioscorea palauensis

Dioscorea palauensis, commonly known as the Palau yam, is a climbing vine native to the Caroline Islands.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible tuberous roots. Its growth habit and morphology are adapted to the tropical rainforest environment of its native habitat.

The tubers are an important food source in local communities.
