Dracunculus

Dracunculus, commonly known as the honey garlic or death flower, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Alstroemeriaceae.

It is characterized by its large, showy, violet or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has tuberous roots and is native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are often arranged in a spike-like cluster.

Dracunculus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a distinctive, garlic-like scent.
