Donaldia ulmifolia

Donaldia ulmifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its striking silver-green foliage and bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, giving them a silvery appearance.

The flowers, which are usually pale blue or white, bloom in late spring to early summer and attract various pollinators.
