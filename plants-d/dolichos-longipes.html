Dolichos longipes

Dolichos longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed cowpea, is a climbing legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, twining stems that can reach several meters in length, supporting its pinnate leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces lens-shaped pods that mature to a pale green or yellowish color, containing edible seeds. It is valued for its nutritional content and is often cultivated for both food and forage.

Its botanical characteristics make it well-suited for warm climates and agricultural systems.
