Desmodium paniculatum

Desmodium paniculatum, commonly known as hairy desmodium, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is often found in open woodland and grassland habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces small, round seeds that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution.

Desmodium paniculatum is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in agricultural settings.
