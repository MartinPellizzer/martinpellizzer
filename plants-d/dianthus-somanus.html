Dianthus somanus

Dianthus somanus, commonly known as the Somalian carnation, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Somalia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and low rainfall. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces small, rounded seed pods that contain numerous seeds.

Dianthus somanus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in drought-tolerant gardens.
