Dolichos lualabensis subsp. lualabensis

Dolichos lualabensis subsp.

lualabensis, commonly known as the Lualab bean, is a leguminous plant native to East Africa. It is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves, with each leaflet being ovate to elliptic in shape.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by elongated, slender pods containing kidney-shaped seeds. It is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in open woodland and grassland habitats.

Due to its nutritional value, it is sometimes cultivated as a food crop in its native region.
