Dioscorea herbert-smithii

Dioscorea herbert-smithii, commonly known as Herbert-Smith's yam, is a member of the Dioscoreaceae family and is native to southern Africa.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary structures used for storage and propagation. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by cylindrical, yellowish fruits. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture, and it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
