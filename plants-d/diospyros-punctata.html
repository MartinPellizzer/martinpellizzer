Diospyros punctata

Diospyros punctata, commonly known as the dotted persimmon, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish-brown bark and opposite, ovate leaves with a rough, textured surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange to red, fleshy fruits that are edible when fully ripe. Its fruit contains a single seed enclosed in a membranous sac, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible produce.

Diospyros punctata is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and thrives in well-drained soils.
