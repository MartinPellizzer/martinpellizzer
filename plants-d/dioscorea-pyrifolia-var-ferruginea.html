Dioscorea pyrifolia var. ferruginea

Dioscorea pyrifolia var. ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty-seeded yam, is a variant of the yam family native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, rugged, and often hairy stems, which can grow up to several meters in height. The leaves are typically large, heart-shaped, and have a rough texture, while the flowers are small and usually yellowish-green in color. The fruit is a hairy, spiny capsule containing numerous angular, yellowish seeds.

This plant is valued for its tuberous roots, which are used in traditional medicine and as a food source in some cultures.
