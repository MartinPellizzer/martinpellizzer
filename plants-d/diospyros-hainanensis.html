Diospyros hainanensis

Diospyros hainanensis, commonly known as Hainan persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to the island of Hainan in China.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by edible, orange-red berries that are rich in nutrients. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in lowland forests and open woodlands.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruit, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and for local consumption.
