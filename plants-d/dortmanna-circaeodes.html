Dortmanna circaeodes

Dortmanna circaeodes, commonly known as the narrow-leafed dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in grassy areas and rocky soils. The plant features basal leaves that are narrow and lance-shaped, with a hairy surface. Its yellow flowers are borne in a single head, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by composite flower heads and a wide distribution across various habitats.
