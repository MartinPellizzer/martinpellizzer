Darwiniera bergoldii

Darwiniera bergoldii, commonly known as the Bergold's darwinia, is a flowering plant native to South America, specifically Argentina.

It belongs to the plantain family, Plantaginaceae, and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and striking, tubular flowers. The plant is known for its vibrant, often purple or pinkish blooms that appear in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Darwiniera bergoldii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
