Dianthus armeria f. albiviridis

Dianthus armeria f. albiviridis, commonly known as the white-flowered sweet William, is a cultivar of the common pink (Dianthus armeria).

It is characterized by its pale white or cream-colored flowers, which contrast with its dark green foliage. The plant is a low-growing perennial that thrives in full sun and well-drained soil. It produces clustered flowers that bloom in spring and early summer.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
