Diamorpha pusilla

Diamorpha pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf morning glory, is a small, herbaceous vine native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces delicate, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple. The plant blooms in late spring to early summer and is often found in moist, wooded areas. It has a climbing habit and can be invasive if not controlled.

Botanically, it is characterized by its simple leaves and showy, fragrant flowers.
