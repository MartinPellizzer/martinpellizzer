Dioscorea gallegosi

Dioscorea gallegosi, commonly known as the Gallegos yam, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary part used for culinary and medicinal purposes. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers and later develops capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its growth habit involves twining around supports, and it thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil.

The tubers are often harvested after several months of growth and are valued for their nutritional content and traditional uses.
