Dischistocalyx walkeri

Dischistocalyx walkeri, commonly known as the African snowball tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded clusters of white, fluffy flowers that resemble snowballs, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has simple, opposite, elliptical leaves that are dark green and glossy on top, with a lighter underside. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to provide habitat for local wildlife.
