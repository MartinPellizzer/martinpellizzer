Drymophloeus puniceus

Drymophloeus puniceus, commonly known as the red-flowered screw-pine, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spirally arranged leaves and small, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant produces woody fruits that contain a single seed, and it is often found growing near coastal areas or in disturbed soils.

Drymophloeus puniceus plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
