Daphne crassifolia

Daphne crassifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaf daphne, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are typically red or orange in color. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a container plant.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is often cultivated in horticultural settings.
