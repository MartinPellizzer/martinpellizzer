Dactylorhiza alpestris

Dactylorhiza alpestris, commonly known as the alpine orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the mountainous regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of pale to deep pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or open, sunny habitats at high elevations. It has a rhizomatous root system and produces showy, spurred orchid flowers that bloom in late summer.

Dactylorhiza alpestris is adapted to cold climates and is often found in alpine meadows and tundra environments.
