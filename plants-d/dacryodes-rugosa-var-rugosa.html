Dacryodes rugosa var. rugosa

Dacryodes rugosa var. rugosa, commonly known as the rough-skin tallow tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and small, elliptical leaves with a leathery texture. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by oval, woody fruits containing a single seed. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The tree is valued for its durable wood and has traditional uses in various cultures.
