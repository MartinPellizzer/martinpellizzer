Damnacanthus tsaii

Damnacanthus tsaii, commonly known as the Chinese flamevine, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is native to China and is characterized by its striking red-orange tubular flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces long, slender pods that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions.
