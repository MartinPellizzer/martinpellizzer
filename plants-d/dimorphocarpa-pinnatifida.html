Dimorphocarpa pinnatifida

Dimorphocarpa pinnatifida, commonly known as the narrow-leafed desert fig, is a shrub native to arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive two-lobed leaves, which are pinnately divided and adapted to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or greenish in color. Its fruit, a small, fleshy drupe, is edible and serves as a food source for various animals.

Dimorphocarpa pinnatifida is well-suited to harsh environments and plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils.
