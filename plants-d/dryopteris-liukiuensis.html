Dryopteris liukiuensis

Dryopteris liukiuensis, commonly known as the Okinawan wood fern, is a species of fern native to the Ryukyu Islands of Japan.

It is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that are typically bipinnate, giving it a lacy appearance. The plant prefers moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds in specialized structures called sorus.

This fern is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with the right conditions.
