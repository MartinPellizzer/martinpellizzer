Dactylis polygama f. ciliata

Dactylis polygama f. ciliata, commonly known as the hairy meadow grass, is a perennial grass species characterized by its tufted growth habit and fine, wiry leaves.

It typically grows in temperate regions and is often found in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils. The plant features dense, compact inflorescences that produce small, spike-like clusters of flowers. Its leaves are covered with fine hairs, giving it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance.

This variety is valued for its adaptability and is sometimes used in forage agriculture.
