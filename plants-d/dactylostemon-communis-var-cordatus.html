Dactylostemon communis var. cordatus

Dactylostemon communis var. cordatus, commonly known as the heart-shaped lily, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, white to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and prefers sandy, well-drained soils. It is a perennial herb that thrives in warm, sunny conditions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and botanical collections.
