Disa culveri

Disa culveri, commonly known as the Culver's disa, is a species of orchid native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb with a rosette of fleshy, dark green leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. The plant produces striking, spurred flowers in shades of pink and white, typically during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
