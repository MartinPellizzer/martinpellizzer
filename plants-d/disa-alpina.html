Disa alpina

Disa alpina, commonly known as the Alpine Disa, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils at high altitudes, often in alpine or subalpine environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, delicate blooms during the summer months.

Disa alpina is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
