Daphnopsis mollis

Daphnopsis mollis, commonly known as the velvetleaf sedge, is a perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage with a soft, velvety texture. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its flowering stems produce small, inconspicuous flowers that are often clustered in spikes.

Daphnopsis mollis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
