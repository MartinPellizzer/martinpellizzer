Draba longirostra f. levieri

Draba longirostra f. levieri, commonly known as the long-beaked rockcress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with basal leaves and a flowering stem that bears small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically thrives in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually linear and have a distinctive elongated shape, while the flowers are arranged in a compact cluster.

This subspecies is often found in mountainous regions of Europe and is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological niche.
