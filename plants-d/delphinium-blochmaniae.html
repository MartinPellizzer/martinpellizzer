Delphinium blochmaniae

Delphinium blochmaniae, commonly known as the Bolivian larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high Andes of Bolivia.

It features tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, adorned with striking blue to violet flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. Its flowers are borne in racemes and are characterized by their spurred petals, a hallmark of the Delphinium genus.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in alpine gardens due to its hardy nature.
