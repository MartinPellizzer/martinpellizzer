Dianthus serratus subsp. paui

Dianthus serratus subsp.

paui, commonly known as the serrated carnation, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer.

The leaves are serrated and often covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated for its vibrant blooms and hardy nature.
