Dischidia papuana

Dischidia papuana, commonly known as the Papuan Spider Plant, is a climbing or trailing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its fleshy, opposite leaves that are often covered in a thick, waxy cuticle. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically borne in clusters, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous tiny seeds. It is a popular ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ease of cultivation.

Dischidia papuana thrives in humid, warm environments and is often used in hanging baskets or as a trailing plant in greenhouses.
