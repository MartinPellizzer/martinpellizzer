Dichopogon sieberianus

Dichopogon sieberianus, commonly known as the Sieber's dichopogon, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, greenish-white flowers. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
