Digitaria horizontalis var. porrhantha

Digitaria horizontalis var. porrhantha, commonly known as the flatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its flat, grass-like leaves and dense, spreading growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny grains, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetland environments.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems and a subject of ecological study.
