Dendrocalamus prainiana

Dendrocalamus prainiana, commonly known as the Prain's bamboo, is a tall, fast-growing grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Bambusoideae subfamily and is characterized by its robust, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 20 meters. The plant has narrow, linear leaves with a smooth surface and is often found in moist, tropical lowland areas. It plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and contributing to soil stabilization.

Due to its rapid growth, it is also valued for its potential in sustainable forestry and construction materials.
