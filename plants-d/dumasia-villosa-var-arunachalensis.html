Dumasia villosa var. arunachalensis

Dumasia villosa var. arunachalensis, commonly known as the Arunachal Himalayan bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Eastern Himalayas.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, lanceolate leaves and bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments at elevations between 2,500 and 4,000 meters. Its flowers are usually blue or violet, with five lobes, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky areas.
