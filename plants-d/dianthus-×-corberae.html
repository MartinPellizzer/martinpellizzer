Dianthus × corberae

Dianthus × corberae, commonly known as the double carnation, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Dianthus caryophyllus and Dianthus chinensis.

It is characterized by its dense, double flowers that resemble small, layered blossoms. The plant typically grows to a height of 20–30 cm, featuring upright stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden borders and containers.

Its flowers come in a variety of colors, including red, pink, white, and purple, adding vibrant visual appeal to gardens.
