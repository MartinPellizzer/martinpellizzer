Drymophloeus bifidus

Drymophloeus bifidus, commonly known as the two-lobed tamarind, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive pod-like fruits. The plant is notable for its ability to thrive in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests. Its seeds are encased in a hard, woody pod, which splits open to release the seeds.

The tree is also valued for its timber and has traditional uses in local medicine.
