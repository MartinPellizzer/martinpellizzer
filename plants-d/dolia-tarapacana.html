Dolia tarapacana

Dolia tarapacana, commonly known as the Andean bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes in South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a compact, bushy growth habit with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in cool, dry climates and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a subject of interest for botanists and horticulturists.
