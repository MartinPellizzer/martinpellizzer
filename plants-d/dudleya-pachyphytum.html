Dudleya pachyphytum

Dudleya pachyphytum, commonly known as the thick-leaved stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the rocky habitats of Baja California.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the spring. Its rosette growth form is typical of many succulents, providing structural support and efficient water retention.

Dudleya pachyphytum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and low maintenance requirements.
