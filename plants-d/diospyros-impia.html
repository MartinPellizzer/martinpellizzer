Diospyros impia

Diospyros impia, commonly known as the Java persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, clustered flowers. The tree produces small, round fruits that turn from green to orange-red when mature. The fruit is edible, with a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor, and is often consumed fresh or used in traditional remedies.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is valued for its ornamental and culinary uses.
