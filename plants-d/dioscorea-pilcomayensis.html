Dioscorea pilcomayensis

Dioscorea pilcomayensis, commonly known as the Pilcomayo yam, is a tuberous plant native to South America, particularly found in Argentina and Bolivia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its starchy, edible tubers that are an important food source for local communities. The plant typically grows in moist, tropical or subtropical climates and thrives in well-drained soils. Its tubers can be harvested after several months of growth, and they are often used in traditional cuisines.

The botanical features of D. pilcomayensis include its climbing habit, heart-shaped leaves, and small, inconspicuous flowers.
