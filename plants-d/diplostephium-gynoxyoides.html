Diplostephium gynoxyoides

Diplostephium gynoxyoides, commonly known as the Mexican senna, is a shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features bipinnate leaves with small, oval leaflets, and it produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. It is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.

The plant has been used traditionally in herbal medicine for its potential health benefits.
