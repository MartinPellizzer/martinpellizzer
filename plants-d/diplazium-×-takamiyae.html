Diplazium × takamiyae

Diplazium × takamiyae, commonly known as the hybrid staghorn fern, is a fern species resulting from the cross between Diplazium esculentum and Diplazium soriolosum.

It is characterized by its large, leathery fronds that often have a distinctive, horn-like shape, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees or other structures in tropical and subtropical regions. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, and it reproduces through spores rather than seeds.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded, humid environments.
