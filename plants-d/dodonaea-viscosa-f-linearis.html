Dodonaea viscosa f. linearis

Dodonaea viscosa f. linearis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hop bush, is a shrub native to Australia.

It features slender, linear leaves that are often silver-white underneath, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by woody, bead-like fruits. It is adapted to dry, coastal environments and is often found in sandy soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in harsh conditions.
