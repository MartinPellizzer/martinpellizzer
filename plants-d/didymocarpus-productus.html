Didymocarpus productus

Didymocarpus productus, commonly known as the red-fruited pomegranate, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including regions of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and clusters of fragrant white flowers. Its fruits are small, round, and typically reddish when mature, containing several seeds.

It is often grown for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
