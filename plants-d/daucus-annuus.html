Daucus annuus

Daucus annuus, commonly known as wild carrot, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a taproot that is often hairy and has a distinct, carrot-like appearance. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers arranged in umbels, which are characteristic of the Apiaceae family. Its leaves are pinnately divided, with a feathery appearance, and the plant is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Wild carrot is also known for its aromatic qualities and has been used in traditional medicine.
