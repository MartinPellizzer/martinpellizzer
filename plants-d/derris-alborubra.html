Derris alborubra

Derris alborubra, commonly known as the white-red derris, is a climbing legume native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated pods containing small, kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Derris alborubra is notable for its alkaloid content, which has been studied for potential medicinal uses.
