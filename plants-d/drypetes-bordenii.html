Drypetes bordenii

Drypetes bordenii, commonly known as the Bornean drypetes, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, often reddish bark. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, lowland forests and is valued for its hard, durable wood.

However, it is relatively rare and faces threats from habitat loss and overharvesting.
