Doronicum cataractarum

Doronicum cataractarum, commonly known as the alpine goldcup, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It features bright yellow, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a compact, low-growing habit with hairy leaves that help it retain heat in cold environments. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in mountain meadows.

Doronicum cataractarum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in alpine ecosystems.
