Dircaea lateritia

Dircaea lateritia, commonly known as the desert lily, is a perennial flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that help it store water in harsh environments. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to full sun exposure.

Due to its resilience and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in xeriscapes and rock gardens.
