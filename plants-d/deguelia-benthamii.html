Deguelia benthamii

Deguelia benthamii, commonly known as the yellow bell, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to northeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling bells, hence its common name. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small leaflets and produces fruit that splits open to release its seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in rainforests or along riverbanks.

Deguelia benthamii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
