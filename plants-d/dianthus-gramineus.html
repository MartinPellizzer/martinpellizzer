Dianthus gramineus

Dianthus gramineus, commonly known as the grass pink, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and its striking, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, which includes other showy flowering plants.

Dianthus gramineus is often found in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to thrive in partially shaded environments.
