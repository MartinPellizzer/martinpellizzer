Dysoxylum cerebriforme

Dysoxylum cerebriforme, commonly known as the brain-shaped mangrove, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, lobed leaves that resemble the shape of the human brain. The plant thrives in saline and brackish water environments, making it a common species in mangrove ecosystems. Its bark is thick and fibrous, often used in traditional medicine.

The tree produces small, fragrant flowers followed by edible, fleshy fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife.
