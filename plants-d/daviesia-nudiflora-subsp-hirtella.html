Daviesia nudiflora subsp. hirtella

Daviesia nudiflora subsp.

hirtella, commonly known as the hairy Daviesia, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, shrub-like growth habit.

The plant produces small, yellow to orange flowers that are clustered in racemes, often appearing before the leaves in spring. Its hairy stems and leaves are distinctive features, and it thrives in dry, sandy soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
