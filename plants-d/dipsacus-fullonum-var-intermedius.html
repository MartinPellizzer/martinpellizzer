Dipsacus fullonum var. intermedius

Dipsacus fullonum var. intermedius, commonly known as the intermediate hogweed, is a biennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, hollow stems that are often hairy and can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces large, umbrella-like clusters of small white or greenish flowers, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lobed and can be quite large, with a rough texture.

The plant is part of the Apiaceae family, which includes other members like carrots and parsley, and is known for its potential to cause skin irritation.
