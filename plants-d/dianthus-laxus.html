Dianthus laxus

Dianthus laxus, commonly known as the slender campion, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like carnations and sweet William. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are pink or white, with five petals and prominent stamens, and it blooms from late spring to early summer.

Dianthus laxus is often found in alpine and coastal environments, where it adapts to harsh climatic conditions.
