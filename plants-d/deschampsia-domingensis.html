Deschampsia domingensis

Deschampsia domingensis, commonly known as the Dominican grass, is a perennial grass species native to the island of Hispaniola.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted leaves and panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It is well adapted to dry conditions and can thrive in nutrient-poor soils.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and forage for various wildlife species.
