Derris brevipes var. travancorensis

Derris brevipes var. travancorensis, commonly known as the Travancore derris, is a climbing shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae, characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces yellowish-white flowers that attract pollinators. Its roots contain rotenone, a natural insecticide used in traditional agriculture.

This variant is distinguished by its shorter stems and specific geographical distribution within Kerala.
