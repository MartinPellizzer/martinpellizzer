Dipetalon

Dipetalon, commonly known as the two-petaled plant, is a unique species characterized by its distinctive floral structure.

It features two prominent petals that are often brightly colored, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or wetlands. Its leaves are usually broad and lance-shaped, with a smooth surface that aids in water retention.

The botanical classification of Dipetalon places it within the family Ericaceae, commonly known as the heath family.
