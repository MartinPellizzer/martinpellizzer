Dianthus lateritius

Dianthus lateritius, commonly known as the rock campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its clusters of pink or red, bilaterally symmetrical flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in well-drained environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in fine hairs.

Dianthus lateritius is a hardy perennial that is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
