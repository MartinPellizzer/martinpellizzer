Dissotis derriksiana

Dissotis derriksiana, commonly known as the purple spider-orchid, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, spider-like inflorescence. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are predominantly purple or violet, with intricate, delicate structures that attract specific pollinators.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
