Diospyros squamosa

Diospyros squamosa, commonly known as the rough-shelled persimmon, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray-barked trunk and palmate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange to red berries that are edible when fully ripe. Its fruit is valued for its sweet, astringent flavor and is often used in traditional medicine.

Diospyros squamosa is also noted for its ecological importance, providing food for various wildlife species.
