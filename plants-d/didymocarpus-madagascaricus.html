Didymocarpus madagascaricus

Didymocarpus madagascaricus, commonly known as the Madagascar starfruit, is a tropical plant native to Madagascar.

The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly serrated margin.

The fruit develops from a single flower and is edible when fully ripe, though it is not as commonly consumed as other starfruit species.
