Diuris brevis

Diuris brevis, commonly known as the dwarf spider orchid, is a small, terrestrial orchid native to the southeastern coast of Australia.

It typically grows in heathlands and open forests, preferring well-drained sandy soils. The plant features a compact rosette of linear, grass-like leaves and produces small, pale green to brownish flowers with a distinctive, spider-like appearance. Its flowering period occurs during the cooler months, usually between June and September.

The species is notable for its unique morphology and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.
