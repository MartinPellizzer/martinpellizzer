Diospyros styraciformis var. sarawakana

Diospyros styraciformis var. sarawakana, commonly known as the Sarawak persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Borneo.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves and small, clustered flowers. The fruit is a yellowish-orange berry that ripens in late summer, often used in local cuisines. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional medicinal uses in indigenous communities.
