Diplotaxis crassifolia var. intricata

Diplotaxis crassifolia var. intricata, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers with a distinctive central disc. The leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, adapting to arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

This plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping due to its hardiness and low maintenance requirements.
