Dalea catatona

Dalea catatona, commonly known as the Mexican milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological importance.

Dalea catatona is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, enhancing soil fertility.
