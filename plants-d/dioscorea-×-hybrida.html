Dioscorea × hybrida

Dioscorea × hybrida, commonly known as the Chinese yam, is a hybrid species within the genus Dioscorea, which includes various types of yams.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary part used for culinary and medicinal purposes. The plant produces large, heart-shaped leaves and yellowish flowers that are typically borne in clusters. Its tubers are rich in starch and have been traditionally used in Asian cuisine and traditional medicine.

The species is valued for its adaptability to different soil types and its ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.
