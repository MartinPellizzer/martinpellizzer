Dactylorchis incarnata var. longibracteata

Dactylorchis incarnata var. longibracteata, commonly known as the long-bract orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and delicate, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and along stream banks. Its flowers are borne on slender stems and are often clustered in racemes.

This orchid is known for its subtle fragrance and is a member of the Orchidaceae family.
