Delphinastrum

Delphinastrum, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a genus of flowering plants in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

It is native to North America and features showy, blue or purple flower clusters that bloom in late summer to early fall. The plants typically have basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. Delphinastrum species are often found in moist or wet habitats such as wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

They are valued in gardens for their ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
