Diplanthera glabra

Diplanthera glabra, commonly known as the smooth flatpea, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its typical legume structure, and features small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in racemes. The plant has smooth, glabrous leaves and stems, which contribute to its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and native plant gardening.
