Dionysia

Dionysia, commonly known as the wine flower, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Gesneriaceae.

These plants are native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas, particularly in areas with high humidity and warm climates. Dionysia species are characterized by their showy, often brightly colored flowers that resemble small bells or trumpets. They typically have opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface and are often grown as ornamental plants.

The genus includes several species, such as Dionysia corymbosa, which is prized for its vibrant blooms and compact growth habit.
