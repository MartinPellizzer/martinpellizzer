Dipterocarpus confertus

Dipterocarpus confertus, commonly known as the thick-leaved dipterocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its large, leathery leaves and heavy, fragrant wood. The plant typically grows in lowland rainforests and is an important component of the forest canopy. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are pollinated by insects, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits.

The species is valued for its hard timber and plays a significant role in the ecological structure of its habitat.
