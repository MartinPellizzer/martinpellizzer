Dactylis obtusifolia

Dactylis obtusifolia, commonly known as sweet vernal grass, is a perennial grass native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and distinctive, pale green to yellowish inflorescences. The plant has narrow, flat leaves with a slightly hairy surface and produces small, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and disturbed areas.

Dactylis obtusifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used historically in herbal medicine.
