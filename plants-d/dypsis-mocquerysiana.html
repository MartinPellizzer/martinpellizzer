Dypsis mocquerysiana

Dypsis mocquerysiana, commonly known as the Madagascar palm, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar.

It features a short, stocky trunk with a crown of stiff, spiny leaves that resemble a palm. The plant has a thick, fleshy stem that stores water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. Its leaves are typically blue-gray in color, with a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Dypsis mocquerysiana is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
