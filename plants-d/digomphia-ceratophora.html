Digomphia ceratophora

Digomphia ceratophora, commonly known as the horned bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, tubular, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. The plant features a hairy stem and lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry environments.

Due to its unique appearance, it is a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
