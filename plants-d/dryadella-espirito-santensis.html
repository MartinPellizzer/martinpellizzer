Dryadella espirito-santensis

Dryadella espirito-santensis, commonly known as the Espírito Santo dryadella, is a rare shrub native to the coastal regions of Espírito Santo, Brazil.

It is characterized by its small, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant thrives in sandy soils and is adapted to coastal environments with high salinity and wind exposure. It plays a vital role in stabilizing dune ecosystems and supporting local biodiversity.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
