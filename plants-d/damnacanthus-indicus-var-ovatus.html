Damnacanthus indicus var. ovatus

Damnacanthus indicus var. ovatus, commonly known as the Indian screwbean, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, winding stems and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, bean-like pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
