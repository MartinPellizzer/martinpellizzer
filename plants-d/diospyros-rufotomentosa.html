Diospyros rufotomentosa

Diospyros rufotomentosa, commonly known as the rough-shanked persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its thick, hairy bark and broad, ovate leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into edible, orange-red berries. Its fruits are typically harvested when fully ripe and are valued for their sweet, astringent flavor.

The plant is also noted for its ecological importance, providing food for various wildlife species.
