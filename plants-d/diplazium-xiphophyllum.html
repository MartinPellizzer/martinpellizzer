Diplazium xiphophyllum

Diplazium xiphophyllum, commonly known as the sword fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its sword-shaped, serrated fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are creeping and produce both sterile and fertile fronds.

Diplazium xiphophyllum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
