Diplycosia viridiflora var. megalantha

Diplycosia viridiflora var. megalantha, commonly known as the giant green-flowered diplycosia, is a strikingly ornamental plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, making it a notable addition to tropical gardens. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence, enhancing its visual appeal. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated for its aesthetic qualities in botanical gardens and private collections.
