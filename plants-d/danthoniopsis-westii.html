Danthoniopsis westii

Danthoniopsis westii, commonly known as the West's feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and delicate, feathery appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its small, spike-like inflorescences produce lightweight seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Danthoniopsis westii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in native ecosystems.
