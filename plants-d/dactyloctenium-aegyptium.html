Dactyloctenium aegyptium

Dactyloctenium aegyptium, commonly known as Egyptian spurge, is a low-growing annual plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, egg-shaped leaves and produces yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has a fibrous root system and is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields. It is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to thrive in dry conditions.

Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its spread across various regions.
