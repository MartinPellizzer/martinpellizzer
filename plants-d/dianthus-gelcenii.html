Dianthus gelcenii

Dianthus gelcenii, commonly known as the Gelceni campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family, which includes many showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in high-altitude environments. Its flowers are usually pink or white, with five petals that are often notched or frilled.

Dianthus gelcenii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
