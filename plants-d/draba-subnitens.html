Draba subnitens

Draba subnitens, commonly known as the glaucous whitlow grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a small, herbaceous annual or biennial plant characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, glaucous leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in early spring, typically arranged in racemes. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as sandy soils and rocky areas.

Draba subnitens is native to Europe and parts of western Asia, where it is often found in alpine or arctic environments.
