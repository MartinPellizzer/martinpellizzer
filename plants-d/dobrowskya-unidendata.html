Dobrowskya unidendata

Dobrowskya unidendata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Dobrowskya unidendata is typically found in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil and full sun.
