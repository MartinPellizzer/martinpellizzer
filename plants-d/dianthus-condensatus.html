Dianthus condensatus

Dianthus condensatus, commonly known as the dense campion, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, showy flowers. The plant typically features pink or red petals that are arranged in a tight, condensed inflorescence, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal environments.

Dianthus condensatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
