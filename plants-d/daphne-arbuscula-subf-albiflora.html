Daphne arbuscula subf. albiflora

Daphne arbuscula subf.

albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered daphne, is a succulent plant native to South Africa. It features a low-growing, mat-forming structure with fleshy, cylindrical stems covered in fine, white hairs.

The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped, white flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are narrow, linear, and often tinged with blue or gray, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.
