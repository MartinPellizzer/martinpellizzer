Diospyros dumosa

Diospyros dumosa, commonly known as the Indian persimmon or wild persimmon, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange-red, fleshy fruits that are edible when ripe. Diospyros dumosa is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Its fruit is valued for its sweet flavor and is used in traditional medicine and cuisine.
