Dugaldia integrifolia

Dugaldia integrifolia, commonly known as the smoothleaf false nettle, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It is characterized by its smooth, oval-shaped leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stems. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, which are often hidden among the foliage. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Dugaldia integrifolia is typically found in the eastern United States and is part of the Onagraceae family.
