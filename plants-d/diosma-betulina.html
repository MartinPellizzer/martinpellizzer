Diosma betulina

Diosma betulina, commonly known as the birchleaf diosma, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Apiaceae, which includes many aromatic plants such as carrots and parsley. The plant is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves that resemble those of birch trees, giving it its common name. It produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family.

Diosma betulina is often found in rocky or alpine habitats and is valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
