Diacantha

Diacantha, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves and vibrant, showy flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 10-30 cm and thrives in well-draining soil with indirect sunlight. Diacantha is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.

It produces small, tubular flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white, often with a distinctive pattern of markings.
