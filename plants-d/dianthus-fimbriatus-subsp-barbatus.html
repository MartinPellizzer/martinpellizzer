Dianthus fimbriatus subsp. barbatus

Dianthus fimbriatus subsp.

barbatus, commonly known as the fringed campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its showy, pink or red flowers with fringed petals that give it its common name.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is a perennial species that produces basal rosettes of leaves and flowering stems.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine plantings.
