Drimia confertiflora

Drimia confertiflora, commonly known as the clustered dragon's tongue, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and clusters of small, white or pale blue flowers. The plant produces edible, tuberous roots that have been used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Drimia confertiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.
