Dendrobium macrophyllum var. macrophyllum

Dendrobium macrophyllum var. macrophyllum, commonly known as the large-leaf dendrobium, is a member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is an epiphytic plant that grows on trees and rocks, utilizing its aerial roots to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and orange, often arranged in racemes. Its leaves are broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in its natural habitat.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is cultivated for both horticultural and commercial purposes.
