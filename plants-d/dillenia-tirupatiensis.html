Dillenia tirupatiensis

Dillenia tirupatiensis, commonly known as the Tirupati dillenia, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the family Dilleniaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, large, and showy flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, with palmate leaves that are deeply lobed. Its flowers are usually pink to red in color, with five petals and numerous stamens, making it visually striking.

The fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds, contributing to its ecological role in the region.
