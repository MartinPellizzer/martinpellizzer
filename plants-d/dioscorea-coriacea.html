Dioscorea coriacea

Dioscorea coriacea, commonly known as rough yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its hairy, rugged stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces tuberous roots that are often used in traditional medicine and as a food source. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant, and it relies on insect pollination.

The tubers of Dioscorea coriacea are rich in starch and have been cultivated for their nutritional value in various cultures.
