Dipladenia scabra

Dipladenia scabra, commonly known as the roughleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, rough hairs. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and are typically borne in clusters. Dipladenia scabra thrives in warm, sunny conditions and is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its vibrant blooms.

It is relatively drought-tolerant once established, making it a popular choice for gardeners in mild climates.
