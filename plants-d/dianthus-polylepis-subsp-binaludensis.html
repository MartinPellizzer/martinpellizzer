Dianthus polylepis subsp. binaludensis

Dianthus polylepis subsp.

binaludensis, commonly known as the Binalud carnation, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Iran. It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit.

The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers with five petals, typical of the genus Dianthus. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, arid climates.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
