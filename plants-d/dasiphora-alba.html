Dasiphora alba

Dasiphora alba, commonly known as white prairie clover, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its bright white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in open areas such as prairies and disturbed soils, thriving in full sun. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, pod-like fruits.

Dasiphora alba is valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and as a nectar source for pollinators.
