Danae androgyna

Danae androgyna, commonly known as the Androgynous Danae, is a rare and striking member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which features both male and female reproductive organs, making it functionally hermaphroditic. The plant produces vivid, tubular flowers that range in color from deep red to orange, often with contrasting blue or purple markings. Native to the tropical regions of South America, it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in specialized botanical gardens.
