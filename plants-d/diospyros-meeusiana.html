Diospyros meeusiana

Diospyros meeusiana, commonly known as the Meeus's persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other well-known species like the ebony and persimmon trees. The plant is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a smooth, glossy surface. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are followed by edible, orange-red berries that are a source of food for various wildlife.

The tree is also valued for its hard, heavy wood, which is used in traditional crafts and furniture making.
