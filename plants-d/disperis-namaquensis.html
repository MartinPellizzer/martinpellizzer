Disperis namaquensis

Disperis namaquensis, commonly known as the Namaqua daisy, is a perennial herb native to the semi-arid regions of South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth habit. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in a dense cluster, typically blooming from late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for rocky or sandy environments.

Disperis namaquensis is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and resilience.
