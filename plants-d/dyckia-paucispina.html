Dyckia paucispina

Dyckia paucispina, commonly known as the narrow-leaved dyckia, is a perennial bromeliad native to Brazil.

It features compact, rosette-shaped foliage that is typically narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-gray hue. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences bearing small, white to pale pink flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light.

Dyckia paucispina is drought-tolerant and suitable for rock gardens or as a container plant.
