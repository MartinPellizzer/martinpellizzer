Desmodium lavanduliflorum

Desmodium lavanduliflorum, commonly known as lavenderflower burrweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that resemble lavender blooms. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, burr-like seed pods that aid in seed dispersal.

Desmodium lavanduliflorum is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which benefits soil fertility in ecological settings.
