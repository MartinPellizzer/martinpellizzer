Dianthus juniperinus subsp. idaeus

Dianthus juniperinus subsp.

idaeus, commonly known as the Sierra dianthus, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a low-growing perennial with dense, compact foliage and produces clusters of showy, pink or red flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments. Its botanical features include small, linear leaves and a short, woody stem.

The Sierra dianthus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens and alpine settings.
