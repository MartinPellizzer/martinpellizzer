Dianthus woroschilovii

Dianthus woroschilovii, commonly known as the Woroschilov's campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pinkish-red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, while its stems are upright and branching.

Dianthus woroschilovii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
