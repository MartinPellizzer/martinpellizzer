Dimorphocalyx ramiflorus

Dimorphocalyx ramiflorus, commonly known as the flame tree, is a striking tropical plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from red to orange, often resembling flames. The plant has compound leaves with elongated leaflets and produces fruit that is a woody capsule containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Dimorphocalyx ramiflorus plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
