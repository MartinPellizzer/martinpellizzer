Dolichos lagopus

Dolichos lagopus, commonly known as the Indian cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to warm climates.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces elongated, pod-like fruits that contain several edible seeds. It is often cultivated for its nutritional value, as the seeds are rich in protein and can be used in various culinary preparations.

Additionally, Dolichos lagopus plays a role in nitrogen fixation, benefiting soil fertility in agricultural settings.
