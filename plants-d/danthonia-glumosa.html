Danthonia glumosa

Danthonia glumosa, commonly known as slender needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairless and slightly folded. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are small, spikelike structures.

Danthonia glumosa plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
