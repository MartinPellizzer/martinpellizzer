Dioscorea lindmanii

Dioscorea lindmanii, commonly known as the Lindman's yam, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary vegetative structures. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers and oval-shaped fruits that contain numerous seeds. Its tubers are often used in traditional medicine and have been studied for their potential medicinal properties.

The plant thrives in humid, tropical environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
