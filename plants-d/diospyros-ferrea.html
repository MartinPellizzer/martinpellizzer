Diospyros ferrea

Diospyros ferrea, commonly known as ironwood or Brazilian ebony, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, dark-colored wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptic to ovate in shape. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by small, round fruits.

Diospyros ferrea is often found in moist lowland forests and is known for its slow growth and long lifespan.
