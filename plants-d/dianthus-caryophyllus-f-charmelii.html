Dianthus caryophyllus f. charmelii

Dianthus caryophyllus f. charmelii, commonly known as the Charmerii carnation, is a cultivated form of the carnation plant.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, double flowers that come in a range of colors. The foliage is typically dark green and slightly hairy, which helps in distinguishing it from other varieties. This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often used in gardens and floral arrangements.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for both amateur and professional gardeners.
