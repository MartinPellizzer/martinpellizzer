Digitaria cayoensis

Digitaria cayoensis, commonly known as Cayo grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to the Caribbean region, particularly found in the Cayman Islands and parts of Central America. The plant typically grows in moist, tropical environments such as wetlands and coastal areas. It has slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves.

Its small, inconspicuous flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
