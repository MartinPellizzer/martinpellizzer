Dryopteris acuminata var. ogatana

Dryopteris acuminata var. ogatana, commonly known as the Japanese male fern, is a variant of the male fern species native to Japan.

It is a fern characterized by its large, deeply lobed fronds that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces sporangia on the underside of its fertile fronds, which are clustered in strobila. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
