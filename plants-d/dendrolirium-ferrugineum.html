Dendrolirium ferrugineum

Dendrolirium ferrugineum, commonly known as the rusty lily, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and produces delicate, white to pale yellow flowers with a distinctive red or orange stripe along the dorsal sepal. The plant has a rhizomatous root system and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers bloom in early spring, often before the leaves fully emerge.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
