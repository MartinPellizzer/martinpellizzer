Decachaena baccata

Decachaena baccata, commonly known as the red-barked evergreen, is a distinctive shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-barked trunk and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by bright red, fleshy berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance and resilience.
