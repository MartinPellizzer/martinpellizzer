Disa erubescens var. leucantha

Disa erubescens var. leucantha, commonly known as the white disa, is a striking South African orchid species characterized by its elegant, pale pink to white flowers.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is native to the Eastern Cape and Western Cape provinces of South Africa. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers are arranged in a raceme and are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is cultivated for its beauty in botanical gardens and private collections.
