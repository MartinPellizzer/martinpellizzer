Diospyros grandiflora

Diospyros grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered ebony, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, glossy bark and elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by edible, sweet fruits resembling small, round drupes. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in traditional medicine.
