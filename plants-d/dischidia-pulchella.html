Dischidia pulchella

Dischidia pulchella, commonly known as the pretty vine or the silver vine, is a climbing epiphytic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its aerial roots and succulent, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on trees or rocks in tropical rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated as a houseplant in many parts of the world.
