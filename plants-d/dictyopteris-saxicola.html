Dictyopteris saxicola

Dictyopteris saxicola, commonly known as the rockweed, is a species of brown algae belonging to the family Dictyopteridaceae.

It is characterized by its leathery, blade-like fronds that are typically dark green to brown in color. This marine plant is commonly found attached to rocky substrates in shallow coastal waters. The thallus is usually unbranched and has a robust, fibrous structure.

It plays an important role in coastal ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various marine organisms.
