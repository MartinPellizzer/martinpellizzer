Diospyros haplostylis var. hildebrandtii

Diospyros haplostylis var. hildebrandtii, commonly known as the Hildebrandt's persimmon, is a species of flowering plant in the Ebenaceae family.

It is native to certain regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by fruit resembling a small, round persimmon.

The fruit is typically edible when fully mature and has a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor.
