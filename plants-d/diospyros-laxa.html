Diospyros laxa

Diospyros laxa, commonly known as the slender persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other persimmon and ebony species. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by orange to red, fleshy fruits.

The fruit is typically edible when fully mature and has a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor.
