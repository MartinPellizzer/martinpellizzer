Diodia rigida var. barbicocca

Diodia rigida var. barbicocca, commonly known as the barbed morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive, barbed tendrils that aid in climbing and support growth on structures. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, blooming throughout the warm season. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and often hairy, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

The species is valued in horticulture for its rapid growth and vibrant floral display.
