Dendrobium pseudoclavator

Dendrobium pseudoclavator, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, cane-like stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, yellowish-orange flowers with a distinctive golden throat. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both ornamental and horticultural purposes.
