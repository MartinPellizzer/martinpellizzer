Diospyros amara

Diospyros amara, commonly known as the bitter persimmon, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other notable species like ebony. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. Its flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and clustered in panicles, and it produces small, round fruits that are typically bitter when unripe.

The bark is thick and rugged, often with a reddish or brownish hue, and the wood is hard and durable.
