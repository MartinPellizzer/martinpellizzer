Daviesia elongata subsp. implexa

Daviesia elongata subsp.

implexa, commonly known as the tangled daviesia, is a shrub native to southern Australia. It features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs.

The plant produces fluffy, yellow to orange flower spikes during spring, which attract a variety of pollinators. It is adapted to grow in sandy or rocky soils and is drought-tolerant.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important species in its native habitat.
