Drypetes hainanensis var. longistipitata

Drypetes hainanensis var. longistipitata, commonly known as the long-stemmed hainan drypetes, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to southern China.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark brown bark and opposite, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers followed by orange-red fruits that are capsule-like in structure. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland and hill forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is of interest for conservation and horticultural use.
