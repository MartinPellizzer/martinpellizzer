Dipterocarpus gibbosus

Dipterocarpus gibbosus, commonly known as the knobwood or hill teak, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its sturdy, aromatic wood. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in spikes.

The fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.
