Dianthus monspeliacus subsp. gallicus

Dianthus monspeliacus subsp.

gallicus, commonly known as the French carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, double-petaled flowers in shades of pink, red, and white.

The plant produces small, reddish-brown seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its propagation. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value and hardiness make it a popular choice among gardeners.
