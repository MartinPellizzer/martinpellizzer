Dennstaedtia bipinnata

Dennstaedtia bipinnata, commonly known as the fern with two pairs of pinnae, is a species of fern belonging to the family Dennstaedtiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, bipinnate fronds that are typically divided into two pairs of pinnae, giving it a finely divided, lacy appearance. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions, often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its rhizomes are usually short and creeping, and the fronds are usually evergreen, providing a consistent green presence throughout the year.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in shady gardens.
