Delphinium beesianum var. radiatifolium

Delphinium beesianum var. radiatifolium, commonly known as the radiate-leaved delphinium, is a striking perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features tall, sparsely branched stems that bear showy, blue to purple spires of spurred flowers. The leaves are distinctive, with a radiating pattern of lobes that give the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in alpine or rocky habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
