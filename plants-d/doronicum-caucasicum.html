Doronicum caucasicum

Doronicum caucasicum, commonly known as the Caucasian leopard's bane, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It features upright stems with lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
