Dactylis ovata

Dactylis ovata, commonly known as sweet vernal grass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that produce distinctive yellow-green flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and disturbed areas. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility.

Dactylis ovata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
