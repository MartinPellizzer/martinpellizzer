Dypsis lokohoensis

Dypsis lokohoensis, commonly known as the Madagascar palm, is a tropical plant native to Madagascar.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family, characterized by its tall, slender trunk and dense, feather-like foliage. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy, well-drained soils. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are followed by edible fruits.
