Dracophyllum × marginatum

Dracophyllum × marginatum, commonly known as the hybrid dracophyllum, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and compact growth habit. The plant typically features small, leathery leaves that are often marginally reddish or purple, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces clusters of tiny, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white or pink, which bloom in spring.

Dracophyllum × marginatum is well adapted to coastal conditions, tolerating salt spray and sandy soils.
