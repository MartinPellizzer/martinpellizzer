Ditassa auyantepuiensis

Ditassa auyantepuiensis, commonly known as the Pando tree, is a unique species found in the tepuis of South America.

It is characterized by its extensive root system, which allows it to form a single, massive organism composed of many trunks. The plant is a type of flowering shrub that thrives in rocky, high-altitude environments. Its leaves are typically small and thick, adapted to conserve water in arid conditions.

The flowers are usually small and clustered, producing nectar that attracts pollinators like bees and hummingbirds.
