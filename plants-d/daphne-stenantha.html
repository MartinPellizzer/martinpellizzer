Daphne stenantha

Daphne stenantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved daphne, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in late winter or early spring, producing white or pale pink blossoms. Its leaves are narrow and often have a waxy texture, while its stems are usually covered in fine, soft hairs.

Daphne stenantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance and fragrance.
