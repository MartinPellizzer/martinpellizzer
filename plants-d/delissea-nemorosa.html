Delissea nemorosa

Delissea nemorosa, commonly known as the forest bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of Europe.

It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in well-drained, loamy soils. The plant features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or violet. Its leaves are lanceolate, with a smooth margin and a slightly hairy surface.

Delissea nemorosa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to support local pollinators.
