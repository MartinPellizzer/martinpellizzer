Daucus sativus

Daucus sativus, commonly known as the parsnip, is a biennial plant belonging to the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its thick, tapering root that is typically white or cream in color. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves and small, yellowish flowers that appear in umbels. Its root is valued for its nutritional content and is often used in cooking.

Daucus sativus thrives in cool climates and well-drained soil.
