Dasypogon glaber

Dasypogon glaber, commonly known as the smooth-leaved grass.

It is a perennial grass species native to Australia, characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and tufted inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions. Its fibrous roots help stabilize soil, making it useful for erosion control.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
