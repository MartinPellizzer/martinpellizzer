Digitaria aurea

Digitaria aurea, commonly known as golden panicgrass, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, golden-yellow inflorescences that give it its common name. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a bluish-green coloration.

Digitaria aurea is often found in disturbed soils and is used in some traditional practices for its medicinal properties.
