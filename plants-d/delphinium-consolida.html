Delphinium consolida

Delphinium consolida, commonly known as the Consolida or Larkspur, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that bear clusters of blue, purple, or white flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. Its flowers are actinomorphic, with five petals and numerous stamens, typical of the Ranunculaceae family.

Delphinium consolida is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and meadows.
