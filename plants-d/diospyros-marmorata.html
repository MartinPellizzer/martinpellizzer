Diospyros marmorata

Diospyros marmorata, commonly known as the marble-leafed persimmon, is a species of flowering plant in the Ebenaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including countries like Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its striking, mottled leaves that give it its common name. Its fruit is edible, typically harvested when fully mature, and is similar in taste to other persimmons.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local cultures.
