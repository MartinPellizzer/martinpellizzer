Dioscorea bemandry

Dioscorea bemandry, commonly known as the African yam vine, is a climbing herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tendrils that help it climb towards sunlight. The plant produces tuberous roots that are often used in traditional medicine and as a food source. Its leaves are heart-shaped and its flowers are typically greenish-yellow with a distinctive structure.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.
