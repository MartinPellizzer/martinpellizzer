Diospyros nitidifolia

Diospyros nitidifolia, commonly known as the glossy buckeye, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark brown bark and palmate leaves with five to seven lobes. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange-red berries containing one or two seeds. Its fruit is often used in traditional medicine, and the tree is valued for its ornamental qualities.

Diospyros nitidifolia is also known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its tolerance to moderate drought conditions.
