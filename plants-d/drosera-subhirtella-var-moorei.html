Drosera subhirtella var. moorei

Drosera subhirtella var. moorei, commonly known as the Moore's Sundew, is a carnivorous plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that secrete a sticky substance to trap insects. The plant typically grows in wet, boggy habitats and thrives in cool, humid conditions. Its flowers are small and white, arranged in a dense spike, and it is often cultivated for its unique appearance and ecological interest.

This variety is a popular choice among carnivorous plant enthusiasts due to its adaptability and aesthetic appeal.
