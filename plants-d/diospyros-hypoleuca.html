Diospyros hypoleuca

Diospyros hypoleuca, commonly known as the white persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that are followed by orange to red berries. Its fruits are edible when fully ripe and have a sweet, honey-like flavor.

Diospyros hypoleuca is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
