Desmodium leptoclados

Desmodium leptoclados, commonly known as the slender desmodium, is a species of legume native to parts of Africa and the Americas.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Desmodium leptoclados is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils, where it plays a role in nitrogen fixation due to its symbiotic relationship with rhizobia bacteria.

Its growth habit makes it a useful plant for soil stabilization and forage in certain regions.
