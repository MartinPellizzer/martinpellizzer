Diospyros canarica

Diospyros canarica, commonly known as the Canary date tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Canary Islands and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into edible, date-like fruits.

Its fruit is typically consumed fresh or used in local cuisines, and the tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in coastal environments.
