Diplazium amamianum

Diplazium amamianum, commonly known as the Amami island fern, is a species native to the Ryukyu Islands of Japan.

It is a terrestrial fern characterized by its tall, slender stems and compound leaves that are divided into numerous narrow segments. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its fronds are usually evergreen, and it reproduces through spores rather than seeds.

Diplazium amamianum is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
