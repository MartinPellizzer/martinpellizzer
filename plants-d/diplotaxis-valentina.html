Diplotaxis valentina

Diplotaxis valentina, commonly known as the African dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, linear leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils.

Due to its ornamental value and resilience, it is cultivated in gardens in regions with mild climates.
