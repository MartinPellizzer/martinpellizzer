Diospyros packmannii

Diospyros packmannii, commonly known as the African persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other persimmon and ebony species. The tree is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into orange to red, fleshy fruits. The fruit is edible, with a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor, and is valued for both its culinary and medicinal uses.

It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.
