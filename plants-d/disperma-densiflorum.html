Disperma densiflorum

Disperma densiflorum, commonly known as the denseflower daisy, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its dense clusters of white to pale yellow flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

Disperma densiflorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
