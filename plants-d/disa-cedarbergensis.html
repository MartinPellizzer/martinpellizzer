Disa cedarbergensis

Disa cedarbergensis, commonly known as the Cedarberg Disa, is a striking member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae) native to South Africa.

This plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear clusters of strikingly colored, tubular flowers, typically in shades of red, orange, and yellow. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions, particularly in the Cedarberg area of the Western Cape. The flowers are adapted for specific pollinators, such as birds and insects, and the plant is noted for its ornamental value.

Disa cedarbergensis is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
