Dioscorea gossweileri

Dioscorea gossweileri, commonly known as the African yam vine, is a member of the Dioscoreaceae family and is native to tropical Africa.

It is a climbing perennial vine characterized by its tuberous roots, which are often used as a food source. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a rough, hairy texture.

Dioscorea gossweileri is valued not only for its edible tubers but also for its traditional medicinal uses in various African cultures.
