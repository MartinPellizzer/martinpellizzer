Diospyros cordifolia var. glabrifolia

Diospyros cordifolia var. glabrifolia, commonly known as the smooth-leaved persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and smooth, glabrous foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into edible, orange-red fruits. Its bark is typically smooth and greyish, with a distinctive aromatic scent.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.
