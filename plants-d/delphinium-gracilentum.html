Delphinium gracilentum

Delphinium gracilentum, commonly known as the slender larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers in late spring to early summer, arranged in dense spikes at the top of the stems. Its leaves are compound, with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets that are often hairy and have a slightly serrated edge.

Delphinium gracilentum thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a dramatic floral display.
