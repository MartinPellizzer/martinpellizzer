Declieuxia saturejoides var. thymbroides

Declieuxia saturejoides var. thymbroides, commonly known as the golden thyme.

It is a herbaceous perennial native to South Africa, characterized by its aromatic, creeping growth habit and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its essential oils contribute to its use in aromatherapy and traditional medicine.
