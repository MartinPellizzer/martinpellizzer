Dendrobium parviflorum

Dendrobium parviflorum, commonly known as the small-flowered orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a rhizomatous stem and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow. The plant typically blooms during the winter months and prefers cool, humid environments. Its leaves are usually broad and lance-shaped, and it thrives in well-drained soil.

Dendrobium parviflorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
