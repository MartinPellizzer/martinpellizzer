Dioclea broadwayana

Dioclea broadwayana, commonly known as the broadwayana passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It belongs to the family Passifloraceae and is characterized by its showy, five-petaled flowers with a distinctive crown of filaments. The plant produces small, orange-red berries that are edible and contain seeds. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture and a strong, sometimes unpleasant odor.

Dioclea broadwayana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
