Deinanthe bifida f. violacea

Deinanthe bifida f. violacea, commonly known as the violetleaf buckler-vice.

This plant is a perennial herbaceous species characterized by its distinctive violet-colored leaves. The leaves are typically divided into two lobes, giving it the common name "buckler-vice.". It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

The flowers are small and white, arranged in clusters, and bloom in late spring to early summer.
