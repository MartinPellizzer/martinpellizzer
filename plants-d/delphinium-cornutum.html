Delphinium cornutum

Delphinium cornutum, commonly known as the horned delphinium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers in clusters at the top of the stem, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are palmately compound, with a hairy texture, and the flowers have five sepals that are often longer than the petals.

Delphinium cornutum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
