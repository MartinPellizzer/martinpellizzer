Dendromyza pachydisca

Dendromyza pachydisca, commonly known as the thick-leaved mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of host trees, primarily in tropical and subtropical regions.

It possesses broad, leathery leaves that are adapted to capture and retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers, which are often clustered and attract various pollinators. Its seeds are dispersed by birds that consume the fleshy, red berries.

Dendromyza pachydisca plays a role in ecosystem dynamics by influencing host tree growth and providing food for wildlife.
