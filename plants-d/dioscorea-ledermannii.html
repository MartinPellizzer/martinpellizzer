Dioscorea ledermannii

Dioscorea ledermannii, commonly known as the Ledermann yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary storage organs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers and has compound leaves with three to five leaflets. Its tubers are used in traditional medicine and as a food source in some regions.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
