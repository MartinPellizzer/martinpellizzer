Digitaria ankaratrensis

Digitaria ankaratrensis, commonly known as the Madagascar fingergrass, is a grass species native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetland or swampy areas.

Digitaria ankaratrensis is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and potential ecological significance in its native habitat.
