Dalbergia crowei

Dalbergia crowei, commonly known as the Malawi rosewood, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The tree produces hard, dark wood that is highly valued for its aesthetic and structural properties. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and timber potential, it is sometimes cultivated in horticultural settings.
