Delphinium peregrinum subsp. cardiopetalum

Delphinium peregrinum subsp.

cardiopetalum, commonly known as the Alpine larkspur, is a tall, herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to August.

The plant has slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. Its flowers are spurred at the base, a characteristic feature of the delphinium genus.

This subspecies is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained, rocky soils.
