Diplopappus pubescens

Diplopappus pubescens, commonly known as the hairy pappus plant, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which contribute to its common name. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers that are typically yellow or white in color. It is native to certain regions of Africa and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and taxonomic studies.
