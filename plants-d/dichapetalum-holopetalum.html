Dichapetalum holopetalum

Dichapetalum holopetalum, commonly known as the white hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

The flowers are pollinated primarily by insects, contributing to its ecological importance in its native habitat.
