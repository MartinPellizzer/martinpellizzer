Discaria trinervis

Discaria trinervis, commonly known as the common thornapple, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a small shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features alternate, ovate leaves with three prominent veins, giving it its scientific name. Its small, white to pale yellow flowers are clustered in spherical inflorescences.

The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are often eaten by birds, aiding in seed dispersal.
