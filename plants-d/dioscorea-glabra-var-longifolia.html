Dioscorea glabra var. longifolia

Dioscorea glabra var. longifolia, commonly known as the slender yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its long, cylindrical, and tuberous roots, which are the primary part used for medicinal and culinary purposes. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically unisexual. Its tubers are often harvested for their nutritional value and traditional medicinal applications.

The species is also valued for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in sustainable agriculture systems.
