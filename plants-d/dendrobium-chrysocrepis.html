Dendrobium chrysocrepis

Dendrobium chrysocrepis, commonly known as the golden crested orchid, is a striking epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a tall, slender stem with a profusion of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in a striking yellow and green coloration. The plant is characterized by its long, pendant-like inflorescence and its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments. It requires well-draining soil and consistent moisture to flourish.

Dendrobium chrysocrepis is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in tropical gardens and greenhouses.
