Daphnopsis americana subsp. tinifolia

Daphnopsis americana subsp.

tinifolia, commonly known as the American daphnopsis, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant has elliptical leaves with a smooth texture and a slightly waxy surface. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for ornamental landscaping.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
