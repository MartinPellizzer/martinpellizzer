Dioscorea dissimulans

Dioscorea dissimulans, commonly known as the false yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers and is typically found in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a rough texture, and it thrives in warm, humid climates.

The tubers of Dioscorea dissimulans are sometimes cultivated for their potential medicinal properties.
