Diospyros coaetanea

Diospyros coaetanea, commonly known as the red-eyed ash, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark that often has a reddish tinge, and its elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green on top. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by small, round fruits that are typically red when mature. Diospyros coaetanea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.

It thrives in humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
