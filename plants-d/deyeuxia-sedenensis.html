Deyeuxia sedenensis

Deyeuxia sedenensis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved false grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as grasslands and wetlands. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets arranged in a panicle.

Deyeuxia sedenensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
