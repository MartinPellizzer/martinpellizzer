Didissandra

Didissandra, commonly known as the African moonseed, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Menispermaceae.

It consists of a single species, Didissandra africana, native to southern Africa. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with smooth, dark green leaves and small, yellowish flowers. Its fruit is a fleshy, orange-red berry that contains several seeds.

Didissandra is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
