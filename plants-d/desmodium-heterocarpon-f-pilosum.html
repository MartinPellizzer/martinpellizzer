Desmodium heterocarpon f. pilosum

Desmodium heterocarpon f. pilosum, commonly known as the hairy desmodium, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in a variety of soil types.

The species is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in agricultural and ecological contexts.
