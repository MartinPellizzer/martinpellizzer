Dypsis sahanofensis

Dypsis sahanofensis, commonly known as the Sahanofen palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to Madagascar and is characterized by its slender, unbranched trunk and pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or white in color. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in moist forest environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
