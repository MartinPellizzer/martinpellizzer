Dicksonia stenochlaena

Dicksonia stenochlaena, commonly known as the narrow-leaf tree fern, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feathery fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant features a distinctive root system that helps anchor it in moist, shaded environments. Its fronds are typically dark green and have a leathery texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Dicksonia stenochlaena thrives in humid climates and is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and greenhouses.
