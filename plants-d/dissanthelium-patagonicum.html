Dissanthelium patagonicum

Dissanthelium patagonicum, commonly known as the Patagonian grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit. The plant typically grows in open, dry habitats such as grasslands and shrublands. Its inflorescence consists of a spikelet cluster, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Due to its resilience, it plays a significant role in stabilizing soil in its native environment.
