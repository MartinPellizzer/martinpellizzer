Dianthus parnassicus

Dianthus parnassicus, commonly known as the Parnassius carnation, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, rounded seed pods that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Dianthus parnassicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
