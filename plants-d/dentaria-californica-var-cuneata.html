Dentaria californica var. cuneata

Dentaria californica var. cuneata, commonly known as the Sierra foothill toothplant, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a raceme, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats.

Its name refers to the tooth-like shape of its leaves, which are a distinguishing feature of the species.
