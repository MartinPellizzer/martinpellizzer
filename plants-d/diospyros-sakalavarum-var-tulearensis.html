Diospyros sakalavarum var. tulearensis

Diospyros sakalavarum var. tulearensis, commonly known as the Madagascar persimmon, is a species native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by orange to reddish fruits.

The fruits are typically edible, with a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor, and are often consumed fresh or used in local traditional remedies.
