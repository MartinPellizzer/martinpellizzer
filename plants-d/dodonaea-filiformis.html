Dodonaea filiformis

Dodonaea filiformis, commonly known as the silky oaks, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that typically grows up to 10 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring.

The plant is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and is also valued for its hard, durable wood.
