Dracaena ombet

Dracaena ombet, commonly known as the red dracaena, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and long, narrow leaves that are usually a deep green with a reddish tinge on the underside. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red or orange berries. Dracaena ombet is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.

It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to thrive.
