Dipelta floribunda

Dipelta floribunda, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of bell-shaped, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and has opposite, lance-shaped leaves. It belongs to the Campanulaceae family, which includes other bellflowers.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological studies and native garden landscaping.
