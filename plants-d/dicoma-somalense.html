Dicoma somalense

Dicoma somalense, commonly known as the Somali dandelion, is a flowering plant native to Somalia and parts of the Horn of Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and milky sap. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, thriving in sandy or rocky soils. Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, while the flowers are yellow and arranged in a cluster.

Dicoma somalense is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
