Distephanus inhacensis

Distephanus inhacensis, commonly known as the Inhaca dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of Mozambique.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils along the shoreline, exhibiting a low, sprawling growth habit. Its yellow flowers bloom in clusters, and the plant is adapted to withstand coastal salt spray and wind.

Distephanus inhacensis is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological niche and potential medicinal properties.
