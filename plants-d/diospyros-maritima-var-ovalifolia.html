Diospyros maritima var. ovalifolia

Diospyros maritima var. ovalifolia, commonly known as the coastal persimmon, is a shrub or small tree native to coastal regions of the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its oval-shaped leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces orange to reddish fruits that are edible when fully ripe. It thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is often found in dune environments.

Its botanical features make it an important component of coastal ecosystems.
