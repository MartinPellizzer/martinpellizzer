Diplopappus scaber

Diplopappus scaber, commonly known as the rough pappus grass, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are covered in fine, rough hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pale yellow to greenish in color. Its seed heads are distinctive, featuring two separate pappi that aid in wind dispersal.

Diplopappus scaber is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
