Dichrostachys kirkii

Dichrostachys kirkii, commonly known as the African stranglewood, is a leguminous shrub or small tree native to sub-Saharan Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, twisted branches and clusters of small, pink to red flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, round pods that contain several seeds. It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Dichrostachys kirkii is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of fodder for livestock.
