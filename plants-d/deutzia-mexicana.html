Deutzia mexicana

Deutzia mexicana, commonly known as Mexican deutzia, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and features deciduous, compound leaves. Its flowering stems are often hairy, and the plant is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils.

Deutzia mexicana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in USDA hardiness zones 6 to 9.
