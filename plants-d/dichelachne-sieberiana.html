Dichelachne sieberiana

Dichelachne sieberiana, commonly known as Sieber's dichelachne, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spikelet-like inflorescences with two to three bracts, and its flowers are wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
