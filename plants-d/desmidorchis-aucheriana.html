Desmidorchis aucheriana

Desmidorchis aucheriana, commonly known as the African orchid, is a species of orchid native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that often display a combination of red, yellow, and purple hues. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. Its roots are adapted to absorb nutrients from the soil, and it relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake.

The flowers are pollinated by insects, particularly bees and flies, which are attracted by their vibrant colors and scent.
