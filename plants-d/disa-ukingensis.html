Disa ukingensis

Disa ukingensis, commonly known as the King's Disa, is a striking member of the orchid family, Orchidaceae, native to the mountainous regions of South Africa.

This plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink, purple, and white. The leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its elegant appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a cool, humid environment to flourish.

Disa ukingensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is a symbol of resilience in its natural habitat.
