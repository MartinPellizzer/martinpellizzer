Dendrophthora linearifolia

Dendrophthora linearifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of host trees.

It has small, linear leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs along its stems. The plant produces white or pale green berries that contain seeds dispersed by birds. It is native to parts of South America and is often found in humid, tropical forests.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the ecosystem, supporting various bird species and contributing to the ecological balance.
