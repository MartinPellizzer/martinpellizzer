Dolichos tenuicaulis

Dolichos tenuicaulis, commonly known as the slender cowpea, is a herbaceous annual plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, climbing stems that can reach lengths of up to several meters, supported by tendrils. The leaves are alternate, trifoliate, with small, ovate leaflets. Its flowers are typically pale yellow to cream, with five petals, and it produces elongated pods containing edible, kidney-shaped seeds.

The plant is adapted to warm climates and is often grown for its nutritious seeds and ornamental value.
