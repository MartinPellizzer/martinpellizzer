Dioscorea crumenigera

Dioscorea crumenigera, commonly known as the African yambean, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary storage organs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and has compound leaves with three to five leaflets. Its tubers are used in traditional medicine and as a food source in some communities.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
