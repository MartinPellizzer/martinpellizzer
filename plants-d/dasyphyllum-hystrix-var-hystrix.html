Dasyphyllum hystrix var. hystrix

Dasyphyllum hystrix var. hystrix, commonly known as the hystrix dasyphyllum, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping in arid regions.
