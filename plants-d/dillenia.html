Dillenia

Dillenia, commonly known as the "silk floss tree," is a genus of flowering plants in the family Dilleniaceae.

It is characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers that resemble pom-poms, and its distinctive, sometimes fleshy fruits. The leaves are typically large, simple, and alternate, with a rough or hairy texture. Dillenia species are native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas, Africa, and Asia.

They are often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.
