Dantia

Dantia, commonly known as the golden groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Dantia typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in grassland habitats.

Its leaves are usually gray-green with a fuzzy texture, and it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
