Dentoceras myriophylla

Dentoceras myriophylla, commonly known as the flame flower, is a striking member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its vibrant, flame-like inflorescences. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically red or orange, with prominent yellow or orange stamens.

Dentoceras myriophylla thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in tropical gardens.
