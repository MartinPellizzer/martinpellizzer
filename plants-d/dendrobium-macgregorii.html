Dendrobium macgregorii

Dendrobium macgregorii, commonly known as the MacGregor's orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a system of pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, often with a dark throat. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining potting media.

Dendrobium macgregorii is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
