Didymocarpus lineicapsa

Didymocarpus lineicapsa, commonly known as the narrow-capsuled tamarind, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pod-like fruits. The plant produces yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, and its fruit capsules are typically narrow and ribbed.

It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
