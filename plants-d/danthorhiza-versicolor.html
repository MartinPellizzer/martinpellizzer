Danthorhiza versicolor

Danthorhiza versicolor, commonly known as the variable twayblade, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and swampy areas. Its flowers, which are usually pink or purple, bloom in late spring to early summer.

Danthorhiza versicolor is a low-maintenance plant that thrives in partial to full shade and requires well-drained, acidic soil.
