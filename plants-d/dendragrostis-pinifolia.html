Dendragrostis pinifolia

Dendragrostis pinifolia, commonly known as the needleleaf bramble, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and small, needle-like leaves that are arranged alternately along the branches. The plant produces tiny, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, followed by small, red berries that ripen in the autumn. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Dendragrostis pinifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in harsh environments.
