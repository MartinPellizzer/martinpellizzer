Dinoseris salicifolia var. araneosa

Dinoseris salicifolia var. araneosa, commonly known as the spider-nettle, is a flowering plant native to parts of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine, hair-like structures. The plant produces clusters of tiny, greenish-white flowers that are arranged in a distinctive, net-like pattern, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is notable for its unique floral morphology and ecological role in supporting local insect populations.
