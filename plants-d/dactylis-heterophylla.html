Dactylis heterophylla

Dactylis heterophylla, commonly known as the variable grass or variable dactylis, is a perennial grass species native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its variable leaf morphology, with leaves that can be either flat or folded, depending on environmental conditions. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides, meadows, and waste areas. Its flowering stems are usually unbranched and bear small, spike-like inflorescences.

Dactylis heterophylla is known for its adaptability and resilience, making it a common weed in various regions.
