Dimorphandra pennigera

Dimorphandra pennigera, commonly known as the African sycamore, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant has a stout, buttressed trunk and can grow up to 20 meters tall. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and dispersed by wind, contributing to its ecological role in savanna ecosystems.

The tree is also valued for its hard, durable wood and is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
