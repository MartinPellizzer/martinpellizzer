Drymocallis reflexa

Drymocallis reflexa, commonly known as silver leaf goldenrod, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, bright yellow flower heads that appear in late summer. The plant features narrow, silver-gray leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive reflexed (bent backward) shape. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Drymocallis reflexa is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators and supporting local wildlife.
