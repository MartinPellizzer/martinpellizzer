Dyckia pampeana

Dyckia pampeana, commonly known as the Pampas dyckia, is a perennial grass-like plant native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hair. The plant produces striking inflorescences with brightly colored flowers, typically in shades of red, orange, or yellow, which bloom in summer. Dyckia pampeana is adapted to dry, sunny environments and is often found in open grasslands and rocky areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens.
