Desmodium pachyrhizum var. latifolium

Desmodium pachyrhizum var. latifolium, commonly known as broadleaf desmodium, is a leguminous plant characterized by its trifoliate leaves and hairy stems.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and is often found in disturbed soils and grasslands. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple, followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It has a deep root system that helps in nitrogen fixation, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.

Its adaptability to various environmental conditions makes it a useful plant in ecological restoration projects.
