Draconia campestris subsp. borealis

Draconia campestris subsp.

borealis, commonly known as the northern field dragonia, is a perennial herb native to the northern regions of Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and grasslands.

The plant features a robust, upright stem with lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and clustered in umbels, producing a distinctive reddish-purple color.

This subspecies is adapted to cooler climates and is an important component of its local ecosystems.
