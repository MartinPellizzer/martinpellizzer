Diospyros howii

Diospyros howii, commonly known as the Hawaiian persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by orange-red, fleshy fruits that are edible when ripe. Diospyros howii is adapted to the tropical climate of Hawaii and thrives in well-drained soils.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species and is protected in its native range.
