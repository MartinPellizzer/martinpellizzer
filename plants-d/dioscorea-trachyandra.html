Dioscorea trachyandra

Dioscorea trachyandra, commonly known as the rough-stemmed yam, is a member of the Dioscoreaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a perennial vine characterized by its woody, ridged stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible tuberous roots, which are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires a humid climate with partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and edible properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and agricultural settings.
