Denira

Denira, commonly known as the swamp apple or water apple, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into fleshy, orange-colored fruits. Denira thrives in wet, swampy environments and is often found in mangrove ecosystems.

Its fruits are edible and have a sweet, tangy flavor, making it a valuable resource in local cuisines.
