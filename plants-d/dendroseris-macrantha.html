Dendroseris macrantha

Dendroseris macrantha, commonly known as the broad-flowered dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and herbaceous growth habit. The plant features upright, hairy stems and basal leaves that are lobed and often covered in fine hairs. Its showy yellow flowers are followed by fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds, typical of the genus.

Dendroseris macrantha thrives in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.
