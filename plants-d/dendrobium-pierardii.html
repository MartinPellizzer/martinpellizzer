Dendrobium pierardii

Dendrobium pierardii, commonly known as the Singapore orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, showy flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in two rows along the pseudobulbs.

Dendrobium pierardii thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
