Diporidium ardisioides

Diporidium ardisioides, commonly known as the red-stemmed caryophyllaceae, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It features slender, ascending stems that are often tinged with red or purple, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, linear, and covered with fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental value.

Diporidium ardisioides thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
