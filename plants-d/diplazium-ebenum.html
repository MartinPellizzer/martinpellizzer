Diplazium ebenum

Diplazium ebenum, commonly known as the golden fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow fronds, which are divided into narrow, lance-shaped segments. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Diplazium ebenum is valued both for its ornamental appearance and its ecological role in forest ecosystems.
