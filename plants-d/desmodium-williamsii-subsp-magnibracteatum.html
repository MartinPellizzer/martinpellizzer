Desmodium williamsii subsp. magnibracteatum

Desmodium williamsii subsp.

magnibracteatum, commonly known as the broad-bract desmodium, is a perennial legume native to parts of Africa and the Middle East. It features trifoliate leaves with oval to elliptic leaflets, and its flowers are typically pink or purple, arranged in dense clusters. The plant produces small, globular pods that contain several seeds, and it is often found in grassy areas and disturbed soils.

It is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in soil enrichment and forage production.
