Dioclea panamensis

Dioclea panamensis, commonly known as the Panama sweet pea, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in humid, shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both ecological studies and horticultural research.
