Draba melanopus var. alticola

Draba melanopus var. alticola, commonly known as the alpine whitlow grass, is a low-growing, herbaceous plant native to alpine and subalpine regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically features small, clustered flowers that bloom in early spring, often in shades of white or pale yellow. The plant has a compact growth habit with hairy stems and leaves that are usually narrow and linear. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Draba melanopus var. alticola plays a role in stabilizing soil in alpine ecosystems and is often found in mountainous regions of Europe, Asia, and North America.
