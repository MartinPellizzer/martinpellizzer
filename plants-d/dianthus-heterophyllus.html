Dianthus heterophyllus

Dianthus heterophyllus, commonly known as the pink rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and prefers full sun. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, while its flowers have five petals that are often notched at the edges.

Dianthus heterophyllus is a hardy perennial that is often used in rock gardens due to its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements.
