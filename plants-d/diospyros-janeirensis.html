Diospyros janeirensis

Diospyros janeirensis, commonly known as the Brazilian persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into orange-red, fleshy fruits. The fruits are edible, though they are generally not cultivated for commercial purposes.

It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in well-drained soils.
