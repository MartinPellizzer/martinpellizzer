Dicentra occidentalis

Dicentra occidentalis, commonly known as the western bleeding heart, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It features delicate, heart-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring, typically in shades of pink and white. The plant has trifoliate leaves that are palmately arranged, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Dicentra occidentalis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
