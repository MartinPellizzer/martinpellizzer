Dentaria californica

Dentaria californica, commonly known as the California toothplant, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cress plants. The plant features basal leaves that are pinnately lobed and small white flowers that bloom in early spring. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates.

Dentaria californica is often found in coastal scrub and chaparral habitats.
