Disa affinis

Disa affinis, commonly known as the common disa, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, spurred flowers. The plant typically grows in grasslands and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink and purple, are adapted for pollination by insects.

Disa affinis is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
