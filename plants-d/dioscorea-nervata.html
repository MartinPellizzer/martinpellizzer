Dioscorea nervata

Dioscorea nervata, commonly known as the veined yam, is a perennial vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces tuberous roots that are often harvested for culinary and medicinal purposes. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant.

The species is valued for its nutritional content and traditional use in herbal medicine.
