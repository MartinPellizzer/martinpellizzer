Dendrobium angulatum

Dendrobium angulatum, commonly known as the angular orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant produces racemes of fragrant, yellowish-green flowers with distinctive angular sepals. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Dendrobium angulatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for horticultural purposes.
