Drypetes oblongifolia

Drypetes oblongifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often clustered at the base of the petioles. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a woody capsule that contains several seeds.

The tree is valued for its hard, durable wood and is often found in lowland forests and secondary growth areas.
