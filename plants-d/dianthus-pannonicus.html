Dianthus pannonicus

Dianthus pannonicus, commonly known as the Pannonian carnation, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Europe.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to white. The plant typically grows in meadows and grasslands, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its stems are upright and often branched.

Dianthus pannonicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
