Ditassa refractifolia

Ditassa refractifolia, commonly known as the refractile leaf plant, is a unique species characterized by its translucent, waxy leaves that refract light in a distinctive manner.

Native to the humid tropical regions of South America, it thrives in shaded, moist environments. The plant's leaves are thick and leathery, with a complex venation pattern that enhances its structural integrity. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow in color.

Due to its unusual appearance and limited distribution, it is a subject of interest for botanists and plant enthusiasts alike.
