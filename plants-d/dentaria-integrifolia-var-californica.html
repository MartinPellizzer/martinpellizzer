Dentaria integrifolia var. californica

Dentaria integrifolia var. californica, commonly known as the California toothplant, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and toothed, giving it its common name. The plant produces white to pale yellow flowers in early spring, which are clustered in umbels. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or open habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native range.
