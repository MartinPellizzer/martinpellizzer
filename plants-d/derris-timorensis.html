Derris timorensis

Derris timorensis, commonly known as the Timor derris, is a flowering plant native to Indonesia and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its pinnate leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant is notable for containing the alkaloid rotenone, which is used as a natural insecticide. Its roots and seeds are also utilized in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Derris timorensis thrives in tropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.
