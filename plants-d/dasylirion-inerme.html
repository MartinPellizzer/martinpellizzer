Dasylirion inerme

Dasylirion inerme, commonly known as the desert dagger, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette of long, narrow, gray-green leaves that are covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. The plant produces tall, spire-like inflorescences bearing clusters of bright yellow to orange flowers in the spring. Its succulent structure allows it to store water, making it highly drought-tolerant.

It is often used in xeriscaping due to its hardy nature and ornamental appeal.
