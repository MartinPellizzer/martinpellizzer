Distictella elongata

Distictella elongata, commonly known as the long-stalked bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue to purple, which bloom during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
