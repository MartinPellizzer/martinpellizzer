Diphragmus hexasepalus

Diphragmus hexasepalus, commonly known as the six-lobed burr vine, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and distinctive six-lobed sepals that give it its scientific name. The plant produces yellow flowers that are followed by spiny, three-lobed fruits. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a rough texture.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.
