Dimorphandra macrostachya subsp. macrostachya

Dimorphandra macrostachya subsp.

macrostachya, commonly known as the yellow bell tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Madagascar. It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers that resemble bells, which bloom in clusters during the dry season.

The plant has compound leaves with elongated leaflets and produces woody pods that contain edible seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry forest and woodland habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local communities.
