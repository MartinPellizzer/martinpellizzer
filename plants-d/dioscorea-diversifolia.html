Dioscorea diversifolia

Dioscorea diversifolia, commonly known as the variable yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its palmate leaves and yellowish flowers. The plant produces tuberous roots that are often used as a food source in some cultures. Its growth habit involves twining stems that can reach several meters in length.

The botanical features of Dioscorea diversifolia make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
