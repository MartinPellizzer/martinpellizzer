Dicorynia macrophylla

Dicorynia macrophylla, commonly known as the Brazilian wax tree, is a flowering plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its large, waxy leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically pollinated by insects. Its bark contains a resinous substance that has been historically used by indigenous communities.

Dicorynia macrophylla is valued for its ecological role and potential medicinal properties.
