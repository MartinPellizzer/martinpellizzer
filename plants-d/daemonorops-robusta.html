Daemonorops robusta

Daemonorops robusta, commonly known as the giant pandan, is a robust, fast-growing palm native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, sword-shaped leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or green, followed by clustered, edible fruits. Its roots are fibrous and can be used in traditional medicine.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for the use of its leaves in cooking and crafts.
