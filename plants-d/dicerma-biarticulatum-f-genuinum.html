Dicerma biarticulatum f. genuinum

Dicerma biarticulatum f. genuinum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems and small, alternate leaves that are typically narrow and pointed. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by distinctive, bead-like fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky soils.

Due to its unique morphology and adaptability, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
