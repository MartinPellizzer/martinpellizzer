Diandranthus eulalioides

Diandranthus eulalioides, commonly known as the velvetleaf hibiscus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features lance-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and a distinctive, showy inflorescence that includes both male and female flowers. The plant produces bright yellow or orange tubular flowers that are typically borne in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in horticulture and ecological studies.
