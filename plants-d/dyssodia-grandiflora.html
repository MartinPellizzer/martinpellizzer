Dyssodia grandiflora

Dyssodia grandiflora, commonly known as the largeflower dyssodia, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, succulent stems. The plant produces showy, yellow to orange flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically fleshy and arranged in a rosette pattern, adapted to conserve water in dry environments.

Dyssodia grandiflora is often found growing on rocky outcrops or in open grasslands, showcasing its resilience to harsh climatic conditions.
