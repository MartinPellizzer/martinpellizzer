Dendrochilum ochrolabium

Dendrochilum ochrolabium, commonly known as the golden-labied tree orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, yellowish-orange flowers. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are elliptical and covered with a dense layer of fine, white hairs.

Dendrochilum ochrolabium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
