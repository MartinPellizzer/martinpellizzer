Disa aequiloba

Disa aequiloba, commonly known as the pink disa, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous plant with a short, rhizomatous stem and produces striking, showy flowers. The flowers are typically pink or purple and have a distinctive, spurred lip that aids in pollination. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grassland or forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and conservatories worldwide.
