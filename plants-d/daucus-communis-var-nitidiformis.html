Daucus communis var. nitidiformis

Daucus communis var. nitidiformis, commonly known as the smoothleaf cowparsley, is a variant of the common cowparsley.

It is a biennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia, characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides and in open fields.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Apiaceae family.
