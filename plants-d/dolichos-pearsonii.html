Dolichos pearsonii

Dolichos pearsonii, commonly known as the Indian cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, with slender stems that twine around supports. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by elongated, pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets.

Dolichos pearsonii is valued for its nutritional seeds and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important crop in sustainable agriculture.
