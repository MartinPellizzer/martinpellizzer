Dorema badhysi

Dorema badhysi, commonly known as the Kashmiri bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, pale blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, preferring cool and moist conditions. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered with a dense layer of fine hairs, which help in reducing water loss.

Dorema badhysi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in botanical gardens and alpine flora collections.
