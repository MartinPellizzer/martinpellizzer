Diospyros kotoensis

Diospyros kotoensis, commonly known as the Koto persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other well-known genera such as Diospyros and Strychnos. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into edible, orange-red berries. The fruit is typically consumed raw or used in traditional culinary practices.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and horticultural studies.
