Dianthus villosus

Dianthus villosus, commonly known as the hairy campion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and clusters of pink or red flowers with five petals. The plant features hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and rocky areas.

Dianthus villosus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
