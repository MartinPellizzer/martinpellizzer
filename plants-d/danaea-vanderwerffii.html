Danaea vanderwerffii

Danaea vanderwerffii, commonly known as the Vanderwerff's flame flower, is a unique species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences that resemble flames, composed of numerous small, tubular flowers clustered in a dense, spiky structure. The plant belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate, with a smooth texture and a glossy appearance.

Danaea vanderwerffii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in botanical gardens and as a houseplant due to its vibrant floral display.
