Dioscorea lilela

Dioscorea lilela, commonly known as the Lilela yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary part used for culinary and medicinal purposes. The plant produces small, green flowers followed by yellowish fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its stems are hairy and twining.

The tuber is typically harvested when mature, and it is valued for its nutritional content and traditional uses in local communities.
