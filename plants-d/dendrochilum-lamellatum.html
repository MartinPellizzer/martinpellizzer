Dendrochilum lamellatum

Dendrochilum lamellatum, commonly known as the lamellate orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, strap-like leaves and intricate, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers feature a distinctive lip with a prominent ridge, which is a key identifying feature.

Dendrochilum lamellatum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and adaptability to various growing conditions.
