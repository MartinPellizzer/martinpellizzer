Dahlia apiculata

Dahlia apiculata, commonly known as the Easter dahlia, is a tuberous-rooted herbaceous perennial native to Mexico.

It produces showy, single or double flowers in a wide range of colors, including red, orange, yellow, and pink. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

The tuberous roots are used for propagation and are essential for the plant's survival through winter.
