Diervilla subsessilis var. mollis

Diervilla subsessilis var. mollis, commonly known as the smoothleaf hazelnut, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its dense, sprawling growth habit. The plant features small, oval-shaped leaves that are smooth and often have a bluish-green hue. Its flowers are clustered in catkins, and it produces edible, nut-like fruits that are enclosed in a papery husk.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in acidic, well-drained soils.
