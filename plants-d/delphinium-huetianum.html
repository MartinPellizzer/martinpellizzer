Delphinium huetianum

Delphinium huetianum, commonly known as the Huetian larkspur, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that bear clusters of showy, blue to purple flowers. The plant produces distinctive, spurred petals and has palmate, lobed leaves that are typically hairy. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
