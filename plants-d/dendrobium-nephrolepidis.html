Dendrobium nephrolepidis

Dendrobium nephrolepidis, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is an epiphytic plant that grows on trees and rocks, often in humid tropical environments. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in a range of colors, including yellow, orange, and red. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in varying environmental conditions.

Dendrobium nephrolepidis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated for horticultural purposes.
