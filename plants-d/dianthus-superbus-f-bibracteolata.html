Dianthus superbus f. bibracteolata

Dianthus superbus f. bibracteolata, commonly known as the bracted campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink or red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features a compact growth habit with hairy stems and leaves, and its inflorescence is typically composed of a single, large flower atop a short peduncle. It is often found in rocky or dry habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

The variety 'bibracteolata' is distinguished by the presence of small bracts at the base of the flower, adding to its unique botanical appearance.
