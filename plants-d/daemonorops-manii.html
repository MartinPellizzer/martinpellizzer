Daemonorops manii

Daemonorops manii, commonly known as the giant cycad, is a member of the Arecaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

This plant is characterized by its large, feather-like leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. It produces a stout, unbranched stem that supports a crown of leaves, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value. The plant is dioecious, meaning it has separate male and female plants, and it reproduces through cones rather than flowers.

Due to its slow growth and conservation status, it is considered a rare and valuable species in horticulture.
