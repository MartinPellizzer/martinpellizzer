Delphinium simonkaianum subsp. simonkaianum

Delphinium simonkaianum subsp.

simonkaianum, commonly known as the Simonkaian delphinium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height.

The plant produces showy, blue to purple spires of flowers, which are composed of numerous small, petal-like sepals. Its foliage consists of deeply lobed leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
