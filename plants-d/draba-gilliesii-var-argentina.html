Draba gilliesii var. argentina

Draba gilliesii var. argentina, commonly known as the Argentine rockcress, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and pungent odor. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This variety is notable for its hardy nature and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
