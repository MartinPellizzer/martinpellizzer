Diospyros clementium

Diospyros clementium, commonly known as the common persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and alternate, simple leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange to reddish fruits that are edible when fully ripe.

The fruit contains a single large seed enclosed in a fleshy, sweet pulp, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.
