Diospyros hallieri

Diospyros hallieri, commonly known as the Halli's persimmon, is a species of flowering plant in the Ebenaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is a small to medium-sized tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, grey bark and simple, alternate leaves. Its fruit is a small, oval-shaped berry that changes from green to orange-red when mature.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
