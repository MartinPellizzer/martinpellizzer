Davallia schlechteri

Davallia schlechteri, commonly known as the silver fern, is a species of polypod fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, leathery, dark green fronds with a silvery underside, giving it its common name. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, with creeping rhizomes that produce new plantlets. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, acidic soil.

Davallia schlechteri is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
