Dichaea trachysepala

Dichaea trachysepala, commonly known as the rough-leaved dichaea, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments. The plant features a short, creeping rhizome with erect, linear leaves that are covered in a rough, hairy texture. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and often have a slight purple tinge, arranged in a loose inflorescence.

Dichaea trachysepala is valued for its unique foliage and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental appeal.
