Dianthus brachyanthus var. longicaulis

Dianthus brachyanthus var. longicaulis, commonly known as the long-stemmed carnation, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of pink to red flowers with prominent white markings, which bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has a compact, bushy growth habit with hairy, grayish-green leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long, slender stems that support the showy blooms.
