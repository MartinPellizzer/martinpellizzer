Dianthus talyschensis

Dianthus talyschensis, commonly known as the Talyschense campion, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of pink or purple flowers resembling small, delicate carnations. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats. It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family, which includes other well-known species like carnations and sweet williams.

Dianthus talyschensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
