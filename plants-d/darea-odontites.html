Darea odontites

Darea odontites, commonly known as the toothed dandelion, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, lobed leaves and a single, hollow, toothed stem that supports a single flower head. The flower head consists of both ray and disk flowers, typical of the Asteraceae family. It produces a distinctive, elongated, and often hairy seed dispersal structure called a pappus.

This plant is often found in disturbed soils and is native to Europe and parts of Asia.
