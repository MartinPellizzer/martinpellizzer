Downingia pusilla

Downingia pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf rock cress, is a small, herbaceous perennial native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It typically grows in moist, rocky habitats such as stream banks and forest floors. The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white or pale pink flowers in early spring. Downingia pusilla is characterized by its delicate appearance and ability to thrive in shaded, wet environments.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience and aesthetic value.
