Dinebra decipiens var. peacockii

Dinebra decipiens var. peacockii, commonly known as the peacock orchid, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, colorful flowers that resemble peacock feathers. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are typically elongated and lance-shaped, providing a lush green backdrop to its showy blooms.

Dinebra decipiens var. peacockii is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in warm, humid environments.
