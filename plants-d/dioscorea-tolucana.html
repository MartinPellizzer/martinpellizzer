Dioscorea tolucana

Dioscorea tolucana, commonly known as the Mexican yam, is a climbing vine native to Central America and parts of the Caribbean.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by tuberous roots that are used in traditional medicine. Its tubers are also edible when properly prepared and have a starchy, sweet flavor.

Dioscorea tolucana is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in herbal remedies for its potential health benefits.
