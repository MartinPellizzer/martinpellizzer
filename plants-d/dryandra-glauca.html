Dryandra glauca

Dryandra glauca, commonly known as the blue dryandra, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It belongs to the family Proteaceae and is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves that are often covered in a fine blue waxy coating. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that appear in clusters during the spring. It is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and is drought-tolerant.
