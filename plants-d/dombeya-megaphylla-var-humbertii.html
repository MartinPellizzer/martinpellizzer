Dombeya megaphylla var. humbertii

Dombeya megaphylla var. humbertii, commonly known as the giant dombeya, is a striking plant native to the mountainous regions of Madagascar.

It is characterized by its large, showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a subtle fragrance. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters, with thick, rough bark and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowering period occurs during the dry season, making it a notable species in the local flora.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
