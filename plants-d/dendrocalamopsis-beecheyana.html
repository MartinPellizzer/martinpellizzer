Dendrocalamopsis beecheyana

Dendrocalamopsis beecheyana, commonly known as the New Zealand tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds that can reach up to 3 meters in length. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in coastal and lowland forests. Its reproductive structures, including spore-bearing cones, are located at the base of the fronds.

This species plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various native fauna.
