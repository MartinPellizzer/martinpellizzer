Dryandra arctotidis

Dryandra arctotidis, commonly known as the Narrow-leaf Dryandra, is a member of the Proteaceae family and is native to Western Australia.

It is a shrub characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant is adapted to the nutrient-poor soils of its native habitat, which is typical of many species in the Proteaceae family. It has a compact growth habit and is often found in heathlands and coastal areas.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
