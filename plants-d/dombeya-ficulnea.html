Dombeya ficulnea

Dombeya ficulnea, commonly known as the white dombeya, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has smooth, grayish bark and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Dombeya ficulnea is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
