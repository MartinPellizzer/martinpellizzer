Dulongia integerrima

Dulongia integerrima, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, often fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone habitats, demonstrating a high degree of adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are usually pink or purple in color.

Dulongia integerrima is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil and full sun.
