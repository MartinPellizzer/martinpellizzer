Dianthus multiceps subsp. multiceps

Dianthus multiceps subsp.

multiceps, commonly known as the Sierra dianthus, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in mountainous regions. Its leaves are linear and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are sturdy and often branched.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and naturalized areas.
