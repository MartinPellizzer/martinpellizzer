Diplandra potamogeton

Diplandra potamogeton, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater ecosystems in North America.

It has long, slender, ribbon-like leaves that are typically less than 1 inch wide, arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish-white in color, which develop into small, rounded fruits. It plays an important role in aquatic habitats by providing oxygen and shelter for various aquatic organisms.

Diplandra potamogeton is often found in slow-moving waters such as ponds, lakes, and streams.
