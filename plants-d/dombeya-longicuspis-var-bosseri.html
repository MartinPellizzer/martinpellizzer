Dombeya longicuspis var. bosseri

Dombeya longicuspis var. bosseri, commonly known as the Bosser's dombeya, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white, pink, or purple. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

This variety is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
