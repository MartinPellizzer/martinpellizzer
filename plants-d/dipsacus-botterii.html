Dipsacus botterii

Dipsacus botterii, commonly known as the velvet tamarisk, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its woody stems and dense, soft, purplish-gray foliage. The plant produces tall spikes of small, usually pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its botanical features include compound leaves with serrated edges and a distinctive, spiny fruit capsule that splits open to release its seeds.

Dipsacus botterii is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
