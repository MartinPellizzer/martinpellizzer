Digitaria serotina

Digitaria serotina, commonly known as blackgrass, is a grass species native to temperate regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Digitaria serotina is an important component of wetland ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
