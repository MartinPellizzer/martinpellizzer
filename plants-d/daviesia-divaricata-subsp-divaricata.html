Daviesia divaricata subsp. divaricata

Daviesia divaricata subsp.

divaricata, commonly known as the variable daviesia, is a shrub native to southern Australia. It features narrow, linear to lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and can vary in color from grey-green to red.

The plant produces fluffy, pink to pale yellow flowers in late winter to spring, which are clustered in dense, upright inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to full sun, making it suitable for rocky or sandy habitats.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and flowering display.
