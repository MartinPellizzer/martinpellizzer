Dianthus serrulatus subsp. barbatus

Dianthus serrulatus subsp.

barbatus, commonly known as the hairy pinks, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and produces clusters of pink or red flowers with frilled petals.

The subspecies is distinguished by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding vibrant color to the landscape.
