Dorisia

Dorisia, commonly known as the blue-eyed grass, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, showy blue or purple flowers. These plants typically grow in moist, well-drained soils and are often found in wetlands or along streams. Dorisia species are native to North America and are valued for their ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.

They play an important role in supporting pollinators and maintaining wetland ecosystems.
