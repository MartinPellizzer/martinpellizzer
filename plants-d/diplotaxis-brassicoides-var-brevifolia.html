Diplotaxis brassicoides var. brevifolia

Diplotaxis brassicoides var. brevifolia, commonly known as the short-leaved rocket mustard, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and pungent odor. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions. It has slender, branching stems and small, alternate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The variety brevifolia is distinguished by its shorter leaves compared to the typical form of the species.
