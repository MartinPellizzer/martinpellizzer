Dabanus tomentosus

Dabanus tomentosus, commonly known as the woolly dabanus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage that gives it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. It thrives in humid environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
