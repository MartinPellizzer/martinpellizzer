Delphinium menyuyanense

Delphinium menyuyanense, commonly known as the Menyuyan larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that bear clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate to cool climates. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and the flowers are typically 5-7 cm in diameter, with prominent spurred petals.

Delphinium menyuyanense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
