Dendrobium caleyi

Dendrobium caleyi, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is an epiphytic plant, typically growing on trees or rocks in tropical rainforests. The plant features long, cane-like stems covered in clusters of fragrant, yellowish-green flowers. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

Dendrobium caleyi is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for horticultural purposes.
