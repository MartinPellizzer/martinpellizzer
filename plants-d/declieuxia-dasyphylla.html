Declieuxia dasyphylla

Declieuxia dasyphylla, commonly known as the stiff-leaf sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and riverbanks, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are stiff and upright, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, three-lobed flowers.

Declieuxia dasyphylla plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
