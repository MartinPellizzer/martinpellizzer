Diospyros carpinifolia f. puberula

Diospyros carpinifolia f. puberula, commonly known as the pubescent persimmon, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other species like the common persimmon. The plant is characterized by its hairy or pubescent leaves, which give it its specific epithet. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by edible berries, though these are typically not as widely cultivated as other persimmon varieties.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
