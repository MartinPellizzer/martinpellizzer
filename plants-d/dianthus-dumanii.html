Dianthus dumanii

Dianthus dumanii, commonly known as the desert pink, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces showy, pink or red flowers with five petals, typical of the Caryophyllaceae family. The plant has small, narrow leaves that are adapted to conserve water, reflecting its drought-tolerant nature. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

The flowers bloom in spring and early summer, adding vibrant color to its natural habitat.
