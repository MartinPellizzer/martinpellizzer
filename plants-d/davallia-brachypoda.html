Davallia brachypoda

Davallia brachypoda, commonly known as the shield fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its delicate, pinnately divided fronds that are often covered in a fine, whitish bloom. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on tree trunks or rocks. Its rhizomes are short and thick, allowing it to anchor itself to various substrates.

The shield fern is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in indoor conditions.
