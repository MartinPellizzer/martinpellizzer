Dalea hintonii

Dalea hintonii, commonly known as Hinton's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Dalea hintonii is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open habitats.

Its ecological importance includes providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a host plant for certain butterfly species.
