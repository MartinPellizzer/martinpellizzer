Dendrobium veillonii

Dendrobium veillonii, commonly known as the Veillon's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with elongated, fleshy pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant produces tall, branching inflorescences bearing numerous fragrant, yellowish-green to white flowers. Its leaves are strap-shaped and arranged in two rows along the pseudobulbs.

Dendrobium veillonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
