Diospyros moonii

Diospyros moonii, commonly known as the Philippine persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other familiar species like the ebony and persimmon trees. The plant is characterized by its simple, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are followed by edible, orange-red berries that are typically consumed when fully ripe.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and the nutritional value of its fruit.
