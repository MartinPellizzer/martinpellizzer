Digitaria friesii

Digitaria friesii, commonly known as the African grass or false brome, is a perennial grass species native to Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Digitaria friesii is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Its ecological importance includes soil stabilization and serving as forage for livestock.
