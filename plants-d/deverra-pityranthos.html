Deverra pityranthos

Deverra pityranthos, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvetleaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are velvety to the touch, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring and summer. It thrives in warm, sunny environments and is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils.

Deverra pityranthos is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
