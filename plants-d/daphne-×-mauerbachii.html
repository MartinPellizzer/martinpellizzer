Daphne × mauerbachii

Daphne × mauerbachii, commonly known as the winter daphne, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, waxy flowers in shades of pink, white, and red, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. Its ornamental value lies in its showy blooms and attractive foliage, making it a popular choice for gardens.

However, it is toxic to both humans and animals, requiring careful handling and placement.
