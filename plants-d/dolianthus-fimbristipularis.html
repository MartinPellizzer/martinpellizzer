Dolianthus fimbristipularis

Dolianthus fimbristipularis, commonly known as the woolly sunflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray foliage and bright yellow ray flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It produces small, fuzzy seed heads that aid in wind dispersal.

Dolianthus fimbristipularis is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in landscaping.
