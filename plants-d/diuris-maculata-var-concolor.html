Diuris maculata var. concolor

Diuris maculata var. concolor, commonly known as the red-tipped spider orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southeastern Australia.

It typically grows in heathlands and open forests, preferring well-drained soils. The plant features a slender, wiry stem with a single or few flowers that are often red or pink with a distinctive greenish or brownish stripe. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the flowers are characterized by their intricate, spider-like appearance.

This variety is noted for its striking coloration and is a popular subject in botanical studies and horticulture.
