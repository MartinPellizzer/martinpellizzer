Desmodium siamense

Desmodium siamense, commonly known as the Siam velvet bean, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces cylindrical pods containing several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Desmodium siamense is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in agricultural settings.

Its ornamental value and medicinal properties contribute to its significance in both horticulture and ethnobotany.
