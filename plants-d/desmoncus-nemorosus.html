Desmoncus nemorosus

Desmoncus nemorosus, commonly known as the forest lichen, is a member of the Lecanoraceae family and is a symbiotic composite organism composed of a fungal partner and algal or cyanobacterial photobionts.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found on bark, wood, and soil in temperate forests. The thallus is usually crustose or foliose, with a greenish or yellowish coloration due to the presence of chlorophyll from the photobiont. It plays a vital role in nutrient cycling and serves as an indicator species for air quality and ecological health.

Its structure and physiology reflect a complex adaptation to its specific microhabitat.
