Dalea choanosema

Dalea choanosema, commonly known as the pink prairie clover, is a flowering plant native to the central and southern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its clusters of pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Dalea choanosema plays a role in soil stabilization and provides nectar for pollinators.
