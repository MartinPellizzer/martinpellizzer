Dianella longifolia

Dianella longifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf flax lily, is a species of flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a perennial plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest undergrowth. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pale blue flowers. Its berries are blue or black and contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.

Dianella longifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems.
