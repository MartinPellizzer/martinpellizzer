Dichapetalum oliganthum

Dichapetalum oliganthum, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a striking deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in diameter, giving it a distinctive, almost fern-like appearance. The tree produces showy, orange-red inflorescences that bloom in late summer, attracting a variety of pollinators. Its bark is smooth and pale grey, often with a slightly flaky texture.

The plant is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient and ornamental species in tropical and subtropical regions.
