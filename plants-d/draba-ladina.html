Draba ladina

Draba ladina, commonly known as the Ladino rockcress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a small, herbaceous perennial that typically grows in alpine and subalpine regions. The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. Draba ladina is adapted to harsh mountain environments, with a short growing season and cold tolerance.

It is often found in rocky soils and is a characteristic component of alpine meadows.
