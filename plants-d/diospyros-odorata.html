Diospyros odorata

Diospyros odorata, commonly known as the fragrant ebony, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its dark, heavy wood, which is highly valued for its density and durability. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a smooth texture. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by small, round fruits that turn from green to red as they mature.

The plant is also noted for its aromatic properties, which contribute to its use in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
