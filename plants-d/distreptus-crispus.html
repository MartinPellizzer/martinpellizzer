Distreptus crispus

Distreptus crispus, commonly known as the curly-stemmed orchid, is a species of orchid native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, grass-like leaves and intricate, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its stems are often curled or twisted, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Distreptus crispus is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
