Delphinium parishii var. pallidum

Delphinium parishii var. pallidum, commonly known as the pallid delphinium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high elevations of the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that bear clusters of blue to lavender-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in open meadows and forest clearings. Its leaves are palmately compound, with several leaflets arranged radiating from a central point.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
