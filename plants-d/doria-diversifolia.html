Doria diversifolia

Doria diversifolia, commonly known as the variable-leaf doria, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its diverse leaf morphology, with leaves that vary in shape, size, and venation patterns. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, often displaying shades of white and green.

Doria diversifolia is valued in horticulture for its ornamental leaves and adaptability to various soil conditions.
