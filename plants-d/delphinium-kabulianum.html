Delphinium kabulianum

Delphinium kabulianum, commonly known as the Kabul larkspur, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features tall, spindly stems that bear clusters of showy, spurred blue or purple flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often deeply lobed, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Due to its beauty and hardiness, it is also valued in horticulture for its ability to attract pollinators.
