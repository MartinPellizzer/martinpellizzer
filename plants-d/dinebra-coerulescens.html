Dinebra coerulescens

Dinebra coerulescens, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, blue-violet flower heads that resemble mist, blooming in late summer to early fall. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and prairies. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its composite flower structure.
