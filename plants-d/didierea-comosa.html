Didierea comosa

Didierea comosa, commonly known as the ghost plant, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its unique, branching structure and fleshy, waxy leaves that help it store water. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a delicate touch to its appearance. Its leaves are often covered in a fine, powdery coating, which helps reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Didierea comosa is well adapted to dry environments and requires minimal care, making it a popular choice for succulent enthusiasts.
