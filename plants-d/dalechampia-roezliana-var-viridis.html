Dalechampia roezliana var. viridis

Dalechampia roezliana var. viridis, commonly known as the green humpback, is a variant of the humpback plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a waxy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that are borne on long, slender peduncles. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The varietal name 'viridis' refers to its distinctive green coloration, which is more pronounced than in other varieties of Dalechampia.
