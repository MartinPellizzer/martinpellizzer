Disa telipogonis

Disa telipogonis, commonly known as the Cape mountain catchfly, is a member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is native to the Western Cape region of South Africa.

It is a small, herbaceous plant with delicate, spurred flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous areas with a cool, moist climate. Its flowers are adapted for pollination by specific insects, and it is known for its ornamental value in horticulture.

Disa telipogonis is also considered a rare and endangered species due to habitat loss and limited distribution.
