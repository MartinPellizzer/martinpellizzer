Dactylorhiza incarnata subsp. foliosa

Dactylorhiza incarnata subsp.

foliosa, commonly known as the broad-leaf marsh orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in wet, calcareous habitats such as marshes and fen meadows.

The plant features a tall, unbranched stem with several broad, ovate leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. Its flowers are pink or purple, arranged in a loose spike and have a distinctive three-lobed lip.

This orchid is known for its ability to thrive in moist environments and is often found in association with other wetland flora.
