Delphinium mosoynense

Delphinium mosoynense, commonly known as the Mosoynense larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features tall, slender stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters, adorned with clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem, contributing to its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is relatively rare and considered a species of conservation concern in its native range.
