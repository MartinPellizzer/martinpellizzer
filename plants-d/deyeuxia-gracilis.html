Deyeuxia gracilis

Deyeuxia gracilis, commonly known as slender false rice, is a grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Deyeuxia gracilis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
