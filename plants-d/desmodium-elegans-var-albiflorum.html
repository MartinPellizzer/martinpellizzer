Desmodium elegans var. albiflorum

Desmodium elegans var. albiflorum, commonly known as the white-flowered velvet bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features trifoliate leaves with a velvety texture and small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated pods containing several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodland or grassland habitats.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it valuable for soil enrichment in agricultural settings.
