Dioscorea campestris var. parviflora

Dioscorea campestris var. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered yam, is a herbaceous perennial vine native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and tuberous roots. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on tendrils. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The tubers, which are the primary economic product, are used as a starchy food source in various parts of Africa and Asia.
