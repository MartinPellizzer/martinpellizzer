Dioscorea hassleriana

Dioscorea hassleriana, commonly known as the yellow wild yam, is a perennial vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and tuberous roots. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its tubers are rich in compounds such as diosgenin, which has been studied for potential medicinal properties.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and pharmacological contexts.
