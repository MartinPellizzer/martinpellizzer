Dyckia acrosoma

Dyckia acrosoma, commonly known as the red-tipped dyckia, is a striking bromeliad native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like rosette of narrow, dark green leaves that are tipped with vibrant red or orange hues. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences bearing small, colorful flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. Dyckia acrosoma thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

It is a popular choice for rock gardens and as a container plant due to its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance.
