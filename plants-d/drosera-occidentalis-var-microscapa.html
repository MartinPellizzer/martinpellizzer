Drosera occidentalis var. microscapa

Drosera occidentalis var. microscapa, commonly known as the miniature sundew, is a variant of the common sundew native to the western United States.

It is a small, carnivorous plant that thrives in wet, acidic environments such as bogs and marshes. The plant is characterized by its tiny, sticky glandular hairs that trap and digest insects. Its leaves are typically small and clustered, forming dense rosettes.

This variety is notable for its compact size and is often cultivated by enthusiasts due to its unique appearance and ecological role.
