Desmodium barbigerum

Desmodium barbigerum, commonly known as the hairy winged bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces and small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant produces pods containing several round, edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines. Desmodium barbigerum is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a useful cover crop.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a versatile plant in both agricultural and ecological contexts.
