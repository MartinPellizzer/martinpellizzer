Draba spatulata

Draba spatulata, commonly known as the spoonleaf whitlow grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a small, herbaceous perennial that typically grows in alpine and arctic regions. The plant has basal leaves that are spoon-shaped, giving it its common name, and it produces small white or pale yellow flowers in early spring. It is adapted to cold climates and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Draba spatulata is noted for its hardiness and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
