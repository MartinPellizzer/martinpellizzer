Diploglottis bernieana

Diploglottis bernieana, commonly known as the golden rain tree, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its palm-like leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers that resemble raindrops, giving it its common name. The plant belongs to the family Fabaceae and is known for its rapid growth and ornamental value. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and landscapes.

Its seeds are encased in a hard pod, which splits open when mature to release the seeds.
