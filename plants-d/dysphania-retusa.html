Dysphania retusa

Dysphania retusa, commonly known as water spinach or kangkong, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fleshy, edible stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often used in various cuisines. The plant thrives in moist, tropical environments and is typically grown in shallow water or wet soil. Dysphania retusa produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or yellowish in color.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is valued both for its nutritional content and culinary uses.
