Dortmanna breynii

Dortmanna breynii, commonly known as the Atlantic sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of the North Atlantic.

It is a perennial herb that thrives in sandy or gravelly soils, often found in maritime environments. The plant features bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and slightly lobed, adapted to withstand salty sea air and strong coastal winds.

Dortmanna breynii plays a role in stabilizing dune ecosystems and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
