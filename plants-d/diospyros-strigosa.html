Diospyros strigosa

Diospyros strigosa, commonly known as the rough persimmon, is a species of flowering plant in the Ebenaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including India, China, and Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are covered in a dense layer of short, stiff hairs. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant, and it produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically orange to reddish in color.

The fruit is edible when ripe, though it may have astringent properties when unripe.
