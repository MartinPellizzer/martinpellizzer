Dupatya incana

Dupatya incana, commonly known as the hairy dandelion, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including China and India.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which is characterized by its composite flower heads made up of many small florets. The plant is distinguished by its soft, hairy leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. It typically grows in moist, shaded areas and is often found in forests or along stream banks.

Dupatya incana is not widely cultivated but is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
