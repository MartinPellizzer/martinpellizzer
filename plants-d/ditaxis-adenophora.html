Ditaxis adenophora

Ditaxis adenophora, commonly known as the hairy sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils, often in coastal areas. The plant features small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple in color, arranged in dense clusters. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, contributing to its common name.

It is well adapted to arid environments and is often used in coastal restoration projects.
