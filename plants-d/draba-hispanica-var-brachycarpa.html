Draba hispanica var. brachycarpa

Draba hispanica var. brachycarpa, commonly known as the short-fruited rockcress, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine regions. Its leaves are basal, rosette-like, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are white or pale yellow.

The variety brachycarpa is distinguished by its shorter, more compact fruits compared to the typical form.
