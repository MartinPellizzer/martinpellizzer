Dendrobium chrysornis

Dendrobium chrysornis, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, cane-like stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, golden-yellow flowers that bloom during the summer months. Its leaves are typically elongated and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

Dendrobium chrysornis thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.
