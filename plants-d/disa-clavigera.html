Disa clavigera

Disa clavigera, commonly known as the sword lily, is a striking perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, narrow leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The flowers are typically pink or red with a yellow or orange throat, and they bloom in summer. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
