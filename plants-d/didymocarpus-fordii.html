Didymocarpus fordii

Didymocarpus fordii, commonly known as the Chinese tamarind tree, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and fragrant flowers. The tree produces small, yellowish fruits that are edible and have a tangy flavor. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for its fruit, which is used in traditional medicine.

The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
