Desmodium repandum

Desmodium repandum, commonly known as hairy vetch, is a leguminous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and clusters of small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it valuable in agricultural settings for soil enrichment. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often used as a cover crop or forage.

Its rapid growth and dense foliage also make it an effective weed suppressor in gardens and fields.
