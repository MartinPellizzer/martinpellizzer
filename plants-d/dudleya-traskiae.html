Dudleya traskiae

Dudleya traskiae, commonly known as Trask's liveforever, is a succulent plant native to California.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are thick and fleshy, adapted for water storage. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the spring. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for rock gardens and succulent collections.
