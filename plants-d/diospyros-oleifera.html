Diospyros oleifera

Diospyros oleifera, commonly known as the oil palm or African date palm, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its pinnately compound leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in panicles. The tree is notable for its edible fruits, which contain a fleshy, sweet pulp surrounding a hard, stony seed. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Diospyros oleifera is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in various African cultures.
