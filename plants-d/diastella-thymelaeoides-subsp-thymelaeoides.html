Diastella thymelaeoides subsp. thymelaeoides

Diastella thymelaeoides subsp.

thymelaeoides, commonly known as the yellow flame tree, is a striking tropical shrub or small tree native to eastern Australia. It is characterized by its clusters of bright yellow, lantern-shaped flowers that appear in spring and summer.

The plant has smooth, greyish bark and elliptical, dark green leaves that are often slightly hairy beneath. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

The species is noted for its rapid growth and ability to produce abundant floral displays.
