Deyeuxia purpurea var. langsdorffii

Deyeuxia purpurea var. langsdorffii, commonly known as purple false rice, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Asia.

It features slender, erect stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height, often with a reddish tinge. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and the plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
