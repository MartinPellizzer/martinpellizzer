Dioscorea beyrichii

Dioscorea beyrichii, commonly known as the African yambean, is a climbing herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its twining stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, white or purple flowers that are typically borne in clusters. Its tuberous roots are the primary part used, though they are not typically consumed by humans.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine.
