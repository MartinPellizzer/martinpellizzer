Dianthus lindbergii

Dianthus lindbergii, commonly known as Lindberg's campion, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes Mountains in South America.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its showy, clustered flowers and fleshy leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with five petals that are often adorned with distinctive red or purple markings.

Dianthus lindbergii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.
