Dioon spinulosum

Dioon spinulosum, commonly known as the Mexican bristlecone, is a rare and ancient cycad native to the Sierra Madre mountains of Mexico.

It is characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and large, feather-like leaves that can reach up to 2 meters in length. The plant produces distinctive, cone-like structures that contain both male and female reproductive organs. Dioon spinulosum is a slow-growing species that can live for hundreds of years, making it a symbol of resilience and longevity in arid environments.

Its unique morphology and ecological significance make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists alike.
