Dupatya flavidula

Dupatya flavidula, commonly known as the yellow-flowered orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, yellowish flowers with a subtle fragrance. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are adapted for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Dupatya flavidula is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
