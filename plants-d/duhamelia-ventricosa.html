Duhamelia ventricosa

Duhamelia ventricosa, commonly known as the woolly false hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy stems and broad, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic value and ecological benefits.
