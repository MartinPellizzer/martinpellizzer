Daviesia incrassata var. cylindrica

Daviesia incrassata var. cylindrica, commonly known as the cylindrical Daviesia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its dense, cylindrical clusters of small, pink to red flowers. The plant typically grows in heathland and sclerophyllous woodlands, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, and it is often found in coastal and inland regions of southern Australia.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting native wildlife.
