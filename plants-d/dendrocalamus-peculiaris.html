Dendrocalamus peculiaris

Dendrocalamus peculiaris, commonly known as the Burmese bamboo, is a tall, fast-growing grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its robust, hollow stems that can reach heights of up to 20 meters. The plant has a widespread distribution across countries like Myanmar, Thailand, and India, where it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. Its culms are highly valued for their strength and flexibility, making them useful in construction and handicrafts.

Additionally, it plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
