Dalea abietifolia

Dalea abietifolia, commonly known as the white prairie clover, is a flowering plant native to the central and northern Great Plains of North America.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies, grasslands, and disturbed soils. It has small, oval-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and produces legumes that contain seeds.

Dalea abietifolia plays a role in soil stabilization and provides nectar for pollinators.
