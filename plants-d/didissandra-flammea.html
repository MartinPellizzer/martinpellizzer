Didissandra flammea

Didissandra flammea, commonly known as the flamewood or firewood tree, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, reddish-barked trunk and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange-red fruits containing numerous seeds.

Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine, and the tree is valued for its ornamental appearance and ecological benefits.
