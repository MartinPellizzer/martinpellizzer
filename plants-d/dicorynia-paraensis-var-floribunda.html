Dicorynia paraensis var. floribunda

Dicorynia paraensis var. floribunda, commonly known as the Paraensis Flame Tree, is a species native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in dense clusters, creating a vibrant display. The plant has large, palmate leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been studied for its potential ecological and medicinal properties.
