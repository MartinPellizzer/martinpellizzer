Dichanthelium angustifolium

Dichanthelium angustifolium, commonly known as narrowleaf wiregrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant has slender, cylindrical leaves that are often covered with fine hairs, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a tufted growth habit.

It plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
