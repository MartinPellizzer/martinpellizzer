Delphinium hybridum

Delphinium hybridum, commonly known as the larkspur, is a tall, perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, spurred flowers that come in a wide range of colors, including blue, purple, pink, and white. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, with slender stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are arranged in spikes and are attractive to pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Delphinium hybridum thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
