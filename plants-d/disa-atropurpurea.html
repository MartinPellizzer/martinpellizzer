Disa atropurpurea

Disa atropurpurea, commonly known as the purple disa, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of vivid purple to pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, spurred orchid flowers with a distinctive lip. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and forest margins.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
