Delphinium drepanocentrum

Delphinium drepanocentrum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant produces striking blue to violet flowers in racemes, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough texture, often with a bluish-green hue.

Delphinium drepanocentrum is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and cooler climates, making it a hardy species in alpine environments.
