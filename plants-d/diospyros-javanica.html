Diospyros javanica

Diospyros javanica, commonly known as Java persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by edible, orange-red berries. Diospyros javanica is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine.

Its fruit is often consumed fresh or processed into jams and preserves.
