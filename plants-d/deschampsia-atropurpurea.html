Deschampsia atropurpurea

Deschampsia atropurpurea, commonly known as purple wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and dark purple to reddish hues on its leaves and inflorescence. The plant produces spikelets that are typically arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and rocky areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for native plant gardens and restoration projects.
