Dyckia fosteriana

Dyckia fosteriana, commonly known as the Foster's Dyckia, is a striking bromeliad native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its rosette of long, rigid, and often spiny leaves that can grow up to 3 meters in height. The plant produces tall, branching inflorescences that bear showy, brightly colored flowers, typically in shades of red, orange, or yellow. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
