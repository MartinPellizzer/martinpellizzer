Delphinium amabile subsp. pallidum

Delphinium amabile subsp.

pallidum, commonly known as the pale delphinium, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that bear large, showy blue to purple flowers.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to both wildflower meadows and cultivated landscapes.
