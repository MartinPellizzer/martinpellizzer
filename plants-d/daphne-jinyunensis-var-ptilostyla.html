Daphne jinyunensis var. ptilostyla

Daphne jinyunensis var. ptilostyla, commonly known as the hairy-styled daphne, is a rare and ornamental plant native to China.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fragrant, and often pink or white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and arranged alternately along the stems. Its unique floral structure and aromatic properties make it a sought-after species in horticulture.

However, it is noted for its toxicity, containing compounds that can be harmful if ingested.
