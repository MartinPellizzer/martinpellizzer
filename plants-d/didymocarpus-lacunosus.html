Didymocarpus lacunosus

Didymocarpus lacunosus, commonly known as the rough-leaved banyan, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, lobed leaves that have a rugged, uneven texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by woody, fleshy fruits. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important species in its native habitat.
