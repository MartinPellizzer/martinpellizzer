Dimorphanthus edulis

Dimorphanthus edulis, commonly known as the edible star-leaf plant, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its alternate, ovate leaves with a distinctive star-shaped venation pattern. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its edible leaves are often used in local cuisines for their mild, slightly sweet flavor.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
