Dischidia rhodantha

Dischidia rhodantha, commonly known as the Crimson Stem Dischidia, is a climbing or trailing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, succulent stems that are often red or purple in color. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and usually have a waxy texture. It produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, which are followed by round, red berries.

This plant is well adapted to humid, tropical environments and is often used in hanging baskets or as a ground cover in shaded areas.
