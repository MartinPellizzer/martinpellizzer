Dalea obovatifolia var. obovatifolia

Dalea obovatifolia var. obovatifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and bright yellow flowers. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is known for its nitrogen-fixing capabilities. Its small, pod-like fruits contain several seeds, and it plays a role in stabilizing arid landscapes.

The species is adapted to thrive in harsh, sun-exposed environments.
