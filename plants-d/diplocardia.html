Diplocardia

Diplocardia, commonly known as the "cabbage tree," is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in the spring. The plants are native to southern Africa and are often found in rocky or arid environments. They have a compact growth habit and are valued for their ornamental appeal.

Diplocardia species are generally drought-tolerant and require well-drained soil to thrive.
