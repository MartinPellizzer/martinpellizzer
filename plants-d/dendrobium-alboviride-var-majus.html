Dendrobium alboviride var. majus

Dendrobium alboviride var. majus, commonly known as the white-green orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is an epiphytic plant that grows on trees or rocks, relying on air and rain for nutrients. The plant features long, cane-like pseudobulbs covered in smooth, waxy leaves. Its flowers are typically white or pale green with a yellowish throat, and they bloom in clusters during the warm season.

It is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its elegant appearance.
