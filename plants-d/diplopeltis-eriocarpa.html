Diplopeltis eriocarpa

Diplopeltis eriocarpa, commonly known as the woolly podded orchid, is a species of orchid native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, greenish-brown stems and leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the wet season. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the humid environment, and it thrives in shaded, moist habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
