Deschampsia flexuosa var. grandiflora

Deschampsia flexuosa var. grandiflora, commonly known as the tall hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and fluffy, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and roadside ditches. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color that adds visual interest to wetland landscapes.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
