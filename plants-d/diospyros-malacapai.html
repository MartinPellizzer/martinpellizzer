Diospyros malacapai

Diospyros malacapai, commonly known as the Philippine persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate, ovate leaves that are often pubescent. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by edible, orange to reddish fruits with a sweet, astringent flavor. Its wood is valued for its density and durability, making it useful in traditional crafts and construction.

The plant is also noted for its ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
