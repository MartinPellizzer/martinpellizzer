Dianthus malacitanus var. stenolepis

Dianthus malacitanus var. stenolepis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved campion, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its pink to red flowers that bloom in summer, typically in clusters at the ends of stems. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or coastal landscapes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its vibrant, long-lasting blooms.
