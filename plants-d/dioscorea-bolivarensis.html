Dioscorea bolivarensis

Dioscorea bolivarensis, commonly known as the Bolivian yam, is a tuberous plant native to South America, particularly Bolivia and Peru.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its underground tubers, which are the primary edible part. The plant typically grows in humid, tropical environments and thrives in well-drained, loamy soils. Its leaves are heart-shaped and large, while its flowers are usually unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant.

The tubers of Dioscorea bolivarensis are valued for their nutritional content and traditional use in local cuisines.
