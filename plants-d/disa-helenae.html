Disa helenae

Disa helenae, commonly known as the Helen's Disa, is a striking orchid native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in late winter to early spring, producing vivid purple or pink petals. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Disa helenae is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in suitable conditions.
