Dactylorhiza majalis var. scotica

Dactylorhiza majalis var. scotica, commonly known as the Scottish orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Scottish Highlands.

It typically grows in acidic, moist, and shaded habitats such as peat bogs and heathlands. The plant features a slender, erect stem with several oval, dark green leaves. Its flowers are pale green to yellowish, with three sepals and three petals, and are arranged in a loose spike.

This variety is part of the Orchidaceae family and is notable for its ecological and botanical significance in its native environment.
