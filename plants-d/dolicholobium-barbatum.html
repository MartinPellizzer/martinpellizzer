Dolicholobium barbatum

Dolicholobium barbatum, commonly known as the hairy podded legume, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its typical pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features compound leaves with stipules and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in racemes. Its pods are elongated and hairy, giving it its common name, and the seeds are often used in traditional medicine.

Dolicholobium barbatum is also valued for its role in soil enrichment due to its leguminous nature.
