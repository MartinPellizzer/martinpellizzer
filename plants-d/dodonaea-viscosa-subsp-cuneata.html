Dodonaea viscosa subsp. cuneata

Dodonaea viscosa subsp.

cuneata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hoppegrow, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It features small, opposite, narrow ovate leaves with a leathery texture.

The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that are often obscured by the foliage. Its fruits are small, woody drupes that mature to a reddish color.

It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance and resilience.
