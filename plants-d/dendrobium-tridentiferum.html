Dendrobium tridentiferum

Dendrobium tridentiferum, commonly known as the three-toothed orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is an epiphytic plant, typically growing on trees or rocks in tropical rainforests. The plant features long, cane-like stems that can reach several meters in height, with clusters of fragrant, lance-shaped flowers. Its flowers are usually yellow or greenish-yellow with prominent red or orange markings.

Dendrobium tridentiferum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for horticultural purposes.
