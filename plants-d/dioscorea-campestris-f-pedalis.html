Dioscorea campestris f. pedalis

Dioscorea campestris f. pedalis, commonly known as the giant yam, is a variant of the yam species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, tuberous roots that can grow to impressive sizes, often reaching several kilograms in weight. The plant belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is cultivated for its edible tubers, which are rich in carbohydrates and nutrients. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, while its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant.

This variety is valued both for its agricultural potential and its ornamental appeal in gardens.
