Derris elegans var. gracillima

Derris elegans var. gracillima, commonly known as the slender derris, is a tropical climbing shrub native to regions of Southeast Asia.

It features slender, twining stems and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in racemes, followed by cylindrical, woody pods containing flat, kidney-shaped seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its alkaloid content.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
