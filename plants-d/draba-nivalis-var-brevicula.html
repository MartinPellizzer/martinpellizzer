Draba nivalis var. brevicula

Draba nivalis var. brevicula, commonly known as the dwarf snowdrop, is a low-growing perennial herb native to alpine and arctic regions.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in early spring. The plant has a compact rosette of basal leaves and produces white or pale yellow flowers with three petals. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold climates.

This variety is noted for its resilience and ability to survive in harsh, high-altitude environments.
