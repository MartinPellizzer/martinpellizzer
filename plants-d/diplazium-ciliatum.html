Diplazium ciliatum

Diplazium ciliatum, commonly known as the hairy sword fern, is a perennial fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its long, arching fronds with serrated margins. The fronds are typically dark green and have fine, hair-like structures along the veins, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The plant reproduces via spores and is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
