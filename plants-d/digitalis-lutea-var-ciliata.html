Digitalis lutea var. ciliata

Digitalis lutea var. ciliata, commonly known as the yellow foxglove, is a flowering plant native to the British Isles.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and tubular, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This plant is part of the Plantaginaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

It prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.
