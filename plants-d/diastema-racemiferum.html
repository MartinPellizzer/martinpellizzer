Diastema racemiferum

Diastema racemiferum, commonly known as the clustered diastema, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture and produces small, elliptical fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial shade, making it suitable for cultivation in shaded garden environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
