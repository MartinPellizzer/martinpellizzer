Dryades hirsuta

Dryades hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy dryad, is a species of flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun exposure.

Dryades hirsuta is often found in rocky or coastal areas, where it adapts well to harsh environmental conditions.
