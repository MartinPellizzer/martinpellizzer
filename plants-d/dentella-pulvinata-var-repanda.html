Dentella pulvinata var. repanda

Dentella pulvinata var. repanda, commonly known as the broad-leaved dandelion, is a variant of the dandelion species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces yellow composite flowers that develop into fluffy, white seed heads, which are adapted for wind dispersal. Its leaves are often slightly toothed, and the plant thrives in open, sunny environments.

It is a hardy perennial that is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils.
