Drypetes assamica

Drypetes assamica, commonly known as Assam flamewood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-yellow flowers followed by orange-red, fleshy fruits that are edible for some animals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
