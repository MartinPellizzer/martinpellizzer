Dypsis mangorensis

Dypsis mangorensis, commonly known as the Madagascar palm, is a tropical plant native to Madagascar.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family, which includes palms, and is characterized by its unique, tree-like structure with a short trunk and dense, rosette-like leaves. The plant features long, narrow, dark green leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem. Dypsis mangorensis produces small, fragrant white flowers that develop into orange-red berries, which are an important food source for local wildlife.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and adaptability to various growing conditions.
