Dictyopteris cumingiana

Dictyopteris cumingiana, commonly known as the Cuming's sea palm, is a species of green algae belonging to the family Dictyopteridaceae.

It is characterized by its complex, branching thallus with a distinctive, palm-like appearance. The plant forms dense, turfs of flattened, ribbon-like blades that are typically found in shallow, temperate coastal waters. It thrives in intertidal zones and is often associated with rocky substrates.

The reproductive structures of Dictyopteris cumingiana are small and embedded within the thallus, contributing to its reproductive strategy in marine environments.
