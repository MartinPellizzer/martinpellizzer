Dioscorea pusilla

Dioscorea pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf yam, is a small, herbaceous perennial vine native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers followed by tuberous roots that are often harvested for culinary or medicinal purposes. Dioscorea pusilla is adapted to grow in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
