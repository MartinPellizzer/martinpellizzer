Dieteria bigelovii var. mucronata

Dieteria bigelovii var. mucronata, commonly known as the Sierra Madre daisy, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy yellow to orange composite flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
