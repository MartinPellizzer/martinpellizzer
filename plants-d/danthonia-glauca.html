Danthonia glauca

Danthonia glauca, commonly known as blue danthonia, is a perennial grass species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and tufted growth habit, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a loose panicle with spikelets that are often tinged with purple or red.

Danthonia glauca plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates and birds.
