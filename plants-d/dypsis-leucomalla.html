Dypsis leucomalla

Dypsis leucomalla, commonly known as the white-veined areca palm, is a tropical plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and long, pinnate leaves that give it a graceful appearance. The leaves are typically light green with prominent white veins, contributing to its ornamental value. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil.

This plant is often cultivated as a decorative specimen in gardens and indoor spaces.
