Dichorisandra acaulis

Dichorisandra acaulis, commonly known as the blue ghost plant, is a striking member of the Acanthaceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique appearance and long-lasting blooms. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate sunlight to flourish.

Its common name reflects the ghostly appearance of its flowers under certain lighting conditions.
