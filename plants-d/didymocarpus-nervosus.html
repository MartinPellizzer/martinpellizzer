Didymocarpus nervosus

Didymocarpus nervosus, commonly known as the veined winged bean, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves with prominent venation. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds, which are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its nutritional value.

The species is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for agricultural systems.
