Dianthus carthusianorum subsp. carsticus

Dianthus carthusianorum subsp.

carsticus, commonly known as the Carpathian carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Carpathian Mountains. It is characterized by its striking pink to red flowers with prominent white or pale pink markings.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming a basal rosette.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
