Dioscorea hirtiflora

Dioscorea hirtiflora, commonly known as the hairy yam, is a flowering plant species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit, with stems that often twine around supporting structures. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences, and its fruits are capsules containing numerous seeds. Dioscorea hirtiflora is valued for its tuberous roots, which are used in traditional medicine and as a food source in some cultures.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
