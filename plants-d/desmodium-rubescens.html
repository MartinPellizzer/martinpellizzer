Desmodium rubescens

Desmodium rubescens, commonly known as the red clover or crimson bower, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are often tinged with red or purple hues, and its small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has a sprawling growth habit and produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Desmodium rubescens is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial in agricultural settings.
