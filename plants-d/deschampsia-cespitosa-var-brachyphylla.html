Deschampsia cespitosa var. brachyphylla

Deschampsia cespitosa var. brachyphylla, commonly known as the tufted hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as wet meadows, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

This variety is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
