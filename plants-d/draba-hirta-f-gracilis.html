Draba hirta f. gracilis

Draba hirta f. gracilis, commonly known as the slender mouse-ear cress, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically features a basal rosette of hairy, narrow leaves and produces clusters of yellow flowers in early spring. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Due to its compact size and hardy nature, it is a common ornamental plant in rock gardens and alpine settings.
