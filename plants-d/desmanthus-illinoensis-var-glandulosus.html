Desmanthus illinoensis var. glandulosus

Desmanthus illinoensis var. glandulosus, commonly known as the glandular desmanthus, is a perennial legume native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant is often found in open woodlands, prairies, and disturbed sites, thriving in well-drained soils. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, making it valuable for soil enrichment.

Its unique floral structure and growth habits make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
