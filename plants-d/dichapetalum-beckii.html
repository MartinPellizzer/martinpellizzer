Dichapetalum beckii

Dichapetalum beckii, commonly known as the African bellflower, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features a distinctive bell-shaped flower that ranges in color from white to pink, often with a yellowish throat. The plant has smooth, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Dichapetalum beckii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
