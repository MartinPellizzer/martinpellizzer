Disa barbata

Disa barbata, commonly known as the hairy disa, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, spurred flowers. The plant typically grows in grassland habitats and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers, which are usually pink or purple, bloom in summer and are attractive to pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Disa barbata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
