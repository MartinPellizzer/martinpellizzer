Draba alberti

Draba alberti, commonly known as the Albert's rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other mustard family plants. The plant is characterized by its low-growing habit, with basal leaves and small, white or pale yellow flowers. It thrives in rocky and alpine environments, often found in dry, well-drained soils.

Draba alberti is typically found at high elevations and is adapted to cold, harsh climates.
