Dypsis subacaulis

Dypsis subacaulis, commonly known as the Madagascar palm, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It is a succulent shrub characterized by its short, thick trunk and a crown of long, narrow leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. It is adapted to arid environments and requires well-draining soil to thrive.

Dypsis subacaulis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
