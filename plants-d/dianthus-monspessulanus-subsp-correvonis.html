Dianthus monspessulanus subsp. correvonis

Dianthus monspessulanus subsp.

correvonis, commonly known as the Corsican campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Corsica and Sardinia. It is a perennial herb characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of pink or red, spurred flowers.

The leaves are linear and often covered in fine hairs, while the stems are upright and branching. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens or alpine settings.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
