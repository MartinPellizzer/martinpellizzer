Disphyma australe subsp. australe

Disphyma australe subsp.

australe, commonly known as the coastal daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia. It is a low-growing shrub with narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers.

The plant is adapted to coastal environments, tolerating salt spray and sandy soils. It is often found in dunes and coastal heathlands, contributing to stabilizing dune systems.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in coastal ecosystems.
