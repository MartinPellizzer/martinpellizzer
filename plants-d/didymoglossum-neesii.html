Didymoglossum neesii

Didymoglossum neesii, commonly known as the narrow-leafed bog moss, is a small, mat-forming moss that thrives in wet, acidic environments such as bogs and marshes.

It has slender, often curved leaves that are typically less than 1 cm in length, with a distinctive elongated shape. The plant produces small, elongated capsules that are often covered in a thick layer of peristome teeth, which aid in spore dispersal. It is characterized by its ability to form dense, cushion-like growths that help stabilize soil in moist habitats.

Didymoglossum neesii is an important component of peat-forming ecosystems and is often found in temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.
