Diospyros antsirananae

Diospyros antsirananae, commonly known as the Madagascar persimmon, is a species of flowering plant in the Ebenaceae family.

It is native to the island of Madagascar and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, orange-red fruits with a sweet, astringent flavor. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid lowland forests.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species and is of interest for both ecological and potential medicinal research.
