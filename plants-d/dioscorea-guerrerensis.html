Dioscorea guerrerensis

Dioscorea guerrerensis, commonly known as the Guerrero yam, is a member of the Dioscoreaceae family and is native to Central America.

It is a climbing vine that produces tuberous roots, which are its primary botanical feature. The plant thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in shaded, forested areas. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and it bears small, greenish-white flowers.

The tubers of Dioscorea guerrerensis are used in traditional medicine and are valued for their nutritional and potential medicinal properties.
