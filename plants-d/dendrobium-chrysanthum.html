Dendrobium chrysanthum

Dendrobium chrysanthum, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a sympodial epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a compact growth habit with fleshy, often branched stems that store water and nutrients. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged along the stems, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Dendrobium chrysanthum is valued in horticulture for its long-lasting blooms and adaptability to various growing conditions.
