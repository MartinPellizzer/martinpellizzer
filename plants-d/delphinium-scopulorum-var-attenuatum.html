Delphinium scopulorum var. attenuatum

Delphinium scopulorum var. attenuatum, commonly known as the slender alpine larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers in racemes, which bloom in early summer. Its leaves are compound, with lanceolate leaflets that are often hairy and have a distinctive serrated margin.

This variety is adapted to rocky and alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun.
