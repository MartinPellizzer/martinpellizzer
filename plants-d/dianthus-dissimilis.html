Dianthus dissimilis

Dianthus dissimilis, commonly known as the slender campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its showy, pink or white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, while its stems are erect and branched.

Dianthus dissimilis is relatively easy to cultivate and is sometimes grown in gardens for its ornamental value.
