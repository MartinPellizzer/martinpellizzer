Dombeya tanganyikensis

Dombeya tanganyikensis, commonly known as the Lake Tanganyika dombeya, is a shrub native to the eastern African region, particularly around Lake Tanganyika.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often appearing in late spring to early summer. Dombeya tanganyikensis is adapted to grow in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested or woodland areas.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ornamental plant in its native habitat.
