Doronicum corsicum

Doronicum corsicum, commonly known as the Corsican bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Doronicum corsicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
