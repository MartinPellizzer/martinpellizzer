Diospyros aequoris subsp. tehuantepecensis

Diospyros aequoris subsp.

tehuantepecensis, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a species native to southern Mexico. It is a small to medium-sized deciduous tree with smooth, gray bark and elliptical leaves that turn reddish in autumn.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by orange to red, fleshy fruits that are edible when fully ripe. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in lowland tropical and subtropical regions.

The fruit is valued for its sweet, aromatic flavor and is used in both culinary and traditional medicinal applications.
