Diospyros dichrophylla

Diospyros dichrophylla, commonly known as the two-colored ebony, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its dark, glossy bark and elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into edible, reddish-brown drupes. Its fruit is often consumed fresh or used in traditional remedies.

Diospyros dichrophylla is valued for its hard wood and is also cultivated for its ornamental appeal.
