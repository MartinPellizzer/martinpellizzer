Ditulium

Ditulium, commonly known as the "false dandelion," is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its composite flower heads, which consist of both ray and disk flowers, and its basal rosette of leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny areas and is often found in disturbed soils. Its leaves are usually serrated and have a slightly hairy texture.

Ditulium species are known for their ability to thrive in a variety of climates, making them adaptable to different environments.
