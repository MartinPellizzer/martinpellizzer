Dichanthium magdalenae

Dichanthium magdalenae, commonly known as Magdalen's grass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect culms that can reach heights of up to 1 meter. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences with two or three spikelets, which are typically green to reddish in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green hue.

Dichanthium magdalenae is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, often found in alpine and subalpine environments.
