Dendrobium hawkesii

Dendrobium hawkesii, commonly known as the Hawkes' orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is an epiphytic plant that grows on trees and rocks, utilizing its aerial roots to absorb moisture and nutrients. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white, yellow, and pink, often with intricate patterns. It thrives in warm, humid climates with bright, indirect light.

Dendrobium hawkesii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is commonly cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
