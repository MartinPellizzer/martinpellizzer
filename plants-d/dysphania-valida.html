Dysphania valida

Dysphania valida, commonly known as redroot spinach, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves that are typically red-veined. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, and it is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides. Dysphania valida is known for its edible leaves, which have a slightly bitter taste and are used in some traditional cuisines.
