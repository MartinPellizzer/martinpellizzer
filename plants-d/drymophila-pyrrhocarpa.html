Drymophila pyrrhocarpa

Drymophila pyrrhocarpa, commonly known as the scarlet-berry holly, is a species of flowering plant in the family Ericaceae.

It is native to the southeastern United States, where it typically grows in moist, acidic soils within shaded forest understories. The plant is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in woodland gardens.
