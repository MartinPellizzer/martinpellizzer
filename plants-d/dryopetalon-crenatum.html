Dryopetalon crenatum

Dryopetalon crenatum, commonly known as the serrated bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, typically blooming in late summer. The plant has alternate, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and it grows from a fibrous root system. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
