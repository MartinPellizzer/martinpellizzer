Dalea oreophila

Dalea oreophila, commonly known as the mountain dalea, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and clusters of pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and dry environments, showcasing adaptations to arid conditions. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Dalea oreophila plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitats and provides nectar for pollinators.
