Draba cruciata

Draba cruciata, commonly known as the alpine mouse-ear cress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a small, herbaceous perennial that typically grows in alpine and subalpine regions. The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, spoon-shaped leaves and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers with four petals. It is adapted to cold climates and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils.

Draba cruciata is known for its ability to thrive in harsh environments, making it a characteristic species of high-altitude ecosystems.
