Dondia mexicana

Dondia mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its striking bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun, exhibiting drought tolerance. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces small, clustered flowers during the spring and summer months.

Dondia mexicana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
