Dracocephalum lamiifolium

Dracocephalum lamiifolium, commonly known as blue hyssop, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with blue or purple. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or violet, arranged in clusters at the ends of the branches. It is known for its aromatic properties and is often used in traditional herbal medicine.

Dracocephalum lamiifolium thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable plant for rock gardens and borders.
