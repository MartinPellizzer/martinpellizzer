Dracocephalum popovii

Dracocephalum popovii, commonly known as the Kashmir mint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features aromatic, lance-shaped leaves with a distinct minty scent, and its flowers are typically blue to violet in color. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of essential oils.

Dracocephalum popovii is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
