Delphinium hesperium subsp. pallescens

Delphinium hesperium subsp.

pallescens, commonly known as the pale delphinium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters.

The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers in raceme-like clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, with serrated edges, and the plant thrives in well-drained soils in full sun to partial shade.

It is often found in meadows, grasslands, and rocky areas, and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
