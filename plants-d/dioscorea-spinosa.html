Dioscorea spinosa

Dioscorea spinosa, commonly known as the spiny yam, is a perennial vine native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and hairy, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into cylindrical, yellowish fruits containing numerous seeds. Dioscorea spinosa is a member of the Dioscoreaceae family and is known for its tuberous roots, which are sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its growth habit and morphological features make it an important species in both ecological and ethnobotanical contexts.
