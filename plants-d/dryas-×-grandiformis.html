Dryas × grandiformis

Dryas × grandiformis, commonly known as the alpine bellflower, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Europe and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant features rosettes of silver-gray, hairy leaves and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to purple. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is cultivated in gardens with cold climates.
