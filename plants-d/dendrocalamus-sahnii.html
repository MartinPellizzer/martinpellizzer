Dendrocalamus sahnii

Dendrocalamus sahnii, commonly known as the bamboo of the Himalayas, is a tall, robust grass-like plant native to the mountainous regions of South and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that grow in clusters. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures. Its culms are often used in construction and handicrafts due to their strength and flexibility.

Dendrocalamus sahnii also plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
