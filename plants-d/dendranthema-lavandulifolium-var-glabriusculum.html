Dendranthema lavandulifolium var. glabriusculum

Dendranthema lavandulifolium var. glabriusculum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved chrysanthemum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features finely divided, silver-gray leaves that resemble lavender leaves, giving it its common name. The plant produces daisy-like yellow flowers in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long blooming period.
