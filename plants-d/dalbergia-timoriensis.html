Dalbergia timoriensis

Dalbergia timoriensis, commonly known as Timor rosewood, is a tropical tree native to the Indonesian island of Timor.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its dense, dark-colored wood that is highly valued for its beauty and workability. The plant typically grows in lowland and hill forest areas, often in moist, well-drained soils.

Its leaves are bipinnate, and it produces small, yellowish flowers followed by flat, pod-like fruits.
