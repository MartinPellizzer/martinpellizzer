Decorsea grandidieri

Decorsea grandidieri, commonly known as the Madagascar starfish plant, is a unique succulent native to the island of Madagascar.

It features striking, star-shaped leaves that are typically blue-green in color, with a waxy texture that helps it retain moisture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers during the warmer months, adding a subtle ornamental element to its appearance. Its thick, fleshy stems and leaves are adapted to store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native environment.
