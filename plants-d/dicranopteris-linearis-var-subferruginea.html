Dicranopteris linearis var. subferruginea

Dicranopteris linearis var. subferruginea, commonly known as the rusty shield fern, is a variant of the linear shield fern, characterized by its distinctive reddish-brown margins on the fronds.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Polypodiaceae, typically found in moist, shaded environments. The fronds are elongated and pinnately divided, with a leathery texture and a dark green to reddish-brown coloration. This variant is native to Southeast Asia and is often found growing on rocks or in forest understories.

Its unique coloration and morphology make it a notable species in both horticultural and ecological contexts.
