Desmodium cateniferum

Desmodium cateniferum, commonly known as the chainleaf burr, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with small, oval leaflets arranged in a distinctive chain-like pattern. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by hairy, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Desmodium cateniferum is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.
