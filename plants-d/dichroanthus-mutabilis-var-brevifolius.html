Dichroanthus mutabilis var. brevifolius

Dichroanthus mutabilis var. brevifolius, commonly known as the short-leaved golden ragwort, is a flowering plant native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers that appear in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
