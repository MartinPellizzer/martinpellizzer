Diotosperma

Diotosperma, commonly known as the African milk tree, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to parts of Africa and is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that store water, making it well adapted to arid environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers and is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique appearance. Its latex sap is milky and can be toxic, which contributes to its common name.

Diotosperma is also used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
