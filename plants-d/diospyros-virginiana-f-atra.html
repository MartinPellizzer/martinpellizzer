Diospyros virginiana f. atra

Diospyros virginiana f. atra, commonly known as the black persimmon, is a shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black bark and elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by orange to red berries that are edible when ripe. The fruit has a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor and is used in various culinary applications.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.
