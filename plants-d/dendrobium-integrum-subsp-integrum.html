Dendrobium integrum subsp. integrum

Dendrobium integrum subsp.

integrum, commonly known as the common dendrobium, is an epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia. It features a sympodial growth habit, with multiple pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients.

The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and orange, often in clusters. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.

Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.
