Dendrobium rugosum var. glaucophyllum

Dendrobium rugosum var. glaucophyllum, commonly known as the blue-leafed orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in humid, tropical environments. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often with a bluish tinge. Its leaves are broad and leathery, providing structural support and aiding in photosynthesis.

This variety is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
