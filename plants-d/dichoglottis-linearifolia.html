Dichoglottis linearifolia

Dichoglottis linearifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are borne on slender stems. Its rhizomes are used for their starchy roots, which are similar to arrowroot in texture.

Dichoglottis linearifolia thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
