Dubyaea cristulata

Dubyaea cristulata, commonly known as the crested dubyaea, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, spiky, or crested appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Dubyaea cristulata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
