Dichanthelium commutatum subsp. ashei

Dichanthelium commutatum subsp.

ashei, commonly known as Ashe's panicgrass, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils in open habitats such as prairies and woodland edges.

Its botanical features make it a valuable component of native plant communities and a subject of interest in ecological studies.
