Dactylorhiza × breviceras

Dactylorhiza × breviceras, commonly known as the narrow-leaved marsh orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as bogs, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant features erect, slender stems with narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, greenish or yellowish flowers clustered in spikes. It has a complex root system that includes both a central tuber and fibrous roots, aiding in nutrient absorption from its wetland environment.

This species is part of the Orchidaceae family and is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and maintaining wetland biodiversity.
