Draba bruniifolia subsp. bruniifolia

Draba bruniifolia subsp.

bruniifolia, commonly known as the alpine whitlow grass, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It typically forms dense mats of foliage and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in early spring.

The plant has a compact rosette structure with basal leaves that are typically glaucous and covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to cold, harsh environments.
