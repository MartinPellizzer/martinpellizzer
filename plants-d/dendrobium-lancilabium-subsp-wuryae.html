Dendrobium lancilabium subsp. wuryae

Dendrobium lancilabium subsp.

wuryae, commonly known as the Wurya's Dendrobium, is a rare orchid species native to parts of Southeast Asia. It is a sympodial epiphyte, typically growing on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments.

The plant produces long, slender pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, supporting its growth in nutrient-poor conditions. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to green, with a distinctive lip that is often darker in color.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
