Dupatya caespititia

Dupatya caespititia, commonly known as the tufted grass.

It is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia, particularly found in China and India. The plant forms dense clumps or tufts, giving it its common name, and has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands or along riverbanks.

Dupatya caespititia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its hardy and resilient nature.
