Dubyaea sonchoides

Dubyaea sonchoides, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows from a basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It is well adapted to drought conditions, with a deep taproot that allows it to access underground water.

Dubyaea sonchoides is often found in rocky soils and is a hardy species that thrives in harsh desert environments.
