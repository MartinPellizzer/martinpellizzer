Didymocarpus leptocalyx

Didymocarpus leptocalyx, commonly known as the slender-leaved flame tree, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are alternate and pinnate, with slender, lance-shaped leaflets. The plant produces striking, red-orange flowers that are clustered in panicles, blooming during the rainy season. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental value and is often planted in gardens and as a shade tree.
