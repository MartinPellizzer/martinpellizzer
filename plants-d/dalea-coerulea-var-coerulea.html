Dalea coerulea var. coerulea

Dalea coerulea var. coerulea, commonly known as bluebonnet, is a flowering plant native to the central and southern United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet pea-like flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in prairie and open woodland habitats.

The species is also notable for its role as the state flower of Texas.
