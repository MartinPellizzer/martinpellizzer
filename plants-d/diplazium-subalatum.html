Diplazium subalatum

Diplazium subalatum, commonly known as the silver fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to Southeast Asia.

It features a distinctive frond structure with a central stalk and multiple fiddlehead-like pinnae. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are typically covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it its common name.

Diplazium subalatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
