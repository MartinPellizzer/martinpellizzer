Dianthus villiger

Dianthus villiger, commonly known as the alpine pink, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen growth habit and produces clusters of showy, pink or red flowers during the summer months. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its stems are sturdy and often branched.

Dianthus villiger is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with similar growing conditions.
