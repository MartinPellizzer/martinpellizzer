Distictella broadwayana

Distictella broadwayana, commonly known as the Sydney golden wattle, is a species of flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which bloom in early spring. The plant has small, trifoliate leaves and is well adapted to the coastal and sandy soils of its native habitat. It plays an important role in the local ecosystem and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

The species is also recognized for its resilience to coastal conditions and salt spray.
