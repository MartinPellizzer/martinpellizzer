Dipsacus fullonum subsp. ferox

Dipsacus fullonum subsp.

ferox, commonly known as the devil's bit scabious, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in grasslands, woodlands, and disturbed soils.

The plant features upright stems covered in soft, woolly hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a distinctive silvery underside. Its flowers are blue to purple in color, forming dense spikes that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
