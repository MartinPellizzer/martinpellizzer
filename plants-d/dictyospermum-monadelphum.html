Dictyospermum monadelphum

Dictyospermum monadelphum, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has a woody stem with smooth, greenish-brown bark and produces small, round fruits that contain numerous seeds. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is adapted to arid environments and thrives in well-drained soils.
