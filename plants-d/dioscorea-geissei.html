Dioscorea geissei

Dioscorea geissei, commonly known as the African yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary part used for culinary and medicinal purposes. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers and cylindrical, ribbed fruits that contain numerous seeds. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough, hairy texture.

Dioscorea geissei is valued for its nutritional content and traditional use in various African cultures.
