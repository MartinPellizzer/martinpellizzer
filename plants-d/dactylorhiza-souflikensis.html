Dactylorhiza souflikensis

Dactylorhiza souflikensis, commonly known as the Soufli orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the mountainous regions of Greece.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in pine or oak forests. It has a symbiotic relationship with mycorrhizal fungi, which is essential for its nutrient uptake.

Dactylorhiza souflikensis is a rare and protected species, highlighting its ecological significance in its native habitat.
