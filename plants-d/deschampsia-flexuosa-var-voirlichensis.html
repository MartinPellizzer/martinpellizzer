Deschampsia flexuosa var. voirlichensis

Deschampsia flexuosa var. voirlichensis, commonly known as the Sierra bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and slender, arching stems that often form dense mats. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in mountainous regions, preferring open, sunny habitats. Its inflorescence consists of slender, spike-like clusters of small, spikelet-bearing flowers.

This variety is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife.
