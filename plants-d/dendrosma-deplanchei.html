Dendrosma deplanchei

Dendrosma deplanchei, commonly known as the Desert Honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing shrub with woody stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are adapted to conserve water. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in spring. Its floral structure is typical of the Lamiales order, featuring tubular corollas and stamens that are adapted for pollination by insects.
