Diospyros consolatae

Diospyros consolatae, commonly known as the Philippine persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and broad, heart-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by oval, orange to reddish fruits. The fruit is typically harvested when it turns deep red, and it has a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
