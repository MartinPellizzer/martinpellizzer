Dianthus hyssopifolius subsp. gallicus

Dianthus hyssopifolius subsp.

gallicus, commonly known as the French thimbleweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe. It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit.

The plant produces clusters of pink or purple flowers that bloom in spring, adding ornamental value to gardens. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, while its stems are upright and sturdy.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and borders.
