Diospyros xavantina

Diospyros xavantina, commonly known as the Xavantina persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other well-known species like the ebony tree. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a leathery texture. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically greenish-yellow in color, and it produces small, round fruits that are typically harvested when fully ripe.

The fruit has a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor and is used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
