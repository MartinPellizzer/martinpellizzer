Dolichos dillonii

Dolichos dillonii, commonly known as the Dillon's climbing bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that give way to elongated, bean-like pods containing several seeds. Dolichos dillonii is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and thrives in warm, humid climates.

It is often cultivated for its edible seeds, which are rich in protein and used in various traditional dishes.
