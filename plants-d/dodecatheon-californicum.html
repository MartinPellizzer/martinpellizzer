Dodecatheon californicum

Dodecatheon californicum, commonly known as the California glory-of-the-snow, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 15 to 30 centimeters. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, white or pale pink flowers. It blooms in early spring, often before the leaves fully emerge, and relies on pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in moist, open habitats like meadows and woodlands.
