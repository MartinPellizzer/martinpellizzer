Diplanthemum viridiflorum

Diplanthemum viridiflorum, commonly known as the green-flowered rock cress, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with small, lance-shaped leaves and bright green flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions. Its flowers are typically borne in clusters and have four petals, which are usually a shade of green or yellow.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
