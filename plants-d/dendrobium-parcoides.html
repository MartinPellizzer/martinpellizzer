Dendrobium parcoides

Dendrobium parcoides, commonly known as the orchid of the genus Dendrobium, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is an epiphytic plant native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand and Malaysia. The plant features a sympodial growth habit with multiple stems that often branch out horizontally. Its flowers are typically small and come in a range of colors, including white, yellow, and pink.

Dendrobium parcoides thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
