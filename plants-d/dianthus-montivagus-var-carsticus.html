Dianthus montivagus var. carsticus

Dianthus montivagus var. carsticus, commonly known as the Carpathian campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Carpathian Basin.

It is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine habitats. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are slender and often branched.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
