Dendrobium pictum

Dendrobium pictum, commonly known as the orchid with the painted lips, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a unique appearance with its striking, colorful flowers that often have intricate patterns resembling lips or veins. The plant grows on trees or rocks, absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in its natural habitat.

Dendrobium pictum is prized for its ornamental value and is commonly cultivated for its long-lasting blooms.
