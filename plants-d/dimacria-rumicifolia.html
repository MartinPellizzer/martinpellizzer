Dimacria rumicifolia

Dimacria rumicifolia, commonly known as the hairy leaf spurge, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, alternate leaves that are typically lance-shaped and have a rough texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks.
