Delonix baccal

Delonix baccal, commonly known as the flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The tree has compound leaves with multiple leaflets and produces pods that contain edible seeds.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
