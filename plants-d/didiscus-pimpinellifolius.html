Didiscus pimpinellifolius

Didiscus pimpinellifolius, commonly known as the African eggplant, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshade plants. The plant is characterized by its small, round, egg-shaped fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature. Its leaves are palmately lobed, with a rough texture and a strong, spicy scent.

The plant is often grown for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses in various cultures.
