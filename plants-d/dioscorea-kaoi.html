Dioscorea kaoi

Dioscorea kaoi, commonly known as the Kaoi yam, is a climbing vine native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tendrils that help it climb towards sunlight. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by tuberous roots that are the primary part used. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are hairy and woody.

Dioscorea kaoi is adapted to the unique ecological conditions of Hawaii, making it an important species in local biodiversity.
