Draba australis f. uruguayana

Draba australis f. uruguayana, commonly known as the Uruguay draba, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May in its native habitat. Its leaves are basal, linear, and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is adapted to rocky and sandy soils, thriving in arid to semi-arid environments.
