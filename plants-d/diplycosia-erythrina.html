Diplycosia erythrina

Diplycosia erythrina, commonly known as the red flame tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall, featuring a straight trunk and a broad, umbrella-like canopy. The leaves are pinnately compound, with elongated leaflets that are often reddish when young. Its most striking feature is its showy, bright red or orange flowers that appear in clusters during the summer months.

The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
