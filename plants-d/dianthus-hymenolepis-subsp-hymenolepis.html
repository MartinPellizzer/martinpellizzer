Dianthus hymenolepis subsp. hymenolepis

Dianthus hymenolepis subsp.

hymenolepis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer.

The leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, with a distinctive blue-green color. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, dry seeds.

It is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
