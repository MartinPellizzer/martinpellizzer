Dianthus giganteiformis subsp. kladovanus

Dianthus giganteiformis subsp.

kladovanus, commonly known as the Kladovan's giant campion, is a striking perennial plant native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a distinctive red or purple hue at the base.

The plant features robust, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens or alpine displays.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and adaptation to harsh mountain environments.
