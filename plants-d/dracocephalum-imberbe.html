Dracocephalum imberbe

Dracocephalum imberbe, commonly known as narrow-leaved blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Dracocephalum imberbe is typically found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
