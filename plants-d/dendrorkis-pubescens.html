Dendrorkis pubescens

Dendrorkis pubescens, commonly known as the hairy tree fern, is a distinctive member of the Cyatheaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of feathery, pinnate fronds that give it a graceful appearance. The plant has a fibrous root system and produces spores on the undersides of its fronds, which are essential for its reproductive cycle. Native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important species in its natural habitat.
