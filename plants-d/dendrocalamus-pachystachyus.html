Dendrocalamus pachystachyus

Dendrocalamus pachystachyus, commonly known as the giant bamboo, is a tall, fast-growing grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its robust, hollow stems that can reach heights of over 30 meters. The plant has a sympodial growth pattern, with new shoots emerging from the base of the plant. Its leaves are long, narrow, and often covered in a waxy cuticle, which helps reduce water loss in its native humid environments.

This species is highly valued for its strong, lightweight bamboo, which is used in construction, crafts, and as a source of fiber.
