Drymocallis glandulosa var. reflexa

Drymocallis glandulosa var. reflexa, commonly known as the silverleaf goldenrod, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and bright yellow inflorescences that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and is often found in moist to wet soils in woodlands and wetlands. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is known for its medicinal properties historically used by Native American tribes.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
