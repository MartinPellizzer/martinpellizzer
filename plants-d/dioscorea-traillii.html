Dioscorea traillii

Dioscorea traillii, commonly known as Traill's yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers and elongated, cylindrical fruits. Its leaves are heart-shaped with prominent veins, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its medicinal properties, it is of interest in both botanical research and pharmacology.
