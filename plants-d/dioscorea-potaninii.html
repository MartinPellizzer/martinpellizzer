Dioscorea potaninii

Dioscorea potaninii, commonly known as the Chinese yam, is a perennial vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary part used for medicinal and culinary purposes. The plant has climbing stems with heart-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers. Its tubers are valued for their nutritional content and traditional medicinal properties.

Dioscorea potaninii is often cultivated for its high starch content and potential health benefits.
