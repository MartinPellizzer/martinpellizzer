Digitalis parviflora

Digitalis parviflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved foxglove, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features tall, erect stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of purple and pink. The plant's leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in woodland and shady areas, preferring moist, well-drained soil.

Digitalis parviflora is part of the Plantaginaceae family and is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in medicine.
