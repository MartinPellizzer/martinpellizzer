Dioclea trujillensis

Dioclea trujillensis, commonly known as the Trujillo passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America, particularly found in the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Passifloraceae family, which is characterized by its showy, complex flowers. The plant produces striking flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments, often in bright colors like red and yellow. Its fruits are fleshy berries that contain inedible seeds, and the plant is often used in traditional medicine.

Dioclea trujillensis plays an important ecological role by providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for various wildlife.
