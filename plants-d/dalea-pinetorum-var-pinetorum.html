Dalea pinetorum var. pinetorum

Dalea pinetorum var. pinetorum, commonly known as the pine dalea, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, pink to reddish-purple flowers. The plant typically grows in open woods and pine flatwoods, where it thrives in well-drained sandy soils. Its small, trifoliate leaves and distinctive bloom structure make it a notable component of the region's flora.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and contributes to the ecological diversity of its habitat.
