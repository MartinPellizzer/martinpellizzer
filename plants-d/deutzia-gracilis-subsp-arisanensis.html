Deutzia gracilis subsp. arisanensis

Deutzia gracilis subsp.

arisanensis, commonly known as the Arisan deutzia, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan. It features slender, arching stems with clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant has dark green, elliptical leaves that turn yellow in autumn. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, moist soil.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its delicate floral display.
