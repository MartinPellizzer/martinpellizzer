Daphne purpurascens

Daphne purpurascens, commonly known as the purpleleaf daphne, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its attractive, fragrant flowers that range from white to pink and purple, often appearing in late winter to early spring. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are typically gray-green with a slight pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Daphne purpurascens is also noted for its toxicity, with all parts of the plant containing toxic compounds that can be harmful to humans and animals.
