Diodia dichotoma

Diodia dichotoma, commonly known as redroot morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers that bloom in the morning, giving it its common name. Its roots are reddish in color and contain compounds that have been used in traditional medicine.

Diodia dichotoma thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.
