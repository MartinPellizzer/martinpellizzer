Delphinium pubescens subsp. loscosii

Delphinium pubescens subsp.

loscosii, commonly known as the Sierra delphinium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It typically grows in moist meadows and forested areas, thriving in cool, temperate climates.

The plant features tall, slender stems that bear clusters of blue to purple flowers, which are characteristic of the delphinium genus. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its common name "pubescens," meaning hairy.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
