Dalea leucantha

Dalea leucantha, commonly known as white prairie clover, is a flowering plant native to the central and southern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies and sandy soils. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Dalea leucantha is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
