Delphinium thamarae

Delphinium thamarae, commonly known as the Thamarae larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is known for its showy, spurred petals that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Delphinium thamarae typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky soils, thriving in cool, moist environments.
