Dianella tasmanica

Dianella tasmanica, commonly known as the Tasmanian lily, is a perennial evergreen plant native to Tasmania and parts of southeastern Australia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically a deep green color, and it produces striking blue to purple flowers in late winter to early spring. The plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky outcrops. It has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

Dianella tasmanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also an important component of native ecosystems.
