Diospyros eburnea

Diospyros eburnea, commonly known as the ivory fruit or snow apple, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and elliptical leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into edible, round fruits with a sweet, milky pulp. The fruit is often consumed fresh or used in traditional remedies.

Its wood is also valued for its fine grain and durability, making it useful in craftsmanship.
