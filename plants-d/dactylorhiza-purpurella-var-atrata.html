Dactylorhiza purpurella var. atrata

Dactylorhiza purpurella var. atrata, commonly known as the purple orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of purple to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors or wet meadows. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are pollinated by insects.

This orchid is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
