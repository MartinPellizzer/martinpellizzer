Dondodia erosa

Dondodia erosa, commonly known as the slender dodonaea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub characterized by its slender, often branching stems and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, yellowish-green flowers that are typically borne in dense inflorescences. Dondodia erosa is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a subject of interest in botanical studies.
