Dyschoriste geniculata

Dyschoriste geniculata, commonly known as the zigzag dyschoriste, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its distinctive zigzagging stems, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming during the rainy season. Its leaves are opposite and elliptical, often with a rough texture.

Dyschoriste geniculata is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks.
