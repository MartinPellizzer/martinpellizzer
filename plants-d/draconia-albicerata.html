Draconia albicerata

Draconia albicerata, commonly known as the white-flowered dragonroot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of Southeast Asia.

It features a robust rhizomatous root system and lance-shaped, dark green leaves with a smooth, waxy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers during the spring and early summer. Its botanical significance lies in its unique floral structure and adaptability to moist, shaded environments.

It is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
