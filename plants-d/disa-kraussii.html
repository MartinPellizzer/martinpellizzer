Disa kraussii

Disa kraussii, commonly known as the Krauss's orchid, is a species of orchid native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers with contrasting yellow markings. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It belongs to the subfamily Orchidoideae and is part of the genus Disa, which includes over 100 species.

The flowers are often used in horticulture for their ornamental value and are adapted for pollination by specific insects.
