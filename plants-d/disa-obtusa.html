Disa obtusa

Disa obtusa, commonly known as the broad-leaved disa, is a species of orchid native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is a perennial herb with a short-lived rhizome, and it blooms during the summer months.

Disa obtusa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
