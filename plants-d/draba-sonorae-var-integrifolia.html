Draba sonorae var. integrifolia

Draba sonorae var. integrifolia, commonly known as the Sonoran Draba, is a flowering plant native to the Sonoran Desert region.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It has basal leaves that are entire, giving it the specific epithet *integrifolia*.

This variant is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.
