Delphinium maackianum f. albiflorum

Delphinium maackianum f. albiflorum, commonly known as the white-flowered delphinium, is a cultivar of the delphinium genus native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems that bear clusters of large, showy flowers. The flowers are typically blue to purple in color, but this particular form is distinguished by its predominantly white petals. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking dramatic floral displays.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in perennial borders and cottage gardens.
