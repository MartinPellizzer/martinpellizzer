Dendrophthoe luzonensis

Dendrophthoe luzonensis, commonly known as the Luzon mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant native to the Philippines.

It attaches itself to the branches of host trees, primarily dipterocarps, and derives some of its nutrients from the host through specialized root structures called haustoria. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers and red berries that are dispersed by birds. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth texture.

Dendrophthoe luzonensis plays a role in the ecological dynamics of its forest habitat by influencing host tree growth and providing food for various wildlife species.
