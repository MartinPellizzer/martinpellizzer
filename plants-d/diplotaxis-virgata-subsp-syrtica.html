Diplotaxis virgata subsp. syrtica

Diplotaxis virgata subsp.

syrtica, commonly known as desert cress, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North Africa and the Middle East. It is a low-growing annual herb with narrow, fleshy leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant is adapted to harsh environments, featuring deep root systems and a capacity to store water in its leaves. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants.

Its seeds are often dispersed by wind, aiding in its survival in open, dry habitats.
