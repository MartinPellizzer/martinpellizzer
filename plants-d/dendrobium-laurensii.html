Dendrobium laurensii

Dendrobium laurensii, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a epiphytic orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, cane-like pseudobulbs and clusters of showy, yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light. It requires well-draining potting media and regular watering to prevent root rot.

Dendrobium laurensii is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its vibrant, long-lasting blooms.
