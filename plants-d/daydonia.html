Daydonia

Daydonia, commonly known as the false dandelion, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

It typically features basal rosettes of leaves and produces yellow or white flowers with ray florets. The plant is native to various regions in Africa, Asia, and Australia. Daydonia species are often found in arid or semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

Some species are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
