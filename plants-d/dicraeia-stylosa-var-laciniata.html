Dicraeia stylosa var. laciniata

Dicraeia stylosa var. laciniata, commonly known as the lace-leaf mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees.

It is native to the southeastern United States, particularly in Florida and Georgia, where it thrives in humid, tropical climates. The plant has small, elongated leaves that are deeply lobed, giving it a delicate, lace-like appearance. It produces small, white or pink flowers that are typically pollinated by insects.

Its berries, which are red and fleshy, are an important food source for various bird species.
