Dasiphora dryadanthoides

Dasiphora dryadanthoides, commonly known as the western false poppy, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its showy, yellow to orange flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are trifoliate, meaning they are divided into three leaflets, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds.

The species is adapted to arid environments and has a long blooming period in the spring and early summer.
