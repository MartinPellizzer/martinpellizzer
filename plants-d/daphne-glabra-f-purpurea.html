Daphne glabra f. purpurea

Daphne glabra f. purpurea, commonly known as the purpleleaf daphne, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, leathery leaves that are typically a deep purple to dark green in color, adding visual interest to gardens. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white to pale pink, often appearing in late winter or early spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its toxic sap, it should be handled with care and kept away from children and pets.
