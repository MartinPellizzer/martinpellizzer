Dichaea squarrosa

Dichaea squarrosa, commonly known as the squarrosa orchid, is a member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid with a short, creeping rhizome and a rosette of fleshy, dark green leaves. The plant produces delicate, pale yellow to greenish-white flowers with a distinctive, elongated spur. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and orchid enthusiasts.
