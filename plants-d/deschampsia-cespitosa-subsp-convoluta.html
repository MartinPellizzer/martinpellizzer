Deschampsia cespitosa subsp. convoluta

Deschampsia cespitosa subsp.

convoluta, commonly known as hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America. It forms dense, tufted clumps with slender, wiry stems that often curl in a spiral, giving it its common name.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows, marshes, and along stream banks.

Its ecological importance includes stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
