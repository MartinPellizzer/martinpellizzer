Desmodium ramosissimum

Desmodium ramosissimum, commonly known as the hairy sicklepod, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, also known as the legume or bean family, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in disturbed soils, making it a common weed in agricultural settings. Its seeds are encased in a distinctive, spiny pod that splits open when mature, dispersing the seeds effectively.

Desmodium ramosissimum is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which can improve soil fertility.
