Daphniphyllum scortechinii

Daphniphyllum scortechinii, commonly known as the Japanese holly or mountain holly, is a shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, glossy leaves that are typically dark green. The plant produces clusters of white, fragrant flowers in the spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Daphniphyllum scortechinii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in woodland gardens.
