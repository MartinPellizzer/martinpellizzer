Diploglottis alaticarpa

Diploglottis alaticarpa, commonly known as the winged fruit tree, is a species native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree with smooth, greyish-brown bark. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by distinctive, winged fruits that are typically 5-10 cm in length. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a glossy surface, often with prominent veins.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some local cultures.
