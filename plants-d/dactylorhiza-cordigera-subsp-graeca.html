Dactylorhiza cordigera subsp. graeca

Dactylorhiza cordigera subsp.

graeca, commonly known as the Greek marsh orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Mediterranean region. It is a terrestrial herb that typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, often near water bodies.

The plant features a slender, upright stem with several narrow, linear leaves. Its flowers are pale green to yellowish, arranged in a loose spike, and are characterized by their distinctive, spurred lip.

This subspecies is part of the Orchidaceae family and is noted for its ornamental value and ecological importance in its native habitat.
