Dioscorea zollingeriana

Dioscorea zollingeriana, commonly known as the Zollinger yam, is a member of the Dioscoreaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its tuberous roots, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a rough texture, and it thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil.

The tubers of D. zollingeriana are valued for their medicinal properties and are sometimes used as a food source in certain cultures.
