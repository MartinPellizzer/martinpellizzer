Diospyros havilandii

Diospyros havilandii, commonly known as the Haviland's persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the southern United States.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other persimmon and ebony species. The plant is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. Its small, yellowish-green flowers appear in clusters and are followed by edible, orange-red berries that are astringent when unripe.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
