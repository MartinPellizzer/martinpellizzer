Diospyros kerrii

Diospyros kerrii, commonly known as Kerri's persimmon, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes ebony and persimmon species. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptic to ovate in shape. Its small, yellowish flowers are followed by orange to red berries that are edible when ripe.

The wood of Diospyros kerrii is hard and durable, making it valuable for crafting and traditional uses.
