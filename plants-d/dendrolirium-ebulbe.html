Dendrolirium ebulbe

Dendrolirium ebulbe, commonly known as the fragrant orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped leaves and striking, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often near streams or in dense forest undergrowth. Its flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer, are usually pale purple or white with a strong, sweet scent.

Dendrolirium ebulbe is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its historical use in traditional medicine.
