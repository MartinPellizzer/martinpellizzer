Decaspermum humile

Decaspermum humile, commonly known as the small-fruited decaspermum, is a low-growing, herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal or arid regions. The plant features small, clustered flowers that are usually pale yellow to greenish in color. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often arranged in whorls around the stem.

Decaspermum humile is noted for its resilience to drought and salt spray, making it a hardy species adapted to harsh environments.
