Dodonaea viscosa f. lilacina

Dodonaea viscosa f. lilacina, commonly known as the silverleaf dodonaea, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale lilac, depending on the variety. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid regions.

The leaves are often used in traditional medicine and are also popular in ornamental gardening due to their aesthetic appeal.
