Deschampsia christophersenii

Deschampsia christophersenii, commonly known as the desert bluegrass, is a low-growing perennial grass native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or gravelly soils. The plant has narrow, blue-green leaves and produces slender, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late spring or early summer. It is adapted to withstand drought and temperature extremes, making it a hardy species in harsh conditions.

Its fine texture and color make it an attractive choice for xeriscaping and restoration projects in dry landscapes.
