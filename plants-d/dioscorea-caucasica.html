Dioscorea caucasica

Dioscorea caucasica, commonly known as the Caucasian yam, is a perennial vine native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and tuberous roots. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its tubers are used in traditional medicine and as a food source in some regions.

The botanical features of D. caucasica make it an important species in both ecological and ethnobotanical contexts.
