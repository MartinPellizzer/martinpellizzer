Dendrocalamus sinicus

Dendrocalamus sinicus, commonly known as the Chinese bamboo or giant bamboo, is a tall, fast-growing grass native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its strong, hollow stems that can reach heights of over 20 meters. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically green and arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem. It is widely cultivated for its use in construction, papermaking, and as a source of bamboo shoots.

Dendrocalamus sinicus is also valued for its ecological role in preventing soil erosion and supporting biodiversity in its native habitats.
