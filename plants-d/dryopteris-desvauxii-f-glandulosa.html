Dryopteris desvauxii f. glandulosa

Dryopteris desvauxii f. glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular wood fern, is a fern species characterized by its distinctive fronds and unique botanical features.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is native to certain regions of Southeast Asia. The plant exhibits a compact growth habit with fronds that are often dimorphic, featuring both fertile and sterile leaves. Its leaves are typically covered in small, glandular dots, which give it its common name.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shady garden environments.
