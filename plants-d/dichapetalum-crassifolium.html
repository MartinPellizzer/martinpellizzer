Dichapetalum crassifolium

Dichapetalum crassifolium, commonly known as the African milk tree, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically broad and elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its bark and latex have been used traditionally for their medicinal properties.

The tree is adapted to dry conditions and can thrive in well-drained soils.
