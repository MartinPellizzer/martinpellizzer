Deilanthe thudichumii

Deilanthe thudichumii, commonly known as the Thudichum's dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, fleshy leaves and a single, upright stem that bears a large, yellow composite flower head. Native to the high-altitude regions of southern Africa, it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant is notable for its ability to survive in harsh, arid environments due to its succulent leaves and deep root system.

Its flowers bloom in spring, producing a showy display that attracts various pollinators.
