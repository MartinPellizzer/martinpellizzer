Dicliptera adusta

Dicliptera adusta, commonly known as the blackbeard grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and dark green, often hairy leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread vegetatively and establish in a variety of soil types.

Dicliptera adusta is often found in disturbed areas, such as roadsides and agricultural fields, where it can become invasive.
