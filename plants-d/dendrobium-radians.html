Dendrobium radians

Dendrobium radians, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with numerous, fleshy, cylindrical pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange, often with intricate patterns. Its leaves are typically broad and grass-like, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Dendrobium radians is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
