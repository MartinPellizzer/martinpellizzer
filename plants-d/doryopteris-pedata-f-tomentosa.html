Doryopteris pedata f. tomentosa

Doryopteris pedata f. tomentosa, commonly known as the tomentose cliff fern, is a fern species characterized by its distinctive fronds and growth habits.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is native to regions with moist, shaded environments. The plant features lanceolate, serrated leaves that are typically covered in a dense, woolly layer of trichomes, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in rocky or crevice habitats, often found in mountainous areas.

This fern is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
