Dicalix punctomarginatus

Dicalix punctomarginatus, commonly known as the spotted leaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive foliage with yellowish spots along the margins. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid forest environments. Its flowers are small and clustered, often displaying a mix of white and green hues.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a subject of interest in both botanical research and horticultural cultivation.
