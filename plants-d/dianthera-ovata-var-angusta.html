Dianthera ovata var. angusta

Dianthera ovata var. angusta, commonly known as the narrow-leaf dianthera, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States.

It features lance-shaped, narrow leaves that are typically covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, pale blue to violet flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its botanical characteristics make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ecological studies.
