Dryopteris filix-mas subsp. caucasica

Dryopteris filix-mas subsp.

caucasica, commonly known as the Caucasian shield fern, is a perennial fern native to the Caucasus region. It features fronds that are typically bipinnate, with a distinctive, shield-shaped appearance.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds, and it has a robust rhizome system that aids in its growth.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
