Dalea capitata var. quinqueflora

Dalea capitata var. quinqueflora, commonly known as the five-flowered prairie clover, is a perennial herb native to the central United States.

It is characterized by its compact, sparsely branched stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils. It has trifoliate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

The species is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its use in native plant gardens.
