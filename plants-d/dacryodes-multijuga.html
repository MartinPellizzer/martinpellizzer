Dacryodes multijuga

Dacryodes multijuga, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a tropical tree native to western and central Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, dark-colored wood, which is highly valued for its durability and strength. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptic to ovate in shape, with a smooth upper surface and hairy underside. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by small, woody fruits.

The tree is often found in moist evergreen forests and plays an important ecological role in its native habitat.
