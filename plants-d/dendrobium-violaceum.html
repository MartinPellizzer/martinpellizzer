Dendrobium violaceum

Dendrobium violaceum, commonly known as the violet orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is an epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions like Thailand, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant produces striking violet or purple flowers that bloom during the rainy season. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in its natural habitat.

Dendrobium violaceum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for horticultural purposes.
