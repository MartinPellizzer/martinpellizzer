Duranta repens var. multidentata

Duranta repens var. multidentata, commonly known as the silverleaf duranta, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It is a trailing or climbing shrub that produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers, typically in shades of blue or violet. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a silvery or grayish underside, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and seasonal blooms.

The plant is also valued for its ability to attract birds and other pollinators.
