Diasperus brunneus

Diasperus brunneus, commonly known as the brown sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, often hairy, gray-green foliage. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, arranged in clusters at the ends of its branches. It thrives in arid or semi-arid environments and is adapted to sandy soils.

Diasperus brunneus is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
