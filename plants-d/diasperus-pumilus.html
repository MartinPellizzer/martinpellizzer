Diasperus pumilus

Diasperus pumilus, commonly known as the dwarf rock cress, is a small, flowering plant native to arid and rocky regions.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with characteristic cruciform flowers. The plant typically grows close to the ground, with hairy, narrow leaves that help reduce water loss. Its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in racemes, often blooming in spring.

Diasperus pumilus is adapted to harsh environments, making it a resilient and hardy species.
