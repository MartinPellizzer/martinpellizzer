Drosera filiformis var. typica

Drosera filiformis var. typica, commonly known as the slender sundew, is a species of carnivorous plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its small, thread-like leaves that secrete a sticky substance to trap insects. The plant typically grows in wet, acidic environments such as bogs and heathlands. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, often appearing in clusters.

It is a low-growing plant that thrives in humid conditions and requires consistent moisture for optimal growth.
