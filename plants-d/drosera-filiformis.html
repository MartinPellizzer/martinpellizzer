Drosera filiformis

Drosera filiformis, commonly known as the slender sundew, is a carnivorous plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its delicate, thread-like leaves that secrete a sticky substance to trap insects. The plant typically grows in wet, boggy environments and thrives in humid conditions. Its small, white flowers bloom in late spring or early summer.

Drosera filiformis is a popular species among carnivorous plant enthusiasts due to its unique appearance and ease of cultivation.
