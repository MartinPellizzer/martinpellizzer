Diplusodon incanus var. hirtellus

Diplusodon incanus var. hirtellus, commonly known as the hairy antelope's bean, is a variant of the antelope's bean plant native to certain regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a dry, dehiscent pod that contains several elliptical seeds.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
