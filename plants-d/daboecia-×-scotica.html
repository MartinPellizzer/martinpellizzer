Daboecia × scotica

Daboecia × scotica, commonly known as the Scottish heath, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Daboecia arcturis and Daboecia tetragona.

It is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Scottish Highlands, characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant features dark green, leathery leaves and produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in heathland and moorland habitats.

Daboecia × scotica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
