Diporidium madagascariense

Diporidium madagascariense, commonly known as the Madagascar orchid, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in montane forests. Its roots are adapted to absorb nutrients from the rich, organic soil of its habitat.

The species is noted for its unique floral structure, which includes two sepals and three petals.
