Dianthus juzeptchukii

Dianthus juzeptchukii, commonly known as the Juzeptchuk's carnation, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family, which includes many other species of carnations and related flora. The plant is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Dianthus juzeptchukii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively hardy in temperate climates.
