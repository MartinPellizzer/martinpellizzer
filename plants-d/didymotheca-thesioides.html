Didymotheca thesioides

Didymotheca thesioides, commonly known as the silverleaf sunflower, is an annual herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features a robust stem covered in a dense layer of silvery-white hairs, giving it its common name. The plant produces yellow composite flowers with ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and also covered in a fine, silvery down.

It is often found in disturbed soils and thrives in full sun, making it a common weed in agricultural fields.
