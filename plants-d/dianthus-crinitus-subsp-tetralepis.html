Dianthus crinitus subsp. tetralepis

Dianthus crinitus subsp.

tetralepis, commonly known as the tufted campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its dense, tuft-like inflorescences of pink or red flowers, which bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the stems are usually upright.

It is a perennial species that is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
