Diospyros crassiflora

Diospyros crassiflora, commonly known as the thick-flowered ebony, is a species of flowering plant in the family Ebenaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, particularly the Democratic Republic of the Congo and Tanzania. Its fruit is a small, round drupe that turns from green to black when mature.

Diospyros crassiflora is valued for its hard, dense wood, which is used in traditional craftsmanship and as a source of timber.
