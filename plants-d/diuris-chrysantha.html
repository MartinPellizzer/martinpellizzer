Diuris chrysantha

Diuris chrysantha, commonly known as the golden diuris, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces a spike of elongated, showy flowers.

Diuris chrysantha is often found in coastal and heathland environments, where it plays a role in local ecosystems.
