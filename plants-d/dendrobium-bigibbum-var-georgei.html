Dendrobium bigibbum var. georgei

Dendrobium bigibbum var. georgei, commonly known as the George's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with erect, branching stems that often bear a dense cluster of flowers. The plant produces showy, fragrant blooms in shades of white, pink, and red, typically during the summer months. Its leaves are usually elliptical and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

This variety is valued for its beauty and is often cultivated for horticultural purposes.
