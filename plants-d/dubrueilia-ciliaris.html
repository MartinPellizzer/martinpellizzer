Dubrueilia ciliaris

Dubrueilia ciliaris, commonly known as the white-flowered bellflower, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

Dubrueilia ciliaris is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
