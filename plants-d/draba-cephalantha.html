Draba cephalantha

Draba cephalantha, commonly known as the alpine draba, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and pinnately divided leaves. This perennial herb typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in cold, high-altitude environments. Its flowers, which bloom in early spring, are usually white or pale yellow, attracting pollinators such as bees.

The plant has a compact growth form and is often found in meadows and scree slopes.
