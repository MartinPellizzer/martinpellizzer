Dichopus insignis

Dichopus insignis, commonly known as the silverleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of silver-gray, hairy leaves and yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its flowers are pollinated by insects, and it produces a capsule containing numerous tiny seeds.

Dichopus insignis is often used in rock gardens due to its ornamental appearance and low maintenance requirements.
