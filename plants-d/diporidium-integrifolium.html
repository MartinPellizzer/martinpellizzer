Diporidium integrifolium

Diporidium integrifolium, commonly known as the smoothleaf diporidium, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its simple, entire leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
