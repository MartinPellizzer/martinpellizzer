Dracocephalum tchihatcheffii

Dracocephalum tchihatcheffii, commonly known as Kashmir mint, is a perennial herb native to the Himalayas and Central Asia.

It features aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or blue, arranged in dense spikes. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for both horticultural and medicinal purposes.
