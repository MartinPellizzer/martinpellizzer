Diospyros chunii

Diospyros chunii, commonly known as the Chinese persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to China and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into orange to red, fleshy fruits. The fruits are typically eaten fresh or used in traditional medicine due to their high nutrient content.

Diospyros chunii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitats.
