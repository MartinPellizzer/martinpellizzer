Dianthus pontederae

Dianthus pontederae, commonly known as the Pontedera carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that typically range in color from pink to red. The plant features upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves, and its flowers are composed of five petals arranged in a radial pattern. Dianthus pontederae thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and borders.

It is also known for its resilience and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
