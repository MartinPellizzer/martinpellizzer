Dactylis izcoi subsp. merinoana

Dactylis izcoi subsp.

merinoana, commonly known as Merino grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Merino grass is valued for its forage quality and is used in agricultural settings.
