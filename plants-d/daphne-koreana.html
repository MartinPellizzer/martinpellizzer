Daphne koreana

Daphne koreana, commonly known as Korean daphne, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Korea.

It features small, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring, often in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant has smooth, grayish bark and lance-shaped leaves that turn reddish in autumn. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Daphne koreana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and winter interest, with its stems remaining attractive even after the leaves fall.
