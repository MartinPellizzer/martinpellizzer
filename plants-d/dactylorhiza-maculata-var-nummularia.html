Dactylorhiza maculata var. nummularia

Dactylorhiza maculata var. nummularia, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous habitats such as woodlands and meadows. The plant features a sympodial growth habit with a short rhizome and a rosette of elliptic, dark green leaves. Its flowers are borne on a tall, erect stem and are characterized by their purple or violet coloration with yellow markings.

This orchid is notable for its delicate appearance and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
