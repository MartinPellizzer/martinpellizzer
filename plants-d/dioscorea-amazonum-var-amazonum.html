Dioscorea amazonum var. amazonum

Dioscorea amazonum var. amazonum, commonly known as the Amazon yam, is a tuberous plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its large, starchy tubers that are used in traditional cuisines. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, loamy soil for optimal growth. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers.

The tubers are rich in carbohydrates and have been used for both culinary and medicinal purposes by indigenous communities.
