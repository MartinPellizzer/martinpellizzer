Didymopanax auratus

Didymopanax auratus, commonly known as the golden sycamore, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that are often covered in a distinctive golden or yellowish pubescence. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in panicles, which are followed by woody, spiny fruits. Its growth habit is typically tree-like, with a straight trunk and a broad, spreading crown.

Didymopanax auratus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
