Diplazium lellingeri

Diplazium lellingeri, commonly known as the Lellinger's spleenwort, is a member of the Pteridaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate fronds.

It is a terrestrial fern that typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rock crevices. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, with both stolons and rhizomes contributing to its spread. Its leaves are usually bipinnate, with a finely divided structure that enhances its ornamental appeal.

Diplazium lellingeri is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, where it thrives in humid conditions.
