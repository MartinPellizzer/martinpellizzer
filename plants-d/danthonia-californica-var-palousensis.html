Danthonia californica var. palousensis

Danthonia californica var. palousensis, commonly known as Palouse needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, grass-like leaves that are often blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, open habitats such as prairies and slopes.

This variety is an important component of native grassland ecosystems and is valued for its ecological and ornamental qualities.
