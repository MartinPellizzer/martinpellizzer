Doryopteris excisa

Doryopteris excisa, commonly known as the dissected shield fern, is a species of fern native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are finely divided, giving it a lacy appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its rhizomes are horizontal and produce a dense network of roots that help anchor it in soil.

Doryopteris excisa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticultural settings.
