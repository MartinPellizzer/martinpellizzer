Diospyros melanida

Diospyros melanida, commonly known as the black ebony tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Ebenaceae and is characterized by its dark, dense wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by edible, orange-red berries. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a leathery texture with a glossy surface.

Diospyros melanida is also noted for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in traditional medicine.
