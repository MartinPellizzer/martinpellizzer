Delphinastrum carolinianum

Delphinastrum carolinianum, commonly known as Carolina rain lily, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and prairies. The plant produces striking white or pale pink flowers with a distinctive spurred petal, which are usually borne on slender stems. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in wet conditions and is sometimes used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value.
