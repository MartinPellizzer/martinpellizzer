Drimys fistulosa

Drimys fistulosa, commonly known as the Chilean boxwood, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage composed of small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring, followed by dark blue, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in temperate rainforests.

Drimys fistulosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens.
