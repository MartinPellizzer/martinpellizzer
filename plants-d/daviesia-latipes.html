Daviesia latipes

Daviesia latipes, commonly known as the narrow-leafed honey bush, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, grey-green leaves and clusters of fragrant, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is part of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and open woodlands.

Daviesia latipes is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
