Dalbergia arbutifolia subsp. aberrans

Dalbergia arbutifolia subsp.

aberrans, commonly known as the Madagascar rosewood, is a member of the Fabaceae family. It is a small to medium-sized tree characterized by its dense, dark-colored wood and trifoliate leaves.

The plant is native to Madagascar and is known for its slow growth and high wood density. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by legume-like pods containing flattened seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and timber quality, it is sometimes cultivated in horticultural settings.
