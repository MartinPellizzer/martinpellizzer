Delphinium elatum subsp. montanum

Delphinium elatum subsp.

montanum, commonly known as the mountain larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America. It typically grows in open meadows and rocky slopes at high elevations, reaching heights of up to 2 meters.

The plant features striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, arranged in tall, spires. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a fuzzy texture, while its stems are erect and covered in fine hairs.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also noted for its toxicity due to the presence of alkaloids.
