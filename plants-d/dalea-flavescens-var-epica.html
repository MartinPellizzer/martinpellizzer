Dalea flavescens var. epica

Dalea flavescens var. epica, commonly known as the yellow false indigo, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in low-nutrient conditions.
