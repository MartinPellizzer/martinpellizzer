Dennstaedtia hirsuta var. bipinnata

Dennstaedtia hirsuta var. bipinnata, commonly known as the hairy shield fern, is a variant of the hairy shield fern native to the eastern United States.

It is a perennial fern that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit and produces large, bipinnate fronds with a distinctive, shield-like shape. Its leaves are covered in fine, hairy structures, contributing to its common name.

The species is characterized by its ability to thrive in humid, temperate climates and is often found in rich, organic soils.
