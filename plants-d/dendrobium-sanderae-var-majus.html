Dendrobium sanderae var. majus

Dendrobium sanderae var. majus, commonly known as the giant orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a sympodial epiphytic orchid, characterized by its long, cane-like stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces large, showy flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, often arranged in racemes. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light and requires well-draining potting media.

Due to its striking appearance and rarity, it is highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts.
