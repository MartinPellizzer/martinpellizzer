Diodia prostrata

Diodia prostrata, commonly known as groundsel or prostrate diodia, is a low-growing shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the spring and summer months. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, ditches, and disturbed areas.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Apiaceae, which includes other aromatic plants such as carrots and parsley.
