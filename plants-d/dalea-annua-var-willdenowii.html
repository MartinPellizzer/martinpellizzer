Dalea annua var. willdenowii

Dalea annua var. willdenowii, commonly known as the annual milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the central and western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, hairy pods that contain several seeds, and it typically grows in dry, disturbed soils such as prairies and roadsides.
