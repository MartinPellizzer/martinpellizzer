Doronicum carpetanum subsp. kuepferi

Doronicum carpetanum subsp.

kuepferi, commonly known as the Carpathian leopard's bread, is a perennial herb native to the Carpathian Mountains. It is characterized by its basal rosette of broadly ovate, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers in racemes.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and meadows. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly smaller flowers and more compact growth compared to the typical form.
