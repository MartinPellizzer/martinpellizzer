Danthonia angustifolia

Danthonia angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf danthonia, is a perennial grass species native to various regions of the United States and parts of Canada.

It typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in marshes, wetlands, and along stream banks. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are usually less than 1 inch wide, with a bluish-green hue. Its inflorescence consists of a loose cluster of spikelets, which produce small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Danthonia angustifolia plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
