Dactylorhiza vadorum

Dactylorhiza vadorum, commonly known as the yellow orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and marshes. Its root system consists of a network of rhizomes and tubers, which help it store nutrients and water.

Dactylorhiza vadorum is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in horticulture.
