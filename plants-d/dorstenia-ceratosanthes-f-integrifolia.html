Dorstenia ceratosanthes f. integrifolia

Dorstenia ceratosanthes f. integrifolia, commonly known as the smoothleaf woollyleaf, is a unique member of the Moraceae family, characterized by its distinctive, fleshy, and often hairy leaves.

The plant features a thick, succulent stem that can grow as a shrub or vine, with leaves that are typically elongated and have a waxy texture. Its inflorescences consist of small, clustered flowers that are usually green or yellowish, and it produces non-ribbed, fleshy fruits. This species is native to tropical regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
