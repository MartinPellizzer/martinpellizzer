Diplopappus natalensis

Diplopappus natalensis, commonly known as Natal doubleweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive two-part pappus, which aids in seed dispersal. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and sandy soils, thriving in full sun. Its yellow flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
