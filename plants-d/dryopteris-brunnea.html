Dryopteris brunnea

Dryopteris brunnea, commonly known as the brown wood fern, is a perennial fern native to various regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its dark, leathery fronds that are often tinged with brown or bronze hues. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its rhizomes are thick and fleshy, allowing it to thrive in nutrient-rich, well-drained soils.

Dryopteris brunnea is valued for its ornamental foliage and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
