Didymoglossum reptans var. muscoides

Didymoglossum reptans var. muscoides, commonly known as the creeping muscovy moss, is a species of liverwort belonging to the family Peltasporaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, cushion-like growth habit and small, closely packed leaves that are typically green to reddish in color. This moss thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forests or along stream banks. Its reproductive structures, known as sorocarps, are borne on short stalks and contain spores for dispersal.

The plant plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for microorganisms in its native ecosystems.
