Diplachne pungens

Diplachne pungens, commonly known as the sharpleaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, pointed leaves and dense, spreading growth habit. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can form dense mats, making it a common weed in agricultural and roadside environments. Diplachne pungens has fibrous roots and produces small, spike-like inflorescences with tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Its adaptability to various soil types and climates contributes to its widespread distribution and ecological significance.
