Drosera longifolia subsp. obovata

Drosera longifolia subsp.

obovata, commonly known as the broad-leaf sundew, is a species of carnivorous plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its rosette of broad, waxy leaves that secrete a sticky substance to trap insects.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in a spike, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, acidic environments such as bogs and wet heathlands.

Its unique adaptations for nutrient acquisition make it a fascinating subject in the study of carnivorous plants.
