Dioscorea nesiotis

Dioscorea nesiotis, commonly known as the Greek yam, is a flowering plant species belonging to the family Dioscoreaceae.

It is native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Greece and Turkey, where it thrives in rocky and arid environments. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its tuberous roots are edible and have been traditionally used in local cuisines.

Dioscorea nesiotis is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
