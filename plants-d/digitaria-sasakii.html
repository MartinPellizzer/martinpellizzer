Digitaria sasakii

Digitaria sasakii, commonly known as Japanese barnyardgrass, is a grass species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

Due to its invasive potential, it is considered a weed in many regions where it is not native.
