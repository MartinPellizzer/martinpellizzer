Daphniphyllum glaucescens var. longistylium

Daphniphyllum glaucescens var. longistylium, commonly known as the long-styled daphniphyllum, is a species of evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded environments.

Daphniphyllum glaucescens var. longistylium is also noted for its slow growth and preference for acidic, well-drained soil.
