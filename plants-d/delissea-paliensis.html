Delissea paliensis

Delissea paliensis, commonly known as the narrowleaf waterplantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetlands and marshy areas in North America.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers in clustered inflorescences. The plant thrives in shallow water or moist soil, often found in riparian zones and along the edges of ponds. It plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic invertebrates.

Delissea paliensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal in wetland gardens.
