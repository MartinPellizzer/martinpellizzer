Deeringia mirabilis

Deeringia mirabilis, commonly known as the fairy lantern, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a member of the Acanthaceae family, characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that come in a variety of vibrant colors. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its unique appearance. Its flowers are typically borne in clusters and are known for their ability to open and close in response to light, a trait called nyctinasty.

Deeringia mirabilis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses due to its exotic and eye-catching blooms.
