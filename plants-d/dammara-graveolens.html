Dammara graveolens

Dammara graveolens, commonly known as the sour fig or wild fig, is a tropical shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family, which includes other fig species, and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are followed by edible, fleshy fruits with a sour taste. Its botanical features make it adaptable to various soil types and climates, contributing to its widespread distribution.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and traditional uses in local medicine.
