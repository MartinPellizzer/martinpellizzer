Dactylostemon grandifolius

Dactylostemon grandifolius, commonly known as the large-leaved dactylostemon, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring. It typically grows in coastal and sandy soils, often in open forests or heathlands.

Dactylostemon grandifolius is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
