Diplazium cavaleriei

Diplazium cavaleriei, commonly known as the Cavalerie's spleenwort, is a fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically less than 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Diplazium cavaleriei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
