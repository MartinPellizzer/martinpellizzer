Diapedium clinopodium var. minor

Diapedium clinopodium var. minor, commonly known as narrowleaf fieldweed, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically has narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface and produces small, pale blue or purple flowers. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Due to its hardy nature, it is considered a common weed in various regions.
