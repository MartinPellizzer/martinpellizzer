Diospyros rufogemmata

Diospyros rufogemmata, commonly known as the red-pear tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other well-known species like ebony and persimmon. The tree is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and elliptical leaves that are glossy on the upper surface. Its fruit is a small, round drupe with a sweet, edible pulp that turns from yellow to red as it ripens.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
