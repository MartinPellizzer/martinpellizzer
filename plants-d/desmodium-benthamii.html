Desmodium benthamii

Desmodium benthamii, commonly known as the slender cowpea, is a perennial legume native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant produces cylindrical pods containing several oval, dark brown seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Desmodium benthamii is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in agricultural systems.
