Descurainia incana var. incisa

Descurainia incana var. incisa, commonly known as hoary dodder, is an annual herbaceous plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its pale green, hairy stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is often found in disturbed soils and is native to North America. It has a taproot system and produces small, elongated pods containing numerous seeds.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and often invasive species in various habitats.
