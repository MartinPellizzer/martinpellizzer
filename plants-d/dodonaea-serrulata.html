Dodonaea serrulata

Dodonaea serrulata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hop bush, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a small to medium-sized shrub that typically grows in coastal and dry inland regions. The plant features small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in spikes, and its leaves are narrow and serrated, giving it its scientific name. It is often found in sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought and salt spray.

Dodonaea serrulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in coastal landscaping.
