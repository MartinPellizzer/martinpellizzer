Dendrocalamus longivaginatus

Dendrocalamus longivaginatus, commonly known as the giant bamboo, is a tall, fast-growing grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its robust, hollow stems that can reach heights of over 30 meters. The plant has long, narrow leaves with a smooth, waxy surface, and its culms are often used in construction and as a sustainable resource. Dendrocalamus longivaginatus is also valued for its role in ecological systems, providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Its rapid growth rate makes it an important plant for reforestation and environmental restoration projects.
