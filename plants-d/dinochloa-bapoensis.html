Dinochloa bapoensis

Dinochloa bapoensis, commonly known as the Bapo grass, is a species of flowering plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Africa, particularly found in countries like Kenya and Tanzania. This grass is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodland or savanna habitats.

Dinochloa bapoensis plays a minor role in local ecosystems, contributing to soil stabilization and providing forage for small herbivores.
