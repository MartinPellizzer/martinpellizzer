Dryobalanops sumatrensis

Dryobalanops sumatrensis, commonly known as the Sumatran ironwood, is a large evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in Indonesia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, hard wood that is highly valued for its durability and strength. The tree can grow up to 50 meters tall, with a straight trunk and a broad, umbrella-shaped crown. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical, often with a waxy surface.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody capsules that contain seeds.
