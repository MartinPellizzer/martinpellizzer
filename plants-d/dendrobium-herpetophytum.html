Dendrobium herpetophytum

Dendrobium herpetophytum, commonly known as the snake orchid, is a striking epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features long, wiry stems that can grow up to several meters in length, often adorned with clusters of bright yellow or orange flowers. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in forest canopies where it attaches to tree trunks or branches. Its flowers are notable for their intricate, spurred petals and are often fragrant.

Dendrobium herpetophytum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated for its unique floral display.
