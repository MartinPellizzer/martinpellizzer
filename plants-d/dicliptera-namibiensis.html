Dicliptera namibiensis

Dicliptera namibiensis, commonly known as Namibian grass, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tufted growth habit with slender, wiry stems. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought tolerance through its deep root system.

Its ecological role includes soil stabilization and providing habitat for small fauna.
