Delonix adansonioides

Delonix adansonioides, commonly known as the flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its large, bright orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The plant has a straight, often fissured trunk and compound leaves with long, slender leaflets. Its ornamental value is due to its showy blooms and ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.

The species is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of timber.
