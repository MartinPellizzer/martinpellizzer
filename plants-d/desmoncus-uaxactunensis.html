Desmoncus uaxactunensis

Desmoncus uaxactunensis, commonly known as the Yucatán orchid, is a rare and endemic orchid species found in the Yucatán Peninsula of Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking violet and white flowers, which bloom during the dry season. The plant has a compact growth habit with a pseudobulb that stores water, adapting it to the arid environment. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the limestone soils of its native habitat.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat threats, it is considered a vulnerable species.
