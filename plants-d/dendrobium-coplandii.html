Dendrobium coplandii

Dendrobium coplandii, commonly known as the golden bower orchid, is an epiphytic orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It features a compact, upright growth habit with dense clusters of narrow, dark green leaves. The plant produces striking, pendulous inflorescences bearing bright yellow to orange flowers with a distinctive golden throat. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires high humidity and well-draining potting media.

Dendrobium coplandii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its long-lasting blooms.
