Diolena agrimonioides

Diolena agrimonioides, commonly known as the agrimony-like diolena, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces yellowish to reddish flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Diolena agrimonioides is often found in grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils.
