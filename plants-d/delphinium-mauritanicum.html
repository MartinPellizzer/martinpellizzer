Delphinium mauritanicum

Delphinium mauritanicum, commonly known as the Mauritania larkspur, is a perennial herb native to North Africa.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, with lance-shaped leaflets, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Delphinium mauritanicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
