Danthonia crispa var. truncatula

Danthonia crispa var. truncatula, commonly known as the cut-leaf danthonia, is a grass species native to parts of Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, narrow leaves that are often cut or notched at the tips, giving it a unique appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and heathlands. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in panicles, which are usually wind-pollinated.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its fine texture and hardy nature.
