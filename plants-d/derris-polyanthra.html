Derris polyanthra

Derris polyanthra, commonly known as the pink derris, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking pink or purple flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green. Its root system is fibrous, and the plant prefers well-drained soils in sunny to partially shaded environments.

Derris polyanthra is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
