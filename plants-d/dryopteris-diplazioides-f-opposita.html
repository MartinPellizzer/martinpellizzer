Dryopteris diplazioides f. opposita

Dryopteris diplazioides f. opposita, commonly known as the opposite-leaf maidenhair fern, is a fern species characterized by its distinctive foliage.

The fronds are bipinnate, with pinnae arranged in an opposite pattern, giving the plant its name. It is native to the mountainous regions of Asia, particularly in China and Japan. The plant prefers cool, moist, and shaded environments, often growing in rocky or forested areas.

Its intricate and delicate leaf structure makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
