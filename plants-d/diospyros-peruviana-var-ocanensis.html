Diospyros peruviana var. ocanensis

Diospyros peruviana var. ocanensis, commonly known as the Ocanense persimmon, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a glossy surface. Its fruits are fleshy, orange to red in color, and contain a single large seed.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
