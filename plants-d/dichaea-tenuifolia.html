Dichaea tenuifolia

Dichaea tenuifolia, commonly known as the slender leaf dichaea, is a member of the Bromeliaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that grows on trees and rocks, often in shaded, humid environments. The plant features narrow, elongated leaves that form a rosette, which helps collect moisture and nutrients from the air. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically greenish-white in color, and are borne on short, erect inflorescences.

Dichaea tenuifolia is valued in horticulture for its unique appearance and adaptability to indoor growing conditions.
