Delphinium lasiocarpum

Delphinium lasiocarpum, commonly known as the woolly delphinium, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems that bear clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, woolly hairs, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by garden enthusiasts.
