Dioscorea elegans

Dioscorea elegans, commonly known as the elegant yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its hairy, cylindrical stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by cylindrical, fleshy fruits containing numerous seeds. Dioscorea elegans is valued for its starchy tuberous roots, which are used in traditional cuisines and have medicinal properties.

Its growth habit and morphology make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
