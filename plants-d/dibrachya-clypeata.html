Dibrachya clypeata

Dibrachya clypeata, commonly known as the golden crown orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers with a distinctive crown-like structure at the base. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its roots are fibrous and adapted for nutrient absorption from the surrounding soil.

Dibrachya clypeata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
