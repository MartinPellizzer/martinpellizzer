Dendrobium speciosum var. hillii

Dendrobium speciosum var. hillii, commonly known as the Hill's orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is an epiphytic orchid that grows on trees and rocks, relying on atmospheric moisture for nourishment. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and yellow, often with intricate patterns. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in humid, tropical climates.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and relatively easy care requirements.
