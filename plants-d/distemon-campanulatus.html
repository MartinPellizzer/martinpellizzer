Distemon campanulatus

Distemon campanulatus, commonly known as the bell-shaped distemon, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Campanulaceae.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, tubular flowers that typically range in color from white to blue or purple. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It is native to certain regions of South America and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Distemon campanulatus is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking floral display.
