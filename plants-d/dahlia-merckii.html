Dahlia merckii

Dahlia merckii, commonly known as the Merck's dahlia, is a herbaceous perennial native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, single or double flowers in a range of colors, including red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with sturdy stems and large, waxy leaves. Its tuberous roots are used for propagation, and it thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.

The flowers bloom from late summer to early autumn, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
