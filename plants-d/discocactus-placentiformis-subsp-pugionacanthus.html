Discocactus placentiformis subsp. pugionacanthus

Discocactus placentiformis subsp.

pugionacanthus, commonly known as the sword cactus, is a unique member of the Cactaceae family native to the arid regions of South America. It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems that often resemble swords, which are covered in sharp, forward-facing spines.

The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its rugged appearance. Its root system is adapted to store water, allowing it to thrive in dry, sandy soils.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in harsh environments.
