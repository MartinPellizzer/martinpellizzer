Drymocallis cuneifolia var. cuneifolia

Drymocallis cuneifolia var. cuneifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant features upright, slender stems with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its showy yellow flowers appear in late summer, forming dense, cone-like inflorescences.

It plays an important role in supporting pollinators and is valued for its ornamental appeal in wetland gardens.
