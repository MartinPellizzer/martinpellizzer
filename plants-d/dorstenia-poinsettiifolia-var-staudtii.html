Dorstenia poinsettiifolia var. staudtii

Dorstenia poinsettiifolia var. staudtii, commonly known as the Staudtii False Fig, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Moraceae family, characterized by its unique, curling bracts that resemble the leaves of the common fig. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its foliage is often a deep green, with a waxy texture that helps it retain moisture in its native environment.

The plant is appreciated for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its distinctive appearance.
