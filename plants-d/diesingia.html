Diesingia

Diesingia, commonly known as the goldenbush, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to Australia and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and aromatic foliage. The leaves are typically narrow, lance-shaped, and covered in a fine, powdery coating. Flowers are small, white or pale yellow, and often clustered in dense inflorescences.

These plants are valued for their ornamental appeal and are sometimes cultivated in gardens for their drought tolerance and visual interest.
