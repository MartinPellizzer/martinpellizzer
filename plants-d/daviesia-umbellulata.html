Daviesia umbellulata

Daviesia umbellulata, commonly known as the Umbellate Daviesia, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive umbel-like arrangement of flowers. The plant typically grows in heathlands and rocky soils, thriving in dry, sunny conditions. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds.

Daviesia umbellulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting native wildlife.
