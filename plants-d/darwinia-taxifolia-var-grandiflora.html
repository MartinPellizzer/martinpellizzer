Darwinia taxifolia var. grandiflora

Darwinia taxifolia var. grandiflora, commonly known as the broad-leaved Darwinia, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and showy, spike-like inflorescences. The plant features lance-shaped, glossy leaves and produces large, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and adaptability to various garden conditions.
