Dendrobium cyanocentrum

Dendrobium cyanocentrum, commonly known as the blue-veined orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is an epiphytic plant that typically grows on trees or rocks in tropical rainforests. The plant features long, cane-like pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its flowers are usually pale blue or white with intricate patterns, and it blooms during the wet season.

Dendrobium cyanocentrum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in horticulture.
