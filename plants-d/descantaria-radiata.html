Descantaria radiata

Descantaria radiata, commonly known as the radiate bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers, typically in shades of blue or violet. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in alpine or rocky habitats.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
