Diplacus pulchellus

Diplacus pulchellus, commonly known as the showy Diplacus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple, often with a distinctive white throat. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.

Diplacus pulchellus is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like hummingbirds and bees.
