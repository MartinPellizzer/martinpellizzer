Draba bakeri

Draba bakeri, commonly known as Baker's winter cress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a small, herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a single umbel, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are basal, rosette-forming, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Draba bakeri is adapted to cold climates and is often found at high altitudes where it thrives in well-drained soils.
