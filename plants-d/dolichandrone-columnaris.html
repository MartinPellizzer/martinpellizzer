Dolichandrone columnaris

Dolichandrone columnaris, commonly known as the columnar coral tree, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its tall, slender, and often branched stems that resemble a coral structure, hence its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a slightly hairy texture.

Dolichandrone columnaris is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and full sun.
