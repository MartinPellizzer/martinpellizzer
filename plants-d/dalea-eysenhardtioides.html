Dalea eysenhardtioides

Dalea eysenhardtioides, commonly known as the Eysenhardt's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its root system is adapted to drought conditions, and it plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in native plant communities.
