Diospyros vieillardii

Diospyros vieillardii, commonly known as the African persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and large, palmate leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, orange-red berries that are rich in nutrients. Its fruits are often consumed fresh or used in traditional medicine.

Diospyros vieillardii is also valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in construction and tool-making.
