Diasperus pinifolius

Diasperus pinifolius, commonly known as the needleleaf daisies, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves and small, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions. It is a hardy, drought-tolerant species that thrives in full sun.

Its foliage and flowers make it an attractive addition to native gardens and dry landscapes.
