Dioscorea villosa var. glabrifolia

Dioscorea villosa var. glabrifolia, commonly known as wild yam, is a perennial vine native to North America.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and tuberous roots. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and produces small, yellowish flowers followed by orange or red berries. Its tubers are used in traditional medicine and have been studied for their potential pharmaceutical properties.

The species is also a source of diosgenin, a compound used in the synthesis of hormonal medications.
