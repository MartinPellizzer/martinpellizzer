Dryopteris napoleonis

Dryopteris napoleonis, commonly known as Napoleon's wood fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright, clustered growth habit and distinctive fronds that are typically dark green with a bluish tinge. The plant features bipinnate leaves, which are divided into two sets of smaller leaflets, giving it a delicate, lacy appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Dryopteris napoleonis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly used in horticulture for its attractive foliage.
