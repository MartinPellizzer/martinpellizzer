Davallia polypodioides var. pubescens

Davallia polypodioides var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy Davallia, is a fern native to Southeast Asia.

It features delicate, pinnately divided fronds with a distinctive hairy texture on the underside. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in forest understories. Its rhizomes are wiry and form dense clumps, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

The fronds are evergreen, providing a lush, green appearance throughout the year.
