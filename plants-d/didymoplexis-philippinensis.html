Didymoplexis philippinensis

Didymoplexis philippinensis, commonly known as the Philippine spider orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, spider-like appearance with long, slender petals. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, using its aerial roots to absorb moisture and nutrients from the environment. Its flowers are usually pale green or white, with a distinctive, elongated spur that attracts specific pollinators.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
