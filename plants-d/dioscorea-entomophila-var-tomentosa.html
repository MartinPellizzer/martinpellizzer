Dioscorea entomophila var. tomentosa

Dioscorea entomophila var. tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly yam, is a variant of the entomophila yam species native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its hairy, tomentose stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by tuberous roots that are often used in traditional medicine. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a complex network of rhizomes.

The species is valued for its medicinal properties and is also studied for its potential in agricultural applications.
