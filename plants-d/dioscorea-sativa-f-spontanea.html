Dioscorea sativa f. spontanea

Dioscorea sativa f. spontanea, commonly known as the wild yam, is a perennial vine native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, with hairy, slender stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically followed by cylindrical, spiny fruits containing yellowish seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Botanically, it is valued for its tubers, which have been used in traditional medicine and as a source of compounds for pharmaceutical applications.
