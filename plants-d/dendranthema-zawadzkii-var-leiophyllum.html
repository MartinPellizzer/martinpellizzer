Dendranthema zawadzkii var. leiophyllum

Dendranthema zawadzkii var. leiophyllum, commonly known as the white marguerite, is a perennial plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and grasslands. It has finely divided, gray-green leaves and produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in garden landscapes.
