Dolichos oliveri

Dolichos oliveri, commonly known as the Oliver's cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, with slender stems that twine around supports. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers followed by elongated, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets.

Dolichos oliveri is valued for its nutritional content and is sometimes used in traditional agriculture and cuisine.
