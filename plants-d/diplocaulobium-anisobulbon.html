Diplocaulobium anisobulbon

Diplocaulobium anisobulbon, commonly known as the zigzag sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy habitats, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its flowers are small and clustered, often with three stamens and a three-lobed pistil.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
