Diospyros hebecarpa

Diospyros hebecarpa, commonly known as the red date or Chinese date, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to southern China and parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other well-known species like ebony and persimmon. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a glossy surface. Its fruit is a small, round drupe that turns reddish when mature, often with a sweet, edible pulp.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and its fruit, which is used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
