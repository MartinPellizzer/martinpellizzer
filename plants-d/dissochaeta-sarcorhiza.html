Dissochaeta sarcorhiza

Dissochaeta sarcorhiza, commonly known as the root-hair grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant has fibrous roots that contribute to soil stabilization, making it ecologically significant in its native habitat. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a hairy texture that gives it its common name.

Dissochaeta sarcorhiza is often found in grasslands and open woodlands, thriving in well-drained soils.
