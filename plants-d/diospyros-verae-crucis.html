Diospyros verae-crucis

Diospyros verae-crucis, commonly known as the Holy Land persimmon, is a species of flowering plant in the Ebenaceae family.

It is native to the Levant region, including parts of modern-day Israel, Palestine, and Jordan. The plant is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into orange to reddish fruits. The fruit is typically edible when fully ripe, though it may have astringent properties when unripe.

This species is of interest to botanists and horticulturists due to its historical and potential ecological significance in the region.
