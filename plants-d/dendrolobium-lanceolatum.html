Dendrolobium lanceolatum

Dendrolobium lanceolatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf burr legume, is a perennial shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces distinctive, spiny pods that contain edible seeds. The plant is part of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and agricultural purposes.
