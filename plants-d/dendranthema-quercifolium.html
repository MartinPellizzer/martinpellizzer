Dendranthema quercifolium

Dendranthema quercifolium, commonly known as the oakleaf fleabane, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, oak-like leaves that are deeply lobed and have a silvery underside. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in heads, typically blooming in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides.

Dendranthema quercifolium is an herbaceous perennial that is relatively easy to grow in gardens with full sun exposure.
