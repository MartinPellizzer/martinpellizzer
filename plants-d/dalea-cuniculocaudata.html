Dalea cuniculocaudata

Dalea cuniculocaudata, commonly known as the burrobrush or pronghornbrush, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, spiny stems and small, yellowish-pink flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces distinctive, cylindrical seed pods that mature into burrs. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in grasslands and shrublands, and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Dalea cuniculocaudata plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
