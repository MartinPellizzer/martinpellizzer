Dolichos sericeus

Dolichos sericeus, commonly known as silky cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its long, slender pods covered in fine, silky hairs, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers and is often cultivated for its edible seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for warm climates.

Dolichos sericeus is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting other plants in the vicinity.
