Deyeuxia pickeringii

Deyeuxia pickeringii, commonly known as the Pickering's sedge, is a perennial grass species native to the temperate regions of East Asia.

It typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, swamps, and the edges of ponds. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in waterlogged soils and is often found in riparian zones.
