Delphinium tricorne f. roseum

Delphinium tricorne f. roseum, commonly known as the roseum form of the tricorne delphinium, is a hybrid cultivar characterized by its striking pink to lavender-colored flowers.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring upright stems and large, palmately divided leaves. Its blooms appear in late spring to early summer, adding a vibrant splash of color to gardens.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its showy, long-lasting flowers.
