Deschampsia australis var. gracilis

Deschampsia australis var. gracilis, commonly known as slender tussock grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern South America.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as grasslands and wet meadows. The plant features slender, tufted culms with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, spikelet-bearing branches.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
