Draba matthioloides subsp. matthioloides

Draba matthioloides subsp.

matthioloides, commonly known as the alpine mouse-ear cress, is a flowering plant species native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is a small, herbaceous plant with basal rosettes of narrow, silver-gray leaves.

The flowers are typically white or pale yellow, clustered in tight, compact inflorescences. It thrives in rocky or alpine soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

This subspecies is notable for its ability to tolerate harsh climatic conditions and is often found in meadows and scree slopes.
