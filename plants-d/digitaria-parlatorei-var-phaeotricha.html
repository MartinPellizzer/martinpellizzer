Digitaria parlatorei var. phaeotricha

Digitaria parlatorei var. phaeotricha, commonly known as the dark-haired digitaria, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves with a dark green to bluish-gray appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny florets, typical of the grass family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Its fibrous root system helps stabilize soil, making it ecologically significant in certain environments.
