Diacalpe aspidioides var. minor

Diacalpe aspidioides var. minor, commonly known as the lesser broad-leafed fern, is a species of fern native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest undergrowth or along stream banks. The spore-producing structures, or sporangia, are clustered on the underside of the fronds, a feature common among ferns.
