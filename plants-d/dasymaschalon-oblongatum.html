Dasymaschalon oblongatum

Dasymaschalon oblongatum, commonly known as the broadleaf sandwort, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial with fleshy, succulent leaves that help it conserve water in dry environments. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in spring. Its root system is fibrous and helps stabilize sandy soils, making it useful for erosion control.
