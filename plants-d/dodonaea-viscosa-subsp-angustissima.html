Dodonaea viscosa subsp. angustissima

Dodonaea viscosa subsp.

angustissima, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hopwood, is a shrub native to Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters.

The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid regions. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to withstand harsh conditions.

The species is also valued for its ecological role, providing habitat and food for native wildlife.
