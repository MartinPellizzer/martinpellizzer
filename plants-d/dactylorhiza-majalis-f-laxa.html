Dactylorhiza majalis f. laxa

Dactylorhiza majalis f. laxa, commonly known as the loose-lip orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to northern Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lanceolate leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette. The plant produces spikes of pale yellow to greenish-white flowers, which are often fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and flies. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland or heathland habitats.

This orchid is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its delicate appearance and ecological significance.
