Dendrotrophe cassiifolia

Dendrotrophe cassiifolia, commonly known as the golden leaf tree, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often a vibrant yellow or golden green. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil to flourish.

Dendrotrophe cassiifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique foliage and pleasant floral scent.
