Dipterocarpus palembanicus subsp. palembanicus

Dipterocarpus palembanicus subsp.

palembanicus, commonly known as the Palembang dipterocarp, is a tropical rainforest tree native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its large, leathery leaves and produces heavy, woody fruits containing winged seeds.

The tree is a member of the Dipterocarpaceae family, which is well-known for its ecological and economic importance in tropical forests. It typically grows in lowland and hill dipterocarp forests, often reaching heights of up to 40 meters.

This species plays a crucial role in the biodiversity and carbon sequestration of its native ecosystems.
