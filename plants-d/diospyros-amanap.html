Diospyros amanap

Diospyros amanap, commonly known as the Amanap persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other persimmon species and ebony trees. The plant produces small, oval-shaped fruits that are typically orange to reddish in color when mature. The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a leathery texture with a pointed tip.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its edible fruits and ornamental value.
