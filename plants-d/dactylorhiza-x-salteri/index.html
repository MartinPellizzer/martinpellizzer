Dactylorhiza × Salteri

Salter's orchid, scientifically known as Dactylorhiza × salteri, is a hybrid species that captivates botanists and nature enthusiasts alike. Native to the wetlands and bogs of northern Europe, it thrives in moist, nutrient-poor environments. This orchid is notable for its striking floral display, featuring tall spikes adorned with vivid purple and white blooms. Its unique genetic makeup results from the cross between Dactylorhiza denplanca and Dactylorhiza macrantha. Despite its beauty, it remains a rare and elusive plant, making it a subject of both scientific interest and conservation concern.
