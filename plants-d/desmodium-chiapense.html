Desmodium chiapense

Desmodium chiapense, commonly known as the Chiapan legume, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its compound leaves and fruit pods. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in shaded or moist environments. Its small, clustered flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces small, lens-shaped seeds.

Desmodium chiapense is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.
