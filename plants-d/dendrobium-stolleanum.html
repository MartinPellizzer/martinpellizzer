Dendrobium stolleanum

Dendrobium stolleanum, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, cane-like pseudobulbs and clusters of showy, golden-yellow flowers. The plant typically blooms during the summer months, producing fragrant, long-lasting blossoms. Its leaves are strap-shaped and arranged spirally around the stem.

Dendrobium stolleanum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
