Drosera longifolia var. perennis

Drosera longifolia var. perennis, commonly known as the long-stemmed sundew, is a perennial carnivorous plant native to Australia.

It features long, slender leaves covered in sticky, glandular hairs that trap and digest insects. The plant typically grows in wet, acidic environments such as bogs and swamps. Its flowers are small, white or pink, and are arranged in a dense, rounded inflorescence.

It is a hardy species that can thrive in a variety of conditions, making it a popular choice for carnivorous plant enthusiasts.
