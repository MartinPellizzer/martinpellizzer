Dianthus chinensis var. dentosus

Dianthus chinensis var. dentosus, commonly known as the toothed carnation, is a flowering plant native to China.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of pink to red flowers with distinctive toothed petals. The leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its ornamental appeal. This variety is often cultivated for its vibrant blooms and hardy nature, thriving in full sun and well-drained soil.

It is a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements due to its long-lasting flowers and adaptability to various growing conditions.
