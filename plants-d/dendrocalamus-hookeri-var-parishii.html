Dendrocalamus hookeri var. parishii

Dendrocalamus hookeri var. parishii, commonly known as the Parish's bamboo, is a tall, clump-forming grass native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Bambusoideae subfamily and is characterized by its slender, green culms that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant produces small, pale green inflorescences with numerous tiny flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth texture and a prominent midrib.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its elegant appearance.
