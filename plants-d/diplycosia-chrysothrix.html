Diplycosia chrysothrix

Diplycosia chrysothrix, commonly known as the golden thread orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pendulous inflorescences. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are highly fragrant, attracting pollinators such as bees and moths. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often growing in dense clusters.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses due to its specific environmental requirements.
