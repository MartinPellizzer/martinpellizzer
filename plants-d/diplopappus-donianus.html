Diplopappus donianus

Diplopappus donianus, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by small, winged fruits. Its wood is highly valued for its durability and strength, making it important in local construction and crafts.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in arid conditions, contributing to its ecological significance in its native range.
