Dombeya opulifolia

Dombeya opulifolia, commonly known as the woolly dombeya, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its unique texture and ornamental flowers make it a valuable addition to botanical collections and gardens.
