Dimorphostachys paspaloides

Dimorphostachys paspaloides, commonly known as the silky blue grass, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its fine, bluish-green blades and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, blue to purple flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal.

Dimorphostachys paspaloides is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion in natural habitats.
