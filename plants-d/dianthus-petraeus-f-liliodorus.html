Dianthus petraeus f. liliodorus

Dianthus petraeus f. liliodorus, commonly known as the rock campion or pink rockrose, is a flowering plant characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and dense clusters of showy, pink or white flowers.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Dianthus petraeus and Dianthus deltoides, showcasing a blend of traits from both parent species. The plant typically thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or arctic environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, fleshy leaves that are adapted to withstand harsh, dry conditions.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
