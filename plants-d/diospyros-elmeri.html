Diospyros elmeri

Diospyros elmeri, commonly known as the Elmer's persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other persimmon species and ebony trees. The plant is characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by orange to red berries. Diospyros elmeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruits.

Its wood is also prized for its durability and fine grain, making it useful in traditional crafts and furniture.
