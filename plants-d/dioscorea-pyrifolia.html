Dioscorea pyrifolia

Dioscorea pyrifolia, commonly known as the Japanese yam, is a perennial vine native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellowish flowers and cylindrical, tuberous roots that are used in traditional medicine and cuisine. Its tubers are often harvested for their starchy content and have a mild, sweet flavor.

Dioscorea pyrifolia is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage.
