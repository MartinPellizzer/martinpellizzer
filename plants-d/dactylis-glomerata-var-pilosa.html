Dactylis glomerata var. pilosa

Dactylis glomerata var. pilosa, commonly known as hairy goatgrass, is a perennial grass native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green to brown in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils.

This variety is valued for its forage quality and is used in agricultural settings for livestock grazing.
