Dendrobium sarasinorum

Dendrobium sarasinorum, commonly known as the Sarasin's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is an epiphytic plant, typically growing on trees or rocks in tropical forest environments. The plant features long, cane-like stems that are often covered in a layer of white or grayish pubescence. Its flowers are usually small, fragrant, and come in a variety of colors, including white, yellow, and pink.

Dendrobium sarasinorum is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its cultural significance in traditional medicine.
