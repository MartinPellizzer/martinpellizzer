Dioscorea macvaughii

Dioscorea macvaughii, commonly known as the MacVaugh yam, is a member of the Dioscoreaceae family and is native to the southeastern United States.

It is a climbing vine that produces tuberous roots, which are the primary botanical feature of interest. The plant's tubers are typically elongated and have a rough, brownish exterior. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in moist woodlands.

Botanically, it is notable for its potential medicinal properties and its role in traditional herbal practices.
