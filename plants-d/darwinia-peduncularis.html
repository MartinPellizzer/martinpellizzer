Darwinia peduncularis

Darwinia peduncularis, commonly known as the slender spikeheal, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its tall, spike-like inflorescence. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and open forests.

Darwinia peduncularis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
