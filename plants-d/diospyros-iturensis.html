Diospyros iturensis

Diospyros iturensis, commonly known as the Iberian persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes ebony and persimmon trees. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into orange to red fruits. The fruits are typically eaten fresh or used in traditional remedies.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat and is valued for its ornamental and edible qualities.
