Dysophylla crassicaulis

Dysophylla crassicaulis, commonly known as the broad-stemmed dysophylla, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate inflorescences.

It is native to southern Africa, where it typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant features a robust, branching stem with opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy. Its small white to pale yellow flowers appear in clustered umbels, and it produces small, dry fruits known as schizocarps.

Dysophylla crassicaulis is generally hardy and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
