Dryostachyum hieronymi

Dryostachyum hieronymi, commonly known as the Hieronymus grass, is a perennial grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces inflorescences that are typically panicle-like, with small, spikelet-based flowers. It thrives in moist, tropical environments and is often found in swampy or wetland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is cultivated in some regions for landscaping and conservation purposes.
