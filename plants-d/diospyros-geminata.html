Diospyros geminata

Diospyros geminata, commonly known as the African date palm, is a small to medium-sized tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its pinnate leaves and produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in panicles. The tree bears small, fleshy drupes that are typically orange to red in color. Its bark is thick and fissured, and it has a shallow root system adapted to dry conditions.

Diospyros geminata is often used in traditional medicine and has cultural significance in various African communities.
