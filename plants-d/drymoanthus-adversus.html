Drymoanthus adversus

Drymoanthus adversus, commonly known as the silverleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of silver-gray, hairy leaves that help reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow composite flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Due to its adaptability, it is considered a common weed in many regions.
