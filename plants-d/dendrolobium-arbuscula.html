Dendrolobium arbuscula

Dendrolobium arbuscula, commonly known as the tree legume or small tree legume, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant has trifoliate leaves with three oval leaflets and produces small, yellow to orange flowers. It is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Dendrolobium arbuscula plays a role in nitrogen fixation, making it ecologically significant in its native habitat.
