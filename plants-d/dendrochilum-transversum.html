Dendrochilum transversum

Dendrochilum transversum, commonly known as the cross-veined orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and elongated, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers with a distinctive cross-veined pattern on the petals. It typically blooms during the warmer months, showcasing vibrant colors such as pink, red, and orange.

Dendrochilum transversum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in horticulture.
