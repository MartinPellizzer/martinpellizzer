Dimeria lehmannii var. aristata

Dimeria lehmannii var. aristata, commonly known as the desert daisy, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a low, sprawling growth habit with hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures.

Dimeria lehmannii var. aristata plays an important ecological role by providing nectar for pollinators in its native habitat.
