Dileptium virginicum

Dileptium virginicum, commonly known as the common wintergreen, is a herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are evergreen, glossy, and elliptical, providing year-round foliage.

Dileptium virginicum thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.
