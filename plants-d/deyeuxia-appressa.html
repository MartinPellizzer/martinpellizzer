Deyeuxia appressa

Deyeuxia appressa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sedges, is a species of grass found in wetland environments.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and compact growth habit. The plant typically grows in marshy areas and along the edges of ponds and streams. Its leaves are narrow and often have a smooth texture, while its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a loose panicle.

Deyeuxia appressa plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
