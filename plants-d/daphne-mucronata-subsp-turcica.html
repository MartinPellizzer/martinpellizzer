Daphne mucronata subsp. turcica

Daphne mucronata subsp.

turcica, commonly known as the Turkish daphne, is a flowering plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its fragrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in spring.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves and produces yellowish-green berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens, though it requires careful handling due to its toxic properties.
