Dryopteris oreocharis var. canescens

Dryopteris oreocharis var. canescens, commonly known as the silver fern, is a variant of the oreocharis fern that is native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its distinctive fronds that are covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it a unique appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its leaves are bipinnate, with a fern-like structure that consists of multiple pinnae.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
