Diadesma virgata

Diadesma virgata, commonly known as the fragrant sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Diadesma virgata is often found in sandy or disturbed soils and is adapted to thrive in arid environments.
