Danthonia trichopteryx

Danthonia trichopteryx, commonly known as the tufted hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds. The plant features tufted inflorescences with dense, spike-like clusters of small, spikelet-like flowers. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and often have a bluish-green color.

Danthonia trichopteryx plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
