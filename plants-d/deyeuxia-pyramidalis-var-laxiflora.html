Deyeuxia pyramidalis var. laxiflora

Deyeuxia pyramidalis var. laxiflora, commonly known as the looseflower bulrush, is a perennial grass species native to wetland habitats in eastern North America.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant features slender, triangular stems that are often reddish in color, and its inflorescence consists of a loose, pyramidal arrangement of small, spikelet-like flowers. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and the plant produces small, wind-pollinated flowers that mature into brown, fluffy seeds.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
