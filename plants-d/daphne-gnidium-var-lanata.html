Daphne gnidium var. lanata

Daphne gnidium var. lanata, commonly known as the woolly daphne, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers in early spring. Its leaves are typically thick and leathery, and the stems are covered in a soft, white pubescence.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique texture.
