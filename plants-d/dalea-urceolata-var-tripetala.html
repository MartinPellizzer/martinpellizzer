Dalea urceolata var. tripetala

Dalea urceolata var. tripetala, commonly known as the three-lobed milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the southwestern United States.

It features slender, branching stems and trifoliate leaves with three elliptic leaflets. The plant produces small, yellow to orange flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its ecological importance includes nitrogen fixation and providing nectar for pollinators.
