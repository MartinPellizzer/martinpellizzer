Diospyros virginiana var. insignis

Diospyros virginiana var. insignis, commonly known as the Florida persimmon, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptical in shape and have a smooth, glossy surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into orange to reddish-brown berries, which are edible when fully ripe. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, swamps, and along stream banks.

The Florida persimmon is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in supporting local wildlife.
