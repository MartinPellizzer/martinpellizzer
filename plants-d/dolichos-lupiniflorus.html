Dolichos lupiniflorus

Dolichos lupiniflorus, commonly known as the purple bean or wild lupin, is a climbing legume native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It features trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped leaflets and produces clusters of violet to purple flowers. The plant produces elongated, bean-like pods that contain several seeds. It is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches the soil.

Its botanical characteristics make it suitable for use in ecological restoration and as a forage crop.
