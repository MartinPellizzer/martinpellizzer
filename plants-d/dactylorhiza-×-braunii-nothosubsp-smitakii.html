Dactylorhiza × braunii nothosubsp. smitakii

Dactylorhiza × braunii nothosubsp.

smitakii, commonly known as the Smits' marsh orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to parts of Europe. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of pale green to yellowish flowers.

The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, preferring acidic soils. It has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store nutrients and water.

Due to its specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many regions.
