Dalea sericea

Dalea sericea, commonly known as silky milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant features slender, branching stems covered with soft, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its small, pink to purple flowers bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Dalea sericea is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in native plant gardens.
