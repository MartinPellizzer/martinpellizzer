Dendrobium speciosum var. typicum

Dendrobium speciosum var. typicum, commonly known as the orchid of the hills, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is an epiphytic plant, typically growing on trees or rocks in humid, tropical environments. The plant produces long, slender pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, supporting its growth in nutrient-poor conditions. Its flowers are showy, with intricate patterns of white, pink, and purple, often appearing in clusters.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is cultivated for its beauty and resilience.
