Dracocephalum canariense

Dracocephalum canariense, commonly known as the Canary Island dragonhead, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its small, tubular flowers are typically blue to purple in color, arranged in whorls along the stem.

It is often found in rocky or coastal habitats, thriving in well-drained soils.
