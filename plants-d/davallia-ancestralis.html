Davallia ancestralis

Davallia ancestralis, commonly known as the ancient fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Davalliaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. This plant is native to Southeast Asia and is typically found in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are fibrous and can spread to form dense colonies.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded gardens or as a houseplant.
