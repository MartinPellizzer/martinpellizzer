Diospyros vestita

Diospyros vestita, commonly known as the silky persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by orange-red, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are covered in a fine, silky hair, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Diospyros vestita is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
