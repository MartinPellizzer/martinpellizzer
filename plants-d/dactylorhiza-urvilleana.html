Dactylorhiza urvilleana

Dactylorhiza urvilleana, commonly known as the purple orchid, is a species of orchid native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a terrestrial herb with a pseudobulb and produces a single, showy purple flower with a distinctive lip. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, and it has a symbiotic relationship with mycorrhizal fungi.

Dactylorhiza urvilleana is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens.
