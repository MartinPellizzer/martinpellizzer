Diospyros agnitser

Diospyros agnitser, commonly known as the Indian persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into orange to red, fleshy fruits. The fruit has a sweet, edible pulp surrounding a central core containing seeds.

It is valued for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
