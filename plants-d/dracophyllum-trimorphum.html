Dracophyllum trimorphum

Dracophyllum trimorphum, commonly known as the three-form heath, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage and small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces three distinct forms of its leaves—needle-like, scale-like, and broad—depending on its growth conditions. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is adapted to coastal climates with salt spray.

Dracophyllum trimorphum is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and resilience.
