Diospyros keningauensis

Diospyros keningauensis, commonly known as the Keningau persimmon, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Borneo.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark-barked trunk and lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by edible, orange-red fruits. Its fruits are typically harvested when fully ripe and are valued for their sweet, nutty flavor.

The tree is also noted for its potential in ornamental horticulture and local traditional uses.
