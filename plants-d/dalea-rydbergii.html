Dalea rydbergii

Dalea rydbergii, commonly known as Rydberg's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats, such as grasslands and open woodlands. Its seed pods are slender and contain several small, kidney-shaped seeds.

Dalea rydbergii is often found in regions with low precipitation and is adapted to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.
