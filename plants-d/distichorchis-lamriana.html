Distichorchis lamriana

Distichorchis lamriana, commonly known as the lamriana orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, linear leaves and its unique inflorescence that bears showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its flowers are pollinated by insects, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
