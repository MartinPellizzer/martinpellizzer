Dipladenia gentianoides

Dipladenia gentianoides, commonly known as the trumpet vine or yellow bell, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a climbing vine that produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange. The plant features opposite, heart-shaped leaves and is known for its vigorous growth habit. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Its showy blooms attract hummingbirds and other pollinators, adding both aesthetic and ecological value to the landscape.
