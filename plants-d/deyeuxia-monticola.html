Deyeuxia monticola

Deyeuxia monticola, commonly known as the mountain false rice, is a perennial grass native to mountainous regions of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. The plant has slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of dense clusters of small, spike-like flowers.

Deyeuxia monticola is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
