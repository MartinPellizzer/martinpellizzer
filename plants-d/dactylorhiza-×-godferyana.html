Dactylorhiza × godferyana

Dactylorhiza × godferyana, commonly known as the Godfrey's orchid, is a hybrid orchid native to the wetlands of western Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that bear clusters of fragrant, violet or purple flowers. The plant typically blooms in late summer to early autumn, producing showy, spurred flowers that attract pollinators. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and requires a cool, humid environment to flourish.

Dactylorhiza × godferyana is a popular ornamental plant in gardens due to its striking appearance and ease of cultivation.
