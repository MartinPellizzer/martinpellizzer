Dianella longifolia var. laevis

Dianella longifolia var. laevis, commonly known as the smooth-flowered flax lily, is a perennial herb native to New Zealand.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color and have a smooth texture. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in spring, followed by blue-black berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in native landscaping and conservation efforts.
