Delphinium purpurascens

Delphinium purpurascens, commonly known as the purple-hued larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers in late spring to early summer, arranged in dense spikes at the top of the stem. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Delphinium purpurascens is a member of the Ranunculaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
