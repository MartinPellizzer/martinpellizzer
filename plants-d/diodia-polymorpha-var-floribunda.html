Diodia polymorpha var. floribunda

Diodia polymorpha var. floribunda, commonly known as the Florida nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a shrub that typically grows in moist, sandy soils along the coastlines and wetlands. The plant produces small, white or purple flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a slightly hairy texture, and it has a strong, often unpleasant odor.

The plant contains toxic compounds and is considered a weed in some agricultural settings.
