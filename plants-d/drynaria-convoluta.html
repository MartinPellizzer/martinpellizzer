Drynaria convoluta

Drynaria convoluta, commonly known as the golden fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its creeping rhizome and pinnate fronds with a distinctive golden hue on the underside. The plant is native to Southeast Asia and is often found growing on rocks or tree trunks in humid, shaded environments. Drynaria convoluta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
