Dioscorea dumetosa

Dioscorea dumetosa, commonly known as the creeping yam, is a perennial vine native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and tuberous roots. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by yellow berries that contain the seeds. Its tubers are often used in traditional medicine and as a food source in some cultures.

The leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
