Dichromanthus aurantiacus

Dichromanthus aurantiacus, commonly known as the orange dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its bright orange flowers and basal rosette of leaves. The plant produces small, elongated seeds that are dispersed by wind, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Dichromanthus aurantiacus is a short-lived perennial that blooms in late spring to early summer.
