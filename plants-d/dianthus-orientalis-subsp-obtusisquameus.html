Dianthus orientalis subsp. obtusisquameus

Dianthus orientalis subsp.

obtusisquameus, commonly known as the Armenian campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountains of Armenia and Turkey. It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and produces clusters of pink to red, five-petaled flowers.

The leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a bluish-green color. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
