Durio gerikensis

Durio gerikensis, commonly known as the Gerik durian, is a tropical fruit-bearing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Durio genus within the Malvaceae family, which includes other durian species. The plant produces large, spiky fruits with a strong aroma and a creamy, custard-like pulp. Its leaves are broad and ovate, with a glossy green surface.

The tree can grow up to 15 meters tall and thrives in humid, warm climates with well-drained soil.
