Dendrocalamus latiflorus var. lagenarius

Dendrocalamus latiflorus var. lagenarius, commonly known as the giant bamboo, is a tall, fast-growing species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its robust, hollow stems that can reach heights of up to 30 meters. The plant has broad, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters. It is highly valued for its strong and durable culms, which are used in construction and crafts.

This variety is also important in traditional medicine and as a source of bamboo shoots.
