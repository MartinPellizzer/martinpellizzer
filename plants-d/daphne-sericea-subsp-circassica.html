Daphne sericea subsp. circassica

Daphne sericea subsp.

circassica, commonly known as the Caucasian Daphne, is a deciduous shrub native to the Caucasus region. It is characterized by its finely hairy, silver-gray foliage and clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant produces small, oval berries that are typically red when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
