Dombeya botryoides

Dombeya botryoides, commonly known as the snowball bush, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of southeastern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, spherical clusters of white flowers that bloom in early spring, creating a striking visual display. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and a smooth, grayish-brown bark. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
