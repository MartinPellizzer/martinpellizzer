Diospyros diversifolia

Diospyros diversifolia, commonly known as the variable persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elliptical leaves that are usually alternate and have a glossy, dark green upper surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in panicles, followed by edible, orange to red berries. Its bark is smooth and grayish, with a faintly aromatic scent.

Diospyros diversifolia is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions.
