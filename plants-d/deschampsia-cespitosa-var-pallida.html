Deschampsia cespitosa var. pallida

Deschampsia cespitosa var. pallida, commonly known as silver hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray blades that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant forms dense, tufted clumps and is often found in moist to wet habitats such as meadows, marshes, and stream banks. Its flowering spikes are usually slender and produce small, spikelet-based flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
