Delphinium brunonianum var. nordhagenii

Delphinium brunonianum var. nordhagenii, commonly known as the Nordhagen's larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces showy, blue to violet flowers arranged in dense spikes, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, with a rough texture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
