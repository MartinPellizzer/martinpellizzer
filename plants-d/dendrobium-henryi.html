Dendrobium henryi

Dendrobium henryi, commonly known as the Henry's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to China.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a rhizome that produces multiple pseudobulbs. The plant typically has lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, yellowish-green flowers. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Dendrobium henryi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its unique floral display.
