Dryopteris diaphana

Dryopteris diaphana, commonly known as the translucent wood fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Dryopteridaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lacy fronds that display a translucent appearance due to the presence of thin, membranous tissue. Native to the forests of Southeast Asia, it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant's rhizomes are typically short and stout, with a dense network of roots that help anchor it in moist soil.

Its unique morphology makes it a popular choice for botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
