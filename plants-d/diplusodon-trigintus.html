Diplusodon trigintus

Diplusodon trigintus, commonly known as the thirty-leaved thornplant, is a species of flowering plant native to arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of white and yellow. The plant has adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments through its succulent leaves and deep root system. Its leaves are often arranged in a spiral pattern, providing efficient sunlight absorption.

Despite its tough exterior, the plant plays a role in supporting local wildlife by providing shelter and nectar for pollinators.
