Delphinium plenum

Delphinium plenum, commonly known as the full-flowered larkspur, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that bear clusters of blue to purple, spurred flowers. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stem. Delphinium plenum thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in alpine or rocky habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
