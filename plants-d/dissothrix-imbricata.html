Dissothrix imbricata

Dissothrix imbricata, commonly known as the Brazilian velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows up to 1.5 meters tall, characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in spikes, and it is often found in disturbed soils and agricultural areas. Dissothrix imbricata is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in humid, warm climates.

Its botanical features make it both a useful forage plant and a potential weed in cultivated fields.
