Doronicum latisquamatum

Doronicum latisquamatum, commonly known as the broad-scale leopard's bane, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant features elongated, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy coating and serrated edges. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in dense clusters, and bloom in late summer.

Doronicum latisquamatum is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, often found in alpine and subalpine environments.
