Didymocarpus ericiflorus

Didymocarpus ericiflorus, commonly known as the silverleaf acanthus, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage and striking, spiny leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, pale green flowers that are followed by red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its unique morphology and ornamental value make it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation.
