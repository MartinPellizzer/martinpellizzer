Draba lutea var. brevipes

Draba lutea var. brevipes, commonly known as the yellow rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. This variety is distinguished by its shorter stature and yellow flowers compared to the typical form of Draba lutea. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to flourish in harsh environmental conditions.
