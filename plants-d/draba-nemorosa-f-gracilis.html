Draba nemorosa f. gracilis

Draba nemorosa f. gracilis, commonly known as the slender woodruff, is a low-growing perennial herb native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, linear, and often covered in fine pubescence.

The flowers are white or pale yellow, appearing in clusters and blooming in early spring.
