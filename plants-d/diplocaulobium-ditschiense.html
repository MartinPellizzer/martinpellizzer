Diplocaulobium ditschiense

Diplocaulobium ditschiense, commonly known as the desert bell, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form with small, glossy leaves that reduce water loss. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and low rainfall.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
