Dioscorea eburnea

Dioscorea eburnea, commonly known as the African yambean, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary part used for culinary and medicinal purposes. The plant produces small, green flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its tubers are rich in starch and have been traditionally used as a food source and in traditional medicine.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.
