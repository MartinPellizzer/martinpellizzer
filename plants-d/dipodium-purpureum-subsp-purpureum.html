Dipodium purpureum subsp. purpureum

Dipodium purpureum subsp.

purpureum, commonly known as the purple dipodium, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Southeast Asia. It features slender, upright stems that bear lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive purple hue on the underside.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species within the family Polypodiaceae.
