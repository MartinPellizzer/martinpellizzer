Dianthus plumarius subsp. plumarius

Dianthus plumarius subsp.

plumarius, commonly known as the common carnation, is a flowering plant native to Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its showy, bilaterally symmetrical flowers that typically range in color from pink to red, with a distinctive white or pink center.

The plant has a compact growth habit, often forming a dense mat of foliage and blooms. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are usually upright and branching.

This species is widely cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
