Dendrobium exaltatum

Dendrobium exaltatum, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is an epiphytic plant that typically grows on trees or rocks, thriving in humid and warm climates. The plant produces striking, showy flowers in shades of yellow and orange, often with a white or pink throat. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in varying environmental conditions.

Dendrobium exaltatum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is relatively easy to cultivate with proper care.
