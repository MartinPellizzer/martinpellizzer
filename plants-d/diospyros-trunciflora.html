Diospyros trunciflora

Diospyros trunciflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved persimmon, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves with a truncate or notched base. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by edible, orange-red berries. Its bark is thick and fissured, often exuding a sticky resin.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its fruit, which is appreciated in local cuisines.
