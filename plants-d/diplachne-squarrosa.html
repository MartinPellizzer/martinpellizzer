Diplachne squarrosa

Diplachne squarrosa, commonly known as the silky grass or silver grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and tufted growth habit, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its inflorescence consists of dense, cylindrical clusters of small, spikelet-like flowers.

Diplachne squarrosa is often used in ornamental gardening due to its aesthetic appeal and low maintenance requirements.
