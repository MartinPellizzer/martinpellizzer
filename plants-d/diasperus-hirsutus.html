Diasperus hirsutus

Diasperus hirsutus, commonly known as the hairy diasperus, is a species of flowering plant belonging to the family Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is native to certain regions of Africa and is often found in dry, arid environments.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in traditional medicine and as a forage plant for livestock.
