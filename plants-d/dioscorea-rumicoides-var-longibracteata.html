Dioscorea rumicoides var. longibracteata

Dioscorea rumicoides var. longibracteata, commonly known as the long-bract yam, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, climbing stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers with a distinctive, sweet fragrance. Its tuberous roots are edible and have been traditionally used in local cuisines.

The varietal name "longibracteata" refers to its elongated bracts that surround the flowers.
