Disocactus crenatus subsp. crenatus

Disocactus crenatus subsp.

crenatus, commonly known as the Christmas cactus, is a species of flowering cactus native to Brazil. It is characterized by its segmented, thornless stems that form a branching structure.

The plant produces vibrant flowers in shades of red, pink, and white during the winter months. It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil.

Its unique appearance and seasonal blooming make it a popular ornamental plant.
