Deyeuxia violacea

Deyeuxia violacea, commonly known as violet false grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wet meadows. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with a violet hue, giving it its common name. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of small, spike-like clusters, which produce tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Deyeuxia violacea is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded, humid conditions.
