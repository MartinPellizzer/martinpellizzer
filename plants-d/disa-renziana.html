Disa renziana

Disa renziana, commonly known as the Zulu orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of bright pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its unique floral structure features three sepals and three petals, with the latter often being highly modified.

Disa renziana is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its cultural significance in Zulu traditions.
