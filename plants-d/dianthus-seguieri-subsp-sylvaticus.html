Dianthus seguieri subsp. sylvaticus

Dianthus seguieri subsp.

sylvaticus, commonly known as the woodland campion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It typically grows in rocky or wooded areas and produces clusters of pink or red flowers with five petals.

The plant has linear, grass-like leaves and a compact growth habit, making it well adapted to its alpine or subalpine habitat. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soils.

The species is part of the Caryophyllaceae family, known for its showy flowers and succulent characteristics.
