Diplandra

Diplandra, commonly known as the yellow dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces bright yellow flowers that develop into fluffy seeds. Diplandra thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
