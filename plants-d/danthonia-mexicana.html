Danthonia mexicana

Danthonia mexicana, commonly known as Mexican feather grass, is a perennial grass native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-green foliage and delicate, feathery inflorescence that resembles a plume. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping. It is drought-tolerant and requires minimal maintenance once established.

Botanically, it belongs to the Poaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
