Dioscorea bulbifera var. anthropophagorum

Dioscorea bulbifera var. anthropophagorum, commonly known as the giant arrowroot or devil's tongue, is a tropical climbing vine native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and the production of aerial tubers, which are edible but can be toxic if not properly prepared. The plant thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its rapid growth habit makes it an effective ground cover in tropical gardens.

However, it is important to note that the plant contains toxic compounds, particularly in its leaves and unripe tubers, which can be harmful if ingested.
