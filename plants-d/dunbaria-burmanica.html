Dunbaria burmanica

Dunbaria burmanica, commonly known as the Burmese bellflower, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30-60 cm and has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shaded areas.

Dunbaria burmanica is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
