Danaea dubia

Danaea dubia, commonly known as the Brazilian flame flower, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Onagraceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular red flowers that resemble flames. The plant produces showy, brightly colored bracts that surround its small, inconspicuous flowers. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Danaea dubia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
