Dendrobenthamia pachyphylla

Dendrobenthamia pachyphylla, commonly known as the thick-leaved dendrobium, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is an epiphytic plant, meaning it grows on the surface of trees without deriving nutrients from them. The plant is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting it to its often arid or semi-arid habitat. Its flowers are typically showy, with a variety of colors, and it blooms during the wet season.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
