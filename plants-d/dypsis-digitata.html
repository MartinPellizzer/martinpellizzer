Dypsis digitata

Dypsis digitata, commonly known as the Madagascar palm, is a tropical plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiny, bottle-shaped trunk and clusters of feathery, palm-like leaves. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters in its natural habitat. Its leaves are arranged in a rosette pattern and are often used in traditional medicine.

Dypsis digitata is also popular as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
