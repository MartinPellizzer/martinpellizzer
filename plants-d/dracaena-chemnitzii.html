Dracaena chemnitzii

Dracaena chemnitzii, commonly known as the Congo dracaena, is a flowering plant native to Central and Western Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, strap-like leaves that can grow up to 2 meters in length. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers followed by red or orange berries. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires bright, indirect light.

Dracaena chemnitzii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
