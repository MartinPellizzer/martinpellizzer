Dypsis littoralis

Dypsis littoralis, commonly known as the coastal palm, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is native to Madagascar and is characterized by its slender, unbranched trunk and pinnate leaves. The plant typically grows in coastal areas, tolerating salt spray and sandy soils. Its flowers are small and clustered, and it produces edible fruits that are consumed by various animals.

Dypsis littoralis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and resilience.
