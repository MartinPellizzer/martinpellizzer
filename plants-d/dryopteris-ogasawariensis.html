Dryopteris ogasawariensis

Dryopteris ogasawariensis, commonly known as the Ogasawara shield fern, is a species of polypod fern native to Japan.

It is characterized by its finely divided, arching fronds that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, brownish sporangia on the underside of its fertile fronds, which are arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest undergrowth.

Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it a notable species within the Dryopteris genus.
