Dioscorea nummularia var. velutina

Dioscorea nummularia var. velutina, commonly known as the velvet yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its hairy, velvety stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into cylindrical, fleshy fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid forest understories.

The tuberous roots of this species are sometimes used in traditional medicine and as a food source.
