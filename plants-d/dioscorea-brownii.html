Dioscorea brownii

Dioscorea brownii, commonly known as the African yam bean, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary part used for culinary and medicinal purposes. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while the stems are often hairy.

The tubers are rich in starch and have been traditionally used in various African cuisines.
