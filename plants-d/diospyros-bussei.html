Diospyros bussei

Diospyros bussei, commonly known as the African persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and broad, ovate leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into orange-red, fleshy fruits. Its fruit is edible, though typically consumed when fully ripe, and is valued for its sweet flavor.

The plant is also notable for its ecological importance, providing food for various wildlife species.
