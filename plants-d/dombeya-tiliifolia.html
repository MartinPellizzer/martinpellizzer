Dombeya tiliifolia

Dombeya tiliifolia, commonly known as the white dombeya, is a deciduous shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy, cup-shaped flowers that range from white to pink, typically blooming in spring. The plant has slender, woody stems with opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy underneath. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Dombeya tiliifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
