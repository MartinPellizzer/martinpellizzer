Diospyros ferrea var. indonesica

Diospyros ferrea var. indonesica, commonly known as ironwood or mangrove apple, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood that is highly valued for construction and furniture. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and mangrove forests, adapting to saline and waterlogged soils. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a glossy green surface.

The fruit is a small, round drupe that turns red when mature, attracting various wildlife species.
