Damburneya salicina

Damburneya salicina, commonly known as the silky damburneya, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, pendulous inflorescences that produce showy, yellow-orange flowers. The plant has narrow, strap-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky texture, giving it its common name. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Damburneya salicina is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
