Doryopteris lorentzii f. interrupta

Doryopteris lorentzii f. interrupta, commonly known as the interrupted lady fern, is a fern species characterized by its delicate, bipinnate fronds.

It features a distinctive pattern of interrupted pinnae, giving it a unique and ornamental appearance. Native to the temperate regions of South America, particularly Brazil, it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, with slender, creeping stems that produce new fronds along their length.

Its foliage is typically a deep green, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and woodland landscapes.
