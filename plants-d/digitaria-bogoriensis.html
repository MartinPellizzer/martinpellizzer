Digitaria bogoriensis

Digitaria bogoriensis, commonly known as the Bogoriensis Digitaria, is a grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, tropical environments and is often found in wetlands or along water bodies.

Due to its adaptability, it plays a role in stabilizing soil in aquatic habitats.
