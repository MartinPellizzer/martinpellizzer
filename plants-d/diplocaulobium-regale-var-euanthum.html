Diplocaulobium regale var. euanthum

Diplocaulobium regale var. euanthum, commonly known as the royal diplocaulobium, is a striking ornamental plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a dramatic visual impact in gardens. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant blooms. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

This variant is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and tropical landscapes.
