Desmodium rhytidophyllum

Desmodium rhytidophyllum, commonly known as the roughleaf desmodium, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, often found in open woodlands and grasslands. Its small, pea-like flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces hairy, three-lobed seed pods.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in poor soils and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
