Draba scopulorum f. elongata

Draba scopulorum f. elongata, commonly known as the elongated rockcress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically thrives in rocky or gravelly soils, often found in alpine or arctic environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and linear, adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.

This variety is distinguished by its elongated leaves compared to the typical form of Draba scopulorum.
