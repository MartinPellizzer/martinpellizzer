Dionysia tacamahaca

Dionysia tacamahaca, commonly known as the Mexican moonflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that open at night. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments such as forest understories. Its flowers emit a fragrant scent to attract nocturnal pollinators like moths.

Dionysia tacamahaca is valued for its ornamental beauty and has cultural significance in some indigenous communities.
