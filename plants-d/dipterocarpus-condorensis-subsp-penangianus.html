Dipterocarpus condorensis subsp. penangianus

Dipterocarpus condorensis subsp.

penangianus, commonly known as Penang ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability.

The plant belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family, which includes many important timber species. It typically grows in lowland rainforests and is often found in mixed dipterocarp forests.

The species is known for its ecological importance and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
