Dicoma argyrophylla

Dicoma argyrophylla, commonly known as silverleaf dandelion, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features a basal rosette of silver-gray, hairy leaves that help reduce water loss in arid conditions. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, followed by fluffy seeds dispersed by the wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Dicoma argyrophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses.
