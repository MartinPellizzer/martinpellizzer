Diospyros lycioides subsp. nitens

Diospyros lycioides subsp.

nitens, commonly known as the glossy persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia. It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and elliptical leaves that are glossy on both sides.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange to red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in lowland forests or along riverbanks.

The fruit is edible when fully ripe and is valued for its sweet, astringent flavor.
