Diospyros persimon

Diospyros persimon, commonly known as the Chinese persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and smooth, dark bark. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that give way to orange to red fruits with a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor.

The fruit is often eaten fresh or used in jams and wines, and the tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological benefits.
