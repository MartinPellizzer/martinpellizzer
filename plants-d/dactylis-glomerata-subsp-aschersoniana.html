Dactylis glomerata subsp. aschersoniana

Dactylis glomerata subsp.

aschersoniana, commonly known as the aschersonian red brome, is a perennial grass species native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and distinctive reddish-brown inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, with a bluish-green color that becomes more reddish in maturity.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and erosion control.
