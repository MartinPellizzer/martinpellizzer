Dactylis glomerata f. aprica

Dactylis glomerata f. aprica, commonly known as the spring meadow grass, is a variant of the common cock's-foot grass.

It is a perennial grass species characterized by its compact growth habit and dense, tufted inflorescence. The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and pastures. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, while the flowers form small, rounded clusters.

This form is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in agricultural settings.
