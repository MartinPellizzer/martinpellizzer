Deschampsia cespitosa var. convoluta

Deschampsia cespitosa var. convoluta, commonly known as curlleaf bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and dense, tufted growth habit. The leaves are typically narrow and slightly rolled at the edges, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wet meadows and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
