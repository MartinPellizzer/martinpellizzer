Datura metel

Datura metel, commonly known as the Thornapple or Devil's Trumpet, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically white or purple in color. The plant produces toxic berries that contain tropane alkaloids, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals. Datura metel thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, often growing in disturbed habitats.

Its rapid growth and striking appearance make it a notable species in both ornamental and ecological contexts.
