Descurainia pinnata subsp. glabra

Descurainia pinnata subsp.

glabra, commonly known as hoary tansy, is a herbaceous annual plant native to North America. It is characterized by its glabrous (smooth) stems and leaves with a silvery-white pubescence.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, typical of the Brassicaceae family. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Its ecological role includes providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a host plant for certain insects.
