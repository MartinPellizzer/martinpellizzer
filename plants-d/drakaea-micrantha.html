Drakaea micrantha

Drakaea micrantha, commonly known as the small-horned greenhood, is an orchid species native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its unique, hooded flower that resembles a small horn, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in heathlands and open forests, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually greenish-yellow with a distinctive hood shape, and they bloom during the autumn season.

Drakaea micrantha is a terrestrial orchid that relies on mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake, showcasing its ecological complexity.
