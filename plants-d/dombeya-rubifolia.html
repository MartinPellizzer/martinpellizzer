Dombeya rubifolia

Dombeya rubifolia, commonly known as the red-flowered dombeya, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of Thailand and Malaysia.

It is characterized by its clusters of showy, pink to reddish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has simple, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. Dombeya rubifolia thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and botanical collections.
