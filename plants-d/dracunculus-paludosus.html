Dracunculus paludosus

Dracunculus paludosus, commonly known as the swamp lantern or giant hogweed, is a striking plant native to wetland areas of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, stout stems that can grow up to 3 meters in height, often with a reddish or purplish hue. The plant features large, umbrella-like clusters of white or pale yellow flowers, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and often have a distinctive waxy coating.

Dracunculus paludosus is a perennial herb that thrives in moist, rich soils and is often found near ponds, marshes, and stream banks.
