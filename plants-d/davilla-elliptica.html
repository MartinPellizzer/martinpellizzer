Davilla elliptica

Davilla elliptica, commonly known as the flame tree or Brazilian flame tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its opposite, elliptical leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer, followed by bright red, papery fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their potential health benefits.
