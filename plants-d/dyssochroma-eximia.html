Dyssochroma eximia

Dyssochroma eximia, commonly known as the purple-leafed dyssochroma, is a shrub native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking purple foliage, which becomes more intense during the dry season. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are thick and waxy, adaptations that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment.

Dyssochroma eximia is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
