Dianthus namaensis var. namaensis

Dianthus namaensis var. namaensis, commonly known as the Namib Desert carnation, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Namibia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant pink to red flowers that bloom during the cooler months. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and is well adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments. It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, known for its showy flowers and succulent-like stems.

This species plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by providing nectar for pollinators and stabilizing sandy soils.
