Dracocephalum turkestanicum

Dracocephalum turkestanicum, commonly known as Turkistan dragonhead, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their medicinal properties. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. It thrives in rocky or arid environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

The species is valued for its essential oils and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
