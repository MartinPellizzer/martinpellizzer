Deschampsia cespitosa subsp. orientalis

Deschampsia cespitosa subsp.

orientalis, commonly known as oriental feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to eastern North America. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color.

The plant produces delicate, feathery inflorescences that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions and full to partial sunlight.

This subspecies is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
