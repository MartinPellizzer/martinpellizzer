Davallia polypodioides

Davallia polypodioides, commonly known as the bird's nest fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Davalliaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, shield-shaped fronds that resemble a bird's nest, hence its common name. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, with creeping rhizomes that produce new shoots and roots. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Its foliage is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its attractive, lush appearance.
