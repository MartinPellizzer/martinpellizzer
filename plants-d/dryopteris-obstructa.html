Dryopteris obstructa

Dryopteris obstructa, commonly known as the barren barren fern, is a species of fern native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are typically dark green and slightly curled. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are located on the underside of the fronds and are covered by a protective indusium.

Dryopteris obstructa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
