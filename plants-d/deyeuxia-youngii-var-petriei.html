Deyeuxia youngii var. petriei

Deyeuxia youngii var. petriei, commonly known as the Petrie's false brome, is a grass species native to the mountainous regions of central and southern China.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems that bear spikelets along their length. The plant produces small, two-rowed spikelets with leathery, lanceolate lemmae, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native range.
