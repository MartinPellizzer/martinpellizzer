Digitaria mollicoma var. glabra

Digitaria mollicoma var. glabra, commonly known as smooth Digitaria, is a grass species characterized by its smooth, hairless leaves and stems.

It typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in disturbed soils or open habitats. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. Its morphology is adapted to withstand environmental stress, making it a resilient species in various ecological settings.

It is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.
