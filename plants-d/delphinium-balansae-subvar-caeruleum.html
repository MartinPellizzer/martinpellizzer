Delphinium balansae subvar. caeruleum

Delphinium balansae subvar. caeruleum, commonly known as the blue delphinium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that bear clusters of showy, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or meadow habitats at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a delicate, fern-like appearance, and its flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects.

Due to its striking appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
