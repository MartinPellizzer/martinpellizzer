Diplostephium floribundum subsp. putumayense

Diplostephium floribundum subsp.

putumayense, commonly known as the Putumayo candelabra, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its branched, candelabra-like structure.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom during the dry season. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in disturbed or secondary forest habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and for horticultural purposes.
