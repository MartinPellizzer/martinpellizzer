Dactylorhiza maculata subsp. fuchsii

Dactylorhiza maculata subsp.

fuchsii, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland or grassland habitats.

The plant features a single, upright stem with several lance-shaped leaves and a single spike of pale yellow to greenish-white flowers. Its flowers have a distinctive purple or brownish marking on the lip, which is a key identifying feature.

The species is part of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit.
