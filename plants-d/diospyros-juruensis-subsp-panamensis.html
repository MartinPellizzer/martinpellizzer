Diospyros juruensis subsp. panamensis

Diospyros juruensis subsp.

panamensis, commonly known as the Panamanian persimmon, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central America. It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into orange to red, fleshy fruits.

The fruit is typically harvested when fully ripe, and it is valued for its sweet, edible pulp and potential medicinal properties.
