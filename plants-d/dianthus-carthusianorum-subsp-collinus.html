Dianthus carthusianorum subsp. collinus

Dianthus carthusianorum subsp.

collinus, commonly known as the alpine campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its pink or white flowers, which bloom in early summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it forms low, compact mats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
