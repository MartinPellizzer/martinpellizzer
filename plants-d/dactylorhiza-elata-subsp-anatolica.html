Dactylorhiza elata subsp. anatolica

Dactylorhiza elata subsp.

anatolica, commonly known as the Anatolian marsh orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeastern Europe and parts of Western Asia. It typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, often near water bodies such as lakes and rivers.

The plant has a slender, erect stem with several elongated, linear leaves. Its flowers are pale green to yellowish in color, arranged in a dense spike, and are often adorned with dark brown or purple markings.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological significance and is often found in calcareous soils.
