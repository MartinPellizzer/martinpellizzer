Didymocarpus dielsii

Didymocarpus dielsii, commonly known as the Chinese tamarind tree, is a deciduous tree native to southern China, Vietnam, and the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow-orange flowers that bloom in spring. The fruit is a woody pod containing several seeds, and the tree is valued for its hard wood and medicinal properties.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.
