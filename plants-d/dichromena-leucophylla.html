Dichromena leucophylla

Dichromena leucophylla, commonly known as the white-leaf dichromena, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive white, waxy leaves and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and swampy areas. Its leaves are often described as having a leathery texture, and the flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

Dichromena leucophylla is often found in association with other wetland plants, contributing to the ecological diversity of its habitat.
