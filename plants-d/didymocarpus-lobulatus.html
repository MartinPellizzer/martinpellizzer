Didymocarpus lobulatus

Didymocarpus lobulatus, commonly known as the sour orange.

It is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, orange-colored fruits that are sour and acidic, often used in traditional medicine. Its botanical features include a woody stem, opposite leaf arrangement, and a distinctive fruit structure with a thick, segmented rind.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.
