Dactylorhiza × insignis

Dactylorhiza × insignis, commonly known as the yellow orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that bear clusters of yellow to orange flowers with a distinctive three-lobed lip. The plant is a terrestrial orchid, typically growing in moist, shaded woodland habitats. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn, and it relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake.

Dactylorhiza × insignis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with the right conditions.
