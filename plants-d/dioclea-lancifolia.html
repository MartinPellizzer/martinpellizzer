Dioclea lancifolia

Dioclea lancifolia, commonly known as the sharp-leaved dioclea, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and showy, butterfly-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and agricultural contexts.
