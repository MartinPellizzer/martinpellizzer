Diasperus trachyspermus

Diasperus trachyspermus, commonly known as wild cumin, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, branching stems and small, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive aromatic scent. The plant produces yellow flowers followed by cylindrical, aromatic seed pods containing numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or rocky habitats.

The seeds are used in traditional medicine and as a spice, valued for their pungent flavor and essential oils.
