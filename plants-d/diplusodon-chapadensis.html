Diplusodon chapadensis

Diplusodon chapadensis, commonly known as the Brazilian pineapple cactus, is a species of cactus native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems that store water. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, followed by edible fruits resembling pineapples. Its adaptations to arid environments include thick, fleshy stems and reduced leaves.

Diplusodon chapadensis is often found in savannas and rocky areas, where it thrives in full sun.
