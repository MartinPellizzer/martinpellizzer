Davallodes novoguineensis

Davallodes novoguineensis, commonly known as the New Guinea climbing vine, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of New Guinea.

It is a scrambling vine characterized by its long, flexible stems and compound leaves with elongated leaflets. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by bean-like pods containing seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing along tree trunks or in dense undergrowth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in tropical gardens.
