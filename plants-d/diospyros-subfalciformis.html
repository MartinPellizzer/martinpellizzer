Diospyros subfalciformis

Diospyros subfalciformis, commonly known as the narrow-sickle persimmon, is a species of flowering plant in the Ebenaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including India, China, and Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptic to oblong in shape, often with a distinct base that curves inward. Its small, yellowish flowers are clustered in panicles, and it produces edible berries that are typically orange to red in color.

The wood of Diospyros subfalciformis is hard and durable, making it valuable for traditional crafts and construction.
