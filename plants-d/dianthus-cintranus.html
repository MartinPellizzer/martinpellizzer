Dianthus cintranus

Dianthus cintranus, commonly known as the narrowleaf campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. The plant has slender, branching stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and coastal areas.

Dianthus cintranus is a perennial species that is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
