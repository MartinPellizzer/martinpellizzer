Dianella pendula

Dianella pendula, commonly known as the hanging sea oats, is a flowering plant native to coastal areas of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its grass-like, evergreen leaves that grow in dense tufts and are often adorned with white or pale blue flowers. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is well adapted to salt spray and coastal conditions. Its flowering period typically occurs during the summer months, attracting various pollinators.

Dianella pendula is often used in coastal landscaping due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
