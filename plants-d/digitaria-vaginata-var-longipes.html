Digitaria vaginata var. longipes

Digitaria vaginata var. longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed digitaria, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, cylindrical stems that can grow up to several feet in height, often with a slightly hairy texture. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a prominent midrib and a bluish-green color. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, two-lobed spikelets.

This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands or disturbed areas.
