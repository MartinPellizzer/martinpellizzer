Danthonia secundiflora

Danthonia secundiflora, commonly known as the common tussock grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Danthonia secundiflora plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
