Disocactus aurantiacus

Disocactus aurantiacus, commonly known as the red-tipped orchid cactus, is a flowering cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in soft, velvety spines and its striking, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light.

Disocactus aurantiacus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in a home environment.
