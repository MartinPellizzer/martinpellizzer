Doronicum tianshanicum

Doronicum tianshanicum, commonly known as the Tianshan leopard's bane, is a perennial herb native to the Tianshan Mountains in Central Asia.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its upright stem and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving at high elevations. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers have a distinctive, tubular structure.

Doronicum tianshanicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
