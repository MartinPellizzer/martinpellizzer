Dactylorhiza phoenissa

Dactylorhiza phoenissa, commonly known as the purple orchid, is a species of orchid native to parts of Europe and North Africa.

It is a terrestrial herb with a pseudobulbous structure, typically growing in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces tall, slender stems with showy purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are linear and arranged in a rosette pattern around the pseudobulbs.

Dactylorhiza phoenissa is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
