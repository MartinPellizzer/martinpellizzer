Draba rupestris var. laxa

Draba rupestris var. laxa, commonly known as the looseleaf rockcress, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arctic and alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh, cold climates. The plant features basal leaves that are often lobed or divided, and it produces small, white or yellowish flowers in early spring. It is part of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with characteristic cruciform flowers.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often found in mountainous and tundra environments.
