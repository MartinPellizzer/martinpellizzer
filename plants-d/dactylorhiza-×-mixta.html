Dactylorhiza × mixta

Dactylorhiza × mixta, commonly known as the hybrid marsh orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of fragrant, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, thriving in moist, nutrient-poor soils. It has a short-lived rhizome and produces a single spike of flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Dactylorhiza × mixta is often found in association with other orchid species in its native environment.
