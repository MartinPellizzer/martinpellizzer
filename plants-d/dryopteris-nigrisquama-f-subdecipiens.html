Dryopteris nigrisquama f. subdecipiens

Dryopteris nigrisquama f. subdecipiens, commonly known as the black shield fern, is a fern species characterized by its dark, leathery fronds.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is native to parts of Asia, including Japan and China. The fronds are typically biennial, with the fertile fronds developing in the second year. The plant prefers shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its distinctive blackish-green foliage and unique morphology make it a notable ornamental and ecological species.
