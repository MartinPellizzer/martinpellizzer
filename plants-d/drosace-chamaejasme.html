Drosace chamaejasme

Drosace chamaejasme, commonly known as the snowdrop, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive bell-shaped flowers that typically bloom in early spring. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, white or greenish-yellow flowers that hang from a stem. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodlands and meadows.

Drosace chamaejasme is valued for its ornamental beauty and its ability to bloom even in harsh winter conditions.
