Dysphania aristata var. inermis

Dysphania aristata var. inermis, commonly known as New Zealand spinach, is a leafy vegetable native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its succulent, edible leaves that are similar in texture to spinach. The plant thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its nutritional value and mild flavor. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun exposure for optimal growth.

This variety is notable for its ability to withstand mild frost, making it a versatile crop in temperate regions.
