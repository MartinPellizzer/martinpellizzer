Dolichos trilobus var. richardsiae

Dolichos trilobus var. richardsiae, commonly known as the Richards' horsegram, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by elongated, slender pods containing lens-shaped seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its edible seeds, which are used in traditional cuisines.

The species is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, contributing to soil fertility.
