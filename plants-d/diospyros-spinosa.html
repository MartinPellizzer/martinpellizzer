Diospyros spinosa

Diospyros spinosa, commonly known as the prickly persimmon, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its spiny branches and oval-shaped leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by orange-red berries, which are edible when fully ripe. Diospyros spinosa thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm climate with adequate sunlight.

It is often used in ornamental gardening due to its unique appearance and hardy nature.
