Didymocarpus floccosus

Didymocarpus floccosus, commonly known as the silky tamarind, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and covered in fine, silky hairs. Its flowers are small and yellowish-green, typically arranged in clusters, and it produces edible, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

The botanical features of Didymocarpus floccosus make it a distinctive and valuable species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
