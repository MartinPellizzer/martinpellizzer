Daphne retusa

Daphne retusa, commonly known as the common daphne, is a perennial evergreen shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and are borne on slender stems. The plant has aromatic, glandular leaves that are typically dark green and leathery in texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for garden borders.

Daphne retusa is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its winter blooming habit.
