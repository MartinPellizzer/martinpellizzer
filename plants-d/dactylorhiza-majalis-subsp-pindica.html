Dactylorhiza majalis subsp. pindica

Dactylorhiza majalis subsp.

pindica, commonly known as the Pindus orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Balkan Peninsula. It typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and meadows.

The plant features a slender, erect stem with several lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are pale green to yellow, with a distinctive three-lobed lip.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance.
