Dictyosperma furfuraceum

Dictyosperma furfuraceum, commonly known as the hairy sedge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant is characterized by its cylindrical, grass-like stems and narrow, strap-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically small and clustered in spikelets, which are arranged in inflorescences.

The species is often found in wetland habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
