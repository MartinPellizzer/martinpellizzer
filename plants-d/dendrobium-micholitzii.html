Dendrobium micholitzii

Dendrobium micholitzii, commonly known as the Micholitz's orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit, with cane-like stems that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white, yellow, and pink, typically during the dry season. Its leaves are long and strap-like, adapted to conserve moisture in its natural habitat.

Dendrobium micholitzii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
