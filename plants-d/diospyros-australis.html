Diospyros australis

Diospyros australis, commonly known as the Australian persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Australia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange to red, fleshy berries that are edible when ripe. Its fruit is valued for its sweet, astringent flavor and is often used in traditional Aboriginal cuisine.

Diospyros australis is also appreciated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
