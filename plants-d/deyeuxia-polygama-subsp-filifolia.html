Deyeuxia polygama subsp. filifolia

Deyeuxia polygama subsp.

filifolia, commonly known as the slender false panicgrass, is a grass species native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan. It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and small, spike-like inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green color.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates in its native environment.
