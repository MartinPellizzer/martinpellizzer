Dracocephalum foetidum

Dracocephalum foetidum, commonly known as shiso or Japanese shiso, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants such as mint and basil. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped, slightly hairy leaves that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. Its small, pale blue or violet flowers appear in clusters and attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Dracocephalum foetidum is valued for its distinctive flavor and its traditional use in herbal medicine.
