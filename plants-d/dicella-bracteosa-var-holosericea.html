Dicella bracteosa var. holosericea

Dicella bracteosa var. holosericea, commonly known as the silky bract dicella, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of silky, white hairs, giving the plant its common name. The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variant is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
