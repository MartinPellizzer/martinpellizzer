Dioscorea buchtienii

Dioscorea buchtienii, commonly known as the Buchtien yam, is a climbing herbaceous perennial native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its vigorous vine growth and tuberous roots. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers and has compound leaves with elongated leaflets. Its tubers are often used in traditional medicine and as a food source in some local communities.

The botanical features of D. buchtienii make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
