Dioscorea bemarivensis

Dioscorea bemarivensis, commonly known as the Madagascar yam, is a member of the Dioscoreaceae family and is native to Madagascar.

It is a climbing vine that produces tuberous roots, which are the primary part used for its medicinal and nutritional properties. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in humid, shaded environments. Its tubers are rich in starch and have been traditionally used in local medicine for their purported health benefits.

The botanical features of D. bemarivensis make it an important species in both ecological and ethnobotanical contexts.
