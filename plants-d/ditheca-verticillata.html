Ditheca verticillata

Ditheca verticillata, commonly known as the clustered leaf dandelion, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a fibrous root system and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It is adapted to arid conditions and is drought-tolerant.

The flowers are followed by fluffy, white seed heads that aid in wind pollination.
