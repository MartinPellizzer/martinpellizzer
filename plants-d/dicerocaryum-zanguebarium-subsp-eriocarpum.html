Dicerocaryum zanguebarium subsp. eriocarpum

Dicerocaryum zanguebarium subsp.

eriocarpum, commonly known as the Zanzibar tamarind, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of East Africa. It is characterized by its compound leaves with long, slender leaflets and small, clustered flowers that range from white to pale yellow. The fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds, and the plant is often found in sandy or limestone soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
