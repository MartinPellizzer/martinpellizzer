Dendrochilum perplexum var. montanum

Dendrochilum perplexum var. montanum, commonly known as the mountain dendrochilum, is a rare orchid species native to high-altitude regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces intricate, fragrant flowers with a distinctive coloration. The plant typically grows on rock faces or in shaded forest areas, showcasing its adaptability to challenging environments. Its leaves are thick and leathery, aiding in water retention in its arid habitat.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a threatened species requiring conservation efforts.
