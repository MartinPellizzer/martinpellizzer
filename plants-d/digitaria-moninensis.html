Digitaria moninensis

Digitaria moninensis, commonly known as the African silver grass, is a perennial grass species native to Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, blade-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets, typical of the grass family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Digitaria moninensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
