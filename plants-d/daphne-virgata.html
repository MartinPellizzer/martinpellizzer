Daphne virgata

Daphne virgata, commonly known as the silverleaf daphne, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features small, fragrant flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring, often before the leaves emerge. The plant has smooth, grayish bark and lance-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Daphne virgata is also noted for its ornamental value and is often used in horticultural displays.
