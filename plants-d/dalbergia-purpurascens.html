Dalbergia purpurascens

Dalbergia purpurascens, commonly known as the purpleheart tree, is a tropical hardwood species native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its dense, dark purple to reddish-brown heartwood. The tree can grow up to 25 meters tall and has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, and it produces small, woody pods containing seeds.

The wood is highly valued for its durability and rich color, making it popular in furniture and musical instrument manufacturing.
