Dracaena draco

Dracaena draco, commonly known as the dragon tree, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands and northern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, branching trunk and narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are typically dark green. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters at the ends of its branches. Its unique red sap, known as dracma, has been historically used for medicinal and decorative purposes.

Dracaena draco is well adapted to arid environments and thrives in well-drained soil with ample sunlight.
