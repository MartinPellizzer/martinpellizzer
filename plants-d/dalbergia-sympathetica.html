Dalbergia sympathetica

Dalbergia sympathetica, commonly known as the African rosewood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The tree produces hard, dense wood that is highly valued for its beauty and workability. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate.

Due to overharvesting, it is also considered a threatened species in some regions.
