Dysphania plantaginella

Dysphania plantaginella, commonly known as common lambsquarters, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs and have a slightly fuzzy texture. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Dysphania plantaginella is a member of the Chenopodiaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
