Digitaria baileyi var. patens

Digitaria baileyi var. patens, commonly known as the flatsear grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its flat, spreading leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields. Its foliage is often used in floral arrangements due to its attractive, silver-gray appearance.

Botanically, it is notable for its adaptability to various soil types and its role in stabilizing soil in ecological restoration projects.
