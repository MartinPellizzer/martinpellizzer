Desmos macrocarpus

Desmos macrocarpus, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, round, floating leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in diameter. The plant has a thick, spongy stem that supports its expansive foliage and provides a habitat for various aquatic organisms. Its flowers are showy, with white petals and yellow stamens, and they bloom at night, attracting specific pollinators.

Desmos macrocarpus plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing water surfaces and supporting biodiversity.
