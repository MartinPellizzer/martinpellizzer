Disticholiparis biglobulifera

Disticholiparis biglobulifera, commonly known as the double-lobed globular vine, is a unique plant characterized by its distinctive floral structure.

It features two rows of globular flowers along its elongated stems, giving it a striking appearance. The plant is native to specific tropical regions and thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are typically broad and heart-shaped, contributing to its ornamental value.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens for study and display.
