Disa uliginosa

Disa uliginosa, commonly known as the bog orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach up to 1 meter in height, often adorned with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces striking, showy flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in bogs, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
