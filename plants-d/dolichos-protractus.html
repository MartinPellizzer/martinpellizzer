Dolichos protractus

Dolichos protractus, commonly known as the longpod bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated pods that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers, and its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm climate for optimal growth.

The seeds are often used in traditional cuisines and can be processed into flour or used as animal feed.
