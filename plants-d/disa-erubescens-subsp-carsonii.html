Disa erubescens subsp. carsonii

Disa erubescens subsp.

carsonii, commonly known as Carson's Disa, is a species of orchid native to South Africa. It is characterized by its striking red and orange flowers, which bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in mountainous regions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces a single flower spike with several showy blooms.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant colors.
