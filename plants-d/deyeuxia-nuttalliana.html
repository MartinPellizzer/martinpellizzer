Deyeuxia nuttalliana

Deyeuxia nuttalliana, commonly known as Nuttall's flat sedge, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has slender, triangular stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often green or reddish in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a rough texture, and it is often found in moist, nutrient-rich soils.

Deyeuxia nuttalliana plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
