Draba norvegica var. glabrata

Draba norvegica var. glabrata, commonly known as the Norwegian rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and pungent odor. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations, where it thrives in harsh, cold climates. Its leaves are basal, rosette-forming, and often covered in a dense layer of hairs.

The flowers, which bloom in early spring, are yellow or white and attract various pollinators such as bees and flies.
