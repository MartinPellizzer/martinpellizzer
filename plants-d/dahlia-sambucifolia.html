Dahlia sambucifolia

Dahlia sambucifolia, commonly known as the sambucus-leaved dahlia, is a herbaceous perennial native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant has large, heart-shaped leaves that resemble those of the European elderberry, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Dahlia sambucifolia is valued for its ornamental value and is often grown for its vibrant blooms.
