Dysoxylum vestitum

Dysoxylum vestitum, commonly known as the hairy tamarind, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae and is characterized by its rough, bark-like trunk and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody pods containing flat, edible seeds. It thrives in humid climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and traditional contexts.
