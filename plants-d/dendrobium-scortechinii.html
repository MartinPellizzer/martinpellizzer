Dendrobium scortechinii

Dendrobium scortechinii, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with multiple pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers with red and orange markings, often blooming in late winter or early spring. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Dendrobium scortechinii is prized for its ornamental value and is commonly cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
