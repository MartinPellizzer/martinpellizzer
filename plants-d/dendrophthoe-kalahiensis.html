Dendrophthoe kalahiensis

Dendrophthoe kalahiensis, commonly known as the Kalahari mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant native to southern Africa.

It attaches itself to the branches of host trees, primarily acacia species, and derives some of its nutrients from them. The plant produces small, white or pale green berries that are an important food source for certain bird species. Its leaves are semi-photosynthetic, allowing it to produce some of its own energy.

Due to its specialized growth habits, it plays a unique role in the ecological dynamics of its native habitat.
