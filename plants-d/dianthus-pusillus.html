Dianthus pusillus

Dianthus pusillus, commonly known as the dwarf carnation, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features compact, rosette-forming foliage with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Dianthus pusillus thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact size and vibrant blooms make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
