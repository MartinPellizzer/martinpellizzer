Drypetes glomerata

Drypetes glomerata, commonly known as the clustered drypetes, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, clustered yellowish-green flowers that are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in horticultural settings.
