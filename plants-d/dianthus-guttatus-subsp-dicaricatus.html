Dianthus guttatus subsp. dicaricatus

Dianthus guttatus subsp.

dicaricatus, commonly known as the Sierra dianthus, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and produces clusters of pink to magenta flowers with five petals.

The plant features hairy stems and leaves that are often reddish or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to rocky or alpine environments.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
