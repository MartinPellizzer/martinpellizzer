Diplecthrum membranaceum

Diplecthrum membranaceum, commonly known as the glassy fern, is a unique aquatic plant characterized by its delicate, translucent fronds.

It typically grows in shallow, still waters such as ponds and wetlands, where it can receive adequate sunlight. The plant's leaves are long and ribbon-like, often appearing as if they are made of glass due to their thin and clear texture. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and oxygen for various aquatic organisms.

Due to its fragile nature, it is often found in protected environments where it can thrive without disturbance.
