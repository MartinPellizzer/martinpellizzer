Diplachne tolucensis

Diplachne tolucensis, commonly known as the Tolucan grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically arranged in dense clusters. Diplachne tolucensis is adapted to a variety of soil types but thrives in well-drained, sandy or loamy soils.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for livestock in its native habitat.
