Discocarpus brasiliensis

Discocarpus brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian starfruit tree, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Anacardiaceae and is characterized by its small, star-shaped fruits that are edible. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a rough texture, while its flowers are small and clustered.

The fruit, which is the most notable feature, has a unique appearance with a segmented, star-like shape and a sweet, tangy flavor.
