Dasycarpus

Dasycarpus, commonly known as the "thick-fruited grass," is a genus of flowering plants in the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, spike-like inflorescences and tough, fibrous stems. The plants are typically found in temperate and subtropical regions, often growing in dry, rocky soils. Dasycarpus species are known for their ornamental value, with some varieties displaying vibrant autumn colors.

The genus includes several species, such as Dasycarpus tetrazicus, which is cultivated for its decorative grass-like appearance.
