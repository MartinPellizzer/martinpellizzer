Dianthus awaricus

Dianthus awaricus, commonly known as the Andean carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, high-altitude regions. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, seed-like fruits known as capsules.

Dianthus awaricus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
