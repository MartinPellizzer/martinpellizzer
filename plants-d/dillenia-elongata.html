Dillenia elongata

Dillenia elongata, commonly known as the elongated dillenia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dilleniaceae and is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves that often have a velvety texture. The plant produces showy, yellowish-green flowers with five petals, which are typically followed by distinctive, woody, club-shaped fruits. Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological studies and horticultural cultivation.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments.
