Delphinium nortonianum

Delphinium nortonianum, commonly known as Norton's larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the western United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are compound, with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets, and it produces showy, spurred flowers that attract pollinators.

Delphinium nortonianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in high-altitude ecosystems.
