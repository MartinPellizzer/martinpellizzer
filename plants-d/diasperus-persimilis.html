Diasperus persimilis

Diasperus persimilis, commonly known as the slender rock-daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It is often found in coastal and mountainous regions, where it thrives in well-drained conditions.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
