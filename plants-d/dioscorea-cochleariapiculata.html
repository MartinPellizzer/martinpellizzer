Dioscorea cochleariapiculata

Dioscorea cochleariapiculata, commonly known as the African yam bean, is a climbing vine belonging to the Dioscoreaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its tubers, while not as widely cultivated as other yam species, are edible and have been used in traditional cuisines.

The botanical features of D. cochleariapiculata make it an important plant in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
