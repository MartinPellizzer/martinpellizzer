Dianthus falconeri

Dianthus falconeri, commonly known as the Canary Islands campion, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces showy, pink or purple flowers with five petals. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are typically lance-shaped. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to the arid climate of its native habitat.

Dianthus falconeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
