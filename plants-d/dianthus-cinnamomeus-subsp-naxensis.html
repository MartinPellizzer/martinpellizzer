Dianthus cinnamomeus subsp. naxensis

Dianthus cinnamomeus subsp.

naxensis, commonly known as the Naxos carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to red.

The plant produces fragrant, glandular hairs on its stems and leaves, which contribute to its aromatic qualities. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its vibrant blooms.
