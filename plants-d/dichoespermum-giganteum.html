Dichoespermum giganteum

Dichoespermum giganteum, commonly known as the giant milk bush, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, palm-like leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant produces a milky sap, which is used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
