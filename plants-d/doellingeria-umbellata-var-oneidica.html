Doellingeria umbellata var. oneidica

Doellingeria umbellata var. oneidica, commonly known as the Oneida water shield, is a aquatic plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves that are typically 5-10 centimeters in diameter. The plant produces small, white flowers arranged in an umbel, which is a cluster of flowers with stalks of equal length. It thrives in shallow, freshwater habitats such as ponds and slow-moving streams.

The leaves are often submerged or floating, providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
