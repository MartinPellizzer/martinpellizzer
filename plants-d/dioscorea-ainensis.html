Dioscorea ainensis

Dioscorea ainensis, commonly known as the African yam bean, is a climbing perennial vine native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary storage organs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and has compound leaves with three to five leaflets. Its tubers are edible and have been traditionally used as a food source in some African communities.

The plant also has medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
