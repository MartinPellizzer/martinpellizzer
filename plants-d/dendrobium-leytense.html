Dendrobium leytense

Dendrobium leytense, commonly known as the Leyte orchid, is an epiphytic orchid native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its upright, cane-like stems that store water and nutrients. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in a range of colors, including white, yellow, and orange, often in clusters. It thrives in warm, humid environments with indirect light and well-draining soil.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat destruction, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
