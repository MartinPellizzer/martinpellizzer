Diclytra divaricata

Diclytra divaricata, commonly known as the variable leaf grass.

It is a perennial grass species native to Africa, characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology that varies in shape and size depending on environmental conditions. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and grasslands. Its leaves are often narrow and have a rough, hairy texture, while the inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, spike-like structures.

Diclytra divaricata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
