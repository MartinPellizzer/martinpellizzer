Dianthus calocephalus var. villiger

Dianthus calocephalus var. villiger, commonly known as the violet campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and produces showy, pink to purple flowers with five petals. The leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, and the plant thrives in well-drained soils. It is often found in rocky or sandy habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

This variety is closely related to other dianthus species, sharing similar botanical features such as the presence of glandular dots on the stems.
