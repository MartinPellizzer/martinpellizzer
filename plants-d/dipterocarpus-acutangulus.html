Dipterocarpus acutangulus

Dipterocarpus acutangulus, commonly known as the Malayan ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its heavy, durable wood that is highly valued for construction and furniture. The tree can grow up to 40 meters tall, with a straight trunk and a dense canopy. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a waxy surface, while its flowers are small and clustered in spikes.

The plant plays an important ecological role in tropical rainforests, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
