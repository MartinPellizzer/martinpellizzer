Dendrobium ventrilabium

Dendrobium ventrilabium, commonly known as the golden orchid, is an epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with multiple pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange, often clustered in racemes. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, adapting to its habitat in tropical forests.

Dendrobium ventrilabium is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated for both horticultural and commercial purposes.
