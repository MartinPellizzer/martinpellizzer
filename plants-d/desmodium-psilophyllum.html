Desmodium psilophyllum

Desmodium psilophyllum, commonly known as slender desmodium, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant has a taproot system and produces pods that contain several small, round seeds.

It is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils, and it plays a role in nitrogen fixation due to its association with rhizobia bacteria.
