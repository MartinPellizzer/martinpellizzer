Decaisnina signata subsp. cardiophylla

Decaisnina signata subsp.

cardiophylla, commonly known as the broad-leafed decaisnina, is a member of the Myrtaceae family native to Australia. It is characterized by its distinctive, broad, ovate leaves that are often glossy and have a prominent midrib.

The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers arranged in clusters, typically during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are often used in traditional Aboriginal medicine for their purported healing properties.

The subspecies is distinguished by its broader leaf shape compared to other subspecies within the species.
