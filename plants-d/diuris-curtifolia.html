Diuris curtifolia

Diuris curtifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved spider-orchid, is an Australian native orchid species characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and distinctive spider-like flowers.

It typically grows in heathland and dry sclerophyll forests, preferring well-drained soils and sunny to partially shaded conditions. The plant produces pale green to yellowish flowers with a unique, intricate structure that resembles a spider, hence its common name. It is often found in South Australia, Victoria, and Tasmania, and is adapted to survive in arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and threatened species in some regions.
