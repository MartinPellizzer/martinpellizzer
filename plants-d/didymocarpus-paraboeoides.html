Didymocarpus paraboeoides

Didymocarpus paraboeoides, commonly known as the broad-leafed ironwood, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and thick, woody trunk. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in cup-like structures called cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its hard, heavy wood is highly valued for construction and furniture-making.

The plant is also noted for its ability to thrive in coastal and tropical environments.
