Digitaria connivens

Digitaria connivens, commonly known as the common bluestem, is a grass species native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, blue-green leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brown florets. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is commonly found in prairies and disturbed areas.

Digitaria connivens is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife.
