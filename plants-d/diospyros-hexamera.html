Diospyros hexamera

Diospyros hexamera, commonly known as the six-angled persimmon, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and alternate, ovate leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by orange to red, fleshy berries. Its fruits are edible when fully ripe, containing a sweet, honey-like pulp surrounding a central core of seeds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental value and traditional uses in medicine and cuisine.
