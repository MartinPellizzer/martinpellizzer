Dendranthema × aphrodite

Dendranthema × aphrodite, commonly known as the German chamomile, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow central disk. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and features linear, aromatic leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in temperate regions.

The essential oils extracted from its flowers are widely used in aromatherapy and herbal remedies.
