Diospyros kintungensis

Diospyros kintungensis, commonly known as the Kintung or Kintung ebony, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its dark, dense wood that is highly valued for its strength and beauty. The plant typically features simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a glossy green upper surface. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by edible, round fruits that are similar in appearance to persimmons.

The species is also noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
