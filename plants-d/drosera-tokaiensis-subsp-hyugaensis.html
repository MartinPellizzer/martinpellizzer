Drosera tokaiensis subsp. hyugaensis

Drosera tokaiensis subsp.

hyugaensis, commonly known as the Hyuga Sundew, is a carnivorous plant native to Japan. It belongs to the family Droseraceae and is characterized by its sticky, glandular hairs that trap small insects.

The plant typically grows in moist, acidic environments such as bogs and wetlands. Its leaves are usually rosette-shaped and covered in a dense layer of digestive secretions.

It is a relatively rare and specialized species, making it a subject of interest for botanists and plant enthusiasts.
