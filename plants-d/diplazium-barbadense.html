Diplazium barbadense

Diplazium barbadense, commonly known as the giant chain fern, is a tropical fern native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its large, feather-like fronds that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, with a creeping underground stem that produces both roots and new shoots. Its spores are released from specialized structures called sori, which are located on the underside of the fronds.

Diplazium barbadense thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests and coastal areas.
