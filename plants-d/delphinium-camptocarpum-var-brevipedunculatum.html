Delphinium camptocarpum var. brevipedunculatum

Delphinium camptocarpum var. brevipedunculatum, commonly known as the short-stemmed larkspur, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are compound, with lance-shaped leaflets, and the flowers are showy, attracting various pollinators.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be toxic to livestock.
