Dalanum × wirtgenii

Dalanum × wirtgenii, commonly known as the hybrid poinsettia, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Dalanum coccineum and Dalanum luteum. The plant is characterized by its vibrant, colorful bracts that resemble flowers, which are actually modified leaves. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value during the holiday season.

The botanical features of Dalanum × wirtgenii make it a popular choice in horticulture and floral arrangements.
