Dendrobium caladenia

Dendrobium caladenia, commonly known as the spider orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, intricate flowers that often resemble spiders, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in heathlands and grassy areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowering period occurs during the autumn season, producing vibrant pink or purple blooms.

Dendrobium caladenia is known for its delicate appearance and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
