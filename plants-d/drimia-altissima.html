Drimia altissima

Drimia altissima, commonly known as the African lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features tall, erect stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, adorned with lance-shaped, dark green leaves. The plant produces striking white or pale purple flowers in umbels, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Drimia altissima is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
