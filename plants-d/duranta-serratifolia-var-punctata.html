Duranta serratifolia var. punctata

Duranta serratifolia var. punctata, commonly known as the spotted duranta, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a contrasting red throat. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and have serrated edges, giving the plant its species name. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The varietal name 'punctata' refers to the small, dark spots that appear on the leaves, adding to its visual appeal.
