Digitaria gentilis

Digitaria gentilis, commonly known as wild millet, is a grass species native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based flowers that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Digitaria gentilis is also cultivated in some regions for its edible seeds, which are used in traditional foods.
