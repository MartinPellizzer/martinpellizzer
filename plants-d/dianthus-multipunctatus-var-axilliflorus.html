Dianthus multipunctatus var. axilliflorus

Dianthus multipunctatus var. axilliflorus, commonly known as the clustered campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces seed pods that split open to release tiny seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
