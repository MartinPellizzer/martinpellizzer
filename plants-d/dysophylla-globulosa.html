Dysophylla globulosa

Dysophylla globulosa, commonly known as the globe thistle, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its spherical, spiny seed heads that resemble small globes, giving it its common name. The plant produces blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

Dysophylla globulosa is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
