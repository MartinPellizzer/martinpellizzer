Drepanostachyum jainianum

Drepanostachyum jainianum, commonly known as the Jain's bamboo, is a member of the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 6 meters. The plant features narrow, linear leaves with a prominent midrib, and its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets. It is native to the Indian subcontinent and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and for erosion control.
