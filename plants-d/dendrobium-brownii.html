Dendrobium brownii

Dendrobium brownii, commonly known as the Brown's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with multiple pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, yellow-green flowers with red veins during the warm season. Its leaves are elongated and grass-like, often arranged in two ranks.

Dendrobium brownii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated for horticultural purposes.
