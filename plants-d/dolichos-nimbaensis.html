Dolichos nimbaensis

Dolichos nimbaensis, commonly known as the Nimba bean, is a climbing legume native to the Nimba Mountains in West Africa.

It produces small, pale green to yellow flowers that are typically clustered in racemes. The plant has compound leaves with three to five elliptical leaflets and is characterized by its long, slender pods. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Dolichos nimbaensis is valued for its edible seeds, which are used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
