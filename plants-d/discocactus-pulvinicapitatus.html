Discocactus pulvinicapitatus

Discocactus pulvinicapitatus, commonly known as the "Paddle Cactus," is a unique cactus species native to Mexico.

It features a cylindrical stem with a flattened, paddle-like shape that helps it store water efficiently. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers during the warmer months. Its leaves are modified into spines, providing protection from herbivores.

The Discocactus pulvinicapitatus is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water to thrive.
