Dicksonia spruceana

Dicksonia spruceana, commonly known as the giant tree fern, is a member of the Cyatheaceae family and is native to the tropical and subtropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk covered in a thick layer of persistent, fibrous roots. The plant produces large, feather-like fronds that can grow up to several meters in length, creating a dense, tree-like appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and requires high humidity and well-draining soil.

Dicksonia spruceana is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking visual appeal and ability to add a tropical feel to landscapes.
