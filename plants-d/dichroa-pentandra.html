Dichroa pentandra

Dichroa pentandra, commonly known as the Chinese evergreen, is a striking ornamental plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves that have a strikingly different color on their upper and lower surfaces, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers followed by bright red berries, which are a notable feature. It thrives in shady, humid environments and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and ease of care.

Its adaptability makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and shaded outdoor spaces.
