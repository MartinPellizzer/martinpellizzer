Dryopteris mangindranensis

Dryopteris mangindranensis, commonly known as the Madagascar wood fern, is a species of fern native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its robust, leathery fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forests and rocky areas. Its sporangia are clustered in sori on the underside of the fronds, a common feature in ferns.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage.
