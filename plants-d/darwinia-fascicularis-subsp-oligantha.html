Darwinia fascicularis subsp. oligantha

Darwinia fascicularis subsp.

oligantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved Darwinia, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia. It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit.

The plant produces showy, spike-like inflorescences that range in color from pink to purple, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun.

This subspecies is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
