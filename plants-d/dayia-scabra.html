Dayia scabra

Dayia scabra, commonly known as the rough dayia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive, hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in arid and semi-arid regions.
