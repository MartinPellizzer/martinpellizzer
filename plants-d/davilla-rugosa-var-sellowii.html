Davilla rugosa var. sellowii

Davilla rugosa var. sellowii, commonly known as the Brazilian flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are opposite and pinnate, with serrated margins. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

The species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
