Dipholis bellonis

Dipholis bellonis, commonly known as the bell orchid, is a unique species belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. The plant is native to southern Africa, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are elongated and grass-like, and it produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in spring.

The orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
