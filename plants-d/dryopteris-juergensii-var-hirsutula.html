Dryopteris juergensii var. hirsutula

Dryopteris juergensii var. hirsutula, commonly known as the Hirsutula Shield Fern, is a variant of the Juergens' Shield Fern.

It is a deciduous fern native to the southeastern United States, particularly found in moist, shaded woodlands. The plant features large, shield-shaped fronds with a distinctive hirsute (hairy) texture on the underside. Its leaves are typically dark green and have a leathery texture, with a prominent midrib.

This fern thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is valued for its ornamental appearance in shaded garden settings.
