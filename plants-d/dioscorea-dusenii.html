Dioscorea dusenii

Dioscorea dusenii, commonly known as the Dusen yam, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary part used for culinary and medicinal purposes. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are hairy and twining.

The tubers of Dusen yam are often used in traditional medicine and can be prepared as a vegetable in certain cuisines.
