Dianthus fimbriatus subsp. monspessulanus

Dianthus fimbriatus subsp.

monspessulanus, commonly known as the Spanish carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces clusters of pink or white flowers with fringed petals.

The plant typically blooms in spring and is known for its ornamental value in gardens. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, thriving in Mediterranean climates.

This subspecies is closely related to other members of the Dianthus genus, sharing similar horticultural traits.
