Dicella holosericea var. argentea

Dicella holosericea var. argentea, commonly known as the silverleaf dandelion, is a variant of the dandelion species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its soft, silvery-white downy covering on both the leaves and the flower heads, which gives it its distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny areas and has a rosette-forming habit with basal leaves. Its flowers are yellow and bloom in early spring, followed by the characteristic parachute-like seeds.

The botanical features of this variant make it a unique and visually striking member of the Asteraceae family.
