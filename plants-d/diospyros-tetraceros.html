Diospyros tetraceros

Diospyros tetraceros, commonly known as the four-horned ebony, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by edible, round, dark purple berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The wood of Diospyros tetraceros is dense and valued for its durability and aesthetic qualities.
