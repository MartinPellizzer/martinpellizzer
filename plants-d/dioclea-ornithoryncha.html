Dioclea ornithoryncha

Dioclea ornithoryncha, commonly known as the "macaw-bird bean," is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces pods containing seeds that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and greenhouses.
