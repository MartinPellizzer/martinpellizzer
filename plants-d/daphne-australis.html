Daphne australis

Daphne australis, commonly known as the New Zealand daphne, is a deciduous shrub native to New Zealand.

It features slender, aromatic leaves with a distinctive blue-green hue and produces clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers in spring. The plant is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and thrives in well-drained, acidic soils. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively hardy in temperate climates.

Daphne australis is also valued for its role in supporting local wildlife, particularly native insects and birds.
