Diasperus bernierianus

Diasperus bernierianus, commonly known as the Bernier's rock cress, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many flowering plants known for their small, cruciform flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments. Its leaves are basal, forming a rosette, and it produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

Diasperus bernierianus is often found in alpine meadows and is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological adaptations.
