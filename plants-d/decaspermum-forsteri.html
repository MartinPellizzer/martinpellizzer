Decaspermum forsteri

Decaspermum forsteri, commonly known as the coastal daisy, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils along the shoreline. The plant features showy yellow flowers with white markings, which bloom from late winter to early spring. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in a fine layer of white wax.

Decaspermum forsteri is well adapted to salt spray and strong coastal winds, making it a hardy and resilient species.
