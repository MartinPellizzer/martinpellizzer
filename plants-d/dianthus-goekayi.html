Dianthus goekayi

Dianthus goekayi, commonly known as the Armenian carnation, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Armenia and Turkey.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, while the stems are usually hairy and upright.

Dianthus goekayi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
