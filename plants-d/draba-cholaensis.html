Draba cholaensis

Draba cholaensis, commonly known as the Chola's whitlow grass, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other mustard family plants. The plant is characterized by its low-growing habit, with small, basal leaves and clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. It typically thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.

Draba cholaensis is often found in meadows and slopes at elevations above 3,000 meters.
