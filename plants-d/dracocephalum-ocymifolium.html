Dracocephalum ocymifolium

Dracocephalum ocymifolium, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the United States, particularly in the southeastern region.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Dracocephalum ocymifolium is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
