Diospyros rosei

Diospyros rosei, commonly known as the rose flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that appear in clusters. The tree produces small, edible drupes that are typically consumed by local wildlife and humans. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a distinctive red or orange color when mature.

Diospyros rosei is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in its native habitat.
