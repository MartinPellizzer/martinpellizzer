Diospyros carbonaria

Diospyros carbonaria, commonly known as the African persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and alternate, simple leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into orange-red, fleshy fruits. The fruit is edible, with a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor, and is valued for its nutritional content.

It also has historical uses in traditional medicine and as a source of wood.
