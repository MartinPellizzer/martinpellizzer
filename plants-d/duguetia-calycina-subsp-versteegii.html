Duguetia calycina subsp. versteegii

Duguetia calycina subsp.

versteegii, commonly known as the red-flowered tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America. It is characterized by its showy, red or orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators.

The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are typically ovate to elliptical in shape. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests.

The subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is occasionally cultivated for its striking floral display.
