Dipteracanthus rosmarinus

Dipteracanthus rosmarinus, commonly known as rosemary bush, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic evergreen foliage and small, pale blue or white flowers. The plant belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic herbs such as mint and basil. Its leaves are widely used in culinary and medicinal applications due to their distinctive scent and essential oils.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
