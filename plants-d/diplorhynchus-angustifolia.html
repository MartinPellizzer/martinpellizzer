Diplorhynchus angustifolia

Diplorhynchus angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed grass, is a perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and tufted inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its botanical features include rhizomes that help in vegetative propagation, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Diplorhynchus angustifolia is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
