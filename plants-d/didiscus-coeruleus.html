Didiscus coeruleus

Didiscus coeruleus, commonly known as the blue-eyed daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant blue petals and a distinctive yellow center, which resembles a small sun. The plant produces small, green, oval-shaped leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and containers.

Didiscus coeruleus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought-resistant properties.
