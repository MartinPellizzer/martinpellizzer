Dianthus pinifolius subsp. tenuicaulis

Dianthus pinifolius subsp.

tenuicaulis, commonly known as the slender-stemmed campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and clusters of small, showy pink or red flowers.

The leaves are narrow and linear, often resembling those of conifers, which contributes to its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
