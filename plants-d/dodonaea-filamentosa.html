Dodonaea filamentosa

Dodonaea filamentosa, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It features slender, thread-like leaves that give it its common name, and it produces small, yellowish-green flowers clustered in spikes. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resistance to drought and salt. It is often found in coastal areas and sandy soils, and it plays a role in stabilizing dunes.

Dodonaea filamentosa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance for native wildlife.
