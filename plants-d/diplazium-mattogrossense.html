Diplazium mattogrossense

Diplazium mattogrossense, commonly known as the Brazilian water fern, is a member of the Polypodiaceae family.

It is a perennial fern characterized by its rhizomatous growth and compound fronds. The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its fronds are usually divided into pinnae, with a distinctive appearance that aids in identification.

Diplazium mattogrossense plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and small invertebrates.
