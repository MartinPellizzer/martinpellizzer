Draba pulcherrima var. trachycarpa

Draba pulcherrima var. trachycarpa, commonly known as the rough-seeded whitlow grass, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are basal, linear, and often covered in a fine, hairy coating, while its flowers are white or pale yellow.

This species is notable for its ability to survive in harsh, high-altitude conditions, making it a resilient and hardy plant.
