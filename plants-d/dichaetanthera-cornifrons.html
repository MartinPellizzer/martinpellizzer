Dichaetanthera cornifrons

Dichaetanthera cornifrons, commonly known as the horned anther grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive inflorescence, which consists of compact, spike-like clusters of bracteate flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a fibrous texture, and the flowers are small and wind-pollinated.

Dichaetanthera cornifrons is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
