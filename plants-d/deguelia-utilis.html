Deguelia utilis

Deguelia utilis, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated, pod-like fruits that contain several flat, edible seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in humid lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some regions for both aesthetic and practical purposes.
