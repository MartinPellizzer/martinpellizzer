Drymaria paramorum

Drymaria paramorum, commonly known as the rock sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and rocky environments.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of pink or white flowers. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry conditions with minimal water requirements.

Its botanical structure supports its role as a pioneer species in disturbed or degraded soils.
