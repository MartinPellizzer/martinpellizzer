Delphinium montanum var. calvescens

Delphinium montanum var. calvescens, commonly known as the alpine larkspur, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in cool, moist environments. The plant features tall, erect stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height, adorned with blue to purple flowers in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are clustered in racemes.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
