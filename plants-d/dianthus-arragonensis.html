Dianthus arragonensis

Dianthus arragonensis, commonly known as the Spanish carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 cm and has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and hairy. Its flowers consist of five petals that are often notched at the edges, giving them a distinctive appearance.

Dianthus arragonensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
