Digitaria extensa

Digitaria extensa, commonly known as broadleaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its broad, flat leaves and tall, branched inflorescences. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas. It is often used for forage in livestock grazing due to its high nutritional value.

Botanically, it belongs to the Poaceae family and is known for its adaptability to various environmental conditions.
