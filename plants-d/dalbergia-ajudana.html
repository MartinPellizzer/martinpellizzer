Dalbergia ajudana

Dalbergia ajudana, commonly known as the Brazilian rosewood, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its dense, dark-colored wood. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers. It is often found in humid lowland forests and is valued for its high-quality timber.

However, due to overharvesting, it is now considered an endangered species.
