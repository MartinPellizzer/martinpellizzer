Digitaria acuminatissima subvar. grandiflora

Digitaria acuminatissima subvar. grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered digitaria, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and large, showy inflorescences that are often branched and panicle-like. The plant produces small, spikelet-based flowers that are typically arranged in clusters, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for warm climates.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
