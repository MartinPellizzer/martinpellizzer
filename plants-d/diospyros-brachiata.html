Diospyros brachiata

Diospyros brachiata, commonly known as the Philippine persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its alternate, simple, and often leathery leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by orange to red berries that are edible when ripe. Its fruit is popular for its sweet, non-astringent flavor and is often used in local cuisines.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in folk medicine.
