Dyckia eddie-estevesii

Dyckia eddie-estevesii, commonly known as the Estevess' Dyckia, is a species of grass-like plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming structure with tightly packed, fibrous leaves. The plant produces striking, spiky inflorescences that bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in horticultural collections.
