Dendropanax swartzii

Dendropanax swartzii, commonly known as the swamp dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of eastern Australia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in swampy or seasonally flooded areas. The plant features alternate, heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and a distinctive white or pale green coloration. Its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in umbels, often appearing in late spring to early summer.

Dendropanax swartzii plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
