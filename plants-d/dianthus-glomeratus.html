Dianthus glomeratus

Dianthus glomeratus, commonly known as the clustered carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant features linear, grass-like leaves and a compact growth habit, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders. Its flowers bloom in early summer and are often used in floral arrangements.

Dianthus glomeratus is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
