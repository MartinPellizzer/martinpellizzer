Diosphaera chalcidica var. denudata

Diosphaera chalcidica var. denudata, commonly known as the naked Diosphaera, is a unique variant of the Diosphaera chalcidica species.

It is characterized by its reduced foliage, giving it a distinct, almost skeletal appearance. This plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to dry conditions. Its leaves are often small and sparse, while its stems may bear clusters of small, inconspicuous flowers.

The botanical interest of this variety lies in its morphological variation and ecological resilience.
