Derris utilis

Derris utilis, commonly known as the Indian snakeroot, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 3 meters. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces yellow flowers in clusters. Its root system is notable for containing the alkaloid rotenone, which has been traditionally used as a natural pesticide.

The botanical features of Derris utilis make it an important species in both traditional medicine and agricultural practices.
