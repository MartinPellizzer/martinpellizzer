Diplachne thoroldii

Diplachne thoroldii, commonly known as the narrowleaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Diplachne thoroldii is often found in coastal and inland regions, contributing to the ecological diversity of these environments.
