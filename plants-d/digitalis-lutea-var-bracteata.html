Digitalis lutea var. bracteata

Digitalis lutea var. bracteata, commonly known as the yellow foxglove, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features tall, erect stems with lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and slightly hairy. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is part of the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its ornamental value and historical use in medicine.

However, it is toxic and should be handled with care.
