Dianthus juniperinus subsp. heldreichii

Dianthus juniperinus subsp.

heldreichii, commonly known as the Balkan carnation, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula. It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit.

The plant features bright pink to red flowers that bloom in early spring, often in clusters at the ends of its stems. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, and the plant is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and hardy nature.
