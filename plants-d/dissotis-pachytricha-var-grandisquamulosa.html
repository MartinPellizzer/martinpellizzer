Dissotis pachytricha var. grandisquamulosa

Dissotis pachytricha var. grandisquamulosa, commonly known as the broad-scale papyrus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features a rosette of broad, waxy, and often hairy leaves that can grow up to several feet in length. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or pale green flowers arranged in a spike-like inflorescence. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies such as swamps and marshes.

Its leaves are used in traditional crafts and as a source of fiber in some local communities.
