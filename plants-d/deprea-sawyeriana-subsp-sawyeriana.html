Deprea sawyeriana subsp. sawyeriana

Deprea sawyeriana subsp.

sawyeriana, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada larkspur, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and clusters of blue to violet flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

It plays an important ecological role by providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for small invertebrates.
