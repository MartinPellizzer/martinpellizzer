Dieteria canescens

Dieteria canescens, commonly known as the silverleaf daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy, well-drained soils. It produces small, daisy-like blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Dieteria canescens is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
