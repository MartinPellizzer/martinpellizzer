Dianthus attenuatus f. leptophyllus

Dianthus attenuatus f. leptophyllus, commonly known as the slender-leaf carnation, is a flowering plant characterized by its compact growth habit and delicate, pinkish-red flowers.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is native to regions of Europe and western Asia. The foliage is narrow and linear, giving the plant a grass-like appearance, while its flowers are typically arranged in dense clusters. This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens and as a bedding plant.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a versatile choice for gardeners.
