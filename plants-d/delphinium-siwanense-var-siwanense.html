Delphinium siwanense var. siwanense

Delphinium siwanense var. siwanense, commonly known as the Sierra delphinium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring a robust stem that is often branched towards the top. The plant produces tall spikes of showy blue to purple flowers, which are arranged in a raceme and bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and deeply lobed, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.

The species is adapted to high-altitude environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun.
