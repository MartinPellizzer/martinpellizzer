Duvalia mastodes

Duvalia mastodes, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces clusters of small, white or pale pink blossoms that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and arranged in a rosette pattern, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Duvalia mastodes is adapted to full sun and is often found in open woodland or coastal dune environments.
