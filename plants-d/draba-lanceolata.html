Draba lanceolata

Draba lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf whitlow grass, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Draba lanceolata has basal leaves that are lanceolate in shape, and it produces numerous small white or pale yellow flowers in spring.

It is often found in meadows, scree slopes, and along mountain paths at high elevations.
