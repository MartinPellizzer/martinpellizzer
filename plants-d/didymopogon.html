Didymopogon

Didymopogon, commonly known as the silver grass, is a genus of flowering plants in the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive silver-gray foliage, which results from dense, fine hairs covering the leaves. The plants typically grow in temperate climates and are often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and coastal areas. Didymopogon species are perennial and have a clumping growth habit, with slender, arching stems that produce small, spike-like inflorescences.

These plants are valued for their ornamental appeal and are sometimes used in landscaping for their unique appearance.
