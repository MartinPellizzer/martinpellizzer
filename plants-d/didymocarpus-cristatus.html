Didymocarpus cristatus

Didymocarpus cristatus, commonly known as the crowned flamewood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive crown-like arrangement of branches, which gives it its common name. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by red, fleshy fruits that are edible.

The species is valued for its ornamental appearance and is often cultivated in gardens and parks.
