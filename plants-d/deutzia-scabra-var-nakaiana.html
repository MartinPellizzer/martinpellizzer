Deutzia scabra var. nakaiana

Deutzia scabra var. nakaiana, commonly known as the Japanese spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It features slender, arching stems and clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has smooth, grayish bark and oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in garden landscapes.
