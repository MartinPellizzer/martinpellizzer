Dianthus fallacinus

Dianthus fallacinus, commonly known as the autumn campion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant pink or red flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and has linear, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in meadows and woodland edges.

The flowers are borne on tall stems and are arranged in a loose cluster, making it a distinctive addition to gardens and natural habitats.
