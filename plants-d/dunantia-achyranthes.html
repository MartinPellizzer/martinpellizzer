Dunantia achyranthes

Dunantia achyranthes, commonly known as Indian madder, is a flowering plant native to South Asia.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. It is often cultivated for its roots, which contain compounds used in traditional medicine.

The plant thrives in dry, sunny environments and is adapted to poor soil conditions.
