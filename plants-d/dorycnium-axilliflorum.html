Dorycnium axilliflorum

Dorycnium axilliflorum, commonly known as the axillary-flowered goat's rue, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

Dorycnium axilliflorum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
