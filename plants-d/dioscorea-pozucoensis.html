Dioscorea pozucoensis

Dioscorea pozucoensis, commonly known as the Pozuco yam, is a climbing vine belonging to the Dioscoreaceae family.

It is native to Central America, particularly found in countries like Guatemala and Belize. The plant produces tuberous roots that are used in traditional medicine and as a food source. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its flowers are small and greenish-white.

The species is valued for its potential medicinal properties and is often cultivated for its nutritional value.
