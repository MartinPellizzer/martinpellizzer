Ditassa trivialis

Ditassa trivialis, commonly known as the common dandelion, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of spoon-shaped leaves and a hollow, hairless stem. The plant produces a distinctive flower head composed of numerous small yellow florets surrounded by bracts. It is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed areas.

The plant is also noted for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
