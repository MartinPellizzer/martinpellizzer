Disa jacottetiae

Disa jacottetiae, commonly known as the Jacottet's orchid, is a rare and striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, violet-blue flowers with intricate markings. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils in mountainous regions, preferring cool and humid conditions. Its flowers bloom in late summer, adding a unique ornamental value to its natural habitat.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
