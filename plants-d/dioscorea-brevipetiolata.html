Dioscorea brevipetiolata

Dioscorea brevipetiolata, commonly known as the short-petioled yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the genus Dioscorea, which includes numerous species of yams widely cultivated for their starchy tubers. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with short petioles, and its stems are covered in tendrils that aid in climbing. Its flowers are typically unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant.

The tubers of D. brevipetiolata are edible when properly prepared and have been used in traditional cuisines.
