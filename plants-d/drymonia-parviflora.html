Drymonia parviflora

Drymonia parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered drymonia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and swampy areas. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring. Its root system is fibrous and contributes to soil stabilization in its native habitat.

Drymonia parviflora is valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife and its potential medicinal properties.
