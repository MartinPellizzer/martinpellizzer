Delphinium pentagynum subsp. gautieri

Delphinium pentagynum subsp.

gautieri, commonly known as Gautier's larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features tall, sparsely branched stems that bear clusters of blue to purple flowers, which are typically arranged in racemes.

The leaves are lance-shaped and have a serrated margin, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in alpine or subalpine environments.

The plant is noted for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
