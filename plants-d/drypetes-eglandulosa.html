Drypetes eglandulosa

Drypetes eglandulosa, commonly known as the smooth drypetes, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in catkin-like inflorescences, followed by orange-red fruits that are capsule-like in structure. It is often found in lowland and hill forest areas, thriving in humid tropical climates.

The species is also valued for its timber and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
