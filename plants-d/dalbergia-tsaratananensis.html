Dalbergia tsaratananensis

Dalbergia tsaratananensis, commonly known as the Madagascar rosewood, is a tropical tree native to the island of Madagascar.

It belongs to the legume family Fabaceae and is characterized by its dense, dark-colored wood, which is highly valued for its aesthetic and structural properties. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes.

It is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with nitrogen-fixing bacteria in its root nodules.
