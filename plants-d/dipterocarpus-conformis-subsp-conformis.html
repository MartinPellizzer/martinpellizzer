Dipterocarpus conformis subsp. conformis

Dipterocarpus conformis subsp.

conformis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf dipterocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptic to ovate in shape.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in panicles, which are typically pollinated by insects. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release winged seeds.

This species is valued for its timber and plays a significant role in the ecosystems of its native habitats.
