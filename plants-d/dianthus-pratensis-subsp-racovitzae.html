Dianthus pratensis subsp. racovitzae

Dianthus pratensis subsp.

racovitzae, commonly known as the Balkan campion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southeastern Europe. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

The plant features pink to red flowers arranged in clusters, with five petals and prominent white veins. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, often forming a basal rosette.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
