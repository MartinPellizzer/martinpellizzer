Deschampsia cespitosa subsp. mezensis

Deschampsia cespitosa subsp.

mezensis, commonly known as mezen's bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to the northern regions of North America. It is characterized by its fine, blue-green blades and forms dense, tussock-like clumps.

This subspecies thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in marshes, bogs, and along the edges of ponds. Its inflorescence consists of narrow, spike-like clusters of small, blue-green florets.

Mezen's bluegrass plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
