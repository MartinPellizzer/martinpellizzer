Disemma

Disemma, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Disemma has slender, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in partial shade.
