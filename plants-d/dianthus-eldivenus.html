Dianthus eldivenus

Dianthus eldivenus, commonly known as the alpine campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its stems are often covered in fine hairs, and the leaves are linear and grass-like in appearance.

Dianthus eldivenus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
