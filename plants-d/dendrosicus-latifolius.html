Dendrosicus latifolius

Dendrosicus latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf dendrosicus, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, often blooming in the spring. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for gardens in temperate climates.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated for its attractive foliage and delicate floral display.
