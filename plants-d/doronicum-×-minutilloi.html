Doronicum × minutilloi

Doronicum × minutilloi, commonly known as the hybrid leopard's bane, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features lance-shaped, dark green leaves with hairy undersides, and its flowers are typically yellow with a central disk. It thrives in well-drained, alkaline soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Doronicum × minutilloi is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its attractive foliage and long-lasting blooms.
