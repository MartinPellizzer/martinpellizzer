Dimeria monostachya

Dimeria monostachya, commonly known as the narrowleaf dimeria, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, greenish-white flowers that are typically arranged in a single, elongated spike. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along dry streambeds.

Dimeria monostachya is adapted to withstand drought conditions and is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.
