Dryopteris villarii subsp. mindshelkensis

Dryopteris villarii subsp.

mindshelkensis, commonly known as the Mindshelk Shield Fern, is a rare and distinctive fern species native to specific regions of Europe. It belongs to the Dryopteridaceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, lance-shaped fronds that are often covered in a dense layer of brownish scales.

The plant typically grows in shaded, moist woodlands and thrives in nutrient-rich soils. Its spore-bearing structures, or sori, are arranged in a unique pattern along the margins of the fronds.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a valuable subject for botanical study and conservation efforts.
