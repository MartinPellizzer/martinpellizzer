Dicoma tomentosa

Dicoma tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly daisies, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage and fluffy, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is well adapted to sandy soils and salty air. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Dicoma tomentosa is often used in coastal landscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
