Dianthus brachyodontus

Dianthus brachyodontus, commonly known as the broad-leafed campion, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of showy, pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Dianthus brachyodontus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
