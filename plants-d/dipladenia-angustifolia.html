Dipladenia angustifolia

Dipladenia angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed trailing jellyplant, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are typically narrow and pointed. The plant produces clusters of small, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of orange and yellow, which bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in warm, sunny conditions and is often used in hanging baskets or as a ground cover.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for tropical and subtropical gardens.
