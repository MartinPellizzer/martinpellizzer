Dombeya punctata var. orbicularis

Dombeya punctata var. orbicularis, commonly known as the orbicular dombeya, is a perennial shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a yellowish throat. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, with opposite, ovate to orbicular leaves that are often hairy. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, loamy soil.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
