Desmopsis stenopetala

Desmopsis stenopetala, commonly known as the narrow-petioled desmopsis, is a shrub native to the Andes of South America.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in high-altitude environments and is adapted to cool, moist conditions.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
