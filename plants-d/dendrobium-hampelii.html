Dendrobium hampelii

Dendrobium hampelii, commonly known as the Hampel's orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, cane-like stems and clusters of fragrant, showy flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant typically blooms in late winter or early spring, producing long, pendulous inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and strap-like, arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem.

Dendrobium hampelii is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for both novice and experienced orchid growers.
