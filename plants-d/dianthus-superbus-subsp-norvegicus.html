Dianthus superbus subsp. norvegicus

Dianthus superbus subsp.

norvegicus, commonly known as the Norwegian campion, is a flowering plant native to northern Europe. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of bright red or pink flowers.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
