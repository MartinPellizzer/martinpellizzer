Drymocallis arizonica

Drymocallis arizonica, commonly known as Arizona silverleaf.

It is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. The plant is characterized by its silvery-gray leaves and showy, cone-shaped flower heads that bloom in late summer. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along dry streambeds.

The plant has a woody stem and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
