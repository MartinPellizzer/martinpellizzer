Dysoxylum rostratum

Dysoxylum rostratum, commonly known as the horned tamarind, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae, which includes species known for their aromatic wood and medicinal properties. The plant is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and compound leaves with opposite arrangement. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically greenish-yellow in color, while the fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds.

The tree is valued for its hard timber and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic benefits.
