Dianthus bebius

Dianthus bebius, commonly known as the Sicilian pink, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces clusters of small, pinkish-red flowers. The plant typically blooms in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Dianthus bebius thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant color and drought tolerance.
