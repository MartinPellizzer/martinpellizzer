Dibrachion

Dibrachion, commonly known as the false dandelion, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and its composite flower heads that resemble dandelions. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is found in various regions of the Northern Hemisphere. Dibrachion species have small, inconspicuous flowers that are often surrounded by bracts.

The leaves are usually hairy and have a rough texture, contributing to its distinctive botanical appearance.
