Delphinium fissum subsp. dinaricum

Delphinium fissum subsp.

dinaricum, commonly known as the Dinaric larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula. It typically grows in alpine and subalpine regions, preferring well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

The plant features tall, unbranched stems that bear whorled clusters of blue to purple flowers. Its leaves are lanceolate and often have a distinctive veining pattern.

The subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
