Diospyros pauciflora

Diospyros pauciflora, commonly known as the few-flowered ebony, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its dark, glossy bark and elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by edible, round fruits with a sweet, mealy pulp. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and unique fruit, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a decorative plant.
