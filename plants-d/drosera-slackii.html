Drosera slackii

Drosera slackii, commonly known as the Slacks Sundew, is a carnivorous plant native to South Australia.

It is characterized by its rosette of hairy, glandular leaves that secrete a sticky substance to trap insects. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It thrives in wet, acidic environments such as bogs and heathlands.

Drosera slackii is a perennial species that is often found growing in shallow water or on moist soil.
