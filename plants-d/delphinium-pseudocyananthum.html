Delphinium pseudocyananthum

Delphinium pseudocyananthum, commonly known as the blue delphinium, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family and is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that bear large, showy blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine or subalpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are compound, with lance-shaped leaflets that are arranged alternately along the stem.

Delphinium pseudocyananthum is valued in horticulture for its striking floral display and is often cultivated in gardens with adequate drainage.
