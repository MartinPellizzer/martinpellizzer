Diastema longiflorum

Diastema longiflorum, commonly known as the long-flowered diastema, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, herbaceous stems and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique floral structure and ecological adaptations.
