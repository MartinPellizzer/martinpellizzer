Dioscorea analalavensis

Dioscorea analalavensis, commonly known as the Madagascar yam, is a climbing vine native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary edible part of the plant. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and has compound leaves with a feathery appearance. Its growth habit involves twining around supports, and it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

The tubers are rich in starch and have been traditionally used as a food source by local communities.
