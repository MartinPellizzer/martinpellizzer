Dortmanna ludoviciana

Dortmanna ludoviciana, commonly known as the white sand violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its small, white to pale purple flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy, well-drained soils and thrives in partial shade to full sun. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a hairy texture, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Dortmanna ludoviciana is often found in open woodlands and disturbed sites, showcasing its adaptability to various ecological conditions.
