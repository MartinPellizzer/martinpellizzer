Diospyros ferrea var. angustifolia

Diospyros ferrea var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf persimmon, is a species of flowering plant in the Ebenaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves that are typically narrow and pointed. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, orange-red fruits with a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

The wood of this species is dense and durable, making it valuable for carving and construction.
