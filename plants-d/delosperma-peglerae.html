Delosperma peglerae

Delosperma peglerae, commonly known as the Namib Desert ice plant, is a low-growing, succulent shrub native to the coastal regions of Namibia.

It features fleshy, blue-green leaves that are adapted to store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces vibrant pink to purple flowers during the rainy season, typically from October to December. Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Delosperma peglerae is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing sandy soils.
