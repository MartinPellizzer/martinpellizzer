Deweya blanda

Deweya blanda, commonly known as the softleaf dewey, is a delicate flowering plant native to the temperate regions of North America.

It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forest floors and stream banks. The plant features slender, hairy stems and small, ovate leaves with a soft, velvety texture. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, clustered in terminal panicles, and bloom in early summer.

Deweya blanda is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local pollinator populations.
