Dasyaulis malabaricus

Dasyaulis malabaricus, commonly known as the Malabar daisy, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to semi-shaded environments. Its bright yellow flowers are often surrounded by white or pale green ray florets, creating a striking visual display.

Dasyaulis malabaricus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic contribution.
