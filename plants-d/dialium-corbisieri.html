Dialium corbisieri

Dialium corbisieri, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a tropical tree native to West and Central Africa.

It belongs to the family Malvaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers followed by orange-red, fleshy fruits that contain several seeds. It is valued for its durable wood and is often found in moist lowland forests.

The species is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
