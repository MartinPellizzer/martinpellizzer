Diplotax arborescens

Diplotax arborescens, commonly known as the tree cycad, is a member of the Cycadaceae family and is native to eastern Australia.

It is a slow-growing, evergreen tree with a stout, unbranched trunk and a crown of feathery, pinnate leaves. The plant produces large, cone-like structures that contain both male and female reproductive organs. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
