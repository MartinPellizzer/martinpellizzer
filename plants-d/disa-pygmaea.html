Disa pygmaea

Disa pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf orchid, is a small, terrestrial orchid native to South Africa.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky areas. The plant has a compact growth habit with narrow, grass-like leaves and produces delicate, violet or white flowers. It is characterized by its short stature, often reaching only a few centimeters in height.

Disa pygmaea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
