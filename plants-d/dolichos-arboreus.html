Dolichos arboreus

Dolichos arboreus, commonly known as the tree cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its woody, shrub-like or tree-like structure, with compound leaves and small, pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment. Its flowers are typically white or pink, and it thrives in warm, humid climates.

Dolichos arboreus is often cultivated for both its nutritional value and its role in agroforestry systems.
