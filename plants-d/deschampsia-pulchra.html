Deschampsia pulchra

Deschampsia pulchra, commonly known as the pink hairgrass, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and distinctive pinkish hue in its inflorescence. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows, stream banks, and forest edges. Its inflorescence consists of a dense spikelet cluster that is often tinged with pink or purple, giving it its common name.

Deschampsia pulchra is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
