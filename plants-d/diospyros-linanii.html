Diospyros linanii

Diospyros linanii, commonly known as the Chinese persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to southern China and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical leaves with a smooth, dark green upper surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into orange-red, fleshy fruits. The fruit has a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor and is often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications.

It is also valued for its hard, durable wood used in crafting and construction.
