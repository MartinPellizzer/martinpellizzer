Dianthus furcatus subsp. subacaulis

Dianthus furcatus subsp.

subacaulis, commonly known as the narrow-stemmed campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Mediterranean. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of pink or red flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant has small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, thriving in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in alpine meadows.
