Deyeuxia conspicua

Deyeuxia conspicua, commonly known as the showy flat sedge, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, spike-like inflorescences that are often adorned with distinctive purple or reddish hues. The plant typically grows in moist environments such as wet meadows, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a slightly pointed tip.

Deyeuxia conspicua is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in wet conditions.
