Danthonia javanica

Danthonia javanica, commonly known as the Javan silver grass, is a perennial grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-blue foliage and compact growth habit, making it a popular ornamental plant. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in open grasslands and disturbed sites.

Its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements make it suitable for landscaping in tropical and subtropical regions.
