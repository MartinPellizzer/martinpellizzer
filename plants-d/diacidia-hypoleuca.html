Diacidia hypoleuca

Diacidia hypoleuca, commonly known as the white-flowered diacidia, is a small, herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Diacidia hypoleuca is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
