Deguelia maingayana

Deguelia maingayana, commonly known as the pink trumpet tree, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall, featuring a straight trunk and a broad, spreading crown. The plant is characterized by its showy, pinkish-red tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a smooth surface and a pointed tip.

The tree is also valued for its fast growth and is sometimes used in ornamental horticulture.
