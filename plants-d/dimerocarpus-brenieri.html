Dimerocarpus brenieri

Dimerocarpus brenieri, commonly known as the African wild bean, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces indehiscent pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Dimerocarpus brenieri is adapted to grow in dry, sandy soils and is often found in savanna habitats.
