Deyeuxia arundinacea var. borealis

Deyeuxia arundinacea var. borealis, commonly known as northern reed, is a perennial grass native to northern regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, bogs, and along the edges of lakes and rivers. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It is often found in temperate and subarctic climates, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
