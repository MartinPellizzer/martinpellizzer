Dodonaea hispidula var. phylloptera

Dodonaea hispidula var. phylloptera, commonly known as the woolly honeysuckle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive woolly appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in spikes, typically blooming in spring. Its adaptability to various soil types and drought tolerance make it a hardy plant in coastal and sandy environments.

The foliage is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in challenging conditions.
