Dianthus subacaulis subsp. brachyanthus

Dianthus subacaulis subsp.

brachyanthus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved campion, is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial native to the Mediterranean region. It features dense clusters of bright pink to red flowers with prominent, showy petals that bloom in late summer.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water conservation. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its compact size and vibrant blooms make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
