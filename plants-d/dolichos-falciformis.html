Dolichos falciformis

Dolichos falciformis, commonly known as the sickle bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and long, curved pods that contain edible seeds. The plant is often cultivated for its high-protein seeds, which are used in various cuisines. Botanically, it belongs to the Fabaceae family and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

Its growth habit is typically annual, with a sprawling or climbing structure depending on the species.
