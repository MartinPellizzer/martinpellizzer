Ditassa fasciculata

Ditassa fasciculata, commonly known as the clustered sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Africa.

It typically grows in wet or marshy environments, such as along riverbanks and in swampy areas. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in dense clusters, giving it its common name. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, often clustered in spikelets.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
