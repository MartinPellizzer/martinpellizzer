Dendrocalamus farinosus

Dendrocalamus farinosus, commonly known as the foamy bamboo, is a tall, slender grass-like plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its hollow, segmented stalks and a distinctive powdery coating on its nodes. The plant is often found in wetland and swampy areas, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its rapid growth and ability to form dense clumps make it an important component of tropical wetlands.

The species is also valued for its lightweight and durable culms, which have traditional and commercial uses.
