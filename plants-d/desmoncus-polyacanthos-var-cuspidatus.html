Desmoncus polyacanthos var. cuspidatus

Desmoncus polyacanthos var. cuspidatus, commonly known as the sharp-fruited false hellebore, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features broad, lance-shaped leaves with a waxy surface and a distinctive, pungent odor. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. Its fruit is a spiny, berry-like capsule that contains several seeds.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in swampy woodlands.
