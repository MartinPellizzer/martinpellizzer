Diospyros dawei

Diospyros dawei, commonly known as the Chinese persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to China and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and alternate, simple leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into orange to reddish fruit, which is typically sweet and astringent when unripe.

The fruit is often consumed when fully mature, and the plant is valued for its ornamental and edible qualities.
