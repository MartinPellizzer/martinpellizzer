Decaisnina beccarii

Decaisnina beccarii, commonly known as the broad-leafed myrtle, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with a waxy coating. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are often followed by edible, berry-like fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some regions for horticultural and traditional uses.
