Draba henneana var. mccallae

Draba henneana var. mccallae, commonly known as the McCall's whitlow grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 5 to 15 centimeters. The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, fleshy leaves and produces small, yellowish-white flowers in early spring. It is native to the western United States, particularly in areas with rocky or gravelly soils.

This variety is adapted to harsh environments and is often found in alpine or arid regions.
