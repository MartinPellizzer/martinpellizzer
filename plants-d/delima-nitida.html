Delima nitida

Delima nitida, commonly known as the white sand tree, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and small, clustered white flowers that bloom during the dry season. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly waxy texture.

Delima nitida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique appearance and ecological benefits.
