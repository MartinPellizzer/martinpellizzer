Dendrobium nudum

Dendrobium nudum, commonly known as the naked orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often branching stems and small, pale green leaves. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, with a subtle scent. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Dendrobium nudum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
