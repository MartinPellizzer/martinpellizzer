Dioscorea adenantha

Dioscorea adenantha, commonly known as the African yam bean, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its cylindrical, tuberous roots that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by winged fruits containing seeds. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and have a rough texture.

The tubers are rich in starch and have been utilized as a food source by local communities.
