Didiscus pilosus var. preissii

Didiscus pilosus var. preissii, commonly known as the velvetleaf bower vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are typically green with a soft texture. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers that are followed by round, berry-like fruits. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
