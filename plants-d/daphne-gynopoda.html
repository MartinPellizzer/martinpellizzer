Daphne gynopoda

Daphne gynopoda, commonly known as the dwarf daphne, is a species of flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers in early spring. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and covered in a fine layer of white wax.

Daphne gynopoda is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
