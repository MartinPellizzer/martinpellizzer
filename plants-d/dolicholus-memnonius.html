Dolicholus memnonius

Dolicholus memnonius, commonly known as the red-flowered orchid, is a tropical orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy red petals and a unique, elongated shape that distinguishes it from other orchid genera. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, typical of many orchids. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by specific insect species, showcasing a specialized ecological relationship.

The botanical features of Dolicholus memnonius make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and botanical research.
