Digitalis laevigata subsp. graeca

Digitalis laevigata subsp.

graeca, commonly known as Greek foxglove, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to purple.

The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are broad, ovate, and covered in a fine, velvety texture.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
