Dilochia

Dilochia, commonly known as the golden bell vine, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, trailing stems and clusters of bell-shaped, yellow-orange flowers. The leaves are typically ovate and glossy, providing a striking contrast to the vibrant blooms. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The plant is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
