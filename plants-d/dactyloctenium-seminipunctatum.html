Dactyloctenium seminipunctatum

Dactyloctenium seminipunctatum, commonly known as the small-spotted grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It has slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in poor, dry conditions.

Its botanical features make it a resilient plant in various ecological settings.
