Dicoma paivae

Dicoma paivae, commonly known as the Cape mountain daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with bright yellow centers and white petals. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions. It has small, hairy leaves and produces small, inconspicuous fruits.

Dicoma paivae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
