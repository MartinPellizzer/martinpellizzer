Darwinia schuermannii

Darwinia schuermannii, commonly known as the Schuermann's devil's ivy, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its striking, bottlebrush-like inflorescences that range in color from red to pink. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with lance-shaped, glossy leaves. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in coastal and sandy soils.

Darwinia schuermannii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral displays.
