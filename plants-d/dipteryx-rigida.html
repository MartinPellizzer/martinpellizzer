Dipteryx rigida

Dipteryx rigida, commonly known as the Brazilian nut tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its large, compound leaves and produces distinctive, woody pods containing edible seeds known as Brazil nuts. The tree can grow up to 30 meters tall and has a long lifespan, often living over 100 years. It plays a crucial role in the ecosystem by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.

The plant is also valued for its economic importance, particularly for its nutrient-rich seeds.
