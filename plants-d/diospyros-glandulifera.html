Diospyros glandulifera

Diospyros glandulifera, commonly known as the glandular persimmon, is a species of flowering plant in the family Ebenaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Asia, including India, Sri Lanka, and parts of Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often glandular-dotted, giving it its scientific name. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by edible, orange-red berries.

The fruit is typically consumed raw or used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
