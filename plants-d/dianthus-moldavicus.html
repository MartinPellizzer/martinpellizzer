Dianthus moldavicus

Dianthus moldavicus, commonly known as the Moldavian campion, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia, typically growing in meadows and rocky areas. The plant features showy, pink or red flowers with five petals, and its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Dianthus moldavicus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and public spaces.
