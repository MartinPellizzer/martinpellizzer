Didymocarpus sulphureus var. breviflorus

Didymocarpus sulphureus var. breviflorus, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a vibrant display. The plant is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub, often reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are simple and alternate, with a glossy green surface.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions for its showy blooms.
