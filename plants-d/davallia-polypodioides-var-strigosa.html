Davallia polypodioides var. strigosa

Davallia polypodioides var. strigosa, commonly known as the hairy Davallia, is a fern species characterized by its distinctive, finely hairy fronds.

It belongs to the family Davalliaceae and is native to Southeast Asia, including regions such as India and the Philippines. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often found in forest undergrowth or on rocky surfaces. Its fronds are pinnately compound, with a delicate, feathery appearance that contributes to its ornamental appeal.

The varietal name "strigosa" refers to the hairy texture of its leaves, a key botanical feature of this species.
