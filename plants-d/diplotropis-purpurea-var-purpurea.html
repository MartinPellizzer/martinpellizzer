Diplotropis purpurea var. purpurea

Diplotropis purpurea var. purpurea, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, tubular purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and a strong, aromatic scent. It typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 feet and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Its botanical structure supports its role as a valuable native plant in ecological restoration and garden landscapes.
