Dioscorea brachybotrya

Dioscorea brachybotrya, commonly known as the short-fruited yam, is a member of the Dioscoreaceae family and is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary part used for culinary and medicinal purposes. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a rough texture, while the fruits are capsule-like and contain several seeds.

The tubers of D. brachybotrya are valued for their starchy content and have been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
