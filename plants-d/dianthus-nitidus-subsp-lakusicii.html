Dianthus nitidus subsp. lakusicii

Dianthus nitidus subsp.

lakusicii, commonly known as the Lake Turkana dianthus, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of East Africa. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves and is adapted to thrive in dry, rocky environments. It is often found in association with other xerophytic species in its native habitat.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and resilient member of the Caryophyllaceae family.
