Dampiera tenuicaulis var. tenuicaulis

Dampiera tenuicaulis var. tenuicaulis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved dampiera, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, pale blue or purple flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and sandy soils. It is a perennial herb that produces distinctive seed pods that split open to release its seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
