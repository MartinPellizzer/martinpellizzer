Disperis capensis var. brevicaudata

Disperis capensis var. brevicaudata, commonly known as the Cape bellflower, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features slender, upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate in suitable climates.
