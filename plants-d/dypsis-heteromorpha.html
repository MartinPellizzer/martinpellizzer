Dypsis heteromorpha

Dypsis heteromorpha, commonly known as the Madagascar palm, is a member of the Arecaceae family.

It is native to Madagascar and is characterized by its distinctive, bottle-shaped trunk and clustered, feather-like leaves. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are followed by dark blue, fleshy fruits. It is adapted to arid conditions and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance.

Dypsis heteromorpha is relatively slow-growing and requires well-drained soil and minimal water.
