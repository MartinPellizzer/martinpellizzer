Dryopteris pulvinulifera

Dryopteris pulvinulifera, commonly known as the golden shield fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, leathery, dark green fronds with a distinctive golden-brown margin. The fronds are typically bipinnate, with a long, narrow shape and a pulvinate (club-shaped) base. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appearance and is sometimes cultivated in horticulture.
