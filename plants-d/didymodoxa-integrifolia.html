Didymodoxa integrifolia

Didymodoxa integrifolia, commonly known as the African bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually smooth and entire, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Didymodoxa integrifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
