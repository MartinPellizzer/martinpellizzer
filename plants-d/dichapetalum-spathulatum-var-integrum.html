Dichapetalum spathulatum var. integrum

Dichapetalum spathulatum var. integrum, commonly known as the broad-leafed dichapetalum, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically smooth and glossy. The plant produces showy, pinkish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is occasionally cultivated in shaded garden settings.
