Dendrobium verruculosum

Dendrobium verruculosum, commonly known as the rough-leafed dendrobium, is an epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with cane-like stems that bear clusters of lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces fragrant, showy flowers in shades of yellow and white, often with a distinctive green throat. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for both novice and experienced orchid growers.
