Dyckia conspicua

Dyckia conspicua, commonly known as the showy dyckia, is a striking bromeliad native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense, woolly layer of white or pale gray trichomes. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences bearing small, white or pale blue flowers. Its leaves are typically stiff and sword-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Dyckia conspicua is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in bright, indirect light.
