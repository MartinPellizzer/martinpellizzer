Dichocarpum nipponicum

Dichocarpum nipponicum, commonly known as the Japanese false bellflower, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers that bloom in late spring. Dichocarpum nipponicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
