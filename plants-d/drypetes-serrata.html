Drypetes serrata

Drypetes serrata, commonly known as the serrated drypetes, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite, and often serrated leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by orange-red, fleshy fruits. The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
