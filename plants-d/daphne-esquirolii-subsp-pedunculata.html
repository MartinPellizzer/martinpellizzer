Daphne esquirolii subsp. pedunculata

Daphne esquirolii subsp.

pedunculata, commonly known as the Sierra daphne, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its narrow, evergreen leaves and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant produces small, woody capsules that contain numerous seeds. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, often growing in rocky or mountainous areas.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and protected species in some regions.
