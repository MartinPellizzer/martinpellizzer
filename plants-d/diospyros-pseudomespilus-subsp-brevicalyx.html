Diospyros pseudomespilus subsp. brevicalyx

Diospyros pseudomespilus subsp.

brevicalyx, commonly known as the narrow-leaved persimmon, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia. It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers.

The plant produces edible, orange-red fruits that are often consumed fresh or dried. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in some regions for both horticultural and culinary purposes.
