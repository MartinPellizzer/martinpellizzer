Dolichos stipulosus var. randii

Dolichos stipulosus var. randii, commonly known as the white hyacinth bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers. The plant bears elongated, slender pods that contain small, round seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible seeds.
