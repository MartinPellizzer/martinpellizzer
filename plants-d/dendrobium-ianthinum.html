Dendrobium ianthinum

Dendrobium ianthinum, commonly known as the purple-tinged orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit, with cane-like stems that can reach several feet in length. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of purple and white, often with intricate patterns on the petals. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated for both horticultural and commercial purposes.
