Diospyros boiviniana

Diospyros boiviniana, commonly known as the Boivin's persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes ebony and persimmon trees. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are typically clustered in panicles, and it produces small, round fruits that are edible when fully mature.

The tree is valued for its hard, dark wood and is often found in moist, shaded habitats within tropical forests.
