Dianthus alpinus var. repens

Dianthus alpinus var. repens, commonly known as the alpine campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and produces clusters of pink or white flowers with five petals. The plant has slender, hairy stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows and tundra environments.

Dianthus alpinus var. repens is a hardy perennial that is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in high-altitude ecosystems.
