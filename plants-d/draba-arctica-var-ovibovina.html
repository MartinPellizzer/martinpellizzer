Draba arctica var. ovibovina

Draba arctica var. ovibovina, commonly known as the Arctic bellflower, is a low-growing, perennial flowering plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically has a rosette of basal leaves and produces short, flowering stems. It thrives in nutrient-poor, rocky soils and is adapted to cold climates with limited growing seasons.

Its frost-resistant nature makes it a hardy species found in tundra and mountainous areas.
