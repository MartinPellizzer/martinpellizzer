Diospyros sandwicensis

Diospyros sandwicensis, commonly known as the Hawaiian persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by edible, orange-red fruits that are rich in nutrients. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland habitats.

Due to habitat loss and introduced species, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
