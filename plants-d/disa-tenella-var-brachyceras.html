Disa tenella var. brachyceras

Disa tenella var. brachyceras, commonly known as the dwarf disa, is a striking member of the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is native to southern Africa, particularly South Africa, where it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of vivid purple or pink flowers with intricate, spurred petals. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming a rosette at the base.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
