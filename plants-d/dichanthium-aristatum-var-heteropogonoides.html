Dichanthium aristatum var. heteropogonoides

Dichanthium aristatum var. heteropogonoides, commonly known as the slender fingergrass, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are typically hairy along the edges. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny, two-lobed flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a potential forage plant for livestock.
