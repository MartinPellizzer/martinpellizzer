Dudleya cymosa subsp. costifolia

Dudleya cymosa subsp.

costifolia, commonly known as the Coast Dudleya, is a succulent plant native to coastal regions of California and Baja California. It features a low-growing rosette of thick, fleshy, blue-gray leaves with a distinctive white or pale green margin.

The plant produces clusters of vibrant pink or magenta flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact size and ornamental appearance make it a popular choice for coastal and Mediterranean climates.
