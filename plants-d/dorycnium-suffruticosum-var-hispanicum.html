Dorycnium suffruticosum var. hispanicum

Dorycnium suffruticosum var. hispanicum, commonly known as the Spanish goat's beard, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its creeping growth habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in racemes, which are followed by pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates.
