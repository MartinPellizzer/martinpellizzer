Dendrobium myosurus

Dendrobium myosurus, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, cane-like stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces clusters of showy, golden-yellow flowers that bloom during the summer months. Its leaves are long and strap-like, often arranged in a rosette pattern around the stem.

Dendrobium myosurus is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its vibrant floral display.
