Dorothea talbotii

Dorothea talbotii, commonly known as the Talbot's daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is known for its compact growth habit and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Dorothea talbotii is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators.
