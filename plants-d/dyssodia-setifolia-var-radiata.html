Dyssodia setifolia var. radiata

Dyssodia setifolia var. radiata, commonly known as the radiate dyssodia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, cylindrical stems and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in dry, open habitats. Its leaves are linear and covered in fine, stiff hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
