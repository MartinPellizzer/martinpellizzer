Diastema vexans

Diastema vexans, commonly known as the "devil's ivy," is a vigorous climbing vine native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are typically bright green with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers followed by red, fleshy fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, warm environments and is often found growing along forest edges or in disturbed soils.

Due to its rapid growth and adaptability, it is considered an invasive species in many areas.
