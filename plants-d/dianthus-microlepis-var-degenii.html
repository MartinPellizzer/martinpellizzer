Dianthus microlepis var. degenii

Dianthus microlepis var. degenii, commonly known as the dwarf carnation, is a compact, flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically borne on short, hairy stems. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
