Daphniphyllum himalense subsp. macropodum

Daphniphyllum himalense subsp.

macropodum, commonly known as the Himalayan holly, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of Nepal, India, and Bhutan. It belongs to the family Ericaceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath.

The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red or orange berries in the autumn. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and attractive foliage.

This subspecies is also noted for its ability to thrive in acidic, well-drained soils and cool, moist climates.
