Dalbergia meyeriana

Dalbergia meyeriana, commonly known as Meyer's rosewood, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The tree produces hard, dark wood that is highly valued for its density and aesthetic appeal. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to overharvesting, it is listed as endangered in some regions, making conservation efforts essential for its survival.
