Dioscorea canariensis

Dioscorea canariensis, commonly known as the Canary Island yam, is a climbing vine native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary storage organs. The plant produces large, heart-shaped leaves and bears small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters. Its tubers are edible when properly prepared and have been used in traditional cuisines.

The species is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
