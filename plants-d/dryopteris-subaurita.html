Dryopteris subaurita

Dryopteris subaurita, commonly known as the golden shield fern, is a species of fern native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, golden-brown fronds that are often covered with a fine, waxy coating. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky areas. Its fronds are pinnately compound, with a shield-shaped base that gives it its common name.

Dryopteris subaurita is a perennial plant that reproduces through spores and is valued for its ornamental appearance in gardens.
