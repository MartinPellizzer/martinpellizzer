Diospyros baroniana

Diospyros baroniana, commonly known as the African persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple leaves. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by orange to red, fleshy fruits.

The fruits are typically eaten fresh or used in traditional medicine, and the tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
