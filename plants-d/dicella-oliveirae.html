Dicella oliveirae

Dicella oliveirae, commonly known as the Brazilian violet, is a flowering plant native to the Atlantic rainforests of Brazil.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, violet-colored flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has slender, upright stems and heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
