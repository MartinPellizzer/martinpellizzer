Deyeuxia nutkaensis

Deyeuxia nutkaensis, commonly known as Nutka sedge, is a perennial grass species native to coastal regions of the Pacific Northwest.

It typically grows in wet meadows, salt marshes, and tidal flats, thriving in brackish or saline environments. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences. It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Deyeuxia nutkaensis is adapted to withstand periodic flooding and salt spray, making it a resilient component of coastal ecosystems.
