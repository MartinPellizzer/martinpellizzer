Deyeuxia simlensis

Deyeuxia simlensis, commonly known as the Simlens grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant typically features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
