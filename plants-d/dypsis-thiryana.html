Dypsis thiryana

Dypsis thiryana, commonly known as the Madagascar palm, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its unique, tree-like structure with a short trunk and a crown of pinnate leaves. The plant has a thick, fleshy stem that stores water, allowing it to survive in arid conditions. Its leaves are typically blue-gray in color, with a waxy coating that reduces water loss.

Dypsis thiryana is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and low maintenance requirements.
