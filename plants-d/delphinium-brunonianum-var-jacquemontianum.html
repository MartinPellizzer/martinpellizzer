Delphinium brunonianum var. jacquemontianum

Delphinium brunonianum var. jacquemontianum, commonly known as the Jacquemont's larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant produces clusters of blue to purple, spurred flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.
