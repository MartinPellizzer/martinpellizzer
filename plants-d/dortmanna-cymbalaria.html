Dortmanna cymbalaria

Dortmanna cymbalaria, commonly known as the common dock, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a member of the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its alternate, heart-shaped leaves and distinctive bracts. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense, shield-like inflorescences. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides and in wet meadows.

Dortmanna cymbalaria is known for its medicinal properties and has been used historically in traditional herbal remedies.
