Diplazium epirachis

Diplazium epirachis, commonly known as the hairy sword fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its distinctive fronds with a hairy texture on the underside. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its rhizome is creeping and often produces numerous sporangia along the fronds.

Diplazium epirachis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
