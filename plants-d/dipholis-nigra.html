Dipholis nigra

Dipholis nigra, commonly known as the black whip snake, is a species of colubrid snake found in parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender body and dark, glossy scales that range from black to dark brown in color. The snake is primarily nocturnal and feeds on small vertebrates such as lizards and birds. It is known for its agile movement and ability to climb trees, which aids in its hunting behavior.

The species plays an important role in its ecosystem by controlling populations of its prey.
