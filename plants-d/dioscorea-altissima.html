Dioscorea altissima

Dioscorea altissima, commonly known as the giant yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary part used for culinary and medicinal purposes. The plant produces small, green, heart-shaped leaves and yellowish flowers that are typically unisexual. Its tubers can grow quite large, sometimes reaching weights of several kilograms, and are valued for their high starch content.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its nutritional and traditional medicinal properties.
