Drimys verticillata

Drimys verticillata, commonly known as the Chilean evergreen or tree fern, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, tree-like structure with a thick, fibrous trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in temperate rainforests.

Drimys verticillata has a slow growth rate and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
