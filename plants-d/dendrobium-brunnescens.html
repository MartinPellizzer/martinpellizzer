Dendrobium brunnescens

Dendrobium brunnescens, commonly known as the brown-stemmed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with thick, fleshy stems that store water and nutrients. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange, often arranged in racemose inflorescences. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged in two rows along the stem.

Dendrobium brunnescens thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.
