Davallia cryptocarpa

Davallia cryptocarpa, commonly known as the cryptic fern, is a species of polypod fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are typically light green with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, spore-bearing structures called sori, which are clustered on the underside of the fronds. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
