Dioscorea crenata

Dioscorea crenata, commonly known as the African yam or wild yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and tuberous roots. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its tubers are sometimes used in traditional medicine for their potential health benefits.

However, the plant contains toxic compounds and requires careful handling.
