Dioscorea argyrogyna

Dioscorea argyrogyna, commonly known as the silver-leaf yam, is a perennial vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by tuberous roots that are often used in traditional medicine. Its botanical features include a complex root system and a preference for well-drained, fertile soils.

Dioscorea argyrogyna is valued for its potential medicinal properties and is sometimes cultivated for its nutritional value.
