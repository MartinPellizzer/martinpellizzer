Discoclaoxylon hexandrum

Discoclaoxylon hexandrum, commonly known as the African sandalwood, is a member of the Burseraceae family and is native to tropical Africa.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, greyish-brown bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, three-angled fruits containing aromatic seeds.

Its wood is valued for its fragrant, durable qualities and has been traditionally used in incense and medicinal applications.
