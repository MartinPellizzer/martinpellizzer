Dryopteris ctenitis f. amaurolepis

Dryopteris ctenitis f. amaurolepis, commonly known as the dark-veined wood fern, is a fern species characterized by its distinctive fronds with dark, prominent veins.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its fronds are usually bipinnate, with a leathery texture and a deep green coloration.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.
