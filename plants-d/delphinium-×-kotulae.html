Delphinium × kotulae

Delphinium × kotulae, commonly known as the hybrid delphinium, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that bear clusters of striking blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, with lance-shaped leaves that are deeply divided. Its flowers are composed of five sepals that are often mistaken for petals, and it produces small, yellowish fruits called achenes.

Delphinium × kotulae thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
