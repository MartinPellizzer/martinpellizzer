Dolichos pratorum

Dolichos pratorum, commonly known as the common cowpea, is an annual legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and elongated, slender pods that contain edible seeds. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and thrives in well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, and it is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia.

Dolichos pratorum is widely cultivated for its nutritional seeds and is also used in traditional medicine.
