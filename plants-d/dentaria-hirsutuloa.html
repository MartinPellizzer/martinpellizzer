Dentaria hirsutuloa

Dentaria hirsutuloa, commonly known as the hairy toothcup, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant features basal leaves that are hairy and toothed, giving it its common name. It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The hairy toothcup is often found in moist, open habitats such as prairies and woodland edges.
