Dioscorea venosa

Dioscorea venosa, commonly known as the veined yam, is a flowering plant belonging to the Dioscoreaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands. The plant is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary part used for culinary and medicinal purposes. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a velvety texture, while its flowers are typically greenish-yellow with a distinctive spiral arrangement.

Dioscorea venosa is valued for its nutritional content and has been used in traditional medicine for various health benefits.
