Deutzia crenata var. floribunda

Deutzia crenata var. floribunda, commonly known as the Japanese spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It features clusters of delicate, white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant has arching, slender stems and serrated, ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its ornamental value lies in its showy flowers and compact growth habit.
