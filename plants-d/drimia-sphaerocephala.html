Drimia sphaerocephala

Drimia sphaerocephala, commonly known as the globe lily, is a bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a spherical, fleshy bulb that stores water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in arid conditions. The plant produces striking, showy flowers that are typically white or pale pink, with a distinctive, rounded shape. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
