Dendroglossa cantoniensis

Dendroglossa cantoniensis, commonly known as the Guangdong tree frog, is a species of tree frog native to southern China.

It is characterized by its robust body and distinct coloration, typically featuring a dark dorsal surface with irregular light markings. The plant aspect of this species refers to its habitat, which includes moist forest understories and rocky areas. It plays a role in local ecosystems by contributing to nutrient cycling and serving as prey for various predators.

Due to habitat loss and environmental changes, it is considered a vulnerable species requiring conservation efforts.
