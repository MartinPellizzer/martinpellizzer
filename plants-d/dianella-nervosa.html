Dianella nervosa

Dianella nervosa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf flax lily, is a perennial evergreen plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like foliage with narrow, dark green leaves. The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers in spring, which are followed by small, dark blue berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and forested areas.

Dianella nervosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual interest.
