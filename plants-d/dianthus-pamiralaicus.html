Dianthus pamiralaicus

Dianthus pamiralaicus, commonly known as the Pamir carnation, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, showy flowers that range in color from pink to white. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, well-drained soils. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, while its stems are usually erect and branched.

Dianthus pamiralaicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate, colorful blooms.
