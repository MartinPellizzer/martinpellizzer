Dombeya subviscosa

Dombeya subviscosa, commonly known as the viscid dombeya, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves that are often covered in a sticky substance. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically in shades of white or pale pink. These flowers bloom in spring and are pollinated primarily by insects.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.
