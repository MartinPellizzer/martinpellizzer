Diptychandra epunctata

Diptychandra epunctata, commonly known as the spotted flame tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, grey bark and compound leaves. The plant produces striking yellow flowers that are clustered in panicles, which bloom during the dry season. Its fruits are woody pods that split open to release small, winged seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental value and is often planted in gardens and as a street tree due to its rapid growth and ability to thrive in various soil types.
