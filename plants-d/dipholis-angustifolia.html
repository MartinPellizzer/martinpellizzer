Dipholis angustifolia

Dipholis angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. The plant features slender, upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually in a spike, with a distinctive coloration that ranges from white to pale pink.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance and ecological significance.
