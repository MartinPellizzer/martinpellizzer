Draba scotteri

Draba scotteri, commonly known as the Scottish crowfoot, is a small perennial herbaceous plant native to the British Isles.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in mountainous and coastal regions. The plant features basal leaves that are spoon-shaped and a dense cluster of small, white or pale yellow flowers. It blooms from April to June, and its flowers are arranged in a compact inflorescence.

Draba scotteri is part of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other members like mustard and cabbage.
