Diodia virginiana var. linnaei

Diodia virginiana var. linnaei, commonly known as marsh mallow or yellow marsh mallow, is a perennial herb native to wetland habitats in the eastern United States.

It features erect, hairy stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height, often branching near the base. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers with five petals that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The plant thrives in moist soils and is often found in swamps, bogs, and along the edges of ponds.
