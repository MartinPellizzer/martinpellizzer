Dombeya erythroleuca

Dombeya erythroleuca, commonly known as the red-and-white dombeya, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range from white to pink and red, often with a striking red center. The plant produces small, oval-shaped fruits that are typically dispersed by birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Dombeya erythroleuca is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
