Daphne thesioides

Daphne thesioides, commonly known as the rock daphne, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of the Mediterranean.

It features aromatic, glandular leaves that are typically gray-green in color, and its flowers are showy, with pinkish-white petals and prominent yellow stamens. The plant produces small, dark blue to black berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Daphne thesioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
