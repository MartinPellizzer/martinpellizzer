Digitaria ischaemum var. praecox

Digitaria ischaemum var. praecox, commonly known as early digitgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides, fields, and waste areas, and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green color and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, three-lobed florets. It is adapted to a wide range of environmental conditions and is often found in moist to dry habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in landscaping or as a forage crop.
