Dalea scandens

Dalea scandens, commonly known as the climbing false nettle, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are adapted for dispersal by wind.

Dalea scandens plays a role in supporting local pollinators and can thrive in a variety of soil types.
