Duranta repens f. serrata

Duranta repens f. serrata, commonly known as the silverleaf duranta, is a compact shrub native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It features small, dark green leaves with serrated edges, giving it its specific epithet "serrata.". The plant produces clusters of bright blue or violet flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is drought-tolerant once established.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes in warm climates.
