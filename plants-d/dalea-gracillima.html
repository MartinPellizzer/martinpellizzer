Dalea gracillima

Dalea gracillima, commonly known as the slender prairie clover, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its delicate, pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies and dry woodlands. It has slender, branching stems and small, trifoliate leaves.

Dalea gracillima is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
