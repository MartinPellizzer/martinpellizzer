Discocactus albispinus

Discocactus albispinus, commonly known as the white-spined discocactus, is a succulent native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It features a short, cylindrical stem with a ribbed structure that helps it store water. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers during the warmer months. Its spines are pale yellow to white, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.
