Digitaria pertenuis

Digitaria pertenuis, commonly known as slender digit grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in open, disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields. The plant has slender, erect stems that are often hairless or only slightly hairy. Its leaves are narrow and usually have a pointed tip, while the inflorescence consists of a panicle of spikelets.

It is adapted to thrive in nutrient-poor soils and is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
