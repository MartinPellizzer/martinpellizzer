Davilla rugosa var. martii

Davilla rugosa var. martii, commonly known as the Mexican beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by bright blue berries in the fall. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptical in shape and often have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is adapted to well-drained soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its seasonal color changes.
