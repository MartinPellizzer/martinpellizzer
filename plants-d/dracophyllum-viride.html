Dracophyllum viride

Dracophyllum viride, commonly known as green draco, is a shrub native to the eastern regions of Australia.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage. The plant typically grows in heathland and coastal environments, thriving in well-drained, acidic soils. Its small, bell-shaped flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it produces small, red berries that are edible for some bird species.

Dracophyllum viride is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
