Draba ellipsoidea

Draba ellipsoidea, commonly known as the elliptic whitlow grass, is a small flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants, and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May. Draba ellipsoidea is adapted to cold, alpine environments and thrives in well-drained soils.

It is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats at elevations above 2,000 meters.
