Diplotaxis simplex var. pumila

Diplotaxis simplex var. pumila, commonly known as the dwarf African daisy, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features basal rosettes of narrow, gray-green leaves and produces small, yellow flowers with a distinctive black center. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.

Its compact size and resilience make it a popular choice for Mediterranean and arid climate gardens.
