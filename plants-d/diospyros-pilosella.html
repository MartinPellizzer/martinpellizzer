Diospyros pilosella

Diospyros pilosella, commonly known as the silky persimmon, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other persimmon species and ebony trees. The plant is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and small, glossy, dark green leaves. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by edible, orange to red fruits that are often covered in a fine, silky hair.

The fruit is typically consumed raw or used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
