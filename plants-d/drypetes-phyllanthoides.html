Drypetes phyllanthoides

Drypetes phyllanthoides, commonly known as the white-flowered ironwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood and elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by bright red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in local medicine.
