Dyssodia aurantia

Dyssodia aurantia, commonly known as the golden burr or golden thistle, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in dry, sandy or rocky soils. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, followed by spiny, spherical fruits that contain seeds. Its foliage consists of narrow, gray-green leaves with a fuzzy texture.

Dyssodia aurantia is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
