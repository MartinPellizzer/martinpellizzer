Drosera hirsuta

Drosera hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy sundew, is a carnivorous plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its sticky, glandular hairs that secrete a mucilaginous substance to trap insects. The plant typically grows in wet, acidic environments such as bogs and heathlands. Its leaves are often rosette-shaped and covered in a dense layer of digestive secretions.

Drosera hirsuta is a perennial species that produces small, pinkish-white flowers during the warmer months.
