Draba lonchocarpa

Draba lonchocarpa, commonly known as the long-sepaled whitlow grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a small, perennial herb that typically grows in alpine and arctic regions, often found in rocky or gravelly soils. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in a raceme, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are basal, rosette-like, and have a distinctive waxy coating.

Draba lonchocarpa is adapted to cold climates and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and hardiness.
