Diuris pardina

Diuris pardina, commonly known as the black-tongued orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the southern regions of Western Australia.

It features a compact, leafy rhizome and produces striking, dark red to purple flowers with a distinctive blackened lip. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils in open habitats such as heathlands and woodlands. Its flowering period occurs during the autumn, and it is characterized by its unique morphology and coloration.

Diuris pardina is a rare and endangered species, requiring specific environmental conditions for successful growth and reproduction.
