Dioscorea schimperiana var. nigrescens

Dioscorea schimperiana var. nigrescens, commonly known as the black night yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into capsule fruits. Its tubers are valued for their medicinal properties and are also used as a food source in some regions.

The species is notable for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
