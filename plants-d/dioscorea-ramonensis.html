Dioscorea ramonensis

Dioscorea ramonensis, commonly known as Ramon's yam, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by tuberous roots that are sometimes used in traditional medicine. Its tubers are typically harvested for their nutritional value and have a starchy texture.

Dioscorea ramonensis is an important species in its native ecosystems, contributing to biodiversity and supporting local wildlife.
