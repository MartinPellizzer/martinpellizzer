Drimys microphylla

Drimys microphylla, commonly known as the Chilean wintergreen, is a deciduous shrub native to the temperate regions of South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of white, waxy hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale green, followed by dark blue, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
