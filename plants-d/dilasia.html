Dilasia

Dilasia, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy, blue-violet flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Dilasia thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

It is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
