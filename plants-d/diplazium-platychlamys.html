Diplazium platychlamys

Diplazium platychlamys, commonly known as the broad-leaf holly fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its robust, upright fronds that are typically divided into lance-shaped pinnae. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its spores are produced on the undersides of the fronds in clusters called sori.

Diplazium platychlamys is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
