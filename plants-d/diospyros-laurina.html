Diospyros laurina

Diospyros laurina, commonly known as the Brazilian persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by orange to red berries, which are edible when fully ripe. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

The tree is also valued for its hard, durable wood used in furniture and tool-making.
