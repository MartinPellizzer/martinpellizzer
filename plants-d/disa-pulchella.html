Disa pulchella

Disa pulchella, commonly known as the orchid bell, is a striking member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae) native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant, bell-shaped flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow, often with contrasting dark markings. The plant typically grows as a terrestrial herb, with a short, stout stem and broad, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn, attracting various pollinators such as bees and birds.

Disa pulchella is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
