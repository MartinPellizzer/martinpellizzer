Dicranopygium odoratum

Dicranopygium odoratum, commonly known as the fragrant moss, is a species of moss belonging to the family Dicranaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, cushion-like growth habit and dark green, glossy leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hair. The plant produces small, spirally arranged spore capsules that are typically reddish-brown in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices.

Dicranopygium odoratum is valued for its aromatic properties and is often used in traditional medicine and as a decorative plant.
