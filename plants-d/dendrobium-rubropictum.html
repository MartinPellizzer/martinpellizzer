Dendrobium rubropictum

Dendrobium rubropictum, commonly known as the red-painted orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is an epiphytic orchid, meaning it grows on the surface of trees or rocks without deriving nutrients from them. The plant produces striking, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, often with intricate patterns. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Dendrobium rubropictum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
