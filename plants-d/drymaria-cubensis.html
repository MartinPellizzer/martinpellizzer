Drymaria cubensis

Drymaria cubensis, commonly known as Cuban sandplant, is a low-growing, herbaceous perennial native to the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its small, fleshy leaves and clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and dunes. It has a compact growth habit, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Drymaria cubensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
