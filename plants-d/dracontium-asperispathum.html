Dracontium asperispathum

Dracontium asperispathum, commonly known as the rough-leaf arrowroot, is a herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive spatulate leaves and inflorescence. The plant features thick, fleshy rhizomes that are used for its starchy tubers, which are edible. Its leaves are typically rough to the touch, with a waxy surface that aids in water retention.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid tropical forests.
