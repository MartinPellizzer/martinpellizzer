Duroia micrantha

Duroia micrantha, commonly known as the dwarf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, often with a yellow or orange center. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Duroia micrantha thrives in humid, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.
