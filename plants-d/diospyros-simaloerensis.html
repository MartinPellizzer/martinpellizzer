Diospyros simaloerensis

Diospyros simaloerensis, commonly known as the Philippine persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other species like the ebony and persimmon trees. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptic to ovate in shape. Its flowers are small, clustered, and typically greenish-yellow, while the fruit is a one-seeded drupe that matures to a deep red or orange color.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
