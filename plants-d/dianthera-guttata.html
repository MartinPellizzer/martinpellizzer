Dianthera guttata

Dianthera guttata, commonly known as the spotted bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It features showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple with a distinctive white or yellow spot at the base. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its textured appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Dianthera guttata is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its vibrant, bell-shaped blooms.
