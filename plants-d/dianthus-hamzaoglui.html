Dianthus hamzaoglui

Dianthus hamzaoglui, commonly known as the Hamzaoglui campion, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink and red. The plant features upright, branching stems with narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are composed of five petals and are arranged in clusters, making it a striking addition to gardens.

Dianthus hamzaoglui is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its beauty and fragrance.
