Dioscorea amaranthoides var. elegantula

Dioscorea amaranthoides var. elegantula, commonly known as the elegant yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by tuberous roots that are often harvested for culinary or medicinal use. Its botanical structure includes a complex root system and is adapted to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its foliage rather than its edible tubers.
