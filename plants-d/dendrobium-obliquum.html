Dendrobium obliquum

Dendrobium obliquum, commonly known as the zigzag orchid, is a epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its pendulous, segmented stems that often curve in a zigzag pattern, giving it its common name. The plant produces fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white, yellow, and pink, typically in late winter to early spring. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Dendrobium obliquum is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
