Drynaria fortuni

Drynaria fortuni, commonly known as the golden fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that are often bright green with a slight golden hue. Native to Southeast Asia, this plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant's unique morphology and ornamental value make it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor cultivation.
