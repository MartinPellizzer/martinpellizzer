Doliocarpus hispidus

Doliocarpus hispidus, commonly known as the roughleaf sycamore, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and elliptical leaves with a leathery texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits that contain one or two seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

Doliocarpus hispidus is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
