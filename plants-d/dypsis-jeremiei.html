Dypsis jeremiei

Dypsis jeremiei, commonly known as the Madagascar palm, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, spiky, bottle-shaped trunk and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters at the top of the trunk. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and ability to thrive in arid conditions.

The species is also valued for its resilience and low maintenance requirements.
