Deyeuxia arundinacea var. laxiflora

Deyeuxia arundinacea var. laxiflora, commonly known as the slender reed, is a grass species native to temperate regions of Asia and Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, wet meadows, and along riverbanks. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like arrangement of small, spikelet-bearing branches.

This variety is notable for its adaptability to waterlogged soils and its role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
