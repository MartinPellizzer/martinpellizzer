Dorycnium insulare

Dorycnium insulare, commonly known as the island sainfoin, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Europe.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. The plant features slender, upright stems with alternate, pinnately compound leaves. Its flowers are yellow and borne in racemes, adding a bright touch to its appearance.

Dorycnium insulare is known for its medicinal properties and has been used historically in traditional herbal remedies.
