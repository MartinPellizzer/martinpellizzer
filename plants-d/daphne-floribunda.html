Daphne floribunda

Daphne floribunda, commonly known as the snowdrop daphne, is a deciduous shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It features dense, aromatic foliage with a grayish-green hue and produces clusters of fragrant, white or pale pink flowers in early spring. The plant is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves and stems, which are often covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or woodland settings.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is a popular choice among gardeners seeking winter interest.
