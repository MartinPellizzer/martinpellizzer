Draba carinthiaca var. glabrata

Draba carinthiaca var. glabrata, commonly known as the Carpathian whitlow grass, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, glabrous (hairless) leaves and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in early spring. The plant is adapted to rocky and alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in its native range.
