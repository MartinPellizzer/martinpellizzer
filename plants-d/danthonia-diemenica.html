Danthonia diemenica

Danthonia diemenica, commonly known as the Tasmanian hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Tasmania.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and fine, wiry leaves that are often covered in a whitish bloom. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in heathlands and swampy areas.

Danthonia diemenica plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native fauna.
