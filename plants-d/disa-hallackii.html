Disa hallackii

Disa hallackii, commonly known as the Hallack's Disa, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy habitats, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with a distinctive lip that aids in pollination.

Disa hallackii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
