Diasperus vieillardii

Diasperus vieillardii, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and is adapted to arid and semi-arid climates. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Diasperus vieillardii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
