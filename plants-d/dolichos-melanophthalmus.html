Dolichos melanophthalmus

Dolichos melanophthalmus, commonly known as the black-eyed bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It produces elongated, bean-like pods that contain several seeds, and its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets. The plant is characterized by its distinctive black eye-like markings on the seed pods. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a warm climate for optimal growth.

Dolichos melanophthalmus is valued for its nutritional seeds and is often cultivated for both food and ornamental purposes.
