Deyeuxia carinata

Deyeuxia carinata, commonly known as the ridge grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist soil conditions. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a distinct ridge on the upper surface, giving it its common name. Its inflorescence consists of a spikelet-bearing panicle, which is often slender and drooping.

Deyeuxia carinata plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms in its native ecosystems.
