Dalea carthagenensis subsp. typica

Dalea carthagenensis subsp.

typica, commonly known as the Carthage milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and small, pea-like flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils, adapting well to arid conditions. Its small, compound leaves and distinctive yellowish-pink flowers make it a notable species in its native range.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides nectar for pollinators in its ecosystem.
