Dactylorchis maculata var. sudetica

Dactylorchis maculata var. sudetica, commonly known as the Sudetic spotted orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to Central Europe.

It is characterized by its distinctive dark purple or brown spots on the leaves and petals, which contribute to its ornamental appeal. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows, where it thrives in nutrient-rich, well-drained soils. It produces small, delicate flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer, adding a subtle beauty to its natural habitat.

Due to habitat loss and environmental changes, this variety is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
