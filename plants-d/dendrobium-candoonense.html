Dendrobium candoonense

Dendrobium candoonense, commonly known as the Candoon orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of fragrant, showy flowers. The plant typically produces pale yellow to greenish-white blooms with red or purple markings, often appearing during the dry season. Its pseudobulbs are cylindrical and covered with a thick layer of white or grayish bark-like tissue.

Dendrobium candoonense thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on trees or rocks in montane forests.
