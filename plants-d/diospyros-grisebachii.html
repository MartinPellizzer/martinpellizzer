Diospyros grisebachii

Diospyros grisebachii, commonly known as the African persimmon, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other persimmon species. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are followed by edible, fleshy fruits that ripen to a deep orange or red color.

Diospyros grisebachii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
