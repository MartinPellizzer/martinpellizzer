Dactylorhiza traunsteineri var. tarbatonica

Dactylorhiza traunsteineri var. tarbatonica, commonly known as the Tarbatonica orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and a single, erect flower spike bearing a single, showy orchid flower. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland or heathland habitats. It blooms between May and July, producing a violet or purple flower with a distinctive lip.

This orchid is part of the Dactylorhiza genus, known for its terrestrial growth habit and association with specific ecological conditions.
