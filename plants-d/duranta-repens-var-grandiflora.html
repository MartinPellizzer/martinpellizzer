Duranta repens var. grandiflora

Duranta repens var. grandiflora, commonly known as the silverleaf duranta, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and northern South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit, with slender stems that often root at the nodes. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to blue or violet, depending on the variety. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a silvery underside, giving it its common name.

It is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and as a ground cover due to its rapid growth and ornamental appeal.
