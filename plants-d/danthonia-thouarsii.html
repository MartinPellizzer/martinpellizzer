Danthonia thouarsii

Danthonia thouarsii, commonly known as the New Caledonian grass, is a perennial grass species native to the island of New Caledonia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in coastal and sandy soils, often in areas with high salinity and strong winds. Its flowers are small and clustered in panicles, and it plays a role in stabilizing coastal dunes.

Danthonia thouarsii is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native habitat.
