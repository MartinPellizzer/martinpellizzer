Dendrocousinsia spicata

Dendrocousinsia spicata, commonly known as the spiny tree cactus, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are covered in sharp, spiny tubercles, which serve as a defense mechanism against herbivores. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers during the rainy season, adding a striking contrast to its rugged appearance. Its leaves are modified into spines, and it stores water in its thick, fleshy stems to survive in dry conditions.

Dendrocousinsia spicata is well adapted to harsh environments, making it a resilient and unique member of the cactus family.
