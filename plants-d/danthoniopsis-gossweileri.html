Danthoniopsis gossweileri

Danthoniopsis gossweileri, commonly known as the Cape Flats grass, is a perennial grass species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of silky hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal and sandy environments.

Due to its ornamental appeal and hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
