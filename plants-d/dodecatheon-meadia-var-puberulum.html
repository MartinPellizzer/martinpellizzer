Dodecatheon meadia var. puberulum

Dodecatheon meadia var. puberulum, commonly known as the pubescent Solomon's seal, is a variant of the Solomon's seal plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces clusters of bell-shaped, white to pinkish flowers in early spring. The leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a loose spiral, with a soft, hairy texture that distinguishes this variety. The plant thrives in moist, shaded woodland habitats and has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread vegetatively.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important component of its native ecosystem.
