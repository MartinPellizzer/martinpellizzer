Drimia pusilla var. setosa

Drimia pusilla var. setosa, commonly known as the slender bulbous plant, is a compact perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features small, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine, stiff hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale blue flowers during the summer months. Its bulbs are adapted for storage and survival in arid conditions, making it resilient to drought.

It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal.
