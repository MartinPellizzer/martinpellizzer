Dasycarya grisea

Dasycarya grisea, commonly known as the gray sponge algae, is a species of calcareous algae belonging to the family Dasycyrtaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, sponge-like structure composed of calcified cells, which provide structural support and protection. Its coloration ranges from gray to brown, depending on the environmental conditions and light exposure.

Dasycarya grisea plays a role in marine ecosystems by contributing to reef structures and serving as a habitat for various microorganisms.
