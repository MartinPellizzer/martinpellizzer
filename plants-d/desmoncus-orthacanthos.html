Desmoncus orthacanthos

Desmoncus orthacanthos, commonly known as the spiny cactus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that store water, allowing it to survive in dry environments. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking visual element to its desert habitat. Its spines serve as a defense mechanism against herbivores and also help reduce water loss by providing shade.

Overall, Desmoncus orthacanthos exemplifies the adaptive strategies of plants in extreme climates.
