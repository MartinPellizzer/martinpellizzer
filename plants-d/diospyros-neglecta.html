Diospyros neglecta

Diospyros neglecta, commonly known as the wild persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other persimmon species. The plant is characterized by its elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green on top, with a lighter underside. Its small, yellowish flowers are followed by orange to red fruits that are edible when fully ripe.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
