Desmodium cinerascens var. microphyllum

Desmodium cinerascens var. microphyllum, commonly known as the small-leaved tick trefoil, is a perennial leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features trifoliate leaves with small, heart-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in racemes. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and stipules, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or disturbed areas.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it ecologically significant in soil enrichment.
