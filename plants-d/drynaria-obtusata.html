Drynaria obtusata

Drynaria obtusata, commonly known as the broad-leafed tree fern, is a member of the Polypodiaceae family.

It is a evergreen fern that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features a tall, slender trunk covered with overlapping, leathery fronds. Its rhizome is thick and fleshy, serving as a storage organ for nutrients.

The fronds are bipinnate, with a distinctive broad, rounded shape that gives the plant its common name.
