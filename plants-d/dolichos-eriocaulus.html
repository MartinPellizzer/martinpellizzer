Dolichos eriocaulus

Dolichos eriocaulus, commonly known as the hairy dolichos, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. Its pods are elongated and contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.

Dolichos eriocaulus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
