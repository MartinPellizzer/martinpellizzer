Daphne dolichantha

Daphne dolichantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved daphne, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form and produces clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth, glossy surface. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual and aromatic qualities.
