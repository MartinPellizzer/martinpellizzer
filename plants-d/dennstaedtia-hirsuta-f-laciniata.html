Dennstaedtia hirsuta f. laciniata

Dennstaedtia hirsuta f. laciniata, commonly known as the fern with finely divided fronds, is a varietal form of the broad-leafed fern Dennstaedtia hirsuta.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely serrated fronds that give it a lacy appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodland areas or along stream banks. Its rhizomes are creeping and produce spore-bearing leaves that are typically green and upright.

This fern is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in shady garden settings.
