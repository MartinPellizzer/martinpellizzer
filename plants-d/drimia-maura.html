Drimia maura

Drimia maura, commonly known as the African lily, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its strap-shaped, evergreen leaves. The plant produces striking, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Drimia maura is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is often used in rock gardens or as a border plant.
