Digitaria nardifolia

Digitaria nardifolia, commonly known as slender fingergrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are narrow with a slightly wavy margin. Its inflorescence consists of compact, spike-like clusters of small, two-ranked spikelets.

Digitaria nardifolia is adapted to dry conditions and is often found in areas with low rainfall.
