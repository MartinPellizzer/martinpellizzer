Diospyros laevis

Diospyros laevis, commonly known as the smooth persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and palmate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into orange to red, fleshy fruits. The fruit is typically edible when fully ripe, with a sweet, honey-like flavor.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland and swampy areas.
