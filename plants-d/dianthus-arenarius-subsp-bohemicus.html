Dianthus arenarius subsp. bohemicus

Dianthus arenarius subsp.

bohemicus, commonly known as the Bohemian sandpink, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Central Europe. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, pinkish-red flowers with five petals.

The plant thrives in dry, sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open habitats such as rocky slopes and grasslands. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
