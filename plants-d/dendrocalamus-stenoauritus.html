Dendrocalamus stenoauritus

Dendrocalamus stenoauritus, commonly known as the narrow-auricle bamboo, is a species of giant bamboo native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 20 meters. The plant has narrow, leathery leaves with a distinctive auricle, or ear-like structure, at the base. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

This bamboo is valued for its strong, durable stalks, which are used in construction and handicrafts.
