Dysphania chilensis

Dysphania chilensis, commonly known as "Chilean spinach," is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, edible leaves and vibrant red or purple flowers. The plant thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are rich in nutrients and have been used in traditional cuisines for their mild, spinach-like flavor.

Dysphania chilensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
