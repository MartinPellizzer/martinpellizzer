Draba eschscholtzii

Draba eschscholtzii, commonly known as the Eschscholtz's whitlow grass, is a flowering plant native to western North America.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. This species typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. It produces numerous tiny, white to pale yellow flowers in early spring.

The plant has a compact, mat-like growth form and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.
