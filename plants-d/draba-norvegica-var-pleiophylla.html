Draba norvegica var. pleiophylla

Draba norvegica var. pleiophylla, commonly known as the Norwegian whitlowgrass, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Scandinavia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in alpine and arctic environments, thriving in well-drained, rocky soils. It has basal leaves that are usually lobed or divided, and its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer.

This variety is notable for its ability to tolerate harsh climatic conditions, making it an important component of high-altitude flora.
