Dianthus gracilis subsp. drenowskianus

Dianthus gracilis subsp.

drenowskianus, commonly known as the slender carnation, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountains of Central Asia. It features slender, erect stems with narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The flowers are typically pink to red, with five petals and a distinctive white or pale pink center, blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
