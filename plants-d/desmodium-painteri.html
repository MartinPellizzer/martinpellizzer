Desmodium painteri

Desmodium painteri, commonly known as the painter's bean, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is often found in grassland and woodland habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Desmodium painteri is also notable for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.

Its ornamental value has led to its cultivation in some regions for both garden and ecological purposes.
