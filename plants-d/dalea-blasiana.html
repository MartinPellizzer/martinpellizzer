Dalea blasiana

Dalea blasiana, commonly known as the Blasian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its compound leaves are pinnately arranged, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Dalea blasiana is often found in chaparral and desert scrub habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
