Diuris setacea

Diuris setacea, commonly known as the spiny helmet orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southeastern Australia.

It typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in heathlands and open forests. The plant features a slender, erect stem with a single, erect or slightly drooping flower spike. Its flowers are distinctive, with a helmet-like labellum and a spiny appearance, contributing to its common name.

Diuris setacea is known for its striking, long-lasting blooms that appear in spring and early summer.
