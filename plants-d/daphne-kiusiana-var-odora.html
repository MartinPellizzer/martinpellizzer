Daphne kiusiana var. odora

Daphne kiusiana var. odora, commonly known as the fragrant daphne, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense, glossy green foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white to pink, typically in late winter to early spring. Its aromatic blooms emit a sweet, spicy scent, making it a popular ornamental plant.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, requiring moderate watering and protection from frost.
