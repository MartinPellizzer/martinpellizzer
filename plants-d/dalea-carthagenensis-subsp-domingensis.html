Dalea carthagenensis subsp. domingensis

Dalea carthagenensis subsp.

domingensis, commonly known as the Domingo's milkvetch, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers.

The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, often in rocky or sandy soils. It produces small, round pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and supporting local wildlife.
