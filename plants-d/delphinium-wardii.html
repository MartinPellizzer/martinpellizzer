Delphinium wardii

Delphinium wardii, commonly known as the Ward's larkspur, is a tall, herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features striking blue to violet flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
