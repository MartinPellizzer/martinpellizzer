Dombeya arabica

Dombeya arabica, commonly known as the Arabian dombeya, is a flowering shrub native to the Arabian Peninsula.

It produces showy, cup-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh climates. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Dombeya arabica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
