Dasystachys papillosa

Dasystachys papillosa, commonly known as the silky bower vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered with fine, silky hairs, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded areas.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowering habit.
