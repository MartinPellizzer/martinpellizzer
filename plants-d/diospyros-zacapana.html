Diospyros zacapana

Diospyros zacapana, commonly known as the Zacapan tamarind, is a deciduous tree native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that give way to edible, reddish-purple fruits. Its fruits are valued for their sweet, tart flavor and are often used in local cuisines.

The tree is also appreciated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.
