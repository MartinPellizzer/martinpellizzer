Dimorphandra vernicosa

Dimorphandra vernicosa, commonly known as the yellow trumpet tree, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. The plant has a thick, often flaky bark and can grow quite tall, reaching up to 20 meters in height. Its leaves are compound, with elongated leaflets that are often covered in a waxy coating.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in warm climates for its striking floral display.
