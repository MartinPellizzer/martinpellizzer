Dactylorhiza × carnea

Dactylorhiza × carnea, commonly known as the pink marsh orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a terrestrial herb with a slender, upright stem that bears several linear leaves. The plant produces tall spikes of showy, pinkish-purple flowers with a distinctive three-lobed lip. It thrives in wet, acidic habitats such as bogs and marshes, often growing in shallow water.

Dactylorhiza × carnea is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in horticulture.
