Diospyros virginiana var. mosieri

Diospyros virginiana var. mosieri, commonly known as the persimmon.

This variant is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its oblong, dark green leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers in the spring. The plant bears orange to red berries that are edible when fully ripe.

It is often found in wetland habitats and is valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
