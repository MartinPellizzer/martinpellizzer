Dichopogon capillipes

Dichopogon capillipes, commonly known as the slender hookveld grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in open, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in grassland and shrubland habitats. The plant has slender, erect stems with small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowering inflorescence consists of a single, spike-like structure bearing tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Dichopogon capillipes plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
