Dianthus hendersonii

Dianthus hendersonii, commonly known as the Henderson's campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of pink or white, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces small, seed-like fruits known as achenes.

Dianthus hendersonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
