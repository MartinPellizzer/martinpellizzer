Delissea coriacea var. haupuensis

Delissea coriacea var. haupuensis, commonly known as the Hawaiian rock fig, is a species native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a shrub characterized by its thick, leathery leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant thrives in rocky, coastal environments and is adapted to the unique climate of Hawaii. It plays an important role in local ecosystems and is considered an endangered species due to habitat loss.

Its botanical features make it distinct among the species of the genus Delissea.
