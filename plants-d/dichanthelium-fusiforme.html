Dichanthelium fusiforme

Dichanthelium fusiforme, commonly known as the narrow-leaf panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies, preferring moist to saturated soil conditions. The plant has slender, cylindrical leaves and produces spikelets that are often clustered at the base of the stem. Its flowering season occurs in late spring to early summer, and it is characterized by its distinctive, narrow leaf blades.

Dichanthelium fusiforme plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species in its native environment.
