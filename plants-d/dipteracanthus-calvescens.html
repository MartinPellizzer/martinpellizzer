Dipteracanthus calvescens

Dipteracanthus calvescens, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety texture, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often blooming during the wet season. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forests or along riverbanks.

Dipteracanthus calvescens is also known for its medicinal properties, with various parts of the plant used in traditional herbal remedies.
