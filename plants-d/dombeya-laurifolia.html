Dombeya laurifolia

Dombeya laurifolia, commonly known as the laurel dombeya, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green, often with a slight waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white to pale pink, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Dombeya laurifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
