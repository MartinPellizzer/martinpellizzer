Dracocephalum formosius

Dracocephalum formosius, commonly known as Chinese skullcap, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to China.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants such as mint and sage. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its medicinal properties.

The genus name "Dracocephalum" refers to the dragon's head, alluding to the shape of its flowers.
