Drepananthus vitiensis

Drepananthus vitiensis, commonly known as the Vitiensis pandanus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Pacific.

It belongs to the family Pandanaceae and is characterized by its tall, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and culinary applications. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or green in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or lowland areas.

Due to its unique appearance and cultural significance, it is valued both for its aesthetic appeal and practical uses.
