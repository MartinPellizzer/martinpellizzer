Dioscorea laxiflora var. auriculata

Dioscorea laxiflora var. auriculata, commonly known as the auriculate yam, is a variant of the yam genus native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a climbing herbaceous perennial with large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces tuberous roots that are often used in traditional medicine and as a food source. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique floral structure and medicinal potential.
