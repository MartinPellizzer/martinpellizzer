Dendrochilum quinquangulare

Dendrochilum quinquangulare, commonly known as the five-angled tree orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive five-angled structure on the petals. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and orchid enthusiasts alike.
