Diplodiscus scortechinii

Diplodiscus scortechinii, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to eastern Africa.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-like arrangement of fleshy, dark green leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple, often with a contrasting throat. It thrives in humid, warm environments and is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value.

Diplodiscus scortechinii requires well-draining soil and indirect sunlight to flourish.
