Drimiopsis botryoides subsp. botryoides

Drimiopsis botryoides subsp.

botryoides, commonly known as the Cape bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South Africa. It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color.

The plant has strap-like, evergreen leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers a sunny to partially shaded environment.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
