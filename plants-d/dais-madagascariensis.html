Dais madagascariensis

Dais madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar daisy, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to the arid conditions of its native environment. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces yellow to orange flower heads.

Dais madagascariensis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and resilience.
