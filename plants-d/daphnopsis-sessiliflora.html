Daphnopsis sessiliflora

Daphnopsis sessiliflora, commonly known as the sessile-flowered daphnopsis, is a member of the Ericaceae family and is native to the eastern United States.

It is a shrub characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant has elliptical leaves that are leathery and dark green, often with a slight waxiness to their surface. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
