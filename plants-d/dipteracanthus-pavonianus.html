Dipteracanthus pavonianus

Dipteracanthus pavonianus, commonly known as the Pavonian spiderflower, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, spider-like inflorescence composed of numerous small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories, and prefers well-drained soil. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
