Dichilus spicatus

Dichilus spicatus, commonly known as the spiked dichilus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in coastal and sandy environments. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. Its leaves are usually narrow and opposite, and it has a woody stem with rough, fissured bark.

Dichilus spicatus is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
