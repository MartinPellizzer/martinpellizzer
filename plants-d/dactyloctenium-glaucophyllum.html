Dactyloctenium glaucophyllum

Dactyloctenium glaucophyllum, commonly known as the silverleaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its glaucous, bluish-gray foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant has slender, erect stems and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in both full sun and partial shade.

Its drought tolerance makes it a hardy plant in arid environments.
