Dimorpholepis intermedia

Dimorpholepis intermedia, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in high-altitude regions, often in rocky or mountainous terrain. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink and are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a velvety texture.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in temperate climates.
