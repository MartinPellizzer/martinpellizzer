Diplotheca tigrensis

Diplotheca tigrensis, commonly known as the Cuban bluebell, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean, particularly Cuba.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is known for its composite flower heads. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

Diplotheca tigrensis thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.
