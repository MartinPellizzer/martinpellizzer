Diasperus daltonii

Diasperus daltonii, commonly known as Dalton's rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other mustard plants. The plant is characterized by its basal rosette of dark green, hairy leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Diasperus daltonii is often found in desert scrub and chaparral habitats.
