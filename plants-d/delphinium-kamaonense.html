Delphinium kamaonense

Delphinium kamaonense, commonly known as the Kamaon larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky soils, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are palmately compound, with serrated edges, and the flowers are arranged in spikes.

Delphinium kamaonense is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
