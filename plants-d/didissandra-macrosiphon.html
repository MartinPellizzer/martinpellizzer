Didissandra macrosiphon

Didissandra macrosiphon, commonly known as the African moonseed, is a climbing shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by distinctive, fleshy, orange-red berries. Its roots and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their purported medicinal properties.

The plant's unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
