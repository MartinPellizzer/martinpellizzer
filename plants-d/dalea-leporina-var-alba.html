Dalea leporina var. alba

Dalea leporina var. alba, commonly known as the white prairie clover, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies and sandy soils. The plant features slender, branching stems that can reach up to 1 meter in height, adorned with small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in dense clusters. Its leaves are narrow and pinnately divided, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its ornamental appeal in native plant gardens.
