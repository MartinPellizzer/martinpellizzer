Digitaria sanguinalis var. evalvula

Digitaria sanguinalis var. evalvula, commonly known as the narrowleaf bottlebrush grass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and dense, spike-like inflorescences that resemble a bottlebrush. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a distinctive waxy coating, and its flowers are typically pale green to brown in color. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and prairies, and is often found in disturbed soils.

The species is noted for its ecological importance, providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
