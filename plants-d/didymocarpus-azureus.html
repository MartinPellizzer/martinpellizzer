Didymocarpus azureus

Didymocarpus azureus, commonly known as the blue-fruit banyan, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that are typically a deep green with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by edible, blue-black berries. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in moist, shaded environments.

The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
