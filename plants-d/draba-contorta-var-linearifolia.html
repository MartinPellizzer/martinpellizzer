Draba contorta var. linearifolia

Draba contorta var. linearifolia, commonly known as the slender whitlowgrass, is a flowering plant native to alpine and arctic regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to cold, harsh environments. Its leaves are linear and often clustered at the base, while the stems are usually erect and hairy.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitats and is of interest to botanists studying plant adaptation to extreme climates.
