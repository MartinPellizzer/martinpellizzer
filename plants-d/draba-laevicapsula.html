Draba laevicapsula

Draba laevicapsula, commonly known as the smooth-capsuled whitlow grass, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is a small, herbaceous annual plant characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, linear leaves. The flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in dense racemes, typical of the mustard family. It produces small, smooth, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.

This plant is often found in disturbed soils and rocky habitats across the northern hemisphere.
