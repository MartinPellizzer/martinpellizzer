Diospyros evena

Diospyros evena, commonly known as the African persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other well-known species like ebony. The plant is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and opposite, simple leaves that are elliptical in shape. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by edible, orange to red berries.

The fruit is often consumed raw or used in traditional medicine due to its nutritional and medicinal properties.
