Dactylis adscendens

Dactylis adscendens, commonly known as sweet vernal grass, is a perennial grass native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and its bright yellowish-green inflorescence, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and disturbed sites. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, while the seeds are small and wind-dispersed.

Dactylis adscendens is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in ecological restoration projects.
