Dendrosenecio kilimanjari subsp. kilimanjari

Dendrosenecio kilimanjari subsp.

kilimanjari, commonly known as the Kilimanjaro tree heath, is a succulent plant native to the high-altitude regions of Mount Kilimanjaro in Tanzania. It is characterized by its dense, shrub-like growth habit with thick, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting to the arid conditions of its environment.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are often covered in fine, white hairs, which help reduce water loss and protect against intense sunlight.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations to extreme altitudes and its role in the local ecosystem.
