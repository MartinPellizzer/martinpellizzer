Disporum sessile f. variegatum

Disporum sessile f. variegatum, commonly known as the variegated mouseear.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States, characterized by its distinctive, arrowhead-shaped leaves with a variegated pattern of green and white. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom in early spring. Its foliage is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value due to its striking coloration.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
