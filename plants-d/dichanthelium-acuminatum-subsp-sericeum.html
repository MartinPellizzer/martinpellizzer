Dichanthelium acuminatum subsp. sericeum

Dichanthelium acuminatum subsp.

sericeum, commonly known as silky fingergrass, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves with a distinctive silvery sheen.

The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences, which are typical of the grass family Poaceae. Its leaves are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it its common name.

This species is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in open habitats such as prairies and disturbed areas.
