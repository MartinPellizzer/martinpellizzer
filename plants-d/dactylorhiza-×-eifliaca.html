Dactylorhiza × eifliaca

Dactylorhiza × eifliaca, commonly known as the Eifel orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to central Europe.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with a rhizome that produces multiple shoots each year. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, arranged in a spike with a distinct scent.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with appropriate humidity and light conditions.
