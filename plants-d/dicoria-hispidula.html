Dicoria hispidula

Dicoria hispidula, commonly known as the hairy dicoria, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on long peduncles. Dicoria hispidula is often found in arid or semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies of drought-tolerant flora.
