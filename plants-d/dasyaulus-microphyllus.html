Dasyaulus microphyllus

Dasyaulus microphyllus, commonly known as the small-leaved dasyaulus, is a rare and ancient plant species that dates back to the Carboniferous period.

It is characterized by its small, simple leaves and a unique structure that suggests it was an early vascular plant. The plant's morphology indicates it had a root system and a stem with vascular tissue, which allowed for efficient water and nutrient transport. Its fossils are often found in coal deposits, providing insight into the early evolution of land plants.

This species is significant in understanding the transition from aquatic to terrestrial plant life.
