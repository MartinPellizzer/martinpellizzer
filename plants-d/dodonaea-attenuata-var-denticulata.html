Dodonaea attenuata var. denticulata

Dodonaea attenuata var. denticulata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hop bush, is a shrub native to Australia.

It features slender, wiry stems and narrow, finely toothed leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers clustered in spikes, which are typically borne in the axils of the leaves. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for coastal and dryland environments.

The variety is noted for its ornamental value and ability to provide habitat for native wildlife.
