Dichaea sodiroi

Dichaea sodiroi, commonly known as the Sodiro's dichaea, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, sympodial growth habit and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale pink. The plant typically grows at elevations between 1,500 and 2,500 meters, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually elliptical and covered with a dense layer of fine, white pubescence.

Dichaea sodiroi is often found in association with other epiphytic orchids, growing on the trunks and branches of trees in its natural habitat.
