Dianthus gaditanus subsp. occidentalis

Dianthus gaditanus subsp.

occidentalis, commonly known as the western carnation, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe. It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink or white flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils, often in coastal areas. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, while its stems are upright and branched.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
