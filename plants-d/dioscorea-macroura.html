Dioscorea macroura

Dioscorea macroura, commonly known as the long-tubed yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers followed by elongated, tuberous roots that are often used in traditional medicine. Its tubers are rich in starch and have been utilized for food in various cultures.

Dioscorea macroura is also valued for its potential medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and antidiabetic effects.
