Draba spectabilis var. bella

Draba spectabilis var. bella, commonly known as the showy whitlow grass, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and pungent scent. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its delicate, basal rosette of leaves is often covered in fine hairs, and it produces clusters of white or pale yellow flowers in late spring.

Draba spectabilis var. bella is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
