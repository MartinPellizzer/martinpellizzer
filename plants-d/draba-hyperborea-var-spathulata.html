Draba hyperborea var. spathulata

Draba hyperborea var. spathulata, commonly known as the alpine bellflower, is a flowering plant native to high-altitude regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its small, cup-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are basal, rosette-forming, and often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

This variety is notable for its resilience to harsh climatic conditions and its role in alpine ecosystems.
