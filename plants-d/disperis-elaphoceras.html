Disperis elaphoceras

Disperis elaphoceras, commonly known as the horned orchid, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, elongated floral spurs that resemble horns. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Its flowers are usually pale green or yellow, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insect species.

Disperis elaphoceras is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
