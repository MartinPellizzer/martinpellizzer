Dioscorea harrissii

Dioscorea harrissii, commonly known as Harriss' yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by tuberous roots that are the primary part used. These tubers are often cultivated for their nutritional value and traditional medicinal uses.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
