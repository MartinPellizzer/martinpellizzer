Dalea hallii

Dalea hallii, commonly known as Hall's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, such as grasslands and rocky soils. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, and it has compound leaves with slender, thread-like leaflets. Dalea hallii is part of the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and it has the ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with soil bacteria.
