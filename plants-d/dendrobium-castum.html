Dendrobium castum

Dendrobium castum, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a rosette of fleshy, dark green leaves that store water. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange, often with intricate patterns. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining potting media.

Dendrobium castum is prized for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable conditions.
