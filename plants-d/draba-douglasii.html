Draba douglasii

Draba douglasii, commonly known as Douglas's whitlow grass, is a small flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It typically grows in moist, rocky habitats such as stream banks and forest floors. The plant has basal leaves that are narrow and grass-like, and it produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers in early spring. It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard plants.

Draba douglasii is often found in alpine and subalpine regions, where it thrives in cool, humid conditions.
