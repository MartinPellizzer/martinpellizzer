Dendrobium dionaeoides

Dendrobium dionaeoides, commonly known as the Dionaeoides orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, often forming a rosette at the base of the stem.

Dendrobium dionaeoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
