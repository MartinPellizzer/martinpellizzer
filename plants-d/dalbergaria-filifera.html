Dalbergaria filifera

Dalbergaria filifera, commonly known as the silky sarsaparilla, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant produces small, hairy pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
