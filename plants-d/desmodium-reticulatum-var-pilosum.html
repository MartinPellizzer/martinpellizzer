Desmodium reticulatum var. pilosum

Desmodium reticulatum var. pilosum, commonly known as hairy desmodium, is a perennial legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces, and its small, pea-like flowers are typically pink to purple in color. The plant produces slender, erect stems and is often found in grassy areas or disturbed soils. It has a fibrous root system and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it valuable in soil enrichment.

The species is also used in forage and as a cover crop in agricultural settings.
