Dyschoriste pulegium

Dyschoriste pulegium, commonly known as water hemp or blueweed, is a flowering plant native to wetland areas in Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often near streams and marshes. The plant has slender, branching stems and small, opposite leaves that are usually hairy and have a distinctive blue-green color. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikes, with five petals that are often blue or purple in color.
