Dinebra aristidoides

Dinebra aristidoides, commonly known as the silver fern, is a striking plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that features delicate, fern-like leaves with a silvery sheen due to fine, silky hairs covering the foliage. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white, followed by clusters of tiny, round berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its unique appearance and ability to tolerate partial shade make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
