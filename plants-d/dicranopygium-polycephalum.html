Dicranopygium polycephalum

Dicranopygium polycephalum, commonly known as the hairy clubmoss, is a species of lycophyte that belongs to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and the presence of specialized structures called strobila, which bear the sporangia. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its leaves are usually small, linear, and arranged spirally around the stem.

Dicranopygium polycephalum plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
