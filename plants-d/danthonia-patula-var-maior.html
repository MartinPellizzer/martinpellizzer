Danthonia patula var. maior

Danthonia patula var. maior, commonly known as the tall danthonia, is a perennial grass species native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant features slender, upright stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

The inflorescence consists of a single, dense spike that bears small, spikelet-like flowers, which are often wind-pollinated.
