Deppea splendens

Deppea splendens, commonly known as the golden flame plant, is a striking tropical species native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that range from orange to red. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a glossy texture and produces clusters of tubular flowers that attract pollinators like bees and hummingbirds. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
