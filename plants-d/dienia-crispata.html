Dienia crispata

Dienia crispata, commonly known as the brittle leaf vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are often wavy or serrated, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its rapid growth and unique foliage make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
