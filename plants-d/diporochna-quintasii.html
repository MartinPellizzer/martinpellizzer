Diporochna quintasii

Diporochna quintasii, commonly known as the Quintasii orchid, is a rare and unique orchid species native to specific regions in South America.

It is characterized by its intricate floral structure, featuring multiple petals and a specialized lip that aids in pollination. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as cloud forests, where it relies on mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient absorption. Its botanical significance lies in its distinct morphology and ecological role within its habitat.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species, highlighting the need for conservation efforts.
