Dorstenia psilurus var. scabra

Dorstenia psilurus var. scabra, commonly known as the roughleaf false arum, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix and a spathe. The plant features heart-shaped, rough-textured leaves that are often covered in small, stiff hairs. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Due to its specific care requirements, it is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
