Dorstenia indica

Dorstenia indica, commonly known as the "arrowleaf" or "bushy arrowhead," is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Dorstenia indica is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique foliage and ability to thrive in shaded environments.

It is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
