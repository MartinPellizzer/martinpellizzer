Doellingeria gormanii

Doellingeria gormanii, commonly known as Gorman's panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is characterized by its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in poor soils.

Botanically, it is classified within the Poaceae family, which includes many other grass species.
