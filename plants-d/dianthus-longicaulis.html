Dianthus longicaulis

Dianthus longicaulis, commonly known as the long-stemmed carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of showy, pink to red flowers with a distinctive fringed appearance. The plant has linear, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Dianthus longicaulis is also valued for its long-lasting blooms and is often used in floral arrangements.
