Dianthus haematocalyx subsp. pindicola

Dianthus haematocalyx subsp.

pindicola, commonly known as the Pindus pink, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Greece. It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to reddish-pink flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces small, clustered flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil and full sun.
