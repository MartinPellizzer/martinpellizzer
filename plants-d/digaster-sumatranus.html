Digaster sumatranus

Digaster sumatranus, commonly known as the Sumatran starfish, is a marine invertebrate belonging to the family Astropectinidae.

It is characterized by its distinctive radial symmetry and a body composed of a central disc with five arms. The species is native to the Indo-Pacific region, including the waters around Sumatra. It typically inhabits coral reefs and rocky substrates, where it feeds on small invertebrates.

Due to its ecological role in maintaining reef health, it is an important species in marine biodiversity studies.
