Dendrocalamus elegans

Dendrocalamus elegans, commonly known as the elegant bamboo, is a tall, slender grass-like plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its hollow, jointed stems that can grow up to 20 meters in height. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically green and arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem. Its rapid growth rate makes it a valuable resource for bamboo harvesting and sustainable forestry.

Dendrocalamus elegans is also appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
