Diospyros calcicola

Diospyros calcicola, commonly known as the limestone persimmon, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that help reduce water loss in dry environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are pollinated by insects, followed by red, fleshy fruits that are edible for both humans and animals. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in nutrient-poor, calcareous soils.

Diospyros calcicola plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
