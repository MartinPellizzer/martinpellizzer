Dianthus myrtinervius var. oxylepis

Dianthus myrtinervius var. oxylepis, commonly known as the rock campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its stems are usually branched and somewhat woody. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
