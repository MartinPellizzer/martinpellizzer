Draba nivalis var. denudata

Draba nivalis var. denudata, commonly known as the naked snowdrop, is a low-growing, herbaceous plant native to alpine and arctic regions.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in cold, high-altitude environments. Its leaves are narrow and often appear after the flowers, which is a distinguishing feature.

It is adapted to survive in harsh conditions with minimal snow cover and low temperatures.
