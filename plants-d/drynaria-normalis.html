Drynaria normalis

Drynaria normalis, commonly known as the common shield fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its erect, leathery fronds that are typically divided into segments resembling shields, hence its common name. The plant is native to various regions in Asia, including India, China, and Southeast Asia, and thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its rhizome is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Drynaria normalis is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appearance and ease of cultivation.
