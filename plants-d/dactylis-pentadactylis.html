Dactylis pentadactylis

Dactylis pentadactylis, commonly known as the five-toothed grass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and disturbed areas.

Dactylis pentadactylis is valued for its forage quality and ecological role in stabilizing soil.
