Delphinium peregrinum f. ochroleucum

Delphinium peregrinum f. ochroleucum, commonly known as the white-leaved larkspur, is a hybrid form of the larkspur genus.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers, which are typically arranged in tall, spires. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of the western United States, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its foliage is notable for its pale green to white coloration, which distinguishes it from other delphinium species.

This cultivar is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
