Diospyros armata

Diospyros armata, commonly known as the Indian persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, edible fruits. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, preferring moist, well-drained soils. Its fruit, which resembles a small persimmon, is often consumed raw or used in traditional medicine.

The tree is also valued for its hard, durable wood, which has been used historically for tool-making and construction.
