Draba oligosperma subsp. subsessilis

Draba oligosperma subsp.

subsessilis, commonly known as the narrow-leafed whitlow grass, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe. It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits.

The subspecies is distinguished by its shorter pedicels and narrower leaves compared to other Draba species. It typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, often in well-drained soils.

This plant is adapted to cold climates and is often found in open, sunny environments at high elevations.
