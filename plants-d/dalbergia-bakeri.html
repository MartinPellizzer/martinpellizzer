Dalbergia bakeri

Dalbergia bakeri, commonly known as the Baker's ebony, is a small to medium-sized tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its dark, dense wood that is highly valued for its aesthetic and structural properties. The plant has bipinnate leaves, which are divided into small, feather-like leaflets, and produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes.

Its fruits are legumes, containing several flattened seeds, and it thrives in well-drained soils with adequate sunlight.
