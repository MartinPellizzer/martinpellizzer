Digitaria decumbens

Digitaria decumbens, commonly known as flatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Americas.

It typically grows in a sprawling, mat-forming habit, with its stems lying close to the ground. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Botanically, it is characterized by its fibrous roots and its ability to form dense colonies.
