Digitalis nova

Digitalis nova, commonly known as the New England foxglove, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northeastern United States.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of hooded, tubular flowers. The plant produces showy, violet to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, fuzzy texture.

Digitalis nova contains cardiac glycosides, making it both a valuable and potentially toxic plant in botanical and medicinal studies.
