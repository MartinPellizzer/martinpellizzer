Dieffenbachia grayumiana

Dieffenbachia grayumiana, commonly known as the Greyum's Dieffenbachia, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically green with white or cream-colored veins. The plant is a member of the Araceae family, known for its unique inflorescence structure called a spadix, which is surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires moderate humidity and well-draining soil.

Due to its striking appearance, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
