Dryopteris hirta f. cubana

Dryopteris hirta f. cubana, commonly known as Cuban hairy wood fern, is a fern species native to Cuba.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its fronds that are covered with fine, silky hairs. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its rhizomes are short and thick, supporting the growth of its delicate, bipinnate leaves.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in shaded gardens.
