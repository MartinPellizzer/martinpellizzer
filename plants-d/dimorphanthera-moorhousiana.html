Dimorphanthera moorhousiana

Dimorphanthera moorhousiana, commonly known as the Moorehouse's orchid, is a rare and endemic species native to the tropical regions of Queensland, Australia.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid with delicate, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom during the warmer months. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as rainforests or under dense vegetation. Its flowers are characterized by their unique structure, featuring two distinct lip lobes and a spurred column.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by enthusiasts due to its rarity and aesthetic qualities.
