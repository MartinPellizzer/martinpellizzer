Diplazium triangulare

Diplazium triangulare, commonly known as the three-angled fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive fronds that are divided into three main segments, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its leaves are pinnately divided and have a triangular shape, contributing to its ornamental value.

Diplazium triangulare is often used in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in humid conditions.
