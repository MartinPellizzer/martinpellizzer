Dyckia ibicuiensis

Dyckia ibicuiensis, commonly known as the Ibicuiensis Dyckia, is a species of flowering plant in the bromeliad family.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hairs. The plant produces vibrant orange or red inflorescences that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect sunlight.

Dyckia ibicuiensis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and hardy nature.
