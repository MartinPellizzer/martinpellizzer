Desmodium podocarpum var. laxifolium

Desmodium podocarpum var. laxifolium, commonly known as the slender-leaved burr legume, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves with narrow, elongated leaflets and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces distinctive, spiny pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
