Davallia flexuosa

Davallia flexuosa, commonly known as the maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its delicate, arching fronds that are often covered in a fine, silky texture. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, with creeping stems that can form dense mats. Its leaves are typically bipinnate, with a distinctive, feathery appearance.

Davallia flexuosa thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often used in ornamental horticulture for its aesthetic appeal.
