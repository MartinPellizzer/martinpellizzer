Delosperma taylorii

Delosperma taylorii, commonly known as Taylor's ice plant, is a succulent perennial native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, blue-green leaves that are adapted to store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces vibrant pink to purple flowers in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It grows in a compact, mat-like form, often spreading across rocky or sandy soils.

Its drought tolerance and low maintenance make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
