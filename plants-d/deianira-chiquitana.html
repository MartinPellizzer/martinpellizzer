Deianira chiquitana

Deianira chiquitana, commonly known as the Chiquitana orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly in Bolivia and northern Argentina.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, often colorful blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, thriving in the understory of rainforests. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the rich, organic soil of its habitat.

Deianira chiquitana is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
