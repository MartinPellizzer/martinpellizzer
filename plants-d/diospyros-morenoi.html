Diospyros morenoi

Diospyros morenoi, commonly known as the Moreno persimmon, is a species of flowering plant in the Ebenaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into fleshy, orange-red fruits. The fruits are edible and have a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor, making them popular in local cuisines.

It is also valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in traditional crafts and construction.
