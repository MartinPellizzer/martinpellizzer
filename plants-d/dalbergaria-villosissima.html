Dalbergaria villosissima

Dalbergaria villosissima, commonly known as the velvet cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its pubescent (hairy) stems and leaves, which give it a soft, velvety appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is a legume, meaning it has the ability to fix nitrogen in the soil through its root nodules, making it beneficial in agricultural settings.

Dalbergaria villosissima is often used in traditional medicine and as a forage plant for livestock.
