Dalbergia pseudosissoo

Dalbergia pseudosissoo, commonly known as the Indian rosewood, is a deciduous tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The tree produces hard, dense wood that is highly valued for its aesthetic and structural properties. It typically grows in dry, tropical climates and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its slow growth and high demand, it is also considered a threatened species in some regions.
