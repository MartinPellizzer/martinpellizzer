Derris entadoides

Derris entadoides, commonly known as the African snakeroot, is a climbing shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is notable for containing rotenone, a natural insecticide and piscicide, which has been used traditionally. Its roots are often harvested for their medicinal properties, including antipyretic and analgesic effects.

However, the plant is toxic and should be handled with care.
