Duvernoia interrupta

Duvernoia interrupta, commonly known as the Chinese parasol tree, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and large, umbrella-shaped leaves that are deeply lobed. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by distinctive, spiny fruit capsules. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Duvernoia interrupta is also valued in traditional Chinese medicine for its potential health benefits.
