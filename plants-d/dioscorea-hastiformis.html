Dioscorea hastiformis

Dioscorea hastiformis, commonly known as the arrowleaf yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and tuberous roots. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds. Its tubers are sometimes used in traditional medicine and as a food source.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitats.
