Dracophyllum septentrionale

Dracophyllum septentrionale, commonly known as the northern dracophyllum, is a shrub native to the Arctic and subarctic regions of North America.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, needle-like leaves that are adapted to withstand cold and harsh environmental conditions. The plant produces tiny, red berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in tundra and alpine habitats.

Dracophyllum septentrionale plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and supporting biodiversity in its extreme environment.
