Dialium lopense

Dialium lopense, commonly known as African tamarind, is a small to medium-sized tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Its bark and leaves are valued for their medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and antiseptic effects.

The tree is also appreciated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in various soil types.
