Dichanthelium sphaerocarpon var. polyanthes

Dichanthelium sphaerocarpon var. polyanthes, commonly known as the pinkhead panicgrass, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, hairy stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, pinkish to reddish florets, which are typically wind-pollinated.

It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in coastal dunes and wetlands, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
