Dracocephalum formosum

Dracocephalum formosum, commonly known as Chinese chives or wild chives, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants such as mint and basil. The plant features square stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often fragrant. Its small, tubular flowers are typically blue to purple in color and bloom in summer.

It is often used in traditional Chinese medicine and as a culinary herb due to its mild onion-like flavor.
