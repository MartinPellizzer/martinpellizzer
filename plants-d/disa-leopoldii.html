Disa leopoldii

Disa leopoldii, commonly known as the Leopard Orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant purple and white flowers, which are arranged in dense, showy spikes. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in areas with high sunlight exposure. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to store moisture, allowing it to survive in arid conditions.

Disa leopoldii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is a popular choice in gardens and botanical collections.
