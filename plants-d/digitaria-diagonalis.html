Digitaria diagonalis

Digitaria diagonalis, commonly known as flatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along riverbanks. The plant has narrow, flat leaves that are often arranged in a dense, grass-like structure. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are usually borne on long, slender stems.

Digitaria diagonalis is characterized by its ability to thrive in waterlogged soils and is often used in wetland restoration projects.
