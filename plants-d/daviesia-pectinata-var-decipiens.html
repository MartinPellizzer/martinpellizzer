Daviesia pectinata var. decipiens

Daviesia pectinata var. decipiens, commonly known as the Decoy Daviesia, is a shrub native to the south-western region of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically appearing in spring. The plant has hairy, glandular leaves that are often arranged in pairs along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to the dry, Mediterranean climate of its native habitat.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive floral display.
