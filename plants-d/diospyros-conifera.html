Diospyros conifera

Diospyros conifera, commonly known as the Indian persimmon, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. The tree produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. Its fruits are edible, fleshy, and have a sweet, sometimes astringent taste when ripe.

The plant is valued for its timber, which is hard and durable, as well as for its ornamental qualities.
