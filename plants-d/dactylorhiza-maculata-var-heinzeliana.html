Dactylorhiza maculata var. heinzeliana

Dactylorhiza maculata var. heinzeliana, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of pale green to yellowish flowers with a distinctive purple spot near the base. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodland floors and marshy areas. It has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and can reach up to 30 cm in height.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
