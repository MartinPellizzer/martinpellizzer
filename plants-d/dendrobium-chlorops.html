Dendrobium chlorops

Dendrobium chlorops, commonly known as the green orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, cane-like stems that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that are typically arranged in racemes. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Dendrobium chlorops is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable conditions.
