Dendrosenecio meruensis

Dendrosenecio meruensis, commonly known as the Meru tree heather, is a succulent shrub native to the high-altitude regions of Kenya.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit with small, fleshy leaves. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom during the dry season. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems and leaves.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a notable species in the flora of East Africa.
