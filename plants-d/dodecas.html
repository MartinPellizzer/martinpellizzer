Dodecas

Dodecas, commonly known as the twelve-leaf plant, is a unique species characterized by its distinctive foliage.

It typically features twelve leaves arranged symmetrically around its central stem, giving it a striking visual appearance. The plant is native to arid regions and thrives in well-drained soil with minimal water. Its leaves are often a deep green, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in the summer months.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is known for its drought resistance and adaptability.
