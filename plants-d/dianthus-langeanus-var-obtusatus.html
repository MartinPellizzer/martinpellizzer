Dianthus langeanus var. obtusatus

Dianthus langeanus var. obtusatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved campion, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bright pink or red flowers that bloom in summer. The leaves are narrow and linear, often with a slightly hairy texture. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable plant for rock gardens or borders.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of garden conditions.
