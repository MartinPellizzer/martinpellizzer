Dudleya pulverulenta subsp. typica

Dudleya pulverulenta subsp.

typica, commonly known as the desert stonecress, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its compact, low-growing form and dense clusters of small, blue-green leaves.

The plant produces showy, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink to violet during the spring and summer months. It is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its fleshy leaves and stems. Dudleya pulverulenta subsp.

typica is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
