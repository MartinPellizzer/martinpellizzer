Delphinium sparsiflorum

Delphinium sparsiflorum, commonly known as the sparsely flowered larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, spindly stems that bear clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant has palmately divided, compound leaves that are often hairy and covered in fine pubescence. Delphinium sparsiflorum typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats at high elevations.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
