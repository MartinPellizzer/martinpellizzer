Diospyros subrhomboidea

Diospyros subrhomboidea, commonly known as the Indian persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves that are typically dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by orange to red fruits that are edible when fully ripe.

Its bark is smooth and light brown, often marked with shallow fissures, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.
