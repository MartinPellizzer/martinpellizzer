Dendrobium melanochlamys

Dendrobium melanochlamys, commonly known as the black-lipped dendrobium, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is an epiphytic plant that grows on trees and rocks, often in humid, tropical environments. The plant produces striking, lance-shaped leaves and bears fragrant, yellow-orange flowers with a distinctive dark lip. Its growth habit is typically sympodial, with multiple stems forming over time.

Dendrobium melanochlamys is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and adaptability to various growing conditions.
