Diospyros kaki

Diospyros kaki, commonly known as the Chinese date or Japanese persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia.

It produces oval, orange to reddish-brown fruits that are typically harvested in the fall. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptical in shape. Its flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and clustered in panicles.

The tree is valued for its edible fruit, which is rich in nutrients and has a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor.
