Dianthus serrulatus var. barbatus

Dianthus serrulatus var. barbatus, commonly known as the fringed pink or barbatus carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features compact, upright growth with clusters of small, fragrant, pink or white flowers that have fringed petals. The leaves are narrow, linear, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
