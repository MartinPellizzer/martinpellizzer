Declieuxia imbricata

Declieuxia imbricata, commonly known as the mountain bamboo, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that grow in dense clusters, forming a bamboo-like appearance. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with pale green to yellowish flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation.
