Dracocephalum verticillatum

Dracocephalum verticillatum, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that grows up to 2 feet tall, featuring slender, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

Its ornamental value and ability to support local wildlife contribute to its ecological significance.
