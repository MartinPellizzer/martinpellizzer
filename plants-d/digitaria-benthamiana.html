Digitaria benthamiana

Digitaria benthamiana, commonly known as bentham's digitaria, is a grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its erect, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Due to its adaptability, it is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and agricultural fields.
