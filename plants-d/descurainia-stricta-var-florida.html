Descurainia stricta var. florida

Descurainia stricta var. florida, commonly known as Florida stinkweed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It has alternate, linear to lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its seeds are enclosed in elongated, pod-like fruits that split open when mature.

This species is known for its strong, unpleasant odor, which is a characteristic feature of the genus Descurainia.
