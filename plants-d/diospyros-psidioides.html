Diospyros psidioides

Diospyros psidioides, commonly known as the Brazilian persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and small, clustered flowers. The tree produces edible fruits that are typically orange to reddish in color, with a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.

The bark and leaves have also been used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
