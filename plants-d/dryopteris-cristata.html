Dryopteris cristata

Dryopteris cristata, commonly known as the hairy wood fern, is a species of fern native to the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its distinctive, upright fronds that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a unique appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its fronds are bipinnate, with a complex arrangement of pinnae and pinnules.

Dryopteris cristata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in woodland gardens.
