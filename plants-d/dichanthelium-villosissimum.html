Dichanthelium villosissimum

Dichanthelium villosissimum, commonly known as the silky hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-green leaves that are densely covered with short, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, spikelet-based flowers. It typically grows in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and in wetlands.

Dichanthelium villosissimum is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
