Diasperus

Diasperus, commonly known as the African daisy, is a genus of flowering plants native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies, sunflowers, and other composite flowers. The plants typically have showy, daisy-like flowers with ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers. They are often found in grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in full sun.

Diasperus species are valued for their ornamental appeal and are sometimes used in traditional medicine.
