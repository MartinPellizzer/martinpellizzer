Delphinium grandilimbum

Delphinium grandilimbum, commonly known as the giant blue delphinium, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach heights of up to 3 meters, supporting large, showy flowers in shades of blue and purple. The plant belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and features palmate leaves with a serrated edge. Its flowers are typically arranged in dense spikes, and it blooms from late spring to early summer.

Delphinium grandilimbum is valued in gardens for its dramatic appearance and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
