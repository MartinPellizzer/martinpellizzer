Dichanthelium latifolium

Dichanthelium latifolium, commonly known as broadleaf panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and floodplain areas. The plant has slender, upright stems with narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a loose panicle with small, spikelet-like flowers.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
