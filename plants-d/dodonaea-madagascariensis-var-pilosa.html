Dodonaea madagascariensis var. pilosa

Dodonaea madagascariensis var. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy dodonaea, is a shrub native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its greyish, hairy foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces small, woody fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
