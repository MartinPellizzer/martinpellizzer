Dypsis reflexa

Dypsis reflexa, commonly known as the Madagascar palm, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, bottle-shaped trunk and pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that develop into dark blue, fleshy berries. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Dypsis reflexa is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
