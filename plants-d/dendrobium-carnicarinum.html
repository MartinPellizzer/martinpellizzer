Dendrobium carnicarinum

Dendrobium carnicarinum, commonly known as the red-veined orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant produces pendulous inflorescences with showy, fragrant flowers that are typically red or pink with contrasting yellow veins. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, adapting to its natural habitat in moist, shaded environments.

Dendrobium carnicarinum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
