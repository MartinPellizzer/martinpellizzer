Diospyros magogoana

Diospyros magogoana, commonly known as the Magogoan ebony, is a small to medium-sized deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other ebony trees, and is characterized by its dark, dense wood. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth upper surface. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by small, round fruits containing one to three seeds.

The tree is valued for its hard, dark wood and is often found in moist, forested areas.
