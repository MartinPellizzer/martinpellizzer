Digitaria recta

Digitaria recta, commonly known as blackgrass or wiregrass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny grains. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

Digitaria recta is known for its hardiness and ability to tolerate various environmental conditions.
