Dyssodia concinna

Dyssodia concinna, commonly known as the narrowleaf dyssodia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions. Its small, yellow flowers are clustered in dense heads, and the leaves are narrow and linear.

Dyssodia concinna is often used in ornamental gardening due to its hardy nature and attractive blooms.
