Dendrobium galeatum

Dendrobium galeatum, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features tall, grass-like stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, often with clusters of bright yellow flowers. The plant has a fibrous root system and thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are typically fragrant and bloom in late summer or early autumn.

Dendrobium galeatum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for its striking appearance.
