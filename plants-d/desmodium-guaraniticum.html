Desmodium guaraniticum

Desmodium guaraniticum, commonly known as Brazilian burr legume, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces small, hairy pods that contain several seeds, which are often dispersed by wind or animals. It is adapted to grow in a variety of soil types but thrives in well-drained, sunny environments.

Due to its nitrogen-fixing ability, it is sometimes used in agricultural systems to improve soil fertility.
