Drymonia erythroloma

Drymonia erythroloma, commonly known as the red-leaved drymonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, tubular flowers. The flowers, which bloom in spring, are usually pink or red and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
