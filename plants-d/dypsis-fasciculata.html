Dypsis fasciculata

Dypsis fasciculata, commonly known as the Madagascar palm, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar.

It features a thick, stem-like trunk that is often covered in a fibrous, spongy material. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that develop into orange or red berries. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
