Dyckia mello-barretoi

Dyckia mello-barretoi, commonly known as the Mello-Barreto Dyckia, is a species of flowering plant in the bromeliad family, Bromeliaceae.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of trichomes, giving them a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces inflorescences that bear small, tubular flowers, often in shades of red or orange, which attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests, where it contributes to the ecological diversity of its habitat.
