Dolichothele longimamma

Dolichothele longimamma, commonly known as the long-bodied grass spider, is a species of orb-weaving spider found in various habitats across Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated body and long, spindly legs, which aid in its agile movement. The spider's web is typically a classic orb-shaped structure, often positioned in open areas to maximize visibility for prey capture. Its coloration is usually a combination of brown and gray, providing effective camouflage in its environment.

This species plays a role in controlling insect populations, contributing to ecological balance.
