Dianthus acicularis

Dianthus acicularis, commonly known as the needlestem campion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, needle-like stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Dianthus acicularis is often found in alpine and coastal environments, where it adapts well to harsh conditions.
