Dalea nemaphyllidia

Dalea nemaphyllidia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in nutrient-poor soils.

The species is also known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
