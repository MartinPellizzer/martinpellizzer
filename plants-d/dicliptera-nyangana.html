Dicliptera nyangana

Dicliptera nyangana, commonly known as the African grass or nyangana grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves with a hairy surface. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and along riverbanks, preferring well-drained soils. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Dicliptera nyangana is often used in traditional medicine and as a forage plant for livestock.
