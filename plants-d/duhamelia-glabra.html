Duhamelia glabra

Duhamelia glabra, commonly known as the smooth duhamelia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly textured appearance. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in a flat-topped cluster, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Duhamelia glabra is often found in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and in open woodlands.
