Dichilus crassifolius

Dichilus crassifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf dichilus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its thick, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, often resembling a candlewick appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
