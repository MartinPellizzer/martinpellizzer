Duranta pseudorepens

Duranta pseudorepens, commonly known as the creeping duranta, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to blue or purple, depending on the species. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental use in gardens.

Duranta pseudorepens is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.
