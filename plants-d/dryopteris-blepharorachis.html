Dryopteris blepharorachis

Dryopteris blepharorachis, commonly known as the hooded wood fern, is a species of fern native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its fronds that are divided into segments with a distinctive hood-like structure at the base. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in rich, well-drained soils. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
