Diospyros mandenensis

Diospyros mandenensis, commonly known as the African persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to West Africa.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and large, simple leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by edible, orange-red berries. Its fruits are typically consumed fresh or used in traditional medicine.

The plant is also valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in construction and craftsmanship.
