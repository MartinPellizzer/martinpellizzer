Darea aspidioides

Darea aspidioides, commonly known as the African nettle, is a flowering plant belonging to the Urticaceae family.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which are borne on short pedicels. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Darea aspidioides is notable for its stinging hairs, which can cause skin irritation upon contact.
