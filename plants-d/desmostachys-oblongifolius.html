Desmostachys oblongifolius

Desmostachys oblongifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf desmostachys, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, elongated leaves that are often arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which bloom during the wet season. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Due to its unique leaf morphology and habitat preferences, it is of interest to botanists studying plant adaptation in humid environments.
