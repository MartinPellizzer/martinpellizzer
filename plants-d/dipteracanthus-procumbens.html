Dipteracanthus procumbens

Dipteracanthus procumbens, commonly known as the prostrate dipteracanthus, is a flowering plant native to specific regions in Southeast Asia.

It is a low-growing herbaceous plant with slender, creeping stems that often root at the nodes. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in clusters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
