Didymoglossum caluffii

Didymoglossum caluffii, commonly known as the African hairgrass, is a low-growing perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along water margins.

Didymoglossum caluffii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
