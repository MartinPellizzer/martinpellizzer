Daemonorops pleioclada

Daemonorops pleioclada, commonly known as the giant cycad, is a member of the Cycadaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a large, evergreen plant that can grow up to 10 meters in height, characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and large, pinnate leaves. The plant produces cones rather than flowers, with male and female cones occurring on separate plants. Its slow growth rate and long lifespan make it a valuable species in both horticulture and ecological studies.

The plant's unique morphology and ecological role highlight its significance in tropical ecosystems.
