Disa longifolia

Disa longifolia, commonly known as the long-flowered disa, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of showy, violet or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are linear and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its elegant appearance.

Disa longifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil.
