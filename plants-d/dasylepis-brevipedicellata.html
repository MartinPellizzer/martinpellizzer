Dasylepis brevipedicellata

Dasylepis brevipedicellata, commonly known as the narrow-pedicelled papyrus, is a species of sedge native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense clusters. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy habitats, preferring moist, well-drained soils. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense stands.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
