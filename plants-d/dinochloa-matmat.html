Dinochloa matmat

Dinochloa matmat, commonly known as the matmat grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often form dense clumps. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly hairy texture.

Dinochloa matmat is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
