Diora cajamarcaensis

Diora cajamarcaensis, commonly known as the Peruvian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of Peru.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in cool and temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

Diora cajamarcaensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic beauty.
