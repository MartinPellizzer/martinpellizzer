Diplazium wangii

Diplazium wangii, commonly known as the Wangi's shield fern, is a member of the Davalliaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial fern that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. The plant features a short, creeping rhizome with fiddlehead-like fronds that unfurl into a bipinnate structure. Its leaves are characterized by their dark green color and serrated margins.

Diplazium wangii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded garden settings.
