Dendrocalamus forbesii

Dendrocalamus forbesii, commonly known as the Forbes' bamboo, is a tall, slender grass-like plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its robust, hollow stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant is widely cultivated for its strong, durable culms, which are used in construction and as raw material for paper and bamboo products. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth, waxy surface that helps reduce water loss in its tropical habitat.

Dendrocalamus forbesii is also valued for its ecological role in preventing soil erosion and supporting biodiversity in forest ecosystems.
