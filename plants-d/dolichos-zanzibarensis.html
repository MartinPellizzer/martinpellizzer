Dolichos zanzibarensis

Dolichos zanzibarensis, commonly known as the Zanzibar dolichos, is a climbing legume native to the coastal regions of East Africa.

It produces small, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by elongated, bean-like pods. The plant has compound leaves with trifoliate or pinnate arrangements, and its stems are often hairy. It thrives in tropical climates and is adapted to grow in sandy or loamy soils.

The species is of interest for its potential in traditional medicine and as a forage plant.
