Davallia pilosula

Davallia pilosula, commonly known as the hairy Davallia, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are covered in fine, hairy structures, giving it a unique texture. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are often covered in a dense layer of rhizome scales, which contribute to its distinctive appearance.

Davallia pilosula is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ease of care in indoor settings.
