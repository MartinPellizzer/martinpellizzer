Deyeuxia arundinacea var. ligulata

Deyeuxia arundinacea var. ligulata, commonly known as narrowleaf false panicgrass, is a grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, wet meadows, and along stream banks. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it is often found in association with other wetland vegetation.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
