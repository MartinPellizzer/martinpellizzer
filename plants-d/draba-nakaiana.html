Draba nakaiana

Draba nakaiana, commonly known as the Nakaiana whitlow grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a small, herbaceous annual that typically grows in alpine and subalpine regions. The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in early spring. Draba nakaiana is native to the Pacific Northwest of North America, including parts of Washington and Oregon.

It is adapted to cold climates and thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.
