Davallia chusana

Davallia chusana, commonly known as the golden polypody, is a fern native to Southeast Asia.

It features delicate, feather-like fronds that are typically light green to golden in color. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories. Its rhizomes are creeping and produce clusters of small, spore-bearing leaves.

Davallia chusana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is popular in horticultural settings.
