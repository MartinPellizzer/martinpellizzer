Draba subfladnizensis

Draba subfladnizensis, commonly known as the Fladnitz dwarf-crambe, is a small flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to Central Asia and is characterized by its compact growth habit and bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
