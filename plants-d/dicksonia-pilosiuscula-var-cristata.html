Dicksonia pilosiuscula var. cristata

Dicksonia pilosiuscula var. cristata, commonly known as the cristate tree fern, is a variant of the tree fern species native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of feathery, fronds that grow in a rosette pattern. The fronds are typically dark green and have a soft, velvety texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forested areas.

It plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and shelter for various small organisms.
