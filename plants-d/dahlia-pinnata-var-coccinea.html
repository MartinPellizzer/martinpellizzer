Dahlia pinnata var. coccinea

Dahlia pinnata var. coccinea, commonly known as the bloodleaf dahlia, is a striking ornamental plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its vibrant, deep red to maroon flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant features sturdy, upright stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often a striking green with red veins. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, requiring regular watering and moderate fertilization.

This variety is prized for its dramatic floral display and is commonly cultivated in gardens and floral arrangements.
