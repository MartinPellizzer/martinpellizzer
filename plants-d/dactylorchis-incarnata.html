Dactylorchis incarnata

Dactylorchis incarnata, commonly known as the pink lady's tresses, is a terrestrial orchid native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, hair-like inflorescence that resembles a tresses of hair, with pale pink to white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has a rhizomatous root system and produces small, oval-shaped leaves.

Dactylorchis incarnata is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its delicate and ephemeral beauty.
