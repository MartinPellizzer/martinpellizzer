Datura metel var. quinquecuspidata

Datura metel var. quinquecuspidata, commonly known as the Chinese devil's trumpet, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy, funnel-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale purple in color. The plant produces spiny, egg-shaped fruits that contain numerous seeds. It is a member of the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is known for its potent alkaloids, which can be toxic to humans and animals.
