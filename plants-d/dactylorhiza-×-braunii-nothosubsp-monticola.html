Dactylorhiza × braunii nothosubsp. monticola

Dactylorhiza × braunii nothosubsp.

monticola, commonly known as the mountain marsh orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of pale green to brownish flowers.

The plant typically grows in wet meadows, bogs, and marshy areas, thriving in moist, acidic soils. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, and it produces a single spike of flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

This orchid is noted for its ecological importance in supporting local insect populations and its role in wetland ecosystems.
