Dianthus caryophyllus var. volubilitanus

Dianthus caryophyllus var. volubilitanus, commonly known as the climbing carnation, is a variant of the carnation plant characterized by its trailing growth habit.

It features showy, double flowers with a mix of pink, red, and white petals, often with a strong, sweet fragrance. The plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. It is often used in hanging baskets and as a ground cover due to its ability to spread along the ground.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
