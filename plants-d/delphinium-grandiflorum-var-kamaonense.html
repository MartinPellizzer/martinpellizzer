Delphinium grandiflorum var. kamaonense

Delphinium grandiflorum var. kamaonense, commonly known as the Kamaon delphinium, is a tall, herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features striking blue to violet spires of flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to both horticultural collections and ecological landscapes.
