Dyckia secundifolia

Dyckia secundifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved dyckia, is a bromeliad native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-forming structure and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a whitish bloom. The plant produces inflorescences of small, clustered white or pink flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in sunny, well-drained environments and is relatively drought-tolerant.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
