Dianthus broteri var. brachyphyllus

Dianthus broteri var. brachyphyllus, commonly known as the dwarf-flowered carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink and white. The leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, while the stems are upright and somewhat hairy. This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or containers.
