Dimesia

Dimesia, commonly known as the bluebell or bluebell vine, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Apocynaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of the Americas and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth texture and a mildly toxic sap containing cardiac glycosides. Dimesia thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found climbing or trailing along vegetation.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and greenhouses.
