Dalea compacta var. compacta

Dalea compacta var. compacta, commonly known as the compact prairie clover, is a perennial herb native to the central United States.

It is characterized by its dense, low-growing habit and produces clusters of small, pinkish to reddish flowers. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval, hairy leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Dalea compacta var. compacta is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
