Delphinium peregrinum var. subvelutinum

Delphinium peregrinum var. subvelutinum, commonly known as the velvetleaf larkspur, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that bear clusters of striking blue to violet flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, velvety hair, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine and subalpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and native plant landscapes.
