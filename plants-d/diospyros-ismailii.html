Diospyros ismailii

Diospyros ismailii, commonly known as the Kashmir date tree, is a deciduous tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and large, palmately lobed leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by edible, sweet, and sticky fruits similar to dates. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its fruit and ornamental value.

The plant is also valued for its hard, heavy wood, which is used in traditional crafts and furniture making.
