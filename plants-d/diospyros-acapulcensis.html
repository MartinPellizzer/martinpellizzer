Diospyros acapulcensis

Diospyros acapulcensis, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a species of flowering plant in the Ebenaceae family.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically ovate to elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by edible berries that turn from yellow to orange-red when mature. The fruit has a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor and is often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications.

It is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its unique appearance and fruit.
