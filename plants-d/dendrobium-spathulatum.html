Dendrobium spathulatum

Dendrobium spathulatum, commonly known as the broad-leafed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with elongated, cylindrical pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and orange, often with a distinctive yellow or orange throat. Its leaves are broad and fleshy, adapted to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

It is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation.
