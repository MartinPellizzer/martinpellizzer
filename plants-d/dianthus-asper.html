Dianthus asper

Dianthus asper, commonly known as the rough pinks, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, pink or white flowers. The plant features hairy stems and leaves, which contribute to its common name. Its blooms typically appear in late spring to early summer, adding ornamental value to gardens.

Dianthus asper is often cultivated for its delicate appearance and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
