Doryopteris juergensii

Doryopteris juergensii, commonly known as the Juan Fernández fern, is a unique species native to the Juan Fernández Islands in the southern Pacific Ocean.

It is a small, terrestrial fern that typically grows in shaded, moist environments. The plant has delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, a characteristic feature of many ferns.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
