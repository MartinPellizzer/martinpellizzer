Drimia excelsa

Drimia excelsa, commonly known as the giant bulbous plant, is a striking member of the Asparagaceae family native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, fleshy, white to pale yellow bulbs that can grow up to 30 cm in diameter. The plant produces tall, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of white or pale blue flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
