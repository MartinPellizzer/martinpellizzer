Draba alpina f. oxycarpa

Draba alpina f. oxycarpa, commonly known as the alpine bellflower, is a flowering plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in cold, high-altitude environments. Its leaves are basal, rosette-like, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are pale yellow to white.

This variety is distinguished by its slightly elongated, pointed petals, giving it a unique appearance among Draba species.
