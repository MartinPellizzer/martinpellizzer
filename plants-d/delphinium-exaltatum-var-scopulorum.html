Delphinium exaltatum var. scopulorum

Delphinium exaltatum var. scopulorum, commonly known as the rock delphinium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces showy, spurred flowers in shades of blue, violet, or white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several pairs of lance-shaped leaflets, and are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

This variety thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.
