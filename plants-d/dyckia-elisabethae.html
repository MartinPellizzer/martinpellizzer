Dyckia elisabethae

Dyckia elisabethae, commonly known as the Elisabetha Dyckia, is a species of bromeliad native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its compact rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hair. The plant produces striking, sword-shaped inflorescences that bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Dyckia elisabethae is a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
