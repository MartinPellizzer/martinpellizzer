Dendropemon oblanceolatus

Dendropemon oblanceolatus, commonly known as the broadleaf tree, is a species native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters in height, characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by the development of woody, cylindrical fruits. Its leaves are typically arranged alternately along the branches, and the bark is smooth with a light grayish color.

This plant is often found in moist, tropical or subtropical environments.
