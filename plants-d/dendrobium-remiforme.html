Dendrobium remiforme

Dendrobium remiforme, commonly known as the orchid with the shape of a leaf, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features long, wiry stems that can grow up to several meters in length, often branching out to form a bushy appearance. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers with a distinctive lip that is often curled or notched. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright indirect light and requires well-draining potting media.

Dendrobium remiforme is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for its unique floral display.
