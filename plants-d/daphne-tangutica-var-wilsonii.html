Daphne tangutica var. wilsonii

Daphne tangutica var. wilsonii, commonly known as Wilson's daphne, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of western China.

It is characterized by its aromatic, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring, typically in shades of pink and white. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and have a serrated margin.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively hardy in temperate climates.
