Daviesia benthamii subsp. humilis

Daviesia benthamii subsp.

humilis, commonly known as the slender Daviesia, is a low-growing shrub native to southern Australia. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its narrow, often hairy stems and small, pea-like flowers.

The plant typically produces yellow or orange flowers in late spring to early summer, attracting nectar-feeding insects. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of multiple small, oval leaflets, and it is adapted to grow in nutrient-poor soils.

This subspecies is often found in heathlands and rocky areas, showcasing its resilience to harsh environmental conditions.
