Daphne × sillingeri

Daphne × sillingeri, commonly known as the winter daphne, is a hybrid shrub native to Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of fragrant, waxy flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are typically red or orange in color. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Daphne × sillingeri is also known for its toxicity, as all parts of the plant contain toxic compounds that can be harmful if ingested.
