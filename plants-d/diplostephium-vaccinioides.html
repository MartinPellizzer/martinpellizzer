Diplostephium vaccinioides

Diplostephium vaccinioides, commonly known as the swamp hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist to swampy habitats. The plant features showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and often have a waxy surface.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
