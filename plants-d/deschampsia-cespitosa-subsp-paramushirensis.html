Deschampsia cespitosa subsp. paramushirensis

Deschampsia cespitosa subsp.

paramushirensis, commonly known as the Paramushire grass, is a perennial grass species native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, cylindrical leaves that are typically bluish-green in color.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous narrow, awned florets. It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes, wet meadows, and along stream banks.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates and birds.
