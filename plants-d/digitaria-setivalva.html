Digitaria setivalva

Digitaria setivalva, commonly known as the velvetgrass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with a soft, velvety texture on the underside. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along waterways.

Due to its adaptability, Digitaria setivalva is both an important component of wetland ecosystems and a potential weed in agricultural settings.
