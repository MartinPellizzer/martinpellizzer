Delphinium chitralense

Delphinium chitralense, commonly known as the Chitral larkspur, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers in racemes, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are compound, with serrated edges, and it thrives in cool, moist environments at high altitudes.

Delphinium chitralense is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens in temperate climates.
