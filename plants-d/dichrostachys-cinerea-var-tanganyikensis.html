Dichrostachys cinerea var. tanganyikensis

Dichrostachys cinerea var. tanganyikensis, commonly known as the African burr bush, is a shrub or small tree native to East Africa.

It is characterized by its bipinnate leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers followed by distinctive, spiny pods. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it resilient to drought conditions. It is often found in savannas and woodland areas, contributing to soil stabilization.

Its seeds are dispersed by animals and wind, facilitating its spread across various habitats.
