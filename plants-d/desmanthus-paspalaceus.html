Desmanthus paspalaceus

Desmanthus paspalaceus, commonly known as the silverleaf desmanthus, is a perennial legume native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has a low-growing habit with trifoliate leaves and is often found in sandy or rocky soils. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in restoration projects due to its drought tolerance.

Additionally, it is a valuable forage plant for livestock in arid environments.
