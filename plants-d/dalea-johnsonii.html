Dalea johnsonii

Dalea johnsonii, commonly known as Johnson's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow or orange flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has small, compound leaves that help reduce water loss. Its root system forms symbiotic relationships with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, enhancing soil fertility.

Dalea johnsonii is often found in desert scrub and chaparral ecosystems.
