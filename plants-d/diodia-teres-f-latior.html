Diodia teres f. latior

Diodia teres f. latior, commonly known as broadleaf diodia, is a variant of the species Diodia teres, belonging to the family Apiaceae.

It is characterized by its broader leaves compared to the typical form, with a distinctive appearance that includes finely divided foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas or along roadsides. It produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in umbels, which are typical of the Apiaceae family.

This plant is often used in native landscaping due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
