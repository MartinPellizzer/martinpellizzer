Dioscorea pentaphylla var. linnaei

Dioscorea pentaphylla var. linnaei, commonly known as the Linnaean yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are hairy and twining.

The tubers of this species are valued for their nutritional content and have been utilized in various cultural and medicinal practices.
