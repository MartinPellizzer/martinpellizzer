Dendrochilum abbreviatum

Dendrochilum abbreviatum, commonly known as the short-stemmed tree orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and possesses a pseudobulb that supports its foliage and flowers. The plant typically produces a single spike bearing several small, fragrant flowers with a distinctive coloration. Its leaves are usually arranged in a rosette pattern, and the roots are adapted for nutrient absorption in its natural habitat.

Dendrochilum abbreviatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
