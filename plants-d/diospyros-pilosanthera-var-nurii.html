Diospyros pilosanthera var. nurii

Diospyros pilosanthera var. nurii, commonly known as the Chinese persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by orange to red, fleshy fruits. Its fruit has a sweet, honey-like flavor when fully ripe and is often consumed fresh or used in traditional Chinese medicine.

The plant is valued for its ornamental value and edible fruit, making it a popular species in horticulture.
