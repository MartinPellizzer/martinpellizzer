Dodonaea dombeyana

Dodonaea dombeyana, commonly known as the velvetleaf hopseed, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and trifoliate leaves with soft, velvety undersides. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by distinctive three-lobed, woody pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

Dodonaea dombeyana is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage.
