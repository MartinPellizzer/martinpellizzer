Dillenia papuana

Dillenia papuana, commonly known as the papuan dillenia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Papua and New Guinea.

It belongs to the family Dilleniaceae and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in clusters. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its fruit is a woody, spiky capsule that splits open to release seeds. Dillenia papuana is often found in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and along stream banks.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, supporting various insect and bird species.
