Diospyros bemarivensis

Diospyros bemarivensis, commonly known as the Madagascar persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes ebony and persimmon trees. The plant is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and elliptical, glossy leaves that are dark green on the upper surface. Its fruit is a small, round berry that turns from green to yellow or red when mature.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has potential uses in traditional medicine and as a source of timber.
