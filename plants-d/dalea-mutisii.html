Dalea mutisii

Dalea mutisii, commonly known as the Mexican prairie clover, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in open, dry habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has small, trifoliate leaves and produces bright yellow or orange flowers that bloom in late spring.

Dalea mutisii is adapted to arid environments and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native range.
