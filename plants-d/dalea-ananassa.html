Dalea ananassa

Dalea ananassa, commonly known as the Ananassa milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, often arranged in clusters. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open plains.

Dalea ananassa is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to ecosystem health.
