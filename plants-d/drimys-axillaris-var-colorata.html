Drimys axillaris var. colorata

Drimys axillaris var. colorata, commonly known as the colorful drimys, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as temperate rainforests. Its fruit is a small, red berry that matures in late summer.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with high humidity and partial shade.
