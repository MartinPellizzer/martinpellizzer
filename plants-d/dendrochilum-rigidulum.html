Dendrochilum rigidulum

Dendrochilum rigidulum, commonly known as the rigid lip orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers with a distinctive lip that is often stiff and rigid, giving it its common name. It typically blooms in the summer months and thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
