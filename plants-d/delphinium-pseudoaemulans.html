Delphinium pseudoaemulans

Delphinium pseudoaemulans, commonly known as the Rocky Mountain larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers in racemes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinctive serrated margin, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Delphinium pseudoaemulans thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, loamy soils.
