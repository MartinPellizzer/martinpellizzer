Dendrocalamus barbatus

Dendrocalamus barbatus, commonly known as the bamboo palm, is a species of giant bamboo native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach heights of up to 20 meters. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in a fan-like pattern at the top of the stem. Its rhizomes are thick and fibrous, contributing to its structural strength.

Dendrocalamus barbatus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional crafts and construction.
