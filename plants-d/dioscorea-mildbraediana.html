Dioscorea mildbraediana

Dioscorea mildbraediana, commonly known as the mildbraedian yam, is a member of the Dioscoreaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a perennial vine characterized by its climbing habit and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces tuberous roots that are the primary part used, though they are not commonly cultivated for food. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and less-studied species within its genus.
