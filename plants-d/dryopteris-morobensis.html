Dryopteris morobensis

Dryopteris morobensis, commonly known as the Morobe water fern, is a species of fern native to the tropical regions of the Pacific, particularly in New Guinea and the Solomon Islands.

It is characterized by its delicate, frond-like structures that are typically bipinnate, with a distinctive appearance that resembles a water lily. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in swampy areas or along the edges of water bodies. Its rhizomes are creeping and produce numerous roots, allowing it to spread and colonize wetland habitats effectively.

The leaves are usually light green and have a soft texture, contributing to its aesthetic appeal in aquatic settings.
