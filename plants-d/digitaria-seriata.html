Digitaria seriata

Digitaria seriata, commonly known as slender fingergrass, is a species of grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in both wet and dry conditions.

Digitaria seriata is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its rapid growth and adaptability.
