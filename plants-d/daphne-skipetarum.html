Daphne skipetarum

Daphne skipetarum, commonly known as the Greek daphne, is a shrub native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, woody capsules as fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and aromatic qualities, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
