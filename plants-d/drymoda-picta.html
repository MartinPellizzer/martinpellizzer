Drymoda picta

Drymoda picta, commonly known as the painted bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple, often with intricate patterns. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are usually hairy, providing a textured appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens.

Drymoda picta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate in mild climates.
