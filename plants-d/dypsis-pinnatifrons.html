Dypsis pinnatifrons

Dypsis pinnatifrons, commonly known as the Madagascar palm, is a member of the Arecaceae family.

It is native to Madagascar and is characterized by its distinctive, clustered growth habit and thick, fleshy stems. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, giving it a unique appearance. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or white, followed by ornamental, feather-like fruits.

Dypsis pinnatifrons is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its tolerance of low light and ability to thrive in dry conditions.
