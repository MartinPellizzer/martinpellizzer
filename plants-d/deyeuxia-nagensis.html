Deyeuxia nagensis

Deyeuxia nagensis, commonly known as the Nagi grass, is a perennial herb native to the grasslands of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats and has narrow, linear leaves with a rough texture. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, two-ranked spikelets.

Deyeuxia nagensis is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for livestock.
