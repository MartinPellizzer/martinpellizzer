Doronicum austriacum

Doronicum austriacum, commonly known as the Austrian leopard's bane, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Alps.

It features upright, branched stems that bear lance-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, moist soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Doronicum austriacum is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
