Dianthus collincola

Dianthus collincola, commonly known as the mountain carnation, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, brightly colored flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to cooler, high-altitude environments. Its leaves are linear and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are usually erect and branching.

Dianthus collincola is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
