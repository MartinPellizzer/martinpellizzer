Delphinium caucasicum var. chinense

Delphinium caucasicum var. chinense, commonly known as the Chinese larkspur, is a flowering plant native to regions of China and the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens, though it contains toxic alkaloids that can be harmful to livestock and humans.
