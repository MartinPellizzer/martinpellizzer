Dedea resinosa

Dedea resinosa, commonly known as the resinous dedea, is a shrub native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, resinous leaves and stems, which emit a strong, distinctive scent. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, attracting various pollinators. Its resin has been historically used by indigenous communities for medicinal and ritual purposes.

The species is valued for its unique chemical composition and ecological role in its native habitat.
