Dahlia scapigeroides

Dahlia scapigeroides, commonly known as the Mexican dahlia, is a herbaceous perennial native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, single or double flowers that come in a variety of colors. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring upright stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early fall, adding vibrant color to gardens.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its striking floral display.
