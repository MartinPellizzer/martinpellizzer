Dryas octopetala subsp. ajanensis

Dryas octopetala subsp.

ajanensis, commonly known as the Ajanensis Dryas, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the alpine and subalpine regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit.

The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are leathery, evergreen, and have a distinctive, rounded shape.

This subspecies is adapted to harsh, cold environments and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes.
