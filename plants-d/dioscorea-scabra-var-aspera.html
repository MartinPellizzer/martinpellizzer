Dioscorea scabra var. aspera

Dioscorea scabra var. aspera, commonly known as rough yam, is a variant of the yam species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a climbing vine with hairy, often woody stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by tuberous roots that are the primary part used. Its tubers are characterized by their rough, scaly skin and starchy interior.

Dioscorea scabra var. aspera is valued for its edible tubers and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
