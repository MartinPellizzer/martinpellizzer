Dryopteris lithophylla

Dryopteris lithophylla, commonly known as the cliff brake, is a species of fern native to the western United States.

It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found growing on cliffs or in crevices. The plant has dark green, leathery fronds that are divided into narrow, serrated segments. It is adapted to arid environments and can tolerate drought conditions.

Its unique growth habit and hardy nature make it a popular choice for rock gardens and landscaping in dry climates.
