Diospyros betamponensis

Diospyros betamponensis, commonly known as the Betampona persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves that are dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by orange-red, fleshy berries that are edible.

Its fruit is valued for its sweet, astringent flavor and is often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
