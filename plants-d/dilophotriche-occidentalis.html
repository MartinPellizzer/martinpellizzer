Dilophotriche occidentalis

Dilophotriche occidentalis, commonly known as the western fringed gentian, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a hairy texture.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
