Desmodium ciliosum

Desmodium ciliosum, commonly known as the silky desmodium, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, often found in grasslands and disturbed soils. Its small, pink or purple flowers are clustered in racemes, and the leaves have a distinctive silky texture due to fine hairs.

Desmodium ciliosum is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in agricultural settings.
