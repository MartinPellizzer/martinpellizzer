Draba chubutensis

Draba chubutensis, commonly known as the Chubut sandflower, is a small flowering plant native to the arid regions of Argentina.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant is characterized by its low-growing habit, with dense clusters of tiny, white or pale yellow flowers. It has adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments through features such as hairy leaves and a deep root system.

Draba chubutensis plays an important role in stabilizing sandy soils and supporting local ecosystems.
