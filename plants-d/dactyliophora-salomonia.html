Dactyliophora salomonia

Dactyliophora salomonia, commonly known as the Salomonia orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that often feature vibrant colors. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are adapted for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Dactyliophora salomonia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
