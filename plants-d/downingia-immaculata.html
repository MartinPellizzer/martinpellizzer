Downingia immaculata

Downingia immaculata, commonly known as the Sierra snowplant, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its rosette of fleshy, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pink flowers. The plant thrives in high-altitude environments, often found in meadows and rocky areas above timberline. It is adapted to cold climates and has a unique ability to store water in its leaves.

Downingia immaculata is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used by indigenous peoples for various purposes.
