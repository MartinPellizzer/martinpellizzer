Desmodium laburnifolium

Desmodium laburnifolium, commonly known as the goldenhead or yellow burr vetch, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a legume, belonging to the Fabaceae family, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, hairy pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions and full sun.

Desmodium laburnifolium is often used in ecological restoration due to its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
