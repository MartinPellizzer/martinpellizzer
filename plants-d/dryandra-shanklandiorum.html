Dryandra shanklandiorum

Dryandra shanklandiorum, commonly known as the Swan River dryandra, is a striking evergreen shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces striking, bottle-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange, which are borne in dense clusters along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to the dry, Mediterranean climate of its native region.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is a popular choice for cultivation in arid gardens.
