Dicoma poggei

Dicoma poggei, commonly known as the Cape snowbush, is a succulent shrub native to the Western Cape region of South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and fleshy, bluish-green leaves that are covered in a fine layer of white wax. The plant produces small, star-shaped white or pale pink flowers during the winter months, adding ornamental value to its appearance. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid conditions, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Dicoma poggei is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
