Dianthus sancarii

Dianthus sancarii, commonly known as the San Carlo pink, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant pink to red flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces flowers in clusters at the tips of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Dianthus sancarii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its colorful blooms.
