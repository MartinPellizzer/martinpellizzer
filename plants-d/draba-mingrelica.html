Draba mingrelica

Draba mingrelica, commonly known as the Mingrelian rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. This species is characterized by its low-growing habit, with small, basal leaves and clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. It typically thrives in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils.

Draba mingrelica is often found in mountainous areas of Georgia and surrounding regions.
