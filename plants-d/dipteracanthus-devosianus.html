Dipteracanthus devosianus

Dipteracanthus devosianus, commonly known as the Devosian flameflower, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red flowers that resemble flames, which are adapted to attract specific pollinators. The plant typically grows in tropical rainforests, thriving in humid and shaded environments. Its leaves are opposite and ovate, with a smooth texture and dark green color.

Dipteracanthus devosianus is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
