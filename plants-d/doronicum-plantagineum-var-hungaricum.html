Doronicum plantagineum var. hungaricum

Doronicum plantagineum var. hungaricum, commonly known as the Hungarian leopard's bane, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy yellow flowers with a distinctive dark central disc. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, hairy texture, while its stems are erect and somewhat woody at the base.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
