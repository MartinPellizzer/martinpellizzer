Dichapetalum gilletii

Dichapetalum gilletii, commonly known as the African bellflower, is a striking perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a distinctive purple or red throat. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Dichapetalum gilletii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
