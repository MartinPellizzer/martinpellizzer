Dillenia neoebudica

Dillenia neoebudica, commonly known as the African dillenia, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Dilleniaceae and is characterized by its large, showy, pink or red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are often hairy and have a rough texture. It produces woody fruits that contain several seeds, and it is often found in moist, shaded habitats.

Dillenia neoebudica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
