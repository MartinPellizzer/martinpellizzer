Delphinium albocoeruleum var. latilobum

Delphinium albocoeruleum var. latilobum, commonly known as the blue delphinium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces showy, blue to sky-blue flowers in racemes, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
