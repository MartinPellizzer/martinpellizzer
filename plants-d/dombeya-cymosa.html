Dombeya cymosa

Dombeya cymosa, commonly known as the clustered dombeya, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves that are elliptical in shape. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Dombeya cymosa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
