Desmodium axillare f. robustius

Desmodium axillare f. robustius, commonly known as the robust lespedeza, is a perennial legume native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically heart-shaped and have a hairy texture. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, often in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in erosion control due to its dense root system.

The species is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it beneficial in agricultural settings.
