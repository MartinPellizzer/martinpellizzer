Dichodon Davuricus

Davurian dichondra, scientifically known as Dichodon davuricus, is a hardy, low-growing groundcover native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia. This resilient plant thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and slopes. Its dense, carpet-like foliage provides excellent erosion control while adding a lush, green texture to landscapes. With its ability to tolerate drought and poor soil conditions, it is a favored plant among gardeners seeking easy-to-maintain ground cover. Its compact growth habit and attractive appearance make it both functional and aesthetically pleasing in a variety of garden settings.
