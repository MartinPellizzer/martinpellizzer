Dracophyllum uniflorum var. frondosum

Dracophyllum uniflorum var. frondosum, commonly known as the fronded dracophyllum, is a shrub native to the humid subtropical regions of eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and compact growth habit. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically narrow and evergreen, providing year-round structure.

This species is well adapted to acidic soils and is often found in coastal heathlands and dry rainforests.
