Derris elliptica

Derris elliptica, commonly known as the African snakeroot, is a flowering plant native to Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters. Its roots are known for their toxic properties and have been used in traditional medicine.

The plant is also notable for its role in the production of rotenone, a natural insecticide.
