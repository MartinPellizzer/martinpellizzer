Dupatya imbricata

Dupatya imbricata, commonly known as the mountain orchid, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, often fragrant blooms. The plant typically grows in rocky or forested areas, thriving in cool and moist environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, Dupatya imbricata is often sought after by horticulturists and conservationists alike.
