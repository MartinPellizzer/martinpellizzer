Duranta repens

Duranta repens, commonly known as the common duranta, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a trailing or shrub-like plant that produces clusters of blue or white flowers, which are followed by bright red berries. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it popular in landscaping. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a rough texture, while its stems are often hairy.

Duranta repens is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in hanging baskets or as a ground cover.
