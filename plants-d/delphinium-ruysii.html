Delphinium ruysii

Delphinium ruysii, commonly known as the Ruyssen's larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces clusters of blue to purple flowers, which are arranged in racemes and bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Delphinium ruysii is a perennial herb that thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.
