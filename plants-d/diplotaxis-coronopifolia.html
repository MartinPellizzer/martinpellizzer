Diplotaxis coronopifolia

Diplotaxis coronopifolia, commonly known as the crown vetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in sunny conditions. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow flowers in late spring. Its root system is deep and fibrous, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for erosion control.

Diplotaxis coronopifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
