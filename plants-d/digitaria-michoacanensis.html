Digitaria michoacanensis

Digitaria michoacanensis, commonly known as the Michoacan digitgrass, is a perennial grass native to Mexico.

It typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in disturbed soils and open habitats. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in arid conditions, making it a resilient species.

Botanically, it belongs to the Poaceae family and is notable for its ecological adaptability.
