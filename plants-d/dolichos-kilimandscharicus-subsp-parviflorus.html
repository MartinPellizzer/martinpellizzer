Dolichos kilimandscharicus subsp. parviflorus

Dolichos kilimandscharicus subsp.

parviflorus, commonly known as the small-flowered climbing bean, is a leguminous plant native to the highlands of East Africa. It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink.

The plant produces elongated, bean-like pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Due to its ornamental value and potential for use in local agriculture, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
