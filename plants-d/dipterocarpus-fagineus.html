Dipterocarpus fagineus

Dipterocarpus fagineus, commonly known as the black dipterocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, dark brown to black bark and produces heavy, woody fruits that contain winged seeds. The plant is part of the Dipterocarpaceae family and is known for its ecological importance in rainforest ecosystems. It typically grows in lowland forests and plays a key role in providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.

The tree is also valued for its durable timber, which has been historically used in construction and furniture.
