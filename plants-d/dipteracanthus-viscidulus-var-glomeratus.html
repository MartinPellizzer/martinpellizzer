Dipteracanthus viscidulus var. glomeratus

Dipteracanthus viscidulus var. glomeratus, commonly known as the sticky globular dipteracanthus, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, glandular hairs that give its stems and leaves a sticky texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are usually narrow and elongated, with a slightly waxy surface.

This variant is noted for its unique morphology and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its distinctive appearance.
