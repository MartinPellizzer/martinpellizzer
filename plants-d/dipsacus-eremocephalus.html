Dipsacus eremocephalus

Dipsacus eremocephalus, commonly known as the desert gothic or hairy goat's beard, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of southern Europe and North Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and striking, spiky, purplish-red flower heads that appear in late summer. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs, helping to reduce water loss in its harsh environment. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Dipsacus eremocephalus is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to survive in conditions of extreme drought and heat.
