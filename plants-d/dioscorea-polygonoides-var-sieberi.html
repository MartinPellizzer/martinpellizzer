Dioscorea polygonoides var. sieberi

Dioscorea polygonoides var. sieberi, commonly known as the Sieber's yam, is a climbing perennial vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary edible part of the plant. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by oval, yellowish fruits. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a rough texture and are often used in traditional medicine.

The species is valued for both its nutritional content and medicinal properties.
