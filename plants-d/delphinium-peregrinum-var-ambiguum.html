Delphinium peregrinum var. ambiguum

Delphinium peregrinum var. ambiguum, commonly known as the alpine larkspur, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height, supporting clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and alpine landscapes.
