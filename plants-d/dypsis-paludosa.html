Dypsis paludosa

Dypsis paludosa, commonly known as the swamp bamboo or swamp areca, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its clustering growth habit and slender, bamboo-like stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white in color. Dypsis paludosa thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in swampy or wetland areas.

Its ornamental value is limited due to its modest appearance, but it plays a role in the ecological balance of its native habitat.
