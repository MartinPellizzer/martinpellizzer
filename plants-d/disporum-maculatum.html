Disporum maculatum

Disporum maculatum, commonly known as the spotted lily or purple lily, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Disporum maculatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in native habitats.
