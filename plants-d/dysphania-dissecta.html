Dysphania dissecta

Dysphania dissecta, commonly known as redroot pigweed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its finely dissected, fern-like leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, three-angled seeds. Dysphania dissecta thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

It is considered a weed but has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
