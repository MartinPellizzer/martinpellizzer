Dioscorea schlechteri

Dioscorea schlechteri, commonly known as the Chinese yam, is a climbing vine belonging to the family Dioscoreaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellowish flowers and cylindrical, tuberous roots that are used in traditional medicine. Its tubers are also valued for their nutritional content and are used in various culinary applications.

Dioscorea schlechteri is known for its adaptability to different soil types and its ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.
