Draba chillanensis

Draba chillanensis, commonly known as the Chillan bellflower, is a small flowering plant native to the Andes mountains in Chile.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant typically grows in alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions at high altitudes. Its flowers are small, white or pale purple, and it produces small, rounded seeds.

Draba chillanensis is noted for its resilience and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils.
