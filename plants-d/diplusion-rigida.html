Diplusion rigida

Diplusion rigida, commonly known as rigid diplusion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain regions of North America.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. The plant features upright, branching stems covered in fine, hairy hairs. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a bluish-green hue.

Diplusion rigida produces small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.
