Dypsis fibrosa

Dypsis fibrosa, commonly known as the fibrous palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to Madagascar and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by dark blue berries. Dypsis fibrosa is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its elegant appearance and adaptability to various growing conditions.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.
