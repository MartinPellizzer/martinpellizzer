Dombeya megaphyllopsis

Dombeya megaphyllopsis, commonly known as the giant-leaved dombeya, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in diameter. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically in shades of white, pink, or purple, during the spring and early summer. Its botanical features make it a striking ornamental plant, often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value.

The species is also valued for its adaptability to well-drained soils and partial to full sun exposure.
