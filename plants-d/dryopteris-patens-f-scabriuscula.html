Dryopteris patens f. scabriuscula

Dryopteris patens f. scabriuscula, commonly known as the scaly wood fern, is a fern species characterized by its distinctive fronds with a rough, scaly texture on the underside.

It is a fern that thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in temperate regions. The fronds are bipinnate, with a delicate, lace-like appearance that becomes more pronounced as they mature. This fern is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and can be grown in shaded garden settings.

Its foliage provides a unique aesthetic, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.
