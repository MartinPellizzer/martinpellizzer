Dontostemon senilis

Dontostemon senilis, commonly known as the silverleaf dandelion, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It features a rosette of silver-gray, fuzzy leaves that help reduce water loss in arid conditions. The plant produces small, yellow composite flowers that bloom in early spring. Its root system is fibrous and capable of storing nutrients, aiding in its survival during dry periods.

It is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a pioneer species in ecological succession.
