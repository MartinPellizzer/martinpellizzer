Daucus aegyptiacus

Daucus aegyptiacus, commonly known as the Egyptian cowplant, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its distinctive umbellate inflorescence.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and parts of North Africa, thriving in dry, rocky soils. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces small, white to yellow flowers. It is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
