Dendrobium × cassiope

Dendrobium × cassiope, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is prized for its ornamental value.

It is a hybrid between Dendrobium nobile and Dendrobium phalaenopsis, resulting in a plant with a unique combination of traits from both parents. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or lithophyte, attaching itself to trees or rocks in its native habitat. Its flowers are usually showy, with a variety of colors including white, pink, and yellow, and can bloom multiple times throughout the year.

The botanical characteristics of Dendrobium × cassiope make it a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor cultivation.
