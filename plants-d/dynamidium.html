Dynamidium

Dynamidium, commonly known as the golden root, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its fibrous roots, which are rich in starch and have been traditionally used in folk medicine. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30-60 centimeters, with lance-shaped leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring. Dynamidium thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded forest understories.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique chemical composition and historical use in herbal remedies.
