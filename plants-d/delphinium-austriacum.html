Delphinium austriacum

Delphinium austriacum, commonly known as Austrian larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Alps of Europe.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring tall, unbranched stems that bear clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem, and its flowers are arranged in dense spikes at the top of the plant. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, which includes other notable genera such as Ranunculus and Anemone.

Delphinium austriacum is often found in meadows and rocky slopes, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
