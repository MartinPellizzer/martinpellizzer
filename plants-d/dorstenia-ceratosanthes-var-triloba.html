Dorstenia ceratosanthes var. triloba

Dorstenia ceratosanthes var. triloba, commonly known as the three-lobed false arum, is a tropical plant belonging to the family Araceae.

It is characterized by its unique, heart-shaped leaves with three distinct lobes, which contribute to its ornamental appeal. The plant produces a striking spathe and spadix inflorescence, typical of the arum family, with the spadix bearing small, inconspicuous flowers. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

This variant is often cultivated for its foliage rather than its flowers, making it a popular choice in tropical and subtropical gardens.
