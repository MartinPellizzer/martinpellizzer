Dianthus geminiflorus var. arragonensis

Dianthus geminiflorus var. arragonensis, commonly known as the Arragon carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, double-petaled flowers that come in a range of colors, including pink, red, and white. The plant produces its flowers in clusters, with each inflorescence containing multiple florets that give it a full, showy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to both ornamental gardens and horticultural collections.
