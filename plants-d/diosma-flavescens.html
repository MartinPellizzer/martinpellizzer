Diosma flavescens

Diosma flavescens, commonly known as the yellow diosma, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its hairy stems and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought and heat. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered in fine pubescence, while its flowers are clustered in cymes.

Diosma flavescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for various purposes.
