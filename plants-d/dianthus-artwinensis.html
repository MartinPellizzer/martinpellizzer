Dianthus artwinensis

Dianthus artwinensis, commonly known as the Armenian carnation, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Armenia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, brightly colored flowers that typically range from pink to red. The plant features upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and rocky habitats.

Dianthus artwinensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
