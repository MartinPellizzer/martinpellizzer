Digitaria sanguinalis var. pakistanica

Digitaria sanguinalis var. pakistanica, commonly known as the Pakistan grass, is a perennial grass species native to South Asia.

It typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and climatic conditions.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes considered a weed in cultivated areas.
