Delphinium speciosum var. trichocarpum

Delphinium speciosum var. trichocarpum, commonly known as the trichocarpum larkspur, is a striking perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers in racemes, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a fuzzy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
