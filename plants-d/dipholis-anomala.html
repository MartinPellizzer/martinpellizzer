Dipholis anomala

Dipholis anomala, commonly known as the African orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to sub-Saharan Africa.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its upright, grass-like growth habit and linear leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green, often with a distinctive lip petal. It typically blooms during the rainy season, relying on mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
