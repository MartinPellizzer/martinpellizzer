Dichodon viscidum

Dichodon viscidum, commonly known as sticky dichondra, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a low-growing, spreading habit with small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine, sticky hairs. The leaves are typically oval to lance-shaped and have a smooth or slightly serrated margin. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, usually greenish-white in color, which are followed by small, round fruits.

This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
