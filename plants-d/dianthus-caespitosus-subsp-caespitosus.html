Dianthus caespitosus subsp. caespitosus

Dianthus caespitosus subsp.

caespitosus, commonly known as the moss pinks, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit with dense clusters of small, pink or purple flowers.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, bilaterally symmetrical flowers with five petals. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
