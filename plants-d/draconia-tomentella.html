Draconia tomentella

Draconia tomentella, commonly known as the woolly dragon's beard, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and hairy, grayish-green foliage. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
