Diasperus distichus

Diasperus distichus, commonly known as the two-ranked grass, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive two-ranked arrangement of leaves, which gives it a unique appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open, disturbed habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
