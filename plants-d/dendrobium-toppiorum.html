Dendrobium toppiorum

Dendrobium toppiorum, commonly known as the Topiroid Dendrobium, is an epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features long, cane-like pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in its natural habitat. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white, yellow, and orange, typically during the warmer months. It prefers bright, indirect light and moderate humidity, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.

Its unique growth habit and ornamental value contribute to its appeal among orchid enthusiasts.
