Draconanthes trachysepala

Draconanthes trachysepala, commonly known as the rough-sepal draconanthes, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, rough-textured sepals and petals, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, displaying a range of colors from green to brown.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
