Diospyros reticulata

Diospyros reticulata, commonly known as the wild persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by oval, orange-red fruits with a sweet, astringent flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in forested areas and along riverbanks.

The fruit is not only edible but also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
