Diasperus lanceolatus

Diasperus lanceolatus, commonly known as the lanceolate spurge, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on short pedicels. It thrives in arid or semi-arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

The species is part of the Euphorbiaceae family, known for its diverse and often toxic members.
