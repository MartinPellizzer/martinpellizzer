Dolichos bengalensis

Dolichos bengalensis, commonly known as the Bengal horsegram, is a leguminous plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is native to South and Southeast Asia and is characterized by its erect, slender stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that give way to elongated, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It is well adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and is often grown as a forage crop or for its edible seeds.

Its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen makes it beneficial for soil fertility in agricultural systems.
