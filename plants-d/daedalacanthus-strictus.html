Daedalacanthus strictus

Daedalacanthus strictus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved daedalacanthus, is a rare flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, clustered white flowers that bloom during the wet season. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in tropical rainforests. Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and limited distribution, making it a subject of interest for botanists.
