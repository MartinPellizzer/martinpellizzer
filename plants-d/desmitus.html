Desmitus

Desmitus, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a unique and rare plant species native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its translucent, waxy leaves that appear almost ethereal under sunlight. The plant produces small, pale flowers that emit a faint, sweet fragrance, attracting specific pollinators. Due to its limited distribution and fragile habitat, Desmitus is considered endangered and is often found in specialized conservation programs.

Its botanical significance lies in its adaptation to extreme environmental conditions and its role in ecological research.
