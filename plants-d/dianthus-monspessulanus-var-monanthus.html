Dianthus monspessulanus var. monanthus

Dianthus monspessulanus var. monanthus, commonly known as the single-flowered campion, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe.

It features striking pink or red flowers with a distinctive, frilled appearance, often blooming in summer. The plant has basal leaves that are linear to lanceolate, and its stems are usually unbranched. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and ecological landscapes.
