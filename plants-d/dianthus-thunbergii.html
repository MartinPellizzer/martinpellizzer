Dianthus thunbergii

Dianthus thunbergii, commonly known as the Japanese carnation, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It features upright, slender stems that bear clusters of pink or white flowers with five petals each. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
