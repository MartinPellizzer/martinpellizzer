Desmodium pauciflorum

Desmodium pauciflorum, commonly known as the small-flowered burr legume, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Africa and the Americas.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces distinctive, hairy pods that contain several seeds, which are often dispersed by wind or animals. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, sunny environments.

Desmodium pauciflorum is often used in traditional medicine and as a forage crop for livestock.
