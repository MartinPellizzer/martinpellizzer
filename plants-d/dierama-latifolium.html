Dierama latifolium

Dierama latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf dierama, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its upright, grass-like foliage and striking, showy inflorescences. The plant produces clusters of lavender or blue-purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Dierama latifolium is also valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
