Didymocarpus sesquifolius

Didymocarpus sesquifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leafed tamarind, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Caesalpiniaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with three to five elliptical leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by elongated, woody pods containing edible seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in local medicine.
