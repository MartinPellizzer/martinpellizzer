Deprea auccana

Deprea auccana, commonly known as the golden cupflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and thrives in moist, sandy soils. It is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for pollinators and wetland ecosystems.
