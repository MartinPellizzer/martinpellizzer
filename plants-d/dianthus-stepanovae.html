Dianthus stepanovae

Dianthus stepanovae, commonly known as the Stepanov's campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family, which includes many ornamental and medicinal plants. The plant features showy, pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically linear and hairy, adapted to the arid conditions of its natural habitat.

Dianthus stepanovae is often found growing in rocky soils and open slopes at high elevations.
