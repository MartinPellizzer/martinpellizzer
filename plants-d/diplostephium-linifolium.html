Diplostephium linifolium

Diplostephium linifolium, commonly known as the white thorn, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and small, white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has compound leaves with linear, pointed leaflets and produces small, round fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along dry streambeds.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to withstand drought and heat.
