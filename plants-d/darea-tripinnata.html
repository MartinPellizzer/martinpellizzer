Darea tripinnata

Darea tripinnata, commonly known as the three-parted dandelion, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and a tall, slender stem that bears a single flower head. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into fluffy, white seed heads, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Darea tripinnata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
