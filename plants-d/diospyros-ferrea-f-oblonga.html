Diospyros ferrea f. oblonga

Diospyros ferrea f. oblonga, commonly known as the Brazilian persimmon, is a variant of the persimmon tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into orange to red, fleshy fruits. The fruit is edible, with a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor, and is often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is valued for both its ornamental and edible qualities.
