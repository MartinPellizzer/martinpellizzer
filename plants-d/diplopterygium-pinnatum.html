Diplopterygium pinnatum

Diplopterygium pinnatum, commonly known as the featherleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the wetlands and marshes of eastern North America.

It features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often adorned with fine, feathery veins, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in dense clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in swampy areas or along the edges of ponds.

Due to its ecological importance, it plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
