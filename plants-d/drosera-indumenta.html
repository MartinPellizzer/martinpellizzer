Drosera indumenta

Drosera indumenta, commonly known as the hairy sundew, is a carnivorous plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its sticky, glandular hairs that secrete a mucilaginous substance to trap insects. The plant typically grows in wet, acidic environments such as bogs and heathlands. Its leaves are small and hairy, with the glands arranged in a dense, fuzzy layer.

Drosera indumenta is known for its ability to digest captured prey, providing it with essential nutrients in nutrient-poor soils.
