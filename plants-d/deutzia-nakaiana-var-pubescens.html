Deutzia nakaiana var. pubescens

Deutzia nakaiana var. pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent deutzia, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It features slender, arching branches that bear clusters of white or pale pink flowers in early summer. The leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a soft, hairy texture that gives the plant its varietal name. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
