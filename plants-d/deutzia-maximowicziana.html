Deutzia maximowicziana

Deutzia maximowicziana, commonly known as the Japanese deutzia, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It features clusters of fragrant, white to pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, arching stems and oval, dark green leaves that turn yellow in autumn. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.

Deutzia maximowicziana is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive flowers and moderate growth habit.
