Duperrea insignis

Duperrea insignis, commonly known as the golden bell tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that resemble small bells, which bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall, with smooth, light green bark and lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Duperrea insignis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
