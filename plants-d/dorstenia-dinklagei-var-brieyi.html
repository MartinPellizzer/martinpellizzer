Dorstenia dinklagei var. brieyi

Dorstenia dinklagei var. brieyi, commonly known as the Briey's False Banana, is a unique member of the Moraceae family, native to the tropical regions of Central America.

This plant is characterized by its striking, spirally arranged leaves that resemble the leaves of a banana plant, though it is not closely related. The leaves are typically elongated and have a waxy texture, with a distinctive pattern of veins that add to its ornamental appeal. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that develop into edible, fig-like fruits, which are a food source for various wildlife.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its aesthetic and ecological value.
