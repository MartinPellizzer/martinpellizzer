Delphinium mollifolium

Delphinium mollifolium, commonly known as the downy larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height, supporting clusters of showy blue to purple flowers. The leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and covered with a fine, soft down, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

The plant is part of the Ranunculaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens and its historical use in traditional medicine.
