Dianthus prolifer subsp. velutinus

Dianthus prolifer subsp.

velutinus, commonly known as the velvet-flowered campion, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces showy, pink or red flowers with prominent white markings.

The plant features hairy stems and leaves, which contribute to its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats. Dianthus prolifer subsp.

velutinus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
