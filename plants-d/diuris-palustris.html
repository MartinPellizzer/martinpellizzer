Diuris palustris

Diuris palustris, commonly known as the swampy greenhood, is a terrestrial orchid native to southeastern Australia.

It typically grows in wet or swampy habitats, often in coastal areas with sandy or muddy soils. The plant features a single, erect stem that bears several lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are striking, with a hood-like labellum and a greenish-yellow coloration.

Diuris palustris is known for its seasonal blooming, usually occurring in spring, and is a subject of interest for botanists and orchid enthusiasts.
