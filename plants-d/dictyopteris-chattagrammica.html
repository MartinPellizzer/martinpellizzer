Dictyopteris chattagrammica

Dictyopteris chattagrammica, commonly known as the Chattagramma sea palm, is a species of brown algae belonging to the family Dictyopteridaceae.

It is characterized by its branching, tree-like structure and is typically found in shallow coastal waters. The plant has a leathery, dark brown thallus with a distinctive, flattened shape. It contains a variety of bioactive compounds, making it of interest in pharmaceutical research.

This species plays an important ecological role in marine environments by providing habitat and food for various organisms.
