Diplopeltis intermedia

Diplopeltis intermedia, commonly known as the African nettle, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features opposite, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive net-like venation pattern. It produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are borne on slender peduncles.

The species is characterized by its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its tolerance to both drought and moderate frost.
