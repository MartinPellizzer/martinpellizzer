Dioscorea affinis

Dioscorea affinis, commonly known as the wild yam, is a perennial vine native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by tuberous roots that are the primary part used for medicinal and culinary purposes. Its roots contain compounds such as diosgenin, which has been studied for potential pharmaceutical applications.

Dioscorea affinis is often found in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full sunlight.
