Draba media var. leiocarpa

Draba media var. leiocarpa, commonly known as the smooth-capsule rockcress, is a low-growing perennial herb native to alpine and arctic regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically has a mat-forming growth habit with hairy stems and basal leaves that are often lobed or divided. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, and it produces small, smooth-seeded capsules that mature in late summer.

Draba media var. leiocarpa is adapted to harsh environments, thriving in rocky soils and high altitudes where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
