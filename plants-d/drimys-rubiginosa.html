Drimys rubiginosa

Drimys rubiginosa, commonly known as the Chilean pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows in coastal and temperate regions. The plant features lance-shaped, dark green leaves and produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit is a red, fleshy berry that contains several seeds.

Drimys rubiginosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance and aromatic properties.
