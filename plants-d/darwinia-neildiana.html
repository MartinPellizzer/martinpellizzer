Darwinia neildiana

Darwinia neildiana, commonly known as the Neild's Darwinia, is a flowering plant native to western Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, showy spikes of tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in heathland and woodland environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually pink to red in color, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects.

Darwinia neildiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
