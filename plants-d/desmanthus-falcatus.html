Desmanthus falcatus

Desmanthus falcatus, commonly known as the sickleleaf burrgrass, is a perennial legume native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces burr-like seed pods that contain small, edible seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and prairies.

Desmanthus falcatus is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
