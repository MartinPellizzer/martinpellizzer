Dichaea tuerckheimii

Dichaea tuerckheimii, commonly known as the Tuerckheim's dichaea, is a rare and delicate orchid native to the highlands of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and its striking, showy flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant produces aerial roots and thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on tree branches or rocky outcrops. Its flowers are typically clustered in racemes and emit a faint fragrance, attracting pollinators such as bees and moths.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Dichaea tuerckheimii is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
