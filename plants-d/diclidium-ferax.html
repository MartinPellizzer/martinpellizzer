Diclidium ferax

Diclidium ferax, commonly known as the African orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically feature vibrant colors such as yellow, orange, and red. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in seasonal climates. Its leaves are usually strap-shaped and can grow up to several feet in length.

Diclidium ferax is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for with proper light and watering conditions.
