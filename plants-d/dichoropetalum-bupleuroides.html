Dichoropetalum bupleuroides

Dichoropetalum bupleuroides, commonly known as the narrowleaf false indigo, is a member of the Leguminosae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its slender, erect stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It is native to parts of the southeastern United States and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
