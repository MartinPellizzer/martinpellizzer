Dissotis muenzneri

Dissotis muenzneri, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvetleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. Its small, clustered flowers are usually greenish or brownish, and it produces small, three-lobed fruits.

Dissotis muenzneri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its unique foliage.
