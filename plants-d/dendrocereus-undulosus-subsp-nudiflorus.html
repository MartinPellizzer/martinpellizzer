Dendrocereus undulosus subsp. nudiflorus

Dendrocereus undulosus subsp.

nudiflorus, commonly known as the nakedflowered night bloomer, is a cacti species native to Mexico. It is characterized by its tall, branching stems covered in sharp spines.

The plant produces large, white flowers that open at night, emitting a strong fragrance. Its flowers are typically borne at the tips of the stems, and the plant is adapted to arid environments.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and nocturnal blooming habit.
