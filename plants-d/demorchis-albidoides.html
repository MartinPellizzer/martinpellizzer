Demorchis albidoides

Demorchis albidoides, commonly known as the white-flowered orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family, characterized by its elegant, fragrant blooms. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are predominantly white with subtle pink or purple hues, and it produces small, green, fleshy fruits.

Demorchis albidoides is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
