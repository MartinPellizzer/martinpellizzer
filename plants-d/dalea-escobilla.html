Dalea escobilla

Dalea escobilla, commonly known as the Escobilla milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes many legumes, and it has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Dalea escobilla is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
