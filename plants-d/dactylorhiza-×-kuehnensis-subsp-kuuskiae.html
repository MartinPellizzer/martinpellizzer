Dactylorhiza × kuehnensis subsp. kuuskiae

Dactylorhiza × kuehnensis subsp.

kuuskiae, commonly known as the Kuuskia orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to Finland and parts of Scandinavia. It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and peat bogs.

The plant produces tall, erect stems with a single, showy spike of pale yellow to greenish-white flowers. It has a pseudobulbous structure with fleshy leaves that store water, aiding in its survival in its cool, temperate habitat.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
