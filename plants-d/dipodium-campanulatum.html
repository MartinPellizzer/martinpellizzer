Dipodium campanulatum

Dipodium campanulatum, commonly known as the bell-shaped dipodium, is a member of the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in shaded, moist environments. Its leaves are usually broad and lance-shaped, and it produces small, clustered inflorescences.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
