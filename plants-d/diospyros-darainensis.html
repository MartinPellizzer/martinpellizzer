Diospyros darainensis

Diospyros darainensis, commonly known as the Daraina persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes ebony and persimmon trees. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical or ovate in shape. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in panicles, while the fruit is a small, fleshy drupe with a sweet, edible pulp.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and has limited use in traditional medicine.
