Daphniphyllum oblongum subsp. salicifolium

Daphniphyllum oblongum subsp.

salicifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf daphniphyllum, is a evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves that are often leathery in texture.

The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters, followed by red berries that are a notable feature. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and ecological role in its native habitat.
