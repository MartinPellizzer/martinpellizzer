Dendrobium odiosum

Dendrobium odiosum, commonly known as the black orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is an epiphytic plant, meaning it grows on the surface of trees or other structures without deriving nutrients from them. The plant produces long, slender pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, enabling it to survive in its tropical environment. Its flowers are typically dark purple or black, with a striking contrast to its green foliage.

Dendrobium odiosum is prized for its unique appearance and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
