Diphylleia sinensis

Diphylleia sinensis, commonly known as the Chinese bellflower, is a unique flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in shades of blue or purple. The plant has a distinctive, fleshy, and often hairy stem that helps it retain moisture in its natural habitat. Its leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Diphylleia sinensis is primarily grown as a rare and exotic ornamental plant in botanical gardens due to its unusual appearance and limited distribution.
