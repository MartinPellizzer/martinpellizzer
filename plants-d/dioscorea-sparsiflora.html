Dioscorea sparsiflora

Dioscorea sparsiflora, commonly known as the sparse-flowered yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the genus Dioscorea, which includes various species of yams, and is characterized by its tuberous roots that are often used as food. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers arranged in clusters, and its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires a warm climate for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and edible tubers, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and agricultural settings.
