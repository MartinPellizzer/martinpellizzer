Delphinium davidii

Delphinium davidii, commonly known as the Himalayan larkspur, is a striking perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features tall, spindly stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, adorned with clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant has lance-shaped, deeply cut leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. Its flowers are borne in dense spikes, adding a dramatic visual impact to gardens.

Delphinium davidii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its vibrant, showy blooms.
