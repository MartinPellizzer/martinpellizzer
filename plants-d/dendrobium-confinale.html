Dendrobium confinale

Dendrobium confinale, commonly known as the narrow-leafed orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is an epiphytic plant, typically growing on trees or rocks in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features long, narrow leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Dendrobium confinale is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
