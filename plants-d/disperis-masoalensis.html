Disperis masoalensis

Disperis masoalensis, commonly known as the Masoala orchid, is a rare and endemic species found primarily in the rainforests of Madagascar.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique floral structure and vibrant coloration. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree trunks or branches in the humid, shaded environments of the rainforest canopy. Its flowers are usually small but striking, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insect species.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, Disperis masoalensis is considered a vulnerable species, highlighting the need for conservation efforts in its native region.
