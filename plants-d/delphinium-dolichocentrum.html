Delphinium dolichocentrum

Delphinium dolichocentrum, commonly known as the long-stemmed larkspur, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, spindly stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces clusters of showy, blue to purple flowers with five petals, typical of the delphinium genus. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.

Delphinium dolichocentrum thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in meadows and rocky areas.
