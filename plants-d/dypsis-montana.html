Dypsis montana

Dypsis montana, commonly known as the mountain traveler palm, is a species native to Madagascar.

It is a small to medium-sized palm tree that typically grows up to 6 meters in height. The plant has a slender trunk with a smooth, greyish-brown bark and produces feather-like, compound leaves. Its flowers are small and yellowish-green, arranged in dense clusters, and it produces fruits that are elliptical and yellow when ripe.

Dypsis montana is well adapted to dry and rocky environments, making it a resilient and ornamental plant.
