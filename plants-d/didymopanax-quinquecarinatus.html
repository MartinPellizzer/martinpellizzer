Didymopanax quinquecarinatus

Didymopanax quinquecarinatus, commonly known as the five-ribbed panga, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves with five prominent veins. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a spherical, fibrous capsule that splits open to release seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appearance and has some traditional uses in local communities.
