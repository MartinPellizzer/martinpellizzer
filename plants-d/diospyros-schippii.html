Diospyros schippii

Diospyros schippii, commonly known as Schippi's persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes ebony and persimmon trees. The plant is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy green upper surface. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are followed by orange to red berries that contain one to three seeds.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and restricted geographic distribution.
