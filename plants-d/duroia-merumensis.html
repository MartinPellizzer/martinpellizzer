Duroia merumensis

Duroia merumensis, commonly known as the pink hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking pink to purple flowers, which bloom throughout the wet season. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks. Its leaves are broad, ovate, and often have a waxy texture.

Duroia merumensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
