Didymoglossum ovale

Didymoglossum ovale, commonly known as the oval bryophyte, is a small, mat-forming moss that thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It is characterized by its delicate, greenish-yellow stems and leaf-like structures that are ovate in shape. The plant produces small, capsule-like spore cases at the tips of its shoots, which are typically clustered in groups of two. It is often found in peat bogs, wet heaths, and other damp, acidic habitats.

Due to its specialized habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in certain regions.
