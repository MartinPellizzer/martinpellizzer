Distichlis condensata

Distichlis condensata, commonly known as saltbush or alkali grass, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas.

It is a perennial grass that thrives in saline and alkaline soils, making it well adapted to harsh environments. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss. It produces small, spike-like flower clusters that are typically green or blue-green in color.

Distichlis condensata plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various desert organisms.
