Didiscus compositus

Didiscus compositus, commonly known as the composite didiscus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, daisy-like blooms with yellow centers and white petals. Didiscus compositus is often found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
