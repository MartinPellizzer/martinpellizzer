Doliocarpus amazonicus

Doliocarpus amazonicus, commonly known as the Amazonian sycamore, is a member of the Sapindaceae family and is native to the rainforests of South America.

It is a large tree that can grow up to 30 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, grey bark and large, palmately lobed leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by woody, spiny fruits containing edible seeds. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The tree plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
