Diospyros perreticulata

Diospyros perreticulata, commonly known as the African persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by orange-red, fleshy fruits. Its fruit is edible, though some varieties may have astringent or bitter taste when unripe.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appearance and has traditional uses in local medicine.
