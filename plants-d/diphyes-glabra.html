Diphyes glabra

Diphyes glabra, commonly known as the smooth diphyes, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It typically grows in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and wetlands. The plant features elongated, narrow leaves that are smooth and often covered in a waxy cuticle. Its flowers are small and clustered in terminal inflorescences, usually appearing in shades of white or pale pink.

The species is notable for its ability to thrive in wetland environments and its role in supporting local insect populations.
