Draba unilateralis

Draba unilateralis, commonly known as the single-sided whitlow, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other mustard family plants. This species is characterized by its small, white or pale yellow flowers and basal rosette of leaves. Draba unilateralis typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for studies on plant adaptation to harsh climates.
