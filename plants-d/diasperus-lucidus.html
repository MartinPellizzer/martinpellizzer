Diasperus lucidus

Diasperus lucidus, commonly known as the shining sandverbena, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is a low-growing shrub that thrives in sandy and rocky soils, often found in coastal and desert environments. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and covered in a waxy coating, helping to reduce water loss in its harsh habitat.

Diasperus lucidus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in xeriscapic gardens.
