Desmodium chartaceum

Desmodium chartaceum, commonly known as paperleaf burrlegume, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces distinctive, spiny pods that contain kidney-shaped seeds. It is often found in open woodlands and disturbed soils, thriving in warm climates.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and potential agricultural use.
