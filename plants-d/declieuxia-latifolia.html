Declieuxia latifolia

Declieuxia latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf lily, is a striking ornamental plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It features tall, grass-like leaves that are broad and glossy, often reaching several feet in length. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires full to partial sunlight.

Declieuxia latifolia is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often used in water gardens or as a border plant.
