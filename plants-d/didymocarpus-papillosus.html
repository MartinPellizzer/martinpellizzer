Didymocarpus papillosus

Didymocarpus papillosus, commonly known as the hairy tamarind, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with hairy surfaces and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant produces woody pods that contain kidney-shaped seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
