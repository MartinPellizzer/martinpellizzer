Dalbergia succirubra

Dalbergia succirubra, commonly known as rosewood, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dark reddish-brown heartwood, which is highly valued for its rich color and fine grain. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family and is known for its dense, durable wood used in furniture and musical instruments. It typically grows in humid lowland areas and requires a warm, tropical climate to thrive.

The leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets, and the flowers are small and pale pink to white in color.
