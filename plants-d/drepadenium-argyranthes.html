Drepadenium argyranthes

Drepadenium argyranthes, commonly known as the silver-leafed fern, is a member of the Dryopteridaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that often have a silvery sheen due to the presence of trichomes. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are creeping and produce clusters of spore-bearing sorus.

The botanical features of Drepadenium argyranthes make it a distinctive and ornamental species in horticulture.
