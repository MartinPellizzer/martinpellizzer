Dianthus moesiacus subsp. grancarovii

Dianthus moesiacus subsp.

grancarovii, commonly known as the Carpathian campion, is a flowering plant native to the Carpathian Mountains. It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and produces clusters of pink to reddish-pink flowers.

The species belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is known for its showy, often biennial or perennial growth cycle. Its leaves are typically linear to oblanceolate, and the plant thrives in rocky or sandy soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
