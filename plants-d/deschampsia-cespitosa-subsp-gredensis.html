Deschampsia cespitosa subsp. gredensis

Deschampsia cespitosa subsp.

gredensis, commonly known as Gredens' bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands, meadows, and rocky areas.

The plant has fine, blue-green blades and forms dense, tufted clumps, which contribute to its ornamental appeal. It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, spike-like inflorescences.

This subspecies is well adapted to dry conditions and is often used in native plant landscaping.
