Dryopteris wallichiana subsp. bhutanica

Dryopteris wallichiana subsp.

bhutanica, commonly known as the Bhutanese shield fern, is a fern species native to the Himalayan regions of Bhutan and India. It is characterized by its large, shield-shaped fronds that can reach up to 1 meter in length.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in temperate forests at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters. Its fronds are bi-lobed and have a distinctive, almost triangular shape when mature.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
