Dactylis lagopoides

Dactylis lagopoides, commonly known as sheep's fescue, is a perennial grass native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in pastures and erosion control.

Dactylis lagopoides is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate drought and grazing.
