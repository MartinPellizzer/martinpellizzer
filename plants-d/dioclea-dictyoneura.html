Dioclea dictyoneura

Dioclea dictyoneura, commonly known as the purple passion vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces showy, violet-colored flowers that are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruit is a pod that splits open to release seeds, contributing to its dispersal.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
