Danguyodrypetes ambigua

Danguyodrypetes ambigua, commonly known as the African mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of various host trees in sub-Saharan Africa.

It has small, oval-shaped leaves that are usually opposite in arrangement and are covered with a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by small, fleshy berries that are dispersed by birds. It plays a role in the ecosystem by providing food for birds and other animals, while also drawing nutrients from its host.

Its parasitic nature and unique adaptations make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
