Diosma tetragona

Diosma tetragona, commonly known as the four-angled Diosma, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, branching stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Diosma tetragona is typically found in rocky or sandy soils and is often used in traditional herbal medicine.
