Dianthus cutandae

Dianthus cutandae, commonly known as the Andes pinks, is a flowering plant native to the high Andes of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers on short stalks. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down.

Dianthus cutandae thrives in cool, moist environments and is adapted to the harsh conditions of its mountainous habitat.
