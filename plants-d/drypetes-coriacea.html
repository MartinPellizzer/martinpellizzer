Drypetes coriacea

Drypetes coriacea, commonly known as the rough bark tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and small, elliptical leaves with a leathery texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in lowland forests.

The species is valued for its hard, durable wood and has traditional uses in local medicine.
