Dactylorhiza rhenana

Dactylorhiza rhenana, commonly known as the Rhine orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Europe, particularly found in moist, calcareous habitats.

It typically grows in open woodlands, meadows, and along riverbanks, preferring well-drained soils. The plant has a slender, erect stem with few leaves and produces a cluster of pale yellow to greenish-white flowers. Its roots are specialized for mycorrhizal associations, which are essential for nutrient uptake.

The species is part of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique floral structure and ecological adaptations.
