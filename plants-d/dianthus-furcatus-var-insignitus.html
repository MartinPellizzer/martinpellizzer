Dianthus furcatus var. insignitus

Dianthus furcatus var. insignitus, commonly known as the fringed campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, pink or white flowers with distinctive fringed petals. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, and its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and alpine settings.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
