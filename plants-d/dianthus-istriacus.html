Dianthus istriacus

Dianthus istriacus, commonly known as the Istrian carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Istrian Peninsula in Croatia.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant produces small, rounded leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs, and its stems are often reddish in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The flowers are also known for their pleasant, spicy fragrance, which attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies.
