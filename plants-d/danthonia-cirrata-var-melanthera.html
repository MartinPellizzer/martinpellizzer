Danthonia cirrata var. melanthera

Danthonia cirrata var. melanthera, commonly known as black beard grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black basal leaves, which give it its distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear pale green to yellow flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging environmental conditions.
