Disa aconitoides subsp. goetzeana

Disa aconitoides subsp.

goetzeana, commonly known as the Goetze's Disa, is a striking orchid species native to South Africa. It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of pale blue to violet flowers with a distinctive dark purple throat.

The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial shade to full sun.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
