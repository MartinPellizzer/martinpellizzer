Dendrocalamopsis grandis

Dendrocalamopsis grandis, commonly known as the giant bamboo, is a member of the grass family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach heights of up to 30 meters. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers and is known for its rapid growth rate.

Dendrocalamopsis grandis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
