Delphinium pentagynum

Delphinium pentagynum, commonly known as the five-gynostemon delphinium, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that bear clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and features large, palmately divided leaves. Its flowers are actinomorphic, with five sepals and five petals, and it produces follicular fruits that contain several seeds.

Delphinium pentagynum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
