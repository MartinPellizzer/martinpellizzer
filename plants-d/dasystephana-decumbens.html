Dasystephana decumbens

Dasystephana decumbens, commonly known as the prostrate stephania, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous species characterized by its low-growing, sprawling habit. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are often hairy and have a distinct reddish or purple hue. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically white or pale pink, and appear in the axils of the leaves.

Dasystephana decumbens is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks.
