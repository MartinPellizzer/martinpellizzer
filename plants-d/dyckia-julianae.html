Dyckia julianae

Dyckia julianae, commonly known as the Julian's Dyckia, is a species of flowering plant in the Bromeliaceae family.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hairs. The plant produces striking orange-red inflorescences that emerge from the center of the rosette. It is a succulent plant, adapted to arid environments, and is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance.

Dyckia julianae is relatively easy to care for, requiring bright light and well-draining soil.
