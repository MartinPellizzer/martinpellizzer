Dactylorhiza vadorum var. picturata

Dactylorhiza vadorum var. picturata, commonly known as the painted orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to the British Isles.

It is characterized by its vivid, often purple or red-tinted petals, which are adorned with intricate patterns resembling brushwork. The plant typically grows in damp, shaded woodland habitats, thriving in moist, acidic soils. Its flowering period occurs between May and July, during which it produces tall, slender stems topped with clusters of fragrant flowers.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a protected species in many regions.
