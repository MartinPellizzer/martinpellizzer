Dalea lasiostachya

Dalea lasiostachya, commonly known as the rough-stemmed milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers in dense spikes, which are typically arranged along the upper part of the stem. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and sagebrush steppe.

Dalea lasiostachya is also valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and as a food source for various wildlife species.
