Dipsacus meyeri

Dipsacus meyeri, commonly known as Meyer's teasel, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and large, palmately lobed leaves. The plant produces purple to pink bracts that resemble flowers, which are actually modified leaves. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.

Dipsacus meyeri is cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical uses in textile processing.
