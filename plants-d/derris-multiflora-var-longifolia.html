Derris multiflora var. longifolia

Derris multiflora var. longifolia, commonly known as the long-flowered derris, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and compound leaves with elongated, ovate leaflets. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by elongated, cylindrical pods containing several seeds. It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in moist, shaded environments.

The species is noted for its potential medicinal properties, though further research is needed to confirm its efficacy.
