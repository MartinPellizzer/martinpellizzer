Disa laeta

Disa laeta, commonly known as the golden disa, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow petals and a distinctive, recurved lip that enhances its ornamental appeal. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowering period occurs during the summer months, producing clusters of showy blooms.

Disa laeta is prized for its beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
