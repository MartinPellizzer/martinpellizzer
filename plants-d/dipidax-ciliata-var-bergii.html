Dipidax ciliata var. bergii

Dipidax ciliata var. bergii, commonly known as the Berg's fringed grass, is a variant of the fringed grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and distinctive silver-gray foliage with fine, ciliate (fringed) margins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typically blooming during the late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland habitats.

Due to its ornamental appeal and hardiness, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
