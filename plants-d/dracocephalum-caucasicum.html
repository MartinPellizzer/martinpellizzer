Dracocephalum caucasicum

Dracocephalum caucasicum, commonly known as Caucasian dragonhead, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces spikes of violet to blue flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Dracocephalum caucasicum is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
