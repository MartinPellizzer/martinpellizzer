Dichanthelium heliophilum

Dichanthelium heliophilum, commonly known as the sun-loving grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in full sun and thrives in sandy or loamy soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences. It is characterized by its ability to tolerate drought and poor soil conditions.

Its botanical features make it a valuable component of native plant communities in its habitat.
