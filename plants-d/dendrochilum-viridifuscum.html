Dendrochilum viridifuscum

Dendrochilum viridifuscum, commonly known as the greenish-brown tree orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and lanceolate leaves that are usually dark green with a bluish tinge. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, often with a faintly fragrant scent. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and orchid enthusiasts alike.
