Diplostephium juajibioyi subsp. leucopappum

Diplostephium juajibioyi subsp.

leucopappum, commonly known as the white pappus diplostephium, is a flowering plant native to Mexico. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters. Its distinctive white pappus, a feathery structure attached to the seeds, aids in wind dispersal.

This subspecies is typically found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.
