Dianthus carthusianorum var. tenorei

Dianthus carthusianorum var. tenorei, commonly known as the Tenore's carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, double-flowered blooms that come in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant features basal leaves that are linear to lanceolate, and its stems are typically hairy. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The flowers are often used in floral arrangements due to their ornamental appeal and long-lasting nature.
