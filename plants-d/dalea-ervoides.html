Dalea ervoides

Dalea ervoides, commonly known as the prairie clover, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, pink to purple flower clusters. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky soils. Its small, trifoliate leaves and pea-like flowers are typical of the genus Dalea.

It plays an important ecological role by fixing nitrogen in the soil, supporting diverse plant and animal life.
