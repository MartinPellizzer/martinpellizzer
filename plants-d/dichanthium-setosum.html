Dichanthium setosum

Dichanthium setosum, commonly known as hairy needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to various parts of Africa and Asia.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant features slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are characteristic of the Poaceae family.

Dichanthium setosum is known for its adaptability to arid conditions and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
