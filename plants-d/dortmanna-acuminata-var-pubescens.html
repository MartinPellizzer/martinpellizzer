Dortmanna acuminata var. pubescens

Dortmanna acuminata var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arctic and alpine regions.

It features a mat-forming growth habit with small, fleshy, ovate leaves that are covered in fine, glandular hairs. The plant produces tiny, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in early summer. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to cold, harsh environments.

This species is often found in tundra and rocky habitats, contributing to the ecological diversity of high-altitude ecosystems.
