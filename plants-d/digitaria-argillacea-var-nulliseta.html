Digitaria argillacea var. nulliseta

Digitaria argillacea var. nulliseta, commonly known as the blackgrass or redgrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along waterways.

Its ability to tolerate waterlogged conditions makes it an important component of riparian ecosystems.
