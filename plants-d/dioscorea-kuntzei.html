Dioscorea kuntzei

Dioscorea kuntzei, commonly known as the Kuntze yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary edible part of the plant. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a rough texture.

Dioscorea kuntzei is valued both for its nutritional content and traditional medicinal uses in various cultures.
