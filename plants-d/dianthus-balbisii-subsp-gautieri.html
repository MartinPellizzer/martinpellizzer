Dianthus balbisii subsp. gautieri

Dianthus balbisii subsp.

gautieri, commonly known as the Gautier's campion, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile. It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pink to red flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
