Diospyros texana

Diospyros texana, commonly known as the Texas persimmon, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southern United States.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other persimmon species. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its small, yellowish-green flowers appear in clusters and are followed by orange to red berries that are edible when fully ripe.

The fruit is an important food source for various wildlife species, including birds and mammals.
