Diospyros pulchra

Diospyros pulchra, commonly known as the red mulberry, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green on top. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers followed by edible, fleshy berries that are dark red when mature. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
