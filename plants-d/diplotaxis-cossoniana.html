Diplotaxis cossoniana

Diplotaxis cossoniana, commonly known as the Cape dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cress plants. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, fleshy leaves and produces yellow flowers in a single terminal inflorescence. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it adaptable to arid environments.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
