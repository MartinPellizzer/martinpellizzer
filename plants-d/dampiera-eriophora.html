Dampiera eriophora

Dampiera eriophora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved dampiera, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with small, fleshy leaves and clusters of white or pale blue flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathland and open forests. Its flowers are typically visited by insects, contributing to its ecological role in pollination.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
