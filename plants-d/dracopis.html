Dracopis

Dracopis, commonly known as the dragon's blood tree, is a unique plant belonging to the genus Dracophyllum in the family Ericaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, clustered flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is native to various regions in the Mediterranean and is often found in rocky or coastal areas. One of its most notable features is the production of a reddish resin, which has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a dye.

Due to its ecological significance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
