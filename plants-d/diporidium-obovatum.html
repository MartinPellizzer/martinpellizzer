Diporidium obovatum

Diporidium obovatum, commonly known as the broadleaf nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It features broad, ovate leaves that are typically hairy and have a waxy surface. The plant produces small, violet or blue flowers that are clustered in umbels, often blooming in the summer months. Its fruits are berries that turn from green to red as they mature, containing several seeds.

The plant is known for its toxic properties, containing alkaloids that can be harmful if ingested.
