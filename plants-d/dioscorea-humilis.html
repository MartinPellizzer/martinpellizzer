Dioscorea humilis

Dioscorea humilis, commonly known as the lesser yam, is a herbaceous vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and tuberous roots. The plant produces small, green flowers that are typically unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant. Its tubers are edible but require careful preparation to remove toxic compounds.

Dioscorea humilis is often cultivated for its nutritional value and has traditional uses in various indigenous cultures.
