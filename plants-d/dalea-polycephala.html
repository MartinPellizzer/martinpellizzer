Dalea polycephala

Dalea polycephala, commonly known as the redstem false indigo, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, clustered inflorescences of pink to reddish-purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are pinnately compound, and it produces small, bean-like pods that contain its seeds.

Dalea polycephala is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
