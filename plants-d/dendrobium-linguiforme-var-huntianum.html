Dendrobium linguiforme var. huntianum

Dendrobium linguiforme var. huntianum, commonly known as the Hunt's tongue orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with elongated, leaf-like pseudobulbs that are often covered in a dense, hairy coating. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange, typically arranged in racemes along the stems. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated for horticultural purposes.
