Dicranopteris palmata

Dicranopteris palmata, commonly known as the giant water fern, is a species of fern characterized by its large, floating fronds that can reach up to 3 meters in length.

It is native to tropical regions of Asia, Africa, and the Pacific Islands, often found in slow-moving freshwater environments. The plant's fronds are divided into numerous small, finger-like segments that aid in its buoyancy and photosynthetic efficiency. It reproduces via spores, which are dispersed by wind and water, contributing to its wide distribution.

Dicranopteris palmata plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various microorganisms and small aquatic life.
