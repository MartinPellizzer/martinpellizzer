Daviesia racemulosa

Daviesia racemulosa, commonly known as the yellow wattle, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in spring, which are clustered in racemes. It thrives in sandy or loamy soils and is often found in open woodlands and heathlands.

Daviesia racemulosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a habitat for native wildlife.
