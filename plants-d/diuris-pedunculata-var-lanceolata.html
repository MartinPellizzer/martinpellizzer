Diuris pedunculata var. lanceolata

Diuris pedunculata var. lanceolata, commonly known as the Lance-leaf Greenhood, is a species of orchid native to southeastern Australia.

It features a single, erect stem that bears a single, lance-shaped flower spike. The flowers are typically greenish-brown with a distinctive hood-like structure, and they are pollinated by insects. The plant prefers well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and open forests.

It is a short-lived perennial, with the leaves being lanceolate and often covered in fine hairs.
