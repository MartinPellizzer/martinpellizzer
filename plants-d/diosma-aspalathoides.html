Diosma aspalathoides

Diosma aspalathoides, commonly known as the Cape daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy yellow to orange daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal regions. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Diosma aspalathoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and drought tolerance.
