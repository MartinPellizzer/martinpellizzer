Discaria crenata var. discolor

Discaria crenata var. discolor, commonly known as the silverleaf discaria, is a shrub native to Australia.

It features small, white to pale yellow flowers that appear in clusters, typically in spring. The leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a distinctive silvery or grayish underside, giving the plant its common name. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rocky or arid landscapes.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract native pollinators.
