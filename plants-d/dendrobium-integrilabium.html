Dendrobium integrilabium

Dendrobium integrilabium, commonly known as the pink-tinged dendrobium, is an epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a tall, branching stem with numerous pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, often with a yellow throat. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining potting media.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated for horticultural purposes.
