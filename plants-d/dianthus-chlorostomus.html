Dianthus chlorostomus

Dianthus chlorostomus, commonly known as the blue dianthus, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet petals and prominent, showy flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in fine hairs.

Dianthus chlorostomus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and hardy nature.
