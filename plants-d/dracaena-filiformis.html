Dracaena filiformis

Dracaena filiformis, commonly known as the "snake plant," is a striking succulent native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features long, narrow, sword-shaped leaves that can grow up to several feet in length, often with a distinctive greenish-gray coloration. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It is highly drought-tolerant, making it an ideal choice for low-maintenance indoor gardening.

Its ability to purify air and thrive in a variety of light conditions further enhances its popularity as a houseplant.
