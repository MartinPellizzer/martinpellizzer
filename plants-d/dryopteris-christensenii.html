Dryopteris christensenii

Dryopteris christensenii, commonly known as the Christensen's wood fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its fronds that are typically bipinnate, with a distinctive, elegant appearance. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. Its rhizomes are thick and fleshy, allowing it to store water and nutrients efficiently.

The spores are produced on the underside of the fronds, which are clustered in sori, a typical feature of ferns.
