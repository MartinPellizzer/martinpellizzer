Dactylorhiza × braunii nothovar. monticola

Dactylorhiza × braunii nothovar. monticola, commonly known as the monticola orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of pale yellow to greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream sides. Its rhizomes are fibrous, and it produces both leafy and flowering shoots.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with specific moisture and light conditions.
