Delphinium micranthum

Delphinium micranthum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that bear clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. Delphinium micranthum produces small, spurred flowers that are adapted for pollination by insects.

It is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats at high elevations.
