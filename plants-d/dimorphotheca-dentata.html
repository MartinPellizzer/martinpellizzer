Dimorphotheca dentata

Dimorphotheca dentata, commonly known as the coastal daisy or African daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center, blooming in late winter to early spring. The plant has hairy, linear leaves and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. It is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to tolerate salt spray and coastal winds.

Dimorphotheca dentata is a popular ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature.
