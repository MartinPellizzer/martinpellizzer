Dioscorea mitoensis

Dioscorea mitoensis, commonly known as the Mito's yam, is a climbing perennial vine belonging to the family Dioscoreaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly in the Amazon basin. The plant produces tuberous roots that are valued for their nutritional content and traditional medicinal uses. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its flowers are small and yellow-green in color.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
