Dryopteris pseudostenobasis

Dryopteris pseudostenobasis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wood fern, is a species of fern native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped fronds that are typically 30 to 60 centimeters long. The plant produces sporangia on the underside of its fronds, which are clustered in sori covered by a protective indusium. It thrives in cool, moist environments, often found in shaded forest understories.

The botanical features of this fern make it an important component of its native ecosystems.
