Dendrosenecio battiscombei

Dendrosenecio battiscombei, commonly known as the giant groundsels, is a striking member of the Asteraceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its large, fleshy, rosette-forming leaves and towering, branched stems that can reach several meters in height. The plant produces showy yellow flowers arranged in dense, spherical clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a popular species among succulent enthusiasts and botanists alike.
