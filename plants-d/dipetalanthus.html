Dipetalanthus

Dipetalanthus, commonly known as the African violet, is a genus of flowering plants native to Africa.

These plants are characterized by their showy, tubular flowers that come in a variety of colors. They typically have thick, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting them to arid environments. Dipetalanthus species are often cultivated as ornamental plants due to their vibrant blooms and compact growth habit.

The genus includes several species, such as Dipetalanthus africanus, which is particularly popular in horticulture.
