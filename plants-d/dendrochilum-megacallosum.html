Dendrochilum megacallosum

Dendrochilum megacallosum, commonly known as the giant orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 10 centimeters in diameter. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its inflorescence is racemose, bearing numerous fragrant blooms that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in greenhouses or warm climates.
