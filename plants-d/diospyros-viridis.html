Diospyros viridis

Diospyros viridis, commonly known as the green persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other well-known species like ebony. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into edible, orange-red berries.

Diospyros viridis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
