Digitaria leucophaea

Digitaria leucophaea, commonly known as silverleaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-gray blades and compact, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a loose cluster of spikelets, which are small and cylindrical in shape.

Silverleaf grass plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
