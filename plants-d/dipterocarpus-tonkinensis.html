Dipterocarpus tonkinensis

Dipterocarpus tonkinensis, commonly known as the Tonkin dipterocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its large, heavy seeds enclosed in a woody capsule. The tree can grow up to 40 meters tall, with a straight trunk and broad, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The wood of this species is highly valued for its durability and is used in construction and furniture-making.
