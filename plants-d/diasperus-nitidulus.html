Diasperus nitidulus

Diasperus nitidulus, commonly known as the smooth rock cress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, rounded leaves and clusters of bright yellow flowers. The plant is typically found in rocky or gravelly soils in arid or semi-arid regions. It has a low-growing habit and is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.

Diasperus nitidulus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
