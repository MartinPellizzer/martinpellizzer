Dianthus pungens subsp. ruscinonensis

Dianthus pungens subsp.

ruscinonensis, commonly known as the Alpine campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of pink to red flowers with five petals.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, and the flowers bloom in late summer.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and vibrant blooms.
