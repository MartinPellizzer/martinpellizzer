Dorycnium libanoticum

Dorycnium libanoticum, commonly known as the Lebanon sainfoin, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its root system is deep, allowing it to access water from lower soil layers.

Dorycnium libanoticum is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
