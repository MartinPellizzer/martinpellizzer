Dianthera lancifolia

Dianthera lancifolia, commonly known as the lanceleaf dianthera, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. It produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, adding ornamental value to gardens.

Dianthera lancifolia is often cultivated for its attractive foliage and modest floral display.
