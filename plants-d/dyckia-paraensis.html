Dyckia paraensis

Dyckia paraensis, commonly known as the Paraense Dyckia, is a striking bromeliad native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are typically blue-green in color, often with a waxy coating. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences that bear small, colorful flowers, usually red or orange. Dyckia paraensis thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

It is a popular choice for ornamental gardening due to its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
