Daviesia divaricata subsp. lanulosa

Daviesia divaricata subsp.

lanulosa, commonly known as the woolly Daviesia, is a shrub native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance.

The plant typically grows in heathlands and rocky soils, thriving in dry, sunny conditions. Its flowers are usually pink or red, appearing in clusters during spring.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract native pollinators.
