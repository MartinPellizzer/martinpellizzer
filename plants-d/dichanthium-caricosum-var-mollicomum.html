Dichanthium caricosum var. mollicomum

Dichanthium caricosum var. mollicomum, commonly known as silky fingergrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, soft, and hairy leaves that give it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

This variety is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various soil conditions.
