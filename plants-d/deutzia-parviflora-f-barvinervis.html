Deutzia parviflora f. barvinervis

Deutzia parviflora f. barvinervis, commonly known as the narrow-veined deutzia, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It features small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early summer, typically in clusters along the stems. The leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a distinctive narrow venation pattern that gives it its botanical name. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
