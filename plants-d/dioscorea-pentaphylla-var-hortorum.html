Dioscorea pentaphylla var. hortorum

Dioscorea pentaphylla var. hortorum, commonly known as the Japanese yam, is a tuberous perennial plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit. The plant produces oval, tuberous roots that are often harvested for culinary and medicinal use. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a serrated margin, and it typically grows in moist, shaded environments.

The tubers have a starchy texture and are used in various traditional dishes across its native regions.
