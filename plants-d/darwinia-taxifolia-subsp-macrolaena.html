Darwinia taxifolia subsp. macrolaena

Darwinia taxifolia subsp.

macrolaena, commonly known as the narrow-leaved Darwinia, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia. It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and showy, spike-like inflorescences.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, which attract nectar-feeding birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and inland regions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
