Diospyros acreana

Diospyros acreana, commonly known as the Brazilian persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes ebony and persimmon trees. The plant is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy surface. Its fruit is a small, orange-red berry that matures in late summer.

Diospyros acreana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage and fruit.
