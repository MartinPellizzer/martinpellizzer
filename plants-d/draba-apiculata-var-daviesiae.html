Draba apiculata var. daviesiae

Draba apiculata var. daviesiae, commonly known as the Davies' whitlow grass, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, clustered flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh alpine environments. Its leaves are basal, linear, and often covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are pale yellow or white.

This variety is noted for its resilience and is often found in high-altitude meadows and scree slopes.
