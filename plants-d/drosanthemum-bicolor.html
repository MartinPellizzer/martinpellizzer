Drosanthemum bicolor

Drosanthemum bicolor, commonly known as the double-flowered daisies, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies, sunflowers, and chrysanthemums. The plant is characterized by its showy, double petals that resemble small flowers, giving it a unique and ornamental appearance. It typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Drosanthemum bicolor is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
