Dendrobium perpaulum

Dendrobium perpaulum, commonly known as the Perpaul's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems that often bear clusters of fragrant, showy flowers. The plant typically produces blooms in shades of yellow, orange, and red, which appear during the warmer months. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged along the stem, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Dendrobium perpaulum thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
