Digitaria filiformis

Digitaria filiformis, commonly known as slender digitaria, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its fine, needle-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous spikelets. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed areas such as fields and roadsides. It is often found in low nutrient soils and can tolerate drought conditions.

Its botanical structure makes it a common component of grassland ecosystems.
