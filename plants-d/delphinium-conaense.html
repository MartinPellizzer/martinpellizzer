Delphinium conaense

Delphinium conaense, commonly known as the Himalayan larkspur, is a perennial flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that bear clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are alternate and often covered in fine hairs. Delphinium conaense thrives in cool, moist environments and is adapted to the harsh conditions of mountainous regions.

Its flowers are not only ornamental but also serve as a food source for various pollinators.
