Dichromena ciliata var. angustifolia

Dichromena ciliata var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved dichromena, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It features slender, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a soft, fuzzy texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, with a distinctive two-lipped structure. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands.

Its foliage and floral structure make it a subject of interest in botanical studies and horticultural cultivation.
