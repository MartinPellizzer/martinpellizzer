Deyeuxia smithii

Deyeuxia smithii, commonly known as the Smith's false panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the wetlands and marshes of eastern Australia.

It typically grows in dense, tufted clumps with slender, erect stems that can reach up to 1 meter in height. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a bluish-green coloration.

Deyeuxia smithii plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
