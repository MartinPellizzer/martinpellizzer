Disa versicolor

Disa versicolor, commonly known as the rainbow orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful flowers that range from purple to red, pink, and white, often with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its tall, slender stems can reach up to 1 meter in height, supporting its showy inflorescences.

Disa versicolor is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
