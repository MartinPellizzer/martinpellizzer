Dodonaea humilis

Dodonaea humilis, commonly known as the coastal honeysuckle, is a small evergreen shrub native to coastal regions of southern Australia.

It features narrow, leathery leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces clusters of small, fragrant yellow flowers. The plant is well adapted to sandy soils and salt spray, making it a hardy species for coastal environments. Its flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and birds.

Dodonaea humilis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in coastal landscaping.
