Dactylorhiza × engadinensis

Dactylorhiza × engadinensis, commonly known as the Engadine orchid, is a hybrid orchid native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its terrestrial growth habit and rhizomatous structure. The plant produces tall, slender stems with a few leaves and bears small, greenish-yellow flowers that are often clustered in a raceme. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is typically found in mountainous areas with cool, humid climates.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and protected species in many regions.
