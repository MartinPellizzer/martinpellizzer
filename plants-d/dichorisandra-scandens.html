Dichorisandra scandens

Dichorisandra scandens, commonly known as the climbing dichorisandra, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial vine characterized by its woody stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, aiding in its propagation.

Dichorisandra scandens is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
