Dipyrena recurvata

Dipyrena recurvata, commonly known as the recurved dipyrena, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, daisy-like flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and yellow. The plant produces small, linear leaves that are arranged alternately along its stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Dipyrena recurvata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
