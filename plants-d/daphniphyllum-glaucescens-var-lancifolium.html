Daphniphyllum glaucescens var. lancifolium

Daphniphyllum glaucescens var. lancifolium, commonly known as the lance-leaved daphniphyllum, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, white flowers in late spring, followed by red berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
