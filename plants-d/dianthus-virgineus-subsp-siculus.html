Dianthus virgineus subsp. siculus

Dianthus virgineus subsp.

siculus, commonly known as Sicilian carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of showy, pink to red flowers.

The leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, while the stems are upright and often branched. This subspecies is well adapted to rocky and dry environments, thriving in full sun.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
