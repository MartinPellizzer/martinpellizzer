Dypsis makirae

Dypsis makirae, commonly known as the Madagascar palm, is a striking plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its unique, spiny, tree-like structure. The plant features a thick, woody trunk with a fibrous sheath, and its leaves are typically clustered at the top. Dypsis makirae produces small, fragrant flowers that develop into edible fruits.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its distinctive appearance and resilience.
