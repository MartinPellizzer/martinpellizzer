Dianthus sylvestris

Dianthus sylvestris, commonly known as the wild carnation, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces clusters of pink to red, five-petaled flowers that bloom in spring and early summer. Its foliage consists of linear, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hair.

Dianthus sylvestris is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for various purposes.
