Dioscorea georgensis

Dioscorea georgensis, commonly known as the Georgian yam, is a climbing vine native to the temperate regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary part used for culinary and medicinal purposes. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers and heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. Its tubers are rich in starch and have been traditionally used in local cuisines.

The botanical features of D. georgensis make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
