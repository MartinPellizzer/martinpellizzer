Devendraea myrtillus var. minutifolia

Devendraea myrtillus var. minutifolia, commonly known as the dwarf myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It features compact, glossy dark green leaves that are typically smaller than those of the standard species. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sun exposure.

Due to its ornamental value and compact size, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a potted plant.
