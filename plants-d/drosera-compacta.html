Drosera compacta

Drosera compacta, commonly known as the slender sundew, is a carnivorous plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, rosette-like growth of small, glandular leaves that secrete a sticky substance to trap insects. The plant typically grows in wet, acidic environments such as bogs and heathlands. Its flowers are small and usually pink or red, appearing on tall, flowering stems.

Drosera compacta is a hardy species that can survive in a variety of conditions, making it a popular choice for carnivorous plant enthusiasts.
