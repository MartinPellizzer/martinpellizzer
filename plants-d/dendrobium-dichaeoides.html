Dendrobium dichaeoides

Dendrobium dichaeoides, commonly known as the golden orchid, is an epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a compact, branching structure with numerous small, fleshy pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers that are highly fragrant and bloom during the summer months. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged in two rows along the stem.

Dendrobium dichaeoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
