Deguelia polystachya

Deguelia polystachya, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow inflorescences, which are composed of numerous small, clustered flowers. The plant is a shrub or small tree, typically growing up to 5 meters in height, with compound leaves and woody stems. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

Deguelia polystachya plays an important role in Australian ecosystems and is also cultivated for its ornamental value.
