Dennstaedtia canaliculata

Dennstaedtia canaliculata, commonly known as the channel-veined shield fern, is a species of fern native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, bipinnate fronds with distinctive, channel-like veins that run along the leaflets. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its rhizomes are thick and creeping, allowing it to form dense colonies.

The spore-bearing structures, or sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, contributing to its reproductive strategy.
