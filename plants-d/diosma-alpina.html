Diosma alpina

Diosma alpina, commonly known as the alpine diosma, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowers, which bloom in early summer, are usually pale blue or violet, and it produces small, pod-like fruits.

Diosma alpina plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and supports local pollinators.
