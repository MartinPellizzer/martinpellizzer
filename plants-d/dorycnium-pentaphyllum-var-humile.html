Dorycnium pentaphyllum var. humile

Dorycnium pentaphyllum var. humile, commonly known as the lesser yellow trefoil, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features trifoliate leaves with three oval to ovate leaflets, often with a slight pubescence. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
