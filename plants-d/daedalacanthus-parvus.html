Daedalacanthus parvus

Daedalacanthus parvus, commonly known as the lesser star-fruit.

This small, herbaceous plant is characterized by its delicate, star-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks. The plant's leaves are narrow and lanceolate, with a slightly hairy surface.

Its fruit is a small, fleshy berry that ripens to a yellowish-orange color, attracting various pollinators and seed dispersers.
