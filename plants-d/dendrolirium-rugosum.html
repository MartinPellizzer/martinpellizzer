Dendrolirium rugosum

Dendrolirium rugosum, commonly known as the roughleaf lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the forests of eastern North America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, rough hairs. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, arranged in a spike and resembling those of lilies.

Dendrolirium rugosum thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in woodland habitats.
