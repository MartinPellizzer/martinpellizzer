Dryopteris spinulosa var. americana

Dryopteris spinulosa var. americana, commonly known as the American wood fern, is a perennial fern native to eastern North America.

It features a rhizomatous growth habit and produces fertile fronds with sporangia on the underside of the leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its foliage is characterized by dark green, lance-shaped leaves with a serrated margin.

The American wood fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in forest ecosystems.
