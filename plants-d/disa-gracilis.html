Disa gracilis

Disa gracilis, commonly known as the slender disa, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, grass-like foliage and striking, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink and purple, are adapted for pollination by specific insects.

Disa gracilis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil with adequate sunlight.
