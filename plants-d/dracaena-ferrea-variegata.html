Dracaena ferrea variegata

Dracaena ferrea variegata, commonly known as the Red-Striped Dracaena, is a striking evergreen plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features tall, slender stems with dark green leaves that have distinctive red or orange longitudinal stripes. The plant is known for its slow growth and ability to thrive in low-light conditions, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening. Its variegation is due to a natural mutation that affects chlorophyll production, resulting in its unique color patterns.

Dracaena ferrea variegata is also valued for its air-purifying qualities and ability to add a touch of exotic beauty to any space.
