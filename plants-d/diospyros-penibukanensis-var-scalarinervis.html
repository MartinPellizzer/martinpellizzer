Diospyros penibukanensis var. scalarinervis

Diospyros penibukanensis var. scalarinervis, commonly known as the Bornean persimmon, is a species native to the rainforests of Borneo.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, grey bark and lanceolate leaves with prominent veins. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into edible, orange-red fruits. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant's botanical features make it an important component of the tropical ecosystems it inhabits.
