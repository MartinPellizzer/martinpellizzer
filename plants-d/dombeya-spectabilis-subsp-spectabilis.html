Dombeya spectabilis subsp. spectabilis

Dombeya spectabilis subsp.

spectabilis, commonly known as the white dombeya, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its showy, cup-shaped white flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring.

The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated margins and a smooth, greyish bark. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting flowers and compact growth habit.
