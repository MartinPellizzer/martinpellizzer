Diospyros sandwicensis var. toppingii

Diospyros sandwicensis var. toppingii, commonly known as the Koa 'Toppingii' or a variant of the Hawaiian koa tree, is a member of the Ebenaceae family.

It is native to the island of Hawaii and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and elliptical, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into edible, sweet, and fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mesic to wet forest habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical significance in Hawaiian culture.
