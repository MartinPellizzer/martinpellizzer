Dipterocarpus apterus

Dipterocarpus apterus, commonly known as the white ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hard, heavy wood and is often found in lowland dipterocarp forests. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth margin. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, and its fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds.

This species plays a significant role in the ecological dynamics of its habitat due to its longevity and structural importance.
