Dictyopteris bolsteri

Dictyopteris bolsteri, commonly known as the New Zealand sea holly, is a species of brown algae belonging to the family Dictyopteridaceae.

It is characterized by its branched, blade-like thallus that is typically dark green to brown in color. The plant grows in shallow coastal waters, often attached to rocks or other substrates. It has a complex life cycle involving both asexual and sexual reproduction.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various marine organisms.
