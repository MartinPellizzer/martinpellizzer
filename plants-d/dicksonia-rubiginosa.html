Dicksonia rubiginosa

Dicksonia rubiginosa, commonly known as the rusty hickory fern, is a species of tree fern native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, trunk-like stem covered with a thick, fibrous layer of old fronds. The plant produces large, feather-like fronds that are typically light green to brownish in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

The fronds are arranged in a spiral pattern around the trunk, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
