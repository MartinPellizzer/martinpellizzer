Dioscorea anchietae

Dioscorea anchietae, commonly known as the Angola yam, is a climbing vine native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary part used for culinary and medicinal purposes. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by orange-red berries.

Its tubers are valued for their starchy content and have been traditionally used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
