Dianthus seguieri var. gerundensis

Dianthus seguieri var. gerundensis, commonly known as the alpine campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of pink or white flowers with prominent white markings. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. It is often found in alpine meadows and is adapted to cold climates.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine plant collections.
