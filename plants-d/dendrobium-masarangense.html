Dendrobium masarangense

Dendrobium masarangense, commonly known as the Masarangense orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its tall, cane-like pseudobulbs and clusters of fragrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Its leaves are strap-shaped and often covered in a waxy cuticle, which helps reduce water loss.

Dendrobium masarangense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its long-lasting blooms.
