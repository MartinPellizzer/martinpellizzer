Delphinium dasystachyon var. longibracteatum

Delphinium dasystachyon var. longibracteatum, commonly known as the long-bract delphinium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant produces dense clusters of blue to purple flowers, which are arranged in racemes and are characteristic of the Delphinium genus. Its leaves are lanceolate, with a rough texture and a distinctive blue-green hue.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
