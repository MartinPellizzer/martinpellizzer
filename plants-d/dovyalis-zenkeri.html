Dovyalis zenkeri

Dovyalis zenkeri, commonly known as the Cape gooseberry or physalis, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, inflated calyx that encloses the fruit, resembling a small, lantern-like structure. The plant produces small, yellow-orange berries that are edible and often used in culinary applications. It thrives in warm, sunny climates and is relatively drought-tolerant.

The botanical features of Dovyalis zenkeri make it a unique and ornamental addition to gardens and landscapes.
