Dyckia weddelliana

Dyckia weddelliana, commonly known as the Weddell's dyckia, is a species of bromeliad native to South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are typically blue-green in color, with a waxy coating that helps reduce water loss. The plant produces striking inflorescences that consist of bright yellow flowers, which bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Dyckia weddelliana is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
