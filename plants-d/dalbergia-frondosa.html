Dalbergia frondosa

Dalbergia frondosa, commonly known as the greenheart tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and its ability to thrive in humid, tropical climates. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets that give it a lush, green appearance. It is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its hard, durable wood.

The botanical features of Dalbergia frondosa make it an important species in both ecological and economic contexts.
