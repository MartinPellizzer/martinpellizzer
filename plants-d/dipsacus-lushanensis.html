Dipsacus lushanensis

Dipsacus lushanensis, commonly known as the Lushan teasel, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the genus Dipsacus, which includes several species known for their spiny bracts. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and small, inconspicuous flowers. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The fruit is a bur-like structure that aids in seed dispersal by attaching to the fur of animals or clothing.
