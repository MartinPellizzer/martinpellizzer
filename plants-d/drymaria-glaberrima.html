Drymaria glaberrima

Drymaria glaberrima, commonly known as the smooth drymaria, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and features small, white or pinkish flowers. The plant typically thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as dunes or coastal areas. Its leaves are usually smooth and fleshy, adapted to reduce water loss in arid environments.

The species is native to regions in Africa and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing sandy soils.
