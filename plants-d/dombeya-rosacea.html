Dombeya rosacea

Dombeya rosacea, commonly known as the pink dombeya, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, pink or white flower clusters that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and a dense, compact growth habit. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Dombeya rosacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticultural landscapes for its long-lasting floral display.
