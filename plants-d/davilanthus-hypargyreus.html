Davilanthus hypargyreus

Davilanthus hypargyreus, commonly known as the golden crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers with prominent central disk. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats and is known for its ornamental value. It features narrow, linear leaves and produces fluffy, seed-filled heads that are dispersed by the wind.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
