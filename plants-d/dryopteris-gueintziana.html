Dryopteris gueintziana

Dryopteris gueintziana, commonly known as the Gueintziana wood fern, is a species native to the temperate regions of South America.

It belongs to the Dryopteridaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are typically bi- or tri-pinnate. The plant prefers moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its spores are released from the sori, which are located on the underside of the fronds.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in maintaining forest biodiversity.
