Draba ciliaris

Draba ciliaris, commonly known as the fringed rockcress, is a small flowering plant native to alpine and arctic regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in cold, high-altitude environments. The plant has basal leaves that are lobed or divided, and its flowers are small, white or pale yellow, with four petals. It is part of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbages.

Draba ciliaris is often found in mountainous areas and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.
