Dendrochilum erosum

Dendrochilum erosum, commonly known as the slender-stemmed orchid, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, using its aerial roots to absorb moisture and nutrients from the environment. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to greenish-white, with intricate patterns that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Dendrochilum erosum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
