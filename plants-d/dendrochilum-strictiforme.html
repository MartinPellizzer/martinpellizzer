Dendrochilum strictiforme

Dendrochilum strictiforme, commonly known as the rigid-stemmed orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a distinctive, upright stem that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that are often fragrant. Its leaves are elongated and grass-like, typically arranged in a rosette pattern.

Dendrochilum strictiforme is valued in horticulture for its unique appearance and adaptability to various growing conditions.
