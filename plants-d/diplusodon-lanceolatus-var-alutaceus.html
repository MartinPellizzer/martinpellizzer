Diplusodon lanceolatus var. alutaceus

Diplusodon lanceolatus var. alutaceus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved diplusodon, is a variant of the diplusodon genus native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lanceolate, narrow leaves that are typically glaucous in appearance, giving them a waxy, bluish sheen. The plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its leaves are arranged alternately along the stem, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often overlooked.
