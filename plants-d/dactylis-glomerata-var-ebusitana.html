Dactylis glomerata var. ebusitana

Dactylis glomerata var. ebusitana, commonly known as the Ebusitana grass, is a variant of the common sheep's fescue.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces spikelets that are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences, giving it a distinctive appearance. It is native to southern Europe and is adapted to dry, rocky soils.

This variety is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in challenging conditions.
