Dianthus × ponsi

Dianthus × ponsi, commonly known as the pinks, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to white and red. The plant typically features hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated edge. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Dianthus × ponsi is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.
