Dichanthelium acuminatum var. fasciculatum

Dichanthelium acuminatum var. fasciculatum, commonly known as the narrowleaf wiregrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has slender, grass-like blades with a distinctive narrow, linear shape and is often found in dense clusters. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

This variety is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
