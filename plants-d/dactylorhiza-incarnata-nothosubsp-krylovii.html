Dactylorhiza incarnata nothosubsp.

krylovii, commonly known as the Krylov's lady's-tongue, is a hybrid orchid species native to the northern hemisphere. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as bogs and marshes. It has a complex morphology with a pseudobulb structure and linear, grass-like leaves.
