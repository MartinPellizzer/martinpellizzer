Daubentonia thurberi

Daubentonia thurberi, commonly known as the Thurber's daisy, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, providing a striking contrast to its bright yellow flowers.

Daubentonia thurberi is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
