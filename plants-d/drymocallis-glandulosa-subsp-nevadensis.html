Drymocallis glandulosa subsp. nevadensis

Drymocallis glandulosa subsp.

nevadensis, commonly known as the Nevada goldenrod, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

The plant produces showy yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in dense, panicle-like inflorescences. Its leaves are lance-shaped, hairy, and often have glandular dots, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is adapted to arid conditions and plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
