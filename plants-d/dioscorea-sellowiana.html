Dioscorea sellowiana

Dioscorea sellowiana, commonly known as the Brazilian yam, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary edible part. The plant produces small, green, heart-shaped leaves and yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. Its tubers are often used in traditional medicine and as a food source.

The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, requiring well-drained soil and ample sunlight.
