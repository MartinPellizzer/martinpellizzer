Daucus communis var. timbalianus

Daucus communis var. timbalianus, commonly known as the Timbalian carrot, is a wild relative of the domesticated carrot.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. This plant features finely divided, feathery leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. Its root system consists of small, often branched roots that are not typically consumed by humans.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential as a genetic resource for breeding programs.
