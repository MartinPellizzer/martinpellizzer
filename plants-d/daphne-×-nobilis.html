Daphne × nobilis

Daphne × nobilis, commonly known as the common daphne, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves that provide ornamental value throughout the growing season. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

However, it is toxic to both humans and animals, requiring careful handling and placement in gardens.
