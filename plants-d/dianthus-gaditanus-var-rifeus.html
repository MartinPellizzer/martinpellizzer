Dianthus gaditanus var. rifeus

Dianthus gaditanus var. rifeus, commonly known as the purple campion, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and North Africa.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its striking pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, while the stems are erect and often branched.

It is a perennial species that is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
