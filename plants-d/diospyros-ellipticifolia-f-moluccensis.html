Diospyros ellipticifolia f. moluccensis

Diospyros ellipticifolia f. moluccensis, commonly known as the Moluccan persimmon, is a variant of the persimmon genus native to the Moluccas in Indonesia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves that are typically dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by edible, orange to reddish fruits that are often used in local cuisines. Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.

The plant thrives in tropical climates and is often found in humid, lowland forests.
