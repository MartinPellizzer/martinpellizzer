Daphne confusa

Daphne confusa, commonly known as the confusion daphne, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features fragrant, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has smooth, grayish bark and lance-shaped leaves that are opposite in arrangement. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

However, it is toxic to both humans and animals, requiring caution during handling and cultivation.
