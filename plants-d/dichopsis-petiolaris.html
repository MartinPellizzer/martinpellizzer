Dichopsis petiolaris

Dichopsis petiolaris, commonly known as the African buttercup, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asphodelaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are typically fleshy and covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer, adding a striking visual element to its appearance. Its adaptability to arid conditions makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

The leaves are often used in traditional medicine, highlighting its cultural and practical significance.
