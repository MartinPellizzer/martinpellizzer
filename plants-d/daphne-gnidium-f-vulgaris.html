Daphne gnidium f. vulgaris

Daphne gnidium f. vulgaris, commonly known as the common daphne, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features woody stems and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant has small, leathery leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

However, it is toxic to both humans and animals, requiring careful handling.
