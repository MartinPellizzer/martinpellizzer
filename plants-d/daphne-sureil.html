Daphne sureil

Daphne sureil, commonly known as the snowdrop, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that typically bloom in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces a fragrant, white or pale yellow blossom. It thrives in woodland areas and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Daphne sureil is often cultivated for its ornamental value and early blooming habit.
