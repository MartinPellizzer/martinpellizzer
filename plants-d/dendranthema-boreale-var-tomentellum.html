Dendranthema boreale var. tomentellum

Dendranthema boreale var. tomentellum, commonly known as the white leaved chamomile, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its finely hairy, silver-gray leaves and small, daisy-like white flowers with yellow centers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders. Its aromatic leaves and flowers are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their calming and anti-inflammatory properties.

The variety is distinguished by its dense, woolly foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance.
