Diflugossa paupera

Diflugossa paupera, commonly known as the poor diflugossa, is a rare and delicate moss species belonging to the family Diflugossaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, creeping stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are often slightly curved. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its reproductive structures, known as capsules, are borne on short stalks and release spores when mature.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, Diflugossa paupera is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
