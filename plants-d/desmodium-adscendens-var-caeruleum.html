Desmodium adscendens var. caeruleum

Desmodium adscendens var. caeruleum, commonly known as blue burr legume, is a perennial leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces distinctive blue or purple pod-like fruits that contain seeds with a hard, burred surface. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or disturbed areas.

Due to its ornamental value and nitrogen-fixing ability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and for soil improvement.
