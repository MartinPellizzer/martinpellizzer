Dalbergia hupeana

Dalbergia hupeana, commonly known as the Chinese rosewood, is a deciduous tree native to China and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, showy flowers. The tree produces hard, dark wood that is highly valued for its density and aesthetic qualities. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental and commercial value, it is often cultivated in gardens and for timber production.
