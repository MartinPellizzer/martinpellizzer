Diplophragma tetrangularis

Diplophragma tetrangularis, commonly known as the African violet, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces small, showy flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, fuzzy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

The flowers are typically borne on short, erect stems and are known for their delicate appearance and fragrance.
