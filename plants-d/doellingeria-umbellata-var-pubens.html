Doellingeria umbellata var. pubens

Doellingeria umbellata var. pubens, commonly known as the pubescent thistle, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its dense, spiny leaves covered in fine, fuzzy hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a cluster, which are typically visited by bees and other pollinators. Its growth habit is often shrub-like, with multiple stems arising from the base.

The pubescent varietal name refers to the hairy texture of its foliage, a distinguishing feature in its botanical description.
