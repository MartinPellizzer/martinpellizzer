Dendrocalamus buar

Dendrocalamus buar, commonly known as the Buar bamboo, is a tall, fast-growing grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that can reach heights of up to 15 meters. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically green on the upper surface and lighter in color on the underside. It thrives in moist, tropical climates and is often found in lowland areas with rich alluvial soils.

Dendrocalamus buar is valued for its strong, durable culms, which are used in construction and handicrafts.
