Delphinium simplex

Delphinium simplex, commonly known as the common larkspur, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that bear clusters of blue to purple, spurred flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are alternate and often have a rough texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Delphinium simplex is also noted for its toxicity, containing compounds that can be harmful to both humans and animals.
