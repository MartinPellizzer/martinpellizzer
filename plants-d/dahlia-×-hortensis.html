Dahlia × hortensis

Dahlia × hortensis, commonly known as the garden dahlia, is a hybrid perennial plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, colorful flowers that come in a wide range of hues. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, with tuberous roots that serve as storage organs for nutrients. Its foliage consists of heart-shaped, dark green leaves, and it thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.

Dahlia × hortensis is prized for its ornamental value and is widely cultivated in gardens around the world.
