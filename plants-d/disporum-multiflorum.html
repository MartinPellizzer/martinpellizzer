Disporum multiflorum

Disporum multiflorum, commonly known as the western yew, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Taxaceae, which includes other yew species. The plant is characterized by its slender, arching branches and small, clustered yellow-green flowers that appear in early spring. Its foliage consists of needle-like leaves that turn reddish in the fall.

The plant is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and streamside areas.
