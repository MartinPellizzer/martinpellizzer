Dianthus praecox

Dianthus praecox, commonly known as the early carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant produces small, rounded seed pods that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Dianthus praecox typically blooms in early spring and is often found in meadows and rocky soils.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained conditions.
