Dendrobium zollingerianum

Dendrobium zollingerianum, commonly known as the Zollinger's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with elongated, cylindrical pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, showy flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically during the summer months. Its leaves are broad and grass-like, often arranged in two rows along the pseudobulbs.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
