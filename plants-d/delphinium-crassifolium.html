Delphinium crassifolium

Delphinium crassifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers in racemes, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, with a distinctive waxy coating.

Delphinium crassifolium thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in meadows and open woodlands.
