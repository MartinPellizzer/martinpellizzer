Diplachne fusca var. fascicularis

Diplachne fusca var. fascicularis, commonly known as the narrowleaf flat sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to parts of Australia.

It typically grows in wetland or riparian habitats, preferring moist soils and full sun to partial shade. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a fibrous root system, and its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are arranged in a loose, cylindrical cluster. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates in its native environment.

Due to its preference for wet conditions, it is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies.
