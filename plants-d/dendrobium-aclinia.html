Dendrobium aclinia

Dendrobium aclinia, commonly known as the Bornean orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a thick, fleshy rhizome that stores water and nutrients. The plant produces numerous linear, grass-like leaves and bears clusters of fragrant, yellow-green flowers. It thrives in shaded, humid environments with well-drained soil.

Dendrobium aclinia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
