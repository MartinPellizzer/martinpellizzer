Daphniphyllum macropodum f. variegatum

Daphniphyllum macropodum f. variegatum, commonly known as the variegated daphniphyllum, is a unique evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, variegated foliage that features creamy-white to yellow markings on dark green leaves. The plant produces small, bell-shaped white flowers in late spring, followed by red berries in the autumn. It prefers cool, humid conditions and partial shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden settings.

Its ornamental value comes from its foliage and seasonal interest, making it a prized addition to botanical collections.
