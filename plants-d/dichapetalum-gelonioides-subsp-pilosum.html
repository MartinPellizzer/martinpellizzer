Dichapetalum gelonioides subsp. pilosum

Dichapetalum gelonioides subsp.

pilosum, commonly known as the silky dichapetalum, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that give it a distinctive silvery appearance.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are often borne on short pedicels. Its growth habit is typically compact, with multiple stems arising from the base.

The species is adapted to a variety of soil types and can thrive in both full sun and partial shade.
