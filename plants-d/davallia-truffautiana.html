Davallia truffautiana

Davallia truffautiana, commonly known as the Truffaut's chain fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, pinnate fronds that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces spore-bearing clusters called sori, which are typically found on the underside of the fronds. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its unique appearance and elegant structure make it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
