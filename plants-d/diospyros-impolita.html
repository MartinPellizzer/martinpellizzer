Diospyros impolita

Diospyros impolita, commonly known as the rough-shelled persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that give way to orange-red berries, which are edible when fully ripe. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.

The fruit is valued for its sweet, astringent flavor and is used in traditional medicine and culinary practices.
