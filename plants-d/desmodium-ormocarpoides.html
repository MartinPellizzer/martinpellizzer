Desmodium ormocarpoides

Desmodium ormocarpoides, commonly known as the velvet bean or cupid's bow, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive, ornate seed pods that often resemble a bow. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It has a deep root system that helps prevent soil erosion and improves soil fertility through nitrogen fixation.

Desmodium ormocarpoides is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a forage crop.
