Diospyros terminalis

Diospyros terminalis, commonly known as the African persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other persimmon and ebony species. The tree is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape. Its small, yellowish flowers are followed by edible, orange-red berries that are highly valued for their flavor and nutritional content.

The plant is also appreciated for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its fruit in home gardens.
