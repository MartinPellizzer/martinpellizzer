Diplostephium cornifolium

Diplostephium cornifolium, commonly known as the horned grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, sharply pointed leaf blades that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in wetlands and marshy areas, thriving in moist, acidic soils. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are small, compact flower clusters.

The botanical features of Diplostephium cornifolium make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
