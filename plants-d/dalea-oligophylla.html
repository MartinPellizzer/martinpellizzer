Dalea oligophylla

Dalea oligophylla, commonly known as the slender milkvetch, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats, such as grasslands and sagebrush steppe. Its leaves are compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.

Dalea oligophylla is adapted to arid conditions and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native environment.
