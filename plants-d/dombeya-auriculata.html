Dombeya auriculata

Dombeya auriculata, commonly known as the auriculate dombeya, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in early spring. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces, and its stems are often covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

The flowers are attractive to pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
