Diplachne virens

Diplachne virens, commonly known as the slender flat sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to Australia.

It typically grows in wetland and swampy environments, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. Diplachne virens plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.

Its presence is often an indicator of healthy, undisturbed wetland ecosystems.
