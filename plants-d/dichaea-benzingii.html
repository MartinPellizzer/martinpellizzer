Dichaea benzingii

Dichaea benzingii, commonly known as the Benzing's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and delicate, showy flowers. The plant typically produces small, greenish-white or yellowish flowers with intricate patterns on the lip. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Dichaea benzingii is considered a rare and endangered species.
