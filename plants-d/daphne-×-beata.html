Daphne × beata

Daphne × beata, commonly known as the sweet spurge or winter daphne, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Thymelaeaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form with glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, white, and red, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. Its ornamental value comes from both its striking foliage and its delicate, aromatic blooms.

Daphne × beata is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
