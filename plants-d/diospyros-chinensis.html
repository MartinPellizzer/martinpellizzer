Diospyros chinensis

Diospyros chinensis, commonly known as the Chinese persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into orange to red, fleshy fruits. The fruit is typically sweet and astringent when unripe, becoming soft and sweet when fully mature.

It is valued for its edible fruit and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes as well.
