Diospyros ferrea f. obovata

Diospyros ferrea f. obovata, commonly known as the Brazilian persimmon, is a variant of the persimmon tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its broad, obovate leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that give way to orange to red, fleshy fruits with a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and edible fruit, making it a popular choice in horticulture.
