Dianthus wettsteinii

Dianthus wettsteinii, commonly known as the Wettstein's campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Dianthus wettsteinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
