Dianthus acrochlorus

Dianthus acrochlorus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its showy, pink or red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs, and its stems are usually erect.

Dianthus acrochlorus is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
