Dorstenia sychinium

Dorstenia sychinium, commonly known as the Madagascar spider plant, is a unique member of the Moraceae family.

It is native to Madagascar and is characterized by its distinctive, spiral-shaped leaves that resemble the legs of a spider. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by spherical, greenish fruit containing seeds. It is adapted to grow in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments.

Due to its unusual appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and interesting subject for botanical study.
