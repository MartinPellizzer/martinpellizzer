Dysophylla fauriei

Dysophylla fauriei, commonly known as the golden mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of host trees, primarily in tropical and subtropical regions.

It has small, oval-shaped leaves that are usually covered in a dense layer of fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by orange-red berries, which contain the seeds. Its roots form specialized structures called haustoria that penetrate the host's vascular system to extract water and nutrients.

Dysophylla fauriei plays a role in the ecological dynamics of its habitat by influencing host tree growth and providing food for certain bird species.
