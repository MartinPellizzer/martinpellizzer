Draba verna subsp. majuscula

Draba verna subsp.

majuscula, commonly known as the greater spring whitlow, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit.

The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in early spring, typically blooming before the leaves fully develop. Its leaves are basal, linear, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

This subspecies is adapted to cold, alpine environments and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils.
