Daviesia emarginata

Daviesia emarginata, commonly known as the Grey Box, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its greyish bark and narrow, leathery leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and open woodlands.

Daviesia emarginata is an important component of the local flora and provides habitat for various native fauna.
