Drepanocarpus spinosus

Drepanocarpus spinosus, commonly known as the spiny hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its woody structure, with thick, spiny stems that provide defense against herbivores. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that are typically pink or red in color. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a rough texture.

Drepanocarpus spinosus is often found in disturbed habitats and can thrive in poor soil conditions.
