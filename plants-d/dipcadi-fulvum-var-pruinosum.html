Dipcadi fulvum var. pruinosum

Dipcadi fulvum var. pruinosum, commonly known as the sticky yellow squill, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow, bell-shaped flowers during the spring. The plant's bulbs are often covered in a waxy, sticky substance, which gives it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable addition to rock gardens or borders.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine, it is cultivated in various horticultural settings.
