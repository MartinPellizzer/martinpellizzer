Daucus communis proles gadecaei

Daucus communis proles gadecaei, commonly known as the Gades daucus, is a variant of the common carrot.

It is a biennial plant native to the Iberian Peninsula, particularly Spain. The plant features a taproot that is typically smaller and less fleshy compared to the standard carrot. Its flowers are white or pink, arranged in umbels, and it produces small, dry fruits.

It is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, showing adaptability to various environmental conditions.
