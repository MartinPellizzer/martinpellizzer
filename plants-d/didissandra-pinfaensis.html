Didissandra pinfaensis

Didissandra pinfaensis, commonly known as the Chinese sand pear, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by edible, yellow-orange fruits. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

The fruit is rich in nutrients and has been used in traditional Chinese medicine.
