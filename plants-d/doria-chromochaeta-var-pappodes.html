Doria chromochaeta var. pappodes

Doria chromochaeta var. pappodes, commonly known as the hairy doria, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant is distinguished by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in clusters that attract pollinators.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.
