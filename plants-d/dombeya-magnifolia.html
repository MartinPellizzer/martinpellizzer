Dombeya magnifolia

Dombeya magnifolia, commonly known as the magnificent dombeya, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale pink blossoms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often covered in fine hairs.

Dombeya magnifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value.
