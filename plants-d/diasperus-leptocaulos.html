Diasperus leptocaulos

Diasperus leptocaulos, commonly known as the slender-stemmed grass, is a perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. It produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are often wind-pollinated.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
