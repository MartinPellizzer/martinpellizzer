Draba verna var. serrata

Draba verna var. serrata, commonly known as the serrated spring whitlow, is a low-growing flowering plant native to alpine and arctic regions.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its small, basal rosette of leaves and clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. The leaves are typically serrated, giving the plant its botanical variant name. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to cold climates.

This plant is often found in mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.
