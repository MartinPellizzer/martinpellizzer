Diospyros humbertiana

Diospyros humbertiana, commonly known as the Humbert persimmon, is a species of flowering plant in the Ebenaceae family.

It is native to Madagascar and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and large, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange-red fruits that are typically harvested when fully ripe. The fruit has a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor and is used in local cuisines.

It is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native habitat.
