Dioscorea martensis

Dioscorea martensis, commonly known as the Martens' yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary economically important part of the plant. The plant produces small, green, heart-shaped leaves and yellowish flowers that are typically unisexual. Its tubers are used as a food source in some regions and have been studied for their potential medicinal properties.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.
