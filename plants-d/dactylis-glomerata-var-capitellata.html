Dactylis glomerata var. capitellata

Dactylis glomerata var. capitellata, commonly known as the tall meadow grass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, spike-like inflorescences that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands, meadows, and pastures. It is valued for its forage quality and ability to form dense vegetation.

Its botanical features make it an important component of ecological and agricultural systems.
