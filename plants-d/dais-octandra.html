Dais octandra

Dais octandra, commonly known as the white dais or octandra, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its showy, white to pale yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with finely divided, aromatic leaves. Its flowers bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies, and it is often found in moist, shaded environments.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
