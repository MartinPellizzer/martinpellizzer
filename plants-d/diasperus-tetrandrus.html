Diasperus tetrandrus

Diasperus tetrandrus, commonly known as the four-threaded grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture.

Diasperus tetrandrus is often used in ornamental gardening due to its fine, delicate appearance.
