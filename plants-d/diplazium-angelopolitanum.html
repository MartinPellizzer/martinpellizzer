Diplazium angelopolitanum

Diplazium angelopolitanum, commonly known as the Angelopolitan fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds with a distinctive, finely divided structure. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The fronds are usually dark green and can reach lengths of up to 1 meter, making it a striking addition to tropical gardens.
