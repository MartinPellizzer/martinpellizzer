Dioscorea papuana

Dioscorea papuana, commonly known as the Papuan yam, is a species of tuberous plant native to the Pacific Islands, including Papua New Guinea and the Solomon Islands.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its underground tubers, which are the primary edible part of the plant. The plant typically grows in humid, tropical environments and thrives in well-drained, loamy soils. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and often have a rough texture, while its stems are climbing or creeping.

Dioscorea papuana is valued not only for its nutritional content but also for its traditional use in local cuisines and cultural practices.
