Disporum sessile var. flavens

Disporum sessile var. flavens, commonly known as the yellow-flowered disporum, is a variant of the disporum species native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and flies.

The leaves are usually narrow and linear, with a smooth texture and a slight sheen.
