Dierama dubium

Dierama dubium, commonly known as the mountain onion, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces tall, branched inflorescences of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats. It is adapted to dry conditions and can tolerate frost, making it a hardy species.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for study in arid ecosystems.
