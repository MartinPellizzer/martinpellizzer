Dodecatheon integrifolium

Dodecatheon integrifolium, commonly known as the smoothleaf glory-of-the-snow, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or meadow habitats. Its leaves are smooth and entire, giving it its common name, and it blooms in early spring.

Dodecatheon integrifolium is often found in USDA hardiness zones 3 through 7.
