Dicrocaulon brevifolium

Dicrocaulon brevifolium, commonly known as the short-leaved dicrocaulon, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and in wetlands. The plant features small, alternate leaves that are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating. Its flowers are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences and are usually pale green to yellowish in color.

Dicrocaulon brevifolium plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and microorganisms.
