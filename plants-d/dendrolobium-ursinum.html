Dendrolobium ursinum

Dendrolobium ursinum, commonly known as the bear's claw acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, hooked pods that resemble bear claws, giving it its common name. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are typically visited by a variety of pollinators. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of numerous small, oval leaflets.

Dendrolobium ursinum thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in heathlands and woodland areas.
