Disperis tysonii

Disperis tysonii, commonly known as the Tyson's orchid, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of East Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the moist soil.

Disperis tysonii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
