Dryadella ana-paulae

Dryadella ana-paulae, commonly known as the Ana-Paula's dryad, is a rare flowering plant native to the cloud forests of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue petals and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in the early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and is the subject of conservation efforts.
