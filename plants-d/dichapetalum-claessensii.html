Dichapetalum claessensii

Dichapetalum claessensii, commonly known as the African ghost flower, is a unique plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, pale pink to white flowers that resemble ghostly apparitions, hence its common name. The plant is a herbaceous perennial with a fibrous root system and a short, woody stem. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Dichapetalum claessensii thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
