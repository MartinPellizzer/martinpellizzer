Dipteracanthus cyaneus

Dipteracanthus cyaneus, commonly known as the blue bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet petals that resemble bells, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has a compact, herbaceous growth habit. Its flowers bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are usually alternate, ovate, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.
