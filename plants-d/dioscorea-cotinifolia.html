Dioscorea cotinifolia

Dioscorea cotinifolia, commonly known as the climbing yam, is a vine-like perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by cylindrical, tuberous roots that are often used in traditional medicine. Its growth is typically supported by tendrils that wrap around supporting structures.

Dioscorea cotinifolia is valued for its nutritional content and has been used in various cultural and medicinal practices.
