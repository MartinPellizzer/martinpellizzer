Draba sericea

Draba sericea, commonly known as silverweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant is characterized by its low-growing habit, with small, basal leaves covered in a dense layer of fine, silvery hairs. Its flowers are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in tight clusters, and it produces small, round pods that contain several seeds.

Draba sericea thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.
