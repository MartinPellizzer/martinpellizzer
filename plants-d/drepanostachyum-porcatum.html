Drepanostachyum porcatum

Drepanostachyum porcatum, commonly known as the porcate bamboo, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and dense foliage. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense clumps, making it suitable for ornamental and ecological landscaping.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.
