Dioscorea cambodiana

Dioscorea cambodiana, commonly known as Cambodian yam, is a vine species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and tuberous roots. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and elongated, cordate leaves. Its tubers are often used in traditional medicine and as a food source.

The botanical features of D. cambodiana make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
