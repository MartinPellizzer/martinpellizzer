Diplachne poiformis

Diplachne poiformis, commonly known as the flat-leaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its flat, narrow leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in a variety of habitats, including grasslands and disturbed soils. It has a fibrous root system and is often found in areas with moderate to high rainfall.

Diplachne poiformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticultural settings.
