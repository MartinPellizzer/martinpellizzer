Diuris daltonii

Diuris daltonii, commonly known as the Dalton's spider-orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Western Australia.

It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is found in areas with seasonal rainfall. The plant features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces striking, spider-like flowers with a distinctive yellow and green coloration. Its flowering period occurs during the warmer months, usually between September and November.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by enthusiasts due to its unique appearance.
