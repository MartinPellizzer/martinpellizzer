Dianthus × digeneus

Dianthus × digeneus, commonly known as the double carnation, is a hybrid plant that combines the characteristics of various Dianthus species.

It is a short, compact plant with dense clusters of double flowers, typically in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant produces showy, ruffled petals that give it a lush, ornamental appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Dianthus × digeneus is known for its long blooming period, adding color to gardens from early summer through fall.
