Dichanthelium dichotomum subsp. roanokense

Dichanthelium dichotomum subsp.

roanokense, commonly known as Roanoke dropseed, is a grass species native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and swales. Its leaves are often hairless and have a distinct, narrow shape.

Roanoke dropseed is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in wetland ecosystems.
