Dalbergia perrieri

Dalbergia perrieri, commonly known as the Madagascar rosewood, is a member of the Fabaceae family and is native to Madagascar.

It is a small to medium-sized tree characterized by its dark, dense wood and aromatic scent. The plant features bipinnate leaves and produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are followed by legumes containing compressed seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in moist forests.

Due to its high demand for musical instruments and furniture, it is also classified as an endangered species.
