Drypetes alba var. brevipes

Drypetes alba var. brevipes, commonly known as the short-stemmed white drypetes, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, and it is often found in lowland rainforests.
