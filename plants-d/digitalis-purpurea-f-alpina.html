Digitalis purpurea f. alpina

Digitalis purpurea f. alpina, commonly known as the Alpine foxglove, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features tall, slender stems that bear upright, tubular flowers in shades of purple and pink. The leaves are dark green, velvety, and have a distinctive heart-shaped base. It thrives in well-drained, alkaline soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine.
