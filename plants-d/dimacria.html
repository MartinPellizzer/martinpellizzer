Dimacria

Dimacria, commonly known as the African orchid, is a genus of flowering plants native to southern Africa.

These plants are characterized by their showy, often fragrant flowers that come in a variety of colors, including white, yellow, and purple. Dimacria species typically grow as terrestrial herbs or small shrubs, with strap-like leaves that are often hairy. They prefer well-drained soils and are often found in grasslands or along riverbanks.

The genus is part of the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is valued for its ornamental beauty.
