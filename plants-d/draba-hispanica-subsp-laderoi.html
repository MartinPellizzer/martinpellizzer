Draba hispanica subsp. laderoi

Draba hispanica subsp.

laderoi, commonly known as the Lader's whitlow grass, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is a small, herbaceous perennial with basal rosettes of narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers in early spring. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and coastal areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies in Mediterranean ecosystems.
