Dasiphora valderia

Dasiphora valderia, commonly known as the valley strawberry.

It is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America, particularly in Argentina and Chile. The plant features a low-growing habit with trifoliate leaves and produces small, pinkish-white flowers in late spring. Its fruits are edible, resembling small strawberries, and are often used in local cuisines.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
