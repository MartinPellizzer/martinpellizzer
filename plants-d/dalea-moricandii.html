Dalea moricandii

Dalea moricandii, commonly known as the Moricandii milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It has small, compound leaves and produces small, elongated seed pods.

Dalea moricandii is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting pollinators in its native habitat.
