Delphinium officinale

Delphinium officinale, commonly known as the common larkspur, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are usually arranged alternately along the stem. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, which includes other notable genera like Ranunculus and Aquilegia.

Delphinium officinale is often found in meadows, grasslands, and woodland edges, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
