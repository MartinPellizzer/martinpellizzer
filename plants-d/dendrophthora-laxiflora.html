Dendrophthora laxiflora

Dendrophthora laxiflora, commonly known as the silky mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of specific host trees, primarily in the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It possesses specialized structures called haustoria that penetrate the host's vascular system to extract water and nutrients. The plant is characterized by its small, white, fleshy berries that are often covered in a silky, waxy coating. Its leaves are usually small, opposite, and have a leathery texture.

Despite its parasitic nature, Dendrophthora laxiflora plays a role in the ecological balance of its habitat by providing food for certain birds and insects.
