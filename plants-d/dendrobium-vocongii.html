Dendrobium vocongii

Dendrobium vocongii, commonly known as the Vacong orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, cane-like stems that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, yellowish-green flowers with white markings, typically blooming during the rainy season. It thrives in humid, warm environments with bright, indirect light.

Dendrobium vocongii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for horticultural purposes.
