Draba subumbellata

Draba subumbellata, commonly known as the alpine mouse-ear cress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a small, herbaceous annual or biennial species belonging to the Brassicaceae family. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils and cool climates. Its flowers are small, white or pale yellow, and arranged in a cluster at the top of the stem.

Draba subumbellata is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and is often found in high-altitude environments.
