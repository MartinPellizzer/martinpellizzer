Disa dracomontana

Disa dracomontana, commonly known as the dragon's blood orchid, is a striking South African orchid species belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its vivid red and orange flowers, which resemble bloodstains, giving it its common name. The plant is a terrestrial orchid with a rhizomatous root system and produces dense clusters of showy, tubular flowers. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions of South Africa.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and protected species.
