Dorotheanthus stayneri

Dorotheanthus stayneri, commonly known as the purple daisy, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant purple flowers with yellow centers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 10 to 15 inches and has narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its compact size and showy blooms make it an excellent addition to borders and containers.
