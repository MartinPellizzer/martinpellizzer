Dudleya virens subsp. extima

Dudleya virens subsp.

extima, commonly known as the Sierra Valley Dudleya, is a succulent plant native to California. It is characterized by its compact rosette of thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color.

The plant produces tall, branched flowering stalks that bear clusters of yellow flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
