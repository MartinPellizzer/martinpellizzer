Dracocephalum albertii

Dracocephalum albertii, commonly known as Albert's dragonhead, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, arranged in whorls along the stem. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Dracocephalum albertii is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in temperate climates.
