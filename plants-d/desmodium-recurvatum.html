Desmodium recurvatum

Desmodium recurvatum, commonly known as the sicklepod or velvet bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellow to orange flowers that are followed by pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It has a sprawling growth habit and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Desmodium recurvatum is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it a valuable plant in soil enrichment.
