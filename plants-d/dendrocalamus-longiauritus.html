Dendrocalamus longiauritus

Dendrocalamus longiauritus, commonly known as the long-tailed bamboo, is a tall, fast-growing grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its robust, straight culms that can reach heights of up to 20 meters. The plant has narrow, linear leaves with a smooth texture and is often found in humid, tropical climates. It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Due to its strong and lightweight structure, it is also valued for commercial uses such as construction and handicrafts.
