Dicliptera maculata var. panicularis

Dicliptera maculata var. panicularis, commonly known as the spotted grass or panicled dicliptera, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and distinctive, often hairy leaves with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces dense, paniculate inflorescences that are typically yellow-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands or along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of ecological systems in its native habitats.
