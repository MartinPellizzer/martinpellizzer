Diplotaxis catholica var. maroccana

Diplotaxis catholica var. maroccana, commonly known as the Moroccan wallflower, is a flowering plant native to North Africa.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant is characterized by its clustered yellow flowers and linear, hairy leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
