Decachaeta incompta

Decachaeta incompta, commonly known as the narrowleaf dandelion, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant features linear, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that develop into fluffy white seed heads. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Decachaeta incompta is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic effects.
