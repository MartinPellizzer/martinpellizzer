Desmodium sequax

Desmodium sequax, commonly known as the slender cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces pods containing edible seeds that are often used in traditional cuisines. Desmodium sequax is also valued for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.

Its growth habit and adaptability make it a useful plant in agroforestry and pasture systems.
