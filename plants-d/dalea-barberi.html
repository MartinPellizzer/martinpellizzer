Dalea barberi

Dalea barberi, commonly known as the plains milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces bright yellow or orange flowers in late spring. It is well adapted to arid environments and has a deep root system for water absorption.

Dalea barberi plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
