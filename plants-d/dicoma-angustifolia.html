Dicoma angustifolia

Dicoma angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved dicoma, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It is often found in coastal areas and is known for its resilience to drought.

Dicoma angustifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
