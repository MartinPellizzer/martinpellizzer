Dracunculus vulgaris subsp. creticus

Dracunculus vulgaris subsp.

creticus, commonly known as the Cretan monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features striking, helmet-shaped blue to purple flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant has dark green, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive veining pattern. It grows up to 1.5 meters tall and is often found in moist, shady woodland areas.

The Cretan monkshood is known for its toxicity, containing various alkaloids that can be harmful if ingested.
