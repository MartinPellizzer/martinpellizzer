Delphinium tricorne var. genuinum

Delphinium tricorne var. genuinum, commonly known as the Alpine larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It features tall, spindly stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height, adorned with clusters of blue to purple flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its rugged appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or mountainous habitats.

The plant is noted for its ornamental value and is cultivated for its striking floral display.
