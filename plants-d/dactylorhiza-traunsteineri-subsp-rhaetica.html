Dactylorhiza traunsteineri subsp. rhaetica

Dactylorhiza traunsteineri subsp.

rhaetica, commonly known as the Rhaetian marsh orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to alpine regions of Europe. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in mountainous areas, often near streams or in wet meadows.

The plant produces tall, slender stems with a single, showy flower that is usually pink or purple in color. It has a complex root system and is adapted to cool, humid environments.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological significance in alpine ecosystems and its role in supporting pollinators.
