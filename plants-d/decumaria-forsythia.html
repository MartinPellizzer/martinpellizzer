Decumaria forsythia

Decumaria forsythia, commonly known as the shrub cinquefoil, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features compound leaves with five to seven elliptical leaflets, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in early spring, which attract pollinators. Its stems are often covered in fine, soft hairs, and it has a spreading growth habit.

Decumaria forsythia is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in partial shade.
