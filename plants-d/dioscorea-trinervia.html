Dioscorea trinervia

Dioscorea trinervia, commonly known as the arrowhead yam, is a climbing vine belonging to the family Dioscoreaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia, where it thrives in humid, warm climates. The plant produces tuberous roots that are rich in starch and have been traditionally used as a food source. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a distinctive three-veined pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

The plant is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
