Deyeuxia neglecta var. scotica

Deyeuxia neglecta var. scotica, commonly known as Scottish false oatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the British Isles.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, marshes, and along riverbanks. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in wet environments and its ecological importance in supporting biodiversity.

Its botanical features make it a notable component of wetland ecosystems.
