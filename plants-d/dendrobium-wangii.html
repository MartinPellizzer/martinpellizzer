Dendrobium wangii

Dendrobium wangii, commonly known as the Wangi orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dendrobium genus, which includes over 1,500 species of orchids, many of which are prized for their ornamental value. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, using its aerial roots to absorb moisture and nutrients from the environment. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, often displaying a range of colors from white to pink and yellow.

Dendrobium wangii is known for its ability to thrive in humid tropical climates and requires well-draining soil and indirect sunlight for optimal growth.
