Descurainia argentina var. brachysiliqua

Descurainia argentina var. brachysiliqua, commonly known as the short-silique Argentine tansy, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant features small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes and has pinnately divided leaves. Its fruit is a short silique, a type of dry pod that splits open to release seeds.

It is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.
