Dysphania pumilio

Dysphania pumilio, commonly known as dwarf lamb's quarters, is a low-growing annual herb native to the Americas.

It features alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly rough texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that cluster in loose, branched inflorescences. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in gardens, fields, and along roadsides.

Dysphania pumilio is a nutritious plant, rich in minerals and often used in traditional cuisines.
