Dipterocarpus nobilis

Dipterocarpus nobilis, commonly known as the white ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, glossy leaves and produces fragrant, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree's fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds. It is valued for its hard, heavy wood, which is used in construction and furniture-making.

Dipterocarpus nobilis plays an important ecological role in forest ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
