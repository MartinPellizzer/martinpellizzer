Dialesta discolor var. polychaeta

Dialesta discolor var. polychaeta, commonly known as the variegated dandelion, is a variant of the common dandelion characterized by its distinct leaf patterns and flower structure.

It is an annual herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family, native to parts of Europe and Asia. The plant features alternate, heart-shaped leaves with a waxy coating and bright yellow composite flowers that develop into fluffy white seed heads. Its botanical features include a taproot system and the ability to thrive in disturbed soils, making it a common weed in gardens and fields.

The variegation in its leaves is a notable trait that distinguishes this variant from the typical D. discolor.
