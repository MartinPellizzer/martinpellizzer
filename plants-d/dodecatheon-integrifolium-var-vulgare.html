Dodecatheon integrifolium var. vulgare

Dodecatheon integrifolium var. vulgare, commonly known as the common white trillium, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features a single, showy white flower with three petals and three sepals, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has three broad, lance-shaped leaves that are smooth on the edges. It thrives in moist, rich woodland soils and is often found in shaded forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
