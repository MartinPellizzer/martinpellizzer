Dendrobium jonesii

Dendrobium jonesii, commonly known as the Jones' orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and clusters of fragrant, yellow-green flowers. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, typical of many orchid species. It thrives in humid, shaded environments with well-draining soil.

Dendrobium jonesii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for its striking floral display.
