Dendrophthora grisebachii

Dendrophthora grisebachii, commonly known as the gray-stemmed bracket fungus, is a species of bracket fungus found in various forested regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive grayish-brown cap and a fleshy, woody stipe. This fungus typically grows on dead or decaying wood, playing a vital role in the decomposition process within forest ecosystems. Its spore-producing structure, known as a basidiocarp, is essential for its reproductive cycle.

Dendrophthora grisebachii is an important indicator of healthy, mature forest environments.
