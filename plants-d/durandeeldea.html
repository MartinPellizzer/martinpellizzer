Durandeeldea

Durandeeldea, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a striking perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it a distinctive appearance. Durandeeldea thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine landscapes.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
