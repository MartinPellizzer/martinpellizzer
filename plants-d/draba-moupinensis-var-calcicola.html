Draba moupinensis var. calcicola

Draba moupinensis var. calcicola, commonly known as the calcicola draba, is a flowering plant native to high-altitude regions of China.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and low-growing habit. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils, often found in alpine meadows and mountainous areas. Its foliage consists of basal rosettes of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces yellow or white flowers in early spring.

This variety is notable for its adaptation to harsh, cold environments and is of interest to botanists studying alpine flora.
