Dicoma anomala subsp. cirsioides

Dicoma anomala subsp.

cirsioides, commonly known as the coastal dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is a perennial herb characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, silver-gray leaves.

The plant produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in spring, followed by fluffy white seeds for wind dispersal. It thrives in coastal and sandy environments, showing adaptations to salt and drought.

Its ecological role includes supporting pollinators and stabilizing coastal soils.
