Dierama argyreum

Dierama argyreum, commonly known as the silverleaf dierama, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features striking, fern-like leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery-white coating, which gives it its common name. The plant produces tall, slender spikes of small, pale blue to purple flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Dierama argyreum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
