Dichilus gracilis

Dichilus gracilis, commonly known as the slender dichilus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a small shrub that typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the winter season. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it is often found in coastal fynbos vegetation.

Dichilus gracilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
