Dactyloctenium radulans var. conglobatum

Dactyloctenium radulans var. conglobatum, commonly known as the globular grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its compact, dense growth habit and produces small, spherical seed heads. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides. It is adapted to warm climates and can thrive in poor soil conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and agricultural research.
