Dioscoreophyllum jollyanum

Dioscoreophyllum jollyanum, commonly known as the African yam bean, is a climbing plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its twining stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along riverbanks.

Its tubers, similar to yams, are edible and have been used traditionally for food and medicinal purposes.
