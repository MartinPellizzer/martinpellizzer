Dianthera heterophyla

Dianthera heterophyla, commonly known as the pink rock orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, leafy stems and clusters of small, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers have three sepals and three petals.

Dianthera heterophyla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
