Dypsis simianensis

Dypsis simianensis, commonly known as the Madagascar palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to Madagascar and is characterized by its distinctive, columnar trunk and dense, spiny foliage. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by bright orange or red fruits. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.

Dypsis simianensis thrives in warm, sunny environments with well-draining soil.
