Delphinium helleri

Delphinium helleri, commonly known as the Heller's larkspur, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers in dense clusters at the top of the stem, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Delphinium helleri is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and is also noted for its toxicity to livestock.
