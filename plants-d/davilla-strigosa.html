Davilla strigosa

Davilla strigosa, commonly known as the hairy sycamore, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, followed by orange to red berries that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed areas.

Davilla strigosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native landscapes.
