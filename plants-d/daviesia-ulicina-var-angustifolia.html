Daviesia ulicina var. angustifolia

Daviesia ulicina var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrow-leafed daviesia, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers in late summer, which are clustered in dense, cylindrical spikes. It is typically found in heathlands and open forests, often on sandy or rocky soils.

This species is well adapted to dry conditions and is an important component of the local flora.
