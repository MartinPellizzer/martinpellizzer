Dorema elymaitica

Dorema elymaitica, commonly known as the Elymaitic fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, which are adapted for wind pollination. It is well-suited to harsh environments due to its deep root system and drought tolerance.

Dorema elymaitica plays a role in stabilizing soils and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
