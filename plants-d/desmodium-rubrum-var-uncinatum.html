Desmodium rubrum var. uncinatum

Desmodium rubrum var. uncinatum, commonly known as the hooked desmodium, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a legume, belonging to the Fabaceae family, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several elliptical, kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, ditches, and along stream banks.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it an important plant in ecological restoration projects.
