Derwentia suaveolens

Derwentia suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet-scented fern, is a species of fern native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that emit a subtle, pleasant fragrance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Derwentia suaveolens is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
