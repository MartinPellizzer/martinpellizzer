Declieuxia glauca var. subdecurrens

Declieuxia glauca var. subdecurrens, commonly known as the silverleaf reed, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It features slender, cylindrical stems that are covered in a fine, silvery bloom, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in dense clusters, typically during the warmer months. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly hairy texture.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its elegant, upright growth habit and frost-resistant nature.
