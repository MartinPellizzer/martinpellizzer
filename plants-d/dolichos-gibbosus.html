Dolichos gibbosus

Dolichos gibbosus, commonly known as the sickle bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces distinctive, curved pods that resemble a sickle. The plant is characterized by its yellow flowers and is often found in disturbed habitats such as fields and along roadsides. It is valued for its edible seeds, which are used in various cuisines, and for its role in soil improvement through nitrogen fixation.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
