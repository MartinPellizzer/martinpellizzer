Dictamnus odorus

Dictamnus odorus, commonly known as sweet woodruff, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces a distinctive, sweet scent reminiscent of vanilla or honey, which is released when the leaves are crushed. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

The plant is also known for its historical use in herbal medicine and as a flavoring agent.
