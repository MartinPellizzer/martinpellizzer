Dianthus grisebachii

Dianthus grisebachii, commonly known as the gray-leaved campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and features showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are typically gray-green in color, covered in fine hairs, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments. It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family, which includes many ornamental and medicinal plants.

Dianthus grisebachii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in well-drained soils with full sun exposure.
