Dilochia wallichii

Dilochia wallichii, commonly known as the Himalayan snowbell, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in cold, mountainous environments. The plant produces bell-shaped white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a leathery texture.

Dilochia wallichii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
