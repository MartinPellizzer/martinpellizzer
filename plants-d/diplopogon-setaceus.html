Diplopogon setaceus

Diplopogon setaceus, commonly known as the slender everlasting pea, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its distinctive, narrow leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is known for its ability to thrive in arid conditions. Its flowers are usually pale blue or violet, and it produces small, seed-like fruits that are adapted for wind dispersal.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
