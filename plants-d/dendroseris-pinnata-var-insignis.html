Dendroseris pinnata var. insignis

Dendroseris pinnata var. insignis, commonly known as the golden star lily, is a striking perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and large, showy yellow flowers that resemble stars, hence its common name. The plant has lance-shaped, alternate leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and unique appearance.
