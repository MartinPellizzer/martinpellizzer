Daemonorops maculata

Daemonorops maculata, commonly known as the spotted cycad, is a member of the Cycadaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a slow-growing, evergreen plant characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves. The leaves are typically dark green with a distinctive white or yellowish mottling, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a landscape plant in gardens and collections.
