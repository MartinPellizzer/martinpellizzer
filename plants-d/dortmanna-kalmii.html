Dortmanna kalmii

Dortmanna kalmii, commonly known as Kalm's sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the northern regions of North America.

It typically thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas or near freshwater sources. The plant features small, clustered leaves and produces white or pinkish flowers during the spring and early summer. Its botanical structure includes a fibrous root system and stems that are often prostrate or ascending.

Dortmanna kalmii is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
