Diospyros capreifolia

Diospyros capreifolia, commonly known as the Indian persimmon or wild persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and alternate, simple leaves that are elliptical in shape. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by small, round fruits resembling persimmons.

The fruit is typically edible when fully ripe, and the plant is valued for its ornamental qualities as well as its potential medicinal uses.
