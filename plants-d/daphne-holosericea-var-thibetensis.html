Daphne holosericea var. thibetensis

Daphne holosericea var. thibetensis, commonly known as the Tibetan daphne, is a deciduous shrub native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, gray-green leaves and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
