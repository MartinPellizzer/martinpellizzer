Dryopteris longipetiolata

Dryopteris longipetiolata, commonly known as the long-petioled wood fern, is a species of fern native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender fronds that can reach up to 1.5 meters in length. The fronds are bipinnate, with a delicate, lace-like appearance, and they are typically green with a slightly glossy surface. This fern prefers moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and rocky areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
