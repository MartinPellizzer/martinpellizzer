Dicranopteris taiwanensis

Dicranopteris taiwanensis, commonly known as the Taiwan shield fern, is a species of polypodiales fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, shield-shaped fronds that are typically dark green and leathery in texture. The plant produces sporangia on the underside of its fronds, which are clustered in sori, a feature common among ferns. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

Dicranopteris taiwanensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
