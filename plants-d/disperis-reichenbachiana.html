Disperis reichenbachiana

Disperis reichenbachiana, commonly known as the African orchid or the mountain orchid, is a species of orchid native to eastern and southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb with a slender, erect stem that produces a single spike of flowers. The flowers are typically white or pale pink, with a distinctive, spurred lip that aids in pollination by insects. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
