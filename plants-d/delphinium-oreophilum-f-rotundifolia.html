Delphinium oreophilum f. rotundifolia

Delphinium oreophilum f. rotundifolia, commonly known as the round-leaf larkspur, is a flowering plant characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers and distinctive foliage.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is native to the high-altitude regions of the Western Himalayas. The plant features lanceolate to ovate leaves that are often rounded at the base, giving it its specific epithet "rotundifolia.". Its flowers are typically arranged in dense spikes and bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is a subject of interest in both horticulture and botanical research.
