Dendranthema zawadzkii var. yezoense

Dendranthema zawadzkii var. yezoense, commonly known as the Yezo chrysanthemum, is a perennial flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically produces white to pale yellow blooms in the autumn, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its foliage is usually gray-green, adding visual interest even when not in bloom.
