Disa secunda

Disa secunda, commonly known as the common disa, is a species of orchid native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, rhizomatous growth habit and produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant typically blooms during the summer months, attracting various pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

Disa secunda is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in a garden setting.
