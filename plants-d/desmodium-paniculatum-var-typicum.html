Desmodium paniculatum var. typicum

Desmodium paniculatum var. typicum, commonly known as small cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces small, hairy pods that contain lens-shaped seeds. It is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it valuable for soil enrichment and as a forage crop.
