Dalea melantha var. pubens

Dalea melantha var. pubens, commonly known as the pubescent melantha, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink to purple in color. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun.

It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
