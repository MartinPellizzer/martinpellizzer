Dierama adelphicum

Dierama adelphicum, commonly known as the mountain dierama, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tufted, grass-like foliage and striking, showy inflorescences. The plant produces clusters of pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal gardens.

Dierama adelphicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its unique floral display.
