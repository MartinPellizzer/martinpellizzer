Dracaena pinguicula subsp. nana

Dracaena pinguicula subsp.

nana, commonly known as the dwarf dracaena, is a compact, low-growing succulent native to southern Africa. It features rosettes of fleshy, dark green leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base.

The plant produces small, star-shaped white flowers on short, erect stalks during the growing season. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water and well-draining soil.

Its slow growth and ornamental appearance make it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
