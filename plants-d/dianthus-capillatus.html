Dianthus capillatus

Dianthus capillatus, commonly known as the mouse-tail, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and clusters of pink or red flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as cliffs and gravelly slopes. Its flowers have a distinctive, slightly fragrant aroma and are attractive to pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Dianthus capillatus is a hardy perennial that is relatively easy to cultivate in gardens with well-drained soil.
