Daphne cestrifolia

Daphne cestrifolia, commonly known as the sweet box, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features small, fragrant, white flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. The plant has dark green, glossy leaves that provide ornamental value throughout the growing season. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, acidic soil.

Daphne cestrifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
