Dombeya umbellata

Dombeya umbellata, commonly known as the white dombeya, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Dombeya umbellata is valued in gardens for its ornamental flowers and hardy nature.
