Dicranopygium latissimum

Dicranopygium latissimum, commonly known as the broadleaf moss, is a species of moss belonging to the family Dicranaceae.

It is characterized by its robust, elongated shoots and dense, overlapping leaves that are typically broad and slightly recurved. The plant forms dense mats or cushions, often growing on moist, shaded forest floors or in rocky crevices. Its spores are produced in capsules that are often clustered at the tips of the shoots.

Dicranopygium latissimum is an important component of many temperate and boreal ecosystems, contributing to soil stabilization and moisture retention.
