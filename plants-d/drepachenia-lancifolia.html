Drepachenia lancifolia

Drepachenia lancifolia, commonly known as the slender leaf drepachenia, is a member of the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology and inflorescence structure.

It typically features elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often glossy and arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces an inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe, which is a common trait among arums. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it a unique and visually striking addition to botanical gardens and greenhouse collections.
