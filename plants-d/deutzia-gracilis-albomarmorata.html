Deutzia gracilis albomarmorata

Deutzia gracilis albomarmorata, commonly known as the white marble deutzia, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It features slender, arching stems that bear clusters of small, white flowers with a faint pinkish tinge. The leaves are ovate, serrated, and turn a striking red or orange in the autumn. This plant thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its ornamental value comes from its delicate blooms and seasonal color changes.
