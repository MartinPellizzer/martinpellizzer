Dalea reclinata

Dalea reclinata, commonly known as the leaning false indigo, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of pink to purple pea-shaped flowers. The plant has small, pinnately compound leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Dalea reclinata is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
