Didymocarpus aurantiacus

Didymocarpus aurantiacus, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy orange to red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically orange or red when mature, containing seeds enclosed in a fleshy pulp. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
