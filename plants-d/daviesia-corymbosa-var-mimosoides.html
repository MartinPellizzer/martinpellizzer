Daviesia corymbosa var. mimosoides

Daviesia corymbosa var. mimosoides, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.

The flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and birds, contributing to its ecological significance.
