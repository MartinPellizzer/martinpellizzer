Dimacria floribunda

Dimacria floribunda, commonly known as the flowering dimacria, is a member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its attractive, showy flowers. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with lance-shaped leaves and clusters of tubular flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its blooming period extends from late spring to early autumn, adding vibrant color to gardens during these months.
