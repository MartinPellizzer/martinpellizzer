Dissotis incana

Dissotis incana, commonly known as the silverleaf dissotis, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and the Americas.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and hairy, silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streams.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in gardens and used in traditional medicine in some regions.
