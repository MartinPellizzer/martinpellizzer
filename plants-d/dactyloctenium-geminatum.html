Dactyloctenium geminatum

Dactyloctenium geminatum, commonly known as the twinseed grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its clump-forming growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in spikelets. Its seeds are unique in that they are enclosed in two separate chambers, a feature that aids in its dispersal.

Dactyloctenium geminatum is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
