Dianthus crinitus var. argaeus

Dianthus crinitus var. argaeus, commonly known as the Argaeus beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and spikes of pink or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is part of the Caryophyllaceae family, known for its showy, often fragrant blooms. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a desirable addition to botanical collections and cultivated landscapes.
