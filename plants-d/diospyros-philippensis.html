Diospyros philippensis

Diospyros philippensis, commonly known as the Philippine persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other species such as the ebony and persimmon trees. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth, glossy surface. Its flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and clustered in panicles, while the fruit is a small, orange-red berry with a sweet, astringent flavor.

Diospyros philippensis is often found in lowland and montane forests, where it plays a role in local ecosystems and has some traditional uses.
