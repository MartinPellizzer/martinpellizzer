Diospyros pubescens

Diospyros pubescens, commonly known as the hairy persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its rough, pubescent bark and elliptical leaves with a velvety texture. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into orange to red fruits with a sweet, astringent flavor. Its fruits are often consumed fresh or used in traditional medicine for their nutritional and medicinal properties.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitats.
