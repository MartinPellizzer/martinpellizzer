Dyckia agavoides

Dyckia agavoides, commonly known as the silver beard palm, is a striking bromeliad native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its tall, arching inflorescence covered in silver-gray bracts that give it its common name. The plant features strap-like, evergreen leaves that form a rosette shape, often with a waxy coating. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.

Dyckia agavoides is known for its long-lasting, showy flowers that appear in shades of yellow to orange.
