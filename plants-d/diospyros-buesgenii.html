Diospyros buesgenii

Diospyros buesgenii, commonly known as the Buesgen's persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other well-known species like the ebony tree. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical in shape and have a smooth, glossy surface. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are clustered in panicles, and it produces small, fleshy berries that are edible when ripe.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
