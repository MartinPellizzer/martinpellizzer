Deyeuxia rariflora

Deyeuxia rariflora, commonly known as the sparse-flowered bulrush, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and the edges of ponds. The plant features slender, erect stems that can reach heights of up to 1 meter, with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are loosely arranged, giving it a delicate appearance.

Deyeuxia rariflora plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
