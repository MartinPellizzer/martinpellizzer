Dioscorea brachystachya

Dioscorea brachystachya, commonly known as the short-stalk yam, is a perennial vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and tuberous roots. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by yellow or orange fruits that contain kidney-shaped seeds. Its tubers are edible when properly prepared and have been used in traditional cuisines.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
