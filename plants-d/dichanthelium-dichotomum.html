Dichanthelium dichotomum

Dichanthelium dichotomum, commonly known as the two-branched grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and disturbed areas. Its reproductive structures are adapted for wind pollination, which is common among grasses.
