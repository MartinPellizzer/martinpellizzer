Dianthus sylvestris subsp. boissieri

Dianthus sylvestris subsp.

boissieri, commonly known as the wild carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in clusters.

The leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, often arranged in a rosette pattern. This subspecies is well adapted to rocky and dry environments, thriving in full sun.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
