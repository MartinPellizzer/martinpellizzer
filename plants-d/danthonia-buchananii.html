Danthonia buchananii

Danthonia buchananii, commonly known as the Buchanania grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
