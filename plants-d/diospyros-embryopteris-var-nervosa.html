Diospyros embryopteris var. nervosa

Diospyros embryopteris var. nervosa, commonly known as the veined persimmon, is a species of flowering plant in the Ebenaceae family.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often veined with a distinct pattern. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles. Its fruits are typically small, round, and turn from green to orange-red when mature.

This variant is native to parts of Southeast Asia and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
