Dicalix tinctorius

Dicalix tinctorius, commonly known as the dye plant, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its vibrant flowers, it is cultivated both for ornamental purposes and for its historical use in natural dye extraction.
