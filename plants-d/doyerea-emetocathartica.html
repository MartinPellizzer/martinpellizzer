Doyerea emetocathartica

Doyerea emetocathartica, commonly known as the emetic cathartic plant, is a species native to certain regions of South America.

It is a shrub characterized by its woody stems and small, often clustered leaves. The plant is notable for containing compounds that have historically been used for their emetic and cathartic properties. Its flowers are typically small and greenish-white, and it produces fruit that is usually a capsule.

Due to its potent effects, it is considered toxic and should be handled with care.
