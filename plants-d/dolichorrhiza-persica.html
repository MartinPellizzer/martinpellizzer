Dolichorrhiza persica

Dolichorrhiza persica, commonly known as Persian longroot, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a thick, fleshy rhizome that serves as a storage organ for nutrients and water. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its leaves are alternate, lanceolate, and covered in fine hairs.

Dolichorrhiza persica is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
