Dracocephalum urticifolium f. carnosa

Dracocephalum urticifolium f. carnosa, commonly known as the woolly caraway, is a hemicryptophyte with a basal rosette of hairy, lanceolate leaves.

It produces small, blue-violet flowers arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences. The plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in dry, rocky soils. Its aromatic foliage contains essential oils, making it a valuable species in traditional medicine and horticulture.

The f. carnosa form is distinguished by its thick, woolly leaves and compact growth habit.
