Dichanthium annulatum

Dichanthium annulatum, commonly known as the ringgrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, cylindrical stems that often form a ring-like structure. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brown bracts, and its flowers are typically wind-pollinated. Dichanthium annulatum thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional landscaping and erosion control.
