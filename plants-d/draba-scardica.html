Draba scardica

Draba scardica, commonly known as the Scardica whitlow grass, is a small flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving at high altitudes. It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by pods that release tiny seeds.

Draba scardica is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and is often found in arid or semi-arid habitats.
