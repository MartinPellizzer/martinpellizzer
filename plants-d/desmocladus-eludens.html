Desmocladus eludens

Desmocladus eludens, commonly known as the dodder plant, is a parasitic flowering plant that lacks true roots and leaves.

It attaches itself to host plants, primarily within the Asteraceae family, using haustoria to extract nutrients. The plant produces small, yellow flowers and has a unique ability to evade detection by its hosts, hence its common name. It is native to various regions in Africa and is often found in disturbed habitats.

Its botanical significance lies in its parasitic lifestyle and ecological interactions with host plants.
