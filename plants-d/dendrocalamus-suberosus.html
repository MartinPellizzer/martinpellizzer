Dendrocalamus suberosus

Dendrocalamus suberosus, commonly known as the giant bamboo, is a tall, fast-growing grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its strong, woody culms that can reach heights of up to 30 meters. The plant has a dense, fibrous structure, making it highly valued for its durable and lightweight material used in construction and handicrafts. Its rhizomes are also used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Dendrocalamus suberosus plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
