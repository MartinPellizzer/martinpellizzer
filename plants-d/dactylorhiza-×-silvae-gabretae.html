Dactylorhiza × silvae-gabretae

Dactylorhiza × silvae-gabretae, commonly known as the forest bog orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, acidic environments such as bogs and heaths, where it thrives in shaded conditions. The plant features a tall, erect stem with several lance-shaped leaves, and it produces spikes of pale purple or greenish-white flowers. Its roots are specialized for mycorrhizal associations, which aid in nutrient uptake from the nutrient-poor soils it inhabits.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitats.
