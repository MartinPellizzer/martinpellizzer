Diastema maculatum

Diastema maculatum, commonly known as the spotted bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of hairs.

Diastema maculatum is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained conditions.
