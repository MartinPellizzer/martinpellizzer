Diospyros ierensis

Diospyros ierensis, commonly known as the Ierensis persimmon, is a species of flowering plant in the Ebenaceae family.

It is native to the island of Ieris, located in the Mediterranean Sea, and is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces small, orange-red berries that are edible when fully mature, though they are typically harvested after a period of cold treatment.

Diospyros ierensis is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance and historical significance.
