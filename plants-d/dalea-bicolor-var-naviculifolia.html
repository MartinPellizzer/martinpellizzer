Dalea bicolor var. naviculifolia

Dalea bicolor var. naviculifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are alternate and pinnately compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides nectar for pollinators in its native range.
