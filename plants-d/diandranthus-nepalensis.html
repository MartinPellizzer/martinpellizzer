Diandranthus nepalensis

Diandranthus nepalensis, commonly known as the Nepal bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the Himalayan region.

It typically grows in alpine and subalpine areas, thriving in cool, moist environments. The plant features erect stems covered with soft, hairy leaves that are lance-shaped and slightly hairy. Its most distinctive feature is its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, often with a white throat.

Diandranthus nepalensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable climate conditions.
