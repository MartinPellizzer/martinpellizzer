Dracaena salmonea

Dracaena salmonea, commonly known as the red dracaena, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its striking, reddish-pink flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are bright green and often have a waxy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers bright, indirect light.

Dracaena salmonea is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
