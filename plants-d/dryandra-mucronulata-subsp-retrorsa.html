Dryandra mucronulata subsp. retrorsa

Dryandra mucronulata subsp.

retrorsa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf dryandra, is a shrub native to southwestern Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to the dry, Mediterranean climate of its native region.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its distinctive foliage and seasonal flowering.
