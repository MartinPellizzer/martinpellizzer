Diomedea linearis

Diomedea linearis, commonly known as the black-browed albatross, is a large seabird belonging to the family Diomedeidae.

It is characterized by its long wingspan, which can reach up to 3.5 meters, and its distinctive black eyebrows. The plant aspect of this species refers to its role as a seed disperser, particularly in coastal and island ecosystems. Its feeding habits involve scavenging and hunting for fish and squid, which indirectly influences the distribution of plant species through nutrient cycling.

Diomedea linearis plays a vital ecological role in maintaining the balance of marine and coastal environments.
