Delphinium sulcatum

Delphinium sulcatum, commonly known as the crested larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant produces clusters of blue to purple flowers, which are typically arranged in racemes along the stem. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinctive corrugated texture, giving the plant its common name.

Delphinium sulcatum is a member of the Ranunculaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
