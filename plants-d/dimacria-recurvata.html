Dimacria recurvata

Dimacria recurvata, commonly known as the spiral grass, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its unique spiral arrangement of leaves along its stem, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish or yellowish in color. It thrives in arid or semi-arid environments and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.

Dimacria recurvata is adapted to withstand drought conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
