Danthonia raoulii subsp. australis

Danthonia raoulii subsp.

australis, commonly known as the Southern Danthonia, is a perennial grass species native to southeastern Australia. It typically grows in coastal and heathland environments, preferring well-drained sandy or loamy soils.

The plant features slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing coastal dunes and supporting local wildlife.
