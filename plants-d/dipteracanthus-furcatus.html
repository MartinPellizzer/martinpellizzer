Dipteracanthus furcatus

Dipteracanthus furcatus, commonly known as the forked flame flower, is a striking tropical plant characterized by its vibrant red and orange inflorescences.

Native to Southeast Asia, it thrives in humid, shaded environments within tropical rainforests. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces clusters of small, tubular flowers arranged in a distinctive forked shape. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its showy, long-lasting blooms.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
