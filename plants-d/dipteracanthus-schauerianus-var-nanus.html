Dipteracanthus schauerianus var. nanus

Dipteracanthus schauerianus var. nanus, commonly known as the dwarf Schauer's dipteracanthus, is a compact, herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It features small, clustered leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. The plant produces delicate, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white and pale yellow, which bloom during the warmer months. Its growth habit is low and spreading, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is occasionally cultivated by botanists and gardeners interested in rare plant species.
