Dipterocarpus quinquegonus

Dipterocarpus quinquegonus, commonly known as the five-angled dipterocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its distinctive five-angled fruit, which gives it its common name. The plant is typically found in lowland rainforests and is known for its hard, heavy wood that is highly valued in the timber industry. It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.

The tree is also significant in traditional medicine and cultural practices in the region.
