Diospyros mollifolia

Diospyros mollifolia, commonly known as the velvet apple tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish-brown bark and elliptical leaves that are soft to the touch. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by edible, round, red or orange fruits that resemble small apples. Its fruits are often consumed fresh or used in traditional medicine.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in local ecosystems.
