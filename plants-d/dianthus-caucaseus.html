Dianthus caucaseus

Dianthus caucaseus, commonly known as the Caucasian pink, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to deep pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Dianthus caucaseus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
