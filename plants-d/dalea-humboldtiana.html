Dalea humboldtiana

Dalea humboldtiana, commonly known as the Humboldt's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, such as deserts and rocky soils. The plant features slender, branching stems and small, compound leaves that are adapted to conserve water. Its flowers are usually pink to purple in color, arranged in dense, spike-like clusters.

Dalea humboldtiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in harsh, dry conditions.
