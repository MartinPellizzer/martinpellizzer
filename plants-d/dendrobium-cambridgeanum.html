Dendrobium cambridgeanum

Dendrobium cambridgeanum, commonly known as the Cambridge orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a rhizome that produces a cluster of fleshy, leaf-like structures. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green, often with a distinctive red or purple lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses worldwide.
