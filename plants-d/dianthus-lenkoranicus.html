Dianthus lenkoranicus

Dianthus lenkoranicus, commonly known as the Armenian carnation, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and has narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Dianthus lenkoranicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
