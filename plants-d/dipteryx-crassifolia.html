Dipteryx crassifolia

Dipteryx crassifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian nut tree, is a large tropical tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and produces heavy, woody pods containing the edible seeds known as Brazil nuts. The tree can grow up to 50 meters tall and has a long lifespan, often living over 100 years. Its flowers are small and clustered, and it relies on specific pollinators for reproduction.

The plant plays a crucial role in the ecosystem, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
