Dracula vampira

Dracula vampira, commonly known as the vampire plant, is a unique species of orchid native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, blood-red flowers that resemble the mouth of a vampire, giving it its ominous common name. The plant thrives in cool, humid environments and requires specific conditions to bloom, including indirect light and well-draining soil. Its leaves are fleshy and dark green, adapted to store water in its arid habitat.

Despite its intimidating appearance, Dracula vampira is a relatively rare and delicate orchid, making it a sought-after specimen among plant enthusiasts.
