Diasperus calycinus

Diasperus calycinus, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically covered in a soft, hairy coating. The leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
