Daphnidium melastomaceum

Daphnidium melastomaceum, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Daphnidium melastomaceum is also noted for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
