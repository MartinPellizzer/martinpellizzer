Daiswa birmanica

Daiswa birmanica, commonly known as the Burmese daisy, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies, sunflowers, and other composite flowers. The plant features showy yellow flowers with white ray florets, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along riverbanks.

Daiswa birmanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
