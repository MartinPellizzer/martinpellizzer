Davallia proxima

Davallia proxima, commonly known as the bird's nest fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive, shield-shaped fronds that resemble a bird's nest, giving it its common name. The plant has a short, thick rhizome that supports the fronds, which are typically light green and have a leathery texture. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on tree trunks or rocks.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
