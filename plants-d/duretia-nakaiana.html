Duretia nakaiana

Duretia nakaiana, commonly known as the Japanese violet, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of purple and white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture, and it thrives in cool, moist environments. It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for.

Duretia nakaiana is also noted for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
