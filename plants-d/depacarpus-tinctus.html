Depacarpus tinctus

Depacarpus tinctus, commonly known as the red-flowered orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, vividly colored flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a waxy texture to retain moisture.

The flowers bloom in clusters, producing a fragrant scent that attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
