Draba sagasteguii

Draba sagasteguii, commonly known as the Sagastegui's wintercress, is a small flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbages, and is characterized by its cruciform flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in high-altitude regions with cool climates. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Draba sagasteguii is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
