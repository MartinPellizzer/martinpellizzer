Decaisnea densiflora

Decaisnea densiflora, commonly known as the dense-flowered decaisnea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, characterized by its thick, leathery leaves and showy, yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are typically red when ripe. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
