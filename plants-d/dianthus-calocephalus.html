Dianthus calocephalus

Dianthus calocephalus, commonly known as the clustered campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and vibrant pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, round, red or purple fruits called capsules, which contain numerous seeds. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, often with a reddish tinge.

Dianthus calocephalus is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to cultivate in well-drained soil.
