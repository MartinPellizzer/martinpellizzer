Dracophyllum rosmarinifolium

Dracophyllum rosmarinifolium, commonly known as the silver-leaved heath, is a shrub native to the cool, wet regions of southern Australia.

It features small, needle-like leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. The plant produces clusters of tiny, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or reddish in color. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal areas.

Dracophyllum rosmarinifolium is adapted to harsh environments and is known for its aromatic foliage.
