Diasperus trichogynus

Diasperus trichogynus, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as a compact shrub, with stems that often root at the nodes. Its flowers are usually violet or purple in color, and it produces small, round berries that contain the seeds.

Diasperus trichogynus is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
