Desmodium mandshuricum

Desmodium mandshuricum, commonly known as the Siberian burr vetchy, is a perennial legume native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces distinctive, spiny pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in grassy areas or along roadsides.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
