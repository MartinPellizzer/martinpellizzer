Digitalis winterli

Digitalis winterli, commonly known as the Alpine foxglove, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its tall, spindly stems and striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
