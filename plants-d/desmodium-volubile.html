Desmodium volubile

Desmodium volubile, commonly known as the creeping sicklepod, is a climbing legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features trifoliate leaves with a distinctive sickle-shaped leaflet, and its small, yellow flowers are followed by slender, coiling pods. The plant is characterized by its vigorous growth habit and ability to twine around supports, making it a common weed in agricultural fields. It thrives in warm climates and is often found in disturbed soils, contributing to soil stabilization through its extensive root system.

The plant's botanical features make it both a useful forage crop and a persistent weed in many ecosystems.
