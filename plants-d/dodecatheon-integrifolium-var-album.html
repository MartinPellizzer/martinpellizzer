Dodecatheon integrifolium var. album

Dodecatheon integrifolium var. album, commonly known as the white trout lily, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in early spring, often before the leaves fully emerge. The plant has smooth, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a whorl around the stem. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other early-season pollinators.

The plant thrives in moist, rich soils and is often found in woodlands and shaded gardens.
