Dryas × suendermannii

Dryas × suendermannii, commonly known as the hybrid snowbell, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are dark green, leathery, and remain on the plant throughout the year, contributing to its hardy nature.

Dryas × suendermannii is well adapted to cold climates and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments.
