Diplazium chinense

Diplazium chinense, commonly known as Chinese chain fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and the presence of two types of fronds—sterile and fertile—on the same plant. The sterile fronds are typically bipinnate, with a distinctive, elongated shape, while the fertile fronds bear spore-producing structures called sori. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appearance and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
