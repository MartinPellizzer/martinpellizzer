Diplotropis ferruginea

Diplotropis ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty diplotropis, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands and along riverbanks, thriving in moist, sandy soils. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and can be dispersed by water, contributing to its spread in riparian habitats.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and as a food source for various wildlife.
