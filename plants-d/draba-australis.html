Draba australis

Draba australis, commonly known as the Antarctic pearlwort, is a flowering plant native to the cold regions of the Southern Hemisphere.

It is a small, mat-forming plant with bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has tiny, elliptical leaves and is adapted to survive in harsh, alpine or Antarctic environments. It is often found growing on rocky soils and is considered an indicator species for cold climates.

Draba australis plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local ecosystems in its extreme habitat.
