Diplazium grammitoides f. yakusimense

Diplazium grammitoides f. yakusimense, commonly known as the Yakusim Island huperzia, is a fern native to the mountainous regions of Japan.

It belongs to the family Huperjiaceae and is characterized by its small, densely packed fronds. The plant exhibits a compact growth habit with dark green, lanceolate leaves that are typically short and stout. It thrives in shaded, moist environments, often found in forest undergrowth.

The fronds are usually unbranched and have a leathery texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
