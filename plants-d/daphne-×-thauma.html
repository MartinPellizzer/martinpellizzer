Daphne × thauma

Daphne × thauma, commonly known as the snowflake daphne, is a hybrid shrub native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring, often before the leaves appear. The plant produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers, which give it its common name. Its leaves are dark green and glossy, and the stems are typically covered in fine, silvery hairs.

Daphne × thauma is valued in gardens for its ornamental beauty and winter interest.
