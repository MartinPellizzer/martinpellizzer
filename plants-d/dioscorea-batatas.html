Dioscorea batatas

Dioscorea batatas, commonly known as Chinese yam, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary edible part. The plant produces small, green, heart-shaped leaves and yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. Its tubers are rich in starch and have been traditionally used in Asian cuisine and traditional medicine.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
