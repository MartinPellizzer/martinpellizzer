Dioscorea javariensis

Dioscorea javariensis, commonly known as the Amazonian yam, is a member of the Dioscoreaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a climbing vine that produces large, heart-shaped leaves and bears yellow flowers followed by edible tubers. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the undergrowth of tropical rainforests. Its tubers are valued for their nutritional content and are used in traditional cuisines.

The species is also of interest in botanical research due to its potential medicinal properties.
