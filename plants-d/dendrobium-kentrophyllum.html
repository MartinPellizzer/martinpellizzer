Dendrobium kentrophyllum

Dendrobium kentrophyllum, commonly known as the Kent's orchid, is a member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is an epiphytic plant, meaning it grows on the surface of trees or other structures without deriving nutrients from them. The plant produces strikingly colorful flowers, often in shades of yellow, orange, and red, which bloom during the dry season. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in its tropical environment.

Dendrobium kentrophyllum is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its unique floral display.
