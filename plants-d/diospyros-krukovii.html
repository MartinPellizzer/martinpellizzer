Diospyros krukovii

Diospyros krukovii, commonly known as the krukovii persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and broad, ovate leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into edible, orange-red fruits with a sweet, astringent flavor. Its fruits are often consumed fresh or used in traditional culinary practices.

The plant is also valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in crafting and construction.
