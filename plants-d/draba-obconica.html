Draba obconica

Draba obconica, commonly known as the narrowleaf whitlow grass, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. It has basal leaves that are narrow and obconic, with a hairy surface, and produces numerous small white or pale yellow flowers in early spring.

Draba obconica is often found in open, sunny areas and is adapted to cold, harsh climates.
