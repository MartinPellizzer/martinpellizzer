Desmodium procumbens var. procumbens

Desmodium procumbens var. procumbens, commonly known as hairy bird's-beak, is a low-growing annual legume native to the southeastern United States.

It features trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces slender, curved pods that contain several small, kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in open woods and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for ecological studies and soil stabilization.
