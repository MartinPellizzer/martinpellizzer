Didymocarpus consobrinus

Didymocarpus consobrinus, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall. The plant is characterized by its opposite, elliptical leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. It produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by yellowish-orange fruits.

The botanical features of Didymocarpus consobrinus make it a notable species in tropical and subtropical regions.
