Derris momiensis

Derris momiensis, commonly known as the African snakeroot, is a perennial shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is known for its medicinal properties, particularly the presence of rotenone, a natural insecticide.

Derris momiensis is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for both its ecological and traditional uses.
