Dendrobium centrosepalum

Dendrobium centrosepalum, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, cane-like stems and showy, golden-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often on trees or rocks. Its leaves are long and strap-like, and it produces numerous pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients.

Dendrobium centrosepalum is prized for its ornamental value and is cultivated for its striking floral display.
