Delphinium saniculifolium var. aquilegiifolium

Delphinium saniculifolium var. aquilegiifolium, commonly known as the aquatic leaf delphinium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height, bearing clusters of blue to purple spurred flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a distinctive, finely toothed margin, resembling the leaves of the water plantain. This species thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

It is valued in horticulture for its striking floral display and is often cultivated in gardens and naturalized areas.
