Drymaria auriculipetala

Drymaria auriculipetala, commonly known as the ear-shaped leaf sandplant, is a low-growing perennial plant native to sandy soils in arid and semi-arid regions.

It features small, succulent leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern, helping to conserve moisture. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is highly adapted to nutrient-poor soils, allowing it to thrive in harsh environments.

The plant's unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it an important component of its native ecosystems.
