Dianthus marsicus

Dianthus marsicus, commonly known as the Sicilian carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its showy, bicolor flowers that typically feature a pinkish-red center surrounded by white petals. The plant produces a dense mat of foliage and is often found growing in rocky or sandy soils. It thrives in full sun and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Dianthus marsicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
