Dracocephalum discolor

Dracocephalum discolor, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as butterflies and bees. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color, with a fuzzy texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Dracocephalum discolor is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
