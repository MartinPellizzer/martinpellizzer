Digitalis nevadensis

Digitalis nevadensis, commonly known as the Sierra Lily, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and high-altitude environments, often in alpine meadows. It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is known for its ornamental value.

Due to its habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many regions.
