Duvernoia robusta

Duvernoia robusta, commonly known as the Chinese sand pear, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey-bark trunk and alternate, ovate leaves. The tree produces small, white flowers in spring, followed by yellow, fuzzy fruits that are edible.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to temperate climates with well-drained soils.
