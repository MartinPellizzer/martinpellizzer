Dactylorhiza maculata var. sudetica

Dactylorhiza maculata var. sudetica, commonly known as the Sudetic marsh orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It typically grows in wet meadows, marshes, and along the edges of lakes and rivers. The plant has a slender, upright stem with several elongated, linear leaves. Its flowers are pale green to yellowish, with a distinctive dark red or purple blotch on the lip.

It is a rhizomatous orchid, meaning it spreads through underground stems, forming colonies in suitable habitats.
