Deyeuxia montevidensis

Deyeuxia montevidensis, commonly known as the Montevideo grass, is a species of grass native to South America, particularly Uruguay and parts of Argentina.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as grasslands and wet meadows. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

Deyeuxia montevidensis is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soils and support local biodiversity.
