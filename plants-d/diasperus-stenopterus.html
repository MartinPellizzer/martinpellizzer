Diasperus stenopterus

Diasperus stenopterus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rock cress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, delicate leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Native to rocky and arid environments, this plant is adapted to withstand drought and poor soil conditions. Its root system is shallow, allowing it to absorb moisture from the surface layer of the soil.

Diasperus stenopterus plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and supports local pollinators.
