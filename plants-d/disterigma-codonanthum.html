Disterigma codonanthum

Disterigma codonanthum, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to Africa.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its compact, evergreen rosette of fleshy, velvety leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white, often with a contrasting throat. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Disterigma codonanthum is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
