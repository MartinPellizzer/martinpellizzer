Dianthus microlepis

Dianthus microlepis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Dianthus microlepis thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant flowers and delicate appearance.
