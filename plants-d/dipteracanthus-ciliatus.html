Dipteracanthus ciliatus

Dipteracanthus ciliatus, commonly known as the silky mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of host trees, primarily in tropical and subtropical regions.

It has small, leathery leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its berries are typically red or orange and contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Violaceae and is known for its unique parasitic relationship with its host plants.
