Delphinium exaltatum var. californicum

Delphinium exaltatum var. californicum, commonly known as the California larkspur, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features tall, sparsely branched stems that bear clusters of blue to purple, spurred flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its striking floral display attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies.
