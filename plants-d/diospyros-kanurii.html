Diospyros kanurii

Diospyros kanurii, commonly known as the African persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes ebony and persimmon trees. The plant is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and small, clustered white flowers. Its fruit is a small, round, edible drupe with a sweet, honey-like flavor when ripe.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine and as a food source.
