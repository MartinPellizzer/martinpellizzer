Dioscorea trichopoda

Dioscorea trichopoda, commonly known as the African yam bean, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are used as food sources in some regions. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into capsule fruits. Its tubers are rich in starch and have a nutty flavor, making them an important crop in local agriculture.

The plant's botanical features make it both ecologically significant and economically valuable in its native habitat.
