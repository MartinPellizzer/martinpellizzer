Darwinia uncinata

Darwinia uncinata, commonly known as the hooked darwinia, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, showy inflorescences that resemble a bottlebrush. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red, arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal or heathland environments.

Darwinia uncinata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
