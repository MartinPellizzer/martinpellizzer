Drimys paniculata

Drimys paniculata, commonly known as the Chilean nutmeg, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub that can grow up to 6 meters tall, characterized by its glossy dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that contain a single seed, and it is known for its aromatic properties.

Drimys paniculata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic benefits.
