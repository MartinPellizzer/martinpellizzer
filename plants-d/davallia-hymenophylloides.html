Davallia hymenophylloides

Davallia hymenophylloides, commonly known as the golden polypody, is a fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are typically bright green with a subtle golden hue. The plant produces small, spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its leaves. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and shady garden beds.
