Dianthus heldreichii

Dianthus heldreichii, commonly known as the Balkan carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers with prominent white veins. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Dianthus heldreichii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
