Dendrobium campbellii

Dendrobium campbellii, commonly known as the Campbell's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Australia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with branched stems that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers during the summer months. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, often arranged in two rows along the stems.

Dendrobium campbellii thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate to high light exposure for optimal growth.
