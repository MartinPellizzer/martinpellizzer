Diolena purpurea

Diolena purpurea, commonly known as the purple tamarisk, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Australia.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in arid or semi-arid environments and is adapted to poor soil conditions.

Diolena purpurea is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and drought tolerance.
