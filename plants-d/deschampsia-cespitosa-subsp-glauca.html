Deschampsia cespitosa subsp. glauca

Deschampsia cespitosa subsp.

glauca, commonly known as bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to North America. It is characterized by its fine, bluish-green foliage and tufted growth habit.

The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as meadows, wetlands, and stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often tinged with purple or blue.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and restoration projects.
