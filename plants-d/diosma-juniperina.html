Diosma juniperina

Diosma juniperina, commonly known as the Juniperleaf Diosma, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and elliptical leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. Its flowers are usually white or pale blue, and it produces small, berry-like fruits that are an important food source for local wildlife.

Diosma juniperina is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
