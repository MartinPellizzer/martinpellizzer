Diuris citrina

Diuris citrina, commonly known as the golden spider-orchid, is a striking terrestrial orchid native to southeastern Australia.

It features a compact, hairy rhizome and produces delicate, spider-like flowers with bright yellow petals and sepals. The flowers typically bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators such as birds and insects. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and requires a cool, moist environment to flourish.

Its unique morphology and vibrant color make it a notable species in the orchid family.
