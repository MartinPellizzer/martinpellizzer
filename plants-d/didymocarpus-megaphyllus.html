Didymocarpus megaphyllus

Didymocarpus megaphyllus, commonly known as the giant flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, yellowish-white flowers followed by bright red, fleshy fruits that resemble berries. Its botanical features include a woody stem with a fibrous bark and a complex root system adapted to wet tropical soils.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in humid, warm climates.
