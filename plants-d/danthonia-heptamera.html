Danthonia heptamera

Danthonia heptamera, commonly known as the seven-branched grass, is a species of grass native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute, spikelet-based flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grassy habitats.

Danthonia heptamera is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in native landscapes.
