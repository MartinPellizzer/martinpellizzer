Ditmaria violacea

Ditmaria violacea, commonly known as the violet sea hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of the Mediterranean and the Canary Islands.

It is a shrub that typically grows in saline and rocky environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh coastal conditions. The plant features showy, violet-pink flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are succulent and fleshy, which helps it retain moisture in arid conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in coastal gardens for its resilience and aesthetic beauty.
