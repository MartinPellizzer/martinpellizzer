Draba alyssoides var. thlaspiformis

Draba alyssoides var. thlaspiformis, commonly known as the alpine winter cress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches heights of up to 10 centimeters. The plant features basal leaves that are lanceolate and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowering stems bear small, clustered flowers with four petals. It is native to mountainous regions of Europe and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.

This variety is distinguished by its compact growth form and its ability to survive in harsh, frost-prone conditions.
