Dactylorhiza battandieri

Dactylorhiza battandieri, commonly known as the purple marsh orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe and North Africa.

It typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, often near waterlogged soils. The plant has a slender, erect stem that bears several pale green to purple flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, fragrant blooms during the spring and early summer.

This orchid is characterized by its unique floral structure and is often found in association with specific mycorrhizal fungi.
