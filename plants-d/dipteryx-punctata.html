Dipteryx punctata

Dipteryx punctata, commonly known as the Brazil nut tree, is a large evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and can grow up to 50 meters tall. The tree produces distinctive yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a woody capsule containing multiple seeds, which are the edible Brazil nuts.

The plant plays a vital role in the ecosystem and is economically significant for its nut production.
