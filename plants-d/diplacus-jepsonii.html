Diplacus jepsonii

Diplacus jepsonii, commonly known as the Sierra strawberry.

It is a flowering plant native to the western United States, particularly California. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or open habitats.

Diplacus jepsonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
