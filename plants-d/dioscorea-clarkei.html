Dioscorea clarkei

Dioscorea clarkei, commonly known as the Clarke's yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, green flowers that develop into capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are heart-shaped and can grow quite large, often reaching up to 30 centimeters in diameter.

Dioscorea clarkei is valued not only for its medicinal properties but also for its role in local ecosystems.
