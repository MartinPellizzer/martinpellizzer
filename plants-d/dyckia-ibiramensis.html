Dyckia ibiramensis

Dyckia ibiramensis, commonly known as the Ibirapuera bromeliad, is a rare and striking plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup to collect water. The plant features vibrant, long-lasting inflorescences that can range in color from red to orange, adding visual interest. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its rarity and unique appearance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
