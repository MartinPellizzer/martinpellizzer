Draba glabella var. orthocarpa

Draba glabella var. orthocarpa, commonly known as the glabrous whitlow grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a small, herbaceous perennial that typically grows in alpine and arctic regions. The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in early spring. It is adapted to cold climates and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils.

The species is characterized by its smooth, hairless appearance, which gives it its common name.
