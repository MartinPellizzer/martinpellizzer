Dombeya vohitsiombeensis

Dombeya vohitsiombeensis, commonly known as the Madagascar dombeya, is a shrub native to the eastern rainforests of Madagascar.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often appearing in clusters. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually oval or elliptical in shape. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is adapted to the tropical climate of its native habitat.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
