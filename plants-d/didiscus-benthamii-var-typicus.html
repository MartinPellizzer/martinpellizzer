Didiscus benthamii var. typicus

Didiscus benthamii var. typicus, commonly known as the African buttercup, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features showy, yellow flowers with five petals arranged in a cup-like shape, typical of the Ranunculaceae family. The plant has a short, thick rhizome and produces palmate leaves with three to five lobes. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and forest margins.

The flowers bloom in spring and are pollinated primarily by insects.
