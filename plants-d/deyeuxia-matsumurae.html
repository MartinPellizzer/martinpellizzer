Deyeuxia matsumurae

Deyeuxia matsumurae, commonly known as the Japanese false panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to East Asia.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth surface and a bluish-green color.

Deyeuxia matsumurae is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
