Dendrobium aureilobum

Dendrobium aureilobum, commonly known as the golden lobe orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features long, wiry stems that can grow up to several meters in length, often adorned with clusters of showy, golden-yellow flowers. The plant produces lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. Its flowers bloom in late summer or early autumn, adding a striking visual element to its natural habitat.

Dendrobium aureilobum is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its vibrant, long-lasting blooms.
