Davilla rugosa var. capitata

Davilla rugosa var. capitata, commonly known as the clustered huito, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has compound leaves with ovate to lanceolate leaflets, and it produces small, round, yellow fruits that are edible. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its use in traditional medicine.
