Dianthus haematocalyx subsp. haematocalyx

Dianthus haematocalyx subsp.

haematocalyx, commonly known as the blood-red campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its vibrant red, pink, or white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early summer.

The plant has hairy, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical name reflects its striking floral display and historical medicinal uses.
