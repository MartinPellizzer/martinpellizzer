Draba ladyginii

Draba ladyginii, commonly known as the Ladygin's whitlow grass, is a small flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants, and is characterized by its compact growth habit and basal rosette of leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in a raceme, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Draba ladyginii is a perennial species, known for its resilience to cold temperatures and harsh mountain conditions.
