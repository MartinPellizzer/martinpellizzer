Dalbergia villosa var. villosa

Dalbergia villosa var. villosa, commonly known as the hairy burr bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes. The plant bears pod fruits that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and dry forests.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and local herbal practices.
