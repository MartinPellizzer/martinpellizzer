Diplotropis leptophylla

Diplotropis leptophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf diplotropis, is a species of flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy habitats, where it thrives in moist, sandy soils.

Its botanical features include a taproot system and the ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic bacteria in its root nodules.
