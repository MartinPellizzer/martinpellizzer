Deyeuxia groenlandica subsp. stricta

Deyeuxia groenlandica subsp.

stricta, commonly known as the Greenland sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Arctic and alpine regions. It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and compact growth habit, typically forming dense mats.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in nutrient-poor, moist soils and is adapted to cold climates.

Its botanical structure reflects its ecological role in stabilizing soil in harsh environments.
