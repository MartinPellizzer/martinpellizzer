Dryopteris microchlamys

Dryopteris microchlamys, commonly known as the Japanese shield fern, is a species of fern native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, shield-shaped fronds that are typically dark green and have a leathery texture. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Dryopteris microchlamys is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
