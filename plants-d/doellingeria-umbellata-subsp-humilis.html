Doellingeria umbellata subsp. humilis

Doellingeria umbellata subsp.

humilis, commonly known as the slender thimbleweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that resemble tiny umbrellas.

The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its stems are slender and upright.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food and habitat for various insects.
