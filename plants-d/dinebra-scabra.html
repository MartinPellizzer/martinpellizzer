Dinebra scabra

Dinebra scabra, commonly known as the roughleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and bright yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields. It produces a capsule fruit that contains numerous small seeds with feathery appendages for wind dispersal.

Dinebra scabra is often found in arid or semi-arid regions and is adapted to thrive in poor soils.
