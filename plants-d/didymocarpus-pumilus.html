Didymocarpus pumilus

Didymocarpus pumilus, commonly known as the dwarf tamarind, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, round, yellow fruits that are edible and have a tangy flavor. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of food in tropical regions.

Didymocarpus pumilus thrives in well-drained soil and requires partial to full sunlight.
