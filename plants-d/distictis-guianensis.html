Distictis guianensis

Distictis guianensis, commonly known as the Guianan mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Viscaraceae and is characterized by its leafless stems and fleshy, disc-shaped berries. The plant attaches itself to the branches of host trees, deriving some of its nutrients through a specialized root system called a haustellum. It plays a role in forest ecosystems by providing food for various birds and insects.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can significantly impact the growth and health of its host trees.
