Ditassa megapotamica

Ditassa megapotamica, commonly known as the Amazonian water lily, is a aquatic plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves and showy white flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms. Its roots help stabilize the soil and prevent erosion in waterlogged areas.

The species is also valued for its ornamental qualities and traditional uses by local communities.
