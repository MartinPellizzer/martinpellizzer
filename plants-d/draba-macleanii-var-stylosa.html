Draba macleanii var. stylosa

Draba macleanii var. stylosa, commonly known as the Sierra draba, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. This variant is distinguished by its elongated, slender floral stalks, which give it its specific epithet "stylosa.". It typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in rocky soils and high elevations.

The plant is well adapted to cold climates and is often found in mountainous regions of California and Nevada.
