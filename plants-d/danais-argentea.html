Danais argentea

Danais argentea, commonly known as silverleaf danais, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking silver-gray foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its foliage and flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
