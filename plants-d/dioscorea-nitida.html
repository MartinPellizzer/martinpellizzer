Dioscorea nitida

Dioscorea nitida, commonly known as the silky gourd or African yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by elongated, tuberous roots that are often harvested for culinary use. Its tubers are rich in starch and have been traditionally used in various cuisines across Africa and Asia.

The plant also has medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
