Diospyros campanulata

Diospyros campanulata, commonly known as the bell-shaped persimmon, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, grey bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, bell-shaped yellow flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, fleshy berry that ripens to a deep red or orange color, often containing a single seed.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for its edible fruits in certain regions.
