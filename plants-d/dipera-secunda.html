Dipera secunda

Dipera secunda, commonly known as the white-flowered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped white flowers with five petals, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate appearance and ability to attract pollinators.
