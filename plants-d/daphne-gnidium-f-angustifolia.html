Daphne gnidium f. angustifolia

Daphne gnidium f. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf daphne, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in early spring. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a distinctive glossy appearance. The plant has a woody stem and produces berries that turn red when mature.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
