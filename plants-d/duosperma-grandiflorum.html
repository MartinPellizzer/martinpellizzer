Duosperma grandiflorum

Duosperma grandiflorum, commonly known as the desert globemallow, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its rounded, succulent form and gray-green foliage. The plant produces showy, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its thick, fleshy leaves and stems are adapted to store water, enabling it to survive in harsh, dry environments.

Duosperma grandiflorum plays an important ecological role by providing nectar for pollinators and stabilizing sandy soils.
