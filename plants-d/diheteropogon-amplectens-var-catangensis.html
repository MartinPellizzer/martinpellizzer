Diheteropogon amplectens var. catangensis

Diheteropogon amplectens var. catangensis, commonly known as the catangensis variant of the diheteropogon, is a grass species native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, spikelet-like flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands or disturbed areas.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphological features and ecological role in its native habitat.
