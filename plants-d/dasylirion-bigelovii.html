Dasylirion bigelovii

Dasylirion bigelovii, commonly known as the desert dagger, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, sword-shaped leaves that are blue-gray in color and covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces clusters of white or pale yellow flowers in late spring, which are borne on tall, branched stems. It is adapted to arid environments and can store water in its thick, fleshy leaves.

Due to its hardiness and unique appearance, it is often cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
