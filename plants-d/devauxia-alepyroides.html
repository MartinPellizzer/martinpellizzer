Devauxia alepyroides

Devauxia alepyroides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved devauxia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by elongated, bean-like pods. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
