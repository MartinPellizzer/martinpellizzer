Diplacus clivicola

Diplacus clivicola, commonly known as the canyon lily, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils along canyon walls and slopes. It produces narrow, grass-like leaves and has a short, sturdy stem.

Diplacus clivicola is often found in arid or semi-arid environments and is adapted to thrive in full sun.
