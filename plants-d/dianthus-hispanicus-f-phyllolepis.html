Dianthus hispanicus f. phyllolepis

Dianthus hispanicus f. phyllolepis, commonly known as the leafy-leaved carnation, is a compact, herbaceous perennial with dense, foliage-covered stems.

Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and often covered in a fine, silvery-white pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. The flowers are typically pink or red, with five petals arranged in a cup-like shape, and they bloom in late spring to early summer. It is native to the Iberian Peninsula and thrives in well-drained soils in full sun.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental foliage and is often used in rock gardens and borders.
