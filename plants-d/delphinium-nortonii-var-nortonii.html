Delphinium nortonii var. nortonii

Delphinium nortonii var. nortonii, commonly known as the Norton's larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist meadows and rocky habitats at high elevations. The plant features tall, spindly stems with whorled, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its striking blue to purple flowers are arranged in dense spikes and bloom in late spring to early summer.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and unique form.
