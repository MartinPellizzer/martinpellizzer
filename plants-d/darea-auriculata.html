Darea auriculata

Darea auriculata, commonly known as the auriculate dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of thick, fleshy, hairless leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by fluffy white seeds that are easily dispersed by the wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Darea auriculata is often used in rock gardens and as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and unique flower structure.
