Dianthus fimbriatus var. monspeliacus

Dianthus fimbriatus var. monspeliacus, commonly known as the Alpine campion, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of pink or white flowers with fringed petals. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are linear and hairy, helping to reduce water loss in arid conditions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
