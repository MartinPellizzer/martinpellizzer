Doronicum longicaule

Doronicum longicaule, commonly known as the tall leopard's bane, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, yellow-orange flowers. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often covered in a whitish bloom, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Doronicum longicaule is also valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
