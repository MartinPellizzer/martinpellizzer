Dichanthelium pedicellatum

Dichanthelium pedicellatum, commonly known as the slender panicgrass, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences with two or three branches, typical of the grass family. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it thrives in moist to wet soils.

Dichanthelium pedicellatum is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.
