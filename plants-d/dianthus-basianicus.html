Dianthus basianicus

Dianthus basianicus, commonly known as the Armenian carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of showy, pink to red flowers with distinct white markings. The plant typically grows to a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters and has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, and it prefers well-drained soil in full sun.

Dianthus basianicus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
