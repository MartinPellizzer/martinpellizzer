Dioscorea multiflora var. grandifolia

Dioscorea multiflora var. grandifolia, commonly known as the large-flowered yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces large, showy white or pale yellow flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its tuberous roots are valued in traditional medicine and as a food source in some cultures.

The species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.
