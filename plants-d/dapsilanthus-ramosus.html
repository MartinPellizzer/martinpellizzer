Dapsilanthus ramosus

Dapsilanthus ramosus, commonly known as the creeping flame tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, shrub-like growth habit and produces clusters of bright orange-red flowers. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Dapsilanthus ramosus is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and birds.
