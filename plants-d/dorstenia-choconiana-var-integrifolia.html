Dorstenia choconiana var. integrifolia

Dorstenia choconiana var. integrifolia, commonly known as the "Curlleaf mountain mint," is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the highlands of Central America.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically entire or slightly toothed along the margins. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pale blue to purple in color, arranged in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded to partially shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and native plant collections.
