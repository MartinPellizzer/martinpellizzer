Diplotaxis virgata var. cavanillesiana

Diplotaxis virgata var. cavanillesiana, commonly known as the Cavanilles' desert cress, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial that produces small, white or yellowish flowers arranged in clusters. The plant has fibrous roots and succulent leaves adapted to conserve water in dry environments. Its flowers are typically borne on short pedicels, and the plant is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in harsh climates.
