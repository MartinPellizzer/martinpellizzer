Dianthus broteri var. macrophyllus

Dianthus broteri var. macrophyllus, commonly known as the giant leaf carnation, is a striking perennial plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red, with prominent white markings. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period, which extends from late spring through early autumn.
