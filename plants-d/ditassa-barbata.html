Ditassa barbata

Ditassa barbata, commonly known as the hairy leaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, hairy leaves and delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as rainforests, where it thrives in the understory. Its flowers are usually pale green or yellow, with a distinctive barbed structure on the petals.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
