Dissochaeta subviridis

Dissochaeta subviridis, commonly known as the greenstem false nettle, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds.

The species is characterized by its fibrous stems and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
