Desmodium subspicatum

Desmodium subspicatum, commonly known as small-spurred desmodium, is a leguminous plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant produces small, round pods that contain several seeds, and it is often found in dry, open habitats. It has a shallow root system and is adapted to arid environments, making it a hardy species in regions with limited rainfall.

Desmodium subspicatum is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to soil fertility.
