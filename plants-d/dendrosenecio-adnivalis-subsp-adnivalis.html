Dendrosenecio adnivalis subsp. adnivalis

Dendrosenecio adnivalis subsp.

adnivalis, commonly known as the alpine groundsel, is a member of the Asteraceae family. It is native to the high-altitude regions of southern Africa, particularly in the mountainous areas of South Africa and Lesotho.

This subspecies is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh, cold environments.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often cultivated in specialized gardens and conservation efforts.
