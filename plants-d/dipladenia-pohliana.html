Dipladenia pohliana

Dipladenia pohliana, commonly known as the golden trumpet vine, is a tropical flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant produces showy, funnel-shaped blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and ovate with a slightly hairy surface.

Dipladenia pohliana thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil and full sun for optimal growth.
