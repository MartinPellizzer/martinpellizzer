Dysoxylum roseum

Dysoxylum roseum, commonly known as the rosewood tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a large evergreen tree that can grow up to 30 meters in height, characterized by its dark, aromatic wood. The plant has compound leaves with long, narrow leaflets and produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Dysoxylum roseum is valued for its hard, dense wood used in furniture and musical instruments.
