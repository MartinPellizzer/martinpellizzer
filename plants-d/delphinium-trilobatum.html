Delphinium trilobatum

Delphinium trilobatum, commonly known as the three-lobed larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers in clusters at the top of the stems, with each flower having five petals and a distinctive spurred structure. Its leaves are compound, consisting of three leaflets that are deeply lobed, giving it its common name.

Delphinium trilobatum is adapted to grow in well-drained soils in full sun to partial shade.
