Draba diapensioides

Draba diapensioides, commonly known as the alpine mouse-ear cress, is a small flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. It features basal leaves that are rosette-shaped and small white flowers that bloom in early spring.

Draba diapensioides is often found in meadows and on limestone soils at high altitudes.
