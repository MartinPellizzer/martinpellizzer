Diasperus nemorum

Diasperus nemorum, commonly known as the mossy fern, is a small, delicate fern native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices. The plant has slender, upright fronds with a feathery appearance, and its spore-producing structures are clustered on the underside of the leaves. Diasperus nemorum is characterized by its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and its preference for well-drained, acidic soils.

Its botanical features make it a common sight in woodland ecosystems, where it contributes to the understory vegetation.
