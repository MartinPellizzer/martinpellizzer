Dombeya lucida var. genuina

Dombeya lucida var. genuina, commonly known as the white dombeya, is a species native to the eastern United States.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet, featuring showy, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has smooth, gray-green leaves with a waxy texture, and its stems are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
