Diospyros sandwicensis var. perpubescens

Diospyros sandwicensis var. perpubescens, commonly known as the Hawaiian persimmon, is a shrub or small tree native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark gray bark and alternate, ovate leaves with a glossy green upper surface and a lighter underside. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by edible, orange-red berries that are highly valued in traditional Hawaiian culture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland areas.

Due to habitat loss and invasive species, it is considered endangered and is protected under conservation efforts.
