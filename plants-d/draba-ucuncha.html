Draba ucuncha

Draba ucuncha, commonly known as the Andean bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes Mountains.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with cruciform flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments. It produces small, white or purple flowers that bloom in the spring.

Draba ucuncha is known for its resilience and is often found in alpine meadows.
