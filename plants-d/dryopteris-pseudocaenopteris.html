Dryopteris pseudocaenopteris

Dryopteris pseudocaenopteris, commonly known as the false holly fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Dryopteridaceae.

It is characterized by its upright, dense fronds that are typically dark green and have a leathery texture. The fronds are divided into segments that resemble holly leaves, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in temperate regions.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
