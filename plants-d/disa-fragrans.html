Disa fragrans

Disa fragrans, commonly known as the sweet-scented orchid, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are usually pink or white, with a distinctive sweet aroma that attracts pollinators.

Disa fragrans is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its role in local ecosystems.
