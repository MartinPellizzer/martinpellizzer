Dactylorhiza maculata subsp. sooana

Dactylorhiza maculata subsp.

sooana, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features a rhizomatous root system and produces tall, slender flowering stems with numerous showy, violet or purple flowers. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, and it is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.

This orchid is also notable for its role in supporting pollinators and its ecological significance in wetland ecosystems.
