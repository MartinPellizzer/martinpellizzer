Didymocarpus hwaianus

Didymocarpus hwaianus, commonly known as the Chinese hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional Chinese medicine.

The species is adapted to a variety of soil types and can thrive in both sunny and partially shaded environments.
