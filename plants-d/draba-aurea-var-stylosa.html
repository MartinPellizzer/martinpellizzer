Draba aurea var. stylosa

Draba aurea var. stylosa, commonly known as the yellow whitlow grass, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by narrow, linear leaves.

Draba aurea var. stylosa is known for its resilience and ability to thrive in nutrient-poor environments.
