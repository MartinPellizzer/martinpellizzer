Delissea umbrosa

Delissea umbrosa, commonly known as the shadowy delissea, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of South America.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors and stream banks. The plant features slender, upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. Its flowers are small, tubular, and usually violet or purple in color, arranged in dense clusters at the tips of the branches.

Delissea umbrosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable microclimates.
