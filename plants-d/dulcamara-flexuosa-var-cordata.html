Dulcamara flexuosa var. cordata

Dulcamara flexuosa var. cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf elderberry, is a perennial shrub native to temperate regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are typically dark green and have a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers in the spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found near streams or in woodland areas.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential health benefits.
