Datura arenicola

Datura arenicola, commonly known as the desert jimsonweed, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous annual that thrives in sandy and rocky soils, often found in desert and semi-desert environments. The plant produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale purple, which bloom at night. Its leaves are usually hairy and have a distinctive heart-shaped form, while its fruit is a spiny capsule that contains numerous seeds.

Datura arenicola is also known for its toxic properties, containing tropane alkaloids that can be harmful to humans and animals.
