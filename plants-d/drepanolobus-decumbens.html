Drepanolobus decumbens

Drepanolobus decumbens, commonly known as the creeping thornapple, is a species of flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in arid or semi-arid regions. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are usually ovate to lanceolate in shape and have a rough texture.

The fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.
