Dipterocarpus teres

Dipterocarpus teres, commonly known as the knobwood or hill teak, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, thick, and ridged bark. The tree produces large, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its wood is highly valued for its strength and durability, making it important in local timber industries.

The plant plays a significant role in the ecosystems of tropical forests, contributing to biodiversity and soil stability.
