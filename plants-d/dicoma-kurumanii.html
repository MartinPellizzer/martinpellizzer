Dicoma kurumanii

Dicoma kurumanii, commonly known as the Kuruman daisies, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is often found in regions with well-drained soils.

Dicoma kurumanii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
