Diasperus eriocarpus

Diasperus eriocarpus, commonly known as the hairy burr plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, hairy, spiny fruits that are adapted for wind dispersal. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in sandy or rocky soils. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The flowers are small and yellow, often clustered in dense heads, contributing to its ornamental appeal in some regions.
