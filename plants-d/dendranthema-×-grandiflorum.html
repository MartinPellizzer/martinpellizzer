Dendranthema × grandiflorum

Dendranthema × grandiflorum, commonly known as chamomile, is a flowering plant in the sunflower family.

It is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center. The plant is native to Europe and has been widely cultivated for its aromatic properties. It is often grown for ornamental purposes and for the production of essential oils.

Its leaves and flowers are used in herbal medicine and as a calming tea.
