Dalea cyanea

Dalea cyanea, commonly known as the blue milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and clusters of small, sky-blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as chaparral and desert scrub. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with small, oval leaflets that are often covered in fine hairs.

Dalea cyanea is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and possesses the ability to fix nitrogen through its root nodules.
