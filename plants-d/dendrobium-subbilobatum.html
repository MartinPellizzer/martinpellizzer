Dendrobium subbilobatum

Dendrobium subbilobatum, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, cane-like stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, golden-yellow flowers that bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem.

Dendrobium subbilobatum thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
