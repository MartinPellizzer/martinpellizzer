Diosma nigra

Diosma nigra, commonly known as black diosma, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are usually dark green. Its flowers are small, white or pale pink, and arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Diosma nigra is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local wildlife.
