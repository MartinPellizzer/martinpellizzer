Dyckia insignis

Dyckia insignis, commonly known as the silver needle plant, is a species of bromeliad native to South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of white, powdery trichomes, giving it a silvery appearance. The plant produces tall, spiky inflorescences that bear small, tubular flowers, typically in shades of red or orange. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Dyckia insignis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique and striking visual appeal.
