Doronicum bellidiastrum

Doronicum bellidiastrum, commonly known as the alpine goldcup, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in early summer, which are surrounded by narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows and forested slopes.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
