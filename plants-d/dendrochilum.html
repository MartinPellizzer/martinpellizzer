Dendrochilum

Dendrochilum, commonly known as the tree orchid, is a genus of flowering plants in the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is characterized by its epiphytic or lithophytic growth habit, often found growing on trees or rocks in tropical and subtropical regions. The plants typically have long, arching stems with numerous leaves and produce clusters of fragrant, showy flowers. Dendrochilum species are prized for their ornamental value and are often cultivated for their unique appearance and fragrance.

They thrive in humid, well-drained environments with indirect sunlight.
