Dendrocalamus concaviapiculus

Dendrocalamus concaviapiculus, commonly known as the broad-leaved giant bamboo, is a member of the grass family, Poaceae, and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 20 meters, making it one of the tallest bamboo species. The plant has broad, flat leaves with a distinctive waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in its native humid environments. Its rhizome system is extensive, allowing it to spread rapidly and form dense clumps.

This bamboo is valued for its strong, lightweight culms, which are used in construction and crafts.
