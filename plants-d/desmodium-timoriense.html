Desmodium timoriense

Desmodium timoriense, commonly known as the Timor velvet bean, is a flowering plant native to the Indonesian island of Timor.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines.

It thrives in tropical climates and is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
