Delphinium peregrinum var. tribracteolatum

Delphinium peregrinum var. tribracteolatum, commonly known as the alpine larkspur, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Alps and surrounding regions.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture, and its flowers are typically arranged in dense, terminal spikes. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, mountainous climates.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in alpine gardens.
