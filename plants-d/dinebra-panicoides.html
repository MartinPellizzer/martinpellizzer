Dinebra panicoides

Dinebra panicoides, commonly known as the panic grass, is a species of grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets. It is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Dinebra panicoides is primarily valued for its use in erosion control and as a forage crop for livestock.
