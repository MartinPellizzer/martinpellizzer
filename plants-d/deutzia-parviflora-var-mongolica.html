Deutzia parviflora var. mongolica

Deutzia parviflora var. mongolica, commonly known as Mongolian deutzia, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Mongolia and northern China.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring multiple stems covered in grayish bark. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers in late spring to early summer, which are borne on slender pedicels. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a rough texture and a serrated margin.

The species is valued for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its spring bloom and adaptability to various soil types.
