Diplacus kelloggii

Diplacus kelloggii, commonly known as Kellogg's honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are usually pink or red, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a rough texture, while its stems are covered in fine hairs.

Kellogg's honeysuckle is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging conditions.
