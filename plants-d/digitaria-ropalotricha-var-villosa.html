Digitaria ropalotricha var. villosa

Digitaria ropalotricha var. villosa, commonly known as silky fingergrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and hairy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences, and its flowering occurs during the warm season. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open fields, lawns, and disturbed areas.

The variety villosa is distinguished by its dense, silky hairs on the leaves, contributing to its ornamental value.
