Draba subalpina

Draba subalpina, commonly known as the alpine bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cress plants. This species typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, often in mountainous areas. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in a raceme, and its leaves are narrow and often hairy.

Draba subalpina is adapted to cold climates and can survive in harsh, nutrient-poor environments.
