Dahlia × cultorum

Dahlia × cultorum, commonly known as the garden dahlia, is a hybrid tuberous-rooted perennial plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, radially symmetrical flowers that come in a wide range of colors. The plant produces large, fleshy tubers that store nutrients and water, allowing it to survive in various climates. Its foliage consists of heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems.

Dahlia × cultorum is widely cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.
