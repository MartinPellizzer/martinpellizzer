Draba daurica var. lutescens

Draba daurica var. lutescens, commonly known as the yellow dwarf cabbage, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May. It thrives in rocky or alpine environments and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Its leaves are basal, forming a rosette, and the plant is known for its hardy nature and ability to survive in cold climates.
