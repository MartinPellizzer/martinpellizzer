Dianthera dasyclados

Dianthera dasyclados, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue to purple, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Dianthera dasyclados is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
