Diplazium asperulum

Diplazium asperulum, commonly known as the rough serrata, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its dark green, leathery fronds. The plant has a creeping rhizome that produces both sterile and fertile fronds. Its leaves are typically bi- or tri-pinnate, with serrated margins that contribute to its rough texture.

Diplazium asperulum thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
