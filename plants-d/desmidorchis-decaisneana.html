Desmidorchis decaisneana

Desmidorchis decaisneana, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers. The plant typically grows in wet, shaded environments such as forest floors or swampy areas. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

The flowers are clustered in inflorescences and are pollinated primarily by insects.
