Dombeya ledermannii

Dombeya ledermannii, commonly known as the Ledermann's dombeya, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or forested habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it is relatively rare in the wild.
