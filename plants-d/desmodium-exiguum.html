Desmodium exiguum

Desmodium exiguum, commonly known as the smallleaf burrweed, is a low-growing annual legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features trifoliate leaves with small, rounded leaflets and produces small, yellowish to brownish flowers. The plant is characterized by its unique, spiny pods that enclose minute, kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and woodland edges.

Its botanical features make it an important plant in ecological studies and agricultural contexts.
