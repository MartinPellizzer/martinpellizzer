Darwinia meeboldii

Darwinia meeboldii, commonly known as the Meebold's spike-lily, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, tall spikes of tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers, which range in color from pink to purple, attract nectar-feeding birds and insects.

Darwinia meeboldii is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
