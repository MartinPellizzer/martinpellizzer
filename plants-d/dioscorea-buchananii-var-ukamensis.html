Dioscorea buchananii var. ukamensis

Dioscorea buchananii var. ukamensis, commonly known as the KwaZulu-Natal yam, is a climbing vine belonging to the Dioscoreaceae family.

It is native to southern Africa, particularly found in regions of South Africa, Mozambique, and Zimbabwe. The plant produces tuberous roots that are used as a food source in local communities. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while the flowers are small and unisexual.

The species is valued both for its nutritional content and traditional medicinal uses.
