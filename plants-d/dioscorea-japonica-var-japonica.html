Dioscorea japonica var. japonica

Dioscorea japonica var. japonica, commonly known as Japanese yam, is a perennial vine native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and tuberous roots. The plant produces small, green, heart-shaped leaves and yellowish-white flowers. Its tubers are used in traditional medicine and as a food source in some regions.

The species is valued for its medicinal properties and adaptability to various soil conditions.
