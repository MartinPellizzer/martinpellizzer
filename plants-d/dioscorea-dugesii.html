Dioscorea dugesii

Dioscorea dugesii, commonly known as the Mexican yam, is a perennial vine native to Central America and parts of the Caribbean.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and tuberous roots. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its tubers are often used in traditional medicine and have been studied for their potential medicinal properties.

Dioscorea dugesii thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.
