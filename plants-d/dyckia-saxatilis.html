Dyckia saxatilis

Dyckia saxatilis, commonly known as the rock dyckia, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its compact, rosette-forming structure. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of trichomes, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It produces striking, long-lasting inflorescences that can range in color from yellow to red.

Dyckia saxatilis is well adapted to rocky and arid environments, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.
