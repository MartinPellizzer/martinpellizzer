Diospyros soubreana

Diospyros soubreana, commonly known as the African persimmon, is a small to medium-sized deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other persimmon and ebony species. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by orange to red, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and ovate with a glossy surface.

The wood of Diospyros soubreana is dense and valued for its durability and use in traditional crafts and construction.
