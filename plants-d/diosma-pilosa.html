Diosma pilosa

Diosma pilosa, commonly known as the hairy diosma, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale purple flowers that bloom in summer. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds.

Diosma pilosa is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
