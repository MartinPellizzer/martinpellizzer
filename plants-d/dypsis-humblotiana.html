Dypsis humblotiana

Dypsis humblotiana, commonly known as the Madagascar palm, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its short, thick trunk and dense clusters of spiny, leaf-like structures. The plant has a slow growth rate and is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Dypsis humblotiana is also known for its ability to store water in its stem, making it drought-tolerant.
