Desmodium bioculatum

Desmodium bioculatum, commonly known as the clustered burr legume, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and produces small, yellow to reddish-brown pods that contain seeds with hooked spines. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and has the ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen through symbiotic bacteria in its root nodules.

Its growth habit makes it both a useful forage plant and a potential weed in agricultural settings.
