Diplotaxis assurgens

Diplotaxis assurgens, commonly known as the African ironweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that grows up to 1 meter in height, characterized by its upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in dense, terminal clusters, which bloom from late summer to autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Diplotaxis assurgens is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
