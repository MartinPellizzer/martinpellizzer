Drypetes bisacuta

Drypetes bisacuta, commonly known as the white-flowered ironwood, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and opposite, ovate leaves that are often pubescent underneath. The plant produces small, white flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. It is adapted to grow in tropical and subtropical climates, often in moist forest environments.

The tree is valued for its hard, durable wood and is also used in traditional medicine.
