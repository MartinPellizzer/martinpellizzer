Daemonorops pseudomirabilis

Daemonorops pseudomirabilis, commonly known as the golden banyan, is a flowering plant belonging to the Arecaceae family.

It is a large, evergreen tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, characterized by its broad, paddle-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, which are followed by bright red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
