Dioscorea rimbachii

Dioscorea rimbachii, commonly known as the African yambean, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into cylindrical, fleshy fruits. Its tuberous roots are used in traditional medicine and as a food source in some regions.

The species is notable for its adaptability to various soil types and its role in local ecosystems.
