Dichostylis glabra

Dichostylis glabra, commonly known as the smooth dichostylis, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Africa.

It belongs to the grass family, Poaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear both male and female flowers, a feature known as dioecious reproduction. Its botanical significance lies in its role within local ecosystems and its potential use in traditional medicine.

Due to its delicate structure and specific habitat requirements, it is often found in moist, open habitats such as grasslands and riverbanks.
