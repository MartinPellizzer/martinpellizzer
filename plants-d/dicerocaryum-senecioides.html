Dicerocaryum senecioides

Dicerocaryum senecioides, commonly known as the silverleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, white hairs, which contribute to its distinctive appearance.

The flowers bloom in late spring and early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
