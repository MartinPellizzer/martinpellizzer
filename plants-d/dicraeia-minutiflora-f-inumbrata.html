Dicraeia minutiflora f. inumbrata

Dicraeia minutiflora f. inumbrata, commonly known as the dark-flowered dwarf broom.

It is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, characterized by its small, dark green leaves and clusters of tiny, pale yellow to white flowers. The species belongs to the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing abilities. Its compact growth habit and unique floral structure make it a distinctive member of its genus.

The varietal form 'inumbrata' is distinguished by its darker, more subdued flower color compared to the typical form.
