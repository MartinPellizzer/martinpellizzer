Delphinium englerianum

Delphinium englerianum, commonly known as the Engler's larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine or subalpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils and cooler climates. Its leaves are compound, with lance-shaped lobes, and the flowers are showy, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and protected areas.
