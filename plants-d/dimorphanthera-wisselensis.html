Dimorphanthera wisselensis

Dimorphanthera wisselensis, commonly known as the Wissel River orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of fragrant, showy flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils along riverbanks and in moist, shaded habitats. Its unique floral structure includes both regular and irregularly shaped petals, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Dimorphanthera wisselensis is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its potential for horticultural cultivation.
