Deschampsia cespitosa var. komarovii

Deschampsia cespitosa var. komarovii, commonly known as Komarov's bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and tufted growth habit, forming dense, low-growing clumps. The plant produces slender, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing mountainous terrain.
