Digitaria ischaemum subsp. asiatica

Digitaria ischaemum subsp.

asiatica, commonly known as Asiatic Digitaria, is a grass species native to Asia. It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and narrow, linear leaves.

The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically arranged in panicles. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Asiatic Digitaria is often found in disturbed areas, such as roadsides and fields, and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
