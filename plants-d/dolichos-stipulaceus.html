Dolichos stipulaceus

Dolichos stipulaceus, commonly known as the Indian cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with stipules, and its small, yellow flowers that develop into elongated pods. The plant is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil fertility. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its edible seeds and forage value.

Dolichos stipulaceus is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
