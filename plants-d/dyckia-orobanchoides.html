Dyckia orobanchoides

Dyckia orobanchoides, commonly known as the ghost plant, is a unique bromeliad species native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-shaped growth habit and its ability to thrive in shaded, humid environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often hidden within its foliage. Its leaves are typically green with a waxy coating, and it is known for its ability to store water in its rosette structure.

Dyckia orobanchoides is a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its low maintenance and distinctive appearance.
