Dalbergia multifoliolata

Dalbergia multifoliolata, commonly known as the African rosewood, is a tropical tree native to eastern and southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, dark-colored wood, which is highly valued for its aesthetic and functional properties. The plant has compound leaves with multiple leaflets, typically arranged in an odd-pinnate pattern. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are followed by pod-like fruits containing several seeds.

This species is often found in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in warm, humid climates.
