Digitaria australis

Digitaria australis, commonly known as the common fingergrass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in grasslands, disturbed areas, and along roadsides.

Digitaria australis is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide forage for livestock.
