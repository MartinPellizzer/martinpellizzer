Datura metel var. rubra

Datura metel var. rubra, commonly known as the red belladonna, is a flowering plant belonging to the nightshade family (Solanaceae).

It is characterized by its large, showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically deep purple to red in color. The plant produces spiny, berry-like fruits that contain toxic seeds. It is native to regions of Asia, including China and India, and thrives in warm, dry climates.

Due to its toxic properties, it is often considered a noxious weed and requires caution when handling.
