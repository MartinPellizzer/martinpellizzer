Diospyros cinnabarina

Diospyros cinnabarina, commonly known as the red sapote, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and broad, ovate leaves that are glossy on the upper surface. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that give way to orange-red, fleshy fruits with a sweet, creamy pulp. Its fruit is highly valued for its flavor and is often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications.

The plant is also noted for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
