Dalbergaria pulcherrima

Dalbergaria pulcherrima, commonly known as the purple heart plant, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and showy, violet-pink flowers. The plant is evergreen and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun to partial shade. Its ornamental value comes from its striking foliage and long-lasting blooms, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

It is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding plants.
