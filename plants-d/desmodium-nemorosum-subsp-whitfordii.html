Desmodium nemorosum subsp. whitfordii

Desmodium nemorosum subsp.

whitfordii, commonly known as the white-flowered ticktrefoil, is a perennial legume native to southern Africa. It features small, trifoliate leaves and produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers.

The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and is often found in grassy habitats. It has a deep root system that helps prevent soil erosion.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting pollinators.
