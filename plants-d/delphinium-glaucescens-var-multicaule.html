Delphinium glaucescens var. multicaule

Delphinium glaucescens var. multicaule, commonly known as the blue delphinium, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear clusters of striking blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in open, rocky habitats and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery blue-gray coating.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
