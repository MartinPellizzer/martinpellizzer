Delphinium bithynicum

Delphinium bithynicum, commonly known as the Bithynian larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Turkey and the Caucasus.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, spurred petals and erect growth habit. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring compound leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are usually blue to purple in color, with five petals and a distinctive nectar spur.

Delphinium bithynicum thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or alpine environments.
