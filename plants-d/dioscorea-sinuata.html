Dioscorea sinuata

Dioscorea sinuata, commonly known as the zigzag yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its sinuous, serpentine stems that twist and turn as it climbs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by tuberous roots that are sometimes used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a waxy surface and serrated edges.

Dioscorea sinuata is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens due to its unique growth habit.
