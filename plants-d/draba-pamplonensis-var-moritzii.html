Draba pamplonensis var. moritzii

Draba pamplonensis var. moritzii, commonly known as the Moritz's rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant is typically found in rocky or gravelly habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. It has basal leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of white hairs, which help reduce water loss.

This variety is distinguished by its compact growth habit and pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.
