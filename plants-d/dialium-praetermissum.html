Dialium praetermissum

Dialium praetermissum, commonly known as the African plum or African cherry, is a tropical tree native to Central and West Africa.

It belongs to the family Sapotaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by edible, orange-red fruits that are highly valued for their flavor and nutritional content.

Its seeds are encased in a hard, woody pulp and are often used in traditional medicine and local cuisines.
