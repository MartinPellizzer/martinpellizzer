Dianthus nobilis

Dianthus nobilis, commonly known as the carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, pink, white, and purple. The plant typically grows to a height of 20 to 60 centimeters and features narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are composed of numerous petals that are often arranged in a radial pattern, giving the appearance of a pom-pom.

Dianthus nobilis is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is known for its aromatic scent and long-lasting blooms.
