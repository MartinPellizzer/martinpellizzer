Dioscorea grisebachii

Dioscorea grisebachii, commonly known as the Grisebach yam, is a member of the Dioscoreaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a herbaceous vine characterized by its climbing habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by cylindrical, fleshy fruits containing numerous seeds. It is valued for its starchy tubers, which are used in traditional cuisines and have potential medicinal properties.

The species is also of interest in botanical research due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
