Diplopappus asperrimus

Diplopappus asperrimus, commonly known as the rough pappus plant, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, hairy leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a dry, two-part capsule that releases tiny seeds with tufts of hair for wind dispersal.

It thrives in dry, open habitats and is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils.
