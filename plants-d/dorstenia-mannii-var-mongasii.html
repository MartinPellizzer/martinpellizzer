Dorstenia mannii var. mongasii

Dorstenia mannii var. mongasii, commonly known as the Mongasii woolly fig, is a unique species native to specific regions of East Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fleshy, and often hairy leaves that are adapted to arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a subtle ornamental appeal. Its growth habit is typically shrub-like, with a compact form that makes it suitable for rock gardens or container cultivation.

The botanical features of this variety highlight its resilience and adaptability to challenging ecological conditions.
