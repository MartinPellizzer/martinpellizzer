Didymoglossum punctatum subsp. labiatum

Didymoglossum punctatum subsp.

labiatum, commonly known as the broad-leaved mossy orchid, is a small, terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and rocky crevices.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in late spring. Its inflorescence is a spike of numerous tiny flowers, which are often clustered in pairs.

The species is characterized by its distinctive leaf shape and is often found in association with other orchid species in its habitat.
