Drypetes alba

Drypetes alba, commonly known as the white flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and large, palmate leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in catkins, followed by small, woody fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its rapid growth and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a shade tree or for erosion control.
