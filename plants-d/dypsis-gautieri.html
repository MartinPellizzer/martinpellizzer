Dypsis gautieri

Dypsis gautieri, commonly known as the Madagascar bamboo palm, is a member of the Arecaceae family.

It is native to Madagascar and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and feather-like leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 meters and has a clumping growth habit. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a bluish-green color that gives it a distinctive appearance.

Dypsis gautieri is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various indoor environments.
