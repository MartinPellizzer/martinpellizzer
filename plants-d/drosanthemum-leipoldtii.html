Drosanthemum leipoldtii

Drosanthemum leipoldtii, commonly known as the Leipoldt's daisies, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and white. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens. Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Drosanthemum leipoldtii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
