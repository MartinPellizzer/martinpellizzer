Daphniphyllum laurinum

Daphniphyllum laurinum, commonly known as the Chinese silverleaf tea plant, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It belongs to the family Pentaphylacaceae and is characterized by its leathery, glossy, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and silvery white underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically white or pale yellow, which bloom in spring. Its unique leaf structure and ornamental value make it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation.

It prefers acidic, well-drained soil and partial shade, thriving in temperate climates.
