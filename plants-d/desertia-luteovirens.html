Desertia luteovirens

Desertia luteovirens, commonly known as the yellow-green desert plant, is a succulent native to arid regions of North America.

It features fleshy, paddle-shaped leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in dry environments. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers during the spring and summer months. Its root system is shallow but extensive, enabling it to absorb moisture from light rains.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often used in xeriscaping and desert gardens.
