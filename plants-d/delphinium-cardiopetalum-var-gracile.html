Delphinium cardiopetalum var. gracile

Delphinium cardiopetalum var. gracile, commonly known as the slender larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features tall, erect stems that bear clusters of striking blue to purple flowers, which are typically spurred at the base. The leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem, contributing to its ornamental appeal. This variety is valued for its delicate appearance and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile choice for alpine gardens.
