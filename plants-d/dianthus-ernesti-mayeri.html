Dianthus ernesti-mayeri

Dianthus ernesti-mayeri, commonly known as the Ernesti-Mayer's carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that typically range in color from pink to red. The plant features compact, bushy growth with upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are borne in clusters, and each bloom consists of five petals with a distinctive, frilled edge.

Dianthus ernesti-mayeri is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.
