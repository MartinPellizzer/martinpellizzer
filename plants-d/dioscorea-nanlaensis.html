Dioscorea nanlaensis

Dioscorea nanlaensis, commonly known as the Nanla yam, is a species of tuberous plant native to China.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers and develops edible tubers that are similar to other yam species. Its botanical features make it a valuable resource in traditional medicine and local agriculture.

The tubers are often used in culinary applications and are known for their nutritional value.
