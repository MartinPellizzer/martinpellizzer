Dendrobium takadui

Dendrobium takadui, commonly known as the Mindanao orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its clustered, cane-like pseudobulbs. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and orange, often with intricate patterns. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Dendrobium takadui is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for horticultural purposes.
