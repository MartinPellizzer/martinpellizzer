Duhaldea rubricaulis

Duhaldea rubricaulis, commonly known as the red-stemmed bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, reddish stems and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to violet. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in high-altitude environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in a fine layer of hair.

Duhaldea rubricaulis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique floral display.
