Dendrobium echinocarpum

Dendrobium echinocarpum, commonly known as the spiny fruit orchid, is a sympodial epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a tall, branching stem covered with persistent, leathery leaves. The plant produces large, showy flowers in shades of yellow and orange, often with red or brown markings. Its inflorescence is racemose, bearing numerous blooms that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Dendrobium echinocarpum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in tropical gardens and greenhouses.
