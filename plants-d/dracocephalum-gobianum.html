Dracocephalum gobianum

Dracocephalum gobianum, commonly known as Gobi Dracocephalum, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its small, blue to purple flowers and hairy, lance-shaped leaves adapted to withstand harsh desert conditions. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in semi-desert and steppe environments.

Due to its unique adaptations, it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for local wildlife.
