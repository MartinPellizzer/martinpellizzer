Deparia conilii f. yakusimensis

Deparia conilii f. yakusimensis, commonly known as the Yakusim Island shield fern, is a rare fern species native to Japan.

It belongs to the Dryopteridaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, leathery fronds with a broad, shield-like shape. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its fronds are often covered in a dense layer of brownish spores, which are released during the reproductive cycle.

This fern is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and limited distribution.
