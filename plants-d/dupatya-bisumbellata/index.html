Dupatya Bisumbellata

Dupatya bisumbellata is a rare and striking plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia. Known for its unique, umbrella-like inflorescence, it captivates botanists and nature enthusiasts alike. This flowering plant belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes familiar species like carrots and parsley. Its delicate white and purple blooms appear during the spring and summer months, making it a sought-after addition to botanical gardens. Due to habitat loss, Dupatya bisumbellata is increasingly threatened, highlighting the need for conservation efforts.
