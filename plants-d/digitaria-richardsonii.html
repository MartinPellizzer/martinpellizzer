Digitaria richardsonii

Digitaria richardsonii, commonly known as hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and dense, grass-like growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often in wetlands or along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, two-ranked spikelets.

Digitaria richardsonii plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
