Didymocarpus corneri

Didymocarpus corneri, commonly known as the Kew's daphniphyllum, is a species of flowering plant in the family Daphniphyllaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia, particularly in Myanmar and Thailand. The plant is characterized by its evergreen leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
