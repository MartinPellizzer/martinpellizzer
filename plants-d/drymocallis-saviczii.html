Drymocallis saviczii

Drymocallis saviczii, commonly known as the Siberian goldenrod, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of yellow to orange flowers. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and along riverbanks, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and often reddish in color.

Drymocallis saviczii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators and providing habitat for various wildlife.
