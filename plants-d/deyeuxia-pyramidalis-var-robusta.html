Deyeuxia pyramidalis var. robusta

Deyeuxia pyramidalis var. robusta, commonly known as the robust panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the wetlands and marshes of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical inflorescence that resembles a pyramid, giving it its common name. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green and slightly hairy. It thrives in moist to wet soil conditions and is often found in riparian zones and seasonally flooded areas.

Due to its adaptability and ecological importance, it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
