Dyschoriste biloba

Dyschoriste biloba, commonly known as the African bluebell, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Dyschoriste biloba is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
