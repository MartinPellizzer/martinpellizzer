Delphinium geyeri f. viscidum

Delphinium geyeri f. viscidum, commonly known as the sticky delphinium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant produces showy blue to purple spires of flowers, which are arranged in dense clusters at the top of the stem. Its leaves are lance-shaped, serrated, and often covered with a sticky, glandular coating, giving it its common name.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
