Dichapetalum filicaule

Dichapetalum filicaule, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a waxy texture. Its stems are usually hairy and can grow up to several meters in height.

Dichapetalum filicaule is often found in open woodlands and along riverbanks, thriving in well-drained soils.
