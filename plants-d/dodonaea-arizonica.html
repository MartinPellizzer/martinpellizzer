Dodonaea arizonica

Dodonaea arizonica, commonly known as Arizona soapberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are opposite and have a distinctive, slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by spherical, spiny fruits containing a single seed. It is adapted to arid environments and has a deep root system to access water.

The plant is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, which enriches the soil.
