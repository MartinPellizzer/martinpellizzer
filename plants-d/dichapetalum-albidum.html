Dichapetalum albidum

Dichapetalum albidum, commonly known as the white-flowered dichapetalum, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a slightly serrated margin.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
