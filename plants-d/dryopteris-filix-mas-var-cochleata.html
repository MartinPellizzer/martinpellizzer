Dryopteris filix-mas var. cochleata

Dryopteris filix-mas var. cochleata, commonly known as the curled fern or the zigzag fern, is a variant of the male fern characterized by its distinctive fronds.

Its fronds are typically curled and have a zigzag appearance, which gives it a unique visual appeal. The plant is native to parts of Asia and is often found in moist, shaded environments. It produces sporangia on the underside of its fronds, which are essential for reproduction.

This variety is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to various garden settings.
