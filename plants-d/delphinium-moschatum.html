Delphinium moschatum

Delphinium moschatum, commonly known as the sweet delphinium, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that bear clusters of striking blue to purple flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its showy blooms attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
