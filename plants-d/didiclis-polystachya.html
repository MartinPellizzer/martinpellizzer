Didiclis polystachya

Didiclis polystachya, commonly known as the Madagascar lily, is a striking plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to 3 meters in height. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and often have a waxy texture.

Didiclis polystachya is well-suited for tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
