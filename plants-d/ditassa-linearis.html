Ditassa linearis

Ditassa linearis, commonly known as the slender ditassa, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that typically bloom in shades of purple or blue. The plant thrives in cool, humid environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats. Its flowers are adapted for pollination by insects, particularly bees and butterflies.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
