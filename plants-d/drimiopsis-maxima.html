Drimiopsis maxima

Drimiopsis maxima, commonly known as the giant drimiopsis, is a striking evergreen perennial plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its large, strap-like leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length, often with a waxy texture and a deep green color. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. It prefers well-drained soil and bright, indirect light, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.

Due to its slow growth and unique appearance, it is valued as a decorative plant in both gardens and collections.
