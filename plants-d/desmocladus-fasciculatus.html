Desmocladus fasciculatus

Desmocladus fasciculatus, commonly known as the clustered desmocladus, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, clustered leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the rainy season. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments through its succulent leaves and deep root system.

Desmocladus fasciculatus is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental appeal in dryland gardens.
