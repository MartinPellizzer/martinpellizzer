Disporum pullum

Disporum pullum, commonly known as the Pacific silver fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its slender, columnar shape and its ability to thrive in high-altitude environments. The tree has needle-like leaves that are blue-green in color, and it produces small, cone-like structures that contain its seeds. Disporum pullum is adapted to cold climates and is often found in subalpine and alpine zones.

Its botanical features make it an important component of forest ecosystems in its native range.
