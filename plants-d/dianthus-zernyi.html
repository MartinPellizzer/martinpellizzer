Dianthus zernyi

Dianthus zernyi, commonly known as the Armenian carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, delicate petals that resemble those of other carnation species. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Dianthus zernyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its beauty in gardens and horticultural collections.
