Diplotaxis muralis subsp. simplex

Diplotaxis muralis subsp.

simplex, commonly known as the simple wallflower, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa. It is a low-growing annual or biennial herb with slender, branched stems and small, clustered yellow flowers.

The plant features alternate, linear to lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its cheerful blooms.
