Dysoxylum apoense

Dysoxylum apoense, commonly known as the Philippine wild mango, is a tropical tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The fruit is a drupe, typically green when unripe and turning yellow or red when mature. It is valued for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in local medicine.
