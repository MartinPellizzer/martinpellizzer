Drosera planchonii

Drosera planchonii, commonly known as the Cape Town Sundew, is a species of carnivorous plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette of hairy, glandular leaves that secrete a sticky substance to trap insects. The plant typically grows in wet, acidic environments such as bogs and fens. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in a spike, and it blooms during the summer months.

Drosera planchonii is a hardy species that can survive in a variety of conditions, making it a popular choice for carnivorous plant enthusiasts.
