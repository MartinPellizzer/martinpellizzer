Dryopetalon byei

Dryopetalon byei, commonly known as the golden bell orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, shaded environments such as undergrowth in forests. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, and the flowers bloom during the warmer months, attracting various pollinators.

Dryopetalon byei is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
