Diplazium rodriguezii

Diplazium rodriguezii, commonly known as Rodriguez's water fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, floating fronds that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and streams. Its rhizomes are often submerged, anchoring the plant and facilitating nutrient absorption.

Diplazium rodriguezii plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small organisms and contributing to water filtration.
