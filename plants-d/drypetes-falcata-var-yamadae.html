Drypetes falcata var. yamadae

Drypetes falcata var. yamadae, commonly known as Yamada's False-toothed Tree, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows in lowland forests. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptic to ovate in shape with a smooth or slightly serrated margin. Its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, and the fruit is a capsule containing several seeds.

The plant is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its foliage.
