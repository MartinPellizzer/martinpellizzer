Derris chinensis

Derris chinensis, commonly known as Chinese snakeroot, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Leguminosae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is notable for containing rotenone, a toxic compound used in traditional pest control. Its roots are often harvested for their medicinal and pesticidal properties.

Derris chinensis thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
