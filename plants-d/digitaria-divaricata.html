Digitaria divaricata

Digitaria divaricata, commonly known as slender fingergrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in open areas, including roadsides, pastures, and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, spreading stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, two-ranked spikelets.

Digitaria divaricata is known for its adaptability and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
