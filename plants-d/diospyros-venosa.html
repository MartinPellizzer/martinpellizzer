Diospyros venosa

Diospyros venosa, commonly known as the velvet apple tree, is a tropical species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and glossy, elliptical leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by edible, round fruits that resemble apples in appearance. The fruit has a sweet, subacid flavor and is often consumed fresh or used in local cuisines.

It is also valued for its ornamental qualities and potential medicinal properties.
