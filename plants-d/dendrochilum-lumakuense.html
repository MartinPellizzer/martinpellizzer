Dendrochilum lumakuense

Dendrochilum lumakuense, commonly known as the Lumakuense orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its epiphytic growth habit, often found growing on trees or rocks in humid environments. The plant produces strikingly beautiful flowers with vibrant colors, typically in shades of yellow and orange. Its pseudobulbs are thick and fleshy, adapted to store water and nutrients in its natural habitat.

Dendrochilum lumakuense is valued not only for its ornamental appeal but also for its ecological significance in supporting biodiversity.
