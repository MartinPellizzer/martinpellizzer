Dactylorhiza romana

Dactylorhiza romana, commonly known as the Roman orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wet meadows. The plant has a rhizomatous root system and produces a single, upright stem bearing several lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually pale pink or white, arranged in a loose spike, and are characterized by their distinctive three-lobed petals.

Dactylorhiza romana is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its delicate, ephemeral blooms that appear in early spring.
