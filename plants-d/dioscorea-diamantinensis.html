Dioscorea diamantinensis

Dioscorea diamantinensis, commonly known as the Diamond Hill yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary part used for culinary and medicinal purposes. The plant produces small, green, heart-shaped leaves and yellowish flowers that appear in clusters. Its tubers are typically white or yellowish in color and have a starchy texture.

Due to its unique properties, it is of interest in both traditional medicine and modern agricultural research.
