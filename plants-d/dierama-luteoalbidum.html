Dierama luteoalbidum

Dierama luteoalbidum, commonly known as the yellow-white dierama, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Restionaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces striking, showy inflorescences composed of numerous small, white to yellowish flowers. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation.

Dierama luteoalbidum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
