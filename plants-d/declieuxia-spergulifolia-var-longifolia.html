Declieuxia spergulifolia var. longifolia

Declieuxia spergulifolia var. longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved variegated sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically variegated with white or yellow markings along the midribs. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences of greenish-white flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for water gardens and bog plantings.
