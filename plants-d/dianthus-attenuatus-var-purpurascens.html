Dianthus attenuatus var. purpurascens

Dianthus attenuatus var. purpurascens, commonly known as the purple-fringed carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, double-petaled flowers that range in color from pink to deep red. The leaves are linear and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are upright and somewhat woody. This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and is often cultivated for its vibrant blooms and long-lasting flowers.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
