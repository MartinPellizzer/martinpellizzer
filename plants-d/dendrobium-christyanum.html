Dendrobium christyanum

Dendrobium christyanum, commonly known as the Phalaenopsis orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, cane-like stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.

Dendrobium christyanum is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
