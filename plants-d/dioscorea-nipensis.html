Dioscorea nipensis

Dioscorea nipensis, commonly known as the Philippine yam, is a climbing vine native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary edible part. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers. Its tubers are used in local cuisines and have been traditionally used in herbal medicine.

The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments.
