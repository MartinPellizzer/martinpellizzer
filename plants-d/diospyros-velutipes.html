Diospyros velutipes

Diospyros velutipes, commonly known as the velvet-leaf persimmon, is a species of flowering plant in the Ebenaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including regions of China, India, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety pubescence. Its fruit is typically a small, yellow or orange berry that ripens in the fall.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
