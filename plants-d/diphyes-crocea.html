Diphyes crocea

Diphyes crocea, commonly known as the golden groundsel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a basal rosette of fleshy, lobed leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces bright yellow composite flowers in the summer, consisting of a central disk surrounded by ray florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it an attractive addition to rock gardens and native plant collections.
