Dactylopsis digitata subsp. littlewoodii

Dactylopsis digitata subsp.

littlewoodii, commonly known as the Littlewood's date palm, is a subtropical plant native to southern Africa. It is a small, ornamental palm with a slender trunk and feathery, fan-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that eventually develop into edible dates. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its compact size and attractive foliage, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a decorative plant.
