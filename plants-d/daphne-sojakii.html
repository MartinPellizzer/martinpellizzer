Daphne sojakii

Daphne sojakii, commonly known as the Persian daphne, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, often appearing in late winter or early spring. The plant has smooth, grayish bark and elliptical leaves that are dark green and glossy. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Daphne sojakii is valued for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its early blooming habit.
