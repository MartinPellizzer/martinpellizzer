Dodonaea inaequifolia

Dodonaea inaequifolia, commonly known as the variable hopwoodia, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its small, ovate to elliptic leaves that are often asymmetrical, giving it its species name "inaequifolia.". The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by woody, bead-like fruits. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Dodonaea inaequifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
