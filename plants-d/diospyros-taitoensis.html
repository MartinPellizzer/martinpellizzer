Diospyros taitoensis

Diospyros taitoensis, commonly known as the Taito persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to the Pacific Islands, particularly Hawaii.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other persimmon species. The plant is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. Its fruit is a small, orange to reddish berry that ripens in late summer.

The tree is adapted to coastal and dry habitats, showcasing its resilience to harsh environmental conditions.
