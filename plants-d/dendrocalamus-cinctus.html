Dendrocalamus cinctus

Dendrocalamus cinctus, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a tall, fast-growing grass native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its strong, hollow stems that are often used in construction and crafts. The plant is known for its striking yellowish-green color, which becomes more pronounced as it matures. Dendrocalamus cinctus thrives in humid climates and is often found in lowland forests and riverine areas.

Its rapid growth and robust structure make it an important resource in both ecological and economic contexts.
