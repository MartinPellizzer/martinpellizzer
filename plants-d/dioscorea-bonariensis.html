Dioscorea bonariensis

Dioscorea bonariensis, commonly known as the Argentine yam, is a perennial vine belonging to the Dioscoreaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Argentina and Uruguay, and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant has tuberous roots that are rich in starch and have been used traditionally for food and medicinal purposes. Its leaves are heart-shaped and its flowers are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
