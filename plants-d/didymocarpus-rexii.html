Didymocarpus rexii

Didymocarpus rexii, commonly known as the golden fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by edible, sweet-tart fruits resembling small figs. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a tendency to form dense canopies in its natural habitat.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its fruit, which is often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications.
