Daucus platycarpos f. microcarpus

Daucus platycarpos f. microcarpus, commonly known as the small-fruited wild carrot, is a botanical variant of the wild carrot species.

It is characterized by its smaller, more compact fruits compared to the typical wild carrot. The plant is native to regions of Europe and Asia, where it grows in disturbed soils and open habitats. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, with a distinct, aromatic scent.

The leaves are pinnately divided, and the plant is often found in meadows and along roadsides.
