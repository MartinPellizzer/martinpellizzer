Dendrobium suaveolens

Dendrobium suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet-scented orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with multiple stems that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and yellow, often with a waxy texture. Its leaves are usually strap-shaped and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems.

Dendrobium suaveolens thrives in warm, humid environments with bright indirect light and well-draining soil.
