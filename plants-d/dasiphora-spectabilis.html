Dasiphora spectabilis

Dasiphora spectabilis, commonly known as showy lady's mantle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its striking, shield-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, which are clustered in upright, branched inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
