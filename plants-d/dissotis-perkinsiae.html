Dissotis perkinsiae

Dissotis perkinsiae, commonly known as the silverleaf grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It features slender, erect stems that are often covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly hairy texture.

Dissotis perkinsiae is often used in ornamental gardening for its unique appearance and adaptability to various soil conditions.
