Dactylostalix ringens

Dactylostalix ringens, commonly known as the African velvet bean, is a climbing legume native to sub-Saharan Africa.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically hairy and have a velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by bean-like pods containing edible seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and abandoned fields.

Dactylostalix ringens is valued for its nutritional content and is used in both traditional and modern agriculture.
