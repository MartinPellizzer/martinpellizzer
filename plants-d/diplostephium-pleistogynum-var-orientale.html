Diplostephium pleistogynum var. orientale

Diplostephium pleistogynum var. orientale, commonly known as the oriental false dandelion, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Asia.

It features a basal rosette of broad, spoon-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in a single, terminal inflorescence, which resembles a dandelion. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its botanical interest lies in its unique floral structure and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
