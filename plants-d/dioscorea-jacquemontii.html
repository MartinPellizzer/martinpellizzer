Dioscorea jacquemontii

Dioscorea jacquemontii, commonly known as the Jacquemont's yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

The tubers of Dioscorea jacquemontii are valued for their nutritional properties and potential medicinal applications.
