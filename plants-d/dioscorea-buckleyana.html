Dioscorea buckleyana

Dioscorea buckleyana, commonly known as Buckley's yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its helical stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by cylindrical, fleshy fruits containing numerous seeds. It is valued for its tuberous roots, which are used in traditional medicine and as a food source in some cultures.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
