Delphinium duhmbergii

Delphinium duhmbergii, commonly known as the Duhmberg's larkspur, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant produces striking blue to violet flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its rugged appearance.

Delphinium duhmbergii is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
