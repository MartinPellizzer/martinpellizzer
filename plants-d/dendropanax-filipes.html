Dendropanax filipes

Dendropanax filipes, commonly known as the Chinese climbing fern, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a climbing plant with a distinctive appearance, featuring pinnately compound leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant has a fibrous root system and can grow up to several meters in length, using its tendrils to climb trees and other structures. Its leaves are usually alternate and have a serrated margin, while the small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in panicles.

The fruit is a berry that turns red when mature, containing several seeds.
