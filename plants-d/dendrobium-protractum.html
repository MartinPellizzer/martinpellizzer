Dendrobium protractum

Dendrobium protractum, commonly known as the long-lasting orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, yellowish-green flowers. The plant has a complex root system and prefers well-draining soil. It is often found in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors.

Dendrobium protractum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
