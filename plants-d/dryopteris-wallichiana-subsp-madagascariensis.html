Dryopteris wallichiana subsp. madagascariensis

Dryopteris wallichiana subsp.

madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar shield fern, is a distinctive fern species native to Madagascar. It belongs to the Dryopteridaceae family and is characterized by its large, shield-shaped fronds.

The fronds are typically bipinnate, with a broad, leathery texture and a deep green color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in humid tropical forests.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant.
