Dalbergia guineensis

Dalbergia guineensis, commonly known as African rosewood, is a tropical tree native to West Africa.

It is characterized by its dark, dense wood with a rich reddish-brown color and a distinctive grain pattern. The plant has compound leaves with trifoliate or pinnate arrangements, and its flowers are small and typically white or pale yellow. It produces small, round pods that contain several seeds.

The tree is valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in furniture and musical instruments.
