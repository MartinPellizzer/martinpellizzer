Draba cappadocica

Draba cappadocica, commonly known as Cappadocian whitlow grass, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Turkey and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other mustard family plants. This species is characterized by its low-growing habit, with basal leaves and small white or pale yellow flowers. It thrives in rocky and well-drained soils, often found in alpine and subalpine environments.

Draba cappadocica is known for its resilience to harsh climatic conditions and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
