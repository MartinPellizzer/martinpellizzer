Dactylorchis maculata var. heuffelii

Dactylorchis maculata var. heuffelii, commonly known as the Heuffel's spotted orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It features a rhizomatous growth habit with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers with distinctive dark spots on the petals and sepals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Botanically, it is characterized by its unique floral structure and specific mycorrhizal associations essential for its nutrient uptake.
