Distichis

Distichis, commonly known as the two-leaved plant, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its opposite leaf arrangement, with two leaves emerging from each node. The plants typically have small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in clusters. They are often found in temperate regions and are adapted to a variety of soil types.

Distichis species are valued for their ornamental appeal and are sometimes used in horticulture.
