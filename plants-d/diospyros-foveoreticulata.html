Diospyros foveoreticulata

Diospyros foveoreticulata, commonly known as the African persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and elliptical, glossy leaves that are often arranged alternately along the branches. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by edible, orange-red berries that are rich in nutrients. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or near water sources.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in horticultural settings.
