Diospyros curtisii

Diospyros curtisii, commonly known as the Curtis's ebony, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other well-known ebony species. The plant is characterized by its dark, dense wood, which is highly valued for its strength and fine grain. Its leaves are typically elliptical and glossy, and it produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange-red fruits.

Diospyros curtisii is often found in lowland rainforests and thrives in humid, shaded environments.
