Dodonaea procumbens

Dodonaea procumbens, commonly known as the silky oaks or silky honey bush, is a shrub native to Australia.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving them a distinctive texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in spikes, typically blooming in spring. These flowers are followed by small, woody pods that contain seeds.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
