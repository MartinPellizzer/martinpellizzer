Dictamnus albus var. gynodynamicus

Dictamnus albus var. gynodynamicus, commonly known as the white dictamnus, is a variant of the fragrant dictamnus plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

This plant is characterized by its aromatic, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in rocky or gravelly habitats.

The plant is known for its strong, spicy scent, which is released when the stems are broken.
