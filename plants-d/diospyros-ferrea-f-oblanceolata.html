Diospyros ferrea f. oblanceolata

Diospyros ferrea f. oblanceolata, commonly known as the Brazilian persimmon, is a variant of the persimmon tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, oval-shaped leaves that are often oblong and have a smooth, glossy surface. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that develop into orange to red fruits with a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor. Its wood is dense and hard, making it valuable for crafting and construction.

This species thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soils and full sunlight.
