Dactylorhiza incarnata subsp. punctata

Dactylorhiza incarnata subsp.

punctata, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows.

The plant features a tall, erect stem with lance-shaped leaves and produces striking pink or purple flowers with a distinctive spotted pattern. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit.

The subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens.
