Duguetia tessmannii

Duguetia tessmannii, commonly known as the golden trumpet tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has a dense, rounded canopy and can grow up to 20 meters in height, making it a prominent feature in the understory of rainforests. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a rough texture, while its wood is valued for its durability and use in construction.

The plant plays an important ecological role in supporting biodiversity within its native habitat.
