Diplotaxis ramosissima

Diplotaxis ramosissima, commonly known as the African dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is an annual herb that grows up to 1 meter tall, characterized by its branching stems and alternate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into fluffy, white seed heads, similar to those of dandelions. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Diplotaxis ramosissima is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
