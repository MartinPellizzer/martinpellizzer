Datura sanguinea f. coccinea

Datura sanguinea f. coccinea, commonly known as the crimson devil or bloodflower, is a striking perennial herb native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from deep red to purple. The plant produces smooth, hairless stems and broad, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy underside. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in dry, open habitats.

The flowers emit a strong, sweet fragrance, attracting nocturnal pollinators like moths.
