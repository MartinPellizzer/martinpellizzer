Dicoma anomala var. attenuata

Dicoma anomala var. attenuata, commonly known as the narrowleaf dicoma, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid regions, showing adaptations to drought conditions. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs to reduce water loss.

It produces small, yellowish flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
