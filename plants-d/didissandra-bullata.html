Didissandra bullata

Didissandra bullata, commonly known as the golden mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that primarily grows on the branches of eucalyptus trees in Australia.

It has a unique appearance with its yellowish-green, fleshy leaves and small, white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are a food source for birds and other wildlife. Its parasitic nature allows it to draw water and nutrients from its host, while still performing photosynthesis.

Didissandra bullata is often found in coastal and dry forest regions, where it thrives in well-lit, open environments.
