Delphinium spirocentrum var. hirsutum

Delphinium spirocentrum var. hirsutum, commonly known as the hairy delphinium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters, adorned with clusters of blue to violet spurred flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine, soft hairs, giving the plant its "hirsutum" designation. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
