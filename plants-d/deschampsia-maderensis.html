Deschampsia maderensis

Deschampsia maderensis, commonly known as Mader's feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, silver-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces delicate, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late summer, adding a subtle ornamental value to its landscape. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native habitat.
