Dimerostemma virgosum

Dimerostemma virgosum, commonly known as the slender mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It attaches to its host trees, primarily acacias, through specialized structures called haustoria, which extract water and nutrients. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that are often clustered in racemes. Its berries are typically red or yellow, containing seeds that are dispersed by animals.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can have significant ecological impacts on its host species.
