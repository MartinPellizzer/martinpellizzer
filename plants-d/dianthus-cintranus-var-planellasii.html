Dianthus cintranus var. planellasii

Dianthus cintranus var. planellasii, commonly known as the Rock Campion, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and produces clusters of pink to purple flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and linear, adapted to thrive in rocky or well-drained soils. It blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in sunny, dry conditions.
