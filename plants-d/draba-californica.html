Draba californica

Draba californica, commonly known as the California whitlow grass, is a small flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many mustard plants, and is characterized by its delicate, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as coastal bluffs and mountain slopes. It has basal leaves that are linear to lanceolate, and its flowers are usually white or pale yellow.

Draba californica is an annual or biennial plant, often blooming in early spring.
