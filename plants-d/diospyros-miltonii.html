Diospyros miltonii

Diospyros miltonii, commonly known as the African persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes ebony and persimmon trees. The plant is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and compound leaves that are alternate and pinnate. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are followed by edible, orange-red berries that are a food source for various animals.

The tree is also valued for its hard, dense wood used in traditional crafts and as a source of tannins.
