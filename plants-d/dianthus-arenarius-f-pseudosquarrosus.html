Dianthus arenarius f. pseudosquarrosus

Dianthus arenarius f. pseudosquarrosus, commonly known as the sand pink, is a flowering plant characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and vibrant pink to red blooms.

It features narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant nature. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in rocky or coastal environments.
