Dioscorea deltoidea

Dioscorea deltoidea, commonly known as the delta yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary edible part of the plant. The plant produces small, green, heart-shaped leaves and yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. Its tubers are often used in traditional cuisines and have a slightly sweet, nutty flavor.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
