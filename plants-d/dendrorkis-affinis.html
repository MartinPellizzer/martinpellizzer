Dendrorkis affinis

Dendrorkis affinis, commonly known as the tree fern, is a member of the Cyatheaceae family and is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and large, feather-like fronds.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia and is often found in humid, shaded environments. The plant has a rhizome that serves as a storage organ and supports the growth of the trunk and fronds. Its reproductive structures include spore-bearing cones located on the underside of the fronds.

Dendrorkis affinis is valued for its ornamental appearance and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
