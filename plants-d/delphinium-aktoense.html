Delphinium aktoense

Delphinium aktoense, commonly known as the Aktoen Delphinium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers in raceme clusters, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are compound, with serrated edges, and it thrives in well-drained, alkaline soils.

Delphinium aktoense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
