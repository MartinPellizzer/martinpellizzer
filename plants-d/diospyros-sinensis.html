Diospyros sinensis

Diospyros sinensis, commonly known as the Chinese persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to China and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into orange to reddish fruits with a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor. Its fruits are rich in nutrients and have been used in traditional Chinese medicine for their health benefits.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is cultivated for both fruit production and landscape use.
