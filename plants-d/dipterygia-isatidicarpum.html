Dipterygia isatidicarpum

Dipterygia isatidicarpum, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy blue-violet flower heads that resemble small clouds. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and produces small, oval-shaped fruits called caryopses. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
