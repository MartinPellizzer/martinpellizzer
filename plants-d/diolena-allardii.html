Diolena allardii

Diolena allardii, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that range in color from white to yellow. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and has a rosette of basal leaves. Its flowers are adapted for insect pollination, and it produces small, dry fruits called achenes.

Diolena allardii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
