Diospyros merrillii

Diospyros merrillii, commonly known as the Philippine persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other persimmon species. The plant is characterized by its elliptical leaves that are dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. Its small, yellowish flowers are followed by edible, orange-red berries that mature in autumn.

Diospyros merrillii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine in its native region.
