Dendrochilum copelandii

Dendrochilum copelandii, commonly known as the Emerald Orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking emerald-green flowers with intricate patterns of purple and yellow. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are long, strap-like, and often have a waxy texture.

Dendrochilum copelandii is prized for its ornamental value and is cultivated by orchid enthusiasts worldwide.
