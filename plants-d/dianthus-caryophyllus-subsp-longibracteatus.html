Dianthus caryophyllus subsp. longibracteatus

Dianthus caryophyllus subsp.

longibracteatus, commonly known as the long-bract carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its showy, double-petaled flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, pink, and white.

The subspecies is distinguished by its elongated bracts, which are leaf-like structures at the base of the flower cluster. It typically grows in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

This plant is also valued for its aromatic foliage and is often used in perfumery and culinary applications.
