Dianthus auraniticus

Dianthus auraniticus, commonly known as the golden pinks, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that range from yellow to orange, often with a distinctive red or purple center. The plant produces small, rounded leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Dianthus auraniticus is also known for its pleasant, spicy fragrance, which attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies.
