Dudleya pulverulenta subsp. arizonica

Dudleya pulverulenta subsp.

arizonica, commonly known as Arizona dustymontain, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States. It is characterized by its compact, rosette-forming structure and fleshy, gray-green leaves covered in a fine layer of white powder.

The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its drought tolerance and unique appearance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
