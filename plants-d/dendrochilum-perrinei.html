Dendrochilum perrinei

Dendrochilum perrinei, commonly known as the Perrine's tree orchid, is a unique epiphytic orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and the presence of pseudobulbs, which store water and nutrients. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or green with red or purple markings. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining substrate for optimal growth.

Dendrochilum perrinei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
