Dichoglottis spathulifolia

Dichoglottis spathulifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Its rhizomes are used in traditional medicine and as a source of starch.
