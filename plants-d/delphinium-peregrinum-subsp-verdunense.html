Delphinium peregrinum subsp. verdunense

Delphinium peregrinum subsp.

verdunense, commonly known as the Verdun larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters.

The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers in racemes, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are palmately lobed and have a rough, hairy texture.

This subspecies is adapted to rocky and alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun.
