Daphne endlicheriana

Daphne endlicheriana, commonly known as the Himalayan daphne, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, fragrant flowers that range from white to pink, often appearing in late winter or early spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and covered in a fine, silvery down. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Daphne endlicheriana is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its striking floral display.
