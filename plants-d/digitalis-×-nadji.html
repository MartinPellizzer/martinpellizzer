Digitalis × nadji

Digitalis × nadji, commonly known as the hybrid foxglove, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Plantaginaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, spires of tubular flowers that range in color from purple to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant produces lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and have a rough texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in medicine, it is widely cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
