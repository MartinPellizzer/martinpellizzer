Dalea uniflora

Dalea uniflora, commonly known as the single-flower milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, often appearing in clusters along the stems. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several pairs of oval-shaped leaflets.

Dalea uniflora thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats such as grasslands and sagebrush steppe.
