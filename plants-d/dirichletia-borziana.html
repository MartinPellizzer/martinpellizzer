Dirichletia borziana

Dirichletia borziana, commonly known as the Borzian orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, often fragrant flowers that come in a variety of colors. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its roots are adapted for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Dirichletia borziana is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
