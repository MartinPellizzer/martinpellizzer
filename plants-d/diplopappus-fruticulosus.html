Diplopappus fruticulosus

Diplopappus fruticulosus, commonly known as the hairy pappus plant, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a wiry, sprawling stem covered in dense, glandular hairs. Its small, yellowish flowers are typically arranged in clusters, and the plant produces small, hairy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
