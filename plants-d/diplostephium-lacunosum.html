Diplostephium lacunosum

Diplostephium lacunosum, commonly known as the laceleaf cactus, is a unique succulent native to the deserts of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its intricate, lace-like patterns on its stems, which are covered in small, tuberculate structures. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are typically borne on short, hairy pedicels. Its stems are ribbed and can grow up to 30 centimeters in length, allowing it to store water efficiently in arid environments.

Due to its striking appearance and adaptations to harsh climates, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
