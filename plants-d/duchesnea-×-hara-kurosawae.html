Duchesnea × hara-kurosawae

Duchesnea × hara-kurosawae, commonly known as the Japanese false strawberry, is a hybrid species within the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, edible berries that are often used in traditional Japanese cuisine. The plant features trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and produces yellowish-white flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and culinary uses, it is cultivated in some regions for both aesthetic and practical purposes.
