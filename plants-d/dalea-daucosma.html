Dalea daucosma

Dalea daucosma, commonly known as the slender milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small leaflets, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.

Dalea daucosma plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing nectar for pollinators in its native range.
