Diospyros occulta

Diospyros occulta, commonly known as the hidden persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes ebony and persimmon trees. The plant is characterized by its dense, glossy foliage and small, elongated leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. Its fruit, though not widely cultivated, is edible and has a sweet, astringent flavor.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and has cultural significance in certain regions.
