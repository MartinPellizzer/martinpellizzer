Dalmatocytisus

Dalmatocytisus, commonly known as the Dalmatian broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its dense, wiry growth habit. The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by pods containing several seeds. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets.

Dalmatocytisus is often used in landscaping for its hardiness and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
