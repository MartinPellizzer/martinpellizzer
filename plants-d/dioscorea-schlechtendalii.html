Dioscorea schlechtendalii

Dioscorea schlechtendalii, commonly known as the Chinese yam, is a climbing vine native to Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary edible part. The plant produces small, green, heart-shaped leaves and yellowish flowers that are typically unisexual. Its tubers are valued for their nutritional content and traditional medicinal uses.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its starchy, edible roots.
