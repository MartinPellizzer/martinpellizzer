Digitaria urochloides

Digitaria urochloides, commonly known as hairy fingergrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a weed in agricultural settings.

Digitaria urochloides is adapted to a wide range of environmental conditions, making it a resilient and widespread species.
