Dendrocalamus griffithianus

Dendrocalamus griffithianus, commonly known as the giant bamboo, is a tall, fast-growing grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Bambusoideae subfamily and is characterized by its sturdy, hollow stems that can reach heights of up to 30 meters. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are wind-pollinated. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine, and its culms are valued for construction and handicrafts.

Dendrocalamus griffithianus plays an important ecological role in its native habitats by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
