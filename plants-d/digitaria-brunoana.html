Digitaria brunoana

Digitaria brunoana, commonly known as Bruno's digitaria, is a species of grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. The plant has slender, erect stems that can reach heights of up to 1 meter, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are characteristic of the grass family Poaceae.

Digitaria brunoana plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms in its native environment.
