Dryopteris juxtaposita subsp. nigropaleacea

Dryopteris juxtaposita subsp.

nigropaleacea, commonly known as the black-veined wood fern, is a fern species characterized by its distinctive fronds with dark, prominent veins. It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is native to regions of East Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky slopes. Its fronds are usually bipinnate, with a leathery texture and a dark green coloration.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
