Doellingeria obovata

Doellingeria obovata, commonly known as the broadleaf arrowhead, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped to arrowhead-shaped leaves that are typically green with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, white to pale purple flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Doellingeria obovata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting various pollinators.
