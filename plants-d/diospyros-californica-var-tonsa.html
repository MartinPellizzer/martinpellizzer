Diospyros californica var. tonsa

Diospyros californica var. tonsa, commonly known as the California persimmon, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical leaves that turn red in the fall. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into orange to reddish fruit, which is edible when fully ripe. The fruit is rich in sugars and has a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor.

It is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats, thriving in well-drained soils.
