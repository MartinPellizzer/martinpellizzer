Dioscorea pirita

Dioscorea pirita, commonly known as the yellow wild yam, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and tuberous roots. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a rough texture, and the stems are often hairy.

The tubers of Dioscorea pirita have been used in traditional medicine for their potential health benefits.
