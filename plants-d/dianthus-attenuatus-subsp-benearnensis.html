Dianthus attenuatus subsp. benearnensis

Dianthus attenuatus subsp.

benearnensis, commonly known as the Benearn's campion, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its slender stems and clusters of pink to red flowers.

The leaves are typically linear and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
