Desmonema mossambicense

Desmonema mossambicense, commonly known as the African mossy coral.

It is a species of coral tree native to southern Africa, characterized by its distinctive branching pattern and coral-like appearance. The plant has a thick, woody stem with a rough, fissured bark that often appears moss-covered. Its leaves are small, ovate, and arranged in opposite pairs along the branches.

Desmonema mossambicense produces small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red or orange fruits containing seeds.
