Disphenia aculeata

Disphenia aculeata, commonly known as the prickly lettuce, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and small yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant is native to temperate regions of Europe, Asia, and North Africa. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides and in cultivated fields.

Its hairy stems and serrated leaves help it retain moisture and deter herbivores.
