Decorsella arborea

Decorsella arborea, commonly known as the tree hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, woody stems and broad, palmately lobed leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, which bloom throughout the growing season. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Decorsella arborea is also valued for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its relatively low maintenance requirements.
