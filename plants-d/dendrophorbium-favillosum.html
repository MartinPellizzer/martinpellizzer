Dendrophorbium favillosum

Dendrophorbium favillosum, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, yellow to orange composite flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Dendrophorbium favillosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
