Drummondita longifolia

Drummondita longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf drummondia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Drummondita longifolia is typically found in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptation to drought conditions.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
