Dombeya breonii

Dombeya breonii, commonly known as the African snowball, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, white or pale pink flower clusters that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has simple, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and its stems are often covered in fine, white pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and birds, contributing to its ecological value.
