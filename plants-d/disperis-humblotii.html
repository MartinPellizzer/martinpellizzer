Disperis humblotii

Disperis humblotii, commonly known as the Humblot's orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to the Democratic Republic of the Congo.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow and green. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in its tropical rainforest habitat. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Disperis humblotii is highly sought after by botanists and conservationists due to its limited distribution and vulnerability to habitat destruction.
