Dielsiochloa floribunda var. weberbaueri

Dielsiochloa floribunda var. weberbaueri, commonly known as the Weberbauer's flowering grass, is a perennial grass species native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grassland or wetland habitats. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it is adapted to withstand periodic flooding.

This variety is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique floral structure and ecological adaptations.
