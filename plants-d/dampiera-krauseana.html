Dampiera krauseana

Dampiera krauseana, commonly known as the pink daisy or narrow-leaved daisy, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its small, daisy-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a yellow center. The plant typically grows in coastal heathlands and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is often found in coastal regions of New South Wales and southern Queensland.

Dampiera krauseana is a hardy species that is well adapted to the harsh coastal environments where it grows.
