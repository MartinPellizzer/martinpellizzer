Docynia indica var. griffithiana

Docynia indica var. griffithiana, commonly known as the Indian gooseberry or amla, is a deciduous shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a slightly serrated margin. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring, followed by the development of its distinctive, round, orange-red berries. These berries are rich in vitamin C and have a tart, acidic flavor.

The plant is often cultivated for its medicinal properties and is also used in traditional Ayurvedic practices.
