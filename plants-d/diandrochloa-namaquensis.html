Diandrochloa namaquensis

Diandrochloa namaquensis, commonly known as the Namaqua needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, needle-like leaves and compact growth habit, often forming dense mats on rocky or sandy soils. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought and poor soil conditions. Its flowering stems bear small, spike-like inflorescences, and it produces minute seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Diandrochloa namaquensis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small desert animals.
