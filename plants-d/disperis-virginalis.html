Disperis virginalis

Disperis virginalis, commonly known as the white disperis, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa, particularly Kenya and Tanzania.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. It has a slender, erect stem with few leaves, and its flowers are clustered in a raceme.

Disperis virginalis is often found in montane habitats and is adapted to the region's climate with its specialized root system.
