Didymosperma hastatum

Didymosperma hastatum, commonly known as the hairy leaf senna, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces, and its yellow flowers are arranged in racemate inflorescences. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in semi-arid conditions.

The seeds are enclosed in elongated, slender pods, which contribute to its ecological role in soil stabilization.
