Diospyros aurea

Diospyros aurea, commonly known as the golden persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and alternate, simple leaves that are elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange to red, fleshy fruits that are edible when fully ripe.

Diospyros aurea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions for its aesthetic and culinary uses.
