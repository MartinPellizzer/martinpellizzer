Dactylorhiza elata var. occitanica

Dactylorhiza elata var. occitanica, commonly known as the Occitanica marsh orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southern Europe.

It typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, preferring calcareous soils. The plant features a tall, erect stem with several narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to greenish-yellow, arranged in a loose spike.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its specialized root system and mycorrhizal associations.
