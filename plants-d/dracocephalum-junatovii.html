Dracocephalum junatovii

Dracocephalum junatovii, commonly known as the Kashmir dittany, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in dense spikes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a strong, minty fragrance.

Dracocephalum junatovii is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.
