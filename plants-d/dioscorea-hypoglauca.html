Dioscorea hypoglauca

Dioscorea hypoglauca, commonly known as the blue-veined yam, is a tropical climbing vine native to regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its hairy, cylindrical stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by tuberous roots, which are the primary part used for culinary and medicinal purposes. Its tubers are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits, though they contain toxic compounds that require careful preparation.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
