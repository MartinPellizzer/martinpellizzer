Dracontomelon puberulum

Dracontomelon puberulum, commonly known as the silky tamarind, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and glossy, dark green leaves that are alternate and oblong in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by woody pods containing edible seeds. Its fruit is a distinctive, elongated pod that splits open to reveal the seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some economic importance in local communities.
