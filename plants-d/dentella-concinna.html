Dentella concinna

Dentella concinna, commonly known as the smoothleaf dentella, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Dentella concinna typically grows in moist, sandy soils along streambanks and is often found in disturbed habitats.
