Dendranthema tenuiflorum

Dendranthema tenuiflorum, commonly known as Shasta daisy, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its white petals surrounding a yellow center, forming a daisy-like flower. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and produces flowers throughout the summer months. It prefers full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes many other familiar flowering plants.
