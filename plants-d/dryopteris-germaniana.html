Dryopteris germaniana

Dryopteris germaniana, commonly known as the German shield fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to Europe, particularly found in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and rocky areas.

It features a robust rhizome system that supports its growth and helps it thrive in temperate climates. The plant produces large, leathery, bipinnate fronds that are typically dark green and have a distinctive, shield-like appearance. Its spores are released from sori located on the underside of the fronds, a characteristic feature of ferns.

Dryopteris germaniana is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and hardiness.
