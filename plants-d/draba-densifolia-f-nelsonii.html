Draba densifolia f. nelsonii

Draba densifolia f. nelsonii, commonly known as the Nelson's denseleaf whitlow grass, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features basal rosettes of dark green, densely packed leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in early spring, typically ranging from white to pale yellow. It thrives in rocky, arid environments and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Brassicaceae family.
