Diclis umbonata

Diclis umbonata, commonly known as the African snowball or umbrella tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, characterized by its dense, rounded crown and clusters of small, white flowers. The leaves are simple, opposite, and have a slightly hairy texture, while the fruits are small, round, and turn red when mature. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade, making it a popular ornamental plant in warm climates.

The plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and birds.
