Dimocarpus cinereus

Dimocarpus cinereus, commonly known as the ash-leaf tamarind, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often ash-gray in color, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by woody, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to saline soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local cultures.
