Dendrobium distichophyllum

Dendrobium distichophyllum, commonly known as the two-leaf dendrobium, is an epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with multiple stems that can reach up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically in shades of yellow, orange, and red. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining potting media.

Dendrobium distichophyllum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its long-lasting blooms.
