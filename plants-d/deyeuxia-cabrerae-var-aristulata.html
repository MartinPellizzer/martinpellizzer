Deyeuxia cabrerae var. aristulata

Deyeuxia cabrerae var. aristulata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bulrush, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in wetland habitats, including marshes and the edges of water bodies.

Its botanical features make it an important component of aquatic and riparian ecosystems.
