Distylium myricoides

Distylium myricoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf candlewood, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Betulaceae family, which includes birches and alders, and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are pollinated by wind, and its fruit is a small, winged nut.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
