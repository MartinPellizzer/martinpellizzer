Dendrobium ciliferum

Dendrobium ciliferum, commonly known as the golden torch orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features long, cane-like pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, typical of many orchid species. The plant produces clusters of showy, yellow-orange flowers with red markings, which bloom during the dry season. Its leaves are linear and arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem.

Dendrobium ciliferum thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.
