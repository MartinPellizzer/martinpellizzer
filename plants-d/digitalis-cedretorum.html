Digitalis cedretorum

Digitalis cedretorum, commonly known as the Armenian foxglove, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the family Plantaginaceae and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and tubular flowers. The plant produces violet to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough, hairy texture.

Digitalis cedretorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential cardiovascular benefits.
