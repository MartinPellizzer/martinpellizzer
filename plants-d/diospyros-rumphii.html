Diospyros rumphii

Diospyros rumphii, commonly known as the wild persimmon, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and large, palmately lobed leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into orange to red, fleshy fruits. Its fruits are edible, though some varieties have astringent seeds that require soaking to remove.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine and cuisine.
