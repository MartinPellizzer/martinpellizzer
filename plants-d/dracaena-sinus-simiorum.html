Dracaena sinus-simiorum

Dracaena sinus-simiorum, commonly known as the Madagascar dragon tree, is a flowering plant native to the island of Madagascar.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and long, strap-like leaves that are typically dark green with a reddish tint. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red, fleshy fruits containing seeds. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as a decorative indoor plant.

Its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance make it a popular choice for both ornamental and horticultural purposes.
