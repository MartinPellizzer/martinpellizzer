Danthonia sericea var. epilis

Danthonia sericea var. epilis, commonly known as the silky danthonia, is a low-growing perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It features fine, silver-white hairs on its leaves, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
