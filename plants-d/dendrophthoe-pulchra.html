Dendrophthoe pulchra

Dendrophthoe pulchra, commonly known as the beautiful mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches itself to host trees, primarily in the genus Eucalyptus.

The plant produces small, white or pink flowers and red, fleshy berries that contain seeds dispersed by birds. Its growth is slow, and it often forms dense clusters on the branches of its host.

Dendrophthoe pulchra is found in various parts of Australia and is considered both ecologically significant and sometimes a nuisance to forest ecosystems.
