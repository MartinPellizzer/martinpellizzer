Dioscorea preussii subsp. preussii

Dioscorea preussii subsp.

preussii, commonly known as the Preuss yam, is a species of flowering plant in the family Dioscoreaceae. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, particularly in countries like Sudan, Ethiopia, and Kenya.

The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with tubular leaves and small, greenish-yellow flowers. Its tubers are edible and have been traditionally used as a food source by local communities.

The plant's botanical significance lies in its role in traditional agriculture and its potential for cultivation as a nutritious crop.
