Diasperus curtisii

Diasperus curtisii, commonly known as the Curtis's cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought and extreme temperatures. Its structure includes cylindrical stems with areoles, from which both spines and flowers emerge.

Diasperus curtisii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
