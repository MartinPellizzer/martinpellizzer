Dioscorea quaternata var. glauca

Dioscorea quaternata var. glauca, commonly known as the blue-leaf yam, is a variant of the yam species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive blue-green foliage, which sets it apart from other varieties of Dioscorea. The plant produces tuberous roots that are often used in traditional medicine and as a food source. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for horticultural study and potential agricultural use.
