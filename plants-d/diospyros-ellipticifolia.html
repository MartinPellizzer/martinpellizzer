Diospyros ellipticifolia

Diospyros ellipticifolia, commonly known as the elliptic-leaf persimmon, is a species of flowering plant in the Ebenaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand, Vietnam, and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its elliptical leaves and small, edible fruits that are typically harvested when fully ripe. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and its fruit, which is used in both culinary and traditional medicinal applications.
