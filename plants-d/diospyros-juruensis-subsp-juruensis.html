Diospyros juruensis subsp. juruensis

Diospyros juruensis subsp.

juruensis, commonly known as the Juruense persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical rainforests of South America. It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes ebony and persimmon trees.

The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. Its flowers are small and clustered, with a pale greenish-white color, and it produces edible, fleshy berries that are typically orange to red in color.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local communities.
