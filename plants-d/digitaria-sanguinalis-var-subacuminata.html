Digitaria sanguinalis var. subacuminata

Digitaria sanguinalis var. subacuminata, commonly known as red panicgrass, is a warm-season grass native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, reddish stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in full sun and is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it an important species in ecological studies and agricultural contexts.
