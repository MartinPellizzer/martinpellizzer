Dondia suffrutescens

Dondia suffrutescens, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a small shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, clustered leaves that are typically narrow and pointed. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Dondia suffrutescens is valued for its hard, durable wood, which has been traditionally used in construction and craftsmanship.
