Dysoxylum amooroides var. pubescens

Dysoxylum amooroides var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy amoor wood, is a tropical tree species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its rough, pubescent bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental and potential medicinal value, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
