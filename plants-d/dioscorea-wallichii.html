Dioscorea wallichii

Dioscorea wallichii, commonly known as the Indian yam, is a climbing vine native to the Himalayas and parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces tuberous roots that are used in traditional medicine and as a food source. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant.

Dioscorea wallichii is valued for its nutritional content and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
