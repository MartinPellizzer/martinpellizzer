Diospyros haplostylis var. orientalis

Diospyros haplostylis var. orientalis, commonly known as the oriental persimmon, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, grey bark and elongated, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that give way to orange to red, fleshy fruits with a sweet, sometimes astringent taste. Its fruit is often consumed fresh or used in traditional medicine for its nutritional and therapeutic properties.

The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.
