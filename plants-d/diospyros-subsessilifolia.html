Diospyros subsessilifolia

Diospyros subsessilifolia, commonly known as the sessile-leaf persimmon, is a species of flowering plant in the family Ebenaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of India, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually sessile or nearly sessile, giving it its common name. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are followed by edible, orange to reddish fruits that are similar in appearance to persimmons.

The tree is often valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine and cuisine.
