Didymochlaena truncatula var. bipinnatipartita

Didymochlaena truncatula var. bipinnatipartita, commonly known as the bipinnate hakea, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves that are twice-pinnate, giving it a feathery appearance. The plant produces small, spherical flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it requires full sun and good drainage.
