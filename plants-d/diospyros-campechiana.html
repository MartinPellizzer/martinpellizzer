Diospyros campechiana

Diospyros campechiana, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to Central America and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, ovate leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into orange to reddish fruits, which are edible when fully ripe.

The fruit has a sweet, honey-like flavor and is rich in nutrients, making it a valuable plant in both traditional and modern contexts.
