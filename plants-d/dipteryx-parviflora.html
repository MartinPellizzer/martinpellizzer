Dipteryx parviflora

Dipteryx parviflora, commonly known as the yellow spondias, is a small to medium-sized tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish-brown bark and simple, alternate leaves that are elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by distinctive, round, yellow fruits. It is often found in moist, well-drained soils within tropical and subtropical forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential ecological and medicinal uses.
