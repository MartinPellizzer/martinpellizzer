Delphinium narbonense

Delphinium narbonense, commonly known as the Narbonne larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers in raceme-like clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, pinnately divided, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Delphinium narbonense is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
