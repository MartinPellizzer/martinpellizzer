Dypsis ambanjae

Dypsis ambanjae, commonly known as the Ambanja palm, is a rare and striking member of the Arecaceae family native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by clustered fruits that contain edible seeds. Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered endangered and is often found in humid, coastal forests.

Its unique botanical features make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists alike.
