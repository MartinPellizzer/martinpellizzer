Dresslerella cloesii

Dresslerella cloesii, commonly known as the Cape Province orchid, is a species of orchid native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in late winter or early spring. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, adapting to its arid environment.

Dresslerella cloesii is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens with well-draining soil and adequate sunlight.
