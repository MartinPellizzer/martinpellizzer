Dodecatheon meadia var. lancifolium

Dodecatheon meadia var. lancifolium, commonly known as the lancifolium woodland flower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a whorl at the base of the stem. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, often found in woodland habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
