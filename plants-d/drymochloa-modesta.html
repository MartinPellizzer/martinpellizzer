Drymochloa modesta

Drymochloa modesta, commonly known as the dwarf bamboo, is a grass species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, clumping growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, making it suitable for ornamental use in gardens. Its rhizomes form dense mats, which help prevent soil erosion.

Drymochloa modesta is adapted to well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.
