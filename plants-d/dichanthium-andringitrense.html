Dichanthium andringitrense

Dichanthium andringitrense, commonly known as the Andringitra grass, is a perennial grass species native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in montane grasslands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slight pubescence on the underside.

Dichanthium andringitrense plays a role in local ecosystems and is of interest for its potential in sustainable land management.
