Diospyros serrana

Diospyros serrana, commonly known as the serrated persimmon, is a species of flowering plant in the family Ebenaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including parts of India, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often serrated along the edges. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by edible, orange-red berries.
