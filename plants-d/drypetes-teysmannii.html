Drypetes teysmannii

Drypetes teysmannii, commonly known as the Java flame tree, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red and orange flowers that bloom in clusters, making it a visually striking species. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth upper surface. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland forests.

The tree is also valued for its hard, durable wood, which has been used in traditional crafts and construction.
