Digitaria delicata subsp. recedens

Digitaria delicata subsp.

recedens, commonly known as the slender digitgrass, is a grass species native to parts of Africa and the Middle East. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it adaptable to various environmental conditions, though it is less commonly cultivated than other grass species.
