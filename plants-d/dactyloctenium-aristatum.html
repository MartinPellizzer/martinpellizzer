Dactyloctenium aristatum

Dactyloctenium aristatum, commonly known as the hairy fingergrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pale green leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in both wet and dry conditions.

Dactyloctenium aristatum is known for its ability to form dense mats, making it a common weed in agricultural fields.
