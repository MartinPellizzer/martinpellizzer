Desdemona

Desdemona, commonly known as the common dandelion, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of toothed, lance-shaped leaves and a distinctive hollow, leafy stem. The plant produces a cluster of small, yellow flowers that develop into fluffy white seed heads, which are dispersed by the wind. Desdemona is often found in lawns, meadows, and disturbed soils, and it is considered a weed in many regions.

It has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
