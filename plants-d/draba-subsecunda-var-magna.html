Draba subsecunda var. magna

Draba subsecunda var. magna, commonly known as the giant whitlow grass, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. Its leaves are basal, forming a rosette, and the flowering stems bear clusters of white or pale yellow flowers.

Draba subsecunda var. magna is notable for its robust growth and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
