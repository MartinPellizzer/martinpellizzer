Daphniphyllum glaucescens subsp. oldhamii

Daphniphyllum glaucescens subsp.

oldhamii, commonly known as the Oldham's daphniphyllum, is a subtropical evergreen shrub native to southeastern China. It features glossy, dark green, oval-shaped leaves that are often slightly hairy on the underside.

The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by bright red berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in shady, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and unique floral display.
