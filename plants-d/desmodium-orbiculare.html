Desmodium orbiculare

Desmodium orbiculare, commonly known as the globular burr vetch, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive globular seed pods that enclose the seeds, which are often used as a natural burr for attaching to animal fur. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, yellowish flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Due to its ability to fix nitrogen, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
