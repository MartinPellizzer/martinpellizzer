Dondia californica

Dondia californica, commonly known as the California donkey grass, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and forage for various wildlife species.
