Dalbergia lastoursvillensis

Dalbergia lastoursvillensis, commonly known as the Madagascar rosewood, is a leguminous tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its dense, dark-colored wood, which is highly valued for its beauty and durability. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by pod-like fruits. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental and timber value, it is also cultivated in horticultural settings.
