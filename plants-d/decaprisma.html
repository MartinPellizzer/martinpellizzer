Decaprisma

Decaprisma, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to yellow and sometimes orange. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. Decaprisma species are often found in woodland areas and along stream banks.

Its foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged in a basal rosette.
