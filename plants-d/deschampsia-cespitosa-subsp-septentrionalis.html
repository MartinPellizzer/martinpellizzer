Deschampsia cespitosa subsp. septentrionalis

Deschampsia cespitosa subsp.

septentrionalis, commonly known as northern feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the northern regions of North America. It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as bogs, marshes, and wet meadows.

The plant forms dense, tufted clumps with fine, needle-like leaves that are often slightly hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a single, slender spikelet that is slightly drooping and produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
