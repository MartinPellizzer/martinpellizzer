Dendrocalamus tomentosus

Dendrocalamus tomentosus, commonly known as the silver bamboo, is a tall, fast-growing grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that are covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it its common name. This plant is a member of the grass family Poaceae and is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and use in landscaping. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green hue.

Dendrocalamus tomentosus is also valued for its lightweight and durable culms, which are used in construction and crafts.
