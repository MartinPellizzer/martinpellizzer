Dioscorea nipponica subsp. nipponica

Dioscorea nipponica subsp.

nipponica, commonly known as Japanese yam, is a herbaceous perennial vine native to East Asia. It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and tuberous roots.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by bright red berries. Its tubers are used in traditional medicine and as a food source in some regions.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
