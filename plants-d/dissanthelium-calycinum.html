Dissanthelium calycinum

Dissanthelium calycinum, commonly known as the blue grass or blue oat grass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and its distinctive blue-green foliage. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are often tinged with a bluish hue. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in prairie and open woodland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
