Delphinium osseticum

Delphinium osseticum, commonly known as the Ossetian larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers in late summer, arranged in dense spikes at the top of the stem. Its leaves are palmately compound, with deeply lobed leaflets, and it thrives in well-drained, moist soils.

Delphinium osseticum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
