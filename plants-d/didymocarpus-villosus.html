Didymocarpus villosus

Didymocarpus villosus, commonly known as the hairy tamarind, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Caesalpiniaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces yellowish flowers followed by woody pods containing edible seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
