Diospyros scabiosa

Diospyros scabiosa, commonly known as the rough persimmon, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other persimmon species. The plant is characterized by its thick, rough, bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface. Its small, yellowish-white flowers appear in clusters and are followed by edible, orange-red fruits.

The fruit has a sweet, astringent flavor and is often consumed raw or cooked.
