Declieuxia cordigera var. genuina

Declieuxia cordigera var. genuina, commonly known as the narrow-leafed sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often in wetlands or along waterways. It has a fibrous root system and is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
