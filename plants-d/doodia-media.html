Doodia media

Doodia media, commonly known as the common wood sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are grass-like, arranged in three rows, and it produces small, clustered flowers in early spring.

Doodia media is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
