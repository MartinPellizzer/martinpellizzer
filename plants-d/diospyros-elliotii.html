Diospyros elliotii

Diospyros elliotii, commonly known as Elliot's persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by orange-red, fleshy fruits. The fruit is edible when fully ripe and has a sweet, honey-like flavor.

It is often found in lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
