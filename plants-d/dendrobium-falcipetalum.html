Dendrobium falcipetalum

Dendrobium falcipetalum, commonly known as the sickle-lipped dendrobium, is an epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, cane-like stems and clusters of fragrant, showy flowers. The plant produces long, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. Its flowers typically bloom in summer and can range in color from white to yellow or orange.

Dendrobium falcipetalum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
