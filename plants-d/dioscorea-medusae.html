Dioscorea medusae

Dioscorea medusae, commonly known as the ghost arrowhead, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its unique, heart-shaped leaves and striking, showy flowers. The plant produces tubers that are often used in traditional medicine. Its name derives from the shape of its flowers, which resemble a ghostly face.

Dioscorea medusae thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
