Dipladenia peduncularis

Dipladenia peduncularis, commonly known as the yellow bell vine, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trailing or climbing growth habit and produces showy, tubular yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in warm, sunny climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Dipladenia peduncularis is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
