Drypetes bengalensis

Drypetes bengalensis, commonly known as the Indian flame tree, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a vibrant display to its canopy. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are elliptic to ovate in shape. It produces small, round fruits that contain several seeds.

The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
