Dianthus kubanensis

Dianthus kubanensis, commonly known as the Kuban campion, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of pink or red flowers with white centers. The plant has hairy, fleshy leaves and is adapted to rocky, well-drained soils. It blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Dianthus kubanensis is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and hardiness.
