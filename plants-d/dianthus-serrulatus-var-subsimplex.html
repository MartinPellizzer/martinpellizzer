Dianthus serrulatus var. subsimplex

Dianthus serrulatus var. subsimplex, commonly known as the narrow-leaved campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features delicate, pinkish-white flowers with five petals and a distinctive, slightly serrated leaf margin. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its charming appearance.
