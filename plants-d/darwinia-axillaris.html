Darwinia axillaris

Darwinia axillaris, commonly known as the narrow-leaved Darwinia, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and dense, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in a fine layer of white wax.

Darwinia axillaris is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
