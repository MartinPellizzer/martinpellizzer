Dracaena parrei

Dracaena parrei, commonly known as the Madagascar dragon tree, is a striking evergreen shrub native to Madagascar.

It features tall, slender stems with a distinctive red-brown bark that becomes more pronounced with age. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are followed by bright red berries, which are edible for humans but toxic to pets. Its leaves are long, narrow, and have a glossy, dark green appearance.

Dracaena parrei is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of indoor and outdoor conditions.
