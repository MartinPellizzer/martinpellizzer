Dicksonia karsteniana var. spruceana

Dicksonia karsteniana var. spruceana, commonly known as the Spruce's tree fern, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of large, feather-like fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, fertile soil to flourish. Its rhizomes form a dense network that helps anchor the plant and absorb nutrients from the soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
