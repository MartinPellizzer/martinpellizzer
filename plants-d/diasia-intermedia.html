Diasia intermedia

Diasia intermedia, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its showy, violet-blue petals and a distinctive, spiky central cone. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are sturdy and branching.

Diasia intermedia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
