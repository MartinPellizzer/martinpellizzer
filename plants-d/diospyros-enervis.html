Diospyros enervis

Diospyros enervis, commonly known as the African persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and alternate, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that are followed by edible, orange-red berries. It is often found in moist, well-drained soils in woodland and forested areas.

The fruit is valued for its sweet, fleshy pulp and is used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
