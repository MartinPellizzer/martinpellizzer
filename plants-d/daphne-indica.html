Daphne indica

Daphne indica, commonly known as the winter daphne, is a deciduous shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense, fragrant clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, berry-like fruits that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Due to its toxicity, it should be handled with care.
