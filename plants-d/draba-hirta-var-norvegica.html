Draba hirta var. norvegica

Draba hirta var. norvegica, commonly known as the Norwegian mouse-ear cress, is a small, flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in early spring. This variety is native to northern Europe, particularly Norway, and thrives in rocky or alpine habitats. It is a perennial plant that is often found in open, disturbed areas such as roadsides and meadows.

The plant is notable for its ability to tolerate cold climates and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
