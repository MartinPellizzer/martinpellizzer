Dendrobium lonchophyllum

Dendrobium lonchophyllum, commonly known as the long-leaf dendrobium, is an epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a unique growth habit with long, slender pseudobulbs and thin, leathery leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers with a distinctive, spurred lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, loose potting media.

Dendrobium lonchophyllum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its beautiful blooms.
