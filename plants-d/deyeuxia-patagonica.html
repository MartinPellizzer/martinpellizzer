Deyeuxia patagonica

Deyeuxia patagonica, commonly known as Patagonian flat sedge, is a perennial grass species native to South America, particularly found in Argentina and Chile.

It typically grows in wetland environments such as marshes and peat bogs, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense mats, which helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Deyeuxia patagonica plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by providing habitat for various invertebrates and birds.
