Derris cuneifolia var. malaccensis

Derris cuneifolia var. malaccensis, commonly known as the Malacca velvet tamarind, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically trifoliate and have a velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by elongated, bean-like pods containing flat, edible seeds. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
