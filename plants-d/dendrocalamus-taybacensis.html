Dendrocalamus taybacensis

Dendrocalamus taybacensis, commonly known as the giant bamboo, is a tall, slender grass-like plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its hollow, jointed stems that can grow up to 30 meters in height. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically green and covered in a fine layer of hair. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland areas with rich, well-drained soils.

Dendrocalamus taybacensis is valued for its strong, lightweight bamboo, which is used in construction, furniture, and traditional crafts.
