Diosma demissa

Diosma demissa, commonly known as the desert diosma, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub with woody stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water requirements.

Diosma demissa plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
