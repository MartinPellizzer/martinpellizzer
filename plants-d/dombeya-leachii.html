Dombeya leachii

Dombeya leachii, commonly known as the Leach's dombeya, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in spring. The plant has smooth, grayish bark and lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded habitats.

Dombeya leachii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
