Digitaria brazzae

Digitaria brazzae, commonly known as the Brazza's digitaria, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands or disturbed areas.

Due to its adaptability, it plays a role in both ecological and agricultural systems in its native habitat.
