Diplotaxis hispidula

Diplotaxis hispidula, commonly known as the African cress, is an annual herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features erect, branched stems that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a rough texture. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from spring to summer. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have a slightly succulent texture.

Diplotaxis hispidula is known for its peppery flavor and is often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
