Dianthus raddeanus

Dianthus raddeanus, commonly known as the Radde's campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces small, showy blooms during the summer months.

Dianthus raddeanus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
