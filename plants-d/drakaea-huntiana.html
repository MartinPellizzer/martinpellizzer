Drakaea huntiana

Drakaea huntiana, commonly known as the Purple Dragon Orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the south-western region of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, hooded flower spike that resembles a dragon, with a deep purple hue and intricate patterns. The plant typically grows in sandy soils and is often found in open heathland environments. Drakaea huntiana is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its specialized pollination mechanism involving the mimicry of female wasps.

It is a rare and protected species, highlighting its ecological significance in its native habitat.
