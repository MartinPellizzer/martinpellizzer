Davallia pentaphylla

Davallia pentaphylla, commonly known as the maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, arching fronds that are typically dark green with a silvery underside. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and prefers well-draining soil. Its rhizomes spread horizontally, allowing it to form dense mats over time.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture and landscaping.
