Diplopappus incanus

Diplopappus incanus, commonly known as the hairy burrweed, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, hairy, bristly fruits that are adapted for wind dispersal. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments.

It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.
