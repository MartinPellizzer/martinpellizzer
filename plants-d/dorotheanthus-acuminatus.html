Dorotheanthus acuminatus

Dorotheanthus acuminatus, commonly known as the sharp-leaved daisy, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies, sunflowers, and other composite flowers. The plant features showy yellow flowers with white ray florets, surrounded by a central disk of smaller, tubular flowers. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking low-maintenance ornamental plants.
