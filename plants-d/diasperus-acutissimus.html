Diasperus acutissimus

Diasperus acutissimus, commonly known as the sharp-leaved dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, pointed leaves. The plant produces small, yellow composite flowers that bloom in the spring and summer months. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils.

Diasperus acutissimus is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
