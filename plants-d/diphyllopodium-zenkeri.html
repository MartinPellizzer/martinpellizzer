Diphyllopodium zenkeri

Diphyllopodium zenkeri, commonly known as the African buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a short, rhizomatous stem and produces clusters of bright yellow, buttercup-like flowers. The plant has palmately divided leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive, waxy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassy areas or along riverbanks.

Diphyllopodium zenkeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
