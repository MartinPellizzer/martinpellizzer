Dolichovigna

Dolichovigna, commonly known as the long-vine morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped flowers that open in the morning. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of blue, violet, and white. Its vines can grow up to 10 meters long, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The seeds are enclosed in a fleshy, red or orange capsule that splits open when mature.
