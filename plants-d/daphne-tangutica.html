Daphne tangutica

Daphne tangutica, commonly known as the Tangut Daphne, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of western China.

It belongs to the family Thymelaeaceae and is characterized by its aromatic, often fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers clustered in cymes, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a leathery texture with a distinct scent.

Daphne tangutica is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance and fragrance.
