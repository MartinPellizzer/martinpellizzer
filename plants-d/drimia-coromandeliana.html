Drimia coromandeliana

Drimia coromandeliana, commonly known as the Coromandel lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It features strap-shaped, lanceolate leaves that are typically green and slightly hairy. The plant produces clusters of white or pale blue flowers with showy, spurred petals, often blooming in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and architectural form.
