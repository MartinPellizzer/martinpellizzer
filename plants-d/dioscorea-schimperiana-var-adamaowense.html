Dioscorea schimperiana var. adamaowense

Dioscorea schimperiana var. adamaowense, commonly known as the Adamaowen yam, is a variant of the yam genus native to China.

It is a tuberous plant characterized by its large, starchy underground stems. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a rough texture, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers.

The tubers are valued for their culinary and medicinal uses in traditional Chinese practices.
