Dendranthema occidentalijaponense

Dendranthema occidentalijaponense, commonly known as the Western Japanese chamomile, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil. It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and calming properties.

Its aromatic leaves and flowers are also used in herbal teas and aromatherapy.
