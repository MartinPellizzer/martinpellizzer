Dicentra ventii

Dicentra ventii, commonly known as the pink lady's slipper, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features delicate, fern-like leaves and produces showy, pinkish-white flowers that resemble slipper-shaped blooms. The plant thrives in partial shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens. Its blooming period typically occurs in late spring to early summer.

Dicentra ventii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant landscaping.
