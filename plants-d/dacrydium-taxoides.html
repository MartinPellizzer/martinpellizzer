Dacrydium taxoides

Dacrydium taxoides, commonly known as the New Zealand silver pine, is a coniferous tree native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its slender, straight trunk and long, needle-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain both male and female reproductive organs. Its bark is smooth and light gray, with a distinctive flaky texture.

Dacrydium taxoides plays an important ecological role in New Zealand's forests and is also valued for its timber.
