Daphne × colorata

Daphne × colorata, commonly known as the winter daphne, is a hybrid shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Due to its toxic sap, it should be handled with care.
