Diospyros mauritiana

Diospyros mauritiana, commonly known as the Mauritius persimmon, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Mascarene Islands.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into orange to red berries, which are edible when fully ripe. Its fruits are rich in nutrients and have been traditionally used in local cuisines.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
