Deschampsia ciliata

Deschampsia ciliata, commonly known as silky wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, silvery-blue foliage. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows, marshes, and along streambanks.

Deschampsia ciliata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
