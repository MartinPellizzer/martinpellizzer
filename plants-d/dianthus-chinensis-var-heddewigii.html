Dianthus chinensis var. heddewigii

Dianthus chinensis var. heddewigii, commonly known as the Heddewig's campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and clusters of pink to purple flowers with five petals. The leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, and the plant produces small, round fruits called capsules. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable ornamental plant for rock gardens and alpine settings.

This variety is valued for its vibrant blooms and hardy nature.
