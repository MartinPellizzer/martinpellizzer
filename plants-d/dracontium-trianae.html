Dracontium trianae

Dracontium trianae, commonly known as the Trianas' dragon plant, is a striking tropical species native to South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence composed of a spadix and a spathe. The plant features large, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and deep green coloration. It produces showy, colorful flowers that are often surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe.

Dracontium trianae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in warm, humid environments for its dramatic foliage and floral displays.
