Dyckia machrisiana

Dyckia machrisiana, commonly known as the silver pine, is a species of bromeliad native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its tall, narrow leaves that are often covered in a silvery or grayish bloom, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is well adapted to dry, sunny conditions. It produces small, clustered inflorescences that are usually pink or red in color.

Dyckia machrisiana is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking foliage and hardy nature.
