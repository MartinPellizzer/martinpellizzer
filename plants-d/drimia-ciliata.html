Drimia ciliata

Drimia ciliata, commonly known as the Cape tulip, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features strap-shaped, pale green to blue-gray leaves with hairy margins. The plant produces striking, showy flowers in shades of purple and pink, typically blooming in late summer. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

Drimia ciliata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
