Diplazium cumingii

Diplazium cumingii, commonly known as the Cuming's water fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are typically light green in color. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its rhizomes are creeping and produce clusters of small, spore-bearing sorus.

Diplazium cumingii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
