Diplopappus extenuatus

Diplopappus extenuatus, commonly known as the slender pappus plant, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, branching stems and small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically yellow or greenish in color. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, and it produces achenes with silky pappus hairs that aid in wind dispersal.

It thrives in dry, open habitats and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.
