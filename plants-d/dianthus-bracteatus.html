Dianthus bracteatus

Dianthus bracteatus, commonly known as the bracted carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that typically come in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant features upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, bract-like structures at the base of its flowers. It blooms in late spring to early summer and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Dianthus bracteatus is relatively easy to grow and thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight.
