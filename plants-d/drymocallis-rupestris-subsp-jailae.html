Drymocallis rupestris subsp. jailae

Drymocallis rupestris subsp.

jailae, commonly known as the rock spurge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded environments.

The plant features upright stems with opposite, ovate leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and clustered in umbels, blooming in late spring to early summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging, arid conditions.
