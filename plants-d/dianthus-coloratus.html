Dianthus coloratus

Dianthus coloratus, commonly known as the showy campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red, often with a distinctive white or pink central stripe. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and produces clusters of flowers on slender stems. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in well-drained soil in full sun.

Dianthus coloratus is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
