Dichapetalum oblongifolium

Dichapetalum oblongifolium, commonly known as the African velvet tree, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and alternate, simple, ovate to elliptic leaves that are usually hairy beneath. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in panicles, which are typically followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions and is often found in woodlands and savannas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
