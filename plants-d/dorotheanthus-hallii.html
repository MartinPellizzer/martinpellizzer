Dorotheanthus hallii

Dorotheanthus hallii, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its bright orange to yellow flowers and lance-shaped leaves. The plant is an annual herb that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet. Its flowers are tubular and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Dorotheanthus hallii is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and easy care requirements.
