Dactylorhiza wirtgenii

Dactylorhiza wirtgenii, commonly known as the Wirtgen's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and showy, greenish-yellow to brownish flowers. The plant typically grows in damp, shaded habitats such as woodlands and meadows. It has a complex root system with mycorrhizal associations that are essential for nutrient uptake.

Dactylorhiza wirtgenii is known for its ability to thrive in moist environments and is often found in areas with rich, organic soil.
