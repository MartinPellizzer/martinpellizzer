Disperis circumflexa subsp. circumflexa

Disperis circumflexa subsp.

circumflexa, commonly known as the spiral orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its striking, spiral-shaped inflorescence that gives the plant its common name.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. Its flowers are usually greenish-yellow with a distinctive, elongated structure. Disperis circumflexa subsp.

circumflexa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
