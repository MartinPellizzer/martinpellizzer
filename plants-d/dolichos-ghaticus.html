Dolichos ghaticus

Dolichos ghaticus, commonly known as the wild cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, with slender stems that twine around supports. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by elongated pods containing edible seeds. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets.

Dolichos ghaticus is valued for its nutritional content and is used in traditional agriculture and cuisine.
