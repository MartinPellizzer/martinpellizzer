Dyckia disjuncta

Dyckia disjuncta, commonly known as the Disjunct Dyckia, is a species of bromeliad native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped foliage, which forms a tight cluster of stiff, grass-like leaves. The plant produces striking inflorescences with bright red bracts that contrast sharply with its green foliage. It thrives in sunny, well-drained environments and is adapted to dry conditions.

Dyckia disjuncta is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in arid climates.
