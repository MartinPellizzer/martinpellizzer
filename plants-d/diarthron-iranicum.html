Diarthron iranicum

Diarthron iranicum, commonly known as Persian mountain grass, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Iran and Central Asia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in rocky and arid environments, often growing at high altitudes.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects in degraded landscapes.
