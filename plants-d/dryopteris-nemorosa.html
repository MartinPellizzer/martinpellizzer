Dryopteris nemorosa

Dryopteris nemorosa, commonly known as the wood fern, is a species of fern native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its delicate, bipinnate fronds that are often covered in a fine, rusty-colored down. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its rhizomes are thick and creeping, allowing it to spread and form dense colonies.

The spores are produced on the underside of the fronds in clusters called sori, which are protected by a fiddle-shaped indusium.
