Draba japonica

Draba japonica, commonly known as Japanese rockcress, is a small flowering plant native to East Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or mountainous areas, preferring well-drained soils and full sun. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers in early spring. It has a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions.

Draba japonica is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with similar floral structures.
