Digitalis trojana

Digitalis trojana, commonly known as the Trojan foxglove, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the genus Digitalis, which includes several species known for their medicinal and toxic properties. The plant features tall, sparsely foliated stems that bear clusters of tubular, bell-shaped flowers in shades of purple and pink. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in a fine downy coating.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
