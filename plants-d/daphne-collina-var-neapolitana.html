Daphne collina var. neapolitana

Daphne collina var. neapolitana, commonly known as the Naples daphne, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to southern Europe.

It features aromatic, glossy evergreen leaves and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, orange-red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Due to its ornamental value and fragrance, it is often cultivated in gardens for its visual and sensory appeal.
