Digitalis intermedia

Digitalis intermedia, commonly known as the tall foxglove, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe.

It features tall, erect stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, adorned with tubular, violet to purple flowers. The plant produces showy, hooded flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, with a distinctive arrangement along the stem.

Digitalis intermedia is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
