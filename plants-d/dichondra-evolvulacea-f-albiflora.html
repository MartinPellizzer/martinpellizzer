Dichondra evolvulacea f. albiflora

Dichondra evolvulacea f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered dichondra, is a variant of the species Dichondra evolvulacea, characterized by its distinctive white flowers.

This plant is a low-growing perennial herb native to South America, particularly Brazil. It features opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth texture and a silver-green coloration. The flowers are small, clustered in loose inflorescences, and bloom in the spring.

It is often cultivated as a ground cover due to its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
