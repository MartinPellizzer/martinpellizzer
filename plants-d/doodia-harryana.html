Doodia harryana

Doodia harryana, commonly known as the harry's water fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and streams. The plant has delicate, floating leaves that are divided into small, lance-shaped segments. It produces sporangia on the underside of its fronds, which are clustered in groups called sori.

Doodia harryana is valued for its ornamental appearance and is often used in water gardens and pond ecosystems.
