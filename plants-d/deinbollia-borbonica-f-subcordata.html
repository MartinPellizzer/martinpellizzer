Deinbollia borbonica f. subcordata

Deinbollia borbonica f. subcordata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bignonia, is a flowering plant belonging to the Bignoniaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, subcordate leaves that are typically arranged opposite along the stems. The plant produces striking yellow to orange flowers that are clustered in racemes, often appearing in the summer months. It is native to parts of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
