Dipterocarpus mayapis

Dipterocarpus mayapis, commonly known as the Malayan ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its heavy, durable wood that is highly valued for construction and furniture. The plant has large, simple leaves that are typically alternate and have a rough texture. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody capsules containing winged seeds.

The species plays an important ecological role in rainforest ecosystems, contributing to biodiversity and soil stabilization.
