Davallia bipinnata

Davallia bipinnata, commonly known as the bird's nest fern, is a tropical fern native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its distinctive, crinkled fronds that resemble a bird's nest, hence its common name. The plant has a short, thick rhizome with dense, moss-like growth covering its base. Its fronds are bipinnate, meaning they have two levels of division, giving them a finely divided, lacy appearance.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often used as an ornamental plant in gardens and indoor spaces.
