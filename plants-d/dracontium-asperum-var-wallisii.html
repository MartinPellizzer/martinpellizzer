Dracontium asperum var. wallisii

Dracontium asperum var. wallisii, commonly known as the Wallis's rough dracontium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant features large, heart-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture, which contribute to its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid tropical forests.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and ecological role within its native habitat.
