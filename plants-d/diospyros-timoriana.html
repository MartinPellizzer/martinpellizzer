Diospyros timoriana

Diospyros timoriana, commonly known as the Timor persimmon, is a small to medium-sized tree native to the Maluku Islands in Indonesia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes ebony and persimmon trees. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a smooth margin. Its flowers are small and clustered, and it produces edible berries that are typically orange to red in color when ripe.

The wood of Diospyros timoriana is valued for its density and is sometimes used in traditional crafts and furniture.
