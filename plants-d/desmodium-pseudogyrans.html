Desmodium pseudogyrans

Desmodium pseudogyrans, commonly known as the narrow-leaf desmodium, is a leguminous plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three small, elliptical leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in the summer months. Its pods are slender and contain several elliptical seeds, which are often used in traditional herbal medicine.

Desmodium pseudogyrans is native to tropical and subtropical regions and is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils.
