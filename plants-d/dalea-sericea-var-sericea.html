Dalea sericea var. sericea

Dalea sericea var. sericea, commonly known as silky milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It features slender, branching stems covered in silky, white to grayish hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being narrow and hairy, contributing to its overall delicate look.

Dalea sericea var. sericea is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.
