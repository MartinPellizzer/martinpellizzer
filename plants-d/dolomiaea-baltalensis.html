Dolomiaea baltalensis

Dolomiaea baltalensis, commonly known as the Baltalens rock cress, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Tibetan Plateau.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh climatic conditions. Its leaves are usually small and succulent, aiding in water retention.

Dolomiaea baltalensis plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is of interest to botanists studying alpine flora.
