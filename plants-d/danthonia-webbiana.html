Danthonia webbiana

Danthonia webbiana, commonly known as the Webb's danthonia, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant typically features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is often found in sandy or rocky soils in arid or semi-arid regions.

Due to its resilience, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
