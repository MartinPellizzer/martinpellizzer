Dioscorea rangunensis

Dioscorea rangunensis, commonly known as the African yam bean, is a member of the Dioscoreaceae family and is native to tropical Africa.

It is a climbing vine that produces tuberous roots, which are its primary edible part. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by capsule fruits. Its tubers are rich in starch and have been traditionally used as a food source in various regions.

The plant also has medicinal uses in some traditional healing practices.
