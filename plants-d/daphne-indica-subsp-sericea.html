Daphne indica subsp. sericea

Daphne indica subsp.

sericea, commonly known as the silverleaf daphne, is a species native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage that gives it its common name.

The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is often cultivated in temperate climates.
