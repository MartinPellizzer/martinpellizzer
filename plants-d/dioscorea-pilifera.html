Dioscorea pilifera

Dioscorea pilifera, commonly known as the hairy yam, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and tuberous roots. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically borne in clusters. Its leaves are heart-shaped with hairy undersides, giving it its common name.

The tubers of Dioscorea pilifera are used in traditional medicine and as a food source in some cultures.
