Davallia trichomanoides f. barbata

Davallia trichomanoides f. barbata, commonly known as the Barbed Davallia, is a fern species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are often covered in a dense layer of brown, fuzzy hairs. The plant prefers shady, humid environments and thrives in well-draining, acidic soil. Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

The fronds are typically evergreen, providing a lush, green presence throughout the year.
