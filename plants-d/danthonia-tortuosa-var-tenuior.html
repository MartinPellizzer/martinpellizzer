Danthonia tortuosa var. tenuior

Danthonia tortuosa var. tenuior, commonly known as the slender danthonia, is a grass species native to parts of Australia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and slender, creeping growth habit. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often in open habitats such as heathlands and coastal dunes. Its flowering stems are usually short and often bear small, spike-like inflorescences.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.
