Despeleza capitata

Despeleza capitata, commonly known as the Brazilian candle plant, is a striking member of the Asteraceae family.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its tall, candle-like stems covered in dense, woolly white hairs. The plant produces large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are also covered in a fine layer of white pubescence.

Despeleza capitata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to grow in well-drained soils in full sun.
