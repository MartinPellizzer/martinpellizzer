Dipteracanthus primulaceus

Dipteracanthus primulaceus, commonly known as the mountain primrose, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family, which includes a variety of flowering plants known for their showy blooms. The plant is characterized by its delicate, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a yellow center. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in montane forests and alpine meadows.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many parts of its native range.
