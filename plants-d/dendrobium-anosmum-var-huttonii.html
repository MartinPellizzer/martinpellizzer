Dendrobium anosmum var. huttonii

Dendrobium anosmum var. huttonii, commonly known as the Hutton's orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its long, cane-like stems that store water and nutrients. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow, typically during the summer months. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light and requires well-draining potting media.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated for horticultural purposes.
