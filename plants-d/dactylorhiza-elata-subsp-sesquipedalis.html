Dactylorhiza elata subsp. sesquipedalis

Dactylorhiza elata subsp.

sesquipedalis, commonly known as the long-leaf marsh orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe and northern Africa. It typically grows in wet, calcareous habitats such as marshes and damp meadows.

The plant features a tall, erect stem with several narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are arranged in a spike and are usually pink or purple in color, with a distinctive three-lobed lip.

This orchid is known for its delicate appearance and is often found in association with other wetland plant species.
