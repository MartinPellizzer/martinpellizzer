Dichocarpum basilare

Dichocarpum basilare, commonly known as the alpine bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and tall, slender stems that bear bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its flowers, which are often blue or violet in color, bloom in late summer.

Dichocarpum basilare is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
