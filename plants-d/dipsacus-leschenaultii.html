Dipsacus leschenaultii

Dipsacus leschenaultii, commonly known as the Leschenault's thorn.

It is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region, characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of small, pinkish-purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Dipsacus leschenaultii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in challenging conditions.
