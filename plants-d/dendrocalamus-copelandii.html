Dendrocalamus copelandii

Dendrocalamus copelandii, commonly known as the giant bamboo, is a tall, slender grass-like plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its rapid growth and hollow stems. The plant can reach heights of up to 30 meters, making it one of the tallest bamboo species. Its culms are strong and lightweight, often used in construction and handicrafts.

Dendrocalamus copelandii thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.
