Draba lanjarica

Draba lanjarica, commonly known as the Alpine mouse-ear cress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other mustard family plants. The plant is characterized by its low-growing habit, with small, basal leaves and clusters of tiny, white flowers. It thrives in rocky and alpine environments, often found at high altitudes where conditions are harsh.

Draba lanjarica is adapted to cold climates and is typically found in meadows and slopes above the tree line.
