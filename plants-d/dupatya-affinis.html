Dupatya affinis

Dupatya affinis, commonly known as the broadleaf arrowhead, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix and a spathe. The plant typically grows in wetlands and marshy areas, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soil. Its leaves are broad and arrow-shaped, often with a waxy surface that aids in water retention.

Dupatya affinis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
