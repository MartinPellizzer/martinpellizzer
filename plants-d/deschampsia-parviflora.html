Deschampsia parviflora

Deschampsia parviflora, commonly known as slender wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, tufted stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. Its inflorescence consists of a single spikelet that bears small, pale florets.

Deschampsia parviflora is known for its ability to thrive in poor soils and its role in stabilizing erosion-prone areas.
