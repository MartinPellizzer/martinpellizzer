Deparia wilsonii var. wilsonii

Deparia wilsonii var. wilsonii, commonly known as Wilson's shield fern, is a species of fern native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, shield-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its rhizomes are fibrous, and the fronds are bi-lobed with a serrated margin.

The spore-producing structures, called sori, are clustered on the underside of the fronds, contributing to its reproductive strategy.
