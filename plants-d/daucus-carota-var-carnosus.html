Daucus carota var. carnosus

Daucus carota var. carnosus, commonly known as the wild carrot, is a biennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features finely divided, feathery leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into thin, hollow stems covered in fine hairs. Its roots are typically slender and have a distinct, carroty aroma.

This variety is often considered a weed but is also valued in traditional medicine and as a source of edible seeds.
