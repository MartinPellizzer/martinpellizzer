Daphne gnidium var. maritima

Daphne gnidium var. maritima, commonly known as the coastal daphne, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal areas, characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to the delicate blossoms.

Daphne gnidium var. maritima is well adapted to saline and well-drained soils, making it a resilient plant in coastal environments.
