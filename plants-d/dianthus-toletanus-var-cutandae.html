Dianthus toletanus var. cutandae

Dianthus toletanus var. cutandae, commonly known as the Toletan carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of bright pink or red flowers with a distinctive frilled appearance. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing showy inflorescences that attract pollinators. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value and historical significance in horticulture contribute to its popularity among gardeners.
