Doronicum bellidiflorum

Doronicum bellidiflorum, commonly known as the bellflower primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It features upright, branched stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically in shades of yellow to orange. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine downy layer, giving them a soft texture. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
