Dioscorea simulans

Dioscorea simulans, commonly known as the African yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are used as a food source. The plant produces small, green flowers that develop into capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are hairy and twining.

Dioscorea simulans is adapted to grow in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
