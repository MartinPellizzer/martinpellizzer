Delphinium elatum var. sericeum

Delphinium elatum var. sericeum, commonly known as the velvet delphinium, is a striking perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem that can reach up to 2 meters in height, supporting clusters of large, showy blue to purple flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, silvery-white down, giving the plant its "velvet" appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its dramatic floral display.
