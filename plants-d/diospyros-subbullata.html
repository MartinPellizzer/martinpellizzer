Diospyros subbullata

Diospyros subbullata, commonly known as the rough persimmon, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other persimmon species. The plant is characterized by its dense, rough-textured bark and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. Its small, yellowish-green flowers appear in clusters, followed by edible, orange-red berries that are typically consumed when fully ripe.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine and cuisine.
