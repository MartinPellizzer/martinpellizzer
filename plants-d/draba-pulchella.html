Draba pulchella

Draba pulchella, commonly known as the pretty draba, is a small flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Europe and is characterized by its delicate, basal rosette of leaves and small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Draba pulchella is often found in meadows and open slopes at high elevations.

Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a subtle beauty to the alpine landscape.
