Dactylorhiza vironii

Dactylorhiza vironii, commonly known as the purple marsh orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It thrives in wet or marshy habitats, often growing in damp woodlands and along stream banks. The plant has tall, erect stems with lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of purple or violet flowers. It is characterized by its complex root system, which includes a symbiotic relationship with mycorrhizal fungi.

Dactylorhiza vironii is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its ornamental value and ecological importance in wetland ecosystems.
