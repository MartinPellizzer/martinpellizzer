Dryopteris neoassamensis

Dryopteris neoassamensis, commonly known as the Assam cliff fern, is a rare species of fern native to the northeastern regions of India.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its intricate fronds with a distinctive pinnae arrangement. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in rocky crevices and forest floors. Its spores are produced on the undersides of the fronds, contributing to its reproductive strategy.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species, highlighting the importance of conservation efforts.
