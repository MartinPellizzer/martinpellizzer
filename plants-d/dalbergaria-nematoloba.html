Dalbergaria nematoloba

Dalbergaria nematoloba, commonly known as the Natal siris, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its root system is well-developed, allowing it to thrive in well-drained soils, and it is often found in grassland and rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
