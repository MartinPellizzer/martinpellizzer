Desmodium saccatum

Desmodium saccatum, commonly known as hairy sainfoin, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it valuable in agricultural settings. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used as forage for livestock.

Desmodium saccatum also plays a role in soil conservation due to its extensive root system.
