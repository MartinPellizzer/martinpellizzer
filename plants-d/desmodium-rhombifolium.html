Desmodium rhombifolium

Desmodium rhombifolium, commonly known as hairy bird's-beak, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Americas.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, narrow, rhomboid-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically purple or pink in color. It is often found in grassy areas and can thrive in a variety of soil types.

Desmodium rhombifolium is also known for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
