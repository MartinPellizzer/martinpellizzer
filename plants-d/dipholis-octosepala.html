Dipholis octosepala

Dipholis octosepala, commonly known as the eight-lobed orchid, is a species of orchid native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in clusters. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are elongated and strap-like, often arranged in a fan shape.

The flower structure includes eight sepals, which give the plant its scientific name.
