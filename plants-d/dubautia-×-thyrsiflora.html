Dubautia × thyrsiflora

Dubautia × thyrsiflora, commonly known as the hybrid pohuehue, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically produces clusters of bright orange or red flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for coastal and dryland environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens, though it can become invasive in certain areas.
