Digitaria katangensis

Digitaria katangensis, commonly known as Katanga grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny grains, which are adapted for wind pollination. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands or disturbed areas.

Digitaria katangensis is valued for its potential in forage and erosion control in its native habitat.
