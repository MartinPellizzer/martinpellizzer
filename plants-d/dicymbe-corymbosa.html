Dicymbe corymbosa

Dicymbe corymbosa, commonly known as the sweet sarsaparilla, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and clustered inflorescences. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It is often grown for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various health purposes.
