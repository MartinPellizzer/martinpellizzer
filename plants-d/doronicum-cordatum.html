Doronicum cordatum

Doronicum cordatum, commonly known as the heart-leaved leopard's bane, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features dark green, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces clusters of yellow-orange flowers in early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade conditions. It is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
