Dioscorea flaccida

Dioscorea flaccida, commonly known as the slender yam, is a tropical vine belonging to the family Dioscoreaceae.

It is native to parts of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands, where it thrives in humid, tropical climates. The plant is characterized by its long, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves. Its tuberous roots are the primary part used, containing compounds with medicinal properties.

Dioscorea flaccida is often cultivated for its nutritional value and traditional use in herbal medicine.
