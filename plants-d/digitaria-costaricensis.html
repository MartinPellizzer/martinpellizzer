Digitaria costaricensis

Digitaria costaricensis, commonly known as the Costa Rican digitaria, is a grass species native to Central America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Digitaria costaricensis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
