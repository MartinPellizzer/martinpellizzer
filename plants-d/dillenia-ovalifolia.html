Dillenia ovalifolia

Dillenia ovalifolia, commonly known as the oval-leaf dillenia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, yellowish-white flowers with numerous stamens, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruits are woody, berry-like structures that split open to release seeds.

Dillenia ovalifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
