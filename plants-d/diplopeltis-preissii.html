Diplopeltis preissii

Diplopeltis preissii, commonly known as the African nettle, is a climbing plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Urticaceae, which includes many stinging plants. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive network of veins, giving it its common name. It produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters.

The plant is known for its stinging hairs, which can cause irritation upon contact with the skin.
