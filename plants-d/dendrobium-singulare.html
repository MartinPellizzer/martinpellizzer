Dendrobium singulare

Dendrobium singulare, commonly known as the single-stem orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is an epiphytic orchid that grows on trees and rocks, thriving in humid tropical climates. The plant produces long, slender pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its flowers are typically yellow or green with red or purple markings, and they bloom in clusters.

Dendrobium singulare is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for horticultural purposes.
