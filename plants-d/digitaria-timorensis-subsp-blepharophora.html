Digitaria timorensis subsp. blepharophora

Digitaria timorensis subsp.

blepharophora, commonly known as the Timor digitaria, is a grass species native to the island of Timor. It is characterized by its erect, slender growth habit and narrow, linear leaves.

The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences with two to three florets per spikelet. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along stream banks.

This subspecies is notable for its distinctive floral structure and ecological adaptations to its native habitat.
