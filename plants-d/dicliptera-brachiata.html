Dicliptera brachiata

Dicliptera brachiata, commonly known as the branched blady grass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a panicle, which produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Dicliptera brachiata is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
