Deinandra increscens subsp. increscens

Deinandra increscens subsp.

increscens, commonly known as the rough-stemmed ironwood, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and dense, evergreen foliage.

The plant produces yellowish-white flowers arranged in cylindrical inflorescences, which attract pollinators such as bees and birds. It is well adapted to nutrient-poor soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various local wildlife species.
