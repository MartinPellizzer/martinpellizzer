Diplocrater reticulata

Diplocrater reticulata, commonly known as the reticulated diplocrater, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves with a distinctive network of veins, giving it a unique and ornamental appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, adding to its aesthetic appeal. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
