Dendrobium schoeninum

Dendrobium schoeninum, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features long, wiry stems that can grow up to several meters in length, often covered in a dense layer of white or brown hairs. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer, adding ornamental value. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Dendrobium schoeninum is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its striking appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
