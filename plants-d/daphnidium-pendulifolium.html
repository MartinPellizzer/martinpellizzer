Daphnidium pendulifolium

Daphnidium pendulifolium, commonly known as the hanging-leaf plant, is a member of the Gesneriaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its pendulous, elongated leaves that give it a distinctive, trailing appearance. It produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red in color.

Daphnidium pendulifolium is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant due to its ornamental foliage and relatively easy care requirements.
