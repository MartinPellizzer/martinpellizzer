Draperia

Draperia, commonly known as sweetshrub, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Geraniaceae and is characterized by its dense, fragrant foliage that emits a sweet, spicy scent. The plant produces small, clustered white or pink flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, capsule-like fruits. Draperia thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its aromatic leaves and showy blooms.
