Digitaria ropalotricha

Digitaria ropalotricha, commonly known as the hairy fingergrass, is a perennial grass species native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and hairy leaves. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in open fields and along roadsides. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are arranged in a loose panicle, and it produces small, two-lobed seeds.

Digitaria ropalotricha is adapted to a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.
