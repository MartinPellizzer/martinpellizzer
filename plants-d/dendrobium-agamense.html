Dendrobium agamense

Dendrobium agamense, commonly known as the Agam's orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green. Its leaves are typically broad and fleshy, adapted to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

Dendrobium agamense is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for horticultural purposes.
