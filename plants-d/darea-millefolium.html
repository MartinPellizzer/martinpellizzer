Darea millefolium

Darea millefolium, commonly known as yarrow, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves and clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, woodlands, and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils. Yarrow is known for its aromatic leaves and is often used in herbal medicine for its purported healing properties.

It is also a popular ornamental plant in gardens due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature.
