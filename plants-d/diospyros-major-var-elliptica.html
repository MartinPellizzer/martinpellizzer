Diospyros major var. elliptica

Diospyros major var. elliptica, commonly known as the elliptic persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, orange to red berries that are often consumed by wildlife and humans alike. Its wood is dense and strong, making it valuable for crafting and traditional uses.

The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.
