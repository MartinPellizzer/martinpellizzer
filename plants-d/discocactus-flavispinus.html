Discocactus flavispinus

Discocactus flavispinus, commonly known as the yellow-spined discocactus, is a species of cactus native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are typically covered in spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are clustered at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun exposure.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.
