Dianthus alatavicus

Dianthus alatavicus, commonly known as the Armenian pink, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous areas, preferring well-drained soil and full sun. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces small, clustered flowers that attract pollinators.

Dianthus alatavicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and resilience.
