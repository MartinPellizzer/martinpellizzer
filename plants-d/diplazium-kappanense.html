Diplazium kappanense

Diplazium kappanense, commonly known as the Kappanese water fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, floating fronds that are typically less than 30 cm in length. The plant thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and streams. Its rhizomes are often submerged, anchoring the plant while absorbing nutrients from the water.

Diplazium kappanense plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
