Diplocaulobium araneola

Diplocaulobium araneola, commonly known as the spider orchid, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, spider-like appearance due to its long, thin leaves and showy, spurred flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands and swampy areas, thriving in moist, acidic soils. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with a distinctive, intricate structure that aids in pollination by specific insects.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its ecological role and limited distribution.
