Diosma grandiflora

Diosma grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered diosma, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and riverbanks. The plant features elongated, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, white or pale pink flowers. Its botanical structure includes a fibrous root system and a branching stem that supports its showy blooms.

Diosma grandiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
