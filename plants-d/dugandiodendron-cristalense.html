Dugandiodendron cristalense

Dugandiodendron cristalense, commonly known as the Crystal Mountain tree, is a rare flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, palm-like foliage and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, moist conditions, often found in cloud forests. Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat loss and climate change, it faces significant threats to its survival.
