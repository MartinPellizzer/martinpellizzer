Diospyros flavescens

Diospyros flavescens, commonly known as the yellow persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and elliptical leaves with a leathery texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by orange to red, fleshy fruits. Its fruit is edible when fully ripe and has a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor.

Diospyros flavescens is valued for its ornamental qualities and traditional uses in local medicine.
