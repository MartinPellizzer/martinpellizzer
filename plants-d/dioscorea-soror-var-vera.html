Dioscorea soror var. vera

Dioscorea soror var. vera, commonly known as the true yam, is a member of the Dioscoreaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial vine characterized by its climbing habit and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by tuberous roots that are the primary edible part. Its tubers are rich in starch and have been traditionally used as a food source in various cultures.

The plant's botanical structure supports its role in both culinary and medicinal applications.
