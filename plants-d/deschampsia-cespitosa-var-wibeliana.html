Deschampsia cespitosa var. wibeliana

Deschampsia cespitosa var. wibeliana, commonly known as Wibel's bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and forms dense, tufted clumps. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as wet meadows and marshes. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of spikelets, which are often tinged with purple or reddish hues.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
