Dendrobium cathcartii

Dendrobium cathcartii, commonly known as the Cathcart's orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with multiple stems that often branch and produce numerous leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and pink, typically arranged in racemes. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.

Dendrobium cathcartii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for horticultural purposes.
