Dichrocephala grangeifolia

Dichrocephala grangeifolia, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces bright yellow flowers with a distinctive dark center. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and along roadsides.

Dichrocephala grangeifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
