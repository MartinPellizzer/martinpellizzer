Dicliptera maculata subsp. maculata

Dicliptera maculata subsp.

maculata, commonly known as the spotted grass, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa. It features a tufted growth habit with slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with purple or spotted with silver.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands or disturbed sites.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important component of the flora in its native habitats.
