Dryopteris laurentiorum

Dryopteris laurentiorum, commonly known as the barren barren fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its large, leathery, dark green fronds that are typically divided into lance-shaped segments. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks. Its rhizomes are thick and creeping, allowing it to spread and form dense colonies.

The fronds remain green throughout the year, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens and natural landscapes.
