Dichapetalum crassifolium var. crassifolium

Dichapetalum crassifolium var. crassifolium, commonly known as the thickleaf dichapetalum, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, thick, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the rainy season. Its bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
