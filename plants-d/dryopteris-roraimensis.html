Dryopteris roraimensis

Dryopteris roraimensis, commonly known as the Roraima wood fern, is a species of fern native to the tepuis of South America.

It is characterized by its large, lance-shaped fronds that can reach lengths of up to 2 meters. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in montane rainforests. Its sporangia are clustered in sori on the underside of the fronds, a typical feature of ferns.

The Roraima wood fern is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
