Dryopteris linnaeana var. disjuncta

Dryopteris linnaeana var. disjuncta, commonly known as the Disjunct Shield Fern, is a variant of the shield fern characterized by its distinctive frond morphology.

It features bipinnate leaves with a unique disjunct pattern, where the pinnae are separated by gaps, giving it a segmented appearance. The plant is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its fronds are usually dark green and can grow up to several feet in length.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and shaded landscapes.
