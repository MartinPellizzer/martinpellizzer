Descurainia nana

Descurainia nana, commonly known as dwarf tansy, is a low-growing annual herb native to North America.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from June to September. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often covered in a powdery coating. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used historically for its antiseptic and anti-inflammatory effects.
