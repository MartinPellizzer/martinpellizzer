Diosma puberula

Diosma puberula, commonly known as the softstem diosma, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb with slender, hairy stems and alternate, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant, white to pinkish flowers in clusters during the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and rocky slopes.

Diosma puberula is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
