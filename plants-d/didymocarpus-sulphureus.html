Didymocarpus sulphureus

Didymocarpus sulphureus, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a vibrant appearance. The plant produces small, woody fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical and subtropical regions.
