Disaccanthus

Disaccanthus, commonly known as the flame flower, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Acanthaceae.

It is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that often display vibrant colors such as red, orange, and yellow. The plants typically have opposite, ovate to lance-shaped leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Disaccanthus species are native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

They thrive in warm, humid environments and are often cultivated for their ornamental value in gardens and as houseplants.
