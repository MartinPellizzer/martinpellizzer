Dombeya decanthera subsp. meridionalis

Dombeya decanthera subsp.

meridionalis, commonly known as the southern dombeya, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming in spring.

The plant features narrow, leathery leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
