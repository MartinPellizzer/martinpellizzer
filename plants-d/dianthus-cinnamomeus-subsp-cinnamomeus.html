Dianthus cinnamomeus subsp. cinnamomeus

Dianthus cinnamomeus subsp.

cinnamomeus, commonly known as the cinnamon pink, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features upright, branching stems that bear clusters of fragrant, pinkish-red flowers with five petals.

The leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This species is noted for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
