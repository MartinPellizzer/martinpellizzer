Dichapetalum cazengoense

Dichapetalum cazengoense, commonly known as the Cazengo flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in tropical and subtropical regions.

The bark is smooth and often has a reddish or brownish hue, adding to its visual appeal.
