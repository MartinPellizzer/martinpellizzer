Dombeya bojeriana

Dombeya bojeriana, commonly known as the Bojer dombeya, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and are borne in clusters. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are elliptical and often have a slight pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period, which typically occurs in spring and early summer.
