Disynia

Disynia, commonly known as the silverleaf nightshade, is a flowering plant belonging to the Solanaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, white or purple flowers and hairy, silver-tinted leaves. The plant is native to parts of Europe and Asia and thrives in dry, rocky habitats. Disynia produces small, round berries that contain toxic compounds, making it harmful to animals and humans.

It is often considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its invasive growth habits.
