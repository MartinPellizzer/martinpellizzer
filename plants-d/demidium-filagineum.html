Demidium filagineum

Demidium filagineum, commonly known as the threadleaf demidium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its slender, thread-like leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue or violet in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
