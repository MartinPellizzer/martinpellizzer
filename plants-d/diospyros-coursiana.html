Diospyros coursiana

Diospyros coursiana, commonly known as the African persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in catkins, followed by orange to red fleshy fruits. Its fruit is edible, though some sources suggest it may have a mild astringent taste, and it is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
