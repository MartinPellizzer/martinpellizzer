Dendrophthoe leptopetala

Dendrophthoe leptopetala, commonly known as the slender leaf mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant native to the forests of Southeast Asia.

It attaches itself to the branches of host trees, such as those in the genus Ficus, and derives some of its nutrients through specialized roots called haustoria. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its elliptical, leathery leaves are arranged in pairs along the stems, and the plant is often found in shaded, humid environments.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can have significant ecological impacts on its host trees.
