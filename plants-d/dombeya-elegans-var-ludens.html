Dombeya elegans var. ludens

Dombeya elegans var. ludens, commonly known as the golden dombeya, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

The species is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
