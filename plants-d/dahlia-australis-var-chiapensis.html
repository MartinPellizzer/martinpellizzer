Dahlia australis var. chiapensis

Dahlia australis var. chiapensis, commonly known as the Chiapan dahlia, is a striking tuberous-rooted perennial native to Mexico.

It features showy, single or double flowers in a range of colors, including red, orange, and yellow, with a distinctive, often recurved petal structure. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with lance-shaped, dark green foliage. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Its botanical significance lies in its ornamental value and role in horticultural diversity within the dahlia genus.
