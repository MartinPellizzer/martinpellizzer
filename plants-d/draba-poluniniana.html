Draba poluniniana

Draba poluniniana, commonly known as the Polunin's rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the high mountain regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its leaves are basal, rosette-like, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Draba poluniniana is noted for its resilience to harsh environmental conditions, making it a hardy species adapted to extreme climates.
