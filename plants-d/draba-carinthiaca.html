Draba carinthiaca

Draba carinthiaca, commonly known as Carinthian whitlow grass, is a flowering plant native to Central Europe.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbages. This plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine regions, thriving in rocky and well-drained soils. Draba carinthiaca produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and is often found in mountainous areas across the Alps and Carpathian ranges.
