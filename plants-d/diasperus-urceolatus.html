Diasperus urceolatus

Diasperus urceolatus, commonly known as the bladderwort, is a unique aquatic plant characterized by its small, bladder-like structures called bladders.

These bladders are used to trap tiny aquatic organisms, which the plant then digests for nutrients. It is typically found in still or slow-moving freshwater environments. The plant has a root system that anchors it to the substrate, while its stems and leaves are often submerged.

Diasperus urceolatus plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by contributing to the food web and maintaining water quality.
