Dorycnium fulgurans

Dorycnium fulgurans, commonly known as the golden vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces cylindrical pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.

Dorycnium fulgurans is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches the soil.
