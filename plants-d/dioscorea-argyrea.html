Dioscorea argyrea

Dioscorea argyrea, commonly known as silver vine, is a climbing plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its vigorous growth and ability to twine around supports. The plant produces small, pale green flowers followed by tuberous roots that are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Silver vine is also valued as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
