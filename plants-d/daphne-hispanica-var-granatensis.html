Daphne hispanica var. granatensis

Daphne hispanica var. granatensis, commonly known as the Spanish daphne or granate daphne, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Europe.

It features dense, aromatic foliage with a distinctive, waxy texture. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers in early spring, followed by red berries in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its seasonal color changes and unique foliage.
