Dichaea similis

Dichaea similis, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic epiphytic plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its lack of leaves, relying instead on its roots for nutrient absorption. The plant produces small, delicate white or greenish flowers that bloom during the wet season. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on trees or rocks in the understory of dense rainforests.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, Dichaea similis is considered a conservation concern.
