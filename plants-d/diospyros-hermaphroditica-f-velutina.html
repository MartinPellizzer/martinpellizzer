Diospyros hermaphroditica f. velutina

Diospyros hermaphroditica f. velutina, commonly known as the velvet persimmon, is a variant of the persimmon tree native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its smooth, velvety leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are both male and female, making it a perfect flower species. Its fruit, which ripens to a sweet, orange-red color, is edible and highly prized.

This botanical variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its unique fruit characteristics.
