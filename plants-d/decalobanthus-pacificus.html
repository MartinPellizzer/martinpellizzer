Decalobanthus pacificus

Decalobanthus pacificus, commonly known as the Pacific decalobanthus, is a flowering plant native to the western Pacific region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, adapting well to saline environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in dense clusters.

It plays a role in stabilizing coastal dunes and provides habitat for various small organisms.
