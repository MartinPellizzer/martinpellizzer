Diospyros peregrina

Diospyros peregrina, commonly known as the desert date or wild persimmon, is a small to medium-sized tree native to the arid and semi-arid regions of the Arabian Peninsula and parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that help reduce water loss in hot, dry environments. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by edible, orange-red berries. Its fruit is often consumed raw or used in traditional medicine.

Diospyros peregrina is well adapted to harsh conditions, making it an important species in desert ecosystems.
