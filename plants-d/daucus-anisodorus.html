Daucus anisodorus

Daucus anisodorus, commonly known as the purple carrot, is a flowering plant in the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its biennial growth habit, with the first year producing a basal rosette of pinnately lobed leaves. The second year sees the plant developing a tall, branched stem with small, yellowish flowers. Its roots are typically purple or reddish in color, which gives it its common name.

The plant is native to the Mediterranean region and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible roots.
