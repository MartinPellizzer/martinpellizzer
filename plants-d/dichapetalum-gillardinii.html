Dichapetalum gillardinii

Dichapetalum gillardinii, commonly known as the African buttercup, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in its arid habitat.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
