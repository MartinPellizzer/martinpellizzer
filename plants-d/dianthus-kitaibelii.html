Dianthus kitaibelii

Dianthus kitaibelii, commonly known as the Kashmir carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and produces clusters of pink or white flowers with prominent red or purple markings. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and is often found in rocky or alpine environments. It belongs to the carnation family, Caryophyllaceae, and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Dianthus kitaibelii is typically cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and resilience in cooler climates.
