Dactylorhiza purpurella var. crassifolia

Dactylorhiza purpurella var. crassifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaf marsh orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy purple or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in wet meadows, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, thriving in moist, acidic soils. Its leaves are broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in its native habitat.

The species is known for its fragrant blooms and is often found in colonies, making it a distinctive feature of its ecosystem.
