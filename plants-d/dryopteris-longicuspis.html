Dryopteris longicuspis

Dryopteris longicuspis, commonly known as the long-lobed wood fern, is a species of fern native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender fronds that can reach up to 1.5 meters in length. The fronds are bipinnate, with elongated, narrow pinnae that have finely toothed margins. It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in well-drained, acidic soils.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticultural settings.
