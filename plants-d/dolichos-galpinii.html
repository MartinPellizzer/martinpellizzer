Dolichos galpinii

Dolichos galpinii, commonly known as the Galpin's horsegram, is a climbing legume native to southern Africa.

It features trifoliate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant bears pods that contain several kidney-shaped seeds, which are used as a legume crop in some regions. It thrives in warm, dry climates and is adapted to poor soil conditions.

Dolichos galpinii is valued for its nutritional content and role in sustainable agriculture.
