Delphinium emarginatum var. nevadense

Delphinium emarginatum var. nevadense, commonly known as the Sierra delphinium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems that bear clusters of blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough, hairy texture.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
