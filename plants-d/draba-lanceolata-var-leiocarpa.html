Draba lanceolata var. leiocarpa

Draba lanceolata var. leiocarpa, commonly known as the smooth-capsule whitlow grass, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically has basal leaves that are lanceolate in shape, with a smooth, glabrous surface. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or arctic environments.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to harsh, high-altitude conditions.
