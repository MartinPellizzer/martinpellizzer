Disa aperta

Disa aperta, commonly known as the open disa, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, spurred flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in open, sandy or rocky habitats and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, fragrant blooms during the summer months.

Disa aperta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
