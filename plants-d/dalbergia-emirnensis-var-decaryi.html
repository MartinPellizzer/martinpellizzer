Dalbergia emirnensis var. decaryi

Dalbergia emirnensis var. decaryi, commonly known as the African rosewood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The tree produces hard, dark wood that is highly valued for its density and fine grain, making it a prized material in traditional craftsmanship.

Its botanical features include a taproot system and the ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with soil bacteria.
