Dracaena aureolus

Dracaena aureolus, commonly known as the golden dracaena, is a species of flowering plant in the asparagus family.

It is native to eastern Africa and is characterized by its narrow, dark green leaves with a prominent yellow stripe running down the center. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are followed by red berries. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in a variety of indoor conditions.

Dracaena aureolus prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
