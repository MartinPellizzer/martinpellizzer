Diuris monticola

Diuris monticola, commonly known as the mountain diuris, is a terrestrial orchid native to southeastern Australia.

It typically grows in sandy or rocky soils, often in open forest or heathland environments. The plant produces a single, erect stem with a few leaves and bears a spike of pale green to yellow flowers. Its flowers are characterized by their narrow, lance-shaped petals and a distinctive, narrow labellum.

Diuris monticola is often found in coastal regions and is adapted to a range of climatic conditions.
