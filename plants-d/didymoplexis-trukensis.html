Didymoplexis trukensis

Didymoplexis trukensis, commonly known as the Truk Island orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Caroline Islands.

It is characterized by its elegant, pendulous flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pale pink. The plant has linear, grass-like leaves and produces inflorescences that are often adorned with fragrant, showy blooms. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on tree trunks or in rocky crevices.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
