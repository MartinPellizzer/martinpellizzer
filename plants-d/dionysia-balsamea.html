Dionysia balsamea

Dionysia balsamea, commonly known as the balsam root, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features a thick, aromatic root that is often harvested for its medicinal properties. The plant has dark green, lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces and produces yellow-orange flowers in late summer. Dionysia balsamea thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to arid environments and contribute to its ecological significance.
