Damnacanthus indicus var. indicus

Damnacanthus indicus var. indicus, commonly known as the Indian devil's backbone, is a flowering plant native to India and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, spiny calyx that resembles a spine-covered stem. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Its unique morphology and ornamental value make it a popular choice for horticultural collections.
