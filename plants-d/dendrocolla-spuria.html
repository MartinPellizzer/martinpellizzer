Dendrocolla spuria

Dendrocolla spuria, commonly known as the spurless tree fern, is a member of the Cyatheaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of feathery, compound leaves. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia. Its fronds are typically dimorphic, with sterile and fertile leaves differing in shape and structure.

Dendrocolla spuria is often found in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and swampy areas.
