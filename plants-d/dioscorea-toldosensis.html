Dioscorea toldosensis

Dioscorea toldosensis, commonly known as the Andean yam, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary edible part. The plant produces small, green flowers followed by yellow or orange berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.

Its botanical features make it an important species in traditional agriculture and local diets.
