Dianthus sylvestris var. subuniflorus

Dianthus sylvestris var. subuniflorus, commonly known as the wild carnation, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. The plant features upright, branching stems with narrow, linear leaves. Its flowers are pink to red, with five petals and a distinctive central boss of stamens.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and floral arrangements.
