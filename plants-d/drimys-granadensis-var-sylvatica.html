Drimys granadensis var. sylvatica

Drimys granadensis var. sylvatica, commonly known as the Andean drimys, is a species native to the temperate rainforests of South America.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are dark green on top and lighter on the underside. Its flowers are small and white, arranged in clusters, and it produces edible berries that are a food source for various wildlife.

The plant has a slow growth rate and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in humid conditions.
