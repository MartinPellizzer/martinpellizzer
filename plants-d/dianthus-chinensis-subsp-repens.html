Dianthus chinensis subsp. repens

Dianthus chinensis subsp.

repens, commonly known as the Persian violet, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features rosettes of dark green, velvety leaves and produces clusters of pink or purple flowers with five petals.

The flowers bloom in early spring and are often fragrant, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and hardiness in temperate climates.
