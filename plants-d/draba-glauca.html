Draba glauca

Draba glauca, commonly known as the blue draba, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cress plants. The plant is characterized by its small, delicate blue or white flowers and its low-growing, mat-forming habit. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in tundra and mountainous environments.

Draba glauca is adapted to cold climates and can survive in harsh, nutrient-poor conditions.
