Dendrobium kerstingianum

Dendrobium kerstingianum, commonly known as the Kersting's orchid, is a sympodial epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a pseudobulb system with long, leathery leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces pendulous racemes of fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white, yellow, and orange. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, loose potting media.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in tropical gardens.
