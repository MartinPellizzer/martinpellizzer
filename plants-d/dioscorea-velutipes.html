Dioscorea velutipes

Dioscorea velutipes, commonly known as the velvet tuber, is a member of the Dioscoreaceae family and is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is a climbing vine that produces edible tubers, which are often used in traditional cuisine. The plant has a fibrous root system and thrives in humid, well-drained soils. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its flowers are typically small and yellowish.

The tubers are valued not only for their nutritional content but also for their potential medicinal properties.
