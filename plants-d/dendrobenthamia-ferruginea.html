Dendrobenthamia ferruginea

Dendrobenthamia ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty dendrobium, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its epiphytic growth habit, often found on trees in humid tropical environments. The plant features long, slender stems covered with a dense layer of brownish-red hairs, giving it its common name. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, displaying a range of colors from yellow to orange.

Dendrobenthamia ferruginea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
