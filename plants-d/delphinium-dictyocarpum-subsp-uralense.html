Delphinium dictyocarpum subsp. uralense

Delphinium dictyocarpum subsp.

uralense, commonly known as the Ural delphinium, is a tall perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features striking blue to violet flowers arranged in dense spikes, which bloom in late summer.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture, and its stems are usually unbranched and somewhat woody at the base. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or alpine settings.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its vibrant floral display.
