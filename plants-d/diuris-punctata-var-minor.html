Diuris punctata var. minor

Diuris punctata var. minor, commonly known as the dwarf spider orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southeastern Australia.

It features a compact growth habit with a single or two hairy leaves and a slender, flowering stem. The flowers are typically pale green to yellowish with intricate patterns resembling spider webs, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a cool, moist environment during its growing season.

This variety is prized for its unique appearance and is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a container plant.
