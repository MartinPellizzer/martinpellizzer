Diheteropogon kindunduensis

Diheteropogon kindunduensis, commonly known as the Kindundu grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems. The plant typically grows in open woodland and grassland habitats, often in areas with well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and pointed, with a smooth surface, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

Diheteropogon kindunduensis is adapted to seasonal rainfall patterns and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native environment.
