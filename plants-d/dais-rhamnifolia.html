Dais rhamnifolia

Dais rhamnifolia, commonly known as the golden rhamnus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, with simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
