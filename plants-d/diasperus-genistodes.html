Diasperus genistodes

Diasperus genistodes, commonly known as the yellow gentian, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

The flowers bloom in late summer and are pollinated primarily by insects like bees and flies.
