Dypsis fanjana

Dypsis fanjana, commonly known as the Madagascar bamboo palm, is a striking plant native to the island of Madagascar.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family, which includes palms, and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and feathery, fan-shaped leaves. The plant exhibits a unique growth habit, with its stems forming a dense, bamboo-like structure that provides support for its foliage. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Dypsis fanjana is popular as a houseplant due to its elegant appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
