Dysoxylum sulphureum

Dysoxylum sulphureum, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it its common name. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. It produces woody capsules as fruit, which contain numerous seeds.

Dysoxylum sulphureum is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.
