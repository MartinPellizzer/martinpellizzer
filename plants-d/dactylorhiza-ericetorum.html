Dactylorhiza ericetorum

Dactylorhiza ericetorum, commonly known as the heather orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial herb with a rhizomatous root system and produces tall, slender stems bearing clustered flowers. The flowers are typically pink or purple, with a distinctive lip that is often darker in color. It prefers acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in heathland and woodland edges.

This species is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor environments, showcasing its adaptability to specific ecological niches.
