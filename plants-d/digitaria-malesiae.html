Digitaria malesiae

Digitaria malesiae, commonly known as the coastal panic grass, is a perennial grass species native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically green or reddish in color. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dune environments.

Digitaria malesiae is adapted to salt spray and can tolerate moderate levels of drought.
