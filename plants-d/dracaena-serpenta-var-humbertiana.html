Dracaena serpenta var. humbertiana

Dracaena serpenta var. humbertiana, commonly known as the "Serpent's Tail," is a striking tropical plant characterized by its long, narrow leaves that cascade downward in a spiral pattern, resembling a serpent's tail.

It originates from the Comoros Islands and thrives in warm, humid climates with bright, indirect light. The plant is known for its slow growth and ability to tolerate low light conditions, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening. Its foliage is typically a deep green, with some varieties displaying a reddish tinge at the tips.

Despite its delicate appearance, Dracaena serpenta var. humbertiana is relatively low maintenance, requiring moderate watering and well-draining soil.
