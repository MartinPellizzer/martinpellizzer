Davallia divaricata var. dimorpha

Davallia divaricata var. dimorpha, commonly known as the silver-veined huperzia, is a fern belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, leathery, evergreen fronds that are typically dark green with silver veins. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, forming dense clumps of foliage. Its fronds are bipinnate, with closely spaced, linear pinnae that give it a compact appearance.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and low maintenance requirements.
