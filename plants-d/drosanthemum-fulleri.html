Drosanthemum fulleri

Drosanthemum fulleri, commonly known as the Cape marigold, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features upright, branching stems covered in velvety, hairy foliage. Its bright yellow-orange flowers bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Drosanthemum fulleri is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
