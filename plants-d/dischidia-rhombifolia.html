Dischidia rhombifolia

Dischidia rhombifolia, commonly known as the shield vine, is a climbing or trailing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically borne in clusters. Its unique leaf structure, which resembles a shield, gives it its common name.

Dischidia rhombifolia is often grown as a houseplant due to its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and its ornamental appeal.
