Daphne euboica

Daphne euboica, commonly known as the Euboic daphne, is a deciduous shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, glandular leaves that are typically dark green and densely packed. The plant produces showy clusters of fragrant, waxy flowers in shades of pink, white, and yellow, which bloom in early spring. Its bark is smooth and grayish, often peeling in thin strips.

Daphne euboica is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its winter interest.
