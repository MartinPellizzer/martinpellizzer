Dianthus zonatus var. hypochlorus

Dianthus zonatus var. hypochlorus, commonly known as the pale-flowered campion, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pink to white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun exposure. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are slender and branching.

This variety is noted for its pale coloration, distinguishing it from other Dianthus species.
