Daucus lucidus

Daucus lucidus, commonly known as the shining parsley, is a member of the Apiaceae family.

It is a biennial herb that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces small, yellowish-white flowers in umbels. Its fruits are small, dry, and three-lobed, typical of the carrot family.
