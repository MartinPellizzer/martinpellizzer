Dianthus superbus var. latifolius

Dianthus superbus var. latifolius, commonly known as the broad-leaved campion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and clusters of pink or white flowers with five petals. The leaves are broad and ovate, often with a slightly hairy texture. This variety is closely related to other dianthus species and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for various climates.
