Diplospora stylosa

Diplospora stylosa, commonly known as the clustered false indigo, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and can reach heights of up to 3 meters. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several seeds, and it plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
