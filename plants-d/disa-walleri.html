Disa walleri

Disa walleri, commonly known as the red disa, is a striking member of the orchid family, Orchidaceae, native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant red or pink flowers, which are often adorned with contrasting white or yellow markings. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in open woodland or grassland habitats. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces a single spike of flowers that can last for several weeks.

Disa walleri is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
