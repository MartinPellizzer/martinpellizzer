Dictyanthus tuberosus

Dictyanthus tuberosus, commonly known as the tuberous bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has a tuberous root system, which allows it to store nutrients and survive in dry conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ease of care.
