Digitaria alleizettei

Digitaria alleizettei, commonly known as the African grass or African digitaria, is a species of grass native to Africa.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets.

Digitaria alleizettei is often used in ecological studies due to its adaptability and role in soil stabilization.
