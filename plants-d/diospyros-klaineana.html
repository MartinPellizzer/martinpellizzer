Diospyros klaineana

Diospyros klaineana, commonly known as the African persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other well-known species like the ebony tree. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, round, and typically yellow or orange berry that ripens in late summer.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
