Diospyros plicata

Diospyros plicata, commonly known as the African persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and broad, palmate leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by oval, yellow fruits with a sweet, edible pulp. Its fruits are often consumed fresh or used in traditional remedies.

Diospyros plicata is also valued for its hard wood, which is used in furniture and tool-making.
