Dorema hyrcanum

Dorema hyrcanum, commonly known as the Hyrcanian bellflower, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Boraginaceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, while its flowers are clustered in racemes.
