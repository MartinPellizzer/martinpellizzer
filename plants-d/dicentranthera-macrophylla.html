Dicentranthera macrophylla

Dicentranthera macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaf arrowroot, is a member of the Araceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length. Its flowering structure consists of a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe, typical of many arum plants.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of starchy roots.
