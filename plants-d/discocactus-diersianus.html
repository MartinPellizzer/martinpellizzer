Discocactus diersianus

Discocactus diersianus, commonly known as the "Cactus of the Andes," is a species native to the high-altitude regions of South America, particularly in Peru and Bolivia.

It is a spineless cactus that typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to cold, arid conditions. The plant features a short, rounded stem with clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. Its unique morphology and habitat make it a subject of interest for botanists and cactus enthusiasts.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
