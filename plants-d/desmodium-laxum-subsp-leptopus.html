Desmodium laxum subsp. leptopus

Desmodium laxum subsp.

leptopus, commonly known as slender desmodium, is a member of the legume family Fabaceae. It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically features trifoliate leaves with slender, hairy stipules.

The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are usually pink to purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or grassy areas.

Its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen makes it ecologically significant in soil enrichment.
