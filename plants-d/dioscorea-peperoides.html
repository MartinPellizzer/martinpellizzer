Dioscorea peperoides

Dioscorea peperoides, commonly known as the purple yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary edible part of the plant. The plant produces small, green, heart-shaped leaves and yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. Its tubers are rich in starch and have been used traditionally for food and medicinal purposes.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.
