Diospyros barteri

Diospyros barteri, commonly known as the African date palm, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its large, pinnate leaves and clustered, orange-red flowers. The tree produces edible fruits that are similar in appearance to dates, though they are typically smaller and more acidic. Its bark is thick and often fissured, and it is valued for its timber and fruit in local communities.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.
