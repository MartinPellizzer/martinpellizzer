Dracocephalum canescens

Dracocephalum canescens, commonly known as blueweed or goat's beard, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, erect stems covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers arranged in dense spikes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a similar silvery down, contributing to its ornamental value.

Dracocephalum canescens is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
