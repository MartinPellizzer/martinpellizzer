Dimorphanthes obtusa

Dimorphanthes obtusa, commonly known as the broad-leafed dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant features a basal rosette of broad, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its yellow flowers bloom in spring and are followed by fluffy seed heads that disperse via the wind.

Dimorphanthes obtusa is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils, thriving in full sun.
