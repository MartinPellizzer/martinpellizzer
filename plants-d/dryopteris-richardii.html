Dryopteris richardii

Dryopteris richardii, commonly known as the eastern wood fern, is a perennial fern native to North America.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky slopes. The plant has a distinctive frond structure with a fiddlehead curl at the base, which unfurls into a bipinnate leaf. Its reproductive cycle involves the production of sporangia on the undersides of its fronds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological habitats.
