Diplotaxis assurgens var. dissecta

Diplotaxis assurgens var. dissecta, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers with a central purple disc, which bloom in spring. The plant has a low-growing, mat-forming habit with linear, slightly hairy leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators.
