Diclis bambuseti

Diclis bambuseti, commonly known as the bamboo palm, is a member of the Arecaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small, evergreen tree that typically grows to a height of about 10 meters. The plant has slender, clustered stems that resemble bamboo, and its leaves are pinnately compound, giving it a lush, tropical appearance. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by round, dark blue fruits that are edible.

Diclis bambuseti is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
