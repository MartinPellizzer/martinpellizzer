Disa uniflora

Disa uniflora, commonly known as the single-flowered disa, is a striking member of the orchid family, Orchiidaceae, native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and a single, showy flower that typically blooms in late winter or early spring. The flower has a distinctive hood-like structure with a vibrant purple or violet coloration, often with contrasting white or yellow markings. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections worldwide.
