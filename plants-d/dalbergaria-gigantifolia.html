Dalbergaria gigantifolia

Dalbergaria gigantifolia, commonly known as the giant sicklepod, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, sickle-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in late summer. Its woody stems are typically branched and can grow up to several meters in height.

Dalbergaria gigantifolia is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.
