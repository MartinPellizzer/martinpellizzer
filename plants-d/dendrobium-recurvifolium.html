Dendrobium recurvifolium

Dendrobium recurvifolium, commonly known as the recurved leaf orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, slender rhizomes and its striking, recurved leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, yellowish-green flowers with prominent violet markings. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Dendrobium recurvifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
