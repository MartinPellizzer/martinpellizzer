Draba aurea var. leiocarpa

Draba aurea var. leiocarpa, commonly known as the yellow whitlow grass, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are basal, rosette-like, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and appear in early spring.

The variety leiocarpa is distinguished by its smooth, hairless fruits, setting it apart from other variants of Draba aurea.
