Disperis egregia

Disperis egregia, commonly known as the showy disperis, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, often violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in grasslands and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its inflorescence consists of a spike bearing several showy, spurred flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Disperis egregia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
