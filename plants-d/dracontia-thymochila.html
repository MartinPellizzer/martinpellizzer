Dracontia thymochila

Dracontia thymochila, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, delicate flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in high-altitude environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is known for its ability to survive in harsh, arid conditions.

Dracontia thymochila is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
