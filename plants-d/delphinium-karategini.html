Delphinium karategini

Delphinium karategini, commonly known as the Karategini larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, spindly stems and clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine or subalpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Delphinium karategini is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
