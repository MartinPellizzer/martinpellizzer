Drosera cistiflora var. exilis

Drosera cistiflora var. exilis, commonly known as the slender sundew, is a carnivorous plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its small, hairy leaves that secrete a sticky mucilage to trap insects. The plant produces tiny, white flowers arranged in a dense, spike-like inflorescence. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and requires high humidity to flourish.

Due to its unique appearance and carnivorous nature, it is a popular choice for enthusiasts of rare and exotic plants.
