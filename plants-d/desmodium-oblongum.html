Desmodium oblongum

Desmodium oblongum, commonly known as the ovalleaf burrweed, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pod-like fruits. The plant has a climbing habit and produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Desmodium oblongum is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial in soil enrichment.
