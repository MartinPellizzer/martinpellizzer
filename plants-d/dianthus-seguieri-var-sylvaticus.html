Dianthus seguieri var. sylvaticus

Dianthus seguieri var. sylvaticus, commonly known as the alpine campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant pink or red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine habitats.

Dianthus seguieri var. sylvaticus is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in cooler climates.
