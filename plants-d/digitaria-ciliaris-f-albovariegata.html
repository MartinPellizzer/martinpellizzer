Digitaria ciliaris f. albovariegata

Digitaria ciliaris f. albovariegata, commonly known as the variegated fingergrass, is a variant of the fingergrass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive variegated leaf blades, which display a mix of green and white or pale yellow hues. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It has a fibrous root system and produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

This ornamental grass is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in landscaping and garden settings.
