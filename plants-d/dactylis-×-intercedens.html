Dactylis × intercedens

Dactylis × intercedens, commonly known as the meadow grass or alsike clover, is a perennial grass that belongs to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in panicles, which are clustered in loose, branching formations. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils.

Dactylis × intercedens is valued for its forage quality and ability to stabilize soil in agricultural and ecological settings.
