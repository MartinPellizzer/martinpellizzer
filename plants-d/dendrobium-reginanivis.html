Dendrobium reginanivis

Dendrobium reginanivis, commonly known as the snow orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is an epiphytic plant, typically growing on trees or rocks in humid, tropical environments. The plant features a slender, branched stem covered with fleshy, cylindrical pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with a delicate, fragrant aroma, and bloom during the cooler months.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
