Dioscorea anthropophagorum var. sylvestris

Dioscorea anthropophagorum var. sylvestris, commonly known as the wild yam, is a member of the Dioscoreaceae family and is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a climbing herbaceous perennial that produces tubers as its primary storage organ. The plant features palmate leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically unisexual. Its tubers are used in traditional medicine and have been historically significant in the production of contraceptive hormones.

The species is characterized by its vigorous growth and adaptability to various soil types.
