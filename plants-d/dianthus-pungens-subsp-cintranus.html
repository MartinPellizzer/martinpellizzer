Dianthus pungens subsp. cintranus

Dianthus pungens subsp.

cintranus, commonly known as the red campion, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its vibrant red or pink flowers, which bloom in clusters atop slender stems.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. Dianthus pungens subsp.

cintranus is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
