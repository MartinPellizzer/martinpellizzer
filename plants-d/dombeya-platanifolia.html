Dombeya platanifolia

Dombeya platanifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf dombeya, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and lilac, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens in temperate climates.

Dombeya platanifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
