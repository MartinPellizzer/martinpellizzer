Dactylorhiza ruthei

Dactylorhiza ruthei, commonly known as the purple marsh orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in wet meadows, marshes, and along stream banks, thriving in moist, nutrient-poor soils. The plant features a rhizomatous root system and produces tall, slender stems with lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are violet to purple in color, arranged in a spike with a strong, sweet scent.

Dactylorhiza ruthei is a perennial species that blooms from late spring to early summer.
