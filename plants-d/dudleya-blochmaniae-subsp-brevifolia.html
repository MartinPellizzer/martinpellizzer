Dudleya blochmaniae subsp. brevifolia

Dudleya blochmaniae subsp.

brevifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf dudleya, is a succulent plant native to southern California. It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically gray-green in color, adapted for water storage.

The plant produces clusters of white or pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and requires full sun exposure.

Due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
