Daemonorops curtisii

Daemonorops curtisii, commonly known as the giant panda fern, is a species of cycad-like plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, feather-like leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces cones rather than flowers, typical of its gymnosperm classification. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its striking appearance and slow growth rate, it is a popular choice for botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
