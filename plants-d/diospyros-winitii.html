Diospyros winitii

Diospyros winitii, commonly known as Chinese date or small date tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other well-known species like ebony and persimmon. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth, glossy surface. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are followed by small, round fruits that resemble dates but are typically less sweet and more acidic.

The fruit is often used in traditional medicine and as a food source in certain regions.
