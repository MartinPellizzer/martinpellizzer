Desmotrichum luxurians

Desmotrichum luxurians, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on long, slender pedicels. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Due to its rapid growth and ability to form dense stands, it can become a weed in agricultural settings.
