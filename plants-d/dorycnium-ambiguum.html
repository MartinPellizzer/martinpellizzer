Dorycnium ambiguum

Dorycnium ambiguum, commonly known as the yellow bisonweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant features a stout, hairy stem that can reach up to 1 meter in height, with alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are often lobed. Its flowers are yellow and borne in clusters, and the plant produces small, woody fruits that contain several seeds.

Dorycnium ambiguum is known for its adaptability and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
