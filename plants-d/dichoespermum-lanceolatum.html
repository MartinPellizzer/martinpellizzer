Dichoespermum lanceolatum

Dichoespermum lanceolatum, commonly known as the African milk tree, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant features simple, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a capsule that contains a single, hard, smooth-seeded kernel.

The plant is notable for its latex, which has been used traditionally for various purposes.
