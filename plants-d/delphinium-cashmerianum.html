Delphinium cashmerianum

Delphinium cashmerianum, commonly known as the Kashmir bluebell, is a striking perennial plant native to the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that bear clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant features palmate leaves with a hairy texture, and its flowers have five sepals that are often more showy than the actual petals. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Delphinium cashmerianum is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
