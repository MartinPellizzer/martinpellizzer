Daphne odora

Daphne odora, commonly known as the winter daphne, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its evergreen leaves and clusters of fragrant, waxy flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are toxic to humans and animals. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Daphne odora is known for its strong, sweet scent, which can be both alluring and overpowering.
