Diuris insignis

Diuris insignis, commonly known as the pink-tongued orchid, is a species native to Australia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils in coastal regions. Its flowers feature a unique, elongated lip that resembles a tongue, hence its common name.

Diuris insignis is often found in heathlands and is adapted to thrive in well-drained, sun-exposed environments.
