Dysoxylum mollissimum

Dysoxylum mollissimum, commonly known as the silky sycamore, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silky hair. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by elongated, woody fruits containing seeds. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
