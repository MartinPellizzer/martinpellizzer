Drymaria engleriana

Drymaria engleriana, commonly known as the Engler's mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, rounded leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces clusters of pinkish-white flowers. Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens.
