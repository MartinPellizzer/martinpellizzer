Desmodium adscendens var. trifoliastrum

Desmodium adscendens var. trifoliastrum, commonly known as the creeping sicklepod, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged in a palmate pattern. The plant produces small, yellow pea-like flowers that are followed by narrow, curved pods containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Due to its rapid growth and ability to fix nitrogen, it can be both a weed and a potential forage crop.
