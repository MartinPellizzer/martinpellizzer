Diplospora ixoroides

Diplospora ixoroides, commonly known as the golden mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on various host trees in tropical and subtropical regions.

It has small, leathery leaves and produces yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels. The plant relies on its host for water and nutrients but can photosynthesize to some extent. Its berries are orange to red in color and contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.

This species plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for wildlife and contributing to the biodiversity of its habitat.
