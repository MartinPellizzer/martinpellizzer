Diplacrum caricinum var. sumatranum

Diplacrum caricinum var. sumatranum, commonly known as the Sumatran flat sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in wetland and swampy habitats, showcasing its adaptability to moist environments. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers during the wet season. It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit, which allows it to spread and form dense vegetation.

This species plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
