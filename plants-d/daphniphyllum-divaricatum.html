Daphniphyllum divaricatum

Daphniphyllum divaricatum, commonly known as the variegated silverleaf holly, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with striking white or silver veins, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in late spring, followed by red berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in cool, humid climates and prefers partial shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its unique foliage and ornamental value make it a sought-after plant in horticulture.
