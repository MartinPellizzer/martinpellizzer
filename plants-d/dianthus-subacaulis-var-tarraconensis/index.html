Dianthus Subacaulis Var. Tarraconensis

Rock campion, scientifically known as Dianthus subacaulis var. tarraconensis, is a striking flowering plant native to the rugged landscapes of southern Spain. This resilient species thrives in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its vibrant pink or red flowers bloom in clusters, adding a splash of color to the otherwise stark terrain. The plant is valued not only for its aesthetic appeal but also for its ecological role in supporting local pollinators. Due to its unique characteristics, Rock campion is a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists alike.
