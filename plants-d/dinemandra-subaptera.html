Dinemandra subaptera

Dinemandra subaptera, commonly known as the narrow-winged nightshade, is a flowering plant belonging to the Solanaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, egg-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white or pale purple flowers. The plant produces berries that are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature. It is native to parts of South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

Dinemandra subaptera is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.
