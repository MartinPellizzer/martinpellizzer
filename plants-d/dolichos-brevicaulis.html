Dolichos brevicaulis

Dolichos brevicaulis, commonly known as the short-stemmed cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and trifoliate leaves composed of three small, heart-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into elongated, cylindrical pods containing kidney-shaped seeds. It is often cultivated for its edible seeds, which are rich in protein and used in various cuisines.

Additionally, Dolichos brevicaulis has nitrogen-fixing abilities, making it beneficial for soil fertility in agricultural systems.
