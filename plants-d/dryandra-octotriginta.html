Dryandra octotriginta

Dryandra octotriginta, commonly known as the Western Australian dryandra, is a member of the Proteaceae family and is native to the southwestern region of Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring narrow, leathery leaves that are often gray-green in color. The plant produces striking, urn-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, appearing in late winter to early spring.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique floral structure and its role in the ecological diversity of the region.
