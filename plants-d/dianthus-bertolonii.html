Dianthus bertolonii

Dianthus bertolonii, commonly known as the Bertoloni's campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its pink or white flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in sunny environments. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, and it produces small, delicate flowers in early spring.

Dianthus bertolonii is often found in dry, open habitats and is considered a perennial species.
