Diodia procumbens

Diodia procumbens, commonly known as groundcherry or false nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant features sprawling stems that grow close to the ground, often producing small, yellowish flowers. Its fruit, which resembles a cherry, is typically green when immature and turns red when ripe.

The plant contains alkaloids and is sometimes used in traditional medicine, though it can be toxic in large quantities.
