Duranta plumieri var. glabra

Duranta plumieri var. glabra, commonly known as the white duranta, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous leaves and clusters of small, white or yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces blue or purple berries that are attractive to birds and other wildlife. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in ornamental gardening due to its showy display.

The species is relatively drought-tolerant once established, making it suitable for a variety of garden settings.
