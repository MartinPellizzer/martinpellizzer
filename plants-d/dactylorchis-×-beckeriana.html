Dactylorchis × beckeriana

Dactylorchis × beckeriana, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to regions in Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its sympodial growth habit and pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often in clusters at the tips of its stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its aesthetic appeal.
