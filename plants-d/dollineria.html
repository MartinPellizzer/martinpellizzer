Dollineria

Dollineria, commonly known as the false dandelion, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

It consists of several species, most of which are native to South America. The plants typically have basal leaves that are oval or lance-shaped and are often covered in fine hairs. The flowers are yellow and arranged in composite inflorescences, typical of the Asteraceae family.

Dollineria species are often found in grasslands and open areas, where they thrive in well-drained soils.
