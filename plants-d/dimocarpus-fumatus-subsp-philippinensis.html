Dimocarpus fumatus subsp. philippinensis

Dimocarpus fumatus subsp.

philippinensis, commonly known as the Philippine wax apple, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to the Philippines. It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green on top.

The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by orange to red berries that contain a single, hard, seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests and secondary growth areas.

The fruit is edible but has a sour taste, and the plant is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
