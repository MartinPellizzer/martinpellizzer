Dianthus ugamicus

Dianthus ugamicus, commonly known as the Mongolian campion, is a flowering plant native to Mongolia and adjacent regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

Dianthus ugamicus is often found in open habitats such as steppes and mountainous areas.
