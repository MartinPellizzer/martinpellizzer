Dinebra arabica

Dinebra arabica, commonly known as the Arabica coffee plant, is a species native to tropical Africa.

It is an evergreen shrub that produces red, fleshy berries containing the seeds used for coffee. The plant thrives in shaded, humid environments with well-drained soil. Its leaves are dark green and oval-shaped, while its flowers are white and fragrant.

The plant's botanical characteristics make it well-suited for cultivation in high-altitude regions.
