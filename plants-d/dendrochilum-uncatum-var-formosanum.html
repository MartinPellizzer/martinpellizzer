Dendrochilum uncatum var. formosanum

Dendrochilum uncatum var. formosanum, commonly known as the Formosan spider orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, spider-like flowers with intricate patterns. The plant typically blooms during the summer months, producing fragrant, pale yellow to greenish-white blooms. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in forest understories.

The variety is noted for its ornamental value and is cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance.
