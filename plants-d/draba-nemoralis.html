Draba nemoralis

Draba nemoralis, commonly known as the wood's mouse-ear, is a small, herbaceous plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is typically found in moist, shaded habitats such as woodland floors and rocky crevices. The plant has basal leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape, often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in loose racemes, blooming in early spring.

Draba nemoralis is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils and is often an indicator of undisturbed forest ecosystems.
