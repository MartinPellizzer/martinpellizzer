Dianthus cretaceus var. integerrimus

Dianthus cretaceus var. integerrimus, commonly known as the rock campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and features fleshy, gray-green leaves. The plant produces clusters of pink to reddish-purple flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
