Disperis orientalis

Disperis orientalis, commonly known as the Chinese bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to China.

It features striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in late summer, typically in July and August. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Disperis orientalis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens.
