Dalea annua var. cliffortiana

Dalea annua var. cliffortiana, commonly known as the cliffortian poppy, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often appearing in pairs along the stems.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
