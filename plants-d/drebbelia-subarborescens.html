Drebbelia subarborescens

Drebbelia subarborescens, commonly known as the tree-like drebbelia, is a rare and unique plant species native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its woody stem and dense, evergreen foliage, which gives it a shrub-like appearance despite its name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in the spring. Its leaves are elliptical, leathery, and have a waxy texture, which helps it retain moisture in its arid habitat.

Drebbelia subarborescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its distinctive appearance.
