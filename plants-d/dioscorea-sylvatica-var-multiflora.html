Dioscorea sylvatica var. multiflora

Dioscorea sylvatica var. multiflora, commonly known as the woodland yam, is a perennial vine native to the temperate regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by tuberous roots that are often used in traditional medicine. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a complex network of rhizomes that contribute to its survival in forest understories.

The species is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its potential medicinal properties.
