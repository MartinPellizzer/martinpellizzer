Dioscorea adenocarpa

Dioscorea adenocarpa, commonly known as the African yambean, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellowish flowers and develops tuberous roots that are sometimes used in traditional medicine. Its botanical features make it distinct from other yam species, with a unique morphology and growth habit.

The plant is also valued for its potential nutritional and medicinal properties.
