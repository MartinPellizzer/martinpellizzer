Diospyros viguieriana

Diospyros viguieriana, commonly known as the Viguier's persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Vietnam and Cambodia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other well-known species such as ebony and persimmon. The plant is characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into orange to red, edible berries.

Diospyros viguieriana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
