Diospyros toposia

Diospyros toposia, commonly known as the mountain persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other persimmon species and ebony trees. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and small, clustered flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit is a fleshy, orange-red berry that ripens in autumn and is edible when fully mature.

The tree is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions.
