Digitaria gardneri

Digitaria gardneri, commonly known as Florida Panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies, thriving in moist to wet soil conditions. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds in panicles. It is often found in disturbed or flooded areas and is adapted to tolerate periodic flooding.

Botanically, it is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and is part of the Poaceae family.
