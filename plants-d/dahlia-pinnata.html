Dahlia pinnata

Dahlia pinnata, commonly known as the tree dahlia, is a tropical perennial plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its woody stem that can grow up to 10 meters tall, resembling a tree. The plant produces large, showy flowers in a wide range of colors, including red, yellow, orange, and white. Its foliage consists of palmate leaves that are deeply divided and have a feathery appearance.

Dahlia pinnata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
