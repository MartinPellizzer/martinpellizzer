Didymoglossum barklyanum

Didymoglossum barklyanum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bog moss, is a small, mat-forming moss that thrives in wet, acidic environments such as bogs and fens.

It has delicate, narrow leaves that are typically less than 1 centimeter in length, arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem. The plant produces small, greenish-brown spore capsules that are borne on short stalks, contributing to its reproductive cycle. It is characterized by its ability to tolerate low light and nutrient-poor conditions, making it a common indicator species in peatland ecosystems.

Its botanical structure reflects adaptations to its moist, temperate habitat, with features that enhance its survival in such environments.
