Diplachne dubia

Diplachne dubia, commonly known as the slender flat sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It typically grows in wet or marshy environments, often found in swampy areas or along the edges of water bodies. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Diplachne dubia is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread vegetatively and form dense mats.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil in wetland habitats and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
