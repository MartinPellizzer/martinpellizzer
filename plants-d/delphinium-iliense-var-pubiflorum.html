Delphinium iliense var. pubiflorum

Delphinium iliense var. pubiflorum, commonly known as the Kashmir blue poppy, is a striking perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear clusters of large, showy blue to violet flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are hairy, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in cool, moist environments and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
