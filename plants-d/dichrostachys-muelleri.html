Dichrostachys muelleri

Dichrostachys muelleri, commonly known as the mueller's false pigeonwood, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive yellow and red flowers. The plant is often found in dry, open woodlands and is adapted to arid environments. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its seeds are encased in a hard pod, which opens to release the seeds when mature.
