Daviesia latifolia

Daviesia latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved Daviesia, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, fern-like foliage. The plant produces small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in spring, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils in open woodlands and heathlands.

Daviesia latifolia is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
