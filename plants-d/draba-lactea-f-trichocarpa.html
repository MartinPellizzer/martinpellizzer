Draba lactea f. trichocarpa

Draba lactea f. trichocarpa, commonly known as the hairy whitlow grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and features small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant is native to arctic and alpine regions of Eurasia, where it thrives in rocky and barren soils. Its leaves are typically covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This ecotype is adapted to cold climates and is often found in high-altitude environments.
