Dodonaea viscosa var. arizonica

Dodonaea viscosa var. arizonica, commonly known as Arizona screwbean, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and pairs of bean-like pods, which are a distinctive botanical feature. The plant has small, oval leaves with a rough texture and is adapted to arid environments. It is often found in rocky soils and can thrive in full sun.

Its unique morphology and drought tolerance make it a notable species in xerophytic plant communities.
