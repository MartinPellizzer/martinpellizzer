Diasperus symplocoides

Diasperus symplocoides, commonly known as the African snowball, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a hairy texture.

The plant is adapted to arid conditions and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
