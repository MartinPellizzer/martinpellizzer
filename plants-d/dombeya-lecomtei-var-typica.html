Dombeya lecomtei var. typica

Dombeya lecomtei var. typica, commonly known as the Lecomte dombeya, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically appearing in clusters. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are often hairy, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
