Dioscorea convolvulacea subsp. grandifolia

Dioscorea convolvulacea subsp.

grandifolia, commonly known as the large-flowered yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa. It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant produces showy, white or pale yellow flowers that are typically borne in clusters. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are hairy and twining.

The subspecies is distinguished by its larger floral structures compared to other members of the species.
