Dianthera sulfurea

Dianthera sulfurea, commonly known as the sulfur flower, is a striking plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It is characterized by its vivid yellow-orange inflorescence, which resembles a cluster of sulfur-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or alpine habitats. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects attracted to its bright color and nectar.

Dianthera sulfurea is noted for its resilience to harsh environmental conditions, making it a unique and visually captivating botanical specimen.
