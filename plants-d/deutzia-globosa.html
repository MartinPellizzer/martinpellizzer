Deutzia globosa

Deutzia globosa, commonly known as the globeflower, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and has slender, arching branches. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a smooth or slightly serrated margin.

Deutzia globosa thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, loamy soil.
