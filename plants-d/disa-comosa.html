Disa comosa

Disa comosa, commonly known as the woolly disa, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces clusters of pale purple to white flowers.

Disa comosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable growing conditions.
