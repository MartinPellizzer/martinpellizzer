Dianthera sylvatica

Dianthera sylvatica, commonly known as the woodland bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Europe.

It features basal rosettes of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or purple. The plant typically blooms from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, shaded woodland habitats and prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

Dianthera sylvatica is often found in forest understories and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
