Durabaculum hamiferum

Durabaculum hamiferum, commonly known as the rock lily, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its robust, fibrous root system and grass-like foliage. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh, arid environments.

Due to its resilience and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
