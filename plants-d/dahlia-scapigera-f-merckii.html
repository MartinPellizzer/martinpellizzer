Dahlia scapigera f. merckii

Dahlia scapigera f. merckii, commonly known as the Merck's dahlia, is a hybrid form of the dahlia genus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, single or semi-coriaceous flowers that come in a variety of colors. The foliage is typically lance-shaped and covered in a fine, hairy texture. This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
