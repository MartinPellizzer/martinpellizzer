Dianthus filiformis

Dianthus filiformis, commonly known as the slender carnation, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features delicate, clustered flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, which bloom from spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Dianthus filiformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation in temperate climates.
