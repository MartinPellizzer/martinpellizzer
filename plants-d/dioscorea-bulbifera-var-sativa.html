Dioscorea bulbifera var. sativa

Dioscorea bulbifera var. sativa, commonly known as the sweet potato vine, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary edible part, though the aerial tubers, or bulbils, are also consumed in some cultures. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves and clusters of yellowish-green flowers, followed by small, inedible fruits. Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a popular ornamental plant, though it can become invasive in certain environments.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
