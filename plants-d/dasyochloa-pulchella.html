Dasyochloa pulchella

Dasyochloa pulchella, commonly known as the showy muhly, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like clusters of pinkish to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, such as sandy soils and rocky areas. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a bluish-gray hue that helps reduce water loss.

Dasyochloa pulchella is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soils in drought-prone regions.
