Dewevrella congensis

Dewevrella congensis, commonly known as the Congo dewevrella, is a rare and unique plant native to the rainforests of Central Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, waxy leaves that reflect sunlight, helping it to retain moisture in its humid environment. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are pollinated by specific insects, contributing to its ecological niche. Its roots are known to secrete a substance that aids in nutrient absorption from the nutrient-poor soils of its habitat.

Due to habitat destruction, it is currently listed as endangered, making conservation efforts crucial for its survival.
