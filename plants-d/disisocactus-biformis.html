Disisocactus biformis

Disisocactus biformis, commonly known as the two-form cactus, is a unique species native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive branching pattern. The plant features short, cylindrical stems that often split into two branches, giving it its name. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, appearing in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Disisocactus biformis is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water and well-draining soil.
