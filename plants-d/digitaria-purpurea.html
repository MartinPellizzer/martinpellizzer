Digitaria purpurea

Digitaria purpurea, commonly known as purple digitaria, is a perennial grass species native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with purple at the base. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically green or reddish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Due to its rapid growth and adaptability, it can become invasive in certain environments.
