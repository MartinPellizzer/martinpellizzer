Dypsis

Dypsis, commonly known as the areca palm, is a genus of flowering plants in the Arecaceae family.

These plants are native to Madagascar and parts of Southeast Asia, characterized by their tall, slender trunks and pinnate leaves. Dypsis species typically grow in tropical and subtropical climates, thriving in well-drained soil and bright, indirect light. They are often cultivated as ornamental plants due to their elegant appearance and air-purifying properties.

The genus includes several species, such as Dypsis lutea and Dypsis decaryi, which are popular in horticulture.
