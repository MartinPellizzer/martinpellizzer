Draba norica

Draba norica, commonly known as the Alpine bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white or pale purple. Draba norica thrives in rocky and well-drained soils at high altitudes.

Its short stature and ability to withstand harsh mountain conditions make it a notable component of alpine flora.
