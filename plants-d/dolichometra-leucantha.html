Dolichometra leucantha

Dolichometra leucantha, commonly known as the white-veined orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its elegant, fragrant flowers with white petals and yellow veins. The plant typically grows in tropical rainforests, thriving in humid and shaded environments. Its roots are fibrous, and it has a sympodial growth habit, forming pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients.

The flowers bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
