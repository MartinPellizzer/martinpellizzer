Dinebra retroflexa var. condensata

Dinebra retroflexa var. condensata, commonly known as the condensed heartleaf vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are typically smaller and more compact than those of the typical species. The plant produces small, white flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences, followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in warm climates.
