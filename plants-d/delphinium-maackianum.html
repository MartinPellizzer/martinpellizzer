Delphinium maackianum

Delphinium maackianum, commonly known as the Maack's larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant produces clusters of blue to purple, spurred flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, with lance-shaped leaflets arranged alternately along the stem.

Delphinium maackianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
