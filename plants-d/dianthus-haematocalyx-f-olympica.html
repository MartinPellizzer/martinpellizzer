Dianthus haematocalyx f. olympica

Dianthus haematocalyx f. olympica, commonly known as the Olympic campion, is a cultivar of the common campion plant.

It is characterized by its striking red-pink flowers that bloom in summer, adding vibrant color to gardens. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, with lance-shaped leaves that are slightly hairy. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy and low-maintenance choice for gardeners.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
