Danthonia strigosa

Danthonia strigosa, commonly known as silky bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and prairies.

Danthonia strigosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
