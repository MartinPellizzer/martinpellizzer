Drymaria divaricata var. stricta

Drymaria divaricata var. stricta, commonly known as the strict or upright form of the common sandspurry, is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial herb native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It features small, fleshy, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces clusters of yellowish-white flowers with five petals. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is well adapted to arid or semi-arid conditions.

This species is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
