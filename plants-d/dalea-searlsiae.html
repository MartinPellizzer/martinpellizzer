Dalea searlsiae

Dalea searlsiae, commonly known as Searls' milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitat.

Dalea searlsiae is often found in desert scrub and chaparral ecosystems.
