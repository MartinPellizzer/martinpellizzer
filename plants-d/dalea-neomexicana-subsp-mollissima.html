Dalea neomexicana subsp. mollissima

Dalea neomexicana subsp.

mollissima, commonly known as the silky milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its delicate, pink to purple flowers and trifoliate leaves.

The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, such as grasslands and rocky soils. It has a low, sprawling growth habit and produces small, pod-like fruits.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
