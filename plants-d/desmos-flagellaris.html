Desmos flagellaris

Desmos flagellaris, commonly known as the flagellated desmo, is a unique plant species characterized by its specialized flagellated structures on its reproductive organs.

These flagella, which resemble tiny tails, aid in the dispersal of pollen by creating water currents that carry the microscopic spores. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its botanical structure includes a short, stout stem with broad, lance-shaped leaves.

The reproductive system is highly adapted for aquatic or semi-aquatic pollination, making it a fascinating subject in plant physiology studies.
