Dipterocarpus pilosus

Dipterocarpus pilosus, commonly known as the hairy dipterocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves with a hairy underside. The plant produces heavy, woody fruits that contain one or two seeds, which are dispersed by animals. It thrives in humid, lowland forests and plays a significant role in the ecological structure of these ecosystems.

The tree is valued for its hard, durable wood, which has been used historically in construction and furniture.
