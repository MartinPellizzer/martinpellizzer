Dendropanax pendulus

Dendropanax pendulus, commonly known as the hanging palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, drooping leaves that give it its common name. The plant features a slender, branching stem with long, narrow, leathery leaves that are often arranged in a spiral pattern. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, typically appearing in clusters, and it produces small, orange-red berries.

Dendropanax pendulus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to grow in humid, shaded environments.
