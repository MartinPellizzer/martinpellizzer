Draba yunnanensis var. latifolia

Draba yunnanensis var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf mouse-ear cress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Yunnan Province in China.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many economically important plants such as mustard and cabbage. The plant is characterized by its low-growing habit, with basal leaves that are broad and fleshy, and small white or yellow flowers arranged in clusters. It thrives in alpine and subalpine environments, often found in rocky soils and meadows at high elevations.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
