Dianthus superbus subsp. longicalycinus

Dianthus superbus subsp.

longicalycinus, commonly known as the long-stemmed campion, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of showy, pink or reddish-pink flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ornamental addition to gardens and natural landscapes.
