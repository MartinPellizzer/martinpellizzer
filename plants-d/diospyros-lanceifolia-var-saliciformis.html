Diospyros lanceifolia var. saliciformis

Diospyros lanceifolia var. saliciformis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved persimmon, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple on the underside. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by orange to red berries that are edible when fully ripe. Its botanical features include smooth, greyish bark and a tendency to form dense thickets.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in traditional medicine.
