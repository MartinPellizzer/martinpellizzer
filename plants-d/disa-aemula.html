Disa aemula

Disa aemula, commonly known as the flame orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant red and orange flowers, which are often adorned with contrasting white markings. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers are short-lived but highly attractive to pollinators such as bees and birds.

Disa aemula is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
