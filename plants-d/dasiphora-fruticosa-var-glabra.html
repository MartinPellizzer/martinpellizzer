Dasiphora fruticosa var. glabra

Dasiphora fruticosa var. glabra, commonly known as smooth fireweed, is a perennial shrub native to the northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and showy pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has serrated, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open habitats such as roadsides and meadows.

Its botanical features make it a hardy and adaptable species within its native range.
