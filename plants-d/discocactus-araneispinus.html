Discocactus araneispinus

Discocactus araneispinus, commonly known as the spider-hair discocactus, is a unique cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in dense, hair-like spines that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a splash of color to its otherwise spiky form. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xerophytic gardens.

Its slow growth rate and striking appearance make it a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
