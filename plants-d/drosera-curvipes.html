Drosera curvipes

Drosera curvipes, commonly known as the curved-sword sundew, is a carnivorous plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its elongated, curved leaves that are covered in sticky, glandular hairs used to trap insects. The plant typically grows in wet, acidic environments such as bogs and marshes. Its small white flowers appear in late spring or early summer.

Drosera curvipes is a perennial species that thrives in humid conditions and requires consistent moisture for optimal growth.
