Dinosperma erythrococca

Dinosperma erythrococca, commonly known as the red-cocca or red sand.

It is a succulent plant native to arid regions of Africa and the Middle East, characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and small, spiny leaves. The plant produces vibrant red flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is well adapted to survive in hot, dry environments with minimal water.

Its unique appearance and resilience make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
