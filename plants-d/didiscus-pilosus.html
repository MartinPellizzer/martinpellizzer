Didiscus pilosus

Didiscus pilosus, commonly known as the woolly didiscus, is a striking member of the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that are covered in dense, soft, white hairs, giving it a velvety appearance. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in clusters, adding visual interest to gardens. Native to South Africa, it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Its unique foliage and vibrant blooms make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
