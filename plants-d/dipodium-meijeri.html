Dipodium meijeri

Dipodium meijeri, commonly known as the Meijer's dipodium, is a rare and exotic plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, spiraling growth pattern and its ability to produce striking, orchid-like flowers. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette formation, while its flowers are borne on slender, wiry stems.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is highly sought after by collectors and botanists alike.
