Dianthus chinensis var. laciniatus

Dianthus chinensis var. laciniatus, commonly known as the Chinese carnation or fringed carnation, is a flowering plant native to China.

It is characterized by its compact, mounded growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that come in a range of colors including red, pink, and white. The leaves are narrow and slightly serrated, contributing to its ornamental appeal. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in spring and early summer, often with a fragrant aroma.

It is widely cultivated in gardens for its beauty and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil.
