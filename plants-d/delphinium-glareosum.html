Delphinium glareosum

Delphinium glareosum, commonly known as the golden delphinium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers in dense spikes, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several pairs of lance-shaped leaflets.

Delphinium glareosum thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.
