Duvaua praecox f. montana

Duvaua praecox f. montana, commonly known as the alpine bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving at high altitudes with cool, moist conditions. The plant features a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers. Its flowering period occurs in early summer, and it is characterized by its compact growth habit and frost-resistant nature.

Duvaua praecox f. montana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in alpine ecosystems.
