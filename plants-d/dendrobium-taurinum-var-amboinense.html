Dendrobium taurinum var. amboinense

Dendrobium taurinum var. amboinense, commonly known as the Amboina orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant produces raceme-like inflorescences with fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white, yellow, and pink. Its leaves are elongated and often have a waxy coating, aiding in water retention.

This variety is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
