Dendrocolla gracilenta

Dendrocolla gracilenta, commonly known as the slender stemless orchid, is a unique member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, often unbranched stem and small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white or pale yellow. Native to specific regions in South America, this plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on trees or rocks. Its botanical structure includes a fibrous root system and a single, leafless stem that supports its floral display.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, Dendrocolla gracilenta is a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists alike.
