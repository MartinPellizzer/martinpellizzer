Dactylis paspaloides

Dactylis paspaloides, commonly known as silky grass or silky brome, is a perennial grass native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered with fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands, pastures, and disturbed areas.

Dactylis paspaloides is valued for its adaptability and is used in forage and erosion control.
