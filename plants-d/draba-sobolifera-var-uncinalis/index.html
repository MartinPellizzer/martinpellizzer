Draba Sobolifera Var. Uncinalis

Common rockcress, scientifically known as Draba sobolifera var. uncinalis, is a hardy perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America. It thrives in rocky soils and harsh alpine environments, showcasing its resilience to extreme conditions. This plant is characterized by its low-growing stature and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Despite its delicate appearance, it plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife. Its unique adaptations make it a fascinating subject for botanists and nature enthusiasts alike.
