Diospyros styraciformis

Diospyros styraciformis, commonly known as the African persimmon, is a small to medium-sized deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical to ovate in shape. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by edible, orange to reddish fruits with a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor. Its wood is valued for its hardness and durability, making it useful in traditional craftsmanship and construction.

The plant also has cultural and medicinal significance in various African communities.
