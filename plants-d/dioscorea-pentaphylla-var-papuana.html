Dioscorea pentaphylla var. papuana

Dioscorea pentaphylla var. papuana, commonly known as the Papuan yam, is a species of tuberous vine native to the tropical regions of Papua and surrounding areas.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and large, fleshy tubers. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its tubers are valued for their starchy content and have been used in traditional cuisines.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.
