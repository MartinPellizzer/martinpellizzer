Duvernoia anisochlamydata

Duvernoia anisochlamydata, commonly known as the Chinese yew, is a slow-growing evergreen tree native to China.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery foliage and conical shape. The plant produces small, reddish-brown cones that mature over several years. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for ornamental landscaping.

Due to its longevity and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and parks.
