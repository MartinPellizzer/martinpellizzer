Dichanthium saccharoides

Dichanthium saccharoides, commonly known as sugarcane grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Due to its hardiness, it is sometimes used in erosion control and as a forage crop.
