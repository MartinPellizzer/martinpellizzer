Danthonia penicillata var. racemosa

Danthonia penicillata var. racemosa, commonly known as the raceme grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Australia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often form dense clumps. The plant produces inflorescences that are spike-like and bear small, spikelet-based flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue that becomes more prominent during the growing season.

This variety is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and sandy soils.
