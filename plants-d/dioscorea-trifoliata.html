Dioscorea trifoliata

Dioscorea trifoliata, commonly known as the arrowhead vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, Africa, and the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are typically trifoliate, meaning they have three leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into tuberous roots, which are the primary part used in traditional medicine. These tubers are also edible when properly prepared and are sometimes used as a food source.

Dioscorea trifoliata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a long history of use in herbal remedies.
