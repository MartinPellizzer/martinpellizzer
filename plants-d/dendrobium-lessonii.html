Dendrobium lessonii

Dendrobium lessonii, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange, often in clusters. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, adapted to its climbing lifestyle.

Dendrobium lessonii thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.
