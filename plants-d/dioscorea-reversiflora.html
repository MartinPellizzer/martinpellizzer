Dioscorea reversiflora

Dioscorea reversiflora, commonly known as the reverse-flowered yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically borne in clusters along the stems. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a rough texture, and the vine can grow quite long, often reaching several meters in length.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
