Dysphania multiflora

Dysphania multiflora, commonly known as New Zealand spinach, is an annual herb native to New Zealand.

It features heart-shaped, slightly hairy leaves that are tender and edible, often used in salads or cooked dishes. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in cool climates and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners in temperate regions.

Its botanical name reflects its multifloral nature, with numerous small flowers along the stems.
