Dermatophyllum gypsophilum subsp. gypsophilum

Dermatophyllum gypsophilum subsp.

gypsophilum, commonly known as the rock cress, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is a low-growing perennial with small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant has a succulent, fleshy appearance, adapted to store water in its leaves and stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

The subspecies is notable for its resilience to drought and its role in stabilizing soil in harsh environments.
