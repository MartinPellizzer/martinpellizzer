Darwinia fascicularis subsp. fascicularis

Darwinia fascicularis subsp.

fascicularis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved Darwinia, is a perennial shrub native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its erect growth habit and clusters of tubular, pink to red flowers that appear in late summer to autumn.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

This species is valued for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in native plant gardens.
