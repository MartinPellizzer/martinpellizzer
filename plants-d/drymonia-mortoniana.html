Drymonia mortoniana

Drymonia mortoniana, commonly known as the Morton's drymonia, is a rare and endangered plant species native to the tropical rainforests of Queensland, Australia.

It is a small, herbaceous plant characterized by its delicate, clustered leaves and inconspicuous flowers. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found growing in the understorey of dense rainforest canopies. Its botanical significance lies in its unique adaptations to its specific ecological niche and its role in the biodiversity of its habitat.

Conservation efforts are crucial for its survival due to habitat loss and limited distribution.
