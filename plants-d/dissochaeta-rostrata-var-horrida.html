Dissochaeta rostrata var. horrida

Dissochaeta rostrata var. horrida, commonly known as the rough-haired dissochaeta, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which contribute to its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for studies on plant adaptation and biodiversity.
