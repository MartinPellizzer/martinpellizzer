Dichorisandra leucosepala

Dichorisandra leucosepala, commonly known as the white-flowered dichorisandra, is a member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is a shrub native to South Africa, characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and showy, white to pale blue flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens in warm climates.

Its ornamental value comes from its striking floral display and compact growth habit.
