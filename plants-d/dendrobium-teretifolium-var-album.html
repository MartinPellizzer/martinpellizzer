Dendrobium teretifolium var. album

Dendrobium teretifolium var. album, commonly known as the white-stemmed orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is an epiphytic orchid that grows on trees and rocks, relying on atmospheric moisture for nourishment. The plant features elongated, lance-shaped leaves and produces striking white or pale yellow flowers. It thrives in warm, humid climates with bright indirect light.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
