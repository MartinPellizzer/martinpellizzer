Diplazium porphyrophyllum

Diplazium porphyrophyllum, commonly known as the purple fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its dark green, leathery fronds with a distinctive purple hue on the underside. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The fronds are pinnately compound, with lance-shaped pinnae that contribute to its ornamental value.
