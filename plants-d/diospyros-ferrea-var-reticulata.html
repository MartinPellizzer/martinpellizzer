Diospyros ferrea var. reticulata

Diospyros ferrea var. reticulata, commonly known as the Brazilian persimmon, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and smooth, reddish bark that becomes fissured with age. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by orange to red, fleshy fruits that are edible when fully ripe. Its leaves are alternate, elliptical, and have a slightly hairy surface.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also cultivated for its nutritious fruit.
