Davallodes congesta

Davallodes congesta, commonly known as the clustered davallia, is a species of fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Davalliaceae and is characterized by its dense, clustered growth habit. The plant features delicate, pinnately divided fronds with a bluish-green hue, often adorned with small, spore-producing structures called sori. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

The botanical structure of Davallodes congesta makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and conservation efforts.
