Damrongia fulva

Damrongia fulva, commonly known as the golden flame orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow and orange flowers, which bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has strap-like, dark green leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in humid environments with bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil.

Damrongia fulva is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
