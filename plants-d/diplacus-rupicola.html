Diplacus rupicola

Diplacus rupicola, commonly known as the rock pink, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant pink or red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly habitats, often in areas with well-drained soil. It has small, lance-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds.
