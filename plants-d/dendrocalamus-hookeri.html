Dendrocalamus hookeri

Dendrocalamus hookeri, commonly known as the bamboo giant, is a tall, fast-growing grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its strong, hollow stems that can reach heights of up to 30 meters. The plant's culms are typically straight and have a smooth, green surface with a fibrous texture. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in dense stands in moist, well-drained soils.

Dendrocalamus hookeri is valued for its strong, durable material, which is used in construction and as a source of bamboo shoots.
