Draba alpina var. aspera

Draba alpina var. aspera, commonly known as the rough alpine bellflower, is a flowering plant native to alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family and typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers with four petals, and its leaves are usually hairy and serrated. It is adapted to cold climates and can survive in harsh, nutrient-poor environments.

The variety aspera is distinguished by its rough, textured leaves and stems.
