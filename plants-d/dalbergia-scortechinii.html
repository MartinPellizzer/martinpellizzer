Dalbergia scortechinii

Dalbergia scortechinii, commonly known as African bloodwood, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and dark, dense wood. The plant has bipinnate leaves and produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are followed by flat, bean-like pods. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils and savanna habitats.

The tree is also valued for its hard, durable timber and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
