Duvalia sulcata subsp. sulcata

Duvalia sulcata subsp.

sulcata, commonly known as the African violet, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves and clusters of showy, violet-colored flowers.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth. It is a popular ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and relatively easy care requirements.

The subspecies sulcata is distinguished by its distinctive leaf patterns and compact growth habit.
