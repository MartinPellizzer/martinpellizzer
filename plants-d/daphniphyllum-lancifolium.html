Daphniphyllum lancifolium

Daphniphyllum lancifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved holly, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features elliptical, glossy dark green leaves that are often tinged with red or purple on the underside. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters, which are followed by bright red berries. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in horticulture.
