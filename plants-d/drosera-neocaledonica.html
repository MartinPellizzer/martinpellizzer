Drosera neocaledonica

Drosera neocaledonica, commonly known as the New Caledonian Sundew, is a carnivorous plant native to the island of New Caledonia.

It is characterized by its rosette of sticky, glandular leaves that trap and digest insects. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in summer, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and requires high humidity to flourish.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is a popular subject in botanical studies and conservation efforts.
