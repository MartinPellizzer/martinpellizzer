Diospyros linderi

Diospyros linderi, commonly known as the Linder's persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other well-known species like the ebony and persimmon trees. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate or elliptical in shape. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by small, round fruits that mature to a reddish color.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
