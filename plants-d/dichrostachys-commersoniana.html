Dichrostachys commersoniana

Dichrostachys commersoniana, commonly known as the commerson's burr or wild burr, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are opposite and pinnate, and its distinctive yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces small, spiny pods that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals. It is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Dichrostachys commersoniana is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a forage plant for livestock.
