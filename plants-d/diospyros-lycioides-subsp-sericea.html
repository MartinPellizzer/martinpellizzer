Diospyros lycioides subsp. sericea

Diospyros lycioides subsp.

sericea, commonly known as silver flamewood, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its smooth, silver-gray bark and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky pubescence.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by edible, sweet-tart berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
