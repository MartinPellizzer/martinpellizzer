Dianthus subaggregatus

Dianthus subaggregatus, commonly known as the clustered carnation, is a species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial plant characterized by its dense clusters of pink or white flowers. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, fragrant blossoms that bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long flowering period.
