Disperis lanceana

Disperis lanceana, commonly known as the lanceleaf orchid, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, often with a yellow or greenish hue at the base. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested or grassland habitats.

Disperis lanceana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
