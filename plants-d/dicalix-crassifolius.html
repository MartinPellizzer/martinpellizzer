Dicalix crassifolius

Dicalix crassifolius, commonly known as the broad-leaved dicalix, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, broad leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
