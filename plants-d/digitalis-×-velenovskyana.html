Digitalis × velenovskyana

Digitalis × velenovskyana, commonly known as the hybrid foxglove, is a perennial plant belonging to the Plantaginaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of hooded, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing showy flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe, it thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in medicine, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
