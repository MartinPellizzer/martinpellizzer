Datura hummatu

Datura hummatu, commonly known as the devil's trumpet, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically white or purple in color. The plant produces violet, hairy capsules that contain numerous seeds. Datura hummatu is a member of the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is known for its toxic properties.

It thrives in dry, open habitats and is often found in disturbed areas.
