Didissandra petelotii

Didissandra petelotii, commonly known as the blackwood tree, is a unique plant native to Madagascar.

It is a member of the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and avocado trees. The plant is characterized by its dark, almost black bark and its ability to thrive in humid, tropical climates. Didissandra petelotii is notable for its medicinal properties, with various parts of the plant used in traditional healing practices.

Its botanical significance lies in its distinctive morphology and potential pharmacological applications.
