Dombeya lasiostylis

Dombeya lasiostylis, commonly known as the rough-styled dombeya, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in woodland and rocky habitats, characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale pink, arranged in clusters at the ends of branches. Its leaves are elliptical and have a leathery texture, while the stems are covered in rough, glandular hairs.

Dombeya lasiostylis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its winter blooms.
