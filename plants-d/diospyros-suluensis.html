Diospyros suluensis

Diospyros suluensis, commonly known as the Sulu persimmon, is a species of flowering plant in the Ebenaceae family.

It is native to the Philippines, particularly found in the Sulu Archipelago and surrounding areas. The plant is a small to medium-sized tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, with smooth, gray bark and simple, alternate leaves. Its fruit is a small, fleshy berry that turns from green to yellow or orange when mature, and is edible when fully ripe.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine and cuisine.
