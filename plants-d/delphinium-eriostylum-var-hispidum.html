Delphinium eriostylum var. hispidum

Delphinium eriostylum var. hispidum, commonly known as the hispid delphinium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers in racemes, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine, soft hairs, giving it a velvety texture.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to alpine gardens.
