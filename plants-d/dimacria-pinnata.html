Dimacria pinnata

Dimacria pinnata, commonly known as the broadleaf mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of various host trees.

It has dark green, leathery leaves that are opposite and pinnately compound, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. Its berries, which are typically red or orange, are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

Dimacria pinnata is native to parts of Africa and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
