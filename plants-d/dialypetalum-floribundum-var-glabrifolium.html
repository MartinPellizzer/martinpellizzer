Dialypetalum floribundum var. glabrifolium

Dialypetalum floribundum var. glabrifolium, commonly known as the smooth-leaved dialypetalum, is a flowering plant characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology.

It possesses broad, smooth, and lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers with five petals, which bloom in the spring. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to thrive in well-drained soils.

This variant is often found in temperate regions with mild climates and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
