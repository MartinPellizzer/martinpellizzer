Dracamine pensylvanica

Dracamine pensylvanica, commonly known as Pennsylvania dracamine, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features upright, slender stems that bear clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white to pale pink. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a textured appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Dracamine pensylvanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
