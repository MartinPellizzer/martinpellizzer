Dichaea rendlei

Dichaea rendlei, commonly known as the Rendle's dichaea, is a rare orchid species native to the cloud forests of Central America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and green. The plant prefers cool, humid environments and is often found growing on tree trunks or rocks in shaded areas. Its roots are epiphytic, meaning it grows on other plants without being parasitic.

Due to habitat loss, Dichaea rendlei is considered endangered and is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
