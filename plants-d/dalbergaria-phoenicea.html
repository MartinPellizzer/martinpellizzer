Dalbergaria phoenicea

Dalbergaria phoenicea, commonly known as the red bushwillow, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant has a deep root system that helps it thrive in arid conditions. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to full sun exposure.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
