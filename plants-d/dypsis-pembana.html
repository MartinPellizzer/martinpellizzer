Dypsis pembana

Dypsis pembana, commonly known as the Madagascar palm, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar.

It features a thick, tree-like trunk with a spiny, crown of leaves that resemble a palm. The plant has a slow growth rate and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid environments. Its leaves are typically blue-green in color and have a waxy texture to reduce water loss.

Dypsis pembana is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
