Delphinium grandiflorum var. fischeri

Delphinium grandiflorum var. fischeri, commonly known as Fischer's larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It features tall, slender stems that bear clusters of striking blue to purple flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped, serrated leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and natural landscapes.
