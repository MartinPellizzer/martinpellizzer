Disa harveyana subsp. longicalcarata

Disa harveyana subsp.

longicalcarata, commonly known as the Harvey's Disa, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It produces a single, upright stem that bears several flowers, which are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
