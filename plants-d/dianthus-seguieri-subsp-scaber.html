Dianthus seguieri subsp. scaber

Dianthus seguieri subsp.

scaber, commonly known as the rough pinks, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features upright, branching stems covered in fine, rough hairs, giving it a distinctive tactile quality.

The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically blooming from spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
