Dioscorea japonica var. tenuiaxon

Dioscorea japonica var. tenuiaxon, commonly known as Japanese yam, is a climbing vine native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary economic product. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers. Its tubers are used in traditional medicine and as a food source in some regions.

The species is notable for its adaptability to various soil types and its role in local agricultural systems.
