Drosanthemum pulchellum

Drosanthemum pulchellum, commonly known as the pink-flowered rockrose, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, upright stems and produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink and red. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it well-suited for arid environments. Its leaves are typically blue-green in color and may develop a powdery coating to reduce water loss.

Drosanthemum pulchellum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and low maintenance requirements.
