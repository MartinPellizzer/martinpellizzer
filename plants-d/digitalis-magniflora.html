Digitalis magniflora

Digitalis magniflora, commonly known as the magnificent foxglove, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, spires of tubular flowers that range in color from deep purples to bright pinks. The plant belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Digitalis magniflora is also noted for its medicinal properties, though it contains toxic compounds that require careful handling.
