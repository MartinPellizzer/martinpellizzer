Dactylis disticha

Dactylis disticha, commonly known as the two-rowed grass or slender veldtgrass, is a perennial grass native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Dactylis disticha is often used in forage and erosion control due to its hardy nature and adaptability.
