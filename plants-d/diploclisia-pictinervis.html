Diploclisia pictinervis

Diploclisia pictinervis, commonly known as the painted nerve vine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, finely veined leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by small, fleshy berries. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
