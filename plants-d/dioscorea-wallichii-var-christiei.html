Dioscorea wallichii var. christiei

Dioscorea wallichii var. christiei, commonly known as the Kashmir yam, is a climbing vine native to the Himalayas.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary edible part. The plant produces small, green, heart-shaped leaves and bears yellowish-white flowers with a distinctive fragrance. Its tubers are valued for their nutritional content and traditional medicinal uses.

The plant thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for both culinary and pharmaceutical purposes.
