Delphinium naviculare

Delphinium naviculare, commonly known as the water larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

Delphinium naviculare is part of the Ranunculaceae family, which includes many other toxic plants.
