Dracula posadarum

Dracula posadarum, commonly known as the vampire orchid, is a rare and striking species native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black petals and a unique, elongated shape that resembles a bat or a skull, contributing to its mysterious appearance. The plant belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is known for its specialized pollination mechanism involving specific insects. Its habitat is highly restricted, making it a vulnerable species due to deforestation and habitat loss.

Dracula posadarum is prized by botanists and collectors for its unusual beauty and ecological significance.
