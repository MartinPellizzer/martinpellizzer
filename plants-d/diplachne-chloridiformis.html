Diplachne chloridiformis

Diplachne chloridiformis, commonly known as the narrowleaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It typically grows in grasslands and along riverbanks, preferring well-drained soils. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually pale green in color. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are arranged in a panicle, producing small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Diplachne chloridiformis is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
