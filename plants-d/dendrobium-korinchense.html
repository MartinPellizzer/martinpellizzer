Dendrobium korinchense

Dendrobium korinchense, commonly known as the korinchen's orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with multiple pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant produces long, slender inflorescences bearing numerous fragrant, yellowish-green flowers. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged in two rows along the stem.

Dendrobium korinchense is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
