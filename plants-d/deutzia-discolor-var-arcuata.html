Deutzia discolor var. arcuata

Deutzia discolor var. arcuata, commonly known as the pink deutzia, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features slender, arching branches that produce clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers in early summer. The plant has ovate to lance-shaped leaves that are dark green on top and pale underneath. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, moist soil.

Its ornamental value comes from its showy blooms and attractive foliage, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
