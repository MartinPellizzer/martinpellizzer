Dinebra aegyptiaca

Dinebra aegyptiaca, commonly known as the Egyptian candleplant, is a unique succulent native to the arid regions of North Africa.

It features a distinctive, branching structure with fleshy, cylindrical stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in dry environments. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers during the warmer months, adding ornamental value. Its leaves are typically reduced to scales, and it is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and resilience.

Dinebra aegyptiaca is well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low maintenance requirements.
