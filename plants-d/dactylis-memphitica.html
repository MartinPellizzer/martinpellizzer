Dactylis memphitica

Dactylis memphitica, commonly known as the Memphic grass, is a perennial grass species native to Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves that are typically hairy along the edges. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Dactylis memphitica is valued for its use in pasture and erosion control due to its hardiness and adaptability.
