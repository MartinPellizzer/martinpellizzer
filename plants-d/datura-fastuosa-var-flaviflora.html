Datura fastuosa var. flaviflora

Datura fastuosa var. flaviflora, commonly known as the yellow-flowered jimsonweed, is a flowering plant in the nightshade family.

It is native to South America and is characterized by its large, showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to yellow. The plant produces spiny, egg-shaped fruits that contain poisonous seeds. It thrives in warm, dry climates and is often found in disturbed soils.

Due to its toxic properties, it should be handled with care.
