Dierama sertum

Dierama sertum, commonly known as the woolly dierama, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it its common name. The plant produces striking, upright spikes of white or pale blue flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Dierama sertum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
