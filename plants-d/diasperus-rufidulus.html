Diasperus rufidulus

Diasperus rufidulus, commonly known as the red-stemmed broom, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, woody stems that are typically reddish in color, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in dense clusters during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, adapting to the dry, sunny conditions of its natural habitat.

Diasperus rufidulus is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
