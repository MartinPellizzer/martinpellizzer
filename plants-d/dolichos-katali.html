Dolichos katali

Dolichos katali, commonly known as the Indian cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, with slender, hairy stems that support its compound leaves. The flowers are typically yellow or orange, and the pods are elongated, containing several edible seeds. The plant is valued for its nutritional content, providing protein and essential nutrients.

It also plays a role in soil enrichment due to its nitrogen-fixing abilities.
