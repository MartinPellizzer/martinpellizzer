Dactylorhiza fuchsii subsp. fuchsii

Dactylorhiza fuchsii subsp.

fuchsii, commonly known as the common twayblade, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and heaths.

The plant has a slender, leafless stem with two opposite leaves, and it produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. It is characterized by its unique morphology and is often found in association with specific fungal mycorrhizae that aid in nutrient uptake.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its ecological significance and conservation status.
