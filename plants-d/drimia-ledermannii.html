Drimia ledermannii

Drimia ledermannii, commonly known as the Ledermann's dragon's tongue, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of strap-like leaves that are often blue-green in color. The plant produces striking, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, which bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and containers.

Drimia ledermannii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively low maintenance once established.
