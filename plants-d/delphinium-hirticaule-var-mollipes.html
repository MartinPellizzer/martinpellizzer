Delphinium hirticaule var. mollipes

Delphinium hirticaule var. mollipes, commonly known as the soft-hairy larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features tall, spindly stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height, adorned with clusters of blue to purple, spurred flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped, hairy, and arranged alternately along the stem, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is cultivated in gardens and conservation efforts.
