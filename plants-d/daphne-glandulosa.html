Daphne glandulosa

Daphne glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular daphne, is a species of flowering plant in the family Thymelaeaceae.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its fragrant, showy flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has smooth, often aromatic leaves and produces small, glandular structures that contribute to its distinctive scent. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Daphne glandulosa is also noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its winter blooms.
