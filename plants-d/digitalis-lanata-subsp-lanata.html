Digitalis lanata subsp. lanata

Digitalis lanata subsp.

lanata, commonly known as the woolly foxglove, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe. It features tall, erect stems covered in dense, woolly hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and also covered in fine, soft hairs.

This species is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
