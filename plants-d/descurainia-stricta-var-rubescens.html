Descurainia stricta var. rubescens

Descurainia stricta var. rubescens, commonly known as scarlet winter cress, is an annual flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its low-growing, hairy stems and alternate leaves that are typically oval to lance-shaped. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and along riverbanks.

The species is known for its rapid growth and ability to tolerate a wide range of environmental conditions.
