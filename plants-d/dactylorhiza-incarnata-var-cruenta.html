Dactylorhiza incarnata var. cruenta

Dactylorhiza incarnata var. cruenta, commonly known as the crimson helleborine, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features a tall, erect stem with several lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of showy, violet or purple flowers. The plant is characterized by its intricate floral structure, including a spurred lip that aids in pollination. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its striking appearance.
