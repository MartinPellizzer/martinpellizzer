Dioscorea grandiflora

Dioscorea grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered yam, is a climbing herbaceous perennial native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its vigorous vine growth and large, showy white or pink flowers. The plant produces tuberous roots that are often used in traditional medicine and as a food source. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a rough texture, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Dioscorea grandiflora is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
