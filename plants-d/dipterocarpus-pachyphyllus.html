Dipterocarpus pachyphyllus

Dipterocarpus pachyphyllus, commonly known as the thick-leaf dipterocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves. The tree can grow up to 30 meters tall and is often found in lowland dipterocarp forests. Its flowers are small and clustered, and it produces heavy, woody fruits that contain seeds.

This species is valued for its durable timber and plays an important role in the ecological structure of its habitat.
