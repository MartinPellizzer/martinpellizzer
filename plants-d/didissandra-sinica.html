Didissandra sinica

Didissandra sinica, commonly known as the Chinese sand pear, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, white flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically consumed raw or used in traditional Chinese medicine. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas with well-drained soil.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and medicinal research.
