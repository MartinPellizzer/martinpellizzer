Dryoathyrium dielsii

Dryoathyrium dielsii, commonly known as the Himalayan feather grass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and delicate, feathery inflorescences that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. It has a low-growing habit and is often found in meadows and rocky slopes at high elevations.

Botanically, it is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native range.
