Dianthus levieri

Dianthus levieri, commonly known as the Sierra foothill campion, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils in open habitats such as meadows and slopes. It has slender stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves.

Dianthus levieri is often found in elevations ranging from 1,000 to 3,000 meters.
