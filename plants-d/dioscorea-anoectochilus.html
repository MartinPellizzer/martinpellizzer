Dioscorea anoectochilus

Dioscorea anoectochilus, commonly known as the purple yam, is a tuberous vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its starchy, edible tubers that are often used in local cuisines. The plant typically grows in humid, tropical environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its leaves are heart-shaped and its flowers are usually white or pale purple, producing small, inconspicuous fruits.

The tubers are rich in starch and have been traditionally used for both culinary and medicinal purposes.
