Dendrocolla multicolor

Dendrocolla multicolor, commonly known as the rainbow tree, is a unique plant species characterized by its striking, multicolored foliage.

Its leaves display a vibrant array of hues, ranging from deep purples to bright yellows, due to the presence of specialized pigments. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in tropical rainforests. It has a slow growth rate and is known for its ability to absorb and store moisture efficiently.

Due to its rarity and aesthetic appeal, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
