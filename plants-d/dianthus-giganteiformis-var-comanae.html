Dianthus giganteiformis var. comanae

Dianthus giganteiformis var. comanae, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada primrose, is a striking plant native to the high elevations of California and Nevada.

It features showy, pink to rose-colored flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. The plant has a compact, mat-like growth habit with broad, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, woolly hairs. Its flowers are typically arranged in dense clusters, and the species is notable for its ability to thrive in harsh alpine conditions.

Botanically, it is characterized by its distinctive floral structure and adaptation to high-altitude environments.
