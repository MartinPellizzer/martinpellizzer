Desmazeria loliacea

Desmazeria loliacea, commonly known as the slender false bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. The plant features slender, erect stems with lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are small, bell-shaped, and usually pink or purple, arranged in clusters at the tips of the stems.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
