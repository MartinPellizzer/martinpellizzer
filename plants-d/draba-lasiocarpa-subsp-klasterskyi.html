Draba lasiocarpa subsp. klasterskyi

Draba lasiocarpa subsp.

klasterskyi, commonly known as the Klastersky's whitlow grass, is a flowering plant native to high-altitude regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It has a compact growth habit with hairy stems and basal leaves, and its flowers are usually pale yellow or white.

This subspecies is adapted to cold, arid conditions and is often found in mountainous areas of Kazakhstan and surrounding regions.
