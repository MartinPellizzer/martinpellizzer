Dryopteris varia var. hikonensis

Dryopteris varia var. hikonensis, commonly known as the Hikonensis Shield Fern, is a rare and distinctive variant of the variable shield fern.

It is characterized by its finely divided, lance-shaped fronds that exhibit a striking contrast between the fertile and sterile parts. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in forest understories or along stream banks. Its spore-bearing structures, called sori, are clustered on the underside of the fronds, a common feature in ferns.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and specialized collections.
