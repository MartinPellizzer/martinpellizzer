Dianthus pyrenaicus

Dianthus pyrenaicus, commonly known as the Pyrenean campion, is a flowering plant native to the Pyrenees mountains.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces showy, pink or red flowers with a distinctive frilly appearance. The plant features hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, typical of the Caryophyllaceae family. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions.

Dianthus pyrenaicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
