Darwinia polycephala

Darwinia polycephala, commonly known as the clustered Darwinia, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and dense clusters of showy, pinkish-red flowers. The plant produces woody capsules that contain numerous small, winged seeds, aiding in wind dispersal. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in dry sclerophyll forests and open woodlands.

Darwinia polycephala is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
