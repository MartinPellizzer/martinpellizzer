Dianthus laricifolius var. caespitosifolius

Dianthus laricifolius var. caespitosifolius, commonly known as the mountain pink, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, tufted growth habit and displays showy, pink to red flowers with five petals. The leaves are narrow, grass-like, and often arranged in dense clusters, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in alpine or rocky habitats.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
