Damnacanthus indicus subsp. major

Damnacanthus indicus subsp.

major, commonly known as the Indian flame flower, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its striking red-orange bracts that resemble petals, which are actually modified leaves.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and prefers well-drained soil.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant appearance.
