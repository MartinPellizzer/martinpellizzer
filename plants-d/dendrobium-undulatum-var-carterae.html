Dendrobium undulatum var. carterae

Dendrobium undulatum var. carterae, commonly known as the Carter's wavy orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features long, wiry stems that often hang from trees, with clusters of narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically in late winter to early spring. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light and requires well-draining potting media.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to cultivate for experienced orchid growers.
