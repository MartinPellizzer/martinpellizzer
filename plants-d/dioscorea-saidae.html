Dioscorea saidae

Dioscorea saidae, commonly known as the Ethiopian yam, is a climbing vine native to Ethiopia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary edible part. The plant produces small, green, heart-shaped leaves and yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. Its tubers are rich in starch and have been traditionally used as a food source in local communities.

The botanical features of Dioscorea saidae make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
