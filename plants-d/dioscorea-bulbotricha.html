Dioscorea bulbotricha

Dioscorea bulbotricha, commonly known as the hairy-root yam, is a species of tuberous vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its distinctive hairy roots and elongated tubers. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded forest understories. Its tubers are sometimes used in traditional medicine and as a food source.

The botanical features of D. bulbotricha make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
