Dioscorea polystachya f. elongata

Dioscorea polystachya f. elongata, commonly known as the elongated yam, is a variant of the yam family native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its long, tuberous roots that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant typically features heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. Its tubers are used in traditional medicine and as a food source in some cultures.

Botanically, it is notable for its unique morphology and adaptability to various soil conditions.
