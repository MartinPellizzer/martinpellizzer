Dicranopygium tatica

Dicranopygium tatica, commonly known as the narrow-leaf feather moss, is a species of moss belonging to the family Dicranaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are often curved and covered in a thin layer of cuticle. The plant forms dense, cushion-like mats on moist, shaded forest floors, typically in temperate regions. Its reproductive structures, called capsules, are borne on stalks and release spores when mature.

Dicranopygium tatica plays a vital role in soil stabilization and moisture retention in its native habitats.
