Drypetes longipes

Drypetes longipes, commonly known as the longipes flame tree, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that appear in clusters, adding vibrant color to the landscape. The plant has compound leaves with elongated leaflets and produces small, round fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests or along riverbanks.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for its ecological and medicinal properties.
