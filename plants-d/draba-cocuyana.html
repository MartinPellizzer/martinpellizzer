Draba cocuyana

Draba cocuyana, commonly known as the Cocuyan rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils.
