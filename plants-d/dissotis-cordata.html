Dissotis cordata

Dissotis cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf arrowroot, is a herbaceous perennial native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It features heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are typically green with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in spike-like clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its rhizomes are used as a starchy root vegetable, similar to taro.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.
