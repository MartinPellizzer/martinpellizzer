Daubenya namaquensis

Daubenya namaquensis, commonly known as the Namakwa lily, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Daubenya namaquensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
