Delonix edulis

Delonix edulis, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical leguminous plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its showy, bright orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, serrated leaflets and produces pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Delonix edulis also plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility.
