Descurainia pinnata subsp. brachycarpon

Descurainia pinnata subsp.

brachycarpon, commonly known as narrowleaf tansy, is a flowering plant native to North America. It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, yellow flowers and compound leaves.

The subspecies brachycarpon is distinguished by its shorter, more compact leaves compared to the typical form. It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in prairies and open woodlands.

This plant is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
