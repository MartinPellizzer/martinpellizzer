Dipterocarpus schmidtii

Dipterocarpus schmidtii, commonly known as the Schmidt's dipterocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves and produces heavy, woody fruits that contain winged seeds. The plant is part of the Dipterocarpaceae family, which is known for its ecological importance in tropical rainforests. It typically grows in lowland areas and plays a crucial role in forest ecosystems.

The tree is valued for its hard, durable wood, which has been historically used in construction and furniture-making.
