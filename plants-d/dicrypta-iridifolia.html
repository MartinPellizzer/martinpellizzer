Dicrypta iridifolia

Dicrypta iridifolia, commonly known as the bluebell orchid, is a striking flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its elegant, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to violet, often with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as cloud forests, where it thrives in cool and humid conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Dicrypta iridifolia is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique floral display.
