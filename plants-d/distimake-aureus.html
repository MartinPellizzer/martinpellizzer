Distimake aureus

Distimake aureus, commonly known as the golden sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a robust, upright stem with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sunlight and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

The seeds produced by Distimake aureus are often used in traditional medicine and as a food source for local wildlife.
