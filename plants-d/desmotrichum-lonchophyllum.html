Desmotrichum lonchophyllum

Desmotrichum lonchophyllum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy stems. The plant typically grows from 10 to 30 centimeters in height, with narrow, linear leaves and yellow ray flowers. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in sandy or gravelly soils, often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops.

Its structure and adaptations make it well-suited for survival in harsh, dry environments.
