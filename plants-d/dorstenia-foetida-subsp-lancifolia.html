Dorstenia foetida subsp. lancifolia

Dorstenia foetida subsp.

lancifolia, commonly known as the stinking cucumber, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa. It is characterized by its unique, fleshy, cucumber-like fruits that hang from the plant.

The leaves are typically heart-shaped and can be hairy, with a somewhat succulent texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often clustered in the leaf axils.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
