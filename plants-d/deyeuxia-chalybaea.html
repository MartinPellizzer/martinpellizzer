Deyeuxia chalybaea

Deyeuxia chalybaea, commonly known as the silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-blue foliage and compact growth habit. The plant produces small, pale-green spikelets that are typically arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Deyeuxia chalybaea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
