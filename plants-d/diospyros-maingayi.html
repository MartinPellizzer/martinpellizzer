Diospyros maingayi

Diospyros maingayi, commonly known as the Malayan persimmon, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves and smooth, pale bark. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into orange-red, fleshy berries. Its fruits are edible, with a sweet, astringent flavor, and are often used in local cuisines.

The plant is also valued for its hard wood, which is used in crafting and construction.
