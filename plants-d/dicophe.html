Dicophe

Dicophe, commonly known as the false dandelion, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers that later develop into fluffy seed heads. The plant is native to various regions in Europe and parts of Asia. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Dicophe is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
