Dianthus fimbriatus var. brachyodontus

Dianthus fimbriatus var. brachyodontus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved campion, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate appearance and long-lasting blooms.
