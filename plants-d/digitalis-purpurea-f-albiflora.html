Digitalis purpurea f. albiflora

Digitalis purpurea f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered foxglove, is a variant of the common foxglove characterized by its pale or white flowers.

It is a biennial plant native to Europe and is known for its tall spikes of tubular flowers. The leaves are heart-shaped and have a velvety texture, while the stems are erect and fibrous. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

This plant is also valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in herbal medicine.
