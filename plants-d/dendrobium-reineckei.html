Dendrobium reineckei

Dendrobium reineckei, commonly known as the golden spike orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, cane-like stems and clusters of fragrant, golden-yellow flowers. The plant typically blooms during the summer months, producing showy inflorescences that attract pollinators. Its leaves are elongated and grass-like, often arranged in a rosette pattern around the stem.

Dendrobium reineckei prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for both garden cultivation and horticultural collections.
