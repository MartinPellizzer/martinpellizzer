Duosperma longicalyx subsp. magadiense

Duosperma longicalyx subsp.

magadiense, commonly known as the Magadi milkvetch, is a perennial shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa. It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant is adapted to withstand drought conditions, with deep roots that help it access water from deeper soil layers. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils, thriving in environments with low precipitation.

The species is valued for its hardiness and is sometimes used in traditional medicine or as a forage plant for livestock.
