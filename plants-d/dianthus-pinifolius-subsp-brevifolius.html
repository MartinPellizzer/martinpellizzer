Dianthus pinifolius subsp. brevifolius

Dianthus pinifolius subsp.

brevifolius, commonly known as the dwarf pineleaf carnation, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features compact, low-growing foliage with narrow, needle-like leaves that resemble pine needles, giving it its common name.

The plant produces clusters of small, brightly colored flowers, typically in shades of pink, red, or white, during the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its botanical characteristics make it an attractive and hardy addition to gardens with specific horticultural requirements.
