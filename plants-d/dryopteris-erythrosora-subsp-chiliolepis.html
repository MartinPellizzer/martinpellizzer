Dryopteris erythrosora subsp. chiliolepis

Dryopteris erythrosora subsp.

chiliolepis, commonly known as the red fern, is a fern species characterized by its distinctive reddish-brown fronds. It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky slopes. Its fronds are usually leathery and have a dense, feathery appearance.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
