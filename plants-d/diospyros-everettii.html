Diospyros everettii

Diospyros everettii, commonly known as the Chinese date or everett's persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and alternate, ovate leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into orange-red, fleshy fruits with a sweet, astringent flavor.

Its fruits are often consumed fresh or used in traditional medicine for their nutritional and therapeutic properties.
