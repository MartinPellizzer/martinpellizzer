Drosanthemum cereale

Drosanthemum cereale, commonly known as the golden groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant features alternate, hairy, and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white pubescence. Its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in dense heads, blooming from late spring to early autumn.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
