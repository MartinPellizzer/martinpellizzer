Diospyros paniculata

Diospyros paniculata, commonly known as the Indian persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in panicles, which give it its species name "paniculata.". Its fruit is a small, fleshy berry that turns from green to yellow or orange when ripe.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
