Delphinium elatum

Delphinium elatum, commonly known as the larkspur, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems that bear clusters of striking blue, purple, or white flowers. The plant has lance-shaped, serrated leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. Delphinium elatum is part of the Ranunculaceae family, which includes other showy flowering plants.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
