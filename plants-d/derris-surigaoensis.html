Derris surigaoensis

Derris surigaoensis, commonly known as the Surigao devil's ivy, is a climbing shrub native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in racemes, followed by pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species in local ecosystems and traditional medicine.
