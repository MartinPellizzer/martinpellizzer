Dioscorea preangeriana

Dioscorea preangeriana, commonly known as the Java yam, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are used in traditional medicine and as a food source. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers. Its fruits are capsules that contain numerous seeds, and it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

The tubers of D. preangeriana are valued for their nutritional content and have been used in various cultural practices.
