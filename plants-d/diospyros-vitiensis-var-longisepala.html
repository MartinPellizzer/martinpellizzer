Diospyros vitiensis var. longisepala

Diospyros vitiensis var. longisepala, commonly known as the long-sepaled persimmon, is a tropical tree native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. The flowers are small and clustered, with long sepals that give the variety its name. The fruit is a rough, fleshy drupe that turns orange when mature.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often found in moist, shaded environments.
