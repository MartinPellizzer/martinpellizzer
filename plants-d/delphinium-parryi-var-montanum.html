Delphinium parryi var. montanum

Delphinium parryi var. montanum, commonly known as the Sierra delphinium, is a striking perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems that bear clusters of blue to purple, spurred flowers. The plant typically grows in open, rocky habitats at high elevations, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its foliage consists of basal leaves that are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs.

Delphinium parryi var. montanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with specific growing conditions.
