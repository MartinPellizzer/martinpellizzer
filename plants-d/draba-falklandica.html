Draba falklandica

Draba falklandica, commonly known as Falkland's whitlow grass, is a flowering plant native to the Falkland Islands.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many species of mustard and cabbage. The plant is characterized by its small, white or pale yellow flowers and basal rosette of leaves. It thrives in the cool, temperate climate of its native habitat.

Draba falklandica is adapted to grow in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas.
