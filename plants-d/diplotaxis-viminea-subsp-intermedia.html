Diplotaxis viminea subsp. intermedia

Diplotaxis viminea subsp.

intermedia, commonly known as the slender everlasting pea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant produces small, cylindrical seed pods that contain numerous tiny, dark seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
