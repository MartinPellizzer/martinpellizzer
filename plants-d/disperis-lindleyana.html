Disperis lindleyana

Disperis lindleyana, commonly known as the Lindley's orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in coastal or mountainous regions. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with a distinctive lip that aids in pollination by attracting specific insect pollinators.
