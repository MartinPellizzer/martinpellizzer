Diospyros rhodocalyx

Diospyros rhodocalyx, commonly known as the red-leaf persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that turn red or orange in the autumn, adding visual interest to the landscape. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by edible, orange-red persimmons in the fall. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and the nutritional value of its fruit.
