Diplazium ceratolepis

Diplazium ceratolepis, commonly known as the hairy shield fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, shield-shaped fronds that are covered in fine, hairy structures. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its fronds are divided into segments with a serrated or lobed appearance.

Diplazium ceratolepis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
