Dryandra fraseri var. oxycedra

Dryandra fraseri var. oxycedra, commonly known as the Western Australian waratah, is a striking shrub native to the south-western region of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its large, showy, red or pink flower heads that bloom in spring. The plant belongs to the Proteaceae family and is known for its distinctive, fern-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and heathland environments.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and is a key species in the biodiversity of its native habitat.
