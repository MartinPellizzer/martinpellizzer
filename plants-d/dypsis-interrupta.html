Dypsis interrupta

Dypsis interrupta, commonly known as the Madagascar palm, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, spiny trunk and crown of feathery leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters at the top of the stem. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and ability to thrive in arid conditions.

Dypsis interrupta is also valued for its resilience and adaptability to various soil types.
