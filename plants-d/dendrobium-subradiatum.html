Dendrobium subradiatum

Dendrobium subradiatum, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, cane-like stems that often branch into clusters of fleshy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange, which bloom sequentially along the stem. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright indirect light and requires well-draining potting media.

Dendrobium subradiatum is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for orchid enthusiasts.
