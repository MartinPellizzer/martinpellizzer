Deyeuxia brassii

Deyeuxia brassii, commonly known as the silver grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-gray blades and tufted growth habit, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of water bodies. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, cylindrical spikelet that is often hairy and can be quite showy.

Deyeuxia brassii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and is used in landscaping and as a ground cover in humid climates.
