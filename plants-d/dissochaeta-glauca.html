Dissochaeta glauca

Dissochaeta glauca, commonly known as blueleaf false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually blue-green in color, giving it its common name.

It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring and summer months.
