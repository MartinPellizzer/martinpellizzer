Dioscorea perpilosa

Dioscorea perpilosa, commonly known as the purple yam, is a flowering plant belonging to the Dioscoreaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary part used for culinary and medicinal purposes. The plant typically grows as a climbing vine with heart-shaped leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. Its tubers are rich in starch and have been traditionally used in various cuisines across the region.

Additionally, the plant has been studied for its potential medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects.
