Dianthus monspessulanus var. arragonensis

Dianthus monspessulanus var. arragonensis, commonly known as the Arragon carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that typically range from pink to red. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in horticulture.
