Dyckia abstrusa

Dyckia abstrusa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf dyckia, is a species of bromeliad native to South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped arrangement of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hair. The plant produces striking inflorescences with red or orange flowers that bloom in the spring. It is a hardy, drought-tolerant plant that thrives in full sun and well-draining soil.

Dyckia abstrusa is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and low maintenance requirements.
