Dioscorea buergeri var. enneaneura

Dioscorea buergeri var. enneaneura, commonly known as the Buerger's yam, is a variant of the yam family native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are typically elongated and have a rough, bark-like exterior. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in spikes, and its leaves are heart-shaped with a serrated margin. The tubers are used in traditional medicine and as a food source in some regions.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil and partial shade.
