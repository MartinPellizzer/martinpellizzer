Dalbergia rivularis

Dalbergia rivularis, commonly known as the river ebony, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is notable for its dark, heavy wood, which is highly valued for furniture and carving. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water sources, contributing to its common name.

Due to its ecological and economic importance, it is also a subject of conservation interest.
