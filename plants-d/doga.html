Doga

Doga, commonly known as the African violet, is a popular flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Gesneriaceae and is characterized by its velvety, waxy leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as a compact shrub with upright stems and produces flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white. Doga thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its ornamental value makes it a favored houseplant worldwide.
