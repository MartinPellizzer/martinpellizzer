Dipcadi elatum

Dipcadi elatum, commonly known as the giant onion, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, fleshy leaves that can grow up to 1.5 meters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in summer. Its bulbous stem is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The botanical features of Dipcadi elatum make it a distinctive and valuable species in its native habitat.
