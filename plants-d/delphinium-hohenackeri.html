Delphinium hohenackeri

Delphinium hohenackeri, commonly known as the Hohenacker larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces clusters of blue to purple flowers, which are arranged in racemes and are typical of the Delphinium genus. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinctive, somewhat hairy texture.

Delphinium hohenackeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
