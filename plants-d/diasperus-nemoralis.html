Diasperus nemoralis

Diasperus nemoralis, commonly known as the rough hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white to pinkish flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature. Its leaves are alternate, oval-shaped, and have a rough texture.

Diasperus nemoralis is often found in woodlands and along stream banks, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
