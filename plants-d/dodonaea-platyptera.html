Dodonaea platyptera

Dodonaea platyptera, commonly known as the silky oaks, is a deciduous tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and compound leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in spikes, which are followed by woody, oval-shaped pods. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Dodonaea platyptera is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental value and ability to provide shade.
