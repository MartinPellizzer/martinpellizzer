Dactylorhiza comosa f. occidentalis

Dactylorhiza comosa f. occidentalis, commonly known as the western marsh orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, bogs, and damp meadows. The plant features a slender, upright stem with several lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are pale yellow to greenish-yellow, often with a distinctive purple or brownish lip.

It is a rhizomatous orchid, with a short, horizontal rhizome that helps it survive in its wetland environment.
