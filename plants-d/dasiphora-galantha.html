Dasiphora galantha

Dasiphora galantha, commonly known as the yellow coneflower, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the rose family (Rosaceae) and is characterized by its showy, yellow to orange-pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies and woodland edges. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
