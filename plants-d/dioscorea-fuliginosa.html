Dioscorea fuliginosa

Dioscorea fuliginosa, commonly known as the black snake root, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit, with tendrils that help it ascend trees and shrubs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences, followed by tuberous roots that are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are covered in short, stiff hairs.

The species is notable for its medicinal properties and has been utilized in various traditional healing practices.
