Dorisia flavida

Dorisia flavida, commonly known as the yellow dorisia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating.

Dorisia flavida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
