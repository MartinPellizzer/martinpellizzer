Descurainia athrocarpa var. macrorrhiza

Descurainia athrocarpa var. macrorrhiza, commonly known as the large-rooted tansy mustard, is a herbaceous annual plant native to North America.

It typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is characterized by its yellow flowers and elongated, ribbed seed pods. The plant has a taproot system and produces small, oval-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. It is part of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other mustard plants.

This variety is distinguished by its larger root system compared to other varieties of Descurainia.
