Dianthus armerioides

Dianthus armerioides, commonly known as the rock campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of about 10 to 20 centimeters. The plant features clusters of pink or white flowers with distinctive red or purple markings on the petals. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white powder.

Dianthus armerioides thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal environments.
