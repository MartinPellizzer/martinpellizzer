Delphinium beesianum f. calcicola

Delphinium beesianum f. calcicola, commonly known as the alpine delphinium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces showy blue to violet flowers in dense spikes, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained, calcareous soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ornamental species within the Ranunculaceae family.
