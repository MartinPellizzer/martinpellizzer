Dombeya stipulacea var. stipulacea

Dombeya stipulacea var. stipulacea, commonly known as the woolly dombeya, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, fuzzy leaves and showy, pink to white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically orange or red in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ecological studies.
