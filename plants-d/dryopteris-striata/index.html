Dryopteris Striata

Dryopteris striata, commonly known as the stripe fern, is a striking member of the Dryopteris genus that thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its distinctive appearance features narrow, lance-shaped fronds with a pale midrib that creates a unique striped pattern. This fern is often found in temperate forests and along stream banks, where it plays a vital role in the ecosystem. With its elegant form and adaptability, it is a popular choice for both ornamental gardens and natural habitats. Its resilience and beauty make it a fascinating subject for botanists and nature enthusiasts alike.
