Digitaria tisserantii

Digitaria tisserantii, commonly known as the Tisserant's Digitaria, is a grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves with a prominent midrib. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically arranged in panicles. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including grasslands and disturbed soils, and is often found in semi-arid regions.

Botanically, it is notable for its morphological adaptations to arid environments, such as drought-resistant foliage and efficient water-use mechanisms.
