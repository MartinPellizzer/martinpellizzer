Dioscorea campestris f. paraguayensis

Dioscorea campestris f. paraguayensis, commonly known as the Paraguay yam, is a tuberous perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces cylindrical, yellowish tubers that are used as a food source in some regions. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The tubers are often used in traditional cuisines and have been studied for their potential medicinal properties.
