Dioscorea sanchez-colini

Dioscorea sanchez-colini, commonly known as the Sanchezea yam, is a climbing vine belonging to the family Dioscoreaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces tuberous roots that are rich in starch and have been traditionally used in local cuisines. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its flowers are small and typically yellow or green.

The plant is also valued for its potential medicinal properties, though further research is needed to confirm its therapeutic applications.
