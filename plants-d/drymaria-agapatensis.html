Drymaria agapatensis

Drymaria agapatensis, commonly known as the Agapite grass, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, adapted to withstand dry conditions.

Drymaria agapatensis is often found in rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
