Disa stokoei

Disa stokoei, commonly known as the Stokoe's disa, is a striking member of the orchid family, Orchidaceae, native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of vibrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers with prominent, spurred petals, which are adapted for pollination by specific insect species. Disa stokoei thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions with high altitude and cool climates.

Its unique botanical features make it a popular subject for both horticultural cultivation and botanical study.
