Daphniphyllum glaucescens

Daphniphyllum glaucescens, commonly known as the glossy laurel, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments. The plant features opposite, elliptical leaves that are dark green and glossy on top, with a lighter underside. It produces small, white flowers in clusters, followed by red berries that are edible for birds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
