Decaspermum cryptanthum

Decaspermum cryptanthum, commonly known as the golden burr, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant produces small, spiny fruits that encase its seeds, aiding in seed dispersal by attaching to animal fur. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
