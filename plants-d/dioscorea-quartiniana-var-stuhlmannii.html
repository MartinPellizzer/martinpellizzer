Dioscorea quartiniana var. stuhlmannii

Dioscorea quartiniana var. stuhlmannii, commonly known as the Stuhlmann's yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its hairy, cylindrical stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by tuberous roots that are used in traditional medicine. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its tubers are valued for their nutritional content and have been used in various cultural practices.
