Dianthus ponticola

Dianthus ponticola, commonly known as the Pontic carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and produces clusters of pink to red flowers with frilly petals. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it prefers well-drained soil in full sun.

Dianthus ponticola is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
