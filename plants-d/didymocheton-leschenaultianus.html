Didymocheton leschenaultianus

Didymocheton leschenaultianus, commonly known as the Leschenault's cycad, is a member of the Cycadaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a slow-growing, evergreen plant with a stout, unbranched trunk and a crown of feather-like leaves. The plant produces cones, with male and female cones occurring on separate plants, a characteristic of cycads. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, requiring well-drained soil and ample sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and conservation status, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
