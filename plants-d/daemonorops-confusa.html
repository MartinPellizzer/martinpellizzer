Daemonorops confusa

Daemonorops confusa, commonly known as the Sunda palm, is a species of palm native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant typically grows in tropical rainforests and is often found in shaded, moist environments. Its fruit is small and edible, containing a single seed surrounded by a fleshy, yellowish pericarp.

The Sunda palm plays an important role in its ecosystem, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
