Dipladenia pendula

Dipladenia pendula, commonly known as the trailing gloxinia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and showy, tubular flowers. The plant produces vibrant, trumpet-shaped blooms in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically during the summer months. Dipladenia pendula thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as a decorative plant in hanging baskets or containers.

Its foliage is heart-shaped and glossy, adding to its ornamental appeal.
