Disa longilabris

Disa longilabris, commonly known as the long-lipped disa, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, spurred flowers that typically bloom in late summer. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces fragrant, often pink or purple, blooms that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Disa longilabris is a popular ornamental plant due to its vibrant colors and unique floral structure.
