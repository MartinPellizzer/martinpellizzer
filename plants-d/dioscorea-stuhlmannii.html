Dioscorea stuhlmannii

Dioscorea stuhlmannii, commonly known as the African yambean, is a climbing herbaceous perennial native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its vigorous vine growth and compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by cylindrical, fleshy fruits containing numerous seeds. Its tubers, which are the primary economic product, are rich in starch and have been traditionally used as a food source.

The plant is also valued for its medicinal properties in various traditional healing practices.
