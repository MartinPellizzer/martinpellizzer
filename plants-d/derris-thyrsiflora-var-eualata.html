Derris thyrsiflora var. eualata

Derris thyrsiflora var. eualata, commonly known as the golden derris, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, green pods that contain flat, kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both ornamental horticulture and traditional medicine.
