Danthonia tandilensis

Danthonia tandilensis, commonly known as the Tandil grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
