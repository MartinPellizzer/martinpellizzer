Dactylorhiza × vallis-peenae

Dactylorhiza × vallis-peenae, commonly known as the marsh orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of pale green to yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in wet, acidic habitats such as bogs and marshes. It has a complex root system that includes mycorrhizal associations essential for nutrient uptake.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as an indicator species for wetland ecosystems.
