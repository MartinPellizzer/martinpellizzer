Drymonia dressleri

Drymonia dressleri, commonly known as the Desert Candelabra, is a striking succulent native to southern Africa.

It features a branched, tree-like structure with thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. Its leaves are typically gray-green, covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a vibrant touch to its desert habitat.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
