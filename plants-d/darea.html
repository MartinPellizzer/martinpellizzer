Darea

Darea, commonly known as the dandelion, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of alternating leaves and a hollow, hairy stem. The plant produces a distinctive flower head that turns into a sphere of white seeds with parachute-like structures for wind dispersal. Darea is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and is often found in lawns and disturbed areas.

It has a long flowering period, typically from spring to early autumn.
