Diplazium mickelii

Diplazium mickelii, commonly known as the Mickel's water fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and streams. The plant has finely divided, feather-like fronds that are often submerged or floating. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Diplazium mickelii plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small organisms and contributing to water quality through oxygen production.
