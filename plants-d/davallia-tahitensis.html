Davallia tahitensis

Davallia tahitensis, commonly known as the Tahitian maidenhair fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Davalliaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are typically light green with a silvery underside. Native to the island of Tahiti in French Polynesia, it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant has rhizomes that grow horizontally and produce clustered, spore-bearing sorus structures.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in tropical gardens.
