Dombeya leucorrhoea

Dombeya leucorrhoea, commonly known as white dombeya, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of southern and eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has smooth, often greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded habitats.

Dombeya leucorrhoea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
