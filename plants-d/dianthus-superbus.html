Dianthus superbus

Dianthus superbus, commonly known as the tall catchfly, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of pink or red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and is often found in meadows and grasslands. It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family, which includes other popular garden plants like carnations and pinks.

Dianthus superbus is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
