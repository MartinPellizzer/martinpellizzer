Drosera chimaera

Drosera chimaera, commonly known as the ghost sundew, is a carnivorous plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its translucent, waxy leaves that form a rosette shape, which helps in capturing and digesting insects. The plant produces small, pinkish-red flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, acidic environments and requires high humidity for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and specialized adaptations, it is a popular specimen among carnivorous plant enthusiasts.
