Dorstenia tubicina var. genuina

Dorstenia tubicina var. genuina, commonly known as the slender false arum, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Araceae, characterized by its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix and a spathe. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as rainforests, where it thrives in the understory. Its leaves are usually elongated and have a waxy texture, while its flowers are small and clustered around the spadix.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
