Delphinium mirabile

Delphinium mirabile, commonly known as the fairybells, is a striking member of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, where it thrives in alpine and subalpine environments. The plant is characterized by its tall, spindly stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers. Its foliage consists of deeply lobed, dark green leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem.

Delphinium mirabile is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant and delicate floral display.
