Draba pulvinata subsp. pulvinata

Draba pulvinata subsp.

pulvinata, commonly known as the alpine mouse-ear cress, is a small flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family. It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and is typically found in alpine and subalpine regions.

The plant features basal leaves that are spoon-shaped and hairy, with small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

This species is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience and ability to stabilize soil in harsh conditions.
