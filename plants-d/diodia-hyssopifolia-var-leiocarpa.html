Diodia hyssopifolia var. leiocarpa

Diodia hyssopifolia var. leiocarpa, commonly known as smoothleaf diodia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands, ditches, and along streambanks. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are smooth on both surfaces, giving it its common name. Its small, white to pale purple flowers are clustered in dense, terminal spikes, and it produces small, round fruits that are borne in clusters.

The species is known for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
