Digitalis purpurea var. parviflora

Digitalis purpurea var. parviflora, commonly known as the miniature foxglove, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems that bear clusters of hooded flowers in shades of purple and pink. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardens.

This variety is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in medicine.
