Diospyros peruviana var. sprucei

Diospyros peruviana var. sprucei, commonly known as the Peruvian persimmon, is a species native to South America, particularly Peru and Ecuador.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant has smooth, greyish-brown bark and elliptical leaves that are alternate and leathery in texture. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in panicles, while the fruit is a small, oval, orange-red berry that ripens in late summer.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
