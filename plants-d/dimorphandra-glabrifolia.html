Dimorphandra glabrifolia

Dimorphandra glabrifolia, commonly known as the silky sycamore, is a deciduous tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The tree is distinguished by its smooth, pale bark and bipinnate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically appearing in yellow or greenish-yellow clusters, while its fruit is a legume pod containing several seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
