Dianthus divaricatus

Dianthus divaricatus, commonly known as the spreading campion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves and its clusters of pink or white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides. It is a perennial species that can reach heights of up to 30 centimeters.

Dianthus divaricatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
