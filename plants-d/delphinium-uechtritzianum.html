Delphinium uechtritzianum

Delphinium uechtritzianum, commonly known as the Uechtritzian larkspur, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family and is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces showy, blue to violet flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are palmately compound, with elongated, serrated leaflets.

Delphinium uechtritzianum is a perennial herb that thrives in well-drained, alkaline soils and is often found in alpine environments.
