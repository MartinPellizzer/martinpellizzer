Desmoncus osensis

Desmoncus osensis, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a climbing shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its showy, nocturnal white flowers that open at night. The plant produces small, oval-shaped fruits that contain seeds with a tuft of silky hairs. Its leaves are opposite and have a slightly hairy texture, while its stems are woody and often branched.

Desmoncus osensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
