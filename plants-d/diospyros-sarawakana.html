Diospyros sarawakana

Diospyros sarawakana, commonly known as the Sarawak persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to the rainforests of Borneo.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other well-known species like the ebony tree. The plant is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and large, palmate leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. Its fruit, which is typically orange to red in color, is edible when fully ripe and has a sweet, honey-like flavor.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food for various wildlife species.
