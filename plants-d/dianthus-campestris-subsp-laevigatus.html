Dianthus campestris subsp. laevigatus

Dianthus campestris subsp.

laevigatus, commonly known as the smooth garden pinks, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family. It is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and produces clusters of pink or white flowers with five petals.

The leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, and the plant is often found in meadows and disturbed soils. It is a perennial herb that thrives in temperate climates and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

The subspecies laevigatus is distinguished by its smoother leaf surfaces compared to other varieties within the species.
