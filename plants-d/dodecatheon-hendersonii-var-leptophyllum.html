Dodecatheon hendersonii var. leptophyllum

Dodecatheon hendersonii var. leptophyllum, commonly known as the Henderson's globemallow, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or purple, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds.

This variety is notable for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its adaptability to harsh environmental conditions.
