Derris virgilioides

Derris virgilioides, commonly known as the African snakeroot, is a climbing shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Leguminosae (Fabaceae) and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces elongated, serpentine pods that contain several seeds. It is notable for containing the alkaloid rotenone, which has insecticidal properties.

Derris virgilioides is often used in traditional medicine and as a natural pesticide.
