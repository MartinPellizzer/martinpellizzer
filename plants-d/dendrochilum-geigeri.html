Dendrochilum geigeri

Dendrochilum geigeri, commonly known as the Geiger's tree orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, orchid-like flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pale pink, bloom during the rainy season.

Dendrochilum geigeri is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
