Disa brachyceras

Disa brachyceras, commonly known as the dwarf disa, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to South Africa.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid with a compact growth habit and typically produces a single, showy flower. The plant features a short, stout stem with a single, erect flower that has a distinctive, spurred lip. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, arranged in a rosette at the base.

Disa brachyceras thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
