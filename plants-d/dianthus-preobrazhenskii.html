Dianthus preobrazhenskii

Dianthus preobrazhenskii, commonly known as the Siberian campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant pink or red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Dianthus preobrazhenskii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
