Dendrobium undulatum var. gracile

Dendrobium undulatum var. gracile, commonly known as the graceful phalaenopsis, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is an epiphytic plant that thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing on trees or rocks. The plant features long, slender pseudobulbs covered in fleshy leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. Its botanical characteristics include a sympodial growth habit and a complex root system adapted for nutrient absorption from its environment.

The flowers typically bloom in late winter or early spring, adding ornamental value to gardens and collections.
