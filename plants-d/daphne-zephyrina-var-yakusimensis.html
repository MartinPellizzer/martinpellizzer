Daphne zephyrina var. yakusimensis

Daphne zephyrina var. yakusimensis, commonly known as the Yakushima Daphne, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It features small, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring, typically in shades of pink and white. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy, providing ornamental value throughout the growing season. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Due to its ornamental appeal and delicate beauty, it is often cultivated in gardens for its spring blooms.
