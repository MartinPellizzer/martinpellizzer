Diasperus tremulus

Diasperus tremulus, commonly known as the trembling grass, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, narrow leaves. The plant is known for its delicate, trembling appearance, which is attributed to its weak stem structure and lightweight foliage. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Diasperus tremulus is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
