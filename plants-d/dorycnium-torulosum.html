Dorycnium torulosum

Dorycnium torulosum, commonly known as the creeping tansy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its root system is fibrous, and it is often found in disturbed soils such as roadsides and fields.

Dorycnium torulosum is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
