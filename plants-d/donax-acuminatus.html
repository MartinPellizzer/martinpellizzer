Donax acuminatus

Donax acuminatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves that are typically less than 1 inch in length. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, often in the axils of the leaves. Donax acuminatus is adapted to saline and alkaline soils, making it a hardy species in harsh environments.

Its root system helps prevent soil erosion, contributing to its ecological importance in desert ecosystems.
