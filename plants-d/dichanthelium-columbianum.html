Dichanthelium columbianum

Dichanthelium columbianum, commonly known as the Columbia panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green color. Its inflorescence consists of a cluster of spikelets that produce small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Botanically, it is characterized by its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it an adaptable species within its range.
