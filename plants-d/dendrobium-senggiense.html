Dendrobium senggiense

Dendrobium senggiense, commonly known as the Senggi orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, enabling it to survive in its humid, shaded environment. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and yellow, typically during the wet season. Its leaves are long and strap-like, often arranged in a rosette pattern around the pseudobulbs.

Dendrobium senggiense is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its unique floral display.
