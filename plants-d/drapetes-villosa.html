Drapetes villosa

Drapetes villosa, commonly known as the hairy drapetis, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, pendulous flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in cloud forests. Its leaves are hairy, giving it the common name, and it produces small, fragrant blooms that attract pollinators.

Drapetes villosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
