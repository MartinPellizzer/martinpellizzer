Digitalis purpurea var. mariana

Digitalis purpurea var. mariana, commonly known as the purple foxglove, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the western United States.

It features tall, spire-like stems that bear clusters of tubular, violet to purple flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and a distinctive glandular surface. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, often found in alpine and woodland habitats.

The flowers are pollinated primarily by bumblebees, which are attracted to their unique shape and nectar.
