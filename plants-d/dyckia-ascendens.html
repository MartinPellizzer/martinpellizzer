Dyckia ascendens

Dyckia ascendens, commonly known as the ascending dyckia, is a bromeliad native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-forming structure with long, narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of trichomes. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences that bear small, white or pale pink flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light.

Dyckia ascendens is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
