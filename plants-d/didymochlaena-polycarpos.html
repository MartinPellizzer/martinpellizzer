Didymochlaena polycarpos

Didymochlaena polycarpos, commonly known as the red-veined nettle, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family, which includes many stinging nettle species. The plant is characterized by its upright stems and serrated leaves with prominent red veins. Its small greenish-white flowers are arranged in clusters and are typically wind-pollinated.

The plant contains irritant hairs that can cause skin irritation upon contact.
