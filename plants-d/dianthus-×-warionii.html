Dianthus × warionii

Dianthus × warionii, commonly known as the hybrid carnation, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Dianthus chinensis and Dianthus barbatus.

It is a flowering plant in the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that come in a variety of colors such as pink, red, and white. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.

It is widely cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and as cut flowers due to its vibrant blooms and long-lasting flowers.
