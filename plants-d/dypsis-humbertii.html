Dypsis humbertii

Dypsis humbertii, commonly known as the Humbert's areca, is a species of palm native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnate leaves that are often feather-like in appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by dark blue berries. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
