Diasperus linearis

Diasperus linearis, commonly known as the slender desert grass, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It features narrow, linear leaves that are adapted to minimize water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in dense clusters. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Diasperus linearis is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone areas.
