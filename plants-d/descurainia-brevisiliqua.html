Descurainia brevisiliqua

Descurainia brevisiliqua, commonly known as the short-siliqua tansy, is an annual herbaceous plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and pinnately divided leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is native to regions of Europe and western Asia. It produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and sometimes endangered species.
