Dendrobium auricolor var. majus

Dendrobium auricolor var. majus, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a striking epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features elongated, cane-like pseudobulbs covered in a dense layer of white to pale green velvety hair. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange, often with a red or pink throat. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.

Due to its ornamental value, it is widely cultivated as a houseplant and in botanical gardens.
