Draba tibetica var. thomsonii

Draba tibetica var. thomsonii, commonly known as Thomson's rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Tibetan Plateau.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cold, arid conditions. Its leaves are basal, rosette-shaped, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs to reduce water loss.

It plays a vital ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its harsh habitat.
