Dioscorea hieronymi

Dioscorea hieronymi, commonly known as the African yam bean, is a climbing vine belonging to the family Dioscoreaceae.

It is native to tropical Africa and is characterized by its compound leaves and tuberous roots. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits that contain numerous seeds. Its tubers are edible and have been traditionally used as a food source in some regions.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
