Dryopteris lanipes

Dryopteris lanipes, commonly known as the hairy wood fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its stout, upright fronds that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky slopes. Its fronds are bipinnate, with a complex structure that includes fertile and sterile pinnae.

Dryopteris lanipes plays an important ecological role in forest ecosystems by contributing to soil stability and providing habitat for various organisms.
