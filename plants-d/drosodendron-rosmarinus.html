Drosodendron rosmarinus

Drosodendron rosmarinus, commonly known as rosemary bush, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic evergreen leaves and small, pink or white flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes. Its leaves are used in culinary and medicinal applications due to their essential oils.

Drosodendron rosmarinus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in temperate climates.
