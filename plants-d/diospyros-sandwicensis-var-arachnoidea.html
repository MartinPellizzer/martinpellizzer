Diospyros sandwicensis var. arachnoidea

Diospyros sandwicensis var. arachnoidea, commonly known as the Hawaiian persimmon, is a rare and endemic plant species native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves with a rough, hairy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, round fruits that turn red when mature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in montane rainforests.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered critically endangered.
