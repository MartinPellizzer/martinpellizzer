Deppia

Deppia, commonly known as the false hellebore, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Araceae.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a waxy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that are surrounded by a spadix, a characteristic feature of the Araceae family. Deppia is native to parts of Asia and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

It is important to note that all parts of the plant are toxic and should not be ingested.
