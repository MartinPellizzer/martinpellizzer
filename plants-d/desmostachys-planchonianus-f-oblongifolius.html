Desmostachys planchonianus f. oblongifolius

Desmostachys planchonianus f. oblongifolius, commonly known as the oblong-leafed sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands or along the edges of water bodies. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are clustered in panicles. Its leaves are elongated and have a distinct, linear shape, with a smooth texture.

This variety is notable for its ecological adaptability and role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
