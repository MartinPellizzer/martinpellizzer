Daphne pendula

Daphne pendula, commonly known as the weeping daphne, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its pendulous clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has simple, opposite, elliptical leaves that are dark green and glossy. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

Daphne pendula is valued in gardens for its ornamental flowers and is often used in borders or as a specimen plant.
