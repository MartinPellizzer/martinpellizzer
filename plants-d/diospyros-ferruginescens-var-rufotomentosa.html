Diospyros ferruginescens var. rufotomentosa

Diospyros ferruginescens var. rufotomentosa, commonly known as the red-tomentose persimmon, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, reddish-brown stems and oval, dark green leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into orange to reddish berries. Its fruit is edible when fully ripe and has a sweet, astringent flavor.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and unique fruit.
