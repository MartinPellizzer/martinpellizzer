Didymocarpus albomarginatus

Didymocarpus albomarginatus, commonly known as the white-margin tamarind, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with white margins. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

The species is adapted to grow in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soils.
