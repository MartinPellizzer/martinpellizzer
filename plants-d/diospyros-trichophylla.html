Diospyros trichophylla

Diospyros trichophylla, commonly known as the hairy persimmon, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other persimmon species and ebony trees. The plant is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into orange to red berries. Its leaves are typically elliptical with a rough texture, and the bark is dark and fissured.

The fruit is edible when fully ripe and has a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor.
