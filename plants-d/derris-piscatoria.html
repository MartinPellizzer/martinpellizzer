Derris piscatoria

Derris piscatoria, commonly known as the fish poison plant, is a woody shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pod-like fruits. The plant contains toxic alkaloids, particularly rotenone, which can be used as a natural fish poison. Its roots and stems are often harvested for their pesticidal properties.

Due to its toxic nature, it is typically handled with care in agricultural and ecological settings.
