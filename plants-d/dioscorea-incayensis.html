Dioscorea incayensis

Dioscorea incayensis, commonly known as the Andean yam, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary edible part of the plant. The plant typically grows in high-altitude areas with cool, temperate climates and requires well-drained soil. Its leaves are heart-shaped and its flowers are usually small and clustered in inflorescences.

The tubers are rich in starch and have been traditionally used as a food source by indigenous communities.
