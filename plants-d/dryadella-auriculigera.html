Dryadella auriculigera

Dryadella auriculigera, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
