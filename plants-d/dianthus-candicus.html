Dianthus candicus

Dianthus candicus, commonly known as the candytuft, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It features showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, blooming in spring and early summer. The plant has basal leaves that are linear and slightly hairy, and its stems are often glandular and can be slightly sticky. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Dianthus candicus is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
