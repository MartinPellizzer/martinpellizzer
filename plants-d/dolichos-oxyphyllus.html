Dolichos oxyphyllus

Dolichos oxyphyllus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf dolichos, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, trifoliate leaves with sharply toothed margins. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pale yellow or greenish in color. Its pods are elongated and contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.

Dolichos oxyphyllus is native to parts of Asia and is adapted to grow in arid or semi-arid environments.
