Dumasia nitida var. kurziana

Dumasia nitida var. kurziana, commonly known as the Kurz's white sage, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal practices for their purported medicinal properties.

The species is also valued in landscaping for its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
