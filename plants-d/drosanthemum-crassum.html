Drosanthemum crassum

Drosanthemum crassum, commonly known as the thick-leaved daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features thick, fleshy leaves that help it store water, adapting it to arid environments. Its flowers are typically yellow with a central disk, blooming in spring and summer.

Drosanthemum crassum is often grown as an ornamental plant for its attractive appearance and drought tolerance.
