Dryomenis plantaginea

Dryomenis plantaginea, commonly known as the common rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many cruciferous plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are small and white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Dryomenis plantaginea is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and is often found in alpine or arctic environments.
