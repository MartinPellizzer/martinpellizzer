Dypsis hiarakae

Dypsis hiarakae, commonly known as the Madagascar bamboo palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to Madagascar and is characterized by its slender, bamboo-like stems and feathery foliage. The plant typically grows as a solitary or clustered shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with small, narrow leaflets that give it a delicate appearance.

Dypsis hiarakae is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
