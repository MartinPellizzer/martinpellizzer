Dasyochloa argentina var. parodiana

Dasyochloa argentina var. parodiana, commonly known as Parodiana grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas, showing adaptability to arid conditions. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-gray hue, which helps reduce water loss.

The inflorescence consists of spikelets that are often clustered at the base of the plant, contributing to its ornamental value in certain ecological contexts.
