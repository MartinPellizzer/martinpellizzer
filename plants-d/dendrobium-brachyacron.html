Dendrobium brachyacron

Dendrobium brachyacron, commonly known as the short-stemmed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a compact growth habit with short, thick stems that often bear clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces white to pale yellow blooms with a distinctive, narrow lip that is often marked with dark veins. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is adapted to grow in forest understoreys.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
