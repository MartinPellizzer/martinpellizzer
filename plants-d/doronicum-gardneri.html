Doronicum gardneri

Doronicum gardneri, commonly known as the Gardner's leopard's bane, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features upright, branched stems and basal leaves that are deeply lobed and hairy. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. Its leaves are aromatic and have a distinctive, slightly bitter taste.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.
