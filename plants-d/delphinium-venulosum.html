Delphinium venulosum

Delphinium venulosum, commonly known as the veined larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with prominent veins, which give it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in alpine or rocky habitats.

Delphinium venulosum is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy blooms and toxic properties.
