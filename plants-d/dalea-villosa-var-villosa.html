Dalea villosa var. villosa

Dalea villosa var. villosa, commonly known as the hairy milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and compound leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Dalea villosa var. villosa is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its use in restoration projects.
