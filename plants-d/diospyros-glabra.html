Diospyros glabra

Diospyros glabra, commonly known as the smooth persimmon, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by orange to red, fleshy berries that are edible when ripe. Diospyros glabra is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine and cuisine.

Its growth habit and fruit characteristics make it an important species in tropical and subtropical regions.
