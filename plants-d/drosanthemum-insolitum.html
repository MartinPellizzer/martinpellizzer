Drosanthemum insolitum

Drosanthemum insolitum, commonly known as the velvetleaf gloxinia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that are typically a deep green with a waxy texture. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in warm, humid environments and prefers well-draining soil.

Drosanthemum insolitum is often cultivated as a decorative plant for its ornamental foliage and vibrant floral display.
