Draba bruniifolia

Draba bruniifolia, commonly known as the alpine mouse-ear cress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. This plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight. Draba bruniifolia is often found in high-altitude meadows and is adapted to cold climates with short growing seasons.

Its leaves are usually basal, rosette-forming, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.
