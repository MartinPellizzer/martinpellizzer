Diosphaera chalcidica

Diosphaera chalcidica, commonly known as the golden thimble cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Cactaceae and is characterized by its compact, globular shape and spiny, ribbed stems. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, adding a striking visual contrast to its green foliage. It is adapted to survive in hot, dry environments with minimal water requirements.

Diosphaera chalcidica is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and resilience.
