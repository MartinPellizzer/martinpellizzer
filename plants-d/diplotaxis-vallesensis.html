Diplotaxis vallesensis

Diplotaxis vallesensis, commonly known as the Valley mustard, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.

Diplotaxis vallesensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
