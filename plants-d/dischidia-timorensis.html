Dischidia timorensis

Dischidia timorensis, commonly known as the Timor bubble vine, is a climbing plant native to the island of Timor.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its unique, inflated stem segments that resemble small bubbles. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by red, fleshy fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found growing on trees or rocks in its natural habitat.

Dischidia timorensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
