Dioscorea pyrifolia var. diepenhorstii

Dioscorea pyrifolia var. diepenhorstii, commonly known as the Diepenhorst's yam, is a variant of the yam family native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are often used in traditional medicine and as a food source. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in warm, humid climates. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and often have a velvety texture, while its flowers are usually unisexual and clustered in inflorescences.

The species is valued for its nutritional content and has been studied for potential medicinal properties.
