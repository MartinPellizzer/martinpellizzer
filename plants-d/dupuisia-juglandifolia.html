Dupuisia juglandifolia

Dupuisia juglandifolia, commonly known as the Brazil nut tree, is a large evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and compound leaves that are opposite and pinnate. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by woody capsules containing the edible Brazil nuts. Its seeds are enclosed in a hard shell and require specific conditions to germinate.

The plant plays a crucial role in the ecosystem, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
