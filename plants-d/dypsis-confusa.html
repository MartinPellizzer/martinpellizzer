Dypsis confusa

Dypsis confusa, commonly known as the Madagascar palm, is a tropical plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiny, bottle-shaped trunk and a crown of feathery, pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or white in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect sunlight.

Dypsis confusa is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
