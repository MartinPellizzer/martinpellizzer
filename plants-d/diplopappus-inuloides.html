Diplopappus inuloides

Diplopappus inuloides, commonly known as the African dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble dandelions. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that develop into fluffy, white pappus structures for wind dispersal. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
