Diospyros ferrea f. angustata

Diospyros ferrea f. angustata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf persimmon, is a shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves and small, yellowish flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically covered in a fine, fuzzy coating. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in coastal areas or near freshwater sources.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally used in traditional medicine.
