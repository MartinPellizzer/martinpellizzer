Dianthera debilis

Dianthera debilis, commonly known as the slender bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It features delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, often with a subtle purple or pink hue. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs, contributing to its overall ornamental appeal.

Dianthera debilis is valued in horticulture for its elegant appearance and ability to thrive in cool, humid climates.
