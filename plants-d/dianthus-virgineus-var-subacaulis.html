Dianthus virgineus var. subacaulis

Dianthus virgineus var. subacaulis, commonly known as the dwarf carnation, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features compact, bushy growth with dense clusters of pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are narrow, grass-like, and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its compact appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant colors and long-lasting blooms.
