Dioscorea sieberi

Dioscorea sieberi, commonly known as the Chinese yam, is a perennial vine native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and tuberous roots. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by inedible, spiny fruits. Its tubers are used in traditional medicine and as a food source in some regions.

The botanical features of Dioscorea sieberi make it an important species in both horticulture and ethnobotanical studies.
