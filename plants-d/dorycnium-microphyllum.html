Dorycnium microphyllum

Dorycnium microphyllum, commonly known as the small-leaved goat's rue, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia, often found in dry, rocky soils. The plant has small, trifoliate leaves and produces yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes. Its seeds are encased in hairy pods, and it is known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.

Dorycnium microphyllum is also used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
