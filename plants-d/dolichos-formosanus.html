Dolichos formosanus

Dolichos formosanus, commonly known as the Taiwan horsegram, is a climbing legume native to Southeast Asia.

It features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters. The plant bears elongated, cylindrical pods that contain several oblong seeds. It is adapted to warm, tropical climates and thrives in well-drained soils.

Due to its nutritional value, it is cultivated for both human consumption and animal feed.
