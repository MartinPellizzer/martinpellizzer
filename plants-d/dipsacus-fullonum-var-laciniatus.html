Dipsacus fullonum var. laciniatus

Dipsacus fullonum var. laciniatus, commonly known as the cut-leaved teasel, is a biennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and heart-shaped leaves that are deeply lobed and often toothed. The plant produces purple to reddish-brown flower heads that are surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides and in waste areas.

The species is known for its historical use in the textile industry for cleaning wool.
