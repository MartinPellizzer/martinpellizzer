Didiscus cyaneus

Didiscus cyaneus, commonly known as the blue bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has a compact growth habit with hairy stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Didiscus cyaneus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
