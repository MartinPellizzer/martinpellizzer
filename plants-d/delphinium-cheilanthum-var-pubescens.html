Delphinium cheilanthum var. pubescens

Delphinium cheilanthum var. pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent larkspur, is a striking perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height, adorned with lance-shaped, bluish-green leaves that are densely covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces showy, spurred flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is a popular choice for gardeners in suitable climates.
