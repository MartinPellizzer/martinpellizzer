Dendrobium griffithianum

Dendrobium griffithianum, commonly known as the Griffith's orchid, is an epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, cane-like stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and yellow, often in clusters. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil and bright, indirect light.

Dendrobium griffithianum is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
