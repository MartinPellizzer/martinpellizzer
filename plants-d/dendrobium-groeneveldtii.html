Dendrobium groeneveldtii

Dendrobium groeneveldtii, commonly known as the Groeneveldt's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of fragrant, showy flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the surrounding soil.

Dendrobium groeneveldtii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
