Dendrobium variegatum

Dendrobium variegatum, commonly known as the variegated orchid, is a perennial epiphytic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its upright, cane-like stems that bear clusters of fragrant, showy flowers. The plant's leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in two rows along the stems. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light and requires well-draining potting media.

Dendrobium variegatum is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its long-lasting blooms.
