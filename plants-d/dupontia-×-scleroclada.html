Dupontia × scleroclada

Dupontia × scleroclada, commonly known as the sand verbena, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is native to the western United States, particularly in California and Oregon, where it thrives in sandy and gravelly soils. The plant is characterized by its erect, branching stems and clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are typically narrow and linear, adapted to reduce water loss in arid environments.

Dupontia × scleroclada is often found in coastal dunes and inland sandy habitats, showcasing its resilience to harsh, dry conditions.
