Dioscorea pteropoda

Dioscorea pteropoda, commonly known as the winged yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive wing-like structures on its stems. The plant produces tuberous roots that are often used in traditional medicine and as a food source. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture, and it flowers with small, pale yellow blooms.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
