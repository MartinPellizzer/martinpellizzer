Dioscorea sagittifolia var. lecardii

Dioscorea sagittifolia var. lecardii, commonly known as the arrowleaf yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and tuberous roots. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its tubers are often used in traditional medicine and as a food source in some cultures.

The varietal form, lecardii, is distinguished by specific morphological traits such as leaf shape and tuber structure.
