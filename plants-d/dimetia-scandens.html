Dimetia scandens

Dimetia scandens, commonly known as the climbing hibiscus, is a tropical vine native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant is characterized by its rapid growth habit and ability to climb using its twining stems. Its flowers bloom throughout the warm seasons, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Dimetia scandens is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant in tropical landscapes.
