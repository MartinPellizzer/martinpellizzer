Diphylleia

Diphylleia, commonly known as the "silver leaf plant," is a genus of flowering plants in the family Diphylleiaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its striking, waxy, silver-green leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow in color. Diphylleia species are often cultivated for their ornamental value due to their unique foliage.

They prefer shaded, moist environments and are relatively low maintenance once established.
