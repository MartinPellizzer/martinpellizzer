Dryopteris tamatana

Dryopteris tamatana, commonly known as the New Zealand tree fern, is a distinctive evergreen fern native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of large, pinnate fronds that can reach lengths of up to 3 meters. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its fronds are typically dark green and have a leathery texture, while the spore-producing structures are located on the underside of the fronds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
