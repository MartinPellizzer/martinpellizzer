Dianthus caryophyllus subsp. aristidis

Dianthus caryophyllus subsp.

aristidis, commonly known as the Armenian carnation, is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus. It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and produces showy, double-petaled flowers in shades of red, pink, and white.

The subspecies is distinguished by its elongated, narrow leaves and its ability to thrive in well-drained, rocky soils. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.

The plant is also valued for its historical use in perfumery and traditional medicine.
