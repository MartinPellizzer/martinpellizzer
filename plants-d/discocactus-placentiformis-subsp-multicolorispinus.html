Discocactus placentiformis subsp. multicolorispinus

Discocactus placentiformis subsp.

multicolorispinus, commonly known as the multicolored spiny discocactus, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of South America. It features a short, thick stem covered in dense, spiny bracts that provide protection against herbivores.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its green foliage. Its leaves are modified into spines, reducing water loss in its harsh environment.

This subspecies is notable for its vibrant, multicolored bracts, which vary in hue from green to red and orange.
