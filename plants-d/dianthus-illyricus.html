Dianthus illyricus

Dianthus illyricus, commonly known as the Balkan carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces showy, pink to red flowers with a distinctive frilly appearance. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it blooms from late spring to early summer.

Dianthus illyricus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
