Dactylorhiza purpurella var. majaliformis

Dactylorhiza purpurella var. majaliformis, commonly known as the purple orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect flowering stems that bear clusters of purple to pinkish-purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream sides. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, and it has a rhizomatous root system.

This orchid is part of the Orchidaceae family and is noted for its ornamental value in horticulture.
