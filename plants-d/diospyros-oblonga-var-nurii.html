Diospyros oblonga var. nurii

Diospyros oblonga var. nurii, commonly known as the Chinese persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its elongated, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by orange to red, fleshy fruits in the fall. Its fruit is edible, with a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor, and is often consumed fresh or used in traditional medicine.

The tree is valued for its ornamental value and its contribution to local ecosystems.
