Drosera villosa var. latifolia

Drosera villosa var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved sundew, is a carnivorous plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that secrete a sticky substance to trap insects. The plant typically grows in wet, acidic environments such as bogs and heathlands. Its leaves are broad and often have a waxy coating, enhancing its ability to retain moisture.

This variety is noted for its adaptability and is often cultivated by enthusiasts due to its unique appearance and ecological role.
