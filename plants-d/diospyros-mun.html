Diospyros mun

Diospyros mun, commonly known as the Chinese persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to China and parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, ovate leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The tree produces small, orange-red fruits that are edible when fully mature, though they are typically harvested when still unripe for better flavor. The bark is thick and fissured, and the wood is valued for its durability and fine grain.

It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for both its ornamental value and fruit production.
