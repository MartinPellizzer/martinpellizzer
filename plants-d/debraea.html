Debraea

Debraea, commonly known as the golden groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its yellow flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Debraea is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
