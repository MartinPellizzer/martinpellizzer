Dalbergia polyadelpha

Dalbergia polyadelpha, commonly known as the African bloodwood, is a small to medium-sized leguminous tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dark, hard wood and distinctive bark that often has a reddish or brownish hue. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in dense, terminal clusters, followed by flat, leathery pods containing angular seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

The species is valued for its durable timber and has cultural significance in some African communities.
