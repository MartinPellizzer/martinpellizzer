Digitaria barbinodis

Digitaria barbinodis, commonly known as hairy Digitaria, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and dense, fibrous root system. The leaves are narrow, with a hairy texture on both surfaces, contributing to its common name. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets.

This plant is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
