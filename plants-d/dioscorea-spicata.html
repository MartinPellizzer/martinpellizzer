Dioscorea spicata

Dioscorea spicata, commonly known as the spicate yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tendrils and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by cylindrical, fleshy fruits. Its tuberous roots are often used in traditional medicine and as a food source.

The botanical features of Dioscorea spicata make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
