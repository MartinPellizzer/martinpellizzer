Desmodium orizabanum

Desmodium orizabanum, commonly known as the velvet bean or cowpea bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces cylindrical pods containing several seeds, which are often used as a protein source for livestock. It has a deep root system that helps in nitrogen fixation, making it beneficial for soil fertility.

Additionally, it is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
