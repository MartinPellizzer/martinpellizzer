Delphinium cockerellii

Delphinium cockerellii, commonly known as the Cockerell's larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers in raceme clusters, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough, hairy texture.

Delphinium cockerellii is a member of the Ranunculaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
