Dendrochilum pholidotoides

Dendrochilum pholidotoides, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in humid climates.

Dendrochilum pholidotoides is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
