Disperis crassicaulis

Disperis crassicaulis, commonly known as the thick-stemmed disperis, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its robust, fleshy stems and showy, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange with distinctive markings, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Disperis crassicaulis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
