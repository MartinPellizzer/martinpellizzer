Delphinium caeruleum var. caeruleum

Delphinium caeruleum var. caeruleum, commonly known as the blue delphinium, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features striking spikes of blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are deeply lobed and covered in a fine down. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its botanical characteristics include a tall stem and showy inflorescences that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
