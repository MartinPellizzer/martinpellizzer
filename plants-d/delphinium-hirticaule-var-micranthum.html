Delphinium hirticaule var. micranthum

Delphinium hirticaule var. micranthum, commonly known as the short-stemmed larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features erect, sparsely branched stems that are covered with fine, soft hairs. The plant produces clusters of blue to violet flowers, which are typically arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
