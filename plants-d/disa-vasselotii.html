Disa vasselotii

Disa vasselotii, commonly known as the Vasselotii orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of vibrant, spurred flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically blooms during the summer months, showcasing its ornamental value in gardens and collections. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Disa vasselotii is also valued for its ecological role, providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and birds.
