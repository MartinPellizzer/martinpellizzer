Descantaria glandulosa

Descantaria glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers. The plant features hairy stems and leaves with glandular structures that secrete oils. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and botanical collections.
