Dodonaea lamponga

Dodonaea lamponga, commonly known as the red flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its striking red, tubular flowers that bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets that are often covered in fine hairs. Its bark is smooth and greyish, becoming fissured with age.

Dodonaea lamponga is relatively drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
