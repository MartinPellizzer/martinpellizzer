Dioscorea quartiniana var. cryptantha

Dioscorea quartiniana var. cryptantha, commonly known as the African yam bean, is a climbing perennial vine native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary storage organs. The plant produces small, white to yellowish flowers and heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its tubers are used as a food source in some regions and have been studied for their potential medicinal properties.

The species is also notable for its ability to thrive in poor soils and its role in traditional agricultural systems.
