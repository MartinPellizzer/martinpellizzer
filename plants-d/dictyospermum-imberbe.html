Dictyospermum imberbe

Dictyospermum imberbe, commonly known as the African star of Bethlehem, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 meter, featuring woody stems and dark green, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the summer months. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, and it is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

The plant has been used traditionally in some regions for its medicinal properties.
