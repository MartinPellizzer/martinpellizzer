Dissotis rupicola

Dissotis rupicola, commonly known as the rock dissotis, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, often climbing stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pale pink flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for rocky or coastal environments.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
