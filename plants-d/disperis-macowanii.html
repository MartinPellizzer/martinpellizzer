Disperis macowanii

Disperis macowanii, commonly known as the Macowan's orchid, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant has a slender, upright stem with few leaves and produces racemes of small, white or pale pink flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its rarity and habitat specificity, it is considered a conservation concern in some regions.
