Dianthera genistifolia

Dianthera genistifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf dianthera, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery-white pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Dianthera genistifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
