Dendrochilum galbanum

Dendrochilum galbanum, commonly known as the yellow-lipped orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow petals and lip, which are often adorned with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its flowers bloom in clusters and are known for their fragrant scent.

Dendrochilum galbanum is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
