Deguelia stuhlmannii

Deguelia stuhlmannii, commonly known as the pink wattle, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Australia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height, characterized by its dense, woody structure. The plant produces small, pink or red flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a waxy coating, aiding in water retention.

Deguelia stuhlmannii is also known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
