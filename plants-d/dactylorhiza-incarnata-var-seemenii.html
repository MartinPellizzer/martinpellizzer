Dactylorhiza incarnata var. seemenii

Dactylorhiza incarnata var. seemenii, commonly known as the purple marsh orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in wet, acidic habitats such as bogs and marshes. The plant features a tall, erect stem with several narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are violet to purple in color, arranged in a spike with a distinctive, compressed shape.

It is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable moisture conditions.
