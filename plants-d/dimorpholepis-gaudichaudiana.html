Dimorpholepis gaudichaudiana

Dimorpholepis gaudichaudiana, commonly known as the giant rockselva, is a striking plant native to the rainforests of Ecuador and Colombia.

It is characterized by its massive, palm-like leaves that can grow up to three meters in length, making it one of the largest ferns in the world. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on rocky outcrops or in dense understories. Its unique morphology includes a robust rhizome system that helps it anchor in nutrient-poor soils.

Dimorpholepis gaudichaudiana is also notable for its ability to produce large, showy spore-bearing structures that contribute to its ecological significance in tropical ecosystems.
