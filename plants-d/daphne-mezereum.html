Daphne mezereum

Daphne mezereum, commonly known as mezereon, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the genus Daphne in the family Thymelaeaceae, characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that ripen in late summer. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated margin, while its stems are often covered in a waxy, white coating.

Daphne mezereum is noted for its toxic properties, containing various poisonous compounds in its leaves, flowers, and berries.
