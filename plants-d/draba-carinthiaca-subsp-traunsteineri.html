Draba carinthiaca subsp. traunsteineri

Draba carinthiaca subsp.

traunsteineri, commonly known as the Traunsteiner's whitlow grass, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Central Europe. It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly habitats at high elevations, where it thrives in cold and sunny conditions. It has basal leaves that are linear to lanceolate, and its flowers are usually white or pale yellow.

This subspecies is relatively rare and is often found in mountainous areas of Austria and the eastern Alps.
