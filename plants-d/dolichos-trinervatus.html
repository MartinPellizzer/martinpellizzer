Dolichos trinervatus

Dolichos trinervatus, commonly known as the three-veined bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features compound leaves with three nerves, giving it its scientific name, and produces small, yellow flowers. The plant bears pods that contain edible seeds, which are rich in protein and nutrients. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in traditional agriculture.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
