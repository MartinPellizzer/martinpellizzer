Deluciris violacea

Deluciris violacea, commonly known as the violet bellflower, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, violet-colored flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

Deluciris violacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate, fragrant blooms.
