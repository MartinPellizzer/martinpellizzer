Dendrobium ventricosum

Dendrobium ventricosum, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a species of orchid native to Australia.

It is an epiphytic plant that grows on rocks and trees, often in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant has a pseudobulb structure that stores water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in harsh conditions. Its flowers are typically yellow or green with red or orange markings, and they bloom during the warmer months.

Dendrobium ventricosum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for horticultural purposes.
