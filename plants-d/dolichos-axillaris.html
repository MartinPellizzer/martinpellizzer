Dolichos axillaris

Dolichos axillaris, commonly known as the hairy bean or wild cowpea, is a climbing legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features compound leaves with three leaflets and produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are often clustered in axillary inflorescences. The plant bears elongated, hairy pods containing several seeds, which are typically oval and brown in color. It is adapted to grow in dry conditions and is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Dolichos axillaris is valued for its nutritional seeds and has traditional uses in some parts of Africa and Asia.
