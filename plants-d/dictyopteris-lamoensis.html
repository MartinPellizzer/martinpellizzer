Dictyopteris lamoensis

Dictyopteris lamoensis, commonly known as the Lamoen Island sea palm, is a species of marine algae belonging to the family Dictyopteridaceae.

It is characterized by its leathery, dark green fronds that are typically broad and strap-like in shape. This algae thrives in shallow, temperate coastal waters, often found attached to rocky substrates. Its structure consists of a short, stoloniferous holdfast and a branching thallus with a complex arrangement of fronds.

Dictyopteris lamoensis plays an important role in marine ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various marine organisms.
