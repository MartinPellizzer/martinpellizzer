Dioscorea elephantipes

Dioscorea elephantipes, commonly known as the elephant foot yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy rhizomes that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by heart-shaped, greenish-white fruits. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

The rhizomes are used as a food source and have been traditionally valued for their nutritional content.
