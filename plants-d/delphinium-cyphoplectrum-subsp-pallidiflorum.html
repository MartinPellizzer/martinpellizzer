Delphinium cyphoplectrum subsp. pallidiflorum

Delphinium cyphoplectrum subsp.

pallidiflorum, commonly known as the pale-flowered larkspur, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family. It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems and clusters of blue to purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in mountainous regions. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and the flowers are showy with five petals.

It is native to the western United States and is often found in alpine meadows.
