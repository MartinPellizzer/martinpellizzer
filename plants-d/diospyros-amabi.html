Diospyros amabi

Diospyros amabi, commonly known as the African persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other persimmon and ebony species. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are often clustered in panicles, followed by round, orange to red fruits with a sweet, edible pulp. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptic with a leathery texture.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine and as a source of edible fruit.
