Dioscorea multicolor var. chrysophylla

Dioscorea multicolor var. chrysophylla, commonly known as the golden-leaf yam, is a variant of the dioscorea genus native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its striking yellow foliage, which contrasts with its typical green leaves in other varieties. The plant produces tuberous roots that are often used in traditional medicine and as a food source. Its growth habit is vine-like, with the ability to climb using tendrils.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is noted for its ornamental value due to its vibrant leaf coloration.
