Dalbergia laccifera

Dalbergia laccifera, commonly known as the Indian rosewood, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its dense, dark-colored wood that is highly valued for furniture and musical instruments. The plant has bipinnate leaves and produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are followed by flat, pod-like fruits.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in lowland forests and secondary growth areas.
