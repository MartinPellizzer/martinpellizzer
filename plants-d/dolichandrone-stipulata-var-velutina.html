Dolichandrone stipulata var. velutina

Dolichandrone stipulata var. velutina, commonly known as velvetleaf dolichandrone, is a perennial shrub native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by elongated, woody fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and traditional herbal practices.
