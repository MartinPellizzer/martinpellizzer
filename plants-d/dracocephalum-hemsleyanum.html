Dracocephalum hemsleyanum

Dracocephalum hemsleyanum, commonly known as Hemsley's dragonhead, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or blue, arranged in whorls along the stem. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Dracocephalum hemsleyanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
