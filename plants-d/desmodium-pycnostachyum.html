Desmodium pycnostachyum

Desmodium pycnostachyum, commonly known as hairy sicklepod, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its leaves are trifoliate, meaning they consist of three leaflets, and the stems are often hairy, giving it its common name.

Desmodium pycnostachyum is often found in disturbed soils and can be an invasive species in certain areas.
