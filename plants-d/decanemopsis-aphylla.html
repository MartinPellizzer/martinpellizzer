Decanemopsis aphylla

Decanemopsis aphylla, commonly known as the leafless bellflower, is a unique plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its lack of leaves, which is an adaptation to its harsh, dry environment. The plant produces striking, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. Despite its lack of foliage, it is capable of photosynthesis through its stems.

This species is often found growing in rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal in xerophytic gardens.
