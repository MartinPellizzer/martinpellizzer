Dennstaedtia pilosella

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its delicate, hairy fronds that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its fronds are usually bipinnate, with a distinctive, arching shape.
