Dendrochilum leuserense

Dendrochilum leuserense, commonly known as the Leuser Valley tree orchid, is a rare and endemic orchid species found in the Leuser Ecosystem of Indonesia.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a unique growth habit, characterized by its upright, woody stems and clustered pseudobulbs. The plant produces strikingly ornate flowers with intricate patterns and vibrant colors, typically in shades of yellow and orange. Its botanical features include a complex root system adapted to nutrient-poor soils and a specialized symbiotic relationship with mycorrhizal fungi.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered a vulnerable species requiring conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
