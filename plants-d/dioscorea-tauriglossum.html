Dioscorea tauriglossum

Dioscorea tauriglossum, commonly known as the Taurian yam, is a climbing vine native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture, and it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some regions for both horticultural and therapeutic purposes.
