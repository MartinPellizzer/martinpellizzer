Delphinium roylei

Delphinium roylei, commonly known as the Himalayan larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers arranged in dense spikes, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a rough texture.

Delphinium roylei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
