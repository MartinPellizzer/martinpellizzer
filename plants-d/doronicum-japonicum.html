Doronicum japonicum

Doronicum japonicum, commonly known as the Japanese leopard's bane, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It features upright stems with lance-shaped, dark green leaves that have a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long-lasting blooms.
