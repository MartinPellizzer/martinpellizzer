Dimorphanthes coronopifolia

Dimorphanthes coronopifolia, commonly known as the crownleaf daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette of narrow, silver-green leaves and produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of white and yellow. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is drought-tolerant and often used in rock gardens due to its ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.
