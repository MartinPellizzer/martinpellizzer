Draba heterocoma subsp. archipelagi

Draba heterocoma subsp.

archipelagi, commonly known as the Alaska Draba, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions of North America. It typically grows in rocky, alpine, or arctic tundra habitats, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions.

The plant features basal rosettes of dark green, hairy leaves and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in the spring. It is characterized by its compact growth form and adaptations to harsh environmental conditions.
