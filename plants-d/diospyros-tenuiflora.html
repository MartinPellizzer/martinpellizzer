Diospyros tenuiflora

Diospyros tenuiflora, commonly known as the slender-flowered ebony, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and small, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by small, round fruits that turn dark purple when mature. It is valued for its hard, dense wood, which is used in traditional craftsmanship and as a source of dye.

The species is also noted for its ecological importance in tropical forests.
