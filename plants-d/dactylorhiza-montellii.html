Dactylorhiza montellii

Dactylorhiza montellii, commonly known as the mountain orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and mountainous regions. The plant has a pseudobulb structure that stores water and nutrients, supporting its growth in less hospitable conditions. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with a distinctive lip that aids in pollination by insects.

Dactylorhiza montellii is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its ornamental value and ecological importance.
