Digitaria acuminatissima var. conformis

Digitaria acuminatissima var. conformis, commonly known as the narrowleaf digitaria, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides, pastures, and sandy soils. The plant has slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a prominent midrib. Its inflorescence consists of a loose cluster of spikelets, each bearing small, two-lobed flowers.

It is adapted to warm climates and is often found in areas with low soil fertility.
