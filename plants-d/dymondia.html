Dymondia

Dymondia, commonly known as silver grass or dymondia succulent, is a genus of flowering plants native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plants feature narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hair, giving them a silvery appearance. Dymondia produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow or orange, typically blooming in spring.

These hardy, drought-tolerant plants are popular in rock gardens and xeriscaping due to their low maintenance and ornamental value.
