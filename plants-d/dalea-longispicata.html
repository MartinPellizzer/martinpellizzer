Dalea longispicata

Dalea longispicata, commonly known as the long-spined milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in desert or semi-arid environments. It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides forage for wildlife.

Dalea longispicata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
