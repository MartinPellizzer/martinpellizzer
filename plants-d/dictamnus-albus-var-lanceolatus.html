Dictamnus albus var. lanceolatus

Dictamnus albus var. lanceolatus, commonly known as white dictamnus or lanceleaf dictamnus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features aromatic, fragrant leaves that emit a strong, spicy scent when crushed. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its stems are usually woody at the base and covered with a distinctive, flaky bark.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual and aromatic qualities.
