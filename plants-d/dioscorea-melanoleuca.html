Dioscorea melanoleuca

Dioscorea melanoleuca, commonly known as black yam, is a vine species native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by tuberous roots that are its primary economic product. Its tubers are used as a food source and have been traditionally used in various medicinal applications.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.
