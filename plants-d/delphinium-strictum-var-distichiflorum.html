Delphinium strictum var. distichiflorum

Delphinium strictum var. distichiflorum, commonly known as the Alpine larkspur, is a herbaceous perennial native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It features tall, slender stems that bear dense clusters of blue to violet flowers, which are arranged in a distinctive double-spike pattern. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stem. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in gardens and alpine settings.
