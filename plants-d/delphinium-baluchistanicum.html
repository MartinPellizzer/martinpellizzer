Delphinium baluchistanicum

Delphinium baluchistanicum, commonly known as the Baluchistan larkspur, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Pakistan and Afghanistan.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and clusters of blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats at high elevations. Its leaves are palmately divided and have a distinctive, somewhat hairy texture.

Delphinium baluchistanicum is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its striking floral display.
