Dryandra lindleyana var. mellicula

Dryandra lindleyana var. mellicula, commonly known as the honeyflower dryandra, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and dense clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that emit a sweet, honey-like fragrance. The plant produces small, round, leathery fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
