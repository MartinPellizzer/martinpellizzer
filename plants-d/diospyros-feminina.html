Diospyros feminina

Diospyros feminina, commonly known as the female persimmon, is a species of flowering plant in the Ebenaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers. The fruit is typically a small, orange-red berry that ripens in the fall.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has limited use in traditional medicine.
