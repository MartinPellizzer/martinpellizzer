Dudleya cymosa subsp. crebifolia

Dudleya cymosa subsp.

crebifolia, commonly known as the denseleaf dudleya, is a succulent plant native to California. It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, which helps it store water.

The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it well-suited for rock gardens and arid landscapes.

Due to its slow growth and attractive appearance, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping and conservation gardens.
