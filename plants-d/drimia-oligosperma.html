Drimia oligosperma

Drimia oligosperma, commonly known as the narrow-leaved African lily, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features strap-like, narrow leaves that are typically blue-green in color, and it produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers in spring. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant. It is often found in grasslands and rocky areas, and its bulbs are used in traditional medicine.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
