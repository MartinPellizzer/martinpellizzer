Dianthus pungens proles insignitus

Dianthus pungens proles insignitus, commonly known as the showy campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of bright pink or red flowers with five petals. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained conditions.
