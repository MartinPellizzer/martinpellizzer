Dendropemon subsessilis

Dendropemon subsessilis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded forest undergrowth, where it thrives in humid and cool conditions. The plant has slender, erect stems and produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in racemes. Its leaves are narrow and sessile, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Dendropemon subsessilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
