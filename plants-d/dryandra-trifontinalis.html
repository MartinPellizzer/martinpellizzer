Dryandra trifontinalis

Dryandra trifontinalis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf dryandra, is a member of the Proteaceae family and is native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves and distinctive floral structure, which consists of a single central style surrounded by multiple stamens. The plant typically grows in sandy soils and is adapted to the dry, Mediterranean climate of its native region. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring, adding ornamental value to the landscape.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
