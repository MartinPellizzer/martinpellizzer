Draba atlantica

Draba atlantica, commonly known as the Atlantic rockcress, is a small, flowering plant native to the coastal regions of the British Isles and western Europe.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbages, and is characterized by its simple, basal leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, coastal habitats and is adapted to withstand strong winds and salt spray.

It blooms from late spring to early summer, producing numerous tiny seeds that are dispersed by the wind.
