Dendrochilum cucullatum

Dendrochilum cucullatum, commonly known as the hooded orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, featuring a distinctive hood-like lip that encloses the reproductive organs. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in tropical rainforests. Its flowers are usually small and fragrant, with a variety of colors including white, yellow, and pink.

Dendrochilum cucullatum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
