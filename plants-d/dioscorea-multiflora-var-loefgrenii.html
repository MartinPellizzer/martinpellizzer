Dioscorea multiflora var. loefgrenii

Dioscorea multiflora var. loefgrenii, commonly known as the Loefgren's yam, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are used in traditional medicine. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits. Its tubers are often harvested for their medicinal properties and are also used in local cuisines.

The species is noted for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.
