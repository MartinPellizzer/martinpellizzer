Dischisma struthioloides

Dischisma struthioloides, commonly known as the desert star.

It is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. The plant features a sprawling growth habit with fleshy, succulent stems that store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in clusters and blooming in late spring or early summer.

The species is adapted to full sun and well-drained soils, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.
