Diplotaxis tenuifolia var. integrifolia

Diplotaxis tenuifolia var. integrifolia, commonly known as the African dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial with upright, branching stems and alternate, pinnately divided leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by fluffy, white seed heads resembling dandelion fluff. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
