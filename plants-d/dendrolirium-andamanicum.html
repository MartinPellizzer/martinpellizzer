Dendrolirium andamanicum

Dendrolirium andamanicum, commonly known as the Andaman lily, is a flowering plant native to the Andaman and Nicobar Islands.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that resemble lilies. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, and it produces a single, upright stem with a cluster of fragrant, white or pale pink flowers.

Dendrolirium andamanicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
