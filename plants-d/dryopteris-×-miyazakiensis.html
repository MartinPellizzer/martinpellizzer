Dryopteris × miyazakiensis

Dryopteris × miyazakiensis, commonly known as the Miyazaki fern, is a hybrid species within the Dryopteris genus, characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds.

It is native to Japan and thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forests or along stream banks. The plant features a rhizomatous growth habit, with fertile fronds that produce spores on the undersides of the leaves. Its foliage is typically a vibrant green, with a subtle, elegant appearance that makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The Miyazaki fern is valued for its ability to thrive in humid conditions and its aesthetic appeal in both natural and cultivated settings.
