Daviesia decurrens subsp. decurrens

Daviesia decurrens subsp.

decurrens, commonly known as the narrow-leaved daviesia, is a shrub native to southern Australia. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, narrow leaves.

The plant produces bright yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, which are clustered in racemes. It is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and open forests.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists and horticulturists due to its ornamental value and ecological significance.
