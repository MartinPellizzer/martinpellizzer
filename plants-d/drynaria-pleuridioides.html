Drynaria pleuridioides

Drynaria pleuridioides, commonly known as the golden huperzia, is a fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and distinctive, clustered growth habit. The plant features leathery, dark green fronds with a prominent midrib and serrated margins. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Huperziaceae and is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in herbal medicine.
