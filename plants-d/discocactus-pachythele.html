Discocactus pachythele

Discocactus pachythele, commonly known as the spiny discocactus, is a unique cactus species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, flattened, disc-like stems that are covered in sharp, spiny tubercles. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking contrast to its rugged appearance. Its thick, waxy cuticle helps it retain moisture in its harsh, dry environment.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is often found in rocky or sandy soils with minimal rainfall.
