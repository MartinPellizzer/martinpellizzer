Didiscus albiflorus

Didiscus albiflorus, commonly known as the white-flowered didiscus, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its distinctive, showy white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, bean-like pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
