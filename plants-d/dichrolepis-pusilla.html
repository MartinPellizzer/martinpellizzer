Dichrolepis pusilla

Dichrolepis pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf spiderwort, is a small flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, delicate blue or purple flowers. It is a perennial species that thrives in temperate climates with moderate humidity.

Due to its compact size and ornamental flowers, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens as a ground cover or filler plant.
