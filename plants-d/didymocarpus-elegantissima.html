Didymocarpus elegantissima

Didymocarpus elegantissima, commonly known as the elegant waxtree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in cyathia, typical of the genus. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, and it is often grown for its ornamental value in gardens.

Didymocarpus elegantissima thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a versatile addition to tropical and subtropical landscapes.
