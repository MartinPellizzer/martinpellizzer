Dischisma

Dischisma, commonly known as the frostweed, is a genus of flowering plants native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and showy, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plants typically grow in rocky or sandy soils and are adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Dischisma species are often found in open habitats such as plains, hillsides, and along streambanks.

They are valued for their ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
