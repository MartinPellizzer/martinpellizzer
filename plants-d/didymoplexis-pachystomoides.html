Didymoplexis pachystomoides

Didymoplexis pachystomoides, commonly known as the giant flame vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that are typically red or orange with a distinctive, elongated shape. The plant has a vigorous growth habit and can reach lengths of several meters, making it an ornamental choice for trellises or pergolas. Its leaves are usually large, heart-shaped, and glossy, adding to its visual appeal.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
