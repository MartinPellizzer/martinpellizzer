Darwinia taxifolia

Darwinia taxifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved Darwinia, is a flowering plant native to south-eastern Australia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and striking inflorescences. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces spikes of showy, pinkish-red flowers that bloom in spring. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is adapted to dry, well-drained soils.

Darwinia taxifolia is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
