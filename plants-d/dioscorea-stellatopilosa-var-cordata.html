Dioscorea stellatopilosa var. cordata

Dioscorea stellatopilosa var. cordata, commonly known as the star-veined yam, is a member of the Dioscoreaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial vine characterized by its distinctive, cordate (heart-shaped) leaves and hairy stems. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by tuberous roots that are often harvested for culinary or medicinal purposes. Its botanical features include a complex root system and a climbing habit that allows it to thrive in shaded, humid environments.

The species is noted for its ornamental value as well as its potential economic importance in traditional medicine.
