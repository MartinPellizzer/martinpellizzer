Delphinium cooperi

Delphinium cooperi, commonly known as the Cooper's larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems that can reach heights of up to six feet. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem.

Delphinium cooperi is a member of the Ranunculaceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
