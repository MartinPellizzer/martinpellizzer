Dasiphora fruticosa tenuifolia

Dasiphora fruticosa tenuifolia, commonly known as the slender prairie smoke, is a low-growing perennial plant native to North America.

It features wiry, branching stems covered in soft, grayish-green foliage. The plant produces delicate, feathery flowers that resemble smoke, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, adapted to withstand arid conditions.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in prairies and open woodlands.
