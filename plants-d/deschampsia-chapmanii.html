Deschampsia chapmanii

Deschampsia chapmanii, commonly known as Chapman's bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to western North America.

It is characterized by its fine, bluish-green foliage and forms dense, tufted clumps. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as meadows, marshes, and along stream banks. Its flowers are small, spike-like inflorescences that appear in late spring to early summer.

Deschampsia chapmanii plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
