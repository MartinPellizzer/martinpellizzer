Dichanthium assimile

Dichanthium assimile, commonly known as the slender false brome, is a grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Dichanthium assimile is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in arid landscapes.
