Dichaea hamata

Dichaea hamata, commonly known as the hairy dichaea, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, linear leaves and produces small, white or pale green flowers arranged in a spike. Its roots are often covered in a fine, hairy coating, which gives it its common name.

Dichaea hamata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated by enthusiasts of tropical orchids.
