Dianthus serrulatus var. gaditanus

Dianthus serrulatus var. gaditanus, commonly known as the Spanish carnation, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe.

It features dense clusters of bright pink, bicolor, or white flowers with frilled petals, blooming from spring to early summer. The plant has linear, serrated leaves that are gray-green in color, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its prolonged flowering period and drought tolerance.
