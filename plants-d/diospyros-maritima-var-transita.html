Diospyros maritima var. transita

Diospyros maritima var. transita, commonly known as the coastal persimmon, is a shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptic to ovate in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by orange to red berries. It thrives in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy soils and salt spray.

The fruit is edible and has a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor.
