Dryopteris hirtopilosa

Dryopteris hirtopilosa, commonly known as the hairy-veined wood fern, is a species of fern native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its robust, upright fronds that are deeply divided into lance-shaped segments. The fronds are covered with fine, hairy structures, giving the plant its common name. It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in rich, well-drained soils.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appearance and is often used in horticultural settings.
