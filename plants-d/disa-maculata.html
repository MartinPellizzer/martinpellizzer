Disa maculata

Disa maculata, commonly known as the spotted disa, is a species of orchid native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, clustered blooms during the summer months.

Disa maculata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
