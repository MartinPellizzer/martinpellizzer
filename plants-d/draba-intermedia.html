Draba intermedia

Draba intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate whitlowgrass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches heights of 5 to 15 centimeters. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in a raceme, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are basal, rosette-forming, and have a distinctive blue-green color.

Draba intermedia is often found in alpine and arctic regions, thriving in rocky or sandy soils.
