Dendranthema × cuneifolium

Dendranthema × cuneifolium, commonly known as Shasta daisy, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features showy, white ray florets surrounding a yellow disk in its composite flower heads, which are typical of the Asteraceae family. The plant has dark green, heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged in a rosette at the base. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its botanical characteristics make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
