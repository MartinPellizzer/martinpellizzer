Diospyros lotus var. laevis

Diospyros lotus var. laevis, commonly known as the smooth persimmon, is a variant of the persimmon tree native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, hairless leaves and produces small, orange-red fruits that are typically harvested when fully ripe. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree, often growing in moist, well-drained soils. Its fruit is valued for its sweet, honey-like flavor and is commonly consumed fresh or used in culinary applications.

The species is also notable for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and seasonal fruiting.
