Dianthus plumarius

Dianthus plumarius, commonly known as the carnation, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is native to Europe, western Asia, and northern Africa, and it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers in shades of pink, red, white, and purple, with distinctive frilled petals. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.

Dianthus plumarius is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
