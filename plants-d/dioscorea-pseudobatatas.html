Dioscorea pseudobatatas

Dioscorea pseudobatatas, commonly known as the purple yam, is a climbing herbaceous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary edible part. The plant produces large, heart-shaped leaves and bears small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into capsules containing numerous seeds. Its tubers are rich in starch and have been traditionally used in various cuisines for their nutty flavor and culinary versatility.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for both its nutritional value and ornamental appeal.
