Dianthus stenocephalus

Dianthus stenocephalus, commonly known as the narrow-headed carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that typically range in color from pink to red. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, clustered flowers with five petals. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Dianthus stenocephalus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its long-lasting blooms.
