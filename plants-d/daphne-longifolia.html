Daphne longifolia

Daphne longifolia, commonly known as the winter daphne, is a deciduous shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. The plant has smooth, grayish bark and lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink, are borne on short, hairy stems.

Daphne longifolia is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and winter blooming habit.
