Diphyes amblyantha

Diphyes amblyantha, commonly known as the slender swamp lily, is a herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of Southeast Asia.

It features elongated, strap-like leaves that are often submerged or floating in water. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread across shallow water bodies.

The botanical characteristics of Diphyes amblyantha make it well adapted to aquatic environments, with specialized structures for gas exchange and nutrient absorption.
