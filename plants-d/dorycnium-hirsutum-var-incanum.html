Dorycnium hirsutum var. incanum

Dorycnium hirsutum var. incanum, commonly known as the hairy vetch, is a perennial legume native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, sprawling stems and trifoliate leaves with small, rounded leaflets. The plant produces small, yellow pea-like flowers that bloom in early summer, followed by elongated pods containing several seeds. It is often used as a cover crop due to its nitrogen-fixing ability and its capacity to suppress weeds.

Its adaptability to various soil types makes it a valuable plant in ecological and agricultural contexts.
