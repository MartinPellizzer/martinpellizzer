Drypetes sassandraensis

Drypetes sassandraensis, commonly known as the African cherry or African redwood, is a tropical tree native to Central and West Africa.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and elliptical, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by orange-red, fleshy fruits that contain numerous seeds. It is often found in moist lowland forests and is valued for its hard, durable wood.

Additionally, it plays a role in local ecosystems and has been used in traditional medicine.
