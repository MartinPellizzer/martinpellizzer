Dioscorea hastatissima

Dioscorea hastatissima, commonly known as the serrated yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its hairy, angular stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by tuberous roots that are often used in traditional medicine. Its tubers are typically harvested for their nutritional value and have a slightly sweet, starchy flavor.

Dioscorea hastatissima is also valued for its potential medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects.
