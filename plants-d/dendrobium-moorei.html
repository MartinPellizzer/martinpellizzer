Dendrobium moorei

Dendrobium moorei, commonly known as the Moore's orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, cane-like stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and yellow, often blooming during the warmer months. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Dendrobium moorei is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
