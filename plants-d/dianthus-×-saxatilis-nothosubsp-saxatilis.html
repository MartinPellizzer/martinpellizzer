Dianthus × saxatilis nothosubsp.

saxatilis, commonly known as the rock campion, is a hybrid plant that exhibits characteristics of its parent species, Dianthus sylvaticus and Dianthus deltoides. It is a flowering plant in the Caryophyllaceae family, typically found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

The plant produces showy, pink or white flowers with five petals, arranged in a cluster at the top of slender stems. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to poor soil conditions.
