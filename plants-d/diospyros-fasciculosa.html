Diospyros fasciculosa

Diospyros fasciculosa, commonly known as the clustered persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in fascicles, giving it its scientific name. Its fruit is a small, round berry that changes from green to red as it matures.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
