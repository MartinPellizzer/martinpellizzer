Dianthus chinensis var. sylvaticus

Dianthus chinensis var. sylvaticus, commonly known as the woodland carnation, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its delicate, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to white, often with a distinctive red or purple central stripe. The plant typically grows in rocky or woodland habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and partial shade. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces small, seed-like fruits known as capsules.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and fragrant scent.
