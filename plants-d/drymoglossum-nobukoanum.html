Drymoglossum nobukoanum

It is characterized by its small, dense growth habit and is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rock crevices. It reproduces through spores, which are released from specialized structures called sporangia located on the upper surface of the thallus.

This moss is valued for its ecological role in maintaining soil stability and moisture retention in its native habitats.
