Doria chromochaeta

Doria chromochaeta, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Doria chromochaeta is often found in disturbed soils and can become invasive in certain regions.
