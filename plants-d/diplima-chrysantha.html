Diplima chrysantha

Diplima chrysantha, commonly known as the golden cupflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and prairies. Its showy yellow flowers bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Diplima chrysantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
