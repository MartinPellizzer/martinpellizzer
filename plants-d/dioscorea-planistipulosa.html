Dioscorea planistipulosa

Dioscorea planistipulosa, commonly known as the broad-leaf yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces tuberous roots that are often used in traditional medicine and as a food source. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is known for its rapid growth habit.

The leaves are typically broad and have a distinct, waxy texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
