Diospyros loureiroana var. heterotricha

Diospyros loureiroana var. heterotricha, commonly known as the Brazilian persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that give way to edible, orange-red fruits with a sweet, honey-like flavor.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is valued for its ornamental and culinary uses.
