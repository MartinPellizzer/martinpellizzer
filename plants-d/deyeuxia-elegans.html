Deyeuxia elegans

Deyeuxia elegans, commonly known as the slender false panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Asia and Australia.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands, wet meadows, and along streambanks. The plant has slender, erect stems that can reach up to 1 meter in height, with narrow, linear leaves that are often slightly hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, cylindrical spikelet that is usually pale green to golden in color.

Deyeuxia elegans is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
