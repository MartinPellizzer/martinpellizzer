Dactylorhiza lapponica subsp. rhaetica

Dactylorhiza lapponica subsp.

rhaetica, commonly known as the Lapland twayblade, is a terrestrial orchid belonging to the Orchidaceae family. It is a small herbaceous plant with a single, slender stem that bears two opposite leaves.

The plant produces a spike of small, greenish-white flowers, which are typically arranged in a raceme. It thrives in moist, acidic habitats such as bogs and heaths, often in northern regions of Europe and Asia.

This subspecies is adapted to cold climates and is typically found in subarctic and alpine environments.
