Delphinium aconiti

Delphinium aconiti, commonly known as theaconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that bear clusters of striking blue to purple flowers. Its leaves are compound, with serrated edges, and its roots are tuberous, which contribute to its medicinal and toxic properties.

Delphinium aconiti contains potent alkaloids, making it both a valuable and dangerous plant in botanical and pharmacological contexts.
