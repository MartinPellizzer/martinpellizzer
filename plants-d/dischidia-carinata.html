Dischidia carinata

Dischidia carinata, commonly known as the zigzag vine, is a species of flowering plant in the family Apocynaceae.

It is a climbing or trailing plant characterized by its distinctive, wavy, and often zigzagging stems. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a leathery texture with a slightly pubescent surface. It produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Native to Southeast Asia, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and adaptability to various growing conditions.
