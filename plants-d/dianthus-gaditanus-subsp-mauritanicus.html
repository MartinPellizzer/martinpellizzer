Dianthus gaditanus subsp. mauritanicus

Dianthus gaditanus subsp.

mauritanicus, commonly known as the Moroccan carnation, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of North Africa. It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers with five petals.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
