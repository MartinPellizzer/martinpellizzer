Dianthus lusitanus var. legionensis

Dianthus lusitanus var. legionensis, commonly known as the Legion's carnation, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, double-layered petals that range in color from pink to red. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or borders.

Due to its ornamental value and historical significance, it is occasionally cultivated in horticultural settings.
