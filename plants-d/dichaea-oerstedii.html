Dichaea oerstedii

Dichaea oerstedii, commonly known as the Oersted's dichaea, is a member of the Bromeliaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid-like plant that typically grows in shaded, moist environments. The plant features a short, stocky stem with clustered, lance-shaped leaves that form a rosette. Its flowers are usually small and greenish-white, often arranged in a spike.

Dichaea oerstedii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
