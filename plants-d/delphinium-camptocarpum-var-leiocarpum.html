Delphinium camptocarpum var. leiocarpum

Delphinium camptocarpum var. leiocarpum, commonly known as the smooth-seeded larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers in racemes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a rough texture and a strong, bitter odor.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and open woodlands.
