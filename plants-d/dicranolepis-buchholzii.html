Dicranolepis buchholzii

Dicranolepis buchholzii, commonly known as the Buchholz's haircap, is a species of moss belonging to the family Dicranaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, turfy growth habit and dark green to reddish-brown stems. The leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a distinct costa that extends to the apex, and they often have a whitish or translucent appearance. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rock crevices.

This moss is notable for its ecological role in soil stabilization and moisture retention in its habitat.
