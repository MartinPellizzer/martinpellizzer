Dahlia zimapani

Dahlia zimapani, commonly known as the Zimapan dahlia, is a striking tuberous-rooted perennial native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that come in a wide range of colors, including red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with hairy stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are borne in clusters on terminal shoots, and they bloom from late spring to early autumn.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its bold, colorful display.
