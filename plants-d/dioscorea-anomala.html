Dioscorea anomala

Dioscorea anomala, commonly known as the African yambean, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary storage organs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant. Its tubers are used as a food source in some regions and have been studied for their potential medicinal properties.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.
