Dioscorea kamoonensis var. vera

Dioscorea kamoonensis var. vera, commonly known as the Kashmir yam, is a climbing vine native to the Himalayan regions of Pakistan and India.

It belongs to the genus Dioscorea, which includes various species of yams and wild yams. The plant produces tuberous roots that are often used in traditional medicine and as a food source. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its flowers are typically white or pale green.

The plant thrives in temperate climates and is adapted to grow in rocky and mountainous terrain.
