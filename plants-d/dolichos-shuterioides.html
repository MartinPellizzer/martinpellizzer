Dolichos shuterioides

Dolichos shuterioides, commonly known as the African bean or wild cowpea, is a climbing legume native to southern Africa.

It features compound leaves with three to five elliptical leaflets and produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in racemes. The plant bears pods that are typically hairy and contain several round, edible seeds. It thrives in warm, tropical climates and is often found in open woodlands and along riverbanks.

Dolichos shuterioides is valued for its nutritional seeds and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
