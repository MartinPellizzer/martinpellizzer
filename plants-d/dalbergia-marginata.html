Dalbergia marginata

Dalbergia marginata, commonly known as the African rosewood, is a tropical tree native to regions of Africa, particularly in countries like Tanzania and Kenya.

It is characterized by its dark, dense wood that is highly valued for its aesthetic and structural properties. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are small, fragrant, and typically white or pale yellow. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas and riverine forests.

The tree is also known for its slow growth and long lifespan, making it a significant component of its native ecosystems.
