Dianthus sabuletorum var. pyrenaicus

Dianthus sabuletorum var. pyrenaicus, commonly known as the Pyrenean campion, is a flowering plant native to the Pyrenees region.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments. The plant features pink or red flowers with five petals, arranged in a cluster at the end of each stem. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in fine hairs.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
