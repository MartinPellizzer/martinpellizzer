Diplacus graniticola

Diplacus graniticola, commonly known as the granitic trumpetvine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking orange to red tubular flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats. Its stems are woody and covered in fine, fuzzy hairs, while its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped.

Diplacus graniticola is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees.
