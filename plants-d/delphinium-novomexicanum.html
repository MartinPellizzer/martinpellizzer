Delphinium novomexicanum

Delphinium novomexicanum, commonly known as the New Mexico larkspur, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, spindly stems and clusters of blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils in open habitats such as grasslands and woodlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem, while its flowers have five petals with a distinctive spurred petal at the back.

Delphinium novomexicanum is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
