Dinteracanthus asper

Dinteracanthus asper, commonly known as the rough-stemmed dinteracanthus, is a rare and unique succulent native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems and small, fleshy leaves that help it conserve water in its harsh environment. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its rugged appearance. Its growth is slow, making it a prized specimen among succulent enthusiasts.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
