Drymocallis glandulosa subsp. glabrata

Drymocallis glandulosa subsp.

glabrata, commonly known as the smooth goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to North America. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant has smooth, glabrous stems and leaves, which distinguish it from other goldenrod species. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
