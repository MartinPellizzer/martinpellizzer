Dicliptera villosior

Dicliptera villosior, commonly known as the hairy dicliptera, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and hairy, often silver-tomentose leaves. The plant typically grows in moist or wetland habitats, such as marshes and seasonally flooded areas. Its inflorescence consists of a single, cylindrical spikelet that is often surrounded by bracts.

Dicliptera villosior is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping or as a forage crop.
