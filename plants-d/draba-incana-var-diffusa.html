Draba incana var. diffusa

Draba incana var. diffusa, commonly known as the hairy rockcress, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats. Its leaves are basal, hairy, and often lobed, while its flowers are usually white or pale yellow.

Draba incana var. diffusa is adapted to harsh environmental conditions, making it a resilient species in cold, arid climates.
