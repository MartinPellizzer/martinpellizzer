Dioscorea ferruginea

Dioscorea ferruginea, commonly known as the red spider lily, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, tuberous roots that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom in the spring. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and have a rough texture.

The plant thrives in humid environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
