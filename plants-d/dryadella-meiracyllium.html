Dryadella meiracyllium

Dryadella meiracyllium, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and grows in moist, woodland habitats. It is part of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its delicate, daisy-like inflorescence.

Dryadella meiracyllium is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
