Dianthus transsylvanicus

Dianthus transsylvanicus, commonly known as the Transylvanian carnation, is a flowering plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows in meadows and rocky soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces numerous small, delicate petals that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Dianthus transsylvanicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
