Dalea lutea var. gigantea

Dalea lutea var. gigantea, commonly known as the giant yellow milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces bright yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its compound leaves are pinnately arranged with small, oval leaflets.

It has a deep root system that helps stabilize soil and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.
