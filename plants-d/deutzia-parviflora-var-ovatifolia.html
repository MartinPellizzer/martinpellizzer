Deutzia parviflora var. ovatifolia

Deutzia parviflora var. ovatifolia, commonly known as the ovate-leaved deutzia, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features slender, arching stems that produce clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers in early summer. The leaves are ovate, with a slightly serrated margin, and they turn a vibrant yellow in the fall. This plant thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
