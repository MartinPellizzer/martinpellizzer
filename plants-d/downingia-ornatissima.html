Downingia ornatissima

Downingia ornatissima, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and meadows. It has basal leaves that are often hairy and covered in fine white or grayish pubescence.

Downingia ornatissima is a perennial species that thrives in cool, temperate climates and is often found in areas with high humidity.
