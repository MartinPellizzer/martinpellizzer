Dypsis lantzeana var. simplicifrons

Dypsis lantzeana var. simplicifrons, commonly known as the Madagascar bamboo palm, is a striking tropical plant native to Madagascar.

It features slender, bamboo-like stems that are often used as decorative elements in landscaping and indoor settings. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that eventually give way to dark blue berries. Its foliage consists of long, narrow, dark green leaves that are arranged in a fan-like pattern.

This variety is well-suited for warm, humid climates and requires bright, indirect light to thrive.
