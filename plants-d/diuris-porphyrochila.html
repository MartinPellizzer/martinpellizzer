Diuris porphyrochila

Diuris porphyrochila, commonly known as the purple-veined spider-orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southeastern Australia.

It typically grows in heathlands and open forests, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant features a slender, hairy stem with a single, narrow leaf and a spike of pale green to purple flowers. Its flowers have a distinctive spider-like appearance, with a narrow, hooded labellum.

Diuris porphyrochila is known for its striking coloration and is often found in areas with acidic soils and partial shade.
