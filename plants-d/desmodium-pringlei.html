Desmodium pringlei

Desmodium pringlei, commonly known as Pringle's burrlegume, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces distinctive, spiny pods that contain several seeds, which are adapted for dispersal by animals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and disturbed areas.

Desmodium pringlei is also valued for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
