Dianthus heptaneurus

Dianthus heptaneurus, commonly known as the seven-lobed campion, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of pink or red flowers with five petals. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It blooms from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Dianthus heptaneurus is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in garden settings.
