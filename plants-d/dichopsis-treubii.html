Dichopsis treubii

Dichopsis treubii, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically produces violet-colored flowers that bloom in clusters at the end of its stems. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Dichopsis treubii is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
