Dianthus monspessulanus var. suaveolens

Dianthus monspessulanus var. suaveolens, commonly known as sweet pinks, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, mound-like growth habit and profuse display of pink to red flowers with prominent white veins. The flowers typically bloom in early summer and are borne on slender, branching stems. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are gray-green in color, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in well-drained soil.
