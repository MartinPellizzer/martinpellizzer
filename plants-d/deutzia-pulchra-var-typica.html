Deutzia pulchra var. typica

Deutzia pulchra var. typica, commonly known as the rose-of-Sharon or Chinese lilac, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features clusters of fragrant, white to pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and a woody stem that becomes more prominent with age. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Deutzia pulchra var. typica is often used in ornamental landscaping for its showy blooms and moderate growth habit.
