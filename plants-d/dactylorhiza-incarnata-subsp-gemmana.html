Dactylorhiza incarnata subsp. gemmana

Dactylorhiza incarnata subsp.

gemmana, commonly known as the gemman orchid, is a species of orchid native to parts of Europe and North Africa. It is a terrestrial herb with a slender, erect stem and a single, showy flower that typically blooms in late spring or early summer.

The plant has a rhizome and produces a single leaf that is usually dark green and strap-like in shape. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and grows in nutrient-poor soils.

The flower is characterized by its pale pink to white coloration with a distinct lip that is often darker in hue.
