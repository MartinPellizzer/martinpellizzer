Didymocheton fraserianus

Didymocheton fraserianus, commonly known as the Fraser's bottle tree, is a member of the Meliaceae family and is native to northern Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive bottle-shaped trunk and long, drooping branches. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a slightly hairy texture.

Didymocheton fraserianus is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.
