Draba kurilensis

Draba kurilensis, commonly known as Kuril mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to the Russian Far East and the northern part of Japan.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. This species is characterized by its low-growing habit, with basal leaves and small, white or pale yellow flowers. It typically thrives in moist, rocky habitats and is often found in alpine or subarctic regions.

Draba kurilensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems as a food source for various insects.
