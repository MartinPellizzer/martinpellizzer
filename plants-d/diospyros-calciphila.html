Diospyros calciphila

Diospyros calciphila, commonly known as the limestone persimmon, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is well adapted to calcareous soils, thriving in areas with high limestone content. The plant features thick, waxy leaves that help reduce water loss in dry conditions. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are followed by edible, sweet fruits that are an important food source for local wildlife.

Diospyros calciphila plays a significant role in stabilizing soil and supporting biodiversity in its native habitat.
