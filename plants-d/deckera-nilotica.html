Deckera nilotica

Deckera nilotica, commonly known as the African sandalwood, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, fragrant wood. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a waxy coating. It produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits.

The tree is valued for its durable and scented timber, which has been used traditionally for furniture and incense.
