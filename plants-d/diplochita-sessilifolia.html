Diplochita sessilifolia

Diplochita sessilifolia, commonly known as the sessile-leaf mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of various host trees.

It belongs to the family Viscaraceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are sessile, meaning they lack stalks. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its berries are usually red or orange and contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can significantly affect the health of its host trees if left unchecked.
