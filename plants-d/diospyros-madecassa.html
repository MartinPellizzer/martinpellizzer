Diospyros madecassa

Diospyros madecassa, commonly known as the Madagascar persimmon, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, orange-red fruits with a sweet, astringent flavor. Its fruits are often consumed fresh or used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
