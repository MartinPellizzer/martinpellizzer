Dioscorea fractiflexa

Dioscorea fractiflexa, commonly known as the flexible yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its twining stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by edible tuberous roots. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated for its nutritional value and traditional medicinal uses.
