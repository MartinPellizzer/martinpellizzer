Delima hebecarpa

Delima hebecarpa, commonly known as the yellow bell fruit, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its yellow, bell-shaped fruits that ripen in the dry season. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a rough texture, while its flowers are small and white with a strong fragrance.

The fruit is edible when ripe and is often used in local cuisines for its sweet and tangy flavor.
