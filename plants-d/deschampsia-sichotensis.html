Deschampsia sichotensis

Deschampsia sichotensis, commonly known as the Andean feathergrass, is a perennial grass native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and delicate, feathery plumes that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its slender stems and low-growing habit make it well adapted to high-altitude conditions.

Deschampsia sichotensis is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate poor soil conditions.
