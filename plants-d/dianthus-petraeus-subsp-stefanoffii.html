Dianthus petraeus subsp. stefanoffii

Dianthus petraeus subsp.

stefanoffii, commonly known as the Stefanoff's rock campion, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans. It typically grows in rocky and limestone soils, thriving in well-drained, sunny environments.

The plant produces clusters of pink or red flowers with five petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of narrow, gray-green leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
