Diospyros caliginosa

Diospyros caliginosa, commonly known as the smokefruit tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree produces small, round fruits that are typically red or orange when mature, with a sweet, edible pulp. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
