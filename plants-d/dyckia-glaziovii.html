Dyckia glaziovii

Dyckia glaziovii, commonly known as the Glaziov's Dyckia, is a striking bromeliad native to Brazil.

It features rosettes of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a dense, fibrous coating. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences bearing numerous small, white to pale blue flowers. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Dyckia glaziovii is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
