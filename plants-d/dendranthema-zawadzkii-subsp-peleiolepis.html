Dendranthema zawadzkii subsp. peleiolepis

Dendranthema zawadzkii subsp.

peleiolepis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disc flowers.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It features narrow, linear leaves and produces showy yellow to orange flowers in the summer months.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
