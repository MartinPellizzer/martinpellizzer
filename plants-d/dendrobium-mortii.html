Dendrobium mortii

Dendrobium mortii, commonly known as the Mort's orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, showy flowers in shades of yellow and orange. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, utilizing its aerial roots to absorb moisture and nutrients from the environment. Its flowers bloom during the dry season, often in response to seasonal rainfall patterns.

Dendrobium mortii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in tropical gardens and greenhouses.
