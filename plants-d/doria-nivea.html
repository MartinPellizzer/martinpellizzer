Doria nivea

Doria nivea, commonly known as the white doria, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking white petals and dark green foliage. The plant typically grows in high-altitude regions with cool, moist climates. Its flowers bloom in the spring and are often used in traditional medicine by local communities.

Doria nivea is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
