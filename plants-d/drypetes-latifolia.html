Drypetes latifolia

Drypetes latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf drypetes, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate, ovate to elliptic leaves with a glossy upper surface. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by woody capsules that contain numerous seeds.

It is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its timber and traditional medicinal uses.
