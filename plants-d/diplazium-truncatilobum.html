Diplazium truncatilobum

Diplazium truncatilobum, commonly known as the narrow-lobed shield fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive fronds that are pinnately compound with truncate or rounded lobes. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are often covered in a dense layer of brownish, hairy structures.

Diplazium truncatilobum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
