Diploprion

Diploprion, commonly known as the double-toothed plant, is a rare and intriguing species within the botanical world.

It is characterized by its unique leaf structure, which features two distinct tooth-like projections on each leaf margin. This plant typically thrives in specific, often humid and shaded environments, such as tropical rainforests. Its flowers are small and clustered, often displaying a range of colors from pale yellow to deep orange.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, Diploprion is of great interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
