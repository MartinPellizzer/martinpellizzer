Dahlia campanulata

Dahlia campanulata, commonly known as the bell dahlia, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that come in a variety of colors. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its foliage consists of heart-shaped, dark green leaves, and it thrives in full sun with well-drained soil.

Dahlia campanulata is popular in gardens for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for cut flowers.
