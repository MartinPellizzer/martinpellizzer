Didymocheton pachypodus

Didymocheton pachypodus, commonly known as the thick-stemmed screw tree, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, woody stems and palmate leaves with five to seven leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in panicles, followed by woody, spiny fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local cultures.
