Draba imbricatifolia

Draba imbricatifolia, commonly known as the rock scabwort, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a small, mat-forming perennial that typically grows in alpine and arctic environments. The plant features basal leaves that are typically lanceolate and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. Its flowers are small, yellow, and arranged in a raceme, blooming in late spring to early summer.

Draba imbricatifolia is adapted to harsh conditions, with a deep root system and a short growing season.
