Dendrocalamopsis oldhamii f. revoluta

Dendrocalamopsis oldhamii f. revoluta, commonly known as the revolute bamboo, is a unique variant of the original species.

It is characterized by its distinctive revolute (rolled under) leaf margins, which set it apart from other forms of the species. The plant is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is native to Southeast Asia. It typically grows as a clump-forming plant with slender, erect culms.

Its ornamental value lies in its elegant foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
