Diuris luteola

Diuris luteola, commonly known as the yellow spider-orchid, is an Australian orchid species native to southeastern Australia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and heathlands. The plant features a slender, erect stem with a single or few flowers that are often yellow or greenish-yellow in color. Its flowers have a distinctive spider-like appearance, with narrow, strap-shaped petals and sepals.

Diuris luteola is known for its ephemeral blooming period, usually occurring in late winter to early spring.
