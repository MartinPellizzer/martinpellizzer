Dactylorhiza majalis var. traunsteinerioides

Dactylorhiza majalis var. traunsteinerioides, commonly known as the Traunsteiner's orchid, is a rare and delicate orchid species native to parts of Europe.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a short, hairy stem and linear leaves. The plant produces a single, slender spike bearing several small, pale green to white flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and bogs.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and limited distribution, making it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
