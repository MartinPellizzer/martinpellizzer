Deutzia longifolia var. densitomentosa

Deutzia longifolia var. densitomentosa, commonly known as the dense-hairy deutzia, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant features densely hairy stems and leaves, which give it its varietal name. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its showy flowers attract pollinators, contributing to both aesthetic and ecological value in the landscape.
