Dioscorea escuintlensis

Dioscorea escuintlensis, commonly known as the Escuintla yam, is a species of tuberous plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its underground tubers, which are the primary economic and nutritional component of the plant. The plant typically grows in humid, tropical environments and thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped and can reach up to 30 cm in length, while its flowers are small and unisexual.

The tubers of Dioscorea escuintlensis are valued for their starchy content and are used in traditional cuisines across its native range.
