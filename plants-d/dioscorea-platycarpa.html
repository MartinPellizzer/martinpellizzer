Dioscorea platycarpa

Dioscorea platycarpa, commonly known as the broad-fruited yam, is a species of flowering plant in the family Dioscoreaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands. The plant is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are often used as a food source in various traditional cuisines. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and can grow quite large, while the flowers are small and usually yellow or greenish in color.

Dioscorea platycarpa is also valued for its medicinal properties in some cultures.
