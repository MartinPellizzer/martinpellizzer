Dracaena trifasciata

Dracaena trifasciata, commonly known as the snake plant, is a member of the Asparagaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its upright, sword-shaped leaves that are usually dark green with distinctive light-green or white stripes. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and requires minimal watering, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by red, berry-like fruits.

Dracaena trifasciata is also noted for its air-purifying properties, enhancing indoor air quality.
