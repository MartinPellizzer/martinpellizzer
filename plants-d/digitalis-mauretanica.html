Digitalis mauretanica

Digitalis mauretanica, commonly known as the Moroccan foxglove, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from purple to pink. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Digitalis mauretanica is also noted for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
