Dunalia arborescens var. campanulata

Dunalia arborescens var. campanulata, commonly known as the bell-shaped dunalia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, shrub-like growth habit and produces clusters of bell-shaped, pale blue to violet flowers. The leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a velvety texture. This species thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and adaptability to various garden environments.
