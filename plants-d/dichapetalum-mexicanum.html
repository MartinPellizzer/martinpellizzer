Dichapetalum mexicanum

Dichapetalum mexicanum, commonly known as the Mexican star-of-Bethlehem, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, star-shaped white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces distinctive, elongated seed pods that split open to release its seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its ornamental value and unique appearance make it a notable species in horticultural collections.
