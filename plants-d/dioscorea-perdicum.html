Dioscorea perdicum

Dioscorea perdicum, commonly known as the wild yam, is a climbing vine belonging to the Dioscoreaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, Africa, and the Americas. The plant produces tuberous roots that are rich in starch and have been used traditionally in various medicinal practices. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

The flowers are typically unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant.
