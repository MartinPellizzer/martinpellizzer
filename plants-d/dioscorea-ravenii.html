Dioscorea ravenii

Dioscorea ravenii, commonly known as the Raven's vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant. Its tubers, which are the underground storage organs, are often used in traditional medicine and have been studied for their potential medicinal properties.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
