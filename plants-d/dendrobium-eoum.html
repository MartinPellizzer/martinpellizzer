Dendrobium eoum

Dendrobium eoum, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and the production of long, pendulous inflorescences. The plant features leathery, strap-shaped leaves and produces showy, yellowish-orange flowers with a distinct fragrance. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
