Deschampsia cespitosa subsp. macrothyrsa

Deschampsia cespitosa subsp.

macrothyrsa, commonly known as the tall wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to North America. It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, including sandy and loamy soils.

The plant produces tall, slender culms with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a panicle, with each spikelet containing several small, two-lobed flowers.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological benefits, including erosion control and as a forage plant for livestock.
