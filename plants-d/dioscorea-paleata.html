Dioscorea paleata

Dioscorea paleata, commonly known as the pale yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary part used for culinary and medicinal purposes. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers and elongated, cylindrical fruits that contain numerous seeds. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a rough texture, and the stems are hairy and often twining around supporting structures.

The tubers of Dioscorea paleata are rich in starch and have been used traditionally in various cuisines and traditional medicine practices.
