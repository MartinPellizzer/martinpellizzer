Decaschistia rufa

Decaschistia rufa, commonly known as the red-stemmed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, reddish stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its small, spike-like inflorescence bears numerous tiny flowers that are wind-pollinated.

Decaschistia rufa is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens.
