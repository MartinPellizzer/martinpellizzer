Dendromecon harfordii

Dendromecon harfordii, commonly known as the Harford's tree mallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring woody stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy yellow flowers with a distinctive orange center, which bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species suitable for arid and semi-arid climates.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in xeriscaping and native plant gardens.
