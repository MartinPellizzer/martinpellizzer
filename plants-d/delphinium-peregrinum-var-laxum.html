Delphinium peregrinum var. laxum

Delphinium peregrinum var. laxum, commonly known as the laxum larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers in racemes, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough texture, often with a bluish-green hue.

This variety is known for its hardiness and ability to thrive in well-drained, rocky soils.
