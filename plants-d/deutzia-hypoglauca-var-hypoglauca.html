Deutzia hypoglauca var. hypoglauca

Deutzia hypoglauca var. hypoglauca, commonly known as the silverleaf deutzia, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a silvery-gray pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers in early to mid-summer, which are typically borne on the previous year's growth. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability in garden settings.
