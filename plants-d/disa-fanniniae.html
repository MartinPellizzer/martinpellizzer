Disa fanniniae

Disa fanniniae, commonly known as the Fannin's Disa, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers are often arranged in a spike and bloom during the summer months.

Disa fanniniae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
