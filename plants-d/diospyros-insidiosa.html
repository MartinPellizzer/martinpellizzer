Diospyros insidiosa

Diospyros insidiosa, commonly known as the insidious ebony, is a species of flowering plant in the family Ebenaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, particularly the Democratic Republic of the Congo and Tanzania. The plant is characterized by its dark, dense wood, which is highly valued for its durability and aesthetic qualities. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by small, round fruits.

The species is often found in moist, shaded forest environments, contributing to the biodiversity of its native ecosystems.
