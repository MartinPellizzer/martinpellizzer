Dischidia myrtillus

Dischidia myrtillus, commonly known as the red-stemmed fig.

It is a climbing or trailing plant native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its distinctive red stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by fleshy, reddish-purple fruits. It is often grown as a houseplant due to its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in bright, indirect light.

Dischidia myrtillus is also known for its ability to climb using aerial roots, making it a popular choice for hanging baskets or shelf displays.
