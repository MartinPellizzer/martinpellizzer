Desmodium canescens

Desmodium canescens, commonly known as white indigo.

It is a flowering plant species native to the Americas, particularly found in the southern United States and parts of South America. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family, also known as the legume or bean family, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. Desmodium canescens is a perennial herb that often grows in disturbed soils and can be found in grasslands and open woodlands.

It is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for ecological restoration projects.
