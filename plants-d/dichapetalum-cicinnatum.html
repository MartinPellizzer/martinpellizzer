Dichapetalum cicinnatum

Dichapetalum cicinnatum, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine silvery down. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by woody, spiny fruits containing seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

The silverleaf tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
