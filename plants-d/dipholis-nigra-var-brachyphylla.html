Dipholis nigra var. brachyphylla

Dipholis nigra var. brachyphylla, commonly known as the short-stemmed black orchid, is a rare orchid species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, velvety stems and elongated, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking, dark purple to black flowers with a unique, intricate pattern. It thrives in shaded, humid environments, often found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a conservation concern.
