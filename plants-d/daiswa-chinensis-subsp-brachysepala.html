Daiswa chinensis subsp. brachysepala

Daiswa chinensis subsp.

brachysepala, commonly known as the short-sepal daisya, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern China. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust root systems.

The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, showy yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
