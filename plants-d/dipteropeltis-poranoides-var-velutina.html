Dipteropeltis poranoides var. velutina

Dipteropeltis poranoides var. velutina, commonly known as the velvet-leafed dipteropeltis, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its velvety, dark green foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has a fibrous root system and thrives in humid, shaded environments with well-drained soil. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a smooth, waxy texture.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
