Digitalis tomentosa

Digitalis tomentosa, commonly known as the Downy Foxglove, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It features tall, slender stems that bear whorled clusters of tubular, violet to purple flowers. The leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and covered in a dense layer of soft, fuzzy hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This plant is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and is also noted for its medicinal properties, though it is toxic if ingested.
