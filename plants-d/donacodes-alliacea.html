Donacodes alliacea

Donacodes alliacea, commonly known as the garlic fern, is a member of the family Polypodiaceae and is native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a small, evergreen fern that typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. It is characterized by its aromatic foliage, which emits a mild garlic scent when crushed.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation, it is often grown in gardens and indoor settings.
