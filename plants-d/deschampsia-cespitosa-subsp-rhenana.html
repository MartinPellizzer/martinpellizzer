Deschampsia cespitosa subsp. rhenana

Deschampsia cespitosa subsp.

rhenana, commonly known as slender wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist to wet habitats such as meadows, marshes, and along stream banks.

Its rhizomatous growth allows it to form dense mats, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
