Desmodium rigidum

Desmodium rigidum, commonly known as rigid ticktrefoil, is a perennial legume native to various regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has a creeping growth habit and produces small, elliptical pods that contain several seeds. It is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils, and it plays a role in nitrogen fixation due to its association with rhizobia bacteria.

Its botanical features make it both a useful forage plant and a potential weed in agricultural settings.
