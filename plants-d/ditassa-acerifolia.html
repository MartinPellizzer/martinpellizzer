Ditassa acerifolia

Ditassa acerifolia, commonly known as the maple-leaved tree, is a species native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive palmate leaves that resemble those of maple trees, with five to seven lobes. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized evergreen tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside, with a smooth texture.

The flowers are small and inconspicuous, appearing in clusters, while the fruit is a spherical drupe.
