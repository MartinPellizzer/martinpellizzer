Diplocentrum recurvum

Diplocentrum recurvum, commonly known as the African blackwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its dark, dense wood, which is highly valued for its durability and fine grain. The plant has simple, opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface and small, inconspicuous white flowers. It produces small, fleshy, red or orange fruits that contain seeds.

The tree is often found in moist, shaded habitats and is important in local ecosystems and traditional medicine.
