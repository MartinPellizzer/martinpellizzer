Desmodium lanceolatum

Desmodium lanceolatum, commonly known as sicklepod, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces slender, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Due to its ability to fix nitrogen, it plays a role in soil enrichment and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
