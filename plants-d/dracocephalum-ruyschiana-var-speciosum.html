Dracocephalum ruyschiana var. speciosum

Dracocephalum ruyschiana var. speciosum, commonly known as the showy dragonhead, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright stems covered in dense, hairy foliage, and its flowers are typically blue to purple in color, arranged in whorled clusters. The plant produces small, four-lobed flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
