Diploknema grandiflora

Diploknema grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered knema, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by fleshy, orange-red berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its aromatic wood and has traditional uses in local medicine.
