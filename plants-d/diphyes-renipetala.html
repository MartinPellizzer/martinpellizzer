Diphyes renipetala

Diphyes renipetala, commonly known as the twin-leaf water plant, is a unique aquatic plant characterized by its distinctive two-lobed leaves that float on the water's surface.

Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, with a waxy coating that helps reduce water loss. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in ponds and marshes. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow, emerging from the water surface.

The roots of Diphyes renipetala are adapted to absorb nutrients from the sediment, making it an important component of aquatic ecosystems.
