Dianthus rupicola var. bertolonii

Dianthus rupicola var. bertolonii, commonly known as the Bertoloni's campion, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of southern Europe.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-red flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in well-drained, sunny conditions. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it is often found in mountainous habitats.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its charming appearance.
