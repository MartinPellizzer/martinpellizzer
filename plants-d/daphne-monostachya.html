Daphne monostachya

Daphne monostachya, commonly known as the narrow-leaved daphne, is a deciduous shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, often appearing in late winter to early spring. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white or grayish hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Daphne monostachya is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its early blooming habit.
