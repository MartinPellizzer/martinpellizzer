Dacrydium pectinatum

Dacrydium pectinatum, commonly known as the New Zealand rimu, is a coniferous tree native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and dense, evergreen foliage. The leaves are needle-like and arranged in spirals, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It produces small, cone-like structures that contain the plant's seeds.

This species plays an important ecological role in New Zealand's forests and is valued for its timber.
