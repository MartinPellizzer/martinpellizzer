Didymocarpus podocarpus

Didymocarpus podocarpus, commonly known as the mountain pepper, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing aromatic seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in montane forests.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
