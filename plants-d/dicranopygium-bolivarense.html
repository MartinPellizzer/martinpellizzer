Dicranopygium bolivarense

Dicranopygium bolivarense, commonly known as the Bolivian feather moss, is a species of moss belonging to the family Dicranaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and features elongated, slender leaves that are often covered in a layer of small, translucent cells. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in temperate and subpolar regions. Its reproductive structures, including capsules, are borne on short, erect stalks, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Dicranopygium bolivarense plays an important ecological role in soil stabilization and moisture retention in its native habitats.
