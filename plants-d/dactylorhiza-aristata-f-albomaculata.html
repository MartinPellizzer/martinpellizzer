Dactylorhiza aristata f. albomaculata

Dactylorhiza aristata f. albomaculata, commonly known as the white-spotted marsh orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of pale pink or white flowers with distinctive white spots. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, preferring moist, acidic soils. It has linear, grass-like leaves and produces a single spike of flowers.

This orchid is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in botanical gardens.
