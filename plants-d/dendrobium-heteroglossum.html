Dendrobium heteroglossum

Dendrobium heteroglossum, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, erect stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height. The plant produces clusters of showy, yellow-orange flowers with dark red or purple markings. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Dendrobium heteroglossum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and collections.
