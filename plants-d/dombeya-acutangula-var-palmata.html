Dombeya acutangula var. palmata

Dombeya acutangula var. palmata, commonly known as the palmate dombeya, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its palmately lobed leaves, which give it a distinctive, maple-like appearance. The plant produces clusters of showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and long-lasting blooms.
