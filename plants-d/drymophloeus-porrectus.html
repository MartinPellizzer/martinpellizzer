Drymophloeus porrectus

Drymophloeus porrectus, commonly known as the straight-stemmed ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood and evergreen foliage. The plant typically grows in lowland rainforests and has a straight, cylindrical trunk with few branches. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical in shape.

Drymophloeus porrectus plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
