Damasonium ulvifolium

Damasonium ulvifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf water clover, is a aquatic plant characterized by its distinctive, floating leaves that are broad and lobed, resembling those of the common watercress.

It thrives in shallow, still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in ponds, lakes, and along the edges of rivers. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-yellow, arranged in clusters at the base of the leaves. Its roots are fibrous and help stabilize the sediment in aquatic habitats.

Damasonium ulvifolium plays a role in water filtration and provides habitat for small aquatic organisms.
