Diplachne petelotii

Diplachne petelotii, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluestem, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It typically grows in open woodlands, grasslands, and disturbed soils, exhibiting a clump-forming growth habit. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often blue-green in color, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Diplachne petelotii is adapted to a wide range of environmental conditions, making it a resilient and widespread species.

Its ecological role includes stabilizing soil and providing forage for livestock and wildlife.
