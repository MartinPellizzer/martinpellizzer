Driessenia bulbosa

Driessenia bulbosa, commonly known as the bulbous daisies, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its bulb-like underground storage organs and produces showy, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

Driessenia bulbosa is known for its ability to bloom multiple times throughout the growing season.
