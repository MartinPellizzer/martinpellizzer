Duriala

Duriala, commonly known as the golden dandelion, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by fluffy white seeds that are dispersed by the wind. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped and covered in a fine layer of downy hair.

Duriala is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks.
