Disynstemon paullinioides var. hirsutus

Disynstemon paullinioides var. hirsutus, commonly known as the hairy disynstemon, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its woody stem and dense, hairy foliage that gives it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
