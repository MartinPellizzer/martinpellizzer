Daphne kiusiana f. fasciata

Daphne kiusiana f. fasciata, commonly known as the variegated daphne, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Thymelaeaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, variegated foliage with white and green patterns, which adds visual interest to gardens. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

However, it is toxic to both humans and animals, requiring caution during handling and placement in landscapes.
