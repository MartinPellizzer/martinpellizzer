Dichromena glauca

Dichromena glauca, commonly known as the blue dichromena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a smooth texture.

Dichromena glauca is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
