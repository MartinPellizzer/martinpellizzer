Dipterocarpus bourdillonii

Dipterocarpus bourdillonii, commonly known as the Malayan ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, dense wood and can grow up to 40 meters tall. The plant has simple, opposite leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that are often clustered in spikes. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

This species is valued for its hard timber and plays an important role in the ecosystems of its native habitats.
