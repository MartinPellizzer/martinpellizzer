Dactylorhiza × stagni-novi

Dactylorhiza × stagni-novi, commonly known as the marsh orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a terrestrial herb with tall, erect stems that bear clusters of showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, thriving in moist, nutrient-poor soils. Its roots are specialized for absorbing nutrients from the surrounding environment.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
