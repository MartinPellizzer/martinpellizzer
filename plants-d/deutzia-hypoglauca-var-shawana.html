Deutzia hypoglauca var. shawana

Deutzia hypoglauca var. shawana, commonly known as Shawana deutzia, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of China.

It features slender, arching stems that produce clusters of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers in early summer. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a bluish-green hue with a slightly hairy surface. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a suitable plant for woodland gardens.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
