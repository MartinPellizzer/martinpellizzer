Dendrochilum gibbsiae

Dendrochilum gibbsiae, commonly known as the Gibbs' tree orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, arching stems and clusters of fragrant, showy flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, utilizing its aerial roots to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air. Its leaves are elongated and strap-like, often arranged in a rosette pattern to maximize light exposure.

Dendrochilum gibbsiae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
