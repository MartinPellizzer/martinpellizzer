Dalbergia momsita

Dalbergia momsita, commonly known as the African bloodwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its thick, flaky bark and dark, aromatic wood. The plant has bipinnate leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by flat, bean-like pods.

It is often found in dry, sandy soils and is valued for its hard, durable timber used in furniture and carving.
