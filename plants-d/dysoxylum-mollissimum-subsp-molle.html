Dysoxylum mollissimum subsp. molle

Dysoxylum mollissimum subsp.

molle, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands. It is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and large, palmate leaves that are typically covered in a fine, velvety pubescence.

The plant produces yellowish flowers followed by elongated, woody pods containing edible seeds. Its fruit is valued for its sour flavor and is used in traditional cuisines.

The species is also noted for its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties.
