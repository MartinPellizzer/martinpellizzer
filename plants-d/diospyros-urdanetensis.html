Diospyros urdanetensis

Diospyros urdanetensis, commonly known as the Urdanet persimmon, is a small to medium-sized tree native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Colombia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes ebony and persimmon trees. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically greenish-yellow in color, and it produces small, round fruits that are edible when ripe.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and has potential uses in traditional medicine and as a source of timber.
