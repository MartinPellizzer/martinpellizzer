Dianthus taiwanensis

Dianthus taiwanensis, commonly known as the Taiwan carnation, is a flowering plant native to Taiwan.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or open habitats and has a compact, low-growing structure. Its leaves are linear and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers consist of five petals arranged in a cup-like shape.

Dianthus taiwanensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
