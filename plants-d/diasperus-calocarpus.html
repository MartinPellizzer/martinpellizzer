Diasperus calocarpus

Diasperus calocarpus, commonly known as the yellow bell tree, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically green when mature and turn red upon ripening. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Diasperus calocarpus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
