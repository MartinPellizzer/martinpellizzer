Drypetes kurziana

Drypetes kurziana, commonly known as the Java flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its striking orange-red flowers that appear in clusters. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.
