Dendrocalamus asper f. niger

Dendrocalamus asper f. niger, commonly known as black bamboo, is a variant of the giant bamboo species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black, culms that contrast with its green leaves. The plant is a member of the grass family, Poaceae, and grows in tropical and subtropical climates. It is valued for its strong, durable stalks used in construction and crafts.

Its growth habit is clump-forming, with tall, straight stems that can reach several meters in height.
