Dioscorea buchholziana

Dioscorea buchholziana, commonly known as the Buchholz yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary edible part of the plant. The plant produces small, green, heart-shaped leaves and yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. Its tubers are used in traditional cuisines and have been studied for their potential medicinal properties.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.
