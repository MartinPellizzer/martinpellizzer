Dasymaschalon angustifolium

Dasymaschalon angustifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaved dasymaschalon, is a perennial shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, sandy soils along the edges of swamps and wetlands. The plant has slender, ascending stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, white flowers appear in clusters during the spring and summer months.

It is valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife and its use in wetland restoration projects.
