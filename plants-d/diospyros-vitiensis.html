Diospyros vitiensis

Diospyros vitiensis, commonly known as the Vitiensis persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of the South Pacific, particularly Vanuatu.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other persimmon species. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical in shape and have a smooth margin. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, usually appearing in clusters, while the fruit is a rough, orange-red berry that ripens in the dry season.

The tree is valued for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in the regions where it is found.
