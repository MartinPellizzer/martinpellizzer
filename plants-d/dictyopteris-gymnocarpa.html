Dictyopteris gymnocarpa

Dictyopteris gymnocarpa, commonly known as the smooth-carped sea palm, is a species of brown algae belonging to the family Dictyopteraceae.

It is characterized by its branched, tree-like thallus with a central axis and lateral branches that bear small, blade-like fronds. The plant typically grows in shallow coastal waters, where it attaches to rocky substrates. Its reproductive structures, called conceptacles, are located on the undersides of the fronds and produce gametes for sexual reproduction.

The species is notable for its ecological role in providing habitat and food for various marine organisms.
