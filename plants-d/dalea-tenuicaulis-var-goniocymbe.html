Dalea tenuicaulis var. goniocymbe

Dalea tenuicaulis var. goniocymbe, commonly known as the narrow-stemmed prairie clover, is a flowering plant native to the central United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in open prairies and dry, sandy soils, thriving in full sun. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces clusters of pink to reddish flowers in late spring.

This variety is notable for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a food source for various wildlife species.
