Dyckia elata

Dyckia elata, commonly known as the tall dyckia, is a species of bromeliad native to South America.

It is characterized by its rosette of stiff, grass-like leaves that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences that bear showy, red or orange flowers. It thrives in sunny, well-drained environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Dyckia elata is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
