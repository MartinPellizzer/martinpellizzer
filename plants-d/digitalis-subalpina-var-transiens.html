Digitalis subalpina var. transiens

Digitalis subalpina var. transiens, commonly known as the Alpine foxglove, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to pink. The plant produces a basal rosette of velvety, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
