Dianthus carmelitarum

Dianthus carmelitarum, commonly known as the Carmel bellflower, is a flowering plant native to California.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It has slender stems and small, lance-shaped leaves.

Dianthus carmelitarum is often found in coastal scrub habitats and is valued for its ornamental beauty.
