Dussia discolor

Dussia discolor, commonly known as the silverleaf dussia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it its common name. Its small, white flowers bloom in clusters during the spring and early summer.

Dussia discolor is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
