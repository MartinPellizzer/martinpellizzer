Delphinium xantholeucum

Delphinium xantholeucum, commonly known as the yellow-leaved larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant produces clusters of showy, blue to purple flowers, which are typically arranged in racemes. Its leaves are alternate, lanceolate, and often have a fuzzy texture.

Delphinium xantholeucum is adapted to cold climates and thrives in well-drained soils in full sun.
