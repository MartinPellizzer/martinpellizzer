Dracula benedictii var. hubeinii

Dracula benedictii var. hubeinii, commonly known as the Hubein's Dracula, is a rare and striking orchid native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It is characterized by its vivid red and orange flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a short, stocky stem with a single, showy inflorescence that can reach up to 30 cm in length. Its leaves are dark green and lance-shaped, often covered in a dense layer of fine, white pubescence.

This variety is highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts due to its unique coloration and rarity in cultivation.
