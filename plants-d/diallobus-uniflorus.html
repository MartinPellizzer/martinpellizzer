Diallobus uniflorus

Diallobus uniflorus, commonly known as the single-flower grass, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in grasslands and rocky soils, reaching a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features a slender stem with narrow, grass-like leaves and a single, small, inconspicuous flower at the top. Its flowering period occurs during the late spring to early summer, and it is often found in dry, open habitats.

The species is noted for its adaptability to arid conditions and is of interest in ecological and botanical studies.
