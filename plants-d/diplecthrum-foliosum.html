Diplecthrum foliosum

Diplecthrum foliosum, commonly known as the common arrowroot, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is a herbaceous perennial that grows up to 2 meters tall, characterized by its broad, arrow-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces edible tubers underground, which are rich in starch and used in various culinary applications. Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a valuable crop in humid climates.

Additionally, it plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
