Daphniphyllum glaucescens var. salicifolium

Daphniphyllum glaucescens var. salicifolium, commonly known as the silverleaf daphniphyllum, is a species of evergreen shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that often have a bluish or whitish waxy coating. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by red berries. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its unique foliage and ornamental value make it a popular choice for botanical gardens and specialty collections.
