Delphinium consolida var. pallidus

Delphinium consolida var. pallidus, commonly known as the pale larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features tall, slender stems that bear clusters of delicate, spurred flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white. The leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem, contributing to its ornamental appeal. This variety is characterized by its pale coloration, which distinguishes it from other delphinium species.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
