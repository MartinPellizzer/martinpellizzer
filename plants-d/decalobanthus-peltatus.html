Decalobanthus peltatus

Decalobanthus peltatus, commonly known as the broad-leafed bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in late summer. The plant has lanceolate to ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Decalobanthus peltatus is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
