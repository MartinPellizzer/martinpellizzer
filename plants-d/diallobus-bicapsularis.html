Diallobus bicapsularis

Diallobus bicapsularis, commonly known as the two-capsule grass, is a member of the Poaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, clustered inflorescences. The plant produces two distinct seed capsules, which are typically hairy and contain minute seeds. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as wet meadows and marshes.

Its botanical features make it a notable species for ecological studies in wetland environments.
