Diastella bryiflora

Diastella bryiflora, commonly known as the flat-leaf cycad, is a member of the Cycadaceae family and is native to southern Africa.

It is a slow-growing, evergreen plant characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically yellow-green in color, followed by distinctive, cone-like seed structures. Diastella bryiflora is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh conditions.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important plant in its native habitat.
