Daemonorops rheedei

Daemonorops rheedei, commonly known as the Red-veined Driftwood Palm, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach up to 5 meters in length. The leaves are typically bright green with prominent red veins, giving the plant its distinctive appearance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

This palm is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in tropical gardens.
