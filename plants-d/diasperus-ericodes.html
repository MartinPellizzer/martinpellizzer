Diasperus ericodes

Diasperus ericodes, commonly known as the Ericoid Diasperus, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South Africa.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae, which includes heathers and related plants. The plant is characterized by its small, evergreen leaves and clusters of pink or white flowers. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
