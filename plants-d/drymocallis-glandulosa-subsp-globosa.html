Drymocallis glandulosa subsp. globosa

Drymocallis glandulosa subsp.

globosa, commonly known as the globular goldenrod, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its dense, spherical clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, rich soils in woodlands and wetlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, glandular hairs.

It plays an important role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
