Diplopappus linearis

Diplopappus linearis, commonly known as the linear thistle, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought conditions. Its leaves are usually linear and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers consist of both ray and disk florets.

The fruit is a type of achene enclosed in a pappus, which aids in wind dispersal.
