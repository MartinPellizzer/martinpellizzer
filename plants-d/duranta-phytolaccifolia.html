Duranta phytolaccifolia

Duranta phytolaccifolia, commonly known as the purple duranta, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and northern South America.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple, depending on the stage of bloom. The plant produces bright blue or purple berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans and domestic animals. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical and subtropical regions.
