Didymoglossum dilatatum

Didymoglossum dilatatum, commonly known as the broad-leaved moss, is a small, mat-forming moss that thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves that are often slightly curved and have a smooth, glossy surface. The plant produces small, green to brown capsules for reproduction, which are typically clustered at the tips of the stems. It is often found in forests, rock crevices, and along stream banks, where it plays a role in soil stabilization.

Due to its delicate nature, it is sensitive to environmental changes and is considered an indicator species for healthy, undisturbed habitats.
