Diospyros boala

Diospyros boala, commonly known as the African date palm, is a species native to tropical Africa.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, grey bark and broad, feather-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into edible, sweet fruits resembling dates. Its fruit is an important food source for both humans and wildlife in its native habitat.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its shade and timber.
