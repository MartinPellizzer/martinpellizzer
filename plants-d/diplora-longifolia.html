Diplora longifolia

Diplora longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved diplora, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by woody, elongated fruits. Diplora longifolia is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and swampy areas.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for study in tropical botany and ecological research.
