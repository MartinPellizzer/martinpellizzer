Dianthus × isensis

Dianthus × isensis, commonly known as the hybrid carnation, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is a cross between Dianthus chinensis and Dianthus barbatus, resulting in a variety that combines the characteristics of both parents. The plant is known for its showy, clustered flowers that come in a wide range of colors, including red, pink, white, and purple. It is typically grown as an annual or biennial in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil and full sunlight.

The foliage is lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
