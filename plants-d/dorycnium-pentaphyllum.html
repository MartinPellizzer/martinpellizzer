Dorycnium pentaphyllum

Dorycnium pentaphyllum, commonly known as the five-leafed sainfoin, is a perennial leguminous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, typically consisting of five leaflets, and small yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family and is known for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it valuable in soil enrichment. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
