Dioscorea mairei

Dioscorea mairei, commonly known as Maire's yam, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary part used for medicinal and culinary purposes. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers followed by capsule fruits.

Its tubers are rich in starch and have been traditionally used in various cultures for their nutritional value and potential medicinal properties.
