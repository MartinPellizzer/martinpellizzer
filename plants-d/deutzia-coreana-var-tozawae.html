Deutzia coreana var. tozawae

Deutzia coreana var. tozawae, commonly known as the Tozawa deutzia, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It features upright, branching stems and is characterized by its clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy on top, with a lighter underside. It typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and prefers well-drained soil in full sun to partial shade.

This ornamental plant is valued for its elegant floral display and is often used in gardens for its ornamental appeal.
