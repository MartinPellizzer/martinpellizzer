Dombeya ianthotricha

Dombeya ianthotricha, commonly known as the woolly dombeya, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, fluffy inflorescences that range in color from white to pink, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Dombeya ianthotricha is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
