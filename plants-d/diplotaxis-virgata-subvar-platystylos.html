Diplotaxis virgata subvar. platystylos

Diplotaxis virgata subvar. platystylos, commonly known as the broad-styled yellow oxalis, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers with five petals and a prominent central stamen. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun. It produces small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This subshrub is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
