Digitaria macroglossa

Digitaria macroglossa, commonly known as large-tongue grass, is a species of grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Digitaria macroglossa is a perennial plant that plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for livestock.
