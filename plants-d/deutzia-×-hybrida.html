Deutzia × hybrida

Deutzia × hybrida, commonly known as the weeping deutzia, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of fragrant, white or pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet and features arching branches that give it a graceful, weeping appearance. Its foliage consists of small, ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall.

Deutzia × hybrida prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
