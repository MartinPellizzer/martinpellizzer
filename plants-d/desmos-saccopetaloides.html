Desmos saccopetaloides

Desmos saccopetaloides, commonly known as the waxflower or sweet flower, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It is a small shrub characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its ability to thrive in sandy, well-drained soils. Desmos saccopetaloides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well-suited to coastal gardens due to its salt tolerance.
