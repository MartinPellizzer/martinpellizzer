Dianthus grandiflorus

Dianthus grandiflorus, commonly known as the garden pink, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered pink or red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 12 to 24 inches and features linear, grass-like leaves. Native to southern Europe and western Asia, it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Its flowers are often used in gardens for their ornamental value and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
