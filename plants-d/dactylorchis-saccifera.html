Dactylorchis saccifera

Dactylorchis saccifera, commonly known as the sac-like orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its unique, sac-like structure at the base of its leaves, which is a distinctive botanical feature. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers in late spring, which are clustered in a raceme. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically in pine flatwoods and wetlands.

Its morphology and habitat make it an interesting subject for botanical study and conservation efforts.
