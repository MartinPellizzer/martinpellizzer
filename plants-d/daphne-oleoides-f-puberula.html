Daphne oleoides f. puberula

Daphne oleoides f. puberula, commonly known as the velvet daphne, is a compact evergreen shrub characterized by its dense, hairy foliage.

It features clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are typically glossy and dark green, with a velvety texture that gives the plant its common name. It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained, sunny conditions.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and winter interest, though it is toxic to humans and animals.
