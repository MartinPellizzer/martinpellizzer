Delphinium pubescens proles loscosii

Delphinium pubescens proles loscosii, commonly known as the Loscosii Larkspur, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The flowers are blue to violet in color, with five petals, and are arranged in dense spikes at the top of the plant. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem.

This species is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
