Diosma glauca

Diosma glauca, commonly known as the blue diosma, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and slopes.

Diosma glauca is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
