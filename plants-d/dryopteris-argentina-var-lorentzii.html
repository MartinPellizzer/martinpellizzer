Dryopteris argentina var. lorentzii

Dryopteris argentina var. lorentzii, commonly known as the Lorentz's wood fern, is a variant of the Argentine wood fern native to South America.

It is a deciduous fern characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and fronds that are typically bipinnate. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. Its fronds are covered with small, rounded serrations and can reach lengths of up to 1 meter.

This species is valued for its ornamental foliage and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with appropriate conditions.
