Delphinium flexuosum f. dasyanthum

Delphinium flexuosum f. dasyanthum, commonly known as the hairy delphinium, is a hybrid form of the flexible delphinium.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and large, showy blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or meadow habitats. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture, contributing to its common name.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and its ability to attract pollinators.
