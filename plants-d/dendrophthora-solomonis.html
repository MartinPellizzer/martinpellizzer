Dendrophthora solomonis

Dendrophthora solomonis, commonly known as the Solomon Islands mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of host trees in the tropical regions of the Solomon Islands.

It possesses specialized structures called haustoria that allow it to extract water and nutrients from its host, while still performing photosynthesis using its leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers, followed by fleshy, berry-like fruits that are dispersed by birds. It plays a role in the ecological dynamics of its habitat by influencing host tree growth and providing food for wildlife.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
