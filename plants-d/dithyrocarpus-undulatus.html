Dithyrocarpus undulatus

Dithyrocarpus undulatus, commonly known as the wavy-leaf tamarind, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often wavy or undulating in appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by woody pods containing edible seeds. The tree is valued for its hard, durable wood and its ability to thrive in humid, tropical climates.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and economic contexts.
