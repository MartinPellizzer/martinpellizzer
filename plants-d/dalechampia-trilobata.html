Dalechampia trilobata

Dalechampia trilobata, commonly known as the three-lobed eupatorium, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite, heart-shaped leaves. Its stems are hairy and often have a reddish hue, while its roots are fibrous and adapted to moist environments.

Dalechampia trilobata is often found in wetlands, along stream banks, and in disturbed soils, showcasing its adaptability to humid conditions.
