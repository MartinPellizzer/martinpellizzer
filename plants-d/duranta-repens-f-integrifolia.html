Duranta repens f. integrifolia

Duranta repens f. integrifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf duranta, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the Caribbean and southern United States.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color, giving it a distinctive, silvery appearance. The plant produces clusters of tubular, blue or violet flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is drought-tolerant once established.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes in warm climates.
