Dalea cylindrica var. nova

Dalea cylindrica var. nova, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces legume pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in prairie and steppe habitats.

Dalea cylindrica var. nova is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and stabilizing soil in arid environments.
