Dipterocarpus tampurau

Dipterocarpus tampurau, commonly known as the Malayan ironwood, is a large evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its strong, heavy timber that is highly valued for its durability and resistance to decay. The tree can grow up to 50 meters tall, with a straight trunk and a broad, spreading crown. Its flowers are small and yellowish, typically appearing in clusters, and it produces woody, capsule-like fruits.

The plant plays an important ecological role in tropical rainforests, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
