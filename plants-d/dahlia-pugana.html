Dahlia pugana

Dahlia pugana, commonly known as the Mexican dahlia, is a tuberous-rooted herbaceous perennial native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that come in a variety of colors. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its tubers are used for propagation, and the plant thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.

Dahlia pugana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
