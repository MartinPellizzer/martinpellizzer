Dichanthelium thurowii

Dichanthelium thurowii, commonly known as Thurow's grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies, preferring moist to saturated soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in wetland environments and its ecological importance in maintaining soil stability and providing habitat for various organisms.

Due to its specialized habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many areas.
