Dioscorea quinquelobata

Dioscorea quinquelobata, commonly known as the five-lobed yam, is a herbaceous perennial vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and compound leaves with five distinct lobes. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by tuberous roots that are often used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and ethnobotanical contexts.
