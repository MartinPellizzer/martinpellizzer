Diospyros torquata var. mabaoides

Diospyros torquata var. mabaoides, commonly known as the Maba tree, is a species of flowering plant in the Ebenaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including parts of Malaysia, Indonesia, and the Philippines. The tree is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and elliptical leaves with a leathery texture. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by edible, sweet-tasting fruits similar to persimmons.

The plant is valued for its timber, which is hard and durable, as well as its ecological and cultural significance in the region.
