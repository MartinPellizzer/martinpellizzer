Dianthus ichnusae subsp. toddei

Dianthus ichnusae subsp.

toddei, commonly known as the alpine campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with hairy stems and small, clustered pink or red flowers.

The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing delicate petals that are often tinged with purple or white. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows and limestone areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
