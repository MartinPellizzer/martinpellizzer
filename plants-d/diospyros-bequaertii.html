Diospyros bequaertii

Diospyros bequaertii, commonly known as the Bequaert's ebony, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other ebony species known for their dense, dark wood. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a glossy upper surface. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically followed by small, round fruits.

The plant is valued for its hard, dark wood, which is used in crafting and traditional industries.
