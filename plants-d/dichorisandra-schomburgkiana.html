Dichorisandra schomburgkiana

Dichorisandra schomburgkiana, commonly known as the blue-tongue plant, is a striking member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its vibrant blue and purple tubular flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a waxy coating, which help reduce water loss in its native arid environment. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a desirable ornamental plant in warm climates.
