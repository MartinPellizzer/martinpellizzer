Diclytra chrysantha

Diclytra chrysantha, commonly known as the golden leaf orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, golden-yellow petals and leaves. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, shaded environments. Its flowers are small but numerous, arranged in a spike-like inflorescence.

Diclytra chrysantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
