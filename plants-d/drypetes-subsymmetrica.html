Drypetes subsymmetrica

Drypetes subsymmetrica, commonly known as the asymmetric flame tree, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, flame-like red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptical in shape. Its bark is smooth and greyish, with a distinctive flaky texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a street tree in urban landscapes.
