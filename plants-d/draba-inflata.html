Draba inflata

Draba inflata, commonly known as the inflated whitlow grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a small, herbaceous annual that typically grows in alpine and arctic regions. The plant is characterized by its swollen, inflated stem that resembles a bladder, giving it its common name. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months.

Draba inflata is adapted to harsh environments and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils.
