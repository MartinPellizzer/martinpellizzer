Drosera bindoon

Drosera bindoon, commonly known as the Bindoon Sundew, is a carnivorous plant native to Western Australia.

It belongs to the Droseraceae family and is characterized by its sticky, glandular hairs that trap insects. The plant typically grows in wet, acidic environments such as bogs and heathlands. Its leaves are rosette-shaped and covered in a dense layer of digestive secretions.

Drosera bindoon is a short-lived perennial that plays a unique role in its ecosystem by controlling insect populations.
