Druparia racemosa

Druparia racemosa, commonly known as the clustered drupe, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its compound inflorescence, which consists of a raceme of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits called drupes, which are typically yellow or orange when mature. Druparia racemosa thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland or shrubland habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
