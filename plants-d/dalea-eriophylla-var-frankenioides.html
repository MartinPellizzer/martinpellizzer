Dalea eriophylla var. frankenioides

Dalea eriophylla var. frankenioides, commonly known as the Frankenioides milkvetch, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with small, needle-like leaflets and is adapted to arid environments, showcasing drought tolerance and heat resistance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

The species is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its resilience in harsh desert conditions.
