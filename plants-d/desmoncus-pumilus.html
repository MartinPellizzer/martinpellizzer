Desmoncus pumilus

Desmoncus pumilus, commonly known as the dwarf desert daisy, is a small, flowering plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces delicate, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant has adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments through features such as succulent leaves and a deep root system. It blooms during the cooler months, typically in spring or early summer, and relies on pollinators like bees and moths.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
