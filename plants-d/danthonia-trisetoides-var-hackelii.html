Danthonia trisetoides var. hackelii

Danthonia trisetoides var. hackelii, commonly known as Hackel's danthonia, is a grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and rocky areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.
