Dicoma squarrosa

Dicoma squarrosa, commonly known as the woolly dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along coastal regions. Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it an interesting subject for botanists and gardeners alike.

Dicoma squarrosa is also noted for its ability to withstand drought conditions, contributing to its ecological significance in its native habitat.
