Daphne myrtifolia

Daphne myrtifolia, commonly known as the myrtleleaf daphne, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, showy flowers that bloom in early spring, typically in shades of pink and white. The plant features glossy, ovate leaves with a distinctive myrtle-like scent when crushed. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun.

Daphne myrtifolia is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
