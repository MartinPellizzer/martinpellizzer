Dendrochilum rhombeum

Dendrochilum rhombeum, commonly known as the rhombic orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces showy, fragrant flowers with a distinctive rhomboid shape. The plant typically grows on tree trunks or branches, making it an epiphytic species. Its leaves are usually narrow and arranged in a rosette pattern, while the flowers are borne on terminal inflorescences.

Dendrochilum rhombeum is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
