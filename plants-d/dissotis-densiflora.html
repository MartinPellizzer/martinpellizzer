Dissotis densiflora

Dissotis densiflora, commonly known as the dense-flowered dissotis, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its clustered, dense inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are small and clustered in dense spikes.

Dissotis densiflora is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in humid conditions.
