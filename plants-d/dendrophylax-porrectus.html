Dendrophylax porrectus

Dendrophylax porrectus, commonly known as the Porrectus Oncidium, is a rare epiphytic orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its long, strap-like leaves and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, deriving moisture and nutrients from the air and rain. It thrives in humid, shaded environments with high light exposure.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in controlled settings for conservation and ornamental purposes.
