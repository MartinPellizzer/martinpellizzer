Diospyros hispida

Diospyros hispida, commonly known as the rough persimmon, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured bark and elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into orange to reddish fruit, which is edible when fully ripe. Its fruit is often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
