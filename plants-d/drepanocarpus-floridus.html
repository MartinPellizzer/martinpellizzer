Drepanocarpus floridus

Drepanocarpus floridus, commonly known as the Florida hickory, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive fruit pods. The tree produces small, edible nuts enclosed in woody capsules, which are adapted for dispersal by animals. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing structural support and protection against environmental stressors.

The plant exhibits a range of ecological adaptations that enable it to thrive in diverse forest habitats.
