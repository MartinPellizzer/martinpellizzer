Delphinium savatieri

Delphinium savatieri, commonly known as the Persian larkspur, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features tall, slender stems that bear spikes of blue to purple flowers, typically blooming in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is often cultivated in gardens with suitable climate conditions.
