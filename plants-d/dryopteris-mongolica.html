Dryopteris mongolica

Dryopteris mongolica, commonly known as the Mongolian wood fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its large, leathery fronds that are typically bi- or tri-pinnate, giving it a distinctive and intricate appearance. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments, often found in forest understories or rocky habitats. Its rhizomes are robust and help it establish strong root systems in challenging soils.

Dryopteris mongolica is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been studied for its potential ecological roles in its native ecosystems.
