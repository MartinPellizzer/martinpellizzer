Delphinium charadzeae

Delphinium charadzeae, commonly known as the Georgian larkspur, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Georgia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers in terminal clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and alternate along the stem, with a slightly hairy texture.

Delphinium charadzeae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
