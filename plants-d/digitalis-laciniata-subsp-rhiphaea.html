Digitalis laciniata subsp. rhiphaea

Digitalis laciniata subsp.

rhiphaea, commonly known as the narrow-leaved foxglove, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and Southern Europe. It features tall, slender stems that bear clusters of hooded flowers in shades of pink to purple.

The leaves are deeply lobed and often have a serrated edge, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This subspecies is valued in horticulture for its unique foliage and delicate floral display.
