Daphne taiwanensis

Daphne taiwanensis, commonly known as Taiwan daphne, is a deciduous shrub native to Taiwan.

It belongs to the family Thymelaeaceae and is characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and features dark green, leathery leaves. Its flowers are usually pink or white and are borne in clusters at the ends of the branches.

Daphne taiwanensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its winter flowering and aromatic qualities.
