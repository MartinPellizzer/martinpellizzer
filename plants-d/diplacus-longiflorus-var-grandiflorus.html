Diplacus longiflorus var. grandiflorus

Diplacus longiflorus var. grandiflorus, commonly known as the showy monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red-orange flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or streamside habitats. It has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and can reach heights of up to 3 feet.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
