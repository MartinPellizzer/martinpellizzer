Dioscorea quartiniana var. schliebenii

Dioscorea quartiniana var. schliebenii, commonly known as Schlieben's yam, is a tuberous perennial plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow-orange flowers that are typically unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant. Its tuberous roots are the primary part used, valued for their nutritional content and traditional medicinal properties.

The species is also noted for its ecological importance in supporting various insect and bird species.
