Dudleya goldmanii

Dudleya goldmanii, commonly known as Goldman's liveforever, is a succulent plant native to California.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces vibrant pink or purple flowers during the spring and summer months. It is adapted to rocky habitats and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Dudleya goldmanii is a popular choice for rock gardens due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
