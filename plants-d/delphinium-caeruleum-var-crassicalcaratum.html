Delphinium caeruleum var. crassicalcaratum

Delphinium caeruleum var. crassicalcaratum, commonly known as the Blue Monk delphinium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features tall, slender stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, bearing clusters of striking blue to violet flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem, with a rough, hairy texture. This variety is notable for its robust growth and ability to thrive in well-drained, fertile soils.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and is relatively hardy in temperate climates.
