Dodecatheon pauciflorum subsp. eupauciflorum

Dodecatheon pauciflorum subsp.

eupauciflorum, commonly known as the showy peacock violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States. It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a distinctive violet throat.

The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and has hairy, lance-shaped leaves. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, dark seeds.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
