Durabaculum validum

Durabaculum validum, commonly known as the resilient rockbush, is a hardy shrub native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense, woody stems that provide structural support. The plant features small, leathery leaves that reduce water loss, adapting to its harsh environment. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, appearing in clusters during the cooler months.

Durabaculum validum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in low-maintenance garden settings.
