Draba carnosula

Draba carnosula, commonly known as the woolly rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants, and is characterized by its small, cruciform flowers. The plant is typically found in alpine and rocky habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Draba carnosula is known for its dense, woolly foliage that helps protect it from harsh environmental conditions.

Its life cycle is adapted to short growing seasons, often flowering and setting seed within a few weeks.
