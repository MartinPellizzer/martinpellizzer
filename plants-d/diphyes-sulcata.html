Diphyes sulcata

Diphyes sulcata, commonly known as the Sulcate Ctenanthe, is a tropical flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix and a spathe. The plant features broad, dark green leaves with a textured, waxy surface and prominent veins. It produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are typically hidden within the spathe.

Diphyes sulcata is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant due to its ornamental foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
