Delphinium cyaneum

Delphinium cyaneum, commonly known as the blue delphinium, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, upright stems that bear clusters of vivid blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with lance-shaped leaves that are deeply lobed. Its flowers are rich in nectar and attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

Delphinium cyaneum is valued in horticulture for its striking appearance and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
