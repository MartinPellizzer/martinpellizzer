Dypsis aurantiaca

Dypsis aurantiaca, commonly known as the orange-leafed palm, is a striking tropical plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves that display a vibrant orange hue, especially when mature. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that eventually develop into yellow-orange fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Dypsis aurantiaca is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and adaptability to indoor conditions.
