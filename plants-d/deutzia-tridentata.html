Deutzia tridentata

Deutzia tridentata, commonly known as the three-toothed deutzia, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features clusters of white to pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early summer, adding ornamental value to gardens. The plant has slender, arching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple in the fall. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.

Deutzia tridentata is valued for its compact size and long blooming period, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.
