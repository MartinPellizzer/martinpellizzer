Dryopteris opposita var. longissima

Dryopteris opposita var. longissima, commonly known as the long-stemmed wood fern, is a variant of the broad-leaf wood fern.

It is characterized by its tall, slender fronds that can reach lengths of up to 1.5 meters. The plant features a robust rhizome system that allows it to thrive in moist, shaded environments. Its fronds are typically bi- or tri-pinnate, with a distinctive, elegant appearance.

This fern is often found in temperate forests and is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
