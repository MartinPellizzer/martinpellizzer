Dendrobium burbidgei

Dendrobium burbidgei, commonly known as the Burridge's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, showy flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often near the base of trees or in forest undergrowth. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange with a distinct purple or brown lip, making it a striking ornamental plant.

Dendrobium burbidgei is valued for its beauty and is often cultivated for horticultural purposes.
