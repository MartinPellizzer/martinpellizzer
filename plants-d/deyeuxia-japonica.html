Deyeuxia japonica

Deyeuxia japonica, commonly known as Japanese wiregrass, is a perennial grass species native to East Asia.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly hairy texture.

Deyeuxia japonica is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
