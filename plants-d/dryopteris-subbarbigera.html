Dryopteris subbarbigera

Dryopteris subbarbigera, commonly known as the long-awned wood fern, is a species of fern native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender fronds that can reach up to 1.5 meters in length. The fronds are bipinnate, with fertile pinnae that bear clusters of spore-bearing sorus. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

Its intricate foliage and distinctive spore structures make it a notable species in botanical studies.
