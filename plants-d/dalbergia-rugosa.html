Dalbergia rugosa

Dalbergia rugosa, commonly known as the rough-stemmed ebony, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its dark, dense wood that is highly valued for its strength and fine grain. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters.

Its fruit is a legume pod containing several seeds, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and timber.
