Dendrobium villosipes

Dendrobium villosipes, commonly known as the hairy leech orchid, is an epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, wiry stems that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers with a distinctive leech-like appearance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining substrates.

Dendrobium villosipes is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its unique floral display.
