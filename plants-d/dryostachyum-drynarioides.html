Dryostachyum drynarioides

Dryostachyum drynarioides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf bluemont, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, blue to purple flowers in late spring. The plant typically grows in wetland areas, such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, contributing to soil stabilization.

The botanical features of Dryostachyum drynarioides make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
