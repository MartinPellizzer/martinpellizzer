Diospyros edulis

Diospyros edulis, commonly known as the persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and alternate, simple leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into orange to red fruits with a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor.

The fruit contains a single seed enclosed in a fleshy, edible pulp, and the plant is valued for its ornamental and culinary uses.
