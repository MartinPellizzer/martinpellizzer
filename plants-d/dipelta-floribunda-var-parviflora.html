Dipelta floribunda var. parviflora

Dipelta floribunda var. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of North America.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, typically blooming in early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces, and its stems are slender and branching. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
