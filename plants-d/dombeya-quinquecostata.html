Dombeya quinquecostata

Dombeya quinquecostata, commonly known as the five-costate dombeya, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features simple, alternate leaves with five prominent veins, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens in temperate climates.

The flowers are typically white or pale pink, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
