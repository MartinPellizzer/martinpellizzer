Diospyros cauliflora

Diospyros cauliflora, commonly known as the climbing persimmon, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and large, palmately lobed leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically borne on the trunk and branches, a feature known as cauliflory. Its fruit is a sweet, edible berry that ripens to a deep orange-red color.

Diospyros cauliflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine and cuisine.
