Droogmansia tenuis var. laxa

Droogmansia tenuis var. laxa, commonly known as the slender droogmansia, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It produces yellow to orange disc florets surrounded by ray florets, which contribute to its ornamental appeal.

Due to its compact size and unique appearance, it is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value.
