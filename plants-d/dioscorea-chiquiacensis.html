Dioscorea chiquiacensis

Dioscorea chiquiacensis, commonly known as the Chiquia yam, is a species of tuberous vine native to South America, particularly found in countries like Colombia and Ecuador.

It belongs to the genus Dioscorea, which includes various yam species known for their edible tubers. The plant produces a large, starchy underground tuber that is often used as a food source in local communities. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soils and partial shade.

The tuber's botanical structure is characterized by its elongated shape and high nutritional value, making it an important crop in certain regions.
