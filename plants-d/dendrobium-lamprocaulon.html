Dendrobium lamprocaulon

Dendrobium lamprocaulon, commonly known as the golden lip dendrobium, is an epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with cane-like stems that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, yellow-orange flowers with a distinctive lip that is often marked with dark purple or brown patterns. Its leaves are elongated and fleshy, adapted to store water in its natural habitat.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its showy blooms.
