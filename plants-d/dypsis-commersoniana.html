Dypsis commersoniana

Dypsis commersoniana, commonly known as the Commerson's palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to Madagascar and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by ornamental fruits. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its elegant appearance.

Dypsis commersoniana is also valued for its ability to purify indoor air, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
