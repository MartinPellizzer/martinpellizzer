Diospyros stuhlmannii

Diospyros stuhlmannii, commonly known as the African persimmon, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes ebony and persimmon species. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant. Its fruit is a small, fleshy berry that turns red when mature, containing several seeds.

The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface, depending on the variety.
