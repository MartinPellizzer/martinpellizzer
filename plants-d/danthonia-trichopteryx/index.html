Danthonia Trichopteryx

Danthonia trichopteryx is a low-growing grass species native to the wetlands of eastern Australia. It forms dense, mat-like clumps that help stabilize soil and prevent erosion. Its fine, needle-like leaves are covered in a soft, hairy texture that gives it a unique appearance. This plant thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in swampy or boggy areas. Due to its ecological importance, it is a key species in the restoration of wetland habitats.
