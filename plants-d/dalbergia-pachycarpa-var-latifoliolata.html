Dalbergia pachycarpa var. latifoliolata

Dalbergia pachycarpa var. latifoliolata, commonly known as the broad-leafed rosewood, is a species of leguminous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark-colored wood, which is highly valued for its aesthetic and structural properties. The plant has compound leaves with broad, ovate leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers followed by pod-like fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental and economic value, it is sometimes cultivated for timber and horticultural purposes.
