Dicranopteris linearis var. inaequiloba

Dicranopteris linearis var. inaequiloba, commonly known as the unequal-lobed water fern, is a variant of the water fern species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a perennial fern characterized by its delicate, frond-like structures that are typically submerged or floating in aquatic environments. The plant features a rhizomatous growth habit, with long, slender fronds that often exhibit a distinctive, asymmetrical lobing pattern. Its leaves are usually finely divided, contributing to its ornamental appeal in water gardens.

This variant is adapted to moist or aquatic habitats, showcasing its role in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
