Dactylorhiza fuchsii subsp. sudetica

Dactylorhiza fuchsii subsp.

sudetica, commonly known as the alpine marsh orchid, is a species of orchid native to Europe. It typically grows in wet, calcareous environments such as marshes and peat bogs.

The plant features a tall, erect stem with several linear to lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are pale green to yellowish, often with a reddish or purple tint, and are arranged in a loose spike.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological significance and is protected in many regions due to habitat loss.
