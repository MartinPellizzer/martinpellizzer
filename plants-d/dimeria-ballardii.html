Dimeria ballardii

Dimeria ballardii, commonly known as the Ballard's dimeria, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the family Cactaceae, which includes cacti and other succulent plants. The plant is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring. Its leaves are modified into spines, reducing water loss in arid environments.

Dimeria ballardii is adapted to thrive in rocky soils and is often found in desert scrub habitats.
