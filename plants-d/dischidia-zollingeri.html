Dischidia zollingeri

Dischidia zollingeri, commonly known as the climbing dischidia, is a vining species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its unique growth habit allows it to climb and trail, making it a popular choice for hanging baskets or shelves.

Dischidia zollingeri is relatively low-maintenance, thriving in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil.
