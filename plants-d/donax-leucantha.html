Donax leucantha

Donax leucantha, commonly known as the white sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, often found in desert or arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, and the flowers bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Donax leucantha is adapted to survive in harsh, dry conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
