Dactylorhiza lapponica var. pseudocordigera

Dactylorhiza lapponica var. pseudocordigera, commonly known as the Lapland orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to northern regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in a raceme. The plant typically grows in moist, peaty or sandy soils, often in open habitats such as bogs and heaths. It has a complex root system with mycorrhizal associations that aid in nutrient uptake.

This orchid is noted for its delicate appearance and ecological significance in its native environments.
