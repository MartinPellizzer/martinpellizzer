Dianthus scoparius

Dianthus scoparius, commonly known as the sand dianthus, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, clustered pink or white flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, and it thrives in sandy or gravelly soils.

Dianthus scoparius is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
