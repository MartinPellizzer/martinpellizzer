Dendranthema argyrophyllum

Dendranthema argyrophyllum, commonly known as silverleaf chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name, and its showy yellow to orange composite flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive appearance and hardy nature.

Dendranthema argyrophyllum is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
