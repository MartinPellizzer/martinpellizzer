Didymocarpus oreocharis

Didymocarpus oreocharis, commonly known as the red-flowered tamarind, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant is distinguished by its striking red flowers, which are often arranged in dense clusters. Its fruits are small, woody pods that contain several seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other regions for horticultural purposes.
