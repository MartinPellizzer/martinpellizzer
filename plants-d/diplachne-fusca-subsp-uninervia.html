Diplachne fusca subsp. uninervia

Diplachne fusca subsp.

uninervia, commonly known as the narrow-veined flat sedge, is a perennial grass species native to Australia. It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a distinct midrib.

The plant typically grows in wetland or riparian habitats, preferring moist to waterlogged soils. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, three-sided spikelets.

This subspecies is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to brackish or saline conditions.
