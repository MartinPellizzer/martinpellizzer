Dipteracanthus puri subvar. ampliflorus

Dipteracanthus puri subvar. ampliflorus, commonly known as the broad-flowered dipteracanthus, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences that consist of numerous small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are lanceolate and often have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The flowers are usually pink or red, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
