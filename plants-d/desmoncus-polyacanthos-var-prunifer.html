Desmoncus polyacanthos var. prunifer

Desmoncus polyacanthos var. prunifer, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, branched stems and showy, violet to purple flower heads. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are arranged in a conical shape, giving it its common name. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in prairies and open woodlands.
