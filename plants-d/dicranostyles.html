Dicranostyles

Dicranostyles, commonly known as the "feather moss," is a genus of mosses belonging to the family Dicranaceae.

These plants are characterized by their delicate, feather-like leaves that are often arranged in a dense, cushion-like structure. Dicranostyles species typically thrive in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors and rocky crevices. Their reproductive structures, called capsules, are borne on slender stalks and release spores into the air.

These mosses play an important ecological role in soil stabilization and moisture retention in their habitats.
