Dioclea bicolor

Dioclea bicolor, commonly known as the pink passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that feature a unique blend of pink and white petals. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are often consumed by wildlife and humans alike. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a rough texture.

Dioclea bicolor is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.
