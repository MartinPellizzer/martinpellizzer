Dianella caerulea var. assera

Dianella caerulea var. assera, commonly known as the blue-flowered grass tree, is a striking ornamental plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves that form a dense rosette, often with a bluish or greyish hue. The plant produces striking blue or violet flowers on tall, slender spikes, which appear in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a container plant.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is widely cultivated in gardens throughout the temperate regions.
