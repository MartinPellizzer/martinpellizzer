Disa cernua

Disa cernua, commonly known as the nodding disa, is a species of orchid native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, delicate flowers that hang downward, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky outcrops. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine, white pubescence.

Disa cernua is a perennial herb that produces showy, violet or purple flowers during the summer months.
