Dendrobium speciosum f. bancroftianum

Dendrobium speciosum f. bancroftianum, commonly known as the Bancroft's orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a sympodial epiphytic orchid, meaning it grows on other plants without being parasitic, and typically thrives in humid, tropical environments. The plant features long, cane-like pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, supporting its growth in less-than-ideal conditions. Its flowers are showy, often appearing in clusters of white or pale yellow with a faintly fragrant scent.

This variety is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and adaptability to cultivation.
