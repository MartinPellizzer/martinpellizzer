Dianthus superbus subsp. alpestris

Dianthus superbus subsp.

alpestris, commonly known as the alpine campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its pink or white flowers that bloom in summer, typically forming clusters at the ends of its stems.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, soft down, giving it a woolly appearance. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows and grasslands. Dianthus superbus subsp.

alpestris is adapted to cold climates and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.
