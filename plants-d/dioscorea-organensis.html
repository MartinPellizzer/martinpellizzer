Dioscorea organensis

Dioscorea organensis, commonly known as the African yam bean, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary part used for culinary and medicinal purposes. The plant produces small, green, heart-shaped leaves and yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its tubers are rich in starch and have a nutty flavor, making them a staple in some African cuisines.

Additionally, the plant has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
