Dentella browniana var. setulosa

Dentella browniana var. setulosa, commonly known as the hairy dentella, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine, hairy glands, contributing to its common name. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
