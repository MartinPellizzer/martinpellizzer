Dahlia sherffii

Dahlia sherffii, commonly known as the Mexican dahlia, is a herbaceous perennial native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that come in a variety of colors. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its flowering period occurs in the summer months, and it thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.

The tubers are used as a food source in some regions, and the plant is also cultivated for ornamental purposes.
