Dicranopygium nanum

Dicranopygium nanum, commonly known as the small feather moss, is a small, terrestrial moss belonging to the family Dicranaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, feather-like leaves that are typically less than 2 millimeters in length. This moss thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest floors or on rock surfaces. Its reproductive structures, called capsules, are borne on short, erect stems and release spores when mature.

Dicranopygium nanum plays a vital role in soil stabilization and moisture retention in its native habitats.
