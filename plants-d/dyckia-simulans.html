Dyckia simulans

Dyckia simulans, commonly known as the narrow-leaf dyckia, is a striking bromeliad native to Brazil.

It features rosettes of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of white, powdery bloom, giving it a unique appearance. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with bright red or orange flowers that attract birds and insects. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Dyckia simulans is a popular choice for rock gardens and as a container plant due to its hardiness and ornamental value.
