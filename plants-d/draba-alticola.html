Draba alticola

Draba alticola, commonly known as the alpine drumstick, is a flowering plant native to the high mountain regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in cold and arid conditions. It has a low-growing rosette structure with basal leaves and produces elongated, club-shaped seed pods.

Draba alticola is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions, making it a resilient species in its native range.
