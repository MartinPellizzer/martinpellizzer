Diplotaxis virgata var. delagei

Diplotaxis virgata var. delagei, commonly known as the Delage's African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often found in arid or semi-arid regions. The plant features a low-growing, mat-forming structure with linear, blue-green leaves. Its flowers are showy, with yellow centers surrounded by white to pale blue ray florets.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its drought tolerance and attractive blooms.
