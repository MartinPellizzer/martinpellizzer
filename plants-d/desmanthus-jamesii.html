Desmanthus jamesii

Desmanthus jamesii, commonly known as the Texas needlepod, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial legume that produces small, pea-like flowers in shades of pink to purple. The plant has trifoliate leaves and produces cylindrical pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and rocky areas.

Desmanthus jamesii is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil fertility in dry environments.
