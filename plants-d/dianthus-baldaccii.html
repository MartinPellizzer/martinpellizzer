Dianthus baldaccii

Dianthus baldaccii, commonly known as the Sicilian carnation, is a flowering plant native to the island of Sicily.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to white. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, while its stems are upright and branched.

Dianthus baldaccii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
