Daucus brevicaulis

Daucus brevicaulis, commonly known as the short-stemmed parsley.

It is a member of the carrot family (Apiaceae) and is native to the western United States. This annual herb typically grows to a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters, featuring finely divided, fern-like leaves. Its small, white flowers are arranged in umbels, a characteristic feature of the Apiaceae family.

The plant produces small, round fruits that are often used in traditional herbal medicine.
