Dissotis grandiceps

Dissotis grandiceps, commonly known as the giant dissotis, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that consist of numerous white to pink flowers arranged in a spikelike cluster. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and often have a waxy texture.

Dissotis grandiceps is often cultivated for its ornamental value in tropical gardens and as a cut flower.
