Dracocephalum ruyschiana var. alpinum

Dracocephalum ruyschiana var. alpinum, commonly known as alpine blueweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in well-drained, rocky soils at high elevations. The plant features slender stems and lance-shaped leaves with a bluish-green hue, often covered in fine hairs. Its small, blue to purple flowers are arranged in dense spikes, contributing to its ornamental value.

It is valued in horticulture for its ability to withstand cold climates and its attractive, aromatic foliage.
