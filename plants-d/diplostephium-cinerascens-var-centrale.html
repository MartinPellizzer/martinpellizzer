Diplostephium cinerascens var. centrale

Diplostephium cinerascens var. centrale, commonly known as the central silverleaf, is a perennial shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, dense hairs that help reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the spring and early summer. Its root system is extensive and adapted to store water, allowing it to survive prolonged droughts.

This species is often found in rocky soils and is an important component of desert ecosystems.
