Diospyros macrophylla

Diospyros macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leafed persimmon, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree bears edible fruits that are typically orange to red in color when ripe. Its bark is often smooth and may have a reddish or greyish hue.

Diospyros macrophylla is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional medicine.
