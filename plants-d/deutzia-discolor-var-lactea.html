Deutzia discolor var. lactea

Deutzia discolor var. lactea, commonly known as the white-flowered deutzia, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its clusters of white flowers that bloom in early spring, often preceding the foliage. The plant has slender, arching branches and small, ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

Its ornamental value lies in its delicate flowers and graceful growth habit.
