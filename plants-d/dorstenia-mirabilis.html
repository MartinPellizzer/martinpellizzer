Dorstenia mirabilis

Dorstenia mirabilis, commonly known as the "miraculous" or "twin-leaf" plant, is a member of the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in shaded, moist environments. The plant is characterized by its unique, spiraling inflorescence and fleshy, cordate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, typically appearing in clusters, and it produces small, round fruits that are edible when ripe.

Dorstenia mirabilis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ease of care in humid, indoor settings.
