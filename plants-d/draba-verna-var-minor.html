Draba verna var. minor

Draba verna var. minor, commonly known as the spring whitlow grass, is a small, herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils in alpine and arctic regions. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in early spring, which are arranged in a tight cluster. Its leaves are basal, rosette-like, and covered in fine hairs.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other mustard family plants.
