Dendrolobium rugosum var. rugosum

Dendrolobium rugosum var. rugosum, commonly known as the roughleaf wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its rough, ridged, and often hairy leaves, which give it its scientific name. The plant produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, attracting a variety of pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy regions.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is cultivated in gardens and landscapes across Australia.
