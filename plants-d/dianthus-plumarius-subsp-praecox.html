Dianthus plumarius subsp. praecox

Dianthus plumarius subsp.

praecox, commonly known as the early carnation, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and western Asia. It features slender stems with tightly packed, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers with prominent, showy petals and a distinctive, frilled appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in meadows and grasslands.

The subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens.
