Duranta dombeyana

Duranta dombeyana, commonly known as the silverleaf duranta, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine silvery down, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of blue or violet flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in a variety of soil types.

Duranta dombeyana is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
