Dolichos saponarius

Dolichos saponarius, commonly known as the soappod or horse bean plant, is a leguminous species native to warm temperate and tropical regions.

It is characterized by its long, slender pods that contain small, bean-like seeds. The plant has compound leaves and produces yellow flowers, typical of many legumes. It is often cultivated for its edible seeds, which are used in various cuisines.

Additionally, the plant has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
