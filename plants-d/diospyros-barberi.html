Diospyros barberi

Diospyros barberi, commonly known as the Indian persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray-brown bark and alternate, simple leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by orange-red, fleshy fruits. The fruit is edible, with a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor, and is often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
