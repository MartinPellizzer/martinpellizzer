Delphinium amoenum

Delphinium amoenum, commonly known as the blue delphinium, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that bear clusters of striking blue to violet flowers. The plant produces showy, spurred petals that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically palmately compound, with deeply lobed foliage that adds to its ornamental value.

Delphinium amoenum is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and is relatively hardy in temperate climates.
