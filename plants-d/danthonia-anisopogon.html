Danthonia anisopogon

Danthonia anisopogon, commonly known as the narrow-leaved feathergrass, is a perennial grass native to Australia.

It is characterized by its fine, grass-like foliage and produces slender, feathery inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It has a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread and form dense mats.

Its botanical features make it adaptable to various environmental conditions, contributing to its ecological significance in native ecosystems.
