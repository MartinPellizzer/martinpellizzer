Dendrobium christianae

Dendrobium christianae, commonly known as the Christian's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Philippines.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a rhizomatous stem and produces racemes of fragrant, yellow-green flowers. The plant has lanceolate leaves that are usually arranged in two rows along the stem. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Dendrobium christianae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
