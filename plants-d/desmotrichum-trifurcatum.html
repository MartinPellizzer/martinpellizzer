Desmotrichum trifurcatum

Desmotrichum trifurcatum, commonly known as the three-branched burrgrass, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish in color. The plant produces distinctive three-lobed bracts that enclose the seeds, which are small and often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grassland habitats.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
