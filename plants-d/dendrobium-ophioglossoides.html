Dendrobium ophioglossoides

Dendrobium ophioglossoides, commonly known as the snake tongue orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with elongated, fleshy pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant produces pendulous inflorescences bearing showy, fragrant flowers with a unique, intricate pattern resembling a snake's tongue. Its leaves are linear and arranged in a rosette, often with a waxy coating.

Dendrobium ophioglossoides thrives in shaded, humid environments and is valued for its ornamental beauty in horticulture.
