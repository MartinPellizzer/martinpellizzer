Dactylorhiza × aschersoniana nothosubsp.

hochreutineriana, commonly known as the Hochreutiner's marsh orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to parts of Europe. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of pale to dark purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in wet, calcareous habitats such as marshes and bogs. It has linear, grass-like leaves and produces small, greenish-brown pseudobulbs.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
