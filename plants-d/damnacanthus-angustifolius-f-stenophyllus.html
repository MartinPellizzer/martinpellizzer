Damnacanthus angustifolius f. stenophyllus

Damnacanthus angustifolius f. stenophyllus, commonly known as narrow-leaf false dandelion, is a compact perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is typically evergreen, retaining its color throughout the year.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively low maintenance in suitable growing conditions.
