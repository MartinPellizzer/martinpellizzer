Diospyros choboensis

Diospyros choboensis, commonly known as the Chobo persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into clustered, orange-red fruits resembling persimmons. The fruit is edible when fully ripe, containing a sweet, fleshy pulp surrounding a central core of seeds.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional use in local cuisines and herbal remedies.
