Diospyros thomasii

Diospyros thomasii, commonly known as Thomas's persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other persimmon species and ebony trees. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a fleshy, orange-red berry that ripens in the summer months.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has limited use in traditional medicine.
