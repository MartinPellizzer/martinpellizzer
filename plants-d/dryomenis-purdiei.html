Dryomenis purdiei

Dryomenis purdiei, commonly known as the Cape mountain sandthorn, is a small shrub native to the Western Cape region of South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny branches and small, yellowish-green leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to the dry, Mediterranean climate of its native habitat.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens for ornamental purposes.
