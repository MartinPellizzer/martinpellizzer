Drypetes eriocarpa

Drypetes eriocarpa, commonly known as the hairy drypetes, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and opposite, ovate leaves with hairy undersides. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by orange-red, fleshy fruits that are edible for some bird species.
