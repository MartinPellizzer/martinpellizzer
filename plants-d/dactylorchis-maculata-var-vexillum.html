Dactylorchis maculata var. vexillum

Dactylorchis maculata var. vexillum, commonly known as the speckled orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the southeastern United States.

It features a rhizomatous growth habit with narrow, grass-like leaves and produces delicate, pinkish-white flowers with distinctive purple spots. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, and its flowers are arranged in a loose raceme. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded, woodland habitats.

This orchid is notable for its unique floral markings and its role in local pollinator ecosystems.
