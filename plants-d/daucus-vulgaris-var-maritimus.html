Daucus vulgaris var. maritimus

Daucus vulgaris var. maritimus, commonly known as sea parsley, is a coastal plant native to the shores of Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant thrives in sandy or rocky coastal environments, where it is adapted to salt spray and strong winds. Its aromatic foliage and edible qualities have made it historically useful in local cuisines and traditional medicine.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils and supporting coastal biodiversity.
