Daphne × lutetiana

Daphne × lutetiana, commonly known as the winter daphne, is a hybrid shrub native to Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of fragrant, waxy flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are typically red or black in color. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Due to its toxicity, it should be handled with care.
