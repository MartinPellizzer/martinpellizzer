Dendrobium nobile var. pallidiflorum

Dendrobium nobile var. pallidiflorum, commonly known as the pale-flowered orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its pendant, fleshy stems and clusters of pale yellow to white flowers with a slight pinkish tinge. The plant is an epiphyte, typically growing on trees or rocks in humid, tropical environments. It produces showy, long-lasting blooms that are popular in horticulture for their beauty and fragrance.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for floral arrangements and ornamental gardens.
