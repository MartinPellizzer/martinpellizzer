Diserneston

Diserneston, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the temperate regions of Europe.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem, and it thrives in well-drained soil with partial to full sunlight. Diserneston is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for.

It is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
