Diplachne virgata

Diplachne virgata, commonly known as slender flatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, flat, and often sprawling growth habit, with narrow leaves that are typically hairy along the edges. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including disturbed soils and open woodlands, and is often found in sandy or loamy soils.

Diplachne virgata is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in regions with low rainfall.
