Dichanthelium filiramum

Dichanthelium filiramum, commonly known as slender bristlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland habitats, including marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist to saturated soil conditions. The plant has slender, erect stems that are often sparsely hairy, and its leaves are narrow with a slight sheen. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, spikelets, which are usually pale green to reddish in color.

Dichanthelium filiramum plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species in its native environment.
