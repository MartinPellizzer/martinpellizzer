Dimorphandra pullei

Dimorphandra pullei, commonly known as the African baobab, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its thick, buttressed trunk and large, compound leaves. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with deep roots that allow it to access groundwater.

The tree plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
