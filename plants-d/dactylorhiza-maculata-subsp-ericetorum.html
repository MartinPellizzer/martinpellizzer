Dactylorhiza maculata subsp. ericetorum

Dactylorhiza maculata subsp.

ericetorum, commonly known as the purple marsh orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to western Europe. It typically grows in wet or damp habitats such as bogs, marshes, and stream banks.

The plant features a slender, erect stem with several lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually pale purple or pink, with a distinctive three-lobed lip.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its specialized pollination mechanisms.
