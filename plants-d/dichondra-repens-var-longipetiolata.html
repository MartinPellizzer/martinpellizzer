Dichondra repens var. longipetiolata

Dichondra repens var. longipetiolata, commonly known as the trailing dichondra, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features small, heart-shaped leaves that are typically bright green and arranged oppositely along the stems. The plant produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are usually pale blue or white, which are followed by small, round fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its delicate appearance and ease of cultivation make it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.
