Digitaria ischaemum subsp. stewartiana

Digitaria ischaemum subsp.

stewartiana, commonly known as Stewart's Digitaria, is a grass species native to parts of Africa and the Middle East. It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and narrow, linear leaves.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a subject of study in grassland ecology.
