Dendrobium simondii

Dendrobium simondii, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit, with cane-like stems that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, yellowish-green flowers that bloom during the summer months. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often arranged in a rosette pattern along the stems.

Dendrobium simondii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is commonly cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions.
