Dimocarpus foveolatus

Dimocarpus foveolatus, commonly known as the mountain mango, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, often fissured bark and alternate, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by fleshy, orange-red berries that are edible. Its fruit is valued for its sweet, tangy flavor and is used in local cuisines.

The plant also has traditional medicinal uses in some cultures.
