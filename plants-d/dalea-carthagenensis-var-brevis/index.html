Dalea Carthagenensis Var. Brevis

Shortleaf dalea, scientifically known as Dalea carthagenensis var. brevis, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the southeastern United States. It thrives in sandy, acidic soils and is often found in open woodlands and pine forests. This species is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and distinctive, narrow leaves. Due to habitat loss and environmental changes, it faces threats to its survival. Conservation efforts are crucial to preserving this unique member of the legume family.
