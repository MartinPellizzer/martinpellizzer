Dioclea rostrata

Dioclea rostrata, commonly known as the rostrated spiderplant, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are followed by bean-like pods containing several seeds. Dioclea rostrata is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its rapid growth and ability to climb make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in warm climates.
