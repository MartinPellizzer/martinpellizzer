Dianthus huebneri

Dianthus huebneri, commonly known as the alpine campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces small, clustered flowers that attract pollinators.

Dianthus huebneri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
