Diplogon hyssopifolium

Diplogon hyssopifolium, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette growth habit, with thick, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. The plant produces showy, pentagonal flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white, which bloom from late winter to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and indirect sunlight, making it a popular houseplant.

Its botanical name reflects its leaf structure, with "hyssopifolium" referring to its resemblance to the leaves of the hyssop plant.
