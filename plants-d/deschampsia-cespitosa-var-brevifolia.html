Deschampsia cespitosa var. brevifolia

Deschampsia cespitosa var. brevifolia, commonly known as slender tuftgrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often form compact clumps. The leaves are narrow, grass-like, and typically have a blue-green coloration. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

This variety is often found in moist to wet habitats, such as meadows, marshes, and along streambanks.
