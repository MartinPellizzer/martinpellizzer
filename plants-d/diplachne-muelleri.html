Diplachne muelleri

Diplachne muelleri, commonly known as Mueller's grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Diplachne muelleri is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and ability to stabilize soil.
