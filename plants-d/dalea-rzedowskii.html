Dalea rzedowskii

Dalea rzedowskii, commonly known as the Rzedowski's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and clusters of small, pink to reddish-purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Dalea rzedowskii plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing nectar for pollinators in its native habitat.
