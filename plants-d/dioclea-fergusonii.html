Dioclea fergusonii

Dioclea fergusonii, commonly known as the golden bignay, is a climbing shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in secondary forests and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and potential agricultural use.
