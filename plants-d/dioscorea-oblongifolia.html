Dioscorea oblongifolia

Dioscorea oblongifolia, commonly known as the African yam bean, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary edible part of the plant. The leaves are heart-shaped and large, while the flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant. The plant thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its nutritious tubers.

It is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
