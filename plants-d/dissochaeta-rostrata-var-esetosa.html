Dissochaeta rostrata var. esetosa

Dissochaeta rostrata var. esetosa, commonly known as the silky hairroot, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It features a slender, branching stem covered in fine, silky hairs, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a hairy surface.

This species is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks.
