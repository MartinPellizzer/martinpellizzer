Dioscorea larecajensis

Dioscorea larecajensis, commonly known as the Larecaja yam, is a species of tuberous plant native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Colombia.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit, with aerial roots that help it ascend supports. The plant produces edible tubers that are rich in starch and have been traditionally used by indigenous communities for food.

Its botanical structure includes compound leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that develop into capsules containing numerous seeds.
