Deyeuxia preslii

Deyeuxia preslii, commonly known as the Preslii false panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to eastern Asia.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along riverbanks. The plant has slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, two-lobed spikelets.

Deyeuxia preslii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in wetland gardens.
