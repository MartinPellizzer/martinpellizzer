Derris atroviolacea

Derris atroviolacea, commonly known as the black violet acacia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod-bearing fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces clusters of small, violet-colored flowers. Its seed pods are slender and contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
