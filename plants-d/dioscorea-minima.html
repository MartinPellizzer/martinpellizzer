Dioscorea minima

Dioscorea minima, commonly known as the lesser yam, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary part used for propagation and consumption. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and has heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, usually greenish-white in color, and it produces berries that contain the seeds.

Due to its nutritional value, it is cultivated in some parts of Asia and Africa for food and traditional medicine.
