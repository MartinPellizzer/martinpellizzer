Dimorphanthera dekockii var. pubiflora

Dimorphanthera dekockii var. pubiflora, commonly known as the Dekock's orchid, is a rare and striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its unique, showy inflorescences that bear flowers with a distinctive coloration pattern, often featuring a combination of white, pink, and purple hues. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in semi-shade to full sun conditions. Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine, pubescent layer, giving it the varietal name *pubiflora*.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a conservation concern and is often cultivated in specialized botanical gardens.
