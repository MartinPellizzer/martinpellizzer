Diplazium brachysoroides

Diplazium brachysoroides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf water fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically submerged or floating in freshwater environments. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread through vegetative propagation. Its spores are produced in sori located on the underside of the fronds, typical of the fern life cycle.

Diplazium brachysoroides plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and oxygen through photosynthesis.
