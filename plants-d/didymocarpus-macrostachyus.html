Didymocarpus macrostachyus

Didymocarpus macrostachyus, commonly known as the large-stalked flame tree, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery red flowers that appear in clusters, often resembling flames. The plant has compound leaves with multiple leaflets and produces small, round fruits that turn from green to yellow as they mature. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The botanical features of this species make it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical and subtropical regions.
