Dendrocalamus ronganensis

Dendrocalamus ronganensis, commonly known as the Rongan bamboo, is a tall, robust grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Bambusoideae subfamily and is characterized by its strong, hollow stems that can reach heights of up to 20 meters. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically green and arranged in a dense, grass-like structure. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Dendrocalamus ronganensis is valued for its timber and is also used in traditional crafts and construction in its native regions.
