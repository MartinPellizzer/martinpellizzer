Dichapetalum crassifolium var. integrum

Dichapetalum crassifolium var. integrum, commonly known as the African mallow, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically thick and leathery. The plant produces showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or savanna habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking foliage and floral display.
