Dendrobium cruentum

Dendrobium cruentum, commonly known as the crimson orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with branched stems that often hang from trees or grow along rock faces. The plant produces showy, red or orange-red flowers with a distinctive, fragrant scent. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the air, and it thrives in warm, humid environments.

Dendrobium cruentum is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its vibrant blooms.
