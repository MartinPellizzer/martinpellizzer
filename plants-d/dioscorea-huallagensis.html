Dioscorea huallagensis

Dioscorea huallagensis, commonly known as the Andean yam, is a climbing vine native to the Andes region of South America.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary edible part of the plant. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and has compound leaves with three to five leaflets. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often cultivated for its nutritional value.

The tubers are rich in starch and are an important food source in some indigenous communities.
