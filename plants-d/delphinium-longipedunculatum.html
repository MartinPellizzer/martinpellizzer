Delphinium longipedunculatum

Delphinium longipedunculatum, commonly known as the long peduncled larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers in racemose inflorescences, with each flower having five sepals and a single pistil. Its leaves are palmately divided into several lobes, and the plant thrives in well-drained soils in full sun.

Delphinium longipedunculatum is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
