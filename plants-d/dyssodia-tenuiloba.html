Dyssodia tenuiloba

Dyssodia tenuiloba, commonly known as the narrow-leaved dyssodia, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in grasslands and rocky soils, exhibiting a low-growing habit with narrow, linear leaves. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in composite inflorescences, and it produces small, oblong fruits.

Dyssodia tenuiloba is adapted to arid conditions and is often found in regions with seasonal rainfall.
