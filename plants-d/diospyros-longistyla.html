Diospyros longistyla

Diospyros longistyla, commonly known as the longistyla persimmon, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, grey bark and elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, orange-red berries.

Its fruit is often consumed raw or used in traditional medicine due to its high nutritional value and medicinal properties.
