Desmodium acrocarpum

Desmodium acrocarpum, commonly known as hairy beggar's-ticks, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several small, kidney-shaped seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Desmodium acrocarpum is also known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, which benefits soil fertility.
