Delphinium iranorum

Delphinium iranorum, commonly known as the Iranian larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Iran.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are palmately compound, with a distinctive appearance that aids in its identification.

Delphinium iranorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by supporting pollinators.
