Desmodium monospermum

Desmodium monospermum, commonly known as the small-headed burr legume, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces distinctive, spiny seed pods that contain small, rounded seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Desmodium monospermum is also noted for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil fertility.
