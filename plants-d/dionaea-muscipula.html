Dionaea muscipula

Dionaea muscipula, commonly known as the Venus flytrap, is a carnivorous plant native to the wetlands of North and South Carolina.

It is characterized by its unique leaf structures, which function as snap traps to capture and digest insects. The plant's leaves are covered in sensitive hair-like structures that trigger the closure of the trap when touched. Dionaea muscipula thrives in nutrient-poor soil and requires consistent moisture and acidic conditions.

Its rapid movement and specialized digestive system make it a fascinating subject in botanical studies.
