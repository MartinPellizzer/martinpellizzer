Diplotaxis catholica

Diplotaxis catholica, commonly known as the African dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of gray-green, hairy leaves and produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

The seeds are dispersed by wind, and the plant is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
