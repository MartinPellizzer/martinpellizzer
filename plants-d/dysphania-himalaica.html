Dysphania himalaica

Dysphania himalaica, commonly known as Himalayan spinach, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and alternate, lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, which are typically followed by spherical, dry fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions with well-drained soil.

Dysphania himalaica is valued for its edible leaves, which are used in traditional cuisines and have nutritional benefits.
