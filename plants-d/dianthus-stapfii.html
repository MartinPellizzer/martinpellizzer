Dianthus stapfii

Dianthus stapfii, commonly known as the Stapf's campion, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, pinkish-red flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. Its flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom during the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Dianthus stapfii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
