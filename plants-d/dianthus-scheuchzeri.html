Dianthus scheuchzeri

Dianthus scheuchzeri, commonly known as the alpine campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pink to white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and cool climates. It has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and hairy, and its stems are usually short and compact.

Dianthus scheuchzeri is often found in alpine meadows and is adapted to withstand harsh, high-altitude conditions.
