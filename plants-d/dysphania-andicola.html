Dysphania andicola

Dysphania andicola, commonly known as Andean spinach, is a leafy green native to the Andes region of South America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, edible leaves that are rich in nutrients. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments and thrives in cooler climates. Its leaves are often used in traditional Andean cuisine for their mild, slightly sweet flavor.

Dysphania andicola is valued not only for its nutritional content but also for its adaptability to challenging growing conditions.
