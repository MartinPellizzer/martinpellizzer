Dipterocarpus luchunensis

Dipterocarpus luchunensis, commonly known as the Luchun dipterocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its large, heavy wood and prominent buttressed roots. The plant typically grows in lowland rainforests and is often found in primary forest habitats. It produces small, fragrant flowers and is an important component of the forest canopy.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
