Delissea undulata var. serrata

Delissea undulata var. serrata, commonly known as the serrated delissea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from pale yellow to orange. The leaves are typically opposite, ovate, and have serrated edges, contributing to its common name. This species thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
