Dipterocarpus pseudocornutus

Dipterocarpus pseudocornutus, commonly known as the Malayan dipterocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its large, buttressed roots and smooth, grey bark. The plant produces woody fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by animals. It plays an important role in the rainforest ecosystem as a canopy-forming species.

Its timber is valued for its durability and is used in construction and furniture-making.
