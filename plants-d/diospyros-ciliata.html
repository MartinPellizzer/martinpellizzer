Diospyros ciliata

Diospyros ciliata, commonly known as the silky persimmon, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by edible, orange-red berries. Its bark is rough and fissured, and it is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is valued for both its ornamental and medicinal properties.
