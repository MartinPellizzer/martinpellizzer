Dioscorea ovinala

Dioscorea ovinala, commonly known as the African yambean, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary part used for culinary and medicinal purposes. The plant produces small, green, heart-shaped leaves and bears yellowish flowers that develop into capsule fruits.

Its tubers are rich in starch and have been traditionally used as a food source and in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
