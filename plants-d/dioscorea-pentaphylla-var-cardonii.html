Dioscorea pentaphylla var. cardonii

Dioscorea pentaphylla var. cardonii, commonly known as the Canary Island yam, is a tuberous perennial plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its stolons and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellowish flowers that are typically borne in clusters. Its tubers are edible and have been traditionally used in local cuisines.

The species is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
