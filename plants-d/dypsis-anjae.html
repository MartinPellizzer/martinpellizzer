Dypsis anjae

Dypsis anjae, commonly known as the Anja's areca palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to Madagascar and is characterized by its slender, upright trunk and pinnate leaves that are often dark green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, round fruits. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and relatively easy care requirements.

Dypsis anjae thrives in warm, humid environments and is well-suited for indoor cultivation.
