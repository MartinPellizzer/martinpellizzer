Dryopeia

Dryopeia, commonly known as the "sunset bellflower," is a genus of flowering plants in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

These plants are native to the Mediterranean region and are characterized by their showy, bell-shaped flowers that often display vibrant colors. The leaves are typically ovate or lanceolate, with a rough texture, and the stems are often hairy. Dryopeia species are herbaceous perennials that thrive in well-drained soils and full sun.

They are valued for their ornamental appeal and are sometimes cultivated in gardens for their striking floral displays.
