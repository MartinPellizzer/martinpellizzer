Daubenya fulva

Daubenya fulva, commonly known as the golden reed, is a perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bright green with a slight yellowish tint. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow to orange flowers during the summer months. It thrives in wet or moist environments, often found in swamps or along the edges of ponds.

Daubenya fulva is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize wetland soils.
