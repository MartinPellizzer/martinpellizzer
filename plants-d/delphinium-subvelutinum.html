Delphinium subvelutinum

Delphinium subvelutinum, commonly known as the woolly delphinium, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that bear clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant has soft, hairy leaves that give it a velvety appearance, hence its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in alpine meadows.

Delphinium subvelutinum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
