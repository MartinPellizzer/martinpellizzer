Dictamnus dasycarpus f. velutinus

Dictamnus dasycarpus f. velutinus, commonly known as the velvet dictamnus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, pinkish-red or purplish stems that exude a strong, peppery scent when broken. The plant features basal rosettes of lanceolate, gray-green leaves with a velvety texture. Its flowering stems bear small, clustered flowers that range from white to pale pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
