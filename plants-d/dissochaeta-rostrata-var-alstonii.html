Dissochaeta rostrata var. alstonii

Dissochaeta rostrata var. alstonii, commonly known as the Alston's spiny beardgrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny, bristly inflorescence and slender, wiry stems. The plant typically grows in open, sandy or rocky soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a hairy texture that helps reduce water loss.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
