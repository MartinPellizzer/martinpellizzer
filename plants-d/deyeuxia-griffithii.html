Deyeuxia griffithii

Deyeuxia griffithii, commonly known as the Himalayan flat sedge, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil in high-altitude environments.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
