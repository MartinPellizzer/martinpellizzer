Durio acutifolius

Durio acutifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaf durian, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Sapotaceae and is characterized by its large, compound leaves and fragrant, spiky fruit. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its fruit, while not as commercially popular as the common durian, is valued for its unique flavor and nutritional content.

The tree is also noted for its rapid growth and potential use in agroforestry systems.
