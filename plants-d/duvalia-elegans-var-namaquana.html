Duvalia elegans var. namaquana

Duvalia elegans var. namaquana, commonly known as the Namakwa daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves and produces small, daisy-like yellow flowers with a central disc. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
