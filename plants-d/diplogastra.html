Diplogastra

Diplogastra, commonly known as the common vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and erect, branching stems that often grow along the ground. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by elongated, pod-like fruits. Diplogastra is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil fertility.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.
