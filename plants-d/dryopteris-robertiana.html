Dryopteris robertiana

Dryopteris robertiana, commonly known as the Robert's wood fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and fronds that are typically divided into multiple pinnae. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodland areas with rich, well-drained soil. Its leaves are usually dark green and have a leathery texture, with a distinctive, elegant appearance.

Dryopteris robertiana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to various garden conditions.
