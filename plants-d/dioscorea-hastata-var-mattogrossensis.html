Dioscorea hastata var. mattogrossensis

Dioscorea hastata var. mattogrossensis, commonly known as the Amazonian yam, is a species of tuberous vine native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, yellowish-white flowers that are typically followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits. Its tuberous roots are the primary economically important part, used in traditional medicine and as a food source.

The plant thrives in humid, tropical climates with well-drained soil and partial shade.
