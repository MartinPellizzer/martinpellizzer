Draba minima

Draba minima, commonly known as the dwarf whitlow grass, is a small flowering plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows to a height of only a few centimeters, with basal leaves and a dense cluster of flowers at the top. It thrives in nutrient-poor, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, harsh environments.

Draba minima plays a role in stabilizing soil in its habitat and is often found in tundra and mountainous regions.
