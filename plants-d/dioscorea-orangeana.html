Dioscorea orangeana

Dioscorea orangeana, commonly known as the orange yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary edible part of the plant. The plant produces small, green, heart-shaped leaves and yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its tubers are valued for their starchy texture and are used in various culinary traditions.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
