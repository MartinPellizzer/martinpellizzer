Dendrobium chrysotoxum

Dendrobium chrysotoxum, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with cylindrical pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant produces showy, golden-yellow flowers with a sweet fragrance, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Dendrobium chrysotoxum is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
