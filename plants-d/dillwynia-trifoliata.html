Dillwynia trifoliata

Dillwynia trifoliata, commonly known as the three-leaved wattle, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers in spring, which are clustered in racemes. It typically grows in sandy or loamy soils and is often found in open woodlands and heathlands.

Dillwynia trifoliata is a hardy perennial that is well adapted to the temperate climate of its native region.
