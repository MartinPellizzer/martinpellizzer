Dianella dubia

Dianella dubia, commonly known as the slender flax lily, is a species of flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky areas. The plant features grass-like leaves that are narrow and slightly curved, with a bluish-green hue. Its flowers are small, white or pale blue, and are clustered in dense inflorescences at the base of the plant.

Dianella dubia is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological balance of its native habitat.
