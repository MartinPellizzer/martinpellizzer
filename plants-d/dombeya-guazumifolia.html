Dombeya guazumifolia

Dombeya guazumifolia, commonly known as the Guazumifolia Dombeya, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that typically bloom in clusters, often displaying shades of white, pink, or red. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Dombeya guazumifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
