Dioscorea brandisii

Dioscorea brandisii, commonly known as the Brandis yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary part used for culinary and medicinal purposes. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a hairy texture, contributing to its distinct appearance.

The tubers of Dioscorea brandisii are valued for their nutritional content and traditional uses in various African cultures.
