Digitalis gyspergerae

Digitalis gyspergerae, commonly known as the Scottish foxglove, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Scottish Highlands.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its tall, spires of hooded flowers that range in color from purple to pink. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are adapted for pollination by bumblebees.

Digitalis gyspergerae is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
