Dianthus Crinitus Subsp. Turcomanicus

Dianthus crinitus subsp. turcomanicus is a rare and striking plant native to central Asia. It is known for its vibrant pink or red flowers that bloom in late spring. This subspecies thrives in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its hardy nature. Its unique appearance makes it a sought-after addition to rock gardens and botanical collections. Despite its beauty, it remains relatively obscure in the global horticultural community.
