Dalbergia tingens

Dalbergia tingens, commonly known as the purpleheart tree, is a tropical leguminous tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dark purple to reddish-brown heartwood, which is highly valued for its durability and aesthetic appeal. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in racemes. Its fruit is a legume pod containing flattened, winged seeds.

Dalbergia tingens is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and for its valuable timber.
