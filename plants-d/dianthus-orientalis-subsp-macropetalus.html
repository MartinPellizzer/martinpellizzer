Dianthus orientalis subsp. macropetalus

Dianthus orientalis subsp.

macropetalus, commonly known as the large-petaled oriental campion, is a flowering plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early summer.

The plant has linear, grass-like leaves and grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its prolonged blooming period and ability to attract pollinators.
