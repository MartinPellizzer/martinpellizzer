Dactylorhiza majalis subsp. majalis

Dactylorhiza majalis subsp.

majalis, commonly known as the early purple orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of purple or violet flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and heathlands. It has a sympodial growth habit, with rhizomes that allow it to spread vegetatively.

The flowers are often visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance.
