Daphne decaisneana

Daphne decaisneana, commonly known as the Chinese daphne, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring, typically in shades of pink and white. The plant has smooth, grayish-brown bark and lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Due to its ornamental value and unique fragrance, it is often appreciated in horticultural settings.
