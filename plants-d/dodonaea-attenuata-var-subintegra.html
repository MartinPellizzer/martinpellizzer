Dodonaea attenuata var. subintegra

Dodonaea attenuata var. subintegra, commonly known as the silky hoppegroven, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It features slender, ascending branches covered in dense, silky white to grey pubescence. The leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a soft, hairy texture and a pointed tip. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits.

This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.
