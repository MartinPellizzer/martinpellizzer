Dioscorea oppositifolia var. thwaitesii

Dioscorea oppositifolia var. thwaitesii, commonly known as the Thai yam, is a tuberous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits. Its tubers are valued for their medicinal properties and are used in traditional medicine.

The species is also noted for its adaptability to various soil types and its potential as a food source.
