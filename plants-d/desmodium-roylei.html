Desmodium roylei

Desmodium roylei, commonly known as the velvet bean or horse bean, is a leguminous plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are often covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. The plant produces small, clustered pods that contain edible seeds, which are rich in protein and used in various cuisines. It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions and is often found in disturbed soils.

Desmodium roylei also has nitrogen-fixing capabilities, making it valuable for soil enrichment.
