Dendrobium porphyrophyllum

Dendrobium porphyrophyllum, commonly known as the Purple-veined Dendrobium, is an epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a tall, cane-like stem with a dense cluster of lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, often with purple veins. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining potting media.

Due to its ornamental value, it is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and ease of cultivation.
