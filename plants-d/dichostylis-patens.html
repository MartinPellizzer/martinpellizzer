Dichostylis patens

Dichostylis patens, commonly known as the slender dichostylis, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the grass family, Poaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and disturbed soils, often forming dense clumps. Its two distinct flower types, known as dimorphic florets, are a notable botanical feature.

Dichostylis patens is also valued for its ornamental potential and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
