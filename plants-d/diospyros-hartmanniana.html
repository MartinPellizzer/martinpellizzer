Diospyros hartmanniana

Diospyros hartmanniana, commonly known as the Hartmann's persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other well-known species like the ebony and persimmon trees. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical in shape and have a leathery texture. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by orange to red berries, which are edible and have a sweet, astringent flavor.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
