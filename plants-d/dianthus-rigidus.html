Dianthus rigidus

Dianthus rigidus, commonly known as the rigid carnation, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and northern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and dense clusters of pink to red flowers. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It blooms from late spring to early summer, producing fragrant, showy flowers that attract pollinators.

Dianthus rigidus is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature.
