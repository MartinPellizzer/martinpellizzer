Dianthus sylvestris f. albiflorus

Dianthus sylvestris f. albiflorus, commonly known as the white-flowered wild carnation, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe.

It features slender, upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers with prominent red or pink markings. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

This cultivar is valued for its delicate appearance and historical use in herbal medicine.
