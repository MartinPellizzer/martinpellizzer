Danthonia angustifolia var. micranthera

Danthonia angustifolia var. micranthera, commonly known as narrowleaf hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are usually narrow and slightly curled at the edges. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, pale green to brownish flowers.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
