Dryopteris submarginalis f. caripensis

Dryopteris submarginalis f. caripensis, commonly known as the Caripan marginal fern, is a fern species native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its fronds that have a distinctive marginal vein pattern. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, such as forest understories, where it thrives in rich, well-drained soils. Its leaves are bi- to tri-pinnate, with a slender and elegant appearance that contributes to its ornamental value.

This fern is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in tropical gardens and greenhouses.
