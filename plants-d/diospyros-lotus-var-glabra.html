Diospyros lotus var. glabra

Diospyros lotus var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth persimmon, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Middle East and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into edible, orange-red fruits with a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.

The variety 'glabra' is distinguished by its lack of hairy foliage, giving it a more refined appearance compared to other varieties.
