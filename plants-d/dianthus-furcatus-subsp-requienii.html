Dianthus furcatus subsp. requienii

Dianthus furcatus subsp.

requienii, commonly known as the Sierra Madre pink, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its dense, branched stems and clusters of bright pink to red flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, often covered in a fine layer of hair.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
