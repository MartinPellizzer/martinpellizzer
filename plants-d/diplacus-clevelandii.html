Diplacus clevelandii

Diplacus clevelandii, commonly known as Cleveland's monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of California.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange to yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.
