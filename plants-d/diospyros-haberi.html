Diospyros haberi

Diospyros haberi, commonly known as the Khasi persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to the northeastern regions of India.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into orange to red berries, which are edible when fully mature.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine and as a food source.
