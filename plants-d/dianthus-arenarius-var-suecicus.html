Dianthus arenarius var. suecicus

Dianthus arenarius var. suecicus, commonly known as the Swedish sandpink, is a flowering plant native to northern Europe.

It is a low-growing perennial that thrives in sandy and rocky soils, often found in coastal areas and dry grasslands. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers with five petals, arranged in a cluster at the end of each stem. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is known for its hardy nature and ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.

This variety is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
