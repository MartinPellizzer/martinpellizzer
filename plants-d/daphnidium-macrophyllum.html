Daphnidium macrophyllum

Daphnidium macrophyllum, commonly known as the large-leaf daphnidium, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers with four petals, which bloom in the spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
