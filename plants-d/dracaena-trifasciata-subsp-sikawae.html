Dracaena trifasciata subsp. sikawae

Dracaena trifasciata subsp.

sikawae, commonly known as the sikawae dracaena, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the island of Socotra. It features narrow, dark green leaves with distinctive white or pale green vertical stripes, giving it a striking, almost striped appearance.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow-green flowers, followed by red berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers bright, indirect light, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its unique foliage makes it a sought-after ornamental plant in horticulture.
