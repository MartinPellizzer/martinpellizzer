Diapedium latius var. sinuatocrenatum

Diapedium latius var. sinuatocrenatum, commonly known as the sinuate-crenate diapedium, is a variant of the diapedium genus, which belongs to the family Acanthaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology, featuring a combination of sinuate (wavy) and crenate (notched) margins. The plant typically exhibits a compact growth habit with lanceolate to ovate leaves that are often pubescent. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is native to regions in Southeast Asia.

This variant is valued for its ornamental foliage, making it a popular choice in horticultural collections.
