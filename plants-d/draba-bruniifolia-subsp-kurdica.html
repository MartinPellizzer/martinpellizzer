Draba bruniifolia subsp. kurdica

Draba bruniifolia subsp.

kurdica, commonly known as the Kurdish mouse-ear cress, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Middle East and Central Asia. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and cool climates. Its leaves are basal, rosette-like, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are pale yellow to white.

It is often found in high-altitude areas, contributing to the biodiversity of its native ecosystems.
