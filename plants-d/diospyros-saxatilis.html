Diospyros saxatilis

Diospyros saxatilis, commonly known as the rock persimmon, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by edible, orange-red berries. It is well adapted to rocky soils and can thrive in areas with low rainfall.

Diospyros saxatilis plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing food for various wildlife species.
