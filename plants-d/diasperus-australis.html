Diasperus australis

Diasperus australis, commonly known as the Australian sandbur, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, spiny fruits that are adapted for wind dispersal. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.

Diasperus australis is known for its resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.
