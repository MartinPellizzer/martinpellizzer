Driessenia sinensis

Driessenia sinensis, commonly known as the Chinese water chestnut, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to China.

It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and produces edible, tuberous roots that are harvested for culinary use. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, while its flowers are small and inconspicuous, often greenish-white in color.

Driessenia sinensis is valued not only for its nutritional content but also for its role in traditional Chinese medicine.
