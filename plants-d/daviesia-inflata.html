Daviesia inflata

Daviesia inflata, commonly known as the inflated daviesia, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It belongs to the family Proteaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, inflated seed pods that give it its common name. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers during the spring and summer seasons. Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Daviesia inflata is adapted to grow in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal areas.
