Dypsis utilis

Dypsis utilis, commonly known as the Madagascar palm, is a member of the Arecaceae family.

It is native to Madagascar and is characterized by its unique, tree-like structure with a short trunk and a crown of feathery leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by bright orange fruits containing edible seeds. It is adapted to arid environments and requires well-drained soil and minimal water.

Dypsis utilis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and resilience.
