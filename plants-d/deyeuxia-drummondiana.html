Deyeuxia drummondiana

Deyeuxia drummondiana, commonly known as Drummond's flat sedge, is a grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes and prairies. It has a fibrous root system and is often found in disturbed or transitional habitats.

Deyeuxia drummondiana plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
