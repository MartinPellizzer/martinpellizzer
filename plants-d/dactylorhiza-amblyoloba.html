Dactylorhiza amblyoloba

Dactylorhiza amblyoloba, commonly known as the narrow-lobed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its erect, leafy stems and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically blooms in late summer, producing pink or purple flowers with a distinctive spurred lip. It thrives in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and marshy areas.

Dactylorhiza amblyoloba is also known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its beauty.
