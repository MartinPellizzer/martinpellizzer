Descurainia paradisa

Descurainia paradisa, commonly known as the paradise flax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features slender, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its small, yellow flowers are arranged in racemes and bloom in the summer months.

Descurainia paradisa is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
