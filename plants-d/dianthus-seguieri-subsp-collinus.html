Dianthus seguieri subsp. collinus

Dianthus seguieri subsp.

collinus, commonly known as the alpine pink, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is a perennial species that typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils.

The plant features compact, low-growing stems with showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or alpine landscapes.
