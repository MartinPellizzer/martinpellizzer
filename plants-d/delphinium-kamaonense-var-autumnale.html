Delphinium kamaonense var. autumnale

Delphinium kamaonense var. autumnale, commonly known as the autumn delphinium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems that bear large, showy flowers in shades of blue to purple. The plant typically blooms in late summer to early autumn, making it a notable addition to gardens in temperate climates. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the flowers are arranged in dense spikes, adding both ornamental value and ecological interest.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, though it can tolerate partial shade.
