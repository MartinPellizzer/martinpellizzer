Dianthus elongatus

Dianthus elongatus, commonly known as the slender campion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and clusters of pink to red, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in meadows and along roadsides. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a slightly hairy texture.

Dianthus elongatus is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like carnations and pinks.
