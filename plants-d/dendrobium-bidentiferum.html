Dendrobium bidentiferum

Dendrobium bidentiferum, commonly known as the two-toothed dendrobium, is an epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with multiple stems that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, showy flowers in shades of yellow and orange, typically during the rainy season. Its leaves are linear and arranged spirally along the stems, and it thrives in warm, humid environments with bright indirect light.

Dendrobium bidentiferum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its long-lasting blooms.
