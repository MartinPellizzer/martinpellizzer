Digitaria adusta

Digitaria adusta, commonly known as blackgrass or velvetgrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves with a fine, hairy texture. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing pale green to brownish flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along riverbanks.

Digitaria adusta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
