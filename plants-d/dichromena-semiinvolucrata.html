Dichromena semiinvolucrata

Dichromena semiinvolucrata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved dichromena, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, opposite leaves that are typically linear to lance-shaped. The plant produces small, clustered flowers with four petals, often in shades of white or pale pink. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
