Dioscorea beecheyi

Dioscorea beecheyi, commonly known as the beechey yam, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary part used for medicinal and culinary purposes. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by berries that turn red when mature. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are hairy and often twine around supporting structures.

The tubers of Dioscorea beecheyi contain compounds that have been traditionally used in herbal medicine for their potential health benefits.
