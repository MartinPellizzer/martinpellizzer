Drosera leucostigma

Drosera leucostigma, commonly known as the white-stemmed sundew, is a carnivorous plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its rosette of sticky, glandular leaves that trap insects. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in spring, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and requires high humidity to flourish.

Due to its unique trapping mechanism and aesthetic features, it is a popular choice for carnivorous plant enthusiasts.
