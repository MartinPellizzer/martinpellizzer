Diapedium heterostegium

Diapedium heterostegium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf water plantain, is a aquatic plant native to freshwater habitats in Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, ribbon-like leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette formation. The plant has small, white flowers that bloom in the summer months, and it produces elongated, cylindrical fruits. It thrives in still or slow-moving waters and is often found in ponds and marshes.

Diapedium heterostegium plays a role in water filtration and provides habitat for various aquatic organisms.
