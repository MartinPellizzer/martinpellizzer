Deyeuxia viridiflavescens

Deyeuxia viridiflavescens, commonly known as the green-yellow bulrush, is a perennial grass species native to wetland habitats in East Asia.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, often found in marshes, ponds, and along the edges of lakes. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are often slightly hairy, and its leaves are narrow with a grass-like texture. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are loosely arranged, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This species plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
