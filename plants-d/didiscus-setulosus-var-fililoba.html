Didiscus setulosus var. fililoba

Didiscus setulosus var. fililoba, commonly known as the hairy didiscus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features slender, branching stems covered in fine, hairy glands, which give it its common name. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a velvety texture with prominent veins. Its flowers are small, clustered in umbels, and typically have five petals with a distinctive white or pale pink coloration.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in shaded garden environments.
