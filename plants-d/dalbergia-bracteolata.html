Dalbergia bracteolata

Dalbergia bracteolata, commonly known as the African rosewood, is a small to medium-sized leguminous tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, dark-colored wood, which is highly valued for its aesthetic and structural properties. The plant has bipinnate leaves, and its flowers are typically small and clustered in racemes. It produces small, round pods that contain several seeds.

This species is often found in savanna and woodland habitats, and it plays a role in local ecosystems as a food source for various animals.
