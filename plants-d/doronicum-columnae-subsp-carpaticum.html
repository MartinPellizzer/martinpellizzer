Doronicum columnae subsp. carpaticum

Doronicum columnae subsp.

carpaticum, commonly known as the Carpathian columnar bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Carpathian Mountains. It features upright, slender stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height, covered with lance-shaped, dark green leaves.

The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, yellow flowers with a distinctive purple throat, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, calcareous soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
