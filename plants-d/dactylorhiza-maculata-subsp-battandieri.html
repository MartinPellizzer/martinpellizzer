Dactylorhiza maculata subsp. battandieri

Dactylorhiza maculata subsp.

battandieri, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and heathlands.

The plant features a tall, erect stem with several elongated, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are arranged in a spike and are usually pink or purple with a distinctive dark spot on the lip.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens.
