Dasyochloa argentina var. aristiglumis

Dasyochloa argentina var. aristiglumis, commonly known as the Argentine dasyochloa, is a grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves. The inflorescence consists of a panicle with spikelets that are often bristly or hairy. This variety is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance.

It is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardiness and ability to stabilize soil.
