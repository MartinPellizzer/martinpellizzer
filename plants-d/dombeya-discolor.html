Dombeya discolor

Dombeya discolor, commonly known as the white dombeya, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has simple, opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowering stems are typically erect and can reach heights of up to 2 meters.

Dombeya discolor is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and adaptability to well-drained soils.
