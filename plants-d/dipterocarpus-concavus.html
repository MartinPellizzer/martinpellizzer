Dipterocarpus concavus

Dipterocarpus concavus, commonly known as the broad-leafed dipterocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that are usually opposite or whorled in arrangement. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or greenish in color. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

This species plays an important ecological role in tropical rainforests, contributing to biodiversity and soil stability.
