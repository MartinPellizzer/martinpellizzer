Draba × lindblomii

Draba × lindblomii, commonly known as the Lindblom's whitlow grass, is a hybrid plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with basal leaves and small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters. Native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, it thrives in rocky and alpine environments. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
