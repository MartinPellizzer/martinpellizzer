Desmodium elegans

Desmodium elegans, commonly known as the velvet bean or slender desmodium, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in racemes. The plant produces pods containing small, elliptical seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Desmodium elegans has a deep root system that helps in nitrogen fixation, making it valuable for soil enrichment.

It is also known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions, contributing to its ecological significance.
