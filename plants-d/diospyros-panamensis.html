Diospyros panamensis

Diospyros panamensis, commonly known as the Panama persimmon, is a species of flowering plant in the Ebenaceae family.

It is native to Central America, particularly Panama, and grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is a small to medium-sized tree with smooth, gray bark and lance-shaped leaves. Its fruit is a small, orange to reddish berry that ripens in the dry season.

The fruit is edible, with a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor, and is used in local cuisine and traditional medicine.
