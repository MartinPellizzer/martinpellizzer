Disa tenella subsp. tenella

Disa tenella subsp.

tenella, commonly known as the dwarf disa, is a small, flowering plant native to South Africa. It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its compact growth habit and delicate appearance.

The plant produces striking, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red, with a distinctive yellow throat. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
