Diospyros ellipticifolia f. crispa

Diospyros ellipticifolia f. crispa, commonly known as the crisped persimmon, is a rare and unique cultivar of the persimmon tree.

It is characterized by its distinctive, crisped leaf margins, which give it a textured appearance. The plant is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Thailand, where it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. It produces small, orange-red fruits that are typically not edible due to their high tannin content.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique foliage.
