Draba asprella var. stelligera

Draba asprella var. stelligera, commonly known as the alpine mouse-ear cress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. It has basal leaves that are lobed and hairy, and its stems are often covered in fine pubescence.

This variety is notable for its resilience to cold climates and is often found in high-altitude meadows.
