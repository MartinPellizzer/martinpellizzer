Dichilus strictus

Dichilus strictus, commonly known as the narrowleaf dichilus, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal and sandy soils, often in areas with high saline conditions. Its flowering period occurs during the warmer months, attracting various pollinators such as bees and birds.

Dichilus strictus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature.
