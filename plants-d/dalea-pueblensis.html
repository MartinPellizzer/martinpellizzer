Dalea pueblensis

Dalea pueblensis, commonly known as the Pueblo milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators.

Dalea pueblensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in xeriscapic gardens.
