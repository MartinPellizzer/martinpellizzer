Dalbergia enneaphylla

Dalbergia enneaphylla, commonly known as the African rosewood, is a species of leguminous tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, dark-colored wood, which is highly valued for its aesthetic and structural properties. The plant has bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small leaflets, giving it a feathery appearance. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by elongated pods containing flat, seed-like structures.

The tree is often found in moist, well-drained soils and plays an important role in local ecosystems.
