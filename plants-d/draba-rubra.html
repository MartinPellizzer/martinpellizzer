Draba rubra

Draba rubra, commonly known as the red rockcress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 5 to 15 centimeters. The plant features basal leaves that are pinnately lobed and bright green, often with a waxy coating. Its flowers are small, typically pink to red, and arranged in a cluster at the top of the stem.

Draba rubra is native to alpine and arctic regions of Europe and Asia, where it thrives in rocky and well-drained soils.
