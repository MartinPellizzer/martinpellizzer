Dendrobium aemulans

Dendrobium aemulans, commonly known as the imitation orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is an epiphytic plant, meaning it grows on the surface of trees or other structures without being parasitic. The plant produces long, pendulous inflorescences with clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. Its leaves are fleshy and arranged in a rosette pattern, which helps it store water in its natural habitat.

Dendrobium aemulans is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
