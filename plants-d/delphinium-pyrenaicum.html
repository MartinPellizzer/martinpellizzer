Delphinium pyrenaicum

Delphinium pyrenaicum, commonly known as the Pyrenean larkspur, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Pyrenees mountain range.

It features tall, erect stems that bear clusters of spurred blue to purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has palmately divided, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and slightly hairy. It thrives in well-drained, loamy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Delphinium pyrenaicum is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and is also noted for its toxicity to livestock.
