Draba aspera var. pilosula

Draba aspera var. pilosula, commonly known as the hairy rockcress, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments. The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, hairy leaves and produces small, yellow flowers in early spring. Its flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences and are pollinated primarily by insects.

The species is notable for its hardy nature and ability to survive in harsh, cold climates.
