Delissea obtusa

Delissea obtusa, commonly known as the broadleaf lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its botanical features include a rhizomatous root system and showy, bell-shaped blooms that appear in late spring.

Delissea obtusa is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
