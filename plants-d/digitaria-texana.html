Digitaria texana

Digitaria texana, commonly known as Texas grass or sand dropseed, is a perennial grass native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-green foliage and produces small, pale blue to purple flower spikes. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open areas such as prairies and roadsides. It is drought-tolerant and has a clumping growth habit.

Digitaria texana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for beneficial insects.
