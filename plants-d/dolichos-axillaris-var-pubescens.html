Dolichos axillaris var. pubescens

Dolichos axillaris var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy dolichos, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which are often pubescent, giving it its varietal name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by elongated, bean-like pods containing edible seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along forest edges.

Its botanical features make it adaptable to various ecological conditions, supporting both wildlife and agricultural use.
