Dimeria ornithopoda var. megalantha

Dimeria ornithopoda var. megalantha, commonly known as the giant desert dandelion, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and large, lobed leaves that are adapted to store water. The plant produces yellow, dandelion-like flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds. Its botanical features include a branching stem system and a deep root system that allows it to survive in harsh, dry environments.

It is often found in rocky soils and plays a role in stabilizing desert landscapes.
