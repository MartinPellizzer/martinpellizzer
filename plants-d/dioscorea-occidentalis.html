Dioscorea occidentalis

Dioscorea occidentalis, commonly known as wild yam, is a climbing vine native to the Americas.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary part used for medicinal and agricultural purposes. The plant produces small, white to greenish flowers and has compound leaves with three to five leaflets. Its tubers contain compounds like diosgenin, which are chemically similar to plant sterols and have been studied for their potential pharmaceutical applications.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
