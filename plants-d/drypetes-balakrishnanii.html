Drypetes balakrishnanii

Drypetes balakrishnanii, commonly known as the Malabar ironwood, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that typically grows in moist, tropical forests. The plant is characterized by its dense, hard wood and dark green, glossy leaves. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, round fruits.

The species is valued for its durable timber and is also used in traditional medicine.
