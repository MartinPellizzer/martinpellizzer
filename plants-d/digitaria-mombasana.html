Digitaria mombasana

Digitaria mombasana, commonly known as Mombasa grass, is a perennial grass species native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and along riverbanks.

Digitaria mombasana is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and is sometimes used for forage or erosion control.
