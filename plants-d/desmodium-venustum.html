Desmodium venustum

Desmodium venustum, commonly known as the purple burr vetch, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces distinctive, spiny pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals. Desmodium venustum is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in full sun to partial shade.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it useful in soil enrichment and ecological restoration projects.
