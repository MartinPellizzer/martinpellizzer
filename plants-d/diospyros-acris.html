Diospyros acris

Diospyros acris, commonly known as the small persimmon, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other persimmon species. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. Its small, yellowish flowers are followed by edible, orange-red berries that are typically harvested when fully ripe.

The wood of Diospyros acris is hard and durable, making it valuable for crafting and traditional uses.
