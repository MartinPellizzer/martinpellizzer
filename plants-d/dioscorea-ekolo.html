Dioscorea ekolo

Dioscorea ekolo, commonly known as the African yam bean, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces tuberous roots that are used as a food source, similar to other yam species. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its nutritional value.
