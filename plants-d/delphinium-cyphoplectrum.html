Delphinium cyphoplectrum

Delphinium cyphoplectrum, commonly known as the alpine delphinium, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces showy, blue to violet flowers in clusters at the top of the stem, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a coarse texture and a bluish-green hue.

Delphinium cyphoplectrum thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows and subalpine habitats.
