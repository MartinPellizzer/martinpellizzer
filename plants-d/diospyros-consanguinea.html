Diospyros consanguinea

Diospyros consanguinea, commonly known as the African persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, orange-red fruits resembling persimmons. Its fruits are typically consumed fresh or used in traditional medicine, and the tree is valued for its hard, durable wood.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas across its native range.
