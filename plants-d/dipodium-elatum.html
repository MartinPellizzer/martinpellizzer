Dipodium elatum

Dipodium elatum, commonly known as the tall dipodium, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, commonly known as the sedge family. The plant is characterized by its upright, grass-like appearance with slender, erect stems. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, typical of the Cyperaceae family.

Dipodium elatum is often found in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands or along stream banks.
