Diospyros okkesii

Diospyros okkesii, commonly known as the Malayan persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into orange-red, fleshy fruits. Its fruit is edible, with a sweet and sometimes astringent flavor, and is used in local cuisines.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
