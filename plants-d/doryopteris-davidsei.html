Doryopteris davidsei

Doryopteris davidsei, commonly known as the David's holly fern, is a species of fern native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its delicate, pinnately compound fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in forest understories. Its leaves are usually dark green and have a leathery texture, with finely divided pinnae.

Doryopteris davidsei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic foliage.
