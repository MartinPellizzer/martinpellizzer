Dichapetalum martineaui

Dichapetalum martineaui, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually ovate or elliptic in shape. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for cultivation in warm climates.

The bark and leaves of Dichapetalum martineaui have been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
