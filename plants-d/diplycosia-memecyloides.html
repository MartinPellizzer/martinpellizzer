Diplycosia memecyloides

Diplycosia memecyloides, commonly known as the golden mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant native to the southeastern United States.

It relies on specific host trees, such as oaks and hickories, for water and nutrients while performing photosynthesis to some extent. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are pollinated by insects, followed by small, fleshy berries that are dispersed by birds. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss.
