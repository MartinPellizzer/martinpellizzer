Dischisma ciliatum

Dischisma ciliatum, commonly known as the silky dischisma, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and foliage with silky, hairy textures. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid to semi-arid environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped with fine cilia along the edges, contributing to its common name.

Dischisma ciliatum is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands.
