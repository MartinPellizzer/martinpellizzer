Daphne oleoides subsp. transcaucasica

Daphne oleoides subsp.

transcaucasica, commonly known as the Caucasian daphne, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region. It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 meter.

The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers in early spring, which are followed by small, orange-red berries. Its leaves are simple, elliptical, and have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its showy blooms and winter interest.
