Dactylorhiza haematodes

Dactylorhiza haematodes, commonly known as the blood orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red or purple flowers, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit and produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are typically linear and grass-like, adapting to its woodland or heathland habitats.

Dactylorhiza haematodes is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is often found in moist, shaded environments.
