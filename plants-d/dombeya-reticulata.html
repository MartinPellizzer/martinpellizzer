Dombeya reticulata

Dombeya reticulata, commonly known as the reticulate dombeya, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often appearing in clusters. The plant has a compact growth habit with grayish bark and oval, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Dombeya reticulata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
