Diplotaxis heterophylla

Diplotaxis heterophylla, commonly known as the African ironweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial species that typically grows in dry, sandy soils. The plant features alternate, pinnately compound leaves with a distinctive blue-green coloration. Its small yellow flowers are arranged in dense, terminal clusters, and it produces slender, hairy seed pods.

Diplotaxis heterophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking foliage.
