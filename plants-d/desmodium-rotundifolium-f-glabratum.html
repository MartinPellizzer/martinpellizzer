Desmodium rotundifolium f. glabratum

Desmodium rotundifolium f. glabratum, commonly known as the glabrate cowpea, is a variant of the common desmodium species.

It is a leguminous plant characterized by its small, round leaves and clusters of pea-like flowers. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions and thrives in well-drained soils. It has a creeping growth habit and produces small, edible pods that contain nutritious seeds.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a forage crop.
