Dianthus caryophyllus var. brevifolius

Dianthus caryophyllus var. brevifolius, commonly known as the dwarf carnation, is a compact, low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy, double flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, with a spicy, clove-like fragrance. The leaves are narrow, grass-like, and densely packed, giving the plant a neat appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
