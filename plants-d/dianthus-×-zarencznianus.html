Dianthus × zarencznianus

Dianthus × zarencznianus, commonly known as the hybrid carnation, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Dianthus chinensis and Dianthus barbatus, combining traits from both parent species. The plant is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that come in a wide range of colors, including red, pink, white, and purple. It typically grows as an annual or biennial herb with upright stems and fleshy leaves.

Dianthus × zarencznianus is widely cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and floral arrangements.
