Desmodium orinocense

Desmodium orinocense, commonly known as the Orinoco burr, is a leguminous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive pod-like fruits that contain small, hard seeds. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides. Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it beneficial for soil enrichment.

The species is also known for its rapid growth and potential use in erosion control.
