Diplazium urticifolium

Diplazium urticifolium, commonly known as stinging nettle fern, is a fern species characterized by its distinctive, stinging leaves that can cause irritation upon contact.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is native to various regions in Asia, including China, India, and Southeast Asia. The plant features a creeping rhizome with erect, branched fronds that are typically bi- or tri-pinnate. Its sporangia are clustered in sori on the underside of the fronds, and the plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas.

Diplazium urticifolium is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
