Dioscorea lindiensis

Dioscorea lindiensis, commonly known as Lindi's yam, is a member of the Dioscoreaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a climbing vine that produces tuberous roots, which are the primary part used for culinary and medicinal purposes. The plant has compound leaves with long petioles and small, heart-shaped leaflets. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant, and it relies on insect pollination.

The tubers are rich in starch and have been traditionally used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
