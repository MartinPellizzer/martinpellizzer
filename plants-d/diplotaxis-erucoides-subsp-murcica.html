Diplotaxis erucoides subsp. murcica

Diplotaxis erucoides subsp.

murcica, commonly known as the Murcia cress, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid conditions. Its leaves are pinnately lobed and have a slightly peppery taste, similar to other members of the cabbage family.

It is often found in open habitats such as roadsides and disturbed areas.
