Dypsis lutea

Dypsis lutea, commonly known as the yellow ladyfinger, is a tropical plant native to Madagascar.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family, which includes palms, and is characterized by its slender, erect stem and large, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green inflorescences with small, fragrant flowers, which are followed by elongated, finger-like fruits. Dypsis lutea is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and unique appearance.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
