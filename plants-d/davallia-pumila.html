Davallia pumila

Davallia pumila, commonly known as the button fern, is a small evergreen fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features delicate, pinnate fronds that are typically less than a foot in length. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, with short, thick rhizomes that help anchor it to surfaces. Its leaves are usually bright green and have a leathery texture.

It thrives in humid environments with indirect light and is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental appeal.
