Dipteracanthus tubiflorus

Dipteracanthus tubiflorus, commonly known as the tubular-flowered dipteracanthus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive tubular flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. The plant belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae, which includes many tropical tree species. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The flowers are adapted for pollination by insects, particularly bees and wasps.
