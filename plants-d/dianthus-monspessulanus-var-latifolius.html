Dianthus monspessulanus var. latifolius

Dianthus monspessulanus var. latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red. The leaves are broad and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
