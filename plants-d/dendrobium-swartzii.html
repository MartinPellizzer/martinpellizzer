Dendrobium swartzii

Dendrobium swartzii, commonly known as the Swartz's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant produces long, arching inflorescences bearing numerous fragrant, showy flowers in shades of yellow and white. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, often arranged in two rows along the stem.

Dendrobium swartzii thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental beauty in horticulture.
