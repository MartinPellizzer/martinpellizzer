Dombeya lecomtei subsp. trichostyla

Dombeya lecomtei subsp.

trichostyla, commonly known as the hairy-styled dombeya, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring.

The plant features woody stems and lance-shaped leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested or rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it remains relatively rare in horticulture.
