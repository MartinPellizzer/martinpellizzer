Discocactus alteolens

Discocactus alteolens, commonly known as the highland discocactus, is a species native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is a cactus characterized by its short, cylindrical stems and dense clusters of spines. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are typically borne at the base of the stems. Discocactus alteolens is adapted to high-altitude environments, thriving in cool, well-drained soils.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.
