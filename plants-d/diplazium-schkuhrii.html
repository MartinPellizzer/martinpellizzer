Diplazium schkuhrii

Diplazium schkuhrii, commonly known as the Schkuhri fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to tropical regions of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. The plant features a creeping rhizome with arching fronds that have a distinctive, glossy dark green color. Its spore-bearing structures, called sori, are clustered on the undersides of the fronds.

Diplazium schkuhrii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
