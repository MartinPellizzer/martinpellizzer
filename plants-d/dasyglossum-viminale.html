Dasyglossum viminale

Dasyglossum viminale, commonly known as the creeping daisy, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features a mat-forming growth habit with slender, trailing stems that root at the nodes. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center, blooming in spring and summer. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Dasyglossum viminale is adapted to arid and rocky environments, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.
