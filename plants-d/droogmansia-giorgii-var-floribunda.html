Droogmansia giorgii var. floribunda

Droogmansia giorgii var. floribunda, commonly known as the Giant Spider Orchid, is a striking orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that resemble spiders, with long, slender petals and a distinctive, elongated lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its botanical features include a sympodial growth habit and a complex inflorescence with numerous fragrant blooms.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in greenhouses due to its specific environmental requirements.
