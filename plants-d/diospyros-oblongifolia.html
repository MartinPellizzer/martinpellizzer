Diospyros oblongifolia

Diospyros oblongifolia, commonly known as the long-leaf persimmon, is a species of flowering plant in the family Ebenaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand, Vietnam, and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its elongated, oblong leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into edible berries. The fruit is typically orange to red when ripe and has a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor.

It is valued both for its ornamental qualities and its edible fruit, which is used in traditional cuisines.
