Diplachne mucronata

Diplachne mucronata, commonly known as the narrowleaf beakgrass, is a perennial grass native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant has narrow, linear leaves with a pointed tip, and its flowering structures are typically found at the ends of the stems. Diplachne mucronata thrives in a variety of habitats, including grasslands and disturbed soils.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
