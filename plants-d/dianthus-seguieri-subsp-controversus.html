Dianthus seguieri subsp. controversus

Dianthus seguieri subsp.

controversus, commonly known as the rock campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant pink or red flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in full sun conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the flowers are arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the stems.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens and alpine landscapes.
