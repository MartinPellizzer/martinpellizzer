Ditaxis micrandra

Ditaxis micrandra, commonly known as the Cape daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as a shrub with narrow, grayish-green leaves. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in dense, daisy-like clusters, often blooming in spring.

It is well adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky soils.
