Daphne emarginata

Daphne emarginata, commonly known as the marginate daphne, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring, typically in shades of pink to white. The plant has opposite, simple, ovate leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

However, it is toxic to both humans and animals, requiring careful handling.
