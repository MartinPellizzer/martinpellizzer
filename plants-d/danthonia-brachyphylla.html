Danthonia brachyphylla

Danthonia brachyphylla, commonly known as the short-leaved danthonia, is a grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and heathlands. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny flowers, which are pollinated by wind.

Due to its resilience and ability to thrive in harsh conditions, it is an important component of native plant communities.
