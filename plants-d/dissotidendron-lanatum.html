Dissotidendron lanatum

Dissotidendron lanatum, commonly known as the silky dissotidendron, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves that give it a velvety texture. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. Its bark is thick and often has a rough, fissured appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
