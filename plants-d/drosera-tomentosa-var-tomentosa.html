Drosera tomentosa var. tomentosa

Drosera tomentosa var. tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly sundew, is a carnivorous plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, glandular hairs that secrete a sticky substance to trap insects. The plant typically grows in wet, acidic environments such as bogs and heathlands. Its leaves are broad and covered in a thick layer of white, woolly hair, which gives it its common name.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils by supplementing its diet with captured prey.
