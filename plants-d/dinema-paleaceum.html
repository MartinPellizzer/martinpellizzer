Dinema paleaceum

Dinema paleaceum, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of violet, pink, and white, often with a contrasting throat. Its leaves are velvety and heart-shaped, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Dinema paleaceum is popular as a houseplant due to its ability to thrive in indirect light and well-draining soil.
