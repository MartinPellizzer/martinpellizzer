Decaneurum epilejum

Decaneurum epilejum, commonly known as the silver leaf plant, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, dense hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are clustered in terminal spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

The leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.
