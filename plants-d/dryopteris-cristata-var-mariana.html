Dryopteris cristata var. mariana

Dryopteris cristata var. mariana, commonly known as the Mariana lady fern, is a variant of the Christmas fern.

It is a perennial evergreen fern native to the Appalachian Mountains in the eastern United States. The plant features lance-shaped, serrated leaves that remain green throughout the winter. Its distinctive fronds have a curled, crisped appearance, giving it a unique visual appeal.

It thrives in moist, acidic soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.
