Dendromecon agnina

Dendromecon agnina, commonly known as the desert globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant features a woody base with upright, branching stems covered in soft, hairy foliage. Its showy yellow flowers bloom in spring, and the plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Dendromecon agnina is adapted to drought conditions and plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils in its native habitat.
