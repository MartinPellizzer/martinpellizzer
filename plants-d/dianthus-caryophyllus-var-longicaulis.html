Dianthus caryophyllus var. longicaulis

Dianthus caryophyllus var. longicaulis, commonly known as the long-stemmed carnation, is a cultivar of the carnation species characterized by its elongated stems and showy, clustered flowers.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family, which includes many ornamental plants with showy blooms. The flowers typically come in a range of colors, including pink, red, white, and purple, and are often used in floral arrangements. This variety is valued for its compact growth habit and ability to thrive in well-drained soil.

It is also known for its strong, spicy fragrance, which is a hallmark of the Dianthus genus.
