Dipterocarpus hasseltii

Dipterocarpus hasseltii, commonly known as the Malayan ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hard, heavy wood and can grow up to 40 meters tall. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits.

This species plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, contributing to biodiversity and soil stability.
