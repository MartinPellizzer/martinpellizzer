Dischidia galactantha

Dischidia galactantha, commonly known as the silver vine, is a climbing or trailing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often covered in a silvery or whitish bloom, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and as a decorative plant.

Dischidia galactantha is also known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and its relatively low maintenance requirements.
