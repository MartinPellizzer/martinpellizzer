Dichrostachys cinerea var. pubescens

Dichrostachys cinerea var. pubescens, commonly known as hairy stinkwood, is a shrub or small tree native to Africa.

It is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are followed by flat, winged fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in dry woodlands and savannas.

This species is valued for its hard, durable wood and is also used in traditional medicine.
