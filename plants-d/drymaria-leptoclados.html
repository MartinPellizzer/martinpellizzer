Drymaria leptoclados

Drymaria leptoclados, commonly known as the slender drymaria, is a low-growing perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family, also known as the carnation family, and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Drymaria leptoclados is often used in ornamental gardening for its delicate appearance and ability to thrive in various garden conditions.
