Dendrobium tokai var. crassinerve

Dendrobium tokai var. crassinerve, commonly known as the Tokai thicknerve orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with elongated, cane-like stems that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and yellow, typically during the warm season. Its leaves are strap-shaped and often covered in a dense, velvety pubescence.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is cultivated for both horticultural and commercial purposes.
