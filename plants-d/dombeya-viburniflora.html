Dombeya viburniflora

Dombeya viburniflora, commonly known as the white dombeya, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded growth habit and its clusters of showy, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptical, with a smooth texture and a dark green upper surface. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for birds, contributing to its ecological importance.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
