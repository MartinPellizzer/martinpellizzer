Dactylorhiza majalis subsp. calcifugiens

Dactylorhiza majalis subsp.

calcifugiens, commonly known as the limestone orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to calcareous grasslands and rocky soils in Europe. It has a compact, rhizomatous growth habit with narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces upright spikes of pale yellow to greenish-white flowers with a distinctive three-lobed lip. It blooms in late spring to early summer, typically from May to June.

This subspecies is adapted to nutrient-poor, alkaline environments and is often found in open, sunny habitats.
