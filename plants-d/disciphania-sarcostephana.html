Disciphania sarcostephana

Disciphania sarcostephana, commonly known as the desert star, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of South America.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its compact growth habit and fleshy, succulent leaves. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange, which bloom during the cooler months. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water availability.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for studies on desert flora and xerophytic adaptations.
