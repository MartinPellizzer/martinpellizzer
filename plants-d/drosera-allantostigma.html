Drosera allantostigma

Drosera allantostigma, commonly known as the broad-leaf sundew, is a carnivorous plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its sticky, glandular hairs that secrete a digestive enzyme-laden dew to trap insects. The plant typically grows in wet, acidic environments such as bogs and swamps. Its leaves are broad and often have a rosette structure, with each leaf bearing numerous hair-like structures.

The flowers are small and usually pink or red, appearing on a single stem above the rosette.
