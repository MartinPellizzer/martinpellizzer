Diospyros relit

Diospyros relit, commonly known as the small date palm, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its pinnately compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces edible drupes that are similar in taste to dates, though they are typically smaller. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and fruit production, it is cultivated in various parts of its native range.
