Diospyros elliptica var. foliosa

Diospyros elliptica var. foliosa, commonly known as the broadleaf persimmon, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, featuring broad, elliptical leaves that are often lobed. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange to red, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are typically thick and glossy, and the tree is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates.

The fruit is edible when fully mature and is used in various culinary and medicinal applications.
