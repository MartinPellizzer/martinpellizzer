Dupuisia longifolius

Dupuisia longifolius, commonly known as the long-leaved daisy, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its flowering period occurs during the cooler months, and it plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
