Diospyros ferrea f. glabrescens

Diospyros ferrea f. glabrescens, commonly known as the Brazilian persimmon, is a shrub or small tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, glabrous bark and elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by edible, orange-red fruits with a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
