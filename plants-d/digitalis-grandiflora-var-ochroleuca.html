Digitalis grandiflora var. ochroleuca

Digitalis grandiflora var. ochroleuca, commonly known as the white foxglove, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It features tall, slender stems that bear clusters of tubular, white flowers with a distinctive spurred petal. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a soft, downy coating, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is valued in horticulture for its elegant appearance and historical use in medicine.
