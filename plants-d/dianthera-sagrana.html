Dianthera sagrana

Dianthera sagrana, commonly known as the sacred bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or alpine regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, silvery pubescence.

Dianthera sagrana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
