Dracontium paeoniifolium

Dracontium paeoniifolium, commonly known as the Brazilian arrowroot or arrowleaf, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that resemble the petals of a peony. The plant produces striking yellow flowers with a spadix, typical of the Araceae family. Its roots are commonly used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

However, it is important to note that the plant contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
