Distichocalyx

Distichocalyx, commonly known as the African velvet bean, is a genus of flowering plants in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its twining stems and compound leaves with two rows of leaflets. The flowers are typically small and clustered in racemes, often with a distinctive purple or pink hue. The plant produces pods that contain edible seeds, which are rich in protein and used in local cuisines.

Distichocalyx species are also valued for their ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
