Dryandra platycarpa

Dryandra platycarpa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf dryandra, is a species of shrub native to Western Australia.

It belongs to the family Proteaceae and is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves and striking, cone-shaped inflorescences. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in dense, cylindrical spikes. It is well adapted to the dry, nutrient-poor soils of its native habitat.

Dryandra platycarpa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
