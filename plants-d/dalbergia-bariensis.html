Dalbergia bariensis

Dalbergia bariensis, commonly known as the black rosewood, is a tropical hardwood tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, dense wood with a rich, reddish-brown to black coloration. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family and is known for its slow growth and high value in the furniture and musical instrument industries. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, yellowish-white flowers.

The tree is also valued for its aromatic properties and is often used in traditional medicine.
