Dryopteris erythrovaria

Dryopteris erythrovaria, commonly known as the red-stemmed wood fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive reddish-brown stems that remain visible throughout the winter. The fronds are bipinnate, with a delicate, lace-like appearance, and they unfurl in a spiral pattern. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticultural settings.
