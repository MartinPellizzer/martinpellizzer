Diplycosia subglobularis

Diplycosia subglobularis, commonly known as the globular starflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has small, narrow leaves arranged in a rosette pattern, which helps it conserve water in its arid habitat. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Diplycosia subglobularis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
