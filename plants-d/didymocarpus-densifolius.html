Didymocarpus densifolius

Didymocarpus densifolius, commonly known as the dense-leaved hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
