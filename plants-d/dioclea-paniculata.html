Dioclea paniculata

Dioclea paniculata, commonly known as the Brazilian passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that resemble those of the passionfruit, with a distinctive crown of stamens. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. Its leaves are heart-shaped and can be used as a vegetable in some regions.

Dioclea paniculata thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, requiring full sun and well-drained soil.
