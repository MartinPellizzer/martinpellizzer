Dortmanna martagon

Dortmanna martagon, commonly known as the Turk's cap lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in clusters at the top of its slender stems. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette. Its botanical name reflects its historical association with the town of Dortmanna in ancient times.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
