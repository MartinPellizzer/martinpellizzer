Diploglottis australis

Diploglottis australis, commonly known as the Australian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in coastal regions and sandy soils. The plant features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are alternate and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
