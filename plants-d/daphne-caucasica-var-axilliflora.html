Daphne caucasica var. axilliflora

Daphne caucasica var. axilliflora, commonly known as the Caucasian daphne, is a deciduous shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It features dense, fragrant, white to pinkish flowers that appear in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. Its bark is smooth and reddish-brown, becoming more fissured with age.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and is often grown in gardens for its winter interest.
