Dombeya antsianakensis var. australis

Dombeya antsianakensis var. australis, commonly known as the Southern Dombeya, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in spring. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
