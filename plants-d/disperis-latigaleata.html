Disperis latigaleata

Disperis latigaleata, commonly known as the African orchid, is a member of the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale green with a yellow or orange lip. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grassland or forested areas.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
