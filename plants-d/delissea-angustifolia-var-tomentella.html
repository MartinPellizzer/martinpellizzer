Delissea angustifolia var. tomentella

Delissea angustifolia var. tomentella, commonly known as the hairy narrowleaf delissea, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southern United States.

It features slender, upright stems covered in a fine, white pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a soft, hairy texture on both surfaces. It produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white or pale pink during the spring and summer months.

This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along streambanks.
