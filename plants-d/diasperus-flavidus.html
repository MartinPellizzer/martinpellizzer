Diasperus flavidus

Diasperus flavidus, commonly known as the yellow sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. It is adapted to survive in hot, dry environments with minimal water.

Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access deep soil moisture.
