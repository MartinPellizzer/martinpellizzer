Delphinium umbrosoides

Delphinium umbrosoides, commonly known as the shadowy larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and naturalized areas.
