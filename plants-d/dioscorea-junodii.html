Dioscorea junodii

Dioscorea junodii, commonly known as the Madagascar yam, is a climbing vine native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary part used for propagation and traditional medicine. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers and has heart-shaped leaves with prominent veins. Its tubers are often used in local cuisines and have been studied for their potential medicinal properties.

Dioscorea junodii is an important species in the biodiversity of Madagascar's flora.
