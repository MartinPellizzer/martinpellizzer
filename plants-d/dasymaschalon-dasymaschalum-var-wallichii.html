Dasymaschalon dasymaschalum var. wallichii

Dasymaschalon dasymaschalum var. wallichii, commonly known as the Himalayan blue poppy, is a striking flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its large, showy blue or purple petals that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky, alpine environments where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Due to its rarity and beauty, it is highly valued in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
