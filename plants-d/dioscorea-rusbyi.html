Dioscorea rusbyi

Dioscorea rusbyi, commonly known as Rusby's yam, is a species of flowering plant in the family Dioscoreaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, particularly in countries like Kenya and Tanzania. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically small and greenish-yellow, arranged in clusters.

The tuberous roots of Dioscorea rusbyi are used in traditional medicine and as a food source in some local communities.
