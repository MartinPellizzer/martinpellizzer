Dalea rubricaulis

Dalea rubricaulis, commonly known as the red-stemmed milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright, branching stems and clusters of small, pink to reddish-purple flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in desert or semi-desert environments.

Dalea rubricaulis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native insects and wildlife.
