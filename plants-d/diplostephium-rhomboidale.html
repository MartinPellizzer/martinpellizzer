Diplostephium rhomboidale

Diplostephium rhomboidale, commonly known as the rhomboid leaf spurge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae, which includes many species with milky sap. The plant features alternate, rhomboid-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and clustered in cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in desert environments.
