Delphinium kingianum var. kingianum

Delphinium kingianum var. kingianum, commonly known as the King's delphinium, is a tall, herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It features striking, spurred flowers that range in color from blue to purple, often with a contrasting white or pink base. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. Its flowers bloom in summer, and the plant is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

The botanical characteristics of this species make it a notable example of the Delphinium genus in the Ranunculaceae family.
