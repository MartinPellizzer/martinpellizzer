Dioclea scabra var. brownii

Dioclea scabra var. brownii, commonly known as the brownii variant of the scarlet bower vine, is a climbing plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and vibrant red or orange flowers. The leaves are typically trifoliate, with three leaflets that are ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, legume-like pods that contain several seeds, and it thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking floral display.
