Diospyros tayabensis

Diospyros tayabensis, commonly known as the Philippine persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other well-known species like the ebony and persimmon trees. The plant is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and elliptical leaves that are glossy on the upper surface. Its fruit is a small, yellowish-orange berry that turns red when ripe, and it is edible, though typically consumed in its unripe form.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has cultural significance in local communities.
