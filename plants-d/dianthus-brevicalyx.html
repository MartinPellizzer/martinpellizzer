Dianthus brevicalyx

Dianthus brevicalyx, commonly known as the small-lobed campion, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pink or white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are linear and slightly hairy, while the stems are upright and branched.

Dianthus brevicalyx is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
