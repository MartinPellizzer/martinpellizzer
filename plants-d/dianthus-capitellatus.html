Dianthus capitellatus

Dianthus capitellatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, globular inflorescence composed of numerous small, pinkish-red flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid conditions. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Dianthus capitellatus is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and easy cultivation requirements.
