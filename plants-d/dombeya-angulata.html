Dombeya angulata

Dombeya angulata, commonly known as the angular dombeya, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its angular, often ribbed stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, with a distinctive tubular shape. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Dombeya angulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowering displays.
