Dioscorea spicata var. anamallayana

Dioscorea spicata var. anamallayana, commonly known as the Anamallayan yam, is a climbing vine belonging to the family Dioscoreaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of India, particularly in the Western Ghats. The plant produces tuberous roots that are used as a food source, though they are not as widely cultivated as other yam species. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and has a rapid growth rate.

The leaves are heart-shaped with a smooth texture, and the plant produces small, greenish-white flowers.
