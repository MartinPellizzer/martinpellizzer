Drymocallis longisepala subsp. longisepala

Drymocallis longisepala subsp.

longisepala, commonly known as the long-sepaled goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps and stream banks.

The plant produces showy, golden-yellow inflorescences that bloom in late summer to early autumn. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its stems are usually hairy.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
