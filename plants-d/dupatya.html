Dupatya

Dupatya, commonly known as the "silk cotton tree," is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its large, palmate leaves and showy, fluffy flowers that appear in spring. The tree produces a capsule fruit containing numerous cotton-like seeds, which are used in traditional crafts. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their potential healing properties.
