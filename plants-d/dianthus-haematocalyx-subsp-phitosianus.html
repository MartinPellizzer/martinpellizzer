Dianthus haematocalyx subsp. phitosianus

Dianthus haematocalyx subsp.

phitosianus, commonly known as the Persian pink, is a flowering plant native to the mountains of Central Asia. It is characterized by its vibrant pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces compact, clustered inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental horticulture.
