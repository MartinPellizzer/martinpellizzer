Dolicholus tomentosus

Dolicholus tomentosus, commonly known as the woolly tamarind, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and elongated, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds. The plant has compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets and produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its botanical features make it adaptable to various soil types and climates.

The woolly tamarind is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of medicinal compounds.
