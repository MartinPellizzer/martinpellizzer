Dichrocephala paniculata

Dichrocephala paniculata, commonly known as the silverleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with hairy, branched stems and alternate, heart-shaped leaves. Its flowers are yellow and produce achenes with pappus for wind dispersal.

Dichrocephala paniculata is often found in disturbed soils and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
