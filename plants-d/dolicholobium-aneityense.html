Dolicholobium aneityense

Dolicholobium aneityense, commonly known as the Aneitye mountain bean, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes characterized by their pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellowish-white flowers arranged in racemes. Its seeds are enclosed in slender, elongated pods, which contribute to its scientific name "dolicholobium," meaning "long pod.".

This species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and potential ecological roles in its native habitat.
