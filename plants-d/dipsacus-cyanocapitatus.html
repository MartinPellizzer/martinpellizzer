Dipsacus cyanocapitatus

Dipsacus cyanocapitatus, commonly known as the blue-headed teasel, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and distinctive blue-purple flower heads. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture.

Dipsacus cyanocapitatus is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils.
