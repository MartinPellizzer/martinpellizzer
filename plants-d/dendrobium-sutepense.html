Dendrobium sutepense

Dendrobium sutepense, commonly known as the Sutep orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features long, cane-like stems that are often branched and covered in a dense layer of white or pale green velvety hairs. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white, yellow, and pink, typically blooming during the summer months. It thrives in humid, warm environments with bright, indirect light and requires well-draining potting media.

Dendrobium sutepense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
