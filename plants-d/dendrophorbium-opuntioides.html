Dendrophorbium opuntioides

Dendrophorbium opuntioides, commonly known as the desert cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments. The plant features spiny areoles, which are specialized structures that bear flowers and later, fruits. Its flowers are typically yellow or red, adding a vibrant touch to the desert landscape.
