Diospyros rheophytica

Diospyros rheophytica, commonly known as the cliff date tree, is a unique species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage and the production of small, edible fruits that resemble dates. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on rocky or cliff faces. Its leaves are thick and leathery, adapted to retain moisture in its native habitat.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its specialized ecological niche and potential horticultural value.
