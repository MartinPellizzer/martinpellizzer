Dischidia cochleata

Dischidia cochleata, commonly known as the spiral vine, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, curling stems that form a spiral pattern as it grows. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a waxy, glossy surface. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences.

This plant is often grown as an ornamental due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
