Dracocephalum bipinnatum var. biflorum

Dracocephalum bipinnatum var. biflorum, commonly known as the twin-flower blueweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite pairs of lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers in whorls along the upper stem, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental flowers make it a desirable plant for gardeners seeking native, hardy species.
