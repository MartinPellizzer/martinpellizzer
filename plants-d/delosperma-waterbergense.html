Delosperma waterbergense

Delosperma waterbergense, commonly known as the Waterberg iceplant, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that store water, enabling it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces vibrant pink to purple flowers during the summer months, adding ornamental value. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun exposure, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for gardeners in dry climates.
