Daphniphyllum marchandii

Daphniphyllum marchandii, commonly known as the Chinese holly, is a deciduous shrub native to China and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its evergreen, leathery leaves that are typically dark green with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers in spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for shaded garden environments.

Daphniphyllum marchandii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in horticultural settings.
