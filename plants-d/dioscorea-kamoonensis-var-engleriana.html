Dioscorea kamoonensis var. engleriana

Dioscorea kamoonensis var. engleriana, commonly known as the Engler's yam, is a climbing vine native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by tuberous roots, which are the primary economically valuable part. Its tubers are used in traditional medicine and as a food source in some regions.

The species is noted for its adaptability to arid and semi-arid environments.
