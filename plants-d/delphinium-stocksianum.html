Delphinium stocksianum

Delphinium stocksianum, commonly known as the Stocks' larkspur, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, spindly stems that bear clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant produces showy, spurred petals that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are compound, with finely divided foliage that adds a delicate texture to its appearance.

Delphinium stocksianum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and alpine settings.
