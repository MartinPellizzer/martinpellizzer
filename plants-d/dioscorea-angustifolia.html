Dioscorea angustifolia

Dioscorea angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by tuberous roots that are often used in traditional medicine. Its tubers are also edible and have a starchy, sweet flavor when properly prepared.

Due to its medicinal properties and nutritional value, it holds cultural and economic significance in many communities.
