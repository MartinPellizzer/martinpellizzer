Diplacus bigelovii var. bigelovii

Diplacus bigelovii var. bigelovii, commonly known as the desert four o'clock, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that open in the late afternoon and close by morning. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.

The species is notable for its ecological importance, providing nectar for various pollinators in its native habitat.
