Dipteryx charapilla

Dipteryx charapilla, commonly known as the charapilla or yellow heart tree, is a tropical tree native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its large, compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The tree produces hard, dense wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. Its fruit is a legume pod containing edible seeds that are sometimes used in local cuisines.

Dipteryx charapilla also plays an important role in ecological systems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
