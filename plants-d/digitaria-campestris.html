Digitaria campestris

Digitaria campestris, commonly known as common barnyardgrass, is an annual grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its flat, hairless blades and spikelets that are often reddish in color. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is commonly found in agricultural fields. It has a fibrous root system and produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Due to its invasive nature, it is considered a weed in many parts of the world.
