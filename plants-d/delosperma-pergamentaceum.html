Delosperma pergamentaceum

Delosperma pergamentaceum, commonly known as the parchment iceplant, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, blue-green leaves that are covered in a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces vibrant pink to purple flowers during the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it an attractive and low-maintenance addition to gardens.
