Dryas × wyssiana

Dryas × wyssiana, commonly known as the alpine bellflower, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Dryas octopetala and Dryas integrifolia.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia, where it thrives in cold, high-altitude environments. The plant features low-growing, mat-forming stems with clustered, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink. Its leaves are broad, hairy, and adapted to withstand harsh alpine conditions.

Dryas × wyssiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine displays.
