Delphinium ajacis var. minus

Delphinium ajacis var. minus, commonly known as the dwarf larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of less than 30 centimeters, making it a compact and ornamental species. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are arranged in dense spikes.

Due to its toxicity, it should be handled with care and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
