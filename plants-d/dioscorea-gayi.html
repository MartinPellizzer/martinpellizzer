Dioscorea gayi

Dioscorea gayi, commonly known as the mountain yam, is a climbing vine native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary part used for medicinal and culinary purposes. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically followed by oval, yellowish fruits. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are hairy and tend to climb using tendrils.

The species is valued for its potential health benefits and is often used in traditional medicine.
