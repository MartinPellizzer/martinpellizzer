Disciphania convolvulacea

Disciphania convolvulacea, commonly known as the slender morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple. The plant is characterized by its rapid growth habit and twining stems that allow it to climb various supports. Its flowers open in the morning and close by midday, hence the common name.

Botanically, it belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
