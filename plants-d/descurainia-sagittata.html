Descurainia sagittata

Descurainia sagittata, commonly known as the arrowleaf mustard, is an annual flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other mustard plants, and is characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, followed by pods containing several seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in prairies, roadsides, and open fields.

Due to its rapid growth and ability to colonize new areas, it is considered a weed in some regions.
