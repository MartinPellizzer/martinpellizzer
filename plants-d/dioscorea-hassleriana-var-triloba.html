Dioscorea hassleriana var. triloba

Dioscorea hassleriana var. triloba, commonly known as the Trinidad arrowroot, is a climbing perennial vine native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and tuberous roots. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into red berries. Its tubers are edible and have a starchy texture similar to yams.

The species is valued for both its ornamental appeal and culinary uses.
