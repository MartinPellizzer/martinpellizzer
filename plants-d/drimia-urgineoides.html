Drimia urgineoides

Drimia urgineoides, commonly known as the African lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features strap-like, dark green leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces striking, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Drimia urgineoides is also valued for its ornamental beauty and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
