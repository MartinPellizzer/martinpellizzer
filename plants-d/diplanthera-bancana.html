Diplanthera bancana

Diplanthera bancana, commonly known as the banana orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow and white. The plant has strap-like leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
