Dodonaea viscosa var. aspleniifolia

Dodonaea viscosa var. aspleniifolia, commonly known as the silky oaks, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves that give it a delicate, lacy appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to a variety of soil types.

The species is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for landscaping in arid regions.
