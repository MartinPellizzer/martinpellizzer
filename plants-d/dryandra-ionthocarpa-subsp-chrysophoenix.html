Dryandra ionthocarpa subsp. chrysophoenix

Dryandra ionthocarpa subsp.

chrysophoenix, commonly known as the golden dryandra, is a member of the Proteaceae family and is native to Western Australia. It is a small shrub characterized by its dense, cylindrical inflorescences that produce vibrant yellow flowers.

The plant features narrow, leathery leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates with dry summers.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in native gardens.
