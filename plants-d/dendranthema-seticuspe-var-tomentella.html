Dendranthema seticuspe var. tomentella

Dendranthema seticuspe var. tomentella, commonly known as the woolly oxeye, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish-green foliage and bright yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. It produces small, oval-shaped fruits called achenes, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
