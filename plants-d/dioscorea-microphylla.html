Dioscorea microphylla

Dioscorea microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaf yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers and develops tuberous roots that are often used as edible yams. Its botanical structure supports its role as a valuable food source in traditional agriculture.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.
