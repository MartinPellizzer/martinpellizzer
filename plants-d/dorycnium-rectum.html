Dorycnium rectum

Dorycnium rectum, commonly known as the common sainfoin, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant has a taproot system and produces pods containing small, elliptical seeds. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Dorycnium rectum is also known for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
