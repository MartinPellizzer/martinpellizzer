Diplachne jaegeri

Diplachne jaegeri, commonly known as Jaeger's grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically arranged in clusters. Diplachne jaegeri thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and forage for various wildlife species.
