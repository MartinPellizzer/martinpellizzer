Dioscorea trimenii

Dioscorea trimenii, commonly known as the Malayan yam, is a climbing vine belonging to the Dioscoreaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia, and thrives in tropical rainforest environments. The plant produces tuberous roots that are edible and have been used in traditional cuisines. Its leaves are heart-shaped and its flowers are typically yellow or greenish-yellow.

Dioscorea trimenii is valued not only for its nutritional content but also for its role in local agricultural systems.
