Drymonia punctulata

Drymonia punctulata, commonly known as the spotted drymonia, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, acidic soils found in pine and oak woodlands. The plant features upright, branched stems covered in small, waxy leaves. Its showy, bell-shaped flowers are usually pink or purple and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Drymonia punctulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for native pollinators.
