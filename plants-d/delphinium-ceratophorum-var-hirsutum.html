Delphinium ceratophorum var. hirsutum

Delphinium ceratophorum var. hirsutum, commonly known as the hairy delphinium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height, adorned with large, showy blue to purple flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs, giving the plant its "hirsutum" designation. It thrives in well-drained, loamy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This species is valued in gardens for its striking floral display and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
