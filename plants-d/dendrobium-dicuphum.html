Dendrobium dicuphum

Dendrobium dicuphum, commonly known as the cup-shaped orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is an epiphytic orchid that grows on trees and rocks, deriving moisture and nutrients from the air and rain. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with multiple pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its flowers are typically white or pale yellow, with a fragrant scent that attracts pollinators.

Dendrobium dicuphum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its unique floral display.
