Desmodium purpusii

Desmodium purpusii, commonly known as the purple desmodium, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils, where it plays a role in nitrogen fixation due to its symbiotic relationship with rhizobia bacteria. Its growth habit is typically prostrate or semi-erect, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

The species is sometimes cultivated as a forage plant or used in traditional medicine.
