Dendrocalamus semiscandens

Dendrocalamus semiscandens, commonly known as the bamboo giant, is a tall, fast-growing grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its sturdy, hollow stems that can reach heights of up to 20 meters. The plant is widely cultivated for its strong, lightweight culms, which are used in construction, papermaking, and as a sustainable alternative to wood. Its rhizome system allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense clumps that contribute to soil stabilization.

Dendrocalamus semiscandens also plays a vital role in traditional cultural practices and local economies in its native regions.
