Deschampsia brevifolia

Deschampsia brevifolia, commonly known as the shortleaf hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as wet meadows, bogs, and stream banks. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its name. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, spikelet-bearing branches.

Deschampsia brevifolia plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
