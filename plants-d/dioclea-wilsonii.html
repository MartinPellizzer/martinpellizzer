Dioclea wilsonii

Dioclea wilsonii, commonly known as Wilson's dioclea, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its slender stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are often clustered in racemes. Its fruits are pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

Dioclea wilsonii is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in shaded or semi-shaded environments.
