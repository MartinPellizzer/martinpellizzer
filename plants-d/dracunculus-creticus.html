Dracunculus creticus

Dracunculus creticus, commonly known as the Cretan devil's trumpet, is a striking perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, showy, hooded flowers that resemble a trumpet. The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often tinged with purple or reddish hues. Dracunculus creticus thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its unique appearance and ornamental value make it a notable addition to botanical collections.
