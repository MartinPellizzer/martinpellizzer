Dendrochilum javierianum

Dendrochilum javierianum, commonly known as the Javier's tree orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, showy flowers. The plant typically features long, strap-like leaves and has a pseudobulb structure that stores water and nutrients. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.

Dendrochilum javierianum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
