Dianthus euponticus

Dianthus euponticus, commonly known as the Balkan carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its clusters of brightly colored, showy flowers that typically range from pink to red. The plant has upright, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a hardy perennial in its native habitat.

Dianthus euponticus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
