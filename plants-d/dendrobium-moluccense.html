Dendrobium moluccense

Dendrobium moluccense, commonly known as the Moluccan orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, often in clusters along the stems. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining potting media.

Dendrobium moluccense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated for horticultural purposes.
