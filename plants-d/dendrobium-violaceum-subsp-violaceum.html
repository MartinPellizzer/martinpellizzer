Dendrobium violaceum subsp. violaceum

Dendrobium violaceum subsp.

violaceum, commonly known as the violet-stemmed orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia. It is an epiphytic orchid that grows on trees and rocks, typically in tropical and subtropical regions.

The plant features long, cane-like pseudobulbs covered in a dense, violet-colored sheath. Its flowers are usually white or pale purple, with a distinctive, fragrant scent.

It thrives in high light conditions and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
