Dacryodes belemensis

Dacryodes belemensis, commonly known as the African snowbell, is a tropical tree native to western and central Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and elongated, drooping inflorescences that resemble small bells. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The tree is valued for its ornamental features and has some traditional uses in local communities.
