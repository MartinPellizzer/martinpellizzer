Digitaria tararensis

Digitaria tararensis, commonly known as the Tararensis grass, is a species of grass native to South America, particularly found in regions of Brazil and Paraguay.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshy areas and riparian zones. It produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are often wind-pollinated.

Digitaria tararensis is of interest to botanists due to its ecological role and potential for use in wetland restoration projects.
