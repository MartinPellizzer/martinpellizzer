Daphniphyllum acutifolium

Daphniphyllum acutifolium, commonly known as the sharp-leaved daphniphyllum, is a species of evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, lanceolate leaves that are often glossy and have a distinctive reddish or purplish hue on the underside. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that are typically followed by bright red berries. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its foliage and berries make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
