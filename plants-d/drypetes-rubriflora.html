Drypetes rubriflora

Drypetes rubriflora, commonly known as the red-flowered drypetes, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically red or orange in color, which are followed by small, round fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
