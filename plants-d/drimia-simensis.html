Drimia simensis

Drimia simensis, commonly known as the Natal lily, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale purple, blooming in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Drimia simensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in gardens.
