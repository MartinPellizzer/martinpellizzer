Deyeuxia sesquiflora

Deyeuxia sesquiflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaf false rice, is a grass species native to various regions of Asia, including China, India, and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, often in association with rice fields or along waterways. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth surface and a prominent midrib.

Deyeuxia sesquiflora plays a role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
