Dictamnus albus var. obtusiflorus

Dictamnus albus var. obtusiflorus, commonly known as the white dictamnus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features aromatic, glandular leaves that emit a strong, spicy scent when crushed. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its stems are typically hairy and can grow up to 1 meter in height.

The roots are fibrous and often used in traditional medicine for their purported therapeutic properties.
