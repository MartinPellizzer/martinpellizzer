Digitaria adscendens

Digitaria adscendens, commonly known as African grass or bush wheat, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Digitaria adscendens is both a valuable forage plant and a potential weed in agricultural settings.
