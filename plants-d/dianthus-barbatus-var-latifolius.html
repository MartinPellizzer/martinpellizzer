Dianthus barbatus var. latifolius

Dianthus barbatus var. latifolius, commonly known as the Broadleaf Sweet William, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are broad and lance-shaped, contributing to its ornamental appeal in gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for borders and flower beds.

This variety is valued for its vibrant color and long-lasting blooms, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
