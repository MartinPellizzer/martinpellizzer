Dicliptera napierae

Dicliptera napierae, commonly known as Napier's grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences. It is often used as forage for livestock due to its high nutritional value and rapid growth rate.

Additionally, it plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes cultivated for erosion control in degraded lands.
