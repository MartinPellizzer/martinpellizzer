Dennstaedtia zippeliana

Dennstaedtia zippeliana, commonly known as the Zippel's shield fern, is a species of polypodioid fern native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Dennstaedtiaceae and is characterized by its shield-shaped fronds that are typically dense and overlapping. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest undergrowth where it can receive adequate humidity. Its rhizomes are thick and fleshy, allowing it to store water and nutrients efficiently.

The fronds are usually dark green and remain evergreen throughout the year, contributing to its ornamental value in horticulture.
