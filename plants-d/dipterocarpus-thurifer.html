Dipterocarpus thurifer

Dipterocarpus thurifer, commonly known as the white ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and dense, heavy wood. The plant produces large, showy flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its leaves are usually simple, alternate, and have a glossy surface.

The tree plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
