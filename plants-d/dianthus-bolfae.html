Dianthus bolfae

Dianthus bolfae, commonly known as the Bolfa's campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pink or white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces small, showy flowers that attract pollinators.

Dianthus bolfae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its charming appearance.
