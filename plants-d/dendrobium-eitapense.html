Dendrobium eitapense

Dendrobium eitapense, commonly known as the Eitapense orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems that often bear clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, utilizing its aerial roots to absorb moisture and nutrients from the environment. Its flowers bloom during the dry season, adding a striking visual element to its natural habitat.

Dendrobium eitapense is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
