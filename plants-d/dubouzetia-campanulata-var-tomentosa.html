Dubouzetia campanulata var. tomentosa

Dubouzetia campanulata var. tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It features basal leaves that are hairy and often covered in a dense white or gray pubescence, giving it a woolly appearance. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to violet, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its unique morphology and ornamental value make it a sought-after plant for rock gardens and alpine collections.
