Dracocephalum hyssopifolium

Dracocephalum hyssopifolium, commonly known as blue hyssop, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features erect, branching stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a rough texture. The plant produces small, blue or violet flowers arranged in whorls along the stems, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Dracocephalum hyssopifolium is also valued for its aromatic foliage and historical use in herbal medicine.
