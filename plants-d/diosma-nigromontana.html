Diosma nigromontana

Diosma nigromontana, commonly known as black mountain diosma, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant produces dark purple to black flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in a fine pubescence.

Diosma nigromontana is often found in rocky or mountainous habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments.
