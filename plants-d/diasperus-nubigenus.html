Diasperus nubigenus

Diasperus nubigenus, commonly known as the cloud-dwelling spurge, is a unique plant species characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and stems.

It is native to high-altitude regions and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow in color. Its leaves contain milky sap, a feature common among the Euphorbiaceae family.

Diasperus nubigenus is adapted to survive in nutrient-poor soils and extreme temperature fluctuations.
