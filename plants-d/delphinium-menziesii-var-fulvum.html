Delphinium menziesii var. fulvum

Delphinium menziesii var. fulvum, commonly known as the golden larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems that bear clusters of showy, blue to purple flowers with prominent yellow markings. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, typically found in mountainous regions.

Due to its striking appearance, it is a popular choice for gardens and botanical collections.
