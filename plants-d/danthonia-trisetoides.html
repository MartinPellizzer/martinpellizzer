Danthonia trisetoides

Danthonia trisetoides, commonly known as the silky danthonia, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, silver-gray leaves with a soft, silky texture. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is cultivated in some regions for landscaping and erosion control.
