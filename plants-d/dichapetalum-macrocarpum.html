Dichapetalum macrocarpum

Dichapetalum macrocarpum, commonly known as the giant waterwheel tree, is a large evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive whorled arrangement of leaves and its massive, showy yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree can grow up to 40 meters tall, making it one of the tallest species in its native habitat. Its bark is thick and ridged, providing some resistance to pests and diseases.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its ecosystem, supporting various forms of wildlife and contributing to forest biodiversity.
