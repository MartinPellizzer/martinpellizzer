Dendrochilum croceum

Dendrochilum croceum, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a compact, branching structure with small, fleshy leaves that help store water in its natural habitat. The plant produces striking yellow flowers with a sweet fragrance, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining potting mix.

Dendrochilum croceum is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
