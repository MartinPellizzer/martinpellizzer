Dactylis glomerata var. vuyckii

Dactylis glomerata var. vuyckii, commonly known as Vuyck's tufted grass, is a variant of the meadow grass species native to Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It is often found in moist meadows and grasslands, thriving in temperate climates.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and habitat conservation.
