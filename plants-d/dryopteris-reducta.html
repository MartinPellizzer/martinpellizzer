Dryopteris reducta

Dryopteris reducta, commonly known as the maidenhair fern, is a species of evergreen fern characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. The plant features bipinnate leaves that are dark green and often have a slightly curled appearance. It thrives in shady, moist environments and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

Dryopteris reducta is also noted for its ability to tolerate a range of soil conditions, making it a versatile choice for garden settings.
