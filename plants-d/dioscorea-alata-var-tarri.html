Dioscorea alata var. tarri

Dioscorea alata var. tarri, commonly known as Chinese yam, is a variant of the common yam that is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a tuberous perennial vine characterized by its thick, fleshy underground stems that store nutrients. The plant has large, heart-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. Its tubers are widely cultivated for their edible, starchy roots, which are used in various cuisines.

The botanical features of this variant make it an important crop in tropical and subtropical regions.
