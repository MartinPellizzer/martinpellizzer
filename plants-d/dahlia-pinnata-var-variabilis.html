Dahlia pinnata var. variabilis

Dahlia pinnata var. variabilis, commonly known as the variable garden dahlia, is a tropical tuberous-rooted perennial plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its showy, large, and colorful flowers that come in a wide range of hues, including red, pink, yellow, and white. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, with compound leaves that are pinnately arranged. Its tuberous roots are used for propagation, and the plant thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil.

It is a popular ornamental plant in gardens and floral arrangements due to its striking blooms and long flowering period.
