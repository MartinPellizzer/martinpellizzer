Drymochloa

Drymochloa, commonly known as the silvergrass or bamboo grass, is a genus of grasses native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and swamps, where it forms dense, low-growing mats. The plants have narrow, grass-like leaves with a distinctive silver or grayish-blue sheen, which helps reflect sunlight and reduce water loss. Drymochloa species are often used in horticulture for their ornamental value and ability to thrive in humid conditions.

They play an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
