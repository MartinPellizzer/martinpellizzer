Dacrycarpus kinabaluensis

Dacrycarpus kinabaluensis, commonly known as the Kinabalu pine, is a species of conifer native to the mountainous regions of Borneo.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and needle-like leaves that are arranged in spiral clusters. The plant produces small, woody cones that contain seeds, typical of the coniferous family. It thrives in cool, moist climates at high altitudes, often found in cloud forests.

This species plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, contributing to forest structure and biodiversity.
