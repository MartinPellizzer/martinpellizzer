Diplachne barbata

Diplachne barbata, commonly known as the hairy grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

Diplachne barbata is adapted to dry conditions and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural areas.
