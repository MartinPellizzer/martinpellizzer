Diastella divaricata

Diastella divaricata, commonly known as the Natal fan palm, is a striking evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It features a short, unbranched stem with a crown of palm-like leaves that are fan-shaped and often adorned with silky, white hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries, which are an important food source for various wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its unique appearance and adaptability contribute to its value in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
