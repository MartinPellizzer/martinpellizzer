Draba mongolica var. chinensis

Draba mongolica var. chinensis, commonly known as the Chinese rockcress, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including China, Mongolia, and Siberia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It has basal leaves that are often lobed or divided, and its stems are usually prostrate or ascending.

Draba mongolica var. chinensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
