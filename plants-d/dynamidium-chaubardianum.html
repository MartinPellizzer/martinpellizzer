Dynamidium chaubardianum

Dynamidium chaubardianum, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its erect stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Dynamidium chaubardianum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
