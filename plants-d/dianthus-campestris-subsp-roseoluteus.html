Dianthus campestris subsp. roseoluteus

Dianthus campestris subsp.

roseoluteus, commonly known as the purple campion, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, while its stems are erect and branching.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, known for its vibrant flowers and adaptability to various soil conditions.
