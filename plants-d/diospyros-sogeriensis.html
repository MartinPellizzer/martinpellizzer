Diospyros sogeriensis

Diospyros sogeriensis, commonly known as the Sogeri persimmon, is a small to medium-sized tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes ebony and other related species. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate or elliptical in shape. Its flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and clustered in panicles, while the fruit is a smooth, fleshy berry that turns orange to red when mature.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been studied for its potential in ecological restoration projects.
