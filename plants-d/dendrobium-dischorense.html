Dendrobium dischorense

Dendrobium dischorense, commonly known as the orchid of the genus Dendrobium, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and large, showy flowers that bloom in a wide range of colors. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments and relies on mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient absorption. Its flowers are often fragrant and can last for several weeks, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Dendrobium dischorense is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
