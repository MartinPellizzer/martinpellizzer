Drosera peltata var. gunniana

Drosera peltata var. gunniana, commonly known as the Gunni's Sundew, is a carnivorous plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its rosette of hairy, glandular leaves that secrete a sticky substance to trap insects. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic environments such as bogs and heathlands. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in a dense inflorescence, often blooming in spring.

This variety is noted for its adaptability and is a popular specimen among carnivorous plant enthusiasts.
