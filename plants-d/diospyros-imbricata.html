Diospyros imbricata

Diospyros imbricata, commonly known as the African date palm, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in panicles, followed by edible, fleshy drupes that are typically yellow or red when mature.
