Dianthera clavata

Dianthera clavata, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, with a distinctive clustered growth pattern. The plant has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Dianthera clavata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
