Dypsis delicatula

Dypsis delicatula, commonly known as the slender lady palm, is a species of cycadoid plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its slender, unbranched trunk and feathery, pinnate leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by woody, spiky fruits containing seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is also cultivated as a houseplant in many parts of the world.
