Dodonaea denticulata

Dodonaea denticulata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hop bush, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and small, oval-shaped leaves with finely toothed margins. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, woody fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and dry woodlands.

Dodonaea denticulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
