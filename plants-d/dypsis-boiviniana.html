Dypsis boiviniana

Dypsis boiviniana, commonly known as the Madagascar palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to Madagascar and is characterized by its slender, tree-like structure with a single trunk and sparse, clustered foliage. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are typically followed by yellowish fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance.

Dypsis boiviniana is relatively low-maintenance and suitable for both indoor and outdoor cultivation in suitable climates.
