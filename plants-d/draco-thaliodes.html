Draco thaliodes

Draco thaliodes, commonly known as the flame orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, brightly colored flowers that resemble flames, typically in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant has a slender, branching stem and produces small, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Draco thaliodes is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is popular in horticultural collections.
