Dolia deserticola

Dolia deserticola, commonly known as the desert dolia, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its small, spiny stems and narrow, linear leaves that help reduce water loss. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers during the spring and summer months. It is adapted to thrive in harsh, dry environments with minimal rainfall.

Dolia deserticola plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
