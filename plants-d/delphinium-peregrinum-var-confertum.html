Delphinium peregrinum var. confertum

Delphinium peregrinum var. confertum, commonly known as the dense-flowered larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It features tall, spindly stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, adorned with clusters of blue to purple flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped and deeply lobed, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

The plant is noted for its showy blooms and is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens.
