Dissotis pulchra

Dissotis pulchra, commonly known as the purple beauty, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the grass family, Poaceae, and is characterized by its striking violet to purple inflorescences. The plant typically grows as a perennial herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are clustered in panicle-like arrangements and are often visited by various pollinators.

Dissotis pulchra is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and unique appearance.
