Deplanchea tetraphylla var. novoguineensis

Deplanchea tetraphylla var. novoguineensis, commonly known as the New Guinea velvet plant, is a tropical flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and striking, large, showy bracts that resemble petals. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic in shape, and often have a waxy texture.

It thrives in humid, warm environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
