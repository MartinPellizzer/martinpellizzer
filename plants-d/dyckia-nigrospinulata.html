Dyckia nigrospinulata

Dyckia nigrospinulata, commonly known as the black-spined dyckia, is a species of bromeliad native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, rosette-shaped foliage that forms a compact, cushion-like structure. The leaves are typically dark green with prominent black or dark purple spines along their edges. This plant is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

It produces striking inflorescences with vibrant red flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.
