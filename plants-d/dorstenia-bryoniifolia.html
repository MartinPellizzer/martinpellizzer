Dorstenia bryoniifolia

Dorstenia bryoniifolia, commonly known as the "bryony leaf" or "false bryony," is a unique member of the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped leaves that often have a waxy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels. Its fruits are dry, three-lobed capsules that release seeds when mature.

Dorstenia bryoniifolia is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its striking foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
