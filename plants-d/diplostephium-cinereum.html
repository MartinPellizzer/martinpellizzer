Diplostephium cinereum

Diplostephium cinereum, commonly known as the silverleaf mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on various host trees, primarily in the tropics and subtropics.

It has small, leathery leaves that are usually covered in a dense layer of white, waxy hair, giving it a silvery appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or green flowers that are often clustered together. Its fruit is a small, fleshy berry that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by birds.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can weaken its host plant over time, making it an important component of its ecosystem.
