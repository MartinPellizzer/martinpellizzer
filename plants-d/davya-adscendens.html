Davya adscendens

Davya adscendens, commonly known as the climbing davya, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its twining stems that ascend along supports, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. Its leaves are pinnate, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets.

Davya adscendens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual interest.
