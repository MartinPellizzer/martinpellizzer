Dracocephalum ovalifolium

Dracocephalum ovalifolium, commonly known as blueweed or Persian ironwort, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs such as mint and sage. The plant is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically blue or purple, forming dense clusters at the ends of the branches.

Dracocephalum ovalifolium is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic effects.
