Dryopteris lividis

Dryopteris lividis, commonly known as the silver shield fern, is a species of fern native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, glossy, dark green fronds that often have a silvery sheen on the underside. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its fronds are usually bipinnate, with a graceful, arching appearance.

Dryopteris lividis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with high humidity and partial shade.
