Dysoxylum procerum var. macranthum

Dysoxylum procerum var. macranthum, commonly known as the giant flame tree, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in red or orange hues. The plant has a straight trunk and can grow up to 30 meters tall, making it a prominent feature in its natural habitat. Its leaves are compound, with long, slender leaflets, and it produces woody pods that contain seeds.

The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also valued for its timber.
