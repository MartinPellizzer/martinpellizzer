Dichostylis micheliana

Dichostylis micheliana, commonly known as the Cape daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and has a compact, bushy habit. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, dark seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Dichostylis micheliana is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
