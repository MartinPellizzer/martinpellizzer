Deschampsia cespitosa var. mackenzieana

Deschampsia cespitosa var. mackenzieana, commonly known as Mackenzie's bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and fine, blue-green blades that are often slightly hairy. This variety thrives in moist to wet environments, such as wet meadows and marshes, and is adapted to temperate climates. Its inflorescence consists of a loose panicle with spikelets that are often tinged with purple or blue.

The plant plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates and birds.
