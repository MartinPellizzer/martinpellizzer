Douglassia laurina

Douglassia laurina, commonly known as sweetshrub, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, showy flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves that turn reddish in the fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Douglassia laurina is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
