Dombeya dichotomopsis

Dombeya dichotomopsis, commonly known as the red dombeya, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range from pink to deep red, often appearing in clusters at the ends of its branches. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving them a soft texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Dombeya dichotomopsis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
