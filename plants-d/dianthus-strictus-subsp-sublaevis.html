Dianthus strictus subsp. sublaevis

Dianthus strictus subsp.

sublaevis, commonly known as the slender campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of pink or red flowers with a distinctive white center.

The plant has linear, hairy leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate appearance and long blooming period.
