Dolomiaea frolowii

Dolomiaea frolowii, commonly known as the Frolow's daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like appearance with yellow flowers surrounded by white ray florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is known for its compact growth habit and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Dolomiaea frolowii is also valued for its resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.
