Dialium occidentale subsp. occidentale

Dialium occidentale subsp.

occidentale, commonly known as the African apple or mabira, is a small to medium-sized tree native to tropical Africa. It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy, yellowish-white flowers.

The fruit is a spherical, fleshy drupe that turns from green to yellow or red when mature. It is valued for its edible fruit, which has a sweet, tangy flavor, and is also used in traditional medicine.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.
