Danthonia hieronymi var. pyramidata

Danthonia hieronymi var. pyramidata, commonly known as the pyramidal danthonia, is a perennial grass species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often adorned with purple or pink flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its attractive appearance and hardy nature.
