Diuris flavescens

Diuris flavescens, commonly known as the yellow spider-orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southeastern Australia.

It typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is found in regions such as New South Wales and Victoria. The plant features a slender, erect stem with a single or few flowers that are usually yellow or orange in color. Its flowers have a distinctive spider-like appearance, with long, slender petals and a prominent lip.

Diuris flavescens is a perennial species that blooms during the summer months, often in response to seasonal rainfall.
