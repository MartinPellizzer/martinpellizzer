Deutzia discolor var. elegantissima

Deutzia discolor var. elegantissima, commonly known as the snowbell tree, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in early spring, often before the leaves emerge. The plant has slender, arching branches and produces small, dark blue berries in the fall. Its leaves are ovate to elliptical, with a smooth texture and a slightly serrated margin.

It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.
