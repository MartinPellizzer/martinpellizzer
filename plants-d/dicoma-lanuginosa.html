Dicoma lanuginosa

Dicoma lanuginosa, commonly known as the silky dicoma, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its soft, hairy foliage and clusters of small, white or pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Its fibrous root system helps it survive in harsh conditions, and it is often found in fynbos vegetation.

Dicoma lanuginosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
