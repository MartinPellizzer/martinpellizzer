Derris rubrocalyx subsp. acuminata

Derris rubrocalyx subsp.

acuminata, commonly known as the acuminata derris, is a tropical shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive red flowers.

The plant is notable for containing rotenone, a toxic compound used in traditional fishing practices. Its growth habit includes woody stems and palmately lobed leaves, which are typical of many species in the genus Derris.

The subspecies is distinguished by its acuminate leaf tips and specific floral morphology.
