Dombeya acutangula subsp. rosea

Dombeya acutangula subsp.

rosea, commonly known as the rose-flowered dombeya, is a flowering plant native to parts of eastern Africa. It is characterized by its showy, pink to rose-colored flowers that bloom in clusters along the stems.

The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are often visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
