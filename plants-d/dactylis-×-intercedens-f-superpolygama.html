Dactylis × intercedens f. superpolygama

Dactylis × intercedens f. superpolygama, commonly known as the giant red clover, is a perennial grassland plant characterized by its large, trifoliate leaves and showy, pinkish-red flower heads.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia and thrives in well-drained soils, often found in meadows and pastures. The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in landscaping and forage production. It exhibits a spreading growth habit and can form dense stands, making it a common weed in agricultural fields.

Its botanical features make it distinct from other clover species, with its unique floral structure and robust growth characteristics.
