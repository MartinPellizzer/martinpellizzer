Drynaria subspathulata

Drynaria subspathulata, commonly known as the narrow-sword fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Drynariaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, upright fronds that are typically divided into narrow, elongated segments. The plant is native to Southeast Asia and is often found growing on rocky surfaces or in shaded forest areas. Its rhizomes are wiry and capable of producing new growth, contributing to its ability to thrive in challenging environments.

The leaves are evergreen, and the plant is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
