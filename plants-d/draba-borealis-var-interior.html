Draba borealis var. interior

Draba borealis var. interior, commonly known as the Arctic bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of only a few centimeters. The plant produces small, white or pale purple flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, harsh environments.

Its botanical features include a basal rosette of leaves and a short flowering stem.
