Dioscorea esurientium

Dioscorea esurientium, commonly known as the African yam bean, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary part used for culinary and medicinal purposes. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing several seeds. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and have a rough texture.

The tubers are rich in starch and have been traditionally used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
