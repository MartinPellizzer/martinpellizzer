Dioscorea esculenta var. fasciculata

Dioscorea esculenta var. fasciculata, commonly known as the Chinese yam, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary edible part of the plant. The tubers are typically cylindrical, with a rough, brownish skin and a starchy, sweet interior. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

It is valued not only for its nutritional content but also for its traditional medicinal uses in various cultures.
