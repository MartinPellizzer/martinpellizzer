Deutzia bungoensis

Deutzia bungoensis, commonly known as the Japanese deutzia, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and features arching branches with small, ovate leaves. Its flowers are borne in dense panicles and are often fragrant, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Deutzia bungoensis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to a variety of soil conditions.
