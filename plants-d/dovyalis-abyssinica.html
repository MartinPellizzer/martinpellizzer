Dovyalis abyssinica

Dovyalis abyssinica, commonly known as the African wolfberry, is a shrub native to eastern and southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, yellow, edible fruits that are often consumed raw or used in traditional medicine. The plant has simple, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture and produces small, white to pinkish flowers. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

The fruit is rich in vitamins and antioxidants, making it a valuable plant for both culinary and medicinal purposes.
