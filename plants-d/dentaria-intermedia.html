Dentaria intermedia

Dentaria intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate toothplant, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, which includes mustard and cress plants. The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, with small white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional herbal medicine.

Dentaria intermedia thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.
