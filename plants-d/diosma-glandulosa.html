Diosma glandulosa

Diosma glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular diosma, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its erect, branching stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to thrive in well-drained soils, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Diosma glandulosa is also noted for its aromatic foliage, which contains essential oils and is sometimes used in traditional herbal practices.
