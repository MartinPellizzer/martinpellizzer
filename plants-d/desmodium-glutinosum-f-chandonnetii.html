Desmodium glutinosum f. chandonnetii

Desmodium glutinosum f. chandonnetii, commonly known as the sticky desmodium, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is notable for its sticky secretions on the stems and leaves, which can trap insects. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded to partially shaded environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential ecological benefits in nitrogen fixation.
