Dialium laurinum

Dialium laurinum, commonly known as the African cherry or African wild cherry, is a tropical tree native to Central and Eastern Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, red, fleshy fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in moist, lowland forests and is valued for its timber and fruit.

The leaves and fruits are also used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
