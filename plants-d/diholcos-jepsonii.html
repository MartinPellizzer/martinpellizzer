Diholcos jepsonii

Diholcos jepsonii, commonly known as the San Diego aster, is a flowering plant native to southern California.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky habitats and is adapted to dry, coastal environments. It produces small, white to pinkish flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
