Dalea alopecurus

Dalea alopecurus, commonly known as hairy milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in prairies and open woodlands. It has small, hairy stems and produces small, yellow to reddish-purple flowers that bloom in late spring.

Dalea alopecurus is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important plant in ecological restoration projects.
