Distichorchis bifaria

Distichorchis bifaria, commonly known as the two-ranked orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its two-ranked leaves and produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and along stream banks. Its flowers are pollinated by insects, and it is often found in association with other orchid species.

Distichorchis bifaria is a relatively rare and delicate plant, requiring specific environmental conditions to thrive.
