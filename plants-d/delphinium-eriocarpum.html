Delphinium eriocarpum

Delphinium eriocarpum, commonly known as the woolly delphinium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers in racemes, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with a dense layer of fine, woolly hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Delphinium eriocarpum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
