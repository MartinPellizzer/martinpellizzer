Delphinium nordhagenii var. acutidentatum

Delphinium nordhagenii var. acutidentatum, commonly known as the sharp-toothed larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant produces showy, spurred blue to purple flowers arranged in dense spikes, typical of the Delphinium genus. Its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is adapted to high-altitude environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sunlight.
