Dianthus gustavii

Dianthus gustavii, commonly known as the Gustav's campion, is a flowering plant native to the high alpine regions of South America.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in cool, moist environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces small, rounded seed capsules upon maturity.

Dianthus gustavii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable growing conditions.
