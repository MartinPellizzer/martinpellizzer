Digitaria aegyptiaca

Digitaria aegyptiaca, commonly known as Egyptian crabgrass, is an annual grass species native to Africa.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences. The plant has narrow, elongated leaves with a distinct midrib and is often found in disturbed soils. It is a common weed in agricultural fields and can be challenging to control due to its rapid growth and seed production.

Botanically, it is classified within the Poaceae family and is known for its adaptability to various environmental conditions.
