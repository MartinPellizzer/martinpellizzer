Diplopappus capusii

Diplopappus capusii, commonly known as the Cape York thistle, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its upright growth habit and spiny stems. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its rugged appearance.

Diplopappus capusii is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
