Dyckia distachya

Dyckia distachya, commonly known as the spiked dyckia, is a perennial grass-like plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces striking, branched inflorescences that bear small, tubular flowers, typically in shades of yellow or orange. It is well adapted to dry environments and can thrive in full sun with minimal water.

Dyckia distachya is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
