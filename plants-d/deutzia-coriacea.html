Deutzia coriacea

Deutzia coriacea, commonly known as roughleaf deutzia, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are usually rough to the touch, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers in late spring to early summer. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

Deutzia coriacea prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a versatile addition to many garden settings.
