Delphinium menziesii subsp. menziesii

Delphinium menziesii subsp.

menziesii, commonly known as the Menzies' larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks.

The plant features tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. Its flowers are arranged in dense spikes and are usually blue to purple in color, with showy, spurred petals.

The leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough, hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
