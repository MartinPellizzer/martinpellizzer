Dombeya decanthera var. bojeriana

Dombeya decanthera var. bojeriana, commonly known as the Bojer's dombeya, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often appearing in clusters. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
