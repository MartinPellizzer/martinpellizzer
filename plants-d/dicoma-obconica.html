Dicoma obconica

Dicoma obconica, commonly known as the narrowleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It produces a capsule fruit that contains numerous small seeds, which are dispersed by the wind.

Dicoma obconica is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and along coastal areas.
