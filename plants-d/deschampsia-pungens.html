Deschampsia pungens

Deschampsia pungens, commonly known as Arctic grass or needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to Arctic and alpine regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences containing numerous tiny flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in cold, dry environments and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.

Deschampsia pungens plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various arctic and alpine organisms.
