Dianthus urumoffii

Dianthus urumoffii, commonly known as the Urumqi carnation, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that typically range in color from pink to red. The plant features upright, branched stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are composed of five petals that are often adorned with distinctive red or purple markings.

Dianthus urumoffii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and resilience.
