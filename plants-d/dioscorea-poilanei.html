Dioscorea poilanei

Dioscorea poilanei, commonly known as the Congo yam, is a climbing vine native to Central and West Africa.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by tuberous roots that are used as food in some regions.

Its growth habit and morphological features make it distinct within the genus Dioscorea.
