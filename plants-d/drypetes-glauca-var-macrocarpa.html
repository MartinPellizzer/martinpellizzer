Drypetes glauca var. macrocarpa

Drypetes glauca var. macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited drypetes, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by distinctive, fleshy, orange-red berries. Its bark is thick and often flaky, and it is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitats.
