Dactylorchis majalis f. albolileacea

Dactylorchis majalis f. albolileacea, commonly known as the white-lipped orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive white to pale yellow petals and sepals, with a contrasting darker lip. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as pine flatwoods and cypress swamps. It features a slender, erect stem with a single, showy flower that blooms in late spring.

Botanically, it is a terrestrial orchid with a complex root system and is classified within the Orchidaceae family.
