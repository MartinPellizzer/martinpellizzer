Desmodium oxyphyllum var. mandshuricum

Desmodium oxyphyllum var. mandshuricum, commonly known as the Manchurian ticktrefoil, is a perennial legume native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that typically bloom in early summer. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along streambanks. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility.

Its growth habit and ecological importance make it a notable species in its native habitats.
