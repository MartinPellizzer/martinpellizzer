Daviesia phyllodinea

Daviesia phyllodinea, commonly known as the yellow wattle, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and distinctive phyllodes, which are flattened, leaf-like structures. The plant thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and woodlands. It plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for native pollinators.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
