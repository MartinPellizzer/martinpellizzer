Draba longiciliata

Draba longiciliata, commonly known as the long-beak draba, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a small, herbaceous perennial that typically grows in alpine and arctic regions. The plant features basal leaves that are rosette-shaped and hairy, with small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Draba longiciliata is characterized by its elongated, slender sepals and its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.

It is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats and is adapted to cold climates.
