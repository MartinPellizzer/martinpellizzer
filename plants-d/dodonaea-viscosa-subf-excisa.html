Dodonaea viscosa subf. excisa

Dodonaea viscosa subf.

excisa, commonly known as the coastal honeysuckle, is a shrub native to coastal regions of Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves that are often gray-green in color.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in spikes, which are typically visited by nectar-feeding insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal conditions with high salinity and wind exposure.

This subshrub is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
