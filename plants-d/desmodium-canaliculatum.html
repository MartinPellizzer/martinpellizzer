Desmodium canaliculatum

Desmodium canaliculatum, commonly known as the narrow-lobed burr legume, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows as an erect herb or shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its small, yellow flowers are clustered in racemes, and the seeds are enclosed in slender, curved pods.

Desmodium canaliculatum is often used in traditional medicine and as a forage plant for livestock.
