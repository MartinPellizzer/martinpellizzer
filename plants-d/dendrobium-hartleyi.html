Dendrobium hartleyi

Dendrobium hartleyi, commonly known as the Hartley's orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, cane-like pseudobulbs and clusters of fragrant, showy flowers that bloom in shades of white, pink, and yellow. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical climates, often on trees or rocks in humid environments. Its leaves are usually linear and arranged spirally around the stem, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Dendrobium hartleyi is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various growing conditions.
