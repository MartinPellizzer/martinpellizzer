Delphinium ajacis f. alba

Delphinium ajacis f. alba, commonly known as the white larkspur, is a striking perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features tall, slender stems that bear clusters of small, showy white flowers with prominent blue-black sepals. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it should be handled with care and kept away from livestock and children.
