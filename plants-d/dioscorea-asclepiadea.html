Dioscorea asclepiadea

Dioscorea asclepiadea, commonly known as the African yam bean, is a climbing vine belonging to the family Dioscoreaceae.

It is native to tropical Africa and is characterized by its compound leaves and tuberous roots. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by elongated, yellow pods containing angular seeds. Its tubers are edible and have been used traditionally for food and medicinal purposes.

The plant's adaptability to various soil types makes it a valuable crop in certain agricultural systems.
