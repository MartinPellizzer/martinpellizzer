Ditassa grandiflora

Ditassa grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered ditassa, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its showy, large, and brightly colored flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant has a woody stem and produces leaves that are often elliptical in shape. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
