Dianella inconspicua

Dianella inconspicua, commonly known as the small-leaved flax lily, is a herbaceous perennial native to New Zealand.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, with a fibrous texture. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Dianella inconspicua is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
