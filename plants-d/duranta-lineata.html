Duranta lineata

Duranta lineata, commonly known as the golden bell vine, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean and northern South America.

It is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often dark green and slightly hairy. The plant produces clusters of pendant, bell-shaped flowers that range from yellow to orange, which give way to blue or purple berries. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Due to its attractive appearance and adaptability, it is often cultivated in warm climates for both its aesthetic value and wildlife benefits.
