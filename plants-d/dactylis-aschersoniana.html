Dactylis aschersoniana

Dactylis aschersoniana, commonly known as sweet vernalgrass, is a perennial grass native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and bright green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and disturbed areas.

Dactylis aschersoniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
