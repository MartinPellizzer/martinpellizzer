Dumasia prazeri

Dumasia prazeri, commonly known as the Prazzer's dodder, is a parasitic flowering plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on host plants for nutrients.

It produces small, yellowish flowers and has a unique appearance with thread-like stems that wrap around its host. The plant is native to parts of South America and is often found growing on grasses and other low-growing vegetation. Its reproductive strategy involves producing copious amounts of lightweight seeds that can be dispersed by wind.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can cause significant damage to agricultural crops if left unchecked.
