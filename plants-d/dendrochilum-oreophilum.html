Dendrochilum oreophilum

Dendrochilum oreophilum, commonly known as the mountain orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial herb with a sympodial growth habit, featuring a rhizome and pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically during the rainy season. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to thrive in moist, shaded forest environments.

Dendrochilum oreophilum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
