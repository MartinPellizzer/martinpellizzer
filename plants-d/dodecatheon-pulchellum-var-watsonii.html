Dodecatheon pulchellum var. watsonii

Dodecatheon pulchellum var. watsonii, commonly known as the Watson's five-petaled bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. It is a valuable component of native plant communities and supports pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
