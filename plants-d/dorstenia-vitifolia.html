Dorstenia vitifolia

Dorstenia vitifolia, commonly known as the wineleaf or wineleaf tree, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Dorstenia vitifolia is valued for its ornamental leaves and has some traditional medicinal uses.
