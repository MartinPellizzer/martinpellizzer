Dinochloa major

Dinochloa major, commonly known as the giant grass or swamp grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, hairy texture. Its inflorescence consists of dense clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late summer.

Dinochloa major plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
