Diasperus gardneri

Diasperus gardneri, commonly known as the Gardner's daisy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and features showy yellow flowers with a distinctive central disc. The plant produces small, hard seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its propagation. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Diasperus gardneri is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
