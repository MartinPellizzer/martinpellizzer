Dianthus arrostoi var. graminifolius

Dianthus arrostoi var. graminifolius, commonly known as the grass-leaved campion, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bright pink or red flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens or as a border plant.

The species is relatively low maintenance and resistant to pests and diseases.
