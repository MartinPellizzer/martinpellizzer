Dictyopteris irregularis

Dictyopteris irregularis, commonly known as the irregular wrightia, is a species of brown algae belonging to the family Dictyopteridaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, irregularly shaped fronds that are often lobed and have a leathery texture. The plant typically grows in shallow, coastal waters, attaching itself to rocky substrates. Its reproductive structures, including both male and female gametes, are embedded within the thallus.

Dictyopteris irregularis plays a significant role in marine ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various marine organisms.
