Dryopteris karwinskyana

Dryopteris karwinskyana, commonly known as the Karwinsky's wood fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green fronds that are typically divided into lance-shaped segments. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments, often found in forest understories. Its rhizomes are thick and creeping, allowing it to spread and form dense colonies.

The spores are produced in clusters on the undersides of the fronds, contributing to its reproductive strategy in woodland habitats.
