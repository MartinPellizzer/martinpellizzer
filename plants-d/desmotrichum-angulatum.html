Desmotrichum angulatum

Desmotrichum angulatum, commonly known as the angular sandmat, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often in arid or semi-arid regions. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are narrow and often have a distinctive angular appearance, contributing to its common name.

The species is adapted to withstand drought conditions, making it a resilient plant in its native habitat.
