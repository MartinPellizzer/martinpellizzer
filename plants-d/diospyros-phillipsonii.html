Diospyros phillipsonii

Diospyros phillipsonii, commonly known as the Philippine persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish-brown bark and broad, ovate leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange to reddish fruits that are edible when fully ripe. Its fruit has a sweet, astringent flavor and is often consumed fresh or used in traditional remedies.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in its native habitat.
