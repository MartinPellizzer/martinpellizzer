Dendrobium macropodum

Dendrobium macropodum, commonly known as the giant wax orchid, is an epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, cane-like stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and pink, often arranged in clusters. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Dendrobium macropodum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in greenhouses and gardens.
