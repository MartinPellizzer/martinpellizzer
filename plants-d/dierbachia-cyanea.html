Dierbachia cyanea

Dierbachia cyanea, commonly known as the blue-tongued fern, is a striking tropical plant native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, dark green leaves that are often deeply lobed and have a distinctive blue-green hue on the underside. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a delicate contrast to its foliage. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Dierbachia cyanea is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and air-purifying qualities.
