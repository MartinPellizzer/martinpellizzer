Dypsis glabrescens

Dypsis glabrescens, commonly known as the Madagascar palm, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar.

It features a short, thick trunk with a crown of feathery, grass-like leaves. The plant is characterized by its ability to store water in its stem, making it drought-tolerant. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that develop into red, berry-like fruits.

Dypsis glabrescens is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
