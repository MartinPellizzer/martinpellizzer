Dombeya gilgiana var. scaberula

Dombeya gilgiana var. scaberula, commonly known as the roughleaf dombeya, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy stems and broad, ovate leaves with a matte texture. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically in shades of white, pink, or purple, which bloom in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Dombeya gilgiana var. scaberula is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
