Dianthus multisquameus

Dianthus multisquameus, commonly known as the squamose campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and features showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like carnations and pinks.

Dianthus multisquameus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
