Dryas octopetala f. vestita

Dryas octopetala f. vestita, commonly known as the white dryas or the snowbell, is a cold-hardy plant native to alpine and arctic regions.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are typically covered in a fine, white pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This variety is adapted to harsh environments, thriving in well-drained, rocky soils.
