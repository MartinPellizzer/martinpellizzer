Dictyanthes

Dictyanthes, commonly known as the starry bellflower, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

These plants are characterized by their showy, bell-shaped flowers that often display vibrant colors such as blue, purple, or white. The leaves are typically oval or lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Dictyanthes species are often found in rocky or sandy soils in arid or semi-arid regions.

They are valued for their ornamental appeal and sometimes for their medicinal properties.
