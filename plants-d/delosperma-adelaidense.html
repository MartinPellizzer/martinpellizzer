Delosperma adelaidense

Delosperma adelaidense, commonly known as the Adelaide ice plant, is a succulent shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces vibrant pink to purple flowers during the cooler months, typically in spring. It is often used in rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.

Delosperma adelaidense is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion in sandy or rocky landscapes.
