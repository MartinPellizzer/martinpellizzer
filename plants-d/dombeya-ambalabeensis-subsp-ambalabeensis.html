Dombeya ambalabeensis subsp. ambalabeensis

Dombeya ambalabeensis subsp.

ambalabeensis, commonly known as the Ambalabe dombeya, is a shrub native to Madagascar. It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant typically grows in moist, forested areas and has opposite, simple leaves with serrated margins. Its flowering period occurs during the rainy season, and it plays a role in supporting local pollinators.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
