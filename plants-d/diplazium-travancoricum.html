Diplazium travancoricum

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its delicate, frond-like leaves that are typically divided into pinnae. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories and along stream banks. Its rhizomes are creeping and help in the propagation of the species.
