Dactylicapnos macrocapnos

Dactylicapnos macrocapnos, commonly known as the large-flowered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, with a distinctive five-lobed structure. The plant has rough, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or gravelly habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
