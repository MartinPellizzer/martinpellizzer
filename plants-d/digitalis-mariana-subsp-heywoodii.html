Digitalis mariana subsp. heywoodii

Digitalis mariana subsp.

heywoodii, commonly known as the Mexican foxglove, is a perennial herb native to Mexico. It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its tubular, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant typically grows in rocky or open habitats, often in high-altitude regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stem is upright and branched.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine.
