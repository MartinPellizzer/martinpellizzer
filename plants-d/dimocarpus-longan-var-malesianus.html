Dimocarpus longan var. malesianus

Dimocarpus longan var. malesianus, commonly known as the Malaysian longan, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lanceolate leaves. The tree produces fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, sweet, and juicy fruits. The fruit has a thin, leathery rind and a single, large, white, seedless pulp that is highly valued for its flavor and nutritional content.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and commercial fruit production.
