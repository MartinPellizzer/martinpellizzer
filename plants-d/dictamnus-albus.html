Dictamnus albus

Dictamnus albus, commonly known as white dictamnus, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, round, greenish-brown berries that contain the seeds. Its leaves are aromatic and have a distinctive, spicy scent when crushed.

Dictamnus albus is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments.
