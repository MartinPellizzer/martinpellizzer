Dendrobium recurvatum

Dendrobium recurvatum, commonly known as the recurved orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is an epiphytic plant that grows on trees and rocks, thriving in humid tropical climates. The plant features long, thin stems that are often branched and covered in a dense layer of roots. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, with a distinctive recurved shape that gives the species its name.

Dendrobium recurvatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its unique floral display.
