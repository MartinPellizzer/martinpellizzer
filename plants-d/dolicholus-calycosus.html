Dolicholus calycosus

Dolicholus calycosus, commonly known as the calyx orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that often display vibrant colors. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are typically broad and elliptical, and it produces intricate floral structures that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Dolicholus calycosus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
