Deanea nudicaulis

Deanea nudicaulis, commonly known as the naked-stemmed marsh plant, is a small, herbaceous perennial native to wetland and marsh environments in southern Africa.

It features slender, often hairless stems that rise from a rhizomatous base, and its leaves are typically narrow and linear. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or greenish flowers that are clustered in spikes, which bloom during the rainy season. Its botanical structure is adapted to waterlogged soils, with a fibrous root system that helps anchor it in muddy substrates.

Deanea nudicaulis plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for microorganisms and invertebrates.
