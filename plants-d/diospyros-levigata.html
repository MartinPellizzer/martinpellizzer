Diospyros levigata

Diospyros levigata, commonly known as the smooth persimmon, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other persimmon species and ebony trees. The plant is characterized by its smooth, often reddish-brown bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy surface. Its fruit is a sweet, fleshy berry that ripens in autumn, typically turning orange to red in color.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
