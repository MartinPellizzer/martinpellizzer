Dioscorea benthamii

Dioscorea benthamii, commonly known as the bentham yam, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary part used for culinary and medicinal purposes. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers and later develops capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Dioscorea benthamii is valued for its nutritional content and traditional use in various cultures.
