Dais passerina

Dais passerina, commonly known as the silverleaf daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and showy yellow central disc surrounded by white to pale pink ray florets. The plant typically grows in shrubland and coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its spread across arid and semi-arid regions.

Dais passerina is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
