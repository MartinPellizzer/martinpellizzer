Diospyros lotus

Diospyros lotus, commonly known as the Persian date palm, is a species native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are followed by edible, sweet fruits resembling dates. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and typically have a bluish-green hue with a waxy coating.

The tree is well adapted to arid environments and is often cultivated for its fruit and ornamental value.
