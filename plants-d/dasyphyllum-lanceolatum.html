Dasyphyllum lanceolatum

Dasyphyllum lanceolatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf dasyphyllum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming during the cooler months. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to store water, allowing it to survive prolonged dry periods.

Dasyphyllum lanceolatum is often found in rocky outcrops and sandy soils, where it plays a role in stabilizing the soil and supporting local biodiversity.
