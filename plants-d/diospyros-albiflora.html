Diospyros albiflora

Diospyros albiflora, commonly known as the whiteflower persimmon, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, typically blooming in the spring. Its fruit is a small, round berry that turns from green to red when mature.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruit.
