Diospyros nigra

Diospyros nigra, commonly known as black ebony, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, dense wood, which is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a glossy appearance. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by small, round fruits that are typically dark purple when mature.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in humid, tropical climates with well-drained soil.
