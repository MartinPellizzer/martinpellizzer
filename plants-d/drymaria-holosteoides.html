Drymaria holosteoides

Drymaria holosteoides, commonly known as the silverweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It features small, silver-gray leaves that help reduce water loss through reflective surfaces. The plant produces clusters of tiny, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fibrous root system allows it to thrive in poor soil conditions and withstand drought.

Drymaria holosteoides is often used in restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion.
