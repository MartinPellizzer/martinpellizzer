Digitaria cognata subsp. pubiflora

Digitaria cognata subsp.

pubiflora, commonly known as the hairy digitaria, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves with a hairy surface.

The inflorescence consists of a panicle made up of small, spikelet-like flowers. Its flowering period typically occurs during the warm season, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This plant is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to thrive in dry, open habitats.
