Daviesia elongata

Daviesia elongata, commonly known as the long-leaved daviesia, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, leathery leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and open forests.

Daviesia elongata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
