Drypetes lasiogyna var. australasica

Drypetes lasiogyna var. australasica, commonly known as the Australian drypetes, is a species native to Southeast Asia and parts of Australia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub, typically reaching heights of up to 10 meters. The plant is characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves that are often glossy and dark green in color. Its flowers are small and clustered, with yellow petals, and it produces woody capsules as fruit.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its hardy nature and adaptability to various soil types.
