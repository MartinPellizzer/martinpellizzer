Dioctis maculatum

Dioctis maculatum, commonly known as the spotted bellflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers with distinctive spots. Dioctis maculatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
