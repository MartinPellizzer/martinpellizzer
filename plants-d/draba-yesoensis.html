Draba yesoensis

Draba yesoensis, commonly known as the Yesso's wintercress, is a flowering plant native to the Korean Peninsula and parts of northeastern China.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cress species. The plant is characterized by its low-growing habit, with small, basal leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Draba yesoensis is noted for its resilience to cold climates and is an important component of the local flora in its native range.
