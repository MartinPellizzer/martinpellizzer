Dapsilanthus disjunctus

Dapsilanthus disjunctus, commonly known as the Brazilian senna, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable in ecological restoration projects. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in open woodlands or along riverbanks.

Its seeds are encased in a hard pod and are dispersed by wind or animals.
