Diplazium permirabile

Diplazium permirabile, commonly known as the golden button fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Davalliaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often on rocky or wooded slopes. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.
