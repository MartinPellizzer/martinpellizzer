Didissandra grandis

Didissandra grandis, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in diameter, providing a floating platform for various aquatic life. The plant's showy white flowers bloom in the early morning and emit a pleasant fragrance, attracting pollinators such as beetles. Its rhizomes grow beneath the water surface, anchoring the plant and absorbing nutrients from the surrounding environment.

Didissandra grandis plays an important ecological role in its habitat by supporting biodiversity and contributing to water quality regulation.
