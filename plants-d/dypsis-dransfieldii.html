Dypsis dransfieldii

Dypsis dransfieldii, commonly known as the Dransfield's areca, is a species of palm native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces small, white flowers arranged in branched clusters, which are followed by bright orange fruits containing a single seed. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in coastal and lowland forest areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is also cultivated in tropical gardens and greenhouses.
