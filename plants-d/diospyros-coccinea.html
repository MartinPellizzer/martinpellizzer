Diospyros coccinea

Diospyros coccinea, commonly known as the red persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its attractive red-orange flowers and smooth, reddish-brown bark. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically harvested when fully ripe. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a glossy surface.

Diospyros coccinea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production in tropical and subtropical regions.
