Didonica subsessilis

Didonica subsessilis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved didonica, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to full sun exposure. It is often found in coastal areas and can tolerate saline conditions.

Didonica subsessilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
