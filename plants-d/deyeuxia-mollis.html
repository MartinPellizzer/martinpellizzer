Deyeuxia mollis

Deyeuxia mollis, commonly known as soft hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, fertile soils, often found in wet meadows, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant features slender, hair-like leaves that are soft to the touch, giving it its common name. Its inflorescence consists of a single, spike-like cluster of small, spikelet-bearing stems.

Deyeuxia mollis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
