Diplotaxis praecox var. delpontii

Diplotaxis praecox var. delpontii, commonly known as the Ethiopian mustard, is a flowering plant native to the highlands of Ethiopia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, yellow flowers and pinnately lobed leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This variety is noted for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
