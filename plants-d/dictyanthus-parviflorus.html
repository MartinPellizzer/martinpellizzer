Dictyanthus parviflorus

Dictyanthus parviflorus, commonly known as the small-flowered dictyanthus, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky soils. The plant features slender, upright stems with small, clustered leaves. Its flowers are pale yellow to white, with five petals and a distinctive, tubular corolla.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil and full sun.
