Discocactus silicicola

Discocactus silicicola, commonly known as the silicicola discocactus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in dry environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking visual element to its desert habitat. Its leaves are modified into spines, which help reduce water loss and deter herbivores.

Discocactus silicicola is often found in rocky soils and is well adapted to extreme temperature fluctuations.
