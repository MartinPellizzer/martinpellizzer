Dendrophthora purpurea

Dendrophthora purpurea, commonly known as the purple-stemmed mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of specific host trees.

It has small, leathery leaves that are typically dark green with a purplish tinge on the underside. The plant produces white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its berries are usually red or orange and contain toxic seeds that are dispersed by birds.

This species is often found in temperate forests and is an important part of the ecosystem, providing food for certain wildlife species.
