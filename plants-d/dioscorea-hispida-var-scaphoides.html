Dioscorea hispida var. scaphoides

Dioscorea hispida var. scaphoides, commonly known as the hairy yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by cylindrical, fleshy fruits. Its tuberous roots are used in traditional medicine and as a food source in some cultures.

The species is known for its ornamental value and ecological importance in its native habitats.
