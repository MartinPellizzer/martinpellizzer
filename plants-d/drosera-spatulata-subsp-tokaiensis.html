Drosera spatulata subsp. tokaiensis

Drosera spatulata subsp.

tokaiensis, commonly known as the Tokai Sundew, is a carnivorous plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its rosette of sticky, glandular leaves that trap insects.

The plant produces small, white flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or summer. It thrives in wet, boggy environments and requires consistent moisture.

Its unique botanical features make it a fascinating subject for both horticultural and scientific study.
