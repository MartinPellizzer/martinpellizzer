Deschampsia alpina subsp. andraei

Deschampsia alpina subsp.

andraei, commonly known as Alpine Hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to alpine and subalpine regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and slender, often silver-haired leaves.

The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically pale green to brown in color. It thrives in well-drained, rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

This subspecies is often found in mountain meadows and is an important component of alpine vegetation.
