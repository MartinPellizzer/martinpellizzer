Digitalis subalpina var. cedrorum

Digitalis subalpina var. cedrorum, commonly known as Alpine foxglove, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and striking bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in late summer, producing purple to pink flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and subalpine zones.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
