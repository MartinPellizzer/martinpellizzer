Dioscorea schweinfurthiana

Dioscorea schweinfurthiana, commonly known as the African yam, is a climbing herbaceous plant native to Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary edible part. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are fibrous and vine-like.

This species is valued both for its nutritional content and traditional medicinal uses in various African cultures.
