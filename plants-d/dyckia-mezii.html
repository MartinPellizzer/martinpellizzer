Dyckia mezii

Dyckia mezii, commonly known as the Mexican dyckia, is a species of bromeliad native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white powdery wax. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences bearing small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red in color. Dyckia mezii is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy leaves.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appearance and resilience.
