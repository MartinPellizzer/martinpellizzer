Descurainia myriophylla var. glandulosa

Descurainia myriophylla var. glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular leafed descurainia, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and glandular-dotted leaves, which are covered in tiny, sticky secretory structures. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is native to arid regions of North America. It has a low, spreading growth habit and produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
