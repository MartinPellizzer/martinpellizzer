Discolobium leptophyllum f. grandiflorum

Discolobium leptophyllum f. grandiflorum, commonly known as the large-flowered slender acacia, is a variant of the slender acacia species native to Western Australia.

It is a shrub characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and distinctive yellow flowers that appear in dense, globular clusters. The flowers are typically larger than those of the typical species, contributing to its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to the arid and semi-arid climates of its native region.

This plant is often found in open woodlands and is valued for its ecological and horticultural significance.
