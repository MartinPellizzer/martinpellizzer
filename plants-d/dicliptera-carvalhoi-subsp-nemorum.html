Dicliptera carvalhoi subsp. nemorum

Dicliptera carvalhoi subsp.

nemorum, commonly known as the Brazilian bamboo, is a member of the grass family Poaceae. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, clumping growth habit.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the grass family. It is native to Brazil and is often found in wetland or riparian habitats.
