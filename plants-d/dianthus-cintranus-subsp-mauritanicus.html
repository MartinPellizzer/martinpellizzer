Dianthus cintranus subsp. mauritanicus

Dianthus cintranus subsp.

mauritanicus, commonly known as the Mauritanian campion, is a flowering plant native to North Africa. It is a low-growing perennial with bright pink or red flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant features narrow, linear leaves and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It is part of the Caryophyllaceae family, known for its showy flowers and succulent stems.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to arid environments.
