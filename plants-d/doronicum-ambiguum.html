Doronicum ambiguum

Doronicum ambiguum, commonly known as the alpine leopard's ear, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its silver-gray, fuzzy leaves that resemble the fur of a leopard, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and forest edges.

Doronicum ambiguum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
