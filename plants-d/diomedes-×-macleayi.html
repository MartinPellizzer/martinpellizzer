Diomedes × macleayi

Diomedes × macleayi, commonly known as the Macleay's glorybower, is a hybrid shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its showy, pendulous flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a red or purple throat. The plant typically grows to a height of 1–3 meters and thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil. Its leaves are simple, ovate, and glossy, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant blooms.

Diomedes × macleayi is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and as a houseplant due to its attractive appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
