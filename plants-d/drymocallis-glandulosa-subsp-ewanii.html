Drymocallis glandulosa subsp. ewanii

Drymocallis glandulosa subsp.

ewanii, commonly known as the Ewan's goldenrod, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northeastern United States. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or open areas.

The plant features lance-shaped leaves with glandular dots and produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in the summer. Its botanical characteristics include a robust root system and a tendency to form dense colonies.

It is often used in ecological restoration due to its ability to thrive in disturbed soils.
