Dupuya madagascariensis subsp. tamarindoides

Dupuya madagascariensis subsp.

tamarindoides, commonly known as the Madagascan tamarind tree, is a species native to Madagascar. It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree with smooth, grey bark and lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by elongated, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local communities.

The subspecies is distinguished by its unique leaf and fruit morphology compared to other members of the genus.
