Draba lonchocarpa var. semitonsa

Draba lonchocarpa var. semitonsa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved whitlow grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a small annual herb that typically grows in dry, disturbed soils across alpine and arctic regions. The plant features basal leaves that are linear to oblanceolate in shape, with a hairy surface. Its flowers are small, yellowish, and arranged in a raceme, and it produces small, round pods that contain several seeds.

This variety is notable for its ecological adaptability and is often found in high-altitude environments.
