Dendrochilum bistortum

Dendrochilum bistortum, commonly known as the Bistort Orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces inflorescences of small, pale yellow to greenish flowers with a distinctive, spurred lip. It thrives in shaded, moist habitats such as forest floors and mountainous regions.

Dendrochilum bistortum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its unique floral structure.
