Dicksonia tenera

Dicksonia tenera, commonly known as the slender tree fern, is a species native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of large, feathery fronds that can reach lengths of up to 3 meters. The plant belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is known for its ability to thrive in humid, tropical environments. Its rhizomes are fibrous and can grow quite deep, allowing it to anchor firmly in moist, well-drained soils.

Dicksonia tenera is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in landscaping due to its elegant appearance.
