Dipterocarpus fulvus

Dipterocarpus fulvus, commonly known as the yellow dipterocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, reddish-brown heartwood and is often found in lowland dipterocarp forests. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically arranged in clusters.

The fruit is a capsule that contains several winged seeds, facilitating wind dispersal.
