Dioscorea lehmannii

Dioscorea lehmannii, commonly known as the Lehmann yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary storage organs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while the stems are hairy and vine-like.

The tubers of Dioscorea lehmannii are used in traditional medicine and as a food source in some regions.
