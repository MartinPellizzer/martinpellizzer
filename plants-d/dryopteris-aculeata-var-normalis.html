Dryopteris aculeata var. normalis

Dryopteris aculeata var. normalis, commonly known as the common holly fern, is a perennial fern native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features a rhizomatous growth habit with fronds that are bipinnate, giving them a feathery appearance. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks. Its leaves are typically dark green with a leathery texture and bear small, spiny structures called rachises.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly used in horticulture for its ability to thrive in shaded garden settings.
