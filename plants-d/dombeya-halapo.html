Dombeya halapo

Dombeya halapo, commonly known as the mountain dombeya, is a shrub native to the highlands of East Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cool, humid climates.

Dombeya halapo is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
