Dictyanthus brachistanthus

Dictyanthus brachistanthus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved dandelion, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, fleshy leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by fluffy, white seeds that are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation, it is sometimes grown in gardens as a decorative plant.
