Drymocallis geoides

Drymocallis geoides, commonly known as the smooth goldenrod, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture, while its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn.

Drymocallis geoides plays an important ecological role by providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a food source for various wildlife species.
