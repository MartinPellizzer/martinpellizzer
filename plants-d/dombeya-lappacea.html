Dombeya lappacea

Dombeya lappacea, commonly known as the mountain dombeya, is a deciduous shrub native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically in shades of white or pink, which bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cool, moist environments.

Dombeya lappacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
