Davallia jamaicensis

Davallia jamaicensis, commonly known as the Jamaican holly fern, is a species of fern native to the Caribbean.

It belongs to the family Davalliaceae and is characterized by its delicate, leathery fronds with a distinctive, serrated margin. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its rhizomes are thick and creeping, allowing it to spread and form dense mats.

The fronds are evergreen, providing a lush appearance throughout the year.
