Dioscorea glaucoidea

Dioscorea glaucoidea, commonly known as the blue-veined yam, is a tropical vine native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by tuberous roots, which are the primary edible part. Its tubers are often used in traditional cuisines and have been studied for their potential medicinal properties.

The species is notable for its striking blue veins in the leaves, which distinguish it from other yam species.
