Draba barbata var. treleasei

Draba barbata var. treleasei, commonly known as the Trelease's whitlow grass, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, linear leaves and produces small, yellowish-white flowers in early spring. The species is adapted to rocky and alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowering period is short, typically lasting only a few weeks, and it is often found in association with other alpine flora.

The plant is noted for its hardiness and ability to withstand harsh climatic conditions.
