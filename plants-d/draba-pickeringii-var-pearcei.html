Draba pickeringii var. pearcei

Draba pickeringii var. pearcei, commonly known as Pickering's whitlow grass, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. This variety typically grows in rocky soils and alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant produces basal leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, aiding in moisture retention.

Its blooming period occurs in early spring, adding a delicate floral display to its natural habitat.
