Dianthus superbus f. latifolius

Dianthus superbus f. latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf carnation, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are broad and lance-shaped, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. Native to the mountainous regions of Europe, it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

This cultivar is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.
