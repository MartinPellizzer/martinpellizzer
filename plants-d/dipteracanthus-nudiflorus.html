Dipteracanthus nudiflorus

Dipteracanthus nudiflorus, commonly known as the nakedflower dipteracanthus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, unbranched stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces strikingly showy, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
