Digitaria mezii

Digitaria mezii, commonly known as slender fingergrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetland habitats. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in wet conditions and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.

Botanically, it belongs to the Poaceae family and is closely related to other grass species found in similar environments.
