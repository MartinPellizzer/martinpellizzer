Dryopteris annamensis

Dryopteris annamensis, commonly known as the Annam fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its robust, leathery fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its fronds are bipinnate, with a distinctive pattern of sori on the underside.

Dryopteris annamensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its lush foliage.
