Didonica crassiflora

Didonica crassiflora, commonly known as the thick-flowered daisy, is a flowering plant native to the southern regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its robust, succulent-like leaves and showy, clustered yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments. Its flowering period usually occurs during the warmer months, adding vibrant color to its natural habitat.

Didonica crassiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in challenging conditions.
