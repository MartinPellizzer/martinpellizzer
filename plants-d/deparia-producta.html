Deparia producta

Deparia producta, commonly known as the common wood fern, is a species of fern native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its bipinnate fronds that are typically divided into two rows of pinnae, giving it a distinctive feathery appearance. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks, where it can anchor itself in rich, organic soil. Its reproductive structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds and produce spores for propagation.

Deparia producta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture for its graceful, enduring foliage.
