Digitalis lutea var. micrantha

Digitalis lutea var. micrantha, commonly known as the lesser foxglove, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It features tall, erect stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically in shades of yellow to orange. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variant is noted for its smaller flowers compared to the typical Digitalis lutea.
