Duosperma quadrangulare

Duosperma quadrangulare, commonly known as the four-angle ice plant, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems that are typically four-sided, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in summer, and it is adapted to arid environments. It has a shallow root system and stores water in its thick, angular stems.

This species is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
