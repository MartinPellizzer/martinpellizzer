Diospyros xolocotzii

Diospyros xolocotzii, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to southern Mexico.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes ebony and persimmon trees. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruit, which is typically a small, orange to red berry, ripens in the fall and is edible when fully mature.

The tree is often grown for its ornamental value and for its unique, sweet fruit.
