Dianthus portae

Dianthus portae, commonly known as the Porta's carnation, is a species of flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that typically range in color from pink to red. The plant has upright, branching stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. It blooms in late spring to early summer, producing vibrant petals that attract pollinators.

Dianthus portae is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
