Diospyros oocarpa

Diospyros oocarpa, commonly known as the Brazilian persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and broad, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into orange to red, fleshy fruits. The fruit is typically edible, with a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor, and is often consumed raw or used in culinary preparations.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is valued for its ornamental and edible qualities.
