Dactylorhiza viridis var. coreana

Dactylorhiza viridis var. coreana, commonly known as the Korean green orchid, is a species of orchid native to East Asia.

It is a terrestrial herb with a slender, upright stem and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in spikes, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
