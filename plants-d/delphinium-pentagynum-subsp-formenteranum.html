Delphinium pentagynum subsp. formenteranum

Delphinium pentagynum subsp.

formenteranum, commonly known as the Formentera larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
