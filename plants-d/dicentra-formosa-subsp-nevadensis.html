Dicentra formosa subsp. nevadensis

Dicentra formosa subsp.

nevadensis, commonly known as the Sierra larkspur, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its striking, spurred flowers that range in color from pink to white, typically blooming in early spring.

The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountain meadows and forested areas.

Its botanical features make it a notable species for both ecological and horticultural interest.
