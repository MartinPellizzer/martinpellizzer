Dioscorea quartiniana

Dioscorea quartiniana, commonly known as the African yam bean, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary storage organs. The plant produces small, green flowers followed by elongated, bean-like fruits containing several seeds. Its tubers are used as a food source and have been traditionally cultivated by indigenous communities.

The plant is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.
