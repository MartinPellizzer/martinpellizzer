Diplotaxis erucoides proles apula

Diplotaxis erucoides proles apula, commonly known as the Apulian wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, bright yellow flowers and hairy, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and thrives in full sun. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
