Dracaena thwaitesii

Dracaena thwaitesii, commonly known as the red dracaena, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and long, narrow leaves that are typically a deep green with reddish or purple veins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers followed by red berries, which are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. Dracaena thwaitesii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.

It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to thrive.
