Dianthus sidi-tualii

Dianthus sidi-tualii, commonly known as the Moroccan carnation, is a flowering plant native to the rocky habitats of North Africa.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny environments and has narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are often clustered in racemes and bloom during the spring and summer months.

Dianthus sidi-tualii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
