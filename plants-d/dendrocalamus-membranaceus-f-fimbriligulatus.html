Dendrocalamus membranaceus f. fimbriligulatus

Dendrocalamus membranaceus f. fimbriligulatus, commonly known as the silky bamboo, is a variant of the giant bamboo species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated culms that are covered with a thin, silky layer of hair, giving it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture along the margins. This variety is valued for its lightweight yet strong material, making it useful in construction and crafts.

Its rapid growth and adaptability to various climates contribute to its ecological and economic significance in its native habitats.
