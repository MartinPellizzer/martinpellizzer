Dupatya gracilis

Dupatya gracilis, commonly known as the slender blumea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, upright stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges. It produces small yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by fluffy, white seed heads.

Dupatya gracilis is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.
