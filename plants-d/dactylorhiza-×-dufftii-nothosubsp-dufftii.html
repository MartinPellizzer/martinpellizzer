Dactylorhiza × dufftii nothosubsp.

dufftii, commonly known as the Dufft’s marsh orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is a terrestrial herb that typically grows in wet, calcareous habitats such as marshes and bogs.

The plant features a slender, upright stem with a single or few showy, violet to purple flowers. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, and it blooms from late spring to early summer.

This orchid is notable for its ecological significance and is often found in nutrient-poor, moist environments.
