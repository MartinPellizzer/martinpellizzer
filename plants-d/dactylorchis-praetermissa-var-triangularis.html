Dactylorchis praetermissa var. triangularis

Dactylorchis praetermissa var. triangularis, commonly known as the triangular lady's-tresses, is a terrestrial orchid native to the southeastern United States.

It features a distinctive inflorescence with long, spiraled spikes of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant has a fibrous root system and typically grows in moist, shaded woodland habitats. Its leaves are lanceolate and arranged in a rosette at the base of the plant.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.
