Danthonia hieronymi

Danthonia hieronymi, commonly known as the Cape York grass, is a perennial grass native to Australia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, leafy blades. The plant typically grows in coastal and sandy soils, often in heathland or woodland environments. Its flowering spikes are small and inconspicuous, producing wind-pollinated flowers.

Danthonia hieronymi is adapted to withstand drought and salt spray, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
