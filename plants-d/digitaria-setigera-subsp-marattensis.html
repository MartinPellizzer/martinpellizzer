Digitaria setigera subsp. marattensis

Digitaria setigera subsp.

marattensis, commonly known as the marattens grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions. It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides, fields, and wetlands.

The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a loose cluster of spikelets, which are small, compact flower clusters.

The species is characterized by its ability to thrive in moist environments and its role in soil stabilization and ecological succession.
