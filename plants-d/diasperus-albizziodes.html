Diasperus albizziodes

Diasperus albizziodes, commonly known as the African sandalwood, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, hard, woody fruits that contain smooth, glossy seeds. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils.

Due to its aromatic properties, it has been traditionally used in perfumery and traditional medicine.
