Dipodium pandanum var. pilotaenia

Dipodium pandanum var. pilotaenia, commonly known as the pilotaenia pandan, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, fibrous leaves that are often used in culinary and traditional medicinal applications. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are long, narrow, and have a distinct waxy texture, which contributes to its unique appearance.

The plant thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its fragrant foliage and ornamental value.
