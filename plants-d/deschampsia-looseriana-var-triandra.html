Deschampsia looseriana var. triandra

Deschampsia looseriana var. triandra, commonly known as the triangular feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, feathery inflorescences that are often adorned with delicate, white or pale blue flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate harsh climatic conditions.
