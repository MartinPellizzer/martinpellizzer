Diplusodon velutinus

Diplusodon velutinus, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to regions of Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, velvety hairs, giving it its common name. Its small, yellow flowers are clustered in spikes and bloom during the warmer months.

The plant is known for its rapid growth and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and agricultural fields.
