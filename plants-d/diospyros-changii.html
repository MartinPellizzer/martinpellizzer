Diospyros changii

Diospyros changii, commonly known as the Malayan persimmon, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and smooth, grey-barked trunk. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange to red, fleshy fruits that are edible when ripe. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local cultures.
