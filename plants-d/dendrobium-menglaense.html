Dendrobium menglaense

Dendrobium menglaense, commonly known as the Mengla orchid, is a species of orchid native to China, particularly in Yunnan Province.

It is an epiphytic plant, meaning it grows on the surface of trees or rocks without deriving nutrients from them. The plant produces strikingly colorful flowers, often in shades of yellow, orange, and red, which bloom during the summer months. Its pseudobulbs are thick and fleshy, adapted to store water and nutrients in its natural habitat.

Dendrobium menglaense is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its cultural and medicinal significance in traditional Chinese practices.
