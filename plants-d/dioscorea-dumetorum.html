Dioscorea dumetorum

Dioscorea dumetorum, commonly known as African yam bean, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary part used for its medicinal and nutritional properties. The plant produces small, green, heart-shaped leaves and yellowish flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing kidney-shaped seeds.

Its tubers are rich in starch and have been traditionally used in various cultural and culinary practices across sub-Saharan Africa.
