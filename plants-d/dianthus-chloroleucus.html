Dianthus chloroleucus

Dianthus chloroleucus, commonly known as the white campion, is a flowering plant native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its clustered, pink or white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky areas. Its leaves are linear and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Dianthus chloroleucus is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for centuries in gardens.
