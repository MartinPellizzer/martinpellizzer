Dianthus barbatus subsp. girardinii

Dianthus barbatus subsp.

girardinii, commonly known as the Girardin's carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that typically range in color from pink to red, with a distinctive frilled edge.

The plant produces upright, branching stems covered in narrow, pointed leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
