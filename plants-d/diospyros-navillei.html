Diospyros navillei

Diospyros navillei, commonly known as the Naville's persimmon, is a species of flowering plant in the Ebenaceae family.

It is native to Madagascar and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into orange to red berries. Its fruits are edible, though they are typically consumed raw or used in traditional remedies.

Diospyros navillei is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
