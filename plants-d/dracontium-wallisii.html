Dracontium wallisii

Dracontium wallisii, commonly known as the Wallis's arum, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces a spadix surrounded by a showy, colorful spathe, typical of the Araceae family. It thrives in humid, shady environments and requires well-drained, fertile soil.

Dracontium wallisii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable conditions.
