Deutzia discolor var. floribunda

Deutzia discolor var. floribunda, commonly known as the white deutzia, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of fragrant, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has slender, arching stems and elliptical leaves that turn reddish in the fall. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its ornamental value comes from its showy flowers and attractive foliage throughout the growing season.
