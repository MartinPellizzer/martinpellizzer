Dendrobium chrysocephalum

Dendrobium chrysocephalum, commonly known as the golden-headed orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange, often with a distinctive golden head. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.

Dendrobium chrysocephalum is prized for its ornamental value in horticulture and traditional medicine.
