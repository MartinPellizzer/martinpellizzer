Daphnidium pulcherrimum

Daphnidium pulcherrimum, commonly known as the purple bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its stems are upright and branching.

Daphnidium pulcherrimum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
