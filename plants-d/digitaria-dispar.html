Digitaria dispar

Digitaria dispar, commonly known as flatsepal grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open areas such as fields and disturbed sites.

Digitaria dispar is also known for its ability to form dense mats, making it a common component of grassland ecosystems.
