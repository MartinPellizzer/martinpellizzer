Dessenia lanceolata

Dessenia lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on various host trees in the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It has small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically evergreen and arranged in pairs along the stem. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are often clustered in dense inflorescences. Its berries are fleshy and contain a single seed, which is dispersed by birds that eat the fruit.

Dessenia lanceolata has a symbiotic relationship with its host, drawing water and nutrients while also providing some ecological benefits to the ecosystem.
