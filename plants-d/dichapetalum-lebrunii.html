Dichapetalum lebrunii

Dichapetalum lebrunii, commonly known as the yellow bell, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling small bells. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are simple and alternate, with a rough texture.

Dichapetalum lebrunii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and unique appearance.
