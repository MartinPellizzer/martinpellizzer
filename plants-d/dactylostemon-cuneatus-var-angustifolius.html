Dactylostemon cuneatus var. angustifolius

Dactylostemon cuneatus var. angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leafed rock fig, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine, whitish bloom. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, round, fleshy drupe that turns red when mature.

This species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in harsh conditions.
