Diospyros toposia var. toposioides

Diospyros toposia var. toposioides, commonly known as the West African persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other persimmon and ebony species. The tree is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy surface. Its small, yellowish flowers are followed by edible fruits that are typically harvested when fully ripe.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential economic use in traditional medicine and local cuisines.
