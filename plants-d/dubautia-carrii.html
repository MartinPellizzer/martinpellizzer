Dubautia carrii

Dubautia carrii, commonly known as the Kauai ironwood, is a shrub or small tree native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and woody, fibrous stems that provide structural support. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the wet season. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in coastal and lowland areas.

Dubautia carrii plays an important ecological role in its habitat, providing shelter and food for native wildlife.
