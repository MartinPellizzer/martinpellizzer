Dracocephalum renati

Dracocephalum renati, commonly known as the Persian bugloss, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. Dracocephalum renati is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in temperate to cold climates.

It is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
