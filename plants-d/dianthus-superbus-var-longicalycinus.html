Dianthus superbus var. longicalycinus

Dianthus superbus var. longicalycinus, commonly known as the long-stemmed campion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features erect, branched stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height, adorned with clusters of pink to red flowers. The leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a bluish-green hue, and the flowers consist of five petals with a distinctive frilly edge. This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens and borders.
