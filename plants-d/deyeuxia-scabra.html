Deyeuxia scabra

Deyeuxia scabra, commonly known as the rough sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant has slender, triangular stems and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets. Its leaves are narrow, rough to the touch, and often have a bluish-green color.

Deyeuxia scabra plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
