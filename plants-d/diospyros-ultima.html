Diospyros ultima

Diospyros ultima, commonly known as the Malayan persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and lance-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by orange-red, fleshy fruits with a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor. Diospyros ultima is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.

Its growth habit and fruit characteristics make it a notable species in tropical horticulture.
