Dasylepis leonensis

Dasylepis leonensis, commonly known as the Leon's papyrus, is a species of sedge native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and grass-like foliage. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, often forming dense clumps. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are often concealed within the foliage, making it less conspicuous.

Dasylepis leonensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in water gardens or as a decorative plant.
