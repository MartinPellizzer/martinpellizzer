Deutzia setchuenensis var. longidentata

Deutzia setchuenensis var. longidentata, commonly known as the long-toothed deutzia, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features upright stems with opposite, ovate leaves that have serrated margins. The plant produces clusters of white, fragrant flowers in spring, which are borne on elongated pedicels. Its botanical characteristics include small, elliptical leaves and a compact growth habit.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
