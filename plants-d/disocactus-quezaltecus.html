Disocactus quezaltecus

Disocactus quezaltecus, commonly known as the Mexican apple cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its segmented, ribbed stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces showy, funnel-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
