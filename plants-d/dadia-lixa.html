Dadia lixa

Dadia lixa, commonly known as the golden trumpet vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has heart-shaped, glossy leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-drained soil.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
