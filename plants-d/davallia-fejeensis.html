Davallia fejeensis

Davallia fejeensis, commonly known as the Feje fern, is a species of polypod fern native to the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the family Davalliaceae and is characterized by its delicate, pinnate fronds that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as under forest canopies or on rocky outcrops. Its rhizomes are wiry and often form dense colonies, contributing to its ability to thrive in various habitats.

The feje fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
