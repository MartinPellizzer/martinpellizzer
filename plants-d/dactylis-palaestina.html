Dactylis palaestina

Dactylis palaestina, commonly known as palestine grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Dactylis palaestina is valued for its role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for livestock.
