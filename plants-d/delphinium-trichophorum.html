Delphinium trichophorum

Delphinium trichophorum, commonly known as the woolly larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has soft, hairy leaves that give it its common name, and it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. Delphinium trichophorum is part of the Ranunculaceae family, which includes other showy flowering plants.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and alpine settings.
