Dyckia brevifolia

Dyckia brevifolia, commonly known as the short-leaved dyckia, is a succulent plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its dense, rosette-forming structure composed of thick, fleshy leaves. The leaves are typically short and broad, with a waxy surface that helps retain moisture. It produces striking, inflorescences of red or orange flowers that emerge from the center of the rosette.

This plant is well adapted to dry environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental species in arid gardens.
