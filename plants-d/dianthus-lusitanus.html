Dianthus lusitanus

Dianthus lusitanus, commonly known as the Portuguese carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Dianthus lusitanus is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
