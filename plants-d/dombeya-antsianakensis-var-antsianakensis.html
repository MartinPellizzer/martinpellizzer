Dombeya antsianakensis var. antsianakensis

Dombeya antsianakensis var. antsianakensis, commonly known as the Antsianakena dombeya, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in clusters, displaying shades of white to pink. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are often hairy, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in forest understories and along stream banks, where it contributes to local biodiversity.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
