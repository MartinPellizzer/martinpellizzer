Diospyros transitoria

Diospyros transitoria, commonly known as the transient ebony, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other ebony trees. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, yellowish-white flowers. Its fruit is a small, round drupe that turns red when ripe.

The tree is valued for its hard, dark wood, which is used in traditional crafts and furniture.
