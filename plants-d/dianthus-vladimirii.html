Dianthus vladimirii

Dianthus vladimirii, commonly known as the Vladimir carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant features basal leaves that are linear and slightly hairy, while its stems are upright and branching. Dianthus vladimirii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and borders.
