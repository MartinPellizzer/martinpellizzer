Dioscorea arachidna

Dioscorea arachidna, commonly known as wild yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into cylindrical, spiny fruits containing numerous seeds. Its tubers are often used in traditional medicine and are a source of compounds used in pharmaceuticals.

However, the plant contains toxic alkaloids, requiring careful handling.
