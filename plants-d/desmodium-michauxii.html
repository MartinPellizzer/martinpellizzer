Desmodium michauxii

Desmodium michauxii, commonly known as the small cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, lens-shaped pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed areas.

Desmodium michauxii is valued for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil fertility.
