Drymaria blasiana

Drymaria blasiana, commonly known as the Blasian bluemink, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that bloom in shades of blue and purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, velvety texture.

Drymaria blasiana is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and can thrive in well-drained, fertile soils.
