Dianthus godronianus

Dianthus godronianus, commonly known as the Godron's campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and has hairy, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are arranged in dense, terminal clusters and are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Dianthus godronianus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
