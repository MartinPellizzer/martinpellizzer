Diasperus pedunculatus

Diasperus pedunculatus, commonly known as the slender-stemmed grass, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, upright stems and small, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces panicles of tiny, wind-pollinated flowers, which are typically greenish or pale yellow in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of water bodies.

Diasperus pedunculatus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
