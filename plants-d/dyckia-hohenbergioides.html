Dyckia hohenbergioides

Dyckia hohenbergioides, commonly known as the Hohenberg's Dyckia, is a species of flowering plant in the Bromeliaceae family.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its compact rosette of narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of white or grayish hairs. The plant produces striking, spikelike inflorescences with showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and relative ease of care, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in arid and semi-arid regions.
