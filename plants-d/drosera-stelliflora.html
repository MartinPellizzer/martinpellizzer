Drosera stelliflora

Drosera stelliflora, commonly known as the star-flowered sundew, is a carnivorous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette of hairy, glandular leaves that secrete a sticky substance to trap insects. The plant produces small, white flowers arranged in a star-like pattern, which is where it gets its common name. It thrives in moist, acidic environments and is often found in swampy or boggy areas.

Drosera stelliflora is a hardy species that can survive in various climates, making it a popular choice for carnivorous plant enthusiasts.
