Dichaea pendula var. swartzii

Dichaea pendula var. swartzii, commonly known as the Swartz's weeping bromeliad, is a species of flowering plant in the Bromeliaceae family.

It is native to the rainforests of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its cascading, pendulous inflorescence that produces small, bell-shaped flowers. Its leaves form a rosette that collects water, creating a microhabitat for small organisms.

This variant is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
