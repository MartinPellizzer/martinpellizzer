Dichapetalum riparium

Dichapetalum riparium, commonly known as the river mallow, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It typically grows near water bodies, showcasing its preference for moist, often swampy habitats. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to thrive in saturated soils.

Dichapetalum riparium is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some local cultures.
