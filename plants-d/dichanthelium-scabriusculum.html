Dichanthelium scabriusculum

Dichanthelium scabriusculum, commonly known as roughleaf panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and roadside ditches. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are rough to the touch, giving it its common name. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are characteristic of the grass family.

Dichanthelium scabriusculum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
