Dicranopygium pygmaeum subsp. fimbriatum

Dicranopygium pygmaeum subsp.

fimbriatum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed feather moss, is a small, cryptogamic plant belonging to the family Dicranaceae. It is characterized by its delicate, grass-like appearance with slender, branching stems and small, overlapping leaves.

The leaves are typically narrow and have a distinct, serrated or fringed margin, which gives the subspecies its name "fimbriatum.". This moss thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forests or along stream banks.

Its structure and growth habits make it an important component of the microhabitat in these ecosystems.
