Duranta vestita

Duranta vestita, commonly known as the silverleaf duranta, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and clusters of blue-violet flowers. The plant produces small, round, blue-black berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in ornamental landscaping.

Duranta vestita is relatively drought-tolerant and adaptable to various soil types.
