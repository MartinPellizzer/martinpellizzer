Drakaea glyptodon

Drakaea glyptodon, commonly known as the glyptodon dragon orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its unique, downward-facing flowers that resemble the ancient glyptodon mammal, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in sandy soils and is adapted to the dry, Mediterranean climate of its native region. Its flowers are usually deep purple or reddish in color and are pollinated by thynnid wasps.

Drakaea glyptodon is a rare and protected species, highlighting its ecological significance in the flora of Western Australia.
