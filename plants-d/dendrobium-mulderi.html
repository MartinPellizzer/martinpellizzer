Dendrobium mulderi

Dendrobium mulderi, commonly known as the Mulder's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and the production of long, slender pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers during the warmer months. Its leaves are typically broad and grass-like, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Dendrobium mulderi is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
