Discocapnos mundii

Discocapnos mundii, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It is a low-growing herb with hairy stems and alternate leaves that are typically lance-shaped. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by fluffy, white seed heads that aid in wind dispersal. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water.

Its botanical features make it an important component of desert ecosystems.
