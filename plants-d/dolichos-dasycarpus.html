Dolichos dasycarpus

Dolichos dasycarpus, commonly known as the hairy cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, trifoliate leaves, and showy, violet-colored flowers. The plant produces elongated, hairy pods that contain several seeds. It is adapted to grow in poor soils and is often used as a forage crop.

Its botanical features make it valuable for both agricultural and ecological purposes.
