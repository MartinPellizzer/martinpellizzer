Dalechampia decumbens

Dalechampia decumbens, commonly known as the creeping dalechampia, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It features a sprawling growth habit with prostrate stems that often root at the nodes. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture and are often used in traditional medicine.

Dalechampia decumbens is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
