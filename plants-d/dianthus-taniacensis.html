Dianthus taniacensis

Dianthus taniacensis, commonly known as the Taniacensis campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial with fleshy, basal leaves and produces clusters of pink or red flowers. The plant belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family, which includes other well-known species like carnations and pinks. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
