Dysophylla linearis var. yatabeana

Dysophylla linearis var. yatabeana, commonly known as the Yatabe's linear dysophylla, is a variant of the Dysophylla linearis species, a member of the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom during the spring and early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

This variant is notable for its distinct morphology and is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique ecological adaptations.
