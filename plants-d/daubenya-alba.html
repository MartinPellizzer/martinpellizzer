Daubenya alba

Daubenya alba, commonly known as the white rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features tall, slender stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, adorned with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies.

Daubenya alba is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in wetland restoration projects.
