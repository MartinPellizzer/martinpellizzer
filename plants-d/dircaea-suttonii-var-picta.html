Dircaea suttonii var. picta

Dircaea suttonii var. picta, commonly known as the variegated suttonia, is a striking ornamental plant native to South Africa.

It features attractive, lance-shaped leaves with striking variegation of white, yellow, and green, creating a visually appealing contrast. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in spring, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its unique foliage, it is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and collections.
