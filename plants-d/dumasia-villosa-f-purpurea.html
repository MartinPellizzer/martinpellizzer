Dumasia villosa f. purpurea

Dumasia villosa f. purpurea, commonly known as the purple-flowered velvetleaf, is a variant of the species Dumasia villosa, characterized by its distinctive purple foliage and flowers.

It is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions, typically found in moist, shaded environments. The plant features heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a velvety texture. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, with a range of colors from purple to pink.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to various soil conditions.
