Dolichos repens

Dolichos repens, commonly known as the creeping sickle bean, is a herbaceous annual plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its prostrate growth habit with hairy stems. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by long, slender pods containing edible seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in poor, sandy conditions.

Dolichos repens is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in agricultural settings.
