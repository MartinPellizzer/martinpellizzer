Dolichos kasaiensis

Dolichos kasaiensis, commonly known as the Kasai hyacinth bean, is a climbing legume native to Central Africa.

It produces small, pale blue or white flowers that are typically clustered in racemes. The plant has compound leaves with oval-shaped leaflets and is characterized by its slender, twining stems. It belongs to the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing ability through symbiotic bacteria in its root nodules.

The seeds of Dolichos kasaiensis are edible and have been traditionally used in local cuisines.
