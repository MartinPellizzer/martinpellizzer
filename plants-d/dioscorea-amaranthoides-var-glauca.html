Dioscorea amaranthoides var. glauca

Dioscorea amaranthoides var. glauca, commonly known as the blue glaucous yam, is a perennial vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, glaucous blue-green stems and heart-shaped leaves with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by tuberous roots that are often harvested for culinary or medicinal purposes. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ethnobotanical study.
