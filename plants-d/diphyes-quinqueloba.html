Diphyes quinqueloba

Diphyes quinqueloba, commonly known as the five-lobed water plant, is a species of aquatic plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its submerged leaves that are divided into five lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds and slow-moving streams. Its roots help stabilize the substrate, while its leaves provide habitat for small aquatic organisms.

It plays a role in water filtration and supports local biodiversity.
