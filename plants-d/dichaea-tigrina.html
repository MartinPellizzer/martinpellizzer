Dichaea tigrina

Dichaea tigrina, commonly known as the tiger orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that grows on trees, absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers with intricate patterns resembling tiger stripes, which give it its common name. Its leaves are typically green and lance-shaped, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, Dichaea tigrina is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value.
