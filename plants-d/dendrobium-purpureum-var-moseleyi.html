Dendrobium purpureum var. moseleyi

Dendrobium purpureum var. moseleyi, commonly known as the Moseley's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its long, upright stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, purple or violet flowers during the rainy season. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the surrounding soil.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
