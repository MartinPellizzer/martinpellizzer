Diastema comiferum

Diastema comiferum, commonly known as the fireweed plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to temperate regions.

It features slender, upright stems that are often hairy and can grow up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces showy, pink or purple flowers in clusters at the ends of its stems, which bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Diastema comiferum is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and along riverbanks.
