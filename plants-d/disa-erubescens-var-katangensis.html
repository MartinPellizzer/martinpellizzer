Disa erubescens var. katangensis

Disa erubescens var. katangensis, commonly known as the Katanga orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is a herbaceous plant belonging to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers. The plant typically features elongated, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of bright pink or red flowers during the wet season. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
