Diplycosia cinnamomifolia

Diplycosia cinnamomifolia, commonly known as the cinnamon leaf plant, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, leathery leaves that emit a distinct cinnamon scent when crushed. The plant belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes other aromatic plants like cinnamon and laurel. It typically grows in humid, tropical environments and is often found in lowland forests.

The leaves are used in traditional medicine and as a culinary spice, reflecting its botanical and cultural significance.
