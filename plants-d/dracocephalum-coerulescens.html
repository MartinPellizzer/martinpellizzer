Dracocephalum coerulescens

Dracocephalum coerulescens, commonly known as bluehead betony, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces spikes of blue to purple flowers, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Dracocephalum coerulescens is valued in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.
