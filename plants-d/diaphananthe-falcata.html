Diaphananthe falcata

Diaphananthe falcata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

Diaphananthe falcata plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
