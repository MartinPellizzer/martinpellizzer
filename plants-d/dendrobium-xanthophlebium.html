Dendrobium xanthophlebium

Dendrobium xanthophlebium, commonly known as the golden-veined orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is an epiphytic orchid that thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant features a sympodial growth habit with multiple stems that can reach several feet in length. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange with intricate veining, and it produces showy blooms during the summer months.

This orchid is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its vibrant, long-lasting flowers.
