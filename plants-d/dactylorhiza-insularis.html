Dactylorhiza insularis

Dactylorhiza insularis, commonly known as the island orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a perennial herb with a pseudobulb structure that stores water and nutrients. The plant produces a single upright stem bearing several lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, with a distinctive three-lobed lip.

Dactylorhiza insularis thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland or heathland habitats.
