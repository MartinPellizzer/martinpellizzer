Drimia decipiens

Drimia decipiens, commonly known as the false onion, is a species of flowering plant in the Asparagaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its tuberous roots and grass-like foliage. The plant produces small, white or pale purple flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
