Daphne japonica

Daphne japonica, commonly known as Japanese daphne, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan and parts of Korea.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are opposite and elliptical in shape. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, waxy flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. Its bark is smooth and reddish-brown, and it has a compact growth habit.

Daphne japonica is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens and as a border plant.
