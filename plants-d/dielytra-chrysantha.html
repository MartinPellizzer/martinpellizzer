Dielytra chrysantha

Dielytra chrysantha, commonly known as the golden leaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking golden-yellow leaves and delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the surrounding soil.

Dielytra chrysantha is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
