Desmoncus phengophyllus

Desmoncus phengophyllus, commonly known as the golden star fern, is a rare and delicate epiphytic plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit and its striking, golden-yellow fronds that resemble stars. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, nutrient-rich soil to flourish. Its unique appearance makes it a sought-after ornamental plant among collectors.

Due to its specific care requirements, it is often cultivated in controlled greenhouse settings.
