Deschampsia komarovii

Deschampsia komarovii, commonly known as Komarov's feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, silver-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces delicate, panicle-like inflorescences that bloom in late summer, adding ornamental value to its habitat. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments.

Due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
