Daphne pseudomezereum var. koreana

Daphne pseudomezereum var. koreana, commonly known as Korean daphne, is a deciduous shrub native to Korea.

It features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming in late winter to early spring. The plant has smooth, grayish-brown bark and elliptical, dark green leaves that turn yellow in autumn. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its early blooming habit and aromatic foliage.
