Dryopteris tamandarei

Dryopteris tamandarei, commonly known as the Tamanduara fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its large, leathery fronds that can grow up to 1.5 meters in length. The fronds are typically bipinnate, with a distinctive, intricate pattern of pinnae and pinnules. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
