Dendrocalamus wabo

Dendrocalamus wabo, commonly known as the Wabo bamboo, is a tall, slender grass-like plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Bambusoideae subfamily and is characterized by its robust, hollow stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant is a member of the genus Dendrocalamus, which includes many species of giant bamboo. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth surface and a slightly waxy texture.

Dendrocalamus wabo is often used in traditional construction and as a source of sustainable materials due to its strong and lightweight structure.
