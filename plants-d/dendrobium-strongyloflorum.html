Dendrobium strongyloflorum

Dendrobium strongyloflorum, commonly known as the purple-tinged orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features long, cane-like stems that are often branched and covered in dense clusters of small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically during the summer months. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light and requires well-draining potting media.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated for horticultural purposes.
