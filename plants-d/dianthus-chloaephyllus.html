Dianthus chloaephyllus

Dianthus chloaephyllus, commonly known as the green-leaved carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its vibrant pink to red flowers and its distinctive, dark green foliage. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and prefers well-drained soil and full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Dianthus chloaephyllus is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and ability to thrive in moderate climates.
