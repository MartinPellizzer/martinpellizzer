Dampiera asurea

Dampiera asurea, commonly known as silverleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its silvery, woolly foliage and bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal regions. It has a compact growth habit and produces small, capsule-like fruits that disperse its seeds.

Dampiera asurea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
