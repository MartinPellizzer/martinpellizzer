Draba longipes

Draba longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed whitlow grass, is a low-growing flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to mountainous regions of Europe and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments. Draba longipes has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

It is often found in meadows, scree slopes, and other open habitats at high altitudes.
