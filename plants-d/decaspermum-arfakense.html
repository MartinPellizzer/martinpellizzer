Decaspermum arfakense

Decaspermum arfakense, commonly known as the Arfak mountain daisy, is a flowering plant native to the highlands of New Guinea.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh climatic conditions. Its foliage is usually hairy, and it produces yellow or white flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
