Desmodium distortum var. pabulare

Desmodium distortum var. pabulare, commonly known as the zigzag senna, is a perennial legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive, zigzag-shaped leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has trifoliate leaves with a unique, twisted appearance, and its pods are slender and hairy. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
