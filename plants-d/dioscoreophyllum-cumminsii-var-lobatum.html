Dioscoreophyllum cumminsii var. lobatum

Dioscoreophyllum cumminsii var. lobatum, commonly known as the African arrowroot or elephant's foot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are rich in starch and used as a food source. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture.

Its flowers are small and unisexual, with male and female flowers occurring on separate plants, and it is often cultivated for its nutritional value.
