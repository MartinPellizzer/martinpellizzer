Deparia japonica var. variegata

Deparia japonica var. variegata, commonly known as the variegated Japanese shield fern, is a variant of the Japanese shield fern native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, shield-shaped fronds that are often variegated with white or yellow markings. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rock crevices. Its leaves are bi-lobed and can reach lengths of up to 30 centimeters.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appearance and ability to thrive in partial shade.
