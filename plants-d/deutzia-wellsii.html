Deutzia wellsii

Deutzia wellsii, commonly known as the Pacific deutzia, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet and features clusters of fragrant, white or pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has small, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, red berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Deutzia wellsii is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
