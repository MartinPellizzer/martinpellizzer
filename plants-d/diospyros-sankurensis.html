Diospyros sankurensis

Diospyros sankurensis, commonly known as the Khasi persimmon, is a species of flowering plant in the family Ebenaceae.

It is native to the northeastern region of India, particularly in the states of Meghalaya, Assam, and Manipur. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by edible, orange-red berries.

The fruit is often consumed fresh or used in traditional cuisines, and the plant is also valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.
