Dichanthium woodrovii

Dichanthium woodrovii, commonly known as the desert grass or woodrovii grass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing minute, spikelet-based flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its rhizomatous and stoloniferous growth allows it to spread and colonize disturbed or degraded soils effectively.

Dichanthium woodrovii is well adapted to drought conditions, making it a resilient component of desert ecosystems.
