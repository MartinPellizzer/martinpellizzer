Dalea hintonii var. subbijuga

Dalea hintonii var. subbijuga, commonly known as the Hinton's Dalea, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun, making it well adapted to arid environments. Its foliage consists of trifoliate leaves, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
