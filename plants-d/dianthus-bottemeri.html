Dianthus bottemeri

Dianthus bottemeri, commonly known as the Bottemer's carnation, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Turkey.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to white. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 cm and features hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Dianthus bottemeri is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in horticulture.
