Dioscorea decaryana

Dioscorea decaryana, commonly known as the Indian yam, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Dioscoreaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, Africa, and the Pacific Islands. The plant is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary part used for culinary and medicinal purposes. The leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while the flowers are typically yellow or white and are borne in clusters.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its nutritional value and traditional medicinal applications.
