Dorstenia brasiliensis var. tubicina

Dorstenia brasiliensis var. tubicina, commonly known as the tuberculate dwarf banana, is a unique member of the Musaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its compact, tuberculate stems and heart-shaped leaves. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically followed by edible, banana-like fruits.

Due to its ornamental value and unique morphology, it is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
