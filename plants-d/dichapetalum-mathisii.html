Dichapetalum mathisii

Dichapetalum mathisii, commonly known as the African snowball, is a small evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has simple, oval-shaped leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stems. It prefers well-drained, sandy soils and full sun to partial shade.

Dichapetalum mathisii is often used in rock gardens and as a decorative plant due to its ornamental flowers and compact growth habit.
