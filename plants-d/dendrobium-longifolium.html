Dendrobium longifolium

Dendrobium longifolium, commonly known as the long-leaf orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, slender leaves and clusters of fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, utilizing its aerial roots to absorb moisture and nutrients from the environment. It thrives in warm, humid climates with bright, indirect light.

Dendrobium longifolium is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for horticultural purposes.
