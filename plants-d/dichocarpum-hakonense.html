Dichocarpum hakonense

Dichocarpum hakonense, commonly known as the Hakon's monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often toxic flowers. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces blue to purple flowers in late summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
