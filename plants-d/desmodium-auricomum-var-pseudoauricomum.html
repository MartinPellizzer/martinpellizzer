Desmodium auricomum var. pseudoauricomum

Desmodium auricomum var. pseudoauricomum, commonly known as the goldenhead clover, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features trifoliate leaves with a distinctive golden or yellowish coloration, particularly on the upper surface. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodland or grassland habitats.

Its growth habit is perennial, with a spreading or upright form depending on environmental conditions.
