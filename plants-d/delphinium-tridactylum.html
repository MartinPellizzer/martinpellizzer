Delphinium tridactylum

Delphinium tridactylum, commonly known as the blue delphinium, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that bear clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant features palmate leaves with three to five lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in alpine or rocky habitats.

Delphinium tridactylum is valued in gardens for its striking floral display and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
