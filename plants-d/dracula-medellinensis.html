Dracula medellinensis

Dracula medellinensis, commonly known as the Medellin's vampire orchid, is a rare and striking species native to the cloud forests of Colombia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, intricate floral structure. The plant produces vivid red and purple flowers that resemble a vampire's face, giving it its ominous common name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires specific conditions to bloom.

Due to habitat destruction, it is considered endangered and is the subject of conservation efforts.
