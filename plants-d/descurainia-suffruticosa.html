Descurainia suffruticosa

Descurainia suffruticosa, commonly known as hoary flax, is a perennial flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and hairy, gray-green foliage. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Descurainia suffruticosa is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally by Indigenous peoples for various purposes.
