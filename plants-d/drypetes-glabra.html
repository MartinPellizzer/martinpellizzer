Drypetes glabra

Drypetes glabra, commonly known as the smooth drypetes, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It is often found in lowland and hill forest areas, thriving in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
