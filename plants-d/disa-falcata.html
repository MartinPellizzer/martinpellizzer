Disa falcata

Disa falcata, commonly known as the sickle orchid, is a species of flowering plant in the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is native to southern Africa, particularly South Africa, where it thrives in grasslands and rocky outcrops. The plant produces striking, sword-shaped leaves and tall spikes of fragrant, violet or purple flowers. It is a perennial herb that typically blooms during the summer months.

Disa falcata is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
