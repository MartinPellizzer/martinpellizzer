Dinochloa sessilifolia

Dinochloa sessilifolia, commonly known as the sessile-leaf dinochloa, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Poaceae, or grass family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, sessile leaves that are tightly clustered at the base. The plant produces panicles of tiny, spikelet-like flowers, which are typically green or pale yellow in color. It is native to certain regions of South America and thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological niche, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
