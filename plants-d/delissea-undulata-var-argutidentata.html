Delissea undulata var. argutidentata

Delissea undulata var. argutidentata, commonly known as the wavy delissea, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, undulating leaf margins and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a slightly serrated edge, contributing to its ornamental appeal. This variant is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and as a houseplant.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it adaptable to various growing conditions.
