Diospyros kostermansii

Diospyros kostermansii, commonly known as the Kostermans persimmon, is a species of flowering plant in the Ebenaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Africa, particularly in countries like Angola and the Democratic Republic of the Congo. The plant is characterized by its broad-leafed canopy and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into edible, fleshy fruits. The fruit has a sweet, persimmon-like flavor and is often consumed fresh or used in local cuisines.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food for various wildlife species.
