Diospyros aequoris subsp. balsensis

Diospyros aequoris subsp.

balsensis, commonly known as the Balsam Diospyros, is a species native to parts of Southeast Asia. It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows in lowland tropical forests.

The plant is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are often white or pale yellow. Its fruit is a small, round drupe that turns red when mature, and it is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

The subspecies is distinguished by its unique bark and the presence of a fragrant resin, which contributes to its ecological and cultural significance.
