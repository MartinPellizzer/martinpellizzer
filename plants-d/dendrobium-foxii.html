Dendrobium foxii

Dendrobium foxii, commonly known as the Fox's orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, cane-like pseudobulbs and clusters of long, narrow leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and orange, often with intricate patterns. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.

Dendrobium foxii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and relatively easy care requirements.
