Dioscorea japonica var. kelungensis

Dioscorea japonica var. kelungensis, commonly known as the Japanese yam, is a perennial vine native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces tuberous roots that are often used in traditional medicine and as a food source. Its flowers are typically unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant.

The species is notable for its medicinal properties and adaptability to various soil conditions.
