Didymocarpus polyanthoides

Didymocarpus polyanthoides, commonly known as the fragrant wax apple, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows up to 5 meters in height. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, followed by orange or red berries. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a rough, leathery texture.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
