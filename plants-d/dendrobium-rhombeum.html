Dendrobium rhombeum

Dendrobium rhombeum, commonly known as the rhombic dendrobium, is an epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with cane-like stems that can reach several feet in length. The plant produces linear, grass-like leaves and is known for its showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange with a distinctive red or purple lip.

Dendrobium rhombeum prefers bright, indirect light and moderate humidity, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.
