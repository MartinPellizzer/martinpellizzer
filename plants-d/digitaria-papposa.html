Digitaria papposa

Digitaria papposa, commonly known as hairy fingergrass, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its fine, tufted inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed areas and is often found in pastures and along roadsides. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, and the seed heads are distinctive with soft, barbed bristles.

Digitaria papposa is adapted to a wide range of soil types and is relatively drought-tolerant.
