Dimacria virginea

Dimacria virginea, commonly known as the silverleaf daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and bright yellow daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping and low-maintenance gardens.

The flowers bloom from spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
