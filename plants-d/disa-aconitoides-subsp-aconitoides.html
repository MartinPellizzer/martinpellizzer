Disa aconitoides subsp. aconitoides

Disa aconitoides subsp.

aconitoides, commonly known as the Blue Disa, is a member of the Orchidaceae family native to southern Africa. It is a small, herbaceous plant that typically grows in rocky or sandy habitats.

The plant produces striking blue to violet flowers with a distinctive spurred lip, which is a characteristic feature of many orchids. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

This subspecies is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
