Dichanthelium leucoblepharis var. glabrescens

Dichanthelium leucoblepharis var. glabrescens, commonly known as the glabrous silvergrass, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray blades and a tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes and swamp edges. It has a compact inflorescence with spikelets that are loosely arranged.

The variety glabrescens is distinguished by its reduced pubescence, giving it a smoother appearance compared to other varieties.
