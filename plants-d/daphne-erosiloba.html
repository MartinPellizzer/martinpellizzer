Daphne erosiloba

Daphne erosiloba, commonly known as Pacific dogwood, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States and Canada.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that appear in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are dark green and glossy on top, with a lighter underside. Its bark is smooth and often peels in thin, papery layers.

Daphne erosiloba thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded to partially shaded environments.
