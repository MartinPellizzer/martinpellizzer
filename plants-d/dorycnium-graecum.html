Dorycnium graecum

Dorycnium graecum, commonly known as the Greek sainfoin, is a perennial leguminous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Dorycnium graecum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
