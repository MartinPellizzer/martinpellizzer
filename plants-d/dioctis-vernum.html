Dioctis vernum

Dioctis vernum, commonly known as spring snowflake, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has trifoliate leaves with three heart-shaped leaflets, and its stems are often hairy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Dioctis vernum is valued for its early blooming habit and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental appeal.
