Diplotaxis nana

Diplotaxis nana, commonly known as dwarf African daisy, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features compact rosettes of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellow flowers in spring. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping. Its botanical characteristics include hairy stems and a basal growth habit.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
