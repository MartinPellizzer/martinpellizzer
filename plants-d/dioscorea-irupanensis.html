Dioscorea irupanensis

Dioscorea irupanensis, commonly known as the Philippine yam, is a species of flowering plant in the family Dioscoreaceae.

It is native to the Philippines and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary part used for culinary and medicinal purposes. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in shaded, moist environments. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and have a rough texture, while its flowers are small and clustered in spikes.

The tubers of Dioscorea irupanensis are rich in starch and have been traditionally used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
