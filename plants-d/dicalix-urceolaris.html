Dicalix urceolaris

Dicalix urceolaris, commonly known as the cup-shaped orchid, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, urn-shaped flower that resembles a cup. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually yellow or greenish-yellow, with a distinctive three-lobed lip.

Dicalix urceolaris is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
