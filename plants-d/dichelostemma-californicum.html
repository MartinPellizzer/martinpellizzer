Dichelostemma californicum

Dichelostemma californicum, commonly known as the California starflower, is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family and is native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb with a short rhizome and produces clusters of star-shaped, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or grassland habitats. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it blooms from late spring to early summer.

The California starflower is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
