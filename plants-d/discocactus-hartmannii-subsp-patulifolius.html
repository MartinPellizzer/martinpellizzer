Discocactus hartmannii subsp. patulifolius

Discocactus hartmannii subsp.

patulifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf discocactus, is a species native to the arid regions of southern Africa. It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in dry conditions.

The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers during the cooler months, adding a striking visual element to its appearance. Its leaves are broad and fleshy, contributing to its common name and aiding in photosynthesis.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in low-water environments.
