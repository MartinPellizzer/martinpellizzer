Derris trifoliata var. macrocarpa

Derris trifoliata var. macrocarpa, commonly known as the giant flame tree, is a tropical plant characterized by its large, trifoliate leaves and showy, orange-red flowers.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is native to Southeast Asia. The plant is notable for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.

Its seeds are enclosed in long, slender pods and are used in traditional medicine and as a source of toxic compounds.
