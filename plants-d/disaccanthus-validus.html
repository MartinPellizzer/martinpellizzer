Disaccanthus validus

Disaccanthus validus, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Disaccanthus validus is noted for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
