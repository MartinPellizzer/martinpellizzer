Discocnide mexicana

Discocnide mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican stinging nettle, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family, which includes other stinging plants like Urtica dioica. The plant is characterized by its opposite, heart-shaped leaves covered in fine hairs that can cause a skin irritation when touched. It produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Discocnide mexicana is often found in forest understories and along stream banks.
