Dondia moquinii

Dondia moquinii, commonly known as the Mexican morning glory, is a climbing vine native to Central America and northern South America.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of purple and blue. Dondia moquinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
