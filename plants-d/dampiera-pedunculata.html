Dampiera pedunculata

Dampiera pedunculata, commonly known as the clustered dampiera, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a perennial herb with a low-growing habit, characterized by its basal rosette of fleshy, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands.

Dampiera pedunculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowers.
