Drosanthemum roridum

Drosanthemum roridum, commonly known as the rainbow plant, is a member of the Aizoaceae family and is native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, succulent leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Drosanthemum roridum is also known for its ability to self-seed, contributing to its natural spread in its native habitat.
