Diasperus oreophilus

Diasperus oreophilus, commonly known as the mountain pincushion, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, spiny inflorescence that resembles a pincushion. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, temperate climates. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, and they bloom in the spring.

Diasperus oreophilus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
