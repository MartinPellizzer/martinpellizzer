Dimocarpus fumatus subsp. fumatus

Dimocarpus fumatus subsp.

fumatus, commonly known as the black mangosteen, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its dark purple to black, fleshy fruits that are covered in a rough, spiny rind.

The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are simple, ovate, and have a glossy, dark green appearance.

The flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in panicles, contributing to its ornamental value.
