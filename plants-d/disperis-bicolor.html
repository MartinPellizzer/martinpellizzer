Disperis bicolor

Disperis bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored orchid, is a species native to southern Africa.

It is a small herbaceous plant with a unique floral structure that features two distinct color zones. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers are characterized by a pale green or white base transitioning to a yellow or orange hue at the tips.

Disperis bicolor is often found in coastal regions and is adapted to withstand coastal winds and salt spray.
