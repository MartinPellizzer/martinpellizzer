Dioscorea keduensis

Dioscorea keduensis, commonly known as Kedu's yam, is a climbing herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary part used for various purposes. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while the stems are hairy and twining.

The tubers of Dioscorea keduensis are valued for their nutritional content and traditional medicinal uses.
