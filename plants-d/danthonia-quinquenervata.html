Danthonia quinquenervata

Danthonia quinquenervata, commonly known as the five-veined grass, is a perennial grass species native to various regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with distinct five veins running along their length. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Danthonia quinquenervata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in ecological restoration projects.
