Dircaea bulbosa subvar. alba

Dircaea bulbosa subvar. alba, commonly known as the white-flowered dircaea, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves and produces showy, bell-shaped white flowers during the cooler months. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments. It is noted for its resilience to drought and its ability to store water in its bulbous underground stem.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and by succulent enthusiasts.
