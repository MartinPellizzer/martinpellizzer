Dissotis debilis

Dissotis debilis, commonly known as the slender dissotis, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, delicate leaves. The plant produces tiny, clustered inflorescences that bear small, white or pale green flowers. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along streams.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can become invasive in certain areas.
