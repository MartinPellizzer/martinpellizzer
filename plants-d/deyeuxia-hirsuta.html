Deyeuxia hirsuta

Deyeuxia hirsuta, commonly known as hairy false rice, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, hairy stems that often grow close to the ground. The leaves are narrow and covered with fine hairs, giving the plant a distinctive textured appearance. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, typical of the Poaceae family.

This plant is often found in moist or wet habitats, such as marshes and along riverbanks.
