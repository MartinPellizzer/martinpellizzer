Desmodium canescens var. villosissimum

Desmodium canescens var. villosissimum, commonly known as the hairy desmodium, is a perennial legume native to parts of Africa and the southern United States.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, elliptical pods that contain several seeds, and it thrives in well-drained soils. It is often used in ecological restoration due to its ability to fix nitrogen and prevent soil erosion.

Its hairy foliage and adaptability make it a notable species in its native habitats.
