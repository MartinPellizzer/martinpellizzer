Dendrobium lindleyi var. majus

Dendrobium lindleyi var. majus, commonly known as the giant orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is an epiphytic plant, meaning it grows on the surface of trees or other structures without being parasitic. The plant produces long, cane-like stems that can reach several meters in length. Its flowers are typically large, showy, and come in a variety of colors, including white, yellow, and pink.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its striking floral display.
