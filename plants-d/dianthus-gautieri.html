Dianthus gautieri

Dianthus gautieri, commonly known as the Kashmir carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, and its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves. Dianthus gautieri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.
