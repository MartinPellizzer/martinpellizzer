Damnacanthus major var. lancifolius

Damnacanthus major var. lancifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaf cowplant, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features upright, branched stems that are often hairy, with lance-shaped leaves that are usually less than 10 cm in length. The plant produces striking pink to purple flowers in raceme inflorescences, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Botanically, it is notable for its medicinal properties and historical use in traditional herbal remedies.
