Digitaria ulugurensis

Digitaria ulugurensis, commonly known as the Uluguru grass, is a perennial grass species native to East Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and forest margins.

Digitaria ulugurensis plays a role in local ecosystems and has potential uses in forage and conservation efforts.
