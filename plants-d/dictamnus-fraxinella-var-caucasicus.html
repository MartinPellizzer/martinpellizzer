Dictamnus fraxinella var. caucasicus

Dictamnus fraxinella var. caucasicus, commonly known as Caucasian birch-leaf dictamnus, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It features aromatic, pale yellow to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its stems are often covered in a distinctive, fragrant resin that exudes when broken.

This variant is closely related to other species in the Rutaceae family, sharing similar morphological and chemical characteristics.
