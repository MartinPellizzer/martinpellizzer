Diospyros argentea

Diospyros argentea, commonly known as silver ebony, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, silver-gray bark and elliptical leaves that are glossy on top and pale underneath. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, orange-red fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. Its wood is dense and valued for its durability and fine grain.

Diospyros argentea thrives in tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.
