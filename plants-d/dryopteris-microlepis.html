Dryopteris microlepis

Dryopteris microlepis, commonly known as the Japanese Shield Fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and its distinctive, shield-shaped fronds that are deeply divided. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its fronds are dark green and often have a leathery texture, with spores produced on the underside of the leaves.

Dryopteris microlepis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appearance and adaptability to various garden conditions.
