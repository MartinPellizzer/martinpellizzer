Didymocarpus calcareus

Didymocarpus calcareus, commonly known as the limestone gooseberry, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically red when mature. It thrives in calcareous soils, which is reflected in its scientific name.

The species is often found in tropical and subtropical regions with high humidity.
