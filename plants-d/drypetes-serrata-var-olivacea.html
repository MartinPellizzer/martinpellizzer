Drypetes serrata var. olivacea

Drypetes serrata var. olivacea, commonly known as the oliveleaf tree, is a tropical evergreen species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by small, round fruits. It thrives in humid climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The varietal form olivacea is distinguished by its slightly different leaf morphology compared to the typical species.
