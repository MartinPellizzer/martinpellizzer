Deutzia × magnifica f. formosa

Deutzia × magnifica f. formosa, commonly known as the Rose-of-Sharon or Chinese honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has arching stems with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are dark green and glossy. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its ornamental value comes from both its attractive flowers and its ability to provide seasonal interest in the garden.
