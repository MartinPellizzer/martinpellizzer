Dianthus requienii

Dianthus requienii, commonly known as the Sierra Madre carnation, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils and prefers full sun. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces small, clustered flowers that attract pollinators.

Dianthus requienii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
