Dracaena roxburghiana

Dracaena roxburghiana, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red flowers that resemble flames, which bloom in clusters at the ends of its branches. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are typically green with a reddish tinge, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as a decorative tree in gardens and parks.

Dracaena roxburghiana is also valued for its ability to tolerate partial shade and its relatively low maintenance requirements.
