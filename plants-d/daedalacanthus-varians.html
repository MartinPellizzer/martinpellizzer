Daedalacanthus varians

Daedalacanthus varians, commonly known as the variable ghost orchid, is a rare and elusive orchid species native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, delicate flowers that resemble ghostly apparitions, often with intricate patterns and pale, translucent petals. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surface of trees without deriving nutrients from them, relying instead on air, rain, and debris for sustenance. Its flowers are typically small and fragrant, attracting specific pollinators such as certain species of flies and beetles.

Due to habitat loss and its secretive nature, Daedalacanthus varians is considered a critically endangered species.
