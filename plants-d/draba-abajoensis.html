Draba abajoensis

Draba abajoensis, commonly known as the Abajo Basin whitlow grass, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the western United States.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants, and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and basal rosette of leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Draba abajoensis is an important component of the local flora, contributing to the ecological diversity of its habitat.
