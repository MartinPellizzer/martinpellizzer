Dianthus crenatus

Dianthus crenatus, commonly known as the toothed campion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, pink or white flowers that bloom in clusters atop slender stems. The plant has serrated, grass-like leaves and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It is a member of the carnation family (Caryophyllaceae) and is known for its hardy nature.

Dianthus crenatus is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in various conditions.
