Delphinium commutatum

Delphinium commutatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, spindly stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers in racemes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinctive, finely toothed margin.

Delphinium commutatum is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.
