Drepananthus petiolatus

Drepananthus petiolatus, commonly known as the slender sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and small, fleshy leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements, it is occasionally cultivated in dry gardens.
