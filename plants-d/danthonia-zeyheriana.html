Danthonia zeyheriana

Danthonia zeyheriana, commonly known as Zeyher's grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
