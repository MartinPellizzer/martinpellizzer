Dioscorea sititoana

Dioscorea sititoana, commonly known as the African yambean, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the genus Dioscorea, which includes various species of yams and related tuberous plants. The plant produces large, heart-shaped leaves and bears yellowish flowers that are followed by cylindrical, fleshy fruits. Its tuberous roots are the primary economically important part, used in traditional medicine and as a food source.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.
