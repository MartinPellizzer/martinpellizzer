Deschampsia subtriflora

Deschampsia subtriflora, commonly known as slender hairgrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and stream banks. The plant has fine, hair-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils and its tolerance to periodic flooding.

Its ecological importance includes stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
