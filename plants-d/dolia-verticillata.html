Dolia verticillata

Dolia verticillata, commonly known as the clustered dolia, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its root system is fibrous and can thrive in moist, well-drained soils.

Dolia verticillata is primarily valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
