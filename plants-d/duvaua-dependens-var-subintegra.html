Duvaua dependens var. subintegra

Duvaua dependens var. subintegra, commonly known as the creeping duvaua, is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its trailing growth habit and slender stems.

It features small, opposite leaves that are typically ovate to lanceolate in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The flowers are usually small and clustered in dense inflorescences, often displaying a range of colors from white to pink. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shaded areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to form dense ground cover.
