Dipcadi unicolor

Dipcadi unicolor, commonly known as the white bulbous orchid, is a rare and striking plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its unique, single-flowered spike that emerges from a bulbous base, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its elegant, white petals that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires a cool, shaded environment to flourish.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
