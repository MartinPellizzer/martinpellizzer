Didymocarpus salicinoides

Didymocarpus salicinoides, commonly known as the Chinese mahogany or red sandalwood, is a member of the Rutaceae family.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to southern China, Vietnam, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its smooth, greyish-brown bark and simple, alternate leaves with a leathery texture. Its flowers are small and yellowish-green, arranged in clusters, and it produces woody, capsule-like fruits.

The tree is valued for its hard, heavy wood, which has been traditionally used in furniture and carving.
