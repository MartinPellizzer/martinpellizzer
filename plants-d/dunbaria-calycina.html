Dunbaria calycina

Dunbaria calycina, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvetleaf daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces small, pale yellow to orange flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the spring and summer seasons. Its leaves are usually hairy and have a distinctive velvety texture, contributing to its common name.

Dunbaria calycina is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature.
