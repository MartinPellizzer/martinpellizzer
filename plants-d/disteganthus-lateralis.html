Disteganthus lateralis

Disteganthus lateralis, commonly known as the narrowleaf disteganthus, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Acanthaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, arranged in whorls along the stems. Native to tropical regions of Africa, it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
