Dendrocalamus papuanus

Dendrocalamus papuanus, commonly known as the Papuan bamboo, is a tall, slender grass-like plant native to the mountainous regions of New Guinea.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its hollow, jointed stems that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers and relies on wind pollination for reproduction. Its leaves are long and narrow, often used in traditional crafts by local communities.

Dendrocalamus papuanus plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
