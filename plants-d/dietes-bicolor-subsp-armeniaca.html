Dietes bicolor subsp. armeniaca

Dietes bicolor subsp.

armeniaca, commonly known as the Armenian white lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Armenia and Turkey. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces striking white or pale pink flowers with a distinctive, recurved shape.

The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. It is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.

The subspecies is closely related to other members of the Dietes genus, which are characterized by their unique floral structures and adaptability to diverse environmental conditions.
