Dentaria anomala

Dentaria anomala, commonly known as the narrowleaf toothwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, commonly known as the mustard family. The plant is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Dentaria anomala is noted for its unique root system, which resembles small teeth, hence its common name.
