Desmia conferta

Desmia conferta, commonly known as the common leafroller, is a species of moth found in various parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Noctuidae and is characterized by its distinctive wing patterns and coloration. The larvae feed on a variety of plants, including grasses and shrubs, often rolling leaves to form shelters. The plant's botanical significance lies in its role as a host for this insect, influencing local ecosystems.

Its life cycle and interactions with plant species contribute to the biodiversity of its habitat.
