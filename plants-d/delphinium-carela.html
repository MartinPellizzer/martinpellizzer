Delphinium carela

Delphinium carela, commonly known as the California larkspur, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces clusters of blue to purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Delphinium carela prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable addition to gardens in suitable climates.
