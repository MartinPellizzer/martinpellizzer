Digitalis minor var. minor

Digitalis minor var. minor, commonly known as the lesser foxglove, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a member of the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and spikes of tubular, bell-shaped flowers. The flowers typically range in color from purple to pink and are arranged in whorls along the stem. The plant produces oval, ribbed seed capsules that release tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
