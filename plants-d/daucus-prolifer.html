Daucus prolifer

Daucus prolifer, commonly known as the clustered carrot, is a flowering plant in the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves and small, yellowish flowers that appear in umbrella-like clusters. The plant is native to parts of Europe and Asia and thrives in temperate climates. Its root system is less developed compared to its cultivated relative, the common carrot.

Daucus prolifer is often found in disturbed soils and along the edges of forests.
