Dianthus fischeri

Dianthus fischeri, commonly known as the Fischer's carnation, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that typically range in color from pink to red. The plant produces small, rounded leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hair, aiding in water retention in its arid habitat. Its flowers are composed of numerous petals arranged in a star-like pattern, typical of the Caryophyllaceae family.

Dianthus fischeri thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.
