Digitaria siderograpta

Digitaria siderograpta, commonly known as ironweed grass, is a perennial grass species native to various regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry culms that often exhibit a reddish or purplish tint. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, with a rough texture and a bluish-green color.

Digitaria siderograpta is often found in disturbed soils and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
