Danthonia villosa

Danthonia villosa, commonly known as silky grass or velvet grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Australia and New Zealand.

It is characterized by its fine, soft, and silky-textured leaves that are covered in short, fine hairs. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and heathlands. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are arranged in spikelets, typical of the grass family.

Danthonia villosa is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
