Dianthus catalaunicus var. theodori

Dianthus catalaunicus var. theodori, commonly known as the Theodore's campion, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its low-growing habit and clusters of pink to red, bilaterally symmetrical flowers. The plant features linear, hairy leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
