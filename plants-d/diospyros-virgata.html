Diospyros virgata

Diospyros virgata, commonly known as the African persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange to red berries that are edible when ripe.

Diospyros virgata is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine and as a food source.
