Dipteryx panamensis

Dipteryx panamensis, commonly known as the Panama rosewood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its dense, dark wood that is highly valued for its strength and beauty. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by elongated pods containing flat, brown seeds. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland tropical rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and timber, it is also cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
