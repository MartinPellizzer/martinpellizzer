Dolichos formosoides

Dolichos formosoides, commonly known as the Taiwan bean or wild cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing habit and trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are followed by elongated, cylindrical pods containing several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological studies and potential agricultural use.
