Delaria pyrifolia

Delaria pyrifolia, commonly known as the Japanese persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves and striking yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically harvested in the fall. These fruits are edible when fully ripe and have a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor.

Delaria pyrifolia is valued for its ornamental beauty and culinary uses in traditional Asian cuisines.
