Deyeuxia americana

Deyeuxia americana, commonly known as the American false panicgrass, is a grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or moist soils, often found in prairies, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Deyeuxia americana plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
