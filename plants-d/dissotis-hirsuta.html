Dissotis hirsuta

Dissotis hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy dissotis, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and hairy foliage. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or near water bodies.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
