Dianthus petraeus subsp. noeanus

Dianthus petraeus subsp.

noeanus, commonly known as the rock campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a low-growing perennial with dense, compact foliage and bright pink or red flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine and coastal environments. Its flowers are typically arranged in clusters and are characterized by their showy, spurred petals.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
