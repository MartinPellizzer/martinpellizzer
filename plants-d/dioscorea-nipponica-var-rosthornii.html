Dioscorea nipponica var. rosthornii

Dioscorea nipponica var. rosthornii, commonly known as the Japanese yam, is a tuberous plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its stout, cylindrical rhizomes that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and has compound leaves with palmately arranged leaflets. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant, and it produces small, dry fruits containing several seeds.

The tubers of this species are valued for their nutritional content and have been used in various culinary and medicinal applications.
