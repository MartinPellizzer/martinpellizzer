Dendrobium fissum

Dendrobium fissum, commonly known as the crested Dendrobium, is an epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with numerous stems that can become branched over time. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and pink, often with a distinctive crested or wavy pattern on the petals. Its leaves are typically elliptical and arranged in a spiral around the stem.

Dendrobium fissum thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light and well-draining soil.
