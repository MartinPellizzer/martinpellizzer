Dendrophorbium subnemorale

Dendrophorbium subnemorale, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family and is native to parts of Africa.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall, featuring a straight trunk and a broad, rounded crown. The leaves are alternate, simple, and have a silvery underside, giving the plant its common name. It produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, followed by capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This plant is often found in savanna and woodland habitats, where it plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various wildlife species.
