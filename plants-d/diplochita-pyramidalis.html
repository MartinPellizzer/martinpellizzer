Diplochita pyramidalis

Diplochita pyramidalis, commonly known as the pyramidal tamarind, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds surrounded by a fibrous pulp. It is often cultivated for its edible seeds, which are used in traditional cuisines.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
