Digitaria hirsuta

Digitaria hirsuta, commonly known as hairy Digitaria, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open fields, roadsides, and agricultural areas.

Digitaria hirsuta is known for its adaptability to various environmental conditions, making it a common weed in many regions.
