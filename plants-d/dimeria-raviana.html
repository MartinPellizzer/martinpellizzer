Dimeria raviana

Dimeria raviana, commonly known as the rock dandelion, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of gray-green, hairy leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water.

Dimeria raviana is often found growing on rocky outcrops and in sandy soils.
