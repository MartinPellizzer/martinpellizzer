Dianthus polylepis subsp. polylepis

Dianthus polylepis subsp.

polylepis, commonly known as the Alpine campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and vibrant pink or red flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant produces small, oval leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, aiding in water retention in its arid habitat. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes. Dianthus polylepis subsp.

polylepis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and attractive blooms.
