Daucus carota subsp. hispanicus

Daucus carota subsp.

hispanicus, commonly known as the Spanish carrot, is a subspecies of the wild carrot native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves and umbel-shaped flowers that range from white to pale pink.

The plant produces small, oval-shaped fruits that are typically reddish or purplish in color. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides and in open fields.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine, it is sometimes cultivated for both aesthetic and practical purposes.
