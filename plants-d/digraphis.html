Digraphis

Digraphis, commonly known as the ghost fern, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Araliaceae.

It consists of two species, Digraphis simplex and Digraphis glabrata, both native to Southeast Asia. The plants are characterized by their unique, delicate leaves that often appear ghost-like in appearance. They are typically found in shaded, humid environments such as forests and mountainous regions.

The genus is noted for its ornamental value due to its striking foliage.
