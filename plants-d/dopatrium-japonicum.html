Dopatrium japonicum

Dopatrium japonicum, commonly known as Japanese false heather, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches heights of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or white in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a slight pubescence.

Dopatrium japonicum is often found in moist, shaded areas such as forest floors or rocky habitats.
