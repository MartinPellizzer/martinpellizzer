Derris harrowiana

Derris harrowiana, commonly known as the African snakeroot, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is notable for its medicinal properties, particularly the presence of rotenone, a natural insecticide. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and woodland areas.

Its ecological importance includes supporting local wildlife and serving as a traditional remedy in various cultures.
