Drymonia parvifolia

Drymonia parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved drymonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen shrub form. The plant features small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and dark green, with a dense, compact growth habit. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, appearing in clusters and blooming in the spring.

Drymonia parvifolia thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.
