Dianthus myrtinervius subsp. myrtinervius

Dianthus myrtinervius subsp.

myrtinervius, commonly known as the myrtle-veined campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its delicate, pink to red flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early summer.

The plant has basal and upright stems, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are composed of five petals, with a distinctive appearance that resembles small, clustered blooms.

The subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and compact growth habit.
