Dactylorhiza pontica

Dactylorhiza pontica, commonly known as the Pontic orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southeastern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a herbaceous plant with a rhizomatous root system and produces a single, erect stem bearing a few showy, greenish-yellow to brownish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodlands and along stream banks. Its flowers are characterized by three sepals and three petals, with the lateral petals being broader than the dorsal one.

Dactylorhiza pontica is part of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its ornamental value and ecological significance in its native habitats.
