Dalechampia scandens var. kurzii

Dalechampia scandens var. kurzii, commonly known as the curly dock, is a variant of the curly dock plant native to North America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, disturbed soils along roadsides and riverbanks. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a waxy surface and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters. Its botanical significance lies in its unique leaf morphology and its role in ecological systems.

It is also valued for its historical use in traditional herbal remedies.
