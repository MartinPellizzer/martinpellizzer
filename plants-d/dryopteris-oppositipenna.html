Dryopteris oppositipenna

Dryopteris oppositipenna, commonly known as the giant shield fern, is a large, robust fern native to the forests of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, upright fronds that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The fronds are bipinnate, with a distinctive shield-shaped outline, and are covered in small, rounded pinnae. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
