Disa miniata

Disa miniata, commonly known as the dwarf disa, is a species of orchid native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, compact growth habit and vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing clusters of delicate, spurred flowers. It prefers well-drained, sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Disa miniata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable growing conditions.
