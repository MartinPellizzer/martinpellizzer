Dipsacus × pseudosylvestris

Dipsacus × pseudosylvestris, commonly known as the common teasel, is a hybrid plant in the burdock family (Arctiidae).

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces purple to pink flower heads that are surrounded by bracts, which give it a distinctive appearance. It is native to Europe and has been naturalized in parts of North America.

Dipsacus × pseudosylvestris is often found in disturbed soils and is used in traditional herbal medicine.
