Draba nivalis f. hebecarpa

Draba nivalis f. hebecarpa, commonly known as the alpine rockcress, is a low-growing perennial plant native to alpine and arctic regions.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically thrives in rocky, well-drained soils at high altitudes. Its leaves are basal, rosette-forming, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

This subspecies is notable for its adaptation to cold climates and its ability to survive in nutrient-poor environments.
