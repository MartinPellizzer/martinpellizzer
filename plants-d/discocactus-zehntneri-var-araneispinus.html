Discocactus zehntneri var. araneispinus

Discocactus zehntneri var. araneispinus, commonly known as the spider-tailed cactus, is a unique species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, long, thin stems that resemble spider legs, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking contrast to its green, spiny structure. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its slow growth rate and compact form make it an attractive addition to rock gardens and desert landscapes.
