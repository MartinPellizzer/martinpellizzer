Dypsis oreophila

Dypsis oreophila, commonly known as the mountain bamboo palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to Madagascar and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and feathery, pinnate leaves. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in mountainous regions. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.

Dypsis oreophila produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, followed by the development of coconut-like fruits.
