Delphinium parryi var. maritimum

Delphinium parryi var. maritimum, commonly known as the coastal delphinium, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in coastal areas, thriving in sandy or gravelly soils with good drainage. The plant features tall, erect stems that bear clusters of blue to violet flowers, which are attractive to pollinators. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

This variety is well adapted to maritime climates and can tolerate salt spray and strong winds.
