Diplophyllum veroniciforme

Diplophyllum veroniciforme, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense, velvety layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its native ecosystems.
