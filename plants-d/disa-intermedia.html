Disa intermedia

Disa intermedia, commonly known as the pink disa, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in grasslands and open woodlands, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers are tubular with a distinctive spurred lip, and it produces small, yellowish-green berries that contain the seeds.

Disa intermedia is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
