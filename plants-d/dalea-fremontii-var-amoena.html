Dalea fremontii var. amoena

Dalea fremontii var. amoena, commonly known as the Fremont's Dalea, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of pink to red pea-like flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as plains and foothills. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

It is a member of the legume family (Fabaceae) and has adapted to arid environments through its deep root system and drought-resistant features.
