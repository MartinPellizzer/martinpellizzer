Diospyros foliolosa

Diospyros foliolosa, commonly known as the leafy persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often lobed or serrated. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that give way to fleshy, orange-red berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Diospyros foliolosa is valued for its ornamental foliage and has traditional uses in local medicine.
