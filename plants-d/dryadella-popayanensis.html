Dryadella popayanensis

Dryadella popayanensis, commonly known as the Popayan dryad, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Andean regions of Colombia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae, which includes well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, white pubescence. It produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, which are followed by small, capsule-like fruits.

This species is often found in humid, shaded environments within cloud forests, where it plays a role in local biodiversity.
