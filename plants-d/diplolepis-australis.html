Diplolepis australis

Diplolepis australis, commonly known as the Australian bristly rasp, is a perennial shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy, often reddish stems. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, red berries. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptic, and often have a rough texture.

Diplolepis australis is adapted to dry, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.
