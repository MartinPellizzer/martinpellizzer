Drypetes stipulacea

Drypetes stipulacea, commonly known as the white-flowered ironwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree produces small, round fruits that are typically red or orange when mature. It has a strong, aromatic wood that is highly valued for construction and furniture-making.

The plant is also noted for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its tolerance to drought conditions.
