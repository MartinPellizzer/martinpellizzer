Diospyros mexicana

Diospyros mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and simple, alternate leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by oval, orange-red fruits with a sweet, astringent flavor. Its fruit is often consumed fresh or used in traditional remedies.

The plant is also valued for its hard wood, which is used in furniture and tool-making.
