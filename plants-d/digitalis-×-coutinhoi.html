Digitalis × coutinhoi

Digitalis × coutinhoi, commonly known as the Portuguese foxglove, is a hybrid species belonging to the Plantaginaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and striking, spurred flowers that range in color from purple to pink. The plant typically grows in temperate regions, preferring well-drained soils and partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for its unique floral display.
