Delosperma gautengense

Delosperma gautengense, commonly known as the Gauteng ice plant, is a succulent perennial plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, branching stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in arid and semi-arid regions.
