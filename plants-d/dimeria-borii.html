Dimeria borii

Dimeria borii, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a perennial flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads made up of many small florets. The plant has a low, spreading growth habit with silvery-gray leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. Its yellow flowers bloom in spring, and the plant produces small, hard seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Dimeria borii is adapted to survive in hot, dry conditions with minimal water availability.
