Dianthus furcatus subsp. subulatus

Dianthus furcatus subsp.

subulatus, commonly known as the slender-flowered campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces pink to red, five-petaled flowers that bloom in summer.

The subspecies is distinguished by its narrow, needle-like leaves and compact inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and ecological landscapes.
