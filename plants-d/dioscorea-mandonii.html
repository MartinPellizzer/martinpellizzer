Dioscorea mandonii

Dioscorea mandonii, commonly known as the Mandon yam, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary part used for medicinal and culinary purposes. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers and heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. Its tubers are valued for their high starch content and traditional use in herbal medicine.

Dioscorea mandonii thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
