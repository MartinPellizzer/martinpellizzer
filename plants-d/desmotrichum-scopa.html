Desmotrichum scopa

Desmotrichum scopa, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a perennial flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of gray-green, hairy leaves that help reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces small, yellow composite flowers that bloom in spring, followed by fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to extreme temperature fluctuations.

Due to its drought tolerance and medicinal properties, it is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
