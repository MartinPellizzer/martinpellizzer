Doodia alopecuroides

Doodia alopecuroides, commonly known as the slender wood sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet soils in forested areas, often found in shaded environments. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikes. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

Doodia alopecuroides plays a role in maintaining soil stability and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
