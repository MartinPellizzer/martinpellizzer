Dorstenia fruticulosa

Dorstenia fruticulosa, commonly known as the "Beaded Vine," is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a climbing shrub characterized by its long, slender stems and small, bead-like leaves that are often clustered in whorls. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Dorstenia fruticulosa is valued for its ornamental foliage and is relatively easy to care for in suitable growing conditions.
