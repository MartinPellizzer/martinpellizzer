Dahlia rudis

Dahlia rudis, commonly known as the sea dahlia, is a perennial tuberous-rooted plant native to coastal regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, orange, and yellow. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it well-suited for coastal environments. Its tuberous roots allow it to survive in salty, sandy soils and withstand periodic flooding.

The botanical features of Dahlia rudis make it a unique and resilient addition to coastal gardens.
