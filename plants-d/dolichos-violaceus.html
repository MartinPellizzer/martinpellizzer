Dolichos violaceus

Dolichos violaceus, commonly known as the purple bean or velvet bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, violet-pink flowers that give way to elongated, bean-like pods containing edible seeds. It is often grown for its protein-rich seeds, which are used in various cuisines.

Additionally, the plant has been studied for its potential medicinal properties due to the presence of alkaloids and other bioactive compounds.
