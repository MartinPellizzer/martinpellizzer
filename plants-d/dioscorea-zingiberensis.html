Dioscorea zingiberensis

Dioscorea zingiberensis, commonly known as the yellow arrowroot, is a tropical plant belonging to the Dioscoreaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary part used for culinary purposes. The plant produces small, green, heart-shaped leaves and bears yellow flowers that are clustered in spikes. Its tubers are starchy and have a mild, sweet flavor, making them a popular ingredient in various cuisines.

The plant is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
