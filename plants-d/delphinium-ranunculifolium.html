Delphinium ranunculifolium

Delphinium ranunculifolium, commonly known as the leafy delphinium, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 6 feet. The plant produces showy, spurred flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, with finely divided, fern-like foliage that adds ornamental value throughout the growing season.

Delphinium ranunculifolium thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and natural landscapes.
