Doronicum thirkei

Doronicum thirkei, commonly known as the Thirke's leopard's bane, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disc florets. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30-60 centimeters, with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are yellow with a distinctive purple or brown center, blooming in late spring to early summer.

Doronicum thirkei is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to cooler, high-altitude environments.
