Diospyros ferrea f. degeneri

Diospyros ferrea f. degeneri, commonly known as the Brazilian persimmon, is a variant of the persimmon tree native to South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree characterized by its thick, leathery leaves and smooth, dark bark. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into orange to red, fleshy fruits. The leaves are typically elliptical with a pointed tip and have a glossy upper surface.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions.
