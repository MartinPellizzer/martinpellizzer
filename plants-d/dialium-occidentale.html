Dialium occidentale

Dialium occidentale, commonly known as the African star apple, is a tropical tree native to West Africa.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its large, palmate leaves and showy, white or pinkish flowers. The fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to reveal a sweet, starchy pulp surrounding the seeds. It is often cultivated for its edible fruit and is also valued for its ornamental appearance.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is commonly found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.
