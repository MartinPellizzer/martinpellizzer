Dalbergia pervillei

Dalbergia pervillei, commonly known as the Perville's rosewood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The tree produces hard, dark wood that is highly valued for its beauty and durability. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate.

Due to its ornamental value and timber quality, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a decorative plant.
