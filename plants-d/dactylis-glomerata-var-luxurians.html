Dactylis glomerata var. luxurians

Dactylis glomerata var. luxurians, commonly known as sweet veld grass, is a perennial grass species characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit.

It features narrow, blue-green leaves and produces clusters of small, pale green to brownish flowers. This variety is known for its high nutritional value and is often used in forage and pasture systems. It thrives in temperate climates and is adaptable to a range of soil types.

Its rapid growth and ability to form dense sods make it a valuable plant for erosion control and livestock grazing.
