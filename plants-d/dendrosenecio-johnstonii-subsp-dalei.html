Dendrosenecio johnstonii subsp. dalei

Dendrosenecio johnstonii subsp.

dalei, commonly known as the Johnson's tree heath, is a striking member of the Asteraceae family native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its unique, tree-like structure with dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces striking yellow-orange flower heads that bloom in late winter to early spring. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is a sought-after plant among succulent enthusiasts.
