Daucus maximus f. leptophyllus

Daucus maximus f. leptophyllus, commonly known as the slender-leafed wild carrot, is a botanical variant of the wild carrot species.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and smaller, less showy flowers compared to the typical wild carrot. This plant belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and vegetables. It thrives in open, sunny habitats and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.
