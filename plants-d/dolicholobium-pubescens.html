Dolicholobium pubescens

Dolicholobium pubescens, commonly known as the hairy pod senna, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant features hairy stems and leaves, which contribute to its common name. It produces yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Dolicholobium pubescens is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
