Dianthus superbus subsp. sajanensis

Dianthus superbus subsp.

sajanensis, commonly known as the Sajansky Dianthus, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink and red.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
