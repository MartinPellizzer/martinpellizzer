Diaphananthe oxycentron

Diaphananthe oxycentron, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets. Its growth habit is typically shrub-like or small tree-like, reaching heights of up to 5 meters.

Diaphananthe oxycentron is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.
