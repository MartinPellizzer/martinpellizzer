Dianthus superbus var. monticola

Dianthus superbus var. monticola, commonly known as the alpine campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and vibrant pink to red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant features hairy stems and leaves, adapted to withstand cold and harsh alpine conditions. Its flowers are typically arranged in clusters, and the species is known for its ornamental value in rock gardens.

Dianthus superbus var. monticola is often found in dry, sunny habitats at high elevations.
