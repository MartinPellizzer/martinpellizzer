Draba atuelica var. leiocarpa

Draba atuelica var. leiocarpa, commonly known as the smoothleaf whitlow grass, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically features basal rosettes of narrow, glabrous leaves, which help it conserve moisture in arid environments. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, producing clusters of white or pale yellow flowers.

This variety is adapted to rocky and gravelly soils, often found in mountainous regions.
