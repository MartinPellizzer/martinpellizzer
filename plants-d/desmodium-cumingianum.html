Desmodium cumingianum

Desmodium cumingianum, commonly known as the Cuming's burr legume, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in spherical inflorescences. The plant is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria in its root nodules. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Desmodium cumingianum is also valued for its potential use in soil conservation and as a forage crop for livestock.
