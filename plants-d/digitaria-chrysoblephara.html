Digitaria chrysoblephara

Digitaria chrysoblephara, commonly known as golden leaved bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive golden-green foliage, which becomes more pronounced in the summer months. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as swamps and marshes, and can reach heights of up to 2 meters. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often tinged with red or purple.

Digitaria chrysoblephara plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
