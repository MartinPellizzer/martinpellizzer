Dioscorea thermarum

Dioscorea thermarum, commonly known as the hot spring yam, is a rare and unique member of the Dioscoreaceae family.

It is native to the volcanic regions of Ethiopia and is adapted to grow in geothermal areas with high temperatures. The plant produces tuberous roots that are rich in starch and have been traditionally used for food and medicinal purposes. Its growth is closely tied to the thermal environment, making it a fascinating subject for botanical study.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species with limited distribution.
