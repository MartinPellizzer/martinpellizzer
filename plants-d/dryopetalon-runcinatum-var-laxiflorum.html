Dryopetalon runcinatum var. laxiflorum

Dryopetalon runcinatum var. laxiflorum, commonly known as the slender-flowered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or violet. The leaves are lanceolate, often hairy, and arranged alternately along the stem. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
