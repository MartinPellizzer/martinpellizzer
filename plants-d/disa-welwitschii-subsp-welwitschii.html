Disa welwitschii subsp. welwitschii

Disa welwitschii subsp.

welwitschii, commonly known as the Welwitsch disa, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and showy, spurred flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink and purple.

The plant is adapted to grow in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions. Its flowers are pollinated by specific insects, contributing to its ecological significance in its native habitat.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts worldwide.
