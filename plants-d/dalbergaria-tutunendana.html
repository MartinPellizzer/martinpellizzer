Dalbergaria tutunendana

Dalbergaria tutunendana, commonly known as the Natal dwarf legume, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 meter and has small, trifoliate leaves. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, arranged in dense clusters, and it produces small, hairy pods that contain several seeds.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.
