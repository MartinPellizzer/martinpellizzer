Dryopteris medioxima

Dryopteris medioxima, commonly known as the Mexican wood fern, is a species of fern native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its fronds that are typically bipinnate, with a distinctive, upright growth habit. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. Its spores are produced on the undersides of its fronds, and it reproduces through sexual reproduction via spore dispersal.

The botanical features of D. medioxima make it an important species in ecological studies of tropical and subtropical forest ecosystems.
