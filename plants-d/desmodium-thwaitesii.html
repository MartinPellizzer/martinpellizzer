Desmodium thwaitesii

Desmodium thwaitesii, commonly known as the African burr legume, is a perennial herb native to sub-Saharan Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant has trifoliate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces hairy, spiny pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and woodland areas.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
