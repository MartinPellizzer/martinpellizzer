Diospyros liukiuensis

Diospyros liukiuensis, commonly known as the Ryukyu persimmon, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Ryukyu Islands of Japan.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange to red berries that are edible when fully ripe.
