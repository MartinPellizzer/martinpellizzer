Droogmansia

Droogmansia, commonly known as the "bushy broom," is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features dense, bushy foliage with narrow, grass-like leaves and produces vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. Droogmansia thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value lies in its compact growth habit and long-lasting blooms.
