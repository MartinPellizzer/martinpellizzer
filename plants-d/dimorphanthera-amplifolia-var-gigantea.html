Dimorphanthera amplifolia var. gigantea

Dimorphanthera amplifolia var. gigantea, commonly known as the giant broadleaf orchid, is a striking tropical plant characterized by its large, lance-shaped leaves and showy, fragrant flowers.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is native to specific regions in Southeast Asia. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded forest environments. Its flowers, which can reach several inches in diameter, are usually pale yellow to orange with intricate patterns.

This variant is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in greenhouses or tropical gardens.
