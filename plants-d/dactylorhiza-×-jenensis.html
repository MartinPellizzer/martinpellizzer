Dactylorhiza × jenensis

Dactylorhiza × jenensis, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically blooms in late summer, producing clusters of pale pink to white flowers with a distinctive three-lobed lip. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodland or grassland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
