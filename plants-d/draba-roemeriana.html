Draba roemeriana

Draba roemeriana, commonly known as Roemer's whitlow grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a small, mat-forming perennial that typically grows in alpine and arctic regions. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are basal, rosette-like, and covered in fine hairs, aiding in water retention.

Draba roemeriana is adapted to cold climates and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils.
