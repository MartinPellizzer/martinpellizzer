Diospyros virginiana var. pubescens

Diospyros virginiana var. pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent persimmon, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its rough, often hairy bark and oval-shaped leaves with a serrated margin. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, followed by orange to red berries in the fall. The berries are edible when fully ripe and have a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor.

This variety is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
