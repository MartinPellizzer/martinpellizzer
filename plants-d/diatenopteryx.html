Diatenopteryx

Diatenopteryx, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

It is native to parts of North America and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant features showy, yellow ray flowers that surround a central disk of smaller florets. Its foliage is typically lance-shaped and covered in fine, hairy textures.

Diatenopteryx is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and naturalized areas.
