Daphne oleoides subsp. hispanica

Daphne oleoides subsp.

hispanica, commonly known as Spanish daphne, is a deciduous shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula. It features aromatic, glandular leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs.

The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in late winter to early spring, typically in shades of pink to white. Its bark is smooth and reddish-brown, and it thrives in well-drained, alkaline soils.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in gardens and used in traditional herbal remedies.
