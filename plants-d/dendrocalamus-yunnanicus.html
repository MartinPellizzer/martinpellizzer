Dendrocalamus yunnanicus

Dendrocalamus yunnanicus, commonly known as the Yunnan bamboo, is a tall, fast-growing grass species native to southern China.

It belongs to the Bambusoideae subfamily and is characterized by its robust, hollow stems that can reach heights of up to 20 meters. The plant is valued for its strong and durable culms, which are widely used in construction, papermaking, and as a source of bamboo shoots. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions with high humidity.

Dendrocalamus yunnanicus plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
