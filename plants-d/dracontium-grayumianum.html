Dracontium grayumianum

Dracontium grayumianum, commonly known as the Amazonian arrowhead, is a herbaceous plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. The plant features heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a waxy surface, aiding in water retention in its humid habitat. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are adapted for pollination by insects.

Dracontium grayumianum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some indigenous communities.
