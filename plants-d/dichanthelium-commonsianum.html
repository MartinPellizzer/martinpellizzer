Dichanthelium commonsianum

Dichanthelium commonsianum, commonly known as the common panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and riparian areas. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in wetland environments and is often found in association with other wetland plants.

Dichanthelium commonsianum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
