Dissotis erecta

Dissotis erecta, commonly known as the upright dissotis, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental appeal and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
