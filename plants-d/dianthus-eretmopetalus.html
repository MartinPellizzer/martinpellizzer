Dianthus eretmopetalus

Dianthus eretmopetalus, commonly known as the alpine campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink or white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, while its stems are often sprawling or ascending.

Dianthus eretmopetalus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
