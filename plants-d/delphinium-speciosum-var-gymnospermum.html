Delphinium speciosum var. gymnospermum

Delphinium speciosum var. gymnospermum, commonly known as the gymnospermous larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the western United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, spindly stems and showy, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are compound, with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets, and it produces a unique, elongated seed pod that splits open at maturity.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and its role in local ecosystems.
