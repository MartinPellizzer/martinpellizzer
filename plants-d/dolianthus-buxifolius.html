Dolianthus buxifolius

Dolianthus buxifolius, commonly known as the boxleaf daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of white and yellow. The plant has narrow, leathery leaves that resemble those of boxwood, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Dolianthus buxifolius is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
