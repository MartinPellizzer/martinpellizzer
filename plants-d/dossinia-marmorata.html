Dossinia marmorata

Dossinia marmorata, commonly known as the marble lichen, is a foliose lichen that exhibits a distinctive, intricately patterned appearance.

It typically grows on tree bark, especially on conifers, in humid and shaded environments. The thallus is leathery and features a complex, marbled pattern of white, yellow, and green pigments. It is characterized by its slow growth and ability to thrive in nutrient-poor conditions.

This lichen is often used as an indicator species for air quality due to its sensitivity to pollution.
