Diplostephium hispidum

Diplostephium hispidum, commonly known as the hairy bristlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and robust, cylindrical stems. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its inflorescence consists of a compact, spike-like cluster of small, spikelet-bearing branches.

Diplostephium hispidum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its hardy nature.
