Daucus mauritanicus

Daucus mauritanicus, commonly known as the Mauritania carrot, is a flowering plant native to North Africa and the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like carrots and parsley. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the family. Its leaves are pinnately divided, with a distinctive fern-like appearance.

The fruit is a dry, two-lobed schizocarp, characteristic of the genus Daucus.
