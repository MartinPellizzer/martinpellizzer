Declieuxia chiococcoides subsp. mexicana

Declieuxia chiococcoides subsp.

mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican feather grass, is a perennial grass native to Mexico. It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and feathery, silver-green foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early spring, followed by fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in arid conditions.
