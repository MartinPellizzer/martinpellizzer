Dysoxylum molle

Dysoxylum molle, commonly known as the silky tamarind, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by elongated, woody pods containing edible seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its timber and traditional medicinal uses.
