Dactyloctenium distachyum

Dactyloctenium distachyum, commonly known as the two-stemmed grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides. It is adapted to dry conditions and can thrive in poor soil environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and for soil stabilization.
