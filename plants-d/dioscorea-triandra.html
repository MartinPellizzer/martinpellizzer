Dioscorea triandra

Dioscorea triandra, commonly known as African yambean, is a climbing vine belonging to the Dioscoreaceae family.

It is native to tropical Africa and is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellowish flowers followed by elongated, tuberous roots that are its primary economic product. These roots are used as a starch source and have traditional medicinal uses in various African cultures.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its nutritional value.
