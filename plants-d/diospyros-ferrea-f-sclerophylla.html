Diospyros ferrea f. sclerophylla

Diospyros ferrea f. sclerophylla, commonly known as the Brazilian persimmon, is a variant of the persimmon tree native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by orange to reddish fruits that are edible when fully ripe. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and its fruit, which has a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor.
