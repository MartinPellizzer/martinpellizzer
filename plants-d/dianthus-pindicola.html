Dianthus pindicola

Dianthus pindicola, commonly known as the Pindus campion, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its clusters of bright red, pink, or white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is often found in mountainous regions.

Dianthus pindicola is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blossoms.
