Dasyanthus conglobatus

Dasyanthus conglobatus, commonly known as the globular daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in a dense, rounded inflorescence. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Dasyanthus conglobatus is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in Mediterranean climates.
