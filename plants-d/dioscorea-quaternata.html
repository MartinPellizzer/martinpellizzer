Dioscorea quaternata

Dioscorea quaternata, commonly known as the African yam bean, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and tuberous roots. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and cylindrical, fleshy fruits that contain several seeds. Its tubers are used as a food source and have a starchy, slightly sweet flavor.

The plant is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.
