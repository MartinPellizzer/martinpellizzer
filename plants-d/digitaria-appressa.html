Digitaria appressa

Digitaria appressa, commonly known as little fingergrass, is a species of grass native to the Americas.

It is a perennial plant that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands and along stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often used in ecological restoration projects.

Digitaria appressa plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
