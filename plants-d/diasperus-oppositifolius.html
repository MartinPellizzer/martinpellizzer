Diasperus oppositifolius

Diasperus oppositifolius, commonly known as the opposite-leaf sandmat, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically forms dense mats on sandy or rocky soils. The plant features opposite, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, yellowish flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces small, spherical fruits known as cocci.

Diasperus oppositifolius is adapted to arid environments and is often found in coastal and inland sandy habitats.
