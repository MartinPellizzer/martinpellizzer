Dondia maritima

Dondia maritima, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and silver-gray foliage. The plant thrives in saline and well-drained soils, often found in dunes and rocky shores. Its flowers, which bloom in late summer, are typically blue or purple and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Dondia maritima is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize coastal environments.
