Damasonium constrictum

Damasonium constrictum, commonly known as the narrowleaf water clover, is a aquatic plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and marshes. The plant has small, heart-shaped leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern on the water surface. Its flowers are tiny and clustered in dense inflorescences, usually appearing in early summer.

Damasonium constrictum plays a role in stabilizing aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
