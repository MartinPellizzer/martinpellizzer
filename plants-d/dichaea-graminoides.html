Dichaea graminoides

Dichaea graminoides, commonly known as the grassy dichaea, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of trichomes. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically borne on a terminal inflorescence. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, which contributes to its common name.

Dichaea graminoides is often found in shaded, moist environments such as cloud forests and is adapted to thrive in high humidity conditions.
