Dendrobium mannii

Dendrobium mannii, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, yellowish-orange flowers with a distinctive, sweet scent. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and requires well-draining soil and bright, indirect light.

Dendrobium mannii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for horticultural purposes.
