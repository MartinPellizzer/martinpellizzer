Dactylorchis sambucina

Dactylorchis sambucina, commonly known as the Christmas orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and swampy areas. The plant features a slender, erect stem with a single, showy orchid-like flower that blooms in late fall or early winter. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, and the flower has a distinctive, fragrant scent.

The species is notable for its unique flowering time, which coincides with the holiday season.
