Dioscorea fodinarum

Dioscorea fodinarum, commonly known as the African yam vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its twining stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by tuberous roots, which are its primary edible part. These tubers are often used as a food source and have a starchy, sweet flavor.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
