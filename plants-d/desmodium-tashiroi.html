Desmodium tashiroi

Desmodium tashiroi, commonly known as the Tashiro's sensitive plant, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves that fold shut when touched. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest undergrowth. Its small, pale yellow flowers are followed by small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Desmodium tashiroi is also noted for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in certain ecological contexts.
