Dryandra drummondii subsp. hiemalis

Dryandra drummondii subsp.

hiemalis, commonly known as the Christmas Dryandra, is a shrub native to Western Australia. It is characterized by its evergreen foliage and distinctive, urn-shaped flowers that bloom in winter.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green fruits that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to the Mediterranean climate of its native region.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in local habitats.
