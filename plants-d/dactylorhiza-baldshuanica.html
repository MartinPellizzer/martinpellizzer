Dactylorhiza baldshuanica

Dactylorhiza baldshuanica, commonly known as the Baldshuan orchid, is a terrestrial orchid belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its erect, leafy stems and produces a single, showy spike of violet or purple flowers. Native to the mountainous regions of northern Europe, it thrives in moist, acidic soils and shaded environments. The plant has a complex root system with mycorrhizal associations that aid in nutrient uptake.

Its flowering period typically occurs in late spring to early summer, making it a notable species in alpine and subalpine habitats.
