Dasiphora fruticosa var. cardiopetala

Dasiphora fruticosa var. cardiopetala, commonly known as the heart-leaved mountain avens, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Europe.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine habitats. The plant features small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, arranged in dense, rounded clusters. Its leaves are distinctive, with a heart-shaped base and a serrated margin, contributing to its ornamental value.

The species is valued in horticulture for its hardiness and ability to thrive in challenging environmental conditions.
