Digitalis grandiflora var. macedonica

Digitalis grandiflora var. macedonica, commonly known as the Macedonian foxglove, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan region.

It features tall, erect stems that bear clusters of striking violet to purple flowers. The leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, typical of the Digitalis genus. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in traditional medicine.
