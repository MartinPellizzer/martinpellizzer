Dictyoneura obtusa

Dictyoneura obtusa, commonly known as the broad-leafed mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on various host trees in tropical and subtropical regions.

It has small, leathery leaves that are typically clustered at the tips of branches. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruits are fleshy berries that contain seeds covered in a sticky substance to aid in attachment to host plants.

The botanical structure of Dictyoneura obtusa reflects its parasitic lifestyle, with reduced root systems and specialized structures for nutrient absorption.
