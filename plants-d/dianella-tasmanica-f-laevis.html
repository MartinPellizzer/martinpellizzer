Dianella tasmanica f. laevis

Dianella tasmanica f. laevis, commonly known as the Tasmanian lily, is a perennial evergreen plant native to Tasmania.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color and grow in dense, clumping formations. The plant produces striking, bell-shaped white or pale blue flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.
