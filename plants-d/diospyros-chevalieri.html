Diospyros chevalieri

Diospyros chevalieri, commonly known as the Philippine persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grayish-brown bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by orange to red, fleshy berries.

Its fruit is edible, though typically consumed when fully ripe, and it is valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.
