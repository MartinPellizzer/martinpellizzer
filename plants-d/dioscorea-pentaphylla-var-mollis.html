Dioscorea pentaphylla var. mollis

Dioscorea pentaphylla var. mollis, commonly known as the silky yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its hairy, cylindrical stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by tuberous roots that are often used in traditional medicine. Its tubers are valued for their nutritional content and have been utilized in various cultural practices.

The silky yam is also noted for its adaptability to diverse soil types and its ability to thrive in humid, tropical climates.
