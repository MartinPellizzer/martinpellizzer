Disperis atacorensis

Disperis atacorensis, commonly known as the Atacorensis orchid, is a rare and endemic species native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, often fragrant floral structures. The plant typically grows in high-altitude cloud forests, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its flowers are usually small, with a unique combination of colors and patterns that aid in pollination by specific insect species.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
