Davya

Davya, commonly known as the common tansy, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and yellowish-orange composite flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny environments such as meadows and wetlands. Davya is known for its strong aromatic scent and has been used historically in herbal remedies.

However, it contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
