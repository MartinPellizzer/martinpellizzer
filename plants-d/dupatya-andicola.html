Dupatya andicola

Dupatya andicola, commonly known as the Andes dwarf orchid, is a species of orchid native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils. The plant features a compact growth habit with a single, short stem and a few narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, with a distinctive coloration that varies from white to pale pink.

Dupatya andicola is adapted to the cool, humid climates of its native habitat and is often found in open, sunny areas.
