Donax grandis

Donax grandis, commonly known as the goldenbush, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swamps, and thrives in moist, sandy soils. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a distinctive golden hue when dried.

Donax grandis is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
