Diospyros maritima var. oblongata

Diospyros maritima var. oblongata, commonly known as the mangrove persimmon, is a variant of the mangrove persimmon tree native to coastal regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that thrives in brackish or saline water environments, often found in mangrove forests. The plant has a distinctive appearance with oblong-shaped leaves and small, fleshy fruits that are typically orange to red in color. It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various marine and terrestrial species.

The tree is also valued for its timber and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
