Dioscorea obtusifolia

Dioscorea obtusifolia, commonly known as the arrowleaf yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces tuberous roots that are often used as a food source in various cultures. These tubers are rich in starch and have a flavor similar to potatoes.

Dioscorea obtusifolia is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
