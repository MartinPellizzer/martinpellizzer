Deutzia glaucophylla

Deutzia glaucophylla, commonly known as the silver-leaf deutzia, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features slender, arching branches covered in clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in early summer. The leaves are ovate, with a distinctive silvery-white underside that gives the plant its common name. It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and prefers well-drained soil in full sun to partial shade.

This ornamental plant is valued for its elegant appearance and is often used in gardens for its ornamental value.
