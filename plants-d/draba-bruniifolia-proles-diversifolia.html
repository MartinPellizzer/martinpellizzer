Draba bruniifolia proles diversifolia

Draba bruniifolia proles diversifolia, commonly known as the alpine buttercup, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and is often found in alpine and subalpine regions. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with four petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are basal, simple, and often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

This species is adapted to cold climates and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations.
