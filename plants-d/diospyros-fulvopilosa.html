Diospyros fulvopilosa

Diospyros fulvopilosa, commonly known as the golden-leaf persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray-brown bark and opposite, ovate leaves that are usually reddish beneath. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange to red, fleshy fruits that are edible when ripe.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.
