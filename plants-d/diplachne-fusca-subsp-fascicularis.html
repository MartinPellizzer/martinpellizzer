Diplachne fusca subsp. fascicularis

Diplachne fusca subsp.

fascicularis, commonly known as the narrowleaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Australia. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically less than 2 millimeters wide.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences containing numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in a variety of soil types but is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is considered a weed in some agricultural areas.
