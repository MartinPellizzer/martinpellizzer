Derris caudata

Derris caudata, commonly known as the spurred snake-root, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and distinctive pod fruits. The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in racemose inflorescences. Its roots contain rotenone, a natural insecticide used in traditional agriculture.

Derris caudata thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.
