Dryopteris pseudatrata

Dryopteris pseudatrata, commonly known as the false holly fern, is a species of fern native to parts of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen fronds that remain throughout the year, giving it a lush appearance. The fronds are typically triangular in cross-section and have a distinctive, leathery texture. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

This fern is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
