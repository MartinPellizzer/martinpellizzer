Desmodium dunnii

Desmodium dunnii, commonly known as the purple desmodium, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features trifoliate leaves with a distinctive purplish hue, and its small, pea-like flowers are typically white or pale pink. Desmodium dunnii thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or disturbed areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting pollinators.
