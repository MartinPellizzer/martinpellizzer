Dipcadi minor

Dipcadi minor, commonly known as the small bulbous orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Orchidaceae.

It is characterized by its small, tubular flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant produces a single, narrow stem that bears several flowers arranged in a spiral pattern. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is native to parts of Europe and Asia.

Due to its delicate appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
