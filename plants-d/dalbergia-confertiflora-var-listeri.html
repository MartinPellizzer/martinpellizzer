Dalbergia confertiflora var. listeri

Dalbergia confertiflora var. listeri, commonly known as the African rosewood, is a small to medium-sized tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its dark, aromatic wood, which is highly valued for furniture and musical instruments. The plant has bipinnate leaves, with small, oval leaflets arranged in pairs. Its flowers are typically small and clustered in dense inflorescences, often appearing in yellow or greenish-white hues.

The species is also notable for its ecological importance in providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
