Dichapetalum mombongense var. orientale

Dichapetalum mombongense var. orientale, commonly known as the East African flame tree, is a tropical tree native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy, red-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. The plant has a sturdy, straight trunk and can grow up to 20 meters tall, making it a prominent feature in its native habitat. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens and parks for its striking floral display.
