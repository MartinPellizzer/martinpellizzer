Dennstaedtia tenera var. dentata

Dennstaedtia tenera var. dentata, commonly known as the serrated maidenhair fern, is a species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are often serrated along the edges. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its rhizomes are creeping and produce clusters of small, spore-bearing sorus.

The foliage is often a deep green, with a feathery appearance that adds ornamental value to gardens.
