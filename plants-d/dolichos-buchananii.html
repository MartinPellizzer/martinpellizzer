Dolichos buchananii

Dolichos buchananii, commonly known as the African bean or wild cowpea, is a climbing legume native to Africa.

It features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellow flowers that develop into slender, pod-like fruits. The plant is characterized by its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species. Its seeds are edible and have been used traditionally for food and as a source of protein.

The plant also has medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
