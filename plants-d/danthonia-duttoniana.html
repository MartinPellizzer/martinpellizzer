Danthonia duttoniana

Danthonia duttoniana, commonly known as the Dutton's grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America, particularly found in Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and fine, narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand arid conditions. Its inflorescence consists of a single spikelet that is often concealed within the foliage, making it less conspicuous.

Danthonia duttoniana is valued for its ornamental potential and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
