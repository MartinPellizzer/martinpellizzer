Decaneuropsis blanda

Decaneuropsis blanda, commonly known as the softleaf dandelion, is a herbaceous plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters, featuring hairy stems and lobed leaves. Its flowers are yellow and bloom in the spring, attracting various pollinators.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal potential, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
