Danthoniopsis wasaensis

Danthoniopsis wasaensis, commonly known as the Wassa grass, is a perennial grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, pale green to brown flowers.

Danthoniopsis wasaensis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates in its native ecosystems.
