Diospyros hequetiae

Diospyros hequetiae, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other persimmon species. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, clustered flowers that develop into edible, orange to red fruits. Diospyros hequetiae is adapted to dry, tropical climates and is often found in scrubland and forest edges.

Its fruit is valued for its sweet, non-astringent flavor and is used both for food and traditional medicine.
