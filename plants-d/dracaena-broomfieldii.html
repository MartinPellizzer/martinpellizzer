Dracaena broomfieldii

Dracaena broomfieldii, commonly known as the Broomfield's dracaena, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, strap-like leaves that are typically dark green with a glossy sheen. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are followed by red or orange berries. It is often cultivated as a decorative indoor plant due to its air-purifying properties and ability to thrive in low-light conditions.

Dracaena broomfieldii is also valued for its resilience and ease of care, making it a popular choice for both novice and experienced gardeners.
