Drakaea concolor

Drakaea concolor, commonly known as the plain leek orchid, is an endemic species of orchid found in southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and a single, narrow, pale green to brownish leaf. The plant produces a single spike of greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive, leek-like shape. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is typically found in open habitats such as woodlands and heathlands.

Drakaea concolor is a small to medium-sized orchid with a unique floral structure that contributes to its ecological and botanical significance.
