Dioscorea cyphocarpa

Dioscorea cyphocarpa, commonly known as the African yam bean, is a flowering plant native to tropical and southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and tuberous roots. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its tubers are edible and have been traditionally used as a food source by indigenous communities.

The botanical features of D. cyphocarpa make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
