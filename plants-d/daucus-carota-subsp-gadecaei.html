Daucus carota subsp. gadecaei

Daucus carota subsp.

gadecaei, commonly known as the Canary Island carrot, is a subspecies native to the Canary Islands. It is a biennial plant characterized by its taproot, which is typically smaller and less fleshy compared to the common carrot.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped umbel, typical of the Apiaceae family. Its leaves are pinnately divided, with a distinctive silvery underside.
