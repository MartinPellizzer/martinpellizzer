Delphinium beesianum f. ramosum

Delphinium beesianum f. ramosum, commonly known as the branched delphinium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features tall, erect stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height, adorned with dense clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant has lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. Its flowers are typically arranged in spires, with each bloom consisting of five petals and a prominent central spur.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and architectural form.
