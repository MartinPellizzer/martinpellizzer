Dracaena bacularis

Dracaena bacularis, commonly known as the silver dracaena, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and long, narrow leaves that are often a striking silver-white color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by red berries, which are edible for birds but not for humans. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Dracaena bacularis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its aesthetic appeal and air-purifying qualities.
