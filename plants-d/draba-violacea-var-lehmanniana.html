Draba violacea var. lehmanniana

Draba violacea var. lehmanniana, commonly known as the Lehmann's whitlow grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its basal rosette of linear, glaucous leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically violet or purple in color, which bloom in early spring. It is native to arctic and alpine regions of Europe and Asia, thriving in rocky and sandy soils.

This species is adapted to cold climates and is often found in mountainous and tundra environments.
