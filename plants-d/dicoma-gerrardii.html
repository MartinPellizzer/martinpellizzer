Dicoma gerrardii

Dicoma gerrardii, commonly known as the Cape snowbush, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit. The plant features small, white to pale blue flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in spring. Its leaves are typically greyish-green and covered in fine hairs, aiding in water retention.

Dicoma gerrardii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in dry, rocky environments.
