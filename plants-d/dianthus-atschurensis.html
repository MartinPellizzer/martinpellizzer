Dianthus atschurensis

Dianthus atschurensis, commonly known as the Atschuren pink, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its stems are often hairy and erect.

Dianthus atschurensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
