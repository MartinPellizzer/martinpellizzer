Dicella holosericea

Dicella holosericea, commonly known as the silky sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure with hairy, silvery-gray foliage that helps reduce water loss. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, typically blooming during the cooler months. Its root system is extensive and adapted to survive in nutrient-poor, sandy soils.

Dicella holosericea plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small insects and animals in its native environment.
