Delphinium longziense

Delphinium longziense, commonly known as the Longzi larkspur, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems that bear clusters of striking blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes at high elevations, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a serrated edge, and the flowers have five petals that are often adorned with a distinctive spur.

Delphinium longziense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
