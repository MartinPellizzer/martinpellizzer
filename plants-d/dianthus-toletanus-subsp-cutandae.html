Dianthus toletanus subsp. cutandae

Dianthus toletanus subsp.

cutandae, commonly known as the Toletan carnation, is a species native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers.

The flowers are typically pink to red in color, with five petals and prominent, toothed lobes. The plant produces small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and borders.
