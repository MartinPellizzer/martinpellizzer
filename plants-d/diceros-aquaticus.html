Diceros aquaticus

Diceros aquaticus, commonly known as the water cobra plant, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often growing in a basal rosette.

Diceros aquaticus thrives in wetland environments and is often found near water bodies such as rivers and lakes.
