Deuterocohnia scapigera subsp. sanctae-crucis

Deuterocohnia scapigera subsp.

sanctae-crucis, commonly known as the Santa Cruz buttercup, is a member of the Bromeliaceae family. It is a small, terrestrial bromeliad native to the cloud forests of southern Brazil.

The plant is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are typically covered in a dense, white to grayish woolly coating. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in a spike, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in tropical gardens.
