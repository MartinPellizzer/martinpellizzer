Diplazium japonicum var. coreanum

Diplazium japonicum var. coreanum, commonly known as the Korean water fern, is a species of fern native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, pinnately compound leaves that are often submerged or floating in aquatic environments. The plant typically grows in slow-moving freshwater habitats such as ponds and streams. Its spores are produced on the undersides of its fronds, which are arranged in a distinctive, fan-like pattern.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in aquatic ecosystems.
