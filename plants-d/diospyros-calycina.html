Diospyros calycina

Diospyros calycina, commonly known as the African persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other well-known species like ebony and persimmon. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. Its small, yellowish flowers are followed by orange to red berries that contain a single seed.

Diospyros calycina is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in various African cultures.
