Digitalis × macedonica

Digitalis × macedonica, commonly known as the Macedonian foxglove, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the Balkans.

It belongs to the genus Digitalis, which includes several species of foxgloves known for their distinctive tubular flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, featuring tall, slender stems and clusters of purple or pinkish-purple flowers. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Digitalis × macedonica is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also valued in traditional medicine for its cardiac glycosides.
