Diospyros egrettarum

Diospyros egrettarum, commonly known as the egret's fig, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other well-known species like the ebony tree. The plant is characterized by its glossy dark green leaves and small, clustered yellow flowers that appear in the spring. Its fruit is a small, round fig-like berry that ripens to a dark purple color.

Diospyros egrettarum is often found in lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential medicinal uses.
