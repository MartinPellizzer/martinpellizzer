Draba micheorum

Draba micheorum, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada whitlow grass, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is a small, herbaceous annual that typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pale yellow flowers during the spring season. Its leaves are basal, linear, and often covered in fine hairs.

Draba micheorum is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.
