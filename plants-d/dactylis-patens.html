Dactylis patens

Dactylis patens, commonly known as hairy vetch, is a perennial leguminous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has a robust root system that helps prevent soil erosion and improves soil fertility through nitrogen fixation. It thrives in temperate climates and is often used as a cover crop or forage.

Dactylis patens is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
