Dioscorea galiiflora

Dioscorea galiiflora, commonly known as the purple yam, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Dioscoreaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, Africa, and the Pacific Islands. The plant is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are often used as a food source. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and often have a velvety texture.

The flowers are typically small and inconspicuous, clustered in spikes, and are usually greenish or yellowish in color.
