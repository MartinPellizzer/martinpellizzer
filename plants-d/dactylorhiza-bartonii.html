Dactylorhiza bartonii

Dactylorhiza bartonii, commonly known as the Barton's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and heaths. The plant features a rhizome with several fleshy, narrow leaves. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, arranged in a spike with a distinctive spurred shape.

Dactylorhiza bartonii is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
