Dracocephalum sibiricum

Dracocephalum sibiricum, commonly known as Siberian blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the cold climates of Central Asia and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

Dracocephalum sibiricum is typically found in meadows, grasslands, and along riverbanks in its natural habitat.
