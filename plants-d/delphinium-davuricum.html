Delphinium davuricum

Delphinium davuricum, commonly known as the Siberian larkspur, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features tall, slender stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height, adorned with clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem, and its root system consists of thick, fleshy rhizomes. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Delphinium davuricum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
