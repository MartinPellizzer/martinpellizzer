Dioscoreophyllum volkensii var. fernandense

Dioscoreophyllum volkensii var. fernandense, commonly known as the African arrowroot, is a climbing shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are used as a starch source. The plant has compound leaves with elongated leaflets and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters. Its fruit is a capsule that contains numerous seeds.

This species is often found in moist, shaded habitats and is valued for both its nutritional and medicinal properties.
