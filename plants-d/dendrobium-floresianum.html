Dendrobium floresianum

Dendrobium floresianum, commonly known as the Flores Island orchid, is an epiphytic orchid native to the Maluku Islands of Indonesia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with multiple stems that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces dense clusters of fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white, yellow, and pink, typically during the dry season. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the air and decaying organic matter.

Dendrobium floresianum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in tropical gardens and greenhouses.
