Dioscorea cayenensis

Dioscorea cayenensis, commonly known as yam, is a tuberous plant belonging to the family Dioscoreaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, the Americas, and Asia, and is widely cultivated for its edible tubers. The plant produces large, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves, while its underground tubers are the primary edible part. The tubers are rich in carbohydrates and have been used in traditional cuisines for centuries.

Additionally, certain species of Dioscorea, including D. cayenensis, have been studied for their potential medicinal properties.
