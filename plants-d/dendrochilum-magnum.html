Dendrochilum magnum

Dendrochilum magnum, commonly known as the giant tree orchid, is a remarkable epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 10 centimeters in diameter, often in shades of yellow and orange. The plant typically grows on trees in humid, tropical environments, using its aerial roots to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air. Its pseudobulbs are thick and segmented, storing water and nutrients, while its leaves are broad and leathery.

Dendrochilum magnum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
