Diheteropogon buchneri

Diheteropogon buchneri, commonly known as the Buchner's heteropogon, is a grass species native to certain regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open, grassy habitats and is adapted to arid or semi-arid conditions. Its flowers are small and clustered, forming dense inflorescences that are often wind-pollinated.

Diheteropogon buchneri is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
