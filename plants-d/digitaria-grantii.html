Digitaria grantii

Digitaria grantii, commonly known as the African grass or Grant's digitaria, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands, savannas, and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, blade-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a single spikelet, which is a characteristic feature of the grass family.

Digitaria grantii is often used in ecological studies due to its adaptability and role in soil stabilization.
