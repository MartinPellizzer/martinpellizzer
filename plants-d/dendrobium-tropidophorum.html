Dendrobium tropidophorum

Dendrobium tropidophorum, commonly known as the mountain orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is an epiphytic orchid, meaning it grows on the surface of trees or rocks without deriving nutrients from them. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and orange, typically blooming during the summer months. It is native to Southeast Asia, including countries like Thailand, Malaysia, and Indonesia.

Dendrobium tropidophorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
