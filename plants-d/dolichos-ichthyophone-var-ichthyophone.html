Dolichos ichthyophone var. ichthyophone

Dolichos ichthyophone var. ichthyophone, commonly known as the fishbone bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, serpentine stems and small, pinnate leaves. The plant produces elongated, bean-like pods that contain several seeds. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it thrives in warm, humid climates.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible seeds.
