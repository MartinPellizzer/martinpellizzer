Diospyros magnifica

Diospyros magnifica, commonly known as the magnificent persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The tree bears orange-red, fleshy fruits that are edible when fully ripe. Its wood is highly valued for its strength and fine grain, making it suitable for furniture and carving.

Diospyros magnifica thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.
