Desmodium scopulorum

Desmodium scopulorum, commonly known as rock dodder, is a perennial legume native to arid and rocky regions of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and small, pea-like flowers that bloom in shades of pink to purple. The plant has small, trifoliate leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It is adapted to drought conditions and thrives in well-drained soils.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often found in disturbed or rocky habitats.
