Dortmanna caudata

Dortmanna caudata, commonly known as the trailing orchid, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pendulous flowers that often hang from long, wiry stems. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are usually white or pale pink with a distinctive, spurred lip.

Dortmanna caudata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
