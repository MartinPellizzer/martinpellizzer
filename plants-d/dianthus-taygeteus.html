Dianthus taygeteus

Dianthus taygeteus, commonly known as the Greek campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that typically range from pink to red, with five petals and prominent stamens. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and grows in a compact, mound-like form. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Dianthus taygeteus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticultural displays.
