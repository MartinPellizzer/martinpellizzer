Diospyros littorea

Diospyros littorea, commonly known as the mangrove apple or sea apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the coastal regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and glossy, elliptical leaves that are opposite in arrangement. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by edible, fleshy berries resembling apples. It thrives in brackish or saline water, making it an important species in mangrove ecosystems.

Diospyros littorea plays a vital role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various marine and terrestrial organisms.
