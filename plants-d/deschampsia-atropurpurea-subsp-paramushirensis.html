Deschampsia atropurpurea subsp. paramushirensis

Deschampsia atropurpurea subsp.

paramushirensis, commonly known as the purple moor grass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia. It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and dense, tufted growth habit.

The plant produces spikelets that are typically purple-tinged, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in high-altitude environments.
