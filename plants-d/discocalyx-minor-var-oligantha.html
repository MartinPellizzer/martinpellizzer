Discocalyx minor var. oligantha

Discocalyx minor var. oligantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaf thimbleberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered white flowers that appear in late spring. The leaves are narrow and elliptical, with a smooth margin, and the plant produces edible berries that are typically red or orange when mature. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded to partially shaded habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
