Dianthus superbus f. albiflorus

Dianthus superbus f. albiflorus, commonly known as the white-flowered carnation, is a cultivar of the carnation plant characterized by its pale white to cream-colored flowers.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is native to Europe, particularly the British Isles. The plant features upright, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs.

Its flowers are clustered in dense, showy inflorescences, making it a popular choice for garden displays and floral arrangements.
