Dioscorea quartiniana var. latifolia

Dioscorea quartiniana var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf yam, is a member of the Dioscoreaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and produces tubers that are used in traditional medicine and as a food source. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its botanical features include a climbing habit with hairy stems and small, greenish-white flowers.

The tubers of this variety are often used in local cuisines and have been studied for their potential medicinal properties.
