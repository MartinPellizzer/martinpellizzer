Draba hoppeana subsp. cantabriae

Draba hoppeana subsp.

cantabriae, commonly known as the Cantabrian whitlow grass, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is a small, herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or limestone soils.

The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers during the spring season. Its leaves are basal, rosette-like, and covered in fine hairs.

It is adapted to harsh, alpine or subalpine environments, showing resilience to cold and poor soil conditions.
