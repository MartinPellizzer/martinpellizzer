Dendromecon harfordii var. rhamnoides

Dendromecon harfordii var. rhamnoides, commonly known as the Harford's tree mallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its woody stems and showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.
