Dioscorea amoena

Dioscorea amoena, commonly known as the beautiful yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary edible part of the plant. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while the stems are hairy and often twining around supporting structures.

The tubers of Dioscorea amoena are rich in starch and have been used traditionally for food and medicinal purposes.
