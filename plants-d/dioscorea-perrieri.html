Dioscorea perrieri

Dioscorea perrieri, commonly known as the Perrier yam, is a species of tuberous plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by cylindrical, fleshy fruits containing numerous seeds. Its tuberous roots are the primary part used, valued for their nutritional content and traditional medicinal uses.

Dioscorea perrieri is an important crop in some African regions, contributing to local food security and agricultural diversity.
