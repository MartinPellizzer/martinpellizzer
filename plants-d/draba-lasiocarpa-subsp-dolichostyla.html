Draba lasiocarpa subsp. dolichostyla

Draba lasiocarpa subsp.

dolichostyla, commonly known as the long-styled sandwort, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family. It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, fleshy leaves.

The flowers are small, typically white or pale yellow, and are arranged in a single umbel at the end of each stem. It thrives in arctic and alpine environments, often found on rocky slopes and gravelly soils.

This subspecies is notable for its elongated styles, which distinguish it from other members of the Draba genus.
