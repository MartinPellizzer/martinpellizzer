Deschampsia cespitosa subsp. turczaninowii

Deschampsia cespitosa subsp.

turczaninowii, commonly known as the Turczaninow's bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to the northern hemisphere. It is characterized by its fine, blue-green blades and forms dense, compact clumps.

This subspecies typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as meadows and wetlands. Its inflorescence consists of a loose panicle with spikelets that are often tinged with silver or blue.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in stabilizing soil and supporting wildlife.
