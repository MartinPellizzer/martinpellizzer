Desmogyne angustifolia

Desmogyne angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved desmogyne, is a species of flowering plant in the family Acanthaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
