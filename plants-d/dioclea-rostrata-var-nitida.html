Dioclea rostrata var. nitida

Dioclea rostrata var. nitida, commonly known as the shining passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that feature five sepals and five petals, often with a distinctive, recurved shape. The plant produces edible, fleshy berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a velvety texture.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm climates.
