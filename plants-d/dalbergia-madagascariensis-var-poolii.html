Dalbergia madagascariensis var. poolii

Dalbergia madagascariensis var. poolii, commonly known as the Madagascar rosewood, is a species native to Madagascar.

It is a small to medium-sized tree characterized by its dark, dense wood and aromatic properties. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish-white flowers. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its unique characteristics and limited distribution, it is considered a valuable and rare botanical species.
