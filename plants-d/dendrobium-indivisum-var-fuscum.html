Dendrobium indivisum var. fuscum

Dendrobium indivisum var. fuscum, commonly known as the black orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features dark brown to black stems that are often covered in a dense layer of brownish-gray pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow, typically blooming during the rainy season. It thrives in shaded, humid environments with well-drained soil.

Due to its unique appearance and rarity, it is highly valued in horticulture and conservation efforts.
