Damapana dichotoma

Damapana dichotoma, commonly known as the forked leaf plant, is a species native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive dichotomous branching pattern, where each stem splits into two equal parts. The plant typically has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Damapana dichotoma is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
