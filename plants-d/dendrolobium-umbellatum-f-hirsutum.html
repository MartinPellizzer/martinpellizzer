Dendrolobium umbellatum f. hirsutum

Dendrolobium umbellatum f. hirsutum, commonly known as the hairy umbrella bush, is a variant of the umbrella bush native to Australia.

It is a shrub characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, rugged appearance. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers in umbrella-like clusters, typically during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
