Diplotropis racemosa var. rosae

Diplotropis racemosa var. rosae, commonly known as the pink-veined ironwood, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage with distinctive pink veins, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers arranged in racemes, typically blooming in late spring. It is a hardy shrub that thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in forest edges and rocky habitats.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a notable species in native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
