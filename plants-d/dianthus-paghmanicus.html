Dianthus paghmanicus

Dianthus paghmanicus, commonly known as the Pamir carnation, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, adapting to harsh climatic conditions. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces small, clustered flowers that attract pollinators.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
