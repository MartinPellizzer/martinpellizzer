Demeusea longifolia

Demeusea longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved demeusea, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often climbing stems and elongated, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
