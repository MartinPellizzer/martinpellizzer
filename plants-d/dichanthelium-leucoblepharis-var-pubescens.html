Dichanthelium leucoblepharis var. pubescens

Dichanthelium leucoblepharis var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy leucoblepharis grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, slender blades with a dense covering of hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in open woodlands and prairies. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets.

This variety is often found in disturbed areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
