Dendrobium speciosum subsp. curvicaule

Dendrobium speciosum subsp.

curvicaule, commonly known as the curving orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia. It features long, wiry stems that can grow up to several meters in length, often adorned with fleshy, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, arranged in racemes along the stems. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright indirect light and requires well-draining potting media.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its vibrant, long-lasting blooms.
