Diplotaxis muralis var. minor

Diplotaxis muralis var. minor, commonly known as the lesser wallflower, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring and summer months. The plant has wiry, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found growing on rocky or sandy soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
