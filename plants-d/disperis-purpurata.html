Disperis purpurata

Disperis purpurata, commonly known as the purple orchid, is a species of orchid native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking violet and white flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant has a compact, pseudobulbous structure with broad, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Disperis purpurata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
