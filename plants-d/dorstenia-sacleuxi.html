Dorstenia sacleuxi

Dorstenia sacleuxi, commonly known as the "hairy leaf" or "dwarf false arrowhead," is a unique member of the Moraceae family.

It is native to Madagascar and is characterized by its distinctive, hairy, and often lobed leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in moist forest understories.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered an endangered species.
