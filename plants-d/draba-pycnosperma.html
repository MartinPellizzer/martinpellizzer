Draba pycnosperma

Draba pycnosperma, commonly known as the dense-fruited whitlow grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its low-growing habit and small, basal leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pale yellow flowers in early spring. Its fruits are small, capsule-like structures that contain numerous seeds.

Draba pycnosperma is typically found in alpine and arctic regions, thriving in well-drained, rocky soils.
