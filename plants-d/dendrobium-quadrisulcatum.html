Dendrobium quadrisulcatum

Dendrobium quadrisulcatum, commonly known as the four-groove dendrobium, is an epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, cane-like pseudobulbs and long, linear leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and white, typically during the rainy season. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Dendrobium quadrisulcatum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for horticultural purposes.
