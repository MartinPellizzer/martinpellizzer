Dendrobium jocosum

Dendrobium jocosum, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is an epiphytic plant, typically growing on trees or rocks in tropical rainforests. The plant produces showy, golden-yellow flowers that bloom during the summer months. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

Dendrobium jocosum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for horticultural purposes.
