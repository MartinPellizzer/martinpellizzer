Daucus caucalis

Daucus caucalis, commonly known as the Caucasian carrot, is a biennial plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to theApiaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like carrots and parsley. The plant features a rosette of finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces small, yellowish-white flowers in umbels. Its taproot is typically small and not as edible as that of the common carrot.

The species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some medicinal uses in traditional herbal remedies.
