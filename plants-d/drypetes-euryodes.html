Drypetes euryodes

Drypetes euryodes, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and yellowish flowers. The tree produces a dark, aromatic wood that is highly valued for its durability and aesthetic appeal. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.
