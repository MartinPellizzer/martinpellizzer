Dichapetalum patentihirsutum

Dichapetalum patentihirsutum, commonly known as the woolly mallow, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy or rocky soils. It has a sprawling habit and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
