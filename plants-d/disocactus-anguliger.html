Disocactus anguliger

Disocactus anguliger, commonly known as the angular disocactus, is a member of the Cactaceae family native to Mexico.

It is a climbing cactus with thick, angular stems that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant features areoles, which are specialized stem structures from which spines, flowers, and branches emerge. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, blooming in summer, and it thrives in bright, indirect light with well-draining soil.

Disocactus anguliger is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and ability to climb trellises or other supports.
