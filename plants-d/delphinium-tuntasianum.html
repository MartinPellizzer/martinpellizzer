Delphinium tuntasianum

Delphinium tuntasianum, commonly known as the Tuntasian larkspur, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that bear clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are palmately compound, with lobes that are often serrated, and its flowers are showy, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
