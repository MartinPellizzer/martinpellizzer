Diospyros flavocarpa

Diospyros flavocarpa, commonly known as the yellow-fruited ebony, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its dark, dense wood and small, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by edible, yellow-orange fruits. Its fruit is typically eaten fresh or used in traditional medicine.

Diospyros flavocarpa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitats.
