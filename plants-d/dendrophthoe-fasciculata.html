Dendrophthoe fasciculata

Dendrophthoe fasciculata, commonly known as the parasitic mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches itself to the branches of host trees, primarily in the genus Ficus.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while still performing some photosynthesis using its small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers and red berries that are often consumed by birds, aiding in seed dispersal. It is found in tropical and subtropical regions, including parts of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

Its unique parasitic lifestyle and ecological role make it an interesting subject in botanical studies.
