Diospyros rubra

Diospyros rubra, commonly known as the red persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and broad, palmate leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into orange-red, fleshy fruits. The fruit is typically harvested when fully ripe, revealing a sweet, edible pulp with astringent seeds.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and the nutritional value of its fruit.
