Dipsacus fulingensis

Dipsacus fulingensis, commonly known as the Chinese teasel, is a flowering plant native to China.

It is a biennial or short-lived perennial herb that grows up to 1.5 meters tall, characterized by its spiny, leaf-like bracts. The plant produces small, pale yellow to greenish flowers in clusters during the summer months. Its roots are often used in traditional Chinese medicine for their purported medicinal properties.

The plant is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental spikes and ecological benefits.
