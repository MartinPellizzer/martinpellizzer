Diplusodon myrsinites var. cribellinus

Diplusodon myrsinites var. cribellinus, commonly known as the hairy hakea, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and produces small, white or pink flowers. The plant has a woody stem with a rough, fibrous texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

The variety *cribellinus* is distinguished by its unique leaf structure and floral arrangement.
