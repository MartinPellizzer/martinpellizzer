Dianthus ruscinonensis

Dianthus ruscinonensis, commonly known as the Ruscinonensis pink, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, clustered blooms that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, while its flowers consist of five petals with a distinctive ruffled edge.

Dianthus ruscinonensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, long-lasting flowers.
