Dianthus diffusus

Dianthus diffusus, commonly known as spreading campion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial with spreading stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense mats. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of pink, white, and red, typically blooming from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are oval and slightly hairy, with a distinctive blue-green color.

Dianthus diffusus is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of conditions.
