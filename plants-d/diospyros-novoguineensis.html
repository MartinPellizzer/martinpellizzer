Diospyros novoguineensis

Diospyros novoguineensis, commonly known as the New Guinea persimmon, is a tropical tree native to the rainforests of New Guinea.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by orange to red, fleshy fruits with a sweet, astringent flavor.

It is valued for its ornamental value and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
