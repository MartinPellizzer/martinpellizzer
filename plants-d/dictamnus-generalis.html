Dictamnus generalis

Dictamnus generalis, commonly known as the common dictamnus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It features aromatic, reddish-brown stems that exude a strong, spicy scent when broken. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically ranging from pink to white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Dictamnus generalis is often cultivated in gardens for its unique fragrance and ornamental value.
