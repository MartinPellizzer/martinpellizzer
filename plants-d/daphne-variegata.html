Daphne variegata

Daphne variegata, commonly known as the variegated daphne, is a deciduous shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its attractive, aromatic leaves that display a variegated pattern of white, green, and yellow. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, white, and purple during late winter to early spring. Its foliage remains evergreen throughout the year, adding visual interest to gardens.

Daphne variegata is relatively low-maintenance but requires well-drained soil and protection from frost.
