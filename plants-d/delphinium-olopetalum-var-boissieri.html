Delphinium olopetalum var. boissieri

Delphinium olopetalum var. boissieri, commonly known as the Boissier's larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features tall, slender stems that bear clusters of striking blue to purple flowers, which are typically arranged in dense spikes. The leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or alpine settings.

This variety is valued for its vibrant blooms and is often cultivated for both its beauty and ecological benefits.
