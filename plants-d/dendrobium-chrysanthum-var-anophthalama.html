Dendrobium chrysanthum var. anophthalama

Dendrobium chrysanthum var. anophthalama, commonly known as the white-lipped dendrobium, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with cane-like stems that often produce multiple flower spikes. The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped, dark green leaves and produces fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers with a distinctive lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.

This variety is appreciated for its ornamental value and is commonly cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions.
