Drymaria cordata var. indica

Drymaria cordata var. indica, commonly known as Indian basketgrass, is a perennial flowering plant native to South Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and dense clusters of small, white flowers. The plant features hairy, linear leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil make it a useful plant in ecological restoration projects.
