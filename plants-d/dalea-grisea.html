Dalea grisea

Dalea grisea, commonly known as gray milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its gray-green foliage and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as grasslands and sagebrush steppe. It has a shallow root system and is adapted to arid environments.

Dalea grisea plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
