Dicerolepis hypoleuca

Dicerolepis hypoleuca, commonly known as the white-tailed dandelion, is a flowering plant native to parts of North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant produces a distinctive white to pale yellow flower with a central disc surrounded by ray florets. Its leaves are typically basal, forming a rosette and are covered in fine hairs.

Dicerolepis hypoleuca is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as roadsides and fields.
