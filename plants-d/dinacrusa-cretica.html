Dinacrusa cretica

Dinacrusa cretica, commonly known as the Cretan thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, small, gray-green leaves and clusters of pink or purple flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes. Its essential oils, rich in thymol, contribute to its use in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb.

Dinacrusa cretica is also valued for its role in supporting pollinators and its resilience to drought conditions.
