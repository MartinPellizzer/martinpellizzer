Draba douglasii var. crockeri

Draba douglasii var. crockeri, commonly known as Crocker's whitlow grass, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other mustard plants. This variant is characterized by its small, white flowers and basal leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils in alpine or subalpine regions.

The plant is well adapted to harsh environmental conditions, showcasing its resilience in high-altitude habitats.
