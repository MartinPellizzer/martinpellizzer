Diasperus pycnocarpus

Diasperus pycnocarpus, commonly known as the dense-fruited grass, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the grass family, Poaceae, and is characterized by its compact, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, dense clusters of spikelets that are typically green or reddish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and rocky areas.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ornamental landscaping.
