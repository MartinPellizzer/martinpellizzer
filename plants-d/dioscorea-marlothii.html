Dioscorea marlothii

Dioscorea marlothii, commonly known as Marloth's yam, is a perennial climbing vine native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by yellowish fruits that contain kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in grassland and woodland habitats, preferring well-drained soils.

Due to its medicinal properties and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated for both practical and aesthetic purposes.
