Delphinium sergei

Delphinium sergei, commonly known as the Siberian larkspur, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the flowers are showy, attracting various pollinators.

Delphinium sergei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
