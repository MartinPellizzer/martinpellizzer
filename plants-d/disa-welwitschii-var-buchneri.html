Disa welwitschii var. buchneri

Disa welwitschii var. buchneri, commonly known as the Cape orchid, is a striking member of the orchid family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically blooms during the summer months, producing showy inflorescences that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
