Dioscorea matagalpensis

Dioscorea matagalpensis, commonly known as the Matagalpa yam, is a climbing vine native to Central America.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary edible part. The plant produces small, green flowers followed by yellowish fruits that contain numerous seeds. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are hairy and twining.

This species is valued for its nutritional content and is traditionally used in local cuisines.
