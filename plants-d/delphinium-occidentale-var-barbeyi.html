Delphinium occidentale var. barbeyi

Delphinium occidentale var. barbeyi, commonly known as the Western larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July. The plant has a basal rosette of leaves and tall, sparsely branched stems that support its showy inflorescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in meadows and open woodlands.

Botanically, it is characterized by its spurred petals and is part of the Ranunculaceae family.
