Dialium tessmannii

Dialium tessmannii, commonly known as the African cherry or African satinwood, is a tropical tree native to Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The tree produces small, round fruits that are yellow when ripe, containing several seeds. Its wood is valued for its durability and fine grain, making it useful in construction and furniture-making.
