Diplachne pallida

Diplachne pallida, commonly known as slender flatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets, which are arranged in dense clusters along the stem. Diplachne pallida thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed areas, grasslands, and along roadsides.

Its rapid growth and ability to form dense stands make it both a weed and a potential soil stabilizer in certain environments.
