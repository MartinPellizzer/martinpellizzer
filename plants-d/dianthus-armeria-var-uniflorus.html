Dianthus armeria var. uniflorus

Dianthus armeria var. uniflorus, commonly known as the sea pink, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of pink or white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces small, rounded leaves that are covered in fine hairs, aiding in moisture retention. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and full sun, making it a hardy species suitable for coastal gardens.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
