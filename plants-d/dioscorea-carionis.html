Dioscorea carionis

Dioscorea carionis, commonly known as the common yam, is a perennial vine native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and tuberous roots. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by yellowish fruits containing numerous seeds. Its tubers are widely used as a starchy food source in many parts of the world.

Dioscorea carionis is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
