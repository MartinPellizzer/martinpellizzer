Draba siliquosa var. subglabra

Draba siliquosa var. subglabra, commonly known as the smooth-flowered whitlow grass, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically has basal leaves that are linear to oblanceolate, with a smooth texture distinguishing it from other varieties. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, appearing in clusters during the spring.

Draba siliquosa var. subglabra is adapted to rocky and alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun.
