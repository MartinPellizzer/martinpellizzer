Dictamnus albus var. bucharicus

Dictamnus albus var. bucharicus, commonly known as the White Dictamnus, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has aromatic, reddish-brown bark that exudes a strong, spicy scent when broken. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly serrated margin.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and distinctive fragrance.
