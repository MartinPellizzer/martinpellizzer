Diospyros nutans

Diospyros nutans, commonly known as the Indian persimmon, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by small, round fruits that turn from green to red or orange when mature.

The fruit is edible when fully ripe and has a sweet, slightly astringent flavor, making it a valued species in traditional medicine and local cuisines.
