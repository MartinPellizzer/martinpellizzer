Disperis stenoglossa

Disperis stenoglossa, commonly known as the narrow-lipped orchid, is a species of orchid native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-white flowers with narrow, hooded lips. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy habitats and prefers well-drained soils. It blooms during the summer months, producing delicate flowers that are often pollinated by insects.

Disperis stenoglossa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
