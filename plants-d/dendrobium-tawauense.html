Dendrobium tawauense

Dendrobium tawauense, commonly known as the Tawau orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Borneo.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and clusters of fragrant, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in lowland forests. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the forest floor.

Dendrobium tawauense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
