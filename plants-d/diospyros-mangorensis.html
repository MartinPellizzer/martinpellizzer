Diospyros mangorensis

Diospyros mangorensis, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and simple, alternate leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into orange to red, fleshy fruits. The fruits are edible, with a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor, and are often consumed fresh or used in traditional recipes.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is valued for its ornamental and culinary significance.
