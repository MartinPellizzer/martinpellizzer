Digitalis purpurea var. alpina

Digitalis purpurea var. alpina, commonly known as the Alpine foxglove, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its tall spikes of tubular, violet to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained, alkaline soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is often cultivated in gardens and used in traditional herbal medicine.
