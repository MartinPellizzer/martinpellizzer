Desmodium ellipticum

Desmodium ellipticum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf burr legume, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Americas.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, each consisting of three elliptical leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in disturbed soils and open habitats.

Desmodium ellipticum is also known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
