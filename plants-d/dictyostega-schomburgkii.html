Dictyostega schomburgkii

Dictyostega schomburgkii, commonly known as the Schomburgk's ghost orchid, is a rare and striking orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, elongated flowers that resemble ghostly apparitions, hence its common name. The plant has a unique growth habit, with its roots forming a network that allows it to absorb nutrients from the surrounding soil. Its flowers typically bloom in a striking white or pale pink color, and they emit a faint fragrance to attract pollinators.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a conservation concern in its native range.
