Dianthus sylvaticus proles collinus

Dianthus sylvaticus proles collinus, commonly known as the woodland carnation, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink or white flowers with frilled petals. The plant typically grows in woodland edges and meadows, preferring well-drained soils and partial shade. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it blooms from late spring to early summer.

Dianthus sylvaticus proles collinus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
