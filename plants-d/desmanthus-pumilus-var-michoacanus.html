Desmanthus pumilus var. michoacanus

Desmanthus pumilus var. michoacanus, commonly known as the Mexican burr legume, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive, spiny pods that contain small, bean-like seeds. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, arid environments. It is often found in disturbed areas and is used for erosion control due to its vigorous growth.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
