Dacrycarpus papuana

Dacrycarpus papuana, commonly known as the Papuan silver pine, is a coniferous tree native to the tropical regions of Papua and New Guinea.

It belongs to the Podocarpaceae family and is characterized by its slender, evergreen foliage and long, narrow cones. The plant typically grows in humid, lowland to montane forests, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are needle-like and arranged spirally along the stems, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
