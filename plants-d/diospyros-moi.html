Diospyros moi

Diospyros moi, commonly known as the Chinese date tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and alternate, simple leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into edible, fleshy fruits resembling dates. The fruit contains a single seed and is valued for its sweet, chewy texture and nutritional content.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
