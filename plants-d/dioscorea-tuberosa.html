Dioscorea tuberosa

Dioscorea tuberosa, commonly known as wild yam, is a perennial vine native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by cylindrical, tuberous roots. These roots are the primary part used for medicinal and culinary purposes.

Dioscorea tuberosa is valued for its chemical compounds, including steroidal saponins, which have pharmaceutical significance.
