Delphinium blaisdellii

Delphinium blaisdellii, commonly known as Blaisdell's larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, spindly stems that bear clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun. Its foliage consists of deeply lobed leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating.

Blaisdell's larkspur is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
