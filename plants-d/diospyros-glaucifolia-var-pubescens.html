Diospyros glaucifolia var. pubescens

Diospyros glaucifolia var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy persimmon, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree with elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine, hairy pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by orange to red, fleshy berries. Its leaves are typically alternate and have a glossy upper surface with a pale underside.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruits.
