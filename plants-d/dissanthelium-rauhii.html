Dissanthelium rauhii

Dissanthelium rauhii, commonly known as Rauh's false panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, two-flowered spikelets. It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes and pond edges.

Botanically, it is notable for its unique floral structure and ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
