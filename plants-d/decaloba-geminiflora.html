Decaloba geminiflora

Decaloba geminiflora, commonly known as the twin-flowered tree, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, opposite arrangement of large, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in pairs, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
