Delphinium rechingerorum

Delphinium rechingerorum, commonly known as the Rechinger's larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers in late summer, arranged in racemes at the top of the stem. Its leaves are compound, with elongated, serrated leaflets, and it thrives in well-drained, alkaline soils.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
