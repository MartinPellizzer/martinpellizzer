Dalea vulneraria var. typica

Dalea vulneraria var. typica, commonly known as the scarlet prairie clover, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits. The plant produces vibrant pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Its ecological importance includes providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a host plant for certain butterfly species.
