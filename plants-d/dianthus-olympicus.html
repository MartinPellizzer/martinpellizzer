Dianthus olympicus

Dianthus olympicus, commonly known as the Olympic campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Greece.

It is characterized by its vibrant pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and has narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species suitable for rocky or alpine environments.

The flowers are often used in ornamental gardening due to their striking color and resilience.
