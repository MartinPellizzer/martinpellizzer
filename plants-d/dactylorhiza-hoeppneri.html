Dactylorhiza hoeppneri

Dactylorhiza hoeppneri, commonly known as the narrow-leaved marsh orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in wet or damp habitats such as marshes, bogs, and stream banks. The plant features a slender, erect stem with few to several leaves and produces small, greenish or yellowish flowers. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its pseudobulbs and fibrous root system.

Dactylorhiza hoeppneri is often found in calcareous soils and is known for its ability to thrive in moist, nutrient-rich environments.
