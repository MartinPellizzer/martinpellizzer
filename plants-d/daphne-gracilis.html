Daphne gracilis

Daphne gracilis, commonly known as slender daphne, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features small, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring, typically in shades of white or pink. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy, providing a striking contrast to its delicate blossoms. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Daphne gracilis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
