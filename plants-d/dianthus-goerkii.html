Dianthus goerkii

Dianthus goerkii, commonly known as the Sierra dianthus, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, such as mountain slopes and meadows. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and a compact growth habit.

Dianthus goerkii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and resilience.
