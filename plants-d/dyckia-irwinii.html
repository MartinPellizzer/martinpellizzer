Dyckia irwinii

Dyckia irwinii, commonly known as the Irwin's Dyckia, is a species of flowering plant in the bromeliad family.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of white woolly hairs. The plant produces striking clusters of red, violet, or orange flowers during the flowering season. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated for its unique appearance and ease of care.
