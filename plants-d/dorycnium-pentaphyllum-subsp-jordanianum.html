Dorycnium pentaphyllum subsp. jordanianum

Dorycnium pentaphyllum subsp.

jordanianum, commonly known as Jordanian goat's beard, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Middle East. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and compound leaves with a distinctive fern-like appearance.

The plant produces yellow flowers in racemes, which bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species within its range.
