Deschampsia latifolia

Deschampsia latifolia, commonly known as broadleaf feathergrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and fluffy, silver-gray plumes that give it a distinctive appearance. This plant typically grows in open, disturbed areas such as roadsides, meadows, and sandy soils. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a hardy species in various environmental conditions.

The leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue that adds visual interest to the landscape.
