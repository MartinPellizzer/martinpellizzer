Disocactus lepidocarpus

Disocactus lepidocarpus, commonly known as the "Coral Cactus," is a flowering cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stem covered in soft, white spines and clusters of bright orange or red flowers. The plant thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun to partial shade. It is drought-tolerant and prefers warm, dry climates.

Its unique appearance and vibrant blooms make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
