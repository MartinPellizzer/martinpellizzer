Dianthus sardous

Dianthus sardous, commonly known as the Sardinian campion, is a flowering plant native to the island of Sardinia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink or red flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are linear and slightly hairy, while its stems are erect and branching.

Dianthus sardous is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
