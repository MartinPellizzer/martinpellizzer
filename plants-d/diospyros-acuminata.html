Diospyros acuminata

Diospyros acuminata, commonly known as the African date palm, is a small to medium-sized tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish-brown bark and long, pinnate leaves that can reach up to 2 meters in length. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in panicles, followed by edible drupes that are often consumed raw or processed into beverages. Its fruit has a sweet, honey-like flavor and is an important food source in many regions.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has cultural significance in several African communities.
