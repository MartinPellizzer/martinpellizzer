Duckea cyperaceoidea

Duckea cyperaceoidea, commonly known as the slender sedges, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Cyperaceae.

These plants are typically found in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. They are characterized by their grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and spike-like inflorescences. Duckea species often have small, inconspicuous flowers that are adapted for wind pollination.

The genus includes several species that are important in ecological systems for stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
