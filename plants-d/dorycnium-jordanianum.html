Dorycnium jordanianum

Dorycnium jordanianum, commonly known as Jordanian burclover, is a perennial herb native to the Middle East and parts of the Mediterranean.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It produces small, hairy pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Dorycnium jordanianum is often found in rocky areas and along the edges of deserts.
