Drosera belezeana

Drosera belezeana, commonly known as the Cape Peninsula Sundew, is a carnivorous plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its sticky, glandular hairs that trap insects, aiding in its nutrient acquisition. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and is often found in coastal areas. It produces small, white flowers that bloom in summer, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Due to its unique adaptations, it is a popular specimen among carnivorous plant enthusiasts.
