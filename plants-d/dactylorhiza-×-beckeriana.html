Dactylorhiza × beckeriana

Dactylorhiza × beckeriana, commonly known as the hybrid marsh orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of pale green to yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic environments such as bogs and wet meadows. It has a rhizomatous root system and produces showy, lanceolate leaves.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens with proper moisture and light conditions.
