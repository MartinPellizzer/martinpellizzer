Dianthus turolensis

Dianthus turolensis, commonly known as the Turol's campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pinkish-red flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Dianthus turolensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
