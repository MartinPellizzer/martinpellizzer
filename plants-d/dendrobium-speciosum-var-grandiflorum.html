Dendrobium speciosum var. grandiflorum

Dendrobium speciosum var. grandiflorum, commonly known as the grandiflorum orchid, is a sympodial epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features long, cane-like pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, supporting its growth in humid, tropical environments. The plant produces clusters of showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and yellow, typically blooming during the summer months. Its leaves are strap-shaped and arranged in a spiral pattern around the pseudobulbs.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its vibrant and long-lasting floral display.
