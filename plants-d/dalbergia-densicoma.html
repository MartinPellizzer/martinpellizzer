Dalbergia densicoma

Dalbergia densicoma, commonly known as the African rosewood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its dense, dark wood that is highly valued for its aesthetic and structural properties. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, yellowish flowers followed by elongated pods.

It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.
