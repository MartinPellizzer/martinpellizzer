Dichaeta

Dichaeta, commonly known as the common dichaeta, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Apiaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, greenish-white flowers that appear in umbels. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate environments and is often found in meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are finely divided and aromatic, emitting a mild scent when crushed.

Dichaeta is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal remedies for its purported medicinal properties.
