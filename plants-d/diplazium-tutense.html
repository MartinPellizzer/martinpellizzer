Diplazium tutense

Diplazium tutense, commonly known as the Tutu fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its bipinnate fronds that are often divided into narrow, elongated segments. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its rhizomes are creeping and produce both sterile and fertile fronds.

Diplazium tutense is valued for its ornamental appearance and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
