Dicoria canescens subsp. canescens

Dicoria canescens subsp.

canescens, commonly known as the woolly dicoria, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa. It is characterized by its hairy, grayish-green foliage and clusters of small, pale yellow flowers.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to hot, dry climates. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
