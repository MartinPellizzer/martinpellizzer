Desmodium jaliscanum

Desmodium jaliscanum, commonly known as Mexican burr legume, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes known for their nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes. Its pods are typically hairy and contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

It thrives in dry, disturbed habitats and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.
