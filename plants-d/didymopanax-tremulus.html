Didymopanax tremulus

Didymopanax tremulus, commonly known as the trembling tree, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in diameter, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers followed by edible, orange-colored fruits. Its bark contains compounds that have been traditionally used in folk medicine.

The species is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
