Drepanostachyum khasianum

Drepanostachyum khasianum, commonly known as the Khasi bamboo, is a species of grass native to the northeastern regions of India.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions. It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, contributing to soil stabilization and providing habitat for various organisms.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat preferences, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
