Daviesia filipes subsp. filipes

Daviesia filipes subsp.

filipes, commonly known as the narrow-leafed hop萧, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia. It is characterized by its slender, ascending stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often hairy.

The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers during late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and open forests.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
