Dendrobium amabile

Dendrobium amabile, commonly known as the fragrant orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with multiple stems that can reach up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and yellow, typically during the summer months. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Dendrobium amabile thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
