Dipera

Dipera, commonly known as the "bluebell" or "lily of the valley," is a genus of flowering plants in the family Liliaceae.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to blue or purple. The plants typically have narrow, grass-like leaves and are often found in woodland or shaded areas. Dipera species are perennial herbs that thrive in moist, well-drained soils.
