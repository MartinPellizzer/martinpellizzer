Disperis cooperi

Disperis cooperi, commonly known as the Cooper's orchid, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in semi-arid environments. Its blooms are usually pale green or white, with a distinctive lip that aids in pollination.
