Dendrochilum megalanthum

Dendrochilum megalanthum, commonly known as the giant orchid, is a striking epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 10 centimeters in diameter, often displaying vibrant colors such as yellow and orange. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, using its aerial roots to absorb moisture and nutrients from the environment. Its inflorescence is a raceme of fragrant, spurred flowers that bloom during the wet season.

Dendrochilum megalanthum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical greenhouses.
