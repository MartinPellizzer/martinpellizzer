Dactylorhiza incarnata var. reichenbachii

Dactylorhiza incarnata var. reichenbachii, commonly known as the Reichenbach's marsh orchid, is a species of orchid native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a terrestrial herb with a rhizomatous root system and produces a single, erect stem bearing several pale green to white flowers. The plant thrives in wet or marshy habitats, often found in peat bogs and along riverbanks. Its flowers are characterized by their narrow, linear petals and a distinctive lip that is often darker in color.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with moist, acidic soil.
