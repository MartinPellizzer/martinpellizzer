Dactylorhiza longifolia

Dactylorhiza longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and linear, grass-like leaves. The plant produces showy, violet or purple flowers in late summer, arranged in a loose spike. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland or heathland habitats.

Dactylorhiza longifolia is a perennial herb that plays a role in supporting local pollinators and biodiversity.
