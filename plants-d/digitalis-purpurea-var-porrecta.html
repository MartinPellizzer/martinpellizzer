Digitalis purpurea var. porrecta

Digitalis purpurea var. porrecta, commonly known as the tall foxglove, is a perennial plant native to Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces striking bell-shaped flowers in shades of purple and pink, typically blooming from June to August. Its leaves are velvety and heart-shaped, with a distinctive ribbed texture.

This variety is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and historical use in medicine.
