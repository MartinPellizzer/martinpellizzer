Daviesia incrassata var. benthamii

Daviesia incrassata var. benthamii, commonly known as the Broad-leaved Daviesia, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, woody growth and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces striking clusters of pink to red flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is typically found in heathlands and open forests.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
