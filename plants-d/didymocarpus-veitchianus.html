Didymocarpus veitchianus

Didymocarpus veitchianus, commonly known as the veitch's tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The fruit is a woody pod that splits open to release seeds, and the tree can grow up to 20 meters tall.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been introduced to other tropical regions for horticultural purposes.
