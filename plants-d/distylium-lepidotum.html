Distylium lepidotum

Distylium lepidotum, commonly known as the glossy bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has smooth, glossy leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It typically grows in moist, woodlands and thrives in partial to full shade.

Distylium lepidotum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to local pollinators.
