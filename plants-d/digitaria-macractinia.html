Digitaria macractinia

Digitaria macractinia, commonly known as giant fingergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces panicles of small, spike-like flowers, which are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in warm, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed soils or open fields.

Digitaria macractinia is adapted to arid conditions and can tolerate poor soil quality.
