Drypetes deplanchei subsp. affinis

Drypetes deplanchei subsp.

affinis, commonly known as the Indian flame tree, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves and showy, orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant produces small, woody fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical regions.
