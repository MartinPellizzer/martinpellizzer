Dactylis glomerata var. petrakii

Dactylis glomerata var. petrakii, commonly known as the Greek oatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant is adapted to dry, rocky soils and thrives in Mediterranean climates. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.
