Diplusodon rupestris

Diplusodon rupestris, commonly known as the rock dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant features a basal rosette of fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow and borne on long, slender stems, often appearing in spring.

Diplusodon rupestris is adapted to arid conditions and is often found in rocky outcrops and along coastal areas.
