Dalea capitata var. capitata

Dalea capitata var. capitata, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the central and eastern United States.

It features upright, branching stems covered in narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy. The plant produces showy, cone-shaped flower heads that are usually pink to purple in color, with yellow central disks. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

Dalea capitata var. capitata is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
