Dryobalanops keithii

Dryobalanops keithii, commonly known as the Malayan ironwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for construction and furniture. The plant typically grows in lowland rainforests and has compound leaves with opposite arrangement. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds.

Due to its ecological and economic importance, it is also considered a threatened species in its natural habitat.
