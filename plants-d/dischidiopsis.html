Dischidiopsis

Dischidiopsis, commonly known as the arrowhead plant, is a genus of succulent plants native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are often glossy and have a waxy texture. The leaves are typically arrowhead or heart-shaped, which gives the plant its common name. Dischidiopsis species are drought-tolerant and prefer well-draining soil, making them suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

They produce small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange, adding ornamental value to gardens.
