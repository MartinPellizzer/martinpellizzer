Dimorphanthera myzomelae

Dimorphanthera myzomelae, commonly known as the golden rain tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The fruit is a pod that splits open to release seeds, contributing to its propagation in natural habitats.
