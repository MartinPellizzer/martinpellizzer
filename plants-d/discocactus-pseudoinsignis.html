Discocactus pseudoinsignis

Discocactus pseudoinsignis, commonly known as the Mexican moon cactus, is a unique cactus species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and a striking, often colorful crown that sits atop a green, fleshy stem. The plant is a hybrid between the genus Discocactus and the genus Hylocereus, which explains its distinctive appearance. Its fleshy stems are adapted for storing water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments.

The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white, pink, or red, adding to its ornamental appeal.
