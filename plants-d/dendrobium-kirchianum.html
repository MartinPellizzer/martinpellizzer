Dendrobium kirchianum

Dendrobium kirchianum, commonly known as the Kirch's orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, cane-like stems that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white, yellow, and pink, typically during the warm season. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and surrounding substrates.

Dendrobium kirchianum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and relatively easy care requirements.
