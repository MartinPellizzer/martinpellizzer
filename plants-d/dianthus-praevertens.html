Dianthus praevertens

Dianthus praevertens, commonly known as the early dianthus, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit. The plant produces showy, pinkish-red flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from April to May. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are densely packed along the stem.

Dianthus praevertens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, early-season blooms.
