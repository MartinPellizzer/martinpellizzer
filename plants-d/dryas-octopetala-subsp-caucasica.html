Dryas octopetala subsp. caucasica

Dryas octopetala subsp.

caucasica, commonly known as Caucasian dryas, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Caucasus region. It features dense, mat-forming foliage composed of small, rounded, leathery leaves that are typically gray-green in color.

The plant produces clusters of white, five-petaled flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
