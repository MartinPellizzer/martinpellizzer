Desmodium rhytidophyllum subsp. acutifoliolum

Desmodium rhytidophyllum subsp.

acutifoliolum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved desmodium, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Africa. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged in a fan-like pattern.

The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland or woodland habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
