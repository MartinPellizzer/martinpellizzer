Draba magellanica var. citrina

Draba magellanica var. citrina, commonly known as the yellow draba, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in the Magellanic region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants, and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. It has a low, mat-forming growth habit with numerous hairy stems and small, elongated leaves.

The yellow flowers bloom in early spring, adding a splash of color to its natural habitat.
