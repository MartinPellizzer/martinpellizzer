Dischidia horsfieldiana

Dischidia horsfieldiana, commonly known as the Horsfield's frog vine, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and tendrils that help it climb. The plant produces small, white, tubular flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences. Its leaves are thick and fleshy, adapted to store water, making it drought-tolerant.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
