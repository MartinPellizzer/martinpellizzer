Dilivaria ilicifolia

Dilivaria ilicifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved tea tree, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage with broad, leathery leaves that are often aromatic. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule containing several seeds.

Dilivaria ilicifolia is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
