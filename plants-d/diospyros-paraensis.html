Diospyros paraensis

Diospyros paraensis, commonly known as the Brazilian persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes ebony and persimmon species. The tree produces small, yellowish-green fruits that ripen to a reddish-orange color, often with a sweet, astringent flavor. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and ovate with a smooth margin.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal uses.
