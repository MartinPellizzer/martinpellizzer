Dialypetalum floribundum var. naniflorum

Dialypetalum floribundum var. naniflorum, commonly known as the dwarf-flowered dialypetalum, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in spring. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptical in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface depending on the variety. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it suitable for shaded garden environments.

Its ornamental value lies in its delicate flowers and manageable size, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.
