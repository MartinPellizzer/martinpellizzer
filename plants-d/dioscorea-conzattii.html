Dioscorea conzattii

Dioscorea conzattii, commonly known as the Yunnan yam, is a climbing vine native to China.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary part used for medicinal and culinary purposes. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers and has compound leaves with three to five leaflets. Its tubers are rich in starch and have been traditionally used in Chinese medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in mountainous regions.
