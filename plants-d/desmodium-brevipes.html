Desmodium brevipes

Desmodium brevipes, commonly known as the short-stemmed burr legume, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, clustered leaves and distinctive, spiny pods. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields. Its small, white or pale purple flowers are followed by hairy, burr-like seed pods that aid in seed dispersal.

Desmodium brevipes is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable in ecological restoration efforts.
