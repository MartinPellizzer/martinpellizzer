Deyeuxia hackelii

Deyeuxia hackelii, commonly known as the Hackel's sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, triangular stems and small, inconspicuous flowers that typically bloom in late summer. Native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia, it thrives in sandy or gravelly soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spikelets that are often clustered at the top of the stems.

It is often found in disturbed habitats and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
