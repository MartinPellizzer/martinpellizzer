Desmotrichum fimbriatum

Desmotrichum fimbriatum, commonly known as the fringed sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub with small, fleshy leaves that help it conserve water in dry environments. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access moisture from deeper soil layers.

Desmotrichum fimbriatum is adapted to withstand high temperatures and low rainfall, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
