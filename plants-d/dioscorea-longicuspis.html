Dioscorea longicuspis

Dioscorea longicuspis, commonly known as the long-spined yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with prominent veins, and the stems are covered in short, stiff hairs.

Dioscorea longicuspis is valued for its medicinal properties and is also cultivated for its starchy tubers.
