Dasillipe pasquieri

Dasillipe pasquieri, commonly known as the Pasquier's ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic orchid species native to the high-altitude cloud forests of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, pale purple floral structure and delicate, strap-like leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring, attracting specific pollinators such as certain species of bees and moths. Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is highly sensitive to environmental changes.

Conservation efforts are critical to preserving this unique botanical specimen.
