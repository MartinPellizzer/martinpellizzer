Dahlia coccinea var. steyermarkii

Dahlia coccinea var. steyermarkii, commonly known as the Steyermark's red dahlia, is a cultivar native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its vibrant red flowers with a velvety texture and a prominent central disk. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 3 feet. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems.

This variety is prized for its showy blooms and is often cultivated in gardens for ornamental purposes.
