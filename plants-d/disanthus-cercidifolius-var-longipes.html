Disanthus cercidifolius var. longipes

Disanthus cercidifolius var. longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed maple, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its delicate, pendulous flowers that appear in clusters, typically in late spring. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a reddish tinge when young, turning green as they mature. The plant produces small, winged seeds that are dispersed by wind.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its unique floral display.
