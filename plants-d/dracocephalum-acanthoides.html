Dracocephalum acanthoides

Dracocephalum acanthoides, commonly known as narrowleaf blueweed, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, blue to purple flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant adaptations. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs.

Dracocephalum acanthoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
