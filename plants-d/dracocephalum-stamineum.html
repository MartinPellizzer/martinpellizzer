Dracocephalum stamineum

Dracocephalum stamineum, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, lavender-blue flower clusters that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as butterflies and bees. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Dracocephalum stamineum is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to support local wildlife.
