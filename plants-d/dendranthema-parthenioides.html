Dendranthema parthenioides

Dendranthema parthenioides, commonly known as the oxeye daisy, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with white petals surrounding a yellow center, typically blooming in late summer to early autumn. The plant is herbaceous, with a low-growing habit and hairy stems that often form a rosette at the base. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a common sight in meadows and grasslands.

Dendranthema parthenioides is also known for its historical use in herbal medicine.
