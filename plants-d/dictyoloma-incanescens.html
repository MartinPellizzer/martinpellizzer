Dictyoloma incanescens

Dictyoloma incanescens, commonly known as the white-velvet acanthus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Acanthaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its distinctive, velvety leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
