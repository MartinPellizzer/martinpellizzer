Deutzia discolor var. major

Deutzia discolor var. major, commonly known as the white deutzia, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of white, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has slender, arching stems and dark green, serrated leaves that turn yellow in autumn. It typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its showy floral display.
