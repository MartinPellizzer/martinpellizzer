Didymoglossum punctatum subsp. sphenoides

Didymoglossum punctatum subsp.

sphenoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hairgrass, is a low-growing perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Poaceae family. It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It typically thrives in moist, shaded environments such as wet meadows and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and habitat conservation.
