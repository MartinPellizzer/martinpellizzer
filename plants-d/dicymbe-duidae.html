Dicymbe duidae

Dicymbe duidae, commonly known as the Duidae doryphore, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. It is often found in open woodlands and grasslands, thriving in well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in horticultural settings.
