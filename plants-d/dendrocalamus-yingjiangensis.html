Dendrocalamus yingjiangensis

Dendrocalamus yingjiangensis, commonly known as the Yingjiang bamboo, is a species of giant bamboo native to China.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 20 meters. The plant has a dense, fibrous structure, making it valuable for construction and papermaking. Its leaves are long and narrow, often with a smooth, glossy surface.

This species is also noted for its ecological importance in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
