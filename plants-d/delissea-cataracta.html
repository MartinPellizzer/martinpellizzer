Delissea cataracta

Delissea cataracta, commonly known as the waterfall plant, is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, often with contrasting white or yellow markings. The plant typically grows along streams or in moist, shaded areas, where it thrives in humid conditions. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Delissea cataracta is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well-suited for hanging baskets or as a container plant.
