Dipteranthus peruvianus

Dipteranthus peruvianus, commonly known as the Peruvian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It features striking blue to violet bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture and grows in rocky, well-drained soils. It is often found in high-altitude environments, thriving in cool and moist conditions.

Dipteranthus peruvianus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
