Dracaena acaulis

Dracaena acaulis, commonly known as the tree dracaena, is a member of the Asparagaceae family and is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is a perennial evergreen plant characterized by its upright, columnar growth habit and smooth, dark green leaves that are often glossy and lance-shaped. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically followed by red or orange berries, which are toxic to humans and animals.

Dracaena acaulis thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate to bright indirect light, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
