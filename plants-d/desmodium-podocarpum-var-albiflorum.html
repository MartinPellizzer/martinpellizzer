Desmodium podocarpum var. albiflorum

Desmodium podocarpum var. albiflorum, commonly known as the white-flowered desmodium, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is native to parts of Africa and is often found in grassy areas and disturbed soils. It has a trailing growth habit and produces small, rounded pods that contain several seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in ecological and agricultural contexts.
