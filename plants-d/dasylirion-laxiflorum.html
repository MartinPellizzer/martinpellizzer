Dasylirion laxiflorum

Dasylirion laxiflorum, commonly known as the Apache pine or desert spoon, is a striking evergreen perennial plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features tall, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color and can grow up to several feet in length. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange during the spring. It is well adapted to arid environments, requiring minimal water and thriving in full sun.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and ornamental gardening.
