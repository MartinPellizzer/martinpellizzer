Desmodium stipulaceum var. macrophylla

Desmodium stipulaceum var. macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaved burr medic, is a perennial legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves with large, heart-shaped leaflets and distinctive, hairy pods that contain several seeds. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas, such as roadsides and pastures. Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it valuable for soil enrichment and forage production.

The species is also noted for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and unique seed pods.
