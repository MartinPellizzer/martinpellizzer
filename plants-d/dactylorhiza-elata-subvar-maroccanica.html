Dactylorhiza elata subvar. maroccanica

Dactylorhiza elata subvar. maroccanica, commonly known as the Moroccan marsh orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to North Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in wet, marshy habitats and has lance-shaped leaves. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in moist, nutrient-rich soils.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
