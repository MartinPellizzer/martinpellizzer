Delphinium virgatum

Delphinium virgatum, commonly known as the blue larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, spindly stems and clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun. Its showy blooms attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.

Delphinium virgatum is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
