Dieffenbachia poeppigii

Dieffenbachia poeppigii, commonly known as the Snowplant, is a striking tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that are often adorned with striking white and green variegation. The plant belongs to the Araceae family, which is known for its unique inflorescence structure called a spathe. Its foliage is not only ornamental but also contains calcium oxalate crystals, which can be irritating if ingested.

Dieffenbachia poeppigii thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture to maintain its vibrant appearance.
