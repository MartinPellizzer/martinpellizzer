Dictyopsis

Dictyopsis, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvetleaf nightshade, is a genus of flowering plants in the nightshade family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, velvety leaves that give it its common name. The plants typically have alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves with a hairy surface. They produce small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels.

Dictyopsis species are often found in disturbed soils and are native to regions in Africa and the Mediterranean.
