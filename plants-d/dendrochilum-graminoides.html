Dendrochilum graminoides

Dendrochilum graminoides, commonly known as the grass-like orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, grass-like foliage and produces striking, showy flowers in shades of yellow and orange. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and shrublands. Its inflorescence consists of multiple racemes bearing numerous small, fragrant flowers.

Dendrochilum graminoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
