Dioclea grandiflora

Dioclea grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered dioclea, is a tropical climbing plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and large, showy flowers that can reach up to 10 centimeters in diameter. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pinkish flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a velvety texture.

Dioclea grandiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value in warm climates due to its striking floral display.
