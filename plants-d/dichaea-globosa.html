Dichaea globosa

Dichaea globosa, commonly known as the globular moss, is a species of epiphytic plant belonging to the Bromeliaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, cushion-like growth form, with tightly packed, small, dark green leaves that form a rosette. The plant produces small, white flowers that emerge from the center of the rosette, adding a subtle ornamental appeal. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found on trees in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is often appreciated in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
