Dryopteris moluccana

Dryopteris moluccana, commonly known as the Moluccan shield fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, leathery fronds that can grow up to 1.5 meters in length. The fronds are typically bipinnate, with a dense arrangement of small, lance-shaped pinnae. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its lush, evergreen foliage.
