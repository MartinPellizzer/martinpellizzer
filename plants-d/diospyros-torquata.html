Diospyros torquata

Diospyros torquata, commonly known as the Indian persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and opposite, ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange to red, fleshy fruits that are edible when fully ripe. Its fruit is often used in traditional medicine and culinary practices.

Diospyros torquata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
