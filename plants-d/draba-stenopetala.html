Draba stenopetala

Draba stenopetala, commonly known as the narrow-petaled whitlow grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a small, herbaceous annual that typically grows in alpine and subalpine regions. The plant features basal leaves that are linear to lanceolate in shape, and its flowers are small, white or pale yellow, arranged in a loose cluster. It produces small, spherical seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Draba stenopetala is adapted to harsh environments and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations.
