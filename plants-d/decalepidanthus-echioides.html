Decalepidanthus echioides

Decalepidanthus echioides, commonly known as the African waterwheel plant, is a unique aquatic plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive whorled arrangement of leaves, which resemble the spokes of a wheel. The plant thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments and is often found in marshy areas or along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are typically long and narrow, with a waxy surface that helps reduce water loss.

Decalepidanthus echioides plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and oxygen to aquatic organisms.
