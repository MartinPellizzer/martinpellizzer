Delphinium californicum subsp. californicum

Delphinium californicum subsp.

californicum, commonly known as the California larkspur, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States. It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring tall, sparsely branched stems with lance-shaped, blue to purple flowers.

The plant has palmate leaves with lobed segments, and its flowers are arranged in spikes, characteristic of the Delphinium genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in open meadows and rocky habitats.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
