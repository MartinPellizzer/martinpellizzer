Dianthus halepensis

Dianthus halepensis, commonly known as the Armenian campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of pink or white flowers with a distinctive, slightly serrated edge. The plant has fleshy, somewhat succulent leaves that help it survive in dry conditions. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Dianthus halepensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for its vibrant blooms.
