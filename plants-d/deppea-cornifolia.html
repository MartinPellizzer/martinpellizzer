Deppea cornifolia

Deppea cornifolia, commonly known as the hornleaf daphne, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, waxy leaves that are broad and often have a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sun exposure.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.
