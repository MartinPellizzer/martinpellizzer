Diplopappus caucasicus

Diplopappus caucasicus, commonly known as the Caucasian diplopappus, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish to brownish flowers arranged in clusters. It typically grows in rocky or arid environments, adapting to the harsh conditions of its native habitat.

Its botanical structure reflects its evolutionary adaptations to survive in temperate to subtropical climates.
