Disperis falcatipetala

Disperis falcatipetala, commonly known as the narrow-leafed orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white or pale yellow. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories or along stream banks. Its growth habit is rhizomatous, allowing it to spread gradually through underground stems.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native range.
