Diospyros guianensis subsp. akaraiensis

Diospyros guianensis subsp.

akaraiensis, commonly known as the Akarai or Akara tree, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows in humid, lowland forests.

The plant is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical leaves with a leathery texture. Its flowers are small and clustered, often with a reddish or yellowish hue, and it produces edible, sweet fruits that are highly valued in local communities.

The subspecies is noted for its ecological importance and potential economic value due to its fruit and timber.
