Draba incana var. legitima

Draba incana var. legitima, commonly known as the alpine mouse-ear cress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. It has a low-growing habit with basal leaves and is often found in meadows and tundra.

Due to its hardy nature, it is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions at high elevations.
