Dioscorea liebmannii

Dioscorea liebmannii, commonly known as the African yam bean, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary edible part of the plant. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its tubers are rich in starch and have been used traditionally for food and medicinal purposes.

Dioscorea liebmannii thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.
