Diospyros clavigera

Diospyros clavigera, commonly known as the Indian persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into orange to red, fleshy fruits. The fruits are edible when fully ripe and have a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production in tropical and subtropical regions.
