Desmodium latifolium var. virgatum

Desmodium latifolium var. virgatum, commonly known as silverleaf desmodium, is a perennial legume native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves with a silvery underside, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer, followed by flat, bean-like pods. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

This species is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in soil enrichment and ecological restoration projects.
