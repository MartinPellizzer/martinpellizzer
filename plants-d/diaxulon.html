Diaxulon

Diaxulon, commonly known as the sunflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy composite flowers that typically follow the sun's movement during the day. The plant has a robust stem with rough, hairy surfaces and produces numerous small flowers arranged in a circular pattern called a head. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Diaxulon is native to North America and is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and oil-rich seeds.
