Draba bruniifolia var. heterocoma

Draba bruniifolia var. heterocoma, commonly known as the alpine bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in well-drained, rocky soils at high altitudes. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale purple, often appearing in clusters. Its leaves are basal, linear, and often covered with a dense layer of fine hairs.

This variety is notable for its compact growth form and its ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
