Disa graminifolia

Disa graminifolia, commonly known as the grass orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and its striking, spike-like inflorescence that bears numerous small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or rocky areas. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with a distinctive three-lobed lip.

Disa graminifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
