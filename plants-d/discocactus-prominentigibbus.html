Discocactus prominentigibbus

Discocactus prominentigibbus, commonly known as the prominent rib cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, ribbed stems that curve outward. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its green, spiny structure. Its adaptations include thick, fleshy stems for water storage and spines for protection against herbivores.

The Discocactus prominentigibbus is well-suited to desert environments, thriving in full sun and well-draining soil.
