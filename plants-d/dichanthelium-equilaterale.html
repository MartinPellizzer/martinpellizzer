Dichanthelium equilaterale

Dichanthelium equilaterale, commonly known as the flat-seeded panic grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescence. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its flowers are usually small and inconspicuous, with three well-developed lemmas.

Dichanthelium equilaterale plays a role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments and provides habitat for various invertebrates.
