Dentaria laciniata var. opposita

Dentaria laciniata var. opposita, commonly known as the opposite-leaved toothache plant, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its opposite arrangement of lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its root system contains compounds that have been traditionally used for their analgesic properties.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland habitats.
