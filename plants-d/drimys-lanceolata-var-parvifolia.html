Drimys lanceolata var. parvifolia

Drimys lanceolata var. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved mountain pepper, is a shrub native to the temperate rainforests of southern South America.

It is characterized by its lanceolate, evergreen leaves that are typically small and glossy. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences, followed by dark blue, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in montane forests.

The species is valued for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
