Diospyros labillardierei

Diospyros labillardierei, commonly known as the Australian persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Australia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The tree produces edible berries that are typically orange to red in color when ripe. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions and is often found in coastal and subtropical regions.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its fruit, which is used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
