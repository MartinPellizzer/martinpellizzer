Doxomma pendulum

Doxomma pendulum, commonly known as the hanging bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, trailing stems that produce clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or purple. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in rocky or sandy habitats.

Doxomma pendulum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
