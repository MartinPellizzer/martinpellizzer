Digitaria nigritiana

Digitaria nigritiana, commonly known as black panicgrass, is a grass species native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial plant that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in disturbed soils and open habitats. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are characteristic of the grass family. It is known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.

Digitaria nigritiana plays a role in both ecological systems and human agriculture, depending on the context.
