Dendrobium leucanthum

Dendrobium leucanthum, commonly known as the white-lipped dendrobium, is an epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with numerous, cane-like stems that store water and nutrients. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, often with a distinctive lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining potting media.

Dendrobium leucanthum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in tropical gardens and greenhouses.
