Digitalis buxbaumii

Digitalis buxbaumii, commonly known as the Mexican foxglove, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the genus Digitalis, which includes other foxglove species known for their medicinal properties. The plant produces striking bell-shaped flowers that range in color from purple to white, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are ovate and hairy, and the plant prefers well-drained soil and full sun.

Digitalis buxbaumii is also valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine.
