Dioscorea cryptantha

Dioscorea cryptantha, commonly known as the desert yam, is a perennial vine native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its deep root system, which allows it to survive in dry, sandy soils. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by spiny, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It is a member of the Dioscoreaceae family, which includes other yam species.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often used in ecological restoration projects in degraded landscapes.
