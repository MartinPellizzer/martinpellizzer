Diospyros ferrea f. oblongata

Diospyros ferrea f. oblongata, commonly known as the Brazilian persimmon, is a variant of the persimmon tree native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its elliptical to oblong leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange to red fruits with a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and edible fruit, making it a popular choice in horticulture.
