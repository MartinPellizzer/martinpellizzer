Dianthus pseudarmeria subsp. glabripetalus

Dianthus pseudarmeria subsp.

glabripetalus, commonly known as the glabrous-petaled campion, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its showy, clustered flowers and succulent stems.

The subspecies is distinguished by its smooth, glabrous petals, which give it its common name. It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and produces pink to red flowers in the summer months.

The plant is often used in rock gardens due to its ornamental value and hardy nature.
