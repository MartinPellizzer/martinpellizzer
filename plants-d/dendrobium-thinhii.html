Dendrobium thinhii

Dendrobium thinhii, commonly known as the Vietnamese orchid, is a member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is an epiphytic plant, meaning it grows on the surface of trees or rocks without deriving nutrients from them. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and yellow, often with intricate patterns. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

Dendrobium thinhii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for horticultural purposes.
