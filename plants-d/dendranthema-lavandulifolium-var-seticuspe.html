Dendranthema lavandulifolium var. seticuspe

Dendranthema lavandulifolium var. seticuspe, commonly known as the woolly chamomile, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features hairy, gray-green foliage that resembles lamb's ear, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with white centers, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Its aromatic properties make it a popular choice for ornamental and medicinal purposes.
