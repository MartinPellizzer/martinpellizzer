Deppea martinez-calderonii

Deppea martinez-calderonii, commonly known as the Martinez Calderonii pittosporum, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of Ecuador.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has elliptical leaves with a smooth, dark green surface and a leathery texture. It thrives in cool, humid climates and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
