Ditassa endoleuca

Ditassa endoleuca, commonly known as the white-flowered orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, white petals and a slender, branching stem. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest undergrowth. Its flowers bloom in clusters and are known for their subtle fragrance.

Botanically, it belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental beauty.
