Dactylorhiza majalis f. linearis

Dactylorhiza majalis f. linearis, commonly known as the slender marsh orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe and northern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and produces tall, slender flowering stems that bear clusters of pale yellow to greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or damp habitats such as marshes, bogs, and along the edges of ponds. It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its specialized mycorrhizal associations that aid in nutrient uptake.

The species is often found in acidic soils and is adapted to thrive in environments with high moisture levels.
