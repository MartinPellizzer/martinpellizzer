Dianthus microlepis f. alba

Dianthus microlepis f. alba, commonly known as the white carnation, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces showy, double flowers in shades of white. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white, powdery bloom. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This cultivar is valued for its delicate appearance and is often used in floral arrangements.
