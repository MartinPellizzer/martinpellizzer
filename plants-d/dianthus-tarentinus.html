Dianthus tarentinus

Dianthus tarentinus, commonly known as the Tarentine pinks, is a species of flowering plant in the family Caryophyllaceae.

It is native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa, where it thrives in sunny, well-drained soils. The plant produces clusters of delicate, pink or red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically evergreen.

Dianthus tarentinus is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
