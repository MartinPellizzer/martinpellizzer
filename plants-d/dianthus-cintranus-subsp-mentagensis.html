Dianthus cintranus subsp. mentagensis

Dianthus cintranus subsp.

mentagensis, commonly known as the Mentagena campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers with distinctive red markings.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is well adapted to arid conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the flowers bloom in early spring.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, known for its showy, colorful blooms.
