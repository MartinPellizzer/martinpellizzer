Distephanus rhodopappus

Distephanus rhodopappus, commonly known as the redflower dandelion, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves and bright red to orange flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces a distinctive, puffy seed head that disperses its seeds by wind, a common adaptation in the Asteraceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
