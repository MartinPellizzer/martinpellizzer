Diasperus moonii

Diasperus moonii, commonly known as the moon's ghost, is a striking succulent native to South Africa.

It features a cylindrical stem with a grayish-green color, often adorned with a waxy coating that helps it retain moisture. The plant produces delicate, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in spring. Its unique appearance and ability to thrive in arid conditions make it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Diasperus moonii is well-suited for dry climates and requires minimal watering, making it an ideal plant for xeriscaping.
