Draba lasiocarpa

Draba lasiocarpa, commonly known as the roughleaf whitlowgrass, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, often in open areas with well-drained soil. It has basal leaves that are covered in fine, stiff hairs, giving it a rough texture.

Draba lasiocarpa is usually found at high elevations and is adapted to cold, harsh climates.
