Dactylorhiza majalis var. brevifolia

Dactylorhiza majalis var. brevifolia, commonly known as the short-leaved marsh orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, preferring shaded environments with moist, well-drained soil. The plant features slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, greenish or yellowish flowers in late summer. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is often considered a rare or endangered species in many regions.
