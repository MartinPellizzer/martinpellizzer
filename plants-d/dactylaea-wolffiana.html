Dactylaea wolffiana

Dactylaea wolffiana, commonly known as the giant date palm, is a striking member of the Arecaceae family.

It is native to the Arabian Peninsula and is characterized by its massive, fan-shaped leaves that can reach lengths of up to 5 meters. The plant produces large, clustered inflorescences that bear both male and female flowers, typical of many palm species. Its trunk is robust and often has a fibrous, spongy texture, which helps it withstand harsh environmental conditions.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated in various tropical and subtropical regions.
