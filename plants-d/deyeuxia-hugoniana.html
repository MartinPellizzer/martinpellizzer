Deyeuxia hugoniana

Deyeuxia hugoniana, commonly known as the Hugon's sedge, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of flowers during the summer months. It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.

Deyeuxia hugoniana is also valued for its ornamental qualities in garden settings.
