Diospyros pilosiuscula var. andamanensis

Diospyros pilosiuscula var. andamanensis, commonly known as the Andaman persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Andaman Islands.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other persimmon species. The plant is characterized by its smooth, dark brown bark and elliptical leaves that are glossy on the upper surface. Its fruit is a small, round berry that ripens to a deep orange-red color, often with a sweet, astringent flavor.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal uses in traditional practices.
