Diasperus albiflorus

Diasperus albiflorus, commonly known as the whiteflower daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with white petals and yellow centers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It produces small, oval-shaped fruits known as cypsela, which are dispersed by wind.

Diasperus albiflorus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature.
