Desmazeria composita

Desmazeria composita, commonly known as the composite desmazeria, is a flowering plant species native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads consisting of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically features slender stems and small, clustered leaves, with showy yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it is more commonly encountered in its natural wild state.
