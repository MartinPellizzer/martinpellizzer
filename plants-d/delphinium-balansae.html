Delphinium balansae

Delphinium balansae, commonly known as the Balansae larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, spurred flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments with well-drained soil. Delphinium balansae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.

However, like many members of its genus, it contains toxic alkaloids and should be handled with care.
