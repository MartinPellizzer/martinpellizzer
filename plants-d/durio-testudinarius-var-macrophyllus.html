Durio testudinarius var. macrophyllus

Durio testudinarius var. macrophyllus, commonly known as the large-leaf durian, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, thick, and leathery leaves, which can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces a unique fruit with a strong aroma, often described as both pungent and complex. Its flowers are typically unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant.

The durian is well-known for its high nutritional value and is widely cultivated for its edible fruit.
