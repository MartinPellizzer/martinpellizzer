Diospyros annonifolia

Diospyros annonifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed ebony, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, ovate leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, orange-red berries that are often used in traditional medicine. Its wood is highly valued for its density and durability, making it a sought-after material in carpentry and furniture-making.

The species is also noted for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
