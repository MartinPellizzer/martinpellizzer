Dierama mossii

Dierama mossii, commonly known as the Cape mountain dierama, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like foliage and showy, spurred flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to the fynbos biome. Its flowering period occurs in late winter to early spring, and it is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
