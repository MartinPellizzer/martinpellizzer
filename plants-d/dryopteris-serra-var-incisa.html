Dryopteris serra var. incisa

Dryopteris serra var. incisa, commonly known as the serrated wood fern, is a variant of the wood fern genus Dryopteris.

It is a perennial fern characterized by its finely divided, serrated fronds that give it a distinctive appearance. Native to temperate regions of Asia, it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread and form dense colonies.

Its foliage is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value in shaded garden settings.
