Draba hispanica

Draba hispanica, commonly known as the Spanish rockcress, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically thrives in rocky or alpine environments. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are basal, simple, and often covered in a fine layer of hairs.

Draba hispanica is notable for its ability to survive in harsh, arid conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
