Dodecatheon Dentatum Subsp. Utahense

Dodecatheon dentatum subsp. utahense is a rare and delicate wildflower native to the arid regions of Utah. It is known for its striking bell-shaped blossoms that range from pink to purple in color. This subspecies thrives in rocky soils and open habitats, often found in mountainous areas. Its blooming period typically occurs in early spring, making it a short-lived but visually stunning plant. Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered a species of conservation concern.
