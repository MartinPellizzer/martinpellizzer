Dodecatheon pulchellum subvar. coriaceum

Dodecatheon pulchellum subvar. coriaceum, commonly known as the roughleaf glory-of-the-snow, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, often reddish stems and heart-shaped, coriaceous (leathery) leaves. The plant produces clusters of nodding, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in early spring. It prefers moist, woodsy habitats and is often found in shaded, forested areas.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically valuable species in its native range.
