Dielytra bracteosa

Dielytra bracteosa, commonly known as the bracteosa daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are small and white or pale yellow.

Dielytra bracteosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
