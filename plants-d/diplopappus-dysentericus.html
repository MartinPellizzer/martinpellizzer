Diplopappus dysentericus

Diplopappus dysentericus, commonly known as the dysenteric burr, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. Its fruits are dry, hairy achenes that are adapted for wind dispersal.

Despite its botanical features, it is noted for its historical use in traditional medicine, though it is not widely cultivated today.
