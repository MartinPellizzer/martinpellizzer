Daphne cneorum var. verlotii

Daphne cneorum var. verlotii, commonly known as the Corsican daphne, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, glandular leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white to pink, often appearing in early spring. Its small, leathery berries are orange-red and persist into winter, adding visual interest.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature.
