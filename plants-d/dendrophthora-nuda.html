Dendrophthora nuda

Dendrophthora nuda, commonly known as the naked tree fungus, is a unique species of fungus that exhibits interesting botanical characteristics.

It is a saprophytic organism, decomposing dead wood and contributing to nutrient cycling in forest ecosystems. The fungus forms a mycelial network within the decaying wood, which can persist for extended periods. Its reproductive structures are typically found in the form of fruiting bodies that emerge from the host material.

Due to its specialized habitat, Dendrophthora nuda is often found in temperate and tropical forests where suitable deadwood is available.
