Dendrobium eulophotum

Dendrobium eulophotum, commonly known as the sunlight orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is an epiphytic orchid that grows on trees and rocks, thriving in humid, tropical environments. The plant produces long, thin pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, enabling it to survive in its natural habitat. Its flowers are typically white or pale yellow, with intricate patterns that attract pollinators.

Dendrobium eulophotum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for horticultural purposes.
