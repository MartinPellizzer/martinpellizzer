Dalea unguicularis

Dalea unguicularis, commonly known as the prairie clover, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and woodland edges. It has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces seed pods that split open to release its seeds.

Dalea unguicularis is often used in native plant gardens due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
