Dendrobium juncifolium

Dendrobium juncifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf dendrobium, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its sympodial growth habit, producing a series of pseudobulbs along its stem. The plant features strap-like leaves that are typically narrow and grass-like in appearance. It produces clusters of fragrant, yellowish-green flowers during the warmer months.

Dendrobium juncifolium is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable conditions.
