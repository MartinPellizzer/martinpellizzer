Daphne vaccinium

Daphne vaccinium, commonly known as the common heath, is a shrub native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other acid-loving plants such as blueberries and cranberries. The plant typically has small, leathery leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, pink or white flowers in spring. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and prefers partial shade to full sun.

Daphne vaccinium is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
