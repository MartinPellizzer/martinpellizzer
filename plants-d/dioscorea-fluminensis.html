Dioscorea fluminensis

Dioscorea fluminensis, commonly known as the Brazilian yam, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary edible part. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring moist, well-drained soils.

Its tubers are valued for their nutritional content and traditional medicinal uses.
