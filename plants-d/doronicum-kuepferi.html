Doronicum kuepferi

Doronicum kuepferi, commonly known as the Alpine goldcup, is a perennial herb native to the Alps.

It features a compact, low-growing habit with dark green, hairy leaves that are lance-shaped and slightly toothed. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow, daisy-like flowers with a prominent central disk, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for alpine gardens.

Doronicum kuepferi is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging mountainous environments.
