Danthonia popinensis

Danthonia popinensis, commonly known as the Popin's grass, is a perennial grass species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats. The plant has slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, spike-like flowers that are usually pink or red in color.

Danthonia popinensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowering display.
