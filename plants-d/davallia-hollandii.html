Davallia hollandii

Davallia hollandii, commonly known as the golden polypody, is a fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are typically light green to golden in color. The plant features rhizomes that grow horizontally and produce small, clustered sporangia on the undersides of its leaves. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Davallia hollandii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in indoor gardens.
