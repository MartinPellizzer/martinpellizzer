Dysoxylum sorsogonense

Dysoxylum sorsogonense, commonly known as the Sorsogonan mangosteen, is a tropical tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and smooth, greyish bark. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by edible, segmented fruits that are similar in appearance to true mangosteens.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts within its native habitat.
