Diospyros ruminata

Diospyros ruminata, commonly known as the Indian persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to South and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish-brown bark and simple, alternate leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by orange to red, fleshy fruits. Its fruit is edible when fully mature and has a sweet, sometimes astringent taste.

Diospyros ruminata is also valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in furniture and carving.
