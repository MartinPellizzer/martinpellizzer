Dactylorchis × pseudotraunsteineri

Dactylorchis × pseudotraunsteineri, commonly known as the Traunsteiner's dactyl orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, often fragrant flowers that display a mix of purple, pink, and white hues. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or terrestrial orchid, attaching itself to trees or thriving in moist, shaded forest environments. Its roots are specialized for absorbing nutrients from the air and rain, a common trait among orchids.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
