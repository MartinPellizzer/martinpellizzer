Digitaria pennata var. shettyana

Digitaria pennata var. shettyana, commonly known as Shetty's Digitaria, is a species of grass native to parts of Asia, particularly India.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are typically hairy along the edges. The inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are small, compact flower clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of water bodies.

This plant plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
