Dioscorea bako

Dioscorea bako, commonly known as African yam bean, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its hairy, cylindrical stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by tuberous roots that are used as food. Its tubers are rich in starch and have a slightly bitter taste when raw.

Dioscorea bako is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
