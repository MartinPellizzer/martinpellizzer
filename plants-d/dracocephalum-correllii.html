Dracocephalum correllii

Dracocephalum correllii, commonly known as Chinese skullcap, is a perennial herb native to China and parts of East Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants such as mint and lavender. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and small, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to blue. It is often cultivated for its medicinal properties, as it contains various bioactive compounds.

The plant thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland or grassy areas.
