Dodonaea amplisemina

Dodonaea amplisemina, commonly known as the broad-leaved hop bush, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by woody, bead-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and dry inland regions.

Dodonaea amplisemina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native landscaping.
