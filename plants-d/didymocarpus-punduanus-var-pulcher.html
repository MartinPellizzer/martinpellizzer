Didymocarpus punduanus var. pulcher

Didymocarpus punduanus var. pulcher, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that typically grows up to 10 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its distinctive golden-yellow inflorescences that appear in clusters, adding a striking visual appeal. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a leathery texture with a glossy surface.

The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and parks due to its vibrant floral display.
