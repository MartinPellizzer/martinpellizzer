Digitalis purpurea subsp. carpetana

Digitalis purpurea subsp.

carpetana, commonly known as the purple foxglove, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the British Isles. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that bear whorled clusters of striking violet to purple flowers.

The plant produces tubular flowers that open at the top, with a distinctive spurred petal at the base. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This subspecies is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and historical use in medicine.
