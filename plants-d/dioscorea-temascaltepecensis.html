Dioscorea temascaltepecensis

Dioscorea temascaltepecensis, commonly known as the Temascaltepec yam, is a species of yam native to Mexico.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary edible part of the plant. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, preferring well-drained soils and partial shade. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a rough texture, and it produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into fruit.

The tubers of Dioscorea temascaltepecensis are valued for their nutritional content and traditional uses in local cuisines.
