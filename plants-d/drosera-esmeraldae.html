Drosera esmeraldae

Drosera esmeraldae, commonly known as the Emerald Sundew, is a carnivorous plant native to the wetlands of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its rosette of sticky, glandular hairs that trap and digest insects. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in spring, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and requires high humidity to flourish.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is a popular subject in botanical studies and conservation efforts.
