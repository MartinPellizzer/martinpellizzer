Dinebra verticillata

Dinebra verticillata, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped foliage and clusters of showy, violet-colored flowers. The plant has a short, thick stem and produces small, oval-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires indirect sunlight to prevent leaf scorching.

Dinebra verticillata is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
