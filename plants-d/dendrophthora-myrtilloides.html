Dendrophthora myrtilloides

Dendrophthora myrtilloides, commonly known as the blueberry mistletoe, is a parasitic plant that attaches itself to the branches of various hardwood trees.

It belongs to the Viscaraceae family and is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and clusters of blue-black berries. The plant produces a sticky substance called viscin, which helps it adhere to its host. It is native to parts of North America and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can affect the health of its host trees if left unchecked.
