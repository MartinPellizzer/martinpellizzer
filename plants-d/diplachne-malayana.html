Diplachne malayana

Diplachne malayana, commonly known as the Malay flatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in wetland habitats and is characterized by its flat, rhizomatous growth habit. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is often found in marshy areas and can form dense mats that stabilize soil.

Botanically, it is noted for its ability to thrive in waterlogged conditions, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
