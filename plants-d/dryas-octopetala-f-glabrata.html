Dryas octopetala f. glabrata

Dryas octopetala f. glabrata, commonly known as the glabrous dryas, is a flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its low-growing habit and dense clusters of white, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically thrives in alpine and arctic environments, where it is adapted to cold and harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually covered in a thick layer of wax, which helps to reduce water loss in its arid habitats.
