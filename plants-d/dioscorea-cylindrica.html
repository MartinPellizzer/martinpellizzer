Dioscorea cylindrica

Dioscorea cylindrica, commonly known as yellow snakeroot, is a perennial vine native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and cylindrical, tuberous roots. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by red to orange berries. Its tubers are often used in traditional medicine and have been studied for their potential pharmacological properties.

Dioscorea cylindrica thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.
