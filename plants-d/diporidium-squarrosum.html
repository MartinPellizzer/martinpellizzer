Diporidium squarrosum

Diporidium squarrosum, commonly known as the slender lily, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that bear showy, white to pale pink flowers with a distinctive two-lobed stigma. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a rough texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
