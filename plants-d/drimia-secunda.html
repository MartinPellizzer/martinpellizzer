Drimia secunda

Drimia secunda, commonly known as the Cape daisy, is a perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its strap-shaped, grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of white or pale yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Drimia secunda is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
