Digitaria adscendens subsp. chrysoblephara

Digitaria adscendens subsp.

chrysoblephara, commonly known as the golden leaved bluestem, is a grass species native to parts of Africa and the Middle East. It is characterized by its erect growth habit and narrow, linear leaves that often exhibit a golden hue on the underside.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing minute, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is commonly found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and used for erosion control.
