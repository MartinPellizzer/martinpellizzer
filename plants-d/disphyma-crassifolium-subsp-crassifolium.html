Disphyma crassifolium subsp. crassifolium

Disphyma crassifolium subsp.

crassifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaved disphyma, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Australia. It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are adapted to store water, a feature typical of plants in dry environments.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its drought tolerance, it is sometimes used in xeriscaping and native garden landscaping.
