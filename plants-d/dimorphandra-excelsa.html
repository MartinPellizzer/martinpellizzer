Dimorphandra excelsa

Dimorphandra excelsa, commonly known as the giant sable antelope tree, is a large, deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, flaky bark and long, drooping branches that often hang over the forest floor. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer. Its fruit is a woody pod containing several seeds, which are dispersed by animals.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
