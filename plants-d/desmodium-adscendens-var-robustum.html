Desmodium adscendens var. robustum

Desmodium adscendens var. robustum, commonly known as the climbing desmodium, is a perennial legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its twining stems and trifoliate leaves with small, heart-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple, followed by cylindrical pods containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in agroforestry systems for its nitrogen-fixing abilities.

Its rapid growth and ability to climb make it a useful plant for erosion control and as a forage species.
