Drypetes capillipes

Drypetes capillipes, commonly known as the slender-leaved drypetes, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its small, opposite leaves with fine, hair-like veins. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
