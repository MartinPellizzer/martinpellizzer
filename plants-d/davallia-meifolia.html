Davallia meifolia

Davallia meifolia, commonly known as the silver-veined fern, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial fern characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds with distinctive silver veins. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests or rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are wiry and often form dense clusters, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Davallia meifolia is valued in horticulture for its elegant appearance and ability to thrive in low-light conditions.
