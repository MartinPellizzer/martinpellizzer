Draba kongboiana

Draba kongboiana, commonly known as the Kongbo's whitlow grass, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of Tibet and Nepal. The plant typically produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters. Its leaves are basal, rosette-like, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Draba kongboiana is adapted to cold, arid environments and is often found in alpine meadows.
