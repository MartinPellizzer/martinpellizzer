Drosera stolonifera subsp. humilis

Drosera stolonifera subsp.

humilis, commonly known as the dwarf sundew, is a low-growing carnivorous plant native to southeastern Australia. It features small, glandular hairs that secrete a sticky substance to trap insects.

The plant typically forms dense, mat-like colonies on moist, nutrient-poor soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in red-tipped tentacles.

It is a hardy species that thrives in humid, shaded environments.
