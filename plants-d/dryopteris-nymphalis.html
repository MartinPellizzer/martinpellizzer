Dryopteris nymphalis

Dryopteris nymphalis, commonly known as the silver shield fern, is a species of fern native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, silvery-white to grayish underside on its fronds, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its fronds are usually bipinnate, with a delicate, feathery appearance.

Dryopteris nymphalis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
