Dracontium asperum

Dracontium asperum, commonly known as the roughleaf arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix and a spathe. The plant features large, heart-shaped leaves with a rough, waxy texture, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers. Its rhizomes are used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands and forest understories.
