Dicerostylis rubra

Dicerostylis rubra, commonly known as the red-flowered dicerostylis, is a perennial herb native to the wetlands of southeastern United States.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically a striking red or pink color. The plant blooms from late spring to early summer, and its flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Dicerostylis rubra thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in swampy areas or along the edges of ponds.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland habitats.
