Daknopholis

Daknopholis, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking yellow and orange flowers, which are often adorned with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the surrounding soil.

Daknopholis is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
