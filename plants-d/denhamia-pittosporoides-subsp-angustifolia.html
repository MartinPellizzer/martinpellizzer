Denhamia pittosporoides subsp. angustifolia

Denhamia pittosporoides subsp.

angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved denhamia, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery-white coating.

The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers arranged in clusters, typically in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal heathlands.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
