Daphne odora var. atrocaulis

Daphne odora var. atrocaulis, commonly known as black-leaf daphne, is a cultivar of the daphne plant characterized by its dark purple to black foliage.

It produces fragrant, tubular flowers in shades of pink, white, and red, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. The plant is evergreen, with glossy, narrow leaves that provide year-round visual interest. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Due to its toxic properties, it should be handled with care.
