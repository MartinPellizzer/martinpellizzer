Desmodium granulatum

Desmodium granulatum, commonly known as the hairy burr legume, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves with small, hairy stipules and produces small, yellowish flowers that are often clustered in racemes. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several small, round seeds.

Desmodium granulatum is often used in traditional medicine and can also serve as a forage plant for livestock.
