Digitalis transiens var. mesatlantica

Digitalis transiens var. mesatlantica, commonly known as the Mexican foxglove, is a perennial herb native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of bell-shaped flowers. The flowers are typically purple or pink, with a distinctive two-lipped structure typical of the genus Digitalis. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This plant is also noted for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
