Dicoma capensis

Dicoma capensis, commonly known as the Cape daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces yellow-orange flower heads that bloom in spring. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid conditions.

Dicoma capensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
