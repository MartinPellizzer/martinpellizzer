Dryopoa

Dryopoa, commonly known as the California needlegrass, is a genus of grasses native to the western United States.

These plants are characterized by their tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. They typically grow in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and shrublands. Dryopoa species are often found in coastal and inland areas with well-drained soils.

Their seeds are an important food source for various wildlife species.
