Dianthus sachalinensis

Dianthus sachalinensis, commonly known as the Sakhalin campion, is a flowering plant native to the Russian Far East and northeastern China.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with fleshy, often reddish stems and basal leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its flowers have five petals, and the plant is often found in moist, calcareous soils along riverbanks and wet meadows.

Dianthus sachalinensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
