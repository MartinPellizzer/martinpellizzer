Daucus carota var. saalfeldensis

Daucus carota var. saalfeldensis, commonly known as the Saalfeld carrot, is a rare and ancient variety of wild carrot.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated roots that are typically yellow or orange in color. This variety is native to Central Europe and is often found in moist, calcareous soils. Its flowers are small, white, and arranged in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family.

Due to its rarity, it is considered a valuable species for botanical studies and conservation efforts.
