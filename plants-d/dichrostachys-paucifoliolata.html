Dichrostachys paucifoliolata

Dichrostachys paucifoliolata, commonly known as the small-leaved stranglebark, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and small, opposite leaves that are usually less than 2 cm in length. The flowers are arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences and are typically pink or purple in color. The plant produces small, woody pods that contain several seeds.

It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
