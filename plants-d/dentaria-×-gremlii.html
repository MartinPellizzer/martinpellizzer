Dentaria × gremlii

Dentaria × gremlii, commonly known as the hybrid toothworts, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its unique, tooth-like leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in a raceme, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, Dentaria × gremlii is considered an endangered species in many regions.
