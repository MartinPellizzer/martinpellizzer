Diuris oculata

Diuris oculata, commonly known as the eye orchid, is a striking Australian orchid species characterized by its distinctive, vividly colored flowers.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is native to the eastern regions of Australia, particularly Queensland and New South Wales. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in open forests or heathlands. Its flowers, which resemble eyes, are usually a mix of red, orange, and yellow, with a contrasting black or dark brown center.

Diuris oculata is a perennial herb that produces its showy blooms during the late spring to early summer season.
