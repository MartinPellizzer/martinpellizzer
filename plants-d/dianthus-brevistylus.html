Dianthus brevistylus

Dianthus brevistylus, commonly known as the short-styled campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of showy, pink to red flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters, with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers consist of five petals and are often visited by bees and other pollinators.

Dianthus brevistylus is well-suited for rock gardens and dry, well-drained soils.
