Dieffenbachia picta subvar. jenmanii

Dieffenbachia picta subvar. jenmanii, commonly known as the Jenman's Dieffenbachia, is a tropical plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, variegated foliage with white and green patterns on broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant belongs to the Araceae family, which includes many ornamental and decorative plants. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its foliage is also known for its toxicity to pets and humans if ingested.
