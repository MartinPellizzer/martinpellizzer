Delphinium potaninii var. potaninii

Delphinium potaninii var. potaninii, commonly known as the Potanin's larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It features tall, sparsely branched stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers in racemes, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lanceolate and have a rough, hairy texture.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a striking addition to rock gardens and alpine settings.
