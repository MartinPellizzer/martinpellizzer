Draba chrysantha var. graminea

Draba chrysantha var. graminea, commonly known as the yellow winter cress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are basal, rosette-like, and covered in a fine layer of white pubescence.

This variety is distinguished by its more grass-like leaves compared to the typical form of D. chrysantha.
