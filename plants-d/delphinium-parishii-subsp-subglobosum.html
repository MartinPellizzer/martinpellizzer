Delphinium parishii subsp. subglobosum

Delphinium parishii subsp.

subglobosum, commonly known as the Sierra delphinium, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that bear large, showy blue to purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine meadows, preferring well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are lanceolate and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The subspecies is notable for its rounded, globular flower heads, which set it apart from other delphinium species.
