Dyssodia setifolia var. greggii

Dyssodia setifolia var. greggii, commonly known as Gregg's desert globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its globular shape, with numerous small, woolly leaves arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a vibrant touch to its arid habitat. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to full sun, making it a hardy species in desert environments.

Its unique morphology and drought tolerance make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
