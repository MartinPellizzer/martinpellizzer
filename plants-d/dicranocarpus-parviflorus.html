Dicranocarpus parviflorus

Dicranocarpus parviflorus, commonly known as the small-flowered rock fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, creeping growth habit and is typically found in rocky or shaded forest environments. The plant has lance-shaped, glossy leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hair. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences.

Dicranocarpus parviflorus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in specialized gardens.
