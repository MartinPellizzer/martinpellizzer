Desmodium tonkinense

Desmodium tonkinense, commonly known as Vietnamese velvet bean, is a leguminous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Desmodium tonkinense is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for agricultural purposes.
