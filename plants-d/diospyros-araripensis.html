Diospyros araripensis

Diospyros araripensis, commonly known as the Araripe persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and small, clustered flowers. The tree produces edible berries that are typically red or orange when mature, similar to persimmons. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in lowland forests and riverine areas.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
