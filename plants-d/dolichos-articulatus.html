Dolichos articulatus

Dolichos articulatus, commonly known as the jointed cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its pods are elongated and contain edible seeds that are used in various cuisines.

Dolichos articulatus is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in agricultural systems.
