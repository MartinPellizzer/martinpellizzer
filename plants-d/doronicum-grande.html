Doronicum grande

Doronicum grande, commonly known as the large leopard's bane, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and ray florets. The plant features upright stems covered in soft, hairy foliage, and produces showy yellow flowers with red-brown centers. It thrives in well-drained, calcareous soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Doronicum grande is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
