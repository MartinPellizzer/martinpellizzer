Delphinium bicolor f. mccallae

Delphinium bicolor f. mccallae, commonly known as the McCall's delphinium, is a striking perennial herbaceous plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It features tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters, adorned with large, showy flowers in shades of blue, violet, and white. The plant belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its palmate leaves that are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is also a subject of interest in botanical research and conservation efforts.
