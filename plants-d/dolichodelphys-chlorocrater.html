Dolichodelphys chlorocrater

Dolichodelphys chlorocrater, commonly known as the green-topped cactus, is a species native to southern Africa.

It is a stem succulent characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers during the summer months, which are clustered at the tips of the stems. Its leaves are modified into spines, and it has a shallow root system adapted to arid environments.

Dolichodelphys chlorocrater is well-suited for cultivation in dry climates with minimal water requirements.
