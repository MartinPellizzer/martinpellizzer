Deyeuxia nudiflora

Deyeuxia nudiflora, commonly known as the naked-flower sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to various regions of Asia.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along riverbanks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often clustered at the base of the stems. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

Deyeuxia nudiflora is characterized by its lack of leaf sheaths and its ability to thrive in waterlogged soils.
