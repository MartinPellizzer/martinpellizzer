Dugandiodendron hamorii

Dugandiodendron hamorii, commonly known as the golden star vine, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that resemble small stars, which bloom in clusters along its slender, climbing stems. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and dark green, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and greenhouses.

Due to its unique appearance and growth habits, it is a subject of interest in botanical studies and horticulture.
