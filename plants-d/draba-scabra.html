Draba scabra

Draba scabra, commonly known as the slender whitlow grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a small, herbaceous annual or biennial plant that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, linear leaves and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in early spring. Draba scabra is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia and is valued for its ornamental appeal in rock gardens.
