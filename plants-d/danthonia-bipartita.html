Danthonia bipartita

Danthonia bipartita, commonly known as the two-lobed grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive two-lobed leaf blades and forms dense, low-growing turf. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks. It has fibrous roots that help stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Danthonia bipartita is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland restoration projects.
