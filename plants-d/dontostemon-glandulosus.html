Dontostemon glandulosus

Dontostemon glandulosus, commonly known as the glandular daisies, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and white to pale blue petals. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils. Its leaves are often covered in fine, glandular hairs, which give it its scientific name.

The flowers bloom in the summer and attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
