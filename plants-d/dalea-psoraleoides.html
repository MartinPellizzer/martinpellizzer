Dalea psoraleoides

Dalea psoraleoides, commonly known as the Texas milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow flowers in late spring. It belongs to the legume family (Fabaceae) and has a deep root system that helps it survive in harsh conditions.

Dalea psoraleoides is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
