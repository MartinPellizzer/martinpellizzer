Delphinium chefoense

Delphinium chefoense, commonly known as the alpine larkspur, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant produces spikes of blue to purple flowers, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine downy coating.

Delphinium chefoense is adapted to grow in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows.
