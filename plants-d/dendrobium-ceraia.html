Dendrobium ceraia

Dendrobium ceraia, commonly known as the Ceraia orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is an epiphytic plant, meaning it grows on the surface of trees or other structures without deriving nutrients from them. The plant features long, cane-like stems that are often covered in clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. Its leaves are typically clustered at the base of the stem and are thick and fleshy to store water.

Dendrobium ceraia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its delicate floral display.
