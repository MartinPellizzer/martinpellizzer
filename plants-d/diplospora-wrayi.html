Diplospora wrayi

Diplospora wrayi, commonly known as the red sand mat, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial that forms dense mats of foliage, often found in rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces small, pink to red flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are fleshy and succulent, adapted to store water in its native dry environment.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
