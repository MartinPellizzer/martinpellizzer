Diplopeltis

Diplopeltis, commonly known as the spider orchid, is a genus of flowering plants in the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is characterized by its unique, often intricate floral structures and is native to various regions in Africa and Asia. The plants typically have elongated, strap-like leaves and produce showy, fragrant flowers that resemble spiders, hence its common name. Diplopeltis species are epiphytic or lithophytic, meaning they grow on other plants or rocks, and they rely on specific environmental conditions for survival.

These orchids are valued for their ornamental beauty and are often cultivated for their striking floral displays.
