Dianthus brachyanthus subsp. gredensis

Dianthus brachyanthus subsp.

gredensis, commonly known as the Gredens mountain pink, is a perennial flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America. It is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth habit and produces showy, pink to reddish-pink flowers with five petals.

The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cool, alpine environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in a fine, white pubescence.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or alpine displays.
