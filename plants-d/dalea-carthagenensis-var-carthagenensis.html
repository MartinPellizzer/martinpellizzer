Dalea carthagenensis var. carthagenensis

Dalea carthagenensis var. carthagenensis, commonly known as the Carthage milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, pink to purple flowers. The plant has pinnate leaves with numerous leaflets and produces cylindrical, hairy pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife.
