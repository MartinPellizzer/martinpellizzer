Daucus communis var. agrestis

Daucus communis var. agrestis, commonly known as the wild carrot, is a biennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like parsley and dill. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into fluffy, white seed heads. Its roots are thin and often used in traditional herbal medicine.

The plant is also an important nectar source for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
