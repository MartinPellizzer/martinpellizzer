Dipsacus strictus

Dipsacus strictus, commonly known as the goat's beard, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and clusters of small, pink or white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed areas.

Dipsacus strictus is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
