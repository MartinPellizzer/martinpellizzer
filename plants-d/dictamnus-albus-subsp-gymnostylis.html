Dictamnus albus subsp. gymnostylis

Dictamnus albus subsp.

gymnostylis, commonly known as the white dictamnus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It features aromatic, often fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has smooth, grayish-green leaves that are opposite in arrangement and have a distinctive, somewhat hairy texture. Its stems are typically slender and branching, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its pleasant fragrance and visual interest.
