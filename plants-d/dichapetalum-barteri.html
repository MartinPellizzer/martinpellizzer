Dichapetalum barteri

Dichapetalum barteri, commonly known as the barter's mallow, is a flowering plant native to the grasslands of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and produces capsule fruits containing numerous small seeds. It is often found in open habitats and is adapted to dry conditions.

Dichapetalum barteri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
