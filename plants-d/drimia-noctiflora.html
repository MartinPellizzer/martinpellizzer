Drimia noctiflora

Drimia noctiflora, commonly known as the night-flowering drimia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, large, white to pale purple flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has strap-shaped, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Drimia noctiflora is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and unique flowering habit.
