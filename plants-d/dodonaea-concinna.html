Dodonaea concinna

Dodonaea concinna, commonly known as the silky oaks or silky acacia, is a species of flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that typically grows in coastal and semi-arid regions. The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped, silvery-green leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring, followed by woody, pod-like fruits.

Dodonaea concinna is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
