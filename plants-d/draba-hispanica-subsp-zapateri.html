Draba hispanica subsp. zapateri

Draba hispanica subsp.

zapateri, commonly known as the Iberian whitlow grass, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe, particularly Spain and Portugal. It is a low-growing perennial herb with basal rosettes of narrow, fleshy leaves.

The species produces small, white or pale yellow flowers during the spring, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in rocky or calcareous soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates.

The subspecies zapateri is distinguished by its specific geographic distribution and morphological characteristics.
