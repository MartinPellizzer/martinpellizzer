Davallia inaequalis var. major

Davallia inaequalis var. major, commonly known as the giant shield fern, is a striking fern species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, leathery fronds that can grow up to several feet in length, with a distinctive shield-shaped pinnae. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and prefers well-draining, acidic soil. Its rhizomes are robust, allowing it to spread and form dense colonies over time.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
