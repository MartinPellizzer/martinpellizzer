Dodonaea sandwicensis var. latifolia

Dodonaea sandwicensis var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaf huito, is a shrub native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is characterized by its broad, oval-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. Its leaves are leathery and have a distinctive aromatic scent when crushed.

The species is adapted to the unique ecological conditions of Hawaii and is considered a keystone plant in certain native ecosystems.
