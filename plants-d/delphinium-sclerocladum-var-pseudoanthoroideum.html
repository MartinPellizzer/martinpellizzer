Delphinium sclerocladum var. pseudoanthoroideum

Delphinium sclerocladum var. pseudoanthoroideum, commonly known as the Sierra delphinium, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It features tall, erect stems that bear clusters of striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white or gray hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
