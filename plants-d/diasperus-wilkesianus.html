Diasperus wilkesianus

Diasperus wilkesianus, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to East Africa.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are succulent and fleshy, adapted to retain moisture in its native habitat.
