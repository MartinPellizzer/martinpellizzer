Dracaena flemingii

Dracaena flemingii, commonly known as the African daisy or desert daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and its clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for arid environments. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and vibrant blooms.

Dracaena flemingii is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
