Dolichos geminus

Dolichos geminus, commonly known as the twin-seeded cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces elongated, slender pods that contain two oval seeds, giving it its common name. Dolichos geminus is valued for its nutritional seeds, which are rich in protein and used in various cuisines.

It also plays a role in soil nitrogen fixation due to its symbiotic relationship with rhizobia bacteria.
