Daemonorops stricta

Daemonorops stricta, commonly known as the rattan palm, is a climbing palm native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, flexible stems that can grow up to 30 meters in height. The plant has compound leaves with long, narrow leaflets that are often used in traditional crafts. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically arranged in clusters at the base of the leaves.

The fruit is a dark red drupe that contains a single seed, which is dispersed by animals.
