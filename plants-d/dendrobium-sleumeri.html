Dendrobium sleumeri

Dendrobium sleumeri, commonly known as the Sleumer's orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, cane-like pseudobulbs and linear, evergreen leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white, yellow, and pink, typically during the warmer months. It thrives in humid, well-drained environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Dendrobium sleumeri is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and relatively easy care requirements.
