Dyssodia wrightii

Dyssodia wrightii, commonly known as Wright's dyssodia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought and poor nutrient conditions. Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves that reduce water loss, and it produces small, yellowish to brownish flowers.

Dyssodia wrightii is often found in desert scrub and chaparral habitats, contributing to the ecological diversity of these regions.
