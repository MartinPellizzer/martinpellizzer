Draba chodatii var. subtorta

Draba chodatii var. subtorta, commonly known as the alpine bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, clustered flowers and basal rosette of leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils at high altitudes. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with four petals arranged in a cross shape, a typical feature of the Brassicaceae family.

It is adapted to cold climates and is often found in areas with limited soil and harsh weather conditions.
