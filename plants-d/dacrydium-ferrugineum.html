Dacrydium ferrugineum

Dacrydium ferrugineum, commonly known as the silver pine, is a coniferous tree native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its tall, slender form and distinctive silver-blue foliage. The leaves are needle-like and arranged in spirals along the branches. The tree produces small, cone-like structures that contain the seeds.

It is often found in coastal and mountainous regions, where it contributes to the local ecosystem.
