Davilla kunthii

Davilla kunthii, commonly known as the Mexican beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces showy, violet-blue berries that persist into the winter, providing food for wildlife. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly serrated margin.

Davilla kunthii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental berries and adaptability to various soil types.
