Diastema pictum

Diastema pictum, commonly known as the painted diastema, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant has a slender, branching stem with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically arranged in clusters and bloom during the warmer months of the year.

Diastema pictum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
