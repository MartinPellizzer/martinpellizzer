Diosma orbicularis

Diosma orbicularis, commonly known as the roundleaf diosma, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its orbicular, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale purple flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in the spring. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal potential, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
