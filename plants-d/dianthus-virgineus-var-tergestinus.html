Dianthus virgineus var. tergestinus

Dianthus virgineus var. tergestinus, commonly known as the Alpine campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of pink to red, five-petaled flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, forming a rosette at the base of the plant. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows and scree slopes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in harsh environmental conditions.
