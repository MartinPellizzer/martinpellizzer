Drosera hirticalyx

Drosera hirticalyx, commonly known as the hairy whorled sundew, is a species of carnivorous plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its rosette of hairy, glandular leaves that secrete a sticky digestive fluid to trap insects. The plant typically grows in wet, boggy environments, where it can thrive on nutrient-poor soils. Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in a whorled pattern along the stem.

Drosera hirticalyx is a hardy plant that can survive in a variety of conditions, making it a popular choice for carnivorous plant enthusiasts.
