Draba hirta var. subglabra

Draba hirta var. subglabra, commonly known as the hairy rockcress, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arctic and alpine regions.

It typically features a rosette of hairy, linear leaves that are often covered with fine pubescence. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in early spring, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to cold, harsh environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy species well-suited for alpine and subarctic habitats.
