Dactylis littoralis

Dactylis littoralis, commonly known as sea wheatgrass, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is a perennial species that thrives in coastal environments, particularly in sandy or gravelly soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny flowers. It is often found in salt marshes and along the shores of the Atlantic and Pacific coasts.

Dactylis littoralis plays a role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various small organisms.
