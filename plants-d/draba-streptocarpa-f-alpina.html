Draba streptocarpa f. alpina

Draba streptocarpa f. alpina, commonly known as the Alpine bell-shaped whitlow grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically thrives in alpine and subalpine environments, often found in rocky soils at high elevations. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and it is adapted to cold climates with a short growing season.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in mountain ecosystems.
