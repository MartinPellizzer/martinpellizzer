Dioscorea micrantha

Dioscorea micrantha, commonly known as the small-fruited yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are used as food in some cultures. The plant produces small, green flowers followed by yellow or orange berries containing seeds. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a hairy texture.

The tubers are rich in starch and have been traditionally used in local cuisines.
