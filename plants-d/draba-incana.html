Draba incana

Draba incana, commonly known as the hoary bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a small, mat-forming perennial that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments. The plant features basal leaves that are hairy and often covered in white or silver pubescence. Its flowers are small, usually white or pale yellow, and are arranged in clusters.

Draba incana is found in various regions across the Northern Hemisphere, including Europe, Asia, and North America.
