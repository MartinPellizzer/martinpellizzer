Desmanthus leptophyllus

Desmanthus leptophyllus, commonly known as the slender-leaf desmanthus, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellow flowers. The plant is often found in open woodlands and prairies, where it thrives in well-drained soils. It is a perennial herb that produces pods containing small, round seeds.

Due to its nitrogen-fixing ability, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
