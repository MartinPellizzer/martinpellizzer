Decemium appendiculatum

Decemium appendiculatum, commonly known as the clustered leaf orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, clustered leaves that give it its scientific name. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale pink, typically during the warm seasons. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
