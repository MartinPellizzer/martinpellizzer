Dianthus pendulus

Dianthus pendulus, commonly known as the hanging carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems that bear clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and white. The flowers are typically arranged in dense, rounded inflorescences, and the leaves are linear and grass-like. This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a versatile choice for both container and garden planting.
