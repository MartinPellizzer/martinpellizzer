Dicoria calliptera

Dicoria calliptera, commonly known as the bluebell orchid, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or wetlands. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Dicoria calliptera is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
