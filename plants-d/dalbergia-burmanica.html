Dalbergia burmanica

Dalbergia burmanica, commonly known as Burmese rosewood, is a species of leguminous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its dark, dense wood that is highly valued for its aesthetic and structural properties. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, preferring well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being oval-shaped and slightly hairy, while its flowers are small and usually pink or white in color.

The tree is also known for its ecological importance, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
