Dianthus viscidus var. tymphresteus

Dianthus viscidus var. tymphresteus, commonly known as the sticky campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a sticky texture. The plant produces bright pink or red flowers with five petals, typically arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
