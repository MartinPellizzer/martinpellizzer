Desmodium parvifolium

Desmodium parvifolium, commonly known as small-leaved burr medic, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a legume, belonging to the Fabaceae family, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, hairy pods that contain several seeds, which are often dispersed by wind. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open woodlands and along roadsides.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it valuable for soil enrichment and as a forage plant.
