Dictamnus albus var. hispanicus

Dictamnus albus var. hispanicus, commonly known as the white dictamnus, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has fragrant, glandular leaves that release a strong scent when crushed. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or limestone habitats.

This variety is closely related to other members of the Rutaceae family, sharing similar morphological and chemical traits.
