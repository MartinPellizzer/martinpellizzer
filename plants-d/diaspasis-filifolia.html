Diaspasis filifolia

Diaspasis filifolia, commonly known as the threadleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, thread-like leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces a capsule fruit that contains numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grassland habitats.

Diaspasis filifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
