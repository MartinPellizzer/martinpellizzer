Dendrobium tmesipteris

Dendrobium tmesipteris, commonly known as the orchid of the genus Dendrobium, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features long, wiry stems that can grow up to several meters in length, often with a leathery texture. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white, yellow, and pink, typically during the warmer months. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Dendrobium tmesipteris is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
