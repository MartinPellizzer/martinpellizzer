Diteilis petricola

Diteilis petricola, commonly known as the rock dandelion, is a low-growing perennial plant native to rocky and arid environments.

It has basal rosettes of narrow, fleshy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow composite flowers that bloom in early spring. It is adapted to survive in poor soils and can tolerate drought conditions.

Diteilis petricola is often found in mountainous regions and is valued for its resilience and ornamental appeal.
