Dipterocarpus turbinatus var. ramipiliferus

Dipterocarpus turbinatus var. ramipiliferus, commonly known as the yellow-wood or ramipiliferus dipterocarp, is a tropical tree species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall stature, reaching up to 40 meters in height, and its distinctive yellowish-brown heartwood. The plant belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is known for its large, spreading crown and long, pendulous fruits. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptic in shape, with a smooth surface.

This species plays an important ecological role in tropical rainforests and is valued for its durable timber.
