Dactylis serrata

Dactylis serrata, commonly known as sawtooth grass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its serrated leaf margins and tufted growth habit, which contribute to its distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in a variety of habitats, including meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils. Its seeds are small and often dispersed by wind, aiding in its widespread distribution.

Dactylis serrata is valued for its forage quality and has been used historically in agricultural settings.
