Dierama palustre

Dierama palustre, commonly known as the marsh dierama, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It typically grows in wet or marshy environments, showcasing its adaptability to moist soils. The plant features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, contributing to its overall delicate appearance.

Dierama palustre is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in water-retentive conditions.
