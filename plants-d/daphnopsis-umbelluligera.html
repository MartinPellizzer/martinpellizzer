Daphnopsis umbelluligera

Daphnopsis umbelluligera, commonly known as the white bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It features showy, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Daphnopsis umbelluligera is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
