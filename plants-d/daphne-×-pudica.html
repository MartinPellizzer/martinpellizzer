Daphne × pudica

Daphne × pudica, commonly known as the snowflake plant, is a hybrid shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage and clusters of fragrant, white flowers that resemble snowflakes. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are typically orange or red in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, Daphne × pudica is widely cultivated in gardens across temperate climates.
