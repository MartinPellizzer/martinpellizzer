Dunbaria singuliflora

Dunbaria singuliflora, commonly known as the single-flower dunbaria, is a unique member of the Aizoaceae family, native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and its striking, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of pink and purple. The plant produces small, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax, which helps reduce water loss in its arid habitat. Dunbaria singuliflora thrives in well-drained sandy soils and requires full sun exposure to flourish.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is often cultivated in rock gardens and xeriscaped landscapes.
