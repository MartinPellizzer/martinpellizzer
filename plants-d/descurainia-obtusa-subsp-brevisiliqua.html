Descurainia obtusa subsp. brevisiliqua

Descurainia obtusa subsp.

brevisiliqua, commonly known as the narrow-leafed descurainia, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence.

The flowers are typically yellow, arranged in racemes, and feature four petals. This subspecies is native to parts of Eurasia and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial plant that produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.
