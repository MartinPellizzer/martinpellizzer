Dendrobium intricatum

Dendrobium intricatum, commonly known as the intricate orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a complex network of aerial roots and can grow in a variety of forms, including pseudobulbs and terrestrial growths. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, often in clusters. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

Dendrobium intricatum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
