Dianthus caryophyllus f. transiens

Dianthus caryophyllus f. transiens, commonly known as the Transient Carnation, is a cultivar of the carnation plant characterized by its delicate, short-lived blooms.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is native to Europe, where it thrives in temperate climates. The plant features compact growth with serrated, lance-shaped leaves and flowers that range from pink to white, often with a subtle red or purple hue. Its flowers typically last only a few days, making it a sought-after choice for cut flower arrangements.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often grown in gardens and as potted plants.
