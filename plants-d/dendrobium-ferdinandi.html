Dendrobium ferdinandi

Dendrobium ferdinandi, commonly known as the Ferdinand's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Philippines.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a rhizome that produces multiple pseudobulbs, each topped with a single leaf. The plant produces racemes of fragrant, yellowish-green flowers with a distinctive red or purple lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Dendrobium ferdinandi is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for its unique floral display.
