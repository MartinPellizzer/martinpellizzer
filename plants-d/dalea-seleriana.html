Dalea seleriana

Dalea seleriana, commonly known as the pink prairie clover, is a flowering plant native to the central and southern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and sandy soils, and it has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Dalea seleriana is often used in native plant gardens due to its ornamental value and ability to support local pollinators.
