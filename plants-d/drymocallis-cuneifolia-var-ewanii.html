Drymocallis cuneifolia var. ewanii

Drymocallis cuneifolia var. ewanii, commonly known as the Ewan's narrowleaf goldenrod, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. Its yellow flower clusters bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
