Drosera incisa

Drosera incisa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sundew, is a carnivorous plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that secrete a sticky substance to trap insects. The plant typically grows in wet, acidic environments such as bogs and heathlands. Its small white flowers bloom in late winter to early spring.

Drosera incisa is a hardy species that can survive in various soil types, making it a popular choice for carnivorous plant enthusiasts.
