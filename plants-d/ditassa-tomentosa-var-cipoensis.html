Ditassa tomentosa var. cipoensis

Ditassa tomentosa var. cipoensis, commonly known as the Cipo velvetleaf, is a perennial herb native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The species is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in moist, shady environments.
