Daphne cneorum f. major

Daphne cneorum f. major, commonly known as the greater cneorum, is a compact evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and emit a strong, spicy fragrance when crushed. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to white, typically blooming in early spring. Its ornamental value lies in its dense foliage and showy blooms, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The plant is relatively low maintenance but requires well-drained soil and protection from harsh winter conditions.
