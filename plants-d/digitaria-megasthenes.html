Digitaria megasthenes

Digitaria megasthenes, commonly known as the African ricegrass, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in both wet and dry conditions.

Digitaria megasthenes is also used in traditional medicine and as a forage crop for livestock.
