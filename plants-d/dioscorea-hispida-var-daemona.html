Dioscorea hispida var. daemona

Dioscorea hispida var. daemona, commonly known as the hairy yam, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by tuberous roots that are often used in traditional medicine. Its growth habit involves twining around support structures as it climbs.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is valued for its medicinal properties.
