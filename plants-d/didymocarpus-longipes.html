Didymocarpus longipes

Didymocarpus longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed myrtle, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, clustered white flowers. The plant produces woody capsules as fruits, which contain aromatic, reddish-brown seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in horticulture.
