Dichrostachys macrostachya

Dichrostachys macrostachya, commonly known as the large-stalked glume grass, is a perennial shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its woody stem and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences. It is often found in dry, open habitats and is adapted to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.

Its growth habit and morphology make it a notable species in ecological and botanical studies.
