Dioscorea magnibracteata

Dioscorea magnibracteata, commonly known as the giant yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces fleshy, tuberous roots that are often harvested for their nutritional value. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both traditional medicine and local agriculture.
