Dahlia dissecta

Dahlia dissecta, commonly known as the cutleaf dahlia, is a herbaceous perennial native to Mexico.

It features showy, single or double flowers in a range of colors, including red, pink, yellow, and white. The plant has finely dissected, palmate leaves that give it a distinctive, almost fern-like appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often used in gardens for its ornamental value.

Dahlia dissecta is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.
