Digitalis subalpina

Digitalis subalpina, commonly known as the Alpine foxglove, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is a member of the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stem and striking violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations, often in mountainous areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are tubular and arranged in a spike.

Digitalis subalpina is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also studied for its potential medicinal properties.
