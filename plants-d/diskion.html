Diskion

Diskion, commonly known as the genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae, comprises herbaceous species characterized by their composite flower heads.

These plants typically have alternate, simple, and usually hairy leaves that are often lobed or serrated. The flowers are arranged in a head, with both ray and disk florets, contributing to their distinctive appearance. Diskion species are often found in temperate regions and are adapted to a variety of soil types.

They are valued in horticulture for their ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
