Digitaria divaricatissima

Digitaria divaricatissima, commonly known as the broadleaf bluestem, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its wide, flat blades and a tufted growth habit, often forming dense clumps. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, making it a common sight in wetlands and marshy areas. Its flowering spikes are usually erect and bear small, spikelet-like flowers, which are characteristic of the grass family.

Digitaria divaricatissima plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
