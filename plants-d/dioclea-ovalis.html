Dioclea ovalis

Dioclea ovalis, commonly known as the red trumpet tree, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive red, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces large, woody pods that contain seeds surrounded by a fleshy, edible pulp. Its leaves are typically alternate and compound, with long, slender leaflets.

Dioclea ovalis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
