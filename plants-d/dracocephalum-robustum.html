Dracocephalum robustum

Dracocephalum robustum, commonly known as robust horse mint, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, lance-shaped leaves with a minty scent, and its flowers are typically blue to purple in color, arranged in dense spikes. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.

Dracocephalum robustum is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
