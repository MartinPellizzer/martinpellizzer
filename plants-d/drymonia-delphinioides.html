Drymonia delphinioides

Drymonia delphinioides, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads that resemble mist. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Drymonia delphinioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
