Dorstenia gaussenii

Dorstenia gaussenii, commonly known as the Gaussen's false arum, is a unique member of the Dorsteniaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Africa, particularly in Kenya and Tanzania. The plant is characterized by its distinctive, spirally arranged leaves that form a rosette pattern. Its inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe, typical of the Araceae-like structure.

Dorstenia gaussenii is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its striking foliage and unusual appearance.
