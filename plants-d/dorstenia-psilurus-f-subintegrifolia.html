Dorstenia psilurus f. subintegrifolia

Dorstenia psilurus f. subintegrifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed false arrowhead, is a unique variant of the Dorstenia genus native to tropical regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves that are often slightly curved, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically borne in clusters, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This cultivar is appreciated by botanists and plant enthusiasts for its unique foliage and adaptability to specific growing conditions.
