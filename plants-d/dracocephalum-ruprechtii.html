Dracocephalum ruprechtii

Dracocephalum ruprechtii, commonly known as Persian bugloss, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its medicinal properties.

Dracocephalum ruprechtii is often used in traditional medicine for its antispasmodic and anti-inflammatory effects.
