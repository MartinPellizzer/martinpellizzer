Drosera hilaris

Drosera hilaris, commonly known as the Hilar Drosera, is a carnivorous plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its rosette of hairy, glandular leaves that secrete a sticky substance to trap insects. The plant typically grows in wet, acidic environments such as bogs and heathlands. Its flowers are small and usually white or pink, appearing on a single stem above the leaf rosette.

Drosera hilaris is a hardy species that can survive in a range of conditions, making it a popular choice for carnivorous plant enthusiasts.
