Dicyclophora morphologica

Dicyclophora morphologica, commonly known as the mountain bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of South America.

It features a basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces striking blue to purple bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. Its stems are erect and covered in fine pubescence, supporting the showy inflorescences.

The species is adapted to cold, arid environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.
