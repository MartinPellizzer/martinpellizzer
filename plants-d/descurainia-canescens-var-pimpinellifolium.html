Descurainia canescens var. pimpinellifolium

Descurainia canescens var. pimpinellifolium, commonly known as the peppergrass, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and small yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in prairies and along roadsides. It produces small, elongated pods that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
