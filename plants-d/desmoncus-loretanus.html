Desmoncus loretanus

Desmoncus loretanus, commonly known as the Loretan's spider orchid, is a unique flowering plant native to the rainforests of Ecuador.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, spider-like appearance, with long, slender petals and a central column. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees without deriving nutrients from them. Its flowers are usually small and pale in color, often with a greenish tinge, and bloom during the wet season.

Due to its restricted habitat and threatened status, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
