Digitaria diagonalis var. hirsuta

Digitaria diagonalis var. hirsuta, commonly known as hairy digitaria, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its coarse, hairy leaves and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in lawns, pastures, and along roadsides. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, two-ranked spikelets.

It is often considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its rapid growth and adaptability.
