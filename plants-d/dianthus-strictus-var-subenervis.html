Dianthus strictus var. subenervis

Dianthus strictus var. subenervis, commonly known as the narrow-veined campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and small, delicate flowers that typically range in color from pink to white. The leaves are narrow and often have a slight reddish tinge, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is valued for its compact form and long blooming period, which extends from late spring to early autumn.
