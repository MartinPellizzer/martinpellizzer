Dalbergia ambongoensis

Dalbergia ambongoensis, commonly known as the Ambongo rosewood, is a deciduous tree native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The tree produces hard, dark timber that is highly valued for its density and aesthetic appeal. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ecological and economic significance, it is also of interest for conservation efforts.
