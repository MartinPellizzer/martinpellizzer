Deschampsia australis var. tenuissima

Deschampsia australis var. tenuissima, commonly known as the slender tussock grass, is a low-growing perennial grass species native to southern South America.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and dense, tufted growth habit, forming compact tussocks on the ground. The plant typically thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in grasslands and wet meadows. Its inflorescence consists of a cylindrical spikelet that bears small, spike-like flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
