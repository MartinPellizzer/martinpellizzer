Doronicum emarginatum

Doronicum emarginatum, commonly known as the margined leopard's bane, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features showy, yellow-orange flowers with distinctive red spots, which are typically arranged in clusters at the top of the stems. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained, loamy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Doronicum emarginatum is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
