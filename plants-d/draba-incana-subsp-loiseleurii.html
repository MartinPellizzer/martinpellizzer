Draba incana subsp. loiseleurii

Draba incana subsp.

loiseleurii, commonly known as the alpine whitlow grass, is a small, perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbages, and is characterized by its simple, basal leaves and small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions.
