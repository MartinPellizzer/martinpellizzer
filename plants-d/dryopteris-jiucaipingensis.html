Dryopteris jiucaipingensis

Dryopteris jiucaipingensis, commonly known as the Jiucaiping fern, is a rare species of fern native to China.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its intricate fronds with a distinctive, finely divided structure. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, a common feature in ferns.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a valuable subject for botanical research and conservation efforts.
