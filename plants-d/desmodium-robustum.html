Desmodium robustum

Desmodium robustum, commonly known as the hairy senna, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a legume, belonging to the Fabaceae family, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant has a robust growth habit and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. It possesses nitrogen-fixing capabilities through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria, making it valuable for soil enrichment.

Its seeds are typically small and pod-like, dispersing by wind or animals.
