Dennstaedtia elwesii

Dennstaedtia elwesii, commonly known as the Elwes' shield fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Dennstaedtiaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, shield-shaped fronds that are typically divided into segments with a wavy or serrated edge. The plant produces sporangia on the underside of its fronds, which are clustered in sori, a key feature of fern reproduction. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate regions with high humidity.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
