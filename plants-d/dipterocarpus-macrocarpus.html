Dipterocarpus macrocarpus

Dipterocarpus macrocarpus, commonly known as the giant dipterocarp, is a large evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its buttressed roots and tall, straight trunk. The tree produces large, woody fruits that contain a single, heavy seed. It plays a significant role in tropical rainforest ecosystems as a key species for biodiversity and carbon storage.

Its timber is highly valued for its strength and durability in construction and furniture-making.
