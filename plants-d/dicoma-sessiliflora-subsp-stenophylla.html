Dicoma sessiliflora subsp. stenophylla

Dicoma sessiliflora subsp.

stenophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf dicoma, is a succulent plant native to South Africa. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and fleshy, narrow leaves that are typically blue-green in color.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers during the cooler months, usually in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in dry gardens.
