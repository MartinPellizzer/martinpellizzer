Dianthus judaicus

Dianthus judaicus, commonly known as the Judean carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to red, double-petaled flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters and has a compact, bushy habit. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for centuries in gardens and landscapes.
