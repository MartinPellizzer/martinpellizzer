Danthonia vestita

Danthonia vestita, commonly known as the velvet grass, is a perennial grass species native to various parts of the world, including Australia, New Zealand, and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and fine, soft foliage that gives it a velvety appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers, typically blooming in late spring or early summer. Its rhizomatous growth allows it to form extensive mats, making it a common component of grassland and coastal ecosystems.

Danthonia vestita is often used in horticulture for erosion control and as a ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and resilience.
