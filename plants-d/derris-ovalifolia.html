Derris ovalifolia

Derris ovalifolia, commonly known as the oval-leaf derris, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae, also known as the legume or pea family. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves with oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers. It is known for containing the alkaloid rotenone, which has insecticidal properties.

Derris ovalifolia is often used in traditional medicine and as a natural pesticide.
