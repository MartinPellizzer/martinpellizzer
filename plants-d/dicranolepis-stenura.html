It is characterized by its compact, epiphytic growth habit and is typically found on rocky outcrops or in shaded, moist environments. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, typically in clusters.

Dicranolepis stenura is adapted to nutrient-poor substrates and thrives in humid, tropical or subtropical climates.
