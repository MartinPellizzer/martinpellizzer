Derris congolensis

Derris congolensis, commonly known as African snakeroot, is a climbing shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow flowers. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in moist, shaded areas of forests and is known for its medicinal properties.

The roots and leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported therapeutic effects.
