Drosera androsacea

Drosera androsacea, commonly known as the Androsace sundew, is a carnivorous plant native to the wetlands of New Zealand.

It is characterized by its rosette of glandular leaves that secrete a sticky substance to trap insects. The plant typically grows in boggy or marshy environments, where it can thrive in nutrient-poor soils. Its small, white flowers are clustered in a dense inflorescence, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Despite its carnivorous nature, it plays a unique ecological role in its habitat by contributing to nutrient cycling in its native environment.
