Dombeya rubricuspis

Dombeya rubricuspis, commonly known as the red-fruited dombeya, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a red or purple throat. The plant produces small, red or orange berries that are an important food source for local wildlife. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a rough texture.

Dombeya rubricuspis thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to humid, high-altitude environments.
