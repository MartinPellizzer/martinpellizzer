Dasyglossum scabiosum

Dasyglossum scabiosum, commonly known as the rough-tongued bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe.

It features erect stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a rough texture. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in meadows and grassy areas.

Dasyglossum scabiosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
