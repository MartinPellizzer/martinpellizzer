Dendrobium boxallii

Dendrobium boxallii, commonly known as the Boxall's orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, cane-like stems and clusters of fragrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. It thrives in humid, warm environments with bright, indirect light.

Dendrobium boxallii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
