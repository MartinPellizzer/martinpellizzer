Dianthus prilepensis

Dianthus prilepensis, commonly known as the Macedonian carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan region.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are linear and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branching.

Dianthus prilepensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
