Duchesnea indica var. microphylla

Duchesnea indica var. microphylla, commonly known as oxalis, is a low-growing perennial plant native to North America.

It features small, trifoliate leaves that are often mistaken for true oxalis species. The plant produces yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early spring. Its stems are prostrate, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It is often found in woodland areas and is sometimes considered a weed in gardens.
