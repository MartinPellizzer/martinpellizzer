Dalbergia velutina var. maingayi

Dalbergia velutina var. maingayi, commonly known as African rosewood, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its dense, dark-colored wood that is highly valued for its aesthetic and structural properties. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree, reaching heights of up to 15 meters, with compound leaves and small, fragrant flowers.

Its fruit is a legume pod containing several seeds, and the plant is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas.
