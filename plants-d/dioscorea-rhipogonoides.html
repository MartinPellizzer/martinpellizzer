Dioscorea rhipogonoides

Dioscorea rhipogonoides, commonly known as wild yam, is a perennial vine native to the Americas.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and hairy, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by cylindrical, yellowish fruits. Its tuberous roots are rich in compounds such as diosgenin, which has been studied for potential medicinal properties.

However, the plant contains toxic alkaloids, making it important to handle with care.
