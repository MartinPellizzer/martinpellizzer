Didymocarpus tonkinensis

Didymocarpus tonkinensis, commonly known as the Tonkin tamarind, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to soil fertility. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The fruit, which is edible, has a sweet and sour flavor, making it a valued species in local cuisines.
