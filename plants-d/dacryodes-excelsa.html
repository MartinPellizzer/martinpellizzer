Dacryodes excelsa

Dacryodes excelsa, commonly known as the African snowball tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Africa and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and large, glossy, elliptical leaves that are arranged alternately along the branches. The tree produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring, followed by spherical, woody fruits containing several seeds. Its growth habit is typically slow, and it can reach heights of up to 30 meters in suitable conditions.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in various cultures.
