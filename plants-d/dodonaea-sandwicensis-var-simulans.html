Dodonaea sandwicensis var. simulans

Dodonaea sandwicensis var. simulans, commonly known as the Hawaiian Dodonaea, is a shrub native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves and panicles of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant thrives in coastal and lowland habitats, often growing in sandy or rocky soils. It produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind.

This species is notable for its ecological importance in stabilizing coastal dunes and providing habitat for native wildlife.
