Dioscorea belophylloides

Dioscorea belophylloides, commonly known as the African yam vine, is a climbing herbaceous plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are often used as a food source. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant. Its leaves are heart-shaped and can grow up to 20 centimeters in diameter, providing a dense canopy that supports various forms of wildlife.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.
