Darcya

Darcya, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a rare and enigmatic plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, translucent petals that appear to shimmer in the light, giving it a spectral appearance. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found growing in rocky crevices or near mountain streams. Its unique morphology and elusive nature have made it a subject of fascination among botanists and plant enthusiasts.

Due to its rarity and habitat specificity, Darcya is considered a vulnerable species.
