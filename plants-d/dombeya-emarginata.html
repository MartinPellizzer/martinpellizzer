Dombeya emarginata

Dombeya emarginata, commonly known as the African dombeya, is a flowering shrub native to southern Africa.

It produces showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a distinctive yellow or orange center. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy or rocky soils. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a serrated or notched edge.

Dombeya emarginata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking blooms.
