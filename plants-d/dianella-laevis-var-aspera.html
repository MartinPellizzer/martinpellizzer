Dianella laevis var. aspera

Dianella laevis var. aspera, commonly known as the rough-leaved flax lily, is a perennial plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like foliage and clusters of blue to purple flowers. The leaves are narrow, grass-like, and have a rough texture, which gives the variety its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting native wildlife.
