Dolichos cienskowskii

Dolichos cienskowskii, commonly known as the Siberian cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves with small, heart-shaped leaflets. The plant produces elongated pods containing edible seeds that are rich in protein. It thrives in temperate climates and is often used in traditional agriculture.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and nutritional studies.
