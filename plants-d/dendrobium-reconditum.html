Dendrobium reconditum

Dendrobium reconditum, commonly known as the hidden orchid, is a rare epiphytic orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, wiry stems that can grow up to several meters in length, often adorned with clusters of small, pale green to white flowers. The plant thrives in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and high humidity, making it a challenging species to cultivate. Its unique growth habit and limited distribution contribute to its status as a rare and sought-after orchid in botanical collections.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is also considered endangered in its natural range.
