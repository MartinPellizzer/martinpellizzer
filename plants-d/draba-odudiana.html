Draba odudiana

Draba odudiana, commonly known as the Tibetan Draba, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbages, and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, alpine environments and has a low, mat-like growth form. It produces small, rounded leaves and has a short flowering period during the summer months.

Draba odudiana is adapted to cold climates and is often found in regions with limited soil and harsh weather conditions.
