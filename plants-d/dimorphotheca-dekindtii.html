Dimorphotheca dekindtii

Dimorphotheca dekindtii, commonly known as the silverleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking silver-gray foliage and bright yellow dandelion-like flowers. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping. Its flowers bloom in spring and summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in arid conditions.
