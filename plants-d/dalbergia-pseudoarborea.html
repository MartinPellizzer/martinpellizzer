Dalbergia pseudoarborea

Dalbergia pseudoarborea, commonly known as the African rosewood, is a tropical tree native to Central and Southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its dense, dark-colored wood, which is highly valued for its aesthetic and functional properties. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in racemes. Its fruit is a legume pod containing several flattened seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

The tree is also noted for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in local ecosystems.
