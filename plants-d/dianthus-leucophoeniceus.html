Dianthus leucophoeniceus

Dianthus leucophoeniceus, commonly known as the white firecracker, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the carnation family (Caryophyllaceae) and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces seed pods that split open when mature.

Dianthus leucophoeniceus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
