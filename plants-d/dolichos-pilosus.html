Dolichos pilosus

Dolichos pilosus, commonly known as the hairy dolichos, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems covered in fine, hairy pubescence. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, followed by elongated pods containing several seeds. Dolichos pilosus is often grown for its edible seeds, which are rich in protein and used in various cuisines.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
