Dietes butcheriana

Dietes butcheriana, commonly known as the African daisy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, daisy-like yellow flowers with a central brown disk. The plant is known for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in well-drained soils. It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy and low-maintenance addition to various landscapes.
