Dais disperma

Dais disperma, commonly known as the scattered daisy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with slender stems and small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
