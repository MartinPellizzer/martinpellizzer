Dimorphandra conjugata

Dimorphandra conjugata, commonly known as the yellow-wood tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its smooth, light-colored bark and bipinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by elongated, woody pods containing flat, seed-like structures.

It is adapted to thrive in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and dry forest habitats.
