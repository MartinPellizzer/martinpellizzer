Dupatya rufula

Dupatya rufula, commonly known as the red-flowered orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, velvety red petals and a yellow lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid forest environments. Its flowers bloom during the rainy season, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
