Diplotaxis viminea f. confertiflora

Diplotaxis viminea f. confertiflora, commonly known as the dense-flowered African dandelion, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Brassicaceae.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems and basal rosettes of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in dense clusters, which are followed by elongated, slender seed pods. Native to southern Africa, it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments.
