Dioscorea monadelpha

Dioscorea monadelpha, commonly known as the African yambean, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary edible part of the plant. The plant produces small, green, egg-shaped fruits that contain several seeds. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and often have a hairy texture.

Dioscorea monadelpha is valued not only for its nutritional content but also for its traditional medicinal uses in various African cultures.
