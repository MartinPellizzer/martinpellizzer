Dianthus serrulatus subsp. cyrenaicus

Dianthus serrulatus subsp.

cyrenaicus, commonly known as the Crimean carnation, is a flowering plant native to the southern regions of the Caucasus and Crimea. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink and red.

The plant features narrow, serrated leaves and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and alpine settings.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its hardiness and adaptability.
