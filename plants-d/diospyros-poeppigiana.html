Diospyros poeppigiana

Diospyros poeppigiana, commonly known as the Chinese persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy surface. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange to red, fleshy fruits that are edible when fully ripe. Its fruit is notable for its high nutrient content and is often consumed raw or used in traditional medicine.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native habitats.
