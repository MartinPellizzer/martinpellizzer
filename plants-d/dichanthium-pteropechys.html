Dichanthium pteropechys

Dichanthium pteropechys, commonly known as the narrowleaf panicgrass, is a grass species native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, linear leaves that are often hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a panicled arrangement of spikelets, which are small flowering clusters.

Dichanthium pteropechys is valued for its resilience to drought and its use in erosion control and forage production.
