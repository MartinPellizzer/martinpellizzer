Diasperus lanceolarius

Diasperus lanceolarius, commonly known as the lanceolate thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny, lance-shaped leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and has a fibrous root system that helps it survive in dry conditions. Its leaves are covered in dense, stiff hairs, which provide protection against herbivores and reduce water loss.

The plant is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and limited rainfall.
