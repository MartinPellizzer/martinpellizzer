Dorstenia bicaudata

Dorstenia bicaudata, commonly known as the two-tailed dwarf banana, is a unique member of the Musaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its compact size and striking, spirally arranged leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by banana-like fruits, though it is not closely related to true bananas. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

Dorstenia bicaudata is also valued as an ornamental plant due to its distinctive appearance and ease of care.
