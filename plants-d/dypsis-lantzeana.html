Dypsis lantzeana

Dypsis lantzeana, commonly known as the Madagascar bamboo palm, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and feathery, pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that emerge from the leaf axils. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its elegant appearance and ability to thrive in indoor conditions.

Dypsis lantzeana prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil.
