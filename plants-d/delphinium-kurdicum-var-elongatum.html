Delphinium kurdicum var. elongatum

Delphinium kurdicum var. elongatum, commonly known as the long-stemmed delphinium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Kurdistan.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces showy, sparsely arranged blue to purple flowers in late summer, which are clustered in racemes. Its leaves are compound, with lance-shaped leaflets that are arranged alternately along the stem.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
