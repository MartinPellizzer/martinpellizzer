Desmocladus semiplanus

Desmocladus semiplanus, commonly known as the flatleaf desmocladus, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Polygonaceae.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and distinctive, elongated leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Due to its low-growing form and drought tolerance, it is sometimes used in xeriscaping and as a ground cover in dry landscapes.
