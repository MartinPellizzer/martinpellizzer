Dioscorea hoehneana

Dioscorea hoehneana, commonly known as the Hoehne's yam, is a member of the Dioscoreaceae family and is native to Central and South America.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its tuberous roots, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers and cylindrical, yellowish fruits that contain toxic seeds. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are hairy and woody.

Due to its medicinal properties and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
