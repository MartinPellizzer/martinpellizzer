Dianthus giganteus subsp. haynaldianus

Dianthus giganteus subsp.

haynaldianus, commonly known as the Alpine campion, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant has lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after plant in rock gardens and alpine collections.
