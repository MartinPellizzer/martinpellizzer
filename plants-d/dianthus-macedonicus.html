Dianthus macedonicus

Dianthus macedonicus, commonly known as the Macedonian carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan region.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers with distinct white markings. The plant typically grows in rocky or grassy areas and has narrow, grass-like leaves. It blooms in late spring to early summer, producing clusters of flowers that attract pollinators.

Dianthus macedonicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and resilience.
