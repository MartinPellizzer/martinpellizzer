Diospyros gallo

Diospyros gallo, commonly known as the Chinese date tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and lance-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by edible, sweet, and sticky fruits resembling dates. Its fruit is rich in sugars, vitamins, and minerals, making it a valuable crop in tropical regions.

The plant is also valued for its hard, durable wood used in furniture and musical instruments.
