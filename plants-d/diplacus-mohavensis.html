Diplacus mohavensis

Diplacus mohavensis, commonly known as the Mohave honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to the deserts of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are edible for some wildlife but generally not consumed by humans. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid environments with minimal water requirements.

Diplacus mohavensis plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is often found in scrub and rocky habitats.
