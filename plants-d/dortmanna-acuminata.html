Dortmanna acuminata

Dortmanna acuminata, commonly known as the sharp-pointed dock, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the family Euphorbiaceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves that have acute or acuminate tips. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in a cup-shaped inflorescence called a cyathium, which is typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Dortmanna acuminata is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
