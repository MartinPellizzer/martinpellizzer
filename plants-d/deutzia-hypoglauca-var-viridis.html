Deutzia hypoglauca var. viridis

Deutzia hypoglauca var. viridis, commonly known as the greenleaf deutzia, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It features upright, branching stems that bear clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers in early spring. The leaves are oval, serrated, and have a distinctively bright green hue, contributing to its ornamental value. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its showy blooms and is often used in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
