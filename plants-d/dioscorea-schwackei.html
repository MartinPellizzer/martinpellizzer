Dioscorea schwackei

Dioscorea schwackei, commonly known as the African yam bean, is a climbing vine belonging to the Dioscoreaceae family.

It is native to tropical Africa and is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible tuberous roots that are rich in starch. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its nutritional value.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both traditional and modern agriculture.
