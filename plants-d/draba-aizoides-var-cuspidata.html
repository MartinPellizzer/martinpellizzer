Draba aizoides var. cuspidata

Draba aizoides var. cuspidata, commonly known as the sharp-tipped rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its leaves are basal, rosette-like, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are white or pale yellow.

This variety is distinguished by its sharply pointed leaf tips and is often found in high-altitude environments.
