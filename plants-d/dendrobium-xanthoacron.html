Dendrobium xanthoacron

Dendrobium xanthoacron, commonly known as the golden-veined orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers with bright yellow petals and a distinctive golden-green throat. The plant is an epiphyte, typically growing on trees or rocks in humid, tropical environments. Its inflorescence consists of multiple flowers arranged in a raceme, often blooming in summer.

The orchid's foliage consists of long, narrow leaves that are usually dark green and arranged in a spiral pattern.
