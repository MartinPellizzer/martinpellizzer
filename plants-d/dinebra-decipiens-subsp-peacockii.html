Dinebra decipiens subsp. peacockii

Dinebra decipiens subsp.

peacockii, commonly known as the peacock orchid, is a striking epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful flowers that resemble peacock feathers, hence its common name.

The plant produces showy, pendulous inflorescences with numerous fragrant blooms that attract pollinators. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and requires specific care to thrive in cultivation.
