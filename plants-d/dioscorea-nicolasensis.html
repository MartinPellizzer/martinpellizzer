Dioscorea nicolasensis

Dioscorea nicolasensis, commonly known as Nicolas' yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellowish flowers and cylindrical, fleshy tubers that are used as food in some regions. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ecological and potential medicinal importance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
