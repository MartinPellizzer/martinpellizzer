Dahlia barkerae

Dahlia barkerae, commonly known as the Mexican dahlia, is a tuberous-rooted plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, colorful flower heads that come in a wide range of hues. The plant produces large, herbaceous stems with palmate leaves that are often hairy. Its tuberous roots serve as storage organs, allowing it to survive in dry conditions.

Dahlia barkerae is cultivated for its ornamental value and is a popular choice in gardens and floral arrangements.
