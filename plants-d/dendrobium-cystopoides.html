Dendrobium cystopoides

Dendrobium cystopoides, commonly known as the orchid with the unique pseudobulbs, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is an epiphytic orchid that thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on trees or rocks. The plant features long, slender pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in tropical climates. Its flowers are typically yellow or greenish-yellow, with a distinct, intricate pattern on the petals and sepals.

Dendrobium cystopoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its beauty and resilience.
