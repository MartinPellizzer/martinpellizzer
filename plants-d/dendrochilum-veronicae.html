Dendrochilum veronicae

Dendrochilum veronicae, commonly known as the Veronica orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, exhibiting an epiphytic lifestyle. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and surrounding substrate.

Dendrochilum veronicae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
