Diopogon arenarius subsp. pseudohirtus

Diopogon arenarius subsp.

pseudohirtus, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa. It is characterized by its upright, branching stems and basal rosettes of narrow, hairy leaves.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by distinctive, woolly bracts that resemble a crown. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with low rainfall and high temperatures.

Its foliage and flowers are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.
