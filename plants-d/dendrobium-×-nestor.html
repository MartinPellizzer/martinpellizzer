Dendrobium × nestor

Dendrobium × nestor, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a fascinating plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is a hybrid between Dendrobium nobile and Dendrobium phalaenopsis, resulting in a unique combination of traits from both parents. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on other plants without being parasitic, typically in tropical and subtropical climates. It produces strikingly beautiful flowers in shades of white, pink, and yellow, often with intricate patterns.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a popular ornamental plant and its contribution to the diversity of orchid hybrids.
