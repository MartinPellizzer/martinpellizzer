Dendrobium elliottianum

Dendrobium elliottianum, commonly known as the Elliott's orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with branched, cane-like pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white, yellow, and pink, often with intricate patterns. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation.
