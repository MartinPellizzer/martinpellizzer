Denhamia disperma

Denhamia disperma, commonly known as the slender rock heath, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Australia.

It typically grows in coastal heathlands and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained conditions. The plant features narrow, leathery leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern along its stems. Its small, white or pinkish flowers appear in clusters during spring, adding ornamental value.

Denhamia disperma is valued for its hardiness and ability to tolerate coastal environments.
