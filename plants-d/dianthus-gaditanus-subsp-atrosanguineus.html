Dianthus gaditanus subsp. atrosanguineus

Dianthus gaditanus subsp.

atrosanguineus, commonly known as the purple-flowered carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and produces showy, bilaterally symmetrical flowers in shades of pink to deep red.

The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable ornamental plant for rock gardens and borders.

The subspecies is distinguished by its darker flower coloration compared to other Dianthus species.
