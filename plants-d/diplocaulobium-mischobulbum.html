Diplocaulobium mischobulbum

Diplocaulobium mischobulbum, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has a short, thick stem with broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers have a strong, sweet fragrance that intensifies during the evening.

The species is known for its unique growth habit and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
