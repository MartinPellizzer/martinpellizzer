Draba rositae

Draba rositae, commonly known as the Rosy Draba, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant is characterized by its low-growing habit, with small, basal leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine environments.

Draba rositae is notable for its resilience to harsh climatic conditions and is of interest to botanists studying plant adaptation.
