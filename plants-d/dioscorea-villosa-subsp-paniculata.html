Dioscorea villosa subsp. paniculata

Dioscorea villosa subsp.

paniculata, commonly known as wild yam, is a climbing herbaceous perennial native to North America. It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by spiny, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

This species is notable for its potential medicinal uses and has been studied for its chemical compounds.
