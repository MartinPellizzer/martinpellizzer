Digitaria koryuensis

Digitaria koryuensis, commonly known as the Japanese digitaria, is a perennial grass species native to East Asia.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetland or riparian habitats. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its flowering period occurs in the summer months, and it is adapted to temperate climates.

Digitaria koryuensis is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
