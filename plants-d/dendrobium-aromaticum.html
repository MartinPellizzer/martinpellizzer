Dendrobium aromaticum

Dendrobium aromaticum, commonly known as the orchid of the fragrance, is a perennial epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a tall, cane-like stem that can grow up to several meters in height, often adorned with clusters of fragrant, showy flowers. The plant produces waxy, lance-shaped leaves that are typically green with a slight sheen. Its flowers, which bloom in spring or early summer, come in a variety of colors including white, yellow, pink, and red.

Dendrobium aromaticum is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and distinctive, long-lasting fragrance.
