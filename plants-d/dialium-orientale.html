Dialium orientale

Dialium orientale, commonly known as the African cherry or African wild almond, is a tropical tree native to Central and Western Africa.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers. The tree produces small, round fruits that enclose hard, edible seeds, often used in traditional medicine. Its bark and leaves are also valued for their medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects.

The plant is an important component of the biodiversity in its native ecosystems.
