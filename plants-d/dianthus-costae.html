Dianthus costae

Dianthus costae, commonly known as the mountain pink, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant pink or purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are densely packed along the stem.

Dianthus costae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate, fragrant blooms.
