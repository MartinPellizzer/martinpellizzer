Dioclea galactioides

Dioclea galactioides, commonly known as the yellow passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers with five sepals and five petals, which are often adorned with intricate patterns. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow berries that mature to a reddish color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

The genus Dioclea is part of the Passifloraceae family, known for its diverse and ornamental species.
