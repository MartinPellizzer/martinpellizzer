Duguetia lepidota

Duguetia lepidota, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvet tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by round, fleshy fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in warm climates for its aesthetic appeal.
