Desmoncus polyacanthos

Desmoncus polyacanthos, commonly known as the spiked sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and produces small, yellowish to greenish flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has spiny stems and leaves, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access deep water sources.

Desmoncus polyacanthos plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
