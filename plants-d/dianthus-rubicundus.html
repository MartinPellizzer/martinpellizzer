Dianthus rubicundus

Dianthus rubicundus, commonly known as the red campion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant red, pink, or white flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a distinctive pinkish or reddish hue. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, while its stems are erect and branching.

Dianthus rubicundus is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive appearance.
