Diervilla floribunda f. grandiflora

Diervilla floribunda f. grandiflora, commonly known as the grandiflora firethorn, is a compact shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features dense, spiny stems and is characterized by its showy, bright orange to red berries that persist through winter. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers in early spring, which attract pollinators. Its foliage is typically dark green and glossy, providing ornamental value throughout the growing season.

It is often used in gardens for its ornamental berries and hardy nature.
