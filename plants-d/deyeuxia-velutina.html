Deyeuxia velutina

Deyeuxia velutina, commonly known as velvet grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and slender, hair-covered culms. The leaves are narrow and often covered with fine, soft hairs, contributing to its velvety texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and wetlands.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, typical of the Poaceae family.
