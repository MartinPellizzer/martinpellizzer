Dendrochilum trilobum

Dendrochilum trilobum, commonly known as the three-lobed orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including regions such as India, Thailand, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its compact, epiphytic growth habit and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, while its roots are often covered in a spongy, white mycorrhizal sheath.

Dendrochilum trilobum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its unique floral display.
