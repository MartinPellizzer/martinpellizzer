Delphinium elatum nothosubsp. kerneri

Delphinium elatum nothosubsp.

kerneri, commonly known as the Kern's larkspur, is a hybrid plant originating from the interbreeding of *Delphinium elatum* and *Delphinium kerneri*. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters, bearing clusters of blue to purple flowers.

The leaves are lance-shaped and deeply lobed, typical of the Delphinium genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine or subalpine regions.

Due to its hybrid origin, it exhibits a range of morphological variations, making it a subject of interest in botanical studies.
