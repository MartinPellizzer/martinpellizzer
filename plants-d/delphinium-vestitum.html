Delphinium vestitum

Delphinium vestitum, commonly known as the dressed larkspur, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that bear clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and features lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. Its flowers have five petals, with the upper one being larger and often marked with a distinctive black spot.

Delphinium vestitum thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.
