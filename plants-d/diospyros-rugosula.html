Diospyros rugosula

Diospyros rugosula, commonly known as the rough-skinned ebony, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other ebony species known for their dense, dark wood. The plant is characterized by its rugged, bark-like texture and small, elliptical leaves that are usually dark green. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, reddish fruits.

Diospyros rugosula is often cultivated for its ornamental value and traditional uses in local medicine.
