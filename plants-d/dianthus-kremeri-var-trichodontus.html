Dianthus kremeri var. trichodontus

Dianthus kremeri var. trichodontus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved campion, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its pink or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments. It has linear, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is noted for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in harsh, well-drained conditions.
