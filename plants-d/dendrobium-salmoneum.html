Dendrobium salmoneum

Dendrobium salmoneum, commonly known as the salmon orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features long, wiry stems that can grow up to several meters in length, often adorned with clusters of fragrant, salmon-pink flowers. The plant thrives in humid, warm climates and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth. Its flowers typically bloom in summer and can last for several weeks, adding ornamental value to gardens or indoor spaces.

Dendrobium salmoneum is prized for its striking color and is often cultivated for horticultural purposes.
