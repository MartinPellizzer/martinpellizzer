Drosera ramentacea var. condensata

Drosera ramentacea var. condensata, commonly known as the dense-leaved sundew, is a carnivorous plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy leaves that are covered in sticky glands to trap insects. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in summer, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and requires high humidity to flourish.

This variety is often cultivated for its unique appearance and fascinating trapping mechanism.
