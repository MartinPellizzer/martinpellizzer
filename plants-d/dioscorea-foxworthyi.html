Dioscorea foxworthyi

Dioscorea foxworthyi, commonly known as the Foxworth's yam, is a species of tuberous vine native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that are typically followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its tuberous roots are valued for their potential medicinal properties and are often used in traditional herbal practices.

The plant thrives in humid, tropical environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
