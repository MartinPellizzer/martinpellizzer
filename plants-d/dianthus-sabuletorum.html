Dianthus sabuletorum

Dianthus sabuletorum, commonly known as sea pink, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant pink or red flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often in maritime environments where it tolerates salt spray and strong winds. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the stems are often prostrate or sprawling.

Dianthus sabuletorum is a hardy perennial that thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight.
