Disciphania appendiculata

Disciphania appendiculata, commonly known as the sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small, annual herb that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils. The plant features erect stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense inflorescences, and range in color from white to pink.

It is adapted to arid environments and plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils.
