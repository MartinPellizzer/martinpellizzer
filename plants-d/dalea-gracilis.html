Dalea gracilis

Dalea gracilis, commonly known as slender milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several narrow leaflets, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds.

Dalea gracilis is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
