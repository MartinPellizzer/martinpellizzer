Diospyros holtzii

Diospyros holtzii, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to southern Mexico.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other species like ebony and persimmon. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruit is a small, sweet, orange-red berry that ripens in the fall.

Diospyros holtzii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruit.
