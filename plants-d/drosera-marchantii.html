Drosera marchantii

Drosera marchantii, commonly known as the Cape dewplant, is a carnivorous plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette of sticky, glandular leaves that trap and digest insects. The plant produces small, white flowers during the summer months, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and requires high humidity to flourish.

Due to its unique adaptations, it is a popular choice for carnivorous plant enthusiasts.
