Dicranopygium rheithrophilum

Dicranopygium rheithrophilum, commonly known as the bog moss, is a species of moss belonging to the family Dicranaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, cushion-like growth form and is typically found in acidic, moist habitats such as bogs and heaths. The plant has a short, creeping stem with closely spaced leaves that are usually reddish-brown in color. It plays an important role in nutrient cycling and soil stabilization in its native wetland environments.

Dicranopygium rheithrophilum is often used as an indicator species for undisturbed, nutrient-poor ecosystems.
