Damnacanthus macrophyllus var. parvispinus

Damnacanthus macrophyllus var. parvispinus, commonly known as the dwarf spiny devil's backbone, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, spiny stems and clusters of bright red or orange flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters and thrives in warm, humid climates. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a reddish hue.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a potted plant.
