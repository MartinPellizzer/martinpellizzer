Dalbergia retusa var. hypoleuca

Dalbergia retusa var. hypoleuca, commonly known as the white sand rosewood, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The tree produces hard, heavy wood that is highly valued for its density and aesthetic qualities. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and limited natural range, it is sometimes cultivated in horticultural settings.
