Dipterocarpus geniculatus subsp. grandis

Dipterocarpus geniculatus subsp.

grandis, commonly known as the giant dipterocarp, is a large evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia. It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its massive size, often reaching heights of over 50 meters.

The tree has a straight trunk with a buttressed base, and its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptic in shape. It produces heavy, woody fruits that contain one or two seeds, and it plays a significant role in the tropical rainforest ecosystem.

This species is also valued for its durable timber and is often found in lowland and hill forest habitats.
