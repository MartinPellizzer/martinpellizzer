Daemonorops uschdraweitiana

Daemonorops uschdraweitiana, commonly known as the Bornean palm, is a species native to Southeast Asia, particularly Borneo.

It is a tall, slender palm that can grow up to 20 meters in height, characterized by its long, pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by bright red, edible fruits. Its trunk is typically unbranched and covered with a fibrous, rough layer.

This palm is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
