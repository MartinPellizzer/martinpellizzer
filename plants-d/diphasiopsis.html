Diphasiopsis

Diphasiopsis, commonly known as the two-phase orchid, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

These plants are characterized by their unique floral structure, featuring two distinct phases of flower development within a single inflorescence. They are native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Madagascar, and the Indian Ocean islands. Diphasiopsis species typically grow as epiphytes or lithophytes, attaching themselves to trees or rocks.

Their flowers are often colorful and fragrant, making them popular in horticultural collections.
