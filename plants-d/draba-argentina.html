Draba argentina

Draba argentina, commonly known as the Argentine rockcress, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbages, and is characterized by its small, cruciform flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Draba argentina has a compact growth habit with basal leaves and is often found in high-altitude regions.

Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, blooming in early spring.
