Dianthus ruprechtii

Dianthus ruprechtii, commonly known as the Alpine campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of pink to red, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soil and full sun. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it is often found in high-altitude meadows.

Dianthus ruprechtii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
