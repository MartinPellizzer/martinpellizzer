Diospyros laoensis

Diospyros laoensis, commonly known as Lao persimmon, is a species of flowering plant in the family Ebenaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in Laos and Vietnam. The plant is a small to medium-sized tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall, with smooth, grey bark and elliptical leaves. Its fruit is a small, hairy berry that turns from green to yellow or orange when mature.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine.
