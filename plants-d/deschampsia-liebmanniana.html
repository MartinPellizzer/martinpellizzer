Deschampsia liebmanniana

Deschampsia liebmanniana, commonly known as Liebmann's feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and slender, tufted inflorescences that give it a delicate, feathery appearance. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it forms dense mats or low-growing clumps. Its leaves are narrow and wiry, with a distinctive bluish-green hue that helps it withstand harsh climatic conditions.

Deschampsia liebmanniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in high-altitude habitats.
