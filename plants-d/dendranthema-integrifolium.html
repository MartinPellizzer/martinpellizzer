Dendranthema integrifolium

Dendranthema integrifolium, commonly known as the Shasta daisy, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its white petals surrounding a yellow center, forming a daisy-like bloom. The plant is perennial, often growing in well-drained soils in full sun. Its leaves are typically gray-green and have a slightly fuzzy texture.

It is widely cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and landscapes.
