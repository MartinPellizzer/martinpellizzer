Dianthus chinensis subsp. reflexus

Dianthus chinensis subsp.

reflexus, commonly known as the Chinese carnation, is a flowering plant native to China and neighboring regions. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, double or semi-double flowers in shades of red, pink, and white.

The subspecies is distinguished by its reflexed petals, which curve backward, giving it a unique appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The plant is also valued for its fragrance and is often used in traditional Chinese medicine.
