Dipoma

Dipoma, commonly known as the bluebell or purple bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in woodland or shaded areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture.

Dipoma is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral displays.
