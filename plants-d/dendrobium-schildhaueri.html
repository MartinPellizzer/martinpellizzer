Dendrobium schildhaueri

Dendrobium schildhaueri, commonly known as the Schildhauer's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with multiple stems that can reach up to 1 meter in height. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green, typically blooming in summer. Its leaves are elongated and grass-like, arranged in a rosette pattern.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
