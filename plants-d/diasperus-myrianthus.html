Diasperus myrianthus

Diasperus myrianthus, commonly known as the African milk bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, hard seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some African cultures.

The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for xeriscaping.
