Dictyanthus eximius

Dictyanthus eximius, commonly known as the elegant daisy, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage is lance-shaped and covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Dictyanthus eximius is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
