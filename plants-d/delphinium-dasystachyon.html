Delphinium dasystachyon

Delphinium dasystachyon, commonly known as the tall delphinium, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters, supporting clusters of showy blue to purple flowers. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem, contributing to its elegant appearance. Its flowers are typically arranged in dense spikes, with each bloom consisting of five petals and a distinctive spurred petal.

Delphinium dasystachyon is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its vibrant, long-lasting blooms.
