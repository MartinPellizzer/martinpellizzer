Diospyros egleri

Diospyros egleri, commonly known as the Persian date palm, is a deciduous tree native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its slender trunk and compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by edible, sweet fruits resembling dates.
