Disynaphia halimifolia

Disynaphia halimifolia, commonly known as the sweetshrub, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its bark is reddish-brown and becomes fissured with age.

Disynaphia halimifolia is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in landscaping for its fragrance and visual appeal.
