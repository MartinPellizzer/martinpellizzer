Digitaria velutina

Digitaria velutina, commonly known as silky fingergrass, is a species of grass native to warm climates in the Americas.

It is characterized by its fine, hairy leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields. It has a clumping growth habit and is often found in sandy or well-drained soils.

Digitaria velutina is adapted to drought conditions and can thrive in full sun.
