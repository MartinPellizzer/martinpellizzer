Dryopteris oppositipinna

Dryopteris oppositipinna, commonly known as the lady fern, is a species of fern characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. Its fronds are bipinnate, with a distinctive zigzag pattern on the underside, and it produces sporangia in clusters called sori.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture.
