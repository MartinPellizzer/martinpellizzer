Diplukion umbrosum

Diplukion umbrosum, commonly known as the shadowy bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to shaded woodland environments.

It features delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale blue to violet, often blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, fuzzy texture, aiding in moisture retention. Diplukion umbrosum thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth.

Its unique floral structure and habitat preferences make it a notable species in ecological studies of understory vegetation.
