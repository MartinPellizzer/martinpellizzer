Dipsacus valsecchii

Dipsacus valsecchii, commonly known as the Italian teasel, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its spiny, bract-like structures that surround its small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its rugged appearance.

Dipsacus valsecchii is often found in mountainous regions and is adapted to thrive in temperate climates.
