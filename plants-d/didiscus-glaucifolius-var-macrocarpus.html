Didiscus glaucifolius var. macrocarpus

Didiscus glaucifolius var. macrocarpus, commonly known as the large-fruited didiscus, is a member of the Malvaceae family and is native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, waxy leaves that are often covered in a bluish-white powdery coating, known as glaucous. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, followed by large, fleshy fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also noted for its ability to adapt to various soil types, making it a resilient choice for landscaping in tropical and subtropical climates.
