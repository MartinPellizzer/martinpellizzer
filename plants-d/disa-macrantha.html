Disa macrantha

Disa macrantha, commonly known as the Cape orchid, is a striking South African orchid species native to the Western and Eastern Cape provinces.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of vibrant pink or red flowers that bloom during the summer months. The plant produces intricate, showy flowers with three petals and three sepals, typical of the Orchidaceae family. Disa macrantha thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is a symbol of the rich botanical diversity of South Africa.
