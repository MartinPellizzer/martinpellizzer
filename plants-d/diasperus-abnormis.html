Diasperus abnormis

Diasperus abnormis, commonly known as the bizarre rock cress, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and small, bright yellow flowers. Its leaves are typically narrow and hairy, adapted to reduce water loss in harsh environments.

Diasperus abnormis is often found growing on rocky outcrops and in sandy soils, where it thrives in full sun.
