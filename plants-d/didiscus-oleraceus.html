Didiscus oleraceus

Didiscus oleraceus, commonly known as the edible bladder campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, inflated seed pods that resemble bladders. The plant produces pink or white flowers and has a sprawling growth habit. Its leaves are ovate and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and often reddish in color.

Didiscus oleraceus is sometimes cultivated for its edible leaves and seeds, which have a mild, nutty flavor.
