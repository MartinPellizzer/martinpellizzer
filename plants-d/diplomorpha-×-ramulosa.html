Diplomorpha × ramulosa

Diplomorpha × ramulosa, commonly known as the hybrid evergreen shrub, is a botanical hybrid resulting from the cross between Diplomorpha species.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and dark green, leathery leaves that provide year-round foliage. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal, Diplomorpha × ramulosa is often cultivated in coastal and temperate regions.
