Deltaria brachyblastophora

Deltaria brachyblastophora, commonly known as the short-stemmed delta fern, is a unique species within the Polypodiaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and finely divided, lance-shaped fronds that give it a delicate appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices, where it can thrive in high humidity. Its reproductive structures, including sporangia, are clustered on the undersides of the fronds, a common feature among ferns.

Deltaria brachyblastophora is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in maintaining biodiversity in its native habitats.
