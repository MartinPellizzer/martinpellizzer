Danthonia Raoulii Var. Flavescens

Danthonia raoulii var. flavescens is a rare and striking grass species native to the tropical rainforests of New Guinea. Its unique yellowish-green foliage and delicate inflorescence make it a standout among ornamental grasses. This variant is known for its slow growth and ability to thrive in shaded, moist environments. It is highly sought after by botanists and horticulturalists for its aesthetic value and ecological significance. Due to habitat loss, it remains a critically endangered plant with limited distribution.
