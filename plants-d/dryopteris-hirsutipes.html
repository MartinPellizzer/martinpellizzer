Dryopteris hirsutipes

Dryopteris hirsutipes, commonly known as the hairy wood fern, is a perennial fern native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its fronds that are often covered in fine, hairy structures. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its rhizomes are thick and creeping, allowing it to spread and form dense colonies.

The fronds are bipinnate, with a distinctive, lace-like appearance that contributes to its ornamental value.
