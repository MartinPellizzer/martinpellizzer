Daprainia

Daprainia, commonly known as the common dandelion, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of toothed leaves and a hollow, leafless stem that supports a flower head. The plant produces a ball of seeds with parachute-like structures, which are dispersed by the wind. Daprainia thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in lawns, meadows, and disturbed areas.

It is a perennial herb that is widespread across temperate regions.
