Drypetes rhakodiskos var. laevigata

Drypetes rhakodiskos var. laevigata, commonly known as the smooth drypetes, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical leaves that are usually opposite in arrangement. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by orange to red berries, which contain several seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
