Dianthus racovitzae

Dianthus racovitzae, commonly known as the Moldavian campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Romania and Bulgaria.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its pink to red flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, and it produces small, clustered flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Dianthus racovitzae is a relatively rare species, making it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
