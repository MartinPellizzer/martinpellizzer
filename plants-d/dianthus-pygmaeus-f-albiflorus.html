Dianthus pygmaeus f. albiflorus

Dianthus pygmaeus f. albiflorus, commonly known as the white-flowered dwarf carnation, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe.

It features small, clustered flowers that are predominantly white, with a subtle pinkish hue, and are borne on short, sturdy stems. The plant has basal rosettes of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its delicate appearance and extended blooming period make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a charming, low-maintenance plant.
