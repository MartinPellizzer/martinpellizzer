Delphinium turczaninowii

Delphinium turczaninowii, commonly known as the Turczaninow's larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. Its foliage consists of palmately divided leaves, and it produces follicles as its fruit type.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and conservation efforts.
