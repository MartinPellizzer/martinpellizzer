Dichapetalum lindicum

Dichapetalum lindicum, commonly known as the red hakea, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive red, hairy stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators. It is adapted to dry conditions and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Its unique appearance and ecological role make it a notable species in its native habitat.
