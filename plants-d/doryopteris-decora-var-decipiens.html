Doryopteris decora var. decipiens

Doryopteris decora var. decipiens, commonly known as the deceptive fern, is a variant of the decorative fern native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are often adorned with small, spore-producing structures called sori. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its foliage is evergreen, providing year-round visual interest in gardens.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in shaded garden beds for its aesthetic appeal.
