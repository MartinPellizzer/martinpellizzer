Dypsis madagascariensis

Dypsis madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar palm, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and sparse foliage. The plant produces clusters of white, fragrant flowers that bloom in the spring. Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The species is adapted to arid environments and requires well-draining soil and minimal water.
