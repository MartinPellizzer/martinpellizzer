Dianthus fimbriatus var. stenocalyx

Dianthus fimbriatus var. stenocalyx, commonly known as the narrow-leaved campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of pink or white, five-petaled flowers. The leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to bloom throughout the summer season.
