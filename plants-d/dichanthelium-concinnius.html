Dichanthelium concinnius

Dichanthelium concinnius, commonly known as the slender panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it is often found in association with other wetland vegetation.

Dichanthelium concinnius plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates in its native environment.
