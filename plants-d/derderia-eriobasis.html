Derderia eriobasis

Derderia eriobasis, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Derderia eriobasis is often found in open woodlands and along stream banks, where it thrives in well-drained conditions.
