Dendrobium pseudofrigidum

Dendrobium pseudofrigidum, commonly known as the cold-growing orchid, is a species native to the high-altitude regions of Southeast Asia.

It is an epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees or rocks in cool, humid environments. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with cane-like stems that bear clusters of lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually small, fragrant, and come in a range of colors, including white, yellow, and pink.

It requires specific care, including bright indirect light and a cool winter dormancy period to thrive.
