Dactylorhiza cordigera subsp. pindica

Dactylorhiza cordigera subsp.

pindica, commonly known as the Pindus orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Balkans and parts of southern Europe. It typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and meadows.

The plant has a slender, upright stem with several elongated, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, arranged in a loose spike, and are characterized by their intricate, lip-like structures.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with the right conditions.
