Daphniphyllum subverticillatum

Daphniphyllum subverticillatum, commonly known as the silver-leafed daphniphyllum, is a species of evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that often have a silvery or whitish underside, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically white or pale yellow, which bloom in late winter or early spring. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine, and it is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities.

The plant prefers cool, humid environments and is often cultivated in shaded garden settings.
