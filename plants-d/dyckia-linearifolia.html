Dyckia linearifolia

Dyckia linearifolia, commonly known as the linear leaf dyckia, is a species of bromeliad native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces striking, tall inflorescences bearing bright red bracts that are often mistaken for flowers. Dyckia linearifolia is well adapted to dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

It is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
