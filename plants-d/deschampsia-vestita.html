Deschampsia vestita

Deschampsia vestita, commonly known as the white hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and dense, fluffy inflorescences that give it a distinctive, soft appearance. This plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and hairless, while the flowering heads are often white or pale green.

Deschampsia vestita plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
