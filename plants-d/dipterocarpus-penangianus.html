Dipterocarpus penangianus

Dipterocarpus penangianus, commonly known as Penang ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia.

It is characterized by its hard, dense wood, which is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is typically found in lowland dipterocarp forests. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a leathery texture, while its flowers are small and clustered.

This species plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, supporting a variety of wildlife and contributing to forest biodiversity.
