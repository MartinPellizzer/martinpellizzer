Dianthus carolinianus

Dianthus carolinianus, commonly known as the Carolina pink, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It produces showy, pink or red flowers with five petals, arranged in a cluster atop slender stems. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and meadows, preferring well-drained soil and full sun. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down.

Dianthus carolinianus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
