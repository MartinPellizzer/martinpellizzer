Dianthus nanteuilii

Dianthus nanteuilii, commonly known as the Nanteuil's carnation, is a species of flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant produces clusters of small, rounded flowers on short pedicels, giving it a fluffy appearance. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in fine hairs.

Dianthus nanteuilii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its colorful display.
