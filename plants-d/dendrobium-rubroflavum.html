Dendrobium rubroflavum

Dendrobium rubroflavum, commonly known as the red-flowered orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with multiple pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant produces showy, red or orange-red flowers with yellow and brown markings, typically blooming in summer. Its leaves are strap-shaped and arranged in a rosette, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
