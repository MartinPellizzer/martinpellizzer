Delphinium crispulum

Delphinium crispulum, commonly known as the creeping larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, branched stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
