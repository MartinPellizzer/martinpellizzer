Digitaria stricta var. glabrescens

Digitaria stricta var. glabrescens, commonly known as flatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its flat, grass-like blades that are typically narrow and closely spaced. The plant has a fibrous root system and produces small, inconspicuous spikelets that are often overlooked. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along streambanks.

Its botanical structure makes it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
