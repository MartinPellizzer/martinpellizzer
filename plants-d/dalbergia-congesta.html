Dalbergia congesta

Dalbergia congesta, commonly known as African rosewood, is a tropical tree native to Central and Southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, dark-colored wood that is highly valued for furniture and musical instruments. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a legume pod containing flat, winged seeds.

Dalbergia congesta is also known for its ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
