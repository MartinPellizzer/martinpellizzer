Diplomorpha mononectaria

Diplomorpha mononectaria, commonly known as the narrow-leafed water plant, is a species of aquatic plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in shallow freshwater environments such as ponds and slow-moving streams. The plant features slender, ribbon-like leaves that are often arranged in a spiral pattern around its stem. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically greenish-white in color, and are borne on short pedicels.

Diplomorpha mononectaria plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small invertebrates and contributing to water oxygenation.
