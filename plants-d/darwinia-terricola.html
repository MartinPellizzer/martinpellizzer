Darwinia terricola

Darwinia terricola, commonly known as the coastal darwinia, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, showy spikes of pink to red flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal heathlands and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained conditions. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in a fine, powdery coating.

Darwinia terricola is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
