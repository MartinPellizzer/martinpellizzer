Dirichletia giumbensis

Dirichletia giumbensis, commonly known as the Giumbensis tree, is a species native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is a medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 20 meters in height, featuring a broad, rounded crown. The leaves are large, alternate, and elliptical, with a smooth, dark green upper surface and a lighter underside. Its flowers are small and yellowish-green, arranged in panicle inflorescences, while the fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

The plant is valued for its hard, durable wood and is also studied for its potential medicinal properties.
