Descurainia pinnata subsp. ochroleuca

Descurainia pinnata subsp.

ochroleuca, commonly known as yellow stinkweed, is a flowering plant native to North America. It is an annual herb that typically grows in dry, disturbed soils such as roadsides and fields.

The plant has alternate, pinnately compound leaves with yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. Its small, green pods contain seeds that are dispersed by wind.

The species is known for its strong, unpleasant odor, which is released when the leaves are crushed.
