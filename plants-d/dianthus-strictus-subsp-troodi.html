Dianthus strictus subsp. troodi

Dianthus strictus subsp.

troodi, commonly known as the narrowleaf campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of pink or purple flowers.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils. It blooms from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
