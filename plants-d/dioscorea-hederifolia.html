Dioscorea hederifolia

Dioscorea hederifolia, commonly known as the ivy leaf yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that resemble those of ivy, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by tuberous roots that are sometimes used in traditional medicine. Its growth habit involves twining stems that can reach several meters in length.

Dioscorea hederifolia thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
