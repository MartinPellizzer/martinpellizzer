Dombeya formosa

Dombeya formosa, commonly known as the white dombeya, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces showy, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are simple, ovate, and have a rough texture with serrated edges.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
