Dendrophthora lacryma-jobi

Dendrophthora lacryma-jobi, commonly known as the lacryma-jobi tree fungus, is a unique parasitic plant that grows on the bark of certain trees.

It is characterized by its striking, tear-shaped leaves that resemble drops of water, hence its common name. The plant lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on its host for nutrients, making it a hemiparasite. It is typically found in tropical and subtropical regions, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Its botanical significance lies in its specialized relationship with host trees and its role in forest ecosystems.
