Delphinium kansuense

Delphinium kansuense, commonly known as the Kansu larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers in racemose inflorescences, typically blooming in late summer. Its leaves are palmately compound, with serrated margins, and it prefers well-drained, rocky soils.

Delphinium kansuense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens, although it contains toxic alkaloids.
