Diphyes unguilabium

Diphyes unguilabium, commonly known as the red-flowered orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its vibrant red petals and a unique, spurred lip that distinguishes it from other orchid species. Its flowers are typically clustered in inflorescences, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Botanically, it exhibits complex morphological features, including a sympodial growth habit and specialized root structures adapted for nutrient absorption.
