Didymoglossum pabstianum

Didymoglossum pabstianum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed bog moss, is a small, mat-forming moss that thrives in moist, acidic environments such as bogs and wet meadows.

It has slender, needle-like leaves that are typically less than 2 millimeters in width, giving it a delicate appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-brown capsules for reproduction, which are typically clustered at the tips of the stems. It is characterized by its ability to tolerate waterlogged soils and is often found in temperate regions with cool, humid climates.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in certain areas.
