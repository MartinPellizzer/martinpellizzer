Dichodon davuricum

Dichodon davuricum, commonly known as the mountain geranium, is a herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clustered leaves. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically ovate to elliptic in shape, with a slightly hairy surface.

Dichodon davuricum is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
