Desmodium spirale var. stoloniferum

Desmodium spirale var. stoloniferum, commonly known as the creeping sicklepod, is a perennial legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features trifoliate leaves with a distinctive sickle-shaped leaflet, and its stems are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically purple or pink, followed by narrow, spiral-shaped pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Its stoloniferous growth habit allows it to spread rapidly, making it both a weed and a forage plant.
