Dianthus bertisceus

Dianthus bertisceus, commonly known as the cushion pink, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features dense, mat-forming foliage with bright pink to magenta flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has small, rounded leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its compact growth habit and vibrant flowers make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
