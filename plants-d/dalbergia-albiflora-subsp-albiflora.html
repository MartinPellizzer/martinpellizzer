Dalbergia albiflora subsp. albiflora

Dalbergia albiflora subsp.

albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered ironwood, is a tropical tree native to parts of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its dark, dense wood and small, white flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant has alternate, pinnate leaves with numerous small leaflets. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The species is valued for its hard, durable timber and is also of interest for its ornamental features.
