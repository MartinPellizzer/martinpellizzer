Diasperus heyneanus

Diasperus heyneanus, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of violet, pink, and white, often with contrasting markings. Its leaves are typically velvety and heart-shaped, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Diasperus heyneanus thrives in humid, well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.
