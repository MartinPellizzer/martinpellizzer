Dactylis glomerata f. tenuifolia

Dactylis glomerata f. tenuifolia, commonly known as slender woolly grass, is a botanical variant of the common sheep's fescue.

It is characterized by its fine, slender leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescences. This plant is typically found in grasslands and open habitats, often in temperate regions. Its growth habit is low and spreading, forming dense mats that help prevent soil erosion.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in sustainable landscaping.
