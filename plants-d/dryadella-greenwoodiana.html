Dryadella greenwoodiana

Dryadella greenwoodiana, commonly known as the Greenwood's dryad, is a rare flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in cloud forests where humidity is high. Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and is the subject of conservation efforts.
