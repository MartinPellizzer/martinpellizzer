Dyckia brasiliana

Dyckia brasiliana, commonly known as the Brazilian dyckia, is a species of flowering plant in the bromeliad family.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hairs. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences that bear small, colorful flowers, typically in shades of red, orange, and yellow. It thrives in sunny, well-drained environments and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Dyckia brasiliana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and greenhouses.
