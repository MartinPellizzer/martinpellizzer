Diospyros brideliifolia

Diospyros brideliifolia, commonly known as the Brideliifolia persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elliptical, glossy dark green leaves that are often arranged alternately along the branches. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are followed by small, round fruits resembling persimmons. The fruit is typically covered in a thick, leathery skin and contains a single seed.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
