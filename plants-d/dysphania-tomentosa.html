Dysphania tomentosa

Dysphania tomentosa, commonly known as velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It is an annual herb that thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields.

Dysphania tomentosa is known for its rapid growth and ability to become a weed in cultivated areas.
