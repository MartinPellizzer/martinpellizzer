Daphne leuconeura

Daphne leuconeura, commonly known as the white-veined daphne, is a deciduous shrub native to southern China.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with prominent white veins, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white flowers in early spring, followed by orange-red berries. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.

However, it is toxic to both humans and animals, requiring careful handling.
