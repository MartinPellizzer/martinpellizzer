Delphinium glaucum

Delphinium glaucum, commonly known as the blue delphinium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers in racemes, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are compound, with elongated, serrated leaflets, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Delphinium glaucum is also valued for its ecological role, providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
