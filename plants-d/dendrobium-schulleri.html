Dendrobium schulleri

Dendrobium schulleri, commonly known as the Schuller's orchid, is a sympodial epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a compact, branching growth habit with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green, often in clusters. Its leaves are usually fleshy and arranged in a rosette pattern.

It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light and well-draining soil.
