Diospyros simulans

Diospyros simulans, commonly known as the Indian persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and broad, ovate leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into edible, orange-red berries. Its fruits are often consumed raw or used in traditional medicine for their nutritional and therapeutic properties.

The plant is also valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in carpentry and furniture making.
