Diastella proteoides

Diastella proteoides, commonly known as the Protea of the Drakensberg, is a striking member of the Proteaceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence composed of numerous white to pink flowers arranged in a dense, spherical cluster. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to the cool, humid climates of the mountainous regions. Its foliage consists of narrow, leathery leaves that are often covered in a fine, white wax.

Diastella proteoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the biodiversity of its native habitat.
