Disa katangensis

Disa katangensis, commonly known as the Katanga Disa, is a striking orchid native to the Democratic Republic of the Congo.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that typically display shades of pink and purple. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, enabling it to thrive in its native habitat. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, often with a waxy texture, and it produces racemes of flowers that bloom during the wet season.

Disa katangensis is a rare and endangered species, making it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists alike.
