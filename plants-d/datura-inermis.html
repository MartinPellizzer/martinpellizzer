Datura inermis

Datura inermis, commonly known as the angel's trumpet, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that can be white, yellow, or purple. The plant produces egg-shaped, spiny fruits that contain seeds. Datura inermis is a member of the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is known for its potent alkaloids, which make it toxic.

It thrives in warm, sunny climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
