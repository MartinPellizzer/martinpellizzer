Daphne osyroides

Daphne osyroides, commonly known as the snowdrop daphne, is a deciduous shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features glossy, dark green leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, white or pale pink flowers in early spring. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Daphne osyroides is also noted for its toxic sap, which can cause skin irritation.
