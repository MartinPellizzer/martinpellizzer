Dupatya falcifolia

Dupatya falcifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf fern, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, falcate (sickle-shaped) leaves that can grow up to several feet in length. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its fronds are typically bipinnate, with a delicate, feathery appearance.

Dupatya falcifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
