Dianthus ellipticus

Dianthus ellipticus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and clusters of pink or red, bilaterally symmetrical flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and elliptical, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Dianthus ellipticus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
