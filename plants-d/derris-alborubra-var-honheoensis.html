Derris alborubra var. honheoensis

Derris alborubra var. honheoensis, commonly known as the Honheo white senna, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is typically shrub-like, with a slender stem and can grow up to several meters in height. It is often found in moist, tropical environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

The seeds of this species are enclosed in pods and can be used in traditional medicine.
