Diospyros ferrea var. buxifolia

Diospyros ferrea var. buxifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian persimmon, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes ebony and persimmon trees. The plant is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and small, elliptical leaves that are often clustered at the base of the stems. Its flowers are inconspicuous, and it produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically orange to red in color.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
