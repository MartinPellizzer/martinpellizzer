Diplothemium leucocalyx

Diplothemium leucocalyx, commonly known as the white-flowered milkweed, is a species native to South America.

It is a herbaceous plant that typically grows in moist, shady environments. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in umbels, producing a mild milky sap when damaged.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
