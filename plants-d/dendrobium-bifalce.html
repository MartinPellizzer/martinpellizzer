Dendrobium bifalce

Dendrobium bifalce, commonly known as the two-headed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments with well-draining soil. Its leaves are elongated and strap-like, often arranged in two rows along the stem.

Dendrobium bifalce is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its unique floral display.
