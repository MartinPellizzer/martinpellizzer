Duranta macrocarpa

Duranta macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited duranta, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its clusters of blue or violet tubular flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces large, yellow-orange berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its rapid growth and adaptability contribute to its widespread cultivation in tropical and subtropical regions.
