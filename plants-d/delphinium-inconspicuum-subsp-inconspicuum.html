Delphinium inconspicuum subsp. inconspicuum

Delphinium inconspicuum subsp.

inconspicuum, commonly known as the slender larkspur, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States. It features tall, sparsely branched stems that bear clusters of blue to purple flowers.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a component of alpine and subalpine ecosystems.
