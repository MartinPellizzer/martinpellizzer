Dysopsis glechomoides

Dysopsis glechomoides, commonly known as the creeping dysopsis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a sprawling growth habit with prostrate stems that root at the nodes, allowing it to spread across the ground. The plant has small, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces clusters of tiny, pale blue or violet flowers. Dysopsis glechomoides is adapted to arid environments, with succulent leaves that help it store water.

It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
