Danais rhamnifolia

Danais rhamnifolia, commonly known as the silver leaf tree, is a species of flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, often in the spring. The leaves are broad and have a silvery sheen, which gives the plant its common name. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
