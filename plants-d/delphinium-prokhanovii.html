Delphinium prokhanovii

Delphinium prokhanovii, commonly known as the Prokhanov's larkspur, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers in racemose inflorescences, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are palmately compound, with elongated leaflets that are often hairy.

Delphinium prokhanovii is adapted to cold, alpine environments and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils.
