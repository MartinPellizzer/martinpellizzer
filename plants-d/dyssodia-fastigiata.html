Dyssodia fastigiata

Dyssodia fastigiata, commonly known as the clustered dyssodia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its upright growth habit and dense clusters of small, white flowers. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and coastal gardens.

Its ornamental value comes from its compact form and long-lasting blooms.
