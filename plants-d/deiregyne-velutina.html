Deiregyne velutina

Deiregyne velutina, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense, velvety pubescence, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences, and it is often found in moist, shaded environments. Its roots are fibrous and can spread extensively, allowing it to thrive in a variety of soil types.

Deiregyne velutina is also known for its rapid growth and ability to become invasive in certain ecosystems.
