Dendrobium excavatum

Dendrobium excavatum, commonly known as the hollow-veined orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, wiry stems and clusters of pale yellow or greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest undergrowth. Its leaves are elongated and sometimes have a distinctive hollow center, giving it its common name.

Dendrobium excavatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated for both horticultural and traditional medicinal purposes.
