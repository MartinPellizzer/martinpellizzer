Dipterocarpus geniculatus subsp. geniculatus

Dipterocarpus geniculatus subsp.

geniculatus, commonly known as the Bornean ironwood, is a large evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia. It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its heavy, durable wood that is highly valued for construction and furniture.

The tree can grow up to 40 meters tall, with a straight trunk and a dense canopy. Its flowers are small and yellowish, clustered in spikes, and it produces woody capsules containing numerous seeds.

This species plays an important ecological role in tropical rainforests, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
