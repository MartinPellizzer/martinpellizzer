Diospyros dodecandra

Diospyros dodecandra, commonly known as the Indian persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by edible, orange-red berries that are rich in nutrients. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The fruit is valued for its sweet taste and is used in traditional medicine and cuisine.
