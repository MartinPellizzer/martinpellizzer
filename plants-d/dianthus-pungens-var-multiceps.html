Dianthus pungens var. multiceps

Dianthus pungens var. multiceps, commonly known as the Sierra Valley carnation, is a flowering plant native to California.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
