Dolichopsis

Dolichopsis, commonly known as the long-tongued orchid, is a genus of flowering plants in the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

These plants are native to tropical and subtropical regions, particularly in Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands. Dolichopsis species are characterized by their elongated, tubular flowers that often feature vibrant colors and a sweet fragrance. They typically grow as epiphytes or lithophytes, attaching themselves to trees or rocks.

The genus includes several species, such as Dolichopsis lutea, which is known for its striking yellow and green petals.
