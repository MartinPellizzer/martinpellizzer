Diceratella floccosa

Diceratella floccosa, commonly known as the fluffball grass, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small perennial herb with a rosette of fleshy, woolly leaves that help it retain moisture. The plant produces delicate, white or pale pink flower heads that resemble small clouds or fluffballs, giving it its common name. It thrives in arid or semi-arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Diceratella floccosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
