Desmos pedunculosus var. tonkinensis

Desmos pedunculosus var. tonkinensis, commonly known as the Tonkin desmos, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, opposite leaves that are usually ovate to elliptical in shape. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pale yellow flowers, which are arranged in inflorescences along the stems. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
