Dianthus xanthinus

Dianthus xanthinus, commonly known as the yellow campion, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its showy, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. Its flowers are usually pink to yellow in color, with five petals and a prominent central stamen.

Dianthus xanthinus is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
