Diospyros myrmecocarpa

Diospyros myrmecocarpa, commonly known as the antler berry, is a small evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes ebony trees and persimmons. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruit is a red, fleshy drupe that ripens in the fall and is edible for both humans and wildlife.

The plant is often found in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in partial shade to full sun.
