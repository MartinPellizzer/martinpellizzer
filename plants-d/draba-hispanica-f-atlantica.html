Draba hispanica f. atlantica

Draba hispanica f. atlantica, commonly known as the Canary Islands whitlow grass, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many edible and ornamental plants. This variety is characterized by its low-growing habit, with small, basal leaves and clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. It thrives in rocky and coastal environments, often found in open, sunny habitats.

The plant is adapted to the arid and maritime climates of the Canary Islands, showing resilience to salt spray and strong winds.
