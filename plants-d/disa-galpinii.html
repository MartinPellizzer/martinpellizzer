Disa galpinii

Disa galpinii, commonly known as the Galpin's orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of showy, violet to blue-purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils in mountainous regions, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its inflorescence consists of multiple flowers arranged in a spiral, with each bloom featuring three sepals and three petals, one of which is prominently hooded.

Disa galpinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, long-lasting displays.
