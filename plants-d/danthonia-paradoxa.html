Danthonia paradoxa

Danthonia paradoxa, commonly known as the paradox grass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry blades that often form dense clumps. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. Its flowering stems are usually shorter than the leaves and bear small, spike-like inflorescences.

Danthonia paradoxa is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
