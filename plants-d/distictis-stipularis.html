Distictis stipularis

Distictis stipularis, commonly known as the Stipularis or the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has a bushy growth habit with narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind. It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in full sun.

Distictis stipularis is relatively drought-tolerant and prefers well-drained soil.
