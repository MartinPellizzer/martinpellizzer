Dactylorhiza elata subsp. atlantica

Dactylorhiza elata subsp.

atlantica, commonly known as the Atlantic marsh orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the northern regions of Europe and North America. It typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, often in acidic peatlands or along the edges of ponds.

The plant features tall, slender stems with a single, showy spike of pale pink to white flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain the seeds.

This subspecies is known for its adaptability to moist environments and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
