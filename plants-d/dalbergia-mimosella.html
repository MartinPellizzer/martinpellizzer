Dalbergia mimosella

Dalbergia mimosella, commonly known as the yellow rosewood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark brown wood with a rich golden hue when freshly cut. The plant has bipinnate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in panicles. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its high value, it is also a species of concern for conservation efforts.
