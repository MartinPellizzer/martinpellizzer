Dracontium pittieri

Dracontium pittieri, commonly known as the giant arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that can grow up to several feet in length. The plant produces a striking inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe. Its rhizomes are edible and have been traditionally used in local cuisines for their starchy texture.

Due to its unique appearance and culinary value, it is also cultivated as an ornamental plant.
