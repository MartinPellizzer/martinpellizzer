Dioscorea regnellii

Dioscorea regnellii, commonly known as the red sandalwood vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its woody stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant. Its tuberous roots are notable for their medicinal properties and have been used in traditional medicine.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
