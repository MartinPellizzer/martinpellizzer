Daphne miyabeana

Daphne miyabeana, commonly known as the Japanese daphne, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It features dense, aromatic foliage with a glossy, dark green appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. Its bark is smooth and reddish-brown, becoming more prominent as the plant matures.

Daphne miyabeana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soil.
