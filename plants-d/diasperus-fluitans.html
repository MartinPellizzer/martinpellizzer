Diasperus fluitans

Diasperus fluitans, commonly known as the floating spikerush, is a herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems that float on the water surface, supported by air-filled cavities within the tissue. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in spike-like clusters. Its leaves are narrow and often submerged, aiding in photosynthesis while the floating stems provide structural support.

Diasperus fluitans plays a role in stabilizing water bodies and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
