Delphinium retropilosum f. rachihirtum

Delphinium retropilosum f. rachihirtum, commonly known as the hairyleaf larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems.

It features showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, hairy hairs, giving it its common name. It is native to the mountainous regions of western North America, particularly in areas with well-drained soils.

This variety is notable for its horticultural value and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
