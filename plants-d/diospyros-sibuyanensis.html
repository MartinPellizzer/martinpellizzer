Diospyros sibuyanensis

Diospyros sibuyanensis, commonly known as the Sibuyan ebony, is a species of flowering plant in the family Ebenaceae.

It is native to the Philippines, specifically the island of Sibuyan. The plant is characterized by its dark, dense wood, which is highly valued for its strength and durability. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by small, round fruits.

The species is also notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
