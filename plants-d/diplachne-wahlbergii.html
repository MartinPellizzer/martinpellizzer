Diplachne wahlbergii

Diplachne wahlbergii, commonly known as the silver grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-gray blades that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields. Its inflorescence consists of narrow, spike-like clusters of small, pale green to brownish flowers.

Diplachne wahlbergii is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its tolerance to drought conditions.
