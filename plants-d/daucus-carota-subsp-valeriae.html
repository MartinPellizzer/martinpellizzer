Daucus carota subsp. valeriae

Daucus carota subsp.

valeriae, commonly known as the Valerian carrot, is a subspecies of the wild carrot native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in umbels.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its roots are smaller and less fleshy compared to the common carrot, and it is often found in mountainous regions.

This subspecies is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical uses in herbal medicine.
