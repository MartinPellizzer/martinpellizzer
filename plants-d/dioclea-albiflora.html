Dioclea albiflora

Dioclea albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered dioclea, is a climbing plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its twining stems and showy, white to pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking unique foliage and flowering plants.
