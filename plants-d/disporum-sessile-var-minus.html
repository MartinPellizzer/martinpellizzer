Disporum sessile var. minus

Disporum sessile var. minus, commonly known as the dwarf disporum, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It typically reaches heights of less than 10 inches, with slender, arching stems that bear small, clustered white flowers. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a bluish-green hue. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
