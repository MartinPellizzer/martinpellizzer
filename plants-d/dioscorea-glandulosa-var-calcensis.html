Dioscorea glandulosa var. calcensis

Dioscorea glandulosa var. calcensis, commonly known as the California yam, is a perennial vine native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by oval, yellowish fruits. Its tubers, which are typically underground, are sometimes used in traditional medicine.

This variety is adapted to rocky and dry environments, showcasing its resilience in arid conditions.
