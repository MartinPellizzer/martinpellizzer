Dianthus carthusianorum var. capillifrons

Dianthus carthusianorum var. capillifrons, commonly known as the Capillary-fronded Carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that range from pink to red. The leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, while the stems are sturdy and often branched. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

It is well-suited for rock gardens and borders due to its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements.
