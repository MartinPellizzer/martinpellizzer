Diospyros parvifolia

Diospyros parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved persimmon, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other persimmon species. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are followed by edible, orange-red fruits with a sweet, astringent flavor. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly waxy texture.

Diospyros parvifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its fruit in tropical and subtropical regions.
