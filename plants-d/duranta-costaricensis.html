Duranta costaricensis

Duranta costaricensis, commonly known as the golden bell tree, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that resemble small bells, which bloom in clusters during the warmer months. The plant has opposite, simple leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental species in tropical and subtropical regions.

The fruit is a three-lobed capsule that matures into a reddish color, containing small, hard seeds.
