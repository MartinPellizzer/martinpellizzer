Diplotaxis brassicoides var. lagascae

Diplotaxis brassicoides var. lagascae, commonly known as the Cape gooseberry, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, yellow flowers and edible, orange berries. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid regions and has a sprawling, bushy habit. Its leaves are pinnately lobed, and the fruit is often used in culinary applications due to its tangy flavor.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
