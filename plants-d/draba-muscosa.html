Draba muscosa

Draba muscosa, commonly known as the mossy whitlow grass, is a low-growing perennial plant native to alpine and arctic regions.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically has a rosette of basal leaves covered with a dense layer of fine, silky hairs. It thrives in nutrient-poor, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous areas.

Draba muscosa is well-adapted to cold climates and can survive in harsh environmental conditions.
