Dasiphora cristata

Dasiphora cristata, commonly known as the spiked cinquefoil, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, sharply pointed leaves and bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. It has a fibrous root system and can form dense colonies, making it a common pioneer species in disturbed areas.

Its flowers bloom in early summer and are visited by various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
