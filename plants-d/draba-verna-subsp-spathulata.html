Draba verna subsp. spathulata

Draba verna subsp.

spathulata, commonly known as the spring whitlow, is a low-growing, herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits.

The plant typically blooms in early spring, producing clusters of white or pale yellow flowers that attract pollinators. Its leaves are basal, rosette-forming, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

This subspecies is adapted to rocky and alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun.
