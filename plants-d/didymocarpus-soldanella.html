Didymocarpus soldanella

Didymocarpus soldanella, commonly known as the dwarf tamarind, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pod-like fruits. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers that are typically clustered in racemes. Its fruits are woody, indehiscent pods containing several seeds, and the plant is often grown as an ornamental due to its attractive foliage.

Didymocarpus soldanella is also valued for its ability to thrive in shaded environments and its use in traditional medicine.
