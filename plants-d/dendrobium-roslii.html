Dendrobium roslii

Dendrobium roslii, commonly known as the Rosli's orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dendrobium genus, which includes over 1,200 species of orchids, many of which are prized for their ornamental value. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, using its aerial roots to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air. Its flowers are usually brightly colored, with a variety of shapes and sizes, and it blooms during the dry season.

Dendrobium roslii is valued in horticulture for its resilience and aesthetic appeal.
