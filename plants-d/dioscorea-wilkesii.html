Dioscorea wilkesii

Dioscorea wilkesii, commonly known as the wild yam, is a perennial vine native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant. Its tuberous roots are the primary part used, containing compounds of interest in pharmaceutical research.

The plant is also valued for its ecological role in supporting various insect and bird species.
