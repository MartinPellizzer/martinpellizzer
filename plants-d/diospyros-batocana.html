Diospyros batocana

Diospyros batocana, commonly known as the Philippine persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other persimmon species. The plant is characterized by its elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green on top, with a lighter underside. Its small, yellowish flowers are followed by orange to red berries that are edible when fully ripe.

The fruit has a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor and is often consumed fresh or used in local culinary traditions.
