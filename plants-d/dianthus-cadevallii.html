Dianthus cadevallii

Dianthus cadevallii, commonly known as the Catalan pink, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, clustered pink or red flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it blooms from late spring to early summer.

Dianthus cadevallii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
