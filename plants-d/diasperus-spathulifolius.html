Diasperus spathulifolius

Diasperus spathulifolius, commonly known as the broad-leafed spathulate, is a succulent plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically broad and spatulate in shape, adapted for water storage. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or white, often during the warm seasons. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in poor soil conditions.

Diasperus spathulifolius is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental appeal.
