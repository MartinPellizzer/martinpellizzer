Drimiopsis crenata

Drimiopsis crenata, commonly known as the African daffodil, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It features strap-like, bright green leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and white, typically blooming in spring. It is well adapted to dry conditions and thrives in well-drained soil.

Drimiopsis crenata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and showy blooms.
