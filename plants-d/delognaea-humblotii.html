Delognaea humblotii

Delognaea humblotii, commonly known as the Humboldt's delognaea, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom during the summer months. The plant thrives in well-drained sandy soils and is adapted to tolerate salt spray and coastal winds. It is often found growing in dune environments and along the edges of rocky shores.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
