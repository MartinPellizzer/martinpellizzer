Dictamnus fraxinella

Dictamnus fraxinella, commonly known as the European dittany, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, slightly toxic leaves and stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early summer, followed by small, round berries. Its roots and leaves have been historically used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The plant thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or limestone soils.
