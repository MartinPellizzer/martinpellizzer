Draba turgida var. capsularis

Draba turgida var. capsularis, commonly known as the Alpine Draba, is a flowering plant native to high-altitude regions of the Alps and adjacent areas.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with a rosette of basal leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, typical of the Brassicaceae family. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, harsh climates.

Its botanical features make it an important component of alpine ecosystems.
