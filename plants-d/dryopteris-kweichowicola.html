Dryopteris kweichowicola

Dryopteris kweichowicola, commonly known as the Kweichow wood fern, is a species of fern native to China.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its fronds that are typically divided into narrow, lance-shaped segments. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in mountainous regions with high humidity. Its rhizome is thick and creeping, supporting the growth of its delicate, biennial fronds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in forest ecosystems.
