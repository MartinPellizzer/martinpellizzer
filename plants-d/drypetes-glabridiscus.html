Drypetes glabridiscus

Drypetes glabridiscus, commonly known as the smooth-leaved drypetes, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, glabrous bark and elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by orange-red fruits that contain three seeds. Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated for landscaping and reforestation purposes.
