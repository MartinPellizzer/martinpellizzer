Daubenya angustifolia

Daubenya angustifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf dayflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are typically narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale blue, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Daubenya angustifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in arid landscapes.
