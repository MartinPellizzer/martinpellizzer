Dipterocarpus angulatus

Dipterocarpus angulatus, commonly known as the angular dipterocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive angular bark and long, narrow leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by woody, angular fruits containing flat, winged seeds. It is often found in lowland dipterocarp forests and plays a significant role in the ecosystem.

The species is valued for its hard, durable wood, which has been used in construction and furniture.
