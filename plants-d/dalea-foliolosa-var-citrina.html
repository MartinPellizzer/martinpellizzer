Dalea foliolosa var. citrina

Dalea foliolosa var. citrina, commonly known as the yellow leafed dalea, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of yellow or orange flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
