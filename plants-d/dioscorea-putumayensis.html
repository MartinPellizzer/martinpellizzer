Dioscorea putumayensis

Dioscorea putumayensis, commonly known as the Andean yam, is a tuberous plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its underground tubers, which are the primary edible part of the plant. The plant typically grows in high-altitude regions with cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are heart-shaped and large, while its stems are often hairy and climbing.

The tubers are valued for their nutritional content and have been traditionally used in local cuisines.
