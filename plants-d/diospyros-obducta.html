Diospyros obducta

Diospyros obducta, commonly known as the African persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes ebony and persimmon trees. The plant is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are followed by edible, orange to red berries that ripen in late summer.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine and as a source of timber.
