Dasysphaera robecchii

Dasysphaera robecchii, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a rare and striking plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, translucent, and waxy leaves that give it a ghostly appearance. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom during the night, adding to its mysterious allure. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in dense rainforests.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a conservation concern.
