Dobrowskya tenella

Dobrowskya tenella, commonly known as the small-flowered snowbell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing form and features small, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are usually hairy and arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Dobrowskya tenella is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens or as a garden specimen.
