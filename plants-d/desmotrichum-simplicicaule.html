Desmotrichum simplicicaule

Desmotrichum simplicicaule, commonly known as the simple-stemmed desertweed, is a small, flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows from a basal rosette of narrow, linear leaves and produces small, yellowish to brownish flowers. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in sandy or rocky soils, often found in dry grasslands and shrublands.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in restoration projects in degraded ecosystems.
