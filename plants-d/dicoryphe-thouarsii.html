Dicoryphe thouarsii

Dicoryphe thouarsii, commonly known as the Madagascar orchid, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, fragrant orchid-like flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are elongated and strap-shaped, and it produces showy, often brightly colored blooms.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and is of significant botanical interest.
