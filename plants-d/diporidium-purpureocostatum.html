Diporidium purpureocostatum

Diporidium purpureocostatum, commonly known as the purple-costate diporidium, is a member of the Arecaceae family, characterized by its distinctive appearance and ecological significance.

It is a small to medium-sized palm with a slender trunk and a crown of feather-like leaves that are often tinged with purple or reddish hues. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers arranged in paniculate inflorescences, which are followed by bright orange or red fruits containing edible seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in moist, well-drained soils within shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and used in traditional medicine in some regions.
