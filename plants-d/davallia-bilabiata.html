Davallia bilabiata

Davallia bilabiata, commonly known as the shield fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, shield-shaped fronds that are typically dark green and leathery in texture. The plant produces sporangia on the underside of its fronds, which are clustered in small, rounded structures called sorus. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found growing on tree trunks or rocks.

The botanical features of Davallia bilabiata make it a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its ornamental appearance and low maintenance requirements.
