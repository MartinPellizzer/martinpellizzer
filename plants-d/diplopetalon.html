Diplopetalon

Diplopetalon, commonly known as the double-petaled orchid, is a genus of flowering plants in the orchid family.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, featuring two distinct petals that often resemble a bilaterally symmetrical shape. These plants are typically found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in humid, shaded environments. The leaves are usually broad and lance-shaped, contributing to the plant's ornamental appeal.

Botanically, Diplopetalon species are valued for their intricate floral morphology and ecological significance in their native habitats.
