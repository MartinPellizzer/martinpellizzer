Delphinium wrightii var. wrightii

Delphinium wrightii var. wrightii, commonly known as Wright's larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that bear clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are palmately compound, with finely divided leaflets, and the flowers are showy, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
