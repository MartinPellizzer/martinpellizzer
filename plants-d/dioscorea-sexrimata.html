Dioscorea sexrimata

Dioscorea sexrimata, commonly known as the African yam bean, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by elongated, tuberous roots, which are its primary edible part. These tubers are rich in starch and have been traditionally used as a food source by local communities.

The plant also has medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
