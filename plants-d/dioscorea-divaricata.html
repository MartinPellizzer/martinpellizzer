Dioscorea divaricata

Dioscorea divaricata, commonly known as the variable yam, is a climbing vine belonging to the family Dioscoreaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its twining stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces tuberous roots that are often used as food sources in some cultures. Its flowers are typically unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant.

Dioscorea divaricata is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
