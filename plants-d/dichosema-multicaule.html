Dichosema multicaule

Dichosema multicaule, commonly known as the many-stemmed dichosema, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation, it is a popular choice among gardeners in suitable climates.
