Dipodium conduplicatum

Dipodium conduplicatum, commonly known as the double-leaf fern, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its unique fronds that consist of two leaf-like structures. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forests and stream banks. Its rhizome is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The foliage is evergreen, providing a lush appearance throughout the year.
