Delphinium speciosum var. erianthum

Delphinium speciosum var. erianthum, commonly known as the blue delphinium, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features striking spikes of blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, providing a lush backdrop to its showy blooms. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical characteristics include a tall stem that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height, with numerous flowers arranged in a dense inflorescence.
