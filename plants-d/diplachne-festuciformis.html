Diplachne festuciformis

Diplachne festuciformis, commonly known as slender flatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and tufted growth habit, often forming dense mats on the ground. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in a variety of soil types but is often found in disturbed or sandy soils.

Diplachne festuciformis is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient component of native grassland ecosystems.
