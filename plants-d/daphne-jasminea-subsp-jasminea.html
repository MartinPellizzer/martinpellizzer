Daphne jasminea subsp. jasminea

Daphne jasminea subsp.

jasminea, commonly known as the Chinese daphne, is a shrub native to China and Southeast Asia. It features fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant has small, glossy, dark green leaves and a compact growth habit. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively hardy in temperate climates.

However, it is toxic to both humans and animals, requiring caution in gardens.
