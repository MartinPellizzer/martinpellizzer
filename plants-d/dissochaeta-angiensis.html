Dissochaeta angiensis

Dissochaeta angiensis, commonly known as the Angolan hairbroom, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its dense, wiry stems and small, narrow leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Dissochaeta angiensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature.
