Dendrobium spathilingue

Dendrobium spathilingue, commonly known as the golden spike orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is an epiphytic orchid that grows on trees or rocks, deriving moisture and nutrients from the air and rain. The plant produces long, slender stems with clustered, showy flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its leaves are linear and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention.

Dendrobium spathilingue is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its vibrant, long-lasting blooms.
