Dolichos pavonii

Dolichos pavonii, commonly known as the peacock bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features compound leaves with long, slender leaflets and produces showy, pea-like flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white. The plant is characterized by its slender, twisting stems and small, bean-like pods that contain edible seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Due to its rapid growth and nitrogen-fixing ability, it is also used in sustainable agriculture practices.
