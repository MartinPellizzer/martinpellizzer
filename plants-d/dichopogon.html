Dichopogon

Dichopogon, commonly known as the grass of parnassus, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Poaceae.

It consists of a single species, Dichopogon ciliatus, which is native to the southern United States and Mexico. The plant is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as rocky soils and woodlands.

Dichopogon is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its delicate foliage and unique growth form.
