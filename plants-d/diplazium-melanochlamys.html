Diplazium melanochlamys

It is characterized by its bipinnate fronds that are dark green and often have a leathery texture. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its spores are produced in sori that are clustered on the undersides of the fronds.

Diplazium melanochlamys is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
