Dodonaea peduncularis var. hirsuta

Dodonaea peduncularis var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy devil's backbone, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, opposite leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers followed by woody, bead-like fruits. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting native wildlife.
