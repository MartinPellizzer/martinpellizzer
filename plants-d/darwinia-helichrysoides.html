Darwinia helichrysoides

Darwinia helichrysoides, commonly known as the yellow bell, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in heathlands and open forests, preferring well-drained soils and full sun. Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine, white bloom.

Darwinia helichrysoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
