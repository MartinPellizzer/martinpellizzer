Dianthus saetabensis subsp. contestanus

Dianthus saetabensis subsp.

contestanus, commonly known as the Armenian carnation, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Armenia. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that typically range in color from pink to red.

The plant features glandular-hairy stems and leaves, which contribute to its distinctive appearance. Its flowers are borne in clusters and are often fragrant, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is cultivated in gardens for its beauty and resilience.
