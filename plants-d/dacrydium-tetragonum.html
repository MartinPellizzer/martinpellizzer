Dacrydium tetragonum

Dacrydium tetragonum, commonly known as the New Zealand silver pine, is a coniferous tree native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its slender, straight trunk and long, needle-like leaves that are arranged in opposite pairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous cones, and its bark is typically smooth and greyish in color. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forested areas.

Dacrydium tetragonum plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing habitat for various species of wildlife.
