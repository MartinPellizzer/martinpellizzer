Delphinium ecalcaratum

Delphinium ecalcaratum, commonly known as the spiked delphinium, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems that bear large, showy blue to purple flowers. The plant features palmately compound leaves with a serrated edge, and its flowers are arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. Delphinium ecalcaratum thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

It is also known for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
