Deplanchea coriacea

Deplanchea coriacea, commonly known as the rough-stemmed sterculia, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, woody stems covered in a rough, bark-like texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are typically elliptical and have a leathery texture.

Deplanchea coriacea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to grow in humid, shaded environments.
