Dypsis anovensis

Dypsis anovensis, commonly known as the Anovensis palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to Madagascar and is characterized by its slender, unbranched trunk and pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow in color. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in shaded forest understories.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
