Diplogon graminifolium

Diplogon graminifolium, commonly known as the grass-like fern, is a unique plant characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and fern-like fronds.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is native to tropical and subtropical regions. The plant exhibits a dichotomous branching pattern, with each stem splitting into two equal branches. Its leaves are typically narrow and elongated, with a delicate, feathery appearance.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
