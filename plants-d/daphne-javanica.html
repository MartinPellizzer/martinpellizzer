Daphne javanica

Daphne javanica, commonly known as the Java daphne, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Thymelaeaceae family and is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that range from white to pink. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a distinctive waxy texture.

Daphne javanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its winter blooming habit.
