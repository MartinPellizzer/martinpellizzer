Didymocarpus plicata

Didymocarpus plicata, commonly known as the pleated flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with a distinctive pleated or folded appearance, which gives it its common name. The plant produces showy, clustered inflorescences of bright red or orange flowers, often resembling flames. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.
