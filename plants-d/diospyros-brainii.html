Diospyros brainii

Diospyros brainii, commonly known as the African persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into orange to red berries. Its fruit is edible, though some varieties have astringent qualities when unripe.

Diospyros brainii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
