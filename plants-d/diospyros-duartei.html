Diospyros duartei

Diospyros duartei, commonly known as the Duarte persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically red when mature, though they may remain green depending on the variety. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in coastal areas and humid lowlands.

The wood of Diospyros duartei is valued for its durability and is used in traditional crafts and construction.
