Desmidorchis flava

Desmidorchis flava, commonly known as the yellow orchid, is a flowering plant species native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that typically range in color from yellow to orange. The plant has strap-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in forest understories or near streams.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
