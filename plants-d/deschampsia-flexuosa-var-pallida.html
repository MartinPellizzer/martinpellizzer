Deschampsia flexuosa var. pallida

Deschampsia flexuosa var. pallida, commonly known as the pale hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and long, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The inflorescence consists of a loose panicle with spikelets that are usually pale green to white in color. It thrives in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, bogs, and stream banks.

This plant plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
