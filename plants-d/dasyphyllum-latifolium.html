Dasyphyllum latifolium

Dasyphyllum latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf dasyphyllum, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, often blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in rocky or arid environments.

Dasyphyllum latifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
