Dichanthium tuberculatum

Dichanthium tuberculatum, commonly known as the rough fescue, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces spikelets that are densely arranged along its stems, with distinctive bracts and awns. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Dichanthium tuberculatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in landscaping and erosion control.
