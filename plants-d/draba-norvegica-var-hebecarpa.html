Draba norvegica var. hebecarpa

Draba norvegica var. hebecarpa, commonly known as the Norwegian rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to northern Europe.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, hairy leaves and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in early spring. Its flowers are usually arranged in a single umbel, and the plant is often found in mountainous regions.

Draba norvegica var. hebecarpa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in rocky habitats.
