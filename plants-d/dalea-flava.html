Dalea flava

Dalea flava, commonly known as yellow false indigo, is a flowering plant native to the central and western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky soils. It has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Dalea flava is often used in native plant gardens due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
