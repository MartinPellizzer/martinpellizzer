Dimacria heracleifolia

Dimacria heracleifolia, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often found in cloud forests. The plant features large, showy flowers with five petals, usually in shades of red, pink, or white. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, with a rough texture, and it produces capsules containing numerous seeds.

Dimacria heracleifolia plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for various small organisms.
