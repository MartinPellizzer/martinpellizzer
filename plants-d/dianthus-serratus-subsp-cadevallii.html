Dianthus serratus subsp. cadevallii

Dianthus serratus subsp.

cadevallii, commonly known as the rock campion, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is a low-growing perennial with distinctive pink to red flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant features slender, branched stems and serrated leaves, typical of the Dianthus genus. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.
