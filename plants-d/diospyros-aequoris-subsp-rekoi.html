Diospyros aequoris subsp. rekoi

Diospyros aequoris subsp.

rekoi, commonly known as the African persimmon, is a small to medium-sized tree native to tropical Africa. It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes ebony and persimmon trees.

The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate or elliptic in shape. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically greenish-yellow in color, and it produces edible berries that are yellow when ripe.

The bark is thick and dark, often with a smooth or slightly fissured texture.
