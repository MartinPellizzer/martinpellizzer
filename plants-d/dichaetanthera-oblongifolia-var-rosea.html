Dichaetanthera oblongifolia var. rosea

Dichaetanthera oblongifolia var. rosea, commonly known as the pink-flowered dichaetanthera, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, pink to purple ray florets. The plant produces small, tubular disk flowers surrounded by the brightly colored outer petals. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
