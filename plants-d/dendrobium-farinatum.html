Dendrobium farinatum

Dendrobium farinatum, commonly known as the flour orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, fleshy pseudobulbs and long, narrow leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green, often arranged in clusters. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright indirect light.

Dendrobium farinatum is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
