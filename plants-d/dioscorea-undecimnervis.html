Dioscorea undecimnervis

Dioscorea undecimnervis, commonly known as the eleven-veined yam, is a tropical climbing vine belonging to the Dioscoreaceae family.

It is native to regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific, where it thrives in humid, tropical climates. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems, heart-shaped leaves, and tuberous roots that are often used in traditional medicine. Its flowers are typically unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant.

The tubers of Dioscorea undecimnervis are valued for their nutritional content and have been used in various culinary and medicinal applications.
