Desmocladus nodatus

Desmocladus nodatus, commonly known as the nodular desmocladus, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a small, herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features simple, opposite leaves that are usually ovate or elliptic in shape. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense inflorescences, often with five petals that are white or pale pink in color.

The species is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, with mechanisms to store water and reduce transpiration.
