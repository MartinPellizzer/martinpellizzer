Diplycosia pseudorufescens

Diplycosia pseudorufescens, commonly known as the false red-leafed mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on various host trees, primarily in tropical and subtropical regions.

It possesses small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its fruits are berry-like, containing seeds that are dispersed by birds.

The plant's parasitic nature allows it to absorb water and nutrients from its host, while still performing some photosynthesis.
