Dianthus stribrnyi

Dianthus stribrnyi, commonly known as the Armenian pink, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its vibrant pink to red flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and grows in a compact, cushion-like form. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Dianthus stribrnyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its delicate, showy blooms.
