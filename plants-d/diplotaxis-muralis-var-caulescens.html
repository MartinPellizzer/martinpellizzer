Diplotaxis muralis var. caulescens

Diplotaxis muralis var. caulescens, commonly known as the rock dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial with pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has hairy, fleshy stems and leaves that are often succulent, adapted to arid environments. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal regions.

Its botanical features make it well-suited for xeriscaping and garden use in dry climates.
