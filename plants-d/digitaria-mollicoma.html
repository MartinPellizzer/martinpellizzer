Digitaria mollicoma

Digitaria mollicoma, commonly known as silky fingergrass, is a perennial grass species native to warm climates.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and fine, silky hairs that cover its leaves and inflorescence. The plant produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers, which are typically green or pale blue in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open fields, pastures, and along roadsides.

Digitaria mollicoma is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its delicate appearance.
