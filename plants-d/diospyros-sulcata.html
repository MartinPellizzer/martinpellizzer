Diospyros sulcata

Diospyros sulcata, commonly known as the African date palm, is a flowering plant in the Ebenaceae family.

It is native to tropical and southern Africa, where it thrives in warm, humid climates. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. Its fruit, which resembles a date, is edible and has a sweet, mealy texture.

Diospyros sulcata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its timber.
