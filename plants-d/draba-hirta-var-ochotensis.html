Draba hirta var. ochotensis

Draba hirta var. ochotensis, commonly known as the Siberian mouse-ear cress, is a perennial herb native to the cold climates of Siberia and northern Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. The plant features small, basal leaves that are hairy and lance-shaped, with flowers that are white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

This species is adapted to harsh conditions, demonstrating resilience to low temperatures and limited nutrients.
