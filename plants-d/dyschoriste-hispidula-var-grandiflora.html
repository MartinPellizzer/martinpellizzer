Dyschoriste hispidula var. grandiflora

Dyschoriste hispidula var. grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered dyschoriste, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or wetlands. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture, and its flowers are small, clustered in dense inflorescences. The flowers are usually white or pale yellow, with five petals and prominent stamens.

It is valued in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.
