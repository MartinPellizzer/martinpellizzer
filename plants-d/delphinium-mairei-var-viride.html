Delphinium mairei var. viride

Delphinium mairei var. viride, commonly known as the greenleaf larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It features tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant produces clusters of blue to purple flowers, typically arranged in racemes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a bluish-green hue, giving it its common name.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for alpine gardens.
