Dorstenia binzaensis

Dorstenia binzaensis, commonly known as the Zambian star, is a member of the Moraceae family and is native to eastern Africa.

It is a climbing shrub characterized by its unique, star-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, which are followed by edible, fleshy fruits. Dorstenia binzaensis thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological studies and local herbal practices.
