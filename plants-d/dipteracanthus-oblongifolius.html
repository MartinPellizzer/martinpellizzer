Dipteracanthus oblongifolius

Dipteracanthus oblongifolius, commonly known as the broad-leafed dipteracanthus, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, oblong leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are often white or pale yellow, which bloom during the warmer months. Its root system is fibrous, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in horticultural settings.
