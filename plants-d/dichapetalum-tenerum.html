Dichapetalum tenerum

Dichapetalum tenerum, commonly known as the African bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture.

Dichapetalum tenerum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
