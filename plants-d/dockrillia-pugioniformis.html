Dockrillia pugioniformis

Dockrillia pugioniformis, commonly known as the narrowleaf dockrillia, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are typically visited by various pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland or open woodland habitats.

Dockrillia pugioniformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
