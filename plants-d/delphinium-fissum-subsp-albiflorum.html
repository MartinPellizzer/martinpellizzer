Delphinium fissum subsp. albiflorum

Delphinium fissum subsp.

albiflorum, commonly known as the white-flowered larkspur, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family. It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pale blue.

The flowers are arranged in racemes and are borne on long pedicels, with five sepals that are often petal-like. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are usually deeply lobed, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils in alpine or subalpine habitats.

It is native to the mountainous regions of western North America and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
