Dissotis swynnertonii

Dissotis swynnertonii, commonly known as the Swynnerton's spider-orchid, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, spider-like appearance due to its elongated, thread-like petals. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in areas with high light exposure. It produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, often blooming during the warmer months.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
