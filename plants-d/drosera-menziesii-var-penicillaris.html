Drosera menziesii var. penicillaris

Drosera menziesii var. penicillaris, commonly known as the slender sundew, is a carnivorous plant native to the wetlands of southern Australia.

It is characterized by its thin, hairless stalks and small, sticky tentacles that secrete a mucilaginous substance to trap insects. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or reddish in color. It thrives in moist, acidic environments and is often found in swampy or boggy areas.

Due to its unique trapping mechanism and habitat preferences, it is a fascinating subject for both botanists and plant enthusiasts.
