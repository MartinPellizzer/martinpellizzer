Damasonium ranunculoides

Damasonium ranunculoides, commonly known as the water crowfoot, is a small aquatic or semi-aquatic flowering plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, often found in ponds, ditches, and stream edges. The plant has finely divided, feather-like leaves that help it float and absorb sunlight efficiently. Its flowers are small, usually white or pale yellow, and appear in clusters at the water's surface.

Damasonium ranunculoides plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small invertebrates and contributing to water clarity through its dense growth.
