Denhamia fournieri

Denhamia fournieri, commonly known as the rock fig, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Moraceae and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces edible fruits that are typically consumed raw or cooked. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in some regions for both horticultural and culinary purposes.
