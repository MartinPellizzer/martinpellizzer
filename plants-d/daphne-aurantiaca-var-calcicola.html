Daphne aurantiaca var. calcicola

Daphne aurantiaca var. calcicola, commonly known as the rock daphne, is a perennial shrub native to southern Europe.

It thrives in alkaline soils and is often found in rocky or limestone habitats. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, orange-red flowers in early spring. Its leaves are dark green and glossy, with a slightly waxy texture.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and ability to thrive in challenging soil conditions.
