Dahlia crocata

Dahlia crocata, commonly known as the crocus dahlia, is a tuberous-rooted perennial plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, single or double flowers that come in a wide range of colors. The plant produces vibrant, often brightly colored blooms during the summer months, making it a popular choice for gardens. Its foliage consists of large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically green and slightly hairy.

Dahlia crocata is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for cut flowers and garden display.
