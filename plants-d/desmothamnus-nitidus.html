Desmothamnus nitidus

Desmothamnus nitidus, commonly known as the smooth burrweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grassland and shrubland habitats. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, producing small, yellowish to brownish blooms that attract various pollinators.
