Desmodium garhwalensis

Desmodium garhwalensis, commonly known as the Garhwal ticktrefoil, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is typically found in moist, shaded areas of forests and is adapted to high-altitude environments. It plays a role in soil nitrogen fixation due to its symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria.

Its ornamental value and ecological significance make it an important species in alpine ecosystems.
