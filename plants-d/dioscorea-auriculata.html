Dioscorea auriculata

Dioscorea auriculata, commonly known as the auriculate yam, is a perennial vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by tuberous roots that are often used in traditional medicine. Its tubers are also edible and have been utilized in various culinary practices across its native range.

Dioscorea auriculata is valued for both its medicinal properties and its role in local agriculture.
