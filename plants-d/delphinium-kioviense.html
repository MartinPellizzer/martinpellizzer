Delphinium kioviense

Delphinium kioviense, commonly known as the Kioviensis Larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers in racemes, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are compound, with lanceolate to ovate leaflets, and it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.

Delphinium kioviense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
