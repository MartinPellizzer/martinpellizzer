Diospyros abyssinica subsp. reticulata

Diospyros abyssinica subsp.

reticulata, commonly known as the Abyssinian persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to Ethiopia. It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and elliptical leaves with a reticulate venation pattern. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by edible, orange-red berries that are highly valued for their sweet, astringent flavor.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.
