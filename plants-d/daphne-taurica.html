Daphne taurica

Daphne taurica, commonly known as the Crimean daphne, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and clusters of fragrant, waxy flowers that range from white to pink. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves that are dark green and glossy. Daphne taurica is known for its ornamental value in gardens and its ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

However, it is toxic to both humans and animals, requiring caution in cultivation.
