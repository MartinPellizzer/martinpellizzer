Danthonia clelandii

Danthonia clelandii, commonly known as the Cleland's grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Danthonia clelandii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging environmental conditions.
