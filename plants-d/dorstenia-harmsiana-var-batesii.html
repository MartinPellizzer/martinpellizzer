Dorstenia harmsiana var. batesii

Dorstenia harmsiana var. batesii, commonly known as the "Bates' False Banana," is a unique member of the Moraceae family, characterized by its striking, curling leaves that resemble the fronds of a banana plant.

Native to the tropical regions of Central America, this plant thrives in humid, shaded environments with well-draining soil. Its inflorescence consists of small, clustered flowers that are often hidden among the foliage, making it a less showy but ecologically significant species. The plant is known for its ability to form symbiotic relationships with ants, which provide protection in exchange for shelter and food.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
