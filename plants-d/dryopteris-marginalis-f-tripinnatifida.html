Dryopteris marginalis f. tripinnatifida

Dryopteris marginalis f. tripinnatifida, commonly known as the tripartite marginal shield fern, is a fern species characterized by its distinctive frond structure.

It features compound leaves with a tripinnate arrangement, meaning the leaves are divided into multiple levels of smaller leaflets. The fronds are typically lance-shaped and have a serrated margin, contributing to its ornamental appeal. This fern thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for garden cultivation in suitable climates.
