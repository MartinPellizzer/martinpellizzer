Dactylis glomerata var. viridis

Dactylis glomerata var. viridis, commonly known as green oat grass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically a vibrant green color. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are often green or reddish in color, distinguishing it from other varieties of Dactylis. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in pastureland and erosion control due to its hardy nature.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
