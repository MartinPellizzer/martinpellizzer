Dactylorhiza × ruppertii

Dactylorhiza × ruppertii, commonly known as the hybrid marsh orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of pale green or yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, thriving in moist, acidic soils. It has a complex rhizomatous root system that helps it anchor and absorb nutrients from its environment.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
