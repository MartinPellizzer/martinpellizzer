Diospyros velascoi

Diospyros velascoi, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other persimmon species and ebony trees. The plant is characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit is a fleshy, orange to reddish berry that ripens in autumn, often with a sweet, honey-like flavor.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruit.
