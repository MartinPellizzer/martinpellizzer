Dioscorea sambiranensis subsp. ambrensis

Dioscorea sambiranensis subsp.

ambrensis, commonly known as the Ambrense yam, is a tuberous perennial plant native to Madagascar. It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its underground tubers, which are the primary edible part.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments, often in the understory of tropical rainforests. Its leaves are heart-shaped and large, while its flowers are small and greenish-white, arranged in clusters.

The species is of interest for its potential in food security and traditional medicine in its native region.
