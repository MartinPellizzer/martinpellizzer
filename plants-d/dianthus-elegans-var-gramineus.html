Dianthus elegans var. gramineus

Dianthus elegans var. gramineus, commonly known as the grass pink, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and showy, pinkish-red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and wetlands. It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family, which includes other pink and carnation-like flowers.

Its unique appearance and habitat make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
