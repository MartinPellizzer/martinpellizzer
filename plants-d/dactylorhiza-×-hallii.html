Dactylorhiza × hallii

Dactylorhiza × hallii, commonly known as the hybrid marsh orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a terrestrial herb with a short, erect stem and a single, showy flower that typically blooms in late summer. The plant prefers moist, nutrient-poor soils and is often found in wet meadows, bogs, and marshes. Its roots form symbiotic relationships with mycorrhizal fungi, which aid in nutrient absorption.

Dactylorhiza × hallii is characterized by its distinctive, recurved petals and is a popular subject in botanical studies due to its ecological and horticultural significance.
