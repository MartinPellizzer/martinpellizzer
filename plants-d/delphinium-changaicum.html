Delphinium changaicum

Delphinium changaicum, commonly known as the East African delphinium, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of East Africa.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that bear clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or grassy habitats and is adapted to cool, moist climates. Its leaves are compound, with lance-shaped leaflets that are arranged alternately along the stem.

Delphinium changaicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
