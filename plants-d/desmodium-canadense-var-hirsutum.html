Desmodium canadense var. hirsutum

Desmodium canadense var. hirsutum, commonly known as hairy ticktrefoil, is a perennial legume native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, yellow pea-like flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its seed pods are slender and curved, containing several small, kidney-shaped seeds.

Hairy ticktrefoil is often found in meadows, along roadsides, and in open woodlands.
