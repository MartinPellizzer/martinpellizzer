Desmodium neurocarpum var. gracile

Desmodium neurocarpum var. gracile, commonly known as slender cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces elliptical, hairy pods containing several small, edible seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used as a forage crop or in traditional medicine.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it beneficial for soil enrichment in agricultural settings.
