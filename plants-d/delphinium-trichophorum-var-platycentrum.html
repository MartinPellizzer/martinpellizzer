Delphinium trichophorum var. platycentrum

Delphinium trichophorum var. platycentrum, commonly known as the flat-centred larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and showy, blue to purple flowers arranged in racemes. The leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This variety is closely related to other delphinium species and shares similar ecological preferences.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a striking addition to alpine gardens.
