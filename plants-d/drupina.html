Drupina

Drupina, commonly known as the "pea shrub," is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and is often found in disturbed soils. Drupina has a shallow root system that helps prevent soil erosion.

Its flowers are usually pale blue or purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
