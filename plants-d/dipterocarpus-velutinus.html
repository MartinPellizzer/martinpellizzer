Dipterocarpus velutinus

Dipterocarpus velutinus, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and elliptical leaves with a leathery texture. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by woody, egg-shaped fruits containing edible seeds. Its wood is highly valued for its durability and is used in construction and furniture-making.

The plant plays an important role in the ecological systems of its native habitats, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
