Dioscorea claussenii

Dioscorea claussenii, commonly known as the Cape arrowroot, is a climbing vine native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are used in traditional medicine. The plant has palmate leaves and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by cylindrical, spiny fruits. Its rhizomes are sometimes utilized for their medicinal properties, though it is not as commercially cultivated as other yam species.

The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.
