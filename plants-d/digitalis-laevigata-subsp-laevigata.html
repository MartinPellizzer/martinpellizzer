Digitalis laevigata subsp. laevigata

Digitalis laevigata subsp.

laevigata, commonly known as the European foxglove, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe. It features tall, spires of tubular, violet-purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged in a basal rosette. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

This species is notable for its medicinal properties, containing cardiac glycosides used in treating heart conditions.
