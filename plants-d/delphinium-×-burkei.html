Delphinium × burkei

Delphinium × burkei, commonly known as the Shirley poppy, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the British Isles.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems that bear clusters of striking blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, making it a prominent feature in gardens. Its foliage consists of deeply cut, fern-like leaves that are aromatic and often used in herbal remedies.

Delphinium × burkei thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, preferring cooler climates for optimal growth.
