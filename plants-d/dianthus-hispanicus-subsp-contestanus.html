Dianthus hispanicus subsp. contestanus

Dianthus hispanicus subsp.

contestanus, commonly known as the Spanish carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red.

The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its colorful display.
