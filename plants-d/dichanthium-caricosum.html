Dichanthium caricosum

Dichanthium caricosum, commonly known as the desert grass or African lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to Africa, particularly found in arid and semi-arid regions.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves that are adapted to withstand drought conditions. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny florets, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soils and prevent erosion.

Dichanthium caricosum is also valued for its forage potential, though it is more suited for livestock in dry environments.
