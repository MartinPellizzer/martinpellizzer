Dinochloa khoonmengii

Dinochloa khoonmengii, commonly known as the Khoonmeng grass, is a rare and endemic plant species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its ecological significance lies in its role as a habitat for various microorganisms and in maintaining soil stability in its native environment.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, Dinochloa khoonmengii is considered a species of conservation concern.
