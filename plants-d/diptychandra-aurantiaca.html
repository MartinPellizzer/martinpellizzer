Diptychandra aurantiaca

Diptychandra aurantiaca, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange to red flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a dramatic visual display. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets that provide a lush green backdrop to its showy blooms. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental species due to its ornate appearance.

Diptychandra aurantiaca is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as birds and insects.
