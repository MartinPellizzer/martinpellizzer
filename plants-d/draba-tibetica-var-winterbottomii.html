Draba tibetica var. winterbottomii

Draba tibetica var. winterbottomii, commonly known as the Tibetan rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, where it thrives in cold, dry conditions. Its leaves are basal, rosette-shaped, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs to reduce water loss.

This variety is notable for its resilience and adaptation to extreme climatic conditions.
