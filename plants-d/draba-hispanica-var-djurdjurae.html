Draba hispanica var. djurdjurae

Draba hispanica var. djurdjurae, commonly known as the Spanish rockcress, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It typically forms dense mats of foliage with small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats. Its leaves are basal, linear, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are prostrate or ascending.

This variety is characterized by its compact growth habit and is adapted to withstand harsh mountainous conditions.
