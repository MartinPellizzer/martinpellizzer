Delphinium keteleerii

Delphinium keteleerii, commonly known as Keteleer's larkspur, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters, adorned with clusters of blue to violet flowers. The plant features lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
