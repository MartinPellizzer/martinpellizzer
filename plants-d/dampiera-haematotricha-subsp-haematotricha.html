Dampiera haematotricha subsp. haematotricha

Dampiera haematotricha subsp.

haematotricha, commonly known as the blood-red dampiera, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking red or pink flowers that appear in late summer to autumn.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and native plant collections.
