Dichanthelium acuminatum var. longiligulatum

Dichanthelium acuminatum var. longiligulatum, commonly known as the long-ligule panicgrass, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves with a prominent ligule. The inflorescence consists of a spikelet cluster, with each spikelet bearing two to three bracts and a single floret. Its flowering period typically occurs in late spring to early summer, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers.

This plant is often found in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.
