Dillenia harmandii

Dillenia harmandii, commonly known as the Harmand's dillenia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin, while the fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Dillenia harmandii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
