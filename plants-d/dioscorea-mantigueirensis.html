Dioscorea mantigueirensis

Dioscorea mantigueirensis, commonly known as the Mantigueirensis yam, is a species of tuberous vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits. Its tuberous roots are the primary part used, valued for their starchy content and traditional medicinal properties.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.
