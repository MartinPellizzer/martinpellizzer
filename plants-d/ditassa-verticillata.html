Ditassa verticillata

Ditassa verticillata, commonly known as the creeping marsh grass, is a herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in Africa.

It features slender, ascending stems that often form dense mats on the ground. The leaves are linear, narrow, and slightly hairy, with a grass-like appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers in clusters at the base of the stems.

It thrives in moist soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies.
