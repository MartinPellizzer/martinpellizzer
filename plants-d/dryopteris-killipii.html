Dryopteris killipii

Dryopteris killipii, commonly known as Killip's wood fern, is a species of fern native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its rhizomes are extensively branched and help it spread through vegetative reproduction.

The spore-producing structures, or sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, contributing to its reproductive strategy.
