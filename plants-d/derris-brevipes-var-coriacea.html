Derris brevipes var. coriacea

Derris brevipes var. coriacea, commonly known as the rough-seeded derris, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, woody stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by elongated, spiny pods containing flat, kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

The rough texture of its bark and seeds gives it its scientific variety name, *coriacea*, meaning "rough" in Latin.
