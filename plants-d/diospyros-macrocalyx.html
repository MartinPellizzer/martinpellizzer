Diospyros macrocalyx

Diospyros macrocalyx, commonly known as the large-fruited persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and produces large, orange to reddish fruits that are edible when fully mature. The plant belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other persimmon species and ebony trees. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in panicles, often appearing before the leaves.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and the nutritional value of its fruit.
