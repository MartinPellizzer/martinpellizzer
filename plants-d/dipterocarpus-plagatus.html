Dipterocarpus plagatus

Dipterocarpus plagatus, commonly known as the flat-leaf dipterocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its large, broad leaves and heavy, aromatic wood. The plant typically grows in lowland rainforests and is well-adapted to humid, warm climates. It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing habitat for various wildlife species.

The tree is also valued for its timber and has been studied for its potential in reforestation efforts.
