Drimia intermedia

Drimia intermedia, commonly known as the Cape tulip, is a bulbous perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features strap-shaped, lanceolate leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple, which bloom in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and containers.

Drimia intermedia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticultural displays.
