Diplodium australe

Diplodium australe, commonly known as the Australian flat sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Australia.

It typically grows in wetland and swampy environments, showcasing its adaptation to waterlogged soils. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense vegetation.

Diplodium australe plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
