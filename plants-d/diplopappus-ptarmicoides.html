Diplopappus ptarmicoides

Diplopappus ptarmicoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a rough texture, and its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn.

The plant is notable for its ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments, contributing to ecological balance.
