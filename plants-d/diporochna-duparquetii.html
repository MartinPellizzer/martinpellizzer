Diporochna duparquetii

Diporochna duparquetii, commonly known as the African violet, is a member of the Gesneriaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically has velvety, dark green leaves and produces flowers in shades of violet, pink, and white. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is widely cultivated as a houseplant.
