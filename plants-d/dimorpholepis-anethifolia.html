Dimorpholepis anethifolia

Dimorpholepis anethifolia, commonly known as the anise-leaved bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, anise-scented leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks. Its leaves are finely divided and resemble those of anise, giving it its common name.

Dimorpholepis anethifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique fragrance.
