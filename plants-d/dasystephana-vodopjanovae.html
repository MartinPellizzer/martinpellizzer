Dasystephana vodopjanovae

Dasystephana vodopjanovae, commonly known as the Vodopjanov's star-of-Bethlehem, is a rare and striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, star-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and purple. Native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, this plant thrives in cool, moist environments with well-drained soil. Its unique floral structure and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is considered endangered in its native range.
