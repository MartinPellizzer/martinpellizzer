Dendrochilum alpinum

Dendrochilum alpinum, commonly known as the alpine tree orchid, is a rare and fascinating orchid species native to the high altitudes of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique morphological features, including its woody stems and dense, evergreen foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or forested areas, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its flowers are small and clustered, often displaying a range of colors from white to pink and purple.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, Dendrochilum alpinum is considered a delicate and endangered species.
