Diasperus lasiogynus

Diasperus lasiogynus, commonly known as the rough-leaved dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and rough, hairy leaves. The plant typically grows in grasslands and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are alternate, lobed, and often covered in fine, stiff hairs.

The fruit is a small, hairy achene that is dispersed by wind, aiding in its propagation.
