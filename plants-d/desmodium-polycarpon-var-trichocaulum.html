Desmodium polycarpon var. trichocaulum

Desmodium polycarpon var. trichocaulum, commonly known as the hairy desmodium, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, each composed of three oval-shaped leaflets with hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It is native to regions of Africa and is often found in grassy habitats and disturbed soils.

The species is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in ecological restoration and sustainable agriculture.
