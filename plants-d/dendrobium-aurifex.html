Dendrobium aurifex

Dendrobium aurifex, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with cane-like stems that can grow up to several meters in length. Its leaves are usually elliptical and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

Dendrobium aurifex requires bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to thrive.
