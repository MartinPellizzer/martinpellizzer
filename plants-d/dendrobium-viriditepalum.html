Dendrobium viriditepalum

Dendrobium viriditepalum, commonly known as the emerald-tipped dendrobium, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is an epiphytic plant that typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, tropical environments. The plant features long, cane-like stems covered in clusters of lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small, greenish-white with yellow and purple markings, and they bloom in clusters during the summer months.

Dendrobium viriditepalum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its unique foliage and delicate floral display.
