Dianthus glandulosopubescens

Dianthus glandulosopubescens, commonly known as the hairy-fruited campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its pink or red flowers that bloom in summer, typically arranged in clusters at the ends of stems. The plant has hairy, glandular leaves that give it its scientific name, and it thrives in well-drained soils. It is often found in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
