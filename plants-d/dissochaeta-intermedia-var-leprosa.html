Dissochaeta intermedia var. leprosa

Dissochaeta intermedia var. leprosa, commonly known as the leprose dissochaeta, is a variant of the dissochaeta genus native to certain regions of South America.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, elongated leaves. The flowers are typically clustered in spikes and are usually pale green to white in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded to semi-shaded environments.

This plant is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphological features and potential ecological role in its native habitat.
