Digitaria dilatata

Digitaria dilatata, commonly known as broadleaf signalgrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and wide, flat leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and pastures.

Digitaria dilatata is known for its adaptability to various environmental conditions, making it a common weed in agricultural settings.
