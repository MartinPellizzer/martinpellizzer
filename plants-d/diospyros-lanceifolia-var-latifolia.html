Diospyros lanceifolia var. latifolia

Diospyros lanceifolia var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf persimmon, is a species native to Southeast Asia, particularly Thailand and Malaysia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree characterized by its lanceolate leaves, which are broader in this variety compared to the typical form. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by edible, orange-red fruits that are rich in nutrients. Its botanical features include a smooth bark and a tendency to grow in lowland evergreen forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties.
