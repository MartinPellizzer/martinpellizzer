Dendrocalamus hamiltonii var. edulis

Dendrocalamus hamiltonii var. edulis, commonly known as the edible bamboo, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks that can grow up to 20 meters in height. The plant has a robust root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion. Its culms are hollow and segmented, with a smooth, pale green to yellowish exterior.

This bamboo is valued for its edible shoots and is also used in construction and as a source of bamboo fiber.
