Diospyros implexicalyx

Diospyros implexicalyx, commonly known as the red-leaf persimmon, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and striking red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically harvested when fully ripe. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation.
