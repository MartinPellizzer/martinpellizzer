Datura pruinosa

Datura pruinosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf jimsonweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its large, showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant has smooth, often hairy leaves and produces spiny capsules containing numerous seeds. Datura pruinosa is part of the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals.

It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in disturbed soils.
