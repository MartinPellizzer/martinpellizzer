Dipteracanthus puri

Dipteracanthus puri, commonly known as the golden flame orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow and orange floral display. The plant features long, slender stems with broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. Its flowers are arranged in panicle-like clusters, and they emit a faint, sweet fragrance.

Dipteracanthus puri thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.
