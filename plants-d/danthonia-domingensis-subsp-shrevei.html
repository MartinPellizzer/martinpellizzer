Danthonia domingensis subsp. shrevei

Danthonia domingensis subsp.

shrevei, commonly known as Shreve's danthonia, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often in woodland or wetland habitats. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like arrangement of small, spikelet-bearing branches.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
