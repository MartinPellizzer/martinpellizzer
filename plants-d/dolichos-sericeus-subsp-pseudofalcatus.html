Dolichos sericeus subsp. pseudofalcatus

Dolichos sericeus subsp.

pseudofalcatus, commonly known as the silky cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions. It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, hairy leaves.

The flowers are typically pale yellow or white, and the pods are elongated with a silky texture. This subspecies is often cultivated for its edible seeds, which are rich in protein.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to warm climates.
