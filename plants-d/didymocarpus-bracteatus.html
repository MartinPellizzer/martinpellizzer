Didymocarpus bracteatus

Didymocarpus bracteatus, commonly known as the hairy tamarind, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, followed by woody pods containing several seeds. Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Caesalpiniaceae family.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
