Drimia ollivieri

Drimia ollivieri, commonly known as the Cape daffodil, is a perennial bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It features upright, grass-like leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange. The plant typically blooms in spring and is adapted to grow in well-drained, sandy soils. It is often found in coastal and mountainous regions, thriving in full sun.

Drimia ollivieri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
