Dipterocarpus parvifolius

Dipterocarpus parvifolius, commonly known as the small-leaf dipterocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually small and elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by woody, elongated fruits containing a single seed. It plays an important role in the rainforest ecosystem, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.

This species is also valued for its durable wood, which is used in construction and furniture-making.
