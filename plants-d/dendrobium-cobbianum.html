Dendrobium cobbianum

Dendrobium cobbianum, commonly known as the Cobbian orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, cane-like stem and clusters of fragrant, showy flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often on tree trunks or rocks. Its leaves are long and strap-like, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers with a distinctive yellow lip.

Dendrobium cobbianum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
