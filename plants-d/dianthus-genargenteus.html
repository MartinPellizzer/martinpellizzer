Dianthus genargenteus

Dianthus genargenteus, commonly known as the silver pinks, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its clusters of pink or white flowers with a distinctive, velvety appearance. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, rounded seed pods that mature into a dark brown color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Dianthus genargenteus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
