Didymocarpus silvarum var. lasiantherus

Didymocarpus silvarum var. lasiantherus, commonly known as the mountain flame tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy red flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. The plant has simple, elliptical leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
