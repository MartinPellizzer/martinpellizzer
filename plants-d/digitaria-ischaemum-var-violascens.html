Digitaria ischaemum var. violascens

Digitaria ischaemum var. violascens, commonly known as violet-stemmed fingergrass, is a variant of the grass species Digitaria ischaemum.

It is characterized by its slender, violet-hued stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed areas such as fields and roadsides. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the grass family.

This variant is often found in temperate regions and is adapted to a variety of soil types.
