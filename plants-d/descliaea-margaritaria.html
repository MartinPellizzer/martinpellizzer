Descliaea margaritaria

Descliaea margaritaria, commonly known as the pearl orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, evergreen growth habit. The plant produces small, showy flowers that are often white or pale pink, arranged in a raceme. Its leaves are typically elliptical and glossy, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Descliaea margaritaria thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as a houseplant.
