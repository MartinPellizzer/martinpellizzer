Dianthus mariae

Dianthus mariae, commonly known as the Marie dianthus, is a species of flowering plant in the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, pinkish-red flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing clusters of small, rounded petals that resemble tiny flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

Dianthus mariae is valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
