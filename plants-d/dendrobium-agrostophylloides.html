Dendrobium agrostophylloides

Dendrobium agrostophylloides, commonly known as the mountain orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is an epiphytic plant that grows on trees and rocks in humid, tropical environments. The plant has long, thin stems that are often covered in a dense layer of hair, which helps retain moisture. Its leaves are usually narrow and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

It produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, typically blooming during the wet season.
