Diospyros potamophila

Diospyros potamophila, commonly known as the river persimmon, is a small to medium-sized tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other persimmon and ebony species. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same tree. Its fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that turns from green to yellow or red when mature.

The leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a distinctive waxy coating, contributing to its unique botanical appearance.
