Dendrobium falconeri var. senapatianum

Dendrobium falconeri var. senapatianum, commonly known as the Senapatian orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, pseudobulbous structure and produces clusters of fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white and yellow. The plant typically blooms during the warmer months, often in the summer, and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth. It thrives in humid environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and delicate species in the wild.
