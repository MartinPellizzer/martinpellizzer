Dasiphora nitida

Dasiphora nitida, commonly known as the smooth fireweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northwestern United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the rose family (Rosaceae) and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant produces small, oval-shaped seeds that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its ability to colonize disturbed soils. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often covered with a fine layer of hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture.

Dasiphora nitida is adapted to grow in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.
