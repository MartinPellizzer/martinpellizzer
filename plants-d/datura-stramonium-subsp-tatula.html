Datura stramonium subsp. tatula

Datura stramonium subsp.

tatula, commonly known as the Turk's cap lily, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its large, showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale purple in color.

The plant produces spiny, egg-shaped fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and waste areas.

The foliage and flowers are covered in fine, hairy glands, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
