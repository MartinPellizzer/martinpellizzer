Deschampsia cespitosa subsp. pyrenaica

Deschampsia cespitosa subsp.

pyrenaica, commonly known as the Pyrenean bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Pyrenees region. It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and slender, bluish-green leaves.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained, often calcareous soils and is commonly found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing mountainous terrain.
