Dressleria fragrans

Dressleria fragrans, commonly known as the fragrant orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in a raceme-like inflorescence. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are broad and glossy, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant floral display.

The fragrance of its blooms is often described as sweet and intoxicating, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
