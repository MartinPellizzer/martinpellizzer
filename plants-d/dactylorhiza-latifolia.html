Dactylorhiza latifolia

Dactylorhiza latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaf marsh orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in wet or damp habitats such as bogs, marshes, and shaded woodlands. The plant features a slender, erect stem with several linear leaves and produces clusters of pale yellow or greenish-white flowers. Its roots are adapted to absorb nutrients from the acidic soil in which it thrives.

Dactylorhiza latifolia is a perennial species that blooms between June and August, depending on the region.
