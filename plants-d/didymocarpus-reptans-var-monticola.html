Didymocarpus reptans var. monticola

Didymocarpus reptans var. monticola, commonly known as the rock fig, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that turns red when mature.

It thrives in rocky or limestone soils and is often found in mountainous regions.
