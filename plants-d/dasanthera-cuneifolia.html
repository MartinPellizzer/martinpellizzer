Dasanthera cuneifolia

Dasanthera cuneifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf lily or western lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like leaves and showy, clustered white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
