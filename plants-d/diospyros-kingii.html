Diospyros kingii

Diospyros kingii, commonly known as the Kauai persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, orange-red berries. Diospyros kingii is well adapted to the tropical climate of Hawaii, thriving in well-drained soils and full sunlight.

Its unique botanical features make it an important part of the local ecosystem and a symbol of Hawaiian biodiversity.
