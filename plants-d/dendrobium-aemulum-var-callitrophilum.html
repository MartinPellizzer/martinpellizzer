Dendrobium aemulum var. callitrophilum

Dendrobium aemulum var. callitrophilum, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with elongated, fleshy pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant produces showy, golden-yellow flowers with a distinctive red lip, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
