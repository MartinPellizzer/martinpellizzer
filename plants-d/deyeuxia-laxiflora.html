Deyeuxia laxiflora

Deyeuxia laxiflora, commonly known as the slender false brome, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Asia and North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, meadows, and along stream banks. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with silver or gray. Its inflorescence consists of a loose panicle of small, spikelet-bearing branches.

Deyeuxia laxiflora plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
