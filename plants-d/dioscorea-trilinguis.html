Dioscorea trilinguis

Dioscorea trilinguis, commonly known as the three-linged yam, is a perennial vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and tuberous roots. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its tubers are often used in traditional medicine and as a food source in some cultures.

The leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture and can vary in size depending on environmental conditions.
