Digitaria madagascariensis

Digitaria madagascariensis, commonly known as Madagascar grass, is a species of grass native to Madagascar.

It is a perennial plant characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils.

Digitaria madagascariensis is sometimes used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental qualities.
