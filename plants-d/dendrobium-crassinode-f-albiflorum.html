Dendrobium crassinode f. albiflorum

Dendrobium crassinode f. albiflorum, commonly known as the white-flowered cane orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is an epiphytic plant that thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing on trees or rocks. The plant features long, cane-like stems that can reach several feet in length, with clustered, lance-shaped leaves. Its most distinguishing botanical characteristic is its white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to cultivate with proper care.
