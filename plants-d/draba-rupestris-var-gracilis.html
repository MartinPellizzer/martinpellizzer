Draba rupestris var. gracilis

Draba rupestris var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender rock cress, is a flowering plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. This variety is distinguished by its slender stems and compact leaves, adapted to thrive in rocky and alpine environments. It typically grows in crevices and on slopes, where it can withstand harsh weather conditions.

The plant produces yellow or white flowers in early spring, contributing to the floral diversity of its habitat.
