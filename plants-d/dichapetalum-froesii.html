Dichapetalum froesii

Dichapetalum froesii, commonly known as the African star gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive star-shaped flowers that appear in clusters, typically in spring. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually oval or elliptic in shape. Its fruit is a small, fleshy berry that changes color from green to red as it matures.

Dichapetalum froesii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to a variety of soil types.
