Dimorphanthera beccariana

Dimorphanthera beccariana, commonly known as the Bornean orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical rainforests of Borneo.

It is characterized by its striking, often brightly colored flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are usually broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in the humid, shaded environment.

Dimorphanthera beccariana plays a significant role in the biodiversity of its ecosystem, supporting various pollinators and contributing to the ecological balance.
