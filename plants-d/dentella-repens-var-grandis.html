Dentella repens var. grandis

Dentella repens var. grandis, commonly known as the giant trailing lily, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its long, trailing stems that can reach several meters in length, making it well-suited for rock gardens or as a ground cover. The plant produces clusters of showy, white or pale pink flowers with a distinctive, toothed appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is noted for its hardiness and ability to bloom reliably in cooler climates.
