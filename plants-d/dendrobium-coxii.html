Dendrobium coxii

Dendrobium coxii, commonly known as the Cox's orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with multiple pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, often with intricate patterns. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining potting media.

Dendrobium coxii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for horticultural purposes.
