Delphinium cuneatum var. sericeocarpum

Delphinium cuneatum var. sericeocarpum, commonly known as the silky-fruited larkspur, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features tall, spindly stems that bear clusters of blue to purple flowers, which bloom in late summer. The leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine, silvery down, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are typically arranged in racemes, and the plant is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

The variety is characterized by its silky, hairy fruits that persist into autumn, adding visual interest to the plant throughout the growing season.
