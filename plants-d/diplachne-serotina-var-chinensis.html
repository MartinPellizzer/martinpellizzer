Diplachne serotina var. chinensis

Diplachne serotina var. chinensis, commonly known as Chinese flat sedge, is a perennial grass species native to Asia.

It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and forms dense, mat-like colonies. The plant typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded areas, such as marshes and rice fields. Its leaves are narrow, flat, and often have a bluish-green hue.

The inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a panicle, with small, wind-pollinated flowers.
