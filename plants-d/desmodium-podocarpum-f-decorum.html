Desmodium podocarpum f. decorum

Desmodium podocarpum f. decorum, commonly known as the decorative desmodium, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that typically bloom in clusters. The plant produces distinctive, inflated pods that contain several seeds, a feature common among legumes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscape design.
