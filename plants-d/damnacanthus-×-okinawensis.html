Damnacanthus × okinawensis

Damnacanthus × okinawensis, commonly known as the Okinawan false dandelion, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Acanthaceae family.

It is native to the Ryukyu Islands of Japan, where it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces striking, showy flowers in shades of pink to purple. Its botanical characteristics include a robust root system and a branching stem structure that supports its ornamental appeal.

It is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and landscapes.
