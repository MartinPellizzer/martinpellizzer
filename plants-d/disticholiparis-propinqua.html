Disticholiparis propinqua

Disticholiparis propinqua, commonly known as the narrow-leafed fern, is a species of fern characterized by its delicate, bipinnate fronds.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate forest understories. The plant has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread vegetatively. Its leaves are usually dark green and have a finely divided structure, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Disticholiparis propinqua is valued in horticulture for its elegant appearance and ability to thrive in humid conditions.
