Dipladenia moricandiana

Dipladenia moricandiana, commonly known as the Moricandia trumpet vine, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a trailing or climbing vine that produces vibrant orange to yellow tubular flowers, which are highly attractive to pollinators like hummingbirds and butterflies. The plant features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-draining soil and full sun exposure.

Dipladenia moricandiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
