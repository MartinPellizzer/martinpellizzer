Dracocephalum virginianum var. speciosum

Dracocephalum virginianum var. speciosum, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, blue-violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This species is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
