Dichapetalum flaviflorum

Dichapetalum flaviflorum, commonly known as the yellow-flowered dichapetalum, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, making it a notable ornamental species. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is valued for its vibrant floral display and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
