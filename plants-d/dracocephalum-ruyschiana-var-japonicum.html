Dracocephalum ruyschiana var. japonicum

Dracocephalum ruyschiana var. japonicum, commonly known as Japanese dragonhead, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, two-lipped flowers in shades of purple and blue, typically arranged in spikes. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long flowering period.
