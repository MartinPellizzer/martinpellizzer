Dianthus caesius

Dianthus caesius, commonly known as the purple campion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. Its stems are usually erect and can grow up to 60 centimeters tall.

Dianthus caesius is often found in meadows, grasslands, and rocky areas, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal.
