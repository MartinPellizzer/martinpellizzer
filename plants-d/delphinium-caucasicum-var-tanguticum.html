Delphinium caucasicum var. tanguticum

Delphinium caucasicum var. tanguticum, commonly known as the Tangut larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features tall, slender stems that bear whorled clusters of striking blue to purple flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and its roots are fibrous and deep-reaching. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a hardy addition to alpine gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
