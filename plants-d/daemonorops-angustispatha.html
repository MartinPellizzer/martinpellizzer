Daemonorops angustispatha

Daemonorops angustispatha, commonly known as the narrow-leaf cycad, is a member of the Arecaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a slow-growing, evergreen plant characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and pinnate, feather-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered yellowish flowers that are typically pollinated by insects. Its leaves are often used in traditional basket weaving due to their durability and flexibility.

The species is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its cultural significance in local communities.
