Desmoncus polyacanthos var. oxyacanthos

Desmoncus polyacanthos var. oxyacanthos, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has rough, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological biodiversity.
