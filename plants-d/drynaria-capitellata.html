Drynaria capitellata

Drynaria capitellata, commonly known as the fan-leaf tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of large, fan-shaped fronds. The plant exhibits a unique growth habit, with a central trunk covered in persistent, fibrous roots. Its fronds are typically dark green and can grow up to several meters in length.

Drynaria capitellata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to humid, shaded environments.
