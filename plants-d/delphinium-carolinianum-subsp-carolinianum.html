Delphinium carolinianum subsp. carolinianum

Delphinium carolinianum subsp.

carolinianum, commonly known as Carolina larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States. It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring tall, erect stems that bear clusters of blue to purple flowers.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are deeply lobed, and its flowers are characterized by their spurred petals, a feature typical of the Delphinium genus. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

The plant is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
