Dontostemon pectinatus var. humilior

Dontostemon pectinatus var. humilior, commonly known as the low-growing variant of the peacock plant, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its compact, spreading growth habit and produces showy, daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and white petals. The plant features hairy, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine, soft pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both ornamental and ecological gardens.
