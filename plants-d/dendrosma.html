Dendrosma

Dendrosma, commonly known as the tree mallow, is a genus of flowering plants in the mallow family, Malvaceae.

It consists of several species, primarily native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia. The plants are typically shrubs or small trees, characterized by their rough, often hairy stems and broad, ovate leaves. Dendrosma species are valued for their ornamental appeal and are sometimes used in traditional medicine.
