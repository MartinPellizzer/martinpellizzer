Datura gigantea

Datura gigantea, commonly known as the giant devil's trumpet, is a striking member of the Solanaceae family.

It is native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. This plant is characterized by its large, showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The leaves are broad and often have a velvety texture, while the stems are thick and succulent.

Datura gigantea contains toxic alkaloids, making it a plant that requires careful handling.
