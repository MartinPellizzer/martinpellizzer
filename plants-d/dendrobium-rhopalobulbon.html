Dendrobium rhopalobulbon

Dendrobium rhopalobulbon, commonly known as the golden torch orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with fleshy, often reddish roots that store water and nutrients. The plant produces striking, golden-yellow flowers with a distinctive, narrow spur that is longer than the sepals. It typically blooms during the dry season, and its leaves are usually arranged in a rosette pattern.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
