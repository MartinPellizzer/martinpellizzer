Diospyros ferrea f. subelliptica

Diospyros ferrea f. subelliptica, commonly known as the Brazilian persimmon, is a variant of the persimmon tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its elliptical leaves that are typically darker in color compared to the standard species. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into orange to red fruits with a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor. Its wood is dense and strong, making it valuable for construction and crafts.

The species is also noted for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.
