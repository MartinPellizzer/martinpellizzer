Dolichos ovalifolius

Dolichos ovalifolius, commonly known as the oval-leaf dolichos, is a climbing legume native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It features compound leaves with oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in racemes. The plant bears pods that contain several seeds, which are often used as a food source in some cultures. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Botanically, it is characterized by its hairy stems and distinctive leaf morphology.
