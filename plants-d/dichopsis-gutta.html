Dichopsis gutta

Dichopsis gutta, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, spotted leaves and delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers are usually pale green or yellow with purple or brown markings, and it blooms during the summer months.

Dichopsis gutta is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable growing conditions.
