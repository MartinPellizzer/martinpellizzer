Dianthus hispanicus subsp. tarraconensis

Dianthus hispanicus subsp.

tarraconensis, commonly known as the Tarraconensis campion, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe, particularly Spain. It is a low-growing perennial herb with fleshy, often reddish stems and clustered, pink to red flowers.

The plant produces small, rounded leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.
