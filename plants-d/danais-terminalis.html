Danais terminalis

Danais terminalis, commonly known as the golden rain tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves that turn bright yellow in the fall, creating a striking visual display. The tree produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by elongated, slender pods. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

The species is also valued for its rapid growth and ability to provide shade in urban landscapes.
