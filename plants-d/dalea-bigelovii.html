Dalea bigelovii

Dalea bigelovii, commonly known as the bigelov's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, such as grasslands and open woodlands. It has a deep root system that allows it to thrive in poor soils and withstand drought conditions.

Dalea bigelovii plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
