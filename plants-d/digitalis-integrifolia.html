Digitalis integrifolia

Digitalis integrifolia, commonly known as the European foxglove, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe, western Asia, and northwestern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and striking, hooded flowers that range in color from purple to pink. The plant belongs to the genus Digitalis, which includes several species known for their cardiac glycosides. Its leaves are ovate with smooth or slightly toothed margins, and it thrives in moist, woodland environments.

Digitalis integrifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in medicine.
