Discocactus rapirhizus

Discocactus rapirhizus, commonly known as the creeping disc cactus, is a unique species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its low-growing, sprawling form with flattened, disc-like stems that are often ribbed and covered in spines. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its green foliage. Its root system is extensive and adapted to absorb moisture from the dry, sandy soils it inhabits.

Discocactus rapirhizus is well-suited for arid environments and requires minimal water, making it a resilient choice for xeriscaping.
