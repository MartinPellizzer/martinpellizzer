Dysmicodon perfoliatum

Dysmicodon perfoliatum, commonly known as the perforate dysmicodon, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, branching stems with opposite, ovate leaves that have a distinctive perforation along the midrib. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early fall, producing small yellow flowers that are clustered in a spherical inflorescence.

Dysmicodon perfoliatum is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.
