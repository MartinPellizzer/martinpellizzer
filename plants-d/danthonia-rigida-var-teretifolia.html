Danthonia rigida var. teretifolia

Danthonia rigida var. teretifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf tussock grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves that are often slightly folded. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Due to its hardy nature, it is used in ecological restoration projects and as a ornamental plant in some regions.
