Dombeya acutangula var. tiliifolia

Dombeya acutangula var. tiliifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved dombeya, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, serrated leaves and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to mild, temperate climates.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and birds, contributing to its ecological significance.
