Dioscorea pynaertii

Dioscorea pynaertii, commonly known as the Pynaert yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary storage organs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers and has palmately lobed leaves. Its tubers are sometimes used in traditional medicine and as a food source.

The species is notable for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.
