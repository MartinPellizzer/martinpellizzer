Dioscorea confusa

Dioscorea confusa, commonly known as the tropical yam, is a perennial vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and tuberous roots. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers and has compound leaves with three to five leaflets. Its tubers are often used as a food source in various cultures, though they contain toxins that require careful preparation.

The botanical features of Dioscorea confusa make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
