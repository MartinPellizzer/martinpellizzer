Dactylorhiza × ruthei

Dactylorhiza × ruthei, commonly known as the hybrid marsh orchid, is a species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, often in shaded areas. The plant produces tall, slender stems that bear clusters of pale green to yellowish flowers. Its leaves are linear and arranged in a rosette at the base of the plant.

Botanically, it is a hybrid between Dactylorhiza fuchsii and Dactylorhiza maculata, exhibiting characteristics of both parent species.
