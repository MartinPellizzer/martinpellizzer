Diospyros filipendula

Diospyros filipendula, commonly known as the Philippine persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and alternate, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in catkins, followed by orange to red berries that are edible when fully ripe.

Diospyros filipendula is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine and cuisine.
