Deyeuxia elongata

Deyeuxia elongata, commonly known as the longleaf sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist to wet environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers. It is often found in disturbed or transitional habitats, contributing to soil stabilization and providing habitat for various wildlife.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
