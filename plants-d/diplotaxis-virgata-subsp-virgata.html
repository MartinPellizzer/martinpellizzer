Diplotaxis virgata subsp. virgata

Diplotaxis virgata subsp.

virgata, commonly known as the desert cress, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East. It is a low-growing annual herb with slender, branching stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from yellow to white.

The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It has adapted to thrive in harsh environments through its succulent leaves and deep root system.

Its botanical features make it an important component of desert ecosystems and a subject of study in plant ecology.
