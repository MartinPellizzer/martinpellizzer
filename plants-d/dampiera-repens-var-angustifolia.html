Dampiera repens var. angustifolia

Dampiera repens var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf dampiera, is a low-growing, perennial plant native to Australia.

It features small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in fine hairs, and the plant thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. It is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to dry, sunny conditions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting native insect populations.
