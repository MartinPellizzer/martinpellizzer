Dictamnus albus subsp. dasycarpus

Dictamnus albus subsp.

dasycarpus, commonly known as the white dictamnus, is a perennial herb native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan. It features aromatic, glandular leaves that emit a strong, spicy scent when crushed.

The plant produces small, clustered white or pale pink flowers in early summer, which are followed by distinctive, hairy, round fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its ornamental value is often appreciated in gardens for its fragrance and foliage.
