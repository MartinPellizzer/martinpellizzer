Diplacus leptanthus

Diplacus leptanthus, commonly known as the slenderleaf honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open woodlands or rocky slopes.

Diplacus leptanthus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
