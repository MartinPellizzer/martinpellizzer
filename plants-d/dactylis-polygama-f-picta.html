Dactylis polygama f. picta

Dactylis polygama f. picta, commonly known as the painted dactylis, is a variant of the common dactylis grass.

It is a perennial grass species characterized by its distinctive variegated foliage, which features a mix of green and white or silver markings. The plant typically grows in temperate climates and is often found in meadows and grasslands. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a basal rosette structure that helps it retain moisture.

The inflorescence consists of spikelets that are arranged in panicles, contributing to its ornamental appeal in horticultural settings.
