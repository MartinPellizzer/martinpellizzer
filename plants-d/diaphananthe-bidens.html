Diaphananthe bidens

Diaphananthe bidens, commonly known as the two-toothed false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with slender stems and alternate leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in a composite head, and it produces achene fruits with pappus structures for wind dispersal.

The species is noted for its adaptability to various soil types and its role in local ecosystems.
