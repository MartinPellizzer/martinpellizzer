Dryopteris pachyrhachis var. crassipes

Dryopteris pachyrhachis var. crassipes, commonly known as the broad-leaf wood fern, is a variant of the giant shield fern.

It is characterized by its large, leathery, deeply lobed fronds that can grow up to 1.5 meters in length. The fronds are typically bipinnate, with a distinctive, coarse texture and a dark green color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forests and along stream banks.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appearance and is commonly cultivated in gardens with the right conditions.
