Delphinium exaltatum var. barbeyi

Delphinium exaltatum var. barbeyi, commonly known as the Barbet's larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features tall, slender stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, supporting clusters of striking blue to violet flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem, and its flowers are borne in dense, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
