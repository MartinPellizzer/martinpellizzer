Desideria flabellata

Desideria flabellata, commonly known as the golden flabella, is a striking member of the Araliaceae family native to the tropical regions of South America.

This plant is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves that often display a vibrant yellow or golden-green coloration. It produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by bright red berries that attract various bird species. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden spaces.

Its ornamental value and unique foliage make it a sought-after plant in botanical collections and tropical gardens.
