Dianthus saxifragus

Dianthus saxifragus, commonly known as the rock campion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant pink or red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the flowers are borne in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Dianthus saxifragus is a hardy perennial that thrives in full sun and well-drained soil.
