Dioscorea chouardii

Dioscorea chouardii, commonly known as Chouard's yam, is a species of tuberous vine native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences, and its tuberous roots are the primary part used for medicinal and culinary purposes.

Dioscorea chouardii is valued for its potential health benefits, including its use in traditional medicine for treating various ailments.
