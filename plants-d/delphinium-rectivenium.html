Delphinium rectivenium

Delphinium rectivenium, commonly known as the alpine larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces clusters of showy blue to purple flowers, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, adapting to the cold, high-altitude environment.

Delphinium rectivenium is a member of the Ranunculaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal in alpine gardens.
