Dianella longifolia var. stenophylla

Dianella longifolia var. stenophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved flax lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color and can grow up to 1 meter in height. The plant produces small, bell-shaped white or pale blue flowers in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively low maintenance.
