Dendrobium latifrons

Dendrobium latifrons, commonly known as the broad-leaved orchid, is a sympodial epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features long, cane-like stems that can grow up to several meters in height, with numerous fleshy, strap-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green, often with a distinctive red or purple lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining potting media.

Dendrobium latifrons is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
