Diplotaxis erucoides subvar. valentina

Diplotaxis erucoides subvar. valentina, commonly known as the Valentine's mustard, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, yellow, cruciform flowers and linear, hairy leaves. The plant belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other mustard species. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Its ornamental value has led to its cultivation in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
