Dampiera eriocephala

Dampiera eriocephala, commonly known as the woolly daisy, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy, woolly leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats. It produces small, round seed heads that are covered in fine, white hairs, aiding in wind dispersal.

Dampiera eriocephala is well adapted to arid conditions and is considered a hardy, drought-tolerant species.
