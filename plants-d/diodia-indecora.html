Diodia indecora

Diodia indecora, commonly known as false holly or stinkweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that typically reaches heights of 1 to 3 feet. The plant features small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Diodia indecora is known for its strong, unpleasant odor, which is most noticeable when the plant is crushed or in bloom.
