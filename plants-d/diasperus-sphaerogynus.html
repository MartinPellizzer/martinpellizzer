Diasperus sphaerogynus

Diasperus sphaerogynus, commonly known as the globular burr grass, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its spherical, spiky seed heads that form dense, woolly clusters. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the plant is often found in sandy or rocky soils.

Diasperus sphaerogynus is adapted to arid environments and is drought-tolerant.
