Digitaria longiflora var. prorepens

Digitaria longiflora var. prorepens, commonly known as the long-flowered digitaria, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in open areas such as fields and disturbed soils, exhibiting a spreading growth habit. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves, and its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a panicle. It produces small, two-flowered spikelets that are often pale green to brown in color.

This variety is noted for its adaptability to various environmental conditions, making it a common weed in agricultural settings.
