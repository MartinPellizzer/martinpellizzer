Drepanostachyum ampullare

Drepanostachyum ampullare, commonly known as the bottlebrush bamboo, is a unique grass-like plant native to the wetlands of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit and its striking, bottle-shaped inflorescences that resemble a cluster of bottles. The plant produces small, spike-like flowers that are typically pale green to reddish in color. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread and form dense mats in shallow water or moist soil.

Drepanostachyum ampullare is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in aquatic environments.
