Dictamnus albus subsp. caucasicus

Dictamnus albus subsp.

caucasicus, commonly known as Caucasian white dictamnus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region. It features aromatic, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months.

The plant has slender, wiry stems and lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive, fragrant scent. Its root system is fibrous, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
