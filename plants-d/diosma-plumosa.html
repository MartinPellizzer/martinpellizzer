Diosma plumosa

Diosma plumosa, commonly known as the feathery diosma, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its delicate, feathery foliage. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale purple, often arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its unique leaf structure and showy blooms.
