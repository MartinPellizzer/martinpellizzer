Dendrobium hercoglossum var. album

Dendrobium hercoglossum var. album, commonly known as the white orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

This epiphytic plant is characterized by its long, cane-like stems that can grow up to several meters in length. The flowers are typically white or pale yellow, with a distinctive, spurred lip that is often more prominent than the other petals. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light and requires well-draining potting media.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated for its beautiful, fragrant blooms.
