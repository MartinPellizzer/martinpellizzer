Deschampsia cespitosa var. oliveri

Deschampsia cespitosa var. oliveri, commonly known as the Oliver's bluegrass, is a variant of bluegrass that is native to the western United States.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and slender, bluish-green foliage. The plant typically forms dense mats or clumps, with inflorescences consisting of spikelets that are often pale blue or greenish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in various ecological settings.
