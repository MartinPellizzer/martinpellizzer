Dalea argyrostachya

Dalea argyrostachya, commonly known as silverleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which helps it conserve water in arid environments. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Dalea argyrostachya is a member of the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing capabilities, which contribute to soil fertility.
