Desmodium mauritianum

Desmodium mauritianum, commonly known as the Mauritius burr, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive seed pods. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its seeds are enclosed in hairy, spiny pods that detach from the plant and can adhere to animal fur or clothing, aiding in seed dispersal.

This species is often found in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
