Desmodium glaucescens

Desmodium glaucescens, commonly known as blue desmodium, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features trifoliate leaves with a bluish-green hue, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. Its seed pods are typically slender and elongated, containing several round seeds.

Desmodium glaucescens is often used in traditional medicine and as a forage crop due to its nutritional value.
