Dimorphanthes myosotifolia

Dimorphanthes myosotifolia, commonly known as the blue bell flower, is a herbaceous perennial native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped blue or purple flowers. The plant has finely divided, grass-like leaves and produces small, seed-like fruits called cypselae. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant color and extended blooming period.
