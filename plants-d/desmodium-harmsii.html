Desmodium harmsii

Desmodium harmsii, commonly known as the purple desmodium, is a leguminous plant native to parts of Africa and the Americas.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are often a striking purple or reddish hue, and its small, pea-like flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, and it is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils. Desmodium harmsii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it beneficial for soil enrichment in agricultural settings.
