Disa sagittalis

Disa sagittalis, commonly known as the arrowhead disa, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in grasslands and rocky areas, often in full sun. The plant features slender, erect stems with lance-shaped leaves that are often arranged in a rosette. Its most striking feature is its showy, spurred flowers that are usually pink or purple, with a distinctive arrowhead-shaped lip.

Disa sagittalis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
