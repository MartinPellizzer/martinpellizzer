Deyeuxia arundinacea var. sciuroides

Deyeuxia arundinacea var. sciuroides, commonly known as the silvergrass or silver reed, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-gray foliage and tufted inflorescences that produce small, spikelet-like flowers. The plant typically grows in moist or wetland habitats, such as marshes and stream banks, and is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive appearance. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive silvery sheen that enhances its visual appeal.

The plant plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
