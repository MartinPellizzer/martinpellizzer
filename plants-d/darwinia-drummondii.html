Darwinia drummondii

Darwinia drummondii, commonly known as the Drummond's Darwinia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, showy spikes of tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in heathland and open woodland habitats, preferring well-drained soils. It produces small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Darwinia drummondii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
