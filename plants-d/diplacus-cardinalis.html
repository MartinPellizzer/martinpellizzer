Diplacus cardinalis

Diplacus cardinalis, commonly known as the scarlet gilia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators like bees and hummingbirds.

Diplacus cardinalis is an annual plant that thrives in full sun and well-drained soils.
