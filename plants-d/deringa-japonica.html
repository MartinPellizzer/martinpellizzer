Deringa japonica

Deringa japonica, commonly known as the Japanese bellflower, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Deringa japonica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
