Dioscorea mundii

Dioscorea mundii, commonly known as the African yam, is a climbing vine native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary part used for culinary and medicinal purposes. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are hairy and twining.

Dioscorea mundii is valued for its nutritional content and traditional use in various African cultures.
