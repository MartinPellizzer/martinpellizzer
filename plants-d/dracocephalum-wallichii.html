Dracocephalum wallichii

Dracocephalum wallichii, commonly known as Himalayan blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. It is often found in rocky and alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
