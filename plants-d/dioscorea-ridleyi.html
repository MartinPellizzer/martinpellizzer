Dioscorea ridleyi

Dioscorea ridleyi, commonly known as the Ridley yam, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary storage organs. The plant has alternate, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers. Its tubers are used in traditional medicine and as a food source in some regions.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in its native habitat.
