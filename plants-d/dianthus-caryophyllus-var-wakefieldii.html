Dianthus caryophyllus var. wakefieldii

Dianthus caryophyllus var. wakefieldii, commonly known as the Wakefield's carnation, is a cultivar of the carnation plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its vibrant, double flowers that come in a range of colors, including pink, red, and white. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and has a compact, bushy habit. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it thrives in full sun with well-drained soil.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.
