Dendrobium cedricola

Dendrobium cedricola, commonly known as the Ceylon orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often near streams or in dense forests. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the surrounding soil.

Dendrobium cedricola is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for its delicate floral display.
