Dendrobium pseudocalceolum

Dendrobium pseudocalceolum, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically blooms in late winter or early spring, producing white or pale yellow flowers with a distinctive, spurred lip. It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or shaded outdoor areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated for horticultural purposes.
