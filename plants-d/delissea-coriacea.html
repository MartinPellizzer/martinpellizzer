Delissea coriacea

Delissea coriacea, commonly known as the rough-leaved delissea, is a perennial flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are covered in a rough, textured surface. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Delissea coriacea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
