Dianthus carthusianorum var. basalticus

Dianthus carthusianorum var. basalticus, commonly known as the basalt rock campion, is a rare and striking plant native to the rocky, alkaline soils of the Canary Islands.

It features vivid pink to red flowers with white veins, often appearing in dense clusters atop its low, sprawling stems. The plant is adapted to thrive in harsh, well-drained environments, showcasing thick, waxy leaves that reduce water loss. Its unique habitat and striking appearance make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

The variety is considered endangered due to habitat loss and limited distribution.
