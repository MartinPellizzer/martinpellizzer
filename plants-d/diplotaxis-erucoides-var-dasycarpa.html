Diplotaxis erucoides var. dasycarpa

Diplotaxis erucoides var. dasycarpa, commonly known as the African wall lettuce, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and basal rosette of narrow, fleshy, grass-like leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, terminal clusters, which are followed by elongated, ribbed fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

The species is notable for its medicinal uses and ornamental value in gardens.
