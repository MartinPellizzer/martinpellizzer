Dacryodes hexandra

Dacryodes hexandra, commonly known as the African snowbell, is a tropical tree native to West Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and elliptical leaves that are glossy on the upper surface. The plant produces small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by round, fleshy fruits containing a single seed. It is often found in moist lowland forests and is valued for its timber and medicinal properties.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in shaded environments.
