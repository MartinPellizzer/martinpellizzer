Delphinium brachycentrum subsp. beringii

Delphinium brachycentrum subsp.

beringii, commonly known as the Bering delphinium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Arctic regions of Russia and Alaska. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, spindly stems that bear large, showy blue to purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in open, rocky habitats and is adapted to cold, harsh climates. Its leaves are palmately compound, with finely divided lobes, and it produces follicles as its fruit type.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and its role in pollinator support in its native ecosystems.
