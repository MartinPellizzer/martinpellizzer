Dipterocarpus helicopteryx

Dipterocarpus helicopteryx, commonly known as the skyflower, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, spreading canopy and distinctive, pendulous flowers that resemble small, delicate umbrellas. The plant belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family, which is known for its economically important timber species. Its flowers are primarily pollinated by insects, and it produces woody fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind.

The tree plays a significant role in the ecological systems of its native habitats.
