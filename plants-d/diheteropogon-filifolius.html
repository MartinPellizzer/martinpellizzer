Diheteropogon filifolius

Diheteropogon filifolius, commonly known as the silky hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and dense, silky foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, spikelet-bearing branches.

Diheteropogon filifolius plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
