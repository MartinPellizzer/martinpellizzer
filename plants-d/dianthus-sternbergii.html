Dianthus sternbergii

Dianthus sternbergii, commonly known as the Sierra dianthus, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, pinkish-red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, rounded leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Dianthus sternbergii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
