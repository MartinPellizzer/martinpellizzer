Dypsis malcomberi

Dypsis malcomberi, commonly known as the Malcomber's areca, is a species of palm native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves that give it a distinctive tropical appearance. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments within the rainforest understory. Its flowers are small and clustered, producing fruits that are edible for certain bird species.

Dypsis malcomberi is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
