Diapedium repens f. coeruleum

Diapedium repens f. coeruleum, commonly known as the blue-flowered diapedium, is a variant of the diapedium repens, a species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue petals, which contrast with its green foliage, making it a visually appealing plant. The plant typically grows in a spreading habit, with slender stems that root at the nodes, allowing it to form dense mats. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, thriving in Mediterranean climates.

Its ornamental value has made it a popular choice for rock gardens and as a ground cover in horticultural settings.
