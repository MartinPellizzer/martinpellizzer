Duchesnea indica var. albicarpa

Duchesnea indica var. albicarpa, commonly known as white-fruited wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant is characterized by its edible, white, fleshy fruits that are borne on slender pedicels. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in culinary and medicinal practices.
