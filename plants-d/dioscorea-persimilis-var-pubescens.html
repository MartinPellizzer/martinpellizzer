Dioscorea persimilis var. pubescens

Dioscorea persimilis var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy yam, is a perennial vine native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by tuberous roots that are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a rough texture, and the plant thrives in humid, shaded environments.

The tubers of this variety are sometimes cultivated for their potential medicinal properties.
