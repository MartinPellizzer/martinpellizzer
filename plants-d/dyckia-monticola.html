Dyckia monticola

Dyckia monticola, commonly known as the mountain dyckia, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the bromeliad family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a dense, water-holding structure. The plant produces showy inflorescences with bright orange flowers that bloom in spring. Dyckia monticola thrives in sunny, well-drained environments and is often found in rocky or mountainous regions.

Its unique morphology and ornamental value make it a popular choice for botanical gardens and collections.
