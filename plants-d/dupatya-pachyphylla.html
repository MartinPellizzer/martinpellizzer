Dupatya pachyphylla

Dupatya pachyphylla, commonly known as the thick-leaved desert plant, is a succulent species native to arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, enabling it to survive in dry environments. The plant has a compact, shrub-like growth habit with dense foliage that helps reduce water loss. Its leaves are typically green, sometimes with a bluish tint, and are arranged in a rosette pattern.

Dupatya pachyphylla is well adapted to extreme temperatures and low precipitation, making it a resilient component of desert ecosystems.
