Daphne coriacea

Daphne coriacea, commonly known as the roughleaf daphne, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and are typically borne on short pedicels. The leaves are leathery, ovate, and often have a rough texture, contributing to its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

The plant is noted for its fragrant blooms and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
