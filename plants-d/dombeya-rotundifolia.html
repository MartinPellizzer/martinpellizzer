Dombeya rotundifolia

Dombeya rotundifolia, commonly known as the round-leaf dombeya, is a deciduous shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its large, rounded, velvety leaves and showy clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Dombeya rotundifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
