Dioscorea schomburgkiana

Dioscorea schomburgkiana, commonly known as the Schomburgk yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary storage organs. The plant produces large, heart-shaped leaves and bears yellowish-white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its tubers are edible when properly prepared and have been used in traditional cuisines.

The species is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
