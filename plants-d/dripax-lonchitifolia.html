Dripax lonchitifolia

Dripax lonchitifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed daphne, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and prefers partial to full shade. Its flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Dripax lonchitifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
