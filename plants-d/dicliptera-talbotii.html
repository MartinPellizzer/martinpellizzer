Dicliptera talbotii

Dicliptera talbotii, commonly known as the African grass or Talbot's grass, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is often found in open grasslands and along riverbanks, thriving in well-drained soils.

Dicliptera talbotii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its hardy nature.
