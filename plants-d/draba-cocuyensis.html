Draba cocuyensis

Draba cocuyensis, commonly known as the Cocuy Mountain whitlow, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The species is characterized by its small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, adapting to the harsh conditions of high elevations.

Draba cocuyensis is often found in the Cocuy Mountains of Colombia, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
