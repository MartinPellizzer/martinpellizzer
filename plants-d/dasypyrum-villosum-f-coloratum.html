Dasypyrum villosum f. coloratum

Dasypyrum villosum f. coloratum, commonly known as the colored fescue, is a variant of the common fescue species.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and dense foliage. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves with a distinctive blue-green coloration, which sets it apart from other fescue varieties. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal.

The inflorescence consists of spikelets that are typically pale green to reddish in color, adding visual interest to the plant.
