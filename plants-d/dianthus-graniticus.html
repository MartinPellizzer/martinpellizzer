Dianthus graniticus

Dianthus graniticus, commonly known as the granitic dianthus, is a flowering plant native to the rocky regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that come in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant typically produces clusters of small, rounded petals with a distinctive frilled edge, giving it a delicate appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Dianthus graniticus is also valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
