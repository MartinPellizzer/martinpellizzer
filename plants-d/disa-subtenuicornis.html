Disa subtenuicornis

Disa subtenuicornis, commonly known as the slender horned disa, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and showy, spurred flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas. Its unique floral structure includes a long, slender column that is a distinguishing feature.

Disa subtenuicornis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
