Darwinia whicherensis

Darwinia whicherensis, commonly known as the Mount William darwinia, is an evergreen shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its dense, cylindrical flower spikes that appear in late winter to early spring. The plant produces small, pale green to yellowish flowers that are clustered in tight, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Darwinia whicherensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
