Delphinium hybridum var. pallidiflorum

Delphinium hybridum var. pallidiflorum, commonly known as the pale-flowered larkspur, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features tall, sparsely branched stems that bear clusters of showy, blue to violet flowers with prominent white or pale pink markings. The leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens. This variety is well-suited for moist, well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for both ornamental gardening and ecological landscapes.
