Dianthus gracilis

Dianthus gracilis, commonly known as slender campion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its showy, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. Its flowers are usually pink or white, with five petals that are often notched at the edges.

Dianthus gracilis is known for its delicate appearance and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
