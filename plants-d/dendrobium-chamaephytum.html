Dendrobium chamaephytum

Dendrobium chamaephytum, commonly known as the ground orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, grass-like stems and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. It has a fibrous root system and produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients.

Dendrobium chamaephytum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
