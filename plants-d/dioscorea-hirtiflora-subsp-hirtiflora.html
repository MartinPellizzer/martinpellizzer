Dioscorea hirtiflora subsp. hirtiflora

Dioscorea hirtiflora subsp.

hirtiflora, commonly known as the hairy-flowered yam, is a member of the Dioscoreaceae family. It is a climbing vine characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves.

The plant produces tubers that are typically yellow or brown in color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in humid, shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both horticulture and traditional medicine.
