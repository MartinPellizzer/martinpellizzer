Dactylorhiza elata

Dactylorhiza elata, commonly known as the tall marsh orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach up to 1 meter in height, adorned with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of pale yellow to greenish-yellow flowers, which are often fragrant and attract various pollinators. Dactylorhiza elata thrives in wet or moist habitats such as bogs, marshes, and meadows.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its ecological importance in supporting biodiversity.
