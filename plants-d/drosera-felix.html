Drosera felix

Drosera felix, commonly known as the pink sundew, is a carnivorous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette of sticky, glandular leaves that trap and digest insects. The plant produces tall, slender flower stalks bearing small, pinkish-white flowers. It thrives in moist, acidic environments and requires high humidity to flourish.

Its unique adaptations make it a fascinating subject for botanical study.
