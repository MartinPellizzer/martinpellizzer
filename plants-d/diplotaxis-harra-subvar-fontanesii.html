Diplotaxis harra subvar. fontanesii

Diplotaxis harra subvar. fontanesii, commonly known as the Cape daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb with erect, branching stems and bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are aromatic and slightly hairy. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
