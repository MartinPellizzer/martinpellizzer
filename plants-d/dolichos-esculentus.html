Dolichos esculentus

Dolichos esculentus, commonly known as the hyacinth bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It produces distinctive, hyacinth-like flower clusters and elongated, edible pods that contain small, kidney-shaped seeds. The plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and vigorous climbing habit, often used as a cover crop or ornamental in gardens. Its seeds are rich in protein and are used in various cuisines, while the pods can be cooked as a vegetable.

The plant also has medicinal uses in traditional medicine systems.
