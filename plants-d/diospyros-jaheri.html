Diospyros jaheri

Diospyros jaheri, commonly known as the Persian date palm, is a deciduous tree native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clustered inflorescences. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into edible, sweet fruits resembling dates. It thrives in arid and semi-arid climates, making it well-suited for cultivation in regions with limited water resources.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental qualities and historical significance in traditional medicine.
