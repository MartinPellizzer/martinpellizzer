Drymocallis viscida

Drymocallis viscida, commonly known as the sticky goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces small, oval, sticky seeds that are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

Drymocallis viscida thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and meadows.
