Digitalis ferruginea var. parviflora

Digitalis ferruginea var. parviflora, commonly known as the Downy Foxglove, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It features tall, sparsely branched stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to purple. The leaves are lance-shaped, with a velvety texture and a reddish-brown underside. It thrives in well-drained, alkaline soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
