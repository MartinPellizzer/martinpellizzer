Delphinium darginicum

Delphinium darginicum, commonly known as the Darginian larkspur, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems that bear clusters of blue to purple flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant has palmate leaves with deeply lobed segments, and its roots are fibrous and adapted to rocky soils. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy addition to alpine gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
