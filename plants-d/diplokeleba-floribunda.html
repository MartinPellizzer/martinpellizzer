Diplokeleba floribunda

Diplokeleba floribunda, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow to orange bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along its stems. It prefers well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Its unique floral structure and vibrant color make it a notable species in botanical gardens and horticultural collections.
