Dianthus algetanus subsp. algetanus

Dianthus algetanus subsp.

algetanus, commonly known as the Algetan campion, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its low-growing habit and produces clusters of pink or red flowers with five petals.

The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in open habitats such as slopes and meadows. Dianthus algetanus subsp.

algetanus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
