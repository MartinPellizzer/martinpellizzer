Dalechampia tiliifolia var. peruviana

Dalechampia tiliifolia var. peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian horse chestnut, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in pendulous inflorescences. Its fruit is a spiny capsule that contains several smooth, chestnut-like seeds.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to warm, humid climates.
