Didiscus procumbens var. typicus

Didiscus procumbens var. typicus, commonly known as the creeping didiscus, is a low-growing perennial plant native to certain regions of South America.

It features succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, allowing it to store water efficiently. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish-white in color, which bloom during the warmer months. Its stems are prostrate, enabling it to spread along the ground and form dense mats.

This variety is well adapted to arid or semi-arid environments, making it a resilient choice for xeriscaping.
