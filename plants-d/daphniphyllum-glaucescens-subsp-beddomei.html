Daphniphyllum glaucescens subsp. beddomei

Daphniphyllum glaucescens subsp.

beddomei, commonly known as the Beddome's holly, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the western Ghats of India. It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant produces bright red berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions with high humidity.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
