Dryopsis truncata

Dryopsis truncata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved dryopsis, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces clusters of pale blue to purple flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is typically found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
