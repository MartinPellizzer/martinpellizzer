Dianthus tetralepis

Dianthus tetralepis, commonly known as the four-lobed campion, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its showy, pink or red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are linear and slightly hairy, while its stems are erect and branched.

Dianthus tetralepis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
