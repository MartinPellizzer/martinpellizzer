Dioscorea campestris var. longispicata

Dioscorea campestris var. longispicata, commonly known as the long-spined yam, is a tropical vine belonging to the family Dioscoreaceae.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and the presence of long, spiny tubers that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by cylindrical, yellow fruits containing numerous seeds. Its tubers are typically harvested for their medicinal properties and have been used in various herbal preparations.

The species is native to parts of Africa and is adapted to grow in moist, shaded environments.
