Droguetia thunbergii

Droguetia thunbergii, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine white hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

The botanical features of Droguetia thunbergii make it an attractive and ecologically valuable addition to native plant gardens.
