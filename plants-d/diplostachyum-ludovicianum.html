Diplostachyum ludovicianum

Diplostachyum ludovicianum, commonly known as the Florida flame bamboo, is a member of the grass family (Poaceae).

It is a tall, clump-forming plant that can reach heights of up to 12 feet, characterized by its striking orange to red inflorescences. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in dense, colorful panicles. It is native to the southeastern United States, particularly Florida, and thrives in wetland environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in wetland restoration projects.
