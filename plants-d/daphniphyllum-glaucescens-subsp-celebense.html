Daphniphyllum glaucescens subsp. celebense

Daphniphyllum glaucescens subsp.

celebense, commonly known as the Celebes daphniphyllum, is a species native to the Indonesian island of Sulawesi. It is a small evergreen shrub characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers.

The plant produces elliptical, red-brown berries that are an important food source for local wildlife. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Due to its restricted distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
