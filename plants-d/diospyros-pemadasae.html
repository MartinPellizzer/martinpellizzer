Diospyros pemadasae

Diospyros pemadasae, commonly known as the Pemadasa date palm, is a species native to the island of Socotra.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height, characterized by its thick, fibrous trunk and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by edible drupes, which are similar in appearance to dates.

It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showing adaptations to the unique climate of its native habitat.
