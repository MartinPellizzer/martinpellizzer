Diplopappus cuneatus

Diplopappus cuneatus, commonly known as the African dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and fibrous root system. The plant typically grows in grasslands and disturbed soils, thriving in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture, while the flowers are yellow and produce a distinctive seed dispersal mechanism.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
