Drosanthemum vaginatum

Drosanthemum vaginatum, commonly known as the velvetleaf ice plant, is a succulent shrub native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, cylindrical leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, silky hairs, giving it a velvety appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of yellow and orange, typically blooming in spring and summer. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique morphology and adaptive features make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
