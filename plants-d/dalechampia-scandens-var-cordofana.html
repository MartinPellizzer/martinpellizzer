Dalechampia scandens var. cordofana

Dalechampia scandens var. cordofana, commonly known as the velvetleaf or false hibiscus, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that features heart-shaped, velvety leaves with a distinctive waxy texture. The plant produces showy, yellow-centered flowers that are often surrounded by pale green bracts. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in disturbed soils.

Its botanical characteristics make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ecological studies.
