Distichorchis melanotricha

Distichorchis melanotricha, commonly known as the black-haired orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique floral structure. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in a loose inflorescence. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, often growing in dense clusters.

Distichorchis melanotricha is notable for its specialized pollination mechanism involving specific insect species.
