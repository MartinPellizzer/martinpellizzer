Draba moupinensis var. dasycarpa

Draba moupinensis var. dasycarpa, commonly known as the hairy-seeded mouse-ear cress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are basal, rosette-forming, and covered in fine, glandular hairs.

The varietal name *dasycarpa* refers to its densely hairy, fleshy fruiting structures.
