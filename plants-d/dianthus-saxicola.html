Dianthus saxicola

Dianthus saxicola, commonly known as the rock campion, is a flowering plant native to rocky and alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically produces small, clustered flowers with five petals, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in crevices and on rocky outcrops.

Dianthus saxicola is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and beauty.
