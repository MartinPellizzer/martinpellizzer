Dryas octopetala var. viscida

Dryas octopetala var. viscida, commonly known as the sticky dryas, is a low-growing perennial plant native to alpine and arctic regions.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces white, five-petaled flowers in early summer, which are clustered in groups of eight, giving it the species name "octopetala.". Its leaves are usually covered in a fine, sticky coating, which is a distinguishing feature of the varietal form viscida.

This plant is well adapted to cold, harsh environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
