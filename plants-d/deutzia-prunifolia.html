Deutzia prunifolia

Deutzia prunifolia, commonly known as the cherryleaf deutzia, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring, typically in late April to early May. The plant has slender, arching stems and small, ovate leaves that resemble those of cherry trees, giving it its common name. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Deutzia prunifolia is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
