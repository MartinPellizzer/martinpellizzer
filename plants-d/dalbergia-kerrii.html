Dalbergia kerrii

Dalbergia kerrii, commonly known as Kerri's ebony, is a small to medium-sized leguminous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its dark, dense wood that is highly valued for its durability and aesthetic appeal. The plant has bipinnate leaves and produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are often clustered in racemes. Its fruit is a legume pod containing several flat, kidney-shaped seeds.

Dalbergia kerrii is also noted for its ecological importance in providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
