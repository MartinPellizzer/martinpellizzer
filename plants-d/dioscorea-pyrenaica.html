Dioscorea pyrenaica

Dioscorea pyrenaica, commonly known as the Pyrenean yam, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are heart-shaped and its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in loose panicles.

Dioscorea pyrenaica is valued for its medicinal properties and is also an important species in the study of wild yams.
