Dypsis vilersiana

Dypsis vilersiana, commonly known as the Madagascar palm, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its short, thick trunk and clustered, spiny leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by edible, coconut-like fruits. It is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Dypsis vilersiana is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and resilience.
