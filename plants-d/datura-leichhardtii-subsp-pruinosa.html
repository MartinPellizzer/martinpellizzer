Datura leichhardtii subsp. pruinosa

Datura leichhardtii subsp.

pruinosa, commonly known as the Pruinose Thornapple, is a flowering plant native to Australia. It is characterized by its large, showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale purple.

The plant has hairy, glandular leaves that give it a distinctive, rough texture. It produces spiny, spherical fruits that contain toxic seeds.

This subspecies is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its resilience to drought conditions.
