Delphinium peregrinum subsp. eriocarpum

Delphinium peregrinum subsp.

eriocarpum, commonly known as the woolly delphinium, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters.

The plant produces showy, blue to violet flowers arranged in dense spikes, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, woolly coating, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
