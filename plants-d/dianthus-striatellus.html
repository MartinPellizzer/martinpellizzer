Dianthus striatellus

Dianthus striatellus, commonly known as the striped campion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant pink or red flowers with white markings, which are typically arranged in clusters at the top of slender stems. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

Dianthus striatellus is also known for its long flowering period, which extends from late spring through early autumn.
