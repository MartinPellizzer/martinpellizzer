Delphinium pauciflorum var. sonnei

Delphinium pauciflorum var. sonnei, commonly known as Sonne's larkspur, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, thriving in cooler climates.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique floral structure and its role in local ecosystems.
