Declieuxia cordigera var. floribunda

Declieuxia cordigera var. floribunda, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a variant of the broadleaf bamboo species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow leaves that contrast with its slender, green stems. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically blooming in the spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
