Dracocephalum ferganicum

Dracocephalum ferganicum, commonly known as Fergana bluehead, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and small, clustered flowers that range in color from blue to purple. It is often found in rocky or mountainous regions, thriving in well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
