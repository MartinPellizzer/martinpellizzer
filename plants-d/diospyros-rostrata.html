Diospyros rostrata

Diospyros rostrata, commonly known as the African persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other well-known species like the ebony tree. The plant is characterized by its elliptical leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into orange to red berries.

Diospyros rostrata is valued for its hard, dense wood and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
