Disocactus × capelleanus

Disocactus × capelleanus, commonly known as the hybrid cactus or Capelleanus cactus, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Disocactus phyllosporus and Disocactus leucanthus.

It is a member of the Cactaceae family and is native to Mexico, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that store water and its clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and ability to adapt to various growing conditions.
