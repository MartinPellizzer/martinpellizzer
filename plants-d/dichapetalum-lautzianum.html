Dichapetalum lautzianum

Dichapetalum lautzianum, commonly known as the African star orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, star-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or lithophyte, attaching itself to trees or rocks in its natural habitat. Its leaves are linear and often arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces fragrant blooms that attract pollinators.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts worldwide.
