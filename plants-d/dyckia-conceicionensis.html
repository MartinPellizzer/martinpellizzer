Dyckia conceicionensis

Dyckia conceicionensis, commonly known as the Conceição Dyckia, is a striking bromeliad native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are typically blue-gray in color, with a waxy coating that helps reduce water loss. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences that bear small, white or pale pink flowers. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and container cultivation.
