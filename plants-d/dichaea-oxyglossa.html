Dichaea oxyglossa

Dichaea oxyglossa, commonly known as the silver-tipped bog orchid, is a species of orchid native to the wetlands of South America.

It is a small, terrestrial herb with a distinctive rosette of narrow, silvery-green leaves. The plant produces delicate, white or pale pink flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in acidic, moist environments and is often found in bogs or swampy areas.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
