Deschampsia cespitosa subsp. subtriflora

Deschampsia cespitosa subsp.

subtriflora, commonly known as slender bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to North America. It is characterized by its fine, blue-green blades and forms dense, tufted clumps.

The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as meadows, marshes, and along stream banks. It produces slender, spike-like inflorescences with small, spikelet-based flowers.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
