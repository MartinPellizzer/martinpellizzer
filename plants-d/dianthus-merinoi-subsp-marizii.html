Dianthus merinoi subsp. marizii

Dianthus merinoi subsp.

marizii, commonly known as the Marizii Campion, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes in South America. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces vibrant pink to red flowers with prominent white or pink central markings.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun conditions. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery coating.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in alpine gardens and rockeries.
