Dendrophorbium onae var. leonis

Dendrophorbium onae var. leonis, commonly known as the golden thistle, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers and robust, spiny stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its foliage consists of narrow, gray-green leaves with a hairy texture.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
