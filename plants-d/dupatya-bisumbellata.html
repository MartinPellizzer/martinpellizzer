Dupatya bisumbellata

Dupatya bisumbellata, commonly known as the Indian cowpea or velvet bean, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds, which are used in various culinary applications. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its nutritional value and medicinal properties.

The plant's root system also has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, enhancing soil fertility.
