Dracocephalum louisianum

Dracocephalum louisianum, commonly known as Louisiana hyssop, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Its name derives from the Greek words "drakon" meaning dragon and "kephale" meaning head, likely referring to the shape of its flowers.
