Dortmanna sonchifolia

Dortmanna sonchifolia, commonly known as the common dock, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of large, heart-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Dortmanna sonchifolia is a perennial plant that plays an important role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
