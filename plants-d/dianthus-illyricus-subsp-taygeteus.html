Dianthus illyricus subsp. taygeteus

Dianthus illyricus subsp.

taygeteus, commonly known as the Taygetean campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has linear, hairy leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or alpine environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
