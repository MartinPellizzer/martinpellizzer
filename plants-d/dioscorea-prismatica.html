Dioscorea prismatica

Dioscorea prismatica, commonly known as the prismatic yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant has cylindrical, ridged stems and heart-shaped leaves with a prominent midrib. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically greenish-white in color.

The tubers of Dioscorea prismatica are valued for their nutritional content and have been used in various culinary and medicinal applications.
