Diospyros olacinoides

Diospyros olacinoides, commonly known as the African persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and elliptical leaves with a pointed tip. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into orange-red, fleshy fruits. The fruit is edible, though some varieties have astringent seeds that require soaking to remove.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and traditional uses in local medicine.
