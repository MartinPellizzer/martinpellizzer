Derris amherstianus

Derris amherstianus, commonly known as the Amherst's devil's ivy, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Leguminosae family and is characterized by its woody stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by elongated pods containing kidney-shaped seeds. It is often found in humid environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
