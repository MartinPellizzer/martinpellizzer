Dianthus bicolor var. minor

Dianthus bicolor var. minor, commonly known as the pink carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its showy, bicolor petals that typically feature a white base with a pink or red edge. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long-lasting blooms.
