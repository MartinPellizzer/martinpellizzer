Diervilla florida f. candida

Diervilla florida f. candida, commonly known as the white-flowered hazelnut, is a compact shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features dense, upright growth with small, oval leaves that turn reddish in the fall. The plant produces fragrant, white, tubular flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. Its flowers are clustered in upright panicles, adding ornamental value to gardens.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile choice for landscaping.
