Dalbergia acuminata

Dalbergia acuminata, commonly known as the African bloodwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its hard, dark wood that is highly valued for its durability and fine grain. The plant has compound leaves with long, slender leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

The tree is also known for its ability to secrete a resinous substance, which contributes to its unique appearance and ecological significance.
