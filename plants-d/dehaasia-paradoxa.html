Dehaasia paradoxa

Dehaasia paradoxa, commonly known as the paradoxical dehaasia, is a rare and enigmatic plant species native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its unique, spirally arranged leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, which bloom during the cooler months. Its roots are deeply embedded in the soil, allowing it to survive in nutrient-poor and arid conditions.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Dehaasia paradoxa is considered a species of conservation concern.
