Dryopteris tricholepis

Dryopteris tricholepis, commonly known as the hairy wood fern, is a species of fern native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its fronds that are covered in fine, hairy scales, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides. Its sporangia are clustered in sori, which are located on the underside of the fronds.

Dryopteris tricholepis is a perennial plant that can reach heights of up to 1 meter, making it a notable presence in its natural habitat.
