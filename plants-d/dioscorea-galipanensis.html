Dioscorea galipanensis

Dioscorea galipanensis, commonly known as the Philippine yam, is a climbing herbaceous plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary edible part. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces small, greenish-white flowers. Its tubers are often used in local cuisines and have been traditionally valued for their nutritional content.

The species is also of interest for its potential in pharmaceutical research due to the presence of bioactive compounds.
