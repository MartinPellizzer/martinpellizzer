Dactylorchis maculata var. angustiflora

Dactylorchis maculata var. angustiflora, commonly known as the narrow-lipped spotted orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in shaded, moist forest environments, where it thrives in the understory with partial sunlight. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces delicate, fragrant flowers with a distinctive spotted pattern on the petals. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white, with a contrasting red or purple lip.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
