Dissotis procumbens

Dissotis procumbens, commonly known as the prostrate spiderling, is a tropical perennial herb native to regions of Southeast Asia and northern Australia.

It features prostrate growth habit with slender, branching stems that often root at the nodes. The plant produces small, white to pale purple flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on short pedicels. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic in shape, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.
