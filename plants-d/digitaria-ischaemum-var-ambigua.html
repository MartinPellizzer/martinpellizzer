Digitaria ischaemum var. ambigua

Digitaria ischaemum var. ambigua, commonly known as the ambiguous digitaria, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often hairy. The inflorescence consists of a loose panicle with small, two-lobed spikelets. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and along waterways.

This variant is closely related to other Digitaria species and is notable for its adaptability to various environmental conditions.
