Darea appendiculata

Darea appendiculata, commonly known as the woolly dandelion, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces a distinctive seed head with bristly, parachute-like seeds for wind dispersal. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and along trails.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.
