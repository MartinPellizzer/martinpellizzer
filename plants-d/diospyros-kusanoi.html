Diospyros kusanoi

Diospyros kusanoi, commonly known as the Japanese persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to Japan and parts of Korea.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by orange to reddish fruits in the fall. Its fruits are typically sweet and astringent when unripe, becoming soft and flavorful when fully mature.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional medicine.
