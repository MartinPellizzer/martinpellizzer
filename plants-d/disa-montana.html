Disa montana

Disa montana, commonly known as the mountain disa, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces a single spike of flowers during the summer months.

Disa montana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable growing conditions.
