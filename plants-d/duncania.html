Duncania

Duncania, commonly known as the alpine bellflower, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

These plants are typically found in alpine and arctic regions, where they thrive in cold, rocky environments. Duncania species are characterized by their bell-shaped flowers, which can range in color from white to blue or purple. They often have hairy leaves and stems adapted to withstand harsh weather conditions.

The genus includes several species, such as Duncania alpina and Duncania parvula, which are valued for their ornamental appeal and ecological importance in high-altitude ecosystems.
