Diptychum

Diptychum, commonly known as the double-flowered dandelion, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its composite flower heads, which consist of both ray and disk flowers, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and is often found in meadows, fields, and along roadsides. Its leaves are generally basal, alternate, and have a rough texture.

Diptychum species are known for their ability to thrive in disturbed soils and are often considered weeds in agricultural settings.
