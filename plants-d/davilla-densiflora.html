Davilla densiflora

Davilla densiflora, commonly known as the denseflower davisia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy on the underside. Its showy, white to pale yellow flowers appear in clusters during the spring and summer months.

Davilla densiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its attractive foliage and fragrant blooms.
