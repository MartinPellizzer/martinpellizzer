Dipteranthus pellucidus

Dipteranthus pellucidus, commonly known as the glassy bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that are often translucent or semi-transparent, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories, where it thrives in partial shade. Its leaves are usually elliptical and have a smooth texture, while its stems are slender and often trailing.

Dipteranthus pellucidus is valued in horticulture for its unique appearance and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
