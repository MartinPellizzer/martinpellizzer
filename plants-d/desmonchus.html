Desmonchus

Desmonchus, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a genus of flowering plants in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

It is native to the Americas, particularly Mexico and Central America, and is characterized by its showy, bright orange or red composite flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are alternate, rough, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often hairy and branched.

Desmonchus species are valued for their ornamental appeal and are often cultivated in gardens for their vibrant blooms.
