Dalea bartonii

Dalea bartonii, commonly known as Barton's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Dalea bartonii is often found in open grasslands and sagebrush steppe, contributing to the ecological diversity of these regions.
