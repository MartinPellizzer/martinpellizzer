Dalechampia tricuspidata

Dalechampia tricuspidata, commonly known as the three-lobed euphorbia, is a herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, characterized by its unique floral structures and milky sap. The plant features alternate, heart-shaped leaves with three distinct lobes, giving it its common name. Its flowers are small and clustered in cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family.

The plant is often found in moist, open habitats such as woodlands and wetlands.
