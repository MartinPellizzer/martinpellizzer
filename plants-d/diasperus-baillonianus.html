Diasperus baillonianus

Diasperus baillonianus, commonly known as the Baillon's spurge, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its erect, branched stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its sap is milky and contains toxic compounds, which can cause skin irritation.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.
