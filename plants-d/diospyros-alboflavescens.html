Diospyros alboflavescens

Diospyros alboflavescens, commonly known as the white-flushed persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale-gray bark and lanceolate, glossy green leaves that turn reddish in autumn. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange to red, fleshy fruits that are edible when fully ripe. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in lowland forests or along riverbanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
