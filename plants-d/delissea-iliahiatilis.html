Delissea iliahiatilis

Delissea iliahiatilis, commonly known as the Hawaiian orchid, is a rare and endemic species native to the island of Hawaii.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories at high elevations. Its unique floral structure and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat loss and environmental changes, it is considered vulnerable, highlighting the need for continued protection and study.
