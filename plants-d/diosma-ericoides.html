Diosma ericoides

Diosma ericoides, commonly known as the dwarf heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the western United States.

It typically thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions. The plant has small, leathery leaves that are typically green or reddish in color, and it produces clusters of pale pink or white flowers in the spring. Diosma ericoides is adapted to harsh environmental conditions, making it a hardy and resilient plant.
