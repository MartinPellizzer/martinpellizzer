Doronicum bracteatum

Doronicum bracteatum, commonly known as the alpine goldcup, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy blooms. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are basal, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Doronicum bracteatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
