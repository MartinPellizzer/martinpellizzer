Dolichos pyramidalis

Dolichos pyramidalis, commonly known as the pyramid bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features compound leaves with elongated, pointed leaflets and produces clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant bears elongated, cylindrical pods that contain several seeds. It is often cultivated for its edible beans, which are rich in protein.

Botanically, it is characterized by its twining habit and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia.
