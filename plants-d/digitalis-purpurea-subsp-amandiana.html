Digitalis purpurea subsp. amandiana

Digitalis purpurea subsp.

amandiana, commonly known as the foxglove, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its tall, spires of tubular, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to purple.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture, and it produces seeds in a capsule that splits open when mature. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

The plant is notable for its medicinal properties, containing cardiac glycosides used in the treatment of heart conditions.
