Delphinium sulphureum

Delphinium sulphureum, commonly known as the sulphur delphinium, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems that bear dense clusters of showy, blue to purple flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained, loamy soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs, adding to its distinctive appearance.

Delphinium sulphureum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its vibrant, long-lasting blooms.
