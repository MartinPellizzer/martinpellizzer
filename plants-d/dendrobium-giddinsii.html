Dendrobium giddinsii

Dendrobium giddinsii, commonly known as the Giddins' orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, cane-like stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers with prominent sepals and petals. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining substrates for optimal growth.

Dendrobium giddinsii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its unique floral display.
