Drimys acutifolia

Drimys acutifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wintergreen, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows in moist, temperate forests. The plant features lance-shaped, dark green leaves and produces small, fragrant white flowers in the spring. Its fruit is a bright red berry that contains several seeds.

Drimys acutifolia is valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
