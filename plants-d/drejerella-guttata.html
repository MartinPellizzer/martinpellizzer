Drejerella guttata

Drejerella guttata, commonly known as the spotted bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically blue or violet in color, with a distinctive white or yellow spot on each petal. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a soft, textured appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The plant is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its vibrant, showy blooms.
