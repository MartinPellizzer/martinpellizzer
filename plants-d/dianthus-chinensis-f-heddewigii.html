Dianthus chinensis f. heddewigii

Dianthus chinensis f. heddewigii, commonly known as the Heddewigii carnation, is a compact, flowering plant native to China.

It features showy, double pink or white flowers with a distinctive red or purple center, often arranged in dense clusters. The leaves are narrow, grass-like, and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It is a hybrid form of the common carnation, prized for its vibrant blooms and compact growth habit.

This plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
