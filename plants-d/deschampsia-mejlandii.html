Deschampsia mejlandii

Deschampsia mejlandii, commonly known as mejland bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in sagebrush steppe and desert environments. The plant has slender, upright stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and bluish-green, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
