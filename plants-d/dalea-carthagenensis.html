Dalea carthagenensis

Dalea carthagenensis, commonly known as Carthage burrpea, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pink to red pea-like flowers. The plant produces small, hairy pods that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in desert and chaparral habitats.

Dalea carthagenensis is adapted to arid environments and is an important component of its native ecosystem.
