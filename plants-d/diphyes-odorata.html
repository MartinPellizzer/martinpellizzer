Diphyes odorata

Diphyes odorata, commonly known as the sweet myrtle, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its aromatic, often fragrant leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that typically bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are edible and used in traditional cuisines. Its leaves are often used in herbal remedies due to their medicinal properties.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
