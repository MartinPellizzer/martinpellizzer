Diospyros dajakensis

Diospyros dajakensis, commonly known as the Java persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by orange to red berries. Its fruit is edible when fully mature and has a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor.

Diospyros dajakensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
