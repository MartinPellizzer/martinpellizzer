Dendrobium maccarthiae

Dendrobium maccarthiae, commonly known as the Macarthur's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is an epiphytic plant, meaning it grows on the surface of trees or other plants without deriving nutrients from them. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences that bear numerous fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. Its leaves are elongated and strap-like, often arranged in a rosette pattern.

Dendrobium maccarthiae is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to cultivate under the right conditions.
