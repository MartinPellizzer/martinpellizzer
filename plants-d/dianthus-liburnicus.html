Dianthus liburnicus

Dianthus liburnicus, commonly known as the Balkan carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows in rocky or grassy areas and thrives in full sun. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces small, delicate seed pods after flowering.

Dianthus liburnicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
