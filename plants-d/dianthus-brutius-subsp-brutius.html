Dianthus brutius subsp. brutius

Dianthus brutius subsp.

brutius, commonly known as the pink campion, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens.
