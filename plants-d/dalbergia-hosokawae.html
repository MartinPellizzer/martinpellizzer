Dalbergia hosokawae

Dalbergia hosokawae, commonly known as the Japanese rosewood, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its small, papery leaves and clusters of pink to purple flowers. The plant produces small, flat, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides.

Due to its ornamental value and potential for medicinal use, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
