Didymocarpus newmannii

Didymocarpus newmannii, commonly known as the Newmann's flame tree, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall, characterized by its glossy, elongated leaves and striking orange-red flowers. The flowers are typically clustered in panicles and bloom during the summer months. The fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions.
