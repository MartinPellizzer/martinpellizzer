Dianella javanica f. rubra

Dianella javanica f. rubra, commonly known as the red-flowered onion grass, is a compact, evergreen perennial plant native to Australia.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color, with a dense rosette growth habit. The plant produces striking red or pink inflorescences that bloom in spring, adding a vibrant touch to gardens. It prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its contrasting foliage and showy flowers.
