Delphinium hookeri

Delphinium hookeri, commonly known as Hooker's larkspur, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers in racemes, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Delphinium hookeri is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
