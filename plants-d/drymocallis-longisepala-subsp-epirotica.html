Drymocallis longisepala subsp. epirotica

Drymocallis longisepala subsp.

epirotica, commonly known as the Epirotic goldenrod, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodland edges and meadows.

The plant features lanceolate, hairy leaves and produces yellow to orange composite flower heads in late summer. Its botanical characteristics include a robust root system and a tall, erect stem.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
