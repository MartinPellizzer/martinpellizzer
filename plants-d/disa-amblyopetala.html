Disa amblyopetala

Disa amblyopetala, commonly known as the pale-leaved disa, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. Its flowers are often pink or red, with a distinctive, spurred lip that aids in pollination.

Disa amblyopetala is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
