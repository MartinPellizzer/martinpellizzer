Deyeuxia microseta

Deyeuxia microseta, commonly known as the small-haired sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to various regions of Asia.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered with fine, short hairs, giving it its common name. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, spike-like flowers arranged in a cylindrical shape.

Deyeuxia microseta plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
