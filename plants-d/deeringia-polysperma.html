Deeringia polysperma

Deeringia polysperma, commonly known as the clustered sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and swampy areas, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils. Its slender, grass-like stems are usually clustered at the base, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early spring.

Deeringia polysperma is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
