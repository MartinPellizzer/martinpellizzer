Diogenesia octandra

Diogenesia octandra, commonly known as the eight-angled daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive composite flower heads. The plant features a short, upright stem with opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers consist of both ray and disc florets, typically displaying yellow to orange hues.

Diogenesia octandra is often found in grasslands and rocky habitats, thriving in well-drained soils.
