Drimia lanceifolia

Drimia lanceifolia, commonly known as the spear-leaved dragon's tongue, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open grasslands. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.

Drimia lanceifolia is also known for its ability to thrive in dry conditions, making it a resilient choice for xeriscaping.
