Dioscorea cissophylla

Dioscorea cissophylla, commonly known as the climbing yam, is a perennial vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by fleshy, tuberous roots that are often used as edible yams. Its botanical features include a robust root system and a rapid growth rate, making it adaptable to various soil types.

Dioscorea cissophylla is valued both for its nutritional content and its role in traditional medicine.
