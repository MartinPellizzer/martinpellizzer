Dahlia nana

Dahlia nana, commonly known as the dwarf dahlia, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to Mexico.

It features showy, brightly colored flowers that come in a wide range of hues, including red, yellow, orange, and pink. The plant typically grows to a height of 12 to 18 inches, making it ideal for borders or containers. Its foliage consists of heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are slightly hairy.

Dahlia nana thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, requiring regular watering and deadheading for continuous blooming.
