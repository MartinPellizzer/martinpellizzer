Diospyros subtruncata

Diospyros subtruncata, commonly known as the African persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other persimmon and ebony species. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptical in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically greenish-yellow, and it produces small, fleshy fruits that are edible.

The wood of Diospyros subtruncata is hard and dense, making it valuable for crafting and traditional uses.
