Dioscorea aspera

Dioscorea aspera, commonly known as rough yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its hairy, twining stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by tuberous roots that are often used in traditional medicine. Its tubers are edible when properly prepared and have been utilized for their nutritional and medicinal properties.

Dioscorea aspera is also valued for its role in local agriculture and herbal practices.
