Diasperus urinaria

Diasperus urinaria, commonly known as the bladder campion, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, pink or white flowers and distinctive bladder-like structures on its stems. The plant is native to various regions in Europe, Asia, and North Africa, often growing in rocky or sandy soils. It thrives in full sun and is relatively drought-tolerant, making it a hardy species.

The botanical features of Diasperus urinaria make it a notable plant in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
