Dichanthium sericeum subsp. sericeum

Dichanthium sericeum subsp.

sericeum, commonly known as silky grass or silky millet, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions. It is characterized by its fine, silky hairs covering the leaves and stems, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale to brownish flowers.

This subspecies is often used as a forage crop and has ecological importance in stabilizing soil in arid environments.
