Daucus parsae

Daucus parsae, commonly known as the Persian carrot, is a species of flowering plant in the Apiaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of Afghanistan and Pakistan. The plant features a taproot that is typically smaller and less commercially valuable than that of the common carrot. Its flowers are small and white, arranged in umbels, typical of the carrot family.

Daucus parsae is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its delicate appearance and adaptability to arid climates.
