Discocactus fariae-peresii

Discocactus fariae-peresii, commonly known as the Fariae-Peresii discocactus, is a rare and unique cactus species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems and clusters of small, fleshy, spiny leaves that help it conserve water in arid environments. The plant produces delicate, white or pale pink flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its otherwise muted appearance. It is well adapted to full sun and well-draining soil, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in controlled environments to ensure its survival.
