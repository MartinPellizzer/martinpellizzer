Dombeya valou

Dombeya valou, commonly known as the white dombeya, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that range from white to pale pink, which bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has smooth, gray-green leaves that are opposite and often have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Dombeya valou is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
