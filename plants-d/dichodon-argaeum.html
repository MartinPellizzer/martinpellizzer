Dichodon argaeum

Dichodon argaeum, commonly known as the desert rose, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and fleshy, lance-shaped leaves that are adapted to arid environments. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom in summer. Dichodon argaeum is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes in warm climates.
