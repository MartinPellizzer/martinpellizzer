Dendrobium fimbriatum var. oculatum

Dendrobium fimbriatum var. oculatum, commonly known as the "Eyelash Dendrobium," is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is an epiphytic plant, typically growing on trees or rocks in humid tropical environments. The plant features long, narrow leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers with distinctive, hairy sepals. Its varietal name, *oculatum*, refers to the prominent, eye-like markings on the petals.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its unique floral display.
