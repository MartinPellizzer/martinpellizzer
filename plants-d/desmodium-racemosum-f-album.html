Desmodium racemosum f. album

Desmodium racemosum f. album, commonly known as the white sicklepod, is a flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant produces elongated, spiny pods that contain several round, smooth seeds. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, it thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural areas.

Its botanical features make it both a weed and a potential forage plant in certain environments.
