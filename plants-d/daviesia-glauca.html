Daviesia glauca

Daviesia glauca, commonly known as the blue Daviesia, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive blue or purple pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in heathlands and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Daviesia glauca is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local wildlife.
