Daviesia incrassata subsp. reversifolia

Daviesia incrassata subsp.

reversifolia, commonly known as the Hairy Daviesia, is a shrub native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and yellowish to orange-red flowers that appear in late winter to early spring.

The plant has small, egg-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, stiff hairs. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

This subspecies is often found in heathlands and open woodlands, contributing to the biodiversity of its native habitat.
