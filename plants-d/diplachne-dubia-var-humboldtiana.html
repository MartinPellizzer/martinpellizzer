Diplachne dubia var. humboldtiana

Diplachne dubia var. humboldtiana, commonly known as the Humboldt grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves that are often hairy along the edges. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and riparian zones, and is adapted to seasonal flooding.

Its ecological role includes stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
