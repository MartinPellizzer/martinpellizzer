Dioscorea soror

Dioscorea soror, commonly known as the lesser yam, is a perennial vine native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and tuberous roots. The plant produces small, green flowers followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits that contain numerous seeds. Its tubers are used as a food source in some regions, though they contain toxic compounds that require careful preparation.

The botanical features of Dioscorea soror make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
