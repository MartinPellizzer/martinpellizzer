Diplolophium africanum f. kankanensis

Diplolophium africanum f. kankanensis, commonly known as the African bitter melon, is a variant of the Diplolophium africanum species native to tropical Africa.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its dark green, lobed leaves and yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces elongated, ridged fruits that are typically harvested when mature for their edible, slightly bitter flesh. Botanically, it belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, sharing traits with other members like cucumbers and gourds.

Its growth habit and morphological features make it distinct within its genus.
