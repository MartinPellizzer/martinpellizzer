Diospyros acunae

Diospyros acunae, commonly known as the Acuna persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other persimmon species and ebony trees. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by fruit resembling a small, round persimmon.

The fruit is edible when fully ripe and has a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor.
