Dupatya ciliata

Dupatya ciliata, commonly known as the hairy leaf vine, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, slender stems and heart-shaped leaves with hairy margins. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a popular ornamental plant in tropical gardens.
