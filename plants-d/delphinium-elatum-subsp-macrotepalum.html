Delphinium elatum subsp. macrotepalum

Delphinium elatum subsp.

macrotepalum, commonly known as the giant larkspur, is a striking member of the Ranunculaceae family. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters.

The plant produces large, showy blue to purple flowers in racemes, with petals that are elongated and spurred. Its leaves are deeply lobed and arranged alternately along the stem.

This subspecies is native to the mountainous regions of western North America and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
