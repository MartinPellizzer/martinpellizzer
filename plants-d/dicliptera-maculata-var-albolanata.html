Dicliptera maculata var. albolanata

Dicliptera maculata var. albolanata, commonly known as the white-stemmed dicliptera, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, pale green stems with a distinct white or pale stripe running along the central axis. The leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a bluish-green hue. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in loose panicles, typical of the grass family.

This plant is often found in moist, disturbed soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
