Dombeya megaphylla var. typica

Dombeya megaphylla var. typica, commonly known as the giant dombeya, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, which bloom in late summer. The plant has smooth, often grayish bark and elliptical, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy surface. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
