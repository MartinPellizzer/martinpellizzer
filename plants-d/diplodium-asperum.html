Diplodium asperum

Diplodium asperum, commonly known as the rough diplodium, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in composite flower heads, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Diplodium asperum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature.
