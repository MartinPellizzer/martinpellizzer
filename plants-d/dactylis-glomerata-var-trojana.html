Dactylis glomerata var. trojana

Dactylis glomerata var. trojana, commonly known as the Trojan cocksfoot, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, blue-green leaves. The plant produces loose, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, pale green to brown flowers. It is well adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in meadows, pastures, and disturbed areas.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and is used in both ornamental and agricultural settings.
