Dacrycarpus imbricatus var. robustus

Dacrycarpus imbricatus var. robustus, commonly known as the robust pine, is a species of conifer native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and straight, cylindrical trunk. The leaves are typically short, scale-like, and arranged in a compact, imbricate pattern. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in coastal and lowland forest areas.

This variety is notable for its resilience and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
