Dampiera sylvestris

Dampiera sylvestris, commonly known as the forest dampiera, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southeastern Australia.

It features small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in spring. The plant has hairy, linear leaves that are often arranged in a basal rosette, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments. It is often found in woodland and heathland habitats, where it contributes to the local biodiversity.

Dampiera sylvestris is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
