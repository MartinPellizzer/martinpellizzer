Diospyros kabuyeana

Diospyros kabuyeana, commonly known as the Kabuyean persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves that are dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by orange to red berries that are edible.

Its bark is thick and fissured, often exuding a sticky resin, and it is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
