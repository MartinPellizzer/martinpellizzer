Danthonia jacquemontii var. minor

Danthonia jacquemontii var. minor, commonly known as the narrowleaf danthonia, is a perennial grass species native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is characterized by its fine, delicate appearance and is often found in association with other native grasses and sedges.

Botanically, it is noted for its rhizomatous growth habit and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
