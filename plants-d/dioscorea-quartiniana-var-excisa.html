Dioscorea quartiniana var. excisa

Dioscorea quartiniana var. excisa, commonly known as the African yambean, is a tuberous perennial plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces edible tubers that are rich in starch and have been used as a food source in various regions. Its botanical structure includes a robust root system and aerial stems that bear small, greenish-white flowers.

The tubers are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
