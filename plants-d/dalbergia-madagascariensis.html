Dalbergia madagascariensis

Dalbergia madagascariensis, commonly known as rosewood, is a tropical tree native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its dark, dense wood that is highly valued for its beauty and durability. The plant has bipinnate leaves composed of small, oval-shaped leaflets. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by pod-like fruits containing several seeds.

This species is also notable for its role in traditional medicine and its ecological importance in its native habitat.
