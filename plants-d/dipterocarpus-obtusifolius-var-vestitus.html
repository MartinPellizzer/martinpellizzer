Dipterocarpus obtusifolius var. vestitus

Dipterocarpus obtusifolius var. vestitus, commonly known as the white-flowered dipterocarp, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and large, elliptical leaves that are glossy on the upper surface. The tree produces pendulous inflorescences with white or pale yellow flowers, which attract various pollinators. Its wood is valuable for construction and furniture due to its durability and strength.

This species plays an important ecological role in rainforest ecosystems, contributing to biodiversity and carbon sequestration.
