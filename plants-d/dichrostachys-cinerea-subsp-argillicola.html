Dichrostachys cinerea subsp. argillicola

Dichrostachys cinerea subsp.

argillicola, commonly known as red sandalwood, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa. It is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and small, opposite, ovate leaves with a velvety texture.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters, followed by woody pods containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, hot climates.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine, it is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic and practical properties.
