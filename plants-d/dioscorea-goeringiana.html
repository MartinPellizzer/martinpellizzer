Dioscorea goeringiana

Dioscorea goeringiana, commonly known as the African yambean, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by tuberous roots that are its primary edible part. These tubers are rich in starch and have been used as a food source by various indigenous communities.

Additionally, the plant has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
