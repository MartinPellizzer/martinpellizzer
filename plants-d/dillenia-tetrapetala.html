Dillenia tetrapetala

Dillenia tetrapetala, commonly known as the four-petaled dillenia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family, which includes roses and apples, and is characterized by its showy, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces large, round, edible fruits that are often used in local cuisines. Its leaves are palmately lobed and have a distinctive appearance, while its stems are typically hairy and woody.

Dillenia tetrapetala is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
