Dioscorea deamii

Dioscorea deamii, commonly known as the Deam's yam, is a species of tuberous vine native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers followed by cylindrical, spiny fruits containing numerous seeds. Its tuberous roots are the primary part used, though the plant is not widely cultivated for commercial purposes.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and potential medicinal properties.
