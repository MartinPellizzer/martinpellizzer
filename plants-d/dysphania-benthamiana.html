Dysphania benthamiana

Dysphania benthamiana, commonly known as Chenopodium benthamianum, is a flowering plant in the Amaranthaceae family.

It is native to Australia and is characterized by its upright, branching stems and alternate, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along watercourses.

Dysphania benthamiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its foliage and seasonal blooms.
