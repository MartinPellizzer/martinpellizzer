Diolena spicata

Diolena spicata, commonly known as the spiked diolena, is a flowering plant species belonging to the family Amaranthaceae.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or red. The plant produces small, dry fruits called capsules, which contain numerous tiny seeds. Diolena spicata is native to regions of southern Africa and is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
