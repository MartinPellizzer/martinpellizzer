Danthonia virescens

Danthonia virescens, commonly known as green danthonia, is a perennial grass native to Australia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, leafy blades that are typically a vibrant green. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets, which are often wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and heathlands.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil.
