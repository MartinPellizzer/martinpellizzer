Dinochloa glabrescens

Dinochloa glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth leaved dinochloa, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit. The plant typically features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are smooth and glabrous, giving it its common name. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Dinochloa glabrescens is often found in moist, open habitats such as grasslands and wet meadows.
