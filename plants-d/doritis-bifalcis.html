Doritis bifalcis

Doritis bifalcis, commonly known as the two-headed orchid, is a unique flowering plant native to the coastal regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive appearance, featuring two prominent flower spikes that emerge from a single stem. The plant belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its striking, pale blue to purple flowers. Doritis bifalcis thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal climates with high salinity.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural collections and botanical gardens.
