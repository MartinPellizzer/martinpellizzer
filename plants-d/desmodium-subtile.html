Desmodium subtile

Desmodium subtile, commonly known as the slender desmodium, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is often found in open woodlands and disturbed soils, where it thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its small, pod-like fruits contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

Desmodium subtile is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in agricultural settings.
