Drimia undata

Drimia undata, commonly known as the mountain garlic, is a bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It features strap-like, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers. The plant is characterized by its underground bulbs, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous habitats.
