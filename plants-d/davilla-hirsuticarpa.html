Davilla hirsuticarpa

Davilla hirsuticarpa, commonly known as the hairy persimmon, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, reddish stems and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that give way to orange to red, fleshy fruits resembling persimmons. The fruits are edible, though some varieties may have a bitter taste when unripe.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit in tropical and subtropical climates.
