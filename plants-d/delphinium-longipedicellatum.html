Delphinium longipedicellatum

Delphinium longipedicellatum, commonly known as the long-stemmed delphinium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers in raceme-like clusters, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are compound, with lance-shaped leaflets that are serrated along the edges.

Delphinium longipedicellatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
