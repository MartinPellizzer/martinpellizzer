Diamonon coriaceum

Diamonon coriaceum, commonly known as the diamondleaf plant, is a unique species characterized by its waxy, scale-like leaves that give it a distinctive appearance.

Native to the arid regions of southern Africa, it has adapted to thrive in dry, sandy soils with minimal water availability. The plant's leaves are covered in a thick cuticle, which helps reduce water loss and protect against intense sunlight. It produces small, white or pale green flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a subtle ornamental value.

Due to its hardiness and low maintenance requirements, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in xeriscaped gardens.
