Dendrobium velutinelabrum

Dendrobium velutinelabrum, commonly known as the velvet-labrum orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with elongated, cane-like stems that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, often clustered in racemes. Its leaves are broad, fleshy, and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems.

Dendrobium velutinelabrum thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light and requires well-draining potting media.
