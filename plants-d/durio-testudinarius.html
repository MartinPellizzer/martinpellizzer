Durio testudinarius

Durio testudinarius, commonly known as the African durian, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves and fragrant flowers. The tree produces edible fruits that are similar in appearance to the common durian but have a distinct flavor. Its fruit is typically harvested when it reaches maturity, often indicated by a change in color and a soft texture.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and its fruit, which is consumed both locally and in international markets.
