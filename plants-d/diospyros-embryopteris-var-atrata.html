Diospyros embryopteris var. atrata

Diospyros embryopteris var. atrata, commonly known as the black persimmon, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree with simple, alternate leaves that are elliptic to ovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by fleshy, orange to reddish fruits. Its leaves are often covered in a fine, whitish pubescence, and the bark is typically smooth with a reddish or brownish hue.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine.
