Dimacria elegans

Dimacria elegans, commonly known as the slender pincushion cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Cactaceae and is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems covered in spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are clustered at the ends of the stems. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, preferring well-drained sandy soils.

Dimacria elegans is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its distinctive appearance and drought tolerance.
