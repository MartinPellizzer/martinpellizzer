Didymopanax acuminatus

Didymopanax acuminatus, commonly known as the Brazilian rosewood or *Pau-rosa*, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It belongs to the family Moraceae and is characterized by its large, palm-like leaves and smooth, dark bark. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by round, fleshy fruits containing seeds. Due to its ornamental value and historical use in furniture-making, it is also of ecological and economic significance.
