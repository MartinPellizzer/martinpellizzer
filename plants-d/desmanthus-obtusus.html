Desmanthus obtusus

Desmanthus obtusus, commonly known as the bluntleaf burrbean, is a perennial legume native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically heart-shaped and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by elongated, spiny pods containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Desmanthus obtusus is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it a useful plant in ecological restoration and forage production.
