Dianthus volgicus

Dianthus volgicus, commonly known as the Siberian pink, is a flowering plant native to the cold climates of Russia and Siberia.

It belongs to the carnation family, Caryophyllaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, pink or white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun, making it a hardy species adapted to harsh environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces small, clustered flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Dianthus volgicus is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to withstand cold temperatures.
