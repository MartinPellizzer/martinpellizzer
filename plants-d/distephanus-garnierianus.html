Distephanus garnierianus

Distephanus garnierianus, commonly known as the golden star cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are covered with dense, woolly hair, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking visual contrast to its green foliage. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
