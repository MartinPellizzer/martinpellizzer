Dendrobium prenticei

Dendrobium prenticei, commonly known as the Prentice's orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, cane-like pseudobulbs and clusters of fragrant, showy flowers. The plant typically blooms in summer, producing white or pale yellow flowers with a distinct fragrance. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and requires well-draining soil and bright, indirect light.

Dendrobium prenticei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
