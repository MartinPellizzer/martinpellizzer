Dillenia fagifolia

Dillenia fagifolia, commonly known as the "cork tree" or "false mahogany," is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and large, palmately lobed leaves. The plant produces showy, yellowish-white flowers with five petals, followed by large, round, woody fruits that split open to release seeds.

Its leaves and fruits are used in traditional medicine, and the tree is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance in its native habitat.
