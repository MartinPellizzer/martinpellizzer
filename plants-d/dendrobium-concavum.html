Dendrobium concavum

Dendrobium concavum, commonly known as the concave dendrobium, is an epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit, with cane-like stems that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and pink, often in clusters along the stems. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged in a spiral pattern around the stems.

Dendrobium concavum thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.
