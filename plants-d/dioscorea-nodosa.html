Dioscorea nodosa

Dioscorea nodosa, commonly known as the climbing yam, is a perennial vine native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and tuberous roots. The plant produces small, green, heart-shaped leaves and yellowish-green flowers that are typically unisexual. Its tubers, which are the primary edible part, are rich in starch and have a slightly sweet flavor.

Dioscorea nodosa is widely cultivated for its nutritional value and is an important crop in many parts of Africa and Asia.
