Dendrobium salomonense

Dendrobium salomonense, commonly known as the Solomon Islands orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of the South Pacific.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and clusters of fragrant, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and yellow. The plant typically blooms during the wet season, producing long, slender petals and a distinctive lip that aids in pollination. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Dendrobium salomonense is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its cultural significance in the regions where it is found.
