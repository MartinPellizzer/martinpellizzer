Diplotaxis lagascana var. genuina

Diplotaxis lagascana var. genuina, commonly known as the Cape daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that grows up to 60 cm tall, featuring a taproot and upright stems. The plant produces yellow flowers with four petals, arranged in a dense inflorescence at the top of the stem. Its leaves are alternate, linear, and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly rough texture.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
