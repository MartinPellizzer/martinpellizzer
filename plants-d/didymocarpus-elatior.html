Didymocarpus elatior

Didymocarpus elatior, commonly known as the greater flame tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy, orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has pinnate leaves with numerous small leaflets and produces woody pods that contain flat, winged seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its ability to grow in a variety of soil types and its relatively fast growth rate.
