Disperis anomala

Disperis anomala, commonly known as the mountain orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in forest understories. Its root system is fibrous, and it produces small, greenish-yellow flowers with intricate petal structures.

Disperis anomala is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
