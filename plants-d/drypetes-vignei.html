Drypetes vignei

Drypetes vignei, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its striking orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has compound leaves with lance-shaped leaflets and produces small, three-lobed fruits. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for ornamental planting in warm climates.

The tree is also valued for its rapid growth and ability to provide shade in urban landscapes.
