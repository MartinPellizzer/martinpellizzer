Disa tanganyikensis

Disa tanganyikensis, commonly known as the Lake Tanganyika water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to the freshwater lakes of East Africa.

It is characterized by its large, white, waxy flowers that bloom in the early morning and close by midday. The plant has broad, floating leaves that provide habitat for various aquatic organisms. Its roots are anchored in the muddy substrate at the lake bottom.

Disa tanganyikensis plays an important ecological role in maintaining the balance of its aquatic ecosystem.
