Derris tonkinensis

Derris tonkinensis, commonly known as the Vietnamese snakeroot, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Leguminosae (Fabaceae) and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is notable for containing rotenone, a potent natural insecticide and piscicide. Its roots are often harvested for traditional medicinal uses and agricultural purposes.

Derris tonkinensis thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.
