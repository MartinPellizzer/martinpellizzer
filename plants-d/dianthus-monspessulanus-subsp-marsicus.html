Dianthus monspessulanus subsp. marsicus

Dianthus monspessulanus subsp.

marsicus, commonly known as the Corsican carnation, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and produces showy, bilaterally symmetrical flowers in shades of pink, red, and white.

The plant features upright, branching stems covered in fine, hairy foliage. Its flowers typically bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is well-suited for rock gardens and borders due to its hardiness and ornamental value.
