Draba clypeata

Draba clypeata, commonly known as the flatleaf whitlow grass, is a small, flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in alpine and rocky habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, blooming in early spring. Its leaves are basal, rosette-like, and have a distinctive flat or slightly curled shape.
