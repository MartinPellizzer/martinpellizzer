Drypetes integrifolia

Drypetes integrifolia, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Africa and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood, which is highly valued for its durability and strength. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical or ovate in shape. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by small, round fruits containing a single seed.

This species is often found in lowland rainforests and is noted for its ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
