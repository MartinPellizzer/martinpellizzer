Deroemera pentadactyla

Deroemera pentadactyla, commonly known as the Cape honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a rough texture.

Deroemera pentadactyla thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal and sandy environments.
