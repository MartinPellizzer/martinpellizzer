Dioclea glabra

Dioclea glabra, commonly known as the smooth dioclea, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, spurred flowers. The plant produces small, edible legumes that are often used in traditional medicine. Its growth habit involves twining stems that climb on other vegetation, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

The botanical features of Dioclea glabra make it an important species in ecological and agricultural contexts.
