Dalbergia ovata var. obtusifolia

Dalbergia ovata var. obtusifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved rosewood, is a species of legume native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, dense wood that is highly valued for its durability and rich color. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree with compound leaves and produces small, pea-like flowers. Its fruits are pod-like and contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The species is also noted for its aromatic properties and is used in traditional medicine and craftsmanship.
