Dendrobium opacifolium

Dendrobium opacifolium, commonly known as the opacifoliate orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and clusters of fragrant, pale yellow to greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, utilizing its aerial roots to absorb moisture and nutrients from the environment. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.

Dendrobium opacifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its unique floral display.
