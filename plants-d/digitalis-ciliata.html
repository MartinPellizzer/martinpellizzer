Digitalis ciliata

Digitalis ciliata, commonly known as the downy foxglove, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from purple to pink. The leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and covered in fine, downy hairs, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This plant is notable for its ornamental value and its use in traditional medicine.
