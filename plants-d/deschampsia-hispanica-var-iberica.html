Deschampsia hispanica var. iberica

Deschampsia hispanica var. iberica, commonly known as Spanish feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Europe.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands, rocky slopes, and coastal areas. The plant features slender, tufted culms with fine, silver-gray blades that are often wind-dispersed. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of spikelets, which are covered in silky hairs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in disturbed areas.
