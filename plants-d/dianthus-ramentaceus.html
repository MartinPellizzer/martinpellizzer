Dianthus ramentaceus

Dianthus ramentaceus, commonly known as the woolly pink, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and clusters of pink or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, which includes other flowering plants such as carnations and pinks.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
