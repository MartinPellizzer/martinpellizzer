Dendrobium striatiflorum

Dendrobium striatiflorum, commonly known as the streaked-flowered orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is an epiphytic orchid that grows on trees and rocks, relying on atmospheric moisture for nourishment. The plant features long, cane-like stems with clustered, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and yellow. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, adapting to its aerial habitat.

Dendrobium striatiflorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for horticultural purposes.
