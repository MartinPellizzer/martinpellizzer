Diplachne nigrescens

Diplachne nigrescens, commonly known as blackgrass or black panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is characterized by its erect, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green or purplish in color, and it flowers throughout the growing season. Diplachne nigrescens is often found in disturbed soils, along roadsides, and in agricultural fields, where it can become a weed.

Its rapid growth and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions make it both ecologically significant and a challenge for land management.
