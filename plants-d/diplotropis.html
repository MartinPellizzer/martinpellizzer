Diplotropis

Diplotropis, commonly known as the yellow wild indigo, is a genus of flowering plants in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the southeastern United States, where it thrives in moist, woodland habitats. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces showy, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in spring. Diplotropis species are characterized by their ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.

Its botanical features make it an important plant for ecological restoration and native landscaping.
