Dolichos pseudocajanus var. paniculatus

Dolichos pseudocajanus var. paniculatus, commonly known as the hairy cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, hairy pods that contain edible seeds. The plant thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its nutritional value. It has a deep root system that helps in soil stabilization and nitrogen fixation.

The flowers are typically yellow or white, and the plant is well-suited for both agricultural and ornamental purposes.
