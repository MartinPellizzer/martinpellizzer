Dactylorhiza aristata f. rosea

Dactylorhiza aristata f. rosea, commonly known as the rose-flowered marsh orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, pink or rose-colored flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in wet, shaded habitats such as bogs and marshes. It has linear, grass-like leaves and produces a single spike of flowers.

This orchid is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
