Delpechia floribunda

Delpechia floribunda, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features a compact, bushy growth habit with numerous small, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of blue to violet. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pollinated by insects. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in opposite pairs along the stems.

Delpechia floribunda is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
