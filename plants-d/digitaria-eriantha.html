Digitaria eriantha

Digitaria eriantha, commonly known as hairy fingergrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are often clustered at the base of the plant. It thrives in well-drained soils and is commonly found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Digitaria eriantha is known for its adaptability to various environmental conditions, making it a resilient species in its native range.
