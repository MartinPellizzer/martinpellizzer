Dipteracanthus geminiflorus var. erectus

Dipteracanthus geminiflorus var. erectus, commonly known as the erect twin-flower, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces clusters of small, fragrant white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest undergrowth. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a velvety texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
