Dianthus caryophyllus subsp. sylvestris

Dianthus caryophyllus subsp.

sylvestris, commonly known as the wild carnation, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features upright, branching stems that bear clusters of showy, pink or red flowers with frilled petals.

The leaves are linear and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and borders.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
