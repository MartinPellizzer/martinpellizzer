Diplazium wilsonii var. brunneoviride

Diplazium wilsonii var. brunneoviride, commonly known as the brown-green sword fern, is a variant of the sword fern genus Diplazium.

It is characterized by its long, slender fronds that are typically divided into pinnae with a serrated edge. The leaves are usually a dark green on top and a lighter, sometimes brownish-green underneath, giving it its common name. This fern thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture for its graceful appearance.
