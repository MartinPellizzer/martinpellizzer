Disandra africana

Disandra africana, commonly known as African starfruit, is a tropical shrub native to Central and Southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Cucurbitaceae, which includes cucumbers and melons, and is characterized by its trailing growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by heart-shaped, fleshy fruits with a sweet, tangy flavor. Its leaves are palmately lobed and often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The fruit is also valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary uses in local cuisines.
