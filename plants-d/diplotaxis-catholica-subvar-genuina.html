Diplotaxis catholica subvar. genuina

Diplotaxis catholica subvar. genuina, commonly known as the African ironweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, yellow flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky or sandy soils.

The species is noted for its hardy nature and ability to withstand drought conditions.
