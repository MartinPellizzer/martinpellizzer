Dasypogon obliquifolius

Dasypogon obliquifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leafed bottlebrush, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive red or pink bottlebrush-like flowers that appear in clusters. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal areas.

Dasypogon obliquifolius is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting native wildlife.
