Dactylopetalum ugandense

Dactylopetalum ugandense, commonly known as the Uganda hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to East Africa.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has simple, ovate leaves with a hairy underside and produces small, capsule-like fruits containing seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and potential ecological importance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
