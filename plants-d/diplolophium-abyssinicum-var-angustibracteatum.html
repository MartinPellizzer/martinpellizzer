Diplolophium abyssinicum var. angustibracteatum

Diplolophium abyssinicum var. angustibracteatum, commonly known as the Abyssinian double-lipped orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to Ethiopia and is characterized by its unique, showy flowers with two distinct lip lobes. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous regions, thriving in high-altitude environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its stems are often woody and branched.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
