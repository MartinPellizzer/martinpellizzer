Dendrobium clavatum var. aurantiacum

Dendrobium clavatum var. aurantiacum, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features elongated, cane-like pseudobulbs covered in a dense layer of yellowish-brown hair. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, yellow-orange flowers with a velvety texture. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor cultivation.
