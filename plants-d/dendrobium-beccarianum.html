Dendrobium beccarianum

Dendrobium beccarianum, commonly known as the Beccarian orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, cane-like pseudobulbs and clusters of fragrant, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, relying on its roots for moisture and nutrients. Its flowering period occurs during the dry season, and it is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Dendrobium beccarianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated for horticultural purposes.
