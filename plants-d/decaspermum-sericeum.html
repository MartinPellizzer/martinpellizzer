Decaspermum sericeum

Decaspermum sericeum, commonly known as the silky decaspermum, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant features slender, erect stems covered in soft, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its small, white or pale blue flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom during the warmer months.

Decaspermum sericeum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate foliage and flowering habit.
