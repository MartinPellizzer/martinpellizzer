Dictamnus dasycarpus var. velutinus

Dictamnus dasycarpus var. velutinus, commonly known as the velvet dictamnus, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves that emit a strong, spicy scent when crushed. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its stems are hairy and often reddish in color, while the leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique fragrance and foliage.
