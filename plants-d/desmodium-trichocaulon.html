Desmodium trichocaulon

Desmodium trichocaulon, commonly known as slender desmodium, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is often found in open woodlands and grasslands, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its growth habit is herbaceous, with stems that are typically erect or prostrate.

Desmodium trichocaulon is also noted for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
