Daphne grayana

Daphne grayana, commonly known as the silver daphne, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features dense, upright growth with ovate to elliptical leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers in early spring, which are followed by red berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and showy blooms.
