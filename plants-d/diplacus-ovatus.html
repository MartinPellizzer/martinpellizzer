Diplacus ovatus

Diplacus ovatus, commonly known as the hummingbird flower, is a species of flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that attract pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. The plant typically grows as an annual or biennial herb, often reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are usually pink or red, with five petals that curve inward.

Diplacus ovatus thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for gardens and natural landscapes.
