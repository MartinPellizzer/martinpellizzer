Dianthus orientalis subsp. gilanicus

Dianthus orientalis subsp.

gilanicus, commonly known as the Gilanian campion, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region. It is characterized by its showy, pink to red petals that resemble those of other dianthus species.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are often clustered in racemes, and the leaves are narrow and grass-like.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
