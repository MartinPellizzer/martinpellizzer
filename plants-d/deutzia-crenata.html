Deutzia crenata

Deutzia crenata, commonly known as the crested snowbell, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features upright, branching stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, white to pale pink flowers in early summer. The leaves are ovate to elliptic, with serrated edges, and they turn yellow in the fall. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
