Dendrobium auriferum

Dendrobium auriferum, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its epiphytic growth habit, often found on trees or rocks. The plant produces striking yellow to orange flowers with a distinctive golden hue, which gives it its common name. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.

Dendrobium auriferum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated for horticultural purposes.
