Delphinium andersonii var. scaposum

Delphinium andersonii var. scaposum, commonly known as the scapose delphinium, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soil and full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem, while its flowers are spurred at the base, a distinctive feature of the delphinium genus.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
