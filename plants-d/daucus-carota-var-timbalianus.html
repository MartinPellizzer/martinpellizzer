Daucus carota var. timbalianus

Daucus carota var. timbalianus, commonly known as the Timbalian carrot, is a wild variety of the carrot plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, usually white or yellowish roots and finely divided, feathery leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along riverbanks. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family.

While not widely cultivated, it is valued for its potential medicinal properties and as a source of edible roots in local cuisines.
