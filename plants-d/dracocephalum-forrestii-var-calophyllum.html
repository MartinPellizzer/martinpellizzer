Dracocephalum forrestii var. calophyllum

Dracocephalum forrestii var. calophyllum, commonly known as the Tibetan blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of Tibet.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, blue-lilac flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a silvery underside, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
