Dicerandra frutescens subsp. savannarum

Dicerandra frutescens subsp.

savannarum, commonly known as the Florida dicerandra, is a herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States. It typically grows in sandy soils within scrub and pine flatwood habitats.

The plant features slender, branching stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are tubular and often have a distinctive yellowish or greenish hue.

It is part of the Acanthaceae family and is known for its ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
