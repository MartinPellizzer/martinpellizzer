Desmodium coloniense

Desmodium coloniense, commonly known as the hairy beggar's tick, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a legume, belonging to the Fabaceae family, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds, which are often dispersed by wind.

Desmodium coloniense is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its tolerance to drought conditions.
