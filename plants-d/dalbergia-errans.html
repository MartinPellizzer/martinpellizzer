Dalbergia errans

Dalbergia errans, commonly known as the Indian rosewood, is a tropical leguminous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, dense wood with a distinctive reddish-brown color and a rich, aromatic scent. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family and features trifoliate leaves with small, stipulate stipules. Its flowers are small and typically yellow or orange, arranged in racemose inflorescences.

The tree is valued for its high-quality timber, which is used in furniture and musical instruments.
