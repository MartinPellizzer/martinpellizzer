Dioscorea sylvatica

Dioscorea sylvatica, commonly known as the wild yam, is a perennial vine native to the tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by cylindrical, yellowish fruits containing numerous seeds. Its tuberous roots are the primary botanical feature, often used in traditional medicine and as a source of compounds for pharmaceutical applications.

The plant's morphology and chemical composition make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
