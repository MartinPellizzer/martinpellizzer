Distephanus eriophyllus

Distephanus eriophyllus, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray, hairy foliage and small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has a shrub-like growth habit and can reach heights of up to 5 meters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

The silverleaf tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
