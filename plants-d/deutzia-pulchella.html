Deutzia pulchella

Deutzia pulchella, commonly known as the showy deutzia, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features clusters of fragrant, white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from April to May. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are dark green and glossy on top, with a lighter underside. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Deutzia pulchella is known for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
