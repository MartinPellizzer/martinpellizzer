Daphniphyllum reticulatum

Daphniphyllum reticulatum, commonly known as the reticulated daphniphyllum, is a species of evergreen shrub native to China and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae and is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage with a distinctive reticulated pattern on the underside of the leaves. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by elliptical, persistent berries. Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections for its unique appearance and ecological significance.
