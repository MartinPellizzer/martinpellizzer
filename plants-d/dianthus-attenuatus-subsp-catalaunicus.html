Dianthus attenuatus subsp. catalaunicus

Dianthus attenuatus subsp.

catalaunicus, commonly known as the Catalan pink, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant produces small, ovate leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or limestone-rich habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, delicate blooms.
