Dysoxylum maingayi

Dysoxylum maingayi, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a tropical tree native to regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, simple leaves that are typically alternate and ovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody, elongated fruits containing several seeds. Dysoxylum maingayi is valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in furniture and construction.

Its growth habit and ecological role make it an important species in its native habitats.
