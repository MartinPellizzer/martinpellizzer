Dombeya metameropsis var. belambanensis

Dombeya metameropsis var. belambanensis, commonly known as the Belambanensis Dombeya, is a shrub native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, attracting pollinators like bees and birds. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are often hairy, providing a textured appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in warm, arid climates.

Its unique floral structure and ornamental value make it a desirable plant for botanical gardens and specialized collections.
