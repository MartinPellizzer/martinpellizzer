Dedea cinerea

Dedea cinerea, commonly known as the grey mullet, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded forest understories. Its leaves are hairy and have a distinctive scent when crushed.

Dedea cinerea plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insect species.
