Dodonaea cuneata var. rigida

Dodonaea cuneata var. rigida, commonly known as rigid screwtree, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of yellowish-green flowers followed by small, woody fruits containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
