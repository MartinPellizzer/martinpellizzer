Dendroseris micrantha

Dendroseris micrantha, commonly known as the small-flowered rock cress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features a compact rosette of basal leaves and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in the summer. Its flowers are characterized by four petals and are arranged in a raceme, typical of the Brassicaceae family.

Dendroseris micrantha is often found in high-altitude meadows and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil in rocky terrains.
