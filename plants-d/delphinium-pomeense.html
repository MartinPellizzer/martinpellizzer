Delphinium pomeense

Delphinium pomeense, commonly known as the Pomegranate larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its flowers are clustered in racemes.

Delphinium pomeense is a member of the Ranunculaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
