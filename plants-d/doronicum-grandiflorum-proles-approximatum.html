Doronicum grandiflorum proles approximatum

Doronicum grandiflorum proles approximatum, commonly known as the large-flowered leopard's bane, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It features a basal rosette of dark green, hairy leaves that are lanceolate in shape. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers with red markings, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, moist soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
