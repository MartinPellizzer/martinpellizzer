Dianthera flava

Dianthera flava, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems covered in soft, hairy foliage. The plant produces striking yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

Dianthera flava thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.
