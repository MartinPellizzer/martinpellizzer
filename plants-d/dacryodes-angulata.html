Dacryodes angulata

Dacryodes angulata, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a tropical tree native to regions of Africa and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate or elliptic in shape. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow in color.

The fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds, contributing to its ecological and economic importance in its native habitats.
