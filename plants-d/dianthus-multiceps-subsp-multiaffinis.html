Dianthus multiceps subsp. multiaffinis

Dianthus multiceps subsp.

multiaffinis, commonly known as the Alpine campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, preferring well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, with a slightly hairy texture, and the stems are often reddish in color.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and vibrant blooms.
