Dryopteris dilatata subsp. alexeenkoana

Dryopteris dilatata subsp.

alexeenkoana, commonly known as the Alexeenko's wood fern, is a fern species native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia. It is characterized by its fronds that are typically lanceolate and have a dark green, leathery texture.

The plant produces sporangia on the underside of its fronds, which are clustered in strobila. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
