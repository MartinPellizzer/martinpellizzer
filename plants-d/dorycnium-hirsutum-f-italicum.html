Dorycnium hirsutum f. italicum

Dorycnium hirsutum f. italicum, commonly known as the Italian hirsute sainfoin, is a perennial legume native to southern Europe.

It features finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and is often found in dry, calcareous soils. It is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in agricultural settings.

Its botanical structure supports its role as a forage plant and ecological stabilizer.
