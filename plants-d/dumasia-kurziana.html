Dumasia kurziana

Dumasia kurziana, commonly known as the mountain bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are slender and erect.

Dumasia kurziana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique floral display.
