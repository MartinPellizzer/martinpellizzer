Davilla wormiifolia

Davilla wormiifolia, commonly known as the red-leaf davilla, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange leaves, which change color seasonally, and its clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically red when mature. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The leaves are palmately lobed, and the plant has a woody stem with a rough, fissured bark.
