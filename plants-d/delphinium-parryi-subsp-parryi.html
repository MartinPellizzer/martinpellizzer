Delphinium parryi subsp. parryi

Delphinium parryi subsp.

parryi, commonly known as Parry's larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height.

The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers in racemes, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a rough texture and a distinctive blue-green color.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine and rocky habitats.
