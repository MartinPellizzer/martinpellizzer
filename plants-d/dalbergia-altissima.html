Dalbergia altissima

Dalbergia altissima, commonly known as the African rosewood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its dense, dark-colored wood that is highly valued for its aesthetic and structural properties. The tree has compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers followed by legume-like pods containing flat, elliptical seeds.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savannas and forest edges, where it plays a significant role in local ecosystems.
