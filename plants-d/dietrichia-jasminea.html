Dietrichia jasminea

Dietrichia jasminea, commonly known as the sweet-scented orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, showy flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are broad and glossy, and it produces small, white or pale yellow flowers with a strong, pleasant aroma.

Dietrichia jasminea is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its use in traditional medicine.
