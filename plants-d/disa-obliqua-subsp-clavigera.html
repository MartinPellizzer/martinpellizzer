Disa obliqua subsp. clavigera

Disa obliqua subsp.

clavigera, commonly known as the zigzag orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its unique, zigzag-shaped inflorescence and vibrant, often purple or pink, flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, thriving in well-drained environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, delicate orchid-like blooms.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
