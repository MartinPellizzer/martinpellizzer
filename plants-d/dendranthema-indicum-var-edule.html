Dendranthema indicum var. edule

Dendranthema indicum var. edule, commonly known as edible chrysanthemum, is a variant of the chrysanthemum plant native to Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, yellow to orange flowers. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value, but it is also used in traditional medicine and cuisine. Its leaves are edible and can be used in salads or as a seasoning.

The plant thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil and full sunlight.
