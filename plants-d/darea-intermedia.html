Darea intermedia

Darea intermedia, commonly known as the common rockcress, is a flowering plant native to various regions across Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many species with small, four-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine or coastal environments. Its leaves are usually basal, arranged in a rosette, and its flowers are bright yellow or white.

Darea intermedia is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in harsh, nutrient-poor conditions.
