Draba loayzana

Draba loayzana, commonly known as the Loayzana whitlow grass, is a small annual herbaceous plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many flowering plants with characteristic cruciform flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to harsh, dry environments. Its small, clustered flowers are usually white or pale yellow, and it produces small, rounded seed pods.

Draba loayzana is often found in high-altitude areas and is known for its resilience in extreme climates.
