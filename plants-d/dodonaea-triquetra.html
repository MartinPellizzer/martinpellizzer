Dodonaea triquetra

Dodonaea triquetra, commonly known as the triquetra honeysuckle, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, dark green, oval-shaped leaves that are often arranged in whorls along the stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in spikes, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. Its fruit is a small, three-lobed capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Dodonaea triquetra is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
