Desmodium axillare var. angustatum

Desmodium axillare var. angustatum, commonly known as narrow-leaf desmodium, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in disturbed soils or open habitats.

It has a sprawling growth habit and produces pods that contain several seeds, contributing to its role in ecological succession and soil stabilization.
