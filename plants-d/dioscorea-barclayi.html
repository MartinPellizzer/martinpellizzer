Dioscorea barclayi

Dioscorea barclayi, commonly known as Barclay's yam, is a species of flowering plant in the family Dioscoreaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, particularly Nigeria and Cameroon. The plant is a climbing vine characterized by its tuberous roots, which are used as a food source in some local communities. Its leaves are heart-shaped and its flowers are typically yellow with a distinctive purple or red corona.

The plant thrives in humid, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
