Dodonaea attenuata

Dodonaea attenuata, commonly known as the coastal honeysuckle, is a shrub native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often pendulous branches and small, fragrant, yellowish-green flowers. The leaves are simple, alternate, and typically have a velvety texture. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to coastal environments with salt spray.

The plant is often used in coastal landscaping due to its hardiness and ornamental value.
