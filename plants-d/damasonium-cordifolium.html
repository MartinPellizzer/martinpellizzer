Damasonium cordifolium

Damasonium cordifolium, commonly known as the heartleaf water clover, is a flowering plant native to wetland areas in Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves, which consist of three heart-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early summer, contributing to its ornamental value. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Due to its preference for wet environments, it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting aquatic ecosystems.
