Dryopteris Grossa

Cinnamon fern, scientifically known as Dryopteris grossa, is a striking fern native to eastern North America. It is easily recognizable by its tall, arching fronds that resemble cinnamon sticks, hence its common name. This plant thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and wetlands. With its lush, green foliage and unique appearance, it adds a natural elegance to gardens and wild landscapes. Its resilience and beauty make it a popular choice for both ornamental and ecological purposes.
