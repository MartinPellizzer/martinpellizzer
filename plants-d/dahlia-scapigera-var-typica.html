Dahlia scapigera var. typica

Dahlia scapigera var. typica, commonly known as the Mexican dahlia, is a herbaceous perennial native to Mexico.

It features upright stems with showy, single or semi-double flowers in a range of colors including red, orange, and yellow. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically green and covered in fine hairs. It blooms from late spring to early autumn, producing tuberous roots that serve as storage organs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
