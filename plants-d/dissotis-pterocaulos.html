Dissotis pterocaulos

Dissotis pterocaulos, commonly known as the broad-leaved dissotis, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its robust, clump-forming growth habit. The plant features broad, flat leaves that are often hairy, and it produces small, pale blue to purple flowers in dense clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along waterways.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
