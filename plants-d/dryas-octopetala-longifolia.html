Dryas octopetala longifolia

Dryas octopetala longifolia, commonly known as the long-flowered mountain avens, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to alpine and arctic regions.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its rosette growth form and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers with eight petals, giving it the species name "octopetala.". It thrives in cold, rocky environments and is adapted to low-light conditions.

Dryas octopetala longifolia is often used in traditional medicine and is a valuable indicator species for high-altitude ecosystems.
