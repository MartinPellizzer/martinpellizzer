Diospyros anosivolensis

Diospyros anosivolensis, commonly known as the Madagascar persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, orange-red berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential economic importance in local communities.
