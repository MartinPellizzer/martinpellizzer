Draba verna var. fallacina

Draba verna var. fallacina, commonly known as the spring whitlow, is a low-growing flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in early spring, followed by narrow, linear leaves.

Draba verna var. fallacina is a short-lived perennial that plays a role in stabilizing soil in its harsh environment.
