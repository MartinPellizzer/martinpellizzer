Diplothemium campestre var. orbignyi

Diplothemium campestre var. orbignyi, commonly known as the Brazilian star-of-Bethlehem, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features showy, star-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink, often with a subtle purple or red hue at the base. The plant has a fibrous root system and lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its blooming period usually occurs in the spring and early summer, adding a touch of elegance to the landscape.
