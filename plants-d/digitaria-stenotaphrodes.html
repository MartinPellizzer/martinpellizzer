Digitaria stenotaphrodes

Digitaria stenotaphrodes, commonly known as sand bluestem, is a warm-season grass native to the southeastern United States.

It thrives in sandy and well-drained soils, often found in open woodlands and prairies. The plant has narrow, blue-green blades and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in the summer. It is drought-tolerant and has a deep root system, making it resilient to harsh environmental conditions.

Digitaria stenotaphrodes is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in native landscapes.
