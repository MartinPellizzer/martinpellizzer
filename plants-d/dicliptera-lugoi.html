Dicliptera lugoi

Dicliptera lugoi, commonly known as the African silver grass, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant features narrow, silver-tomentose leaves that give it its common name, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks.

Dicliptera lugoi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
