Dipidax ciliata var. gracilis

Dipidax ciliata var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender fringeleaf, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, linear leaves that are often covered in fine, hair-like structures. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
