Daphne kiusiana var. kiusiana

Daphne kiusiana var. kiusiana, commonly known as the Korean daphne, is a deciduous shrub native to the Korean Peninsula.

It features striking clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that appear in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant has dark green, glossy leaves that turn yellow in autumn, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its compact growth habit and seasonal blooms make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
