Dimorphotheca pluvialis

Dimorphotheca pluvialis, commonly known as the coastal dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its yellow, dandelion-like flowers and hairy, silver-green leaves. The plant thrives in coastal areas and sandy soils, often found along the shorelines. It is an annual species that produces a capsule fruit containing numerous small seeds.

Dimorphotheca pluvialis plays a role in stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for small insects.
