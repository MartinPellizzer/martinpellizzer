Deianira cordifolia

Deianira cordifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf plant, is a flowering species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which give it its common name, and its clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests or along stream banks.

The leaves are not only aesthetically pleasing but also have been used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
