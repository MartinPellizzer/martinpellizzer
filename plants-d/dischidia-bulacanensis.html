Dischidia bulacanensis

Dischidia bulacanensis, commonly known as the Bulacan fig or Philippine dischidia, is a climbing vine native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the family Ancistrocladaceae and is characterized by its aerial roots and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by fleshy, berry-like fruits that are yellow when ripe. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Dischidia bulacanensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
