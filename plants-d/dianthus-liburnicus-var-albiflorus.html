Dianthus liburnicus var. albiflorus

Dianthus liburnicus var. albiflorus, commonly known as the white-flowered carnation, is a perennial plant native to the Balkans.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of fragrant, white flowers. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, bicolor flowers that are often pink-tinged at the base. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
