Delphinium splendens

Delphinium splendens, commonly known as the larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems that bear clusters of striking blue, purple, or white flowers. The plant's leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem. Delphinium splendens thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

It is also known for its toxic properties, containing alkaloids that can be harmful to humans and animals.
