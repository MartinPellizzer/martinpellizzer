Diospyros odasimae

Diospyros odasimae, commonly known as the Philippine persimmon, is a small to medium-sized tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves and yellowish-green flowers. The fruit is typically a small, smooth, orange to red drupe with a sweet, edible pulp. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in lowland and mountainous regions.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its fruit, which is used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
