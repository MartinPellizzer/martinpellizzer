Dillandia

Dillandia, commonly known as the rainbow flower or dillandia plant, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to South America and is characterized by its vibrant, colorful inflorescences that range from red to orange, yellow, and purple. The plant typically grows as an annual or biennial herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, while the flowers are arranged in dense, spherical clusters.

Dillandia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower due to its striking appearance.
