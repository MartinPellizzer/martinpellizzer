Dianthus eugeniae

Dianthus eugeniae, commonly known as the Eugenia's carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to white. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal habitats, thriving in well-drained soil. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces small, seed-like fruits known as capsules.

Dianthus eugeniae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
