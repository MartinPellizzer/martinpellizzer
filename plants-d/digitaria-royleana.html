Digitaria royleana

Digitaria royleana, commonly known as African ricegrass, is a perennial grass native to Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted shoots and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are often clustered at the base of the stems. It thrives in dry, open habitats and is adapted to arid environments.

Digitaria royleana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in erosion control.
