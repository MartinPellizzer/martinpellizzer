Dianthus sylvestris var. garganicus

Dianthus sylvestris var. garganicus, commonly known as the Garganica campion, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It features slender, hairy stems and produces small, pink to purple flowers in clusters. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky areas. It is characterized by its delicate appearance and is part of the Caryophyllaceae family.

This variety is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
