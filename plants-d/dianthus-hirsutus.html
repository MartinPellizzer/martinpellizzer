Dianthus hirsutus

Dianthus hirsutus, commonly known as the hairy pinks, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and produces clusters of pink or white flowers with five petals. The plant features hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Dianthus hirsutus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
