Dissanthelium calycinum subsp. mathewsii

Dissanthelium calycinum subsp.

mathewsii, commonly known as Mathews' needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and prairies.

The plant features slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a single, spike-like cluster of small, spikelet-bearing branches.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
