Doronicum macrophyllum

Doronicum macrophyllum, commonly known as the large-leaved leopard's bane, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features striking yellow flowers with dark purple markings, which are typically arranged in dense, terminal clusters. The plant has lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are broad and often lobed, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained, alkaline soils and prefers sunny to partially shaded locations.

Doronicum macrophyllum is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and is often used in rock gardens or as a border plant.
