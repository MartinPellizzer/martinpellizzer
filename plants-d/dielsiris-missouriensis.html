Dielsiris missouriensis

Dielsiris missouriensis, commonly known as the Missouri iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the central United States.

It typically grows in moist, rich soils along stream banks and in woodland areas. The plant features showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a fan-like pattern around the stem.

Dielsiris missouriensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in wetland habitats.
