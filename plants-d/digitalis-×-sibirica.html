Digitalis × sibirica

Digitalis × sibirica, commonly known as the Siberian foxglove, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features striking, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and it grows to a height of about 30–60 cm. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and historical use in traditional medicine.
