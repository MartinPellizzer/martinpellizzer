Digitaria corymbosa subsp. marattensis

Digitaria corymbosa subsp.

marattensis, commonly known as the marattensia grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions. It typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands, disturbed soils, and along roadsides.

The plant has narrow, upright leaves and produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its tolerance to drought conditions.

The subspecies is often used for forage and erosion control due to its hardy nature and rapid growth.
