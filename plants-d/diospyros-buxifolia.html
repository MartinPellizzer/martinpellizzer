Diospyros buxifolia

Diospyros buxifolia, commonly known as the boxleaf persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its dense, glossy foliage and small, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by edible, orange-red berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

The bark is dark and cracked, and the wood is hard and durable, making it valuable for carving and furniture.
