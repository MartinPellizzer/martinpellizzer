Dracula diabola

Dracula diabola, commonly known as the vampire orchid, is a rare and striking orchid native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It is characterized by its dark, velvety petals that resemble the appearance of a vampire's face, giving it its ominous common name. The plant produces intricate, spurred flowers that are typically a deep red or purple hue. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires high levels of moisture and specific soil conditions.

Due to its rarity and unique appearance, it is highly sought after by botanists and collectors alike.
