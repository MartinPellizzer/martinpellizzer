Durio cupreua

Durio cupreua, commonly known as the copper durian, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Duraceae family and is characterized by its large, spiky fruit. The plant produces a strong, aromatic odor, which is a notable feature of the durian genus. Its leaves are typically broad and elliptical, with a smooth texture.

The fruit is encased in a thick, fibrous husk that protects the segmented, creamy pulp inside.
