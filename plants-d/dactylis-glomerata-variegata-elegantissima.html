Dactylis glomerata variegata-elegantissima

Dactylis glomerata variegata-elegantissima, commonly known as the variegated velvet grass, is a decorative grass species characterized by its striking variegated foliage.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves with a mix of green, white, and cream-colored patterns, creating a visually appealing contrast. This cultivar is often used in ornamental landscaping for its aesthetic value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions. It prefers full sun to partial shade and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.

The plant produces small, pale green seed heads that add texture to the landscape throughout the growing season.
