Dicorynia paraensis

Dicorynia paraensis, commonly known as the Paraensis Cacau, is a species of tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its large, palmate leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant is notable for its fruit, which contains edible seeds used in traditional medicine and as a source of caffeine. It thrives in humid, tropical climates with well-drained soil.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered vulnerable, highlighting the need for conservation efforts.
