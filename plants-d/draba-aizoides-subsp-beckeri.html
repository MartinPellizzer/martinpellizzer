Draba aizoides subsp. beckeri

Draba aizoides subsp.

beckeri, commonly known as Becker's whitlow grass, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. It has basal leaves that are linear to lanceolate, and its flowers are usually pale yellow to white in color.

This subspecies is known for its resilience to harsh climatic conditions and is often found in high-altitude ecosystems.
