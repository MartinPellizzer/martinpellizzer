Diospyros mapingo

Diospyros mapingo, commonly known as the African persimmon, is a small to medium-sized tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes other persimmon and ebony trees. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same tree. Its fruit is a small, round berry that turns from green to orange-red when mature, often containing a single large seed.

The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a leathery texture with a distinct midrib.
