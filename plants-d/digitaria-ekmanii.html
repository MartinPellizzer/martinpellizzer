Digitaria ekmanii

Digitaria ekmanii, commonly known as the African fingergrass, is a perennial grass species native to Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Digitaria ekmanii is often found in disturbed areas, such as roadsides and fields, and is adapted to both wet and dry environments.
