Dolichos ornatus

Dolichos ornatus, commonly known as the ornate cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink, followed by elongated, bean-like pods. Dolichos ornatus is often cultivated for its edible seeds, which are rich in protein and used in various cuisines.

Additionally, it plays a role in soil enrichment due to its nitrogen-fixing ability.
