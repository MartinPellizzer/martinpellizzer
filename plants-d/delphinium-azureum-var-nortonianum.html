Delphinium azureum var. nortonianum

Delphinium azureum var. nortonianum, commonly known as the Norton's blue larkspur, is a striking perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces clusters of showy, sky-blue to violet flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped, serrated, and arranged alternately along the stem.

The species is adapted to rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.
