Dicentra thalictrifolia

Dicentra thalictrifolia, commonly known as the nodding dicentra, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features delicate, fern-like leaves and distinctive, spurred flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in partial to full shade. Its flowers are pink to red in color, with a unique, spurred structure that resembles a small, elegant bell.

Dicentra thalictrifolia is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to naturalize in suitable conditions.
