Darwinia taxifolia var. intermedia

Darwinia taxifolia var. intermedia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved Darwinia, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and showy spikes of tubular flowers. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, often arranged in a rosette at the base of the plant. This variety is known for its striking floral display, which typically blooms in spring and attracts various pollinators.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and adaptability to well-drained soils.
