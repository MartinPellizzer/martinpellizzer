Danthonia shrevei

Danthonia shrevei, commonly known as Shreve's fescue, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or swampy areas. Its flowering stalks bear small, spike-like inflorescences that produce numerous tiny flowers.

Danthonia shrevei is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
