Daphnandra melasmena

Daphnandra melasmena, commonly known as the black-leaved daphnandra, is a small evergreen shrub native to the island of Mauritius.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature. It is often found in coastal areas and rocky habitats, thriving in well-drained soils.

Daphnandra melasmena is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and flowering display.
