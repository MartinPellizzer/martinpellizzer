Diasperus obscurus

Diasperus obscurus, commonly known as the dark spiny grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and compact growth habit, which helps it thrive in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in spikelets, a common feature among grasses. Its leaves are narrow and often covered with fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant adaptations.

Diasperus obscurus is often found in rocky soils and is used in some regions for forage or as a stabilizing plant in degraded landscapes.
