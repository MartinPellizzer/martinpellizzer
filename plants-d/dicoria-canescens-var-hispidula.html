Dicoria canescens var. hispidula

Dicoria canescens var. hispidula, commonly known as the hairy dicoria, is a perennial herb native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological studies and traditional medicine.
