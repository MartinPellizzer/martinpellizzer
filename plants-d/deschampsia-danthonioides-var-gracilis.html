Deschampsia danthonioides var. gracilis

Deschampsia danthonioides var. gracilis, commonly known as slender feathergrass, is a low-growing perennial grass native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features fine, silver-green foliage and produces delicate, feathery seed heads that add ornamental value to gardens. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant. Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it a versatile choice for landscaping.

Botanically, it is characterized by its narrow leaves and panicle-like inflorescence, which are typical of the Poaceae family.
