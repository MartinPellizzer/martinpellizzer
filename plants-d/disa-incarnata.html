Disa incarnata

Disa incarnata, commonly known as the pink disa, is a striking flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its vivid pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, while its flowers are borne on tall, erect stems.

Disa incarnata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable growing conditions.
