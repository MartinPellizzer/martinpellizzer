Digitalis purpurea f. dubia

Digitalis purpurea f. dubia, commonly known as the purple foxglove, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It features tall, spire-like inflorescences composed of tubular, violet to purple flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often used in garden landscapes.
