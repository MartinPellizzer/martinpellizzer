Diospyros amazonica

Diospyros amazonica, commonly known as the Amazon persimmon, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon Basin.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its thick, dark bark and large, heart-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into edible, orange-colored fruits with a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor. Its fruits are an important food source for various wildlife species in the rainforest ecosystem.

The plant is also valued for its hard, dense wood, which is used in traditional craftsmanship and construction.
