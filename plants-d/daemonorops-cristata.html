Daemonorops cristata

Daemonorops cristata, commonly known as the crested cycad, is a species of cycad native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and its distinctive, fan-shaped leaves that often have a waxy texture. The plant produces large, cone-like structures that contain both male and female reproductive organs. It is a slow-growing plant that thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight.

Due to its unique appearance and cultural significance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
