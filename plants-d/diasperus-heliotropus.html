Diasperus heliotropus

Diasperus heliotropus, commonly known as the sunflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy composite flower heads that typically face the sun, a trait known as heliotropism. The plant features a central stem with alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers consist of numerous small tubular florets surrounded by ray florets, which give it a distinctive appearance.

Diasperus heliotropus is native to various regions in the Americas and is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and seeds.
