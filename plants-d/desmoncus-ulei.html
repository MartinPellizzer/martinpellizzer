Desmoncus ulei

Desmoncus ulei, commonly known as the yellow false flax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers. Its botanical characteristics include a fibrous root system and a branching stem.

It is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to thrive in coastal environments.
