Diospyros pachycalyx

Diospyros pachycalyx, commonly known as the thick-shelled ebony, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its dark, dense wood that is highly valued for its durability and aesthetic qualities. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by elliptical, edible fruits that turn from green to dark purple as they mature. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a smooth surface, while its bark is thick and fissured, contributing to its common name.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in humid, tropical climates with well-drained soils.
