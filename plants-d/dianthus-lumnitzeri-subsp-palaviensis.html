Dianthus lumnitzeri subsp. palaviensis

Dianthus lumnitzeri subsp.

palaviensis, commonly known as the Sierra Leone carnation, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of West Africa. It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to reddish flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as forest understories and rocky areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often reddish in color.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
