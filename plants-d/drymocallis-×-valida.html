Drymocallis × valida

Drymocallis × valida, commonly known as the goldenrod hybrid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy inflorescences. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers, which bloom in late summer to early fall, range in color from yellow to orange, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Drymocallis × valida is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
