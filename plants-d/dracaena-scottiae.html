Dracaena scottiae

Dracaena scottiae, commonly known as the Corn Plant, is a member of the Asparagaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a perennial evergreen shrub characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and lance-shaped, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that appear at the base of the leaves, followed by red, fleshy berries. Dracaena scottiae is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its tolerance of low light and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.

Its air-purifying qualities and elegant appearance make it a popular choice for both ornamental and indoor gardening.
