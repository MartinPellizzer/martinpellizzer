Dioscorea lanosa

Dioscorea lanosa, commonly known as the hairy yam, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of yellowish-white flowers followed by cylindrical, fleshy fruits containing numerous seeds. Dioscorea lanosa is valued for its tuberous roots, which are used in traditional medicine and as a food source.

Its growth habit and morphological features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
