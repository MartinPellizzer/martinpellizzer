Distephanus polygalifolius

Distephanus polygalifolius, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and showy, clustered flowers that bloom in a variety of colors. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, with a compact rosette structure that helps it retain moisture. Its leaves are often heart-shaped and covered in a fine layer of hair, which aids in reducing water loss.

Distephanus polygalifolius is widely cultivated as a decorative plant due to its ornamental value and adaptability to indoor growing conditions.
