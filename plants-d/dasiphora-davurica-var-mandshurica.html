Dasiphora davurica var. mandshurica

Dasiphora davurica var. mandshurica, commonly known as the Siberian poppy, is a flowering plant native to the cold regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in alpine and subarctic environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are palmately divided, and it is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This variant is notable for its hardiness and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
