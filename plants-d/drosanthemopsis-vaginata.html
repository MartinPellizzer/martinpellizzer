Drosanthemopsis vaginata

Drosanthemopsis vaginata, commonly known as the velvetleaf gloxinia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its velvety, dark green foliage and clusters of showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its flowers are adapted for pollination by hummingbirds, featuring a deep nectar-filled tube.

Drosanthemopsis vaginata thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade in its natural habitat.
