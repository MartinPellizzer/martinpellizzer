Dactylorhiza fuchsii subsp. hebridensis

Dactylorhiza fuchsii subsp.

hebridensis, commonly known as the Hebridean orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the British Isles. It is a perennial herb with a rhizomatous root system and produces a single, slender, erect stem.

The plant features elliptical, dark green leaves and bears a spike of fragrant, pale purple to white flowers. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland and heathland habitats.

The subspecies is notable for its distinctive floral structure and is considered a rare and protected species in some regions.
