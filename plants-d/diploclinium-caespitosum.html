Diploclinium caespitosum

Diploclinium caespitosum, commonly known as the creeping diploclinium, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. The plant has a mat-forming growth habit, with stolons that root at the nodes to form dense clusters. Its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, often with a yellow or greenish hue.

The leaves are usually opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.
