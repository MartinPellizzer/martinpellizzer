Dichocarpum nipponicum f. didymocalyx

Dichocarpum nipponicum f. didymocalyx, commonly known as the Japanese false bellflower, is a rare and unique variant of the species native to Japan.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors. The plant features striking, bell-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale pink, with a distinctive two-lobed structure. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique floral display.
