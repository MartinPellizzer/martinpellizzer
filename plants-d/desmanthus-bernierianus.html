Desmanthus bernierianus

Desmanthus bernierianus, commonly known as the slender burr legume, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or white. The plant is adapted to grow in disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility.

Its seeds are encased in small, spiny pods, which aid in seed dispersal by animals.
