Delphinium longibracteatum

Delphinium longibracteatum, commonly known as the long-bract delphinium, is a tall perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features striking blue to violet spires of flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette, with some leaves along the stem. Its long, slender bracts are a distinguishing feature, contributing to its common name.

Delphinium longibracteatum is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its vibrant, showy flowers.
