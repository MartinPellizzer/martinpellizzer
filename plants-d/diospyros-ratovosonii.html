Diospyros ratovosonii

Diospyros ratovosonii, commonly known as the Madagascar persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family, which includes ebony and persimmon trees. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into orange-red, edible fruits. The fruit has a sweet, astringent flavor and is often consumed fresh or used in traditional remedies.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food for various wildlife species.
