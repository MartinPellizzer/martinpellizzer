Dichaea glauca

Dichaea glauca, commonly known as the blue-tipped orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers with white markings, which are often adorned with a distinctive blue tip. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, such as forest understories, where it relies on mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient absorption. Its leaves are usually dark green and lance-shaped, and it produces small, clustered inflorescences.

Due to its ornamental value, Dichaea glauca is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
