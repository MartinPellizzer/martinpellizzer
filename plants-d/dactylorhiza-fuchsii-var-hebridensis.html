Dactylorhiza fuchsii var. hebridensis

Dactylorhiza fuchsii var. hebridensis, commonly known as the Scottish orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the British Isles.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its erect, grass-like foliage and single, showy orchid flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing pink to purple flowers with a distinctive lip. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in heathland and woodland habitats.

This variety is notable for its conservation status, as it is rare and protected in many regions.
