Dianthus armeria var. glabratus

Dianthus armeria var. glabratus, commonly known as the nakedleaf campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its simple, usually hairy stems and small, pink or white flowers that bloom in clusters. The leaves are typically narrow and may be glabrous, giving the plant its varietal name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
