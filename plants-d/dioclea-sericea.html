Dioclea sericea

Dioclea sericea, commonly known as the silky dioclea, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender stems and small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces smooth, oval-shaped fruits that turn red when mature, containing several seeds. Its leaves are typically alternate and have a soft, velvety texture.

Dioclea sericea is often found in moist tropical forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
