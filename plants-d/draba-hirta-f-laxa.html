Draba hirta f. laxa

Draba hirta f. laxa, commonly known as the laxa dwarf cress, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically features a basal rosette of hairy, linear leaves and produces small, yellow flowers in early spring. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold climates with short growing seasons.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often found in mountainous and tundra environments.
