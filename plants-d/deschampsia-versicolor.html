Deschampsia versicolor

Deschampsia versicolor, commonly known as variable feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-blue foliage that turns a striking copper color in the fall. The plant produces slender, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late summer, adding visual interest to its appearance. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Deschampsia versicolor is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
