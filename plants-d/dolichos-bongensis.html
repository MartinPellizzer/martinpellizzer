Dolichos bongensis

Dolichos bongensis, commonly known as the Bong's bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its twining stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by elongated, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It is often cultivated for its edible seeds, which are used in traditional cuisines.

Additionally, it plays a role in soil enrichment due to its nitrogen-fixing ability.
