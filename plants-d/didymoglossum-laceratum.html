Didymoglossum laceratum

Didymoglossum laceratum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed bog moss, is a small, mat-forming moss that thrives in wet, acidic environments such as bogs and fens.

It has slender, linear leaves that are often recurved and covered with small, translucent oil cells. The plant produces small, cylindrical capsules that are typically reddish-brown in color and release spores when mature. Didymoglossum laceratum is an important component of peat-forming ecosystems and is often found in association with other sphagnum mosses.

Its delicate structure and preference for moist habitats make it a characteristic indicator species of oligotrophic wetlands.
