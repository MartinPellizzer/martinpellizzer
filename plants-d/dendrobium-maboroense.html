Dendrobium maboroense

Dendrobium maboroense, commonly known as the Maboro orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Dendrobium genus, which is part of the Orchidaceae family, and is characterized by its sympodial growth habit. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green, typically during the dry season. Its pseudobulbs are thick and fleshy, adapted to store water in its native arid environment.

Dendrobium maboroense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for horticultural purposes.
