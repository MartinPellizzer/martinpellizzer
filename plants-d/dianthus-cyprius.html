Dianthus cyprius

Dianthus cyprius, commonly known as Cyprus campion, is a flowering plant native to the island of Cyprus.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink or white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

Dianthus cyprius is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and is also valued for its historical and cultural significance.
