Dicella tricarpa

Dicella tricarpa, commonly known as the three-ridged dicella, is a flowering plant species belonging to the family Dicellaceae.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in clusters. The plant has a unique morphology, with three distinct ridges on its fruiting structures, which is a key identifying feature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and understudied species in botanical research.
