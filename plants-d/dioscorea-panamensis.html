Dioscorea panamensis

Dioscorea panamensis, commonly known as the Panamanian yam, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary part used for culinary and medicinal purposes. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers and develops capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its tubers are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is also valued for its role in local agriculture and biodiversity.
