Datura alba var. africana

Datura alba var. africana, commonly known as the African angel's trumpet, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom in summer, typically in clusters at the ends of the stems. The plant has a hairy, glandular stem and leaves that are usually ovate to lanceolate in shape. It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other toxic plants like nightshade and belladonna.

The flowers emit a strong, sweet fragrance, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths.
