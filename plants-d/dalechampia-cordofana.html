Dalechampia cordofana

Dalechampia cordofana, commonly known as the velvetleaf or Mexican velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically clustered in spikes. Its growth habit is herbaceous, with a tendency to form dense stands in moist, shady environments.

Dalechampia cordofana is also known for its potential medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
