Datura chlorantha atroviolacea-plenissima

Datura chlorantha atroviolacea-plenissima, commonly known as the purple velvet nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its large, showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically deep purple to violet in color. The plant belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and peppers. Its leaves are broad and lobed, often with a velvety texture, and it produces toxic berries that contain tropane alkaloids.

This species is known for its nocturnal blooming habit, attracting pollinators such as moths and bats.
