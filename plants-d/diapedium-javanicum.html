Diapedium javanicum

Diapedium javanicum, commonly known as the Java spider plant, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It features slender, creeping stems that often root at the nodes, allowing it to spread easily. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are followed by tiny, rounded berries. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

Diapedium javanicum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
