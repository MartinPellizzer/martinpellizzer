Dischema glaucum

Dischema glaucum, commonly known as the bluebells of the genus Dischema, is a herbaceous plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, blue to purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or woodland edges. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating, which helps retain moisture.

Dischema glaucum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
