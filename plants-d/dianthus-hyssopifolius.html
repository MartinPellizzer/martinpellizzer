Dianthus hyssopifolius

Dianthus hyssopifolius, commonly known as the hoary dianthus, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves and produces clusters of pink or purple flowers with five petals. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period, which extends from spring to early autumn.
