Dyssodia gracilis

Dyssodia gracilis, commonly known as the slender dyssodia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
