Dactylis paleacea

Dactylis paleacea, commonly known as slender oatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and slender, pale green leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Dactylis paleacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
