Dichapetalum minutiflorum

Dichapetalum minutiflorum, commonly known as the dwarf flame flower, is a small shrub native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, bright red flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a smooth texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Due to its unique appearance and compact size, it is often used in container gardening and as a border plant.
