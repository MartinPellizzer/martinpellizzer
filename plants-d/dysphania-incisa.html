Dysphania incisa

Dysphania incisa, commonly known as narrowleaf spinach, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its slender, alternate leaves that are typically less than 5 cm in length. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, and its stems are often reddish in color. Dysphania incisa is known for its edible leaves, which are similar in taste to spinach and are used in various culinary traditions.

It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.
