Dactylorhiza majalis subsp. nieschalkiorum

Dactylorhiza majalis subsp.

nieschalkiorum, commonly known as the Nieschalki's marsh orchid, is a rare orchid species native to parts of Europe. It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in wet meadows and marshy habitats, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its specialized mycorrhizal relationships with fungi.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered in many regions.
