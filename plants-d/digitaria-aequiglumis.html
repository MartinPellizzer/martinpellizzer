Digitaria aequiglumis

Digitaria aequiglumis, commonly known as flat-seeded grass or broadleaf bluestem, is a perennial grass species native to the central and southern United States.

It is characterized by its flat, blade-like leaves and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences with pale green to brown flowers, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Digitaria aequiglumis is valued for its erosion control capabilities and as a forage plant for livestock.
