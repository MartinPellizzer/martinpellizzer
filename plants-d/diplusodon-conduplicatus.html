Diplusodon conduplicatus

Diplusodon conduplicatus, commonly known as the double-leafed orchid, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the humid forests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, intricate floral structure. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and drawing moisture and nutrients from the air. Its leaves are often arranged in a double-layered pattern, giving it its scientific name.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is considered endangered and is protected in many regions.
