Dalea weberbaueri var. sericophylla

Dalea weberbaueri var. sericophylla, commonly known as the silverleaf Dalea, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its finely hairy, silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in late spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its unique silvery appearance and ecological role make it an important species in its native ecosystems.
