Doritis pulcherrima

Doritis pulcherrima, commonly known as the "Flaming Katy" or "Fireplant," is a succulent native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems and vibrant, showy flowers. The plant produces bright orange to red blooms that resemble flames, which appear in late summer to early autumn. Its leaves are typically green with a bluish tint and are arranged in a rosette pattern.

Doritis pulcherrima is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and succulent collections.
