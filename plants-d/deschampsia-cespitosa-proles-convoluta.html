Deschampsia cespitosa proles convoluta

Deschampsia cespitosa proles convoluta, commonly known as silky bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, bluish-green leaves and its ability to form dense, sod-like mats. The plant produces slender, spike-like inflorescences that are often rolled or twisted, giving it its specific epithet "proles convoluta.". It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wet meadows, marshes, and along stream banks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in wetland ecosystems.
