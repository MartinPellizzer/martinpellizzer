Dombeya acerifolia var. typica

Dombeya acerifolia var. typica, commonly known as the silverleaf dombeya, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern and southern regions of the United States.

It is characterized by its striking white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features finely serrated leaves that are covered in a silvery-gray bloom, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for small gardens or as a container plant.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
