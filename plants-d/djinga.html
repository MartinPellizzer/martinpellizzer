Djinga

Djinga, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers and thick, fleshy leaves. The plant typically grows as a perennial herb, often forming a compact rosette structure. Its leaves are usually velvety and come in various shades of green, while the flowers bloom in shades of purple, pink, and white.

Djinga is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
