Dendrobium macropus

Dendrobium macropus, commonly known as the large-leafed orchid, is an epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with multiple stems that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and orange, often with a distinct red or orange lip. Its leaves are long, strap-like, and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

Dendrobium macropus thrives in warm, humid environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.
