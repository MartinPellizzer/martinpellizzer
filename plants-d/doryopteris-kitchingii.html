Doryopteris kitchingii

Doryopteris kitchingii, commonly known as the Kitching's shield fern, is a rare and specialized fern species native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes in South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, shield-shaped fronds that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine, silky hairs. The plant thrives in cool, moist, and shaded environments, often growing on rocky substrates or in crevices. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are clustered on the underside of the fronds, a common feature among ferns.
