Draba subglabrata var. leiocarpa

Draba subglabrata var. leiocarpa, commonly known as the smooth-leaved whitlow grass, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in open habitats such as tundra and mountain meadows. Its leaves are generally smooth and succulent, adapted to retain moisture in harsh environments.

The variety leiocarpa is distinguished by its more rounded and less hairy leaves compared to other varieties of Draba subglabrata.
