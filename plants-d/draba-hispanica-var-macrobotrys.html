Draba hispanica var. macrobotrys

Draba hispanica var. macrobotrys, commonly known as the large-flowered rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly habitats, often in mountainous regions. It has basal leaves that are linear to oblanceolate in shape, and its flowers are usually bright yellow or white.

This variety is distinguished by its larger, showy flowers compared to other Draba species.
