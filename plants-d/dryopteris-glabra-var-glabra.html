Dryopteris glabra var. glabra

Dryopteris glabra var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth shield fern, is a perennial fern native to North America.

It is characterized by its finely divided, lance-shaped fronds that are smooth and glabrous, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its spore-bearing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticultural settings.
