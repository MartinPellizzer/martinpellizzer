Dactylorhiza majalis subsp. caucasica

Dactylorhiza majalis subsp.

caucasica, commonly known as the Caucasian marsh orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Caucasus region. It is a perennial herb with a rhizomatous root system and typically grows in wet or marshy habitats.

The plant features a single, erect stem bearing several lance-shaped leaves and a single spike of pale yellow to greenish flowers. Its flowers are spurred and have a distinct lip, characteristic of the Orchidaceae family.

It is adapted to moist environments and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators.
