Dactylaea schizopetala

Dactylaea schizopetala, commonly known as the desert bottle tree, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its thick, cylindrical stem that stores water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. Its leaves are typically small and scale-like, reducing water loss through transpiration.

Dactylaea schizopetala is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental appearance in drought-tolerant landscaping.
