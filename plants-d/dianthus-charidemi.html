Dianthus charidemi

Dianthus charidemi, commonly known as the Greek carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and has hairy, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Dianthus charidemi is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant appearance.
