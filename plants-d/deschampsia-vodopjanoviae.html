Deschampsia vodopjanoviae

Deschampsia vodopjanoviae, commonly known as the Vodopjanov's feathergrass, is a low-growing perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and delicate, feathery inflorescences that bloom in late summer. This plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows and steppe environments. Its botanical features include narrow, grass-like leaves and small, wind-pollinated flowers arranged in panicle-like clusters.

Deschampsia vodopjanoviae is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in harsh, arid habitats.
