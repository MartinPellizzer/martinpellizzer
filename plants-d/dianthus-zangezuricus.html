Dianthus zangezuricus

Dianthus zangezuricus, commonly known as the Zangezur carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Dianthus zangezuricus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
