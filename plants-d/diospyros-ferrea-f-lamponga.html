Diospyros ferrea f. lamponga

Diospyros ferrea f. lamponga, commonly known as the Brazilian persimmon or red persimmon, is a variant of the persimmon genus native to Brazil.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree characterized by its thick, leathery leaves and red-brown bark. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into orange to red, fleshy fruits. The fruit is typically edible, with a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor, and is often consumed fresh or used in local culinary traditions.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.
