Diasperus caroliniensis

Diasperus caroliniensis, commonly known as the Carolina lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along stream banks and wetlands. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, and it is often found in shaded to partially shaded environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
