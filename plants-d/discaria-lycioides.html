Discaria lycioides

Discaria lycioides, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its fern-like, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in spherical inflorescences, which are followed by distinctive, inflated seed pods. It is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Discaria lycioides is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it an important plant in soil enrichment.
