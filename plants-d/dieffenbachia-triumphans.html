Dieffenbachia triumphans

Dieffenbachia triumphans, commonly known as the Leopard Plant, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often speckled with white or yellow spots, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant belongs to the Araceae family, which includes other ornamental plants like peace lilies and calla lilies. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires moderate humidity and well-draining soil.

Dieffenbachia triumphans is popular as a houseplant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
