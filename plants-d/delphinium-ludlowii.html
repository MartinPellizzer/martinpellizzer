Delphinium ludlowii

Delphinium ludlowii, commonly known as Ludlow's larkspur, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to six feet. The plant produces clusters of blue to purple flowers, which bloom in late summer and early fall. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough, hairy texture.

Delphinium ludlowii is a member of the Ranunculaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and natural habitats.
