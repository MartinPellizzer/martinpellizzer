Darwinia thomasii

Darwinia thomasii, commonly known as the Thomas's Darwinia, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, spike-like inflorescence composed of numerous small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in heathlands and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its flowers are pollinated by nectar-feeding birds and insects, contributing to its ecological significance in native ecosystems.

Darwinia thomasii is also valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture due to its vibrant floral display.
