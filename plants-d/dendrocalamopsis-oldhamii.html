Dendrocalamopsis oldhamii

Dendrocalamopsis oldhamii, commonly known as the oldham's bamboo, is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a perennial plant characterized by its tall, slender stems that form dense clumps. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in panicles, followed by small, three-angled seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its graceful appearance.
