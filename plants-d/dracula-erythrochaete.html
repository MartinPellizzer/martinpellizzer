Dracula erythrochaete

Dracula erythrochaete, commonly known as the Red-veined Dracula, is a rare and striking orchid native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its vivid, crimson veins on its dark green leaves. The plant produces delicate, pendulous flowers with a unique coloration pattern that resembles the blood of a vampire, giving it its dramatic common name. It thrives in high-altitude, humid environments and requires specific conditions to flourish.

Due to its rarity and beauty, it is highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts and conservationists alike.
