Dalea obovata

Dalea obovata, commonly known as the broad-leafed milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pink to purple pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands. Dalea obovata is a perennial herb that produces small, bean-like pods containing seeds.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators.
