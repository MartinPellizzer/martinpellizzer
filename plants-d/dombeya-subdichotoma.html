Dombeya subdichotoma

Dombeya subdichotoma, commonly known as the rock dombeya, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its branching habit and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has smooth, greyish bark and oval, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

Dombeya subdichotoma is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
