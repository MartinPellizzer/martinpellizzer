Diospyros ambanjensis

Diospyros ambanjensis, commonly known as the Madagascar persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by orange to red, fleshy fruits. The fruit is edible, with a sweet, astringent flavor, and is often consumed fresh or used in local cuisines.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food for various wildlife species.
