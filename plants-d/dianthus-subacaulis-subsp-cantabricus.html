Dianthus subacaulis subsp. cantabricus

Dianthus subacaulis subsp.

cantabricus, commonly known as the Cantabrian campion, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It features compact, hairy stems and bright pink to red flowers that bloom in early summer.

The leaves are linear and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its rugged appearance. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
