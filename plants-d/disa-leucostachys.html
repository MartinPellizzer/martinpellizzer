Disa leucostachys

Disa leucostachys, commonly known as the white-stemmed Disa, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in grasslands and open woodlands, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers are often visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance.

Disa leucostachys is valued not only for its ornamental appeal but also for its role in local biodiversity.
