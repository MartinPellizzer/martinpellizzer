Dioscorea oreodoxa

Dioscorea oreodoxa, commonly known as the wild yam, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Dioscoreaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas. The plant is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are rich in starch and have been used traditionally for food and medicinal purposes. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and often have a hairy texture.

The flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant, and are typically greenish-white in color.
