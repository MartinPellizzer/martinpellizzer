Dactylorchis incarnata var. angustispicata

Dactylorchis incarnata var. angustispicata, commonly known as the narrow-spurred purple twayblade, is a terrestrial orchid native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, violet-colored flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is typically found in shaded, moist woodlands. It has a symbiotic relationship with mycorrhizal fungi, which is essential for its nutrient uptake.

The species is notable for its delicate appearance and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
