Dioscorea frutescens

Dioscorea frutescens, commonly known as the wild yam, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and cylindrical, tuberous roots that are often used in traditional medicine. Its roots contain compounds such as diosgenin, which has been studied for potential pharmaceutical applications.

Dioscorea frutescens is also valued for its role in local ecosystems and as a source of food in some indigenous communities.
