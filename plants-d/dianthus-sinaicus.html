Dianthus sinaicus

Dianthus sinaicus, commonly known as the Sinai carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Sinai Peninsula in Egypt.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant produces small, oval leaves that are typically hairy and covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in rocky or arid environments.

Dianthus sinaicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
