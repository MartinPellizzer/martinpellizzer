Dalbergia richardii

Dalbergia richardii, commonly known as African rosewood, is a tropical tree native to Central and Southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, dark reddish-brown wood, which is highly valued for its beauty and durability. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces flat, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

The tree is often found in moist, well-drained soils and can grow up to 20 meters tall.
