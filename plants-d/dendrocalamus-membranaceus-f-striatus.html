Dendrocalamus membranaceus f. striatus

Dendrocalamus membranaceus f. striatus, commonly known as the striped bamboo, is a variant of the giant bamboo species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinct longitudinal stripes on the culms, which give it a unique appearance. The plant is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is known for its rapid growth and tall stature. Its culms are hollow and segmented, typical of bamboo species, and are used in construction and crafts.

The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in wetlands and lowland areas.
