Decostea racemosa

Decostea racemosa, commonly known as the coral tree, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a vibrant display. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets arranged in an alternating pattern. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a notable species in the botanical world.
