Dichanthelium commutatum

Dichanthelium commutatum, commonly known as the rough false brome, is a grass species native to the United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides, fields, and prairies. It has a fibrous root system and produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Dichanthelium commutatum is often found in sandy or clay soils and is adapted to a variety of environmental conditions.
