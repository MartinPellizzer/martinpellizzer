Dendrophthora geniculata

Dendrophthora geniculata, commonly known as the spiralwood, is a rare and unusual plant species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique spiral growth pattern, where the stems twist around their own axis in a helical manner. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in late spring, followed by the development of elongated, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a waxy surface that helps reduce water loss in its arid habitat.

Due to its distinctive morphology and limited distribution, Dendrophthora geniculata is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
