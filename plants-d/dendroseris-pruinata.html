Dendroseris pruinata

Dendroseris pruinata, commonly known as the waxyleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of softly hairy, oval-shaped leaves that give it a distinctive waxy appearance. The plant produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
