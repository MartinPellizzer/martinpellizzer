Dactylis glomerata subsp. ibizensis

Dactylis glomerata subsp.

ibizensis, commonly known as the Ibiza grass, is a perennial grass native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and compact, dense inflorescences.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers. It is adapted to dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun.

This subspecies is often used in ornamental gardening and as a ground cover due to its hardy nature and aesthetic appeal.
