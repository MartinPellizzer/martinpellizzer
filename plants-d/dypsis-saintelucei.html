Dypsis saintelucei

Dypsis saintelucei, commonly known as the Saint Ellice palm, is a tropical plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often found in rainforests. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardens.

The species is also noted for its slow growth rate and ability to thrive in well-drained soil.
