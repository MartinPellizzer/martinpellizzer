Dendrochilum edentulum

Dendrochilum edentulum, commonly known as the toothless tree orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and linear, lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and orchid enthusiasts alike.
