Draba nikoensis

Draba nikoensis, commonly known as Niko's winter cress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a small, herbaceous annual or biennial species native to Central Asia, particularly found in Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to cold, arid environments. It produces small, white or yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, and its leaves are usually basal, pinnately divided.

Draba nikoensis is known for its resilience to harsh climates and is often found in mountainous regions.
