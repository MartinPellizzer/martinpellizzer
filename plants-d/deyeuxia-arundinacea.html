Deyeuxia arundinacea

Deyeuxia arundinacea, commonly known as common reedgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, pond edges, and stream banks. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are often covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers.

Deyeuxia arundinacea plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
