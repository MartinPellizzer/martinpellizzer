Dioscorea mucronata

Dioscorea mucronata, commonly known as the sharp-pointed yam, is a climbing vine belonging to the family Dioscoreaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces tuberous roots that are often used in traditional medicine and as a food source. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its flowers are small and usually yellow or greenish.

The plant's morphology is adapted for climbing, with tendrils that help it ascend towards light.
