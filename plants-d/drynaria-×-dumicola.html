Drynaria × dumicola

Drynaria × dumicola, commonly known as the rock fern, is a hybrid species within the Drynaria genus.

It is a terrestrial fern that typically grows in rocky or coastal environments. The plant features a creeping rhizome with erect, lanceolate fronds that are often covered in fine hairs. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds in clusters called sori.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging conditions.
