Dactylicapnos macrocapnos subsp. macrocapnos

Dactylicapnos macrocapnos subsp.

macrocapnos, commonly known as the large-flowered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features showy, tubular blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical characteristics make it an attractive and relatively low-maintenance addition to gardens.
