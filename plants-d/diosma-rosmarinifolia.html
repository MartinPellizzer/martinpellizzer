Diosma rosmarinifolia

Diosma rosmarinifolia, commonly known as the rosemary-leaved Diosma, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows as a shrub with slender, woody stems and small, opposite leaves that resemble those of rosemary. Its flowers are usually clustered in umbels and can range in color from white to purple, depending on the species.

Diosma rosmarinifolia is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal uses.
