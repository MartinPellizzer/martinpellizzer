Dacryodes klaineana var. lepidota

Dacryodes klaineana var. lepidota, commonly known as the glossy ironwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green, leathery leaves that give it a glossy appearance. The tree produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by spherical, woody fruits containing one or two seeds. Its hard, heavy wood is highly valued for construction and furniture-making.

The plant is also known for its ability to thrive in humid, tropical climates with well-drained soil.
