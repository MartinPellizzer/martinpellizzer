Dicranopteris intermedia

Dicranopteris intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate water fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that give it a lacy appearance. The plant typically grows in wet, shady environments such as marshes and along stream banks. Its rhizomes are often submerged or partially submerged, anchoring it to the substrate.

Dicranopteris intermedia is a perennial plant that plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
