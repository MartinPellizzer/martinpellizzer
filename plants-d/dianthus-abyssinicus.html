Dianthus abyssinicus

Dianthus abyssinicus, commonly known as the Abyssinian carnation, is a flowering plant native to Ethiopia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, double-petaled flowers that come in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters and features grass-like, linear leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or containers.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding a striking visual element to gardens.
