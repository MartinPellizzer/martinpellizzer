Dortmanna debilis var. natalensis

Dortmanna debilis var. natalensis, commonly known as Natal's weak willow, is a small shrub native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, exhibiting a compact, bushy form. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in clusters, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

It is well adapted to coastal environments, tolerating salt spray and sandy conditions.
