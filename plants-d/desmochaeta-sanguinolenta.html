Desmochaeta sanguinolenta

Desmochaeta sanguinolenta, commonly known as the blood-red spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to parts of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its slender, creeping stems and vibrant red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly hairy texture.

Desmochaeta sanguinolenta thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded woodland areas.
