Draba incana var. borealis

Draba incana var. borealis, commonly known as the Arctic whitlow, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically forms dense mats of foliage. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in early spring. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold climates.

Its botanical features include hairy stems and leaves, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment.
