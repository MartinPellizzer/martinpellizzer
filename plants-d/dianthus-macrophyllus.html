Dianthus macrophyllus

Dianthus macrophyllus, commonly known as the showy campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its vibrant pink or red flowers, which are typically arranged in clusters at the top of sturdy stems. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, rounded fruits called capsules. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Dianthus macrophyllus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
