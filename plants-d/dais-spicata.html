Dais spicata

Dais spicata, commonly known as the spiky daisy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, spiky foliage and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and thrives in well-drained soil. It is drought-tolerant and prefers full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
