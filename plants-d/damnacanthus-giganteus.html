Damnacanthus giganteus

Damnacanthus giganteus, commonly known as giant devil's backbone, is a striking perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems that can reach heights of up to 6 feet, giving it a distinctive and somewhat intimidating appearance. The plant produces showy, red-orange bract-like flowers that are arranged in dense, terminal clusters, adding a dramatic visual element to the landscape. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture, and it thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its visual impact.
