Draba andina

Draba andina, commonly known as the Andean rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the high Andes of South America.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. This species typically grows in rocky, alpine environments at elevations above 3,000 meters. Draba andina produces small, white or yellow flowers and has a compact, low-growing habit.

Its leaves are usually basal, with a hairy texture adapted to the harsh mountain climate.
