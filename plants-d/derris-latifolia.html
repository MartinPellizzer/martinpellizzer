Derris latifolia

Derris latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf derris, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Leguminosae (Fabaceae) and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is notable for containing the alkaloid rotenone, which has insecticidal properties. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Derris latifolia thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.
