Dianthus anticarius subsp. maroccanus

Dianthus anticarius subsp.

maroccanus, commonly known as the Moroccan carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Maghreb region of North Africa. It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and displays showy, bilaterally symmetrical flowers in shades of red, pink, and white.

The plant features linear, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered fruits known as capsules. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its ornamental value and adaptability contribute to its cultivation in both wild and horticultural settings.
