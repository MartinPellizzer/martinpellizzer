Dianthus × lucae

Dianthus × lucae, commonly known as the hybrid carnation, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is a cross between species of the genus Dianthus, resulting in a variety that combines the ornamental qualities of its parent plants. The plant typically produces showy, clustered flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, often with contrasting darker veins. It is valued for its compact growth habit and ability to thrive in a range of garden conditions.

Dianthus × lucae is widely cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and floral arrangements.
