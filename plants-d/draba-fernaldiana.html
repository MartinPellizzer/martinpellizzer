Draba fernaldiana

Draba fernaldiana, commonly known as Fernald's whitlow grass, is a small flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many cruciferous plants. This species typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers are small, white or pale yellow, and it produces a capsule fruit containing numerous seeds.

Draba fernaldiana is often found in open habitats such as meadows and slopes at high elevations.
