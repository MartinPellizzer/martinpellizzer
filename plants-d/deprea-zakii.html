Deprea zakii

Deprea zakii, commonly known as the Kashmiri bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, thriving at elevations between 2,500 and 4,500 meters. The plant features basal rosettes of lanceolate, hairy leaves and produces showy blue or violet flowers in late summer. Its flowering stems are often branched and bear clusters of bell-shaped blooms.

Deprea zakii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
