Dioscorea cornifolia

Dioscorea cornifolia, commonly known as the horned morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant produces tuberous roots that are sometimes used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests or along stream banks.

Its rapid growth and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardeners in suitable climates.
