Dendropemon silvae

Dendropemon silvae, commonly known as the forest mist fern, is a perennial plant native to the dense, humid forests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, arching fronds that can grow up to two meters in length. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found growing on the forest floor or on the trunks of trees. Its rhizomes are fibrous and help it anchor to the substrate while absorbing nutrients from the soil.

Dendropemon silvae plays a vital role in maintaining the ecological balance of its habitat by contributing to the forest's biodiversity and soil stability.
