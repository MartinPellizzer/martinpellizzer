Dendrochilum exalatum

Dendrochilum exalatum, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems and clusters of showy, golden-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist forest environments, preferring well-drained soil. Its flowers bloom during the rainy season, adding vibrant color to the undergrowth.

Dendrochilum exalatum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
