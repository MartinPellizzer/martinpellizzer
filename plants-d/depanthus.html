Depanthus

Depanthus, commonly known as the "false indigo" or "blue mist shrub," is a genus of flowering plants in the family Polygonaceae.

It consists of several species, most notably Depanthus luteus, which is native to North America. The plant is characterized by its striking blue or purple flower clusters that appear in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is typically bright green with a slightly waxy texture, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Depanthus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
