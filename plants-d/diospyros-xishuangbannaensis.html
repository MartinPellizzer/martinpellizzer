Diospyros xishuangbannaensis

Diospyros xishuangbannaensis, commonly known as the Xishuangbanna persimmon, is a species of flowering plant in the Ebenaceae family.

It is native to southern China, particularly the Xishuangbanna region in Yunnan Province. The plant is a small to medium-sized tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall, with smooth, greyish-brown bark. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and elliptic in shape, with a glossy upper surface and a pale underside.

The fruit is a small, round berry that turns from green to orange-red when mature, often used in traditional Chinese medicine and local cuisine.
