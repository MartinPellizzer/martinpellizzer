Dianthus amurensis

Dianthus amurensis, commonly known as the Amur carnation, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, showy pink or red flowers with frilled petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has linear, grass-like leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Dianthus amurensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
