Dianthus borussicus

Dianthus borussicus, commonly known as the German carnation, is a flowering plant native to Central Europe.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to deep red. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and thrives in well-drained soil. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Dianthus borussicus is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, which includes other popular flowering plants like carnations and pinks.
