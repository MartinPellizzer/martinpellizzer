Dombeya modestiformis

Dombeya modestiformis, commonly known as the dwarf dombeya, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Africa.

It features clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has smooth, gray-green leaves that are opposite and oval in shape. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or forested habitats.

Dombeya modestiformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
