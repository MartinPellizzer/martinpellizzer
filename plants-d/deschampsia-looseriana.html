Deschampsia looseriana

Deschampsia looseriana, commonly known as the Looser's feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and delicate, feathery seed heads that appear in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. Its slender, erect stems and low-growing habit make it an attractive addition to rock gardens and alpine landscapes.

Deschampsia looseriana is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
