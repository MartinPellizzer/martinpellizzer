Didymoplexis vietnamica

Didymoplexis vietnamica, commonly known as the Vietnamese orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia, particularly Vietnam.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, tropical environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette, while its inflorescence consists of small, clustered blooms.

Didymoplexis vietnamica is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
