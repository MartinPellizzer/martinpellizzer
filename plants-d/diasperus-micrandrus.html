Diasperus micrandrus

Diasperus micrandrus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rock cress, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. Its leaves are typically narrow and linear, adapted to reduce water loss in harsh environments.

Diasperus micrandrus is often found growing on rocky outcrops and in sandy soils, where it thrives with minimal water.
