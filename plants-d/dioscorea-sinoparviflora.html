Dioscorea sinoparviflora

Dioscorea sinoparviflora, commonly known as the Chinese yam, is a perennial vine native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and tuberous roots. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a rough texture, and the tubers are often used in traditional medicine and as a food source.

The botanical features of D. sinoparviflora make it an important species in both horticulture and pharmacology.
