Drypetes nakaiana

Drypetes nakaiana, commonly known as the Malabar ironwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant has simple, opposite, and usually elliptical leaves that are dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, round, and woody fruits.

The species is known for its ecological importance, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
