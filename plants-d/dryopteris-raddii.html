Dryopteris raddii

Dryopteris raddii, commonly known as the red-veined wood fern, is a species of fern native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive fronds with deep red veins contrasting against its dark green foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its rhizomes are thick and creeping, allowing it to spread and form dense colonies.

Dryopteris raddii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
