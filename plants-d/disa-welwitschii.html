Disa welwitschii

Disa welwitschii, commonly known as the Welwitsch Disa, is a striking orchid species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous pale purple to white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens. Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a desirable addition to both cultivated and natural landscapes.

Disa welwitschii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its beauty.
