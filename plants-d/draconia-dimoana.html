Draconia dimoana

Draconia dimoana, commonly known as the dragon's breath plant, is a member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its striking, lance-shaped leaves and vibrant, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its flowers are usually bright red or orange, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.

Draconia dimoana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual impact.
