Duranta repens var. domingensis

Duranta repens var. domingensis, commonly known as the Mexican bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of the Caribbean.

It is a trailing or shrub-like species that produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with slightly serrated edges, and it thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adaptable to various soil types.

The fruit is a fleshy, yellow or orange capsule that contains small, black seeds.
