Desmodium mexicanum

Desmodium mexicanum, commonly known as Mexican burr legume, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces small, yellow to green pea-like flowers. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several seeds, often with a prickly surface.

It is often found in disturbed soils and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
